> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: an unannounced gift of staple or non-staple food that one has received, when not ill, in a dubious and risky wilderness lodging. 2) Effort: One eats the food
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If it is announced, if he is ill; if he eats the remainder of (a meal) if it was announced or of one who was ill; if having accepted it outside the monastery he makes use of it inside the monastery; if he makes use of a root or bark, or a leaf or a flower or a fruit growing there; if when there is a reason he makes use of (food to be eaten) during a watch of the night, during seven days, during life; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Wilderness lodging as one at least one kilometer, from the nearest village, measuring by the shortest walkable path between the two, and not as the crow flies. Such a lodging is considered dubious if signs of thieves have been seen. A bhikkhu counts as ill if he is unable to go for alms. penalties apply not only to the bhikkhu who accepts these items directly from the donor(s), but also to all other bhikkhus who take these items and eat them. a wise policy for a bhikkhu living in such a lodging is to announce to all his supporters beforehandâ€”and ask them to spread the wordâ€”that if they want to bring him gifts of food, they have to come and announce the gifts in advance and his dwelling is dangerous
