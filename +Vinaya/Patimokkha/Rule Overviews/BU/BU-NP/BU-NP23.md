# bhikkhus - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Pattavagga - Bowl
## BU-NP23 - Bhesajja - Tonics Five

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yāni kho pana tāni gilānānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ paṭisāyanīyāni bhesajjāni, seyyathidaṃ – sappi navanītaṃ telaṃ madhu phāṇitaṃ, tāni paṭiggahetvā sattāhaparamaṃ sannidhikārakaṃ paribhuñjitabbāni, taṃ atikkāmayato nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ | After being received, the tonics allowable for sick bhikkhus—that is, ghee, butter, oil, honey, and syrup— should be used from storage for at most seven days. If one uses them longer than that, one commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: any of the five tonics. 2) Effort: One keeps the tonic past the seventh dawnrise after receiving it
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If within seven days the tonic gets lost, destroyed, burnt, snatched away, or taken on trust; or if the bhikkhu determines it for use, abandons it, or—having given it away to an unordained person, abandoning desire for it—he receives it in return and makes use of it if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Acquiring something that has been forfeited,it must not be made use of for bodily enjoyment nor consumed. any tonic received today may be eaten mixed with food or juice drinks received today, but not with food or juice drinks received on a later day. one may take the tonic at any time during those seven days regardless of whether one is ill
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Idaṁ me bhante bhesajjaṁ sattāhātikkantaṁ nissaggiyaṁ. Imāhaṁ āyasmato nissajjāmi, which means, “This medicine of mine, venerable sir, kept beyond seven days, is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to
>
