# bhikkhus - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Pattavagga - Bowl
## BU-NP22 - Ūnapañcabandhana - Bowl With Fewer Than 5 Mends

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu ūnapañcabandhanena pattena aññaṁ navaṁ pattaṁ cetāpeyya, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Tena bhikkhunā so patto bhikkhuparisāya nissajjitabbo. Yo ca tassā bhikkhuparisāya pattapariyanto so tassa bhikkhuno padātabbo— ‘ayaṁ te, bhikkhu, patto yāva bhedanāya dhāretabbo’ti. Ayaṁ tattha sāmīcī | If a monk exchanges an almsbowl with fewer than five mends for a new almsbowl, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession. That monk should relinquish that almsbowl to a gathering of bhikkhus. He should then be given the last almsbowl belonging to that gathering: “Monk, this bowl is yours. Keep it until it breaks.” This is the proper procedure |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Effort: Before one’s alms bowl is beyond repair, one asks for 2) Object: a new almsbowl fit to be determined for use. 3) Result: One obtains the bowl
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If the bowl is destroyed, if the bowl is broken, if they belong to relations, if they are invited, if it is for another, if it is by means of his own property; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Relinquish to Sangha according to procedure. this rule allows a bhikkhu whose bowl is beyond repair to ask for a new one. Community chooses one of its members as bowl exchanger. If the new bowl is preferable to one's own and yet one do not take it out of sympathy for the offender, is a dukkaṭa, but for contentment with what he has, no offense
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Ayaṁ me bhante patto ūna-pañca-bandhanena pattena cetāpito nissaggiyo. Imāhaṁ saṅghassa nissajjāmi. This means, “This bowl of mine, venerable sirs, asked for when the (previous) bowl had less than five mends, is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to the Community.”
>
