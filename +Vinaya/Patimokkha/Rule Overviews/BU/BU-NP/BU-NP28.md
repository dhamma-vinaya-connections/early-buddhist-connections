# bhikkhus - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Pattavagga - Bowl
## BU-NP28 - Accekacīvara - Robe in Urgency

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Dasāhānāgataṁ kattikatemāsikapuṇṇamaṁ bhikkhuno paneva accekacīvaraṁ uppajjeyya, accekaṁ maññamānena bhikkhunā paṭiggahetabbaṁ, paṭiggahetvā yāva cīvarakālasamayaṁ nikkhipitabbaṁ. Tato ce uttari nikkhipeyya, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyan | When there are ten days left to the Kattika full moon that ends the first rainy-season residence and haste-cloth is given to a monk, he may receive it if he regards it as urgent. He may then store it until the end of the robe season. If he stores it beyond that, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: Robe-cloth offered in urgency is any piece of the six allowable kinds of robe-cloth, measuring at least four by eight fingerbreadths. The does not want to wait until the robe season to make an offering. 2 effort—one keeps it past the end of the robe season
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If, within the season, it is allotted, assigned, bestowed, lost, destroyed, burnt, if they tear it (from him), if they take it on trust; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > The third-month Kattika full moon is the first full moon in October. This is the final day of the first Rains-residence, and the day before the beginning of the robe season. donor offers robe-material to a particular monk before the end of the Rains-Retreat, then it may be accepted by him, but in no circumstances does this count as an acceka-cīvara. An acceka-cīvara ia a Rains-Retreat gift given to the Sangha at a monastery within ten days of the end of the Rains-Retreat
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Idaṁ me bhante acceka-cīvaraṁ cīvara-kāla-samayaṁ atikkāmitaṁ nissaggiyaṁ. Imāhaṁ āyasmato nissajjāmi, which means, “This robe-cloth-offered-in-urgency of mine, venerable sir, kept beyond the robe season, is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to you.”
>
