> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: any piece of the six kinds of suitable robe-cloth, measuring at least four by eight fingerbreadths. 2) Effort: One asks for more than the allowable amount of robe-cloth from an unrelated householder who has not previously made an invitation to ask. 3) Result: One gets the excess robe-cloth
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > One takes excess cloth with the promise to return the excess when one has finished one's robe(s), and the donors tell one to keep the excess the donors are offering cloth for reasons other than that one's robes were snatched away or destroyed one is asking from one's relatives or people who have previously made one an invitation to ask for cloth or one gets the cloth by means of one's own resources
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > The allowance in the preceding rule applies only when robes from one's basic set of three, If one's spare robes get snatched away or destroyed, one may not make use of the allowance to ask for robe-cloth at all. one should take only enough cloth to make the allowable number of robes. one may take excess cloth if one promises to return the excess when one has finished making one's robe(s). And if the donor tells one to keep the excess, one may do so without penalty
