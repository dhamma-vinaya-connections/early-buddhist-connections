> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: A piece of any of the six allowable types of robe-cloth, measuring at least four by eight fingerbreadths, which is being made for oneâ€™s sake by the arrangement of a donor who is unrelated and has not given an invitation to ask. 2 Effort: One approaches the weavers and gets them to improve the cloth in any of the seven ways mentioned in the rule. 3 Result: One obtains the cloth
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If it belongs to relations; if they are invited; if it is for another; if it is by means of his own property; if desirous of having costly (robe-material) woven he has (robe-material) costing little woven; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Whether or not a gift is offered to the weaver, the offences under this rule are still the same. (Some have objected that it should be all right for the bhikkhu to pay in full for the improvements in the robe, but remember that to do so would be an insult to the donors.)
