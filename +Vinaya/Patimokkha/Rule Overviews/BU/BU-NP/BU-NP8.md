> > > From BMC1

> > 1 Object. The texts mention only that this rule concerns funds for a robe (cÄ«vara) would probably mean a finished robe. 2 Intention. One wants to get a better robe than the lay person is planning to buy. \"better quality, higher price.\" 3 Effort. One requests the unrelated lay person to improve the robe. 4 Result. One gets the long, broad, etc., robe that the householder bought in line with one's request
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If they belong to relations, if they are invited; if it is for another; if it is by means of his own property; if he gets something of small value in exchange while he desires to get something costly in exchange; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
