# bhikkhus - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Kosiyavagga - Silk
## BU-NP20 - Kayavikkaya - Bartering

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu nānappakārakaṃ kayavikkayaṃ samāpajjeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ | If any bhikkhu should engage in the various kinds of trading, [this is a case] involving expiation with forfeiture |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object. All types of trade not covered in previous rule. 2 Effort. Engaging in trade by proposing and ones goods ending up with the other person, and the other person’s goods ending up with the bhikkhu
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he asks the price of an object; if he tells a steward; if he tells the seller, “I have this. I have need of such-and-such\", and then lets the seller arrange the exchange as he/she sees fit. if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Any transaction in which the end product is other than gold or money, i.e. buying as well as bartering the forfeited article should be returned to the original offender not to return the article would be a dukkata. No exemptions for a bhikkhu who trades using goods belonging to someone else. The Vibhaṅga makes no mention of what the bhikkhu may and may not do with the article after receiving it in return, but commentary have protocols. Present rule thus covers trades made only with not one’s co-religionists. bhikkhus should not bargain, if he sees that the steward is getting a bad deal, he may simply tell the steward, “Don’t take it Checks, credit cards, debit cards, and traveler’s checks do not count as gold or money. However, any trade arranged with them would come under this rule
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Ahaṁ bhante nānappakārakaṁ kaya-vikkayaṁ samāpajjiṁ. Idaṁ me nissaggiyaṁ. Imāhaṁ āyasmato nissajjāmi, which means, “Venerable sir, I have engaged in various types of trade. This of mine is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to you.” To return the article: Imaṁ āyasmato dammi, which means, “I give this to you.”
>
