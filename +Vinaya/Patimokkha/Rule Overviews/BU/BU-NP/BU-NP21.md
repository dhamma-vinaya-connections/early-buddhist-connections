# bhikkhus - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Pattavagga - Bowl
## BU-NP21 - Patta - Bowl Extra

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Ayaṃ te bhikkhu patto yāva bhedanāya dhāretabbo\"ti, ayaṃ tattha sāmīci | A monk should keep an extra almsbowl for ten days at the most. If he keeps it longer than that, he commits an offense entailing relinquishment and confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: an alms bowl fit to be determined for use. 2) Effort: One keeps it for more than ten days without determining it for use, placing it under shared ownership, abandoning it (giving or throwing it away); and without its being lost, destroyed, burnt, snatched away, or taken by someone else on trust within that time
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If within ten days it is allotted, assigned, bestowed, lost, destroyed, broken, if they tear it from him, if they take it on trust; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Should be given back after relinquish and confess. bowl fit to be determined for use must be— 1) made of the proper material; 2) the proper size; 3) fully paid for; 4) properly fired; and 5) not damaged beyond repair
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Ayaṁ me bhante patto dasāhātikkanto nissaggiyo. Imāhaṁ āyasmato nissajjāmi, which means, “This bowl of mine, venerable sir, kept beyond ten days, is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to you.”
>
