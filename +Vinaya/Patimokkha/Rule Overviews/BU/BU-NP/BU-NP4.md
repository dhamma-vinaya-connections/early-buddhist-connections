# bhikkhus - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Kathinavagga - Robes
## BU-NP4 - Purāṇacīvara - Robes Used

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpeyya vā rajāpeyya vā ākoṭāpeyya vā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ | If any bhikkhu should have an old robe [-cloth] washed, dyed, or beaten by an unrelated bhikkhunī, [this is a case] involving relinquishing with confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: a used robe. sitting cloths and bed sheets are grounds for a dukkaṭa. 2 Effort. One tells an unrelated bhikkhunī to wash, dye, or beat the robe. Female trainees and female novices are not grounds for an offense. 3 Result. The bhikkhunī washes, dyes, or beats the robe as requested
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > When a female relation is washing it if a woman assistant who is not a relation is (helping); if she washes it unasked; if he makes her wash an unused one; if he makes her wash another requisite, except the robe; if it is (washed) by a female probationer, by a female novice; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Unrelated is explained by the Vibhaṅga as meaning unrelated back through seven grandfathers. In-laws, are not. But good advise is to regard as his relatives only those blood-relations with whom ties of kinship are actually felt
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Idaṁ me bhante cīvaraṁ nissaggiyaṁ. Imāhaṁ āyasmato nissajjāmi, which means, “This robe of mine, venerable sir, is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to you.”
>
