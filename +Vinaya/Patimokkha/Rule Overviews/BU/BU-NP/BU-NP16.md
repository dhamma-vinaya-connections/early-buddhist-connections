# bhikkhus - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Kosiyavagga - Silk
## BU-NP16 - Eḷakaloma - Carrying Wool

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Bhikkhuno paneva addhānamaggappaṭipannassa eḷakalomāni uppajjeyyuṃ, ākaṅkhamānena bhikkhunā paṭiggahetabbāni, paṭiggahetvā tiyojanaparamaṃ sahatthā haritabbāni asante hārake. tato ce uttari hareyya, asantepi hārake, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ | Now, if sheep’s wool should become available to a bhikkhu who is travelling on a main road, by a bhikkhu who is wishing [so, it] can be accepted, having accepted [it, it] can be carried with his own hand for three yojanas at the most when there is no one present who can carry it; if he should carry it further than that, even when there is no one present who can carry it, [this is a case] involving relinquishment and confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object. Wool, under this rule, refers to wool that has not been made into goods. 2 Effort includes not only carrying unmade wool more than three leagues (48 km.=30 miles) oneself, but also placing it in a bundle or vehicle belonging to someone else without his/her knowing about it, and then letting him/her take it more than three leagues. 3 Intention. to carry wool past the three-league mark for motives other than looking for a place to stay
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > When someone else carries the wool for him, When he is unable to find a suitable place to stay within 3 yojanas and so he carries the wool further, looking for such a place. When, having lost possession of that wool, it is later returned to him and he carries it up to another 3 yojana
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > 1 yojana is around 10 miles or 16 kilometres
> 
>
