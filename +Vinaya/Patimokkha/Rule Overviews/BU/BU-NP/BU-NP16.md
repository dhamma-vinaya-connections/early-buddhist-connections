> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object. Wool, under this rule, refers to wool that has not been made into goods. 2 Effort includes not only carrying unmade wool more than three leagues (48 km.=30 miles) oneself, but also placing it in a bundle or vehicle belonging to someone else without his/her knowing about it, and then letting him/her take it more than three leagues. 3 Intention. to carry wool past the three-league mark for motives other than looking for a place to stay
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > When someone else carries the wool for him, When he is unable to find a suitable place to stay within 3 yojanas and so he carries the wool further, looking for such a place. When, having lost possession of that wool, it is later returned to him and he carries it up to another 3 yojana
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > 1 yojana is around 10 miles or 16 kilometres
