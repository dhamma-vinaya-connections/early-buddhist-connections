# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 2 Bhūtagāma-Vaggo - 2 Plants
## BU-PC17 - Nikkaḍḍhana - Throwing Out

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuṃ kupito anattamano saṅghikā vihārā nikkaḍḍheyya vā nikkaḍḍhāpeyya vā, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk, in anger, throws a monk out of a dwelling belonging to the Sangha, or has him thrown out, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: a bhikkhu. 2) Effort: One evicts him from a dwelling belonging to the Community. 3) Intention: One’s prime impulse is anger
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > In evicting anyone when one’s primary impulse is not anger, belongings from one’s own dwelling or one take on trust, if evicting anyone—or the requisites of anyone—who is insane, unconscientious in his/her behavior, or a maker of quarrels, strife, and dissension in the Community. if evict one’s student or his belongings from his dwelling if he is not properly observing his duties
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Bhikkhu is pācittiya, while dukkaṭa: a bhikkhu’s belongings, an unordained person, and an unordained person’s belongings. dukkaṭa in telling someone else to evict the bhikkhu. if one’s primary motive is greed, anger was simply a function of frustrated greed, and the two emotions would easily go together to infraction of this rule
> 
>
