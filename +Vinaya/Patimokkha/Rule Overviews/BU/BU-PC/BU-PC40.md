# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 4 Bhojana-Vaggo - 4 Food
## BU-PC40 - Dantapona - Eating Unoffered Food

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu adinnaṃ mukhadvāraṃ āhāraṃ āhareyya aññatra udakadantaponā, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk eats food that has not been given, except for water and tooth cleaners, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: edible is whatever is fit to eat, that has not been given. 2 Effort: dukkaṭa if, with the intention of eating it, he takes food that hasn’t been properly given; and a pācittiya for every mouthful he eats
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > In regard to water for cleansing the teeth; if himself, having taken the four foul things, he makes use of them when there is a reason (and if) there is no one to make them allowable; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Allowance in Cv 5 26 states that if food accidentally falls while being offered, a bhikkhu may pick it up himself and eat it without committing an offense
>
