# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 4 Bhojana-Vaggo - 4 Food
## BU-PC33 - Paramparabhojana - Eating Successive Meals

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Paramparabhojane aññatra samayā pācittiyaṃ. tatthāyaṃ samayo, gilānasamayo, cīvaradānasamayo, cīvarakārasamayo, ayaṃ tattha samayo | If a monk eats a meal before another, except on an appropriate occasion, he commits an offense entailing confession. These are the appropriate occasions: he is sick; it is the robe-giving season; it is a time of making robes |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: term out-of-turn meal covers two A bhikkhu has been invited to a meal consisting of any of the five staple foods but then either 1 goes elsewhere and eats another meal consisting of any of the five staple foods at the same time he was invited or 2 before the invitation 2 effort: accepting with the thought of eating food that will constitute an out-of-turn meal,
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If it is at a right time; if he eats, having assigned (a meal); if he eats two or three invitations together; if he eats the invitations in succession; if invited by a whole village he eats anywhere in that village; if invited by a whole guild he eats anywhere in that guild; if being invited, he speaks saying: “I will take alms-food”; if it is the regular supply of food; if it is food (allowed by) ticket; if it is (food given) on a day of the waxing or waning of the moon,if it is (given) on an Observance day, or the day after; setting aside the five (kinds of) meals, there is no offence in (eating) any other; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > “I allow you, bhikkhus, having assigned (food to another), to eat an out-of-turn meal. If eat before non staple food, one should not take so much as to spoil one’s appetite for the meal. where there are fewer bhikkhus than there are donors. One bhikkhu would be allowed to accept more than one meal so that no family’s meal would go without a recipient
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Should it be assigned: ‘I will give the meal that I am expecting to so and so.’
>
