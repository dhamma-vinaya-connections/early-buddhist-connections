# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 3 Bhikkhunovāda-Vaggo - 3 Instruction
## BU-PC27 - Saṃvidhāna - Travelling by Arrangement

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjeyya antamaso gāmantarampi aññatra samayā, pācittiyaṃ. tatthāyaṃ samayo, satthagamanīyo hoti maggo, sāsaṅkasammato, sappaṭibhayo, ayaṃ tattha samayo | If a monk travels by arrangement with a nun, even just to the next village, except on an appropriate occasion, he commits an offense entailing confession. This is the appropriate occasion: the road is considered risky and dangerous and should be traveled with a group |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: a bhikkhunī. 2) Effort: (a) Having made an arrangement together with her to travel together, (b) one actually travels together with her as arranged (c) from one village to another (d) except on the allowable occasions
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If it is at the right time; if he goes not having arranged; if the nun arranges (and) the monk does not arrange; if they go without (making) a appointment; if there are dangers; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > As long as the bhikkhu and bhikkhunī do not address each other—directly or through an intermediary—about making the trip, there would be no offense in joining a group
> 
>
