# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 2 Bhūtagāma-Vaggo - 2 Plants
## BU-PC18 - Vehāsakuṭi - Upper Story

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu saṅghike vihāre uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ vā pīṭhaṃ vā abhinisīdeyya vā abhinipajjeyya vā, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk sits down or lies down on a bed or a bench with detachable legs on an upper story in a dwelling belonging to the Sangha, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: loft is a partial second story in a dwelling; and bed or bench with detachable legs 2) Effort: sitting and lying down on it
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If there is no one under the loft; if the area under the loft cannot be used as a dwelling, if the bed or bench with detachable legs is on the ground; or are securely fixed to their frame
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Perhaps the Buddha’s purpose in formulating this rule was to discourage detachable legs their being placed in the loft in the first place
> 
>
