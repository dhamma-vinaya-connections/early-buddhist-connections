# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 6 Surāpāna-Vaggo - 6 Alcoholic Drinks
## BU-PC59 - Vikappana - Shared Ownership

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhussa vā bhikkhuniyā vā sikkhamānāya vā sāmaṇerassa vā sāmaṇeriyā vā sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ vikappetvā appaccuddhāraṇaṃ paribhuñjeyya, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk himself assigns the ownership of a robe to a monk, to a nun, to a trainee nun, to a novice monk, or to a novice nun, and he then uses it without the other first relinquishing it, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: any one of the six kinds of robe-cloth, measuring at least four by eight fingerbreadths, that one has placed under shared ownership; 2 Effort: one uses the cloth without the shared ownership’s being rescinded
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he gives it, or if putting his trust in him, if the shared ownership has been rescinded then he makes use of it; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer. for legitimately taking an item on trust are as follows (Mv 8 19.1): 1) The other person is a friend. 2) He/she is an intimate. 3) He/she has spoken of the matter. 4) He/she is still alive. 5) One knows that he/she will be pleased at one’s taking it
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Cloth that has not been made into a finished robe, rains-bathing cloths being kept outside of the rainy season, and skin-eruption covering cloths being kept when they are not needed, may all be placed under shared ownership for any length of time. The three basic robes, miscellaneous requisites, handkerchiefs, and the sitting cloth may not
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > “I place this robe-cloth under shared ownership with you (plural)” or “with so-and-so.” To rescinde ownership the second owner to say: “Use what is mine, give it away, or do as you like with it.”
>
