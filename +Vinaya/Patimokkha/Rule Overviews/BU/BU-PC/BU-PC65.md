# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 7 Sappāṇaka-Vaggo - 7 Animals
## BU-PC65 - Ūnavīsativassa - Ordaining Under 20

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ ūnavīsativassaṃ puggalaṃ upasampādeyya, so ca puggalo anupasampanno, te ca bhikkhū gārayhā, idaṃ tasmiṃ pācittiyaṃ | If a monk gives the full ordination to a person he knows is less than twenty years old, he commits an offense entailing confession. Moreover, that person has not received the full ordination and those bhikkhus are blameworthy |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: a man less than 20 years old. 2) Perception: One knows that he is less than 20 years old—either from knowing on one’s own, from having been told by the man, or from having been told by others. 3) Effort: One acts as the preceptor in his full Acceptance as a bhikkhu
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he confers the upasampadā ordination on one under twenty years of age thinking that he has completed twenty years of age; if he confers the upasampadā ordination on one who has completed twenty years of age thinking that they are completed; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Fro Mv 1 75 a person’s age for the purose of this rule is counted from the time he becomes a fetus in his mother’s womb , the usual practice in calculating a person’s age is to add six months if the individual is really less than 20 years old when he is accepted, then—regardless of whether he or anyone else knows of the fact—he does not count as a bhikkhu and is only a novice
>
