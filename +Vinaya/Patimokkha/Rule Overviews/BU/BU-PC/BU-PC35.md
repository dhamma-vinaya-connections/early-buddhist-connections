# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 4 Bhojana-Vaggo - 4 Food
## BU-PC35 - Pavāraṇā - Eating More After Refusing an Invitation

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu bhuttāvī pavārito anatirittaṃ khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk has finished his meal and refused an invitation to eat more, and then eats fresh or cooked food that is not left over, he commits an offense entailing confession.’” |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: staple or non-staple food that is not leftover. 2) Effort: One eats the food after having eaten and turned down an offer of further food
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If, having caused it to be made left over, he accepts it, eat it or thinks: “I will eat”; if he goes away, conveying it for the sake of another; if he eats the remainder of an ill (monk’s meal); if, when there is a reason, he makes use of (food to be eaten) during a watch of the night, during seven days, during life; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > A donor could feel insulted if bhikkhus refused further offers of food, finished their meal, and then went to eat someplace else. It is also designed to protect bhikkhus from being forced to go hungry by stingy or impoverished donors
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > What is left over should be made left over, saying, ‘All this is enough
>
