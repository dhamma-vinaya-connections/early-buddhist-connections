# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 4 Bhojana-Vaggo - 4 Food
## BU-PC31 - Āvasathapiṇḍa - Eating at a Public Guest-house

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Agilānena bhikkhunā eko āvasathapiṇḍo bhuñjitabbo. tato ce uttari bhuñjeyya, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk who is not sick eats more than one alms-meal at a public guesthouse, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: public alms center is a place where all comers are offered as much food as they want, free of charge. 2 Effort: eating in the center two days running,
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he is ill; if he eats once when he is not ill; if he eats going out or coming in; if the proprietors, having invited him, offer him food, if it is specially prepared (for him); if the center determines the amount of food the recipients may take; setting aside the five (kinds of) meals there is no offence in (eating) any others; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Eating in the same center two days running, To eat one day in one center and the next day in another entails no penalty
> 
>
