# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 6 Surāpāna-Vaggo - 6 Alcoholic Drinks
## BU-PC51 - Surāpāna - Alcohol Drinking

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Surāmerayapāne pācittiyaṃ | ‘If a monk drinks this or that kind of alcoholic drink, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: any alcoholic beverages 2 Effort: Consume it, even as little as the tip of a blade of grass
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he drinks that which is not strong drink though it comes to be the colour of strong drink, the smell of strong drink, the taste of strong drink; if eat dishes to which alcoholic beverages, such as wine, are added for flavoring before they are cooked;. if he drinks a distilled liquor that is not strong drink; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Alcohol is criticized for it destroys one’s sense of shame, weakens one’s discernment, and can put one into a stupor, so it seems reasonable to extend this rule to other intoxicants, narcotics, and hallucinogens with these effects as well. Coffee, tea, tobacco, and betel do not have this effect, though
>
