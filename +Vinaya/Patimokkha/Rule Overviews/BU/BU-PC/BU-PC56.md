# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 6 Surāpāna-Vaggo - 6 Alcoholic Drinks
## BU-PC56 - Jotika - Fire Kindling

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu agilāno visibbanāpekkho jotiṃ samādaheyya vā samādahāpeyya vā aññatra tathārūpappaccayā, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk who is not sick lights a fire to warm himself, or has one lit, except if there is a suitable reason, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: One is not ill. 2) Effort: One lights a fire or gets someone else to light one 3) Intention: for the purpose of warming oneself
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he is ill; if he warms himself at one made by another; if he warms himself over raked-out embers; if at a lamp, in a fire-room, in a bath-room; if there is a sufficient reason for it; if there are dangers; if he is mad; if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Solar or electric heating systems, which do not use flames, would not be included here
> 
>
