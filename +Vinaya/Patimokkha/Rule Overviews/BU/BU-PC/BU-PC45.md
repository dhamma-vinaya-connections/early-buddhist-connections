# bhikkhus - Pacittiya - Confession
## 5 Acelaka-Vaggo - 5 Ascetics
## BU-PC45 - Rahonisajja - Sitting in Private

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappeyya, pācittiyaṃ | If a monk sits down in private alone with a woman, he commits an offense entailing confession |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Effort: One sits with her alone—without another person present—in a place private to the ear and to the eye, but not secluded. 2) Object: woman is defined as female human being who knows what is properly an what is lewd. Paṇḍakas, female petas, female yakkhas, and animals in the form of a woman are grounds for a dukkaṭa. 3) Intention: One must be aiming at privacy for this factor to be fulfilled
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If some learned friend comes to be (present); if he stands, does not sit; if he is not desirous of a private place; if he sits down thinking about something else; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > If it would be convenient for committing sexual intercourse, it would fall under the preceding rule; if not, it would fall here. In this rule the other person whose presence exempts one from this factor can be either a man or a woman
>
