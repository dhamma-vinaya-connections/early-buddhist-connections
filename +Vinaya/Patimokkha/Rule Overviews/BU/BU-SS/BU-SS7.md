# bhikkhus - Sanghadisesa - Suspension
##  - 1 Admonition
## BU-SS7 - Vihārakāra - Building a Dwelling

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Mahallakaṃ pana bhikkhunā vihāraṃ kārayamānena sassāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ bhikkhū abhinetabbā vatthudesanāya, tehi bhikkhūhi vatthu desetabbaṃ anārambhaṃ saparikkamanaṃ. sārambhe ce bhikkhu vatthusmiṃ aparikkamane mahallakaṃ vihāraṃ kāreyya, bhikkhū vā anabhineyya vatthudesanāya, saṅghādiseso | When a large dwelling (vihara) with a donor is being made by a bhikkhu and is intended for himself then bhikkhus should be invited in order to appoint a site and they should appoint a site which is not dangerous and has a clearing around it. If a bhikkhu should make a large dwelling on a site which is dangerous and without a clearing, or should not invite bhikkhus in order to appoint a site, it is a sanghadisesa offence |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1. Plastered kuñi 2. 3. Not being approved by the Saïgha 4. Being built to reside in it 5. Giving the last lump of plaster 6. Intended for oneself 7. Maximum measurement
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he finishes by himself what was imperfectly executed by himself, if it is (built) in a mountain-cave as a hut, as a hut of tiṇa-grass, for the good of another except it be as a house, there is no offence in any of these circumstances, nor if he is out of his mind or a beginner
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > All explanations for this rule may be inferred from those above, the only difference being that, as the dwelling here has a sponsor, no begging is involved in its construction and so there is no need to limit its size
> 
>
