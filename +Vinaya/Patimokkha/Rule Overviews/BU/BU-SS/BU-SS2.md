# bhikkhus - Sanghadisesa - Suspension
##  - 1 Admonition
## BU-SS2 - Kāyasaṁsagga - Physical Contact

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu otiṇṇo vipariṇatena cittena mātugāmena saddhiṃ kāyasaṃsaggaṃ samāpajjeyya hatthaggāhaṃ vā veṇiggāhaṃ vā aññatarassa vā aññatarassa vā aṅgassa parāmasanaṃ, saṅghādiseso | Whatever bhikkhu being lustful and with perverted mind should engage in physical contact with a woman; holding her hand, her hair or touching any other part of her body, has committed a sanghadisesa offence |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1. The object is a human female 2. Perceives the object to be a woman 3. Acting with lustful intension 4. Make an effort to have physical contact with her 5. Engages in contact by holding hands etc
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If it is not on purpose, not intentional, not knowing, not agreeing, if he is mad, unhinged, in pain, a beginner, the bhikkhu does not desire contact or he does desire contact and yet makes no effort
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > This rule not because women are bad, but because bhikkhus sometimes can be. thullaccaya for making bodily contact with a paṇḍaka, a female yakkha, or a dead woman; and a dukkaṭa for bodily contact with a man (or boy), a wooden doll, or a male or female animal. a bhikkhu who caresses his mother, sister, daughter out of familiar affection is dukkata Even a momentary desire to enjoy the sensation of physical contact is enough. Lustful bodily contact with a male transvestite perceived to be a woman dukkaṭa. For concept of anāmāsa not based in the Canon. the Buddha never ask the bhikkhu if he made contact with the clothed or unclothed portions of the woman’s body. This suggests whether she was clothed or unclothed is irrelevant to the offense. In passive contact he either desire the contact or the desire to escape
>
