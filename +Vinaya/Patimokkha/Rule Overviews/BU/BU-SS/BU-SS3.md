# bhikkhus - Sanghadisesa - Suspension
##  - 1 Admonition
## BU-SS3 - Duṭṭhullavācā - Indecent Speech

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Yo pana bhikkhu otiṇṇo vipariṇatena cittena mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāseyya yathā taṃ yuvā yuvatiṃ methunupasaṃhitāhi, saṅghādiseso | Whatever bhikkhu being lustful and with perverted mind should speak lewdly to a woman, just like a young man speaks to a young woman concerning sex, has committed a sanghadisesa offence |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: a woman, i.e., any female human being experienced enough to know what is properly said and improperly said, what is lewd and not lewd. 2) Perception: The bhikkhu perceives her to be a woman. 3) Intention: He is impelled by lust: He wants to enjoy saying something lewd or improper. 4) Effort: He makes remarks praising, criticizing, begging, imploring, asking, quizzing, advising, instructing, or insulting with reference to her genitals or anus, or to her performing sexual intercourse. 5) Result: The woman immediately understands
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he is aiming at (explaining) the meaning, if he is aiming at (explaining) dhamma, if he is aiming at (explaining) the teaching, if he is mad, if he is a beginner
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > A woman who does not know the language in which one is speaking would also not fulfill the factor of object here. Not necessarily having any desire actually to have sex with her. The Vibhaṅga makes clear that this rule does not cover statements or insults made in anger, they're cover in Pc 2. using slang expressions, euphemisms, or indirect statements fullfil this factor. If she understands only later, that does not turn the lesser offense into a saṅghādisesa
>
