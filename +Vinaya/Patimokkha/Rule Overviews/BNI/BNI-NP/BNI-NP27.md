# Nuns - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Jātaruparajata - Gold and Silver
## BNI-NP27 - Suttaviññatti - Thread Asking

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Mahāpesakāra: Yā pana bhikkhunī... tantavāyaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadeyya... nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. | 27. Instructing Weavers: Should any bhikkhunī instruct a weaver (paid by someone else) to make the robe better/wider/longer... it is to be forfeited and confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: Thread or yarn of the six allowable types for robe-cloth that a bhikkhu has himself requested from others. 2 Effort: One gets weavers to weave robe-cloth using the thread. 3 Result: One obtains the cloth
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > Is no offence to sew a robe to a binding, to a belt, to a shoulder-strap, to a bag for carrying the bowl in, to a water-strainer; if it belongs to relations; if they are invited; if it is for another; if it is by means of his own property; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > This rule covers two actions—asking for thread and getting weavers to weave it into robe-cloth
> 
>
