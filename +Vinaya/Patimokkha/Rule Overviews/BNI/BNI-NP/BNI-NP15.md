> [!Info] Analisis of the rule

> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: (a) out-of-season robe-cloth, made of any of the proper six kinds of material, in pieces measuring at least four by eight fingerbreadths; (b) the cloth is not enough to make the cloth requisite one has in mind, but one expects to receive more. 2) Effort: One keeps the cloth for more than 30 days, except when the kaá¹­hina privileges are in effect
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If within a month it is allotted, assigned, bestowed, lost, destroyed, burnt, if they tear it from him, if they take it on trust; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Cloth accruing to a bhikkhu during the one-month or five-month robe season can count as out-of-season cloth if the donors dedicate it to that purpose, in order to extend the period it can be kept or visiting bhikkhus. If the second cloth turns out to be of different quality from the first, one is under no compulsion to put the two cloths together to make up the requisite if one does not want to. out-of-season cloth received shortly before the beginning of the robe season, the countdown would begin when it is received, would be suspended throughout the robe season, and would resume at the robe season's end
