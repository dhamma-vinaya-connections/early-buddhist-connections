# Nuns - Nissaggiya Pacittiya - Relinquish with confession
## Jātaruparajata - Gold and Silver
## BNI-NP26 - Cīvaraacchindana - Robe-Cloth Taking Back

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Suttaviññāpana: Yā pana bhikkhunī attanā viññāpetvā suttaṃ tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. | 26. Asking for Yarn: Should any bhikkhunī ask for yarn and have a robe woven by weavers... it is to be forfeited and confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: A piece of any of the six allowable kinds of robe-cloth, measuring at least four by eight fingerbreadths. 2 Effort: One has given the cloth to another bhikkhu on one condition or another and then either snatches it back or has someone else snatch it back. 3 Intention: One is impelled by anger or displeasure
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he gives it or takes (from him) in a friendly manner; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > This rule is for cases where one perceives the robe-cloth as being rightfully one’s own even after having given it away. But when not out of anger or annoyance, but ‘with the mind of a thief’ (theyya-citta) then is Pj 2. dukkaṭa for angrily snatching back from a bhikkhu requisites other than cloth; and for angrily snatching back any kind of requisite—cloth or otherwise—that one has given to someone who is not a bhikkhu
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Idaṁ me bhante cīvaraṁ bhikkhussa sāmaṁ datvā acchinnaṁ nissaggiyaṁ. Imāhaṁ āyasmato nissajjāmi, which means, “This robe (robe-cloth) of mine, venerable sir, snatched back after I myself gave it to a bhikkhu, is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to you.”
>
