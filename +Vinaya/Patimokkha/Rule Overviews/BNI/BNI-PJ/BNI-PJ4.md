# Nuns - Parajika - Expulsion

## BNI-PJ4 - Uttarimanussadhamma - False Claim to Superhuman Attainments

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Uttarimanussadhamma: Yā pana bhikkhunī anabhijānaṃ uttarimanussadhammaṃ attūpanāyikaṃ ullapeyya... ayampi pārājikā hoti asaṃvāsā. | 4. False Claim of Superior States: Should any bhikkhunī, without direct knowledge, claim a superior human state (such as Jhana or Enlightenment)... she is defeated and no longer in communion. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1. Object - a superior human state. 2. Perception - one perceives it as not present in oneself. 3. Effort - one addresses a human being, mentioning the state in connection with oneself. 4. Intention - with the intent to misrepresent the truth, motivated by an evil desire. 5. Result - the other person understands what is being said The commentaries add a fifth factor—result—saying that one’s listener must understand what one is saying but this factor appears to be based on a misreading of the Vibhaṅga
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If one makes the claim) because of overestimation; for one not intending to make a claim; for one who is insane; for one who is deranged; for one who is overwhelmed by pain; for the first offender
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > This is the ultimate great thief: he who claims an unfactual, non-existent superior human state. Statement explicitly mentions oneself, although the reference to the state may be either explicit or implicit full offense. A statement in which one mentions oneself—rather than the state—implicitly in connection with a superior human state is not grounds for a pārājika. motive is an essential part of the intention. evil desire is the wish to have others believe that skillful states not present within oneself are actually there. pārājika if the bhikkhu is aware of what he is saying, and a thullaccaya if not
>
