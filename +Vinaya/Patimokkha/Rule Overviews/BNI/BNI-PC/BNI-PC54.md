> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: staple or non-staple food that is not leftover. 2) Effort: One eats the food after having eaten and turned down an offer of further food
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If she has refused an offer to eat more, but not an invitation; if she drinks congee; if she eats more after getting permission from the person who invited her; if, when there is a reason, she uses post-midday tonics, seven-day tonics, or lifetime tonics; if she is insane; if she is the first offender
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > A donor could feel insulted if bhikkhus refused further offers of food, finished their meal, and then went to eat someplace else. It is also designed to protect bhikkhus from being forced to go hungry by stingy or impoverished donors
>
