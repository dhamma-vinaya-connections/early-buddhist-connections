# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 11 Bhūtagāma-Vaggo - 11 Plants
## BNI-PC116 - Sappāṇaka - Water Living Creatures Use

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Sappāṇaka: Yā pana bhikkhunī jānaṁ sappāṇakaṁ udakaṁ tiṇaṁ vā mattikaṁ vā siñceyya vā siñcāpeyya vā, pācittiyaṁ. | 116. Pouring Water: Should any bhikkhunī knowingly pour water containing living beings... on grass or on clay, it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: water contain living creatures 2) Perception: either knows on one’s own or has been told that the living creatures are there. 3) Effort: pouring the water or having it poured 4) Intention: aim of pouring the water or having it poured
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If it was unintentional, if he was not thinking, if he did not know; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Under this rule to prevent the pouring of lethal pollutants into water. no allowance for kappiya-vohāra (“wording it right”) under this rule
>
