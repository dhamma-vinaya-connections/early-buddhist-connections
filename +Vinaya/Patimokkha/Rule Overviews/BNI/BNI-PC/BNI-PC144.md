# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 14 Jotivaggo - 14 Fire
## BNI-PC144 - Ukkoṭana - Legal Issue Reopening

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Theyyasattha: Yā pana bhikkhunī jānaṃ theyyasatthena saddhiṁ... ekaddhānamaggaṁ paṭipajjeyya, pācittiyaṁ. | 144. Thieves: Should any bhikkhunī... travel together with a caravan of thieves... it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: an issue that has been dealt with properly. 2) Perception: One knows that it was dealt with properly, either because one was directly involved or one has been told of the matter. 3) Effort: One says—in the presence of another bhikkhu—that it was dealt with improperly
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he opens it up knowingly, thinking: ‘The (formal) act was carried out according to what is not the rule, or by an incomplete congregation, or against one who is not suitable for a (formal) act’; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > An issue (adhikaraṇa) is a matter that, must be dealt with formally in a prescribed manner. 1) dispute-issues (vivādādhikaraṇa) concerning Dhamma and Vinaya (see Sg 10), Community declare which side is right and which wrong; 2) accusation-issues (anuvādādhikaraṇa) concerning offenses (see Sg 8 & 9; Ay 1 & 2), Community judge them true or false; 3) offense-issues (āpattādhikaraṇa), the commission of offenses and their penalties (confession, penance, or expulsion from the Community); 4) duty-issues (kiccādhikaraṇa)—Community transactions, such ordination and holding the Pāṭimokkha recitation
>
