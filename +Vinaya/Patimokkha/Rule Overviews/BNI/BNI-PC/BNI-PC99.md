# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 10 Musāvāda-Vaggo - 10 Lies
## BNI-PC99 - Pesuñña - Divisive Talebearing

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Pesuñña: Bhikkhupesuññe pācittiyaṃ. | 99. Slandering: Should any bhikkhunī slander another... it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: Both Z's listener and X are bhikkhus; X has made remarks about Y that qualify as a direct insult under the preceding rule (or, if he didn't make them in Y's presence, remarks that would have qualified as a direct insult had he done so). 2) Effort: Z reports X's remarks to his listener verbally or by gesture (as in writing a letter), 3) Intent: with the intent of ingratiating himself with his listener, or of causing a rift between his listener and X
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he is not making dear, if he is not desiring dissension, if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Telling a bhikkhu about insulting remarks made by another bhikkhu—in hopes of winning favor or causing a rift
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > For other categories of offenses, change dukkaṭāyo to thullaccayāyo, nissaggiyāyo pācittiyāyo, pācittiyāyo, or dubbhāsitāyo,
>
