# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 16 Dhammikavaggo - 16 In-accordance-with-the-Rule
## BNI-PC166 - Sugatacīvara - Robes Size

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Tūlonaddha: Yā pana bhikkhunī mañcaṁ vā pīṭhaṁ vā tūlonaddhaṁ kārāpeyya, uddālanakaṁ pācittiyaṁ. | 166. Cotton Bedding: Should any bhikkhunī have a bed or bench made stuffed with cotton, it is to be torn open and confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: any robe larger than the standard measure. 2) Effort: One obtains it after making it or having it made 3) Intention: for one’s own use
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he makes it to the (proper) measure; if he makes it less than the (proper) measure; if having acquired what was made for another (but) exceeding the (proper) measure, having cut it down, he makes use of it; if he makes a canopy or a ground-covering or a screen-wall or a mattress or a squatting-mat; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Taking the sugata span to be 25 cm. would put the size of the Buddha’s robes at 2.25 m. by 1.50 m.—much larger than the lower robes used at present, but much smaller than present-day upper and outer robes
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > Here we take the sugata span to equal 25 cm.,
>
