# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 11 Bhūtagāma-Vaggo - 11 Plants
## BNI-PC110 - Mañcasanthārana - Leaving Furniture Outside

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Paṭhamasenāsana: Yā pana bhikkhunī saṅghikaṁ mañcaṁ vā pīṭhaṁ vā... ajjhokāse santharitvā vā... na paṭinissajjeyya... pācittiyaṁ. | 110. Furniture in Open: Should any bhikkhunī set a bed, bench, mattress, or stool belonging to the Community out in the open... and leave without putting it away... it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: any bed, bench, mattress, or stool belonging to the Community. 2) Effort: One sets such furnishings out in the open and then departs without taking leave, putting the furnishings away, or getting them put away in the proper place. 3) Intention: One has set them out for some purpose other than sunning them
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If, having removed it, he goes away; if, having caused it to be removed, he goes away; if, having asked (for permission), he goes away; if, drying himself in the sun, he goes away; if it comes to be taken possession of by something; if there are accidents; if it belongs to the individual himself, if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > In dry season may also be kept in an open pavilion roofed with slats or branches, or under tree without droppings. Purpose is to prevent negligence and to teach one to care for things. It should be taken as a general model.”
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > BU-PC12 Frustrating Venerable sirs, may the Community listen to me. This Bhikkhu (name), when questioned in the midst of the Community about an offense, frustrates the Community by remaining silent. If the Community is ready, it should make public Bhikkhu (name)’s act of causing frustration. This is the motion
>
