# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 15 Diṭṭhivaggo - 15 Views
## BNI-PC153 - Talasattika - Rising a Hand

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Talasattika: Yā pana bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā kupitā anattamanā talasattikaṁ uggireyya, pācittiyaṁ. | 153. Raising Palm: Should any bhikkhunī... raise the palm of her hand against (another) bhikkhunī, it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: another bhikkhu is grounds for the full offense here; anyone unordained, grounds for a dukkaṭa. 2) Effort: Raising the palm of one’s hand or raising any part of one’s body or anything attached to the body (a stick, a rock, a gun, a bow and arrow) in a threatening manner. 3) Intention: out of anger
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If being in some difficulty, he raises the palm of the hand desiring freedom; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Sub-commentary also notes that if an animal, for example, is making a mess and a bhikkhu raises his hand against it, this would be included under desiring freedom—i.e., from the mess—and so would not be an offense
> 
>
