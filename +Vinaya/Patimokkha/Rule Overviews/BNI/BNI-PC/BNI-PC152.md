# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 15 Diṭṭhivaggo - 15 Views
## BNI-PC152 - Pahāra - Hitting

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Pahāra: Yā pana bhikkhunī bhikkhuniyā kupitā anattamanā pahāraṁ dadeyya, pācittiyaṁ. | 152. Hitting: Should any bhikkhunī, angered and displeased, give a blow to (another) bhikkhunī, it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: another bhikkhu is grounds for the full offense here; anyone unordained, grounds for a dukkaṭa. 2) Effort: One gives him a blow: with one’s own body, with something attached to the body or something that can be “thrown” 3) Intention: out of anger
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If, being in some difficulty, he gives a blow desiring freedom; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > “Result” is not a factor here. Whether the other person is hurt—or how badly he/she is hurt—does not affect the offense. In self-defense even if anger or displeasure arises in one’s mind in cases like this, there is no penalty
>
