# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 16 Dhammikavaggo - 16 In-accordance-with-the-Rule
## BNI-PC160 - Pariṇāmana - Diverting Gains

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Pariṇāmana: Yā pana bhikkhunī jānaṃ saṅghikaṁ lābhaṃ pariṇataṁ puggalassa pariṇāmeyya, pācittiyaṁ. | 160. Diverting: Should any bhikkhunī knowingly divert a gain dedicated to the Community to an individual... it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1 Object: Any requisite that donors have indicated by word or gesture that they intend to give to a Community. 2 Perception: One perceives that the donors have allocated the requisite for a Community. 3 Effort: One tries to persuade them that they should give it to another individual instead. 4 Result: he obtains the article from the donors
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he himself being asked, ‘Where do we give?’ says, ‘Give wherever your gift could be used or could be mended or should be for a long time or when for you the mind is peaceful;’ if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Divert items allocated for a Community to another individual full offense. To divert items allocated for one Community of bhikkhus to another Community or to a shrine (cetiya) entails a dukkaṭa. Or for a shrine to a Community, to an individual, or to another shrine; and for diverting items allocated for an individual to a Community, to a shrine, or to another individual
>
