# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 11 Bhūtagāma-Vaggo - 11 Plants
## BNI-PC111 - Seyyasanthārana - Leaving Bedding Outside

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Dutiyasenāsana: Yā pana bhikkhunī saṅghike vihāre seyyaṁ santharitvā vā... na paṭinissajjeyya... pācittiyaṁ. | 111. Bedding in Dwelling: Should any bhikkhunī set out bedding in a dwelling belonging to the Community... and leave without putting it away... it is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: Bedding here includes mattresses, pillows, rugs, sheets, mats, sitting cloths, blankets, bedspreads, animal skins, throw rugs, etc., 2) Effort: One sets such bedding out in the open and then departs without taking leave, putting them away, or getting them put away in the proper place. 3) Intention: One has set them out and plans to return
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If, having removed it, he goes away; if, having caused it to be removed, he goes away; if, having asked (for permission), he goes away; if it comes to be taken possession of by something; if going with the expectation, standing there, he asks (for permission); if he becomes taken possession of by something; if there are accidents; if belong to individual himself, if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > The bedding is the responsibility of the bhikkhu with the claim on the dwelling, and not of the visitor. putting it away is back in the safe place where it was kept before being spread out
> 
>
