# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 16 Dhammikavaggo - 16 In-accordance-with-the-Rule
## BNI-PC164 - Tūlonaddha - Upholstery

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Sūcighara: Yā pana bhikkhunī aṭṭhimayaṁ vā... sūcigharaṁ kārāpeyya, bhedanakaṁ pācittiyaṁ. | 164. Needle Box: Should any bhikkhunī have a needle box made of bone, ivory, or horn, it is to be broken and confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: a bed or bench stuffed with cotton down. 2) Effort: One obtains it after making it or having it made 3) Intention: for one’s own use
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If it is for a binding, for a girdle, for a shoulder-strap, for a bag for carrying the bowl in, for a water-strainer; if he is making a squatting-mat; if, having acquired what was made for another, having torn it off, he makes use of it; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > The purpose of all this is to keep bhikkhus from using furnishings that are extravagant and ostentatious, according to present day standard. one must tear off the upholstery before confessing the offense
> 
>
