# Nuns - Pacittiya - Confession
## 11 Bhūtagāma-Vaggo - 11 Plants
## BNI-PC109 - Ujjhāpanaka - Complaining

### 🌐Overview

| Rule in Pali  | Rule in English  |
| :-----------: | :--------------: |
| Ujjhāpanaka: Ujjhāpanake khiyyanake pācittiyaṁ. | 109. Criticizing Officials: Criticizing or complaining (about a Community official) is to be confessed. |


> [!Info] Analisis of the rule
> 
> >[!info]- Offence Factors 
> > 
> > > From BMC1
> > 
> > 1) Object: (1) a bhikkhu who (2) has been properly authorized as a Community official and (3) does not habitually act out of the four causes for bias: desire, aversion, delusion, or fear. 2) Effort: criticizing or complaining about a Community official is a pācittiya if one’s listener is a fellow bhikkhu, and a dukkaṭa if listener is an unordained person 3) Intention: motive is to make him lose face, lose status, or feel abashed
> 
> > [!info]- No Offence 
> > > From Vibhanga
> > 
> > If he makes (someone) look down upon or if he criticises one acting by nature from desire, from hatred, from stupidity, from fear; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer
> 
> > [!info]- Useful Notes
> > > From various authors
> > 
> > Wrong doing for criticism of unordained person, a Community official without having been authorized or having been improperly authorized,
> 
> > [!info]-  Formulas 
> > >If applicable from BMC1 Appendices  [[bmc1#^ch13]]
> > 
> > BU-PC12 Frustrating Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho. Ayaṁ Itthannāmo bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjiyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṁ viheseti. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṁ, saṅgho Itthannāmassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṁ ropeyya. Esā ñatti
>
