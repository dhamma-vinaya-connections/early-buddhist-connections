## Vinaya Piṭaka - Monastic Rules

## Bhikkhunī Vibhaṅga - Sekhiya 40:  i.e. The bhikkhus' Sekhiya 40 To prepare mouthfuls of suitable proportions.
### B. Brahmali

- Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.   

#### Origin

1At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the bhikkhus from the group of six were making elongated mouthfuls. …

#### Final ruling

2“‘I will make rounded mouthfuls,’ this is how you should train.”

3One should make rounded mouthfuls. If a bhikkhu, out of disrespect, makes an elongated mouthful, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

#### Non-offenses

4There is no offense: if it is unintentional; if he is not mindful; if he does not know; if he is sick; if it is a fresh food; if it is any kind of fruit; if it is a non-bean curry; if there is an emergency; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.


5The tenth training rule is finished.  
The fourth sub-chapter on respectfully is finished.
