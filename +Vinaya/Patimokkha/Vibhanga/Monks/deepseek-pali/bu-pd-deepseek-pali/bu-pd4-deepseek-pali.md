# BU-PD 4

## 4. Catutthapāṭidesanīyasikkhāpadaṃ

## 4.The Fourth Training Rule Requiring Acknowledgment.

570. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sākiyadāsakā avaruddhā honti. Sākiyāniyo icchanti āraññakesu senāsanesu bhattaṃ kātuṃ. Assosuṃ kho sākiyadāsakā – ‘‘sākiyāniyo kira āraññakesu senāsanesu bhattaṃ kattukāmā’’ti. Te magge pariyuṭṭhiṃsu. Sākiyāniyo paṇītaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ ādāya āraññakaṃ senāsanaṃ agamaṃsu. Sākiyadāsakā nikkhamitvā sākiyāniyo acchindiṃsu ca dūsesuñca. Sākiyā nikkhamitvā te core sabhaṇḍe [saha bhaṇḍena (ka.)] gahetvā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhadantā ārāme core paṭivasante nārocessantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū sākiyānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññapessāmi dasa atthavase paṭicca – saṅghasuṭṭhutāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

570.At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu, in the Banyan Tree Park. Now at that time the Sakyan servants were hostile. The Sakyan women wanted to prepare meals in forest dwellings. The Sakyan servants heard, “The Sakyan women, it seems, want to prepare meals in forest dwellings.” They blocked the road. The Sakyan women, taking choice hard food and soft food, went to the forest dwelling. The Sakyan servants, coming out, robbed and violated the Sakyan women. The Sakyans, coming out, seized those thieves along with their goods and complained, criticized, and protested: “How can these venerable ones not report when thieves are dwelling in the monastery!” The bhikkhus heard the Sakyans complaining, criticizing, and protesting. Then those bhikkhus reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in connection with this incident, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “Therefore, bhikkhus, I will formulate a training rule for the bhikkhus, based on ten considerations: for the welfare of the Sangha… and so forth. And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

‘‘Yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanāni sāsaṅkasammatāni sappaṭibhayāni, yo pana bhikkhu tathārūpesu senāsanesu [senāsanesu viharanto (sī. syā.)] pubbe appaṭisaṃviditaṃ khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā ajjhārāme sahatthā paṭiggahetvā khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā, paṭidesetabbaṃ tena bhikkhunā – ‘gārayhaṃ, āvuso, dhammaṃ āpajjiṃ asappāyaṃ pāṭidesanīyaṃ taṃ paṭidesemī’’’ti.

“Whatever remote dwellings are considered perilous and dangerous, if a bhikkhu, without having been announced beforehand, receives with his own hand hard food or soft food inside such a dwelling and eats or consumes it, that bhikkhu should acknowledge it: ‘Friends, I have committed a blameworthy, unsuitable act that requires acknowledgment. I acknowledge it.’”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.

571. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu āraññakesu senāsanesu gilāno hoti. Manussā khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā ādāya āraññakaṃ senāsanaṃ agamaṃsu. Atha kho te manussā taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘bhuñjatha, bhante’’ti. Atha kho so bhikkhu – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ āraññakesu senāsanesu khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā sahatthā paṭiggahetvā khādituṃ bhuñjitu’’nti kukkuccāyanto na paṭiggahesi, nāsakkhi piṇḍāya carituṃ [pavisituṃ (ka.)], chinnabhatto ahosi. Atha kho so bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānena bhikkhunā āraññakesu senāsanesu pubbe appaṭisaṃviditaṃ khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā sahatthā paṭiggahetvā khādituṃ bhuñjituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

571.At that time, a certain bhikkhu was ill in a forest dwelling. People brought hard food or soft food and went to the forest dwelling. Then those people said to that bhikkhu: “Venerable sir, please eat.” Then that bhikkhu, feeling scruples and thinking, “The Blessed One has prohibited eating hard food or soft food received with one’s own hand in forest dwellings,” did not accept it. He was unable to go for alms, and so he went without food. Then that bhikkhu informed the bhikkhus about this matter. The bhikkhus informed the Blessed One about this matter. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in connection with this incident, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “I allow, bhikkhus, a sick bhikkhu in a forest dwelling to receive with his own hand and eat or consume hard food or soft food that has not been announced beforehand. And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

572. ‘‘Yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanāni sāsaṅkasammatāni sappaṭibhayāni, yo pana bhikkhu tathārūpesu senāsanesu  pubbe appaṭisaṃviditaṃ khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā ajjhārāme sahatthā paṭiggahetvā agilāno khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā, paṭidesetabbaṃ tena bhikkhunā – ‘gārayhaṃ, āvuso, dhammaṃ āpajjiṃ asappāyaṃ pāṭidesanīyaṃ, taṃ paṭidesemī’’’ti.

572.“Whatever remote dwellings are considered perilous and dangerous, if a bhikkhu, not being ill, without having been announced beforehand, receives with his own hand hard food or soft food inside such a dwelling and eats or consumes it, that bhikkhu should acknowledge it: ‘Friends, I have committed a blameworthy, unsuitable act that requires acknowledgment. I acknowledge it.’”

573. Yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanānīti āraññakaṃ nāma senāsanaṃ pañcadhanusatikaṃ pacchimaṃ.

573.What are called ‘forest dwellings’? A forest dwelling is defined as one that is at least five hundred bow-lengths from the boundary.

Sāsaṅkaṃ nāma ārāme ārāmūpacāre corānaṃ niviṭṭhokāso dissati, bhuttokāso dissati, ṭhitokāso dissati, nisinnokāso dissati, nipannokāso dissati.

What is called ‘perilous’? In the monastery or its vicinity, a place where thieves have settled is seen, a place where they have eaten is seen, a place where they have stood is seen, a place where they have sat is seen, a place where they have lain down is seen.

Sappaṭibhayaṃ nāma ārāme ārāmūpacāre corehi manussā hatā dissanti, viluttā dissanti, ākoṭitā dissanti.

What is called ‘dangerous’? In the monastery or its vicinity, people killed by thieves are seen, people plundered are seen, people beaten are seen.

Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

‘Whatever’ means whatever kind of… ‘bhikkhu’… the bhikkhu intended in this case.

Tathārūpesu senāsanesūti evarūpesu senāsanesu.

‘In such dwellings’ means in dwellings of that kind.

Appaṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma pañcannaṃ paṭisaṃviditaṃ, etaṃ appaṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma. Ārāmaṃ ārāmūpacāraṃ ṭhapetvā paṭisaṃviditaṃ, etaṃ [etampi (sī.)] appaṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma.

What is called ‘not announced beforehand’? Even if it has been announced to five fellow Dhamma-farers, it is still called ‘not announced beforehand’. Even if it has been announced outside the monastery or its vicinity, it is still called ‘not announced beforehand’.

Paṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma yo koci itthī vā puriso vā ārāmaṃ ārāmūpacāraṃ āgantvā āroceti – ‘‘itthannāmassa, bhante, khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā āharissantī’’ti. Sace sāsaṅkaṃ hoti, sāsaṅkanti ācikkhitabbaṃ; sace sappaṭibhayaṃ hoti, sappaṭibhayanti ācikkhitabbaṃ; sace – ‘‘hotu, bhante, āhariyissatī’’ti bhaṇati, corā vattabbā – ‘‘manussā idhūpacaranti apasakkathā’’ti. Yāguyā paṭisaṃvidite tassā parivāro āhariyyati, etaṃ paṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma. Bhattena paṭisaṃvidite tassa parivāro āhariyyati, etaṃ paṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma. Khādanīyena paṭisaṃvidite tassa parivāro āhariyyati, etaṃ paṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma. Kulena paṭisaṃvidite yo tasmiṃ kule manusso khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā āharati, etaṃ paṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma. Gāmena paṭisaṃvidite yo tasmiṃ gāme manusso khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā āharati, etaṃ paṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma. Pūgena paṭisaṃvidite yo tasmiṃ pūge manusso khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā āharati, etaṃ paṭisaṃviditaṃ nāma.

What is called ‘announced beforehand’? It is when any woman or man who comes to the monastery or its vicinity announces: ‘Venerable sir, so-and-so will bring hard food or soft food.’ If it is perilous, it should be stated as perilous; if it is dangerous, it should be stated as dangerous; if they say, ‘Very well, venerable sir, it will be brought,’ the thieves should be told: ‘People frequent this place, get away!’ When porridge has been announced beforehand, whatever is brought as its accompaniment is considered announced beforehand. When a meal has been announced beforehand, whatever is brought as its accompaniment is considered announced beforehand. When hard food has been announced beforehand, whatever is brought as its accompaniment is considered announced beforehand. When a family has been announced beforehand, whatever hard food or soft food a person from that family brings is considered announced beforehand. When a village has been announced beforehand, whatever hard food or soft food a person from that village brings is considered announced beforehand. When a guild has been announced beforehand, whatever hard food or soft food a person from that guild brings is considered announced beforehand.

Khādanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ ṭhapetvā avasesaṃ khādanīyaṃ nāma.

What is called ‘hard food’? It is the remainder, excluding the five kinds of soft food, and excluding food allowable for a watch-period, for seven days, or for a lifetime.

Bhojanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṃsaṃ.

What is called ‘soft food’? It is the five kinds of soft food: cooked rice, gruel, flour, fish, and meat.

Ajjhārāmo nāma parikkhittassa ārāmassa antoārāmo. Aparikkhittassa upacāro.

What is called ‘inside the monastery’? For an enclosed monastery, it is the inner area of the monastery. For an unenclosed monastery, it is the precinct.

Agilāno nāma sakkoti piṇḍāya carituṃ [gantuṃ (ka.)].

What is called ‘not being ill’? One is able to go for alms.

Gilāno nāma na sakkoti piṇḍāya carituṃ [gantuṃ (ka.)].

What is called ‘being ill’? One is unable to go for alms.

Appaṭisaṃviditaṃ agilāno ‘‘khādissāmi bhuñjissāmī’’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa.

If, not being ill, one receives what has not been announced beforehand, thinking, ‘I will eat, I will consume,’ there is an offense of wrong-doing. For each mouthful, there is an offense requiring acknowledgment.

574. Appaṭisaṃvidite appaṭisaṃviditasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā ajjhārāme sahatthā paṭiggahetvā agilāno khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. Appaṭisaṃvidite vematiko khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā ajjhārāme sahatthā paṭiggahetvā agilāno khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa. Appaṭisaṃvidite paṭisaṃviditasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā ajjhārāme sahatthā paṭiggahetvā agilāno khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pāṭidesanīyassa.

574.If, regarding what has not been announced beforehand, one perceives it as not announced beforehand, and not being ill, receives with his own hand hard food or soft food inside the monastery and eats or consumes it, there is an offense requiring acknowledgment. If, regarding what has not been announced beforehand, one is uncertain, and not being ill, receives with his own hand hard food or soft food inside the monastery and eats or consumes it, there is an offense requiring acknowledgment. If, regarding what has not been announced beforehand, one perceives it as announced beforehand, and not being ill, receives with his own hand hard food or soft food inside the monastery and eats or consumes it, there is an offense requiring acknowledgment.

Yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ āhāratthāya paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paṭisaṃvidite appaṭisaṃviditasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paṭisaṃvidite vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paṭisaṃvidite paṭisaṃviditasaññī, anāpatti.

If one receives food allowable for a watch-period, for seven days, or for a lifetime for the purpose of eating, there is an offense of wrong-doing. For each mouthful, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, regarding what has been announced beforehand, one perceives it as not announced beforehand, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, regarding what has been announced beforehand, one is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, regarding what has been announced beforehand, one perceives it as announced beforehand, there is no offense.

575. Anāpatti paṭisaṃvidite, gilānassa, paṭisaṃvidite vā gilānassa vā sesakaṃ bhuñjati, bahārāme paṭiggahetvā antoārāme bhuñjati, tattha jātakaṃ mūlaṃ vā tacaṃ vā pattaṃ vā pupphaṃ vā phalaṃ vā bhuñjati, yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ sati paccaye paribhuñjati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

575.There is no offense for one for whom it has been designated; for one who is sick; for one who eats what is left over from that which was designated or for a sick person; for one who receives food outside the monastery and eats it inside the monastery; for one who eats roots, bark, leaves, flowers, or fruits that have grown there; for one who uses what is allowable for a day, for seven days, or for a lifetime when there is a condition; for one who is insane; or for one who is the first to offend.

Catutthapāṭidesanīyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The fourth training rule requiring acknowledgment is complete.

Uddiṭṭhā kho, āyasmanto, cattāro pāṭidesanīyā dhammā. Tatthāyasmante pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Dutiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Tatiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Parisuddhetthāyasmanto, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmīti.

Venerable sirs, the four rules requiring acknowledgment have been recited. In regard to these, I ask the venerable sirs: “Are you pure in this matter?” A second time I ask the venerable sirs: “Are you pure in this matter?” A third time I ask the venerable sirs: “Are you pure in this matter?” The venerable sirs are pure in this matter, therefore they are silent. Thus I hold it.

Pāṭidesanīyakaṇḍaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The section on rules requiring acknowledgment is finished.
