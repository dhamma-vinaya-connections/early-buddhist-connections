# BU-SN 9

## 9. Dutiyaduṭṭhadosasikkhāpadaṃ

## 9.The second training rule concerning malicious accusation.

391. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū gijjhakūṭā pabbatā orohantā addasaṃsu chagalakaṃ [chakalakaṃ (syā.)] ajikāya vippaṭipajjantaṃ. Disvāna evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘handa mayaṃ, āvuso, imaṃ chagalakaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ nāma karoma. Imaṃ ajikaṃ mettiyaṃ nāma bhikkhuniṃ karoma. Evaṃ mayaṃ voharissāma. Pubbe mayaṃ, āvuso, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ sutena avocumhā. Idāni pana amhehi sāmaṃ diṭṭho mettiyāya bhikkhuniyā vippaṭipajjanto’’ti. Te taṃ chagalakaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ nāma akaṃsu. Taṃ ajikaṃ mettiyaṃ nāma bhikkhuniṃ akaṃsu. Te bhikkhūnaṃ ārocesuṃ – ‘‘pubbe mayaṃ, āvuso, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ sutena avocumhā. Idāni pana amhehi sāmaṃ diṭṭho mettiyāya bhikkhuniyā vippaṭipajjanto’’ti. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘māvuso, evaṃ avacuttha. Nāyasmā dabbo mallaputto evaṃ karissatī’’ti.

391.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels’ Feeding Place. At that time, the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka, descending from Vulture Peak Mountain, saw a young goat copulating with a female goat. Seeing this, they said, “Come, friends, let us name this young goat Dabba Mallaputta. Let us name this female goat the nun Mettiya. Thus we will speak. Previously, friends, we spoke about Dabba Mallaputta based on hearsay. But now we have seen him ourselves copulating with the nun Mettiya.” They named that young goat Dabba Mallaputta. They named that female goat the nun Mettiya. They announced to the bhikkhus, “Previously, friends, we spoke about Dabba Mallaputta based on hearsay. But now we have seen him ourselves copulating with the nun Mettiya.” The bhikkhus said, “Friends, do not speak thus. The Venerable Dabba Mallaputta would not do such a thing.”

Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ paṭipucchi – ‘‘sarasi tvaṃ, dabba, evarūpaṃ kattā yathayime bhikkhū āhaṃsū’’ti? ‘‘Yathā maṃ, bhante, bhagavā jānātī’’ti. Dutiyampi kho bhagavā…pe… tatiyampi kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sarasi tvaṃ, dabba, evarūpaṃ kattā yathayime bhikkhū āhaṃsū’’ti? ‘‘Yathā maṃ, bhante, bhagavā jānātī’’ti. ‘‘Na kho, dabba, dabbā evaṃ nibbeṭhenti. Sace tayā kataṃ katanti vadehi, sace tayā akataṃ akatanti vadehī’’ti. ‘‘Yato ahaṃ, bhante, jāto nābhijānāmi supinantenapi methunadhammaṃ paṭisevitā, pageva jāgaro’’ti! Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhuve, ime bhikkhū anuyuñjathā’’ti. Idaṃ vatvā bhagavā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ pāvisi.

Then those bhikkhus informed the Blessed One about this matter. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and for this reason, having convened the Sangha of bhikkhus, questioned the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta: “Dabba, do you remember committing such an act as these bhikkhus allege?” “As the Blessed One knows me, venerable sir.” A second time the Blessed One… a third time the Blessed One said to the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta: “Dabba, do you remember committing such an act as these bhikkhus allege?” “As the Blessed One knows me, venerable sir.” “Dabba, people named Dabba do not prevaricate like this. If you did it, say ‘I did it’; if you didn’t do it, say ‘I didn’t do it.’” “Since I was born, venerable sir, I do not recall engaging in sexual activity even in a dream, much less while awake!” Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Well then, bhikkhus, interrogate those bhikkhus.” Having said this, the Blessed One rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.

Atha kho te bhikkhū mettiyabhūmajake bhikkhū anuyuñjiṃsu. Te bhikkhūhi anuyuñjīyamānā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ – ‘‘kiṃ pana tumhe, āvuso, āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ aññabhāgiyassa adhikaraṇassa kiñcidesaṃ lesamattaṃ upādāya pārājikena dhammena anuddhaṃsethā’’ti? ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ aññabhāgiyassa adhikaraṇassa kiñci desaṃ lesamattaṃ upādāya pārājikena dhammena anuddhaṃsessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū mettiyabhūmajake bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ aññabhāgiyassa adhikaraṇassa kiñci desaṃ lesamattaṃ upādāya pārājikena dhammena anuddhaṃsethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… ‘‘kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ aññabhāgiyassa adhikaraṇassa kiñci desaṃ lesamattaṃ upādāya pārājikena dhammena anuddhaṃsessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then those bhikkhus questioned the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka. Being questioned by the bhikkhus, they informed the bhikkhus of the matter: “But friends, did you accuse the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta of a pārājika offense, seizing upon some trivial aspect, a mere trifle, of a different legal issue?” “Yes, friends.” Those bhikkhus who were modest… criticized and complained: “How could the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka accuse the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta of a pārājika offense, seizing upon some trivial aspect, a mere trifle, of a different legal issue?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka in many ways, informed the Blessed One of the matter… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you accused Dabba Mallaputta of a pārājika offense, seizing upon some trivial aspect, a mere trifle, of a different legal issue?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “Foolish men, how could you accuse Dabba Mallaputta of a pārājika offense, seizing upon some trivial aspect, a mere trifle, of a different legal issue? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

392. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuṃ duṭṭho doso appatīto aññabhāgiyassa adhikaraṇassa kiñcidesaṃ lesamattaṃ upādāya pārājikena dhammena anuddhaṃseyya – ‘appeva nāma naṃ imamhā brahmacariyā cāveyya’nti. Tato  aparena samayena samanuggāhīyamāno vā asamanuggāhīyamāno vā aññabhāgiyañceva taṃ adhikaraṇaṃ hoti kocideso lesamatto upādinno, bhikkhu ca dosaṃ patiṭṭhāti, saṅghādiseso’’ti.

392.“If a bhikkhu, being angry and displeased, should accuse another bhikkhu of a pārājika offense, seizing upon some trivial aspect, a mere suggestion, of a different legal issue, thinking, ‘Perhaps I can make him fall away from this holy life,’ then, whether he is later examined or not, if that legal issue is indeed a different one, and some trivial aspect, a mere suggestion, was seized upon, and the bhikkhu persists in his anger, it is a Saṅghādisesa offense.”

393. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

393.“Yo pana” means whoever… “Bhikkhu” means… Here, this bhikkhu is the one intended in this context.

Bhikkhunti aññaṃ bhikkhuṃ.

“Bhikkhuṃ” means another bhikkhu.

Duṭṭho dosoti kupito anattamano anabhiraddho āhatacitto khilajāto.

“Duṭṭho doso” means angry, displeased, dissatisfied, unhappy, with an afflicted mind, and obstructed.

Appatītoti tena ca kopena, tena ca dosena, tāya ca anattamanatāya, tāya ca anabhiraddhiyā appatīto hoti.

“Appatīto” means displeased due to that anger, due to that malice, due to that mental displeasure, and due to that lack of delight.

Aññabhāgiyassa adhikaraṇassāti āpattaññabhāgiyaṃ vā hoti adhikaraṇaññabhāgiyaṃ vā. Kathaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ adhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ? Vivādādhikaraṇaṃ anuvādādhikaraṇassa āpattādhikaraṇassa kiccādhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ. Anuvādādhikaraṇaṃ āpattādhikaraṇassa kiccādhikaraṇassa vivādādhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ. Āpattādhikaraṇaṃ kiccādhikaraṇassa vivādādhikaraṇassa anuvādādhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ. Kiccādhikaraṇaṃ vivādādhikaraṇassa anuvādādhikaraṇassa āpattādhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ. Evaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ adhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ.

“Aññabhāgiyassa adhikaraṇassa” means it may be an offense of a different category or a legal issue of a different category. How is a legal issue of a different category from another legal issue? A dispute legal issue is of a different category from an accusation legal issue, an offense legal issue, or a procedural legal issue. An accusation legal issue is of a different category from an offense legal issue, a procedural legal issue, or a dispute legal issue. An offense legal issue is of a different category from a procedural legal issue, a dispute legal issue, or an accusation legal issue. A procedural legal issue is of a different category from a dispute legal issue, an accusation legal issue, or an offense legal issue. Thus, a legal issue is of a different category from another legal issue.

Kathaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ adhikaraṇassa tabbhāgiyaṃ? Vivādādhikaraṇaṃ vivādādhikaraṇassa tabbhāgiyaṃ. Anuvādādhikaraṇaṃ anuvādādhikaraṇassa tabbhāgiyaṃ. Āpattādhikaraṇaṃ āpattādhikaraṇassa siyā tabbhāgiyaṃ siyā aññabhāgiyaṃ.

How is a legal issue of the same category as another legal issue? A dispute legal issue is of the same category as a dispute legal issue. An accusation legal issue is of the same category as an accusation legal issue. An offense legal issue may be of the same category or of a different category as an offense legal issue.

Kathaṃ āpattādhikaraṇaṃ āpattādhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ? Methunadhammapārājikāpatti adinnādānapārājikāpattiyā manussaviggahapārājikāpattiyā uttarimanussadhammapārājikāpattiyā aññabhāgiyā. Adinnādānapārājikāpatti manussaviggahapārājikāpattiyā uttarimanussadhammapārājikāpattiyā methunadhammapārājikāpattiyā aññabhāgiyā. Manussaviggahapārājikāpatti uttarimanussadhammapārājikāpattiyā methunadhammapārājikāpattiyā adinnādānapārājikāpattiyā aññabhāgiyā. Uttarimanussadhammapārājikāpatti methunadhammapārājikāpattiyā adinnādānapārājikāpattiyā manussaviggahapārājikāpattiyā aññabhāgiyā. Evaṃ āpattādhikaraṇaṃ āpattādhikaraṇassa aññabhāgiyaṃ.

How is an offense issue distinct from another offense issue? The offense entailing defeat concerning sexual intercourse is distinct from the offense entailing defeat concerning taking what is not given, from the offense entailing defeat concerning depriving a human being of life, and from the offense entailing defeat concerning claiming a superior human state. The offense entailing defeat concerning taking what is not given is distinct from the offense entailing defeat concerning depriving a human being of life, from the offense entailing defeat concerning claiming a superior human state, and from the offense entailing defeat concerning sexual intercourse. The offense entailing defeat concerning depriving a human being of life is distinct from the offense entailing defeat concerning claiming a superior human state, from the offense entailing defeat concerning sexual intercourse, and from the offense entailing defeat concerning taking what is not given. The offense entailing defeat concerning claiming a superior human state is distinct from the offense entailing defeat concerning sexual intercourse, from the offense entailing defeat concerning taking what is not given, and from the offense entailing defeat concerning depriving a human being of life. Thus, an offense issue is distinct from another offense issue.

Kathaṃ āpattādhikaraṇaṃ āpattādhikaraṇassa tabbhāgiyaṃ? Methunadhammapārājikāpatti methunadhammapārājikāpattiyā tabbhāgiyā. Adinnādānapārājikāpatti adinnādānapārājikāpattiyā tabbhāgiyā. Manussaviggahapārājikāpatti manussaviggahapārājikāpattiyā tabbhāgiyā. Uttarimanussadhammapārājikāpatti uttarimanussadhammapārājikāpattiyā tabbhāgiyā. Evaṃ āpattādhikaraṇaṃ āpattādhikaraṇassa tabbhāgiyaṃ.

How is an offense issue of the same class as another offense issue? The offense entailing defeat concerning sexual intercourse is of the same class as another offense entailing defeat concerning sexual intercourse. The offense entailing defeat concerning taking what is not given is of the same class as another offense entailing defeat concerning taking what is not given. The offense entailing defeat concerning depriving a human being of life is of the same class as another offense entailing defeat concerning depriving a human being of life. The offense entailing defeat concerning claiming a superior human state is of the same class as another offense entailing defeat concerning claiming a superior human state. Thus, an offense issue is of the same class as another offense issue.

Kiccādhikaraṇaṃ kiccādhikaraṇassa tabbhāgiyaṃ. Evaṃ adhikaraṇaṃ adhikaraṇassa tabbhāgiyaṃ.

A procedural issue is of the same class as another procedural issue. Thus, an issue is of the same class as another issue.

394. Kiñci desaṃ lesamattaṃ upādāyāti leso nāma dasa lesā – jātileso, nāmaleso, gottaleso, liṅgaleso, āpattileso, pattaleso, cīvaraleso, upajjhāyaleso, ācariyaleso, senāsanaleso.

394.“Taking up some aspect, even a mere trace”: The trace (lesa) is of ten kinds: trace of birth, trace of name, trace of clan, trace of physical characteristic, trace of an offense, trace of a bowl, trace of a robe, trace of a preceptor, trace of a teacher, trace of a dwelling.

395. Jātileso nāma khattiyo diṭṭho hoti pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpajjanto. Aññaṃ khattiyaṃ passitvā codeti – ‘‘khattiyo mayā diṭṭho [ettha ‘‘pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpajjanto’’ti pāṭho ūno maññe, aṭṭhakathāyaṃ hi ‘‘khattiyo mayā diṭṭho pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpajjanto’’ti dissati]. Pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpannosi, assamaṇosi, asakyaputtiyosi, natthi tayā saddhiṃ uposatho vā pavāraṇā vā saṅghakammaṃ vā’’ti, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

395.“Trace of birth”: A noble is seen falling into an offense entailing defeat. Seeing another noble, one accuses him: “I have seen a noble falling into an offense entailing defeat. You have fallen into an offense entailing defeat; you are no ascetic, no Sakyan son; there is no Uposatha, Pavāraṇā, or Sangha-kamma with you.” For each utterance, there is an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Sangha.

Brāhmaṇo diṭṭho hoti…pe… vesso diṭṭho hoti…pe… suddo diṭṭho hoti pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpajjanto. Aññaṃ suddaṃ passitvā codeti – ‘‘suddo mayā diṭṭho. Pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpannosi, assamaṇosi, asakyaputtiyosi’’…pe… āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

You have fallen into an offense entailing defeat; you are no ascetic, no Sakyan son.”… For each utterance, there is an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Sangha.

404. Senāsanaleso nāma itthannāmasenāsanavāsiko diṭṭho hoti pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpajjanto. Aññaṃ itthannāmasenāsanavāsikaṃ passitvā codeti – ‘‘itthannāmasenāsanavāsiko mayā diṭṭho. Pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpannosi, assamaṇosi, asakyaputtiyosi, natthi tayā saddhiṃ uposatho vā pavāraṇā vā saṅghakammaṃ vā’’ti, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

404.“Trace of a dwelling”: A dweller of so-and-so’s dwelling is seen falling into an offense entailing defeat. Seeing another dweller of so-and-so’s dwelling, he accuses him: “A dweller of so-and-so’s dwelling was seen by me. You have fallen into an offense entailing defeat; you are no ascetic, no Sakyan son; there is no Uposatha, Pavāraṇā, or Sangha-kamma with you.” For each utterance, there is an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Sangha.

405. Pārājikena dhammenāti catunnaṃ aññatarena.

405.“By an offense entailing defeat” means by one of the four [offenses entailing defeat].

Anuddhaṃseyyāti codeti vā codāpeti vā.

“He should harass” means he accuses or causes to accuse.

Appeva nāma naṃ imamhā brahmacariyācāveyyanti bhikkhubhāvā cāveyyaṃ, samaṇadhammā cāveyyaṃ, sīlakkhandhā cāveyyaṃ, tapoguṇā cāveyyaṃ.

“Perhaps I might cause him to fall away from this spiritual life” means I might cause him to fall away from bhikkhuhood, from the qualities of a contemplative, from the aggregate of virtue, or from ascetic practices.

Tato aparena samayenāti yasmiṃ khaṇe anuddhaṃsito hoti, taṃ khaṇaṃ taṃ layaṃ taṃ muhuttaṃ vītivatte.

“After some time” means after that moment, that instant, that occasion when he was harassed has passed.

Samanuggāhīyamānoti yena vatthunā anuddhaṃsito hoti tasmiṃ vatthusmiṃ samanuggāhīyamāno.

“Being pressed for details” means being pressed for details about the matter by which he was harassed.

Asamanuggāhīyamānoti na kenaci vuccamāno.

“Not being pressed for details” means not being spoken to by anyone.

Adhikaraṇaṃ nāma cattāri adhikaraṇāni – vivādādhikaraṇaṃ, anuvādādhikaraṇaṃ, āpattādhikaraṇaṃ, kiccādhikaraṇaṃ.

An issue is of four kinds: dispute issue, accusation issue, offense issue, and procedural issue.

Koci deso lesamatto upādinnoti tesaṃ dasannaṃ lesānaṃ aññataro leso upādinno hoti.

“Some aspect, a mere trace, is taken up” means one of those ten traces is taken up.

Bhikkhu ca dosaṃ patiṭṭhātīti tucchakaṃ mayā bhaṇitaṃ, musā mayā bhaṇitaṃ, abhūtaṃ mayā bhaṇitaṃ, ajānantena mayā bhaṇitaṃ.

‘A bhikkhu is established in aversion’—that was spoken by me vainly, spoken falsely by me, spoken untruthfully by me, spoken by me without knowing.

Saṅghādisesoti …pe… tenapi vuccati saṅghādisesoti.

‘Entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha’—[...]—for that reason it is called an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha.

406. Bhikkhu saṅghādisesaṃ ajjhāpajjanto diṭṭho hoti, saṅghādisese saṅghādisesadiṭṭhi hoti. Tañce pārājikena codeti – ‘‘assamaṇosi, asakyaputtiyosi, natthi tayā saddhiṃ uposatho vā pavāraṇā vā saṅghakammaṃ vā’’ti, evampi āpattaññabhāgiyaṃ hoti leso ca upādinno, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

406.A bhikkhu is seen committing an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha, and regarding that offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha, one has the perception of it as an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha. If one accuses him of a pārājika offense, saying: ‘You are no ascetic, no son of the Sakyan, there is no Uposatha or Pavāraṇā or Saṅgha act with you,’ even so, it is a case involving a different part of the offense, and a trace is incurred. There is an offense by speech, a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

Bhikkhu saṅghādisesaṃ ajjhāpajjanto diṭṭho hoti, saṅghādisese thullaccayadiṭṭhi hoti…pe… pācittiyadiṭṭhi hoti… pāṭidesanīyadiṭṭhi hoti… dukkaṭadiṭṭhi hoti… dubbhāsitadiṭṭhi hoti. Tañce pārājikena codeti – ‘‘assamaṇosi’’…pe… evampi āpattaññabhāgiyaṃ hoti leso ca upādinno, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

A bhikkhu is seen committing an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha, and regarding that offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha, one has the perception of it as a grave offense, or as a pācittiya offense, or as a pāṭidesanīya offense, or as a dukkaṭa offense, or as an offense of wrong speech. If one accuses him of a pārājika offense, saying: ‘You are no ascetic’—[...]—even so, it is a case involving a different part of the offense, and a trace is incurred. There is an offense by speech, a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

Bhikkhu thullaccayaṃ ajjhāpajjanto diṭṭho hoti thullaccaye thullaccayadiṭṭhi hoti…pe… thullaccaye pācittiyadiṭṭhi hoti… pāṭidesanīyadiṭṭhi hoti… dukkaṭadiṭṭhi hoti… dubbhāsitadiṭṭhi hoti… saṅghādisesadiṭṭhi hoti. Tañce pārājikena codeti – ‘‘assamaṇosi’’ …pe… evampi āpattaññabhāgiyaṃ hoti leso ca upādinno, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

A bhikkhu is seen committing a grave offense, and regarding that grave offense, one has the perception of it as a grave offense, or as a pācittiya offense, or as a pāṭidesanīya offense, or as a dukkaṭa offense, or as an offense of wrong speech, or as an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha. There is an offense by speech, a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

Bhikkhu pācittiyaṃ…pe… pāṭidesanīyaṃ… dukkaṭaṃ… dubbhāsitaṃ ajjhāpajjanto diṭṭho hoti, dubbhāsite dubbhāsitadiṭṭhi hoti…pe… dubbhāsite saṅghādisesadiṭṭhi hoti… thullaccayadiṭṭhi hoti… pācittiyadiṭṭhi hoti… pāṭidesanīyadiṭṭhi hoti… dukkaṭadiṭṭhi hoti. Tañce pārājikena codeti – ‘‘assamaṇosi, asakyaputtiyosi, natthi tayā saddhiṃ uposatho vā pavāraṇā vā saṅghakammaṃ vā’’ti, evampi āpattaññabhāgiyaṃ hoti leso ca upādinno, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

A bhikkhu is seen committing a pācittiya offense, or a pāṭidesanīya offense, or a dukkaṭa offense, or an offense of wrong speech. Regarding that offense of wrong speech, one has the perception of it as an offense of wrong speech, or as an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha, or as a grave offense, or as a pācittiya offense, or as a pāṭidesanīya offense, or as a dukkaṭa offense. If one accuses him of a pārājika offense, saying: ‘You are no ascetic, no son of the Sakyan, there is no Uposatha or Pavāraṇā or Saṅgha act with you,’ even so, it is a case involving a different part of the offense, and a trace is incurred. There is an offense by speech, a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

Ekekaṃ mūlaṃ kātuna cakkaṃ bandhitabbaṃ.

Each root should be made into a wheel and bound.

407. Bhikkhu saṅghādisesaṃ ajjhāpajjanto diṭṭho hoti saṅghādisese saṅghādisesadiṭṭhi hoti. Tañce pārājikena codāpeti – ‘‘assamaṇosi’’…pe… evampi āpattaññabhāgiyaṃ hoti leso ca upādinno, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

407.A bhikkhu is seen committing an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha, and regarding that offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha, one has the perception of it as an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha. A bhikkhu is seen committing a grave offense, and regarding that grave offense, one has the perception of it as a grave offense, or as a pācittiya offense, or as a pāṭidesanīya offense, or as a dukkaṭa offense, or as an offense of wrong speech, or as an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha. Tañce pārājikena codāpeti – ‘‘assamaṇosi, asakyaputtiyosi, natthi tayā saddhiṃ uposatho vā pavāraṇā vā saṅghakammaṃ vā’’ti, evampi āpattaññabhāgiyaṃ hoti leso ca upādinno, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

408. Anāpatti tathāsaññī codeti vā codāpeti vā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

408.There is no offense for one who, perceiving it as such, accuses or has someone accused; for an insane person; for the first offender.

Dutiyaduṭṭhadosasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The second training rule on malicious accusation is concluded, the ninth.
