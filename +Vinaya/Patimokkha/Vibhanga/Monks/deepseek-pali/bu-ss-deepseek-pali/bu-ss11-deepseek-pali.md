# BU-SN 11

## 11. Bhedānuvattakasikkhāpadaṃ

## 11.The Training Rule Concerning Siding with Schism.

417. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena devadatto saṅghabhedāya parakkamati cakkabhedāya. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘adhammavādī devadatto, avinayavādī devadatto. Kathañhi nāma devadatto saṅghabhedāya parakkamissati cakkabhedāyā’’ti! Evaṃ vutte kokāliko kaṭamodakatissako khaṇḍadeviyā putto samuddadatto te bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha. Dhammavādī devadatto, vinayavādī devadatto. Amhākañca devadatto chandañca ruciñca ādāya voharati jānāti no bhāsati amhākaṃpetaṃ khamatī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū devadattassa saṅghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā bhavissanti vaggavādakā’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū te anuvattake bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū devadattassa saṅghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā honti vaggavādakā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… ‘‘kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā devadattassa saṅghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā bhavissanti vaggavādakā! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

417.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrel Sanctuary. Now at that time Devadatta was striving to cause a schism in the Saṅgha, to break up the assembly. The bhikkhus said: “Devadatta speaks against the Dhamma, Devadatta speaks against the Discipline. How can Devadatta strive to cause a schism in the Saṅgha, to break up the assembly?” When this was said, Kokālika, Kaṭamodakatissaka, the son of Khaṇḍadevī, and Samuddadatta said to those bhikkhus: “Do not say that, venerable ones. Devadatta speaks in accordance with the Dhamma, Devadatta speaks in accordance with the Discipline. Devadatta knows our wishes and preferences, and he speaks (with us), and this is agreeable to us.” The bhikkhus who were modest … they complained and criticized: “How can these bhikkhus side with Devadatta who is striving to cause a schism in the Saṅgha, how can they be faction-makers?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked those supporting bhikkhus in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One … “Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus side with Devadatta who is striving to cause a schism in the Saṅgha, that they are faction-makers?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them … “How can these foolish men side with Devadatta who is striving to cause a schism in the Saṅgha, how can they be faction-makers? This will not lead to faith in those without it … And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

418. ‘‘Tasseva kho pana bhikkhussa bhikkhū honti anuvattakā vaggavādakā eko vā dve vā tayo vā. Te evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘māyasmanto etaṃ bhikkhuṃ kiñci avacuttha. Dhammavādī ceso bhikkhu, vinayavādī ceso bhikkhu. Amhākañceso bhikkhu chandañca ruciñca ādāya voharati jānāti no bhāsati amhākampetaṃ khamatī’ti, te bhikkhū bhikkhūhi evamassu vacanīyā – ‘māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha, na ceso bhikkhu dhammavādī, na ceso bhikkhu vinayavādī, māyasmantānampi saṅghabhedo ruccittha, sametāyasmantānaṃ saṅghena, samaggo hi saṅgho sammodamāno avivadamāno ekuddeso phāsu viharatī’ti. Evañca te bhikkhū bhikkhūhi vuccamānā tatheva paggaṇheyyuṃ, te bhikkhū bhikkhūhi yāvatatiyaṃ samanubhāsitabbā tassa paṭinissaggāya. Yāvatatiyañce samanubhāsiyamānā taṃ paṭinissajjeyyuṃ, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ, no ce paṭinissajjeyyuṃ, saṅghādiseso’’ti.

418.“If, regarding that same bhikkhu, there are bhikkhus who are his followers and faction-makers—one or two or three—and they say: ‘Venerable ones, do not say anything against that bhikkhu. That bhikkhu speaks in accordance with the Dhamma, that bhikkhu speaks in accordance with the Discipline. That bhikkhu knows our wishes and preferences, and he speaks (with us), and this is agreeable to us,’ those bhikkhus should be addressed by the bhikkhus thus: ‘Venerable ones, do not say that. That bhikkhu does not speak in accordance with the Dhamma, that bhikkhu does not speak in accordance with the Discipline. Do you approve of schism in the Saṅgha? Venerable ones, be in harmony with the Saṅgha, for the Saṅgha—united, harmonious, without dispute, with a single recitation—dwells in comfort.’ If those bhikkhus, being spoken to thus by the bhikkhus, persist as before, those bhikkhus should be formally admonished up to three times to abandon that view. If, being formally admonished up to three times, they abandon it, that is good. If they do not abandon it, it is a saṅghādisesa offense.”

419. Tasseva kho panāti tassa saṅghabhedakassa bhikkhuno.

419.Regarding that very: This refers to that schismatic bhikkhu.

Bhikkhū hontīti aññe bhikkhū honti.

There are bhikkhus: This means there are other bhikkhus.

Anuvattakāti yaṃdiṭṭhiko so hoti yaṃkhantiko yaṃruciko tepi taṃdiṭṭhikā honti taṃkhantikā taṃrucikā.

Followers: Whatever view, inclination, or preference he has, they also have that view, inclination, and preference.

Vaggavādakāti tassa vaṇṇāya pakkhāya ṭhitā honti.

Faction-makers: They stand for his praise and his side.

Eko vā dve vā tayo vāti eko vā hoti dve vā tayo vā. Te evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘māyasmanto etaṃ bhikkhuṃ kiñci avacuttha, dhammavādī ceso bhikkhu, vinayavādī ceso bhikkhu, amhākañceso bhikkhu chandañca ruciñca ādāya voharati jānāti no bhāsati amhākaṃpetaṃ khamatī’ti.

One or two or three: This means there is one, or two, or three. They might say this: ‘Venerable ones, do not say anything to this bhikkhu. This bhikkhu speaks in accordance with the Dhamma, this bhikkhu speaks in accordance with the Discipline. Moreover, this bhikkhu knows our wishes and preferences, and he speaks (with us), and this is agreeable to us.’

Te bhikkhūti ye te anuvattakā bhikkhū.

Those bhikkhus: This refers to those bhikkhus who are followers.

Bhikkhūhīti aññehi bhikkhūhi.

By the bhikkhus: This means by other bhikkhus.

Ye passanti ye suṇanti tehi vattabbā – ‘‘māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha. Na ceso bhikkhu dhammavādī, na ceso bhikkhu vinayavādī. Māyasmantānampi saṅghabhedo ruccittha. Sametāyasmantānaṃ saṅghena. Samaggo hi saṅgho sammodamāno avivadamāno ekuddeso phāsu viharatī’’ti. Dutiyampi vattabbā. Tatiyampi vattabbā. Sace paṭinissajjanti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce paṭinissajjanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Sutvā na vadanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Te bhikkhū saṅghamajjhampi ākaḍḍhitvā vattabbā – ‘‘māyasmanto evaṃ avacuttha. Na ceso bhikkhu dhammavādī, na ceso bhikkhu vinayavādī. Māyasmantānampi saṅghabhedo ruccittha. Sametāyasmantānaṃ saṅghena. Samaggo hi saṅgho sammodamāno avivadamāno ekuddeso phāsu viharatī’’ti. Dutiyampi vattabbā. Tatiyampi vattabbā. Sace paṭinissajjanti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce paṭinissajjanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Te bhikkhū samanubhāsitabbā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, samanubhāsitabbā. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

Those who see or hear should tell them: ‘Venerable ones, do not speak thus. This bhikkhu is not a Dhamma-speaker, nor is this bhikkhu a Vinaya-speaker. Venerable ones, do not let schism in the Saṅgha be pleasing to you. Venerable ones, be in harmony with the Saṅgha, for the Saṅgha—united, harmonious, without dispute, with a single recitation—dwells in comfort.’ A second time they should be told. A third time they should be told. If they abandon it, that is good; if they do not abandon it, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If they hear and do not speak, it is an offense of wrong-doing. Those bhikkhus should be brought into the midst of the Saṅgha and told: ‘Venerable ones, do not speak thus. This bhikkhu is not a Dhamma-speaker, nor is this bhikkhu a Vinaya-speaker. Venerable ones, do not let schism in the Saṅgha be pleasing to you. Venerable ones, be in harmony with the Saṅgha, for the Saṅgha—united, harmonious, without dispute, with a single recitation—dwells in comfort.’ A second time they should be told. A third time they should be told. If they abandon it, that is good; if they do not abandon it, it is an offense of wrong-doing. Those bhikkhus should be formally admonished. And thus, bhikkhus, should they be formally admonished. A competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Saṅgha:

420. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Itthannāmo ca itthanāmo ca bhikkhū itthannāmassa bhikkhuno saṅghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā vaggavādakā. Te taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū samanubhāseyya tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Esā ñatti.

420.“Venerable sir, may the Saṅgha hear me. The bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so are followers and faction-makers of the bhikkhu named so-and-so, who is striving to cause a schism in the Saṅgha. They do not relinquish that matter. If it seems right to the Saṅgha, the Saṅgha should formally admonish the bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so regarding the relinquishment of that matter. This is the motion.

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Itthannāmo ca itthannāmo ca bhikkhū itthannāmassa bhikkhuno saṅghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā vaggavādakā. Te taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. Saṅgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū samanubhāsati tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa ca itthannāmassa ca bhikkhūnaṃ samanubhāsanā tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sir, may the Saṅgha hear me. The bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so are followers and faction-makers of the bhikkhu named so-and-so, who is striving to cause a schism in the Saṅgha. They do not relinquish that matter. The Saṅgha formally admonishes the bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so regarding the relinquishment of that matter. If it is agreeable to the venerable ones that the bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so be formally admonished regarding the relinquishment of that matter, let them be silent; if it is not agreeable, let them speak.”

‘‘Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi…pe… tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi – suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Itthannāmo ca itthannāmo ca bhikkhū itthannāmassa bhikkhuno saṅghabhedāya parakkamantassa anuvattakā vaggavādakā. Te taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjanti. Saṅgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū samanubhāsati tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa ca itthannāmassa ca bhikkhūnaṃ samanubhāsanā tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“A second time I speak on this matter… and a third time I speak on this matter—Venerable sir, may the Saṅgha hear me. The bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so are followers and faction-makers of the bhikkhu named so-and-so, who is striving to cause a schism in the Saṅgha. They do not relinquish that matter. The Saṅgha formally admonishes the bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so regarding the relinquishment of that matter. If it is agreeable to the venerable ones that the bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so be formally admonished regarding the relinquishment of that matter, let them be silent; if it is not agreeable, let them speak.”

‘‘Samanubhaṭṭhā saṅghena itthannāmo ca itthannāmo ca bhikkhū tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The bhikkhus named so-and-so and so-and-so have been formally admonished by the Saṅgha regarding the relinquishment of that matter. The Saṅgha approves, therefore it is silent. I shall remember it thus.”

421. Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ, dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā, kammavācāpariyosāne āpatti saṅghādisesassa. Saṅghādisesaṃ ajjhāpajjantānaṃ ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ, dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā paṭippassambhanti. Dve tayo ekato samanubhāsitabbā, taduttari na samanubhāsitabbā.

421.An offense of wrong-doing in the motion, a grave offense with two formal acts, and a saṅghādisesa offense at the conclusion of the formal act. For those who incur a saṅghādisesa offense, the offense of wrong-doing in the motion and the grave offense with two formal acts cease. Two or three may be formally admonished together; more than that should not be formally admonished together.

Saṅghādisesoti…pe… tenapi vuccati ‘‘saṅghādiseso’’ti.

Saṅghādisesa: …and therefore it is called a saṅghādisesa offense.

422. Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

422.If, in a lawful act, one perceives it as a lawful act and does not relinquish it, there is a saṅghādisesa offense.

Dhammakamme vematikā na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

If, in a lawful act, one is in doubt and does not relinquish it, there is a saṅghādisesa offense.

Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjanti, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

If, in a lawful act, one perceives it as an unlawful act and does not relinquish it, there is a saṅghādisesa offense.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If, in an unlawful act, one perceives it as a lawful act, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

Adhammakamme vematikā, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If, in an unlawful act, one is in doubt, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If, in an unlawful act, one perceives it as an unlawful act, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

423. Anāpatti asamanubhāsantānaṃ, paṭinissajjantānaṃ, ummattakānaṃ, khittacittānaṃ, vedanāṭṭānaṃ, ādikammikānanti.

423.There is no offense for those who are not being formally admonished, for those who relinquish, for the insane, for those with deranged minds, for those afflicted by pain, and for beginners.

Bhedānuvattakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ ekādasamaṃ.

The training rule concerning following schismatics is finished, the eleventh.
