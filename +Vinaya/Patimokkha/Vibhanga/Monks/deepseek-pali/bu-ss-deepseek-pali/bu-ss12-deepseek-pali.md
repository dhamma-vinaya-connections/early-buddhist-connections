# BU-SN 12

## 12. Dubbacasikkhāpadaṃ

## 12.The Training Rule on Being Difficult to Admonish

424. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā channo anācaraṃ ācarati. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘māvuso, channa, evarūpaṃ akāsi. Netaṃ kappatī’’ti. So evaṃ vadeti – ‘‘kiṃ nu kho nāma tumhe, āvuso, maṃ vattabbaṃ maññatha? Ahaṃ kho nāma tumhe vadeyyaṃ. Amhākaṃ buddho amhākaṃ dhammo amhākaṃ ayyaputtena dhammo abhisamito. Seyyathāpi nāma mahāvāto vāyanto tiṇakaṭṭhapaṇṇasaṭaṃ [tiṇakaṭṭhapaṇṇakasaṭaṃ (ka.)] ekato ussāreyya, seyyathā vā pana nadī pabbateyyā saṅkhasevālapaṇakaṃ ekato ussāreyya, evameva tumhe nānānāmā nānāgottā nānājaccā nānākulā pabbajitā ekato ussaritā. Kiṃ nu kho nāma tumhe, āvuso, maṃ vattabbaṃ maññatha? Ahaṃ kho nāma tumhe vadeyyaṃ! Amhākaṃ buddho amhākaṃ dhammo amhākaṃ ayyaputtena dhammo abhisamito’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā channo bhikkhūhi sahadhammikaṃ vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karissatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ channaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, channa, bhikkhūhi sahadhammikaṃ vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karosī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… ‘‘kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, bhikkhūhi sahadhammikaṃ vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

424.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambi in Ghosita’s monastery. Now at that time Venerable Channa was behaving improperly. The bhikkhus said to him: “Do not, friend Channa, do such a thing. That is not allowable.” He replied thus: “What do you think, friends, that you should speak to me? It is I who should speak to you. Our Buddha, our Dhamma, the Dhamma has been realized by our noble son. Just as a great wind might sweep up grass, sticks, and old leaves all together, or as a mountain stream might sweep up moss and water plants all together, even so, you, with various names, various clans, various castes, various families, have gone forth and are all swept up together. What do you think, friends, that you should speak to me? It is I who should speak to you! Our Buddha, our Dhamma, the Dhamma has been realized by our noble son.” Those bhikkhus who were modest… they complained and criticized and spread it about: “How can Venerable Channa, when legitimately corrected by the bhikkhus, make himself uncorrectable?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Channa in many ways, informed the Blessed One of the matter… “Is it true, Channa, that when legitimately corrected by the bhikkhus, you make yourself uncorrectable?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “Foolish man, how can you, when legitimately corrected by the bhikkhus, make yourself uncorrectable! This will not, foolish man, inspire confidence in those without it… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

425. ‘‘Bhikkhu paneva dubbacajātiko hoti uddesapariyāpannesu sikkhāpadesu bhikkhūhi sahadhammikaṃ vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karoti – ‘mā maṃ āyasmanto kiñci avacuttha kalyāṇaṃ vā pāpakaṃ vā, ahaṃpāyasmante na kiñci vakkhāmi kalyāṇaṃ vā pāpakaṃ vā, viramathāyasmanto mama vacanāyā’ti, so bhikkhu bhikkhūhi evamassa vacanīyo – ‘māyasmā attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ akāsi, vacanīyamevāyasmā attānaṃ karotu, āyasmāpi bhikkhū vadetu sahadhammena, bhikkhūpi āyasmantaṃ vakkhanti sahadhammena. Evaṃ saṃvaddhā hi tassa bhagavato parisā yadidaṃ aññamaññavacanena aññamaññavuṭṭhāpanenāti. Evañca so bhikkhuṃ bhikkhūhi vuccamāno tatheva paggaṇheyya, so bhikkhu bhikkhūhi yāvatatiyaṃ samanubhāsitabbo tassa paṭinissaggāya. Yāvatatiyañce samanubhāsīyamāno taṃ paṭinissajjeyya, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce paṭinissajjeyya, saṅghādiseso’’ti.

425.“If a bhikkhu is by nature difficult to instruct, and when being spoken to by bhikkhus in accordance with the Dhamma regarding the training rules included in the Pātimokkha, he makes himself unadmonishable, saying: ‘Do not, venerable ones, say anything to me, good or bad, and I will not say anything to you, good or bad. Refrain, venerable ones, from speaking to me.’ That bhikkhu should be addressed by the bhikkhus thus: ‘Do not, venerable one, make yourself unadmonishable. Let the venerable one make himself admonishable. Let the venerable one admonish the bhikkhus in accordance with the Dhamma, and let the bhikkhus admonish the venerable one in accordance with the Dhamma. For thus the assembly of the Blessed One grows, namely, through mutual speaking to one another and mutual raising up.’ If, being thus addressed by the bhikkhus, that bhikkhu persists as before, that bhikkhu should be formally admonished by the bhikkhus up to the third time for the relinquishment of that matter. If, being formally admonished up to the third time, he relinquishes it, it is good. If he does not relinquish it, it is a Saṅghādisesa offense.”

426. Bhikkhū paneva dubbacajātiko hotīti dubbaco hoti dovacassakaraṇehi dhammehi samannāgato akkhamo appadakkhiṇaggāhī anusāsaniṃ.

426.“A bhikkhu who is by nature difficult to instruct” means he is difficult to speak to, endowed with qualities that make him difficult to speak to, intolerant, and not accepting instruction respectfully.

Uddesapariyāpannesu sikkhāpadesūti pātimokkhapariyāpannesu sikkhāpadesu.

“Regarding the training rules included in the enunciation” means the training rules included in the Pātimokkha enunciation.

Bhikkhūhīti aññehi bhikkhūhi.

“By bhikkhus” means by other bhikkhus.

Sahadhammikaṃ nāma yaṃ bhagavatā paññattaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ, etaṃ sahadhammikaṃ nāma.

“In accordance with the Dhamma” means a training rule that has been laid down by the Blessed One.

Tena vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karoti – ‘‘mā maṃ āyasmanto kiñci avacuttha kalyāṇaṃ vā pāpakaṃ vā, ahaṃpāyasmante na kiñci vakkhāmi kalyāṇaṃ vā pāpakaṃ vā. Viramathāyasmanto mama vacanāyā’’ti.

Being thus addressed, he makes himself unadmonishable, saying: ‘Do not, venerable ones, say anything to me, good or bad, and I will not say anything to you, good or bad. Refrain, venerable ones, from speaking to me.’

So bhikkhūti yo so dubbacajātiko bhikkhu.

“That bhikkhu” refers to the bhikkhu who is obstinate and difficult to instruct.

Bhikkhūhīti aññehi bhikkhūhi. Ye passanti ye suṇanti tehi vattabbo – ‘‘māyasmā attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ akāsi. Vacanīyameva āyasmā attānaṃ karotu. Āyasmāpi bhikkhū vadetu sahadhammena, bhikkhūpi āyasmantaṃ vakkhanti sahadhammena. Evaṃ saṃvaddhā hi tassa bhagavato parisā yadidaṃ aññamaññavacanena aññamaññavuṭṭhāpanenā’’ti. Dutiyampi vattabbo. Tatiyampi vattabbo. Sace paṭinissajjati, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce paṭinissajjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Sutvā na vadanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. So bhikkhu saṅghamajjhampi ākaḍḍhitvā vattabbo – ‘‘māyasmā attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ akāsi…pe… aññamaññavuṭṭhāpanenā’’ti. Dutiyampi vattabbo. Tatiyampi vattabbo. Sace paṭinissajjati, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce paṭinissajjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. So bhikkhu samanubhāsitabbo. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, samanubhāsitabbo. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

“By bhikkhus” means by other bhikkhus. Those who see or hear should address him: ‘Venerable, do not make yourself unadmonishable. Let the venerable make himself admonishable. Let the venerable admonish the bhikkhus in accordance with the Dhamma, and let the bhikkhus admonish the venerable in accordance with the Dhamma. For thus the assembly of the Blessed One grows, namely, through mutual speaking to one another and mutual raising up.’ A second time he should be addressed. A third time he should be addressed. If he relinquishes it, it is good; if he does not relinquish it, there is an offense of wrong-doing. Those who hear but do not speak incur an offense of wrong-doing. That bhikkhu should be brought into the midst of the Saṅgha and addressed: ‘Venerable, do not make yourself unadmonishable… through mutual raising up.’ A second time he should be addressed. A third time he should be addressed. If he relinquishes it, it is good; if he does not relinquish it, there is an offense of wrong-doing. That bhikkhu should be formally admonished. And thus, bhikkhus, should he be formally admonished. A competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Saṅgha:

427. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu bhikkhūhi sahadhammikaṃ vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karoti. So taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ samanubhāseyya tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Esā ñatti.

427.“Venerable Saṅgha, please listen to me. This bhikkhu named so-and-so, when being spoken to by bhikkhus in accordance with the Dhamma, makes himself unamenable to correction. He does not relinquish that matter. If the Saṅgha is ready, the Saṅgha should formally admonish the bhikkhu named so-and-so for the relinquishment of that matter. This is the motion.”

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu bhikkhūhi sahadhammikaṃ vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karoti. So taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjati. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ samanubhāsati tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno samanubhāsanā tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sir, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, when being spoken to by bhikkhus in accordance with the Dhamma, makes himself unamenable to correction. He does not relinquish that matter. The Saṅgha formally admonishes the bhikkhu, named so-and-so, for the relinquishment of that matter. Whoever of the venerable ones approves of the formal admonishment of the bhikkhu, named so-and-so, for the relinquishment of that matter, let him be silent. Whoever does not approve, let him speak.”

‘‘Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi…pe… tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi – ‘‘suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu bhikkhūhi sahadhammikaṃ vuccamāno attānaṃ avacanīyaṃ karoti. So taṃ vatthuṃ na paṭinissajjati. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ samanubhāsati tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno samanubhāsanā tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“A second time I speak on this matter… and a third time I speak on this matter: ‘Venerable sir, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, when being spoken to by bhikkhus in accordance with the Dhamma, makes himself unamenable to correction. He does not relinquish that matter. The Saṅgha formally admonishes the bhikkhu, named so-and-so, for the relinquishment of that matter. Whoever of the venerable ones approves of the formal admonishment of the bhikkhu, named so-and-so, for the relinquishment of that matter, let him remain silent; whoever does not approve, let him speak.’

‘‘Samanubhaṭṭho saṅghena itthannāmo bhikkhu tassa vatthussa paṭinissaggāya. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The bhikkhu named so-and-so has been formally admonished by the Saṅgha for the relinquishment of that matter. The Saṅgha approves, therefore it is silent. Thus do I hold it.”

428. Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ, dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā, kammavācāpariyosāne āpatti saṅghādisesassa. Saṅghādisesaṃ ajjhāpajjantassa ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ, dvīhi kammavācāhi thullaccayā paṭippassambhanti.

428.For the motion, a wrong-doing; with two formal announcements, a grave offense; at the conclusion of the formal act, an offense entailing suspension. For one incurring a Saṅghādisesa offense, the wrong-doing for the motion and the grave offense with two formal announcements are remitted.

Saṅghādisesoti…pe… tenapi vuccati saṅghādisesoti.

It is called Saṅghādisesa… and so it is called Saṅghādisesa.

429. Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjati, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

429.In a lawful act, one perceiving it as a lawful act does not relinquish it, [commits] a Saṅghādisesa offense.

Dhammakamme vematiko na paṭinissajjati, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

In a lawful act, being uncertain, he does not relinquish it: a Saṅghādisesa offense.

Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjati, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

In a lawful act, perceiving it as an unlawful act, he does not relinquish it: a Saṅghādisesa offense.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

In an unlawful act, perceiving it as a lawful act: an offense of wrong-doing.

Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

In an unlawful act, being uncertain: an offense of wrong-doing.

Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

In an unlawful act, perceiving it as an unlawful act: an offense of wrong-doing.

430. Anāpatti asamanubhāsantassa, paṭinissajjantassa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

430.There is no offense for one who is not being formally admonished, for one who relinquishes, for one who is insane, and for one who is a beginner.

Dubbacasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dvādasamaṃ.

The training rule on being difficult to admonish is concluded, the twelfth.
