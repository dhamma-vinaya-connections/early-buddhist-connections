# BU-SN 8

## 8. Duṭṭhadosasikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The Training Rule on Offenses Arising from Anger

380. [idaṃ vatthu cūḷava. 189] Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmatā dabbena mallaputtena jātiyā sattavassena arahattaṃ sacchikataṃ hoti. Yaṃ kiñci [yañca kiñci (sī. ka.)] sāvakena pattabbaṃ sabbaṃ tena anuppattaṃ hoti. Natthi cassa kiñci uttari karaṇīyaṃ, katassa vā paticayo. Atha kho āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘‘mayā kho jātiyā sattavassena arahattaṃ sacchikataṃ. Yaṃ kiñci sāvakena pattabbaṃ sabbaṃ mayā anuppattaṃ. Natthi ca me kiñci uttari karaṇīyaṃ, katassa vā paticayo. Kinnu kho ahaṃ saṅghassa veyyāvaccaṃ kareyya’’nti?

380.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels’ Feeding Ground. Now at that time, the Venerable Dabba the Mallian had realized arahantship at the age of seven. Whatever can be attained by a disciple, all that had been attained by him. There was nothing further for him to do, nor any accumulation of what had been done. Then, while the Venerable Dabba the Mallian was in seclusion, withdrawn, this thought arose in his mind: 'I have realized arahantship at the age of seven. Whatever can be attained by a disciple, all that has been attained by me. There is nothing further for me to do, nor any accumulation of what has been done. What service to the Saṅgha should I perform?'

Atha kho āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa etadahosi – ‘‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ saṅghassa senāsanañca paññapeyyaṃ bhattāni ca uddiseyya’’nti. Atha kho āyasmā dabbo mallaputto sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā dabbo mallaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idha mayhaṃ, bhante, rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi mayā kho jātiyā sattavassena arahattaṃ sacchikataṃ, yaṃ kiñci sāvakena pattabbaṃ, sabbaṃ mayā anupattaṃ, natthi ca me kiñci uttari karaṇīyaṃ, katassa vā paticayo, kiṃ nu kho ahaṃ saṅghassa veyyāvaccaṃ kareyya’’nti. Tassa mayhaṃ bhante, etadahosi yaṃnūnāhaṃ ‘‘saṅghassa senāsanañca paññapeyyaṃ bhattāni ca uddiseyyanti. Icchāmahaṃ, bhante, saṅghassa senāsanañca paññapetuṃ bhattāni ca uddisitu’’nti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, dabba. Tena hi tvaṃ, dabba, saṅghassa senāsanañca paññapehi bhattāni ca uddisā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā dabbo mallaputto bhagavato paccassosi. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, saṅgho dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ senāsanapaññāpakañca bhattuddesakañca sammannatu. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbo. Paṭhamaṃ dabbo mallaputto yācitabbo. Yācitvā byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

Then Venerable Dabba the Mallian thought: 'Why don't I assign lodgings and designate meals for the Saṅgha?' Then, in the evening, Venerable Dabba emerged from seclusion and approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, Venerable Dabba the Mallian said to the Blessed One: 'Just now, venerable sir, while I was in seclusion, withdrawn, this thought arose in my mind: "I have realized arahantship at the age of seven. Whatever can be attained by a disciple, all that I have attained. There is nothing further for me to do, nor any accumulation of what has been done. So what service should I perform for the Saṅgha?" Then, venerable sir, it occurred to me: "Why don't I assign lodgings and designate meals for the Saṅgha?" I wish, venerable sir, to assign lodgings and designate meals for the Saṅgha.' 'Good, good, Dabba. Then, Dabba, you should assign lodgings and designate meals for the Saṅgha.' 'Yes, venerable sir,' replied Venerable Dabba the Mallian to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and regarding this matter, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: 'Well then, bhikkhus, let the Saṅgha appoint Dabba the Mallian as the assigner of lodgings and the designator of meals. And this is how he should be appointed. First, Dabba the Mallian should be asked. Having asked him, a competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Saṅgha:'

381. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ saṅgho āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ senāsanapaññāpakañca bhattuddesakañca sammanneyya. Esā ñatti.

381.'Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. If it is suitable for the Saṅgha, the Saṅgha should appoint Venerable Dabba the Mallian as the assigner of lodgings and the designator of meals. This is the motion.'

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Saṅgho āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ senāsanapaññāpakañca bhattuddesakañca sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa senāsanapaññāpakassa ca bhattuddesakassa ca sammuti, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

'Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. The Saṅgha appoints Venerable Dabba the Mallian as the assigner of lodgings and the designator of meals. If the appointment of Venerable Dabba the Mallian as the assigner of lodgings and the designator of meals is agreeable to any venerable one, let him be silent; if it is not agreeable, let him speak.'

‘‘Sammato saṅghena āyasmā dabbo mallaputto senāsanapaññāpako ca bhattuddesako ca. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

'Venerable Dabba the Mallian has been appointed by the Saṅgha as the assigner of lodgings and the designator of meals. The Saṅgha approves, therefore it is silent. Thus I hold it.'

382. Sammato ca panāyasmā dabbo mallaputto sabhāgānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ ekajjhaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapeti. Ye te bhikkhū suttantikā tesaṃ ekajjhaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapeti – ‘‘te aññamaññaṃ suttantaṃ saṅgāyissantī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū vinayadharā tesaṃ ekajjhaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapeti – ‘‘te aññamaññaṃ vinayaṃ vinicchinissantī’’ti [vinicchissantīti (ka.)]. Ye te bhikkhū dhammakathikā tesaṃ ekajjhaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapeti – ‘‘te aññamaññaṃ dhammaṃ sākacchissantī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū jhāyino tesaṃ ekajjhaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapeti – ‘‘te aññamaññaṃ na byābādhissantī’’ti [na byābāhissantīti (ka.)]. Ye te bhikkhū tiracchānakathikā kāyadaḷhibahulā [kāyadaḍḍhibahulā (sī.)] viharanti tesampi ekajjhaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapeti – ‘‘imāyapime āyasmanto ratiyā acchissantī’’ti. Yepi te bhikkhū vikāle āgacchanti tesampi tejodhātuṃ samāpajjitvā teneva ālokena senāsanaṃ paññapeti. Apisu bhikkhū sañcicca vikāle āgacchanti – ‘‘mayaṃ āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa iddhipāṭihāriyaṃ passissāmā’’ti.

382.And furthermore, Venerable Dabba the Mallian was appointed to assign lodgings to bhikkhus of similar inclinations. To those bhikkhus who were versed in the Suttas, he assigned lodgings together, thinking, 'They will recite the Suttas to each other.' To those bhikkhus who were versed in the Vinaya, he assigned lodgings together, thinking, 'They will examine the Vinaya with each other.' To those bhikkhus who were Dhamma speakers, he assigned lodgings together, thinking, 'They will discuss the Dhamma with each other.' To those bhikkhus who were meditators, he assigned lodgings together, thinking, 'They will not disturb each other.' Even to those bhikkhus who engaged in worldly talk and were much given to bodily comfort, he assigned lodgings together, thinking, 'These venerable ones will be content with this enjoyment.' And even for those bhikkhus who arrived at an unseemly hour, he would enter into the fire element, and by that light, he would assign lodgings. Moreover, some bhikkhus deliberately arrived at an unseemly hour, thinking, 'We will see the miracle of Venerable Dabba the Mallian’s psychic power.'

Te āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘amhākaṃ, āvuso dabba, senāsanaṃ paññapehī’’ti. Te āyasmā dabbo mallaputto evaṃ vadeti – ‘‘katthāyasmantā icchanti, kattha paññapemī’’ti? Te sañcicca dūre apadisanti – ‘‘amhākaṃ, āvuso dabba, gijjhakūṭe pabbate senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, corapapāte senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, isigilipasse kāḷasilāyaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, vebhārapasse sattapaṇṇiguhāyaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, sītavane sappasoṇḍikapabbhāre senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, gotamakakandarāyaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, tindukakandarāyaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, tapodakandarāyaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, tapodārāme senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, jīvakambavane senāsanaṃ paññapehi. Amhākaṃ, āvuso, maddakucchismiṃ migadāye senāsanaṃ paññapehī’’ti.

They approached the Venerable Dabba the Mallian and said, "Friend Dabba, please allocate lodgings for us." The Venerable Dabba the Mallian replied, "Where do the venerable ones wish? Where shall I allocate lodgings?" Deliberately, they pointed to distant places, saying, "Friend Dabba, please allocate lodgings for us on Vulture Peak. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us at Robbers' Precipice. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us on the Black Rock of Isigili Slope. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in the Seven-Leaf Cave on Vebhara Slope. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in the Cool Wood, at Snake's Pool Rock Shelter. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in the Gotamaka Cave. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in the Tinduka Cave. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in the Tapoda Cave. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in the Tapoda Monastery. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in Jīvaka's Mango Grove. Friend, please allocate lodgings for us in the Deer Park at Maddakucchi."

Tesaṃ āyasmā dabbo mallaputto tejodhātuṃ samāpajjitvā aṅguliyā jalamānāya purato purato gacchati. Tepi teneva ālokena āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa piṭṭhito piṭṭhito gacchanti. Tesaṃ āyasmā dabbo mallaputto evaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapeti – ‘‘ayaṃ mañco, idaṃ pīṭhaṃ, ayaṃ bhisi, idaṃ bimbohanaṃ, idaṃ vaccaṭṭhānaṃ, idaṃ passāvaṭṭhānaṃ, idaṃ pānīyaṃ, idaṃ paribhojanīyaṃ, ayaṃ kattaradaṇḍo, idaṃ saṅghassa katikasaṇṭhānaṃ, imaṃ kālaṃ pavisitabbaṃ, imaṃ kālaṃ nikkhamitabba’’nti. Tesaṃ āyasmā dabbo mallaputto evaṃ senāsanaṃ paññapetvā punadeva veḷuvanaṃ paccāgacchati.

The Venerable Dabba the Mallian then entered the fire element and, with his finger ablaze, walked ahead. They followed behind the Venerable Dabba the Mallian, guided by that light. The Venerable Dabba the Mallian assigned them lodgings thus: "Here is a bed, here is a seat, here is a mattress, here is a pillow, here is the toilet, here is the place for urination, here is drinking water, here is water for use, here is a broom, here is the Sangha's schedule of rules, this is the time to enter, this is the time to leave." Having assigned them lodgings in this way, the Venerable Dabba the Mallian returned again to the Bamboo Grove.

383. Tena kho pana samayena mettiyabhūmajakā [bhummajakā (sī. syā.)] bhikkhū navakā ceva honti appapuññā ca. Yāni saṅghassa lāmakāni senāsanāni tāni tesaṃ pāpuṇanti lāmakāni ca bhattāni. Tena kho pana samayena rājagahe manussā icchanti therānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ abhisaṅkhārikaṃ piṇḍapātaṃ dātuṃ sappimpi telampi uttaribhaṅgampi. Mettiyabhūmajakānaṃ pana bhikkhūnaṃ pākatikaṃ denti yathārandhaṃ kaṇājakaṃ bilaṅgadutiyaṃ. Te pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātappaṭikkantā there bhikkhū pucchanti – ‘‘tumhākaṃ, āvuso, bhattagge kiṃ ahosi? Tumhākaṃ, āvuso, bhattagge kiṃ ahosī’’ti? Ekacce therā evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘amhākaṃ, āvuso, sappi ahosi telaṃ ahosi uttaribhaṅgaṃ ahosī’’ti. Mettiyabhūmajakā pana bhikkhū evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘amhākaṃ, āvuso, na kiñci ahosi, pākatikaṃ yathārandhaṃ kaṇājakaṃ bilaṅgadutiya’’nti.

383.Now at that time the bhikkhus of Mettiya and Bhūmajaka were both newly ordained and of little merit. They received the poor lodgings assigned by the Sangha and poor almsfood. At that time in Rājagaha, people wished to give specially prepared almsfood to the senior bhikkhus, including ghee, oil, and extra dishes. But to the bhikkhus of Mettiya and Bhūmajaka, they gave only ordinary food—broken rice accompanied by bilaṅga gruel. After their meal, on returning from almsround, they asked the senior bhikkhus, "Friends, what did you have in the dining hall? Friends, what did you have in the dining hall?" Some senior bhikkhus replied, "Friends, we had ghee, we had oil, we had extra dishes." But the bhikkhus of Mettiya and Bhūmajaka said, "Friends, we had nothing special—just ordinary broken rice accompanied by bilaṅga gruel."

Tena kho pana samayena kalyāṇabhattiko gahapati saṅghassa catukkabhattaṃ deti niccabhattaṃ. So bhattagge saputtadāro upatiṭṭhitvā parivisati. Aññe odanena pucchanti, aññe sūpena pucchanti, aññe telena pucchanti, aññe uttaribhaṅgena pucchanti. Tena kho pana samayena kalyāṇabhattikassa gahapatino bhattaṃ svātanāya mettiyabhūmajakānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ uddiṭṭhaṃ hoti. Atha kho kalyāṇabhattiko gahapati ārāmaṃ agamāsi kenacideva karaṇīyena. So yenāyasmā dabbo mallaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho kalyāṇabhattikaṃ gahapatiṃ āyasmā dabbo mallaputto dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho kalyāṇabhattiko gahapati āyasmatā dabbena mallaputtena dhammiyā kathāya sandassito samādapito samuttejito sampahaṃsito āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kassa, bhante, amhākaṃ ghare svātanāya bhattaṃ uddiṭṭha’’nti? ‘‘Mettiyabhūmajakānaṃ kho, gahapati, bhikkhūnaṃ tumhākaṃ ghare svātanāya bhattaṃ uddiṭṭha’’nti. Atha kho kalyāṇabhattiko gahapati anattamano ahosi – ‘‘kathañhi nāma pāpabhikkhū amhākaṃ ghare bhuñjissantī’’ti! Gharaṃ gantvā dāsiṃ āṇāpesi – ‘‘ye, je, sve bhattikā āgacchanti te koṭṭhake āsanaṃ paññapetvā kaṇājakena bilaṅgadutiyena parivisā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ ayyā’’ti kho sā dāsī kalyāṇabhattikassa gahapatino paccassosi.

At that time, the householder Kalyāṇabhattika was offering a fourfold meal to the Sangha as a regular alms-giving. He stood in the dining hall with his children and wife, serving the food. Some asked for rice, some for soup, some for oil, and some for extra side dishes. Now at that time, the meal for the next day at the householder Kalyāṇabhattika’s house had been assigned to the bhikkhus of Mettiya and Bhūmajaka. Then the householder Kalyāṇabhattika went to the monastery for some business. He approached Venerable Dabba the Mallian; having approached, he paid homage to him and sat down to one side. As he sat there, Venerable Dabba the Mallian instructed, roused, and gladdened the householder Kalyāṇabhattika with a Dhamma talk. Then the householder Kalyāṇabhattika, having been instructed, roused, and gladdened by Venerable Dabba the Mallian, said to him: “Venerable sir, for whom has the meal at our house been assigned for tomorrow?” “Householder, the meal at your house for tomorrow has been assigned to the bhikkhus of Mettiya and Bhūmajaka.” Then the householder Kalyāṇabhattika became displeased, thinking, “How can these wicked bhikkhus eat at our house?” Returning home, he ordered his female servant: “Girl, when those who are to receive alms arrive tomorrow, prepare seats for them in the storeroom and serve them broken rice accompanied by bilaṅga gruel.” “Yes, master,” replied the female servant to the householder Kalyāṇabhattika.

Atha kho mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū – ‘‘hiyyo kho, āvuso, amhākaṃ kalyāṇabhattikassa gahapatino ghare bhattaṃ uddiṭṭhaṃ, sve amhe kalyāṇabhattiko gahapati saputtadāro upatiṭṭhitvā parivisissati; aññe odanena pucchissanti, aññe sūpena pucchissanti, aññe telena pucchissanti, aññe uttaribhaṅgena pucchissantī’’ti. Te teneva somanassena na cittarūpaṃ rattiyā supiṃsu. Atha kho mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaraṃ ādāya yena kalyāṇabhattikassa gahapatino nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu. Addasā kho sā dāsī mettiyabhūmajake bhikkhū dūratova āgacchante. Disvāna koṭṭhake āsanaṃ paññapetvā mettiyabhūmajake bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘nisīdatha, bhante’’ti. Atha kho mettiyabhūmajakānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘nissaṃsayaṃ kho na tāva bhattaṃ siddhaṃ bhavissati! Yathā [yāva (sī.)] mayaṃ koṭṭhake nisīdeyyāmā’’ti [nisīdāpīyeyyāmāti (sī.), nisīdāpīyāmāti (syā.)]. Atha kho sā dāsī kaṇājakena bilaṅgadutiyena upagacchi – ‘‘bhuñjatha, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Mayaṃ kho, bhagini, niccabhattikā’’ti. ‘‘Jānāmi ayyā niccabhattikāttha. Apicāhaṃ hiyyova gahapatinā āṇattā – ‘ye, je, sve bhattikā āgacchanti te koṭṭhake āsanaṃ paññapetvā kaṇājakena bilaṅgadutiyena parivisā’ti. Bhuñjatha, bhante’’ti. Atha kho mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū – ‘‘hiyyo kho, āvuso, kalyāṇabhattiko gahapati ārāmaṃ agamāsi dabbassa mallaputtassa santike. Nissaṃsayaṃ kho mayaṃ dabbena mallaputtena gahapatino antare [santike (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] paribhinnā’’ti. Te teneva domanassena na cittarūpaṃ bhuñjiṃsu. Atha kho mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātappaṭikkantā ārāmaṃ gantvā pattacīvaraṃ paṭisāmetvā bahārāmakoṭṭhake saṅghāṭipallatthikāya nisīdiṃsu tuṇhībhūtā maṅkubhūtā pattakkhandhā adhomukhā pajjhāyantā appaṭibhānā.

Then the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka thought: "Yesterday, friends, a meal was announced for us at the house of the householder Kalyāṇabhattika. Tomorrow, the householder Kalyāṇabhattika, along with his children and wife, will attend to us and serve us. Some will ask for rice, some will ask for soup, some will ask for oil, and some will ask for extra dishes." Delighted by this thought, they did not sleep well that night. Then the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka, having dressed in the morning and taken their bowls and robes, went to the residence of the householder Kalyāṇabhattika. The female servant saw the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka coming from afar. Seeing them, she prepared seats in the storeroom and said to the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka: "Please sit down, venerable sirs." Then the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka thought: "Surely the meal is not ready yet! That’s why we are being asked to sit in the storeroom." Then the female servant approached them with broken rice accompanied by bilaṅga gruel, saying: "Please eat, venerable sirs." "We, sister, are regular meal recipients." "I know, sirs, that you are regular meal recipients. But yesterday the householder instructed me: ‘For those who come tomorrow for the meal, prepare seats for them in the storeroom and serve them broken rice accompanied by bilaṅga gruel.’ Please eat, venerable sirs." Then the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka thought: "Yesterday, friends, the householder Kalyāṇabhattika went to the monastery to see Dabba the Mallian. Surely, we have been disparaged to the householder by Dabba the Mallian." Distressed by this, they ate without enjoyment. Then the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka, after the meal, on their return from almsround, went to the monastery, put away their bowls and robes, and sat down in the outer gatehouse of the monastery with their outer robes draped over them, silent, sullen, their shoulders drooping, heads down, brooding, at a loss for words.

Atha kho mettiyā bhikkhunī yena mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā mettiyabhūmajake bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘vandāmi, ayyā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū nālapiṃsu. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho mettiyā bhikkhunī mettiyabhūmajake bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘vandāmi, ayyā’’ti. Tatiyampi kho mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū nālapiṃsu. ‘‘Kyāhaṃ ayyānaṃ aparajjhāmi? Tañce codāpeti – ‘‘parisaṅkitosi, diṭṭhosi…pe… parisaṅkitosi, sutosi…pe… parisaṅkitosi, diṭṭhosi, sutosi, pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpannosi, assamaṇosi’’…pe… āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

It is not seen of him that he is committing an offense entailing expulsion. If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You have been seen and heard… You have been seen and are suspected… You have been seen, heard, and are suspected; you have committed an offense entailing expulsion'… It is not heard of him: 'He has committed an offense entailing expulsion.'… It is not suspected of him: 'He has committed an offense entailing expulsion.' If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You are suspected and seen… You are suspected and heard… You are suspected, seen, and heard; you have committed an offense entailing expulsion; you are no ascetic'… there is a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

Diṭṭhassa hoti pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpajjanto. Tañce codāpeti – ‘‘sutosi’’…pe… tañce codāpeti – ‘‘parisaṅkitosi’’…pe… tañce codāpeti – ‘‘sutosi, parisaṅkitosi, pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpannosi, assamaṇosi’’…pe… āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

It is seen of him that he is committing an offense entailing expulsion. If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You have been heard'… If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You are suspected'… If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You have been heard, you are suspected; you have committed an offense entailing expulsion; you are no ascetic'… there is a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

Sutassa hoti – ‘‘pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpanno’’ti…pe… parisaṅkitassa hoti – ‘‘pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpanno’’ti. Tañce ‘‘diṭṭhosi’’…pe… tañce codāpeti – ‘‘sutosi’’…pe… tañce codāpeti – ‘‘diṭṭhosi, sutosi, pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpannosi, assamaṇosi’’…pe… āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

It is heard of him: 'He has committed an offense entailing expulsion.'… It is suspected of him: 'He has committed an offense entailing expulsion.' If he accuses him, saying, 'You have been seen'… If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You have been heard'… If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You have been seen, you have been heard; you have committed an offense entailing expulsion; you are no ascetic'… there is a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

Diṭṭhassa hoti pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpajjanto. Diṭṭhe vematiko diṭṭhaṃ no kappeti diṭṭhaṃ nassarati diṭṭhaṃ pamuṭṭho hoti…pe… sute vematiko sutaṃ no kappeti sutaṃ nassarati sutaṃ pamuṭṭho hoti…pe… parisaṅkite vematiko parisaṅkitaṃ no kappeti parisaṅkitaṃ nassarati parisaṅkitaṃ pamuṭṭho hoti. Tañce codāpeti – ‘‘parisaṅkitosi, diṭṭhosi’’…pe… parisaṅkitaṃ pamuṭṭho hoti, tañce codāpeti – ‘‘parisaṅkitosi sutosi’’…pe… parisaṅkitaṃ pamuṭṭho hoti, tañce codāpeti – ‘‘parisaṅkitosi, diṭṭhosi, sutosi, pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpannosi, assamaṇosi, asakyaputtiyosi, natthi tayā saddhiṃ uposatho vā pavāraṇā vā saṅghakammaṃ vā’’ti, āpatti vācāya, vācāya saṅghādisesassa.

It is seen of him that he is committing an offense entailing expulsion. He is doubtful about what was seen, does not trust what was seen, forgets what was seen, is completely forgetful of what was seen… He is doubtful about what was heard, does not trust what was heard, forgets what was heard, is completely forgetful of what was heard… He is doubtful about what was suspected, does not trust what was suspected, forgets what was suspected, is completely forgetful of what was suspected. If he makes him accuse, saying, 'You are suspected, you have been seen'… 'You are suspected, you have been heard'… 'You are suspected, you have been seen, you have been heard; you have committed an offense entailing expulsion; you are no ascetic, you are no son of the Sakyan; there is no Uposatha or Pavāraṇā or Saṅgha-kamma with you,' there is a Saṅghādisesa offense by speech.

389. Asuddhe suddhadiṭṭhi, suddhe asuddhadiṭṭhi, asuddhe asuddhadiṭṭhi, suddhe suddhadiṭṭhi.

389.One with a pure view regarding an impure person, one with an impure view regarding a pure person, one with an impure view regarding an impure person, one with a pure view regarding a pure person.

Asuddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpanno. Tañce suddhadiṭṭhi samāno anokāsaṃ kārāpetvā cāvanādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti saṅghādisesena dukkaṭassa.

A person is impure, having committed one of the pārājika offenses. If he, being of pure view, speaks with the intention of making him leave without giving an opportunity, there is a dukkaṭa offense in connection with a Saṅghādisesa.

Asuddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpanno. Tañce suddhadiṭṭhi samāno okāsaṃ kārāpetvā cāvanādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

A person is impure, having committed one of the pārājika offenses. If he, being of pure view, having given an opportunity, speaks with the intention of making him leave, there is a Saṅghādisesa offense.

Asuddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpanno. Tañce suddhadiṭṭhi samāno anokāsaṃ kārāpetvā akkosādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti omasavādena dukkaṭassa.

A person is impure, having committed one of the pārājika offenses. If he, being of pure view, speaks with the intention of insulting without giving an opportunity, there is a dukkaṭa offense for abusive speech.

Asuddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpanno. Tañce suddhadiṭṭhi samāno okāsaṃ kārāpetvā akkosādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti omasavādassa.

A person is impure, having committed one of the pārājika offenses. If he, being of pure view, having given an opportunity, speaks with the intention of insulting, there is a Pācittiya offense for abusive speech.

Suddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ anajjhāpanno. Tañce asuddhadiṭṭhi samāno anokāsaṃ kārāpetvā cāvanādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

A person is pure, not having committed any pārājika offense. If he, being of impure view, speaks with the intention of making him leave without giving an opportunity, there is a dukkaṭa offense.

Suddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ anajjhāpanno. Tañce asuddhadiṭṭhi samāno okāsaṃ kārāpetvā cāvanādhippāyo vadeti, anāpatti.

A person is pure, not having committed any pārājika offense. If he, being of impure view, having given an opportunity, speaks with the intention of making him leave, there is no offense.

Suddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ anajjhāpanno. Tañce asuddhadiṭṭhi samāno anokāsaṃ kārāpetvā akkosādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti omasavādena dukkaṭassa.

An impure person has committed one of the pārājika offenses. If, holding an impure view, without creating an opportunity, one speaks with the intention of insulting, there is a dukkaṭa for insulting speech.

Asuddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ ajjhāpanno. Tañce asuddhadiṭṭhi samāno okāsaṃ kārāpetvā akkosādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti omasavādassa.

An impure person has committed one of the pārājika offenses. If, holding an impure view, having created an opportunity, one speaks with the intention of insulting, there is an offense of insulting speech.

Suddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ anajjhāpanno. Tañce suddhadiṭṭhi samāno anokāsaṃ kārāpetvā cāvanādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti saṅghādisesena dukkaṭassa.

A pure person has not committed any of the pārājika offenses. If, holding a pure view, without creating an opportunity, one speaks with the intention to cause disrobing, there is a dukkaṭa for a Saṅghādisesa offense.

Suddho hoti puggalo aññataraṃ pārājikaṃ dhammaṃ anajjhāpanno. Tañce suddhadiṭṭhi samāno okāsaṃ kārāpetvā cāvanādhippāyo vadeti, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

390. Anāpatti suddhe asuddhadiṭṭhissa, asuddhe asuddhadiṭṭhissa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

390.There is no offense for one who is pure but holds an impure view, for one who is impure and holds an impure view, for the insane, or for the first offender.

Duṭṭhadosasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The eighth training rule concerning malicious accusation is concluded.
