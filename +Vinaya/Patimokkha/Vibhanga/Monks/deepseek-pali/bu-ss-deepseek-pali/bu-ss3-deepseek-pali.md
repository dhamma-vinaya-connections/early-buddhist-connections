# BU-SN 3

## 3. Duṭṭhullavācāsikkhāpadaṃ

## 3.The Training Rule on Crude Speech.

283. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī araññe viharati. Tassāyasmato vihāro abhirūpo hoti dassanīyo pāsādiko. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā itthiyo ārāmaṃ āgamaṃsu vihārapekkhikāyo. Atha kho tā itthiyo yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘icchāma mayaṃ, bhante, ayyassa vihāraṃ pekkhitu’’nti. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī tā itthiyo vihāraṃ pekkhāpetvā tāsaṃ itthīnaṃ vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati yācatipi āyācatipi pucchatipi paṭipucchatipi ācikkhatipi anusāsatipi akkosatipi. Yā tā itthiyo chinnikā dhuttikā ahirikāyo tā āyasmatā udāyinā saddhiṃ uhasantipi ullapantipi ujjagghantipi uppaṇḍentipi. Yā pana tā itthiyo hirimanā tā nikkhamitvā bhikkhū ujjhāpenti – ‘‘idaṃ, bhante, nacchannaṃ nappatirūpaṃ. Sāmikenapi mayaṃ evaṃ vuttā na iccheyyāma, kiṃ panāyyena udāyinā’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsissatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā – ‘‘ananucchavikaṃ, moghapurisa, ananulomikaṃ appatirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. Kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsissasi! Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito no sarāgāya…pe… kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto. Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

283.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time Venerable Udāyī was dwelling in the forest. His dwelling was beautiful, attractive, and delightful. At that time, several women came to the monastery wishing to see the dwelling. Then those women approached Venerable Udāyī and said, “Venerable, we wish to see the venerable one’s dwelling.” Then Venerable Udāyī showed them the dwelling and, pointing out the genital passage and the urinary passage of those women, he spoke words of praise and blame, he begged and entreats, questioned and cross-questioned, explained and advised, and he reviled them. Those women who were loose, deceitful, and shameless smiled, engaged in enticing talk, laughed loudly, and teased Venerable Udāyī. But those women who were modest, having left, complained to the bhikkhus: “Venerables, this is not proper, not suitable. We would not want our own husbands to speak to us like this, let alone Venerable Udāyī!” Those bhikkhus who were of few desires…they complained, grumbled, and spread it about: “How could Venerable Udāyī address a woman with crude words?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Udāyī in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on account of this incident, on account of this case, having assembled the Saṅgha, questioned Venerable Udāyī: “Is it true, Udāyī, that you addressed a woman with crude words?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked him: “Senseless man, this is improper, unsuitable, unbecoming, unworthy of an ascetic, not allowable, not to be done. How could you, senseless man, address a woman with crude words! Have I not, senseless man, taught the Dhamma in many ways for dispassion, not for passion… for the stilling of the fever of sensual desire? This, senseless man, does not lead to faith in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:

284. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu otiṇṇo vipariṇatena cittena mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāseyya yathā taṃ yuvā yuvatiṃ methunupasaṃhitāhi, saṅghādiseso’’ti.

284.“If a bhikkhu, with lustful thoughts, addresses a woman with crude words such as a young man might speak to a young woman concerning sexual intercourse, it is an offense entailing suspension.”

285. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

285.“Whoever” means: whoever… “bhikkhu” means… here, “bhikkhu” is the intended meaning.

Otiṇṇo nāma sāratto apekkhavā paṭibaddhacitto.

“Infatuated” means one who is lustful, desirous, with a mind attached.

Vipariṇatanti rattampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ, duṭṭhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ mūḷhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ. Apica, rattaṃ cittaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetaṃ vipariṇatanti.

“Perverted” means: a passionate mind is perverted, a hateful mind is perverted, a deluded mind is perverted. However, in this context, a passionate mind is the intended meaning of “perverted.”

Mātugāmo nāma manussitthī, na yakkhī, na petī, na tiracchānagatā. Viññū paṭibalā subhāsitadubbhāsitaṃ duṭṭhullāduṭṭhullaṃ ājānituṃ.

“Woman” means a human female, not a female yakkha, not a female peta, not an animal female. She is intelligent and capable of understanding well-spoken and ill-spoken language, and crude and not crude language.

Duṭṭhullā nāma vācā vaccamaggapassāvamaggamethunadhammappaṭisaṃyuttā vācā.

“Crude speech” means speech connected with the genital passage, the urinary passage, or sexual intercourse.

Obhāseyyāti ajjhācāro vuccati.

“Addresses” means: transgression is what is meant.

Yathā taṃ yuvā yuvatinti daharo dahariṃ, taruṇo taruṇiṃ, kāmabhogī kāmabhoginiṃ.

“Such as a young man to a young woman” means: a young man to a young woman, a youth to a maiden, a sensualist to a sensualist woman.

Methunupasaṃhitāhīti methunadhammappaṭisaṃyuttāhi.

“Connected with sexual intercourse” means associated with sexual intercourse.

Saṅghādisesoti …pe… tenapi vuccati saṅghādisesoti.

“Saṅghādisesa” means… thus it is called a saṅghādisesa.

Dve magge ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati, avaṇṇampi bhaṇati, yācatipi, āyācatipi, pucchatipi, paṭipucchatipi, ācikkhatipi, anusāsatipi, akkosatipi.

He indicates the two passages, speaks in praise, speaks in dispraise, begs, entreats, questions, cross-questions, explains, advises, or reviles.

Vaṇṇaṃ bhaṇati nāma dve magge thometi vaṇṇeti pasaṃsati.

“Speaks in praise” means he commends, extols, or praises the two passages.

Avaṇṇaṃ bhaṇati nāma dve magge khuṃseti vambheti garahati.

“Speaks in dispraise” means he insults, belittles, or blames the two passages.

Yācati nāma dehi me, arahasi me dātunti.

“Begs” means saying, “Give to me; you are able to give to me.”

Āyācati nāma kadā te mātā pasīdissati, kadā te pitā pasīdissati, kadā te devatāyo pasīdissanti, kadā [kadā te (syā.)] sukhaṇo sulayo sumuhutto bhavissati, kadā te methunaṃ dhammaṃ labhissāmīti.

“Entreats” means he says, “When will your mother be pleased? When will your father be pleased? When will the deities be pleased? When will the auspicious occasion, the favorable conjunction, the auspicious moment come? When will I obtain sexual relations?”

Pucchati nāma kathaṃ tvaṃ sāmikassa desi, kathaṃ jārassa desīti?

“Questions” means he asks, “How do you give to your husband? How do you give to your lover?”

Paṭipucchati nāma evaṃ kira tvaṃ sāmikassa desi, evaṃ jārassa desīti.

“Cross-questions” means he asks, “Is it true that you give in this way to your husband, and in this way to your lover?”

Ācikkhati nāma puṭṭho bhaṇati – ‘‘evaṃ dehi. Evaṃ dentā sāmikassa piyā bhavissasi manāpā cā’’ti.

“Explains” means, when asked, he says, “Give in this way. By giving in this way, you will become dear and pleasing to your husband.”

Anusāsati nāma apuṭṭho bhaṇati – ‘‘evaṃ dehi. Evaṃ dentā sāmikassa piyā bhavissati manāpā cā’’ti.

“Advises” means, speaking unasked, he says, “Give in this way. By giving in this way, you will be dear and pleasing to your husband.”

Akkosati nāma animittāsi, nimittamattāsi, alohitāsi, dhuvalohitāsi, dhuvacoḷāsi, paggharantīsi, sikharaṇīsi, itthipaṇḍakāsi, vepurisikāsi, sambhinnāsi, ubhatobyañjanāsīti.

“Reviles” means he says: “You are without a characteristic sign, you possess merely the appearance of a sign, you are bloodless, you are constantly bloody, you are constantly wearing a loincloth, you are dripping, you have a prominent vulva, you are a female neuter, you are a masculine woman, you have a split genital and urinary passage, you are of double gender.”

286. Itthī ca hoti itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati yācatipi āyācatipi pucchatipi paṭipucchatipi ācikkhatipi anusāsatipi akkosatipi, āpatti saṅghādisesassa…pe….

286.There is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he is lustful. And a bhikkhu, referring to the genital passage or the urinary passage of that woman, speaks in praise, speaks in dispraise, begs, entreats, questions, cross-questions, explains, advises, or reviles—he commits an offense entailing suspension…

Dve itthiyo dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇaṃpi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dvinnaṃ saṅghādisesānaṃ…pe….

There are two women, he perceives them as women, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, referring to the genital passage or the urinary passage of those two women, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits two offenses entailing suspension…

Itthī ca paṇḍako ca ubhinnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ ubhinnaṃ vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti saṅghādisesena dukkaṭassa…pe….

There is a woman and a eunuch, he perceives both as women, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, referring to the genital passage or the urinary passage of both of them, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits an offense entailing suspension and an offense of wrong conduct…

Itthī ca hoti itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ṭhapetvā adhakkhakaṃ ubbhajāṇumaṇḍalaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti thullaccayassa…pe….

There is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, having excluded the genital passage and the urinary passage, speaks to her in praise or dispraise of the area from below the armpits to above the knees… or reviles—he commits a grave offense…

Dve itthiyo dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ṭhapetvā adhakkhakaṃ ubbhajāṇumaṇḍalaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dvinnaṃ thullaccayānaṃ…pe….

There are two women, he perceives them as women, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, having excluded the genital passage and the urinary passage, speaks in praise or dispraise of the area from below the armpits to above the knees of the two women… or reviles—he commits two grave offenses…

Itthī ca paṇḍako ca ubhinnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ ubhinnaṃ vaccamaggaṃ passāvamaggaṃ ṭhapetvā adhakkhakaṃ ubbhajāṇumaṇḍalaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti thullaccayena dukkaṭassa…pe….

There is a woman and a eunuch, he perceives both as women, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, having excluded the genital passage and the urinary passage, referring to the area from below the armpits to above the knees of both of them, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits an offense entailing a grave offense and an offense of wrong conduct…

Itthī ca hoti itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā ubbhakkhakaṃ adhojāṇumaṇḍalaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dukkaṭassa…pe….

There is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, referring to the area above the armpits or below the knees of that woman, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits an offense of wrong conduct…

Dve itthiyo dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ ubbhakkhakaṃ adhojāṇumaṇḍalaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ…pe….

There are two women, he perceives them as women, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, referring to the area above the armpits or below the knees of those two women, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits two offenses of wrong conduct…

Itthī ca paṇḍako ca ubhinnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ ubhinnaṃ ubbhakkhakaṃ adhojāṇumaṇḍalaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ…pe….

There is a woman and a eunuch, he perceives both as women, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, referring to the area above the armpits or below the knees of both of them, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits two offenses of wrong conduct…

Itthī ca hoti itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā kāyapaṭibaddhaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dukkaṭassa…pe….

There is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, referring to something connected to that woman’s body, such as clothing, flowers, or ornaments, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits an offense of wrong conduct…

Dve itthiyo dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ kāyapaṭibaddhaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ…pe….

There are two women, he perceives them as women, and he is lustful. A bhikkhu, referring to something connected to the bodies of those two women, such as clothing, flowers, or ornaments, speaks in praise or dispraise… or reviles—he commits two offenses of wrong conduct…

Itthī ca paṇḍako ca ubhinnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ ubhinnaṃ kāyapaṭibaddhaṃ ādissa vaṇṇampi bhaṇati avaṇṇampi bhaṇati…pe… akkosatipi, āpatti dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ…pe….

A woman and a eunuch, both perceived as women, and with lust. If a bhikkhu, referring to something connected to their bodies, speaks praise or blame... or insults them, he incurs two offenses of wrong-doing...

287. Anāpatti atthapurekkhārassa, dhammapurekkhārassa, anusāsanipurekkhārassa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

287.There is no offense for one who has the purpose of explaining the meaning, for one who has the purpose of the Dhamma, for one who has the purpose of instruction; for one who is insane; or for the first offender.

Vinītavatthuuddānagāthā

Summary Verses on the Cases of Discipline

Lohitaṃ kakkasākiṇṇaṃ, kharaṃ dīghañca vāpitaṃ;Kacci saṃsīdati maggo, saddhā dānena kammunāti.

Blood, coarse-matted, rough, long, and sown; 'Does the path sink?' Faith, generosity, and deeds.

Vinītavatthu

Cases of Discipline

288. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī navarattaṃ kambalaṃ pārutā hoti. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘lohitaṃ kho te, bhaginī’’ti. Sā na paṭivijāni. ‘‘Āmāyya, navaratto kambalo’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi ‘‘bhagavatā sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ, kacci nu kho ahaṃ saṅghādisesaṃ āpattiṃ āpanno’’ti? Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

288.On one occasion, a certain woman was wrapped in a new red blanket. A lustful bhikkhu said to that woman, "You are red, sister." She did not understand. "Yes, venerable sir, it is a new red blanket." He felt remorse: "The Blessed One has laid down a training rule—could I have incurred a saṅghādisesa offense?" He reported the matter to the Blessed One. "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred an offense of wrong-doing."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kharakambalaṃ pārutā hoti. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kakkasalomaṃ kho te, bhaginī’’ti. Sā na paṭivijāni. ‘‘Āmāyya, kharakambalako’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

On another occasion, a certain woman was wrapped in a rough blanket. A lustful bhikkhu said to that woman, "Your hair is coarse, sister." She did not understand. "Yes, venerable sir, it is a rough blanket." He felt remorse... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred an offense of wrong-doing."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī navāvutaṃ kambalaṃ pārutā hoti. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ākiṇṇalomaṃ kho te, bhaginī’’ti. Sā na paṭivijāni. ‘‘Āmāyya, navāvuto kambalo’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain woman was wearing a newly woven blanket. A lustful bhikkhu said to that woman, "Your hair is matted, sister." She did not understand. "Yes, venerable sir, it is a newly woven blanket." He felt remorse... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred an offense of wrong-doing."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kharakambalaṃ pārutā hoti. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kharalomaṃ kho te, bhaginī’’ti. Sā na paṭivijāni. ‘‘Āmāyya, kharakambalako’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain woman was wearing a rough blanket. A lustful bhikkhu said to that woman, "Your hair is rough, sister." She did not understand. "Yes, venerable sir, it is a rough blanket." He felt remorse... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred an offense of wrong-doing."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī pāvāraṃ [dīghapāvāraṃ (syā.)] pārutā hoti. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘dīghalomaṃ kho te, bhaginī’’ti. Sā na paṭivijāni. ‘‘Āmāyya, pāvāro’’ [dīghapāvāro (syā.)] ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain woman was wearing a cloak. A lustful bhikkhu said to that woman, "Your hair is long, sister." She did not understand. "Yes, venerable sir, it is a cloak." He felt remorse... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred an offense of wrong-doing."

289. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī khettaṃ vapāpetvā āgacchati. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘vāpitaṃ kho te, bhaginī’’ti? Sā na paṭivijāni. ‘‘Āmāyya, no ca kho paṭivutta’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

289.At that time, a certain woman, having had a field sown, was returning. A lustful bhikkhu said to that woman, "Yours is sown, sister." She did not understand. "Yes, venerable sir, but it has not yet sprouted." He felt remorse... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred an offense of wrong-doing."

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu paribbājikaṃ paṭipathe passitvā sāratto taṃ paribbājikaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, bhagini, maggo saṃsīdatī’’ti? Sā na paṭivijāni. ‘‘Āma bhikkhu, paṭipajjissasī’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti thullaccayassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu saw a female wanderer on the road and, being lustful, said to that female wanderer, "Sister, is the path yielding?" She did not understand. "Yes, bhikkhu, you may proceed." He felt remorse... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred a grave offense."

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu sāratto aññataraṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saddhāsi tvaṃ, bhagini. Apica, yaṃ sāmikassa desi taṃ nāmhākaṃ desī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante’’ti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

At that time, a lustful bhikkhu said to a certain woman: "Sister, you are faithful. Moreover, what you give to your husband, give that to us also." "What, venerable sir?" "The sexual act." He felt remorse... "bhikkhu, you have incurred a saṅghādisesa offense."

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu sāratto aññataraṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saddhāsi tvaṃ, bhagini. Apica, yaṃ aggadānaṃ taṃ nāmhākaṃ desī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante, aggadāna’’nti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

At that time, a lustful bhikkhu said to a certain woman: "Sister, you are faithful. Moreover, what is the foremost gift, that you should give to us." "What is the foremost gift, venerable sir?" "Sexual intercourse." He felt remorse... "bhikkhu, you have incurred a saṅghādisesa offense."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kammaṃ karoti. Aññataro bhikkhu sāratto taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tiṭṭha, bhagini, ahaṃ karissāmīti…pe… nisīda, bhagini, ahaṃ karissāmīti…pe… nipajja, bhagini, ahaṃ karissāmī’’ti. Sā na paṭivijāni. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain woman was working. A lustful bhikkhu said to that woman, "Stand, sister, I will do it... Sit, sister, I will do it... Lie down, sister, I will do it." She did not understand. He felt remorse... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; you have incurred an offense of wrong-doing."

Duṭṭhullavācāsikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The third training rule on lewd speech is concluded.
