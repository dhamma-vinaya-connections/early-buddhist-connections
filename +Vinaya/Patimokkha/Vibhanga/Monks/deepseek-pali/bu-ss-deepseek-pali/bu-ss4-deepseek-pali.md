# BU-SN 4

## 4. Attakāmapāricariyasikkhāpadaṃ

## 4.The training rule concerning ministration for one’s own sexual gratification.

290. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī sāvatthiyaṃ kulūpako hoti, bahukāni kulāni upasaṅkamati. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī matapatikā abhirūpā hoti dassanīyā pāsādikā. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena tassā itthiyā nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho sā itthī yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho taṃ itthiṃ āyasmā udāyī dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho sā itthī āyasmatā udāyinā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘vadeyyātha, bhante, yena attho. Paṭibalā mayaṃ ayyassa dātuṃ yadidaṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāra’’nti.

290.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time, Venerable Udāyī was a frequenter of families in Sāvatthī, visiting many households. At that time, a certain woman, whose husband had died, was beautiful, lovely, and pleasing. Then, Venerable Udāyī, having dressed in the morning and taken his bowl and robe, went to the residence of that woman. Having approached, he sat down on the seat prepared. Then that woman approached Venerable Udāyī, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, Venerable Udāyī instructed, aroused, and gladdened her with a Dhamma talk. Then, having been instructed, aroused, and gladdened by Venerable Udāyī’s Dhamma talk, that woman said to him: “Speak, venerable sir, as to what is needed. We are able to give the venerable one robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites for the sick.”

‘‘Na kho te, bhagini, amhākaṃ dullabhā yadidaṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārā. Apica, yo amhākaṃ dullabho taṃ dehī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante’’ti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. ‘‘Attho, bhante’’ti? ‘‘Attho, bhaginī’’ti. ‘‘Ehi, bhante’’ti, ovarakaṃ pavisitvā sāṭakaṃ nikkhipitvā mañcake uttānā nipajji. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī yena sā itthī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā – ‘‘ko imaṃ vasalaṃ duggandhaṃ āmasissatī’’ti, niṭṭhuhitvā pakkāmi. Atha kho sā itthī ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘alajjino ime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā dussīlā musāvādino. Ime hi nāma dhammacārino samacārino brahmacārino saccavādino sīlavanto kalyāṇadhammā paṭijānissanti! Natthi imesaṃ sāmaññaṃ natthi imesaṃ brahmaññaṃ, naṭṭhaṃ imesaṃ sāmaññaṃ naṭṭhaṃ imesaṃ brahmaññaṃ, kuto imesaṃ sāmaññaṃ kuto imesaṃ brahmaññaṃ, apagatā ime sāmaññā apagatā ime brahmaññā. Kathañhi nāma samaṇo udāyī maṃ sāmaṃ methunadhammaṃ yācitvā, ‘ko imaṃ vasalaṃ duggandhaṃ āmasissatī’’ti niṭṭhuhitvā pakkamissati! Kiṃ me pāpakaṃ kiṃ me duggandhaṃ, kassāhaṃ kena hāyāmī’’ti? Aññāpi itthiyo ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘alajjino ime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā dussīlā musāvādino…pe… kathañhi nāma samaṇo udāyī imissā sāmaṃ methunadhammaṃ yācitvā, ‘ko imaṃ vasalaṃ duggandhaṃ āmasissatī’ti niṭṭhuhitvā pakkamissati! Kiṃ imissā pāpakaṃ kiṃ imissā duggandhaṃ, kassāyaṃ kena hāyatī’’ti? Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tāsaṃ itthīnaṃ ujjhāyantīnaṃ khiyyantīnaṃ vipācentīnaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissatī’’ti!

“Sister, it is not difficult for us to obtain robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites for the sick. But what is difficult for us, that you may give.” “What is it, venerable sir?” “Sexual intercourse.” “Is it needed, venerable sir?” “It is needed, sister.” “Come then, venerable sir,” she said, entering a private room, removing her outer garment, and lying down on the bed on her back. Then Venerable Udāyī approached that woman. Having approached, he said, “Who would touch this foul-smelling outcast?” and, having spat, he left. Then that woman complained, criticized, and spread it about: “These shameless Sākyan ascetics are immoral and deceitful. They claim to be practitioners of the Dhamma, practitioners of proper conduct, practitioners of the holy life, speakers of truth, virtuous, and of good qualities! There is no asceticism in them, no holy life in them. They have lost their asceticism, they have lost their holy life. How could they have asceticism? How could they have holy life? They are devoid of asceticism, devoid of holy life. How could the ascetic Udāyī, after personally soliciting me for sexual intercourse, spit and say, ‘Who would touch this foul-smelling outcast?’ and then leave? What is bad about me? What is foul about me? In what way am I deficient, and for whom?” Other women also complained, criticized, and spread it about: “These shameless Sākyan ascetics are immoral and deceitful… How could the ascetic Udāyī, after personally soliciting this woman for sexual intercourse, spit and say, ‘Who would touch this foul-smelling outcast?’ and then leave? What is bad about her? What is foul about her? In what way is she deficient, and for whom?” The bhikkhus heard those women complaining, criticizing, and spreading it about. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, criticized, and spread it about: “How could Venerable Udāyī praise ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in the presence of a woman?”

Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā – ‘‘ananucchavikaṃ, moghapurisa, ananulomikaṃ appatirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. Kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissasi! Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito no sarāgāya…pe… kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto? Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Udāyī in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on that occasion and because of this matter, convened the Saṅgha of bhikkhus and questioned Venerable Udāyī: “Is it true, Udāyī, that you praised ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in the presence of a woman?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him: “It is unseemly, foolish man, unsuitable, unbecoming, unworthy of an ascetic, improper, and not to be done. How could you, foolish man, praise ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in the presence of a woman? Haven’t I, foolish man, taught the Dhamma in many ways for dispassion, not for passion… for the subsiding of the burning of sensual desires? This will not, foolish man, inspire confidence in the uninspired… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

291. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu otiṇṇo vipariṇatena cittena mātugāmassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāseyya – ‘etadaggaṃ, bhagini, pāricariyānaṃ yā mādisaṃ sīlavantaṃ kalyāṇadhammaṃ brahmacāriṃ etena dhammena paricareyyāti methunupasaṃhitena’, saṅghādiseso’’ti.

291.“If a bhikkhu, overcome by a changed mind, should praise ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in the presence of a woman, saying, ‘Sister, this is the foremost of ministrations, that one should serve one like me, who is virtuous, of good qualities, a practitioner of the holy life, with this practice connected with sexual intercourse,’ he commits an offense entailing suspension.”

292. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

292.“Whoever” (yo pana) means whoever of whatever kind… “bhikkhu” (bhikkhu) means… in this case, a bhikkhu is intended here.

Otiṇṇo nāma sāratto apekkhavā paṭibaddhacitto.

“Overcome” (otiṇṇo) means one who is lustful, desirous, with an attached mind.

Vipariṇatanti rattampi cittaṃ vipariṇattaṃ, duṭṭhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ, mūḷhampi cittaṃ vipariṇataṃ. Apica, rattaṃ cittaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetaṃ vipariṇatanti.

“Changed” (vipariṇataṃ) means a lustful mind is changed, a hateful mind is changed, a deluded mind is changed. Moreover, in this context, a lustful mind is meant by “changed.”

Mātugāmo nāma manussitthī, na yakkhī, na petī, na tiracchānagatā. Viññū paṭibalā subhāsitadubbhāsitaṃ duṭṭhallāduṭṭhullaṃ ājānituṃ.

“Woman” (mātugāmo) means a human female, not a yakkhinī, not a petī, not an animal female. She is intelligent and capable of distinguishing well-spoken and ill-spoken words, coarse and not-coarse language.

Mātugāmassa santiketi mātugāmassa sāmantā, mātugāmassa avidūre.

“In the presence of a woman” (mātugāmassa santike) means in the vicinity of a woman, nearby a woman, not far from a woman.

Attakāmanti attano kāmaṃ attano hetuṃ attano adhippāyaṃ attano pāricariyaṃ.

“One’s own desire” (attakāmaṃ) means one’s own desire, one’s own reason, one’s own intention, one’s own ministration.

Etadagganti etaṃ aggaṃ etaṃ seṭṭhaṃ etaṃ mokkhaṃ etaṃ uttamaṃ etaṃ pavaraṃ.

“This is the foremost” (etadaggaṃ) means this is the chief, this is the best, this is the liberation, this is the supreme, this is the excellent.

Yāti khattiyī [khattiyā (syā.)] vā brāhmaṇī vā vessī vā suddī vā.

“She” (yā) means a noblewoman, a brahmin woman, a merchant woman, or a worker woman.

Mādisanti khattiyaṃ vā brāhmaṇaṃ vā vessaṃ vā suddaṃ vā.

“One like me” (mādisaṃ) means a noble, a brahmin, a merchant, or a worker.

Sīlavantanti pāṇātipātā paṭivirataṃ, adinnādānā paṭivirataṃ, musāvādā paṭivirataṃ.

“Virtuous” (sīlavantaṃ) means abstaining from the destruction of life, abstaining from taking what is not given, abstaining from false speech.

Brahmacārinti methunadhammā paṭivirataṃ.

“A practitioner of the holy life” (brahmacāriṃ) means abstaining from sexual intercourse.

Kalyāṇadhammo nāma tena ca sīlena tena ca brahmacariyena kalyāṇadhammo hoti.

“One of good qualities” (kalyāṇadhammo) means that by that virtue and by that holy life, one has good qualities.

Etena dhammenāti methunadhammena.

“With this practice” (etena dhammena) means with sexual intercourse.

Paricareyyāti abhirameyya.

“Should serve” (paricareyya) means should delight in.

Methunupasaṃhitenāti methunadhammappaṭisaṃyuttena.

“Connected with sexual intercourse” (methunupasaṃhitena) means associated with sexual intercourse.

Saṅghādisesoti…pe… tenapi vuccati saṅghādisesoti.

“Offense entailing suspension” (saṅghādiseso) means… and so it is called an offense entailing suspension.

293. Itthī ca hoti itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti saṅghādisesassa.

293.If she is a woman, and he perceives her as a woman, and he is lustful, and a bhikkhu praises ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in her presence, he commits an offense entailing suspension.

Itthī ca hoti vematiko…pe… paṇḍakasaññī… purisasaññī… tiracchānagatasaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ itthiyā santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti thullaccayassa.

If she is a woman and he is uncertain… or perceives her as a eunuch… or perceives her as a man… or perceives her as an animal, and he is lustful, and a bhikkhu praises ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in her presence, he commits a grave offense.

Paṇḍako ca hoti paṇḍakasaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ paṇḍakassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti thullaccayassa.

If he is a eunuch, and he perceives him as a eunuch, and he is lustful, and a bhikkhu praises ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in his presence, he commits a grave offense.

Paṇḍako ca hoti vematiko…pe… purisasaññī… tiracchānagatasaññī… itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ paṇḍakassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he is a eunuch, and he is uncertain… or perceives him as a man… or perceives him as an animal… or perceives him as a woman, and he is lustful, and a bhikkhu praises ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in his presence, he commits an offense of wrong-doing.

Puriso ca hoti…pe… tiracchānagato ca hoti tiracchānagatasaññī…pe… vematiko itthisaññī… paṇḍakasaññī… purisasaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ tiracchānagatassa santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he is a man… or if he is an animal, and he perceives it as an animal… or he is uncertain, or perceives it as a woman… or perceives it as a eunuch… or perceives it as a man, and he is lustful, and a bhikkhu praises ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in the presence of that animal, he commits an offense of wrong-doing.

Dve itthiyo dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ dvinnaṃ itthīnaṃ santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti dvinnaṃ saṅghādisesānaṃ…pe….

If there are two women, and he perceives them as women, and he is lustful, and a bhikkhu praises ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in the presence of those two women, he commits two offenses entailing suspension… and so on.

Itthī ca paṇḍako ca ubhinnaṃ itthisaññī sāratto ca. Bhikkhu ca naṃ ubhinnaṃ santike attakāmapāricariyāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, āpatti saṅghādisesena dukkaṭassa…pe….

If there is a woman and a eunuch, and he perceives both as women, and he is lustful, and a bhikkhu praises ministration for one’s own sexual gratification in their presence, he commits an offense entailing suspension and an offense of wrong-doing… and so on.

294. Anāpatti ‘‘cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārena upaṭṭhahā’’ti bhaṇati, ummattakassa ādikammikassāti.

294.There is no offense for one who says, "Provide me with robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicine as requisites for the sick," if he is insane or the first offender.

Vinītavatthuuddānagāthā

The Summary Verses on the Matters of Discipline

Kathaṃ vañjhā labhe puttaṃ, piyā ca subhagā siyaṃ;Kiṃ dajjaṃ kenupaṭṭheyyaṃ, kathaṃ gaccheyyaṃ suggatinti.

How can a barren woman obtain a son, and become beloved and fortunate? What should I give, whom should I serve, how can I reach a happy destiny?

Vinītavatthu

Matters of Discipline

295. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā vañjhā itthī kulūpakaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kathāhaṃ, bhante, vijāyeyya’’nti? ‘‘Tena hi, bhagini, aggadānaṃ dehī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante, aggadāna’’nti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

295.Now on that occasion, a certain barren woman said to a bhikkhu who frequented her family: "How, venerable sir, could I conceive?" "Well then, sister, give the foremost gift." "What, venerable sir, is the foremost gift?" "Sexual intercourse." He became remorseful… "You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā vijāyinī itthī kulūpakaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kathāhaṃ, bhante, puttaṃ labheyya’’nti? ‘‘Tena hi, bhagini, aggadānaṃ dehī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante, aggadāna’’nti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

Now on that occasion, a certain woman who had given birth said to a bhikkhu who frequented her family: "How, venerable sir, might I obtain a son?" "Well then, sister, give the foremost gift." "What, venerable sir, is the foremost gift?" "Sexual intercourse." He became remorseful… "You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kulūpakaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kathāhaṃ, bhante, sāmikassa piyā assa’’nti? ‘‘Tena hi, bhagini, aggadānaṃ dehī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante, aggadāna’’nti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

Now on that occasion, a certain woman said to a bhikkhu who frequented her family: "How, venerable sir, might I be dear to my husband?" "Well then, sister, give the foremost gift." "What, venerable sir, is the foremost gift?" "Sexual intercourse." He became remorseful… "You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kulūpakaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kathāhaṃ, bhante, subhagā assa’’nti? ‘‘Tena hi, bhagini, aggadānaṃ dehī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante, aggadāna’’nti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

Now on that occasion, a certain woman said to a bhikkhu who frequented families: "How, venerable sir, can I become fortunate?" "Well then, sister, give the foremost gift." "What, venerable sir, is the foremost gift?" "Sexual intercourse." He became remorseful… "You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kulūpakaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kyāhaṃ, bhante, ayyassa dajjāmī’’ti? ‘‘Aggadānaṃ, bhaginī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante, aggadāna’’nti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

Now on that occasion, a certain woman said to a bhikkhu who frequented families: "What should I give you, venerable sir?" "The foremost gift, sister." "What, venerable sir, is the foremost gift?" "Sexual intercourse." He became remorseful… "You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Saṅgha."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī kulūpakaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kenāhaṃ, bhante, ayyaṃ upaṭṭhemī’’ti? ‘‘Aggadānena, bhaginī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ, bhante, aggadāna’’nti? ‘‘Methunadhamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno saṅghādisesa’’nti.

The fourth training rule, on attending to one's own desires, is concluded.
