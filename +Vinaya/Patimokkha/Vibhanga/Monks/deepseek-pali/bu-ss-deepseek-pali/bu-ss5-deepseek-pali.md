# BU-SN 5

## 5. Sañcarittasikkhāpadaṃ

## 5.The training rule on acting as a go-between.

296. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī sāvatthiyaṃ kulūpako hoti. Bahukāni kulāni upasaṅkamati. Yattha passati kumārakaṃ vā apajāpatikaṃ, kumārikaṃ vā apatikaṃ, kumārakassa mātāpitūnaṃ santike kumārikāya vaṇṇaṃ bhaṇati – ‘‘amukassa kulassa kumārikā abhirūpā dassanīyā pāsādikā paṇḍitā byattā medhāvinī dakkhā analasā. Channā sā kumārikā imassa kumārakassā’’ti. Te evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘ete kho, bhante, amhe na jānanti – ‘ke vā ime kassa vā’ti. Sace, bhante, ayyo dāpeyya āneyyāma mayaṃ taṃ kumārikaṃ imassa kumārakassā’’ti. Kumārikāya mātāpitūnaṃ santike kumārakassa vaṇṇaṃ bhaṇati – ‘‘amukassa kulassa kumārako abhirūpo dassanīyo pāsādiko paṇḍito byatto medhāvī dakkho analaso. Channāyaṃ kumārikā tassa kumārakassā’’ti [channo so kumārako imissā kumārikāyāti (syā.)]. Te evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘ete kho, bhante, amhe na jānanti – ‘ke vā ime kassa vā’ti, kismiṃ viya kumārikāya vattuṃ. Sace, bhante, ayyo yācāpeyya dajjeyyāma mayaṃ imaṃ kumārikaṃ tassa kumārakassā’’ti. Eteneva upāyena āvāhānipi kārāpeti, vivāhānipi kārāpeti, vāreyyānipi kārāpeti.

296.Now on that occasion, the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now on that occasion, the Venerable Udāyī was a family-frequenter in Sāvatthī. He frequented many families. Wherever he saw a young man without a wife or a young woman without a husband, he would praise the girl in the presence of the boy’s parents, saying: "The girl from such-and-such family is beautiful, lovely, pleasing, wise, clever, intelligent, capable, and diligent. That girl is suitable for this young man." They would say to him: "Venerable sir, we do not know these people—who they are or whose they are. If, venerable sir, you would arrange for her to be given, we would bring that girl for this young man." Then he would praise the boy in the presence of the girl’s parents, saying: "The boy from such-and-such family is handsome, lovely, pleasing, wise, clever, intelligent, capable, and diligent. This girl is suitable for that young man." They would say to him: "Venerable sir, we do not know these people—who they are or whose they are; how could we speak for the girl? If, venerable sir, you would arrange for her to be asked for, we would give this girl to that young man." In this way, he arranged marriages, weddings, and choices of a spouse.

297. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarissā purāṇagaṇakiyā dhītā abhirūpā hoti dassanīyā pāsādikā. Tirogāmakā ājīvakasāvakā āgantvā taṃ gaṇakiṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘dehāyye, imaṃ kumārikaṃ amhākaṃ kumārakassā’’ti. Sā evamāha – ‘‘ahaṃ khvayyo [khvayyā (syā.), khvāyyo (ka.)] tumhe na jānāmi – ‘ke vā ime kassa vā’ti. Ayañca me ekadhītikā, tirogāmo ca gantabbo, nāhaṃ dassāmī’’ti. Manussā te ājīvakasāvake etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kissa tumhe, ayyo, āgatatthā’’ti? ‘‘Idha mayaṃ, ayyo, amukaṃ nāma gaṇakiṃ dhītaraṃ yācimhā amhākaṃ kumārakassa. Sā evamāha – ‘ahaṃ, khvayyo tumhe na jānāmi – ke vā ime kassa vā’ti. Ayañca me ekadhītikā, tirogāmo ca gantabbo, nāhaṃ dassāmī’’ti. ‘‘Kissa tumhe, ayyo, taṃ gaṇakiṃ dhītaraṃ yācittha? Nanu ayyo udāyī vattabbo. Ayyo udāyī dāpessatī’’ti.

297.Now on that occasion, the daughter of a certain old accountant's wife was beautiful, attractive, and pleasing. Some disciples of the Ājīvakas from another village came and said to that accountant's wife: "Give us, madam, this girl for our boy." She replied: "Sirs, I do not know you—who these people are or whose they are. And this is my only daughter, and she has to go to another village. I will not give her." People said to those disciples of the Ājīvakas: "Why have you come, sirs?" "Here, sirs, we asked such-and-such an accountant's wife for her daughter for our boy. She replied: 'Sirs, I do not know you—who these people are or whose they are. And this is my only daughter, and she has to go to another village. I will not give her.'" "Why, sirs, did you ask that accountant's wife for her daughter? Shouldn't the Venerable Udāyī be spoken to? The Venerable Udāyī will arrange for her to be given."

Atha kho te ājīvakasāvakā yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idha mayaṃ, bhante, amukaṃ nāma gaṇakiṃ dhītaraṃ yācimhā amhākaṃ kumārakassa. Sā evamāha – ‘ahaṃ khvayyo tumhe na jānāmi – ke vā ime kassa vāti. Ayañca me ekadhītikā, tirogāmo ca gantabbo, nāhaṃ dassāmī’ti. Sādhu, bhante, ayyo taṃ gaṇakiṃ dhītaraṃ dāpetu amhākaṃ kumārakassā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī yena sā gaṇakī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ gaṇakiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kissimesaṃ dhītaraṃ na desī’’ti? ‘‘Ahaṃ khvayya, ime na jānāmi – ‘ke vā ime kassa vā’ti. Ayañca me ekadhītikā, tirogāmo ca gantabbo, nāhaṃ dassāmī’’ti. ‘‘Dehimesaṃ. Ahaṃ ime jānāmī’’ti. ‘‘Sace, bhante, ayyo jānāti, dassāmī’’ti. Atha kho sā gaṇakī tesaṃ ājīvakasāvakānaṃ dhītaraṃ adāsi. Atha kho te ājīvakasāvakā taṃ kumārikaṃ netvā māsaṃyeva suṇisabhogena bhuñjiṃsu. Tato aparena dāsibhogena bhuñjanti.

Then those disciples of the Ājīvakas approached the Venerable Udāyī. Having approached, they said to the Venerable Udāyī: "Here, venerable sir, we asked the daughter of a certain accountant's wife, so-and-so by name, for our boy. She said: 'I do not know you—who are these people, or whose are they? And this is my only daughter, and she has to go to another village. I will not give her.' It would be good, venerable sir, if the venerable one would arrange for that accountant's wife to give her daughter to our boy." Then the Venerable Udāyī approached that accountant's wife. Having approached, he said to that accountant's wife: "Why do you not give your daughter to these people?" "Venerable sir, I do not know them—who these people are or whose they are? And this is my only daughter, and she has to go to another village. I will not give her." "Give her to these people. I know them." "If, venerable sir, you know them, I will give her." Then that accountant's wife gave her daughter to those disciples of the Ājīvakas. Then those disciples of the Ājīvakas took that girl and enjoyed her for only a month with the enjoyment of a daughter-in-law. After that, they enjoyed her with the enjoyment of a slave-girl.

Atha kho sā kumārikā mātuyā santike dūtaṃ pāhesi – ‘‘ahamhi duggatā dukkhitā, na sukhaṃ labhāmi. Māsaṃyeva maṃ suṇisabhogena bhuñjiṃsu. Tato aparena dāsibhogena bhuñjanti. Āgacchatu me mātā, maṃ nessatū’’ti. Atha kho sā gaṇakī yena te ājīvakasāvakā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te ājīvakasāvake etadavoca – ‘‘māyyo, imaṃ kumārikaṃ dāsibhogena bhuñjittha. Suṇisabhogena imaṃ kumārikaṃ bhuñjathā’’ti. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘natthamhākaṃ tayā saddhiṃ āhārūpahāro, samaṇena saddhiṃ amhākaṃ āhārūpahāro. Gaccha tvaṃ. Na mayaṃ taṃ jānāmā’’ti. Atha kho sā gaṇakī tehi ājīvakasāvakehi apasāditā punadeva sāvatthiṃ paccāgañchi. Dutiyampi kho sā kumārikā mātuyā santike dūtaṃ pāhesi – ‘‘ahamhi duggatā dukkhitā, na sukhaṃ labhāmi. Māsaṃyeva maṃ suṇisabhogena bhuñjiṃsu. Tato aparena dāsibhogena bhuñjanti. Āgacchatu me mātā, maṃ nessatū’’ti. Atha kho sā gaṇakī yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sā kira, bhante, kumārikā duggatā dukkhitā, na sukhaṃ labhati. Māsaṃyeva naṃ suṇisabhogena bhuñjiṃsu. Tato aparena dāsibhogena bhuñjanti. Vadeyyātha, bhante – ‘māyyo, imaṃ kumārikaṃ dāsibhogena bhuñjittha. Suṇisabhogena imaṃ kumārikaṃ bhuñjithā’’’ti.

Then the young girl sent a message to her mother: "I am miserable and suffering, I find no happiness. For a month they enjoyed me with the enjoyment of a daughter-in-law. Then afterwards they enjoy me with the enjoyment of a slave-girl. Let my mother come and take me away." Then that accountant's wife approached those disciples of the Ājīvakas. Having approached, she said to those disciples of the Ājīvakas: "Sirs, do not enjoy this girl with the enjoyment of a slave-girl. Enjoy this girl with the enjoyment of a daughter-in-law." They replied: "We have no dealings with you regarding food and gifts; our dealings regarding food and gifts are with the ascetic. Go away. We do not know you." Then, being rejected by those disciples of the Ājīvakas, that accountant's wife returned to Sāvatthī again. A second time, the young girl sent a message to her mother: "I am miserable and suffering, I find no happiness. For a month they enjoyed me with the enjoyment of a daughter-in-law. Then afterwards they enjoy me with the enjoyment of a slave-girl. Let my mother come and take me away." Then that accountant's wife approached the Venerable Udāyī. Having approached, she said to the Venerable Udāyī: "Venerable sir, that young girl is miserable and suffering, she finds no happiness. For a month they enjoyed her with the enjoyment of a daughter-in-law. Then afterwards they enjoy her with the enjoyment of a slave-girl. Venerable sir, you should say: 'Sirs, do not enjoy this girl with the enjoyment of a slave-girl. Enjoy this girl with the enjoyment of a daughter-in-law.'"

Atha kho āyasmā udāyī yena te ājīvakasāvakā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te ājīvakasāvake etadavoca – ‘‘māyyo, imaṃ kumārikaṃ dāsibhogena bhujjittha. Suṇisabhogena imaṃ kumārikaṃ bhuñjathā’’ti. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘natthamhākaṃ tayā saddhiṃ āhārūpahāro, gaṇakiyā saddhiṃ amhākaṃ āhārūpahāro. Samaṇena bhavitabbaṃ abyāvaṭena. Samaṇo assa susamaṇo, gaccha tvaṃ, na mayaṃ taṃ jānāmā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī tehi ājīvakasāvakehi apasādito punadeva sāvatthiṃ paccāgañchi. Tatiyampi kho sā kumārikā mātuyā santike dūtaṃ pāhesi – ‘‘ahamhi duggatā dukkhitā, na sukhaṃ labhāmi. Māsaṃyeva maṃ suṇisabhogena bhuñjiṃsu. Tato aparena dāsibhogena bhuñjanti. Āgacchatu me mātā, maṃ nessatū’’ti. Dutiyampi kho sā gaṇakī yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sā kira, bhante, kumārikā duggatā dukkhitā, na sukhaṃ labhati. Māsaṃyeva naṃ suṇisabhogena bhuñjiṃsu. Tato aparena dāsibhogena bhuñjanti. Vadeyyātha, bhante – ‘māyyo, imaṃ kumārikaṃ dāsibhogena bhuñjittha, suṇisabhogena imaṃ kumārikaṃ bhuñjathā’’’ti. ‘‘Paṭhamaṃpāhaṃ tehi ājīvakasāvakehi apasādito. Gaccha tvaṃ. Nāhaṃ gamissāmī’’ti.

Then Venerable Udāyī approached those Ājīvaka disciples and said to them, “Sirs, do not treat this girl as a slave. Treat this girl as a daughter-in-law.” They replied, “We have no dealings with you. We have dealings with the accountant. A bhikkhu should be uninvolved. A bhikkhu should be a proper bhikkhu. Go away, we do not know you.” Then Venerable Udāyī, having been rejected by those Ājīvaka disciples, returned once more to Sāvatthī. For the third time, that girl sent a messenger to her mother: “I am destitute and suffering, I find no happiness. For a month they treated me as a daughter-in-law. After that, they treat me as a slave. Let my mother come and take me away.” A second time, that female accountant approached Venerable Udāyī and said to him, “Sir, that girl is destitute and suffering, she finds no happiness. For a month they treated her as a daughter-in-law. After that, they treat her as a slave. Please, sir, tell them, ‘Sirs, do not treat this girl as a slave. Treat this girl as a daughter-in-law.’” [Udāyī replied:] “I was already rejected by those Ājīvaka disciples the first time. You go. I will not go.”

298. Atha kho sā gaṇakī ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘evaṃ duggato hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ dukkhito hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ mā sukhaṃ labhatu ayyo udāyī, yathā me kumārikā duggatā dukkhitā na sukhaṃ labhati pāpikāya sassuyā pāpakena sasurena pāpakena sāmikenā’’ti. Sāpi kho kumārikā ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘evaṃ duggato hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ dukkhito hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ mā sukhaṃ labhatu ayyo udāyī, yathāhaṃ duggatā dukkhitā na sukhaṃ labhāmi pāpikāya sassuyā pāpakena sasurena pāpakena sāmikenā’’ti. Aññāpi itthiyo asantuṭṭhā sassūhi vā sasurehi vā sāmikehi vā, tā evaṃ oyācanti – ‘‘evaṃ duggato hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ dukkhito hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ mā sukhaṃ labhatu ayyo udāyī, yathā mayaṃ duggatā dukkhitā na sukhaṃ labhāma pāpikāhi sassūhi pāpakehi sasurehi pāpakehi sāmikehī’’ti. Yā pana tā itthiyo santuṭṭhā sassūhi vā sasurehi vā sāmikehi vā tā evaṃ āyācanti – ‘‘evaṃ sukhito hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ sajjito hotu ayyo udāyī, evaṃ sukhamedho [sukhamedhito (sī. ka.)] hotu ayyo udāyī, yathā mayaṃ sukhitā sajjitā sukhamedhā bhaddikāhi sassūhi bhaddakehi sasurehi bhaddakehi sāmikehī’’ti.

298.Then that female accountant complained, grumbled, and criticized: “May Venerable Udāyī be as destitute as this, may Venerable Udāyī be as suffering as this, may Venerable Udāyī not find happiness, just as my daughter is destitute, suffering, and finds no happiness because of her wicked mother-in-law, wicked father-in-law, and wicked husband!” And that young girl also complained, grumbled, and criticized: “May Venerable Udāyī be as destitute as this, may Venerable Udāyī be as suffering as this, may Venerable Udāyī not find happiness, just as I am destitute, suffering, and find no happiness because of my wicked mother-in-law, wicked father-in-law, and wicked husband!” Other women too, dissatisfied with their mothers-in-law, fathers-in-law, or husbands, complained in the same way: “May Venerable Udāyī be as destitute as this, may Venerable Udāyī be as suffering as this, may Venerable Udāyī not find happiness, just as we are destitute, suffering, and find no happiness because of our wicked mothers-in-law, wicked fathers-in-law, and wicked husbands!” But those women who were satisfied with their mothers-in-law, fathers-in-law, or husbands prayed thus: “May Venerable Udāyī be as happy as this, may Venerable Udāyī be as well-provided as this, may Venerable Udāyī be as prosperous as this, just as we are happy, well-provided, and prosperous because of our virtuous mothers-in-law, virtuous fathers-in-law, and virtuous husbands!”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū ekaccānaṃ itthīnaṃ oyācantīnaṃ ekaccānaṃ itthīnaṃ āyācantīnaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī sañcarittaṃ samāpajjissatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, sañcarittaṃ samāpajjasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, sañcarittaṃ samāpajjasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

The bhikkhus heard that some women were cursing and some women were blessing. Those bhikkhus who were modest, content, observant, energetic, and diligent complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How could Venerable Udāyī engage in matchmaking?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Udāyī in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and in this matter, had the Sangha of bhikkhus assembled and questioned Venerable Udāyī: “Is it true, Udāyī, that you engaged in matchmaking?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked him in many ways: “Foolish man, how could you engage in matchmaking? This, foolish man, is not for the confidence of those without it, nor for the increase of confidence in those who have it, but rather for the lack of confidence in those without it, and for the turning away of some of those who have it.” And then, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

299. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu sañcarittaṃ samāpajjeyya, itthiyā vā purisamatiṃ purisassa vā itthimatiṃ, jāyattane vā jārattane vā, saṅghādiseso’’ti.

299.“Should any bhikkhu engage in matchmaking, conveying a man’s message to a woman or a woman’s message to a man, for the purpose of marriage or for the purpose of illicit intercourse, it entails an offense entailing initial and subsequent meetings of the Sangha.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.

300. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā dhuttā uyyāne paricārentā aññatarissā vesiyā santike dūtaṃ pāhesuṃ – ‘‘āgacchatu uyyāne, paricāressāmā’’ti. Sā evamāha – ‘‘ahaṃ khvayyo tumhe na jānāmi – ‘ke vā ime kassa vā’ti. Ahañcamhi bahubhaṇḍā bahuparikkhārā, bahinagarañca gantabbaṃ. Nāhaṃ gamissāmī’’ti. Atha kho so dūto tesaṃ dhuttānaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Evaṃ vutte, aññataro puriso te dhutte etadavoca – ‘‘kissa tumhe ayyo etaṃ vesiṃ yācittha? Nanu ayyo udāyī vattabbo! Ayyo udāyī uyyojessatī’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, aññataro upāsako taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘māyyo evaṃ avaca. Na kappati samaṇānaṃ sakyaputtiyānaṃ evarūpaṃ kātuṃ. Nāyyo udāyī evaṃ karissatī’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, ‘‘karissati na karissatī’’ti abbhutamakaṃsu. Atha kho te dhuttā yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idha mayaṃ, bhante, uyyāne paricārentā asukāya nāma vesiyā santike dūtaṃ pahiṇimhā – ‘āgacchatu uyyāne, paricāressāmā’ti. Sā evamāha – ‘ahaṃ khvayyo tumhe na jānāmi – ke vā ime kassa vāti, ahañcamhi bahubhaṇḍā bahuparikkhārā, bahinagarañca gantabbaṃ. Nāhaṃ gamissāmī’ti. Sādhu, bhante, ayyo taṃ vasiṃ uyyojetū’’ti.

300.Now at that time, several rogues amusing themselves in the park sent a messenger to a certain courtesan, saying: “Come to the park, we will entertain ourselves.” She replied: “Sirs, I do not know you—who are these people, and whose are they? I have many goods and much equipment, and I have to go outside the city. I will not go.” Then the messenger reported this matter to those rogues. When this was said, a certain man said to those rogues: “Why did you sirs ask that courtesan? Shouldn’t you ask Venerable Udāyī? Venerable Udāyī will persuade her.” When this was said, a certain lay follower said to that man: “Do not say so, sir. It is not proper for the Sakyan ascetics to do such a thing. Venerable Udāyī will not do such a thing.” When this was said, they made a bet: “He will do it; he will not do it.” Then those rogues approached Venerable Udāyī; having approached, they said to Venerable Udāyī: “Here, venerable sir, while we were amusing ourselves in the park, we sent a messenger to a certain courtesan, saying: ‘Come to the park, we will entertain ourselves.’ She replied: ‘Sirs, I do not know you—who are these people, and whose are they? I have many goods and much equipment, and I have to go outside the city. I will not go.’ It would be good, venerable sir, if the venerable one would persuade her.”

Atha kho āyasmā udāyī yena sā vesī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ vesiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kissimesaṃ na gacchasī’’ti? ‘‘Ahaṃ khvayya ime na jānāmi – ‘ke vā ime kassa vā’ti. Ahañcamhi bahubhaṇḍā bahuparikkhārā, bahinagarañca gantabbaṃ. Nāhaṃ gamissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Gacchimesaṃ. Ahaṃ ime jānāmī’’ti. ‘‘Sace, bhante, ayyo jānāti ahaṃ gamissāmī’’ti. Atha kho te dhuttā taṃ vesiṃ ādāya uyyānaṃ agamaṃsu. Atha kho so upāsako ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma ayyo udāyī taṅkhaṇikaṃ sañcarittaṃ samāpajjissatī’’ti. Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tassa upāsakassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī taṅkhaṇikaṃ sañcarittaṃ samāpajjissatī’’ti! ‘One brought by a banner’ means one who is said to be brought as a captive.

Gacchanto visaṃvādeti, āgacchanto visaṃvādeti, anāpatti.

If he fails (to accept) while going, and fails (to report back) while returning, there is no offense.

340. Anāpatti saṅghassa vā cetiyassa vā gilānassa vā karaṇīyena gacchati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

340.There is no offense if he goes for a matter concerning the Saṅgha, or a cetiya, or a sick person; for an insane person; or for the first offender.

Vinītavatthuuddānagāthā

Summary Verses for the Matters of Discipline

Suttā matā ca nikkhantā, anitthī itthipaṇḍakā;Kalahaṃ katvāna sammodi, sañcarittañca paṇḍaketi.

"She was asleep, dead, had departed, was not a woman, or was a female eunuch. Having quarreled, she reconciled, and there was association with a eunuch."

Vinītavatthu

Matters of Discipline

341. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro puriso aññataraṃ bhikkhuṃ āṇāpesi – [āṇāpeti (syā. ka.)] ‘‘gaccha, bhante, itthannāmaṃ itthiṃ vīmaṃsā’’ti. So gantvā manusse pucchi – ‘‘kahaṃ itthannāmā’’ti? ‘‘Suttā, bhante’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi – ‘‘bhagavatā sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ, kacci nu kho ahaṃ saṅghādisesaṃ āpattiṃ āpanno’’ti? Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

341.On one occasion, a certain man instructed a certain bhikkhu: "Go, venerable sir, and investigate such-and-such a woman." He went and asked people: "Where is such-and-such woman?" They replied: "She is asleep, venerable sir." He became anxious: "The Blessed One has laid down a training rule—could I have committed a saṅghādisesa offense?" He reported the matter to the Blessed One. "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; there is an offense of wrong-doing."

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro puriso aññataraṃ bhikkhuṃ āṇāpesi – ‘‘gaccha, bhante, itthannāmaṃ itthiṃ vīmaṃsā’’ti. So gantvā manusse pucchi – ‘‘kahaṃ itthannāmā’’ti? ‘‘Matā, bhante’’ti…pe… ‘‘nikkhantā, bhante’’ti… ‘‘anitthī, bhante’’ti… ‘‘itthipaṇḍakā, bhante’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

On one occasion, a certain man instructed a certain bhikkhu: "Go, venerable sir, and investigate such-and-such a woman." He went and asked people: "Where is such-and-such woman?" They replied: "She is dead, venerable sir." ... "She has left, venerable sir." ... "She is not a woman, venerable sir." ... "She is a female eunuch, venerable sir." He became anxious ... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; there is an offense of wrong-doing."

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarā itthī sāmikena saha bhaṇḍitvā mātugharaṃ agamāsi. Kulūpako bhikkhu sammodanīyaṃ akāsi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘alaṃvacanīyā, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Nālaṃvacanīyā, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, nālaṃvacanīyāyā’’ti.

On one occasion, a certain woman quarreled with her husband and went to her mother’s house. A bhikkhu named Kulūpaka engaged in friendly conversation with her. He became anxious ... "Is he blameworthy, bhikkhus?" "He is not blameworthy, Blessed One." "There is no offense, bhikkhu; he is not blameworthy."

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu paṇḍake sañcarittaṃ samāpajji. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, saṅghādisesassa; āpatti thullaccayassā’’ti.

On one occasion, a certain bhikkhu engaged in sexual intercourse with a eunuch. He became anxious ... "There is no saṅghādisesa offense, bhikkhu; however, there is an offense of thullaccaya."

Sañcarittasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The fifth training rule on intimacy is concluded.
