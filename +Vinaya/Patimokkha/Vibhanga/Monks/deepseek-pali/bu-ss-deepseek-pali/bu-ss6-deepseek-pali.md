# BU-SN 6

## 6. Kuṭikārasikkhāpadaṃ

## 6.The training rule concerning hut-building.

342. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena āḷavakā bhikkhū saññācikāyo kuṭiyo kārāpenti assāmikāyo attuddesikāyo appamāṇikāyo. Tāyo na niṭṭhānaṃ gacchanti. Te yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharanti – ‘‘purisaṃ detha, purisatthakaraṃ detha, goṇaṃ detha, sakaṭaṃ detha, vāsiṃ detha, parasuṃ detha, kuṭhāriṃ detha, kudālaṃ detha, nikhādanaṃ detha, valliṃ detha, veḷuṃ detha, muñjaṃ detha, pabbajaṃ detha, tiṇaṃ detha, mattikaṃ dethā’’ti. Manussā upaddutā yācanāya upaddutā viññattiyā bhikkhū disvā ubbijjantipi uttasantipi palāyantipi aññenapi gacchanti aññenapi mukhaṃ karonti dvārampi thakenti, gāvimpi disvā palāyanti bhikkhūti maññamānā.

342.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels' Feeding Place. Now at that time the bhikkhus from Āḷavī were having huts built by begging for materials, without owners, intended for themselves, and without set limits. These huts did not reach completion. They lived being persistent in begging and requesting: "Give a man, give a laborer, give an ox, give a cart, give an axe, give a hatchet, give an adze, give a hoe, give a digging stick, give a creeper, give bamboo, give munja grass, give pabbaja grass, give straw, give clay." People were harassed by the begging, harassed by the requests. Seeing the bhikkhus, they became agitated, terrified, and ran away. They went another way, turned their faces another way, or even shut their doors. Even seeing a cow, they ran away, thinking, "bhikkhus!"

Atha kho āyasmā mahākassapo rājagahe vassaṃvuṭṭho yena āḷavī tena pakkāmi. Anupubbena yena āḷavī tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ āyasmā mahākassapo āḷaviyaṃ viharati aggāḷave cetiye. Atha kho āyasmā mahākassapo pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya āḷaviṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Manussā āyasmantaṃ mahākassapaṃ passitvā ubbijjantipi uttasantipi palāyantipi aññenapi gacchanti aññenapi mukhaṃ karonti dvārampi thakenti. Atha kho āyasmā mahākassapo āḷaviyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātappaṭikkanto bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘pubbāyaṃ, āvuso, āḷavī subhikkhā ahosi sulabhapiṇḍā sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ; etarahi panāyaṃ āḷavī dubbhikkhā dullabhapiṇḍā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ. Ko nu kho, āvuso, hetu ko paccayo, yenāyaṃ āḷavī dubbhikkhā dullabhapiṇḍā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetu’’nti? Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmato mahākassapassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ.

Then the Venerable Mahākassapa, having spent the rains in Rājagaha, set out for Āḷavī. Traveling in stages, he arrived at Āḷavī. There the Venerable Mahākassapa stayed at the Aggāḷava Shrine. Then the Venerable Mahākassapa, having dressed in the morning, taking his bowl and robe, entered Āḷavī for alms. The people, seeing the Venerable Mahākassapa, became agitated, terrified, and ran away; they went another way, turned their faces another way, or even shut their doors. Then the Venerable Mahākassapa, having walked for alms in Āḷavī and returned from his alms round after the meal, addressed the bhikkhus: "Formerly, friends, Āḷavī was prosperous, alms were easy to obtain, and it was easy to make a living by gleaning. But now, Āḷavī is suffering famine, alms are hard to obtain, and it is not easy to make a living by gleaning. What is the cause, friends, what is the reason, why Āḷavī is now suffering famine, alms are hard to obtain, and it is not easy to make a living by gleaning?" Then those bhikkhus informed the Venerable Mahākassapa of the matter.

343. Atha kho bhagavā rājagahe yathābhirantaṃ viharitvā yena āḷavī tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena āḷavī tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā āḷaviyaṃ viharati aggāḷave cetiye. Atha kho āyasmā mahākassapo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā mahākassapo bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āḷavake bhikkhū paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, saññācikāyo kuṭiyo kārāpetha assāmikāyo attuddesikāyo appamāṇikāyo. Tāyo na niṭṭhānaṃ gacchanti. Te tumhe yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharatha – ‘purisaṃ detha purisatthakaraṃ detha…pe… tiṇaṃ detha mattikaṃ dethā’ti. Manussā upaddutā yācanāya upaddutā viññattiyā bhikkhū disvā ubbijjantipi uttasantipi palāyantipi aññenapi gacchanti aññenapi mukhaṃ karonti dvārampi thakenti, gāvimpi disvā palāyanti bhikkhūti maññamānā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… ‘‘kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, saṃyācikāyo kuṭiyo kārāpessatha assāmikāyo attuddesikāyo appamāṇikāyo! Tāyo na niṭṭhānaṃ gacchanti. Te tumhe yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharissatha – ‘purisaṃ detha purisatthakaraṃ detha…pe… tiṇaṃ detha mattikaṃ dethā’ti! Netaṃ moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe…’’ vigarahitvā dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi –

343.Then the Blessed One, having stayed at Rājagaha as long as he pleased, set out on a journey toward Āḷavī. Traveling stage by stage, he arrived at Āḷavī. There the Blessed One stayed at the Aggāḷava Shrine in Āḷavī.Then the Venerable Mahākassapa approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the Venerable Mahākassapa informed the Blessed One about the matter.Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and in this connection, had the Sangha of bhikkhus assembled and questioned the Āḷavī bhikkhus: "Is it true, bhikkhus, that you have huts built by begging—ownerless, intended for yourselves, without set limits—that do not reach completion? And that you live being persistent in begging and requesting: 'Give a man, give a laborer... give grass, give clay'? People are harassed by the begging, harassed by the requests; seeing bhikkhus, they become agitated, terrified, and run away. They go another way, turn their faces another way, or even shut their doors. Even seeing a cow, they run away, thinking, 'Here come the bhikkhus!'""It is true, Blessed One."The Blessed One rebuked them: "How can you, foolish men, have huts built by begging—ownerless, intended for yourselves, without set limits—that do not reach completion? And that you live being persistent in begging and requesting: 'Give a man, give a laborer... give grass, give clay'? This will not, foolish men, inspire confidence in those without it..." Having rebuked them, he gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus:

344. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, dve bhātaro isayo gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ upanissāya vihariṃsu. Atha kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ uttaritvā yena kaniṭṭho isi tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā kaniṭṭhaṃ isiṃ sattakkhattuṃ bhogehi parikkhipitvā uparimuddhani mahantaṃ phaṇaṃ karitvā aṭṭhāsi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, kaniṭṭho isi tassa nāgassa bhayā kiso ahosi lūkho dubbaṇṇo uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto. Addasa kho, bhikkhave, jeṭṭho isi kaniṭṭhaṃ isiṃ kisaṃ lūkhaṃ dubbaṇṇaṃ uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajātaṃ dhamanisanthatagattaṃ. Disvāna kaniṭṭhaṃ isiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kissa tvaṃ, bho, kiso lūkho dubbaṇṇo uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto’’ti? ‘‘Idha, bho, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ uttaritvā yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ sattakkhattuṃ bhogehi parikkhipitvā uparimuddhani mahantaṃ phaṇaṃ karitvā aṭṭhāsi. Tassāhaṃ, bho, nāgassa bhayā [bhayāmhi (sī.)] kiso lūkho dubbaṇṇo uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto’’ti. ‘‘Icchasi pana tvaṃ, bho, tassa nāgassa anāgamana’’nti? ‘‘Icchāmahaṃ, bho, tassa nāgassa anāgamana’’nti. ‘‘Api pana tvaṃ, bho, tassa nāgassa kiñci passasī’’ti? ‘‘Passāmahaṃ, bho, maṇimassa [maṇissa (sī. ka.)] kaṇṭhe pilandhana’’nti. ‘‘Tena hi tvaṃ, bho, taṃ nāgaṃ maṇiṃ yāca – ‘maṇiṃ me, bho, dehi; maṇinā me attho’’’ti.

344."Once upon a time, bhikkhus, two brother ascetics lived near the Ganges River. Then, bhikkhus, the Naga king named Maṇikaṇṭha crossed the Ganges River and approached the younger ascetic. Having approached, he coiled around the younger ascetic seven times with his body, spread a large hood over his head, and stood there. Then, bhikkhus, the younger ascetic, out of fear of that Naga, became thin, wretched, pale, discolored, with veins standing out all over his body. The elder ascetic saw the younger ascetic thin, wretched, pale, discolored, with veins standing out all over his body. Seeing this, he said to the younger ascetic: 'Why, friend, are you thin, wretched, pale, discolored, with veins standing out all over your body?' 'Here, friend, the Naga king Maṇikaṇṭha crossed the Ganges River and approached me. Having approached, he coiled around me seven times with his body, spread a large hood over my head, and stood there. Out of fear of that Naga, friend, I have become thin, wretched, pale, discolored, with veins standing out all over my body.' 'But do you wish, friend, for that Naga not to come again?' 'I do wish, friend, for that Naga not to come again.' 'And do you see anything of that Naga, friend?' 'I see, friend, a jewel worn around his neck.' 'Well then, friend, ask that Naga for the jewel—"Give me the jewel, friend; I need the jewel."'"

Atha kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ uttaritvā yena kaniṭṭho isi tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitaṃ kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭhaṃ nāgarājānaṃ kaniṭṭho isi etadavoca – ‘‘maṇiṃ me, bho, dehi; maṇinā me attho’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā – ‘bhikkhu maṇiṃ yācati, bhikkhussa maṇinā attho’ti khippaññeva agamāsi. Dutiyampi kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ uttaritvā yena kaniṭṭho isi tenupasaṅkami. Addasa kho, bhikkhave, kaniṭṭho isi maṇikaṇṭhaṃ nāgarājānaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna maṇikaṇṭhaṃ nāgarājānaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘maṇiṃ me, bho, dehi; maṇinā me attho’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā – ‘‘bhikkhu maṇiṃ yācati, bhikkhussa maṇinā attho’’ti tatova paṭinivatti. Tatiyampi kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ uttarati. Addasa kho, bhikkhave, kaniṭṭho isi maṇikaṇṭhaṃ nāgarājānaṃ gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ uttarantaṃ. Disvāna maṇikaṇṭhaṃ nāgarājānaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘maṇiṃ me, bho, dehi; maṇinā me attho’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā kaniṭṭhaṃ isiṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

Then, bhikkhus, Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha, having crossed the Ganges River, approached the younger ascetic. Having approached, he stood to one side. While he was standing to one side, bhikkhus, the younger ascetic said to Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha: “Give me the jewel, good sir; I need the jewel.” Then, bhikkhus, Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha, thinking: ‘The ascetic asks for the jewel, the ascetic needs the jewel,’ quickly departed. A second time, bhikkhus, Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha, having crossed the Ganges River, approached the younger ascetic. The younger ascetic, bhikkhus, saw Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha coming from afar. Having seen him, he said to Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha: “Give me the jewel, good sir; I need the jewel.” Then, bhikkhus, Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha, thinking: ‘The ascetic asks for the jewel, the ascetic needs the jewel,’ turned back from that very place. A third time, bhikkhus, Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha crossed the Ganges River. The younger ascetic, bhikkhus, saw Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha crossing the Ganges River. Having seen Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha, he said: “Give me the jewel, good sir; I need the jewel.” Then, bhikkhus, Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha addressed the younger ascetic with verses:

[jā. 1.3.7 maṇikaṇṭhajātakepi] ‘‘Mamannapānaṃ vipulaṃ uḷāraṃ,Uppajjatīmassa maṇissa hetu;Taṃ te na dassaṃ atiyācakosi;Na cāpi te assamamāgamissaṃ.

“My food and drink are ample and exquisite, / They arise because of this jewel. / I will not give it to you, for you are an excessive beggar; / Nor will I come to your hermitage.”

[jā. 1.3.8 maṇikaṇṭhajātakepi] ‘‘Susū yathā sakkharadhotapāṇī;Tāsesi maṃ selamāyācamāno;Taṃ te na dassaṃ atiyācakosi;Na cāpi te assamamāgamissa’’nti.

“As a young man with a sword sharpened on a stone in hand, / You frighten me, begging for the jewel. / I will not give it to you, for you are an excessive beggar; / Nor will I come to your hermitage.”

Atha kho, bhikkhave, maṇikaṇṭho nāgarājā – ‘‘bhikkhu maṇiṃ yācati, bhikkhussa maṇinā attho’’ti pakkāmi. Tathā pakkantova [tadāpakkantova (ka.)] ahosi, na puna paccāgañchi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, kaniṭṭho isi tassa nāgassa dassanīyassa adassanena bhiyyosomattāya kiso ahosi lūkho dubbaṇṇo, uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto. Addasa kho, bhikkhave, jeṭṭho isi kaniṭṭhaṃ isiṃ bhiyyosomattāya kisaṃ lūkhaṃ dubbaṇṇaṃ uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajātaṃ dhamanisanthatagattaṃ. Disvāna kaniṭṭhaṃ isiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kissa tvaṃ, bho, bhiyyosomattāya kiso lūkho dubbaṇṇo uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto’’ti? ‘‘Tassāhaṃ, bho, nāgassa dassanīyassa adassanena bhiyyosomattāya kiso lūkho dubbaṇṇo uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, jeṭṭho isi kaniṭṭhaṃ isiṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

Then, bhikkhus, Nāga king Maṇikaṇṭha, thinking: ‘The ascetic asks for the jewel, the ascetic needs the jewel,’ departed. He departed and did not return again. Then, bhikkhus, the younger ascetic, because of not seeing that lovely Nāga, became much more emaciated, rough, unsightly, pale and wan, with veins showing all over his body. The elder ascetic, bhikkhus, saw the younger ascetic much more emaciated, rough, unsightly, pale and wan, with veins showing all over his body. Having seen him, he said to the younger ascetic: “Why, good sir, are you so very emaciated, rough, unsightly, pale and wan, with veins showing all over your body?” “Because, good sir, I have not seen that lovely Nāga, I have become much more emaciated, rough, unsightly, pale and wan, with veins showing all over my body.” Then, bhikkhus, the elder ascetic addressed the younger ascetic with a verse:

[jā. 1.3.9 maṇikaṇṭhajātakepi] ‘‘Na taṃ yāce yassa piyaṃ jigīse,Videsso [desso (sī.), desso ca (syā.)] hoti atiyācanāya;Nāgo maṇiṃ yācito brāhmaṇena;Adassanaññeva tadajjhagamā’’ti.

“One should not beg from one whose affection one desires, / For by excessive begging one becomes disliked. / The Nāga, when begged for a jewel by the ascetic, / Simply disappeared.”

Tesañhi nāma, bhikkhave, tiracchānagatānaṃ pāṇānaṃ amanāpā bhavissati yācanā amanāpā viññatti. Kimaṅgaṃ [kimaṅga (sī.)] pana manussabhūtānaṃ!

Indeed, bhikkhus, for animals, begging will be displeasing, making requests will be displeasing. How much more so for human beings!

345. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, aññataro bhikkhu himavantapasse viharati aññatarasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe. Tassa kho, bhikkhave, vanasaṇḍassa avidūre mahantaṃ ninnaṃ pallalaṃ. Atha kho,

345.Once upon a time, bhikkhus, a certain bhikkhu was dwelling on the slopes of the Himalayas, in a certain forest grove. Not far from that forest grove, bhikkhus, was a large, low-lying swamp. Then,

Vāsāya upagacchati. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so bhikkhu tassa sakuṇasaṅghassa saddena ubbāḷho yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘kacci, bhikkhu, khamanīyaṃ kacci yāpanīyaṃ kaccisi appakilamathena addhānaṃ āgato? Kuto ca tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āgacchasī’ti? ‘Khamanīyaṃ, bhagavā, yāpanīyaṃ, bhagavā. Appakilamathena cāhaṃ, bhante, addhānaṃ āgato. Atthi, bhante, himavantapasse mahāvanasaṇḍo. Tassa kho pana, bhante, vanasaṇḍassa avidūre mahantaṃ ninnaṃ pallalaṃ. Atha kho, bhante, mahāsakuṇasaṅgho tasmiṃ pallale divasaṃ gocaraṃ caritvā sāyaṃ taṃ vanasaṇḍaṃ vāsāya upagacchati. Tato ahaṃ, bhagavā, āgacchāmi – tassa sakuṇasaṅghassa saddena ubbāḷho’ti. ‘Icchasi pana tvaṃ, bhikkhu, tassa sakuṇasaṅghassa anāgamana’nti? ‘Icchāmahaṃ, bhagavā, tassa sakuṇasaṅghassa anāgamana’nti. ‘Tena hi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, tattha gantvā taṃ vanasaṇḍaṃ ajjhogāhetvā rattiyā paṭhamaṃ yāmaṃ tikkhattuṃ saddamanussāvehi – suṇantu me, bhonto sakuṇā, yāvatikā imasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe vāsaṃ upagatā, pattena me attho. Ekekaṃ me, bhonto, pattaṃ dadantū’ti. Rattiyā majjhimaṃ yāmaṃ… rattiyā pacchimaṃ yāmaṃ tikkhattuṃ saddamanussāvehi – ‘suṇantu me, bhonto sakuṇā, yāvatikā imasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe vāsaṃ upagatā, pattena me attho. Ekekaṃ me, bhonto, pattaṃ dadantū’ti.

A great flock of birds approaches that forest grove for lodging. Then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu, disturbed by the noise of that flock of birds, approached me. Having approached, he paid homage to me and sat down to one side. While he was sitting to one side, bhikkhus, I said to that bhikkhu: “I hope, bhikkhu, you are well; I hope you are comfortable; I hope you have come this distance without too much fatigue. And where have you come from, bhikkhu?” “I am well, Blessed One; I am comfortable, Blessed One. And I have come this distance, venerable sir, without too much fatigue. There is, venerable sir, a great forest grove on the slopes of the Himalayas. Not far from that forest grove, venerable sir, is a large low-lying marsh. Then, venerable sir, a great flock of birds, having wandered for food in that marsh during the day, approaches that forest grove in the evening for lodging. From there, Blessed One, I have come, disturbed by the noise of that flock of birds.” “Do you wish, bhikkhu, for that flock of birds not to come?” “I wish, Blessed One, for that flock of birds not to come.” “Then, bhikkhu, go there and enter that forest grove. In the first watch of the night, announce three times: ‘Listen to me, good sirs, the birds, all who have taken up residence in this forest grove, I have need of a leaf. Let each of you, good sirs, give me one leaf.’ In the middle watch of the night… in the last watch of the night, announce three times: ‘Listen to me, good sirs, the birds, all who have taken up residence in this forest grove, I have need of a leaf. Let each of you, good sirs, give me one leaf.’”

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, so bhikkhu tattha gantvā taṃ vanasaṇḍaṃ ajjhogāhetvā rattiyā paṭhamaṃ yāmaṃ tikkhattuṃ saddamanussāvesi – ‘suṇantu me, bhonto sakuṇā, yāvatikā imasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe vāsaṃ upagatā, pattena me attho. Ekekaṃ me, bhonto, pattaṃ dadantū’ti. Rattiyā majjhima yāmaṃ… rattiyā pacchimaṃ yāmaṃ tikkhattuṃ saddamanussāvesi – ‘suṇantu me, bhonto sakuṇā, yāvatikā imasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe vāsaṃ upagatā, pattena me attho. Ekekaṃ me, bhonto, pattaṃ dadantū’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so sakuṇasaṅgho – ‘bhikkhu pattaṃ yācati bhikkhussa pattena attho’ti tamhā vanasaṇḍā pakkāmi. Tathā pakkantova ahosi na puna paccāgañchi. Tesañhi nāma, bhikkhave, tiracchānagatānaṃ pāṇānaṃ amanāpā bhavissati yācanā amanāpā viññatti. Kimaṅgaṃ pana manussabhūtānaṃ’’!

Then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu went there, entered that forest grove, and during the first watch of the night, he three times made an announcement: ‘Listen to me, good sirs, the birds, all who have taken up residence in this forest grove, I have need of a leaf. Let each of you, good sirs, give me one leaf.’ During the middle watch of the night… during the last watch of the night, he three times made an announcement: ‘Listen to me, good sirs, the birds, all who have taken up residence in this forest grove, I have need of a leaf. Let each of you, good sirs, give me one leaf.’ Then, bhikkhus, that flock of birds—‘The bhikkhu asks for a leaf; the bhikkhu needs a leaf’—departed from that forest grove. 346. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, raṭṭhapālassa kulaputtassa pitā raṭṭhapālaṃ kulaputtaṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

346.Once upon a time, bhikkhus, the father of the clansman Raṭṭhapāla addressed the clansman Raṭṭhapāla with a verse:

‘Apāhaṃ te na jānāmi, raṭṭhapāla bahū janā;[jā. 1.7.54] Te maṃ saṅgamma yācanti, kasmā maṃ tvaṃ na yācasī’ti.

‘I do not know these many people, Raṭṭhapāla; / They come together and beg from me. / Why do you not beg from me?’

‘The beggar is disliked; / And the one who does not give what is asked is disliked. / Therefore I do not beg from you, / Lest there be dislike for me.’

‘‘So hi nāma, bhikkhave, raṭṭhapālo kulaputto sakaṃ pitaraṃ evaṃ vakkhati. Kimaṅgaṃ pana jano janaṃ!

Indeed, bhikkhus, the clansman Raṭṭhapāla would speak thus to his own father. How much more so one person to another!

347. ‘‘Gihīnaṃ, bhikkhave, dussaṃharāni bhogāni sambhatānipi durakkhiyāni. Tattha nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, evaṃ dussaṃharesu bhogesu sambhatesupi durakkhiyesu yācanabahulā viññattibahulā viharissatha – ‘purisaṃ detha, purisatthakaraṃ detha, goṇaṃ detha, sakaṭaṃ detha, vāsiṃ detha, parasuṃ detha, kuṭhāriṃ detha, kudālaṃ detha, nikhādanaṃ detha, valliṃ detha, veḷuṃ detha, muñjaṃ detha, pabbajaṃ detha, tiṇaṃ detha, mattikaṃ dethā’’ti! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

347.For householders, bhikkhus, wealth is hard to obtain, and even when amassed, difficult to protect. Yet here you are, foolish men, in the midst of such hard-to-obtain and hard-to-protect wealth, constantly begging and requesting: ‘Give us a man, give us a male worker, give us an ox, give us a cart, give us an adze, give us an axe, give us a hatchet, give us a spade, give us a digging stick, give us a creeper, give us bamboo, give us muñja grass, give us reed grass, give us grass, give us clay!’ This is not, foolish men, for inspiring confidence in the faithless… And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

348. ‘‘Saññācikāya pana bhikkhunā kuṭiṃ kārayamānena assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ pamāṇikā kāretabbā. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ – dīghaso dvādasa vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ sattantarā. Bhikkhū abhinetabbā vatthudesanāya. Tehi bhikkhūhi vatthu desetabbaṃ – anārambhaṃ ti.

348.But if a bhikkhu is having a hut built by means of solicitation, it should be built on common land, for his own use, and according to the proper measure. Here is the proper measure: twelve spans long, according to the Sugata span; and seven spans wide inside. bhikkhus should be brought to designate the site. Those bhikkhus should designate a site that is free from disturbance and with space for circumambulation. If a bhikkhu has a hut built by means of solicitation on a site with disturbance or without space for circumambulation, or does not bring bhikkhus to designate the site, or exceeds the measure, he commits an offense entailing suspension.

349. Saññācikā nāma sayaṃ yācitvā purisampi purisatthakarampi goṇampi sakaṭampi vāsimpi parasumpi kuṭhārimpi kudālampi nikhādanampi vallimpi veḷumpi muñjampi pabbajampi tiṇampi mattikampi.

349.Saññācikā means begging for oneself: a man, a male worker, an ox, a cart, an adze, an axe, a hatchet, a spade, a digging stick, a creeper, bamboo, muñja grass, reed grass, grass, or clay.

Kuṭi nāma ullittā vā hoti avalittā vā ullittāvalittā vā.

A hut is either plastered inside, plastered outside, or plastered inside and outside.

Kārayamānenāti karonto vā kārāpento vā.

Kārayamānena means doing or causing to be done.

Assāmikanti na añño koci sāmiko hoti, itthī vā puriso vā gahaṭṭho vā pabbajito vā.

Assāmika means no other owner exists, whether a woman or a man, a householder or a renunciate.

Attuddesanti attano atthāya.

Attuddesa means for one's own purpose.

Pamāṇikā kāretabbā. Tatridaṃ pamāṇaṃ – dīghaso dvādasa vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyāti bāhirimena mānena.

It should be made according to a standard. Here is the standard: twelve handspans in length, by the Sugata's handspan, according to the outer measure.

Tiriyaṃ sattantarāti abbhantarimena mānena.

Seven across, by the inner measure.

Bhikkhū abhinetabbā vatthudesanāyāti tena kuṭikārakena bhikkhunā kuṭivatthuṃ sodhetvā saṅghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘‘ahaṃ, bhante, saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. Sohaṃ, bhante, saṅghaṃ kuṭivatthuolokanaṃ yācāmī’’ti. Dutiyampi yācitabbā. Tatiyampi yācitabbā. Sace sabbo saṅgho ussahati kuṭivatthuṃ oloketuṃ, sabbena saṅghena oloketabbaṃ. No ce sabbo saṅgho ussahati kuṭivatthuṃ oloketuṃ, ye tattha honti bhikkhū byattā paṭibalā sārambhaṃ anārambhaṃ saparikkamanaṃ aparikkamanaṃ jānituṃ te yācitvā sammannitabbā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbā. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

Therefore, the bhikkhu who wishes to build the hut should clean the hut site, approach the Sangha, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in a squatting position, raise his joined palms, and say this: "Venerable sirs, I wish to build a hut by personal solicitation, ownerless, for my own purpose. Venerable sirs, I request the Sangha to inspect the hut site." He should make this request a second time. He should make this request a third time. If the entire Sangha is able to inspect the hut site, then the whole Sangha should inspect it. If the entire Sangha is not able to inspect the hut site, then those bhikkhus present who are intelligent and capable of knowing what is dangerous or not dangerous, what has a circumambulation space or lacks a circumambulation space, should be requested and appointed. And, bhikkhus, they should be appointed like this. An intelligent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Sangha:

350. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. So saṅghaṃ kuṭivatthuolokanaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmañca itthannāmañca bhikkhū sammanneyya itthannāmassa bhikkhuno kuṭivatthuṃ oloketuṃ. Esā ñatti.

350."Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, wishes to build a hut by personal solicitation, ownerless, for his own purpose. This is the motion."

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. So saṅghaṃ kuṭivatthuolokanaṃ yācati. "Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, wishes to build a hut by personal solicitation, ownerless, for his own purpose. He requests the Sangha for an inspection of the hut site. The Sangha appoints bhikkhus so-and-so and so-and-so to inspect the hut site for the bhikkhu named so-and-so. "The Sangha has appointed bhikkhus so-and-so and so-and-so to inspect the hut site for the bhikkhu named so-and-so. The Sangha approves; therefore it is silent. Thus I hold it."

351. Tehi sammatehi bhikkhūhi tattha gantvā kuṭivatthu oloketabbaṃ, sārambhaṃ anārambhaṃ saparikkamanaṃ aparikkamanaṃ jānitabbaṃ. Sace sārambhaṃ hoti aparikkamanaṃ, ‘mā idha karī’ti vattabbo. Tena kuṭikārakena bhikkhunā saṅghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘‘ahaṃ, bhante, saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. Sohaṃ, bhante, saṅghaṃ kuṭivatthudesanaṃ yācāmī’’ti. Dutiyampi yācitabbā. Tatiyampi yācitabbā. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

351.The appointed bhikkhus should go there and inspect the hut site. They should determine whether it is dangerous or not dangerous, whether it has a circumambulation space or lacks a circumambulation space. If it is dangerous and lacks a circumambulation space, they should say, "Do not build here." If it is not dangerous and has a circumambulation space, they should inform the Sangha: "It is not dangerous and has a circumambulation space." The bhikkhu who wishes to build the hut should approach the Sangha, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in a squatting position, raise his joined palms, and say: "Venerable sirs, I wish to build a hut by personal solicitation, ownerless, for my own purpose. Venerable sirs, I request the Sangha to designate a site for the hut." He should make this request a second time and a third time. A competent and capable bhikkhu should then inform the Sangha:

352. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. So saṅghaṃ kuṭivatthudesanaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno kuṭivatthuṃ deseyya. Esā ñatti.

352."Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. This bhikkhu named so-and-so wishes to build a hut by personal solicitation, ownerless, for his own purpose. He requests the Sangha for the designation of a hut site. If it is fitting for the Sangha, the Sangha should designate the hut site for the bhikkhu named so-and-so. This is the motion."

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. So saṅghaṃ kuṭivatthudesanaṃ yācati. Saṅgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno kuṭivatthuṃ deseti. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno kuṭivatthussa desanā, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

"Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, wishes to build a hut by personal solicitation, ownerless, for his own purpose. He requests the Sangha for the designation of the hut site. The Sangha designates the hut site for the bhikkhu named so-and-so. Whoever among the venerable ones approves of the designation of the hut site for the bhikkhu named so-and-so, let him remain silent; whoever does not approve, let him speak."

‘‘Desitaṃ saṅghena itthannāmassa bhikkhuno kuṭivatthu. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

"The hut site for the bhikkhu named so-and-so has been designated by the Sangha. The Sangha approves; therefore it is silent. Thus I hold it."

353.What is called a dangerous site is one that serves as a dwelling for ants, or termites, or rats, or snakes, or scorpions, or centipedes, or elephants, or horses, or lions, or tigers, or leopards, or bears, or hyenas—any place that serves as a dwelling for animals. Or it may be dependent on early crops, or dependent on late crops, or dependent on a place of torture, or dependent on a place of execution, or dependent on a cemetery, or dependent on a park, or dependent on royal property, or dependent on an elephant stable, or dependent on a horse stable, or dependent on a prison, or dependent on a tavern, or dependent on a slaughterhouse, or dependent on a main road, or dependent on a public square, or dependent on an assembly hall, or dependent on a thoroughfare. This is called a dangerous site.

Aparikkamanaṃ nāma na sakkā hoti yathāyuttena sakaṭena anuparigantuṃ samantā nisseṇiyā anuparigantuṃ. Etaṃ aparikkamanaṃ nāma.

What is called a site without circumambulation space is one where it is not possible to go around with a cart properly yoked, or to go around completely with ladders. This is called a site without circumambulation space.

What is called a not dangerous site is one that does not serve as a dwelling for ants, or termites, or rats, or snakes, or scorpions, or centipedes… nor is it dependent on a thoroughfare. This is called a not dangerous site.

Saparikkamanaṃ nāma sakkā hoti yathāyuttena sakaṭena anuparigantuṃ, samantā nisseṇiyā anuparigantuṃ. Etaṃ saparikkamanaṃ nāma.
