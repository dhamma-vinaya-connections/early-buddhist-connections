# BU-PJ 4

## 4. Catutthapārājikaṃ

## 4.The fourth offense entailing defeat.

193. [idaṃ vatthu pāci. 67] Tena samayena buddho bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā sandiṭṭhā sambhattā bhikkhū vaggumudāya nadiyā tīre vassaṃ upagacchiṃsu. Tena kho pana samayena vajjī dubbhikkhā hoti dvīhitikā setaṭṭhikā salākāvuttā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ. Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘etarahi kho vajjī dubbhikkhā dvīhitikā setaṭṭhikā salākāvuttā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ. Kena nu kho mayaṃ upāyena samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vaseyyāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilameyyāmā’’ti? Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘handa mayaṃ, āvuso, gihīnaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhema, evaṃ te amhākaṃ dātuṃ maññissanti. Evaṃ mayaṃ samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasissāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilamissāmā’’ti. Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘alaṃ, āvuso, kiṃ gihīnaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhitena! Handa mayaṃ, āvuso, gihīnaṃ dūteyyaṃ harāma, evaṃ te amhākaṃ dātuṃ maññissanti. Evaṃ mayaṃ samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasissāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilamissāmā’’ti. Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘alaṃ, āvuso, kiṃ gihīnaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhitena! Kiṃ gihīnaṃ dūteyyaṃ haṭena! Handa mayaṃ, āvuso, gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissāma – ‘asuko bhikkhu paṭhamassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu dutiyassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu tatiyassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu catutthassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu sotāpanno, asuko bhikkhu sakadāgāmī, asuko bhikkhu anāgāmī, asuko bhikkhu arahā, asuko bhikkhu tevijjo, asuko bhikkhu chaḷabhiñño’ti. Evaṃ te amhākaṃ dātuṃ maññissanti. Evaṃ mayaṃ samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasissāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilamissāmā’’ti. ‘‘Esoyeva kho, āvuso, seyyo yo amhākaṃ gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇo bhāsito’’ti.

193.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Vesālī in the Great Wood, in the Gabled Hall. Now at that time several bhikkhus, well-known and congenial, had entered the rains residence on the bank of the Vaggumudā River. At that time the Vajjis were experiencing famine, with scarce food, rice being expensive, sesamum being like broken rice, and food being distributed by ticket; it was not easy to sustain oneself by gleaning alms. Then those bhikkhus thought, “Now the Vajjis are experiencing famine, with scarce food, rice being expensive, sesamum being like broken rice, and food being distributed by ticket; it is not easy to sustain oneself by gleaning alms. By what means can we dwell in concord, harmoniously, without dispute, and comfortably during the rains, without being exhausted by alms gathering?” Some said, “Come, friends, let us supervise the affairs of the laypeople. Then they will think to give to us. Thus we will dwell in concord, harmoniously, without dispute, and comfortably during the rains, without being exhausted by alms gathering.” Others said, “Enough, friends! What is the use of supervising the affairs of the laypeople! Come, friends, let us carry messages for the laypeople. Then they will think to give to us. Thus we will dwell in concord, harmoniously, without dispute, and comfortably during the rains, without being exhausted by alms gathering.” Still others said, “Enough, friends! What is the use of supervising the affairs of the laypeople! What is the use of carrying messages for the laypeople! Come, friends, let us praise to the laypeople one another’s superior human qualities: ‘This bhikkhu is an attainter of the first jhāna, this bhikkhu an attainter of the second jhāna, this bhikkhu an attainter of the third jhāna, this bhikkhu an attainter of the fourth jhāna. This bhikkhu is a stream-enterer, this bhikkhu a once-returner, this bhikkhu a non-returner, this bhikkhu an arahant. This bhikkhu has the threefold knowledge, this bhikkhu the sixfold direct knowledge.’ Then they will think to give to us. Thus we will dwell in concord, harmoniously, without dispute, and comfortably during the rains, without being exhausted by alms gathering.” “This, friends, is indeed better: that we praise to the laypeople one another’s superior human qualities.”

194. Atha kho te bhikkhū gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsiṃsu – ‘‘asuko bhikkhu paṭhamassa jhānassa lābhī…pe… asuko bhikkhu chaḷabhiñño’’ti. Atha kho te manussā – ‘‘lābhā vata no, suladdhaṃ vata no, yesaṃ vata no evarūpā bhikkhū vassaṃ upagatā; na vata no ito pubbe evarūpā bhikkhū vassaṃ upagatā yathayime bhikkhū sīlavanto kalyāṇadhammā’’ti, te na tādisāni bhojanāni attanā paribhuñjanti mātāpitūnaṃ denti puttadārassa denti dāsakammakaraporisassa denti mittāmaccānaṃ denti ñātisālohitānaṃ denti, yādisāni bhikkhūnaṃ denti. Te na tādisāni khādanīyāni sāyanīyāni pānāni attanā khādanti sāyanti pivanti mātāpitūnaṃ denti puttadārassa denti dāsakammakaraporisassa denti mittāmaccānaṃ denti ñātisālohitānaṃ denti, yādisāni bhikkhūnaṃ denti. Atha kho te bhikkhū vaṇṇavā ahesuṃ pīṇindriyā pasannamukhavaṇṇā vippasannachavivaṇṇā.

194.Then those bhikkhus praised one another’s superior human qualities to the householders: “This bhikkhu is an attainter of the first jhāna… this bhikkhu has the sixfold direct knowledge.” Then those people thought: “We are indeed fortunate, truly blessed, that such bhikkhus have come to spend the rains residence with us. Never before have bhikkhus like these—virtuous, of good character—come to spend the rains residence with us.” They did not consume such food themselves, nor did they give it to their parents, wives and children, servants and workers, friends and acquaintances, and relatives, as they gave to the bhikkhus. They did not consume such hard foods, soft foods, and drinks themselves, nor did they give them to their parents, wives and children, servants and workers, friends and acquaintances, and relatives, as they gave to the bhikkhus. Then those bhikkhus became lustrous in complexion, with plump faculties, bright faces, and radiant skin.

Āciṇṇaṃ kho panetaṃ vassaṃvuṭṭhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bhagavantaṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Atha kho te bhikkhū vassaṃvuṭṭhā temāsaccayena senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaraṃ ādāya yena vesālī tena pakkamiṃsu. Anupubbena yena vesālī mahāvanaṃ kūṭāgārasālā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu.

It is customary for bhikkhus who have completed the rains residence to approach the Blessed One for an audience. Then those bhikkhus, having completed the rains residence, after three months dismantled their lodgings, took their bowls and robes, and set out for Vesālī. Gradually, they arrived at Vesālī, the Great Wood, the Gabled Hall, where the Blessed One was staying. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side.

Tena kho pana samayena disāsu vassaṃvuṭṭhā bhikkhū kisā honti lūkhā dubbaṇṇā uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajātā dhamanisanthatagattā; vaggumudātīriyā pana bhikkhū vaṇṇavā honti pīṇindriyā pasannamukhavaṇṇā vippasannachavivaṇṇā. Āciṇṇaṃ kho panetaṃ buddhānaṃ bhagavantānaṃ āgantukehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ paṭisammodituṃ. Atha kho bhagavā vaggumudātīriye bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, bhikkhave, khamanīyaṃ kacci yāpanīyaṃ kacci samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasittha na ca piṇḍakena kilamitthā’’ti? ‘‘Khamanīyaṃ, bhagavā, yāpanīyaṃ, bhagavā. Samaggā ca mayaṃ, bhante, sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasimhā, na ca piṇḍakena kilamimhā’’ti. Jānantāpi tathāgatā pucchanti, jānantāpi na pucchanti…pe… dvīhākārehi buddhā bhagavanto bhikkhū paṭipucchanti – dhammaṃ vā desessāma, sāvakānaṃ vā sikkhāpadaṃ paññāpessāmāti. Atha kho bhagavā vaggumudātīriye bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘yathā kathaṃ pana tumhe, bhikkhave, samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasittha na ca piṇḍakena kilamitthā’’ti? Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ – ‘‘kacci pana vo, bhikkhave, bhūta’’nti? ‘‘Abhūtaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā – ‘‘ananucchavikaṃ, moghapurisā, ananulomikaṃ appatirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. Kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, udarassa kāraṇā gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissatha! Varaṃ tumhehi, moghapurisā, tiṇhena govikantanena [govikattanena (sī. ka.)] kucchiṃ parikanto, na tveva udarassa kāraṇā gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇo bhāsito! Taṃ kissa hetu? Tato nidānañhi, moghapurisā, maraṇaṃ vā nigaccheyya maraṇamattaṃ vā dukkhaṃ, na tveva tappaccayā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeya. Ito nidānañca kho, moghapurisā, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeyya. Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya’’…pe… vigarahitvā dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi –

At that time, the bhikkhus who had spent the rains in the districts were thin, haggard, pale, discolored, with veins protruding all over their bodies. But the bhikkhus from the banks of the Vaggumudā were of good complexion, with flourishing faculties, radiant complexions, and very clear skin. Now, it is customary for the Blessed Buddhas to exchange greetings with visiting bhikkhus. Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus from the banks of the Vaggumudā: "I hope, bhikkhus, you are well and comfortable, that you lived in harmony, mutual appreciation, and free from disputes during the rains, and that you did not suffer from lack of almsfood?" "We are well, Blessed One, and comfortable. We lived in harmony, mutual appreciation, and free from disputes during the rains, Venerable Sir, and we did not suffer from lack of almsfood." The Tathāgatas ask questions knowing the answers, and they also refrain from asking knowing the answers... The Blessed Buddhas question the bhikkhus for two reasons: either to teach the Dhamma or to lay down a training rule for the disciples. Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus from the banks of the Vaggumudā: "But how, bhikkhus, did you live in harmony, mutual appreciation, and free from disputes during the rains, without suffering from lack of almsfood?" Then those bhikkhus explained the matter to the Blessed One. "bhikkhus, is this true?" "It is not true, Blessed One." The Blessed One rebuked them: "It is improper, foolish men, unsuitable, unbecoming, unworthy of ascetics, and unrighteous. How could you, foolish men, for the sake of your bellies, praise one another’s superhuman qualities to householders! Foolish men, it would be better for your bellies to be slit open with a sharp butcher’s knife than for you to praise one another’s superhuman qualities to householders for the sake of your bellies! For what reason? Because of that, foolish men, you might incur death or deadly suffering, but you would not, on account of that, after the breakup of the body, be reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the nether world, hell. But because of this, foolish men, after the breakup of the body, you might be reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the nether world, hell. This, foolish men, does not inspire confidence in those without it..." Having rebuked them and given a Dhamma talk, he addressed the bhikkhus:

195. ‘‘Pañcime, bhikkhave, mahācorā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame pañca? Idha, bhikkhave, ekaccassa mahācorassa evaṃ hoti – ‘kudāssu nāmāhaṃ satena vā sahassena vā parivuto gāmanigamarājadhānīsu āhiṇḍissāmi hananto ghātento chindanto chedāpento pacanto pācento’ti! So aparena samayena satena vā sahassena vā parivuto gāmanigamarājadhānīsu āhiṇḍati hananto ghātento chindanto chedāpento pacanto pācento. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, idhekaccassa pāpabhikkhuno evaṃ hoti – ‘kudāssu nāmāhaṃ satena vā sahassena vā parivuto gāmanigamarājadhānīsu cārikaṃ carissāmi sakkato garukato mānito pūjito apacito gahaṭṭhānañceva pabbajitānañca, lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārāna’nti! So aparena samayena satena vā sahassena vā parivuto gāmanigamarājadhānīsu cārikaṃ carati sakkato garukato mānito pūjito apacito gahaṭṭhānañceva pabbajitānañca, lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, paṭhamo mahācoro santo saṃvijjamāno lokasmiṃ.

195."bhikkhus, these five great thieves exist in the world. Which five? Here, bhikkhus, a certain great thief thinks: ‘When will I roam through villages, towns, and royal cities surrounded by a hundred or a thousand men, killing, slaughtering, cutting, having others cut off, cooking, and having others cook?’ Then after some time, he roams through villages, towns, and royal cities surrounded by a hundred or a thousand men, killing, slaughtering, cutting, having others cut off, cooking, and having others cook. In the same way, bhikkhus, a certain wicked bhikkhu thinks: ‘When will I wander through villages, towns, and royal cities surrounded by a hundred or a thousand men, respected, honored, revered, venerated, and worshipped by both householders and renunciates, receiving robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites?’ Then after some time, he wanders through villages, towns, and royal cities surrounded by a hundred or a thousand men, respected, honored, revered, venerated, and worshipped by both householders and renunciates, receiving robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. This, bhikkhus, is the first great thief existing in the world."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, idhekacco pāpabhikkhu tathāgatappaveditaṃ dhammavinayaṃ pariyāpuṇitvā attano dahati. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dutiyo mahācoro santo saṃvijjamāno lokasmiṃ.

"Furthermore, bhikkhus, here some wicked bhikkhu, having learned the Dhamma and Vinaya proclaimed by the Tathagata, appropriates it to himself. This, bhikkhus, is the second great thief existing in the world."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, idhekacco pāpabhikkhu suddhaṃ brahmacāriṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carantaṃ amūlakena abrahmacariyena anuddhaṃseti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, tatiyo mahācoro santo saṃvijjamāno lokasmiṃ.

"Furthermore, bhikkhus, here some wicked bhikkhu falsely accuses a pure celibate, one living the perfectly pure spiritual life, of unchastity without any basis. This, bhikkhus, is the third great thief existing in the world."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, idhekacco pāpabhikkhu yāni tāni saṅghassa garubhaṇḍāni garuparikkhārāni, seyyathidaṃ – ārāmo ārāmavatthu vihāro vihāravatthu mañco pīṭhaṃ bhisi bimbohanaṃ [bimbohanaṃ (sī. syā.)] lohakumbhī lohabhāṇakaṃ lohavārako lohakaṭāhaṃ vāsī parasu [pharasu (sī. syā.)] kuṭhārī kudālo nikhādanaṃ valli veḷu muñjaṃ pabbajaṃ tiṇaṃ mattikā dārubhaṇḍaṃ mattikābhaṇḍaṃ, tehi gihīṃ saṅgaṇhāti upalāpeti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, catuttho mahācoro santo saṃvijjamāno lokasmiṃ.

"Furthermore, bhikkhus, here some wicked bhikkhu takes the heavy goods and heavy requisites belonging to the Saṅgha—such as a park, park land, a monastery, monastery land, a bed, a seat, a mattress, a pillow, an iron pot, an iron bowl, an iron stand, an iron cauldron, an adze, an axe, a hatchet, a spade, a mattock, vines, bamboo, muñja grass, pabbaja grass, thatch grass, clay, woodenware, or earthenware—and with these he ingratiates himself with laypeople. This, bhikkhus, is the fourth great thief existing in the world."

‘‘Sadevake, bhikkhave, loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya ayaṃ aggo mahācoro yo asantaṃ abhūtaṃ uttarimanussadhammaṃ ullapati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Theyyāya vo, bhikkhave, raṭṭhapiṇḍo bhutto’’ti.

"In the world with its gods, Māra, and Brahmā, in this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, gods and humans, this is the foremost great thief: one who claims a superhuman state that is untrue and unreal. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, he has consumed the alms of the country by theft."

[saṃ. ni. 1.35] Aññathā santamattānaṃ, aññathā yo pavedaye;Nikacca kitavasseva, bhuttaṃ theyyena tassa taṃ.

Being one thing, he declares himself to be another; like a cunning gambler, what he enjoys is gained by theft.

[dha. pa. 307 dhammapadepi] Kāsāvakaṇṭhā bahavo, pāpadhammā asaññatā;Pāpā pāpehi kammehi, nirayaṃ te upapajjare.

Many wearing the yellow robe are unrestrained and of wicked character; by their evil deeds, those evil ones are reborn in hell.

[dha. pa. 307 dhammapadepi] Seyyo ayoguḷo bhutto, tatto aggisikhūpamo;Yañce bhuñjeyya dussīlo, raṭṭhapiṇḍaṃ asaññatoti.

Better to eat an iron ball, heated like a flame, than that an immoral person should eat the alms of the land without restraint.

Atha kho bhagavā te vaggumudātīriye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya dupposatāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One, having rebuked those bhikkhus from the banks of the Vaggumudā River in many ways for being difficult to maintain, difficult to support… … And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu anabhijānaṃ uttarimanussadhammaṃ attupanāyikaṃ alamariyañāṇadassanaṃ samudācareyya – ‘iti jānāmi iti passāmī’ti, tato aparena samayena samanuggāhīyamāno vā asamanuggāhīyamāno vā āpanno visuddhāpekkho evaṃ vadeyya – ‘ajānamevaṃ, āvuso, avacaṃ jānāmi, apassaṃ passāmi. Tucchaṃ musā vilapi’nti, ayampi pārājiko hoti asaṃvāso’’ti.

"Should any bhikkhu, not knowing, claim a superior human state, a truly noble knowledge and vision, as present in himself, saying, ‘Thus I know; thus I see,’ and then at a later time, whether questioned or unasked, being desirous of purification, should say, ‘Friends, not knowing, I said I know; not seeing, I said I see—vainly, falsely, idly,’ this too is a defeat, he is no longer in communion."

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.

196. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū adiṭṭhe diṭṭhasaññino apatte pattasaññino anadhigate adhigatasaññino asacchikate sacchikatasaññino adhimānena aññaṃ byākariṃsu. Tesaṃ aparena samayena rāgāyapi cittaṃ namati dosāyapi cittaṃ namati mohāyapi cittaṃ namati. Tesaṃ kukkuccaṃ ahosi – ‘‘bhagavatā sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ. Mayañcamha adiṭṭhe diṭṭhasaññino apatte pattasaññino anadhigate adhigatasaññino asacchikate sacchikatasaññino, adhimānena aññaṃ byākarimhā. Kacci nu kho mayaṃ pārājikaṃ āpattiṃ āpannā’’ti? Te āyasmato ānandassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Āyasmā ānando bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Honti ye te, ānanda [honti yevānanda (syā.), honti te ānanda (sī.)], bhikkhū adiṭṭhe diṭṭhasaññino apatte pattasaññino anadhigate adhigatasaññino asacchikate sacchikatasaññino adhimānena aññaṃ byākaronti. Tañca kho etaṃ abbohārika’’nti.

196.At that time, several bhikkhus, perceiving the unseen as seen, the unattained as attained, the unreached as reached, the unverified as verified, declared higher attainments out of conceit. Later, their minds inclined toward lust, toward hatred, and toward delusion. They became anxious: "The Blessed One has laid down a training rule. We, perceiving the unseen as seen, the unattained as attained, the unreached as reached, the unverified as verified, declared higher attainments out of conceit. Could it be that we have committed an offense entailing defeat?" They informed Venerable Ānanda of this matter. Venerable Ānanda reported this matter to the Blessed One. "Ānanda, there are bhikkhus who, perceiving the unseen as seen, the unattained as attained, the unreached as reached, the unverified as verified, declare higher attainments out of conceit. But that is inconsequential."

‘‘Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

"Thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:"

197. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu anabhijānaṃ uttarimanussadhammaṃ attupanāyikaṃ alamariyañāṇadassanaṃ samudācareyya – ‘iti jānāmi iti passāmī’ti, tato aparena samayena samanuggāhīyamāno vā asamanuggāhīyamāno vā āpanno visuddhāpekkho evaṃ vadeyya – ‘ajānamevaṃ, āvuso, avacaṃ jānāmi, apassaṃ passāmi. Tucchaṃ musā vilapi’nti, aññatra adhimānā, ayampi pārājiko hoti asaṃvāso’’ti.

197."If a bhikkhu, without direct knowledge, boasts of a superior human state, a noble knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones, saying, 'Thus I know, thus I see,' and later, whether questioned or not, wishing to be purified, should say, 'Friends, not knowing, I said I know; not seeing, I said I see. It was empty, false babbling,' except it be from overestimation, he too is defeated, he is no longer in communion."

221.By three modes… by seven modes, a bhikkhu who used your robe… who used your alms-food… who used your lodging… who used your medicinal requisites for the sick, that bhikkhu attained the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling… attains… has attained… that bhikkhu is a gainer of the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling… a master of the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling. If, speaking a deliberate falsehood, he is aware (of the truth), he incurs a grave offense; if he is not aware, he incurs a minor offense. … abandoning his true state.

Tīhākārehi…pe… sattahākārehi yena te vihāro paribhutto… yena te cīvaraṃ paribhuttaṃ… yena te piṇḍapāto paribhutto… yena te senāsanaṃ paribhuttaṃ… yena te gilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāro paribhutto… so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajji… samāpajjati… samāpanno… so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthassa jhānassa lābhī… vasī… tena bhikkhunā suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikatanti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa paṭivijānantassa āpatti thullaccayassa; na paṭivijānantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa…pe… vinidhāya bhāvaṃ.

By three modes… by seven modes, by whom your dwelling was used… by whom your robe was used… by whom your alms-food was used… by whom your lodging was used… by whom your medicinal requisites for the sick were used, that bhikkhu attained the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling… attains… has attained… that bhikkhu is a gainer of the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling… a master of the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling. If, speaking a deliberate falsehood, he is aware (of the truth), he incurs a grave offense; if he is not aware, he incurs a minor offense. … abandoning his true state.

Tīhākārehi …pe… sattahākārehi yaṃ tvaṃ āgamma vihāraṃ adāsi… cīvaraṃ adāsi… piṇḍapātaṃ adāsi… senāsanaṃ adāsi… gilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāraṃ adāsi so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajji… samāpajjati… samāpanno… so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthassa jhānassa lābhī… vasī… tena bhikkhunā suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikatanti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa paṭivijānantassa āpatti thullaccayassa; na paṭivijānantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa. Pubbevassa hoti musā bhaṇissanti, bhaṇantassa hoti musā bhaṇāmīti, bhaṇitassa hoti musā mayā bhaṇitanti, vinidhāya diṭṭhiṃ, vinidhāya khantiṃ, vinidhāya ruciṃ, vinidhāya bhāvaṃ.

By three modes… by seven modes, because of whom you gave a dwelling… gave a robe… gave alms-food… gave lodging… gave medicinal requisites for the sick, that bhikkhu attained the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling… attains… has attained… that bhikkhu is a gainer of the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling… a master of the fourth jhana in an empty dwelling. If, speaking a deliberate falsehood, he is aware (of the truth), he incurs a grave offense; if he is not aware, he incurs a minor offense. Beforehand, he has the thought, “I will speak falsely”; while speaking, he has the thought, “I am speaking falsely”; after speaking, he has the thought, “I have spoken falsely,” abandoning his view, abandoning his acceptance, abandoning his inclination, abandoning his true state.

Vinītavatthuuddānagāthā

Summary Verses on the Cases of Discipline

Adhimāne [adhimānena (pī.)] araññamhi, piṇḍopajjhāriyāpatho;Saṃyojanā rahodhammā, vihāro paccupaṭṭhito.

Conceit, forest dwelling, alms-round, preceptor, and postures; Fetters, secluded practices, monastery, and attendance.

Na dukkaraṃ vīriyamathopi maccuno;Bhāyāvuso vippaṭisāri sammā;Viriyena yogena ārādhanāya;Atha vedanāya adhivāsanā duve.

Not difficult, effort, and also death; Fear, friend, and remorse rightly; Through effort and discipline for accomplishment; Then, two cases of enduring feelings.

Brāhmaṇe pañca vatthūni, aññaṃ byākaraṇā tayo;Agārāvaraṇā kāmā, rati cāpi apakkami.

For the brahmin, five cases; three other declarations; Obstruction of the house, sensual pleasures; and delight also ceased.

Aṭṭhi pesi ubho gāvaghātakā;Piṇḍo sākuṇiko nicchavi orabbhi;Asi ca sūkariko satti māgavi;Usu ca kāraṇiko sūci sārathi.

Bone, flesh, both cattle-killers; Lump, bird-hunter, skinless, lamb-slaughterer; Sword, swine-butcher, spear, deer-stalker; Arrow, executioner, needle, charioteer.

Yo ca sibbīyati sūcako hi so;Aṇḍabhāri ahu gāmakūṭako;Kūpe nimuggo hi so pāradāriko;Gūthakhādī ahu duṭṭhabrāhmaṇo.

He who sews, he is indeed a tailor; The egg-bearer was a village cheat; Drowned in a well was the adulterer; The dung-eater was a wicked brahmin.

Nicchavitthī aticārinī ahu;Maṅgulitthī ahu ikkhaṇitthikā;Okilinī hi sapattaṅgārokiri;Sīsacchinno ahu coraghātako.

The skinless woman was an adulteress; The crippled woman was a fortune-teller; The filthy one was a leper who covered her body with ashes; The headless one was a robber-executioner.

Bhikkhu bhikkhunī sikkhamānā;Sāmaṇero atha sāmaṇerikā;Kassapassa vinayasmiṃ pabbajaṃ;Pāpakammamakariṃsu tāvade.

bhikkhus, nuns, female trainees; Novice bhikkhus and then novice nuns; Having gone forth in Kassapa's discipline; They committed evil deeds right then.

Tapodā rājagahe yuddhaṃ, nāgānogāhanena ca;Sobhito arahaṃ bhikkhu, pañcakappasataṃ sareti.

At Tapodā in Rājagaha, a battle, and the elephant’s plunge; The venerable Sobhita, an arahant bhikkhu, remembers five hundred eons.

Vinītavatthu

Cases of Discipline

223. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu adhimānena aññaṃ byākāsi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi – ‘‘bhagavatā sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ. Kacci nu kho ahaṃ pārājikaṃ āpattiṃ āpanno’’ti? Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, adhimānenā’’ti.

223.Now at that time, a certain bhikkhu, out of conceit, declared a state of profound knowledge. He became anxious: “The Blessed One has laid down a training rule. Could it be that I have committed an offense entailing expulsion?” He reported the matter to the Blessed One. “There is no offense, bhikkhu, due to conceit.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu paṇidhāya araññe viharati – ‘‘evaṃ maṃ jano sambhāvessatī’’ti. Taṃ jano sambhāvesi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa. Na ca, bhikkhave, paṇidhāya araññe vatthabbaṃ. Yo vaseyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

Now at that time, a certain bhikkhu, having made a resolution, dwelt in the forest, thinking, “Thus people will respect me.” People respected him. He became anxious… “There is no offense, bhikkhu, entailing expulsion. But, bhikkhus, one should not dwell in the forest having made a resolution. Whoever does so commits an offense of wrong-doing.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu paṇidhāya piṇḍāya carati – ‘‘evaṃ maṃ jano sambhāvessatī’’ti. Taṃ jano sambhāvesi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa. Na ca, bhikkhave, paṇidhāya piṇḍāya caritabbaṃ. Yo careyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

Now at that time, a certain bhikkhu, intending [to gain respect], went for alms, [thinking], “In this way people will respect me.” People respected him. He became anxious… “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu. But, bhikkhus, one should not go for alms intending [to gain respect]. Whoever does so commits an offense of wrong-doing.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu aññataraṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ye, āvuso, amhākaṃ upajjhāyassa saddhivihārikā sabbeva arahanto’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘kiṃcitto tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Ullapanādhippāyo ahaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa; āpatti thullaccayassā’’ti.

Now at that time, a certain bhikkhu said to another bhikkhu: “Friend, all the pupils who live with our preceptor are arahants.” He became anxious… “What was your state of mind, bhikkhu?” “I had the intention of ingratiating, Blessed One.” “There is no offense, bhikkhu, entailing expulsion; there is an offense of grave matter.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu aññataraṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ye, āvuso, amhākaṃ upajjhāyassa antevāsikā sabbeva mahiddhikā mahānubhāvā’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘kiṃcitto tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Ullapanādhippāyo ahaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa; āpatti thullaccayassā’’ti.

Now at that time, a certain bhikkhu said to another bhikkhu: “Friend, all the pupils of our preceptor are powerful and mighty.” He became anxious… “With what intention, bhikkhu?” “I intended it as flattery, Blessed One.” “There is no offense, bhikkhu, entailing expulsion; but there is an offense of grave matter.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu paṇidhāya caṅkamati… paṇidhāya tiṭṭhati… paṇidhāya nisīdati… paṇidhāya seyyaṃ kappeti – ‘‘evaṃ maṃ jano sambhāvessatī’’ti. Taṃ jano sambhāvesi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa. Na ca, bhikkhave, paṇidhāya seyyā kappetabbā. Yo kappeyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu walked with intent… stood with intent… sat with intent… arranged his lying down with intent, thinking, “In this way, people will respect me.” People did respect him. He felt remorse… “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu. But, bhikkhus, one should not arrange one's lying down with intent. Whoever does so commits an offense of wrong-doing.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu aññatarassa bhikkhuno uttarimanussadhammaṃ ullapati. Sopi evamāha – ‘‘mayhampi, āvuso, saṃyojanā pahīnā’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno pārājika’’nti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu declared to another bhikkhu [that he had attained] a superhuman state. That other bhikkhu also said, “Friend, my fetters too have been abandoned.” He became remorseful… “bhikkhu, you have committed an offense entailing expulsion.”

224. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu rahogato uttarimanussadhammaṃ ullapati. Paracittavidū bhikkhu taṃ bhikkhuṃ apasādesi – ‘‘mā, āvuso, evarūpaṃ abhaṇi. Nattheso tuyha’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

224.At that time, a certain bhikkhu, while alone, spoke boastfully about a superhuman state. A bhikkhu who could read minds rebuked that bhikkhu: “Friend, do not speak like that. This is not yours.” He became remorseful… “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu; there is an offense of wrong-doing.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu rahogato uttarimanussadhammaṃ ullapati. Devatā taṃ bhikkhuṃ apasādesi – ‘‘mā, bhante, evarūpaṃ abhaṇi. Nattheso tuyha’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa; āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti.

At that time a certain bhikkhu, while alone, spoke of possessing a superhuman quality. A deity rebuked that bhikkhu, saying: “Venerable, do not speak thus. That is not yours.” He felt remorse… “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu, but there is an offense of wrong-doing.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu aññataraṃ upāsakaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yo, āvuso, tuyhaṃ vihāre vasati so bhikkhu arahā’’ti. So ca tassa vihāre vasati. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘kiṃcitto tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Ullapanādhippāyo ahaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa; āpatti thullaccayassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu said to a certain lay follower: “Friend, the bhikkhu who lives in your monastery is an arahant.” And that bhikkhu did live in his monastery. He became remorseful… “What was your intention, bhikkhu?” “I intended to flatter, Blessed One.” “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu; there is an offense of grave matter.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu aññataraṃ upāsakaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yaṃ tvaṃ, āvuso, upaṭṭhesi cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārena so bhikkhu arahā’’ti. So ca taṃ upaṭṭheti cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārena. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘kiṃcitto tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Ullapanādhippāyo ahaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa; āpatti thullaccayassā’’ti.

At that time a certain bhikkhu said to a certain lay follower: “Friend, the bhikkhu whom you attend to with robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites is an arahant.” And that lay follower attended to him with robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites. He became remorseful… “What was your intention, bhikkhu?” “I intended to flatter, Blessed One.” “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu; there is an offense of grave matter.”

225. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘atthāyasmato uttarimanussadhammo’’ti? ‘‘Nāvuso, dukkaraṃ aññaṃ byākātu’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi – ‘‘ye kho te bhagavato sāvakā te evaṃ vadeyyuṃ. Ahañcamhi na bhagavato sāvako. Kacci nu kho ahaṃ pārājikaṃ āpattiṃ āpanno’’ti? Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Kiṃcitto tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Anullapanādhippāyo ahaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

225.At that time, a certain bhikkhu was sick. The bhikkhus said to him, “Does the venerable one possess a superhuman state?” “Friend, it is not difficult to declare an attainment.” He became remorseful: “Those who are disciples of the Blessed One would speak like this. But I am not a disciple of the Blessed One. Could I have committed an offense entailing expulsion?” He reported the matter to the Blessed One. “What was your state of mind, bhikkhu?” “I had no intention of deceiving, Blessed One.” “There is no offense, bhikkhu, for one who had no intention of deceiving.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘atthāyasmato uttarimanussadhammo’’ti? ‘‘Ārādhanīyo kho, āvuso, dhammo āraddhavīriyenā’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu was sick. The bhikkhus said to him: “Venerable, do you possess a superhuman quality?” “Friend, the Dhamma is to be attained by one who is energetic.” He became remorseful... “There is no offense, bhikkhu, for one who had no intention of speaking boastfully.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mā kho, āvuso, bhāyī’’ti. Nāhaṃ, āvuso, maccuno bhāyāmī’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

At that time a certain bhikkhu was ill. The bhikkhus said to him, “Do not be afraid, friend.” He said, “I am not afraid of death, friend.” He had a misgiving... “There is no offense, bhikkhu, for one without the intention to brag.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mā kho, āvuso, bhāyī’’ti. ‘‘Yo nūnāvuso, vippaṭisārī assa so bhāyeyyā’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu was sick. The bhikkhus said to him, “Don’t be afraid, friend.” He said, “Only one who has remorse should be afraid, friend.” He had scruples… “There is no offense, bhikkhu, for one without the intention of boasting.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘atthāyasmato uttarimanussadhammo’’ti? ‘‘Ārādhanīyo kho, āvuso, dhammo sammāpayuttenā’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

At that time a certain bhikkhu was ill. The bhikkhus said to him, “Does the venerable have any superhuman attainments?” He replied, “The Dhamma is to be attained, friend, by one who is rightly applying himself.” He became remorseful… “There is no offense, bhikkhu, if there was no intention to boast.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘atthāyasmato uttarimanussadhammo’’ti? ‘‘Ārādhanīyo kho, āvuso, dhammo āraddhavīriyenā’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu was sick. The bhikkhus said to him, “Does the venerable have any superhuman attainments?” He replied, “The Dhamma, friend, is to be attained by one who has aroused energy.” He felt remorse… “There is no offense, bhikkhu, for one who did not have the intention to boast.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘atthāyasmato uttarimanussadhammo’’ti? ‘‘Ārādhanīyo kho, āvuso, dhammo yuttayogenā’’ti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

At that time a certain bhikkhu was ill. The bhikkhus said to him, “Does the venerable have any superhuman quality?” He replied, “The Dhamma is to be attained, friend, through appropriate effort.” He felt remorse... “There is no offense, bhikkhu, for one who did not intend to boast.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kaccāvuso, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīya’’nti? ‘‘Nāvuso, sakkā yena vā tena vā adhivāsetu’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, anullapanādhippāyassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu was sick. The bhikkhus said to him, “Is it bearable, friend? Is it getting better?” He replied, “It cannot be tolerated, friend, in any way.” He became remorseful… “There is no offense, bhikkhu, if there was no intention of exaggerating.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu gilāno hoti. Taṃ bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kaccāvuso khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīya’’nti? ‘‘Nāvuso, sakkā puthujjanena adhivāsetu’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘kiṃcitto tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Ullapanādhippāyo ahaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa; āpatti thullaccayassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu was sick. The bhikkhus said to him, “Is it bearable, friend? Is it endurable?” He replied, “It cannot be endured, friend, by an ordinary person.” He became remorseful… “What was your intention, bhikkhu?” “I intended to boast, Blessed One.” “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu, but there is a grave offense.”

226. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro brāhmaṇo bhikkhū nimantetvā etadavoca – ‘‘āyantu, bhonto arahanto’’ti. Tesaṃ kukkuccaṃ ahosi – ‘‘mayañcamha na arahanto [anarahanto (sī.)]. Ayañca brāhmaṇo amhe arahantavādena samudācarati. Kathaṃ nu kho amhehi paṭipajjitabba’’nti? Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhave, pasādabhaññe’’ti.

226.Now on that occasion, a certain brahmin invited the bhikkhus and said: “Come, venerable ones, arahants.” They became anxious, thinking: “We are not arahants, yet this brahmin addresses us as arahants. How should we proceed?” They reported the matter to the Blessed One. “There is no offense, bhikkhus, in a declaration of faith.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro brāhmaṇo bhikkhū nimantetvā etadavoca – ‘‘nisīdantu, bhonto arahanto’’ti… ‘‘bhuñjantu, bhonto arahanto’’ti… ‘‘tappentu, bhonto arahanto’’ti… ‘‘gacchantu, bhonto arahanto’’ti. Tesaṃ kukkuccaṃ ahosi – ‘‘mayañcamha na arahanto. Ayañca brāhmaṇo amhe arahantavādena samudācarati. Kathaṃ nu kho amhehi paṭipajjitabba’’nti? He kept making a sound of agony... This bhikkhu, bhikkhus, was a wicked bhikkhu in the dispensation of the Perfectly Self-Enlightened Buddha Kassapa..."

‘‘Idhāhaṃ, āvuso, gijjhakūṭā pabbatā orohanto addasaṃ bhikkhuniṃ… addasaṃ sikkhamānaṃ… addasaṃ sāmaṇeraṃ… addasaṃ sāmaṇeriṃ vehāsaṃ gacchantiṃ. Tassā saṅghāṭipi ādittā sampajjalitā sajotibhūtā, pattopi āditto sampajjalito sajotibhūto, kāyabandhanampi ādittaṃ sampajjalitaṃ sajotibhūtaṃ, kāyopi āditto sampajjalito sajotibhūto. Sā sudaṃ aṭṭassaraṃ karoti. Tassa mayhaṃ, āvuso, etadahosi – ‘acchariyaṃ vata bho, abbhutaṃ vata bho, evarūpopi nāma satto bhavissati! Evarūpopi nāma yakkho bhavissati! Evarūpopi nāma attabhāvappaṭilābho bhavissatī’’’ti! Bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘uttarimanussadhammaṃ āyasmā mahāmoggallāno ullapatī’’ti.

"Friends, as I was descending from Vulture Peak Mountain, I saw a bhikkhuni… I saw a trainee nun… I saw a novice bhikkhu… I saw a novice nun moving through the air. Her outer robe was ablaze, blazing and glowing; her alms bowl was ablaze, blazing and glowing; her belt was ablaze, blazing and glowing; her body was ablaze, blazing and glowing. She kept making a sound of agony. Then, friends, it occurred to me: ‘How astonishing, how amazing, that such a being could exist! That such a yakkha could exist! That such an obtaining of a body could exist!’" The bhikkhus complained, grumbled, and criticized: "Venerable Mahāmoggallāna is claiming superhuman qualities!"

Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘cakkhubhūtā vata, bhikkhave, sāvakā viharanti. Ñāṇabhūtā vata, bhikkhave, sāvakā viharanti. Yatra hi nāma sāvako evarūpaṃ ñassati vā dakkhati vā sakkhiṃ vā karissati! Pubbeva me sā, bhikkhave, sāmaṇerī diṭṭhā ahosi. Api cāhaṃ na byākāsiṃ. Ahañcetaṃ byākareyyaṃ pare ca me na saddaheyyuṃ. Ye me na saddaheyyuṃ tesaṃ taṃ assa dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāya. Esā, bhikkhave, sāmaṇerī kassapassa sammāsambuddhassa pāvacane pāpasāmaṇerī ahosi. Sā tassa kammassa vipākena bahūni vassāni bahūni vassasatāni bahūni vassasahassāni bahūni vassasatasahassāni niraye paccitvā tasseva kammassa vipākāvasesena evarūpaṃ attabhāvappaṭilābhaṃ paṭisaṃvedeti. Saccaṃ, bhikkhave, moggallāno āha. Anāpatti, bhikkhave, moggallānassā’’ti.

"Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "Truly, bhikkhus, the disciples live as those with vision. Truly, bhikkhus, the disciples live as those with knowledge. For, indeed, a disciple will know, see, or witness such a thing! Even before, bhikkhus, I had seen that novice nun. Yet I did not declare it. Had I declared it, others would not have believed me. For those who would not believe me, that would be for their long-term harm and suffering. That novice nun, bhikkhus, was a wicked novice nun in the dispensation of the Perfectly Self-Enlightened Buddha Kassapa. Due to the result of that kamma, having experienced suffering in hell for many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years, many hundreds of thousands of years, now, due to the remaining result of that kamma, she experiences such a regaining of existence. It is true, bhikkhus, what Moggallāna said. There is no offense, bhikkhus, for Moggallāna."

231. Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘yatāyaṃ, āvuso, tapodā sandati so daho acchodako sītodako sātodako setako suppatittho ramaṇīyo pahūtamacchakacchapo cakkamattāni ca padumāni pupphantī’’ti. Bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā mahāmoggallāno evaṃ vakkhati – ‘yatāyaṃ, āvuso, tapodā sandati so daho acchodako sītodako sātodako setako suppatittho ramaṇīyo pahūtamacchakacchapo cakkamattāni ca padumāni pupphantī’ti. Atha ca panāyaṃ tapodā kuthitā sandati. Uttarimanussadhammaṃ āyasmā mahāmoggallāno ullapatī’’ti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Yatāyaṃ, bhikkhave, tapodā sandati so daho acchodako sītodako sātodako setako suppatittho ramaṇīyo pahūtamacchakacchapo cakkamattāni ca padumāni pupphanti. Api cāyaṃ, bhikkhave, tapodā dvinnaṃ mahānirayānaṃ antarikāya āgacchati. Tenāyaṃ tapodā kuthitā sandati. Saccaṃ, bhikkhave, moggallāno āha. Anāpatti, bhikkhave, moggallānassā’’ti.

231."Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna addressed the bhikkhus: "Friends, this Tapodā flows; that pool is clear, cool, sweet, clean, with good bathing places, delightful, abounding in fish and turtles, and lotuses the size of wheels are in bloom." The bhikkhus complained, grumbled, and protested: "How can the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna say such a thing: 'Friends, this Tapodā flows; that pool is clear, cool, sweet, clean, with good bathing places, delightful, abounding in fish and turtles, and lotuses the size of wheels are in bloom'? Yet this Tapodā flows boiling hot. The Venerable Mahāmoggallāna is boasting of superhuman qualities!" They reported this matter to the Blessed One. "Bhikkhus, this Tapodā flows; that pool is clear, cool, sweet, clean, with good bathing places, delightful, abounding in fish and turtles, and lotuses the size of wheels are in bloom. However, bhikkhus, this Tapodā passes between two great hells. That is why this Tapodā flows boiling hot. Moggallāna spoke truthfully, bhikkhus. There is no offense for Moggallāna."

Tena kho pana samayena rājā māgadho seniyo bimbisāro licchavīhi saddhiṃ saṅgāmento pabhaggo ahosi. Atha rājā pacchā senaṃ saṅkaḍḍhitvā licchavayo parājesi. Saṅgāme ca nandi carati – ‘‘raññā licchavī pabhaggā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘rājā, āvuso, licchavīhi pabhaggo’’ti. Bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā mahāmoggallāno evaṃ vakkhati – ‘rājā, āvuso, licchavīhi pabhaggo’ti! Saṅgāme ca nandiṃ carati – ‘raññā licchavī pabhaggā’ti! Uttarimanussadhammaṃ āyasmā mahāmoggallāno ullapatī’’ti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Paṭhamaṃ, bhikkhave, rājā licchavīhi pabhaggo. Atha rājā pacchā senaṃ saṅkaḍḍhitvā licchavayo parājesi. Saccaṃ, bhikkhave, moggallāno āha. Anāpatti, bhikkhave, moggallānassā’’ti.

"At that time, King Seniya Bimbisāra of Magadha, while engaged in battle with the Licchavīs, was defeated. Then the king later rallied his army and defeated the Licchavīs. And in the battle, a shout of victory went around—"The Licchavīs were defeated by the king." Then Venerable Mahāmoggallāna addressed the bhikkhus: "Friends, the king was defeated by the Licchavīs." The bhikkhus complained, criticized, and protested: "How can the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna say such a thing—'Friends, the king was defeated by the Licchavīs'! And in the battle, a shout of victory goes around—'The Licchavīs were defeated by the king'! Venerable Mahāmoggallāna is claiming a superhuman state!" They reported this matter to the Blessed One. "First, bhikkhus, the king was defeated by the Licchavīs. Then the king later rallied his army and defeated the Licchavīs. What Moggallāna said is true, bhikkhus. There is no offense for Moggallāna."

232. Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘idhāhaṃ, āvuso, sappinikāya nadiyā tīre āneñjaṃ samādhiṃ samāpanno nāgānaṃ ogayha uttarantānaṃ koñcaṃ karontānaṃ saddaṃ assosi’’nti. Bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā mahāmoggallāno āneñjaṃ samādhiṃ samāpanno saddaṃ sossati! Uttarimanussadhammaṃ āyasmā mahāmoggallāno ullapatī’’ti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Attheso, bhikkhave, samādhi so ca kho aparisuddho. Saccaṃ, bhikkhave, moggallāno āha. Anāpatti, bhikkhave, moggallānassā’’ti.

232.Then Venerable Mahāmoggallāna addressed the bhikkhus: "Here, friends, while I was attaining the imperturbable samādhi on the bank of the Sappinikā River, I heard the sound of elephants plunging in and emerging, trumpeting." The bhikkhus complained, criticized, and protested: "How could Venerable Mahāmoggallāna, while attaining the imperturbable samādhi, hear a sound? Venerable Mahāmoggallāna is boasting of superhuman states!" They reported this matter to the Blessed One. "There is, bhikkhus, such a samādhi, but it is impure. Truly, bhikkhus, Moggallāna spoke the truth. There is no offense for Moggallāna."

Atha kho āyasmā sobhito bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘ahaṃ, āvuso, pañca kappasatāni anussarāmī’’ti. Bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā sobhito evaṃ vakkhati – ‘ahaṃ, āvuso, pañca kappasatāni anussarāmī’ti! Uttarimanussadhammaṃ āyasmā sobhito ullapatī’’ti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Atthesā, bhikkhave, sobhitassa. Sā ca kho ekāyeva jāti. Saccaṃ, bhikkhave, sobhito āha. Anāpatti, bhikkhave, sobhitassāti.

Then Venerable Sobhita addressed the bhikkhus: "Friends, I recall five hundred eons." The bhikkhus complained, criticized, and disparaged him: "How could Venerable Sobhita say, 'Friends, I recall five hundred eons'? Venerable Sobhita is claiming superhuman states!" They reported this matter to the Blessed One. "bhikkhus, there is such a recollection for Sobhita, but it was only one birth. Truly, bhikkhus, Sobhita spoke the truth. There is no offense for Sobhita."

Catutthapārājikaṃ samattaṃ.

The fourth pārājika is complete.

233. Uddiṭṭhā kho āyasmanto cattāro pārājikā dhammā, yesaṃ bhikkhu aññataraṃ vā aññataraṃ vā āpajjitvā na labhati bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ saṃvāsaṃ, yathā pure tathā pacchā, pārājiko hoti asaṃvāso. Tatthāyasmante pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Dutiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Tatiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Parisuddhetthāyasmanto, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmīti.

233.Venerable ones, the four pārājika rules have been recited. If a bhikkhu commits any one of these, he does not obtain the right to live in communion with the bhikkhus—as before, so after—he is pārājika and no longer in communion. In this matter, I ask the venerable ones: "Are you pure in this?" A second time I ask: "Are you pure in this?" A third time I ask: "Are you pure in this?" The venerable ones are pure in this, therefore they are silent. Thus I hold it.

Pārājikaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Pārājika is completed.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Methunādinnādānañca, manussaviggahuttari;Pārājikāni cattāri, chejjavatthū asaṃsayāti.

Sexual intercourse, taking what is not given, killing a human being, and claiming superhuman attainments; These four are defeats, matters entailing expulsion, without doubt.

Pārājikakaṇḍaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Pārājika Section is concluded.
