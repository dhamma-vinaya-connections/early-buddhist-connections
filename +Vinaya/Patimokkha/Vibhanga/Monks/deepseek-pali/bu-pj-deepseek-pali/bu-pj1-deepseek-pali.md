# BU-PJ 1

## 1. Paṭhamapārājikaṃ

## 1. The First Pārājika

Sudinnabhāṇavāro

The Recitation [Connected] with Sudinna

24. Tena kho pana samayena vesāliyā avidūre kalandagāmo nāma atthi [kalandagāmo nāma hoti (sī.), kalandagāmo hoti (syā.)]. Tattha sudinno nāma kalandaputto seṭṭhiputto hoti. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto sambahulehi [sampahūlehi (sī.)] sahāyakehi saddhiṃ vesāliṃ agamāsi kenacideva karaṇīyena. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavā mahatiyā parisāya parivuto dhammaṃ desento nisinno hoti. Addasa kho sudinno kalandaputto bhagavantaṃ mahatiyā parisāya parivutaṃ dhammaṃ desentaṃ nisinnaṃ. Disvānassa etadahosi – ‘‘yaṃnūnāhampi dhammaṃ suṇeyya’’nti. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto yena sā parisā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnassa kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa etadahosi – ‘‘yathā yathā kho ahaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, nayidaṃ sukaraṃ agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā ekantaparipuṇṇaṃ ekantaparisuddhaṃ saṅkhalikhitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ; yaṃnūnāhaṃ kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajeyya’’nti. Atha kho sā parisā bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi.

24. At that time, not far from Vesālī, there was a village named Kalandagāma. There lived Sudinna, a Kalandaputta, the son of a wealthy merchant. Then Sudinna, a Kalandaputta, accompanied by several friends, went to Vesālī on some business. At that time, the Blessed One was seated, teaching the Dhamma surrounded by a large assembly. Sudinna, a Kalandaputta, saw the Blessed One, surrounded by a large assembly, seated and teaching the Dhamma. Seeing this, he thought: "Surely I should listen to the Dhamma." Then Sudinna, a Kalandaputta, approached that assembly; having approached, he sat down to one side. As Sudinna, a Kalandaputta, was sitting to one side, this thought occurred to him: "As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, it is not easy for one living in a house to lead the perfectly complete, perfectly pure, conch-shell polished holy life. Surely I should shave off my hair and beard, put on the ochre robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness." Then that assembly, having been shown, roused, uplifted, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, rose from their seats, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and departed.

25. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto aciravuṭṭhitāya parisāya yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho sudinno kalandaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yathā yathāhaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, nayidaṃ sukaraṃ agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā ekantaparipuṇṇaṃ ekantaparisuddhaṃ saṅkhalikhitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ; icchāmahaṃ, bhante, kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Pabbājetu maṃ bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Anuññātosi pana tvaṃ, suddinna, mātāpitūhi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, bhante, anuññāto mātāpitūhi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. ‘‘Na kho, sudinna, tathāgatā ananuññātaṃ mātāpitūhi puttaṃ pabbājentī’’ti. ‘‘Sohaṃ, bhante, tathā karissāmi yathā maṃ mātāpitaro anujānissanti agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti.

25. Soon after the assembly had departed, Sudinna Kalandaputta approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated there, Sudinna Kalandaputta said to the Blessed One, "As I understand the Dhamma taught by you, Blessed One, it is not easy for one living at home to lead the perfectly complete, perfectly pure, conch-shell polished holy life. I wish, Blessed One, to shave off my hair and beard, put on the ochre robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness. Please ordain me, Blessed One." "Have you been permitted, Sudinna, by your parents to go forth from the household life into homelessness?" "No, Blessed One, I have not been permitted by my parents to go forth from the household life into homelessness." "The Tathāgatas do not ordain a son without the permission of his parents." "Then, Blessed One, I will act in such a way that my parents will permit me to go forth from the household life into homelessness."

26. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto vesāliyaṃ taṃ karaṇīyaṃ tīretvā yena kalandagāmo yena mātāpitaro tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā mātāpitaro etadavoca – ‘‘ammatātā, yathā yathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, nayidaṃ sukaraṃ agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā ekantaparipuṇṇaṃ ekantaparisuddhaṃ saṅkhalikhitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ; icchāmahaṃ kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Anujānātha maṃ agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte sudinnassa kalandaputtassa mātāpitaro sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘tvaṃ khosi, tāta sudinna, amhākaṃ ekaputtako piyo manāpo sukhedhito sukhaparihato. Na tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, kiñci dukkhassa jānāsi. Maraṇenapi mayaṃ te akāmakā vinā bhavissāma, kiṃ pana mayaṃ taṃ jīvantaṃ anujānissāma agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho sudinno kalandaputto mātāpitaro etadavoca – ‘‘ammatātā, yathā yathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, nayidaṃ sukaraṃ agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā ekantaparipuṇṇaṃ ekantaparisuddhaṃ saṅkhalikhitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ; icchāmahaṃ kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Anujānātha maṃ agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa mātāpitaro sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘tvaṃ khosi, tāta sudinna, amhākaṃ ekaputtako piyo manāpo sukhedhito sukhaparihato. Na tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, kiñci dukkhassa jānāsi. Maraṇenapi mayaṃ te akāmakā vinā bhavissāma, kiṃ pana mayaṃ taṃ jīvantaṃ anujānissāma agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti! Tatiyampi kho sudinno kalandaputto mātāpitaro etadavoca – ‘‘ammatātā, yathā yathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, nayidaṃ sukaraṃ agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā ekantaparipuṇṇaṃ ekantaparisuddhaṃ saṅkhalikhitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ; icchāmahaṃ kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Anujānātha maṃ agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Tatiyampi kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa mātāpitaro sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘tvaṃ khosi, tāta sudinna, amhākaṃ ekaputtako piyo manāpo sukhedhito sukhaparihato. Na tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, kiñci dukkhassa jānāsi. Maraṇenapi mayaṃ te akāmakā vinā bhavissāma, kiṃ pana mayaṃ taṃ jīvantaṃ anujānissāma agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti!

26. Then Sudinna the Kalandaka, having completed that business in Vesālī, went to the Kalandaka village where his parents were. Approaching them, he said: "Mother and father, as far as I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, it is not easy for one living at home to lead the perfectly complete, perfectly pure holy life, bright as a polished shell. I wish to shave off my hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness. Please give me permission to go forth from the household life into homelessness." When this was said, Sudinna’s parents replied: "Dear Sudinna, you are our only son, dear and beloved, raised in comfort, nurtured in comfort. You, dear Sudinna, know nothing of suffering. Even against our will, we would be separated from you by death—how much more so would we refuse permission while you are still alive to go forth from the household life into homelessness!" A second time, Sudinna the Kalandaka said to his parents: "Mother and father, as far as I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, it is not easy for one living at home to lead the perfectly complete, perfectly pure holy life, bright as a polished shell. I wish to shave off my hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness. Please give me permission to go forth from the household life into homelessness." A second time, Sudinna’s parents replied: "Dear Sudinna, you are our only son, dear and beloved, raised in comfort, nurtured in comfort. You, dear Sudinna, know nothing of suffering. Even against our will, we would be separated from you by death—how much more so would we refuse permission while you are still alive to go forth from the household life into homelessness!" A third time, Sudinna the Kalandaka said to his parents: "Mother and father, as far as I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, it is not easy for one living at home to lead the perfectly complete, perfectly pure holy life, bright as a polished shell. I wish to shave off my hair and beard, put on the saffron robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness. Please give me permission to go forth from the household life into homelessness." A third time, Sudinna’s parents replied: "Dear Sudinna, you are our only son, dear and beloved, raised in comfort, nurtured in comfort. You, dear Sudinna, know nothing of suffering. Even against our will, we would be separated from you by death—how much more so would we refuse permission while you are still alive to go forth from the household life into homelessness!"

27. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto – ‘‘na maṃ mātāpitaro anujānanti agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti, tattheva anantarahitāya bhūmiyā nipajji – idheva me maraṇaṃ bhavissati pabbajjā vāti. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto ekampi bhattaṃ na bhuñji, dvepi bhattāni na bhuñji, tīṇipi bhattāni na bhuñji, cattāripi bhattāni na bhuñji, pañcapi bhattāni na bhuñji, chapi bhattāni na bhuñji, sattapi bhattāni na bhuñji.

27. Then Sudinna the Kalandaka, thinking, "My parents do not permit me to go forth from the household life into homelessness," lay down right there on the bare ground, resolving: "Here I will either die or attain the going forth." Then Sudinna the Kalandaka did not eat even one meal, nor two meals, nor three meals, nor four meals, nor five meals, nor six meals, nor seven meals.

28. Atha kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa mātāpitaro sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘tvaṃ khosi, tāta sudinna, amhākaṃ ekaputtako piyo manāpo sukhedhito sukhaparihato. Na tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, kiñci dukkhassa jānāsi. Maraṇenapi mayaṃ te akāmakā vinā bhavissāma, kiṃ pana mayaṃ taṃ jīvantaṃ anujānissāma agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāya? Uṭṭhehi, tāta sudinna, bhuñja ca piva ca paricārehi ca, bhuñjanto pivanto paricārento kāme paribhuñjanto puññāni karonto abhiramassu. Na taṃ mayaṃ anujānāma agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte sudinno kalandaputto tuṇhī ahosi. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa mātāpitaro sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘tvaṃ khosi, tāta sudinna, amhākaṃ ekaputtako piyo manāpo sukhedhito sukhaparihato. Na tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, kiñci dukkhassa jānāsi. Maraṇenapi mayaṃ te akāmakā vinā bhavissāma, kiṃ pana mayaṃ taṃ jīvantaṃ anujānissāma agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāya! Uṭṭhehi, tāta sudinna, bhuñja ca piva ca paricārehi ca, bhuñjanto pivanto paricārento kāme paribhuñjanto puññāni karonto abhiramassu. Na taṃ mayaṃ anujānāma agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Tatiyampi kho sudinno kalandaputto tuṇhī ahosi.

28. Then Sudinna the Kalandaka’s parents said to him: “Dear Sudinna, you are our only son, dear and beloved, raised in comfort, nurtured in comfort. You, dear Sudinna, know nothing of suffering. Even against our will, we would be separated from you by death—how much more so would we permit you, while you are still alive, to go forth from the household life into homelessness! Rise, dear Sudinna, eat and drink and enjoy yourself. Eating and drinking, enjoying yourself, enjoying sensual pleasures, performing meritorious deeds, delight in these things. We do not permit you to go forth from the household life into homelessness.” When this was said, Sudinna the Kalandaka remained silent. A second time… A third time, Sudinna the Kalandaka’s parents said to him: “Dear Sudinna, you are our only son, dear and beloved, raised in comfort, nurtured in comfort. You, dear Sudinna, know nothing of suffering. Even against our will, we would be separated from you by death—how much more so would we permit you, while you are still alive, to go forth from the household life into homelessness! Rise, dear Sudinna, eat and drink and enjoy yourself. Eating and drinking, enjoying yourself, enjoying sensual pleasures, performing meritorious deeds, delight in these things. We do not permit you to go forth from the household life into homelessness.” A third time, Sudinna the Kalandaka remained silent.

Atha kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa sahāyakā yena sudinno kalandaputto tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘tvaṃ khosi, samma sudinna, mātāpitūnaṃ ekaputtako piyo manāpo sukhedhito sukhaparihato. Na tvaṃ, samma sudinna, kiñci dukkhassa jānāsi. Maraṇenapi te mātāpitaro akāmakā vinā bhavissanti, kiṃ pana taṃ jīvantaṃ anujānissanti agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajāya! Uṭṭhehi, samma sudinna, bhuñja ca piva ca paricārehi ca, bhuñjanto pivanto paricārento kāme paribhuñjanto puññāni karonto abhiramassu, na taṃ mātāpitaro anujānissanti agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, sudinno kalandaputto tuṇhī ahosi. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa sahāyakā sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘tvaṃ khosi, samma sudinna…pe… tatiyampi kho sudinno kalandaputto tuṇhī ahosi.

Then Sudinna the Kalandaka’s friends approached Sudinna the Kalandaka and said to him: "Friend Sudinna, you are your parents’ only son, dear and beloved, raised in comfort, nurtured in comfort. You, friend Sudinna, know nothing of suffering. Even at your death, your parents would unwillingly be parted from you—how much more so while you are alive would they permit you to go forth from the household life into homelessness! Rise, friend Sudinna, eat and drink and enjoy yourself. Eating and drinking, enjoying yourself, enjoying sensual pleasures, performing meritorious deeds, delight in these things. Your parents will not permit you to go forth from the household life into homelessness." When this was said, Sudinna the Kalandaka remained silent. A second time… A third time, Sudinna the Kalandaka’s friends said to Sudinna the Kalandaka: "Friend Sudinna… A third time, Sudinna the Kalandaka remained silent.

29. Atha kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa sahāyakā yena sudinnassa kalandaputtassa mātāpitaro tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā sudinnassa kalandaputtassa mātāpitaro etadavocuṃ – ‘‘ammatātā, eso sudinno anantarahitāya bhūmiyā nipanno – ‘idheva me maraṇaṃ bhavissati pabbajjā vā’ti. Sace tumhe sudinnaṃ nānujānissatha agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāya, tattheva maraṇaṃ āgamissati. Sace pana tumhe sudinnaṃ anujānissatha agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāya, pabbajitampi naṃ dakkhissatha. Sace sudinno nābhiramissati agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāya, kā tassa aññā gati bhavissati, idheva paccāgamissati. Anujānātha sudinnaṃ agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. ‘‘Anujānāma, tātā, sudinnaṃ agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti. Atha kho sudinnassa kalandaputtassa sahāyakā yena sudinno kalandaputto tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā sudinnaṃ kalandaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘uṭṭhehi, samma sudinna, anuññātosi mātāpitūhi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti.

29. Then Sudinna the Kalandaka’s friends approached Sudinna the Kalandaka's parents and said: "Dear mother and father, Sudinna is lying on the bare ground, thinking, ‘Here I will die or go forth.’ If you do not allow Sudinna to go forth from the household life into homelessness, he will meet his death right there. But if you allow Sudinna to go forth from the household life into homelessness, you will see him even after he has gone forth. And if Sudinna does not find satisfaction in going forth from the household life into homelessness, what other choice will he have? He will return right here. Please allow Sudinna to go forth from the household life into homelessness." "We allow, dear ones, Sudinna to go forth from the household life into homelessness." Then Sudinna the Kalandaka’s friends approached Sudinna the Kalandaka and said: "Get up, dear Sudinna, your parents have given you permission to go forth from the household life into homelessness."

30. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto – ‘‘anuññātomhi kira mātāpitūhi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāyā’’ti, haṭṭho udaggo pāṇinā gattāni paripuñchanto vuṭṭhāsi. Atha kho sudinno kalandaputto katipāhaṃ balaṃ gāhetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisanno kho sudinno kalandaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘anuññāto [anuññātomhi (sī. syā.)] ahaṃ, bhante, mātāpitūhi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāya. Pabbājetu maṃ bhagavā’’ti. Alattha kho sudinno kalandaputto bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ, alattha upasampadaṃ. Acirūpasampanno ca panāyasmā sudinno evarūpe dhutaguṇe samādāya vattati, āraññiko hoti piṇḍapātiko paṃsukūliko sapadānacāriko, aññataraṃ vajjigāmaṃ upanissāya viharati.

30. Then Sudinna the Kalandaka, thinking, "I have been permitted by my parents to go forth from the household life into homelessness," rose up joyful and elated, brushing his limbs with his hand. Then Sudinna the Kalandaka, having regained his strength after a few days, approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Sudinna the Kalandaka said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, I have been permitted by my parents to go forth from the household life into homelessness. Let the Blessed One ordain me." Sudinna the Kalandaka received the going forth from the Blessed One, and he received full ordination. Not long after his full ordination, the Venerable Sudinna undertook such ascetic practices: he was a forest dweller, an almsfood eater, a rag-robe wearer, and a house-to-house alms wanderer. He resided dependent on a certain Vajjian village.

Tena kho pana samayena vajjī dubbhikkhā hoti dvīhitikā setaṭṭhikā salākāvuttā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa etadahosi – ‘‘etarahi kho vajjī dubbhikkhā dvīhitikā setaṭṭhikā salākāvuttā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ. Bahū kho pana me vesāliyaṃ ñātī aḍḍhā mahaddhanā mahābhogā pahūtajātarūparajatā pahūtavittūpakaraṇā pahūtadhanadhaññā. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ ñātī upanissāya vihareyyaṃ! Ñātī maṃ [ñātakāpi maṃ (syā.)] nissāya dānāni dassanti puññāni karissanti, bhikkhū ca lābhaṃ lacchanti, ahañca piṇḍakena na kilamissāmī’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena vesālī tena pakkāmi. Anupubbena yena vesālī tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ āyasmā sudinno vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Assosuṃ kho āyasmato sudinnassa ñātakā – ‘‘sudinno kira kalandaputto vesāliṃ anuppatto’’ti. Te āyasmato sudinnassa saṭṭhimatte thālipāke bhattābhihāraṃ abhihariṃsu. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno te saṭṭhimatte thālipāke bhikkhūnaṃ vissajjetvā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya kalandagāmaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Kalandagāme sapadānaṃ piṇḍāya caramāno yena sakapitu nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami.

At that time, the Vajjians were suffering from a famine, 'white-boned' and with 'food measured out by ticket,' and it was not easy to sustain oneself by gleaning and gathering. Then it occurred to Venerable Sudinna: "Now the Vajjians are suffering from a famine, 'white-boned' and with 'food measured out by ticket,' and it is not easy to sustain oneself by gleaning and gathering. But I have many relatives in Vesālī who are rich, with great wealth, great possessions, abundant gold and silver, abundant goods and chattels, and abundant wealth and grain. Why don’t I dwell supported by my relatives? My relatives, supported by me, will give gifts and make merit, the bhikkhus will gain offerings, and I won’t be exhausted by almsfood." Then Venerable Sudinna, having put his lodging in order, took his bowl and robe and set out for Vesālī. Gradually, he arrived at Vesālī. There, Venerable Sudinna resided in Vesālī, in the Great Wood, at the Hall with the Gabled Roof. Venerable Sudinna’s relatives heard: "It seems Sudinna, the son of the Kalandas, has arrived in Vesālī." They brought about sixty pots of food as an offering to Venerable Sudinna. Then Venerable Sudinna distributed those sixty pots of food among the bhikkhus and, in the morning, dressed, took his bowl and robe, and entered Kalandagāma for alms. Wandering for alms in Kalandagāma, from house to house, he approached his own father’s residence.

31. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato sudinnassa ñātidāsī ābhidosikaṃ kummāsaṃ chaḍḍetukāmā [chaṭṭetukāmā (ka.)] hoti. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno taṃ ñātidāsiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sace taṃ, bhagini, chaḍḍanīyadhammaṃ, idha me patte ākirā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa ñātidāsī taṃ ābhidosikaṃ kummāsaṃ āyasmato sudinnassa patte ākirantī hatthānañca pādānañca sarassa ca nimittaṃ aggahesi. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa ñātidāsī yenāyasmato sudinnassa mātā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmato sudinnassa mātaraṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yaggheyye, jāneyyāsi, ayyaputto sudinno anuppatto’’ti. ‘‘Sace, je, tvaṃ saccaṃ bhaṇasi, adāsiṃ taṃ karomī’’ti.

31. Now at that time, a slave woman of Venerable Sudinna's relatives wanted to throw away some leftover bad porridge. Then Venerable Sudinna said to that slave woman: "Sister, if that is to be thrown away, pour it here into my bowl." Then the slave woman of Venerable Sudinna's relatives, while pouring that leftover bad porridge into Venerable Sudinna's bowl, recognized the hands, feet, and voice. Then the slave woman of Venerable Sudinna's relatives went to Venerable Sudinna's mother and said: "Madam, you should know, your son Sudinna has arrived." "If, dear, you speak the truth, I will make you a free woman."

32. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā sudinno taṃ ābhidosikaṃ kummāsaṃ aññataraṃ kuṭṭamūlaṃ [kuḍḍamūlaṃ (sī. syā.)] nissāya paribhuñjati. Pitāpi kho āyasmato sudinnassa kammantā āgacchanto addasa āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ taṃ ābhidosikaṃ kummāsaṃ aññataraṃ kuṭṭamūlaṃ nissāya paribhuñjantaṃ. Disvāna yenāyasmā sudinno tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘atthi nāma, tāta sudinna, ābhidosikaṃ kummāsaṃ paribhuñjissasi! Nanu nāma, tāta sudinna, sakaṃ gehaṃ gantabba’’nti? ‘‘Agamimha [agamamhā (ka.)] kho te gahapati, gehaṃ. Tatoyaṃ ābhidosiko kummāso’’ti. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa pitā āyasmato sudinnassa bāhāyaṃ gahetvā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ehi, tāta sudinna, gharaṃ gamissāmā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno yena sakapitu nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa pitā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘bhuñja, tāta sudinnā’’ti. ‘‘Alaṃ, gahapati, kataṃ me ajja bhattakicca’’nti. ‘‘Adhivāsehi, tāta sudinna, svātanāya bhatta’’nti. Adhivāsesi kho āyasmā sudinno tuṇhībhāvena. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.

32. At that time, Venerable Sudinna was eating that leftover bad porridge at the base of a certain wall. Venerable Sudinna’s father, who was returning from work, saw Venerable Sudinna eating that leftover bad porridge at the base of a certain wall. Having seen him, he approached Venerable Sudinna and said, “Can it be, dear Sudinna, that you are eating leftover bad porridge? Surely, dear Sudinna, you should go to your own home?” “We did go to your house, householder. This leftover bad porridge is from there.” Then Venerable Sudinna’s father took Venerable Sudinna by the arm and said, “Come, dear Sudinna, let us go home.” Then Venerable Sudinna went to his father’s residence and sat down on the prepared seat. Then Venerable Sudinna’s father said to Venerable Sudinna, “Eat, dear Sudinna.” “Enough, householder, I have already finished my meal today.” “Please accept, dear Sudinna, a meal for tomorrow.” Venerable Sudinna consented by remaining silent. Then Venerable Sudinna rose from his seat and departed.

33. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā tassā rattiyā accayena haritena gomayena pathaviṃ opuñjāpetvā [opucchāpetvā (sī. syā.)] dve puñje kārāpesi – ekaṃ hiraññassa, ekaṃ suvaṇṇassa. Tāva mahantā puñjā ahesuṃ, orato ṭhito puriso pārato ṭhitaṃ purisaṃ na passati; pārato ṭhito puriso orato ṭhitaṃ purisaṃ na passati. Te puñje kilañjehi paṭicchādāpetvā majjhe āsanaṃ paññāpetvā tirokaraṇīyaṃ parikkhipitvā āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, vadhu, yena alaṅkārena alaṅkatā puttassa me sudinnassa piyā ahosi manāpā tena alaṅkārena alaṅkarā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, ayye’’ti, kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā āyasmato sudinnassa mātuyā paccassosi.

33. Then, Venerable Sudinna’s mother, after the night had passed, had the ground smeared with fresh cow dung and had two piles made—one of silver and one of gold. The piles were so large that a man standing on this side could not see a man standing on that side; a man standing on that side could not see a man standing on this side. She had the piles covered with reed mats, prepared a seat in the middle, and screened it off. Then she addressed Venerable Sudinna’s former wife: "Well then, dear, adorn yourself with the same ornaments that made you dear and pleasing to my son Sudinna." "Yes, madam," replied Venerable Sudinna’s former wife to his mother.

34. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena sakapitu nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa pitā yenāyasmā sudinno tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te puñje vivarāpetvā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ te, tāta sudinna, mātu mattikaṃ itthikāya itthidhanaṃ, aññaṃ pettikaṃ aññaṃ pitāmahaṃ. Labbhā, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhogā ca bhuñjituṃ puññāni ca kātuṃ. Ehi tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhoge ca bhuñjassu puññāni ca karohī’’ti. ‘‘Tāta, na ussahāmi na visahāmi, abhirato ahaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carāmī’’ti. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho āyasmato sudinnassa pitā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ te, tāta sudinna, mātu mattikaṃ, itthikāya itthidhanaṃ, aññaṃ pettikaṃ, aññaṃ pitāmahaṃ. Labbhā, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhogā ca bhuñjituṃ puññāni ca kātuṃ. Ehi tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhoge ca bhuñjassu puññāni ca karohī’’ti. ‘‘Vadeyyāma kho taṃ, gahapati, sace tvaṃ nātikaḍḍheyyāsī’’ti. ‘‘Vadehi, tāta sudinnā’’ti. Tena hi tvaṃ, gahapati, mahante mahante sāṇipasibbake kārāpetvā hiraññasuvaṇṇassa pūrāpetvā sakaṭehi nibbāhāpetvā majjhe gaṅgāya sote opātehi [osādehi (sī. syā.)]. Taṃ kissa hetu? Yañhi te, gahapati, bhavissati tatonidānaṃ bhayaṃ vā chambhitattaṃ vā lomahaṃso vā ārakkho vā so te na bhavissatī’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmato sudinnassa pitā anattamano ahosi – ‘‘kathañhi nāma putto sudinno evaṃ vakkhatī’’ti!

34. Then, in the morning, Venerable Sudinna dressed, took his bowl and robe, and went to his father’s dwelling. Upon arriving, he sat down on the prepared seat. Then Venerable Sudinna’s father approached him and, uncovering the piles, said: “Dear Sudinna, this is your mother’s inheritance, a woman's wealth. Here is another from your father, and another from your grandfather. It is possible, dear Sudinna, to return to the lower life, enjoy wealth, and make merit. Come, dear Sudinna, return to the lower life, enjoy wealth, and make merit.” “Father, I cannot, I am unable. I delight in living the spiritual life.” A second time… A third time, Venerable Sudinna’s father said: “Dear Sudinna, this is your mother’s inheritance, a woman's wealth. Here is another from your father, and another from your grandfather. It is possible, dear Sudinna, to return to the lower life, enjoy wealth, and make merit. Come, dear Sudinna, return to the lower life, enjoy wealth, and make merit.” “Householder, I would speak to you if you would not be annoyed.” “Speak, dear Sudinna.” “Well then, householder, have large hemp sacks made, fill them with gold and silver, load them onto carts, and dump them into the middle of the Ganges current. Why is that? Because, householder, whatever fear, terror, goosebumps, or need for protection arises from that, that will not exist for you.” When this was said, Venerable Sudinna’s father became displeased, thinking, “How can my son Sudinna say such a thing!”

35. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa pitā āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, vadhu, tvaṃ piyā ca manāpā ca [tvampi yāca (sī.)]. Appeva nāma putto sudinno tuyhampi vacanaṃ kareyyā’’ti! Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā āyasmato sudinnassa pādesu gahetvā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kīdisā nāma tā, ayyaputta, accharāyo yāsaṃ tvaṃ hetu brahmacariyaṃ carasī’’ti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, bhagini, accharānaṃ hetu brahmacariyaṃ carāmī’’ti. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā – ‘‘ajjatagge maṃ ayyaputto sudinno bhaginivādena samudācaratī’’ti, tattheva mucchitā papatā.

35. Then Venerable Sudinna’s father addressed Venerable Sudinna’s former wife: “Well then, daughter-in-law, you are dear and pleasing. Perhaps my son Sudinna will listen to you too.” Then Venerable Sudinna’s former wife, taking Venerable Sudinna by the feet, said to him: “What sort of nymphs are they, dear sir, for whose sake you lead the spiritual life?” “Sister, it is not for the sake of nymphs that I lead the spiritual life.” Then Venerable Sudinna’s former wife, thinking, “From today onwards Venerable Sudinna addresses me as ‘sister’,” fainted and fell right there.

Atha kho āyasmā sudinno pitaraṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sace, gahapati, bhojanaṃ dātabbaṃ detha, mā no viheṭhayitthā’’ti. ‘‘Bhuñja, tāta sudinnā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā ca pitā ca āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappesuṃ sampavāresuṃ. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ bhuttāviṃ onītapattapāṇiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, tāta sudinna, kulaṃ aḍḍhaṃ mahaddhanaṃ mahābhogaṃ pahūtajātarūparajataṃ pahūtavittūpakaraṇaṃ pahūtadhanadhaññaṃ. Labbhā, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhogā ca bhuñjituṃ puññāni ca kātuṃ. Ehi tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhoge ca bhuñjassu puññāni ca karohī’’ti. ‘‘Amma, na ussahāmi na visahāmi, abhirato ahaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carāmī’’ti. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, tāta sudinna, kulaṃ aḍḍhaṃ mahaddhanaṃ mahābhogaṃ pahūtajātarūparajataṃ pahūtavittūpakaraṇaṃ pahūtadhanadhaññaṃ [pahūtadhanadhaññaṃ (pa. carāmīti) itipāṭho sabbattha natthi, ūno maññe]. Tena hi, tāta sudinna, bījakampi dehi – mā no aputtakaṃ sāpateyyaṃ licchavayo atiharāpesu’’nti. ‘‘Etaṃ kho me, amma, sakkā kātu’’nti. ‘‘Kahaṃ pana, tāta sudinna, etarahi viharasī’’ti? ‘‘Mahāvane, ammā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.

Atha kho āyasmā sudinno pitaraṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sace, gahapati, bhojanaṃ dātabbaṃ detha, mā no viheṭhayitthā’’ti. ‘‘Bhuñja, tāta sudinnā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā ca pitā ca āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappesuṃ sampavāresuṃ. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ bhuttāviṃ onītapattapāṇiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, tāta sudinna, kulaṃ aḍḍhaṃ mahaddhanaṃ mahābhogaṃ pahūtajātarūparajataṃ pahūtavittūpakaraṇaṃ pahūtadhanadhaññaṃ. Labbhā, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhogā ca bhuñjituṃ puññāni ca kātuṃ. Ehi tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhoge ca bhuñjassu puññāni ca karohī’’ti. ‘‘Amma, na ussahāmi na visahāmi, abhirato ahaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carāmī’’ti. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, tāta sudinna, kulaṃ aḍḍhaṃ mahaddhanaṃ mahābhogaṃ pahūtajātarūparajataṃ pahūtavittūpakaraṇaṃ pahūtadhanadhaññaṃ . Tena hi, tāta sudinna, bījakampi dehi – mā no aputtakaṃ sāpateyyaṃ licchavayo atiharāpesu’’nti. ‘‘Etaṃ kho me, amma, sakkā kātu’’nti. ‘‘Kahaṃ pana, tāta sudinna, etarahi viharasī’’ti? ‘‘Mahāvane, ammā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sudinno uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.

Then Venerable Sudinna said to his father: "Householder, if food is to be given, give it; do not harass us." "Eat, dear Sudinna." Then Venerable Sudinna's mother and father satisfied and pleased Venerable Sudinna with their own hands, offering him choice hard and soft food. When Venerable Sudinna had eaten and withdrawn his hand from the bowl, his mother said to him: "Dear Sudinna, this family is rich, with great wealth, abundant possessions, plenty of gold and silver, plenty of goods and equipment, plenty of wealth and grain. Dear Sudinna, it is possible to return to the lower life, to enjoy wealth and to make merit. Come, dear Sudinna, return to the lower life, enjoy wealth, and make merit." "Mother, I am not able, I cannot. I delight in living the holy life." A second time... A third time, Venerable Sudinna's mother said to him: "Dear Sudinna, this family is rich, with great wealth, abundant possessions, plenty of gold and silver, plenty of goods and equipment, plenty of wealth and grain. Therefore, dear Sudinna, at least give us an heir—do not let the Licchavis seize our property as ownerless, without an heir." "That, mother, I can do." "But where do you live now, dear Sudinna?" "In the Great Wood, mother." Then Venerable Sudinna rose from his seat and departed.

36. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, vadhu, yadā utunī hosi, pupphaṃ te uppannaṃ hoti, atha me āroceyyāsī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ ayye’’ti kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā āyasmato sudinnassa mātuyā paccassosi. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā nacirasseva utunī ahosi, pupphaṃsā uppajji. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā āyasmato sudinnassa mātaraṃ etadavoca – ‘‘utunīmhi, ayye, pupphaṃ me uppanna’’nti. ‘‘Tena hi, vadhu, yena alaṅkārena alaṅkatā puttassa sudinnassa piyā ahosi manāpā tena alaṅkārena alaṅkarā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ ayye’’ti kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā āyasmato sudinnassa mātuyā paccassosi. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikaṃ ādāya yena mahāvanaṃ yenāyasmā sudinno tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, tāta sudinna, kulaṃ aḍḍhaṃ mahaddhanaṃ mahābhogaṃ pahūtajātarūparajataṃ pahūtavittūpakaraṇaṃ pahūtadhanadhaññaṃ. Labbhā, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhogā ca bhuñjituṃ puññāni ca kātuṃ. Ehi tvaṃ, tāta sudinna, hīnāyāvattitvā bhoge ca bhuñjassu puññāni ca karohī’’ti. ‘‘Amma, na ussahāmi na visahāmi, abhirato ahaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carāmī’’ti. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho āyasmato sudinnassa mātā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, tāta sudinna, kulaṃ aḍḍhaṃ mahaddhanaṃ mahābhogaṃ pahūtajātarūparajataṃ pahūtavittūpakaraṇaṃ pahūtadhanadhaññaṃ. Tena hi, tāta sudinna, bījakampi dehi – mā no aputtakaṃ sāpateyyaṃ licchavayo atiharāpesu’’nti. ‘‘Etaṃ kho me, amma, sakkā kātu’’nti, purāṇadutiyikāya bāhāyaṃ gahetvā mahāvanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā apaññatte sikkhāpade anādīnavadasso purāṇadutiyikāya tikkhattuṃ methunaṃ dhammaṃ abhiviññāpesi. Sā tena gabbhaṃ gaṇhi. Bhummā devā saddamanussāvesuṃ – ‘‘nirabbudo vata, bho, bhikkhusaṅgho nirādīnavo; sudinnena kalandaputtena abbudaṃ uppāditaṃ, ādīnavo uppādito’’ti. Bhummānaṃ devānaṃ saddaṃ sutvā cātumahārājikā [cātummahārājikā (sī. syā.)] devā saddamanussāvesuṃ…pe… tāvatiṃsā devā… yāmā devā … tusitā devā… nimmānaratī devā… paranimmitavasavattī devā… brahmakāyikā devā saddamanussāvesuṃ – ‘‘nirabbudo vata, bho, bhikkhusaṅgho nirādīnavo; sudinnena kalandaputtena abbudaṃ uppāditaṃ, ādīnavo uppādito’’ti. Itiha tena khaṇena tena muhuttena yāva brahmalokā saddo abbhuggacchi.

36. Then Venerable Sudinna’s mother addressed his former wife: “Well then, daughter-in-law, when you are in season and the flower has arisen, then you should inform me.” “Yes, madam,” replied Venerable Sudinna’s former wife to his mother. Not long after, Venerable Sudinna’s former wife was in season, and a flower arose. Then she informed Venerable Sudinna’s mother: “Madam, I am in season, a flower has arisen.” “Well then, daughter-in-law, adorn yourself with the same ornaments that our son Sudinna found pleasing and agreeable.” “Yes, madam,” replied Venerable Sudinna’s former wife to his mother. Then Venerable Sudinna’s mother, taking his former wife, went to the Great Wood where Venerable Sudinna was staying. Approaching him, she said: “Dear Sudinna, this family is rich, with great wealth and abundant possessions, plenty of gold and silver, plenty of valuable assets and grain. It is possible, dear Sudinna, to return to the lower life, enjoy wealth, and make merit. Come, dear Sudinna, return to the lower life, enjoy wealth, and make merit.” “Mother, I cannot, I am not able. I am dedicated to living the spiritual life.” A second time… A third time, Venerable Sudinna’s mother said to him: “Dear Sudinna, this family is rich, with great wealth and abundant possessions, plenty of gold and silver, plenty of valuable assets and grain. At least, dear Sudinna, give us some seed—don’t let the Licchavis seize our inheritance as ownerless property.” “Well, mother, I can do that.” Taking his former wife by the arm, he entered the Great Wood, and, not seeing the danger in a not-yet-established training rule, he engaged in sexual intercourse with his former wife three times. She conceived from that. The earth deities cried out: “Indeed, the Saṅgha of bhikkhus is without flaw and without danger! Sudinna the Kalandaka's son has caused a flaw, a danger has arisen!” Hearing the cry of the earth deities, the deities of the Four Great Kings cried out… the Thirty-Three gods… the Yāma gods… the Tusita gods… the Nimmānarati gods… the Paranimmitavasavatti gods… the gods of Brahmā’s host cried out: “Indeed, the Saṅgha of bhikkhus is without flaw and without danger! Sudinna the Kalandaka's son has caused a flaw, a danger has arisen!” And so in that moment, in that instant, the cry rose as far as the Brahmā world.

Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikā tassa gabbhassa paripākamanvāya puttaṃ vijāyi. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa sahāyakā tassa dārakassa ‘bījako’ti nāmaṃ akaṃsu. Āyasmato sudinnassa purāṇadutiyikāya bījakamātāti nāmaṃ akaṃsu. Āyasmato sudinnassa bījakapitāti nāmaṃ akaṃsu. Te aparena samayena ubho agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitvā arahattaṃ sacchākaṃsu.

Then, in due course, Venerable Sudinna’s former wife, as the pregnancy came to term, gave birth to a son. Then Venerable Sudinna’s friends named the boy ‘Bījaka’. They named Venerable Sudinna’s former wife ‘Bījaka's Mother.’ They named Venerable Sudinna ‘Bījaka's Father.’ After some time, both of them went forth from the household life into homelessness and realized arahantship.

37. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa ahudeva kukkuccaṃ, ahu vippaṭisāro – ‘‘alābhā vata me, na vata me lābhā! Dulladdhaṃ vata me, na vata me suladdhaṃ! Yohaṃ evaṃ svākkhāte dhammavinaye pabbajitvā nāsakkhiṃ yāvajīvaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ caritu’’nti. So teneva kukkuccena tena vippaṭisārena kiso ahosi lūkho dubbaṇṇo uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto antomano līnamano dukkhī dummano vippaṭisārī pajjhāyi.

37. Then Venerable Sudinna had a misgiving, a remorse, thinking: “Truly, it is a loss for me, it is no gain for me! Truly, it is ill-gained for me, not well-gained! That I, having gone forth in such a well-proclaimed Dhamma and discipline, was unable to live the wholly perfect, utterly pure spiritual life for as long as life lasts.” Because of that misgiving, because of that remorse, he became thin, rough, ugly, yellowish-pale, his body covered with visible veins, his mind inwardly turned and dispirited, miserable, depressed, and given to remorse, and he brooded.

38. Atha kho āyasmato sudinnassa sahāyakā bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘pubbe kho tvaṃ, āvuso sudinna, vaṇṇavā ahosi pīṇindriyo pasannamukhavaṇṇo vippasannachavivaṇṇo; so dāni tvaṃ etarahi kiso lūkho dubbaṇṇo uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāto dhamanisanthatagatto antomano līnamano dukkhī dummano vippaṭisārī pajjhāyasi. Kacci no tvaṃ, āvuso sudinna, anabhirato brahmacariyaṃ carasī’’ti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, anabhirato brahmacariyaṃ carāmi. Atthi me pāpakammaṃ kataṃ; purāṇadutiyikāya methuno dhammo paṭisevito; tassa mayhaṃ, āvuso, ahudeva kukkuccaṃ ahu vippaṭisāro – ‘alābhā vata me, na vata me lābhā; dulladdhaṃ vata me, na vata me suladdhaṃ; yohaṃ evaṃ svākkhāte dhammavinaye pabbajitvā nāsakkhiṃ yāvajīvaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ caritu’’nti. ‘‘Alañhi te, āvuso sudinna, kukkuccāya alaṃ vippaṭisārāya yaṃ tvaṃ evaṃ svākkhāte dhammavinaye pabbajitvā na sakkhissasi yāvajīvaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ. Nanu, āvuso, bhagavatā anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito, no sarāgāya; visaṃyogāya dhammo desito, no saṃyogāya; anupādānāya dhammo desito, no saupādānāya. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā virāgāya dhamme desite sarāgāya cetessasi, visaṃyogāya dhamme desite saṃyogāya cetessasi, anupādānāya dhamme desite saupādānāya cetessasi! Nanu, āvuso, bhagavatā anekapariyāyena rāgavirāgāya dhammo desito, madanimmadanāya pipāsavinayāya ālayasamugghātāya vaṭṭupacchedāya taṇhākkhayāya virāgāya nirodhāya nibbānāya dhammo desito! Nanu, āvuso, bhagavatā anekapariyāyena kāmānaṃ pahānaṃ akkhātaṃ, kāmasaññānaṃ pariññā akkhātā, kāmapipāsānaṃ paṭivinayo akkhāto, kāmavitakkānaṃ samugghāto akkhāto, kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto! Netaṃ, āvuso, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya, pasannānaṃ vā bhiyyobhāvāya. Atha khvetaṃ, āvuso, appasannānañceva appasādāya pasannānañca ekaccānaṃ aññathattāyā’’ti.

38. Then Venerable Sudinna’s fellow bhikkhus said to him: “Formerly, friend Sudinna, you were handsome, with keen faculties, a clear and serene complexion. But now you are thin, wretched, pale, with veins standing out all over your body, dejected, downcast, sorrowful, distressed, remorseful, and brooding. Could it be, friend Sudinna, that you are dissatisfied with the spiritual life?” “Friends, it is not that I am dissatisfied with the spiritual life. I have committed a bad deed: I had sexual intercourse with my former wife. Because of that, friends, I have been remorseful and regretful, thinking: ‘Alas, it’s a loss for me, not a gain; it’s ill-gotten, not well-gotten, that, having gone forth in this well-proclaimed Dhamma and Vinaya, I am unable to maintain the spiritual life in its perfection and purity for life.’” “Friend Sudinna, enough of remorse and regret! That, having gone forth in this well-proclaimed Dhamma and Vinaya, you will be unable to maintain the spiritual life in its perfection and purity for life—enough! Has not the Blessed One taught the Dhamma in many ways for dispassion, not for passion; for detachment, not for attachment; for non-clinging, not for clinging? And yet here you are, friend, when the Blessed One has taught the Dhamma for dispassion, setting your mind on passion; when he has taught it for detachment, setting your mind on attachment; when he has taught it for non-clinging, setting your mind on clinging! Has not the Blessed One taught the Dhamma in many ways for the fading away of lust, for the removal of conceit, for the subduing of thirst, for the uprooting of attachment, for the cutting off of the round of existence, for the destruction of craving, for dispassion, for cessation, for Nibbāna? Has not the Blessed One taught in many ways the abandoning of sensual pleasures, the full understanding of sensual perceptions, the stilling of sensual thirst, the eradication of sensual thoughts, the calming of the fever of sensual desires? This, friend, does not lead to confidence in those without confidence or to the greater confidence of those with confidence. Rather, friend, this leads to the lack of confidence in those without confidence and to the wavering of some of those with confidence.”

39. Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, sudinna, purāṇadutiyikāya methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā – ‘‘ananucchavikaṃ [ananucchaviyaṃ (sī.)], moghapurisa, ananulomikaṃ appaṭirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. Kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, evaṃ svākkhāte dhammavinaye pabbajitvā na sakkhissasi yāvajīvaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ! Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito, no sarāgāya; visaṃyogāya dhammo desito, no saṃyogāya; anupādānāya dhammo desito, no saupādānāya! Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mayā virāgāya dhamme desite sarāgāya cetessasi, visaṃyogāya dhamme desite saṃyogāya cetessasi, anupādānāya dhamme desite saupādānāya cetessasi! Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena rāgavirāgāya dhammo desito! Madanimmadanāya pipāsavinayāya ālayasamugghātāya vaṭṭupacchedāya taṇhākkhayāya virāgāya nirodhāya nibbānāya dhammo desito! Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena kāmānaṃ pahānaṃ akkhātaṃ, kāmasaññānaṃ pariññā akkhātā, kāmapipāsānaṃ paṭivinayo akkhāto, kāmavitakkānaṃ samugghāto akkhāto, kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto! Varaṃ te, moghapurisa, āsivisassa [āsīvisassa (sī. syā.)] ghoravisassa mukhe aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ, na tveva mātugāmassa aṅgajāte aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ. Varaṃ te, moghapurisa, kaṇhasappassa mukhe aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ, na tveva mātugāmassa aṅgajāte aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ. Varaṃ te, moghapurisa, aṅgārakāsuyā ādittāya sampajjalitāya sajotibhūtāya aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ, na tveva mātugāmassa aṅgajāte aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tatonidānañhi, moghapurisa, maraṇaṃ vā nigaccheyya maraṇamattaṃ vā dukkhaṃ, na tveva tappaccayā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeyya. Itonidānañca kho, moghapurisa, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeyya. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, yaṃ tvaṃ asaddhammaṃ gāmadhammaṃ vasaladhammaṃ duṭṭhullaṃ odakantikaṃ rahassaṃ dvayaṃdvayasamāpattiṃ samāpajjissasi, bahūnaṃ kho tvaṃ, moghapurisa, akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ādikattā pubbaṅgamo. Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya, pasannānaṃ vā bhiyyobhāvāya; atha khvetaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānañceva appasādāya, pasannānañca ekaccānaṃ aññathattāyā’’ti.

39. Then those bhikkhus, having reproached the Venerable Sudinna in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and in this connection, having assembled the Sangha of bhikkhus, questioned the Venerable Sudinna: “Is it true, Sudinna, that you engaged in sexual intercourse with your former wife?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him: “It is unseemly, foolish man, unsuitable, unbecoming, unworthy of an ascetic, not allowable, not to be done. How could you, foolish man, having gone forth in this well-proclaimed Dhamma and Discipline, not be able to live the perfectly complete and pure holy life for as long as you live! Have I not, foolish man, taught the Dhamma in many ways for dispassion, not for passion; for detachment, not for attachment; for nonclinging, not for clinging! There, foolish man, when the Dhamma has been taught by me for dispassion, you think in terms of passion; when the Dhamma has been taught by me for detachment, you think in terms of attachment; when the Dhamma has been taught by me for nonclinging, you think in terms of clinging! Have I not, foolish man, taught the Dhamma in many ways for the fading away of lust: for the crushing of intoxication, for the overcoming of thirst, for the uprooting of attachment, for the cutting off of the round, for the destruction of craving, for dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna? Have I not, foolish man, taught in many ways the abandoning of sensual pleasures, the complete understanding of sensual perceptions, the overcoming of sensual thirst, the eradication of sensual thoughts, the stilling of sensual fevers? Better for you, foolish man, that your penis were thrust into the mouth of a deadly, poisonous snake than into a woman’s vagina. Better for you, foolish man, that your penis were thrust into the mouth of a black serpent than into a woman’s vagina. Better for you, foolish man, that your penis were thrust into a pit of burning coals, blazing and glowing, than into a woman’s vagina. Why is that? For on that account, foolish man, you might meet with death or with pain as severe as death, but you would not, as a result of that, on the breakup of the body, after death, be reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. But on account of this, foolish man, on the breakup of the body, after death, you are liable to be reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. How could you, foolish man, engage in such an unrighteous practice, a village practice, a base practice, a coarse practice, a water-side practice, a secret pair-by-pair practice? You, foolish man, are the originator and leader in many unwholesome things. This will not lead to faith in those without faith or to the growth of faith in those with faith; rather, foolish man, it will lead to lack of faith in those without faith and to wavering in some of the faithful.”

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ sudinnaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya dupposatāya mahicchatāya asantuṭṭhitāya [asantuṭṭhatāya (syā.)] saṅgaṇikāya kosajjassa avaṇṇaṃ bhāsitvā anekapariyāyena subharatāya suposatāya appicchassa santuṭṭhassa sallekhassa dhutassa pāsādikassa apacayassa vīriyārambhassa [vīriyārabbhassa (ka.)] vaṇṇaṃ bhāsitvā bhikkhūnaṃ tadanucchavikaṃ tadanulomikaṃ dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññapessāmi [paññāpessāmi (sī. syā.)] dasa atthavase paṭicca – saṅghasuṭṭhutāya, saṅghaphāsutāya, dummaṅkūnaṃ puggalānaṃ niggahāya, pesalānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ phāsuvihārāya, diṭṭhadhammikānaṃ āsavānaṃ saṃvarāya, samparāyikānaṃ āsavānaṃ paṭighātāya, appasannānaṃ pasādāya, pasannānaṃ bhiyyobhāvāya, saddhammaṭṭhitiyā, vinayānuggahāya. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One, in many ways, rebuked Venerable Sudinna, criticizing him for being difficult to support, difficult to maintain, full of desires, discontented, fond of company, and lazy. In many ways, he spoke in praise of being easy to support, easy to maintain, having few wishes, content, austere, detached, inspiring confidence, reducing [defilements], and initiating effort. Then, having given the bhikkhus a Dhamma talk appropriate and suited to the occasion, he addressed them, "Well then, bhikkhus, I will formulate a training rule for bhikkhus, motivated by ten considerations: for the well-being of the Saṅgha, for the comfort of the Saṅgha, for the restraint of shameless individuals, for the ease of well-behaved bhikkhus, for the restraint of defilements in this very life, for preventing future defilements, for inspiring confidence in those who lack it, for the increase of confidence in those who have it, for the endurance of the true Dhamma, and for the support of the discipline. And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭiseveyya, pārājiko hoti asaṃvāso’’ti.

Should any bhikkhu engage in sexual intercourse, he is defeated and no longer in communion.

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.

Sudinnabhāṇavāro niṭṭhito.

The Recitation [Connected] with Sudinna is finished.

Makkaṭīvatthu

The Story of the Female bhikkhuey

40. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu vesāliyaṃ mahāvane makkaṭiṃ āmisena upalāpetvā tassā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevati. Atha kho so bhikkhu pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaraṃ ādāya vesāliṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū senāsanacārikaṃ āhiṇḍantā yena tassa bhikkhuno vihāro tenupasaṅkamiṃsu. Addasa kho sā makkaṭī te bhikkhū dūratova āgacchante. Disvāna yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ purato kaṭimpi cālesi cheppampi cālesi, kaṭimpi oḍḍi, nimittampi akāsi. Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘nissaṃsayaṃ kho so bhikkhu imissā makkaṭiyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevatī’’ti. Ekamantaṃ nilīyiṃsu. Atha kho so bhikkhu vesāliyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā piṇḍapātaṃ ādāya paṭikkami.

40. Now at that time a certain bhikkhu in the Great Wood at Vesālī enticed a female bhikkhuey with food and engaged in the sexual act with her. Then that bhikkhu, having dressed in the morning and taken his bowl and robe, entered Vesālī for alms. At that time, several bhikkhus wandering on a tour of the lodgings approached that bhikkhu’s dwelling. That female bhikkhuey saw those bhikkhus coming from a distance. Having seen them, she approached the bhikkhus; having approached, she swayed her hips in front of those bhikkhus, swayed her tail, presented her hips, and made suggestive gestures. Then those bhikkhus thought, "Surely this bhikkhu is engaging in the sexual act with this female bhikkhuey." They hid to one side. Then that bhikkhu, having walked for alms in Vesālī and taken his almsfood, returned.

41. Atha kho sā makkaṭī yena so bhikkhu tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho so bhikkhu taṃ piṇḍapātaṃ ekadesaṃ bhuñjitvā ekadesaṃ tassā makkaṭiyā adāsi. Atha kho sā makkaṭī taṃ piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjitvā tassa bhikkhuno kaṭiṃ oḍḍi. Atha kho so bhikkhu tassā makkaṭiyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevati. Atha kho te bhikkhū taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘nanu, āvuso, bhagavatā sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ; kissa tvaṃ, āvuso, makkaṭiyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ; tañca kho manussitthiyā, no tiracchānagatāyā’’ti. ‘‘Nanu, āvuso, tatheva taṃ hoti. Ananucchavikaṃ, āvuso, ananulomikaṃ appaṭirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. Kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, āvuso, evaṃ svākkhāte dhammavinaye pabbajitvā na sakkhissasi yāvajīvaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ! Nanu, āvuso, bhagavatā anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito, no sarāgāya…pe… kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto! Netaṃ, āvuso, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya pasannānaṃ vā bhiyyobhāvāya. Atha khvetaṃ, āvuso, appasannānañceva appasādāya, pasannānañca ekaccānaṃ aññathattāyā’’ti. Atha kho te bhikkhū taṃ bhikkhuṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ arocesuṃ.

41. Then that female bhikkhuey approached the bhikkhu. The bhikkhu ate a portion of the almsfood and gave a portion to the female bhikkhuey. After eating the almsfood, the female bhikkhuey presented her hips to the bhikkhu. Then the bhikkhu engaged in sexual activity with her. The other bhikkhus said to him, “Friend, hasn’t the Blessed One laid down a training rule? Why, friend, do you engage in sexual activity with a bhikkhuey?” He replied, “It is true, friends, that the Blessed One has laid down a training rule—but that is for human females, not for animal females.” They said, “Friend, it is just the same. It is unsuitable, improper, unbecoming, unworthy of a recluse, unlawful, and should not be done. How could you, friend, having gone forth in this well-proclaimed Dhamma and Discipline, be unable to live the completely perfect and pure spiritual life as long as you live! “Friend, hasn’t the Blessed One taught the Dhamma in many ways for the sake of dispassion, not for passion? … The stilling of sensual fever has been proclaimed! “This will not lead to confidence in those who are without confidence, nor to increase of confidence in those who have confidence. Rather, friend, it will lead to non-confidence in those who are without confidence, and to change of heart in some of those who have confidence.” Having rebuked that bhikkhu in many ways, the bhikkhus informed the Blessed One of the matter.

42. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, makkaṭiyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā – ‘‘ananucchavikaṃ, moghapurisa, ananulomikaṃ appatirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. Kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, evaṃ svākkhāte dhammavinaye pabbajitvā na sakkhissasi yāvajīvaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carituṃ! Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena virāgāya dhammo desito, no sarāgāya …pe… kāmapariḷāhānaṃ vūpasamo akkhāto! Varaṃ te, moghapurisa, āsīvisassa ghoravisassa mukhe aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ, na tveva makkaṭiyā aṅgajāte aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ. Varaṃ te, moghapurisa, kaṇhasappassa mukhe aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ, na tveva makkaṭiyā aṅgajāte aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ. Varaṃ te, moghapurisa, aṅgārakāsuyā ādittāya sampajjalitāya sajotibhūtāya aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ, na tveva makkaṭiyā aṅgajāte aṅgajātaṃ pakkhittaṃ. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tatonidānañhi, moghapurisa, maraṇaṃ vā nigaccheyya maraṇamattaṃ vā dukkhaṃ; na tveva tappaccayā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeyya. Itonidānañca kho, moghapurisa, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeyya. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, yaṃ tvaṃ asaddhammaṃ gāmadhammaṃ vasaladhammaṃ duṭṭhullaṃ odakantikaṃ rahassaṃ dvayaṃdvayasamāpattiṃ samāpajjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

42. Then the Blessed One, regarding this incident, regarding this occurrence, convened the Sangha of bhikkhus and questioned that bhikkhu: "Is it true, bhikkhu, that you engaged in sexual activity with a female bhikkhuey?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked him: "It is unseemly, foolish man, unsuitable, improper, unworthy of a recluse, unlawful, not to be done. How could you, foolish man, having gone forth in such a well-proclaimed Dhamma and Discipline, be unable to live the utterly complete and pure spiritual life as long as you live! Have I not, foolish man, taught the Dhamma in many ways for dispassion, not for passion? ... The stilling of sensual fever has been declared! Better for you, foolish man, to have your penis thrust into the mouth of a deadly, fiercely venomous snake than into the vulva of a female bhikkhuey. Better for you, foolish man, to have your penis thrust into the mouth of a black snake than into the vulva of a female bhikkhuey. Better for you, foolish man, to have your penis thrust into a charcoal pit, burning, blazing, and glowing than into the vulva of a female bhikkhuey. Why is that? Because from that cause, foolish man, one might meet with death or pain equal to death; but one would not, on account of that, after the breakup of the body, after death, be reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell. But from this cause, foolish man, after the breakup of the body, after death, one would be reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell. How could you, foolish man, engage in such unrighteous conduct, vulgar conduct, low conduct, coarse, culminating in the emission of semen, secret, an act between two! This will not, foolish man, lead to confidence in those who lack it... And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭiseveyya antamaso tiracchānagatāyapi, pārājiko hoti asaṃvāso’’ti.

"Should any bhikkhu engage in sexual intercourse, even with a female animal, he is defeated and no longer in affiliation."

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.

Makkaṭīvatthu niṭṭhitaṃ.

The story of the female bhikkhuey is concluded.

Yāvadatthaṃ bhuñjitvā yāvadatthaṃ supitvā yāvadatthaṃ nhāyitvā ayoniso manasi karitvā sikkhaṃ apaccakkhāya dubbalyaṃ anāvikatvā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭiseviṃsu. Te aparena samayena ñātibyasanenapi phuṭṭhā bhogabyasanenapi phuṭṭhā rogabyasanenapi phuṭṭhā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘na mayaṃ, bhante ānanda, buddhagarahino na dhammagarahino na saṅghagarahino; attagarahino mayaṃ, bhante ānanda, anaññagarahino. Idāni cepi [idānipi ce (syā.)] mayaṃ, bhante ānanda, labheyyāma bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ labheyyāma upasampadaṃ, idānipi mayaṃ vipassakā kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pubbarattāpararattaṃ bodhipakkhikānaṃ dhammānaṃ bhāvanānuyogamanuyuttā vihareyyāma. Sādhu, bhante ānanda, bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocehī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho āyasmā ānando vesālikānaṃ vajjiputtakānaṃ paṭissuṇitvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi.

43. Now at that time, several Vajjiputtaka bhikkhus from Vesālī ate to their fill, slept to their fill, and bathed to their fill. Having eaten to their fill, slept to their fill, and bathed to their fill, without proper reflection, without renouncing the training, and without declaring their weakness, they engaged in sexual intercourse. Later, those bhikkhus were afflicted by the loss of relatives, the loss of wealth, and the loss of health. They approached Venerable Ānanda and said: "Venerable Ānanda, we do not disparage the Buddha, the Dhamma, or the Saṅgha; we disparage ourselves, Venerable Ānanda, not others. We are indeed unfortunate and of little merit, that having gone forth in this well-proclaimed Dhamma and Discipline, we were unable to live the holy life perfectly and purely for the rest of our lives. If now, Venerable Ānanda, we could receive the going forth in the presence of the Blessed One, if we could receive full ordination, even now we would be discerning of wholesome qualities and devoted to the cultivation of the factors of enlightenment, practicing them during the first and last watches of the night. Please, Venerable Ānanda, report this matter to the Blessed One." "Yes, friends," Venerable Ānanda replied to the Vesalian Vajjiputtaka bhikkhus, and he approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he reported the matter to the Blessed One.

‘‘Aṭṭhānametaṃ, ānanda, anavakāso yaṃ tathāgato vajjīnaṃ vā vajjiputtakānaṃ vā kāraṇā sāvakānaṃ pārājikaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ samūhaneyyā’’ti.

"It is impossible, Ananda, there is no such possibility for the Tathagata to revoke a training rule entailing defeat for his disciples because of the Vajjis or the Vajjian princes."

Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘yo, bhikkhave [yo pana bhikkhave bhikkhu (sī.) yo kho bhikkhave bhikkhu (syā.)], sikkhaṃ apaccakkhāya dubbalyaṃ anāvikatvā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevati so āgato na upasampādetabbo; yo ca kho, bhikkhave [yo ca kho bhikkhave bhikkhu (sī. syā.)], sikkhaṃ paccakkhāya dubbalyaṃ āvikatvā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevati so āgato upasampādetabbo. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in connection with this matter, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: "bhikkhus, one who, without having renounced the training and without declaring weakness, engages in sexual intercourse, is not to be given the higher ordination. But one who, having renounced the training and declared weakness, engages in sexual intercourse, is to be given the higher ordination." And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

44. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāsājīvasamāpanno sikkhaṃ apaccakkhāya dubbalyaṃ anāvikatvā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭiseveyya antamaso tiracchānagatāyapi, pārājiko hoti asaṃvāso’’ti.

44."Should any bhikkhu, while sharing in the training and livelihood of bhikkhus, engage in sexual intercourse without having renounced the training and without declaring weakness, even with a female animal, he is defeated and separated from communion."

45. Yo panāti yo yādiso yathāyutto yathājacco yathānāmo yathāgotto yathāsīlo yathāvihārī yathāgocaro thero vā navo vā majjhimo vā. Eso vuccati ‘yo panā’ti.

45."Yo panā" refers to any individual — whatever their nature, condition, birth, name, lineage, conduct, dwelling, or range — whether senior, junior, or middle. This is what is meant by "yo panā."

[vibha. 510, jhānavibhaṅgepi] Bhikkhūti bhikkhakoti bhikkhu, bhikkhācariyaṃ ajjhupagatoti bhikkhu, bhinnapaṭadharoti bhikkhu, samaññāya bhikkhu, paṭiññāya bhikkhu, ehi bhikkhūti bhikkhu, tīhi saraṇagamanehi upasampannoti bhikkhu, bhadro bhikkhu, sāro bhikkhu, sekho bhikkhu, asekho bhikkhu, samaggena saṅghena ñatticatutthena kammena akuppena ṭhānārahena upasampannoti bhikkhu. Tatra yvāyaṃ bhikkhu samaggena saṅghena ñatticatutthena kammena akuppena ṭhānārahena upasampanno, ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

A bhikkhu is so called because he is a beggar; a bhikkhu because he has undertaken the practice of begging; a bhikkhu because he wears robes made of patches; a bhikkhu by designation; a bhikkhu by proclamation; a bhikkhu by the invitation "Come, bhikkhu"; a bhikkhu ordained by taking the three refuges; a noble bhikkhu; a bhikkhu of substance; a sekha bhikkhu; an asekha bhikkhu; a bhikkhu ordained by a fully constituted Sangha through a formal procedure requiring a motion and three announcements, which is unshakable and stands firm. Herein, the bhikkhu ordained by a fully constituted Sangha through a formal procedure requiring a motion and three announcements, which is unshakable and stands firm—this is the bhikkhu intended in this context.

[dī. ni. 3.305] Sikkhāti tisso sikkhā – adhisīlasikkhā, adhicittasikkhā, adhipaññāsikkhā. Tatra yāyaṃ adhisīlasikkhā, ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippetā sikkhāti.

There are three trainings: the training in higher virtue, the training in higher mind, and the training in higher wisdom. Of these, the training in higher virtue is the training meant in this context.

Sājīvaṃ nāma yaṃ bhagavatā paññattaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ, etaṃ sājīvaṃ nāma. Tasmiṃ sikkhati, tena vuccati sājīvasamāpannoti.

The training rule laid down by the Blessed One is called proper living. One who trains in it is called one who has attained proper living.

Sikkhaṃ apaccakkhāya dubbalyaṃ anāvikatvāti atthi, bhikkhave, dubbalyāvikammañceva hoti sikkhā ca apaccakkhātā; atthi, bhikkhave, dubbalyāvikammañceva hoti sikkhā ca paccakkhātā.

There is, bhikkhus, both the declaration of infirmity and the training not renounced; there is, bhikkhus, both the declaration of infirmity and the training renounced.

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, dubbalyāvikammañceva hoti sikkhā ca apaccakkhātā. Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ukkaṇṭhito anabhirato sāmaññā cavitukāmo bhikkhubhāvaṃ aṭṭīyamāno harāyamāno jigucchamāno gihibhāvaṃ patthayamāno upāsakabhāvaṃ patthayamāno ārāmikabhāvaṃ patthayamāno sāmaṇerabhāvaṃ patthayamāno titthiyabhāvaṃ patthayamāno titthiyasāvakabhāvaṃ patthayamāno assamaṇabhāvaṃ patthayamāno asakyaputtiyabhāvaṃ patthayamāno – ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ buddhaṃ paccakkheyya’nti vadati viññāpeti. Evampi, bhikkhave, dubbalyāvikammañceva hoti sikkhā ca apaccakkhātā.

And how, bhikkhus, is there both the declaration of infirmity and the training not renounced? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is discontented, dissatisfied, wishing to leave the ascetic life, troubled by the state of a bhikkhu, vexed, disgusted, desiring household life, desiring the state of a lay follower, desiring the state of a monastery attendant, desiring the state of a novice, desiring the state of a sectarian, desiring the state of a sectarian disciple, desiring the state of a non-ascetic, desiring the state of a non-follower of the Sakyans—he says and makes known, 'Perhaps I should renounce the Buddha.' Thus, bhikkhus, there is both the declaration of infirmity and the training not renounced.

‘‘Atha vā pana ukkaṇṭhito anabhirato sāmaññā cavitukāmo bhikkhubhāvaṃ aṭṭīyamāno harāyamāno jigucchamāno gihibhāvaṃ patthayamāno…pe… asakyaputtiyabhāvaṃ patthayamāno – ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ dhammaṃ paccakkheyya’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… yaṃnūnāhaṃ saṅghaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ sikkhaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ vinayaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ pātimokkhaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ uddesaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ upajjhāyaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ ācariyaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ saddhivihārikaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ antevāsikaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ samānupajjhāyakaṃ… yaṃnūnāhaṃ samānācariyakaṃ yaṃnūnāhaṃ sabrahmacāriṃ paccakkheyya’nti vadati viññāpeti. ‘Yaṃnūnāhaṃ gihī assa’nti vadati viññāpeti. ‘Yaṃnūnāhaṃ upāsako assa’nti… ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ ārāmiko assa’nti… ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ sāmaṇero assa’nti… ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ titthiyo assa’nti… ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ titthiyasāvako assa’nti… ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ assamaṇo assa’nti… ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ asakyaputtiyo assa’nti vadati viññāpeti. Evampi, bhikkhave, dubbalyāvikammañceva hoti sikkhā ca apaccakkhātā.

Or else, weary, discontented, longing to fall away from the contemplative life, afflicted by the state of a bhikkhu, ashamed, disgusted, desiring the life of a householder… desiring the state of a non-Sakyan—he says and makes known, ‘Perhaps I should renounce the Dhamma’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the Sangha’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the training’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the discipline’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the recitation of the Pātimokkha’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the teaching’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the preceptor’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the teacher’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the fellow resident’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the junior pupil’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the co-preceptor’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the co-teacher’… ‘Perhaps I should renounce the fellow monastic.’ ‘Perhaps I should become a householder,’ he says and makes known. ‘Perhaps I should become a lay follower’… ‘Perhaps I should become a monastery attendant’… ‘Perhaps I should become a novice’… ‘Perhaps I should become a sectarian’… ‘Perhaps I should become a sectarian follower’… ‘Perhaps I should become a non-recluse’… ‘Perhaps I should become a non-Sakyan,’ he says and makes known. 46. ‘‘Atha vā pana ukkaṇṭhito anabhirato sāmaññā cavitukāmo bhikkhubhāvaṃ aṭṭīyamāno harāyamāno jigucchamāno gihibhāvaṃ patthayamāno…pe… asakyaputtiyabhāvaṃ patthayamāno – ‘yadi panāhaṃ buddhaṃ paccakkheyya’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… ‘yadi panāhaṃ asakyaputtiyo assa’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… ‘apāhaṃ buddhaṃ paccakkheyya’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… ‘apāhaṃ asakyaputtiyo assa’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… ‘handāhaṃ buddhaṃ paccakkheyya’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… ‘handāhaṃ asakyaputtiyo assa’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… ‘hoti me buddhaṃ paccakkheyya’nti vadati viññāpeti…pe… ‘hoti me asakyaputtiyo assa’nti vadati viññāpeti. 54.“And how, bhikkhus, is the training not renounced? Here, bhikkhus, by the same characteristics, signs, and indications by which the training is renounced, a madman renounces the training—the training is not renounced. In the presence of someone mentally deranged, one renounces the training—the training is not renounced. One renounces the training while overwhelmed by painful feeling—the training is not renounced. In the presence of someone overwhelmed by painful feeling, one renounces the training—the training is not renounced. In the presence of a deity, one renounces the training—the training is not renounced. In the presence of an animal, one renounces the training—the training is not renounced. A noble person renounces the training in the presence of a barbarian, and that person does not understand—the training is not renounced. A barbarian renounces the training in the presence of a noble person, and that person does not understand—the training is not renounced. A noble person renounces the training in the presence of a noble person, and that person does not understand—the training is not renounced. A barbarian renounces the training in the presence of a barbarian, and that person does not understand—the training is not renounced. One renounces the training while speaking suddenly and unintentionally—the training is not renounced. One renounces the training while speaking indistinctly—the training is not renounced. Not wishing to make it heard, one makes it heard—the training is not renounced. Wishing to make it heard, one does not make it heard—the training is not renounced. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu nāgiyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevi… yakkhiniyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevi… petiyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevi … paṇḍakassa methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno pārājika’’nti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu engaged in sexual intercourse with a nāga woman… with a yakkhini… with a female ghost… with a hermaphrodite. He felt remorse… “You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing expulsion.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu upahatindriyo hoti. So – ‘nāhaṃ vediyāmi [vedayāmi (ka.)] sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā, anāpatti me bhavissatī’ti, methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevi. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Vedayi vā so, bhikkhave, moghapuriso na vā vedayi, āpatti pārājikassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu had impaired faculties. He, thinking, 'I do not feel pleasure or pain, so there will be no offense for me,' engaged in sexual intercourse. They reported this matter to the Blessed One. 'Whether that foolish man felt it or not, bhikkhus, there is an offense entailing expulsion.'

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu – ‘itthiyā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevissāmī’ti, chupitamatte vippaṭisārī ahosi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa. Āpatti saṅghādisesassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu, thinking, 'I will engage in sexual intercourse with a woman,' experienced remorse as soon as he was touched. He felt remorse… “There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu, but there is an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Sangha.”

74. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu bhaddiye jātiyāvane divāvihāragato nipanno hoti. Tassa aṅgamaṅgāni vātūpatthaddhāni honti. Aññatarā itthī passitvā aṅgajāte abhinisīditvā yāvadatthaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Bhikkhū kilinnaṃ passitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, ākārehi aṅgajātaṃ kammaniyaṃ hoti – rāgena, vaccena, passāvena, vātena, uccāliṅgapāṇakadaṭṭhena. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahākārehi aṅgajātaṃ kammaniyaṃ hoti. Aṭṭhānametaṃ, bhikkhave, anavakāso yaṃ tassa bhikkhuno rāgena aṅgajātaṃ kammaniyaṃ assa. Arahaṃ so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu. Anāpatti, bhikkhave, tassa bhikkhuno’’ti.

74.Now at that time, a certain bhikkhu, having gone to the Jātiyavana grove in Bhaddiya for daytime abiding, lay down. His genitals were stiffened by wind. A certain woman, seeing him, sat on his genitals, did as she pleased, and departed. The bhikkhus, seeing him soiled, reported this matter to the Blessed One. 'bhikkhus, the genitals become functional for five reasons: by lust, by feces, by urine, by wind, or by the bite of a creeping creature. These, bhikkhus, are the five reasons for which the genitals become functional. It is impossible, it is not possible, bhikkhus, that the genitals of that bhikkhu became functional by lust. That bhikkhu, bhikkhus, is an arahant. There is no offense, bhikkhus, for that bhikkhu.'

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu sāvatthiyā andhavane divāvihāragato nipanno hoti. Aññatarā gopālikā passitvā aṅgajāte abhinisīdi. So bhikkhu pavesanaṃ sādiyi, paviṭṭhaṃ sādiyi, ṭhitaṃ sādiyi, uddharaṇaṃ sādiyi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno pārājika’’nti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu in Andhavana near Sāvatthī had gone for daytime abiding and was lying down. A certain cowherd woman saw him and sat on his genitals. The bhikkhu consented to the entry, consented to the penetration, consented to the staying, and consented to the withdrawal. He felt remorse… “You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing expulsion.”

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu sāvatthiyā andhavane divāvihāragato nipanno hoti. Aññatarā ajapālikā passitvā… aññatarā kaṭṭhahārikā passitvā… aññatarā gomayahārikā passitvā aṅgajāte abhinisīdi. So bhikkhu pavesanaṃ sādiyi, paviṭṭhaṃ sādiyi, ṭhitaṃ sādiyi, uddharaṇaṃ sādiyi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno pārājika’’nti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu in Andhavana near Sāvatthī had gone for daytime abiding and was lying down. A certain goat-herd woman saw him… a certain wood-gatherer saw him… a certain cow-dung gatherer saw him and sat on his genitals. The bhikkhu consented to the entry, consented to the penetration, consented to the staying, and consented to the withdrawal. He felt remorse… “You, bhikkhu, have committed an offense entailing expulsion.”

75. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu vesāliyaṃ mahāvane divāvihāragato nipanno hoti. Aññatarā itthī passitvā aṅgajāte abhinisīditvā yāvadatthaṃ katvā sāmantā hasamānā ṭhitā hoti. So bhikkhu paṭibujjhitvā taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tuyhidaṃ kamma’’nti? ‘‘Āma, mayhaṃ kamma’’nti. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘sādiyi tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhagavā, jānāmī’’ti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, ajānantassā’’ti.

75.At that time, a certain bhikkhu in the Great Wood near Vesālī had gone for daytime abiding and was lying down. A certain woman saw him, sat on his genitals, fulfilled her desire, and stood nearby laughing. The bhikkhu woke up and said to her, 'Is this your doing?' 'Yes, it is my doing,' she replied. He felt remorse… 'Did you consent, bhikkhu?' 'No, Blessed One, I did not know.' 'There is no offense for one who is unaware.'

76. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu vesāliyaṃ mahāvane divāvihāragato rukkhaṃ apassāya nipanno hoti. Aññatarā itthī passitvā aṅgajāte abhinisīdi. So bhikkhu sahasā vuṭṭhāsi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘sādiyi tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhagavā, sādiyi’’nti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, asādiyantassā’’ti.

76.At that time, a certain bhikkhu in the Great Wood near Vesālī had gone for daytime abiding and was lying down without leaning against a tree. A certain woman saw him and sat on his genitals. The bhikkhu suddenly got up. He felt remorse… 'Did you consent, bhikkhu?' 'I did not consent, Blessed One.' 'There is no offense for one who does not consent.'

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu vesāliyaṃ mahāvane divāvihāragato rukkhaṃ apassāya nipanno hoti. Aññatarā itthī passitvā aṅgajāte abhinisīdi. So bhikkhu akkamitvā pavattesi [pavaṭṭesi (sī. syā.)]. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘sādiyi tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhagavā, sādiyi’’nti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, asādiyantassā’’ti.

At that time, a certain bhikkhu in the Great Wood near Vesālī had gone for daytime abiding and was lying down without leaning against a tree. A certain woman saw him and sat on his genitals. The bhikkhu rolled about. He felt remorse… 'Did you consent, bhikkhu?' 'I did not consent, Blessed One.' 'There is no offense for one who does not consent.'

77. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu vesāliyaṃ mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ divāvihāragato dvāraṃ vivaritvā nipanno hoti. Tassa aṅgamaṅgāni vātūpatthaddhāni honti. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā itthiyo gandhañca mālañca ādāya ārāmaṃ āgamaṃsu vihārapekkhikāyo. Atha kho tā itthiyo taṃ bhikkhuṃ passitvā aṅgajāte abhinisīditvā yāvadatthaṃ katvā, purisūsabho vatāyanti vatvā gandhañca mālañca āropetvā pakkamiṃsu. Bhikkhū kilinnaṃ passitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, ākārehi aṅgajātaṃ kammaniyaṃ hoti – rāgena, vaccena, passāvena, vātena, uccāliṅgapāṇakadaṭṭhena. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahākārehi aṅgajātaṃ kammaniyaṃ hoti. Aṭṭhānametaṃ, bhikkhave, anavakāso, yaṃ tassa bhikkhuno rāgena aṅgajātaṃ kammaniyaṃ assa. Arahaṃ so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu. Anāpatti, bhikkhave, tassa bhikkhuno. Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, divā paṭisallīyantena dvāraṃ saṃvaritvā paṭisallīyitu’’nti.

77.At that time, a certain bhikkhu in the Great Wood near Vesālī had gone for daytime abiding in a peaked hall and was lying down with the door open. His genitals were stiffened by wind. At that time, several women came to the monastery bringing perfumes and garlands, seeking a place to stay. Seeing the bhikkhu, they sat on his genitals, satisfied their desire, and said, 'What a bull of a man!' Then they adorned him with perfumes and garlands and left. The bhikkhus, seeing him soiled, reported this matter to the Blessed One. 'bhikkhus, the genitals become functional for five reasons: by lust, by feces, by urine, by wind, or by the bite of a creeping creature. These, bhikkhus, are the five reasons for which the genitals become functional. It is impossible, it is not possible, bhikkhus, that that bhikkhu's genitals became functional by lust. That bhikkhu, bhikkhus, is an arahant. There is no offense for that bhikkhu. I allow, bhikkhus, that when one is abiding during the day, one should close the door before abiding.'

78. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhārukacchako bhikkhu supinante [supinantena (sī. syā.)] purāṇadutiyikāya methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevitvā – ‘assamaṇo ahaṃ, vibbhamissāmī’ti, bhārukacchaṃ gacchanto antarāmagge āyasmantaṃ upāliṃ passitvā etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Āyasmā upāli evamāha – ‘‘anāpatti, āvuso, supinantenā’’ti.

78.At that time, a certain bhikkhu from Bhārukaccha, while dreaming, engaged in sexual intercourse with his former wife. Thinking, 'I am no longer an ascetic; I will abandon the practice,' as he was going to Bhārukaccha, he met Venerable Upāli on the road and told him about this matter. Venerable Upāli said, 'There is no offense, friend, in a dream.'

Tena kho pana samayena rājagahe supabbā nāma upāsikā mudhappasannā [muddhappasannā (sī.)] hoti. Sā evaṃdiṭṭhikā hoti – ‘‘yā methunaṃ dhammaṃ deti sā aggadānaṃ detī’’ti. Sā bhikkhuṃ passitvā etadavoca – ‘‘ehi, bhante, methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevā’’ti. ‘‘Alaṃ, bhagini, netaṃ kappatī’’ti. ‘‘Ehi, bhante, ūruntarikāya [ūrantarikāya (sī.)] ghaṭṭehi, evaṃ te anāpatti bhavissatī’’ti…pe… ehi, bhante, nābhiyaṃ ghaṭṭehi… ehi, bhante, udaravaṭṭiyaṃ ghaṭṭehi… ehi, bhante, upakacchake ghaṭṭehi… ehi, bhante, gīvāyaṃ ghaṭṭehi… ehi, bhante, kaṇṇacchidde ghaṭṭehi… ehi, bhante, kesavaṭṭiyaṃ ghaṭṭehi… ehi, bhante, aṅgulantarikāya ghaṭṭehi… ‘‘ehi, bhante, hatthena upakkamitvā mocessāmi, evaṃ te anāpatti bhavissatī’’ti. So bhikkhu tathā akāsi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa. Āpatti saṅghādisesassā’’ti.

At that time in Rājagaha, there was a laywoman named Supabbā who was foolishly devoted. She held this view: 'One who gives the gift of sexual intercourse gives the highest gift.' Seeing a bhikkhu, she said to him, 'Come, venerable sir, engage in sexual intercourse.' 'Enough, sister, that is not allowable.' 'Come, venerable sir, rub between the thighs—then there will be no offense for you.' … 'Come, venerable sir, rub at the navel…' 'Come, venerable sir, rub around the belly…' 'Come, venerable sir, rub in the armpits…' 'Come, venerable sir, rub at the neck…' 'Come, venerable sir, rub in the earhole…' 'Come, venerable sir, rub around the hairline…' 'Come, venerable sir, rub between the fingers…' 'Come, venerable sir, I will touch you with my hand and cause you release—then there will be no offense for you.' The bhikkhu acted accordingly. He felt remorse… 'There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu, but there is an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Sangha.'

79. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ saddhā nāma upāsikā mudhappasannā hoti. Sā evaṃdiṭṭhikā hoti – ‘‘yā methunaṃ dhammaṃ deti sā aggadānaṃ detī’’ti. Sā bhikkhuṃ passitvā etadavoca – ‘‘ehi, bhante, methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevā’’ti. ‘‘Alaṃ, bhagini, netaṃ kappatī’’ti. ‘‘Ehi, bhante, ūruntarikāya ghaṭṭehi…pe… ehi, bhante, hatthena upakkamitvā mocessāmi, evaṃ te anāpatti bhavissatī’’ti. So bhikkhu tathā akāsi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘anāpatti, bhikkhu, pārājikassa. Āpatti saṅghādisesassā’’ti.

79.Now at that time in Sāvatthī, there was a laywoman named Saddhā who was foolishly devoted. She held this view: 'One who gives the gift of sexual intercourse gives the supreme gift.' Seeing a bhikkhu, she said to him, 'Come, venerable sir, engage in sexual intercourse.' 'Enough, sister, that is not allowable.' 'Come, venerable sir, rub between the thighs… Come, venerable sir, I will touch you with my hand and cause you release—then there will be no offense for you.' The bhikkhu acted accordingly. He felt remorse… 'There is no offense entailing expulsion, bhikkhu, but there is an offense entailing a formal meeting of the Sangha.'

80. Tena kho pana samayena vesāliyaṃ licchavikumārakā bhikkhuṃ gahetvā bhikkhuniyā vippaṭipādesuṃ… sikkhamānāya vippaṭipādesuṃ… sāmaṇeriyā vippaṭipādesuṃ. Ubho sādiyiṃsu. Ubho nāsetabbā. Ubho na sādiyiṃsu. Ubhinnaṃ anāpatti.

80.At that time, the Licchavi youths of Vesālī, having seized a bhikkhu, caused him to commit misconduct with a nun… with a female probationer… with a female novice. If both consented, both should be expelled. If both did not consent, there is no offense for either.

81. Tena kho pana samayena vesāliyaṃ licchavikumārakā bhikkhuṃ gahetvā vesiyā vippaṭipādesuṃ… paṇḍake vippaṭipādesuṃ… gihiniyā vippaṭipādesuṃ. Bhikkhu sādiyi. Bhikkhu nāsetabbo. Bhikkhu na sādiyi. Bhikkhussa anāpatti.

81.At that time, the Licchavi youths of Vesālī, having seized a bhikkhu, caused him to have intercourse with a prostitute… with a eunuch… with a married woman. The bhikkhu consented. The bhikkhu should be expelled. The bhikkhu did not consent. The bhikkhu is without offense.

Tena kho pana samayena vesāliyaṃ licchavikumārakā bhikkhū gahetvā aññamaññaṃ vippaṭipādesuṃ. Ubho sādiyiṃsu. Ubho nāsetabbā. Ubho na sādiyiṃsu. Ubhinnaṃ anāpatti.

At that time in Vesālī, Licchavi youths, having seized bhikkhus, caused them to engage in mutual misconduct. If both consented, both should be expelled. If both did not consent, there is no offense for either.

82. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro vuḍḍhapabbajito bhikkhu purāṇadutiyikāya dassanaṃ agamāsi. Sā – ‘ehi, bhante, vibbhamā’ti aggahesi. So bhikkhu paṭikkamanto uttāno paripati. Sā ubbhajitvā [ubbhujitvā (sī. syā.)] aṅgajāte [aṅgajātena (sī.)] abhinisīdi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi …pe… ‘‘sādiyi tvaṃ, bhikkhū’’ti? ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhagavā, sādiyi’’nti. ‘‘Anāpatti, bhikkhu, asādiyantassā’’ti.

82.Now at that time a certain elderly bhikkhu who had gone forth visited his former companion. She said, 'Come, venerable sir, enjoy yourself,' and grabbed him. As the bhikkhu was retreating, he fell onto his back. She got on top and sat on his genitals. He became remorseful… 'Did you consent, bhikkhu?' 'No, Blessed One, I did not consent.' 'There is no offense, bhikkhu, for one who does not consent.'

83. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu araññe viharati. Migapotako tassa passāvaṭṭhānaṃ āgantvā passāvaṃ pivanto mukhena aṅgajātaṃ aggahesi. So bhikkhu sādiyi. Tassa kukkuccaṃ ahosi…pe… ‘‘āpattiṃ tvaṃ, bhikkhu, āpanno pārājika’’nti.

83.At that time a certain bhikkhu was living in the wilderness. A deer calf came to the place where he urinated, and while drinking urine, took hold of his genitals with its mouth. The bhikkhu consented. He had remorse… 'bhikkhu, you have incurred an offense entailing expulsion.'

Paṭhamapārājikaṃ samattaṃ.

The first expulsion offense is completed.
