# BU-NP 18

## 8. Rūpiyasikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The training rule concerning money.

582. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā upanando sakyaputto aññatarassa kulassa kulūpako hoti niccabhattiko. Yaṃ tasmiṃ kule uppajjati khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā tato āyasmato upanandassa sakyaputtassa paṭiviso ṭhapiyyati. Tena kho pana samayena sāyaṃ tasmiṃ kule maṃsaṃ uppannaṃ hoti. Tato āyasmato upanandassa sakyaputtassa paṭiviso ṭhapito hoti. Tassa kulassa dārako rattiyā paccūsasamayaṃ paccuṭṭhāya rodati – ‘‘maṃsaṃ me dethā’’ti. Atha kho so puriso pajāpatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayyassa paṭivisaṃ dārakassa dehi. Aññaṃ cetāpetvā ayyassa dassāmā’’ti.

582.At that time the Blessed One was staying at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the squirrel sanctuary. At that time, Venerable Upananda the Sakyan was a frequent visitor to a certain family, receiving regular meals. Whatever food—hard or soft—arose in that household, a portion was set aside for Venerable Upananda the Sakyan. Now at that time, in the evening, meat appeared in that household. A portion was set aside for Venerable Upananda the Sakyan. The child of that family, getting up in the pre-dawn period of the night, cried, “Give me meat!” Then the man said to his wife, “Give the venerable’s portion to the child. We’ll obtain something else for the venerable.”

Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaraṃ ādāya yena taṃ kulaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho so puriso yenāyasmā upanando sakyaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so puriso āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘hiyyo kho, bhante, sāyaṃ maṃsaṃ uppannaṃ ahosi. Tato ayyassa paṭiviso ṭhapito. Ayaṃ, bhante, dārako rattiyā paccūsasamayaṃ paccuṭṭhāya rodati – ‘maṃsaṃ me dethā’ti. Ayyassa paṭiviso dārakassa dinno. Kahāpaṇena, bhante, kiṃ āhariyyatū’’ti? ‘‘Pariccatto me, āvuso, kahāpaṇo’’ti? ‘‘Āma, bhante, pariccatto’’ti. ‘‘Taññeva me, āvuso, kahāpaṇaṃ dehī’’ti.

Then, in the morning, Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having dressed, took his bowl and robe, and went to that family; having gone, he sat down on the prepared seat. Then that man went up to Venerable Upananda the Sakyan; having gone, he bowed to Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, sat down to one side, and said, “Venerable, yesterday evening meat was obtained. A portion was set aside for the venerable one. But this child, getting up in the pre-dawn period of the night, cried, ‘Give me meat!’ So your portion was given to the child. Venerable, what should be bought with the kahapana?” “Is the kahapana given up to me, householder?” “Yes, Venerable.” “Well then, householder, give me that very kahapana.”

Atha kho so puriso āyasmato upanandassa sakyaputtassa kahāpaṇaṃ datvā ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘tatheva mayaṃ rūpiyaṃ paṭiggaṇhāma evamevime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā rūpiyaṃ paṭiggaṇhantī’’ti. Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tassa purisassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto rūpiyaṃ paṭiggahessatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, rūpiyaṃ paṭiggahesī’’ti [paṭiggaṇhāsīti (syā.)]? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, rūpiyaṃ paṭiggahessasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then that man, having given a kahapana to Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, complained, grumbled, and criticized: "Just as we accept money, so too do these Sakyan ascetics accept money." The bhikkhus heard that man complaining, grumbling, and criticizing. Those bhikkhus who were modest… they too complained, grumbled, and criticized: "How could Venerable Upananda the Sakyan accept money?" Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Upananda the Sakyan in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… "Is it true, Upananda, that you accepted money?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Blessed One rebuked him… "Foolish man, how could you accept money? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And then, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

583. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu jātarūparajataṃ uggaṇheyya vā uggaṇhāpeyya vā upanikkhittaṃ vā sādiyeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

583."Whatever bhikkhu should take or cause to be taken gold or silver, or approve of its being kept in deposit for him, it is to be forfeited and confessed."

584. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

584."Yo pana" means whoever, of any sort..."Bhikkhu" means...the bhikkhu who is referred to in this case.

Jātarūpaṃ nāma satthuvaṇṇo vuccati.

"Gold" is said to refer to the complexion of the Teacher.

Rajataṃ nāma kahāpaṇo lohamāsako dārumāsako jatumāsako ye vohāraṃ gacchanti.

"Silver" means a kahapana coin, a copper masaka, a wooden masaka, a lacquer masaka, or whatever is used in commerce.

Uggaṇheyyāti sayaṃ gaṇhāti nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ [nissaggiyaṃ hoti (syā.)].

"Take" means he himself takes; it is to be forfeited and confessed.

Uggaṇhāpeyyāti aññaṃ gāhāpeti nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ [nissaggiyaṃ hoti (syā.)].

"Cause another to take" means he causes another to take it; it is to be forfeited and confessed.

Upanikkhittaṃ vā sādiyeyyāti idaṃ ayyassa hotūti upanikkhittaṃ sādiyati, nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Saṅghamajjhe nissajjitabbaṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ – tena bhikkhunā saṅghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘‘ahaṃ, bhante, rūpiyaṃ paṭiggahesiṃ. Idaṃ me nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmī’’ti. Nissajjitvā āpatti desetabbā. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena āpatti paṭiggahetabbā. Sace tattha āgacchati ārāmiko vā upāsako vā so vattabbo – ‘‘āvuso, imaṃ jānāhī’’ti. Sace so bhaṇati – ‘‘iminā kiṃ āhariyyatū’’ti, na vattabbo – ‘‘imaṃ vā imaṃ vā āharā’’ti. Kappiyaṃ ācikkhitabbaṃ – sappi vā telaṃ vā madhu vā phāṇitaṃ vā. Sace so tena parivattetvā kappiyaṃ āharati rūpiyappaṭiggāhakaṃ ṭhapetvā sabbeheva paribhuñjitabbaṃ. Evañcetaṃ labhetha, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce labhetha, so vattabbo – ‘‘āvuso, imaṃ chaḍḍehī’’ti. Sace so chaḍḍeti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce chaḍḍeti, pañcahaṅgehi samannāgato bhikkhu rūpiyachaḍḍako sammannitabbo – yo na chandāgatiṃ gaccheyya, na dosāgatiṃ gaccheyya, na mohāgatiṃ gaccheyya, na bhayāgatiṃ gaccheyya, chaḍḍitāchaḍḍitañca jāneyya. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbo. Paṭhamaṃ bhikkhu yācitabbo. Yācitvā byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

"If one approves of it when it has been placed nearby"—this means approving of something placed nearby by saying, "Let this belong to the venerable one." It becomes forfeitable. It must be relinquished in the midst of the Sangha. And this, bhikkhus, is how it should be relinquished: That bhikkhu should approach the Sangha, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay respect at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in a squatting position, raise his joined palms, and say: "Venerable sirs, I have accepted money. This is forfeitable by me. I relinquish it to the Sangha." After relinquishing it, the offense should be confessed. A competent and capable bhikkhu should receive the confession. If a monastery worker or a lay follower arrives there, he should be told: "Friend, be aware of this." If he says: "What should be obtained with this?" he should not be told: "Bring this or that." Instead, what is allowable should be indicated—ghee, oil, honey, or molasses. If he exchanges it and brings back what is allowable, everyone may make use of it, except for the one who received the money. If this can be done, it is good. If not, he should be told: "Friend, discard this." If he discards it, it is good. If he does not discard it, a bhikkhu possessing five qualities should be appointed as a money-discarder: one who does not go to a decision out of favoritism, ill will, delusion, or fear, and who knows what has and has not been discarded. And this, bhikkhus, is how he should be appointed: First, a bhikkhu should be requested. Having been requested, a competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Sangha:

585. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ rūpiyachaḍḍakaṃ sammanneyya. Esā ñatti.

585."Venerable sir, may the Sangha hear me. If the Sangha is ready, let the Sangha appoint the bhikkhu named so-and-so to discard money. This is the motion."

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ rūpiyachaḍḍakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno rūpiyachaḍḍakassa sammuti, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

"Venerable sir, may the Sangha hear me. The Sangha is appointing the bhikkhu named so-and-so as the money-discarder. If it is acceptable to the venerable ones that the bhikkhu named so-and-so be appointed as the money-discarder, let him be silent; if it is not acceptable, let him speak."

‘‘Sammato saṅghena itthannāmo bhikkhu rūpiyachaḍḍako. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

"The Sangha has approved the bhikkhu named thus-and-so, the money-discarder. The Sangha approves, therefore it is silent. Thus I hold it."

Tena sammatena bhikkhunā animittaṃ katvā pātetabbaṃ. Sace nimittaṃ katvā pāteti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

By that agreed-upon bhikkhu, it should be discarded without making a sign. If he discards it after making a sign, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

586. Rūpiye rūpiyasaññī rūpiyaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Rūpiye vematiko rūpiyaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Rūpiye arūpiyasaññī rūpiyaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

586.If one perceives money as money and accepts money, it is to be forfeited and confessed. If one is in doubt as to whether it is money and accepts money, it is to be forfeited and confessed. If one perceives money as not money and accepts money, it is to be forfeited and confessed.

Arūpiye rūpiyasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Arūpiye vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Arūpiye arūpiyasaññī, anāpatti.

Perceiving what is not money as money, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Being doubtful about what is not money, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Perceiving what is not money as not money, there is no offense.

Anāpatti ajjhārāme vā ajjhāvasathe vā uggahetvā vā uggahāpetvā vā nikkhipati – yassa bhavissati so harissatīti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense for one who, having taken possession of it or having had it taken possession of, deposits it in a monastery or dwelling, thinking, "Whoever owns it will take it," for one who is insane, or for the first offender.

Rūpiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The eighth training rule, concerning money, is concluded.
