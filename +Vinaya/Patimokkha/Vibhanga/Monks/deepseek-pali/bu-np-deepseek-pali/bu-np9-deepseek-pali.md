# BU-NP 9

## 9. Dutiyaupakkhaṭasikkhāpadaṃ

## 9.The Second Training Rule on Readiness

532. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro puriso aññataraṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’ti. Sopi evamāha – ‘‘ahampi ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’ti. Assosi kho aññataro piṇḍacāriko bhikkhu tesaṃ purisānaṃ imaṃ kathāsallāpaṃ. Atha kho so bhikkhu yenāyasmā upanando sakyaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mahāpuññosi tvaṃ, āvuso upananda. Amukasmiṃ okāse aññataro puriso aññataraṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’ti. Sopi evamāha – ‘ahampi ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’’ti. ‘‘Atthāvuso, maṃ te upaṭṭhākā’’ti.

532.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. Now on that occasion a certain man said to another man: “I shall clothe the Venerable Upananda with a robe.” The other replied: “I too shall clothe the Venerable Upananda with a robe.” A certain alms-seeking bhikkhu overheard this conversation of those men. Then that bhikkhu approached the Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, and having approached, said to him: “You are of great merit, friend Upananda. In such-and-such a place, a certain man said to another man: ‘I shall clothe the Venerable Upananda with a robe.’ The other replied: ‘I too shall clothe the Venerable Upananda with a robe.’” “Friend, they are my supporters.”

Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto yena te purisā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te purise etadavoca – ‘‘saccaṃ kira maṃ tumhe, āvuso, cīvarehi acchādetukāmātthā’’ti? ‘‘Api nayya, evaṃ hoti – ‘ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarehi acchādessāmā’’’ti. ‘‘Sace kho maṃ tumhe, āvuso, cīvarehi acchādetukāmāttha, evarūpena cīvarena acchādetha, kyāhaṃ tehi acchannopi karissāmi, yānāhaṃ na paribhuñjissāmī’’ti. Atha kho te purisā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘mahicchā ime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā asantuṭṭhā. Nayime sukarā cīvarehi acchādetuṃ. Kathañhi nāma ayyo upanando amhehi pubbe appavārito upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti!

Then Venerable Upananda the Sakyan approached those men and said: “Is it true, friends, that you intend to clothe me with robes?” “Yes, venerable sir, it is so: ‘We will clothe Venerable Upananda with robes.’” “If you intend to clothe me with robes, friends, then clothe me with a robe of such a kind. For what shall I do even if I am clothed, if I cannot make use of those robes?” Then those men complained, criticized, and protested: “These Sakyan ascetics are full of desires and never content. It is not easy to clothe them with robes. How could Venerable Upananda, uninvited by us beforehand, approach us and make stipulations about robes?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ purisānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto pubbe appavārito gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, pubbe appavārito gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Ñātakā te, upananda, aññātakā’’ti? ‘‘Aññātakā, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Aññātako, moghapurisa, aññātakānaṃ na jānāti patirūpaṃ vā appatirūpaṃ vā santaṃ vā asantaṃ vā. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, pubbe appavārito aññātake gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

The bhikkhus heard those men complaining, criticizing, and protesting. The bhikkhus of few desires… complained, criticized, and protested: “How could Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having not been previously invited, approach householders and make stipulations about robes?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Upananda the Sakyan in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, Upananda, that you, having not been previously invited, approached householders and made stipulations about robes?” “It is true, Blessed One.” “Are they relatives of yours, Upananda, or not?” “They are not relatives, Blessed One.” “Foolish man, a non-relative does not know what is proper or improper, what exists or does not exist. How could you, foolish man, having not been previously invited, approach non-relative householders and make stipulations about robes? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

533. ‘‘Bhikkhuṃ paneva uddissa ubhinnaṃ aññātakānaṃ gahapatīnaṃ vā gahapatānīnaṃ vā paccekacīvaracetāpannāni upakkhaṭāni honti – ‘imehi mayaṃ paccekacīvaracetāpannehi paccekacīvarāni cetāpetvā itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ cīvarehi acchādessāmā’ti; tatra ce so bhikkhu pubbe appavārito upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjeyya – ‘sādhu vata maṃ āyasmanto imehi paccekacīvaracetāpannehi evarūpaṃ vā evarūpaṃ vā cīvaraṃ cetāpetvā acchādetha, ubhova santā ekenā’ti, kalyāṇakamyataṃ upādāya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

533.“If, regarding a bhikkhu, separate purchasing funds for robes have been prepared for two unrelated householders or housewives, thinking, ‘With these separate purchasing funds for robes, we will purchase separate robes and clothe the bhikkhu named so-and-so’; and that bhikkhu, without prior invitation, approaches them and makes a specification regarding the robe, saying, ‘It would be good if the venerable ones, with these separate purchasing funds for robes, would purchase such-and-such a robe for me and clothe me, both of you combining your funds for one,’ motivated by a desire for something fine—it entails relinquishment and confession.”

534. Bhikkhuṃ paneva uddissāti bhikkhussatthāya, bhikkhuṃ ārammaṇaṃ karitvā, bhikkhuṃ acchādetukāmā.

534.‘Regarding a bhikkhu’ means: for the sake of a bhikkhu, having a bhikkhu as the object, desiring to clothe a bhikkhu.

Ubhinnanti dvinnaṃ.

‘Both’ means two.

Aññātakā nāma mātito vā pitito vā yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā asambaddhā.

‘Unrelated’ means not connected through mother or father up to the seventh ancestral generation.

Gahapatī nāma ye keci agāra ajjhāvasanti.

‘Householders’ means any who dwell in houses.

Gahapatāniyo nāma yā kāci agāraṃ ajjhāvasanti.

‘Housewives’ means any women who dwell in a house.

Cīvaracetāpannāni nāma hiraññā vā suvaṇṇā vā muttā vā maṇī vā pavāḷā vā phalikā vā paṭakā vā suttā vā kappāsā vā.

‘Funds for robes’ means gold, silver, pearls, gems, coral, crystal, cloth, thread, or cotton.

Imehi paccekacīvaracetāpannehiti paccupaṭṭhitehi.

‘With these separate purchasing funds for robes’ means with what has been provided.

Cetāpetvāti parivattetvā.

‘Cetāpetvā’ means to exchange.

Acchādessāmāti dassāma.

‘Acchādessāma’ means ‘we will give’.

Tatra ce so bhikkhūti yaṃ bhikkhuṃ uddissa cīvaracetāpannāni upakkhaṭāni honti so bhikkhu.

‘If that bhikkhu’ means the bhikkhu for whom funds for robes have been prepared.

Pubbe appavāritoti pubbe avutto hoti – ‘‘kīdisena te, bhante, cīvarena attho, kīdisaṃ te cīvaraṃ cetāpemā’’ti.

‘Without prior invitation’ means not having been previously asked: ‘What kind of robe do you need, venerable sir? What kind of robe should we have made for you?’

Upasaṅkamitvāti gharaṃ gantvā yattha katthaci upasaṅkamitvā.

‘Approaching’ means going to the house or approaching anywhere at all.

Cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjeyyāti āyataṃ vā hotu vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā saṇhaṃ vā.

‘Makes a specification regarding the robe’ means: ‘May it be long, or wide, or closely woven, or smooth.’

Imehi paccekacīvaracetāpannehīti paccupaṭṭhitehi.

‘With these separate purchasing funds for robes’ means with what has been offered.

Evarūpaṃ vā evarūpaṃ vāti āyataṃ vā vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā saṇhaṃ vā.

‘Such-and-such’ means long or wide, closely woven or smooth.

Cetāpetvāti parivattetvā.

‘Cetāpetvā’ means having exchanged.

Acchādethāti dajjetha.

‘Acchādetha’ means ‘give’.

Ubhova santā ekenāti dvepi janā ekena.

‘Both of you combining your funds for one’ means two people with one.

Kalyāṇakamyataṃ upādāyāti sādhatthiko mahagghatthiko.

‘Motivated by a desire for something fine’ means having a purpose that is beneficial and of great value.

Tassa vacanena āyataṃ vā vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā saṇhaṃ vā cetāpenti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ pubbe appavārito aññātake gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpannaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

If, at his request, they get a long, wide, closely woven or smooth robe made, there is an offense of wrong-doing for the effort. On receipt, it entails relinquishment. It is to be relinquished to the Sangha, a group, or an individual. And thus, bhikkhus, it is to be relinquished… ‘Venerable sirs, this robe, which I obtained formerly uninvited, by approaching unrelated householders and making a specification regarding a robe, entails relinquishment. I relinquish this to the Sangha.’… He should give it… they should give it… ‘I give (this) to the venerable’.

535. Aññātake aññātakasaññī pubbe appavārito gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake vematiko pubbe appavārito gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake ñātakasaññī pubbe appavārito gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

535.If, perceiving unrelated people as unrelated, he approaches householders without prior invitation and makes a specification regarding a robe, it entails relinquishment and confession. If he is in doubt whether they are unrelated and, without prior invitation, approaches householders and makes a specification regarding a robe, it entails relinquishment and confession. If, perceiving unrelated people as related, he approaches householders without prior invitation and makes a specification regarding a robe, it entails relinquishment and confession.

Ñātake aññātakasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake ñātakasaññī, anāpatti.

If, perceiving a relative as a non-relative, he makes a specification regarding a robe, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he is in doubt whether someone is a relative and makes a specification regarding a robe, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving a relative as a relative, he makes a specification regarding a robe, there is no offense.

536. Anāpatti – ñātakānaṃ, pavāritānaṃ, aññassatthāya, attano dhanena, mahagghaṃ cetāpetukāmānaṃ appagghaṃ cetāpeti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

536.There is no offense: for relatives; for those who have made an invitation; for the sake of another person; with one’s own wealth; for one who, intending to purchase something valuable, purchases something less valuable; for the insane; for the first offender.

Dutiyaupakkhaṭasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The Second Training Rule on Readiness is finished.
