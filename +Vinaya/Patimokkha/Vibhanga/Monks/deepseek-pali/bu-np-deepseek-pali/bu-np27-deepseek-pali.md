# BU-NP 27

## 7. Mahāpesakārasikkhāpadaṃ

## 7.The Great Weaver Training Rule

641. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro puriso pavāsaṃ gacchanto pajāpatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘suttaṃ dhārayitvā amukassa tantavāyassa dehi, cīvaraṃ vāyāpetvā nikkhipa, āgato ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’ti. Assosi kho aññataro piṇḍacāriko bhikkhu tassa purisassa imaṃ vācaṃ bhāsamānassa. Atha kho so bhikkhu yenāyasmā upanando sakyaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mahāpuññosi tvaṃ, āvuso upananda, amukasmiṃ okāse aññataro puriso pavāsaṃ gacchanto pajāpatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘suttaṃ dhārayitvā amukassa tantavāyassa dehi, cīvaraṃ vāyāpetvā nikkhipa, āgato ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’ti. ‘‘Atthāvuso, maṃ so upaṭṭhāko’’ti. Sopi kho tantavāyo āyasmato upanandassa sakyaputtassa upaṭṭhāko hoti. Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto yena so tantavāyo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ tantavāyaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ kho, āvuso, cīvaraṃ maṃ uddissa viyyati; āyatañca karohi vitthatañca. Appitañca suvītañca suppavāyitañca suvilekhitañca suvitacchitañca karohī’’ti. ‘‘Ete kho me, bhante, suttaṃ dhārayitvā adaṃsu; iminā suttena cīvaraṃ vināhī’’ti. ‘‘Na, bhante, sakkā āyataṃ vā vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā kātuṃ. Sakkā ca kho, bhante, suvītañca suppavāyitañca suvilekhitañca suvitacchitañca kātu’’nti. ‘‘Iṅgha tvaṃ, āvuso, āyatañca karohi vitthatañca appitañca. Na tena suttena paṭibaddhaṃ bhavissatī’’ti.

641.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time a certain man, about to go on a journey, said to his wife: “Take this thread and give it to such-and-such a weaver. Have a robe woven and set it aside. When I return, I will present Venerable Upananda with the robe.” A certain alms-collecting bhikkhu heard that man speaking these words. Then that bhikkhu approached Venerable Upananda the Sakyan; having approached, he said to Venerable Upananda the Sakyan: “You are very fortunate, friend Upananda. In such-and-such a place, a certain man about to go on a journey said to his wife: ‘Take this thread and give it to such-and-such a weaver. Have a robe woven and set it aside. When I return, I will present Venerable Upananda with the robe.’” “Indeed, friend, he is my supporter.” That weaver too was a supporter of Venerable Upananda the Sakyan. Then Venerable Upananda the Sakyan approached that weaver; having approached, he said to that weaver: “Friend, this robe is being woven for me. Make it long and wide. Make it closely woven, well-woven, well-spread, well-marked, and well-finished.” “Venerable sir, they gave me this thread to hold; with this thread, a robe is woven.” “Venerable sir, it is not possible to make it long or wide or closely woven. But it is possible, Venerable sir, to make it well-woven, well-spread, well-marked, and well-finished.” “Come now, friend, make it long and wide and closely woven. It will not be restricted by that thread.”

Atha kho so tantavāyo yathābhataṃ suttaṃ tante upanetvā yena sā itthī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘suttena, ayye, attho’’ti. ‘‘Nanu tvaṃ ayyo [ayya (syā.)] mayā vutto – ‘iminā suttena cīvaraṃ vināhī’’’ti. ‘‘Saccāhaṃ, ayye, tayā vutto – ‘iminā suttena cīvaraṃ vināhī’ti. Apica, maṃ ayyo upanando evamāha – ‘iṅgha tvaṃ, āvuso, āyatañca karohi vitthatañca appitañca, na tena suttena paṭibaddhaṃ bhavissatī’’’ti. Atha kho sā itthī yattakaṃyeva suttaṃ paṭhamaṃ adāsi tattakaṃ pacchā adāsi. Assosi kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto – ‘‘so kira puriso pavāsato āgato’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto yena tassa purisassa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho so puriso yenāyasmā upanando sakyaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so puriso pajāpatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘vītaṃ taṃ cīvara’’nti? ‘‘Āmāyya, vītaṃ taṃ cīvara’’nti. ‘‘Āhara, ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’ti. Atha kho sā itthī taṃ cīvaraṃ nīharitvā sāmikassa datvā etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Atha kho so puriso āyasmato upanandassa sakyaputtassa cīvaraṃ datvā ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘mahicchā ime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā asantuṭṭhā. Nayime sukarā cīvarena acchādetuṃ. Kathañhi nāma ayyo upanando mayā pubbe appavārito tantavāye [gahapatikassa tantavāye (ka.)] upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti.

Then that weaver, having set the prepared thread on the loom, approached that woman. Having approached, he said to her: “Madam, the thread is needed.” “But, sir, didn’t I tell you—‘With this thread, weave a robe’?” “Indeed, madam, you told me—‘With this thread, weave a robe.’ But also, Venerable Upananda told me—‘Come, friend, make it long and wide and closely woven; it won’t be limited by that thread.’” Then that woman gave the same amount of thread again as she had given at first. Venerable Upananda the Sakyan heard: “That man, it seems, has returned from his journey.” Then Venerable Upananda the Sakyan went to that man’s house. Having approached, he sat down on the prepared seat. Then that man approached Venerable Upananda the Sakyan. Having approached, he paid homage to Venerable Upananda the Sakyan and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, that man said to his wife: “Is that robe finished?” “Yes, sir, the robe is finished.” “Bring it, I will clothe Venerable Upananda with the robe.” Then that woman took out the robe, gave it to her husband, and informed him of the matter. Then that man gave the robe to Venerable Upananda the Sakyan and complained, grumbled, and criticized: “These Sakyan ascetics are of great desires and are not content. It is not easy to please them with robes. How could Venerable Upananda, without being asked, approach the weaver and interfere about the robe?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tassa purisassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto pubbe appavārito gahapatikassa tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, pubbe appavārito gahapatikassa tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Ñātako te, upananda, aññātako’’ti? ‘‘Aññātako, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Aññātako, moghapurisa, aññātakassa na jānāti patirūpaṃ vā appatirūpaṃ vā santaṃ vā asantaṃ vā. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, pubbe appavārito aññātakassa gahapatikassa tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

The bhikkhus heard that man complaining, grumbling, and criticizing. Those bhikkhus who were modest… they complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How could Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having gone to the householder's weaver without being authorized, make stipulations about the robe?” Then those bhikkhus, after rebuking Venerable Upananda the Sakyan in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, Upananda, that you, without being authorized, having gone to the householder's weaver, made stipulations about the robe?” “It is true, Blessed One.” “Is he a relative of yours, Upananda, or not a relative?” “Not a relative, Blessed One.” “Foolish man, a stranger does not know what is suitable or unsuitable, what exists or does not exist. How could you, foolish man, without being authorized, having gone to the householder's weaver, who is a stranger, make stipulations about the robe? This is not for the pleasing of those who are not pleased… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:—”

642. ‘‘Bhikkhuṃ paneva uddissa aññātako gahapati vā gahapatānī vā tantavāyehi cīvaraṃ vāyāpeyya, tatra ce so bhikkhu pubbe appavārito tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjeyya – ‘idaṃ kho, āvuso, cīvaraṃ maṃ uddissa viyyati. Āyatañca karotha vitthatañca. Appitañca suvītañca suppavāyitañca suvilekhitañca suvitacchitañca karotha. Appeva nāma mayampi āyasmantānaṃ kiñcimattaṃ anupadajjeyyāmā’ti. Evañca so bhikkhu vatvā kiñcimattaṃ anupadajjeyya antamaso piṇḍapātamattampi, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

642.“If, for a bhikkhu, a householder or a householder's wife, who is not a relative, should have a robe woven by weavers, and if that bhikkhu, not previously invited, should approach the weavers and make stipulations about the robe, saying: ‘Friends, this robe is being woven for me. Make it long and wide; closely woven, well-woven, well-spread, well-marked, and well-finished. Perhaps we too may give the venerable ones some small thing.’ And if that bhikkhu, having said this, should give them some small thing, even so little as a mouthful of alms food, it is to be forfeited, requiring confession.”

643. Bhikkhuṃ paneva uddissāti bhikkhussatthāya bhikkhuṃ ārammaṇaṃ karitvā bhikkhuṃ acchādetukāmo.

643.“Specifically for a bhikkhu” means for the sake of a bhikkhu, having a bhikkhu as the object, intending to clothe a bhikkhu.

Aññātako nāma mātito vā pitito vā yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā asambaddho.

“Not a relative” means not connected through mother or father up to the seventh generation of ancestors.

Gahapati nāma yo koci agāraṃ ajjhāvasati.

“Householder” refers to anyone who dwells in a house.

Gahapatānī nāma yā kāci agāraṃ ajjhāvasati.

“A householder’s wife” refers to any woman who dwells in a house.

Tantavāyehīti pesakārehi.

“By weavers” means by weavers.

Cīvaraṃ nāma channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ vikappanupagaṃ pacchimaṃ.

“Robe” means any one of the six robes, suitable for designation, that is last.

Vāyāpeyyāti vināpeti.

“Vāyāpeyya” means “has it woven.”

Tatra ce so bhikkhūti yaṃ bhikkhuṃ uddissa cīvaraṃ viyyati so bhikkhu.

“There, the ‘bhikkhu’” means that bhikkhu for whom the robe is being woven.

Pubbe appavāritoti pubbe avutto hoti – ‘‘kīdisena te, bhante, cīvarena attho, kīdisaṃ te cīvaraṃ vāyāpemī’’ti?

“Without prior invitation” means not having been told beforehand, “Venerable, what kind of robe do you need? What kind of robe should I have woven for you?”

Tantavāye upasaṅkamitvāti gharaṃ gantvā yattha katthaci upasaṅkamitvā.

“Approaching the weavers” means going to their house or approaching them wherever they may be.

Cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjeyyāti – ‘‘idaṃ kho, āvuso, cīvaraṃ maṃ uddissa viyyati, āyatañca karotha vitthatañca. Appitañca suvītañca suppavāyitañca suvilekhitañca suvitacchitañca karotha. Appeva nāma mayampi āyasmantānaṃ kiñcimattaṃ anupadajjeyyāmā’’ti.

“Makes a stipulation about the robe” means saying: “Friends, this robe is being woven for me. Make it long and wide. Make it closely woven, well-woven, well-spread, well-marked, and well-finished. Perhaps we too may give the venerable ones some small thing in return.”

Evañca so bhikkhu vatvā kiñcimattaṃ anupadajjeyya antamaso piṇḍapātamattampīti. Piṇḍapāto nāma yāgupi bhattampi khādanīyampi cuṇṇapiṇḍopi dantakaṭṭhampi dasikasuttampi, antamaso dhammampi bhaṇati.

“And if that bhikkhu, having spoken thus, gives them even the smallest thing, even as little as alms food.” “Alms food” means gruel, cooked rice, hard food, a ball of powder, a toothpick, or thread—or even if he teaches them Dhamma.

Tassa vacanena āyataṃ vā vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā karoti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ pubbe appavārito aññātakassa gahapatikassa tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpannaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

If, because of his words, they make it long or wide or closely woven, there is an offense of wrong conduct in the attempt. Upon acquisition, it becomes to be forfeited. It must be forfeited to the Sangha, a group, or an individual. And it should be forfeited thus... “Venerable Sirs, this robe, which I obtained by approaching the weavers of an unknown householder without prior authorization and making a stipulation about it, is to be forfeited. I forfeit this to the Sangha.” ... It should be given back... “I give this to the venerable one.”

644. Aññātake aññātakasaññī pubbe appavārito gahapatikassa tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake vematiko pubbe appavārito gahapatikassa tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake ñātakasaññī pubbe appavārito gahapatikassa tantavāye upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

644.If, perceiving an unknown person as unknown and, without prior invitation, approaching a householder's weavers and making stipulations about a robe, it is to be forfeited and confessed. If, being uncertain about an unknown person and, without prior invitation, approaching a householder's weavers and making stipulations about a robe, it is to be forfeited and confessed. If, perceiving an unknown person as a known person and, without prior invitation, approaching a householder's weavers and making stipulations about a robe, it is to be forfeited and confessed.

Ñātake añātakasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake ñātakasaññī, anāpatti.

Perceiving a known person as unknown, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Being uncertain about a known person, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Perceiving a known person as known, there is no offense.

645. Anāpatti – ñātakānaṃ, pavāritānaṃ, aññassatthāya, attano dhanena, mahagghaṃ vāyāpetukāmassa appagghaṃ vāyāpeti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

645.No offense—for relatives, for those who have invited, for the sake of another, with one's own wealth, when one wishing to have an expensive robe woven has a cheap one woven instead, for one who is insane, for the first offender.

Mahāpesakārasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The seventh training rule, that on making large robes, is concluded.
