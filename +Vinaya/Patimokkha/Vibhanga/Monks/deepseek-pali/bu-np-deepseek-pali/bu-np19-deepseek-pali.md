# BU-NP 19

## 9. Rūpiyasaṃvohārasikkhāpadaṃ

## 9.The Training Rule on Monetary Transactions.

587. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū nānappakārakaṃ rūpiyasaṃvohāraṃ samāpajjanti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā nānappakārakaṃ rūpiyasaṃvohāraṃ samāpajjissanti, seyyathāpi gihī kāmabhogino’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū nānappakārakaṃ rūpiyasaṃvohāraṃ samāpajjissantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, nānappakārakaṃ rūpiyasaṃvohāraṃ samāpajjathā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, nānappakārakaṃ rūpiyasaṃvohāraṃ samāpajjissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

587.At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were engaging in various kinds of monetary transactions. People complained, criticized, and spread it about: “How can these Sakyan ascetics engage in various kinds of monetary transactions, just like householders who enjoy sensual pleasures?” The bhikkhus heard these people complaining, criticizing, and spreading it about. Those bhikkhus who were modest… they too complained, criticized, and spread it about: “How can the group-of-six bhikkhus engage in various kinds of monetary transactions?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the group-of-six bhikkhus in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One. “…Is it true, bhikkhus, that you engage in various kinds of monetary transactions?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked them: “…How can you, foolish men, engage in various kinds of monetary transactions? This will not lead to faith in those without it… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

588. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu nānappakārakaṃ rūpiyasaṃvohāraṃ samāpajjeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

588.“Whatever bhikkhu should engage in various kinds of monetary transactions, it is to be forfeited and confessed.”

589. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

589.“Yo panā”ti means whoever… “bhikkhū”ti… here, in this connection, a “bhikkhu” is intended.

Nānappakārakaṃ nāma katampi akatampi katākatampi. Kataṃ nāma sīsūpagaṃ gīvūpagaṃ hatthūpagaṃ pādūpagaṃ kaṭūpagaṃ. Akataṃ nāma ghanakataṃ vuccati. Katākataṃ nāma tadubhayaṃ.

“Various kinds” means made, unmade, and both made and unmade. “Made” means what pertains to the head, what pertains to the neck, what pertains to the hands, what pertains to the feet, and what pertains to the waist. “Unmade” is called densely made. “Both made and unmade” means both of those.

Rūpiyaṃ nāma satthuvaṇṇo kahāpaṇo, lohamāsako, dārumāsako, jatumāsako ye vohāraṃ gacchanti.

“Money” means the silver color, the kahāpaṇa coin, the copper māsaka, the wooden māsaka, the lac māsaka—whatever is used in transactions.

Samāpajjeyyāti katena kataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ [nissaggiyaṃ hoti (syā.)]. Katena akataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Katena katākataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Akatena kataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Akatena akataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Akatena katākataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Katākatena kataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Katākatena akataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Katākatena katākataṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Saṅghamajjhe nissajjitabbaṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. Tena bhikkhunā saṅghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘‘ahaṃ, bhante, nānappakārakaṃ rūpiyasaṃvohāraṃ samāpajjiṃ. Idaṃ me nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmī’’ti. Nissajjitvā āpatti desetabbā. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena āpatti paṭiggahetabbā. Sace tattha āgacchati ārāmiko vā upāsako vā so vattabbo – ‘‘āvuso, imaṃ jānāhī’’ti. Sace so bhaṇati – ‘‘iminā kiṃ āhariyyatū’’ti, na vattabbo – ‘‘imaṃ vā imaṃ vā āharā’’ti. Kappiyaṃ ācikkhitabbaṃ – sappi vā telaṃ vā madhu vā phāṇitaṃ vā. Sace so tena parivattetvā kappiyaṃ āharati, rūpiyacetāpakaṃ ṭhapetvā, sabbeheva paribhuñjitabbaṃ. Evañcetaṃ labhetha, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce labhetha, so vattabbo – ‘‘āvuso, imaṃ chaḍḍehī’’ti. Sace so chaḍḍeti, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce chaḍḍeti, pañcahaṅgehi samannāgato bhikkhu rūpiyachaḍḍako sammannitabbo – yo na chandāgatiṃ gaccheyya, na dosāgatiṃ gaccheyya, na mohāgatiṃ gaccheyya, na bhayāgatiṃ gaccheyya, chaḍḍitāchaḍḍitañca jāneyya. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbo. Paṭhamaṃ bhikkhu yācitabbo. Yācitvā byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

If he causes a made (coin) to be exchanged by means of a made (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes an unmade (coin) to be exchanged by means of a made (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes a made-and-unmade (coin) to be exchanged by means of a made (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes a made (coin) to be exchanged by means of an unmade (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes an unmade (coin) to be exchanged by means of an unmade (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes a made-and-unmade (coin) to be exchanged by means of an unmade (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes a made (coin) to be exchanged by means of a made-and-unmade (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes an unmade (coin) to be exchanged by means of a made-and-unmade (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If he causes a made-and-unmade (coin) to be exchanged by means of a made-and-unmade (coin), it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. It is to be forfeited in the midst of the Sangha. And thus, bhikkhus, should it be forfeited. That bhikkhu should approach the Sangha, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit on his heels, raise his hands in añjali, and say this: ‘Venerable sirs, I have engaged in various kinds of monetary transactions. This is to be forfeited by me. I forfeit this to the Sangha.’ Having forfeited it, the offense should be confessed. The offense should be received by a competent bhikkhu. If an attendant or a lay follower comes there, he should be told: ‘Friend, be aware of this.’ If he says: ‘What should be obtained with this?’ he should not be told: ‘Obtain this or that.’ What is allowable should be indicated: ghee, oil, honey, or molasses. If he exchanges it for what is allowable and brings it, except for the money-changer’s fee, it should all be partaken of. If it is obtained in this way, that is skillful; if it is not obtained, he should be told: ‘Friend, discard this.’ If he discards it, that is skillful; if he does not discard it, a bhikkhu possessing five qualities should be appointed as the money-discarder: one who does not go to favoritism, one who does not go to aversion, one who does not go to delusion, one who does not go to fear, and one who knows what has been discarded and what has not been discarded. And thus, bhikkhus, should he be appointed. First, a bhikkhu should be requested. Having been requested, the Sangha should be informed by a competent bhikkhu.

590. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ rūpiyachaḍḍakaṃ sammanneyya. Esā ñatti.

590.“Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. If the Sangha is ready, let the Sangha appoint the bhikkhu named so-and-so as the money-discarder. This is the motion.”

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ rūpiyachaḍḍakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno rūpiyachaḍḍakassa sammuti, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. The Sangha is appointing the bhikkhu named so-and-so as the money-discarder. If it is agreeable to the venerable ones to appoint the bhikkhu named so-and-so as the money-discarder, let him be silent; if it is not agreeable, let him speak.”

‘‘Sammato saṅghena itthannāmo bhikkhu rūpiyachaḍḍako. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The Sangha has approved the bhikkhu named so-and-so as the money-discarder. The Sangha approves, therefore it is silent. Thus I hold it.”

Tena sammatena bhikkhunā animittaṃ katvā pātetabbaṃ. Sace nimittaṃ katvā pāteti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

By that appointed bhikkhu, it should be discarded without making a sign. If he discards it after making a sign, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

591. Rūpiye rūpiyasaññī rūpiyaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Rūpiye vematiko rūpiyaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Rūpiye arūpiyasaññī rūpiyaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Arūpiye rūpiyasaññī rūpiyaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Arūpiye vematiko rūpiyaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Arūpiye arūpiyasaññī rūpiyaṃ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

591.If one perceives money as money and causes money to be exchanged, it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If one is doubtful about money and causes money to be exchanged, it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If one perceives money as not money and causes money to be exchanged, it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If one perceives what is not money as money and causes money to be exchanged, it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If one is doubtful about what is not money and causes money to be exchanged, it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense. If one perceives what is not money as not money and causes money to be exchanged, it is to be forfeited and entails a confessional offense.

Arūpiye rūpiyasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Arūpiye vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Arūpiye arūpiyasaññī, anāpatti.

If one perceives what is not money as money, it is an offense of wrong conduct. If one is doubtful about what is not money, it is an offense of wrong conduct. If one perceives what is not money as not money, there is no offense.

592. Anāpatti ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

592.There is no offense for one who is insane or for the first wrongdoer.

Rūpiyasaṃvohārasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The Ninth Training Rule on Monetary Transactions is finished.
