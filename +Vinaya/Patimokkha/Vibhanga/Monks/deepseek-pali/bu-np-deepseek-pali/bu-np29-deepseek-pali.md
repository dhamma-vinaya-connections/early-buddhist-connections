# BU-NP 29

## 9. Sāsaṅkasikkhāpadaṃ

## 9.The training rule on what is risky.

652. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū vutthavassā āraññakesu senāsanesu viharanti. Kattikacorakā bhikkhū – ‘‘laddhalābhā’’ti paripātenti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, āraññakesu senāsanesu viharantena tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitu’’nti.

652.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time bhikkhus who had completed the rains residence were dwelling in wilderness lodgings. Kattika thieves went around plundering the bhikkhus, saying, “We’ve got loot!” They reported this matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and because of this issue, gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, I allow one dwelling in wilderness lodgings to deposit one of the three robes in a village.”

Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū – ‘‘bhagavatā anuññātaṃ āraññakesu senāsanesu viharantena tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitu’’nti tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasanti. Tāni cīvarāni nassantipi vinassantipi ḍayhantipi undūrehipi khajjanti. Bhikkhū duccoḷā honti lūkhacīvarā. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘kissa tumhe, āvuso, duccoḷā lūkhacīvarā’’ti? Atha kho te bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasissantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū te anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasantīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipitvā atirekachārattaṃ vippavasissanti! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

At that time bhikkhus, thinking, “The Blessed One has allowed those dwelling in wilderness lodgings to store one of their three robes in a village,” stored one of their three robes in a village and stayed away for more than six nights. Those robes were lost, destroyed, burned, or eaten by mice. The bhikkhus became poorly clad, with coarse robes. Other bhikkhus asked, “Why are you poorly clad, with coarse robes?” Then those bhikkhus told the others what had happened. Those bhikkhus who were of few desires… complained and criticized them: “How can bhikkhus store one of their three robes in a village and stay away for more than six nights?” Then those bhikkhus, having criticized them in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus store one of their three robes in a village and stay away for more than six nights?” “It’s true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “How can these foolish men… This will not inspire confidence in those without it…” … “And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

653. ‘‘Upavassaṃ kho pana kattikapuṇṇamaṃ yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanāni sāsaṅkasammatāni sappaṭibhayāni tathārūpesu bhikkhu senāsanesu viharanto ākaṅkhamāno tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ antaraghare nikkhipeyya, siyā ca tassa bhikkhuno kocideva paccayo tena cīvarena vippavāsāya. Chārattaparamaṃ tena bhikkhunā tena cīvarena vippavasitabbaṃ. Tato ce uttari vippavaseyya, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

653.“For one who has completed the rains residence, at the Kattika full moon, if a bhikkhu is dwelling in wilderness lodgings that are considered alarming and dangerous, and he wishes to deposit one of his three robes in a village, he may do so. If there should be some reason for that bhikkhu to be separated from that robe, he may be separated from that robe for a maximum of six nights. If he stays away longer than that, unless with the Saṅgha’s agreement, it is to be forfeited and confessed.”

654. Upavassaṃ kho panāti vuṭṭhavassānaṃ.

654.Regarding ‘Upavassaṃ kho pana’: for those who have completed the rains residence.

Kattikapuṇṇamanti kattikacātumāsinī vuccati.

Regarding ‘Kattikapuṇṇamaṃ’: it is said to be the Kattika-cātumāsinī.

[pāci. 573] Yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanānīti āraññakaṃ nāma senāsanaṃ pañcadhanusatikaṃ pacchimaṃ.

Regarding ‘Yāni kho pana tāni āraññakāni senāsanāni’: a wilderness lodging is one that is at least five hundred bow-lengths away.

[pāci. 573] Sāsaṅkaṃ nāma ārāme ārāmūpacāre corānaṃ niviṭṭhokāso dissati, bhuttokāso dissati, ṭhitokāso dissati, nisinnokāso dissati, nipannokāso dissati.

Regarding ‘Sāsaṅkaṃ’: in the monastery or its vicinity, places are seen where thieves have settled, eaten, stood, sat, or lain down.

[pāci. 573] Sappaṭibhayaṃ nāma ārāme ārāmūpacāre corehi manussā hatā dissanti, viluttā dissanti, ākoṭitā dissanti.

Regarding ‘Sappaṭibhayaṃ’: in the monastery or its vicinity, people are seen killed, plundered, or assaulted by thieves.

[pāci. 573] Tathārūpesu bhikkhu senāsanesu viharantoti evarūpesu bhikkhu senāsanesu viharanto.

Regarding ‘Tathārūpesu bhikkhu senāsanesu viharanto’: that is, a bhikkhu dwelling in such lodgings.

Ākaṅkhamānoti icchamāno.

Regarding ‘Ākaṅkhamāno’: intending.

Tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaranti saṅghāṭiṃ vā uttarāsaṅgaṃ vā antaravāsakaṃ vā.

Regarding ‘Tiṇṇaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ’: that is, the outer robe, the upper robe, or the lower robe.

Antaraghare nikkhipeyyāti samantā gocaragāme nikkhipeyya.

Regarding ‘Antaraghare nikkhipeyya’: one should deposit it in the surrounding alms-village.

Siyā ca tassa bhikkhuno kocideva paccayo tena cīvarena vippavāsāyāti siyā paccayo siyā karaṇīyaṃ.

Regarding ‘Siyā ca tassa bhikkhuno kocideva paccayo tena cīvarena vippavāsāya’: there might be a reason, there might be a need.

Chārattaparamaṃ tena bhikkhunā tena cīvarena vippavasitabbanti chārattaparamatā vippavasitabbaṃ.

Regarding ‘Chārattaparamaṃ tena bhikkhunā tena cīvarena vippavasitabbaṃ’: separation for a maximum of six nights is allowable.

Aññatra bhikkhusammutiyāti ṭhapetvā bhikkhusammutiṃ.

Regarding ‘Aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā’: excluding the agreement of the bhikkhus.

Tato ce uttari vippavaseyyāti sattame aruṇuggamane nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ. ‘‘Idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ atirekachārattaṃ vippavuṭṭhaṃ, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmī’’ti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

Regarding ‘Tato ce uttari vippavaseyya’: at the seventh dawn it becomes something to be forfeited. It should be forfeited to the Saṅgha, a group, or an individual. And this, bhikkhus, is how it should be forfeited: “Venerable sirs, this robe of mine has been kept beyond the allowed time while I was away, without the agreement of the bhikkhus. It is to be forfeited. I forfeit this to the Saṅgha.” … should give … should give … “I give this to the venerable one.”

655. Atirekachāratte atirekasaññī vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Atirekachāratte vematiko vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Atirekachāratte ūnakasaññī vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Appaccuddhaṭe paccuddhaṭasaññī…pe… avissajjite vissajjitasaññī… anaṭṭhe naṭṭhasaññī… avinaṭṭhe vinaṭṭhasaññī… adaḍḍhe daḍḍhasaññī … avilutte viluttasaññī vippavasati, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

655.If he remains separated for more than six nights, perceiving it as more, without the agreement of the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and confessed. If he remains separated for more than six nights, being in doubt, without the agreement of the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and confessed. If he remains separated for more than six nights, perceiving it as less, without the agreement of the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and confessed. If he remains separated from a robe that has not been set aside, perceiving it as set aside… from a robe that has not been relinquished, perceiving it as relinquished… from a robe that has not been destroyed, perceiving it as destroyed… from a robe that has not been burned, perceiving it as burned… from a robe that has not been plundered, perceiving it as plundered—if he remains separated for more than six nights without the agreement of the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and confessed.

Nissaggiyaṃ cīvaraṃ anissajjitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakachāratte atirekasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakachāratte vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakachāratte ūnakasaññī, anāpatti.

If he uses a robe requiring forfeiture without forfeiting it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he remains separated for less than six nights, perceiving it as more, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he remains separated for less than six nights, being in doubt, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he remains separated for less than six nights, perceiving it as less, there is no offense.

656. Anāpatti – chārattaṃ vippavasati, ūnakachārattaṃ vippavasati, chārattaṃ vippavasitvā puna gāmasīmaṃ okkamitvā vasitvā pakkamati, anto chārattaṃ paccuddharati, vissajjeti, nassati, vinassati, ḍayhati, acchinditvā gaṇhanti, vissāsaṃ gaṇhanti, bhikkhusammutiyā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

656.There is no offense—if he remains separated for six nights, if he remains separated for less than six nights, if, having remained separated for six nights, he re-enters the village boundary, stays, and then departs, if he takes it back within six nights, if he relinquishes it, if it is lost, if it is destroyed, if it is burned, if they seize it, if they take it with trust, with the bhikkhus’ agreement, if he is insane, if he is the first offender.

Sāsaṅkasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The ninth training rule, on what is risky, is concluded.
