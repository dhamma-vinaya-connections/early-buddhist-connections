# BU-NP 30

## 10. Pariṇatasikkhāpadaṃ

## 10.The training rule concerning what is dedicated.

657. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ aññatarassa pūgassa saṅghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ hoti – ‘‘bhojetvā cīvarena acchādessāmā’’ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū yena so pūgo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ pūgaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘dethāvuso, amhākaṃ imāni cīvarānī’’ti. ‘‘Na mayaṃ, bhante, dassāma. Amhākaṃ saṅghassa anuvassaṃ sacīvarabhikkhā paññattā’’ti. ‘‘Bahū, āvuso, saṅghassa dāyakā, bahū saṅghassa bhattā [bhaddā (ka.)]. Mayaṃ tumhe nissāya tumhe sampassantā idha viharāma. Tumhe ce amhākaṃ na dassatha, atha ko carahi amhākaṃ dassati? Dethāvuso, amhākaṃ imāni cīvarānī’’ti. Atha kho so pūgo chabbaggiyehi bhikkhūhi nippīḷiyamāno yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ datvā saṅghaṃ bhattena parivisi. Ye te bhikkhū jānanti saṅghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ, na ca jānanti chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ dinnanti, te evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘oṇojethāvuso, saṅghassa cīvara’’nti. ‘‘Natthi, bhante. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ ayyā chabbaggiyā attano pariṇāmesu’’nti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

657.At that time the Blessed One was staying at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time in Sāvatthī, a certain community had prepared a meal with robes for the Sangha, thinking, “We will feed them and then present them with robes.” Then the group-of-six bhikkhus approached that community; having approached, they said to that community, “Friends, give us these robes.” “Venerables, we will not give them. Our annual offering of robes is designated for the Sangha.” “Friends, the Sangha has many donors, many benefactors. We live here relying on you, seeing you. If you do not give to us, then who will give to us? Friends, give us these robes.” Pressured by the group-of-six bhikkhus, that community gave the prepared robes to the group-of-six bhikkhus and served the meal to the Sangha. Those bhikkhus who knew that the meal with robes had been prepared for the Sangha but did not know it had been given to the group-of-six bhikkhus said, “Friends, offer the robes to the Sangha.” “There is none, Venerable. The prepared robes have been appropriated by the venerables of the group of six for themselves.” Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained and criticized them, “How could the group-of-six bhikkhus knowingly appropriate for themselves what was dedicated to the Sangha?” Then those bhikkhus criticized the group-of-six bhikkhus in many ways and reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you knowingly appropriated for yourselves what was dedicated to the Sangha?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “Foolish men, how could you knowingly appropriate for yourselves what was dedicated to the Sangha? This will not inspire confidence in those without it…” … “And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

658. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

658.“If a bhikkhu, knowing that a gain belonging to the Sangha has been dedicated, diverts it to himself, it is an offense entailing forfeiture and confession.”

659. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… aya imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

659.Whoever: … A bhikkhu: … The bhikkhu intended in this connection is this one.

[pāci. 491] Jānāti nāma sāmaṃ vā jānāti aññe vā tassa ārocenti so vā āroceti.

Knowingly: either he knows himself, or others inform him, or he informs.

[pāci. 491] Saṅghikaṃ nāma saṅghassa dinnaṃ hoti pariccattaṃ.

Belonging to the Sangha: means it has been given and relinquished to the Sangha.

[pāci. 491] Lābho nāma cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārā. Antamaso cuṇṇapiṇḍopi, dantakaṭṭhampi, dasikasuttampi.

Gain: means robes, almsfood, dwellings, requisites for the sick, and medicines. Even a lump of bath powder, a toothpick, or a piece of string.

[pāci. 491] Pariṇataṃ nāma dassāma karissāmāti vācā bhinnā hoti.

Dedicated: means the words 'we will give' or 'we will do' have been uttered.

Attano pariṇāmeti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ attano pariṇāmitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

Diverts it to himself: there is an offense of wrong-doing for the attempt. Upon acquisition, it becomes subject to forfeiture. It must be forfeited to the Sangha, a group, or an individual. And, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited thus: … 'Venerable sirs, this gain belonging to the Sangha, which I knowingly diverted to myself, is subject to forfeiture. I forfeit this to the Sangha.' … 'The Sangha should give…' … 'I give this to the venerable.'

660. Pariṇate pariṇatasaññī attano pariṇāmeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

660.If, when it has been dedicated, one perceives it as dedicated and diverts it to oneself, it entails an offense entailing forfeiture and confession.

Pariṇate vematiko attano pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Pariṇate apariṇatasaññī attano pariṇāmeti, anāpatti. Saṅghassa pariṇataṃ aññasaṅghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Cetiyassa pariṇataṃ aññacetiyassa vā saṅghassa vā puggalassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalassa pariṇataṃ aññapuggalassa vā saṅghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apariṇate pariṇatasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apariṇate vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apariṇate apariṇatasaññī, anāpatti.

If, when it has been dedicated, one is uncertain about it and diverts it to oneself, it entails an offense of wrong-doing. If, when it has been dedicated, one perceives it as not dedicated and diverts it to oneself, there is no offense. If one diverts what has been dedicated to the Sangha to another Sangha or to a shrine, it entails an offense of wrong-doing. If one diverts what has been dedicated to a shrine to another shrine, to the Sangha, or to an individual, it entails an offense of wrong-doing. If one diverts what has been dedicated to an individual to another individual, to the Sangha, or to a shrine, it entails an offense of wrong-doing. If, when it has not been dedicated, one perceives it as dedicated, it entails an offense of wrong-doing. If, when it has not been dedicated, one is uncertain about it, it entails an offense of wrong-doing. If, when it has not been dedicated, one perceives it as not dedicated, there is no offense.

661. Anāpatti kattha demāti pucchiyamāno yattha tumhākaṃ deyyadhammo paribhogaṃ vā labheyya paṭisaṅkhāraṃ vā labheyya ciraṭṭhitiko vā assa yattha vā pana tumhākaṃ cittaṃ pasīdati tattha dethāti bhaṇati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

661.There is no offense: if, when asked, 'Where should we give?' one says, 'Give it where your donation would be used, or would be restored, or would be long-lasting; or wherever your mind is pleased'; if one is insane; if one is a first offender.

Pariṇatasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ.

The training rule concerning what is dedicated, the tenth, is finished.

Pattavaggo tatiyo.

The third chapter, on bowls, is concluded.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Dve ca pattāni bhesajjaṃ, vassikā dānapañcamaṃ;Sāmaṃ vāyāpanacceko, sāsaṅkaṃ saṅghikena cāti.

Two bowls and medicine, the fifth, rain-cloths; Requesting oneself, commissioning alone, doubtful, along with Sangha property.

662. Uddiṭṭhā kho, āyasmanto, tiṃsa nissaggiyā pācittiyā dhammā. Tatthāyasmante pucchāmi – ‘kaccittha parisuddhā’? Dutiyampi pucchāmi – ‘kaccittha parisuddhā’? Tatiyampi pucchāmi – ‘kaccittha parisuddhā’? Parisuddhetthāyasmanto, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmīti.

662.Venerables, the thirty offenses entailing forfeiture and confession have been recited. In regard to these, I ask the venerables: 'Are you pure in this?' A second time I ask: 'Are you pure in this?' A third time I ask: 'Are you pure in this?' The venerables are pure in this, therefore they are silent. Thus I hold it.

Nissaggiyakaṇḍaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Nissaggiya Section is concluded.

Pārājikapāḷi niṭṭhitā.

The Pārājika Pāḷi is concluded.
