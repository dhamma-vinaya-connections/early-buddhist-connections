# BU-NP 12

## 2. Suddhakāḷakasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2.The Training Rule on Pure Black Wool (Suddhakāḷakasikkhāpada)

547. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpenti. Manussā vihāracārikaṃ āhiṇḍantā passitvā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessanti, seyyathāpi gihī kāmabhogino’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

547.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Vesālī, in the Great Wood, at the Kūṭāgāra Hall. At that time the group of six bhikkhus were having rugs made entirely of pure black sheep’s wool. People, while wandering about on a monastery tour, saw them and complained, grumbled, and spread it about: “How can these Sakyan ascetics have rugs made entirely of pure black sheep’s wool, just like householders who indulge in sensual pleasures!” The bhikkhus heard those people complaining, grumbling, and spreading it about. Those bhikkhus who were modest…pe… complained, grumbled, and spread it about: “How can the group of six bhikkhus have rugs made entirely of pure black sheep’s wool!” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the group of six bhikkhus in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One…pe… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you are having rugs made entirely of pure black sheep’s wool?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them…pe… “How can you, foolish men, have rugs made entirely of pure black sheep’s wool! This will not inspire confidence in those without it…pe… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

548. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

548.“Whatever bhikkhu should have a rug made entirely of pure black sheep’s wool, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession.”

549. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

549.‘Whatever’ means whoever, of whatever kind…pe… ‘bhikkhu’ means…pe… The bhikkhu intended in this case is this one.

Kāḷakaṃ nāma dve kāḷakāni – jātiyā kāḷakaṃ vā rajanakāḷakaṃ vā.

‘Black’ means two kinds of black: naturally black or dyed black.

Santhataṃ nāma santharitvā kataṃ hoti avāyimaṃ.

‘Rug’ means something made by spreading, not woven.

Kārāpeyyāti karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ …pe… idaṃ me, bhante, suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

‘Has made’ means if one makes it or has it made, there is an offense of wrong-doing in the attempt. Upon acquisition, it becomes subject to forfeiture. It must be forfeited to the Sangha, to a group, or to an individual. And thus, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited…pe… ‘Venerable sirs, this rug made entirely of pure black sheep’s wool, which I had made, is subject to forfeiture. I forfeit it to the Sangha.’…pe… ‘It should be given.’…pe… ‘They should give it.’…pe… ‘I give it to the venerable one.’

550. Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

550.If one begins it oneself and finishes it oneself, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession. If one begins it oneself and others finish it, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession. If others begin it and one finishes it oneself, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession. If others begin it and others finish it, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If one makes it or has it made for another’s sake, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If one obtains something made by another and uses it, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

551. Anāpatti vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

551.There is no offense if one makes a canopy, a ground covering, a matting wall, a mattress, or a bolster; for one who is insane; or for the first offender.

Suddhakāḷakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The second training rule on pure black wool is concluded.
