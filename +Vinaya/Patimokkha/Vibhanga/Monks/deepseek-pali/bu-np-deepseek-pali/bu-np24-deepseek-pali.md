# BU-NP 24

## 4. Vassikasāṭikasikkhāpadaṃ

## 4.The training rule concerning the rains-bathing cloth.

626. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ vassikasāṭikā anuññātā hoti. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū – ‘‘bhagavatā vassikasāṭikā anuññātā’’ti, paṭikacceva [paṭigacceva (sī.)] vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesanti, paṭikacceva katvā nivāsenti, jiṇṇāya vassikasāṭikāya naggā kāyaṃ ovassāpenti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū paṭikacceva vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesissanti, paṭikacceva katvā nivāsessanti, jiṇṇāya vassikasāṭikāya naggā kāyaṃ ovassāpessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, paṭikacceva vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesatha? Paṭikacceva katvā nivāsetha? Jiṇṇāya vassikasāṭikāya naggā kāyaṃ ovassāpethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe moghapurisā, paṭikacceva vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesissatha, paṭikacceva katvā nivāsessatha, jiṇṇāya vassikasāṭikāya naggā kāyaṃ ovassāpessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

626.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta’s Grove, Anathapindika’s Park. At that time the Blessed One had allowed the rains-bathing cloth for the bhikkhus. The bhikkhus of the group of six, thinking, ‘The Blessed One has allowed the rains-bathing cloth,’ sought out the rains-bathing cloth in advance, had it made in advance, and wore it in advance. When their rains-bathing cloths wore out, they let the rain fall on their naked bodies. Those bhikkhus who were of few desires… criticized, complained, and spread it about: ‘How can these bhikkhus of the group of six seek out the rains-bathing cloth in advance, have it made in advance, wear it in advance, and when their rains-bathing cloths wore out, let the rain fall on their naked bodies?’ Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the bhikkhus of the group of six in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… ‘Is it true, bhikkhus, that you seek out the rains-bathing cloth in advance, have it made in advance, wear it in advance, and when your rains-bathing cloths wore out, let the rain fall on your naked bodies?’ ‘It is true, Blessed One.’ The Blessed One rebuked them… ‘How can you, foolish men, seek out the rains-bathing cloth in advance, have it made in advance, wear it in advance, and when your rains-bathing cloths wore out, let the rain fall on your naked bodies? This, foolish men, is not for the confidence of those who are not yet confident… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus –

627. ‘‘Māso seso gimhāna’nti bhikkhunā vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesitabbaṃ; ‘addhamāso seso gimhāna’nti katvā nivāsetabbaṃ. ‘Orena ce māso seso gimhāna’nti vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyeseyya, ‘orenaddhamāso seso gimhāna’nti katvā nivāseyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

627.‘When a month of the hot season remains,’ a bhikkhu should seek a rains-bathing cloth; ‘when half a month of the hot season remains,’ he should have it made and wear it. If he seeks a rains-bathing cloth when more than a month of the hot season remains, or has it made and wears it when more than half a month of the hot season remains, it entails forfeiture and confession.

628. ‘Māso seso gimhāna’nti bhikkhunā vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesitabbanti. Ye manussā pubbepi vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ denti te upasaṅkamitvā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘‘kālo vassikasāṭikāya, samayo vassikasāṭikāya, aññepi manussā vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ dentī’’ti. Na vattabbā – ‘‘detha me vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ, āharatha me vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ, parivattetha me vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ, cetāpetha me vassikasāṭikacīvara’’nti.

628.The phrase ‘When a month of the hot season remains,’ means that a bhikkhu should seek a rains-bathing cloth. Those people who have previously given rains-bathing cloths should be approached and addressed thus: ‘It is time for the rains-bathing cloth, the season for the rains-bathing cloth; other people also are giving rains-bathing cloths.’ One should not say: ‘Give me a rains-bathing cloth, bring me a rains-bathing cloth, exchange for me a rains-bathing cloth, have a rains-bathing cloth bought for me.’

‘Addhamāso seso gimhāna’nti katvā nivāsetabbanti. Addhamāse sese gimhāne katvā nivāsetabbaṃ.

The phrase ‘When half a month of the hot season remains, he should have it made and wear it’ means that he should have it made and wear it when half a month of the hot season remains.

‘Orena ce māso seso gimhāna’nti atirekamāse sesa gimhāne vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

If, when more than a month of the hot season remains, one seeks a rains-bathing cloth, it entails forfeiture and confession.

‘Orenaddhamāso seso gimhāna’nti atirekaddhamāse sese gimhāne katvā nivāseti, nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ atirekamāse sese gimhāne pariyiṭṭhaṃ atirekaddhamāse sese gimhāne katvā paridahitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti.…Pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

If, when more than half a month of the hot season remains, one has it made and wears it, it entails forfeiture. It must be forfeited to the Sangha, to a group, or to an individual. And this is how, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited: [The bhikkhu] should say: ‘Venerable sirs, this rains-bathing cloth of mine, which I sought when more than a month of the hot season remained, and had made and wore when more than half a month remained, entails forfeiture. I forfeit this to the Sangha.’ [Then the Sangha, a group, or an individual should say:] ‘Give it.’ [The bhikkhu should say:] ‘I give it.’ [The Sangha, a group, or an individual should say:] ‘We give it.’ [The bhikkhu should say:] ‘I give it to the Venerable One.’

629. Atirekamāse sese gimhāne atirekasaññī vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Atirekamāse sese gimhāne vematiko vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Atirekamāse sese gimhāne ūnakasaññī vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

629.If, when more than a month of the hot season remains, one perceives it as more and seeks a rains-bathing cloth, it entails forfeiture and confession. If, when more than a month of the hot season remains, one is doubtful and seeks a rains-bathing cloth, it entails forfeiture and confession. If, when more than a month of the hot season remains, one perceives it as less and seeks a rains-bathing cloth, it entails forfeiture and confession.

Atirekaddhamāse sese gimhāne atirekasaññī katvā nivāseti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Atirekaddhamāse sese gimhāne vematiko katvā nivāseti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Atirekaddhamāse sese gimhāne ūnakasaññī katvā nivāseti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

If, when more than half a month of the hot season remains, one perceives it as more and has it made and wears it, it entails forfeiture and confession. If, when more than half a month of the hot season remains, one is doubtful and has it made and wears it, it entails forfeiture and confession. If, when more than half a month of the hot season remains, one perceives it as less and has it made and wears it, it entails forfeiture and confession.

Satiyā vassikasāṭikāya naggo kāyaṃ ovassāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakamāse sese gimhāne atirekasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakamāse sese gimhāne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakamāse sese gimhāne ūnakasaññī, anāpatti.

If, having a rains-bathing cloth, he bathes his body naked, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, when less than a month remains of the hot season, he perceives it as more, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, when less than a month remains of the hot season, he is in doubt, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, when less than a month remains of the hot season, he perceives it as less, there is no offense.

Ūnakaddhamāse sese gimhāne atirekasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakaddhamāse sese gimhāne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakaddhamāse sese gimhāne ūnakasaññī, anāpatti.

If, with less than half a month remaining in the hot season, one perceives it as more, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, with less than half a month remaining in the hot season, one is in doubt, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, with less than half a month remaining in the hot season, one perceives it as less, there is no offense.

630. Anāpatti ‘māso seso gimhāna’nti vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesati, ‘addhamāso seso gimhāna’nti katvā nivāseti, ‘ūnakamāso seso gimhāna’nti vassikasāṭikacīvaraṃ pariyesati, ‘ūnakaddhamāso seso gimhāna’nti katvā nivāseti, pariyiṭṭhāya vassikasāṭikāya vassaṃ ukkaḍḍhiyyati, nivatthāya vassikasāṭikāya vassaṃ ukkaḍḍhiyyati, dhovitvā nikkhipitabbaṃ; samaye nivāsetabbaṃ, acchinnacīvarassa, naṭṭhacīvarassa, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

630.There is no offense if a bhikkhu seeks a rains-bathing cloth thinking, "There is a month remaining of the hot season," or makes it and wears it thinking, "There is half a month remaining of the hot season," or seeks a rains-bathing cloth thinking, "There is less than a month remaining of the hot season," or makes it and wears it thinking, "There is less than half a month remaining of the hot season," or if the rains-bathing cloth has been searched for and the rains are prolonged, or if the rains-bathing cloth has been worn and the rains are prolonged, or if it has been washed and put away, or if it is worn at the proper time, or by one whose robe has been snatched away, or by one whose robe is lost, or in emergencies, or by one who is insane, or by the first offender.

Vassikasāṭikasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The fourth training rule on the rains-bathing robe is finished.
