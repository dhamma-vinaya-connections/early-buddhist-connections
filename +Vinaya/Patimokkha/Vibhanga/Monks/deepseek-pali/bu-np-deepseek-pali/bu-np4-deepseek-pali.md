# BU-NP 4

## 4. Purāṇacīvarasikkhāpadaṃ

## 4.The training rule concerning old robes.

503. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato udāyissa purāṇadutiyikā bhikkhunīsu pabbajitā hoti. Sā āyasmato udāyissa santike abhikkhaṇaṃ āgacchati. Āyasmāpi udāyī tassā bhikkhuniyā santike abhikkhaṇaṃ gacchati. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī tassā bhikkhuniyā santike bhattavissaggaṃ karoti. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena sā bhikkhunī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā tassā bhikkhuniyā purato aṅgajātaṃ vivaritvā āsane nisīdi. Sāpi kho bhikkhunī āyasmato udāyissa purato aṅgajātaṃ vivaritvā āsane nisīdi. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī sāratto tassā bhikkhuniyā aṅgajātaṃ upanijjhāyi. Tassa asuci mucci. Atha kho āyasmā udāyī taṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavoca – ‘‘gaccha, bhagini, udakaṃ āhara, antaravāsakaṃ dhovissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Āharayya, ahameva dhovissāmī’’ti taṃ asuciṃ ekadesaṃ mukhena aggahesi ekadesaṃ aṅgajāte pakkhipi. Sā tena gabbhaṃ gaṇhi. Bhikkhuniyo evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘abrahmacārinī ayaṃ bhikkhunī, gabbhinī’’ti. ‘‘Nāhaṃ, ayye, abrahmacārinī’’ti bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Bhikkhuniyo ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma ayyo udāyī bhikkhuniyā purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpessatī’’ti! Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī bhikkhuniyā purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpessatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, bhikkhuniyā purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpesī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Ñātikā te, udāyi, aññātikā’’ti? ‘‘Aññātikā, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Aññātako, moghapurisa, aññātikāya na jānāti patirūpaṃ vā appatirūpaṃ vā pāsādikaṃ vā apāsādikaṃ vā. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpessasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

503.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time Venerable Udāyī’s former wife had gone forth among the bhikkhunīs. She frequently visited Venerable Udāyī, and Venerable Udāyī frequently visited her. On one occasion, Venerable Udāyī provided food to her. Then, in the morning, Venerable Udāyī dressed, took his bowl and robe, and went to that bhikkhunī. Upon arriving, he exposed his genitals in front of her and sat down on a seat. That bhikkhunī also exposed her genitals in front of Venerable Udāyī and sat down on a seat. Then Venerable Udāyī, lusting after that bhikkhunī, stared at her genitals, and he emitted semen. Venerable Udāyī then said to that bhikkhunī, “Go, sister, fetch some water. I will wash my under-robe.” “Venerable, I will bring the water, I myself will wash it,” she said, and she took some of the semen in her mouth and placed the rest on her genitals. She then became pregnant. The bhikkhunīs said, “This bhikkhunī is unchaste; she is pregnant.” “Sisters, I am not unchaste,” she told the bhikkhunīs what had happened. The bhikkhunīs complained and criticized him, “How could Venerable Udāyī have a bhikkhunī wash his old under-robe?” They then told the bhikkhus. Those bhikkhus of few desires… complained and criticized him, “How could Venerable Udāyī have a bhikkhunī wash his old under-robe?” After rebuking Venerable Udāyī in many ways, they informed the Blessed One… “Is it true, Udāyī, that you had a bhikkhunī wash your old under-robe?” “It is true, Blessed One.” “Is she a relative of yours, Udāyī, or not a relative?” “Not a relative, Blessed One.” “Foolish man, one who is not a relative does not know what is proper or improper, what is acceptable or unacceptable. How could you, foolish man, have a bhikkhunī who is not a relative wash your old under-robe! This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

504. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpeyya vā rajāpeyya vā ākoṭāpeyya vā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

504."If a bhikkhu should have a non-relative bhikkhunī wash, dye, or beat an old robe for him, it entails forfeiture and confession."

505. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

505."Yo pana": The word 'yo' means whoever… 'bhikkhu': …in this case, 'bhikkhu' refers to the bhikkhu intended.

Aññātikā nāma mātito vā pitito vā yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā asambaddhā.

"Not a relative" means someone not connected, either through the mother's or father's side, back to the seventh generation of ancestors.

Bhikkhunī nāma ubhatosaṅghe upasampannā.

"Bhikkhunī" means one who has received the full ordination in both Saṅghas.

Purāṇacīvaraṃ nāma sakiṃ nivatthampi sakiṃ pārutampi.

"Old robe" means a robe that has been worn or used even once.

Dhovāti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Dhotaṃ nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Rajāti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Rattaṃ nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Ākoṭehīti āṇāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Sakiṃ pāṇippahāraṃ vā muggarappahāraṃ vā dinne nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, purāṇacīvaraṃ aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā dhovāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

He orders (someone): “Wash it,” there is an offense of wrong-doing. What has been washed entails forfeiture. He orders (someone): “Dye it,” there is an offense of wrong-doing. What has been dyed entails forfeiture. He orders (someone): “Beat it,” there is an offense of wrong-doing. Once a blow of the hand or a blow of the mallet has been given, it entails forfeiture. It is to be forfeited to the Saṅgha, a group, or an individual. And thus, bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited… (as before)… “Venerable sirs, this old robe of mine, having been washed by a bhikkhunī unrelated (to me), entails forfeiture. I forfeit it to the Saṅgha.”… (as before)… “The Saṅgha should give… should give… I give (this) to the venerable one.”

506. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpeti rajāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpeti ākoṭāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpeti rajāpeti ākoṭāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ.

506.If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe washed, it entails forfeiture and confession. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe washed and dyed, it entails forfeiture and an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe washed and beaten, it entails forfeiture and an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe washed, dyed, and beaten, it entails forfeiture and two offenses of wrong-doing.

Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ rajāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ rajāpeti ākoṭāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ rajāpeti dhovāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ rajāpeti ākoṭāpeti dhovāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ.

If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe dyed, it entails forfeiture and confession. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe dyed and beaten, it entails forfeiture and an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe dyed and washed, it entails forfeiture and an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe dyed, beaten, and washed, it entails forfeiture and two offenses of wrong-doing.

Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ ākoṭāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ ākoṭāpeti dhovāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ ākoṭāpeti rajāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī purāṇacīvaraṃ ākoṭāpeti dhovāpeti rajāpeti, nissaggiyena āpatti dvinnaṃ dukkaṭānaṃ.

If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe beaten, it entails forfeiture and confession. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe beaten and washed, it entails forfeiture and an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe beaten and dyed, it entails forfeiture and an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving an unrelated bhikkhunī as such, he has an old robe beaten, washed, and dyed, it entails forfeiture and two offenses of wrong-doing.

Aññātikāya vematiko…pe… aññātikāya ñātikasaññī…pe… aññassa purāṇacīvaraṃ dhovāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Nisīdanapaccattharaṇaṃ dhovāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ekatoupasampannāya dhovāpeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātikāya aññātikasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātikāya vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātikāya ñātikasaññī, anāpatti.

If he is in doubt whether she is unrelated… if he perceives an unrelated bhikkhunī as related… if he has another’s old robe washed, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he has a sitting cloth or spread washed, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he has it washed by a bhikkhunī ordained only by the bhikkhunī Saṅgha, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he perceives a related bhikkhunī as unrelated, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he is in doubt whether she is related, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he perceives a related bhikkhunī as related, there is no offense.

507. Anāpatti ñātikāya dhovantiyā aññātikā dutiyā hoti, avuttā dhovati, aparibhuttaṃ dhovāpeti, cīvaraṃ ṭhapetvā aññaṃ parikkhāraṃ dhovāpeti, sikkhamānāya, sāmaṇeriyā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

507.There is no offense if a female relative is washing and an unrelated one assists; if she washes without being asked; if he has an unused robe washed; if he has requisites other than robes washed; if it is done by a female trainee, a female novice, an insane person, or the first offender.

Purāṇacīvarasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The training rule on using cast-off robes, the fourth, is finished.
