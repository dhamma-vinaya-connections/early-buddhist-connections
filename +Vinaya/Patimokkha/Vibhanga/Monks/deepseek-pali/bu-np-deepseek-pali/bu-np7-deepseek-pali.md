# BU-NP 7

## 7. Tatuttarisikkhāpadaṃ

## 7.The training rule concerning exceeding the limit.

522. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū acchinnacīvarake bhikkhū upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘bhagavatā, āvuso, anuññātaṃ – ‘acchinnacīvarassa vā naṭṭhacīvarassa vā aññātakaṃ gahapatiṃ vā gahapatāniṃ vā cīvaraṃ viññāpetuṃ’; viññāpetha, āvuso, cīvara’’nti. ‘‘Alaṃ, āvuso, laddhaṃ amhehi cīvara’’nti. ‘‘Mayaṃ āyasmantānaṃ viññāpemā’’ti. ‘‘Viññāpetha, āvuso’’ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū gahapatike upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ – ‘‘acchinnacīvarakā, āvuso, bhikkhū āgatā. Detha nesaṃ cīvarānī’’ti, bahuṃ cīvaraṃ viññāpesuṃ.

522.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus approached bhikkhus whose robes had not been taken away and said, “Friends, the Blessed One has allowed asking for robes from an unacquainted householder or householder’s wife when one’s robes are lost or taken away. Ask for robes, friends.” “Enough, friends, we have obtained robes.” “We will ask on your behalf.” “Ask, friends.” Then the group of six bhikkhus approached householders and said, “Friends, bhikkhus whose robes had not been taken away have arrived. Give them robes.” They asked for a large amount of robes.

Tena kho pana samayena aññataro puriso sabhāyaṃ nisinno aññataraṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘acchinnacīvarakā ayyo bhikkhū āgatā. Tesaṃ mayā cīvaraṃ dinna’’nti. Sopi evamāha – ‘‘mayāpi dinna’’nti. Aparopi evamāha – ‘‘mayāpi dinna’’nti. Te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā na mattaṃ jānitvā bahuṃ cīvaraṃ viññāpessanti, dussavāṇijjaṃ vā samaṇā sakyaputtiyā karissanti, paggāhikasālaṃ [paṭaggāhikasālaṃ (?)] vā pasāressantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū na mattaṃ jānitvā bahuṃ cīvaraṃ viññāpessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, na mattaṃ jānitvā bahuṃ cīvaraṃ viññāpethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, na mattaṃ jānitvā bahuṃ cīvaraṃ viññāpessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

At that time, a certain man sitting in the assembly hall said to another man: “Venerable bhikkhus whose robes had not been taken away have arrived. I have given them robes.” The other replied, “I too have given.” Another also said, “I too have given.” They complained, criticized, and grumbled: “How can these Sakyan ascetics, not knowing moderation, ask for many robes? Are the Sakyan ascetics going to engage in the cloth trade or set up a shop for selling cloth?” The bhikkhus heard those people complaining, criticizing, and grumbling. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, criticized, and grumbled: “How can the bhikkhus from the group of six ask for many robes without knowing moderation?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the bhikkhus from the group of six in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you ask for many robes without knowing moderation?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked them… “Foolish men, how can you ask for many robes without knowing moderation? This will not inspire confidence in those without it or increase the confidence of those who have it…” “And so, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

523. ‘‘Tañce aññātako gahapati vā gahapatānī vā bahūhi cīvarehi abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreyya santaruttaraparamaṃ tena bhikkhunā tato cīvaraṃ sāditabbaṃ. Tato ce uttari sādiyeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

523.“If an unacquainted householder or householder’s wife invites that bhikkhu with many robes, having brought them forth, that bhikkhu may accept at most an inner and an outer robe. If he accepts more than that, it is to be forfeited and confessed.”

524. Tañceti acchinnacīvarakaṃ bhikkhuṃ.

524.“That” refers to a bhikkhu whose robes have not been taken away.

Aññātako nāma mātito vā pitito vā yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā asambaddho.

“Unacquainted” means not connected through mother or father up to the seventh generation of ancestors.

Gahapati nāma yo koci agāraṃ ajjhāvasati.

“Householder” means anyone who dwells in a house.

Gahapatānī nāma yā kāci agāraṃ ajjhāvasati.

“Householder’s wife” means any woman who dwells in a house.

Bahūhi cīvarehīti bahukehi cīvarehi.

“With many robes” means with numerous robes.

Abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreyyāti yāvatakaṃ icchasi tāvatakaṃ gaṇhāhīti.

“Invites, having brought forth” means: “Take as much as you wish.”

Santaruttaraparamaṃ tena bhikkhunā tato cīvaraṃ sāditabbanti sace tīṇi naṭṭhāni honti dve sāditabbāni, dve naṭṭhāni ekaṃ sāditabbaṃ, ekaṃ naṭṭhaṃ na kiñci sāditabbaṃ.

“Up to the limit of an inner and outer robe, that bhikkhu may accept a robe from that [offering]” means: if three are lost, two may be accepted; if two are lost, one may be accepted; if one is lost, nothing may be accepted.

Tato ce uttari sādiyeyyāti tatuttari viññāpeti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ aññātakaṃ gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā tatuttari viññāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

“If he accepts more than that” means if he asks for more than that, there is an offense of wrong conduct in the attempt. Upon receipt, it becomes subject to forfeiture. It is to be forfeited to the Sangha, to a group, or to an individual. And so, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited… “Venerable sirs, this robe, having approached an unacquainted householder, I asked for more than allowed—it is subject to forfeiture. I forfeit this to the Sangha.” … He should give… They should give… “I give this to the venerable.”

525. Aññātake aññātakasaññī tatuttari cīvaraṃ viññāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake vematiko tatuttari cīvaraṃ viññāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake ñātakasaññī tatuttari cīvaraṃ viññāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

525.If, perceiving an unacquainted person as unacquainted, he asks for a robe beyond the limit, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed. If, being in doubt about whether the person is unacquainted, he asks for a robe beyond the limit, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed. If, perceiving an unacquainted person as an acquaintance, he asks for a robe beyond the limit, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed.

Ñātake aññātakasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake ñātakasaññī, anāpatti.

If, perceiving an acquainted person as unacquainted, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, being in doubt about whether the person is acquainted, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, perceiving an acquainted person as acquainted, there is no offense.

526. Anāpatti – ‘‘sesakaṃ āharissāmī’’ti haranto gacchati, ‘‘sesakaṃ tuyheva hotū’’ti denti, na acchinnakāraṇā denti, na naṭṭhakāraṇā denti, ñātakānaṃ, pavāritānaṃ, attano dhanena, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

526.There is no offense: if one takes it thinking, “I will bring the remainder”; if they give it, saying, “The remainder is yours”; if they give it not because the robes were taken away; if they give it not because the robes were lost; for relatives; for those who have invited; with one’s own wealth; for an insane person; for the first offender.

Tatuttarisikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The seventh training rule concerning exceeding the limit is finished.
