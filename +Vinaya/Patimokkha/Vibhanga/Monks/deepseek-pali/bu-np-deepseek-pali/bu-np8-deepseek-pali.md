# BU-NP 8

## 8. Upakkhaṭasikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The training rule concerning what is prepared.

527. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro puriso pajāpatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’ti. Assosi kho aññataro piṇḍacāriko bhikkhu tassa purisassa imaṃ vācaṃ bhāsamānassa. Atha kho so bhikkhu yenāyasmā upanando sakyaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mahāpuññosi tvaṃ, āvuso upananda, amukasmiṃ okāse aññataro puriso pajāpatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’’ti. ‘‘Atthāvuso, maṃ so upaṭṭhāko’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto yena so puriso tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saccaṃ kira maṃ tvaṃ, āvuso, cīvarena acchādetukāmosī’’ti? ‘‘Api meyya, evaṃ hoti – ‘ayyaṃ upanandaṃ cīvarena acchādessāmī’’’ti. ‘‘Sace kho maṃ tvaṃ, āvuso, cīvarena acchādetukāmosi, evarūpena cīvarena acchādehi. Kyāhaṃ tena acchannopi karissāmi yāhaṃ na paribhuñjissāmī’’ti.

527.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. Now on that occasion, a certain man said to his wife: “I will clothe the venerable Upananda with a robe.” A certain alms-collecting bhikkhu heard that man speaking these words. Then that bhikkhu approached the Venerable Upananda the Sakyan; having approached, he said to the Venerable Upananda the Sakyan: “You are very fortunate, friend Upananda. In such-and-such a place, a certain man said to his wife: ‘I will clothe the venerable Upananda with a robe.’” “Indeed, friend, he is my patron.” Then the Venerable Upananda the Sakyan approached that man; having approached, he said to that man: “Is it true, friend, that you wish to clothe me with a robe?” “Yes, friend, it is so—‘I will clothe the venerable Upananda with a robe.’” “If you wish to clothe me with a robe, friend, then clothe me with such a robe. For what use is a robe to me if I will not use it?”

Atha kho so puriso ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘mahicchā ime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā asantuṭṭhā. Nayime sukarā cīvarena acchādetuṃ. Kathañhi nāma ayyo upanando mayā pubbe appavārito maṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tassa purisassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto pubbe appavārito gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ …pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, pubbe appavārito gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Ñātako te, upananda, aññātako’’ti? ‘‘Aññātako, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Aññātako, moghapurisa, aññātakassa na jānāti patirūpaṃ vā appatirūpaṃ vā santaṃ vā asantaṃ vā. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, pubbe appavārito aññātakaṃ gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then that man complained, grumbled, and protested: “These Sakyan ascetics are greedy and insatiable. It is not easy to clothe them with robes. How could the venerable Upananda, without being previously invited, approach me and make a stipulation about a robe?” The bhikkhus heard that man complaining, grumbling, and protesting. Those bhikkhus who were modest… they too complained, grumbled, and protested: “How could the Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, without being previously invited, approach a householder and make a stipulation about a robe?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the Venerable Upananda the Sakyan in many ways, informed the Blessed One of the matter… “Is it true, Upananda, that you, without being previously invited, approached a householder and made a stipulation about a robe?” “It is true, Blessed One.” “Was he a relative of yours, Upananda, or a non-relative?” “A non-relative, Blessed One.” “Foolish man, a non-relative does not know what is suitable or unsuitable, existent or non-existent. How could you, foolish man, without being previously invited, approach a non-relative householder and make a stipulation about a robe? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… Therefore, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule as follows:

528. ‘‘Bhikkhuṃ paneva uddissa aññātakassa gahapatissa vā gahapatāniyā vā cīvaracetāpannaṃ nti.

528.“If a robe-fund intended for a bhikkhu is prepared by an unrelated householder or householder’s wife, thinking: ‘Having purchased a robe with this robe-fund, I will clothe such-and-such a bhikkhu’; and if that bhikkhu, not having been previously invited, approaches and makes a stipulation concerning the robe, saying: ‘It would be good, venerable sir, if you would purchase such-and-such a robe with this robe-fund and clothe me,’ motivated by the desire for something excellent, it is a nissaggiya pācittiya.”

529. Bhikkhuṃ paneva uddissāti bhikkhussatthāya, bhikkhuṃ ārammaṇaṃ karitvā, bhikkhuṃ acchādetukāmo.

529.“Intended for a bhikkhu” means: for the sake of a bhikkhu, making a bhikkhu the object, wishing to clothe a bhikkhu.

Aññātako nāma mātito vā pitito vā yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā asambaddho.

“Unrelated” means not connected through mother or father up to the seventh generation of ancestors.

Gahapati nāma yo koci agāraṃ ajjhāvasati.

“Householder” means anyone who dwells in a house.

Gahapatānī nāma yā kāci agāraṃ ajjhāvasati.

“Householder’s wife” means any woman who dwells in a house.

Cīvaracetāpannaṃ nāma hiraññaṃ vā suvaṇṇaṃ vā muttā vā maṇi vā pavāḷo vā phaliko vā paṭako vā suttaṃ vā kappāso vā.

“Robe-fund” means gold, silver, pearls, gems, coral, crystal, cloth, thread, or cotton.

Iminā cīvaracetāpannenāti paccupaṭṭhitena.

“With this robe-fund” means what is at hand.

Cetāpetvāti parivattetvā.

“Cetāpetvā” means having exchanged.

Acchādessāmīti dassāmi.

“I will clothe” means I will give.

Tatra ce so bhikkhūti yaṃ bhikkhuṃ uddissa cīvaracetāpannaṃ upakkhaṭaṃ hoti so bhikkhu.

“If that bhikkhu” refers to the bhikkhu for whom the robe-fund has been prepared.

Pubbe appavāritoti pubbe avutto hoti – ‘‘kīdisena te, bhante, cīvarena attho, kīdisaṃ te cīvaraṃ cetāpemī’’ti?

“Not having been previously invited” means not having been told beforehand: ‘Venerable, what kind of robe do you need? What kind of robe shall I have purchased for you?’

Upasaṅkamitvāti gharaṃ gantvā yattha katthaci upasaṅkamitvā .

“Approaching” means going to the house or approaching anywhere.

Cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjeyyāti āyataṃ vā hotu vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā saṇhaṃ vā.

“Makes a stipulation concerning the robe” means: ‘May it be long, or wide, or closely woven, or smooth.’

Iminā cīvaracetāpannenāti paccupaṭṭhitena.

“With this robe-fund” means what is at hand.

Evarūpaṃ vā evarūpaṃ vāti. Āyataṃ vā vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā saṇhaṃ vā.

“Such and such” means long, or wide, or closely woven, or smooth.

Cetāpetvāti parivattetvā.

“Cetāpetvā” means having exchanged.

Acchādehīti dajjehi.

“Clothe me” means give me.

Kalyāṇakamyataṃ upādāyāti sādhatthiko [sādhutthiko (syā.)] mahagghatthiko. Tassa vacanena āyataṃ vā vitthataṃ vā appitaṃ vā saṇhaṃ vā cetāpeti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ pubbe appavārito aññātakaṃ gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpannaṃ nissaggiyaṃ, imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

“Motivated by the desire for something excellent” means desiring something good, something expensive. If, due to his words, they purchase a robe that is long, or wide, or closely woven, or smooth, there is an offense of wrong conduct in the effort. Upon acquisition, it becomes subject to forfeiture. It should be forfeited to the Saṅgha, to a group, or to an individual. And thus, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited: ‘Venerable sirs, this robe, which I obtained by approaching an uninvited, unrelated householder and making a stipulation concerning the robe, is subject to forfeiture. I forfeit it to the Saṅgha.’ … It should be given … It should be given to you … I give this to the venerable.

530. Aññātake aññātakasaññī pubbe appavārito gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake vematiko pubbe appavārito gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātake ñātakasaññī pubbe appavārito gahapatikaṃ upasaṅkamitvā cīvare vikappaṃ āpajjati, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

530.If he perceives an unrelated person as unrelated and, not having been previously invited, approaches a householder and makes a stipulation concerning a robe, it is a nissaggiya pācittiya. If he is in doubt whether the person is unrelated and, not having been previously invited, approaches a householder and makes a stipulation concerning a robe, it is a nissaggiya pācittiya. If he perceives an unrelated person as related and, not having been previously invited, approaches a householder and makes a stipulation concerning a robe, it is a nissaggiya pācittiya.

Ñātake aññātakasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātake ñātakasaññī, anāpatti.

If he perceives a relative as unrelated and makes a stipulation, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If he is in doubt whether the person is a relative and makes a stipulation, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If he perceives a relative as a relative and makes a stipulation, there is no offense.

531. Anāpatti ñātakānaṃ, pavāritānaṃ, aññassatthāya, attano dhanena, mahagghaṃ cetāpetukāmassa appagghaṃ cetāpeti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

531.There is no offense for relatives; for those who have been invited; for the sake of another; with one’s own wealth; if, when something expensive was intended, one causes something inexpensive to be purchased; for one who is insane; or for the first offender.

Upakkhaṭasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The eighth training rule, concerning what is prepared, is concluded.
