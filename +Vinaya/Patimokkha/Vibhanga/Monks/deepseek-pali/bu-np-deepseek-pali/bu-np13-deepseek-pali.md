# BU-NP 13

## 3. Dvebhāgasikkhāpadaṃ

## 3.The Training Rule on Two Parts

552. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpetu’’nti, te thokaṃyeva odātaṃ ante ādiyitvā tatheva suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpenti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū thokaṃyeva odātaṃ ante ādiyitvā tatheva suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, thokaṃyeva odātaṃ ante ādiyitvā tatheva suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, thokaṃyeva odātaṃ ante ādiyitvā tatheva suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ santhataṃ kārāpessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

552.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus, thinking, 'The Blessed One has prohibited making a sitting cloth entirely of black wool,' took just a little white wool for the border and had a sitting cloth made entirely of black wool. Those bhikkhus who were modest... complained, murmured, and criticized, saying, 'How can the group of six bhikkhus, taking just a little white wool for the border, have a sitting cloth made entirely of black wool?' Then those bhikkhus, having criticized the group of six bhikkhus in many ways, reported this matter to the Blessed One. The Blessed One... asked, 'Is it true, bhikkhus, that having taken just a little white wool for the border, you are having a sitting cloth made entirely of black wool?' 'It is true, Blessed One.' The Blessed One rebuked them... 'How can you, foolish men, having taken just a little white wool for the border, have a sitting cloth made entirely of black wool? This will not lead to the confidence of the unconfident... And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

553. ‘‘Navaṃ pana bhikkhunā santhataṃ kārayamānena dve bhāgā suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ ādātabbā tatiyaṃ odātānaṃ catutthaṃ gocariyānaṃ. Anādā ce bhikkhu dve bhāge suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ tatiyaṃ odātānaṃ catutthaṃ gocariyānaṃ navaṃ santhataṃ kārāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

553."When a bhikkhu is having a new sitting cloth made, he must take two parts of pure black sheep's wool, a third part of white, and a fourth part of brown. If a bhikkhu does not take two parts of pure black sheep's wool, a third part of white, and a fourth part of brown, and has a new sitting cloth made, it is to be forfeited and confessed."

554. Navaṃ nāma karaṇaṃ upādāya vuccati.

554."New" is said with reference to making.

Santhataṃ nāma santharitvā kataṃ hoti avāyimaṃ.

"A sitting cloth" means something spread out and prepared, not woven.

Kārayamānenāti karonto vā kārāpento vā.

Doing or causing to be done.

Dve bhāgā suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ ādātabbāti dhārayitvā dve tulā ādātabbā.

"Two parts of entirely black sheep’s wool are to be taken’ means that two measures are to be taken, having kept this in mind.

Tatiyaṃ odātānanti tulaṃ odātānaṃ.

"A third of white’ means a measure of white [wool].

Catutthaṃ gocariyānanti tulaṃ gocariyānaṃ.

"A fourth of brown’ means a measure of brown [wool].

Anādā ce bhikkhu dve bhāge suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ tatiyaṃ odātānaṃ catutthaṃ gocariyānanti. Anādiyitvā dve tule suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ tulaṃ odātānaṃ tulaṃ gocariyānaṃ navaṃ santhataṃ karoti vā kārāpeti vā payoge dukkaṭaṃ, paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, santhataṃ anādiyitvā dve tule suddhakāḷakānaṃ eḷakalomānaṃ tulaṃ odātānaṃ tulaṃ gocariyānaṃ kārāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

‘If a bhikkhu does not take two parts of entirely black sheep’s wool, a third of white, and a fourth of brown’ means: if, without taking two measures of entirely black sheep’s wool, one measure of white, and one measure of brown, he makes or has made a new sitting cloth, there is an offense of wrong-doing in the effort, and upon acquisition it becomes subject to forfeiture. It must be forfeited to the Saṅgha, to a group, or to an individual. And thus, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited: ‘Venerable sirs, this sitting cloth of mine, made without taking two measures of entirely black sheep’s wool, one measure of white, and one measure of brown, is subject to forfeiture. I forfeit it to the Saṅgha.’... ‘The Saṅgha should give it’... ‘They should give it’... ‘I give it to the venerable one.’

555. Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

555.If he himself finishes what he himself started, it is to be forfeited and requires confession. If he finishes through others what he himself started, it is to be forfeited and requires confession. If he himself finishes what others started, it is to be forfeited and requires confession. If he finishes through others what others started, it is to be forfeited and requires confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he makes it himself or has another make it for the benefit of another, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If, having received something made by another, he uses it, it is an offense of wrong-doing.

556. Anāpatti tulaṃ odātānaṃ tulaṃ gocariyānaṃ ādiyitvā karoti, bahutaraṃ odātānaṃ bahutaraṃ gocariyānaṃ ādiyitvā karoti, suddhaṃ odātānaṃ suddhaṃ gocariyānaṃ ādiyitvā karoti, vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

556.There is no offense if, having taken one measure of white and one measure of brown, he makes it; if, having taken more white and more brown, he makes it; if, having taken purely white or purely brown, he makes it; if he makes a canopy, or a floor covering, or a wall screen, or a mattress, or a bolster; if he is insane; if he is the first to offend.

Dvebhāgasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The training rule on two parts is finished, the third [is next].
