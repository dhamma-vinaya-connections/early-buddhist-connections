# BU-NP 11

## 1. Kosiyasikkhāpadaṃ

## 1.The Training Rule on Silk (Kosiyasikkhāpada)

542. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā āḷaviyaṃ viharati aggāḷave cetiye. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kosiyakārake upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘bahū, āvuso, kosakārake pacatha, amhākampi dassatha, mayampi icchāma kosiyamissakaṃ santhataṃ kātu’’nti. Te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā amhe upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vakkhanti – ‘bahū, āvuso, kosakārake pacatha, amhākampi dassatha, mayampi icchāma kosiyamissakaṃ santhataṃ kātu’nti! Amhākampi alābhā, amhākampi dulladdhaṃ, ye mayaṃ ājīvassa hetu puttadārassa kāraṇā bahū khuddake pāṇe saṅghātaṃ āpādemā’’ti. Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kosiyakārake upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vakkhanti – ‘bahū, āvuso, kosakārake pacatha, amhākampi dassatha, mayampi icchāma kosiyamissakaṃ santhataṃ kātu’’’nti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, kosiyakārake upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadetha – ‘bahū, āvuso, kosakārake pacatha, amhākampi dassatha, mayampi icchāma kosiyamissakaṃ santhataṃ kātu’’’nti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, kosiyakārake upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vakkhatha – bahū, āvuso, kosakārake pacatha, amhākampi dassatha, mayampi icchāma kosiyamissakaṃ santhataṃ kātunti. Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

542.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Āḷavī, at the Aggāḷava Shrine. Now at that time, the group of six bhikkhus approached silk workers and said: “Friends, prepare many silk cocoons; give some to us too; we also wish to make a rug with silk mixed in.” They complained, grumbled, and spread it about: “How can these Sakyan ascetics approach us and say, ‘Friends, prepare many silk cocoons; give some to us too; we also wish to make a rug with silk mixed in’? It is our misfortune, our bad luck, that for the sake of livelihood and for the sake of wives and children, we must bring about the destruction of many small creatures!” The bhikkhus heard those people complaining, grumbling, and spreading it about. Those bhikkhus who were modest…pe… complained, grumbled, and spread it about: “How can the group of six bhikkhus approach silk workers and say, ‘Friends, prepare many silk cocoons; give some to us too; we also wish to make a rug with silk mixed in’?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the group of six bhikkhus in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One…pe… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you approached silk workers and said, ‘Friends, prepare many silk cocoons; give some to us too; we also wish to make a rug with silk mixed in’?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them…pe… “How can you, foolish men, approach silk workers and say, ‘Friends, prepare many silk cocoons; give some to us too; we also wish to make a rug with silk mixed in’? This will not inspire confidence in those without it…pe… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

543. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu kosiyamissakaṃ santhataṃ kārāpeyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

543.“Whatever bhikkhu should have a rug made with silk mixed in, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession.”

544. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

544.‘Whatever’ means whoever, of whatever kind…pe… ‘bhikkhu’ means…pe… The bhikkhu intended in this case is this one.

Santhataṃ nāma santharitvā kataṃ hoti avāyimaṃ.

‘Rug’ means something made by spreading, not woven.

Kārāpeyyāti ekenapi kosiyaṃsunā missitvā karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabba…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, kosiyamissakaṃ santhataṃ kārāpitaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

‘Has made’ means if one makes it or has it made with even a single silk thread mixed in, there is an offense of wrong-doing in the attempt. Upon acquisition, it becomes subject to forfeiture. It must be forfeited to the Sangha, to a group, or to an individual. And thus, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited…pe… ‘Venerable sirs, this rug made with silk mixed in, which I had made, is subject to forfeiture. I forfeit it to the Sangha.’…pe… ‘It should be given.’…pe… ‘They should give it.’…pe… ‘I give it to the venerable one.’

545. Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

545.If one begins it oneself and finishes it oneself, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession. If one begins it oneself and others finish it, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession. If others begin it and one finishes it oneself, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession. If others begin it and others finish it, it is subject to forfeiture and entails a confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If one makes it or has it made for another’s sake, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If one obtains something made by another and uses it, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

546. Anāpatti vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

546.There is no offense if one makes a canopy, a ground covering, a matting wall, a mattress, or a bolster; for one who is insane; or for the first offender.

Kosiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The first training rule on silk is concluded.
