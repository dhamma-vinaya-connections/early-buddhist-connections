# BU-NP 5

## 5. Cīvarapaṭiggahaṇasikkhāpadaṃ

## 5.The training rule concerning the acceptance of robes.

508. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī sāvatthiyaṃ viharati. Atha kho uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Sāvatthiyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātappaṭikkantā yena andhavanaṃ tenupasaṅkami divāvihārāya. Andhavanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle divāvihāraṃ nisīdi. Tena kho pana samayena corā katakammā gāviṃ vadhitvā maṃsaṃ gahetvā andhavanaṃ pavisiṃsu. Addasā kho coragāmaṇiko uppalavaṇṇaṃ bhikkhuniṃ aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle divāvihāraṃ nisinnaṃ. Disvānassa etadahosi – ‘‘sace me puttabhātukā passissanti viheṭhissanti imaṃ bhikkhuni’’nti aññena maggena agamāsi. Atha kho so coragāmaṇiko maṃse pakke varamaṃsāni gahetvā paṇṇapuṭaṃ [paṇṇena puṭaṃ (syā.)] bandhitvā uppalavaṇṇāya bhikkhuniyā avidūre rukkhe ālaggetvā – ‘‘yo passati samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā dinnaṃyeva haratū’’ti, vatvā pakkāmi. Assosi kho uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī samādhimhā vuṭṭhahitvā tassa coragāmaṇikassa imaṃ vācaṃ bhāsamānassa. Atha kho uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī taṃ maṃsaṃ gahetvā upassayaṃ agamāsi. Atha kho uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī tassā rattiyā accayena taṃ maṃsaṃ sampādetvā uttarāsaṅgena bhaṇḍikaṃ bandhitvā vehāsaṃ abbhuggantvā veḷuvane paccuṭṭhāsi [paccupaṭṭhāsi (?)].

508.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels' Sanctuary. Now at that time the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā was dwelling in Sāvatthī. Then, in the morning, the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā dressed, took her bowl and robe, and entered Sāvatthī for alms. Having wandered for alms in Sāvatthī and returned from her almsround after the meal, she went to the Dark Wood for the day's abiding. Having entered the Dark Wood, she sat down at the foot of a certain tree for the day's abiding. Now at that time some bandits, having done their deed, killed a cow, took the meat, and entered the Dark Wood. The bandit chief saw the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā sitting at the foot of a certain tree for the day's abiding. Upon seeing her, he thought: "If my sons and brothers see her, they will harass this bhikkhunī." So he went away by another path. Then that bandit chief took the best pieces of cooked meat, tied them in a leaf bundle, and hung it on a tree not far from the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā, saying: "Whoever sees this—whether ascetic or brahmin—let him take it as a gift." Having said this, he departed. The bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā, having emerged from meditation, heard that bandit chief speaking these words. Then the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā took that meat and went to her dwelling. When the night had passed, the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā prepared that meat, tied it in a bundle with her upper robe, rose into the air, and stood in the Bamboo Grove.

Tena kho pana samayena bhagavā gāmaṃ piṇḍāya paviṭṭho hoti. Āyasmā udāyī ohiyyako hoti vihārapālo. Atha kho uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī yenāyasmā udāyī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kahaṃ, bhante, bhagavā’’ti? ‘‘Paviṭṭho, bhagini, bhagavā gāmaṃ piṇḍāyā’’ti. ‘‘Imaṃ, bhante, maṃsaṃ bhagavato dehī’’ti. ‘‘Santappito tayā, bhagini, bhagavā maṃsena. Sace me tvaṃ antaravāsakaṃ dadeyyāsi, evaṃ ahampi santappito bhaveyyaṃ antaravāsakenā’’ti. ‘‘Mayaṃ kho, bhante, mātugāmā nāma kicchalābhā. Idañca me antimaṃ pañcamaṃ cīvaraṃ. Nāhaṃ dassāmī’’ti. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhagini, puriso hatthiṃ datvā kacche sajjeyya [vissajjeyya (syā.)] evameva kho tvaṃ bhagini bhagavato maṃsaṃ datvā mayi antaravāsake sajjasī’’ti [antaravāsakaṃ na sajjasīti (ka.), mayhaṃ antaravāsakaṃ vissajjehīti (syā.)]. Atha kho uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī āyasmatā udāyinā nippīḷiyamānā antaravāsakaṃ datvā upassayaṃ agamāsi. Bhikkhuniyo uppalavaṇṇāya bhikkhuniyā pattacīvaraṃ paṭiggaṇhantiyo uppalavaṇṇaṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kahaṃ te, ayye, antaravāsako’’ti? Uppalavaṇṇā bhikkhunī bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Bhikkhuniyo ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma ayyo udāyī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ paṭiggahessati kicchalābho mātugāmo’’ti. Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ paṭiggahessatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, bhikkhuniyā cīvaraṃ paṭiggahesī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Ñātikā te, udāyi, aññātikā’’ti? ‘‘Aññātikā, bhagavā’’ti. ‘‘Aññātako, moghapurisa, aññātikāya na jānāti patirūpaṃ vā appatirūpaṃ vā santaṃ vā asantaṃ vā. Tattha nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā hatthato cīvaraṃ paṭiggahessasi! Netaṃ moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

At that time, the Blessed One had entered the village for alms. Venerable Udāyī was the ohiyyaka, the monastery keeper. Then the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā approached Venerable Udāyī and said, "Where, Venerable, is the Blessed One?" "Sister, the Blessed One has entered the village for alms." "Venerable, please give this meat to the Blessed One." "Sister, the Blessed One is satisfied with meat. If you were to give me your undergarment, then I too would be satisfied with an undergarment." "Venerable, we women are hard-pressed to get such things. And this is my last fifth robe. I will not give it." "Sister, just as a man, having given an elephant, would be stingy about a belt, so too, sister, having given meat to the Blessed One, you are stingy with me about an undergarment." Then, being pressured by Venerable Udāyī, the bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā gave him the undergarment and went to her dwelling. The bhikkhunīs, while receiving Uppalavaṇṇā’s bowl and robe, asked her, "Sister, where is your undergarment?" The bhikkhunī Uppalavaṇṇā told the bhikkhunīs what had happened. The bhikkhunīs complained, criticized, and protested: "How could Venerable Udāyī accept a robe from a bhikkhunī, when women are hard-pressed to get such things!" Then those bhikkhunīs informed the bhikkhus about the matter. Those bhikkhus who were modest… criticized and complained: "How could Venerable Udāyī accept a robe from a bhikkhunī?" Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Udāyī in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… "Is it true, Udāyī, that you accepted a robe from a bhikkhunī?" "It is true, Blessed One." "Is she a relative of yours, Udāyī, or not?" "Not a relative, Blessed One." "Foolish man, one who is not a relative does not know what is proper or improper, good or bad. How could you, foolish man, accept a robe directly from a bhikkhunī who is not a relative? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… Thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited—

509. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā hatthato cīvaraṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

509."If any bhikkhu should receive a robe from the hand of a bhikkhunī who is not a relative, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed."

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.

510. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū kukkuccāyantā bhikkhunīnaṃ pārivattakacīvaraṃ na paṭiggaṇhanti. Bhikkhuniyo ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma ayyā amhākaṃ pārivattakacīvaraṃ na paṭiggahessantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tāsaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ ujjhāyantīnaṃ khiyyantīnaṃ vipācentīnaṃ. Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ pārivattakaṃ paṭiggahetuṃ – bhikkhussa, bhikkhuniyā, sikkhamānāya, sāmaṇerassa, sāmaṇeriyā. Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, imesaṃ pañcannaṃ pārivattakaṃ paṭiggahetuṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha.

510.At that time, bhikkhus, being scrupulous, did not accept exchange robes from the bhikkhunīs. The bhikkhunīs complained, criticized, and protested: "How can the venerables not accept our exchange robes?" The bhikkhus heard the bhikkhunīs complaining, criticizing, and protesting. Then those bhikkhus reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in this connection, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: "I allow, bhikkhus, the acceptance of exchange robes from five: a bhikkhu, a bhikkhunī, a female trainee, a novice bhikkhu, and a novice nun. I allow, bhikkhus, the acceptance of exchange robes from these five. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

511. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā hatthato cīvaraṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, aññatra pārivattakā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

511."If a bhikkhu should receive a robe from the hand of a bhikkhunī who is not a relative, except in exchange, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed."

512. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

512."Yo pana" means: whoever... "Bhikkhu" means: the bhikkhu intended in this case...

Aññātikā nāma mātito vā pitito vā yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā asambaddhā.

"Aññātikā" (not a relative) means one who is not connected through the mother's or father's side up to the seventh generation of ancestors.

Bhikkhunī nāma ubhatosaṅghe upasampannā.

"Bhikkhunī" means one who has received the full ordination in both Saṅghas.

Cīvaraṃ nāma channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ vikappanupagaṃ pacchimaṃ.

"Cīvara" (robe) means any one of the six kinds of robes that is the last (of the allowable robes) to be designated.

Aññatra pārivattakāti ṭhapetvā pārivattakaṃ.

"Except in exchange" means: excluding an exchange.

Paṭiggaṇhāti, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ aññātikāya bhikkhuniyā hatthato paṭiggahitaṃ, aññatra pārivattakā, nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

If he accepts: in the act of receiving, there is an offense of wrong-doing. Upon acquisition (i.e., acceptance), it is subject to forfeiture. And in this regard, whether one gives it into his hand, or places it at his feet, or throws it over him, if he assents, it is considered accepted. But if he accepts what has been sent into the hand of a female trainee, a novice bhikkhu, a novice nun, a male lay follower, a female lay follower, etc., there is no offense. When one is teaching the Dhamma, if the four assemblies bring robes and various requisites for detachment and place them at his feet, or stand within the precinct or throw them from outside the precinct, if he accepts what belongs to bhikkhunīs there, other than an exchange, it is an offense. However, if they are thrown at night, and it is not possible to know 'this belongs to a bhikkhunī, this belongs to others,' and there is no need for an exchange, it is stated in the Mahāpaccariya and Kurundī that this does not accord with the absence of intention. If a bhikkhunī gives a rain-robe, it must be an exchange. But if she places it on a rubbish heap or similar place, it is permissible to take it, having designated it as a rag-robe, thinking, 'They will take it as a rag-robe.' It is to be forfeited to the Saṅgha, to a group, or to an individual. And thus, bhikkhus, it should be forfeited... 'Venerable sirs, this robe of mine, which I received from the hand of a bhikkhunī who is not a relative, except in exchange, is subject to forfeiture. I forfeit this to the Saṅgha.'... It should be given... It should be given to the venerable.

513. Aññātikāya aññātikasaññī cīvaraṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, aññatra pārivattakā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātikāya vematiko cīvaraṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, aññatra pārivattakā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Aññātikāya ñātikasaññī cīvaraṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, aññatra pārivattakā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

513.If he perceives an unrelated bhikkhunī as unrelated and accepts a robe from her, except in exchange, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed. If he is uncertain whether she is unrelated and accepts a robe from her, except in exchange, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed. If he perceives an unrelated bhikkhunī as related and accepts a robe from her, except in exchange, it is subject to forfeiture and should be confessed.

Ekatoupasampannāya hatthato cīvaraṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, aññatra pārivattakā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātikāya aññātikasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātikāya vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ñātikāya ñātikasaññī, anāpatti.

If he accepts a robe from the hand of a bhikkhunī ordained on one side, except in exchange, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If he perceives a relative (bhikkhunī) as a non-relative, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If he is in doubt about whether she is a relative, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If he perceives a relative (bhikkhunī) as a relative, there is no offense.

514. Anāpatti ñātikāya, pārivattakaṃ parittena vā vipulaṃ, vipulena vā parittaṃ, bhikkhu vissāsaṃ gaṇhāti, tāvakālikaṃ gaṇhāti, cīvaraṃ ṭhapetvā aññaṃ parikkhāraṃ gaṇhāti, sikkhamānāya, sāmaṇeriyā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

514.There is no offense if it is from a relative; if it is an exchange, small for large or large for small; if the bhikkhu receives on trust; if he receives temporarily; if he receives any requisite other than a robe; if it is from a female probationer, a novice nun; if he is insane; or if he is an initial offender.

Cīvarapaṭiggahaṇasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The fifth training rule, on receiving robes, is finished.
