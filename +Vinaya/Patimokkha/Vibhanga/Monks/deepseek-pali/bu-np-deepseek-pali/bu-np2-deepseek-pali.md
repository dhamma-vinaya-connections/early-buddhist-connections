# BU-NP 2

## 2. Udositasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2.The Training Rule on the Concluded Robe Season

471. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamanti. Tāni cīvarāni ciraṃ nikkhittāni kaṇṇakitāni honti. Tāni bhikkhū otāpenti. Addasā kho āyasmā ānando senāsanacārikaṃ āhiṇḍanto te bhikkhū tāni cīvarāni otāpente. Disvāna yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘kassimāni, āvuso, cīvarāni kaṇṇakitānī’’ti? Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Āyasmā ānando ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissantī’’ti! Atha kho āyasmā ānando te bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamantī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā bhikkhūnaṃ hatthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā santaruttarena janapadacārikaṃ pakkamissanti! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

471.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. At that time, bhikkhus, having placed robes in the hands of other bhikkhus, were setting out on wanderings in the countryside with their inner and outer robes. Those robes, left for a long time, became stained. The bhikkhus warmed them. Venerable Ānanda, while wandering on his rounds of the lodgings, saw those bhikkhus warming the robes. Seeing them, he approached those bhikkhus and said, “Friends, whose robes are these that are stained?” Then those bhikkhus informed Venerable Ānanda of the matter. Venerable Ānanda complained, criticized, and reproached them: “How can bhikkhus, having placed robes in the hands of other bhikkhus, set out on wanderings in the countryside with their inner and outer robes?” Then, after rebuking those bhikkhus in many ways, he informed the Blessed One of the matter. … “Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus, having placed robes in the hands of other bhikkhus, set out on wanderings in the countryside with their inner and outer robes?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them: “How can these foolish men, having placed robes in the hands of other bhikkhus, set out on wanderings in the countryside with their inner and outer robes? This will not inspire confidence in those who are without it … And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

472. ‘‘Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kathine ekarattampi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyya, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

472.“When a bhikkhu’s robe-making is finished and the Kathina has been withdrawn, if that bhikkhu stays apart from any of his three robes even for a single night, it is subject to forfeiture and entails confession.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.

473. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu kosambiyaṃ gilāno hoti. Ñātakā tassa bhikkhuno santike dūtaṃ pāhesuṃ – ‘‘āgacchatu bhadanto, mayaṃ, upaṭṭhahissāmā’’ti. Bhikkhūpi evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘gacchāvuso, ñātakā taṃ upaṭṭhahissantī’’ti. So evamāha – ‘‘bhagavatāvuso, sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ – ‘na ticīvarena vippavasitabba’nti. Ahañcamhi gilāno. Na sakkomi ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. Nāhaṃ gamissāmī’’ti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ dātuṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, dātabbā. Tena gilānena bhikkhunā saṅghaṃ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṃ nisīditvā añjaliṃ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘ahaṃ, bhante, gilāno. Na sakkomi ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. Sohaṃ, bhante, saṅghaṃ ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ yācāmī’ti. Dutiyampi yācitabbā. Tatiyampi yācitabbā. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

473.At that time, a certain bhikkhu in Kosambi was ill. His relatives sent a messenger to that bhikkhu, saying: “Come, venerable one, we will look after you.” The bhikkhus also said: “Go, friend, your relatives will look after you.” He replied: “Friends, the Blessed One has laid down a training rule: ‘One should not be separated from the three robes.’ I am sick. I am unable to take the three robes and leave. I will not go.” They reported this matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in this connection, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus, saying: “I allow, bhikkhus, granting permission to a sick bhikkhu not to be separated from the three robes. And this is how, bhikkhus, it should be granted. That sick bhikkhu should approach the Sangha, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, squat down, raise his joined palms, and say: ‘Venerable sirs, I am sick. I am unable to take the three robes and leave. Therefore, venerable sirs, I ask the Sangha for permission not to be separated from the three robes.’ He should ask a second time. He should ask a third time. A competent bhikkhu should then inform the Sangha:

474. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu gilāno. Na sakkoti ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. So saṅghaṃ ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ dadeyya. Esā ñatti.

474.“Venerable sir, may the Sangha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, is ill. He is unable to leave, taking his three robes. He asks the Sangha for agreement to remain with his three robes. If it seems right to the Sangha, the Sangha should grant the bhikkhu, named so-and-so, agreement to remain with his three robes. This is the motion.”

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu gilāno. Na sakkoti ticīvaraṃ ādāya pakkamituṃ. So saṅghaṃ ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ yācati. Saṅgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiṃ deti. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammutiyā dānaṃ, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sir, may the Sangha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, is ill. He is unable to depart taking his three robes. He asks the Sangha for the agreement of non-separation from his three robes. The Sangha grants the bhikkhu, named so-and-so, the agreement of non-separation from his three robes. Whoever agrees that the Sangha should grant the agreement of non-separation from his three robes to the bhikkhu, named so-and-so, let him remain silent; whoever disagrees, let him speak.”

‘‘Dinnā saṅghena itthannāmassa bhikkhuno ticīvarena avippavāsasammuti. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The Saṅgha has granted the agreement of non-separation from the three robes to the bhikkhu named so-and-so. It is approved by the Saṅgha, therefore it is silent. Thus I hold it.”

Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be promulgated:

475. ‘‘Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kathine ekarattampi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyya, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiya’’nti.

475.“When a bhikkhu’s robe-making is finished and the Kathina has been withdrawn, if that bhikkhu stays apart from any of his three robes even for a single night, except by agreement of the bhikkhus, it is subject to forfeiture and entails confession.”

476. ‘‘Niṭṭhitacīvarasminti bhikkhuno cīvaraṃ kataṃ vā hoti naṭṭhaṃ vā vinaṭṭhaṃ vā daḍḍhaṃ vā cīvarāsā vā upacchinnā.

476.“When a bhikkhu’s robe-making is finished” means a bhikkhu’s robe has been made, or it is lost, or it is destroyed, or it is burned, or the expectation of getting a robe is cut off.

Ubbhatasmiṃ kathineti aṭṭhannaṃ mātikānaṃ aññatarāya mātikāya ubbhataṃ hoti, saṅghena vā antarā ubbhataṃ hoti.

“When the Kathina has been withdrawn” means it has been withdrawn by one of the eight grounds, or it has been withdrawn by the Sangha in the intervening period.

Ekarattampi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyyāti saṅghāṭiyā vā uttarāsaṅgena vā antaravāsakena vā.

“If that bhikkhu stays apart from any of his three robes even for a single night” means from the outer robe, or the upper robe, or the inner robe.

Aññatra bhikkhusammutiyāti ṭhapetvā bhikkhusammutiṃ.

“Except by agreement of the bhikkhus” means excluding the agreement of the bhikkhus.

Nissaggiyaṃ hotīti saha aruṇuggamanā [aruṇuggamanena (sī. syā.)] nissaggiyaṃ hoti. Nissajjitabbaṃ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṃ…pe… idaṃ me, bhante, cīvaraṃ rattivippavutthaṃ [rattiṃ vippavutthaṃ (sī.)] aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā nissaggiyaṃ, imāhaṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmīti…pe… dadeyyāti…pe… dadeyyunti…pe… āyasmato dammīti.

It is to be forfeited—it is to be forfeited with the rising of dawn. It is to be forfeited to the Sangha, to a group, or to an individual. And this is how, bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited… ‘Venerable Sirs, this robe of mine, which has been kept overnight separated from me without the bhikkhus’ agreement, is to be forfeited. I forfeit it to the Sangha.’… it is to be given… it is to be given… ‘I give it to the venerable one.’

477. Gāmo ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Nivesanaṃ ekūpacāraṃ nānūpacāraṃ. Udosito ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Aṭṭo ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Māḷo ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Pāsādo ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Hammiyaṃ ekūpacāraṃ nānūpacāraṃ. Nāvā ekūpacārā nānūpacārā. Sattho ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Khettaṃ ekūpacāraṃ nānūpacāraṃ. Dhaññakaraṇaṃ ekūpacāraṃ nānūpacāraṃ. Ārāmo ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Vihāro ekūpacāro nānūpacāro. Rukkhamūlaṃ ekūpacāraṃ nānūpacāraṃ. Ajjhokāso ekūpacāro nānūpacāro.

477.A village is single-approach or multiple-approach. A dwelling is single-approach or multiple-approach. A storehouse is single-approach or multiple-approach. A watchtower is single-approach or multiple-approach. A palace is single-approach or multiple-approach. A mansion is single-approach or multiple-approach. A flat-roofed mansion is single-approach or multiple-approach. A boat is single-approach or multiple-approach. A caravan is single-approach or multiple-approach. A field is single-approach or multiple-approach. A threshing floor is single-approach or multiple-approach. A park is single-approach or multiple-approach. A monastery is single-approach or multiple-approach. The foot of a tree is single-approach or multiple-approach. Open space is single-approach or multiple-approach.

478. Gāmo ekūpacāro nāma ekakulassa gāmo hoti parikkhitto ca. Antogāme cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antogāme vatthabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ ghare cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ ghare vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

478.A village that is single-approach is a village belonging to a single owner and is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the village, one should dwell inside the village. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the house where the robe is placed, or one should not leave arm’s reach.

479. Nānākulassa gāmo hoti parikkhitto ca. Yasmiṃ ghare cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ ghare vatthabbaṃ sabhāye vā dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Sabhāyaṃ gacchantena hatthapāse cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā sabhāye vā vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Sabhāye cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā sabhāye vā vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ ghare cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ ghare vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

479.A village belonging to various owners is enclosed. One should dwell in the house where the robe is placed, or in the assembly hall or at the city gate, and should not leave arm’s reach. When going to the assembly hall, having placed the robe within arm’s reach, one should dwell in the assembly hall or at the city gate, and should not leave arm’s reach. Having placed the robe in the assembly hall, one should dwell in the assembly hall or at the city gate, and should not leave arm’s reach. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the house where the robe is placed, and should not leave arm’s reach.

480. Ekakulassa nivesanaṃ hoti parikkhittañca, nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Antonivesane cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antonivesane vatthabbaṃ. Aparikkhittaṃ hoti, yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

480.A dwelling belonging to a single owner is enclosed, with various rooms and chambers. Having placed the robe inside the dwelling, one should dwell inside the dwelling. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the room where the robe has been placed, or should not leave arm’s reach.

481. Nānākulassa nivesanaṃ hoti parikkhittañca, nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Aparikkhittaṃ hoti, yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

481.A dwelling belonging to various owners is enclosed, with various rooms and chambers. One should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, or at the doorway, and should not leave arm’s reach. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, and should not leave arm’s reach.

482. Ekakulassa udosito hoti parikkhitto ca, nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Antoudosite cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antoudosite vatthabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

482.A storehouse belonging to a single owner is enclosed, with various rooms and chambers. Having placed the robe inside the storehouse, one should dwell inside the storehouse. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, or should not leave arm’s reach.

483. Nānākulassa udosito hoti parikkhitto ca, nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

483.A storehouse belonging to various owners is enclosed, with various rooms and chambers. One should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, or at the doorway, and should not leave arm’s reach. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, and should not leave arm’s reach.

484. Ekakulassa aṭṭo hoti, antoaṭṭe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antoaṭṭe vatthabbaṃ. Nānākulassa aṭṭo hoti, nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

484.A watchtower belonging to a single owner. Having placed the robe inside the watchtower, one should dwell inside the watchtower. A watchtower belonging to various owners has various rooms and chambers. One should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, or at the doorway, and should not leave arm’s reach.

485. Ekakulassa māḷo hoti, antomāḷe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antomāḷe vatthabbaṃ. Nānākulassa māḷo hoti nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā, yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

485.A palace belonging to a single owner. Having placed the robe inside the palace, one should dwell inside the palace. A palace belonging to various owners has various rooms and chambers. One should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, or at the doorway, and should not leave arm’s reach.

486. Ekakulassa pāsādo hoti, antopāsāde cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antopāsāde vatthabbaṃ. Nānākulassa pāsādo hoti, nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

486.A mansion belonging to a single owner. Having placed the robe inside the mansion, one should dwell inside the mansion. A mansion belonging to various owners has various rooms and chambers. One should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, or at the doorway, and should not leave arm’s reach.

487. Ekakulassa hammiyaṃ hoti. Antohammiye cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antohammiye vatthabbaṃ. Nānākulassa hammiyaṃ hoti, nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Yasmiṃ gabbhe cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ gabbhe vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

487.A flat-roofed mansion belonging to a single owner. Having placed the robe inside the flat-roofed mansion, one should dwell inside the flat-roofed mansion. A flat-roofed mansion belonging to various owners has various rooms and chambers. One should dwell in the room where the robe is placed, or at the doorway, and should not leave arm’s reach.

488. Ekakulassa nāvā hoti. Antonāvāya cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antonāvāya vatthabbaṃ. Nānākulassa nāvā hoti nānāgabbhā nānāovarakā. Yasmiṃ ovarake cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ ovarake vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

488.A boat belonging to a single owner. Having placed the robe inside the boat, one should dwell inside the boat. A boat belonging to various owners has various rooms and chambers. One should dwell in the chamber where the robe is placed, and should not leave arm’s reach.

489. Ekakulassa sattho hoti. Satthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā purato vā pacchato vā sattabbhantarā na vijahitabbā, passato abbhantaraṃ na vijahitabbaṃ. Nānākulassa sattho hoti, satthe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ.

489.A caravan belonging to a single owner. Having placed the robe in the caravan, one should not leave seven abbhantaras ahead or behind, nor should one leave the range of sight. A caravan belonging to various owners. Having placed the robe in the caravan, one should not leave arm’s reach.

490. Ekakulassa khettaṃ hoti parikkhittañca. Antokhette cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antokhette vatthabbaṃ. Aparikkhittaṃ hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ. Nānākulassa khettaṃ hoti parikkhittañca. Antokhette cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā dvāramūle vā vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Aparikkhittaṃ hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ.

490.A field belonging to a single owner is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the field, one should dwell inside the field. If it is unenclosed, one should not leave arm’s reach. A field belonging to various owners is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the field, one should dwell at the field gate, or should not leave arm’s reach. If it is unenclosed, one should not leave arm’s reach.

491. Ekakulassa dhaññakaraṇaṃ hoti parikkhittañca. Antodhaññakaraṇe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antodhaññakaraṇe vatthabbaṃ. Aparikkhittaṃ hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ. Nānākulassa dhaññakaraṇaṃ hoti parikkhittañca. Antodhaññakaraṇe cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā dvāramūle vā vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Aparikkhittaṃ hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ.

491.A threshing floor belonging to a single owner is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the threshing floor, one should dwell inside the threshing floor. If it is unenclosed, one should not leave arm’s reach. A threshing floor belonging to various owners is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the threshing floor, one should dwell at the doorway, or should not leave arm’s reach. If it is unenclosed, one should not leave arm’s reach.

492. Ekakulassa ārāmo hoti parikkhitto ca. Antoārāme cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antoārāme vatthabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ. Nānākulassa ārāmo hoti parikkhitto ca. Antoārāme cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā dvāramūle vā vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ.

492.A park belonging to a single owner is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the park, one should dwell inside the park. If it is unenclosed, one should not leave arm’s reach. A park belonging to various owners is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the park, one should dwell at the doorway, or should not leave arm’s reach. If it is unenclosed, one should not leave arm’s reach.

493. Ekakulassa vihāro hoti parikkhitto ca. Antovihāre cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antovihāre vatthabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ vihāre cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ vihāre vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Nānākulassa vihāro hoti parikkhitto ca. Yasmiṃ vihāre cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ vihāre vatthabbaṃ dvāramūle vā, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ. Aparikkhitto hoti, yasmiṃ vihāre cīvaraṃ nikkhittaṃ hoti tasmiṃ vihāre vatthabbaṃ, hatthapāsā vā na vijahitabbaṃ.

493.A monastery belonging to a single owner is enclosed. Having placed the robe inside the monastery, one should dwell inside the monastery. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the monastery where the robe is placed, or should not leave arm’s reach. A monastery belonging to various owners is enclosed. One should dwell in the monastery where the robe is placed, or at the doorway, or should not leave arm’s reach. If it is unenclosed, one should dwell in the monastery where the robe is placed, or should not leave arm’s reach.

494. Ekakulassa rukkhamūlaṃ hoti, yaṃ majjhanhike kāle samantā chāyā pharati, antochāyāya cīvaraṃ nikkhipitvā antochāyāya vatthabbaṃ. Nānākulassa rukkhamūlaṃ hoti, hatthapāsā na vijahitabbaṃ.

494.At the root of a tree with a unified shade, where the shade spreads all around at midday, a bhikkhu should place his robe within the shade and dwell within the shade. At the root of a tree with scattered shade, one’s arm’s reach should not be abandoned.

Ajjhokāso ekūpacāro nāma agāmake araññe samantā sattabbhantarā ekūpacāro, tato paraṃ nānūpacāro.

An open space is considered a single vicinity when, in a wilderness without a village, it extends seven abbhantaras (about 28 meters) all around; beyond that, it is considered multiple vicinities.

495. Vippavutthe vippavutthasaññī aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Vippavutthe vematiko, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Vippavutthe avippavutthasaññī, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Appaccuddhaṭe paccuddhaṭasaññī…pe… avissajjite vissajjitasaññī… anaṭṭhe naṭṭhasaññī… avinaṭṭhe vinaṭṭhasaññī… adaḍḍhe daḍḍhasaññī…pe… avilutte viluttasaññī, aññatra bhikkhusammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.

495.If one is separated from the robe, perceives it as separated, without agreement among the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and entails a pācittiya. If one is separated from the robe and is in doubt, without agreement among the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and entails a pācittiya. If one is separated from the robe, perceives it as not separated, without agreement among the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and entails a pācittiya. If something has not been withdrawn and one perceives it as withdrawn…pe… if something has not been given away and one perceives it as given away… if something is not lost and one perceives it as lost… if something is not destroyed and one perceives it as destroyed… if something is not burned and one perceives it as burned…pe… if something is not stolen and one perceives it as stolen, without agreement among the bhikkhus, it is to be forfeited and entails a pācittiya.

Nissaggiyaṃ cīvaraṃ anissajjitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Avippavutthe vippavutthasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Avippavutthe vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Avippavutthe avippavutthasaññī, anāpatti.

Using a nissaggiya robe without relinquishing it, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If one is not separated from the robe, but thinks one is, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If one is not separated from the robe and is in doubt, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If one is not separated from the robe and thinks one is not, there is no offense.

496. Anāpatti antoaruṇe paccuddharati, vissajjeti, nassati, vinassati, ḍayhati, acchinditvā gaṇhanti, vissāsaṃ gaṇhanti, bhikkhusammutiyā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

496.There is no offense if one recovers it before dawn, relinquishes it, it is lost, it perishes, it is burned, they take it without severing [ownership], they take it on trust, by agreement of the bhikkhus, of one who is insane, of the first offender.

Udositasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The second training rule concerning dwelling apart from the robe is concluded.
