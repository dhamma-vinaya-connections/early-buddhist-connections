# BU-AN 2

## 2. Dutiyaaniyatasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2.The Second Undetermined Rule.

452. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho paṭicchanne āsane alaṃkammaniye nisajjaṃ kappetu’’nti tassāyeva kumārikāya saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappesi kālayuttaṃ samullapanto kālayuttaṃ dhammaṃ bhaṇanto. Dutiyampi kho visākhā migāramātā nimantitā taṃ kulaṃ agamāsi. Addasā kho visākhā migāramātā āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ tassāyeva kumārikāya saddhiṃ ekaṃ ekāya raho nisinnaṃ. Disvāna āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, bhante, nacchannaṃ nappatirūpaṃ yaṃ ayyo mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappeti. Kiñcāpi, bhante, ayyo anatthiko tena dhammena, apica dussaddhāpayā appasannā manussā’’ti. Evampi kho āyasmā udāyī visākhāya migāramātuyā vuccamāno nādiyi. Atha kho visākhā migāramātā nikkhamitvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappessatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ udāyiṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ …pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappesī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappessasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

452.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time Venerable Udāyī, thinking, “The Blessed One has prohibited sitting alone with a woman in a private, concealed, and sexually suitable seat,” sat alone in private with that very girl, engaging in timely conversation and timely speaking of the Dhamma. A second time, Visākhā Migāramātā, having been invited, went to that household. Visākhā Migāramātā saw Venerable Udāyī sitting alone in private with that very girl. Having seen this, she said to Venerable Udāyī: “This, venerable sir, is not proper or suitable—that the venerable sir should sit alone in private with a woman. Even though, venerable sir, you have no need for that sort of thing, still people of little faith and weak confidence might be led astray.” Though Venerable Udāyī was spoken to thus by Visākhā Migāramātā, he paid no attention. Then Visākhā Migāramātā left and reported this matter to the bhikkhus. Those bhikkhus who were modest… criticized and complained: “How could Venerable Udāyī sit alone in private with a woman?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Udāyī in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, Udāyī, that you sat alone in private with a woman?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him: “… Foolish man, how could you sit alone in private with a woman? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus—”

453. ‘‘Na heva kho pana paṭicchannaṃ āsanaṃ hoti nālaṃ kammaniyaṃ, alañca kho hoti mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsituṃ. Yo pana bhikkhu tathārūpe āsane mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappeyya, tamenaṃ saddheyyavacasā upāsikā disvā dvinnaṃ dhammānaṃ aññatarena vadeyya – saṅghādisesena vā pācittiyena vā. Nisajjaṃ bhikkhu paṭijānamāno dvinnaṃ dhammānaṃ aññatarena kāretabbo – saṅghādisesena vā pācittiyena vā. Yena vā sā saddheyyavacasā upāsikā vadeyya tena so bhikkhu kāretabbo. Ayampi dhammo aniyato’’ti.

453.“However, the seat is neither secluded nor sexually suitable, but it is suitable for addressing a woman with coarse language. If a bhikkhu should engage in private sitting alone with a woman on such a seat, and a female lay follower who is trustworthy should see him and accuse him of one of two kinds of offenses—either a Saṅghādisesa or a Pācittiya—then that bhikkhu, if he admits to the sitting, must be dealt with according to one of those two kinds of offenses—either a Saṅghādisesa or a Pācittiya. Or that bhikkhu must be dealt with according to what that trustworthy female lay follower declares. This too is an undetermined rule.”

454. Na heva kho pana paṭicchannaṃ āsanaṃ hotīti appaṭicchannaṃ hoti kuṭṭena vā kavāṭena vā kilañjena vā sāṇipākārena vā rukkhena vā thambhena vā kotthaḷiyā vā yena kenaci appaṭicchannaṃ hoti.

454.“However, the seat is not secluded” means it is unscreened by a wall, a door, a screen, a cloth partition, a tree, a pillar, a storehouse, or anything else; it is unscreened.

Nālaṃ kammaniyanti na sakkā hoti methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevituṃ.

“Not sexually suitable” means it is not possible to engage in sexual intercourse.

Alañca kho hoti mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsitunti sakkā hoti mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsituṃ.

“But it is suitable for addressing a woman with coarse language” means it is possible to address a woman with coarse language.

Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

“Now, whoever” means whoever of whatever sort… “A bhikkhu” means… in this context, a bhikkhu is intended.

Tathārūpe āsaneti evarūpe āsane.

“On such a seat” means on such a seat.

Mātugāmo nāma manussitthī, na yakkhī na petī na tiracchānagatā, viññū paṭibalā subhāsitadubbhāsitaṃ duṭṭhullāduṭṭhullaṃ ājānituṃ.

“A woman” means a human female, not a female yakkha, a female peta, or an animal. She is intelligent and capable of understanding well-spoken and ill-spoken words, and coarse and non-coarse conduct.

Saddhinti ekato.

“With” means together.

Eko ekāyāti bhikkhu ceva hoti mātugāmo ca.

“Alone with a woman” means there is a bhikkhu and there is a woman.

Raho nāma cakkhussa raho, sotassa raho. Cakkhussa raho nāma na sakkā hoti akkhiṃ vā nikhaṇīyamāne bhamukaṃ vā ukkhipīyamāne sīsaṃ vā ukkhipīyamāne passituṃ. Sotassa raho nāma na sakkā hoti pakatikathā sotuṃ.

“Secluded” means secluded from the eye, secluded from the ear. Secluded from the eye means it is not possible to see when an eye is being dug out, or an eyebrow is being raised, or a head is being lifted. Secluded from the ear means it is not possible to hear ordinary talk.

Nisajjaṃ kappeyyāti mātugāme nisinne bhikkhu upanisinno vā hoti upanipanno vā. Bhikkhu nisinne mātugāmo upanisinno vā hoti upanipanno vā. Ubho vā nisinnā honti ubho vā nipannā.

“Should engage in sitting” means that when the woman is seated, the bhikkhu is seated close by or lying close by; or when the bhikkhu is seated, the woman is seated close by or lying close by; or both are seated, or both are lying down.

Saddheyyavacasā nāma āgataphalā abhisametāvinī viññātasāsanā.

“Trustworthy in speech” means one who has attained a fruit, is accomplished in higher insight, and has understood the Teaching.

Upāsikā nāma buddhaṃ saraṇaṃ gatā, dhammaṃ saraṇaṃ gatā, saṅghaṃ saraṇaṃ gatā.

“A female lay follower” means one who has gone for refuge to the Buddha, gone for refuge to the Dhamma, and gone for refuge to the Saṅgha.

Disvāti passitvā.

“Having seen” means having seen.

Dvinnaṃ dhammānaṃ aññatarena vadeyya saṅghādisesena vā pācittiyena vā. Nisajjaṃ bhikkhu paṭijānamāno dvinnaṃ dhammānaṃ aññatarena kāretabbo – saṅghādisesena vā pācittiyena vā. Yena vā sā saddheyyavacasā upāsikā vadeyya, tena so bhikkhu kāretabbo.

She should accuse him of one of two kinds of offenses—either a Saṅghādisesa or a Pācittiya. That bhikkhu, if he admits to the sitting, must be dealt with according to one of those two kinds of offenses—either a Saṅghādisesa or a Pācittiya. Or that bhikkhu must be dealt with according to what that trustworthy female lay follower declares.

455. Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyo mayā diṭṭho nisinno mātugāmena saddhiṃ kāyasaṃsaggaṃ samāpajjanto’’ti, so ca taṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo. Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyo mayā diṭṭho nisinno mātugāmena saddhiṃ kāyasaṃsaggaṃ samāpajjanto’’ti, so ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘saccāhaṃ nisinno, no ca kho kāyasaṃsaggaṃ samāpajji’’nti, nisajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nisinno, apica kho nipannoti, nipajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nisinno, apica kho ṭhitoti, na kāretabbo.

455.If she should say: “Venerable, I saw you sitting with a woman and engaging in physical contact,” and he admits it, he must be dealt with for the offense. If she should say: “Venerable, I saw you sitting with a woman and engaging in physical contact,” and he should say: “It’s true I was sitting, but I did not engage in physical contact,” he must be dealt with for the sitting. If he says: “I was not sitting, but lying down,” he must be dealt with for the lying down. If he says: “I was not sitting, but standing,” he must not be dealt with.

Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyo mayā diṭṭho nipanno mātugāmena saddhiṃ kāyasaṃsaggaṃ samāpajjanto’’ti, so ca taṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo…pe… saccāhaṃ nipanno, no ca kho kāyasaṃsaggaṃ samāpajjinti, nipajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nipanno, apica kho nisinnoti, nisajjāya kāretabbo …pe… nāhaṃ nipanno, apica kho ṭhitoti, na kāretabbo.

If she should say: “Venerable, I saw you lying down with a woman and engaging in physical contact,” and he admits it, he must be dealt with for the offense… If he says: “It’s true I was lying down, but I did not engage in physical contact,” he must be dealt with for the lying down… If he says: “I was not lying down, but sitting,” he must be dealt with for the sitting… If he says: “I was not lying down, but standing,” he must not be dealt with.

Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyassa mayā sutaṃ nisinnassa mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsantassā’’ti, so ca taṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo. Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyassa mayā sutaṃ nisinnassa mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsantassā’’ti, so ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘saccāhaṃ nisinno, no ca kho duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsi’’nti, nisajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nisinno, apica kho nipannoti, nipajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nisinno, apica kho ṭhitoti, na kāretabbo.

If she should say: “Venerable, I heard you, while sitting, addressing a woman with coarse words,” and he admits it, he must be dealt with for the offense. If she should say: “Venerable, I heard you, while sitting, addressing a woman with coarse words,” and he should say: “It’s true I was sitting, but I did not address her with coarse words,” he must be dealt with for the sitting. If he says: “I was not sitting, but lying down,” he must be dealt with for the lying down. If he says: “I was not sitting, but standing,” he must not be dealt with.

Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyassa mayā sutaṃ nipannassa mātugāmaṃ duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsantassā’’ti, so ca taṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo…pe… saccāhaṃ nipanno no ca kho duṭṭhullāhi vācāhi obhāsinti, nipajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nipanno, apica kho nisinnoti, nisajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nipanno, apica kho ṭhitoti, na kāretabbo.

If she should say: “Venerable, I heard you, while lying down, addressing a woman with coarse words,” and he admits it, he must be dealt with for the offense… If he says: “It’s true I was lying down, but I did not address her with coarse words,” he must be dealt with for the lying down… If he says: “I was not lying down, but sitting,” he must be dealt with for the sitting… If he says: “I was not lying down, but standing,” he must not be dealt with.

456. Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyo mayā diṭṭho mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisinno’’ti, so ca taṃ paṭijānāti, nisajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nisinno, apica kho nipannoti, nipajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nisinno, apica kho ṭhitoti, na kāretabbo.

456.If she should say: “Venerable, I saw you sitting alone with a woman in a private place,” and he admits it, he must be dealt with for the sitting… If he says: “I was not sitting, but lying down,” he must be dealt with for the lying down… If he says: “I was not sitting, but standing,” he must not be dealt with.

Sā ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ayyo mayā diṭṭho mātugāmena saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nipanno’’ti, so ca taṃ paṭijānāti, nipajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nipanno, apica kho nisinnoti, nisajjāya kāretabbo…pe… nāhaṃ nipanno, apica kho ṭhitoti, na kāretabbo.

If she should say: “Venerable, I saw you lying down alone with a woman in a private place,” and he admits it, he must be dealt with for the lying down… If he says: “I was not lying down, but sitting,” he must be dealt with for the sitting… If he says: “I was not lying down, but standing,” he must not be dealt with.

Ayampīti purimaṃ upādāya vuccati.

“This too” is said with reference to the preceding.

Aniyatoti na niyato, saṅghādiseso vā pācittiyaṃ vā.

“Undetermined” means it is not determined, either a Saṅghādisesa or a Pācittiya.

457. Gamanaṃ paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ paṭijānāti āpattiṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo. Gamanaṃ paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ na paṭijānāti āpattiṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo. Gamanaṃ paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ paṭijānāti āpattiṃ na paṭijānāti, nisajjāya kāretabbo. Gamanaṃ paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ na paṭijānāti āpattiṃ na paṭijānāti, na kāretabbo.

457.If he admits to the going, admits to the sitting, and admits to the offense, he must be dealt with for the offense. If he admits to the going, does not admit to the sitting, but admits to the offense, he must be dealt with for the offense. If he admits to the going, admits to the sitting, but does not admit to the offense, he must be dealt with for the sitting. If he admits to the going, does not admit to the sitting, and does not admit to the offense, he must not be dealt with.

Gamanaṃ na paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ paṭijānāti āpattiṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo. Gamanaṃ na paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ na paṭijānāti āpattiṃ paṭijānāti, āpattiyā kāretabbo. Gamanaṃ na paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ paṭijānāti āpattiṃ na paṭijānāti, nisajjāya kāretabbo. Gamanaṃ na paṭijānāti nisajjaṃ na paṭijānāti āpattiṃ na paṭijānāti, na kāretabboti.

He does not admit to going, he admits to sitting, he admits to the offense—he should be dealt with according to the offense. He does not admit to going, he does not admit to sitting, he admits to the offense—he should be dealt with according to the offense. He does not admit to going, he admits to sitting, he does not admit to the offense—he should be dealt with according to the sitting. He does not admit to going, he does not admit to sitting, he does not admit to the offense—he should not be dealt with.

Dutiyo aniyato niṭṭhito.

The second indeterminate rule is concluded.

458. Uddiṭṭhā kho āyasmanto dve aniyatā dhammā. Tatthāyasmante pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Dutiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Tatiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Parisuddhetthāyasmanto; tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmīti.

458.Venerables, these two indeterminate rules have been recited. Concerning them, I ask the Venerables: “Are you pure in this?” A second time I ask: “Are you pure in this?” A third time I ask: “Are you pure in this?” The Venerables are pure in this, and therefore they are silent. Thus do I hold it.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Alaṃ kammaniyañceva, tatheva ca naheva kho;Aniyatā supaññattā, buddhaseṭṭhena tādināti.

Enough with what is actionable, and likewise with what is not actionable; The indeterminate rules, well-proclaimed, by the foremost Buddha, the Such One.

Aniyatakaṇḍaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Section on the Indeterminate Rules is Concluded.
