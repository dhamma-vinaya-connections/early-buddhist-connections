# BU-SK 14

## 588. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū uccāsaddaṃ mahāsaddaṃ karontā antaraghare gacchanti…pe….

## 588.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were going into inhabited areas making loud and great noises... and so on.

‘‘Appasaddo antaraghare gamissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will go into inhabited areas with little noise”—this is a training to be observed.

Appasaddena antaraghare gantabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca uccāsaddaṃ mahāsaddaṃ karonto antaraghare gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not go into inhabited areas making loud and great noises. Whoever, out of disrespect, goes into inhabited areas making loud and great noises, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, in times of danger, if insane, or if one is a beginner.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The third training rule is concluded.
