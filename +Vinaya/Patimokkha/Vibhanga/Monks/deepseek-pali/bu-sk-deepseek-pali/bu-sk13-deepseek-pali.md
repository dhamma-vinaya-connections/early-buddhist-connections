# BU-SK 13

## 587. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mahāhasitaṃ hasantā antaraghare nisīdanti…pe….

## 587.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were sitting in inhabited areas laughing loudly... and so on.

‘‘Na ujjagghikāya antaraghare nisīdissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not sit in inhabited areas with loud laughter”—this is a training to be observed.

Na ujjagghikāya antaraghare nisīditabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca mahāhasitaṃ hasanto antaraghare nisīdati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not sit in inhabited areas with loud laughter. Whoever, out of disrespect, sits in inhabited areas laughing loudly, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, hasanīyasmiṃ vatthusmiṃ mihitamattaṃ karoti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, if one merely smiles in a laughable situation, in times of danger, if insane, or if one is a beginner.

Dutiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The second training rule is concluded.
