# BU-SK 17

## 591. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kāyappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdanti, kāyaṃ olambentā…pe….

## 591.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were sitting in inhabited areas swaying their bodies, letting their bodies droop... and so on.

‘‘Na kāyappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not sit in inhabited areas swaying the body”—this is a training to be observed.

Na kāyappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīditabbaṃ. Kāyaṃ paggahetvā nisīditabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca kāyappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdati kāyaṃ olambento, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not sit in inhabited areas swaying the body. One should sit with the body held steady. Whoever, out of disrespect, sits in inhabited areas swaying the body, letting the body droop, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, vāsūpagatassa, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, if one has taken shelter, in times of danger, if insane, or if one is a beginner.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The sixth training rule is concluded.
