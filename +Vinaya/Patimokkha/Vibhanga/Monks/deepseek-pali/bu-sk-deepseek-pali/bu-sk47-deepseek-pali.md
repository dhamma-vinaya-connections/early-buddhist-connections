# BU-SK 47

## 621. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjanti…pe….

## 621.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus were eating by breaking off mouthfuls…and so on.

‘‘Na kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not eat by breaking off mouthfuls”—this is a training to be observed.

Na kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjitabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca kabaḷāvacchedakaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not eat by breaking off mouthfuls. Whoever, through lack of respect, eats by breaking off mouthfuls, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, khajjake phalāphale, uttaribhaṅge, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, unmindful, or unknowing; if one is sick; in the case of chewable food and fruits; if exceeding the limit; in times of danger; if insane; or if one is a first offender.

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The fifth training rule is concluded.
