# BU-SK 12

## 586. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mahāhasitaṃ hasantā antaraghare gacchanti…pe….

## 586.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were going into inhabited areas laughing loudly... and so on.

‘‘Na ujjagghikāya antaraghare gamissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not go into inhabited areas with loud laughter”—this is a training to be observed.

Na ujjagghikāya antaraghare gantabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca mahāhasitaṃ hasanto antaraghare gacchati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not go into inhabited areas with loud laughter. Whoever, out of disrespect, goes into inhabited areas laughing loudly, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, hasanīyasmiṃ vatthusmiṃ mihitamattaṃ karoti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, if one merely smiles in a laughable situation, in times of danger, if insane, or if one is a beginner.

Paṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The first training rule is concluded.
