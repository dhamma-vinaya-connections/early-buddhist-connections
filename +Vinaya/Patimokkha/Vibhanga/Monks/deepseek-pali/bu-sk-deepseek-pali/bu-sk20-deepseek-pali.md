# BU-SK 20

## 594. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍakassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare gacchanti sīsaṃ olambentā…pe….

## 594.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were going into inhabited areas swaying their heads, letting their heads droop... and so on.

‘‘Na sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare gamissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not go into inhabited areas swaying my head”—this is a training to be observed.

Na sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare gantabbaṃ. Sīsaṃ paggahetvā gantabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare gacchati sīsaṃ olambento, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not go into inhabited areas swaying the head. One should go with the head held steady. Whoever, out of disrespect, goes into inhabited areas swaying his head, letting the head droop, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional... or if one is a beginner.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The ninth training rule is concluded.
