# BU-SK 34

## 608. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū tahaṃ tahaṃ omasitvā [omadditvā (ka.)] piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjanti…pe….

## 608.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group of six bhikkhus were eating almsfood after picking here and there….

‘‘Sapadānaṃ piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will eat almsfood systematically’—this is a training to be observed.

Sapadānaṃ piṇḍapāto bhuñjitabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca tahaṃ tahaṃ omasitvā [omadditvā (ka.)] piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Almsfood should be eaten systematically. Whoever, being disrespectful, eats almsfood by picking here and there, commits an offense of wrongdoing.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, aññesaṃ dento omasati, aññassa bhājane ākiranto omasati, uttaribhaṅge, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, if one touches while giving to others, if one touches while scattering into another’s bowl, when there is a surplus portion, in times of danger, if insane, or for the first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The third training rule is completed.
