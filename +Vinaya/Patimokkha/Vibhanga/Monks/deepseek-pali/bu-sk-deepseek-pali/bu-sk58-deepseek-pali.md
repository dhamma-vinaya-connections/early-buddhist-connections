# BU-SK 58

## 632. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā bhaggesu viharati susumāragire bhesakaḷāvane migadāye. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū kokanade pāsāde sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍenti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessanti, seyyathāpi gihī kāmabhogino’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍentīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessanti! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

## 632.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Bhaggas on Susumāragiri, in the Bhesakaḷā Grove, the Deer Park. Now at that time bhikkhus were discarding bowl-wash water containing rice grains inside the Kokanada Mansion. People complained, murmured, and criticized, “How can these Sakyan ascetics discard bowl-wash water containing rice grains inside, just like householders who indulge in sensual pleasures?” The bhikkhus heard the complaints of those people who were complaining, murmuring, and criticizing. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, murmured, and criticized them, “How can bhikkhus discard bowl-wash water containing rice grains inside?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus discard bowl-wash water containing rice grains inside?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “How can you, foolish men, discard bowl-wash water containing rice grains inside! This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:

‘‘Na sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not discard bowl-wash water containing rice grains inside a dwelling’—this is a training to be undertaken.

Na sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍetabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sasitthakaṃ pattadhovanaṃ antaraghare chaḍḍeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Bowl-wash water containing rice grains should not be discarded inside a dwelling. Whoever, being disrespectful, discards bowl-wash water containing rice grains inside a dwelling, commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, uddharitvā vā bhinditvā vā paṭiggahe vā nīharitvā vā chaḍḍeti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional, if one is unmindful, if one does not know, if one is ill, if one discards it after separating the rice grains, or after crushing them, or into a receptacle, or outside, in times of danger, for an insane person, or for the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The sixth training rule is completed.
