# BU-SK 35

## 609. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjantā sūpaññeva bahuṃ bhuñjanti…pe….

## 609.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus, while eating almsfood, were eating a large amount of soup….

‘‘Samasūpakaṃ piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will eat almsfood with an equal proportion of soup’—this is a training to be observed.

Sūpo nāma dve sūpā – muggasūpo, māsasūpo hatthahāriyo. Samasūpako piṇḍapāto bhuñjitabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sūpaññeva bahuṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Soup refers to two kinds of soup: mung bean soup and black gram soup, which are hand-carried. Almsfood should be eaten with an equal proportion of soup. Whoever, being disrespectful, eats only a large amount of soup, commits an offense of wrongdoing.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, rasarase, ñātakānaṃ pavāritānaṃ, attano dhanena, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, when enjoying the taste, when relatives have invited one, with one’s own wealth, in times of danger, if insane, or for the first offender.

Catutthasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The fourth training rule is completed.
