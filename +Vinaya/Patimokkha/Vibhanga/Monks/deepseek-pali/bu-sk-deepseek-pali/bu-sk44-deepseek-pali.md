# BU-SK 44

## 618. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhuñjamānā sabbaṃ hatthaṃ mukhe pakkhipanti…pe….

## 618.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus, while eating, put their entire hand into their mouths…and so on.

‘‘Na bhuñjamāno sabbaṃ hatthaṃ mukhe pakkhipissāmīti sikkhākaraṇīyā’’ti.

“While eating, I will not put my entire hand into my mouth”—this is a training to be observed.

Na bhuñjamānena sabbo hattho mukhe pakkhipitabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca bhuñjamāno sabbaṃ hatthaṃ mukhe pakkhipati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

While eating, one should not put the entire hand into the mouth. Whoever, through lack of respect, while eating, puts the entire hand into the mouth, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional…or if one is a first offender.

Dutiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The second training rule is concluded.
