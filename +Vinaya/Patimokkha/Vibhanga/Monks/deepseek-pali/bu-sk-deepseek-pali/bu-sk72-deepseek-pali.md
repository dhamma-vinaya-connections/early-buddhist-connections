# BU-SK 72

## 646. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desenti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desessantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desethāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi –

## 646.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus, sitting on low seats, were teaching the Dhamma to one seated on a high seat. Those bhikkhus who were modest [...] complained, grumbled, and criticized: ‘How indeed can the group of six bhikkhus, sitting on low seats, teach the Dhamma to one seated on a high seat?’ [...] ‘Is it true, bhikkhus, that you, sitting on low seats, teach the Dhamma to one seated on a high seat?’ ‘It is true, Blessed One.’ The Blessed One rebuked them [...] ‘How indeed, foolish men, can you, sitting on low seats, teach the Dhamma to one seated on a high seat! This, foolish men, is not for the confidence of the unconfident [...]’ Having rebuked them [...], he gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus:
