# BU-SK 15

## 589. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū uccāsaddaṃ mahāsaddaṃ karontā antaraghare nisīdanti…pe….

## 589.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were sitting in inhabited areas making loud and great noises... and so on.

‘‘Appasaddo antaraghare nisīdissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not sit in inhabited areas with little noise”—this is a training to be observed.

Appasaddena antaraghare nisīditabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca uccāsaddaṃ mahāsaddaṃ karonto antaraghare nisīdati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not sit in inhabited areas making loud and great noises. Whoever, out of disrespect, sits in inhabited areas making loud and great noises, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional... or if one is a beginner.

Catutthasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The fourth training rule is concluded.
