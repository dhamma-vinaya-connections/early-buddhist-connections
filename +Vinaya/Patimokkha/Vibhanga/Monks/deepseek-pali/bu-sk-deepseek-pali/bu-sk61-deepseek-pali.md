# BU-SK 61

## 635. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū daṇḍapāṇissa dhammaṃ desenti…pe….

## 635.At that time the Blessed One, the Buddha, was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were teaching the Dhamma to one holding a staff…

‘‘Na daṇḍapāṇissa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not teach the Dhamma to one who is not ill and is holding a staff,” this is a training to be observed.

Daṇḍo nāma majjhimassa purisassa catuhattho daṇḍo. Tato ukkaṭṭho adaṇḍo, omako adaṇḍo.

A staff is a staff four cubits long for a man of average height. Anything longer than that is not a staff, and anything shorter than that is not a staff.

Na daṇḍapāṇissa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca daṇḍapāṇissa agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Dhamma should not be taught to one who is not ill and is holding a staff. Whoever, out of disrespect, teaches the Dhamma to one who is not ill and is holding a staff incurs an offense of wrong-doing.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional… or for the first offender.

Aṭṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The eighth training rule is completed.
