# BU-SK 64

## 638. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pādukāruḷhassa dhammaṃ desenti…pe….

## 638.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus were teaching the Dhamma to one wearing sandals [...].

‘‘Na pādukāruḷhassa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not teach the Dhamma to one wearing sandals who is not ill’—this is a training to be observed.

Na pādukāruḷhassa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca akkantassa vā paṭimukkassa vā omukkassa vā agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

The Dhamma should not be taught to one wearing sandals who is not ill. Whoever, out of disrespect, teaches the Dhamma to one who is not ill and is standing on, has put on, or is taking off sandals commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional [...] for the first offender.

Paṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The first training rule is concluded.
