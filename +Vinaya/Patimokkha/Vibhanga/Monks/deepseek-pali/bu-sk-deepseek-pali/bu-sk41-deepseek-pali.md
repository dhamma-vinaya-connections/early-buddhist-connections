# BU-SK 41

## 615. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mahantaṃ kabaḷaṃ karonti…pe….

## 615.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus made large mouthfuls…and so on.

‘‘Nātimahantaṃ kabaḷaṃ karissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not make too large a mouthful”—this is a training to be observed.

Nātimahanto kabaḷo kātabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca mahantaṃ kabaḷaṃ karoti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

A mouthful should not be too large. Whoever, through lack of respect, makes a large mouthful, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, khajjake, phalāphale, uttaribhaṅge, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, unmindful, or unknowing; if one is sick; in the case of chewable food; in the case of fruits; if exceeding the limit; in times of danger; if insane; or if one is a first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The ninth training rule is concluded.
