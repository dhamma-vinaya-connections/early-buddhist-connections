# BU-SK 38

## 612. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjanti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissanti! Kassa sampannaṃ na manāpaṃ! Kassa sāduṃ na ruccatī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjathāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, sūpampi odanampi attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

## 612.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus were eating soup or rice after requesting it for their own sake. People complained, grumbled, and spread it about: ‘How can these Sakyan recluses eat soup or rice after requesting it for their own sake! Who doesn’t like what is well-provided? Who doesn’t enjoy what is delicious?’ The bhikkhus heard these people complaining, grumbling, and spreading it about. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, grumbled, and spread it about: ‘How can the group of six bhikkhus eat soup or rice after requesting it for their own sake!’… ‘Is it true, bhikkhus, that you eat soup or rice after requesting it for your own sake?’ ‘It is true, Blessed One.’ The Blessed One rebuked them… ‘How can you, foolish men, eat soup or rice after requesting it for your own sake! This is not, foolish men, for inspiring confidence in the unconfident…’ And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

‘‘Na sūpaṃ vā odanaṃ vā attano atthāya viññāpetvā bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not eat soup or rice after requesting it for my own sake’—this is a training to be observed.

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down by the Blessed One for the bhikkhus.
