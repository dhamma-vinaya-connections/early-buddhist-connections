# BU-SK 57

## 631. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā bhaggesu viharati susumāragire [suṃsumāragire (sī. syā.), saṃsumāragire (ka.)] bhesakaḷāvane migadāye. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū kokanade [kokanude (ka.)] pāsāde sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālakaṃ paṭiggaṇhanti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālakaṃ paṭiggahessanti, seyyathāpi gihī kāmabhogino’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālakaṃ paṭiggahessantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālakaṃ paṭiggaṇhantīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālakaṃ paṭiggahessanti! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

## 631.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Bhaggas on Susumāragiri, in the Bhesakaḷā Grove, the Deer Park. Now at that time bhikkhus at the Kokanada Mansion were receiving a water vessel with a hand soiled with food. People complained and criticized them, “How can these Sakyan ascetics receive a water vessel with a hand soiled with food, just like householders who indulge in sensual pleasures?” The bhikkhus heard the complaints of those people. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained and criticized them, “How can these bhikkhus receive a water vessel with a hand soiled with food?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus receive a water vessel with a hand soiled with food?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “How can you, foolish men, receive a water vessel with a hand soiled with food! This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:

‘‘Na sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālakaṃ paṭiggahessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not accept a water vessel with a hand soiled with food’—this is a training to be undertaken.

Na sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālako paṭiggahetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sāmisena hatthena pānīyathālakaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

A water vessel should not be accepted with a hand soiled with food. Whoever, being disrespectful, accepts a water vessel with a hand soiled with food, commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, ‘‘dhovissāmī’’ti vā ‘‘dhovāpessāmī’’ti vā paṭiggaṇhāti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional, if one is unmindful, if one does not know, if one is ill, if one accepts it thinking, “I will wash it” or “I will have it washed,” in times of danger, for an insane person, or for the first offender.

Pañcamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The fifth training rule is completed.
