# BU-SK 62

## 636. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū satthapāṇissa dhammaṃ desenti…pe….

## 636.At that time the Blessed One, the Buddha, was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were teaching the Dhamma to one holding a weapon…

‘‘Na satthapāṇissa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not teach the Dhamma to one who is not ill and is holding a weapon,” this is a training to be observed.

Satthaṃ nāma ekatodhāraṃ ubhatodhāraṃ paharaṇaṃ.

A weapon is that which has one edge or two edges, used for striking.

Na satthapāṇissa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca satthapāṇissa agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Dhamma should not be taught to one who is not ill and is holding a weapon. Whoever, out of disrespect, teaches the Dhamma to one who is not ill and is holding a weapon incurs an offense of wrong-doing.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional… or for the first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The ninth training rule is completed.
