# BU-SK 30

## 604. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū piṇḍapātaṃ paṭiggaṇhantā sūpaññeva bahuṃ paṭiggaṇhanti…pe….

## 604.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. Now at that time the group-of-six bhikkhus, while receiving almsfood, would receive an excessive amount of soup...and so on.

‘‘Samasūpakaṃ piṇḍapātaṃ paṭiggahessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will receive almsfood with an equal proportion of soup”—this is a training to be observed.

Sūpo nāma dve sūpā – muggasūpo, māsasūpo. Hatthahāriyo samasūpako piṇḍapāto paṭiggahetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sūpaññeva bahuṃ paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Soup is of two kinds: mung bean soup and black gram soup. Almsfood with an equal proportion of soup, obtained by hand, should be received. Whoever, being disrespectful, receives only an excessive amount of soup, commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, rasarase, ñātakānaṃ pavāritānaṃ, aññassatthāya, attano dhanena, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional, unmindful, unknowing, if one is sick, when there are various flavors, when invited by relatives, for the sake of another, with one's own property, in times of danger, if one is insane, or if one is the first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The ninth training rule is concluded.
