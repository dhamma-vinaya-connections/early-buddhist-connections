# BU-SK 21

## 595. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdanti sīsaṃ olambentā…pe….

## 595.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. Now at that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were sitting indoors swaying their heads, dangling their heads down...and so on.

‘‘Na sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not sit swaying my head indoors”—this is a training to be observed.

Na sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīditabbaṃ. Sīsaṃ paggahetvā nisīditabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sīsappacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdati sīsaṃ olambento, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not sit indoors swaying the head. One should sit holding the head up. Whoever, being disrespectful, sits indoors swaying the head or letting the head droop, commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, vāsūpagatassa, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional, unmindful, unknowing, if one is sick, if one has entered a dwelling, in times of danger, if one is insane, or if one is the first offender.

Dasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The tenth training rule is concluded.

Ujjagghikavaggo dutiyo.

The Chapter on Giggling, the Second.
