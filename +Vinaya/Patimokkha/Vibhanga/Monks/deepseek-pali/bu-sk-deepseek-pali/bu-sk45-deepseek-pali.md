# BU-SK 45

## 619. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sakabaḷena mukhena byāharanti…pe….

## 619.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus spoke with their mouths full…and so on.

‘‘Na sakabaḷena mukhena byāharissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not speak with my mouth full”—this is a training to be observed.

Na sakabaḷena mukhena byāharitabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca sakabaḷena mukhena byāharati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not speak with a mouthful. Whoever, through lack of respect, speaks with a mouthful, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional…or if one is a first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The third training rule is concluded.
