# BU-SK 19

## 593. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bāhuppacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdanti bāhuṃ olambentā…pe….

## 593.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were sitting in inhabited areas swaying their arms, letting their arms droop... and so on.

‘‘Na bāhuppacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not sit in inhabited areas swaying my arms”—this is a training to be observed.

Na bāhuppacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīditabbaṃ. Bāhuṃ paggahetvā nisīditabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca bāhuppacālakaṃ antaraghare nisīdati bāhuṃ olambento, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not sit in inhabited areas swaying the arms. One should sit with the arms held steady. Whoever, out of disrespect, sits in inhabited areas swaying his arms, letting them droop, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, vāsūpagatassa, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, if one has taken shelter, in times of danger, if insane, or if one is a beginner.

Aṭṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The eighth training rule is concluded.
