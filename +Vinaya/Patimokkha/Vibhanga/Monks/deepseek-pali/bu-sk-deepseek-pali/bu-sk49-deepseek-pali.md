# BU-SK 49

## 623. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjanti…pe….

## 623.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus were eating while shaking their hands…and so on.

‘‘Na hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not eat while shaking my hand”—this is a training to be observed.

Na hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjitabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca hatthaniddhunakaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not eat while shaking the hand. Whoever, through lack of respect, eats while shaking the hand, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, kacavaraṃ chaḍḍento hatthaṃ niddhunāti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, unmindful, or unknowing; if one is sick; if one shakes the hand while discarding refuse; in times of danger; if insane; or if one is a first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The seventh training rule is concluded.
