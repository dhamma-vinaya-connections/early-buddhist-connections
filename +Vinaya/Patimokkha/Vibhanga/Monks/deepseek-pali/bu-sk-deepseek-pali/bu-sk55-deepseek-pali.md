# BU-SK 55

## 629. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pattanillehakaṃ bhuñjanti…pe….

## 629.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were eating, licking their bowls… and so on.

‘‘Na pattanillehakaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not eat licking the bowl’—this is a training to be undertaken.

Na pattanillehakaṃ bhuñjitabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca pattanillehakaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not eat licking the bowl. Whoever, being disrespectful, eats licking the bowl, commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, parittake sese ekato saṅkaḍḍhitvā nillehitvā bhuñjati, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional, if one is unmindful, if one does not know, if one is ill, if, having gathered a small amount of leftovers, one licks them before eating, in times of danger, for an insane person, or for the first offender.

Tatiyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The third training rule is completed.
