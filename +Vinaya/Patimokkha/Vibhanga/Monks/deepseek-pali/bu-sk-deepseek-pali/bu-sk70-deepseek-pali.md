# BU-SK 70

## 644. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū oguṇṭhitasīsassa dhammaṃ desenti…pe….

## 644.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus were teaching the Dhamma to one with a covered head [...].

‘‘Na oguṇṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not teach the Dhamma to one with a covered head who is not ill’—this is a training to be observed.

Oguṇṭhitasīso nāma sasīsaṃ pāruto vuccati.

A ‘covered head’ means one who is covered together with the head.

Na oguṇṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca oguṇṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

The Dhamma should not be taught to one with a covered head who is not ill. Whoever, out of disrespect, teaches the Dhamma to one with a covered head who is not ill commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, sīsaṃ vivarāpetvā deseti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, unmindful, not knowing, if one is ill, if one teaches after having the head uncovered, in emergencies, if mad, or for the first offender.

Sattamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The seventh training rule is concluded.
