# BU-SK 69

## 643. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū veṭhitasīsassa dhammaṃ desenti…pe….

## 643.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus were teaching the Dhamma to one with a wrapped head [...].

‘‘Na veṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not teach the Dhamma to one with a wrapped head who is not ill’—this is a training to be observed.

Veṭhitasīso nāma kesantaṃ na dassāpetvā veṭhito hoti.

A ‘wrapped head’ means one who is wrapped so that the hair-ends are not visible.

Na veṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca veṭhitasīsassa agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

The Dhamma should not be taught to one with a wrapped head who is not ill. Whoever, out of disrespect, teaches the Dhamma to one with a wrapped head who is not ill commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, kesantaṃ vivarāpetvā deseti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, unmindful, not knowing, if one is ill, if one teaches after having the hair-ends uncovered, in emergencies, if mad, or for the first offender.

Chaṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The sixth training rule is concluded.
