# BU-SK 53

## 627. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarena brāhmaṇena saṅghassa payopānaṃ paṭiyattaṃ hoti. Bhikkhū surusurukārakaṃ khīraṃ pivanti. Aññataro naṭapubbako bhikkhu evamāha – ‘‘sabboyaṃ maññe saṅgho sītīkato’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhu saṅghaṃ ārabbha davaṃ karissatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, saṅghaṃ ārabbha davaṃ akāsīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, saṅghaṃ ārabbha davaṃ karissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘na, bhikkhave, buddhaṃ vā dhammaṃ vā saṅghaṃ vā ārabbha davo kātabbo. Yo kareyya, āpatti dukkaṭassā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā taṃ bhikkhuṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

## 627.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambī in Ghosita’s Monastery. Now at that time a certain brahmin had prepared a milk-drink offering for the Saṅgha. The bhikkhus were drinking milk making a slurping sound. A certain bhikkhu who had formerly been an actor said, “It seems the whole Saṅgha has cooled down.” Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained and criticized him: “How could a bhikkhu make a disparaging remark about the Saṅgha?”… “Is it true, bhikkhu, that you made a disparaging remark about the Saṅgha?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “Foolish man, how could you make a disparaging remark about the Saṅgha! This will not inspire confidence in those without it…” Having rebuked him… the Blessed One gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, one should not make disparaging remarks about the Buddha, the Dhamma, or the Saṅgha. Whoever does so commits an offense of wrong conduct.” Then the Blessed One, having rebuked that bhikkhu in many ways for his being difficult to support… laid down this training rule:

‘‘Na surusurukārakaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not eat making a slurping sound’—this is a training to be undertaken.

Na surusurukārakaṃ bhuñjitabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca surusurukārakaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not eat making a slurping sound. Whoever, being disrespectful, eats making a slurping sound, commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional… or for the first offender.

Paṭhamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The first training rule is completed.
