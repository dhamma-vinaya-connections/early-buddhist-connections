# BU-SK 11

## 585. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū ukkhittakāya antaraghare nisīdanti…pe….

## 585.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were sitting in inhabited areas with their robes hitched up... and so on.

‘‘Na ukkhittakāya antaraghare nisīdissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not sit in inhabited areas with my robe hitched up”—this is a training to be observed.

Na ukkhittakāya antaraghare nisīditabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca ekato vā ubhato vā ukkhipitvā antaraghare nisīdati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not sit in inhabited areas with the robe hitched up. Whoever, out of disrespect, sits in inhabited areas with the robe hitched up on one side or both, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, gilānassa, vāsūpagatassa, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional, if unmindful, if unknowing, if sick, if one has taken shelter, in times of danger, if insane, or if one is a beginner.

Dasamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The tenth training rule is concluded.

Parimaṇḍalavaggo paṭhamo.

The First Chapter on Proper Robe-Wearing.

2. Ujjagghikavaggo

2.The Chapter on Loud Laughter.
