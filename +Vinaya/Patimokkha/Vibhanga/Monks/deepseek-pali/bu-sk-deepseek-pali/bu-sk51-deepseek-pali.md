# BU-SK 51

## 625. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jivhānicchārakaṃ bhuñjanti…pe….

## 625.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time the group of six bhikkhus were eating while sticking out their tongues…and so on.

‘‘Na jivhānicchārakaṃ bhuñjissāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

“I will not eat while sticking out my tongue”—this is a training to be observed.

Na jivhānicchārakaṃ bhuñjitabbaṃ. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca jivhānicchārakaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not eat while sticking out the tongue. Whoever, through lack of respect, eats while sticking out the tongue, incurs an offense of wrong conduct.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if unintentional…or if one is a first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The ninth training rule is concluded.
