# BU-SK 73

## 647. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, bārāṇasiyaṃ aññatarassa chapakassa [chavakassa (syā.)] pajāpati gabbhinī ahosi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, sā chapakī taṃ chapakaṃ etadavoca – ‘gabbhinīmhi, ayyaputta! Icchāmi ambaṃ khāditu’nti. ‘Natthi ambaṃ [ambo (syā.)], akālo ambassā’ti. ‘Sace na labhissāmi marissāmī’ti. Tena kho pana samayena, rañño ambo dhuvaphalo hoti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so chapako yena so ambo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ ambaṃ abhiruhitvā nilīno acchi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā purohitena brāhmaṇena saddhiṃ yena so ambo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ucce āsane nisīditvā mantaṃ pariyāpuṇāti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, tassa chapakassa etadahosi – ‘yāva adhammiko ayaṃ rājā, yatra hi nāma ucce āsane nisīditvā mantaṃ pariyāpuṇissati. Ayañca brāhmaṇo adhammiko, yatra hi nāma nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa mantaṃ vācessati. Ahañcamhi adhammiko, yohaṃ itthiyā kāraṇā rañño ambaṃ avaharāmi. Sabbamidaṃ carimaṃ kata’nti tattheva paripati.

## 647.‘Once upon a time, bhikkhus, in Bārāṇasī, the wife of a certain outcaste was pregnant. Then, bhikkhus, that female outcaste said to the outcaste, ‘My dear, I’m pregnant! I wish to eat a mango.’ ‘There are no mangoes; it’s not mango season.’ ‘If I don’t get one, I’ll die!’ Now at that time, the king had a mango tree that bore fruit continuously. Then, bhikkhus, that outcaste went to that mango tree; having gone there, he climbed that mango tree and hid himself. Just then, bhikkhus, the king, together with his brahmin chaplain, went to that mango tree; having gone there, sitting on a high seat, he was learning a mantra. Then, bhikkhus, that outcaste thought: ‘How unrighteous is this king, who indeed, sitting on a high seat, learns a mantra! And this brahmin is unrighteous, who indeed, sitting on a low seat, makes one seated on a high seat recite a mantra! And I am unrighteous, who, for the sake of a woman, steals the king’s mango. All this is my final deed.’ And right there he fell down.

[imā gāthāyo jā. 1.4.33-35 jātake aññathā dissanti] ‘‘Ubho atthaṃ na jānanti, ubho dhammaṃ na passare;Yo cāyaṃ mantaṃ vāceti, yo cādhammenadhīyati.

“Both do not understand the meaning, both do not see the Dhamma—both the one who recites the mantra and the one who learns it unrighteously.”

‘‘Sālīnaṃ odano bhutto, sucimaṃsūpasecano;Tasmā dhamme na vattāmi, dhammo ariyebhi vaṇṇito.

“‘Having eaten rice and curry seasoned with pure meat, therefore I do not act according to the Dhamma, though the Dhamma is praised by the noble ones.’”

‘‘Dhiratthu taṃ dhanalābhaṃ, yasalābhañca brāhmaṇa;Yā vutti vinipātena, adhammacaraṇena vā.

“Shame on that gain of wealth and fame, O brahmin, that livelihood which comes by way of ruin or unrighteous conduct.

‘‘Paribbaja mahābrahme, pacantaññepi pāṇino;Mā tvaṃ adhammo ācarito, asmā kumbhamivābhidā’’ti.

‘Wander forth, great brahmin! Even other beings cook and eat. Do not practice unrighteousness, lest you be broken like a pot.’”

‘‘Tadāpi me, bhikkhave, amanāpā nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa mantaṃ vācetuṃ, kimaṅga pana etarahi na amanāpā bhavissati nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa dhammaṃ desetuṃ. Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

“Even then, bhikkhus, it was displeasing to me for one sitting on a low seat to recite a mantra to one sitting on a high seat. How much more displeasing would it be now for one sitting on a low seat to teach the Dhamma to one sitting on a high seat! This, bhikkhus, is not for inspiring confidence in the uninspired… And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

‘‘Na nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa agilānassa dhammaṃ desessāmīti sikkhā karaṇīyā’’ti.

‘I will not, while sitting on a low seat, teach the Dhamma to one sitting on a high seat who is not ill’: thus is the training to be observed.”

Na nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa agilānassa dhammo desetabbo. Yo anādariyaṃ paṭicca nīce āsane nisīditvā ucce āsane nisinnassa agilānassa dhammaṃ deseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

One should not, while sitting on a low seat, teach the Dhamma to one sitting on a high seat who is not ill. Whoever, out of disrespect, while sitting on a low seat, teaches the Dhamma to one sitting on a high seat who is not ill, incurs an offense of wrong-doing.

Anāpatti asañcicca…pe… ādikammikassāti.

There is no offense if it is unintentional… or for the first offender.

Navamasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The ninth training rule is concluded.
