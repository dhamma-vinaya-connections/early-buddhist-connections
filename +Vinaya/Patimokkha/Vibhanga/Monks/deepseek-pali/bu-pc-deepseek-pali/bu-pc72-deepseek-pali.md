# BU-PC 72

## 2. Vilekhanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2.The Training Rule on Disparagement.

438. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. [idaṃ vatthu cūḷava. 320] Tena kho pana samayena bhagavā bhikkhūnaṃ anekapariyāyena vinayakathaṃ katheti, vinayassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, vinayapariyattiyā vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, ādissa ādissa āyasmato upālissa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati. Bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi [bhikkhū bhagavā (syā. ka.)] – ‘‘bhagavā kho anekapariyāyena vinayakathaṃ katheti, vinayassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, vinayapariyattiyā vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, ādissa ādissa āyasmato upālissa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati. Handa mayaṃ, āvuso, āyasmato upālissa santike vinayaṃ pariyāpuṇāmā’’ti, te ca bahū bhikkhū therā ca navā ca majjhimā ca āyasmato upālissa santike vinayaṃ pariyāpuṇanti.

438.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. At that time the Blessed One was in many ways telling the bhikkhus about the Discipline, speaking in praise of the Discipline, speaking in praise of the learning of the Discipline, and repeatedly speaking in praise of Venerable Upāli. The bhikkhus had this thought: "The Blessed One is in many ways telling about the Discipline, speaking in praise of the Discipline, speaking in praise of the learning of the Discipline, and repeatedly speaking in praise of Venerable Upāli. Come, friends, let us learn the Discipline from Venerable Upāli." And many bhikkhus, both senior, newly ordained, and those in between, learned the Discipline from Venerable Upāli.

Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘etarahi kho, āvuso, bahū bhikkhū therā ca navā ca majjhimā ca āyasmato upālissa santike vinayaṃ pariyāpuṇanti. Sace ime vinaye pakataññuno bhavissanti amhe yenicchakaṃ yadicchakaṃ yāvadicchakaṃ ākaḍḍhissanti parikaḍḍhissanti. Handa mayaṃ, āvuso, vinayaṃ vivaṇṇemā’’ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhū upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘kiṃ panimehi khuddānukhuddakehi sikkhāpadehi uddiṭṭhehi, yāvadeva kukkuccāya vihesāya vilekhāya saṃvattantī’’ti! Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū vinayaṃ vivaṇṇessantīti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, vinayaṃ vivaṇṇethāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, vinayaṃ vivaṇṇessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the bhikkhus from the group of six thought: “Now, friends, many bhikkhus—elders, new ones, and those in between—are learning the Discipline from Venerable Upāli. If these become knowledgeable in the Discipline, they will drag us around as they please, pull us this way and that as much as they want. Come, friends, let us speak in disparagement of the Discipline.” Then the bhikkhus from the group of six approached the bhikkhus and said: “What is the use of these minor and trifling training rules that are recited? They only lead to scrupulousness, vexation, and mental torment!” Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained and criticised, saying: “How can the bhikkhus from the group of six speak in disparagement of the Discipline?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you speak in disparagement of the Discipline?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha rebuked them… “How can you, foolish men, speak in disparagement of the Discipline? This will not lead to confidence in the unconvinced…” … “And, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:—”

439. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadeyya – ‘kiṃ panimehi khuddānukhuddakehi sikkhāpadehi uddiṭṭhehi, yāvadeva kukkuccāya vihesāya vilekhāya saṃvattantī’ti, sikkhāpadavivaṇṇake pācittiya’’nti.

439."If any bhikkhu, while the Pātimokkha is being recited, should say: 'Why are these minor and insignificant training rules recited? They only lead to scrupulousness, annoyance, and mental torment,' he commits a pācittiya offense for disparaging the training rules."

440. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

440."Yo pana" means whoever, of whatever sort... "bhikkhu" means... A "bhikkhu" in this connection is one who is understood to be such.

Pātimokkhe uddissamāneti uddisante vā uddisāpente vā sajjhāyaṃ vā karonte.

"While the Pātimokkha is being recited" means while one is reciting it, or causing it to be recited, or making a recitation.

Evaṃ vadeyyāti – ‘‘kiṃ panimehi khuddānukhuddakehi sikkhāpadehi uddiṭṭhehi, yāvadeva kukkuccāya vihesāya vilekhāya saṃvattantīti. ‘‘Ye imaṃ pariyāpuṇanti tesaṃ kukkuccaṃ hoti, vihesā hoti, vilekhā hoti, ye imaṃ na pariyāpuṇanti tesaṃ kukkuccaṃ na hoti vihesā na hoti vilekhā na hoti. Anuddiṭṭhaṃ idaṃ varaṃ, anuggahitaṃ idaṃ varaṃ, apariyāpuṭaṃ idaṃ varaṃ, adhāritaṃ idaṃ varaṃ, vinayo vā antaradhāyatu, ime vā bhikkhū apakataññuno hontū’’ti upasampannassa vinayaṃ vivaṇṇeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

"Should say" means: "Why are these minor and insignificant training rules recited? They only lead to scrupulousness, vexation, and mental torment." "Those who learn this experience scrupulousness, vexation, and mental torment; those who do not learn this do not experience scrupulousness, vexation, or mental torment. It would be better if this were not recited, not learned, not grasped, not retained. May the Vinaya disappear, or may these bhikkhus become ignorant." If one disparages the Vinaya to one who is fully ordained, it is a pācittiya offense.

441. Upasampanne upasampannasaññī vinayaṃ vivaṇṇeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Upasampanne vematiko vinayaṃ vivaṇṇeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Upasampanne anupasampannasaññī vinayaṃ vivaṇṇeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

441.If one, perceiving another as fully ordained, disparages the Vinaya to that fully ordained person, it is a pācittiya offense. If one, being uncertain of another's full ordination, disparages the Vinaya to that person, it is a pācittiya offense. If one, perceiving another as not fully ordained, disparages the Vinaya to that fully ordained person, it is a pācittiya offense.

Aññaṃ dhammaṃ vivaṇṇeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anupasampannassa vinayaṃ vā aññaṃ vā dhammaṃ vivaṇṇeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anupasampanne upasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anupasampanne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anupasampanne anupasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If one disparages another teaching, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If one disparages the Vinaya or another teaching to one who is not fully ordained, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If one disparages to one who is not fully ordained, perceiving them as fully ordained, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If one disparages to one who is not fully ordained, being uncertain, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If one disparages to one who is not fully ordained, perceiving them as not fully ordained, it is a dukkaṭa offense.

442. Anāpatti na vivaṇṇetukāmo, ‘‘iṅgha tvaṃ suttante vā gāthāyo vā abhidhammaṃ vā pariyāpuṇassu, pacchā vinayaṃ pariyāpuṇissasī’’ti bhaṇati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

442.There is no offense if one does not intend to disparage and says, "Come, you should study the Suttas, the verses, or the Abhidhamma first; later you can study the Vinaya," or if one is insane, or if one is a first offender.

Vilekhanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The second training rule on disparagement is concluded.
