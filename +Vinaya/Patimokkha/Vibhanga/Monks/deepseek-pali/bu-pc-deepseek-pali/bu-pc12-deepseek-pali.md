# BU-PC 12

## 2. Aññavādakasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2.The training rule on evasive speech.

94. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā channo anācāraṃ ācaritvā saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati – ‘‘ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā’’ti? Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā channo saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarissati – ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, channa, saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarasi – ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarissasi – ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathāti! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, saṅgho channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropetu. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, ropetabbaṃ. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

94.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambī in Ghosita’s monastery. At that time, Venerable Channa behaved improperly, and when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, he evaded by diverting the issue: “Who is at fault? What is the offense? In regard to what? How is one at fault? Whom are you speaking to? What are you saying?” Those bhikkhus of few desires… complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How can Venerable Channa, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, evade by diverting the issue: ‘Who is at fault? What is the offense? In regard to what? How is one at fault? Whom are you speaking to? What are you saying?’” … “Is it true, Channa, that you, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, evade by diverting the issue: ‘Who is at fault? What is the offense? In regard to what? How is one at fault? Whom are you speaking to? What are you saying?’” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “Foolish man, how can you, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, evade by diverting the issue: ‘Who is at fault? What is the offense? In regard to what? How is one at fault? Whom are you speaking to? What are you saying?’ This, foolish man, is not for the confidence of those without it, nor for increasing the confidence of those who have it…” After rebuking him…, the Blessed One gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus: “Therefore, bhikkhus, let the Saṅgha impose the penalty of evasive speech on the bhikkhu Channa. And it should be imposed like this: A competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Saṅgha:

95. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropeyya. Esā ñatti.

95.“Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu Channa, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, evades by diverting the issue. If the Saṅgha is ready, let the Saṅgha impose the penalty of evasive speech on the bhikkhu Channa. This is the motion.

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati. Saṅgho channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropeti. Yassāyasmato khamati channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakassa ropanā, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu Channa, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, evades by diverting the issue. The Saṅgha imposes the penalty of evasive speech on the bhikkhu Channa. Whoever among the venerables approves of the imposition of the penalty of evasive speech on the bhikkhu Channa, let him remain silent; whoever does not approve, let him speak.

‘‘Ropitaṃ saṅghena channassa bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The Saṅgha has imposed the penalty of evasive speech on the bhikkhu Channa. The Saṅgha approves; therefore, it is silent. Thus I hold it.”

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ channaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One, in many ways, rebuked Venerable Channa for being difficult to support… And, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule as follows:

‘‘Aññavādake pācittiya’’nti.

“In the case of evasive speech, there is an offense entailing confession.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

96. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā channo saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno ‘‘aññenaññaṃ paṭicaranto – ‘‘āpattiṃ āpajjissāmī’’ti tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā channo saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ vihesessatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, channa, saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ vihesesīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ vihesessasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, saṅgho channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropetu. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, ropetabbaṃ. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

96.At that time, Venerable Channa, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, remained silent and harassed the Saṅgha. Those bhikkhus of few desires… complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How can Venerable Channa, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, remain silent and harass the Saṅgha?”… “Is it true, Channa, that you, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, remained silent and harassed the Saṅgha?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “Foolish man, how can you, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, remain silent and harass the Saṅgha! This, foolish man, is not for the confidence of those without it, nor for increasing the confidence of those who have it…” After rebuking him…, the Blessed One gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus: “Therefore, bhikkhus, let the Saṅgha impose the penalty of harassment on the bhikkhu Channa. And it should be imposed like this: A competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Saṅgha:

97. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropeyya. Esā ñatti.

97.“Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu Channa, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, remains silent and harasses the Saṅgha. If the Saṅgha is ready, let the Saṅgha impose the penalty of harassment on the bhikkhu Channa. This is the motion.”

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ channo bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjīyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti. Saṅgho channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropeti. Yassāyasmāto khamati channassa bhikkhuno vihesakassa ropanā, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu Channa, when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Saṅgha, remains silent and harasses the Saṅgha. The Saṅgha imposes the penalty of harassment on the bhikkhu Channa. Whoever among the venerables approves of the imposition of the penalty of harassment on the bhikkhu Channa, let him remain silent; whoever does not approve, let him speak.

‘‘Ropitaṃ saṅghena channassa bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The Saṅgha has imposed the penalty of harassment on the bhikkhu Channa. The Saṅgha approves; therefore, it is silent. Thus I hold it.”

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ channaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One, in many ways, rebuked Venerable Channa for being difficult to support… And, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule as follows:

98. ‘‘Aññavādake  vihesake pācittiya’’nti.

98.“In the case of evasive speech and harassment, there is an offense entailing confession.”

99. Aññavādako nāma saṅghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati – ‘‘ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā’’ti. Eso aññavādako nāma.

99.Aññavādaka is defined as one who, when questioned in the midst of the Saṅgha about a matter or an offense, being unwilling to speak about it or reveal it, responds evasively with other matters: “Who is at fault? What is the offense? In what respect is there an offense? How is there an offense? Whom are you addressing? What are you saying?” This is called aññavādaka.

Vihesako nāma saṅghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti. Eso vihesako nāma.

Vihesaka is defined as one who, when questioned in the midst of the Saṅgha about a matter or an offense, being unwilling to speak about it or reveal it, remains silent and harasses the Saṅgha. This is called vihesaka.

100. Āropite aññavādake saṅghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati – ‘‘ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā’’ti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Āropite vihesake saṅghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ropite aññavādake saṅghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati – ‘‘ko āpanno, kiṃ āpanno, kismiṃ āpanno, kathaṃ āpanno, kaṃ bhaṇatha, kiṃ bhaṇathā’’ti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Ropite vihesake saṅghamajjhe vatthusmiṃ vā āpattiyā vā anuyuñjīyamāno taṃ na kathetukāmo taṃ na ugghāṭetukāmo tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

100.When the penalty of evasive speech has not been imposed on an aññavādaka, if he, when questioned in the midst of the Saṅgha about a matter or an offense, being unwilling to speak about it or reveal it, responds evasively with other matters: “Who is at fault? What is the offense? In what respect is there an offense? How is there an offense? Whom are you addressing? What are you saying?”—there is an offense of wrong-doing. When the penalty of harassment has not been imposed on a vihesaka, if he, when questioned in the midst of the Saṅgha about a matter or an offense, being unwilling to speak about it or reveal it, remains silent and harasses the Saṅgha, there is an offense of wrong-doing. When the penalty of evasive speech has been imposed on an aññavādaka, if he, when questioned in the midst of the Saṅgha about a matter or an offense, being unwilling to speak about it or reveal it, responds evasively with other matters: “Who is at fault? What is the offense? In what respect is there an offense? How is there an offense? Whom are you addressing? What are you saying?”—there is an offense entailing confession. When the penalty of harassment has been imposed on a vihesaka, if he, when questioned in the midst of the Saṅgha about a matter or an offense, being unwilling to speak about it or reveal it, remains silent and harasses the Saṅgha, there is an offense entailing confession.

101. Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī aññavādake vihesake, āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme vematiko aññavādake vihesake, āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī aññavādake vihesake, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

101.In a legitimate formal act, perceiving it as a legitimate formal act, if he performs evasive speech and harassment, there is an offense entailing confession. In a legitimate formal act, being uncertain, if he performs evasive speech and harassment, there is an offense entailing confession. In a legitimate formal act, perceiving it as an illegitimate formal act, if he performs evasive speech and harassment, there is an offense entailing confession. In an illegitimate formal act, perceiving it as a legitimate formal act, there is an offense of wrong-doing. In an illegitimate formal act, being uncertain, there is an offense of wrong-doing. In an illegitimate formal act, perceiving it as an illegitimate formal act, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

102. Anāpatti ajānanto pucchati, gilāno vā na katheti; ‘‘saṅghassa bhaṇḍanaṃ vā kalaho vā viggaho vā vivādo vā bhavissatī’’ti na katheti; ‘‘saṅghabhedo vā saṅgharāji vā bhavissatī’’ti na katheti; ‘‘adhammena vā vaggena vā nakammārahassa vā kammaṃ karissatī’’ti na katheti; ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

102.There is no offense if he asks out of ignorance; or if he is sick and cannot speak; or if he does not speak thinking, “There will be quarreling, dispute, contention, or argument in the Saṅgha”; or if he does not speak thinking, “There will be a schism in the Saṅgha or a faction in the Saṅgha”; or if he does not speak thinking, “They will perform an unlawful act, or an act by a faction, or an act against one who is unfit for it”; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Aññavādakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The second training rule on evasive speech is finished.
