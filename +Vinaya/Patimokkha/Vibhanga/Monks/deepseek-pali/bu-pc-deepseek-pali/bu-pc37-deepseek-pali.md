# BU-PC 37

## 7. Vikālabhojanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 7.The Training Rule on Eating at the Wrong Time.

247. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena rājagahe giraggasamajjo hoti. Sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū giraggasamajjaṃ dassanāya agamaṃsu. Manussā sattarasavaggiye bhikkhū passitvā nahāpetvā vilimpetvā bhojetvā khādanīyaṃ adaṃsu. Sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū khādanīyaṃ ādāya ārāmaṃ gantvā chabbaggiye bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘gaṇhāthāvuso, khādanīyaṃ khādathā’’ti. ‘‘Kuto tumhehi, āvuso, khādanīyaṃ laddha’’nti? Sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Kiṃ pana tumhe, āvuso, vikāle bhojanaṃ bhuñjathā’’ti? ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū vikāle bhojanaṃ bhuñjissantī’’ti! Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū vikāle bhojanaṃ bhuñjissantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, vikāle bhojanaṃ bhuñjathāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, vikāle bhojanaṃ bhuñjissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

247.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrel Sanctuary. Now at that time, a mountain-top festival was held in Rājagaha. The bhikkhus of the group of seventeen went to see the mountain-top festival. People, having seen the bhikkhus of the group of seventeen, bathed them, anointed them, fed them, and gave them chewable food. The bhikkhus of the group of seventeen, taking the chewable food, went to the monastery and said to the bhikkhus of the group of six: “Take, friends, eat the chewable food.” “Friends, where did you get this chewable food?” The bhikkhus of the group of seventeen reported this matter to the bhikkhus of the group of six. “But friends, do you eat food at the wrong time?” “Yes, friends.” The bhikkhus of the group of six complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How can the bhikkhus of the group of seventeen eat food at the wrong time!” Then the bhikkhus of the group of six reported this matter to the bhikkhus. Those bhikkhus who were modest [...] complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How can the bhikkhus of the group of seventeen eat food at the wrong time!” [...] “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you eat food at the wrong time?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them [...] “Foolish men, how can you eat food at the wrong time! Foolish men, this will not inspire confidence in those who are not yet confident [...] And so, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule thus:

248. Yo pana bhikkhu vikāle khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

248.“Whatever bhikkhu should chew or eat chewable food or eatable food at the wrong time, there is an offense entailing expiation.”

249. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

249.By ‘whatever’ is meant whoever, of whatever kind [...] By ‘bhikkhu’ is meant a bhikkhu intended in this sense.

Vikālo nāma majjhanhike vītivatte yāva aruṇuggamanā.

‘Wrong time’ means after midday has passed until the rising of dawn.

Khādanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ ṭhapetvā avasesaṃ khādanīyaṃ nāma.

‘Chewable food’ means, excluding the five staple foods, and excluding what is allowable for a period, for seven days, or for life; the remainder is called ‘chewable food’.

Bhojanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṃsaṃ.

‘Eatable food’ means the five staple foods: cooked rice, porridge, flour of roasted grain, fish, and meat.

‘‘Khādissāmi bhuñjissāmī’’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he accepts with the thought, ‘I will chew, I will eat,’ there is an offense of wrong-doing. With each mouthful, there is an offense entailing expiation.

250. Vikāle vikālasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Vikāle vematiko khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Vikāle kālasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

250.If it is the wrong time and he perceives it as the wrong time, and he chews or eats chewable food or eatable food, there is an offense entailing expiation. If it is the wrong time and he is doubtful, and he chews or eats chewable food or eatable food, there is an offense entailing expiation. If it is the wrong time and he perceives it as the right time, and he chews or eats chewable food or eatable food, there is an offense entailing expiation.

Yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ āhāratthāya paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti dukkaṭassa. Kāle vikālasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Kāle vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Kāle kālasaññī, anāpatti.

If he accepts what is allowable for a period, for seven days, or for life, for the purpose of food, there is an offense of wrong-doing. With each mouthful, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is the right time and he perceives it as the wrong time, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is the right time and he is doubtful, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is the right time and he perceives it as the right time, there is no offense.

251. Anāpatti yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ sati paccaye paribhuñjati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

251.There is no offense if he consumes what is allowable for a period, for seven days, or for life, when there is a reason; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Vikālabhojanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The Training Rule on Eating at the Wrong Time, the seventh, is finished.
