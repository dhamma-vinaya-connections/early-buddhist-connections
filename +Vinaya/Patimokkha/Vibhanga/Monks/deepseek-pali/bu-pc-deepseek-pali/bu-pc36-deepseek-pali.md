# BU-PC 36

## 6. Dutiyapavāraṇāsikkhāpadaṃ

## 6.The Second Training Rule Concerning Invitation.

242. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena dve bhikkhū kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ addhānamaggappaṭipannā honti. Eko bhikkhu anācāraṃ ācarati. Dutiyo bhikkhu taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘māvuso, evarūpamakāsi, netaṃ kappatī’’ti. So tasmiṃ upanandhi. Atha kho te bhikkhū sāvatthiṃ agamaṃsu. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ aññatarassa pūgassa saṅghabhattaṃ hoti. Dutiyo bhikkhu bhuttāvī pavārito hoti. Upanaddho [upanandho (ka.)] bhikkhu ñātikulaṃ gantvā piṇḍapātaṃ ādāya yena so bhikkhu tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘bhuñjāhi, āvuso’’ti. ‘‘Alaṃ, āvuso, paripuṇṇomhī’’ti. ‘‘Sundaro, āvuso, piṇḍapāto, bhuñjāhī’’ti. Atha kho so bhikkhu tena bhikkhunā nippīḷiyamāno taṃ piṇḍapātaṃ bhuñji. Upanaddho bhikkhu taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tvampi [tvaṃ hi (syā.)] nāma, āvuso, maṃ vattabbaṃ maññasi yaṃ tvaṃ bhuttāvī pavārito anatirittaṃ bhojanaṃ bhuñjasī’’ti. ‘‘Nanu, āvuso, ācikkhitabba’’nti. ‘‘Nanu, āvuso, pucchitabba’’nti.

242.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time, two bhikkhus were traveling along the road in the Kosalan country toward Savatthi. One bhikkhu behaved improperly. The second bhikkhu said to him, “Friend, do not do such a thing; it is not proper.” The first bhikkhu resented this. Then those bhikkhus arrived in Savatthi. At that time, a certain group in Savatthi was offering a meal to the Saṅgha. The second bhikkhu had finished eating and had declined further food. The resentful bhikkhu went to his relatives’ home, took almsfood, and approached that bhikkhu. Having approached, he said to him, “Eat, friend.” “Enough, friend, I am full.” “The almsfood is delicious, friend, eat.” Then, being pressured by that bhikkhu, he ate the almsfood. The resentful bhikkhu said to him, “You, friend, who think you can admonish me, yet you yourself eat food that is not extra, having eaten and declined!” “Shouldn’t you have told me, friend?” “Shouldn’t you have asked, friend?”

Atha kho so bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhu bhikkhuṃ bhuttāviṃ pavāritaṃ anatirittena bhojanena abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāressatī’’ ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhikkhuṃ bhuttāviṃ pavāritaṃ anatirittena bhojanena abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāresīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, bhikkhuṃ bhuttāviṃ pavāritaṃ anatirittena bhojanena abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāressasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana bhikkhave imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then that bhikkhu informed the bhikkhus about this matter. Those bhikkhus who were modest… grumbled, criticized, and condemned him, saying: “How could a bhikkhu invite another bhikkhu who has finished eating and declined, to partake of food that is not extra?”… “Is it true, bhikkhu, that you invited a bhikkhu who had finished eating and declined, to partake of food that is not extra?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha rebuked him… “How could you, foolish man, invite a bhikkhu who has finished eating and declined, to partake of food that is not extra! This, foolish man, is not for the benefit of those who are not yet convinced… Thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be announced:

243. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuṃ bhuttāviṃ pavāritaṃ anatirittena khādanīyena vā bhojanīyena vā abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreyya – ‘handa, bhikkhu, khāda vā bhuñja vā’ti, jānaṃ āsādanāpekkho, bhuttasmiṃ, pācittiya’’nti.

243.“If a bhikkhu, knowing that another bhikkhu has finished eating and has declined further food, invites him to consume hard food or soft food that is not extra, saying, ‘Come, bhikkhu, eat or consume this,’ intending to cause annoyance, and he eats, it is an offense entailing confession.”

244. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

244.By ‘whatever bhikkhu’ is meant: whatever bhikkhu… By ‘bhikkhu’ is meant: the bhikkhu intended in this context.

Bhikkhunti aññaṃ bhikkhuṃ.

By ‘bhikkhu’ (accusative) is meant another bhikkhu.

Bhuttāvī nāma pañcannaṃ bhojanānaṃ aññataraṃ bhojanaṃ, antamaso kusaggenapi bhuttaṃ hoti.

By ‘one who has eaten’ is meant one who has partaken of any one of the five kinds of food, even if it is as little as the tip of a blade of kusa grass.

Pavārito nāma asanaṃ paññāyati, bhojanaṃ paññāyati, hatthapāse ṭhito abhiharati, paṭikkhepo paññāyati.

By ‘one who has declined further food’ is meant: seating is evident, food is evident, someone standing within arm’s reach offers it, and a refusal is evident.

Anatirittaṃ nāma akappiyakataṃ hoti, appaṭiggahitakataṃ hoti, anuccāritakataṃ hoti, ahatthapāse kataṃ hoti, abhuttāvinā kataṃ hoti, bhuttāvinā pavāritena āsanā vuṭṭhitena kataṃ hoti, ‘‘alametaṃ sabba’’nti avuttaṃ hoti, na gilānātirittaṃ hoti – etaṃ anatirittaṃ nāma.

By ‘not extra’ is meant: it has not been made allowable, it has not been accepted, it has not been announced, it has not been placed within arm’s reach, it has been made allowable by one who has not eaten, it has been made allowable by one who has eaten, declined, and risen from his seat, ‘Enough of this’ has not been said, and it is not the remaining food permitted to a sick person.

Khādanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ ṭhapetvā avasesaṃ khādanīyaṃ nāma.

By ‘hard food’ is meant: any food other than the five staple foods, one-night food, seven-day food, and lifetime food.

Bhojanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṃsaṃ.

By ‘soft food’ is meant: the five kinds of food—cooked rice, gruel, barley meal, fish, and meat.

Abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreyyāti yāvatakaṃ icchasi tāvatakaṃ gaṇhāhīti.

By ‘invites him to consume’ is meant: he invites him, saying, ‘Take as much as you wish.’

Jānāti nāma sāmaṃ vā jānāti, aññe vā tassa ārocenti, so vā āroceti.

By ‘knowing’ is meant: he knows himself, or others inform him, or he informs (others).

Āsādanāpekkhoti ‘‘iminā imaṃ codessāmi sāressāmi paṭicodessāmi paṭisāressāmi maṅku karissāmī’’ti abhiharati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Tassa vacanena ‘‘khādissāmi bhuñjissāmī’’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti dukkaṭassa. Bhojanapariyosāne āpatti pācittiyassa.

By ‘intending to cause annoyance’ is meant: he offers it thinking, ‘With this, I will accuse him, remind him, counter-accuse him, counter-remind him, or humiliate him’—there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, at his word, one accepts it thinking, ‘I will eat, I will consume’—there is an offense of wrong conduct. For each mouthful, there is an offense of wrong conduct. At the conclusion of the meal, there is an offense entailing confession.

245. Pavārite pavāritasaññī anatirittena khādanīyena vā bhojanīyena vā abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Pavārite vematiko anatirittena khādanīyena vā bhojanīyena vā abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Pavārite appavāritasaññī anatirittena khādanīyena vā bhojanīyena vā abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreti, anāpatti. Yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ āhāratthāya abhiharati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Tassa vacanena ‘‘khādissāmi bhuñjissāmī’’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti dukkaṭassa. Appavārite pavāritasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Appavārite vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Appavārite appavāritasaññī, anāpatti.

245.If, when the other bhikkhu has declined further food, one perceives him as having declined further food, and invites him to consume hard food or soft food that is not extra, there is an offense entailing confession. If, when the other bhikkhu has declined further food, one is uncertain, and invites him to consume hard food or soft food that is not extra, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, when the other bhikkhu has declined further food, one perceives him as not having declined further food, and invites him to consume hard food or soft food that is not extra, there is no offense. If one brings one-night food, seven-day food, or lifetime food for the purpose of eating, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, at his word, one accepts it thinking, ‘I will eat, I will consume,’ there is an offense of wrong conduct. For each mouthful, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, when the other bhikkhu has not declined further food, one perceives him as having declined further food, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, when the other bhikkhu has not declined further food, one is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, when the other bhikkhu has not declined further food, one perceives him as not having declined further food, there is no offense.

246. Anāpatti atirittaṃ kārāpetvā deti, ‘‘atirittaṃ kārāpetvā bhuñjāhī’’ti deti, aññassatthāya haranto gacchāhīti deti, gilānassa sesakaṃ deti, ‘‘yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ sati paccaye paribhuñjā’’ti deti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

246.There is no offense: if one has something made extra and gives it; if one gives something saying, ‘Have it made extra and then consume it’; if one gives something saying, ‘Go, carrying this for another’s sake’; if one gives leftovers to the sick; if one gives something saying, ‘Consume this one-night food, seven-day food, or lifetime food when there is a condition’; in the case of one who is insane; or in the case of the first offender.

Dutiyapavāraṇāsikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The second training rule concerning invitation, the sixth, is finished.
