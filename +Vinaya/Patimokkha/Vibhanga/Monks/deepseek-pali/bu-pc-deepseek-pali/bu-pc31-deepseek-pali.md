# BU-PC 31

## 1. Āvasathapiṇḍasikkhāpadaṃ

## 1.The Training Rule Concerning Almsfood at a Lodging

203. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyā avidūre aññatarassa pūgassa āvasathapiṇḍo paññatto hoti. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiṃ piṇḍāya pavisitvā piṇḍaṃ alabhamānā āvasathaṃ agamaṃsu. Manussā – ‘‘cirassampi bhadantā āgatā’’ti te sakkaccaṃ parivisiṃsu. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū dutiyampi divasaṃ…pe… tatiyampi divasaṃ pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiṃ piṇḍāya pavisitvā piṇḍaṃ alabhamānā āvasathaṃ gantvā bhuñjiṃsu. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘kiṃ mayaṃ karissāma ārāmaṃ gantvā! Hiyyopi idheva āgantabbaṃ bhavissatī’’ti, tattheva anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjanti. Titthiyā apasakkanti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjissanti! Nayimesaññeva āvasathapiṇḍo paññatto; sabbesaññeva āvasathapiṇḍo paññatto’’ti.

203.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time, not far from Sāvatthī, a lodging meal had been designated for a certain group. The group-of-six bhikkhus, having dressed in the forenoon and taken their bowls and robes, entered Sāvatthī for almsfood. Not obtaining any almsfood, they went to the lodging. The people said, “Venerables, you have come after a long time,” and they served them respectfully. Then the group-of-six bhikkhus went again the next day… and again the third day, having dressed in the forenoon and taken their bowls and robes, entered Sāvatthī for almsfood. Not obtaining any almsfood, they went to the lodging and ate. Then the group-of-six bhikkhus thought, “What shall we do by going to the monastery? Tomorrow too we will have to come back here.” So they stayed there, remaining there again and again, eating the lodging meal. The sectarians departed. The people complained, grumbled, and criticized, “How can these Sakyan ascetics stay there again and again and eat the lodging meal? This lodging meal was not designated just for them; it was designated for everyone!”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjissantīti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjathāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

The bhikkhus heard those people complaining, grumbling, and criticizing. Those bhikkhus of few desires… complained, grumbled, and criticized, “How can the group-of-six bhikkhus stay there again and again and eat the lodging meal?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you stay there again and again and eat the lodging meal?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “Foolish persons, how can you stay there again and again and eat the lodging meal? This, foolish persons, is not for the confidence of those without confidence… And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

‘‘Eko āvasathapiṇḍo bhuñjitabbo. Tato ce uttariṃ bhuñjeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

‘One lodging meal should be eaten. If he eats more than that, it is an offense entailing expiation.’

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus this training rule was laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

204. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā sāriputto kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ gacchanto yena aññataro āvasatho tenupasaṅkami. Manussā – ‘‘cirassampi thero āgato’’ti sakkaccaṃ parivisiṃsu. Atha kho āyasmato sāriputtassa bhuttāvissa kharo ābādho uppajji, nāsakkhi tamhā āvasathā pakkamituṃ. Atha kho te manussā dutiyampi divasaṃ āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘bhuñjatha, bhante’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjitu’’nti kukkuccāyanto na paṭiggahesi; chinnabhatto ahosi. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānena bhikkhunā anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

204.At that time, the Venerable Sāriputta, while traveling through the Kosala country on his way to Sāvatthī, arrived at a certain lodging. The people said, “The elder has come after a long time,” and they served him respectfully. Then, after the Venerable Sāriputta had eaten, a severe illness arose, and he was unable to depart from that lodging. The next day, those people again said this to the Venerable Sāriputta: “Eat, Venerable Sir.” But the Venerable Sāriputta, feeling remorse, did not accept, thinking, “The Blessed One has prohibited eating almsfood from a lodging again and again.” He was without food. Then the Venerable Sāriputta, having gone to Sāvatthī, informed the bhikkhus about this matter. The bhikkhus reported this matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on account of this incident, on account of this matter, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, I allow a sick bhikkhu to eat almsfood from a lodging again and again. And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

205. ‘‘Agilānena bhikkhunā eko āvasathapiṇḍo bhuñjitabbo. Tato ce uttari bhuñjeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

205.“A bhikkhu who is not sick should eat one lodging meal. If he eats more than that, it is an offense entailing expiation.”

206. Agilāno nāma sakkoti tamhā āvasathā pakkamituṃ.

206.One who is not sick is able to depart from that lodging.

Gilāno nāma na sakkoti tamhā āvasathā pakkamituṃ.

One who is sick is unable to depart from that lodging.

Āvasathapiṇḍo nāma pañcannaṃ bhojanānaṃ aññataraṃ bhojanaṃ – sālāya vā maṇḍape vā rukkhamūle vā ajjhokāse vā anodissa yāvadattho paññatto hoti. Agilānena bhikkhunā sakiṃ bhuñjitabbo. Tato ce uttari ‘bhuñjissāmī’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pācittiyassa.

A lodging meal is any one of the five kinds of food—designated without specifying [for whom], as much as one needs, whether in a hall, a pavilion, at the foot of a tree, or in the open air. A bhikkhu who is not sick should eat it once. If he accepts more with the intention, “I will eat [it],” there is an offense of wrong-doing. For each mouthful [swallowed], there is an offense entailing expiation.

207. Agilāno agilānasaññī tatuttari āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Agilāno vematiko tatuttari āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Agilāno gilānasaññī tatuttariṃ āvasathapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

207.A bhikkhu who is not sick, perceiving himself as not sick, who eats further lodging meal, commits an offense entailing expiation. A bhikkhu who is not sick, being doubtful, who eats further lodging meal, commits an offense entailing expiation. A bhikkhu who is not sick, perceiving himself as sick, who eats further lodging meal, commits an offense entailing expiation.

Gilāno agilānasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Gilāno vematiko āpatti dukkaṭassa. Gilāno gilānasaññī, anāpatti.

One who is sick, perceiving himself as not sick, there is an offense of wrong-doing. One who is sick, being doubtful, there is an offense of wrong-doing. One who is sick, perceiving himself as sick, there is no offense.

208. Anāpatti gilānassa, agilāno sakiṃ bhuñjati, gacchanto, vā āgacchanto vā bhuñjati, sāmikā nimantetvā bhojenti, odissa paññatto hoti, na yāvadattho paññatto hoti, pañca bhojanāni ṭhapetvā sabbattha anāpatti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

208.There is no offense for one who is sick; for one who is not sick and eats once; for one who eats while going or coming; when the owners invite and offer food; when it is designated for a specific person or group; when it is not designated as much as one needs; in all instances except for the five foods; for one who is insane; for one who is the first wrong-doer.

Āvasathapiṇḍasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The first training rule, concerning almsfood at a lodging, is finished.
