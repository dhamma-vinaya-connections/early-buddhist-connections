# BU-PC 5

## 5. Sahaseyyasikkhāpadaṃ

## 5.The Training Rule on Joint Sleeping.

49. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā āḷaviyaṃ viharati aggāḷave cetiye. Tena kho pana samayena upāsakā ārāmaṃ āgacchanti dhammassavanāya. Dhamme bhāsite therā bhikkhū yathāvihāraṃ gacchanti. Navakā bhikkhū tattheva upaṭṭhānasālāyaṃ upāsakehi saddhiṃ muṭṭhassatī, asampajānā, naggā, vikūjamānā, kākacchamānā seyyaṃ kappenti. Upāsakā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhadantā muṭṭhassatī asampajānā naggā vikūjamānā kākacchamānā seyyaṃ kappessantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ upāsakānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū anupasampannena sahaseyyaṃ kappessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū te navake bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū anupasampannena sahaseyyaṃ kappentī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā anupasampannena sahaseyyaṃ kappessanti! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

49.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Āḷavī, at the Aggāḷava Shrine. At that time, lay followers came to the monastery to listen to the Dhamma. After the Dhamma discourse, the elder bhikkhus went to their respective residences. The newly ordained bhikkhus, however, remained in the assembly hall; together with the lay followers, they prepared sleeping places heedlessly, unmindfully, naked, babbling, and making nonsensical nasal sounds. The lay followers grumbled, found fault, and exclaimed: ‘How can these venerable ones prepare sleeping places heedlessly, unmindfully, naked, babbling, and making nonsensical nasal sounds!’ The bhikkhus heard the grumbling, finding fault, and exclamations of those lay followers. Those bhikkhus who were content with little, etc., grumbled, found fault, and exclaimed: ‘How can these bhikkhus engage in joint sleeping with one who is not fully ordained!’ Then, those bhikkhus, after reprimanding the new bhikkhus in various ways, reported this matter to the Blessed One… ‘Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus engage in joint sleeping with one who is not fully ordained?’ ‘It is true, Blessed One.’ The Blessed One rebuked them: ‘How, bhikkhus, can these worthless fellows engage in joint sleeping with one who is not fully ordained! This is not conducive to the confidence of those who lack confidence, nor to the increase of confidence in the confident… And then, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule thus:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu anupasampannena sahaseyyaṃ kappeyya pācittiya’’nti.

“Whatever bhikkhu engages in joint sleeping with one not fully ordained, it is a Pācittiya.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

50. Atha kho bhagavā āḷaviyaṃ yathābhirantaṃ viharitvā yena kosambī tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena kosambī tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati badarikārāme. Bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ rāhulaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘bhagavatā, āvuso rāhula, sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ – ‘na anupasampannena sahaseyyā kappetabbā’ti. Seyyaṃ, āvuso rāhula, jānāhī’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā rāhulo seyyaṃ alabhamāno vaccakuṭiyā seyyaṃ kappesi. Atha kho bhagavā rattiyā paccūsasamayaṃ paccuṭṭhāya yena vaccakuṭi tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ukkāsi. Āyasmāpi rāhulo ukkāsi. ‘‘Ko etthā’’ti? ‘‘Ahaṃ, bhagavā, rāhulo’’ti. ‘‘Kissa tvaṃ, rāhula, idha nisinnosī’’ti? Atha kho āyasmā rāhulo bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, anupasampannena dirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ kappetuṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

50.Then the Blessed One, having stayed at Āḷavī as long as he pleased, set out on a journey to Kosambī. Traveling by stages, he arrived at Kosambī. There the Blessed One stayed at the Badarikārāma. The bhikkhus said to Venerable Rāhula: ‘Venerable Rāhula, the Blessed One has laid down a training rule: “One should not engage in joint sleeping with one who is not fully ordained.” Venerable Rāhula, you must find your own sleeping place.’ Unable to find a sleeping place, Venerable Rāhula prepared one in the privy. Then the Blessed One, rising in the latter part of the night, went to the privy. Approaching it, he coughed. Venerable Rāhula too coughed. ‘Who is there?’ ‘It is I, Blessed One, Rāhula.’ ‘Why are you sitting here, Rāhula?’ Then Venerable Rāhula reported the matter to the Blessed One. The Blessed One, on this occasion and regarding this incident, having delivered a discourse, addressed the bhikkhus: ‘bhikkhus, I permit [one] not fully ordained to engage in joint sleeping for two or three nights. And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule thus:

51. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu anupasampannena uttaridirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ kappeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

51.“Whatever bhikkhu engages in joint sleeping with one not fully ordained for more than two or three nights, it is a Pācittiya.”

52. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

52.“Yo pana” means “whatever kind of person”… “Bhikkhu” means… a bhikkhu intended in this context.

Anupasampanno nāma bhikkhuṃ ṭhapetvā avaseso anupasampanno nāma.

“Not fully ordained” means anyone except a bhikkhu.

Uttaridirattatirattanti atirekadirattatirattaṃ.

“Exceeding two or three nights” means more than two or three nights.

Sahāti ekato.

“Sharing” means together.

Seyyā nāma sabbacchannā, sabbaparicchannā, yebhuyyenacchannā, yebhuyyena paricchannā.

A bed is called fully roofed over, fully enclosed, mostly roofed over, mostly enclosed.

Seyyaṃ kappeyyāti catutthe divase atthaṅgate sūriye, anupasampanne nipanne, bhikkhu nipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Bhikkhu nipanne, anupasampanno nipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Ubho vā nipajjanti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Uṭṭhahitvā punappunaṃ nipajjanti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

If a bhikkhu engages in joint sleeping when the sun has set on the fourth day, and an unordained person is lying down, and the bhikkhu lies down, it is an offense entailing confession. If the bhikkhu is lying down and an unordained person lies down, it is an offense entailing confession. If both lie down, it is an offense entailing confession. If they get up and lie down again and again, it is an offense entailing confession.

53. Anupasampanne anupasampannasaññī uttaridirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Anupasampanne vematiko uttaridirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Anupasampanne upasampannasaññī uttaridirattatirattaṃ sahaseyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

53.If, perceiving an unordained person as unordained, one engages in joint sleeping with them for more than two or three nights, it is an offense entailing confession. If one is doubtful about whether the person is ordained or not and engages in joint sleeping with them for more than two or three nights, it is an offense entailing confession. If, perceiving an unordained person as ordained, one engages in joint sleeping with them for more than two or three nights, it is an offense entailing confession.

Upaḍḍhacchanne upaḍḍhaparicchanne, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Upasampanne anupasampannasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Upasampanne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Upasampanne upasampannasaññī, anāpatti.

If the place is half-covered or half-enclosed, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, being ordained, one perceives an unordained person as ordained, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, being ordained, one is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, being ordained, one perceives an ordained person as ordained, there is no offense.

54. Anāpatti dvetisso rattiyo vasati, ūnakadvetisso rattiyo vasati, dve rattiyo vasitvā tatiyāya rattiyā purāruṇā nikkhamitvā puna vasati, sabbacchanne, sabbaaparicchanne, sabbaparicchanne sabbaacchanne [vasati, sabbaacchanne sabbaaparicchanne, (sī.)], yebhuyyena acchanne, yebhuyyena aparicchanne, anupasampanne nipanne bhikkhu nisīdati, bhikkhu nipanne anupasampanno nisīdati, ubho vā nisīdanti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

54.There is no offense if one stays for two or three nights; if one stays for less than two or three nights; if, having stayed for two nights, one leaves before dawn on the third night and then stays again; if the dwelling is fully unroofed; if it is fully unenclosed; if it is fully enclosed and fully unroofed; if it is mostly unroofed; if it is mostly unenclosed; if an unordained person is lying down and a bhikkhu sits; if a bhikkhu is lying down and an unordained person sits; if both are sitting; if one is insane; if one is an initial offender.

Sahaseyyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The fifth training rule on joint sleeping is finished.
