# BU-PC 27

## 7. Saṃvidhānasikkhāpadaṃ

## 7.The training rule concerning arrangements.

180. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhunīhi saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjanti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘yatheva mayaṃ sapajāpatikā āhiṇḍāma, evamevime samaṇā sakyaputtiyā bhikkhunīhi saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya āhiṇḍantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhunīhi saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjissantī’’ti…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, bhikkhunīhi saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjathā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, bhikkhunīhi saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha.

180.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery. At that time the bhikkhus of the group of six were, having made an arrangement, traveling together with nuns on the same road. People complained and criticized, “Just as we wander about with our wives, so too these Sakyan ascetics, having made an arrangement, wander about together with nuns!” The bhikkhus heard the complaints of those people who were complaining, criticizing, and disapproving. Those bhikkhus of few desires … complained and criticized, “How can the bhikkhus of the group of six, having made an arrangement, travel together with nuns on the same road?” … “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you, having made an arrangement, are traveling together with nuns on the same road?” “It’s true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them … “Foolish men, how can you, having made an arrangement, travel together with nuns on the same road! This is not for the confidence of those who are not (yet) confident …” … “And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjeyya, antamaso gāmantarampi, pācittiya’’nti.

“If a bhikkhu should undertake to travel on the same road together with a nun, having made an arrangement with her, even as far as the next village, it is an offense entailing expiation.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

181. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū ca bhikkhuniyo ca sāketā sāvatthiṃ addhānamaggappaṭipannā honti. Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo te bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mayampi ayyehi saddhiṃ gamissāmā’’ti. ‘‘Na, bhaginī, kappati bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjituṃ. Tumhe vā paṭhamaṃ gacchatha mayaṃ vā gamissāmā’’ti. ‘‘Ayyā, bhante, aggapurisā. Ayyāva paṭhamaṃ gacchantū’’ti. Atha kho tāsaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ pacchā gacchantīnaṃ antarāmagge corā acchindiṃsu ca dūsesuñca. Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhunīnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Bhikkhuniyo bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, satthagamanīye magge sāsaṅkasammate sappaṭibhaye bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

181.At that time, several bhikkhus and nuns were traveling on the road from Sāketa to Sāvatthī. Then those nuns said to the bhikkhus, “Venerable sirs, we too will go together with you.” “Sisters, it is not allowable to travel together with a nun, having made an arrangement, on the same road. Either you go first, or we will go.” “Venerable sirs, the venerable ones are the foremost. Let the venerable ones go first.” Then, as those nuns were traveling behind, bandits on the road robbed and violated them. Afterwards, those nuns, having arrived at Sāvatthī, informed the nuns of the matter. The nuns informed the bhikkhus of the matter. The bhikkhus informed the Blessed One of the matter. Then the Blessed One, in connection with this incident, on this occasion, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, I allow to travel together with a nun, having made an arrangement, on a road suitable for caravans, considered dangerous and fearful. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

182. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjeyya, antamaso gāmantarampi, aññatra samayā, pācittiyaṃ. Tatthāyaṃ samayo . Satthagamanīyo hoti maggo sāsaṅkasammato sappaṭibhayo – ayaṃ tattha samayo’’ti.

182.“If a bhikkhu should undertake to travel along the same road with a nun, even as far as to another village, except at an allowable occasion, it is an offense entailing expiation. Herein, this is an allowable occasion: when the road is suitable for a caravan, acknowledged as dangerous and frightening—this is an allowable occasion in that case.”

183. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

183.“Whoever” means whoever… etc…. “bhikkhu” means… etc…. In this connection, “bhikkhu” means the bhikkhu intended in this case.

Bhikkhunī nāma ubhatosaṅghe upasampannā.

A bhikkhunī is one who has received the full ordination in both assemblies.

Saddhinti ekato.

“Together with” means in one group.

Saṃvidhāyāti – ‘‘gacchāma, bhagini, gacchāmāyya; gacchāmāyya, gacchāma, bhagini; ajja vā hiyyo vā pare vā gacchāmā’’ti saṃvidahati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

“Having made an arrangement,” saying: ‘Let us go, sister, let us go, sir; let us go, sir, let us go, sister; whether today or tomorrow or the next day, let us go’—one makes an arrangement: there is an offense entailing wrong-doing.

Antamaso gāmantarampīti kukkuṭasampāte gāme, gāmantare gāmantare āpatti pācittiyassa. Agāmake araññe, addhayojane addhayojane āpatti pācittiyassa.

“Even as far as the next village” means: even as far as the next village, which is a village within a chicken’s flight; for each subsequent village, there is an offense entailing expiation. In an uninhabited area, a wilderness, for every half-league there is an offense entailing expiation.

Aññatra samayāti ṭhapetvā samayaṃ.

“Except at an allowable occasion” means excluding the occasion.

Satthagamanīyo nāma maggo na sakkā hoti vinā satthena gantuṃ.

A road suitable for caravans is one that cannot be traveled without a caravan.

Sāsaṅkaṃ nāma tasmiṃ magge [yasmiṃ magge (?)] corānaṃ niviṭṭhokāso dissati, bhuttokāso dissati, ṭhitokāso dissati, nisinnokāso dissati, nipannokāso dissati.

A place “causing apprehension” is one where on that road places where robbers have settled are seen, places where they have eaten are seen, places where they have stood are seen, places where they have sat are seen, places where they have lain down are seen.

Sappaṭibhayaṃ nāma tasmiṃ magge corehi manussā hatā dissanti, viluttā dissanti, ākoṭitā dissanti, sappaṭibhayaṃ gantvā appaṭibhayaṃ dassetvā uyyojetabbā – ‘‘gacchatha bhaginiyo’’ti.

A place “frightening” is one where on that road people are seen killed by robbers, seen robbed, and seen beaten. Having gone through the dangerous section and shown the safe section, they should be sent off, saying, “Go, sisters.”

184. Saṃvidahite saṃvidahitasaññī ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, aññatra samayā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṃvidahite vematiko ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, aññatra samayā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṃvidahite, asaṃvidahitasaññī ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, aññatra samayā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

184.If, when it is arranged, one perceives it as arranged and travels together on the same road, even as far as the next village, except at the proper time, there is an offense entailing expiation. If, when it is arranged, one is in doubt and travels together on the same road, even as far as the next village, except at the proper time, there is an offense entailing expiation. If, when it is arranged, one perceives it as not arranged and travels together on the same road, even as far as the next village, except at the proper time, there is an offense entailing expiation.

Bhikkhu saṃvidahati bhikkhunī na saṃvidahati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Asaṃvidahite saṃvidahitasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Asaṃvidahite vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Asaṃvidahite asaṃvidahitasaññī, anāpatti.

If a bhikkhu makes an arrangement but a nun does not, there is an offense entailing wrong-doing. If, when it is not arranged, one perceives it as arranged, there is an offense entailing wrong-doing. If, when it is not arranged, one is in doubt, there is an offense entailing wrong-doing. If, when it is not arranged, one perceives it as not arranged, there is no offense.

185. Anāpatti samaye, asaṃvidahitvā gacchati, bhikkhunī saṃvidahati, bhikkhu na saṃvidahati, visaṅketena gacchanti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

185.There is no offense in these instances: at the allowable occasion; going without arranging beforehand; when the nun makes an arrangement but the bhikkhu does not; going by changing the time of the arrangement; in times of danger; for one who is insane; or for the first offender.

Saṃvidhānasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The seventh training rule on arrangements is finished.
