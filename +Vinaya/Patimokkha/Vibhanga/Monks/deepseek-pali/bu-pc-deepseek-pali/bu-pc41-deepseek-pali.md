# BU-PC 41

## 1. Acelakasikkhāpadaṃ

## 1.The Training Rule on Naked Ascetics

269. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena saṅghassa khādanīyaṃ ussannaṃ hoti. Atha kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Tenahānanda, vighāsādānaṃ pūvaṃ dehī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissuṇitvā vighāsāde paṭipāṭiyā nisīdāpetvā ekekaṃ pūvaṃ dento aññatarissā paribbājikāya ekaṃ maññamāno dve pūve adāsi. Sāmantā paribbājikāyo taṃ paribbājikaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘jāro te eso samaṇo’’ti. ‘‘Na me so samaṇo jāro, ekaṃ maññamāno dve pūve adāsī’’ti. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho āyasmā ānando ekekaṃ pūvaṃ dento tassāyeva paribbājikāya ekaṃ maññamāno dve pūve adāsi. Sāmantā paribbājikāyo taṃ paribbājikaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘jāro te eso samaṇo’’ti. ‘‘Na me so samaṇo jāro, ekaṃ maññamāno dve pūve adāsī’’ti. ‘‘Jāro na jāro’’ti bhaṇḍiṃsu. Aññataropi ājīvako parivesanaṃ agamāsi. Aññataro bhikkhu pahūtena sappinā odanaṃ madditvā tassa ājīvakassa mahantaṃ piṇḍaṃ adāsi. Atha kho so ājīvako taṃ piṇḍaṃ ādāya agamāsi. Aññataro ājīvako taṃ ājīvakaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kuto tayā, āvuso, piṇḍo laddho’’ti? ‘‘Tassāvuso, samaṇassa gotamassa muṇḍagahapatikassa parivesanāya laddho’’ti.

269.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Vesālī in the Great Wood, in the Hall with the Peaked Roof. Now at that time the Saṅgha had an abundance of food. Then the Venerable Ānanda informed the Blessed One of this matter. [The Blessed One said:] “Well then, Ānanda, give cakes to those who eat scraps.” “Yes, Venerable Sir,” replied the Venerable Ānanda to the Blessed One. Having assented, he seated those who eat scraps in a row and, while giving one cake to each, mistakenly gave two cakes to a certain female wanderer. The neighboring female wanderers said to that female wanderer, “This bhikkhu is your lover.” “This bhikkhu is not my lover; he gave two cakes thinking it was one,” she replied. A second time… a third time the Venerable Ānanda, while giving one cake to each, mistakenly gave two cakes to that same female wanderer. The neighboring female wanderers said to that female wanderer, “This bhikkhu is your lover.” “This bhikkhu is not my lover; he gave two cakes thinking it was one,” she replied. They quarreled, saying, “Lover or not lover.” Meanwhile, a certain Ājīvaka went to the distribution. A certain bhikkhu, having mixed rice with abundant ghee, gave a large lump to that Ājīvaka. Then that Ājīvaka took the lump and departed. Another Ājīvaka asked him, “Friend, where did you get this lump?” “Friend, I got it at the distribution of that shaven householder, the ascetic Gotama,” he replied.

Assosuṃ kho upāsakā tesaṃ ājīvakānaṃ imaṃ kathāsallāpaṃ. Atha kho te upāsakā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te upāsakā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘ime, bhante, titthiyā avaṇṇakāmā buddhassa avaṇṇakāmā dhammassa avaṇṇakāmā saṅghassa. Sādhu, bhante, ayyā titthiyānaṃ sahatthā na dadeyyu’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā te upāsake dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho te upāsakā bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkamiṃsu. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññapessāmi dasa atthavase paṭicca – saṅghasuṭṭhutāya, saṅghaphāsutāya …pe… saddhammaṭṭhitiyā, vinayānuggahāya. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

The lay followers heard this conversation among those Ājīvakas. Then those lay followers approached the Blessed One; having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, those lay followers said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, these sectarians are intent on disparaging the Buddha, intent on disparaging the Dhamma, intent on disparaging the Saṅgha. It would be good, venerable sir, if the venerables would not give with their own hands to the sectarians.” Then the Blessed One instructed, inspired, and gladdened those lay followers with a Dhamma talk. Having been instructed, inspired, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, those lay followers rose from their seats, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and departed. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in this matter, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “Well then, bhikkhus, I will lay down a training rule for the bhikkhus for ten reasons: for the well-being of the Saṅgha, for the comfort of the Saṅgha … for the endurance of the true Dhamma, for supporting the discipline. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited—

270. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu acelakassa vā paribbājakassa vā paribbājikāya vā sahatthā khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā dadeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

270.“If any bhikkhu gives, with his own hand, hard food or soft food to a naked ascetic, a male wanderer, or a female wanderer, it is an offense entailing confession.”

271. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

271.“Any” (yo pana) means whoever… “Bhikkhu” means… The bhikkhu intended in this case is this one.

Acelako nāma yo koci paribbājakasamāpanno naggo.

A naked ascetic (acelaka) is anyone who has undertaken the wanderer’s life and is naked.

Paribbājako nāma bhikkhuñca sāmaṇerañca ṭhapetvā yo koci paribbājakasamāpanno.

A male wanderer (paribbājaka) is anyone who has undertaken the wanderer’s life, excluding a bhikkhu and a novice.

Paribbājikā nāma bhikkhuniñca sikkhamānañca sāmaṇeriñca ṭhapetvā yā kāci paribbājikasamāpannā.

A female wanderer (paribbājikā) is any woman who has undertaken the wanderer’s life, excluding a bhikkhunī, a female trainee, and a female novice.

Khādanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – udakadantaponaṃ ṭhapetvā avasesaṃ khādanīyaṃ nāma.

Hard food (khādanīya) is everything chewable, excluding water and tooth-cleaners.

Bhojanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṃsaṃ.

Soft food (bhojanīya) refers to the five kinds of food: cooked rice, gruel, barley flour, fish, and meat.

Dadeyyāti kāyena vā kāyapaṭibaddhena vā nissaggiyena vā deti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he gives with his body, or with something connected to his body, or with something to be relinquished, it is an offense entailing confession.

272. Titthiye titthiyasaññī sahatthā khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā deti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Titthiye vematiko sahatthā khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā deti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Titthiye atitthiyasaññī sahatthā khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā deti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

272.If one gives hard food or soft food with one’s own hand to a sectarian, perceiving them as a sectarian, it is an offense entailing confession. If one gives hard food or soft food with one’s own hand to a sectarian, being in doubt, it is an offense entailing confession. If one gives hard food or soft food with one’s own hand to a sectarian, perceiving them as a non-sectarian, it is an offense entailing confession.

Udakadantaponaṃ deti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Atitthiye titthiyasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Atitthiye vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Atitthiye atitthiyasaññī, anāpatti.

If one gives tooth-cleaning water, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If one gives to a non-sectarian, perceiving them as a sectarian, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If one gives to a non-sectarian, being in doubt, it is an offense of wrong-doing. If one gives to a non-sectarian, perceiving them as a non-sectarian, there is no offense.

273. Anāpatti dāpeti na deti, upanikkhipitvā deti, bāhirālepaṃ deti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

273.There is no offense for one who has another give but does not give oneself, who gives by depositing it, who gives an external application, for one who is insane, and for the one initially undertaking.

Acelakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The first training rule concerning naked ascetics is concluded.
