# BU-PC 8

## 8. Bhūtārocanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The training rule on revealing what is true.

67. [pārā. 193] Tena samayena buddho bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā sandiṭṭhā sambhattā bhikkhū vaggumudāya nadiyā tīre vassaṃ upagacchiṃsu. Tena kho pana samayena vajjī dubbhikkhā hoti – dvīhitikā setaṭṭhikā salākāvuttā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ. Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘etarahi kho vajjī dubbhikkhā – dvīhitikā setaṭṭhikā salākāvuttā, na sukarā uñchena paggahena yāpetuṃ. Kena nu kho mayaṃ upāyena samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vaseyyāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilameyyāmā’’ti? Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘handa mayaṃ, āvuso, gihīnaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhema. Evaṃ te amhākaṃ dātuṃ maññissanti. Evaṃ mayaṃ samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasissāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilamissāmā’’ti. Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘alaṃ, āvuso, kiṃ gihīnaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhitena? Handa mayaṃ, āvuso, gihīnaṃ dūteyyaṃ harāma. Evaṃ te amhākaṃ dātuṃ maññissanti. Evaṃ mayaṃ samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasissāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilamissāmā’’ti. Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘alaṃ, āvuso; kiṃ gihīnaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhitena! Kiṃ gihīnaṃ dūteyyaṃ haṭena! Handa mayaṃ, āvuso, gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissāma – ‘asuko bhikkhu paṭhamassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu dutiyassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu tatiyassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu catutthassa jhānassa lābhī, asuko bhikkhu sotāpanno, asuko bhikkhu sakadāgāmī, asuko bhikkhu anāgāmī, asuko bhikkhu arahā, asuko bhikkhu tevijjo, asuko bhikkhu chaḷabhiññoti. Evaṃ te amhākaṃ dātuṃ maññissanti. Evaṃ mayaṃ samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasissāma, na ca piṇḍakena kilamissāmā’’ti. Eso yeva kho, āvuso, seyyo, yo amhākaṃ gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇo bhāsito’’ti.

67.At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was dwelling at Vesālī in the Great Wood, in the Hall with the Peaked Roof. Now at that time several monks who were intimate friends and companions had entered the rains residence on the bank of the Vaggumudā River. At that time the Vajjians were experiencing a famine—food was scarce, white-grained rice was costly, and rations were distributed by lot; it was not easy to sustain oneself by gleaning. Then those monks thought: "Now the Vajjians are experiencing a famine—food is scarce, white-grained rice is costly, and rations were distributed by lot; it is not easy to sustain oneself by gleaning. By what means can we dwell in unity, harmony, and non-dispute, spending the rains comfortably without being exhausted by alms gathering?" Some said: "Come, friends, let us undertake work for householders. Then they will think to give to us. Thus we will dwell in unity, harmony, and non-dispute, spending the rains comfortably without being exhausted by alms gathering." Others said: "Enough, friends! What is the use of undertaking work for householders? Come, friends, let us carry messages for householders. Then they will think to give to us. Thus we will dwell in unity, harmony, and non-dispute, spending the rains comfortably without being exhausted by alms gathering." Still others said: "Enough, friends! What is the use of undertaking work for householders? What is the use of carrying messages for householders? Come, friends, let us praise each other's superhuman qualities to householders: 'This monk has attained the first jhāna, this monk the second jhāna, this monk the third jhāna, this monk the fourth jhāna. This monk is a stream-enterer, this monk a once-returner, this monk a non-returner, this monk an arahant. This monk has the threefold knowledge, this monk the sixfold direct knowledge.' Then they will think to give to us. Thus we will dwell in unity, harmony, and non-dispute, spending the rains comfortably without being exhausted by alms gathering." "Indeed, friends, this is better: that we praise each other's superhuman qualities to householders."

Atha kho te bhikkhū gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsiṃsu – ‘‘asuko bhikkhu paṭhamassa jhānassa lābhī…pe… asuko bhikkhu chaḷabhiñño’’ti. Atha kho te manussā – ‘‘lābhā vata no, suladdhaṃ vata no, yesaṃ no evarūpā bhikkhū vassaṃ upagatā, na vata no ito pubbe evarūpā bhikkhū vassaṃ upagatā, yathayime bhikkhū sīlavanto kalyāṇadhammā’’ti. Te na tādisāni bhojanāni attanā bhuñjanti, mātāpitūnaṃ denti puttadārassa denti dāsakammakaraporisassa denti mittāmaccānaṃ denti ñātisālohitānaṃ denti yādisāni bhikkhūnaṃ denti. Na tādisāni khādanīyāni sāyanīyāni pānāni attanā khādanti sāyanti pivanti [attanā pivanti (syā. ka.)] mātāpitūnaṃ denti puttadārassa denti dāsakammakaraporisassa denti mittāmaccānaṃ denti ñātisālohitānaṃ denti, yādisāni bhikkhūnaṃ denti. Atha kho te bhikkhū vaṇṇavā ahesuṃ pīṇindriyā pasannamukhavaṇṇā vippasannachavivaṇṇā.

Then those monks spoke in praise of each other's superhuman qualities to the householders: "This monk has attained the first jhāna... this monk possesses the sixfold direct knowledges." And those people thought: "We are indeed fortunate, truly blessed, that such monks have come to spend the rains retreat with us. Never before have monks like these come to spend the rains retreat with us—monks so virtuous, of such excellent qualities." They did not eat such foods themselves, nor did they give to their mothers and fathers, children and wives, servants and workers, friends and companions, or relatives and kin, the kind of foods they gave to the monks. They did not consume such delicacies, staple foods, or drinks themselves, nor did they give to their mothers and fathers, children and wives, servants and workers, friends and companions, or relatives and kin, the kind of delicacies, staple foods, or drinks they gave to the monks. Then those monks became radiant, with gratified senses, serene faces, and clear complexions.

68. Āciṇṇaṃ kho panetaṃ vassaṃvuṭṭhānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bhagavantaṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Atha kho te bhikkhū vassaṃvuṭṭhā temāsaccayena senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena vesālī tena pakkamiṃsu. Anupubbena yena vesālī mahāvanaṃ kūṭāgārasālā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Tena kho pana samayena disāsu vassaṃvuṭṭhā bhikkhū kisā honti lūkhā dubbaṇṇā uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajātā dhamanisanthatagattā. Vaggumudātīriyā pana bhikkhū vaṇṇavā honti pīṇindriyā pasannamukhavaṇṇā vippasannachavivaṇṇā. Āciṇṇaṃ kho panetaṃ buddhānaṃ bhagavantānaṃ āgantukehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ paṭisammodituṃ. Atha kho bhagavā vaggumudātīriye bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, bhikkhave, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyaṃ, kacci samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasittha, na ca piṇḍakena kilamitthā’’ti? ‘‘Khamanīyaṃ bhagavā, yāpanīyaṃ bhagavā. Samaggā ca mayaṃ, bhante, sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasimhā, na ca piṇḍakena kilamimhā’’ti. Jānantāpi tathāgatā pucchanti, jānantāpi na pucchanti. Kālaṃ viditvā pucchanti, kālaṃ viditvā na pucchanti. Atthasañhitaṃ tathāgatā pucchanti, no anatthasañhitaṃ. Anatthasañhite setughāto tathāgatānaṃ. Dvīhākārehi buddhā bhagavanto bhikkhū paṭipucchanti – dhammaṃ vā desessāma, sāvakānaṃ vā sikkhāpadaṃ paññapessāmāti.

68.It is customary for monks who have completed the rains retreat to visit the Blessed One. So those monks, having completed the rains retreat after the three-month period, packed up their lodgings, took their bowls and robes, and set out for Vesālī. Gradually, they arrived at the Great Wood in Vesālī, the hall with the peaked roof, and approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. At that time, monks who had completed the rains retreat from other regions were thin, haggard, pale, with veins protruding all over their bodies, and their skin was yellowish. But the monks from the banks of the Vaggumudā were radiant, with gratified senses, serene faces, and clear complexions. It is customary for the Buddhas, the Blessed Ones, to exchange greetings with visiting monks. Then the Blessed One addressed the monks from the banks of the Vaggumudā: "I hope, monks, you have been well, at ease, living in harmony, without dispute, spending the rains comfortably, and not suffering for alms?" "We have been well, Blessed One, at ease, living in harmony, without dispute, spending the rains comfortably, and not suffering for alms." Even though Tathāgatas know, they ask; even though they know, they do not ask. Knowing the right time, they ask; knowing the right time, they do not ask. Tathāgatas ask what is connected with the goal, not what is not connected with the goal. For Tathāgatas, there is a barrier to what is not connected with the goal. The Buddhas, the Blessed Ones, question the monks in two ways: either to teach the Dhamma or to lay down a training rule for the disciples.

Atha kho bhagavā vaggumudātīriye bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘yathā kathaṃ pana tumhe, bhikkhave, samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā phāsukaṃ vassaṃ vasittha, na ca piṇḍakena kilamitthā’’ti? Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Kacci pana vo, bhikkhave, bhūta’’nti? ‘‘Bhūtaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, bhikkhave, udarassa kāraṇā gihīnaṃ aññamaññassa uttarimanussadhammassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissatha! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One addressed the monks on the bank of the Vaggumudā River: "How, monks, did you spend the rains in concord, mutual appreciation, without contention, comfortably, and without difficulty in obtaining alms?" Then those monks related the matter to the Blessed One. "Is it true, monks?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked them... "How could you, monks, for the sake of your bellies, praise to householders each other's superhuman qualities? This will not lead to confidence in the unconfident..." ...And thus, monks, this training rule should be recited:

69. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu anupasampannassa uttarimanussadhammaṃ āroceyya bhūtasmiṃ, pācittiya’’nti.

69.Whatever monk should declare a superhuman quality to one not fully ordained, if it is factual, commits a pācittiya offense.

70. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

70.'Yo pana': whoever, of whatsoever kind...; 'bhikkhu': the monk intended in this context.

Anupasampanno nāma bhikkhuñca bhikkhuniñca ṭhapetvā, avaseso anupasampanno nāma.

'Not fully ordained' means anyone except a bhikkhu or a bhikkhuni; the rest are called 'not fully ordained.'

[pārā. 198] Uttarimanussadhammo nāma jhānaṃ, vimokkho, samādhi, samāpatti, ñāṇadassanaṃ, maggabhāvanā, phalasacchikiriyā, kilesappahānaṃ, vinīvaraṇatā cittassa, suññāgāre abhirati.

'Superhuman quality' means jhāna, liberation, samādhi, attainment, knowledge and vision, cultivation of the path, realization of the fruits, abandonment of defilements, freedom from the hindrances of the mind, delight in secluded dwellings.

[pārā. 199] Jhānanti paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ, dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ, tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ, catutthaṃ jhānaṃ.

'Jhāna' means the first jhāna, the second jhāna, the third jhāna, the fourth jhāna.

[pārā. 199] Vimokkhoti suññato vimokkho, animitto vimokkho, appaṇihito vimokkho.

'Liberation' means liberation through emptiness, liberation through signlessness, liberation through desirelessness.

[pārā. 199] Samādhīti suññato samādhi, animitto samādhi, appaṇihito samādhi.

'samādhi' means samādhi on emptiness, samādhi on the signless, samādhi on the desireless.

[pārā. 199] Samāpattīti suññatā samāpatti, animittā samāpatti, appaṇihitā samāpatti.

'Attainment' means the attainment of emptiness, the attainment of the signless, the attainment of the desireless.

[pārā. 199] Ñāṇadassananti tisso vijjā.

'Knowledge and vision' refers to the three knowledges.

[pārā. 199] Maggabhāvanāti cattāro satipaṭṭhānā, cattāro sammappadhānā, cattāro iddhipādā, pañcindriyāni, pañca balāni, satta bojjhaṅgā, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo.

'Development of the path' means the four establishments of mindfulness, the four right exertions, the four bases of success, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, the noble eightfold path.

[pārā. 199] Phalasacchikiriyāti sotāpattiphalassa sacchikiriyā, sakadāgāmiphalassa sacchikiriyā, anāgāmiphalassa sacchikiriyā, arahattassa [arahattaphalassa (syā.)] sacchikiriyā.

'Realization of the fruits' means the realization of the fruit of stream-entry, the realization of the fruit of once-returning, the realization of the fruit of non-returning, the realization of the fruit of arahantship.

[pārā. 199] Kilesappahānanti rāgassa pahānaṃ, dosassa pahānaṃ, mohassa pahānaṃ.

'Abandoning defilements' means abandoning lust, abandoning aversion, abandoning delusion.

[pārā. 199] Vinīvaraṇatā cittassāti rāgā cittaṃ vinīvaraṇatā, dosā cittaṃ vinīvaraṇatā, mohā cittaṃ vinīvaraṇatā.

'Freedom from hindrances of the mind' means that the mind is free from hindrances due to lust, the mind is free from hindrances due to aversion, the mind is free from hindrances due to delusion.

[pārā. 199] Suññāgāre abhiratīti paṭhamena jhānena suññāgāre abhirati, dutiyena jhānena suññāgāre abhirati, tatiyena jhānena suññāgāre abhirati, catutthena jhānena suññāgāre abhirati.

'Delight in solitude' means delighting in solitude through the first jhāna, delighting in solitude through the second jhāna, delighting in solitude through the third jhāna, delighting in solitude through the fourth jhāna.

71. Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajji’’nti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

71.'Discloses' means if one says to someone not fully ordained, "I attained the first jhāna," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajjāmī’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Discloses': If he says to one who is not fully ordained, "I am attaining the first jhāna," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ samāpanno’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Discloses' means if he says to one not fully ordained, "I have attained the first jhāna," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘paṭhamassa jhānassa lābhimhī’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Discloses' means that if he says to one who is not fully ordained, "I have attained the first jhāna," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘paṭhamassa jhānassa vasimhī’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Discloses' means if he says to one who is not fully ordained, "I have mastery in the first jhāna," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikataṃ mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to one not yet fully ordained, saying, "I have realized the first jhāna," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ… tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; catutthassa jhānassa lābhimhi, vasimhi; catutthaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikataṃ mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person: "I have attained the second jhāna... the third jhāna... the fourth jhāna; I am attaining it, I am attained to it; I am one who gains the fourth jhāna, I am master of it; the fourth jhāna has been realized by me"—he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘suññataṃ vimokkhaṃ… animittaṃ vimokkhaṃ… appaṇihitaṃ vimokkhaṃ… suññataṃ samādhiṃ… animittaṃ samādhiṃ… appaṇihitaṃ samādhiṃ samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; appaṇihitassa samādhissa lābhimhi, vasimhi; appaṇihito samādhi sacchikato mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person, "I have attained emptiness liberation... signless liberation... desireless liberation... emptiness samādhi... signless samādhi... desireless samādhi; I am attaining it, I am attained to it; I am one who gains desireless samādhi, I am master of it; I have realized desireless samādhi"—he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘suññataṃ samāpattiṃ… animittaṃ samāpattiṃ… appaṇihitaṃ samāpattiṃ samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; appaṇihitāya samāpattiyā lābhimhi, vasimhi; appaṇihitā samāpatti sacchikatā mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person, saying: “I have attained the emptiness attainment… the signless attainment… the desireless attainment; I attained it, I am attaining it, I have attained it; I am one who gains the desireless attainment, I am master of it; I have realized the desireless attainment”—he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘tisso vijjā samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; tissannaṃ vijjānaṃ lābhimhi, vasimhi; tisso vijjā sacchikatā mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person, saying: "I attained the three knowledges; I am attaining them; I have attained them; I have gained the three knowledges, I am master of them; I have realized the three knowledges," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘cattāro satipaṭṭhāne… cattāro sammappadhāne… cattāro iddhipāde samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ lābhimhi, vasimhi; cattāro iddhipādā sacchikatā mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person: “I attained the four establishments of mindfulness… the four right exertions… the four bases of success; I am attaining them, I have attained them; I am one who gains the four bases of success, I am master of them; I have realized the four bases of success,” he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘pañcindriyāni… pañca balāni samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; pañcannaṃ balānaṃ lābhimhi, vasimhi; pañca balāni sacchikatāni mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person, saying: "I have attained the five faculties... the five powers; I am attaining them, I have attained them; I have gained the five powers, I am master of them; I have realized the five powers" — he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘satta bojjhaṅge samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; sattannaṃ bojjhaṅgānaṃ lābhimhi, vasimhi; satta bojjhaṅgā sacchikatā mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person, "I have attained the seven factors of enlightenment, I am attaining them, I have attained them; I gain the seven factors of enlightenment, I have mastery over them; I have realized the seven factors of enlightenment," he commits a pācittiya offense.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; ariyassa aṭṭhaṅgikassa maggassa lābhimhi, vasimhi; ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo sacchikato mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person: "I have attained, am attaining, or am one who has attained the noble eightfold path; I am one who has gained and mastered the noble eightfold path; the noble eightfold path has been realized by me," he commits an offense entailing expiation.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘sotāpattiphalaṃ… sakadāgāmiphalaṃ… anāgāmiphalaṃ… arahattaṃ samāpajjiṃ, samāpajjāmi, samāpanno; arahattassa lābhimhi, vasimhi; arahattaṃ [arahattaphalaṃ (syā.)] sacchikataṃ mayā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person: "I have attained, am attaining, or am one who has attained the fruit of stream-entry... the fruit of once-returning... the fruit of non-returning... arahantship; I am one who has gained and mastered arahantship; arahantship has been realized by me," he commits an offense entailing expiation.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘rāgo me catto… doso me catto… moho me catto, vanto, mutto, pahīno, paṭinissaṭṭho, ukkheṭito, samukkheṭito’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person: "My lust has been abandoned... my hatred has been abandoned... my delusion has been abandoned, disgorged, released, relinquished, given up, uprooted, completely uprooted," he commits an offense entailing expiation.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘rāgā me cittaṃ vinīvaraṇaṃ… dosā me citta vinīvaraṇaṃ… mohā me cittaṃ vinīvaraṇa’’nti bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he declares to an unordained person: "My mind is unhindered by lust... my mind is unhindered by hatred... my mind is unhindered by delusion," he commits an offense entailing expiation.

Should he disclose to one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk who stayed in your dwelling attained the second jhāna… the third jhāna, the fourth jhāna, is attaining it, has attained it; that monk is a gainer of the fourth jhāna, a master of it; by that monk, the fourth jhāna has been realized"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘yo te vihāre vasi so bhikkhu suññataṃ vimokkhaṃ…pe… animittaṃ vimokkhaṃ appaṇihitaṃ vimokkhaṃ suññataṃ samādhiṃ animittaṃ samādhiṃ appaṇihitaṃ samādhiṃ samāpajji, samāpajjati, samāpanno; so bhikkhu appaṇihitassa samādhissa lābhī, vasī; tena bhikkhunā appaṇihito samādhi sacchikato’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Should he disclose to one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk who stayed in your dwelling attained the deliverance of emptiness… the signless deliverance, the desireless deliverance, the samādhi of emptiness, the signless samādhi, the desireless samādhi, is attaining it, has attained it; that monk is a gainer of the desireless samādhi, a master of it; by that monk, the desireless samādhi has been realized"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘yo te vihāre vasi so bhikkhu suññataṃ samāpattiṃ…pe… animittaṃ samāpattiṃ appaṇihitaṃ samāpattiṃ samāpajji, samāpajjati, samāpanno; appaṇihitāya samāpattiyā lābhī, vasī; tena bhikkhunā appaṇihitā samāpatti sacchikatā’’ti bhaṇantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Should he disclose to one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk who stayed in your dwelling attained the attainment of emptiness… the signless attainment, the desireless attainment, is attaining it, has attained it; that monk is a gainer of the desireless attainment, a master of it; by that monk, the desireless attainment has been realized"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘yo te vihāre vasi so bhikkhu tisso vijjā …pe… cattāro satipaṭṭhāne, cattāro sammappadhāne, cattāro iddhipāde, pañcindriyāni, pañca balāni, satta bojhaṅge, ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ, sotāpattiphalaṃ, sakadāgāmiphalaṃ, anāgāmiphalaṃ, arahattaṃ [arahattaphalaṃ (syā.)] samāpajji…pe… samāpajjati, samāpanno…pe… tassa bhikkhuno rāgo catto, doso catto, moho catto, vanto, mutto, pahīno, paṭinissaṭṭho, ukkheṭito, samukkheṭito; tassa bhikkhuno rāgā cittaṃ vinīvaraṇaṃ, dosā cittaṃ vinīvaraṇaṃ, mohā cittaṃ vinīvaraṇa’’nti bhaṇantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Should he disclose to one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk who stayed in your dwelling attained the three knowledges… the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right exertions, the four bases of spiritual power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, the Noble Eightfold Path, the fruit of stream-entry, the fruit of once-returning, the fruit of non-returning, arahantship… is attaining it, has attained it… that monk’s greed is abandoned, hatred is abandoned, delusion is abandoned; discarded, released, abandoned, relinquished; uprooted, completely uprooted; that monk’s mind is unhindered by greed, his mind is unhindered by hatred, his mind is unhindered by delusion"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘yo te vihāre vasi so bhikkhu suññāgāre paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ…pe… dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajji, samāpajjati, samāpanno; so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthassa jhānassa lābhī, vasī; tena bhikkhunā suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikata’’nti bhaṇantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he informs one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk who stayed in your dwelling attained the first jhāna in solitude… the second jhāna, the third jhāna, the fourth jhāna, is attaining it, has attained it; that monk is a gainer of the fourth jhāna in solitude, a master of it; by that monk, the fourth jhāna in solitude has been realized"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘yo te cīvaraṃ paribhuñji, yo te piṇḍapātaṃ paribhuñji, yo te senāsanaṃ paribhuñji, yo te gilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāraṃ paribhuñji so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajji, samāpajjati, samāpanno; so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthassa jhānassa lābhī, vasī; tena bhikkhunā suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikata’’nti bhaṇantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he informs one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk who used your robe, who used your almsfood, who used your lodging, who used your medicinal requisites attained the fourth jhāna in solitude, is attaining it, has attained it; that monk is a gainer of the fourth jhāna in solitude, a master of it; by that monk, the fourth jhāna in solitude has been realized"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

76. Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘yena te vihāro paribhutto…pe… yena te cīvaraṃ paribhuttaṃ, yena te piṇḍapāto paribhutto, yena te senāsanaṃ paribhuttaṃ, yena te gilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāro paribhutto so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajji, samāpajjati, samāpanno; so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthassa jhānassa lābhī, vasī; tena bhikkhunā suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikata’’nti bhaṇantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa.

76.If he informs one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk by whom your dwelling was used… by whom your robe was used, by whom your almsfood was used, by whom your lodging was used, by whom your medicinal requisites were used, attained the fourth jhāna in solitude, is attaining it, has attained it; that monk is a gainer of the fourth jhāna in solitude, a master of it; by that monk, the fourth jhāna in solitude has been realized"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

Āroceyyāti anupasampannassa – ‘‘yaṃ tvaṃ āgamma vihāraṃ adāsi…pe… cīvaraṃ adāsi, piṇḍapātaṃ adāsi, senāsanaṃ adāsi, gilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāraṃ adāsi so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ samāpajji, samāpajjati, samāpanno; so bhikkhu suññāgāre catutthassa jhānassa lābhī, vasī; tena bhikkhunā suññāgāre catutthaṃ jhānaṃ sacchikata’’nti bhaṇantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he informs one not fully ordained, saying: "The monk for whose sake you gave a dwelling… gave a robe, gave almsfood, gave lodging, gave medicinal requisites, attained the fourth jhāna in solitude, is attaining it, has attained it; that monk is a gainer of the fourth jhāna in solitude, a master of it; by that monk, the fourth jhāna in solitude has been realized"—he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

77. Anāpatti upasampannassa, bhūtaṃ āroceti, ādikammikassāti.

77.There is no offense for one fully ordained, if he discloses what is true, or for a first offender.

Bhūtārocanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The eighth training rule on truthful disclosure is concluded.
