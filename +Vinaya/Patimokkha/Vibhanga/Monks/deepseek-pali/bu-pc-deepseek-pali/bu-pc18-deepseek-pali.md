# BU-PC 18

## 8. Vehāsakuṭisikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The training rule concerning the upper-story dwelling.

129. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena dve bhikkhū saṅghike vihāre uparivehāsakuṭiyā viharanti?. Eko heṭṭhā viharati [viharanti, eko heṭṭhā (?)], eko upari. Uparimo bhikkhu āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ sahasā abhinisīdi. Mañcapādo nippatitvā [patitvā (syā.)] heṭṭhimassa bhikkhuno matthake avatthāsi. So bhikkhu vissaramakāsi. Bhikkhū upadhāvitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kissa tvaṃ, āvuso, vissaramakāsī’’ti? Atha kho so bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhu saṅghike vihāre uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ sahasā abhinisīdissatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū taṃ bhikkhuṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, saṅghike vihāre uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ sahasā abhinisīdasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, saṅghike vihāre uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ sahasā abhinisīdissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

129.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. At that time, two bhikkhus were dwelling in a Sangha residence, in the upper story of a dwelling. One was dwelling below; the other, above. The bhikkhu in the upper story sat down abruptly on a bed with detachable legs. A leg of the bed fell out, striking the head of the bhikkhu below. That bhikkhu cried out in pain. The bhikkhus rushed up and said to him, “Why did you cry out in pain, friend?” Then that bhikkhu told the bhikkhus what had happened. Those bhikkhus who were modest, etc., found fault, grumbled, and criticized, “How could a bhikkhu sit down abruptly on a bed with detachable legs in the upper story of a dwelling in a Sangha residence?” Then, after rebuking that bhikkhu in many ways, they told the Blessed One about the matter, saying: “Is it true, bhikkhu, that you sat down abruptly on a bed with detachable legs in the upper story of a dwelling in a Sangha residence?” “It’s true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him, saying: “Foolish man, how could you sit down abruptly on a bed with detachable legs in the upper story of a dwelling in a Sangha residence! This, foolish man, is not for the confidence of those who are not yet confident, nor for increasing the confidence of those who are already confident, etc. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

130. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu saṅghike vihāre uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ vā pīṭhaṃ vā abhinisīdeyya vā abhinipajjeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

130.Should any bhikkhu sit down or lie down on a bed or a bench with detachable legs in a Sangha residence, in the upper story of a dwelling, there is an offense entailing confession.

131. Yo  panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

131.'Should any bhikkhu': 'should any' means any such person... 'bhikkhu': ...the bhikkhu intended in this context.

Saṅghiko nāma vihāro saṅghassa dinno hoti pariccatto.

A Sangha residence is one that has been given and relinquished to the Sangha.

Vehāsakuṭi nāma majjhimassa purisassa asīsaghaṭṭā.

A vehāsakuṭi is one where a medium-sized person's head does not strike (the ceiling/beams).

Āhaccapādako nāma mañco aṅge vijjhitvā ṭhito hoti. Āhaccapādakaṃ nāma pīṭhaṃ aṅge vijjhitvā ṭhitaṃ hoti.

A bed called 'āhaccapādaka' is one whose legs are inserted into its body. A bench called 'āhaccapādaka' is one whose legs are inserted into its body.

Abhinisīdeyyāti tasmiṃ abhinisīdati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Should sit down': if one sits down on it, there is an offense entailing confession.

Abhinipajjeyyāti tasmiṃ abhinipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Should lie down': if one lies down on it, there is an offense entailing confession.

132. Saṅghike saṅghikasaññī uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ vā pīṭhaṃ vā abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike vematiko…pe… saṅghike puggalikasaññī uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṃ mañcaṃ vā pīṭhaṃ vā abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

132.If, in a Sangha residence, one perceives it as a Sangha residence, and sits down or lies down on a bed or a bench with detachable legs in the upper story of a dwelling, there is an offense entailing confession. If, in a Sangha residence, one is in doubt, etc. If, in a Sangha residence, one perceives it as individual property, and sits down or lies down on a bed or a bench with detachable legs in the upper story of a dwelling, there is an offense entailing confession.

Puggalike saṅghikasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike puggalikasaññī aññassa puggalike, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Attano puggalike, anāpatti.

If, in individual property, one perceives it as Sangha property, there is a dukkaṭa offense. If, in individual property, one is in doubt, there is a dukkaṭa offense. If, in individual property, one perceives it as individual property, and it belongs to another individual, there is a dukkaṭa offense. If it is one's own individual property, there is no offense.

133. Anāpatti – avehāsakuṭiyā sīsaghaṭṭāya heṭṭhā aparibhogaṃ hoti, padarasañcitaṃ hoti, paṭāṇi dinnā hoti, tasmiṃ ṭhito gaṇhati vā laggeti vā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

133.There is no offense: in a dwelling that is not an upper-story dwelling; in one where the head strikes (the ceiling/beams); where the space below is not used; where the upper floor is solid; where the legs are fixed with pegs; when standing on it one takes or affixes something; for one who is insane; for one who is a first-time offender.

Vehāsakuṭisikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The training rule concerning the upper-story dwelling is finished, the eighth.
