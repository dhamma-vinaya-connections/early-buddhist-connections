# BU-PC 6

## 6. Dutiyasahaseyyasikkhāpadaṃ

## 6.The Second Training Rule on Sharing a Sleeping Place.

55. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā anuruddho kosalesu janapade [ettha ambaṭṭhasuttādiṭīkā oloketabbā] sāvatthiṃ gacchanto sāyaṃ aññataraṃ gāmaṃ upagacchi. Tena kho pana samayena tasmiṃ gāme aññatarissā itthiyā āvasathāgāraṃ paññattaṃ hoti. Atha kho āyasmā anuruddho yena sā itthī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ itthiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sace te, bhagini, agaru, vaseyyāma ekarattaṃ āvasathāgāre’’ti. ‘‘Vaseyyātha, bhante’’ti. Aññepi addhikā yena sā itthī tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ itthiṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘sace te, ayye, agaru vaseyyāma ekarattaṃ āvasathāgāre’’ti. ‘‘Eso kho ayyo samaṇo paṭhamaṃ upagato; sace so anujānāti, vaseyyāthā’’ti. Atha kho te addhikā yenāyasmā anuruddho tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘sace te, bhante, agaru, vaseyyāma ekarattaṃ āvasathāgāre’’ti. ‘‘Vaseyyātha, āvuso’’ti. Atha kho sā itthī āyasmante anuruddhe saha dassanena paṭibaddhacittā ahosi. Atha kho sā itthī yenāyasmā anuruddho tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayyo, bhante, imehi manussehi ākiṇṇo na phāsu viharissati. Sādhāhaṃ, bhante, ayyassa mañcakaṃ abbhantaraṃ paññapeyya’’nti. Adhivāsesi kho āyasmā anuruddho tuṇhībhāvena. Atha kho sā itthī āyasmato anuruddhassa mañcakaṃ abbhantaraṃ paññapetvā alaṅkatappaṭiyattā gandhagandhinī yenāyasmā anuruddho tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayyo, bhante, abhirūpo dassanīyo pāsādiko, ahaṃ camhi abhirūpā dassanīyā pāsādikā. Sādhāhaṃ, bhante, ayyassa pajāpati bhaveyya’’nti. Evaṃ vutte āyasmā anuruddho tuṇhī ahosi. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho sā itthī āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayyo, bhante, abhirūpo dassanīyo pāsādiko, ahañcamhi abhirūpā dassanīyā pāsādikā. Sādhu, bhante, ayyo mañceva paṭicchatu [sampaṭicchatu (syā.)] sabbañca sāpateyya’’nti. Tatiyampi kho āyasmā anuruddho tuṇhī ahosi. Atha kho sā itthī sāṭakaṃ nikkhipitvā āyasmato anuruddhassa purato caṅkamatipi tiṭṭhatipi nisīdatipi seyyampi kappeti. Atha kho āyasmā anuruddho indriyāni okkhipitvā taṃ itthiṃ neva olokesi napi ālapi. Atha kho sā itthī – ‘‘acchariyaṃ vata bho, abbhutaṃ vata bho! Bahū me manussā satenapi sahassenapi pahiṇanti. Ayaṃ pana samaṇo – mayā sāmaṃ yāciyamāno – na icchati mañceva paṭicchituṃ sabbañca sāpateyya’’nti sāṭakaṃ nivāsetvā āyasmato anuruddhassa pādesu sirasā nipatitvā āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘accayo maṃ, bhante, accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ yāhaṃ evamakāsiṃ. Tassā me, bhante, ayyo accayaṃ accayato paṭiggaṇhātu āyatiṃ saṃvarāyā’’ti. ‘‘Taggha tvaṃ, bhagini, accayo accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ yā tvaṃ evamakāsi. Yato ca kho tvaṃ, bhagini, accayaṃ accayato disvā yathādhammaṃ paṭikarosi, taṃ te mayaṃ paṭiggaṇhāma. Vuddhi hesā, bhagini, ariyassa vinaye yo accayaṃ accayato disvā yathādhammaṃ paṭikaroti āyatiñca saṃvaraṃ āpajjatī’’ti.

55.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. At that time, the Venerable Anuruddha was traveling through Kosala on his way to Sāvatthī. In the evening, he approached a certain village. At that time, in that village, a certain woman had a lodging-house prepared. Then the Venerable Anuruddha approached that woman and said, “Sister, if it is not inconvenient, may we stay one night in the lodging-house?” “You may stay, venerable sir.” Other travelers also approached that woman and said, “Madam, if it is not inconvenient, may we stay one night in the lodging-house?” “This venerable ascetic arrived first. If he consents, you may stay.” Then those travelers approached the Venerable Anuruddha and said, “Venerable sir, if it is not inconvenient, may we stay one night in the lodging-house?” “You may stay, friends.” Then that woman, upon seeing the Venerable Anuruddha, became attached to him. She approached him and said, “Venerable sir, you will not be comfortable staying amidst these people. I would gladly prepare a bed for you inside.” The Venerable Anuruddha consented by silence. Then that woman prepared a bed for the Venerable Anuruddha inside, adorned and perfumed herself, and approached the Venerable Anuruddha. She said, “Venerable sir, you are handsome, attractive, and pleasing. I too am beautiful, attractive, and pleasing. Venerable sir, it would be good if I could be your wife.” When this was said, the Venerable Anuruddha remained silent. A second time... a third time she said, “Venerable sir, you are handsome, attractive, and pleasing. I too am beautiful, attractive, and pleasing. Venerable sir, please accept me and all my wealth.” A third time, the Venerable Anuruddha remained silent. Then that woman, having discarded her outer garment, walked back and forth, stood, sat, and lay down in front of the Venerable Anuruddha. But the Venerable Anuruddha, having restrained his senses, neither looked at her nor addressed her. Then that woman thought, “How wonderful, how marvelous! Many men send me invitations, offering hundreds and thousands. Yet this ascetic, though I personally entreat him, does not wish to accept me and all my wealth!” Putting on her garment, she prostrated herself at the Venerable Anuruddha’s feet and said, “Venerable sir, I have transgressed—foolishly, ignorantly, and unskillfully—by acting in this way. May the venerable one accept my transgression as such, for the sake of future restraint.” “Indeed, sister, you have transgressed—foolishly, ignorantly, and unskillfully—by acting in this way. But since you recognize your transgression as such and make amends correctly, we accept your confession. For it is growth in the Noble One’s Discipline for one to recognize a transgression as such, make amends correctly, and undertake restraint for the future.”

Atha kho sā itthī tassā rattiyā accayena āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappetvā sampavāretvā, āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ bhuttāviṃ onītapattapāṇiṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho taṃ itthiṃ āyasmā anuruddho dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho sā itthī – āyasmatā anuruddhena dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā – āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bhante, abhikkantaṃ, bhante! Seyyathāpi, bhante, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya – cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantīti, evamevaṃ ayyena anuruddhena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ, bhante, taṃ bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsikaṃ maṃ ayyo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti.

Then, when the night had passed, that woman served and satisfied the Venerable Anuruddha with choice solid and soft food. When the Venerable Anuruddha had finished eating and had put away his bowl, she paid homage to him and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the Venerable Anuruddha instructed, urged, and gladdened her with a Dhamma talk. Then, having been instructed, urged, and gladdened by the Venerable Anuruddha with a Dhamma talk, that woman said to him: “Excellent, venerable sir! Excellent, venerable sir! Just as one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the way to one who was lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness so that those with eyes might see forms—even so has the Venerable Anuruddha explained the Dhamma in many ways. I go for refuge to the Blessed One, the Dhamma, and the Sangha of bhikkhus. May the venerable one count me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forward, for life.”

Atha kho āyasmā anuruddho sāvatthiyaṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā anuruddho mātugāmena sahaseyyaṃ kappessatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, anuruddha, mātugāmena sahaseyyaṃ kappesī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, anuruddha, mātugāmena sahaseyyaṃ kappessasi! Netaṃ, anuruddha, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then, when the Venerable Anuruddha had gone to Sāvatthī, he informed the bhikkhus of this matter. Those bhikkhus who were few in their desires... murmured, criticized, and complained: “How could the Venerable Anuruddha share a sleeping place with a woman?” Then those bhikkhus criticized the Venerable Anuruddha in many ways and reported the matter to the Blessed One. ... “Is it true, Anuruddha, that you shared a sleeping place with a woman?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him: ... “How could you, Anuruddha, share a sleeping place with a woman? This will not inspire confidence in those without it ... ” And then, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule as follows:

56. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu mātugāmena sahaseyyaṃ kappeyya pācittiya’’nti.

56.“Whatever bhikkhu shares a sleeping place with a woman, there is an offense entailing confession.”

57. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

57.“Now whoever” means: ... A “bhikkhu” ... means: the bhikkhu intended in this case.

Mātugāmo nāma manussitthī, na yakkhī [yakkhinī (ka.)], na petī, na tiracchānagatā; antamaso tadahujātāpi dārikā, pageva mahattarī.

“Woman” means a human female, not a female yakkha, not a female peta, not a female animal—even a girl born on that very day, how much more so an older one.

Sahāti ekato.

“Together” means in one place.

Seyyā nāma sabbacchannā, sabbaparicchannā, yebhuyyenacchannā, yebhuyyena paricchannā.

A “sleeping place” is one that is fully roofed, fully enclosed; or mostly roofed, mostly enclosed.

Seyyaṃ  kappeyyāti atthaṅgate sūriye, mātugāme nipanne bhikkhu nipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Bhikkhu nipanne mātugāmo nipajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Ubho vā nipajjanti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Uṭṭhahitvā punappunaṃ nipajjanti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

“If he shares a sleeping place” means: after the sun has set, if a woman lies down and a bhikkhu lies down, there is an offense entailing confession. If a bhikkhu lies down and a woman lies down, there is an offense entailing confession. Or if both lie down, there is an offense entailing confession. If, having gotten up, they lie down repeatedly, there is an offense entailing confession.

58. Mātugāme mātugāmasaññī sahaseyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Mātugāme vematiko sahaseyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Mātugāme amātugāmasaññī sahaseyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

58.If, perceiving a woman as a woman, he shares a sleeping place with her, it is an offense entailing confession. If, being uncertain whether it is a woman, he shares a sleeping place with her, it is an offense entailing confession. If, perceiving a woman as not a woman, he shares a sleeping place with her, it is an offense entailing confession.

Upaḍḍhacchanne upaḍḍhaparicchanne, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Yakkhiyā vā petiyā vā paṇḍakena vā tiracchānagatitthiyā vā sahaseyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amātugāme mātugāmasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amātugāme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amātugāme amātugāmasaññī, anāpatti.

In a place half-roofed or half-enclosed, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he shares a sleeping place with a female yakkha, a female peta, a eunuch, or a female animal, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving a non-woman as a woman, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, being uncertain whether it is a non-woman, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving a non-woman as a non-woman, there is no offense.

59. Anāpatti sabbacchanne sabbaaparicchanne, sabbaparicchanne sabbaacchanne [anāpatti sabbaacchanne sabbaaparicchanne, (sī.)], yebhuyyena acchanne, yebhuyyena aparicchanne, mātugāme nipanne bhikkhu nisīdati, bhikkhu nipanne mātugāmo nisīdati, ubho vā nisīdanti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

59.There is no offense if it is fully roofed and fully unenclosed; fully enclosed and fully unroofed; mostly unroofed; mostly unenclosed; if a woman lies down and a bhikkhu sits; if a bhikkhu lies down and a woman sits; or if both sit; if he is insane; or if he is the first offender.

Dutiyasahaseyyasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The Second Training Rule on Sharing a Sleeping Place is finished.
