# BU-PC 38

## 8. Sannidhikārakasikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The training rule concerning stored-up food.

252. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato ānandassa upajjhāyo āyasmā belaṭṭhasīso [belaṭṭhisīso (sī.) veḷaṭṭhasīso (syā.)] araññe viharati. So piṇḍāya caritvā sukkhakuraṃ ārāmaṃ haritvā sukkhāpetvā nikkhipati. Yadā āhārena attho hoti, tadā udakena temetvā temetvā bhuñjati, cirena gāmaṃ piṇḍāya pavisati. Bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ belaṭṭhasīsaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kissa tvaṃ, āvuso, cirena gāmaṃ piṇḍāya pavisasī’’ti? Atha kho āyasmā belaṭṭhasīso bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Kiṃ pana tvaṃ, āvuso, sannidhikārakaṃ bhojanaṃ bhuñjasī’’ti? ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā belaṭṭhasīso sannidhikārakaṃ bhojanaṃ bhuñjissatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, belaṭṭhasīsa, sannidhikārakaṃ bhojanaṃ bhuñjasīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, belaṭṭhasīsa, sannidhikārakaṃ bhojanaṃ bhuñjissasi! Netaṃ, belaṭṭhasīsa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

252.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. Now at that time, Venerable Ananda’s preceptor, Venerable Belaṭṭhasīsa, was dwelling in the forest. After walking for alms, he would bring dried food back to the monastery, dry it, and set it aside. When he needed food, he would moisten it with water again and again before eating it, and he would enter the village for alms only after a long time. The bhikkhus said to Venerable Belaṭṭhasīsa, “Why do you, friend, enter the village for alms only after a long time?” Then Venerable Belaṭṭhasīsa told the bhikkhus the reason. “But do you, friend, eat stored-up food?” “Yes, friends.” Those bhikkhus who were modest… criticized and complained, “How can Venerable Belaṭṭhasīsa eat stored-up food?”… “Is it true, Belaṭṭhasīsa, that you eat stored-up food?” “It’s true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “How can you, Belaṭṭhasīsa, eat stored-up food? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… Therefore, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

253. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu sannidhikārakaṃ khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

253.“Whatever bhikkhu should eat or consume stored-up hard food or soft food, he commits an offense entailing confession.”

254. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

254.“Now whoever”: Whoever… “bhikkhu”: … in this connection, this bhikkhu is intended.

Sannidhikārakaṃ nāma ajja paṭiggahitaṃ aparajju khāditaṃ hoti.

“Stored-up” means what is received today and eaten on the following day.

Khādanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ ṭhapetvā avasesaṃ khādanīyaṃ nāma.

“Hard food” refers to food that is allowable until midday, being the remainder after excluding that which is allowable for a yāma, for seven days, or for a lifetime.

Bhojanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṃsaṃ.

“Soft food” means the five staple foods: cooked rice, gruel, flour, fish, and meat.

‘‘Khādissāmi bhuñjissāmī’’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he accepts it thinking, “I will chew and swallow,” there is an offense of wrong-doing. With each mouthful swallowed, there is an offense entailing confession.

255. Sannidhikārake sannidhikārakasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Sannidhikārake vematiko khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Sannidhikārake asannidhikārakasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

255.If, regarding stored-up food, he perceives it as stored-up and eats or consumes hard food or soft food, he commits an offense entailing confession. If, regarding stored-up food, he is in doubt and eats or consumes hard food or soft food, he commits an offense entailing confession. If, regarding stored-up food, he perceives it as not stored-up and eats or consumes hard food or soft food, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ āhāratthāya paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti dukkaṭassa. Asannidhikārake sannidhikārakasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Asannidhikārake vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Asannidhikārake asannidhikārakasaññī, anāpatti.

If he accepts what is allowable for a yāma, for seven days, or for a lifetime for the purpose of nourishment, there is an offense of wrong-doing. For each mouthful, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, regarding what is not stored-up, he perceives it as stored-up, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, regarding what is not stored-up, he is in doubt, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, regarding what is not stored-up, he perceives it as not stored-up, there is no offense.

256. Anāpatti yāvakālikaṃ yāvakāle nidahitvā bhuñjati, yāmakālikaṃ yāme nidahitvā bhuñjati, sattāhakālikaṃ sattāhaṃ nidahitvā bhuñjati, yāvajīvikaṃ sati paccaye paribhuñjati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

256.There is no offense if one eats food allowable until midday, having kept it until midday; food allowable for a yāma, having kept it until that yāma; food allowable for seven days, having kept it for seven days; food allowable for a lifetime, using it when there is a reason; for an insane person; for the first offender.

Sannidhikārakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The training rule concerning stored-up food is finished, the eighth.
