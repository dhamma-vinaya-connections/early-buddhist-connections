# BU-PC 15

## 5. Dutiyasenāsanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 5.The second training rule concerning lodgings.

114. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū sahāyakā honti. Te vasantāpi ekatova vasanti, pakkamantāpi ekatova pakkamanti. Te aññatarasmiṃ saṅghike vihāre seyyaṃ santharitvā taṃ pakkamantā neva uddhariṃsu na uddharāpesuṃ, anāpucchā pakkamiṃsu. Senāsanaṃ upacikāhi khāyitaṃ hoti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū saṅghike vihāre seyyaṃ santharitvā taṃ pakkamantā neva uddharissanti na uddharāpessanti, anāpucchā pakkamissanti, senāsanaṃ upacikāhi khāyita’’nti! Atha kho te bhikkhū sattarasavaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū saṅghike vihāre seyyaṃ santharitvā taṃ pakkamantā neva uddhariṃsu na uddharāpesuṃ, anāpucchā pakkamiṃsu, senāsanaṃ upacikāhi khāyita’’nti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā saṅghike vihāre seyyaṃ santharitvā taṃ pakkamantā neva uddharissanti na uddharāpessanti, anāpucchā pakkamissanti, senāsanaṃ upacikāhi khāyitaṃ! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

114.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-seventeen bhikkhus were companions. They stayed together when dwelling and departed together when departing. Having spread out bedding in a certain communal dwelling, when departing, they neither put it away nor had it put away, and departed without asking leave. The lodging was eaten by termites. The bhikkhus who were modest … complained, criticized, and spread the word: “How can the group-of-seventeen bhikkhus, having spread out bedding in a communal dwelling, when departing, neither put it away nor have it put away, and depart without asking leave, so that the lodging is eaten by termites?” Then those bhikkhus criticized the group-of-seventeen bhikkhus in many ways and reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that the group-of-seventeen bhikkhus, having spread out bedding in a communal dwelling, when departing, neither put it away nor had it put away, and departed without asking leave, so that the lodging was eaten by termites?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them: “…How can you, foolish men, having spread out bedding in a communal dwelling, when departing, neither put it away nor have it put away, and depart without asking leave, so that the lodging is eaten by termites! This will not inspire confidence in those who lack it … And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

115. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu saṅghike vihāre seyyaṃ santharitvā vā santharāpetvā vā taṃ pakkamanto neva uddhareyya na uddharāpeyya, anāpucchaṃ vā gaccheyya, pācittiya’’nti.

115.Whatever bhikkhu, having spread out bedding in a communal dwelling or having had it spread out, then departing, neither puts it away nor has it put away, or goes away without asking leave, there is an offense entailing confession.

116. Yo  panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

116.‘Whatever’ means whoever, of whatever sort… ‘Bhikkhu’… here in this connection, a bhikkhu is intended.

Saṅghiko nāma vihāro saṅghassa dinno hoti pariccatto.

‘Communal’ means a dwelling given and relinquished to the Sangha.

Seyyaṃ nāma bhisi, cimilikā uttarattharaṇaṃ, bhūmattharaṇaṃ, taṭṭikā, cammakhaṇḍo, nisīdanaṃ, paccattharaṇaṃ, tiṇasanthāro, paṇṇasanthāro.

‘Bedding’ means a mattress, a coverlet with long fleece, a blanket, a floor covering, a mat, a piece of hide, a sitting cloth, a rug, a grass spread, a leaf spread.

Santharitvāti sayaṃ santharitvā.

‘Having spread it out’ means having spread it out oneself.

Santharāpetvāti aññaṃ santharāpetvā.

‘Having had it spread out’ means having another spread it out.

Taṃ pakkamanto neva uddhareyyāti na sayaṃ uddhareyya.

‘When departing, he neither puts it away’ means he does not put it away himself.

Na uddharāpeyyāti na aññaṃ uddharāpeyya.

‘Nor has it put away’ means he does not cause another to put it away.

Anāpucchaṃ vā gaccheyyāti bhikkhuṃ vā sāmaṇeraṃ vā ārāmikaṃ vā anāpucchā parikkhittassa ārāmassa parikkhepaṃ atikkamantassa āpatti pācittiyassa. Aparikkhittassa ārāmassa upacāraṃ atikkamantassa āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike saṅghikasaññī seyyaṃ santharitvā vā santharāpetvā vā taṃ pakkamanto neva uddhareyya na uddharāpeyya, anāpucchaṃ vā gaccheyya, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike vematiko seyyaṃ santharitvā vā santharāpetvā vā taṃ pakkamanto neva uddhareyya na uddharāpeyya, anāpucchaṃ vā gaccheyya, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike puggalikasaññī seyyaṃ santharitvā vā santharāpetvā vā taṃ pakkamanto neva uddhareyya na uddharāpeyya, anāpucchaṃ vā gaccheyya, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘Or goes away without asking leave’ means that if, without asking leave of a bhikkhu, a novice, or an ārāmika, one transgresses the boundary of an enclosed monastery, there is an offense entailing confession. If one transgresses the precinct of an unenclosed monastery, there is an offense entailing confession. If, having spread out bedding or had it spread out in a communal dwelling, perceiving it as communal property, when departing, one neither puts it away nor has it put away, or goes away without asking leave, there is an offense entailing confession. If, having spread out bedding or had it spread out in a communal dwelling, being uncertain about it, when departing, one neither puts it away nor has it put away, or goes away without asking leave, there is an offense entailing confession. If, having spread out bedding or had it spread out in a communal dwelling, perceiving it as personal property, when departing, one neither puts it away nor has it put away, or goes away without asking leave, there is an offense entailing confession.

117. Vihārassa upacāre vā upaṭṭhānasālāyaṃ vā maṇḍape vā rukkhamūle vā seyyaṃ santharitvā vā santharāpetvā vā taṃ pakkamanto neva uddhareyya na uddharāpeyya, anāpucchaṃ vā gaccheyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Mañcaṃ vā pīṭhaṃ vā vihāre vā vihārassūpacāre vā upaṭṭhānasālāyaṃ vā maṇḍape vā rukkhamūle vā santharitvā vā santharāpetvā vā taṃ pakkamanto neva uddhareyya na uddharāpeyya, anāpucchaṃ vā gaccheyya, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

117.If, having spread out bedding or had it spread out in the precinct of a dwelling, in a dining hall, in a pavilion, or at the foot of a tree, when departing, one neither puts it away nor has it put away, or goes away without asking leave, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, having spread out a couch or a seat or had it spread out in a dwelling, in the precinct of a dwelling, in a dining hall, in a pavilion, or at the foot of a tree, when departing, one neither puts it away nor has it put away, or goes away without asking leave, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

Puggalike saṅghikasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike puggalikasaññī aññassa puggalike āpatti dukkaṭassa. Attano puggalike anāpatti.

If, in personal property, one perceives it as communal property, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, in personal property, one is uncertain about it, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, in personal property, one perceives it as personal property, in another’s personal property, there is an offense of wrong-doing. In one’s own personal property, there is no offense.

118. Anāpatti uddharitvā gacchati, uddharāpetvā gacchati, āpucchaṃ gacchati, kenaci palibuddhaṃ hoti, sāpekkho gantvā tattha ṭhito āpucchati, kenaci palibuddho hoti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

118.There is no offense for one who departs having put it away, who departs having had it put away, who departs having asked leave, who is hindered by someone, who goes with an expectation and, having arrived there, asks leave, who is hindered by someone, in times of danger, for one who is insane, for the first offender.

Dutiyasenāsanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The second training rule concerning lodgings, the fifth, is finished.
