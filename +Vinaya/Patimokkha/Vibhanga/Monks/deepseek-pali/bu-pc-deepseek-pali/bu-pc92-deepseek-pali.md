# BU-PC 92

## 10. Nandasikkhāpadaṃ

## 10.The training rule concerning Nanda.

547. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā nando bhagavato mātucchāputto abhirūpo hoti dassanīyo pāsādiko caturaṅgulomako bhagavatā [bhagavato (ka.)]. So sugatacīvarappamāṇaṃ cīvaraṃ dhāreti. Addasaṃsu kho therā bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ nandaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna – ‘‘bhagavā āgacchatī’’ti āsanā vuṭṭhahanti. Te upagate jānitvā [upagataṃ sañjānitvā (syā.), upagate sañjānitvā (sī.)] ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā nando sugatacīvarappamāṇaṃ cīvaraṃ dhāressatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, nanda, sugatacīvarappamāṇaṃ cīvaraṃ dhāresīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, nanda, sugatacīvarappamāṇaṃ cīvaraṃ dhāressasi! Netaṃ, nanda, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

547.At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. Now at that time, Venerable Nanda, the Blessed One's maternal cousin, was handsome, good-looking, pleasing, and four fingers short of the proper measure. He wore a robe the size of the Sugata's robe. The senior bhikkhus saw Venerable Nanda coming from afar. Seeing him, they rose from their seats, thinking, "The Blessed One is coming." When they realized it was him upon his approach, they complained, criticized, and protested: "How can Venerable Nanda wear a robe the size of the Sugata's robe?" … "Is it true, Nanda, that you wear a robe the size of the Sugata's robe?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked him: … "How can you, Nanda, wear a robe the size of the Sugata's robe? This is not, Nanda, for inspiring confidence in those without it…" And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

548. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu sugatacīvarappamāṇaṃ cīvaraṃ kārāpeyya atirekaṃ vā, chedanakaṃ pācittiyaṃ. Tatridaṃ sugatassa sugatacīvarappamāṇaṃ – dīghaso nava vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ cha vidatthiyo. Idaṃ sugatassa sugatacīvarappamāṇa’’nti.

548."Should any bhikkhu have a robe made to the Sugata’s measure or exceeding it, it is to be forfeited and confessed. Now, this is the Sugata's robe measure: nine spans long, by the Sugata's span; and six spans wide. This is the Sugata's robe measure."

549. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

549."Yo panāti" means whoever... "bhikkhūti"... in this connection, the term "bhikkhu" is intended.

Sugatacīvarappamāṇaṃ [sugatacīvaraṃ (ka.)] nāma dīghaso nava vidatthiyo, sugatavidatthiyā; tiriyaṃ cha vidatthiyo.

The measurement of the Sugata's robe is nine Sugata-spans in length and six spans in width.

Kārāpeyyāti karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena chinditvā pācittiyaṃ desetabbaṃ.

He makes or has it made; in the effort, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Having obtained it, it must be cut off, and a confession entailing expiation should be made.

550. Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

550.If one completes what one has oneself begun, there is an offense entailing confession. If one has others complete what one has oneself begun, there is an offense entailing confession. If one oneself completes what others have begun, there is an offense entailing confession. If one has others complete what others have begun, there is an offense entailing confession.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he makes it or has it made for another, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he receives something made by another and uses it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct.

551. Anāpatti ūnakaṃ karoti, aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā chinditvā paribhuñjati, vitānaṃ vā bhūmattharaṇaṃ vā sāṇipākāraṃ vā bhisiṃ vā bibbohanaṃ vā karoti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

551.There is no offense for making it incomplete, for obtaining what was made by another, cutting it up, and then using it; for making a canopy, a floor covering, a sackcloth screen, a cushion, or a bolster; for one who is insane, for the first offender.

Nandasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ.

The tenth training rule on Nanda is concluded.

Ratanavaggo [rājavaggo (sī.)] navamo.

The ninth chapter is the Jewel Chapter.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Rañño ca ratanaṃ santaṃ, sūci mañcañca tūlikaṃ;Nisīdanañca kaṇḍuñca, vassikā sugatena cāti.

The king, the jewel, the peaceful, the needle, the bed, the cotton stuffing; the sitting mat, the scratching tool, and the rain cloak by the Well-Gone.

Uddiṭṭhā kho, āyasmanto, dvenavuti pācittiyā dhammā. Tatthāyasmante pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Dutiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Tatiyampi pucchāmi – ‘‘kaccittha parisuddhā’’? Parisuddhetthāyasmanto, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmīti.

Venerable ones, the ninety-two rules entailing confession have been recited. In regard to them, I ask the venerable ones: "Are you clear in this matter?" A second time I ask: "Are you clear in this matter?" A third time I ask: "Are you clear in this matter?" The venerable ones are clear in this matter, therefore they are silent. Thus I hold it.

Khuddakaṃ samattaṃ.

The minor section is concluded.

Pācittiyakaṇḍaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Pācittiya Section is concluded.
