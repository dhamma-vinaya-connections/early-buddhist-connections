# BU-PC 47

## 7. Mahānāmasikkhāpadaṃ

## 7.The Training Rule concerning Mahānāma

303. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Tena kho pana samayena mahānāmassa sakkassa bhesajjaṃ ussannaṃ hoti. Atha kho mahānāmo sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘icchāmahaṃ, bhante, saṅghaṃ catumāsaṃ [catummāsaṃ (sī.) cātumāsaṃ (syā.)] bhesajjena pavāretu’’nti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, mahānāma! Tena hi tvaṃ, mahānāma, saṅghaṃ catumāsaṃ bhesajjena pavārehī’’ti. Bhikkhū kukkuccāyantā nādhivāsenti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… anujānāmi, bhikkhave, catumāsaṃ bhesajjappaccayapavāraṇaṃ sāditunti.

303.At that time, the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans in Kapilavatthu, in the Banyan Park. Now at that time, the Sakyan Mahānāma had an abundance of medicine. Then the Sakyan Mahānāma approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Having sat down to one side, the Sakyan Mahānāma said to the Blessed One: “Bhante, I wish to invite the Sangha to accept an invitation for medicine for four months.” “Good, good, Mahānāma! Then, Mahānāma, invite the Sangha to accept medicine for four months.” The bhikkhus, feeling scrupulous, did not accept. They reported this matter to the Blessed One… “bhikkhus, I allow the acceptance of an invitation for requisites of medicine for four months.”

304. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ parittaṃ bhesajjaṃ viññāpenti. Tatheva mahānāmassa sakkassa bhesajjaṃ ussannaṃ hoti. Dutiyampi kho mahānāmo sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘icchāmahaṃ, bhante, saṅghaṃ aparampi catumāsaṃ bhesajjena pavāretu’’nti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, mahānāma! Tena hi tvaṃ, mahānāma, saṅghaṃ aparampi catumāsaṃ bhesajjena pavārehī’’ti. Bhikkhū kukkuccāyantā nādhivāsenti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… anujānāmi, bhikkhave, puna pavāraṇampi sāditunti.

304.At that time, the bhikkhus requested a small amount of medicine from the Sakyan Mahānāma. Still, the Sakyan Mahānāma had an abundance of medicine. A second time, the Sakyan Mahānāma approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Having sat down to one side, the Sakyan Mahānāma said to the Blessed One: “Bhante, I wish to invite the Sangha to accept an invitation for medicine for another four months.” “Good, good, Mahānāma! Then, Mahānāma, invite the Sangha to accept medicine for another four months.” The bhikkhus, feeling scrupulous, did not accept. They reported this matter to the Blessed One… “bhikkhus, I allow the acceptance of a renewed invitation.”

305. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ parittaṃyeva bhesajjaṃ viññāpenti. Tatheva mahānāmassa sakkassa bhesajjaṃ ussannaṃ hoti. Tatiyampi kho mahānāmo sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘icchāmahaṃ, bhante, saṅghaṃ yāvajīvaṃ bhesajjena pavāretu’’nti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, mahānāma! Tena hi tvaṃ, mahānāma, saṅghaṃ yāvajīvaṃ bhesajjena pavārehī’’ti. Bhikkhū kukkuccāyantā nādhivāsenti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… anujānāmi, bhikkhave, niccapavāraṇampi sāditunti.

305.At that time, the bhikkhus requested only a small amount of medicine from the Sakyan Mahānāma. Still, the Sakyan Mahānāma had an abundance of medicine. A third time, the Sakyan Mahānāma approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Having sat down to one side, the Sakyan Mahānāma said to the Blessed One: “Bhante, I wish to invite the Sangha to accept an invitation for medicine for life.” “Good, good, Mahānāma! Then, Mahānāma, invite the Sangha to accept medicine for life.” The bhikkhus, feeling scrupulous, did not accept. They reported this matter to the Blessed One… “bhikkhus, I allow the acceptance of a permanent invitation.”

Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū dunnivatthā honti duppārutā anākappasampannā. Mahānāmo sakko vattā hoti – ‘‘kissa tumhe, bhante, dunnivatthā duppārutā anākappasampannā? Nanu nāma pabbajitena sunivatthena bhavitabbaṃ supārutena ākappasampannenā’’ti? Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mahānāme sakke upanandhiṃsu. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘kena nu kho mayaṃ upāyena mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ maṅku kareyyāmā’’ti? Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘mahānāmena kho, āvuso, sakkena saṅgho bhesajjena pavārito. Handa mayaṃ, āvuso, mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ sappiṃ viññāpemā’’ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū yena mahānāmo sakko tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘doṇena, āvuso, sappinā attho’’ti. ‘‘Ajjaṇho, bhante, āgametha. Manussā vajaṃ gatā sappiṃ āharituṃ. Kālaṃ āharissathā’’ti [kāle harissathāti (syā.)].

At that time, the group-of-six bhikkhus were badly dressed, badly covered, and lacking in proper deportment. The Sakyan Mahānāma was one who spoke out. He said: “Bhantes, why are you badly dressed, badly covered, and lacking in proper deportment? Surely one who has gone forth should be well-dressed, well-covered, and endowed with proper deportment?” The group-of-six bhikkhus were displeased with the Sakyan Mahānāma. Then the group-of-six bhikkhus thought: “By what means could we embarrass the Sakyan Mahānāma?” Then the group-of-six bhikkhus thought: “Friends, the Sakyan Mahānāma has invited the Sangha to medicine. Come, friends, let us ask the Sakyan Mahānāma for ghee.” Then the group-of-six bhikkhus approached the Sakyan Mahānāma; having approached, they said to the Sakyan Mahānāma: “Friend, we need a doṇa of ghee.” “Bhantes,” he replied, “wait until this afternoon. The people have gone to the cattle station to fetch ghee. You will get it in due time.”

Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘doṇena, āvuso, sappinā attho’’ti. ‘‘Ajjaṇho, bhante, āgametha. Manussā vajaṃ gatā sappiṃ āharituṃ. Kālaṃ āharissathā’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ pana tayā, āvuso, adātukāmena pavāritena, yaṃ tvaṃ pavāretvā na desī’’ti! Atha kho mahānāmo sakko ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhadantā – ‘ajjaṇho, bhante, āgamethā’ti vuccamānā nāgamessantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū mahānāmassa sakkassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū mahānāmena sakkena – ‘ajjaṇho, bhante, āgamethā’ti vuccamānā nāgamessantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, mahānāmena sakkena – ‘‘ajjaṇho, bhante, āgamethā’’ti vuccamānā nāgamethāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, mahānāmena sakkena – ‘‘ajjaṇho, bhante āgamethā’’ti vuccamānā nāgamessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

A second time… a third time too, the group-of-six bhikkhus said to the Sakyan Mahānāma: “Friend, we need a doṇa of ghee.” “Bhantes,” he replied, “wait until this afternoon. The people have gone to the cattle station to fetch ghee. You will get it in due time.” “But friend, why do you invite us if you are unwilling to give? Why do you not give after having invited us?” Then the Sakyan Mahānāma complained, grumbled, and protested: “How can these venerable ones, when told, ‘Bhantes, wait until this afternoon,’ not wait?” The bhikkhus heard the Sakyan Mahānāma complaining, grumbling, and protesting. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, grumbled, and protested: “How can the group-of-six bhikkhus, when told by the Sakyan Mahānāma, ‘Bhantes, wait until this afternoon,’ not wait?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that when told by the Sakyan Mahānāma, ‘Bhantes, wait until this afternoon,’ you did not wait?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “Foolish men, how can you, when told by the Sakyan Mahānāma, ‘Bhantes, wait until this afternoon,’ not wait? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… Therefore, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule thus—”

306. ‘‘Agilānena bhikkhunā catumāsappaccayapavāraṇā sāditabbā, aññatra punapavāraṇāya, aññatra niccapavāraṇāya; tato ce uttari sādiyeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

306.“A non-sick bhikkhu should accept an invitation for requisites for four months, except for a renewed invitation, except for a permanent invitation; if he accepts beyond that, it is a pācittiya.”

307. Agilānena bhikkhunā catumāsappaccayapavāraṇā sāditabbāti gilānappaccayapavāraṇā sāditabbā.

307.“A non-sick bhikkhu should accept an invitation for requisites for four months” means that an invitation for requisites for the sick should be accepted.

Punapavāraṇāpi sāditabbāti yadā gilāno bhavissāmi tadā viññāpessāmīti.

“A renewed invitation should also be accepted” means: “When I am sick, I will inform you.”

Niccapavāraṇāpi sāditabbāti yadā gilāno bhavissāmi tadā viññāpessāmīti.

“A permanent invitation should also be accepted” means: “When I am sick, I will inform you.”

Tato ce uttari sādiyeyyāti atthi pavāraṇā bhesajjapariyantā na rattipariyantā, atthi pavāraṇā rattipariyantā na bhesajjapariyantā, atthi pavāraṇā bhesajjapariyantā ca rattipariyantā ca, atthi pavāraṇā neva bhesajjapariyantā na rattipariyantā.

“If he accepts beyond that” means: there is an invitation limited by medicine but not by nights; there is an invitation limited by nights but not by medicine; there is an invitation limited by both medicine and nights; there is an invitation limited by neither medicine nor nights.

Bhesajjapariyantā nāma bhesajjāni pariggahitāni honti – ‘‘ettakehi bhesajjehi pavāremī’’ti. Rattipariyantā nāma rattiyo pariggahitāyo honti – ‘‘ettakāsu rattīsu pavāremī’’ti. Bhesajjapariyantā ca rattipariyantā ca nāma bhesajjāni ca pariggahitāni honti rattiyo ca pariggahitāyo honti – ‘‘ettakehi bhesajjehi ettakāsu rattīsu pavāremī’’ti. Neva bhesajjapariyantā na rattipariyantā nāma bhesajjāni ca apariggahitāni honti rattiyo ca apariggahitāyo honti.

Limited by medicine means that medicines are specified: “I invite with this much medicine.” Limited by nights means that nights are specified: “I invite for this many nights.” Limited by both medicine and nights means that both medicines and nights are specified: “I invite with this much medicine for this many nights.” Limited by neither medicine nor nights means that neither medicines nor nights are specified.

308. Bhesajjapariyante – yehi bhesajjehi pavārito hoti tāni bhesajjāni ṭhapetvā aññāni bhesajjāni viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Rattipariyante – yāsu rattīsu pavārito hoti, tā rattiyo ṭhapetvā aññāsu rattīsu viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Bhesajjapariyante ca rattipariyante ca – yehi bhesajjehi pavārito hoti, tāni bhesajjāni ṭhapetvā yāsu rattīsu pavārito hoti, tā rattiyo ṭhapetvā aññāni bhesajjāni aññāsu rattīsu viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Neva bhesajjapariyante na rattipariyante, anāpatti.

308.In the case of an invitation limited by medicine: if, having set aside those medicines with which he has been invited, he requests other medicines, there is a pācittiya. In the case of an invitation limited by nights: if, having set aside those nights for which he has been invited, he requests other nights, there is a pācittiya. In the case of an invitation limited by both medicine and nights: if, having set aside those medicines with which he has been invited, and having set aside those nights for which he has been invited, he requests other medicines on other nights, there is a pācittiya. In the case of an invitation limited by neither medicine nor nights, there is no offense.

309. Na bhesajjena karaṇīyena [karaṇīye (sī. syā.)] bhesajjaṃ viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Aññena bhesajjena karaṇīyena aññaṃ bhesajjaṃ viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Tatuttari tatuttarisaññī bhesajjaṃ viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Tatuttari vematiko bhesajjaṃ viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Tatuttari natatuttarisaññī bhesajjaṃ viññāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

309.If he requests medicine when there is no need for medicine, there is a pācittiya. If he requests one medicine when there is need for another medicine, there is a pācittiya. If it is beyond the limit and he perceives it as beyond the limit, he requests medicine, there is a pācittiya. If it is beyond the limit and he is doubtful whether it is beyond the limit, he requests medicine, there is a pācittiya. If it is beyond the limit but he perceives it as not beyond the limit, he requests medicine, there is a pācittiya.

Natatuttari tatuttarisaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Natatuttari vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Natatuttari natatuttarisaññī, anāpatti.

If it is not beyond the limit but he perceives it as beyond the limit, there is a dukkaṭa. If it is not beyond the limit but he is doubtful whether it is beyond the limit, there is a dukkaṭa. If it is not beyond the limit and he perceives it as not beyond the limit, there is no offense.

310. Anāpatti yehi bhesajjehi pavārito hoti tāni bhesajjāni viññāpeti, yāsu rattīsu pavārito hoti tāsu rattīsu viññāpeti, ‘‘imehi tayā bhesajjehi pavāritāmha, amhākañca iminā ca iminā ca bhesajjena attho’’ti ācikkhitvā viññāpeti, ‘‘yāsu tayā rattīsu pavāritāmha tāyo ca rattiyo vītivattā amhākañca bhesajjena attho’’ti ācikkhitvā viññāpeti, ñātakānaṃ pavāritānaṃ, aññassatthāya, attano dhanena, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

310.There is no offense if he asks for those medicines for which he was invited; if he asks for those nights for which he was invited; if, having informed them, 'We were invited by you for these medicines, and we have need of this and that medicine,' he asks; if, having informed them, 'Those nights for which you invited us have passed, and we have need of medicine,' he asks; if it is from relatives who have invited him; if it is for the sake of another; if it is with his own property; if he is insane; if he is the first wrongdoer.

Mahānāmasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The seventh training rule concerning Mahānāma is concluded.
