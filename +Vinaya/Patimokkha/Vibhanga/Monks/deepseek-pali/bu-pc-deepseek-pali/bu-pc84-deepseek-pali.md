# BU-PC 84

## 2. Ratanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2.The training rule concerning jewels.

502. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu aciravatiyā nadiyā nahāyati. Aññataropi brāhmaṇo pañcasatānaṃ thavikaṃ thale nikkhipitvā aciravatiyā nadiyā nahāyanto vissaritvā agamāsi. Atha kho so bhikkhu – ‘‘tassāyaṃ brāhmaṇassa thavikā, mā idha nassī’’ti aggahesi. Atha kho so brāhmaṇo saritvā turito ādhāvitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘api me, bho, thavikaṃ passeyyāsī’’ti? ‘‘Handa, brāhmaṇā’’ti adāsi. Atha kho tassa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘kena nu kho ahaṃ upāyena imassa bhikkhuno puṇṇapattaṃ na dadeyya’’nti! ‘‘Na me, bho, pañcasatāni, sahassaṃ me’’ti palibundhetvā muñci. Atha kho so bhikkhu ārāmaṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhu ratanaṃ uggahessatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, ratanaṃ uggahesīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, ratanaṃ uggahessasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

502.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now on that occasion a certain bhikkhu was bathing in the Aciravatī River. A certain brahmin, having placed a bag of five hundred (coins) on the bank, forgot it while bathing in the Aciravatī River and went away. Then that bhikkhu thought, “This is that brahmin’s bag; let it not be lost here,” and he took it. Then that brahmin, remembering, quickly ran back and said to that bhikkhu, “Sir, have you seen my bag?” “Here, brahmin,” and he gave it to him. Then that brahmin thought, “By what means can I avoid giving this bhikkhu a full bowl?” “Sir, it was not five hundred, it was a thousand,” he lied and took it back. Then that bhikkhu, having gone to the monastery, told this matter to the bhikkhus. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, criticized, and spread it about: “How can a bhikkhu take a jewel?”… “Is it true, bhikkhu, that you took a jewel?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha rebuked him… “Foolish man, how can you take a jewel? This is not, foolish man, for inspiring confidence in the faithless…” And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu ratanaṃ vā ratanasammataṃ vā uggaṇheyya vā uggaṇhāpeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

“Should any bhikkhu take a jewel or what is considered a jewel, or cause it to be taken, he commits an offense entailing expiation.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

503. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyā ussavo hoti. Manussā alaṅkatappaṭiyattā uyyānaṃ gacchanti. Visākhāpi migāramātā alaṅkatappaṭiyattā ‘‘uyyānaṃ gamissāmī’’ti gāmato nikkhamitvā – ‘‘kyāhaṃ karissāmi uyyānaṃ gantvā, yaṃnūnāhaṃ bhagavantaṃ payirupāseyya’’nti ābharaṇaṃ omuñcitvā uttarāsaṅgena bhaṇḍikaṃ bandhitvā dāsiyā adāsi – ‘‘handa, je, imaṃ bhaṇḍikaṃ gaṇhāhī’’ti. Atha kho visākhā migāramātā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho visākhaṃ migāramātaraṃ bhagavā dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho visākhā migāramātā bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Atha kho sā dāsī taṃ bhaṇḍikaṃ vissaritvā agamāsi. Bhikkhū passitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. ‘‘Tena hi, bhikkhave, uggahetvā nikkhipathā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, ratanaṃ vā ratanasammataṃ vā ajjhārāme uggahetvā vā uggahāpetvā vā nikkhipituṃ – ‘‘yassa bhavissati so harissatī’’ti. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

503.Now at that time there was a festival in Sāvatthī. People, dressed up and adorned, were going to the park. Visākhā, Migāra’s mother, also dressed up and adorned, thought, “I’ll go to the park.” But after leaving the village, she reconsidered, “What will I do by going to the park? Why don’t I visit the Blessed One instead?” So she took off her ornaments, tied them up in her upper robe, and gave the bundle to her maid, saying, “Here, take this bundle, girl.” Then Visākhā, Migāra’s mother, approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. The Blessed One instructed, inspired, encouraged, and gladdened her with a Dhamma talk. Having been instructed, inspired, encouraged, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, she rose from her seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and left. Meanwhile, that maid forgot the bundle and went away. The bhikkhus saw it and reported the matter to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said, “Well then, bhikkhus, pick it up and set it aside.” Then, on this occasion, because of this incident, the Blessed One gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus, I allow you to pick up a jewel or what is considered a jewel, or have it picked up, within the monastery and set it aside, thinking, ‘Whoever owns it will take it.’ And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu ratanaṃ vā ratanasammataṃ vā, aññatra ajjhārāmā, uggaṇheyya vā uggaṇhāpeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

“Should any bhikkhu take a jewel or what is considered a jewel, or cause it to be taken, except within a monastery, he commits an offense entailing expiation.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

504. Tena kho pana samayena kāsīsu janapade anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa kammantagāmo hoti. Tena ca gahapatinā antevāsī āṇatto hoti – ‘‘sace bhadantā āgacchanti bhattaṃ kareyyāsī’’ti. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū kāsīsu janapade cārikaṃ caramānā yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa kammantagāmo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu. Addasā kho so puriso te bhikkhū dūratova āgacchante. Disvāna yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū abhivādetvā etadavoca – ‘‘adhivāsentu, bhante, ayyā svātanāya gahapatino bhatta’’nti. Adhivāsesuṃ kho te bhikkhū tuṇhībhāvena. Atha kho so puriso tassā rattiyā accayena paṇītaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ paṭiyādāpetvā kālaṃ ārocāpetvā aṅgulimuddikaṃ omuñcitvā te bhikkhū bhattena parivisitvā – ‘‘ayyā bhuñjitvā gacchantu, ahampi kammantaṃ gamissāmī’’ti aṅgulimuddikaṃ vissaritvā agamāsi. Bhikkhū passitvā – ‘‘sace mayaṃ gamissāma nassissatāyaṃ aṅgulimuddikā’’ti tattheva acchiṃsu. Atha kho so puriso kammantā āgacchanto te bhikkhū passitvā etadavoca – ‘‘kissa, bhante, ayyā idheva acchantī’’ti? Atha kho te bhikkhū tassa purisassa etamatthaṃ ārocetvā sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, ratanaṃ vā ratanasammataṃ vā ajjhārāme vā ajjhāvasathe vā uggahetvā vā uggahāpetvā vā nikkhipituṃ – yassa bhavissati so harissatī’’ti. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

504.Now at that time, in the Kāsi country, there was a work village belonging to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika. That householder had instructed his attendant: “If the venerable ones come, prepare a meal.” Now at that time, several bhikkhus, wandering in the Kāsi country, approached that work village of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika. That man saw the bhikkhus coming from afar. Having seen them, he approached the bhikkhus, paid homage to them, and said: “Venerable sirs, may the venerable ones accept the householder’s meal for tomorrow.” The bhikkhus consented by their silence. Then that man, after the night had passed, had fine foods prepared, announced the time, took off his signet ring, served the bhikkhus with the meal, and said: “Venerable sirs, eat and then go; I too will go to work.” Forgetting his signet ring, he left. The bhikkhus, seeing it, thought: “If we go, this signet ring will be lost,” so they stayed right there. Then that man, returning from work, saw the bhikkhus and said: “Why, venerable sirs, are the venerable ones still here?” Then those bhikkhus explained the matter to that man, went to Sāvatthī, and informed the bhikkhus about the matter. The bhikkhus informed the Blessed One about the matter. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in this connection, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus, I allow you to take a jewel or what is considered a jewel, or have it taken, within a monastery or dwelling, and to put it away, thinking: ‘Whoever owns it will take it.’ And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

505. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu ratanaṃ vā ratanasammataṃ vā, aññatra ajjhārāmā vā ajjhāvasathā vā, uggaṇheyya vā uggaṇhāpeyya vā, pācittiyaṃ. Ratanaṃ vā pana bhikkhunā ratanasammataṃ vā ajjhārāme vā ajjhāvasathe vā uggahetvā vā uggahāpetvā vā nikkhipitabbaṃ – ‘yassa bhavissati so harissatī’ti. Ayaṃ tattha sāmīcī’’ti.

505.Should any bhikkhu take or have taken a jewel or what is considered a jewel, except in a monastery or dwelling, it is a pācittiya. But a bhikkhu, having taken or having had taken a jewel or what is considered a jewel in a monastery or dwelling, should deposit it, thinking, ‘Whoever it belongs to will take it.’ This is the proper course of action in that case.

506. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

506.‘Yo pana’ means ‘whoever’… ‘bhikkhu’… in this case, the bhikkhu intended by this refers to this.

Ratanaṃ nāma muttā maṇi veḷuriyo saṅkho silā pavālaṃ rajataṃ jātarūpaṃ lohitaṅko [lohitako (?)] masāragallaṃ.

‘Jewel’ means pearl, gem, beryl, conch, rock crystal, coral, silver, gold, ruby, and cat’s eye.

Ratanasammataṃ nāma yaṃ manussānaṃ upabhogaparibhogaṃ, etaṃ ratanasammataṃ nāma.

‘What is considered a jewel’ means whatever people consider as an object of use or enjoyment; this is what is called ‘what is considered a jewel.’

Aññatra ajjhārāmā vā ajjhāvasathā vāti ṭhapetvā ajjhārāmaṃ ajjhāvasathaṃ.

‘Except in a monastery or dwelling’ means excluding a monastery or a dwelling.

Ajjhārāmo nāma parikkhittassa ārāmassa anto ārāmo, aparikkhittassa upacāro.

‘Monastery’ (ajjhārāma) means the interior of an enclosed monastery, or the vicinity of an unenclosed one.

Ajjhāvasatho nāma parikkhittassa āvasathassa anto āvasatho, aparikkhittassa upacāro.

‘Dwelling’ (ajjhāvasatha) means the interior of an enclosed dwelling, or the vicinity of an unenclosed one.

Uggaṇheyyāti sayaṃ gaṇhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘He takes’ means he takes it himself; there is an offense entailing confession.

Uggaṇhāpeyyāti aññaṃ gāhāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘He has taken’ means he causes another to take; there is an offense entailing confession.

Ratanaṃ vā pana bhikkhunā ratanasammataṃ vā ajjhārāme vā ajjhāvasathe vā uggahetvā vā uggahāpetvā vā nikkhipitabbanti rūpena vā nimittena vā saññāṇaṃ katvā nikkhipitvā ācikkhitabbaṃ – ‘‘yassa bhaṇḍaṃ naṭṭhaṃ so āgacchatū’’ti. Sace tattha āgacchati so vattabbo – ‘‘āvuso, kīdisaṃ te bhaṇḍa’’nti? Sace rūpena vā nimittena vā sampādeti dātabbaṃ, no ce sampādeti ‘‘vicināhi āvuso’’ti vattabbo. Tamhā āvāsā pakkamantena ye tattha honti bhikkhū patirūpā, tesaṃ hatthe nikkhipitvā pakkamitabbaṃ. No ce honti bhikkhū patirūpā, ye tattha honti gahapatikā patirūpā, tesaṃ hatthe nikkhipitvā pakkamitabbaṃ.

‘A bhikkhu should deposit a jewel or what is considered a jewel, having taken or had it taken in a monastery or dwelling’ means that having made a mark or sign by its appearance or distinguishing feature, he should deposit it and announce: ‘Let whoever’s property is lost come forward.’ If someone comes there, he should be asked: ‘Friend, what is your property like?’ If he describes it by appearance or distinguishing feature, it should be given to him. If he does not describe it, he should be told: ‘Search for it, friend.’ When leaving that residence, if there are suitable bhikkhus present, it should be placed in their hands before departing. If there are no suitable bhikkhus, it should be placed in the hands of suitable lay householders present before departing.

Ayaṃ tattha sāmīcīti ayaṃ tattha anudhammatā.

‘This is the proper course of action in that case’ means this is the conduct conforming to the Dhamma in that case.

507. Anāpatti ratanaṃ vā ratanasammataṃ vā ajjhārāme vā ajjhāvasathe vā uggahetvā vā uggahāpetvā vā nikkhipati – ‘‘yassa bhavissati so harissatī’’ti, ratanasammataṃ vissāsaṃ gaṇhāti, tāvakālikaṃ gaṇhāti, paṃsukūlasaññissa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

507.There is no offense for one who, in a monastery or dwelling, having taken or had taken a jewel or what is considered a jewel, deposits it, thinking, ‘Whoever it belongs to will take it.’ There is no offense for one who takes what is considered a jewel on trust, for one who takes it temporarily, for one who has the perception of rags, for one who is insane, or for one who is a first offender.

Ratanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The second training rule on jewels is concluded.
