# BU-PC 73

## 3. Mohanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 3.The Training Rule on Delusion.

443. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū anācāraṃ ācaritvā ‘‘aññāṇakena āpannāti jānantū’’ti pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadanti – ‘‘idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vakkhanti – idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadetha – ‘‘idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vakkhatha – ‘‘idāneva kho mayaṃ jānāma, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’’ti! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

443.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time, the group of six bhikkhus, having misbehaved, said this when the Pātimokkha was being recited, thinking: “Let them know that we have committed an offense through ignorance”: “Only now do we know that this rule too is included in the Suttas, comes up for recitation every half-month.” Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, murmured, and spread it about, saying: “How can the group of six bhikkhus say this when the Pātimokkha is being recited: ‘Only now do we know that this rule too is included in the Suttas, comes up for recitation every half-month’?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you say this when the Pātimokkha is being recited: ‘Only now do we know that this rule too is included in the Suttas, comes up for recitation every half-month’?” “It’s true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “Foolish men, how can you say this when the Pātimokkha is being recited: ‘Only now do we know that this rule too is included in the Suttas, comes up for recitation every half-month’? This, foolish men, is not for the confidence of the unconfident… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

444. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu anvaddhamāsaṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadeyya – ‘idāneva kho ahaṃ jānāmi, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’ti. Tañce bhikkhuṃ aññe bhikkhū jāneyyuṃ nisinnapubbaṃ iminā bhikkhunā dvattikkhattuṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne, ko pana vādo bhiyyo nti.

444.“If a bhikkhu, while the Pātimokkha is being recited every half-month, should say: ‘Only now do I understand that this Dhamma, come down in the Suttas, included in the Suttas, comes up for recitation every half-month,’ and other bhikkhus should know that this bhikkhu has sat down two or three times before while the Pātimokkha was being recited, not to speak of more—there is no exemption for that bhikkhu on grounds of ignorance. Whatever offense he has committed is to be dealt with according to the Dhamma, and furthermore, he is to be charged with delusion: ‘It is a loss for you, friend, it is an ill-gained thing for you, that you do not properly pay attention and take it to heart when the Pātimokkha is being recited.’ This is a pācittiya in the case of such delusion.”

445. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

445.‘Whatever’ means: whatever kind of… ‘bhikkhu’ means:… In this context, ‘bhikkhu’ is intended.

Anvaddhamāsanti anuposathikaṃ.

‘Every half-month’ means: on every Uposatha day.

Pātimokkhe uddissamāneti uddisante.

‘When the Pātimokkha is being recited’ means: while it is being recited.

Evaṃ vadeyyāti anācāraṃ ācaritvā – ‘‘aññāṇakena āpannoti jānantū’’ti pātimokkhe uddissamāne evaṃ vadeti – ‘‘idāneva kho ahaṃ jānāmi, ayampi kira dhammo suttāgato suttapariyāpanno anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchatī’’ti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

‘Speaks thus’ means: having engaged in improper conduct, he says, when the Pātimokkha is being recited, ‘Let them know I have fallen into this unknowingly,’ and speaks thus: ‘Only now do I know that this too is a Dhamma come down in the Suttas, included in the Suttas, and comes up for recitation every half-month.’ There is an offense of wrong-doing.

Tañce mohetukāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ aññe bhikkhū jāneyyuṃ nisinnapubbaṃ iminā bhikkhunā dvattikkhattuṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne, ko pana vādo bhiyyo, na ca tassa bhikkhuno aññāṇakena mutti atthi, yañca tattha āpattiṃ āpanno, tañca yathādhammo kāretabbo, uttari cassa moho āropetabbo. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, āropetabbo. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

If other bhikkhus know that a bhikkhu, desiring to delude, has previously sat through the recitation of the Pātimokkha two or three times, not to mention more often, then there is no exemption for that bhikkhu on grounds of unknowing. Whatever offense he has committed is to be dealt with according to the Dhamma, and furthermore, he is to be charged with delusion. And thus, bhikkhus, he should be charged: A competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Saṅgha:

446. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu pātimokkhe uddissamāne na sādhukaṃ aṭṭhiṃ katvā manasi karoti. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno mohaṃ āropeyya. Esā ñatti.

446.“Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, while the Pātimokkha is being recited, does not properly pay attention and take it to heart. If the Saṅgha is ready, the Saṅgha may charge the bhikkhu named so-and-so with delusion. This is the motion.”

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Ayaṃ itthannāmo bhikkhu pātimokkhe uddissamāne na sādhukaṃ aṭṭhiṃ katvā manasi karoti. Saṅgho itthannāmassa bhikkhuno mohaṃ āropeti. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno mohassa āropanā, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sirs, may the Saṅgha hear me. This bhikkhu, named so-and-so, while the Pātimokkha is being recited, does not properly pay attention and take it to heart. The Saṅgha charges the bhikkhu named so-and-so with delusion. Any venerable one who approves of charging the bhikkhu named so-and-so with delusion should remain silent; any who disapproves may speak.”

‘‘Āropito saṅghena itthannāmassa bhikkhuno moho. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The Saṅgha has charged the bhikkhu named so-and-so with delusion. The Saṅgha approves, therefore it is silent. Thus I hold it.”

Anāropite mohe moheti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Āropite mohe moheti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

If, when delusion has not been charged, he acts deludedly, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, when delusion has been charged, he acts deludedly, there is an offense entailing confession.

447. Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme vematiko, āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī moheti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

447.If it is a legitimate legal procedure (of charging with delusion), and he perceives it as such, there is an offense entailing confession. If it is a legitimate legal procedure (of charging with delusion), and he is in doubt, there is an offense entailing confession. If it is a legitimate legal procedure (of charging with delusion), but he perceives it as an illegitimate legal procedure, he acts deludedly, there is an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If it is an illegitimate legal procedure (of charging with delusion), and he perceives it as a legitimate legal procedure, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure (of charging with delusion), and he is in doubt, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is an illegitimate legal procedure (of charging with delusion), and he perceives it as an illegitimate legal procedure, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

448. Anāpatti na vitthārena sutaṃ hoti, ūnakadvattikkhattuṃ vitthārena sutaṃ hoti, na mohetukāmassa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

448.There is no offense if it has not been heard in detail, if it has been heard in detail less than two or three times, if he does not desire to delude, if he is insane, or if he is the first offender.

Mohanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The third training rule on delusion is concluded.
