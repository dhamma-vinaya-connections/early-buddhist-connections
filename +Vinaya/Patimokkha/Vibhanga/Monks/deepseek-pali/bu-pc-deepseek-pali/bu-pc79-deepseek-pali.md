# BU-PC 79

## 9. Kammapaṭibāhanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 9.The Training Rule on the Rejection of a Sangha Act.

474. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū anācāraṃ ācaritvā ekamekassa kamme kayiramāne paṭikkosanti. Tena kho pana samayena saṅgho sannipatito hoti kenacideva karaṇīyena. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū cīvarakammaṃ karontā ekassa chandaṃ adaṃsu. Atha kho saṅgho – ‘‘ayaṃ, āvuso, chabbaggiyo bhikkhu ekako āgato, handassa mayaṃ kammaṃ karomā’’ti tassa kammaṃ akāsi. Atha kho so bhikkhu yena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami. Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kiṃ, āvuso, saṅgho akāsī’’ti? ‘‘Saṅgho me, āvuso, kammaṃ akāsī’’ti. ‘‘Na mayaṃ, āvuso, etadatthāya chandaṃ adamhā – ‘‘tuyhaṃ kammaṃ karissatī’’ti. Sace ca mayaṃ jāneyyāma ‘‘tuyhaṃ kammaṃ karissatī’’ti, na mayaṃ chandaṃ dadeyyāmā’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū dhammikānaṃ kammānaṃ chandaṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ [khiyyadhammaṃ (itipi)] āpajjissantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, dhammikānaṃ kammānaṃ chandaṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ [khiyyadhammaṃ (itipi)] āpajjathāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, dhammikānaṃ kammānaṃ chandaṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ [khiyyadhammaṃ (itipi)] āpajjissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

474.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time, the group of six bhikkhus, having engaged in misconduct, objected to each act being performed. At that time, the Sangha had gathered for some business. The group of six bhikkhus, while engaged in robe-making, gave their consent to one bhikkhu. Then the Sangha thought, “This bhikkhu from the group of six has come alone. Let us perform the act for him.” So they performed the act for him. Then that bhikkhu approached the group of six bhikkhus. The group of six bhikkhus said to him, “What did the Sangha do, friend?” “The Sangha performed an act for me, friend.” “We did not give our consent for that purpose, friend, thinking, ‘They will perform an act for you.’ If we had known, ‘They will perform an act for you,’ we would not have given our consent.” Those bhikkhus who were of few desires… complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How can the group of six bhikkhus, after giving their consent to legitimate acts, later fall into a state of regret?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you gave your consent to legitimate acts and later fell into a state of regret?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “Foolish men, how can you give your consent to legitimate acts and later fall into a state of regret? This is not for the confidence of those who are not yet confident, nor for the increase of confidence in those who are confident… Therefore, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:

475. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu dhammikānaṃ kammānaṃ chandaṃ datvā pacchā khīyanadhammaṃ āpajjeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

475.“If a bhikkhu, having given his consent to legitimate acts, afterwards falls into a state of regret, he commits an offense entailing confession.”

476. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

476.‘Yo pana’ means: whoever, of whatever sort… ‘bhikkhu’ means… in this context, the bhikkhu intended is this one.

Dhammikaṃ nāma kammaṃ apalokanakammaṃ ñattikammaṃ ñattidutiyakammaṃ ñatticatutthakammaṃ dhammena vinayena satthusāsanena kataṃ, etaṃ dhammikaṃ nāma kammaṃ. Chandaṃ datvā khiyyati āpatti pācittiyassa.

A legitimate act is one that is done according to the Dhamma, the Discipline, and the Teacher’s instruction, such as an act requiring leave, a motion act, a motion-and-second act, or a motion-and-fourth act. This is called a legitimate act. If, having given consent, one regrets it, there is an offense entailing confession.

477. Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī chandaṃ datvā khiyyati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme vematiko chandaṃ datvā khiyyati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī chandaṃ datvā khiyyati, anāpatti. Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, anāpatti.

477.In a legitimate act, perceiving it as legitimate, if one gives consent and afterwards regrets it, there is an offense entailing confession. In a legitimate act, being uncertain, if one gives consent and afterwards regrets it, there is a dukkaṭa offense. In a legitimate act, perceiving it as illegitimate, if one gives consent and afterwards regrets it, there is no offense. In an illegitimate act, perceiving it as legitimate, there is a dukkaṭa offense. In an illegitimate act, being uncertain, there is a dukkaṭa offense. In an illegitimate act, perceiving it as illegitimate, there is no offense.

478. Anāpatti – ‘‘adhammena vā vaggena vā na kammārahassa vā kammaṃ kata’’nti jānanto khiyyati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

478.No offense: if, knowing that “the act was done unlawfully, or by a faction, or by those unfit for the act,” one regrets it; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Kammapaṭibāhanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The Ninth Training Rule on the Rejection of a Sangha Act is finished.
