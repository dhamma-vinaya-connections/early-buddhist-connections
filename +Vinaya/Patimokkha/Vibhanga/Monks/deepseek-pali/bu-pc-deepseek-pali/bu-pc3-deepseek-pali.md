# BU-PC 3

## 3. Pesuññasikkhāpadaṃ

## 3.The training rule concerning divisive speech.

36. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ bhaṇḍanajātānaṃ kalahajātānaṃ vivādāpannānaṃ pesuññaṃ upasaṃharanti; imassa sutvā amussa akkhāyanti, imassa bhedāya; amussa sutvā imassa akkhāyanti, amussa bhedāya. Tena anuppannāni ceva bhaṇḍanāni uppajjanti, uppannāni ca bhaṇḍanāni bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya saṃvattanti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhūnaṃ bhaṇḍanajātānaṃ kalahajātānaṃ vivādāpannānaṃ pesuññaṃ upasaṃharissanti, imassa sutvā amussa akkhāyissanti, imassa bhedāya; amussa sutvā imassa akkhāyissanti, amussa bhedāya! Tena anuppannāni ceva bhaṇḍanāni uppajjanti, uppannāni ca bhaṇḍanāni bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya saṃvattantī’’ti. Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ bhaṇḍanajātānaṃ kalahajātānaṃ vivādāpannānaṃ pesuññaṃ upasaṃharatha, imassa sutvā amussa akkhāyatha, imassa bhedāya, amussa sutvā imassa akkhāyatha, amussa bhedāya? Tena anuppannāni ceva bhaṇḍanāni uppajjanti, uppannāni ca bhaṇḍanāni bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya saṃvattantī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, bhikkhūnaṃ bhaṇḍanajātānaṃ kalahajātānaṃ vivādāpannānaṃ pesuññaṃ upasaṃharissatha! Imassa sutvā amussa akkhāyissatha, imassa bhedāya! Amussa sutvā imassa akkhāyissatha, amussa bhedāya! Tena anuppannāni ceva bhaṇḍanāni uppajjanti, uppannāni ca bhaṇḍanāni bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya saṃvattanti. Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya. Pasannānaṃ vā bhiyyobhāvāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

36.At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was staying at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery. Now at that time, the bhikkhus of the group of six were conveying divisive speech among bhikkhus who were quarreling, disputing, and engaged in arguments—telling what they heard from one to another to create division, and telling what they heard from another to the first to create division. Thus, quarrels that had not arisen now arose, and those that had arisen grew worse and more extensive. The bhikkhus who were modest… criticized and complained: “How can the bhikkhus of the group of six convey divisive speech among bhikkhus who are quarreling, disputing, and engaged in arguments—telling what they heard from one to another to create division, and telling what they heard from another to the first to create division? Thus, quarrels that had not arisen now arise, and those that had arisen grow worse and more extensive!” Then those bhikkhus, having criticized the bhikkhus of the group of six in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you are conveying divisive speech among bhikkhus who are quarreling, disputing, and engaged in arguments—telling what you heard from one to another to create division, and telling what you heard from another to the first to create division? Thus, quarrels that had not arisen now arise, and those that had arisen grow worse and more extensive?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha rebuked them… “Foolish men, how can you convey divisive speech among bhikkhus who are quarreling, disputing, and engaged in arguments—telling what you heard from one to another to create division, and telling what you heard from another to the first to create division? Thus, quarrels that had not arisen now arise, and those that had arisen grow worse and more extensive. This will not inspire confidence in those without it or increase confidence in those who have it…” …And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

37. ‘‘Bhikkhupesuññe pācittiya’’nti.

37.Divisive speech concerning bhikkhus is an offense entailing confession.

38. Pesuññaṃ nāma dvīhākārehi pesuññaṃ hoti – piyakamyassa vā bhedādhippāyassa vā. Dasahākārehi pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – jātitopi, nāmatopi, gottatopi, kammatopi, sippatopi, ābādhatopi, liṅgatopi, kilesatopi, āpattitopi, akkosatopi.

38.Divisive speech is of two kinds: from a desire for affection or with the intention to create division. One conveys divisive speech in ten ways: regarding birth, regarding name, regarding clan, regarding occupation, regarding craft, regarding illness, regarding characteristic, regarding defilement, regarding offense, or regarding abuse.

Jāti nāma dve jātiyo – hīnā ca jāti ukkaṭṭhā ca jāti. Hīnā nāma jāti – caṇḍālajāti venajāti nesādajāti rathakārajāti pukkusajāti. Esā hīnā nāma jāti. Ukkaṭṭhā nāma jāti – khattiyajāti brāhmaṇajāti. Esā ukkaṭṭhā nāma jāti…pe….

Birth is of two kinds: low birth and superior birth. Low birth is birth as a caṇḍāla, a veṇa, a nesada, a rathakāra, or a pukkusa. This is low birth. Superior birth is birth as a khattiya or a brahmin. This is superior birth…pe….

Akkoso nāma dve akkosā – hīno ca akkoso ukkaṭṭho ca akkoso. Hīno nāma akkoso – oṭṭhosi, meṇḍosi, goṇosi, gadrabhosi, tiracchānagatosi, nerayikosi; natthi tuyhaṃ sugati; duggati yeva tuyhaṃ pāṭikaṅkhāti, yakārena vā bhakārena vā kāṭakoṭacikāya vā. Eso hīno nāma akkoso. Ukkaṭṭho nāma akkoso – paṇḍitosi, byattosi, medhāvīsi, bahussutosi, dhammakathikosi; natthi tuyhaṃ duggati; sugati yeva tuyhaṃ pāṭikaṅkhāti. Eso ukkaṭṭho nāma akkoso.

Abuse is of two kinds: low abuse and high abuse. Low abuse is this: “You are a camel, a ram, an ox, a donkey, an animal, a hell-being; you have no good destination; only a bad destination awaits you,” or by using terms like “yak,” “bhaka,” or “kāṭakoṭacikā.” This is low abuse. High abuse is this: “You are wise, clever, intelligent, learned, a Dhamma speaker; you have no bad destination; only a good destination awaits you.” This is high abuse.

39. Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘caṇḍālo veno nesādo rathakāro pukkuso’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

39.If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you an outcast, a bamboo worker, a hunter, a cartwright, or a scavenger,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘khattiyo brāhmaṇo’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you a noble, a brahmin,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘avakaṇṇako javakaṇṇako dhaniṭṭhako saviṭṭhako kulavaḍḍhako’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you avakaṇṇako, javakaṇṇako, dhaniṭṭhako, saviṭṭhako, kulavaḍḍhako,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘buddharakkhito dhammarakkhito saṅgharakkhito’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you Buddharakkhita, Dhammarakkhita, Saṅgharakkhita,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘kosiyo bhāradvājo’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you Kosiya Bhāradvāja,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘gotamo moggallāno kaccāno vāsiṭṭho’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you Gotama, Moggallāna, Kaccāna, or Vāsiṭṭha,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘koṭṭhako pupphachaḍḍako’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you koṭṭhako pupphachaḍḍako,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘kassako vāṇijo gorakkho’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you a farmer, a merchant, or a cowherd,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘naḷakāro kumbhakāro pesakāro cammakāro nahāpito’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you a reed-worker, a potter, a weaver, a leather-worker, or a barber,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘muddiko gaṇako lekhako’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you an accountant, a reckoner, or a scribe,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

40. Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘kuṭṭhiko gaṇḍiko kilāsiko sosiko apamāriko’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

40.If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you a leper, a boil-covered person, a skin-diseased person, a consumptive, or an epileptic,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘madhumehiko’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you a diabetic,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘atidīgho atirasso atikaṇho accodāto’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you too tall, too short, too dark, or too fair,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘nātidīgho nātirasso nātikaṇho nāccodāto’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you not too tall, not too short, not too dark, or not too fair,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘rāgapariyuṭṭhito dosapariyuṭṭhito mohapariyuṭṭhito’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you overwhelmed by lust, overwhelmed by hate, or overwhelmed by delusion,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘vītarāgo vītadoso vītamoho’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you without lust, without hate, or without delusion,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘pārājikaṃ ajjhāpanno, saṅghādisesaṃ ajjhāpanno, thullaccayaṃ ajjhāpanno, pācittiyaṃ ajjhāpanno, pāṭidesanīyaṃ ajjhāpanno, dukkaṭaṃ ajjhāpanno, dubbhāsitaṃ ajjhāpanno’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you one who has committed a pārājika offense, a saṅghādisesa offense, a thullaccaya offense, a pācittiya offense, a pāṭidesanīya offense, a dukkaṭa offense, or a dubbhāsita offense,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘sotāpanno’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you a stream-enterer,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘oṭṭho meṇḍo goṇo gadrabho tiracchānagato nerayiko, natthi tassa sugati, duggatiyeva tassa pāṭikaṅkhā’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you a camel, a ram, an ox, a donkey, a creature of the animal realm, or a hell-dweller; for you there is no happy destination, only a painful destination is to be expected,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo taṃ ‘paṇḍito byatto medhāvī bahussuto dhammakathiko, natthi tassa duggati, sugati yeva tassa pāṭikaṅkhā’ti bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so calls you wise, accomplished, intelligent, learned, or a Dhamma speaker; for you there is no painful destination, only a happy destination is to be expected,” there is an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

41. Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo ‘santi idhekacce caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā, pukkusā’ti bhaṇati, na so aññaṃ bhaṇati, taññeva bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

41.If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so says: ‘There are here certain outcastes, bamboo workers, hunters, cartwrights, or scavengers,’ and he says nothing else, he says just that,” there is an offense of wrong conduct for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo ‘santi idhekacce khattiyā brāhmaṇā’ti bhaṇati, na so aññaṃ bhaṇati, taññeva bhaṇatī’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa…pe….

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so says: ‘There are here some nobles or brahmins,’ and he says nothing else, he says just that,” there is an offense of wrong conduct for each utterance…pe….

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo ‘santi idhekacce paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā, natthi tesaṃ duggati, sugati yeva tesaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’ti bhaṇati, na so aññaṃ bhaṇati, taññeva bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys divisive speech concerning him, saying, “So-and-so says: ‘There are here some wise, accomplished, intelligent, learned, or Dhamma speakers; for them there is no painful destination, only a happy destination is to be expected,’ and he says nothing else, he says just that,” there is an offense of wrong conduct for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo ‘ye nūna caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’ti bhaṇati, na so aññaṃ bhaṇati, taññeva bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys tale-bearing about another fully ordained bhikkhu, saying: “So-and-so says, ‘They are surely outcasts, bamboo workers, hunters, chariot-makers, or refuse collectors,’ and he says nothing else; he says just that.” For each utterance, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo ‘ye nūna paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā’ti bhaṇati, na so aññaṃ bhaṇati, taññeva bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys tale-bearing about another fully ordained bhikkhu, saying: “So-and-so says, ‘Surely they are wise, articulate, intelligent, learned, Dhamma-speakers,’ and he says nothing else; he says just that.” For each utterance, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo ‘na mayaṃ caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’ti bhaṇati, na so aññaṃ bhaṇati, taññeva bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys tale-bearing about another fully ordained bhikkhu, saying: “So-and-so says, ‘We are not outcasts, bamboo workers, hunters, chariot-makers, or refuse collectors,’ and he says nothing else; he says just that.” For each utterance, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati – ‘‘itthannāmo ‘na mayaṃ paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā, natthamhākaṃ duggati, sugati yeva amhākaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’ti bhaṇati, na so aññaṃ bhaṇati, taññeva bhaṇatī’’ti. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys tale-bearing about another fully ordained bhikkhu, saying: “So-and-so says, ‘We are not wise, articulate, intelligent, learned, Dhamma-speakers; we have no bad destination, only a good destination is to be expected for us,’ and he says nothing else; he says just that.” For each utterance, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

42. Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati; āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

42.A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys tale-bearing about another fully ordained bhikkhu; for each utterance, there is an offense entailing confession.

Upasampanno upasampannassa sutvā anupasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from another fully ordained bhikkhu, conveys tale-bearing about one who is not fully ordained; there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Upasampanno anupasampannassa sutvā upasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from one who is not fully ordained, conveys tale-bearing about another fully ordained bhikkhu; there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Upasampanno anupasampannassa sutvā anupasampannassa pesuññaṃ upasaṃharati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, having heard from one who is not fully ordained, conveys tale-bearing about one who is not fully ordained; there is an offense of wrong conduct.

43. Anāpatti napiyakamyassa, nabhedādhippāyassa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

43.There is no offense for one who has no desire for what is disagreeable, nor for one whose intention is not division, nor for one who is insane, nor for one who is the first offender.

Pesuññasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The third training rule on tale-bearing is concluded.
