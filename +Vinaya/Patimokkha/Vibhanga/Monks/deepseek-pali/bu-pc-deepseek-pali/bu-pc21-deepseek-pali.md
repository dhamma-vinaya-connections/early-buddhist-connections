# BU-PC 21

## 1. Ovādasikkhāpadaṃ

## 1.The Training Rule on Giving Admonition

144. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena therā bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo ovadantā lābhino honti cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘etarahi kho, āvuso, therā bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo ovadantā lābhino honti cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Handāvuso, mayampi bhikkhuniyo ovadāmā’’ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ – ‘‘amhepi, bhaginiyo, upasaṅkamatha; mayampi ovadissāmā’’ti.

144.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now at that time the senior bhikkhus, when instructing the nuns, received gains of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. Then the bhikkhus of the group of six thought, “At present, friends, the senior bhikkhus, when instructing the nuns, receive gains of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. Come, friends, let us also instruct the nuns.” Then the bhikkhus of the group of six approached the nuns and said, “Sisters, come to us; we will also instruct you.”

Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo yena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā chabbaggiye bhikkhū abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhunīnaṃ parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojesuṃ – ‘‘gacchatha, bhaginiyo’’ti. Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, bhikkhuniyo, ovādo iddho ahosī’’ti? ‘‘Kuto, bhante, ovādo iddho bhavissati! Ayyā chabbaggiyā parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojesu’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā tā bhikkhuniyo dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkamiṃsu.

Then those nuns approached the group of six bhikkhus. Having approached, they paid homage to the group of six bhikkhus and sat down to one side. Then the group of six bhikkhus gave the nuns only a brief talk on Dhamma and spent the day in animal talk before dismissing them, saying, "Go, sisters." Then those nuns approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, the Blessed One said to them, "Nuns, was the instruction successful?" "How, venerable sir, could the instruction be successful! The group of six bhikkhus gave us only a brief talk on Dhamma and spent the day in animal talk before dismissing us." Then the Blessed One instructed, aroused, and gladdened those nuns with a talk on Dhamma. Then, having been instructed, aroused, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a talk on Dhamma, those nuns paid homage to him, circumambulated him keeping him on their right, and departed.

Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā chabbaggiye bhikkhū paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, bhikkhunīnaṃ parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, bhikkhunīnaṃ parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammannituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbo. Paṭhamaṃ bhikkhu yācitabbo. Yācitvā byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

Then the Blessed One, on that occasion, in connection with that matter, having convened the Sangha of bhikkhus, questioned the bhikkhus of the group of six: "Is it true, bhikkhus, that after giving the nuns just a brief talk on Dhamma, you spent the day engaged in animal talk and then dismissed them?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Blessed One rebuked them…: "How could you, foolish men, after giving the nuns just a brief talk on Dhamma, spend the day engaged in animal talk and then dismiss them! This is not, foolish men, for the pleasing of those who are not (yet) pleased…" Having rebuked them… he then, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: "I allow, bhikkhus, the appointment of a bhikkhu to instruct nuns. And thus, bhikkhus, he should be appointed. First, a bhikkhu should be requested. Having requested, an experienced and capable bhikkhu should inform the Sangha:

145. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammanneyya. Esā ñatti.

145."Venerable sir, may the Sangha hear me. If the Sangha is ready, the Sangha should appoint the bhikkhu named so-and-so as an instructor of nuns. This is the motion."

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno bhikkhunovādakassa sammuti, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

"Venerable sir, let the Sangha hear me. The Sangha appoints the bhikkhu so-and-so as an instructor of nuns. If any venerable approves of the appointment of the bhikkhu so-and-so as an instructor of nuns, let him remain silent; if any does not approve, let him speak."

‘‘Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi…pe… tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi – suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno bhikkhunovādakassa sammuti, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

For the second time, I speak on this matter... For the third time, I speak on this matter—Venerable sir, let the Sangha hear me. The Sangha appoints the bhikkhu named so-and-so as an instructor of nuns. If any venerable approves of the appointment of the bhikkhu named so-and-so as an instructor of nuns, let him remain silent; if any does not approve, let him speak."

‘‘Sammato saṅghena itthannāmo bhikkhu bhikkhunovādako. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

The bhikkhu named so-and-so has been appointed by the Sangha as an instructor of nuns. The Sangha approves of it, and therefore it is silent. Thus I understand it to be."

Atha kho bhagavā chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One, having rebuked the bhikkhus of the group of six in many ways for their being difficult to support... And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

146. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu asammato bhikkhuniyo ovadeyya pācittiya’’nti.

146."Whatever bhikkhu, not being authorized, should instruct nuns, is to be confessed."

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

147. Tena kho pana samayena therā bhikkhū sammatā bhikkhuniyo ovadantā tatheva lābhino honti cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘etarahi kho, āvuso, therā bhikkhū sammatā bhikkhuniyo ovadantā tatheva lābhino honti cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Handāvuso, mayampi nissīmaṃ gantvā aññamaññaṃ bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammannitvā bhikkhuniyo ovadāmā’’ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū nissīmaṃ gantvā aññamaññaṃ bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammannitvā bhikkhuniyo upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mayampi, bhaginiyo, sammatā. Amhepi upasaṅkamatha. Mayampi ovadissāmā’’ti.

147.Now at that time the elder bhikkhus, being authorized to advise the nuns, continued to receive gains of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. Then the bhikkhus of the group of six thought, "At present, friends, the elder bhikkhus authorized to advise the nuns continue to receive gains of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. Come, friends, let us also go outside the boundary, authorize each other as instructors of nuns, and instruct the nuns." Then the bhikkhus of the group of six went outside the boundary, authorized each other as instructors of nuns, approached the nuns, and said, "Sisters, we too are authorized. Approach us as well. We too will instruct you."

Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo yena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā chabbaggiye bhikkhū abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū bhikkhunīnaṃ parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojesuṃ – ‘‘gacchatha bhaginiyo’’ti. Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, bhikkhuniyo, ovādo iddho ahosī’’ti? ‘‘Kuto, bhante, ovādo iddho bhavissati! Ayyā chabbaggiyā parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojesu’’nti.

Then those nuns approached the group of six bhikkhus. Having approached, they paid homage to the group of six bhikkhus and sat down to one side. Then the group of six bhikkhus gave the nuns only a brief talk on Dhamma and spent the day in animal talk before dismissing them, saying, "Go, sisters." Then those nuns approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, the Blessed One said to them: "Nuns, was the instruction successful?" "How, venerable sir, could the instruction be successful! The group of six bhikkhus gave us only a brief talk on Dhamma and spent the day in animal talk before dismissing us."

Atha kho bhagavā tā bhikkhuniyo dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi…pe… atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkamiṃsu. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā chabbaggiye bhikkhū paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, bhikkhunīnaṃ parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, bhikkhunīnaṃ parittaññeva dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā divasaṃ tiracchānakathāya vītināmetvā uyyojessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgataṃ bhikkhuṃ bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammannituṃ. Sīlavā hoti, pātimokkhasaṃvarasaṃvuto viharati ācāragocarasampanno aṇumattesu vajjesu bhayadassāvī, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu; bahussuto hoti sutadharo sutasannicayo, ye te dhammā ādikalyāṇā majjhekalyāṇā pariyosānakalyāṇā sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ abhivadanti tathārūpāssa dhammā bahussutā honti dhātā vacasā paricitā manasā anupekkhitā diṭṭhiyā suppaṭividdhā; ubhayāni kho panassa pātimokkhāni vitthārena svāgatāni honti suvibhattāni suppavattīni suvinicchitāni suttaso anubyañjanaso; kalyāṇavāco hoti kalyāṇavākkaraṇo; yebhuyyena bhikkhunīnaṃ piyo hoti manāpo; paṭibalo hoti bhikkhuniyo ovadituṃ; na kho panetaṃ [pana taṃ (?)] bhagavantaṃ uddissa pabbajitāya kāsāyavatthavasanāya garudhammaṃ ajjhāpannapubbo hoti; vīsativasso vā hoti atirekavīsativasso vā – anujānāmi, bhikkhave, imehi aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgataṃ bhikkhuṃ bhikkhunovādakaṃ sammannitu’’nti.

Then the Blessed One instructed, urged, roused, and encouraged those nuns with a Dhamma talk… Then those nuns, having been instructed, urged, roused, and encouraged by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, paid homage to him, circumambulated him, keeping him to their right, and departed.Then the Blessed One, on that occasion, in connection with that matter, having convened the Sangha of bhikkhus, questioned the bhikkhus of the group of six: "Is it true, bhikkhus, that after giving just a brief Dhamma talk to the nuns, you spent the day engaged in animal talk and then dismissed them?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Blessed One rebuked them… "How could you, foolish men, after giving just a brief Dhamma talk to the nuns, spend the day engaged in animal talk and then dismiss them? This is not for the pleasing of those who are not (yet) pleased…." Having rebuked them… he gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus: "bhikkhus, I allow the appointment of a bhikkhu endowed with eight qualities as an instructor of nuns. He is virtuous, he dwells restrained by the Pātimokkha, perfect in conduct and resort, seeing danger in the slightest faults, and he trains by undertaking the training rules. He is learned, a bearer of the teachings, an accumulator of what he has heard. Those teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with the right meaning and phrasing, which proclaim the perfectly complete and pure holy life—such teachings he has learned much of, retained, recited verbally, mentally investigated, and penetrated well by view. Both Pātimokkhas have been well received by him in detail, well analyzed, well mastered, well determined in terms of the wording and their detailed explanations. He is a good speaker with a good way of speaking. He is usually pleasing and agreeable to the nuns. He is capable of instructing the nuns. He has never before committed a weighty offense against a woman gone forth under the Blessed One, wearing the ochre robe. He is twenty years or more in seniority. bhikkhus, I allow the appointment of a bhikkhu endowed with these eight qualities as an instructor of nuns."

148. Yo  panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

148.‘Whatever bhikkhu’ means whoever, of whatever sort… ‘bhikkhu’… in this sense, ‘bhikkhu’ is intended.

Asammato nāma ñatticatutthena kammena asammato.

Unapproved means not approved by a motion-and-four-proclamations formal act.

Bhikkhuniyo nāma ubhatosaṅghe upasampannā.

Nuns are those ordained in both communities.

Ovadeyyāti aṭṭhahi garudhammehi ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Aññena dhammena ovadati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ekatoupasampannaṃ ovadati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

To instruct means: if one instructs with the eight weighty rules, it is an offense entailing confession. If one instructs with another teaching, it is an offense of wrong doing. If one instructs a nun ordained in only one community, it is an offense of wrong doing.

149. Tena sammatena bhikkhunā pariveṇaṃ sammajjitvā pānīyaṃ paribhojanīyaṃ upaṭṭhāpetvā āsanaṃ paññapetvā dutiyaṃ gahetvā nisīditabbaṃ. Bhikkhunīhi tattha gantvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīditabbaṃ. Tena bhikkhunā pucchitabbā – ‘‘samaggāttha, bhaginiyo’’ti? Sace ‘‘samaggāmhāyyā’’ti bhaṇanti, ‘‘vattanti, bhaginiyo, aṭṭha garudhammā’’ti? Sace ‘‘vattantāyyā’’ti bhaṇanti, ‘‘eso, bhaginiyo, ovādo’’ti niyyādetabbo [niyyātetabbo (itipi)]. Sace ‘‘na vattantāyyā’’ti bhaṇanti, osāretabbā. ‘‘Vassasatūpasampannāya bhikkhuniyā tadahupasampannassa bhikkhuno abhivādanaṃ paccuṭṭhānaṃ añjalikammaṃ sāmīcikammaṃ kātabbaṃ; ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. Na bhikkhuniyā abhikkhuke āvāse vassaṃ vasitabbaṃ; ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo. Anvaddhamāsaṃ bhikkhuniyā bhikkhusaṅghato dve dhammā paccāsīsitabbā [paccāsiṃsitabbā (itipi)] uposathapucchakañca ovādupasaṅkamanañca, ayampi dhammo…pe… vassaṃ vuṭṭhāya bhikkhuniyā ubhatosaṅghe tīhi ṭhānehi pavāretabbaṃ diṭṭhena vā sutena vā parisaṅkāya vā; ayampi dhammo…pe… garudhammaṃ ajjhāpannāya bhikkhuniyā ubhatosaṅghe pakkhamānattaṃ caritabbaṃ; ayampi dhammo…pe… dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhitasikkhāya sikkhamānāya ubhatosaṅghe upasampadā pariyesitabbā; ayampi dhammo…pe… na bhikkhuniyā kena ci pariyāyena bhikkhu akkositabbo paribhāsitabbo; ayampi dhammo…pe… ajjatagge ovaṭo bhikkhunīnaṃ bhikkhūsu vacanapatho, anovaṭo bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīsu vacanapatho; ayampi dhammo sakkatvā garukatvā mānetvā pūjetvā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyo’’ti.

149.The appointed bhikkhu should sweep the dwelling, set out drinking water and water for use, prepare a seat, take a companion, and sit down. The nuns should go there, pay respects to that bhikkhu, and sit to one side. The bhikkhu should ask them: "Sisters, are you in harmony?" If they say, "We are in harmony, venerable sir," he should ask, "Sisters, are the eight weighty rules being followed?" If they say, "They are being followed, venerable sir," he should present the advice: "Sisters, this is the advice." If they say, "They are not being followed, venerable sir," he should recite the rules. "A nun who has been ordained for a hundred years should pay respects, rise up, greet with joined palms, and properly attend to a bhikkhu ordained that very day. This rule too should be honored, respected, esteemed, and revered, and not transgressed for life. A nun should not spend the rains in a residence without bhikkhus. This rule too should be honored, respected, esteemed, and revered, and not transgressed for life. Every half-month, nuns should seek two things from the community of bhikkhus: asking about the observance day and approaching for instruction. This rule too... After the rains, nuns should invite criticism from both communities regarding three matters: what was seen, heard, or suspected. This rule too... A nun who has violated a weighty rule should undergo penance for half a month in both communities. This rule too... A trainee who has trained for two years in the six rules should seek full ordination in both communities. This rule too... A nun should not, in any way, revile or abuse a bhikkhu. This rule too... From this day forward, nuns are prohibited from rebuking bhikkhus, but bhikkhus are not prohibited from rebuking nuns. This rule too should be honored, respected, esteemed, and revered, and not transgressed for life."

Sace ‘‘samaggāmhāyyā’’ti bhaṇantaṃ aññaṃ dhammaṃ bhaṇati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Sace ‘‘vaggāmhāyyā’’ti bhaṇantaṃ aṭṭha garudhamme bhaṇati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ovādaṃ aniyyādetvā aññaṃ dhammaṃ bhaṇati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If, when they say, "We are in harmony, venerable sir," he speaks of another teaching, it is an offense of wrong doing. If, when they say, "We are in discord, venerable sir," he speaks of the eight weighty rules, it is an offense of wrong doing. If, without presenting the advice, he speaks of another teaching, it is an offense of wrong doing.

150. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunīsaṅghaṃ vematiko ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ samaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

150.Perceiving an unlawful act as unlawful, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as divided, there is an offense entailing confession. Perceiving an unlawful act as unlawful, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, being uncertain about its being divided, there is an offense entailing confession. Perceiving an unlawful act as unlawful, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as united, there is an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme vematiko vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme vematiko vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vematiko ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme vematiko vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ samaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

Being uncertain about an unlawful act, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as divided, there is an offense entailing confession. Being uncertain about an unlawful act, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, being uncertain about its being divided, there is an offense entailing confession. Being uncertain about an unlawful act, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as united, there is an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vematiko ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ samaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

Perceiving an unlawful act as a lawful act, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as divided, there is an offense entailing confession. Perceiving an unlawful act as a lawful act, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, being uncertain about its being divided, there is an offense entailing confession. Perceiving an unlawful act as a lawful act, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as united, there is an offense entailing confession.

Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī samaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī samaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vematiko ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī samaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ samaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

151. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī vaggaṃ bhikkhunisaṅghaṃ vematiko ovadati…pe… samaggasaññī ovadati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

151.Perceiving a lawful act as unlawful, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as divided, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Perceiving a lawful act as unlawful, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, being uncertain about its being divided, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Perceiving a lawful act as unlawful, if one advises a divided bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as united, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Perceiving a lawful act as lawful, if one advises a united bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as divided, there is an offense of wrong conduct. Perceiving a lawful act as lawful, if one advises a united bhikkhunī Saṅgha, perceiving it as united, there is no offense.

152. Anāpatti uddesaṃ dento, paripucchaṃ dento, ‘‘osārehi ayyā’’ti vuccamāno, osāreti, pañhaṃ pucchati, pañhaṃ puṭṭho katheti, aññassatthāya bhaṇantaṃ bhikkhuniyo suṇanti, sikkhamānāya, sāmaṇeriyā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

152.There is no offense when reciting the Pāḷi of the eight Garudhammas; when explaining the commentary on the Garudhamma Pāḷi; when, being told, ‘Ayyā, recite (the Garudhammas),’ one recites them; when asking a question; when answering a question asked; when bhikkhunīs listen to a bhikkhu teaching Dhamma for others’ benefit; or for a sikkhamānā, a sāmaṇerī, one who is insane, or the initial offender.

Ovādasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The first training rule on advice is concluded.
