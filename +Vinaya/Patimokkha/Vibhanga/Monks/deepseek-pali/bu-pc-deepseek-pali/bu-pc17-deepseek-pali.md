# BU-PC 17

## 7. Nikkaḍḍhanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 7.The Training Rule on Expulsion.

124. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū aññataraṃ paccantimaṃ mahāvihāraṃ paṭisaṅkharonti – ‘‘idha mayaṃ vassaṃ vasissāmā’’ti. Addasaṃsu kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sattarasavaggiye bhikkhū vihāraṃ paṭisaṅkharonte. Disvāna evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘ime, āvuso, sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū vihāraṃ paṭisaṅkharonti. Handa ne vuṭṭhāpessāmā’’ti! Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘āgamethāvuso, yāva paṭisaṅkharonti; paṭisaṅkhate vuṭṭhāpessāmā’’ti.

124.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. Now at that time a group-of-seventeen bhikkhus were repairing a certain large outlying monastery, thinking, 'Here we will spend the rains.' The group-of-six bhikkhus saw the group-of-seventeen bhikkhus repairing the monastery. Having seen them, they said, 'Friends, these group-of-seventeen bhikkhus are repairing a monastery. Come, let us make them leave!' Some said, 'Wait, friends, until they finish repairing it; when it is repaired, we will make them leave.'

Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sattarasavaggiye bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘uṭṭhethāvuso, amhākaṃ vihāro pāpuṇātī’’ti. ‘‘Nanu, āvuso, paṭikacceva [paṭigacceva (sī.)] ācikkhitabbaṃ, mayañcaññaṃ paṭisaṅkhareyyāmā’’ti. ‘‘Nanu, āvuso, saṅghiko vihāro’’ti? ‘‘Āmāvuso, saṅghiko vihāro’’ti. ‘‘Uṭṭhethāvuso, amhākaṃ vihāro pāpuṇātī’’ti. ‘‘Mahallako, āvuso, vihāro. Tumhepi vasatha, mayampi vasissāmā’’ti. ‘‘Uṭṭhethāvuso, amhākaṃ vihāro pāpuṇātī’’ti kupitā anattamanā gīvāyaṃ gahetvā nikkaḍḍhanti. Te nikkaḍḍhīyamānā rodanti. Bhikkhū evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘kissa tumhe, āvuso, rodathā’’ti? ‘‘Ime, āvuso, chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā amhe saṅghikā vihārā nikkaḍḍhantī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kupitā anattamanā bhikkhū saṅghikā vihārā nikkaḍḍhissantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, kupitā anattamanā bhikkhū saṅghikā vihārā nikkaḍḍhathā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, kupitā anattamanā bhikkhū saṅghikā vihārā nikkaḍḍhissatha? Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the group-of-six bhikkhus said to the group-of-seventeen bhikkhus, 'Get up, friends, our dwelling has arrived.' 'But, friends, shouldn't it have been announced beforehand so we could prepare another dwelling?' 'Isn't the dwelling communal, friends?' 'Yes, friends, the dwelling is communal.' 'Get up, friends, our dwelling has arrived.' 'The dwelling is large, friends. You too can stay, and we too will stay.' 'Get up, friends, our dwelling has arrived.' Angry and displeased, they grabbed them by the neck and dragged them out. As they were being dragged out, they wept. The bhikkhus asked, 'Why are you weeping, friends?' 'These group-of-six bhikkhus, angry and displeased, are dragging us out of the communal dwelling,' they replied. Those bhikkhus who were of few desires... complained and criticized, saying, 'How could the group-of-six bhikkhus, angry and displeased, drag bhikkhus out of a communal dwelling?' Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the group-of-six bhikkhus in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One. [The Blessed One asked,] 'Is it true, bhikkhus, that you, angry and displeased, dragged bhikkhus out of a communal dwelling?' 'It is true, Blessed One.' The Blessed One rebuked them... 'Foolish men, how could you, angry and displeased, drag bhikkhus out of a communal dwelling? This will not inspire confidence in the unconverted... And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

125. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuṃ kupito anattamano saṅghikā vihārā nikkaḍḍheyya vā nikkaḍḍhāpeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

125.'If any bhikkhu, being angry and displeased, evicts a bhikkhu from a Sangha's dwelling, or causes him to be evicted, he commits an offense of pācittiya.'

126. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

126.'Whoever' (yo pana) means whoever, of such a kind... 'bhikkhu' (bhikkhūti) means bhikkhu... here, in this connection, the term 'bhikkhu' is intended.

Bhikkhunti aññaṃ bhikkhuṃ.

'Bhikkhu' means another bhikkhu.

Kupito  anattamanoti anabhiraddho āhatacitto khilajāto.

'Angry, displeased' (kupito anattamano) means discontented, with a mind struck by affliction, obstructed.

Saṅghiko nāma vihāro saṅghassa dinno hoti pariccatto.

'Communal' (saṅghika) means a dwelling given and relinquished to the Sangha.

Nikkaḍḍheyyāti gabbhe gahetvā pamukhaṃ nikkaḍḍhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Pamukhe gahetvā bahi nikkaḍḍhati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Ekena payogena bahukepi dvāre atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Evicts' (nikkaḍḍheyya) means if, having taken (a bhikkhu) from the inner chamber, he drags him to the entrance, there is an offense of pācittiya. If, having taken him from the entrance, he drags him outside, there is an offense of pācittiya. If, with a single effort, he makes him pass through many doorways, there is an offense of pācittiya.

Nikkaḍḍhāpeyyāti aññaṃ āṇāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa [dukkaṭassa (katthaci)]. Sakiṃ āṇatto bahukepi dvāre atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

'Causes to be evicted' (nikkaḍḍhāpeyya) means he orders another, there is an offense of pācittiya. If, having been ordered once, (the other person) makes (the bhikkhu) pass through many doorways, there is an offense of pācittiya.

127. Saṅghike saṅghikasaññī kupito anattamano nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike vematiko kupito anattamano nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike puggalikasaññī kupito anattamano nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

127.If, perceiving a Sangha's dwelling as a Sangha's dwelling, he, being angry and displeased, evicts (a bhikkhu) or causes (him) to be evicted, there is an offense of pācittiya. If, being doubtful about a Sangha's dwelling, he, being angry and displeased, evicts (a bhikkhu) or causes (him) to be evicted, there is an offense of pācittiya. If, perceiving a Sangha's dwelling as an individual's dwelling, he, being angry and displeased, evicts (a bhikkhu) or causes (him) to be evicted, there is an offense of pācittiya.

Tassa parikkhāraṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Vihārassa upacārā vā upaṭṭhānasālāya vā maṇḍapā vā rukkhamūlā vā ajjhokāsā vā nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Tassa parikkhāraṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anupasampannaṃ vihārā vā vihārassa upacārā vā upaṭṭhānasālāya vā maṇḍapā vā rukkhamūlā vā ajjhokāsā vā nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Tassa parikkhāraṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he removes or causes to be removed the bhikkhu's belongings, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If he removes or causes to be removed (the bhikkhu) from the vicinity of a dwelling, or an assembly hall, or a pavilion, or the foot of a tree, or an open space, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If he removes or causes to be removed the bhikkhu's belongings, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If he removes or causes to be removed a non-ordained person from a dwelling, or the vicinity of a dwelling, or an assembly hall, or a pavilion, or the foot of a tree, or an open space, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If he removes or causes to be removed the bhikkhu's belongings, there is an offense of dukkaṭa.

Puggalike saṅghikasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike puggalikasaññī aññassa puggalike āpatti dukkaṭassa. Attano puggalike anāpatti.

If, perceiving an individual's dwelling as a Sangha's dwelling, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If, being doubtful about an individual's dwelling, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. If, perceiving an individual's dwelling as an individual's dwelling, (and it is) another's individual dwelling, there is an offense of dukkaṭa. There is no offense in regard to one's own individual dwelling.

128. Anāpatti alajjiṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, tassa parikkhāraṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, ummattakaṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, tassa parikkhāraṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, bhaṇḍanakārakaṃ kalahakārakaṃ vivādakārakaṃ bhassakārakaṃ saṅghe adhikaraṇakārakaṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, tassa parikkhāraṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, antevāsikaṃ vā saddhivihārikaṃ vā na sammā vattantaṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, tassa parikkhāraṃ nikkaḍḍhati vā nikkaḍḍhāpeti vā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

128.There is no offense if he evicts or causes to be evicted a shameless one, or removes or causes to be removed that person's belongings; if he evicts or causes to be evicted an insane one, or removes or causes to be removed that person's belongings; if he evicts or causes to be evicted one who causes strife, quarrels, disputes, arguments, or legal issues in the Sangha, or removes or causes to be removed that person's belongings; if he evicts or causes to be evicted a pupil or co-resident who is not behaving properly, or removes or causes to be removed that person's belongings; for an insane person; for the first offender.

Nikkaḍḍhanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The Seventh Training Rule on Expulsion is finished.
