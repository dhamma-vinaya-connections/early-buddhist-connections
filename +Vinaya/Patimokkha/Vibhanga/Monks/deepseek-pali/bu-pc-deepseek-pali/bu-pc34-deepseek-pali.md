# BU-PC 34

## 4. Kāṇamātusikkhāpadaṃ

## 4.The training rule concerning Kāṇamātā.

230. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena kāṇamātā upāsikā saddhā hoti pasannā. Kāṇā gāmake aññatarassa purisassa dinnā hoti. Atha kho kāṇā mātugharaṃ agamāsi kenacideva karaṇīyena. Atha kho kāṇāya sāmiko kāṇāya santike dūtaṃ pāhesi – ‘‘āgacchatu kāṇā, icchāmi kāṇāya āgata’’nti. Atha kho kāṇamātā upāsikā ‘‘kismiṃ viya rittahatthaṃ gantu’’nti pūvaṃ [pūpaṃ (ṇvādi)] paci. Pakke pūve aññataro piṇḍacāriko bhikkhu kāṇamātāya upāsikāya nivesanaṃ pāvisi. Atha kho kāṇamātā upāsikā tassa bhikkhuno pūvaṃ dāpesi. So nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi pūvaṃ dāpesi. Sopi nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi pūvaṃ dāpesi. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ pūvaṃ parikkhayaṃ agamāsi. Dutiyampi kho kāṇāya sāmiko kāṇāya santike dūtaṃ pāhesi – ‘‘āgacchatu kāṇā, icchāmi kāṇāya āgata’’nti. Dutiyampi kho kāṇamātā upāsikā ‘‘kismiṃ viya rittahattaṃ gantu’’nti pūvaṃ paci. Pakke pūve aññataro piṇḍacāriko bhikkhu kāṇamātāya upāsikāya nivesanaṃ pāvisi. Atha kho kāṇamātā upāsikā tassa bhikkhuno pūvaṃ dāpesi. So nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi pūvaṃ dāpesi. Sopi nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi pūvaṃ dāpesi. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ pūvaṃ parikkhayaṃ agamāsi. Tatiyampi kho kāṇāya sāmiko kāṇāya santike dūtaṃ pāhesi – ‘‘āgacchatu kāṇā, icchāmi kāṇāya āgataṃ. Sace kāṇā nāgacchissati, ahaṃ aññaṃ pajāpatiṃ ānessāmī’’ti. Tatiyampi kho kāṇamātā upāsikā kismiṃ viya rittahatthaṃ gantunti pūvaṃ paci. Pakke pūve aññataro piṇḍacāriko bhikkhu kāṇamātāya upāsikāya nivesanaṃ pāvisi. Atha kho kāṇamātā upāsikā tassa bhikkhuno pūvaṃ dāpesi. So nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi pūvaṃ dāpesi. Sopi nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi pūvaṃ dāpesi. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ pūvaṃ parikkhayaṃ agamāsi. Atha kho kāṇāya sāmiko aññaṃ pajāpatiṃ ānesi.

230.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time the lay follower Kāṇamātā was faithful and devoted. Kāṇā had been given in marriage to a certain man in a village. Then Kāṇā went to her mother’s house for some business. Then Kāṇā’s husband sent a messenger to Kāṇā: ‘Let Kāṇā come; I wish for Kāṇā to come.’ Then the lay follower Kāṇamātā thought, ‘How could she go empty-handed?’ and baked some cakes. When the cakes were ready, a certain alms-seeking bhikkhu entered the dwelling of the lay follower Kāṇamātā. Then the lay follower Kāṇamātā gave a cake to that bhikkhu. He went out and told another. She gave a cake to him too. He went out and told another. She gave a cake to him too. The prepared cakes were exhausted. A second time, Kāṇā’s husband sent a messenger to Kāṇā: ‘Let Kāṇā come; I wish for Kāṇā to come.’ A second time, the lay follower Kāṇamātā thought, ‘How could she go empty-handed?’ and baked some cakes. When the cakes were ready, a certain alms-seeking bhikkhu entered the dwelling of the lay follower Kāṇamātā. Then the lay follower Kāṇamātā gave a cake to that bhikkhu. He went out and told another. She gave a cake to him too. He went out and told another. She gave a cake to him too. The prepared cakes were exhausted. A third time, Kāṇā’s husband sent a messenger to Kāṇā: ‘Let Kāṇā come; I wish for Kāṇā to come. If Kāṇā does not come, I will bring another wife.’ A third time, the lay follower Kāṇamātā thought, ‘How could she go empty-handed?’ and baked some cakes. When the cakes were ready, a certain alms-seeking bhikkhu entered the dwelling of the lay follower Kāṇamātā. Then the lay follower Kāṇamātā gave a cake to that bhikkhu. He went out and told another. She gave a cake to him too. He went out and told another. She gave a cake to him too. The prepared cakes were exhausted. Then Kāṇā’s husband brought another wife.

Assosi kho kāṇā – ‘‘tena kira purisena aññā pajāpati ānītā’’ti. Sā rodantī aṭṭhāsi. Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena kāṇamātāya upāsikāya nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho kāṇamātā upāsikā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho kāṇamātaraṃ upāsikaṃ bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘kissāyaṃ kāṇā rodatī’’ti? Atha kho kāṇamātā upāsikā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Atha kho bhagavā kāṇamātaraṃ upāsikaṃ dhammiyā kathāya sandassetvā samādapetvā samuttejetvā sampahaṃsetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.

Then Kāṇā heard, ‘That man had brought another wife.’ She stood there weeping. Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the morning, taking his bowl and robe, went to the dwelling of the lay follower Kāṇamātā. Having approached, he sat down on the prepared seat. Then the lay follower Kāṇamātā approached the Blessed One; having approached, she paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. As the lay follower Kāṇamātā was sitting to one side, the Blessed One said to her, ‘Why is Kāṇā weeping?’ Then the lay follower Kāṇamātā explained this matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One instructed, encouraged, inspired, and gladdened the lay follower Kāṇamātā with a Dhamma talk, and rose from his seat and departed.

231. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro sattho rājagahā paṭiyālokaṃ gantukāmo hoti. Aññataro piṇḍacāriko bhikkhu taṃ satthaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Aññataro upāsako tassa bhikkhuno sattuṃ dāpesi. So nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi sattuṃ dāpesi. So nikkhamitvā aññassa ācikkhi. Tassapi sattuṃ dāpesi. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ pātheyyaṃ parikkhayaṃ agamāsi. Atha kho so upāsako te manusse etadavoca – ‘‘ajjaṇho, ayyā, āgametha, yathāpaṭiyattaṃ pātheyyaṃ ayyānaṃ dinnaṃ. Pātheyyaṃ paṭiyādessāmī’’ti. ‘‘Nāyyo [nāyya (syā.)] sakkā āgametuṃ, payāto sattho’’ti agamaṃsu. Atha kho tassa upāsakassa pātheyyaṃ paṭiyādetvā pacchā gacchantassa corā acchindiṃsu. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā na mattaṃ jānitvā paṭiggahessanti! Ayaṃ imesaṃ datvā pacchā gacchanto corehi acchinno’’ti. Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññapessāmi dasa atthavase paṭicca – saṅghasuṭṭhutāya, saṅghaphāsutāya…pe… vinayānuggahāya. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

231.At that time, a certain caravan intended to go from Rājagaha to Paṭiyāloka. A certain alms-collecting bhikkhu entered that caravan for alms. A certain lay follower gave that bhikkhu some flour. Having left, he told another. He also gave him flour. Having left, he told another. He also gave him flour. The prepared provisions were exhausted. Then that lay follower said to those people, ‘Venerable sirs, please come back this evening; the prepared provisions have been given to you. I will prepare more provisions.’ ‘Venerable sir, it is not possible to return; the caravan has departed,’ they said. Then, as that lay follower, having prepared provisions, was following behind, robbers robbed him. People complained, criticized, and grumbled, ‘How can these Sakyan ascetics accept without knowing moderation? This man, having given to them, was robbed by thieves while following behind.’ The bhikkhus heard those people complaining, criticizing, and grumbling. Then those bhikkhus reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and in connection with this matter, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: ‘Well then, bhikkhus, I will lay down a training rule for the bhikkhus for ten reasons: for the well-being of the Sangha, for the comfort of the Sangha… for the upholding of the discipline. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

232. ‘‘Bhikkhuṃ paneva kulaṃ upagataṃ pūvehi vā manthehi vā abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreyya, ākaṅkhamānena bhikkhunā dvattipattapūrā paṭiggahetabbā tato ce uttari paṭiggaṇheyya pācittiyaṃ. Dvattipattapūre paṭiggahetvā tato nīharitvā bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ saṃvibhajitabbaṃ. Ayaṃ tattha sāmīcī’’ti.

232.‘If a bhikkhu, having approached a family, should be invited to accept cakes or flour, the bhikkhu, if he wishes, may accept two or three bowlfuls. If he accepts more than that, it is an offense entailing confession. Having accepted two or three bowlfuls, he should take them away and share them with the bhikkhus. This is the proper course of action in that case.’

233. Bhikkhuṃ paneva kulaṃ upagatanti kulaṃ nāma cattāri kulāni – khattiyakulaṃ, brāhmaṇakulaṃ, vessakulaṃ, suddakulaṃ.

233.‘If a bhikkhu, having approached a family’: A family means four kinds of families—the Khattiya family, the Brahmin family, the Vessa family, and the Sudda family.

Upagatanti tattha gataṃ.

‘Having approached’: means having gone there.

Pūvaṃ nāma yaṃkiñci paheṇakatthāya paṭiyattaṃ.

‘Cakes’: means whatever is prepared as a gift.

Manthaṃ nāma yaṃkiñci pātheyyatthāya paṭiyattaṃ.

‘Flour’: means whatever is prepared as provisions for a journey.

Abhihaṭṭhuṃ pavāreyyāti yāvatakaṃ icchasi tāvatakaṃ gaṇhāhīti.

‘Should be invited to accept’: means ‘Take as much as you wish.’

Ākaṅkhamānenāti icchamānena.

‘If he wishes’: means if he wishes.

Dvattipattapūrā paṭiggahetabbāti dvetayo pattapūrā paṭiggahetabbā.

‘May accept two or three bowlfuls’: means two or three bowlfuls should be accepted.

Tato ce uttari paṭigaṇheyyāti tatuttari paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘If he accepts more than that’: means if he accepts more than that, it is an offense entailing confession.

Dvattipattapūre paṭiggahetvā tato nikkhamantena bhikkhuṃ passitvā ācikkhitabbaṃ – ‘‘amutra mayā dvattipattapūrā paṭiggahitā, mā kho tattha paṭiggaṇhī’’ti. Sace passitvā na ācikkhati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Sace ācikkhite paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

Having accepted two or three bowlfuls, when leaving, if he sees a bhikkhu, he should inform him, saying, ‘I have accepted two or three bowlfuls there; do not accept any there.’ If he sees him and does not inform him, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If he informs him, and he still accepts, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Tato nīharitvā bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ saṃvibhajitabbanti paṭikkamanaṃ nīharitvā saṃvibhajitabbaṃ.

‘Having taken them away and shared them with the bhikkhus’: means having taken them to the refectory, they should be shared.

Ayaṃ tattha sāmīcīti ayaṃ tattha anudhammatā.

‘This is the proper course of action in that case’: means this is the conduct conforming to the Dhamma in that case.

234. Atirekadvattipattapūre atirekasaññī paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Atirekadvattipattapūre vematiko paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Atirekadvattipattapūre ūnakasaññī paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

234.If he accepts more than two or three bowlfuls, perceiving it as more, it is an offense entailing confession. If he accepts more than two or three bowlfuls, being uncertain, it is an offense entailing confession. If he accepts more than two or three bowlfuls, perceiving it as less, it is an offense entailing confession.

Ūnakadvattipattapūre atirekasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakadvattipattapūre vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakadvattipattapūre ūnakasaññī, anāpatti.

If he accepts less than two or three bowlfuls, perceiving it as more, it is an offense of wrong conduct. If he accepts less than two or three bowlfuls, being uncertain, it is an offense of wrong conduct. If he accepts less than two or three bowlfuls, perceiving it as less, there is no offense.

235. Anāpatti dvattipattapūre paṭiggaṇhāti, ūnakadvattipattapūre paṭiggaṇhāti, na paheṇakatthāya na pātheyyatthāya paṭiyattaṃ denti, paheṇakatthāya vā pātheyyatthāya vā paṭiyattasesakaṃ denti, gamane paṭippassaddhe denti, ñātakānaṃ pavāritānaṃ, aññassatthāya, attano dhanena, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

235.There is no offense if he accepts two or three bowlfuls; if he accepts less than two or three bowlfuls; if they give what was not prepared as a gift or as provisions for a journey; if they give what remains of what was prepared as a gift or as provisions for a journey; if they give when the journey has been canceled; if it is for relatives or those who have invited him; if it is for someone else; if it is from his own wealth; if he is insane; or if he is a first offender.

Kāṇamātusikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The fourth training rule concerning Kāṇamātā is concluded.
