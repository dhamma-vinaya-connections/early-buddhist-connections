# BU-PC 86

## 4. Sūcigharasikkhāpadaṃ

## 4.The Training Rule on the Needle-Case

516. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarena dantakārena bhikkhū pavāritā honti – ‘‘yesaṃ ayyānaṃ sūcigharena attho ahaṃ sūcigharenā’’ti. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū bahū sūcighare viññāpenti. Yesaṃ khuddakā sūcigharā te mahante sūcighare viññāpenti. Yesaṃ mahantā sūcigharā te khuddake sūcighare viññāpenti. Atha kho so dantakāro bhikkhūnaṃ bahū sūcighare karonto na sakkoti aññaṃ vikkāyikaṃ bhaṇḍaṃ kātuṃ, attanāpi na yāpeti, puttadāropissa kilamati. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā na mattaṃ jānitvā bahū sūcighare viññāpessanti! Ayaṃ imesaṃ bahū sūcighare karonto na sakkoti aññaṃ vikkāyikaṃ bhaṇḍaṃ kātuṃ, attanāpi na yāpeti, puttadāropissa kilamatī’’ti. Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū na mattaṃ jānitvā bahū sūcighare viññāpessantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū na mattaṃ jānitvā bahū sūcighare viññāpentīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā na mattaṃ jānitvā bahū sūcighare viññāpessanti! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

516.At that time the Blessed One was staying among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Banyan Tree Park. Now at that time a certain ivory carver had offered to the bhikkhus, saying, “If any venerable ones have need of a needle-case, I will provide a needle-case.” Then at that time the bhikkhus requested many needle-cases. Those who had small needle-cases requested large ones. Those who had large needle-cases requested small ones. Then that ivory carver, making many needle-cases for the bhikkhus, was unable to produce other saleable goods. He could not even support himself, and his family too was burdened. People complained, criticized, and grumbled, “How can these Sakyan recluses not know moderation and request so many needle-cases? This man, making many needle-cases for them, cannot produce other saleable goods. He cannot even support himself, and his family is burdened.” The bhikkhus heard the complaints, criticisms, and grumblings of those people. Those bhikkhus who were modest … criticized them, saying, “How can these bhikkhus not know moderation and request so many needle-cases?” “Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus, not knowing moderation, request many needle-cases?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them … “How can you, foolish men, not knowing moderation, request many needle-cases? This will not inspire confidence in those who are not confident … Therefore, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus: —

517. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu aṭṭhimayaṃ vā dantamayaṃ vā visāṇamayaṃ vā sūcigharaṃ kārāpeyya bhedanakaṃ, pācittiya’’nti.

517.“Should any bhikkhu have a needle-case made of bone, ivory, or horn that is of a splitting nature, it is a pācittiya.”

518. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

518.‘Any bhikkhu whatsoever’ (yo pana): any bhikkhu of whatever sort… ‘bhikkhu’ (bhikkhu): the bhikkhu intended in this context.

Aṭṭhi nāma yaṃ kiñci aṭṭhi.

Bone (aṭṭhi) means any kind of bone.

Danto nāma hatthidanto vuccati.

Ivory (danto) is said to be elephant tusk.

Visāṇaṃ nāma yaṃ kiñci visāṇaṃ.

Horn (visāṇaṃ) means any kind of horn.

Kārāpeyyāti karoti vā kārāpeti vā, payoge dukkaṭaṃ. Paṭilābhena bhinditvā pācittiyaṃ desetabbaṃ.

‘Have made’ (kārāpeyya): he makes it or has it made. In the attempt, there is an offense of dukkata. Upon obtaining it, it should be broken and confessed as a pācittiya.

519. Attanā vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Attanā vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti [pariyosāvāpeti (ka.)], āpatti pācittiyassa. Parehi vippakataṃ attanā pariyosāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Parehi vippakataṃ parehi pariyosāpeti [pariyosāvāpeti (ka.)], āpatti pācittiyassa.

519.If one finishes what was started by oneself, there is an offense of pācittiya. If one has others finish what was started by oneself, there is an offense of pācittiya. If one finishes what was started by others, there is an offense of pācittiya. If one has others finish what was started by others, there is an offense of pācittiya.

Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññena kataṃ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he makes it or has it made for another, there is an offense of dukkata. If he receives something made by another and uses it, there is an offense of dukkata.

520. Anāpatti gaṇṭhikāya [gaṇḍikāya (syā.)], araṇike, vidhe [vīṭhe (sī.), vīthe (syā.)], añjaniyā, añjanisalākāya, vāsijaṭe, udakapuñchaniyā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

520.There is no offense: regarding a knot; a fire-drill; a boring tool; a collyrium container; a collyrium stick; an adze handle; a water-wiper; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Sūcigharasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The fourth training rule on the needle-case is concluded.
