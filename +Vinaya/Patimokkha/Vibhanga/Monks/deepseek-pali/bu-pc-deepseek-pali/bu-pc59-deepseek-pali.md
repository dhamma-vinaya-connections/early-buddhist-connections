# BU-PC 59

## 9. Vikappanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 9.The Training Rule on Assignment.

372. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā upanando sakyaputto bhātuno saddhivihārikassa bhikkhuno sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ vikappetvā appaccuddhāraṇaṃ [apaccuddhārakaṃ (sī. syā.)] paribhuñjati. Atha kho so bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi – ‘‘ayaṃ, āvuso, āyasmā upanando sakyaputto mayhaṃ cīvaraṃ sāmaṃ vikappetvā appaccuddhāraṇaṃ paribhuñjatī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto bhikkhussa sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ vikappetvā appaccuddhāraṇaṃ paribhuñjissatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, bhikkhussa sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ vikappetvā appaccuddhāraṇaṃ paribhuñjasīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, bhikkhussa sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ vikappetvā appaccuddhāraṇaṃ paribhuñjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

372.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having himself assigned a robe to a bhikkhu, his brother’s pupil, used it without having formally rescinded the assignment. Then that bhikkhu informed the bhikkhus about this matter: “Friends, this Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having himself assigned a robe to me, used it without having formally rescinded the assignment.” Those bhikkhus who were modest, etc., criticized and complained: “How could Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having himself assigned a robe to a bhikkhu, use it without having formally rescinded the assignment?” … “Is it true, Upananda, that you did this?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him: “Foolish man, how could you, having himself assigned a robe to a bhikkhu, use it without having formally rescinded the assignment! This will not inspire confidence in those without it, nor increase the confidence of those who have it…” “And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

373. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhussa vā bhikkhuniyā vā sikkhamānāya vā sāmaṇerassa vā sāmaṇeriyā vā sāmaṃ cīvaraṃ vikappetvā appaccuddhāraṇaṃ [apaccuddhārakaṃ (sī. syā.)] paribhuñjeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

373.“Whatever bhikkhu, having himself assigned a robe to a bhikkhu, a bhikkhunī, a sikkhamānā, a sāmaṇera, or a sāmaṇerī, should use it without formally rescinding the assignment, there is an offense entailing confession.”

374. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

374.“Whatever” means whoever, etc. “Bhikkhu” means, etc., here in this case, the bhikkhu is intended by this term.

Bhikkhussāti aññassa bhikkhussa.

“To a bhikkhu” means to another bhikkhu.

Bhikkhunī nāma ubhatosaṅghe upasampannā.

A bhikkhunī is one who has received the full ordination in both Saṅghas.

Sikkhamānā nāma dve vassāni chasu dhammesu sikkhitasikkhā.

A sikkhamānā is one who has trained for two years in the six principles.

Sāmaṇero nāma dasasikkhāpadiko.

A sāmaṇera is one who observes the ten training rules.

Sāmaṇerī nāma dasasikkhāpadikā.

A sāmaṇerī is one who observes the ten training rules.

Sāmanti sayaṃ vikappetvā.

“Himself” means having himself assigned.

Cīvaraṃ nāma channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ vikappanupagaṃ pacchimaṃ.

A robe means any one of the six kinds of robes, the last of which is suitable for assignment.

Vikappanā nāma dve vikappanā – sammukhāvikappanā ca parammukhāvikappanā ca.

Assignment means two kinds of assignment: assignment in one’s presence and assignment in one’s absence.

Sammukhāvikappanā nāma ‘‘imaṃ cīvaraṃ tuyhaṃ vikappemi itthannāmassa vā’’ti.

Assignment in one’s presence means: “I assign this robe to you, or to so-and-so.”

Parammukhāvikappanā nāma ‘‘imaṃ cīvaraṃ vikappanatthāya tuyhaṃ dammī’’ti. Tena vattabbo – ‘‘ko te mitto vā sandiṭṭho vā’’ti? ‘‘Itthannāmo ca itthannāmo cā’’ti. Tena vattabbo – ‘‘ahaṃ tesaṃ dammi, tesaṃ santakaṃ paribhuñja vā vissajjehi vā yathāpaccayaṃ vā karohī’’ti.

Assignment in one’s absence means: “I give you this robe for the purpose of assignment.” He should then be told: “Who is your friend or acquaintance?” “So-and-so and so-and-so.” He should then be told: “I give it to them; use what belongs to them, or give it away, or do with it as you wish.”

Appaccuddhāraṇaṃ nāma tassa vā adinnaṃ, tassa vā avissasanto paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

“Without formally rescinding the assignment” means he uses it when it has not been given back to him, or without trusting the one who assigned it. There is an offense entailing confession.

375. Appaccuddhāraṇe appaccuddhāraṇasaññī paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Appaccuddhāraṇe vematiko paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Appaccuddhāraṇe appaccuddhāraṇasaññī paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

375.If one uses what has not been formally rescinded, perceiving it as not formally rescinded, there is an offense entailing confession. If one is in doubt whether it has been formally rescinded and uses it, there is an offense entailing confession. If one uses what has not been formally rescinded, perceiving it as not formally rescinded, there is an offense entailing confession.

Adhiṭṭheti vā vissajjeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paccuddhāraṇe appaccuddhāraṇasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paccuddhāraṇe vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paccuddhāraṇe paccuddhāraṇasaññī, anāpatti.

If he determines it or relinquishes it, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he perceives it as not formally rescinded when it is formally rescinded, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he is in doubt whether it is formally rescinded, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he perceives it as formally rescinded when it is formally rescinded, there is no offense.

376. Anāpatti so vā deti, tassa vā vissasanto paribhuñjati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

376.There is no offense if he (the one to whom it was assigned) gives it back, or if one uses it trusting him (the one who assigned it), or if he is insane, or if he is the first offender.

Vikappanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The ninth training rule on Assignment is finished.
