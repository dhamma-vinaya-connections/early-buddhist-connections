# BU-PC 2

## 2. Omasavādasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2. The training rule on offensive speech.

12. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pesalehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ bhaṇḍantā [bhaṇḍentā (itipi)] pesale bhikkhū omasanti – jātiyāpi, nāmenapi, gottenapi, kammenapi, sippenapi, ābādhenapi, liṅgenapi, kilesenapi, āpattiyāpi; hīnenapi akkosena khuṃsenti vambhenti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pesalehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ bhaṇḍantā pesale bhikkhū omasissanti – jātiyāpi, nāmenapi, gottenapi, kammenapi, sippenapi, ābādhenapi, liṅgenapi, kilesenapi, āpattiyāpi; hīnenapi akkosena khuṃsessanti vambhessantī’’ti!

12. At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was dwelling at Savatthi in Jeta's Grove, Anathapindika's Park. Now at that time the group-of-six bhikkhus, quarreling with well-behaved bhikkhus, insulted the well-behaved bhikkhus—by birth, by name, by clan, by work, by craft, by ailment, by sign, by defilement, by offense; they abused and disparaged them with low and inferior abuse. Those bhikkhus who were of few desires… they complained, grumbled, and criticized them: "How can the group-of-six bhikkhus, quarreling with well-behaved bhikkhus, insult the well-behaved bhikkhus—by birth, by name, by clan, by work, by craft, by ailment, by sign, by defilement, by offense; how can they abuse and disparage them with low and inferior abuse?"

Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, pesalehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ bhaṇḍantā pesale bhikkhū omasatha – jātiyāpi…pe… hīnenapi akkosena khuṃsetha vambhethāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, pesalehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ bhaṇḍantā pesale bhikkhū omasissatha – jātiyāpi…pe… hīnenapi akkosena khuṃsessatha vambhessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi –

Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked the group-of-six bhikkhus in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that while quarreling with well-behaved bhikkhus, you insult the well-behaved bhikkhus—by birth, by name, by clan, by work, by craft, by ailment, by sign, by defilement, by offense; and abuse and disparage them with low and inferior abuse?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked them… “How can you, foolish men, while quarreling with well-behaved bhikkhus, insult the well-behaved bhikkhus—by birth, by name, by clan, by work, by craft, by ailment, by sign, by defilement, by offense; and abuse and disparage them with low and inferior abuse! This is not, foolish men, for the pleasing of those who are not (yet) pleased…” Having rebuked them… he gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus:

13. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, takkasilāyaṃ [takkasīlāyaṃ (ka.)] aññatarassa brāhmaṇassa nandivisālo nāma balībaddo [balivaddo (sī.), balibaddo (syā.)] ahosi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, nandivisālo balībaddo taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘gaccha tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, seṭṭhinā saddhiṃ sahassena abbhutaṃ karohi – mayhaṃ balībaddo sakaṭasataṃ atibaddhaṃ pavaṭṭessatī’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so brāhmaṇo seṭṭhinā saddhiṃ sahassena abbhutaṃ akāsi – mayhaṃ balībaddo sakaṭasataṃ atibaddhaṃ pavaṭṭessatīti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so brāhmaṇo sakaṭasataṃ atibandhitvā nandivisālaṃ balībaddaṃ yuñjitvā etadavoca – ‘‘gaccha, kūṭa [añcha kūṭa (sī. syā.)], vahassu, kūṭā’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, nandivisālo balībaddo tattheva aṭṭhāsi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so brāhmaṇo sahassena parājito pajjhāyi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, nandivisālo balībaddo taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kissa tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, pajjhāyasī’’ti? ‘Tathā hi panāhaṃ, bho, tayā sahassena parājito’’ti. ‘Kissa pana maṃ tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, akūṭaṃ kūṭavādena pāpesi? Gaccha tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, seṭṭhinā saddhiṃ dvīhi sahassehi abbhutaṃ karohi – ‘‘mayhaṃ balībaddo sakaṭasataṃ atibaddhaṃ pavaṭṭessatī’’ti. ‘‘Mā ca maṃ akūṭaṃ kūṭavādena pāpesī’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so brāhmaṇo seṭṭhinā saddhiṃ dvīhi sahassehi abbhutaṃ akāsi – ‘‘mayhaṃ balībaddo sakaṭasataṃ atibaddhaṃ pavaṭṭessatī’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, so brāhmaṇo sakaṭasataṃ atibandhitvā nandivisālaṃ balībaddaṃ yuñjitvā etadavoca – ‘‘accha, bhadra, vahassu, bhadrā’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, nandivisālo balībaddo sakaṭasataṃ atibaddhaṃ pavaṭṭesi.

13. "Once upon a time, bhikkhus, in Takkasilā, there was a brahmin who owned a bull named Nandivisāla. Then, bhikkhus, the bull Nandivisāla said to that brahmin: 'Go, brahmin, make a wager with the merchant for a thousand—my bull will pull a hundred carts yoked together.' Then, bhikkhus, that brahmin made a wager with the merchant for a thousand—'My bull will pull a hundred carts yoked together.' Then, bhikkhus, that brahmin yoked a hundred carts together, harnessed the bull Nandivisāla, and said: 'Go, rogue, pull, rogue!' Then, bhikkhus, the bull Nandivisāla stood right there. Then, bhikkhus, that brahmin, having lost the thousand, became distressed. Then, bhikkhus, the bull Nandivisāla said to that brahmin: 'Why are you distressed, brahmin?' 'Because, sir, I have lost a thousand because of you.' 'But why did you call me, who am not a rogue, a rogue? Go, brahmin, make a wager with the merchant for two thousand—"My bull will pull a hundred carts yoked together." And do not call me, who am not a rogue, a rogue.' Then, bhikkhus, that brahmin made a wager with the merchant for two thousand—'My bull will pull a hundred carts yoked together.' Then, bhikkhus, that brahmin yoked a hundred carts together, harnessed the bull Nandivisāla, and said: 'Go, good one, pull, good one!' Then, bhikkhus, the bull Nandivisāla pulled the hundred yoked carts."

[jā. 1.1.28 nandivisālajātakepi, tattha pana manuññasaddo dissati] ‘‘Manāpameva bhāseyya, nā, manāpaṃ kudācanaṃ;Manāpaṃ bhāsamānassa, garuṃ bhāraṃ udabbahi;Dhanañca naṃ alābhesi, tena ca, ttamano ahūti.

Speak only what is pleasing, never what is unpleasing;
To the one speaking pleasingly, a heavy burden was lifted;
And he procured wealth for him; and was delighted by it.

‘‘Tadāpi me, bhikkhave, amanāpā khuṃsanā vambhanā. Kimaṅgaṃ pana etarahi manāpā bhavissati khuṃsanā vambhanā? Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe…. ‘‘Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

"Even then, bhikkhus, disapproval and disparagement were displeasing to me. Still less now, bhikkhus, will disapproval and disparagement be pleasing? This, bhikkhus, is not for inspiring confidence in the faithless… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

14. ‘‘Omasavāde pācittiya’’nti.

14. 'There is an offense entailing expiation for scornful speech.'

15. Omasavādo nāma dasahi ākārehi omasati – jātiyāpi, nāmenapi, gottenapi, kammenapi, sippenapi, ābādhenapi, liṅgenapi, kilesenapi, āpattiyāpi, akkosenapi.

15. Scornful speech means scorning in ten ways: by birth, by name, by lineage, by occupation, by skill, by affliction, by physical characteristic, by defilement, by offense, or by insult.

Jāti nāma dve jātiyo – hīnā ca jāti ukkaṭṭhā ca jāti. Hīnā nāma jāti – caṇḍālajāti, venajāti, nesādajāti, rathakārajāti, pukkusajāti. Esā hīnā nāma jāti. Ukkaṭṭhā nāma jāti – khattiyajāti, brāhmaṇajāti. Esā ukkaṭṭhā nāma jāti.

Birth means two kinds of birth: a low birth and a high birth. A low birth means the birth of a caṇḍāla, a veṇa, a nesāda, a rathakāra, or a pukkusa. This is called a low birth. A high birth means the birth of a khattiya or a brahmin. This is called a high birth.

Nāmaṃ nāma dve nāmāni – hīnañca nāmaṃ ukkaṭṭhañca nāmaṃ. Hīnaṃ nāma nāmaṃ – avakaṇṇakaṃ, javakaṇṇakaṃ, dhaniṭṭhakaṃ, saviṭṭhakaṃ, kulavaḍḍhakaṃ, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu oññātaṃ avaññātaṃ hīḷitaṃ paribhūtaṃ acittīkataṃ, etaṃ hīnaṃ nāma nāmaṃ. Ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma nāmaṃ – buddhappaṭisaṃyuttaṃ, dhammappaṭisaṃyuttaṃ, saṅghappaṭisaṃyuttaṃ, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu anoññātaṃ anavaññātaṃ ahīḷitaṃ aparibhūtaṃ cittīkataṃ, etaṃ ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma nāmaṃ.

Name is of two kinds: a low name and a superior name. A low name is a name such as Avakaṇṇaka, Javakaṇṇaka, Dhaniṭṭhaka, Saviṭṭhaka, Kulavaḍḍhaka, or any name that is disreputable, despised, abhorred, scorned, and unregarded in various regions—this is called a low name. A superior name is a name associated with the Buddha, the Dhamma, or the Saṅgha, or any name that is reputable, not despised, not abhorred, not scorned, and regarded in various regions—this is called a superior name.

Gottaṃ nāma dve gottāni – hīnañca gottaṃ ukkaṭṭhañca gottaṃ. Hīnaṃ nāma gottaṃ – kosiyagottaṃ, bhāradvājagottaṃ, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu oññātaṃ avaññātaṃ hīḷitaṃ paribhūtaṃ acittīkataṃ, etaṃ hīnaṃ nāma gottaṃ. Ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma gottaṃ – gotamagottaṃ, moggallānagottaṃ, kaccānagottaṃ, vāsiṭṭhagottaṃ, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu anoññātaṃ anavaññātaṃ ahīḷitaṃ aparibhūtaṃ cittīkataṃ, etaṃ ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma gottaṃ.

Lineage is of two kinds: a low lineage and a superior lineage. A low lineage is the Kosiya lineage, the Bhāradvāja lineage, or any lineage that is disreputable, despised, abhorred, scorned, and unregarded in various regions—this is called a low lineage. A superior lineage is the Gotama lineage, the Moggallāna lineage, the Kaccāna lineage, the Vāsiṭṭha lineage, or any lineage that is reputable, not despised, not abhorred, not scorned, and regarded in various regions—this is called a superior lineage.

Kammaṃ nāma dve kammāni – hīnañca kammaṃ ukkaṭṭhañca kammaṃ. Hīnaṃ nāma kammaṃ – koṭṭhakakammaṃ, pupphachaḍḍakakammaṃ, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu oññātaṃ avaññātaṃ hīḷitaṃ paribhūtaṃ acittīkataṃ, etaṃ hīnaṃ nāma kammaṃ. Ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma kammaṃ – kasi, vaṇijjā, gorakkhā, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu anoññātaṃ anavaññātaṃ ahīḷitaṃ aparibhūtaṃ cittīkataṃ. Etaṃ ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma kammaṃ.

Work is of two kinds: a low work and a superior work. A low work is work such as carpentry, flower-sweeping, or any work that is disreputable, despised, abhorred, scorned, and unregarded in various regions—this is called a low work. A superior work is work such as farming, trade, cattle-raising, or any work that is reputable, not despised, not abhorred, not scorned, and regarded in various regions—this is called a superior work.

Sippaṃ nāma dve sippāni – hīnañca sippaṃ ukkaṭṭhañca sippaṃ. Hīnaṃ nāma sippaṃ – naḷakārasippaṃ, kumbhakārasippaṃ, pesakārasippaṃ, cammakārasippaṃ, nahāpitasippaṃ, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu oññātaṃ avaññātaṃ hīḷitaṃ paribhūtaṃ acittīkataṃ. Etaṃ hīnaṃ nāma sippaṃ. Ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma sippaṃ – muddā, gaṇanā, lekhā, tesu tesu vā pana janapadesu anoññātaṃ anavaññātaṃ ahīḷitaṃ aparibhūtaṃ cittīkataṃ, etaṃ ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma sippaṃ.

Craft is of two kinds: a low craft and a superior craft. A low craft is craft such as reed-making, pottery, weaving, leatherwork, barbering, or any craft that in those various regions is disreputable, despised, abhorred, scorned, and unregarded. This is called a low craft. A superior craft is accounting, calculation, writing, or any craft that in those various regions is reputable, not despised, not abhorred, not scorned, and regarded. This is called a superior craft.

Sabbepi ābādhā hīnā, apica madhumeho ābādho ukkaṭṭho.

All illnesses are low, but diabetes is a superior illness.

Liṅgaṃ nāma dve liṅgāni – hīnañca liṅgaṃ ukkaṭṭhañca liṅgaṃ. Hīnaṃ nāma liṅgaṃ – atidīghaṃ, atirassaṃ, atikaṇhaṃ, accodātaṃ, etaṃ hīnaṃ nāma liṅgaṃ. Ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma liṅgaṃ – nātidīghaṃ, nātirassaṃ, nātikaṇhaṃ, nāccodātaṃ. Etaṃ ukkaṭṭhaṃ nāma liṅgaṃ.

Appearance is of two kinds: a low appearance and a superior appearance. A low appearance is being excessively long, excessively short, excessively black, or excessively white—this is called a low appearance. A superior appearance is not being excessively long, not excessively short, not excessively black, and not excessively white. This is called a superior appearance.

Sabbepi kilesā hīnā.

All defilements are low.

Sabbāpi āpattiyo hīnā. Apica, sotāpattisamāpatti ukkaṭṭhā.

All offenses are low. But the attainment of stream-entry is superior.

Akkoso nāma dve akkosā – hīno ca akkoso ukkaṭṭho ca akkoso. Hīno nāma akkoso – oṭṭhosi, meṇḍosi, goṇosi, gadrabhosi, tiracchānagatosi, nerayikosi; natthi tuyhaṃ sugati, duggati yeva tuyhaṃ pāṭikaṅkhāti, yakārena vā bhakārena vā, kāṭakoṭacikāya vā, eso hīno nāma akkoso. Ukkaṭṭho nāma akkoso – paṇḍitosi, byattosi, medhāvīsi, bahussutosi, dhammakathikosi, natthi tuyhaṃ duggati, sugatiyeva tuyhaṃ pāṭikaṅkhāti, eso ukkaṭṭho nāma akkoso.

Insult is of two kinds: a low insult and a superior insult. A low insult is saying, "You are a camel, you are a ram, you are an ox, you are a donkey, you are an animal, you are a hell-dweller; there is no good destination for you, only a bad destination is to be expected for you," by using words with the letter 'ya' or the letter 'bha', or by using obscene words—this is called a low insult. A superior insult is saying, "You are wise, you are clever, you are intelligent, you are learned, you are a Dhamma speaker; there is no bad destination for you, only a good destination is to be expected for you"—this is called a superior insult.

16. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo hīnena hīnaṃ vadeti, caṇḍālaṃ venaṃ nesādaṃ rathakāraṃ pukkusaṃ – ‘‘caṇḍālosi, venosi, nesādosi, rathakārosi, pukkusosī’’ti bhaṇati [vadetīti uddeso. bhaṇatīti vitthāro (vajirabuddhi)], āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

16. If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to scold, berate, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls him low by means of a low designation—an outcaste, a bamboo worker, a hunter, a cart-maker, or a scavenger—saying, "You are an outcaste, a bamboo worker, a hunter, a cart-maker, or a scavenger," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo hīnena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, khattiyaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ – ‘‘caṇḍālosi, venosi, nesādosi, rathakārosi, pukkusosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to scold, berate, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls a superior person low by means of a low designation—a noble or a brahmin—saying, "You are an outcaste, a bamboo worker, a hunter, a cartwright, a scavenger," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo ukkaṭṭhena hīnaṃ vadeti, caṇḍālaṃ venaṃ nesādaṃ rathakāraṃ pukkusaṃ – ‘‘khattiyosi, brāhmaṇosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to scold, berate, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls a lowly person by means of a superior designation—an outcaste, a bamboo worker, a hunter, a cartwright, or a scavenger—saying, "You are a noble, a brahmin," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo ukkaṭṭhena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, khattiyaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ – ‘‘khattiyosi, brāhmaṇosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to scold, berate, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls a superior person by means of a superior designation—a noble or a brahmin—saying, "You are a noble, you are a brahmin," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

17. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo hīnena hīnaṃ vadeti, avakaṇṇakaṃ javakaṇṇakaṃ dhaniṭṭhakaṃ saviṭṭhakaṃ kulavaḍḍhakaṃ – ‘‘avakaṇṇakosi, javakaṇṇakosi, dhaniṭṭhakosi, saviṭṭhakosi, kulavaḍḍhakosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

17. If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to scold, berate, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls him low by means of a low designation—one with defective ears, one with quick ears, a miser, one who corrupts associations, or one who brings shame to his family—saying, "You are one with defective ears, you are one with quick ears, you are a miser, you are one who corrupts associations, you are one who brings shame to his family," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo hīnena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, buddharakkhitaṃ dhammarakkhitaṃ saṅgharakkhitaṃ – ‘‘avakaṇṇakosi, javakaṇṇakosi, dhaniṭṭhakosi, saviṭṭhakosi, kulavaḍḍhakosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to scold, berate, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls a superior person by means of a low designation—such as Buddharakkhita, Dhammarakkhita, or Saṅgharakkhita—saying, "You are one with defective ears, you are one with quick ears, you are a miser, you are one who corrupts associations, you are one who brings shame to his family," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo ukkaṭṭhena hīnaṃ vadeti, avakaṇṇakaṃ javakaṇṇakaṃ dhaniṭṭhakaṃ saviṭṭhakaṃ kulavaḍḍhakaṃ – ‘‘buddharakkhitosi, dhammarakkhitosi, saṅgharakkhitosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls an inferior person by means of a superior designation—one with defective ears, one with quick ears, a miser, one who corrupts associations, or one who brings shame to his family—saying, "You are under the Buddha’s protection, the Dhamma’s protection, the Sangha’s protection," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo ukkaṭṭhena ukaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, buddharakkhitaṃ dhammarakkhitaṃ saṅgharakkhitaṃ – ‘‘buddharakkhitosi, dhammarakkhitosi, saṅgharakkhitosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to scold, berate, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, calls a superior person by means of a superior designation—such as Buddharakkhita, Dhammarakkhita, or Saṅgharakkhita—saying, "You are under the Buddha’s protection, the Dhamma’s protection, the Sangha’s protection," he commits an offense entailing confession for each utterance.

18. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo hīnena hīnaṃ vadeti, kosiyaṃ bhāradvājaṃ – ‘‘kosiyosi, bhāradvājosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya pācittiyassa.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘santi idhekacce avakaṇṇakā javakaṇṇakā dhaniṭṭhakā saviṭṭhakā kulavaḍḍhakā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce buddharakkhitā dhammarakkhitā saṅgharakkhitā’’ti bhaṇati …pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce kosiyā bhāradvājā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce gotamā moggallānā kaccānā vāsiṭṭhā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce koṭṭhakā pupphachaḍḍakā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce kassakā vāṇijā gorakkhā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce naḷakārā kumbhakārā pesakārā cammakārā nahāpitā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce muddikā gaṇakā lekhakā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce kuṭṭhikā gaṇḍikā kilāsikā sosikā apamārikā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce madhumehikā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce atidīghā atirassā atikaṇhā accodātā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce nātidīghā nātirassā nātikaṇhā nāccodātā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce rāgapariyuṭṭhitā dosapariyuṭṭhitā mohapariyuṭṭhitā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce vītarāgā vītadosā vītamohā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce pārājikaṃ ajjhāpannā…pe… dubbhāsitaṃ ajjhāpannā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce sotāpannā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce oṭṭhā meṇḍā goṇā gadrabhā tiracchānagatā nerayikā, natthi tesaṃ sugati, duggatiyeva tesaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

27.If a bhikkhu, wishing to insult, disparage, or humiliate another bhikkhu, says, "There are here some with ears cut off, those with ears hanging down, those with large teeth, those with protruding teeth, those who destroy families; there are here some protected by the Buddha, protected by the Dhamma, protected by the Saṅgha; there are here some Kosiyas, Bhāradvājas; there are here some Gotamas, Moggallānas, Kaccānas, Vāsiṭṭhas; there are here some who live in granaries, flower-tossers; there are here some farmers, merchants, cowherds; there are here some reed workers, potters, weavers, leather workers, barbers; there are here some seal-cutters, calculators, scribes; there are here some lepers, persons with boils, persons with white leprosy, consumptive persons, epileptic persons; there are here some diabetic persons; there are here some excessively tall, excessively short, excessively dark, excessively pale; there are here some not excessively tall, not excessively short, not excessively dark, not excessively pale; there are here some overcome by lust, overcome by hatred, overcome by delusion; there are here some free from lust, free from hatred, free from delusion; there are here some who have incurred a pārājika offense... or a dubbhāsita offense; there are here some stream-enterers; there are here some camels, rams, oxen, donkeys, animals, denizens of hell, for whom there is no happy destination; only a bad destination is to be expected for them," he incurs a dukkaṭa offense for each utterance.

28. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘santi idhekacce paṇḍitā byattā, medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā, natthi tesaṃ duggati, sugatiyeva tesaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

28.If a bhikkhu, wishing to insult, disparage, or humiliate another bhikkhu, says, "There are here some wise, articulate, intelligent, learned Dhamma speakers, for whom there is no bad destination; only a happy destination is to be expected for them," he incurs a dukkaṭa offense for each utterance.

29. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘ye nūna caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa…pe….

29.If a bhikkhu, wishing to insult, disparage, or humiliate another bhikkhu, says, "Surely there are outcastes, bamboo workers, hunters, chariot makers, scavengers," he incurs a dukkaṭa offense for each utterance.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘ye nūna paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, says: “Indeed, there are wise, capable, intelligent, learned Dhamma-speakers,” for each utterance there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong doing.

30. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘na mayaṃ caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Na mayaṃ paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā, natthamhākaṃ duggati, sugatiyeva amhākaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

30.If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, says: “We are not outcastes, reed workers, hunters, chariot makers, or scavengers,” and so on… “We are not wise, capable, intelligent, learned Dhamma-speakers; there is no bad destination for us, only a good destination is to be expected for us.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong doing.

31. Upasampanno anupasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo hīnena hīnaṃ vadeti,…pe… hīnena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti…pe… ukkaṭṭhena hīnaṃ vadeti…pe… ukkaṭṭhena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, paṇḍitaṃ byattaṃ medhāviṃ bahussutaṃ dhammakathikaṃ – ‘‘paṇḍitosi, byattosi, medhāvīsi, bahussutosi, dhammakathikosi, natthi tuyhaṃ duggati, sugatiyeva tuyhaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

31.If a fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate an unordained person, speaks of the low to the low, and so on… speaks of the low to the high, and so on… speaks of the high to the low, and so on… speaks of the high to the high, and says to one who is wise, capable, intelligent, learned, and a Dhamma-speaker: “You are wise, capable, intelligent, learned, a Dhamma-speaker; there is no bad destination for you, only a good destination is to be expected for you.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong doing.

Upasampanno anupasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘santi idhekacce caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā, natthi tesaṃ duggati, sugatiyeva tesaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate an unordained person, says: “There are here some outcastes, reed workers, hunters, chariot makers, or scavengers,” and so on… “There are here some wise, capable, intelligent, learned Dhamma-speakers; there is no bad destination for them, only a good destination is to be expected.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong doing.

Upasampanno anupasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘ye nūna caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Ye nūna paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate an unordained person, says: “Indeed, there are outcastes, reed workers, hunters, chariot makers, or scavengers,” and so on… “Indeed, there are wise, capable, intelligent, learned Dhamma-speakers.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong doing.

Upasampanno anupasampannaṃ khuṃsetukāmo vambhetukāmo maṅkukattukāmo evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘na mayaṃ caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Na mayaṃ paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā, natthamhākaṃ duggati, sugatiyeva amhākaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati. Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dukkaṭassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate an unordained person, says: “We are not outcastes, reed workers, hunters, chariot makers, or scavengers,” and so on… “We are not wise, capable, intelligent, learned Dhamma-speakers; there is no bad destination for us, only a good destination is to be expected.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong doing.

32. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ na khuṃsetukāmo na vambhetukāmo na maṅkukattukāmo, davakamyatā hīnena hīnaṃ vadeti, caṇḍālaṃ venaṃ nesādaṃ rathakāraṃ pukkusaṃ – ‘‘caṇḍālosi, venosi, nesādosi, rathakārosi, pukkusosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

32.A fully ordained bhikkhu, not wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, but intending to be amusing, speaks of the low to the low, saying to an outcaste, a reed worker, a hunter, a chariot maker, or a scavenger: “You are an outcaste, a reed worker, a hunter, a chariot maker, a scavenger.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong speech.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ na khuṃsetukāmo na vambhetukāmo na maṅkukattukāmo, davakamyatā hīnena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, khattiyaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ – ‘‘caṇḍālosi, venosi, nesādosi, rathakārosi, pukkusosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, not wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, but intending to be amusing, speaks of the low to the high, saying to a noble or a brahmin: “You are an outcaste, a reed worker, a hunter, a chariot maker, a scavenger.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong speech.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ na khuṃsetukāmo na vambhetukāmo na maṅkukattukāmo, davakamyatā ukkaṭṭhena hīnaṃ vadeti, caṇḍālaṃ venaṃ nesādaṃ rathakāraṃ pukkusaṃ – ‘‘khattiyosi, brāhmaṇosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, not wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, but intending to be amusing, speaks of the high to the low, saying to an outcaste, a reed worker, a hunter, a chariot maker, or a scavenger: “You are a noble, a brahmin.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong speech.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ na khuṃsetukāmo na vambhetukāmo na maṅkukattukāmo, davakamyatā ukkaṭṭhena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, khattiyaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ – ‘‘khattiyosi, brāhmaṇosī’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, not wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, but intending to be amusing, speaks of the high to the high, saying to a noble or a brahmin: “You are a noble, a brahmin.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong speech.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ na khuṃsetukāmo na vambhetukāmo na maṅkukattukāmo, davakamyatā hīnena hīnaṃ vadeti…pe… hīnena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti…pe… ukkaṭṭhena hīnaṃ vadeti…pe… ukkaṭṭhena ukkaṭṭhaṃ vadeti, paṇḍitaṃ byattaṃ medhāviṃ bahussutaṃ dhammakathikaṃ – ‘‘paṇḍitosi, byattosi, medhāvīsi, bahussutosi, dhammakathikosi, natthi tuyhaṃ duggati, sugati yeva tuyhaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

33. Upasampanno upasampannaṃ na khuṃsetukāmo na vambhetukāmo na maṅkukattukāmo, davakamyatā evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘santi idhekacce caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Santi idhekacce paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā, natthi tesaṃ duggati, sugati yeva tesaṃ pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

33.A fully ordained bhikkhu, not wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, but out of playfulness, speaks thus: “There are here some outcastes, reed workers, hunters, chariot makers, or scavengers,” and so on… “There are here some wise, capable, intelligent, learned Dhamma-speakers; there is no bad destination for them, only a good destination is to be expected.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong speech.

Upasampanno upasampannaṃ na khuṃsetukāmo na vambhetukāmo na maṅkukattukāmo, davakamyatā evaṃ vadeti, ‘‘ye nūna caṇḍālā venā nesādā rathakārā pukkusā’’ti bhaṇati…pe…. ‘‘Ye nūna paṇḍitā byattā medhāvī bahussutā dhammakathikā’’ti bhaṇati, āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

A fully ordained bhikkhu, not wishing to revile, disparage, or humiliate another fully ordained bhikkhu, but intending to be amusing, speaks thus: “Indeed, there are outcastes, reed workers, hunters, chariot makers, or scavengers,” and so on… “Indeed, there are wise, capable, intelligent, learned Dhamma-speakers.” For each utterance, there is an offense of speech, an offense of wrong speech.

Āpatti vācāya, vācāya dubbhāsitassa.

34. 35. Anāpatti atthapurekkhārassa, dhammapurekkhārassa, anusāsanipurekkhārassa, ummattakassa, khittacittassa, vedanāṭṭassa, ādikammikassāti.

35.There is no offense for one who explains the meaning of the Pali text, for one who recites the Pali text, for one who gives instruction, for one who is insane, for one whose mind is deranged, for one afflicted by pain, or for the first offender.

Omasavādasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The second training rule on malicious speech is finished.
