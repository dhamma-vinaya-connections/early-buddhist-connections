# BU-PC 19

## 9. Mahallakavihārasikkhāpadaṃ

## 9.The Training Rule Concerning a Large Dwelling

134. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato channassa upaṭṭhāko mahāmatto āyasmato channassa vihāraṃ kārāpeti. Atha kho āyasmā channo katapariyositaṃ vihāraṃ punappunaṃ chādāpeti, punappunaṃ lepāpeti. Atibhārito vihāro paripati. Atha kho āyasmā channo tiṇañca kaṭṭhañca saṃkaḍḍhanto aññatarassa brāhmaṇassa yavakhettaṃ dūsesi. Atha kho so brāhmaṇo ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhadantā amhākaṃ yavakhettaṃ dūsessantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tassa brāhmaṇassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā channo katapariyositaṃ vihāraṃ punappunaṃ chādāpessati, punappunaṃ lepāpessati, atibhārito vihāro paripatī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ channaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, channa, katapariyositaṃ vihāraṃ punappunaṃ chādāpesi, punappunaṃ lepāpesi, atibhārito vihāro paripatī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, katapariyositaṃ vihāraṃ punappunaṃ chādāpessasi, punappunaṃ lepāpessasi, atibhārito vihāro paripati! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

134.At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was dwelling at Kosambī in Ghosita’s Park. Now at that time a high official, the attendant of Venerable Channa, was having a dwelling built for Venerable Channa. Then Venerable Channa repeatedly had the completed dwelling re-roofed and re-plastered. The dwelling, being overburdened, collapsed. Then Venerable Channa, while gathering grass and wood, damaged a certain brahmin’s barley field. Then that brahmin complained, grumbled, and protested, saying, “How can these venerable ones damage our barley field?” The bhikkhus heard that brahmin complaining, grumbling, and protesting. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, grumbled, and protested, saying, “How can Venerable Channa repeatedly have a completed dwelling re-roofed and re-plastered, so that the dwelling, being overburdened, collapses?” Then those bhikkhus, having rebuked Venerable Channa in many ways, informed the Blessed One of the matter… “Is it true, Channa, that you repeatedly had a completed dwelling re-roofed and re-plastered, so that the dwelling, being overburdened, collapses?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked him… “Foolish man, how can you repeatedly have a completed dwelling re-roofed and re-plastered, so that the dwelling, being overburdened, collapses? This will not lead to confidence in the unconvinced…” … “And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:

135. ‘‘Mahallakaṃ pana bhikkhunā vihāraṃ kārayamānena yāvadvārakosā aggaḷaṭṭhapanāya ālokasandhiparikammāya dvatticchadanassa pariyāya appaharite ṭhitena adhiṭṭhātabbaṃ. Tato ce uttariṃ appaharitepi ṭhito adhiṭṭhaheyya pācittiya’’nti.

135.“When a bhikkhu is having a large dwelling built, he may designate (the work) up to the door frame, for the fixing of the bolt, for the making of window spaces, and for two or three layers of roofing, while standing in an unplanted area. If he designates more than that while standing in an unplanted area, he commits an offense entailing confession.”

136. Mahallako nāma vihāro sassāmiko vuccati.

136.A large dwelling is called one that has an owner.

Vihāro nāma ullitto vā hoti avalitto vā ullittāvalitto vā.

A dwelling is either plastered, unplastered, or both plastered and unplastered.

Kārayamānenāti karonto vā kārāpento vā.

“Having built” means doing it oneself or having it done.

Yāva dvārakosāti piṭṭhasaṅghāṭassa [pīṭhasaṃghāṭassa (itipi), piṭṭhisaṅghāṭassa (syā.)] samantā hatthapāsā.

“Up to the door frame” means a hand’s span all around the back doorjamb.

Aggaḷaṭṭhapanāyāti dvāraṭṭhapanāya.

“For the fixing of the bolt” means for the fixing of the door.

Ālokasandhiparikammāyāti vātapānaparikammāya setavaṇṇaṃ kāḷavaṇṇaṃ gerukaparikammaṃ mālākammaṃ latākammaṃ makaradantakaṃ pañcapaṭikaṃ.

“For the making of window spaces” means for the making of ventilation holes, whitewashing, blackening, red-ochre work, garland work, creeper work, makara-tooth work, or five-layered work.

Dvatticchadanassa  pariyāyaṃ appaharite ṭhitena adhiṭṭhātabbanti – haritaṃ nāma pubbaṇṇaṃ aparaṇṇaṃ. Sace harite ṭhito adhiṭṭhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Maggena chādentassa dve magge adhiṭṭhahitvā tatiyaṃ maggaṃ āṇāpetvā pakkamitabbaṃ. Pariyāyena chādentassa dve pariyāye adhiṭṭhahitvā tatiyaṃ pariyāyaṃ āṇāpetvā pakkamitabbaṃ.

“For two or three layers of roofing, while standing in an unplanted area, he may designate (the work)”: “Planted” means early crops and late crops. If he designates (the work) while standing in a planted area, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If one is roofing in a straight line, having designated two lines, one should order for the third line and depart. If one is roofing in layers, having designated two layers, one should order for the third layer and depart.

137. Tato ce uttari appaharitepi ṭhito adhiṭṭhaheyyāti iṭṭhakāya 2.0262 chādentassa iṭṭhakiṭṭhakāya āpatti pācittiyassa. Silāya chādentassa silāya silāya āpatti pācittiyassa. Sudhāya chādentassa piṇḍe piṇḍe āpatti pācittiyassa. Tiṇena chādentassa karaḷe karaḷe āpatti pācittiyassa. Paṇṇena chādentassa paṇṇe paṇṇe āpatti pācittiyassa.

137.If, after that, even if he stands in an unplanted area and designates more: for roofing with bricks, for each brick, there is an offense entailing confession. For roofing with stones, for each stone, there is an offense entailing confession. For roofing with plaster, for each lump, there is an offense entailing confession. For roofing with grass, for each bundle, there is an offense entailing confession. For roofing with leaves, for each leaf, there is an offense entailing confession.

Atirekadvattipariyāye atirekasaññī adhiṭṭhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Atirekadvattipariyāye vematiko adhiṭṭhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Atirekadvattipariyāye ūnakasaññī adhiṭṭhāti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

If, in the case of more than two or three layers, one perceives it as more and designates (the work), there is an offense entailing confession. If, in the case of more than two or three layers, one is uncertain and designates (the work), there is an offense entailing confession. If, in the case of more than two or three layers, one perceives it as less and designates (the work), there is an offense entailing confession.

Ūnakadvattipariyāye atirekasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakadvattipariyāye vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakadvattipariyāye ūnakasaññī, anāpatti.

If, in the case of less than two or three layers, one perceives it as more, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, in the case of less than two or three layers, one is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, in the case of less than two or three layers, one perceives it as less, there is no offense.

138. Anāpatti dvattipariyāye, ūnakadvattipariyāye, leṇe, guhāya, tiṇakuṭikāya, aññassatthāya, attano dhanena, vāsāgāraṃ ṭhapetvā sabbattha anāpatti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

138.There is no offense in two or three layers, in less than two or three layers, in a rock dwelling, in a cave, in a grass hut, for another’s benefit, with one’s own wealth, or in all cases except for a dwelling place; for one who is insane, for a first offender.

Mahallakavihārasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The training rule concerning a large dwelling, the ninth, is finished.
