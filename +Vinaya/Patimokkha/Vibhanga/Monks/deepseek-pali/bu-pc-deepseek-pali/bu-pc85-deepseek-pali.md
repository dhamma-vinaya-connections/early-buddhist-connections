# BU-PC 85

## 3. Vikālagāmappavisanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 3.The training rule on entering a village at an unseasonable time.

508. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitvā sabhāyaṃ nisīditvā anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathenti, seyyathidaṃ – [imā tiracchānakathāyo dī. 1.17; ma. ni. 2.223; saṃ. ni.; a. ni. 10.69] rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ mahāmattakathaṃ senākathaṃ bhayakathaṃ yuddhakathaṃ annakathaṃ pānakathaṃ vatthakathaṃ sayanakathaṃ mālākathaṃ gandhakathaṃ ñātikathaṃ yānakathaṃ gāmakathaṃ nigamakathaṃ nagarakathaṃ janapadakathaṃ itthikathaṃ [itthikathaṃ purisakathaṃ (ka.) majjhimapaṇṇāsaṭṭhakathā 156 piṭṭhe oloketabbā] sūrakathaṃ visikhākathaṃ kumbhaṭṭhānakathaṃ pubbapetakathaṃ nānattakathaṃ lokakkhāyikaṃ samuddakkhāyikaṃ itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vā. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitvā sabhāyaṃ nisīditvā anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathessanti, seyyathidaṃ – rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ…pe… itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vā, seyyathāpi gihī kāmabhogino’’ti!

508.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group of six bhikkhus, having entered a village at an unseasonable time, sat down in the assembly hall and engaged in various kinds of animal talk, namely, talk about kings, talk about thieves, talk about ministers, talk about armies, talk about dangers, talk about battles, talk about food, talk about drink, talk about clothes, talk about beds, talk about garlands, talk about perfumes, talk about relatives, talk about vehicles, talk about villages, talk about towns, talk about cities, talk about countries, talk about women, talk about heroes, talk about streets, talk about places where pots are kept, talk about past relatives, talk about various things, stories about the world, stories about the ocean, talk about existence and non-existence, and so on. People complained, grumbled, and criticized, saying: “How can these Sakyan ascetics, having entered a village at an unseasonable time, sit down in the assembly hall and engage in various kinds of animal talk—namely, talk about kings, talk about thieves… talk about existence and non-existence—just like householders who enjoy sensual pleasures?”

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitvā sabhāyaṃ nisīditvā anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathessanti, seyyathidaṃ – rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ…pe… itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vā’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitvā sabhāyaṃ nisīditvā anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathetha, seyyathidaṃ – rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ…pe… itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitvā sabhāyaṃ nisīditvā anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathessatha, seyyathidaṃ – rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ…pe… itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vā! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

The bhikkhus heard these people complaining, grumbling, and criticizing. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, grumbled, and criticized, saying: “How can the group of six bhikkhus, having entered a village at an unseasonable time, sit in the assembly hall and engage in various kinds of animal talk, namely, talk about kings, talk about thieves… or about existence and non-existence?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that having entered a village at an unseasonable time, you sit in the assembly hall and engage in various kinds of animal talk, namely, talk about kings, talk about thieves… or about existence and non-existence, just like householders who enjoy sensual pleasures?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “How can you, foolish men, having entered a village at an unseasonable time, sit in the assembly hall and engage in various kinds of animal talk, namely, talk about kings, talk about thieves… or about existence and non-existence! This will not lead to confidence in the unconvinced… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu vikāle gāmaṃ paviseyya, pācittiya’’nti.

“Should any bhikkhu enter a village at an unseasonable time, it is a pācittiya.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

509. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ gacchantā sāyaṃ aññataraṃ gāmaṃ upagacchiṃsu. Manussā te bhikkhū passitvā etadavocuṃ – ‘‘pavisatha, bhante’’ti. Atha kho te bhikkhū – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitu’’nti kukkuccāyantā na pavisiṃsu. Corā te bhikkhū acchindiṃsu. Atha kho te bhikkhū sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, āpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ pavisituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

509.Now at that time, several bhikkhus traveling through the Kosala countryside towards Sāvatthī arrived at a certain village in the evening. Seeing the bhikkhus, the people said, “Enter, venerable sirs.” But those bhikkhus, being scrupulous and thinking, “Entering a village at an unseasonable time has been prohibited by the Blessed One,” did not enter. Thieves robbed those bhikkhus. Then those bhikkhus, having gone to Sāvatthī, informed the bhikkhus about the matter. The bhikkhus informed the Blessed One about the matter. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, in connection with this incident, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “I allow, bhikkhus, to enter a village at an unseasonable time after asking. And thus, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu anāpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ paviseyya, pācittiya’’nti.

“Should any bhikkhu enter a village at an unseasonable time without asking, it is a pācittiya.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

510. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ gacchanto sāyaṃ aññataraṃ gāmaṃ upagacchi. Manussā taṃ bhikkhuṃ passitvā etadavocuṃ – ‘‘pavisatha, bhante’’ti. Atha kho so bhikkhu – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ anāpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitu’’nti kukkuccāyanto na pāvisi. Corā taṃ bhikkhuṃ acchindiṃsu. Atha kho so bhikkhu sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, santaṃ bhikkhuṃ āpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ pavisituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

510.Now at that time, a certain bhikkhu, while traveling through the Kosala countryside towards Sāvatthī, arrived in the evening at a certain village. The people, seeing that bhikkhu, said to him, “Enter, venerable sir.” Then that bhikkhu, thinking, “The Blessed One has prohibited entering a village at an unseasonable time without asking,” hesitated and did not enter. Thieves robbed that bhikkhu. Then that bhikkhu, having gone to Sāvatthī, informed the bhikkhus about this matter. The bhikkhus informed the Blessed One about this matter. Then the Blessed One, having given a Dhamma talk on this occasion, in connection with this incident, addressed the bhikkhus: “I allow, bhikkhus, to enter a village at an unseasonable time after asking a resident bhikkhu. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ paviseyya, pācittiya’’nti.

“Should any bhikkhu enter a village at an unseasonable time without asking a resident bhikkhu, it is a pācittiya.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

511. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu ahinā daṭṭho hoti. Aññataro bhikkhu ‘‘aggiṃ āharissāmī’’ti gāmaṃ gacchati. Atha kho so bhikkhu – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ pavisitu’’nti kukkuccāyanto na pāvisi…pe… bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi, bhikkhave, tathārūpe accāyike karaṇīye santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ pavisituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

511.At that time, a certain bhikkhu was bitten by a snake. Another bhikkhu, thinking, "I will fetch fire," went to the village. Then that bhikkhu, being apprehensive—"The Blessed One has prohibited entering a village at an unseasonable time without informing an available bhikkhu"—did not enter… they reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, having given a Dhamma talk on this occasion and regarding this matter, addressed the bhikkhus: "I allow, bhikkhus, in such urgent matters, to enter a village at an unseasonable time without informing an available bhikkhu. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

512. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā vikāle gāmaṃ paviseyya, aññatra tathārūpā accāyikā karaṇīyā, pācittiya’’nti.

512.“Should any bhikkhu, without informing an available bhikkhu, enter a village at an unseasonable time, unless there is such an urgent matter, it is a pācittiya.”

513. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

513.‘Any bhikkhu whatsoever’ (yo pana): any bhikkhu of whatever sort… ‘bhikkhu’ (bhikkhu): the bhikkhu intended in this context.

Santo nāma bhikkhu sakkā hoti āpucchā pavisituṃ.

An available bhikkhu (santo) is one who can be informed before entering.

Asanto nāma bhikkhu na sakkā hoti āpucchā pavisituṃ.

An unavailable bhikkhu (asanto) is one who cannot be informed before entering.

Vikālo nāma majjhanhike vītivatte yāva aruṇuggamanā.

Unseasonable time (vikālo) is from the passing of midday until dawn.

Gāmaṃ paviseyyāti parikkhittassa gāmassa parikkhepaṃ atikkamantassa āpatti pācittiyassa. Aparikkhittassa gāmassa upacāraṃ okkamantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘Enters a village’ (gāmaṃ paviseyya): for one who crosses the boundary of an enclosed village, there is an offense of pācittiya. For one who steps into the precincts of an unenclosed village, there is an offense of pācittiya.

Aññatra tathārūpā accāyikā karaṇīyāti ṭhapetvā tathārūpaṃ accāyikaṃ karaṇīyaṃ.

‘Unless there is such an urgent matter’ (aññatra tathārūpā accāyikā karaṇīyā): this means excluding such an urgent matter.

514. Vikāle vikālasaññī santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā gāmaṃ pavisati, aññatra tathārūpā accāyikā karaṇīyā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Vikāle vematiko santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā gāmaṃ pavisati, aññatra tathārūpā accāyikā karaṇīyā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Vikāle kālasaññī santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā gāmaṃ pavisati, aññatra tathārūpā accāyikā karaṇīyā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

514.If it is an unseasonable time, and he perceives it as an unseasonable time, and without informing an available bhikkhu, he enters a village, unless there is such an urgent matter, there is an offense of pācittiya. If it is an unseasonable time, but he is uncertain, and without informing an available bhikkhu, he enters a village, unless there is such an urgent matter, there is an offense of pācittiya. If it is an unseasonable time, but he perceives it as the seasonable time, and without informing an available bhikkhu, he enters a village, unless there is such an urgent matter, there is an offense of pācittiya.

Kāle vikālasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Kāle vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Kāle kālasaññī, anāpatti.

If it is the seasonable time, and he perceives it as an unseasonable time, there is an offense of dukkata. If it is the seasonable time, and he is uncertain, there is an offense of dukkata. If it is the seasonable time, and he perceives it as the seasonable time, there is no offense.

515. Anāpatti tathārūpe accāyike karaṇīye, santaṃ bhikkhuṃ āpucchā pavisati, asantaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā pavisati, antarārāmaṃ gacchati, bhikkhunupassayaṃ gacchati, titthiyaseyyaṃ gacchati, paṭikkamanaṃ gacchati, gāmena maggo hoti, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

515.There is no offense: in such an urgent matter; if he enters after informing an available bhikkhu; if he enters without informing an unavailable bhikkhu; if he goes within the monastery grounds; if he goes to the nun's residence; if he goes to a sectarian's lodging; if he goes to relieve himself; if the path goes through the village; in dangers; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Vikālagāmappavisanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The third training rule on entering a village at an unseasonable time is concluded.
