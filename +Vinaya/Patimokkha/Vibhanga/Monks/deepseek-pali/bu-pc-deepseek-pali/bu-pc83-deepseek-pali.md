# BU-PC 83

## 1. Antepurasikkhāpadaṃ

## 1.The Training Rule Concerning the Inner Apartments.

494. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena rājā pasenadi kosalo uyyānapālaṃ āṇāpesi – ‘‘gaccha, bhaṇe, uyyānaṃ sodhehi. Uyyānaṃ gamissāmā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, devā’’ti kho so uyyānapālo rañño pasenadissa kosalassa paṭissutvā uyyānaṃ sodhento addasa bhagavantaṃ aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle nisinnaṃ. Disvāna yena rājā pasenadi kosalo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘suddhaṃ, deva, uyyānaṃ. Apica, bhagavā tattha nisinno’’ti. ‘‘Hotu, bhaṇe! Mayaṃ bhagavantaṃ payirupāsissāmā’’ti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo uyyānaṃ gantvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro upāsako bhagavantaṃ payirupāsanto nisinno hoti. Addasā kho rājā pasenadi kosalo taṃ upāsakaṃ bhagavantaṃ payirupāsantaṃ nisinnaṃ. Disvāna bhīto aṭṭhāsi. Atha kho rañño pasenadissa kosalassa etadahosi – ‘‘nārahatāyaṃ puriso pāpo hotuṃ, yathā bhagavantaṃ payirupāsatī’’ti. Yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Atha kho so upāsako bhagavato gāravena rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ neva abhivādesi na paccuṭṭhāsi. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo anattamano ahosi – ‘‘kathañhi nāmāyaṃ puriso mayi āgate neva abhivādessati na paccuṭṭhessatī’’ti! Atha kho bhagavā rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ anattamanaṃ viditvā rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘eso kho, mahārāja, upāsako bahussuto āgatāgamo kāmesu vītarāgo’’ti. Atha kho rañño pasenadissa kosalassa etadahosi – ‘‘nārahatāyaṃ upāsako orako hotuṃ, bhagavāpi imassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsatī’’ti. Taṃ upāsakaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘vadeyyāsi, upāsaka, yena attho’’ti. ‘‘Suṭṭhu, devā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassito samādapito samuttejito sampahaṃsito uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi.

494.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Now at that time, King Pasenadi of Kosala ordered his park-keeper: “Go, good fellow, clean the park. We will visit the park.” “Yes, Your Majesty,” replied the park-keeper to King Pasenadi of Kosala. While cleaning the park, he saw the Blessed One seated at the foot of a certain tree. Having seen him, he approached King Pasenadi of Kosala and said: “The park is clean, Your Majesty. Moreover, the Blessed One is seated there.” “Very well, good fellow! We shall pay respects to the Blessed One.” Then King Pasenadi of Kosala went to the park and approached the Blessed One. Now at that time, a certain lay follower was seated, paying respects to the Blessed One. King Pasenadi of Kosala saw that lay follower seated, attending on the Blessed One. Upon seeing him, he was afraid and stood there. Then it occurred to King Pasenadi of Kosala: “This man cannot be wicked, since he attends on the Blessed One.” He approached the Blessed One, paid homage to the Blessed One, and sat down to one side. Then that lay follower, out of reverence for the Blessed One, neither paid homage to King Pasenadi of Kosala nor rose to greet him. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala became displeased: “How can this man not pay homage to me or rise to greet me when I have arrived?” Then the Blessed One, knowing that King Pasenadi of Kosala was displeased, said to him: “This lay follower, great king, is very learned, versed in tradition, and free from lust for sensual pleasures.” Then it occurred to King Pasenadi of Kosala: “This lay follower cannot be insignificant, since even the Blessed One speaks his praise.” He said to that lay follower: “Speak, lay follower, if there is anything you wish.” “Very well, Your Majesty.” Then the Blessed One instructed, aroused, inspired, and gladdened King Pasenadi of Kosala with a Dhamma talk. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala, having been instructed, aroused, inspired, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and departed.

495. Tena kho pana samayena rājā pasenadi kosalo uparipāsādavaragato hoti. Addasā kho rājā pasenadi kosalo taṃ upāsakaṃ rathikāya [rathiyāya (itipi)] chattapāṇiṃ gacchantaṃ. Disvāna pakkosāpetvā etadavoca – ‘‘tvaṃ kira, upāsaka, bahussuto āgatāgamo. Sādhu, upāsaka, amhākaṃ itthāgāraṃ dhammaṃ vācehī’’ti. ‘‘Yamahaṃ [yampāhaṃ (sī.)], deva, jānāmi ayyānaṃ vāhasā, ayyāva devassa itthāgāraṃ dhammaṃ vācessantī’’ti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo – ‘‘saccaṃ kho upāsako āhā’’ti yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho rājā pasenadi kosalo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sādhu, bhante, bhagavā ekaṃ bhikkhuṃ āṇāpetu yo amhākaṃ itthāgāraṃ dhammaṃ vācessatī’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi…pe… padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘tenahānanda, rañño itthāgāraṃ dhammaṃ vācehī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissutvā kālena kālaṃ pavisitvā rañño itthāgāraṃ dhammaṃ vāceti. Atha kho āyasmā ānando pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena rañño pasenadissa kosalassa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami.

495.At that time, King Pasenadi of Kosala had gone up to the upper palace terrace. He saw that lay follower walking along the street with a parasol in hand. Having seen him, the king summoned him and said, “It seems, lay follower, that you are learned and versed in tradition. Good, lay follower, please recite the Dhamma to our women’s quarters.” “What I know, sire, is through the noble ones; the noble ones themselves should recite the Dhamma to your women’s quarters.” Then King Pasenadi of Kosala, thinking, “The lay follower speaks the truth,” approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, King Pasenadi of Kosala said to the Blessed One, “Good, venerable sir, let the Blessed One appoint a bhikkhu to recite the Dhamma to our women’s quarters.” Then the Blessed One instructed, aroused, inspired, and gladdened King Pasenadi of Kosala with a Dhamma talk… Having circumambulated the Blessed One, he departed. Then the Blessed One addressed Venerable Ānanda: “Well then, Ānanda, recite the Dhamma to the king’s women’s quarters.” “Yes, venerable sir,” Venerable Ānanda replied to the Blessed One. Then, entering at the proper time, he recited the Dhamma to the king’s women’s quarters. Then Venerable Ānanda, having dressed in the morning and taken his bowl and robe, went to the residence of King Pasenadi of Kosala.

496. Tena kho pana samayena rājā pasenadi kosalo mallikāya deviyā saddhiṃ sayanagato hoti. Addasā kho mallikā devī āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna sahasā vuṭṭhāsi; pītakamaṭṭhaṃ dussaṃ pabhassittha. Atha kho āyasmā ānando tatova paṭinivattitvā ārāmaṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā ānando pubbe appaṭisaṃvidito rañño antepuraṃ pavisissatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, ānanda, pubbe appaṭisaṃvidito rañño antepuraṃ pavisasīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, ānanda, pubbe appaṭisaṃvidito rañño antepuraṃ pavisissasi! Netaṃ, ānanda, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… vigarahitvā…pe… dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi –

496.At that time, King Pasenadi of Kosala was lying down with Queen Mallikā. Queen Mallikā saw Venerable Ānanda coming from afar. Having seen him, she quickly got up, and her yellow robe slipped off. Then Venerable Ānanda turned back right there, went to the monastery, and informed the bhikkhus about the matter. Those bhikkhus who were modest… grumbled, criticized, and condemned him: “How could Venerable Ānanda enter the king’s inner palace without prior notice?”… “Is it true, Ānanda, that you entered the king’s inner palace without prior notice?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “How could you, Ānanda, enter the king’s inner palace without prior notice! This will not lead to confidence in those who are without it…” Having rebuked him… he gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus:

497. [a. ni. 10.45] ‘‘Dasayime, bhikkhave, ādīnavā rājantepurappavesane. Katame dasa? Idha, bhikkhave, rājā mahesiyā saddhiṃ nisinno hoti, tattha bhikkhu pavisati. Mahesī vā bhikkhuṃ disvā sitaṃ pātukaroti. Bhikkhu vā mahesiṃ disvā sitaṃ pātukaroti. Tattha rañño evaṃ hoti – ‘‘addhā imesaṃ kataṃ vā karissanti vā’’ti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, paṭhamo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

497.“bhikkhus, there are these ten drawbacks in entering the royal inner palace. What ten? Here, bhikkhus, the king is sitting with his chief queen, and a bhikkhu enters. The chief queen, seeing the bhikkhu, might smile. Or the bhikkhu, seeing the chief queen, might smile. Then the king thinks, ‘Surely, these two have done something, or will do something.’ This, bhikkhus, is the first drawback in entering the royal inner palace.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rājā bahukicco bahukaraṇīyo. Aññataraṃ itthiṃ gantvā nassarati. Sā tena gabbhaṃ gaṇhi. Tattha rañño evaṃ hoti – ‘‘na kho idha añño koci pavisati aññatra pabbajitena. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dutiyo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a king is very busy and has much to do. He goes to a certain woman and then forgets about her. She conceives a child by him. Then the king thinks: ‘No one else enters here except a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the second drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rañño antepure aññataraṃ ratanaṃ nassati. Tattha rañño evaṃ hoti – ‘‘na kho idha añño koci pavisati aññatra pabbajitena. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, tatiyo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, in the royal inner palace, some treasure is lost. Then the king thinks: ‘No one else enters here except a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the third drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rañño antepure abbhantarā guyhamantā bahiddhā sambhedaṃ gacchanti. Tattha rañño evaṃ hoti – ‘‘na kho idha añño koci pavisati aññatra pabbajitena. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, catuttho ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, in the royal inner palace, secret counsels from within are divulged outside. Then the king thinks: ‘No one else enters here except a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the fourth drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rañño antepure putto vā pitaraṃ pattheti pitā vā puttaṃ pattheti. Tesaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘‘na kho idha añño koci pavisati aññatra pabbajitena. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, pañcamo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, in the royal inner palace, a son might desire to kill his father, or a father might desire to kill his son. Then they think: ‘No one else enters here except a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the fifth drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rājā nīcaṭṭhāniyaṃ ucce ṭhāne ṭhapeti. Yesaṃ taṃ amanāpaṃ tesaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘‘rājā kho pabbajitena saṃsaṭṭho. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, chaṭṭho ādīnavo, rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a king may appoint a person of low status to a high position. Those who dislike this will think: ‘The king is associated with a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the sixth drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rājā uccaṭṭhāniyaṃ nīce ṭhāne ṭhapeti. Yesaṃ taṃ amanāpaṃ tesaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘‘rājā kho pabbajitena saṃsaṭṭho. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, sattamo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a king may place a person of high status in a low position. Those who dislike this will think: ‘The king is associated with a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the seventh drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rājā akāle senaṃ uyyojeti. Yesaṃ taṃ amanāpaṃ tesaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘‘rājā kho pabbajitena saṃsaṭṭho. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, aṭṭhamo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a king sends out the army at an inopportune time. Those who dislike this will think: ‘The king is associated with a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the eighth drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rājā kāle senaṃ uyyojetvā antarāmaggato nivattāpeti. Yesaṃ taṃ amanāpaṃ tesaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘‘‘rājā kho pabbajitena saṃsaṭṭho. Siyā nu kho pabbajitassa kamma’’nti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, navamo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a king, having sent forth an army at the right time, orders it to turn back along the way. Those who dislike this will think: ‘The king is associated with a renunciate. Could this be the renunciate’s doing?’ This, bhikkhus, is the ninth drawback in entering the royal inner palace.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, rañño rājantepuraṃ hatthisammaddaṃ assasammaddaṃ rathasammaddaṃ rajjanīyāni [rajjanīyāni (ka.)] rūpasaddagandharasaphoṭṭhabbāni, yāni na pabbajitassa sāruppāni. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dasamo ādīnavo rājantepurappavesane. Ime kho, bhikkhave, dasa ādīnavā rājantepurappavesane’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā dubbharatāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, in the king’s inner palace there is the throng of elephants, the throng of horses, the throng of chariots, and sights, sounds, smells, tastes, and tactile objects that incite passion, none of which are suitable for one gone forth. This, bhikkhus, is the tenth drawback in entering the king’s inner palace. These, bhikkhus, are the ten drawbacks in entering the king’s inner palace.” Then the Blessed One, having rebuked the Venerable Ānanda in many ways for being difficult to support… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

498. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu rañño khattiyassa muddhāvasittassa nti.

498.“Should any bhikkhu, without prior notification, cross the threshold of a consecrated Kshatriya king where the king has not departed and the chief queen (the jewel) has not departed, he commits an offense entailing expiation.”

499. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

499.‘Yo panā’ti: whoever… ‘bhikkhū’ti: …a bhikkhu is intended in this connection.

Khattiyo nāma ubhato sujāto hoti, mātito ca pitito ca saṃsuddhagahaṇiko, yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā akkhitto anupakuṭṭho jātivādena.

A Kshatriya is one who is well-born on both sides, from mother and from father, of pure conception, unblemished and irreproachable in regard to birth up to the seventh generation of ancestors.

Muddhāvasitto nāma khattiyābhisekena abhisitto hoti.

‘Muddhāvasitto’ means one who has been consecrated with a Kshatriya’s head-anointing.

Anikkhantarājaketi rājā sayanigharā anikkhanto hoti.

‘Anikkhantarājaka’ means the king has not departed from the sleeping chamber.

Aniggataratanaketi mahesī sayanigharā anikkhantā hoti, ubho vā anikkhantā honti.

‘Aniggataratanaka’ means the chief queen (the jewel) has not departed from the sleeping chamber, or both (the king and queen) have not departed.

Pubbe appaṭisaṃviditoti pubbe anāmantetvā.

‘Pubbe appaṭisaṃvidito’ means without prior consultation.

Indakhīlo nāma sayanigharassa ummāro vuccati.

‘Indakhīla’ is said to be the threshold of the sleeping chamber.

Sayanigharaṃ nāma yattha katthaci rañño sayanaṃ paññattaṃ hoti, antamaso sāṇipākāraparikkhittampi.

‘Sayanighara’ means any place where a bed is prepared for the king, even if it is enclosed only by a hemp-cloth wall.

Indakhīlaṃ atikkāmeyyāti paṭhamaṃ pādaṃ ummāraṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Dutiyaṃ pādaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘Indakhīlaṃ atikkāmeyya’ means: If he crosses the threshold with the first foot, there is an offense of wrong-doing. When he crosses with the second foot, there is an offense entailing expiation.

500. Appaṭisaṃvidite appaṭisaṃviditasaññī indakhīlaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Appaṭisaṃvidite vematiko indakhīlaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Appaṭisaṃvidite paṭisaṃviditasaññī indakhīlaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

500.If he crosses the threshold without notification, perceiving it as without notification, he commits an offense entailing expiation. If he crosses the threshold without notification, being uncertain, he commits an offense entailing expiation. If he crosses the threshold without notification, perceiving it as notified, he commits an offense entailing expiation.

Paṭisaṃvidite appaṭisaṃviditasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paṭisaṃvidite vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paṭisaṃvidite paṭisaṃviditasaññī, anāpatti.

If it is notified, and he perceives it as not notified, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is notified, and he is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is notified, and he perceives it as notified, there is no offense.

501. Anāpatti paṭisaṃvidite, na khattiyo hoti, na khattiyābhisekena abhisitto hoti, rājā sayanigharā nikkhanto hoti, mahesī sayanigharā nikkhantā hoti, ubho vā nikkhantā honti, na sayanighare, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

501.There is no offense if it is notified; if he is not a Kshatriya; if he has not been consecrated with a Kshatriya’s anointing; if the king has departed from the sleeping chamber; if the chief queen has departed from the sleeping chamber; or if both have departed; if it is not in the sleeping chamber; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Antepurasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The first training rule concerning the inner palace is concluded.
