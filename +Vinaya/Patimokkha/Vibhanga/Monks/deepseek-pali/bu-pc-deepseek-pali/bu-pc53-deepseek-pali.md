# BU-PC 53

## 3. Hasadhammasikkhāpadaṃ

## 3.The Training Rule Concerning Water-Play

335. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū aciravatiyā nadiyā udake kīḷanti. Tena kho pana samayena rājā pasenadi kosalo mallikāya deviyā saddhiṃ uparipāsādavaragato hoti. Addasā kho rājā pasenadi kosalo sattarasavaggiye bhikkhū aciravatiyā nadiyā udake kīḷante. Disvāna mallikaṃ deviṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ete te, mallike, arahanto udake kīḷantī’’ti. ‘‘Nissaṃsayaṃ kho, mahārāja, bhagavatā sikkhāpadaṃ apaññattaṃ. Te vā bhikkhū appakataññuno’’ti. Atha kho rañño pasenadissa kosalassa etadahosi – ‘‘kena nu kho ahaṃ upāyena bhagavato ca na āroceyyaṃ, bhagavā ca jāneyya ime bhikkhū udake kīḷitā’’ti? Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo sattarasavaggiye bhikkhū pakkosāpetvā mahantaṃ guḷapiṇḍaṃ adāsi – ‘‘imaṃ, bhante, guḷapiṇḍaṃ bhagavato dethā’’ti. Sattarasavaggiyā bhikkhū taṃ guḷapiṇḍaṃ ādāya yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘imaṃ, bhante, guḷapiṇḍaṃ rājā pasenadi kosalo bhagavato detī’’ti. ‘‘Kahaṃ pana tumhe, bhikkhave, rājā addasā’’ti. ‘‘Aciravatiyā nadiyā, bhagavā, udake kīḷante’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, udake kīḷissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

335.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika's Monastery. Now at that time, a group of seventeen bhikkhus were playing in the water of the Aciravati River. At that time, King Pasenadi of Kosala had gone to the upper story of his palace with Queen Mallika. King Pasenadi of Kosala saw the group of seventeen bhikkhus playing in the water of the Aciravati River. Having seen them, he said to Queen Mallika: "Mallika, these arahants of yours are playing in the water!" "Surely, great king, the Blessed One has not yet laid down a training rule. Or perhaps those bhikkhus are ignorant of the teaching." Then King Pasenadi of Kosala thought: "How can I avoid reporting this to the Blessed One, yet the Blessed One will know that these bhikkhus were playing in the water?" Then King Pasenadi of Kosala had the group of seventeen bhikkhus summoned and gave them a large lump of jaggery, saying: "Venerables, give this lump of jaggery to the Blessed One." The group of seventeen bhikkhus took that lump of jaggery and approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, King Pasenadi of Kosala gives this lump of jaggery to the Blessed One." "But where, bhikkhus, did the king see you?" "Venerable sir, he saw us playing in the water of the Aciravati River." The Blessed One rebuked them: “… Foolish men, how can you play in the water? This is not, foolish men, for inspiring confidence in the faithless… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus: —

336. ‘‘Udake hasadhamme [hassadhamme (sī. syā.)] pācittiya’’nti.

336."There is a pācittiya offense for playing in water."

337. Udake hasadhammo nāma uparigopphake udake hasādhippāyo nimujjati vā ummujjati vā palavati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

337.Water-play means that if, with the intention of playing, one submerges, emerges, or floats in water above the ankles, there is a pācittiya offense.

338. Udake hasadhamme hasadhammasaññī, āpatti pācittiyassa. Udake hasadhamme vematiko, āpatti pācittiyassa. Udake hasadhamme ahasadhammasaññī, āpatti pācittiyassa.

338.If, when there is water-play, one perceives it as water-play, there is a pācittiya offense. If, when there is water-play, one is doubtful, there is a pācittiya offense. If, when there is water-play, one perceives it as not water-play, there is a pācittiya offense.

Heṭṭhāgopphake udake kīḷati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Udake nāvāya kīḷati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Hatthena vā pādena vā kaṭṭhena vā kaṭhalāya vā udakaṃ paharati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Bhājanagataṃ udakaṃ vā kañjikaṃ vā khīraṃ vā takkaṃ vā rajanaṃ vā passāvaṃ vā cikkhallaṃ vā kīḷati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If one plays in water that is below the ankles, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one plays with a boat in water, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one strikes the water with one's hand, foot, a stick, or a potsherd, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one plays with water, sour gruel, milk, buttermilk, dye, urine, or mud that is in a vessel, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Udake ahasadhamme hasadhammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Udake ahasadhamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Udake ahasadhamme ahasadhammasaññī, anāpatti.

If, when there is not water-play, one perceives it as water-play, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, when there is not water-play, one is doubtful, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, when there is not water-play, one perceives it as not water-play, there is no offense.

339. Anāpatti na hasādhippāyo, sati karaṇīye udakaṃ otaritvā nimujjati vā ummujjati vā palavati vā, pāraṃ gacchanto nimujjati vā ummujjati vā palavati vā, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

339.There is no offense if there is no intention to play; if, while performing a necessary task, one enters the water and submerges, emerges, or floats; if, while crossing to the far shore, one submerges, emerges, or floats; in emergencies; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Hasadhammasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The Training Rule Concerning Water-Play, the third, is concluded.
