# BU-PC 82

## 12. Pariṇāmanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 12.The training rule concerning diversion.

489. [idaṃ vatthu pārā. 657] Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ aññatarassa pūgassa saṅghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ hoti – ‘‘bhojetvā cīvarena acchādessāmā’’ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū yena so pūgo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ pūgaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘dethāvuso, imāni cīvarāni imesaṃ bhikkhūna’’nti. ‘‘Na mayaṃ, bhante, dassāma. Amhākaṃ saṅghassa anuvassaṃ sacīvarabhikkhā paññattā’’ti. ‘‘Bahū, āvuso, saṅghassa dāyakā, bahū saṅghassa bhattā [bhaddā (ka.)]. Ime tumhe nissāya tumhe sampassantā idha viharanti. Tumhe ce imesaṃ na dassatha, atha ko carahi imesaṃ dassati? Dethāvuso, imāni cīvarāni imesaṃ bhikkhūna’’nti. Atha kho so pūgo chabbaggiyehi bhikkhūhi nippīḷiyamāno yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ datvā saṅghaṃ bhattena parivisi. Ye te bhikkhū jānanti saṅghassa sacīvarabhattaṃ paṭiyattaṃ ‘‘na ca jānanti chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ dinna’’nti te evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘oṇojethāvuso, saṅghassa cīvara’’nti. ‘‘Natthi, bhante. Yathāpaṭiyattaṃ cīvaraṃ ayyā chabbaggiyā ayyānaṃ chabbaggiyānaṃ pariṇāmesu’’nti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmessantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmethāti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

489.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park in Jeta’s Grove. Now at that time a certain group in Sāvatthī had prepared a meal with robes for the Saṅgha, thinking, “After feeding them, we will present the robes.” Then the group of six bhikkhus approached that group and said, “Friends, give these robes to these bhikkhus.” “We will not give them, venerable sirs. Our annual offering of robes with meals is designated for the Saṅgha.” “Friends, there are many donors to the Saṅgha, many who offer meals to the Saṅgha. These bhikkhus dwell here relying on you, looking up to you. If you do not give to them, then who will give to them? Friends, give these robes to these bhikkhus.” Pressured by the group of six bhikkhus, that group gave the prepared robes to the group of six bhikkhus and served the meal to the Saṅgha. Those bhikkhus who knew that the meal with robes had been prepared for the Saṅgha but did not know that it had been given to the group of six bhikkhus said, “Friends, offer the robes to the Saṅgha.” “There are none, venerable sirs. The prepared robes have been diverted by the venerable group of six bhikkhus to themselves.” The bhikkhus who were modest … criticized and complained, “How could the group of six bhikkhus knowingly divert what was designated for the Saṅgha to individuals?” … “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you knowingly diverted what was designated for the Saṅgha to individuals?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them … “Foolish men, how could you knowingly divert what was designated for the Saṅgha to individuals? This will not inspire confidence in those without it, nor increase it in those who have it…” And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

490. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ saṅghikaṃ lābhaṃ pariṇataṃ puggalassa pariṇāmeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

490.“Whatever bhikkhu, knowing that a gain designated for the Saṅgha, should divert it to an individual, incurs a pācittiya.”

491. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

491.‘Whatever bhikkhu’ means whatever kind of bhikkhu… ‘bhikkhu’ means a bhikkhu intended in this case.

Jānāti nāma sāmaṃ vā jānāti, aññe vā tassa ārocenti, so vā āroceti.

‘Knowing’ means he knows himself, or others inform him, or he informs others.

Saṅghikaṃ nāma saṅghassa dinnaṃ hoti pariccattaṃ.

‘Belonging to the Saṅgha’ means it has been given or relinquished to the Saṅgha.

Lābho nāma cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārā, antamaso cuṇṇapiṇḍopi, dantakaṭṭhampi, dasikasuttampi.

‘Gain’ means robes, almsfood, lodgings, and requisites for the sick, including medicines; even a lump of powder, a tooth-stick, or a piece of string.

Pariṇataṃ nāma ‘‘dassāma karissāmā’’ti vācā bhinnā hoti, taṃ puggalassa pariṇāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘Designated’ means the words ‘we will give’ or ‘we will do’ have been uttered; if he diverts it to an individual, he incurs a pācittiya.

492. Pariṇate pariṇatasaññī puggalassa pariṇāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Pariṇate vematiko puggalassa pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Pariṇate apariṇatasaññī puggalassa pariṇāmeti, anāpatti. Saṅghassa pariṇataṃ aññasaṅghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Cetiyassa pariṇataṃ aññacetiyassa vā saṅghassa vā puggalassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalassa pariṇataṃ aññapuggalassa vā saṅghassa vā cetiyassa vā pariṇāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apariṇate pariṇatasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apariṇate vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apariṇate apariṇatasaññī, anāpatti.

492.If, when it is designated, he diverts it to an individual, perceiving it as designated, he incurs a pācittiya. If, when it is designated, he diverts it to an individual, being in doubt, he incurs a dukkaṭa. If, when it is designated, he diverts it to an individual, not perceiving it as designated, there is no offense. If he diverts what is designated for the Saṅgha to another Saṅgha or to a shrine, he incurs a dukkaṭa. If he diverts what is designated for a shrine to another shrine, to the Saṅgha, or to an individual, he incurs a dukkaṭa. If he diverts what is designated for an individual to another individual, to the Saṅgha, or to a shrine, he incurs a dukkaṭa. If, when it is not designated, he perceives it as designated, he incurs a dukkaṭa. If, when it is not designated, he is in doubt, he incurs a dukkaṭa. If, when it is not designated, he does not perceive it as designated, there is no offense.

493. Anāpatti – ‘‘kattha demā’’ti pucchīyamāno – ‘‘yattha tumhākaṃ deyyadhammo paribhogaṃ vā labheyya paṭisaṅkhāraṃ vā labheyya ciraṭṭhitiko vā assa yattha vā pana tumhākaṃ cittaṃ pasīdati tattha dethā’’ti bhaṇati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

493.There is no offense: if, when asked, ‘Where should we give?’, he says, ‘Give where your gift would be used or refurbished, or would last for a long time, or where your mind is pleased’; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

Pariṇāmanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dvādasamaṃ.

The training rule concerning diversion is concluded, the twelfth.

Sahadhammikavaggo aṭṭhamo.

The section on What Is in Accordance with the Dhamma, the eighth, is concluded.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Sahadhamma-vivaṇṇañca, mohāpanaṃ pahārakaṃ;Talasatti amūlañca, sañcicca ca upassuti;Paṭibāhanachandañca, dabbañca pariṇāmananti.

What Is in Accordance with the Dhamma, disparagement, causing bewilderment, striking; A Talasatti, groundless, intentional eavesdropping; Obstruction of a wish, Dabba, and diversion.
