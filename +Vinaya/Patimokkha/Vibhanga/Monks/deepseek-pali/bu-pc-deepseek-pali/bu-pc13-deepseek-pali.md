# BU-PC 13

## 3. Ujjhāpanakasikkhāpadaṃ

## 3.The Training Rule on Finding Fault.

103. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā dabbo mallaputto saṅghassa senāsanañca paññapeti bhattāni ca uddisati. Tena kho pana samayena mettiyabhūmajakā [mettiyabhūmmajakā (sī. syā.)] bhikkhū navakā ceva honti appapuññā ca. Yāni saṅghassa lāmakāni senāsanāni tāni tesaṃ pāpuṇanti lāmakāni ca bhattāni. Te āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ bhikkhū ujjhāpenti – ‘‘chandāya dabbo mallaputto senāsanaṃ paññapeti, chandāya ca bhattāni uddisatī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ bhikkhū ujjhāpessantī’’ti…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ bhikkhū ujjhāpethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ bhikkhū ujjhāpessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

103.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels' Feeding Place. At that time, Venerable Dabba the Mallian was assigning lodgings and allocating meals for the Sangha. At that time, the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka were newly ordained and of little merit. The inferior lodgings of the Sangha fell to them, and inferior meals. They disparaged Venerable Dabba the Mallian to the bhikkhus, saying: "Dabba the Mallian assigns lodgings out of favoritism, and he allocates meals out of favoritism." Those bhikkhus who were modest… found fault, disparaged, and condemned them, saying: "How could the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka disparage Venerable Dabba the Mallian to the bhikkhus?" … "Is it true, bhikkhus, that you disparaged Dabba the Mallian to the bhikkhus?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Blessed One rebuked them: "… How could you foolish men disparage Dabba the Mallian to the bhikkhus! This will not lead to confidence in the unconvinced…" "… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus—"

‘‘Ujjhāpanake pācittiya’’nti.

"For causing to disparage, there is an offense entailing confession."

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

104. Tena kho pana samayena mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū – ‘‘bhagavatā ujjhāpanakaṃ paṭikkhitta’’nti, ‘‘ettāvatā bhikkhū sossantī’’ti [vihesissantīti (itipi)] bhikkhūnaṃ sāmantā āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ khiyyanti – ‘‘chandāya dabbo mallaputto senāsanaṃ paññapeti, chandāya ca bhattāni uddisatī’’ti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma mettiyabhūmajakā bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ khiyyissantī’’ti…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ khiyyathā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, dabbaṃ mallaputtaṃ khiyyissatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

104.At that time the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka, thinking, "Causing to disparage has been prohibited by the Blessed One," and "This much will make the bhikkhus listen," found fault with Venerable Dabba the Mallian in the vicinity of other bhikkhus, saying, "Dabba the Mallian assigns lodgings out of favoritism, and he allocates meals out of favoritism." Those bhikkhus who were modest… found fault, disparaged, and condemned them, saying, "How could the bhikkhus Mettiya and Bhūmajaka find fault with Venerable Dabba the Mallian?"… "Is it true, bhikkhus, that you find fault with Dabba the Mallian?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Blessed One rebuked them: "How could you, foolish men, find fault with Dabba the Mallian! This will not lead to confidence in the unconvinced…" "And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus—"

105. ‘‘Ujjhāpanake khiyyanake pācittiya’’nti.

105."For causing to disparage and for finding fault, there is an offense entailing confession."

106. Ujjhāpanakaṃ nāma upasampannaṃ saṅghena sammataṃ senāsanapaññāpakaṃ vā bhattuddesakaṃ vā yāgubhājakaṃ vā phalabhājakaṃ vā khajjabhājakaṃ vā appamattakavissajjakaṃ vā avaṇṇaṃ kattukāmo, ayasaṃ kattukāmo, maṅkukattukāmo, upasampannaṃ ujjhāpeti vā khiyyati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī ujjhāpanake khiyyanake āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme vematiko ujjhāpanake khiyyanake āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī ujjhāpanake khiyyanake āpatti pācittiyassa.

106.Causing to disparage means: If, wishing to cause disrepute, wishing to cause dishonor, wishing to cause embarrassment to a fully ordained bhikkhu approved by the Sangha as an assigner of lodgings, or a distributor of meals, or a distributor of gruel, or a distributor of fruit, or a distributor of sweets, or a distributor of minor requisites, he causes to disparage or finds fault with that fully ordained bhikkhu, there is an offense entailing confession. If, regarding a legitimate act, he perceives it as a legitimate act, and causes to disparage or finds fault, there is an offense entailing confession. If, regarding a legitimate act, he is uncertain, and causes to disparage or finds fault, there is an offense entailing confession. If, regarding a legitimate act, he perceives it as an illegitimate act, and causes to disparage or finds fault, there is an offense entailing confession.

Anupasampannaṃ ujjhāpeti vā khiyyati vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Upasampannaṃ saṅghena asammataṃ senāsanapaññāpakaṃ vā bhattuddesakaṃ vā yāgubhājakaṃ vā phalabhājakaṃ vā khajjabhājakaṃ vā appamattakavissajjakaṃ vā avaṇṇaṃ kattukāmo, ayasaṃ kattukāmo, maṅkukattukāmo, upasampannaṃ vā anupasampannaṃ vā ujjhāpeti vā khiyyati vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anupasampannaṃ saṅghena sammataṃ vā asammataṃ vā senāsanapaññāpakaṃ vā bhattuddesakaṃ vā yāgubhājakaṃ vā phalabhājakaṃ vā khajjabhājakaṃ vā appamattakavissajjakaṃ vā avaṇṇaṃ kattukāmo, ayasaṃ kattukāmo, maṅkukattukāmo, upasampannaṃ vā anupasampannaṃ vā ujjhāpeti vā khiyyati vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If he causes to disparage or finds fault with one who is not fully ordained, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, wishing to cause disrepute, wishing to cause dishonor, wishing to cause embarrassment to a fully ordained bhikkhu not approved by the Sangha as an assigner of lodgings, or a distributor of meals, or a distributor of gruel, or a distributor of fruit, or a distributor of sweets, or a distributor of minor requisites, he causes to disparage or finds fault with a fully ordained bhikkhu or one not fully ordained, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, wishing to cause disrepute, wishing to cause dishonor, wishing to cause embarrassment to one not fully ordained, whether approved or not approved by the Sangha as an assigner of lodgings, or a distributor of meals, or a distributor of gruel, or a distributor of fruit, or a distributor of sweets, or a distributor of minor requisites, he causes to disparage or finds fault with a fully ordained bhikkhu or one not fully ordained, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, regarding an illegitimate act, he perceives it as a legitimate act, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, regarding an illegitimate act, he is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If, regarding an illegitimate act, he perceives it as an illegitimate act, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

107. Anāpatti pakatiyā chandā dosā mohā bhayā karontaṃ ujjhāpeti vā khiyyati vā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

107.There is no offense for one who causes to disparage or finds fault with one who acts naturally out of favoritism, hatred, delusion, or fear; for one who is insane; or for the first offender.

Ujjhāpanakasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The third training rule on finding fault is finished.
