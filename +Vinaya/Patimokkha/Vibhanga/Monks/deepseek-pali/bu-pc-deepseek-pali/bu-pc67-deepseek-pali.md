# BU-PC 67

## 7. Saṃvidhānasikkhāpadaṃ

## 7.The training rule concerning arrangements.

412. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṃ gacchanto aññatarena gāmadvārena atikkamati. Aññatarā itthī sāmikena saha bhaṇḍitvā gāmato nikkhamitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ passitvā etadavoca – ‘‘kahaṃ, bhante, ayyo gamissatī’’ti? ‘‘Sāvatthiṃ kho ahaṃ, bhagini, gamissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Ahaṃ ayyena saddhiṃ gamissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Eyyāsi, bhaginī’’ti. Atha kho tassā itthiyā sāmiko gāmato nikkhamitvā manusse pucchi – ‘‘apāyyo [apayyā (sī. syā.)] evarūpiṃ itthiṃ passeyyāthā’’ti? ‘‘Esāyyo, pabbajitena saha gacchatī’’ti. Atha kho so puriso anubandhitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ gahetvā ākoṭetvā muñci. Atha kho so bhikkhu aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle padhūpento nisīdi. Atha kho sā itthī taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘nāyyo so bhikkhu maṃ nippātesi; apica, ahameva tena bhikkhunā saddhiṃ gacchāmi; akārako so bhikkhu; gaccha, naṃ khamāpehī’’ti. Atha kho so puriso taṃ bhikkhuṃ khamāpesi. Atha kho so bhikkhu sāvatthiṃ gantvā bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhu mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjissatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjasīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ, uddiseyyātha –

412.At that time, the Blessed One was staying at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery. On that occasion, a certain bhikkhu was traveling through the Kosala countryside on his way to Sāvatthī when he passed through a village gate. A certain woman, having quarreled with her husband, left the village and, seeing that bhikkhu, said to him: “Where are you going, Venerable?” “I am going to Sāvatthī, sister.” “I will go with you, Venerable.” “Come along, sister.” Then that woman’s husband, having left the village, asked people: “Did you see such a woman?” “Yes, sir, she is going with a monastic.” That man followed them, seized the bhikkhu, beat him, and then released him. The bhikkhu then sat down at the foot of a tree, fuming. The woman said to the man: “That bhikkhu did not take me away against my will. Rather, I myself went with that bhikkhu. That bhikkhu is innocent. Go and apologize to him.” The man then apologized to the bhikkhu. The bhikkhu went to Sāvatthī and informed the bhikkhus about the matter. Those bhikkhus who were modest … criticized and complained: “How could a bhikkhu arrange to travel together with a woman on the same road?” … “Is it true, bhikkhu, that you arranged to travel together with a woman on the same road?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him: “… Foolish man, how could you arrange to travel together with a woman on the same road! This will not inspire confidence in those without it …” And then, bhikkhus, you should recite this training rule:

413. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu mātugāmena saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjeyya, antamaso gāmantarampi, pācittiya’’nti.

413.“Whatever bhikkhu, having made an arrangement, should travel a road together with a woman, even as far as to another village, it is a Pācittiya.”

414. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

414.“Now whoever”: whoever of whatever sort … “A bhikkhu”: … the bhikkhu intended in this context.

Mātugāmo nāma manussitthī, na yakkhī na petī na tiracchānagatā viññū paṭibalā subhāsitadubbhāsitaṃ duṭṭhullāduṭṭhullaṃ ājānituṃ.

“A woman” means a human female, not a female yakkhini, not a female peta, not a female animal. She is intelligent, capable, and able to distinguish well-spoken from ill-spoken, and vulgar from non-vulgar.

Saddhinti ekato.

“Saddhiṃ” means “together.”

Saṃvidhāyāti – ‘‘gacchāma bhagini, gacchāmāyya, gacchāmāyya, gacchāma bhagini, ajja vā hiyyo vā pare vā gacchāmā’’ti saṃvidahati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

“Arranging”: If he arranges, saying, “Let’s go, sister; let’s go, venerable one; let’s go, venerable one; let’s go, sister; let’s go today or tomorrow or the day after,” there is an offense of wrong-doing.

Antamaso gāmantarampīti kukkuṭasampāte gāme gāmantare gāmantare āpatti pācittiyassa. Agāmake araññe addhayojane addhayojane āpatti pācittiyassa.

“Even as far as to another village”: in a village within cock-crow distance, between villages, at each interval of a village, there is an offense entailing confession. In an uninhabited area, in the wilderness, for every half-yojana, there is an offense entailing confession.

415. Mātugāme mātugāmasaññī saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti pācittiyassa. Mātugāme vematiko saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti pācittiyassa. Mātugāme amātugāmasaññī saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti pācittiyassa.

415.If a bhikkhu, perceiving a woman as a woman, arranges to travel together on a single road with her, even as far as another village, he commits a pācittiya offense. If a bhikkhu is in doubt whether it is a woman, and arranges to travel together on a single road with her, even as far as another village, he commits a pācittiya offense. If a bhikkhu does not perceive a woman as a woman, and arranges to travel together on a single road with her, even as far as another village, he commits a pācittiya offense.

Bhikkhu saṃvidahati mātugāmo na saṃvidahati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Yakkhiyā vā petiyā paṇḍakena vā tiracchānagatamanussaviggahitthiyā vā saddhiṃ saṃvidhāya ekaddhānamaggaṃ paṭipajjati, antamaso gāmantarampi, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amātugāme mātugāmasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amātugāme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amātugāme amātugāmasaññī, anāpatti.

If a bhikkhu arranges it, but a woman does not, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he, having made an arrangement, undertakes a single road with a female yakkha, a female ghost, a eunuch, or a female animal in human form, even as far as another village, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If in what is not a woman, he perceives a woman, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he is in doubt about what is not a woman, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If in what is not a woman, he perceives what is not a woman, there is no offense.

416. Anāpatti asaṃvidahitvā gacchati, mātugāmo saṃvidahati bhikkhu na saṃvidahati, visaṅketena gacchati, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

416.There is no offense if he departs without arranging, if a woman arranges but the bhikkhu does not, if he departs without prearrangement, in emergencies, if he is insane, or if he is the first wrong-doer.

Saṃvidhānasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The seventh training rule on arrangements is finished.
