# BU-PC 51

## 1. Surāpānasikkhāpadaṃ

## 1.The Training Rule on Intoxicants

326. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā cetiyesu cārikaṃ caramāno yena bhaddavatikā tena pāyāsi. Addasaṃsu kho gopālakā pasupālakā kassakā pathāvino bhagavantaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mā kho, bhante, bhagavā ambatitthaṃ agamāsi. Ambatitthe, bhante, jaṭilassa assame nāgo paṭivasati iddhimā āsiviso [āsīviso (sī. syā.)] ghoraviso. So bhagavantaṃ mā viheṭhesī’’ti. Evaṃ vutte bhagavā tuṇhī ahosi. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho gopālakā pasupālakā kassakā pathāvino bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mā kho, bhante, bhagavā ambatitthaṃ agamāsi. Ambatitthe, bhante, jaṭilassa assame nāgo paṭivasati iddhimā āsiviso ghoraviso. So bhagavantaṃ mā viheṭhesī’’ti. Tatiyampi kho bhagavā tuṇhī ahosi.

326.At that time the Blessed One was wandering on tour among the Cetiyas, and he proceeded to Bhaddavatika. Cowherds, shepherds, farmers, and wayfarers saw the Blessed One coming from afar. Seeing him, they said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, may the Blessed One not go to Ambatittha. At Ambatittha, venerable sir, in the hermitage of the ascetic with matted hair, there lives a powerful, venomous, deadly nāga. May he not harm the Blessed One.” When this was said, the Blessed One remained silent. A second time… A third time the cowherds, shepherds, farmers, and wayfarers said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, may the Blessed One not go to Ambatittha. At Ambatittha, venerable sir, in the hermitage of the ascetic with matted hair, there lives a powerful, venomous, deadly nāga. May he not harm the Blessed One.” A third time the Blessed One remained silent.

Atha kho bhagavā anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena bhaddavatikā tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā bhaddavatikāyaṃ viharati. Atha kho āyasmā sāgato yena ambatitthassa [ambatitthakassa (sī.), ambatitthaṃ (syā.)] jaṭilassa assamo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā agyāgāraṃ pavisitvā tiṇasanthārakaṃ paññapetvā nisīdi pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā. Addasā kho so nāgo āyasmantaṃ sāgataṃ paviṭṭhaṃ. Disvāna dummano [dukkhī dummano (sī. syā.)] padhūpāyi [padhūpāsi (syā. ka.)]. Āyasmāpi sāgato padhūpāyi [padhūpāsi (syā. ka.)]. Atha kho so nāgo makkhaṃ asahamāno pajjali. Āyasmāpi sāgato tejodhātuṃ samāpajjitvā pajjali. Atha kho āyasmā sāgato tassa nāgassa tejasā tejaṃ pariyādiyitvā yena bhaddavatikā tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho bhagavā bhaddavatikāyaṃ yathābhirantaṃ viharitvā yena kosambī tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Assosuṃ kho kosambikā upāsakā – ‘‘ayyo kira sāgato ambatitthikena nāgena saddhiṃ saṅgāmesī’’ti.

Then the Blessed One, wandering on tour by stages, arrived at Bhaddavatika. There the Blessed One dwelt in Bhaddavatika. Then Venerable Sāgata approached the hermitage of the matted-hair ascetic of Ambatittha; having approached, he entered the fire chamber, spread out a grass mat, sat down cross-legged, straightened his body, and established mindfulness in front of him. That nāga saw Venerable Sāgata enter. Seeing him, it became displeased and emitted smoke. Venerable Sāgata also emitted smoke. Then, unable to endure the affront, that nāga burst into flames. Venerable Sāgata also entered the fire element and burst into flames. Then Venerable Sāgata, having consumed the nāga’s power with his own, approached Bhaddavatika. Then the Blessed One, having dwelt at Bhaddavatika as long as he pleased, set out on tour toward Kosambī. The lay followers of Kosambī heard: “It seems Venerable Sāgata engaged in battle with the Ambatittha nāga.”

Atha kho bhagavā anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena kosambī tadavasari. Atha kho kosambikā upāsakā bhagavato paccuggamanaṃ karitvā yenāyasmā sāgato tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sāgataṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho kosambikā upāsakā āyasmantaṃ sāgataṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘kiṃ, bhante, ayyānaṃ dullabhañca manāpañca, kiṃ paṭiyādemā’’ti? Evaṃ vutte chabbaggiyā bhikkhū kosambike upāsake etadavocuṃ – ‘‘atthāvuso, kāpotikā nāma pasannā bhikkhūnaṃ dullabhā ca manāpā ca, taṃ paṭiyādethā’’ti. Atha kho kosambikā upāsakā ghare ghare kāpotikaṃ pasannaṃ paṭiyādetvā āyasmantaṃ sāgataṃ piṇḍāya paviṭṭhaṃ disvāna āyasmantaṃ sāgataṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘pivatu, bhante, ayyo sāgato kāpotikaṃ pasannaṃ, pivatu, bhante, ayyo sāgato kāpotikaṃ pasanna’’nti. Atha kho āyasmā sāgato ghare ghare kāpotikaṃ pasannaṃ pivitvā nagaramhā nikkhamanto nagaradvāre paripati.

Then the Blessed One, wandering on tour by stages, arrived at Kosambī. Then the lay followers of Kosambī, having gone out to meet the Blessed One, approached Venerable Sāgata; having approached, they paid homage to Venerable Sāgata and stood to one side. Standing to one side, the lay followers of Kosambī said this to Venerable Sāgata: “Venerable sir, what is both rare and pleasing to the venerables? What should we have prepared?” When this was said, the group-of-six bhikkhus said this to the lay followers of Kosambī: “Friends, there is a kāpotikā clear liquor that is rare and pleasing to the bhikkhus. Have that prepared.” Then the lay followers of Kosambī had kāpotikā clear liquor prepared in every house. Seeing Venerable Sāgata entering for alms, they said this to Venerable Sāgata: “May Venerable Sāgata drink the kāpotikā clear liquor, Venerable Sir. May Venerable Sāgata drink the kāpotikā clear liquor, Venerable Sir.” Then Venerable Sāgata, having drunk the kāpotikā clear liquor in every house, collapsed at the city gate as he was leaving the city.

Atha kho bhagavā sambahulehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ nagaramhā nikkhamanto addasa āyasmantaṃ sāgataṃ nagaradvāre paripatantaṃ. Disvāna bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘gaṇhatha, bhikkhave, sāgata’’nti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissuṇitvā āyasmantaṃ sāgataṃ ārāmaṃ netvā yena bhagavā tena sīsaṃ katvā nipātesuṃ. Atha kho āyasmā sāgato parivattitvā yena bhagavā tena pāde karitvā seyyaṃ kappesi. Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘nanu, bhikkhave, pubbe sāgato tathāgate sagāravo ahosi sappatisso’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Api nu kho, bhikkhave, sāgato etarahi tathāgate sagāravo sappatisso’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Nanu, bhikkhave, sāgato ambatitthikena nāgena [ḍeḍḍubhenāpi (sī. syā.)] saddhiṃ saṅgāmesī’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Api nu kho, bhikkhave, sāgato etarahi pahoti nāgena saddhiṃ saṅgāmetu’’nti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Api nu kho, bhikkhave, taṃ pātabbaṃ yaṃ pivitvā visaññī assā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Ananucchavikaṃ, bhikkhave, sāgatassa ananulomikaṃ appatirūpaṃ assāmaṇakaṃ akappiyaṃ akaraṇīyaṃ. Kathañhi nāma, bhikkhave, sāgato majjaṃ pivissati! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then the Blessed One, leaving the city with a number of bhikkhus, saw Venerable Sāgata staggering at the city gate. Seeing him, he addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, take Sāgata.” “Yes, Venerable Sir,” those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. They took Venerable Sāgata to the monastery and laid him down with his head toward the Blessed One. Then Venerable Sāgata turned over and placed his feet toward the Blessed One, making his bed. Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, wasn't Sāgata formerly respectful and deferential toward the Tathāgata?” “Yes, Venerable Sir.” “But now, bhikkhus, is Sāgata respectful and deferential toward the Tathāgata?” “No, Venerable Sir.” “bhikkhus, didn't Sāgata engage in battle with the Ambatittha nāga?” “Yes, Venerable Sir.” “But now, bhikkhus, is Sāgata capable of engaging in battle with a nāga?” “No, Venerable Sir.” “bhikkhus, is that drinkable which, when drunk, makes one senseless?” “No, Venerable Sir.” “bhikkhus, it is unbecoming, unsuitable, improper, unworthy of a contemplative, unallowable, not to be done. How could Sāgata drink intoxicants! This will not lead to faith in the faithless… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

327. ‘‘Surāmerayapāne pācittiya’’nti.

327.“For drinking intoxicants, there is an offense entailing confession.”

328. Surā nāma piṭṭhasurā pūvasurā odanasurā kiṇṇapakkhittā sambhārasaṃyuttā.

328.Surā is flour liquor, cake liquor, rice liquor, liquor with yeast added, or liquor mixed with various ingredients.

Merayo nāma pupphāsavo phalāsavo madhvāsavo guḷāsavo sambhārasaṃyutto.

Meraya is flower liquor, fruit liquor, honey liquor, sugar liquor, or liquor mixed with various ingredients.

Piveyyāti antamaso kusaggenapi pivati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

“If he drinks, even with a blade of grass, there is an offense entailing confession.”

Majje majjasaññī pivati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Majje vematiko pivati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Majje amajjasaññī pivati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

If, concerning an intoxicant, one perceives it as an intoxicant and drinks it, there is an offense entailing confession. If, concerning an intoxicant, one is doubtful and drinks it, there is an offense entailing confession. If, concerning an intoxicant, one perceives it as a non-intoxicant and drinks it, there is an offense entailing confession.

Amajje majjasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amajje vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Amajje amajjasaññī, anāpatti.

If, concerning a non-intoxicant, one perceives it as an intoxicant, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, concerning a non-intoxicant, one is doubtful, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, concerning a non-intoxicant, one perceives it as a non-intoxicant, there is no offense.

329. Anāpatti amajjañca hoti majjavaṇṇaṃ majjagandhaṃ majjarasaṃ taṃ pivati, sūpasampāke, maṃsasampāke, telasampāke, āmalakaphāṇite, amajjaṃ ariṭṭhaṃ pivati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

329.There is no offense if it is a non-intoxicant that has the color, smell, or taste of an intoxicant and one drinks it; in soup preparations; in meat preparations; in oil preparations; in āmalaka syrup; if one drinks a non-intoxicant ariṭṭha; for one who is insane; or for the first offender.

Surāpānasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The first training rule on intoxicants is finished.
