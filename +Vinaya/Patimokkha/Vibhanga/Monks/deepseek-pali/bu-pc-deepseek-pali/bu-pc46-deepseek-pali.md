# BU-PC 46

## 6. Cārittasikkhāpadaṃ

## 6.The Training Rule on Conduct

294. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato upanandassa sakyaputtassa upaṭṭhākakulaṃ āyasmantaṃ upanandaṃ sakyaputtaṃ bhattena nimantesi. Aññepi bhikkhū bhattena nimantesi. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā upanando sakyaputto purebhattaṃ, kulāni payirupāsati. Atha kho te bhikkhū te manusse etadavocuṃ – ‘‘dethāvuso bhatta’’nti. ‘‘Āgametha, bhante, yāvāyyo upanando āgacchatī’’ti. Dutiyampi kho te bhikkhū…pe… tatiyampi kho te bhikkhū te manusse etadavocuṃ – ‘‘dethāvuso, bhattaṃ; pure kālo atikkamatī’’ti. ‘‘Yampi mayaṃ, bhante, bhattaṃ karimhā ayyassa upanandassa kāraṇā. Āgametha, bhante, yāvāyyo upanando āgacchatī’’ti.

294.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrel Sanctuary. At that time, the supporting family of Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son invited Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son for a meal, and also other bhikkhus. Now at that time, Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son was visiting families before the meal. Then those bhikkhus said to those people, “Friends, give us the meal.” “Wait, venerable sirs, until Venerable Upananda arrives.” A second time… A third time those bhikkhus said to those people, “Friends, give us the meal; the time is passing.” “Venerable sirs, we prepared this meal on account of Venerable Upananda. Please wait until Venerable Upananda arrives.”

Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto purebhattaṃ kulāni payirupāsitvā divā āgacchati. Bhikkhū na cittarūpaṃ bhuñjiṃsu. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto nimantito sabhatto samāno purebhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, nimantito sabhatto samāno purebhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjasīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, nimantito sabhatto samāno purebhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son, thinking, “The Blessed One has prohibited visiting families before the meal,” visited families after the meal and returned during the day. The solid food was carried away. Those bhikkhus who were of few desires… complained and criticized him: “How could Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son, having been invited for a meal, visit families before the meal?”… “Is it true, Upananda, that you, having been invited for a meal, visit families before the meal?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “Foolish person, how could you, having been invited for a meal, visit families before the meal! This does not lead to the confidence of the unconfident…” … “And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu nimantito sabhatto samāno purebhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

‘Should any bhikkhu, having been invited for a meal, visit families before the meal, it is a Pācittiya.’

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

295. [mahāva. 277] Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato upanandassa sakyaputtassa upaṭṭhākakulaṃ saṅghassatthāya khādanīyaṃ pāhesi – ‘‘ayyassa upanandassa dassetvā saṅghassa dātabba’’nti. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā upanando sakyaputto gāmaṃ piṇḍāya paviṭṭho hoti. Atha kho te manussā ārāmaṃ gantvā bhikkhū pucchiṃsu – ‘‘kahaṃ, bhante, ayyo upanando’’ti? ‘‘Esāvuso, āyasmā upanando sakyaputto gāmaṃ piṇḍāya paviṭṭho’’ti. ‘‘Idaṃ, bhante, khādanīyaṃ ayyassa upanandassa dassetvā saṅghassa dātabba’’nti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, paṭiggahetvā nikkhipatha yāva upanando āgacchatī’’ti.

295.At that time, the supporting family of Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son sent some solid food to the Saṅgha, saying, “After showing it to Venerable Upananda, it is to be given to the Saṅgha.” Now at that time, Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son had gone into the village for alms. Then those people went to the monastery and asked the bhikkhus, “Where, venerable sirs, is Venerable Upananda?” “Friend, Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son has gone into the village for alms.” “Venerable sirs, this solid food is to be shown to Venerable Upananda and then given to the Saṅgha.” They reported this matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and in this connection, gave a Dhamma talk and addressed the bhikkhus: “Well then, bhikkhus, receive it and set it aside until Upananda returns.”

Atha kho āyasmā upanando sakyaputto – ‘‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṃ purebhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjitu’’nti pacchābhattaṃ kulāni payirupāsitvā divā paṭikkami, khādanīyaṃ ussāriyittha. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā upanando sakyaputto pacchābhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjissatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, upananda, pacchābhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjasīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, pacchābhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjissasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana bhikkhave imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son, thinking, “The Blessed One has prohibited visiting families before the meal,” visited families after the meal and returned during the day. The solid food was carried away. Those bhikkhus who were of few desires… complained and criticized him: “How could Venerable Upananda the Sakyan son visit families after the meal?”… “Is it true, Upananda, that you visited families after the meal?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “Foolish person, how could you visit families after the meal! This does not lead to the confidence of the unconfident…” … “And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu nimantito sabhatto samāno purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

“Should any bhikkhu, having been invited for a meal, visit families whether before or after the meal, it is a Pācittiya.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

296. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū cīvaradānasamaye kukkuccāyantā kulāni na payirupāsanti. Cīvaraṃ parittaṃ uppajjati. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… anujānāmi, bhikkhave, cīvaradānasamaye kulāni payirupāsituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

296.At that time, the bhikkhus, being scrupulous, did not visit families during the robe-offering season. Few robes were obtained. They reported this matter to the Blessed One… “I allow, bhikkhus, visiting families during the robe-offering season. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu nimantito sabhatto samāno purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjeyya, aññatra samayā, pācittiyaṃ. Tatthāyaṃ samayo. Cīvaradānasamayo – ayaṃ tattha samayo’’ti.

“Should any bhikkhu, having been invited for a meal, visit families whether before or after the meal, except on a suitable occasion, it is a Pācittiya. Herein, this is the suitable occasion: the robe-offering season—this is the suitable occasion herein.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

297. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū cīvarakammaṃ karonti, attho ca hoti sūciyāpi suttenapi satthakenapi. Bhikkhū kukkuccāyantā kulāni na payirupāsanti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… anujānāmi, bhikkhave, cīvarakārasamaye kulāni payirupāsituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

297.At that time the bhikkhus were doing robe-making work, and there was a need for needles, thread, and scissors. The bhikkhus, being scrupulous, did not visit families. They reported this matter to the Blessed One. … “I allow, bhikkhus, visiting families during the robe-making season. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu nimantito sabhatto samāno purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjeyya, aññatra samayā, pācittiyaṃ. Tatthāyaṃ samayo. Cīvaradānasamayo, cīvarakārasamayo – ayaṃ tattha samayo’’ti.

“Should any bhikkhu, having been invited for a meal, visit families whether before or after the meal, except on a suitable occasion, it is a Pācittiya. Herein, this is the suitable occasion: the robe-offering season, the robe-making season—this is the suitable occasion herein.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

298. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū gilānā honti, attho ca hoti bhesajjehi. Bhikkhū kukkuccāyantā kulāni na payirupāsanti. Bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ…pe… anujānāmi, bhikkhave, santaṃ bhikkhuṃ āpucchā kulāni payirupāsituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

298.At that time, bhikkhus were ill, and there was a need for medicines. The bhikkhus, being scrupulous, did not visit families. They reported the matter to the Blessed One… “I allow, bhikkhus, after asking an available bhikkhu, to visit families.” And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

299. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu nimantito sabhatto samāno santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjeyya, aññatra samayā, pācittiyaṃ. Tatthāyaṃ samayo. Cīvaradānasamayo, cīvarakārasamayo – ayaṃ tattha samayo’’ti.

299.“Should any bhikkhu, having been invited for a meal, without asking an available bhikkhu, visit families whether before or after the meal, except on a suitable occasion, it is a Pācittiya. Herein, this is the suitable occasion: the robe-offering season, the robe-making season—this is the suitable occasion herein.”

300. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

300.‘Yo pana’ means whoever, of whatever kind… ‘Bhikkhu’ means… In this context, ‘bhikkhu’ means this bhikkhu.

Nimantito nāma pañcannaṃ bhojanānaṃ aññatarena bhojanena nimantito.

‘Invited’ means having been invited to one of the five kinds of meals.

Sabhatto nāma yena nimantito tena sabhatto.

‘Having a meal’ means having a meal arranged by the one who invited him.

Santaṃ nāma bhikkhuṃ sakkā hoti āpucchā pavisituṃ.

‘Available’ means a bhikkhu whom one can ask permission before entering.

Asantaṃ nāma bhikkhuṃ na sakkā hoti āpucchā pavisituṃ.

‘Unavailable’ means a bhikkhu who cannot be asked permission before entering.

Purebhattaṃ nāma yena nimantito taṃ abhuttāvī.

‘Before the meal’ means before the one who invited him has eaten.

Pacchābhattaṃ nāma yena nimantito taṃ antamaso kusaggenapi [aruṇuggamanepi (sī.)] bhuttaṃ hoti.

‘After the meal’ means when the one who invited has eaten, even if it is only a blade of kusa grass.

Kulaṃ nāma cattāri kulāni – khattiyakulaṃ, brāhmaṇakulaṃ, vessakulaṃ, suddakulaṃ.

‘Family’ means four kinds of families: the khattiya family, the brahmin family, the vessa family, the sudda family.

Kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjeyyāti aññassa gharūpacāraṃ okkamantassa āpatti dukkaṭassa. Paṭhamaṃ pādaṃ ummāraṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Dutiyaṃ pādaṃ atikkāmeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘Visits families’ means for one who steps into the vicinity of another’s house, there is an offense of wrong-doing. When the first foot crosses the threshold, there is an offense of wrong-doing. When the second foot crosses, there is a Pācittiya offense.

Aññatra samayāti ṭhapetvā samayaṃ.

‘Except on a suitable occasion’ means excluding the occasion.

Cīvaradānasamayo nāma anatthate kathine vassānassa pacchimo māso, atthate kathine pañca māsā.

‘Robe-offering season’ means the last month of the rains retreat when the kaṭhina is not laid out, or the five months when the kaṭhina is laid out.

Cīvarakārasamayo nāma cīvare kayiramāne.

‘Robe-making season’ means when robes are being made.

301. Nimantite nimantitasaññī santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjati, aññatra samayā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Nimantite vematiko santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjati, aññatra samayā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Nimantite animantitasaññī santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjati, aññatra samayā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

301.If, being invited, he, perceiving himself as invited, without asking an available bhikkhu, visits families either before or after the meal, except on a suitable occasion, there is a Pācittiya offense. If, being invited, he is uncertain, without asking an available bhikkhu, visits families either before or after the meal, except on a suitable occasion, there is a Pācittiya offense. If, being invited, he, perceiving himself as uninvited, without asking an available bhikkhu, visits families either before or after the meal, except on a suitable occasion, there is a Pācittiya offense.

Animantite nimantitasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Animantite vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Animantite animantitasaññī, anāpatti.

If, not being invited, he perceives himself as invited, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, not being invited, he is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, not being invited, he perceives himself as uninvited, there is no offense.

302. Anāpatti samaye, santaṃ bhikkhuṃ āpucchā pavisati, asantaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā pavisati, aññassa gharena maggo hoti, gharūpacārena maggo hoti, antarārāmaṃ gacchati, bhikkhunupassayaṃ gacchati, titthiyaseyyaṃ gacchati, paṭikkamanaṃ gacchati, bhattiyagharaṃ gacchati, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

302.There is no offense: at the proper time; if one enters after asking a bhikkhu who is present; if one enters without asking a bhikkhu who is absent; if the path is through another's house; if the path is for access to a dwelling; if one goes to a monastery within the village; if one goes to a nunnery; if one goes to a lodging of sectarians; if one goes to a place of retreat; if one goes to a house of invited meals; in times of danger; in the case of one who is insane; in the case of a beginner.

Cārittasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The sixth training rule concerning conduct is concluded.
