# BU-PC 22

## 2. Atthaṅgatasikkhāpadaṃ

## 2.The Training Rule on Sunset.

153. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena therā bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo ovadanti pariyāyena. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato cūḷapanthakassa pariyāyo hoti bhikkhuniyo ovadituṃ. Bhikkhuniyo evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘na dāni ajja ovādo iddho bhavissati, taññeva dāni udānaṃ ayyo cūḷapanthako punappunaṃ bhaṇissatī’’ti. Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo yenāyasmā cūḷapanthako tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ cūḷapanthakaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho tā bhikkhuniyo āyasmā cūḷapanthako etadavoca – ‘‘samaggāttha, bhaginiyo’’ti? ‘‘Samaggāmhāyyā’’ti. ‘‘Vattanti, bhaginiyo, aṭṭha garudhammā’’ti? ‘‘Vattantāyyā’’ti. ‘‘Eso, bhaginiyo, ovādo’’ti niyyādetvā imaṃ udānaṃ punappunaṃ abhāsi –

153.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time the senior bhikkhus were instructing the nuns in turn. And at that time it was Venerable Cūḷapanthaka's turn to instruct the nuns. The nuns said: "Now, today the instruction will not be successful, for Venerable Cūḷapanthaka will just keep repeating the same utterance over and over again." Then those nuns approached Venerable Cūḷapanthaka; having approached, they paid homage to him and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Venerable Cūḷapanthaka said to them: "Are you in concord, sisters?" "We are in concord, venerable sir." "Do the eight weighty principles prevail, sisters?" "They prevail, venerable sir." "This, sisters, is the instruction," and having delivered it, he repeated this utterance again and again:

[udā. 37] ‘‘Adhicetaso appamajjato, munino monapathesu sikkhato;Sokā na bhavanti tādino, upasantassa sadā satīmato’’ti.

“For one with lofty mind, heedful,For one training in the paths of the silent sage,Sorrows do not arise for such a one,Tranquil, ever mindful.”

Bhikkhuniyo evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘nanu avocumhā – na dāni ajja ovādo iddho bhavissati, taññeva dāni udānaṃ ayyo cūḷapanthako punappunaṃ bhaṇissatī’’ti! Assosi kho āyasmā cūḷapanthako tāsaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ imaṃ kathāsallāpaṃ. Atha kho āyasmā cūḷapanthako vehāsaṃ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe caṅkamatipi tiṭṭhatipi nisīdatipi seyyampi kappeti dhūmāyatipi pajjalatipi antaradhāyatipi, tañceva [taññeva (itipi)] udānaṃ bhaṇati aññañca bahuṃ buddhavacanaṃ. Bhikkhuniyo evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘acchariyaṃ vata bho, abbhutaṃ vata bho, na vata no ito pubbe ovādo evaṃ iddho bhūtapubbo yathā ayyassa cūḷapanthakassā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā cūḷapanthako tā bhikkhuniyo yāva samandhakārā ovaditvā uyyojesi – gacchatha bhaginiyoti.

The nuns said, “Didn’t we say that today’s instruction wouldn’t be successful, and that Venerable Cūḷapanthaka would just keep repeating the same utterance?” Venerable Cūḷapanthaka heard their conversation. Then he rose into the air and walked, stood, sat, and lay down in the sky; he emitted smoke, blazed with flames, disappeared, and recited that utterance along with much more of the Buddha’s teachings. The nuns said, “How amazing, how astonishing! Never before has an instruction been so successful as Venerable Cūḷapanthaka’s today!” Then Venerable Cūḷapanthaka instructed those nuns until twilight and dismissed them, saying, “Go, sisters.”

Atha kho tā bhikkhuniyo nagaradvāre thakite bahinagare vasitvā kālasseva nagaraṃ pavisanti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘abrahmacāriniyo imā bhikkhuniyo; ārāme bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ vasitvā idāni nagaraṃ pavisantī’’ti. Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā cūḷapanthako atthaṅgate sūriye bhikkhuniyo ovadissatī’’ti…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, cūḷapanthaka, atthaṅgate sūriye bhikkhuniyo ovadasī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, cūḷapanthaka, atthaṅgate sūriye bhikkhuniyo ovadissasi! Netaṃ, cūḷapanthaka, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then those nuns, finding the city gate shut, stayed outside the city and entered the city early in the morning. People complained, criticized, and murmured, “These nuns are unchaste; they stayed in the monastery with the bhikkhus and are now entering the city.” The bhikkhus heard the complaints, criticisms, and murmurings of those people. Those bhikkhus who were modest… criticized him, saying, “How could Venerable Cūḷapanthaka instruct the nuns after sunset?”… “Is it true, Cūḷapanthaka, that you instruct the nuns after sunset?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Buddha rebuked him… “How could you, Cūḷapanthaka, instruct the nuns after sunset? This will not lead to confidence in those who lack it…” … “And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

154. ‘‘Sammatopi ce bhikkhu atthaṅgate sūriye bhikkhuniyo ovadeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

154.“If an appointed bhikkhu instructs nuns after sunset, he commits an offense entailing confession.”

155. Sammato nāma ñatticatutthena kammena sammato.

155.Appointed means appointed by a formal act consisting of one motion and three proclamations.

Atthaṅgate sūriyeti oggate sūriye.

After sunset means when the sun has set.

Bhikkhunī nāma ubhatosaṅghe upasampannā.

A bhikkhunī is one who has received the full ordination in both communities.

Ovadeyyāti aṭṭhahi vā garudhammehi aññena vā dhammena ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

If he advises her — that is, advises her with the eight weighty rules or with any other teaching — he commits an offense entailing confession.

156. Atthaṅgate atthaṅgatasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Atthaṅgate vematiko ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Atthaṅgate anatthaṅgatasaññī ovadati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

156.If he advises, perceiving the sun as set when it has set, he commits a pācittiya offense. If he advises, being uncertain whether the sun has set, he commits a pācittiya offense. If he advises, perceiving the sun as not set when it has set, he commits a pācittiya offense.

Ekatoupasampannāya ovadati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anatthaṅgate atthaṅgatasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anatthaṅgate vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anatthaṅgate anatthaṅgatasaññī, anāpatti.

If he advises one who has received ordination from only one community, he commits an offense of wrong-doing. If he advises, thinking the sun has set when it has not, he commits an offense of wrong-doing. If he is uncertain whether the sun has set when it has not, and advises, he commits an offense of wrong-doing. If he advises, thinking the sun has not set when it has not, there is no offense.

157. Anāpatti uddesaṃ dento, paripucchaṃ dento, ‘‘osārehi ayyā’’ti vuccamāno, osāreti, pañhaṃ pucchati, pañhaṃ puṭṭho katheti, aññassatthāya bhaṇantaṃ bhikkhuniyo suṇanti, sikkhamānāya sāmaṇeriyā, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

157.There is no offense when giving a recitation, when giving an exposition, when told, "Dismiss, venerable ones," and he dismisses them, when asking a question, when answering a question asked, when bhikkhunīs listen to someone speaking for another's benefit, for a female trainee, for a female novice, for an insane person, or for one committing the first offense.

Atthaṅgatasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The second training rule on sunset is concluded.
