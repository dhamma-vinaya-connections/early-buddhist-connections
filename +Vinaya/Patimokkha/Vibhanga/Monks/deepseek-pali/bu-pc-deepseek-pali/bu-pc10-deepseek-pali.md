# BU-PC 10

## 10. Pathavīkhaṇanasikkhāpadaṃ

## 10.The Training Rule on Digging the Earth.

84. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā āḷaviyaṃ viharati aggāḷave cetiye. Tena kho pana samayena āḷavakā [āḷavikā (syā.)] bhikkhū navakammaṃ karontā pathaviṃ khaṇantipi khaṇāpentipi. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā pathaviṃ khaṇissantipi khaṇāpessantipi! Ekindriyaṃ samaṇā sakyaputtiyā jīvaṃ viheṭhentī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āḷavakā bhikkhū pathaviṃ khaṇissantipi khaṇāpessantipī’’ti…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, pathaviṃ khaṇathapi khaṇāpethapī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, pathaviṃ khaṇissathapi khaṇāpessathapi! Jīvasaññino hi, moghapurisā, manussā pathaviyā. Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

84.At that time the Buddha, the Blessed One, was dwelling at Āḷavī, at the Aggāḷava Shrine. Now at that time the bhikkhus of Āḷavī, while doing new construction work, were digging the earth themselves and having it dug by others. People complained, criticized, and spread it about: "How can these Sakyan ascetics dig the earth themselves and have it dug by others! These Sakyan ascetics are harming one-facultied life!" The bhikkhus heard those people complaining, criticizing, and spreading it about. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, criticized, and spread it about: "How can the bhikkhus of Āḷavī dig the earth themselves and have it dug by others!"… "Is it true, bhikkhus, that you dig the earth yourselves and have it dug by others?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Buddha, the Blessed One, rebuked them… "How can you foolish men dig the earth yourselves and have it dug by others! For foolish men, people perceive life in the earth. This will not lead to faith in those without it… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

85. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu pathaviṃ khaṇeyya vā khaṇāpeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

85."Whatever bhikkhu should dig the earth or have it dug, there is an offense of expiation."

86. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

86.Whoever: that is, any…pe… Bhikkhu:…pe… The bhikkhu intended in this case is this one.

Pathavī nāma dve pathaviyo – jātā ca pathavī ajātā ca pathavī.

Earth is of two kinds: born earth and unborn earth.

Jātā nāma pathavī – suddhapaṃsu suddhamattikā appapāsāṇā appasakkharā appakaṭhalā appamarumbā appavālikā, yebhuyyenapaṃsukā, yebhuyyenamattikā. Adaḍḍhāpi vuccati jātā pathavī. Yopi paṃsupuñjo vā mattikāpuñjo vā atirekacātumāsaṃ ovaṭṭho, ayampi vuccati jātā pathavī.

Born earth: pure dust, pure clay, with few stones, few pebbles, few potsherds, few hard gravel, little sand; mostly dust, mostly clay. Even unfired earth is called born earth. Even a heap of dust or a heap of clay that has been piled up for more than four months is also called born earth.

Ajātā nāma pathavī – suddhapāsāṇā suddhasakkharā suddhakaṭhalā suddhamarumbā suddhavālikā appapaṃsukā appamattikā, yebhuyyenapāsāṇā, yebhuyyenasakkharā, yebhuyyenakaṭhalā, yebhuyyenamarumbā, yebhuyyenavālikā. Daḍḍhāpi vuccati ajātā pathavī. Yopi paṃsupuñjo vā mattikāpuñjo vā omakacātumāsaṃ ovaṭṭho, ayampi vuccati ajātā pathavī.

Unborn earth: pure rock, pure pebbles, pure potsherds, pure hard gravel, pure sand; with little dust, little clay; mostly rock, mostly pebbles, mostly potsherds, mostly hard gravel, mostly sand. Even fired earth is called unborn earth. Even a heap of dust or a heap of clay that has been piled up for less than four months is also called unborn earth.

Khaṇeyyāti sayaṃ khaṇati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

He digs himself: there is an offense of expiation.

Khaṇāpeyyāti aññaṃ āṇāpeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Sakiṃ āṇatto bahukampi khaṇati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

He has it dug: he orders another to dig; there is an offense of expiation. If, having been ordered once, one digs a lot, there is an offense of expiation.

87. Pathaviyā pathavisaññī khaṇati vā khaṇāpeti vā, bhindati vā bhedāpeti vā, dahati vā dahāpeti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

87.If one, perceiving earth as earth, digs it or has it dug, breaks it or has it broken, burns it or has it burned, there is an offense of expiation.

Pathaviyā vematiko khaṇati vā khaṇāpeti vā, bhindati vā bhedāpeti vā, dahati vā dahāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

If one, being uncertain about earth, digs it or has it dug, breaks it or has it broken, burns it or has it burned, there is an offense of wrong conduct.

Pathaviyā apathavisaññī khaṇati vā khaṇāpeti vā, bhindati vā bhedāpeti vā, dahati vā dahāpeti vā, anāpatti.

If one, perceiving earth as not-earth, digs it or has it dug, breaks it or has it broken, burns it or has it burned, there is no offense.

Apathaviyā pathavisaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apathaviyā vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Apathaviyā apathavisaññī, anāpatti.

If one, perceiving non-earth as earth, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one, being uncertain about non-earth, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one, perceiving non-earth as non-earth, there is no offense.

88. Anāpatti – ‘‘imaṃ jāna, imaṃ dehi, imaṃ āhara, iminā attho, imaṃ kappiyaṃ karohī’’ti bhaṇati, asañcicca, asatiyā, ajānantassa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

88.There is no offense: if one says, "Know this, give this, fetch this, this is needed, make this allowable"; if it is unintentional; if unmindful; if unknowing; if insane; if the first offender.

Pathavīkhaṇanasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ.

The Training Rule on Digging the Earth is finished, the tenth.

Musāvādavaggo paṭhamo.

The First Chapter: False Speech.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Musā omasapesuññaṃ, padaseyyāya ve duve;Aññatra viññunā bhūtā, duṭṭhullāpatti khaṇanāti.

False speech, abusive speech, and divisive speech; And two on sleeping arrangements; Except when true to the wise; Harsh speech, and digging.
