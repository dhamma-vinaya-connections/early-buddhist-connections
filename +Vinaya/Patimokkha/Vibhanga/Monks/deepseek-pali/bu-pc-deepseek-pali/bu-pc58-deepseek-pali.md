# BU-PC 58

## 8. Dubbaṇṇakaraṇasikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The training rule on discolouring.

367. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū ca paribbājakā ca sāketā sāvatthiṃ addhānamaggappaṭipannā honti. Antarāmagge corā nikkhamitvā te acchindiṃsu. Sāvatthiyā rājabhaṭā nikkhamitvā te core sabhaṇḍe gahetvā bhikkhūnaṃ santike dūtaṃ pāhesuṃ – ‘‘āgacchantu, bhadantā, sakaṃ sakaṃ cīvaraṃ sañjānitvā gaṇhantū’’ti. Bhikkhū na sañjānanti. Te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhadantā attano attano cīvaraṃ na sañjānissantī’’ti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Atha kho te bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā bhikkhūnaṃ tadanucchavikaṃ tadanulomikaṃ dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññapessāmi dasa atthavase paṭicca – saṅghasuṭṭhutāya, saṅghaphāsutāya…pe… saddhammaṭṭhitiyā, vinayānuggahāya. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

367.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anathapindika’s Monastery. Now on that occasion, a number of bhikkhus and wanderers were traveling along the road from Saketa to Savatthi. Along the way, bandits came out and robbed them. The king’s guards from Savatthi went out, captured the bandits along with their loot, and sent a messenger to the bhikkhus: “Come, venerable ones, identify and take your own robes.” The bhikkhus could not identify them. People complained, criticized, and grumbled: “How can these venerable ones not recognize their own robes?” The bhikkhus heard these people complaining, criticizing, and grumbling. Then those bhikkhus reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, in connection with this incident, on this occasion, convened the Sangha of bhikkhus, and having given the bhikkhus a talk on Dhamma that was suitable and relevant, addressed them: “Well then, bhikkhus, I will lay down a training rule for bhikkhus for ten reasons: for the well-being of the Sangha, for the comfort of the Sangha… for the longevity of the true Dhamma, for supporting the discipline. And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

368. ‘‘Navaṃ pana bhikkhunā cīvaralābhena tiṇṇaṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇānaṃ aññataraṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇaṃ ādātabbaṃ – nīlaṃ vā kaddamaṃ vā kāḷasāmaṃ vā. Anādā ce bhikkhu tiṇṇaṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇānaṃ aññataraṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇaṃ navaṃ cīvaraṃ paribhuñjeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

368.“When a bhikkhu obtains a new robe, he should apply one of the three kinds of sizing-marks: blue, mud, or blackish-brown. If a bhikkhu uses a new robe without applying one of the three kinds of sizing-marks, it is an offense entailing confession.”

369. Navaṃ nāma akatakappaṃ vuccati.

369.‘New’ means not yet marked.

Cīvaraṃ nāma channaṃ cīvarānaṃ aññataraṃ cīvaraṃ.

‘Robe’ means one of the six kinds of robes.

Tiṇṇaṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇānaṃ aññataraṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇaṃ ādātabbanti antamaso kusaggenapi ādātabbaṃ.

‘One of the three kinds of sizing-marks must be applied’—even if only with a blade of kusa grass.

Nīlaṃ nāma dve nīlāni – kaṃsanīlaṃ, palāsanīlaṃ.

‘Blue’ refers to two blues: bronze-blue and palasa-blue.

Kaddamo nāma odako vuccati.

‘Mud’ means water mixed with mud.

Kāḷasāmaṃ nāma yaṃkiñci kāḷasāmakaṃ [kāḷakaṃ (sī. syā.)].

‘Blackish-brown’ refers to anything of a blackish-brown hue.

Anādā ce bhikkhu tiṇṇaṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇānaṃ aññataraṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇanti antamaso kusaggenapi anādiyitvā tiṇṇaṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇānaṃ aññataraṃ dubbaṇṇakaraṇaṃ navaṃ cīvaraṃ paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

“If a bhikkhu uses a new robe without having applied one of the three kinds of sizing-marks—even so much as with a blade of kusa grass—it is an offense entailing confession.”

370. Anādinne anādinnasaññī paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Anādinne vematiko paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Anādinne ādinnasaññī paribhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

370.If he uses it without having applied the mark, perceiving it as unmarked, it is an offense entailing confession. If he uses it without having applied the mark, being in doubt, it is an offense entailing confession. If he uses it without having applied the mark, perceiving it as marked, it is an offense entailing confession.

Ādinne anādinnasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ādinne vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ādinne ādinnasaññī, anāpatti.

If he uses it after having applied the mark, perceiving it as unmarked, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he uses it after having applied the mark, being in doubt, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If he uses it after having applied the mark, perceiving it as marked, there is no offense.

371. Anāpattiādiyitvā paribhuñjati, kappo naṭṭho hoti, kappakatokāso jiṇṇo hoti, kappakatena akappakataṃ saṃsibbitaṃ hoti, aggaḷe anuvāte paribhaṇḍe, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

371.There is no offense: if he uses it after having applied the mark; if the mark is lost; if the marked portion is worn out; if a marked robe is sewn together with an unmarked one; if it is a border, a lining, or a patch; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Dubbaṇṇakaraṇasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The eighth training rule on discolouring is finished.
