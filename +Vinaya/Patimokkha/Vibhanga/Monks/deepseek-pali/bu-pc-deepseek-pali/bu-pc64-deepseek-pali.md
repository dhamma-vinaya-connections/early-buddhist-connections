# BU-PC 64

## 4. Duṭṭhullasikkhāpadaṃ

## 4.The training rule on concealing grave offenses.

397. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā upanando sakyaputto sañcetanikaṃ sukkavissaṭṭhiṃ āpattiṃ āpajjitvā bhātuno saddhivihārikassa bhikkhuno ārocesi – ‘‘ahaṃ, āvuso, sañcetanikaṃ sukkavissaṭṭhiṃ āpattiṃ āpanno. Mā kassaci ārocehī’’ti. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro bhikkhu sañcetanikaṃ sukkavissaṭṭhiṃ āpattiṃ āpajjitvā saṅghaṃ tassā āpattiyā parivāsaṃ yāci. Tassa saṅgho tassā āpattiyā parivāsaṃ adāsi. So parivasanto taṃ bhikkhuṃ passitvā etadavoca – ‘‘ahaṃ, āvuso, sañcetanikaṃ sukkavissaṭṭhiṃ āpattiṃ āpajjitvā saṅghaṃ tassā āpattiyā parivāsaṃ yāciṃ, tassa me saṅgho tassā āpattiyā parivāsaṃ adāsi, sohaṃ parivasāmi, vediyāmahaṃ [vedayāmahaṃ (syā.)], āvuso, vediyatī’’ti maṃ āyasmā dhāretū’’ti.

397.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park, Jeta’s Grove. Now at that time Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having committed an intentional emission of semen, informed his co-resident bhikkhu, his brother, saying, “Friend, I have committed an intentional emission of semen. Do not tell anyone.” Now at that time another bhikkhu, having committed an intentional emission of semen, requested the Sangha for probation for that offense. The Sangha granted him probation for that offense. While undergoing probation, he saw that bhikkhu and said, “Friend, I committed an intentional emission of semen and requested the Sangha for probation for that offense. The Sangha granted me probation for that offense, and I am now undergoing probation. Friend, I know, I know. Venerable, please remember me.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, yo aññopi imaṃ āpattiṃ āpajjati sopi evaṃ karotī’’ti? ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti. ‘‘Ayaṃ, āvuso, āyasmā upanando sakyaputto sañcetanikaṃ sukkavissaṭṭhiṃ āpattiṃ āpajjitvā [āpajji (?)] so me āroceti mā kassaci ārocehī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ pana tvaṃ, āvuso, paṭicchādesī’’ti? ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti. Atha kho so bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṃ etamatthaṃ ārocesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhu bhikkhussa jānaṃ duṭṭhullaṃ āpattiṃ paṭicchādessatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhikkhussa jānaṃ duṭṭhullaṃ āpattiṃ paṭicchādesīti. ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, bhikkhussa jānaṃ duṭṭhullaṃ āpattiṃ paṭicchādessasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

“Friend, does anyone else who commits this offense do the same?” “Yes, friend.” “Friend, this Venerable Upananda the Sakyan, having committed an intentional emission of semen, told me, ‘Do not tell anyone.’” “But, friend, did you conceal it?” “Yes, friend.” Then that bhikkhu informed the bhikkhus about this matter. Those bhikkhus who were modest… complained, grumbled, and criticized: “How can a bhikkhu knowingly conceal a grave offense for another bhikkhu?”… “Is it true, bhikkhu, that you knowingly concealed a grave offense for another bhikkhu?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “How can you, worthless man, knowingly conceal a grave offense for another bhikkhu! This does not inspire confidence in the unconfident… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule is to be recited:

398. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhussa jānaṃ duṭṭhullaṃ āpattiṃ paṭicchādeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

398.“If any bhikkhu should knowingly conceal a grave offense of another bhikkhu, it is a pācittiya offense.”

399. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

399.“Any” means any such person as… “Bhikkhu” means… the bhikkhu intended in this case.

Bhikkhussāti aññassa bhikkhussa.

“Of a bhikkhu” means of another bhikkhu.

Jānāti nāma sāmaṃ vā jānāti aññe vā tassa ārocenti, so vā āroceti.

“Knowing” means he knows himself, or others inform him, or he informs others.

Duṭṭhullā nāma āpatti cattāri ca pārājikāni terasa ca saṅghādisesā.

“Grave offense” means the four pārājika offenses and the thirteen saṅghādisesa offenses.

Paṭicchādeyyāti ‘‘imaṃ jānitvā codessanti, sāressanti khuṃsessanti, vambhessanti, maṅkuṃ karissanti nārocessāmī’’ti dhuraṃ nikkhittamatte, āpatti pācittiyassa.

“Conceal” means he conceals, thinking, “If they know this, they will accuse, remind, find fault, disparage, or make ashamed—I will not report it.” As soon as he sets aside the responsibility, it is a pācittiya offense.

400. Duṭṭhullāya āpattiyā duṭṭhullāpattisaññī paṭicchādeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Duṭṭhullāya āpattiyā vematiko paṭicchādeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Duṭṭhullāya āpattiyā aduṭṭhullāpattisaññī paṭicchādeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aduṭṭhullaṃ āpattiṃ paṭicchādeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Anupasampannassa duṭṭhullaṃ vā aduṭṭhullaṃ vā ajjhācāraṃ paṭicchādeti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aduṭṭhullāya āpattiyā duṭṭhullāpattisaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aduṭṭhullāya āpattiyā vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aduṭṭhullāya āpattiyā aduṭṭhullāpattisaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

400.If, regarding a grave offense, he perceives it as a grave offense and conceals it, it is a pācittiya offense. If, regarding a grave offense, he is doubtful and conceals it, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If, regarding a grave offense, he perceives it as a non-grave offense and conceals it, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If he conceals a non-grave offense, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If he conceals a grave or non-grave transgression of an unordained person, it is a dukkaṭa offense. If, regarding a non-grave offense, he perceives it as a grave offense [and conceals it], it is a dukkaṭa offense. If, regarding a non-grave offense, he is doubtful [and conceals it], it is a dukkaṭa offense. If, regarding a non-grave offense, he perceives it as a non-grave offense [and conceals it], it is a dukkaṭa offense.

401. Anāpatti – ‘‘saṅghassa bhaṇḍanaṃ vā kalaho vā viggaho vā vivādo vā bhavissatī’’ti nāroceti, ‘‘saṅghabhedo vā saṅgharāji vā bhavissatī’’ti nāroceti, ‘‘ayaṃ kakkhaḷo pharuso jīvitantarāyaṃ vā brahmacariyantarāyaṃ vā karissatī’’ti nāroceti, aññe patirūpe bhikkhū apassanto nāroceti, nachādetukāmo nāroceti, ‘‘paññāyissati sakena kammenā’’ti nāroceti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

401.There is no offense for one who does not report it, thinking, “There will be brawls, quarrels, strife, or disputes in the Sangha,” or “There will be schism or division in the Sangha,” or “This person is harsh and rough—he may cause danger to life or danger to the holy life”; for one not seeing other suitable bhikkhus; for one who does not intend to conceal; for one who thinks, “It will become known through his own action”; for one who is insane; for the first offender.

Duṭṭhullasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The fourth training rule on grave offenses is concluded.
