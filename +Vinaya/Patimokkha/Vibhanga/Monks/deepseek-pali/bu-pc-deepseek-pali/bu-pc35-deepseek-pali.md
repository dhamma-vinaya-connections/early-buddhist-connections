# BU-PC 35

## 5. Paṭhamapavāraṇāsikkhāpadaṃ

## 5.The First Training Rule on Invitation.

236. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññataro brāhmaṇo bhikkhū nimantetvā bhojesi. Bhikkhū bhuttāvī pavāritā ñātikulāni gantvā ekacce bhuñjiṃsu ekacce piṇḍapātaṃ ādāya agamaṃsu. Atha kho so brāhmaṇo paṭivissake [paṭivisake (yojanā)] etadavoca – ‘‘bhikkhū mayā ayyā santappitā. Etha, tumhepi santappessāmī’’ti. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘kiṃ tvaṃ, ayyo [ayya (syā.)], amhe santappessasi? Yepi tayā nimantitā tepi amhākaṃ gharāni āgantvā ekacce bhuñjiṃsu ekacce piṇḍapātaṃ ādāya agamaṃsū’’ti!

236.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time, a certain brahmin invited bhikkhus and served them a meal. The bhikkhus, having eaten and declined further offerings, went to visit their relatives’ families; some ate (again), and some took almsfood and departed. Then that brahmin said to his neighbors, “I have satisfied the venerable bhikkhus. Come, I will satisfy you too.” They replied, “Why would you satisfy us, sir? Even those you invited came to our homes; some ate (again), and some took almsfood and departed!”

Atha kho so brāhmaṇo ujjhāyati khiyyati vipāceti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhadantā amhākaṃ ghare bhuñjitvā aññatra bhuñjissanti! Na cāhaṃ paṭibalo yāvadatthaṃ dātu’’nti! Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tassa brāhmaṇassa ujjhāyantassa khiyyantassa vipācentassa. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma bhikkhū bhuttāvī pavāritā aññatra bhuñjissantī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, bhikkhū bhuttāvī pavāritā aññatra bhuñjantīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma te, bhikkhave, moghapurisā bhuttāvī pavāritā aññatra bhuñjissanti! Netaṃ, bhikkhave, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

Then that brahmin complained, grumbled, and protested, “How can the venerables eat in our homes and then eat elsewhere! And I am not capable of giving as much as is needed!” The bhikkhus heard that brahmin complaining, grumbling, and protesting. Those bhikkhus who were modest… grumbled and protested, “How can bhikkhus, having eaten and declined further offerings, eat elsewhere?”… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus, having eaten and declined further offerings, ate elsewhere?” “It is true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “How can these foolish men, having eaten and declined further offerings, eat elsewhere! This will not inspire confidence in those without it, nor increase confidence in those who already have it…” … “And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhuttāvī pavārito khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

“If a bhikkhu, having eaten and declined further offerings, should eat or consume edible food or staple food, it is an offense entailing confession.”

Evañcidaṃ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ paññattaṃ hoti.

Thus was this training rule laid down for the bhikkhus by the Blessed One.

237. Tena kho pana samayena bhikkhū gilānānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ paṇīte piṇḍapāte nīharanti. Gilānā na cittarūpaṃ bhuñjanti. Tāni bhikkhū chaṭṭenti. Assosi kho bhagavā uccāsaddaṃ mahāsaddaṃ kākoravasaddaṃ. Sutvāna āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘kiṃ nu kho so, ānanda, uccāsaddo mahāsaddo kākoravasaddo’’ti? Atha kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Bhuñjeyyuṃ panānanda, bhikkhū gilānātiritta’’nti. ‘‘Na bhuñjeyyuṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe dhammiṃ kathaṃ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘anujānāmi bhikkhave, gilānassa ca agilānassa ca atirittaṃ bhuñjituṃ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, atirittaṃ kātabbaṃ – ‘‘alametaṃ sabba’’nti. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

237.At that time, bhikkhus were taking choice almsfood for sick bhikkhus. The sick bhikkhus did not eat to their satisfaction. The bhikkhus discarded those (leftovers). The Blessed One heard a loud noise, a great noise, the sound of crows. Having heard, he addressed Venerable Ānanda: “Ānanda, what is this loud noise, great noise, the sound of crows?” Then Venerable Ānanda informed the Blessed One of the matter. “But Ānanda, do bhikkhus eat the surplus from the sick?” “They do not, Blessed One.” Then the Blessed One, on this occasion, because of this incident, having given a Dhamma talk, addressed the bhikkhus: “I allow, bhikkhus, both the sick and the healthy to eat the surplus. And thus, bhikkhus, the surplus should be made thus: ‘This is enough for all.’ And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

238. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhuttāvī pavārito anatirittaṃ khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā, pācittiya’’nti.

238.“If a bhikkhu, having eaten and declined further offerings, should eat or consume any non-surplus edible food or staple food, it is an offense entailing confession.”

239. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

239.“Whoever” means: whoever of whatever kind… “bhikkhu” means… the bhikkhu intended in this case.

Bhuttāvī nāma pañcannaṃ bhojanānaṃ aññataraṃ bhojanaṃ antamaso kusaggenapi bhuttaṃ hoti.

“Having eaten” means having eaten any of the five kinds of food, even as little as that which adheres to the tip of a kusa blade.

Pavārito nāma asanaṃ paññāyati, bhojanaṃ paññāyati, hatthapāse ṭhito abhiharati, paṭikkhepo paññāyati.

“Declined further offerings” means: an unfinished meal is discernible; food is discernible; one standing within arm’s reach offers it; a refusal is discernible.

Anatirittaṃ nāma akappiyakataṃ hoti, appaṭiggahitakataṃ hoti, anuccāritakataṃ hoti, ahatthapāse kataṃ hoti, abhuttāvinā kataṃ hoti, bhuttāvinā pavāritena āsanā vuṭṭhitena kataṃ hoti, ‘‘alametaṃ sabbanti avuttaṃ hoti, na gilānātirittaṃ hoti’’ – etaṃ anatirittaṃ nāma.

“Non-surplus” means: it is made unallowable; it is not received; it is not announced; it is done beyond arm’s reach; it is done by one who has not eaten; it is done by one who has eaten and declined further offerings and risen from the seat; “This is enough for all” is not said; it is not a sick person’s surplus—this is called “non-surplus.”

Atirittaṃ nāma kappiyakataṃ hoti, paṭiggahitakataṃ hoti, uccāritakataṃ hoti, hatthapāse kataṃ hoti, bhuttāvinā kataṃ hoti, bhuttāvinā pavāritena āsanā avuṭṭhitena kataṃ hoti, ‘‘alametaṃ sabba’’nti vuttaṃ hoti, gilānātirittaṃ hoti – etaṃ atirittaṃ nāma.

“Surplus” means: it is made allowable; it is received; it is announced; it is done within arm’s reach; it is done by one who has eaten; it is done by one who has eaten and declined further offerings, without rising from the seat; “This is enough for all” is said; it is a sick person’s surplus—this is called “surplus.”

Khādanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ ṭhapetvā avasesaṃ khādanīyaṃ nāma.

“Edible food” means: apart from the five staple foods, the remainder, excluding what is allowable for a day, for a week, or for life, is called “edible food.”

Bhojanīyaṃ nāma pañca bhojanāni – odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṃsaṃ.

“Staple food” means these five kinds of staple food: cooked rice, gruel, flour, fish, and meat.

‘‘Khādissāmi bhuñjissāmī’’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pācittiyassa.

If one receives it thinking, “I will eat, I will consume,” there is an offense of wrong conduct. With each swallow, there is an offense entailing confession.

240. Anatiritte anatirittasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Anatiritte vematiko khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Anatiritte atirittasaññī khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādati vā bhuñjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

240.If one perceives non-surplus as non-surplus and eats or consumes edible food or staple food, there is an offense entailing confession. If one is doubtful about non-surplus and eats or consumes edible food or staple food, there is an offense entailing confession. If one perceives non-surplus as surplus and eats or consumes edible food or staple food, there is an offense entailing confession.

Yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ āhāratthāya paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti dukkaṭassa. Atiritte anatirittasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Atiritte vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Atiritte atirittasaññī, anāpatti.

If one receives what is allowable for a day, for a week, or for life for the purpose of food, there is an offense of wrong conduct. With each swallow, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one perceives surplus as non-surplus, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one is doubtful about surplus, there is an offense of wrong conduct. If one perceives surplus as surplus, there is no offense.

241. Anāpatti atirittaṃ kārāpetvā bhuñjati, ‘‘atirittaṃ kārāpetvā bhuñjissāmī’’ti paṭiggaṇhāti, aññassatthāya haranto gacchati, gilānassa sesakaṃ bhuñjati, yāmakālikaṃ sattāhakālikaṃ yāvajīvikaṃ sati paccaye paribhuñjati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

241.There is no offense for one who eats after having surplus prepared; for one who receives it with the intention, “I will eat after having surplus prepared”; for one who carries it away for another; for one who eats the surplus of a sick person; for one who partakes of what is allowable for a day, for a week, or for life, when there is a reason; for one who is insane; and for one who is the first offender.

Paṭhamapavāraṇāsikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The first training rule concerning invitation is concluded, the fifth.
