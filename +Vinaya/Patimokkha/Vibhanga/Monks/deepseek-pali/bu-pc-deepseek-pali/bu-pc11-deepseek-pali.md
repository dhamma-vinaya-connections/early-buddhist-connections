# BU-PC 11

## 1. Bhūtagāmasikkhāpadaṃ

## 1.The training rule regarding injury to seeds and plants.

89. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā āḷaviyaṃ viharati aggāḷave cetiye. Tena kho pana samayena āḷavakā bhikkhū navakammaṃ karontā rukkhaṃ chindantipi chedāpentipi. Aññataropi āḷavako bhikkhu rukkhaṃ chindati. Tasmiṃ rukkhe adhivatthā devatā taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mā, bhante, attano bhavanaṃ kattukāmo mayhaṃ bhavanaṃ chindī’’ti. So bhikkhu anādiyanto chindi yeva, tassā ca devatāya dārakassa bāhuṃ ākoṭesi. Atha kho tassā devatāya etadahosi – ‘‘yaṃnnūnāhaṃ imaṃ bhikkhuṃ idheva jīvitā voropeyya’’nti. Atha kho tassā devatāya etadahosi – ‘‘na kho metaṃ patirūpaṃ yāhaṃ imaṃ bhikkhuṃ idheva jīvitā voropeyyaṃ. Yannūnāhaṃ bhagavato etamatthaṃ āroceyya’’nti. Atha kho sā devatā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesi. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu devate! Sādhu kho tvaṃ, devate, taṃ bhikkhuṃ jīvitā na voropesi. Sacajja tvaṃ, devate, taṃ bhikkhuṃ jīvitā voropeyyāsi, bahuñca tvaṃ, devate, apuññaṃ pasaveyyāsi. Gaccha tvaṃ, devate, amukasmiṃ okāse rukkho vivitto tasmiṃ upagacchā’’ti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā rukkhaṃ chindissantipi chedāpessantipi ekindriyaṃ samaṇā sakyaputtiyā jīvaṃ viheṭhessantī’’ti!

89.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Āḷavī in the Aggāḷava Shrine. At that time the bhikkhus of Āḷavī, while engaged in new construction, were cutting down trees and having them cut down. A certain bhikkhu of Āḷavī was cutting down a tree. The deity living in that tree said to that bhikkhu: "Venerable, do not cut down my home, wishing to make your own home." The bhikkhu, disregarding her, kept on cutting, and struck the arm of the deity's child. Then that deity thought: "I might as well deprive this bhikkhu of his life right here." Then that deity thought: "It is not proper for me to deprive this bhikkhu of his life right here. I might as well inform the Blessed One of this matter." Then that deity approached the Blessed One; having approached, she informed the Blessed One of the matter. "Good, good, deity! It is good, deity, that you did not deprive that bhikkhu of his life. If today, deity, you had deprived that bhikkhu of his life, you would have produced much demerit. Go, deity, there is a secluded tree in a certain place; go to that one." People complained, criticized, and denounced: "How can these Sakyan ascetics cut down trees and have them cut down? These Sakyan ascetics are harming life, beings with a single faculty!"

Assosuṃ kho bhikkhū tesaṃ manussānaṃ ujjhāyantānaṃ khiyyantānaṃ vipācentānaṃ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āḷavakā bhikkhū rukkhaṃ chindissantipi chedāpessantipī’’ti…pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, rukkhaṃ chindathāpi chedāpethāpī’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, rukkhaṃ chindissathāpi, chedāpessathāpi! Jīvasaññino hi, moghapurisā, manussā rukkhasmiṃ, netaṃ moghapurisā appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

The bhikkhus heard those people complaining, criticizing, and denouncing. Those bhikkhus who were modest... complained, criticized, and denounced: "How can the bhikkhus of Āḷavī cut down trees and have them cut down?"... "Is it true, bhikkhus, that you cut down trees and have them cut down?" "It is true, Blessed One." The Blessed One rebuked them... "How can you, foolish men, cut down trees and have them cut down! Indeed, foolish men, people perceive life in trees. This will not conduce to the confidence of those who are unconvinced..."... "And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

90. ‘‘Bhūtagāmapātabyatāya pācittiya’’nti.

90.For the destruction of plants and trees, it is an offense of expiation.

91. Bhūtagāmo nāma pañca bījajātāni – mūlabījaṃ, khandhabījaṃ, phaḷubījaṃ, aggabījaṃ, bījabījameva [bījabījañceva (itipi)] pañcamaṃ.

91.Plants and trees are of five kinds of plant-origins: root-born plants, stem-born plants, joint-born plants, tip-born plants, and seed-born plants as the fifth.

Mūlabījaṃ nāma – haliddi, siṅgiveraṃ, vacā, vacattaṃ, ativisā, kaṭukarohiṇī, usīraṃ, bhaddamūttakaṃ, yāni vā panaññānipi atthi mūle jāyanti, mūle sañjāyanti, etaṃ mūlabījaṃ nāma.

A root-born plant means: turmeric, ginger, sweet flag, white sweet flag, ativisa, katukarohini, vetiver, bhaddamuttaka, or whatever else grows or originates from a root—this is called a root-born plant.

Khandhabījaṃ nāma – assattho, nigrodho, pilakkho, udumbaro, kacchako, kapitthano, yāni vā panaññānipi atthi khandhe jāyanti, khandhe sañjāyanti, etaṃ khandhabījaṃ nāma.

A stem-born plant means: assattha (bodhi tree), banyan tree, pilakkha, udumbara, kacchaka, kapitthana, or whatever else grows or originates from a stem—this is called a stem-born plant.

Phaḷubījaṃ nāma – ucchu, veḷu, naḷo, yāni vā panaññānipi atthi pabbe jāyanti, pabbe sañjāyanti, etaṃ phaḷubījaṃ nāma.

A joint-born plant means: sugarcane, bamboo, reeds, or whatever else grows or originates at a joint—this is called a joint-born plant.

Aggabījaṃ nāma – ajjukaṃ, phaṇijjakaṃ, hiriveraṃ, yāni vā panaññānipi atthi agge jāyanti, agge sañjāyanti, etaṃ aggabījaṃ nāma.

A tip-born plant means: ajjukaṃ, phaṇijjakaṃ, hirivera, or whatever else grows or originates from the tip—this is called a tip-born plant.

Bījabījaṃ nāma – pubbaṇṇaṃ, aparaṇṇaṃ, yāni vā panaññānipi atthi bīje jāyanti, bīje sañjāyanti, etaṃ bījabījaṃ nāma.

A seed-born plant means: early-ripening grains, late-ripening grains, or whatever else grows or originates from seeds—this is called a seed-born plant.

92. Bīje bījasaññī chindati vā chedāpeti vā, bhindati vā bhedāpeti vā, pacati vā pacāpeti vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Bīje vematiko chindati vā chedāpeti vā, bhindati vā bhedāpeti vā, pacati vā pacāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Bīje abījasaññī chindati vā chedāpeti vā, bhindati vā bhedāpeti vā, pacati vā pacāpeti vā, anāpatti. Abīje bījasaññī āpatti dukkaṭassa. Abīje vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Abīje abījasaññī, anāpatti.

92.If, perceiving a plant as a plant, one cuts or causes to cut, breaks or causes to break, cooks or causes to cook it, there is an offense of expiation. If, being in doubt about a plant, one cuts or causes to cut, breaks or causes to break, cooks or causes to cook it, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving a plant as a non-plant, one cuts or causes to cut, breaks or causes to break, cooks or causes to cook it, there is no offense. If, perceiving a non-plant as a plant, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, being in doubt about a non-plant, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If, perceiving a non-plant as a non-plant, there is no offense.

93. Anāpatti – ‘‘imaṃ jāna, imaṃ dehi, imaṃ āhara, iminā attho, imaṃ kappiyaṃ karohī’’ti bhaṇati, asañcicca, assatiyā, ajānantassa, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

93.No offense if one says, “Know this, give this, fetch this, this is needed, make this allowable,” or if one acts unintentionally, unmindfully, unknowingly, or if one is insane, or if one is the first offender.

Bhūtagāmasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The first training rule on injuring seeds and plants is concluded.
