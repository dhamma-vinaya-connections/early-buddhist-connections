# BU-PC 16

## 6. Anupakhajjasikkhāpadaṃ

## 6.The Training Rule on Encroaching

119. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena chabbaggiyā bhikkhū varaseyyāyo palibundhenti, therā bhikkhū vuṭṭhāpenti [ettha te iti kammapadaṃ ūnaṃ viya dissati]. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘kena nu kho mayaṃ upāyena idheva vassaṃ vaseyyāmā’’ti? Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū there bhikkhū anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappenti – yassa sambādho bhavissati so pakkamissatīti. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū there bhikkhū anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappessantī’’ti! Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ …pe… ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, there bhikkhū anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappethā’’ti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, there bhikkhū anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappessatha! Netaṃ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

119.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time the group-of-six bhikkhus were obstructing the choice sleeping places, causing the senior bhikkhus to leave. Then the group-of-six bhikkhus thought, “By what means can we spend the rains residence right here?” So the group-of-six bhikkhus, intruding upon the senior bhikkhus, prepared sleeping places, thinking, “Whoever feels crowded will leave.” Those bhikkhus who were modest… criticized and complained: “How can the group-of-six bhikkhus, intruding upon the senior bhikkhus, prepare sleeping places?” Then those bhikkhus, having criticized the group-of-six bhikkhus in many ways, reported the matter to the Blessed One… “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you, intruding upon the senior bhikkhus, prepared sleeping places?” “It’s true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked them… “Foolish men, how can you, intruding upon the senior bhikkhus, prepare sleeping places? This will not inspire confidence in those without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

120. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu saṅghike vihāre jānaṃ pubbupagataṃ bhikkhuṃ anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappeyya – yassa sambādho bhavissati so pakkamissatī’ti, etadeva paccayaṃ karitvā anaññaṃ, pācittiya’’nti.

120.“If a bhikkhu, knowing that a bhikkhu has arrived first, prepares a sleeping place intruding upon him in a dwelling belonging to the Sangha, thinking, ‘Whoever feels crowded will leave,’ and does so for just this reason and no other, he commits an offense entailing confession.”

121. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ 2.0251 atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

121.“Yo pana”: whoever… “Bhikkhu”: … in this case, the intended meaning is “bhikkhu.”

Saṅghiko nāma vihāro saṅghassa dinno hoti pariccatto.

“Belonging to the Sangha” means a dwelling given and relinquished to the Sangha.

Jānāti nāma vuḍḍhoti jānāti, gilānoti jānāti, saṅghena dinnoti jānāti.

“Knowing”: He knows that the other is senior; he knows that he is sick; he knows that it has been given to the Sangha.

Anupakhajjāti anupavisitvā.

“Anupakhajja”: having intruded.

Seyyaṃ kappeyyāti mañcassa vā pīṭhassa vā pavisantassa vā nikkhamantassa vā upacāre seyyaṃ santharati vā santharāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

“Prepares a sleeping place”: if, in the precinct of a bed or a bench, or in the precinct of one entering or leaving, he spreads out a sleeping place or has it spread out, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he sits down or lies down, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Etadeva paccayaṃ karitvā anaññanti na añño koci paccayo hoti anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappetuṃ.

“Just because of this, no other”: there is no other reason to prepare a bed, encroaching.

122. Saṅghike saṅghikasaññī anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike vematiko anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Saṅghike puggalikasaññī anupakhajja seyyaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

122.If it belongs to the Saṅgha, and he perceives it as belonging to the Saṅgha, and he prepares a sleeping place by intruding, he commits an offense entailing confession. If it belongs to the Saṅgha, and he is in doubt about it, and he prepares a sleeping place by intruding, he commits an offense entailing confession. If it belongs to the Saṅgha, and he perceives it as belonging to an individual, and he prepares a sleeping place by intruding, he commits an offense entailing confession.

Mañcassa vā pīṭhassa vā pavisantassa vā nikkhamantassa vā upacāraṃ ṭhapetvā seyyaṃ santharati vā santharāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Abhinisīdati vā abhinipajjati vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Vihārassa upacāre vā upaṭṭhānasālāyaṃ vā maṇḍape vā rukkhamūle vā ajjhokāse vā seyyaṃ santharati vā santharāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Abhinisīdati vā abhinippajjati vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike saṅghikasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Puggalike puggalikasaññī aññassa puggalike āpatti dukkaṭassa. Attano puggalike anāpatti.

If he spreads or has spread a sleeping place outside the precinct of a bed or a bench, or one entering or leaving, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he sits down or lies down, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he spreads or has spread a sleeping place in the precinct of a dwelling, or in an assembly hall, or in a pavilion, or at the foot of a tree, or in the open air, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If he sits down or lies down, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If it belongs to an individual, and he perceives it as belonging to the Sangha, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If it belongs to an individual, and he is in doubt about it, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. If it belongs to an individual, and he perceives it as belonging to an individual, but it belongs to another individual, he commits an offense of wrong conduct. There is no offense if it is his own personal property.

123. Anāpatti gilāno pavisati, sītena vā uṇhena vā pīḷito pavisati, āpadāsu, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

123.There is no offense for a sick person entering, for one oppressed by cold or heat entering, in emergencies, for one who is insane, for the one initially doing (it).

Anupakhajjasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The sixth training rule on encroaching is completed.
