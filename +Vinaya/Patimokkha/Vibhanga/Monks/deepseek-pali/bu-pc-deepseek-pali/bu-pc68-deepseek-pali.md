# BU-PC 68

## 8. Ariṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ

## 8.The Training Rule on Ariṭṭha

417. [idaṃ vatthu cūḷava. 65; ma. ni. 1.234] Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena ariṭṭhassa nāma bhikkhuno gaddhabādhipubbassa [gandhabādhipubbassa (syā. ka.)] evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ hoti – ‘‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’ti. Assosuṃ kho sambahulā bhikkhū – ‘‘ariṭṭhassa kira nāma bhikkhuno gaddhabādhipubbassa evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ – ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’’ti. Atha kho te bhikkhū yena ariṭṭho bhikkhu gaddhabādhipubbo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā ariṭṭhaṃ bhikkhuṃ gaddhabādhipubbaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘saccaṃ kira te, āvuso ariṭṭha, evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ – ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃbyākho ahaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi – ‘yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’’ti.

417.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time, a bhikkhu named Ariṭṭha, formerly a vulture trainer, had arisen in him such a wicked view: “As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them.” A number of bhikkhus heard: “It seems that the bhikkhu Ariṭṭha, formerly a vulture trainer, has arisen in him such a wicked view: ‘As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them.’” Then those bhikkhus approached the bhikkhu Ariṭṭha, formerly a vulture trainer, and having approached, they said to the bhikkhu Ariṭṭha, formerly a vulture trainer: “Is it true, friend Ariṭṭha, that such a wicked view has arisen in you: ‘As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them’?” “Yes, friends, as I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them.”

‘‘Mā, āvuso ariṭṭha, evaṃ avaca. Mā bhagavantaṃ abbhācikkhi. Na hi sādhu bhagavato abbhakkhānaṃ [abbhācikkhanaṃ (itipi)]. Na hi bhagavā evaṃ vadeyya. Anekapariyāyenāvuso ariṭṭha, antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā bhagavatā. Alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāya. Appassādā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo. Aṭṭhikaṅkalūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo. Maṃsapesūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā…pe… tiṇukkūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā… aṅgārakāsūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā… supinakūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā… yācitakūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā… rukkhaphalūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā… asisūnūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā… sattisūlūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā… sappasirūpamā kāmā vuttā bhagavatā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo’’ti.

“Do not speak thus, friend Ariṭṭha. Do not misrepresent the Blessed One. It is not good to misrepresent the Blessed One. The Blessed One would not speak thus. In many ways, friend Ariṭṭha, the Blessed One has declared obstructive things to be obstructive. And indeed, for one who indulges in them, it is sufficient to cause obstruction. Sensual pleasures have been declared by the Blessed One as having little satisfaction, much suffering, much distress, and the danger in them is greater. Sensual pleasures have been declared by the Blessed One as like a skeleton—much suffering, much distress, and the danger in them is greater. Sensual pleasures have been declared by the Blessed One as like a lump of flesh… like a grass torch… like a pit of glowing coals… like a dream… like borrowed goods… like fruits on a tree… like a butcher’s knife… like a stake of spears… like a snake’s head—much suffering, much distress, and the danger in them is greater.”

Evampi kho ariṭṭho bhikkhu gaddhabādhipubbo tehi bhikkhūhi vuccamāno tatheva taṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ thāmasā parāmāsā abhinivissa voharati – ‘‘evaṃbyākho ahaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, – ‘yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’’ti. Yato ca kho te bhikkhū nāsakkhiṃsu ariṭṭhaṃ bhikkhuṃ gaddhabādhipubbaṃ etasmā pāpakā diṭṭhigatā vivecetuṃ, atha kho te bhikkhū yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṃ nidāne etasmiṃ pakaraṇe bhikkhusaṅghaṃ sannipātāpetvā ariṭṭhaṃ bhikkhuṃ gaddhabādhipubbaṃ paṭipucchi – ‘‘saccaṃ kira te, ariṭṭha, evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ – ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃbyākho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi – ‘yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’’ti.

Even when the bhikkhu Ariṭṭha, formerly a vulture trainer, was spoken to by those bhikkhus, he still obstinately clung to that wicked view, holding on to it and insisting: “Yes, friends, as I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them.” When those bhikkhus were unable to dissuade the bhikkhu Ariṭṭha, formerly a vulture trainer, from that wicked view, then those bhikkhus approached the Blessed One; and having approached, they reported the matter to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, on this occasion and because of this matter, having assembled the Sangha of bhikkhus, questioned the bhikkhu Ariṭṭha, formerly a vulture trainer: “Is it true, Ariṭṭha, that such a wicked view has arisen in you: ‘As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them’?” “Indeed, venerable sir, as I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them.”

Kassa nu kho nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, mayā evaṃ dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāsi? Nanu mayā, moghapurisa, anekapariyāyena antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā. Alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāya. Appassādā kāmā vuttā mayā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo. Aṭṭhikaṅkalūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā…pe… maṃsapesūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… tiṇukkūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… aṅgārakāsūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… supinakūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… yācitakūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… rukkhaphalūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… asisūnūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… sattisūlūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā… sappasirūpamā kāmā vuttā mayā bahudukkhā bahupāyāsā, ādīnavo ettha bhiyyo. Atha ca pana tvaṃ, moghapurisa, attanā duggahitena amhe ceva abbhācikkhasi, attānañca khaṇasi, bahuñca apuññaṃ pasavasi. Tañhi te, moghapurisa, bhavissati dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāya. Netaṃ, moghapurisa, appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe…. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

“Foolish man, under whose name do you claim to understand the Dhamma taught by me in this way? Have I not, foolish man, in many ways declared obstructive things to be obstructive? And for one who indulges in them, it is sufficient to cause obstruction. I have declared sensual pleasures as having little satisfaction, much suffering, much distress, and the danger in them is greater. I have declared sensual pleasures as like a skeleton… like a lump of flesh… like a grass torch… like a pit of glowing coals… like a dream… like borrowed goods… like fruits on a tree… like a butcher’s knife… like a stake of spears… like a snake’s head—much suffering, much distress, and the danger in them is greater. Yet you, foolish man, through your own wrong grasp, both misrepresent us and harm yourself, and generate much demerit. This, foolish man, will be for your long-term harm and suffering. This, foolish man, is not for the conviction of the unconvinced, or for increasing the conviction of the convinced… And thus, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited:

418. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu evaṃ vadeyya – ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’ti so bhikkhu bhikkhūhi evamassa vacanīyo – ‘māyasmā evaṃ avaca, mā bhagavantaṃ abbhācikkhi, na hi sādhu bhagavato abbhakkhānaṃ, na hi bhagavā evaṃ vadeyya, anekapariyāyenāvuso, antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā bhagavatā, alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāyā’ti. Evañca nti.

418.‘If any bhikkhu should say: “As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things that the Blessed One has declared to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them,” that bhikkhu should be addressed by the bhikkhus thus: “Do not say so, venerable one. Do not misrepresent the Blessed One, for it is not right to misrepresent the Blessed One. The Blessed One would not say such a thing. In many ways, friend, the Blessed One has declared obstructive things to be obstructive, and for one who indulges in them, it is sufficient to cause obstruction.” If that bhikkhu, being admonished thus by the bhikkhus, persists in that view, he should be formally admonished by the bhikkhus up to three times for the relinquishment of that view. If he relinquishes it on being formally admonished up to three times, that is good. If he does not relinquish it, it is a pācittiya offense.’

419. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

419.‘Yo pana’ means ‘whoever, of whatever kind’… ‘bhikkhu’… in this context, the bhikkhu intended here.

Evaṃ vadeyyāti – ‘‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’’ti.

‘Should say’ means: “As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things the Blessed One declared to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them.”

So bhikkhūti yo so evaṃvādī bhikkhu.

‘So bhikkhu’ means that bhikkhu who speaks thus.

Bhikkhūhīti aññehi bhikkhūhi. Ye passanti ye suṇanti tehi vattabbo – ‘‘māyasmā evaṃ avaca, mā bhagavantaṃ abbhācikkhi, na hi sādhu bhagavato abbhakkhānaṃ, na hi bhagavā evaṃ vadeyya, anekapariyāyenāvuso antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā bhagavatā. Alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāyā’’ti. Dutiyampi vattabbo. Tatiyampi vattabbo. Sace paṭinissajjati, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ; no ce paṭinissajjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Sutvā na vadanti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. So bhikkhu saṅghamajjhampi ākaḍḍhitvā vattabbo – ‘‘māyasmā evaṃ avaca, mā bhagavantaṃ abbhācikkhi, na hi sādhu bhagavato abbhakkhānaṃ, na hi bhagavā evaṃ vadeyya, anekapariyāyenāvuso antarāyikā dhammā antarāyikā vuttā bhagavatā. Alañca pana te paṭisevato antarāyāyā’’ti. Dutiyampi vattabbo. Tatiyampi vattabbo. Sace paṭinissajjati, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ. No ce paṭinissajjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa. So bhikkhu samanubhāsitabbo. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, samanubhāsitabbo. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo –

‘Bhikkhūhi’ means by other bhikkhus. Those who see or hear should say to him: “Venerable, do not speak thus. Do not misrepresent the Blessed One. Misrepresenting the Blessed One is not good. Surely, the Blessed One would not speak thus. In many ways, friend, the Blessed One has declared obstructive things to be obstructive. And for one who indulges in them, it is sufficient to cause obstruction.” He should be told a second time. He should be told a third time. If he gives it up, that is good. If he does not give it up, there is an offense of wrong-doing. Those who hear and do not speak, there is an offense of wrong-doing. That bhikkhu should be taken into the midst of the Sangha and told: “Venerable, do not speak thus. Do not misrepresent the Blessed One. Misrepresenting the Blessed One is not good. Surely, the Blessed One would not speak thus. In many ways, friend, the Blessed One has declared obstructive things to be obstructive. And for one who indulges in them, it is sufficient to cause obstruction.” He should be told a second time. He should be told a third time. If he gives it up, that is good. If he does not give it up, there is an offense of wrong-doing. That bhikkhu should be formally admonished. And thus, bhikkhus, should he be formally admonished. A competent and capable bhikkhu should inform the Sangha:

420. ‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Itthannāmassa bhikkhuno evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ – ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’ti. So taṃ diṭṭhiṃ na paṭinissajjati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ samanubhāseyya – tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Esā ñatti.

420.“Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. A certain bhikkhu named so-and-so has arisen in him such a wicked view: ‘As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructions are not sufficient to cause obstruction for one who indulges in them.’ He does not relinquish that view. If the Sangha is agreeable, the Sangha should formally admonish bhikkhu so-and-so for the relinquishment of that view. This is the motion.”

‘‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Itthannāmassa bhikkhuno evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ – ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’ti. So taṃ diṭṭhiṃ na paṭinissajjati. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ samanubhāsati tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno samanubhāsanā, tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. A bhikkhu named so-and-so has developed this sort of evil view: ‘As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructive are not capable of obstructing one who engages in them.’ He does not relinquish that view. The Sangha admonishes the bhikkhu so-and-so for the relinquishment of that view. If it is agreeable to the venerable one that the bhikkhu so-and-so should be admonished for the relinquishment of that view, let him be silent; whoever does not agree, let him speak.”

‘‘Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi…pe… tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi – ‘‘suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Itthannāmassa bhikkhuno evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ – ‘tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi yathā yeme antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyā’ti. So taṃ diṭṭhiṃ na paṭinissajjati. Saṅgho itthannāmaṃ bhikkhuṃ samanubhāsati tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati itthannāmassa bhikkhuno samanubhāsanā, tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya, so tuṇhassa; yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

“A second time I speak on this matter… A third time I speak on this matter: ‘Venerable sirs, may the Sangha hear me. A bhikkhu named so-and-so has developed this sort of evil view: “As I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One, those things declared by the Blessed One to be obstructive are not capable of obstructing one who engages in them.” He does not relinquish that view. The Sangha admonishes the bhikkhu so-and-so for the relinquishment of that view. If it is agreeable to the venerable one that the bhikkhu so-and-so should be admonished for the relinquishment of that view, let him be silent; whoever does not agree, let him speak.’”

‘‘Samanubhaṭṭho saṅghena itthannāmo bhikkhu, tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmī’’ti.

“The bhikkhu named so-and-so has been admonished by the Sangha for the relinquishment of that view. The Sangha approves of it, and thus it is silent. Thus do I hold it.”

Ñattiyā dukkaṭaṃ. Dvīhi kammavācāhi dukkaṭā. Kammavācāpariyosāne āpatti pācittiyassa.

There is an offense of wrong-doing for the motion. There are offenses of wrong-doing for the two formal announcements. At the conclusion of the formal announcement, there is an offense of expiation.

421. Dhammakamme dhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Dhammakamme vematiko na paṭinissajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa dhammakamme adhammakammasaññī na paṭinissajjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

421.In a lawful act, perceiving it as a lawful act, if one does not relinquish, there is an offense of expiation. In a lawful act, being doubtful, if one does not relinquish, there is an offense of expiation. In a lawful act, perceiving it as an unlawful act, if one does not relinquish, there is an offense of expiation.

Adhammakamme dhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Adhammakamme adhammakammasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

In an unlawful act, perceiving it as a lawful act, there is an offense of wrong-doing. In an unlawful act, being doubtful, there is an offense of wrong-doing. In an unlawful act, perceiving it as an unlawful act, there is an offense of wrong-doing.

422. Anāpatti asamanubhāsantassa, paṭinissajjantassa, ummattakassāti.

422.There is no offense for one who does not admonish, for one who relinquishes, or for one who is insane.

Ariṭṭhasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The eighth training rule, concerning Ariṭṭha, is finished.
