# BU-PC 30

## 10. Rahonisajjasikkhāpadaṃ

## 10.The training rule on sitting in seclusion.

198. Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato udāyissa purāṇadutiyikā bhikkhunīsu pabbajitā hoti. Sā āyasmato udāyissa santike abhikkhaṇaṃ āgacchati, āyasmāpi udāyī tassā bhikkhuniyā santike abhikkhaṇaṃ gacchati. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā udāyī tassā bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappesi. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā…pe… te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti – ‘‘kathañhi nāma āyasmā udāyī bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappessatī’’ti…pe… saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, udāyi, bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappesīti? ‘‘Saccaṃ, bhagavā’’ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā…pe… kathañhi nāma tvaṃ, moghapurisa, bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappessasi! Netaṃ, moghapurisa appasannānaṃ vā pasādāya…pe… evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṃ sikkhāpadaṃ uddiseyyātha –

198.At that time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. Now at that time a former wife of the Venerable Udāyī had gone forth among the nuns. She frequently visited the Venerable Udāyī, and the Venerable Udāyī frequently visited her. On one occasion, the Venerable Udāyī arranged a private, solitary sitting with that nun. Those bhikkhus of few desires… complained, murmured, and criticized, saying: “How could the Venerable Udāyī arrange a private, solitary sitting with a nun?”… “Is it true, Udāyī, that you arranged a private, solitary sitting with a nun?” “It’s true, Blessed One.” The Blessed One rebuked him… “Foolish man, how could you arrange a private, solitary sitting with a nun? This will not inspire confidence in those who are without it… And so, bhikkhus, this training rule should be recited thus:

199. ‘‘Yo pana bhikkhu bhikkhuniyā saddhiṃ eko ekāya raho nisajjaṃ kappeyya, pācittiya’’nti.

199.Should any bhikkhu sit alone with a bhikkhunī in a private place, it entails confession.

200. Yo panāti yo yādiso…pe… bhikkhūti…pe… ayaṃ imasmiṃ atthe adhippeto bhikkhūti.

200.‘Should any’ means any whatsoever… ‘Bhikkhu’… in this context, ‘bhikkhu’ is intended.

Bhikkhunī nāma ubhatosaṅghe upasampannā.

A bhikkhunī is one who has received the full ordination in both Saṅghas.

Saddhinti ekato.

‘Saddhi’ means together.

Eko ekāyāti bhikkhu ceva hoti bhikkhunī ca.

‘Alone with’ means there is a bhikkhu and a bhikkhunī.

[pāci. 286, 291; pārā. 445,454] Raho nāma cakkhussa raho sotassa raho. Cakkhussa raho nāma na sakkā hoti akkhiṃ vā nikhaṇīyamāne bhamukaṃ vā ukkhipīyamāne sīsaṃ vā ukkhipīyamāne passituṃ. Sotassa raho nāma na sakkā hoti pakatikathā sotuṃ.

‘Private’ means private to the eye and private to the ear. Private to the eye means it is not possible to see if one is winking, raising an eyebrow, or raising one’s head. Private to the ear means it is not possible to hear ordinary speech.

Nisajjaṃ kappeyyāti bhikkhuniyā nisinnāya bhikkhu upanisinno vā hoti upanipanno vā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

‘Arranges a sitting’ means: when a bhikkhunī is sitting, if a bhikkhu sits near or lies near, there is an offense entailing confession.

Bhikkhu nisinne bhikkhunī upanisinnā vā hoti upanipannā vā, āpatti pācittiyassa. Ubho vā nisinnā honti ubho vā nipannā, āpatti pācittiyassa.

If a bhikkhu is sitting, and a bhikkhunī is sitting nearby or lying down nearby, there is an offense entailing confession. If both are sitting or both are lying down, there is an offense entailing confession.

201. Raho rahosaññī eko ekāya nisajjaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Raho vematiko eko ekāya nisajjaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa. Raho arahosaññī eko ekāya nisajjaṃ kappeti, āpatti pācittiyassa.

201.If it is private and he perceives it as private, and he arranges to sit alone with a bhikkhunī, there is an offense entailing confession. If it is private and he is uncertain whether it is private, and he arranges to sit alone with a bhikkhunī, there is an offense entailing confession. If it is private and he perceives it as not private, and he arranges to sit alone with a bhikkhunī, there is an offense entailing confession.

Araho rahosaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Araho vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Araho arahosaññī, anāpatti.

If it is not private and he perceives it as private, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is not private and he is uncertain, there is an offense of wrong-doing. If it is not private and he perceives it as not private, there is no offense.

202. Anāpatti yo koci viññū dutiyo hoti, tiṭṭhati na nisīdati, arahopekkho, aññavihito nisīdati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

202.There is no offense if any knowledgeable person is present as a second; if one stands and does not sit; if one is expecting it to be not private; if one sits with one's mind on something else; if one is insane; or if one is a beginner.

Rahonisajjasikkhāpadaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ.

The training rule on secluded sitting, the tenth, is finished.

Ovādavaggo tatiyo.

The Section on Admonition, the third.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary is:

Asammataatthaṅgatū, passayāmisadānena;Sibbati addhānaṃ nāvaṃ bhuñjeyya, eko ekāya te dasāti.

Unauthorized, sunset, lodging, food, giving;
Sewing, journey, boat, eating, alone with one—these ten.

4. Bhojanavaggo

4.The Section on Food
