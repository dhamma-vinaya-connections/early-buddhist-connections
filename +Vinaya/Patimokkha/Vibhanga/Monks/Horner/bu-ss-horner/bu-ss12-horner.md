# bhikkhus’ Formal Meeting (_Saṅghādisesa_) 12

*BD.1.309* *Bu-Ss.12.1.1* … at Kosambī in Ghosita’s park. At that time the venerable Channa[^1] indulged in bad habits. The bhikkhus said: “Reverend Channa, do not do that, it is not suitable.”[^2]

He said: “What do you, your reverences, think should be said to me? It is I who should tell you.[^3] The enlightened one is for us, _dhamma_ is for us, _dhamma_ is realised for us by a master.[^4] Just as a great wind blowing would raise up grass, sticks, ferns and rubbish together; or just as a mountain-born[^5] river would raise up various water plants[^6] together, so you, having gone forth from various names, from various clans, from various lineages, from various families, are raised up together. What do you, your reverences, think should be said to me? It is I who should tell you. The enlightened one is for us, _dhamma_ is for us, _dhamma_ is realised for us by a master.”

Then those who were modest bhikkhus were … angry, and said: “How can the venerable Channa, himself *BD.1.310* being spoken to by the bhikkhus in accordance with _dhamma_, reckon himself as one not to be spoken to?”

Then these bhikkhus told this matter to the lord. He said:

“Is it true, as they say, Channa, that you, yourself being spoken to by the bhikkhus in accordance with _dhamma_, reckon yourself as one not to be spoken to?”

“It is true, lord,” he said.

The enlightened one, the lord, rebuked him, saying:

“How can you, foolish man, yourself being spoken to by the bhikkhus in accordance with _dhamma_, reckon yourself as one not to be spoken to? It is not, foolish man, *Vin.3.178* for the benefit of unbelievers … Thus, bhikkhus, this course of training should be set forth:

If a bhikkhu is one who is difficult to speak to,[^7] and if himself being spoken to by the bhikkhus according to dhamma[^8] concerning the courses of training included in the exposition,[^9] he reckons himself as one not to be spoken to, saying: ‘Do not say anything to me, venerable ones, either good or bad, and I will not say anything to the venerable ones, either good or bad; refrain, venerable ones, from speaking to me (then) that bhikkhu should be spoken to thus by the bhikkhus: ‘Do not, venerable one, reckon yourself as one not to be spoken to; let the venerable one reckon himself as one to be spoken to; let the venerable one speak to the bhikkhus in accordance with _dhamma_,[^10] and then the bhikkhus will *BD.1.311* speak to the venerable one in accordance with _dhamma_. Thus is the multitude increased for the lord, that is to say by speaking with one another, by assisting one another.[^11] And if that bhikkhu when he has been spoken to by the bhikkhus should persist as before, then that bhikkhu should be admonished up to three times by the bhikkhus together for giving up his course. And if after being admonished up to three times by the bhikkhus together, he gives up his course, that is good; if he does not give it up, there is an offence entailing a formal meeting of the Order.”

* * *

*Bu-Ss.12.2.1* If a bhikkhu is one who is difficult to speak to means: he is difficult to speak to, endowed with qualities which make him difficult to speak to,[^12] intractable,[^13] incapable of being instructed.[^14]

In the courses of training included in the exposition means: in the courses of training included in the Pātimokkha.

By the bhikkhus means: by other bhikkhus.

According to dhamma means: that course of training made known by the lord, this is called according to _dhamma_.

Himself being spoken to he reckons himself as one not to be spoken to, saying : “Do not, venerable ones, say *BD.1.312* anything to me, either good or bad, and I will not say anything to the venerable ones, either good or bad; refrain, venerable ones, from speaking to me ”—(then) that bhikkhu means: that bhikkhu who is difficult to speak to.

By the bhikkhus means: by other bhikkhus, these see, these hear. He should be spoken to by these, saying: “Venerable one, do not reckon yourself as one not to be spoken to, let the venerable one reckon himself as one to be spoken to, let the venerable one speak to the bhikkhus in accordance with _dhamma_, and then the bhikkhus will speak to the venerable one in accordance with _dhamma_. Thus is the multitude increased for the lord, that is to say by speaking to one another, by assisting one another.” A second time he should be spoken to … A third time he should be spoken to … If *Vin.3.179* he gives it up, that is good; but if he does not give it up, there is an offence of wrong-doing. If, having heard, they do not speak, there is an offence of wrong-doing. That bhikkhu, having been pulled into the middle of the assembly, should be told: “Do not, venerable one, reckon yourself as one not to be spoken to … by ordaining one another.” A second time he should be told … A third time he should be told … If he gives it up, that is good; if he does not give it up, there is an offence of wrong-doing. That bhikkhu should be admonished. And thus, bhikkhus, should he be admonished. The Order should be informed by an experienced, competent bhikkhu: “Honoured sirs, let the Order hear me. This bhikkhu, so and so, being remonstrated with by the bhikkhus in accordance with _dhamma_, reckons himself as one not to be spoken to: he does not give up this course. If it is the right time for the Order, let the Order admonish this bhikkhu so that he may give up this course. That is the motion. Honoured sirs, let the Order hear me. This bhikkhu, so and so … Thus do I understand.”

According to the motion there is an offence of wrong-doing; according to the two resolutions there are grave offences; at the end of a resolution there is an offence entailing a formal meeting of the Order. If he is com *BD.1.313* mitting an offence entailing a formal meeting of the Order, the offence of wrong-doing according to the motion and the grave offences according to the two resolutions, subside.[^15]

An offence entailing a formal meeting of the Order means: … on account of this it is called an offence entailing a formal meeting of the Order.

* * *

*Bu-Ss.12.3.1* Thinking a legally valid act to be a legally valid act, he does not give it up, there is an offence entailing a formal meeting of the Order. Being in doubt as to whether it is a legally valid act … Not thinking an act that is legally valid to be an act that is not legally valid is an offence of wrong-doing.[^16]

* * *

*Bu-Ss.12.3.2* There is no offence if he has not been admonished, if he gives it up, if he is mad, if he is a beginner.[^17]

Told is the Twelfth Offence entailing a Formal Meeting of the Order: that concerning one to whom it is difficult to speak
