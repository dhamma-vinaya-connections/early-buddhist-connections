# bhikkhus’ Expiation (_Pācittiya_) 5

*Bu-Pc.5.1.1* *BD.2.194* … at Āḷavī in the chief shrine at Āḷavī.[^1] Now at that time lay-followers came to the monastery for the sake of hearing _dhamma_. When _dhamma_ had been spoken, the bhikkhus who were elders went to their own dwelling-place,[^2] (but) the bhikkhus who were novices lay down in a sleeping-place[^3] just there in the attendance hall[^4] together with the lay-followers, careless, thoughtless, naked, mumbling,[^5] snoring.[^6] The lay-followers[^7] looked down upon, criticised, spread it about, saying:

“How can the revered sirs lie down in a sleeping-place careless, thoughtless, naked, mumbling, snoring?”

bhikkhus heard these lay-followers who looked down upon, criticised, spread it about. Those who were modest bhikkhus looked down upon, *Vin.4.16* criticised, spread it about, saying:

“How can these bhikkhus lie down in a sleeping-place with one who is not ordained?”

Then these bhikkhus told this matter to the lord. He said:

“Is it true, as is said, bhikkhus, that bhikkhus lay down in a sleeping-place with one who is not ordained?”

*BD.2.195* “It is true, lord,” they said.

The enlightened one, the lord, rebuked them, saying:

“How, bhikkhus, can these foolish men lie down in a sleeping-place with one who is not ordained? It is not, bhikkhus, for pleasing those who are not (yet) pleased … And thus, bhikkhus, this rule of training should be set forth:

“Whatever bhikkhu should lie down in a sleeping-place with one who is not ordained, there is an offence of expiation.”

And thus this rule of training for bhikkhus came to be laid down by the lord.

* * *

*Bu-Pc.5.2.1* Then the lord,[^8] having stayed at Āḷavī for as long as he found suitable, set out on tour for Kosambī. Going along on tour, he arrived in due course at Kosambī. The lord stayed there at Kosambī in the Badarikā monastery.[^9] bhikkhus spoke thus to the venerable Rāhula:

“Reverend Rāhula, a rule of training laid down by the lord says that there should be no lying down in a sleeping-place with one who is not ordained. Reverend Rāhula, find a sleeping-place.”[^10]

Then the venerable Rāhula, not obtaining a sleeping-place, lay down in a privy. Then the lord, getting up in the night towards morning, approached this privy, and having approached, he coughed and the venerable Rāhula also coughed.

“Who is here?” he said.

“It is I, lord, Rahula,” he said.

“Why are you sitting there, Rahula?”

Then the venerable Rahula told this matter to the *BD.2.196* lord. Then the lord in this connection, on this occasion, having given reasoned talk,[^11] addressed the bhikkhus, saying:

“I allow you, bhikkhus, to lie down in a sleeping-place with one who is not ordained for two or three nights. And thus, bhikkhus, this rule of training should be set forth:

“Whatever bhikkhu should lie down in a sleeping-place with one who is not ordained for more than two or three nights, there is an offence of expiation.”

* * *

*Bu-Pc.5.3.1* Whatever means: … is bhikkhu to be understood in this case.

Not ordained means: setting aside bhikkhu, the rest are called not ordained.[^12]

More than two or three nights means: exceeding two or three nights. *Vin.4.17*

With means: together with.

Sleeping-place[^13] means: if it is fully covered,[^14] if it is fully closed round, if it is partially covered, if it is partially closed round.

Should lie down in a sleeping-place means: if at sunset on the fourth day a bhikkhu lies down[^15] while one who is not ordained is lying down, there is an offence of expiation. If one who is not ordained lies down while a bhikkhu is lying down,[^16] there is an offence of expiation. Or if both lie down, there is an offence of expiation. If getting up, they lie down again, there is an offence of expiation.

* * *

*Bu-Pc.5.3.2* *BD.2.197* If he thinks that one is not ordained when he is not ordained (and) lies down in a sleeping-place for more than two or three nights, there is an offence of expiation. If he is in doubt as to whether one is not ordained (and) lies down in a sleeping-place for more than two or three nights, there is an offence of expiation. If he thinks that one is ordained when he is not ordained (and) lies down in a sleeping-place for more than two or three nights, there is an offence of expiation. If it is half covered, half closed round, there is an offence of wrong-doing. If he thinks that one is not ordained when he is ordained, there is an offence of wrong-doing. If he is in doubt as to whether one is ordained, there is an offence of wrong-doing. If he thinks that one is ordained when he is ordained, there is no offence.

* * *

*Bu-Pc.5.3.3* There is no offence if he stays for two or three nights; if he stays for less than two or three nights; if having stayed for two nights, departing before dawn on the third night, he stays again[^17]; if it is fully covered (but) not fully closed round; if it is fully closed round (but) not fully covered; if it is partially uncovered, partially not closed round; if the bhikkhu sits down while one who is not ordained is lying down; if one who is not ordained sits down while the bhikkhu is lying down; or if both sit down; if he is mad, if he is the first wrong-doer.

* * *
