# MN138 - Uddesavibhaṅga Sutta - Analysis of a Recitation Passage
## 🌐 Overview

> [!info] Summary
> The Buddha  gives a brief and enigmatic statement on the ways consciousness may become attached. Venerable mahākaccāna is invited by the bhikkhus to draw out the implications

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> Consciousness is not scattered and diffused externally when doesn’t follow after the features of that any object in contact with  the 6 senses, and is not tied, attached, and fettered to gratification in its features
Don’t regard form as self, self as having form, form in self, or self in form nor feelings, perceptions, fabrications or consciousness
> 
> Consciousness is not stuck internally when they enter and remain in the 4 jhanas but doesn’t follow after that rapture, bliss, equanimity or neutral feeling, and is not tied, attached, and fettered to gratification in those l feelings
 that form of theirs decays and perishes, consciousness doesn’t latch on to the perishing of form. Anxieties don’t occupy their mind. So They don’t become frightened, worried, concerned, or anxious because of grasping
>
