# MN23 - Vammika Sutta - Ant-Hill
## 🌐 Overview

> [!info] Summary
> In a curious discourse laden with evocative imagery, a deity presents a riddle to a bhikkhu, who seeks an answer from the Buddha

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> Ant-hill’ is a term for this body liable to impermanence; thinking  all night about what you did during the day—this is the fuming at night. The Work you apply yourself to during the day by body, speech, and mind after thinking about it all night—this is the flaming by day; brahmin’ is a term for the realized one;
‘sage’ is a term for the trainee bhikkhu; sword’ is a term for noble wisdom.
> 
> ‘Digging’ is a term for being energetic; throw out the bar’ means ‘give up ignorance; throw out the bullfrog’ means ‘give up anger and distress; throw out the forked path’ means ‘give up doubt; throw out the box’ means ‘give up the five hindrances; throw out the tortoise’ means ‘give up the five grasping aggregates; throw out the axe and block’ means ‘give up the five kinds of sensual stimulation; throw out the lump of meat’ means ‘give up desire with relishing; dragon’ is a term for a bhikkhu who has ended the defilements. 
This is the meaning of: ‘leave the dragon!, Worship the dragon
>
