# MN10 - Mahāsatipaṭṭhāna Sutta - Mindfulness Meditation
## 🌐 Overview

> [!info] Summary
> Here the Buddha  details the seventh factor of the noble eightfold path, mindfulness meditation. This collects many of the meditation teachings found throughout the canon, especially the foundational practices focusing on the body, and is regarded as one of the most important meditation discourses

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> It’s when a bhikkhu ![[Satipatthanas]]
They meditate observing an aspect of the feelings internally, externally, and both internally and externally. They Meditate observing feelings as liable to originate, as liable to vanish, and as liable to both originate and vanish. Or Mindfulness is established that feelings exist, to the extent necessary for knowledge and mindfulness
> 
> Observing the body1.1. Mindfulness of breathing, 1.2 the postures, 1.3. Situational awareness, 1.4. Focusing on the repulsive, 1.5. Focusing on the elements, 1.6. The charnel ground contemplations,
2. Observing the feelings, 3. Observing the mind, 4. Observing principles, 4.1. The hindrances, 4.2. The aggregates, 4.3. The sense fields, 4.4. The awakening factors, 4.5. The truths
> 

> [!info] Similes
> Carpenter making a deep or shallow cut knows what he is doing
