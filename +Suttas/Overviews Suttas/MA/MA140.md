# MA140 - è‡³é‚Š - Lowliest
## ğŸŒ Overview

> [!info] Summary
> A monk who is half-hearted in his training  misses out on the rewards of both lay life and monastic life. one should let go of bad thoughts and wrong views

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> Yet fools are covetous, inflamed by lust for sensual pleasures, with a mind full of ill will, with intentions corrupted by hate, muddle-minded, lacking clear comprehension, unconcentrated has missed the goal
> 
> This is the lowest form of livelihood, that is, gathering alms, yet clansman has gone forth, disenchantment with birth, aging, disease, death, grief, worry, weeping, sorrow, and distress
> 

> [!info] Similes
> Wood left over from a cremation place in a forest; like bad intentioned bhikkhus
