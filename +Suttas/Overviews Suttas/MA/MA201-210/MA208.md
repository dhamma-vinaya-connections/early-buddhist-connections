# MA208 - 箭毛 - With Sakuludāyī 2
## 🌐 Overview

> [!info] Summary
> A wanderer teaches his doctrine of the “highest splendor” but is unable to give a satisfactory account of what that means. The Buddha  memorably compares him to someone who is in love with an idealized women who he has never met

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> There is a world of perfect happiness, udāyī. And there is a grounded path for realizing a world of perfect happiness
The bhikkhus don’t live the spiritual life under me for the sake of realizing this perfectly happy world. There Are other things that are finer, for the sake of which the bhikkhus live the spiritual life under me
> 
> It’s when, giving up pleasure and pain, and ending former happiness and sadness, a bhikkhu enters and remains in the fourth absorption. There Are deities who have been reborn in a perfectly happy world
Gradual training abbreviated
> 

> [!info] Similes
> A men who desire a woman that never even known or seen, like the promise of other teachings
