> [!info] Summary
> The Buddha  gives a parable of a large log floating downstream, which, if it steers clear of the many obstacles, will eventually reach the ocean. A cowherd named nanda was listening, and determined to go forth.

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> If you do not veer towards the shores, do not sink in, do not get cast up on high ground, do not get caught by human  nor  by nonhuman beings, do not get caught in a whirlpool, and do not become inwardly rotten, you will  slope, and incline towards nibbaÌ„na
> 
> Because right view slants, slopes, and inclines towards nibbaÌ„na
