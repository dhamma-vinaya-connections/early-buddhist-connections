# SN22.129 - AssÄdasutta - Gratification (1)
## ğŸŒ Overview

> [!info] Summary
> The Buddha  explains to a mendicant that ignorance is not understanding the aggregates in terms of arising and ceasing, while knowledge is understanding the aggregates.

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> Ignorance is no to understand that the 5 aggregates are subject to arising and vanishing
> 
> True knowledge is understanding the origin and the passing away, the gratification, the danger, and the escape in the case of form, feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness
>
