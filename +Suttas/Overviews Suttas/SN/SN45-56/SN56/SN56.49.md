# SN56.49 - Paá¹­hamasinerupabbatarÄjasutta - Sineru (1)
## ğŸŒ Overview

> [!info] Summary
> As seven bits of gravel are smaller than Mount Sineru, so is the suffering remaining to one who has seen the four noble truths

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> Sineru, the king of mountains, would be destroyed and eliminated except for seven grains of gravel the size of mung beans
> 
> Same for a noble disciple, a person accomplished in view who has comprehended the suffering that has been utterly destroyed and eliminated is more, while that which remains is trifling.
> 

> [!info] Similes
> Mountain compared with 7 bits of gravel, like amount of suffering destroyed by awakened person
