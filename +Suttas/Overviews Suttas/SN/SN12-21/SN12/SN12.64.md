# SN12.64 - AtthirÄgasutta - If There Is Lust
## ğŸŒ Overview

> [!info] Summary
> The Buddha  describes four factors to which the mind habitually clings. Those who succeed in abandoning passion for these "nutriments" can realize the cessation of birth, aging, and death

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> When there is lust for the nutriment physical food,sense-impression... volitional thought... consciousness,, then consciousness takes a hold therein and grows. Where consciousness takes a hold and grows, there will be occurrence of mind-and-body
> 
> Where there is occurrence of mind-and-body, there is growth of kamma-formations then, there is a future arising of renewed existence, there is future birth, decay and death, suffering
> 

> [!info] Similes
> House on water where sun rays land, and painter paint a figure of man or woman; like consciousness land because nutriments
