# SN14.12 - SanidÄnasutta - With a Source
## ğŸŒ Overview

> [!info] Summary
> Thoughts of sensuality, ill-will, and delusion arise due to a series of causes.

> [!info] Key Excerpts 
> Dependence on the renunciation element there arises perception, then intentions, then desire then passion then pursue and actions by body, speech and mind
> 
> An educated noble disciple on pursue for renunciation, good will and  harmlessness behaves well in three ways: by body, speech, and mind.
> 

> [!info] Similes
> Burning torch dropped  in a thicket of dry grass but quickly extinguished, like quickly eliminate a corrupt perception
