## ğŸŒ Overview

> [!info] Summary
> The newly crowned king AjÄtasattu is disturbed by the violent means by which he achieved the crown. He visits the Buddha  to find peace of mind, and asks him about the benefits of spiritual practice. This is one of the greatest literary and spiritual texts of early buddhism.
King ajatasattu asks the Buddha , "what are the fruits of the contemplative life, visible in the here and now?" The Buddha  replies by painting a comprehensive portrait of the buddhist path of training, illustrating each stage of the training with vivid similes.

> There is no merit or outcome of merit, beings are purified without cause, annihilationism, seven substances are not made & there is no-one, the fourfold restraint, evasiveness,
Fruit of bhikkhu life: mundane is respect even from the king independently of past caste
Hearing the Buddha 's  teaching, gain conviction, give up lay life, act skillfully by body and speech, purified in livelihood and accomplished in ethical conduct, guard the sense doors, have mindfulness and situational awareness, are content, abandon 5 hindrances, 4 jhanas, 8 knowledges, freedom
Avoid & refrain from: killing, stealing, sex, lying, divisive speech, harsh speech, unworthy talk, arguing, flattery, injuring plants, eat after the morning,  entertainment, adorning themselves, money, property, buying and selling, cheating, luxury,  violence, storing up goods, gambling, hinting, and belittling, and using material possessions to pursue other material possessions
Refrain from earn a living with unworthy branches of knowledge like: reading the marks, making predictions, arts, sciences, wedding ceremonies, casting spells, rites & rituals, prescribe medicines
Knowledge and vision, mind-made body, many kinds of psychic power, clairaudience, comprehending the minds of others, recollection of past lives, clairvoyance, knowledge of the ending of defilements
