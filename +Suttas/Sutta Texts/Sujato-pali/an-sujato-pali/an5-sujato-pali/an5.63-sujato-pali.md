## Aṅguttara Nikāya - The Numerical Discourses

## 5: The Book of the Fives

### VII. Perceptions — AN 5.63: Growth (1st)
#### B. Sujato


- © Translated from the Pali by Bhante Sujato. 

1“bhikkhus, a male noble disciple who grows in five ways grows nobly, taking on what is essential and excellent in this life. What five? He grows in faith, ethics, learning, generosity, and wisdom. A male noble disciple who grows in these five ways grows nobly, taking on what is essential and excellent in this life.


2He who grows in faith and ethics,  
wisdom, and both generosity and learning—  
a good man such as he sees clearly,  
and takes on what is essential for himself in this life.”

1"Pañcahi, bhikkhave, vaḍḍhīhi vaḍḍhamāno ariyasāvako ariyāya vaḍḍhiyā vaḍḍhati, sārādāyī ca hoti varādāyī ca kāyassa. Katamāhi pañcahi? Saddhāya vaḍḍhati, sīlena vaḍḍhati, sutena vaḍḍhati, cāgena vaḍḍhati, paññāya vaḍḍhati – imāhi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi vaḍḍhīhi vaḍḍhamāno ariyasāvako ariyāya vaḍḍhiyā vaḍḍhati, sārādāyī ca hoti varādāyī ca kāyassā"ti.


2"Saddhāya sīlena ca yo pavaḍḍhati,  
Paññāya cāgena sutena cūbhayaṁ;  
So tādiso sappuriso vicakkhaṇo,  
Ādīyatī sāramidheva attano"ti.

Tatiyaṁ.
