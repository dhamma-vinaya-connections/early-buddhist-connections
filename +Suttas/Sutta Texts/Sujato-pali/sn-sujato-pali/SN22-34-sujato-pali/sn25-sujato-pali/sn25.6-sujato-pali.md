## Saṁyutta Nikāya — The Linked Discourses



## 25. Okkantasaṁyutta: On Arrival

### I. The Eye — SN25.6: Perception
#### B. Sujato


- © Translated from the Pali by Bhante Sujato. 

1At Sāvatthī.

“bhikkhus, perception of sights is impermanent, perishing, and changing. Perception of sounds, perception of smells, perception of tastes, perception of touches, and perception of thoughts are impermanent, perishing, and changing.

Someone who has faith and confidence in these principles is called a follower by faith. …”

#### Pali

1Sāvatthinidānaṁ.

"Rūpasaññā, bhikkhave, aniccā vipariṇāmī aññathābhāvī; saddasaññā … gandhasaññā … rasasaññā … phoṭṭhabbasaññā … dhammasaññā aniccā vipariṇāmī aññathābhāvī.

Yo, bhikkhave, ime dhamme evaṁ saddahati adhimuccati, ayaṁ vuccati ‘saddhānusārī … pe … sambodhiparāyano’"ti.

Chaṭṭhaṁ.
