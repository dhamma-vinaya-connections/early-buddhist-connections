## Saṁyutta Nikāya — The Linked Discourses

## 22. Khandhasaṁyutta: On the Aggregates

### XIV. Burning Chaff — SN22.139: Impermanence (3rd)
#### B. Sujato


- © Translated from the Pali by Bhante Sujato. 

1At Sāvatthī.

“bhikkhus, you should give up desire and greed for what is impermanent. And what is impermanent?

Form is impermanent; you should give up desire and greed for it. Feeling … Perception … Choices … Consciousness is impermanent; you should give up desire and greed for it.

You should give up desire and greed for what is impermanent.”

#### Pali

1Sāvatthinidānaṁ.

"Yaṁ, bhikkhave, aniccaṁ; tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo.

Kiñca, bhikkhave, aniccaṁ? Rūpaṁ, bhikkhave, aniccaṁ, tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo. Vedanā aniccā … saññā … saṅkhārā … viññāṇaṁ aniccaṁ; tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo.

Yaṁ, bhikkhave, aniccaṁ; tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo"ti.

Catutthaṁ.
