## Saṁyutta Nikāya — The Linked Discourses

## 2. Devaputtasaṁyutta: On Gods

### II. With Anāthapiṇḍika — SN2.16: With Vāsudatta
#### B. Sujato


- © Translated from the Pali by Bhante Sujato.  

1Standing to one side, the god Vāsudatta recited this verse in the Buddha’s presence:

2“Like they’re struck by a sword,  
like their head was on fire,  
a bhikkhu should go forth mindfully,  
to give up sensual desire.”

3“Like they’re struck by a sword,  
like their head was on fire,  
a bhikkhu should go forth mindfully,  
to give up identity view.”

#### Pali

1Ekamantaṁ ṭhito kho vāsudatto devaputto bhagavato santike imaṁ gāthaṁ abhāsi: 

2"Sattiyā viya omaṭṭho,  
ḍayhamānova matthake;  
Kāmarāgappahānāya,  
sato bhikkhu paribbaje"ti.

3"Sattiyā viya omaṭṭho,  
ḍayhamānova matthake;  
Sakkāyadiṭṭhippahānāya,  
sato bhikkhu paribbaje"ti.
