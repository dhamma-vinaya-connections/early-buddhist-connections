## AN6.46 Cunda

## ## Cunda Sutta


On one occasion Ven. Mahā Cunda was staying among the Cetis in Sañjāti. There he addressed the bhikkhus, “Friend bhikkhus!”

“Yes, friend,” the bhikkhus responded to him.

Ven. Mahā Cunda said, “Friends, there is the case where Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus [those devoted to memorizing and analyzing the Dhamma] disparage jhāna bhikkhus, saying, ‘These people are absorbed and besorbed in jhāna, saying, “We are absorbed, we are absorbed.” But why, indeed, are they absorbed? For what purpose are they absorbed? How are they absorbed?’ In that, the Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus do not shine brightly, and the jhāna bhikkhus do not shine brightly. That is not practicing for the welfare of the masses, for the happiness of the masses, for the good of the masses, nor for the welfare & happiness of devas & human beings.

“And further, there is the case where jhāna bhikkhus disparage Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus, saying, ‘These people say, “We are Dhamma-devotees, we are Dhamma-devotees,’ but they are excitable, boisterous, unsteady, mouthy, loose in their talk, muddled in their mindfulness, unalert, uncollected, their minds wandering, their senses uncontrolled. Why, indeed, are they Dhamma devotees? For what purpose are they Dhamma devotees? How are they Dhamma devotees?’ In that, the jhāna bhikkhus do not shine brightly, and the Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus do not shine brightly. That is not practicing for the welfare of the masses, for the happiness of the masses, for the good of the masses, nor for the welfare & happiness of devas & human beings.

“And further, there is the case where Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus praise only Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus, and not jhāna bhikkhus. In that, the Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus do not shine brightly, and the jhāna bhikkhus do not shine brightly. That is not practicing for the welfare of the masses, for the happiness of the masses, for the good of the masses, nor for the welfare & happiness of devas & human beings.

“And further, there is the case where jhāna bhikkhus praise only jhāna bhikkhus, and not Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus. In that, the jhāna bhikkhus do not shine brightly, and the Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus do not shine brightly. That is not practicing for the welfare of the masses, for the happiness of the masses, for the good of the masses, nor for the welfare & happiness of devas & human beings.

“Thus, friends, you should train yourselves: ‘Being Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus, we will speak in praise of jhāna bhikkhus.’ That’s how you should train yourselves. Why is that? Because these are amazing people, hard to find in the world: those who dwell touching the deathless element with the body.[1](#^[[an6-thanissaro]].46note01) ^[[an6-thanissaro]].46note01-ref

“And thus, friends, you should train yourselves: ‘Being jhāna bhikkhus, we will speak in praise of Dhamma-devotee bhikkhus.’ That’s how you should train yourselves. Why is that? Because these are amazing people, hard to find in the world: those who penetrate with discernment statements of profound meaning.”

Note

1. [[an9.43-thanissaro]] and 44 make a distinction between touching a meditative dimension with the body and knowing it with discernment. In both cases, the experience is direct and personal, and in both it leads to the ending of the mental effluents. Thus, “touching with the body” seems to have a more precise meaning than simple personal experience. It could mean that there is a somatic aspect to the experience or that the awareness of the deathless occupies the same fullness of awareness that had been occupied by the body.
