## AN7.21 Conditions for No Decline among the bhikkhus

## ## Bhikkhu-aparihāniya Sutta


I have heard that on one occasion the Blessed One was staying near Rājagaha on Vulture Peak Mountain. There he addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, I will teach you the seven conditions that lead to no decline. Listen & pay close attention. I will speak.”

“As you say, lord,” the bhikkhus responded to the Blessed One.

The Blessed One said: “And which seven are the conditions that lead to no decline?

[1] “As long as the bhikkhus meet often, meet a great deal, their growth can be expected, not their decline.

[2] “As long as the bhikkhus meet in harmony, adjourn from their meetings in harmony, and conduct Saṅgha business in harmony, their growth can be expected, not their decline.

[3] “As long as the bhikkhus neither decree what has been undecreed nor repeal what has been decreed, but practice undertaking the training rules as they have been decreed, their growth can be expected, not their decline.

[4] “As long as the bhikkhus honor, respect, venerate, and do homage to the elder bhikkhus—those with seniority who have long been ordained, the fathers of the Saṅgha, leaders of the Saṅgha—regarding them as worth listening to, their growth can be expected, not their decline.

[5] “As long as the bhikkhus do not submit to the power of any arisen craving that leads to further becoming, their growth can be expected, not their decline.

[6] “As long as the bhikkhus see their own benefit in wilderness dwellings, their growth can be expected, not their decline.

[7] “As long as the bhikkhus each keep firmly in mind: ‘If there are any well-behaved companions in the holy life who have yet to come, may they come; and may the well-behaved companions in the holy life who have come live in comfort,’ their growth can be expected, not their decline.

“As long as the bhikkhus remain steadfast in these seven conditions, and as long as these seven conditions endure among the bhikkhus, the bhikkhus’ growth can be expected, not their decline.”
