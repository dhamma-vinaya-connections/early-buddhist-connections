## MN33 The Greater Cowherd Discourse

## ## Mahā Gopālaka Sutta


I have heard that on one occasion the Blessed One was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's monastery. There he addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus!”

“Yes, lord,” the bhikkhus responded to him.

The Blessed One said, “bhikkhus, a cowherd endowed with eleven factors is incapable of looking after a herd so that it prospers & grows. Which eleven? There is the case where a cowherd is not well-versed in forms [appearances], unskilled in characteristics,[1](#^mn33note01) doesn’t pick out flies’ eggs, doesn’t dress wounds, doesn’t fumigate (the cattle pen), doesn’t know fords, doesn’t know what it is (for the cattle) to have drunk, doesn’t know the road, isn’t skilled in pastures, milks dry, and shows no extra respect for the bulls who are fathers & leaders of the herd. A cowherd endowed with these eleven factors is incapable of looking after a herd so that it prospers & grows. ^mn33note01-ref

“A bhikkhu endowed with these eleven factors is incapable of attaining growth, increase, & abundance in this Dhamma-Vinaya. Which eleven? There is the case where a bhikkhu is not well-versed in forms, unskilled in characteristics, doesn’t pick out flies’ eggs, doesn’t dress wounds, doesn’t fumigate, doesn’t know fords, doesn’t know what it is to have drunk, doesn’t know the road, is not skilled in pastures, milks dry, and shows no extra respect for the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha.

“And how is a bhikkhu not well-versed in forms? There is the case where a bhikkhu doesn’t discern, as it has come to be, that every form whatsoever is composed of the four great elements [earth, water, fire, & wind] and the forms dependent on them. This is how a bhikkhu is not well-versed in forms.

“And how is a bhikkhu unskilled in characteristics? There is the case where a bhikkhu doesn’t discern, as it has come to be, that a fool is characterized by his actions, a wise person is characterized by his actions.[2](#^mn33note02) This is how a bhikkhu is unskilled in characteristics. ^mn33note02-ref

“And how does a bhikkhu not pick out flies’ eggs? There is the case where a bhikkhu acquiesces with an arisen thought of sensuality. He doesn’t abandon it, dispel it, demolish it, or wipe it out of existence. He acquiesces with an arisen thought of ill will... an arisen thought of harmfulness. He doesn’t abandon it, dispel it, demolish it, or wipe it out of existence. He acquiesces with arisen evil, unskillful qualities. He doesn’t abandon them, dispel them demolish them, or wipe them out of existence. This is how a bhikkhu doesn’t pick out flies’ eggs.

“And how does a bhikkhu not dress wounds? There is the case where a bhikkhu, on seeing a form with the eye, grasps at themes or details by which—as he dwells without restraint over the faculty of the eye—evil, unskillful qualities such as greed or distress might assail him. He doesn’t practice for its restraint. He doesn’t protect the faculty of the eye. On hearing a sound with the ear... On smelling an aroma with the nose... On tasting a flavor with the tongue... On touching a tactile sensation with the body... On cognizing an idea with the intellect, he grasps at themes or details by which—as he dwells without restraint over the faculty of the intellect—evil, unskillful qualities such as greed or distress might assail him. He doesn’t practice for its restraint. He doesn’t protect the faculty of the intellect. This is how a bhikkhu doesn’t dress wounds.

“And how does a bhikkhu not fumigate? There is the case where a bhikkhu doesn’t teach others in detail the Dhamma as he has heard and mastered it. This is how a bhikkhu doesn’t fumigate.

“And how does a bhikkhu not know fords? There is the case where a bhikkhu goes time & again to the bhikkhus who are learned, well-versed in the tradition, who have memorized the Dhamma, the Vinaya, and the Mātikās,[3](#^mn33note03) but doesn’t question them, doesn’t present them with his problems: ‘How is this, venerable sir? What is the meaning of this?’ These venerable ones do not reveal what has not been revealed to him, do not make plain what has not been made plain to him, do not resolve his doubts about the many teachings that might give rise to doubt. This is how a bhikkhu doesn’t know fords. ^mn33note03-ref

“And how does a bhikkhu not know what it is to have drunk? There is the case where a bhikkhu, when the Dhamma-Vinaya proclaimed by the Tathāgata is being taught, doesn’t gain understanding of the meaning, doesn’t gain understanding of the Dhamma, doesn’t gain joy connected with the Dhamma. This is how a bhikkhu doesn’t know what it is to have drunk.

“And how does a bhikkhu not know the road? There is the case where a bhikkhu doesn’t discern, as it has come to be, the noble eightfold path. This is how a bhikkhu doesn’t know the road.

“And how is a bhikkhu unskilled in pastures? There is the case where a bhikkhu doesn’t discern, as they have come to be, the four establishings of mindfulness.[4](#^mn33note04) This is how a bhikkhu is unskilled in pastures. ^mn33note04-ref

“And how does a bhikkhu milk dry? There is the case where a bhikkhu—when faithful householders invite him to accept gifts of cloth, alms food, lodgings, and medicinal requisites for curing the sick—knows no moderation in taking. This is how a bhikkhu milks dry.

“And how does a bhikkhu show no extra respect for the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha? There is the case where a bhikkhu doesn’t establish himself in bodily acts of goodwill, in public & in private, toward the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha. He doesn’t establish himself in verbal acts of goodwill... in mental acts of goodwill, in public & in private, toward the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha.

“A bhikkhu endowed with these eleven factors is incapable of attaining growth, increase, & abundance in this Dhamma-Vinaya.

“bhikkhus, a cowherd endowed with eleven factors is capable of looking after a herd so that it prospers & grows. Which eleven? There is the case where a cowherd is well-versed in forms (appearances), skilled in characteristics, picks out flies’ eggs, dresses wounds, fumigates, knows fords, knows what it is to have drunk, knows the road, is skilled in pastures, doesn’t milk dry, and shows extra respect for the bulls who are fathers & leaders of the herd. A cowherd endowed with these eleven factors is capable of looking after a herd so that it prospers & grows.

“A bhikkhu endowed with these eleven factors is capable of attaining growth, increase, & abundance in this Dhamma-Vinaya. Which eleven? There is the case where a bhikkhu is well-versed in forms, skilled in characteristics, picks out flies’ eggs, dresses wounds, fumigates, knows fords, knows what it is to have drunk, knows the road, is skilled in pastures, doesn’t milk dry, and shows extra respect for the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha.

“And how is a bhikkhu well-versed in forms? There is the case where a bhikkhu discerns, as it has come to be, that every form whatsoever is composed of the four great elements and the forms dependent on them. This is how a bhikkhu is well-versed in forms.

“And how is a bhikkhu skilled in characteristics? There is the case where a bhikkhu discerns, as it has come to be, that a fool is characterized by his actions, a wise person is characterized by his actions. This is how a bhikkhu is skilled in characteristics.

“And how does a bhikkhu pick out flies’ eggs? There is the case where a bhikkhu doesn’t acquiesce with an arisen thought of sensuality. He abandons it, dispels it, demolishes it, & wipes it out of existence. He doesn’t acquiesce with an arisen thought of ill will... an arisen thought of harmfulness. He abandons it, dispels it, demolishes it, & wipes it out of existence. He doesn’t acquiesce with arisen evil, unskillful qualities. He abandons them, dispels them, demolishes them, & wipes them out of existence. This is how a bhikkhu picks out flies’ eggs.

“And how does a bhikkhu dress wounds? There is the case where a bhikkhu, on seeing a form with the eye, doesn’t grasp at any theme or details by which—if he were to dwell without restraint over the faculty of the eye—evil, unskillful qualities such as greed or distress might assail him. He practices for its restraint. He protects the faculty of the eye. On hearing a sound with the ear... On smelling an aroma with the nose... On tasting a flavor with the tongue... On touching a tactile sensation with the body... On cognizing an idea with the intellect, he doesn’t grasp at any theme or details by which—if he were to dwell without restraint over the faculty of the intellect—evil, unskillful qualities such as greed or distress might assail him. He practices for its restraint. He protects the faculty of the intellect. This is how a bhikkhu dresses wounds.

“And how does a bhikkhu fumigate? There is the case where a bhikkhu teaches others in detail the Dhamma as he has heard and mastered it. This is how a bhikkhu fumigates.

“And how does a bhikkhu know fords? There is the case where a bhikkhu goes time & again to the bhikkhus who are learned, well-versed in the tradition, who have memorized the Dhamma, the Vinaya, and the Mātikās. He questions them, presents them with his problems: ‘How is this, venerable sir? What is the meaning of this?’ These venerable ones reveal what has not been revealed to him, make plain what has not been made plain to him, resolve his doubts about the many teachings that might give rise to doubt. This is how a bhikkhu knows fords.

“And how does a bhikkhu know what it is to have drunk? There is the case where a bhikkhu, when the Dhamma-Vinaya proclaimed by the Tathāgata is being taught, gains understanding of the meaning, gains understanding of the Dhamma, gains joy connected with the Dhamma. This is how a bhikkhu knows what it is to have drunk.

“And how does a bhikkhu know the road? There is the case where a bhikkhu discerns, as it has come to be, the noble eightfold path. This is how a bhikkhu knows the roads.

“And how is a bhikkhu skilled in pastures? There is the case where a bhikkhu discerns, as they have come to be, the four establishings of mindfulness. This is how a bhikkhu is skilled in pastures.

“And how does a bhikkhu not milk dry? There is the case where a bhikkhu—when faithful householders invite him to accept gifts of cloth, alms food, lodgings, and medicinal requisites for curing the sick—knows moderation in taking. This is how a bhikkhu doesn’t milk dry.

“And how does a bhikkhu show extra respect for the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha? There is the case where a bhikkhu establishes himself in bodily acts of goodwill, in public & in private, toward the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha. He establishes himself in verbal acts of goodwill... in mental acts of goodwill, in public & in private, toward the elder bhikkhus with seniority, who have been ordained long, who are fathers & leaders of the Saṅgha.

“A bhikkhu endowed with these eleven factors is capable of attaining growth, increase, & abundance in this Dhamma-Vinaya.”

### Notes

1. According to the Commentary, a cowherd “unskilled in characteristics” is one who doesn’t recognize the branding marks used on cattle. [↩︎](#^mn33note01-ref) ^mn33note01

2. See [[an3.2-thanissaro]]. [↩︎](#^mn33note02-ref) ^mn33note02

3. The Mātikā are lists of Dhamma topics that eventually were developed into the Abhidhamma. [↩︎](#^mn33note03-ref) ^mn33note03

4. See [[sn47.6-thanissaro]]. [↩︎](#^mn33note04-ref) ^mn33note04
