### Translator: Charles Patton

## [12. Factors of Awakening](index.html)

### 20 (723). Junior bhikkhus

1. Thus I have heard:<a id="fnref1"></a>[^1] One time, the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in Jeta’s Grove in Śrāvastī.

2. It was then that the Bhagavān addressed the bhikkhus, “It’s good, bhikkhus, to rely on someone to hear the Dharma. Junior bhikkhus are to support and serve the venerable elders. Why is that? From time to time, junior bhikkhus who support and serve senior bhikkhus can hear the profound and subtle Dharma. After hearing the profound Dharma, they’ll achieve two ways of being correct: Correctness of body and correctness of mind.

3. “At that point, they’ll cultivate the awakening factor of mindfulness. After cultivating the awakening factor of mindfulness, they’ll fulfill the awakening factor of mindfulness. After fulfilling the awakening factor of mindfulness, they’ll discriminate qualities, discern qualities, and deliberate on qualities. At that point, they’ll skillfully cultivate the awakening factor of discriminating qualities … they’ll cultivate the fulfillment of the awakening factor of equanimity.”

4. After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikkhus who heard what he taught rejoiced and approved.
