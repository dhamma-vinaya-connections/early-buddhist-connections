### Translator: Charles Patton

## [13. Factors of the Noble Path](index.html)

### 8 (755). Desire (2)

1. Thus I have heard:<a id="fnref1"></a>[^1] One time, the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in Jeta’s Grove of Śrāvastī.

2. It was then that the Bhagavān addressed the bhikkhus, “There’s the expression ‘desire.’ What is it that’s called ‘desire’? Desire refers to the qualities of the five desires. What are the five? They are forms cognized by the eye that are lovely, agreeable, memorable, and nurture sensual pleasures. Thus, they are the contacts cognized by the ear … nose … tongue … body that are lovely, agreeable, memorable, and nurture sensual pleasures. They are called desires, but that is not desire. It’s the craving for them that’s called desire.”

3. The Bhagavān then spoke in verse,

- “The world is a mixture of five forms,  
    But they aren’t the craving desire.  
    It’s craving for perceptions and notions,  
    Then, that’s the desire of men.  
    Myriad forms are always present in the world,  
    But the practitioner ends his mind’s desires.”

4. The bhikkhus said to the Buddha, “Bhagavān, isn’t there a path and steps to ending this craving desire?”

The Buddha told the bhikkhus, “There is the right eightfold path that can end craving desire, which is right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right method, right mindfulness, and right samādhi.”

5. After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, the bhikkhus who heard what the Buddha taught rejoiced and approved.
