## 2\. Influxes Abandoned by Restraint

And what are the influxes that should be abandoned by restraint? Take a bhikkhu who, reflecting in light of the origin, abides restraining the faculty of the eye. For the influxes, trouble, and affliction that would arise in someone who abides without restraint do not arise when there is such restraint. These are called the influxes that should be abandoned by restraint.

## 3\. Influxes Abandoned by Using

And what are the influxes that should be abandoned by using? Take a bhikkhu who, reflecting in light of the origin, makes use of robes: â€˜Only for the sake of warding off cold and heat; for warding off the touch of flies, mosquitoes, wind, sun, and reptiles; and for covering up the private parts.â€™

## 4\. Influxes Abandoned by Bearing

And what are the influxes that should be abandoned by bearing? Take a bhikkhu who, reflecting in light of the origin, endures cold, heat, hunger, and thirst. He endures the touch of flies, mosquitoes, wind, sun, and reptiles. He endures rude and unwelcome criticism. And he endures bodily feelings that are painful, sharp, harsh, acute, unpleasant, disagreeable, and life-threatening. 

For the influxes, trouble, and affliction that would arise in someone who abides without bearing these things do not arise when they are endured. These are called the influxes that should be abandoned by bearing.

## 5\. Influxes Abandoned by Avoiding

And what are the influxes that should be abandoned by avoiding? Take a bhikkhu who, reflecting in light of the origin, avoids a wild elephant, a wild horse, a wild ox, a wild dog, a snake, a stump, thorny ground, a pit, a cliff, a swamp, and a sewer. Reflecting in light of the origin, he avoids sitting on inappropriate seats, walking in inappropriate neighborhoods, and mixing with bad friendsâ€”whatever wise fellow renunciates would take to be an unsuitable setting.

For the influxes, trouble, and affliction that would arise in someone who abides without avoiding these things do not arise when they are avoided. These are called the influxes that should be abandoned by avoiding.

## 6\. Influxes Abandoned by Dispelling

And what are the influxes that should be abandoned by dispelling? A bhikkhu, reflecting in light of the origin, does not tolerate a sensual, averse, or cruel thought that has arisen, but abandones it, dispels it, eliminates it, and obliterates it. He does not tolerate any unbeneficial phenomena that have arisen, but abandons them, dispels them, eliminates them, and obliterates them. 

For the influxes, trouble, and affliction that would arise in someone who abides without dispelling these things do not arise when they are dispelled. These are called the influxes that should be abandoned by dispelling.

## 7\. Influxes Abandoned by Developing

And what are the influxes that should be abandoned by developing? It is when a bhikkhu, reflecting in light of the origin, develops the awakening factors of recollectedness,  discernment of phenomena,  effort, joy, calm, composure, and equanimity, which rely on withdrawal, dispassion, and cessation, and ripen in relinquishment.

For the influxes, trouble, and affliction that would arise in someone who abides without developing these things do not arise when they are developed. These are called the influxes that should be abandoned by developing.
