#### 4.3.1. Virtue

##### 4.3.1.1. The Shorter Section on Virtue

And how, great king, is the bhikkhu accomplished in virtue? It is when the bhikkhu gives up and abstains from killing living creatures, renouncing the rod and the sword. He is scrupulous and kind, living compassionate for the welfare of all creatures and beings. This pertains to his virtue.

He gives up and abstains from taking what is not given. He takes only what is given, and expects only what is given. He keeps himself clean by not thieving. This pertains to his virtue.

He gives up and abstains from incelibacy. He is celibate, set apart, avoiding the vulgar act of sex. This pertains to his virtue.

He gives up and abstains from lying. He speaks the truth and sticks to the truth. He is honest and trustworthy, and does not trick the world with his words. This pertains to his virtue.

He gives up and abstains from divisive speech. He does not repeat in one place what he heard in another to divide people against each other. Instead, he reconciles those who are divided, supporting unity, delighting in harmony, enjoying harmony, and speaking words that promote harmony. This pertains to his virtue.

He gives up and abstains from harsh speech. He speaks in a way that is gentle, pleasing to the ear, affectionate, going to the heart, polite, likable, and agreeable to the people. This pertains to his virtue.

He gives up and abstains from frivolous speech. His words are timely, true, and meaningful, in line with the teaching-and-discipline. He says things at the right time which are valuable, reasonable, succinct, and beneficial. This pertains to his virtue.

He abstains from violence towards plants and seeds. He eats in one part of the day, abstaining from eating at night and at the wrong time. He abstains from dancing, singing, music, and watching shows. He avoids beautifying and adorning himself with garlands, perfumes, and makeup. He avoids high and luxurious beds. He abstains from receiving gold and money, raw grains, raw meat, women and girls, male and female bondservants, goats and sheep, chickens and pigs, elephants, cows, horses, and mares, and fields and land. He abstains from running errands and messages; buying and selling; falsifying weights, metals, or measures; bribery, fraud, cheating, and duplicity; mutilation, murder, abduction, banditry, plunder, and violence. This pertains to his virtue.

(The shorter section on virtue is finished).
