# AN 10.76 - Three Things

â€œBhikkhus, if three things were not found, the Realized One, the Arahant, the Rightly Self-Awakened One would not arise in the world, and the teaching-and-discipline proclaimed by the Realized One would not shine in the world. What three? Birth, aging, and death. If these three things were not found, the Realized One, the Arahant, the Rightly Self-Awakened One would not arise in the world, and the teaching-and-discipline proclaimed by the Realized One would not shine in the world. But since these three things are found, the Realized One, the Arahant, the Rightly Self-Awakened One arises in the world, and the teaching-and-discipline proclaimed by the Realized One shines in the world.

Without giving up these three things, one cannot give up birth, aging, and death. What three? Passion, aversion, and muddledness. Without giving up these three things, one cannot give up birth, aging, and death.

Without giving up these three things, one cannot give up passion, aversion, and muddledness. What three? Personality-view, doubt, and the grasping at virtue-and-duty. Without giving up these three things, one cannot give up passion, aversion, and muddledness.
