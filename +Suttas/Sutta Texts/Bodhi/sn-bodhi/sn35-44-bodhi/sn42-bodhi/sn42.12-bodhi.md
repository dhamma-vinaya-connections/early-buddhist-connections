## I

â€œThere are, headman, these two extremes which should not be cultivated by one who has gone forth into homelessness: the pursuit of sensual happiness in sensual pleasures, which is low, vulgar, the way of worldlings, ignoble, unbeneficial; and the pursuit of self-mortification, which is painful, ignoble, unbeneficial. And what is that middle way awakened to by the TathaÌ„gata, which gives rise to vision â€¦ leads to NibbaÌ„na? It is this Noble Eightfold Path; that is, right view ... right concentration. ## II

â€œThere are, headman, these three persons who enjoy sensual pleasures existing in the world. What three?

### (i)

â€œHere, headman, someone who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth unlawfully, by violence. Having done so, he does not make himself happy and pleased, nor does he share it and do meritorious deeds.

### (ii)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth unlawfully, by violence. Having done so, he makes himself happy and pleased, but he does not share it and do meritorious deeds.

### (iii)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth unlawfully, by violence. Having done so, he makes himself happy and pleased, and he shares it and does meritorious deeds.

### (iv)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth both lawfully and unlawfully, both by violence and without violence. Having done so, \[332\] he does not make himself happy and pleased, nor does he share it and do meritorious deeds.

### (v)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth both lawfully and unlawfully, both by violence and without violence. Having done so, he makes himself happy and pleased, but he does not share it and do meritorious deeds.

### (vi)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth both lawfully and unlawfully, both by violence and without violence. Having done so, he makes himself happy and pleased, and he shares it and does meritorious deeds.

### (vii)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth lawfully, without violence. Having done so, he does not make himself happy and pleased, nor does he share it and do meritorious deeds.

### (viii)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth lawfully, without violence. Having done so, he makes himself happy and pleased, but he does not share it and do meritorious deeds.

### (ix)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth lawfully, without violence. Having done so, he makes himself happy and pleased, and he shares it and does meritorious deeds. # (x)

â€œThen, headman, someone here who enjoys sensual pleasures seeks wealth lawfully, without violence. Having done so, \[333\] he makes himself happy and pleased, and he shares it and does meritorious deeds. â€œThere are, headman, these three kinds of wearing away that are directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, applicable, to be personally experienced by the wise. What three?
