# Iti 44 Paṭisallānasuttaṃ

8.The Retreat Discourse
45. Vuttañhetaṃ bhagavatā, vuttamarahatāti me sutaṃ –
45.This was said by the Blessed One, said by the Arahant—so I heard.
‘‘Paṭisallānārāmā [paṭisallānārāmā (ka.)], bhikkhave, viharatha paṭisallānaratā, ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyuttā, anirākatajjhānā, vipassanāya samannāgatā, brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ. Paṭisallānārāmānaṃ, bhikkhave, viharataṃ paṭisallānaratānaṃ ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyuttānaṃ anirākatamajjhānānaṃ vipassanāya samannāgatānaṃ brūhetānaṃ suññāgārānaṃ dvinnaṃ phalānaṃ aññataraṃ phalaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ – diṭṭheva dhamme aññā, sati vā upādisese anāgāmitā’’ti. Etamatthaṃ bhagavā avoca. Tatthetaṃ iti vuccati –
"Dwell, monks, delighting in seclusion, devoted to seclusion, devoted to inner stillness of mind, not neglecting jhāna, endowed with insight, cultivating empty dwellings. For monks who dwell delighting in seclusion, devoted to seclusion, devoted to inner stillness of mind, not neglecting jhāna, endowed with insight, cultivating empty dwellings, one of two fruits is to be expected: either final knowledge in this very life or, if there is a residue, non-returning." The Blessed One said this. In this regard, it is said:
‘‘Ye santacittā nipakā, satimanto ca [satimantova (sī. ka.)] jhāyino;Sammā dhammaṃ vipassanti, kāmesu anapekkhino.
"Those with peaceful minds, discerning, mindful, and meditative, rightly contemplate the Dhamma, unattached to sensual pleasures."
‘‘Appamādaratā santā, pamāde bhayadassino;Abhabbā parihānāya, nibbānasseva santike’’ti.
"The peaceful delight in diligence, seeing fear in negligence; they are incapable of falling away, and are close to Nibbana."
Ayampi attho vutto bhagavatā, iti me sutanti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.
This meaning, too, was spoken by the Blessed One, thus I heard. Eighth.
