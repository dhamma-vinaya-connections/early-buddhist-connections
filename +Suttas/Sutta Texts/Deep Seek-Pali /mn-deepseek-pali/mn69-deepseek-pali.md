# 69. Goliyānisuttaṃ

## 69. Goliyāni Sutta

173. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena goliyāni [gulissāni (sī. pī.), golissāni (syā. kaṃ.)] nāma bhikkhu āraññiko [āraññako (sabbattha)] padasamācāro [padarasamācāro (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] saṅghamajjhe osaṭo hoti kenacideva karaṇīyena. Tatra kho āyasmā sāriputto goliyāniṃ bhikkhuṃ ārabbha bhikkhū āmantesi –

173.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrel Sanctuary. Now at that time, a bhikkhu named Goliyāni, a forest-dweller of coarse conduct, had been summoned to the Sangha assembly for some reason. There, Venerable Sāriputta addressed the bhikkhus concerning the bhikkhu Goliyāni:

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena sabrahmacārīsu sagāravena bhavitabbaṃ sappatissena. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto sabrahmacārīsu agāravo hoti appatisso, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena, yo ayamāyasmā sabrahmacārīsu agāravo hoti appatisso’ti – tassa [appatissotissa (sī. pī.)] bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena sabrahmacārīsu sagāravena bhavitabbaṃ sappatissena.

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should be respectful and reverential toward his spiritual companions. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha but is disrespectful and irreverent toward his spiritual companions, there will be those who speak against him: 'What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller's solitary, independent dwelling in the forest, when this venerable one is disrespectful and irreverent toward his spiritual companions?' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should be respectful and reverential toward his spiritual companions.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena āsanakusalena bhavitabbaṃ – ‘iti there ca bhikkhū nānupakhajja nisīdissāmi nave ca bhikkhū na āsanena paṭibāhissāmī’ti. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto na āsanakusalo hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena, yo ayamāyasmā āsanakusalo na hotī’ti [yo ayamāyasmā ābhisamācārikampi dhammaṃ na jānātīti (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena āsanakusalena bhavitabbaṃ.

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should be skilled in seating, thinking: 'I will sit without encroaching on the elder bhikkhus, and I will not hinder the new bhikkhus with my seat.' If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha but is not skilled in seating, there will be those who speak against him: 'What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller's solitary, independent dwelling in the forest, since this venerable one is not skilled in seating?' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should be skilled in seating.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena ābhisamācārikopi dhammo jānitabbo. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto ābhisamācārikampi dhammaṃ na jānāti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā ābhisamācārikampi dhammaṃ na jānātī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena ābhisamācārikopi dhammo jānitabbo [ayaṃ ābhisamācārikatatiyavāro sī. syā. kaṃ. pī. potthakesu na dissati].

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should also know the rules of proper conduct. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha but does not know the rules of proper conduct, there will be those who speak against him: 'What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller's solitary, independent dwelling in the forest, when this venerable one does not even know the rules of proper conduct?' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should know the rules of proper conduct.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena nātikālena gāmo pavisitabbo nātidivā [na divā (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] paṭikkamitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto atikālena gāmaṃ pavisati atidivā paṭikkamati, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā atikālena gāmaṃ pavisati atidivā paṭikkamatī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena nātikālena gāmo pavisitabbo, nātidivā paṭikkamitabbaṃ.

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should not enter the village too late, nor return too late in the day. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha and enters the village too late or returns too late in the day, there will be those who speak against him: 'What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller's solitary, independent dwelling in the forest, when this venerable one enters the village too late and returns too late in the day?' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should not enter the village too late, nor return too late in the day.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena na purebhattaṃ pacchābhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto purebhattaṃ pacchābhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjati, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Ayaṃ nūnimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena viharato vikālacariyā bahulīkatā, tamenaṃ saṅghagatampi samudācaratī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena na purebhattaṃ pacchābhattaṃ kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjitabbaṃ.

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should not engage in visiting families before or after the meal. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha and engages in visiting families before or after the meal, there will be those who speak against him: 'Surely, for this venerable forest-dweller, dwelling alone in the forest with independent living, unseasonable conduct has become frequent; it affects him even when he has come to the Sangha.' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should not engage in visiting families before or after the meal.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena anuddhatena bhavitabbaṃ acapalena. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto uddhato hoti capalo, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Idaṃ nūnimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena viharato uddhaccaṃ cāpalyaṃ bahulīkataṃ, tamenaṃ saṅghagatampi samudācaratī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena anuddhatena bhavitabbaṃ acapalena.

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should be not uplifted and not fickle. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha and is uplifted and fickle, there will be those who speak against him: 'Surely, for this venerable forest-dweller, dwelling alone in the forest with independent living, upliftedness and fickleness have become frequent; it affects him even when he has come to the Sangha.' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should be not uplifted and not fickle.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena amukharena bhavitabbaṃ avikiṇṇavācena. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto mukharo hoti vikiṇṇavāco, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā mukharo vikiṇṇavāco’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena amukharena bhavitabbaṃ avikiṇṇavācena.

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should be not garrulous and not of scattered speech. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha and is garrulous and of scattered speech, there will be those who speak against him: 'What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller's solitary, independent dwelling in the forest, when this venerable one is garrulous and of scattered speech?' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should be not garrulous and not of scattered speech.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena suvacena [subbacena (sī. ka.)] bhavitabbaṃ kalyāṇamittena. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu saṅghagato saṅghe viharanto dubbaco hoti pāpamitto, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā dubbaco pāpamitto’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā saṅghagatena saṅghe viharantena suvacena bhavitabbaṃ kalyāṇamittena.

Bhikkhus, when a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha, he should be easy to speak to and have good friends. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha and is difficult to speak to and has bad friends, there will be those who speak against him: 'What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller's solitary, independent dwelling in the forest, when this venerable one is difficult to speak to and has bad friends?' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu who has come to the Sangha and is dwelling in the Sangha should be easy to speak to and have good friends.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā indriyesu guttadvārena bhavitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu indriyesu aguttadvāro hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā indriyesu aguttadvāro’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā indriyesu guttadvārena bhavitabbaṃ.

Bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be one who guards the doors of the senses. If, bhikkhus, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu is one who does not guard the doors of the senses, there will be those who speak against him: 'What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller's solitary, independent dwelling in the forest, when this venerable one does not guard the doors of the senses?' There will be those who speak against him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be one who guards the doors of the senses.

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā bhojane mattaññunā bhavitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhojane amattaññū hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā bhojane amattaññū’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā bhojane mattaññunā bhavitabbaṃ.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be moderate in eating. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu is not moderate in eating, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since this venerable one is not moderate in eating?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be moderate in eating.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā jāgariyaṃ anuyuttena bhavitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu jāgariyaṃ ananuyutto hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā jāgariyaṃ ananuyutto’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā jāgariyaṃ anuyuttena bhavitabbaṃ.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be devoted to wakefulness. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu is not devoted to wakefulness, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since this venerable one is not devoted to wakefulness?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be devoted to wakefulness.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā āraddhavīriyena bhavitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu kusīto hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā kusīto’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā āraddhavīriyena bhavitabbaṃ.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be energetic. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu is lazy, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since this venerable one is lazy?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be energetic.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā upaṭṭhitassatinā bhavitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu muṭṭhassatī hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā muṭṭhassatī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā upaṭṭhitassatinā bhavitabbaṃ.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should have mindfulness established. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu is unmindful, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since this venerable one is unmindful?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should have mindfulness established.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā samāhitena bhavitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu asamāhito hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā asamāhito’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā samāhitena bhavitabbaṃ.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be collected. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu is not collected, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since this venerable one is not collected?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be collected.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā paññavatā bhavitabbaṃ. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu duppañño hoti, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā duppañño’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā paññavatā bhavitabbaṃ.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be wise. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu is unwise, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since this venerable one is unwise?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should be wise.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā abhidhamme abhivinaye yogo karaṇīyo. Santāvuso, āraññikaṃ bhikkhuṃ abhidhamme abhivinaye pañhaṃ pucchitāro. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu abhidhamme abhivinaye pañhaṃ puṭṭho na sampāyati, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā abhidhamme abhivinaye pañhaṃ puṭṭho na sampāyatī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā abhidhamme abhivinaye yogo karaṇīyo.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should apply himself to the Abhidhamma and the Abhivinaya. For, friend, there are those who question a forest-dwelling bhikkhu about the Abhidhamma and the Abhivinaya. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu, when questioned about the Abhidhamma and the Abhivinaya, does not satisfy, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since he does not satisfy when questioned about the Abhidhamma and the Abhivinaya?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should apply himself to the Abhidhamma and the Abhivinaya.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā tattha yogo karaṇīyo. Santāvuso, āraññikaṃ bhikkhuṃ ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā tattha pañhaṃ pucchitāro. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā tattha pañhaṃ puṭṭho na sampāyati, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā tattha pañhaṃ puṭṭho na sampāyatī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā tattha yogo karaṇīyo.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should apply himself to those peaceful liberations, the formless attainments, which transcend form. For, friend, there are those who question a forest-dwelling bhikkhu about those peaceful liberations, the formless attainments, which transcend form. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu, when questioned about those peaceful liberations, the formless attainments, which transcend form, does not satisfy, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since he does not satisfy when questioned about those peaceful liberations, the formless attainments, which transcend form?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should apply himself to those peaceful liberations, the formless attainments, which transcend form.”

‘‘Āraññikenāvuso, bhikkhunā uttari manussadhamme yogo karaṇīyo. Santāvuso, āraññikaṃ bhikkhuṃ uttari manussadhamme pañhaṃ pucchitāro. Sace, āvuso, āraññiko bhikkhu uttari manussadhamme pañhaṃ puṭṭho na sampāyati, tassa bhavanti vattāro. ‘Kiṃ panimassāyasmato āraññikassa ekassāraññe serivihārena yo ayamāyasmā yassatthāya pabbajito tamatthaṃ na jānātī’ti – tassa bhavanti vattāro. Tasmā āraññikena bhikkhunā uttari manussadhamme yogo karaṇīyo’’ti.

“Friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should apply himself to the superior human states. For, friend, there are those who question a forest-dwelling bhikkhu about the superior human states. If, friend, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu, when questioned about the superior human states, does not satisfy, there will be those who criticize him. ‘What is the use of this venerable forest-dweller living alone in the wilderness as he pleases, since this venerable one does not know the purpose for which he went forth?’—there will be those who criticize him. Therefore, a forest-dwelling bhikkhu should apply himself to the superior human states.”

Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā mahāmoggallāno [mahāmoggalāno (ka.)] āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘āraññikeneva nu kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā ime dhammā samādāya vattitabbā udāhu gāmantavihārināpī’’ti? ‘‘Āraññikenāpi kho, āvuso moggallāna, bhikkhunā ime dhammā samādāya vattitabbā pageva gāmantavihārinā’’ti.

When this was said, Venerable Mahāmoggallāna said to Venerable Sāriputta: “Friend Sāriputta, should these qualities be undertaken and practiced only by a forest-dwelling bhikkhu, or also by a village-dwelling bhikkhu?” “Friend Moggallāna, these qualities should be undertaken and practiced by a forest-dwelling bhikkhu, all the more so by a village-dwelling bhikkhu.”

Goliyānisuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The Goliyāni Sutta, the Ninth, is finished.
