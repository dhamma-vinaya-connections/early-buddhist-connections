# 42. Verañjakasuttaṃ

## 42. The Verañjaka Sutta

444. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena verañjakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā sāvatthiyaṃ paṭivasanti kenacideva karaṇīyena. Assosuṃ kho verañjakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā – ‘‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ; kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti’. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’ti.

444.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. At that time, the brahmin householders of Verañja were staying in Sāvatthī for some business. The brahmin householders of Verañja heard: "The ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, who has gone forth from the Sakyan clan, is dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. And about that Venerable Gotama, such a good report has spread: ‘That Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in true knowledge and conduct, the Fortunate One, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One. He makes known this world with its devas, its Māras, its Brahmās, this generation with its recluses and brahmins, its rulers and people, having realized it by his own direct knowledge. He teaches the Dhamma which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and phrasing; he reveals a life of complete perfection and purity.’ It is good to see such Arahants."

Atha kho verañjakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā appekacce bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu, sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce yena bhagavā tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce bhagavato santike nāmagottaṃ sāvetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce tuṇhībhūtā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho verañjakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘ko nu kho, bho gotama, hetu, ko paccayo yena midhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti? Ko pana, bho gotama, hetu, ko paccayo yena midhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjantī’’ti?

Then the brahmin householders of Verañja approached the Blessed One. Having approached, some paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some exchanged greetings with the Blessed One and, after exchanging polite and amiable talk, sat down to one side; some raised their hands in reverential salutation towards the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some announced their name and clan in the Blessed One's presence and sat down to one side; some sat down to one side in silence. Sitting to one side, the brahmin householders of Verañja said to the Blessed One: "What, Master Gotama, is the cause, what is the reason, why some beings here, upon the breakup of the body after death, are reborn in a state of deprivation, in a bad destination, in the nether world, in hell? And what, Master Gotama, is the cause, what is the reason, why some beings here, upon the breakup of the body after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world?"

‘‘Adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti. Dhammacariyāsamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjantī’’ti.

Householders, it is because of unrighteous and improper conduct that some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in the plane of misery, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell. It is because of righteous and proper conduct that some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a happy destination, in the heavenly world.

‘‘Na kho mayaṃ imassa bhoto gotamassa saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāma. Sādhu no bhavaṃ gotamo tathā dhammaṃ desetu yathā mayaṃ imassa bhoto gotamassa saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, vitthārena atthaṃ ājāneyyāmā’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, gahapatayo, suṇātha sādhukaṃ manasi karotha, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ bho’’ti kho verañjakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

"We do not understand in detail the meaning of Master Gotama’s brief statement, which he has not analyzed in detail. It would be good if Master Gotama would teach us the Dhamma in such a way that we might understand in detail the meaning of his brief statement, which he has not analyzed in detail." "Then, householders, listen and pay close attention, I will speak." "Yes, sir," replied the brahmin householders of Veranja to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said:

445. ‘‘Tividhaṃ kho, gahapatayo, kāyena adhammacārī visamacārī hoti, catubbidhaṃ vācāya adhammacārī visamacārī hoti, tividhaṃ manasā adhammacārī visamacārī hoti.

445."Householders, there are three ways one behaves unrighteously and improperly with the body, four ways with speech, and three ways with the mind."

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena adhammacārī visamacārī hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco pāṇātipātī hoti. Luddo lohitapāṇi hatappahate niviṭṭho adayāpanno pāṇabhūtesu. Adinnādāyī kho pana hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ… taṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti. Kāmesumicchācārī kho pana hoti. Yā tā māturakkhitā… tathārūpāsu cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena adhammacārī visamacārī hoti.

"And how, householders, does one behave unrighteously and improperly with the body in three ways? Here, householders, someone is a killer of living beings—violent, bloody-handed, given to striking and killing, merciless to living beings. Furthermore, he is one who takes what is not given. He takes as theft what belongs to others, the wealth and possessions of others, that is not given. Furthermore, he engages in sexual misconduct—he has intercourse with those protected by their mothers, fathers, brothers, sisters, relatives, or the law, or with those already betrothed. Thus, householders, one behaves unrighteously and improperly with the body in three ways."

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya adhammacārī visamacārī hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco musāvādī hoti. Sabhāgato vā… sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti. Pisuṇavāco kho pana hoti. Ito sutvā amutra akkhātā… vaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti. Pharusavāco kho pana hoti. Yā sā vācā aṇḍakā kakkasā… tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti. Samphappalāpī kho pana hoti. Akālavādī… apariyantavatiṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya adhammacārī visamacārī hoti.

"And how, householders, does one behave unrighteously and improperly in speech in four ways? Here, householders, someone is a liar. When summoned to a council, or an assembly, or to his relatives, questioned as a witness, he says, ‘I know,’ when he doesn’t know, or ‘I don’t know,’ when he knows, or ‘I saw,’ when he didn’t see, or ‘I didn’t see,’ when he saw—thus consciously speaking a lie for his own sake, for another’s sake, or for some trifling gain. Moreover, he is a divisive speaker. Having heard something here, he repeats it there to divide people, or having heard something there, he repeats it here to divide people. Thus he is one who divides those who are united and incites further division in those who are already divided. He delights in division, enjoys division, and speaks words that cause division. Moreover, he is a harsh speaker. He utters such words as are rough, hard, hurtful to others, offensive, bordering on anger, not conducive to samādhi. Moreover, he is a gossip. He speaks out of season, speaks what is untrue, speaks what is useless, speaks what is contrary to the Dhamma and the Discipline, speaks words that are worthless, not accompanied by reason, unbounded. Thus, householders, one behaves unrighteously and improperly in speech in four ways."

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā adhammacārī visamacārī hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco abhijjhālu hoti…pe… taṃ mamassā’ti. Byāpannacitto kho pana hoti paduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ime sattā haññantu vā… mā vā ahesu’nti. Micchādiṭṭhiko kho pana hoti viparītadassano – ‘natthi dinnaṃ, natthi yiṭṭhaṃ… sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā adhammacārī visamacārī hoti.

"And how, householders, does one behave unrighteously and improperly mentally in three ways? Here, householders, someone is covetous—he covets the wealth and possessions of others, thinking, 'Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!' He is of a mind of ill will—his thoughts are corrupted by malice: 'Let these beings be killed, slaughtered, destroyed, or annihilated!' He is of wrong view—his perception is distorted: 'There is no gift, no sacrifice, no offering; there is no fruit or result of good or bad deeds; there is no this world, no other world; there is no mother, no father; no beings who are reborn spontaneously; there are in the world no ascetics or brahmins who have attained right view and proclaim this world and the next, having realized it by themselves!' Thus, householders, one behaves unrighteously and improperly mentally in three ways."

‘‘Evaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti.

"Indeed, householders, due to such unrighteous and improper conduct, some beings here, upon the breakup of the body after death, are reborn in a state of misery, in a bad destination, in the lower realms, in hell."

446. ‘‘Tividhaṃ kho, gahapatayo, kāyena dhammacārī samacārī hoti, catubbidhaṃ vācāya dhammacārī samacārī hoti, tividhaṃ manasā dhammacārī samacārī hoti.

446."Householders, there are these three ways of behaving righteously and properly with the body, four with speech, and three with the mind."

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena dhammacārī samacārī hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco pāṇātipātaṃ pahāya pāṇātipātā paṭivirato hoti, nihitadaṇḍo nihitasattho lajjī dayāpanno sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharati. Adinnādānaṃ pahāya adinnādānā paṭivirato hoti, yaṃ taṃ parassa… taṃ nādinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti. Kāmesumicchācāraṃ pahāya… tathārūpāsu na cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena dhammacārī samacārī hoti.

“And how, householders, does one live righteously and evenly in three ways by body? Here, householders, a certain person, having abandoned the taking of life, abstains from taking life, laying aside rod and weapon, conscientious, merciful, he dwells compassionate for the welfare of all living beings. Having abandoned taking what is not given, he abstains from taking what is not given; he does not take by way of theft what belongs to another and has not been given. Having abandoned sexual misconduct, he abstains from sexual misconduct; he does not engage in such conduct with those who are protected. Thus, householders, one lives righteously and evenly in three ways by body.”

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya dhammacārī samacārī hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco musāvādaṃ pahāya musāvādā paṭivirato hoti. Sabhāgato vā…pe… na sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti. Pisuṇaṃ vācaṃ pahāya… samaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti. Pharusaṃ vācaṃ pahāya… tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti. Samphappalāpaṃ pahāya… kālena sāpadesaṃ pariyantavatiṃ atthasaṃhitaṃ. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya dhammacārī samacārī hoti.

“And how, householders, does one live righteously and evenly in four ways by speech? Here, householders, a certain person, having abandoned false speech, abstains from false speech; he speaks the truth, adheres to the truth, is trustworthy and reliable, and does not deceive the world. Having abandoned divisive speech, he abstains from divisive speech; he is a reconciler of those who are divided and a promoter of unity among those who are united; he delights in concord, rejoices in concord, is fond of concord, and speaks words that create concord. Having abandoned harsh speech, he abstains from harsh speech; he speaks words that are gentle, pleasing to the ear, agreeable, going to the heart, courteous, appealing to the many, and welcome to the many. Having abandoned idle chatter, he abstains from idle chatter; he speaks at the right time, what is factual, purposeful, connected with the Dhamma, and connected with the discipline; he speaks words that are worth treasuring, timely, reasonable, well-defined, and beneficial. Thus, householders, one lives righteously and evenly in four ways by speech.”

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā dhammacārī samacārī hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco anabhijjhālu hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ taṃ nābhijjhātā hoti ‘aho vata yaṃ parassa, taṃ mamassā’ti. Abyāpannacitto kho pana hoti appaduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ‘ime sattā averā abyābajjhā anīghā sukhī attānaṃ pariharantū’ti. Sammādiṭṭhiko kho pana hoti aviparītadassano – ‘atthi dinnaṃ, atthi yiṭṭhaṃ… sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā dhammacārī samacārī hoti.

“And how, householders, does one live righteously and evenly in three ways by mind? Here, householders, a certain person is uncovetous; he does not covet the wealth and possessions of others, thinking, ‘Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!’ He is of uncorrupted mind, without hateful thoughts: ‘May these beings be free from enmity, free from affliction, free from trouble, and may they maintain happiness!’ He is of right view, having unperverted vision: ‘There is what is given, what is offered, what is sacrificed; there is fruit and result of good and bad actions; there is this world and the other world; there is mother and father; there are spontaneously arisen beings; there are in the world recluses and brahmins who are rightly practising, who have rightly entered the way, who proclaim this world and the other world, having realised them for themselves with direct knowledge.’ Thus, householders, one lives righteously and evenly in three ways by mind.”

‘‘Evaṃ dhammacariyāsamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti.

“Householders, it is due to such righteous and even conduct that some beings here, with the breakup of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world.”

447. ‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā khattiyamahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā khattiyamahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

447.“If, householders, one who lives righteously and evenly should wish: ‘Oh, that with the breakup of the body, after death, I might be reborn into the company of wealthy khattiyas!’—it is indeed possible that this may happen. Why is that? Because he lives righteously and evenly.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā brāhmaṇamahāsālānaṃ gahapatimahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā gahapatimahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

“If, householders, one who lives righteously and evenly should wish: ‘Oh, that with the breakup of the body, after death, I might be reborn into the company of wealthy brahmins or wealthy householders!’—it is indeed possible that this may happen. Why is that? Because he lives righteously and evenly.”

‘‘Ākaṅkeyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā cātumahārājikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā cātumahārājikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

“If, householders, one who lives righteously and evenly should wish: ‘Oh, that with the breakup of the body, after death, I might be reborn into the company of the devas of the Four Great Kings!’—it is indeed possible that this may happen. Why is that? Because he lives righteously and evenly.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā tāvatiṃsānaṃ devānaṃ… yāmānaṃ devānaṃ… tusitānaṃ devānaṃ… nimmānaratīnaṃ devānaṃ… paranimmitavasavattīnaṃ devānaṃ… brahmakāyikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā brahmakāyikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

“If, householders, one who lives righteously and evenly should wish: ‘Oh, that with the breakup of the body, after death, I might be reborn into the company of the Tāvatiṃsa devas... the Yāma devas... the Tusita devas... the Nimmānaratī devas... the Paranimmitavasavattī devas... the Brahmakāyika devas!’—it is indeed possible that this may happen. Why is that? Because he lives righteously and evenly.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā ābhānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā ābhānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

“If, householders, one who lives righteously and evenly should wish: ‘Oh, that with the breakup of the body, after death, I might be reborn into the company of the Ābhā devas!’—it is indeed possible that this may happen. Why is that? Because he lives righteously and evenly.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā parittābhānaṃ devānaṃ…pe… appamāṇābhānaṃ devānaṃ… ābhassarānaṃ devānaṃ… parittasubhānaṃ devānaṃ… appamāṇasubhānaṃ devānaṃ… subhakiṇhānaṃ devānaṃ … vehapphalānaṃ devānaṃ… avihānaṃ devānaṃ… atappānaṃ devānaṃ… sudassānaṃ devānaṃ… sudassīnaṃ devānaṃ… akaniṭṭhānaṃ devānaṃ… ākāsānañcāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ… viññāṇañcāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ… ākiñcaññāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ… nevasaññānāsaññāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā nevasaññānāsaññāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

“If, householders, one who lives righteously and evenly should wish: ‘Oh, that with the breakup of the body, after death, I might be reborn into the company of the devas of limited radiance... the devas of boundless radiance... the devas of streaming radiance... the devas of limited glory... the devas of boundless glory... the devas of refulgent glory... the devas of great fruit... the devas of Aviha... the devas of Atappa... the devas of Sudassa... the devas of Sudassi... the devas of Akaniṭṭha... the devas of the realm of the base of infinite space... the devas of the realm of the base of infinite consciousness... the devas of the realm of the base of nothingness... the devas of the realm of the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception!’—it is indeed possible that with the breakup of the body, after death, he might be reborn into the company of the devas of the realm of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. Why is that? Because he lives righteously and evenly.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce gahapatayo dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, ‘yaṃ so āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī’’’ti.

“If, householders, one who lives righteously and evenly should wish: ‘Oh, that by the destruction of the taints I might, in this very life, realize for myself with direct knowledge the taintless liberation of mind and liberation by wisdom, and having attained it, abide therein!’—it is indeed possible that this may happen. Why is that? Because he lives righteously and evenly.”

448. Evaṃ vutte, verañjakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ bho gotama! Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya, cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantīti; evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Ete mayaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāma dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsake no bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupete saraṇaṃ gate’’ti.

448.When this was said, the Verañja brahmins and householders said to the Blessed One: “Magnificent, Master Gotama, magnificent! Just as, Master Gotama, one might set upright what had been overturned, or reveal what was hidden, or show the way to one who was lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness so that those with eyes might see forms—in the same way, the Dhamma has been made clear by Master Gotama in many ways. We go to Master Gotama for refuge, and to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of bhikkhus. May Master Gotama accept us as lay followers who have gone for refuge from this day forth, for as long as life lasts.”

Verañjakasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The Verañjaka Sutta, the second, is concluded.
