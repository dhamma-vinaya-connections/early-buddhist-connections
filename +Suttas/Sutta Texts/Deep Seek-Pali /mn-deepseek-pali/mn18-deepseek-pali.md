# 18. Madhupiṇḍikasuttaṃ

## 18. The Honeyball Sutta

199. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya kapilavatthuṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Kapilavatthusmiṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto yena mahāvanaṃ tenupasaṅkami divāvihārāya. Mahāvanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā beluvalaṭṭhikāya mūle divāvihāraṃ nisīdi. Daṇḍapāṇipi kho sakko jaṅghāvihāraṃ [jaṅghavihāraṃ (ka.)] anucaṅkamamāno anuvicaramāno yena mahāvanaṃ tenupasaṅkami. Mahāvanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā yena beluvalaṭṭhikā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā daṇḍamolubbha ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho daṇḍapāṇi sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃvādī samaṇo kimakkhāyī’’ti? ‘‘Yathāvādī kho, āvuso, sadevake loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya na kenaci loke viggayha tiṭṭhati, yathā ca pana kāmehi visaṃyuttaṃ viharantaṃ taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ akathaṃkathiṃ chinnakukkuccaṃ bhavābhave vītataṇhaṃ saññā nānusenti – evaṃvādī kho ahaṃ, āvuso, evamakkhāyī’’ti.

199.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu, in Nigrodha's Park. Then, in the morning, the Blessed One dressed, took his bowl and robe, and entered Kapilavatthu for alms. Having wandered for alms in Kapilavatthu, after the meal, on returning from his alms round, he went to the Great Wood for his day's abiding. Having entered the Great Wood, he sat down at the foot of a beluva sapling for his day's abiding. Now, Daṇḍapāṇi the Sakyan, while walking and wandering for exercise, approached the Great Wood. Having entered the Great Wood, he went to the beluva sapling where the Blessed One was. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. When this courteous and amiable talk was concluded, leaning on his staff, he stood to one side. Standing to one side, Daṇḍapāṇi the Sakyan said to the Blessed One: "What doctrine does the ascetic profess? What does he proclaim?" "Friend, I profess and proclaim in such a way that I do not quarrel with anyone in the world—with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, with its population of ascetics and brahmins, devas and humans. And perceptions do not attend upon that brahmin who lives disjoined from sensual pleasures, free of doubt, rid of perplexity, devoid of craving for being and non-being. Thus, friend, I speak; thus I proclaim."

‘‘Evaṃ vutte daṇḍapāṇi sakko sīsaṃ okampetvā, jivhaṃ nillāḷetvā, tivisākhaṃ nalāṭikaṃ nalāṭe vuṭṭhāpetvā daṇḍamolubbha pakkāmi.

When this was said, Daṇḍapāṇi the Sakyan shook his head, stuck out his tongue, and drew up three wrinkles upon his forehead. Then, leaning on his staff, he departed.

200. Atha kho bhagavā sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yena nigrodhārāmo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘idhāhaṃ, bhikkhave, pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya kapilavatthuṃ piṇḍāya pāvisiṃ. Kapilavatthusmiṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto yena mahāvanaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃ divāvihārāya. Mahāvanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā beluvalaṭṭhikāya mūle divāvihāraṃ nisīdiṃ. Daṇḍapāṇipi kho, bhikkhave, sakko jaṅghāvihāraṃ anucaṅkamamāno anuvicaramāno yena mahāvanaṃ tenupasaṅkami. Mahāvanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā yena beluvalaṭṭhikā yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā mayā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā daṇḍamolubbha ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho, bhikkhave, daṇḍapāṇi sakko maṃ etadavoca – ‘kiṃvādī samaṇo kimakkhāyī’ti?

200.Then, in the evening, the Blessed One rose from his seclusion and went to Nigrodha's Park. Having arrived, he sat down on the prepared seat. Having sat down, the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "Here, bhikkhus, in the morning, having dressed and taken bowl and robe, I entered Kapilavatthu for alms. Having wandered for alms in Kapilavatthu, after the meal, on returning from my alms round, I went to the Great Wood for my day's abiding. Having entered the Great Wood, I sat down at the foot of a beluva sapling for my day's abiding. Now, bhikkhus, Daṇḍapāṇi the Sakyan, while walking and wandering for exercise, approached the Great Wood. Having entered the Great Wood, he came to the beluva sapling where I was. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with me. When this courteous and amiable talk was concluded, leaning on his staff, he stood to one side. Standing to one side, bhikkhus, Daṇḍapāṇi the Sakyan said to me: 'What doctrine does the ascetic profess? What does he proclaim?'"

‘‘Evaṃ vutte ahaṃ, bhikkhave, daṇḍapāṇiṃ sakkaṃ etadavocaṃ – yathāvādī kho, āvuso, sadevake loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya na kenaci loke viggayha tiṭṭhati, yathā ca pana kāmehi visaṃyuttaṃ viharantaṃ taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ akathaṃkathiṃ chinnakukkuccaṃ bhavābhave vītataṇhaṃ saññā nānusenti – evaṃvādī kho ahaṃ, āvuso, evamakkhāyī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte bhikkhave, daṇḍapāṇi sakko sīsaṃ okampetvā, jivhaṃ nillāḷetvā, tivisākhaṃ nalāṭikaṃ nalāṭe vuṭṭhāpetvā daṇḍamolubbha pakkāmī’’ti.

"When this was said, bhikkhus, I said to Daṇḍapāṇi the Sakyan: 'Friend, I profess and proclaim in such a way that I do not quarrel with anyone in the world—with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, with its population of ascetics and brahmins, devas and humans. And perceptions do not attend upon that brahmin who lives disjoined from sensual pleasures, free of doubt, rid of perplexity, devoid of craving for being and non-being. Thus, friend, I speak; thus I proclaim.'""When this was said, bhikkhus, Daṇḍapāṇi the Sakyan shook his head, stuck out his tongue, and drew up three wrinkles upon his forehead. Then, leaning on his staff, he departed."

201. Evaṃ vutte aññataro bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃvādī pana, bhante, bhagavā sadevake loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya na kenaci loke viggayha tiṭṭhati? Kathañca pana, bhante, bhagavantaṃ kāmehi visaṃyuttaṃ viharantaṃ taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ akathaṃkathiṃ chinnakukkuccaṃ bhavābhave vītataṇhaṃ saññā nānusentī’’ti? ‘‘Yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti. Ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ. Esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ, esevanto paṭighānusayānaṃ, esevanto diṭṭhānusayānaṃ, esevanto vicikicchānusayānaṃ, esevanto mānānusayānaṃ, esevanto bhavarāgānusayānaṃ, esevanto avijjānusayānaṃ, esevanto daṇḍādāna-satthādāna-kalaha-viggaha-vivāda-tuvaṃtuvaṃ-pesuñña-musāvādānaṃ. Etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’ti. Idamavoca bhagavā. Idaṃ vatvāna sugato uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ pāvisi.

201."When this was said, a certain bhikkhu addressed the Blessed One: "But what, Venerable Sir, does the Blessed One say, that he stands not quarreling with anyone in the world—with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, with its population of ascetics and brahmins, devas and humans? And how, Venerable Sir, do perceptions not attend upon the Blessed One, that brahmin who dwells disjoined from sensual pleasures, free of doubt, rid of perplexity, devoid of craving for being and non-being?" "Bhikkhu, from whatever source perceptions and notions of proliferation come to assail a person, if there is nothing there to be delighted in, spoken of, or clung to—that is the end of the underlying tendency to lust, the end of the underlying tendency to aversion, the end of the underlying tendency to views, the end of the underlying tendency to doubt, the end of the underlying tendency to conceit, the end of the underlying tendency to craving for existence, the end of the underlying tendency to ignorance. That is the end of taking up sticks and weapons, of quarrels, disputes, contention, mutual recrimination, slander, and false speech. Here these evil unwholesome states cease without remainder." This the Blessed One said. Having said this, the Sugata rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.

202. Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā, uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti. Ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ. Esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ…pe… etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’ti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’’ti? Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā mahākaccāno satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’’ti.

202.Then, soon after the Blessed One had departed, this thought occurred to those bhikkhus: "Friends, the Blessed One has given us this brief exposition, without elaborating on its meaning in detail, and has risen from his seat and entered his dwelling, saying: ‘Bhikkhu, from whatever source perceptions and notions of proliferation come to assail a person, if there is nothing there to be delighted in, spoken of, or clung to—that is the end of the underlying tendency to lust... (etc.)... Here these evil unwholesome states cease without remainder.’ Now, who could explain in detail the meaning of this brief exposition given by the Blessed One, the meaning of which has not been elaborated?" Then this thought occurred to those bhikkhus: "This Venerable Mahākaccāna is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his discerning fellow bhikkhus. Venerable Mahākaccāna is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief exposition given by the Blessed One, the meaning of which has not been elaborated. Suppose we approached Venerable Mahākaccāna and asked him about this matter."

Atha kho te bhikkhū yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā mahākaccānena saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso kaccāna, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti. Ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ. Esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ…pe… etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’ti. Tesaṃ no, āvuso kaccāna, amhākaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘‘yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti. Ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ. Esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ…pe… etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’’ti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyāti? Tesaṃ no, āvuso kaccāna, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā mahākaccāno satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ, pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’ti. Vibhajatāyasmā mahākaccāno’’ti.

Then those bhikkhus approached Venerable Mahākaccāna. Having approached, they exchanged greetings with Venerable Mahākaccāna. When this courteous and amiable talk was concluded, they sat down to one side. Having sat down to one side, those bhikkhus said to Venerable Mahākaccāna: "Friend Kaccāna, the Blessed One has given us this brief exposition, without elaborating on its meaning in detail, and has risen from his seat and entered his dwelling, saying: ‘Bhikkhu, from whatever source perceptions and notions of proliferation come to assail a person, if there is nothing there to be delighted in, spoken of, or clung to—that is the end of the underlying tendency to lust... (etc.)... Here these evil unwholesome states cease without remainder.’ Soon after the Blessed One had departed, this thought occurred to us: ‘Friends, the Blessed One has given us this brief exposition, without elaborating on its meaning in detail, and has risen from his seat and entered his dwelling. Now, who could explain in detail the meaning of this brief exposition given by the Blessed One, the meaning of which has not been elaborated?’ Then this thought occurred to us: ‘This Venerable Mahākaccāna is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his discerning fellow bhikkhus. Venerable Mahākaccāna is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief exposition given by the Blessed One, the meaning of which has not been elaborated. Suppose we approached Venerable Mahākaccāna and asked him about this matter.’ May Venerable Mahākaccāna explain it to us."

203. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, āvuso, puriso sāratthiko sāragavesī sārapariyesanaṃ caramāno mahato rukkhassa tiṭṭhato sāravato atikkammeva mūlaṃ, atikkamma khandhaṃ, sākhāpalāse sāraṃ pariyesitabbaṃ maññeyya; evaṃsampadamidaṃ āyasmantānaṃ satthari sammukhībhūte, taṃ bhagavantaṃ atisitvā, amhe etamatthaṃ paṭipucchitabbaṃ maññatha. So hāvuso, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti, passaṃ passati, cakkhubhūto ñāṇabhūto dhammabhūto brahmabhūto, vattā pavattā, atthassa ninnetā, amatassa dātā, dhammassāmī tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi, yaṃ bhagavantaṃyeva etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha. Yathā vo bhagavā byākareyya tathā naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti. ‘‘Addhāvuso kaccāna, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti, passaṃ passati, cakkhubhūto ñāṇabhūto dhammabhūto brahmabhūto, vattā pavattā, atthassa ninnetā, amatassa dātā, dhammassāmī tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi, yaṃ bhagavantaṃyeva etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāma. Yathā no bhagavā byākareyya tathā naṃ dhāreyyāma. Api cāyasmā mahākaccāno satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ, pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Vibhajatāyasmā mahākaccāno agaruṃ katvā’’ti [agarukatvā (sī.), agarukaritvā (syā. pī.)]. ‘‘Tena hāvuso, suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasikarotha, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato mahākaccānassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā mahākaccāno etadavoca –

203.Just as, friends, a man in need of heartwood, seeking heartwood, wandering in search of heartwood, would pass over the root and trunk of a great standing tree full of heartwood, thinking to look for heartwood among the branches and leaves—such is this case with you venerable ones, who, having the Teacher present before you, pass over that Blessed One and think to ask me about this matter. For, friends, the Blessed One, knowing, knows; seeing, sees; he is vision, he is knowledge, he is the Dhamma, he is the Brahma; he is the speaker, the proclaimer, the elucidator of meaning, the giver of the Deathless, the lord of the Dhamma, the Tathagata. That was the time when you should have asked the Blessed One about this matter. Whatever the Blessed One would have answered, thus you should have remembered it." "Surely, friend Kaccāna, the Blessed One, knowing, knows; seeing, sees; he is vision, he is knowledge, he is the Dhamma, he is the Brahma; he is the speaker, the proclaimer, the elucidator of meaning, the giver of the Deathless, the lord of the Dhamma, the Tathagata. That was the time when we should have asked the Blessed One about this matter. Whatever the Blessed One would have answered, thus we should have remembered it. Yet the Venerable Mahākaccāna is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise fellow bhikkhus. The Venerable Mahākaccāna is capable of analyzing in detail the meaning of this brief summary given by the Blessed One, which has not been elaborated. Let the Venerable Mahākaccāna analyze it without hesitation." "Then, friends, listen and attend carefully. I will speak." "Yes, friend," those bhikkhus replied to the Venerable Mahākaccāna. The Venerable Mahākaccāna said this:

204. ‘‘Yaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti. Ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ, esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ…pe… etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’ti, imassa kho ahaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi –

204.Friends, the Blessed One, having given this brief summary without analyzing the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: 'Dependent on what, bhikkhus, do perceptions and notions of proliferation assail a man? If there is nothing to be delighted in, welcomed, or clung to, that is the end of the underlying tendency to lust…here those evil, unwholesome states cease without remainder.' Friends, I understand the detailed meaning of this brief summary given by the Blessed One in this way:

‘‘Cakkhuñcāvuso, paṭicca rūpe ca uppajjati cakkhuviññāṇaṃ, tiṇṇaṃ saṅgati phasso, phassapaccayā vedanā, yaṃ vedeti taṃ sañjānāti, yaṃ sañjānāti taṃ vitakketi, yaṃ vitakketi taṃ papañceti, yaṃ papañceti tatonidānaṃ purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti atītānāgatapaccuppannesu cakkhuviññeyyesu rūpesu. Sotañcāvuso, paṭicca sadde ca uppajjati sotaviññāṇaṃ…pe… ghānañcāvuso, paṭicca gandhe ca uppajjati ghānaviññāṇaṃ…pe… jivhañcāvuso, paṭicca rase ca uppajjati jivhāviññāṇaṃ…pe… kāyañcāvuso, paṭicca phoṭṭhabbe ca uppajjati kāyaviññāṇaṃ…pe… manañcāvuso, paṭicca dhamme ca uppajjati manoviññāṇaṃ, tiṇṇaṃ saṅgati phasso, phassapaccayā vedanā, yaṃ vedeti taṃ sañjānāti, yaṃ sañjānāti taṃ vitakketi, yaṃ vitakketi taṃ papañceti, yaṃ papañceti tatonidānaṃ purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti atītānāgatapaccuppannesu manoviññeyyesu dhammesu.

Friend, dependent on the eye and forms, eye-consciousness arises. The meeting of the three is contact. From contact as condition, feeling arises. What one feels, one perceives. What one perceives, one thinks about. What one thinks about, one proliferates. What one proliferates, from that as a source, perceptions and notions of proliferation assail a person in regard to past, future, and present forms cognizable by the eye. Friend, dependent on the ear and sounds, ear-consciousness arises… Friend, dependent on the nose and smells, nose-consciousness arises… Friend, dependent on the tongue and tastes, tongue-consciousness arises… Friend, dependent on the body and tactile objects, body-consciousness arises… Friend, dependent on the mind and mental phenomena, mind-consciousness arises. The meeting of the three is contact. From contact as condition, feeling arises. What one feels, one perceives. What one perceives, one thinks about. What one thinks about, one proliferates. What one proliferates, from that as a source, perceptions and notions of proliferation assail a person in regard to past, future, and present mental phenomena cognizable by the mind.

‘‘So vatāvuso, cakkhusmiṃ sati rūpe sati cakkhuviññāṇe sati phassapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Phassapaññattiyā sati vedanāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Vedanāpaññattiyā sati saññāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Saññāpaññattiyā sati vitakkapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Vitakkapaññattiyā sati papañcasaññāsaṅkhāsamudācaraṇapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. So vatāvuso, sotasmiṃ sati sadde sati…pe… ghānasmiṃ sati gandhe sati…pe… jivhāya sati rase sati…pe… kāyasmiṃ sati phoṭṭhabbe sati…pe… manasmiṃ sati dhamme sati manoviññāṇe sati phassapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Phassapaññattiyā sati vedanāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Vedanāpaññattiyā sati saññāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Saññāpaññattiyā sati vitakkapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Vitakkapaññattiyā sati papañcasaññāsaṅkhāsamudācaraṇapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati.

Indeed, friend, when there is the eye, when there are forms, and when there is eye-consciousness, it is possible to designate the concept of contact. When there is the concept of contact, it is possible to designate the concept of feeling. When there is the concept of feeling, it is possible to designate the concept of perception. When there is the concept of perception, it is possible to designate the concept of thought. When there is the concept of thought, it is possible to designate the concept of the arising of perceptions and notions of proliferation. Indeed, friend, when there is the ear, when there are sounds… when there is the nose, when there are smells… when there is the tongue, when there are tastes… when there is the body, when there are tactile sensations… when there is the mind, when there are mental phenomena, and when there is mind-consciousness, it is possible to designate the concept of contact. When there is the concept of contact, it is possible to designate the concept of feeling. When there is the concept of feeling, it is possible to designate the concept of perception. When there is the concept of perception, it is possible to designate the concept of thought. When there is the concept of thought, it is possible to designate the concept of the arising of perceptions and notions of proliferation.

‘‘So vatāvuso, cakkhusmiṃ asati rūpe asati cakkhuviññāṇe asati phassapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Phassapaññattiyā asati vedanāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Vedanāpaññattiyā asati saññāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Saññāpaññattiyā asati vitakkapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Vitakkapaññattiyā asati papañcasaññāsaṅkhāsamudācaraṇapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. So vatāvuso, sotasmiṃ asati sadde asati…pe… ghānasmiṃ asati gandhe asati…pe… jivhāya asati rase asati…pe… kāyasmiṃ asati phoṭṭhabbe asati…pe… manasmiṃ asati dhamme asati manoviññāṇe asati phassapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Phassapaññattiyā asati vedanāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Vedanāpaññattiyā asati saññāpaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Saññāpaññattiyā asati vitakkapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Vitakkapaññattiyā asati papañcasaññāsaṅkhāsamudācaraṇapaññattiṃ paññāpessatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

Indeed, friend, when there is no eye, no forms, no eye-consciousness, it is not possible to designate the concept of contact. When there is no concept of contact, it is not possible to designate the concept of feeling. When there is no concept of feeling, it is not possible to designate the concept of perception. When there is no concept of perception, it is not possible to designate the concept of thought. When there is no concept of thought, it is not possible to designate the concept of the arising of perceptions and notions of proliferation. Indeed, friend, when there is no ear, no sounds, no ear-consciousness… When there is no nose, no smells, no nose-consciousness… When there is no tongue, no tastes, no tongue-consciousness… When there is no body, no tactile objects, no body-consciousness… When there is no mind, no mental phenomena, no mind-consciousness, it is not possible to designate the concept of contact. When there is no concept of contact, it is not possible to designate the concept of feeling. When there is no concept of feeling, it is not possible to designate the concept of perception. When there is no concept of perception, it is not possible to designate the concept of thought. When there is no concept of thought, it is not possible to designate the concept of the arising of perceptions and notions of proliferation.

‘‘Yaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ…pe… etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’ti, imassa kho ahaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. Ākaṅkhamānā ca pana tumhe āyasmanto bhagavantaṃyeva upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha. Yathā no bhagavā byākaroti tathā naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti.

Friends, the Blessed One, having given this brief summary without analyzing the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: ‘bhikkhus, dependent on what do perceptions and notions of proliferation assail a person? If there is nothing to be delighted in, welcomed, or clung to, that is the end of the underlying tendency to lust…here those evil, unwholesome states cease without remainder.’ Friends, this is how I understand in detail the meaning of this brief summary given by the Blessed One, without it being elaborated. If you wish, venerable sirs, you may approach the Blessed One himself and ask him about this matter. You should remember it as the Blessed One explains it to you.

205. Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmato mahākaccānassa bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘yaṃ kho no, bhante, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti. Ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ. Esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ…pe… etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’ti. Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘‘yatonidānaṃ, bhikkhu, purisaṃ papañcasaññāsaṅkhā samudācaranti. Ettha ce natthi abhinanditabbaṃ abhivaditabbaṃ ajjhositabbaṃ. Esevanto rāgānusayānaṃ, esevanto paṭighānusayānaṃ, esevanto diṭṭhānusayānaṃ, esevanto vicikicchānusayānaṃ, esevanto mānānusayānaṃ, esevanto bhavarāgānusayānaṃ, esevanto avijjānusayānaṃ, esevanto daṇḍādāna-satthādāna-kalaha-viggaha-vivādatuvaṃtuvaṃ-pesuñña-musāvādānaṃ. Etthete pāpakā akusalā dhammā aparisesā nirujjhantī’’ti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’ti? Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā mahākaccāno satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ, pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ, yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’ti. Atha kho mayaṃ, bhante, yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkamimha; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipucchimha. Tesaṃ no, bhante, āyasmatā mahākaccānena imehi ākārehi imehi padehi imehi byañjanehi attho vibhatto’’ti. ‘‘Paṇḍito, bhikkhave, mahākaccāno; mahāpañño, bhikkhave, mahākaccāno. Maṃ cepi tumhe, bhikkhave, etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha, ahampi taṃ evamevaṃ byākareyyaṃ yathā taṃ mahākaccānena byākataṃ. Eso cevetassa attho. Evañca [evemeva ca (ka.)] naṃ dhārethā’’ti.

205.Then those bhikkhus, having rejoiced in and approved of Venerable Mahākaccāna’s words, rose from their seats and went to the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, those bhikkhus said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, the Blessed One gave us a brief summary without expounding the detailed meaning, and then rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: ‘bhikkhus, dependent on what do perceptions and thoughts of proliferation beset a person? If there is nothing there to delight in, welcome, or remain fixed upon, this is the end of the underlying tendency to lust… this is the end of the underlying tendency to aversion… this is the end of the underlying tendency to views… this is the end of the underlying tendency to doubt… this is the end of the underlying tendency to conceit… this is the end of the underlying tendency to craving for existence… this is the end of the underlying tendency to ignorance… this is the end of taking up sticks and weapons, of quarrels, disputes, arguments, strife, contentiousness, slander, and false speech. Here these evil unwholesome states cease without remainder.’ Venerable sir, soon after the Blessed One had left, we considered: ‘Friends, the Blessed One gave us this brief summary without expounding the detailed meaning, and then rose from his seat and entered his dwelling… Who could explain the detailed meaning of this brief summary given by the Blessed One?’ Then, venerable sir, it occurred to us: ‘This Venerable Mahākaccāna is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise fellow practitioners. He is capable of explaining the detailed meaning of this brief summary given by the Blessed One. Let us go to him and ask him about this matter.’ So, venerable sir, we approached Venerable Mahākaccāna and asked him about this matter. Venerable sir, Venerable Mahākaccāna explained the meaning to us in these ways, with these terms, with these phrases." "Mahākaccāna is wise, bhikkhus. Mahākaccāna has great wisdom. bhikkhus, if you had asked me about this matter, I too would have explained it in the same way as Mahākaccāna did. That is its meaning, and so you should remember it."

Evaṃ vutte āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘seyyathāpi, bhante, puriso jighacchādubbalyapareto madhupiṇḍikaṃ adhigaccheyya, so yato yato sāyeyya, labhetheva sādurasaṃ asecanakaṃ. Evameva kho, bhante, cetaso bhikkhu dabbajātiko, yato yato imassa dhammapariyāyassa paññāya atthaṃ upaparikkheyya, labhetheva attamanataṃ, labhetheva cetaso pasādaṃ. Ko nāmo ayaṃ [ko nāmāyaṃ (syā.)], bhante, dhammapariyāyo’’ti? ‘‘Tasmātiha tvaṃ, ānanda, imaṃ dhammapariyāyaṃ madhupiṇḍikapariyāyo tveva naṃ dhārehī’’ti.

When this was said, Venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One: "Just as, Bhante, a man overcome by hunger and weakness might obtain a lump of honey, and wherever he would taste it, he would find a sweet and delightful flavor, free from any unpleasantness. In the same way, Bhante, a monk who is wise by nature, wherever he examines the meaning of this exposition of the Dhamma with wisdom, he would obtain gladness, he would obtain serenity of mind. What, Bhante, is the name of this exposition of the Dhamma?" "Therefore, Ānanda, you should remember this exposition of the Dhamma as the 'Honeycomb Discourse.'"

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā ānando bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

Thus spoke the Blessed One. The Venerable Ānanda, delighted, rejoiced at the Blessed One's words.

Madhupiṇḍikasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The Honey-lump Discourse is finished, the eighth.
