# 76. Sandakasuttaṃ

## 76. The Sandaka Sutta

223. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sandako paribbājako pilakkhaguhāyaṃ paṭivasati mahatiyā paribbājakaparisāya saddhiṃ pañcamattehi paribbājakasatehi. Atha kho āyasmā ānando sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘āyāmāvuso, yena devakatasobbho tenupasaṅkamissāma guhādassanāyā’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa paccassosuṃ. Atha kho āyasmā ānando sambahulehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ yena devakatasobbho tenupasaṅkami. Tena kho pana samayena sandako paribbājako mahatiyā paribbājakaparisāya saddhiṃ nisinno hoti unnādiniyā uccāsaddamahāsaddāya anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathentiyā, seyyathidaṃ – rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ mahāmattakathaṃ senākathaṃ bhayakathaṃ yuddhakathaṃ annakathaṃ pānakathaṃ vatthakathaṃ sayanakathaṃ mālākathaṃ gandhakathaṃ ñātikathaṃ yānakathaṃ gāmakathaṃ nigamakathaṃ nagarakathaṃ janapadakathaṃ itthikathaṃ sūrakathaṃ visikhākathaṃ kumbhaṭṭhānakathaṃ pubbapetakathaṃ nānattakathaṃ lokakkhāyikaṃ samuddakkhāyikaṃ itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vā. Addasā kho sandako paribbājako āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna sakaṃ parisaṃ saṇṭhāpesi – ‘‘appasaddā bhonto hontu, mā bhonto saddamakattha; ayaṃ samaṇassa gotamassa sāvako āgacchati samaṇo ānando. Yāvatā kho pana samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakā kosambiyaṃ paṭivasanti, ayaṃ tesaṃ aññataro samaṇo ānando. Appasaddakāmā kho pana te āyasmanto appasaddavinītā appasaddassa vaṇṇavādino; appeva nāma appasaddaṃ parisaṃ viditvā upasaṅkamitabbaṃ maññeyyā’’ti. Atha kho te paribbājakā tuṇhī ahesuṃ.

223.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambī, in Ghosita’s Park. Now at that time the wanderer Sandaka was staying in the Pilakkha cave together with a large assembly of wanderers, about five hundred in number. Then in the evening, the Venerable Ānanda arose from seclusion and addressed the bhikkhus: "Come, friends, let us go to Devakata’s Sobha to see the cave." "Yes, friend," those bhikkhus replied to the Venerable Ānanda. Then the Venerable Ānanda, together with several bhikkhus, went to Devakata’s Sobha. At that time, the wanderer Sandaka was seated with his large assembly of wanderers, making a loud, noisy, and uproarious talk on various kinds of unprofitable talk—such as talk about kings, robbers, ministers, armies, dangers, battles, food, drink, clothes, beds, garlands, perfumes, relatives, vehicles, villages, towns, cities, countries, women, heroes, streets, wells, the departed, miscellaneous topics, talk about the world, talk about the ocean, and talk about being and non-being. The wanderer Sandaka saw the Venerable Ānanda coming from afar. Upon seeing him, he quieted his assembly: "Be quiet, sirs, do not make a sound. This ascetic Ānanda is a disciple of the ascetic Gotama, and he is coming. Among all the disciples of the ascetic Gotama dwelling in Kosambī, this ascetic Ānanda is one of them. These venerable ones love quiet, are trained in quiet, and praise quiet. Perhaps, noticing a quiet assembly, he might think it worthwhile to approach." Then those wanderers fell silent.

224. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena sandako paribbājako tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho sandako paribbājako āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘etu kho bhavaṃ ānando, svāgataṃ bhoto ānandassa. Cirassaṃ kho bhavaṃ ānando imaṃ pariyāyamakāsi yadidaṃ idhāgamanāya. Nisīdatu bhavaṃ ānando, idamāsanaṃ paññatta’’nti. Nisīdi kho āyasmā ānando paññatte āsane. Sandakopi kho paribbājako aññataraṃ nīcaṃ āsanaṃ gahetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho sandakaṃ paribbājakaṃ āyasmā ānando etadavoca – ‘‘kāyanuttha, sandaka, etarahi kathāya sannisinnā, kā ca pana vo antarākathā vippakatā’’ti? ‘‘Tiṭṭhatesā, bho ānanda, kathā yāya mayaṃ etarahi kathāya sannisinnā. Nesā bhoto ānandassa kathā dullabhā bhavissati pacchāpi savanāya. Sādhu vata bhavantaṃyeva ānandaṃ paṭibhātu sake ācariyake dhammīkathā’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, sandaka, suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ bho’’ti kho sandako paribbājako āyasmato ānandassa paccassosi. Āyasmā ānando etadavoca – ‘‘cattārome, sandaka, tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena abrahmacariyavāsā akkhātā cattāri ca anassāsikāni brahmacariyāni akkhātāni, yattha viññū puriso sasakkaṃ brahmacariyaṃ na vaseyya, vasanto ca [vasanto vā (sī. pī.) evamuparipi anārādhanapakkhe] nārādheyya ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusala’’nti. ‘‘Katame pana te, bho ānanda, tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena cattāro abrahmacariyavāsā akkhātā, yattha viññū puriso sasakkaṃ brahmacariyaṃ na vaseyya, vasanto ca nārādheyya ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusala’’nti?

224.Then the Venerable Ānanda approached the wanderer Sandaka. The wanderer Sandaka said to the Venerable Ānanda: "Come, Venerable Ānanda, welcome! It has been a long time since Venerable Ānanda took the trouble to come here. Let the Venerable Ānanda be seated; this seat is prepared." The Venerable Ānanda sat down on the prepared seat. The wanderer Sandaka also took a low seat and sat to one side. Sitting to one side, the wanderer Sandaka said to the Venerable Ānanda: "What discussion were you engaged in just now, Sandaka? And what conversation was interrupted?" "Let that discussion be, Venerable Ānanda. That talk will not be difficult for you to hear later. It would be good if Venerable Ānanda himself would speak on the Dhamma concerning his own teacher." "Then, Sandaka, listen carefully and pay close attention; I will speak." "Yes, Venerable," replied the wanderer Sandaka to the Venerable Ānanda. The Venerable Ānanda said: "Sandaka, the Blessed One, who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, has declared four kinds of non-celibate lives and four kinds of fruitless celibate lives. A wise person would not deliberately live the celibate life in these cases, and if he did, he would not attain the right path, the wholesome Dhamma." "But, Venerable Ānanda, what are those four kinds of non-celibate lives declared by the Blessed One, who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, where a wise person would not deliberately live the celibate life, and if he did, he would not attain the right path, the wholesome Dhamma?"

225. ‘‘Idha, sandaka, ekacco satthā evaṃvādī hoti evaṃdiṭṭhi – ‘natthi dinnaṃ, natthi yiṭṭhaṃ, natthi hutaṃ, natthi sukatadukkaṭānaṃ kammānaṃ phalaṃ vipāko, natthi ayaṃ loko, natthi paroloko, natthi mātā, natthi pitā, natthi sattā opapātikā, natthi loke samaṇabrāhmaṇā sammaggatā sammāpaṭipannā ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedenti. Cātumahābhūtiko ayaṃ puriso yadā kālaṅkaroti, pathavī pathavīkāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, āpo āpokāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, tejo tejokāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, vāyo vāyokāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, ākāsaṃ indriyāni saṅkamanti. Āsandipañcamā purisā mataṃ ādāya gacchanti, yāvāḷāhanā padāni paññāyanti. Kāpotakāni aṭṭhīni bhavanti. Bhassantā āhutiyo; dattupaññattaṃ yadidaṃ dānaṃ. Tesaṃ tucchā musā vilāpo ye keci atthikavādaṃ vadanti. Bāle ca paṇḍite ca kāyassa bhedā ucchijjanti vinassanti na honti paraṃ maraṇā’ti.

225."Here, Sandaka, some teacher holds such a view and says: 'There is no giving, no sacrifice, no offering; there is no fruit or result of good or bad deeds; there is no this world, no other world; there is no mother, no father; there are no spontaneously reborn beings; there are no ascetics or brahmins in the world who have attained the right path, who practice rightly, and who proclaim this world and the other world after realizing it by direct knowledge. A person is composed of the four great elements. When one dies, earth returns to the earth-body, water returns to the water-body, fire returns to the fire-body, and air returns to the air-body. The faculties pass into space. The pallbearers, five in all, carry the corpse away; footprints are discernible as far as the charnel ground. The bones become the color of pigeons. Offerings are reduced to ashes. Giving is a doctrine of fools. It is an empty, false babble to claim there is any fruit. Fools and wise alike are annihilated and perish at the breaking up of the body; they do not exist after death.'"

‘‘Tatra, sandaka, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘ayaṃ kho bhavaṃ satthā evaṃvādī evaṃdiṭṭhi – natthi dinnaṃ, natthi yiṭṭhaṃ, natthi hutaṃ, natthi sukatadukkaṭānaṃ kammānaṃ phalaṃ vipāko, natthi ayaṃ loko, natthi paroloko, natthi mātā, natthi pitā, natthi sattā opapātikā, natthi loke samaṇabrāhmaṇā sammaggatā sammāpaṭipannā ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedenti. Cātumahābhūtiko ayaṃ puriso yadā kālaṅkaroti, pathavī pathavīkāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, āpo āpokāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, tejo tejokāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, vāyo vāyokāyaṃ anupeti anupagacchati, ākāsaṃ indriyāni saṅkamanti. Āsandipañcamā purisā mataṃ ādāya gacchanti, yāvāḷāhanā padāni paññāyanti. Kāpotakāni aṭṭhīni bhavanti. Bhassantā āhutiyo; dattupaññattaṃ yadidaṃ dānaṃ. Tesaṃ tucchā musā vilāpo ye keci atthikavādaṃ vadanti. Bāle ca paṇḍite ca kāyassa bhedā ucchijjanti vinassanti na honti paraṃ maraṇā’ti. Sace imassa bhoto satthuno saccaṃ vacanaṃ, akatena me ettha kataṃ, avusitena me ettha vusitaṃ. Ubhopi mayaṃ ettha samasamā sāmaññaṃ pattā, yo cāhaṃ na vadāmi ‘ubho kāyassa bhedā ucchijjissāma, vinassissāma, na bhavissāma paraṃ maraṇā’ti. Atirekaṃ kho panimassa bhoto satthuno naggiyaṃ muṇḍiyaṃ ukkuṭikappadhānaṃ kesamassulocanaṃ yohaṃ puttasambādhasayanaṃ [puttasambādhavasanaṃ (sī.)] ajjhāvasanto kāsikacandanaṃ paccanubhonto mālāgandhavilepanaṃ dhārento jātarūparajataṃ sādiyanto iminā bhotā satthārā samasamagatiko bhavissāmi. Abhisamparāyaṃ sohaṃ kiṃ jānanto kiṃ passanto imasmiṃ satthari brahmacariyaṃ carissāmi? ‘So abrahmacariyavāso aya’nti – iti viditvā tasmā brahmacariyā nibbijja pakkamati [nibbijjāpakkamati (sī.)]. Ayaṃ kho, sandaka, tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena paṭhamo abrahmacariyavāso akkhāto yattha viññū puriso sasakkaṃ brahmacariyaṃ na vaseyya, vasanto ca nārādheyya ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusalaṃ.

"Here, Sandaka, a wise person reflects thus: 'This teacher holds such a view and doctrine: there is no giving, no sacrifice, no offering, no fruit or result of good and bad deeds, no this world, no other world, no mother, no father, no spontaneously reborn beings, no ascetics or brahmins who have attained right practice, who proclaim this world and the other world after realizing them themselves through direct knowledge. This person is composed of the four great elements, and when they die, earth returns to the earth element, water returns to the water element, fire returns to the fire element, air returns to the air element, and the faculties pass into space. The pallbearers, five in all, carry the corpse away; footprints are discernible as far as the charnel ground. The bones become dove-colored. Offerings end in ashes, and gifts are an empty display. Their talk is empty, false babble, those who speak of existence. Fools and the wise alike are annihilated and destroyed at the breaking of the body; they do not exist after death.' If this teacher’s words are true, then what I have done would be undone, and what I have lived would be unlived. Both of us would attain the same common state, even though I do not claim that we both will be annihilated, destroyed, and cease to exist after death. Moreover, this teacher goes to excess in nakedness, shaving, devoted to the squatting posture, and plucking out hair—while I live at home, enjoying the pleasures of children, using sandalwood from Kāsi, wearing garlands, perfumes, and ointments, accepting gold and silver. I will have the same destiny as this teacher! Knowing and seeing what for the next life should I live the holy life under this teacher? Realizing, 'This is an unholy life,' he becomes disenchanted and leaves. This, Sandaka, is the first kind of unholy life declared by the Blessed One, who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, where a wise person would not deliberately live the holy life, and if they did, they would not attain the right path, the wholesome Dhamma."

226. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, sandaka, idhekacco satthā evaṃvādī hoti evaṃdiṭṭhi – ‘karoto kārayato chindato chedāpayato pacato pācāpayato socayato socāpayato kilamato kilamāpayato phandato phandāpayato pāṇamatipātayato adinnaṃ ādiyato sandhiṃ chindato nillopaṃ harato ekāgārikaṃ karoto paripanthe tiṭṭhato paradāraṃ gacchato musā bhaṇato karoto na karīyati pāpaṃ. Khurapariyantena cepi cakkena yo imissā pathaviyā pāṇe ekaṃ maṃsakhalaṃ ekaṃ maṃsapuñjaṃ kareyya, natthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, natthi pāpassa āgamo. Dakkhiṇañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya hananto ghātento chindanto chedāpento pacanto pacāpento, natthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, natthi pāpassa āgamo. Uttarañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya dadanto dāpento yajanto yajāpento, natthi tatonidānaṃ puññaṃ, natthi puññassa āgamo. Dānena damena saṃyamena saccavajjena natthi puññaṃ, natthi puññassa āgamo’ti.

226."Furthermore, Sandaka, there is another teacher who speaks thus, holds such a view: 'In doing, in causing to do, in cutting, in causing to cut, in cooking, in causing to cook, in grieving, in causing to grieve, in tormenting, in causing to torment, in agitating, in causing to agitate, in killing living beings, in taking what is not given, in breaking into houses, in plundering, in committing burglary, in lying in wait, in committing adultery, in speaking falsely, in doing this, no evil is done. Even if with a razor-edged wheel one were to make all the living beings on this earth into a single mass of flesh, a single heap of flesh, no evil would come from that, no evil would result. Even if one were to go along the southern bank of the Ganges killing, slaughtering, cutting, causing to cut, cooking, causing to cook, no evil would come from that, no evil would result. Even if one were to go along the northern bank of the Ganges giving, causing to give, sacrificing, causing to sacrifice, no merit would come from that, no merit would result. Through generosity, taming, restraint, and truthful speech, there is no merit, no result of merit.'

‘‘Tatra, sandaka, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘ayaṃ kho bhavaṃ satthā evaṃvādī evaṃdiṭṭhi – karoto kārayato chindato chedāpayato pacato pācāpayato socato socāpayato kilamato kilamāpayato phandato phandāpayato pāṇamatipātayato adinnaṃ ādiyato sandhiṃ chindato nillopaṃ harato ekāgārikaṃ karoto paripanthe tiṭṭhato paradāraṃ gacchato musā bhaṇato karoto na karīyati pāpaṃ khurapariyantena cepi cakkena yo imissā pathaviyā pāṇe ekaṃ maṃsakhalaṃ ekaṃ maṃsapuñjaṃ kareyya, natthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, natthi pāpassa āgamo. Dakkhiṇañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya hananto ghātento chindanto chedāpento pacanto pacāpento, natthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, natthi pāpassa āgamo. Uttarañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya dadanto dāpento yajanto yajāpento, natthi tatonidānaṃ puññaṃ, natthi puññassa āgamo. Dānena damena saṃyamena saccavajjena natthi puññaṃ, natthi puññassa āgamo’ti. Sace imassa bhoto satthuno saccaṃ vacanaṃ, akatena me ettha kataṃ, avusitena me ettha vusitaṃ. Ubhopi mayaṃ ettha samasamā sāmaññaṃ pattā, yo cāhaṃ na vadāmi ‘ubhinnaṃ kurutaṃ na karīyati pāpa’nti. Atirekaṃ kho panimassa bhoto satthuno naggiyaṃ muṇḍiyaṃ ukkuṭikappadhānaṃ kesamassulocanaṃ yohaṃ puttasambādhasayanaṃ ajjhāvasanto kāsikacandanaṃ paccanubhonto mālāgandhavilepanaṃ dhārento jātarūparajataṃ sādiyanto iminā bhotā satthārā samasamagatiko bhavissāmi. Abhisamparāyaṃ sohaṃ kiṃ jānanto kiṃ passanto imasmiṃ satthari brahmacariyaṃ carissāmi? ‘So abrahmacariyavāso aya’nti iti viditvā tasmā brahmacariyā nibbijja pakkamati. Ayaṃ kho, sandaka, tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena dutiyo abrahmacariyavāso akkhāto yattha viññū puriso sasakkaṃ brahmacariyaṃ na vaseyya, vasanto ca nārādheyya ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusalaṃ.

"Here, Sandaka, a wise person reflects thus: 'This venerable teacher holds such a view and doctrine: "In acting, in making others act; in cutting, in making others cut; in tormenting, in making others torment; in causing sorrow, in making others cause sorrow; in causing trouble, in making others cause trouble; in causing to tremble, in making others cause to tremble; in killing living beings, in taking what is not given, in breaking into houses, in plundering, in committing burglary, in lying in wait on highways, in committing adultery, in speaking falsehood—no evil is done. Even if one were to make all the living beings on this earth into a single heap of flesh, a single mass of flesh, with a razor-edged wheel, no evil would arise from that, no influx of evil. If one were to go along the south bank of the Ganges killing, slaughtering, cutting off, causing to cut off, tormenting, causing to torment, no evil would arise from that, no influx of evil. If one were to go along the north bank of the Ganges giving, causing to give, sacrificing, causing to sacrifice, no merit would arise from that, no influx of merit. Through generosity, taming, restraint, and truthful speech, there is no merit, no influx of merit." If this venerable teacher’s statement is true, then what I have not done is as good as done, and what I have not lived is as good as lived. Both of us here have attained the same state of religious life, yet I do not say, ‘No evil is done by both who act.’ Yet this venerable teacher has gone to excess in nakedness, in shaving his head, in squatting, in plucking out his hair and beard, while I, dwelling in a house crowded with children, enjoying Kāsi sandalwood, wearing garlands, perfumes, and ointments, accepting gold and silver, will be on equal terms with this venerable teacher. What would I know, what would I see, that I should lead the holy life under this teacher with a view to the future? ‘This is a non-holy life,’ he understands, and becoming disenchanted with that holy life, he departs. This, Sandaka, is the second non-holy life declared by the Blessed One, who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, where a wise person would not live the holy life with confidence, and living it, would not succeed in the true Dhamma that is wholesome.

227. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, sandaka, idhekacco satthā evaṃvādī hoti evaṃdiṭṭhi – ‘natthi hetu, natthi paccayo sattānaṃ saṃkilesāya; ahetū appaccayā sattā saṃkilissanti; natthi hetu, natthi paccayo sattānaṃ visuddhiyā; ahetū appaccayā sattā visujjhanti; natthi balaṃ, natthi vīriyaṃ, natthi purisathāmo, natthi purisaparakkamo; sabbe sattā sabbe pāṇā sabbe bhūtā sabbe jīvā avasā abalā avīriyā niyatisaṅgatibhāvapariṇatā chasvevābhijātīsu sukhadukkhaṃ paṭisaṃvedentī’ti.

227."Furthermore, Sandaka, some teacher is one who speaks thus, who holds such a view: ‘There is no cause, no condition for the defilement of beings; beings are defiled without cause or condition. There is no cause, no condition for the purification of beings; beings are purified without cause or condition. There is no power, no energy, no human strength, no human exertion. All beings, all creatures, all living creatures, all souls are without mastery, without strength, without energy, transformed by destiny, coincidence, and inherent nature, and experience pleasure and pain in the six classes of births.’"

‘‘Tatra, sandaka, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘ayaṃ kho bhavaṃ satthā evaṃvādī evaṃdiṭṭhi – natthi hetu, natthi paccayo sattānaṃ saṃkilesāya, ahetū appaccayā sattā saṃkilissanti. Natthi hetu natthi paccayo sattānaṃ visuddhiyā, ahetū appaccayā sattā visujjhanti. Natthi balaṃ, natthi vīriyaṃ, natthi purisathāmo, natthi purisaparakkamo, sabbe sattā sabbe pāṇā sabbe bhūtā sabbe jīvā avasā abalā avīriyā niyatisaṅgatibhāvapariṇatā chasvevābhijātīsu sukhadukkhaṃ paṭisaṃvedentī’ti. Sace imassa bhoto satthuno saccaṃ vacanaṃ, akatena me ettha kataṃ, avusitena me ettha vusitaṃ. Ubhopi mayaṃ ettha samasamā sāmaññaṃ pattā, yo cāhaṃ na vadāmi ‘ubho ahetū appaccayā visujjhissāmā’ti. Atirekaṃ kho panimassa bhoto satthuno naggiyaṃ muṇḍiyaṃ ukkuṭikappadhānaṃ kesamassulocanaṃ yohaṃ puttasambādhasayanaṃ ajjhāvasanto kāsikacandanaṃ paccanubhonto mālāgandhavilepanaṃ dhārento jātarūparajataṃ sādiyanto iminā bhotā satthārā samasamagatiko bhavissāmi. Abhisamparāyaṃ sohaṃ kiṃ jānanto kiṃ passanto imasmiṃ satthari brahmacariyaṃ carissāmi? ‘So abrahmacariyavāso aya’nti – iti viditvā tasmā brahmacariyā nibbijja pakkamati. Ayaṃ kho, sandaka, tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena tatiyo abrahmacariyavāso akkhāto yattha viññū puriso sasakkaṃ brahmacariyaṃ na vaseyya, vasanto ca nārādheyya ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusalaṃ.

"Here, Sandaka, a wise person reflects thus: 'This revered teacher holds such a view and doctrine: "There is no cause, no condition for the defilement of beings; without cause or condition, beings are defiled. Then, when asked "What is unwholesome?" he says, "It is not like that for me." When asked "Is it otherwise from both?" he says, "It is not otherwise for me." Then, when told "So your doctrine is not in any of the three ways?" he says, "It is not not for me." Atha kho sandako paribbājako sakaṃ parisaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘carantu bhonto samaṇe gotame brahmacariyavāso. Na dāni sukaraṃ amhehi lābhasakkārasiloke pariccajitu’’nti. Iti hidaṃ sandako paribbājako sakaṃ parisaṃ uyyojesi bhagavati brahmacariyeti.

236.But, Venerable Ānanda, how many are the emancipated in this Dhamma and Discipline?" "Not just one hundred, Sandaka, nor two hundred, nor three hundred, nor four hundred, nor five hundred, but indeed many more than that are the emancipated in this Dhamma and Discipline." "It is wonderful, Venerable Ānanda, it is marvelous, Venerable Ānanda! There is no self-praise of one's own Dhamma, no disparagement of others' Dhamma, and in the sphere of Dhamma teaching so many emancipated ones appear! But these Ājīvakas, like the sons of a mother whose son has died, praise themselves and disparage others, and declare only three are emancipated, namely, Nanda Vaccha, Kisa Saṅkicca, and Makkhali Gosāla." Then the wanderer Sandaka addressed his own assembly: "Let the venerable ones live the holy life under the ascetic Gotama. It is not easy for us now to abandon gain, honor, and renown." Thus the wanderer Sandaka exhorted his own assembly to the holy life under the Blessed One.

Sandakasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The Sandaka Sutta, the sixth, is concluded.
