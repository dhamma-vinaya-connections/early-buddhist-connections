# 34. Cūḷagopālakasuttaṃ

## 34. The Minor Discourse on the Cowherd Boy

350. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā vajjīsu viharati ukkacelāyaṃ gaṅgāya nadiyā tīre. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

350.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Vajjians at Ukkacelā, on the bank of the river Ganges. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus.” “Venerable sir,” those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, māgadhako gopālako duppaññajātiko, vassānaṃ pacchime māse saradasamaye, asamavekkhitvā gaṅgāya nadiyā orimaṃ tīraṃ, asamavekkhitvā pārimaṃ tīraṃ, atittheneva gāvo patāresi uttaraṃ tīraṃ suvidehānaṃ. Atha kho, bhikkhave, gāvo majjhegaṅgāya nadiyā sote āmaṇḍaliyaṃ karitvā tattheva anayabyasanaṃ āpajjiṃsu. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so, bhikkhave, māgadhako gopālako duppaññajātiko, vassānaṃ pacchime māse saradasamaye, asamavekkhitvā gaṅgāya nadiyā orimaṃ tīraṃ, asamavekkhitvā pārimaṃ tīraṃ, atittheneva gāvo patāresi uttaraṃ tīraṃ suvidehānaṃ. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye hi keci [ye keci (syā. kaṃ.)] samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā akusalā imassa lokassa akusalā parassa lokassa, akusalā māradheyyassa akusalā amāradheyyassa, akusalā maccudheyyassa akusalā amaccudheyyassa, tesaṃ ye sotabbaṃ saddahātabbaṃ maññissanti, tesaṃ taṃ bhavissati dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāya.

“Once upon a time, bhikkhus, there was a Magadhan cowherd who was foolish by nature. At the end of the rainy season, in the autumn, without examining this shore of the Ganges river, without examining the far shore, he drove his cattle across to the far shore of Suvideha by an unsuitable ford. Then, bhikkhus, the cattle, caught in the middle of the Ganges river’s current, swirled around and there met with disaster and ruin. Why was that? Because, bhikkhus, that Magadhan cowherd was foolish by nature. At the end of the rainy season, in the autumn, without examining this shore of the Ganges river, without examining the far shore, he drove his cattle across to the far shore of Suvideha by an unsuitable ford. Just so, bhikkhus, whatever ascetics or brahmins are unskilled regarding this world, unskilled regarding the next world, unskilled regarding Māra’s domain, unskilled regarding what is beyond Māra’s domain, unskilled regarding Death’s domain, unskilled regarding what is beyond Death’s domain—those who think they should listen to them and place faith in them, that will lead to their long-term harm and suffering.”

351. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, māgadhako gopālako sappaññajātiko, vassānaṃ pacchime māse saradasamaye, samavekkhitvā gaṅgāya nadiyā orimaṃ tīraṃ, samavekkhitvā pārimaṃ tīraṃ, tittheneva gāvo patāresi uttaraṃ tīraṃ suvidehānaṃ. So paṭhamaṃ patāresi ye te usabhā gopitaro gopariṇāyakā. Te tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu. Athāpare patāresi balavagāvo dammagāvo. Tepi tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu. Athāpare patāresi vacchatare vacchatariyo. Tepi tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu. Athāpare patāresi vacchake kisābalake [kisabalake (sī. syā. pī.)]. Tepi tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu. Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, vacchako taruṇako tāvadeva jātako mātugoravakena vuyhamāno, sopi tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamāsi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so, bhikkhave, māgadhako gopālako sappaññajātiko, vassānaṃ pacchime māse saradasamaye, samavekkhitvā gaṅgāya nadiyā orimaṃ tīraṃ, samavekkhitvā pārimaṃ tīraṃ, tittheneva gāvo patāresi uttaraṃ tīraṃ suvidehānaṃ. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye hi keci samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā kusalā imassa lokassa kusalā parassa lokassa, kusalā māradheyyassa kusalā amāradheyyassa, kusalā maccudheyyassa kusalā amaccudheyyassa, tesaṃ ye sotabbaṃ saddahātabbaṃ maññissanti, tesaṃ taṃ bhavissati dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya.

351.“Once upon a time, bhikkhus, there was a wise cowherd from Magadha. At the end of the rainy season, in the autumn, after surveying the near shore of the Ganges River and surveying the far shore, he led his cattle across by a ford to the northern shore, the land of the Videhas, safely. First, he led across the bulls, the fathers and leaders of the herd. They cut across the current of the Ganges diagonally and reached the far shore safely. Next, he led across the strong and tame oxen. They too cut across the current of the Ganges diagonally and reached the far shore safely. Then, he led across the young bullocks and heifers. They also cut across the current of the Ganges diagonally and reached the far shore safely. After that, he led across the calves and weaklings. They too cut across the current of the Ganges diagonally and reached the far shore safely. Once, bhikkhus, there was a newborn calf, just born, being carried along by its mother’s respect. Even it cut across the current of the Ganges diagonally and reached the far shore safely. Why was that? Because that wise cowherd from Magadha, at the end of the rainy season, in the autumn, after surveying the near shore of the Ganges River and surveying the far shore, safely led his cattle across by a ford to the northern shore, the land of the Videhas. In the same way, bhikkhus, whatever ascetics or brahmins are skilled in this world, skilled in the next world, skilled in the domain of Māra, skilled in the domain beyond Māra, skilled in the domain of death, skilled in the domain beyond death—those who think they should listen and place faith in them—that will be for their long-term welfare and happiness.”

352. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, ye te usabhā gopitaro gopariṇāyakā te tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu, evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye te bhikkhū arahanto khīṇāsavā vusitavanto katakaraṇīyā ohitabhārā anuppattasadatthā parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojanā sammadaññā vimuttā, te tiriyaṃ mārassa sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ gatā.

352.“bhikkhus, just as those bulls, leaders of the herd, cross the Ganges’ current safely to the far shore by cutting across it, so too, bhikkhus, those bhikkhus who are arahants—whose taints are destroyed, who have lived the life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, attained their own goal, utterly destroyed the fetters of being, and are completely liberated through right knowledge—they have cut across Māra’s stream and gone safely to the far shore.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi te, bhikkhave, balavagāvo dammagāvo tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu, evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye te bhikkhū pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā opapātikā tattha parinibbāyino anāvattidhammā tasmā lokā, tepi tiriyaṃ mārassa sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ gamissanti.

“Just as, bhikkhus, strong, trained bulls cut across the Ganges current and reach the far shore in safety, so too, bhikkhus, those bhikkhus who, with the destruction of the five lower fetters, are reborn spontaneously, attain final Nibbāna there, and are not liable to return from that world—they too will cut across Māra’s current and reach the far shore in safety.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi te, bhikkhave, vacchatarā vacchatariyo tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu, evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye te bhikkhū tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmino sakiṃdeva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karissanti, tepi tiriyaṃ mārassa sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ gamissanti.

“Just as, bhikkhus, those young bullocks and heifers, having crossed the Ganges’ current sideways, safely reached the far shore, even so, bhikkhus, those bhikkhus who, with the destruction of the three fetters, and with the thinning out of lust, hatred, and delusion, are once-returners, coming back to this world only once more to make an end of suffering—they too, having crossed Māra’s current sideways, will safely reach the far shore.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi te, bhikkhave, vacchakā kisābalakā tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamaṃsu, evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye te bhikkhū tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyanā, tepi tiriyaṃ mārassa sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ gamissanti.

“Just as, bhikkhus, those weak, young calves, having cut across the current of the Ganges, reached the far shore in safety; even so, bhikkhus, those bhikkhus who, with the destruction of the three fetters, are stream-enterers, no longer subject to downfall, assured, destined for enlightenment—they too, having cut across the current of Māra, will reach the far shore in safety.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi so, bhikkhave, vacchako taruṇako tāvadeva jātako mātugoravakena vuyhamāno tiriyaṃ gaṅgāya sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ agamāsi, evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye te bhikkhū dhammānusārino saddhānusārino, tepi tiriyaṃ mārassa sotaṃ chetvā sotthinā pāraṃ gamissanti.

“Just as, bhikkhus, that young calf, just born, being carried along by the mother cow’s regard, having cut across the current of the Ganges, safely reached the far shore; even so, bhikkhus, those bhikkhus who are followers of the Dhamma, followers through faith—they too, having cut across the current of Māra, will safely reach the far shore.”

‘‘Ahaṃ kho pana, bhikkhave, kusalo imassa lokassa kusalo parassa lokassa, kusalo māradheyyassa kusalo amāradheyyassa, kusalo maccudheyyassa kusalo amaccudheyyassa. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, ye sotabbaṃ saddahātabbaṃ maññissanti, tesaṃ taṃ bhavissati dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti.

“But I, bhikkhus, am skilled regarding this world, skilled regarding the other world, skilled regarding the realm of Māra, skilled regarding the realm beyond Māra, skilled regarding the realm of death, skilled regarding the realm beyond death. For those of you, bhikkhus, who think they should listen to and place faith in me, that will be for their long-term welfare and happiness.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Idaṃ vatvā sugato athāparaṃ etadavoca satthā –

The Blessed One said this. Having said this, the Fortunate One, the Teacher, then said further:

‘‘Ayaṃ loko paro loko, jānatā suppakāsito;Yañca mārena sampattaṃ, appattaṃ yañca maccunā.

“This world and the next world are well proclaimed by the Knower; and also what is reached by Māra, and what is not reached by Death.”

‘‘Sabbaṃ lokaṃ abhiññāya, sambuddhena pajānatā;Vivaṭaṃ amatadvāraṃ, khemaṃ nibbānapattiyā.

“Having fully known the entire world, comprehended by the Awakened One, the Buddha; The door to the Deathless is open, secure for the attainment of Nibbāna.”

‘‘Chinnaṃ pāpimato sotaṃ, viddhastaṃ vinaḷīkataṃ;Pāmojjabahulā hotha, khemaṃ pattattha [patthetha (syā. kaṃ. ka. aṭṭhakathāyaṃ saṃvaṇṇetabbapāṭho)] bhikkhavo’’ti.

“Cut off is the stream of the Evil One, shattered and destroyed; Be full of joy, bhikkhus, you have attained security.”

Cūḷagopālakasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The Shorter Discourse on the Cowherd, the fourth, is finished.
