# 77. Mahāsakuludāyisuttaṃ

## 77. The Greater Sakkuludāyi Discourse.

237. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā abhiññātā abhiññātā paribbājakā moranivāpe paribbājakārāme paṭivasanti, seyyathidaṃ – annabhāro varadharo sakuludāyī ca paribbājako aññe ca abhiññātā abhiññātā paribbājakā. Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya rājagahaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘atippago kho tāva rājagahe piṇḍāya carituṃ. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ yena moranivāpo paribbājakārāmo yena sakuludāyī paribbājako tenupasaṅkameyya’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā yena moranivāpo paribbājakārāmo tenupasaṅkami. Tena kho pana samayena sakuludāyī paribbājako mahatiyā paribbājakaparisāya saddhiṃ nisinno hoti unnādiniyā uccāsaddamahāsaddāya anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathentiyā, seyyathidaṃ – rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ mahāmattakathaṃ senākathaṃ bhayakathaṃ yuddhakathaṃ annakathaṃ pānakathaṃ vatthakathaṃ sayanakathaṃ mālākathaṃ gandhakathaṃ ñātikathaṃ yānakathaṃ gāmakathaṃ nigamakathaṃ nagarakathaṃ janapadakathaṃ itthikathaṃ sūrakathaṃ visikhākathaṃ kumbhaṭṭhānakathaṃ pubbapetakathaṃ nānattakathaṃ lokakkhāyikaṃ samuddakkhāyikaṃ itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vā. Addasā kho sakuludāyī paribbājako bhagavantaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna sakaṃ parisaṃ saṇṭhāpeti – ‘‘appasaddā bhonto hontu; mā bhonto saddamakattha. Ayaṃ samaṇo gotamo āgacchati; appasaddakāmo kho pana so āyasmā appasaddassa vaṇṇavādī. Appeva nāma appasaddaṃ parisaṃ viditvā upasaṅkamitabbaṃ maññeyyā’’ti. Atha kho te paribbājakā tuṇhī ahesuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā yena sakuludāyī paribbājako tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho sakuludāyī paribbājako bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘etu kho, bhante, bhagavā. Svāgataṃ, bhante, bhagavato. Cirassaṃ kho, bhante, bhagavā imaṃ pariyāyamakāsi yadidaṃ idhāgamanāya. Nisīdatu, bhante, bhagavā; idamāsanaṃ paññatta’’nti. Nisīdi bhagavā paññatte āsane. Sakuludāyīpi kho paribbājako aññataraṃ nīcaṃ āsanaṃ gahetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho sakuludāyiṃ paribbājakaṃ bhagavā etadavoca –

237.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels’ Sanctuary. Now at that time, several well-known wanderers were staying at the Peacocks’ Park, the wanderers’ park—namely, Annabhāra, Varadhara, the wanderer Sakuludāyī, and other well-known wanderers. Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the morning, took his bowl and robe and entered Rājagaha for alms. Then it occurred to the Blessed One: "It is still too early to walk for alms in Rājagaha. Why don’t I go to the Peacocks’ Park, the wanderers’ park, where Sakuludāyī the wanderer is staying?" So the Blessed One went to the Peacocks’ Park, the wanderers’ park. Now at that time, Sakuludāyī the wanderer was sitting with a large assembly of wanderers, making a loud, boisterous noise, engaged in various kinds of trifling talk—such as talk about kings, robbers, ministers, armies, dangers, battles, food, drink, clothes, beds, garlands, perfumes, relatives, vehicles, villages, towns, cities, countries, women, heroes, streets, crossroads, the departed, miscellaneous tales, speculations about the world and the ocean, and talk about being or not being. Sakuludāyī the wanderer saw the Blessed One approaching from afar. Upon seeing him, he quieted his own assembly, saying: "Be quiet, sirs, do not make a noise. This ascetic Gotama is coming; he appreciates quiet and speaks in praise of silence. Perhaps, knowing that our assembly is quiet, he may think to approach us." Then those wanderers fell silent. The Blessed One approached Sakuludāyī the wanderer. Sakuludāyī then said to the Blessed One: "Come, Venerable Sir, Blessed One! Welcome, Venerable Blessed One! It has been a long time since the Blessed One found an opportunity to come here. Please, Venerable Sir, sit down. This seat has been prepared." The Blessed One sat down on the prepared seat. Sakuludāyī the wanderer also took a low seat and sat to one side. When Sakuludāyī the wanderer was seated to one side, the Blessed One said to him:

238. ‘‘Kāyanuttha, udāyi, etarahi kathāya sannisinnā, kā ca pana vo antarākathā vippakatā’’ti? ‘‘Tiṭṭhatesā, bhante, kathā yāya mayaṃ etarahi kathāya sannisinnā. Nesā, bhante, kathā bhagavato dullabhā bhavissati pacchāpi savanāya. Purimāni, bhante, divasāni purimatarāni nānātitthiyānaṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ kutūhalasālāyaṃ sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ ayamantarākathā udapādi – ‘lābhā vata, bho, aṅgamagadhānaṃ, suladdhalābhā vata, bho, aṅgamagadhānaṃ! Tatrime [yatthime (sī.)] samaṇabrāhmaṇā saṅghino gaṇino gaṇācariyā ñātā yasassino titthakarā sādhusammatā bahujanassa rājagahaṃ vassāvāsaṃ osaṭā. Ayampi kho pūraṇo kassapo saṅghī ceva gaṇī ca gaṇācariyo ca ñāto yasassī titthakaro sādhusammato bahujanassa; sopi rājagahaṃ vassāvāsaṃ osaṭo. Ayampi kho makkhali gosālo…pe… ajito kesakambalo… pakudho kaccāyano… sañjayo belaṭṭhaputto… nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto saṅghī ceva gaṇī ca gaṇācariyo ca ñāto yasassī titthakaro sādhusammato bahujanassa; sopi rājagahaṃ vassāvāsaṃ osaṭo. Ayampi kho samaṇo gotamo saṅghī ceva gaṇī ca gaṇācariyo ca ñāto yasassī titthakaro sādhusammato bahujanassa; sopi rājagahaṃ vassāvāsaṃ osaṭo. Ko nu kho imesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saṅghīnaṃ gaṇīnaṃ gaṇācariyānaṃ ñātānaṃ yasassīnaṃ titthakarānaṃ sādhusammatānaṃ bahujanassa sāvakānaṃ sakkato garukato mānito pūjito, kañca pana sāvakā sakkatvā garuṃ katvā [garukatvā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] upanissāya viharantī’’’ti?

238."Udāyī, just now you were sitting together in conversation. What topic of conversation was interrupted?""Venerable sir, let that conversation, for which we were sitting together just now, remain. That conversation will not be difficult for the Blessed One to hear later. Venerable sir, some days ago, quite some time ago, when ascetics and brahmins of various sects were sitting together, gathered together in the assembly hall, this discussion arose among them: ‘Truly, good sirs, it is a gain for the people of Aṅga and Magadha, a great gain indeed for the people of Aṅga and Magadha, that these ascetics and brahmins—leaders of followings, heads of groups, teachers of groups, well-known, famous, founders of schools, esteemed by many people—have come to spend the rains in Rājagaha. This Pūraṇa Kassapa is a leader of a following, a head of a group, a teacher of a group, well-known, famous, a founder of a school, esteemed by many people; he too has come to spend the rains in Rājagaha. This Makkhali Gosāla… Ajita Kesakambala… Pakudha Kaccāyana… Sañjaya Belaṭṭhaputta… Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta is a leader of a following, a head of a group, a teacher of a group, well-known, famous, a founder of a school, esteemed by many people; he too has come to spend the rains in Rājagaha. This ascetic Gotama is a leader of a following, a head of a group, a teacher of a group, well-known, famous, a founder of a school, esteemed by many people; he too has come to spend the rains in Rājagaha. Now, which of these venerable ascetics and brahmins—leaders of followings, heads of groups, teachers of groups, well-known, famous, founders of schools, esteemed by many people—are honored, respected, revered, and venerated by their disciples, and which ones do their disciples live in dependence on, honoring and respecting them?’"

239. ‘‘Tatrekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘ayaṃ kho pūraṇo kassapo saṅghī ceva gaṇī ca gaṇācariyo ca ñāto yasassī titthakaro sādhusammato bahujanassa; so ca kho sāvakānaṃ na sakkato na garukato na mānito na pūjito, na ca pana pūraṇaṃ kassapaṃ sāvakā sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti. Bhūtapubbaṃ pūraṇo kassapo anekasatāya parisāya dhammaṃ deseti. Tatraññataro pūraṇassa kassapassa sāvako saddamakāsi – ‘‘mā bhonto pūraṇaṃ kassapaṃ etamatthaṃ pucchittha; neso etaṃ jānāti; mayametaṃ jānāma, amhe etamatthaṃ pucchatha; mayametaṃ bhavantānaṃ byākarissāmā’’ti. Bhūtapubbaṃ pūraṇo kassapo bāhā paggayha kandanto na labhati – ‘‘appasaddā bhonto hontu, mā bhonto saddamakattha. Nete, bhavante, pucchanti, amhe ete pucchanti; mayametesaṃ byākarissāmā’’ti. Bahū kho pana pūraṇassa kassapassa sāvakā vādaṃ āropetvā apakkantā – ‘‘na tvaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānāsi, ahaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānāmi, kiṃ tvaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānissasi? Micchāpaṭipanno tvamasi, ahamasmi sammāpaṭipanno, sahitaṃ me, asahitaṃ te, purevacanīyaṃ pacchā avaca, pacchāvacanīyaṃ pure avaca, adhiciṇṇaṃ te viparāvattaṃ, āropito te vādo, niggahitosi, cara vādappamokkhāya, nibbeṭhehi vā sace pahosī’’ti. Iti pūraṇo kassapo sāvakānaṃ na sakkato na garukato na mānito na pūjito, na ca pana pūraṇaṃ kassapaṃ sāvakā sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti. Akkuṭṭho ca pana pūraṇo kassapo dhammakkosenā’’’ti.

239."There, some said this: 'This Pūraṇa Kassapa is indeed the leader of a group, the head of a community, the teacher of a school, well-known, famous, a founder of a doctrine, esteemed by many people; and yet he is not honored, respected, revered, or venerated by his disciples, nor do his disciples live in dependence on him, honoring and respecting him. Once, Pūraṇa Kassapa was teaching the Dhamma to an assembly of many hundreds. There, one of Pūraṇa Kassapa’s disciples made a noise, saying: "Do not ask Pūraṇa Kassapa about this matter; he does not know it. We know it; ask us about this matter. We will explain it to you." Once, Pūraṇa Kassapa, stretching out his arms and weeping, could not get them to be quiet, saying: "Be quiet, do not make noise. They are not asking you; they are asking me. I will explain it to them." Moreover, many of Pūraṇa Kassapa’s disciples, having raised a dispute, departed, saying: "You do not understand this Dhamma and Discipline; I understand it. How could you understand it? You are practicing wrongly; I am practicing rightly. What I say is to the point; what you say is not to the point. What should have been said first, you said last; what should have been said last, you said first. What you have long adhered to is overturned. Your argument is refuted; you are defeated. Go, free yourself from your argument, or extricate yourself if you can." Thus, Pūraṇa Kassapa is not honored, respected, revered, or venerated by his disciples, nor do his disciples live in dependence on him, honoring and respecting him. Moreover, Pūraṇa Kassapa is rebuked with the rebuke about the Dhamma.'"

‘‘Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘ayampi kho makkhali gosālo…pe… ajito kesakambalo… pakudho kaccāyano… sañjayo belaṭṭhaputto… nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto saṅghī ceva gaṇī ca gaṇācariyo ca ñāto yasassī titthakaro sādhusammato bahujanassa; so ca kho sāvakānaṃ na sakkato na garukato na mānito na pūjito, na ca pana nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ sāvakā sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti. Bhūtapubbaṃ nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto anekasatāya parisāya dhammaṃ deseti. Tatraññataro nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa sāvako saddamakāsi – mā bhonto nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etamatthaṃ pucchittha; neso etaṃ jānāti; mayametaṃ jānāma, amhe etamatthaṃ pucchatha; mayametaṃ bhavantānaṃ byākarissāmāti. Bhūtapubbaṃ nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto bāhā paggayha kandanto na labhati – ‘‘appasaddā bhonto hontu, mā bhonto saddamakattha. Nete bhavante pucchanti, amhe ete pucchanti; mayametesaṃ byākarissāmā’’ti. Bahū kho pana nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa sāvakā vādaṃ āropetvā apakkantā – ‘‘na tvaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānāsi, ahaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānāmi. Kiṃ tvaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānissasi? Micchāpaṭipanno tvamasi. Ahamasmi sammāpaṭipanno. Sahitaṃ me asahitaṃ te, purevacanīyaṃ pacchā avaca, pacchāvacanīyaṃ pure avaca, adhiciṇṇaṃ te viparāvattaṃ, āropito te vādo, niggahitosi, cara vādappamokkhāya, nibbeṭhehi vā sace pahosī’’ti. Iti nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto sāvakānaṃ na sakkato na garukato na mānito na pūjito, na ca pana nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ sāvakā sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti. Akkuṭṭho ca pana nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto dhammakkosenā’’’ti.

"Some have said this: 'This Makkhali Gosāla… Ajita Kesakambala… Pakudha Kaccāyana… Sañjaya Belaṭṭhaputta… Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta is also a leader of a community, a head of a group, a teacher of a group, well-known and famous, a founder of a sect, highly esteemed by many people. Yet, he is not honored, respected, esteemed, or venerated by his disciples, nor do his disciples live in dependence on Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, honoring and respecting him. Once, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta was teaching the Dhamma to an assembly of many hundreds. There, one of Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta’s disciples made a loud noise: "Do not ask Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta about this matter; he does not know it. We know this; ask us about it. We will explain it to you." Once, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, stretching out his arms, weeping, could not obtain silence, saying: "Be quiet, do not make noise. They are not asking you; they are asking us. We will answer them." Many of Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta’s disciples, having leveled an accusation against him, departed, saying: "You do not understand this Dhamma and Discipline; I understand it. How could you understand this Dhamma and Discipline? You are wrongly practicing; I am rightly practicing. My word is consistent; yours is inconsistent. What should have been said first, you said later; what should have been said later, you said first. What you have long practiced has been overturned. A charge has been leveled against your argument; you are defeated. Go, free yourself from the charge, or resolve it if you can." Thus, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta is not honored, respected, esteemed, or venerated by his disciples, nor do his disciples live in dependence on Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, honoring and respecting him. Moreover, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta is verbally abused with accusations concerning the Dhamma.'"

240. ‘‘Ekacce evamāhaṃsu – ‘ayampi kho samaṇo gotamo saṅghī ceva gaṇī ca gaṇācariyo ca ñāto yasassī titthakaro sādhusammato bahujanassa; so ca kho sāvakānaṃ sakkato garukato mānito pūjito, samaṇañca pana gotamaṃ sāvakā sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti. Bhūtapubbaṃ samaṇo gotamo anekasatāya parisāya dhammaṃ desesi. Tatraññataro samaṇassa gotamassa sāvako ukkāsi. Tamenāññataro sabrahmacārī jaṇṇukena [jaṇṇuke (sī.)] ghaṭṭesi – ‘‘appasaddo āyasmā hotu, māyasmā saddamakāsi, satthā no bhagavā dhammaṃ desesī’’ti. Yasmiṃ samaye samaṇo gotamo anekasatāya parisāya dhammaṃ deseti, neva tasmiṃ samaye samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakānaṃ khipitasaddo vā hoti ukkāsitasaddo vā. Tamenaṃ mahājanakāyo paccāsīsamānarūpo [paccāsiṃ samānarūpo (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] paccupaṭṭhito hoti – ‘‘yaṃ no bhagavā dhammaṃ bhāsissati taṃ no sossāmā’’ti. Seyyathāpi nāma puriso cātummahāpathe khuddamadhuṃ [khuddaṃ madhuṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] anelakaṃ pīḷeyya [uppīḷeyya (sī.)]. Tamenaṃ mahājanakāyo paccāsīsamānarūpo paccupaṭṭhito assa. Evameva yasmiṃ samaye samaṇo gotamo anekasatāya parisāya dhammaṃ deseti, neva tasmiṃ samaye samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakānaṃ khipitasaddo vā hoti ukkāsitasaddo vā. Tamenaṃ mahājanakāyo paccāsīsamānarūpo paccupaṭṭhito hoti – ‘‘yaṃ no bhagavā dhammaṃ bhāsissati taṃ no sossāmā’’ti. Yepi samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakā sabrahmacārīhi sampayojetvā sikkhaṃ paccakkhāya hīnāyāvattanti tepi satthu ceva vaṇṇavādino honti, dhammassa ca vaṇṇavādino honti, saṅghassa ca vaṇṇavādino honti, attagarahinoyeva honti anaññagarahino, ‘‘mayamevamhā alakkhikā mayaṃ appapuññā te mayaṃ evaṃ svākkhāte dhammavinaye pabbajitvā nāsakkhimhā yāvajīvaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ caritu’’nti. Te ārāmikabhūtā vā upāsakabhūtā vā pañcasikkhāpade samādāya vattanti. Iti samaṇo gotamo sāvakānaṃ sakkato garukato mānito pūjito, samaṇañca pana gotamaṃ sāvakā sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharantī’’’ti.

240."Some say this: 'This ascetic Gotama is a leader of a community, a head of a group, a teacher of a group, known, famous, a founder of a sect, highly esteemed by many people. And his disciples honor, respect, esteem, and venerate him, and they live in dependence on the ascetic Gotama, having honored and respected him. Once, the ascetic Gotama was teaching the Dhamma to an assembly of many hundreds. There, one of the ascetic Gotama’s disciples coughed. Then another fellow practitioner nudged him with his knee and said, "Be quiet, venerable one, do not make a sound. Our Teacher, the Blessed One, is teaching the Dhamma." When the ascetic Gotama teaches the Dhamma to an assembly of many hundreds, his disciples do not make the sound of clearing their throats or coughing. The great crowd waits eagerly, attentively, thinking, "We will listen to whatever Dhamma the Blessed One will teach us." Just as if a man were to distribute pure wild honey at a major intersection, the great crowd would wait eagerly, attentively. In the same way, when the ascetic Gotama teaches the Dhamma to an assembly of many hundreds, his disciples do not make the sound of clearing their throats or coughing. The great crowd waits eagerly, attentively, thinking, "We will listen to whatever Dhamma the Blessed One will teach us." Even those disciples of the ascetic Gotama who, having engaged in a dispute with fellow practitioners, renounce the training and return to a lower life still praise the Teacher, praise the Dhamma, and praise the Saṅgha. They blame only themselves and no one else, saying, "We are unfortunate, we have little merit, for though we went forth in this well-proclaimed Dhamma and Discipline, we were unable to live the holy life perfectly and purely for as long as we live." They become monastery attendants or lay followers, undertaking the five precepts. Thus, the ascetic Gotama is honored, respected, esteemed, and venerated by his disciples, and they live in dependence on the ascetic Gotama, having honored and respected him.'"

241. ‘‘Kati pana tvaṃ, udāyi, mayi dhamme samanupassasi, yehi mamaṃ [mama (sabbattha)] sāvakā sakkaronti garuṃ karonti [garukaronti (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] mānenti pūjenti, sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharantī’’ti? ‘‘Pañca kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavati dhamme samanupassāmi yehi bhagavantaṃ sāvakā sakkaronti garuṃ karonti mānenti pūjenti, sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti. Katame pañca? Bhagavā hi, bhante, appāhāro, appāhāratāya ca vaṇṇavādī. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā appāhāro, appāhāratāya ca vaṇṇavādī imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavati paṭhamaṃ dhammaṃ samanupassāmi yena bhagavantaṃ sāvakā sakkaronti garuṃ karonti mānenti pūjenti, sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti.

241."But, Udayi, how many qualities do you observe in me because of which my disciples honor, respect, esteem, and venerate me, and live in dependence on me, having honored and respected me?" "Venerable sir, I observe five qualities in the Blessed One because of which his disciples honor, respect, esteem, and venerate him, and live in dependence on him, having honored and respected him. What are the five? Venerable sir, the Blessed One is of few meals and speaks in praise of few meals. Venerable sir, because the Blessed One is of few meals and speaks in praise of few meals, this is the first quality I observe in him because of which his disciples honor, respect, esteem, and venerate him, and live in dependence on him, having honored and respected him."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā santuṭṭho itarītarena cīvarena, itarītaracīvarasantuṭṭhiyā ca vaṇṇavādī. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā santuṭṭho itarītarena cīvarena, itarītaracīvarasantuṭṭhiyā ca vaṇṇavādī, imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavati dutiyaṃ dhammaṃ samanupassāmi yena bhagavantaṃ sāvakā sakkaronti garuṃ karonti mānenti pūjenti, sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti.

"Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One is content with any humble robe, and he speaks in praise of contentment with any humble robe. Evameva kho, udāyi, bhikkhu imameva kāyaṃ samādhijena pītisukhena abhisandeti parisandeti paripūreti parippharati, nāssa kiñci sabbāvato kāyassa samādhijena pītisukhena apphuṭaṃ hoti.

Furthermore, Udāyi, with the subsiding of thought and examination, a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the second jhāna, which is characterized by internal confidence and unification of mind, free from thought and examination, born of samādhi, filled with rapture and joy. He permeates, pervades, suffuses, and fills this very body with the rapture and joy born of samādhi, so that there is no part of his entire body unpervaded by the rapture and joy born of samādhi. Just as, Udāyi, there might be a deep lake with water welling up from below, having no source of water flowing in from the east, west, north, or south, nor replenished from time to time by showers of rain; yet a cool stream of water welling up from within the lake would suffuse, fill, and pervade the entire lake with cool water, so that no part of the entire lake would remain untouched by the cool water—so too, Udāyi, a bhikkhu permeates, pervades, suffuses, and fills this very body with the rapture and joy born of samādhi, so that there is no part of his entire body unpervaded by the rapture and joy born of samādhi.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, udāyi, bhikkhu pītiyā ca virāgā…pe… tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. So imameva kāyaṃ nippītikena sukhena abhisandeti parisandeti paripūreti parippharati, nāssa kiñci sabbāvato kāyassa nippītikena sukhena apphuṭaṃ hoti. He permeates, pervades, suffuses, and fills this very body with happiness devoid of rapture, so that there is no part of his entire body unpervaded by happiness devoid of rapture. Just as, Udāyi, in a pond of blue lotuses, or red lotuses, or white lotuses, some lotuses are born in the water, grown in the water, rising no further than the water, nourished from within the water. So too, Udāyi, a bhikkhu permeates, pervades, suffuses, and fills this very body with happiness devoid of rapture, so that there is no part of his entire body unpervaded by happiness devoid of rapture.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, udāyi, bhikkhu sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā pubbeva somanassadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamā adukkhamasukhaṃ upekkhāsatipārisuddhiṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. So imameva kāyaṃ parisuddhena cetasā pariyodātena pharitvā nisinno hoti, nāssa kiñci sabbāvato kāyassa parisuddhena cetasā pariyodātena apphuṭaṃ hoti. Seyyathāpi, udāyi, puriso odātena vatthena sasīsaṃ pārupitvā nisinno assa, nāssa kiñci sabbāvato kāyassa odātena vatthena apphuṭaṃ assa; evameva kho, udāyi, bhikkhu imameva kāyaṃ parisuddhena cetasā pariyodātena pharitvā nisinno hoti, nāssa kiñci sabbāvato kāyassa parisuddhena cetasā pariyodātena apphuṭaṃ hoti. Tatra ca pana me sāvakā bahū abhiññāvosānapāramippattā viharanti.

Tatra ca pana me sāvakā bahū abhiññāvosānapāramippattā viharanti.

257.Furthermore, Udāyi, I have declared to my disciples the path by which, when my disciples have followed it, they recollect their manifold past lives—that is, one birth, two births, three births, four births, five births, ten births, twenty births, thirty births, forty births, fifty births, a hundred births, a thousand births, a hundred thousand births, many eons of world-contraction, many eons of world-expansion, many eons of world-contraction and expansion: 'There I was, of such a name, such a clan, such an appearance, such food, such experiences of pleasure and pain, such a lifespan. Passing away from there, I arose elsewhere. There too I was of such a name, such a clan, such an appearance, such food, such experiences of pleasure and pain, such a lifespan. Passing away from there, I was reborn here.' Thus they recollect their manifold past lives with details and specifics. Just as, Udāyi, a man might go from his own village to another village, and from that village to yet another village, and then return from that village back to his own village. He would think: 'I went from my own village to that other village, where I stood thus, sat thus, spoke thus, remained silent thus. From that village, I went to that other village, where I stood thus, sat thus, spoke thus, remained silent thus. And now I have returned from that village back to my own village.' In the same way, Udāyi, I have declared to my disciples the path by which, when my disciples have followed it, they recollect their manifold past lives—that is, one birth, two births, three births, four births, five births, ten births, twenty births, thirty births, forty births, fifty births, a hundred births, a thousand births, a hundred thousand births, many eons of world-contraction, many eons of world-expansion, many eons of world-contraction and expansion: 'There I was, of such a name, such a clan, such an appearance, such food, such experiences of pleasure and pain, such a lifespan. Passing away from there, I arose elsewhere. There too I was of such a name, such a clan, such an appearance, such food, such experiences of pleasure and pain, such a lifespan. Passing away from there, I was reborn here.' Thus they recollect their manifold past lives with details and specifics. And in this matter, many of my disciples abide having attained the ultimate perfection in direct knowledge.

258. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, udāyi, akkhātā mayā sāvakānaṃ paṭipadā, yathāpaṭipannā me sāvakā dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passanti cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajānanti – ‘ime vata bhonto sattā kāyaduccaritena samannāgatā vacīduccaritena samannāgatā manoduccaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ upavādakā micchādiṭṭhikā micchādiṭṭhikammasamādānā, te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannā; ime vā pana bhonto sattā kāyasucaritena samannāgatā vacīsucaritena samannāgatā manosucaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ anupavādakā sammādiṭṭhikā sammādiṭṭhikammasamādānā, te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapannā’ti. Iti dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passanti cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajānanti. Seyyathāpi, udāyi, dve agārā sadvārā [sannadvārā (ka.)]. Tatra cakkhumā puriso majjhe ṭhito passeyya manusse gehaṃ pavisantepi nikkhamantepi anucaṅkamantepi anuvicarantepi; evameva kho, udāyi, akkhātā mayā sāvakānaṃ paṭipadā, yathāpaṭipannā me sāvakā dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passanti cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajānanti…pe… tatra ca pa me sāvakā bahū abhiññāvosānapāramippattā viharanti.

258.Furthermore, Udāyi, I have declared to my disciples the path by which, when my disciples have followed it, they with the divine eye—purified and surpassing the human—see beings passing away and being reborn, inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, and understand how beings go according to their actions: ‘Indeed, these beings who engaged in misconduct of body, speech, and mind, who were revilers of the noble ones, held wrong views, and undertook actions based on wrong views—after the breakup of the body, after death, have arisen in a state of loss, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. But these beings who engaged in good conduct of body, speech, and mind, who were not revilers of the noble ones, held right views, and undertook actions based on right views—after the breakup of the body, after death, have arisen in a good destination, a heavenly world.’ Thus, with the divine eye—purified and surpassing the human—they see beings passing away and being reborn, inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, and understand how beings go according to their actions. Just as, Udāyi, if there were two houses with doors, and a clear-sighted man standing in the middle could see people entering, exiting, walking about, and moving around—in the same way, Udāyi, I have declared to my disciples the path by which, when my disciples have followed it, they with the divine eye—purified and surpassing the human—see beings passing away and being reborn, inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, and understand how beings go according to their actions. And in this matter, many of my disciples abide having attained the ultimate perfection in direct knowledge.

259. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, udāyi, akkhātā mayā sāvakānaṃ paṭipadā, yathāpaṭipannā me sāvakā āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharanti. Seyyathāpi, udāyi, pabbatasaṅkhepe udakarahado accho vippasanno anāvilo, tattha cakkhumā puriso tīre ṭhito passeyya sippisambukampi [sippikasambukampi (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] sakkharakaṭhalampi macchagumbampi carantampi tiṭṭhantampi. Tassa evamassa – ‘ayaṃ kho udakarahado accho vippasanno anāvilo, tatrime sippisambukāpi sakkharakaṭhalāpi macchagumbāpi carantipi tiṭṭhantipī’ti. Evameva kho, udāyi, akkhātā mayā sāvakānaṃ paṭipadā, yathāpaṭipannā me sāvakā āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharanti. Tatra ca pana me sāvakā bahū abhiññāvosānapāramippattā viharanti. Ayaṃ kho, udāyi, pañcamo dhammo yena mama sāvakā sakkaronti garuṃ karonti mānenti pūjenti, sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharanti.

259.Furthermore, Udāyi, I have declared to my disciples the path by which, when my disciples have followed it, they realize for themselves the destruction of the taints and dwell having attained the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life, having directly known and realized it for themselves. Just as, Udāyi, in a mountain depression there is a pool of water, clear, limpid, undisturbed, and a clear-sighted man standing on the bank would see shells and snails, gravel and pebbles, and schools of fish moving about or staying still. He would think: ‘This pool is clear, limpid, undisturbed, and here are these shells and snails, gravel and pebbles, and schools of fish moving about or staying still.’ In the same way, Udāyi, I have declared to my disciples the path by which, when my disciples have followed it, they realize for themselves the destruction of the taints and dwell having attained the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life, having directly known and realized it for themselves. And in this matter, many of my disciples abide having attained the ultimate perfection in direct knowledge. This, Udāyi, is the fifth quality because of which my disciples honor, respect, revere, and venerate me, and having honored and respected me, they dwell in dependence on me.

‘‘Ime kho, udāyi, pañca dhammā yehi mamaṃ sāvakā sakkaronti garuṃ karonti mānenti pūjenti, sakkatvā garuṃ katvā upanissāya viharantī’’ti.

These, Udāyi, are the five qualities by which my disciples honor, respect, revere, and venerate me; and having honored and respected me, they dwell in dependence on me.

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano sakuludāyī paribbājako bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

Thus spoke the Blessed One. The wanderer Sakuludāyī, delighted, rejoiced at the Blessed One's words.

Mahāsakuludāyisuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The Great Sakuludāyī Sutta, the seventh, is concluded.
