# 133. Lomasakaṅgiyabhaddekarattasuttaṃ

## 133. The Discourse on Lomasakaṅgiya and One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night

286. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā lomasakaṅgiyo [lomasakakaṅgiyo (ṭīkā)] sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Atha kho candano devaputto abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇo kevalakappaṃ nigrodhārāmaṃ obhāsetvā yenāyasmā lomasakaṅgiyo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho candano devaputto āyasmantaṃ lomasakaṅgiyaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘dhāresi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’’ti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’’ti? ‘‘Ahampi kho, bhikkhu, na dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Dhāresi pana tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’’ti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’’ti? ‘‘Dhāremi kho ahaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana tvaṃ, āvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’’ti? ‘‘Ekamidaṃ, bhikkhu, samayaṃ bhagavā devesu tāvatiṃsesu viharati pāricchattakamūle paṇḍukambalasilāyaṃ. Tatra bhagavā devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca abhāsi –

286.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. Now at that time, the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya was dwelling among the Sakyans in the Nigrodha Park at Kapilavatthu. Then, in the far reaches of the night, Candana the deva, of surpassing beauty, illuminating the entire Nigrodha Park, approached the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya. Having approached, he stood to one side. Standing to one side, Candana the deva said to the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya: “Do you, bhikkhu, remember the summary and the exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?” “I do not, friend, remember the summary and the exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. But do you, friend, remember the summary and the exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?” “I too, bhikkhu, do not remember the summary and the exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. But do you, bhikkhu, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?” “I do not, friend, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. But do you, friend, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?” “I do, bhikkhu, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night.” “But how, friend, do you remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?” “Once, bhikkhu, the Blessed One was dwelling among the Tāvatiṃsa devas at the foot of the Pāricchattaka tree on the Paṇḍukambala stone. There the Blessed One taught the summary and the exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night to the Tāvatiṃsa devas:

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya, nappaṭikaṅkhe anāgataṃ;Yadatītaṃ pahīnaṃ taṃ, appattañca anāgataṃ.

One should not pursue the past, nor anticipate the future. What is past is abandoned, and the future has not yet arrived.

‘‘Paccuppannañca yo dhammaṃ, tattha tattha vipassati;Asaṃhīraṃ asaṃkuppaṃ, taṃ vidvā manubrūhaye.

And whoever discerns the present phenomenon, here and there, unswerving, unshakeable—knowing that, one should develop it.

‘‘Ajjeva kiccamātappaṃ, ko jaññā maraṇaṃ suve;Na hi no saṅgaraṃ tena, mahāsenena maccunā.

Today is the ardent task. Who knows, death may come tomorrow? For there is no bargaining with Death and his mighty host.

‘‘Evaṃ vihāriṃ ātāpiṃ, ahorattamatanditaṃ;Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

The peaceful sage calls that person ‘one who has had a single excellent night’—one who dwells thus, ardent, untiring day and night.

‘‘Evaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhu, dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā. Uggaṇhāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; pariyāpuṇāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; dhārehi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Atthasaṃhito, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddeso ca vibhaṅgo ca ādibrahmacariyako’’ti. Idamavoca candano devaputto. Idaṃ vatvā tatthevantaradhāyi.

“Thus, bhikkhu, I remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. Learn, bhikkhu, the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night; master, bhikkhu, the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night; remember, bhikkhu, the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. The summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night, bhikkhu, is connected with the goal, fundamental to the holy life.” This is what Candana the deva said. Having said this, he vanished right there.

287. Atha kho āyasmā lomasakaṅgiyo tassā rattiyā accayena senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena sāvatthi tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena sāvatthi jetavanaṃ anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāmo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā lomasakaṅgiyo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

287.Then, when that night had passed, the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya arranged his lodging, took his bowl and robe, and set out on wanderings towards Sāvatthī. Wandering stage by stage, he arrived at Sāvatthī, at Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park, and approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Ekamidāhaṃ, bhante, samayaṃ sakkesu viharāmi kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Atha kho, bhante, aññataro devaputto abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇo kevalakappaṃ nigrodhārāmaṃ obhāsetvā yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho, bhante, so devaputto maṃ etadavoca – ‘dhāresi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’ti? Evaṃ vutte ahaṃ, bhante, taṃ devaputtaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’ti? ‘Ahampi kho, bhikkhu, na dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Dhāresi pana tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’ti? ‘Na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’ti? ‘Dhāremi kho ahaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’ti. ‘Yathā kathaṃ pana tvaṃ, āvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’ti? Ekamidaṃ, bhikkhu, samayaṃ bhagavā devesu tāvatiṃsesu viharati pāricchattakamūle paṇḍukambalasilāyaṃ. Tatra kho bhagavā devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca abhāsi –

“Once, venerable sir, I was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu, in the Nigrodha Park. Then, venerable sir, a certain deva’s son, as the night was far spent, with surpassing radiance illuminating the entire Nigrodha Park, approached me. Having approached, he stood to one side. Standing to one side, venerable sir, that deva’s son said to me: ‘Do you, bhikkhu, remember the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?’ When this was said, venerable sir, I replied to that deva’s son: ‘I do not, friend, remember the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. But do you, friend, remember the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?’ ‘I too, bhikkhu, do not remember the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. But do you, bhikkhu, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?’ ‘I do not, friend, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night. But do you, friend, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?’ ‘I do, bhikkhu, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night.’ ‘But how do you, friend, remember the verses of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night?’ ‘Once, bhikkhu, the Blessed One was dwelling among the Tāvatiṃsa devas at the foot of the Pāricchattaka tree on the Paṇḍukambala stone. There the Blessed One spoke to the Tāvatiṃsa devas about the summary and exposition of One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night:

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya…pe…Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

One should not pursue the past... The peaceful sage calls that person ‘one who has had a single excellent night.’

‘‘Evaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhu, dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā. Uggaṇhāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; pariyāpuṇāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; dhārehi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Atthasaṃhito, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddeso ca vibhaṅgo ca ādibrahmacariyako’ti. Idamavoca, bhante, so devaputto; idaṃ vatvā tatthevantaradhāyi. Sādhu me, bhante, bhagavā bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca desetū’’ti.

'Thus, bhikkhu, I remember these verses of the Fortunate One. Learn, bhikkhu, the outline and the exposition of the Fortunate One; master, bhikkhu, the outline and the exposition of the Fortunate One; remember, bhikkhu, the outline and the exposition of the Fortunate One. The outline and the exposition of the Fortunate One, bhikkhu, is connected with the meaning and is fundamental to the holy life.' That is what that devaputta said, venerable sir. Having said this, he vanished right there. 'It would be good, venerable sir, if the Blessed One would teach me the outline and the exposition of the Fortunate One.'

288. ‘‘Jānāsi pana tvaṃ, bhikkhu, taṃ devaputta’’nti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, bhante, jānāmi taṃ devaputta’’nti. ‘‘Candano nāma so, bhikkhu, devaputto. Candano, bhikkhu, devaputto aṭṭhiṃ katvā [aṭṭhikatvā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] manasikatvā sabbacetasā [sabbaṃ cetaso (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.), sabbaṃ cetasā (ka.)] samannāharitvā ohitasoto dhammaṃ suṇāti. Tena hi, bhikkhu, suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā lomasakaṅgiyo bhagavato paccassosi. Bhagavā etadavoca –

288."Do you know that devaputta, bhikkhu?" "No, venerable sir, I do not know that devaputta." "That devaputta, bhikkhu, is named Candana. Candana, bhikkhu, the devaputta, having made it his core, attending to it with all his heart, listening to the Dhamma with an attentive ear. Therefore, bhikkhu, listen and attend closely; I shall speak." "Yes, venerable sir," the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya, nappaṭikaṅkhe anāgataṃ;Yadatītaṃ pahīnaṃ taṃ, appattañca anāgataṃ.

"One should not pursue the past, nor long for the future. What is past is abandoned, and the future has not been reached.

‘‘Paccuppannañca yo dhammaṃ, tattha tattha vipassati;Asaṃhīraṃ asaṃkuppaṃ, taṃ vidvā manubrūhaye.

"But whoever sees the present Dhamma, here and there, unwavering, unshakeable—that, the wise one should develop.

‘‘Ajjeva kiccamātappaṃ, ko jaññā maraṇaṃ suve;Na hi no saṅgaraṃ tena, mahāsenena maccunā;

"Today the effort must be made. Who knows if death will come tomorrow? For there is no bargaining with Death, the mighty host.

‘‘Evaṃ vihāriṃ ātāpiṃ, ahorattamatanditaṃ;Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate muni’’.

"One who dwells thus ardently, tirelessly, day and night—that one, the peaceful sage declares, is truly 'one of a single auspicious night.'"

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhu, atītaṃ anvāgameti…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhu, atītaṃ anvāgameti. Kathañca, bhikkhu, atītaṃ nānvāgameti…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhu, atītaṃ nānvāgameti. Kathañca, bhikkhu, anāgataṃ paṭikaṅkhati…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhu, anāgataṃ paṭikaṅkhati. Kathañca, bhikkhu, anāgataṃ nappaṭikaṅkhati…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhu, anāgataṃ nappaṭikaṅkhati. Kathañca, bhikkhu, paccuppannesu dhammesu saṃhīrati…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhu, paccuppannesu dhammesu saṃhīrati. Kathañca, bhikkhu, paccuppannesu dhammesu na saṃhīrati…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhu, paccuppannesu dhammesu na saṃhīrati.

"And how, bhikkhu, does one pursue the past? ... Thus, bhikkhu, one pursues the past. And how, bhikkhu, does one not pursue the past? ... Thus, bhikkhu, one does not pursue the past. And how, bhikkhu, does one long for the future? ... Thus, bhikkhu, one longs for the future. And how, bhikkhu, does one not long for the future? ... Thus, bhikkhu, one does not long for the future. And how, bhikkhu, does one waver regarding present phenomena? ... Thus, bhikkhu, one wavers regarding present phenomena. And how, bhikkhu, does one not waver regarding present phenomena? ... Thus, bhikkhu, one does not waver regarding present phenomena.

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya, nappaṭikaṅkhe anāgataṃ;Yadatītaṃ pahīnaṃ taṃ, appattañca anāgataṃ.

"One should not pursue the past, nor long for the future. What is past is abandoned, and the future has not been reached.

‘‘Paccuppannañca yo dhammaṃ, tattha tattha vipassati;Asaṃhīraṃ asaṃkuppaṃ, taṃ vidvā manubrūhaye.

"But whoever sees the present Dhamma, here and there, unwavering, unshakeable—that, the wise one should develop.

‘‘Ajjeva kiccamātappaṃ, ko jaññā maraṇaṃ suve;Na hi no saṅgaraṃ tena, mahāsenena maccunā.

"Today the effort must be made. Who knows if death will come tomorrow? For there is no bargaining with Death, the mighty host.

‘‘Evaṃ vihāriṃ ātāpiṃ, ahorattamatanditaṃ;Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

"One who dwells thus ardently, tirelessly, day and night—that one, the peaceful sage declares, is truly 'one of a single auspicious night.'"

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā lomasakaṅgiyo bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

Thus spoke the Blessed One. The Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya was gratified and delighted by the Blessed One's words.

Lomasakaṅgiyabhaddekarattasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The Discourse on the Fortunate One by Lomasakaṅgiya (Lomasakaṅgiyabhaddekaratta Sutta) is concluded, the fourth.

5. Cūḷakammavibhaṅgasuttaṃ [subhasuttantipi vuccati]

## 133. The Shorter Exposition of Kamma Sutta

289. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane, anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Atha kho subho māṇavo todeyyaputto yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho subho māṇavo todeyyaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

289.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta's Grove, Anathapindika's Park. Then the young man Subha, Todeyya's son, approached the Blessed One; having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. After an exchange of friendly and courteous greetings, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the young man Subha, Todeyya's son, said this to the Blessed One:

‘‘Ko nu kho, bho gotama, hetu ko paccayo yena manussānaṃyeva sataṃ manussabhūtānaṃ dissanti hīnappaṇītatā? Dissanti hi, bho gotama, manussā appāyukā, dissanti dīghāyukā; dissanti bavhābādhā [bahvābādhā (syā. kaṃ. ka.)], dissanti appābādhā; dissanti dubbaṇṇā, dissanti vaṇṇavanto; dissanti appesakkhā, dissanti mahesakkhā; dissanti appabhogā, dissanti mahābhogā; dissanti nīcakulīnā, dissanti uccākulīnā; dissanti duppaññā, dissanti paññavanto [paññāvanto (sī. pī.)]. Ko nu kho, bho gotama, hetu ko paccayo yena manussānaṃyeva sataṃ manussabhūtānaṃ dissanti hīnappaṇītatā’’ti?

"Now, what is the cause, Master Gotama, what is the reason why, among human beings—being just human beings—there is seen such inferiority and superiority? For, Master Gotama, among humans some are seen to be short-lived, some long-lived; some are seen to be prone to illness, some free from illness; some are seen to be ugly, some beautiful; some are seen to be uninfluential, some influential; some are seen to be poor, some wealthy; some are seen to be of low birth, some of high birth; some are seen to be unintelligent, some intelligent. Now, what is the cause, Master Gotama, what is the reason why, among human beings—being just human beings—there is seen such inferiority and superiority?"

‘‘Kammassakā, māṇava, sattā kammadāyādā kammayonī kammabandhū [kammayoni kammabandhu (sī.)] kammappaṭisaraṇā. Kammaṃ satte vibhajati yadidaṃ – hīnappaṇītatāyāti. Na kho ahaṃ imassa bhoto gotamassa saṃkhittena bhāsitassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. Sādhu me bhavaṃ gotamo tathā dhammaṃ desetu yathā ahaṃ imassa bhoto gotamassa saṃkhittena bhāsitassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ ājāneyya’’nti.

"Beings are owners of their actions, young man, heirs to their actions, actions are their origin, actions are their relatives, and actions are their refuge. It is action that divides beings, that is, by inferiority and superiority.' 'I do not understand the detailed meaning of this brief statement of Master Gotama, which has not been analyzed in detail. It would be good if Master Gotama would teach me the Dhamma in such a way that I might understand the detailed meaning of this brief statement of Master Gotama.'

290. ‘‘Tena hi, māṇava, suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho subho māṇavo todeyyaputto bhagavato paccassosi. Bhagavā etadavoca –

290."Well then, young man, listen and attend closely; I shall speak." "Yes, Master," Subha the Todeyya's son replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Idha, māṇava, ekacco itthī vā puriso vā pāṇātipātī hoti luddo lohitapāṇi hatapahate niviṭṭho adayāpanno pāṇabhūtesu [sabbapāṇabhūtesu (sī. ka.)]. So tena kammena evaṃ samattena evaṃ samādinnena [samādiṇṇena (pī. ka.)] kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati. No ce kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, sace manussattaṃ āgacchati yattha yattha paccājāyati appāyuko hoti. Appāyukasaṃvattanikā esā, māṇava, paṭipadā yadidaṃ – pāṇātipātī hoti luddo lohitapāṇi hatapahate niviṭṭho adayāpanno pāṇabhūtesu.

"Here, young man, a certain woman or man kills living beings; they are brutal, bloody-handed, devoted to killing and striking, and merciless to living beings. Due to that action, thus completed and undertaken, at the breaking up of the body, after death, they are reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the lower realms, in hell. But if, at the breaking up of the body, after death, they are not reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the lower realms, in hell, and instead come to the human state, wherever they are reborn, they are short-lived. This is the course, young man, that leads to a short life—namely, being one who kills living beings; brutal, bloody-handed, devoted to killing and striking, and merciless to living beings."

‘‘Idha pana, māṇava, ekacco itthī vā puriso vā pāṇātipātaṃ pahāya pāṇātipātā paṭivirato hoti nihitadaṇḍo nihitasattho, lajjī dayāpanno sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharati. So tena kammena evaṃ samattena evaṃ samādinnena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati. No ce kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati, sace manussattaṃ āgacchati yattha yattha paccājāyati dīghāyuko hoti. Dīghāyukasaṃvattanikā esā, māṇava, paṭipadā yadidaṃ – pāṇātipātaṃ pahāya pāṇātipātā paṭivirato hoti nihitadaṇḍo nihitasattho, lajjī dayāpanno sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharati.

"But here, young man, a certain woman or man, having abandoned the destruction of life, abstains from destroying living beings. They have laid down the rod and the weapon, are scrupulous, compassionate, and dwell full of compassion for the welfare of all living beings. Due to that action, thus completed and thus undertaken, at the breakup of the body after death, they are reborn in a good destination, in the heavenly world. If they are not reborn in a good destination, in the heavenly world, after the breakup of the body after death, but instead come to human existence, wherever they are reborn, they are long-lived. This, young man, is the course that leads to a long life: abstaining from the destruction of life, having laid down the rod and the weapon, being scrupulous, compassionate, and dwelling full of compassion for the welfare of all living beings."

291. ‘‘Idha, māṇava, ekacco itthī vā puriso vā sattānaṃ viheṭhakajātiko hoti, pāṇinā vā leḍḍunā vā daṇḍena vā satthena vā. Beings are the owners of their kamma, young man, heirs of their kamma, they have kamma as their origin, kamma as their kin, and kamma as their refuge. It is kamma that differentiates beings in terms of inferiority and superiority.”

Evaṃ vutte, subho māṇavo todeyyaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama! Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya – ‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’ti; evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsakaṃ maṃ bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti.

When this was said, the young man Subha, son of Todeyya, said to the Blessed One: “Excellent, Master Gotama, excellent! Just as if one were to set upright what was overturned, or reveal what was hidden, or show the way to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness, so that those with eyes may see forms—in the same way the Dhamma has been made clear by Master Gotama in many ways. I go to Master Gotama for refuge, and to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of bhikkhus. May Master Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forward, for as long as life lasts.”

Cūḷakammavibhaṅgasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The Shorter Exposition of Kamma, the fifth, is finished.
