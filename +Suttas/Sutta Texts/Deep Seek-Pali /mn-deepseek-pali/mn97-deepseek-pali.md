# 97. Dhanañjānisuttaṃ

## 97. The Dhanañjāni Sutta

445. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā sāriputto dakkhiṇāgirismiṃ cārikaṃ carati mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ. Atha kho aññataro bhikkhu rājagahe vassaṃvuṭṭho [vassaṃvuttho (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] yena dakkhiṇāgiri yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā sāriputtena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho taṃ bhikkhuṃ āyasmā sāriputto etadavoca – ‘‘kaccāvuso, bhagavā arogo ca balavā cā’’ti? ‘‘Arogo cāvuso, bhagavā balavā cā’’ti. ‘‘Kacci panāvuso, bhikkhusaṅgho arogo ca balavā cā’’ti? ‘‘Bhikkhusaṅghopi kho, āvuso, arogo ca balavā cā’’ti. ‘‘Ettha, āvuso, taṇḍulapālidvārāya dhanañjāni [dhānañjāni (sī. pī.)] nāma brāhmaṇo atthi. Kaccāvuso, dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo arogo ca balavā cā’’ti? ‘‘Dhanañjānipi kho, āvuso, brāhmaṇo arogo ca balavā cā’’ti. ‘‘Kacci panāvuso, dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo appamatto’’ti? ‘‘Kuto panāvuso, dhanañjānissa brāhmaṇassa appamādo? Dhanañjāni, āvuso, brāhmaṇo rājānaṃ nissāya brāhmaṇagahapatike vilumpati, brāhmaṇagahapatike nissāya rājānaṃ vilumpati. Yāpissa bhariyā saddhā saddhakulā ānītā sāpi kālaṅkatā; aññāssa bhariyā assaddhā assaddhakulā ānītā’’. ‘‘Dussutaṃ vatāvuso, assumha, dussutaṃ vatāvuso, assumha; ye mayaṃ dhanañjāniṃ brāhmaṇaṃ pamattaṃ assumha. Appeva ca nāma mayaṃ kadāci karahaci dhanañjāninā brāhmaṇena saddhiṃ samāgaccheyyāma, appeva nāma siyā kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti?

445.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrel Sanctuary. Now at that time, the Venerable Sāriputta was wandering in the Southern Hills with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Then a certain monk who had spent the rains in Rājagaha approached the Southern Hills, where the Venerable Sāriputta was. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Venerable Sāriputta. When the courteous and amiable talk was finished, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Sāriputta said to that monk: "I hope, friend, the Blessed One is free from illness and strong?" "The Blessed One is free from illness and strong, friend." "I hope the Saṅgha of bhikkhus is also free from illness and strong?" "The Saṅgha of bhikkhus is also free from illness and strong, friend." "Here, friend, at Taṇḍulapāli there is a brahmin named Dhanañjāni. I hope, friend, the brahmin Dhanañjāni is free from illness and strong?" "The brahmin Dhanañjāni is also free from illness and strong, friend." "But I hope, friend, the brahmin Dhanañjāni is heedful?" "How could the brahmin Dhanañjāni be heedful, friend? The brahmin Dhanañjāni, relying on the king, robs the brahmin householders; relying on the brahmin householders, he robs the king. His wife, who was faithful and came from a faithful family, has passed away; another wife, who is faithless and comes from a faithless family, has been brought in." "It is bad for us, friend, that we have heard this; it is bad for us, friend, that we have heard this—that the brahmin Dhanañjāni is heedless. Perhaps someday we might meet with the brahmin Dhanañjāni; perhaps there might be some conversation."

446. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto dakkhiṇāgirismiṃ yathābhirantaṃ viharitvā yena rājagahaṃ tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena rājagahaṃ tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ āyasmā sāriputto rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya rājagahaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Tena kho pana samayena dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo bahinagare gāvo goṭṭhe duhāpeti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto rājagahe piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto yena dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo tenupasaṅkami. Addasā kho dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ito, bho sāriputta, payo, pīyataṃ tāva bhattassa kālo bhavissatī’’ti. ‘‘Alaṃ, brāhmaṇa. Kataṃ me ajja bhattakiccaṃ. Amukasmiṃ me rukkhamūle divāvihāro bhavissati. Tattha āgaccheyyāsī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo āyasmato sāriputtassa paccassosi. Atha kho dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo pacchābhattaṃ bhuttapātarāso yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā sāriputtena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho dhanañjāniṃ brāhmaṇaṃ āyasmā sāriputto etadavoca – ‘‘kaccāsi, dhanañjāni, appamatto’’ti? ‘‘Kuto, bho sāriputta, amhākaṃ appamādo yesaṃ no mātāpitaro posetabbā, puttadāro posetabbo, dāsakammakarā posetabbā, mittāmaccānaṃ mittāmaccakaraṇīyaṃ kātabbaṃ, ñātisālohitānaṃ ñātisālohitakaraṇīyaṃ kātabbaṃ, atithīnaṃ atithikaraṇīyaṃ kātabbaṃ, pubbapetānaṃ pubbapetakaraṇīyaṃ kātabbaṃ, devatānaṃ devatākaraṇīyaṃ kātabbaṃ, rañño rājakaraṇīyaṃ kātabbaṃ, ayampi kāyo pīṇetabbo brūhetabbo’’ti?

446.Then the Venerable Sāriputta, having stayed in the Southern Hills as long as he pleased, set out on a journey to Rājagaha. Traveling stage by stage, he arrived at Rājagaha. There the Venerable Sāriputta stayed in Rājagaha, at the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels' Sanctuary. Then the Venerable Sāriputta, having dressed in the morning, took his bowl and robe and entered Rājagaha for alms. Now at that time the brahmin Dhanañjāni was having cows milked in a cow pen outside the city. Then the Venerable Sāriputta, having walked for alms in Rājagaha, after the meal, on his return from the alms round, approached the brahmin Dhanañjāni. The brahmin Dhanañjāni saw the Venerable Sāriputta coming from afar. Seeing him, he approached the Venerable Sāriputta and said: "Here, Master Sāriputta, drink some milk. It will soon be time for the meal." "Enough, brahmin. My meal for today is done. I will spend the day at the foot of a certain tree. You may come there." "Very well, Master," replied the brahmin Dhanañjāni to the Venerable Sāriputta. Then the brahmin Dhanañjāni, after his breakfast, approached the Venerable Sāriputta. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Venerable Sāriputta. When the courteous and amiable talk was over, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the brahmin Dhanañjāni: "I hope, Dhanañjāni, you are diligent?" "How, Master Sāriputta, can we be diligent? We must support our parents, support our wives and children, support our servants and workers, do our duties to friends and companions, do our duties to relatives, do our duties to guests, do our duties to the departed, do our duties to the deities, do our duties to the king, and this body must be nourished and strengthened."

447. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco mātāpitūnaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho mātāpitūnaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, mātāpitaro vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

447."What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here were to behave unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of his parents. Because of this unrighteous and unjust behavior, the hell-wardens would drag him off to hell. Would he be able to say, 'I behaved unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of my parents, may the hell-wardens not [drag] me to hell'? Or would his parents be able to say, 'He behaved unrighteously and unjustly for our sake; may the hell-wardens not [drag] him to hell'?" "No, venerable Sāriputta. Even as he cries out, the hell-wardens would throw him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco puttadārassa hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho puttadārassa hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, puttadāro vā panassa labheyya ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here acts unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of wife and children, and because of that unrighteous and unjust conduct, the hell-wardens were to drag him to hell. Could he then obtain [release] by saying, ‘I acted unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of wife and children—may the hell-wardens not [drag] me to hell’? Or could his wife and children obtain [release for him] by saying, ‘He acted unrighteously and unjustly for our sake—may the hell-wardens not [drag] him to hell’?" "No, indeed, venerable Sāriputta. Rather, while he is crying out, the hell-wardens would throw him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco dāsakammakaraporisassa hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho dāsakammakaraporisassa hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, dāsakammakaraporisā vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here acts unrighteously and unjustly because of slaves, workers, and servants. Then, because of that unrighteous and unjust conduct, the wardens of hell drag him off to hell. Could he then obtain [release] by saying, ‘I acted unrighteously and unjustly because of slaves, workers, and servants; may the wardens of hell not [drag] me to hell!’ Or would his slaves, workers, and servants obtain [release for him] by saying, ‘This one acted unrighteously and unjustly because of us; may the wardens of hell not [drag] him to hell!’?" "No, venerable Sāriputta. But rather, while he cries out, the wardens of hell would hurl him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco mittāmaccānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho mittāmaccānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, mittāmaccā vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here acts unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of friends and associates. Because of that unrighteous and unjust conduct, the wardens of hell drag him to hell. Would he obtain [release] by saying, ‘I acted unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of friends and associates; may the wardens of hell not [drag] me to hell’? Or would his friends and associates obtain [release for him] by saying, ‘He acted unrighteously and unjustly for our sake; may the wardens of hell not [drag] him to hell’?" "No, indeed, Venerable Sāriputta. Rather, even as he cries out, the wardens of hell would hurl him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco ñātisālohitānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho ñātisālohitānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, ñātisālohitā vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here were to act unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of relatives and blood-kin, and because of that unrighteous and unjust conduct, the wardens of hell were to drag him to hell. Would he be able to say, ‘I acted unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of relatives and blood-kin—may the wardens of hell not [drag] me to hell’? Or would his relatives and blood-kin be able to say, ‘He acted unrighteously and unjustly for our sake—may the wardens of hell not [drag] him to hell’?" "No, indeed, Venerable Sāriputta. Rather, while he cries out, the wardens of hell would throw him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco atithīnaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho atithīnaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, atithī vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here acts unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of guests; and because of that unrighteous and unjust conduct, the hell-wardens were to drag him to hell. Would he succeed in saying, ‘I acted unrighteously and unjustly for the sake of guests—may the hell-wardens not [drag] me to hell’? Or would his guests succeed in saying, ‘He acted unrighteously and unjustly for our sake—may the hell-wardens not [drag] him to hell’?" "No, venerable Sāriputta. Rather, while he is crying out, the hell-wardens would hurl him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco pubbapetānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho pubbapetānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, pubbapetā vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here were to behave unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of deceased relatives, and because of that unrighteous and unprincipled behavior, the wardens of hell were to drag him to hell. Could he say, ‘I behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of my deceased relatives, may the wardens of hell not drag me to hell’? Or could his deceased relatives say, ‘He behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for our sake, may the wardens of hell not drag him to hell’?” "No, indeed, Venerable Sāriputta. But rather, even as he cries out, the wardens of hell would cast him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco devatānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho devatānaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, devatā vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here were to behave unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of the gods, and because of that unrighteous and unprincipled behavior, the wardens of hell were to drag him to hell. Could he say, ‘I behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of the gods, may the wardens of hell not drag me to hell’? Or could the gods say, ‘He behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for our sake, may the wardens of hell not drag him to hell’?” "No, indeed, Venerable Sāriputta. But rather, even as he cries out, the wardens of hell would cast him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco rañño hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho rañño hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, rājā vā panassa labheyya ‘eso kho amhākaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here were to behave unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of a king, and because of that unrighteous and unprincipled behavior, the wardens of hell were to drag him to hell. Could he say, ‘I behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of the king, may the wardens of hell not drag me to hell’? Or could the king say, ‘He behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for my sake, may the wardens of hell not drag him to hell’?” "No, indeed, Venerable Sāriputta. But rather, even as he cries out, the wardens of hell would cast him into hell."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, idhekacco kāyassa pīṇanāhetu brūhanāhetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, tamenaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu nirayaṃ nirayapālā upakaḍḍheyyuṃ. Labheyya nu kho so ‘ahaṃ kho kāyassa pīṇanāhetu brūhanāhetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosiṃ, mā maṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’ti, pare vā panassa labheyyuṃ ‘eso kho kāyassa pīṇanāhetu brūhanāhetu adhammacārī visamacārī ahosi, mā naṃ nirayaṃ nirayapālā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho sāriputta. Atha kho naṃ vikkandantaṃyeva niraye nirayapālā pakkhipeyyuṃ’’.

"What do you think, Dhanañjāni? Suppose someone here were to behave unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of pleasing and strengthening the body, and because of that unrighteous and unprincipled behavior, the wardens of hell were to drag him to hell. Could he say, ‘I behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of pleasing and strengthening the body, may the wardens of hell not drag me to hell’? Or could others say, ‘He behaved unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of pleasing and strengthening the body, may the wardens of hell not drag him to hell’?” "No, indeed, Venerable Sāriputta. But rather, even as he cries out, the wardens of hell would cast him into hell."

448. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, yo vā mātāpitūnaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, yo vā mātāpitūnaṃ hetu dhammacārī samacārī assa; katamaṃ seyyo’’ti? ‘‘Yo hi, bho sāriputta, mātāpitūnaṃ hetu adhammacārī visamacārī assa, na taṃ seyyo; yo ca kho, bho sāriputta, mātāpitūnaṃ hetu dhammacārī samacārī assa, tadevettha seyyo. Adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhi, bho sāriputta, dhammacariyāsamacariyā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Atthi kho, dhanañjāni, aññesaṃ hetukā dhammikā kammantā, yehi sakkā mātāpitaro ceva posetuṃ, na ca pāpakammaṃ kātuṃ, puññañca paṭipadaṃ paṭipajjituṃ.

448."What do you think, Dhanañjāni? If someone were to behave unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of their parents, or if someone were to behave righteously and principledly for the sake of their parents—which is better?" "Indeed, Venerable Sāriputta, if someone were to behave unrighteously and unprincipledly for the sake of their parents, that would not be better. But if someone, Venerable Sāriputta, were to behave righteously and principledly for the sake of their parents, that indeed is better. Righteous and principled conduct, Venerable Sāriputta, is better than unrighteous and unprincipled conduct." "There are, Dhanañjāni, other righteous undertakings motivated by other causes by which one can both support one’s parents without doing evil deeds and while practicing a meritorious path."

449.Then the brahmin Dhanañjāni, having rejoiced and expressed his appreciation for Venerable Sāriputta’s words, rose from his seat and departed. He pays homage at the Blessed One’s feet with his head.’ Then approach Venerable Sāriputta. Having approached, pay homage at Venerable Sāriputta’s feet with your head in my name, saying: ‘Venerable sir, the brahmin Dhanañjāni is sick, afflicted, and gravely ill. He pays homage at Venerable Sāriputta’s feet with his head.’ And say this: ‘It would be good, venerable sir, if Venerable Sāriputta, out of compassion, would come to the residence of the brahmin Dhanañjāni.’” “Yes, sir,” replied that man to the brahmin Dhanañjāni. Then he approached the Blessed One, paid homage, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, that man said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, the brahmin Dhanañjāni is sick, afflicted, and gravely ill. He pays homage at the Blessed One’s feet with his head.” Then he approached Venerable Sāriputta, paid homage, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, that man said to Venerable Sāriputta: “Venerable sir, the brahmin Dhanañjāni is sick, afflicted, and gravely ill. He pays homage at Venerable Sāriputta’s feet with his head and says this: ‘It would be good, venerable sir, if Venerable Sāriputta, out of compassion, would come to the residence of the brahmin Dhanañjāni.’” Venerable Sāriputta consented in silence.

450. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena dhanañjānissa brāhmaṇassa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Na me, bho sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ, na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti. Abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo. Seyyathāpi, bho sāriputta, balavā puriso daḷhena varattakkhaṇḍena [varattabandhanena (sī. pī.)] sīse sīsaveṭhaṃ dadeyya; evameva kho, bho sāriputta, adhimattā sīse sīsavedanā. Na me, bho sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti. Abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo. Seyyathāpi, bho sāriputta, dakkho goghātako vā goghātakantevāsī vā tiṇhena govikantanena kucchiṃ parikanteyya; evameva kho, bho sāriputta, adhimattā vātā kucchiṃ parikantanti. Na me, bho sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ, na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti. Abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo. Seyyathāpi, bho sāriputta, dve balavanto purisā dubbalataraṃ purisaṃ nānābāhāsu gahetvā aṅgārakāsuyā santāpeyyuṃ samparitāpeyyuṃ; evameva kho, bho sāriputta, adhimatto kāyasmiṃ ḍāho. Na me, bho sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti. Abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo’’ti.

450.Then Venerable Sāriputta, having dressed, taking his bowl and robe, went to the dwelling of the brahmin Dhanañjāni. Having approached, he sat down on the seat prepared for him. Sitting there, Venerable Sāriputta said to the brahmin Dhanañjāni: “I hope you are comfortable, Dhanañjāni, I hope you are able to manage? I hope your painful feelings are decreasing, not increasing? Is a decrease apparent, and not an increase?” “I am not comfortable, Venerable Sāriputta, I am not able to manage. My painful feelings are increasing, not decreasing. An increase is apparent, and not a decrease. Just as, Venerable Sāriputta, a strong man might crush one’s head with a sharp blade, so too, violent winds are pounding my head. I am not comfortable, Venerable Sāriputta, I am not able to manage. My painful feelings are increasing, not decreasing. An increase is apparent, and not a decrease. Just as, Venerable Sāriputta, a strong man might tie a tight leather strap around one’s head, so too, there is an extreme headache in my head. I am not comfortable, Venerable Sāriputta, I am not able to manage. My painful feelings are increasing, not decreasing. An increase is apparent, and not a decrease. Just as, Venerable Sāriputta, a skilled butcher or a butcher’s apprentice might carve open one’s belly with a sharp butcher’s knife, so too, violent winds are carving up my belly. I am not comfortable, Venerable Sāriputta, I am not able to manage. My painful feelings are increasing, not decreasing. An increase is apparent, and not a decrease. Just as, Venerable Sāriputta, two strong men might seize a weaker man by both arms and heat him, scorching him, over a pit of coals, so too, there is an extreme burning in my body. I am not comfortable, Venerable Sāriputta, I am not able to manage. My painful feelings are increasing, not decreasing. An increase is apparent, and not a decrease.”

451. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – nirayo vā tiracchānayoni vā’’ti? ‘‘Nirayā, bho sāriputta, tiracchānayoni seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – tiracchānayoni vā pettivisayo vā’’ti? ‘‘Tiracchānayoniyā, bho sāriputta, pettivisayo seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – pettivisayo vā manussā vā’’ti? ‘‘Pettivisayā, bho sāriputta, manussā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – manussā vā cātumahārājikā [cātummahārājikā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] vā devā’’ti? ‘‘Manussehi, bho sāriputta, cātumahārājikā devā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – cātumahārājikā vā devā tāvatiṃsā vā devā’’ti? ‘‘Cātumahārājikehi, bho sāriputta, devehi tāvatiṃsā devā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – tāvatiṃsā vā devā yāmā vā devā’’ti? ‘‘Tāvatiṃsehi, bho sāriputta, devehi yāmā devā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – yāmā vā devā tusitā vā devā’’ti? ‘‘Yāmehi, bho sāriputta, devehi tusitā devā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – tusitā vā devā nimmānaratī vā devā’’ti? ‘‘Tusitehi, bho sāriputta, devehi nimmānaratī devā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo – nimmānaratī vā devā paranimmitavasavattī vā devā’’ti? ‘‘Nimmānaratīhi, bho sāriputta, devehi paranimmitavasavattī devā seyyo’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, dhanañjāni, katamaṃ seyyo paranimmitavasavattī vā devā brahmaloko vā’’ti? ‘‘‘Brahmaloko’ti [bhavaṃ sāriputto āhāti, katamaṃ sāriputto āha brahmalokoti. (ka.)] – bhavaṃ sāriputto āha; ‘brahmaloko’ti – bhavaṃ sāriputto āhā’’ti [bhavaṃ sāriputto āhāti, katamaṃ sāriputto āha brahmalokoti. (ka.)].

451."What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—hell or the animal realm?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the animal realm is better than hell." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the animal realm or the realm of ghosts?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the realm of ghosts is better than the animal realm." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the realm of ghosts or humans?" "Venerable Sāriputta, humans are better than the realm of ghosts." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—humans or the gods of the Four Great Kings?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the gods of the Four Great Kings are better than humans." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the gods of the Four Great Kings or the Tāvatiṃsa gods?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the Tāvatiṃsa gods are better than the gods of the Four Great Kings." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the Tāvatiṃsa gods or the Yāma gods?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the Yāma gods are better than the Tāvatiṃsa gods." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the Yāma gods or the Tusita gods?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the Tusita gods are better than the Yāma gods." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the Tusita gods or the Nimmānaratī gods?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the Nimmānaratī gods are better than the Tusita gods." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the Nimmānaratī gods or the Paranimmitavasavattī gods?" "Venerable Sāriputta, the Paranimmitavasavattī gods are better than the Nimmānaratī gods." "What do you think, Dhanañjāni, which is better—the Paranimmitavasavattī gods or the Brahmā world?" "'The Brahmā world,' Venerable Sāriputta says; 'the Brahmā world,' Venerable Sāriputta says."

Atha kho āyasmato sāriputtassa etadahosi – ‘‘ime kho brāhmaṇā brahmalokādhimuttā. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ dhanañjānissa brāhmaṇassa brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggaṃ deseyya’’nti. ‘‘Brahmānaṃ te, dhanañjāni, sahabyatāya maggaṃ desessāmi; taṃ suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo āyasmato sāriputtassa paccassosi. Āyasmā sāriputto etadavoca – ‘‘katamo ca, dhanañjāni, brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggo? Idha, dhanañjāni, bhikkhu mettāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharati, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ; iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ mettāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyābajjhena pharitvā viharati. Ayaṃ kho, dhanañjāni, brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggo’’.

Then it occurred to the Venerable Sāriputta: “These brahmins are devoted to the Brahmā world. Why don’t I teach Dhanañjāni the brahmin the path to fellowship with Brahmā?” “Dhanañjāni, I will teach you the path to fellowship with Brahmā. Listen carefully, pay close attention, and I will speak.” “Yes, sir,” Dhanañjāni the brahmin replied to the Venerable Sāriputta. The Venerable Sāriputta said this: “And what, Dhanañjāni, is the path to fellowship with Brahmā? Here, Dhanañjāni, a bhikkhu dwells pervading one direction with a mind imbued with loving-kindness, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, completely, he dwells pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with loving-kindness—abundant, exalted, measureless, without hostility and without ill-will. This, Dhanañjāni, is the path to fellowship with Brahmā.”

452. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, dhanañjāni, bhikkhu karuṇāsahagatena cetasā…pe… muditāsahagatena cetasā… upekkhāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharati, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ; iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ upekkhāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyābajjhena pharitvā viharati. Ayaṃ kho, dhanañjāni, brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggo’’ti. Tena hi, bho sāriputta, mama vacanena bhagavato pāde sirasā vandāhi – ‘dhanañjāni, bhante, brāhmaṇo ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So bhagavato pāde sirasā vandatī’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto dhanañjāniṃ brāhmaṇaṃ sati uttarikaraṇīye hīne brahmaloke patiṭṭhāpetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi. Atha kho dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo acirapakkante āyasmante sāriputte kālamakāsi, brahmalokañca upapajji.

452."Furthermore, Dhanañjāni, a monk dwells pervading one direction with a mind imbued with compassion... with a mind imbued with sympathetic joy... with a mind imbued with equanimity, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth; thus above, below, across, everywhere, in every way, he dwells pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with equanimity—vast, expansive, immeasurable, without enmity, without ill will. This, Dhanañjāni, is the path to fellowship with Brahmā." "Then, Venerable Sāriputta, in my name, bow with your head at the Blessed One’s feet and say: ‘The brahmin Dhanañjāni, venerable sir, is afflicted, suffering, gravely ill. He bows with his head at the Blessed One’s feet.’" Then the Venerable Sāriputta, having established the brahmin Dhanañjāni in an inferior Brahmā world, though there was still something further to be done, rose from his seat and departed. Not long after the Venerable Sāriputta had left, the brahmin Dhanañjāni passed away and was reborn in the Brahmā world.

453. Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘eso, bhikkhave, sāriputto dhanañjāniṃ brāhmaṇaṃ sati uttarikaraṇīye hīne brahmaloke patiṭṭhāpetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkanto’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami, upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi, ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘dhanañjāni, bhante, brāhmaṇo ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno, so bhagavato pāde sirasā vandatī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ pana tvaṃ sāriputta dhanañjāniṃ brāhmaṇaṃ sati uttarikaraṇīye hīne brahmaloke patiṭṭhāpetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkanto’’ti? ‘‘Mayhaṃ kho, bhante, evaṃ ahosi – ‘ime kho brāhmaṇā brahmalokādhimuttā, yaṃnūnāhaṃ dhanañjānissa brāhmaṇassa brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggaṃ deseyya’nti. ‘‘Kālaṅkatoca [kālaṅkatova (syā. kaṃ. ka.)], sāriputta, dhanañjāni brāhmaṇo, brahmalokañca upapanno’’ti.

453.Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "bhikkhus, Sāriputta has left after establishing the brahmin Dhanañjāni, while there was still something further to be done, in an inferior Brahmā world." Then the Venerable Sāriputta approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, sat down to one side, and said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, the brahmin Dhanañjāni was sick, suffering, gravely ill, and he bows his head at the Blessed One’s feet." "But Sāriputta, did you leave after establishing the brahmin Dhanañjāni, while there was still something further to be done, in an inferior Brahmā world?" "Venerable sir, it occurred to me: ‘These brahmins are devoted to the Brahmā world. Why don’t I teach Dhanañjāni the path to fellowship with Brahmā?’" "Dhanañjāni the brahmin has passed away, Sāriputta, and has been reborn in the Brahmā world."

Dhanañjānisuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

The seventh discourse, the Dhanañjāni Sutta, is complete.
