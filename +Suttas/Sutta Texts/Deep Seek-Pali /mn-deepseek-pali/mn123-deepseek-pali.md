# 123. Bākulasuttaṃ

## 123. The Discourse on Bākula

209. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā bākulo [bakkulo (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Atha kho acelakassapo āyasmato bākulassa purāṇagihisahāyo yenāyasmā bākulo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā bākulena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho acelakassapo āyasmantaṃ bākulaṃ etadavoca –

209.Thus have I heard. At one time the Venerable Bākula was dwelling at Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels' Sanctuary. Then the naked ascetic Kassapa, a former householder acquaintance of the Venerable Bākula, approached the Venerable Bākula. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Venerable Bākula. Having exchanged courteous and amiable conversation, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the naked ascetic Kassapa said to the Venerable Bākula:

‘‘Kīvaciraṃ pabbajitosi, āvuso bākulā’’ti? ‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassā’’ti. ‘‘Imehi pana te, āvuso bākula, asītiyā vassehi katikkhattuṃ methuno dhammo paṭisevito’’ti? ‘‘Na kho maṃ, āvuso kassapa, evaṃ pucchitabbaṃ – ‘imehi pana te, āvuso bākula, asītiyā vassehi katikkhattuṃ methuno dhammo paṭisevito’ti. Evañca kho maṃ, āvuso kassapa, pucchitabbaṃ – ‘imehi pana te, āvuso bākula, asītiyā vassehi katikkhattuṃ kāmasaññā uppannapubbā’’’ti? ( ) [(imehi pana te āvuso bakkula asītiyo vassehi katikkhattuṃ kāmasaññā uppannapubbāti.) (sī. pī.)]

"How long have you been ordained, friend Bākula?" "It has been eighty years, friend, since I was ordained." "In these eighty years, friend Bākula, how many times have you engaged in the sexual act?" "You should not ask me, friend Kassapa, like this: 'In these eighty years, friend Bākula, how many times have you engaged in the sexual act?' Rather, you should ask me like this: 'In these eighty years, friend Bākula, how many times has a perception connected with sensual desire arisen in you?'"

210. ‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi kāmasaññaṃ uppannapubbaṃ. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo asītiyā vassehi nābhijānāti kāmasaññaṃ uppannapubbaṃ idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema.

210."For eighty years, friend, since I went forth, I do not recall any sensual perception ever arising. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall any sensual perception arising in eighty years—this also we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi byāpādasaññaṃ…pe… vihiṃsāsaññaṃ uppannapubbaṃ. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo asītiyā vassehi nābhijānāti vihiṃsāsaññaṃ uppannapubbaṃ, idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema.

"For eighty years, friend, since I went forth, I do not recall any perception of ill will... or any perception of cruelty ever arising. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall any perception of cruelty arising in eighty years—this too we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi kāmavitakkaṃ uppannapubbaṃ. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo asītiyā vassehi nābhijānāti kāmavitakkaṃ uppannapubbaṃ, idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema.

"For eighty years, friend, since I went forth, I do not recall ever having a sensual thought arise. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall ever having a sensual thought arise in eighty years—this also we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi byāpādavitakkaṃ…pe… vihiṃsāvitakkaṃ uppannapubbaṃ. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo asītiyā vassehi nābhijānāti vihiṃsāvitakkaṃ uppannapubbaṃ, idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema.

"For eighty years, friend, since I went forth, I do not recall a thought of ill will... or a thought of harmfulness ever arising. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall a thought of harmfulness arising in eighty years—this also we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

211. ‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi gahapaticīvaraṃ sāditā. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo asītiyā vassehi nābhijānāti gahapaticīvaraṃ sāditā, idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema.

211."For eighty years, friend, since I went forth, I do not recall ever having enjoyed a robe provided by a householder. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall ever having enjoyed a robe provided by a householder in eighty years—this also we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi satthena cīvaraṃ chinditā. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo asītiyā vassehi nābhijānāti satthena cīvaraṃ chinditā…pe… dhārema.

"For eighty years, friend, since I went forth, I do not recall cutting a robe with a knife. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall cutting a robe with a knife in eighty years—this also we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi sūciyā cīvaraṃ sibbitā…pe… nābhijānāmi rajanena cīvaraṃ rajitā… nābhijānāmi kathine [kaṭhine (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] cīvaraṃ sibbitā… nābhijānāmi sabrahmacārīnaṃ cīvarakamme vicāritā [sabrahmacārī cīvarakamme byāpāritā (sī. pī.)] … nābhijānāmi nimantanaṃ sāditā… nābhijānāmi evarūpaṃ cittaṃ uppannapubbaṃ – ‘aho vata maṃ koci nimanteyyā’ti… nābhijānāmi antaraghare nisīditā… nābhijānāmi antaraghare bhuñjitā… nābhijānāmi mātugāmassa anubyañjanaso nimittaṃ gahetā… nābhijānāmi mātugāmassa dhammaṃ desitā antamaso catuppadampi gāthaṃ… nābhijānāmi bhikkhunupassayaṃ upasaṅkamitā… nābhijānāmi bhikkhuniyā dhammaṃ desitā… nābhijānāmi sikkhamānāya dhammaṃ desitā… nābhijānāmi sāmaṇeriyā dhammaṃ desitā… nābhijānāmi pabbājetā… nābhijānāmi upasampādetā… nābhijānāmi nissayaṃ dātā… nābhijānāmi sāmaṇeraṃ upaṭṭhāpetā… nābhijānāmi jantāghare nhāyitā… nābhijānāmi cuṇṇena nhāyitā… nābhijānāmi sabrahmacārīgattaparikamme vicāritā [byāpāritā (sī. pī.)] … nābhijānāmi ābādhaṃ uppannapubbaṃ, antamaso gaddūhanamattampi… nābhijānāmi bhesajjaṃ upaharitā, antamaso haritakikhaṇḍampi… nābhijānāmi apassenakaṃ apassayitā… nābhijānāmi seyyaṃ kappetā. Yaṃpāyasmā…pe… dhārema.

"Friend, in my eighty years since going forth, I do not recall ever sewing a robe with a needle... I do not recall ever dyeing a robe with dye... I do not recall ever sewing a robe for the Kathina... I do not recall ever participating in robe-making discussions with fellow monastics... I do not recall ever accepting an invitation... I do not recall ever having such a thought arise—‘If only someone would invite me!’... I do not recall ever sitting inside a house... I do not recall ever eating inside a house... I do not recall ever taking notice of a woman’s distinctive features... I do not recall ever teaching the Dhamma to a woman, not even a four-line verse... I do not recall ever visiting a nun’s dwelling... I do not recall ever teaching the Dhamma to a nun... I do not recall ever teaching the Dhamma to a female probationer... I do not recall ever teaching the Dhamma to a female novice... I do not recall ever giving the going forth... I do not recall ever giving full ordination... I do not recall ever giving dependence... I do not recall ever attending to a novice... I do not recall ever bathing in a hot room... I do not recall ever bathing with bath powder... I do not recall ever participating in discussions about body care with fellow monastics... I do not recall ever falling ill, not even for as long as it takes to milk a cow... I do not recall ever taking medicine, not even a piece of myrobalan... I do not recall ever leaning against a back-rest... I do not recall ever arranging a bed. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall these things—this also we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

‘‘Asīti me, āvuso, vassāni pabbajitassa nābhijānāmi gāmantasenāsane vassaṃ upagantā. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo asītiyā vassehi nābhijānāti gāmantasenāsane vassaṃ upagantā, idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema.

"Friend, in the eighty years since I went forth, I do not recall ever spending the rains in a village monastery. That the Venerable Bākula does not recall, in eighty years, spending the rains in a village monastery—this too we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

‘‘Sattāhameva kho ahaṃ, āvuso, saraṇo raṭṭhapiṇḍaṃ bhuñjiṃ; atha aṭṭhamiyaṃ aññā udapādi. Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo sattāhameva saraṇo raṭṭhapiṇḍaṃ bhuñji; atha aṭṭhamiyaṃ aññā udapādi idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema.

"For seven days, friend, I ate almsfood while still subject to defilements; then on the eighth day, final knowledge arose. That the Venerable Bākula ate almsfood while still subject to defilements for seven days, and then final knowledge arose on the eighth day—this too we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

212. ‘‘Labheyyāhaṃ, āvuso bākula, imasmiṃ dhammavinaye pabbajjaṃ, labheyyaṃ upasampada’’nti. Alattha kho acelakassapo imasmiṃ dhammavinaye pabbajjaṃ, alattha upasampadaṃ. Acirūpasampanno panāyasmā kassapo eko vūpakaṭṭho appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto nacirasseva – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihāsi. ‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti abbhaññāsi. Aññataro kho panāyasmā kassapo arahataṃ ahosi.

212."Friend Bākula, may I obtain the going forth in this Dhamma and Discipline, may I obtain full ordination." Then the naked ascetic Kassapa obtained the going forth in this Dhamma and Discipline, he obtained full ordination. And not long after his full ordination, the Venerable Kassapa, dwelling alone, withdrawn, diligent, ardent, and resolute, realized for himself—in this very life—that unsurpassed consummation of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness. He directly knew: ‘Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming back to any state of being.’ And the Venerable Kassapa became one of the arahants.

Atha kho āyasmā bākulo aparena samayena avāpuraṇaṃ [apāpuraṇaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] ādāya vihārena vihāraṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamāha – ‘‘abhikkamathāyasmanto, abhikkamathāyasmanto. Ajja me parinibbānaṃ bhavissatī’’ti. ‘‘Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo avāpuraṇaṃ ādāya vihārena vihāraṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamāha – ‘abhikkamathāyasmanto, abhikkamathāyasmanto; ajja me parinibbānaṃ bhavissatī’ti, idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhārema’’.

Then, the Venerable Bākula, taking a key, went from dwelling to dwelling and said: "Approach, venerables, approach, venerables. Today I will attain final Nibbāna." "That the Venerable Bākula took a key, went from dwelling to dwelling, and said: 'Approach, venerables, approach, venerables. Today I will attain final Nibbāna'—this too we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

Āyasmā bākulo majjhe bhikkhusaṅghassa nisinnakova parinibbāyi. ‘‘Yaṃpāyasmā bākulo majjhe bhikkhusaṅghassa nisinnakova parinibbāyi, idampi mayaṃ āyasmato bākulassa acchariyaṃ abbhutadhammaṃ dhāremā’’ti.

The Venerable Bākula attained final Nibbāna while seated in the midst of the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. "That the Venerable Bākula attained final Nibbāna while seated in the midst of the Saṅgha of bhikkhus—this too we consider a marvelous and astonishing quality of the Venerable Bākula."

Bākulasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The Bākula Sutta, the fourth, is concluded.
