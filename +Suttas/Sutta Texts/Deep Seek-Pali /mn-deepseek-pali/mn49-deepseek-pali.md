# 49. Brahmanimantanikasuttaṃ

## 49. The Brahmanimantanika Sutta

501. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

501.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "Bhikkhus!" "Venerable sir," those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Ekamidāhaṃ, bhikkhave, samayaṃ ukkaṭṭhāyaṃ viharāmi subhagavane sālarājamūle. Tena kho pana, bhikkhave, samayena bakassa brahmuno evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ hoti – ‘idaṃ niccaṃ, idaṃ dhuvaṃ, idaṃ sassataṃ, idaṃ kevalaṃ, idaṃ acavanadhammaṃ, idañhi na jāyati na jīyati na mīyati na cavati na upapajjati, ito ca panaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇaṃ natthī’ti. Atha khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, bakassa brahmuno cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evameva – ukkaṭṭhāyaṃ subhagavane sālarājamūle antarahito tasmiṃ brahmaloke pāturahosiṃ. Addasā kho maṃ, bhikkhave, bako brahmā dūratova āgacchantaṃ; disvāna maṃ etadavoca – ‘ehi kho, mārisa, svāgataṃ, mārisa! Cirassaṃ kho, mārisa, imaṃ pariyāyamakāsi yadidaṃ idhāgamanāya. Idañhi, mārisa, niccaṃ, idaṃ dhuvaṃ, idaṃ sassataṃ, idaṃ kevalaṃ, idaṃ acavanadhammaṃ, idañhi na jāyati na jīyati na mīyati na cavati na upapajjati. Ito ca panaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇaṃ natthī’’’ti.

"Bhikkhus, I recall a time when I was staying at Ukkaṭṭhā in the Subhaga Grove at the foot of a sal-tree. At that time, bhikkhus, the evil view had arisen in Brahmā Baka: ‘This is permanent, this is stable, this is eternal, this is complete, this is not subject to change, this does not generate, age, die, disappear, or reappear. And there is no further escape beyond this.’ Then, bhikkhus, knowing with my mind the thought in Brahmā Baka’s mind—just as a strong man might bend his arm or stretch it out—so I vanished from the Subhaga Grove at the foot of the sal-tree in Ukkaṭṭhā and appeared in that Brahmā world. Brahmā Baka saw me coming from afar and, on seeing me, said: ‘Come, good sir! Welcome, good sir! It has been long, good sir, since you took the trouble to come here. For this, good sir, is permanent, this is stable, this is eternal, this is complete, this is not subject to change, this does not generate, age, die, disappear, or reappear. And there is no further escape beyond this.’"

Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bakaṃ brahmānaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘‘avijjāgato vata, bho, bako brahmā; avijjāgato vata, bho, bako brahmā; yatra hi nāma aniccaṃyeva samānaṃ niccanti vakkhati, addhuvaṃyeva samānaṃ dhuvanti vakkhati, asassataṃyeva samānaṃ sassatanti vakkhati, akevalaṃyeva samānaṃ kevalanti vakkhati, cavanadhammaṃyeva samānaṃ acavanadhammanti vakkhati; yattha ca pana jāyati jīyati mīyati cavati upapajjati tañca vakkhati – ‘idañhi na jāyati na jīyati na mīyati na cavati na upapajjatī’ti; santañca panaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇaṃ ‘natthaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇa’nti vakkhatī’’ti.

When this was said, I, bhikkhus, said to Brahmā Baka: "Alas, venerable Brahmā Baka is immersed in ignorance! Alas, venerable Brahmā Baka is immersed in ignorance! For he declares what is impermanent to be permanent, what is unstable to be stable, what is non-eternal to be eternal, what is incomplete to be complete, what is subject to change to be not subject to change; and where one is born, ages, dies, passes away, and reappears, he declares: 'This does not get born, does not age, does not die, does not pass away, does not reappear'; and though there is another, higher escape, he declares: 'There is no other, higher escape.'"

502. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, māro pāpimā aññataraṃ brahmapārisajjaṃ anvāvisitvā maṃ etadavoca – ‘bhikkhu, bhikkhu, metamāsado metamāsado, eso hi, bhikkhu, brahmā mahābrahmā abhibhū anabhibhūto aññadatthudaso vasavattī issaro kattā nimmātā seṭṭho sajitā [sajjitā (syā. kaṃ. ka.), sañjitā (sī. pī.)] vasī pitā bhūtabhabyānaṃ. Ahesuṃ kho ye, bhikkhu, tayā pubbe samaṇabrāhmaṇā lokasmiṃ pathavīgarahakā pathavījigucchakā, āpagarahakā āpajigucchakā, tejagarahakā tejajigucchakā, vāyagarahakā vāyajigucchakā, bhūtagarahakā bhūtajigucchakā, devagarahakā devajigucchakā, pajāpatigarahakā pajāpatijigucchakā, brahmagarahakā brahmajigucchakā – te kāyassa bhedā pāṇupacchedā hīne kāye patiṭṭhitā ahesuṃ. Ye pana, bhikkhu, tayā pubbe samaṇabrāhmaṇā lokasmiṃ pathavīpasaṃsakā pathavābhinandino, āpapasaṃsakā āpābhinandino, tejapasaṃsakā tejābhinandino, vāyapasaṃsakā vāyābhinandino, bhūtapasaṃsakā bhūtābhinandino, devapasaṃsakā devābhinandino, pajāpatipasaṃsakā pajāpatābhinandino, brahmapasaṃsakā brahmābhinandino – te kāyassa bhedā pāṇupacchedā paṇīte kāye patiṭṭhitā. Taṃ tāhaṃ, bhikkhu, evaṃ vadāmi – ‘iṅgha tvaṃ, mārisa, yadeva te brahmā āha tadeva tvaṃ karohi, mā tvaṃ brahmuno vacanaṃ upātivattittho’. Sace kho tvaṃ, bhikkhu, brahmuno vacanaṃ upātivattissasi, seyyathāpi nāma puriso siriṃ āgacchantiṃ daṇḍena paṭippaṇāmeyya, seyyathāpi vā pana, bhikkhu, puriso narakappapāte papatanto hatthehi ca pādehi ca pathaviṃ virādheyya, evaṃ sampadamidaṃ, bhikkhu, tuyhaṃ bhavissati. ‘Iṅghaṃ tvaṃ, mārisa, yadeva te brahmā āha tadeva tvaṃ karohi, mā tvaṃ brahmuno vacanaṃ upātivattittho. Nanu tvaṃ, bhikkhu, passasi brahmaparisaṃ sannipatita’nti? Iti kho maṃ, bhikkhave, māro pāpimā brahmaparisaṃ upanesi.

502."Then, bhikkhus, Māra the Evil One, having entered into a certain attendant of Brahmā, said this to me: ‘Bhikkhu, bhikkhu, do not slight this! Do not slight this! For this, bhikkhu, is Brahmā, the Great Brahmā, the Conqueror, the Unconquered, the All-Seeing, the All-Powerful, the Lord, the Maker, the Creator, the Chief, the Arranger, the Ruler, the Father of all that have been and will be. Those ascetics and brahmins, bhikkhu, who in the past in this world despised earth, reviled earth, despised water, reviled water, despised fire, reviled fire, despised air, reviled air, despised beings, reviled beings, despised gods, reviled gods, despised Pajāpati, reviled Pajāpati, despised Brahmā, reviled Brahmā—on the dissolution of the body, after death, have arisen in an inferior body. But those ascetics and brahmins, bhikkhu, who in the past in this world praised earth, rejoiced in earth, praised water, rejoiced in water, praised fire, rejoiced in fire, praised air, rejoiced in air, praised beings, rejoiced in beings, praised gods, rejoiced in gods, praised Pajāpati, rejoiced in Pajāpati, praised Brahmā, rejoiced in Brahmā—on the dissolution of the body, after death, have arisen in a superior body. Therefore, bhikkhu, I say this to you: "Come now, good sir, just do what Brahmā tells you, do not transgress Brahmā’s word." If you, bhikkhu, transgress Brahmā’s word, it will be just as if a man were to push back prosperity coming his way with a stick, or as if, bhikkhu, a man falling into a hellish abyss were to fail to gain a foothold with his hands and feet. So will this attainment be for you, bhikkhu. "Come now, good sir, just do what Brahmā tells you, do not transgress Brahmā’s word. Do you not see, bhikkhu, the assembly of Brahmā gathered here?"’ Thus, bhikkhus, Māra the Evil One presented the assembly of Brahmā to me.

‘‘Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ, bhikkhave, māraṃ pāpimantaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘jānāmi kho tāhaṃ, pāpima; mā tvaṃ maññittho – na maṃ jānātī’ti. Māro tvamasi, pāpima. Yo ceva, pāpima, brahmā, yā ca brahmaparisā, ye ca brahmapārisajjā, sabbeva tava hatthagatā sabbeva tava vasaṃgatā. Tuyhañhi, pāpima, evaṃ hoti – ‘esopi me assa hatthagato, esopi me assa vasaṃgato’ti. Ahaṃ kho pana, pāpima, neva tava hatthagato neva tava vasaṃgato’’ti.

"When this was said, bhikkhus, I said to Māra the Evil One: 'I know you, evil one; do not think, "He does not know me." You are Māra, evil one. That Brahmā, and Brahmā's assembly, and Brahmā's attendants—all have fallen into your hands, all have come under your control. For you think, evil one: "This one too has fallen into my hands, this one too has come under my control." But I, evil one, have not fallen into your hands, nor have I come under your control.'"

503. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte, bhikkhave, bako brahmā maṃ etadavoca – ‘ahañhi, mārisa, niccaṃyeva samānaṃ niccanti vadāmi, dhuvaṃyeva samānaṃ dhuvanti vadāmi, sassataṃyeva samānaṃ sassatanti vadāmi, kevalaṃyeva samānaṃ kevalanti vadāmi, acavanadhammaṃyeva samānaṃ acavanadhamma’nti vadāmi, yattha ca pana na jāyati na jīyati na mīyati na cavati na upapajjati tadevāhaṃ vadāmi – ‘idañhi na jāyati na jīyati na mīyati na cavati na upapajjatī’ti. Asantañca panaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇaṃ ‘natthaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇa’nti vadāmi. Ahesuṃ kho, bhikkhu, tayā pubbe samaṇabrāhmaṇā lokasmiṃ yāvatakaṃ tuyhaṃ kasiṇaṃ āyu tāvatakaṃ tesaṃ tapokammameva ahosi. Te kho evaṃ jāneyyuṃ – ‘santañca panaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇaṃ atthaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇanti, asantaṃ vā aññaṃ uttari nissaraṇaṃ natthaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇa’nti. Taṃ tāhaṃ, bhikkhu, evaṃ vadāmi – ‘na cevaññaṃ uttari nissaraṇaṃ dakkhissasi, yāvadeva ca pana kilamathassa vighātassa bhāgī bhavissasi. Sace kho tvaṃ, bhikkhu, pathaviṃ ajjhosissasi, opasāyiko me bhavissasi vatthusāyiko, yathākāmakaraṇīyo bāhiteyyo. Sace āpaṃ… tejaṃ… vāyaṃ… bhūte… deve… pajāpatiṃ… brahmaṃ ajjhosissasi, opasāyiko me bhavissasi vatthusāyiko, yathākāmakaraṇīyo bāhiteyyo’ti.

503."When this was said, bhikkhus, Brahmā Baka said to me: ‘Indeed, good sir, I declare what is permanent to be permanent, what is stable to be stable, what is eternal to be eternal, what is complete to be complete, what is not subject to change to be not subject to change. And where there is no birth, no aging, no death, no passing away, no reappearing—that alone I declare: "Here there is no birth, no aging, no death, no passing away, no reappearing." And I declare that there is no further, higher escape than this: "There is no further, higher escape."’ Bhikkhu, before you there were ascetics and brahmins in the world whose sphere of meditative accomplishment lasted as long as your entire lifespan. Their practice was solely austerity. They might know: "There is a further, higher escape beyond this," or "There is no further, higher escape beyond this." Therefore, bhikkhu, I say to you: "You will not see any further, higher escape beyond this, and you will only partake of weariness and frustration. If you adhere to earth, bhikkhu, you will be lying near me, lying on my ground, to be used as I wish, to be rejected. If you adhere to water... fire... air... beings... gods... Pajāpati... Brahmā, you will be lying near me, lying on my ground, to be used as I wish, to be rejected."’

‘‘Ahampi kho evaṃ, brahme, jānāmi – sace pathaviṃ ajjhosissāmi, opasāyiko te bhavissāmi vatthusāyiko, yathākāmakaraṇīyo bāhiteyyo. ‘Sace āpaṃ… tejaṃ… vāyaṃ… bhūte… deve… pajāpatiṃ… brahmaṃ ajjhosissāmi, opasāyiko te bhavissāmi vatthusāyiko, yathākāmakaraṇīyo bāhiteyyo’ti api ca te ahaṃ, brahme, gatiñca pajānāmi, jutiñca pajānāmi – evaṃ mahiddhiko bako brahmā, evaṃ mahānubhāvo bako brahmā, evaṃ mahesakkho bako brahmā’’ti.

"I too, Brahmā, know thus: If I were to adhere to earth, I would become one lying near you, lying on your ground, to be used as you wish, to be rejected. 'If I were to adhere to water... fire... air... beings... gods... Pajāpati... Brahmā, I would become one lying near you, lying on your ground, to be used as you wish, to be rejected.' But I know your destiny and your radiance, Brahmā: 'So mighty is Brahmā Baka, so majestic is Brahmā Baka, so influential is Brahmā Baka.'"

‘‘Yathākathaṃ pana me tvaṃ, mārisa, gatiñca pajānāsi, jutiñca pajānāsi – ‘evaṃ mahiddhiko bako brahmā, evaṃ mahānubhāvo bako brahmā, evaṃ mahesakkho bako brahmā’ti?

"But how, good sir, do you know my destiny and my radiance—that ‘Brahmā Baka is so mighty, Brahmā Baka is so powerful, Brahmā Baka is so influential’?"

‘‘Yāvatā candimasūriyā, pariharanti disā bhanti virocanā;Tāva sahassadhā loko, ettha te vattate [vattatī (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] vaso.

"As far as the moon and sun revolve, illuminating the directions, shining forth, so vast is the thousandfold world where your power prevails."

‘‘Paroparañca [parovarañca (sī. pī.)] jānāsi, atho rāgavirāginaṃ;Itthabhāvaññathābhāvaṃ, sattānaṃ āgatiṃ gati’’nti.

"You know the high and the low, and also those with and without passion; this state and other states, the coming and going of beings."

‘‘Evaṃ kho te ahaṃ, brahme, gatiñca pajānāmi jutiñca pajānāmi – ‘evaṃ mahiddhiko bako brahmā, evaṃ mahānubhāvo bako brahmā, evaṃ mahesakkho bako brahmā’ti.

"Thus, Brahmā, I know your destiny and your radiance: 'So mighty is Brahmā Baka, so majestic is Brahmā Baka, so influential is Brahmā Baka.'"

504. ‘‘Atthi kho, brahme, añño kāyo, taṃ tvaṃ na jānāsi na passasi; tamahaṃ jānāmi passāmi. Atthi kho, brahme, ābhassarā nāma kāyo yato tvaṃ cuto idhūpapanno. Tassa te aticiranivāsena sā sati pamuṭṭhā, tena taṃ tvaṃ na jānāsi na passasi; tamahaṃ jānāmi passāmi. Evampi kho ahaṃ, brahme, neva te samasamo abhiññāya, kuto nīceyyaṃ? Atha kho ahameva tayā bhiyyo. Atthi kho, brahme, subhakiṇho nāma kāyo, vehapphalo nāma kāyo, abhibhū nāma kāyo, taṃ tvaṃ na jānāsi na passasi; tamahaṃ jānāmi passāmi. Evampi kho ahaṃ, brahme, neva te samasamo abhiññāya, kuto nīceyyaṃ? Atha kho ahameva tayā bhiyyo. Pathaviṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme, pathavito abhiññāya yāvatā pathaviyā pathavattena ananubhūtaṃ tadabhiññāya pathaviṃ nāpahosiṃ, pathaviyā nāpahosiṃ, pathavito nāpahosiṃ, pathaviṃ meti nāpahosiṃ, pathaviṃ nābhivadiṃ. Evampi kho ahaṃ, brahme, neva te samasamo abhiññāya, kuto nīceyyaṃ? Atha kho ahameva tayā bhiyyo. Āpaṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… tejaṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… vāyaṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… bhūte kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… deve kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… pajāpatiṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… brahmaṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… ābhassare kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… subhakiṇhe kho ahaṃ, brahme… …pe… vehapphale kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… abhibhuṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme…pe… sabbaṃ kho ahaṃ, brahme, sabbato abhiññāya yāvatā sabbassa sabbattena ananubhūtaṃ tadabhiññāya sabbaṃ nāpahosiṃ sabbasmiṃ nāpahosiṃ sabbato nāpahosiṃ sabbaṃ meti nāpahosiṃ, sabbaṃ nābhivadiṃ. Evampi kho ahaṃ, brahme, neva te samasamo abhiññāya, kuto nīceyyaṃ? Atha kho ahameva tayā bhiyyo’’ti.

504.“There is, brahmin, another body that you do not know or see, but I know and see it. There is, brahmin, a body called the Ābhassara, from which you passed away and reappeared here. Due to your long stay there, your memory of it has lapsed, so you do not know or see it, but I know and see it. Thus, brahmin, I am not even your equal in direct knowledge—how could I be inferior? Rather, I surpass you. There is, brahmin, a body called Subhakiṇha, a body called Vehapphala, a body called Abhibhū Brahma—these you do not know or see, but I know and see them. Thus, brahmin, I am not even your equal in direct knowledge—how could I be inferior? Rather, I surpass you. I have directly known earth as earth, brahmin. Having directly known earth as earth, whatever unexperienced aspect of earth there is to earthness, having directly known that, I did not conceive it to be earth, did not conceive myself in earth, did not conceive myself from earth, did not conceive ‘earth is mine,’ nor did I delight in earth. Thus, brahmin, I am not even your equal in direct knowledge—how could I be inferior? Rather, I surpass you. I have directly known water as water, brahmin… fire as fire, brahmin… air as air, brahmin… beings as beings, brahmin… gods as gods, brahmin… Pajāpati as Pajāpati, brahmin… Brahmā as Brahmā, brahmin… the Ābhassara gods as Ābhassara gods, brahmin… the Subhakiṇha gods as Subhakiṇha gods, brahmin… the Vehapphala gods as Vehapphala gods, brahmin… the Abhibhū Brahma as Abhibhū Brahma, brahmin… I have directly known the all as the all, brahmin. Having directly known the all as the all, whatever unexperienced aspect of the all there is to allness, having directly known that, I did not conceive it to be the all, did not conceive myself in the all, did not conceive myself from the all, did not conceive ‘the all is mine,’ nor did I delight in the all. Thus, brahmin, I am not even your equal in direct knowledge—how could I be inferior? Rather, I surpass you.”

‘‘Sace kho, mārisa, sabbassa sabbattena ananubhūtaṃ, tadabhiññāya mā heva te rittakameva ahosi, tucchakameva ahosī’’ti.

“If, friend, the all is unexperienced by way of allness, then may it not be that your statement was merely empty, merely vain.”

‘‘‘Viññāṇaṃ anidassanaṃ anantaṃ sabbato pabhaṃ’, taṃ pathaviyā pathavattena ananubhūtaṃ, āpassa āpattena ananubhūtaṃ, tejassa tejattena ananubhūtaṃ, vāyassa vāyattena ananubhūtaṃ, bhūtānaṃ bhūtattena ananubhūtaṃ, devānaṃ devattena ananubhūtaṃ, pajāpatissa pajāpatittena ananubhūtaṃ, brahmānaṃ brahmattena ananubhūtaṃ, ābhassarānaṃ ābhassarattena ananubhūtaṃ, subhakiṇhānaṃ subhakiṇhattena ananubhūtaṃ, vehapphalānaṃ vehapphalatte ananubhūtaṃ, abhibhussa abhibhuttena ananubhūtaṃ, sabbassa sabbattena ananubhūtaṃ’’.

“‘Consciousness that is signless, infinite, radiant all around’—that is unexperienced by earth by way of earthness, unexperienced by water by way of waterness, unexperienced by fire by way of fireness, unexperienced by air by way of airness, unexperienced by beings by way of beingness, unexperienced by gods by way of godness, unexperienced by Pajāpati by way of Pajāpatiness, unexperienced by Brahmā by way of Brahmāness, unexperienced by the Ābhassara gods by way of Ābhassara-ness, unexperienced by the Subhakiṇha gods by way of Subhakiṇha-ness, unexperienced by the Vehapphala gods by way of Vehapphala-ness, unexperienced by Abhibhū by way of Abhibhū-ness, unexperienced by the all by way of allness.”

‘‘Handa carahi [handa ca hi (sī. pī.)] te, mārisa, passa antaradhāyāmī’’ti. ‘Handa carahi me tvaṃ, brahme, antaradhāyassu, sace visahasī’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, bako brahmā ‘antaradhāyissāmi samaṇassa gotamassa, antaradhāyissāmi samaṇassa gotamassā’ti nevassu me sakkoti antaradhāyituṃ.

“Come now, good sir, watch me disappear.” “Come now, Brahma, disappear from me, if you are able.” Then, bhikkhus, Baka Brahma, thinking, “I will disappear from the ascetic Gotama, I will disappear from the ascetic Gotama,” was not able to disappear from me.

‘‘Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bakaṃ brahmānaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘handa carahi te brahme antaradhāyāmī’ti. ‘Handa carahi me tvaṃ, mārisa, antaradhāyassu sace visahasī’ti. Atha kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhāsiṃ – ‘ettāvatā brahmā ca brahmaparisā ca brahmapārisajjā ca saddañca me sossanti [saddameva suyyanti (ka.)], na ca maṃ dakkhantī’ti. Antarahito imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsiṃ –

When this was said, I, bhikkhus, said to Baka the Brahma: ‘Well then, Brahma, I will now disappear from you.’ ‘Well then, good sir, disappear if you can.’ Then, bhikkhus, I performed such a feat of psychic power that Brahma, the Brahma assembly, and the Brahma retinue heard my voice, but would not see me. Having disappeared, I uttered this verse:

‘‘Bhavevāhaṃ bhayaṃ disvā, bhavañca vibhavesinaṃ;Bhavaṃ nābhivadiṃ kiñci, nandiñca na upādiyi’’nti.

‘Having seen fear in existence, and those who seek non-existence in existence, I did not affirm any existence, nor did I grasp at delight.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, brahmā ca brahmaparisā ca brahmapārisajjā ca acchariyabbhutacittajātā ahesuṃ – ‘acchariyaṃ vata bho, abbhutaṃ vata bho! Samaṇassa gotamassa mahiddhikatā mahānubhāvatā, na ca vata no ito pubbe diṭṭho vā, suto vā, añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā evaṃ mahiddhiko evaṃ mahānubhāvo yathāyaṃ samaṇo gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito. Bhavarāmāya vata, bho, pajāya bhavaratāya bhavasammuditāya samūlaṃ bhavaṃ udabbahī’ti.

Then, bhikkhus, Brahmā, the Brahmā assembly, and the Brahmā retinue were filled with wonder and amazement, saying: ‘It is wonderful, good sirs, it is marvelous! The ascetic Gotama has such great psychic power and might. Never before have we seen or heard of any other ascetic or brahmin with such great psychic power and might as this ascetic Gotama, the Sakyan, who has gone forth from the Sakyan clan. This generation, good sirs, delights in existence, takes pleasure in existence, rejoices in existence, and he has uprooted existence by the root!’

505. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, māro pāpimā aññataraṃ brahmapārisajjaṃ anvāvisitvā maṃ etadavoca – ‘sace kho tvaṃ, mārisa, evaṃ pajānāsi, sace tvaṃ evaṃ anubuddho, mā sāvake upanesi, mā pabbajite; mā sāvakānaṃ dhammaṃ desesi, mā pabbajitānaṃ; mā sāvakesu gedhimakāsi, mā pabbajitesu. Ahesuṃ kho, bhikkhu, tayā pubbe samaṇabrāhmaṇā lokasmiṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā paṭijānamānā. Te sāvake upanesuṃ pabbajite, sāvakānaṃ dhammaṃ desesuṃ pabbajitānaṃ, sāvakesu gedhimakaṃsu pabbajitesu, te sāvake upanetvā pabbajite, sāvakānaṃ dhammaṃ desetvā pabbajitānaṃ, sāvakesu gedhitacittā pabbajitesu, kāyassa bhedā pāṇupacchedā hīne kāye patiṭṭhitā. Ahesuṃ ye pana, bhikkhu, tayā pubbe samaṇabrāhmaṇā lokasmiṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā paṭijānamānā. Te na sāvake upanesuṃ na pabbajite, na sāvakānaṃ dhammaṃ desesuṃ na pabbajitānaṃ, na sāvakesu gedhimakaṃsu na pabbajitesu, te na sāvake upanetvā na pabbajite, na sāvakānaṃ dhammaṃ desetvā na pabbajitānaṃ, na sāvakesu gedhitacittā na pabbajitesu, kāyassa bhedā pāṇupacchedā paṇīte kāye patiṭṭhitā. Taṃ tāhaṃ, bhikkhu, evaṃ vadāmi – iṅgha tvaṃ, mārisa, appossukko diṭṭhadhammasukhavihāramanuyutto viharassu, anakkhātaṃ kusalañhi, mārisa, mā paraṃ ovadāhī’ti.

505.“Then, bhikkhus, Māra the Evil One, having taken possession of a certain member of Brahmā’s assembly, addressed me thus: ‘If, dear sir, you understand thus, if you are awakened thus, do not lead disciples, do not lead those gone forth; do not teach the Dhamma to disciples, do not teach those gone forth; do not be greedy for disciples, do not be greedy for those gone forth. There have been, monk, before you in the world ascetics and brahmins who claimed to be arahants, fully enlightened. They led disciples, they led those gone forth; they taught the Dhamma to disciples, they taught those gone forth; they were greedy for disciples, they were greedy for those gone forth. Having led disciples, having led those gone forth; having taught the Dhamma to disciples, having taught those gone forth; being greedy-minded for disciples, being greedy-minded for those gone forth—with the breakup of the body, after death, they were reborn in an inferior state. But there have been, monk, before you in the world ascetics and brahmins who claimed to be arahants, fully enlightened. They did not lead disciples, they did not lead those gone forth; they did not teach the Dhamma to disciples, they did not teach those gone forth; they were not greedy for disciples, they were not greedy for those gone forth. Not having led disciples, not having led those gone forth; not having taught the Dhamma to disciples, not having taught those gone forth; not being greedy-minded for disciples, not being greedy-minded for those gone forth—with the breakup of the body, after death, they were reborn in a superior state. Therefore, monk, I say this to you: Please, dear sir, dwell unconcerned, devoted to pleasant abiding in the present life; for the good, dear sir, is better left undeclared. Do not advise others.’”

‘‘Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ, bhikkhave, māraṃ pāpimantaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘jānāmi kho tāhaṃ, pāpima, mā tvaṃ maññittho – na maṃ jānātī’ti. Māro tvamasi, pāpima. Na maṃ tvaṃ, pāpima, hitānukampī evaṃ vadesi; ahitānukampī maṃ tvaṃ, pāpima, evaṃ vadesi. Tuyhañhi, pāpima, evaṃ hoti – ‘yesaṃ samaṇo gotamo dhammaṃ desessati, te me visayaṃ upātivattissantī’ti. Asammāsambuddhāva pana te, pāpima, samānā sammāsambuddhāmhāti paṭijāniṃsu. Ahaṃ kho pana, pāpima, sammāsambuddhova samāno sammāsambuddhomhīti paṭijānāmi. Desentopi hi, pāpima, tathāgato sāvakānaṃ dhammaṃ tādisova adesentopi hi, pāpima, tathāgato sāvakānaṃ dhammaṃ tādisova. Upanentopi hi, pāpima, tathāgato sāvake tādisova, anupanentopi hi, pāpima, tathāgato sāvake tādisova. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathāgatassa, pāpima, ye āsavā saṃkilesikā ponobbhavikā sadarā dukkhavipākā āyatiṃ jātijarāmaraṇiyā – te pahīnā ucchinnamūlā tālāvatthukatā anabhāvaṃkatā āyatiṃ anuppādadhammā. Seyyathāpi, pāpima, tālo matthakacchinno abhabbo puna virūḷhiyā; evameva kho, pāpima, tathāgatassa ye āsavā saṃkilesikā ponobbhavikā sadarā dukkhavipākā āyatiṃ jātijarāmaraṇiyā – te pahīnā ucchinnamūlā tālāvatthukatā anabhāvaṃkatā āyatiṃ anuppādadhammāti.

“Thus addressed, I said to Māra the Evil One: ‘I know you, Evil One. Do not think, “He does not know me.” You are Māra, Evil One. You do not speak to me out of compassion for my welfare, Evil One; you speak to me out of lack of compassion, Evil One. For you think, Evil One, “Those to whom the ascetic Gotama teaches the Dhamma will escape my domain.” But those who are not truly fully enlightened, Evil One, have claimed to be fully enlightened. I, however, Evil One, being truly fully enlightened, claim to be fully enlightened. Even when the Tathāgata teaches the Dhamma to his disciples, Evil One, he is such; even when the Tathāgata does not teach his disciples the Dhamma, Evil One, he is such. Even when the Tathāgata guides his disciples, Evil One, he is such; even when the Tathāgata does not guide his disciples, Evil One, he is such. Why is that? Because, Evil One, the taints—defiling, leading to rebirth, troublesome, resulting in suffering, destined for future birth, aging, and death—have been abandoned by the Tathāgata, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, so that they are no more subject to future arising. Just as, Evil One, a palm tree whose crown is cut off is incapable of growing again, so too, Evil One, the taints—defiling, leading to rebirth, troublesome, resulting in suffering, destined for future birth, aging, and death—have been abandoned by the Tathāgata, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, so that they are no more subject to future arising.’”

‘‘Iti hidaṃ mārassa ca anālapanatāya brahmuno ca abhinimantanatāya, tasmā imassa veyyākaraṇassa brahmanimantanikaṃteva adhivacana’’nti.

“Thus, because of not addressing Māra and the invitation of Brahmā, this exposition is called ‘The Invitation of Brahmā.’”

Brahmanimantanikasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The Brahmanimantanika Sutta, the ninth, is concluded.
