# 78. Samaṇamuṇḍikasuttaṃ

## 78. The Samaṇamuṇḍika Sutta

260. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena uggāhamāno paribbājako samaṇamuṇḍikāputto [samaṇamaṇḍikāputto (sī. pī.)] samayappavādake tindukācīre ekasālake mallikāya ārāme paṭivasati mahatiyā paribbājakaparisāya saddhiṃ pañcamattehi paribbājakasatehi. Atha kho pañcakaṅgo thapati sāvatthiyā nikkhami divā divassa bhagavantaṃ dassanāya. Atha kho pañcakaṅgassa thapatissa etadahosi – ‘‘akālo kho tāva bhagavantaṃ dassanāya; paṭisallīno bhagavā. Manobhāvaniyānampi bhikkhūnaṃ asamayo dassanāya; paṭisallīnā manobhāvaniyā bhikkhū. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ yena samayappavādako tindukācīro ekasālako mallikāya ārāmo yena uggāhamāno paribbājako samaṇamuṇḍikāputto tenupasaṅkameyya’’nti. Atha kho pañcakaṅgo thapati yena samayappavādako tindukācīro ekasālako mallikāya ārāmo yena uggāhamāno paribbājako samaṇamuṇḍikāputto tenupasaṅkami.

260.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. Now at that time the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, was staying at the Place of Doctrines, the Tindukācīra, the Single-Hall, in Mallikā’s Park, with a large assembly of wanderers, with about five hundred wanderers. Then the carpenter Pañcakaṅga left Savatthi in the middle of the day to see the Blessed One. Then it occurred to the carpenter Pañcakaṅga: “It is not the right time to see the Blessed One; he is in seclusion. It is also not the right time to see the bhikkhus who cultivate the mind; they are in seclusion. Why don't I go to the Place of Doctrines, the Tindukācīra, the Single-Hall, in Mallikā’s Park, where the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, is staying?” So the carpenter Pañcakaṅga went to the Place of Doctrines, the Tindukācīra, the Single-Hall, in Mallikā’s Park, where the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, was staying.

Tena kho pana samayena uggāhamāno paribbājako samaṇamuṇḍikāputto mahatiyā paribbājakaparisāya saddhiṃ nisinno hoti unnādiniyā uccāsaddamahāsaddāya anekavihitaṃ tiracchānakathaṃ kathentiyā, seyyathidaṃ – rājakathaṃ corakathaṃ mahāmattakathaṃ senākathaṃ bhayakathaṃ yuddhakathaṃ annakathaṃ pānakathaṃ vatthakathaṃ sayanakathaṃ mālākathaṃ gandhakathaṃ ñātikathaṃ yānakathaṃ gāmakathaṃ nigamakathaṃ nagarakathaṃ janapadakathaṃ itthikathaṃ sūrakathaṃ visikhākathaṃ kumbhaṭṭhānakathaṃ pubbapetakathaṃ nānattakathaṃ lokakkhāyikaṃ samuddakkhāyikaṃ itibhavābhavakathaṃ iti vā.

Now at that time, Uggāhamāna the wanderer, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, was sitting with a large assembly of wanderers, making a loud and noisy racket, engaged in various kinds of frivolous talk—such as talk about kings, robbers, ministers, armies, dangers, battles, food, drink, clothes, beds, garlands, perfumes, relatives, vehicles, villages, towns, cities, countries, women, heroes, streets, pot-stands, the departed, miscellaneous matters, world-tales, sea-tales, and talk about being this or that.

Addasā kho uggāhamāno paribbājako samaṇamuṇḍikāputto pañcakaṅgaṃ thapatiṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna sakaṃ parisaṃ saṇṭhāpesi – ‘‘appasaddā bhonto hontu, mā bhonto saddamakattha; ayaṃ samaṇassa gotamassa sāvako āgacchati pañcakaṅgo thapati. Yāvatā kho pana samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakā gihī odātavasanā sāvatthiyaṃ paṭivasanti ayaṃ tesaṃ aññataro pañcakaṅgo thapati. Appasaddakāmā kho pana te āyasmanto appasaddavinītā appasaddassa vaṇṇavādino; appeva nāma appasaddaṃ parisaṃ viditvā upasaṅkamitabbaṃ maññeyyā’’ti. Atha kho te paribbājakā tuṇhī ahesuṃ.

The wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, saw the carpenter Pañcakaṅga coming from afar. Seeing him, he quieted his assembly: “Be quiet, sirs, do not make noise. Here comes a disciple of the Ascetic Gotama, the carpenter Pañcakaṅga. Among the lay disciples of the Ascetic Gotama who are white-clad and live in Savatthi, this carpenter Pañcakaṅga is one. Those venerable ones prefer quiet, are trained in quiet, and praise quiet. Perhaps, knowing our assembly is quiet, he may think it fit to approach.” Then those wanderers fell silent.

261. Atha kho pañcakaṅgo thapati yena uggāhamāno paribbājako samaṇamuṇḍikāputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā uggāhamānena paribbājakena samaṇamuṇḍikāputtena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho pañcakaṅgaṃ thapatiṃ uggāhamāno paribbājako samaṇamuṇḍikāputto etadavoca – ‘‘catūhi kho ahaṃ, gahapati, dhammehi samannāgataṃ purisapuggalaṃ paññapemi sampannakusalaṃ paramakusalaṃ uttamapattipattaṃ samaṇaṃ ayojjhaṃ. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, gahapati, na kāyena pāpakammaṃ karoti, na pāpakaṃ vācaṃ bhāsati, na pāpakaṃ saṅkappaṃ saṅkappeti, na pāpakaṃ ājīvaṃ ājīvati – imehi kho ahaṃ, gahapati, catūhi dhammehi samannāgataṃ purisapuggalaṃ paññapemi sampannakusalaṃ paramakusalaṃ uttamapattipattaṃ samaṇaṃ ayojjha’’nti.

261.Then the carpenter Pañcakaṅga approached the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with Uggāhamāna the wanderer, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā. After exchanging pleasant and polite conversation, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Uggāhamāna the wanderer, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, said this to him: “Householder, I declare a person possessing four qualities to be accomplished in skillfulness, supremely skillful, having attained the highest attainment, an invincible ascetic. What four? Here, householder, one does no evil deed by body, speaks no evil speech, intends no evil thought, and lives by no evil livelihood. Householder, I declare a person possessing these four qualities to be accomplished in skillfulness, supremely skillful, having attained the highest attainment, an invincible ascetic.”

Atha kho pañcakaṅgo thapati uggāhamānassa paribbājakassa samaṇamuṇḍikāputtassa bhāsitaṃ neva abhinandi nappaṭikkosi. Anabhinanditvā appaṭikkositvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi – ‘‘bhagavato santike etassa bhāsitassa atthaṃ ājānissāmī’’ti. Atha kho pañcakaṅgo thapati yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho pañcakaṅgo thapati yāvatako ahosi uggāhamānena paribbājakena samaṇamuṇḍikāputtena saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo taṃ sabbaṃ bhagavato ārocesi.

Then the carpenter Pañcakaṅga neither approved nor disapproved of the statement of the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā. Without approving or disapproving, he rose from his seat and left, thinking: “I will learn the meaning of this statement in the presence of the Blessed One.” Then the carpenter Pañcakaṅga approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, he reported to the Blessed One the entire conversation he had with the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā.

262. Evaṃ vutte, bhagavā pañcakaṅgaṃ thapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘evaṃ sante kho, thapati, daharo kumāro mando uttānaseyyako sampannakusalo bhavissati paramakusalo uttamapattipatto samaṇo ayojjho, yathā uggāhamānassa paribbājakassa samaṇamuṇḍikāputtassa vacanaṃ. Daharassa hi, thapati, kumārassa mandassa uttānaseyyakassa kāyotipi na hoti, kuto pana kāyena pāpakammaṃ karissati, aññatra phanditamattā! Daharassa hi, thapati, kumārassa mandassa uttānaseyyakassa vācātipi na hoti, kuto pana pāpakaṃ vācaṃ bhāsissati, aññatra roditamattā! Daharassa hi, thapati, kumārassa mandassa uttānaseyyakassa saṅkappotipi na hoti, kuto pana pāpakaṃ saṅkappaṃ saṅkappissati, aññatra vikūjitamattā [vikujjitamattā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)]! Daharassa hi, thapati, kumārassa mandassa uttānaseyyakassa ājīvotipi na hoti, kuto pana pāpakaṃ ājīvaṃ ājīvissati, aññatra mātuthaññā! Evaṃ sante kho, thapati, daharo kumāro mando uttānaseyyako sampannakusalo bhavissati paramakusalo uttamapattipatto samaṇo ayojjho, yathā uggāhamānassa paribbājakassa samaṇamuṇḍikāputtassa vacanaṃ.

262.When this was said, the Blessed One said to the carpenter Pañcakaṅga: “In that case, carpenter, according to the words of the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, a tender, feeble infant lying on its back would be accomplished in skillfulness, supremely skillful, would have attained the highest attainment, an invincible ascetic. For a tender, feeble infant lying on its back, carpenter, has no body-awareness—how then could it do evil with the body, except for a mere twitch? A tender, feeble infant lying on its back, carpenter, has no speech—how then could it speak evil speech, except for a mere cry? A tender, feeble infant lying on its back, carpenter, has no intention—how then could it intend an evil intention, except for a mere whimper? A tender, feeble infant lying on its back, carpenter, has no livelihood—how then could it live an evil livelihood, except from its mother’s breast? In that case, carpenter, according to the words of the wanderer Uggāhamāna, son of Samaṇamuṇḍikā, a tender, feeble infant lying on its back would be accomplished in skillfulness, supremely skillful, would have attained the highest attainment, an invincible ascetic.”

263. ‘‘Catūhi kho ahaṃ, thapati, dhammehi samannāgataṃ purisapuggalaṃ paññapemi na ceva sampannakusalaṃ na paramakusalaṃ na uttamapattipattaṃ samaṇaṃ ayojjhaṃ, api cimaṃ daharaṃ kumāraṃ mandaṃ uttānaseyyakaṃ samadhigayha tiṭṭhati. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, thapati, na kāyena pāpakammaṃ karoti, na pāpakaṃ vācaṃ bhāsati, na pāpakaṃ saṅkappaṃ saṅkappeti, na pāpakaṃ ājīvaṃ ājīvati – imehi kho ahaṃ, thapati, catūhi dhammehi samannāgataṃ purisapuggalaṃ paññapemi na ceva sampannakusalaṃ na paramakusalaṃ na uttamapattipattaṃ samaṇaṃ ayojjhaṃ, api cimaṃ daharaṃ kumāraṃ mandaṃ uttānaseyyakaṃ samadhigayha tiṭṭhati.

263.“Carpenter, I declare an individual possessing four qualities to be neither accomplished in skillfulness, nor supremely skillful, nor having attained the highest attainment, nor an invincible ascetic; even this young, tender infant lying on its back surpasses him. What four? Here, carpenter, one does not commit evil actions with his body, does not speak evil words, does not think evil thoughts, and does not live by evil means. Carpenter, I declare an individual possessing these four qualities to be neither accomplished in skillfulness, nor supremely skillful, nor having attained the highest attainment, nor an invincible ascetic; even this young, tender infant lying on its back surpasses him.”

‘‘Dasahi kho ahaṃ, thapati, dhammehi samannāgataṃ purisapuggalaṃ paññapemi sampannakusalaṃ paramakusalaṃ uttamapattipattaṃ samaṇaṃ ayojjhaṃ. Ime akusalā sīlā; tamahaṃ [kahaṃ (sī.), tahaṃ (pī.)], thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Itosamuṭṭhānā akusalā sīlā; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Idha akusalā sīlā aparisesā nirujjhanti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Evaṃ paṭipanno akusalānaṃ sīlānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi.

“Carpenter, I declare a person endowed with ten qualities to be accomplished in skillfulness, supremely skillful, having attained the highest attainment, an invincible ascetic. These are unwholesome moralities; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. These unwholesome moralities arise from this; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. Here, these unwholesome moralities cease without remainder; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. One who practices thus is practicing for the cessation of unwholesome moralities; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood.”

‘‘Ime kusalā sīlā; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Itosamuṭṭhānā kusalā sīlā; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Idha kusalā sīlā aparisesā nirujjhanti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Evaṃ paṭipanno kusalānaṃ sīlānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi.

“These are skillful moralities; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. These skillful moralities arise from this; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. Here, these skillful moralities cease without remainder; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. One who practices thus is practicing for the cessation of skillful moralities; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood.

‘‘Ime akusalā saṅkappā; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Itosamuṭṭhānā akusalā saṅkappā; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Idha akusalā saṅkappā aparisesā nirujjhanti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Evaṃ paṭipanno akusalānaṃ saṅkappānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi.

“These are unwholesome thoughts; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. These unwholesome thoughts arise from this; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. Here these unwholesome thoughts cease without remainder; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. One who practices thus is practicing for the cessation of unwholesome thoughts; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood.

‘‘Ime kusalā saṅkappā; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Itosamuṭṭhānā kusalā saṅkappā; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Idha kusalā saṅkappā aparisesā nirujjhanti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi. Evaṃ paṭipanno kusalānaṃ saṅkappānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti; tamahaṃ, thapati, veditabbanti vadāmi.

“These are skillful intentions; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. Skillful intentions arise from this; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. Here, skillful intentions cease without remainder; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood. One who practices thus is practicing for the cessation of skillful intentions; this, carpenter, I declare should be understood.

264. ‘‘Katame ca, thapati, akusalā sīlā? Akusalaṃ kāyakammaṃ, akusalaṃ vacīkammaṃ, pāpako ājīvo – ime vuccanti, thapati, akusalā sīlā.

264.“And what, carpenter, are unwholesome moralities? Unwholesome bodily action, unwholesome verbal action, and wrong livelihood—these, carpenter, are called unwholesome moralities.”

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, akusalā sīlā kiṃsamuṭṭhānā? Samuṭṭhānampi nesaṃ vuttaṃ. ‘Cittasamuṭṭhānā’tissa vacanīyaṃ. Katamaṃ cittaṃ? Cittampi hi bahuṃ anekavidhaṃ nānappakārakaṃ. Yaṃ cittaṃ sarāgaṃ sadosaṃ samohaṃ, itosamuṭṭhānā akusalā sīlā.

“And, carpenter, from what do these unwholesome moralities arise? Their origin has been declared. It should be said: ‘They arise from the mind.’ What mind? For mind is manifold, of many kinds, of various sorts. Those unwholesome moralities arise from the mind that is accompanied by lust, by hatred, by delusion.”

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, akusalā sīlā kuhiṃ aparisesā nirujjhanti? Nirodhopi nesaṃ vutto. Idha, thapati, bhikkhu kāyaduccaritaṃ pahāya kāyasucaritaṃ bhāveti, vacīduccaritaṃ pahāya vacīsucaritaṃ bhāveti, manoduccaritaṃ pahāya manosucaritaṃ bhāveti, micchājīvaṃ pahāya sammājīvena jīvitaṃ kappeti – etthete akusalā sīlā aparisesā nirujjhanti.

"And these unwholesome moral habits, master builder, where do they cease without remainder? Their cessation has been stated. Here, master builder, a bhikkhu, having abandoned bodily misconduct, develops wholesome bodily conduct; having abandoned verbal misconduct, develops wholesome verbal conduct; having abandoned mental misconduct, develops wholesome mental conduct; having abandoned wrong livelihood, sustains his life by right livelihood. It is here that these unwholesome moral habits cease without remainder."

‘‘Kathaṃ paṭipanno, thapati, akusalānaṃ sīlānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti? Idha, thapati, bhikkhu anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ anuppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; uppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; anuppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ uppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Evaṃ paṭipanno kho, thapati, akusalānaṃ sīlānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti.

"And how, master builder, does one practice for the cessation of unwholesome moral habits? Here, master builder, a bhikkhu generates desire, makes an effort, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the non-arising of unarisen evil unwholesome states. He generates desire, makes an effort, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the abandoning of arisen evil unwholesome states. He generates desire, makes an effort, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the arising of unarisen wholesome states. He generates desire, makes an effort, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the maintenance, non-degeneration, increase, abundance, development, and perfection of arisen wholesome states. Practicing in this way, master builder, one practices for the cessation of unwholesome moral habits."

265. ‘‘Katame ca, thapati, kusalā sīlā? Kusalaṃ kāyakammaṃ, kusalaṃ vacīkammaṃ, ājīvaparisuddhampi kho ahaṃ, thapati, sīlasmiṃ vadāmi. Ime vuccanti, thapati, kusalā sīlā.

265."And what, master builder, are wholesome moral habits? Wholesome bodily action, wholesome verbal action, and I declare, master builder, purity of livelihood as part of moral habits. These are called, master builder, wholesome moral habits."

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, kusalā sīlā kiṃsamuṭṭhānā? Samuṭṭhānampi nesaṃ vuttaṃ. ‘Cittasamuṭṭhānā’tissa vacanīyaṃ. Katamaṃ cittaṃ? Cittampi hi bahuṃ anekavidhaṃ nānappakārakaṃ. Yaṃ cittaṃ vītarāgaṃ vītadosaṃ vītamohaṃ, itosamuṭṭhānā kusalā sīlā.

"And these wholesome moral habits, master builder, what is their origin? Their origin has been stated. It should be said, 'They originate from the mind.' What mind? For the mind is indeed diverse, of many kinds and various. Wholesome moral habits originate from the mind that is without lust, without hatred, without delusion."

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, kusalā sīlā kuhiṃ aparisesā nirujjhanti? Nirodhopi nesaṃ vutto. Idha, thapati, bhikkhu sīlavā hoti no ca sīlamayo, tañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti; yatthassa te kusalā sīlā aparisesā nirujjhanti.

"And these wholesome moral habits, master builder, where do they cease without remainder? Their cessation has been stated. Here, master builder, a bhikkhu is virtuous but not identified with virtue, and he understands as it really is that liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom; it is here that these wholesome moral habits cease without remainder."

‘‘Kathaṃ paṭipanno ca, thapati, kusalānaṃ sīlānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti? Idha, thapati, bhikkhu anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ anuppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; uppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya…pe… anuppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ uppādāya…pe… uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Evaṃ paṭipanno kho, thapati, kusalānaṃ sīlānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti.

"And how, master builder, does one practice for the cessation of wholesome moral habits? Here, master builder, a bhikkhu generates desire, makes an effort, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the non-arising of unarisen evil unwholesome states; for the abandoning of arisen evil unwholesome states... for the arising of unarisen wholesome states... for the maintenance, non-degeneration, increase, abundance, development, and perfection of arisen wholesome states. Practicing in this way, master builder, one practices for the cessation of wholesome moral habits."

266. ‘‘Katame ca, thapati, akusalā saṅkappā? Kāmasaṅkappo, byāpādasaṅkappo, vihiṃsāsaṅkappo – ime vuccanti, thapati, akusalā saṅkappā.

266."And what, master builder, are unwholesome intentions? Intentions of sensual desire, intentions of ill-will, intentions of cruelty—these are called, master builder, unwholesome intentions."

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, akusalā saṅkappā kiṃsamuṭṭhānā? Samuṭṭhānampi nesaṃ vuttaṃ. ‘Saññāsamuṭṭhānā’tissa vacanīyaṃ. Katamā saññā? Saññāpi hi bahū anekavidhā nānappakārakā. Kāmasaññā, byāpādasaññā, vihiṃsāsaññā – itosamuṭṭhānā akusalā saṅkappā.

"And these unwholesome intentions, master builder, what is their origin? Their origin has been stated. It should be said, 'They originate from perception.' What perception? For perception is indeed diverse, of many kinds and various. The perception of sensual pleasure, the perception of ill-will, the perception of violence—unwholesome intentions originate from these."

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, akusalā saṅkappā kuhiṃ aparisesā nirujjhanti? Nirodhopi nesaṃ vutto. Idha, thapati, bhikkhu vivicceva kāmehi…pe… paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati; etthete akusalā saṅkappā aparisesā nirujjhanti.

"And these unwholesome intentions, master builder, where do they cease without remainder? Their cessation has been stated. Here, master builder, a bhikkhu, quite secluded from sensual pleasures… enters and dwells in the first jhāna; it is here that these unwholesome intentions cease without remainder."

‘‘Kathaṃ paṭipanno ca, thapati, akusalānaṃ saṅkappānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti? Idha, thapati, bhikkhu anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ anuppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; uppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya…pe… anuppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ uppādāya…pe… uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Evaṃ paṭipanno kho, thapati, akusalānaṃ saṅkappānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti.

"And how, master builder, does one practice for the cessation of unwholesome intentions? Here, master builder, a bhikkhu generates desire, makes an effort, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the non-arising of unarisen evil unwholesome states; for the abandoning of arisen evil unwholesome states... for the arising of unarisen wholesome states... for the maintenance, non-degeneration, increase, abundance, development, and perfection of arisen wholesome states. Practicing in this way, master builder, one practices for the cessation of unwholesome intentions."

267. ‘‘Katame ca, thapati, kusalā saṅkappā? Nekkhammasaṅkappo, abyāpādasaṅkappo, avihiṃsāsaṅkappo – ime vuccanti, thapati, kusalā saṅkappā.

267."And what, master builder, are wholesome intentions? The intention of renunciation, the intention of non-ill-will, the intention of non-harming—these, master builder, are called wholesome intentions."

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, kusalā saṅkappā kiṃsamuṭṭhānā? Samuṭṭhānampi nesaṃ vuttaṃ. ‘Saññāsamuṭṭhānā’tissa vacanīyaṃ. Katamā saññā? Saññāpi hi bahū anekavidhā nānappakārakā. Nekkhammasaññā, abyāpādasaññā, avihiṃsāsaññā – itosamuṭṭhānā kusalā saṅkappā.

"And these wholesome intentions, master builder, what is their origin? Their origin has been stated. It should be said, 'They originate from perception.' What perception? For perception is indeed diverse, of many kinds and various. The perception of renunciation, the perception of non-ill-will, the perception of non-harming—wholesome intentions originate from these."

‘‘Ime ca, thapati, kusalā saṅkappā kuhiṃ aparisesā nirujjhanti? Nirodhopi nesaṃ vutto. Idha, thapati, bhikkhu vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā…pe… dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati; etthete kusalā saṅkappā aparisesā nirujjhanti.

"And these wholesome intentions, master builder, where do they cease without remainder? Their cessation has been stated. Here, master builder, a bhikkhu, with the stilling of applied and sustained thought… enters and dwells in the second jhāna; it is here that these wholesome intentions cease without remainder."

‘‘Kathaṃ paṭipanno ca, thapati, kusalānaṃ saṅkappānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti? Idha, thapati, bhikkhu anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ anuppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati; uppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya…pe… anuppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ uppādāya…pe… uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Evaṃ paṭipanno kho, thapati, kusalānaṃ saṅkappānaṃ nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti.

"And how, master builder, does one practice for the cessation of wholesome intentions? Here, master builder, a bhikkhu generates desire, makes an effort, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the non-arising of unarisen evil unwholesome states; for the abandoning of arisen evil unwholesome states... for the arising of unarisen wholesome states... for the maintenance, non-degeneration, increase, abundance, development, and perfection of arisen wholesome states. Practicing in this way, master builder, one practices for the cessation of wholesome intentions."

268. ‘‘Katamehi cāhaṃ, thapati, dasahi dhammehi samannāgataṃ purisapuggalaṃ paññapemi sampannakusalaṃ paramakusalaṃ uttamapattipattaṃ samaṇaṃ ayojjhaṃ? Idha, thapati, bhikkhu asekhāya sammādiṭṭhiyā samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāsaṅkappena samannāgato hoti, asekhāya sammāvācāya samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammākammantena samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāājīvena samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāvāyāmena samannāgato hoti, asekhāya sammāsatiyā samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāsamādhinā samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāñāṇena samannāgato hoti, asekhāya sammāvimuttiyā samannāgato hoti – imehi kho ahaṃ, thapati, dasahi dhammehi samannāgataṃ purisapuggalaṃ paññapemi sampannakusalaṃ paramakusalaṃ uttamapattipattaṃ samaṇaṃ ayojjha’’nti.

268."And by what ten qualities, master builder, do I describe a person as accomplished in what is wholesome, supreme in what is wholesome, as having attained the highest attainment, an invincible contemplative? Here, master builder, a bhikkhu is endowed with the right view of one beyond training, endowed with the right intention of one beyond training, endowed with the right speech of one beyond training, endowed with the right action of one beyond training, endowed with the right livelihood of one beyond training, endowed with the right effort of one beyond training, endowed with the right mindfulness of one beyond training, endowed with the right samādhi of one beyond training, endowed with the right knowledge of one beyond training, and endowed with the right liberation of one beyond training. It is by these ten qualities, master builder, that I describe a person as accomplished in what is wholesome, supreme in what is wholesome, as having attained the highest attainment, an invincible contemplative."

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano pañcakaṅgo thapati bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

"Thus spoke the Blessed One. The master builder Pañcakaṅga, pleased, rejoiced at the Blessed One's words."

Samaṇamuṇḍikasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

"The Samaṇamuṇḍika Sutta, the eighth, is concluded."
