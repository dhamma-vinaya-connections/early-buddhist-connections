# 24. Rathavinītasuttaṃ

## 24. The Relay Chariots Discourse

252. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Atha kho sambahulā jātibhūmakā bhikkhū jātibhūmiyaṃ vassaṃvuṭṭhā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinne kho te bhikkhū bhagavā etadavoca –

252.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, Kalandaka’s Feeding Ground. Then a number of bhikkhus from Jātibhūmi, having completed the rains residence in Jātibhūmi, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. The Blessed One then addressed those bhikkhus who were sitting to one side:

‘‘Ko nu kho, bhikkhave, jātibhūmiyaṃ jātibhūmakānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ evaṃ sambhāvito – ‘attanā ca appiccho appicchakathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca santuṭṭho santuṭṭhikathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca pavivitto pavivekakathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca asaṃsaṭṭho asaṃsaggakathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca āraddhavīriyo vīriyārambhakathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca sīlasampanno sīlasampadākathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca samādhisampanno samādhisampadākathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca paññāsampanno paññāsampadākathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca vimuttisampanno vimuttisampadākathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampanno vimuttiñāṇadassanasampadākathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, ovādako viññāpako sandassako samādapako samuttejako sampahaṃsako sabrahmacārīna’’’nti? ‘‘Puṇṇo nāma, bhante, āyasmā mantāṇiputto jātibhūmiyaṃ jātibhūmakānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ evaṃ sambhāvito – ‘attanā ca appiccho appicchakathañca bhikkhūnaṃ kattā, attanā ca santuṭṭho…pe… ovādako viññāpako sandassako samādapako samuttejako sampahaṃsako sabrahmacārīna’’’nti.

“Who, bhikkhus, among the bhikkhus from Jātibhūmi, your fellow Dhamma-farers, is thus esteemed: ‘He himself has few desires and speaks to bhikkhus about having few desires; he himself is content and speaks to bhikkhus about contentment; he himself is secluded and speaks to bhikkhus about seclusion; he himself is unentangled and speaks to bhikkhus about being unentangled; he himself has aroused energy and speaks to bhikkhus about arousing energy; he himself is accomplished in virtue and speaks to bhikkhus about accomplishment in virtue; he himself is accomplished in samādhi and speaks to bhikkhus about accomplishment in samādhi; he himself is accomplished in wisdom and speaks to bhikkhus about accomplishment in wisdom; he himself is accomplished in liberation and speaks to bhikkhus about accomplishment in liberation; he himself is accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation and speaks to bhikkhus about accomplishment in the knowledge and vision of liberation; he is an instructor, an informer, a persuader, an arouser, an encourager, a gladdener of his fellow Dhamma-farers’?” “Venerable sir, it is the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta who is thus esteemed by the bhikkhus from Jātibhūmi, our fellow Dhamma-farers: ‘He himself has few desires… he is an instructor, an informer, a persuader, an arouser, an encourager, a gladdener of his fellow Dhamma-farers.’ ”

253. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā sāriputto bhagavato avidūre nisinno hoti. Atha kho āyasmato sāriputtassa etadahosi – ‘‘lābhā āyasmato puṇṇassa mantāṇiputtassa, suladdhalābhā āyasmato puṇṇassa mantāṇiputtassa, yassa viññū sabrahmacārī satthu sammukhā anumassa anumassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti, tañca satthā abbhanumodati. Appeva nāma mayampi kadāci karahaci āyasmatā puṇṇena mantāṇiputtena saddhiṃ samāgaccheyyāma [samāgamaṃ gaccheyya (ka.)], appeva nāma siyā kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti.

253.At that time, the Venerable Sāriputta was sitting not far from the Blessed One. Then it occurred to the Venerable Sāriputta: “It is a gain for the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta, a supreme gain for the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta, that his wise fellow Dhamma-farers praise him again and again in the presence of the Teacher, and the Teacher approves of it. Perhaps I too might sometime meet with the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta; perhaps there might be some conversation between us.”

254. Atha kho bhagavā rājagahe yathābhirantaṃ viharitvā yena sāvatthi tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena sāvatthi tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Assosi kho āyasmā puṇṇo mantāṇiputto – ‘‘bhagavā kira sāvatthiṃ anuppatto; sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme’’ti.

254.Then the Blessed One, having dwelt at Rājagaha for as long as he wished, set out on tour towards Sāvatthī. Journeying by stages, he eventually arrived at Sāvatthī. There the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. The Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta heard: “The Blessed One has arrived at Sāvatthī and is dwelling there in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park.”

255. Atha kho āyasmā puṇṇo mantāṇiputto senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena sāvatthi tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena sāvatthi jetavanaṃ anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāmo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ puṇṇaṃ mantāṇiputtaṃ bhagavā dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho āyasmā puṇṇo mantāṇiputto bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassito samādapito samuttejito sampahaṃsito bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā yena andhavanaṃ tenupasaṅkami divāvihārāya.

255.Then the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta, having put his lodging in order and taken his bowl and robe, set out on tour towards Sāvatthī. Journeying by stages, he eventually arrived at Sāvatthī, at Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park, and approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. The Blessed One instructed, urged, roused, and gladdened the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta with a Dhamma talk as he sat to one side. Then the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta, having been instructed, urged, roused, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, rejoiced in and approved of the Blessed One’s words. He rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and went to the Andhavana for his day’s abiding.

256. Atha kho aññataro bhikkhu yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yassa kho tvaṃ, āvuso sāriputta, puṇṇassa nāma bhikkhuno mantāṇiputtassa abhiṇhaṃ kittayamāno ahosi, so bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassito samādapito samuttejito sampahaṃsito bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā yena andhavanaṃ tena pakkanto divāvihārāyā’’ti.

256.Then a certain bhikkhu approached the Venerable Sāriputta; having approached, he said to him: “Friend Sāriputta, that bhikkhu named Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta, whom you often praised—he was instructed, urged, roused, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk. Having rejoiced in and approved of the Blessed One’s words, he rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and departed towards the Andhavana for his day’s abiding.”

Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto taramānarūpo nisīdanaṃ ādāya āyasmantaṃ puṇṇaṃ mantāṇiputtaṃ piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandhi sīsānulokī. Atha kho āyasmā puṇṇo mantāṇiputto andhavanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle divāvihāraṃ nisīdi. Āyasmāpi kho sāriputto andhavanaṃ ajjhogāhetvā aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle divāvihāraṃ nisīdi.

Then the Venerable Sāriputta, in a hurry, took his sitting cloth and followed closely behind the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta, keeping his eye on him. Then the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta entered the Andhavana and sat down at the foot of a certain tree for his day’s abiding. The Venerable Sāriputta also entered the Andhavana and sat down at the foot of a certain tree for his day’s abiding.

Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yenāyasmā puṇṇo mantāṇiputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā puṇṇena mantāṇiputtena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ puṇṇaṃ mantāṇiputtaṃ etadavoca –

Then, in the evening, the Venerable Sāriputta arose from seclusion and approached the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta. When they had concluded their greetings and polite conversation, he sat down to one side. As he sat to one side, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta:

257. ‘‘Bhagavati no, āvuso, brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

257.“Friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One?”

‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti.

“Yes, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, sīlavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

“Friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification of virtue?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso, cittavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

“But friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification of mind?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, diṭṭhivisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

“Friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification of view?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso, kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

“But friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification by overcoming doubt?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

“Friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision of what is the path and what is not the path?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso, paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

“But friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision of the way?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, ñāṇadassanavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti?

“Friend, is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, sīlavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’ti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘no hidaṃ, āvuso’ti vadesi. ‘Kiṃ panāvuso, cittavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’ti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘no hidaṃ, āvuso’ti vadesi. ‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, diṭṭhivisuddhatthaṃ…pe… kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhatthaṃ…pe… maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhatthaṃ…pe… paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhatthaṃ…pe… kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, ñāṇadassanavisuddhatthaṃ bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’ti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘no hidaṃ āvuso’ti vadesi. Kimatthaṃ carahāvuso, bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti? ‘‘Anupādāparinibbānatthaṃ kho, āvuso, bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti.

“Friend, when you were asked: ‘Is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification of virtue?’ you replied: ‘No, friend.’ When you were asked: ‘Is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification of mind?’ you replied: ‘No, friend.’ When you were asked: ‘Is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification of view?’… ‘for the sake of purification by overcoming doubt?’… ‘for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision of what is the path and what is not the path?’… ‘for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision of the way?’… ‘Friend, when you were asked: ‘Is the holy life lived under the Blessed One for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision?’ you replied: ‘No, friend.’ Then, friend, for what purpose is the holy life lived under the Blessed One?” “Friend, the holy life is lived under the Blessed One for the sake of final Nibbāna without clinging.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, sīlavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

“Friend, is purification of virtue final Nibbāna without clinging?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso, cittavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

“But friend, is purification of mind final Nibbāna without clinging?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, diṭṭhivisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

“Friend, is purification of view final Nibbāna without clinging?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

“But friend, is purification by overcoming doubt final Nibbāna without clinging?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

“Friend, is purification by knowledge and vision of what is the path and what is not the path final Nibbāna without clinging?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso, paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

“But friend, is purification by knowledge and vision of the way final Nibbāna without clinging?”

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

“No, friend.”

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, ñāṇadassanavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

Is purification of knowledge and vision, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

No, friend.

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso, aññatra imehi dhammehi anupādāparinibbāna’’nti?

But friend, is final Nibbāna without clinging apart from these qualities?

‘‘No hidaṃ, āvuso’’.

No, friend.

‘‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, sīlavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’nti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘no hidaṃ, āvuso’ti vadesi. ‘Kiṃ panāvuso, cittavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’nti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘no hidaṃ, āvuso’ti vadesi. ‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, diṭṭhivisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’nti…pe… kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhi… maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhi… paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhi… ‘kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, ñāṇadassanavisuddhi anupādāparinibbāna’nti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘no hidaṃ, āvuso’ti vadesi. ‘Kiṃ panāvuso, aññatra imehi dhammehi anupādāparinibbāna’nti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘no hidaṃ, āvuso’ti vadesi. Yathākathaṃ panāvuso, imassa bhāsitassa attho daṭṭhabbo’’ti?

‘Is purification of virtue, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ ‘Is purification of mind, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ ‘Is purification of view, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ ‘Is purification by overcoming doubt, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ ‘Is purification by knowledge and vision of what is the path and what is not the path, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ ‘Is purification by knowledge and vision of the way, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ ‘Is purification of knowledge and vision, friend, final Nibbāna without clinging?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ ‘Is final Nibbāna without clinging, friend, apart from these qualities?’ When asked this, you say, ‘No, friend.’ But then, friend, how should the meaning of this statement be understood?

258. ‘‘Sīlavisuddhiṃ ce, āvuso, bhagavā anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya, saupādānaṃyeva samānaṃ anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya [paññāpessa (sī. syā.) evamaññatthapi]. Cittavisuddhiṃ ce, āvuso, bhagavā anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya, saupādānaṃyeva samānaṃ anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya. Diṭṭhivisuddhiṃ ce, āvuso, bhagavā anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya, saupādānaṃyeva samānaṃ anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya. Kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhiṃ ce, āvuso, bhagavā anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya, saupādānaṃyeva samānaṃ anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya. Maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhiṃ ce, āvuso, bhagavā anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya, saupādānaṃyeva samānaṃ anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya. Paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhiṃ ce, āvuso, bhagavā anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya, saupādānaṃyeva samānaṃ anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya. Ñāṇadassanavisuddhiṃ ce, āvuso, bhagavā anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya, saupādānaṃyeva samānaṃ anupādāparinibbānaṃ paññapeyya. Aññatra ce, āvuso, imehi dhammehi anupādāparinibbānaṃ abhavissa, puthujjano parinibbāyeyya. Puthujjano hi, āvuso, aññatra imehi dhammehi. Tena hāvuso, upamaṃ te karissāmi; upamāyapidhekacce viññū purisā bhāsitassa atthaṃ ājānanti.

258.Friend, if the Blessed One were to declare final Nibbāna without clinging by means of purification of virtue, he would be declaring final Nibbāna with clinging for one who still has clinging. If the Blessed One were to declare final Nibbāna without clinging by means of purification of mind, he would be declaring final Nibbāna with clinging for one who still has clinging. If the Blessed One were to declare final Nibbāna without clinging by means of purification of view, he would be declaring final Nibbāna with clinging for one who still has clinging. If the Blessed One were to declare final Nibbāna without clinging by means of purification through overcoming doubt, he would be declaring final Nibbāna with clinging for one who still has clinging. If the Blessed One were to declare final Nibbāna without clinging by means of purification by knowledge and vision of what is the path and what is not the path, he would be declaring final Nibbāna with clinging for one who still has clinging. If the Blessed One were to declare final Nibbāna without clinging by means of purification by knowledge and vision of the way, he would be declaring final Nibbāna with clinging for one who still has clinging. If the Blessed One were to declare final Nibbāna without clinging by means of purification of knowledge and vision, he would be declaring final Nibbāna with clinging for one who still has clinging. Friend, if final Nibbāna without clinging were possible apart from these qualities, then an ordinary person would attain final Nibbāna. For an ordinary person is apart from these qualities. Therefore, friend, I shall give you a simile; for by means of a simile, some wise people understand the meaning of what is said.

259. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, āvuso, rañño pasenadissa kosalassa sāvatthiyaṃ paṭivasantassa sākete kiñcideva accāyikaṃ karaṇīyaṃ uppajjeyya. Tassa antarā ca sāvatthiṃ antarā ca sāketaṃ satta rathavinītāni upaṭṭhapeyyuṃ. Atha kho, āvuso, rājā pasenadi kosalo sāvatthiyā nikkhamitvā antepuradvārā paṭhamaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruheyya, paṭhamena rathavinītena dutiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇeyya, paṭhamaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjeyya dutiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruheyya. Dutiyena rathavinītena tatiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇeyya, dutiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjeyya, tatiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruheyya. Tatiyena rathavinītena catutthaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇeyya, tatiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjeyya, catutthaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruheyya. Catutthena rathavinītena pañcamaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇeyya, catutthaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjeyya, pañcamaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruheyya. Pañcamena rathavinītena chaṭṭhaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇeyya, pañcamaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjeyya, chaṭṭhaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruheyya. Chaṭṭhena rathavinītena sattamaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇeyya, chaṭṭhaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjeyya, sattamaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruheyya. Sattamena rathavinītena sāketaṃ anupāpuṇeyya antepuradvāraṃ. Tamenaṃ antepuradvāragataṃ samānaṃ mittāmaccā ñātisālohitā evaṃ puccheyyuṃ – ‘iminā tvaṃ, mahārāja, rathavinītena sāvatthiyā sāketaṃ anuppatto antepuradvāra’nti? Kathaṃ byākaramāno nu kho, āvuso, rājā pasenadi kosalo sammā byākaramāno byākareyyā’’ti?

259.Just as, friend, if King Pasenadi of Kosala, while residing in Savatthi, were to have some urgent business arise in Saketa, seven relay chariots would be stationed for him between Savatthi and Saketa. Then, friend, King Pasenadi of Kosala, having departed from Savatthi, would mount the first relay chariot at the palace gate. By means of the first relay chariot, he would reach the second relay chariot, then dismiss the first and mount the second. By means of the second relay chariot, he would reach the third relay chariot, then dismiss the second and mount the third. By means of the third relay chariot, he would reach the fourth relay chariot, then dismiss the third and mount the fourth. By means of the fourth relay chariot, he would reach the fifth relay chariot, then dismiss the fourth and mount the fifth. By means of the fifth relay chariot, he would reach the sixth relay chariot, then dismiss the fifth and mount the sixth. By means of the sixth relay chariot, he would reach the seventh relay chariot, then dismiss the sixth and mount the seventh. By means of the seventh relay chariot, he would arrive at Saketa, at the palace gate. When he had arrived at the palace gate, his friends, ministers, relatives, and blood kin would ask him: ‘Great king, did you come from Savatthi to Saketa by means of this relay chariot?’ How, friend, should King Pasenadi of Kosala answer in order to answer rightly?

‘‘Evaṃ byākaramāno kho, āvuso, rājā pasenadi kosalo sammā byākaramāno byākareyya – ‘idha me sāvatthiyaṃ paṭivasantassa sākete kiñcideva accāyikaṃ karaṇīyaṃ uppajji [uppajjati (ka.)]. Tassa me antarā ca sāvatthiṃ antarā ca sāketaṃ satta rathavinītāni upaṭṭhapesuṃ. Atha khvāhaṃ sāvatthiyā nikkhamitvā antepuradvārā paṭhamaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruhiṃ. Paṭhamena rathavinītena dutiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇiṃ, paṭhamaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjiṃ dutiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruhiṃ. Dutiyena rathavinītena tatiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇiṃ, dutiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjiṃ, tatiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruhiṃ. Tatiyena rathavinītena catutthaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇiṃ, tatiyaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjiṃ, catutthaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruhiṃ. Catutthena rathavinītena pañcamaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇiṃ, catutthaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjiṃ, pañcamaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruhiṃ. Pañcamena rathavinītena chaṭṭhaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇiṃ, pañcamaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjiṃ, chaṭṭhaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruhiṃ. Chaṭṭhena rathavinītena sattamaṃ rathavinītaṃ pāpuṇiṃ, chaṭṭhaṃ rathavinītaṃ vissajjiṃ, sattamaṃ rathavinītaṃ abhiruhiṃ. Sattamena rathavinītena sāketaṃ anuppatto antepuradvāra’nti. Evaṃ byākaramāno kho, āvuso, rājā pasenadi kosalo sammā byākaramāno byākareyyā’’ti.

Friend, if King Pasenadi Kosala were to answer in this way, he would be answering correctly: ‘While I was residing in Savatthi, some urgent business arose for me in Saketa. So between Savatthi and Saketa, seven relay chariots were provided for me. Then, having left Savatthi from the palace gate, I mounted the first relay chariot. With the first relay chariot, I reached the second relay chariot. I dismissed the first relay chariot and mounted the second relay chariot. With the second relay chariot, I reached the third relay chariot. I dismissed the second relay chariot and mounted the third relay chariot. With the third relay chariot, I reached the fourth relay chariot. I dismissed the third relay chariot and mounted the fourth relay chariot. With the fourth relay chariot, I reached the fifth relay chariot. I dismissed the fourth relay chariot and mounted the fifth relay chariot. With the fifth relay chariot, I reached the sixth relay chariot. I dismissed the fifth relay chariot and mounted the sixth relay chariot. With the sixth relay chariot, I reached the seventh relay chariot. I dismissed the sixth relay chariot and mounted the seventh relay chariot. With the seventh relay chariot, I arrived at Saketa at the palace gate.’ Friend, if King Pasenadi Kosala were to answer in this way, he would be answering correctly.

‘‘Evameva kho, āvuso, sīlavisuddhi yāvadeva cittavisuddhatthā, cittavisuddhi yāvadeva diṭṭhivisuddhatthā, diṭṭhivisuddhi yāvadeva kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhatthā, kaṅkhāvitaraṇavisuddhi yāvadeva maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhatthā, maggāmaggañāṇadassanavisuddhi yāvadeva paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhatthā, paṭipadāñāṇadassanavisuddhi yāvadeva ñāṇadassanavisuddhatthā, ñāṇadassanavisuddhi yāvadeva anupādāparinibbānatthā. Anupādāparinibbānatthaṃ kho, āvuso, bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’’ti.

Just so, friend, purification of virtue is for the sake of purification of mind; purification of mind is for the sake of purification of view; purification of view is for the sake of purification by overcoming doubt; purification by overcoming doubt is for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision of what is the path and what is not the path; purification by knowledge and vision of what is the path and what is not the path is for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision of the way; purification by knowledge and vision of the way is for the sake of purification by knowledge and vision; purification by knowledge and vision is for the sake of final Nibbāna without clinging. It is for the sake of final Nibbāna without clinging, friend, that the holy life is lived under the Blessed One.

260. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ puṇṇaṃ mantāṇiputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘konāmo āyasmā, kathañca panāyasmantaṃ sabrahmacārī jānantī’’ti? ‘‘Puṇṇoti kho me, āvuso, nāmaṃ; mantāṇiputtoti ca pana maṃ sabrahmacārī jānantī’’ti. ‘‘Acchariyaṃ, āvuso, abbhutaṃ, āvuso! Yathā taṃ sutavatā sāvakena sammadeva satthusāsanaṃ ājānantena, evameva āyasmatā puṇṇena mantāṇiputtena gambhīrā gambhīrapañhā anumassa anumassa byākatā. Lābhā sabrahmacārīnaṃ, suladdhalābhā sabrahmacārīnaṃ, ye āyasmantaṃ puṇṇaṃ mantāṇiputtaṃ labhanti dassanāya, labhanti payirūpāsanāya. Celaṇḍukena [celaṇḍakena (ka.), celaṇḍupekena (?)] cepi sabrahmacārī āyasmantaṃ puṇṇaṃ mantāṇiputtaṃ muddhanā pariharantā labheyyuṃ dassanāya, labheyyuṃ payirūpāsanāya, tesampi lābhā tesampi suladdhaṃ, amhākampi lābhā amhākampi suladdhaṃ, ye mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ puṇṇaṃ mantāṇiputtaṃ labhāma dassanāya, labhāma payirūpāsanāyā’’ti.

260.When this was said, Venerable Sāriputta said to Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta: “What, Venerable, is your name, and how do your fellow monastics know you?” “My name is Puṇṇa, friend, and my fellow monastics know me as Mantāṇiputta.” “It is wonderful, friend, it is amazing! Just as a learned disciple rightly understands the Teacher’s instruction, so too has Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta answered these deep and profound questions detail by detail. It is a gain for the spiritual companions, a great gain for the spiritual companions, who get to see and attend upon Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta. Even if the spiritual companions had to carry Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta on their heads to see and attend upon him, it would still be a gain for them, a great gain for them. It is a gain for us, a great gain for us, that we get to see and attend upon Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta.”

Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā puṇṇo mantāṇiputto āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ko nāmo āyasmā, kathañca panāyasmantaṃ sabrahmacārī jānantī’’ti? ‘‘Upatissoti kho me, āvuso, nāmaṃ; sāriputtoti ca pana maṃ sabrahmacārī jānantī’’ti. ‘‘Satthukappena vata kira, bho [kho (ka.)], sāvakena saddhiṃ mantayamānā na jānimha – ‘āyasmā sāriputto’ti. Sace hi mayaṃ jāneyyāma ‘āyasmā sāriputto’ti, ettakampi no nappaṭibhāseyya [nappaṭibheyya (?)]. Acchariyaṃ, āvuso, abbhutaṃ, āvuso! Yathā taṃ sutavatā sāvakena sammadeva satthusāsanaṃ ājānantena, evameva āyasmatā sāriputtena gambhīrā gambhīrapañhā anumassa anumassa pucchitā. Lābhā sabrahmacārīnaṃ suladdhalābhā sabrahmacārīnaṃ, ye āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ labhanti dassanāya, labhanti payirūpāsanāya. Celaṇḍukena cepi sabrahmacārī āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ muddhanā pariharantā labheyyuṃ dassanāya, labheyyuṃ payirūpāsanāya, tesampi lābhā tesampi suladdhaṃ, amhākampi lābhā amhākampi suladdhaṃ, ye mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ labhāma dassanāya, labhāma payirūpāsanāyā’’ti.

When this was said, Venerable Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta said to Venerable Sāriputta: “What is your name, venerable, and how do your spiritual companions know you?” “My name is Upatissa, friend, and my spiritual companions know me as Sāriputta.” “Indeed, friend, while conversing with a disciple comparable to the Teacher, we did not realize it was Venerable Sāriputta! If we had known it was Venerable Sāriputta, even this much would not have occurred to us. Wonderful, friend, amazing, friend! Just as a learned disciple rightly comprehends the Teacher’s instruction, so has Venerable Sāriputta asked these deep and profound questions, examining point by point. It is a gain for our spiritual companions, well gained for our spiritual companions, who obtain the opportunity to see and attend upon Venerable Sāriputta. Even if spiritual companions were to carry Venerable Sāriputta on their heads, using a cloth cushion, and thereby obtain the opportunity to see and attend upon him, it would be a gain for them, well gained for them. It is a gain for us, well gained for us, that we obtain the opportunity to see and attend upon Venerable Sāriputta.”

Itiha te ubhopi mahānāgā aññamaññassa subhāsitaṃ samanumodiṃsūti.

Thus, both of these great beings rejoiced in each other's well-spoken words.

Rathavinītasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The Rathavinīta Sutta, the fourth, is concluded.
