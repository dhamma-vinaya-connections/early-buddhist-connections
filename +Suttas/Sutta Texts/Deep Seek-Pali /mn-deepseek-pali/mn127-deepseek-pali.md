# 127. Upakkilesasuttaṃ

## 127. The Discourse on Taints

236. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena kosambiyaṃ bhikkhū bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharanti. Atha kho aññataro bhikkhu yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho so bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idha, bhante, kosambiyaṃ bhikkhū bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharanti. Sādhu, bhante, bhagavā yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkamatu anukampaṃ upādāyā’’ti. Adhivāsesi bhagavā tuṇhībhāvena. Atha kho bhagavā yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘alaṃ, bhikkhave, mā bhaṇḍanaṃ, mā kalahaṃ, mā viggahaṃ, mā vivāda’’nti.

236.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambi, in Ghosita’s Park. At that time the bhikkhus at Kosambi were quarrelsome, given to contention, addicted to disputes, stabbing each other with verbal weapons. Then a certain bhikkhu approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, that bhikkhu said to the Blessed One: “Here, venerable sir, the bhikkhus at Kosambi are quarrelsome, given to contention, addicted to disputes, stabbing each other with verbal weapons. It would be good, venerable sir, if the Blessed One would approach those bhikkhus out of compassion for them.” The Blessed One consented by silence. Then the Blessed One approached those bhikkhus and said to them: “Enough, bhikkhus! No quarreling, no contention, no strife, no dispute!”

Evaṃ vutte, aññataro bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘āgametu, bhante! Bhagavā dhammassāmī; appossukko, bhante, bhagavā diṭṭhadhammasukhavihāraṃ anuyutto viharatu; mayametena bhaṇḍanena kalahena viggahena vivādena paññāyissāmā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho bhagavā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘alaṃ, bhikkhave, mā bhaṇḍanaṃ, mā kalahaṃ, mā viggahaṃ, mā vivāda’’nti. Dutiyampi kho so bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘āgametu, bhante! Bhagavā dhammassāmī; appossukko, bhante, bhagavā diṭṭhadhammasukhavihāraṃ anuyutto viharatu; mayametena bhaṇḍanena kalahena viggahena vivādena paññāyissāmā’’ti. Tatiyampi kho bhagavā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘alaṃ, bhikkhave, mā bhaṇḍanaṃ, mā kalahaṃ, mā viggahaṃ, mā vivāda’’nti. Tatiyampi kho so bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘āgametu, bhante, bhagavā dhammassāmī; appossukko, bhante, bhagavā diṭṭhadhammasukhavihāraṃ anuyutto viharatu; mayametena bhaṇḍanena kalahena viggahena vivādena paññāyissāmā’’ti.

When this was said, a certain bhikkhu addressed the Blessed One: “Please, venerable sir, let the Blessed One be! The Blessed One is the Master of the Dhamma. Let the Blessed One, venerable sir, dwell without concern, devoted to a pleasant abiding in this very life. We will be known by this quarreling, contention, strife, and dispute.” A second time, the Blessed One addressed those bhikkhus: “Enough, bhikkhus! No quarreling, no contention, no strife, no dispute!” A second time, that bhikkhu addressed the Blessed One: “Please, venerable sir, let the Blessed One be! The Blessed One is the Master of the Dhamma. Let the Blessed One, venerable sir, dwell without concern, devoted to a pleasant abiding in this very life. We will be known by this quarreling, contention, strife, and dispute.” A third time, the Blessed One addressed those bhikkhus: “Enough, bhikkhus! No quarreling, no contention, no strife, no dispute!” A third time, that bhikkhu addressed the Blessed One: “Please, venerable sir, let the Blessed One be! The Blessed One is the Master of the Dhamma. Let the Blessed One, venerable sir, dwell without concern, devoted to a pleasant abiding in this very life. We will be known by this quarreling, contention, strife, and dispute.”

Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya kosambiṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Kosambiyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaramādāya ṭhitakova imā gāthā abhāsi –

Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the forenoon, taking his bowl and robe, entered Kosambi for almsfood. Having wandered for almsfood in Kosambi and returned from his alms round after the meal, he put away his lodging, took his bowl and robe, and standing, he spoke these verses:

237.

‘‘Puthusaddo samajano, na bālo koci maññatha;Saṅghasmiṃ bhijjamānasmiṃ, nāññaṃ bhiyyo amaññaruṃ.

“The crowd makes a loud noise, not thinking anyone is a fool; When the Saṅgha is breaking apart, they did not think of anything else more important.”

‘‘Parimuṭṭhā paṇḍitābhāsā, vācāgocarabhāṇino;Yāvicchanti mukhāyāmaṃ, yena nītā na taṃ vidū.

“Though appearing wise, they are deluded, mere speakers of words; They desire only what the mouth utters, and do not know the way by which they are led.”

‘‘Akkocchi maṃ avadhi maṃ, ajini maṃ ahāsi me;Ye ca taṃ upanayhanti, veraṃ tesaṃ na sammati.

“He abused me, he struck me, he overcame me, he robbed me.” In those who harbor such thoughts, hatred will never cease.

‘‘Akkocchi maṃ avadhi maṃ, ajini maṃ ahāsi me;Ye ca taṃ nupanayhanti, veraṃ tesūpasammati.

“He abused me, he struck me, he overcame me, he robbed me.” Those who do not harbor such thoughts, their hatred is appeased.

‘‘Na hi verena verāni, sammantīdha kudācanaṃ;Averena ca sammanti, esa dhammo sanantano.

“Hatred is never appeased by hatred in this world; it is appeased by love. This is an eternal law.”

‘‘Pare ca na vijānanti, mayamettha yamāmase;Ye ca tattha vijānanti, tato sammanti medhagā.

“Others do not realize that here we must perish; those who realize this, their quarrels are calmed.”

‘‘Aṭṭhicchinnā pāṇaharā, gavassadhanahārino;Raṭṭhaṃ vilumpamānānaṃ, tesampi hoti saṅgati;Kasmā tumhākaṃ no siyā.

“Even mutilators, those who take life, steal cattle and wealth, and plunder the country find companionship. Why should there not be any for you?”

‘‘Sace labhetha nipakaṃ sahāyaṃ,Saddhiṃ caraṃ sādhuvihāri dhīraṃ;Abhibhuyya sabbāni parissayāni,Careyya tenattamano satīmā.

“If one finds a wise companion, A fellow traveler, virtuous and steadfast, Overcoming all dangers, One should wander with them, joyful and mindful.”

‘‘No ce labhetha nipakaṃ sahāyaṃ,Saddhiṃ caraṃ sādhuvihāri dhīraṃ;Rājāva raṭṭhaṃ vijitaṃ pahāya,Eko care mātaṅgaraññeva nāgo.

“If one does not find a prudent companion, A fellow traveler, virtuous and steadfast, Like a king who has abandoned a conquered realm, One should wander alone like a tusker in the elephant forest.”

‘‘Ekassa caritaṃ seyyo, natthi bāle sahāyatā;Eko care na ca pāpāni kayirā,Appossukko mātaṅgaraññeva nāgo’’ti.

“Better is a solitary life; there is no companionship with a fool. Let one wander alone, doing no evil, with few wants, like an elephant in the Mātaṅga forest.”

238. Atha kho bhagavā ṭhitakova imā gāthā bhāsitvā yena bālakaloṇakāragāmo [bālakaloṇakagāmo (ka.), tathā vinayepi] tenupasaṅkami. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā bhagu bālakaloṇakāragāme viharati. Addasā kho āyasmā bhagu bhagavantaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna āsanaṃ paññapesi udakañca pādānaṃ dhovanaṃ [udakañca pādānaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)]. Nisīdi bhagavā paññatte āsane. Nisajja pāde pakkhālesi. Āyasmāpi kho bhagu bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ bhaguṃ bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, bhikkhu, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyaṃ, kacci piṇḍakena na kilamasī’’ti? ‘‘Khamanīyaṃ bhagavā, yāpanīyaṃ bhagavā, na cāhaṃ, bhante, piṇḍakena kilamāmī’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ bhaguṃ dhammiyā kathāya sandassetvā samādapetvā samuttejetvā sampahaṃsetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena pācīnavaṃsadāyo tenupasaṅkami.

238.Then the Blessed One, standing right there, spoke these verses and proceeded to the village of Bālakaloṇakāra. At that time, the Venerable Bhagu was dwelling in the village of Bālakaloṇakāra. The Venerable Bhagu saw the Blessed One coming from afar. Having seen him, he prepared a seat and water for washing the feet. The Blessed One sat down on the prepared seat and washed his feet. The Venerable Bhagu then paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Blessed One said to the Venerable Bhagu: “I hope you are keeping well, bhikkhu, I hope you are comfortable, I hope you are not suffering from lack of almsfood?” “I am keeping well, Blessed One, I am comfortable, Blessed One, and I do not suffer from lack of almsfood, venerable sir.” Then the Blessed One instructed, urged, roused, and gladdened the Venerable Bhagu with a Dhamma talk. Rising from his seat, he proceeded to the Eastern Bamboo Grove.

Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā ca anuruddho āyasmā ca nandiyo [bhaddiyo (ma. ni. 2.166 naḷakapāne] āyasmā ca kimilo [kimbilo (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] pācīnavaṃsadāye viharanti. Addasā kho dāyapālo bhagavantaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mā, mahāsamaṇa, etaṃ dāyaṃ pāvisi. Santettha tayo kulaputtā attakāmarūpā viharanti. Mā tesaṃ aphāsumakāsī’’ti. Assosi kho āyasmā anuruddho dāyapālassa bhagavatā saddhiṃ mantayamānassa. Sutvāna dāyapālaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mā, āvuso dāyapāla, bhagavantaṃ vāresi. Satthā no bhagavā anuppatto’’ti.

At that time, the Venerable Anuruddha, the Venerable Nandiya, and the Venerable Kimbila were dwelling in the Eastern Bamboo Grove. The grove keeper saw the Blessed One coming from afar. Having seen him, he said to the Blessed One: “Great ascetic, do not enter this grove. There are three young men of good family dwelling here who are devoted to their own practice. Do not cause them discomfort.” The Venerable Anuruddha heard the grove keeper speaking with the Blessed One. Having heard this, he said to the grove keeper: “Friend grove keeper, do not prevent the Blessed One. The Blessed One, our Teacher, has arrived.”

239. Atha kho āyasmā anuruddho yenāyasmā ca nandiyo yenāyasmā ca kimilo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantañca nandiyaṃ āyasmantañca kimilaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkamathāyasmanto, abhikkamathāyasmanto, satthā no bhagavā anuppatto’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā ca anuruddho āyasmā ca nandiyo āyasmā ca kimilo bhagavantaṃ paccuggantvā eko bhagavato pattacīvaraṃ paṭiggahesi, eko āsanaṃ paññapesi, eko pādodakaṃ upaṭṭhapesi. Nisīdi bhagavā paññatte āsane. Nisajja pāde pakkhālesi. Tepi kho āyasmanto bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘kacci vo, anuruddhā, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyaṃ, kacci piṇḍakena na kilamathā’’ti? ‘‘Khamanīyaṃ bhagavā, yāpanīyaṃ bhagavā, na ca mayaṃ, bhante, piṇḍakena kilamāmā’’ti. ‘‘Kacci pana vo, anuruddhā, samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā khīrodakībhūtā aññamaññaṃ piyacakkhūhi sampassantā viharathā’’ti? ‘‘Taggha mayaṃ, bhante, samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā khīrodakībhūtā aññamaññaṃ piyacakkhūhi sampassantā viharāmā’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana tumhe, anuruddhā, samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā khīrodakībhūtā aññamaññaṃ piyacakkhūhi sampassantā viharathā’’ti? ‘‘Idha mayhaṃ, bhante, evaṃ hoti – ‘lābhā vata me, suladdhaṃ vata me yohaṃ evarūpehi sabrahmacārīhi saddhiṃ viharāmī’ti. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, imesu āyasmantesu mettaṃ kāyakammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ āvi ceva raho ca, mettaṃ vacīkammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ āvi ceva raho ca, mettaṃ manokammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ āvi ceva raho ca. Tassa, mayhaṃ, bhante, evaṃ hoti – ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ sakaṃ cittaṃ nikkhipitvā imesaṃyeva āyasmantānaṃ cittassa vasena vatteyya’nti. So kho ahaṃ, bhante, sakaṃ cittaṃ nikkhipitvā imesaṃyeva āyasmantānaṃ cittassa vasena vattāmi. Nānā hi kho no, bhante, kāyā, ekañca pana maññe citta’’nti.

239.Then the Venerable Anuruddha approached the Venerable Nandiya and the Venerable Kimbila. Having approached, he said to the Venerable Nandiya and the Venerable Kimbila: “Come forward, venerables, come forward! The Blessed One, our Teacher, has arrived.” Then the Venerable Anuruddha, the Venerable Nandiya, and the Venerable Kimbila went out to meet the Blessed One. One received the Blessed One’s bowl and robe, one prepared a seat, and one set out water for washing the feet. The Blessed One sat down on the prepared seat and washed his feet. Those venerables paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Blessed One said to the Venerable Anuruddha: “I hope, Anuruddha, that you are keeping well, I hope you are comfortable, I hope you are not suffering from lack of almsfood?” “We are keeping well, Blessed One, we are comfortable, Blessed One, and we do not suffer from lack of almsfood, venerable sir.” “I hope, Anuruddha, that you are living in concord, with mutual appreciation, without dispute, blending like milk and water, regarding each other with friendly eyes?” “Surely, venerable sir, we are living in concord, with mutual appreciation, without dispute, blending like milk and water, regarding each other with friendly eyes.” “But how, Anuruddha, do you live in concord, with mutual appreciation, without dispute, blending like milk and water, regarding each other with friendly eyes?” “Here, venerable sir, it occurs to me: ‘It is a gain for me, a great gain for me, that I live together with such companions in the holy life.’ Towards these venerable ones, I maintain bodily acts of loving-kindness, both in public and in private; I maintain verbal acts of loving-kindness, both in public and in private; I maintain mental acts of loving-kindness, both in public and in private. Then, venerable sir, it occurs to me: ‘Why don’t I set aside my own mind and follow according to the minds of these venerable ones?’ And so, venerable sir, I set aside my own mind and follow according to the minds of these venerable ones. Indeed, venerable sir, we are different in body, but I think we are one in mind.”

Āyasmāpi kho nandiyo…pe… āyasmāpi kho kimilo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mayhampi kho, bhante, evaṃ hoti – ‘lābhā vata me, suladdhaṃ vata me yohaṃ evarūpehi sabrahmacārīhi saddhiṃ viharāmī’ti. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, imesu āyasmantesu mettaṃ kāyakammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ āvi ceva raho ca, mettaṃ vacīkammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ āvi ceva raho ca, mettaṃ manokammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ āvi ceva raho ca. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, evaṃ hoti – ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ sakaṃ cittaṃ nikkhipitvā imesaṃyeva āyasmantānaṃ cittassa vasena vatteyya’nti. So kho ahaṃ, bhante, sakaṃ cittaṃ nikkhipitvā imesaṃyeva āyasmantānaṃ cittassa vasena vattāmi. Nānā hi kho no, bhante, kāyā, ekañca pana maññe cittanti. Evaṃ kho mayaṃ, bhante, samaggā sammodamānā avivadamānā khīrodakībhūtā aññamaññaṃ piyacakkhūhi sampassantā viharāmā’’ti.

Venerable Nandiya… and Venerable Kimila also said to the Blessed One: “For me too, venerable sir, this thought occurred: ‘It is truly my gain, my great good fortune, that I dwell with such spiritual companions.’ In regard to these venerable ones, bodily acts of loving-kindness are present to me, both openly and in private; verbal acts of loving-kindness are present to me, both openly and in private; and mental acts of loving-kindness are present to me, both openly and in private. It occurred to me, venerable sir: ‘Why don’t I set aside my own mind and act according to the minds of these venerable ones?’ So, venerable sir, I set aside my own mind and act according to the minds of these venerable ones. For though our bodies are different, our minds, I think, are one. Thus, venerable sir, we dwell in concord, mutual appreciation, without disputes, blending like milk and water, regarding each other with eyes of affection.”

240. ‘‘Sādhu, sādhu, anuruddhā! Kacci pana vo, anuruddhā, appamattā ātāpino pahitattā viharathā’’ti? ‘‘Taggha mayaṃ, bhante, appamattā ātāpino pahitattā viharāmā’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana tumhe, anuruddhā, appamattā ātāpino pahitattā viharathā’’ti? ‘‘Idha, bhante, amhākaṃ yo paṭhamaṃ gāmato piṇḍāya paṭikkamati, so āsanāni paññapeti, pānīyaṃ paribhojanīyaṃ upaṭṭhāpeti, avakkārapātiṃ upaṭṭhāpeti. Yo pacchā gāmato piṇḍāya paṭikkamati – sace hoti bhuttāvaseso, sace ākaṅkhati, bhuñjati; no ce ākaṅkhati, appaharite vā chaḍḍeti apāṇake vā udake opilāpeti – so āsanāni paṭisāmeti, pānīyaṃ paribhojanīyaṃ paṭisāmeti, avakkārapātiṃ dhovitvā paṭisāmeti, bhattaggaṃ sammajjati. Yo passati pānīyaghaṭaṃ vā paribhojanīyaghaṭaṃ vā vaccaghaṭaṃ vā rittaṃ tucchaṃ so upaṭṭhāpeti. Sacassa hoti avisayhaṃ, hatthavikārena dutiyaṃ āmantetvā hatthavilaṅghakena upaṭṭhāpema [upaṭṭhapeti (sī.)], na tveva mayaṃ, bhante, tappaccayā vācaṃ bhindāma. Pañcāhikaṃ kho pana mayaṃ, bhante, sabbarattiṃ dhammiyā kathāya sannisīdāma. Evaṃ kho mayaṃ, bhante, appamattā ātāpino pahitattā viharāmā’’ti.

240.“Good, good, Anuruddhas! Are you, Anuruddhas, dwelling heedfully, ardent, and resolute?” “Indeed, venerable sir, we dwell heedfully, ardent, and resolute.” “And how, Anuruddhas, do you dwell heedfully, ardent, and resolute?” “Here, venerable sir, whoever among us returns first from the alms round prepares the seats, sets out drinking water and water for use, and places the refuse bucket. Whoever returns later from the alms round—if there are leftovers and he wishes, he eats; if he does not wish, he discards them where there is little greenery or drops them into water where there are no living beings—he puts away the seats, drinking water, and water for use, washes the refuse bucket and puts it away, and sweeps the dining area. Whoever sees that the water pot for drinking, the water pot for use, or the toilet pot is empty or low, he replenishes them. If it is too heavy for him, he calls another by a gesture, and together we replenish it with our hands—but we do not, venerable sir, break into speech because of that. Every five days, we gather together the whole night for a Dhamma talk. Thus, venerable sir, we dwell heedfully, ardent, and resolute.”

241. ‘‘Sādhu, sādhu, anuruddhā! Atthi pana vo, anuruddhā, evaṃ appamattānaṃ ātāpīnaṃ pahitattānaṃ viharataṃ uttarimanussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanaviseso adhigato phāsuvihāro’’ti? ‘‘Idha mayaṃ, bhante, appamattā ātāpino pahitattā viharantā obhāsañceva sañjānāma dassanañca rūpānaṃ. So kho pana no obhāso nacirasseva antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ; tañca nimittaṃ nappaṭivijjhāmā’’ti.

241.“Good, good, Anuruddhas! But Anuruddhas, while you are dwelling thus heedfully, ardent, and resolute, has any superior human state, any distinction in knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones, any abiding in comfort, been attained by you?” “Here, venerable sir, while dwelling heedfully, ardent, and resolute, we perceive light and see forms. But that light soon disappears, and so does the seeing of forms; and we do not penetrate that sign.”

‘‘Taṃ kho pana vo, anuruddhā, nimittaṃ paṭivijjhitabbaṃ. Ahampi sudaṃ, anuruddhā, pubbeva sambodhā anabhisambuddho bodhisattova samāno obhāsañceva sañjānāmi dassanañca rūpānaṃ. So kho pana me obhāso nacirasseva antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘ko nu kho hetu ko paccayo yena me obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpāna’nti? Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘vicikicchā kho me udapādi, vicikicchādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissatī’’’ti.

“Anuruddhas, that sign should be penetrated by you. I too, Anuruddhas, before my full enlightenment, while still a bodhisatta, not yet fully enlightened, perceived light and saw forms. But soon after, that light and the seeing of forms disappeared. Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘What could be the cause, what the reason, why my light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears?’ Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Doubt had arisen in me, and because of doubt, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that doubt will not arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā, appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto obhāsañceva sañjānāmi dassanañca rūpānaṃ. So kho pana me obhāso nacirasseva antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘ko nu kho hetu ko paccayo yena me obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpāna’nti? Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘amanasikāro kho me udapādi, amanasikārādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati na amanasikāro’’’ti.

“So, Anuruddhas, dwelling heedfully, ardent, and resolute, I perceived light and saw forms. But soon after, that light and the seeing of forms disappeared. Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘What is the cause, what is the condition, by which my light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears?’ Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Lack of attention had arisen in me, and because of lack of attention, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that neither doubt nor lack of attention will arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘thinamiddhaṃ kho me udapādi, thinamiddhādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati na amanasikāro na thinamiddha’’’nti.

“So, Anuruddhas… Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Sloth and torpor had arisen in me, and because of sloth and torpor, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light vanishes, and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that neither doubt, nor lack of attention, nor sloth and torpor will arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘chambhitattaṃ kho me udapādi, chambhitattādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Seyyathāpi, anuruddhā, puriso addhānamaggappaṭipanno, tassa ubhatopasse vaṭṭakā [vadhakā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] uppateyyuṃ, tassa tatonidānaṃ chambhitattaṃ uppajjeyya; evameva kho me, anuruddhā, chambhitattaṃ udapādi, chambhitattādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati na amanasikāro na thinamiddhaṃ na chambhitatta’’’nti.

“So, Anuruddhas… Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Fearfulness had arisen in me, and because of fearfulness, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light vanishes, and the seeing of forms disappears. Just as, Anuruddhas, a man walking along a road might have quails fly up on both sides, and because of that, fearfulness would arise in him—so too, Anuruddhas, fearfulness had arisen in me, and because of fearfulness, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light vanishes, and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that neither doubt, nor lack of attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness will arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘uppilaṃ [ubbillaṃ (sī. pī.), ubbilaṃ (syā. kaṃ.)] kho me udapādi, uppilādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Seyyathāpi, anuruddhā, puriso ekaṃ nidhimukhaṃ gavesanto sakideva pañcanidhimukhāni adhigaccheyya, tassa tatonidānaṃ uppilaṃ uppajjeyya; evameva kho me, anuruddhā, uppilaṃ udapādi, uppilādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati, na amanasikāro, na thinamiddhaṃ, na chambhitattaṃ, na uppila’’’nti.

“So, Anuruddhas… Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Agitation had arisen in me, and because of agitation, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. Just as, Anuruddhas, a man seeking one treasure might suddenly find five treasure troves, and because of that, agitation would arise in him; in the same way, Anuruddhas, agitation had arisen in me, and because of agitation, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that neither doubt, nor lack of attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness, nor agitation will arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘duṭṭhullaṃ kho me udapādi, duṭṭhullādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati, na amanasikāro, na thinamiddhaṃ, na chambhitattaṃ, na uppilaṃ, na duṭṭhulla’’’nti.

“So, Anuruddhas… Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Impudence had arisen in me, and because of impudence, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears, and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that neither doubt, nor lack of attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness, nor agitation, nor impudence will arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘accāraddhavīriyaṃ kho me udapādi, accāraddhavīriyādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Seyyathāpi, anuruddhā, puriso ubhohi hatthehi vaṭṭakaṃ gāḷhaṃ gaṇheyya, so tattheva patameyya [matameyya (bahūsu) pa + taṃ + eyya = patameyya-iti padavibhāgo]; evameva kho me, anuruddhā, accāraddhavīriyaṃ udapādi, accāraddhavīriyādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati, na amanasikāro, na thinamiddhaṃ, na chambhitattaṃ, na uppilaṃ, na duṭṭhullaṃ, na accāraddhavīriya’’’nti.

“So, Anuruddhas… Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Over-exertion had arisen in me, and because of over-exertion, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. Just as, Anuruddhas, a man might grasp a quail tightly with both hands, and it would die right there; in the same way, Anuruddhas, over-exertion had arisen in me, and because of over-exertion, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that neither doubt, nor lack of attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness, nor agitation, nor impudence, nor over-exertion will arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘atilīnavīriyaṃ kho me udapādi, atilīnavīriyādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Seyyathāpi, anuruddhā, puriso vaṭṭakaṃ sithilaṃ gaṇheyya, so tassa hatthato uppateyya; evameva kho me, anuruddhā, atilīnavīriyaṃ udapādi, atilīnavīriyādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati, na amanasikāro, na thinamiddhaṃ, na chambhitattaṃ, na uppilaṃ, na duṭṭhullaṃ, na accāraddhavīriyaṃ, na atilīnavīriya’’’nti.

“So, Anuruddhas… Then, Anuruddhas, it occurred to me: ‘Under-exertion had arisen in me, and because of under-exertion, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. Just as, Anuruddhas, a man might grasp a quail loosely, and it would fly away from his hand; in the same way, Anuruddhas, under-exertion had arisen in me, and because of under-exertion, my samādhi wavered. When samādhi wavers, the light disappears and the seeing of forms disappears. I will act in such a way that neither doubt, nor lack of attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness, nor agitation, nor impudence, nor over-exertion, nor under-exertion will arise in me again.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘abhijappā kho me udapādi, abhijappādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati, na amanasikāro, na thinamiddhaṃ, na chambhitattaṃ, na uppilaṃ, na duṭṭhullaṃ, na accāraddhavīriyaṃ, na atilīnavīriyaṃ, na abhijappā’’’ti.

Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘Hankering has arisen in me, and because of hankering my samādhi has lapsed. When samādhi lapses, the light disappears and the vision of forms vanishes. I will act in such a way that doubt does not arise in me again, nor non-attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness, nor excitement, nor agitation, nor excessive energy, nor deficient energy, nor hankering.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā…pe… tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘nānattasaññā kho me udapādi, nānattasaññādhikaraṇañca pana me samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati, na amanasikāro, na thinamiddhaṃ, na chambhitattaṃ, na uppilaṃ, na duṭṭhullaṃ, na accāraddhavīriyaṃ, na atilīnavīriyaṃ, na abhijappā, na nānattasaññā’’’ti.

Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘Diversity of perception has arisen in me, and because of diversity of perception my samādhi has lapsed. When samādhi lapses, the light disappears and the vision of forms vanishes. I will act in such a way that doubt will not arise in me again, nor non-attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness, nor excitement, nor agitation, nor excessive energy, nor deficient energy, nor hankering, nor diversity of perception.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā, appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto obhāsañceva sañjānāmi dassanañca rūpānaṃ. So kho pana me obhāso nacirasseva antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ anuruddhā etadahosi – ‘ko nu kho hetu ko paccayo yena me obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpāna’nti. Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘atinijjhāyitattaṃ kho me rūpānaṃ udapādi, atinijjhāyitattādhikaraṇañca pana me rūpānaṃ samādhi cavi. Samādhimhi cute obhāso antaradhāyati dassanañca rūpānaṃ. Sohaṃ tathā karissāmi yathā me puna na vicikicchā uppajjissati, na amanasikāro, na thinamiddhaṃ, na chambhitattaṃ, na uppilaṃ, na duṭṭhullaṃ, na accāraddhavīriyaṃ, na atilīnavīriyaṃ, na abhijappā, na nānattasaññā, na atinijjhāyitattaṃ rūpāna’’’nti.

Then, Anuruddha, dwelling diligent, ardent, and resolute, I both perceived light and had the vision of forms. But soon after, that light and vision of forms disappeared. Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘What is the cause, what is the reason, why that light disappears and the vision of forms vanishes?’ Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘Excessive pondering over forms has arisen in me, and because of excessive pondering over forms my samādhi has lapsed. When samādhi lapses, the light disappears and the vision of forms vanishes. I will act in such a way that doubt does not arise in me again, nor non-attention, nor sloth and torpor, nor fearfulness, nor excitement, nor agitation, nor excessive energy, nor deficient energy, nor hankering, nor diversity of perception, nor excessive pondering over forms.’

242. ‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā, ‘vicikicchā cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā vicikicchaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘amanasikāro cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā amanasikāraṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘thinamiddhaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā thinamiddhaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘chambhitattaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā chambhitattaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘uppilaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā uppilaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘duṭṭhullaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā duṭṭhullaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘accāraddhavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā accāraddhavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘atilīnavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā atilīnavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘abhijappā cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā abhijappaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘nānattasaññā cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā nānattasaññaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ, ‘atinijjhāyitattaṃ rūpānaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā atinijjhāyitattaṃ rūpānaṃ cittassa upakkilesaṃ pajahiṃ.

242.So, Anuruddha, knowing that ‘doubt is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned doubt as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘non-attention is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned non-attention as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘sloth and torpor are a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned sloth and torpor as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘fearfulness is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned fearfulness as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘excitement is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned excitement as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘agitation is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned agitation as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘excessive energy is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned excessive energy as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘deficient energy is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned deficient energy as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘hankering is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned hankering as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘diversity of perception is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned diversity of perception as a defilement of the mind. Knowing that ‘excessive pondering over forms is a defilement of the mind,’ I abandoned excessive pondering over forms as a defilement of the mind.

243. ‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā, appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto obhāsañhi kho sañjānāmi, na ca rūpāni passāmi; rūpāni hi kho passāmi, na ca obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi – ‘kevalampi rattiṃ, kevalampi divaṃ [divasaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)], kevalampi rattindivaṃ’ [rattidivaṃ (ka.)]. Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘ko nu kho hetu ko paccayo yvāhaṃ obhāsañhi kho sañjānāmi na ca rūpāni passāmi; rūpāni hi kho [kho tasmiṃ samaye (sī. ka.)] passāmi na ca obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi – kevalampi rattiṃ, kevalampi divaṃ, kevalampi rattindiva’nti. Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘yasmiñhi kho ahaṃ samaye rūpanimittaṃ amanasikaritvā obhāsanimittaṃ manasi karomi, obhāsañhi kho tasmiṃ samaye sañjānāmi, na ca rūpāni passāmi. Yasmiṃ panāhaṃ samaye obhāsanimittaṃ amanasikaritvā rūpanimittaṃ manasi karomi, rūpāni hi kho tasmiṃ samaye passāmi na ca obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi – kevalampi rattiṃ, kevalampi divaṃ, kevalampi rattindiva’’’nti.

243.So, Anuruddha, dwelling diligent, ardent, and resolute, I cognized light, but I did not see forms; or I saw forms, but I did not cognize light—the entire night, the entire day, the entire night and day. Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘What is the cause, what is the reason, why I cognize light but do not see forms, or see forms but do not cognize light—the entire night, the entire day, the entire night and day?’ Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘When I do not attend to the sign of forms but attend to the sign of light, at that time I cognize light but do not see forms. But when I do not attend to the sign of light but attend to the sign of forms, at that time I see forms but I do not cognize light—the entire night, the entire day, the entire night and day.’

‘‘So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā, appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto parittañceva obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi, parittāni ca rūpāni passāmi; appamāṇañceva obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi, appamāṇāni ca rūpāni passāmi – kevalampi rattiṃ, kevalampi divaṃ, kevalampi rattindivaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘ko nu kho hetu ko paccayo yvāhaṃ parittañceva obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi, parittāni ca rūpāni passāmi; appamāṇañceva obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi, appamāṇāni ca rūpāni passāmi – kevalampi rattiṃ, kevalampi divaṃ, kevalampi rattindiva’nti. Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘yasmiṃ kho me samaye paritto samādhi hoti, parittaṃ me tasmiṃ samaye cakkhu hoti. Sohaṃ parittena cakkhunā parittañceva obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi, parittāni ca rūpāni passāmi. Yasmiṃ pana me samaye appamāṇo samādhi hoti, appamāṇaṃ me tasmiṃ samaye cakkhu hoti. Sohaṃ appamāṇena cakkhunā appamāṇañceva obhāsaṃ sañjānāmi, appamāṇāni ca rūpāni passāmi – kevalampi rattiṃ, kevalampi divaṃ, kevalampi rattindiva’’’nti.

Then, Anuruddha, dwelling diligent, ardent, and resolute, I perceived limited light and saw limited forms; I perceived unlimited light and saw unlimited forms—the entire night, the entire day, the entire night and day. Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘What is the cause, what is the reason, why I perceive limited light and see limited forms, and perceive unlimited light and see unlimited forms—the entire night, the entire day, the entire night and day?’ Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘When my samādhi is limited, my eye is also limited. With a limited eye, I perceive limited light and see limited forms. But when my samādhi is unlimited, my eye is also unlimited. With an unlimited eye, I perceive unlimited light and see unlimited forms—the entire night, the entire day, the entire night and day.’

244. Yato kho me, anuruddhā, ‘vicikicchā cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā vicikicchā cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘amanasikāro cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā amanasikāro cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘thinamiddhaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā thinamiddhaṃ cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘chambhitattaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā chambhitattaṃ cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘uppilaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā uppilaṃ cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘duṭṭhullaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā duṭṭhullaṃ cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘accāraddhavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā accāraddhavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘atilīnavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā atilīnavīriyaṃ cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘abhijappā cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā abhijappā cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘nānattasaññā cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā nānattasaññā cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi, ‘atinijjhāyitattaṃ rūpānaṃ cittassa upakkileso’ti – iti viditvā atinijjhāyitattaṃ rūpānaṃ cittassa upakkileso pahīno ahosi.

244.Then, Anuruddha, when I understood that ‘doubt is a defilement of the mind,’ doubt, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘non-attention is a defilement of the mind,’ non-attention, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘sloth and torpor are a defilement of the mind,’ sloth and torpor, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘fearfulness is a defilement of the mind,’ fearfulness, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘excitement is a defilement of the mind,’ excitement, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘agitation is a defilement of the mind,’ agitation, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘excessive energy is a defilement of the mind,’ excessive energy, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘deficient energy is a defilement of the mind,’ deficient energy, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘hankering is a defilement of the mind,’ hankering, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘diversity of perception is a defilement of the mind,’ diversity of perception, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned. When I understood that ‘excessive pondering over forms is a defilement of the mind,’ excessive pondering over forms, a defilement of the mind, was abandoned.

245. ‘‘Tassa mayhaṃ, anuruddhā, etadahosi – ‘ye kho me cittassa upakkilesā te me pahīnā. Handa, dānāhaṃ tividhena samādhiṃ bhāvemī’ti [bhāvesinti (sī. syā. kaṃ.)]. So kho ahaṃ, anuruddhā, savitakkampi savicāraṃ samādhiṃ bhāvesiṃ [bhāvemi (ka.)], avitakkampi vicāramattaṃ samādhiṃ bhāvesiṃ, avitakkampi avicāraṃ samādhiṃ bhāvesiṃ, sappītikampi samādhiṃ bhāvesiṃ, nippītikampi samādhiṃ bhāvesiṃ, sātasahagatampi samādhiṃ bhāvesiṃ, upekkhāsahagatampi samādhiṃ bhāvesiṃ. Yato kho me, anuruddhā, savitakkopi savicāro samādhi bhāvito ahosi, avitakkopi vicāramatto samādhi bhāvito ahosi, avitakkopi avicāro samādhi bhāvito ahosi, sappītikopi samādhi bhāvito ahosi, nippītikopi samādhi bhāvito ahosi, sātasahagatopi samādhi bhāvito ahosi, upekkhāsahagatopi samādhi bhāvito ahosi. Ñāṇañca pana me dassanaṃ udapādi, akuppā me cetovimutti. Ayamantimā jāti, natthi dāni punabbhavo’’ti.

245.Then, Anuruddha, it occurred to me: ‘The defilements of my mind have been abandoned. Now, then, let me develop samādhi in three ways.’ So, Anuruddha, I developed samādhi with applied thought and sustained thought; I developed samādhi without applied thought, with sustained thought only; I developed samādhi without applied thought and without sustained thought; I developed samādhi accompanied by rapture; I developed samādhi without rapture; I developed samādhi accompanied by happiness; I developed samādhi accompanied by equanimity. When, Anuruddha, I had developed samādhi with applied thought and sustained thought, samādhi without applied thought, with sustained thought only, samādhi without applied thought and without sustained thought, samādhi accompanied by rapture, samādhi without rapture, samādhi accompanied by happiness, and samādhi accompanied by equanimity—knowledge and vision arose in me, and my mind was unshakeably liberated. This is my last birth; now there is no more renewed existence.

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā anuruddho bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

Thus spoke the Blessed One. The Venerable Anuruddha, pleased, rejoiced at the Blessed One's words.

Upakkilesasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The eighth Upakkilesa Sutta is concluded.
