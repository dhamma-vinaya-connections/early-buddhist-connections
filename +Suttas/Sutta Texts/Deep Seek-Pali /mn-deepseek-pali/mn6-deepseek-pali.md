# 6. Ākaṅkheyyasuttaṃ

## 6. The Aspiration Discourse

64. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

64.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jeta’s Grove, Anathapindika’s park. There the Blessed One addressed the monks: “Monks.” “Venerable sir,” those monks replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Sampannasīlā, bhikkhave, viharatha sampannapātimokkhā; pātimokkhasaṃvarasaṃvutā viharatha ācāragocarasampannā aṇumattesu vajjesu bhayadassāvino; samādāya sikkhatha sikkhāpadesu.

“Live, monks, accomplished in virtue, accomplished in the Pāṭimokkha restraint. Live restrained by the Pāṭimokkha restraint, possessing good conduct and resort, seeing danger in the slightest faults. Undertake and train yourselves in the training rules.”

65. ‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘sabrahmacārīnaṃ piyo ca assaṃ manāpo ca garu ca bhāvanīyo cā’ti [manāpo garubhāvaniyo cāti (sī.)], sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno vipassanāya samannāgato brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

65.“If, monks, a monk should wish, ‘May I be dear and agreeable to my fellow monks, honored and esteemed by them,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘lābhī assaṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārāna’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno vipassanāya samannāgato brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘May I be a recipient of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘yesāhaṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsana gilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhāraṃ paribhuñjāmi tesaṃ te kārā mahapphalā assu mahānisaṃsā’ti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno vipassanāya samannāgato brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish, ‘May my use of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites be of great fruit and great benefit to those who provide them,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘ye maṃ [ye me (sī. syā.)] ñātī sālohitā petā kālaṅkatā [kālakatā (sī. syā. pī.)] pasannacittā anussaranti tesaṃ taṃ mahapphalaṃ assa mahānisaṃsa’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno vipassanāya samannāgato brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘May my departed relatives and kin who remember me with confident minds receive great fruit and great benefit,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

66. ‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘aratiratisaho assaṃ, na ca maṃ arati saheyya, uppannaṃ aratiṃ abhibhuyya abhibhuyya vihareyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

66.“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘May I be one who overcomes and withstands displeasure and pleasure; may displeasure not overcome me; may I dwell having overcome arisen displeasure,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘bhayabheravasaho assaṃ, na ca maṃ bhayabheravaṃ saheyya, uppannaṃ bhayabheravaṃ abhibhuyya abhibhuyya vihareyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘May I be one who withstands fear and dread, and may fear and dread not withstand me; may I dwell having conquered fear and dread whenever they arise,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī assaṃ akicchalābhī akasiralābhī’ti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘May I attain at will, without difficulty, without trouble, the four jhānas that pertain to the higher mind and are a pleasant abiding in the present,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā, te kāyena phusitvā vihareyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘May I dwell having directly experienced with my body those peaceful liberations that transcend form, the formless attainments,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

67. ‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sotāpanno assaṃ avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo’ti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

67.“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘Having destroyed the three fetters, may I be a stream-enterer, no longer subject to the realm of woe, assured, destined for enlightenment,’ he should fulfill the precepts, be devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmī assaṃ sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ kareyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘With the destruction of three fetters and the attenuation of greed, hatred, and delusion, may I be a once-returner, returning only once to this world to make an end of suffering,’ he should fulfill the precepts, be devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā opapātiko assaṃ tattha parinibbāyī anāvattidhammo tasmā lokā’ti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If, monks, a monk should wish: ‘With the destruction of the five lower fetters, may I be one who is reborn spontaneously, attaining final Nibbāna there, not liable to return from that world,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

68. ‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhaveyyaṃ – ekopi hutvā bahudhā assaṃ, bahudhāpi hutvā eko assaṃ; āvibhāvaṃ tirobhāvaṃ; tirokuṭṭaṃ tiropākāraṃ tiropabbataṃ asajjamāno gaccheyyaṃ, seyyathāpi ākāse; pathaviyāpi ummujjanimujjaṃ kareyyaṃ, seyyathāpi udake; udakepi abhijjamāne gaccheyyaṃ, seyyathāpi pathaviyaṃ; ākāsepi pallaṅkena kameyyaṃ, seyyathāpi pakkhī sakuṇo; imepi candimasūriye evaṃmahiddhike evaṃmahānubhāve pāṇinā parāmaseyyaṃ parimajjeyyaṃ; yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

68.“Monks, if a monk should wish: ‘May I wield the various kinds of psychic power: being one, may I become many; being many, may I become one; may I appear and vanish; may I go unhindered through walls, ramparts, and mountains as if through space; may I dive in and out of the earth as if it were water; may I walk on water without sinking as if it were earth; may I travel through the sky like a winged bird while seated cross-legged; may I touch and stroke with my hand even the moon and sun, so mighty and powerful; may I exercise mastery with the body as far as the Brahmā world’—he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘dibbāya sotadhātuyā visuddhāya atikkantamānusikāya ubho sadde suṇeyyaṃ – dibbe ca mānuse ca ye dūre santike cā’ti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“Monks, if a monk should wish: ‘May I, with the divine ear element, purified and surpassing the human, hear both kinds of sounds, both divine and human, those far and near,’ he should fulfill the precepts, be devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘parasattānaṃ parapuggalānaṃ cetasā ceto paricca pajāneyyaṃ – sarāgaṃ vā cittaṃ sarāgaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, vītarāgaṃ vā cittaṃ vītarāgaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ; sadosaṃ vā cittaṃ sadosaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, vītadosaṃ vā cittaṃ vītadosaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ; samohaṃ vā cittaṃ samohaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, vītamohaṃ vā cittaṃ vītamohaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ; saṃkhittaṃ vā cittaṃ saṃkhittaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, vikkhittaṃ vā cittaṃ vikkhittaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ; mahaggataṃ vā cittaṃ mahaggataṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, amahaggataṃ vā cittaṃ amahaggataṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ; sauttaraṃ vā cittaṃ sauttaraṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, anuttaraṃ vā cittaṃ anuttaraṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ; samāhitaṃ vā cittaṃ samāhitaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, asamāhitaṃ vā cittaṃ asamāhitaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ; vimuttaṃ vā cittaṃ vimuttaṃ cittanti pajāneyyaṃ, avimuttaṃ vā cittaṃ avimuttaṃ cittanti pajāneyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“Monks, if a monk should wish: ‘May I understand the minds of other beings, other persons, having encompassed them with my own mind—may I know a mind with lust as 'a mind with lust'; a mind without lust as 'a mind without lust'; a mind with hate as 'a mind with hate'; a mind without hate as 'a mind without hate'; a mind with delusion as 'a mind with delusion'; a mind without delusion as 'a mind without delusion'; a constricted mind as 'a constricted mind'; a distracted mind as 'a distracted mind'; a developed mind as 'a developed mind'; an undeveloped mind as 'an undeveloped mind'; a surpassable mind as 'a surpassable mind'; an unsurpassed mind as 'an unsurpassed mind'; a collected mind as 'a collected mind'; an uncollected mind as 'an uncollected mind'; a liberated mind as 'a liberated mind'; an unliberated mind as 'an unliberated mind'’—he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussareyyaṃ, seyyathidaṃ – ekampi jātiṃ dvepi jātiyo tissopi jātiyo catassopi jātiyo pañcapi jātiyo dasapi jātiyo vīsampi jātiyo tiṃsampi jātiyo cattālīsampi jātiyo paññāsampi jātiyo jātisatampi jātisahassampi jāti satasahassampi anekepi saṃvaṭṭakappe anekepi vivaṭṭakappe anekepi saṃvaṭṭavivaṭṭakappe – amutrāsiṃ evaṃnāmo evaṃgotto evaṃvaṇṇo evamāhāro evaṃsukhadukkhappaṭisaṃvedī evamāyupariyanto, so tato cuto amutra udapādiṃ; tatrāpāsiṃ evaṃnāmo evaṃgotto evaṃvaṇṇo evamāhāro evaṃsukhadukkhappaṭisaṃvedī evamāyupariyanto, so tato cuto idhūpapannoti. Iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussareyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī…pe… brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“If a monk, monks, should wish: ‘May I recollect my manifold past lives, that is, one birth, two births, three births, four births, five births, ten births, twenty births, thirty births, forty births, fifty births, a hundred births, a thousand births, a hundred thousand births, many aeons of contraction, many aeons of expansion, many aeons of contraction and expansion: "There, I was so named, of such a clan, with such an appearance, such food, such an experience of pleasure and pain, such a life-span; passing away from there, I arose elsewhere; and there too, I was so named, of such a clan, with such an appearance, such food, such an experience of pleasure and pain, such a life-span; passing away from there, I arose here." Thus with aspects and details, may I recollect my manifold past lives,’ he should be one who fulfills the precepts, is devoted to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passeyyaṃ cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajāneyyaṃ – ime vata bhonto sattā kāyaduccaritena samannāgatā vacīduccaritena samannāgatā manoduccaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ upavādakā micchādiṭṭhikā micchādiṭṭhikammasamādānā, te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannā; ime vā pana bhonto sattā kāyasucaritena samannāgatā vacīsucaritena samannāgatā manosucaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ anupavādakā sammādiṭṭhikā sammādiṭṭhikammasamādānā, te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapannāti, iti dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passeyyaṃ cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajāneyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno vipassanāya samannāgato brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

“Monks, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘With the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, may I see beings passing away and being reborn, lowly and eminent, fair and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, and discern how beings fare according to their kamma: "These beings, who were endowed with misconduct of body, speech, and mind, who reviled the noble ones, held wrong views, and undertook actions based on wrong views—with the breakup of the body, after death, they have reappeared in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in perdition, in hell; but these beings, who were endowed with good conduct of body, speech, and mind, who did not revile the noble ones, held right views, and undertook actions based on right views—with the breakup of the body, after death, they have reappeared in a happy destination, in a heavenly world." Thus with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, may I see beings passing away and being reborn, lowly and eminent, fair and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, and discern how beings fare according to their kamma,’ he should be one who is complete in virtue, devoted to internal serenity of mind, not unmindful of jhana, endowed with insight, and a cultivator of secluded dwellings.”

69. ‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu – ‘āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭhevadhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyya’nti, sīlesvevassa paripūrakārī ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno vipassanāya samannāgato brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ.

69.“Monks, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘By the destruction of the taints, having realized and actualized for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life, the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, may I dwell therein,’ he should be one who is complete in virtue, devoted to internal serenity of mind, not unmindful of jhana, endowed with insight, and a cultivator of secluded dwellings.”

‘‘Sampannasīlā, bhikkhave, viharatha sampannapātimokkhā; pātimokkhasaṃvarasaṃvutā viharatha ācāragocarasampannā aṇumattesu vajjesu bhayadassāvino; samādāya sikkhatha sikkhāpadesū’’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vutta’’nti.

“Dwell, bhikkhus, complete in virtue, complete in the Pāṭimokkha. Dwell restrained by the Pāṭimokkha, complete in conduct and resort, seeing danger in the slightest faults. Train yourselves, undertaking the training rules.” This is what was said, and it was said with reference to this.

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

The Blessed One said this. The bhikkhus were pleased and delighted with the Blessed One’s words.

Ākaṅkheyyasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The Ākaṅkheyya Sutta, the Sixth, is concluded.
