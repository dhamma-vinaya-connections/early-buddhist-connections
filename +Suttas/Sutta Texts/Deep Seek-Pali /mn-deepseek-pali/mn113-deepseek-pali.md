# 113. Sevitabbāsevitabbasuttaṃ

## 113. The Discourse on What Should Be Cultivated and What Should Not Be Cultivated.

109. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘sevitabbāsevitabbaṃ vo, bhikkhave, dhammapariyāyaṃ desessāmi. Taṃ suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

109.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus!” “Venerable sir,” they replied. The Blessed One then said: “Bhikkhus, I shall teach you a discourse on what should be cultivated and what should not be cultivated. Listen to it and attend carefully, and I shall speak.” “Yes, venerable sir,” those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One then said:

‘‘Kāyasamācāraṃpāhaṃ [pahaṃ (sabbattha)], bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ kāyasamācāraṃ. Vacīsamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ vacīsamācāraṃ. Manosamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ manosamācāraṃ. Cittuppādaṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ cittuppādaṃ. Saññāpaṭilābhaṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ saññāpaṭilābhaṃ. Diṭṭhipaṭilābhaṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ diṭṭhipaṭilābhaṃ. Attabhāvapaṭilābhaṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ attabhāvapaṭilābha’’nti.

“Bhikkhus, I declare bodily conduct to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of bodily conduct are distinct. I declare verbal conduct to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of verbal conduct are distinct. I declare mental conduct to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of mental conduct are distinct. I declare thought-arising to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of thought-arising are distinct. I declare the acquisition of perceptions to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of perceptual acquisition are distinct. I declare the acquisition of views to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of acquisition of views are distinct. I declare the acquisition of a self-existence to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of acquisition of a self-existence are distinct.”

Evaṃ vutte āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘imassa kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi.

When this was said, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, I understand in detail the meaning of what the Blessed One has spoken in brief, without having analyzed the meaning in detail, as follows.

110. ‘‘‘Kāyasamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ kāyasamācāra’nti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, kāyasamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti, evarūpo kāyasamācāro na sevitabbo; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, kāyasamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, evarūpo kāyasamācāro sevitabbo.

110.“‘Bhikkhus, I declare bodily conduct to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of bodily conduct are distinct.’ This was said by the Blessed One. In reference to what was this said? Venerable sir, bodily conduct of such a kind that when cultivated unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline—such bodily conduct should not be cultivated. And bodily conduct of such a kind that when cultivated unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase—such bodily conduct should be cultivated.

111. ‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, kāyasamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti? Idha, bhante, ekacco pāṇātipātī hoti luddo lohitapāṇi hatappahate niviṭṭho adayāpanno pāṇabhūtesu; adinnādāyī kho pana hoti, yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ gāmagataṃ vā araññagataṃ vā taṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti; kāmesumicchācārī kho pana hoti, yā tā māturakkhitā piturakkhitā mātāpiturakkhitā bhāturakkhitā bhaginirakkhitā ñātirakkhitā gottarakkhitā dhammarakkhitā sassāmikā saparidaṇḍā antamaso mālāguḷaparikkhittāpi tathārūpāsu cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti – evarūpaṃ, bhante, kāyasamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti.

111.“Venerable sir, what kind of bodily conduct, when cultivated, leads to the increase of unwholesome states and the decline of wholesome states? Here, venerable sir, someone is a killer of living beings, cruel, bloody-handed, given to striking and beating, without compassion for living beings. He is a taker of what is not given, taking by way of theft what belongs to another, the wealth and property of others, whether in the village or in the wilderness. He engages in sexual misconduct, having intercourse with those women who are protected by their mother, protected by their father, protected by both mother and father, protected by their brother, protected by their sister, protected by their relatives, protected by their clan, protected by the Dhamma, who have a husband, who are subject to punishment, or even those garlanded. Venerable sir, such bodily conduct, when cultivated, leads to the increase of unwholesome states and the decline of wholesome states.”

‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, kāyasamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti? Idha, bhante, ekacco pāṇātipātaṃ pahāya pāṇātipātā paṭivirato hoti nihitadaṇḍo nihitasattho, lajjī dayāpanno sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharati; adinnādānaṃ pahāya adinnādānā paṭivirato hoti, yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ gāmagataṃ vā araññagataṃ vā taṃ nādinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti; kāmesumicchācāraṃ pahāya kāmesumicchācārā paṭivirato hoti, yā tā māturakkhitā piturakkhitā mātāpiturakkhitā bhāturakkhitā bhaginirakkhitā ñātirakkhitā gottarakkhitā dhammarakkhitā sassāmikā saparidaṇḍā antamaso mālāguḷaparikkhittāpi tathārūpāsu na cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti – evarūpaṃ, bhante, kāyasamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti. ‘Kāyasamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ kāyasamācāra’nti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

“Venerable sir, what kind of bodily conduct, when cultivated, leads to the decline of unwholesome states and the increase of wholesome states? Here, venerable sir, someone, having abandoned killing living beings, abstains from killing living beings; having laid down the rod and the weapon, scrupulous, compassionate, dwells concerned for the welfare of all living beings. Having abandoned taking what is not given, he abstains from taking what is not given; he does not take by way of theft what belongs to another, the wealth and property of others, whether in the village or in the wilderness. Having abandoned sexual misconduct, he abstains from sexual misconduct, not having intercourse with such women as those protected by their mother, protected by their father, protected by both mother and father, protected by their brother, protected by their sister, protected by their relatives, protected by their clan, protected by the Dhamma, who have a husband, who are subject to punishment, or even those garlanded. Venerable sir, cultivating such bodily conduct leads to the decline of unwholesome states and the increase of wholesome states. ‘Bhikkhus, I declare bodily conduct to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of bodily conduct are distinct.’ This was said by the Blessed One, and it was said in reference to this.”

‘‘‘Vacīsamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ vacīsamācāra’nti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, vacīsamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti, evarūpo vacīsamācāro na sevitabbo; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, vacīsamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpo vacīsamācāro sevitabbo.

“‘Bhikkhus, I declare verbal conduct to be twofold: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of verbal conduct are distinct.’ This was said by the Blessed One. In reference to what was this said? Venerable sir, verbal conduct of such a kind that when cultivated unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline—such verbal conduct should not be cultivated. And verbal conduct of such a kind that when cultivated unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase—such verbal conduct should be cultivated.”

112. ‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, vacīsamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti? Idha, bhante, ekacco musāvādī hoti, sabhāgato [sabhaggato (bahūsu)] vā parisāgato [parisaggato (bahūsu)] vā ñātimajjhagato vā pūgamajjhagato vā rājakulamajjhagato vā abhinīto sakkhipuṭṭho – ‘ehambho purisa, yaṃ jānāsi taṃ vadehī’ti so ajānaṃ vā āha – ‘jānāmī’ti, jānaṃ vā āha – ‘na jānāmī’ti; apassaṃ vā āha – ‘passāmī’ti, passaṃ vā āha – ‘na passāmī’ti – iti [passa ma. ni. 1.440 sāleyyakasutte] attahetu vā parahetu vā āmisakiñcikkhahetu [kiñcakkhahetu (sī.)] vā sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti; pisuṇavāco kho pana hoti, ito sutvā amutra akkhātā imesaṃ bhedāya, amutra vā sutvā imesaṃ akkhātā amūsaṃ bhedāya – iti samaggānaṃ vā bhettā, bhinnānaṃ vā anuppadātā, vaggārāmo, vaggarato, vagganandī, vaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti; pharusavāco kho pana hoti, yā sā vācā kaṇḍakā kakkasā pharusā parakaṭukā parābhisajjanī kodhasāmantā asamādhisaṃvattanikā, tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti; samphappalāpī kho pana hoti akālavādī abhūtavādī anatthavādī adhammavādī avinayavādī, anidhānavatiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti akālena anapadesaṃ apariyantavatiṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ – evarūpaṃ, bhante, vacīsamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti.

112.“Venerable sir, what kind of verbal conduct, when cultivated, leads to the increase of unwholesome states and the decline of wholesome states? Here, venerable sir, someone is a liar. Whether in a court, or in an assembly, or in a gathering of relatives, or in a guild, or in the royal court, if summoned as a witness and asked: ‘Come, good man, speak what you know,’ then, not knowing, he says, ‘I know,’ or knowing, he says, ‘I do not know’; not seeing, he says, ‘I see,’ or seeing, he says, ‘I do not see.’ Thus, for his own sake, for another’s sake, or for some trifling material gain, he knowingly speaks a falsehood. He is a divisive speaker. Having heard something here, he repeats it elsewhere to create division among these people; having heard something there, he repeats it here to create division among those people. Thus he is a splitter of those who are united and an instigator of those who are divided; he delights in dissension, enjoys dissension, rejoices in dissension; and he speaks words that create dissension. He is a harsh speaker. His speech is prickly, rough, bitter, abusive, bordering on anger, and unconducive to samādhi; he speaks such words. He is an idle chatterer. He speaks out of season, speaks what is not factual, speaks what is unbeneficial, speaks what is contrary to the Dhamma, speaks what is contrary to the Vinaya. His speech is without foundation, untimely, without reason, unbounded, and unconnected with the goal. Such, venerable sir, is the verbal conduct that, when cultivated, leads to the increase of unwholesome states and the decline of wholesome states.”

‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, vacīsamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti? Idha, bhante, ekacco musāvādaṃ pahāya musāvādā paṭivirato hoti sabhāgato vā parisāgato vā ñātimajjhagato vā pūgamajjhagato vā rājakulamajjhagato vā abhinīto sakkhipuṭṭho – ‘ehambho purisa, yaṃ jānāsi taṃ vadehī’ti so ajānaṃ vā āha – ‘na jānāmī’ti, jānaṃ vā āha – ‘jānāmī’ti, apassaṃ vā āha – ‘na passāmī’ti, passaṃ vā āha – ‘passāmī’ti – iti attahetu vā parahetu vā āmisakiñcikkhahetu vā na sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti; pisuṇaṃ vācaṃ pahāya pisuṇāya vācāya paṭivirato hoti, ito sutvā na amutra akkhātā imesaṃ bhedāya, amutra vā sutvā na imesaṃ akkhātā amūsaṃ bhedāya – iti bhinnānaṃ vā sandhātā sahitānaṃ vā anuppadātā samaggārāmo samaggarato samagganandī samaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti; pharusaṃ vācaṃ pahāya pharusāya vācāya paṭivirato hoti, yā sā vācā nelā kaṇṇasukhā pemanīyā hadayaṅgamā porī bahujanakantā bahujanamanāpā tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti; samphappalāpaṃ pahāya samphappalāpā paṭivirato hoti kālavādī bhūtavādī atthavādī dhammavādī vinayavādī, nidhānavatiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti kālena sāpadesaṃ pariyantavatiṃ atthasaṃhitaṃ – evarūpaṃ, bhante, vacīsamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti. ‘Vacīsamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ vacīsamācāra’nti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"Venerable sir, what kind of verbal conduct, when cultivated, leads to the decline of unwholesome qualities and the growth of wholesome qualities? Here, venerable sir, someone gives up false speech and abstains from false speech. When summoned to a council, an assembly, a gathering of relatives, a guild, or the royal court, and questioned as a witness—'Come, good man, tell what you know'—if he does not know, he says, 'I do not know,' or if he knows, he says, 'I know.' If he has not seen, he says, 'I have not seen,' or if he has seen, he says, 'I have seen.' Thus, he does not knowingly speak falsehood for his own sake, for another's sake, or for some trifling gain. He gives up divisive speech and abstains from divisive speech. What he hears here, he does not repeat there to create division, nor does he repeat what he hears there to create division here. Thus, he reconciles those who are divided and encourages those who are united. He delights in harmony, rejoices in harmony, is fond of harmony, and speaks words that create harmony. He gives up harsh speech and abstains from harsh speech. He speaks such words as are gentle, pleasing to the ear, affectionate, touching the heart, courteous, liked by many, and agreeable to many. He gives up idle chatter and abstains from idle chatter. He speaks at the right time, speaks what is true, speaks what is meaningful, speaks on the Dhamma, and speaks on the Vinaya. His words are worth treasuring, are timely, are supported by reason, are well-defined, and are connected with the goal. Venerable sir, cultivating such verbal conduct leads to the decline of unwholesome qualities and the growth of wholesome qualities. 'Monks, I declare verbal conduct to be of two kinds: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of verbal conduct are distinct.' This was said by the Blessed One, and it was with reference to this that it was said."

‘‘‘Manosamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ manosamācāra’nti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, manosamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpo manosamācāro na sevitabbo; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, manosamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpo manosamācāro sevitabbo.

"‘Monks, I declare mental conduct to be of two kinds: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of mental conduct are distinct.’ Thus, indeed, was it spoken by the Blessed One. With reference to what was this said? Whatever kind of mental conduct, venerable sir, when cultivated leads to the increase of unwholesome qualities and the decline of wholesome qualities—such mental conduct should not be cultivated. And whatever kind of mental conduct, venerable sir, when cultivated leads to the decline of unwholesome qualities and the increase of wholesome qualities—such mental conduct should be cultivated."

113. ‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, manosamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti? Idha, bhante, ekacco abhijjhālu hoti, yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ taṃ abhijjhātā hoti – ‘aho vata yaṃ parassa taṃ mamassā’ti; byāpannacitto kho pana hoti paduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ‘ime sattā haññantu vā vajjhantu vā ucchijjantu vā vinassantu vā mā vā ahesu’nti – evarūpaṃ, bhante, manosamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti.

113."Venerable sir, what kind of mental conduct, when cultivated, leads to the increase of unwholesome qualities and the decline of wholesome qualities? Here, venerable sir, someone is covetous; he is full of longing for the wealth and possessions of others, thinking, ‘Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!’ And he is malevolent in thought, with corrupted intentions: ‘May these beings be killed or slaughtered or cut off or destroyed, or may they not exist at all!’ Venerable sir, when one cultivates such mental conduct, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline."

‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, manosamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti? Idha, bhante, ekacco anabhijjhālu hoti, yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ taṃ nābhijjhātā hoti – ‘aho vata yaṃ parassa taṃ mamassā’ti; abyāpannacitto kho pana hoti appaduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ‘ime sattā averā abyābajjhā [abyāpajjhā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] anīghā sukhī attānaṃ pariharantū’ti – evarūpaṃ, bhante, manosamācāraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti. ‘Manosamācāraṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ manosamācāra’nti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"Venerable sir, what kind of mental conduct, when cultivated, leads to the decline of unwholesome qualities and the growth of wholesome qualities? Here, venerable sir, someone is free from covetousness; they do not covet the wealth and possessions of others, thinking, ‘Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!’ Moreover, they have a mind free from ill will, with intentions free of hatred: ‘May these beings be free from enmity, affliction, and anxiety; may they live happily!’ Venerable sir, when one cultivates mental conduct of this kind, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase. ‘Monks, I declare mental conduct to be of two kinds: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of mental conduct are distinct.’ This was said by the Blessed One, and it was with reference to this that it was said."

114. ‘‘‘Cittuppādaṃpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi; tañca aññamaññaṃ cittuppāda’nti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, cittuppādaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpo cittuppādo na sevitabbo; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, cittuppādaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpo cittuppādo sevitabbo.

114."‘Monks, I declare the arising of thought to be of two kinds: that which should be cultivated and that which should not be cultivated; and these two kinds of thought-arising are distinct.’ Thus, indeed, was this said by the Blessed One. With reference to what was this said? ‘Venerable sir, when the arising of a certain kind of thought, if cultivated, leads to the increase of unwholesome qualities and the decline of wholesome qualities, that kind of thought should not be cultivated. But when the arising of a certain kind of thought, if cultivated, leads to the decline of unwholesome qualities and the increase of wholesome qualities, that kind of thought should be cultivated.’"

‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, cittuppādaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti? Idha, bhante, ekacco abhijjhālu hoti, abhijjhāsahagatena cetasā viharati; byāpādavā hoti, byāpādasahagatena cetasā viharati; vihesavā hoti, vihesāsahagatena cetasā viharati – evarūpaṃ, bhante, cittuppādaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti.

"Venerable sir, what kind of thought-arising, when cultivated, leads to the increase of unwholesome qualities and the decline of wholesome qualities? Here, venerable sir, someone is covetous, dwelling with a mind accompanied by covetousness; they are with ill will, dwelling with a mind accompanied by ill will; they are with harmful intent, dwelling with a mind accompanied by harmful intent. Venerable sir, when one cultivates this kind of thought-arising, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline."

‘‘Kathaṃrūpaṃ, bhante, cittuppādaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti? Sāriputta, I declare mental phenomena cognizable by mind-consciousness to be of two kinds—to be cultivated and not to be cultivated."

Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘imassa kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. ‘Cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpaṃ cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ na sevitabbaṃ; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpaṃ cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ sevitabbaṃ. ‘Cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

When this was said, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, I understand the meaning of this brief statement spoken by the Blessed One, which was not analyzed in detail, in detail in this way: 'Forms cognizable by eye-consciousness, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds—to be cultivated and not to be cultivated.' Thus was it spoken by the Blessed One. With reference to what was this said? Venerable sir, when, by cultivating forms cognizable by eye-consciousness, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline, such forms cognizable by eye-consciousness should not be cultivated. But when, by cultivating forms cognizable by eye-consciousness, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase, such forms cognizable by eye-consciousness should be cultivated. 'Forms cognizable by eye-consciousness, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds—to be cultivated and not to be cultivated.' This is what was said by the Blessed One, and it was said in reference to this."

‘‘Sotaviññeyyaṃ saddaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta…pe… evarūpo sotaviññeyyo saddo na sevitabbo… evarūpo sotaviññeyyo saddo sevitabbo… evarūpo ghānaviññeyyo gandho na sevitabbo… evarūpo ghānaviññeyyo gandho sevitabbo… evarūpo jivhāviññeyyo raso na sevitabbo… evarūpo jivhāviññeyyo raso sevitabbo… kāyaviññeyyaṃ phoṭṭhabbaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta … evarūpo kāyaviññeyyo phoṭṭhabbo na sevitabbo… evarūpo kāyaviññeyyo phoṭṭhabbo sevitabbo.

"Venerable sir, as for sounds cognizable by ear-consciousness: when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline, such sounds cognizable by ear-consciousness should not be cultivated; but when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase, such sounds cognizable by ear-consciousness should be cultivated. As for odors cognizable by nose-consciousness: when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline, such odors cognizable by nose-consciousness should not be cultivated; but when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase, such odors cognizable by nose-consciousness should be cultivated. As for flavors cognizable by tongue-consciousness: when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline, such flavors cognizable by tongue-consciousness should not be cultivated; but when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase, such flavors cognizable by tongue-consciousness should be cultivated. As for tangibles cognizable by body-consciousness: when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline, such tangibles cognizable by body-consciousness should not be cultivated; but when, by cultivating them, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase, such tangibles cognizable by body-consciousness should be cultivated."

‘‘‘Manoviññeyyaṃ dhammaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, manoviññeyyaṃ dhammaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpo manoviññeyyo dhammo na sevitabbo; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, manoviññeyyaṃ dhammaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpo manoviññeyyo dhammo sevitabbo. ‘Manoviññeyyaṃ dhammaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ. Imassa kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmī’’ti.

"'Sāriputta, I declare mental phenomena cognizable by mind-consciousness to be of two kinds—to be cultivated and not to be cultivated.' This was said by the Blessed One. With reference to what was this said? Venerable sir, when, by cultivating mental phenomena cognizable by mind-consciousness, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline, such mental phenomena cognizable by mind-consciousness should not be cultivated. But when, by cultivating mental phenomena cognizable by mind-consciousness, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase, such mental phenomena cognizable by mind-consciousness should be cultivated. 'Sāriputta, I declare mental phenomena cognizable by mind-consciousness to be of two kinds—to be cultivated and not to be cultivated.' This is what was said by the Blessed One, and it was said in reference to this. Venerable sir, I understand the meaning of this brief statement spoken by the Blessed One, which was not analyzed in detail, in detail in this way."

120. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Sādhu kho tvaṃ, sāriputta, imassa mayā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāsi. ‘Cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ mayā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, sāriputta, cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpaṃ cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ na sevitabbaṃ; yathārūpañca kho, sāriputta, cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpaṃ cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃ sevitabbaṃ. ‘Cakkhuviññeyyaṃ rūpaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ mayā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

120."Good, good, Sāriputta! It is good, Sāriputta, that you understand the meaning of this brief statement spoken by me, which was not analyzed in detail, in detail in this way. 'Sāriputta, I declare forms cognizable by eye-consciousness to be of two kinds—to be cultivated and not to be cultivated.' This was said by me. With reference to what was this said? Sāriputta, when, by cultivating forms cognizable by eye-consciousness, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline, such forms cognizable by eye-consciousness should not be cultivated. But when, by cultivating forms cognizable by eye-consciousness, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase, such forms cognizable by eye-consciousness should be cultivated. 'Sāriputta, I declare forms cognizable by eye-consciousness to be of two kinds—to be cultivated and not to be cultivated.' This is what was said by me, and it was said in reference to this."

‘‘Sotaviññeyyaṃ saddaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta…pe… evarūpo sotaviññeyyo saddo na sevitabbo… evarūpo sotaviññeyyo saddo sevitabbo… evarūpo ghānaviññeyyo gandho na sevitabbo… evarūpo ghānaviññeyyo gandho sevitabbo… evarūpo jivhāviññeyyo raso na sevitabbo… evarūpo jivhāviññeyyo raso sevitabbo… evarūpo kāyaviññeyyo phoṭṭhabbo na sevitabbo… evarūpo kāyaviññeyyo phoṭṭhabbo sevitabbo.

"I declare, Sāriputta, that sounds cognizable by the ear... Such a sound cognizable by the ear should not be resorted to... Such a sound cognizable by the ear should be resorted to... Such an odor cognizable by the nose should not be resorted to... Such an odor cognizable by the nose should be resorted to... Such a taste cognizable by the tongue should not be resorted to... Such a taste cognizable by the tongue should be resorted to... Such a tactile object cognizable by the body should not be resorted to... Such a tactile object cognizable by the body should be resorted to."

‘‘Manoviññeyyaṃ dhammaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta…pe… evarūpo manoviññeyyo dhammo na sevitabbo… evarūpo manoviññeyyo dhammo sevitabbo. ‘Manoviññeyyaṃ dhammaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ mayā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ. Imassa kho, sāriputta, mayā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa evaṃ vitthārena attho daṭṭhabbo.

"Mind-cognizable phenomena, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be resorted to and those not to be resorted to... Such a mind-cognizable phenomenon should not be resorted to... Such a mind-cognizable phenomenon should be resorted to. 'Mind-cognizable phenomena, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be resorted to and those not to be resorted to'—this was said by me with reference to this matter. Sāriputta, the meaning of what I have stated briefly should be understood in detail in this way."

121. ‘‘Cīvaraṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampi…pe… piṇḍapātaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta… senāsanaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta… gāmaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta… nigamaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta… nagaraṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta… janapadaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta… puggalaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’’ti.

121."Sāriputta, I declare robes to be of two kinds: those to be resorted to and those not to be resorted to... Sāriputta, I declare almsfood... Sāriputta, I declare dwellings... Sāriputta, I declare villages... Sāriputta, I declare market towns... Sāriputta, I declare cities... Sāriputta, I declare districts... Sāriputta, I declare persons to be of two kinds: those to be associated with and those not to be associated with."

Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘imassa kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. ‘Cīvaraṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, cīvaraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpaṃ cīvaraṃ na sevitabbaṃ; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, cīvaraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpaṃ cīvaraṃ sevitabbaṃ. ‘Cīvaraṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

When this was said, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, I understand in detail the meaning of this brief statement, which the Blessed One has not elaborated in detail, in this way: 'Robes, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be resorted to and those not to be resorted to'—this was said by the Blessed One. With reference to what was it said? Venerable sir, when resorting to a certain kind of robe, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline—such a robe should not be resorted to. But when resorting to a certain kind of robe, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase—such a robe should be resorted to. 'Robes, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be resorted to and those not to be resorted to'—this was said by the Blessed One with reference to this matter."

‘‘Piṇḍapātaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta…pe… evarūpo piṇḍapāto na sevitabbo… evarūpo piṇḍapāto sevitabbo… senāsanaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta…pe… evarūpaṃ senāsanaṃ na sevitabbaṃ… evarūpaṃ senāsanaṃ sevitabbaṃ… gāmaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta …pe… evarūpo gāmo na sevitabbo… evarūpo gāmo sevitabbo… evarūpo nigamo na sevitabbo… evarūpo nigamo sevitabbo… evarūpaṃ nagaraṃ na sevitabbaṃ… evarūpaṃ nagaraṃ sevitabbaṃ… evarūpo janapado na sevitabbo… evarūpo janapado sevitabbo.

"Almsfood, Sāriputta, I declare... Such almsfood should not be resorted to... Such almsfood should be resorted to... Dwellings, Sāriputta, I declare... Such a dwelling should not be resorted to... Such a dwelling should be resorted to... Villages, Sāriputta, I declare... Such a village should not be resorted to... Such a village should be resorted to... Such a market town should not be resorted to... Such a market town should be resorted to... Such a city should not be resorted to... Such a city should be resorted to... Such a district should not be resorted to... Such a district should be resorted to."

‘‘‘Puggalaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, bhante, puggalaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpo puggalo na sevitabbo; yathārūpañca kho, bhante, puggalaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpo puggalo sevitabbo. ‘Puggalaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttanti. Imassa kho ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavatā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmī’’ti.

"‘Persons, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be associated with and those not to be associated with’—this was said by the Blessed One. With reference to what was it said? Venerable sir, when associating with a certain kind of person, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline—such a person should not be associated with. But when associating with a certain kind of person, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase—such a person should be associated with. ‘Persons, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be associated with and those not to be associated with’—this was said by the Blessed One with reference to this matter. Venerable sir, I understand in detail the meaning of this brief statement, which the Blessed One has not elaborated in detail, in this way."

122. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Sādhu kho tvaṃ, sāriputta, imassa mayā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāsi. ‘Cīvaraṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ mayā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, sāriputta, cīvaraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpaṃ cīvaraṃ na sevitabbaṃ; yathārūpañca kho, sāriputta, cīvaraṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpaṃ cīvaraṃ sevitabbaṃ. ‘Cīvaraṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ mayā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ. (Yathā paṭhamaṃ tathā vitthāretabbaṃ) evarūpo piṇḍapāto… evarūpaṃ senāsanaṃ… evarūpo gāmo… evarūpo nigamo… evarūpaṃ nagaraṃ… evarūpo janapado.

122."Good, good, Sāriputta! It is good, Sāriputta, that you understand in detail the meaning of what I have stated briefly, which I have not elaborated in detail, in this way. 'Robes, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be resorted to and those not to be resorted to.' This was said by me. With reference to what was it said? Sāriputta, when resorting to a certain kind of robe, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline—such a robe should not be resorted to. But when resorting to a certain kind of robe, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase—such a robe should be resorted to. 'Robes, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be resorted to and those not to be resorted to.' This was said by me with reference to this matter. (Thus it should be elaborated in detail.) Such almsfood... such a dwelling... such a village... such a market town... such a city... such a district."

‘‘‘Puggalaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ mayā. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Yathārūpaṃ, sāriputta, puggalaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, kusalā dhammā parihāyanti evarūpo puggalo na sevitabbo; yathārūpañca kho, sāriputta, puggalaṃ sevato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti evarūpo puggalo sevitabbo. ‘Puggalaṃpāhaṃ, sāriputta, duvidhena vadāmi – sevitabbampi, asevitabbampī’ti – iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ mayā idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ. Imassa kho, sāriputta, mayā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa evaṃ vitthārena attho daṭṭhabbo.

"‘Persons, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be associated with and those not to be associated with.’ This was said by me. With reference to what was it said? Sāriputta, when associating with a certain kind of person, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline—such a person should not be associated with. But when associating with a certain kind of person, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase—such a person should be associated with. ‘Persons, Sāriputta, I declare to be of two kinds: those to be associated with and those not to be associated with.’ This was said by me with reference to this matter. Sāriputta, the meaning of what I have stated briefly should be understood in detail in this way."

123. ‘‘Sabbepi ce, sāriputta, khattiyā imassa mayā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājāneyyuṃ, sabbesānampissa khattiyānaṃ dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya. Sabbepi ce, sāriputta, brāhmaṇā…pe… sabbepi ce, sāriputta, vessā… sabbepi ce, sāriputta, suddā imassa mayā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājāneyyuṃ, sabbesānampissa suddānaṃ dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya. Sadevakopi ce, sāriputta, loko samārako sabrahmako sassamaṇabrāhmaṇī pajā sadevamanussā imassa mayā saṃkhittena bhāsitassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājāneyya, sadevakassapissa lokassa samārakassa sabrahmakassa sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti.

123."If, Sāriputta, all the nobles were to understand in detail the meaning of what I have stated briefly in this way, it would be for the long-term welfare and happiness of all those nobles. If, Sāriputta, all the brahmins... all the merchants... all the sudras were to understand in detail the meaning of what I have stated briefly in this way, it would be for the long-term welfare and happiness of all those sudras. If, Sāriputta, the world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, together with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans, were to understand in detail the meaning of what I have stated briefly in this way, it would be for the long-term welfare and happiness of the world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, together with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans."

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā sāriputto bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

This is what the Blessed One said. The Venerable Sāriputta was pleased and delighted in the Blessed One’s words.

Sevitabbāsevitabbasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ catutthaṃ.

The Discourse on What Should and Should Not Be Resorted To is concluded, the fourth.
