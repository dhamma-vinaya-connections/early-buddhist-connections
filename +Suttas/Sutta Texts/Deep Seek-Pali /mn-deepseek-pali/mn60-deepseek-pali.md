# 60. Apaṇṇakasuttaṃ

## 60. The Infallible Discourse

92. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ yena sālā nāma kosalānaṃ brāhmaṇagāmo tadavasari. Assosuṃ kho sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā – ‘‘samaṇo khalu bho gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ sālaṃ anuppatto. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’ti. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’ti. Atha kho sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā appekacce bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Appekacce bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu; sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Appekacce yena bhagavā tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Appekacce bhagavato santike nāmagottaṃ sāvetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Appekacce tuṇhībhūtā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu.

92.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was traveling among the Kosalans with a large company of monks, and he arrived at Sālā, a Kosalan brahmin village. The brahmin householders of Sālā heard: “The ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, who went forth from the Sakyan clan, has arrived at Sālā while traveling among the Kosalans with a large company of monks. Now a good report of that Master Gotama has spread: ‘That Blessed One is an Arahant, a perfectly enlightened one, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, Well-gone, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of those who can be trained, teacher of gods and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One.’ He has realized with his own direct knowledge and declared this world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans. He teaches the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and wording, and he reveals a holy life that is utterly perfect and pure. It is good indeed to see such Arahants.” Then the brahmin householders of Sālā approached the Blessed One. Having approached, some paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Some exchanged greetings with the Blessed One and, when the conversation of greeting and politeness was over, sat down to one side. Some raised their hands in reverential salutation toward the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Some announced their name and clan in the presence of the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Some remained silent and sat down to one side.

93. Ekamantaṃ nisinne kho sāleyyake brāhmaṇagahapatike bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘atthi pana vo, gahapatayo, koci manāpo satthā yasmiṃ vo ākāravatī saddhā paṭiladdhā’’ti? ‘‘Natthi kho no, bhante, koci manāpo satthā yasmiṃ no ākāravatī saddhā paṭiladdhā’’ti. ‘‘Manāpaṃ vo, gahapatayo, satthāraṃ alabhantehi ayaṃ apaṇṇako dhammo samādāya vattitabbo. Apaṇṇako hi, gahapatayo, dhammo samatto samādinno, so vo bhavissati dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya. Katamo ca, gahapatayo, apaṇṇako dhammo’’?

93.When the Brahmin householders of Sālā were seated to one side, the Blessed One said to them: “Householders, is there any teacher agreeable to you in whom you have acquired faith that accords with reason?” “No, venerable sir, there is no teacher agreeable to us in whom we have acquired faith that accords with reason.” “Since you have not found an agreeable teacher, householders, you should undertake and practice this infallible teaching. For an infallible teaching, householders, when properly undertaken, will be for your long-term welfare and happiness. And what, householders, is the infallible teaching?”

94. ‘‘Santi, gahapatayo, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi dinnaṃ, natthi yiṭṭhaṃ, natthi hutaṃ; natthi sukatadukkaṭānaṃ [sukaṭadukkaṭānaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] kammānaṃ phalaṃ vipāko, natthi ayaṃ loko, natthi paro loko; natthi mātā, natthi pitā; natthi sattā opapātikā; natthi loke samaṇabrāhmaṇā sammaggatā [samaggatā (ka.)] sammā paṭipannā ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Tesaṃyeva kho, gahapatayo, samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā ujuvipaccanīkavādā. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘atthi dinnaṃ, atthi yiṭṭhaṃ, atthi hutaṃ; atthi sukatadukkaṭānaṃ kammānaṃ phalaṃ vipāko; atthi ayaṃ loko, atthi paro loko; atthi mātā, atthi pitā; atthi sattā opapātikā; atthi loke samaṇabrāhmaṇā sammaggatā sammā paṭipannā ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, gahapatayo – ‘nanume samaṇabrāhmaṇā aññamaññassa ujuvipaccanīkavādā’’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’.

94.“Householders, there are some ascetics and brahmins who are of such a doctrine and view: ‘There is no gift, no sacrifice, no offering; there is no fruit or result of good or bad deeds; there is no this world, no other world; there is no mother, no father; there are no beings reborn spontaneously; there are in the world no ascetics and brahmins who have gone rightly, who practice rightly, and who, having realized this world and the other world for themselves with direct knowledge, make them known.’ But, householders, there are other ascetics and brahmins whose doctrines are directly contrary to those. They say: ‘There is gift, there is sacrifice, there is offering; there is fruit and result of good and bad deeds; there is this world, there is the other world; there is mother, there is father; there are beings reborn spontaneously; there are in the world ascetics and brahmins who have gone rightly, who practice rightly, and who, having realized this world and the other world for themselves with direct knowledge, make them known.’ What do you think, householders? Are not those ascetics and brahmins proponents of directly contradictory views toward one another?” “Yes, venerable sir.”

95. ‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi dinnaṃ, natthi yiṭṭhaṃ…pe… ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti tesametaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ? Yamidaṃ [yadidaṃ (ka.)] kāyasucaritaṃ, vacīsucaritaṃ, manosucaritaṃ – ime tayo kusale dhamme abhinivajjetvā [abhinibbajjetvā (syā. kaṃ.), abhinibbijjitvā (ka.)] yamidaṃ [yadidaṃ (ka.)] kāyaduccaritaṃ, vacīduccaritaṃ, manoduccaritaṃ – ime tayo akusale dhamme samādāya vattissanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā passanti akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ, kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ vodānapakkhaṃ. Santaṃyeva pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘natthi paro loko’ tissa diṭṭhi hoti; sāssa hoti micchādiṭṭhi. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘natthi paro loko’ti saṅkappeti; svāssa hoti micchāsaṅkappo. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘natthi paro loko’ti vācaṃ bhāsati; sāssa hoti micchāvācā. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘natthi paro loko’ti āha; ye te arahanto paralokaviduno tesamayaṃ paccanīkaṃ karoti. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘natthi paro loko’ti paraṃ saññāpeti [paññāpeti (ka.)]; sāssa hoti asaddhammasaññatti [assaddhammapaññatti (ka.)]. Tāya ca pana asaddhammasaññattiyā attānukkaṃseti, paraṃ vambheti. Iti pubbeva kho panassa susīlyaṃ pahīnaṃ hoti, dussīlyaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ – ayañca micchādiṭṭhi micchāsaṅkappo micchāvācā ariyānaṃ paccanīkatā asaddhammasaññatti attukkaṃsanā paravambhanā. Evamassime [evaṃ’si’me’ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti micchādiṭṭhipaccayā.

95."In this regard, householders, concerning those ascetics and brahmins who hold such views and such doctrines—'There is no giving, no sacrifice, no offering; there is no fruit or result of good and bad actions; there is no this world, no other world; there is no mother, no father, no spontaneously reborn beings; there are no ascetics and brahmins in the world who are rightly practicing, who are rightly attained, who proclaim this world and the next, having realized them by their own direct knowledge'—what is to be expected of them? They will abandon these three kinds of good conduct—good bodily conduct, good verbal conduct, good mental conduct—and will engage in these three kinds of bad conduct—bad bodily conduct, bad verbal conduct, bad mental conduct. Why is that? Because those venerable ascetics and brahmins do not see the danger, degradation, and defilement in unwholesome qualities, nor the benefit of renunciation and its aspect of purity in wholesome qualities. Though the next world truly exists, they have the view, 'There is no next world.' This is their wrong view. Though the next world truly exists, they intend, 'There is no next world.' This is their wrong intention. Though the next world truly exists, they speak, 'There is no next world.' This is their wrong speech. Though the next world truly exists, they say, 'There is no next world,' thereby opposing those arahants who know the next world. Though the next world truly exists, they persuade others, 'There is no next world.' This is their false communication. Through this false communication, they praise themselves and disparage others. Thus, their good conduct is abandoned beforehand, and bad conduct is established—namely, wrong view, wrong intention, wrong speech, opposition to the noble ones, false communication, self-praise, and disparagement of others. In this way, many evil, unwholesome qualities arise in him conditioned by wrong view."

‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘sace kho natthi paro loko evamayaṃ bhavaṃ purisapuggalo kāyassa bhedā sotthimattānaṃ karissati; sace kho atthi paro loko evamayaṃ bhavaṃ purisapuggalo kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjissati. Kāmaṃ kho pana māhu paro loko, hotu nesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ vacanaṃ; atha ca panāyaṃ bhavaṃ purisapuggalo diṭṭheva dhamme viññūnaṃ gārayho – dussīlo purisapuggalo micchādiṭṭhi natthikavādo’ti. Sace kho attheva paro loko, evaṃ imassa bhoto purisapuggalassa ubhayattha kaliggaho – yañca diṭṭheva dhamme viññūnaṃ gārayho, yañca kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjissati. Evamassāyaṃ apaṇṇako dhammo dussamatto samādinno, ekaṃsaṃ pharitvā tiṭṭhati, riñcati kusalaṃ ṭhānaṃ.

"Here, householders, a wise person reflects thus: 'If there is no other world, then this worthy individual, upon the breakup of the body, will make himself safe. But if there is another world, then this worthy individual, upon the breakup of the body, after death, will be reborn in a state of loss, a bad destination, a lower realm, even in hell. Now, even if there is no other world, let the words of those venerable ascetics and brahmins be true; still, in this very life, this worthy individual is censured by the wise as an immoral person of wrong view, a nihilist.' But if there is indeed another world, then this worthy individual faces loss on both counts: he is censured by the wise in this very life, and upon the breakup of the body, after death, he will be reborn in a state of loss, a bad destination, a lower realm, even in hell. Thus, this unerring principle is poorly undertaken, leaning entirely to one side, forsaking a skillful state."

96. ‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘atthi dinnaṃ…pe… ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti tesametaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ? Yamidaṃ kāyaduccaritaṃ, vacīduccaritaṃ, manoduccaritaṃ – ime tayo akusale dhamme abhinivajjetvā yamidaṃ kāyasucaritaṃ, vacīsucaritaṃ, manosucaritaṃ – ime tayo kusale dhamme samādāya vattissanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Passanti hi te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ, kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ vodānapakkhaṃ. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘atthi paro loko’ tissa diṭṭhi hoti; sāssa hoti sammādiṭṭhi. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘atthi paro loko’ti saṅkappeti; svāssa hoti sammāsaṅkappo. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘atthi paro loko’ti vācaṃ bhāsati; sāssa hoti sammāvācā. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘atthi paro loko’ti āha; ye te arahanto paralokaviduno tesamayaṃ na paccanīkaṃ karoti. Santaṃyeva kho pana paraṃ lokaṃ ‘atthi paro loko’ti paraṃ saññāpeti; sāssa hoti saddhammasaññatti. Tāya ca pana saddhammasaññattiyā nevattānukkaṃseti, na paraṃ vambheti. Iti pubbeva kho panassa dussīlyaṃ pahīnaṃ hoti, susīlyaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ – ayañca sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā ariyānaṃ apaccanīkatā saddhammasaññatti anattukkaṃsanā aparavambhanā. Evamassime aneke kusalā dhammā sambhavanti sammādiṭṭhipaccayā.

96."In this regard, householders, what can be expected of those ascetics and brahmins who hold such views and such doctrines—'There is what is given, there is what is sacrificed, there is what is offered; there is fruit and result of good and bad actions; there is this world, there is another world; there is mother, there is father, there are spontaneously reborn beings; there are ascetics and brahmins in the world who are rightly practicing, who are rightly attained, who proclaim this world and the next, having realized it themselves through direct knowledge'? They will abandon these three unwholesome actions—misconduct by body, speech, and mind—and undertake these three wholesome actions—good conduct by body, speech, and mind. Why is that? Because those venerable ascetics and brahmins see the danger, degradation, and defilement in unwholesome qualities, and they see the benefit of renunciation and its aspect of purity in wholesome qualities. Since there is indeed another world, the view 'There is another world' arises in them; this is their right view. Since there is indeed another world, they resolve 'There is another world'; this is their right intention. Since there is indeed another world, they speak the words 'There is another world'; this is their right speech. Since there is indeed another world, they declare 'There is another world'; in regard to those arahants who know the other world, they do not act contrary to them. Since there is indeed another world, they persuade others 'There is another world'; this is their teaching in accordance with the Dhamma. And through this teaching in accordance with the Dhamma, they neither praise themselves nor disparage others. Thus, their immoral conduct is abandoned beforehand, and virtuous conduct is established—namely, right view, right intention, right speech, not acting in opposition to the noble ones, teaching in accordance with the Dhamma, absence of self-praise, and absence of disparagement of others. In this way, many wholesome qualities arise in them conditioned by right view."

‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘sace kho atthi paro loko, evamayaṃ bhavaṃ purisapuggalo kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjissati. Kāmaṃ kho pana māhu paro loko, hotu nesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ vacanaṃ; atha ca panāyaṃ bhavaṃ purisapuggalo diṭṭheva dhamme viññūnaṃ pāsaṃso – sīlavā purisapuggalo sammādiṭṭhi atthikavādo’ti. Sace kho attheva paro loko, evaṃ imassa bhoto purisapuggalassa ubhayattha kaṭaggaho – yañca diṭṭheva dhamme viññūnaṃ pāsaṃso, yañca kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjissati. Evamassāyaṃ apaṇṇako dhammo susamatto samādinno, ubhayaṃsaṃ pharitvā tiṭṭhati, riñcati akusalaṃ ṭhānaṃ.

"Here, householders, a wise person reflects thus: 'If there is indeed an afterlife, then this worthy individual, upon the breakup of the body, after death, will be reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. Even if there is no afterlife, let the words of those venerable ascetics and brahmins be true; yet in this very life, this worthy individual is praised by the wise as a virtuous person with right view, one who affirms existence.' If there indeed is an afterlife, then this worthy individual gains on both counts: he is praised by the wise in this very life, and upon the breakup of the body, after death, he will be reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. Thus, for him, this unerring principle is well undertaken, embracing both possibilities, and forsaking the unwholesome state."

97. ‘‘Santi, gahapatayo, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘karoto kārayato, chindato chedāpayato, pacato pācāpayato, socayato socāpayato, kilamato kilamāpayato, phandato phandāpayato, pāṇamatipātayato [pāṇamatimāpayato (sī. pī.), pāṇamatipātāpayato (syā. kaṃ.), pāṇamatipāpayato (ka.)], adinnaṃ ādiyato, sandhiṃ chindato, nillopaṃ harato, ekāgārikaṃ karoto, paripanthe tiṭṭhato, paradāraṃ gacchato, musā bhaṇato; karoto na karīyati pāpaṃ. Khurapariyantena cepi cakkena yo imissā pathaviyā pāṇe ekaṃ maṃsakhalaṃ ekaṃ maṃsapuñjaṃ kareyya, natthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, natthi pāpassa āgamo. Dakkhiṇañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya hananto ghātento, chindanto chedāpento, pacanto pācento; natthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, natthi pāpassa āgamo. Uttarañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya dadanto dāpento, yajanto yajāpento; natthi tatonidānaṃ puññaṃ, natthi puññassa āgamo. Dānena damena saṃyamena saccavajjena [saccavācena (ka.)] natthi puññaṃ, natthi puññassa āgamo’ti. Tesaṃyeva kho, gahapatayo, samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā ujuvipaccanīkavādā te evamāhaṃsu – ‘karoto kārayato, chindato chedāpayato, pacato pācāpayato, socayato socāpayato, kilamato kilamāpayato, phandato phandāpayato, pāṇamatipātayato, adinnaṃ ādiyato, sandhiṃ chindato, nillopaṃ harato, ekāgārikaṃ karoto, paripanthe tiṭṭhato, paradāraṃ gacchato, musā bhaṇato; karoto karīyati pāpaṃ. Khurapariyantena cepi cakkena yo imissā pathaviyā pāṇe ekaṃ maṃsakhalaṃ ekaṃ maṃsapuñjaṃ kareyya, atthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, atthi pāpassa āgamo. Dakkhiṇañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya hananto ghātento, chindanto chedāpento, pacanto pācento; atthi tatonidānaṃ pāpaṃ, atthi pāpassa āgamo. Uttarañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya dadanto dāpento, yajanto yajāpento; atthi tatonidānaṃ puññaṃ, atthi puññassa āgamo. Dānena damena saṃyamena saccavajjena atthi puññaṃ, atthi puññassa āgamo’ti. Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, gahapatayo, nanume samaṇabrāhmaṇā aññamaññassa ujuvipaccanīkavādā’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’.

97."Householders, there are some ascetics and brahmins who hold such a view and declare: ‘For one who acts or causes others to act, who cuts or causes to cut, who torments or causes to torment, who grieves or causes to grieve, who wearies or causes to weary, who agitates or causes to agitate, who kills living beings, takes what is not given, breaks into houses, plunders, commits single-house robbery, stands on the highway, commits adultery, speaks falsely—for the doer, no evil is done. Even if one were to make the creatures on this earth into one mass of flesh, one heap of flesh, with a razor-edged wheel, there would be no evil from that cause, no accrual of evil. If one were to go along the southern bank of the Ganges killing, slaughtering, cutting, causing to be cut, tormenting, causing to torment, there would be no evil from that cause, no accrual of evil. If one were to go along the northern bank of the Ganges giving gifts, making others give, performing sacrifices, causing others to perform sacrifices, there would be no merit from that cause, no accrual of merit. By giving, by taming, by restraint, by truthful speech, there is no merit, no accrual of merit.’" "Now, householders, there are other ascetics and brahmins who directly contradict them, saying: ‘For one who acts or causes others to act, who cuts or causes to cut, who torments or causes to torment, who grieves or causes to grieve, who wearies or causes to weary, who agitates or causes to agitate, who kills living beings, takes what is not given, breaks into houses, plunders, commits single-house robbery, stands on the highway, commits adultery, speaks falsely—for the doer, evil is done. Even if one were to make the creatures on this earth into one mass of flesh, one heap of flesh, with a razor-edged wheel, there would be evil from that cause, an accrual of evil. If one were to go along the southern bank of the Ganges killing, slaughtering, cutting, causing to be cut, tormenting, causing to torment, there would be evil from that cause, an accrual of evil. If one were to go along the northern bank of the Ganges giving gifts, making others give, performing sacrifices, causing others to perform sacrifices, there would be merit from that cause, an accrual of merit. By giving, by taming, by restraint, by truthful speech, there is merit, an accrual of merit.’" "What do you think, householders? Don’t these ascetics and brahmins directly contradict each other?" "Yes, venerable sir."

98. ‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘karoto kārayato, chindato chedāpayato, pacato pācāpayato, socayato socāpayato, kilamato kilamāpayato, phandato phandāpayato, pāṇamatipātayato, adinnaṃ ādiyato, sandhiṃ chindato, nillopaṃ harato, ekāgārikaṃ karoto, paripanthe tiṭṭhato, paradāraṃ gacchato, musā bhaṇato; karoto na karīyati pāpaṃ. Santaṃyeva kho pana hetuṃ ‘atthi hetū’ti vācaṃ bhāsati; sāssa hoti sammāvācā. Santaṃyeva kho pana hetuṃ ‘atthi hetū’ti āha, ye te arahanto hetuvādā tesamayaṃ na paccanīkaṃ karoti. Santaṃyeva kho pana hetuṃ ‘atthi hetū’ti paraṃ saññāpeti; sāssa hoti saddhammasaññatti. Tāya ca pana saddhammasaññattiyā nevattānukkaṃseti, na paraṃ vambheti. Iti pubbeva kho panassa dussīlyaṃ pahīnaṃ hoti, susīlyaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ – ayañca sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā ariyānaṃ apaccanīkatā saddhammasaññatti anattukkaṃsanā aparavambhanā. Evamassime aneke kusalā dhammā sambhavanti sammādiṭṭhipaccayā.

102.Here, householders, those ascetics and brahmins who hold such a view and declare: 'There is a cause, there is a condition for the defilement of beings; with cause and condition, beings become defiled. There is a cause, there is a condition for the purification of beings; with cause and condition, beings become purified. There is strength, there is energy, there is human strength, there is human exertion; not all beings, not all living creatures, not all existing things, not all life are powerless, without strength, without energy, subject to destiny, determined by fate, transformed by association, experiencing pleasure and pain in the six classes of birth'—it is to be expected of them that they will abandon these three unwholesome actions—wrong conduct of body, wrong conduct of speech, wrong conduct of mind—and undertake these three wholesome actions—right conduct of body, right conduct of speech, right conduct of mind. Why is that? Because those venerable ascetics and brahmins see the danger, the degradation, and the defilement in unwholesome qualities, and they see the renunciation, the benefit, and the purifying aspect in wholesome qualities. Since there is indeed a cause, their view is 'There is a cause'—that is their right view. Since there is indeed a cause, they resolve 'There is a cause'—that is their right intention. Since there is indeed a cause, they speak the words 'There is a cause'—that is their right speech. Since there is indeed a cause, they say 'There is a cause'—they do not oppose those Arahants who assert causality. Since there is indeed a cause, they persuade others 'There is a cause'—that is their teaching of the true Dhamma. And by that teaching of the true Dhamma, they neither exalt themselves nor disparage others. Thus, their immorality is abandoned, and morality is established—and this is right view, right intention, right speech, non-opposition to the noble ones, teaching of the true Dhamma, neither self-exaltation nor disparagement of others. In this way, many wholesome qualities arise in them conditioned by right view.

‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘sace kho atthi hetu, evamayaṃ bhavaṃ purisapuggalo kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjissati. Kāmaṃ kho pana māhu hetu, hotu nesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ vacanaṃ; atha ca panāyaṃ bhavaṃ purisapuggalo diṭṭheva dhamme viññūnaṃ pāsaṃso – sīlavā purisapuggalo sammādiṭṭhi hetuvādo’ti. Sace kho atthi hetu, evaṃ imassa bhoto purisapuggalassa ubhayattha kaṭaggaho – yañca diṭṭheva dhamme viññūnaṃ pāsaṃso, yañca kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjissati. Evamassāyaṃ apaṇṇako dhammo susamatto samādinno, ubhayaṃsaṃ pharitvā tiṭṭhati, riñcati akusalaṃ ṭhānaṃ.

Here, householders, a wise person reflects thus: ‘If indeed there is a cause, then this worthy person, on the dissolution of the body, after death, will reappear in a good destination, a heavenly world. Even if there is no cause, let the words of those venerable ascetics and brahmins be true; yet still, this worthy person is praised by the discerning in this very life as a virtuous person with right view, one who asserts causality.’ If indeed there is a cause, then for this worthy person there is a gain on both sides—both being praised by the discerning in this very life and, on the dissolution of the body, after death, reappearing in a good destination, a heavenly world. Thus, this unerring principle is well undertaken and embraced by him, it stands pervading both sides, and he forsakes the unwholesome state.

103. ‘‘Santi, gahapatayo, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi sabbaso āruppā’ti. Tesaṃyeva kho, gahapatayo, samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā ujuvipaccanīkavādā. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘atthi sabbaso āruppā’ti. Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, gahapatayo, nanume samaṇabrāhmaṇā aññamaññassa ujuvipaccanīkavādā’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ye kho te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi sabbaso āruppā’ti, idaṃ me adiṭṭhaṃ; yepi te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘atthi sabbaso āruppā’ti, idaṃ me aviditaṃ. Ahañceva [ahañce (?)] kho pana ajānanto apassanto ekaṃsena ādāya vohareyyaṃ – idameva saccaṃ, moghamaññanti, na metaṃ assa patirūpaṃ. Ye kho te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi sabbaso āruppā’ti, sace tesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ vacanaṃ, ṭhānametaṃ vijjati – ye te devā rūpino manomayā, apaṇṇakaṃ me tatrūpapatti bhavissati. Ye pana te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘atthi sabbaso āruppā’ti, sace tesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ vacanaṃ, ṭhānametaṃ vijjati – ye te devā arūpino saññāmayā, apaṇṇakaṃ me tatrūpapatti bhavissati. Dissanti kho pana rūpādhikaraṇaṃ [rūpakāraṇā (ka.)] daṇḍādāna-satthādāna-kalaha-viggaha-vivāda-tuvaṃtuvaṃ-pesuñña-musāvādā. ‘Natthi kho panetaṃ sabbaso arūpe’’’ti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya rūpānaṃyeva nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti.

103.Householders, there are some ascetics and brahmins who hold such a view and declare: ‘There is no formless realm at all.’ But other ascetics and brahmins directly contradict them, saying: ‘There is indeed a formless realm.’ What do you think, householders? Don’t these ascetics and brahmins directly contradict each other?” “Yes, venerable sir.” “Here, householders, a wise person reflects thus: ‘Those venerable ascetics and brahmins who declare and hold the view, "There is no formless realm at all"—this I have not seen. And those venerable ascetics and brahmins who declare and hold the view, "There is indeed a formless realm"—this I have not known. If I, not knowing and not seeing, were to take a one-sided stance and declare, "This alone is true, the rest is false," that would not be fitting for me. If the statement of those venerable ascetics and brahmins who say, "There is no formless realm at all," is true, then it is possible that I will be reborn among the deities who are form-beings and mind-made; in that case, my unerring principle will hold true for that rebirth. But if the statement of those venerable ascetics and brahmins who say, "There is indeed a formless realm," is true, then it is possible that I will be reborn among the formless deities, who are perception-made; in that case, my unerring principle will hold true for that rebirth. Moreover, instances of taking up sticks and weapons, quarrels, disputes, conflicts, bickering, slander, and false speech are seen because of form. But there is none of this at all in the formless realm.’ Reflecting thus, he practices for disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation regarding forms.

104. ‘‘Santi, gahapatayo, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti. Tesaṃyeva kho, gahapatayo, samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā ujuvipaccanīkavādā. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘atthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti. Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, gahapatayo, nanume samaṇabrāhmaṇā aññamaññassa ujuvipaccanīkavādā’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Tatra, gahapatayo, viññū puriso iti paṭisañcikkhati – ye kho te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti, idaṃ me adiṭṭhaṃ; yepi te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘atthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti, idaṃ me aviditaṃ. Ahañceva kho pana ajānanto apassanto ekaṃsena ādāya vohareyyaṃ – idameva saccaṃ, moghamaññanti, na metaṃ assa patirūpaṃ. Ye kho te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti, sace tesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ vacanaṃ, ṭhānametaṃ vijjati – ye te devā arūpino saññāmayā apaṇṇakaṃ me tatrūpapatti bhavissati. Ye pana te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘atthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti, sace tesaṃ bhavataṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ vacanaṃ, ṭhānametaṃ vijjati – yaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme parinibbāyissāmi. Ye kho te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘natthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti, tesamayaṃ diṭṭhi sārāgāya [sarāgāya (syā. kaṃ.)] santike, saṃyogāya santike, abhinandanāya santike, ajjhosānāya santike, upādānāya santike. Ye pana te bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘atthi sabbaso bhavanirodho’ti, tesamayaṃ diṭṭhi asārāgāya santike, asaṃyogāya santike, anabhinandanāya santike, anajjhosānāya santike, anupādānāya santike’’’ti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya bhavānaṃyeva nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti.

104.Householders, there are some ascetics and brahmins who speak thus, holding such a view: 'There is no cessation of becoming at all.' And indeed, householders, some other ascetics and brahmins hold a view directly opposed to them. They say: 'There is a cessation of becoming at all.' What do you think, householders? Don’t these ascetics and brahmins directly contradict each other?” “Yes, venerable sir.” “Here, householders, a wise person reflects thus: 'Those venerable ascetics and brahmins who speak thus, holding the view "There is no cessation of becoming at all"—this I have not seen. Those venerable ascetics and brahmins who speak thus, holding the view "There is a cessation of becoming at all"—this I have not known. Without knowing or seeing, if I were to take a one-sided stance and declare, "This alone is true, all else is false," that would not be fitting for me. If the statement of those venerable ascetics and brahmins who speak thus "There is no cessation of becoming at all" is true, then it is possible that I will have rebirth among the formless deities, who are perception-made; in that case, my unerring principle will hold true for that rebirth. But if the statement of those venerable ascetics and brahmins who speak thus "There is a cessation of becoming at all" is true, then it is possible that I will attain final Nibbāna in this very life. The view of those venerable ascetics and brahmins who speak thus "There is no cessation of becoming at all" is close to passion, close to bondage, close to delight, close to obsession, close to clinging. But the view of those venerable ascetics and brahmins who speak thus "There is a cessation of becoming at all" is free from passion, free from bondage, free from delight, free from obsession, free from clinging.' Having reflected thus, he practices for disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation regarding becoming itself.

105. ‘‘Cattārome, gahapatayo, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame cattāro? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco puggalo attantapo hoti attaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto. Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco puggalo parantapo hoti paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto. Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco puggalo attantapo ca hoti attaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto parantapo ca paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto. Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco puggalo nevattantapo hoti nāttaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto na parantapo na paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto; so anattantapo aparantapo diṭṭheva dhamme nicchāto nibbuto sītībhūto sukhappaṭisaṃvedī brahmabhūtena attanā viharati.

105."Householders, these four persons are found existing in the world. Which four? Here, householders, a certain person is given to self-torment, devoted to the practice of self-mortification. Here, householders, a certain person is given to the torment of others, devoted to the practice of tormenting others. Here, householders, a certain person is given to self-torment and to the torment of others, devoted to the practice of self-mortification and to the practice of tormenting others. Here, householders, a certain person is given neither to self-torment nor to the torment of others, nor to the practice of self-mortification nor to the practice of tormenting others; being without self-torment and without the torment of others, in this very life he is without thirst, quenched, cooled, experiencing happiness, abiding with a self that has become Brahma."

106. ‘‘Katamo ca, gahapatayo, puggalo attantapo attaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco puggalo acelako hoti muttācāro hatthāpalekhano…pe… [vitthāro ma. ni. 2.6-7 kandarakasutte] iti evarūpaṃ anekavihitaṃ kāyassa ātāpanaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto viharati. Ayaṃ vuccati, gahapatayo, puggalo attantapo attaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto.

106."And what, householders, is the person given to self-torment, devoted to the practice of self-mortification? Here, householders, a certain person is naked, unrestrained in his habits, licks his hand... and so on, he dwells devoted to the practice of mortifying and tormenting the body in various ways. This, householders, is called the person given to self-torment, devoted to the practice of self-mortification."

‘‘Katamo ca, gahapatayo, puggalo parantapo paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco puggalo orabbhiko hoti sūkariko…pe… ye vā panaññepi keci kurūrakammantā. Ayaṃ vuccati, gahapatayo, puggalo parantapo paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto.

"And what, householders, is the person given to the torment of others, devoted to the practice of tormenting others? Here, householders, a certain person is a slaughterer of sheep, a slaughterer of pigs... or anyone else engaged in cruel occupations. This, householders, is called the person given to the torment of others, devoted to the practice of tormenting others."

‘‘Katamo ca, gahapatayo, puggalo attantapo ca attaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto parantapo ca paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco puggalo rājā vā hoti khattiyo muddhāvasitto…pe… tepi daṇḍatajjitā bhayatajjitā assumukhā rudamānā parikammāni karonti. Ayaṃ vuccati, gahapatayo, puggalo attantapo ca attaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto parantapo ca paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto.

"And what, householders, is the person given to self-torment and to the torment of others, devoted to the practice of self-mortification and to the practice of tormenting others? Here, householders, a certain person is a king or a noble warrior consecrated as king... and so on... even those people, threatened with the rod, threatened with fear, with tearful faces, weeping, do the work. This, householders, is called the person given to self-torment and to the torment of others, devoted to the practice of self-mortification and to the practice of tormenting others."

‘‘Katamo ca, gahapatayo, puggalo nevattantapo nāttaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto na parantapo na paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto; so anattantapo aparantapo diṭṭheva dhamme nicchāto nibbuto sītībhūto sukhappaṭisaṃvedī brahmabhūtena attanā viharati? Idha, gahapatayo, tathāgato loke uppajjati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho…pe… so ime pañca nīvaraṇe pahāya cetaso upakkilese paññāya dubbalīkaraṇe vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā ajjhattaṃ sampasādanaṃ cetaso ekodibhāvaṃ avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhijaṃ pītisukhaṃ dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ…pe… tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati.

"And what, householders, is the person given neither to self-torment nor to the torment of others, nor to the practice of self-mortification nor to the practice of tormenting others; who, being without self-torment and without the torment of others, in this very life is without thirst, quenched, cooled, experiencing happiness, abiding with a self that has become Brahma? Here, householders, a Tathagata arises in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One... Having abandoned these five hindrances, defilements of the mind that weaken wisdom, quite secluded from sensual pleasures, secluded from unwholesome states, he enters and dwells in the first jhana, which is accompanied by thought and examination, with rapture and happiness born of seclusion. With the subsiding of thought and examination, with internal serenity, with unification of mind, without thought, without examination, he enters and dwells in the second jhana, which is accompanied by rapture and happiness born of samādhi... the third jhana... the fourth jhana."

‘‘So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte pubbenivāsānussatiñāṇāya cittaṃ abhininnāmeti. So anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati seyyathidaṃ – ekampi jātiṃ dvepi jātiyo…pe… iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati. So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte sattānaṃ cutūpapātañāṇāya cittaṃ abhininnāmeti. So dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passati cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe, sugate duggate…pe… yathākammūpage satte pajānāti. So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte āsavānaṃ khayañāṇāya cittaṃ abhininnāmeti. So ‘idaṃ dukkha’nti yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti…pe… ‘ayaṃ āsavanirodhagāminī paṭipadā’ti yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Tassa evaṃ jānato evaṃ passato kāmāsavāpi cittaṃ vimuccati, bhavāsavāpi cittaṃ vimuccati, avijjāsavāpi cittaṃ vimuccati. Vimuttasmiṃ vimuttamiti ñāṇaṃ hoti. ‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāti. Ayaṃ vuccati, gahapatayo, puggalo nevattantapo nāttaparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto na parantapo na paraparitāpanānuyogamanuyutto; so anattantapo aparantapo diṭṭheva dhamme nicchāto nibbuto sītībhūto sukhappaṭisaṃvedī brahmabhūtena attanā viharatī’’ti.

"When his mind is thus collected, purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilements, malleable, workable, firm, and unshakable, he directs and inclines it to the knowledge of the recollection of past lives. He recollects his manifold past existences, that is, one birth, two births... and so on... Thus with their aspects and details he recollects his manifold past existences. When his mind is thus collected, purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilements, malleable, workable, firm, and unshakable, he directs and inclines it to the knowledge of the passing away and reappearance of beings. With the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, he sees beings passing away and reappearing, inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate... and so on... He understands how beings pass on according to their actions. When his mind is thus collected, purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilements, malleable, workable, firm, and unshakable, he directs and inclines it to the knowledge of the destruction of the taints. He understands as it actually is: ‘This is suffering’... and so on... ‘This is the path leading to the cessation of the taints.’ As he knows and sees thus, his mind is liberated from the taint of sensual desire, the taint of becoming, and the taint of ignorance. When liberated, there is the knowledge: ‘Liberated.’ He understands: ‘Birth is destroyed, the spiritual journey has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is nothing further for this state of being.’ This, householders, is called the person given neither to self-torment nor to the torment of others, nor to the practice of self-mortification nor to the practice of tormenting others; who, being without self-torment and without the torment of others, in this very life is without thirst, quenched, cooled, experiencing happiness, abiding with a self that has become Brahma."

Evaṃ vutte, sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama! Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya ‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’ti; evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Ete mayaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāma dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsake no bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gate’’ti.

"When this was said, the brahmin householders of Sālā said to the Blessed One: "Magnificent, Master Gotama! Magnificent! Just as, Master Gotama, one might set upright what has been overturned, or reveal what has been hidden, or show the path to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness, thinking ‘those with eyes will see forms’—in the same way, the Dhamma has been made clear by Master Gotama in manifold ways. We go to Master Gotama for refuge, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of monks. May Master Gotama remember us as lay followers who have gone for refuge from this day forth as long as life lasts."

Apaṇṇakasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ.

"The Apaṇṇaka Sutta, the tenth, is concluded."

Gahapativaggo niṭṭhito paṭhamo.

"The Chapter on Householders, the First, is concluded."
