# 41. Sāleyyakasuttaṃ

## 41. The Sāleyyaka Sutta

439. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ yena sālā nāma kosalānaṃ brāhmaṇagāmo tadavasari. Assosuṃ kho sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā – ‘‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ sālaṃ anuppatto. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ; kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti’. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’ti.

439.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was traveling in the Kosala country with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus, and he arrived at Sālā, a Kosalan brahmin village. The brahmin householders of Sālā heard: “Friends, it is said, the ascetic Gotama, son of the Sakyans, who went forth from the Sakyan clan, is traveling in the Kosala country with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus, and has arrived at Sālā. Now regarding that Master Gotama, such a good report has spread: ‘That Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, perfect in knowledge and conduct, fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of those who can be trained, teacher of gods and humans, enlightened, blessed. He makes known this world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its gods and humans, having realized it himself with direct knowledge. He teaches the Dhamma which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and phrasing; he reveals a life of complete purity, entirely perfect.’ It is good indeed to see such Arahants.”

Atha kho sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā appekacce bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu, sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce yena bhagavā tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce bhagavato santike nāmagottaṃ sāvetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce tuṇhībhūtā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘ko nu kho, bho gotama, hetu, ko paccayo, yena midhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti? Ko pana, bho gotama, hetu, ko paccayo, yena midhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjantī’’ti?

Then the brahmins and householders of Sālā approached the Blessed One. Having approached, some paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some exchanged greetings with the Blessed One and, after polite and amiable talk, sat down to one side; some extended their hands in reverential salutation toward the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some announced their name and lineage in the presence of the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some remained silent and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the brahmins and householders of Sālā said to the Blessed One: “What is the cause, Master Gotama, what is the reason, why some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell? And what is the cause, Master Gotama, what is the reason, why some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a happy destination, in a heavenly world?”

‘‘Adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti. Dhammacariyāsamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjantī’’ti.

“Householders, it is because of unrighteous and uneven conduct that some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell. It is because of righteous and even conduct that some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a happy destination, in a heavenly world.”

‘‘Na kho mayaṃ imassa bhoto gotamassa saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāma. Sādhu no bhavaṃ gotamo tathā dhammaṃ desetu, yathā mayaṃ imassa bhoto gotamassa saṃkhittena bhāsitassa, vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa, vitthārena atthaṃ ājāneyyāmā’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, gahapatayo, suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

“We do not understand in detail the meaning of this brief statement of Master Gotama, which has not been analyzed in detail. It would be good if Master Gotama would teach us the Dhamma in such a way that we might understand in detail the meaning of this brief statement of Master Gotama, which has not been analyzed in detail.” “Then, householders, listen and pay close attention. I will speak.” “Yes, Master,” replied the brahmin householders of Sālā. The Blessed One said:

440. ‘‘Tividhaṃ kho, gahapatayo, kāyena adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti, catubbidhaṃ vācāya adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti, tividhaṃ manasā adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti.

440.“Householders, there are three kinds of unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of the body, four kinds of unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of speech, and three kinds of unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of the mind.”

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco pāṇātipātī hoti, luddo [luddo dāruṇo (ka.) ṭīkā oloketabbā] lohitapāṇi hatappahate niviṭṭho adayāpanno pāṇabhūtesu [sabbapāṇabhūtesu (syā. kaṃ. ka.)].

“And how, householders, is there a threefold bodily unrighteous and uneven conduct? Here, householders, someone is a killer of living beings—fierce, bloody-handed, devoted to striking and slaying, merciless to living beings.

‘‘Adinnādāyī kho pana hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ, gāmagataṃ vā araññagataṃ vā, taṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti.

He takes what is not given. He takes what belongs to others, their possessions, whether in a village or the wilderness, with the intention of theft.”

‘‘Kāmesumicchācārī kho pana hoti. Yā tā māturakkhitā piturakkhitā mātāpiturakkhitā bhāturakkhitā bhaginirakkhitā ñātirakkhitā gottarakkhitā dhammarakkhitā sassāmikā saparidaṇḍā antamaso mālāguḷaparikkhittāpi, tathārūpāsu cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti.

He engages in sexual misconduct. He engages in sexual misconduct with women who are protected by their mother, protected by their father, protected by both mother and father, protected by their brother, protected by their sister, protected by their relatives, protected by their clan, protected by the Dhamma, who have husbands, who are liable to punishment, or even those encircled with garlands as a sign of betrothal. This, householders, is how there is a threefold unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of the body.”

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco musāvādī hoti. Sabhāgato vā parisāgato vā, ñātimajjhagato vā pūgamajjhagato vā rājakulamajjhagato vā, abhinīto sakkhipuṭṭho – ‘ehambho purisa, yaṃ jānāsi taṃ vadehī’ti, so ajānaṃ vā āha – ‘jānāmī’ti, jānaṃ vā āha – ‘na jānāmī’ti, apassaṃ vā āha – ‘passāmī’ti, passaṃ vā āha – ‘na passāmī’ti [so āha ajānaṃ vā ahaṃ jānāmīti jānaṃ vā ahaṃ na jānāmīti apassaṃ vā ahaṃ passāmīti passaṃ vā ahaṃ na passāmīti (ka.)]. Iti attahetu vā parahetu vā āmisakiñcikkhahetu vā sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti.

“And how, householders, is there a fourfold unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of speech? Here, householders, someone is a liar. When summoned to an assembly, a gathering, among relatives, in a guild, or in the royal court, and brought forward as a witness, being asked: ‘Good man, tell what you know,’ he, not knowing, says ‘I know’; or knowing, says ‘I do not know’; or not seeing, says ‘I see’; or seeing, says ‘I do not see.’ Thus, for his own sake, or for another’s sake, or for some trifling gain, he speaks a deliberate lie.

‘‘Pisuṇavāco kho pana hoti. Ito sutvā amutra akkhātā imesaṃ bhedāya, amutra vā sutvā imesaṃ akkhātā amūsaṃ bhedāya. Iti samaggānaṃ vā bhettā [bhedakā (ka.), bhedetā (syā. kaṃ.), tadaṭṭhakathāyaṃ pana bhettāti dissati], bhinnānaṃ vā anuppadātā, vaggārāmo vaggarato vagganandī vaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

He is one who speaks divisively. Having heard something here, he reports it there to create dissension, or having heard something there, he reports it here to create dissension. Thus, he is a divisive influence on those who are united, or he inflames those who are divided; he enjoys discord, delights in discord, is pleased by discord, and speaks words that create discord.

‘‘Pharusavāco kho pana hoti. Yā sā vācā aṇḍakā [kaṇḍakā (ka.)] kakkasā parakaṭukā parābhisajjanī kodhasāmantā asamādhisaṃvattanikā, tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

“Furthermore, he is one of harsh speech. That speech of his is abusive, rough, biting, provoking enmity, connected with anger, and not conducive to samādhi. Such speech does he utter.”

‘‘Samphappalāpī kho pana hoti. Akālavādī abhūtavādī anatthavādī adhammavādī avinayavādī. Anidhānavatiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti akālena anapadesaṃ apariyantavatiṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti.

“He is given to idle chatter. He speaks out of season, speaks what is not factual, speaks what is unbeneficial, speaks what is not Dhamma, speaks what is not discipline. He speaks speech that is ungrounded, untimely, without reference, rambling, and unbeneficial. Thus, householders, there is this fourfold unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of speech.”

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco abhijjhālu hoti, yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ taṃ abhijjhātā hoti – ‘aho vata yaṃ parassa taṃ mamassā’’’ti!

“And how, householders, is there a threefold unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of mind? Here, householders, someone is covetous. He covets the wealth and possessions of others, thinking, ‘Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!’”

‘‘Byāpannacitto kho pana hoti paduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ‘ime sattā haññantu vā vajjhantu vā ucchijjantu vā vinassantu vā mā vā ahesu’’’nti [mā vā ahesuṃ iti vāti (sī. pī. ka.)].

“He is of a mind affected by ill will, with thoughts of malice: ‘May these beings be killed, or be destroyed, or be cut off, or perish, or may they not exist!’”

‘‘Micchādiṭṭhiko kho pana hoti viparītadassano – ‘natthi dinnaṃ natthi yiṭṭhaṃ natthi hutaṃ, natthi sukatadukkaṭānaṃ kammānaṃ phalaṃ vipāko, natthi ayaṃ loko natthi paro loko, natthi mātā natthi pitā, natthi sattā opapātikā, natthi loke samaṇabrāhmaṇā sammaggatā sammāpaṭipannā ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā adhammacariyāvisamacariyā hoti.

“One is of wrong view, distorted in outlook, holding: ‘There is no merit in giving, no merit in sacrifice, no merit in offerings. There is no result or consequence of good and bad deeds. There is no this world, no other world. There is no mother, no father. There are no beings who are reborn spontaneously. There are no ascetics and brahmins in the world who have gone rightly and practice rightly, and who, having realized this world and the other world by themselves with direct knowledge, make them known.’ Thus, householders, there is a threefold unrighteous and uneven conduct by way of mind.”

‘‘Evaṃ adhammacariyāvisamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti.

“Thus, householders, it is because of such unrighteous and uneven conduct that some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell.

441. ‘‘Tividhaṃ kho, gahapatayo, kāyena dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti, catubbidhaṃ vācāya dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti, tividhaṃ manasā dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti.

441.“Householders, there is a threefold righteous and even conduct by way of the body, a fourfold righteous and even conduct by way of speech, and a threefold righteous and even conduct by way of mind.

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco pāṇātipātaṃ pahāya pāṇātipātā paṭivirato hoti, nihitadaṇḍo nihitasattho lajjī dayāpanno sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharati.

“And how, householders, is there a threefold righteous and even conduct by way of the body? Here, householders, someone, having abandoned the killing of living beings, abstains from the killing of living beings; with rod and weapon laid aside, scrupulous, merciful, he dwells sympathetic for the welfare of all living beings.

‘‘Adinnādānaṃ pahāya adinnādānā paṭivirato hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ, gāmagataṃ vā araññagataṃ vā, taṃ nādinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti.

Having abandoned taking what is not given, he abstains from taking what is not given. He does not take, with thievish intent, what belongs to another, whether property or equipment, in a village or in the wilderness.

‘‘Kāmesumicchācāraṃ pahāya kāmesumicchācārā paṭivirato hoti. Yā tā māturakkhitā piturakkhitā mātāpiturakkhitā bhāturakkhitā bhaginirakkhitā ñātirakkhitā gottarakkhitā dhammarakkhitā sassāmikā saparidaṇḍā antamaso mālāguḷaparikkhittāpi, tathārūpāsu na cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ kāyena dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti.

Having abandoned sexual misconduct, he abstains from sexual misconduct. Regarding those protected by their mother, protected by their father, protected by both mother and father, protected by a brother, protected by a sister, protected by relatives, protected by lineage, protected by the Dhamma, those with husbands, those liable to punishment, or even those betrothed with a garland of flowers, he does not engage in sexual misconduct with such women. Thus, householders, there is this threefold righteous conduct and practice in accordance with the Dhamma by way of body.

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco musāvādaṃ pahāya musāvādā paṭivirato hoti. Sabhāgato vā parisāgato vā, ñātimajjhagato vā pūgamajjhagato vā rājakulamajjhagato vā, abhinīto sakkhipuṭṭho – ‘ehambho purisa, yaṃ jānāsi taṃ vadehī’ti, so ajānaṃ vā āha – ‘na jānāmī’ti, jānaṃ vā āha – ‘jānāmī’ti, apassaṃ vā āha – ‘na passāmī’ti, passaṃ vā āha – ‘passāmī’ti. Iti attahetu vā parahetu vā āmisakiñcikkhahetu vā na sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti.

And how, householders, is there a fourfold righteous conduct and practice in accordance with the Dhamma by way of speech? Here, householders, a certain person, having abandoned false speech, abstains from false speech. When summoned to a council, an assembly, a gathering of relatives, a gathering of guilds, or the king’s court, and questioned as a witness—‘Come, good man, speak what you know’—not knowing, he says, ‘I do not know’; knowing, he says, ‘I know’; not seeing, he says, ‘I do not see’; seeing, he says, ‘I see.’ Thus, he does not knowingly speak falsehood for his own sake, for another’s sake, or for some trifling material gain.

‘‘Pisuṇaṃ vācaṃ pahāya pisuṇāya vācāya paṭivirato hoti, ito sutvā na amutra akkhātā imesaṃ bhedāya, amutra vā sutvā na imesaṃ akkhātā amūsaṃ bhedāya. Iti bhinnānaṃ vā sandhātā, sahitānaṃ vā anuppadātā, samaggārāmo samaggarato samagganandī samaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

Having abandoned divisive speech, he abstains from divisive speech. What he has heard here, he does not repeat there so as to cause dissension among those people; nor does he repeat what he has heard there so as to cause dissension among these people. Thus he is a reconciler of those who are divided and an encourager of those who are united. He delights in concord, rejoices in concord, and speaks words that promote concord.

‘‘Pharusaṃ vācaṃ pahāya pharusāya vācāya paṭivirato hoti. Yā sā vācā nelā kaṇṇasukhā pemanīyā hadayaṅgamā porī bahujanakantā bahujanamanāpā – tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

Having abandoned harsh speech, he abstains from harsh speech. He speaks words that are gentle, pleasing to the ear, affectionate, going to the heart, urbane, liked by many, and agreeable to many.

‘‘Samphappalāpaṃ pahāya samphappalāpā paṭivirato hoti. Kālavādī bhūtavādī atthavādī dhammavādī vinayavādī nidhānavatiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti kālena sāpadesaṃ pariyantavatiṃ atthasaṃhitaṃ. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, catubbidhaṃ vācāya dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti.

Having abandoned idle chatter, he abstains from idle chatter. He speaks at the right time, speaks what is true, speaks what is meaningful, speaks on the Dhamma, and speaks on the Discipline. His speech is a treasure, uttered at the right time, with reason, limited, and connected with the goal. Thus, householders, there is this fourfold righteous conduct and practice in accordance with the Dhamma by way of speech.

‘‘Kathañca, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti? Idha, gahapatayo, ekacco anabhijjhālu hoti, yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ taṃ nābhijjhātā hoti – ‘aho vata yaṃ parassa taṃ mamassā’ti!

And how, householders, is there a threefold righteous conduct and practice in accordance with the Dhamma by way of mind? Here, householders, a certain person is without covetousness; he does not covet the wealth and possessions of others, thinking, ‘Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!’

‘‘Abyāpannacitto kho pana hoti appaduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ‘ime sattā averā abyābajjhā anīghā sukhī attānaṃ pariharantū’ti.

Furthermore, he abides with a mind free from ill will, with uncorrupted thought. He thinks: ‘May these beings be free from animosity, free from malice, free from trouble, and may they live happily.’

‘‘Sammādiṭṭhiko kho pana hoti aviparītadassano – ‘atthi dinnaṃ atthi yiṭṭhaṃ atthi hutaṃ, atthi sukatadukkaṭānaṃ kammānaṃ phalaṃ vipāko, atthi ayaṃ loko atthi paro loko, atthi mātā atthi pitā, atthi sattā opapātikā, atthi loke samaṇabrāhmaṇā sammaggatā sammāpaṭipannā ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Evaṃ kho, gahapatayo, tividhaṃ manasā dhammacariyāsamacariyā hoti.

He has right view, an undistorted view, seeing thus: ‘There is what is given, what is offered, what is sacrificed; there is fruit and result of good and bad deeds; there is this world and the next world; there is mother and father; there are beings who are reborn spontaneously; there are in the world ascetics and brahmins who have attained to the right path, who practice rightly, and who proclaim this world and the next after having directly known and realized it for themselves.’ Thus, householders, there is this threefold righteous conduct and practice in accordance with the Dhamma by way of mind.

‘‘Evaṃ dhammacariyāsamacariyāhetu kho, gahapatayo, evamidhekacce sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti.

Householders, it is because of such righteous conduct and practice in accordance with the Dhamma that some beings here, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, the heavenly world.

442. ‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā khattiyamahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā khattiyamahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

442.Householders, if a person who practices the Dhamma and lives righteously should wish: ‘Oh, that upon the dissolution of the body, after death, I might be reborn into fellowship with wealthy nobles!’—this possibility exists, that he might be reborn into fellowship with wealthy nobles upon the dissolution of the body, after death. Why is that? Because he practices the Dhamma and lives righteously.

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā brāhmaṇamahāsālānaṃ…pe… gahapatimahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā gahapatimahāsālānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

If, householders, a person who practices the Dhamma and lives righteously should wish: ‘Oh, that upon the dissolution of the body, after death, I might be reborn into fellowship with wealthy brahmins… or wealthy householders!’—this possibility exists, that he might be reborn into fellowship with wealthy householders upon the dissolution of the body, after death. Why is that? Because he practices the Dhamma and lives righteously.

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā cātumahārājikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā cātumahārājikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

Householders, if a person who practices the Dhamma and lives righteously should wish: ‘Oh, that upon the dissolution of the body, after death, I might be reborn into fellowship with the devas of the Four Great Kings!’—this possibility exists, that he might be reborn into fellowship with the devas of the Four Great Kings upon the dissolution of the body, after death. Why is that? Because he practices the Dhamma and lives righteously.

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā tāvatiṃsānaṃ devānaṃ…pe… yāmānaṃ devānaṃ… tusitānaṃ devānaṃ… nimmānaratīnaṃ devānaṃ… paranimmitavasavattīnaṃ devānaṃ… brahmakāyikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā brahmakāyikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

Householders, if a person who practices the Dhamma and lives righteously should wish: ‘Oh, that upon the dissolution of the body, after death, I might be reborn into fellowship with the Tāvatiṃsa devas… the Yāma devas… the Tusita devas… the Nimmānarati devas… the Paranimmitavasavattī devas… the Brahmakāyika devas!’—this possibility exists, that he might be reborn into fellowship with the Brahmakāyika devas upon the dissolution of the body, after death. Why is that? Because he practices the Dhamma and lives righteously.

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā ābhānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā ābhānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

Householders, if a person who practices the Dhamma and lives righteously should wish: ‘Oh, that upon the dissolution of the body, after death, I might be reborn into fellowship with the Ābhā devas!’—this possibility exists, that he might be reborn into fellowship with the Ābhā devas upon the dissolution of the body, after death. Why is that? Because he practices the Dhamma and lives righteously.

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā parittābhānaṃ devānaṃ…pe… appamāṇābhānaṃ devānaṃ… ābhassarānaṃ devānaṃ… parittasubhānaṃ devānaṃ… appamāṇasubhānaṃ devānaṃ… subhakiṇhānaṃ devānaṃ… vehapphalānaṃ devānaṃ… avihānaṃ devānaṃ… atappānaṃ devānaṃ… sudassānaṃ devānaṃ… sudassīnaṃ devānaṃ… akaniṭṭhānaṃ devānaṃ… ākāsānañcāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ… viññāṇañcāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ … ākiñcaññāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ… nevasaññānāsaññāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā nevasaññānāsaññāyatanūpagānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī.

Householders, if a person who practices the Dhamma and lives righteously should wish: ‘Oh, that upon the dissolution of the body, after death, I might be reborn into fellowship with the devas of limited radiance… the devas of boundless radiance… the devas of streaming radiance… the devas of limited glory… the devas of boundless glory… the devas of refulgent glory… the devas of great fruit… the devas of Aviha… the devas of Atappa… the devas of Sudassa… the devas of Sudassi… the devas of Akaniṭṭha… the devas associated with the base of the infinity of space… the devas associated with the base of the infinity of consciousness… the devas associated with the base of nothingness… the devas associated with the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception!’—this possibility exists, that he might be reborn into fellowship with the devas associated with the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception upon the dissolution of the body, after death. Why is that? Because he practices the Dhamma and lives righteously.

‘‘Ākaṅkheyya ce, gahapatayo, dhammacārī samacārī – ‘aho vatāhaṃ āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyya’nti; ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ so āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi so dhammacārī samacārī’’ti.

Householders, if a person who practices the Dhamma and lives righteously should wish: ‘Oh, that I might, by realizing it for myself with direct knowledge, in this very life, enter and abide in the taintless liberation of mind and liberation by wisdom, having destroyed the taints!’—this possibility exists, that he might, by realizing it for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life, enter and abide in the taintless liberation of mind and liberation by wisdom, having destroyed the taints. Why is that? Because he practices the Dhamma and lives righteously.

443. Evaṃ vutte, sāleyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama! Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya, cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantīti. Evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Ete mayaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāma dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsake no bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupete [pāṇupetaṃ (ka.)] saraṇaṃ gate’’ti.

443.When this was said, the brahmin householders of Sālā said to the Blessed One: "Excellent, Master Gotama! Excellent, Master Gotama! Just as one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the way to one who was lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness, so that those with eyes might see forms—so has the Dhamma been made clear by Master Gotama in many ways. We go for refuge to Master Gotama, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of bhikkhus. From today, may Master Gotama remember us as lay followers who have gone for refuge for life."

Sāleyyakasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The Sāleyyaka Sutta, the first, is concluded.
