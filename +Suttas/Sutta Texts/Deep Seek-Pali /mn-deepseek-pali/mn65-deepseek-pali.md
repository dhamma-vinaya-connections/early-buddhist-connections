# 65. Bhaddālisuttaṃ

## 65. The Bhaddāli Sutta

134. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘ahaṃ kho, bhikkhave, ekāsanabhojanaṃ bhuñjāmi; ekāsanabhojanaṃ kho, ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhuñjamāno appābādhatañca sañjānāmi appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Etha, tumhepi, bhikkhave, ekāsanabhojanaṃ bhuñjatha; ekāsanabhojanaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tumhepi bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānissatha appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañcā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā bhaddāli bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ahaṃ kho, bhante, na ussahāmi ekāsanabhojanaṃ bhuñjituṃ; ekāsanabhojanañhi me, bhante, bhuñjato siyā kukkuccaṃ, siyā vippaṭisāro’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi tvaṃ, bhaddāli, yattha nimantito assasi tattha ekadesaṃ bhuñjitvā ekadesaṃ nīharitvāpi bhuñjeyyāsi. Evampi kho tvaṃ, bhaddāli, bhuñjamāno ekāsano yāpessasī’’ti [bhuñjamāno yāpessasīti (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)]. ‘‘Evampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, na ussahāmi bhuñjituṃ; evampi hi me, bhante, bhuñjato siyā kukkuccaṃ, siyā vippaṭisāro’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā bhaddāli bhagavatā sikkhāpade paññāpiyamāne bhikkhusaṅghe sikkhaṃ samādiyamāne anussāhaṃ pavedesi. Atha kho āyasmā bhaddāli sabbaṃ taṃ temāsaṃ na bhagavato sammukhībhāvaṃ adāsi, yathā taṃ satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī.

134.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī, in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. There the Blessed One addressed the monks: "Monks!" "Venerable sir," those monks replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this: "Monks, I eat a one-sitting meal; eating a one-sitting meal, I experience little illness, little affliction, lightness, strength, and a comfortable abiding. Come, monks, you too eat a one-sitting meal; eating a one-sitting meal, you too will experience little illness, little affliction, lightness, strength, and a comfortable abiding." When this was said, Venerable Bhaddāli said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, I am unable to eat a one-sitting meal. For if I were to eat a one-sitting meal, I might have scruples and remorse." "Then, Bhaddāli, when you are invited somewhere, you may eat part of it there and having removed a portion, eat it later. Even eating in this way, Bhaddāli, you would still be eating at one sitting." "Even in this way, Venerable sir, I am unable to eat. Even eating like this, I might have scruples and remorse." Then, when the Blessed One was instituting a training rule and the Sangha was undertaking the training, Venerable Bhaddāli declared his unwillingness. And so, Venerable Bhaddāli did not present himself before the Blessed One for that whole three months, as one who does not fulfill the training in the Teacher's instruction.

135. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karonti – niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissatīti. Atha kho āyasmā bhaddāli yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā tehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ bhaddāliṃ te bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idaṃ kho, āvuso bhaddāli, bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karīyati [karaṇīyaṃ (ka.)]. Niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissati. Iṅghāvuso bhaddāli, etaṃ dosakaṃ sādhukaṃ manasi karohi, mā te pacchā dukkarataraṃ ahosī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho āyasmā bhaddāli tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā bhaddāli bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘accayo maṃ, bhante, accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ, yohaṃ bhagavatā sikkhāpade paññāpiyamāne bhikkhusaṅghe sikkhaṃ samādiyamāne anussāhaṃ pavedesiṃ. Tassa me, bhante, bhagavā accayaṃ accayato paṭiggaṇhātu āyatiṃ saṃvarāyā’’ti.

135.At that time, a number of monks were making robes for the Blessed One, thinking, "When the robes are finished, the Blessed One will set out on a journey after three months." Then Venerable Bhaddāli approached those monks, exchanged courteous greetings with them, and after an exchange of friendly, polite conversation, sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those monks said to Venerable Bhaddāli: "Friend Bhaddāli, these robes are being made for the Blessed One. When they are finished, the Blessed One will set out on a journey after three months. Come, friend Bhaddāli, carefully consider this matter, lest it be more difficult for you later." "Very well, friends," replied Venerable Bhaddāli to those monks. Then he approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, sat down to one side, and said: "An offense has overcome me, venerable sir, as one foolish, deluded, and unskillful, in that when the Blessed One was instituting a training rule and the Sangha was undertaking the training, I expressed unwillingness. Venerable sir, may the Blessed One accept my offense as an offense, so that I may exercise restraint in the future."

‘‘Taggha tvaṃ, bhaddāli, accayo accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ, yaṃ tvaṃ mayā sikkhāpade paññāpiyamāne bhikkhusaṅghe sikkhaṃ samādiyamāne anussāhaṃ pavedesi. Samayopi kho te, bhaddāli, appaṭividdho ahosi – ‘bhagavā kho sāvatthiyaṃ viharati, bhagavāpi maṃ jānissati – bhaddāli nāma bhikkhu satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī’ti. Ayampi kho te, bhaddāli, samayo appaṭividdho ahosi. Samayopi kho te, bhaddāli, appaṭividdho ahosi – ‘sambahulā kho bhikkhu sāvatthiyaṃ vassaṃ upagatā, tepi maṃ jānissanti – bhaddāli nāma bhikkhu satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī’ti. Ayampi kho te, bhaddāli, samayo appaṭividdho ahosi. Samayopi kho te, bhaddāli, appaṭividdho ahosi – ‘sambahulā kho bhikkhuniyo sāvatthiyaṃ vassaṃ upagatā, tāpi maṃ jānissanti – bhaddāli nāma bhikkhu satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī’ti. Ayampi kho te, bhaddāli, samayo appaṭividdho ahosi. Samayopi kho te, bhaddāli, appaṭividdho ahosi – ‘sambahulā kho upāsakā sāvatthiyaṃ paṭivasanti, tepi maṃ jānissanti – bhaddāli nāma bhikkhu satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī’ti. Ayampi kho te, bhaddāli, samayo appaṭividdho ahosi. Samayopi kho te, bhaddāli, appaṭividdho ahosi – ‘sambahulā kho upāsikā sāvatthiyaṃ paṭivasanti, tāpi maṃ jānissanti – bhaddāli nāma bhikkhu satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī’ti. Ayampi kho te, bhaddāli, samayo appaṭividdho ahosi. Samayopi kho te, bhaddāli, appaṭividdho ahosi – ‘sambahulā kho nānātitthiyā samaṇabrāhmaṇā sāvatthiyaṃ vassaṃ upagatā, tepi maṃ jānissanti – bhaddāli nāma bhikkhu samaṇassa gotamassa sāvako theraññataro bhikkhu sāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī’ti. Ayampi kho te, bhaddāli, samayo appaṭividdho ahosī’’ti.

"Indeed, Bhaddāli, an offense has overcome you, as one foolish, deluded, and unskillful, in that, as I was instituting a training rule and the Sangha was undertaking the training, you declared your unwillingness. A time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you: ‘The Blessed One is dwelling at Sāvatthī. The Blessed One will know about me: “The monk named Bhaddāli is not fulfilling the training in the Teacher’s dispensation.”’ This time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you. A time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you: ‘Many monks have entered the rains residence at Sāvatthī. They too will know about me: “The monk named Bhaddāli is not fulfilling the training in the Teacher’s dispensation.”’ This time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you. A time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you: ‘Many nuns have entered the rains residence at Sāvatthī. They too will know about me: “The monk named Bhaddāli is not fulfilling the training in the Teacher’s dispensation.”’ This time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you. A time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you: ‘Many laymen reside at Sāvatthī. They too will know about me: “The monk named Bhaddāli is not fulfilling the training in the Teacher’s dispensation.”’ This time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you. A time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you: ‘Many laywomen reside at Sāvatthī. They too will know about me: “The monk named Bhaddāli is not fulfilling the training in the Teacher’s dispensation.”’ This time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you. A time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you: ‘Many ascetics and brahmins of other sects have entered the rains residence at Sāvatthī. They too will know about me: “The monk named Bhaddāli, a certain senior disciple of the ascetic Gotama, is not fulfilling the training in his dispensation.”’ This time, Bhaddāli, was not discerned by you."

‘‘Accayo maṃ, bhante, accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ, yohaṃ bhagavatā sikkhāpade paññāpiyamāne bhikkhusaṅghe sikkhaṃ samādiyamāne anussāhaṃ pavedesiṃ. Tassa me, bhante, bhagavā accayaṃ accayato paṭiggaṇhātu āyatiṃ saṃvarāyā’’ti. ‘‘Taggha tvaṃ, bhaddāli, accayo accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ, yaṃ tvaṃ mayā sikkhāpade paññāpiyamāne bhikkhusaṅghe sikkhaṃ samādiyamāne anussāhaṃ pavedesi’’.

"An offense has overcome me, venerable sir, as one foolish, deluded, and unskillful, in that I declared unwillingness when the Blessed One was instituting a training rule and the Sangha was undertaking the training. May the Blessed One, venerable sir, accept my offense as an offense for the sake of restraint in the future.""Indeed, Bhaddāli, an offense has overcome you, as one foolish, deluded, and unskillful, in that you declared unwillingness when I was instituting a training rule and the Sangha was undertaking the training."

136. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, bhaddāli, idhassa bhikkhu ubhatobhāgavimutto, tamahaṃ evaṃ vadeyyaṃ – ‘ehi me tvaṃ, bhikkhu, paṅke saṅkamo hohī’ti, api nu kho so saṅkameyya vā aññena vā kāyaṃ sannāmeyya, ‘no’ti vā vadeyyā’’ti?

136."What do you think, Bhaddāli? Suppose there were a monk here who is liberated in both ways, and I were to say to him: 'Come, monk, be my companion for going into the mud.' Would he go with me, or would he turn his body away, or would he say 'no'?

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

"No, venerable sir."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, bhaddāli, idhassa bhikkhu paññāvimutto… kāyasakkhi… diṭṭhippatto… saddhāvimutto… dhammānusārī… saddhānusārī, tamahaṃ evaṃ vadeyyaṃ – ‘ehi me tvaṃ, bhikkhu, paṅke saṅkamo hohī’ti, api nu kho so saṅkameyya vā aññena vā kāyaṃ sannāmeyya, ‘no’ti vā vadeyyā’’ti?

"What do you think, Bhaddāli? Suppose there were a monk who is liberated by wisdom... a body-witness... one attained to view... liberated by faith... a Dhamma-follower... a faith-follower. If I were to say to him, ‘Come, monk, be my companion for going into the mud,’ would he go with me, or turn his body away, or would he say ‘no’?"

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

"No, venerable sir."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, bhaddāli, api nu tvaṃ, bhaddāli, tasmiṃ samaye ubhatobhāgavimutto vā hosi paññāvimutto vā kāyasakkhi vā diṭṭhippatto vā saddhāvimutto vā dhammānusārī vā saddhānusārī vā’’ti?

"What do you think, Bhaddāli? At that time, were you, Bhaddāli, liberated in both ways, or liberated by wisdom, or a body-witness, or one attained to view, or liberated by faith, or a Dhamma-follower, or a faith-follower?"

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

"No, venerable sir."

‘‘Nanu tvaṃ, bhaddāli, tasmiṃ samaye ritto tuccho aparaddho’’ti?

"Bhaddāli, weren't you empty, hollow, and at fault at that time?"

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante. Accayo maṃ, bhante, accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ, yohaṃ bhagavatā sikkhāpade paññāpiyamāne bhikkhusaṅghe sikkhaṃ samādiyamāne anussāhaṃ pavedesiṃ. Tassa me, bhante, bhagavā accayaṃ accayato paṭiggaṇhātu āyatiṃ saṃvarāyā’’ti. ‘‘Taggha tvaṃ, bhaddāli, accayo accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ, yaṃ tvaṃ mayā sikkhāpade paññāpiyamāne bhikkhusaṅghe sikkhaṃ samādiyamāne anussāhaṃ pavedesi. Yato ca kho tvaṃ, bhaddāli, accayaṃ accayato disvā yathādhammaṃ paṭikarosi, taṃ te mayaṃ paṭiggaṇhāma. Vuddhihesā, bhaddāli, ariyassa vinaye yo accayaṃ accayato disvā yathādhammaṃ paṭikaroti, āyatiṃ saṃvaraṃ āpajjati’’.

"Yes, venerable sir. An offense has overcome me, venerable sir, as one foolish, deluded, and unskillful, in that I declared unwillingness when the Blessed One was instituting a training rule and the Sangha was undertaking the training. May the Blessed One, venerable sir, accept my offense as an offense, for my restraint in the future.""Indeed, Bhaddāli, an offense has overcome you, as one foolish, deluded, and unskillful, in that you declared unwillingness when I was instituting a training rule and the Sangha was undertaking the training. But since you, Bhaddāli, having seen your offense as an offense, make amends in accordance with the Dhamma, we accept it from you. For this, Bhaddāli, is growth in the Noble One’s Discipline: that one sees an offense as an offense, makes amends in accordance with the Dhamma, and commits to restraint in the future."

137. ‘‘Idha, bhaddāli, ekacco bhikkhu satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārī hoti. Tassa evaṃ hoti – ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ vivittaṃ senāsanaṃ bhajeyyaṃ araññaṃ rukkhamūlaṃ pabbataṃ kandaraṃ giriguhaṃ susānaṃ vanapatthaṃ abbhokāsaṃ palālapuñjaṃ. Appeva nāmāhaṃ uttari [uttariṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikareyya’nti. So vivittaṃ senāsanaṃ bhajati araññaṃ rukkhamūlaṃ pabbataṃ kandaraṃ giriguhaṃ susānaṃ vanapatthaṃ abbhokāsaṃ palālapuñjaṃ. Tassa tathāvūpakaṭṭhassa viharato satthāpi upavadati, anuviccapi viññū sabrahmacārī upavadanti, devatāpi upavadanti, attāpi attānaṃ upavadati. So satthārāpi upavadito, anuviccapi viññūhi sabrahmacārīhi upavadito, devatāhipi upavadito, attanāpi attānaṃ upavadito na uttari manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikaroti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhi taṃ, bhaddāli, hoti yathā taṃ satthusāsane sikkhāya aparipūrakārissa.

137."Here, Bhaddāli, a certain bhikkhu is one who does not fulfill the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely. He thinks: ‘Let me frequent a secluded dwelling—a forest, the root of a tree, a mountain, a ravine, a hillside cave, a charnel ground, a jungle thicket, an open space, a heap of straw. Perhaps I might realize a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision proper to the noble ones.’ He frequents a secluded dwelling—a forest, the root of a tree, a mountain, a ravine, a hillside cave, a charnel ground, a jungle thicket, an open space, a heap of straw. While he dwells thus secluded, the Teacher finds fault with him, discerning fellow monks, having investigated, find fault with him, deities find fault with him, and he himself finds fault with himself. Being found fault with by the Teacher, by discerning fellow monks having investigated, by deities, and by himself, he does not realize a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision proper to the noble ones. For what reason? Because, Bhaddāli, that is how it is for one who does not fulfill the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely."

138. ‘‘Idha pana, bhaddāli, ekacco bhikkhu satthusāsane sikkhāya paripūrakārī hoti. Tassa evaṃ hoti – ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ vivittaṃ senāsanaṃ bhajeyyaṃ araññaṃ rukkhamūlaṃ pabbataṃ kandaraṃ giriguhaṃ susānaṃ vanapatthaṃ abbhokāsaṃ palālapuñjaṃ. Appeva nāmāhaṃ uttari manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikareyya’nti. So vivittaṃ senāsanaṃ bhajati araññaṃ rukkhamūlaṃ pabbataṃ kandaraṃ giriguhaṃ susānaṃ vanapatthaṃ abbhokāsaṃ palālapuñjaṃ. Tassa tathāvūpakaṭṭhassa viharato satthāpi na upavadati, anuviccapi viññū sabrahmacārī na upavadanti, devatāpi na upavadanti, attāpi attānaṃ na upavadati. So satthārāpi anupavadito, anuviccapi viññūhi sabrahmacārīhi anupavadito, devatāhipi anupavadito, attanāpi attānaṃ anupavadito uttari manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikaroti. So vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhi taṃ, bhaddāli, hoti yathā taṃ satthusāsane sikkhāya paripūrakārissa.

138."Here, Bhaddāli, a certain bhikkhu is one who fulfills the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely. He thinks: ‘Let me frequent a secluded dwelling—a forest, the root of a tree, a mountain, a ravine, a hillside cave, a charnel ground, a jungle thicket, an open space, a heap of straw. Perhaps I might realize a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision proper to the noble ones.’ He frequents a secluded dwelling—a forest, the root of a tree, a mountain, a ravine, a hillside cave, a charnel ground, a jungle thicket, an open space, a heap of straw. While he dwells thus secluded, the Teacher does not find fault with him, discerning fellow monks, having investigated, do not find fault with him, deities do not find fault with him, and he himself does not find fault with himself. Being not found fault with by the Teacher, by discerning fellow monks having investigated, by deities, and by himself, he realizes a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision proper to the noble ones. Quite secluded from sensual pleasures, secluded from unwholesome states, he enters and dwells in the first jhāna, which is accompanied by thought and examination, with rapture and happiness born of seclusion. For what reason? Because, Bhaddāli, that is how it is for one who fulfills the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely."

139. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhaddāli, bhikkhu vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā ajjhattaṃ sampasādanaṃ cetaso ekodibhāvaṃ avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhijaṃ pītisukhaṃ dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhi taṃ, bhaddāli, hoti yathā taṃ satthusāsane sikkhāya paripūrakārissa.

139."Furthermore, Bhaddāli, with the subsiding of thought and examination, a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the second jhāna, which has internal assurance and unification of mind, is without thought and examination, and has rapture and happiness born of samādhi. For what reason? Because, Bhaddāli, that is how it is for one who fulfills the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhaddāli, bhikkhu pītiyā ca virāgā upekkhako ca viharati, sato ca sampajāno sukhañca kāyena paṭisaṃvedeti, yaṃ taṃ ariyā ācikkhanti – ‘upekkhako satimā sukhavihārī’ti tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhi taṃ, bhaddāli, hoti yathā taṃ satthusāsane sikkhāya paripūrakārissa.

"Furthermore, Bhaddāli, with the fading away of rapture, a bhikkhu dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending, and experiences happiness with the body; he enters and dwells in the third jhāna, of which the noble ones declare: ‘Equanimous and mindful, he dwells happily.’ For what reason? Because, Bhaddāli, that is how it is for one who fulfills the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhaddāli, bhikkhu sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā pubbeva somanassadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamā adukkhamasukhaṃ upekkhāsatipārisuddhiṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhi taṃ, bhaddāli, hoti yathā taṃ satthusāsane sikkhāya paripūrakārissa.

"Furthermore, Bhaddāli, with the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and with the prior passing away of joy and sorrow, a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna, which is neither painful nor pleasant, and has purity of mindfulness due to equanimity. For what reason? Because, Bhaddāli, that is how it is for one who fulfills the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely."

‘‘So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte pubbenivāsānussatiñāṇāya cittaṃ abhininnāmeti. So anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati, seyyathidaṃ – ekampi jātiṃ dvepi jātiyo…pe… iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhi taṃ, bhaddāli, hoti yathā taṃ satthusāsane sikkhāya paripūrakārissa.

"When his mind is thus collected, purified, bright, spotless, rid of defilements, malleable, wieldy, firm, and attained to imperturbability, he directs his mind to the knowledge of the recollection of past lives. He recollects many kinds of past lives, that is, one birth, two births… thus with their aspects and particulars he recollects many kinds of past lives. For what reason? Because, Bhaddāli, that is how it is for one who fulfills the training in the Teacher’s Dispensation completely."

So abhiṇhakāraṇā anupubbakāraṇā tasmiṃ ṭhāne parinibbāyati. Yato kho, bhaddāli, bhadro assājānīyo abhiṇhakāraṇā anupubbakāraṇā tasmiṃ ṭhāne parinibbuto hoti, tamenaṃ assadamako uttari vaṇṇiyañca pāṇiyañca [valiyañca (sī. pī.), baliyañca (syā. kaṃ.)] anuppavecchati. Imehi kho, bhaddāli, dasahaṅgehi samannāgato bhadro assājānīyo rājāraho hoti rājabhoggo rañño aṅganteva saṅkhyaṃ gacchati.

147.“Just as, Bhaddāli, a skilled horse trainer, having obtained a fine thoroughbred horse, first trains it to accept the mouth-piece. While it is being trained to accept the mouth-piece, it will struggle and squirm in various ways, as is the case when being trained in something never done before. But through repeated and gradual training, it becomes settled in that practice. When, Bhaddāli, the fine thoroughbred horse has become settled in that practice through repeated and gradual training, the horse trainer then further trains it to accept the yoke. While it is being trained to accept the yoke, it will struggle and squirm in various ways, as is the case when being trained in something never done before. But through repeated and gradual training, it becomes settled in that practice. When, Bhaddāli, the fine thoroughbred horse has become settled in that practice through repeated and gradual training, the horse trainer then further trains it in the paces, in circling, in silent treading, in sprinting, in fearlessness, in royal qualities, in noble lineage, in supreme speed, in supreme excellence, and in supreme docility. While it is being trained in supreme speed, supreme excellence, and supreme docility, it will struggle and squirm in various ways, as is the case when being trained in something never done before. But through repeated and gradual training, it becomes settled in that practice. When, Bhaddāli, the fine thoroughbred horse has become settled in that practice through repeated and gradual training, the horse trainer then further provides it with adornments and drink. Endowed with these ten qualities, Bhaddāli, a fine thoroughbred horse becomes worthy of a king, fit for a king’s use, and reckoned as a limb of the king.

‘‘Evameva kho, bhaddāli, dasahi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu āhuneyyo hoti pāhuneyyo dakkhiṇeyyo añjalikaraṇīyo anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa. Katamehi dasahi? Idha, bhaddāli, bhikkhu asekhāya sammādiṭṭhiyā samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāsaṅkappena samannāgato hoti, asekhāya sammāvācāya samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammākammantena samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāājīvena samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāvāyāmena samannāgato hoti, asekhāya sammāsatiyā samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāsamādhinā samannāgato hoti, asekhena sammāñāṇena samannāgato hoti, asekhāya sammāvimuttiyā samannāgato hoti – imehi kho, bhaddāli, dasahi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu āhuneyyo hoti pāhuneyyo dakkhiṇeyyo añjalikaraṇīyo anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassā’’ti.

“Just so, Bhaddāli, a bhikkhu endowed with these ten qualities is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverence, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. What ten? Here, Bhaddāli, a bhikkhu is endowed with consummate right view, consummate right intention, consummate right speech, consummate right action, consummate right livelihood, consummate right effort, consummate right mindfulness, consummate right samādhi, consummate right knowledge, and consummate right liberation. It is by these ten qualities, Bhaddāli, that a bhikkhu is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverence, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā bhaddāli bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

This the Blessed One said. The Venerable Bhaddāli was satisfied and delighted with the Blessed One’s words.

Bhaddālisuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The Bhaddāli Sutta, the fifth, is concluded.
