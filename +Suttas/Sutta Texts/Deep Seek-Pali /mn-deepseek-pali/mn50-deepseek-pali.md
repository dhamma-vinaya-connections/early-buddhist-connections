# 50. Māratajjanīyasuttaṃ

## 50. The Discourse on Rebuking Māra.

506. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhaggesu viharati susumāragire bhesakaḷāvane migadāye. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā mahāmoggallāno abbhokāse caṅkamati. Tena kho pana samayena māro pāpimā āyasmato mahāmoggallānassa kucchigato hoti koṭṭhamanupaviṭṭho. Atha kho āyasmato mahāmoggallānassa etadahosi – ‘‘kiṃ nu kho me kucchi garugaro viya [garu garu viya (sī. pī. ṭīkāyaṃ pāṭhantaraṃ)]? Māsācitaṃ maññe’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno caṅkamā orohitvā vihāraṃ pavisitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno paccattaṃ yoniso manasākāsi. Addasā kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno māraṃ pāpimantaṃ kucchigataṃ koṭṭhamanupaviṭṭhaṃ. Disvāna māraṃ pāpimantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘nikkhama, pāpima; nikkhama, pāpima! Mā tathāgataṃ vihesesi, mā tathāgatasāvakaṃ. Mā te ahosi dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāyā’’ti. Atha kho mārassa pāpimato etadahosi – ‘‘ajānameva kho maṃ ayaṃ samaṇo apassaṃ evamāha – ‘nikkhama, pāpima; nikkhama, pāpima! Mā tathāgataṃ vihesesi, mā tathāgatasāvakaṃ. Mā te ahosi dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāyā’ti. Yopissa so satthā sopi maṃ neva khippaṃ jāneyya, kuto pana [kuto ca pana (syā.)] maṃ ayaṃ sāvako jānissatī’’ti? Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno māraṃ pāpimantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘evampi kho tāhaṃ, pāpima, jānāmi, mā tvaṃ maññittho – ‘na maṃ jānātī’ti. Māro tvamasi, pāpima; tuyhañhi, pāpima, evaṃ hoti – ‘ajānameva kho maṃ ayaṃ samaṇo apassaṃ evamāha – nikkhama, pāpima; nikkhama, pāpima! Mā tathāgataṃ vihesesi, mā tathāgatasāvakaṃ. Mā te ahosi dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāyāti. Yopissa so satthā sopi maṃ neva khippaṃ jāneyya, kuto pana maṃ ayaṃ sāvako jānissatī’’’ti?

506.Thus have I heard. At one time the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna was dwelling among the Bhaggas, on Susumāragiri mountain, in the Bhesakaḷā Forest, the deer park. At that time, the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna was walking in the open. At that time, Māra the Evil One had entered the belly of the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna and lodged within his intestines. Then it occurred to the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna: "Why does my belly feel so heavy? I feel as if I have overeaten." Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna, descending from his walking, entered his dwelling and sat down on the prepared seat. Sitting there, the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna directed his mind inward with thorough attention. The Venerable Mahāmoggallāna saw Māra the Evil One lodged in his belly, having entered his intestines. Seeing him, he said to Māra the Evil One: "Get out, Evil One! Get out, Evil One! Do not harass the Tathāgata, do not harass the Tathāgata’s disciple. May it not be for your long-term harm and suffering!" Then Māra the Evil One thought: "This ascetic, without knowing me, without seeing me, says: 'Get out, Evil One! Get out, Evil One! Do not harass the Tathāgata, do not harass the Tathāgata’s disciple. May it not be for your long-term harm and suffering!' Even his teacher would not quickly recognize me, how much less this disciple of his?" Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna said to Māra the Evil One: "Even so, Evil One, I know you. Do not think, 'He does not know me.' You are Māra, Evil One. For you think thus: 'This ascetic, without knowing me, without seeing me, says: "Get out, Evil One! Get out, Evil One! Do not harass the Tathāgata, do not harass the Tathāgata’s disciple. May it not be for your long-term harm and suffering!" Even his teacher would not quickly recognize me, how much less this disciple of his?'"

Atha kho mārassa pāpimato etadahosi – ‘‘jāname kho maṃ ayaṃ samaṇo passaṃ evamāha – ‘nikkhama, pāpima; nikkhama, pāpima! Mā tathāgataṃ vihesesi, mā tathāgatasāvakaṃ. Mā te ahosi dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāyā’’’ti. Atha kho māro pāpimā āyasmato mahāmoggallānassa mukhato uggantvā paccaggaḷe aṭṭhāsi.

Then Māra the Evil One thought: "This ascetic knows me. Seeing me, he says, 'Depart, Evil One! Depart, Evil One! Do not harass the Tathāgata; do not harass the Tathāgata’s disciple. May this not lead to your long-term harm and suffering.'" Then Māra the Evil One came out of the mouth of the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna and stood by the door bolt.

507. Addasā kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno māraṃ pāpimantaṃ paccaggaḷe ṭhitaṃ; disvāna māraṃ pāpimantaṃ etadavoca – ‘etthāpi kho tāhaṃ, pāpima, passāmi; mā tvaṃ maññittho ‘‘na maṃ passatī’’ti. Eso tvaṃ, pāpima, paccaggaḷe ṭhito. Bhūtapubbāhaṃ, pāpima, dūsī nāma māro ahosiṃ, tassa me kāḷī nāma bhaginī. Tassā tvaṃ putto. So me tvaṃ bhāgineyyo ahosi. Tena kho pana, pāpima, samayena kakusandho bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke uppanno hoti. Kakusandhassa kho pana, pāpima, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa vidhurasañjīvaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Yāvatā kho pana, pāpima, kakusandhassa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa sāvakā. Tesu na ca koci āyasmatā vidhurena samasamo hoti yadidaṃ dhammadesanāya. Iminā kho evaṃ [etaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)], pāpima, pariyāyena āyasmato vidhurassa vidhuroteva [vidhurassa vidhuro vidhurotveva (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] samaññā udapādi.

507.The Venerable Mahāmoggallāna saw Māra the Evil One standing by the door bolt. Seeing him, he said to Māra the Evil One: "Here too, Evil One, I see you. Do not think, 'He does not see me.' You, Evil One, are standing by the door bolt. Once, Evil One, I was a Māra named Dūsī, and I had a sister named Kāḷī. You are her son. So you were my nephew. At that time, Evil One, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, had arisen in the world. And, Evil One, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, had a pair of disciples named Vidhura and Sañjīva, the foremost and auspicious pair. Among all the disciples of the Blessed One Kakusandha, Evil One, none was equal to Venerable Vidhura in teaching the Dhamma. In this way, Evil One, the designation 'Vidhura' arose for Venerable Vidhura.

‘‘Āyasmā pana, pāpima, sañjīvo araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi appakasireneva saññāvedayitanirodhaṃ samāpajjati. Bhūtapubbaṃ, pāpima, āyasmā sañjīvo aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle saññāvedayitanirodhaṃ samāpanno nisinno hoti. Addasaṃsu kho, pāpima, gopālakā pasupālakā kassakā pathāvino āyasmantaṃ sañjīvaṃ aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle saññāvedayitanirodhaṃ samāpannaṃ nisinnaṃ; disvāna tesaṃ etadahosi – ‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho! Ayaṃ samaṇo nisinnakova kālaṅkato! Handa naṃ dahāmā’ti. Atha kho te, pāpima, gopālakā pasupālakā kassakā pathāvino tiṇañca kaṭṭhañca gomayañca saṃkaḍḍhitvā āyasmato sañjīvassa kāye upacinitvā aggiṃ datvā pakkamiṃsu. Atha kho, pāpima, āyasmā sañjīvo tassā rattiyā accayena tāya samāpattiyā vuṭṭhahitvā cīvarāni papphoṭetvā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya gāmaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Addasaṃsu kho te, pāpima, gopālakā pasupālakā kassakā pathāvino āyasmantaṃ sañjīvaṃ piṇḍāya carantaṃ; disvāna nesaṃ etadahosi – ‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho! Ayaṃ samaṇo nisinnakova kālaṅkato, svāyaṃ paṭisañjīvito’ti. Iminā kho evaṃ, pāpima, pariyāyena āyasmato sañjīvassa sañjīvoteva [sañjīvo sañjīvotveva (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] samaññā udapādi.

"Furthermore, Evil One, Venerable Sañjīva, even when gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, attains the cessation of perception and feeling with little difficulty. Once, Evil One, Venerable Sañjīva was seated at the foot of a certain tree, having attained the cessation of perception and feeling. Cowherds, shepherds, farmers, and travelers saw Venerable Sañjīva seated at the foot of a certain tree, having attained the cessation of perception and feeling; having seen him, this occurred to them: 'How amazing, how wonderful! This ascetic has died sitting down! Come, let us burn him!' Then, Evil One, those cowherds, shepherds, farmers, and travelers gathered grass, wood, and cow dung, piled it up on Venerable Sañjīva's body, set fire to it, and departed. Then, Evil One, at the end of that night, Venerable Sañjīva, having arisen from that attainment, shook out his robes, dressed in the morning, took his bowl and robe, and entered the village for alms. Those cowherds, shepherds, farmers, and travelers saw Venerable Sañjīva going for alms; having seen him, this occurred to them: 'How amazing, how wonderful! This ascetic had died sitting down, and now he has come back to life!' In this way, Evil One, the designation 'Sañjīva' arose for Venerable Sañjīva."

508. ‘‘Atha kho, pāpima, dūsissa mārassa etadahosi – ‘imesaṃ kho ahaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ sīlavantānaṃ kalyāṇadhammānaṃ neva jānāmi āgatiṃ vā gatiṃ vā. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ brāhmaṇagahapatike anvāviseyyaṃ – etha, tumhe bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme akkosatha paribhāsatha rosetha vihesetha. Appeva nāma tumhehi akkosiyamānānaṃ paribhāsiyamānānaṃ rosiyamānānaṃ vihesiyamānānaṃ siyā cittassa aññathattaṃ, yathā taṃ dūsī māro labhetha otāra’nti. Atha kho te, pāpima, dūsī māro brāhmaṇagahapatike anvāvisi – ‘etha, tumhe bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme akkosatha paribhāsatha rosetha vihesetha. Appeva nāma tumhehi akkosiyamānānaṃ paribhāsiyamānānaṃ rosiyamānānaṃ vihesiyamānānaṃ siyā cittassa aññathattaṃ, yathā taṃ dūsī māro labhetha otāra’nti.

508."Then, Evil One, this thought occurred to Māra Dūsī: ‘I do not know the coming or going of these bhikkhus who are virtuous and of good character. Suppose I were to possess brahmins and householders, (telling them): "Come, revile, insult, provoke, and harass these bhikkhus who are virtuous and of good character. Perhaps, when they are being reviled, insulted, provoked, and harassed by you, there might be a change of heart, so that Māra Dūsī might find an opportunity."’ Then, Evil One, Māra Dūsī possessed the brahmins and householders, (telling them): ‘Come, revile, insult, provoke, and harass these bhikkhus who are virtuous and of good character. Perhaps, when they are being reviled, insulted, provoked, and harassed by you, there might be a change of heart, so that Māra Dūsī might find an opportunity.’"

‘‘Atha kho te, pāpima, brāhmaṇagahapatikā anvāvisiṭṭhā dūsinā mārena bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme akkosanti paribhāsanti rosenti vihesenti – ‘ime pana muṇḍakā samaṇakā ibbhā kiṇhā [kaṇhā (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] bandhupādāpaccā ‘‘jhāyinosmā jhāyinosmā’’ti pattakkhandhā adhomukhā madhurakajātā jhāyanti pajjhāyanti nijjhāyanti apajjhāyanti. Seyyathāpi nāma ulūko rukkhasākhāyaṃ mūsikaṃ maggayamāno jhāyati pajjhāyati nijjhāyati apajjhāyati; evamevime muṇḍakā samaṇakā ibbhā kiṇhā bandhupādāpaccā ‘‘jhāyinosmā jhāyinosmā’’ti pattakkhandhā adhomukhā madhurakajātā jhāyanti pajjhāyanti nijjhāyanti apajjhāyanti. Seyyathāpi nāma kotthu nadītīre macche maggayamāno jhāyati pajjhāyati nijjhāyati apajjhāyati; evamevime muṇḍakā samaṇakā ibbhā kiṇhā bandhupādāpaccā ‘‘jhāyinosmā jhāyinosmā’’ti pattakkhandhā adhomukhā madhurakajātā jhāyanti pajjhāyanti nijjhāyanti apajjhāyanti. Seyyathāpi nāma biḷāro sandhisamalasaṅkaṭīre mūsikaṃ maggayamāno jhāyati pajjhāyati nijjhāyati apajjhāyati; evamevime muṇḍakā samaṇakā ibbhā kiṇhā bandhupādāpaccā ‘‘jhāyinosmā jhāyinosmā’’ti pattakkhandhā adhomukhā madhurakajātā jhāyanti pajjhāyanti nijjhāyanti apajjhāyanti. Seyyathāpi nāma gadrabho vahacchinno sandhisamalasaṅkaṭīre jhāyati pajjhāyati nijjhāyati apajjhāyati, evamevime muṇḍakā samaṇakā ibbhā kiṇhā bandhupādāpaccā ‘‘jhāyinosmā jhāyinosmā’’ti pattakkhandhā adhomukhā madhurakajātā jhāyanti pajjhāyanti nijjhāyanti apajjhāyantī’’ti.

"Then, Evil One, those brahmins and householders, possessed by Māra Dūsī, reviled, insulted, provoked, and harassed the virtuous bhikkhus of good character: ‘These shaven ones, these mere ascetics, these householders, these dark-skinned ones, offspring from the feet of Brahmā, go around saying, "We are meditators, we are meditators!" With their robes draped over their shoulders, faces down, lazy by nature, they meditate, contemplate, scrutinize, and examine—just like an owl on a tree branch waiting for a mouse meditates, contemplates, scrutinizes, and examines. In the same way, these shaven ones, these mere ascetics, these householders, these dark-skinned ones, offspring from the feet of Brahmā, go around saying, "We are meditators, we are meditators!" With their robes draped over their shoulders, faces down, lazy by nature, they meditate, contemplate, scrutinize, and examine—just like a jackal on a riverbank waiting for fish meditates, contemplates, scrutinizes, and examines. In the same way, these shaven ones, these mere ascetics, these householders, these dark-skinned ones, offspring from the feet of Brahmā, go around saying, "We are meditators, we are meditators!" With their robes draped over their shoulders, faces down, lazy by nature, they meditate, contemplate, scrutinize, and examine—just like a cat in a crevice, a drain, or a rubbish heap waiting for a mouse meditates, contemplates, scrutinizes, and examines. In the same way, these shaven ones, these mere ascetics, these householders, these dark-skinned ones, offspring from the feet of Brahmā, go around saying, "We are meditators, we are meditators!" With their robes draped over their shoulders, faces down, lazy by nature, they meditate, contemplate, scrutinize, and examine—just like a donkey, relieved of its burden, in a crevice, a drain, or a rubbish heap meditates, contemplates, scrutinizes, and examines.’"

‘‘Ye kho pana, pāpima, tena samayena manussā kālaṅkaronti yebhuyyena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti.

"And, Evil One, those people who died at that time mostly, upon the breakup of the body after death, were reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, specifically in hell."

509. ‘‘Atha kho, pāpima, kakusandho bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘anvāviṭṭhā kho, bhikkhave, brāhmaṇagahapatikā dūsinā mārena – etha, tumhe bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme akkosatha paribhāsatha rosetha vihesetha, appeva nāma tumhehi akkosiyamānānaṃ paribhāsiyamānānaṃ rosiyamānānaṃ vihesiyamānānaṃ siyā cittassa aññathattaṃ, yathā taṃ dūsī māro labhetha otāra’nti. Etha, tumhe, bhikkhave, mettāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharatha, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ mettāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyābajjhena pharitvā viharatha. Karuṇāsahagatena cetasā…pe… muditāsahagatena cetasā…pe… upekkhāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharatha, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ upekkhāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyābajjhena pharitvā viharathā’ti.

509.Then, O Wicked One, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, addressed the bhikkhus: 'bhikkhus, the brahmin householders have been possessed by Māra Dūsī, with the thought: "Come, you bhikkhus, insult, revile, anger, and trouble those virtuous ones of good character. Perhaps, when insulted, reviled, angered, and troubled by you, their minds may change, so that Māra Dūsī may find an opportunity." Come, bhikkhus, dwell pervading one direction with a mind imbued with loving-kindness, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, in all ways, pervade the entire world with a mind imbued with loving-kindness—vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility and without ill will. Dwell with a mind imbued with compassion… with a mind imbued with sympathetic joy… with a mind imbued with equanimity, pervading one direction, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, in all ways, pervade the entire world with a mind imbued with equanimity—vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility and without ill will.'

‘‘Atha kho te, pāpima, bhikkhū kakusandhena bhagavatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena evaṃ ovadiyamānā evaṃ anusāsiyamānā araññagatāpi rukkhamūlagatāpi suññāgāragatāpi mettāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā vihariṃsu, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ mettāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyābajjhena pharitvā vihariṃsu. Karuṇāsahagatena cetasā…pe… muditāsahagatena cetasā…pe… upekkhāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā vihariṃsu, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ upekkhāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyābajjhena pharitvā vihariṃsu.

Then, O Wicked One, those bhikkhus, being thus advised and instructed by the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, even dwelling in the forest, at the root of a tree, or in an empty dwelling, dwelt pervading one direction with a mind imbued with loving-kindness, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, in all ways, they dwelt pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with loving-kindness—vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility and without ill will. They dwelt with a mind imbued with compassion… they dwelt with a mind imbued with sympathetic joy… they dwelt pervading one direction with a mind imbued with equanimity, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, in all ways, they dwelt pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with equanimity—vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility and without ill will.

510. ‘‘Atha kho, pāpima, dūsissa mārassa etadahosi – ‘evampi kho ahaṃ karonto imesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ sīlavantānaṃ kalyāṇadhammānaṃ neva jānāmi āgatiṃ vā gatiṃ vā, yaṃnūnāhaṃ brāhmaṇagahapatike anvāviseyyaṃ – etha, tumhe bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme sakkarotha garuṃ karotha mānetha pūjetha, appeva nāma tumhehi sakkariyamānānaṃ garukariyamānānaṃ māniyamānānaṃ pūjiyamānānaṃ siyā cittassa aññathattaṃ, yathā taṃ dūsī māro labhetha otāra’nti. Atha kho te, pāpima, dūsī māro brāhmaṇagahapatike anvāvisi – ‘etha, tumhe bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme sakkarotha garuṃ karotha mānetha pūjetha, appeva nāma tumhehi sakkariyamānānaṃ garukariyamānānaṃ māniyamānānaṃ pūjiyamānānaṃ siyā cittassa aññathattaṃ, yathā taṃ dūsī māro labhetha otāra’nti. Atha kho te, pāpima, brāhmaṇagahapatikā anvāviṭṭhā dūsinā mārena bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme sakkaronti garuṃ karonti mānenti pūjenti.

510.Then, O Wicked One, Māra Dūsī had this thought: 'Even doing this, I do not know the coming or going of these virtuous ones of good character. Suppose I were to possess brahmins and householders, with the thought: "Come, honor, respect, esteem, and venerate these virtuous ones of good character. Perhaps, when they are honored, respected, esteemed, and venerated by you, their minds might change, so that Māra Dūsī might find an opportunity."' Then, O Wicked One, Māra Dūsī possessed the brahmin householders, with the thought: 'Come, honor, respect, esteem, and venerate these virtuous ones of good character. Perhaps, when they are honored, respected, esteemed, and venerated by you, their minds might change, so that Māra Dūsī might find an opportunity.' Then, O Wicked One, those brahmins and householders, possessed by Māra Dūsī, honored, respected, esteemed, and venerated the virtuous ones of good character.

‘‘Ye kho pana, pāpima, tena samayena manussā kālaṅkaronti yebhuyyena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti.

At that time, O Wicked One, most of the people who died, after the breakup of the body, were reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world.

511. ‘‘Atha kho, pāpima, kakusandho bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘anvāviṭṭhā kho, bhikkhave, brāhmaṇagahapatikā dūsinā mārena – etha, tumhe bhikkhū sīlavante kalyāṇadhamme sakkarotha garuṃ karotha mānetha pūjetha, appeva nāma tumhehi sakkariyamānānaṃ garukariyamānānaṃ māniyamānānaṃ pūjiyamānānaṃ siyā cittassa aññathattaṃ, yathā taṃ dūsī māro labhetha otāranti. Etha, tumhe, bhikkhave, asubhānupassino kāye viharatha, āhāre paṭikūlasaññino, sabbaloke anabhiratisaññino [anabhiratasaññīno (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)], sabbasaṅkhāresu aniccānupassino’ti.

511.Then, O Wicked One, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, addressed the bhikkhus: 'bhikkhus, the brahmin householders have been possessed by Māra Dūsī, with the thought: "Come, you bhikkhus, honor, respect, esteem, and venerate those virtuous ones of good character. Perhaps, when honored, respected, esteemed, and venerated by you, their minds may change, so that Māra Dūsī may find an opportunity." Come, bhikkhus, dwell contemplating the unattractive aspects of the body, perceiving the repulsiveness of food, perceiving non-delight in the entire world, and contemplating impermanence in all formations.'

‘‘Atha kho te, pāpima, bhikkhū kakusandhena bhagavatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena evaṃ ovadiyamānā evaṃ anusāsiyamānā araññagatāpi rukkhamūlagatāpi suññāgāragatāpi asubhānupassino kāye vihariṃsu, āhāre paṭikūlasaññino, sabbaloke anabhiratisaññino, sabbasaṅkhāresu aniccānupassino.

Then, O Wicked One, those bhikkhus, being thus advised and instructed by the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, even dwelling in the forest, at the foot of trees, or in empty dwellings, dwelt contemplating the unattractive aspects of the body, perceiving the repulsiveness of food, perceiving non-delight in the entire world, and contemplating impermanence in all formations.

512. ‘‘Atha kho, pāpima, kakusandho bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya āyasmatā vidhurena pacchāsamaṇena gāmaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Atha kho, pāpima, dūsī māro aññataraṃ kumārakaṃ [kumāraṃ (sī. pī.)] anvāvisitvā sakkharaṃ gahetvā āyasmato vidhurassa sīse pahāramadāsi; sīsaṃ vobhindi [sīsaṃ te bhindissāmīti (ka.)]. Atha kho, pāpima, āyasmā vidhuro bhinnena sīsena lohitena gaḷantena kakusandhaṃyeva bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandhi. Atha kho, pāpima, kakusandho bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho nāgāpalokitaṃ apalokesi – ‘na vāyaṃ dūsī māro mattamaññāsī’ti. Sahāpalokanāya ca pana, pāpima, dūsī māro tamhā ca ṭhānā cavi mahānirayañca upapajji.

512.Then, O Wicked One, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, having put on his lower robe and taken his bowl and outer robe in the morning, entered a village for alms, with the Venerable Vidhura as his rear attendant. Then, O Wicked One, Māra Dūsī, possessing a certain young boy, took a stone and struck the Venerable Vidhura on the head; he split his head open. Then, O Wicked One, the Venerable Vidhura, with his head split open and blood flowing, followed closely behind the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. Then, O Wicked One, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, cast an elephant's backward glance, saying: 'This Māra Dūsī does not know his measure.' And with that backward glance, O Wicked One, Māra Dūsī fell from that very spot and was reborn in the Great Hell.

‘‘Tassa kho pana, pāpima, mahānirayassa tayo nāmadheyyā honti – chaphassāyataniko itipi, saṅkusamāhato itipi, paccattavedaniyo itipi. Atha kho maṃ, pāpima, nirayapālā upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ – yadā kho te [yato te (ka.)], mārisa, saṅkunā saṅku hadaye samāgaccheyya. Atha naṃ tvaṃ jāneyyāsi – ‘vassasahassaṃ me niraye paccamānassā’ti. So kho ahaṃ, pāpima, bahūni vassāni bahūni vassasatāni bahūni vassasahassāni tasmiṃ mahāniraye apacciṃ. Dasavassasahassāni tasseva mahānirayassa ussade apacciṃ vuṭṭhānimaṃ nāma vedanaṃ vediyamāno. Tassa mayhaṃ, pāpima, evarūpo kāyo hoti, seyyathāpi manussassa. Evarūpaṃ sīsaṃ hoti, seyyathāpi macchassa.

Indeed, O Wicked One, that great hell has three names—the sixfold base of contact, pierced by spikes, and individually experienced. Then, O Wicked One, the guardians of hell approached me and said: 'When, dear sir, spike meets spike in your heart, then you will know: "I have been cooked in hell for a thousand years."' And so, O Wicked One, I was cooked in that great hell for many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years. For ten thousand years, I was cooked in the outskirts of that very hell, experiencing the feeling called 'arising of results.' At that time, O Wicked One, my body was like that of a human; my head was like that of a fish.

513.

‘‘Kīdiso nirayo āsi, yattha dūsī apaccatha;Vidhuraṃ sāvakamāsajja, kakusandhañca brāhmaṇaṃ.

What kind of hell was it where Dūsī was cooked, having assaulted the disciple Vidhura and Kakusandha, the brahmin?

‘‘Sataṃ āsi ayosaṅkū, sabbe paccattavedanā;Īdiso nirayo āsi, yattha dūsī apaccatha;Vidhuraṃ sāvakamāsajja, kakusandhañca brāhmaṇaṃ.

There were a hundred iron stakes, all individually experienced; Such was the hell where Dūsī was cooked, having assaulted the disciple Vidhura and Kakusandha, the brahmin.

‘‘Yo etamabhijānāti, bhikkhu buddhassa sāvako;Tādisaṃ bhikkhumāsajja, kaṇha dukkhaṃ nigacchasi.

Whoever, a monk, a disciple of the Buddha, understands this; Assaulting such a monk, Kaṇha, you go to suffering.

‘‘Majjhe sarassa tiṭṭhanti, vimānā kappaṭṭhāyino;Veḷuriyavaṇṇā rucirā, accimanto pabhassarā;Accharā tattha naccanti, puthu nānattavaṇṇiyo.

In the middle of the lake stand mansions that endure for an eon; lapis lazuli in color, lovely, radiant, and resplendent; There nymphs dance, diverse and of various hues.

‘‘Yo etamabhijānāti, bhikkhu buddhassa sāvako;Tādisaṃ bhikkhumāsajja, kaṇha dukkhaṃ nigacchasi.

Whoever, a monk, a disciple of the Buddha, understands this; Assaulting such a monk, Kaṇha, you go to suffering.

‘‘Yo ve buddhena codito, bhikkhu saṅghassa pekkhato;Migāramātupāsādaṃ, pādaṅguṭṭhena kampayi.

Urged by the Buddha, a monk, while the Sangha was looking on, with his big toe shook Migāramātā's palace.

‘‘Yo etamabhijānāti, bhikkhu buddhassa sāvako;Tādisaṃ bhikkhumāsajja, kaṇha dukkhaṃ nigacchasi.

Whoever, a monk, a disciple of the Buddha, understands this; Assaulting such a monk, Kaṇha, you go to suffering.

‘‘Yo vejayantaṃ pāsādaṃ, pādaṅguṭṭhena kampayi;Iddhibalenupatthaddho, saṃvejesi ca devatā.

He who shook the Vejayanta Palace with his big toe, supported by his psychic power, and stirred the devas.

‘‘Yo etamabhijānāti, bhikkhu buddhassa sāvako;Tādisaṃ bhikkhumāsajja, kaṇha dukkhaṃ nigacchasi.

Whoever, a monk, a disciple of the Buddha, understands this; Assaulting such a monk, Kaṇha, you go to suffering.

‘‘Yo vejayantapāsāde, sakkaṃ so paripucchati;Api vāsava jānāsi, taṇhākkhayavimuttiyo;Tassa sakko viyākāsi, pañhaṃ puṭṭho yathātathaṃ.

He who questioned Sakka in the Vejayanta Palace, 'O Vāsava, do you know the liberations by the destruction of craving?' Sakka then answered him truthfully when questioned.

‘‘Yo etamabhijānāti, bhikkhu buddhassa sāvako;Tādisaṃ bhikkhumāsajja, kaṇha dukkhaṃ nigacchasi.

Whoever, a monk, a disciple of the Buddha, understands this; Assaulting such a monk, Kaṇha, you go to suffering.

‘‘Yo brahmaṃ paripucchati, sudhammāyābhito sabhaṃ;Ajjāpi tyāvuso diṭṭhi, yā te diṭṭhi pure ahu;Passasi vītivattantaṃ, brahmaloke pabhassaraṃ.

He who questioned Brahmā in the Sudhammā assembly, 'Friend, is your view still the same as it was before? Do you see the resplendent Brahmā world passing away?'

‘‘Tassa brahmā viyākāsi, anupubbaṃ yathātathaṃ;Na me mārisa sā diṭṭhi, yā me diṭṭhi pure ahu.

Then Brahmā explained to him step by step, in accordance with reality: 'Good sir, that view which I formerly held is not my view now.'

‘‘Passāmi vītivattantaṃ, brahmaloke pabhassaraṃ;Sohaṃ ajja kathaṃ vajjaṃ, ahaṃ niccomhi sassato.

I see the radiant one passing away in the Brahmā world; How then could I today assert: 'I am permanent and eternal'?

‘‘Yo etamabhijānāti, bhikkhu buddhassa sāvako;Tādisaṃ bhikkhumāsajja, kaṇha dukkhaṃ nigacchasi.

Whoever, a monk, a disciple of the Buddha, understands this; Encountering such a monk, Kaṇha, you will go to suffering.

‘‘Yo mahāmeruno kūṭaṃ, vimokkhena aphassayi;Vanaṃ pubbavidehānaṃ, ye ca bhūmisayā narā.

He who touched the peak of great Meru by liberation; The forest of the ancient Videhas, and those men who lie on the ground.

‘‘Yo etamabhijānāti, bhikkhu buddhassa sāvako;Tādisaṃ bhikkhumāsajja, kaṇha dukkhaṃ nigacchasi.

Whoever, a monk, a disciple of the Buddha, understands this; Encountering such a monk, Kaṇha, you will go to suffering.

‘‘Na ve aggi cetayati [veṭhayati (sī.)], ‘ahaṃ bālaṃ ḍahāmī’ti;Bālo ca jalitaṃ aggiṃ, āsajja naṃ sa ḍayhati.

Fire does not intend: 'I will burn the fool.' But the fool, contacting blazing fire, gets burnt.

‘‘Evameva tuvaṃ māra, āsajja naṃ tathāgataṃ;Sayaṃ ḍahissasi attānaṃ, bālo aggiṃva saṃphusaṃ.

Just so, Māra, by attacking the Tathāgata, you will burn yourself, like a fool touching fire.

‘‘Apuññaṃ pasavī māro, āsajja naṃ tathāgataṃ;Kinnu maññasi pāpima, na me pāpaṃ vipaccati.

Māra, you have generated demerit by attacking the Tathāgata; What do you think, Evil One, that this demerit will not ripen for me?

‘‘Karoto cīyati pāpaṃ, cirarattāya antaka;Māra nibbinda buddhamhā, āsaṃ mākāsi bhikkhusu.

Evil accumulates for the doer, O Destroyer, for a long time; Māra, be repelled by the Buddha, do not place your hope in the bhikkhus.

‘‘Iti māraṃ atajjesi, bhikkhu bhesakaḷāvane;Tato so dummano yakkho, natatthevantaradhāyathā’’ti.

Thus the monk rebuked Māra in Bhesakaḷāvana; Then that disheartened yakkha vanished right there.

Māratajjanīyasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ.

The Māratajjanīya Sutta, the tenth, is concluded.

Cūḷayamakavaggo niṭṭhito pañcamo.

The Cūḷayamaka Vagga, the fifth, is concluded.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Sāleyya verañjaduve ca tuṭṭhi, cūḷamahādhammasamādānañca;Vīmaṃsakā kosambi ca brāhmaṇo, dūsī ca māro dasamo ca vaggo.

Sāleyya, Verañja, the two Tuṭṭhi, the Cūḷadhammasamādāna and Mahādhammasamādāna; Vīmaṃsaka, Kosambī, the Brāhmaṇa, Dūsī, and Māra—this is the tenth vagga.

Sāleyyavaggo niṭṭhito pañcamo.

The Sāleyyaka Vagga, the fifth, is concluded.

Idaṃ vaggānamuddānaṃ –

This is the summary of the vaggas:

Mūlapariyāyo ceva, sīhanādo ca uttamo;Kakaco ceva gosiṅgo, sāleyyo ca ime pañca.

The Mūlapariyāya, the Sīhanāda, the Uttama, the Kakaca, and the Gosiṅga, and the Sāleyyaka—these five.

Mūlapaṇṇāsakaṃ samattaṃ.

The Mūlapaṇṇāsaka is concluded.
