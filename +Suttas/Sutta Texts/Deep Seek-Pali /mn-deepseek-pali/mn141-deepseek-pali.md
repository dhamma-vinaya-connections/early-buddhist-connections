# 141. Anāthapiṇḍikovādasuttaṃ

## 141. The Anāthapiṇḍika Admonishment Discourse

383. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati ābādhiko hoti dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, ambho purisa, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mama vacanena bhagavato pāde sirasā vandāhi [vandāhi evañca vadehi (sabbattha) aññasuttesu pana natthi] – ‘anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So bhagavato pāde sirasā vandatī’ti. Yena cāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mama vacanena āyasmato sāriputtassa pāde sirasā vandāhi [vandāhi evañca vadehi (sabbattha) aññasuttesu pana natthi] – ‘anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So āyasmato sāriputtassa pāde sirasā vandatī’ti. Evañca vadehi – ‘sādhu kira, bhante, āyasmā sāriputto yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkamatu anukampaṃ upādāyā’’’ti.

383.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. Now at that time, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika was sick, suffering, gravely ill. Then the householder Anāthapiṇḍika addressed a certain man: “Come, good man, approach the Blessed One; having approached, in my name pay homage to the Blessed One’s feet with your head and say: ‘Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is sick, suffering, gravely ill. He pays homage to the Blessed One’s feet with his head.’ Then approach Venerable Sāriputta; having approached, in my name pay homage to Venerable Sāriputta’s feet with your head and say: ‘Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is sick, suffering, gravely ill. He pays homage to Venerable Sāriputta’s feet with his head.’ And say this: ‘It would be good, venerable sir, if Venerable Sāriputta would come to the residence of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika out of compassion.’”

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho so puriso anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so puriso bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So bhagavato pāde sirasā vandatī’’ti. Yena cāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so puriso āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So āyasmato sāriputtassa pāde sirasā vandati; evañca vadeti – ‘sādhu kira, bhante, āyasmā sāriputto yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkamatu anukampaṃ upādāyā’’’ti. Adhivāsesi kho āyasmā sāriputto tuṇhībhāvena.

“Yes, venerable sir,” replied that man to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika. Having assented, he approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, that man said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is sick, suffering, gravely ill. He pays homage to the Blessed One’s feet with his head.” Then he approached Venerable Sāriputta; having approached, he paid homage to Venerable Sāriputta and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, that man said to Venerable Sāriputta: “Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is sick, suffering, gravely ill. He pays homage to Venerable Sāriputta’s feet with his head and says this: ‘It would be good, venerable sir, if Venerable Sāriputta would come to the residence of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika out of compassion.’” Venerable Sāriputta consented in silence.

384. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya āyasmatā ānandena pacchāsamaṇena yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho āyasmā sāriputto anāthapiṇḍikaṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kacci te, gahapati, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyaṃ? Kacci te dukkhā vedanā paṭikkamanti, no abhikkamanti; paṭikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no abhikkamo’’ti?

384.Then Venerable Sāriputta, having dressed, taking his bowl and robe, accompanied by Venerable Ānanda as his attendant, went to the residence of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika. Having arrived, he sat down on the prepared seat. Seated, Venerable Sāriputta said to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika: “I hope, householder, you are comfortable, I hope you are keeping well? I hope your painful feelings are subsiding, not increasing; that their subsiding is apparent, not their increasing?”

‘‘Na me, bhante sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti; abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo. Seyyathāpi, bhante sāriputta, balavā puriso tiṇhena sikharena muddhani [muddhānaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] abhimattheyya [abhimantheyya (sī. pī.)]; evameva kho me, bhante sāriputta, adhimattā vātā muddhani [ohananti (syā. kaṃ.)] ūhananti [adhimattā vātā sīlaṃ parikantanti (sī. syā. kaṃ.)]. Na me, bhante sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti; abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo. Seyyathāpi, bhante sāriputta, balavā puriso daḷhena varattakhaṇḍena sīse sīsaveṭhaṃ dadeyya; evameva kho me, bhante sāriputta, adhimattā sīse sīsavedanā. Na me, bhante sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti; abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo. Seyyathāpi, bhante sāriputta, dakkho goghātako vā goghātakantevāsī vā tiṇhena govikantanena kucchiṃ parikanteyya; evameva kho me, bhante sāriputta, adhimattā vātā kucchiṃ parikantanti. Na me, bhante sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti; abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo. Seyyathāpi, bhante sāriputta, dve balavanto purisā dubbalataraṃ purisaṃ nānābāhāsu gahetvā aṅgārakāsuyā santāpeyyuṃ, samparitāpeyyuṃ; evameva kho me, bhante sāriputta, adhimatto kāyasmiṃ ḍāho. Na me, bhante sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti; abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo’’ti.

“Venerable Sāriputta, I cannot endure it, I cannot continue. Severe painful feelings are assailing me, not subsiding; their increase is evident, not their decrease. Venerable Sāriputta, just as if a strong man were to bore into my head with a sharp spike, so too, venerable Sāriputta, violent winds are boring into my head. I cannot endure it, I cannot continue. Severe painful feelings are assailing me, not subsiding; their increase is evident, not their decrease. Venerable Sāriputta, just as if a strong man were to tighten a tough leather strap around my head as a headband, so too, venerable Sāriputta, I have an intense pain in my head. I cannot endure it, I cannot continue. Severe painful feelings are assailing me, not subsiding; their increase is evident, not their decrease. Venerable Sāriputta, just as if a skilled butcher or his apprentice were to carve up my belly with a sharp butcher’s knife, so too, venerable Sāriputta, violent winds are carving up my belly. I cannot endure it, I cannot continue. Severe painful feelings are assailing me, not subsiding; their increase is evident, not their decrease. Venerable Sāriputta, just as if two strong men were to seize a weaker man by the arms and heat him over a pit of hot embers, burning and scorching him, so too, venerable Sāriputta, there is a fierce burning in my body. I cannot endure it, I cannot continue. Severe painful feelings are assailing me, not subsiding; their increase is evident, not their decrease.”

385. ‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na cakkhuṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me cakkhunissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

385.“Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at the eye, nor will my consciousness be dependent on the eye.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself.

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na sotaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me sotanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na ghānaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me ghānanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na jivhaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me jivhānissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na kāyaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me kāyanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na manaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me manonissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at the ear, nor will my consciousness be dependent on the ear.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at the nose, nor will my consciousness be dependent on the nose.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at the tongue, nor will my consciousness be dependent on the tongue.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at the body, nor will my consciousness be dependent on the body.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at the mind, nor will my consciousness be dependent on the mind.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself.”

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na rūpaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me rūpanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na saddaṃ upādiyissāmi…pe… na gandhaṃ upādiyissāmi… na rasaṃ upādiyissāmi… na phoṭṭhabbaṃ upādiyissāmi… na dhammaṃ upādiyissāmi na ca me dhammanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at form, nor will my consciousness be dependent on form.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at sound, nor will my consciousness be dependent on sound. I will not grasp at odor, nor will my consciousness be dependent on odor. I will not grasp at taste, nor will my consciousness be dependent on taste. I will not grasp at touch, nor will my consciousness be dependent on touch. I will not grasp at mental phenomena, nor will my consciousness be dependent on mental phenomena.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself.

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na cakkhuviññāṇaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me cakkhuviññāṇanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na sotaviññāṇaṃ upādiyissāmi… na ghānaviññāṇaṃ upādiyissāmi… na jivhāviññāṇaṃ upādiyissāmi… na kāyaviññāṇaṃ upādiyissāmi… na manoviññāṇaṃ upādiyissāmi na ca me manoviññāṇanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at eye-consciousness, nor will consciousness dependent on eye-consciousness arise in me.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at ear-consciousness, nor will consciousness dependent on ear-consciousness arise in me. I will not grasp at nose-consciousness, nor will consciousness dependent on nose-consciousness arise in me. I will not grasp at tongue-consciousness, nor will consciousness dependent on tongue-consciousness arise in me. I will not grasp at body-consciousness, nor will consciousness dependent on body-consciousness arise in me. I will not grasp at mind-consciousness, nor will consciousness dependent on mind-consciousness arise in me.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself.”

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na cakkhusamphassaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me cakkhusamphassanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na sotasamphassaṃ upādiyissāmi… na ghānasamphassaṃ upādiyissāmi… na jivhāsamphassaṃ upādiyissāmi… na kāyasamphassaṃ upādiyissāmi… na manosamphassaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me manosamphassanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at eye-contact, nor will consciousness dependent on eye-contact arise in me.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at ear-contact, nor will consciousness dependent on ear-contact arise in me. I will not grasp at nose-contact, nor will consciousness dependent on nose-contact arise in me. I will not grasp at tongue-contact, nor will consciousness dependent on tongue-contact arise in me. I will not grasp at body-contact, nor will consciousness dependent on body-contact arise in me. I will not grasp at mind-contact, nor will consciousness dependent on mind-contact arise in me.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself.”

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na cakkhusamphassajaṃ vedanaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me cakkhusamphassajāvedanānissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na sotasamphassajaṃ vedanaṃ upādiyissāmi… na ghānasamphassajaṃ vedanaṃ upādiyissāmi… na jivhāsamphassajaṃ vedanaṃ upādiyissāmi… na kāyasamphassajaṃ vedanaṃ upādiyissāmi… na manosamphassajaṃ vedanaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me manosamphassajāvedanānissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at feeling born of eye-contact, nor will my consciousness be dependent on feeling born of eye-contact.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not grasp at feeling born of ear-contact, nor will my consciousness be dependent on feeling born of ear-contact. I will not grasp at feeling born of nose-contact, nor will my consciousness be dependent on feeling born of nose-contact. I will not grasp at feeling born of tongue-contact, nor will my consciousness be dependent on feeling born of tongue-contact. I will not grasp at feeling born of body-contact, nor will my consciousness be dependent on feeling born of body-contact. I will not grasp at feeling born of mind-contact, nor will my consciousness be dependent on feeling born of mind-contact.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself.”

386. ‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na pathavīdhātuṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me pathavīdhātunissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na āpodhātuṃ upādiyissāmi… na tejodhātuṃ upādiyissāmi… na vāyodhātuṃ upādiyissāmi… na ākāsadhātuṃ upādiyissāmi… na viññāṇadhātuṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me viññāṇadhātunissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

386."Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: 'I will not cling to the earth element, and consciousness will not be dependent on the earth element for me.' Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: 'I will not cling to the water element... I will not cling to the fire element... I will not cling to the air element... I will not cling to the space element... I will not cling to the consciousness element, and consciousness will not be dependent on the consciousness element for me.' Thus, householder, you should train yourself."

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na rūpaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me rūpanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na vedanaṃ upādiyissāmi… na saññaṃ upādiyissāmi… na saṅkhāre upādiyissāmi… na viññāṇaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me viññāṇanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

"Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not cling to form, and consciousness will not be dependent on form for me.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not cling to feeling… I will not cling to perception… I will not cling to formations… I will not cling to consciousness, and consciousness will not be dependent on consciousness for me.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself."

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me ākāsānañcāyatananissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ upādiyissāmi… na ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ upādiyissāmi… na nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ upādiyissāmi na ca me nevasaññānāsaññāyatananissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ.

"Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not cling to the dimension of infinite space, and my consciousness will not be dependent on the dimension of infinite space.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not cling to the dimension of infinite consciousness… I will not cling to the dimension of nothingness… I will not cling to the dimension of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, and my consciousness will not be dependent on the dimension of neither-perception-nor-non-perception.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself."

‘‘Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na idhalokaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me idhalokanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘na paralokaṃ upādiyissāmi, na ca me paralokanissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabbaṃ. Tasmātiha te, gahapati, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘yampi me diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anupariyesitaṃ anucaritaṃ manasā tampi na upādiyissāmi, na ca me taṃnissitaṃ viññāṇaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, gahapati, sikkhitabba’’nti.

"Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not cling to this world, and my consciousness will not be based on this world.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘I will not cling to the other world, and my consciousness will not be based on the other world.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself. Therefore, householder, you should train yourself thus: ‘Whatever is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, examined, and pondered over by the mind—I will not cling to that, and my consciousness will not be based on that.’ Thus, householder, you should train yourself."

387. Evaṃ vutte, anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati parodi, assūni pavattesi. Atha kho āyasmā ānando anāthapiṇḍikaṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘olīyasi kho tvaṃ, gahapati, saṃsīdasi kho tvaṃ, gahapatī’’ti? ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhante ānanda, olīyāmi, napi saṃsīdāmi; api ca me dīgharattaṃ satthā payirupāsito manobhāvanīyā ca bhikkhū; na ca me evarūpī dhammī kathā sutapubbā’’ti. ‘‘Na kho, gahapati, gihīnaṃ odātavasanānaṃ evarūpī dhammī kathā paṭibhāti; pabbajitānaṃ kho, gahapati, evarūpī dhammī kathā paṭibhātī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, bhante sāriputta, gihīnampi odātavasanānaṃ evarūpī dhammī kathā paṭibhātu. Santi hi, bhante, kulaputtā apparajakkhajātikā, assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti; bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro’’ti.

387.When this was said, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika wept and shed tears. Then Venerable Ānanda said to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika, "Are you drooping, householder? Are you dejected, householder?" "I am not drooping, Venerable Ānanda, nor am I dejected; but for a long time I have attended on the Teacher and the bhikkhus who inspire the mind, yet never before have I heard such a Dhamma talk." "Such Dhamma talks, householder, do not occur to laypeople clad in white; they are apparent to those gone forth." "Well then, Venerable Sāriputta, let such Dhamma talks be apparent to laypeople clad in white. For there are, Venerable Sir, young men of good family with little dust in their eyes who decline through not hearing the Dhamma; there will be those who understand the Dhamma."

Atha kho āyasmā ca sāriputto āyasmā ca ānando anāthapiṇḍikaṃ gahapatiṃ iminā ovādena ovaditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamiṃsu. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati, acirapakkante āyasmante ca sāriputte āyasmante ca ānande, kālamakāsi tusitaṃ kāyaṃ upapajji. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko devaputto abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇo kevalakappaṃ jetavanaṃ obhāsetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho anāthapiṇḍiko devaputto bhagavantaṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

Then the Venerable Sāriputta and the Venerable Ānanda, having given this advice to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika, rose from their seats and departed. Then, not long after the Venerable Sāriputta and the Venerable Ānanda had left, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika passed away and was reborn in the Tusita heaven. Then Anāthapiṇḍika, the young god, when the night was far advanced, illuminating the entire Jetavana with surpassing radiance, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, Anāthapiṇḍika, the young god, addressed the Blessed One in verses:

‘‘Idañhi taṃ jetavanaṃ, isisaṅghanisevitaṃ;Āvutthaṃ dhammarājena, pītisañjananaṃ mama.

"Indeed, this is that Jeta's Grove, frequented by the community of bhikkhus, inhabited by the King of Dhamma, it generates joy in me."

‘‘Kammaṃ vijjā ca dhammo ca, sīlaṃ jīvitamuttamaṃ;Etena maccā sujjhanti, na gottena dhanena vā.

"Deeds, knowledge, Dhamma, and virtue—this is the supreme life; By these mortals are purified, not by lineage or wealth."

‘‘Tasmā hi paṇḍito poso, sampassaṃ atthamattano;Yoniso vicine dhammaṃ, evaṃ tattha visujjhati.

"Therefore, a wise person, seeing their own benefit, should carefully investigate the Dhamma. Thus, they are purified therein."

‘‘Sāriputtova paññāya, sīlena upasamena;Yopi pāraṅgato bhikkhu, etāvaparamo siyā’’ti.

"Sāriputta is supreme in wisdom, virtue, and calm; any monk who has reached the far shore would be no more than his equal."

Idamavoca anāthapiṇḍiko devaputto. Samanuñño satthā ahosi. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko devaputto – ‘‘samanuñño me satthā’’ti bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tatthevantaradhāyi.

This Anāthapiṇḍika-deva said. The Teacher approved. Then Anāthapiṇḍika-deva, thinking, "The Teacher approves of me," paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and vanished right there.

388. Atha kho bhagavā tassā rattiyā accayena bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘imaṃ, bhikkhave, rattiṃ aññataro devaputto abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇo kevalakappaṃ jetavanaṃ obhāsetvā yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho so devaputto maṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

388.Then, when the night had passed, the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "Last night, bhikkhus, a certain deity, when the night was far advanced, with surpassing radiance illuminating the entire Jeta Grove, approached me. Having approached, he paid homage to me and stood to one side. Standing to one side, that deity addressed me in verses:

‘‘Idañhi taṃ jetavanaṃ, isisaṅghanisevitaṃ;Āvutthaṃ dhammarājena, pītisañjananaṃ mama.

"Indeed, this is that Jeta's Grove, frequented by the community of bhikkhus, inhabited by the King of Dhamma, it generates joy in me."

‘‘Kammaṃ vijjā ca dhammo ca, sīlaṃ jīvitamuttamaṃ;Etena maccā sujjhanti, na gottena dhanena vā.

"Deeds, knowledge, Dhamma, and virtue—this is the supreme life; By these mortals are purified, not by lineage or wealth."

‘‘Tasmā hi paṇḍito poso, sampassaṃ atthamattano;Yoniso vicine dhammaṃ, evaṃ tattha visujjhati.

"Therefore, a wise person, seeing their own benefit, should carefully investigate the Dhamma; thus, they are purified therein."

‘‘Sāriputtova paññāya, sīlena upasamena;Yopi pāraṅgato bhikkhu, etāvaparamo siyā’’ti.

"In wisdom, virtue, and tranquility, Sāriputta is supreme; Even a monk who has reached the far shore would only be his equal."

‘‘Idamavoca, bhikkhave, so devaputto. ‘Samanuñño me satthā’ti maṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tatthevantaradhāyī’’ti.

"This is what that young deva said, bhikkhus. "The Teacher approves of me," then, having paid homage to me and circumambulated me, he vanished right there."

Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘so hi nūna so, bhante, anāthapiṇḍiko devaputto bhavissati. Anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati āyasmante sāriputte abhippasanno ahosī’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu, sādhu, ānanda! Yāvatakaṃ kho, ānanda, takkāya pattabbaṃ, anuppattaṃ taṃ tayā. Anāthapiṇḍiko so, ānanda, devaputto’’ti.

When this was said, the Venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One: "Surely, venerable sir, that must be Anāthapiṇḍika, the deva-son. For the householder Anāthapiṇḍika had deep faith in the Venerable Sāriputta." "Good, good, Ānanda! As far as can be attained by reasoning, Ānanda, you have attained it. That was indeed Anāthapiṇḍika, the deva-son."

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā ānando bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

Thus spoke the Blessed One. The Venerable Ānanda, pleased, rejoiced at the Blessed One's words.

Anāthapiṇḍikovādasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The Discourse on the Advice to Anāthapiṇḍika (1).
