# 8. Sallekhasuttaṃ

## 8. The Discourse on Effacement

81. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Atha kho āyasmā mahācundo sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā mahācundo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yā imā, bhante, anekavihitā diṭṭhiyo loke uppajjanti – attavādapaṭisaṃyuttā vā lokavādapaṭisaṃyuttā vā – ādimeva nu kho, bhante, bhikkhuno manasikaroto evametāsaṃ diṭṭhīnaṃ pahānaṃ hoti, evametāsaṃ diṭṭhīnaṃ paṭinissaggo hotī’’ti?

81.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Then, in the evening, the Venerable Mahācunda, having emerged from seclusion, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the Venerable Mahācunda said to the Blessed One: “Bhante, these various kinds of views that arise in the world—connected with theories of self or connected with theories of the world—is it, Bhante, by attending from the very start that a bhikkhu abandons these views, relinquishes these views?”

82. ‘‘Yā imā, cunda, anekavihitā diṭṭhiyo loke uppajjanti – attavādapaṭisaṃyuttā vā lokavādapaṭisaṃyuttā vā – yattha cetā diṭṭhiyo uppajjanti yattha ca anusenti yattha ca samudācaranti taṃ ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na me so attā’ti – evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññā passato evametāsaṃ diṭṭhīnaṃ pahānaṃ hoti, evametāsaṃ diṭṭhīnaṃ paṭinissaggo hoti.

82.“Cunda, these various kinds of views that arise in the world—connected with theories of self or connected with theories of the world—where these views arise, where they lie latent, and where they come into play, one who sees this as it really is with right wisdom: ‘This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self,’ thus abandons these views, thus relinquishes these views.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārā ete ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, having quite secluded himself from sensual pleasures, secluded from unwholesome states, enters and dwells in the first jhana, which is accompanied by applied thought and sustained thought, with rapture and happiness born of seclusion. He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called dwellings of bliss here and now in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā ajjhattaṃ sampasādanaṃ cetaso ekodibhāvaṃ avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhijaṃ pītisukhaṃ dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārā ete ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, with the stilling of applied thought and sustained thought, with the internal pacification and unification of mind, enters and dwells in the second jhana, which is without applied thought and sustained thought, and has rapture and happiness born of samādhi. He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called dwellings of bliss here and now in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu pītiyā ca virāgā upekkhako ca vihareyya, sato ca sampajāno sukhañca kāyena paṭisaṃvedeyya, yaṃ taṃ ariyā ācikkhanti – ‘upekkhako satimā sukhavihārī’ti tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārā ete ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, with the fading away of rapture, he dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending, and experiences happiness with the body, of which the noble ones declare: ‘He dwells happily with equanimity and mindfulness,’ and enters and dwells in the third jhana. He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called dwellings of bliss here and now in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā pubbeva somanassadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamā adukkhamasuṃ upekkhāsatipārisuddhiṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārā ete ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, with the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and with the previous disappearance of joy and sorrow, enters and dwells in the fourth jhana, which is neither painful nor pleasant, and has purity of mindfulness due to equanimity. He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called dwellings of bliss here and now in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu sabbaso rūpasaññānaṃ samatikkamā, paṭighasaññānaṃ atthaṅgamā, nānattasaññānaṃ amanasikārā, ‘ananto ākāso’ti ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Santā ete vihārā ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, with the complete transcending of perceptions of form, with the disappearance of perceptions of sensory impact, and with non-attention to perceptions of diversity, enters and dwells in the base of infinite space, thinking: ‘Space is infinite.’ He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called peaceful dwellings in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu sabbaso ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘anantaṃ viññāṇa’nti viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Santā ete vihārā ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, with the complete transcending of the base of infinite space, enters and dwells in the base of infinite consciousness, thinking: ‘Consciousness is infinite.’ He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called peaceful dwellings in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu sabbaso viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘natthi kiñcī’ti ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Santā ete vihārā ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, with the complete transcending of the base of infinite consciousness, enters and dwells in the base of nothingness, thinking: ‘There is nothing.’ He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called peaceful dwellings in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ idhekacco bhikkhu sabbaso ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ samatikkamma nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya. Tassa evamassa – ‘sallekhena viharāmī’ti. Na kho panete, cunda, ariyassa vinaye sallekhā vuccanti. Santā ete vihārā ariyassa vinaye vuccanti.

“It is possible, Cunda, that a certain bhikkhu, with the complete transcending of the base of nothingness, enters and dwells in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. He might think: ‘I am dwelling by effacement.’ But these, Cunda, are not called effacements in the Noble One’s Discipline. These are called peaceful dwellings in the Noble One’s Discipline.”

83. ‘‘Idha kho pana vo, cunda, sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare vihiṃsakā bhavissanti, mayamettha avihiṃsakā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare pāṇātipātī bhavissanti, mayamettha pāṇātipātā paṭiviratā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare adinnādāyī bhavissanti, mayamettha adinnādānā paṭiviratā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare abrahmacārī bhavissanti, mayamettha brahmacārī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare musāvādī bhavissanti, mayamettha musāvādā paṭiviratā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare pisuṇavācā [pisuṇā vācā (sī. pī.)] bhavissanti, mayamettha pisuṇāya vācāya paṭiviratā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare pharusavācā [pharusā vācā (sī. pī.)] bhavissanti, mayamettha pharusāya vācāya paṭiviratā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare samphappalāpī bhavissanti, mayamettha samphappalāpā paṭiviratā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare abhijjhālū bhavissanti, mayamettha anabhijjhālū bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare byāpannacittā bhavissanti, mayamettha abyāpannacittā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchādiṭṭhī bhavissanti, mayamettha sammādiṭṭhī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāsaṅkappā bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāsaṅkappā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāvācā bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāvācā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchākammantā bhavissanti, mayamettha sammākammantā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāājīvā bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāājīvā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāvāyāmā bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāvāyāmā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāsatī bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāsatī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāsamādhi bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāsamādhī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāñāṇī bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāñāṇī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare micchāvimuttī bhavissanti, mayamettha sammāvimuttī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo.

83. “Here, Cunda, purification should be practiced. ‘Others will be harmful, but here we shall be harmless’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will kill living beings, but here we shall abstain from killing living beings’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will take what is not given, but here we shall abstain from taking what is not given’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will be unchaste, but here we shall be chaste’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will speak falsely, but here we shall abstain from false speech’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will speak maliciously, but here we shall abstain from malicious speech’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will speak harshly, but here we shall abstain from harsh speech’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will engage in idle chatter, but here we shall abstain from idle chatter’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will be covetous, but here we shall be without covetousness’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have corrupted minds, but here we shall have uncorrupted minds’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong view, but here we shall have right view’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong resolve, but here we shall have right resolve’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong speech, but here we shall have right speech’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong action, but here we shall have right action’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong livelihood, but here we shall have right livelihood’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong effort, but here we shall have right effort’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong mindfulness, but here we shall have right mindfulness’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong samādhi, but here we shall have right samādhi’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong knowledge, but here we shall have right knowledge’—purification should be practiced. ‘Others will have wrong liberation, but here we shall have right liberation’—purification should be practiced.”

‘‘‘Pare thīnamiddhapariyuṭṭhitā bhavissanti, mayamettha vigatathīnamiddhā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare uddhatā bhavissanti, mayamettha anuddhatā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare vicikicchī [vecikicchī (sī. pī. ka.)] bhavissanti, mayamettha tiṇṇavicikicchā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare kodhanā bhavissanti, mayamettha akkodhanā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare upanāhī bhavissanti, mayamettha anupanāhī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare makkhī bhavissanti, mayamettha amakkhī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare paḷāsī bhavissanti, mayamettha apaḷāsī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare issukī bhavissanti, mayamettha anissukī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare maccharī bhavissanti, mayamettha amaccharī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare saṭhā bhavissanti, mayamettha asaṭhā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare māyāvī bhavissanti, mayamettha amāyāvī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare thaddhā bhavissanti, mayamettha atthaddhā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare atimānī bhavissanti, mayamettha anatimānī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare dubbacā bhavissanti, mayamettha suvacā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare pāpamittā bhavissanti, mayamettha kalyāṇamittā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare pamattā bhavissanti, mayamettha appamattā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare assaddhā bhavissanti, mayamettha saddhā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare ahirikā bhavissanti, mayamettha hirimanā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare anottāpī [anottappī (ka.)] bhavissanti, mayamettha ottāpī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare appassutā bhavissanti, mayamettha bahussutā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare kusītā bhavissanti, mayamettha āraddhavīriyā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare muṭṭhassatī bhavissanti, mayamettha upaṭṭhitassatī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare duppaññā bhavissanti, mayamettha paññāsampannā bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo. ‘Pare sandiṭṭhiparāmāsī ādhānaggāhī duppaṭinissaggī bhavissanti, mayamettha asandiṭṭhiparāmāsī anādhānaggāhī suppaṭinissaggī bhavissāmā’ti sallekho karaṇīyo.

“‘Others will be overcome by sloth and torpor, but here we will be free from sloth and torpor.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be restless, but here we will be without restlessness.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be of wavering faith, but here we will have passed beyond wavering.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be angry, but here we will be without anger.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be resentful, but here we will be without resentment.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be hypocritical, but here we will be without hypocrisy.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be spiteful, but here we will be without spite.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be envious, but here we will be without envy.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be stingy, but here we will be without stinginess.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be deceitful, but here we will be without deceit.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be deceptive, but here we will be without deception.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be stubborn, but here we will be without stubbornness.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be arrogant, but here we will be without arrogance.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be difficult to admonish, but here we will be easy to admonish.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will have bad friends, but here we will have good friends.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be heedless, but here we will be heedful.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be faithless, but here we will have faith.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be shameless, but here we will have a sense of shame.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be unconscientious, but here we will be conscientious.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be unlearned, but here we will be learned.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be lazy, but here we will be energetic.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will have unestablished mindfulness, but here we will have established mindfulness.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be unwise, but here we will be endowed with wisdom.’ This reflection should be practiced. ‘Others will be attached to views, grasping, and tenacious, but here we will be unattached to views, not grasping, and letting go.’ This reflection should be practiced.

84. ‘‘Cittuppādampi kho ahaṃ, cunda, kusalesu dhammesu bahukāraṃ [bahūpakāraṃ (ka.)] vadāmi, ko pana vādo kāyena vācāya anuvidhīyanāsu! Tasmātiha, cunda, ‘pare vihiṃsakā bhavissanti, mayamettha avihiṃsakā bhavissāmā’ti cittaṃ uppādetabbaṃ. ‘Pare pāṇātipātī bhavissanti, mayamettha pāṇātipātā paṭiviratā bhavissāmā’ti cittaṃ uppādetabbaṃ…‘pare sandiṭṭhiparāmāsī ādhānaggāhī duppaṭinissaggī bhavissanti, mayamettha asandiṭṭhiparāmāsī anādhānaggāhī suppaṭinissaggī bhavissāmā’ti cittaṃ uppādetabbaṃ.

84.“Cunda, I say that even the arising of a thought is of great help in regard to wholesome states; what then of following through with body and speech! Therefore, Cunda, you should generate the thought thus: ‘Others will be harmful, but we here shall be harmless.’ You should generate the thought: ‘Others will kill living beings, but we here shall abstain from killing living beings.’… You should generate the thought: ‘Others will be clinging to their own views, holding on to them tenaciously, and difficult to relinquish, but we here shall be not clinging to our own views, not holding on to them tenaciously, and easy to relinquish.’”

85. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, cunda, visamo maggo assa, tassa [maggo tassāssa (sī. syā. pī.)] añño samo maggo parikkamanāya; seyyathā vā pana, cunda, visamaṃ titthaṃ assa, tassa aññaṃ samaṃ titthaṃ parikkamanāya; evameva kho, cunda, vihiṃsakassa purisapuggalassa avihiṃsā hoti parikkamanāya, pāṇātipātissa purisapuggalassa pāṇātipātā veramaṇī hoti parikkamanāya, adinnādāyissa purisapuggalassa adinnādānā veramaṇī hoti parikkamanāya, abrahmacārissa purisapuggalassa abrahmacariyā veramaṇī hoti parikkamanāya, musāvādissa purisapuggalassa musāvādā veramaṇī hoti parikkamanāya, pisuṇavācassa purisapuggalassa pisuṇāya vācāya veramaṇī hoti parikkamanāya, pharusavācassa purisapuggalassa pharusāya vācāya veramaṇī hoti parikkamanāya, samphappalāpissa purisapuggalassa samphappalāpā veramaṇī hoti parikkamanāya, abhijjhālussa purisapuggalassa anabhijjhā hoti parikkamanāya, byāpannacittassa purisapuggalassa abyāpādo hoti parikkamanāya, micchādiṭṭhissa purisapuggalassa sammādiṭṭhi hoti parikkamanāya, micchāsaṅkappassa purisapuggalassa sammāsaṅkappo hoti parikkamanāya, micchāvācassa purisapuggalassa sammāvācā hoti parikkamanāya, micchākammantassa purisapuggalassa sammākammanto hoti parikkamanāya, micchāājīvassa purisapuggalassa sammāājīvo hoti parikkamanāya, micchāvāyāmassa purisapuggalassa sammāvāyāmo hoti parikkamanāya, micchāsatissa purisapuggalassa sammāsati hoti parikkamanāya, micchāsamādhissa purisapuggalassa sammāsamādhi hoti parikkamanāya, micchāñāṇissa purisapuggalassa sammāñāṇaṃ hoti parikkamanāya, micchāvimuttissa purisapuggalassa sammāvimutti hoti parikkamanāya.

85.“Just as, Cunda, there might be a rough path, and nearby a smooth path for traversing it; or just as there might be a rough ford, and nearby a smooth ford for traversing it—so too, Cunda, for a person who is harmful, harmlessness is the way to traverse; for a person who kills living beings, abstaining from killing living beings is the way to traverse; for a person who takes what is not given, abstaining from taking what is not given is the way to traverse; for a person who engages in unchastity, abstaining from unchastity is the way to traverse; for a person who speaks falsely, abstaining from false speech is the way to traverse; for a person who speaks slanderously, abstaining from slanderous speech is the way to traverse; for a person who speaks harshly, abstaining from harsh speech is the way to traverse; for a person who engages in idle chatter, abstaining from idle chatter is the way to traverse; for a person who is covetous, non-covetousness is the way to traverse; for a person who has a mind of ill will, non-ill will is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong view, right view is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong thought, right thought is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong speech, right speech is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong action, right action is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong livelihood, right livelihood is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong effort, right effort is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong mindfulness, right mindfulness is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong samādhi, right samādhi is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong knowledge, right knowledge is the way to traverse; for a person who has wrong liberation, right liberation is the way to traverse.”

‘‘Thīnamiddhapariyuṭṭhitassa purisapuggalassa vigatathinamiddhatā hoti parikkamanāya, uddhatassa purisapuggalassa anuddhaccaṃ hoti parikkamanāya, vicikicchissa purisapuggalassa tiṇṇavicikicchatā hoti parikkamanāya, kodhanassa purisapuggalassa akkodho hoti parikkamanāya, upanāhissa purisapuggalassa anupanāho hoti parikkamanāya, makkhissa purisapuggalassa amakkho hoti parikkamanāya, paḷāsissa purisapuggalassa apaḷāso hoti parikkamanāya, issukissa purisapuggalassa anissukitā hoti parikkamanāya, maccharissa purisapuggalassa amacchariyaṃ hoti parikkamanāya, saṭhassa purisapuggalassa asāṭheyyaṃ hoti parikkamanāya, māyāvissa purisapuggalassa amāyā [amāyāvitā (ka.)] hoti parikkamanāya, thaddhassa purisapuggalassa atthaddhiyaṃ hoti parikkamanāya, atimānissa purisapuggalassa anatimāno hoti parikkamanāya, dubbacassa purisapuggalassa sovacassatā hoti parikkamanāya, pāpamittassa purisapuggalassa kalyāṇamittatā hoti parikkamanāya, pamattassa purisapuggalassa appamādo hoti parikkamanāya, assaddhassa purisapuggalassa saddhā hoti parikkamanāya, ahirikassa purisapuggalassa hirī hoti parikkamanāya, anottāpissa purisapuggalassa ottappaṃ hoti parikkamanāya, appassutassa purisapuggalassa bāhusaccaṃ hoti parikkamanāya, kusītassa purisapuggalassa vīriyārambho hoti parikkamanāya, muṭṭhassatissa purisapuggalassa upaṭṭhitassatitā hoti parikkamanāya, duppaññassa purisapuggalassa paññāsampadā hoti parikkamanāya, sandiṭṭhiparāmāsi-ādhānaggāhi-duppaṭinissaggissa purisapuggalassa asandiṭṭhiparāmāsianādhānaggāhi-suppaṭinissaggitā hoti parikkamanāya.

“For a person overcome by sloth and torpor, freedom from sloth and torpor is the way to traverse. For a restless person, non-restlessness is the way to traverse. For a doubtful person, transcendence of doubt is the way to traverse. For an angry person, non-anger is the way to traverse. For a resentful person, non-resentment is the way to traverse. For a denigrating person, non-denigration is the way to traverse. For a rivalrous person, non-rivalrousness is the way to traverse. For a jealous person, non-jealousy is the way to traverse. For a stingy person, non-stinginess is the way to traverse. For a crafty person, non-craftiness is the way to traverse. For a fraudulent person, non-fraud is the way to traverse. For an obstinate person, non-obstinacy is the way to traverse. For an arrogant person, non-arrogance is the way to traverse. For a difficult-to-admonish person, amenability to admonishment is the way to traverse. For a person with bad friends, good friendship is the way to traverse. For a heedless person, heedfulness is the way to traverse. For a faithless person, faith is the way to traverse. For a shameless person, shame is the way to traverse. For a reckless person, conscientiousness is the way to traverse. For an unlearned person, great learning is the way to traverse. For a lazy person, exertion of energy is the way to traverse. For a forgetful person, established mindfulness is the way to traverse. For an unwise person, accomplishment in wisdom is the way to traverse. For a person clinging to views, holding on tenaciously, and difficult to relinquish, non-clinging to views, non-tenacity, and ease of relinquishment is the way to traverse.”

86. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, cunda, ye keci akusalā dhammā sabbe te adhobhāgaṅgamanīyā [adhobhāvaṅgamanīyā (sī. syā. pī.)], ye keci kusalā dhammā sabbe te uparibhāgaṅgamanīyā [uparibhāvaṅgamanīyā (sī. syā. pī.)], evameva kho, cunda, vihiṃsakassa purisapuggalassa avihiṃsā hoti uparibhāgāya [uparibhāvāya (sī. syā. ka.)], pāṇātipātissa purisapuggalassa pāṇātipātā veramaṇī hoti uparibhāgāya…pe… sandiṭṭhiparāmāsi-ādhānaggāhi-duppaṭinissaggissa purisapuggalassa asandiṭṭhiparāmāsi-anādhānaggāhi-suppaṭinissaggitā hoti uparibhāgāya.

86.“Just as, Cunda, whatever unwholesome qualities there are, all of them lead downwards; whatever wholesome qualities there are, all of them lead upwards. So too, Cunda, for a person who is harmful, harmlessness leads upwards; for a person who kills living beings, abstaining from killing living beings leads upwards… For a person clinging to views, holding on tenaciously, and difficult to relinquish, non-clinging to views, non-tenacity, and ease of relinquishment leads upwards.”

87. ‘‘So vata, cunda, attanā palipapalipanno paraṃ palipapalipannaṃ uddharissatīti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. So vata, cunda, attanā apalipapalipanno paraṃ palipapalipannaṃ uddharissatīti ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. So vata, cunda, attanā adanto avinīto aparinibbuto paraṃ damessati vinessati parinibbāpessatīti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. So vata, cunda, attanā danto vinīto parinibbuto paraṃ damessati vinessati parinibbāpessatīti ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Evameva kho, cunda, vihiṃsakassa purisapuggalassa avihiṃsā hoti parinibbānāya, pāṇātipātissa purisapuggalassa pāṇātipātā veramaṇī hoti parinibbānāya. Adinnādāyissa purisapuggalassa adinnādānā veramaṇī hoti parinibbānāya. Abrahmacārissa purisapuggalassa abrahmacariyā veramaṇī hoti parinibbānāya. Musāvādissa purisapuggalassa musāvādā veramaṇī hoti parinibbānāya. Pisuṇavācassa purisapuggalassa pisuṇāya vācāya veramaṇī hoti parinibbānāya. Pharusavācassa purisapuggalassa pharusāya vācāya veramaṇī hoti parinibbānāya. Samphappalāpissa purisapuggalassa samphappalāpā veramaṇī hoti parinibbānāya. Abhijjhālussa purisapuggalassa anabhijjhā hoti parinibbānāya. Byāpannacittassa purisapuggalassa abyāpādo hoti parinibbānāya. Micchādiṭṭhissa purisapuggalassa sammādiṭṭhi hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāsaṅkappassa purisapuggalassa sammāsaṅkappo hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāvācassa purisapuggalassa sammāvācā hoti parinibbānāya. Micchākammantassa purisapuggalassa sammākammanto hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāājīvassa purisapuggalassa sammāājīvo hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāvāyāmassa purisapuggalassa sammāvāyāmo hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāsatissa purisapuggalassa sammāsati hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāsamādhissa purisapuggalassa sammāsamādhi hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāñāṇissa purisapuggalassa sammāñāṇaṃ hoti parinibbānāya. Micchāvimuttissa purisapuggalassa sammāvimutti hoti parinibbānāya.

87.“Indeed, Cunda, it is impossible for one who is themselves immersed in the mire to extricate another who is immersed in the mire. But it is possible for one who is themselves free from the mire to extricate another who is immersed in the mire. Indeed, Cunda, it is impossible for one who is untamed, undisciplined, and unliberated to tame, discipline, and liberate another. But it is possible for one who is tamed, disciplined, and liberated to tame, discipline, and liberate another. In the same way, Cunda, for a person who is harmful, harmlessness leads to liberation; for a person who kills living beings, abstaining from killing living beings leads to liberation; for a person who takes what is not given, abstaining from taking what is not given leads to liberation; for a person who engages in unchastity, abstaining from unchastity leads to liberation; for a person who speaks falsely, abstaining from false speech leads to liberation; for a person who speaks slanderously, abstaining from slanderous speech leads to liberation; for a person who speaks harshly, abstaining from harsh speech leads to liberation; for a person who engages in idle chatter, abstaining from idle chatter leads to liberation; for a person who is covetous, non-covetousness leads to liberation; for a person who has a mind of ill will, non-ill will leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong view, right view leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong thought, right thought leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong speech, right speech leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong action, right action leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong livelihood, right livelihood leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong effort, right effort leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong mindfulness, right mindfulness leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong samādhi, right samādhi leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong knowledge, right knowledge leads to liberation; for a person who has wrong liberation, right liberation leads to liberation.”

‘‘Thīnamiddhapariyuṭṭhitassa purisapuggalassa vigatathinamiddhatā hoti parinibbānāya. Uddhatassa purisapuggalassa anuddhaccaṃ hoti parinibbānāya. Vicikicchissa purisapuggalassa tiṇṇavicikicchatā hoti parinibbānāya. Kodhanassa purisapuggalassa akkodho hoti parinibbānāya. Upanāhissa purisapuggalassa anupanāho hoti parinibbānāya. Makkhissa purisapuggalassa amakkho hoti parinibbānāya. Paḷāsissa purisapuggalassa apaḷāso hoti parinibbānāya. Issukissa purisapuggalassa anissukitā hoti parinibbānāya. Maccharissa purisapuggalassa amacchariyaṃ hoti parinibbānāya. Saṭhassa purisapuggalassa asāṭheyyaṃ hoti parinibbānāya. Māyāvissa purisapuggalassa amāyā hoti parinibbānāya. Thaddhassa purisapuggalassa atthaddhiyaṃ hoti parinibbānāya. Atimānissa purisapuggalassa anatimāno hoti parinibbānāya. Dubbacassa purisapuggalassa sovacassatā hoti parinibbānāya. Pāpamittassa purisapuggalassa kalyāṇamittatā hoti parinibbānāya. Pamattassa purisapuggalassa appamādo hoti parinibbānāya. Assaddhassa purisapuggalassa saddhā hoti parinibbānāya. Ahirikassa purisapuggalassa hirī hoti parinibbānāya. Anottāpissa purisapuggalassa ottappaṃ hoti parinibbānāya. Appassutassa purisapuggalassa bāhusaccaṃ hoti parinibbānāya. Kusītassa purisapuggalassa vīriyārambho hoti parinibbānāya. Muṭṭhassatissa purisapuggalassa upaṭṭhitassatitā hoti parinibbānāya. Duppaññassa purisapuggalassa paññāsampadā hoti parinibbānāya. Sandiṭṭhiparāmāsi-ādhānaggāhi-duppaṭinissaggissa purisapuggalassa asandiṭṭhiparāmāsi-anādhānaggāhi-suppaṭinissaggitā hoti parinibbānāya.

For a person overcome by sloth and torpor, the absence of sloth and torpor leads to Nibbāna. For a restless person, non-restlessness leads to Nibbāna. For a doubting person, the overcoming of doubt leads to Nibbāna. For an angry person, non-anger leads to Nibbāna. For a resentful person, the abandoning of resentment leads to Nibbāna. For a deceitful person, honesty leads to Nibbāna. For a hypocritical person, lack of hypocrisy leads to Nibbāna. For an envious person, non-envy leads to Nibbāna. For a stingy person, generosity leads to Nibbāna. For a crafty person, artlessness leads to Nibbāna. For a fraudulent person, non-fraudulence leads to Nibbāna. For a stubborn person, non-stubbornness leads to Nibbāna. For a conceited person, humility leads to Nibbāna. For an unruly person, tractability leads to Nibbāna. For a person with evil companions, good companionship leads to Nibbāna. For a heedless person, heedfulness leads to Nibbāna. For a person without faith, faith leads to Nibbāna. For a shameless person, moral shame leads to Nibbāna. For a person without conscience, conscience leads to Nibbāna. For one of limited learning, extensive learning leads to Nibbāna. For a lazy person, energetic exertion leads to Nibbāna. For a person of muddled mindfulness, the establishing of mindfulness leads to Nibbāna. For a person of poor wisdom, the attainment of wisdom leads to Nibbāna. For a person attached to views, clinging to views, and difficult to dissuade, non-attachment to views, non-clinging, and ease of dissuasion lead to Nibbāna.

88. ‘‘Iti kho, cunda, desito mayā sallekhapariyāyo, desito cittuppādapariyāyo, desito parikkamanapariyāyo, desito uparibhāgapariyāyo, desito parinibbānapariyāyo. Yaṃ kho, cunda, satthārā karaṇīyaṃ sāvakānaṃ hitesinā anukampakena anukampaṃ upādāya, kataṃ vo taṃ mayā. ‘Etāni, cunda, rukkhamūlāni, etāni suññāgārāni, jhāyatha, cunda, mā pamādattha, mā pacchāvippaṭisārino ahuvattha’ – ayaṃ kho amhākaṃ anusāsanī’’ti.

88.“Thus, Cunda, I have taught the mode of purification, the mode of the arising of thought, the mode of traversing, the mode of the higher stage, the mode of final Nibbāna. Whatever, Cunda, a teacher should do for his disciples, as a well-wisher, out of compassion—that I have done for you. ‘These, Cunda, are the roots of trees, these are empty dwellings. Meditate, Cunda, do not be heedless, do not afterwards have remorse.’ This is our instruction.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā mahācundo bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

The Blessed One said this. Venerable Mahācunda was pleased and rejoiced in the Blessed One’s words.

Catuttālīsapadā vuttā, sandhayo pañca desitā;Sallekho nāma suttanto, gambhīro sāgarūpamoti.

Forty-four lines were spoken, five junctures explained; The Discourse on Purification, profound as the ocean.

Sallekhasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The Sallekha Sutta, the eighth, is finished.
