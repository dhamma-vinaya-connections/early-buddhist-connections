# 91. Brahmāyusuttaṃ

## 91. The Brahmāyu Sutta

383. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā videhesu cārikaṃ carati mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi. Tena kho pana samayena brahmāyu brāhmaṇo mithilāyaṃ paṭivasati jiṇṇo vuḍḍho mahallako addhagato vayoanuppatto, vīsavassasatiko jātiyā, tiṇṇaṃ vedānaṃ [bedānaṃ (ka.)] pāragū sanighaṇḍukeṭubhānaṃ sākkharappabhedānaṃ itihāsapañcamānaṃ, padako, veyyākaraṇo, lokāyatamahāpurisalakkhaṇesu anavayo. Assosi kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo – ‘‘samaṇo khalu bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito videhesu cārikaṃ carati mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’’ti.

383.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was traveling in the Videha country with a large community of bhikkhus, with about five hundred bhikkhus. At that time, the brahmin Brahmāyu was living in Mithilā, old, aged, advanced in years, having reached the final stage of life, one hundred and twenty years old by birth. He was a master of the three Vedas, with their vocabularies, ritual texts, phonology, etymology, and history as the fifth; he was a philologist and grammarian, and was perfectly versed in cosmology and the marks of a great man. The brahmin Brahmāyu heard: “The ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, who went forth from the Sakyan clan, is traveling in the Videha country with a large community of bhikkhus, with about five hundred bhikkhus. Now regarding that Master Gotama, such a good report has spread: ‘That Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, the Fortunate One, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of those who can be trained, teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One. He has realized with his own direct knowledge and declared this world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, this population with its ascetics and brahmins, gods and humans. He teaches the Dhamma which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with the right meaning and phrasing; he reveals the holy life that is perfectly complete and pure.’ It is good to see such Arahants.”

384. Tena kho pana samayena brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa uttaro nāma māṇavo antevāsī hoti tiṇṇaṃ vedānaṃ pāragū sanighaṇḍukeṭubhānaṃ sākkharappabhedānaṃ itihāsapañcamānaṃ, padako, veyyākaraṇo, lokāyatamahāpurisalakkhaṇesu anavayo. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo uttaraṃ māṇavaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ayaṃ, tāta uttara, samaṇo gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito videhesu cārikaṃ carati mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho…pe… sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’ti. Ehi tvaṃ, tāta uttara, yena samaṇo gotamo tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ jānāhi yadi vā taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ tathā santaṃyeva saddo abbhuggato, yadi vā no tathā; yadi vā so bhavaṃ gotamo tādiso, yadi vā na tādiso. Tathā mayaṃ taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ vedissāmā’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ panāhaṃ, bho, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ jānissāmi yadi vā taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ tathā santaṃyeva saddo abbhuggato, yadi vā no tathā; yadi vā so bhavaṃ gotamo tādiso, yadi vā na tādiso’’ti. ‘‘Āgatāni kho, tāta uttara, amhākaṃ mantesu dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yehi samannāgatassa mahāpurisassa dveyeva gatiyo bhavanti anaññā. Sace agāraṃ ajjhāvasati, rājā hoti cakkavattī dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāvī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni satta ratanāni bhavanti, seyyathidaṃ – cakkaratanaṃ, hatthiratanaṃ, assaratanaṃ, maṇiratanaṃ, itthiratanaṃ, gahapatiratanaṃ, pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā bhavanti sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena [dhammena samena (ka.)] abhivijiya ajjhāvasati. Sace kho pana agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajati, arahaṃ hoti sammāsambuddho loke vivaṭṭacchado. Ahaṃ kho pana, tāta uttara, mantānaṃ dātā; tvaṃ mantānaṃ paṭiggahetā’’ti.

384.“Yes, sir,” replied Uttara the young brahmin, assenting to Brahmāyu the brahmin. He rose from his seat, paid homage to Brahmāyu, circumambulated him, and set out on a journey to the Videhas, to where the Blessed One was. Traveling stage by stage, he approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. When the courteous and amiable talk was concluded, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the brahmin Brahmāyu said to the young man Uttara: “Well then, dear Uttara, is the report that has spread about the Venerable Gotama accurate and not otherwise? And is the Venerable Gotama truly such and not otherwise?” “The report that has spread about the Venerable Gotama is accurate, sir, and not otherwise. The Venerable Gotama is truly such and not otherwise. And the Venerable Gotama is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man.”

385. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho uttaro māṇavo brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa paṭissutvā uṭṭhāyāsanā brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā videhesu yena bhagavā tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho uttaro māṇavo bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni samannesi. Addasā kho uttaro māṇavo bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yebhuyyena thapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe, pahūtajivhatāya ca. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘passati kho me ayaṃ uttaro māṇavo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yebhuyyena thapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe, pahūtajivhatāya cā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhāsi yathā addasa uttaro māṇavo bhagavato kosohitaṃ vatthaguyhaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā jivhaṃ ninnāmetvā ubhopi kaṇṇasotāni anumasi paṭimasi [parimasi (sī. ka.)]; ubhopi nāsikasotāni [nāsikāsotāni (sī.)] anumasi paṭimasi; kevalampi nalāṭamaṇḍalaṃ jivhāya chādesi. Atha kho uttarassa māṇavassa etadahosi – ‘‘samannāgato kho samaṇo gotamo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ anubandheyyaṃ, iriyāpathamassa passeyya’’nti. Atha kho uttaro māṇavo sattamāsāni bhagavantaṃ anubandhi chāyāva anapāyinī [anupāyinī (syā. kaṃ. ka.)].

385.“Yes, sir,” replied Uttara the young brahmin, assenting to Brahmāyu the brahmin. He rose from his seat, paid homage to Brahmāyu, circumambulated him, and set out on a journey to the Videhas, to where the Blessed One was. Traveling stage by stage, he approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. When the courteous and amiable talk was concluded, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Uttara the young brahmin scrutinized the Blessed One’s body for the thirty-two marks of a great man. Uttara the young brahmin saw the thirty-two marks of a great man on the Blessed One’s body, mostly, except for two. Regarding two marks of a great man, he was doubtful, uncertain, unconvinced, and unsatisfied—namely, the concealed genitals and the large tongue. Then the Blessed One thought, “This Uttara the young brahmin sees the thirty-two marks of a great man on my body, mostly, except for two. Regarding two marks of a great man, he is doubtful, uncertain, unconvinced, and unsatisfied—namely, the concealed genitals and the large tongue.” Then the Blessed One performed such a feat of psychic power that Uttara the young brahmin saw the Blessed One’s concealed genitals. Then the Blessed One extended his tongue and stroked and massaged both ear holes and both nostrils, and covered his entire forehead with his tongue. Then Uttara the young brahmin thought, “The ascetic Gotama is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man. Why don’t I follow the ascetic Gotama and observe his deportment?” So Uttara the young brahmin followed the Blessed One for seven months like an inseparable shadow.

386. Atha kho uttaro māṇavo sattannaṃ māsānaṃ accayena videhesu yena mithilā tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena mithilā yena brahmāyu brāhmaṇo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho uttaraṃ māṇavaṃ brahmāyu brāhmaṇo etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, tāta uttara, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ tathā santaṃyeva saddo abbhuggato, no aññathā? Kacci pana so bhavaṃ gotamo tādiso, no aññādiso’’ti? ‘‘Tathā santaṃyeva, bho, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saddo abbhuggato, no aññathā; tādisova [tādisova bho (sī. pī.), tādiso ca kho (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] so bhavaṃ gotamo, no aññādiso. Samannāgato ca [samannāgato ca bho (sabbattha)] so bhavaṃ gotamo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi.

386.Then, after seven months had passed, the young man Uttara set out on a journey to Mithilā in Videha. Traveling stage by stage, he arrived at Mithilā and approached the brahmin Brahmāyu. Having approached, he paid homage to the brahmin Brahmāyu and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the brahmin Brahmāyu said to the young man Uttara: “Well then, dear Uttara, is the report that has spread about the Venerable Gotama accurate and not otherwise? And is the Venerable Gotama truly such and not otherwise?” “The report that has spread about the Venerable Gotama is accurate, sir, and not otherwise. The Venerable Gotama is truly such and not otherwise. And the Venerable Gotama is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man.”

‘‘Suppatiṭṭhitapādo kho pana bhavaṃ gotamo; idampi tassa bhoto gotamassa mahāpurisassa mahāpurisalakkhaṇaṃ bhavati.

“Furthermore, the Venerable Gotama has well-planted feet; this too is a mark of a great man in the Venerable Gotama.”

‘‘Heṭṭhā kho pana tassa bhoto gotamassa pādatalesu cakkāni jātāni sahassārāni sanemikāni sanābhikāni sabbākāraparipūrāni…

“Furthermore, beneath the Venerable Gotama’s feet, wheels have arisen, thousand-spoked, with rims and hubs, complete in every detail…”

‘‘Āyatapaṇhi kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Now, the Venerable Gotama has long heels…”

‘‘Dīghaṅguli kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“The Venerable Gotama has long digits…”

‘‘Mudutalunahatthapādo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Now the Venerable Gotama has soft and tender hands and feet…”

‘‘Jālahatthapādo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Now, the Venerable Gotama has webbed hands and feet…”

‘‘Ussaṅkhapādo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Now, the Venerable Gotama has feet with high arches…”

‘‘Eṇijaṅgho kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Now, the Venerable Gotama has legs like an antelope’s…”

‘‘Ṭhitako kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo anonamanto ubhohi pāṇitalehi jaṇṇukāni parimasati parimajjati…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama stands without bending and strokes and rubs his knees with both hands…”

‘‘Kosohitavatthaguyho kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama’s male organ is enclosed in a sheath…”

‘‘Suvaṇṇavaṇṇo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo kañcanasannibhattaco…

“Now, the Venerable Gotama is of golden complexion, with skin like burnished gold…”

‘‘Sukhumacchavi kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo. Sukhumattā chaviyā rajojallaṃ kāye na upalimpati…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama has delicate skin—so delicate that dust and dirt do not adhere to his body…”

‘‘Ekekalomo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo; ekekāni lomāni lomakūpesu jātāni…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama has a single hair growing from each pore…”

‘‘Uddhaggalomo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo; uddhaggāni lomāni jātāni nīlāni añjanavaṇṇāni kuṇḍalāvaṭṭāni dakkhiṇāvaṭṭakajātāni…

“Now, the Venerable Gotama has hairs that grow upwards; the hairs grow upwards, blue, the color of añjana, curled in rings, turning to the right…”

‘‘Brahmujugatto kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama has an upright body like Brahma’s…”

‘‘Sattussado kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama has seven convexities…”

‘‘Sīhapubbaddhakāyo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama has a lion-shaped torso…”

‘‘Citantaraṃso kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama is well-filled between the shoulders…”

‘‘Nigrodhaparimaṇḍalo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo; yāvatakvassa kāyo tāvatakvassa byāmo, yāvatakvassa byāmo tāvatakvassa kāyo…

“Indeed, the Venerable Gotama is perfectly proportioned like a banyan tree: his height is the same as his arm-span, and his arm-span is the same as his height…”

‘‘Samavaṭṭakkhandho kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

“The Venerable Gotama has evenly rounded shoulders…”

‘‘Rasaggasaggī kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

Now, it is said that the venerable Gotama has a heightened sense of taste…

‘‘Sīhahanu kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

Now, it seems that the venerable Gotama has jaws like a lion…

‘‘Cattālīsadanto kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

The venerable Gotama has forty teeth…

‘‘Samadanto kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

Indeed, the venerable Gotama has even teeth…

‘‘Aviraḷadanto kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

Indeed, the venerable Gotama has teeth without gaps…

‘‘Susukkadāṭho kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

Now, the venerable Gotama has perfectly white teeth…

‘‘Pahūtajivho kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

Indeed, the venerable Gotama has a large tongue…

‘‘Brahmassaro kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo karavikabhāṇī…

Now the venerable Gotama has a voice like Brahmā’s, a fine voice…

‘‘Abhinīlanetto kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

The venerable Gotama, however, has deep blue eyes…

‘‘Gopakhumo kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo…

Now, it is said that the venerable Gotama has eyelashes like a cow's…

‘‘Uṇṇā kho panassa bhoto gotamassa bhamukantare jātā odātā mudutūlasannibhā…

Indeed, between the eyebrows of the venerable Gotama grows a white tuft of hair, soft like cotton…

‘‘Uṇhīsasīso kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo; idampi tassa bhoto gotamassa mahāpurisassa mahāpurisalakkhaṇaṃ bhavati.

Furthermore, the venerable Gotama has a head like a turban; this too is a mark of a great man for the venerable Gotama.

‘‘Imehi kho, bho, so bhavaṃ gotamo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi samannāgato.

Indeed, good sirs, the venerable Gotama is endowed with these thirty-two marks of a great man.

387. ‘‘Gacchanto kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo dakkhiṇeneva pādena paṭhamaṃ pakkamati. So nātidūre pādaṃ uddharati, nāccāsanne pādaṃ nikkhipati; so nātisīghaṃ gacchati, nātisaṇikaṃ gacchati; na ca adduvena adduvaṃ saṅghaṭṭento gacchati, na ca gopphakena gopphakaṃ saṅghaṭṭento gacchati. So gacchanto na satthiṃ unnāmeti, na satthiṃ onāmeti; na satthiṃ sannāmeti, na satthiṃ vināmeti. Gacchato kho pana tassa bhoto gotamassa adharakāyova [aḍḍhakāyova (ka.), āraddhakāyova (syā. kaṃ.)] iñjati, na ca kāyabalena gacchati. Apalokento kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo sabbakāyeneva apaloketi; so na uddhaṃ ulloketi, na adho oloketi; na ca vipekkhamāno gacchati, yugamattañca pekkhati; tato cassa uttari anāvaṭaṃ ñāṇadassanaṃ bhavati. So antaragharaṃ pavisanto na kāyaṃ unnāmeti, na kāyaṃ onāmeti; na kāyaṃ sannāmeti, na kāyaṃ vināmeti. So nātidūre nāccāsanne āsanassa parivattati, na ca pāṇinā ālambitvā āsane nisīdati, na ca āsanasmiṃ kāyaṃ pakkhipati. So antaraghare nisinno samāno na hatthakukkuccaṃ āpajjati, na pādakukkuccaṃ āpajjati; na adduvena adduvaṃ āropetvā nisīdati; na ca gopphakena gopphakaṃ āropetvā nisīdati; na ca pāṇinā hanukaṃ upadahitvā [upādiyitvā (sī. pī.)] nisīdati. So antaraghare nisinno samāno na chambhati na kampati na vedhati na paritassati. So achambhī akampī avedhī aparitassī vigatalomahaṃso. Vivekavatto ca so bhavaṃ gotamo antaraghare nisinno hoti. So pattodakaṃ paṭiggaṇhanto na pattaṃ unnāmeti, na pattaṃ onāmeti; na pattaṃ sannāmeti, na pattaṃ vināmeti. So pattodakaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti nātithokaṃ nātibahuṃ. So na khulukhulukārakaṃ [bulubulukārakaṃ (sī.)] pattaṃ dhovati, na samparivattakaṃ pattaṃ dhovati, na pattaṃ bhūmiyaṃ nikkhipitvā hatthe dhovati; hatthesu dhotesu patto dhoto hoti, patte dhote hatthā dhotā honti. So pattodakaṃ chaḍḍeti nātidūre nāccāsanne, na ca vicchaḍḍayamāno. So odanaṃ paṭiggaṇhanto na pattaṃ unnāmeti, na pattaṃ onāmeti; na pattaṃ sannāmeti, na pattaṃ vināmeti. So odanaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti nātithokaṃ nātibahuṃ. Byañjanaṃ kho pana bhavaṃ gotamo byañjanamattāya āhāreti, na ca byañjanena ālopaṃ atināmeti. Dvattikkhattuṃ kho bhavaṃ gotamo mukhe ālopaṃ samparivattetvā ajjhoharati; na cassa kāci odanamiñjā asambhinnā kāyaṃ pavisati, na cassa kāci odanamiñjā mukhe avasiṭṭhā hoti; athāparaṃ ālopaṃ upanāmeti. Rasapaṭisaṃvedī kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo āhāraṃ āhāreti, no ca rasarāgapaṭisaṃvedī.

387.When the venerable Gotama walks, he steps forward with his right foot first. He does not lift his foot too far, nor place it too near; he walks neither too fast nor too slow. He does not walk striking knee against knee, nor ankle against ankle. When walking, he does not raise his thigh, nor lower his thigh; he does not bend his thigh, nor extend his thigh. Only the venerable Gotama's lower body moves as he walks; he does not walk by force of his body. When looking around, the venerable Gotama looks around with his whole body; he does not look upward or downward, nor does he go looking around. He looks only a yoke’s length ahead, and his unobstructed knowledge and vision is beyond that. When entering a house, he does not raise his body, nor lower his body; he does not bend his body, nor extend his body. He does not turn too far from or too close to the seat. He does not lean on his hand to sit down, nor does he throw his body onto the seat. When seated indoors, he does not fidget with his hands or fidget with his feet. He does not sit with his knee placed on his knee, nor does he sit with his ankle placed on his ankle. He does not sit with his chin resting on his hand. When seated indoors, he is not startled, trembling, or agitated; he is free from fear, unshaken, untroubled, and without goosebumps. The venerable Gotama remains secluded while seated indoors. When receiving water for his bowl, he does not raise his bowl, nor lower his bowl; he does not bend his bowl, nor extend his bowl. He receives water for his bowl neither too little nor too much. He does not scrub his bowl making a 'khulu khulu' sound, nor does he wash his bowl swirling it around. He does not place the bowl on the ground and then wash his hands; with his hands washed, the bowl is washed, with the bowl washed, his hands are washed. He discards the bowl water neither too far nor too close, nor scattering it around. When receiving rice, he does not raise his bowl, nor lower his bowl; he does not bend his bowl, nor extend his bowl. He receives rice neither too little nor too much. The venerable Gotama eats curry only in proportion to the rice, and does not extend his mouthful for the sake of the curry. The venerable Gotama chews each mouthful two or three times in his mouth before swallowing it; no unchewed lump of rice enters his body, and no lump of rice remains in his mouth before taking the next mouthful. The venerable Gotama eats food experiencing the taste, but not experiencing craving for the taste.

‘‘Aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgataṃ [aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgato (ka.)] kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo āhāraṃ āhāreti – neva davāya, na madāya na maṇḍanāya na vibhūsanāya, yāvadeva imassa kāyassa ṭhitiyā yāpanāya, vihiṃsūparatiyā brahmacariyānuggahāya – ‘iti purāṇañca vedanaṃ paṭihaṅkhāmi navañca vedanaṃ na uppādessāmi, yātrā ca me bhavissati anavajjatā ca phāsuvihāro cā’ti. So bhuttāvī pattodakaṃ paṭiggaṇhanto na pattaṃ unnāmeti, na pattaṃ onāmeti; na pattaṃ sannāmeti, na pattaṃ vināmeti. So pattodakaṃ paṭiggaṇhāti nātithokaṃ nātibahuṃ. So na khulukhulukārakaṃ pattaṃ dhovati, na samparivattakaṃ pattaṃ dhovati, na pattaṃ bhūmiyaṃ nikkhipitvā hatthe dhovati; hatthesu dhotesu patto dhoto hoti, patte dhote hatthā dhotā honti. So pattodakaṃ chaḍḍeti nātidūre nāccāsanne, na ca vicchaḍḍayamāno. So bhuttāvī na pattaṃ bhūmiyaṃ nikkhipati nātidūre nāccāsanne, na ca anatthiko pattena hoti, na ca ativelānurakkhī pattasmiṃ. So bhuttāvī muhuttaṃ tuṇhī nisīdati, na ca anumodanassa kālamatināmeti. So bhuttāvī anumodati, na taṃ bhattaṃ garahati, na aññaṃ bhattaṃ paṭikaṅkhati; aññadatthu dhammiyā kathāya taṃ parisaṃ sandasseti samādapeti samuttejeti sampahaṃseti. So taṃ parisaṃ dhammiyā kathāya sandassetvā samādapetvā samuttejetvā sampahaṃsetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamati. So nātisīghaṃ gacchati, nātisaṇikaṃ gacchati, na ca muccitukāmo gacchati; na ca tassa bhoto gotamassa kāye cīvaraṃ accukkaṭṭhaṃ hoti na ca accokkaṭṭhaṃ, na ca kāyasmiṃ allīnaṃ na ca kāyasmā apakaṭṭhaṃ; na ca tassa bhoto gotamassa kāyamhā vāto cīvaraṃ apavahati; na ca tassa bhoto gotamassa kāye rajojallaṃ upalimpati. So ārāmagato nisīdati paññatte āsane. Nisajja pāde pakkhāleti; na ca so bhavaṃ gotamo pādamaṇḍanānuyogamanuyutto viharati. So pāde pakkhāletvā nisīdati pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā. So neva attabyābādhāya ceteti, na parabyābādhāya ceteti, na ubhayabyābādhāya ceteti; attahitaparahitaubhayahitasabbalokahitameva so bhavaṃ gotamo cintento nisinno hoti. So ārāmagato parisati dhammaṃ deseti, na taṃ parisaṃ ussādeti, na taṃ parisaṃ apasādeti; aññadatthu dhammiyā kathāya taṃ parisaṃ sandasseti samādapeti samuttejeti sampahaṃseti.

The venerable Gotama takes food with eight factors: not for amusement, not for intoxication, not for beautification, not for adornment, but only for the support and maintenance of this body, for keeping it unharmed, for helping in the holy life, thinking: 'Thus I shall put an end to old feelings and not give rise to new ones, and I shall maintain myself and be blameless and live in comfort.' When he has finished eating, while accepting the bowl-rinsing water, he does not tilt the bowl up, or tilt the bowl down; he does not push the bowl around, or move the bowl away. He accepts just enough bowl-rinsing water, not too little or too much. He does not scrub his bowl making a 'khulu khulu' sound, nor does he wash his bowl swirling it around, nor does he put the bowl on the ground and then wash his hands; when his hands are washed, the bowl is washed, and when the bowl is washed, his hands are washed. He discards the bowl-rinsing water neither too far nor too near, and not scattering it. Having finished eating, he does not place the bowl on the ground too far or too near, nor is he without need of the bowl, nor does he excessively guard the bowl. After eating, he sits silently for a moment, not letting the time for appreciation pass. When the time comes, he appreciates, but he does not criticize the meal or express a longing for another meal; but rather he instructs, encourages, inspires, and gladdens the assembly with a talk on the Dhamma. Having instructed, encouraged, inspired, and gladdened the assembly with a talk on the Dhamma, he rises from his seat and departs. He does not walk too quickly or too slowly, nor does he walk wishing to get away; his robe does not hang too high or too low on the venerable Gotama's body, nor does it cling to his body or hang away from his body; the wind does not blow his robe away from the venerable Gotama's body, nor does dust and dirt cling to the venerable Gotama's body. When he goes to the monastery, he sits on a prepared seat. Sitting down, he washes his feet; but the venerable Gotama does not live devoted to decorating his feet. Having washed his feet, he sits cross-legged, sets his body straight, and establishes mindfulness in front of him. He does not intend harm for himself, for others, or for both. The venerable Gotama sits contemplating only the welfare of himself, others, both, and the whole world. When he teaches the Dhamma to an assembly in the monastery, he does not excite or discourage the assembly; but rather he instructs, encourages, inspires, and gladdens the assembly with a talk on the Dhamma.

‘‘Aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgato kho panassa bhoto gotamassa mukhato ghoso niccharati – vissaṭṭho ca, viññeyyo ca, mañju ca, savanīyo ca, bindu ca, avisārī ca, gambhīro ca, ninnādī ca. Yathāparisaṃ kho pana so bhavaṃ gotamo sarena viññāpeti, na cassa bahiddhā parisāya ghoso niccharati. Te tena bhotā gotamena dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamanti avalokayamānāyeva [apalokayamānāyeva (sī. ka.)] avijahitattā [avijahantābhāvena (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)]. Addasāma kho mayaṃ, bho, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ gacchantaṃ, addasāma ṭhitaṃ, addasāma antaragharaṃ pavisantaṃ, addasāma antaraghare nisinnaṃ tuṇhībhūtaṃ, addasāma antaraghare bhuñjantaṃ, addasāma bhuttāviṃ nisinnaṃ tuṇhībhūtaṃ, addasāma bhuttāviṃ anumodantaṃ, addasāma ārāmaṃ gacchantaṃ, addasāma ārāmagataṃ nisinnaṃ tuṇhībhūtaṃ, addasāma ārāmagataṃ parisati dhammaṃ desentaṃ. Ediso ca ediso ca so bhavaṃ gotamo, tato ca bhiyyo’’ti.

Moreover, the sound that issues from the mouth of the venerable Gotama is endowed with eight qualities: it is distinct, intelligible, pleasing, audible, articulate, non-scattered, deep, and resonant. The venerable Gotama makes his meaning known according to the assembly, but his voice does not extend beyond the assembly. Those who have been shown, encouraged, roused, and gladdened by the venerable Gotama with a talk on the Dhamma rise from their seats and depart, looking back, as if unable to leave. We have seen the venerable Gotama walking, seen him standing, seen him entering a house, seen him sitting silently inside a house, seen him eating inside a house, seen him sitting silently after eating, seen him giving thanks after eating, seen him going to the park, seen him sitting silently having gone to the park, seen him teaching the Dhamma to an assembly having gone to the park. Such and such is the venerable Gotama, and even more than that.

388. Evaṃ vutte, brahmāyu brāhmaṇo uṭṭhāyāsanā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā yena bhagavā tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā tikkhattuṃ udānaṃ udāneti –

388.When this was said, the brahmin Brahmāyu rose from his seat, arranged his upper robe over one shoulder, extended his joined palms toward the Blessed One, and three times uttered this exclamation:

‘‘Namo tassa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa.

Homage to that Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.

‘‘Namo tassa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa.

Homage to that Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.

‘‘Namo tassa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassā’’ti.

Homage to that Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.

‘‘Appeva nāma mayaṃ kadāci karahaci tena bhotā gotamena samāgaccheyyāma? Appeva nāma siyā kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti!

Perhaps we may someday meet the venerable Gotama! Perhaps there might be some conversation!

389. Atha kho bhagavā videhesu anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena mithilā tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā mithilāyaṃ viharati maghadevambavane. Assosuṃ kho mithileyyakā [methileyyakā (sī. pī.)] brāhmaṇagahapatikā – ‘‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito videhesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi mithilaṃ anuppatto, mithilāyaṃ viharati maghadevambavane. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’’ti.

389.Then the Blessed One, traveling stage by stage in the Videha country, arrived at Mithilā. There the Blessed One stayed in Maghadeva Mango Grove. The brahmins and householders of Mithilā heard: “The ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, who has gone forth from the Sakyan clan, has been traveling in the Videha country with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus, with about five hundred bhikkhus, and has arrived at Mithilā. He is staying in Maghadeva Mango Grove. A good report about the Venerable Gotama has been circulating: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, the Fortunate One, knower of the world, unexcelled trainer of persons to be tamed, teacher of devas and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One. Having realized with his own direct knowledge this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, this generation with its recluses and brahmins, its devas and humans, he makes it known to others. He teaches the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and phrasing, and he reveals the perfectly complete and purified holy life. It is good to see such Arahants.’

Atha kho mithileyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā appekacce bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu, sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce yena bhagavā tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce bhagavato santike nāmagottaṃ sāvetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu; appekacce tuṇhībhūtā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu.

Then the brahmins and householders of Mithilā approached the Blessed One. Having approached, some paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some exchanged greetings with the Blessed One, and when that courteous and amiable talk was concluded, sat down to one side; some raised their joined hands in salutation towards the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some announced their name and clan in the presence of the Blessed One and sat down to one side; some kept silent and sat down to one side.

390. Assosi kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo – ‘‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito mithilaṃ anuppatto, mithilāyaṃ viharati maghadevambavane’’ti. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo sambahulehi sāvakehi saddhiṃ yena maghadevambavanaṃ tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho brahmāyuno brāhmaṇassa avidūre ambavanassa etadahosi – ‘‘na kho metaṃ patirūpaṃ yohaṃ pubbe appaṭisaṃvidito samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ dassanāya upasaṅkameyya’’nti. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo aññataraṃ māṇavakaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, māṇavaka, yena samaṇo gotamo tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mama vacanena samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ appābādhaṃ appātaṅkaṃ lahuṭṭhānaṃ balaṃ phāsuvihāraṃ puccha – ‘brahmāyu, bho gotama, brāhmaṇo bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ appābādhaṃ appātaṅkaṃ lahuṭṭhānaṃ balaṃ phāsuvihāraṃ pucchatī’ti. Evañca vadehi – ‘brahmāyu, bho gotama, brāhmaṇo jiṇṇo vuḍḍho mahallako addhagato vayoanuppatto, vīsavassasatiko jātiyā, tiṇṇaṃ vedānaṃ pāragū sanighaṇḍukeṭubhānaṃ sākkharappabhedānaṃ itihāsapañcamānaṃ, padako, veyyākaraṇo, lokāyatamahāpurisalakkhaṇesu anavayo. Yāvatā, bho, brāhmaṇagahapatikā mithilāyaṃ paṭivasanti, brahmāyu tesaṃ brāhmaṇo aggamakkhāyati – yadidaṃ bhogehi; brahmāyu tesaṃ brāhmaṇo aggamakkhāyati – yadidaṃ mantehi; brahmāyu tesaṃ brāhmaṇo aggamakkhāyati – yadidaṃ āyunā ceva yasasā ca. So bhoto gotamassa dassanakāmo’’’ti.

390.The brahmin Brahmāyu heard: “Indeed, the ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, who has gone forth from the Sakyan clan, has arrived at Mithilā and is dwelling in the Maghadeva Mango Grove.” Then the brahmin Brahmāyu, together with many disciples, approached the Maghadeva Mango Grove. Then, when the brahmin Brahmāyu was not far from the mango grove, this thought occurred to him: “It would not be proper for me to approach the ascetic Gotama without first being introduced.” Then the brahmin Brahmāyu addressed a certain young man: “Come, young man, approach the ascetic Gotama. Having approached, ask the ascetic Gotama in my name whether he is free from illness and affliction, light in his movements, strong, and dwelling comfortably—saying: ‘Venerable Gotama, the brahmin Brahmāyu asks whether you are free from illness and affliction, light in your movements, strong, and dwelling comfortably.’ And say this: ‘Venerable Gotama, the brahmin Brahmāyu is old, aged, burdened with years, advanced in life, having reached the final stage of life, being one hundred and twenty years old by birth. He is a master of the three Vedas, with their vocabularies, their rituals, their phonology, their etymology, and the histories as a fifth; skilled in philology and grammar, he is unerring concerning natural philosophy and the marks of a great man. As many brahmins and householders as dwell in Mithilā, Brahmāyu is declared foremost among them—that is, in regard to wealth, in regard to the mantras, and in regard to both longevity and fame. He wishes to see the Venerable Gotama.’

‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho so māṇavako brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho so māṇavako bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘brahmāyu, bho gotama, brāhmaṇo bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ appābādhaṃ appātaṅkaṃ lahuṭṭhānaṃ balaṃ phāsuvihāraṃ pucchati; evañca vadeti – ‘brahmāyu, bho gotama, brāhmaṇo jiṇṇo vuḍḍho mahallako addhagato vayoanuppatto, vīsavassasatiko jātiyā, tiṇṇaṃ vedānaṃ pāragū sanighaṇḍukeṭubhānaṃ sākkharappabhedānaṃ itihāsapañcamānaṃ, padako, veyyākaraṇo, lokāyatamahāpurisalakkhaṇesu anavayo. Yāvatā, bho, brāhmaṇagahapatikā mithilāyaṃ paṭivasanti, brahmāyu tesaṃ brāhmaṇo aggamakkhāyati – yadidaṃ bhogehi; brahmāyu tesaṃ brāhmaṇo aggamakkhāyati – yadidaṃ mantehi; brahmāyu tesaṃ brāhmaṇo aggamakkhāyati – yadidaṃ āyunā ceva yasasā ca. So bhoto gotamassa dassanakāmo’’’ti. ‘‘Yassadāni, māṇava, brahmāyu brāhmaṇo kālaṃ maññatī’’ti. Atha kho so māṇavako yena brahmāyu brāhmaṇo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘katāvakāso khomhi bhavatā samaṇena gotamena. Yassadāni bhavaṃ kālaṃ maññatī’’ti.

“Yes, sir,” replied the young man to the brahmin Brahmāyu, and he approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. And when that courteous and amiable talk was concluded, he stood to one side. Standing to one side, the young man said to the Blessed One: “Brahmāyu, Venerable Gotama, the brahmin, asks after your health—whether you are free from illness, free from affliction, light in your movements, strong, and dwelling comfortably. And he says this: ‘Brahmāyu, Venerable Gotama, the brahmin, is old, aged, burdened with years, advanced in life, having reached the final stage of life; he is one hundred and twenty years old by birth, a master of the three Vedas, with their vocabularies, their rituals, their phonology, their etymology, and the histories as a fifth; skilled in philology and grammar, and unerring concerning natural philosophy and the marks of a great man. As many brahmins and householders as dwell in Mithilā, Brahmāyu is declared foremost among them—that is, in regard to wealth, in regard to the mantras, and in regard to both longevity and fame. He wishes to see the Venerable Gotama.’ ” “Let the brahmin Brahmāyu come when he deems it the time, young man,” replied the Blessed One. Then the young man returned to the brahmin Brahmāyu and said: “I have made an opportunity for you with the ascetic Gotama, sir. Let the venerable one come when he deems it the time.”

391. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami. Addasā kho sā parisā brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna oramiya [oramattha (syā. kaṃ. pī.), oramatha, oramati (ka.), atha naṃ (sī.), oramiyāti pana tvāpaccayantatathasaṃvaṇṇanānurūpaṃ visodhitapadaṃ] okāsamakāsi yathā taṃ ñātassa yasassino. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo taṃ parisaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘alaṃ, bho! Nisīdatha tumhe sake āsane. Idhāhaṃ samaṇassa gotamassa santike nisīdissāmī’’ti.

391.Then the brahmin Brahmāyu approached the Blessed One. The assembly saw the brahmin Brahmāyu coming from afar. Seeing him, they made space for him, as was fitting for one who was known and famous. Then the brahmin Brahmāyu addressed that assembly: “Enough, sirs! Sit on your own seats. Here I will sit near the ascetic Gotama.”

Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni samannesi. Addasā kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe, pahūtajivhatāya ca. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

Then the brahmin Brahmāyu approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. And when that courteous and amiable talk was concluded, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the brahmin Brahmāyu examined the thirty-two marks of a great man on the Blessed One’s body. The brahmin Brahmāyu saw the thirty-two marks of a great man on the Blessed One’s body, mostly, except for two. In two marks of a great man he doubts, is uncertain, is not convinced, and is not satisfied—the sheath-enclosed genitals and the abundant tongue. Then the brahmin Brahmāyu addressed the Blessed One with verses:

‘‘Ye me dvattiṃsāti sutā, mahāpurisalakkhaṇā;Duve tesaṃ na passāmi, bhoto kāyasmiṃ gotama.

“Of the thirty-two marks of a great man that I have heard, I do not see two of them on your body, Master Gotama.

‘‘Kacci kosohitaṃ bhoto, vatthaguyhaṃ naruttama;Nārīsamānasavhayā, kacci jivhā na dassakā [nārīsahanāma savhayā, kacci jivhā narassikā; (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)].

Is your sheath-enclosed genitals concealed, O best of men? Is your tongue not visible, that which is called ‘woman-like’?

‘‘Kacci pahūtajivhosi, yathā taṃ jāniyāmase;Ninnāmayetaṃ pahūtaṃ, kaṅkhaṃ vinaya no ise.

Do you indeed have an abundant tongue, so that we may know it; Extend that abundant one forth, dispel our doubt, O sage.

‘‘Diṭṭhadhammahitatthāya, samparāyasukhāya ca;Katāvakāsā pucchāma, yaṃ kiñci abhipatthita’’nti.

For welfare in this present life, and for happiness in the life to come; having been given the opportunity, we ask whatever is desired.”

392. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘passati kho me ayaṃ brahmāyu brāhmaṇo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe, pahūtajivhatāya cā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhāsi yathā addasa brahmāyu brāhmaṇo bhagavato kosohitaṃ vatthaguyhaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā jivhaṃ ninnāmetvā ubhopi kaṇṇasotāni anumasi paṭimasi; ubhopi nāsikasotāni anumasi paṭimasi; kevalampi nalāṭamaṇḍalaṃ jivhāya chādesi. Atha kho bhagavā brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ gāthāhi paccabhāsi –

392.Then this thought occurred to the Blessed One: “This brahmin Brahmāyu sees in me the thirty-two marks of a great man, mostly, except for two. In two marks of a great man he doubts, is uncertain, is not convinced, and is not satisfied—the sheath-enclosed genitals and the abundant tongue.” Then the Blessed One performed such a feat of psychic power that the brahmin Brahmāyu saw the Blessed One’s sheath-enclosed genitals. Then the Blessed One extended his tongue and stroked and massaged both ear-holes; he stroked and massaged both nostrils; and he covered his entire forehead with his tongue. Then the Blessed One addressed the brahmin Brahmāyu in verses:

‘‘Ye te dvattiṃsāti sutā, mahāpurisalakkhaṇā;Sabbe te mama kāyasmiṃ, mā te [mā vo (ka.)] kaṅkhāhu brāhmaṇa.

“The thirty-two marks of a great man that you have heard of—all these are in my body, brahmin; do not doubt.

‘‘Abhiññeyyaṃ abhiññātaṃ, bhāvetabbañca bhāvitaṃ;Pahātabbaṃ pahīnaṃ me, tasmā buddhosmi brāhmaṇa.

What was to be known has been known; what was to be developed has been developed; what was to be abandoned has been abandoned by me; therefore, I am the Buddha, O Brahmin.

‘‘Diṭṭhadhammahitatthāya, samparāyasukhāya ca;Katāvakāso pucchassu, yaṃ kiñci abhipatthita’’nti.

Having been given the opportunity, ask whatever you wish, for your welfare in this present life and for happiness in the life to come.”

393. Atha kho brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘katāvakāso khomhi samaṇena gotamena. Kiṃ nu kho ahaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ puccheyyaṃ – ‘diṭṭhadhammikaṃ vā atthaṃ samparāyikaṃ vā’’’ti. Atha kho brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘kusalo kho ahaṃ diṭṭhadhammikānaṃ atthānaṃ. Aññepi maṃ diṭṭhadhammikaṃ atthaṃ pucchanti. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ samparāyikaṃyeva atthaṃ puccheyya’’nti. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

393.Then this thought occurred to the brahmin Brahmāyu: “I have been given an opportunity by the ascetic Gotama. What should I ask the ascetic Gotama—welfare in this present life or welfare in the life to come?” Then this thought occurred to the brahmin Brahmāyu: “I am skilled in matters of welfare in this present life. Others also ask me about welfare in this present life. Why don’t I ask the ascetic Gotama about welfare in the life to come?” Then the brahmin Brahmāyu addressed the Blessed One in verses:

‘‘Kathaṃ kho brāhmaṇo hoti, kathaṃ bhavati vedagū;Tevijjo bho kathaṃ hoti, sotthiyo kinti vuccati.

“How, sir, does one become a brahmin? How does one become a master of the Vedas? How, sir, does one become a possessor of the threefold knowledge? How is one called a holy man?

‘‘Arahaṃ bho kathaṃ hoti, kathaṃ bhavati kevalī;Muni ca bho kathaṃ hoti, buddho kinti pavuccatī’’ti.

How, sir, is one an Arahant? How, sir, is one perfected? How, sir, is one a sage? How is one called a Buddha?”

394. Atha kho bhagavā brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ gāthāhi paccabhāsi –

394.Then the Blessed One replied to Brahmāyu the brahmin in verse:

‘‘Pubbenivāsaṃ yo vedi, saggāpāyañca passati;Atho jātikkhayaṃ patto, abhiññā vosito muni.

“He who knows his former abodes, sees heaven and states of woe, and has attained the destruction of birth—that sage who has completed higher knowledge—

‘‘Cittaṃ visuddhaṃ jānāti, muttaṃ rāgehi sabbaso;Pahīnajātimaraṇo, brahmacariyassa kevalī;Pāragū sabbadhammānaṃ, buddho tādī pavuccatī’’ti.

He knows his mind is purified, entirely freed from passion; he has abandoned birth and death, perfected in the holy life; gone beyond all phenomena—such a one is called a Buddha, the Such One.”

Evaṃ vutte, brahmāyu brāhmaṇo uṭṭhāyāsanā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā bhagavato pādesu sirasā nipatitvā bhagavato pādāni mukhena ca paricumbati, pāṇīhi ca parisambāhati, nāmañca sāveti – ‘‘brahmāyu ahaṃ, bho gotama, brāhmaṇo; brahmāyu ahaṃ, bho gotama, brāhmaṇo’’ti. Atha kho sā parisā acchariyabbhutacittajātā ahosi – ‘‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho! Yatra hi nāmāyaṃ brahmāyu brāhmaṇo ñāto yasassī evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karissatī’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘alaṃ, brāhmaṇa, uṭṭhaha nisīda tvaṃ sake āsane yato te mayi cittaṃ pasanna’’nti. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo uṭṭhahitvā sake āsane nisīdi.

When this was said, Brahmāyu the brahmin rose from his seat, arranged his upper robe over one shoulder, bowed his head at the Blessed One’s feet, kissed them with his mouth, stroked them with his hands, and announced his name: “I am Brahmāyu, Master Gotama, a brahmin; I am Brahmāyu, Master Gotama, a brahmin.” Then that assembly was filled with amazement and wonder: “It is wonderful, truly amazing! That this Brahmāyu the brahmin, so well-known and esteemed, should pay such utmost respect!” Then the Blessed One said to Brahmāyu the brahmin: “Enough, brahmin, rise and sit in your own seat, since your mind is pleased with me.” Then Brahmāyu the brahmin rose and sat in his own seat.

395. Atha kho bhagavā brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa anupubbiṃ kathaṃ kathesi, seyyathidaṃ – dānakathaṃ, sīlakathaṃ, saggakathaṃ; kāmānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Yadā bhagavā aññāsi brahmāyuṃ brāhmaṇaṃ kallacittaṃ muducittaṃ vinīvaraṇacittaṃ udaggacittaṃ pasannacittaṃ, atha yā buddhānaṃ sāmukkaṃsikā dhammadesanā taṃ pakāsesi – dukkhaṃ, samudayaṃ, nirodhaṃ, maggaṃ. Seyyathāpi nāma suddhaṃ vatthaṃ apagatakāḷakaṃ sammadeva rajanaṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, evameva brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa tasmiṃyeva āsane virajaṃ vītamalaṃ dhammacakkhuṃ udapādi – ‘‘yaṃ kiñci samudayadhammaṃ sabbaṃ taṃ nirodhadhamma’’nti. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo diṭṭhadhammo pattadhammo viditadhammo pariyogāḷhadhammo tiṇṇavicikiccho vigatakathaṃkatho vesārajjappatto aparappaccayo satthusāsane bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama! Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya – cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantīti – evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsakaṃ maṃ bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gataṃ. Adhivāsetu ca me bhavaṃ gotamo svātanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’ti. Adhivāsesi bhagavā tuṇhībhāvena. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo bhagavato adhivāsanaṃ viditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo tassā rattiyā accayena sake nivesane paṇītaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ paṭiyādāpetvā bhagavato kālaṃ ārocāpesi – ‘‘kālo, bho gotama, niṭṭhitaṃ bhatta’’nti.

395.Then the Blessed One gave the brahmin Brahmāyu a progressive discourse—that is, on generosity, on virtue, on heaven; he explained the danger, degradation, and defilement of sensual pleasures and the benefit of renunciation. When the Blessed One knew that Brahmāyu the brahmin had a ready mind, a pliant mind, an unhindered mind, a joyful mind, a confident mind, he then revealed the teaching unique to the Buddhas: suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Just as a clean cloth free from stains would properly absorb dye, so too, right there in his seat, the dust-free, stainless vision of the Dhamma arose in Brahmāyu the brahmin: “Whatever is subject to arising is all subject to cessation.” Then Brahmāyu the brahmin, having seen the Dhamma, attained the Dhamma, understood the Dhamma, fathomed the Dhamma, crossed over doubt, dispelled perplexity, gained confidence, and became independent of others in the Teacher’s dispensation, said to the Blessed One: “Excellent, Master Gotama! Excellent, Master Gotama! Just as one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the way to one who was lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness so that those with eyes could see forms—in the same way, Master Gotama has made the Dhamma clear in many ways. I go for refuge to Master Gotama, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of bhikkhus. May Master Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forward, for life. And may Master Gotama, together with the Sangha of bhikkhus, accept my meal for tomorrow.” The Blessed One consented by silence. Then Brahmāyu the brahmin, understanding the Blessed One’s consent, rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and departed. Then, when the night had passed, Brahmāyu the brahmin had delicious food, both hard and soft, prepared in his own home and had the time announced to the Blessed One: “It is time, Master Gotama, the meal is ready.”

Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena brahmāyussa brāhmaṇassa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghena. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo sattāhaṃ buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappesi sampavāresi. Atha kho bhagavā tassa sattāhassa accayena videhesu cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Atha kho brahmāyu brāhmaṇo acirapakkantassa bhagavato kālamakāsi. Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘brahmāyu, bhante, brāhmaṇo kālaṅkato. Tassa kā gati, ko abhisamparāyo’’ti? ‘‘Paṇḍito, bhikkhave, brahmāyu brāhmaṇo paccapādi dhammassānudhammaṃ, na ca maṃ dhammādhikaraṇaṃ vihesesi. Brahmāyu, bhikkhave, brāhmaṇo pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā opapātiko hoti, tattha parinibbāyī, anāvattidhammo tasmā lokā’’ti.

Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the morning, took his bowl and robe and went to the residence of the brahmin Brahmāyu. Having approached, he sat down on the prepared seat together with the community of bhikkhus. Then the brahmin Brahmāyu personally served and satisfied the community of bhikkhus headed by the Buddha with fine hard and soft food for seven days. Then, after those seven days had passed, the Blessed One set out to wander in the Videha country. Not long after the Blessed One had departed, the brahmin Brahmāyu died. Then several bhikkhus approached the Blessed One; having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, those bhikkhus said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, the brahmin Brahmāyu has died. What is his destination, what is his future state?” “bhikkhus, the brahmin Brahmāyu was wise. He practiced the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, and did not trouble me concerning the Dhamma. bhikkhus, the brahmin Brahmāyu, with the destruction of the five lower fetters, has arisen spontaneously and will attain final Nibbāna there, not being subject to return from that world.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

The Blessed One said this. The bhikkhus, satisfied, rejoiced at the Blessed One's words.

Brahmāyusuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ paṭhamaṃ.

The Brahmāyu Sutta, the first, is concluded.
