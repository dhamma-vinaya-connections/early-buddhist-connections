# 48. Kosambiyasuttaṃ

## 48. The Kosambiya Sutta

491. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena kosambiyaṃ bhikkhū bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharanti. Te na ceva aññamaññaṃ saññāpenti na ca saññattiṃ upenti, na ca aññamaññaṃ nijjhāpenti, na ca nijjhattiṃ upenti. Atha kho aññataro bhikkhu yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idha, bhante, kosambiyaṃ bhikkhū bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharanti, te na ceva aññamaññaṃ saññāpenti, na ca saññattiṃ upenti, na ca aññamaññaṃ nijjhāpenti, na ca nijjhattiṃ upentī’’ti.

491.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambī in Ghosita’s Park. At that time the bhikkhus at Kosambī were quarrelsome, given to contention, engaged in disputes, and kept wounding each other with verbal weapons. They neither persuaded each other nor accepted persuasion, neither convinced each other nor accepted conviction. Then a certain monk approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, that monk said to the Blessed One: “Here, venerable sir, the bhikkhus at Kosambī are quarrelsome, given to contention, engaged in disputes, and keep wounding each other with verbal weapons. They neither persuaded each other nor accepted persuasion, neither convinced each other nor accepted conviction.”

Atha kho bhagavā aññataraṃ bhikkhuṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, mama vacanena te bhikkhū āmantehi – ‘satthā vo āyasmante āmantetī’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho so bhikkhu bhagavato paṭissutvā yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘satthā āyasmante āmantetī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū tassa bhikkhuno paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinne kho te bhikkhū bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘saccaṃ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharatha, te na ceva aññamaññaṃ saññāpetha, na ca saññattiṃ upetha, na ca aññamaññaṃ nijjhāpetha, na ca nijjhattiṃ upethā’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, bhikkhave, yasmiṃ tumhe samaye bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharatha, api nu tumhākaṃ tasmiṃ samaye mettaṃ kāyakammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ hoti sabrahmacārīsu āvi ceva raho ca, mettaṃ vacīkammaṃ…pe… mettaṃ manokammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ hoti sabrahmacārīsu āvi ceva raho cā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Iti kira, bhikkhave, yasmiṃ tumhe samaye bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharatha, neva tumhākaṃ tasmiṃ samaye mettaṃ kāyakammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ hoti sabrahmacārīsu āvi ceva raho ca, na mettaṃ vacīkammaṃ…pe… na mettaṃ manokammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ hoti sabrahmacārīsu āvi ceva raho ca. Atha kiñcarahi tumhe, moghapurisā, kiṃ jānantā kiṃ passantā bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharatha, te na ceva aññamaññaṃ saññāpetha, na ca saññattiṃ upetha, na ca aññamaññaṃ nijjhāpetha, na ca nijjhattiṃ upetha? Tañhi tumhākaṃ, moghapurisā, bhavissati dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāyā’’ti.

Then the Blessed One addressed a certain monk: “Come, monk, in my name call those bhikkhus, saying, ‘The Teacher calls the venerable ones.’” “Yes, venerable sir,” that monk replied to the Blessed One. Having assented, he went to those bhikkhus and said, “The Teacher calls the venerable ones.” “Yes, friend,” those bhikkhus replied to that monk. Then they approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. When they were seated, the Blessed One said to them: “Is it true, bhikkhus, that you have been quarreling, disputing, and engaged in contention, stabbing each other with verbal weapons, and that you neither persuade each other nor accept persuasion, neither convince each other nor accept conviction?” “Yes, venerable sir.” “What do you think, bhikkhus? At such a time, when you are quarreling, disputing, and engaged in contention, stabbing each other with verbal weapons, is loving bodily action present among you toward your fellow monastics, both in public and in private? Is loving verbal action… loving mental action present among you toward your fellow monastics, both in public and in private?” “No, venerable sir.” “So it seems, bhikkhus, that at such a time, when you are quarreling, disputing, and engaged in contention, stabbing each other with verbal weapons, loving bodily action is not present among you toward your fellow monastics, neither in public nor in private; loving verbal action… loving mental action is not present among you, neither in public nor in private. Then what, foolish men, knowing and seeing what, do you quarrel, dispute, and engage in contention, stabbing each other with verbal weapons, neither persuading each other nor accepting persuasion, neither convincing each other nor accepting conviction? This, foolish men, will be to your harm and suffering for a long time.”

492. Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘chayime, bhikkhave, dhammā sāraṇīyā piyakaraṇā garukaraṇā saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekībhāvāya saṃvattanti. Katame cha? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno mettaṃ kāyakammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ hoti sabrahmacārīsu āvi ceva raho ca. Ayampi dhammo sāraṇīyo piyakaraṇo garukaraṇo saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekībhāvāya saṃvattati.

492.Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, these six principles of cordiality lead to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity. What six? Here, bhikkhus, a monk maintains bodily acts of loving-kindness toward his fellow monastics, both in public and in private. This principle of cordiality leads to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno mettaṃ vacīkammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ hoti sabrahmacārīsu āvi ceva raho ca. Ayampi dhammo sāraṇīyo piyakaraṇo garukaraṇo saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekibhāvāya saṃvattati.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a monk maintains verbal acts of loving-kindness toward his fellow monastics, both in public and in private. This principle of cordiality leads to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno mettaṃ manokammaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ hoti sabrahmacārīsu āvi ceva raho ca. Ayampi dhammo sāraṇīyo piyakaraṇo garukaraṇo saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekībhāvāya saṃvattati.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a monk maintains mental acts of loving-kindness toward his fellow monastics, both in public and in private. This principle of cordiality leads to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ye te lābhā dhammikā dhammaladdhā antamaso pattapariyāpannamattampi, tathārūpehi lābhehi appaṭivibhattabhogī hoti sīlavantehi sabrahmacārīhi sādhāraṇabhogī. Ayampi dhammo sāraṇīyo piyakaraṇo garukaraṇo saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekībhāvāya saṃvattati.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a monk shares without reservation any lawful gains obtained righteously, even just the contents of his alms bowl, enjoying them in common with his virtuous fellow monastics. This principle of cordiality leads to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu yāni tāni sīlāni akhaṇḍāni acchiddāni asabalāni akammāsāni bhujissāni viññuppasatthāni aparāmaṭṭhāni samādhisaṃvattanikāni tathārūpesu sīlesu sīlasāmaññagato viharati sabrahmacārīhi āvi ceva raho ca. Ayampi dhammo sāraṇīyo piyakaraṇo garukaraṇo saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekībhāvāya saṃvattati.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a monk dwells practicing in common with his fellow monastics, both in public and in private, with regard to those virtues that are unbroken, flawless, unspotted, untainted, liberating, praised by the wise, ungrasped at, and conducive to samādhi. This principle of cordiality leads to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu yāyaṃ diṭṭhi ariyā niyyānikā niyyāti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāya tathārūpāya diṭṭhiyā diṭṭhisāmaññagato viharati sabrahmacārīhi āvi ceva raho ca. Ayampi dhammo sāraṇīyo piyakaraṇo garukaraṇo saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekībhāvāya saṃvattati.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a monk dwells practicing in common with his fellow monastics, both in public and in private, with regard to that noble view which is emancipating and leads one who practices it rightly to the complete destruction of suffering. This principle of cordiality leads to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity.”

‘‘Ime kho, bhikkhave, cha sāraṇīyā dhammā piyakaraṇā garukaraṇā saṅgahāya avivādāya sāmaggiyā ekībhāvāya saṃvattanti. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, channaṃ sāraṇīyānaṃ dhammānaṃ etaṃ aggaṃ etaṃ saṅgāhikaṃ [saṅgāhakaṃ (?)] etaṃ saṅghāṭanikaṃ – yadidaṃ yāyaṃ diṭṭhi ariyā niyyānikā niyyāti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāya. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, kūṭāgārassa etaṃ aggaṃ etaṃ saṅgāhikaṃ etaṃ saṅghāṭanikaṃ yadidaṃ kūṭaṃ; evameva kho, bhikkhave, imesaṃ channaṃ sāraṇīyānaṃ dhammānaṃ etaṃ aggaṃ etaṃ saṅgāhikaṃ etaṃ saṅghāṭanikaṃ yadidaṃ yāyaṃ diṭṭhi ariyā niyyānikā niyyāti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāya.

“These, bhikkhus, are the six principles of cordiality that lead to affection, esteem, respect, cohesion, non-dispute, concord, and unity. Among these six principles of cordiality, bhikkhus, this is the foremost, this is the most inclusive, this is the most cohesive: namely, that noble view which is emancipating and leads one who practices it rightly to the complete destruction of suffering. Just as, bhikkhus, the roof-peak is the foremost, the most inclusive, the most cohesive part of a gabled house; so too, bhikkhus, among these six principles of cordiality, that noble view is the foremost, the most inclusive, the most cohesive—the one that is emancipating and leads one who practices it rightly to the complete destruction of suffering.”

493. ‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, yāyaṃ diṭṭhi ariyā niyyānikā niyyāti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāya? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu araññagato vā rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘atthi nu kho me taṃ pariyuṭṭhānaṃ ajjhattaṃ appahīnaṃ, yenāhaṃ pariyuṭṭhānena pariyuṭṭhitacitto yathābhūtaṃ nappajāneyyaṃ na passeyya’nti? Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāmarāgapariyuṭṭhito hoti, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu byāpādapariyuṭṭhito hoti, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu thīnamiddhapariyuṭṭhito hoti, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu uddhaccakukkuccapariyuṭṭhito hoti, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu vicikicchāpariyuṭṭhito hoti, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu idhalokacintāya pasuto hoti, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu paralokacintāya pasuto hoti, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. Sace, bhikkhave, bhikkhu bhaṇḍanajāto kalahajāto vivādāpanno aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudanto viharati, pariyuṭṭhitacittova hoti. So evaṃ pajānāti – ‘natthi kho me taṃ pariyuṭṭhānaṃ ajjhattaṃ appahīnaṃ, yenāhaṃ pariyuṭṭhānena pariyuṭṭhitacitto yathābhūtaṃ nappajāneyyaṃ na passeyyaṃ. Suppaṇihitaṃ me mānasaṃ saccānaṃ bodhāyā’ti. Idamassa paṭhamaṃ ñāṇaṃ adhigataṃ hoti ariyaṃ lokuttaraṃ asādhāraṇaṃ puthujjanehi.

493.“And how, bhikkhus, is that noble view emancipating and leading one who practices it rightly to the complete destruction of suffering? Here, bhikkhus, a monk, gone to the forest, or to the root of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, reflects thus: ‘Is there any underlying defilement within me unabandoned, due to which, with my mind affected by that defilement, I might not truly know or see?’ If, bhikkhus, a monk is affected by sensual lust, his mind is indeed affected. If, bhikkhus, a monk is affected by ill will, his mind is indeed affected. If, bhikkhus, a monk is affected by sloth and torpor, his mind is indeed affected. If, bhikkhus, a monk is affected by restlessness and remorse, his mind is indeed affected. If, bhikkhus, a monk is affected by doubt, his mind is indeed affected. If, bhikkhus, a monk is devoted to thinking about this world, his mind is indeed affected. If, bhikkhus, a monk is devoted to thinking about the other world, his mind is indeed affected. If, bhikkhus, a monk is given to quarrels, contention, and disputes, attacking each other with weapons of the mouth, his mind is indeed affected. He understands thus: ‘There is no underlying defilement within me unabandoned, due to which, with my mind affected by that defilement, I might not truly know or see. My mind is well composed for the awakening to the truths.’ This is the first knowledge attained by him—noble, supramundane, unshared by ordinary people.

494. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘imaṃ nu kho ahaṃ diṭṭhiṃ āsevanto bhāvento bahulīkaronto labhāmi paccattaṃ samathaṃ, labhāmi paccattaṃ nibbuti’nti? So evaṃ pajānāti – ‘imaṃ kho ahaṃ diṭṭhiṃ āsevanto bhāvento bahulīkaronto labhāmi paccattaṃ samathaṃ, labhāmi paccattaṃ nibbuti’nti. Idamassa dutiyaṃ ñāṇaṃ adhigataṃ hoti ariyaṃ lokuttaraṃ asādhāraṇaṃ puthujjanehi.

494.“Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple reflects thus: ‘When I cultivate, develop, and frequently practice this view, do I attain internal serenity, do I attain internal stilling?’ He understands thus: ‘When I cultivate, develop, and frequently practice this view, I attain internal serenity, I attain internal stilling.’ This is the second knowledge attained by him—noble, supramundane, unshared by ordinary people.”

495. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘yathā rūpāyāhaṃ diṭṭhiyā samannāgato, atthi nu kho ito bahiddhā añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā tathārūpāya diṭṭhiyā samannāgato’ti? So evaṃ pajānāti – ‘yathārūpāyāhaṃ diṭṭhiyā samannāgato, natthi ito bahiddhā añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā tathārūpāya diṭṭhiyā samannāgato’ti. Idamassa tatiyaṃ ñāṇaṃ adhigataṃ hoti ariyaṃ lokuttaraṃ asādhāraṇaṃ puthujjanehi.

495.Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple reflects thus: ‘As to the kind of view I am endowed with, is there any other ascetic or brahmin outside who is endowed with such a view?’ He understands thus: ‘As to the kind of view I am endowed with, there is no other ascetic or brahmin outside who is endowed with such a view.’ This is the third knowledge attained by him, noble, supramundane, unshared by ordinary people.

496. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘yathārūpāya dhammatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya dhammatāya samannāgato’ti. Kathaṃrūpāya ca, bhikkhave, dhammatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato? Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, diṭṭhisampannassa puggalassa – ‘kiñcāpi tathārūpiṃ āpattiṃ āpajjati, yathārūpāya āpattiyā vuṭṭhānaṃ paññāyati, atha kho naṃ khippameva satthari vā viññūsu vā sabrahmacārīsu deseti vivarati uttānīkaroti; desetvā vivaritvā uttānīkatvā āyatiṃ saṃvaraṃ āpajjati’. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, daharo kumāro mando uttānaseyyako hatthena vā pādena vā aṅgāraṃ akkamitvā khippameva paṭisaṃharati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, dhammatā esā diṭṭhisampannassa puggalassa – ‘kiñcāpi tathārūpiṃ āpattiṃ āpajjati yathārūpāya āpattiyā vuṭṭhānaṃ paññāyati, atha kho naṃ khippameva satthari vā viññūsu vā sabrahmacārīsu deseti vivarati uttānīkaroti; desetvā vivaritvā uttānīkatvā āyatiṃ saṃvaraṃ āpajjati’. So evaṃ pajānāti – ‘yathārūpāya dhammatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya dhammatāya samannāgato’ti. Idamassa catutthaṃ ñāṇaṃ adhigataṃ hoti ariyaṃ lokuttaraṃ asādhāraṇaṃ puthujjanehi.

496.Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple reflects thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a quality, and I too am endowed with such a quality.’ And what, bhikkhus, is the quality with which a person accomplished in view is endowed? It is this quality, bhikkhus, of a person accomplished in view: ‘Even if he commits such an offense for which recovery is possible, he quickly confesses, reveals, and discloses it to the Teacher or to wise spiritual companions; having confessed, revealed, and disclosed it, he undertakes restraint for the future.’ Just as, bhikkhus, a tender infant lying on its back, if he touches a hot ember with his hand or foot, quickly withdraws it; in the same way, bhikkhus, it is this quality of a person accomplished in view: ‘Even if he commits such an offense for which recovery is possible, he quickly confesses, reveals, and discloses it to the Teacher or to wise spiritual companions; having confessed, revealed, and disclosed it, he undertakes restraint for the future.’ He understands thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a quality, and I too am endowed with such a quality.’ This is the fourth knowledge attained by him, noble, supramundane, unshared by ordinary people.

497. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘yathārūpāya dhammatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya dhammatāya samannāgato’ti. Kathaṃrūpāya ca, bhikkhave, dhammatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato? Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, diṭṭhisampannassa puggalassa – ‘kiñcāpi yāni tāni sabrahmacārīnaṃ uccāvacāni kiṃkaraṇīyāni tattha ussukkaṃ āpanno hoti, atha khvāssa tibbāpekkhā hoti adhisīlasikkhāya adhicittasikkhāya adhipaññāsikkhāya’. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gāvī taruṇavacchā thambañca ālumpati vacchakañca apacinati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, dhammatā esā diṭṭhisampannassa puggalassa – ‘kiñcāpi yāni tāni sabrahmacārīnaṃ uccāvacāni kiṃkaraṇīyāni tattha ussukkaṃ āpanno hoti, atha khvāssa tibbāpekkhā hoti adhisīlasikkhāya adhicittasikkhāya adhipaññāsikkhāya’. So evaṃ pajānāti – ‘yathārūpāya dhammatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya dhammatāya samannāgato’ti. Idamassa pañcamaṃ ñāṇaṃ adhigataṃ hoti ariyaṃ lokuttaraṃ asādhāraṇaṃ puthujjanehi.

497.Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple reflects thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a quality, and I too am endowed with such a quality.’ And what, bhikkhus, is the quality with which a person accomplished in view is endowed? It is this quality, bhikkhus, of a person accomplished in view: ‘Even though he is diligent in various higher and lower tasks for his spiritual companions, nevertheless, he has a strong aspiration for the higher training in moral conduct, the higher training in mind, and the higher training in wisdom.’ Just as, bhikkhus, a cow with a young calf grazes and also keeps an eye on her calf; in the same way, bhikkhus, it is this quality of a person accomplished in view: ‘Even though he is diligent in various higher and lower tasks for his spiritual companions, nevertheless, he has a strong aspiration for the higher training in moral conduct, the higher training in mind, and the higher training in wisdom.’ He understands thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a quality, and I too am endowed with such a quality.’ This is the fifth knowledge attained by him, noble, supramundane, unshared by ordinary people.

498. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘yathārūpāya balatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya balatāya samannāgato’ti. Kathaṃrūpāya ca, bhikkhave, balatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato? Balatā esā, bhikkhave, diṭṭhisampannassa puggalassa yaṃ tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye desiyamāne aṭṭhiṃkatvā manasikatvā sabbacetasā [sabbacetaso (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.), sabbaṃ cetasā (ka.)] samannāharitvā ohitasoto dhammaṃ suṇāti. So evaṃ pajānāti – ‘yathārūpāya balatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya balatāya samannāgato’ti. Idamassa chaṭṭhaṃ ñāṇaṃ adhigataṃ hoti ariyaṃ lokuttaraṃ asādhāraṇaṃ puthujjanehi.

498.Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple reflects thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a strength, and I too am endowed with such a strength.’ And what, bhikkhus, is the strength with which a person accomplished in view is endowed? It is this strength, bhikkhus, of a person accomplished in view: when the Dhamma and Discipline proclaimed by the Tathāgata is being taught, he gives it due attention, focuses his mind, applies all his awareness, and listens to the Dhamma with an attentive ear. He understands thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a strength, and I too am endowed with such a strength.’ This is the sixth knowledge attained by him, noble, supramundane, unshared by ordinary people.

499. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘yathārūpāya balatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya balatāya samannāgato’ti. Kathaṃrūpāya ca, bhikkhave, balatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato? Balatā esā, bhikkhave, diṭṭhisampannassa puggalassa yaṃ tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye desiyamāne labhati atthavedaṃ, labhati dhammavedaṃ, labhati dhammūpasaṃhitaṃ pāmojjaṃ. So evaṃ pajānāti – ‘yathārūpāya balatāya diṭṭhisampanno puggalo samannāgato, ahampi tathārūpāya balatāya samannāgato’ti. Idamassa sattamaṃ ñāṇaṃ adhigataṃ hoti ariyaṃ lokuttaraṃ asādhāraṇaṃ puthujjanehi.

499.Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple reflects thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a strength, and I too am endowed with such a strength.’ And what, bhikkhus, is the strength with which a person accomplished in view is endowed? It is this strength, bhikkhus, of a person accomplished in view: when the Dhamma and Discipline proclaimed by the Tathāgata is being taught, he gains understanding of the meaning, gains understanding of the Dhamma, gains joy connected with the Dhamma. He understands thus: ‘A person accomplished in view is endowed with such a strength, and I too am endowed with such a strength.’ This is the seventh knowledge attained by him, noble, supramundane, unshared by ordinary people.

500. ‘‘Evaṃ sattaṅgasamannāgatassa kho, bhikkhave, ariyasāvakassa dhammatā susamanniṭṭhā hoti sotāpattiphalasacchikiriyāya. Evaṃ sattaṅgasamannāgato kho, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako sotāpattiphalasamannāgato hotī’’ti.

500.When a noble disciple, bhikkhus, is endowed with these seven factors, it is naturally well-established for the realization of the fruit of stream-entry. A noble disciple endowed with these seven factors, bhikkhus, is one who is endowed with the fruit of stream-entry.

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

This the Blessed One said. The bhikkhus were pleased and rejoiced at the Blessed One’s words.

Kosambiyasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ aṭṭhamaṃ.

The Kosambī Sutta, the eighth, is concluded.
