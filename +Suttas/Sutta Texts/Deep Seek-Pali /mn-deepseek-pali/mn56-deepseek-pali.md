# 56. Upālisuttaṃ

## 56. The Upāli Sutta

56. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā nāḷandāyaṃ viharati pāvārikambavane. Tena kho pana samayena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto [nāthaputto (sī.), nātaputto (pī.)] nāḷandāyaṃ paṭivasati mahatiyā nigaṇṭhaparisāya saddhiṃ. Atha kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho nāḷandāyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto yena pāvārikambavanaṃ yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitaṃ kho dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘saṃvijjanti kho, tapassi [dīghatapassi (syā. kaṃ. ka.)], āsanāni; sace ākaṅkhasi nisīdā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho aññataraṃ nīcaṃ āsanaṃ gahetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘kati pana, tapassi, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto kammāni paññapeti pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā’’ti?

56.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Nāḷandā in Pāvārika's mango grove. Now at that time Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta was residing at Nāḷandā with a large assembly of Nigaṇṭhas. Then Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha, having wandered for alms in Nāḷandā and returned from his alms-round after the meal, approached the Pāvārika's mango grove where the Blessed One was staying. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. Having exchanged courteous greetings and amiable talk, he stood to one side. Standing to one side, the Blessed One said to Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha: “There are seats available, Tapassī; if you wish, sit down.” When this was said, Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha took a low seat and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Blessed One said to Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha: “How many actions, Tapassī, does Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta teach for the doing of evil deeds, for the occurrence of evil deeds?”

‘‘Na kho, āvuso gotama, āciṇṇaṃ nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa ‘kammaṃ, kamma’nti paññapetuṃ; ‘daṇḍaṃ, daṇḍa’nti kho, āvuso gotama, āciṇṇaṃ nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa paññapetu’’nti.

“Friend Gotama, it is not the habit of Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta to declare ‘action, action’; rather, friend Gotama, it is the habit of Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta to declare ‘rod, rod’.”

‘‘Kati pana, tapassi, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto daṇḍāni paññapeti pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā’’ti?

“But, Tapassī, how many rods does Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta teach for the doing of evil deeds, for the occurrence of evil deeds?”

‘‘Tīṇi kho, āvuso gotama, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto daṇḍāni paññapeti pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyāti, seyyathidaṃ – kāyadaṇḍaṃ, vacīdaṇḍaṃ, manodaṇḍa’’nti.

“Friend Gotama, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta teaches three rods for the doing of evil deeds and for the occurrence of evil deeds, namely: the bodily rod, the verbal rod, and the mental rod.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana, tapassi, aññadeva kāyadaṇḍaṃ, aññaṃ vacīdaṇḍaṃ, aññaṃ manodaṇḍa’’nti?

“But, Tapassī, is the bodily rod one thing, the verbal rod another, and the mental rod another?”

‘‘Aññadeva, āvuso gotama, kāyadaṇḍaṃ, aññaṃ vacīdaṇḍaṃ, aññaṃ manodaṇḍa’’nti.

“Indeed, friend Gotama, the bodily rod is one thing, the verbal rod another, and the mental rod yet another.”

‘‘Imesaṃ pana, tapassi, tiṇṇaṃ daṇḍānaṃ evaṃ paṭivibhattānaṃ evaṃ paṭivisiṭṭhānaṃ katamaṃ daṇḍaṃ nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto mahāsāvajjataraṃ paññapeti pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, yadi vā kāyadaṇḍaṃ, yadi vā vacīdaṇḍaṃ, yadi vā manodaṇḍa’’nti?

“Among these three rods, Tapassī, thus distinguished and differentiated, which rod does Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta declare to be the most blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds, for the occurrence of evil deeds—whether the bodily rod, the verbal rod, or the mental rod?”

‘‘Imesaṃ kho, āvuso gotama, tiṇṇaṃ daṇḍānaṃ evaṃ paṭivibhattānaṃ evaṃ paṭivisiṭṭhānaṃ kāyadaṇḍaṃ nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto mahāsāvajjataraṃ paññapeti pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā vacīdaṇḍaṃ, no tathā manodaṇḍa’’nti.

“Among these three rods, friend Gotama, thus distinguished and differentiated, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta declares the bodily rod to be the most blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds and the occurrence of evil deeds—not so the verbal rod, not so the mental rod.”

‘‘Kāyadaṇḍanti, tapassi, vadesi’’?

“Tapassī, do you speak of the bodily rod?”

‘‘Kāyadaṇḍanti, āvuso gotama, vadāmi’’.

“Friend Gotama, I speak of the bodily rod.”

‘‘Kāyadaṇḍanti, tapassi, vadesi’’?

“Tapassī, do you speak of the bodily rod?”

‘‘Kāyadaṇḍanti, āvuso gotama, vadāmi’’.

“Friend Gotama, I speak of the bodily rod.”

‘‘Kāyadaṇḍanti, tapassi, vadesi’’?

“Tapassī, do you speak of the bodily rod?”

‘‘Kāyadaṇḍanti, āvuso gotama, vadāmī’’ti.

“Friend Gotama, I speak of the bodily rod.”

Itiha bhagavā dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ imasmiṃ kathāvatthusmiṃ yāvatatiyakaṃ patiṭṭhāpesi.

Thus, the Blessed One established Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha in this topic of discussion up to the third time.

57. Evaṃ vutte, dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tvaṃ panāvuso gotama, kati daṇḍāni paññapesi pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā’’ti?

57.When this was said, Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha said to the Blessed One: “But you, friend Gotama, how many rods do you teach for the doing of evil deeds, for the occurrence of evil deeds?”

‘‘Na kho, tapassi, āciṇṇaṃ tathāgatassa ‘daṇḍaṃ, daṇḍa’nti paññapetuṃ; ‘kammaṃ, kamma’nti kho, tapassi, āciṇṇaṃ tathāgatassa paññapetu’’nti?

“It is not the habit of the Tathāgata, Tapassī, to declare ‘rod, rod.’ It is the habit of the Tathāgata, Tapassī, to declare ‘kamma, kamma’.”

‘‘Tvaṃ panāvuso gotama, kati kammāni paññapesi pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā’’ti?

“But you, friend Gotama, how many kamma do you teach for the doing of evil deeds and for the occurrence of evil deeds?”

‘‘Tīṇi kho ahaṃ, tapassi, kammāni paññapemi pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, seyyathidaṃ – kāyakammaṃ, vacīkammaṃ, manokamma’’nti.

“I declare three kamma, Tapassī, for the doing of evil deeds and the occurrence of evil deeds, namely: bodily kamma, verbal kamma, and mental kamma.”

‘‘Kiṃ panāvuso gotama, aññadeva kāyakammaṃ, aññaṃ vacīkammaṃ, aññaṃ manokamma’’nti?

“But, friend Gotama, is bodily kamma one thing, verbal kamma another, and mental kamma yet another?”

‘‘Aññadeva, tapassi, kāyakammaṃ, aññaṃ vacīkammaṃ, aññaṃ manokamma’’nti.

“Indeed, Tapassī, bodily kamma is one thing, verbal kamma another, and mental kamma yet another.”

‘‘Imesaṃ panāvuso gotama, tiṇṇaṃ kammānaṃ evaṃ paṭivibhattānaṃ evaṃ paṭivisiṭṭhānaṃ katamaṃ kammaṃ mahāsāvajjataraṃ paññapesi pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, yadi vā kāyakammaṃ, yadi vā vacīkammaṃ, yadi vā manokamma’’nti?

“Now, friend Gotama, among these three kamma, thus distinguished and differentiated, which kamma do you declare to be the most blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds and the occurrence of evil deeds—whether bodily kamma, verbal kamma, or mental kamma?”

‘‘Imesaṃ kho ahaṃ, tapassi, tiṇṇaṃ kammānaṃ evaṃ paṭivibhattānaṃ evaṃ paṭivisiṭṭhānaṃ manokammaṃ mahāsāvajjataraṃ paññapemi pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā kāyakammaṃ, no tathā vacīkamma’’nti.

“Among these three kamma, Tapassī, thus distinguished and differentiated, I declare mental kamma to be the most blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds and the occurrence of evil deeds—not so bodily kamma, not so verbal kamma.”

‘‘Manokammanti, āvuso gotama, vadesi’’?

“Friend Gotama, do you speak of mental kamma?”

‘‘Manokammanti, tapassi, vadāmi’’.

“Tapassī, I speak of mental kamma.”

‘‘Manokammanti, āvuso gotama, vadesi’’?

“Friend Gotama, do you speak of mental kamma?”

‘‘Manokammanti, tapassi, vadāmi’’.

“Tapassī, I speak of mental kamma.”

‘‘Manokammanti, āvuso gotama, vadesi’’?

“Friend Gotama, do you speak of mental kamma?”

‘‘Manokammanti, tapassi, vadāmī’’ti.

“Tapassī, I speak of mental kamma.”

Itiha dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho bhagavantaṃ imasmiṃ kathāvatthusmiṃ yāvatatiyakaṃ patiṭṭhāpetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto tenupasaṅkami.

Thus, Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha, having established the Blessed One in this topic of discussion up to the third time, rose from his seat and approached Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta.

58. Tena kho pana samayena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto mahatiyā gihiparisāya saddhiṃ nisinno hoti bālakiniyā parisāya upālipamukhāya. Addasā kho nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ; disvāna dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘handa, kuto nu tvaṃ, tapassi, āgacchasi divā divassā’’ti? ‘‘Ito hi kho ahaṃ, bhante, āgacchāmi samaṇassa gotamassa santikā’’ti. ‘‘Ahu pana te, tapassi, samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti? ‘‘Ahu kho me, bhante, samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana te, tapassi, ahu samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti? Atha kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho yāvatako ahosi bhagavatā saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo taṃ sabbaṃ nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa ārocesi. Evaṃ vutte, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sādhu sādhu, tapassi! Yathā taṃ sutavatā sāvakena sammadeva satthusāsanaṃ ājānantena evameva dīghatapassinā nigaṇṭhena samaṇassa gotamassa byākataṃ. Kiñhi sobhati chavo manodaṇḍo imassa evaṃ oḷārikassa kāyadaṇḍassa upanidhāya! Atha kho kāyadaṇḍova mahāsāvajjataro pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā vacīdaṇḍo, no tathā manodaṇḍo’’ti.

58.At that time, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta was seated with a large assembly of householders, along with the assembly from Bālakini headed by Upāli. Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta saw Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha coming from afar. Upon seeing him, he said to Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha: “Well then, from where do you come, Tapassī, in the middle of the day?” “I come, venerable sir, from the presence of the ascetic Gotama.” “Did you have any conversation at all, Tapassī, with the ascetic Gotama?” “I did, venerable sir, have some conversation with the ascetic Gotama.” “And what, Tapassī, was your conversation with the ascetic Gotama like?” Then Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha recounted to Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta the entire conversation he had had with the Blessed One. When this was said, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta said to Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha: “Good, good, Tapassī! Just as a learned disciple, knowing the Teacher’s instruction well, should explain it, so too has Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha explained the words of the ascetic Gotama. For what indeed is the wretched mental rod compared to this gross bodily rod! Indeed, the bodily rod is far more blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds and the occurrence of evil deeds; not so the verbal rod, nor the mental rod.”

59. Evaṃ vutte, upāli gahapati nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sādhu sādhu, bhante dīghatapassī [tapassī (sī. pī.)]! Yathā taṃ sutavatā sāvakena sammadeva satthusāsanaṃ ājānantena evamevaṃ bhadantena tapassinā samaṇassa gotamassa byākataṃ. Kiñhi sobhati chavo manodaṇḍo imassa evaṃ oḷārikassa kāyadaṇḍassa upanidhāya! Atha kho kāyadaṇḍova mahāsāvajjataro pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā vacīdaṇḍo, no tathā manodaṇḍo. Handa cāhaṃ, bhante, gacchāmi samaṇassa gotamassa imasmiṃ kathāvatthusmiṃ vādaṃ āropessāmi. Sace me samaṇo gotamo tathā patiṭṭhahissati yathā bhadantena tapassinā patiṭṭhāpitaṃ; seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso dīghalomikaṃ eḷakaṃ lomesu gahetvā ākaḍḍheyya parikaḍḍheyya samparikaḍḍheyya, evamevāhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ vādena vādaṃ ākaḍḍhissāmi parikaḍḍhissāmi samparikaḍḍhissāmi. Seyyathāpi nāma balavā soṇḍikākammakāro mahantaṃ soṇḍikākilañjaṃ gambhīre udakarahade pakkhipitvā kaṇṇe gahetvā ākaḍḍheyya parikaḍḍheyya samparikaḍḍheyya, evamevāhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ vādena vādaṃ ākaḍḍhissāmi parikaḍḍhissāmi samparikaḍḍhissāmi. Seyyathāpi nāma balavā soṇḍikādhutto vālaṃ [thālaṃ (ka.)] kaṇṇe gahetvā odhuneyya niddhuneyya nipphoṭeyya [nicchādeyya (sī. pī. ka.), niccoṭeyya (ka.), nippoṭheyya (syā. kaṃ.)], evamevāhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ vādena vādaṃ odhunissāmi niddhunissāmi nipphoṭessāmi. Seyyathāpi nāma kuñjaro saṭṭhihāyano gambhīraṃ pokkharaṇiṃ ogāhetvā sāṇadhovikaṃ nāma kīḷitajātaṃ kīḷati, evamevāhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ sāṇadhovikaṃ maññe kīḷitajātaṃ kīḷissāmi. Handa cāhaṃ, bhante, gacchāmi samaṇassa gotamassa imasmiṃ kathāvatthusmiṃ vādaṃ āropessāmī’’ti. ‘‘Gaccha tvaṃ, gahapati, samaṇassa gotamassa imasmiṃ kathāvatthusmiṃ vādaṃ āropehi. Ahaṃ vā hi, gahapati, samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropeyyaṃ, dīghatapassī vā nigaṇṭho, tvaṃ vā’’ti.

59.When this was said, the householder Upāli said to Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Excellent, excellent, venerable Tapassī! Just as a learned disciple who rightly understands the Teacher’s instruction, so too has the venerable Tapassī rightly explained the words of the ascetic Gotama. For what indeed is the wretched mental rod compared to this gross bodily rod! Indeed, the bodily rod is far more blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds and the occurrence of evil deeds; not so the verbal rod, nor the mental rod. Come now, venerable sir, I will go and engage the ascetic Gotama in debate in this matter. If the ascetic Gotama takes the same position as the venerable Tapassī has established, then just as a strong man might seize a long-haired goat by the hair and drag it back and forth, so too will I drag the ascetic Gotama back and forth in debate. Just as a strong brewer’s worker might plunge a large brewer’s sieve into a deep pool of water and, taking it by the rim, drag it back and forth, so too will I drag the ascetic Gotama back and forth in debate. Just as a strong brewer’s rogue might take a hair-sieve by the ears and shake it, beat it, and knock it about, so too will I shake, beat, and knock about the ascetic Gotama in debate. Just as a sixty-year-old elephant might plunge into a deep lotus pond and play a game called ‘washerman’, so too will I play a game called ‘washerman’ with the ascetic Gotama. Come now, venerable sir, I will go and engage the ascetic Gotama in debate in this matter.” “Go, householder, and engage the ascetic Gotama in debate in this matter. For either I, householder, or Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha, or you should engage the ascetic Gotama in debate.”

60. Evaṃ vutte, dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘na kho metaṃ, bhante, ruccati yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropeyya. Samaṇo hi, bhante, gotamo māyāvī āvaṭṭaniṃ māyaṃ jānāti yāya aññatitthiyānaṃ sāvake āvaṭṭetī’’ti. ‘‘Aṭṭhānaṃ kho etaṃ, tapassi, anavakāso yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyya. Ṭhānañca kho etaṃ vijjati yaṃ samaṇo gotamo upālissa gahapatissa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyya. Gaccha, tvaṃ, gahapati, samaṇassa gotamassa imasmiṃ kathāvatthusmiṃ vādaṃ āropehi. Ahaṃ vā hi, gahapati, samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropeyyaṃ, dīghatapassī vā nigaṇṭho, tvaṃ vā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho dīghatapassī…pe… tatiyampi kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘na kho metaṃ, bhante, ruccati yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropeyya. Samaṇo hi, bhante, gotamo māyāvī āvaṭṭaniṃ māyaṃ jānāti yāya aññatitthiyānaṃ sāvake āvaṭṭetī’’ti. ‘‘Aṭṭhānaṃ kho etaṃ, tapassi, anavakāso yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyya. Ṭhānañca kho etaṃ vijjati yaṃ samaṇo gotamo upālissa gahapatissa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyya. Gaccha tvaṃ, gahapati, samaṇassa gotamassa imasmiṃ kathāvatthusmiṃ vādaṃ āropehi. Ahaṃ vā hi, gahapati, samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropeyyaṃ, dīghatapassī vā nigaṇṭho, tvaṃ vā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho upāli gahapati nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa paṭissutvā uṭṭhāyāsanā nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā yena pāvārikambavanaṃ yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho upāli gahapati bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘āgamā nu khvidha, bhante, dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho’’ti?

60. When this was said, the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Venerable sir, it does not please me that the householder Upāli should engage the ascetic Gotama in debate. For the ascetic Gotama, venerable sir, is a deceiver; he knows a converting trick by which he converts the disciples of other sects.” “It is impossible, ascetic, there is no chance that the householder Upāli could go over to the ascetic Gotama’s following. But it is possible that the ascetic Gotama could go over to the householder Upāli’s following. Go, householder, and engage the ascetic Gotama in debate on this matter. For either I, householder, or the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī, or you, should engage the ascetic Gotama in debate.” A second time the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī … A third time the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Venerable sir, it does not please me that the householder Upāli should engage the ascetic Gotama in debate. For the ascetic Gotama, venerable sir, is a deceiver; he knows a converting trick by which he converts the disciples of other sects.” “It is impossible, ascetic, there is no chance that the householder Upāli could go over to the ascetic Gotama’s following. But it is possible that the ascetic Gotama could go over to the householder Upāli’s following. Go, householder, and engage the ascetic Gotama in debate on this matter. For either I, householder, or the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī, or you, should engage the ascetic Gotama in debate.” “Very well, venerable sir,” the householder Upāli replied to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta. Then he rose from his seat, paid homage to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, and, keeping his right side toward him, circumambulated him. Then he went to Pāvārika’s Mango Grove, where the Blessed One was. He approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the householder Upāli asked the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, has the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī come here?”

‘‘Āgamā khvidha, gahapati, dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho’’ti.

“Has Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha arrived here, householder?”

‘‘Ahu kho pana te, bhante, dīghatapassinā nigaṇṭhena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti?

“But, venerable sir, did you have any conversation with Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha?”

‘‘Ahu kho me, gahapati, dīghatapassinā nigaṇṭhena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti.

“Householder, I had a certain conversation with Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha.”

‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana te, bhante, ahu dīghatapassinā nigaṇṭhena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti?

“But venerable sir, what was your conversation with Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha like?”

Atha kho bhagavā yāvatako ahosi dīghatapassinā nigaṇṭhena saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo taṃ sabbaṃ upālissa gahapatissa ārocesi.

Then the Blessed One told the householder Upāli everything that had been conversed about with Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha.

61. Evaṃ vutte, upāli gahapati bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sādhu sādhu, bhante tapassī! Yathā taṃ sutavatā sāvakena sammadeva satthusāsanaṃ ājānantena evamevaṃ dīghatapassinā nigaṇṭhena bhagavato byākataṃ. Kiñhi sobhati chavo manodaṇḍo imassa evaṃ oḷārikassa kāyadaṇḍassa upanidhāya? Atha kho kāyadaṇḍova mahāsāvajjataro pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā vacīdaṇḍo, no tathā manodaṇḍo’’ti. ‘‘Sace kho tvaṃ, gahapati, sacce patiṭṭhāya manteyyāsi siyā no ettha kathāsallāpo’’ti. ‘‘Sacce ahaṃ, bhante, patiṭṭhāya mantessāmi; hotu no ettha kathāsallāpo’’ti.

61.When this was said, the householder Upāli said to the Blessed One: “Excellent, excellent, Venerable Sir! As it should be for a learned disciple who rightly understands the Teacher’s instruction, so has Dīgha Tapassī the Nigaṇṭha explained the words of the Blessed One. For what indeed is the wretched mental rod compared to this gross bodily rod! Indeed, the bodily rod is far more blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds, for the occurrence of evil deeds; not so the verbal rod, not so the mental rod.” “If you, householder, were to speak established in truth, there might be a discussion between us.” “I will speak established in truth, Venerable Sir; let there be a discussion between us.”

62. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, idhassa nigaṇṭho ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno sītodakapaṭikkhitto uṇhodakapaṭisevī. So sītodakaṃ alabhamāno kālaṅkareyya. Imassa pana, gahapati, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto katthūpapattiṃ paññapetī’’ti?

62.“What do you think, householder? Suppose here a Nigaṇṭha is sick, suffering, gravely ill, rejecting cold water and resorting to hot water. If he cannot obtain cold water, he might die. Now, householder, where would Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta declare his reappearance?”

‘‘Atthi, bhante, manosattā nāma devā tattha so upapajjati’’.

“Venerable sir, there are gods called mind-beings, and he reappears there.”

‘‘Taṃ kissa hetu’’?

“What is the reason for that?”

‘‘Asu hi, bhante, manopaṭibaddho kālaṅkarotī’’ti.

“Venerable sir, indeed he dies bound to the mind.”

‘‘Manasi karohi, gahapati [gahapati gahapati manasi karohi (sī. syā. kaṃ.), gahapati manasi karohi (ka.), gahapati gahapati (pī.)], manasi karitvā kho, gahapati, byākarohi. Na kho te sandhiyati purimena vā pacchimaṃ, pacchimena vā purimaṃ. Bhāsitā kho pana te, gahapati, esā vācā – ‘sacce ahaṃ, bhante, patiṭṭhāya mantessāmi, hotu no ettha kathāsallāpo’’’ti. ‘‘Kiñcāpi, bhante, bhagavā evamāha, atha kho kāyadaṇḍova mahāsāvajjataro pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā vacīdaṇḍo, no tathā manodaṇḍo’’ti.

“Attend, householder, attend carefully, householder, and then reply. Your earlier and later statements do not cohere, nor does the later with the earlier. Yet you have spoken this word, householder: ‘Venerable sir, I will speak based on truth; let there be a discussion between us.’ ” “Although the Blessed One says this, venerable sir, still the bodily rod is more greatly blameworthy for the doing of evil deeds and for the occurrence of evil deeds, than is the verbal rod, or the mental rod.”

63. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, idhassa nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto cātuyāmasaṃvarasaṃvuto sabbavārivārito sabbavāriyutto sabbavāridhuto sabbavāriphuṭo. So abhikkamanto paṭikkamanto bahū khuddake pāṇe saṅghātaṃ āpādeti. Imassa pana, gahapati, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto kaṃ vipākaṃ paññapetī’’ti?

63.“What do you think, householder? Suppose here there is a Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, restrained by the fourfold restraint, completely restrained by all restraints, completely bound by all bonds, completely cleansed by all cleansings, completely pervaded by all pervading. While walking back and forth, he causes the destruction of many tiny living beings. What ripening, householder, does Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta declare for this?”

‘‘Asañcetanikaṃ, bhante, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto no mahāsāvajjaṃ paññapetī’’ti.

“Venerable sir, Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta does not declare unintentional actions to be greatly blameworthy.”

‘‘Sace pana, gahapati, cetetī’’ti?

“But if, householder, he intends?”

‘‘Mahāsāvajjaṃ, bhante, hotī’’ti.

“Venerable sir, it is greatly blameworthy.”

‘‘Cetanaṃ pana, gahapati, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto kismiṃ paññapetī’’ti?

“But, householder, in what does Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta declare intention?”

‘‘Manodaṇḍasmiṃ, bhante’’ti.

“Venerable sir, in mental action.”

‘‘Manasi karohi, gahapati, manasi karitvā kho, gahapati, byākarohi. Na kho te sandhiyati purimena vā pacchimaṃ, pacchimena vā purimaṃ. Bhāsitā kho pana te, gahapati, esā vācā – ‘sacce ahaṃ, bhante, patiṭṭhāya mantessāmi; hotu no ettha kathāsallāpo’’’ti. ‘‘Kiñcāpi, bhante, bhagavā evamāha, atha kho kāyadaṇḍova mahāsāvajjataro pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā vacīdaṇḍo, no tathā manodaṇḍo’’ti.

“Attend closely, householder, attend closely, and then reply. Your earlier and later statements do not cohere; nor does the later with the earlier. Yet, householder, this statement was made by you: ‘Venerable sir, I shall speak having taken my stand on truth; let there be a conversation between us here.’" “Although the Blessed One says this, venerable sir, still bodily action is more gravely culpable for the performance of unwholesome kamma and for the perpetuation of unwholesome kamma, not so verbal action, not so mental action.”

64. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, ayaṃ nāḷandā iddhā ceva phītā ca bahujanā ākiṇṇamanussā’’ti?

64.“What do you think, householder? Is this Nāḷandā prosperous and flourishing, populous and teeming with people?”

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante, ayaṃ nāḷandā iddhā ceva phītā ca bahujanā ākiṇṇamanussā’’ti.

“Yes, venerable sir, this Nāḷandā is prosperous and flourishing, populous and teeming with people.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, idha puriso āgaccheyya ukkhittāsiko. So evaṃ vadeyya – ‘ahaṃ yāvatikā imissā nāḷandāya pāṇā te ekena khaṇena ekena muhuttena ekaṃ maṃsakhalaṃ ekaṃ maṃsapuñjaṃ karissāmī’ti. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, pahoti nu kho so puriso yāvatikā imissā nāḷandāya pāṇā te ekena khaṇena ekena muhuttena ekaṃ maṃsakhalaṃ ekaṃ maṃsapuñjaṃ kātu’’nti?

“What do you think, householder? Suppose a man were to come here with a drawn sword and say: ‘I will make all the living beings in this Nāḷandā into a single mass of flesh, a single heap of flesh, in a single instant, in a single moment.’ What do you think, householder, could that man make all the living beings in this Nāḷandā into a single mass of flesh, a single heap of flesh, in a single instant, in a single moment?”

‘‘Dasapi, bhante, purisā, vīsampi, bhante, purisā, tiṃsampi, bhante, purisā, cattārīsampi, bhante, purisā, paññāsampi, bhante, purisā nappahonti yāvatikā imissā nāḷandāya pāṇā te ekena khaṇena ekena muhuttena ekaṃ maṃsakhalaṃ ekaṃ maṃsapuñjaṃ kātuṃ. Kiñhi sobhati eko chavo puriso’’ti!

“Venerable sir, not even ten men, nor twenty, nor thirty, nor forty, nor fifty men could manage to make all the living beings in this Nāḷandā into a single mass of flesh, a single heap of flesh, in a single instant, in a single moment. What good is one mere man?”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, idha āgaccheyya samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā iddhimā cetovasippatto. So evaṃ vadeyya – ‘ahaṃ imaṃ nāḷandaṃ ekena manopadosena bhasmaṃ karissāmī’ti. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, pahoti nu kho so samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā iddhimā cetovasippatto imaṃ nāḷandaṃ ekena manopadosena bhasmaṃ kātu’’nti?

“What do you think, householder? Suppose a recluse or brahmin endowed with psychic power, who has attained mastery of mind, were to come here and say: ‘I will reduce this Nāḷandā to ashes with a single thought of ill will.’ What do you think, householder, would that recluse or brahmin endowed with psychic power, who has attained mastery of mind, be able to reduce this Nāḷandā to ashes with a single thought of ill will?”

‘‘Dasapi, bhante, nāḷandā, vīsampi nāḷandā, tiṃsampi nāḷandā, cattārīsampi nāḷandā, paññāsampi nāḷandā pahoti so samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā iddhimā cetovasippatto ekena manopadosena bhasmaṃ kātuṃ. Kiñhi sobhati ekā chavā nāḷandā’’ti!

“Venerable sir, that recluse or brahmin endowed with psychic power, who has attained mastery of mind, could reduce to ashes ten Nāḷandās, twenty Nāḷandās, thirty Nāḷandās, forty Nāḷandās, or even fifty Nāḷandās with a single thought of ill will. What good is one mere Nāḷandā?”

‘‘Manasi karohi, gahapati, manasi karitvā kho, gahapati, byākarohi. Na kho te sandhiyati purimena vā pacchimaṃ, pacchimena vā purimaṃ. Bhāsitā kho pana te, gahapati, esā vācā – ‘sacce ahaṃ, bhante, patiṭṭhāya mantessāmi; hotu no ettha kathāsallāpo’’’ti.

“Attend closely, householder, attend closely, and then reply. Your earlier and later statements do not cohere; nor does the later with the earlier. Yet, householder, this statement was made by you: ‘Venerable sir, I shall speak having taken my stand on truth; let there be a conversation between us here.’"

‘‘Kiñcāpi, bhante, bhagavā evamāha, atha kho kāyadaṇḍova mahāsāvajjataro pāpassa kammassa kiriyāya pāpassa kammassa pavattiyā, no tathā vacīdaṇḍo, no tathā manodaṇḍo’’ti.

“Although the Blessed One says this, venerable sir, still bodily action is more gravely culpable for the performance of unwholesome kamma and for the perpetuation of unwholesome kamma, not so verbal action, not so mental action.”

65. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, sutaṃ te daṇḍakīraññaṃ [daṇḍakāraññaṃ (sī. pī.)] kāliṅgāraññaṃ majjhāraññaṃ [mejjhāraññaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] mātaṅgāraññaṃ araññaṃ araññabhūta’’nti?

65.“What do you think, householder? Have you heard of the Daṇḍaka wilderness, the Kāliṅga wilderness, the Majjha wilderness, the Mātaṅga wilderness, a wilderness that is truly a wilderness?”

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante, sutaṃ me daṇḍakīraññaṃ kāliṅgāraññaṃ majjhāraññaṃ mātaṅgāraññaṃ araññaṃ araññabhūta’’nti.

“Yes, venerable sir, I have heard that the Daṇḍaka wilderness, the Kāliṅga wilderness, the Majjha wilderness, and the Mātaṅga wilderness became wildernesses.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gahapati, kinti te sutaṃ kena taṃ daṇḍakīraññaṃ kāliṅgāraññaṃ majjhāraññaṃ mātaṅgāraññaṃ araññaṃ araññabhūta’’nti?

“What do you think, householder? How have you heard that the Daṇḍaka wilderness, the Kāliṅga wilderness, the Majjha wilderness, and the Mātaṅga wilderness became wildernesses?”

‘‘Sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante, isīnaṃ manopadosena taṃ daṇḍakīraññaṃ kāliṅgāraññaṃ majjhāraññaṃ mātaṅgāraññaṃ araññaṃ araññabhūta’’nti.

“I have heard, venerable sir, that through the mental corruption of seers, the Daṇḍaka wilderness, the Kāliṅga wilderness, the Majjha wilderness, and the Mātaṅga wilderness became wildernesses.”

‘‘Manasi karohi, gahapati, manasi karitvā kho, gahapati, byākarohi. Na kho te sandhiyati purimena vā pacchimaṃ, pacchimena vā purimaṃ. Bhāsitā kho pana te, gahapati, esā vācā – ‘sacce ahaṃ, bhante, patiṭṭhāya mantessāmi; hotu no ettha kathāsallāpo’’’ti.

“Attend closely, householder, attend closely, and then reply. Your earlier and later statements do not cohere; nor does the later with the earlier. Yet, householder, this statement was made by you: ‘Venerable sir, I shall speak having taken my stand on truth; let there be a conversation between us here.’"

66. ‘‘Purimenevāhaṃ, bhante, opammena bhagavato attamano abhiraddho. Api cāhaṃ imāni bhagavato vicitrāni pañhapaṭibhānāni sotukāmo, evāhaṃ bhagavantaṃ paccanīkaṃ kātabbaṃ amaññissaṃ. Abhikkantaṃ, bhante, abhikkantaṃ, bhante! Seyyathāpi, bhante, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya – cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantīti; evamevaṃ bhagavatā anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ, bhante, bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsakaṃ maṃ bhagavā dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti.

66.“Venerable sir, I was already pleased and delighted by the Blessed One’s earlier simile. Yet I wished to hear these diverse and brilliant replies of the Blessed One, thinking I should make a rejoinder to him. Excellent, venerable sir, excellent! Just as one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the path to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness so that those with eyes may see forms—so too has the Blessed One made the Dhamma clear in many ways. I go for refuge, venerable sir, to the Blessed One, to the Dhamma, and to the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. May the Blessed One remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forward, for as long as life lasts.”

67. ‘‘Anuviccakāraṃ kho, gahapati, karohi, anuviccakāro tumhādisānaṃ ñātamanussānaṃ sādhu hotī’’ti. ‘‘Imināpāhaṃ, bhante, bhagavato bhiyyosomattāya attamano abhiraddho yaṃ maṃ bhagavā evamāha – ‘anuviccakāraṃ kho, gahapati, karohi, anuviccakāro tumhādisānaṃ ñātamanussānaṃ sādhu hotī’ti. Mañhi, bhante, aññatitthiyā sāvakaṃ labhitvā kevalakappaṃ nāḷandaṃ paṭākaṃ parihareyyuṃ – ‘upāli amhākaṃ gahapati sāvakattaṃ upagato’ti. Atha ca pana maṃ bhagavā evamāha – ‘anuviccakāraṃ kho, gahapati, karohi, anuviccakāro tumhādisānaṃ ñātamanussānaṃ sādhu hotī’ti. Esāhaṃ, bhante, dutiyampi bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsakaṃ maṃ bhagavā dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti.

67.“Householder, make an investigation. Making an investigation is good for people like you, who are known to be thoughtful.” “Venerable sir, by this, I am even more delighted and gratified toward the Blessed One, that the Blessed One said to me: ‘Householder, make an investigation. Making an investigation is good for people like you, who are known to be thoughtful.’ For, venerable sir, if the followers of other sects were to gain me as a disciple, they would carry a banner all over Nāḷanda, proclaiming: ‘Upāli the householder has come over to our discipleship!’ But the Blessed One tells me: ‘Householder, make an investigation. Making an investigation is good for people like you, who are known to be thoughtful.’ And so, venerable sir, for the second time, I go for refuge to the Blessed One, to the Dhamma, and to the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. May the Blessed One remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forth, for as long as life lasts.”

68. ‘‘Dīgharattaṃ kho te, gahapati, nigaṇṭhānaṃ opānabhūtaṃ kulaṃ yena nesaṃ upagatānaṃ piṇḍakaṃ dātabbaṃ maññeyyāsī’’ti. ‘‘Imināpāhaṃ, bhante, bhagavato bhiyyosomattāya attamano abhiraddho yaṃ maṃ bhagavā evamāha – ‘dīgharattaṃ kho te, gahapati, nigaṇṭhānaṃ opānabhūtaṃ kulaṃ yena nesaṃ upagatānaṃ piṇḍakaṃ dātabbaṃ maññeyyāsī’ti. Sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante, samaṇo gotamo evamāha – ‘mayhameva dānaṃ dātabbaṃ, nāññesaṃ dānaṃ dātabbaṃ; mayhameva sāvakānaṃ dānaṃ dātabbaṃ, nāññesaṃ sāvakānaṃ dānaṃ dātabbaṃ; mayhameva dinnaṃ mahapphalaṃ, nāññesaṃ dinnaṃ mahapphalaṃ; mayhameva sāvakānaṃ dinnaṃ mahapphalaṃ, nāññesaṃ sāvakānaṃ dinnaṃ mahapphala’nti. Atha ca pana maṃ bhagavā nigaṇṭhesupi dāne samādapeti. Api ca, bhante, mayamettha kālaṃ jānissāma. Esāhaṃ, bhante, tatiyampi bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsakaṃ maṃ bhagavā dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti.

68.“Householder, for a long time your family has been like a watering place for the Nigaṇṭhas, where you might think almsfood should be given to them when they come.” “Venerable sir, by this, I am even more delighted and gratified toward the Blessed One, that the Blessed One said to me: ‘Householder, for a long time your family has been like a watering place for the Nigaṇṭhas, where you might think almsfood should be given to them when they come.’ For I have heard, venerable sir, that the recluse Gotama says: ‘Gifts should be given to me alone, not to others; gifts should be given to my disciples alone, not to the disciples of others; what is given to me alone is of great fruit, what is given to others is not of great fruit; what is given to my disciples alone is of great fruit, what is given to the disciples of others is not of great fruit.’ Yet the Blessed One encourages me to give to the Nigaṇṭhas as well. But, venerable sir, we will know the proper time for this. I go for refuge to the Blessed One, to the Dhamma, and to the Saṅgha of bhikkhus for the third time, venerable sir. May the Blessed One remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forth, for as long as life lasts.”

69. Atha kho bhagavā upālissa gahapatissa anupubbiṃ kathaṃ [ānupubbīkathaṃ (sī.), ānupubbikathaṃ (pī.), anupubbikathaṃ (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] kathesi, seyyathidaṃ – dānakathaṃ sīlakathaṃ saggakathaṃ, kāmānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ, nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Yadā bhagavā aññāsi upāliṃ gahapatiṃ kallacittaṃ muducittaṃ vinīvaraṇacittaṃ udaggacittaṃ pasannacittaṃ, atha yā buddhānaṃ sāmukkaṃsikā dhammadesanā taṃ pakāsesi – dukkhaṃ, samudayaṃ, nirodhaṃ, maggaṃ. Seyyathāpi nāma suddhaṃ vatthaṃ apagatakāḷakaṃ sammadeva rajanaṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, evameva upālissa gahapatissa tasmiṃyeva āsane virajaṃ vītamalaṃ dhammacakkhuṃ udapādi – ‘yaṃ kiñci samudayadhammaṃ sabbaṃ taṃ nirodhadhamma’nti. Atha kho upāli gahapati diṭṭhadhammo pattadhammo viditadhammo pariyogāḷhadhammo tiṇṇavicikiccho vigatakathaṃkatho vesārajjappatto aparappaccayo satthusāsane bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘handa ca dāni mayaṃ, bhante, gacchāma, bahukiccā mayaṃ bahukaraṇīyā’’ti. ‘‘Yassadāni tvaṃ, gahapati, kālaṃ maññasī’’ti.

69.Then the Blessed One gave householder Upāli a progressive discourse, that is, a talk on generosity, on morality, on heaven; on the danger, degradation, and defilement of sensual pleasures; and on the benefits of renunciation. When the Blessed One knew that householder Upāli's mind was ready, pliable, free from hindrances, uplifted, and confident, he revealed the teaching unique to the Buddhas: suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Just as a clean cloth free from stains would properly absorb dye, so too in that very seat the dust-free, stainless vision of the Dhamma arose in householder Upāli: ‘Whatever is subject to arising is all subject to cessation.’ Then householder Upāli, having seen the Dhamma, attained the Dhamma, known the Dhamma, plunged into the Dhamma, crossed over doubt, dispelled uncertainty, gained assurance, and become independent of others in the Teacher’s dispensation, said to the Blessed One: “Now, venerable sir, we wish to depart. We have much to do and many duties.” “You may go, householder, at your own convenience.”

70. Atha kho upāli gahapati bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā yena sakaṃ nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā dovārikaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ajjatagge, samma dovārika, āvarāmi dvāraṃ nigaṇṭhānaṃ nigaṇṭhīnaṃ, anāvaṭaṃ dvāraṃ bhagavato bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ upāsakānaṃ upāsikānaṃ. Sace koci nigaṇṭho āgacchati tamenaṃ tvaṃ evaṃ vadeyyāsi – ‘tiṭṭha, bhante, mā pāvisi. Ajjatagge upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato. Āvaṭaṃ dvāraṃ nigaṇṭhānaṃ nigaṇṭhīnaṃ, anāvaṭaṃ dvāraṃ bhagavato bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ upāsakānaṃ upāsikānaṃ. Sace te, bhante, piṇḍakena attho, ettheva tiṭṭha, ettheva te āharissantī’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho dovāriko upālissa gahapatissa paccassosi.

70.Then the householder Upāli, having delighted in and approved the Blessed One’s discourse, rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, and circumambulated him clockwise. He then went to his own residence. Arriving there, he addressed the gatekeeper: “From today onward, good gatekeeper, I close the door to the Nigaṇṭhas and Nigaṇṭhīs, but I open the door to the Blessed One, his bhikkhus and nuns, and his male and female lay followers. If any Nigaṇṭha comes, you should say to him: ‘Wait, venerable sir, do not enter. From today onward, the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama. The door is closed to the Nigaṇṭhas and Nigaṇṭhīs, but open to the Blessed One, his bhikkhus and nuns, and his male and female lay followers. If you, venerable sir, need alms, just wait here, and they will bring them to you.’” “Yes, venerable sir,” the gatekeeper replied to the householder Upāli.

71. Assosi kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho – ‘‘upāli kira gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato’’ti. Atha kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho yena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante, upāli kira gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato’’ti. ‘‘Aṭṭhānaṃ kho etaṃ, tapassi, anavakāso yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyya. Ṭhānañca kho etaṃ vijjati yaṃ samaṇo gotamo upālissa gahapatissa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyyā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho…pe… tatiyampi kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante …pe… upālissa gahapatissa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyyā’’ti. ‘‘Handāhaṃ, bhante, gacchāmi yāva jānāmi yadi vā upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato yadi vā no’’ti. ‘‘Gaccha tvaṃ, tapassi, jānāhi yadi vā upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato yadi vā no’’ti.

71.Then the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī heard: “The householder Upāli, it is said, has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama.” So the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī approached the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta. Having approached, he said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Venerable sir, I have heard that the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama.” “That is impossible, Tapassī, it is impossible that the householder Upāli would go for refuge to the ascetic Gotama. But it is possible that the ascetic Gotama might go for refuge to the householder Upāli.” A second time the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī… A third time the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Venerable sir, I have heard that the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama.” “Come now, venerable sir, I shall go and ascertain whether the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama or not.” “Go, Tapassī, ascertain whether the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama or not.”

72. Atha kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho yena upālissa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami. Addasā kho dovāriko dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna dīghatapassiṃ nigaṇṭhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tiṭṭha, bhante, mā pāvisi. Ajjatagge upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato. Āvaṭaṃ dvāraṃ nigaṇṭhānaṃ nigaṇṭhīnaṃ, anāvaṭaṃ dvāraṃ bhagavato bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ upāsakānaṃ upāsikānaṃ. Sace te, bhante, piṇḍakena attho, ettheva tiṭṭha, ettheva te āharissantī’’ti. ‘‘Na me, āvuso, piṇḍakena attho’’ti vatvā tato paṭinivattitvā yena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saccaṃyeva kho, bhante, yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato. Etaṃ kho te ahaṃ, bhante, nālatthaṃ na kho me, bhante, ruccati yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropeyya. Samaṇo hi, bhante, gotamo māyāvī āvaṭṭaniṃ māyaṃ jānāti yāya aññatitthiyānaṃ sāvake āvaṭṭetīti. Āvaṭṭo kho te, bhante, upāli gahapati samaṇena gotamena āvaṭṭaniyā māyāyā’’ti. ‘‘Aṭṭhānaṃ kho etaṃ, tapassi, anavakāso yaṃ upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyya. Ṭhānañca kho etaṃ vijjati yaṃ samaṇo gotamo upālissa gahapatissa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyyā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saccaṃyeva, bhante…pe… upālissa gahapatissa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyyā’’ti. Tatiyampi kho dīghatapassī nigaṇṭho nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saccaṃyeva kho, bhante…pe… upālissa gahapatissa sāvakattaṃ upagaccheyyā’’ti. ‘‘Handa cāhaṃ, tapassi, gacchāmi yāva cāhaṃ sāmaṃyeva jānāmi yadi vā upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato yadi vā no’’ti.

72.Then the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī approached the residence of the householder Upāli. The gatekeeper saw the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī coming from afar. On seeing him, he said to the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī: “Stop, venerable sir, do not enter. From today onward, the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama. The door is shut to Nigaṇṭhas and Nigaṇṭhīs; the door is open to the Blessed One’s bhikkhus and nuns, and his male and female lay followers. If you, venerable sir, need alms-food, just stand here, and they will bring it to you.” “I have no need of alms-food, friend,” he replied, and turning back from there, he went to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta. Having approached, he said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “It is indeed true, venerable sir, that the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama. It is not proper for me, venerable sir, and it does not please me, that the householder Upāli should adopt the ascetic Gotama’s teaching. For the ascetic Gotama, venerable sir, is a sorcerer; he knows a method of conversion by which he wins over disciples of other sects. The householder Upāli, venerable sir, has been won over by the ascetic Gotama’s method of conversion.” “It is impossible, Tapassī, it is impossible that the householder Upāli would go for refuge to the ascetic Gotama. But it is possible that the ascetic Gotama might go for refuge to the householder Upāli.” A second time the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “It is indeed true, venerable sir… would go for refuge to the householder Upāli.” A third time the Nigaṇṭha Dīghatapassī said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “It is indeed true, venerable sir… would go for refuge to the householder Upāli.” “Come now, Tapassī, I shall go and ascertain for myself whether the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama or not.”

Atha kho nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto mahatiyā nigaṇṭhaparisāya saddhiṃ yena upālissa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami. Addasā kho dovāriko nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvāna nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tiṭṭha, bhante, mā pāvisi. Ajjatagge upāli gahapati samaṇassa gotamassa sāvakattaṃ upagato. Āvaṭaṃ dvāraṃ nigaṇṭhānaṃ nigaṇṭhīnaṃ, anāvaṭaṃ dvāraṃ bhagavato bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ upāsakānaṃ upāsikānaṃ. Sace te, bhante, piṇḍakena attho, ettheva tiṭṭha, ettheva te āharissantī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samma dovārika, yena upāli gahapati tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā upāliṃ gahapatiṃ evaṃ vadehi – ‘nigaṇṭho, bhante, nāṭaputto mahatiyā nigaṇṭhaparisāya saddhiṃ bahidvārakoṭṭhake ṭhito; so te dassanakāmo’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho dovāriko nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa paṭissutvā yena upāli gahapati tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā upāliṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘nigaṇṭho, bhante, nāṭaputto mahatiyā nigaṇṭhaparisāya saddhiṃ bahidvārakoṭṭhake ṭhito; so te dassanakāmo’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samma dovārika, majjhimāya dvārasālāya āsanāni paññapehī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho dovāriko upālissa gahapatissa paṭissutvā majjhimāya dvārasālāya āsanāni paññapetvā yena upāli gahapati tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā upāliṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘paññattāni kho, bhante, majjhimāya dvārasālāya āsanāni. Yassadāni kālaṃ maññasī’’ti.

Then the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, accompanied by a great assembly of Nigaṇṭhas, approached the residence of the householder Upāli. The gatekeeper saw the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta coming from afar. On seeing him, he said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Stop, venerable sir, do not enter. From today onward, the householder Upāli has gone for refuge to the ascetic Gotama. The door is closed to Nigaṇṭhas and Nigaṇṭhīs; the door is open to the Blessed One’s bhikkhus and nuns, and his male and female lay followers. If you, venerable sir, need alms, wait right here, and they will bring them to you.” “Then, good gatekeeper, go to the householder Upāli and say this: ‘Venerable sir, the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta is standing at the outer gatehouse with a great assembly of Nigaṇṭhas; he wishes to see you.’” “Yes, venerable sir,” the gatekeeper replied to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta. He then went to the householder Upāli and said: “Venerable sir, the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta is standing at the outer gatehouse with a great assembly of Nigaṇṭhas; he wishes to see you.” “Then, good gatekeeper, prepare seats in the middle gatehouse.” “Yes, venerable sir,” the gatekeeper replied to the householder Upāli. Having prepared seats in the middle gatehouse, he returned to Upāli and said: “Venerable sir, seats have been prepared in the middle gatehouse. You may now proceed as you think fit.”

73. Atha kho upāli gahapati yena majjhimā dvārasālā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā yaṃ tattha āsanaṃ aggañca seṭṭhañca uttamañca paṇītañca tattha sāmaṃ nisīditvā dovārikaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘tena hi, samma dovārika, yena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ evaṃ vadehi – ‘upāli, bhante, gahapati evamāha – pavisa kira, bhante, sace ākaṅkhasī’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho dovāriko upālissa gahapatissa paṭissutvā yena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘upāli, bhante, gahapati evamāha – ‘pavisa kira, bhante, sace ākaṅkhasī’’’ti. Atha kho nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto mahatiyā nigaṇṭhaparisāya saddhiṃ yena majjhimā dvārasālā tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho upāli gahapati – yaṃ sudaṃ pubbe yato passati nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ disvāna tato paccuggantvā yaṃ tattha āsanaṃ aggañca seṭṭhañca uttamañca paṇītañca taṃ uttarāsaṅgena sammajjitvā [pamajjitvā (sī. pī.)] pariggahetvā nisīdāpeti so – dāni yaṃ tattha āsanaṃ aggañca seṭṭhañca uttamañca paṇītañca tattha sāmaṃ nisīditvā nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saṃvijjanti kho, bhante, āsanāni; sace ākaṅkhasi, nisīdā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto upāliṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ummattosi tvaṃ, gahapati, dattosi tvaṃ, gahapati! ‘Gacchāmahaṃ, bhante, samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropessāmī’ti gantvā mahatāsi vādasaṅghāṭena paṭimukko āgato. Seyyathāpi, gahapati, puriso aṇḍahārako gantvā ubbhatehi aṇḍehi āgaccheyya, seyyathā vā pana gahapati puriso akkhikahārako gantvā ubbhatehi akkhīhi āgaccheyya; evameva kho tvaṃ, gahapati, ‘gacchāmahaṃ, bhante, samaṇassa gotamassa vādaṃ āropessāmī’ti gantvā mahatāsi vādasaṅghāṭena paṭimukko āgato. Āvaṭṭosi kho tvaṃ, gahapati, samaṇena gotamena āvaṭṭaniyā māyāyā’’ti.

73.Then the householder Upāli approached the middle gatehouse. Having arrived there, he himself sat down on the seat that was the foremost, best, highest, and most excellent, and addressed the gatekeeper: “Well then, good gatekeeper, go to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta. Having approached, say this to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: ‘Venerable sir, the householder Upāli says this: “Please enter, venerable sir, if you wish.”’” “Yes, venerable sir,” the gatekeeper replied to the householder Upāli. He then went to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta and said: “Venerable sir, the householder Upāli says this: ‘Please enter, venerable sir, if you wish.’” Then the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, accompanied by a great assembly of Nigaṇṭhas, approached the middle gatehouse. Now, the householder Upāli—who previously, whenever he saw the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta coming from afar, would rise to meet him, dust off the seat there that was the foremost, best, highest, and most excellent with his upper robe, offer it, and invite him to sit—now himself sat on that seat there that was the foremost, best, highest, and most excellent, and said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Venerable sir, seats are available; if you wish, sit down.” When this was said, the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta said to the householder Upāli: “You are mad, householder! You are foolish, householder! You went saying, ‘I shall, venerable sir, bring a charge against the ascetic Gotama’s teaching,’ but you have returned defeated by a great mass of arguments. Just as, householder, a man might go to fetch eggs but return with plucked-out eggs, or as a man might go to fetch eyes but return with plucked-out eyes; just so, householder, you went saying, ‘I shall, venerable sir, bring a charge against the ascetic Gotama’s teaching,’ but you have returned defeated by a great mass of arguments. You have been ensnared, householder, by the ascetic Gotama with his ensnaring magic!”

74. ‘‘Bhaddikā, bhante, āvaṭṭanī māyā; kalyāṇī, bhante, āvaṭṭanī māyā; piyā me, bhante, ñātisālohitā imāya āvaṭṭaniyā āvaṭṭeyyuṃ; piyānampi me assa ñātisālohitānaṃ dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya; sabbe cepi, bhante, khattiyā imāya āvaṭṭaniyā āvaṭṭeyyuṃ; sabbesānampissa khattiyānaṃ dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya; sabbe cepi, bhante, brāhmaṇā…pe… vessā…pe… suddā imāya āvaṭṭaniyā āvaṭṭeyyuṃ; sabbesānampissa suddānaṃ dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya; sadevako cepi, bhante, loko samārako sabrahmako sassamaṇabrāhmaṇī pajā sadevamanussā imāya āvaṭṭaniyā āvaṭṭeyyuṃ; sadevakassapissa lokassa samārakassa sabrahmakassa sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyāti. Tena hi, bhante, upamaṃ te karissāmi. Upamāya pidhekacce viññū purisā bhāsitassa atthaṃ ājānanti.

74.“Venerable sir, this ensnaring magic is auspicious; venerable sir, this ensnaring magic is beautiful. If my dear relatives by blood were to be ensnared by this ensnaring magic, it would be for the long-term benefit and happiness of my dear relatives by blood. If all the nobles, venerable sir, were to be ensnared by this ensnaring magic, it would be for the long-term benefit and happiness of all those nobles. If all the brahmins…the merchants…the laborers, venerable sir, were to be ensnared by this ensnaring magic, it would be for the long-term benefit and happiness of all those laborers. If the world with its devas, Maras, and Brahmas, this company of ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans, venerable sir, were to be ensnared by this ensnaring magic, it would be for the long-term benefit and happiness of the world with its devas, Maras, and Brahmas, this company of ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans. Therefore, venerable sir, I will give you a simile. For through a simile, some intelligent persons understand the meaning of what is said.”

75. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhante, aññatarassa brāhmaṇassa jiṇṇassa vuḍḍhassa mahallakassa daharā māṇavikā pajāpatī ahosi gabbhinī upavijaññā. Atha kho, bhante, sā māṇavikā taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘gaccha tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, āpaṇā makkaṭacchāpakaṃ kiṇitvā ānehi, yo me kumārakassa kīḷāpanako bhavissatī’ti. Evaṃ vutte, so brāhmaṇo taṃ māṇavikaṃ etadavoca – ‘āgamehi tāva, bhoti, yāva vijāyati. Sace tvaṃ, bhoti, kumārakaṃ vijāyissasi, tassā te ahaṃ āpaṇā makkaṭacchāpakaṃ kiṇitvā ānessāmi, yo te kumārakassa kīḷāpanako bhavissati. Sace pana tvaṃ, bhoti, kumārikaṃ vijāyissasi, tassā te ahaṃ āpaṇā makkaṭacchāpikaṃ kiṇitvā ānessāmi, yā te kumārikāya kīḷāpanikā bhavissatī’ti. Dutiyampi kho, bhante, sā māṇavikā…pe… tatiyampi kho, bhante, sā māṇavikā taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘gaccha tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, āpaṇā makkaṭacchāpakaṃ kiṇitvā ānehi, yo me kumārakassa kīḷāpanako bhavissatī’ti. Atha kho, bhante, so brāhmaṇo tassā māṇavikāya sāratto paṭibaddhacitto āpaṇā makkaṭacchāpakaṃ kiṇitvā ānetvā taṃ māṇavikaṃ etadavoca – ‘ayaṃ te, bhoti, āpaṇā makkaṭacchāpako kiṇitvā ānīto, yo te kumārakassa kīḷāpanako bhavissatī’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhante, sā māṇavikā taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘gaccha tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, imaṃ makkaṭacchāpakaṃ ādāya yena rattapāṇi rajataputto tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā rattapāṇiṃ rajakaputtaṃ evaṃ vadehi – icchāmahaṃ, samma rattapāṇi, imaṃ makkaṭacchāpakaṃ pītāvalepanaṃ nāma raṅgajātaṃ rajitaṃ ākoṭitapaccākoṭitaṃ ubhatobhāgavimaṭṭha’nti.

75.“Once upon a time, venerable sir, there was a certain brahmin who was old, aged, and advanced in years. His young wife was pregnant and nearing delivery. Then, venerable sir, that young wife said to the brahmin: ‘Go, brahmin, buy a young monkey from the market and bring it back to be a plaything for my son.’ When this was said, the brahmin replied to her: ‘Wait, madam, until you give birth. If you give birth to a boy, I will buy a young male monkey from the market and bring it back to be a plaything for your son. But if you give birth to a girl, I will buy a young female monkey from the market and bring it back to be a plaything for your daughter.’ A second time, venerable sir, that young wife… and a third time, venerable sir, that young wife said to the brahmin: ‘Go, brahmin, buy a young monkey from the market and bring it back to be a plaything for my son.’ Then, venerable sir, that brahmin, affected and with his mind bound to his young wife, bought a young monkey from the market, brought it back, and said to her: ‘Here, madam, is a young monkey bought from the market to be a plaything for your son.’ When this was said, venerable sir, that young wife replied to the brahmin: ‘Go, brahmin, take this young monkey to Rattapāṇi, the dyer’s son, and say to him: “Friend Rattapāṇi, I wish to have this young monkey dyed with a dye called Pītāvalepana, pounded and re-pounded, and smoothed on both sides.”’”

‘‘Atha kho, bhante, so brāhmaṇo tassā māṇavikāya sāratto paṭibaddhacitto taṃ makkaṭacchāpakaṃ ādāya yena rattapāṇi rajakaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rattapāṇiṃ rajakaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘icchāmahaṃ, samma rattapāṇi, imaṃ makkaṭacchāpakaṃ pītāvalepanaṃ nāma raṅgajātaṃ rajitaṃ ākoṭitapaccākoṭitaṃ ubhatobhāgavimaṭṭha’nti. Evaṃ vutte, bhante, rattapāṇi rajakaputto taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘ayaṃ kho te, makkaṭacchāpako raṅgakkhamo hi kho, no ākoṭanakkhamo, no vimajjanakkhamo’ti. Evameva kho, bhante, bālānaṃ nigaṇṭhānaṃ vādo raṅgakkhamo hi kho bālānaṃ no paṇḍitānaṃ, no anuyogakkhamo, no vimajjanakkhamo. Atha kho, bhante, so brāhmaṇo aparena samayena navaṃ dussayugaṃ ādāya yena rattapāṇi rajakaputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rattapāṇiṃ rajakaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘icchāmahaṃ, samma rattapāṇi, imaṃ navaṃ dussayugaṃ pītāvalepanaṃ nāma raṅgajātaṃ rajitaṃ ākoṭitapaccākoṭitaṃ ubhatobhāgavimaṭṭha’nti. Evaṃ vutte, bhante, rattapāṇi rajakaputto taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘idaṃ kho te, bhante, navaṃ dussayugaṃ raṅgakkhamañceva ākoṭanakkhamañca vimajjanakkhamañcā’ti. Evameva kho, bhante, tassa bhagavato vādo arahato sammāsambuddhassa raṅgakkhamo ceva paṇḍitānaṃ no bālānaṃ, anuyogakkhamo ca vimajjanakkhamo cā’’ti.

“Then, venerable sir, that brahmin, infatuated and with mind attached to that maiden, took that young monkey and approached Rattapāṇi the dyer. Having approached, he said to Rattapāṇi the dyer: ‘Friend Rattapāṇi, I wish to have this young monkey dyed with the yellow dye called pītāvalepana, beaten and re-beaten, and smoothed on both sides.’ When this was said, venerable sir, Rattapāṇi the dyer said to that brahmin: ‘This young monkey of yours can endure the dye, but not the beating, nor the smoothing.’ Just so, venerable sir, the doctrine of the foolish Nigaṇṭhas can endure the dye among fools, but not among the wise, not under questioning, nor under examination. Then, venerable sir, that brahmin at another time took a new pair of cloths and approached Rattapāṇi the dyer. Having approached, he said to Rattapāṇi the dyer: ‘Friend Rattapāṇi, I wish to have this new pair of cloths dyed with the yellow dye called pītāvalepana, beaten and re-beaten, and smoothed on both sides.’ When this was said, venerable sir, Rattapāṇi the dyer said to that brahmin: ‘This new pair of cloths of yours, sir, can endure the dye, the beating, and the smoothing.’ Just so, venerable sir, the doctrine of the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, can endure the dye among the wise, not among fools, and it can endure questioning and examination.”

‘‘Sarājikā kho, gahapati, parisā evaṃ jānāti – ‘upāli gahapati nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa sāvako’ti. Kassa taṃ, gahapati, sāvakaṃ dhāremā’’ti? Evaṃ vutte, upāli gahapati uṭṭhāyāsanā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā yena bhagavā tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā nigaṇṭhaṃ nāṭaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tena hi, bhante, suṇohi yassāhaṃ sāvako’’ti –

“Householder, this assembly with the king knows thus: ‘Upāli, the householder, is a disciple of the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta.’ Whose disciple do you claim to be, householder?” When this was said, the householder Upāli rose from his seat, arranged his upper robe over one shoulder, and, raising his joined hands in reverence toward the Blessed One, said to the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta: “Well then, venerable sir, listen to of whom I am a disciple.”

76.

‘‘Dhīrassa vigatamohassa, pabhinnakhīlassa vijitavijayassa;Anīghassa susamacittassa, vuddhasīlassa sādhupaññassa;Vesamantarassa [vessantarassa (sī. pī.)] vimalassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

“I am a disciple of that Blessed One, the wise, free from delusion, with broken fetters, victorious, untroubled, with a very composed mind, advanced in virtue, with excellent wisdom, Vesantara, the stainless one.”

‘‘Akathaṃkathissa tusitassa, vantalokāmisassa muditassa;Katasamaṇassa manujassa, antimasārīrassa narassa;Anopamassa virajassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

“I am a disciple of that Blessed One, free from doubt, content, rid of worldly enticements, joyful, a perfected contemplative among humans, bearing his last body, incomparable, free from defilement.”

‘‘Asaṃsayassa kusalassa, venayikassa sārathivarassa;Anuttarassa ruciradhammassa, nikkaṅkhassa pabhāsakassa [pabhāsakarassa (sī. syā. pī.)];Mānacchidassa vīrassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

“I am a disciple of the Blessed One, free from uncertainty about the good, the guide, the best charioteer, the unsurpassed, the one with lovely Dhamma, free from doubt, the illuminator, the hero who has cut off conceit.”

‘‘Nisabhassa appameyyassa, gambhīrassa monapattassa;Khemaṅkarassa vedassa, dhammaṭṭhassa saṃvutattassa;Saṅgātigassa muttassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

“I am a disciple of that Blessed One, the bull, immeasurable, profound, attained to silence, Khemankara, the knower, established in Dhamma, restrained, gone beyond attachment, liberated.”

‘‘Nāgassa pantasenassa, khīṇasaṃyojanassa muttassa;Paṭimantakassa [paṭimantassa (ka.)] dhonassa, pannadhajassa vītarāgassa;Dantassa nippapañcassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

“I am a disciple of the Blessed One, the noble one, dwelling in seclusion, with destroyed fetters, liberated, the wise one, the pure one, with banner lowered, free from passion, tamed, free from proliferation.”

‘‘Isisattamassa akuhassa, tevijjassa brahmapattassa;Nhātakassa [nahātakassa (sī. syā. pī.)] padakassa, passaddhassa viditavedassa;Purindadassa sakkassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

“I am a disciple of that Blessed One, the seventh of the sages, the non-deceiver, the possessor of threefold knowledge, the attainer of Brahma; the bathed one, the one who has reached the goal, the tranquil one, the knower of the Vedas; the giver of gifts, the powerful one.”

‘‘Ariyassa bhāvitattassa, pattipattassa veyyākaraṇassa;Satimato vipassissa, anabhinatassa no apanatassa;Anejassa vasippattassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

“I am a disciple of the Blessed One, whose self is developed, who has attained attainment, who is skilled in explanation, who is mindful and discerning, who is neither attached nor detached, who is unagitated, and who has attained mastery.

‘‘Samuggatassa [sammaggatassa (sī. syā. pī.)] jhāyissa, ananugatantarassa suddhassa;Asitassa hitassa [appahīnassa (sī. pī.), appabhītassa (syā.)], pavivittassa aggappattassa;Tiṇṇassa tārayantassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

Of the one who has risen above, the jhana-attainer, whose inner being is not followed by others, the pure; of the unattached, the beneficial, the secluded, who has attained the highest; of the one who has crossed over, who helps others cross, I am a disciple of that Blessed One.

‘‘Santassa bhūripaññassa, mahāpaññassa vītalobhassa;Tathāgatassa sugatassa, appaṭipuggalassa asamassa;Visāradassa nipuṇassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmi.

Of the calm, of vast wisdom, of great wisdom, rid of greed; of the Tathagata, the Well-Gone One, without a peer, unequalled; of the confident, the skillful, the Blessed One—I am a disciple.

‘‘Taṇhacchidassa buddhassa, vītadhūmassa anupalittassa;Āhuneyyassa yakkhassa, uttamapuggalassa atulassa;Mahato yasaggapattassa, bhagavato tassa sāvakohamasmī’’ti.

I am a disciple of that Blessed One, the Awakened One, who has cut off craving, free from defilements, unsoiled, worthy of offerings, a being, the supreme person, incomparable, who has attained the peak of glory.”

77. ‘‘Kadā saññūḷhā pana te, gahapati, ime samaṇassa gotamassa vaṇṇā’’ti? ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhante, nānāpupphānaṃ mahāpuppharāsi, tamenaṃ dakkho mālākāro vā mālākārantevāsī vā vicittaṃ mālaṃ gantheyya; evameva kho, bhante, so bhagavā anekavaṇṇo anekasatavaṇṇo. Ko hi, bhante, vaṇṇārahassa vaṇṇaṃ na karissatī’’ti? Atha kho nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa bhagavato sakkāraṃ asahamānassa tattheva uṇhaṃ lohitaṃ mukhato uggacchīti [uggañchi (sī. syā. pī.)].

77.“But when, householder, were these qualities of the ascetic Gotama gathered by you?” “Just as, venerable sir, a skilled garland-maker or a garland-maker’s apprentice might weave a variegated garland from a great heap of various flowers; in the same way, venerable sir, that Blessed One is of many qualities, of many hundreds of qualities. For who, venerable sir, would not praise one who is worthy of praise?” Then, unable to bear the honor shown to the Blessed One, the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta then and there hot blood gushed from his mouth.

Upālisuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The Upāli Sutta, the sixth, is finished.
