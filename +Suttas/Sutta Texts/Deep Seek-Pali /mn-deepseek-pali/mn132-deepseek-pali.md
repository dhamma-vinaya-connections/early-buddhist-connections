# 132. Mahākaccānabhaddekarattasuttaṃ

## 132. The Great Kaccāna’s Discourse on the Ideal Wayfarer

279. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati tapodārāme. Atha kho āyasmā samiddhi rattiyā paccūsasamayaṃ paccuṭṭhāya yena tapodo [tapodā (sī.)] tenupasaṅkami gattāni parisiñcituṃ. Tapode gattāni parisiñcitvā paccuttaritvā ekacīvaro aṭṭhāsi gattāni pubbāpayamāno [sukkhāpayamāno (ka.)]. Atha kho aññatarā devatā abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇā kevalakappaṃ tapodaṃ obhāsetvā yenāyasmā samiddhi tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho sā devatā āyasmantaṃ samiddhiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘dhāresi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’’ti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’’ti? ‘‘Ahampi kho, bhikkhu, na dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Dhāresi pana tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’’ti? ‘‘Na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthāti. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’’ti? ‘‘Ahampi kho, bhikkhu na dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthāti. Uggaṇhāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; pariyāpuṇāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; dhārehi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Atthasaṃhito, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddeso ca vibhaṅgo ca ādibrahmacariyako’’ti. Idamavoca sā devatā; idaṃ vatvā tatthevantaradhāyi.

279.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in the Tapoda Park. Then, when the night had passed, Venerable Samiddhi rose at dawn and went to Tapoda to bathe his limbs. Having bathed his limbs in Tapoda and emerged, he stood in one robe drying his limbs. Then a certain deity, of stunning beauty, illuminating the entire Tapoda, approached Venerable Samiddhi. Standing to one side, the deity said to him: "Do you, bhikkhu, remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta?" "No, friend, I do not remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta. But do you, friend, remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta?" "I too, bhikkhu, do not remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta. But do you, bhikkhu, remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta?" "No, friend, I do not remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta. But do you, friend, remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta?" "I too, bhikkhu, do not remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta. Learn, bhikkhu, the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta; master, bhikkhu, the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta; remember, bhikkhu, the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta. The summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta, bhikkhu, is meaningful and pertains to the beginning of the holy life." Having said this, the deity vanished right there.

280. Atha kho āyasmā samiddhi tassā rattiyā accayena yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā samiddhi bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

280.Then, when the night had passed, Venerable Samiddhi approached the Blessed One, and after approaching, paid homage to him and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, Venerable Samiddhi said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Idhāhaṃ, bhante, rattiyā paccūsasamayaṃ paccuṭṭhāya yena tapodo tenupasaṅkamiṃ gattāni parisiñcituṃ. Tapode gattāni parisiñcitvā paccuttaritvā ekacīvaro aṭṭhāsiṃ gattāni pubbāpayamāno. Atha kho bhante, aññatarā devatā abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇā kevalakappaṃ tapodaṃ obhāsetvā yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho sā devatā maṃ etadavoca – ‘dhāresi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’’’ti?

"Venerable sir, here, in the pre-dawn hours of the night, I got up and went to the Tapoda to bathe my limbs. Having bathed my limbs in the Tapoda and come back out, I stood in one robe drying my limbs. Then, venerable sir, a certain deity of surpassing radiance, illuminating the entire Tapoda, approached me. Having approached, she stood to one side. Standing to one side, that deity said to me: ‘Do you, monk, remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta?’"

‘‘Evaṃ vutte ahaṃ, bhante, taṃ devataṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañcā’ti? ‘Ahampi kho, bhikkhu, na dhāremi bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Dhāresi pana tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’ti? ‘Na kho ahaṃ, āvuso, dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthāti. Tvaṃ panāvuso, dhāresi bhaddekarattiyo gāthā’ti? ‘Ahampi kho, bhikkhu, na dhāremi bhaddekarattiyo gāthāti. Uggaṇhāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; pariyāpuṇāhi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca; dhārehi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca. Atthasaṃhito, bhikkhu, bhaddekarattassa uddeso ca vibhaṅgo ca ādibrahmacariyako’ti. Idamavoca, bhante, sā devatā; idaṃ vatvā tatthevantaradhāyi. Sādhu me, bhante, bhagavā bhaddekarattassa uddesañca vibhaṅgañca desetū’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, bhikkhu, suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā samiddhi bhagavato paccassosi. Bhagavā etadavoca –

"When this was said, venerable sir, I said to that deity: ‘Friend, I do not remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta. But do you, friend, remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta?’ ‘I too, monk, do not remember the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta. But do you, monk, remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta?’ ‘Friend, I do not remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta. But do you, friend, remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta?’ ‘I too, monk, do not remember the verses of the Bhaddekaratta. Learn, monk, the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta; master, monk, the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta; remember, monk, the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta. The summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta, monk, is meaningful and pertains to the beginning of the holy life.’ Thus, venerable sir, that deity spoke; having said this, she vanished right there. It would be good, venerable sir, if the Blessed One would teach me the summary and analysis of the Bhaddekaratta." "Then, monk, listen and attend closely; I will speak." "Yes, venerable sir," replied the venerable Samiddhi to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said:

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya, nappaṭikaṅkhe anāgataṃ;Yadatītaṃ pahīnaṃ taṃ, appattañca anāgataṃ.

Do not dwell on the past,Nor long for the future.For the past is left behind,And the future is yet to come.

‘‘Paccuppannañca yo dhammaṃ, tattha tattha vipassati;Asaṃhīraṃ asaṃkuppaṃ, taṃ vidvā manubrūhaye.

"One who sees the present Dhamma, here and there, unswerving and unwavering—let the wise one develop that."

‘‘Ajjeva kiccamātappaṃ, ko jaññā maraṇaṃ suve;Na hi no saṅgaraṃ tena, mahāsenena maccunā.

"Today, with utmost effort, do what must be done. Who knows, death may come tomorrow? There is no bargain to be struck with Death and his mighty host."

‘‘Evaṃ vihāriṃ ātāpiṃ, ahorattamatanditaṃ;Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

That one, indeed, the peaceful sage declares to be an ideal wayfarer.

Idamavoca bhagavā; idaṃ vatvāna sugato uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ pāvisi. Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ, acirapakkantassa bhagavato, etadahosi – ‘‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho –

The Blessed One said this. Having said this, the Well-Gone One rose from his seat and entered his dwelling. Then, not long after the Blessed One had left, those bhikkhus considered: "Friends, the Blessed One stated this summary teaching, and without analyzing the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya, nappaṭikaṅkhe anāgataṃ;Yadatītaṃ pahīnaṃ taṃ, appattañca anāgataṃ.

Do not dwell on the past,Nor long for the future.For the past is left behind,And the future is yet to come.

‘‘Paccuppannañca yo dhammaṃ, tattha tattha vipassati;Asaṃhīraṃ asaṃkuppaṃ, taṃ vidvā manubrūhaye.

"One who sees the present Dhamma, here and there, unswerving and unwavering—let the wise one develop that."

‘‘Ajjeva kiccamātappaṃ, ko jaññā maraṇaṃ suve;Na hi no saṅgaraṃ tena, mahāsenena maccunā.

"Today the effort must be made; who knows, death may come tomorrow? There is no bargaining with Death and his great army."

‘‘Evaṃ vihāriṃ ātāpiṃ, ahorattamatanditaṃ;Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

That one, indeed, the peaceful sage declares to be an ideal wayfarer.

‘‘Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’’ti?

"Who indeed could explain in detail the meaning of this brief statement uttered by the Blessed One, a statement whose meaning has not been analyzed in detail?"

Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā mahākaccāno satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ; pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’’ti.

Then those bhikkhus thought: “This Venerable Mahākaccāna is both praised and esteemed by the Teacher and by his wise fellow practitioners. And Venerable Mahākaccāna is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief statement, stated by the Blessed One, which has not been analyzed in detail. Why don't we go to Venerable Mahākaccāna and ask him about this matter?”

281. Atha kho te bhikkhū yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā mahākaccānena saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso kaccāna, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho –

281.Then those bhikkhus approached Venerable Mahākaccāna; having approached, they exchanged courteous greetings with Venerable Mahākaccāna. After exchanging friendly and polite conversation, they sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those bhikkhus said to Venerable Mahākaccāna: "Friend Kaccāna, the Blessed One recited a brief statement, without analyzing the meaning in detail, and then rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya…pe…Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

‘One should not pursue the past… That one, indeed, the peaceful sage declares to be an ideal wayfarer.’

‘‘Tesaṃ no, āvuso kaccāna, amhākaṃ, acirapakkantassa bhagavato, etadahosi – idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho –

"Friend Kaccāna, soon after the Blessed One had left, this occurred to us: 'The Blessed One, having stated a brief summary without analyzing the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.'"

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya…pe…Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

‘One should not pursue the past… That one, indeed, the peaceful sage declares to be an ideal wayfarer.’

‘‘Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyāti? Tesaṃ no, āvuso kaccāna, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā mahākaccāno satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’ti. Vibhajatāyasmā mahākaccāno’’ti.

"Who indeed could analyze in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One, which has not been analyzed in detail? Then, friend Kaccāna, we thought: ‘The Venerable Mahākaccāna is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise fellow practitioners. He is capable of analyzing in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One, which has not been analyzed in detail. Why don’t we go to the Venerable Mahākaccāna, and ask him about this matter?’ May the Venerable Mahākaccāna analyze it.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, āvuso, puriso sāratthiko sāragavesī sārapariyesanaṃ caramāno mahato rukkhassa tiṭṭhato sāravato atikkammeva mūlaṃ atikkamma khandhaṃ sākhāpalāse sāraṃ pariyesitabbaṃ maññeyya; evaṃ sampadamidaṃ āyasmantānaṃ satthari sammukhībhūte taṃ bhagavantaṃ atisitvā amhe etamatthaṃ paṭipucchitabbaṃ maññatha [maññetha (pī.)]. So hāvuso, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti, passaṃ passati, cakkhubhūto ñāṇabhūto dhammabhūto brahmabhūto vattā pavattā atthassa ninnetā amatassa dātā dhammassāmī tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi yaṃ bhagavantaṃyeva etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha, yathā vo bhagavā byākareyya tathā naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti.

"Just as, friend, a man needing heartwood, seeking heartwood, wandering in search of heartwood, might pass over the root and trunk of a great standing tree possessing heartwood, thinking to seek heartwood among the branches and leaves—so it is with you venerable ones, who, with the Blessed One present, bypass him and think to question us about this matter. For, friend, the Blessed One knows and sees; he is vision, he is knowledge, he is Dhamma, he is Brahma; he is the speaker, the proclaimer, the elucidator of meaning, the giver of the Deathless, the lord of the Dhamma, the Tathāgata. That would indeed be the appropriate time for you to question the Blessed One himself about this matter, and as the Blessed One explains it to you, so you should remember it."

‘‘Addhāvuso kaccāna, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti, passaṃ passati, cakkhubhūto ñāṇabhūto dhammabhūto brahmabhūto vattā pavattā atthassa ninnetā amatassa dātā dhammassāmī tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi yaṃ bhagavantaṃyeva etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāma; yathā no bhagavā byākareyya tathā naṃ dhāreyyāma. Api cāyasmā mahākaccāno satthuceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ; pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Vibhajatāyasmā mahākaccāno agaruṃ karitvā’’ti [agarukaritvā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)].

“Certainly, friends Kaccāna, the Blessed One knows, sees; he has become the eye, has become knowledge, has become the Dhamma, has become the Holy One; he is the expounder, the proclaimer, the elucidator of meaning, the giver of the Deathless, the lord of the Dhamma, the Tathāgata. This was indeed the time to question the Blessed One himself about this matter, and as the Blessed One would explain it to us, so we would bear it in mind. But the Venerable Mahākaccāna is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise fellow practitioners; the Venerable Mahākaccāna is capable of expounding in detail the meaning of this brief statement, this summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration. Let the Venerable Mahākaccāna explain it without making it a burden.”

‘‘Tena hāvuso, suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato mahākaccānassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā mahākaccāno etadavoca –

“Then, friends, listen and apply your mind well. I will speak.” “Yes, friends,” those bhikkhus responded to the Venerable Mahākaccāna. The Venerable Mahākaccāna said this:

‘‘Yaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho –

“Friends, the Blessed One, having stated this brief statement without expounding the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling—

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya…pe…Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

‘One should not pursue the past… That indeed is what the peaceful sage calls ‘one with a fortunate night’.’

Imassa kho ahaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi –

“Friends, this is how I understand the meaning in detail of this brief statement stated by the Blessed One, unexpounded in detail—

282. ‘‘Kathañca, āvuso, atītaṃ anvāgameti? Iti me cakkhu ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti rūpāti – tattha chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ [chandarāgappaṭibandhaṃ (ka.)] hoti viññāṇaṃ, chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa tadabhinandati, tadabhinandanto atītaṃ anvāgameti. Iti me sotaṃ ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti saddāti…pe… iti me ghānaṃ ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti gandhāti… iti me jivhā ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti rasāti… iti me kāyo ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti phoṭṭhabbāti… iti me mano ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti dhammāti – tattha chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ hoti viññāṇaṃ, chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa tadabhinandati, tadabhinandanto atītaṃ anvāgameti – evaṃ kho, āvuso, atītaṃ anvāgameti.

282.“And how, friends, does one pursue the past? Thinking, ‘Such was my eye in the past, such were forms’—consciousness is bound by desire and lust in regard to it. Because consciousness is bound by desire and lust, one delights in it. Delighting in it, one pursues the past. Thinking, ‘Such was my ear in the past, such were sounds’… ‘Such was my nose in the past, such were smells’… ‘Such was my tongue in the past, such were tastes’… ‘Such was my body in the past, such were tactile sensations’… ‘Such was my mind in the past, such were mental phenomena’—consciousness is bound by desire and lust in regard to it. Because consciousness is bound by desire and lust, one delights in it. Delighting in it, one pursues the past. This, friends, is how one pursues the past.

‘‘Kathañca, āvuso, atītaṃ nānvāgameti? Iti me cakkhu ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti rūpāti – tattha na chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ hoti viññāṇaṃ, na chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa na tadabhinandati, na tadabhinandanto atītaṃ nānvāgameti. Iti me sotaṃ ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti saddāti…pe… iti me ghānaṃ ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti gandhāti… iti me jivhā ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti rasāti… iti me kāyo ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti phoṭṭhabbāti… iti me mano ahosi atītamaddhānaṃ iti dhammāti – tattha na chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ hoti viññāṇaṃ, na chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa, na tadabhinandati, na tadabhinandanto atītaṃ nānvāgameti – evaṃ kho, āvuso, atītaṃ nānvāgameti.

“And how, friends, does one not pursue the past? Thinking, ‘Such was my eye in the past, such were forms’—consciousness is not bound by desire and lust in regard to it. Because consciousness is not bound by desire and lust, one does not delight in it. Not delighting in it, one does not pursue the past. Thinking, ‘Such was my ear in the past, such were sounds’… ‘Such was my nose in the past, such were smells’… ‘Such was my tongue in the past, such were tastes’… ‘Such was my body in the past, such were tactile sensations’… ‘Such was my mind in the past, such were mental phenomena’—consciousness is not bound by desire and lust in regard to it. Because consciousness is not bound by desire and lust, one does not delight in it. Not delighting in it, one does not pursue the past. This, friends, is how one does not pursue the past.

283. ‘‘Kathañca, āvuso, anāgataṃ paṭikaṅkhati? Iti me cakkhu siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti rūpāti – appaṭiladdhassa paṭilābhāya cittaṃ paṇidahati, cetaso paṇidhānapaccayā tadabhinandati, tadabhinandanto anāgataṃ paṭikaṅkhati. Iti me sotaṃ siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti saddāti…pe… iti me ghānaṃ siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti gandhāti… iti me jivhā siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti rasāti… iti me kāyo siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti phoṭṭhabbāti… iti me mano siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti dhammāti – appaṭiladdhassa paṭilābhāya cittaṃ paṇidahati, cetaso paṇidhānapaccayā tadabhinandati, tadabhinandanto anāgataṃ paṭikaṅkhati – evaṃ kho, āvuso, anāgataṃ paṭikaṅkhati.

283.“And how, friends, does one anticipate the future? Thinking, ‘Such may my eye be in the future, such may forms be’—one sets one’s mind on gaining what has not been gained. Because of the mind’s aspiration, one delights in it. Delighting in it, one anticipates the future. Thinking, ‘Such may my ear be in the future, such may sounds be’… ‘Such may my nose be in the future, such may smells be’… ‘Such may my tongue be in the future, such may tastes be’… ‘Such may my body be in the future, such may tactile sensations be’… ‘Such may my mind be in the future, such may mental phenomena be’—one sets one’s mind on gaining what has not been gained. Because of the mind’s aspiration, one delights in it. Delighting in it, one anticipates the future. This, friends, is how one anticipates the future.

‘‘Kathañca, āvuso, anāgataṃ nappaṭikaṅkhati? Iti me cakkhu siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti rūpāti – appaṭiladdhassa paṭilābhāya cittaṃ nappaṇidahati, cetaso appaṇidhānapaccayā na tadabhinandati, na tadabhinandanto anāgataṃ nappaṭikaṅkhati. Iti me sotaṃ siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti saddāti…pe… iti me ghānaṃ siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti gandhāti… iti me jivhā siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti rasāti… iti me kāyo siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti phoṭṭhabbāti… iti me mano siyā anāgatamaddhānaṃ iti dhammāti – appaṭiladdhassa paṭilābhāya cittaṃ nappaṇidahati, cetaso appaṇidhānapaccayā na tadabhinandati, na tadabhinandanto anāgataṃ nappaṭikaṅkhati – evaṃ kho, āvuso, anāgataṃ nappaṭikaṅkhati.

“And how, friends, does one not anticipate the future? Thinking, ‘Such may my eye be in the future, such may forms be’—one does not set one’s mind on gaining what has not been gained. Because of the mind’s non-aspiration, one does not delight in it. Not delighting in it, one does not anticipate the future. Thinking, ‘Such may my ear be in the future, such may sounds be’… ‘Such may my nose be in the future, such may smells be’… ‘Such may my tongue be in the future, such may tastes be’… ‘Such may my body be in the future, such may tactile sensations be’… ‘Such may my mind be in the future, such may mental phenomena be’—one does not set one’s mind on gaining what has not been gained. Because of the mind’s non-aspiration, one does not delight in it. Not delighting in it, one does not anticipate the future. This, friends, is how one does not anticipate the future.

284. ‘‘Kathañca, āvuso, paccuppannesu dhammesu saṃhīrati? Yañcāvuso, cakkhu ye ca rūpā – ubhayametaṃ paccuppannaṃ. Tasmiṃ ce paccuppanne chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ hoti viññāṇaṃ, chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa tadabhinandati, tadabhinandanto paccuppannesu dhammesu saṃhīrati. Yañcāvuso, sotaṃ ye ca saddā…pe… yañcāvuso, ghānaṃ ye ca gandhā… yā cāvuso, jivhā ye ca rasā… yo cāvuso, kāyo ye ca phoṭṭhabbā… yo cāvuso, mano ye ca dhammā – ubhayametaṃ paccuppannaṃ. Tasmiṃ ce paccuppanne chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ hoti viññāṇaṃ, chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa tadabhinandati, tadabhinandanto paccuppannesu dhammesu saṃhīrati – evaṃ kho, āvuso, paccuppannesu dhammesu saṃhīrati.

284.“And how, friends, is one agitated by present phenomena? Whatever eye, friends, and whatever forms—both of these are present. If consciousness is bound by desire and lust in regard to that present [phenomenon], because consciousness is bound by desire and lust, one delights in it. Delighting in it, one is agitated by present phenomena. Whatever ear, friends, and whatever sounds… Whatever nose, friends, and whatever odors… Whatever tongue, friends, and whatever tastes… Whatever body, friends, and whatever tactile sensations… Whatever mind, friends, and whatever mental phenomena—both of these are present. If consciousness is bound by desire and lust in regard to that present [phenomenon], because consciousness is bound by desire and lust, one delights in it. Delighting in it, one is agitated by present phenomena. This, friends, is how one is agitated by present phenomena.

‘‘Kathañca, āvuso, paccuppannesu dhammesu na saṃhīrati? Yañcāvuso, cakkhu ye ca rūpā – ubhayametaṃ paccuppannaṃ. Tasmiṃ ce paccuppanne na chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ hoti viññāṇaṃ, na chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa na tadabhinandati, na tadabhinandanto paccuppannesu dhammesu na saṃhīrati. Yañcāvuso, sotaṃ ye ca saddā…pe… yañcāvuso, ghānaṃ ye ca gandhā… yā cāvuso, jivhā ye ca rasā… yo cāvuso, kāyo ye ca phoṭṭhabbā… yo cāvuso, mano ye ca dhammā – ubhayametaṃ paccuppannaṃ. Tasmiṃ ce paccuppanne na chandarāgappaṭibaddhaṃ hoti viññāṇaṃ, na chandarāgappaṭibaddhattā viññāṇassa na tadabhinandati, na tadabhinandanto paccuppannesu dhammesu na saṃhīrati – evaṃ kho, āvuso, paccuppannesu dhammesu na saṃhīrati.

“And how, friends, is one not agitated by present phenomena? Whatever eye, friends, and whatever forms—both of these are present. If consciousness is not bound by desire and lust in regard to that present [phenomenon], because consciousness is not bound by desire and lust, one does not delight in it. Not delighting in it, one is not agitated by present phenomena. Whatever ear, friends, and whatever sounds… Whatever nose, friends, and whatever odors… Whatever tongue, friends, and whatever tastes… Whatever body, friends, and whatever tactile sensations… Whatever mind, friends, and whatever mental phenomena—both of these are present. If consciousness is not bound by desire and lust in regard to that present [phenomenon], because consciousness is not bound by desire and lust, one does not delight in it. Not delighting in it, one is not agitated by present phenomena. This, friends, is how one is not agitated by present phenomena.

285. ‘‘Yaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho –

285.“Friends, the Blessed One, having stated this brief statement without expounding the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling—

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya…pe… Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

‘One should not pursue the past… That indeed is what the peaceful sage calls ‘one with a fortunate night’.’

‘‘Imassa kho ahaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. Ākaṅkhamānā ca pana tumhe āyasmanto bhagavantaṃyeva upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha, yathā vo bhagavā byākaroti tathā naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti.

“Friends, this is how I understand the meaning in detail of this brief statement stated by the Blessed One, unexpounded in detail. And if you venerable ones wish, you should approach the Blessed One himself and ask about this matter. Then you should remember it as the Blessed One explains it to you.”

Atha kho te bhikkhū āyasmato mahākaccānassa bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘yaṃ kho no, bhante, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho –

Then those bhikkhus, having delighted in and approved of the Venerable Mahākaccāna’s words, rose from their seats and went to the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, those bhikkhus said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, the Blessed One, having stated a brief statement without expounding the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling—

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya…pe…Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

One should not pursue the past... The peaceful sage calls that person ‘one who has had a single excellent night.’

Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ, acirapakkantassa bhagavato, etadahosi – ‘‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho –

Then, venerable sir, soon after the Blessed One had departed, this thought occurred to us: ‘The Blessed One has stated this brief summary to us, and without having analyzed its meaning in detail, he rose from his seat and entered his lodging—

‘‘Atītaṃ nānvāgameyya, nappaṭikaṅkhe anāgataṃ;Yadatītaṃ pahīnaṃ taṃ, appattañca anāgataṃ.

One should not pursue the past, nor anticipate the future. What is past is abandoned, and the future has not yet arrived.

‘‘Paccuppannañca yo dhammaṃ, tattha tattha vipassati;Asaṃhīraṃ asaṃkuppaṃ, taṃ vidvā manubrūhaye.

And whoever discerns the present phenomenon, here and there, unswerving, unshakeable—knowing that, one should develop it.

‘‘Ajjeva kiccamātappaṃ, ko jaññā maraṇaṃ suve;Na hi no saṅgaraṃ tena, mahāsenena maccunā.

Today is the ardent task. Who knows, death may come tomorrow? For there is no bargaining with Death and his mighty host.

‘‘Evaṃ vihāriṃ ātāpiṃ, ahorattamatanditaṃ;Taṃ ve bhaddekarattoti, santo ācikkhate munī’’ti.

The peaceful sage calls that person ‘one who has had a single excellent night’—one who dwells thus, ardent, untiring day and night.

‘‘‘Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’ti? Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā mahākaccāno satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā mahākaccāno imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’ti. Atha kho mayaṃ, bhante, yenāyasmā mahākaccāno tenupasaṅkamimha; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākaccānaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipucchimha. Tesaṃ no, bhante, āyasmatā mahākaccānena imehi ākārehi imehi padehi imehi byañjanehi attho vibhatto’’ti.

‘Who now could explain in detail the meaning of this brief statement stated by the Blessed One, which has not been analyzed in detail?’ Then, venerable sir, it occurred to us: ‘This Venerable Mahākaccāna is both praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise companions in the holy life. Venerable Mahākaccāna is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief statement stated by the Blessed One, which has not been analyzed in detail. Why don’t we go to him and ask him about this matter?’ Then, venerable sir, we approached Venerable Mahākaccāna and asked him about this matter. Venerable sir, Venerable Mahākaccāna explained the meaning to us with these reasons, in these terms, and with these expressions.

‘‘Paṇḍito, bhikkhave, mahākaccāno; mahāpañño, bhikkhave mahākaccāno. Maṃ cepi tumhe, bhikkhave, etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha, ahampi taṃ evamevaṃ byākareyyaṃ yathā taṃ mahākaccānena byākataṃ. Eso, cevetassa attho. Evañca naṃ dhārethā’’ti.

Wise, bhikkhus, is Mahākaccāna; of great wisdom, bhikkhus, is Mahākaccāna. Even if you were to ask me about this matter, I would explain it in the same way as Mahākaccāna has explained it. This is indeed its meaning. And so you should remember it.

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

The Blessed One said this. The bhikkhus, delighted, rejoiced at the Blessed One’s words.

Mahākaccānabhaddekarattasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The Discourse on Mahākaccāna and One Who Has Had a Single Excellent Night, the Third, is finished.
