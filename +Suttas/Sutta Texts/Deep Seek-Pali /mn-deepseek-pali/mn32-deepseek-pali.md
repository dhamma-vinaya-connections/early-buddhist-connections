# 32. Mahāgosiṅgasuttaṃ

## 32. The Great Gosinga Discourse

332. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā gosiṅgasālavanadāye viharati sambahulehi abhiññātehi abhiññātehi therehi sāvakehi saddhiṃ – āyasmatā ca sāriputtena āyasmatā ca mahāmoggallānena āyasmatā ca mahākassapena āyasmatā ca anuruddhena āyasmatā ca revatena āyasmatā ca ānandena, aññehi ca abhiññātehi abhiññātehi therehi sāvakehi saddhiṃ. Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yenāyasmā mahākassapo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahākassapaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘āyāmāvuso, kassapa, yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkamissāma dhammassavanāyā’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho āyasmā mahākassapo āyasmato mahāmoggallānassa paccassosi. Atha kho āyasmā ca mahāmoggallāno āyasmā ca mahākassapo āyasmā ca anuruddho yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkamiṃsu dhammassavanāya. Addasā kho āyasmā ānando āyasmantañca mahāmoggallānaṃ āyasmantañca mahākassapaṃ āyasmantañca anuruddhaṃ yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkamante dhammassavanāya. Disvāna yenāyasmā revato tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ revataṃ etadavoca – ‘‘upasaṅkamantā kho amū, āvuso [āyasmantāvuso (ka.)] revata, sappurisā yenāyasmā sāriputto tena dhammassavanāya. Āyāmāvuso revata, yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkamissāma dhammassavanāyā’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho āyasmā revato āyasmato ānandassa paccassosi. Atha kho āyasmā ca revato āyasmā ca ānando yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkamiṃsu dhammassavanāya.

332.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling in the Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood, together with many well-known and eminent senior disciples: the Venerable Sāriputta, the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna, the Venerable Mahākassapa, the Venerable Anuruddha, the Venerable Revata, the Venerable Ānanda, and other well-known and eminent senior disciples. Then, in the evening, the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna rose from seclusion and approached the Venerable Mahākassapa. Having approached, he said to the Venerable Mahākassapa: “Come, friend Kassapa, let us approach the Venerable Sāriputta to listen to the Dhamma.” “Very well, friend,” replied the Venerable Mahākassapa to the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna. Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna, the Venerable Mahākassapa, and the Venerable Anuruddha approached the Venerable Sāriputta to listen to the Dhamma. The Venerable Ānanda saw the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna, the Venerable Mahākassapa, and the Venerable Anuruddha approaching the Venerable Sāriputta to listen to the Dhamma. Having seen this, he approached the Venerable Revata. Having approached, he said to the Venerable Revata: “These good men, friend Revata, are approaching the Venerable Sāriputta to listen to the Dhamma. Come, friend Revata, let us approach the Venerable Sāriputta to listen to the Dhamma.” “Very well, friend,” replied the Venerable Revata to the Venerable Ānanda. Then the Venerable Revata and the Venerable Ānanda approached the Venerable Sāriputta to listen to the Dhamma.

333. Addasā kho āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantañca revataṃ āyasmantañca ānandaṃ dūratova āgacchante. Disvāna āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘etu kho āyasmā ānando! Svāgataṃ āyasmato ānandassa bhagavato upaṭṭhākassa bhagavato santikāvacarassa! Ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso ānanda, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā [sabbapāliphullā (sī.)] sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti; kathaṃrūpena, āvuso ānanda, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti? ‘‘Idhāvuso sāriputta, bhikkhu bahussuto hoti sutadharo sutasannicayo. Ye te dhammā ādikalyāṇā majjhekalyāṇā pariyosānakalyāṇā sātthā sabyañjanā; kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ abhivadanti, tathārūpāssa dhammā bahussutā honti, dhātā [dhatā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)], vacasā paricitā, manasānupekkhitā, diṭṭhiyā suppaṭividdhā. So catassannaṃ parisānaṃ dhammaṃ deseti parimaṇḍalehi padabyañjanehi anuppabandhehi [appabaddhehi (sī. pī.)] anusayasamugghātāya. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti.

333.Venerable Sāriputta saw Venerable Revata and Venerable Ānanda approaching from afar. Upon seeing them, he said to Venerable Ānanda: “Come, Venerable Ānanda! Welcome, Venerable Ānanda, attendant of the Blessed One, who stays close to the Blessed One! Delightful, friend Ānanda, is Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood. The night is moonlit, all the sāla trees are in full bloom, and heavenly fragrances, it seems, waft around. In what way, friend Ānanda, would a monk beautify Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood?” “Here, friend Sāriputta, a monk is very learned, a repository of learning, one who retains what he has heard. Those teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with meaning and phrasing, proclaiming a holy life that is perfectly complete and pure—such teachings he has heard much, retained, recited, considered in mind, and penetrated well by view. He teaches the Dhamma to the four assemblies with complete words and phrases, connected, for the elimination of underlying tendencies. In this way, friend Sāriputta, a monk would beautify Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood.”

334. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ revataṃ etadavoca – ‘‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso revata, āyasmatā ānandena yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ revataṃ pucchāma – ‘ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso revata, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti; kathaṃrūpena, āvuso revata, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti? ‘‘Idhāvuso sāriputta, bhikkhu paṭisallānārāmo hoti paṭisallānarato, ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno, vipassanāya samannāgato, brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti.

334.When this was said, Venerable Sāriputta said to Venerable Revata: “Venerable Revata, Venerable Ānanda has explained according to his own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Revata: ‘Delightful, Venerable Revata, is the Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood; the night is moonlit; the Sāla trees are all in full bloom; divine scents, I think, are wafting around. In what way, Venerable Revata, would a bhikkhu beautify the Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood?’” “Here, Venerable Sāriputta, a bhikkhu is fond of seclusion, devoted to seclusion, dedicated to internal tranquility of mind, not neglecting jhāna, endowed with insight, and one who cultivates empty dwellings. In such a way, Venerable Sāriputta, would a bhikkhu beautify the Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood.”

335. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso anuruddha, āyasmatā revatena yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ pucchāma – ‘ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso anuruddha, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti; kathaṃrūpena, āvuso anuruddha, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti? ‘‘Idhāvuso sāriputta, bhikkhu dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena sahassaṃ lokānaṃ voloketi. Seyyathāpi, āvuso sāriputta, cakkhumā puriso uparipāsādavaragato sahassaṃ nemimaṇḍalānaṃ volokeyya; evameva kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhu dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena sahassaṃ lokānaṃ voloketi. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti.

335.When this was said, Venerable Sāriputta addressed Venerable Anuruddha: “Venerable Anuruddha, Venerable Revata has spoken according to his own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Anuruddha: ‘Delightful, friend Anuruddha, is the Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood, the night is moonlit, all the Sāla trees are in full bloom, divine, I think, fragrances are wafting. By what kind of monk, friend Anuruddha, would the Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood be beautified?’” “Here, friend Sāriputta, a monk surveys a thousand worlds with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human. Just as, friend Sāriputta, a man with good eyes standing on the upper terrace of a palace could survey a thousand wheel-rims; even so, friend Sāriputta, a monk surveys a thousand worlds with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human. Such a monk, friend Sāriputta, would beautify the Gosinga Sāla-tree Wood.”

336. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ mahākassapaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso kassapa, āyasmatā anuruddhena yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ mahākassapaṃ pucchāma – ‘ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso kassapa, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti; kathaṃrūpena, āvuso kassapa, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti? ‘‘Idhāvuso sāriputta, bhikkhu attanā ca āraññiko hoti āraññikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca piṇḍapātiko hoti piṇḍapātikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca paṃsukūliko hoti paṃsukūlikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca tecīvariko hoti tecīvarikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca appiccho hoti appicchatāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca santuṭṭho hoti santuṭṭhiyā ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca pavivitto hoti pavivekassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca asaṃsaṭṭho hoti asaṃsaggassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca āraddhavīriyo hoti vīriyārambhassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca sīlasampanno hoti sīlasampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca samādhisampanno hoti samādhisampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca paññāsampanno hoti paññāsampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca vimuttisampanno hoti vimuttisampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampanno hoti vimuttiñāṇadassanasampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti.

336.When this was said, Venerable Sāriputta said to Venerable Mahākassapa: “Venerable Kassapa, Venerable Anuruddha has explained according to his own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Mahākassapa: ‘Delightful, Venerable Kassapa, is the Gosiṅga Sāla forest, the night is moonlit, all the Sāla trees are in full bloom, divine fragrances, it seems, are wafting. What kind of monk, Venerable Kassapa, would adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest?’”“Here, Venerable Sāriputta, a monk is a forest dweller himself, and speaks in praise of forest dwelling; he is an almsfood seeker himself, and speaks in praise of almsfood seeking; he is a wearer of rag-robes himself, and speaks in praise of wearing rag-robes; he is a wearer of three robes himself, and speaks in praise of wearing three robes; he is of few desires himself, and speaks in praise of having few desires; he is content himself, and speaks in praise of contentment; he is secluded himself, and speaks in praise of seclusion; he is unmingled himself, and speaks in praise of being unmingled; he is one who has aroused energy himself, and speaks in praise of arousing energy; he is endowed with virtue himself, and speaks in praise of endowment with virtue; he is endowed with samādhi himself, and speaks in praise of endowment with samādhi; he is endowed with wisdom himself, and speaks in praise of endowment with wisdom; he is endowed with liberation himself, and speaks in praise of endowment with liberation; he is endowed with the knowledge and vision of liberation himself, and speaks in praise of endowment with the knowledge and vision of liberation. Such a monk, Venerable Sāriputta, would adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.”

337. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso moggallāna, āyasmatā mahākassapena yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ pucchāma – ‘ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso moggallāna, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti; kathaṃrūpena, āvuso moggallāna, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti? ‘‘Idhāvuso sāriputta, dve bhikkhū abhidhammakathaṃ kathenti, te aññamaññaṃ pañhaṃ pucchanti, aññamaññassa pañhaṃ puṭṭhā vissajjenti, no ca saṃsādenti [saṃsārenti (ka.)], dhammī ca nesaṃ kathā pavattinī hoti. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti.

337.When this was said, Venerable Sāriputta said to Venerable Mahāmoggallāna: “Venerable Moggallāna, Venerable Mahākassapa has explained according to his own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Mahāmoggallāna: ‘Delightful, friend Moggallāna, is the Gosiṅga Sāla forest, the night is moonlit, all the Sāla trees are in full bloom, divine fragrances, it seems, are wafting. What kind of monk, friend Moggallāna, would adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest?’”“Here, friend Sāriputta, two bhikkhus discuss the Abhidhamma; they ask each other questions, and questioned, they explain to each other; they do not falter, and their talk proceeds in accordance with the Dhamma. Such a monk, friend Sāriputta, would adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.”

338. Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso sāriputta, amhehi sabbeheva yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ pucchāma – ‘ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso sāriputta, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti; kathaṃrūpena, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti? ‘‘Idhāvuso moggallāna, bhikkhu cittaṃ vasaṃ vatteti, no ca bhikkhu cittassa vasena vattati. So yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati majjhanhikasamayaṃ [majjhantikasamayaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati sāyanhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā sāyanhasamayaṃ viharati. Seyyathāpi, āvuso moggallāna, rañño vā rājamahāmattassa vā nānārattānaṃ dussānaṃ dussakaraṇḍako pūro assa. So yaññadeva dussayugaṃ ākaṅkheyya pubbaṇhasamayaṃ pārupituṃ, taṃ tadeva dussayugaṃ pubbaṇhasamayaṃ pārupeyya; yaññadeva dussayugaṃ ākaṅkheyya majjhanhikasamayaṃ pārupituṃ, taṃ tadeva dussayugaṃ majjhanhikasamayaṃ pārupeyya; yaññadeva dussayugaṃ ākaṅkheyya sāyanhasamayaṃ pārupituṃ, taṃ tadeva dussayugaṃ sāyanhasamayaṃ pārupeyya. Evameva kho, āvuso moggallāna, bhikkhu cittaṃ vasaṃ vatteti, no ca bhikkhu cittassa vasena vattati. So yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati sāyanhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā sāyanhasamayaṃ viharati. Evarūpena kho, āvuso moggallāna, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti.

338.Then Venerable Mahāmoggallāna said to Venerable Sāriputta: “Friend Sāriputta, we have each declared according to our own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Sāriputta: ‘Delightful, friend Sāriputta, is the Gosiṅga Sāla forest, the night is moonlit, all the Sāla trees are in full bloom, divine fragrances, it seems, are wafting. What kind of monk, friend Sāriputta, would adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest?’”“Here, friend Moggallāna, a monk wields mastery over his mind, and his mind does not wield mastery over the monk. Whatever dwelling attainment he wishes to enter upon in the morning, he enters upon that dwelling attainment in the morning; whatever dwelling attainment he wishes to enter upon at midday, he enters upon that dwelling attainment at midday; whatever dwelling attainment he wishes to enter upon in the evening, he enters upon that dwelling attainment in the evening. Just as, friend Moggallāna, a king or a royal minister might have a chest full of cloths of various colors. Whatever pair of cloths he wishes to wear in the morning, he wears that very pair in the morning; whatever pair of cloths he wishes to wear at midday, he wears that very pair at midday; whatever pair of cloths he wishes to wear in the evening, he wears that very pair in the evening. In the same way, friend Moggallāna, a monk wields mastery over his mind, and his mind does not wield mastery over the monk. Whatever dwelling attainment he wishes to enter upon in the morning, he enters upon that dwelling attainment in the morning; whatever dwelling attainment he wishes to enter upon at midday, he enters upon that dwelling attainment at midday; whatever dwelling attainment he wishes to enter upon in the evening, he enters upon that dwelling attainment in the evening. Such a monk, friend Moggallāna, would adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.”

339. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto te āyasmante etadavoca – ‘‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso, amhehi sabbeheva yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Āyāmāvuso, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamissāma; upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ bhagavato ārocessāma. Yathā no bhagavā byākarissati tathā naṃ dhāressāmā’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te āyasmanto āyasmato sāriputtassa paccassosuṃ. Atha kho te āyasmanto yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘addasaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhante, āyasmantañca revataṃ āyasmantañca ānandaṃ dūratova āgacchante. Disvāna āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘etu kho āyasmā ānando! Svāgataṃ āyasmato ānandassa bhagavato upaṭṭhākassa bhagavato santikāvacarassa! Ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso ānanda, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti; kathaṃrūpena, āvuso ānanda, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’ti? Evaṃ vutte, bhante, āyasmā ānando maṃ etadavoca – ‘idhāvuso, sāriputta, bhikkhu bahussuto hoti sutadharo…pe… anusayasamugghātāya. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Yathā taṃ ānandova sammā byākaramāno byākareyya. Ānando hi, sāriputta, bahussuto sutadharo sutasannicayo. Ye te dhammā ādikalyāṇā majjhekalyāṇā pariyosānakalyāṇā sātthā sabyañjanā; kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ abhivadanti, tathārūpāssa dhammā bahussutā honti, dhātā, vacasā paricitā, manasānupekkhitā, diṭṭhiyā suppaṭividdhā. So catassannaṃ parisānaṃ dhammaṃ deseti parimaṇḍalehi padabyañjanehi anuppabandhehi anusayasamugghātāyā’’ti.

339.Then Venerable Sāriputta addressed those venerable ones: “Friends, we have each declared according to our own inspiration. Come, friends, let us approach the Blessed One; having approached, we will report this matter to the Blessed One. As the Blessed One explains it to us, so we shall remember it.” “Yes, friend,” those venerable ones replied to Venerable Sāriputta. Then those venerable ones approached the Blessed One; having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Venerable Sāriputta said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, I saw Venerable Revata and Venerable Ānanda coming from a distance. Seeing them, I said to Venerable Ānanda: ‘Come, Venerable Ānanda! Welcome to Venerable Ānanda, the Blessed One’s attendant who dwells near the Blessed One! Delightful, friend Ānanda, is the Gosiṅga Sāla forest, the night is moonlit, all the Sāla trees are in full bloom, divine fragrances, it seems, are wafting. By what kind of monk, friend Ānanda, would the Gosiṅga Sāla forest be adorned?’ When this was said, Venerable Ānanda said to me: ‘Here, friend Sāriputta, a monk is very learned, a bearer of the teachings… for the uprooting of underlying tendencies. By such a monk, friend Sāriputta, would the Gosiṅga Sāla forest be adorned.’ ” “Good, good, Sāriputta! As Ānanda would rightly declare, so he has declared. For Ānanda, Sāriputta, is very learned, a bearer of the teachings, an accumulator of what he has heard. Those teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, good in the end, with the right meaning and phrasing, proclaiming the perfectly complete and pure holy life—such teachings he has heard much, retained, recited verbally, examined mentally, and penetrated well by view. He teaches the Dhamma to the four assemblies with well-rounded phrases and syllables, connected, leading to the uprooting of underlying tendencies.”

340. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ, bhante, āyasmantaṃ revataṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso revata āyasmatā ānandena yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ revataṃ pucchāma – ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso revata, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā maññe gandhā sampavanti. Kathaṃrūpena, āvuso revata, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’ti? Evaṃ vutte, bhante, āyasmā revato maṃ etadavoca – ‘idhāvuso sāriputta bhikkhu paṭisallānārāmo hoti paṭisallānarato, ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto, anirākatajjhāno, vipassanāya samannāgato, brūhetā suññāgārānaṃ. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Yathā taṃ revatova sammā byākaramāno byākareyya. Revato hi, sāriputta, paṭisallānārāmo paṭisallānarato, ajjhattaṃ cetosamathamanuyutto anirākatajjhāno, vipassanāya samannāgato brūhetā suññāgārāna’’nti.

340.“When this was said, Venerable sir, I said to Venerable Revata: ‘Venerable Ānanda has explained according to his own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Revata: Delightful, friend Revata, is the Gosiṅga Sāla forest, the night is moonlit, all the Sāla trees are in full bloom, divine fragrances, it seems, are wafting. By what kind of monk, friend Revata, would the Gosiṅga Sāla forest be adorned?’ When this was said, Venerable Revata said to me: ‘Here, friend Sāriputta, a monk delights in seclusion, is devoted to seclusion, applies himself to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates empty dwellings. By such a monk, friend Sāriputta, would the Gosiṅga Sāla forest be adorned.’ ” “Good, good, Sāriputta! As Revata would rightly declare, so he has declared. For Revata, Sāriputta, delights in seclusion, is devoted to seclusion, applies himself to internal serenity of mind, does not neglect jhāna, is endowed with insight, and cultivates empty dwellings.”

341. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ, bhante, āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘byākataṃ kho āvuso anuruddha āyasmatā revatena…pe… kathaṃrūpena, āvuso anuruddha, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhante, āyasmā anuruddho maṃ etadavoca – ‘idhāvuso sāriputta, bhikkhu dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena sahassaṃ lokānaṃ voloketi. Seyyathāpi, āvuso sāriputta, cakkhumā puriso…pe… evarūpena kho āvuso sāriputta bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta, yathā taṃ anuruddhova sammā byākaramāno byākareyya. Anuruddho hi, sāriputta, dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena sahassaṃ lokānaṃ voloketī’’ti.

341.When this was said, I said to Venerable Anuruddha: ‘Venerable Anuruddha, Venerable Revata has explained it… In what way, friend Anuruddha, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest?’ When this was said, Venerable Anuruddha said to me: ‘Here, friend Sāriputta, a monk with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, surveys a thousand worlds. Just as, friend Sāriputta, a keen-sighted man… In such a way, friend Sāriputta, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.’ “Good, good, Sāriputta! Just as Anuruddha himself would rightly explain, so he has explained. For Anuruddha, Sāriputta, with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, surveys a thousand worlds.”

342. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ, bhante, āyasmantaṃ mahākassapaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso kassapa āyasmatā anuruddhena yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ mahākassapaṃ pucchāma…pe… kathaṃ rūpena kho, āvuso kassapa, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’ti? Evaṃ vutte bhante, āyasmā mahākassapo maṃ etadavoca – ‘idhāvuso sāriputta, bhikkhu attanā ca āraññiko hoti āraññikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca piṇḍapātiko hoti…pe… attanā ca paṃsukūliko hoti…pe… attanā ca tecīvariko hoti…pe… attanā ca appiccho hoti…pe… attanā ca santuṭṭho hoti…pe… attanā ca pavivitto hoti…pe… attanā ca asaṃsaṭṭho hoti…pe… attanā ca āraddhavīriyo hoti…pe… attanā ca sīlasampanno hoti…pe… attanā ca samādhisampanno hoti…pe… attanā ca paññāsampanno hoti… attanā ca vimuttisampanno hoti… attanā ca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampanno hoti vimuttiñāṇadassanasampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Yathā taṃ kassapova sammā byākaramāno byākareyya. Kassapo hi, sāriputta, attanā ca āraññiko āraññikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca piṇḍapātiko piṇḍapātikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca paṃsukūliko paṃsukūlikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca tecīvariko tecīvarikattassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca appiccho appicchatāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca santuṭṭho santuṭṭhiyā ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca pavivitto pavivekassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca asaṃsaṭṭho asaṃsaggassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca āraddhavīriyo vīriyārambhassa ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca sīlasampanno sīlasampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca samādhisampanno samādhisampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca paññāsampanno paññāsampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca vimuttisampanno vimuttisampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī, attanā ca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampanno vimuttiñāṇadassanasampadāya ca vaṇṇavādī’’ti.

342.When this was said, I said to Venerable Mahākassapa: ‘Venerable Kassapa, Venerable Anuruddha has explained according to his own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Mahākassapa… In what way, friend Kassapa, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest?’ When this was said, Venerable Mahākassapa said to me: ‘Here, friend Sāriputta, a monk is himself a forest-dweller and speaks in praise of forest-dwelling; he is himself an alms-goer and speaks in praise of alms-going; he is himself a rag-robe wearer and speaks in praise of wearing rag-robes; he is himself a wearer of three robes and speaks in praise of wearing three robes; he is himself of few wishes and speaks in praise of having few wishes; he is himself content and speaks in praise of contentment; he is himself secluded and speaks in praise of seclusion; he is himself unentangled and speaks in praise of non-entanglement; he is himself energetic and speaks in praise of the arousal of energy; he is himself accomplished in virtue and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of virtue; he is himself accomplished in samādhi and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of samādhi; he is himself accomplished in wisdom and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of wisdom; he is himself accomplished in liberation and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of liberation; he is himself accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of the knowledge and vision of liberation. In such a way, friend Sāriputta, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.’ “Good, good, Sāriputta! Just as Kassapa himself would rightly explain, so he has explained. For Kassapa, Sāriputta, is himself a forest-dweller and speaks in praise of forest-dwelling; he is himself an alms-goer and speaks in praise of alms-going; he is himself a rag-robe wearer and speaks in praise of wearing rag-robes; he is himself a wearer of three robes and speaks in praise of wearing three robes; he is himself of few wishes and speaks in praise of having few wishes; he is himself content and speaks in praise of contentment; he is himself secluded and speaks in praise of seclusion; he is himself unentangled and speaks in praise of non-entanglement; he is himself energetic and speaks in praise of the arousal of energy; he is himself accomplished in virtue and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of virtue; he is himself accomplished in samādhi and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of samādhi; he is himself accomplished in wisdom and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of wisdom; he is himself accomplished in liberation and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of liberation; he is himself accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation and speaks in praise of the accomplishment of the knowledge and vision of liberation.”

343. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte, ahaṃ bhante āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso moggallāna, āyasmatā mahākassapena yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ pucchāma…pe… kathaṃrūpena, āvuso moggallāna, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’ti? Evaṃ vutte, bhante, āyasmā mahāmoggallāno maṃ etadavoca – ‘idhāvuso sāriputta, dve bhikkhū abhidhammakathaṃ kathenti. Te aññamaññaṃ pañhaṃ pucchanti, aññamaññassa pañhaṃ puṭṭhā vissajjenti, no ca saṃsādenti, dhammī ca nesaṃ kathā pavattinī hoti. Evarūpena kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta, yathā taṃ moggallānova sammā byākaramāno byākareyya. Moggallāno hi, sāriputta, dhammakathiko’’ti.

343.When this was said, I said to Venerable Mahāmoggallāna: ‘Venerable Moggallāna, Venerable Mahākassapa has explained according to his own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Mahāmoggallāna… In what way, friend Moggallāna, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest?’ When this was said, Venerable Mahāmoggallāna said to me: ‘Here, friend Sāriputta, two bhikkhus engage in a discussion on the Abhidhamma. They ask each other questions, and having been asked, they answer each other without getting bogged down, and their talk proceeds in accordance with the Dhamma. In such a way, friend Sāriputta, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.’ “Good, good, Sāriputta! Just as Moggallāna himself would rightly explain, so he has explained. For Moggallāna, Sāriputta, is a Dhamma speaker.”

344. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘atha khvāhaṃ, bhante, āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso sāriputta, amhehi sabbeheva yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Tattha dāni mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ pucchāma – ramaṇīyaṃ, āvuso sāriputta, gosiṅgasālavanaṃ, dosinā ratti, sabbaphāliphullā sālā, dibbā, maññe, gandhā sampavanti. Kathaṃrūpena, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’ti? Evaṃ vutte, bhante, āyasmā sāriputto maṃ etadavoca – ‘idhāvuso, moggallāna, bhikkhu cittaṃ vasaṃ vatteti no ca bhikkhu cittassa vasena vattati. So yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati sāyanhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā sāyanhasamayaṃ viharati. Seyyathāpi, āvuso moggallāna, rañño vā rājamahāmattassa vā nānārattānaṃ dussānaṃ dussakaraṇḍako pūro assa. So yaññadeva dussayugaṃ ākaṅkheyya pubbaṇhasamayaṃ pārupituṃ, taṃ tadeva dussayugaṃ pubbaṇhasamayaṃ pārupeyya; yaññadeva dussayugaṃ ākaṅkheyya majjhanhikasamayaṃ pārupituṃ, taṃ tadeva dussayugaṃ majjhanhikasamayaṃ pārupeyya; yaññadeva dussayugaṃ ākaṅkheyya sāyanhasamayaṃ pārupituṃ, taṃ tadeva dussayugaṃ sāyanhasamayaṃ pārupeyya. Evameva kho, āvuso moggallāna, bhikkhu cittaṃ vasaṃ vatteti, no ca bhikkhu cittassa vasena vattati. So yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati sāyanhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā sāyanhasamayaṃ viharati. Evarūpena kho, āvuso moggallāna, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, moggallāna! Yathā taṃ sāriputtova sammā byākaramāno byākareyya. Sāriputto hi, moggallāna, cittaṃ vasaṃ vatteti no ca sāriputto cittassa vasena vattati. So yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā majjhanhikasamayaṃ viharati; yāya vihārasamāpattiyā ākaṅkhati sāyanhasamayaṃ viharituṃ, tāya vihārasamāpattiyā sāyanhasamayaṃ viharatī’’ti.

344.When this was said, Venerable Mahāmoggallāna addressed the Blessed One: “Then, venerable sir, I said to Venerable Sāriputta: ‘Friend Sāriputta, we have all explained according to our own inspiration. Now we ask Venerable Sāriputta: The Gosiṅga Sāla forest is delightful, friend Sāriputta, the night is moonlit, the sāla trees are in full bloom, and divine fragrances, I think, are wafting through the air. In what way, friend Sāriputta, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest?’ When this was said, venerable sir, Venerable Sāriputta said to me: ‘Here, friend Moggallāna, a monk controls his mind and is not controlled by his mind. Whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the morning, he abides in that abiding during the morning; whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the midday, he abides in that abiding during the midday; whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the evening, he abides in that abiding during the evening. Just as, friend Moggallāna, a king or a royal minister might have a chest full of garments of various hues. Whatever pair of garments he wishes to put on in the morning, he puts on that pair in the morning; whatever pair he wishes to put on at midday, he puts on that pair at midday; whatever pair he wishes to put on in the evening, he puts on that pair in the evening. In the same way, friend Moggallāna, a monk controls his mind and is not controlled by his mind. Whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the morning, he abides in that abiding during the morning; whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the midday, he abides in that abiding during the midday; whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the evening, he abides in that abiding during the evening. In such a way, friend Moggallāna, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.’ “Good, good, Moggallāna! Just as Sāriputta himself would rightly explain, so he has explained. For Sāriputta, Moggallāna, controls his mind and is not controlled by his mind. Whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the morning, he abides in that abiding during the morning; whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the midday, he abides in that abiding during the midday; whatever abiding he wishes to abide in during the evening, he abides in that abiding during the evening.”

345. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kassa nu kho, bhante, subhāsita’’nti? ‘‘Sabbesaṃ vo, sāriputta, subhāsitaṃ pariyāyena. Api ca mamapi suṇātha yathārūpena bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyya. Idha, sāriputta, bhikkhu pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto nisīdati pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ imaṃ pallaṅkaṃ bhindissāmi yāva me nānupādāya āsavehi cittaṃ vimuccissatī’ti. Evarūpena kho, sāriputta, bhikkhunā gosiṅgasālavanaṃ sobheyyā’’ti.

345.When this was said, Venerable Sāriputta said to the Blessed One: “Whose words, venerable sir, were well spoken?” “All of your words, Sāriputta, were well spoken in a way. Nevertheless, listen to me as well as to how a monk would adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest. Here, Sāriputta, a monk, after his meal, on his return from the alms round, sits down, folding his legs crosswise, setting his body erect, and establishing mindfulness in front of him, thinking: ‘I shall not break this sitting posture until my mind is liberated from the taints without clinging.’ In such a way, Sāriputta, would a monk adorn the Gosiṅga Sāla forest.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te āyasmanto [te bhikkhū (ka.)] bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

The Blessed One said this. The venerable ones were pleased and rejoiced at the Blessed One’s words.

Mahāgosiṅgasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The Great Gosiṅga Sutta, the second, is concluded.
