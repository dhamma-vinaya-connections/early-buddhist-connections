# 70. Kīṭāgirisuttaṃ

## 70. The Kīṭāgiri Sutta

174. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kāsīsu cārikaṃ carati mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘ahaṃ kho, bhikkhave, aññatreva rattibhojanā [rattibhojanaṃ (ka.)] bhuñjāmi. Aññatra kho panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, rattibhojanā bhuñjamāno appābādhatañca sañjānāmi appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Etha, tumhepi, bhikkhave, aññatreva rattibhojanā bhuñjatha. Aññatra kho pana, bhikkhave, tumhepi rattibhojanā bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānissatha appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañcā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā kāsīsu anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena kīṭāgiri nāma kāsīnaṃ nigamo tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā kīṭāgirismiṃ viharati kāsīnaṃ nigame.

174.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was traveling on tour in the Kasi country with a large company of monks. There the Blessed One addressed the monks, saying: "Monks, I abstain from the evening meal. Abstaining from the evening meal, monks, I am aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. Come, monks, do you too abstain from the evening meal. Abstaining from the evening meal, monks, you too will be aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding." "Yes, venerable sir," those monks replied to the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One, traveling by stages through the Kasi country, arrived at a market town of the Kasis named Kīṭāgiri. There the Blessed One stayed at Kīṭāgiri, in the market town of the Kasis.

175. Tena kho pana samayena assajipunabbasukā nāma bhikkhū kīṭāgirismiṃ āvāsikā honti. Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū yena assajipunabbasukā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā assajipunabbasuke bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘bhagavā kho, āvuso, aññatreva rattibhojanā bhuñjati bhikkhusaṅgho ca. Aññatra kho panāvuso, rattibhojanā bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānanti appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Etha, tumhepi, āvuso, aññatreva rattibhojanā bhuñjatha. Aññatra kho panāvuso, tumhepi rattibhojanā bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānissatha appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañcā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, assajipunabbasukā bhikkhū te bhikkhū etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mayaṃ kho, āvuso, sāyañceva bhuñjāma pāto ca divā ca vikāle. Te mayaṃ sāyañceva bhuñjamānā pāto ca divā ca vikāle appābādhatañca sañjānāma appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Te mayaṃ kiṃ sandiṭṭhikaṃ hitvā kālikaṃ anudhāvissāma? Sāyañceva mayaṃ bhuñjissāma pāto ca divā ca vikāle’’ti.

175.Now at that time, monks named Assaji and Punabbasu were resident at Kīṭāgiri. Then a number of monks approached the monks Assaji and Punabbasu, and said to them: "The Blessed One, friends, abstains from the evening meal, and so does the Sangha of monks. Abstaining from the evening meal, friends, they are aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. Come, friends, you too should abstain from the evening meal. Abstaining from the evening meal, friends, you too will be aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding." When this was said, the monks Assaji and Punabbasu said to those monks: "We, friends, eat in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time. Eating in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time, we are aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. Why should we abandon what is visible here and now and pursue what is subject to time? We will continue to eat in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time."

Yato kho te bhikkhū nāsakkhiṃsu assajipunabbasuke bhikkhū saññāpetuṃ, atha yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idha mayaṃ, bhante, yena assajipunabbasukā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkamimha; upasaṅkamitvā assajipunabbasuke bhikkhū etadavocumha – ‘bhagavā kho, āvuso, aññatreva rattibhojanā bhuñjati bhikkhusaṅgho ca; aññatra kho panāvuso, rattibhojanā bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānanti appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Etha, tumhepi, āvuso, aññatreva rattibhojanā bhuñjatha. Aññatra kho panāvuso, tumhepi rattibhojanā bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānissatha appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañcā’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhante, assajipunabbasukā bhikkhū amhe etadavocuṃ – ‘mayaṃ kho, āvuso, sāyañceva bhuñjāma pāto ca divā ca vikāle. Te mayaṃ sāyañceva bhuñjamānā pāto ca divā ca vikāle appābādhatañca sañjānāma appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Te mayaṃ kiṃ sandiṭṭhikaṃ hitvā kālikaṃ anudhāvissāma? Sāyañceva mayaṃ bhuñjissāma pāto ca divā ca vikāle’ti. Yato kho mayaṃ, bhante, nāsakkhimha assajipunabbasuke bhikkhū saññāpetuṃ, atha mayaṃ etamatthaṃ bhagavato ārocemā’’ti.

When those monks were unable to persuade the monks Assaji and Punabbasu, they approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those monks said to the Blessed One: "Here, Venerable Sir, we approached the monks Assaji and Punabbasu and said to them: ‘Friends, the Blessed One abstains from the evening meal, and so does the Sangha of monks. Abstaining from the evening meal, friends, they are aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. Come, friends, you too should abstain from the evening meal. Abstaining from the evening meal, friends, you too will be aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding.’ When this was said, Venerable Sir, the monks Assaji and Punabbasu replied to us: ‘Friends, we eat in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time. Eating in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time, we are aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. Why should we abandon what is visible here and now and pursue what is subject to time? We will continue to eat in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time.’ Since we were unable to persuade the monks Assaji and Punabbasu, Venerable Sir, we have reported this matter to the Blessed One."

176. Atha kho bhagavā aññataraṃ bhikkhuṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, mama vacanena assajipunabbasuke bhikkhū āmantehi – ‘satthā āyasmante āmantetī’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho so bhikkhu bhagavato paṭissutvā yena assajipunabbasukā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā assajipunabbasuke bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘satthā āyasmante āmantetī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho assajipunabbasukā bhikkhū tassa bhikkhuno paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinne kho assajipunabbasuke bhikkhū bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘saccaṃ kira, bhikkhave, sambahulā bhikkhū tumhe upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ – ‘bhagavā kho, āvuso, aññatreva rattibhojanā bhuñjati bhikkhusaṅgho ca. Aññatra kho panāvuso, rattibhojanā bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānanti appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Etha, tumhepi, āvuso, aññatreva rattibhojanā bhuñjatha. Aññatra kho panāvuso, tumhepi rattibhojanā bhuñjamānā appābādhatañca sañjānissatha appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañcā’ti. Evaṃ vutte kira [kiṃ nu (ka.)], bhikkhave, tumhe te bhikkhū evaṃ avacuttha – ‘mayaṃ kho panāvuso, sāyañceva bhuñjāma pāto ca divā ca vikāle. Te mayaṃ sāyañceva bhuñjamānā pāto ca divā ca vikāle appābādhatañca sañjānāma appātaṅkatañca lahuṭṭhānañca balañca phāsuvihārañca. Te mayaṃ kiṃ sandiṭṭhikaṃ hitvā kālikaṃ anudhāvissāma? Sāyañceva mayaṃ bhuñjissāma pāto ca divā ca vikāle’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’.

176.Then the Blessed One addressed a certain monk: "Come, monk, in my name call the monks Assaji and Punabbasu, saying, 'The Teacher calls the venerable ones.'" "Yes, venerable sir," that monk replied to the Blessed One. Having replied, he went to the monks Assaji and Punabbasu; having approached, he said to the monks Assaji and Punabbasu: "The Teacher calls the venerable ones." "Yes, friend," the monks Assaji and Punabbasu replied to that monk. Then they approached the Blessed One; having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Blessed One said to the monks Assaji and Punabbasu: "Is it true, monks, that many monks approached you and said: 'Friends, the Blessed One abstains from the evening meal, and so does the Sangha of monks. Abstaining from the evening meal, friends, they are aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. Come, friends, you too should abstain from the evening meal. Abstaining from the evening meal, friends, you too will be aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding.' When this was said, monks, did you reply to those monks: 'But, friends, we eat in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time. Eating in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time, we are aware of little illness, little affliction, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. Why should we abandon what is visible here and now and pursue what is subject to time? We will continue to eat in the evening, in the morning, at midday, and at the wrong time'?" "Yes, venerable sir."

177. ‘‘Kiṃ nu me tumhe, bhikkhave, evaṃ dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānātha yaṃ kiñcāyaṃ purisapuggalo paṭisaṃvedeti sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tassa akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Nanu me tumhe, bhikkhave, evaṃ dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānātha idhekaccassa yaṃ evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyanti, idha panekaccassa evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyanti, idha panekaccassa evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti, idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyanti, idha panekaccassa evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’.

177."Monks, do you understand the Dhamma taught by me thus: ‘Whatever pleasure, pain, or neither-pleasure-nor-pain this person experiences, their unwholesome qualities decline, and wholesome qualities increase’?" "No, venerable sir." "Monks, do you understand the Dhamma taught by me thus: ‘Here, when a certain person experiences such a pleasant feeling, their unwholesome qualities increase, and wholesome qualities decline; but here, when another person experiences such a pleasant feeling, their unwholesome qualities decline, and wholesome qualities increase. Here, when a certain person experiences such a painful feeling, their unwholesome qualities increase, and wholesome qualities decline; but here, when another person experiences such a painful feeling, their unwholesome qualities decline, and wholesome qualities increase. Here, when a certain person experiences such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, their unwholesome qualities increase, and wholesome qualities decline; but here, when another person experiences such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, their unwholesome qualities decline, and wholesome qualities increase’?" "Yes, venerable sir."

178. ‘‘Sādhu, bhikkhave! Mayā cetaṃ, bhikkhave, aññātaṃ abhavissa adiṭṭhaṃ aviditaṃ asacchikataṃ aphassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyantī’ti, evāhaṃ ajānanto ‘evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ pajahathā’ti vadeyyaṃ; api nu me etaṃ, bhikkhave, patirūpaṃ abhavissā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Yasmā ca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, mayā ñātaṃ diṭṭhaṃ viditaṃ sacchikataṃ phassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyantī’ti, tasmāhaṃ ‘evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ pajahathā’ti vadāmi. Mayā cetaṃ, bhikkhave, aññātaṃ abhavissa adiṭṭhaṃ aviditaṃ asacchikataṃ aphassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’ti, evāhaṃ ajānanto ‘evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti vadeyyaṃ; api nu me etaṃ, bhikkhave, patirūpaṃ abhavissā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Yasmā ca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, mayā ñātaṃ diṭṭhaṃ viditaṃ sacchikataṃ phassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti, kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’ti, tasmāhaṃ ‘evarūpaṃ sukhaṃ vedanaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti vadāmi.

178."Good, monks! If I, monks, had not known, seen, understood, realized, and experienced with wisdom that 'for some person here, experiencing such a pleasant feeling, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline,' then, not knowing this, would it be appropriate for me to say, 'Abandon such a pleasant feeling'?" "No, venerable sir." "But because I, monks, have known, seen, understood, realized, and experienced with wisdom that 'for some person here, experiencing such a pleasant feeling, unwholesome qualities increase and wholesome qualities decline,' therefore I say, 'Abandon such a pleasant feeling.' If I, monks, had not known, seen, understood, realized, and experienced with wisdom that 'for some person here, experiencing such a pleasant feeling, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase,' then, not knowing this, would it be appropriate for me to say, 'Dwell having attained such a pleasant feeling'?" "No, venerable sir." "But because I, monks, have known, seen, understood, realized, and experienced with wisdom that 'for some person here, experiencing such a pleasant feeling, unwholesome qualities decline and wholesome qualities increase,' therefore I say, 'Dwell having attained such a pleasant feeling.'"

179. ‘‘Mayā cetaṃ, bhikkhave, aññātaṃ abhavissa adiṭṭhaṃ aviditaṃ asacchikataṃ aphassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyantī’ti, evāhaṃ ajānanto ‘evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ pajahathā’ti vadeyyaṃ; api nu me etaṃ, bhikkhave, patirūpaṃ abhavissā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Yasmā ca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, mayā ñātaṃ diṭṭhaṃ viditaṃ sacchikataṃ phassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyantī’ti, tasmāhaṃ ‘evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ pajahathā’ti vadāmi. Mayā cetaṃ, bhikkhave, aññātaṃ abhavissa adiṭṭhaṃ aviditaṃ asacchikataṃ aphassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’ti, evāhaṃ ajānanto ‘evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti vadeyyaṃ; api nu me etaṃ, bhikkhave, patirūpaṃ abhavissā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Yasmā ca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, mayā ñātaṃ diṭṭhaṃ viditaṃ sacchikataṃ phassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’ti, tasmāhaṃ ‘evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ vedanaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti vadāmi.

179."If I, bhikkhus, had not known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a painful feeling, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline,’ then, not knowing this, would it have been proper for me to say, ‘Abandon such a painful feeling’?" "No, Bhante." "But because I, bhikkhus, have known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a painful feeling, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline,’ therefore I say, ‘Abandon such a painful feeling.’ If I, bhikkhus, had not known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a painful feeling, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase,’ then, not knowing this, would it have been proper for me to say, ‘Dwell having entered upon such a painful feeling’?" "No, Bhante." "But because I, bhikkhus, have known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a painful feeling, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase,’ therefore I say, ‘Dwell having entered upon such a painful feeling.’"

180. ‘‘Mayā cetaṃ, bhikkhave, aññātaṃ abhavissa adiṭṭhaṃ aviditaṃ asacchikataṃ aphassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyantī’ti, evāhaṃ ajānanto ‘evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ pajahathā’ti vadeyyaṃ; api nu me etaṃ, bhikkhave, patirūpaṃ abhavissā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Yasmā ca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, mayā ñātaṃ diṭṭhaṃ viditaṃ sacchikataṃ phassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhanti kusalā dhammā parihāyantī’ti, tasmāhaṃ ‘evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ pajahathā’ti vadāmi’’. Mayā cetaṃ, bhikkhave, aññātaṃ abhavissa adiṭṭhaṃ aviditaṃ asacchikataṃ aphassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’ti, evāhaṃ ajānanto ‘evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti vadeyyaṃ; api nu me etaṃ, bhikkhave, patirūpaṃ abhavissā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’. ‘‘Yasmā ca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, mayā ñātaṃ diṭṭhaṃ viditaṃ sacchikataṃ phassitaṃ paññāya – ‘idhekaccassa evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ vedayato akusalā dhammā parihāyanti kusalā dhammā abhivaḍḍhantī’ti, tasmāhaṃ ‘evarūpaṃ adukkhamasukhaṃ vedanaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti vadāmi.

180."Bhikkhus, if I had not known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline,’ then, not knowing this, would it have been proper for me to say, ‘Abandon such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling’?" "No, Bhante." "But because I have known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, unwholesome states increase and wholesome states decline,’ therefore I say, ‘Abandon such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling.’""Bhikkhus, if I had not known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase,’ then, not knowing this, would it have been proper for me to say, ‘Dwell having entered upon such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling’?" "No, Bhante." "But because I have known, seen, understood, realized, and touched with wisdom that ‘for a certain person here, experiencing such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, unwholesome states decline and wholesome states increase,’ therefore I say, ‘Dwell having entered upon such a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling.’"

181. ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sabbesaṃyeva bhikkhūnaṃ ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi; na panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sabbesaṃyeva bhikkhūnaṃ ‘na appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Ye te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū arahanto khīṇāsavā vusitavanto katakaraṇīyā ohitabhārā anuppattasadatthā parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojanā sammadaññā vimuttā, tathārūpānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ ‘na appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Kataṃ tesaṃ appamādena. Abhabbā te pamajjituṃ. Ye ca kho te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū sekkhā appattamānasā anuttaraṃ yogakkhemaṃ patthayamānā viharanti, tathārūpānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāmime āyasmanto anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamānā kalyāṇamitte bhajamānā indriyāni samannānayamānā – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyunti! Imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, imesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ appamādaphalaṃ sampassamāno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi.

181."Bhikkhus, I do not say that for all bhikkhus ‘there is something to be done with heedfulness’; nor do I say, bhikkhus, that for all bhikkhus ‘there is nothing to be done with heedfulness.’ Those bhikkhus who are arahants, whose taints are destroyed, who have lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, attained their own welfare, utterly destroyed the fetters of being, and are liberated through perfect knowledge—for such bhikkhus, I say, ‘there is nothing to be done with heedfulness.’ Why is that? What was to be done with heedfulness has been done by them. They are incapable of being negligent. But those bhikkhus who are trainees, who have not yet attained their mind's goal, who dwell aspiring for the unsurpassed security from bondage—for such bhikkhus, I say, ‘there is something to be done with heedfulness.’ Why is that? Perhaps these venerable ones, frequenting congenial dwellings, associating with good friends, balancing their faculties, might realize for themselves with direct knowledge in this very life that unsurpassed goal of the holy life for the sake of which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having realized it, abide in it! Seeing this fruit of heedfulness for these bhikkhus, I say ‘there is something to be done with heedfulness.’"

182. ‘‘Sattime, bhikkhave, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame satta? Ubhatobhāgavimutto, paññāvimutto, kāyasakkhi, diṭṭhippatto, saddhāvimutto, dhammānusārī, saddhānusārī.

182."Bhikkhus, these seven persons are found existing in the world. Which seven? The one liberated in both ways, the one liberated by wisdom, the body-witness, the view-attainer, the one liberated by faith, the Dhamma-follower, and the faith-follower.

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, puggalo ubhatobhāgavimutto? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā te kāyena phusitvā [phassitvā (sī. pī.)] viharati paññāya cassa disvā āsavā parikkhīṇā honti. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, puggalo ubhatobhāgavimutto imassa kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ‘na appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Kataṃ tassa appamādena. Abhabbo so pamajjituṃ.

"And what, bhikkhus, is the person liberated in both ways? Here, bhikkhus, a certain person here dwells having contacted with the body those peaceful liberations that transcend forms, the formless attainments, and by wisdom, having seen, his taints are destroyed. This, bhikkhus, is called the person liberated in both ways. Of this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say: ‘There is nothing to be done with heedfulness.’ Why is that? What was to be done with heedfulness has been done by him. He is incapable of being negligent."

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, puggalo paññāvimutto? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā te na kāyena phusitvā viharati, paññāya cassa disvā āsavā parikkhīṇā honti. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, puggalo paññāvimutto. Imassapi kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ‘na appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Kataṃ tassa appamādena. Abhabbo so pamajjituṃ.

"And what, bhikkhus, is the person liberated by wisdom? Here, bhikkhus, a certain person here does not dwell having contacted with the body those peaceful liberations that transcend forms, the formless attainments, but by wisdom, having seen, his taints are destroyed. This, bhikkhus, is called the person liberated by wisdom. Of this bhikkhu too, bhikkhus, I say: ‘There is nothing to be done with heedfulness.’ Why is that? What was to be done with heedfulness has been done by him. He is incapable of being negligent."

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, puggalo kāyasakkhi? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā te kāyena phusitvā viharati, paññāya cassa disvā ekacce āsavā parikkhīṇā honti. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, puggalo kāyasakkhi. Imassa kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyāti! Imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, imassa bhikkhuno appamādaphalaṃ sampassamāno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi.

"And what, bhikkhus, is the body-witness? Here, bhikkhus, a certain person here dwells having contacted with the body those peaceful liberations that transcend forms, the formless attainments, and by wisdom, having seen, some of his taints are destroyed. This, bhikkhus, is called the body-witness. Of this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say: ‘There is something to be done with heedfulness.’ Why is that? Perhaps this venerable one, frequenting congenial dwellings, associating with good friends, balancing his faculties, might realize for himself with direct knowledge in this very life that unsurpassed goal of the holy life for the sake of which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having realized it, abide in it! Seeing this fruit of heedfulness for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say: ‘There is something to be done with heedfulness.’"

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, puggalo diṭṭhippatto? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā te na kāyena phusitvā viharati, paññāya cassa disvā ekacce āsavā parikkhīṇā honti, tathāgatappaveditā cassa dhammā paññāya vodiṭṭhā honti vocaritā. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, puggalo diṭṭhippatto. Imassapi kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyāti! Imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, imassa bhikkhuno appamādaphalaṃ sampassamāno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi.

"And what, bhikkhus, is the view-attainer? Here, bhikkhus, a certain person here does not dwell having contacted with the body those peaceful liberations that transcend forms, the formless attainments, but by wisdom, having seen, some of his taints are destroyed, and the teachings proclaimed by the Tathāgata are well seen and well examined by wisdom. This, bhikkhus, is called the view-attainer. Of this bhikkhu too, bhikkhus, I say: ‘There is something to be done with heedfulness.’ Why is that? Perhaps this venerable one, frequenting congenial dwellings, associating with good friends, balancing his faculties, might realize for himself with direct knowledge in this very life that unsurpassed goal of the holy life for the sake of which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having realized it, abide in it! Seeing this fruit of heedfulness for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say: ‘There is something to be done with heedfulness.’"

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, puggalo saddhāvimutto. Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā te na kāyena phusitvā viharati, paññāya cassa disvā ekacce āsavā parikkhīṇā honti, tathāgate cassa saddhā niviṭṭhā hoti mūlajātā patiṭṭhitā. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, puggalo saddhāvimutto. Imassapi kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyāti! Imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, imassa bhikkhuno appamādaphalaṃ sampassamāno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi.

And what, bhikkhus, is a person liberated by faith? Here, bhikkhus, a certain person does not dwell having contacted with the body those peaceful liberations that transcend forms, the formless states, yet some of his taints are destroyed by seeing with wisdom. Moreover, his faith in the Tathāgata is settled, rooted, and established. This, bhikkhus, is called a person liberated by faith. Even for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say that heedfulness should be cultivated. Why is that? Perhaps this venerable one, by frequenting suitable lodgings, associating with good friends, and balancing his faculties, might realize for himself, through direct knowledge, here and now, that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having attained it, dwell therein. Seeing this fruit of heedfulness for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say that heedfulness should be cultivated.

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, puggalo dhammānusārī? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā te na kāyena phusitvā viharati, paññāya cassa disvā ekacce āsavā parikkhīṇā [disvā āsavā aparikkhīṇā (sī. pī.)] honti, tathāgatappaveditā cassa dhammā paññāya mattaso nijjhānaṃ khamanti, api cassa ime dhammā honti, seyyathidaṃ – saddhindriyaṃ, vīriyindriyaṃ, satindriyaṃ, samādhindriyaṃ, paññindriyaṃ. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, puggalo dhammānusārī. Imassapi kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyāti! Imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, imassa bhikkhuno appamādaphalaṃ sampassamāno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi.

And what, bhikkhus, is a person who follows the Dhamma? Here, bhikkhus, a certain person does not dwell having contacted with the body those peaceful liberations that transcend forms, the formless states, yet some of his taints are destroyed by seeing with wisdom. Moreover, the teachings proclaimed by the Tathāgata are accepted by him after reflecting on them to a moderate degree with wisdom. And he has these qualities: the faculty of faith, the faculty of energy, the faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of samādhi, the faculty of wisdom. This, bhikkhus, is called a person who follows the Dhamma. Even for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say that heedfulness should be cultivated. Why is that? Perhaps this venerable one, by frequenting suitable lodgings, associating with good friends, and balancing his faculties, might realize for himself, through direct knowledge, here and now, that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having attained it, dwell therein. Seeing this fruit of heedfulness for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say that heedfulness should be cultivated.

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, puggalo saddhānusārī? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo ye te santā vimokkhā atikkamma rūpe āruppā te na kāyena phusitvā viharati, paññāya cassa disvā ekacce āsavā parikkhīṇā [disvā āsavā aparikkhīṇā (sī. pī.)] honti, tathāgate cassa saddhāmattaṃ hoti pemamattaṃ, api cassa ime dhammā honti, seyyathidaṃ – saddhindriyaṃ, vīriyindriyaṃ, satindriyaṃ, samādhindriyaṃ, paññindriyaṃ. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, puggalo saddhānusārī. Imassapi kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyāti! Imaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, imassa bhikkhuno appamādaphalaṃ sampassamāno ‘appamādena karaṇīya’nti vadāmi.

And what, bhikkhus, is a person who is a faith-follower? Here, bhikkhus, a certain person does not dwell having contacted with the body those peaceful liberations that transcend forms, the formless states, but having seen with wisdom, some of his taints are destroyed, and he has merely faith, merely love for the Tathāgata. And he has these qualities: the faculty of faith, the faculty of energy, the faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of samādhi, the faculty of wisdom. This, bhikkhus, is called a person who is a faith-follower. Even for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say that heedfulness should be cultivated. Why is that? Perhaps this venerable one, by frequenting suitable lodgings, associating with good friends, and balancing his faculties, might realize for himself, through direct knowledge, here and now, that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having attained it, dwell therein. Seeing this fruit of heedfulness for this bhikkhu, bhikkhus, I say that heedfulness should be cultivated.

183. ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ādikeneva aññārādhanaṃ vadāmi; api ca, bhikkhave, anupubbasikkhā anupubbakiriyā anupubbapaṭipadā aññārādhanā hoti. Kathañca, bhikkhave, anupubbasikkhā anupubbakiriyā anupubbapaṭipadā aññārādhanā hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, saddhājāto upasaṅkamati, upasaṅkamanto payirupāsati, payirupāsanto sotaṃ odahati, ohitasoto dhammaṃ suṇāti, sutvā dhammaṃ dhāreti, dhatānaṃ [dhātānaṃ (ka.)] dhammānaṃ atthaṃ upaparikkhati, atthaṃ upaparikkhato dhammā nijjhānaṃ khamanti, dhammanijjhānakkhantiyā sati chando jāyati, chandajāto ussahati, ussāhetvā tuleti, tulayitvā padahati, pahitatto samāno kāyena ceva paramasaccaṃ sacchikaroti, paññāya ca naṃ ativijjha passati. Sāpi nāma, bhikkhave, saddhā nāhosi; tampi nāma, bhikkhave, upasaṅkamanaṃ nāhosi; sāpi nāma, bhikkhave, payirupāsanā nāhosi; tampi nāma, bhikkhave, sotāvadhānaṃ nāhosi; tampi nāma, bhikkhave, dhammassavanaṃ nāhosi; sāpi nāma, bhikkhave, dhammadhāraṇā nāhosi; sāpi nāma, bhikkhave, atthūpaparikkhā nāhosi; sāpi nāma, bhikkhave, dhammanijjhānakkhanti nāhosi; sopi nāma, bhikkhave, chando nāhosi; sopi nāma, bhikkhave, ussāho nāhosi; sāpi nāma, bhikkhave, tulanā nāhosi; tampi nāma, bhikkhave, padhānaṃ nāhosi. Vippaṭipannāttha, bhikkhave, micchāpaṭipannāttha, bhikkhave. Kīva dūrevime, bhikkhave, moghapurisā apakkantā imamhā dhammavinayā.

183.Bhikkhus, I do not say that the attainment of knowledge is achieved all at once. Rather, bhikkhus, the attainment of knowledge is achieved by gradual training, gradual activity, and gradual progress. And how, bhikkhus, is the attainment of knowledge achieved by gradual training, gradual activity, and gradual progress? Here, bhikkhus, one who has faith approaches. Having approached, they attend. Having attended, they lend an ear. Having lent an ear, they hear the Dhamma. Having heard the Dhamma, they remember it. Remembering the teachings, they examine their meaning. Examining the meaning, the teachings find acceptance through consideration. When there is acceptance of the teachings through consideration, desire arises. With desire arisen, they strive. Having striven, they evaluate. Having evaluated, they exert. Being resolute, they realize the supreme truth with the body and see it by penetrating it with wisdom. But, bhikkhus, suppose there was no faith—then there would be no approaching. Suppose there was no approaching—then there would be no attending. Suppose there was no attending—then there would be no lending an ear. Suppose there was no lending an ear—then there would be no hearing the Dhamma. Suppose there was no hearing the Dhamma—then there would be no remembering the teachings. Suppose there was no remembering the teachings—then there would be no examining their meaning. Suppose there was no examining their meaning—then there would be no acceptance of the teachings through consideration. Suppose there was no acceptance of the teachings through consideration—then there would be no desire. Suppose there was no desire—then there would be no striving. Suppose there was no striving—then there would be no evaluating. Suppose there was no evaluating—then there would be no exertion. These misguided persons, bhikkhus, are practicing wrongly and have deviated from the path. How very far these worthless men have strayed from this Dhamma and Discipline!

184. ‘‘Atthi, bhikkhave, catuppadaṃ veyyākaraṇaṃ yassuddiṭṭhassa viññū puriso nacirasseva paññāyatthaṃ ājāneyya. Uddisissāmi vo [uddiṭṭhassāpi (ka.)], bhikkhave, ājānissatha me ta’’nti? ‘‘Ke ca mayaṃ, bhante, ke ca dhammassa aññātāro’’ti? Yopi so, bhikkhave, satthā āmisagaru āmisadāyādo āmisehi saṃsaṭṭho viharati tassa pāyaṃ evarūpī paṇopaṇaviyā na upeti – ‘evañca no assa atha naṃ kareyyāma, na ca no evamassa na naṃ kareyyāmā’ti, kiṃ pana, bhikkhave, yaṃ tathāgato sabbaso āmisehi visaṃsaṭṭho viharati. Saddhassa, bhikkhave, sāvakassa satthusāsane pariyogāhiya [pariyogāya (sī. pī. ka.), pariyogayha (syā. kaṃ.)] vattato ayamanudhammo hoti – ‘satthā bhagavā, sāvakohamasmi; jānāti bhagavā, nāhaṃ jānāmī’ti. Saddhassa, bhikkhave, sāvakassa satthusāsane pariyogāhiya vattato ruḷhanīyaṃ [rumhaniyaṃ (sī. pī.)] satthusāsanaṃ hoti ojavantaṃ. Saddhassa, bhikkhave, sāvakassa satthusāsane pariyogāhiya vattato ayamanudhammo hoti – ‘kāmaṃ taco ca nhāru ca aṭṭhi ca avasissatu, sarīre upasussatu [upasussatu sarīre (sī.), sarīre avasussatu (ka.)] maṃsalohitaṃ, yaṃ taṃ purisathāmena purisavīriyena purisaparakkamena pattabbaṃ na taṃ apāpuṇitvā vīriyassa saṇṭhānaṃ [santhānaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)] bhavissatī’ti. Saddhassa, bhikkhave, sāvakassa satthusāsane pariyogāhiya vattato dvinnaṃ phalānaṃ aññataraṃ phalaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ – diṭṭheva dhamme aññā, sati vā upādisese anāgāmitā’’ti.

184.Bhikkhus, there is a four-part statement of meaning, upon hearing which a wise person would quickly understand the meaning. Shall I state it for you, bhikkhus? Will you understand it?" "But who are we, venerable sir, and who are ignorant of the Dhamma?" For that teacher, bhikkhus, who is attached to material gain, devoted to receiving material gifts, and associated with material things—such a one is likely not treated with such deference and supplication as, "If it is thus for us, then we will do it; and if it is not thus for us, then we will not do it." How much more so, bhikkhus, when the Tathāgata dwells entirely unassociated with material things? For a faithful disciple, bhikkhus, who lives devotedly in accordance with the Teacher’s instruction, this is according to the Dhamma: "The Teacher is the Blessed One, I am a disciple. The Blessed One knows, I do not know." For a faithful disciple, bhikkhus, who lives devotedly in accordance with the Teacher’s instruction, the Teacher’s instruction becomes firmly rooted and full of vitality. For a faithful disciple, bhikkhus, who lives devotedly in accordance with the Teacher’s instruction, this is according to the Dhamma: "Let only skin, sinews, and bone remain, and let the flesh and blood dry up in the body; what is to be attained by human strength, human energy, and human persistence, without attaining that, there will be no stopping of effort." For a faithful disciple, bhikkhus, who lives devotedly in accordance with the Teacher’s instruction, one of two fruits is to be expected: either final knowledge in this very life or, if there is a remaining basis, non-returning.

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

This the Blessed One said. Satisfied, those bhikkhus rejoiced at the Blessed One's words.

Kīṭāgirisuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dasamaṃ.

The Kīṭāgiri Sutta, the tenth, is finished.

Bhikkhuvaggo niṭṭhito dutiyo.

The Chapter on Bhikkhus, the Second, is finished.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Kuñjara-rāhula-sassataloko, mālukyaputto ca bhaddāli-nāmo;Khudda-dijātha-sahampatiyācaṃ, nāḷaka-raññikiṭāgirināmo.
