# 92. Selasuttaṃ

## 92. The Sela Sutta

396. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā aṅguttarāpesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ aḍḍhateḷasehi bhikkhusatehi yena āpaṇaṃ nāma aṅguttarāpānaṃ nigamo tadavasari. Assosi kho keṇiyo jaṭilo – ‘‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito aṅguttarāpesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ aḍḍhateḷasehi bhikkhusatehi āpaṇaṃ anuppatto. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’’ti.

396.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was traveling through the Aṅguttarāpa country with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus, numbering twelve hundred and fifty, when he arrived at Āpaṇa, a market town of the Aṅguttarāpas. Now the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya heard: “Indeed, good sirs, the ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, who went forth from the Sakyan clan, is traveling through the Aṅguttarāpa country with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus, numbering twelve hundred and fifty, and has arrived at Āpaṇa. And regarding that venerable Gotama, such a glorious report has spread: ‘Indeed, that Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, Fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One.’ He makes known this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans, having realized it himself with direct knowledge. He teaches the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with meaning and spirit, and proclaims the holy life that is entirely perfect and pure. It is indeed good to see such Arahants.”

Atha kho keṇiyo jaṭilo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho keṇiyaṃ jaṭilaṃ bhagavā dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi. Atha kho keṇiyo jaṭilo bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassito samādapito samuttejito sampahaṃsito bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘adhivāsetu me bhavaṃ gotamo svātanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhagavā keṇiyaṃ jaṭilaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mahā kho, keṇiya, bhikkhusaṅgho aḍḍhateḷasāni bhikkhusatāni, tvañca brāhmaṇesu abhippasanno’’ti. Dutiyampi kho keṇiyo jaṭilo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiñcāpi kho, bho gotama, mahā bhikkhusaṅgho aḍḍhateḷasāni bhikkhusatāni, ahañca brāhmaṇesu abhippasanno; adhivāsetu me bhavaṃ gotamo svātanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho bhagavā keṇiyaṃ jaṭilaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mahā kho, keṇiya, bhikkhusaṅgho aḍḍhateḷasāni bhikkhusatāni, tvañca brāhmaṇesu abhippasanno’’ti. Tatiyampi kho keṇiyo jaṭilo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiñcāpi kho, bho gotama, mahā bhikkhusaṅgho aḍḍhateḷasāni bhikkhusatāni, ahañca brāhmaṇesu abhippasanno; adhivāsetu me bhavaṃ gotamo svātanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’ti. Adhivāsesi bhagavā tuṇhībhāvena. Atha kho keṇiyo jaṭilo bhagavato adhivāsanaṃ viditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena sako assamo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā mittāmacce ñātisālohite āmantesi – ‘‘suṇantu me bhonto, mittāmaccā ñātisālohitā; samaṇo me gotamo nimantito svātanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghena. Yena me kāyaveyyāvaṭikaṃ [kāyaveyāvaṭṭikaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ.), kāyaveyyāvatikaṃ (ka.)] kareyyāthā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho keṇiyassa jaṭilassa mittāmaccā ñātisālohitā keṇiyassa jaṭilassa paṭissutvā appekacce uddhanāni khaṇanti, appekacce kaṭṭhāni phālenti, appekacce bhājanāni dhovanti, appekacce udakamaṇikaṃ patiṭṭhāpenti, appekacce āsanāni paññapenti. Keṇiyo pana jaṭilo sāmaṃyeva maṇḍalamālaṃ paṭiyādeti.

Then the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. When this courteous and amiable talk was concluded, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya was instructed, inspired, encouraged, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk. Then the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya, having been instructed, inspired, encouraged, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, said to the Blessed One: “May the Blessed Gotama consent to accept a meal from me tomorrow together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus.” When this was said, the Blessed One said to the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya: “The Saṅgha of bhikkhus is large, Keṇiya, numbering twelve hundred and fifty bhikkhus, and you are devoted to the brahmins.” A second time the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya said to the Blessed One: “Even though, Blessed Gotama, the Saṅgha of bhikkhus is large, numbering twelve hundred and fifty bhikkhus, and I am devoted to the brahmins; may the Blessed Gotama consent to accept a meal from me tomorrow together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus.” A second time the Blessed One said to the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya: “The Saṅgha of bhikkhus is large, Keṇiya, numbering twelve hundred and fifty bhikkhus, and you are devoted to the brahmins.” A third time the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya said to the Blessed One: “Even though, Blessed Gotama, the Saṅgha of bhikkhus is large, numbering twelve hundred and fifty bhikkhus, and I am devoted to the brahmins; may the Blessed Gotama consent to accept a meal from me tomorrow together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus.” The Blessed One consented by silence. Then the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya, understanding the Blessed One's consent, rose from his seat and went to his own hermitage. Having gone there, he addressed his friends, companions, relatives, and kin: “Listen to me, friends, companions, relatives, and kin; the ascetic Gotama has been invited by me for a meal tomorrow together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Please assist me with the bodily preparations.” “Yes, sir,” replied Keṇiya's friends, companions, relatives, and kin, and some dug fire pits, some split wood, some washed dishes, some set up water pots, some prepared seats. Keṇiya the matted-hair ascetic himself prepared a circular pavilion.

397. Tena kho pana samayena selo brāhmaṇo āpaṇe paṭivasati tiṇṇaṃ vedānaṃ pāragū sanighaṇḍukeṭubhānaṃ sākkharappabhedānaṃ itihāsapañcamānaṃ, padako, veyyākaraṇo, lokāyatamahāpurisalakkhaṇesu anavayo, tīṇi ca māṇavakasatāni mante vāceti. Tena kho pana samayena keṇiyo jaṭilo sele brāhmaṇe abhippasanno hoti. Atha kho selo brāhmaṇo tīhi māṇavakasatehi parivuto jaṅghāvihāraṃ anucaṅkamamāno anuvicaramāno yena keṇiyassa jaṭilassa assamo tenupasaṅkami. Addasā kho selo brāhmaṇo keṇiyassa jaṭilassa assame appekacce uddhanāni khaṇante, appekacce kaṭṭhāni phālente, appekacce bhājanāni dhovante, appekacce udakamaṇikaṃ patiṭṭhāpente, appekacce āsanāni paññapente, keṇiyaṃ pana jaṭilaṃ sāmaṃyeva maṇḍalamālaṃ paṭiyādentaṃ. Disvāna keṇiyaṃ jaṭilaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃ nu bhoto keṇiyassa āvāho vā bhavissati vivāho vā bhavissati mahāyañño vā paccupaṭṭhito, rājā vā māgadho seniyo bimbisāro nimantito svātanāya saddhiṃ balakāyenā’’ti? ‘‘Na me, bho sela, āvāho bhavissati napi vivāho bhavissati napi rājā māgadho seniyo bimbisāro nimantito svātanāya saddhiṃ balakāyena; api ca kho me mahāyañño paccupaṭṭhito. Atthi, bho, samaṇo gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito aṅguttarāpesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ aḍḍhateḷasehi bhikkhusatehi āpaṇaṃ anuppatto. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’ti. So me nimantito svātanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’ti.

397.Now at that time the brahmin Sela was staying at Āpaṇa, a master of the three Vedas, along with their glossaries, ritual texts, phonology and etymology, and the histories as fifth. Skilled in philology and grammar, he was versed in cosmology and the marks of a great man. He taught the mantras to three hundred students. Now at that time the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya was devoted to the brahmin Sela. Then Sela, surrounded by his three hundred students, walking and wandering for exercise, approached Keṇiya’s hermitage. There he saw some digging fire pits, some splitting wood, some washing dishes, some setting up water jars, and some preparing seats, while Keṇiya himself was preparing a circular pavilion. Seeing this, he said to Keṇiya, “What’s going on, Master Keṇiya? Is there to be a marriage or a giving in marriage? Or is a great sacrifice planned? Or has King Seniya Bimbisāra of Magadha been invited for tomorrow along with his retinue?” “There’s no marriage or giving in marriage for me, Master Sela, nor has the king been invited. But I have prepared a great sacrifice. For the ascetic Gotama, a Sakyan, has gone forth from the Sakyan clan. He is wandering in the Aṅguttarāpa country with a large Saṅgha of twelve hundred and fifty bhikkhus, and has arrived at Āpaṇa. He has this good reputation: ‘That Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, Fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One.’ He has been invited by me for tomorrow’s meal together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus.”

‘‘Buddhoti – bho keṇiya, vadesi’’?

“Buddha”—is that what you say, Master Keṇiya?

‘‘Buddhoti – bho sela, vadāmi’’.

“Buddha, Master Sela, I say.”

‘‘Buddhoti – bho keṇiya, vadesi’’?

“Buddha”—do you say, Master Keṇiya?

‘‘Buddhoti – bho sela, vadāmī’’ti.

“Buddha, Master Sela, I say.”

398. Atha kho selassa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘ghosopi kho eso dullabho lokasmiṃ – yadidaṃ ‘buddho’ti [yadidaṃ buddho buddhoti (ka.)]. Āgatāni kho panamhākaṃ mantesu dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yehi samannāgatassa mahāpurisassa dveyeva gatiyo bhavanti anaññā. Sace agāraṃ ajjhāvasati, rājā hoti cakkavattī dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāvī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni satta ratanāni bhavanti, seyyathidaṃ – cakkaratanaṃ, hatthiratanaṃ, assaratanaṃ, maṇiratanaṃ, itthiratanaṃ, gahapatiratanaṃ, pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā bhavanti sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena abhivijiya ajjhāvasati. Sace pana agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajati, arahaṃ hoti sammāsambuddho loke vivaṭṭacchado’’.

398.Then the brahmin Sela thought: “Indeed, this sound is rare in the world – the word ‘Buddha.’ And in our mantras, the thirty-two marks of a great man are described. For a great man endowed with these, there are only two destinies, no other. If he lives the household life, he becomes a wheel-turning monarch—a righteous king, a king of righteousness, conqueror of the four quarters, who has attained stability in the country, possessing the seven treasures. These are his seven treasures: the wheel-treasure, the elephant-treasure, the horse-treasure, the jewel-treasure, the woman-treasure, the householder-treasure, and the counselor-treasure as the seventh. He has over a thousand sons who are valiant, heroic, and crush the armies of his enemies. He conquers and rules this earth bounded by the ocean without rod or sword, by righteousness. But if he goes forth from the household life into homelessness, he becomes an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, one who removes the veil in the world.”

‘‘Kahaṃ pana, bho keṇiya, etarahi so bhavaṃ gotamo viharati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho’’ti? Evaṃ vutte, keṇiyo jaṭilo dakkhiṇaṃ bāhuṃ paggahetvā selaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yenesā, bho sela, nīlavanarājī’’ti. Atha kho selo brāhmaṇo tīhi māṇavakasatehi saddhiṃ yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho selo brāhmaṇo te māṇavake āmantesi – ‘‘appasaddā bhonto āgacchantu pade padaṃ [pāde pādaṃ (sī.)] nikkhipantā; durāsadā [dūrasaddā (ka.)] hi te bhagavanto sīhāva ekacarā. Yadā cāhaṃ, bho, samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ manteyyaṃ, mā me bhonto antarantarā kathaṃ opātetha. Kathāpariyosānaṃ me bhavanto āgamentū’’ti. Atha kho selo brāhmaṇo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho selo brāhmaṇo bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni samannesi.

“Where, Master Keṇiya, is the Blessed Gotama now staying, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One?” When this was said, the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya stretched out his right arm and said to the brahmin Sela: “Over there, Master Sela, where that line of dark forest trees is.” Then the brahmin Sela, together with three hundred young men, approached the Blessed One. Then the brahmin Sela addressed those young men: “Come quietly, sirs, placing your feet step by step. For those Blessed Ones are difficult to approach, like lions who live alone. And when I, sirs, am conversing with the ascetic Gotama, do not interrupt my talk. Wait for me to finish speaking.” Then the brahmin Sela approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. When this courteous and amiable talk was concluded, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the brahmin Sela examined the thirty-two marks of a great man on the Blessed One's body.

Addasā kho selo brāhmaṇo bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe, pahūtajivhatāya ca. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘passati kho me ayaṃ selo brāhmaṇo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe, pahūtajivhatāya cā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhāsi, yathā addasa selo brāhmaṇo bhagavato kosohitaṃ vatthaguyhaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā jivhaṃ ninnāmetvā ubhopi kaṇṇasotāni anumasi paṭimasi; ubhopi nāsikasotāni anumasi paṭimasi; kevalampi nalāṭamaṇḍalaṃ jivhāya chādesi. Atha kho selassa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘samannāgato kho samaṇo gotamo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi paripuṇṇehi, no aparipuṇṇehi; no ca kho naṃ jānāmi buddho vā no vā. Sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ – ‘ye te bhavanti arahanto sammāsambuddhā te sake vaṇṇe bhaññamāne attānaṃ pātukarontī’ti. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ sammukhā sāruppāhi gāthāhi abhitthaveyya’’nti.

Then the brahmin Sela saw on the Blessed One's body the thirty-two marks of a great man, for the most part, except for two. He was doubtful, perplexed, not convinced, and not satisfied concerning two of the great man's marks: the concealed sheath of the genitals and the large tongue. Then it occurred to the Blessed One: 'This brahmin Sela sees on my body the thirty-two marks of a great man, for the most part, except for two. He is doubtful, perplexed, not convinced, and not satisfied concerning two of the great man's marks: the concealed sheath of the genitals and the large tongue.' Then the Blessed One willed such a psychic manifestation that the brahmin Sela saw the Blessed One's concealed sheath of the genitals. Then the Blessed One put out his tongue and stroked and touched both ear holes; stroked and touched both nostrils; and covered his entire forehead with his tongue. Then it occurred to the brahmin Sela: 'The ascetic Gotama is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man, complete, not incomplete. But I do not know whether he is a Buddha or not. I have heard from aged, venerable brahmins, teachers of teachers, who say, 'Those who are arahants, perfectly self-enlightened Buddhas, reveal themselves when their own qualities are spoken of.' Why don't I extol the ascetic Gotama to his face with appropriate verses?'

399. Atha kho selo brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṃ sammukhā sāruppāhi gāthāhi abhitthavi –

399.Then the brahmin Sela extolled the Blessed One to his face with appropriate verses:

‘‘Paripuṇṇakāyo suruci, sujāto cārudassano;Suvaṇṇavaṇṇosi bhagavā, susukkadāṭhosi vīriyavā [viriyavā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)].

Perfect in body, beautiful, well-formed, lovely to behold, / Golden in complexion, Blessed One, with very white teeth, you are energetic.

‘‘Narassa hi sujātassa, ye bhavanti viyañjanā;Sabbe te tava kāyasmiṃ, mahāpurisalakkhaṇā.

For whatever auspicious marks there are in a well-formed man, / All those marks of a great man are on your body.

‘‘Pasannanetto sumukho, brahā [brahmā (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] uju patāpavā;Majjhe samaṇasaṅghassa, ādiccova virocasi.

With serene eyes, a bright face, majestic, upright, resplendent, / Amidst the Saṅgha of ascetics, you shine like the sun.

‘‘Kalyāṇadassano bhikkhu, kañcanasannibhattaco;Kiṃ te samaṇabhāvena, evaṃ uttamavaṇṇino.

O bhikkhu, lovely to behold, with skin like burnished gold, / What use is your ascetic life, so excellent of hue?

‘‘Rājā arahasi bhavituṃ, cakkavattī rathesabho;Cāturanto vijitāvī, jambusaṇḍassa [jambumaṇḍassa (ka.)] issaro.

You are fit to be a king, a wheel-turning monarch, foremost charioteer; / A conqueror of the four quarters, the ruler of Jambudīpa.

‘‘Khattiyā bhogirājāno, anuyantā [anuyuttā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] bhavantu te;Rājābhirājā manujindo, rajjaṃ kārehi gotama’’.

May Khattiya kings, lords of enjoyment, be your followers; / Supreme king of kings, lord of men, Gotama, may you rule the kingdom.

‘‘Rājāhamasmi selāti, dhammarājā anuttaro;Dhammena cakkaṃ vattemi, cakkaṃ appaṭivattiyaṃ’’.

I am a king, Sela, a king of Dhamma, unsurpassed; / By Dhamma I set the wheel in motion, a wheel that cannot be turned back.

‘‘Sambuddho paṭijānāsi, dhammarājā anuttaro;‘Dhammena cakkaṃ vattemi’, iti bhāsasi gotama.

You declare yourself to be the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, the unsurpassed king of Dhamma; / 'By Dhamma I set the wheel in motion,' so you say, Gotama.

‘‘Ko nu senāpati bhoto, sāvako satthuranvayo;Ko te tamanuvatteti, dhammacakkaṃ pavattitaṃ’’.

Who then is your general, your disciple, who follows the Teacher? / Who continues to turn after you the Wheel of Dhamma that has been set in motion?

‘‘Mayā pavattitaṃ cakkaṃ, (selāti bhagavā dhammacakkaṃ anuttaraṃ;Sāriputto anuvatteti, anujāto tathāgataṃ.

The wheel I have set in motion, Sela,

‘‘Abhiññeyyaṃ abhiññātaṃ, bhāvetabbañca bhāvitaṃ;Pahātabbaṃ pahīnaṃ me, tasmā buddhosmi brāhmaṇa.

What was to be directly known has been directly known; what was to be developed has been developed; / What was to be abandoned has been abandoned by me; therefore, I am the Buddha, O brahmin.

‘‘Vinayassu mayi kaṅkhaṃ, adhimuccassu brāhmaṇa;Dullabhaṃ dassanaṃ hoti, sambuddhānaṃ abhiṇhaso.

Dispel your doubt about me, be convinced, O brahmin; / Rare is the sight of the Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones.

‘‘Yesaṃ ve dullabho loke, pātubhāvo abhiṇhaso;Sohaṃ brāhmaṇa sambuddho, sallakatto anuttaro.

Those whose appearance in the world is indeed rare— / I, O brahmin, am the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, the unexcelled surgeon.

‘‘Brahmabhūto atitulo, mārasenappamaddano;Sabbāmitte vasī katvā, modāmi akutobhayo’’.

The Brahma-become, incomparable, crusher of Māra’s army; / Having subdued all enemies, I rejoice, fearless from any quarter.

‘‘Imaṃ bhonto nisāmetha, yathā bhāsati cakkhumā;Sallakatto mahāvīro, sīhova nadatī vane.

Listen, sirs, to this, how the One with Vision speaks. / The great hero, the surgeon, roars like a lion in the forest.

‘‘Brahmabhūtaṃ atitulaṃ, mārasenappamaddanaṃ;Ko disvā nappasīdeyya, api kaṇhābhijātiko.

The Brahma-become, incomparable, crusher of Māra’s army; / Who, seeing him, would not be pleased, even one of dark birth?

‘‘Yo maṃ icchati anvetu, yo vā nicchati gacchatu;Idhāhaṃ pabbajissāmi, varapaññassa santike’’.

Whoever wishes to, let them follow me; whoever does not wish to, may they go; / Here I shall go forth into homelessness, in the presence of the one of supreme wisdom.

‘‘Etañce [evañce (syā. kaṃ.)] ruccati bhoto, sammāsambuddhasāsanaṃ [sammāsambuddhasāsane (katthaci suttanipāte)];Mayampi pabbajissāma, varapaññassa santike’’.

If this teaching of the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One is pleasing to you, sirs; / We too shall go forth, into the presence of the one of excellent wisdom.

‘‘Brāhmaṇā tisatā ime, yācanti pañjalīkatā;Brahmacariyaṃ carissāma, bhagavā tava santike’’.

These three hundred brahmins, with joined palms, entreat: / 'We shall lead the holy life under you, Blessed One.'

‘‘Svākkhātaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, (selāti bhagavā sandiṭṭhikamakālikaṃ;Yattha amoghā pabbajjā, appamattassa sikkhato’’ti.

Well proclaimed is the holy life, Sela,

Alattha kho selo brāhmaṇo sapariso bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ, alattha upasampadaṃ.

Then the brahmin Sela and his following received the going forth in the presence of the Blessed One, and they received the higher ordination.

400. Atha kho keṇiyo jaṭilo tassā rattiyā accayena sake assame paṇītaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ paṭiyādāpetvā bhagavato kālaṃ ārocāpesi – ‘‘kālo, bho gotama, niṭṭhitaṃ bhatta’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena keṇiyassa jaṭilassa assamo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghena. Atha kho keṇiyo jaṭilo buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappesi, sampavāresi. Atha kho keṇiyo jaṭilo bhagavantaṃ bhuttāviṃ onītapattapāṇiṃ aññataraṃ nīcaṃ āsanaṃ gahetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho keṇiyaṃ jaṭilaṃ bhagavā imāhi gāthāhi anumodi –

400.Then, when that night had passed, the matted-hair ascetic Keṇiya had fine foods prepared in his hermitage and announced the time to the Blessed One: 'It is time, Master Gotama, the meal is ready.' Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the morning, took his bowl and robe and went to the hermitage of Keṇiya the matted-hair ascetic. Having arrived, he sat down on the prepared seat together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Then Keṇiya the matted-hair ascetic personally served and satisfied the Saṅgha of bhikkhus headed by the Buddha with fine foods. When the Blessed One had finished eating and had withdrawn his hand from the bowl, Keṇiya took a low seat and sat to one side. Sitting to one side, the Blessed One expressed his appreciation to Keṇiya the matted-hair ascetic with these verses:

‘‘Aggihuttamukhā yaññā, sāvittī chandaso mukhaṃ;Rājā mukhaṃ manussānaṃ, nadīnaṃ sāgaro mukhaṃ.

The fire offering is foremost among sacrifices; the Sāvitrī is foremost among meters; / The king is foremost among men; the ocean is foremost among rivers.

‘‘Nakkhattānaṃ mukhaṃ cando, ādicco tapataṃ mukhaṃ;Puññaṃ ākaṅkhamānānaṃ, saṅgho ve yajataṃ mukha’’nti.

The moon is foremost among constellations; the sun is foremost among those that heat; / For those desiring merit, the Saṅgha is indeed foremost among those who make offerings.

Atha kho bhagavā keṇiyaṃ jaṭilaṃ imāhi gāthāhi anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.

Then the Blessed One, having expressed his appreciation to Keṇiya the matted-hair ascetic with these verses, rose from his seat and departed.

Atha kho āyasmā selo sapariso eko vūpakaṭṭho appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto nacirasseva – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihāsi. ‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti abbhaññāsi. Aññataro kho panāyasmā selo sapariso arahataṃ ahosi. Atha kho āyasmā selo sapariso yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekaṃsaṃ cīvaraṃ katvā yena bhagavā tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā bhagavantaṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

Then the Venerable Sela, together with his following, dwelling alone, secluded, diligent, ardent, and resolute, not long after, by his own direct knowledge, realized and dwelt in that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from home into homelessness. He understood: 'Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more for this state of being.' And the Venerable Sela, together with his following, became one of the Arahants. Then the Venerable Sela, together with his following, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he arranged his robe over one shoulder, raised his joined palms toward the Blessed One, and addressed the Blessed One with verses:

‘‘Yaṃ taṃ saraṇamāgamma, ito aṭṭhami cakkhumā;Sattarattena [anuttarena (ka.)] bhagavā, dantamha tava sāsane.

Having come for refuge to you, the One with Vision, on the eighth day from then, / Within seven nights, O Blessed One, we have been tamed in your dispensation.

‘‘Tuvaṃ buddho tuvaṃ satthā, tuvaṃ mārābhibhū muni;Tuvaṃ anusaye chetvā, tiṇṇo tāresimaṃ pajaṃ.

You are the Buddha, you are the Teacher, you are the Muni who has overcome Māra; / You have cut off the underlying tendencies, crossed over, and you ferry this generation across.

‘‘Upadhī te samatikkantā, āsavā te padālitā;Sīhova anupādāno, pahīnabhayabheravo.

Your attachments are fully transcended, your taints are crushed; / Like a lion, without clinging, you have abandoned fear and dread.

‘‘Bhikkhavo tisatā ime, tiṭṭhanti pañjalīkatā;Pāde vīra pasārehi, nāgā vandantu satthuno’’ti.

These three hundred bhikkhus stand, with joined palms; / O Hero, extend your feet, let the arahants venerate the Teacher.

Selasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ dutiyaṃ.

The Selasutta, the second, is concluded.
