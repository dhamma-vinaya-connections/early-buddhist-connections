# 89. Dhammacetiyasuttaṃ

## 89. The Discourse on Monuments to the Dhamma

364. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati medāḷupaṃ [metaḷūpaṃ (sī.), medaḷumpaṃ (pī.)] nāma sakyānaṃ nigamo. Tena kho pana samayena rājā pasenadi kosalo nagarakaṃ anuppatto hoti kenacideva karaṇīyena. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo dīghaṃ kārāyanaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘yojehi, samma kārāyana, bhadrāni bhadrāni yānāni, uyyānabhūmiṃ gacchāma subhūmiṃ dassanāyā’’ti [subhūmidassanāyāti (dī. ni. 2.43)]. ‘‘Evaṃ, devā’’ti kho dīgho kārāyano rañño pasenadissa kosalassa paṭissutvā bhadrāni bhadrāni yānāni yojāpetvā rañño pasenadissa kosalassa paṭivedesi – ‘‘yuttāni kho te, deva, bhadrāni bhadrāni yānāni. Yassadāni kālaṃ maññasī’’ti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo bhadraṃ yānaṃ abhiruhitvā bhadrehi bhadrehi yānehi nagarakamhā niyyāsi mahaccā rājānubhāvena. Yena ārāmo tena pāyāsi. Yāvatikā yānassa bhūmi yānena gantvā yānā paccorohitvā pattikova ārāmaṃ pāvisi. Addasā kho rājā pasenadi kosalo ārāme jaṅghāvihāraṃ anucaṅkamamāno anuvicaramāno rukkhamūlāni pāsādikāni pasādanīyāni appasaddāni appanigghosāni vijanavātāni manussarāhasseyyakāni [manussarāhaseyyakāni (sī. pī.)] paṭisallānasāruppāni. Disvāna bhagavantaṃyeva ārabbha sati udapādi – ‘‘imāni kho tāni rukkhamūlāni pāsādikāni pasādanīyāni appasaddāni appanigghosāni vijanavātāni manussarāhasseyyakāni paṭisallānasāruppāni, yattha sudaṃ mayaṃ taṃ bhagavantaṃ payirupāsāma arahantaṃ sammāsambuddha’’nti.

364.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans in a Sakyan town named Medāḷupa. Now at that time King Pasenadi of Kosala had arrived at Nagaraka for some business. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala addressed Dīgha Kārāyana: “Prepare, dear Kārāyana, excellent carriages. We will go to the pleasure ground to see the beautiful scenery.” “Yes, lord,” replied Dīgha Kārāyana. Having prepared excellent carriages, he informed King Pasenadi of Kosala: “Your excellent carriages are ready, lord. Now, great king, do as you deem fit.” Then King Pasenadi of Kosala mounted an excellent carriage and departed from Nagaraka in great royal splendor with his excellent carriages. He proceeded toward the park. Having gone as far as the carriage could go, he dismounted and entered the park on foot. King Pasenadi of Kosala saw in the park, while pacing up and down for exercise, spots at the foot of trees—charming and inspiring confidence, quiet, free from clamor, secluded from people, suitable for private deliberation, suitable for solitude. Seeing this, mindfulness of the Blessed One arose in him: “These are indeed such charming and inspiring spots at the foot of trees—quiet, free from clamor, secluded from people, suitable for private deliberation, suitable for solitude—in places like which we used to visit that Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.”

365. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo dīghaṃ kārāyanaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘imāni kho, samma kārāyana, tāni rukkhamūlāni pāsādikāni pasādanīyāni appasaddāni appanigghosāni vijanavātāni manussarāhasseyyakāni paṭisallānasāruppāni, yattha sudaṃ mayaṃ taṃ bhagavantaṃ payirupāsāma arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ. Kahaṃ nu kho, samma kārāyana, etarahi so bhagavā viharati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho’’ti? ‘‘Atthi, mahārāja, medāḷupaṃ nāma sakyānaṃ nigamo. Tattha so bhagavā etarahi viharati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho’’ti. ‘‘Kīvadūre [kīvadūro (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] pana, samma kārāyana, nagarakamhā medāḷupaṃ nāma sakyānaṃ nigamo hotī’’ti? ‘‘Na dūre, mahārāja; tīṇi yojanāni; sakkā divasāvasesena gantu’’nti. ‘‘Tena hi, samma kārāyana, yojehi bhadrāni bhadrāni yānāni, gamissāma mayaṃ taṃ bhagavantaṃ dassanāya arahantaṃ sammāsambuddha’’nti. ‘‘Evaṃ, devā’’ti kho dīgho kārāyano rañño pasenadissa kosalassa paṭissutvā bhadrāni bhadrāni yānāni yojāpetvā rañño pasenadissa kosalassa paṭivedesi – ‘‘yuttāni kho te, deva, bhadrāni bhadrāni yānāni. Yassadāni kālaṃ maññasī’’ti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo bhadraṃ yānaṃ abhiruhitvā bhadrehi bhadrehi yānehi nagarakamhā yena medāḷupaṃ nāma sakyānaṃ nigamo tena pāyāsi. Teneva divasāvasesena medāḷupaṃ nāma sakyānaṃ nigamaṃ sampāpuṇi. Yena ārāmo tena pāyāsi. Yāvatikā yānassa bhūmi yānena gantvā yānā paccorohitvā pattikova ārāmaṃ pāvisi.

365.Then King Pasenadi of Kosala addressed Dīgha Kārāyana: “Dear Kārāyana, those tree roots are charming and inspiring confidence, quiet, free from clamor, secluded from people, suitable for private deliberation, suitable for solitude—in places like which we used to visit that Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. Where, dear Kārāyana, is the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, now dwelling?” “There is, great king, a Sakyan town named Medāḷupa. There the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, is now dwelling.” “But how far, dear Kārāyana, is the Sakyan town named Medāḷupa from Nagaraka?” “Not far, great king—three yojanas; it is possible to go there by the end of the day.” “Then, dear Kārāyana, prepare excellent carriages. We shall go to see the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.” “Yes, lord,” replied Dīgha Kārāyana to King Pasenadi of Kosala. Having prepared excellent carriages, he informed the king: “Your excellent carriages are ready, lord. Now, great king, do as you deem fit.” Then King Pasenadi of Kosala mounted an excellent carriage and, with excellent carriages, set out from Nagaraka toward the Sakyan town named Medāḷupa. By the end of that very day, he reached the Sakyan town named Medāḷupa. He proceeded to the park. He went as far as the carriage could go, then dismounted from the carriage and entered the park on foot.

366. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū abbhokāse caṅkamanti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘kahaṃ nu kho, bhante, etarahi so bhagavā viharati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho? Dassanakāmā hi mayaṃ taṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddha’’nti. ‘‘Eso, mahārāja, vihāro saṃvutadvāro. Tena appasaddo upasaṅkamitvā ataramāno āḷindaṃ pavisitvā ukkāsitvā aggaḷaṃ ākoṭehi. Vivarissati bhagavā te dvāra’’nti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo tattheva khaggañca uṇhīsañca dīghassa kārāyanassa pādāsi. Atha kho dīghassa kārāyanassa etadahosi – ‘‘rahāyati kho dāni rājā [mahārājā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)], idheva [tenidheva (sī.)] dāni mayā ṭhātabba’’nti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo yena so vihāro saṃvutadvāro tena appasaddo upasaṅkamitvā ataramāno āḷindaṃ pavisitvā ukkāsitvā aggaḷaṃ ākoṭesi. Vivari bhagavā dvāraṃ. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo vihāraṃ pavisitvā bhagavato pādesu sirasā nipatitvā bhagavato pādāni mukhena ca paricumbati, pāṇīhi ca parisambāhati, nāmañca sāveti – ‘‘rājāhaṃ, bhante, pasenadi kosalo; rājāhaṃ, bhante, pasenadi kosalo’’ti.

366.Now at that time several bhikkhus were walking up and down in the open air. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala approached those bhikkhus; having approached, he said to them: “Where, venerable sirs, is the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, now dwelling? We wish to see that Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.” “That, great king, is the Perfumed Hut with the door closed. Approach it quietly, without haste, enter the porch, clear your throat, and knock on the bolt. The Blessed One will open the door for you.” Then King Pasenadi of Kosala right there entrusted his sword and turban to Dīgha Kārāyana. Then it occurred to Dīgha Kārāyana: “Now the king is going alone; now I should stay right here.” Then King Pasenadi of Kosala approached that Perfumed Hut with the door closed quietly, without haste, entered the porch, cleared his throat, and knocked on the bolt. The Blessed One opened the door. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala entered the Perfumed Hut, bowed his head at the Blessed One’s feet, kissed the Blessed One’s feet with his mouth, and stroked them with his hands, announcing his name: “I, venerable sir, am King Pasenadi of Kosala; I, venerable sir, am King Pasenadi of Kosala.”

367. ‘‘Kiṃ pana tvaṃ, mahārāja, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno imasmiṃ sarīre evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karosi, mittūpahāraṃ [cittūpahāraṃ (sī.)] upadaṃsesī’’ti? ‘‘Atthi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo – ‘hoti sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’ti. Idhāhaṃ, bhante, passāmi eke samaṇabrāhmaṇe pariyantakataṃ brahmacariyaṃ carante dasapi vassāni, vīsampi vassāni, tiṃsampi vassāni, cattārīsampi vassāni. Te aparena samayena sunhātā suvilittā kappitakesamassū pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappitā samaṅgībhūtā paricārenti. Idha panāhaṃ, bhante, bhikkhū passāmi yāvajīvaṃ āpāṇakoṭikaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ carante. Na kho panāhaṃ, bhante, ito bahiddhā aññaṃ evaṃ paripuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ samanupassāmi. Ayampi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo hoti – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’’ti.

367.“Great king, seeing what purpose you perform such supreme prostration with this body and offer a gift of friendship?” “Venerable sir, I have this reason for confidence in the Blessed One through the Dhamma: ‘The Blessed One is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One; the Dhamma is well-expounded by the Blessed One; the Sangha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing well.’ Here, venerable sir, I see some ascetics and brahmins leading a limited holy life for ten years, twenty years, thirty years, or forty years. Then, after some time, well-bathed, well-anointed, with hair and beards trimmed, they live indulging in the five strands of sensual pleasure, furnished and endowed with them. But here, venerable sir, I see bhikkhus leading the holy life, complete and pure, for as long as life lasts. I do not see elsewhere another holy life so complete and pure. This too, venerable sir, is a reason for my confidence in the Blessed One: ‘The Blessed One is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One; the Dhamma is well-expounded by the Blessed One; the Sangha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing well.’ ”

368. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, rājānopi rājūhi vivadanti, khattiyāpi khattiyehi vivadanti, brāhmaṇāpi brāhmaṇehi vivadanti, gahapatayopi gahapatīhi vivadanti, mātāpi puttena vivadati, puttopi mātarā vivadati, pitāpi puttena vivadati, puttopi pitarā vivadati, bhātāpi bhaginiyā vivadati, bhaginīpi bhātarā vivadati, sahāyopi sahāyena vivadati. Idha panāhaṃ, bhante, bhikkhū passāmi samagge sammodamāne avivadamāne khīrodakībhūte aññamaññaṃ piyacakkhūhi sampassante viharante. Na kho panāhaṃ, bhante, ito bahiddhā aññaṃ evaṃ samaggaṃ parisaṃ samanupassāmi. Ayampi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo hoti – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’ti.

368.“Furthermore, venerable sir, kings quarrel with kings, nobles with nobles, brahmins with brahmins, householders with householders, a mother quarrels with her son, a son with his mother, a father quarrels with his son, a son with his father, a brother quarrels with his sister, a sister quarrels with her brother, a friend quarrels with a friend. But here, venerable sir, I see bhikkhus living in concord, with mutual appreciation, without quarreling, blending like milk and water, regarding each other with eyes of affection. I do not see, outside of this, another community so harmonious. This too, venerable sir, is a reason for my confidence in the Blessed One: ‘The Blessed One is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One; the Dhamma is well-expounded by the Blessed One; the Sangha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing well.’ ”

369. ‘‘Puna caparāhaṃ, bhante, ārāmena ārāmaṃ, uyyānena uyyānaṃ anucaṅkamāmi anuvicarāmi. Sohaṃ tattha passāmi eke samaṇabrāhmaṇe kise lūkhe dubbaṇṇe uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajāte dhamanisanthatagatte, na viya maññe cakkhuṃ bandhante janassa dassanāya. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, etadahosi – ‘addhā ime āyasmanto anabhiratā vā brahmacariyaṃ caranti, atthi vā tesaṃ kiñci pāpaṃ kammaṃ kataṃ paṭicchannaṃ; tathā hi ime āyasmanto kisā lūkhā dubbaṇṇā uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajātā dhamanisanthatagattā, na viya maññe cakkhuṃ bandhanti janassa dassanāyā’ti. Tyāhaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadāmi – ‘kiṃ nu kho tumhe āyasmanto kisā lūkhā dubbaṇṇā uppaṇḍuppaṇḍukajātā dhamanisanthatagattā, na viya maññe cakkhuṃ bandhatha janassa dassanāyā’ti? Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘bandhukarogo no [paṇḍukarogino (ka.)], mahārājā’ti. Idha panāhaṃ, bhante, bhikkhū passāmi haṭṭhapahaṭṭhe udaggudagge abhiratarūpe pīṇindriye [pīṇitindriye (sī. pī.)] appossukke pannalome paradattavutte migabhūtena cetasā viharante. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, etadahosi – ‘addhā ime āyasmanto tassa bhagavato sāsane uḷāraṃ pubbenāparaṃ visesaṃ jānanti; tathā hi ime āyasmanto haṭṭhapahaṭṭhā udaggudaggā abhiratarūpā pīṇindriyā appossukkā pannalomā paradattavuttā migabhūtena cetasā viharantī’ti. Ayampi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo hoti – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’ti.

369.Furthermore, venerable sir, I wander from park to park, from grove to grove. There I see some ascetics and brahmins who are emaciated, wretched, unsightly, pallid, with veins protruding all over their bodies, not at all pleasing to the eye for people to see. Then, venerable sir, it occurs to me: 'Surely these venerable ones are leading the holy life dissatisfied, or they have some evil deed done and concealed; for these venerable ones are emaciated, wretched, unsightly, pallid, with veins protruding all over their bodies, not at all pleasing to the eye for people to see.' I approach them and ask: 'Why are you venerable ones emaciated, wretched, unsightly, pallid, with veins protruding all over your bodies, not at all pleasing to the eye for people to see?' They reply: 'We are afflicted with a family disease, great king.' But here, venerable sir, I see bhikkhus who are cheerful and happy, elated, delighted, with serene faculties, untroubled, with hair laid down, living on what is given by others, with minds like wild deer. Then, venerable sir, it occurs to me: 'Surely these venerable ones have realized in this Blessed One's teaching a superior, further distinction; for these venerable ones are cheerful and happy, elated, delighted, with serene faculties, untroubled, with hair laid down, living on what is given by others, with minds like wild deer.' This too, venerable sir, is a reason for my conviction in the Blessed One: 'The Blessed One is perfectly enlightened, the Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One, the Sangha of the Blessed One's disciples is practicing the good way.'

370. ‘‘Puna caparāhaṃ, bhante, rājā khattiyo muddhāvasitto; pahomi ghātetāyaṃ vā ghātetuṃ, jāpetāyaṃ vā jāpetuṃ, pabbājetāyaṃ vā pabbājetuṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, aḍḍakaraṇe nisinnassa antarantarā kathaṃ opātenti. Sohaṃ na labhāmi – ‘mā me bhonto aḍḍakaraṇe nisinnassa antarantarā kathaṃ opātetha [opātentu (sī.) upariselasutte pana ‘‘opātethā’’tiyeva dissati], kathāpariyosānaṃ me bhonto āgamentū’ti. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, antarantarā kathaṃ opātenti. Idha panāhaṃ, bhante, bhikkhū passāmi; yasmiṃ samaye bhagavā anekasatāya parisāya dhammaṃ deseti, neva tasmiṃ samaye bhagavato sāvakānaṃ khipitasaddo vā hoti ukkāsitasaddo vā. Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhante, bhagavā anekasatāya parisāya dhammaṃ deseti. Tatraññataro bhagavato sāvako ukkāsi. Tamenaṃ aññataro sabrahmacārī jaṇṇukena ghaṭṭesi – ‘appasaddo āyasmā hotu, māyasmā saddamakāsi; satthā no bhagavā dhammaṃ desetī’ti. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, etadahosi – ‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho! Adaṇḍena vata kira, bho, asatthena evaṃ suvinītā parisā bhavissatī’ti! Na kho panāhaṃ, bhante, ito bahiddhā aññaṃ evaṃ suvinītaṃ parisaṃ samanupassāmi. Ayampi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo hoti – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’ti.

370.Furthermore, venerable sir, I am a consecrated khattiya king; I have the power to execute those who deserve execution, to fine those who deserve to be fined, or to banish those who deserve to be banished. Yet, venerable sir, while I am sitting in court, people interject with talk. I do not get the chance to say, ‘Do not interject with talk while I am sitting in court; let the gentlemen wait until I have finished speaking.’ But they keep interjecting with talk, venerable sir. Here, however, venerable sir, I see bhikkhus—when the Blessed One is teaching the Dhamma to an assembly of many hundreds, not even the sound of clearing the throat or coughing is heard among his disciples. Once, venerable sir, the Blessed One was teaching the Dhamma to an assembly of many hundreds. There, one of his disciples coughed. Then a fellow monk nudged him with his knee and said, ‘Be quiet, venerable one, do not make a noise; the Blessed One, our teacher, is teaching the Dhamma.’ At that, venerable sir, I thought, ‘It is wonderful, truly astonishing! Without rod or weapon, how well-disciplined this assembly is!’ I do not see, venerable sir, any other assembly outside of this so well-disciplined. This too, venerable sir, inspires my confidence in the Blessed One: ‘The Blessed One is perfectly enlightened, the Dhamma is well-proclaimed by the Blessed One, the Sangha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way.’

371. ‘‘Puna caparāhaṃ, bhante, passāmi idhekacce khattiyapaṇḍite nipuṇe kataparappavāde vālavedhirūpe. Te bhindantā [vobhindantā (sī.)] maññe caranti paññāgatena diṭṭhigatāni. Te suṇanti – ‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo amukaṃ nāma gāmaṃ vā nigamaṃ vā osarissatī’ti. Te pañhaṃ abhisaṅkharonti – ‘imaṃ mayaṃ pañhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ upasaṅkamitvā pucchissāma. Evaṃ ce no puṭṭho evaṃ byākarissati, evamassa mayaṃ vādaṃ āropessāma; evaṃ cepi no puṭṭho evaṃ byākarissati, evampissa mayaṃ vādaṃ āropessāmā’ti. Te suṇanti – ‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo amukaṃ nāma gāmaṃ vā nigamaṃ vā osaṭo’ti. Te yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamanti. Te bhagavā dhammiyā kathāya sandasseti samādapeti samuttejeti sampahaṃseti. Te bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā na ceva bhagavantaṃ pañhaṃ pucchanti, kuto vādaṃ āropessanti? Aññadatthu bhagavato sāvakā sampajjanti. Ayampi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo hoti – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’ti.

371.Furthermore, venerable sir, I see here some Khattiyas who are reputedly wise, clever, skilled in debate, and razor-sharp in demolishing the arguments of others. They seem to go around shattering the views of others with their intellect. They hear, ‘The ascetic Gotama, they say, will be visiting such and such a village or town.’ They formulate a question, thinking, ‘We will approach the ascetic Gotama and ask him this question. If, when asked, he answers thus, we will refute his doctrine thus; and if, when asked, he answers otherwise, we will refute his doctrine otherwise.’ They hear, ‘The ascetic Gotama, they say, has arrived at such and such a village or town.’ They approach the Blessed One. The Blessed One instructs, encourages, inspires, and gladdens them with a talk on Dhamma. Having been instructed, encouraged, inspired, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a talk on Dhamma, they do not even ask the Blessed One a question—much less refute him. Instead, they become disciples of the Blessed One. This too, venerable sir, is how the Dhamma accords with the Blessed One: ‘The Blessed One is perfectly enlightened, the Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One, and the Sangha of the Blessed One's disciples is practicing the good way.’

372. ‘‘Puna caparāhaṃ, bhante, passāmi idhekacce brāhmaṇapaṇḍite…pe… gahapatipaṇḍite…pe… samaṇapaṇḍite nipuṇe kataparappavāde vālavedhirūpe. Te bhindantā maññe caranti paññāgatena diṭṭhigatāni. Te suṇanti – ‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo amukaṃ nāma gāmaṃ vā nigamaṃ vā osarissatī’ti. Te pañhaṃ abhisaṅkharonti – ‘imaṃ mayaṃ pañhaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ upasaṅkamitvā pucchissāma. Evaṃ ce no puṭṭho evaṃ byākarissati, evamassa mayaṃ vādaṃ āropessāma; evaṃ cepi no puṭṭho evaṃ byākarissati, evampissa mayaṃ vādaṃ āropessāmā’ti. Te suṇanti – ‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo amukaṃ nāma gāmaṃ vā nigamaṃ vā osaṭo’ti. Te yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamanti. Te bhagavā dhammiyā kathāya sandasseti samādapeti samuttejeti sampahaṃseti. Te bhagavatā dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṃsitā na ceva bhagavantaṃ pañhaṃ pucchanti, kuto vādaṃ āropessanti? Aññadatthu bhagavantaṃyeva okāsaṃ yācanti agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajjāya. Te bhagavā pabbājeti. Te tathāpabbajitā samānā ekā vūpakaṭṭhā appamattā ātāpino pahitattā viharantā nacirasseva – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharanti. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘manaṃ vata, bho, anassāma; manaṃ vata, bho, panassāma’. Mayañhi pubbe assamaṇāva samānā samaṇāmhāti paṭijānimhā, abrāhmaṇāva samānā brāhmaṇāmhāti paṭijānimhā, anarahantova samānā arahantāmhāti paṭijānimhā. ‘Idāni khomha samaṇā, idāni khomha brāhmaṇā, idāni khomha arahanto’ti. Ayampi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo hoti – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’ti.

372.Furthermore, venerable sir, I see here some learned brahmins, learned householders, learned ascetics who are subtle, skilled in refuting the doctrines of others, hair-splitters. They wander about, as it were, breaking apart the views of others with their intellect. They hear: 'Indeed, the ascetic Gotama will visit such and such a village or town.' They prepare a question: 'We will approach the ascetic Gotama and ask him this question. If, when asked, he answers thus, we will refute his doctrine thus; and if, when asked, he answers otherwise, we will refute his doctrine otherwise.' They hear: 'Indeed, the ascetic Gotama has arrived at such and such a village or town.' They approach the Blessed One. The Blessed One instructs, inspires, fires up, and gladdens them with a Dhamma talk. Having been instructed, inspired, fired up, and gladdened by the Blessed One with a Dhamma talk, they do not even ask the Blessed One a question—much less refute his doctrine. Instead, they ask the Blessed One for the opportunity to go forth from the household life into homelessness. The Blessed One gives them the going forth. Having gone forth in this way, living alone, withdrawn, diligent, ardent, and resolute, they soon realize for themselves with direct knowledge, even in this very life, that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having realized it, they dwell in it. They say: 'We were nearly lost, friends; we were nearly ruined. For though we were not ascetics, we claimed to be ascetics; though we were not brahmins, we claimed to be brahmins; though we were not arahants, we claimed to be arahants. But now we are ascetics, now we are brahmins, now we are arahants.' This too, venerable sir, is a conformity of mine with the Dhamma of the Blessed One: 'The Blessed One is perfectly enlightened, the Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One, the Sangha of the Blessed One's disciples is practicing the good way.'

373. ‘‘Puna caparāhaṃ, bhante, ime isidattapurāṇā thapatayo mamabhattā mamayānā, ahaṃ nesaṃ jīvikāya [jīvitassa (sī.), jīvikaṃ (sī. aṭṭha.), jīvitaṃ (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] dātā, yasassa āhattā; atha ca pana no tathā mayi nipaccakāraṃ karonti yathā bhagavati. Bhūtapubbāhaṃ, bhante, senaṃ abbhuyyāto samāno ime ca isidattapurāṇā thapatayo vīmaṃsamāno aññatarasmiṃ sambādhe āvasathe vāsaṃ upagacchiṃ. Atha kho, bhante, ime isidattapurāṇā thapatayo bahudeva rattiṃ dhammiyā kathāya vītināmetvā, yato ahosi bhagavā [assosuṃ kho bhagavantaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] tato sīsaṃ katvā maṃ pādato karitvā nipajjiṃsu. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, etadahosi – ‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho! Ime isidattapurāṇā thapatayo mamabhattā mamayānā, ahaṃ nesaṃ jīvikāya dātā, yasassa āhattā; atha ca pana no tathā mayi nipaccakāraṃ karonti yathā bhagavati. Addhā ime āyasmanto tassa bhagavato sāsane uḷāraṃ pubbenāparaṃ visesaṃ jānantī’ti. Ayampi kho me, bhante, bhagavati dhammanvayo hoti – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho’ti.

373.Furthermore, venerable sir, these carpenters Isidatta and Purāṇa are those who eat my food and use my vehicles; I am the one who gives them their livelihood and brings them honor. Yet they do not show me the same respect as they do to the Blessed One. Once, venerable sir, when I was on a military campaign, I observed these carpenters Isidatta and Purāṇa. I stayed overnight in a crowded lodging. Then, venerable sir, these carpenters Isidatta and Purāṇa spent much of the night in religious discussion. Then, placing their heads in the direction of where the Blessed One was and their feet toward me, they lay down to sleep. It occurred to me, venerable sir: 'It is wonderful, sirs, it is astonishing, sirs! These carpenters Isidatta and Purāṇa are those who eat my food and use my vehicles; I am the one who gives them their livelihood and brings them honor. Yet they do not show me the same respect as they do to the Blessed One. Surely these venerable ones know some profound and superior distinction in the Blessed One’s teaching.' This too, venerable sir, is a conviction in the Dhamma for me regarding the Blessed One: 'The Blessed One is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, the Dhamma has been well-expounded by the Blessed One, and the Sangha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way.'

374. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavāpi khattiyo, ahampi khattiyo; bhagavāpi kosalo, ahampi kosalo; bhagavāpi āsītiko, ahampi āsītiko. Yampi, bhante, bhagavāpi khattiyo ahampi khattiyo, bhagavāpi kosalo ahampi kosalo, bhagavāpi āsītiko ahampi āsītiko; imināvārahāmevāhaṃ [imināpāhaṃ (ka.)], bhante, bhagavati paramanipaccakāraṃ kātuṃ, mittūpahāraṃ upadaṃsetuṃ. Handa, ca dāni mayaṃ, bhante, gacchāma; bahukiccā mayaṃ bahukaraṇīyā’’ti. ‘‘Yassadāni tvaṃ, mahārāja, kālaṃ maññasī’’ti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Atha kho bhagavā acirapakkantassa rañño pasenadissa kosalassa bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘eso, bhikkhave, rājā pasenadi kosalo dhammacetiyāni bhāsitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkanto. Uggaṇhatha, bhikkhave, dhammacetiyāni; pariyāpuṇātha, bhikkhave, dhammacetiyāni; dhāretha, bhikkhave, dhammacetiyāni. Atthasaṃhitāni, bhikkhave, dhammacetiyāni ādibrahmacariyakānī’’ti.

374.Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One is a noble, and I am a noble; the Blessed One is a Kosalan, and I am a Kosalan; the Blessed One is eighty years old, and I am eighty years old. Because the Blessed One is a noble and I am a noble, the Blessed One is a Kosalan and I am a Kosalan, the Blessed One is eighty years old and I am eighty years old, for this reason alone, venerable sir, I am worthy to show the utmost reverence to the Blessed One and to offer the gift of friendship. Now, venerable sir, we shall depart. We have many duties and much to do." "Now, great king, do as you think fit." Then King Pasenadi of Kosala rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him keeping his right side toward him, and departed. Soon after King Pasenadi of Kosala had departed, the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "Bhikkhus, King Pasenadi of Kosala has uttered these Dhamma memorials and then departed from his seat. Learn these Dhamma memorials, bhikkhus, master them, remember them. These Dhamma memorials are beneficial and fundamental to the holy life."

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

This is what the Blessed One said. The bhikkhus were pleased and delighted in the Blessed One’s words.

Dhammacetiyasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ navamaṃ.

The Dhammacetiya Sutta, the ninth, is concluded.
