# 25. Nivāpasuttaṃ

## 25. The Nivāpa Sutta

261. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

261.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was staying at Sāvatthī, in Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Monastery. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus.” “Venerable sir,” they replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Na, bhikkhave, nevāpiko nivāpaṃ nivapati migajātānaṃ – ‘imaṃ me nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ migajātā paribhuñjantā dīghāyukā vaṇṇavanto ciraṃ dīghamaddhānaṃ yāpentū’ti. Evañca kho, bhikkhave, nevāpiko nivāpaṃ nivapati migajātānaṃ – ‘imaṃ me nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ migajātā anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjissanti, anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā madaṃ āpajjissanti, mattā samānā pamādaṃ āpajjissanti, pamattā samānā yathākāmakaraṇīyā bhavissanti imasmiṃ nivāpe’ti.

“Bhikkhus, a hunter does not set out bait for the deer thinking, 'May the deer who feed on this bait I have set out live long, be beautiful, and endure for a long time.' Rather, bhikkhus, a hunter sets out bait for the deer thinking, 'The deer, approaching and becoming infatuated with this bait I have set out, will eat the food. While eating the food, approaching and becoming infatuated, they will become intoxicated. Being intoxicated, they will become negligent. Being negligent, they will be at my disposal in this bait.'

262. ‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, paṭhamā migajātā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu, te tattha anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, mattā samānā pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, pamattā samānā yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe. Evañhi te, bhikkhave, paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā.

262.“There, bhikkhus, the first group of deer, approaching and becoming infatuated with that bait laid out by the hunter, ate the food. While eating the food, approaching and becoming infatuated, they became intoxicated. Being intoxicated, they became negligent. Being negligent, they were at the disposal of the hunter in that bait. In this way, bhikkhus, the first group of deer did not escape the hunter’s sphere of influence.

263. ‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, dutiyā migajātā evaṃ samacintesuṃ – ‘ye kho te paṭhamā migajātā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu. Te tattha anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, mattā samānā pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, pamattā samānā yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe. Evañhi te paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭivirameyyāma, bhayabhogā paṭiviratā araññāyatanāni ajjhogāhetvā vihareyyāmā’ti. Te sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭiviramiṃsu, bhayabhogā paṭiviratā araññāyatanāni ajjhogāhetvā vihariṃsu. Tesaṃ gimhānaṃ pacchime māse, tiṇodakasaṅkhaye, adhimattakasimānaṃ patto kāyo hoti. Tesaṃ adhimattakasimānaṃ pattakāyānaṃ balavīriyaṃ parihāyi. Balavīriye parihīne tameva nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa paccāgamiṃsu. Te tattha anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu. Te tattha anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, mattā samānā pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, pamattā samānā yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe. Evañhi te, bhikkhave, dutiyāpi migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā.

263.“There, bhikkhus, the second group of deer reflected thus: 'The first group of deer, approaching and becoming infatuated with that bait laid out by the hunter, ate the food. While eating the food, approaching and becoming infatuated, they became intoxicated. Being intoxicated, they became negligent. Being negligent, they were at the disposal of the hunter in that bait. In this way, the first group of deer did not escape the hunter’s sphere of influence. What if we were to completely abstain from eating the bait, abstain from food involving danger, and dwell having plunged into the wilderness?' They completely abstained from eating the bait, abstained from food involving danger, and dwelled having plunged into the wilderness. But when the last month of the hot season arrived, when grass and water were scarce, their bodies became extremely emaciated. With their bodies extremely emaciated, their strength and vigor waned. With their strength and vigor waned, they returned to that very bait laid out by the hunter. There, approaching and becoming infatuated, they ate the food. While eating the food, approaching and becoming infatuated, they became intoxicated. Being intoxicated, they became negligent. Being negligent, they were at the disposal of the hunter in that bait. In this way, bhikkhus, the second group of deer also did not escape the hunter’s sphere of influence.

264. ‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, tatiyā migajātā evaṃ samacintesuṃ – ‘ye kho te paṭhamā migajātā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa…pe… evañhi te paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yepi te dutiyā migajātā evaṃ samacintesuṃ – ye kho te paṭhamā migajātā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa…pe… evañhi te paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭivirameyyāma, bhayabhogā paṭiviratā araññāyatanāni ajjhogāhetvā vihareyyāmāti. Te sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭiviramiṃsu, bhayabhogā paṭiviratā araññāyatanāni ajjhogāhetvā vihariṃsu. Tesaṃ gimhānaṃ pacchime māse tiṇodakasaṅkhaye adhimattakasimānaṃ patto kāyo hoti. Tesaṃ adhimattakasimānaṃ pattakāyānaṃ balavīriyaṃ parihāyi. Balavīriye parihīne tameva nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa paccāgamiṃsu. Te tattha anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu. Te tattha anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, mattā samānā pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, pamattā samānā yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe. Evañhi te dutiyāpi migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa upanissāya āsayaṃ kappeyyāma. Tatrāsayaṃ kappetvā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjissāma, ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā na madaṃ āpajjissāma, amattā samānā na pamādaṃ āpajjissāma, appamattā samānā na yathākāmakaraṇīyā bhavissāma nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe’ti. Te amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa upanissāya āsayaṃ kappayiṃsu. Tatrāsayaṃ kappetvā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu, te tattha ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā na madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, amattā samānā na pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, appamattā samānā na yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe.

The first group of deer, approaching and becoming infatuated with that bait laid out by the hunter, ate the food. While eating the food, approaching and becoming infatuated, they became intoxicated. Being intoxicated, they became negligent. Being negligent, they were at the disposal of the hunter in that bait. In this way, those first group of deer did not escape the hunter’s sphere of influence. What if we were to completely abstain from eating the bait, abstain from food involving danger, and dwell having plunged into the wilderness?

‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, nevāpikassa ca nevāpikaparisāya ca etadahosi – ‘saṭhāssunāmime tatiyā migajātā ketabino, iddhimantāssunāmime tatiyā migajātā parajanā; imañca nāma nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ paribhuñjanti, na ca nesaṃ jānāma āgatiṃ vā gatiṃ vā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ imaṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mahatīhi daṇḍavākarāhi [daṇḍavāgurāhi (syā.)] samantā sappadesaṃ anuparivāreyyāma – appeva nāma tatiyānaṃ migajātānaṃ āsayaṃ passeyyāma, yattha te gāhaṃ gaccheyyu’nti. Te amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mahatīhi daṇḍavākarāhi samantā sappadesaṃ anuparivāresuṃ. Addasaṃsu kho, bhikkhave, nevāpiko ca nevāpikaparisā ca tatiyānaṃ migajātānaṃ āsayaṃ, yattha te gāhaṃ agamaṃsu. Evañhi te, bhikkhave, tatiyāpi migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā.

"Then, bhikkhus, the trapper and his company had this thought: ‘These third kind of deer are truly cunning, like those with psychic power, belonging to another group; they consume this laid-out bait, yet we know neither their coming nor their going. Suppose we were to completely surround this laid-out bait with large stick-and-net traps on all sides, covering a wide area. Perhaps then we might discover the lair of these third kind of deer, where they might be caught.’ So they completely surrounded that laid-out bait with large stick-and-net traps on all sides, covering a wide area. And indeed, bhikkhus, the trapper and his company saw the lair of the third kind of deer, where they were caught. Thus, bhikkhus, even those third kind of deer did not escape the trapper’s sphere of influence."

265. ‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, catutthā migajātā evaṃ samacintesuṃ – ‘ye kho te paṭhamā migajātā…pe… evañhi te paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yepi te dutiyā migajātā evaṃ samacintesuṃ ‘ye kho te paṭhamā migajātā…pe… evañhi te paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭivirameyyāma, bhayabhogā paṭiviratā araññāyatanāni ajjhogāhetvā vihareyyāmā’ti. Te sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭiviramiṃsu…pe… evañhi te dutiyāpi migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yepi te tatiyā migajātā evaṃ samacintesuṃ ‘ye kho te paṭhamā migajātā…pe… evañhi te paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yepi te dutiyā migajātā evaṃ samacintesuṃ ‘ye kho te paṭhamā migajātā…pe… evañhi te paṭhamā migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭivirameyyāma, bhayabhogā paṭiviratā araññāyatanāni ajjhogāhetvā vihareyyāmā’ti. Te sabbaso nivāpabhojanā paṭiviramiṃsu…pe… evañhi te dutiyāpi migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa upanissāya āsayaṃ kappeyyāma, tatrāsayaṃ kappetvā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjissāma, ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā na madaṃ āpajjissāma, amattā samānā na pamādaṃ āpajjissāma, appamattā samānā na yathākāmakaraṇīyā bhavissāma nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe’ti. Te amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa upanissāya āsayaṃ kappayiṃsu, tatrāsayaṃ kappetvā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu, te tattha ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā na madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, amattā samānā na pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, appamattā samānā na yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe.

265."Therein, bhikkhus, the fourth kind of deer thought thus: ‘Those first kind of deer… thus indeed those first kind of deer were not freed from the trapper’s sphere of influence. And those second kind of deer… thus indeed those second kind of deer were not freed from the trapper’s sphere of influence. And those third kind of deer… thus indeed those third kind of deer were not freed from the trapper’s sphere of influence. What if we were to completely abstain from the bait-food, having ceased fearful enjoyments, and dwell having resorted to wilderness abodes?’ They completely abstained from the bait-food, having ceased fearful enjoyments, and dwelt having resorted to wilderness abodes. Then they thought: ‘What if we were to establish a lair near this laid-out bait of the trapper? Having established a lair there, we would eat the food without being ensnared or infatuated by this laid-out bait of the trapper. Eating without being ensnared or infatuated, we would not fall into intoxication; not being intoxicated, we would not fall into heedlessness; being unheedful, we would not come under the trapper’s control regarding that bait.’ They established a lair near this laid-out bait of the trapper. Having established a lair there, they ate the food without being ensnared or infatuated by this laid-out bait of the trapper. Eating there without being ensnared or infatuated, they did not fall into intoxication; not being intoxicated, they did not fall into heedlessness; being unheedful, they did not come under the trapper’s control regarding that bait."

‘‘Tatra nevāpikassa ca nevāpikaparisāya ca etadahosi – ‘saṭhāssunāmime tatiyā migajātā ketabino, iddhimantāssunāmime tatiyā migajātā parajanā, imañca nāma nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ paribhuñjanti. Na ca nesaṃ jānāma āgatiṃ vā gatiṃ vā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ imaṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mahatīti daṇḍavākarāhi samantā sappadesaṃ anuparivāreyyāma, appeva nāma tatiyānaṃ migajātānaṃ āsayaṃ passeyyāma, yattha te gāhaṃ gaccheyyu’nti. Te amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mahatīti daṇḍavākarāhi samantā sappadesaṃ anuparivāresuṃ. Addasaṃsu kho nevāpiko ca nevāpikaparisā ca tatiyānaṃ migajātānaṃ āsayaṃ, yattha te gāhaṃ agamaṃsu. Evañhi te tatiyāpi migajātā na parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yattha agati nevāpikassa ca nevāpikaparisāya ca tatrāsayaṃ kappeyyāma, tatrāsayaṃ kappetvā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjissāma, ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā na madaṃ āpajjissāma, amattā samānā na pamādaṃ āpajjissāma, appamattā samānā na yathākāmakaraṇīyā bhavissāma nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe’ti. Te yattha agati nevāpikassa ca nevāpikaparisāya ca tatrāsayaṃ kappayiṃsu. Tatrāsayaṃ kappetvā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ nevāpikassa ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu, te tattha ananupakhajja amucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā na madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, amattā samānā na pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, appamattā samānā na yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ nevāpikassa amusmiṃ nivāpe.

"Then the trapper and his company had this thought: ‘These third kind of deer are truly cunning, like those with psychic power, belonging to another group; they consume this laid-out bait, yet we know neither their coming nor their going. Suppose we were to completely surround this laid-out bait with large stick-and-net traps on all sides, covering a wide area. Perhaps then we might discover the lair of these third kind of deer, where they might be caught.’ So they completely surrounded that laid-out bait with large stick-and-net traps on all sides, covering a wide area. And indeed, the trapper and his company saw the lair of the third kind of deer, where they were caught. Thus, even those third kind of deer did not escape the trapper’s sphere of influence. Then the deer thought: ‘What if we were to establish a lair where the trapper and his company have no access or range? Having established a lair there, we would eat the food without being ensnared or infatuated by this laid-out bait of the trapper. Eating without being ensnared or infatuated, we would not fall into intoxication; not being intoxicated, we would not fall into heedlessness; being unheedful, we would not come under the trapper’s control regarding that bait.’ So they established a lair where the trapper and his company had no access or range. Having established a lair there, they ate the food without being ensnared or infatuated by this laid-out bait of the trapper. Eating there without being ensnared or infatuated, they did not fall into intoxication; not being intoxicated, they did not fall into heedlessness; being unheedful, they did not come under the trapper’s control regarding that bait."

‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, nevāpikassa ca nevāpikaparisāya ca etadahosi – ‘saṭhāssunāmime catutthā migajātā ketabino, iddhimantāssunāmime catutthā migajātā parajanā. Imañca nāma nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ paribhuñjanti, na ca nesaṃ jānāma āgatiṃ vā gatiṃ vā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ imaṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mahatīhi daṇḍavākarāhi samantā sappadesaṃ anuparivāreyyāma, appeva nāma catutthānaṃ migajātānaṃ āsayaṃ passeyyāma yattha te gāhaṃ gaccheyyu’nti. Te amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mahatīhi daṇḍavākarāhi samantā sappadesaṃ anuparivāresuṃ. Neva kho, bhikkhave, addasaṃsu nevāpiko ca nevāpikaparisā ca catutthānaṃ migajātānaṃ āsayaṃ, yattha te gāhaṃ gaccheyyuṃ. Tatra, bhikkhave, nevāpikassa ca nevāpikaparisāya ca etadahosi – ‘sace kho mayaṃ catutthe migajāte ghaṭṭessāma, te ghaṭṭitā aññe ghaṭṭissanti te ghaṭṭitā aññe ghaṭṭissanti. Evaṃ imaṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ sabbaso migajātā parimuñcissanti. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ catutthe migajāte ajjhupekkheyyāmā’ti. Ajjhupekkhiṃsu kho, bhikkhave, nevāpiko ca nevāpikaparisā ca catutthe migajāte. Evañhi te, bhikkhave, catutthā migajātā parimucciṃsu nevāpikassa iddhānubhāvā.

"Then, bhikkhus, the trapper and his company had this thought: ‘These fourth kind of deer are truly cunning, like those with psychic power, belonging to another group. They consume this laid-out bait, yet we know neither their coming nor their going. Suppose we were to completely surround this laid-out bait with large stick-and-net traps on all sides, covering a wide area. Perhaps then we might discover the lair of these fourth kind of deer, where they might be caught.’ So they completely surrounded that laid-out bait with large stick-and-net traps on all sides, covering a wide area. But, bhikkhus, neither the trapper nor his company saw the lair of the fourth kind of deer, where they might be caught. Then, bhikkhus, the trapper and his company had this thought: ‘If we harass the fourth kind of deer, those harassed will harass others, and those harassed will harass still others. In this way, all the kinds of deer will completely abandon this laid-out bait. What if we were to disregard the fourth kind of deer?’ So, bhikkhus, the trapper and his company disregarded the fourth kind of deer. Thus, bhikkhus, those fourth kind of deer escaped the trapper’s sphere of influence."

266. ‘‘Upamā kho me ayaṃ, bhikkhave, katā atthassa viññāpanāya. Ayaṃ cevettha attho – nivāpoti kho, bhikkhave, pañcannetaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Nevāpikoti kho, bhikkhave, mārassetaṃ pāpimato adhivacanaṃ. Nevāpikaparisāti kho, bhikkhave, māraparisāyetaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Migajātāti kho, bhikkhave, samaṇabrāhmaṇānametaṃ adhivacanaṃ.

266."bhikkhus, I have given this simile to make the meaning clear. And here is the meaning: ‘The bait,’ bhikkhus, is a designation for the five cords of sensual pleasure. ‘The trapper,’ bhikkhus, is a designation for Māra, the Evil One. ‘The trapper’s company,’ bhikkhus, is a designation for Māra’s company. ‘The kinds of deer,’ bhikkhus, is a designation for ascetics and brahmins."

267. ‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, paṭhamā samaṇabrāhmaṇā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mārassa amūni ca lokāmisāni anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu. Te tattha anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjamānā madaṃ āpajjiṃsu, mattā samānā pamādaṃ āpajjiṃsu, pamattā samānā yathākāmakaraṇīyā ahesuṃ mārassa amusmiṃ nivāpe amusmiñca lokāmise. Evañhi te, bhikkhave, paṭhamā samaṇabrāhmaṇā na parimucciṃsu mārassa iddhānubhāvā. Seyyathāpi te, bhikkhave, paṭhamā migajātā tathūpame ahaṃ ime paṭhame samaṇabrāhmaṇe vadāmi.

267."Here, bhikkhus, the first ascetics and brahmins, consuming that laid-out bait of Māra and those worldly allurements, became infatuated and indulged in food. Indulging in food there, infatuated, they fell into intoxication; intoxicated, they fell into heedlessness; heedless, they came under Māra’s control regarding that bait and those worldly allurements. Thus, bhikkhus, those first ascetics and brahmins did not escape Māra’s sphere of influence. Just as those first kinds of deer, bhikkhus, so too, I say, are these first ascetics and brahmins."

268. ‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, dutiyā samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃ samacintesuṃ – ‘ye kho te paṭhamā samaṇabrāhmaṇā amuṃ nivāpaṃ nivuttaṃ mārassa amūni ca lokāmisāni anupakhajja mucchitā bhojanāni bhuñjiṃsu. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabbaso ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘anantaṃ viññāṇa’nti viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave…pe… pāpimato.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, with the complete transcending of the sphere of infinite space, [thinking,] ‘Consciousness is infinite,’ a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the sphere of infinite consciousness. This, bhikkhus, is called a bhikkhu who has blinded Māra, destroyed Māra’s eye, leaving it without a trace, and gone out of sight of the Evil One.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabbaso viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘natthi kiñcī’ti ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave…pe… pāpimato.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, with the complete transcending of the sphere of infinite consciousness, [thinking,] ‘There is nothing,’ a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the sphere of nothingness. This, bhikkhus, is called a bhikkhu who has blinded Māra, destroyed Māra’s eye, leaving it without a trace, and gone out of sight of the Evil One.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabbaso ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ samatikkamma nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave…pe… pāpimato.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, with the complete transcending of the sphere of nothingness, a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception. This, bhikkhus, is called a bhikkhu who has blinded Māra, destroyed Māra’s eye, leaving it without a trace, and gone out of sight of the Evil One.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabbaso nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ samatikkamma saññāvedayitanirodhaṃ upasampajja viharati. Paññāya cassa disvā āsavā parikkhīṇā honti. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu andhamakāsi māraṃ, apadaṃ vadhitvā māracakkhuṃ adassanaṃ gato pāpimato tiṇṇo loke visattika’’nti.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, with the complete transcending of the sphere of neither perception nor non-perception, a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the cessation of perception and feeling. And, having seen with wisdom, his taints are completely destroyed. This, bhikkhus, is called a bhikkhu who has blinded Māra, destroyed Māra’s eye, leaving it without a trace, and gone out of sight of the Evil One, having crossed over the world’s entanglement.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

This the Blessed One said. The bhikkhus were pleased and rejoiced at the Blessed One’s words.

Nivāpasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.

The Nivāpa Sutta, the fifth, is concluded.
