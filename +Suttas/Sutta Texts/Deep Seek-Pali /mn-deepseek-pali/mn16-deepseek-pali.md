# 16. Cetokhilasuttaṃ

## 16. The Discourse on Mental Barrenness

185. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

185.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus!” “Venerable sir,” they replied. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Yassa kassaci, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno pañca cetokhilā appahīnā, pañca cetasovinibandhā [cetasovinibaddhā (sī.), cetovinibaddhā (sāratthadīpanīṭīkā)] asamucchinnā, so vatimasmiṃ dhammavinaye vuddhiṃ virūḷhiṃ vepullaṃ āpajjissatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

“Bhikkhus, it is impossible that any bhikkhu who has not abandoned the five mental barrennesses and has not cut off the five mental bonds will achieve growth, increase, and abundance in this Dhamma and Discipline.

‘‘Katamāssa pañca cetokhilā appahīnā honti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu satthari kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu satthari kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ paṭhamo cetokhilo appahīno hoti.

“What are the five mental barrennesses he has not abandoned? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu doubts the Teacher, is uncertain, is not resolved, and is not confident. When a bhikkhu doubts the Teacher, is uncertain, is not resolved, and is not confident, his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the first mental barrenness he has not abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu dhamme kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati…pe… evamassāyaṃ dutiyo cetokhilo appahīno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu doubts the Dhamma, is uncertain, is not resolved, and is not confident…Thus, this second mental barrenness he has not abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu saṅghe kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati…pe… evamassāyaṃ tatiyo cetokhilo appahīno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu doubts the Saṅgha, is uncertain, is not resolved, and is not confident…Thus, this third mental barrenness he has not abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sikkhāya kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sikkhāya kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati, tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ catuttho cetokhilo appahīno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu doubts the training, is uncertain, is not resolved, and is not confident. When a bhikkhu doubts the training, is uncertain, is not resolved, and is not confident, his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the fourth mental barrenness he has not abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabrahmacārīsu kupito hoti anattamano āhatacitto khilajāto. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabrahmacārīsu kupito hoti anattamano āhatacitto khilajāto, tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ pañcamo cetokhilo appahīno hoti. Imāssa pañca cetokhilā appahīnā honti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is angry, displeased, with a wounded mind, and has become barren towards his fellow practitioners. When a bhikkhu is angry, displeased, with a wounded mind, and has become barren towards his fellow practitioners, his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the fifth mental barrenness he has not abandoned. These five mental barrennesses he has not abandoned.

186. ‘‘Katamāssa pañca cetasovinibandhā asamucchinnā honti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāme avītarāgo [avigatarāgo (katthaci)] hoti avigatacchando avigatapemo avigatapipāso avigatapariḷāho avigatataṇho. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāme avītarāgo hoti avigatacchando avigatapemo avigatapipāso avigatapariḷāho avigatataṇho, tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ paṭhamo cetasovinibandho asamucchinno hoti.

186.“What are the five mental bonds he has not cut off? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is not rid of passion for sensual pleasures; his desire, affection, infatuation, thirst, burning, and craving for them are not gone. When a bhikkhu is not rid of passion for sensual pleasures, his desire, affection, infatuation, thirst, burning, and craving for them are not gone, his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the first mental bond he has not cut off.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye avītarāgo hoti…pe… evamassāyaṃ dutiyo cetasovinibandho asamucchinno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is not rid of passion for the body…Thus, this is the second mental bond he has not cut off.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu rūpe avītarāgo hoti…pe… evamassāyaṃ tatiyo cetasovinibandho asamucchinno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is not rid of passion for forms…Thus, this is the third mental bond he has not cut off.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu yāvadatthaṃ udarāvadehakaṃ bhuñjitvā seyyasukhaṃ passasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ anuyutto viharati. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu yāvadatthaṃ udarāvadehakaṃ bhuñjitvā seyyasukhaṃ passasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ anuyutto viharati, tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ catuttho cetasovinibandho asamucchinno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu, having eaten his fill, dwells devoted to the pleasure of lying down, the pleasure of reclining, and the pleasure of sleep. When a bhikkhu, having eaten his fill, dwells devoted to the pleasure of lying down, the pleasure of reclining, and the pleasure of sleep, his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the fourth mental bond he has not cut off.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu aññataraṃ devanikāyaṃ paṇidhāya brahmacariyaṃ carati – ‘imināhaṃ sīlena vā vatena vā tapena vā brahmacariyena vā devo vā bhavissāmi devaññataro vā’ti. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu aññataraṃ devanikāyaṃ paṇidhāya brahmacariyaṃ carati – ‘imināhaṃ sīlena vā vatena vā tapena vā brahmacariyena vā devo vā bhavissāmi devaññataro vā’ti, tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ pañcamo cetasovinibandho asamucchinno hoti. Imāssa pañca cetasovinibandhā asamucchinnā honti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu undertakes the spiritual life aspiring to a certain group of deities, thinking: ‘By this virtue, or observance, or austerity, or spiritual life, may I become a god or one of the gods.’ When a bhikkhu undertakes the spiritual life aspiring to a certain group of deities, thinking: ‘By this virtue, or observance, or austerity, or spiritual life, may I become a god or one of the gods,’ his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind does not incline to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the fifth mental bond he has not cut off. These five mental bonds he has not cut off.

‘‘Yassa kassaci, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ime pañca cetokhilā appahīnā, ime pañca cetasovinibandhā asamucchinnā, so vatimasmiṃ dhammavinaye vuddhiṃ virūḷhiṃ vepullaṃ āpajjissatīti – netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

“Bhikkhus, it is impossible that any bhikkhu in whom these five mental barrennesses are not abandoned and these five mental bonds are not cut off will achieve growth, increase, and abundance in this Dhamma and Discipline.

187. ‘‘Yassa kassaci, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno pañca cetokhilā pahīnā, pañca cetasovinibandhā susamucchinnā, so vatimasmiṃ dhammavinaye vuddhiṃ virūḷhiṃ vepullaṃ āpajjissatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati.

187.“Bhikkhus, it is possible that any bhikkhu who has abandoned the five mental barrennesses and has completely cut off the five mental bonds will achieve growth, increase, and abundance in this Dhamma and Discipline.

‘‘Katamāssa pañca cetokhilā pahīnā honti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu satthari na kaṅkhati na vicikicchati adhimuccati sampasīdati. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu satthari na kaṅkhati na vicikicchati adhimuccati sampasīdati, tassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ paṭhamo cetokhilo pahīno hoti.

“What are the five mental barrennesses he has abandoned? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu does not doubt the Teacher, is not uncertain, is resolved, and is confident. When a bhikkhu does not doubt the Teacher, is not uncertain, is resolved, and is confident, his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the first mental barrenness he has abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu dhamme na kaṅkhati na vicikicchati adhimuccati sampasīdati…pe… evamassāyaṃ dutiyo cetokhilo pahīno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu does not doubt the Dhamma, is not uncertain, is resolved, and is confident…Thus, this is the second mental barrenness he has abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu saṅghe na kaṅkhati na vicikicchati adhimuccati sampasīdati…pe… evamassāyaṃ tatiyo cetokhilo pahīno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu does not doubt the Saṅgha, is not uncertain, is resolved, and is confident…Thus, this is the third mental barrenness he has abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sikkhāya na kaṅkhati na vicikicchati adhimuccati sampasīdati…pe… evamassāyaṃ catuttho cetokhilo pahīno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu does not doubt the training, is not uncertain, is resolved, and is confident…Thus, this is the fourth mental barrenness he has abandoned.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabrahmacārīsu na kupito hoti na anattamano [attamano (sī. pī.)] anāhatacitto akhilajāto. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabrahmacārīsu na kupito hoti na anattamano anāhatacitto akhilajāto, tassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ pañcamo cetokhilo pahīno hoti. Imāssa pañca cetokhilā pahīnā honti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is not angry, is not displeased, his mind is not wounded, and he has not become barren towards his fellow practitioners. When a bhikkhu is not angry, is not displeased, his mind is not wounded, and he has not become barren towards his fellow practitioners, his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the fifth mental barrenness he has abandoned. These five mental barrennesses he has abandoned.

188. ‘‘Katamāssa pañca cetasovinibandhā susamucchinnā honti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāme vītarāgo hoti vigatacchando vigatapemo vigatapipāso vigatapariḷāho vigatataṇho. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāme vītarāgo hoti vigatacchando vigatapemo vigatapipāso vigatapariḷāho vigatataṇho, tassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ paṭhamo cetasovinibandho susamucchinno hoti.

188.“What are the five mental bonds he has completely cut off? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is rid of passion for sensual pleasures; his desire, affection, infatuation, thirst, burning, and craving for them are gone. When a bhikkhu is rid of passion for sensual pleasures, his desire, affection, infatuation, thirst, burning, and craving for them are gone, his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the first mental bond he has completely cut off.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye vītarāgo hoti…pe… rūpe vītarāgo hoti…pe… na yāvadatthaṃ udarāvadehakaṃ bhuñjitvā seyyasukhaṃ passasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ anuyutto viharati. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu na yāvadatthaṃ udarāvadehakaṃ bhuñjitvā seyyasukhaṃ passasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ anuyutto viharati, tassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ catuttho cetasovinibandho susamucchinno hoti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is rid of passion for the body…is rid of passion for forms…does not, having eaten his fill, dwell devoted to the pleasure of lying down, the pleasure of reclining, and the pleasure of sleep. When a bhikkhu does not, having eaten his fill, dwell devoted to the pleasure of lying down, the pleasure of reclining, and the pleasure of sleep, his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the fourth mental bond he has completely cut off.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu na aññataraṃ devanikāyaṃ paṇidhāya brahmacariyaṃ carati – ‘imināhaṃ sīlena vā vatena vā tapena vā brahmacariyena vā devo vā bhavissāmi devaññataro vā’ti. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu na aññataraṃ devanikāyaṃ paṇidhāya brahmacariyaṃ carati – ‘imināhaṃ sīlena vā vatena vā tapena vā brahmacariyena vā devo vā bhavissāmi devaññataro vā’ti, tassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, evamassāyaṃ pañcamo cetasovinibandho susamucchinno hoti. Imāssa pañca cetasovinibandhā susamucchinnā honti.

“Further, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu does not undertake the spiritual life aspiring to a certain group of deities, thinking: ‘By this virtue, or observance, or austerity, or spiritual life, may I become a god or one of the gods.’ When a bhikkhu does not undertake the spiritual life aspiring to a certain group of deities, thinking: ‘By this virtue, or observance, or austerity, or spiritual life, may I become a god or one of the gods,’ his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving. When his mind inclines to ardour, application, perseverance, and striving, this is the fifth mental bond he has completely cut off. These five mental bonds he has completely cut off.

‘‘Yassa kassaci, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno ime pañca cetokhilā pahīnā, ime pañca cetasovinibandhā susamucchinnā, so vatimasmiṃ dhammavinaye vuddhiṃ virūḷhiṃ vepullaṃ āpajjissatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati.

"bhikkhus, for any monk who has abandoned these five mental barrennesses and has utterly uprooted these five mental fetters, it is to be expected that he will attain growth, increase, and abundance in this Dhamma and Discipline.

189. ‘‘So chandasamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, cittasamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīmaṃsāsamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, ussoḷhīyeva pañcamī. Sa kho so, bhikkhave, evaṃ ussoḷhīpannarasaṅgasamannāgato bhikkhu bhabbo abhinibbidāya, bhabbo sambodhāya, bhabbo anuttarassa yogakkhemassa adhigamāya. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, kukkuṭiyā aṇḍāni aṭṭha vā dasa vā dvādasa vā. Tānassu kukkuṭiyā sammā adhisayitāni sammā pariseditāni sammā paribhāvitāni. Kiñcāpi tassā kukkuṭiyā na evaṃ icchā uppajjeyya – ‘aho vatime kukkuṭapotakā pādanakhasikhāya vā mukhatuṇḍakena vā aṇḍakosaṃ padāletvā sotthinā abhinibbhijjeyyu’nti. Atha kho bhabbāva te kukkuṭapotakā pādanakhasikhāya vā mukhatuṇḍakena vā aṇḍakosaṃ padāletvā sotthinā abhinibbhijjituṃ. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, evaṃ ussoḷhipannarasaṅgasamannāgato bhikkhu bhabbo abhinibbidāya, bhabbo sambodhāya, bhabbo anuttarassa yogakkhemassa adhigamāyā’’ti.

189.He develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of desire, accompanied by the formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of energy, accompanied by the formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of mind, accompanied by the formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of investigation, accompanied by the formations of striving, with exertion as the fifth. Indeed, bhikkhus, a monk endowed with these fifteen factors, with exertion as the fifteenth, is capable of penetration, capable of full enlightenment, capable of attaining the unsurpassed security from bondage. Suppose, bhikkhus, a hen has eight, ten, or twelve eggs. They are properly brooded over by the hen, properly warmed, properly nurtured. Even if that hen does not have such a wish as: 'Oh, may these chicks pierce the eggshell with their claw-tips or beak-tips and hatch safely!' Still, those chicks are capable of piercing the eggshell with their claw-tips or beak-tips and hatching safely. In the same way, bhikkhus, a monk endowed with these fifteen factors, with exertion as the fifteenth, is capable of penetration, capable of full enlightenment, capable of attaining the unsurpassed security from bondage."

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

This is what the Blessed One said. The bhikkhus were pleased and delighted with the Blessed One’s words.

Cetokhilasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The Discourse on Mental Obstacles: the Sixth, ended.
