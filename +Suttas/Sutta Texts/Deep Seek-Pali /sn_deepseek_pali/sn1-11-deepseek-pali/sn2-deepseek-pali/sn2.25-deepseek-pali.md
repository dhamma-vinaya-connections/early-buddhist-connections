# SN 2.25 Jantusuttaṃ

## SN 2.25 Jantu Sutta

106. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ sambahulā bhikkhū, kosalesu viharanti himavantapasse araññakuṭikāya uddhatā unnaḷā capalā mukharā vikiṇṇavācā muṭṭhassatino asampajānā asamāhitā vibbhantacittā pākatindriyā.

106.Thus have I heard. On one occasion a number of bhikkhus were dwelling in the Kosala country, in a forest hut on the slopes of the Himalayas. They were restless, haughty, fickle, harsh-spoken, prone to scattered talk, unmindful, uncomprehending, uncollected, with straying minds, and with uncontrolled senses.

Atha kho jantu devaputto tadahuposathe pannarase yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

Then the devaputta Jantu, on the fifteenth, the Uposatha day, approached those bhikkhus. Having approached, he addressed the bhikkhus in verses:

‘‘Sukhajīvino pure āsuṃ, bhikkhū gotamasāvakā;Anicchā piṇḍamesanā [piṇḍamesānā (?)], anicchā sayanāsanaṃ;Loke aniccataṃ ñatvā, dukkhassantaṃ akaṃsu te.

“Formerly, the bhikkhus, disciples of Gotama, lived happily; Without craving for alms-food, without craving for lodging; Having known impermanence in the world, they made an end of suffering.”

‘‘Dupposaṃ katvā attānaṃ, gāme gāmaṇikā viya;Bhutvā bhutvā nipajjanti, parāgāresu mucchitā.

“Having made themselves hard to support, like village headmen in a village, having eaten and eaten, they lie down, stupefied in others' houses.”

‘‘Saṅghassa añjaliṃ katvā, idhekacce vadāmahaṃ [vandāmahaṃ (ka.)];Apaviddhā anāthā te, yathā petā tatheva te [tatheva ca (sī.)].

“Having paid homage to the Saṅgha, I declare this to some here: Cast out and without refuge are they, just like the dead.”

‘‘Ye kho pamattā viharanti, te me sandhāya bhāsitaṃ;Ye appamattā viharanti, namo tesaṃ karomaha’’nti.

“It is with those who dwell heedlessly in mind that I speak; To those who dwell diligently, I pay homage.”
