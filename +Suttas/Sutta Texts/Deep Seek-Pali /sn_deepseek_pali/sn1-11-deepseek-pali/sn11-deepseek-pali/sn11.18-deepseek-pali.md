# SN 11.18 Gahaṭṭhavandanāsuttaṃ

## SN 11.18 The Homage to Householders Sutta

264. Sāvatthiyaṃ. Tatra…pe… etadavoca – ‘‘bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, sakko devānamindo mātaliṃ saṅgāhakaṃ āmantesi – ‘yojehi, samma mātali, sahassayuttaṃ ājaññarathaṃ. Uyyānabhūmiṃ gacchāma subhūmiṃ dassanāyā’ti. ‘Evaṃ bhaddantavā’ti kho, bhikkhave, mātali saṅgāhako sakkassa devānamindassa paṭissutvā sahassayuttaṃ ājaññarathaṃ yojetvā sakkassa devānamindassa paṭivedesi – ‘yutto kho te, mārisa, sahassayutto ājaññaratho. Yassa dāni kālaṃ maññasī’’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, sakko devānamindo vejayantapāsādā orohanto añjaliṃ katvā [pañjaliko (pī.), pañjaliṃ katvā (ka.)] sudaṃ puthuddisā namassati. Atha kho, bhikkhave, mātali saṅgāhako sakkaṃ devānamindaṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

264.At Savatthi. There… the Blessed One said this: "Once upon a time, monks, Sakka, the lord of the devas, addressed Matali the charioteer: ‘Harness the thousand-yoked chariot, dear Matali. We will go to the pleasure grove to see the beautiful grounds.’ ‘Yes, lord,’ replied Matali the charioteer to Sakka, the lord of the devas. Having harnessed the thousand-yoked chariot, he informed Sakka, the lord of devas: ‘Your thousand-yoked chariot is ready, sir. Now is the time for whatever you deem fit.’" Then, monks, Sakka, the lord of the devas, descending from the Vejayanta Palace, paid homage with joined palms to the various directions. Then, monks, Matali the charioteer addressed Sakka, the lord of the devas, in verse:

‘‘Taṃ namassanti tevijjā, sabbe bhummā ca khattiyā;Cattāro ca mahārājā, tidasā ca yasassino;Atha ko nāma so yakkho, yaṃ tvaṃ sakka namassasī’’ti.

"The three knowers worship him, all earthly nobles too; The four great kings and the glorious gods of the Thirty-three; Then what is the name of that spirit, O Sakka, whom you worship thus?"

‘‘Maṃ namassanti tevijjā, sabbe bhummā ca khattiyā;Cattāro ca mahārājā, tidasā ca yasassino.

"Those with the threefold knowledge pay homage to me, as do all earthly rulers and warriors, and the four Great Kings, and the glorious Thirty-three."

‘‘Ahañca sīlasampanne, cirarattasamāhite;Sammāpabbajite vande, brahmacariyaparāyane.

"I venerate one endowed with virtue, long steadfast, well-collected; Rightly gone forth, devoted to the life of purity."

‘‘Ye gahaṭṭhā puññakarā, sīlavanto upāsakā;Dhammena dāraṃ posenti, te namassāmi mātalī’’ti.

"Those householders who perform meritorious deeds, virtuous lay followers, Who support their spouses righteously, I honor them, Mātali."

‘‘Seṭṭhā hi kira lokasmiṃ, ye tvaṃ sakka namassasi;Ahampi te namassāmi, ye namassasi vāsavā’’ti.

"Indeed, the best in the world are they, whom you, Sakka, revere; I too revere those whom you revere, Vāsava."

‘‘Idaṃ vatvāna maghavā, devarājā sujampati;Puthuddisā namassitvā, pamukho rathamāruhī’’ti.

"Having said this, Maghavā, Sujampati, the king of gods; Having bowed to the various directions, ascended the foremost chariot."
