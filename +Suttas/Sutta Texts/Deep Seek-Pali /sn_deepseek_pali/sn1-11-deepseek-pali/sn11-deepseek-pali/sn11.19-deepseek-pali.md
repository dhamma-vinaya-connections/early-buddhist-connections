# SN 11.19 Satthāravandanāsuttaṃ

## SN 11.19 The Homage to the Teacher Discourse

265. Sāvatthiyaṃ jetavane. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, sakko devānamindo mātaliṃ saṅgāhakaṃ āmantesi – ‘yojehi, samma mātali, sahassayuttaṃ ājaññarathaṃ, uyyānabhūmiṃ gacchāma subhūmiṃ dassanāyā’ti. ‘Evaṃ bhaddantavā’ti kho, bhikkhave, mātali saṅgāhako sakkassa devānamindassa paṭissutvā sahassayuttaṃ ājaññarathaṃ yojetvā sakkassa devānamindassa paṭivedesi – ‘yutto kho te, mārisa, sahassayutto ājaññaratho. Yassa dāni kālaṃ maññasī’’’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, sakko devānamindo vejayantapāsādā orohanto añjaliṃ katvā sudaṃ bhagavantaṃ namassati. Atha kho, bhikkhave, mātali saṅgāhako sakkaṃ devānamindaṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

265.At Savatthi, in Jeta's Grove. "Once upon a time, bhikkhus, Sakka, the lord of the gods, addressed Mātali the charioteer: ‘Harness the thousand-team chariot, dear Mātali, we will go to the pleasure-ground to see the beautiful land.’ ‘Yes, lord,’ replied Mātali the charioteer to Sakka, the lord of the gods. Having harnessed the thousand-team chariot, he announced to Sakka, ‘Your thousand-team chariot is ready, sir. Now is the time for whatever you think fit.’ Then, bhikkhus, Sakka, descending from the Vejayanta Palace, raised his joined palms and paid homage to the Blessed One. Thereupon, Mātali the charioteer addressed Sakka in verse:

‘‘Yañhi devā manussā ca, taṃ namassanti vāsava;Atha ko nāma so yakkho, yaṃ tvaṃ sakka namassasī’’ti.

"Indeed, whatever gods and humans revere, O Vāsava, that they worship. But who is that yakkha, O Sakka, that you yourself revere?"

‘‘Yo idha sammāsambuddho, asmiṃ loke sadevake;Anomanāmaṃ satthāraṃ, taṃ namassāmi mātali.

"Here in this world with its gods, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, the Teacher of peerless name—him I worship, O Mātali."

‘‘Yesaṃ rāgo ca doso ca, avijjā ca virājitā;Khīṇāsavā arahanto, te namassāmi mātali.

"Those whose lust, hate, and delusion are dispelled, The taintless ones, the Arahants— Them I honor, Mātali."

‘‘Ye rāgadosavinayā, avijjāsamatikkamā;Sekkhā apacayārāmā, appamattānusikkhare;Te namassāmi mātalī’’ti.

"Those who have abandoned lust and hate, Who have transcended ignorance, The trainees delighting in relinquishment, Constantly mindful, training diligently— Them I honor, Mātali."

‘‘Seṭṭhā hi kira lokasmiṃ, ye tvaṃ sakka namassasi;Ahampi te namassāmi, ye namassasi vāsavā’’ti.

"Indeed, the best in the world are they, whom you, Sakka, revere; I too revere those whom you revere, Vāsava."

‘‘Idaṃ vatvāna maghavā, devarājā sujampati;Bhagavantaṃ namassitvā, pamukho rathamāruhī’’ti.

"Having said this, Maghavā, the king of gods, Sujampati, bowed down to the Blessed One and, at the forefront, mounted his chariot."
