# SN 3.21 Puggalasuttaṃ

## SN 3.21 The Discourse on Individuals

132. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho rājānaṃ pasenadiṃ kosalaṃ bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘cattārome, mahārāja, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame cattāro? Tamotamaparāyano, tamojotiparāyano, jotitamaparāyano, jotijotiparāyano’’.

132.At Sāvatthī. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. The Blessed One then said to King Pasenadi of Kosala as he was sitting there: “Great king, these four types of individuals are found existing in the world. Which four? One who is destined from darkness to darkness; one who is destined from darkness to light; one who is destined from light to darkness; and one who is destined from light to light.”

‘‘Kathañca, mahārāja puggalo tamotamaparāyano hoti? Idha, mahārāja, ekacco puggalo nīce kule paccājāto hoti, caṇḍālakule vā venakule [veṇakule (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] vā nesādakule vā rathakārakule vā pukkusakule vā dalidde appannapānabhojane kasiravuttike, yattha kasirena ghāsacchādo labbhati. So ca hoti dubbaṇṇo duddasiko okoṭimako bavhābādho [bahvābādho (ka.)] kāṇo vā kuṇī vā khañjo vā pakkhahato vā, na lābhī annassa pānassa vatthassa yānassa mālāgandhavilepanassa seyyāvasathapadīpeyyassa. So kāyena duccaritaṃ carati, vācāya duccaritaṃ carati, manasā duccaritaṃ carati. So kāyena duccaritaṃ caritvā vācāya duccaritaṃ caritvā manasā duccaritaṃ caritvā, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati.

“And how, great king, is an individual one who is destined from darkness to darkness? Here, great king, a certain individual is reborn in a low family—a family of outcastes, or bamboo workers, or hunters, or cart makers, or refuse collectors—one that is poor, with little food and drink, living miserably, where food and clothing are obtained with difficulty. And he is ugly, unsightly, deformed, sickly—blind, or crippled, or lame, or paralyzed. He does not obtain food, drink, clothing, vehicles, garlands, scents, ointments, beds, dwellings, or lighting. He practices misconduct by body, speech, and mind. Having practiced misconduct by body, speech, and mind, upon the breakup of the body, after death, he reappears in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, mahārāja, puriso andhakārā vā andhakāraṃ gaccheyya, tamā vā tamaṃ gaccheyya, lohitamalā vā lohitamalaṃ gaccheyya. Tathūpamāhaṃ, mahārāja, imaṃ puggalaṃ vadāmi. Evaṃ kho, mahārāja, puggalo tamotamaparāyano hoti.

“Just as, great king, a person might go from darkness to darkness, or from gloom to gloom, or from defilement to defilement. So too, great king, do I declare this individual. In this way, great king, an individual is one who is destined from darkness to darkness.”

‘‘Kathañca, mahārāja, puggalo tamojotiparāyano hoti? Idha, mahārāja, ekacco puggalo nīce kule paccājāto hoti, caṇḍālakule vā venakule vā nesādakule vā rathakārakule vā pukkusakule vā dalidde appannapānabhojane kasiravuttike, yattha kasirena ghāsacchādo labbhati. So ca kho hoti dubbaṇṇo duddasiko okoṭimako bavhābādho, kāṇo vā kuṇī vā khañjo vā pakkhahato vā, na lābhī annassa pānassa vatthassa yānassa mālāgandhavilepanassa seyyāvasathapadīpeyyassa. So kāyena sucaritaṃ carati, vācāya sucaritaṃ carati, manasā sucaritaṃ carati. So kāyena sucaritaṃ caritvā vācāya sucaritaṃ caritvā manasā sucaritaṃ caritvā, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati.

“And how, great king, is an individual one who is destined from darkness to light? Here, great king, a certain individual is reborn in a low family—a family of outcastes, or bamboo workers, or hunters, or cart makers, or refuse collectors—one that is poor, with little food and drink, living miserably, where food and clothing are obtained with difficulty. And he is ugly, unsightly, deformed, sickly—blind, or crippled, or lame, or paralyzed. He does not obtain food, drink, clothing, vehicles, garlands, scents, ointments, beds, dwellings, or lighting. He practices good conduct by body, speech, and mind. Having practiced good conduct by body, speech, and mind, upon the breakup of the body, after death, he reappears in a good destination, in a heavenly world.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, mahārāja, puriso pathaviyā vā pallaṅkaṃ āroheyya, pallaṅkā vā assapiṭṭhiṃ āroheyya, assapiṭṭhiyā vā hatthikkhandhaṃ āroheyya, hatthikkhandhā vā pāsādaṃ āroheyya. Tathūpamāhaṃ, mahārāja, imaṃ puggalaṃ vadāmi. Evaṃ kho, mahārāja, puggalo tamojotiparāyano hoti.

“Just as, great king, a person might climb from the ground onto a couch, or from a couch onto a horse’s back, or from a horse’s back onto an elephant’s shoulder, or from an elephant’s shoulder onto a palace. So too, great king, do I declare this individual. In this way, great king, an individual is one who is destined from darkness to light.”

‘‘Kathañca, mahārāja, puggalo jotitamaparāyano hoti? Idha, mahārāja, ekacco puggalo ucce kule paccājāto hoti, khattiyamahāsālakule vā brāhmaṇamahāsālakule vā gahapatimahāsālakule vā, aḍḍhe mahaddhane mahābhoge pahūtajātarūparajate pahūtavittūpakaraṇe pahūtadhanadhaññe. So ca hoti abhirūpo dassanīyo pāsādiko, paramāya vaṇṇapokkharatāya samannāgato, lābhī annassa pānassa vatthassa yānassa mālāgandhavilepanassa seyyāvasathapadīpeyyassa. So kāyena duccaritaṃ carati, vācāya duccaritaṃ carati, manasā duccaritaṃ carati. So kāyena duccaritaṃ caritvā vācāya duccaritaṃ caritvā manasā duccaritaṃ caritvā, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati.

“And how, great king, is an individual one who is destined from light to darkness? Here, great king, a certain individual is reborn in a high family—a wealthy noble warrior family, or a wealthy brahmin family, or a wealthy householder family—one that is rich, with great wealth and great possessions, possessing abundant gold and silver, abundant means of livelihood, and abundant wealth and grain. And he is handsome, attractive, and pleasing, possessing supreme beauty of complexion; he obtains food, drink, clothing, vehicles, garlands, scents, ointments, beds, dwellings, and lighting. He practices misconduct by body, speech, and mind. Having practiced misconduct by body, speech, and mind, upon the breakup of the body, after death, he reappears in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, in hell.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, mahārāja, puriso pāsādā vā hatthikkhandhaṃ oroheyya, hatthikkhandhā vā assapiṭṭhiṃ oroheyya, assapiṭṭhiyā vā pallaṅkaṃ oroheyya, pallaṅkā vā pathaviṃ oroheyya, pathaviyā vā andhakāraṃ paviseyya. Tathūpamāhaṃ, mahārāja, imaṃ puggalaṃ vadāmi. Evaṃ kho, mahārāja, puggalo jotitamaparāyano hoti.

“Just as, great king, a person might descend from a palace to an elephant’s shoulder, or from an elephant’s shoulder to a horse’s back, or from a horse’s back to a couch, or from a couch to the ground, or from the ground enter darkness. So too, great king, do I declare this individual. In this way, great king, an individual is one who is destined from light to darkness.”

‘‘Kathañca, mahārāja, puggalo jotijotiparāyano hoti? Idha, mahārāja, ekacco puggalo ucce kule paccājāto hoti, khattiyamahāsālakule vā brāhmaṇamahāsālakule vā gahapatimahāsālakule vā, aḍḍhe mahaddhane mahābhoge pahūtajātarūparajate pahūtavittūpakaraṇe pahūtadhanadhaññe. So ca hoti abhirūpo dassanīyo pāsādiko, paramāya vaṇṇapokkharatāya samannāgato, lābhī annassa pānassa vatthassa yānassa mālāgandhavilepanassa seyyāvasathapadīpeyyassa. So kāyena sucaritaṃ carati, vācāya sucaritaṃ carati, manasā sucaritaṃ carati. So kāyena sucaritaṃ caritvā vācāya sucaritaṃ caritvā manasā sucaritaṃ caritvā, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati.

“And how, great king, is an individual one who is destined from light to light? Here, great king, a certain individual is reborn in a high family—a wealthy noble warrior family, or a wealthy brahmin family, or a wealthy householder family—one that is rich, with great wealth and great possessions, possessing abundant gold and silver, abundant means of livelihood, and abundant wealth and grain. And he is handsome, attractive, and pleasing, possessing supreme beauty of complexion; he obtains food, drink, clothing, vehicles, garlands, scents, ointments, beds, dwellings, and lighting. He practices good conduct by body, speech, and mind. Having practiced good conduct by body, speech, and mind, upon the breakup of the body, after death, he reappears in a good destination, in a heavenly world.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, mahārāja, puriso pallaṅkā vā pallaṅkaṃ saṅkameyya, assapiṭṭhiyā vā assapiṭṭhiṃ saṅkameyya, hatthikkhandhā vā hatthikkhandhaṃ saṅkameyya, pāsādā vā pāsādaṃ saṅkameyya. Tathūpamāhaṃ, mahārāja, imaṃ puggalaṃ vadāmi. Evaṃ kho, mahārāja, puggalo jotijotiparāyano hoti. Ime kho, mahārāja, cattāro puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmi’’nti. Idamavoca…pe…

“Just as, great king, a person might move from one couch to another couch, or from one horse’s back to another horse’s back, or from one elephant’s shoulder to another elephant’s shoulder, or from one palace to another palace. So too, great king, do I declare this individual. In this way, great king, an individual is one who is destined from light to light. These, great king, are the four types of individuals found existing in the world.” The Blessed One said this…

‘‘Daliddo puriso rāja, assaddho hoti maccharī;Kadariyo pāpasaṅkappo, micchādiṭṭhi anādaro.

“A poor man, O king, is faithless and stingy, miserly, with evil intentions, holding wrong views, disrespectful.

‘‘Samaṇe brāhmaṇe vāpi, aññe vāpi vanibbake;Akkosati paribhāsati, natthiko hoti rosako.

Whether ascetics or brahmins, or other bhikkhus, he insults and reviles them, being a nihilist and quarrelsome.

‘‘Dadamānaṃ nivāreti, yācamānāna bhojanaṃ;Tādiso puriso rāja, mīyamāno janādhipa;Upeti nirayaṃ ghoraṃ, tamotamaparāyano.

He prevents one who is giving, and food from those who ask.
Such a person, O king, when dying, lord of men,
Goes to a terrible hell, one who is destined from darkness to darkness.

‘‘Daliddo puriso rāja, saddho hoti amaccharī;Dadāti seṭṭhasaṅkappo, abyaggamanaso naro.

“A poor man, O king, is faithful and unselfish; he gives with a noble resolve, a man with an undistracted mind.

‘‘Samaṇe brāhmaṇe vāpi, aññe vāpi vanibbake;Uṭṭhāya abhivādeti, samacariyāya sikkhati.

Whether ascetics or brahmins, or other bhikkhus, rising, he greets them with respect and trains in even conduct.

‘‘Dadamānaṃ na vāreti [na nivāreti (sī.)], yācamānāna bhojanaṃ;Tādiso puriso rāja, mīyamāno janādhipa;Upeti tidivaṃ ṭhānaṃ, tamojotiparāyano.

He does not hinder one who is giving, nor (refuse) food to those who ask.
Such a person, O king, when dying, lord of men,
Goes to a heavenly state, one who is destined from darkness to light.

‘‘Aḍḍho ce [aḍḍho ve (pī. ka.)] puriso rāja, assaddho hoti maccharī;Kadariyo pāpasaṅkappo, micchādiṭṭhi anādaro.

“If a man, O king, is wealthy, yet faithless and stingy, miserly, with evil intentions, holding wrong views, disrespectful.

‘‘Samaṇe brāhmaṇe vāpi, aññe vāpi vanibbake;Akkosati paribhāsati, natthiko hoti rosako.

Whether ascetics or brahmins, or other bhikkhus, he insults and reviles them, being a nihilist and quarrelsome.

‘‘Dadamānaṃ nivāreti, yācamānāna bhojanaṃ;Tādiso puriso rāja, mīyamāno janādhipa;Upeti nirayaṃ ghoraṃ, jotitamaparāyano.

He prevents one who is giving, and food from those who ask.
Such a person, O king, when dying, lord of men,
Goes to a terrible hell, one who is destined from light to darkness.

‘‘Aḍḍho ce puriso rāja, saddho hoti amaccharī;Dadāti seṭṭhasaṅkappo, abyaggamanaso naro.

“If a man, O king, is wealthy, faithful, and unselfish; he gives with a noble resolve, a man with an undistracted mind.

‘‘Samaṇe brāhmaṇe vāpi, aññe vāpi vanibbake;Uṭṭhāya abhivādeti, samacariyāya sikkhati.

Whether ascetics or brahmins, or other bhikkhus, rising, he greets them with respect and trains in even conduct.

‘‘Dadamānaṃ na vāreti, yācamānāna bhojanaṃ;Tādiso puriso rāja, mīyamāno janādhipa;Upeti tidivaṃ ṭhānaṃ, jotijotiparāyano’’ti.

He does not hinder one who is giving, nor (refuse) food to those who ask.
Such a person, O king, when dying, lord of men,
Goes to a heavenly state, one who is destined from light to light.”
