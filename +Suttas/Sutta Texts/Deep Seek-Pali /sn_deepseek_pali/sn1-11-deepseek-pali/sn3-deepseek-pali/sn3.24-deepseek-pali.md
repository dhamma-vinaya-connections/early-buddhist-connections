# SN 3.24 Issattasuttaṃ

## SN 3.24 The Arrow Discourse

135. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho rājā pasenadi kosalo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kattha nu kho, bhante, dānaṃ dātabba’’nti? ‘‘Yattha kho, mahārāja, cittaṃ pasīdatī’’ti. ‘‘Kattha pana, bhante, dinnaṃ mahapphala’’nti? ‘‘Aññaṃ kho etaṃ, mahārāja, kattha dānaṃ dātabbaṃ, aññaṃ panetaṃ kattha dinnaṃ mahapphalanti? Sīlavato kho, mahārāja, dinnaṃ mahapphalaṃ, no tathā dussīle. Tena hi, mahārāja, taññevettha paṭipucchissāmi. Yathā, te khameyya, tathā naṃ byākareyyāsi. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, mahārāja, idha tyassa yuddhaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ saṅgāmo samupabyūḷho [samūpabbūḷho (sī.), samupabbuḷho (pī.)]. Atha āgaccheyya khattiyakumāro asikkhito akatahattho akatayoggo akatūpāsano bhīru chambhī utrāsī palāyī. Bhareyyāsi taṃ purisaṃ, attho ca te tādisena purisenā’’ti? ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhante, bhareyyaṃ taṃ purisaṃ, na ca me attho tādisena purisenā’’ti. ‘‘Atha āgaccheyya brāhmaṇakumāro asikkhito…pe… atha āgaccheyya vessakumāro asikkhito…pe… atha āgaccheyya suddakumāro asikkhito…pe… na ca me attho tādisena purisenā’’ti.

135.At Savatthi. King Pasenadi of Kosala sat to one side and said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, where should a gift be given?" "Great king, a gift should be given where the mind is inclined." "But, venerable sir, where does a gift given bear great fruit?" "This is one thing, great king—where a gift should be given—and that is another—where a gift given bears great fruit. A gift given to a virtuous person bears great fruit, not so to an unvirtuous one. Now, great king, I will question you in return. Answer as you see fit. What do you think, great king? Suppose a battle is imminent, the army is arrayed. Then comes a young noble, untrained, unskilled, inexperienced, without practice, timid, trembling, frightened, fleeing. Would you hire that man, and would such a man be of use to you?" "No, venerable sir, I would not hire that man, nor would such a man be of use to me." "Then comes a young brahmin untrained... a young merchant untrained... a young laborer untrained... nor would such a man be of use to me."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, mahārāja, idha tyassa yuddhaṃ paccupaṭṭhitaṃ saṅgāmo samupabyūḷho. Atha āgaccheyya khattiyakumāro susikkhito katahattho katayoggo katūpāsano abhīru acchambhī anutrāsī apalāyī. Bhareyyāsi taṃ purisaṃ, attho ca te tādisena purisenā’’ti? ‘‘Bhareyyāhaṃ, bhante, taṃ purisaṃ, attho ca me tādisena purisenā’’ti. ‘‘Atha āgaccheyya brāhmaṇakumāro…pe… atha āgaccheyya vessakumāro…pe… atha āgaccheyya suddakumāro susikkhito katahattho katayoggo katūpāsano abhīru acchambhī anutrāsī apalāyī. Bhareyyāsi taṃ purisaṃ, attho ca te tādisena purisenā’’ti? ‘‘Bhareyyāhaṃ, bhante, taṃ purisaṃ, attho ca me tādisena purisenā’’ti.

"What do you think, great king? Suppose a battle were imminent, a war were arrayed. And then a noble youth were to arrive—well-trained, skilled in arms, adept, expert in combat, fearless, unperturbed, unterrified, not fleeing. Would you hire that man? And would such a man be of use to you?" "I would hire that man, venerable sir, and such a man would be of use to me." "Then suppose a brahmin youth were to arrive… Suppose a merchant youth were to arrive… Suppose a worker youth were to arrive—well-trained, skilled in arms, adept, expert in combat, fearless, unperturbed, unterrified, not fleeing. Would you hire that man? And such a man would be of use to you?" "I would hire that man, venerable sir, and such a man would be of use to me."

‘‘Evameva kho, mahārāja, yasmā kasmā cepi [yasmā cepi (sī. syā. kaṃ. ka.)] kulā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito hoti, so ca hoti pañcaṅgavippahīno pañcaṅgasamannāgato, tasmiṃ dinnaṃ mahapphalaṃ hoti. Katamāni pañcaṅgāni pahīnāni honti? Kāmacchando pahīno hoti, byāpādo pahīno hoti, thinamiddhaṃ pahīnaṃ hoti, uddhaccakukkuccaṃ pahīnaṃ hoti, vicikicchā pahīnā hoti. Imāni pañcaṅgāni pahīnāni honti. Katamehi pañcahaṅgehi samannāgato hoti? Asekkhena sīlakkhandhena samannāgato hoti, asekkhena samādhikkhandhena samannāgato hoti, asekkhena paññākkhandhena samannāgato hoti, asekkhena vimuttikkhandhena samannāgato hoti, asekkhena vimuttiñāṇadassanakkhandhena samannāgato hoti. Imehi pañcahaṅgehi samannāgato hoti. Iti pañcaṅgavippahīne pañcaṅgasamannāgate dinnaṃ mahapphala’’nti. Idamavoca bhagavā…pe… satthā –

"Just so, great king, when someone from any family goes forth from the household life into homelessness, and they have abandoned five factors and are endowed with five factors, what is given to them is of great fruit. What are the five factors they have abandoned? Sensual desire is abandoned, ill will is abandoned, sloth and torpor are abandoned, restlessness and remorse are abandoned, and doubt is abandoned. These are the five factors they have abandoned. With what five factors are they endowed? They are endowed with the consummate aggregate of moral discipline, the consummate aggregate of samādhi, the consummate aggregate of wisdom, the consummate aggregate of liberation, and the consummate aggregate of the knowledge and vision of liberation. These are the five factors they are endowed with. Thus, when one has abandoned five factors and is endowed with five factors, what is given is of great fruit." This is what the Blessed One said... the Teacher—

‘‘Issattaṃ [issatthaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ.)] balavīriyañca [balaviriyañca (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)], yasmiṃ vijjetha māṇave;Taṃ yuddhattho bhare rājā, nāsūraṃ jātipaccayā.

"In whom are found skill in archery, strength, and energy, a youth; such a one the king should enlist for war, not one deficient in courage because of birth."

‘‘Tatheva khantisoraccaṃ, dhammā yasmiṃ patiṭṭhitā;Ariyavuttiṃ medhāviṃ, hīnajaccampi pūjaye.

"Just so, patience and gentleness, in whom these qualities are established, the wise one of noble conduct, even though of low birth, should be honored."

‘‘Kāraye assame ramme, vāsayettha bahussute;Papañca vivane kayirā, dugge saṅkamanāni ca.

"Let one build delightful hermitages, and settle learned ascetics there. One should make watering places in the wilderness, and causeways in rugged terrain."

‘‘Annaṃ pānaṃ khādanīyaṃ, vatthasenāsanāni ca;Dadeyya ujubhūtesu, vippasannena cetasā.

"Food, drink, and edibles,
Clothing and lodgings, too;
One should give to the upright,
With a mind serene and clear."

‘‘Yathā hi megho thanayaṃ, vijjumālī satakkaku;Thalaṃ ninnañca pūreti, abhivassaṃ vasundharaṃ.

"Just as a thundering cloud, adorned with lightning, with a hundred peaks,
Fills the lowlands and the highlands, raining down upon the earth."

‘‘Tatheva saddho sutavā, abhisaṅkhacca bhojanaṃ;Vanibbake tappayati, annapānena paṇḍito.

Just so, the faithful, learned, wise one, having prepared food thoughtfully, delights the wandering ascetics with food and drink.

‘‘Āmodamāno pakireti, detha dethāti bhāsati;Taṃ hissa gajjitaṃ hoti, devasseva pavassato;Sā puññadhārā vipulā, dātāraṃ abhivassatī’’ti.

"Rejoicing, he scatters, saying, 'Give, give!'That is his roar, like the rain-god's downpour;That vast stream of merit drenches the giver."
