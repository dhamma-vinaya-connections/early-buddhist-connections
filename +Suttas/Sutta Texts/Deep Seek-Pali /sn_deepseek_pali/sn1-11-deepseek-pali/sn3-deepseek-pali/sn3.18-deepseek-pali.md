# SN 3.18 Kalyāṇamittasuttaṃ

## SN 3.18 The Discourse on Good Friendship

129. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho rājā pasenadi kosalo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idha mayhaṃ, bhante, rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, so ca kho kalyāṇamittassa kalyāṇasahāyassa kalyāṇasampavaṅkassa, no pāpamittassa no pāpasahāyassa no pāpasampavaṅkassā’’’ti.

129.At Sāvatthī. Having sat down to one side, King Pasenadi of Kosala said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, while I was alone in seclusion, this thought arose in my mind: ‘The Dhamma has been well expounded by the Blessed One, but it is for one who has good friends, good companions, good comrades, not for one who has bad friends, bad companions, bad comrades.’”

‘‘Evametaṃ, mahārāja, evametaṃ, mahārāja! Svākkhāto, mahārāja, mayā dhammo. So ca kho kalyāṇamittassa kalyāṇasahāyassa kalyāṇasampavaṅkassa, no pāpamittassa no pāpasahāyassa no pāpasampavaṅkassāti.

“So it is, great king, so it is! The Dhamma has been well expounded by me, great king. And it is for one who has good friends, good companions, good comrades, not for one who has bad friends, bad companions, bad comrades.”

‘‘Ekamidāhaṃ, mahārāja, samayaṃ sakkesu viharāmi nagarakaṃ nāma sakyānaṃ nigamo. Atha kho, mahārāja, ānando bhikkhu yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho, mahārāja, ānando bhikkhu maṃ etadavoca – ‘upaḍḍhamidaṃ, bhante, brahmacariyassa – yadidaṃ kalyāṇamittatā kalyāṇasahāyatā kalyāṇasampavaṅkatā’’’ti.

“Once, great king, I was dwelling among the Sakyans at a Sakyan town named Nagaraka. Then, great king, the Venerable Ānanda approached me; having approached, he paid homage to me and sat down to one side. Having sat down to one side, great king, the Venerable Ānanda said to me: ‘Venerable sir, this is half of the holy life, that is, good friendship, good companionship, good comradeship.’”

‘‘Evaṃ vuttāhaṃ, mahārāja, ānandaṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘mā hevaṃ, ānanda, mā hevaṃ, ānanda! Sakalameva hidaṃ, ānanda, brahmacariyaṃ – yadidaṃ kalyāṇamittatā kalyāṇasahāyatā kalyāṇasampavaṅkatā. Kalyāṇamittassetaṃ, ānanda, bhikkhuno pāṭikaṅkhaṃ kalyāṇasahāyassa kalyāṇasampavaṅkassa ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvessati ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkarissati’’’.

“When this was said, great king, I said to the Venerable Ānanda: ‘Do not say so, Ānanda! Do not say so, Ānanda! This entire holy life, Ānanda, is good friendship, good companionship, good comradeship. For a bhikkhu who has good friends, good companions, good comrades, it is to be expected that he will develop the Noble Eightfold Path and make it abundant.’”

‘‘Kathañca, ānanda, bhikkhu kalyāṇamitto kalyāṇasahāyo kalyāṇasampavaṅko ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāveti, ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaroti? Idhānanda, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ, sammāsaṅkappaṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāvācaṃ bhāveti…pe… sammākammantaṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāājīvaṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāvāyāmaṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāsatiṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ. Evaṃ kho, ānanda, bhikkhu kalyāṇamitto kalyāṇasahāyo kalyāṇasampavaṅko ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāveti, ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaroti. Tadamināpetaṃ, ānanda, pariyāyena veditabbaṃ yathā sakalamevidaṃ brahmacariyaṃ – yadidaṃ kalyāṇamittatā kalyāṇasahāyatā kalyāṇasampavaṅkatā’’ti.

“And how, Ānanda, does a bhikkhu who has good friends, good companions, good comrades develop the ![[Noble Path]], which is based on seclusion, dispassion, and cessation, and culminates in relinquishment. Thus, Ānanda, a bhikkhu who has good friends, good companions, good comrades develops the Noble Eightfold Path and makes it abundant. In this way, Ānanda, it should be understood that this entire holy life is good friendship, good companionship, good comradeship.”

‘‘Mamañhi, ānanda, kalyāṇamittaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti, jarādhammā sattā jarāya parimuccanti, byādhidhammā sattā byādhito parimuccanti, maraṇadhammā sattā maraṇena parimuccanti, sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccanti. Iminā kho etaṃ, ānanda, pariyāyena veditabbaṃ yathā sakalamevidaṃ brahmacariyaṃ – yadidaṃ kalyāṇamittatā kalyāṇasahāyatā kalyāṇasampavaṅkatā’’ti.

“For, Ānanda, by relying on me as a good friend, beings subject to birth are liberated from birth; beings subject to aging are liberated from aging; beings subject to illness are liberated from illness; beings subject to death are liberated from death; beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and despair are liberated from sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and despair. In this way, Ānanda, it should be understood that this entire holy life is good friendship, good companionship, good comradeship.”

‘‘Tasmātiha te, mahārāja, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘kalyāṇamitto bhavissāmi kalyāṇasahāyo kalyāṇasampavaṅko’ti. Evañhi te, mahārāja, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Therefore, great king, you should train yourself in this way: ‘I will be one who has good friends, good companions, good comrades.’ That is how you should train yourself, great king.

‘‘Kalyāṇamittassa te, mahārāja, kalyāṇasahāyassa kalyāṇasampavaṅkassa ayaṃ eko dhammo upanissāya vihātabbo – appamādo kusalesu dhammesu.

For you, great king, who has good friends, good companions, good comrades, this one quality should be resorted to and dwelt upon: heedfulness in wholesome qualities.

‘‘Appamattassa te, mahārāja, viharato appamādaṃ upanissāya, itthāgārassa anuyantassa evaṃ bhavissati – ‘rājā kho appamatto viharati, appamādaṃ upanissāya. Handa, mayampi appamattā viharāma, appamādaṃ upanissāyā’’’ti.

When you, great king, dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness, it will occur to your harem: ‘The king dwells heedfully, resorting to heedfulness. Come, let us also dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness.’

‘‘Appamattassa te, mahārāja, viharato appamādaṃ upanissāya, khattiyānampi anuyantānaṃ evaṃ bhavissati – ‘rājā kho appamatto viharati appamādaṃ upanissāya. Handa, mayampi appamattā viharāma, appamādaṃ upanissāyā’’’ti.

When you, great king, dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness, even your Khattiya followers will think: ‘The king dwells heedfully, resorting to heedfulness. Come, let us also dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness.’

‘‘Appamattassa te, mahārāja, viharato appamādaṃ upanissāya, balakāyassapi evaṃ bhavissati – ‘rājā kho appamatto viharati appamādaṃ upanissāya. Handa, mayampi appamattā viharāma, appamādaṃ upanissāyā’’’ti.

When you, great king, dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness, your army will also think: ‘The king dwells heedfully, resorting to heedfulness. Come, let us also dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness.’

‘‘Appamattassa te, mahārāja, viharato appamādaṃ upanissāya, negamajānapadassapi evaṃ bhavissati – ‘rājā kho appamatto viharati, appamādaṃ upanissāya. Handa, mayampi appamattā viharāma, appamādaṃ upanissāyā’’’ti?

When you, great king, dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness, your townsfolk and countryfolk will also think: ‘The king dwells heedfully, resorting to heedfulness. Come, let us also dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness.’

‘‘Appamattassa te, mahārāja, viharato appamādaṃ upanissāya, attāpi gutto rakkhito bhavissati – itthāgārampi guttaṃ rakkhitaṃ bhavissati, kosakoṭṭhāgārampi guttaṃ rakkhitaṃ bhavissatī’’ti. Idamavoca…pe…

When you, great king, dwell heedfully, resorting to heedfulness, you yourself will be guarded and protected—your harem will be guarded and protected, and your treasury and storehouse will be guarded and protected.” The Blessed One then spoke these verses:

‘‘Bhoge patthayamānena, uḷāre aparāpare;Appamādaṃ pasaṃsanti, puññakiriyāsu paṇḍitā.

“The wise praise heedfulness in deeds of merit, for one who desires abundant, successive enjoyments.

‘‘Appamatto ubho atthe, adhiggaṇhāti paṇḍito;Diṭṭhe dhamme ca yo attho, yo cattho samparāyiko;Atthābhisamayā dhīro, paṇḍitoti pavuccatī’’ti.

The heedful wise one grasps both kinds of welfare: welfare in this present life and welfare in the life to come. The steadfast one, by the attainment of welfare, is called a wise one.”
