# SN 1.37 Samayasuttaṃ

## SN 1.37 The Meeting Discourse

37. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ mahāvane mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi sabbeheva arahantehi; dasahi ca lokadhātūhi devatā yebhuyyena sannipatitā honti bhagavantaṃ dassanāya bhikkhusaṅghañca. Atha kho catunnaṃ suddhāvāsakāyikānaṃ devatānaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ mahāvane mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi sabbeheva arahantehi; dasahi ca lokadhātūhi devatā yebhuyyena sannipatitā honti bhagavantaṃ dassanāya bhikkhusaṅghañca. Yaṃnūna mayampi yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato santike paccekaṃ gāthaṃ [paccekagāthaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] bhāseyyāmā’’ti.

37.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Great Wood, together with a great assembly of bhikkhus, five hundred bhikkhus, all of them arahants; and deities from ten world-systems had mostly assembled to see the Blessed One and the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Then it occurred to four deities of the Pure Abodes: "This Blessed One is dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Great Wood, together with a great assembly of bhikkhus, five hundred bhikkhus, all of them arahants; and deities from ten world-systems have mostly assembled to see the Blessed One and the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Suppose we were to approach the Blessed One, and having approached, were each to recite a verse in his presence."

Atha kho tā devatā – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya. Evameva – suddhāvāsesu devesu antarahitā bhagavato purato pāturahesuṃ. Atha kho tā devatā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho ekā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

Then those deities—just as a strong man might extend his drawn-in arm or draw in his extended arm—so they vanished from the Pure Abodes and appeared before the Blessed One. Then those deities, having paid homage to the Blessed One, stood to one side. Standing to one side, one deity recited this verse in the presence of the Blessed One:

‘‘Mahāsamayo pavanasmiṃ, devakāyā samāgatā;Āgatamha imaṃ dhammasamayaṃ, dakkhitāye aparājitasaṅgha’’nti.

"A great assembly is in the wood, where deities have gathered. We have come to this assembly of the Dhamma, to see the Saṅgha that is unconquered."

Atha kho aparā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

Then another deity recited this verse in the Blessed One's presence:

‘‘Tatra bhikkhavo samādahaṃsu, cittamattano ujukaṃ akaṃsu [ujukamakaṃsu (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)];Sārathīva nettāni gahetvā, indriyāni rakkhanti paṇḍitā’’ti.

"There, the bhikkhus collected, made their minds upright; Like charioteers holding the reins, the wise protect their faculties."

Atha kho aparā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

Then another deity recited this verse in the Blessed One's presence:

‘‘Chetvā khīlaṃ chetvā palighaṃ, indakhīlaṃ ūhacca manejā;Te caranti suddhā vimalā, cakkhumatā sudantā susunāgā’’ti.

"Having cut off the stake, having cut off the bar, having uprooted the pillar, devoid of craving, they wander, pure, stainless, with vision, well-tamed, supreme elephants."

Atha kho aparā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

Then another deity recited this verse in the Blessed One's presence:

‘‘Ye keci buddhaṃ saraṇaṃ gatāse, na te gamissanti apāyabhūmiṃ;Pahāya mānusaṃ dehaṃ, devakāyaṃ paripūressantī’’ti.

"All those who have gone to the Buddha for refuge will not go to the plane of misery; Having abandoned the human body, they will fill the ranks of the devas."
