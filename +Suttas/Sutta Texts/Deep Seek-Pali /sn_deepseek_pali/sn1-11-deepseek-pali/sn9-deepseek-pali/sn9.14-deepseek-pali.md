# SN 9.14 Gandhatthenasuttaṃ

## SN 9.14 The Discourse on the Scent Thief

234. Ekaṃ samayaṃ aññataro bhikkhu kosalesu viharati aññatarasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe. Tena kho pana samayena so bhikkhu pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto pokkharaṇiṃ ogāhetvā padumaṃ upasiṅghati. Atha kho yā tasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe adhivatthā devatā tassa bhikkhuno anukampikā atthakāmā taṃ bhikkhuṃ saṃvejetukāmā yena so bhikkhu tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ bhikkhuṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

234.At one time a certain bhikkhu was dwelling in the Kosala country in a certain woodland grove. Now at that time, after the meal, on returning from the alms round, that bhikkhu entered a lotus pond and sniffed at a lotus. Then the deity who dwelt in that woodland grove, out of compassion and wishing for his welfare, desiring to stir a sense of urgency in that bhikkhu, approached him and addressed him with this verse:

‘‘Yametaṃ vārijaṃ pupphaṃ, adinnaṃ upasiṅghasi;Ekaṅgametaṃ theyyānaṃ, gandhatthenosi mārisā’’ti.

"This water-born flower you sniff without permission—This is one aspect of theft, you scent-thief, sir!"

‘‘Na harāmi na bhañjāmi, ārā siṅghāmi vārijaṃ;Atha kena nu vaṇṇena, gandhatthenoti vuccati.

"I do not steal, I do not break, I smell the water-born lotus from afar; Then by what attribute am I called a scent-thief?"

‘‘Yvāyaṃ bhisāni khanati, puṇḍarīkāni bhañjati;Evaṃ ākiṇṇakammanto, kasmā eso na vuccatī’’ti.

"He who digs up the bulbs, who breaks the lotuses—Engaged in such harmful actions, why is he not spoken to?"

‘‘Ākiṇṇaluddo puriso, dhāticelaṃva makkhito;Tasmiṃ me vacanaṃ natthi, tvañcārahāmi vattave.

"A man defiled by greed, like a cloth smeared with dye. I have nothing to say to him, but you are worthy to be spoken to."

‘‘Anaṅgaṇassa posassa, niccaṃ sucigavesino;Vālaggamattaṃ pāpassa, abbhāmattaṃva khāyatī’’ti.

"To a person without defilements, ever seeking purity, A hair-tip of evil appears as vast as a cloud."

‘‘Addhā maṃ yakkha jānāsi, atho me anukampasi;Punapi yakkha vajjāsi, yadā passasi edisa’’nti.

"Surely, spirit, you know me, and have compassion for me. Speak to me again, spirit, whenever you see someone like this."

‘‘Neva taṃ upajīvāma, napi te bhatakāmhase;Tvameva bhikkhu jāneyya, yena gaccheyya suggati’’nti.

"We do not live depending on that, nor are we your hired servants. You yourself, bhikkhu, should know by what means one goes to a good destination."

Atha kho so bhikkhu tāya devatāya saṃvejito saṃvegamāpādīti.

Then that bhikkhu, stirred by that deity, attained a sense of urgency.

Vanasaṃyuttaṃ samattaṃ.

The Forest Saṃyutta is completed.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Vivekaṃ upaṭṭhānañca, kassapagottena sambahulā;Ānando anuruddho ca, nāgadattañca kulagharaṇī.

Seclusion and attendance, Kassapa of the Gotra and many others, Ānanda and Anuruddha, Nāgadatta and Kulagharaṇī.

Vajjiputto ca vesālī, sajjhāyena ayoniso;Majjhanhikālamhi pākatindriya, padumapupphena cuddasa bhaveti.

The Vajjian bhikkhus of Vesālī, through unwise recitation; At midday, with uncontrolled faculties; And the lotus flower—these are the fourteen.
