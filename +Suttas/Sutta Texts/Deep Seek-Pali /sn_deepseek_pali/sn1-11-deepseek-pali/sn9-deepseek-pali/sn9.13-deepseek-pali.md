# SN 9.13 Pākatindriyasuttaṃ

## SN 9.13 The Discourse on Unrestrained Faculties

233. Ekaṃ samayaṃ sambahulā bhikkhū kosalesu viharanti aññatarasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe uddhatā unnaḷā capalā mukharā vikiṇṇavācā muṭṭhassatino asampajānā asamāhitā vibbhantacittā pākatindriyā. Atha kho yā tasmiṃ vanasaṇḍe adhivatthā devatā tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ anukampikā atthakāmā te bhikkhū saṃvejetukāmā yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

233.At one time a number of bhikkhus were dwelling in the Kosala country in a certain woodland grove—restless, arrogant, fickle, talkative, indulging in scattered talk, unmindful, lacking clear comprehension, uncollected, with wandering minds, with uncontrolled faculties. Then the deity who dwelt in that woodland grove, out of compassion and wishing for their welfare, desiring to stir them to urgency, approached those bhikkhus and addressed them with these verses:

‘‘Sukhajīvino pure āsuṃ, bhikkhū gotamasāvakā;Anicchā piṇḍamesanā, anicchā sayanāsanaṃ;Loke aniccataṃ ñatvā, dukkhassantaṃ akaṃsu te.

"In the past, the bhikkhus, disciples of Gotama, lived at ease; Without desire, they sought alms, without desire for lodging. Having understood the impermanence of the world, they made an end to suffering."

‘‘Dupposaṃ katvā attānaṃ, gāme gāmaṇikā viya;Bhutvā bhutvā nipajjanti, parāgāresu mucchitā.

"Having made themselves hard to support, like village headmen in village after village, having eaten and eaten, they lie down, stupefied in others' houses."

‘‘Saṅghassa añjaliṃ katvā, idhekacce vadāmahaṃ;Apaviddhā [apaviṭṭhā (syā. kaṃ.)] anāthā te, yathā petā tatheva te.

"Having paid homage to the Saṅgha, some here I declare: They are outcasts, without refuge, just like departed spirits."

‘‘Ye kho pamattā viharanti, te me sandhāya bhāsitaṃ;Ye appamattā viharanti, namo tesaṃ karomaha’’nti.

"It is with those who dwell heedlessly in mind that I speak; To those who dwell diligently, I pay homage."

Atha kho te bhikkhū tāya devatāya saṃvejitā saṃvegamāpādunti.

Then those bhikkhus, stirred by that deity, attained a sense of urgency.
