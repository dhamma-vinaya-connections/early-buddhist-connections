# SN 46.51 Āhārasuttaṃ

## SN 46.51 The Discourse on Nutriments

232. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Pañcannañca, bhikkhave, nīvaraṇānaṃ sattannañca bojjhaṅgānaṃ āhārañca anāhārañca desessāmi; taṃ suṇātha. Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā kāmacchandassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā kāmacchandassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, subhanimittaṃ. Tattha ayonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā kāmacchandassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā kāmacchandassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

232.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, I will teach you the nutriment and the non-nutriment for the five hindrances and the seven factors of enlightenment. Listen to that. And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen sensual desire, and for the increase and abundance of arisen sensual desire? There is, monks, the attractive sign. Frequent unsystematic attention to it—this is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen sensual desire, and for the increase and abundance of arisen sensual desire.

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā byāpādassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā byāpādassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, paṭighanimittaṃ. Tattha ayonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā byāpādassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā byāpādassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen ill will, or for the increase and abundance of arisen ill will? There is, monks, the sign of aversion. Frequent unsystematic attention to it—this is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen ill will, or for the increase and abundance of arisen ill will.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā thinamiddhassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā thinamiddhassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, arati tandi vijambhitā bhattasammado cetaso ca līnattaṃ. Tattha ayonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā thinamiddhassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā thinamiddhassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen sloth and torpor, or for the increase and abundance of arisen sloth and torpor? There is, monks, discontent, languor, yawning, drowsiness after meals, and mental sinking. Frequent unsystematic attention to these—this is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen sloth and torpor, or for the increase and abundance of arisen sloth and torpor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, cetaso avūpasamo. Tattha ayonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen restlessness and remorse, or for the increase and abundance of arisen restlessness and remorse? There is, monks, non-quieting of the mind. Frequent unsystematic attention to this—this is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen restlessness and remorse, or for the increase and abundance of arisen restlessness and remorse.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannāya vā vicikicchāya uppādāya, uppannāya vā vicikicchāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, vicikicchāṭṭhānīyā dhammā. Tattha ayonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannāya vā vicikicchāya uppādāya, uppannāya vā vicikicchāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen doubt, or for the increase and abundance of arisen doubt? There are, monks, things that are grounds for doubt. Frequent unsystematic attention to these—this is the nutriment for the arising of unarisen doubt, or for the increase and abundance of arisen doubt.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, satisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhānīyā dhammā. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen mindfulness enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen mindfulness enlightenment factor? There are, monks, things that are supportive of the mindfulness enlightenment factor. Frequent wise attention to these—this is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen mindfulness enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen mindfulness enlightenment factor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, kusalākusalā dhammā sāvajjānavajjā dhammā hīnapaṇītā dhammā kaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāgā dhammā. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen investigation-of-states enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen investigation-of-states enlightenment factor? There are, monks, skillful and unskillful qualities, blameworthy and blameless qualities, inferior and superior qualities, dark and bright qualities with their counterparts. Frequent wise attention to these—this is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen investigation-of-states enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen investigation-of-states enlightenment factor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, ārambhadhātu nikkamadhātu parakkamadhātu. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen energy enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen energy enlightenment factor? There are, monks, the element of initiative, the element of persistence, and the element of exertion. Frequent wise attention to these—this is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen energy enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen energy enlightenment factor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, pītisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhānīyā dhammā. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen joy enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen joy enlightenment factor? There are, monks, things that are supportive of the joy enlightenment factor. Frequent wise attention to them—this is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen joy enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen joy enlightenment factor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, kāyappassaddhi cittappassaddhi. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen serenity enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen serenity enlightenment factor? There is, monks, serenity of body and serenity of mind. Frequent wise attention to these—this is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen serenity enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen serenity enlightenment factor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, samathanimittaṃ abyagganimittaṃ. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen samādhi enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen samādhi enlightenment factor? There is, monks, the sign of serenity, the sign of undistractedness. Frequent wise attention to these—this is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen samādhi enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen samādhi enlightenment factor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, āhāro anuppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, upekkhāsambojjhaṅgaṭṭhānīyā dhammā. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamāhāro anuppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

“And what, monks, is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen equanimity enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen equanimity enlightenment factor? There are, monks, things that are supportive of the equanimity enlightenment factor. Frequent wise attention to them—this is the nutriment for the arising of the unarisen equanimity enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen equanimity enlightenment factor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā kāmacchandassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā kāmacchandassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, asubhanimittaṃ. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā kāmacchandassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā kāmacchandassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen sensual desire, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen sensual desire? There is, monks, the sign of unattractiveness. Frequent wise attention to that—this is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen sensual desire, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen sensual desire.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā byāpādassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā byāpādassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, mettācetovimutti. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā byāpādassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā byāpādassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen ill will, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen ill will? There is, monks, the liberation of mind by loving-kindness. Frequent wise attention to that—this is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen ill will, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen ill will.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā thinamiddhassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā thinamiddhassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, ārambhadhātu nikkamadhātu parakkamadhātu. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā thinamiddhassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā thinamiddhassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen sloth and torpor, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen sloth and torpor? There are, monks, the element of initiative, the element of persistence, and the element of exertion. Frequent wise attention to these—this is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen sloth and torpor, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen sloth and torpor.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, cetaso vūpasamo. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā uddhaccakukkuccassa bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

“And what, monks, is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen restlessness and remorse, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen restlessness and remorse? There is, monks, the stilling of the mind. Frequent wise attention to that—this is the non-nutriment for the non-arising of unarisen restlessness and remorse, and for the non-increase and non-abundance of arisen restlessness and remorse.”

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannāya vā vicikicchāya uppādāya, uppannāya vā vicikicchāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya? Atthi, bhikkhave, kusalākusalā dhammā sāvajjānavajjā dhammā hīnapaṇītā dhammā kaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāgā dhammā. Tattha yonisomanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannāya vā vicikicchāya uppādāya, uppannāya vā vicikicchāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of unarisen doubt, or for the growth and abundance of arisen doubt? There are, monks, skillful and unskillful qualities, blameworthy and blameless qualities, inferior and superior qualities, dark and bright qualities with their counterparts. Frequent wise attention to them is the non-nourishment for the arising of unarisen doubt, or for the growth and abundance of arisen doubt."

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, satisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhānīyā dhammā. Tattha amanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā satisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen mindfulness enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen mindfulness enlightenment factor? There are, monks, qualities that are conducive to the mindfulness enlightenment factor. Frequent inattention to them is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen mindfulness enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen mindfulness enlightenment factor."

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, kusalākusalā dhammā sāvajjānavajjā dhammā hīnapaṇītā dhammā kaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāgā dhammā. Tattha amanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen investigation-of-qualities enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen investigation-of-qualities enlightenment factor? There are, monks, skillful and unskillful qualities, blameworthy and blameless qualities, inferior and superior qualities, dark and bright qualities with their counterparts. Frequent inattention to them is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen investigation-of-qualities enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen investigation-of-qualities enlightenment factor."

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, ārambhadhātu nikkamadhātu parakkamadhātu. Tattha amanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen energy enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen energy enlightenment factor? There are, monks, the elements of initiative, persistence, and exertion. Frequent inattention to these is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen energy enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen energy enlightenment factor."

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, pītisambojjhaṅgaṭṭhānīyā dhammā. Tattha amanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen rapture enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen rapture enlightenment factor? There are, monks, qualities that are conducive to the rapture enlightenment factor. Frequent inattention to them is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen rapture enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen rapture enlightenment factor."

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, kāyappassaddhi cittappassaddhi. Tattha amanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen tranquility enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen tranquility enlightenment factor? There are, monks, bodily tranquility and mental tranquility. Frequent inattention to them is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen tranquility enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen tranquility enlightenment factor."

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, samathanimittaṃ abyagganimittaṃ. Tattha amanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen samādhi enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen samādhi enlightenment factor? There is, monks, the sign of serenity and the sign of non-distraction. Frequent inattention to them is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen samādhi enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen samādhi enlightenment factor."

‘‘Ko ca, bhikkhave, anāhāro anuppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā? Atthi, bhikkhave, upekkhāsambojjhaṅgaṭṭhānīyā dhammā. Tattha amanasikārabahulīkāro – ayamanāhāro anuppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa uppādāya, uppannassa vā upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya pāripūriyā’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen equanimity enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen equanimity enlightenment factor? There are, monks, qualities that are conducive to the equanimity enlightenment factor. Frequent inattention to them is the non-nourishment for the arising of the unarisen equanimity enlightenment factor, or for the development and fulfillment of the arisen equanimity enlightenment factor."
