# SN 46.53 Aggisuttaṃ

## SN 46.53 The Fire Discourse

234. Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiyaṃ piṇḍāya pavisiṃsu. (Pariyāyasuttasadisaṃ).

234.Then, in the morning, several bhikkhus, having dressed, took their bowls and robes and entered Savatthi for alms. (Similar to the Pariyaya Sutta.)

‘‘Evaṃvādino, bhikkhave, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye līnaṃ cittaṃ hoti, katamesaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bojjhaṅgānaṃ akālo bhāvanāya, katamesaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bojjhaṅgānaṃ kālo bhāvanāya? Yasmiṃ panāvuso, samaye uddhataṃ cittaṃ hoti, katamesaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bojjhaṅgānaṃ akālo bhāvanāya, katamesaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bojjhaṅgānaṃ kālo bhāvanāyā’ti? Evaṃ puṭṭhā, bhikkhave, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā na ceva sampāyissanti, uttariñca vighātaṃ āpajjissanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Yathā taṃ, bhikkhave, avisayasmiṃ.

“When wanderers of other sects speak thus, bhikkhus, they should be addressed like this: ‘Friends, when the mind is sluggish, for which enlightenment factors is it the wrong time for development, and for which is it the right time for development? And again, friends, when the mind is agitated, for which enlightenment factors is it the wrong time for development, and for which is it the right time for development?’ Questioned thus, bhikkhus, wanderers of other sects will not be able to satisfy [the question], and furthermore, they will meet with frustration. Why is that? Because it is beyond their domain.”

‘‘Nāhaṃ taṃ, bhikkhave, passāmi sadevake loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya yo imesaṃ pañhānaṃ veyyākaraṇena cittaṃ ārādheyya, aññatra tathāgatena vā tathāgatasāvakena vā ito vā pana sutvā.

“Bhikkhus, I see no one in this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, in this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its princes and people, who could satisfy the mind with an explanation of these questions, except for the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata, or someone who has heard it from me.”

‘‘Yasmiṃ, bhikkhave, samaye līnaṃ cittaṃ hoti, akālo tasmiṃ samaye passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Līnaṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi dussamuṭṭhāpayaṃ hoti.

“Bhikkhus, when the mind is sluggish, that is not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of tranquility, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of samādhi, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of equanimity. Why is that? A sluggish mind is hard to rouse with those qualities.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, puriso parittaṃ aggiṃ ujjāletukāmo assa. So tattha allāni ceva tiṇāni pakkhipeyya, allāni ca gomayāni pakkhipeyya, allāni ca kaṭṭhāni pakkhipeyya, udakavātañca dadeyya, paṃsukena ca okireyya; bhabbo nu kho so puriso parittaṃ aggiṃ ujjālitu’’nti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suppose, bhikkhus, a man wanted to kindle a small fire. If he were to throw wet grass, wet cow dung, and wet sticks into it, and blow water on it, and scatter dust over it, could he kindle that small fire?” “No, venerable sir.”

‘‘Evameva kho, bhikkhave, yasmiṃ samaye līnaṃ cittaṃ hoti, akālo tasmiṃ samaye passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Līnaṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi dussamuṭṭhāpayaṃ hoti.

“In the same way, bhikkhus, when the mind is sluggish, that is not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of tranquility, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of samādhi, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of equanimity. Why is that? Because a sluggish mind is not easily roused by those qualities.”

‘‘Yasmiñca kho, bhikkhave, samaye līnaṃ cittaṃ hoti, kālo tasmiṃ samaye dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Līnaṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi susamuṭṭhāpayaṃ hoti.

“But, bhikkhus, when the mind is sluggish, that is the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of investigation of phenomena, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of energy, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of joy. Why is that? A sluggish mind is easily roused by those qualities.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, puriso parittaṃ aggiṃ ujjāletukāmo assa. So tattha sukkhāni ceva tiṇāni pakkhipeyya, sukkhāni gomayāni pakkhipeyya, sukkhāni kaṭṭhāni pakkhipeyya, mukhavātañca dadeyya, na ca paṃsukena okireyya; bhabbo nu kho so puriso parittaṃ aggiṃ ujjālitu’’nti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suppose, bhikkhus, a man wanted to kindle a small fire. If he were to throw dry grass, dry cow dung, and dry sticks onto it, blow on it with his mouth, and not scatter dust over it, would he be able to kindle that small fire?” “Yes, venerable sir.”

‘‘Evameva kho, bhikkhave, yasmiṃ samaye līnaṃ cittaṃ hoti, kālo tasmiṃ samaye dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Līnaṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi susamuṭṭhāpayaṃ hoti.

“In the same way, bhikkhus, when the mind is sluggish, at that time it is the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of investigation of phenomena, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of energy, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of joy. Why is that? Because a sluggish mind is easily roused by those qualities.”

‘‘Yasmiṃ, bhikkhave, samaye uddhattaṃ cittaṃ hoti, akālo tasmiṃ samaye dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Uddhataṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi duvūpasamayaṃ hoti.

“Bhikkhus, when the mind is agitated, that is not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of investigation of phenomena, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of energy, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of joy. Why is that? An agitated mind is not easily calmed by those qualities.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, puriso mahantaṃ aggikkhandhaṃ nibbāpetukāmo assa. So tattha sukkhāni ceva tiṇāni pakkhipeyya, sukkhāni ca gomayāni pakkhipeyya, sukkhāni ca kaṭṭhāni pakkhipeyya, mukhavātañca dadeyya, na ca paṃsukena okireyya; bhabbo nu kho so puriso mahantaṃ aggikkhandhaṃ nibbāpetu’’nti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suppose, bhikkhus, a man wanted to extinguish a large fire. If he were to throw dry grass, dry cow dung, and dry sticks onto it, blow on it with his mouth, and not scatter dust over it, would that man be able to extinguish that large fire?” “No, venerable sir.”

‘‘Evameva kho, bhikkhave, yasmiṃ samaye uddhataṃ cittaṃ hoti, akālo tasmiṃ samaye dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo vīriyasambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, akālo pītisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Uddhataṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi duvūpasamayaṃ hoti.

“Just so, bhikkhus, when the mind is agitated, at that time it is not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of investigation of phenomena, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of energy, not the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of joy. Why is that? An agitated mind, bhikkhus, is not easily calmed by these qualities.”

‘‘Yasmiñca kho, bhikkhave, samaye uddhataṃ cittaṃ hoti, kālo tasmiṃ samaye passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Uddhataṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi suvūpasamayaṃ hoti.

“But when the mind is agitated, bhikkhus, that is the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of tranquility, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of samādhi, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of equanimity. Why is that? Because an agitated mind, bhikkhus, is easily calmed by those qualities.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, puriso mahantaṃ aggikkhandhaṃ nibbāpetukāmo assa. So tattha allāni ceva tiṇāni pakkhipeyya, allāni ca gomayāni pakkhipeyya, allāni ca kaṭṭhāni pakkhipeyya, udakavātañca dadeyya, paṃsukena ca okireyya; bhabbo nu kho so puriso mahantaṃ aggikkhandhaṃ nibbāpetu’’nti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suppose, bhikkhus, a man wanted to extinguish a great heap of fire. He would throw wet grass into it, wet cow dung, and wet sticks; he would blow water on it and scatter dust upon it. Would that man be able to extinguish that great heap of fire?” “Yes, venerable sir.”

‘‘Evameva kho, bhikkhave, yasmiṃ samaye uddhataṃ cittaṃ hoti, kālo tasmiṃ samaye passaddhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo samādhisambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya, kālo upekkhāsambojjhaṅgassa bhāvanāya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Uddhataṃ, bhikkhave, cittaṃ taṃ etehi dhammehi suvūpasamayaṃ hoti. Satiñca khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sabbatthikaṃ vadāmī’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

“Just so, bhikkhus, when the mind is agitated, at that time it is the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of tranquility, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of samādhi, the time to cultivate the enlightenment factor of equanimity. Why is that? An agitated mind, bhikkhus, is easily calmed by these qualities. And I say, bhikkhus, that mindfulness is useful everywhere.” Third.
