# SN 46.3 Sīlasuttaṃ

## SN 46.3 The Discourse on Virtue

184. ‘‘Ye te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū sīlasampannā samādhisampannā ñāṇasampannā vimuttisampannā vimuttiñāṇadassanasampannā, dassanampāhaṃ, bhikkhave, tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bahukāraṃ [bahūpakāraṃ (syā.)] vadāmi; savanampāhaṃ, bhikkhave, tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bahukāraṃ vadāmi; upasaṅkamanampāhaṃ, bhikkhave, tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bahukāraṃ vadāmi; payirupāsanampāhaṃ, bhikkhave, tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bahukāraṃ vadāmi; anussatimpāhaṃ, bhikkhave, tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bahukāraṃ vadāmi; anupabbajjampāhaṃ, bhikkhave, tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bahukāraṃ vadāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathārūpānaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhūnaṃ dhammaṃ sutvā dvayena vūpakāsena vūpakaṭṭho [dvayena vūpakaṭṭho (sī. syā.)] viharati – kāyavūpakāsena ca cittavūpakāsena ca. So tathā vūpakaṭṭho viharanto taṃ dhammaṃ anussarati anuvitakketi.

184.Bhikkhus, those bhikkhus who are endowed with virtue, endowed with samādhi, endowed with wisdom, endowed with liberation, and endowed with the knowledge and vision of liberation—I declare, bhikkhus, that seeing such bhikkhus is of great benefit; hearing such bhikkhus is of great benefit; approaching such bhikkhus is of great benefit; attending upon such bhikkhus is of great benefit; recollecting such bhikkhus is of great benefit; and following such bhikkhus into homelessness is of great benefit. For what reason? Bhikkhus, having heard the Dhamma from such bhikkhus, one dwells secluded by two kinds of seclusion: physical seclusion and mental seclusion. Dwelling thus secluded, one recollects that Dhamma and thinks it over.

‘‘Yasmiṃ samaye, bhikkhave, bhikkhu tathā vūpakaṭṭho viharanto taṃ dhammaṃ anussarati anuvitakketi, satisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno āraddho hoti; satisambojjhaṅgaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu bhāveti; satisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchati. So tathā sato viharanto taṃ dhammaṃ paññāya pavicinati pavicarati parivīmaṃsamāpajjati.

On whatever occasion, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu dwells thus secluded, recollecting that Dhamma and thinking it over, on that occasion the mindfulness enlightenment factor is aroused in the bhikkhu; on that occasion the bhikkhu develops the mindfulness enlightenment factor; and on that occasion the mindfulness enlightenment factor comes to fulfillment in the bhikkhu. Dwelling thus mindful, he investigates, examines, and scrutinizes that Dhamma with wisdom.

‘‘Yasmiṃ samaye, bhikkhave, bhikkhu tathā sato viharanto taṃ dhammaṃ paññāya pavicinati pavicarati parivīmaṃsamāpajjati, dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno āraddho hoti; dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu bhāveti; dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchati. Tassa taṃ dhammaṃ paññāya pavicinato pavicarato parivīmaṃsamāpajjato āraddhaṃ hoti vīriyaṃ asallīnaṃ.

On whatever occasion, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu dwells thus mindful and investigates, examines, and scrutinizes that Dhamma with wisdom, on that occasion the investigation-of-states enlightenment factor is aroused in the bhikkhu; on that occasion the bhikkhu develops the investigation-of-states enlightenment factor; and on that occasion the investigation-of-states enlightenment factor comes to fulfillment in the bhikkhu. As he investigates, examines, and scrutinizes that Dhamma with wisdom, his energy is aroused, unsluggish.

‘‘Yasmiṃ samaye, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno taṃ dhammaṃ paññāya pavicinato pavicarato parivīmaṃsamāpajjato āraddhaṃ hoti vīriyaṃ asallīnaṃ, vīriyasambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno āraddho hoti; vīriyasambojjhaṅgaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu bhāveti; vīriyasambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchati. Āraddhavīriyassa uppajjati pīti nirāmisā.

On whatever occasion, bhikkhus, when unworldly joy arises in the bhikkhu whose energy is aroused, on that occasion the joy enlightenment factor is aroused in the bhikkhu; on that occasion the bhikkhu develops the joy enlightenment factor; and on that occasion the joy enlightenment factor comes to fulfillment in the bhikkhu. In one whose energy is aroused, unworldly joy arises.

‘‘Yasmiṃ samaye, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno āraddhavīriyassa uppajjati pīti nirāmisā, pītisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno āraddho hoti; pītisambojjhaṅgaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu bhāveti; pītisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchati. Pītimanassa kāyopi passambhati, cittampi passambhati.

On whatever occasion, bhikkhus, when the bhikkhu, whose mind is joyful, has his body and mind tranquilized, on that occasion the joy enlightenment factor is aroused in the bhikkhu; on that occasion the bhikkhu develops the joy enlightenment factor; and on that occasion the joy enlightenment factor comes to fulfillment in the bhikkhu. For one whose mind is joyful, the body also becomes tranquil, and the mind becomes tranquil.

‘‘Yasmiṃ samaye, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno pītimanassa kāyopi passambhati cittampi passambhati, passaddhisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno āraddho hoti; passaddhisambojjhaṅgaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu bhāveti; passaddhisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchati. Passaddhakāyassa sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati.

On whatever occasion, bhikkhus, when the bhikkhu, whose mind is joyful, has his body and mind tranquilized, on that occasion the tranquility enlightenment factor is aroused in the bhikkhu; on that occasion the bhikkhu develops the tranquility enlightenment factor; and on that occasion the tranquility enlightenment factor comes to fulfillment in the bhikkhu. For one whose body is tranquil and happy, the mind becomes collected.

‘‘Yasmiṃ samaye, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno passaddhakāyassa sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati, samādhisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno āraddho hoti; samādhisambojjhaṅgaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu bhāveti; samādhisambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchati. So tathāsamāhitaṃ cittaṃ sādhukaṃ ajjhupekkhitā hoti.

On whatever occasion, bhikkhus, when the mind of the bhikkhu whose body is tranquil and happy becomes collected, on that occasion the samādhi enlightenment factor is aroused in the bhikkhu; on that occasion the bhikkhu develops the samādhi enlightenment factor; and on that occasion the samādhi enlightenment factor comes to fulfillment in the bhikkhu. He regards with equanimity the mind thus collected.

‘‘Yasmiṃ samaye, bhikkhave, bhikkhu tathāsamāhitaṃ cittaṃ sādhukaṃ ajjhupekkhitā hoti, upekkhāsambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno āraddho hoti; upekkhāsambojjhaṅgaṃ tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu bhāveti; upekkhāsambojjhaṅgo tasmiṃ samaye bhikkhuno bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchati.

On whatever occasion, bhikkhus, when the bhikkhu regards with equanimity the mind thus collected, on that occasion the equanimity enlightenment factor is aroused in the bhikkhu; on that occasion the bhikkhu develops the equanimity enlightenment factor; and on that occasion the equanimity enlightenment factor comes to fulfillment in the bhikkhu.

‘‘Evaṃ bhāvitesu kho, bhikkhave, sattasu sambojjhaṅgesu evaṃ bahulīkatesu satta phalā sattānisaṃsā pāṭikaṅkhā. Katame satta phalā sattānisaṃsā? Diṭṭheva dhamme paṭikacca aññaṃ ārādheti. No ce diṭṭheva dhamme paṭikacca aññaṃ ārādheti, atha maraṇakāle aññaṃ ārādheti. No ce diṭṭheva dhamme paṭikacca aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce maraṇakāle aññaṃ ārādheti, atha pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti. No ce diṭṭheva dhamme paṭikacca aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce maraṇakāle aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti, atha pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti. No ce diṭṭheva dhamme paṭikacca aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce maraṇakāle aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti, atha pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti. No ce diṭṭheva dhamme paṭikacca aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce maraṇakāle aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti, atha pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti. No ce diṭṭheva dhamme paṭikacca aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce maraṇakāle aññaṃ ārādheti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti, no ce pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti, atha pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā uddhaṃsoto hoti akaniṭṭhagāmī. Evaṃ bhāvitesu kho, bhikkhave, sattasu bojjhaṅgesu evaṃ bahulīkatesu ime satta phalā sattānisaṃsā pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

Bhikkhus, when the seven factors of enlightenment are thus developed and frequently cultivated, seven fruits and seven benefits may be expected. What are the seven fruits and seven benefits? He attains Nibbāna in this very life, immediately. If he does not attain Nibbāna in this very life, immediately, then he attains Nibbāna at the time of death. If he does not attain Nibbāna in this very life, immediately, and does not attain Nibbāna at the time of death, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is an antarāparinibbāyī (one who is reborn spontaneously and attains Nibbāna in the interval). If he does not attain Nibbāna in this very life, immediately, and does not attain Nibbāna at the time of death, and is not an antarāparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is an upahaccaparinibbāyī (one who is reborn spontaneously and attains Nibbāna upon landing). If he does not attain Nibbāna in this very life, immediately, and does not attain Nibbāna at the time of death, and is not an antarāparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, and is not an upahaccaparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is an asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī (one who is reborn spontaneously and attains Nibbāna without exertion). If he does not attain Nibbāna in this very life, immediately, and does not attain Nibbāna at the time of death, and is not an antarāparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, and is not an upahaccaparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, and is not an asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is a sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī (one who is reborn spontaneously and attains Nibbāna with exertion). If he does not attain Nibbāna in this very life, immediately, and does not attain Nibbāna at the time of death, and is not an antarāparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, and is not an upahaccaparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, and is not an asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, and is not a sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī with the destruction of the five lower fetters, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is one bound upstream, going to the Akaniṭṭha realm. Bhikkhus, when the seven factors of enlightenment are thus developed and frequently cultivated, these seven fruits and seven benefits may be expected.” The Third Discourse.
