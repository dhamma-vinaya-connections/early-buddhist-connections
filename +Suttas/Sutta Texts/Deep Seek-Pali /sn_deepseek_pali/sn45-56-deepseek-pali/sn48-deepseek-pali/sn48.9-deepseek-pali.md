# SN 48.9 Paṭhamavibhaṅgasuttaṃ

## SN 48.9 The First Analysis Discourse

479. ‘‘Pañcimāni, bhikkhave, indriyāni. Katamāni pañca? Saddhindriyaṃ…pe… paññindriyaṃ. Katamañca, bhikkhave, saddhindriyaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako saddho hoti, saddahati tathāgatassa bodhiṃ – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’ti – idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, saddhindriyaṃ.

479."Bhikkhus, there are these five faculties. Which five? The faculty of faith [...] the faculty of wisdom. And what, Bhikkhus, is the faculty of faith? Here, Bhikkhus, a noble disciple is endowed with faith, he places his faith in the enlightenment of the Tathāgata: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is an arahant, perfectly enlightened, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, enlightened, blessed.' This, Bhikkhus, is called the faculty of faith.

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, vīriyindriyaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako āraddhavīriyo viharati akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya, kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ upasampadāya, thāmavā daḷhaparakkamo anikkhittadhuro kusalesu dhammesu – idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, vīriyindriyaṃ.

And what, Bhikkhus, is the faculty of energy? Here, Bhikkhus, a noble disciple dwells with energy aroused for abandoning unwholesome qualities and acquiring wholesome qualities, firm, strong in effort, not shirking his duty in regard to wholesome qualities. This, Bhikkhus, is called the faculty of energy.

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, satindriyaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako satimā hoti paramena satinepakkena samannāgato cirakatampi cirabhāsitampi saritā anussaritā – idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, satindriyaṃ.

And what, Bhikkhus, is the faculty of mindfulness? Here, Bhikkhus, a noble disciple is mindful, possessing supreme mindfulness and discernment, one who remembers and recollects what was done long ago and spoken long ago. This, Bhikkhus, is called the faculty of mindfulness.

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, samādhindriyaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako vossaggārammaṇaṃ karitvā labhati samādhiṃ, labhati cittassa ekaggataṃ – idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, samādhindriyaṃ.

And what, Bhikkhus, is the faculty of samādhi? Here, Bhikkhus, a noble disciple, having made relinquishment the object, gains samādhi, gains one-pointedness of mind. This is called the faculty of samādhi.

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, paññindriyaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako paññavā hoti udayatthagāminiyā paññāya samannāgato ariyāya nibbedhikāya, sammā dukkhakkhayagāminiyā – idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, paññindriyaṃ. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, pañcindriyānī’’ti. Navamaṃ.

And what, Bhikkhus, is the faculty of wisdom? Here, Bhikkhus, a noble disciple is wise, possessing wisdom that discerns arising and passing away, which is noble and penetrative, leading to the complete destruction of suffering. This, Bhikkhus, is called the faculty of wisdom. These, Bhikkhus, are the five faculties. The ninth.
