# SN 48.49 Piṇḍolabhāradvājasuttaṃ

## SN 48.49 The Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja Sutta

519. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmatā piṇḍolabhāradvājena aññā byākatā hoti – ‘‘khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānāmī’’ti. Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ –

519.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambi, in Ghosita’s Park. Now at that time, the Venerable Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja had declared final knowledge: “Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming back to any state of existence.” Then a number of monks approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those monks said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Āyasmatā, bhante, piṇḍolabhāradvājena aññā byākatā – ‘khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāmīti. Kiṃ nu kho, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamānena āyasmatā piṇḍolabhāradvājena aññā byākatā – ‘khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāmī’’ti?

“Venerable sir, the Venerable Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja has declared final knowledge: ‘Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being.’ Venerable sir, seeing what purpose did the Venerable Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja declare final knowledge: ‘Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being’?”

‘‘Tiṇṇannaṃ kho, bhikkhave, indriyānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā piṇḍolabhāradvājena bhikkhunā aññā byākatā – ‘khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāmīti. Katamesaṃ tiṇṇannaṃ? Satindriyassa, samādhindriyassa, paññindriyassa – imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tiṇṇannaṃ indriyānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā piṇḍolabhāradvājena bhikkhunā aññā byākatā – ‘khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāmīti. Imāni ca, bhikkhave, tīṇindriyāni kimantāni? Khayantāni. Kissa khayantāni? Jātijarāmaraṇassa. ‘Jātijarāmaraṇaṃ khaya’nti kho, bhikkhave, sampassamānena piṇḍolabhāradvājena bhikkhunā aññā byākatā – ‘khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāmī’’ti. Navamaṃ.

“Monks, it is because three faculties were developed and cultivated that the monk Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja declared final knowledge: ‘Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming back to any state of existence.’ Which three? The faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of samādhi, and the faculty of wisdom. Monks, it is because these three faculties were developed and cultivated that the monk Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja declared final knowledge: ‘Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming back to any state of existence.’ And what is the end of these three faculties? Extinction. The extinction of what? The extinction of birth, aging, and death. Monks, it is by seeing ‘the extinction of birth, aging, and death’ that the monk Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja declared final knowledge: ‘Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming back to any state of existence.’” The ninth.
