# SN 48.50 Āpaṇasuttaṃ

## SN 48.50 The Āpaṇa Sutta

520. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā aṅgesu viharati āpaṇaṃ nāma aṅgānaṃ nigamo. Tatra kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘yo so, sāriputta, ariyasāvako tathāgate ekantagato [ekantigato (sī.)] abhippasanno, na so tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā kaṅkheyya vā vicikiccheyya vā’’ti?

520.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Aṅgas, at Āpaṇa, an Aṅgan town. There the Blessed One addressed the Venerable Sāriputta, saying: “Sāriputta, a noble disciple who has gone solely to the Tathāgata and has deep confidence—would such a one have doubt or uncertainty about the Tathāgata or about the Tathāgata’s teaching?”

‘‘Yo so, bhante, ariyasāvako tathāgate ekantagato abhippasanno, na so tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā kaṅkheyya vā vicikiccheyya vā. Saddhassa hi, bhante, ariyasāvakassa evaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ āraddhavīriyo viharissati – akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya, kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ upasampadāya, thāmavā daḷhaparakkamo anikkhittadhuro kusalesu dhammesu. Yaṃ hissa, bhante, vīriyaṃ tadassa vīriyindriyaṃ.

“Venerable sir, a noble disciple who has gone solely to the Tathāgata and has deep confidence would not have doubt or uncertainty about the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata’s teaching. For a faithful noble disciple, venerable sir, it is to be expected that they will dwell with aroused energy—for abandoning unwholesome qualities and acquiring wholesome qualities, strong, firm in effort, not shirking the burden of wholesome qualities. Whatever energy they have, venerable sir, that is their faculty of energy.”

‘‘Saddhassa hi, bhante, ariyasāvakassa āraddhavīriyassa etaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ satimā bhavissati, paramena satinepakkena samannāgato, cirakatampi cirabhāsitampi saritā anussaritā. Yā hissa, bhante, sati tadassa satindriyaṃ.

“For a faithful noble disciple, venerable sir, who has aroused energy, it is to be expected that they will be mindful, possessing supreme mindfulness and proficiency, remembering and recollecting what was done long ago and said long ago. Whatever mindfulness they have, venerable sir, that is their faculty of mindfulness.”

‘‘Saddhassa hi, bhante, ariyasāvakassa āraddhavīriyassa upaṭṭhitassatino etaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ vossaggārammaṇaṃ karitvā labhissati samādhiṃ, labhissati cittassa ekaggataṃ. Yo hissa, bhante, samādhi tadassa samādhindriyaṃ.

“For a faithful noble disciple, venerable sir, who has aroused energy and has established mindfulness, it is to be expected that, having made relinquishment the object, they will gain samādhi, they will gain unification of mind. Whatever samādhi they have, venerable sir, that is their faculty of samādhi.”

‘‘Saddhassa hi, bhante, ariyasāvakassa āraddhavīriyassa upaṭṭhitassatino samāhitacittassa etaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ evaṃ pajānissati – anamataggo kho saṃsāro. Pubbā koṭi na paññāyati avijjānīvaraṇānaṃ sattānaṃ taṇhāsaṃyojanānaṃ sandhāvataṃ saṃsarataṃ. Avijjāya tveva tamokāyassa asesavirāganirodho santametaṃ padaṃ paṇītametaṃ padaṃ, yadidaṃ – sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbānaṃ [nibbānanti (?)]. Yā hissa, bhante, paññā tadassa paññindriyaṃ.

“For a faithful noble disciple, venerable sir, who has aroused energy, has established mindfulness, and whose mind is collected, it is to be expected that they will understand thus: ‘Beginningless is this saṃsāra. No first point is discerned of beings roaming and wandering on, hindered by ignorance and fettered by craving. But the complete fading away and cessation of this mass of darkness, due to the fading away of ignorance, is this peaceful state, this sublime state, namely, the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all attachments, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna.’ Whatever wisdom they have, venerable sir, that is their faculty of wisdom.”

‘‘Saddho so [sa kho so (sī. syā. kaṃ.)], bhante, ariyasāvako evaṃ padahitvā padahitvā evaṃ saritvā saritvā evaṃ samādahitvā samādahitvā evaṃ pajānitvā pajānitvā evaṃ abhisaddahati – ‘ime kho te dhammā ye me pubbe sutavā ahesuṃ. Tenāhaṃ etarahi kāyena ca phusitvā viharāmi, paññāya ca ativijjha [paṭivijjha (sī. ka.) tadaṭṭhakathāsu pana ativijjhitvāti vaṇṇitaṃ] passāmī’ti. Yā hissa, bhante, saddhā tadassa saddhindriya’’nti.

“That noble disciple, venerable sir, possessing faith, thus repeatedly applying effort, thus repeatedly recollecting, thus repeatedly concentrating, thus repeatedly understanding, deeply believes: ‘These are the qualities that I formerly only heard of. But now I dwell having contacted them with the body and see them penetratingly with wisdom.’ Whatever faith they have, venerable sir, that is their faculty of faith.”

‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Yo so, sāriputta, ariyasāvako tathāgate ekantagato abhippasanno, na so tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā kaṅkheyya vā vicikiccheyya vā. Saddhassa hi, sāriputta, ariyasāvakassa etaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ āraddhavīriyo viharissati – akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya, kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ upasampadāya, thāmavā daḷhaparakkamo anikkhittadhuro kusalesu dhammesu. Yaṃ hissa, sāriputta, vīriyaṃ tadassa vīriyindriyaṃ.

“Good, good, Sāriputta! That noble disciple, Sāriputta, who has gone solely to the Tathāgata and has deep confidence would not have doubt or uncertainty about the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata’s teaching. For a faithful noble disciple, Sāriputta, it is to be expected that they will dwell with aroused energy—for abandoning unwholesome qualities and acquiring wholesome qualities, strong, firm in effort, not shirking the burden of wholesome qualities. Whatever energy they have, Sāriputta, that is their faculty of energy.”

‘‘Saddhassa hi, sāriputta, ariyasāvakassa āraddhavīriyassa etaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ satimā bhavissati, paramena satinepakkena samannāgato, cirakatampi cirabhāsitampi saritā anussaritā. Yā hissa, sāriputta, sati tadassa satindriyaṃ.

“For a faithful noble disciple, Sāriputta, who has aroused energy, it is to be expected that they will be mindful, possessing supreme mindfulness and proficiency, remembering and recollecting what was done long ago and said long ago. Whatever mindfulness they have, Sāriputta, that is their faculty of mindfulness.”

‘‘Saddhassa hi, sāriputta, ariyasāvakassa āraddhavīriyassa upaṭṭhitassatino etaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ vossaggārammaṇaṃ karitvā labhissati samādhiṃ, labhissati cittassa ekaggataṃ. Yo hissa, sāriputta, samādhi tadassa samādhindriyaṃ.

“For a faithful noble disciple, Sāriputta, who has aroused energy and has established mindfulness, it is to be expected that, having made relinquishment the object, they will gain samādhi, they will gain unification of mind. Whatever samādhi they have, Sāriputta, that is their faculty of samādhi.”

‘‘Saddhassa hi, sāriputta, ariyasāvakassa āraddhavīriyassa upaṭṭhitassatino samāhitacittassa etaṃ pāṭikaṅkhaṃ yaṃ evaṃ pajānissati – anamataggo kho saṃsāro. Pubbā koṭi na paññāyati avijjānīvaraṇānaṃ sattānaṃ taṇhāsaṃyojanānaṃ sandhāvataṃ saṃsarataṃ. Avijjāya tveva tamokāyassa asesavirāganirodho santametaṃ padaṃ paṇītametaṃ padaṃ, yadidaṃ – sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbānaṃ. Yā hissa, sāriputta, paññā tadassa paññindriyaṃ.

“For a faithful noble disciple, Sāriputta, who has aroused energy, has established mindfulness, and whose mind is collected, it is to be expected that they will understand thus: ‘Beginningless is this saṃsāra. No first point is discerned of beings roaming and wandering on, hindered by ignorance and fettered by craving. But the complete fading away and cessation of this mass of darkness, due to the fading away of ignorance, is this peaceful state, this sublime state, namely, the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all attachments, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna.’ Whatever wisdom they have, Sāriputta, that is their faculty of wisdom.”

‘‘Saddho so [sa kho so (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)], sāriputta, ariyasāvako evaṃ padahitvā padahitvā evaṃ saritvā saritvā evaṃ samādahitvā samādahitvā evaṃ pajānitvā pajānitvā evaṃ abhisaddahati – ‘ime kho te dhammā ye me pubbe sutavā ahesuṃ. Tenāhaṃ etarahi kāyena ca phusitvā viharāmi, paññāya ca ativijjha [paṭivijjha (ka. sī. ka.)] passāmī’ti. Yā hissa, sāriputta, saddhā tadassa saddhindriya’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

“That noble disciple, Sāriputta, possessing faith, thus repeatedly applying effort, thus repeatedly recollecting, thus repeatedly concentrating, thus repeatedly understanding, deeply believes: ‘These are the qualities that I formerly only heard of. But now I dwell having contacted them with the body and see them penetratingly with wisdom.’ Whatever faith they have, Sāriputta, that is their faculty of faith.” The tenth.

Jarāvaggo pañcamo.

The Fifth Chapter: Old Age.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Jarā uṇṇābho brāhmaṇo, sāketo pubbakoṭṭhako;Pubbārāme ca cattāri, piṇḍolo āpaṇena cāti [saddhena te dasāti (syā. kaṃ. ka.)].
