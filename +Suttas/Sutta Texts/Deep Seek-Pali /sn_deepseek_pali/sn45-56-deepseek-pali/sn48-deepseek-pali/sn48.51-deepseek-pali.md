# SN 48.51 Sālasuttaṃ

## SN 48.51 The Sāla Sutta

521. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosalesu viharati sālāya brāhmaṇagāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, ye keci tiracchānagatā pāṇā, sīho migarājā tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati, yadidaṃ – thāmena javena sūrena [sūriyena (sī. syā. kaṃ.)]; evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye keci bodhipakkhiyā dhammā, paññindriyaṃ tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati, yadidaṃ – bodhāya’’.

521.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Kosalans at Sālā, a brahmin village. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus, just as among all creatures that walk on their feet, the lion, the king of beasts, is declared the foremost—that is, in strength, speed, and courage—so too, bhikkhus, among all the qualities conducive to enlightenment, the faculty of wisdom is declared the foremost, that is, for awakening.”

‘‘Katame ca, bhikkhave, bodhipakkhiyā dhammā? Saddhindriyaṃ, bhikkhave, bodhipakkhiyo dhammo, taṃ bodhāya saṃvattati; vīriyindriyaṃ bodhipakkhiyo dhammo, taṃ bodhāya saṃvattati; satindriyaṃ bodhipakkhiyo dhammo, taṃ bodhāya saṃvattati; samādhindriyaṃ bodhipakkhiyo dhammo, taṃ bodhāya saṃvattati; paññindriyaṃ bodhipakkhiyo dhammo, taṃ bodhāya saṃvattati. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, ye keci tiracchānagatā pāṇā, sīho migarājā tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati, yadidaṃ – thāmena javena sūrena; evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye keci bodhipakkhiyā dhammā, paññindriyaṃ tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati, yadidaṃ – bodhāyā’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

“And what, bhikkhus, are the qualities conducive to enlightenment? The faculty of faith, bhikkhus, is a quality conducive to enlightenment; it conduces to awakening. The faculty of energy is a quality conducive to enlightenment; it conduces to awakening. The faculty of mindfulness is a quality conducive to enlightenment; it conduces to awakening. The faculty of samādhi is a quality conducive to enlightenment; it conduces to awakening. The faculty of wisdom is a quality conducive to enlightenment; it conduces to awakening. Just as among all creatures that walk on their feet, the lion, the king of beasts, is declared the foremost—that is, in strength, speed, and courage—so too, bhikkhus, among all the qualities conducive to enlightenment, the faculty of wisdom is declared the foremost, that is, for awakening.” This is the first.
