# SN 48.58 Sūkarakhatasuttaṃ

## SN 48.58 The Discourse on the Boar's Cave

528. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati gijjhakūṭe pabbate sūkarakhatāyaṃ. Tatra kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘kiṃ nu kho, sāriputta, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti [pavattetīti (sī.)]? ‘‘Anuttarañhi, bhante, yogakkhemaṃ sampassamāno khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Anuttarañhi, sāriputta, yogakkhemaṃ sampassamāno khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti.

528.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha on Vulture Peak, at the Boar's Cave. There the Blessed One addressed Venerable Sāriputta: "What reason, Sāriputta, does a monk whose taints are destroyed see, that he engages in the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's teaching?" "It is because he sees the unsurpassed security from bondage, venerable sir, that a monk whose taints are destroyed engages in the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's teaching." "Good, good, Sāriputta! It is indeed because he sees the unsurpassed security from bondage, Sāriputta, that a monk whose taints are destroyed engages in the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's teaching."

‘‘Katamo ca, sāriputta, anuttaro yogakkhemo yaṃ sampassamāno khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti? ‘‘Idha, bhante, khīṇāsavo bhikkhu saddhindriyaṃ bhāveti upasamagāmiṃ sambodhagāmiṃ, vīriyindriyaṃ bhāveti…pe… satindriyaṃ bhāveti…pe… samādhindriyaṃ bhāveti…pe… paññindriyaṃ bhāveti upasamagāmiṃ sambodhagāmiṃ. Ayaṃ kho, bhante, anuttaro yogakkhemo yaṃ sampassamāno khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Eso hi, sāriputta, anuttaro yogakkhemo yaṃ sampassamāno khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti.

"And what, Sāriputta, is the unsurpassed security from bondage, seeing which a monk whose taints are destroyed behaves with the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata’s teaching?" "Here, venerable sir, a monk whose taints are destroyed develops the faculty of faith, which leads to stilling, leads to enlightenment. He develops the faculty of energy... the faculty of mindfulness... the faculty of samādhi... the faculty of wisdom, which leads to stilling, leads to enlightenment. This, venerable sir, is the unsurpassed security from bondage, seeing which a monk whose taints are destroyed behaves with the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata’s teaching." "Good, good, Sāriputta! For this, Sāriputta, is indeed the unsurpassed security from bondage, seeing which a monk whose taints are destroyed behaves with the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata’s teaching."

‘‘Katamo ca, sāriputta, paramanipaccakāro yaṃ khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti? ‘‘Idha, bhante, khīṇāsavo bhikkhu satthari sagāravo viharati sappatisso [sappaṭisso (syā. kaṃ. ka.)], dhamme sagāravo viharati sappatisso, saṅghe sagāravo viharati sappatisso, sikkhāya sagāravo viharati sappatisso, samādhismiṃ sagāravo viharati sappatisso. Ayaṃ kho, bhante, paramanipaccakāro yaṃ khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, sāriputta! Eso hi, sāriputta, paramanipaccakāro yaṃ khīṇāsavo bhikkhu tathāgate vā tathāgatasāsane vā paramanipaccakāraṃ pavattamāno pavattatī’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

"And what, Sāriputta, is the highest reverence by which a monk whose taints are destroyed behaves with the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's teaching?" "Here, venerable sir, a monk whose taints are destroyed dwells with reverence and deference toward the Teacher, with reverence and deference toward the Dhamma, with reverence and deference toward the Saṅgha, with reverence and deference toward the training, and with reverence and deference toward samādhi. This, venerable sir, is the highest reverence by which a monk whose taints are destroyed behaves with the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's teaching." "Good, good, Sāriputta! This indeed is the highest reverence by which a monk whose taints are destroyed behaves with the highest reverence toward the Tathāgata or the Tathāgata's teaching." The eighth.
