499."Monks, there are these six faculties. Which six? The faculty of the eye, the faculty of the ear, the faculty of the nose, the faculty of the tongue, the faculty of the body, and the faculty of the mind. Monks, whatever recluses or brahmins do not understand as it actually is the origin, the cessation, the gratification, the danger, and the escape from these six faculties, they are not regarded by me as true recluses among recluses or true brahmins among brahmins, nor do these venerable ones, by realizing it for themselves with direct knowledge, here and now, enter upon and abide in the goal of recluseship or the goal of brahminhood. But, monks, whatever recluses or brahmins do understand as it actually is the origin, the cessation, the gratification, the danger, and the escape from these six faculties, they are regarded by me as true recluses among recluses and true brahmins among brahmins, and these venerable ones, by realizing it for themselves with direct knowledge, here and now, enter upon and abide in the goal of recluseship and the goal of brahminhood."
