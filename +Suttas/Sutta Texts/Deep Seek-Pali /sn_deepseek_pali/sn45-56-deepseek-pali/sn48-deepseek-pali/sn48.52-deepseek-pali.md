# SN 48.52 Mallikasuttaṃ

## SN 48.52 The Mallika Sutta

522. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā mallesu [mallakesu (sī. syā. kaṃ.), mallikesu (ka.)] viharati uruvelakappaṃ nāma mallānaṃ nigamo. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘yāvakīvañca, bhikkhave, ariyasāvakassa ariyañāṇaṃ na uppannaṃ hoti neva tāva catunnaṃ indriyānaṃ saṇṭhiti hoti, neva tāva catunnaṃ indriyānaṃ avaṭṭhiti hoti. Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, ariyasāvakassa ariyañāṇaṃ uppannaṃ hoti, atha catunnaṃ indriyānaṃ saṇṭhiti hoti, atha catunnaṃ indriyānaṃ avaṭṭhiti hoti’’.

522.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Mallas at Uruvelakappa, a town of the Mallas. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus, as long as noble wisdom has not arisen in a noble disciple, there is neither stability nor firmness of the four faculties. But when, bhikkhus, noble wisdom arises in a noble disciple, then there is stability of the four faculties, then there is firmness of the four faculties.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yāvakīvañca kūṭāgārassa kūṭaṃ na ussitaṃ hoti, neva tāva gopānasīnaṃ saṇṭhiti hoti, neva tāva gopānasīnaṃ avaṭṭhiti hoti. Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, kūṭāgārassa kūṭaṃ ussitaṃ hoti, atha gopānasīnaṃ saṇṭhiti hoti, atha gopānasīnaṃ avaṭṭhiti hoti. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, yāvakīvañca ariyasāvakassa ariyañāṇaṃ na uppannaṃ hoti, neva tāva catunnaṃ indriyānaṃ saṇṭhiti hoti, neva tāva catunnaṃ indriyānaṃ avaṭṭhiti hoti. Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, ariyasāvakassa ariyañāṇaṃ uppannaṃ hoti, atha catunnaṃ indriyānaṃ…pe… avaṭṭhiti hoti.

“Bhikkhus, just as as long as the gable of a peaked house has not been raised, there is neither stability nor firmness of the rafters. But when, bhikkhus, the gable of a peaked house has been raised, then there is stability of the rafters, then there is firmness of the rafters. So too, bhikkhus, as long as noble wisdom has not arisen in a noble disciple, there is neither stability nor firmness of the four faculties. But when, bhikkhus, noble wisdom arises in a noble disciple, then there is stability of the four faculties… then there is firmness of the four faculties.”

‘‘Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Saddhindriyassa, vīriyindriyassa, satindriyassa, samādhindriyassa. Paññavato, bhikkhave, ariyasāvakassa tadanvayā saddhā saṇṭhāti, tadanvayaṃ vīriyaṃ saṇṭhāti, tadanvayā sati saṇṭhāti, tadanvayo samādhi saṇṭhātī’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

“Which four? The faculty of faith, the faculty of energy, the faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of samādhi. In a wise noble disciple, bhikkhus, faith is established accordingly, energy is established accordingly, mindfulness is established accordingly, samādhi is established accordingly.” This is the second.
