# SN 48.70 Catuttharukkhasuttaṃ

## SN 48.70 The Fourth Tree Discourse

540. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, ye keci supaṇṇānaṃ rukkhā, kūṭasimbalī [koṭasimbali (syā. kaṃ.)] tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye keci bodhipakkhiyā dhammā, paññindriyaṃ tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati, yadidaṃ – bodhāya. Katame ca, bhikkhave, bodhipakkhiyā dhammā? Saddhindriyaṃ, bhikkhave, bodhipakkhiyo dhammo, taṃ bodhāya saṃvattati…pe… paññindriyaṃ bodhipakkhiyo dhammo, taṃ bodhāya saṃvattati. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, ye keci supaṇṇānaṃ rukkhā, kūṭasimbalī tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, ye keci bodhipakkhiyā dhammā, paññindriyaṃ tesaṃ aggamakkhāyati, yadidaṃ – bodhāyā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

540.“Just as, monks, among whatever trees there are of the supaṇṇas, the silk-cotton tree is declared the foremost; even so, monks, among whatever qualities conducive to enlightenment there are, the faculty of wisdom is declared the foremost, that is, for enlightenment. And what, monks, are the qualities conducive to enlightenment? The faculty of faith, monks, is a quality conducive to enlightenment; it conduces to enlightenment…the faculty of wisdom is a quality conducive to enlightenment; it conduces to enlightenment. Just as, monks, among whatever trees there are of the supaṇṇas, the silk-cotton tree is declared the foremost; even so, monks, among whatever qualities conducive to enlightenment there are, the faculty of wisdom is declared the foremost, that is, for enlightenment.” The tenth.

Bodhipakkhiyavaggo sattamo.

The Chapter on the Qualities Conducive to Enlightenment is the seventh.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The summary of it:

Saṃyojanā anusayā, pariññā āsavakkhayā;Dve phalā caturo rukkhā, vaggo tena pavuccatīti.

Fetters, latent tendencies; full understanding, destruction of taints; Two fruits, four trees—thus is the chapter called.

8. Gaṅgāpeyyālavaggo

8.The Chapter on the Ganges Repetition Section

## 1-12. Pācīnādisuttadvādasakaṃ

### 1–12. The Dozen Discourses Beginning with the Eastern Direction

541-552. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gaṅgā nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pañcindriyāni bhāvento pañcindriyāni bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pañcindriyāni bhāvento pañcindriyāni bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu saddhindriyaṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ, vīriyindriyaṃ…pe… satindriyaṃ… samādhindriyaṃ… paññindriyaṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pañcindriyāni bhāvento pañcindriyāni bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Dvādasamaṃ.

541–552. “Just as, bhikkhus, the river Ganges slopes, tends, and gravitates to the east, so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu who develops and cultivates the five faculties slopes, tends, and gravitates to Nibbāna. And how, bhikkhus, does a bhikkhu who develops and cultivates the five faculties slope, tend, and gravitate to Nibbāna? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops the faculty of faith, which is based upon seclusion, based upon dispassion, based upon cessation, culminating in relinquishment. He develops the faculty of energy… the faculty of mindfulness… the faculty of samādhi… the faculty of wisdom, which is based upon seclusion, based upon dispassion, based upon cessation, culminating in relinquishment. It is in this way, bhikkhus, that a bhikkhu who develops and cultivates the five faculties slopes, tends, and gravitates to Nibbāna.” The twelfth.

Gaṅgāpeyyālavaggo aṭṭhamo.

The Chapter on the Ganges Repetition Section is the eighth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Cha pācīnato ninnā, cha ninnā ca samuddato;Dvete cha dvādasa honti, vaggo tena pavuccatīti.

Six sloping to the east, six sloping to the ocean; These two sixes make twelve, thus it is called a chapter.

Appamādavaggo vitthāretabbo.

The Chapter on Heedfulness should be elaborated.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Tathāgataṃ padaṃ kūṭaṃ, mūlaṃ sārena vassikaṃ;Rājā candimasūriyā, vatthena dasamaṃ padanti.

The Tathāgata, the foot, the peak, the root, the heartwood, the jasmine; The king, the moon, the sun, the tenth word is ‘with the robe’.

Balakaraṇīyavaggo vitthāretabbo.

The Chapter on What is to be Done by Strength should be elaborated.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Balaṃ bījañca nāgo ca, rukkho kumbhena sūkiyā;Ākāsena ca dve meghā, nāvā āgantukā nadīti.

Strength, the seed, and the elephant, the tree, the pot, and the winnowing basket; The sky and the two clouds, the boat, the guest, and the river.

Esanāvaggo vitthāretabbo.

The Chapter on Searches should be elaborated.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Esanā vidhā āsavo, bhavo ca dukkhatā tisso;Khilaṃ malañca nīgho ca, vedanā taṇhā tasinā cāti.

Searches, modes, taints, existence, and the three kinds of suffering; Barrenness, defilement, affliction, feeling, craving, and thirst.

12. Oghavaggo

12.The Chapter on Floods

## 1-10. Oghādisuttadasakaṃ

### 1–10. The Decade Beginning with the Flood.

587-596. ‘‘Pañcimāni, bhikkhave, uddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Katamāni pañca? Rūparāgo, arūparāgo, māno, uddhaccaṃ, avijjā – imāni kho, bhikkhave, pañcuddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya pañcindriyāni bhāvetabbāni. Katamāni pañca? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu saddhindriyaṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ…pe… paññindriyaṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya imāni pañcindriyāni bhāvetabbānī’’ti. Dasamaṃ. (Yathā maggasaṃyuttaṃ, tathā vitthāretabbaṃ.)

587–596. “Bhikkhus, there are these five higher fetters. Which five? Lust for form, lust for the formless, conceit, restlessness, and ignorance. These, bhikkhus, are the five higher fetters. For the direct knowledge, full understanding, utter destruction, and abandoning of these five higher fetters, the five faculties should be developed. Which five? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops the faculty of faith, which is based upon seclusion, based upon dispassion, based upon cessation, culminating in relinquishment… He develops the faculty of wisdom, which is based upon seclusion, based upon dispassion, based upon cessation, culminating in relinquishment. For the direct knowledge, full understanding, utter destruction, and abandoning of these five higher fetters, these five faculties should be developed.” The tenth. (This should be elaborated as in the Connected Discourses on the Path.)

Oghavaggo dvādasamo.

The Chapter on Floods is the twelfth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Ogho yogo upādānaṃ, ganthā anusayena ca;Kāmaguṇā nīvaraṇā, khandhā oruddhambhāgiyāti.

Floods, yokes, clinging, bonds, and latent tendencies; Sensual pleasures, hindrances, aggregates, and lower fetters.

13. Gaṅgāpeyyālavaggo

13.The Chapter on the Ganges Repetition Section

## 1-12. Pācīnādisuttadvādasakaṃ

### 1–12. The Dozen Discourses Beginning with the Eastern Direction

597-608. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gaṅgā nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pañcindriyāni bhāvento pañcindriyāni bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pañcindriyāni bhāvento pañcindriyāni bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu saddhindriyaṃ bhāveti rāgavinayapariyosānaṃ dosavinayapariyosānaṃ mohavinayapariyosānaṃ…pe… paññindriyaṃ bhāveti rāgavinayapariyosānaṃ dosavinayapariyosānaṃ mohavinayapariyosānaṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pañcindriyāni bhāvento pañcindriyāni bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Dvādasamaṃ.

597–608. “Just as, bhikkhus, the river Ganges slopes, tends, and gravitates to the east, so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu who develops and cultivates the five faculties slopes, tends, and gravitates to Nibbāna. And how, bhikkhus, does a bhikkhu who develops and cultivates the five faculties slope, tend, and gravitate to Nibbāna? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops the faculty of faith, culminating in the removal of lust, the removal of hatred, the removal of delusion… He develops the faculty of wisdom, culminating in the removal of lust, the removal of hatred, the removal of delusion. It is in this way, bhikkhus, that a bhikkhu who develops and cultivates the five faculties slopes, tends, and gravitates to Nibbāna.” The twelfth.

Gaṅgāpeyyālavaggo terasamo.

The Chapter on the Ganges Repetition Section is the thirteenth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Cha pācīnato ninnā, cha ninnā ca samuddato;Dvete cha dvādasa honti, vaggo tena pavuccatīti.

Six sloping to the east, six sloping to the ocean; These two sixes make twelve, thus it is called a chapter.

Appamādavagga-balakaraṇīyavagga-esanāvaggā vitthāretabbā.

The Chapters on Heedfulness, What is to be Done by Strength, and Searches should be elaborated.

17. Oghavaggo

17.The Section on Floods.

## 1-10. Oghādisuttadasakaṃ

### 1–10. The Group of Ten Discourses Beginning with the Flood.

641-650. ‘‘Pañcimāni, bhikkhave, uddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Katamāni pañca? Rūparāgo, arūparāgo, māno, uddhaccaṃ, avijjā – imāni kho, bhikkhave, pañcuddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya pañcindriyāni bhāvetabbāni. Katamāni pañca? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu saddhindriyaṃ bhāveti rāgavinayapariyosānaṃ dosavinayapariyosānaṃ mohavinayapariyosānaṃ. Vīriyindriyaṃ …pe… satindriyaṃ… samādhindriyaṃ … paññindriyaṃ bhāveti rāgavinayapariyosānaṃ dosavinayapariyosānaṃ mohavinayapariyosānaṃ. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya imāni pañcindriyāni bhāvetabbānī’’ti.

641–650. “Monks, there are these five higher fetters. Which five? Craving for form, craving for the formless, conceit, restlessness, ignorance—these, monks, are the five higher fetters. For the direct knowledge, full understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these five higher fetters, the five faculties should be developed. Which five? Here, monks, a monk develops the faculty of faith, culminating in the fading away of lust, the fading away of hatred, the fading away of delusion. He develops the faculty of energy [...] the faculty of mindfulness [...] the faculty of samādhi [...] the faculty of wisdom, culminating in the fading away of lust, the fading away of hatred, the fading away of delusion. For the direct knowledge, full understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these five higher fetters, these five faculties should be developed.”

Oghavaggo sattarasamo.

The Seventeenth Section: The Floods.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Ogho yogo upādānaṃ, ganthā anusayena ca;Kāmaguṇā nīvaraṇā, khandhā oruddhambhāgiyāti.

Floods, yokes, clinging, bonds, and latent tendencies;The strands of sensual pleasure, hindrances, aggregates, and lower fetters.

Indriyasaṃyuttaṃ catutthaṃ.

The Connected Discourses on the Faculties, the Fourth.
