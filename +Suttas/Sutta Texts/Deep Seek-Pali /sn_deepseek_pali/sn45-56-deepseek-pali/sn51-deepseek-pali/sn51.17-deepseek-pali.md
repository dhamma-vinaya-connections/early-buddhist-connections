# SN 51.17 Dutiyasamaṇabrāhmaṇasuttaṃ

## SN 51.17 The Second Recluses and Brahmins Discourse

829. ‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, atītamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhosuṃ – ekopi hutvā bahudhā ahesuṃ, bahudhāpi hutvā eko ahesuṃ; āvibhāvaṃ, tirobhāvaṃ; tirokuṭṭaṃ tiropākāraṃ tiropabbataṃ asajjamānā agamaṃsu, seyyathāpi ākāse; pathaviyāpi ummujjanimujjaṃ akaṃsu, seyyathāpi udake; udakepi abhijjamāne [abhijjamānā (sī. pī. ka.)] agamaṃsu, seyyathāpi pathaviyaṃ; ākāsepi pallaṅkena kamiṃsu, seyyathāpi pakkhī sakuṇo; imepi candimasūriye evaṃmahiddhike evaṃmahānubhāve pāṇinā parimasiṃsu [parāmasiṃsu (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] parimajjiṃsu; yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vattesuṃ, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā.

829.“Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins in the past experienced various kinds of psychic power—being one, they became many; being many, they became one; they appeared and vanished; they went unhindered through walls, ramparts, and mountains as if through space; they emerged from and submerged into the earth as if it were water; they walked on water without breaking through as if it were earth; they traveled through the sky while sitting cross-legged like a winged bird; they even touched and stroked with their hand the moon and sun, so mighty and powerful; they wielded bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world—all these did so because they had developed and cultivated the four bases of psychic power.

‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, anāgatamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhossanti – ekopi hutvā bahudhā bhavissanti, bahudhāpi hutvā eko bhavissanti; āvibhāvaṃ, tirobhāvaṃ; tirokuṭṭaṃ tiropākāraṃ tiropabbataṃ asajjamānā gamissanti, seyyathāpi ākāse; pathaviyāpi ummujjanimujjaṃ karissanti, seyyathāpi udake; udakepi abhijjamāne gamissanti, seyyathāpi pathaviyaṃ; ākāsepi pallaṅkena kamissanti, seyyathāpi pakkhī sakuṇo; imepi candimasūriye evaṃmahiddhike evaṃmahānubhāve pāṇinā parimasissanti parimajjissanti; yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vattissanti, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā.

“Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins in the future will experience various kinds of psychic power—being one, they will become many; being many, they will become one; they will appear and vanish; they will go unhindered through walls, ramparts, and mountains as if through space; they will emerge from and submerge into the earth as if it were water; they will walk on water without breaking through as if it were earth; they will travel through the sky while sitting cross-legged like a winged bird; they will even touch and stroke with their hand the moon and sun, so mighty and powerful; they will wield bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world—all these will do so because they will have developed and cultivated the four bases of psychic power.”

‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, etarahi samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhonti – ekopi hutvā bahudhā honti, bahudhāpi hutvā eko honti; āvibhāvaṃ, tirobhāvaṃ; tirokuṭṭaṃ tiropākāraṃ tiropabbataṃ asajjamānā gacchanti, seyyathāpi ākāse; pathaviyāpi ummujjanimujjaṃ karonti, seyyathāpi udake; udakepi abhijjamāne gacchanti, seyyathāpi pathaviyaṃ; ākāsepi pallaṅkena kamanti, seyyathāpi pakkhī sakuṇo; imepi candimasūriye evaṃmahiddhike evaṃmahānubhāve pāṇinā parimasanti [parāmasanti (syā. kaṃ.)] parimajjanti; yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vattenti, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattāti.

“Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins at present experience various kinds of psychic power—being one, they become many; being many, they become one; they appear and vanish; they go unhindered through walls, ramparts, and mountains as if through space; they emerge from and submerge into the earth as if it were water; they walk on water without breaking through as if it were earth; they travel through the sky while sitting cross-legged like a winged bird; they even touch and stroke with their hand the moon and sun, so mighty and powerful; they wield bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world—all these do so because they have developed and cultivated the four bases of psychic power.”

‘‘Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi…pe… vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, atītamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhosuṃ – ekopi hutvā bahudhā ahesuṃ…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vattesuṃ, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā.

“Which four? Here, monks, a monk develops the basis of psychic power that possesses samādhi of desire, accompanied by the volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis of psychic power that possesses samādhi of energy, accompanied by the volitional formations of striving; he develops the basis of psychic power that possesses samādhi of mind, accompanied by the volitional formations of striving; he develops the basis of psychic power that possesses samādhi of investigation, accompanied by the volitional formations of striving. Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins in the past experienced various kinds of psychic power—being one, they became many; [...] they wielded bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world—all these did so because they had developed and cultivated these same four bases of psychic power.”

‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, anāgatamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhossanti – ekopi hutvā bahudhā bhavissanti…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vattissanti, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā.

“Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins in the future will experience various kinds of psychic power—being one, they will become many; [...] they will wield bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world—all of them will do so because they will have developed and cultivated these same four bases of psychic power.”

‘‘Ye hi keci bhikkhave etarahi samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhonti – ekopi hutvā bahudhā honti…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vattenti, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

“Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins at present experience various kinds of psychic power—being one, they become many; [...] they wield bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world—all these do so because they have developed and cultivated these same four bases of psychic power.” The seventh.
