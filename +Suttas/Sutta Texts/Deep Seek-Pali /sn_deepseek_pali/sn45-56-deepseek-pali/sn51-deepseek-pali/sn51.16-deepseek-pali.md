# SN 51.16 Paṭhamasamaṇabrāhmaṇasuttaṃ

## SN 51.16 The First Recluses and Brahmins Discourse

828. ‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, atītamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā mahiddhikā ahesuṃ mahānubhāvā, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, anāgatamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā mahiddhikā bhavissanti mahānubhāvā, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, etarahi samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā mahiddhikā mahānubhāvā, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā.

828.“Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins in the past were of great psychic power and influence, all of them were such because they had developed and cultivated the four bases of psychic power. Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins in the future will be of great psychic power and influence, all of them will be such because they will have developed and cultivated the four bases of psychic power. Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins at present are of great psychic power and influence, all of them are such because they have developed and cultivated the four bases of psychic power.”

‘‘Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi…pe… vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, atītamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā mahiddhikā ahesuṃ mahānubhāvā, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, anāgatamaddhānaṃ samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā mahiddhikā bhavissanti mahānubhāvā, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, etarahi samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā mahiddhikā mahānubhāvā, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“Which four? Here, monks, a monk develops the ![[Iddhipadas]] that possesses samādhi of energy, accompanied by the volitional formations of striving; he develops the ![[Iddhipadas]] that possesses samādhi of investigation, accompanied by the volitional formations of striving. Monks, whatever ascetics or brahmins in the past were of great psychic power and influence, all of them were such because they had developed and cultivated these same four bases of psychic power. Whatever ascetics or brahmins in the future will be of great psychic power and influence, all of them will be such because they will have developed and cultivated these same four bases of psychic power. Whatever ascetics or brahmins at present are of great psychic power and influence, all of them are such because they have developed and cultivated these same four bases of psychic power.” The sixth.
