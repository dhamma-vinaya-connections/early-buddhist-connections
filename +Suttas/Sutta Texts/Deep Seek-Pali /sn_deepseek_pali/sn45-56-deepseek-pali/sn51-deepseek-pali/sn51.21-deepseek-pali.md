# SN 51.21 Maggasuttaṃ

## SN 51.21 The Path Discourse

833. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Pubbeva me, bhikkhave, sambodhā anabhisambuddhassa bodhisattasseva sato etadahosi – ‘ko nu kho maggo, kā paṭipadā iddhipādabhāvanāyā’ti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – ‘idha bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti – iti me chando na ca atilīno bhavissati, na ca atippaggahito bhavissati, na ca ajjhattaṃ saṃkhitto bhavissati, na ca bahiddhā vikkhitto bhavissati. Pacchāpuresaññī ca viharati – yathā pure tathā pacchā, yathā pacchā tathā pure; yathā adho tathā uddhaṃ, yathā uddhaṃ tathā adho; yathā divā tathā rattiṃ, yathā rattiṃ tathā divā. Iti vivaṭena cetasā apariyonaddhena sappabhāsaṃ cittaṃ bhāveti. Vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi…pe… vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti – iti me vīmaṃsā na ca atilīnā bhavissati, na ca atippaggahitā bhavissati, na ca ajjhattaṃ saṃkhittā bhavissati, na ca bahiddhā vikkhittā bhavissati. Pacchāpuresaññī ca viharati – yathā pure tathā pacchā, yathā pacchā tathā pure; yathā adho tathā uddhaṃ, yathā uddhaṃ tathā adho; yathā divā tathā rattiṃ yathā rattiṃ tathā divā’ – iti vivaṭena cetasā apariyonaddhena sappabhāsaṃ cittaṃ bhāveti.

833.At Sāvatthī. “Before my full enlightenment, bhikkhus, while I was still an unawakened Bodhisatta, this thought occurred to me: ‘What now is the path, what is the way for the development of the bases of spiritual power?’ Then, bhikkhus, this thought occurred to me: ‘Here, a bhikkhu develops the basis of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to desire and volitional formations of striving, thinking: “My desire will not be overly sluggish, nor over-exerted, nor internally constricted, nor externally scattered.” And he dwells perceiving before and after: as before, so after; as after, so before; as below, so above; as above, so below; as by day, so by night; as by night, so by day. Thus, with a mind that is open, unconfined, and luminous, he develops a luminous mind. He develops the basis of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to energy… samādhi due to mind… He develops the basis of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to investigation and volitional formations of striving, thinking: “My investigation will not be overly sluggish, nor over-exerted, nor internally constricted, nor externally scattered.” And he dwells perceiving before and after: as before, so after; as after, so before; as below, so above; as above, so below; as by day, so by night; as by night, so by day’—thus, with a mind that is open, unconfined, and luminous, he develops a luminous mind.”

‘‘Evaṃ bhāvitesu kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu catūsu iddhipādesu evaṃ bahulīkatesu anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti – ekopi hutvā bahudhā hoti, bahudhāpi hutvā eko hoti…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti. Evaṃ bhāvitesu kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu catūsu iddhipādesu evaṃ bahulīkatesu, āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharatī’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

“Bhikkhus, when these four bases of spiritual power are thus developed and cultivated, a bhikkhu experiences various kinds of spiritual power: having been one, he becomes many; having been many, he becomes one… he wields bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world. When these four bases of spiritual power are thus developed and cultivated, a bhikkhu, through the destruction of the taints, by realizing for himself with direct knowledge, enters and dwells in the taintless liberation of mind and liberation by wisdom in this very life.” The first.

(Chapi abhiññāyo vitthāretabbā).

(The six kinds of direct knowledge should be expanded upon.)
