# SN 51.19 Iddhādidesanāsuttaṃ

## SN 51.19 The Discourse on the Exposition of Spiritual Power

831. ‘‘Iddhiṃ vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi iddhipādañca iddhipādabhāvanañca iddhipādabhāvanāgāminiñca paṭipadaṃ. Taṃ suṇātha.

831.Monks, I will teach you about spiritual power, the bases of spiritual power, the development of the bases of spiritual power, and the path leading to the development of the bases of spiritual power. Listen carefully.

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, iddhi? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti – ekopi hutvā bahudhā hoti, bahudhāpi hutvā eko hoti…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, iddhi.

And what, monks, is spiritual power? Here, monks, a monk experiences various kinds of spiritual power: having been one, he becomes many; having been many, he becomes one… he exercises mastery with the body as far as the Brahmā world. This is called, monks, spiritual power.

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, iddhipādo? Yo so, bhikkhave, maggo yā paṭipadā iddhilābhāya iddhipaṭilābhāya saṃvattati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, iddhipādo.

And what, monks, is the basis of spiritual power? That path, monks, that practice which conduces to the gaining of spiritual power, to the attainment of spiritual power—this is called, monks, the basis of spiritual power.

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, iddhipādabhāvanā? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi …pe… vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, iddhipādabhāvanā.

And what, monks, is the development of the bases of spiritual power? Here, monks, a monk develops the basis of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to desire and the volitional formations of striving; he develops the basis of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to energy, samādhi due to mind, and samādhi due to investigation and the volitional formations of striving. This is called, monks, the development of the bases of spiritual power.

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, iddhipādabhāvanāgāminī paṭipadā? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi, sammāsaṅkappo, sammāvācā, sammākammanto, sammāājīvo, sammāvāyāmo, sammāsati, sammāsamādhi – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, iddhipādabhāvanāgāminī paṭipadā’’ti. Navamaṃ.

And what, monks, is the practice that leads to the development of the bases of spiritual power? It is this Noble Eightfold Path, namely: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samādhi. This is called, monks, the practice that leads to the development of the bases of spiritual power.
