# SN 51.14 Moggallānasuttaṃ

## SN 51.14 The Moggallāna Sutta

826. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati pubbārāme migāramātupāsāde. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū heṭṭhāmigāramātupāsāde viharanti uddhatā unnaḷā capalā mukharā vikiṇṇavācā muṭṭhassatino asampajānā asamāhitā bhantacittā [vibbhantacittā (sī. syā. kaṃ.)] pākatindriyā.

826.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in the Eastern Park, at the mansion of Migara's mother. Now at that time, several monks were dwelling below the Migara's mother's mansion—restless, haughty, flighty, talkative, given to idle chatter, unmindful, without clear comprehension, uncollected, with agitated minds, and with unrestrained senses.

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ete kho, moggallāna, sabrahmacārino heṭṭhāmigāramātupāsāde viharanti uddhatā unnaḷā capalā mukharā vikiṇṇavācā muṭṭhassatino asampajānā asamāhitā bhantacittā pākatindriyā. Gaccha, moggallāna, te bhikkhū saṃvejehī’’ti.

Then the Blessed One addressed Venerable Mahāmoggallāna: "Moggallāna, these fellow monks dwelling below the Migāra’s-mother mansion are restless, haughty, flighty, talkative, given to idle chatter, unmindful, without clear comprehension, uncollected, with agitated minds, and with unrestrained senses. Go, Moggallāna, and rouse those monks."

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhagavato paṭissutvā tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhāresi [abhisaṅkharesi (syā. pī. ka.)] yathā pādaṅguṭṭhakena migāramātupāsādaṃ saṅkampesi sampakampesi sampacālesi. Atha kho te bhikkhū saṃviggā lomahaṭṭhajātā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu – ‘‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho! Nivātañca vata ayañca migāramātupāsādo gambhīranemo sunikhāto acalo asampakampī, atha ca pana saṅkampito sampakampito sampacālito’’ti!

"Yes, venerable sir," replied Venerable Mahāmoggallāna to the Blessed One. Then he performed such a willed act of psychic power that he made Migāra’s mother’s mansion shake, tremble, and quake with just his big toe. Then those monks, agitated and with their hair standing on end, stood aside, exclaiming: "How astonishing, how marvelous! This mansion of Migāra’s mother is sheltered from the wind, deeply founded, well-fixed, immovable, and unshakable—yet now it shakes, trembles, and quakes!"

Atha kho bhagavā yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃ nu tumhe, bhikkhave, saṃviggā lomahaṭṭhajātā ekamantaṃ ṭhitā’’ti? ‘‘Acchariyaṃ, bhante, abbhutaṃ bhante! Nivātañca vata ayañca migāramātupāsādo gambhīranemo sunikhāto acalo asampakampī, atha ca pana saṅkampito sampakampito sampacālito’’ti! ‘‘Tumheva kho, bhikkhave, saṃvejetukāmena moggallānena bhikkhunā pādaṅguṭṭhakena migāramātupāsādo, saṅkampito sampakampito sampacālito. Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, bhikkhave, katamesaṃ dhammānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā moggallāno bhikkhu evaṃmahiddhiko evaṃmahānubhāvo’’ti? ‘‘Bhagavaṃmūlakā no, bhante, dhammā, bhagavaṃnettikā bhagavaṃpaṭisaraṇā. Sādhu vata, bhante, bhagavantaṃyeva paṭibhātu etassa bhāsitassa attho. Bhagavato sutvā bhikkhū dhāressantī’’ti.

Then the Blessed One approached those monks; having approached them, the Blessed One said this: "Why, monks, are you agitated, with hair standing on end, standing aside?" "Venerable Sir, it is astonishing, it is marvelous! This Migāra’s mother’s mansion is sheltered from the wind, deeply founded, well-fixed, immovable, and unshakable—yet it has been shaken, trembled, and quaked!" "Monks, it was the monk Moggallāna, wishing to stir you to diligence, who made Migāra’s mother’s mansion shake, tremble, and quake with just his big toe. What do you think, monks, by the development and cultivation of which qualities does the monk Moggallāna possess such great psychic power and might?" "Venerable Sir, our teachings have the Blessed One as their root, the Blessed One as our guide, and the Blessed One as our refuge. It would be good if the Blessed One himself would make known the meaning of this statement. Having heard it from the Blessed One, the monks will remember it."

‘‘Tena hi, bhikkhave, suṇātha. Catunnaṃ kho, bhikkhave, iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā moggallāno bhikkhu evaṃmahiddhiko evaṃmahānubhāvo. Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, moggallāno bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi…pe… vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti – ‘iti me vīmaṃsā na ca atilīnā bhavissati, na ca atippaggahitā bhavissati; na ca ajjhattaṃ saṃkhittā bhavissati, na ca bahiddhā vikkhittā bhavissati’. Pacchāpuresaññī ca viharati – yathā pure tathā pacchā, yathā pacchā tathā pure; yathā adho tathā uddhaṃ yathā uddhaṃ tathā adho; yathā divā tathā rattiṃ, yathā rattiṃ tathā divā. Iti vivaṭena cetasā apariyonaddhena sappabhāsaṃ cittaṃ bhāveti. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā moggallāno bhikkhu evaṃmahiddhiko evaṃmahānubhāvo. Imesañca pana, bhikkhave, catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā moggallāno bhikkhu anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti…pe… imesañca pana, bhikkhave, catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā moggallāno bhikkhu āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharatī’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

"Therefore, monks, listen. It is through the development and cultivation of the four bases of spiritual power that the monk Moggallāna possesses such great psychic power and might. Which four? Here, monks, the monk Moggallāna develops the base of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to desire and the necessary effort. He develops the base of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to energy and the necessary effort. He develops the base of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to mind and the necessary effort. He develops the base of spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to investigation and the necessary effort. He thinks: 'Thus my investigation will not be too slack nor too strained; it will not be constricted internally nor distracted externally.' He dwells perceiving the front and the back—as the front is, so is the back; as the back is, so is the front. As below is, so is above; as above is, so is below. As by day, so by night; as by night, so by day. Thus, with an open and unenclosed mind, he develops a radiant mind. It is through the development and cultivation of these four bases of spiritual power that the monk Moggallāna possesses such great psychic power and might. And through the development and cultivation of these four bases of spiritual power, the monk Moggallāna experiences the various kinds of spiritual power... even up to the Brahmā world, he wields mastery with the body... And through the development and cultivation of these four bases of spiritual power, the monk Moggallāna, by the destruction of the taints, having directly known it for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life enters and dwells in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom." The fourth.
