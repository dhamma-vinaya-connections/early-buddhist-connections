# SN 51.13 Chandasamādhisuttaṃ

## SN 51.13 The Discourse on samādhi Due to Desire

825. ‘‘Chandaṃ ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu nissāya labhati samādhiṃ, labhati cittassa ekaggataṃ – ayaṃ vuccati chandasamādhi. So anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ anuppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Uppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Anuppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ uppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Ime vuccanti ‘padhānasaṅkhārā’ti. Iti ayañca chando, ayañca chandasamādhi, ime ca padhānasaṅkhārā – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, ‘chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgato iddhipādo’’’.

825."Monks, if a monk, by means of desire, gains samādhi, gains one-pointedness of mind—this is called samādhi due to desire. He generates desire for the non-arising of unarisen unwholesome states, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives. He generates desire for the abandoning of arisen unwholesome states, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives. He generates desire for the arising of unarisen wholesome states, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives. He generates desire for the maintenance, non-disappearance, increase, abundance, development, and completion of arisen wholesome states, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives. These are called ‘the volitional formations of exertion.’ Thus, this desire, this samādhi due to desire, and these volitional formations of exertion—this, monks, is called ‘the basis of psychic power endowed with samādhi due to desire and volitional formations of exertion.’"

‘‘Vīriyaṃ ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu nissāya labhati samādhiṃ, labhati cittassa ekaggataṃ – ayaṃ vuccati ‘vīriyasamādhi’. So anuppannānaṃ…pe… uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Ime vuccanti ‘padhānasaṅkhārā’ti. Iti idañca vīriyaṃ, ayañca vīriyasamādhi, ime ca padhānasaṅkhārā – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, ‘vīriyasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgato iddhipādo’’’.

"Monks, if a monk, by means of energy, gains samādhi, gains one-pointedness of mind—this is called ‘samādhi due to energy.’ He generates desire, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the non-arising of unarisen unwholesome states... for the maintenance, non-disappearance, increase, abundance, development, and completion of arisen wholesome states. These are called ‘the volitional formations of exertion.’ Thus this energy, this samādhi due to energy, and these volitional formations of exertion—this, monks, is called ‘the basis of psychic power endowed with samādhi due to energy and volitional formations of exertion.’"

‘‘Cittaṃ ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu nissāya labhati samādhiṃ, labhati cittassa ekaggataṃ – ayaṃ vuccati ‘cittasamādhi’. So anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ…pe… uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Ime vuccanti ‘padhānasaṅkhārā’ti. Iti idañca cittaṃ, ayañca cittasamādhi, ime ca padhānasaṅkhārā – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, ‘cittasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgato iddhipādo’’’.

"Monks, if a monk, by means of mind, gains samādhi, gains one-pointedness of mind, this is called ‘samādhi due to mind.’ He generates desire for the non-arising of unarisen unwholesome states...for the maintenance...increase, abundance, development, and completion of arisen wholesome states, he generates desire, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives. These are called ‘the volitional formations of exertion.’ Thus this mind, this samādhi due to mind, and these volitional formations of exertion—this, monks, is called ‘the basis of psychic power endowed with samādhi due to mind and volitional formations of exertion.’"

‘‘Vīmaṃsaṃ ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu nissāya labhati samādhiṃ, labhati cittassa ekaggataṃ – ayaṃ vuccati ‘vīmaṃsāsamādhi’. So anuppannānaṃ pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ anuppādāya chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati…pe… uppannānaṃ kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ ṭhitiyā asammosāya bhiyyobhāvāya vepullāya bhāvanāya pāripūriyā chandaṃ janeti vāyamati vīriyaṃ ārabhati cittaṃ paggaṇhāti padahati. Ime vuccanti ‘padhānasaṅkhārā’ti. Iti ayañca vīmaṃsā, ayañca vīmaṃsāsamādhi, ime ca padhānasaṅkhārā – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, ‘vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgato iddhipādo’’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

"Monks, if a monk, by means of investigation, gains samādhi, gains one-pointedness of mind—this is called ‘samādhi due to investigation.’ He generates desire, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the non-arising of unarisen unwholesome states... he generates desire, endeavors, arouses energy, exerts his mind, and strives for the maintenance, non-disappearance, increase, abundance, development, and completion of arisen wholesome states. These are called ‘the volitional formations of exertion.’ Thus, this investigation, this samādhi due to investigation, and these volitional formations of exertion—this, monks, is called ‘the basis of psychic power endowed with samādhi due to investigation and volitional formations of exertion.’" This is the third.
