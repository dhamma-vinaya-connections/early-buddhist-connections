# SN 51.27 Paṭhamaānandasuttaṃ

## SN 51.27 The First Ānanda Sutta

839. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

839.At Sāvatthī. Then Venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. While sitting to one side, Venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Katamā nu kho, bhante, iddhi, katamo iddhipādo, katamā iddhipādabhāvanā, katamā iddhipādabhāvanāgāminī paṭipadā’’ti? ‘‘Idhānanda, bhikkhu anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti – ekopi hutvā bahudhā hoti, bahudhāpi hutvā eko hoti…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti – ayaṃ vuccatānanda, iddhi’’.

“Venerable sir, what is spiritual power, what is the basis of spiritual power, what is the development of the basis of spiritual power, and what is the practice leading to the development of the basis of spiritual power?” “Here, Ānanda, a bhikkhu experiences various kinds of spiritual power: having been one, he becomes many; having been many, he becomes one… he wields mastery with the body as far as the Brahma world—this, Ānanda, is called spiritual power.”

‘‘Katamo cānanda, iddhipādo? Yo, ānanda, maggo yā paṭipadā iddhilābhāya iddhipaṭilābhāya saṃvattati – ayaṃ vuccatānanda, iddhipādo.

“And what, Ānanda, is the basis of spiritual power? It is, Ānanda, the path, the practice that leads to the gaining of spiritual power, to the attainment of spiritual power. This, Ānanda, is called the basis of spiritual power.”

‘‘Katamā cānanda, iddhipādabhāvanā? Idhānanda, bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi…pe… vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti – ayaṃ vuccatānanda, iddhipādabhāvanā.

“And what, Ānanda, is the development of the basis of spiritual power? Here, Ānanda, a bhikkhu develops the basis of spiritual power possessed of samādhi due to zeal and the volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis of spiritual power possessed of samādhi due to energy and the volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis of spiritual power possessed of samādhi due to mind and the volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis of spiritual power possessed of samādhi due to investigation and the volitional formations of striving. This, Ānanda, is called the development of the basis of spiritual power.”

‘‘Katamā cānanda, iddhipādabhāvanāgāminī paṭipadā? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi – ayaṃ vuccatānanda, iddhipādabhāvanāgāminī paṭipadā’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

“And what, Ānanda, is the practice leading to the development of the basis of spiritual power? It is just this ![[Noble Path]]. This, Ānanda, is called the practice leading to the development of the basis of spiritual power.” The seventh.
