# SN 51.10 Cetiyasuttaṃ

## SN 51.10 The Discourse on Shrines

822. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya vesāliṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Vesāliyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘gaṇhāhi, ānanda, nisīdanaṃ. Yena cāpālaṃ cetiyaṃ [pāvālacetiyaṃ (syā. kaṃ.)] tenupasaṅkamissāma divāvihārāyā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissutvā nisīdanaṃ ādāya bhagavantaṃ piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandhi. Atha kho bhagavā yena cāpālaṃ cetiyaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Āyasmāpi kho ānando bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ bhagavā etadavoca –

822.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Vesālī in the Great Wood at the Kūṭāgāra Hall. Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the morning, took his bowl and robe and entered Vesālī for alms. Having wandered for alms in Vesālī and returned from the alms round after the meal, he addressed Venerable Ānanda: “Take a sitting cloth, Ānanda. Let us go to the Cāpāla Shrine for the day’s sojourn.” “Yes, venerable sir,” replied Venerable Ānanda, and taking a sitting cloth, he followed behind the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One went to the Cāpāla Shrine, and upon arrival, sat down on the prepared seat. Venerable Ānanda also, having paid homage to the Blessed One, sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Blessed One said to Venerable Ānanda:

‘‘Ramaṇīyā, ānanda, vesālī, ramaṇīyaṃ udenaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ gotamakaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ sattambaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ bahuputtaṃ cetiyaṃ [bahuputtakacetiyaṃ (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)], ramaṇīyaṃ sārandadaṃ cetiyaṃ [ānandacetiyaṃ (ka.), sānandaraṃ (ka.)], ramaṇīyaṃ cāpālaṃ cetiyaṃ. Yassa kassaci, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā, so ākaṅkhamāno kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā. Tathāgatassa kho, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā. Ākaṅkhamāno, ānanda, tathāgato kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā’’ti.

“Delightful, Ānanda, is Vesālī; delightful is the Udena Shrine; delightful is the Gotamaka Shrine; delightful is the Sattamba Shrine; delightful is the Bahuputta Shrine; delightful is the Sārandada Shrine; delightful is the Cāpāla Shrine. Anyone, Ānanda, who has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle, made a basis, sustained, consolidated, and properly undertaken the four bases of spiritual power could, if he wished, remain for an eon or the remainder of an eon. The Tathāgata, Ānanda, has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle, made a basis, sustained, consolidated, and properly undertaken the four bases of spiritual power. If he wished, Ānanda, the Tathāgata could remain for an eon or the remainder of an eon.”

Evampi kho āyasmā ānando bhagavatā oḷārike nimitte kayiramāne oḷārike obhāse kayiramāne nāsakkhi paṭivijjhituṃ; na bhagavantaṃ yāci – ‘‘tiṭṭhatu, bhante, bhagavā kappaṃ, tiṭṭhatu sugato kappaṃ [sugato kappāvasesaṃ (pī. ka.) dī. ni. 2.167] bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’’nti yathā taṃ mārena pariyuṭṭhitacitto.

Even so, though the Blessed One gave such a plain sign, made such a clear indication, Venerable Ānanda was not able to fathom it. He did not beseech the Blessed One: “May the Blessed One remain, venerable sir, for an eon, may the Fortunate One remain for an eon, for the benefit and happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the welfare, benefit, and happiness of gods and humans”—his mind being possessed by Māra.

Dutiyampi kho bhagavā…pe… tatiyampi kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ramaṇīyā, ānanda, vesālī, ramaṇīyaṃ udenaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ gotamakaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ sattambaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ bahuputtaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ sārandadaṃ cetiyaṃ, ramaṇīyaṃ cāpālaṃ cetiyaṃ. Yassa kassaci, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā, so ākaṅkhamāno kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā. Tathāgatassa kho, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā. Ākaṅkhamāno, ānanda, tathāgato kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā’’ti.

A second time the Blessed One … A third time the Blessed One addressed Venerable Ānanda: “Delightful, Ānanda, is Vesālī; delightful is the Udena Shrine; delightful is the Gotamaka Shrine; delightful is the Sattamba Shrine; delightful is the Bahuputta Shrine; delightful is the Sārandada Shrine; delightful is the Cāpāla Shrine. Ānanda, anyone who has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle, made a basis, sustained, consolidated, and properly undertaken the four bases of spiritual power could, if they wished, remain for an eon or for the remainder of an eon. Ānanda, the Tathāgata has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle, made a basis, sustained, consolidated, and properly undertaken the four bases of spiritual power. If he wished, Ānanda, the Tathāgata could remain for an eon or for the remainder of an eon.”

Evampi kho āyasmā ānando bhagavatā oḷārike nimitte kayiramāne oḷārike obhāse kayiramāne nāsakkhi paṭivijjhituṃ; na bhagavantaṃ yāci – ‘‘tiṭṭhatu, bhante, bhagavā kappaṃ, tiṭṭhatu sugato kappaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’’nti yathā taṃ mārena pariyuṭṭhitacitto.

Even so, though the Blessed One gave such a clear sign and made such a plain indication, Venerable Ānanda was unable to fathom it. He did not beseech the Blessed One: “May the Blessed One remain, venerable sir, for an aeon, may the Fortunate One remain for an aeon, for the benefit and happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the welfare, benefit, and happiness of gods and humans”—his mind being possessed by Māra.

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘gaccha kho tvaṃ, ānanda, yassa dāni kālaṃ maññasī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissutvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā avidūre aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle nisīdi. Atha kho māro pāpimā, acirapakkante āyasmante ānande, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā, parinibbātu dāni sugato [parinibbātu sugato (sī. syā. kaṃ.) evamuparipi]! Parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato. Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me bhikkhū na sāvakā bhavissanti viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacārino, sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhissanti desessanti paññapessanti paṭṭhapessanti vivarissanti vibhajissanti uttānīkarissanti [uttāniṃ karissanti (ka.), uttānikarissanti (pī.)], uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desessantī’’’ti.

Then the Blessed One addressed Venerable Ānanda: “Go now, Ānanda, do what seems fit to you.” “Yes, Venerable Sir,” replied Venerable Ānanda to the Blessed One. Having risen from his seat, he paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and sat down at the foot of a tree not far away. Then, soon after Venerable Ānanda had left, Māra the Evil One approached the Blessed One and said: “Now may the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna! Now may the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna! Now is the time for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna. For the Blessed One has spoken these words: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until my monks become disciples—competent, well-trained, confident, learned, Dhamma-holders, practicing the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing properly and living according to the Dhamma—until, having learned the teaching of their own teacher, they can explain it, teach it, proclaim it, establish it, reveal it, analyze it, and clarify it; and until they can thoroughly refute any opposing doctrine that has arisen by means of the Dhamma and teach the Dhamma with its wonders.’”

Santi kho pana, bhante, etarahi bhikkhū bhagavato sāvakā viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacārino, sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhanti desenti paññapenti paṭṭhapenti vivaranti vibhajanti uttānīkaronti, uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desenti. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā, parinibbātu dāni, sugato! Parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato.

"Venerable sir, now there are bhikkhus, disciples of the Blessed One, who are well-trained, disciplined, confident, very learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing properly, living in accordance with the Dhamma. Having learned their own teacher's doctrine, they explain, teach, declare, establish, reveal, analyze, and make it clear. Having thoroughly refuted with the Dhamma any arisen opposing doctrine, they teach the Dhamma with its marvel. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna! Let the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna! The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna."

‘‘Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me bhikkhuniyo na sāvikā bhavissanti viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo, sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhissanti desessanti paññapessanti paṭṭhapessanti vivarissanti vibhajissanti uttānīkarissanti, uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desessantī’’’ti.

"Venerable sir, this word was spoken by the Blessed One: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until my bhikkhunīs are disciples—well-trained, disciplined, confident, very learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing properly, living in accordance with the Dhamma—who, having learned their own teacher's doctrine, will explain, teach, declare, establish, reveal, analyze, and make it clear; and who, having thoroughly refuted with the Dhamma any arisen opposing doctrine, will teach the Dhamma with its marvel.’"

‘‘Santi kho pana, bhante, etarahi bhikkhuniyo bhagavato sāvikā viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo, sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhanti desenti paññapenti paṭṭhapenti vivaranti vibhajanti uttānīkaronti, uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desenti. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā, parinibbātu dāni, sugato! Parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato.

"Venerable sir, now there are bhikkhunīs, disciples of the Blessed One, who are well-trained, disciplined, confident, very learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing properly, living in accordance with the Dhamma. Having learned their own teacher's doctrine, they explain, teach, declare, establish, reveal, analyze, and make it clear. Having thoroughly refuted with the Dhamma any arisen opposing doctrine, they teach the Dhamma with its marvel. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna! Let the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna! The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna."

‘‘Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me upāsakā…pe… yāva me upāsikā na sāvikā bhavissanti viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo, sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhissanti desessanti paññapessanti paṭṭhapessanti vivarissanti vibhajissanti uttānīkarissanti, uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desessantī’’’ti.

"Venerable sir, this word was spoken by the Blessed One: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until my male lay followers... until my female lay followers are disciples—well-trained, disciplined, confident, very learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing properly, living in accordance with the Dhamma—who, having learned their own teacher's doctrine, will explain, teach, declare, establish, reveal, analyze, and make it clear; and who, having thoroughly refuted with the Dhamma any arisen opposing doctrine, will teach the Dhamma with its marvel.’"

‘‘Santi kho pana, bhante, etarahi upāsakā…pe… upāsikā bhagavato sāvikā viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo, sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhanti desenti paññapenti paṭṭhapenti vivaranti vibhajanti uttānīkaronti, uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desenti. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā, parinibbātu dāni, sugato! Parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato.

"Venerable sir, now there are male lay followers... female lay followers, disciples of the Blessed One, who are well-trained, disciplined, confident, very learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing properly, living in accordance with the Dhamma. Having learned their own teacher's doctrine, they explain, teach, declare, establish, reveal, analyze, and make it clear. Having thoroughly refuted with the Dhamma any arisen opposing doctrine, they teach the Dhamma with its marvel. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna! Let the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna! The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna."

‘‘Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me idaṃ brahmacariyaṃ na iddhañceva bhavissati phītañca vitthāritaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsita’nti. Tayidaṃ, bhante, bhagavato brahmacariyaṃ iddhañceva phītañca vitthāritaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā, parinibbātu dāni sugato. Parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato’’ti.

"Venerable sir, this word was spoken by the Blessed One: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until this holy life of mine is prosperous, flourishing, widespread, become numerous, and become mighty, and is well-proclaimed among gods and humans.’ Now, venerable sir, the Blessed One’s holy life is prosperous, flourishing, widespread, become numerous, and become mighty, and is well-proclaimed among gods and humans. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna! Let the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna! The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna."

Evaṃ vutte bhagavā māraṃ pāpimantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘appossukko tvaṃ, pāpima, hohi. Na ciraṃ [nacirasseva (ka.)] tathāgatassa parinibbānaṃ bhavissati. Ito tiṇṇaṃ māsānaṃ accayena tathāgato parinibbāyissatī’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā cāpāle cetiye sato sampajāno āyusaṅkhāraṃ ossaji. Ossaṭṭhe ca [osajje pana (ka.)] bhagavatā āyusaṅkhāre mahābhūmicālo ahosi bhiṃsanako lomahaṃso, devadundubhiyo [devadudrabhiyo (ka.)] ca phaliṃsu. Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

When this was said, the Blessed One said to Māra the Evil One: “Be unconcerned, Evil One. The Tathāgata’s final Nibbāna will not be long. After three months from now the Tathāgata will attain final Nibbāna.” Then, at the Cāpāla Cetiya, the Blessed One, mindful and clearly comprehending, relinquished the life-formation. When the Blessed One relinquished the life-formation, a great earthquake occurred, terrible and hair-raising, and the divine drums burst. Then, understanding this matter, the Blessed One on that occasion uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Tulamatulañca sambhavaṃ, bhavasaṅkhāramavassaji muni;Ajjhattarato samāhito, abhindi kavacamivattasambhava’’nti. dasamaṃ;

The sage relinquished the comparable and the incomparable, the source of becoming, the formation for renewed existence. Delighting inwardly and collected, he shattered his own self-originated armor as if it were a breastplate.

Cāpālavaggo paṭhamo.

First, the Cāpāla Chapter.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Apārāpi viraddho ca, ariyā nibbidāpi ca;Padesaṃ samattaṃ bhikkhu, buddhaṃ ñāṇañca cetiyanti.

Apārā, Viraddho, Ariyā, Nibbidā, Padesa, Samatta, Bhikkhu, Buddha, Ñāṇa, and Cetiya.
