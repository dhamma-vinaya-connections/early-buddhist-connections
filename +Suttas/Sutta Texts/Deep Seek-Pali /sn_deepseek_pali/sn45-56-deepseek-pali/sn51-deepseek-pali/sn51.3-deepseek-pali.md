# SN 51.3 Ariyasuttaṃ

## SN 51.3 The Noble Discourse

815. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ariyā niyyānikā niyyanti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāya. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi…pe… iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ariyā niyyānikā niyyanti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāyā’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

815.“Monks, these four bases for spiritual power, when developed and cultivated, are noble, leading to deliverance, leading the one who practices them to the complete destruction of suffering. Which four? Here, monks, a bhikkhu develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of zeal, accompanied by volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of energy, accompanied by volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of mind, accompanied by volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi of investigation, accompanied by volitional formations of striving. These, monks, are the four bases for spiritual power that, when developed and cultivated, are noble, leading to deliverance, leading the one who practices them to the complete destruction of suffering.” The third.
