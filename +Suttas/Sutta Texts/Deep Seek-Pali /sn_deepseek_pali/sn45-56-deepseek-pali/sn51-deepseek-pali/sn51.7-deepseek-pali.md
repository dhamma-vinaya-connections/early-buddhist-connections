# SN 51.7 Bhikkhusuttaṃ

## SN 51.7 The Monk Discourse

819. ‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, atītamaddhānaṃ bhikkhū āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihariṃsu, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, anāgatamaddhānaṃ bhikkhū āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharissanti, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, etarahi bhikkhū āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharanti, sabbe te catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā.

819."Monks, whatever monks in the past, through the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized it for themselves with direct knowledge, entered and dwelled in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life—all of them did so because they had developed and cultivated the four bases of spiritual power. Monks, whatever monks in the future, through the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized it for themselves with direct knowledge, will enter and dwell in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life—all of them will do so because they will have developed and cultivated the four bases of spiritual power. Monks, whatever monks at present, through the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized it for themselves with direct knowledge, enter and dwell in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life—all of them do so because they have developed and cultivated the four bases of spiritual power."

‘‘Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi…pe… cittasamādhi…pe… vīmaṃsāsamādhippadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, atītamaddhānaṃ bhikkhū āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihariṃsu sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, anāgatamaddhānaṃ bhikkhū āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharissanti, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, etarahi bhikkhū āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharanti, sabbe te imesaṃyeva catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

"Which four? Here, monks, a monk develops the ![[Iddhipadas]] that possesses samādhi due to energy... samādhi due to mind... samādhi due to investigation and the striving of formations. Monks, whatever monks in the past, through the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized it for themselves with direct knowledge, entered and dwelled in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life—all of them did so by developing and cultivating these four bases of psychic power. Whatever monks in the future, through the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized it for themselves with direct knowledge, will enter and dwell in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life—all of them will do so by developing and cultivating these four bases of psychic power. Whatever monks at present, through the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized it for themselves with direct knowledge, enter and dwell in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life—all of them do so by developing and cultivating these four bases of psychic power." The seventh.
