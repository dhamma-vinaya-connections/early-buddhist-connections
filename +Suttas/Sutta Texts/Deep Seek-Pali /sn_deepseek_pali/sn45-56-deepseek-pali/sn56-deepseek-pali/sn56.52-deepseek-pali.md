1122."Suppose, bhikkhus, there were a pond fifty yojanas long, fifty yojanas wide, and fifty yojanas deep, filled to the brim with water, enough for a crow to drink from. Then a man were to draw up water from it with the tip of a blade of kusa grass. What do you think, bhikkhus, which is more: the water drawn up with the tip of the blade of kusa grass or the water in the pond?""Venerable sir, the water in the pond is far more; the water drawn up with the tip of the blade of kusa grass is insignificant. What remains is insignificant.
