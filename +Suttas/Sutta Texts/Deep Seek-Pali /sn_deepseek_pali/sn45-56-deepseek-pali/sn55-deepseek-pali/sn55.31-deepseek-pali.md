# SN 55.31 Paṭhamapuññābhisandasuttaṃ

## SN 55.31 The First Discourse on the Streams of Merit

1027. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, puññābhisandā kusalābhisandā sukhassāhārā. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Ayaṃ paṭhamo puññābhisando kusalābhisando sukhassāhāro.

1027.At Sāvatthī. "Bhikkhus, there are these four streams of merit, streams of the wholesome, nourishments for happiness. What four? Here, bhikkhus, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha thus: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is...the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.' This is the first stream of merit, stream of the wholesome, nourishment for happiness.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako dhamme aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo…pe… paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhīti. Ayaṃ dutiyo puññābhisando kusalābhisando sukhassāhāro.

"Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma thus: 'The Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One… to be personally experienced by the wise.' This is the second stream of merit, stream of the wholesome, nourishment for happiness."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako saṅghe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassāti. Ayaṃ tatiyo puññābhisando kusalābhisando sukhassāhāro.

"Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Saṅgha thus: 'The Saṅgha of the Blessed One's disciples is practicing the good way... the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.' This is the third stream of merit, stream of the wholesome, nourishment for happiness."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Ayaṃ catuttho puññābhisando kusalābhisando sukhassāhāro. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cattāro puññābhisandā kusalābhisandā sukhassāhārā’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

"Furthermore, bhikkhus, a noble disciple is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken… conducive to samādhi. This is the fourth stream of merit, stream of the wholesome, nourishment for happiness. These, bhikkhus, are the four streams of merit, streams of the wholesome, nourishments for happiness." The first.
