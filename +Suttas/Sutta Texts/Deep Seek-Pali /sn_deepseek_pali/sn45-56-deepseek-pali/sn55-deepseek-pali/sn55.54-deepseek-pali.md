# SN 55.54 Gilānasuttaṃ

## SN 55.54 The Discourse on the Sick

1050. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karonti – ‘‘niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissatī’’ti. Assosi kho mahānāmo sakko – ‘‘sambahulā kira bhikkhū bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karonti – ‘niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissatī’’’ti. Atha kho mahānāmo sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutametaṃ, bhante – ‘sambahulā kira bhikkhū bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karonti – niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissatī’ti. Na kho netaṃ [na kho te etaṃ (sī. pī.)], bhante, bhagavato sammukhā sutaṃ sammukhā paṭiggahitaṃ sappaññena upāsakena sappañño upāsako ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno ovaditabbo’’ti.

1050.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu, in the Nigrodha Park. Now at that time a number of bhikkhus were making robes for the Blessed One, thinking: “When the Blessed One has finished his robes, he will set out on tour after three months.” Mahānāma the Sakyan heard: “A number of bhikkhus, it seems, are making robes for the Blessed One, thinking: ‘When the Blessed One has finished his robes, he will set out on tour after three months.’” Then Mahānāma the Sakyan approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. When he had sat down, Mahānāma the Sakyan said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, I have heard: ‘A number of bhikkhus, it seems, are making robes for the Blessed One, thinking: “When the Blessed One has finished his robes, he will set out on tour after three months.”’ But, venerable sir, I have not heard this directly from the Blessed One, nor received it directly from him: ‘How should a wise lay follower advise a wise lay follower who is sick, suffering, and gravely ill?’”

‘‘Sappaññena mahānāma, upāsakena sappañño upāsako ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno catūhi assāsanīyehi dhammehi assāsetabbo – ‘assāsatāyasmā – atthāyasmato buddhe aveccappasādo itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Assāsatāyasmā – atthāyasmato dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantāni sīlāni akhaṇḍāni…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikānī’’’ti.

“Mahānāma, a wise lay follower should comfort a wise lay follower who is sick, suffering, and gravely ill with four grounds for reassurance: ‘Be reassured, venerable sir—you have unwavering confidence in the Buddha: “Indeed, the Blessed One is an Arahant, perfectly enlightened, accomplished in true knowledge and conduct, fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of devas and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.” Be reassured, venerable sir—you have unwavering confidence in the Dhamma: “The Dhamma is well expounded by the Blessed One, directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, applicable, to be personally experienced by the wise.” Be reassured, venerable sir—you have unwavering confidence in the Saṅgha: “The Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way, practicing the straight way, practicing the true way, practicing the proper way; that is, the four pairs of persons, the eight types of individuals—this Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverential salutation, the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.” Be reassured, venerable sir—you possess the virtues dear to the noble ones, unbroken, untorn, unblemished, untainted, liberating, praised by the wise, ungrasped, conducive to samādhi.’

‘‘Sappaññena, mahānāma, upāsakena sappañño upāsako ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno imehi catūhi assāsanīyehi dhammehi assāsetvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘atthāyasmato mātāpitūsu apekkhā’ti? So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘atthi me mātāpitūsu apekkhā’ti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘āyasmā kho māriso maraṇadhammo. Sace pāyasmā mātāpitūsu apekkhaṃ karissati, marissateva; no ce pāyasmā mātāpitūsu apekkhaṃ karissati, marissateva. Sādhāyasmā, yā te mātāpitūsu apekkhā taṃ pajahā’’’ti.

“Having comforted a wise lay follower who is sick, suffering, and gravely ill with these four grounds for reassurance, Mahānāma, a wise lay follower should then say to him: ‘Do you have any longing for your parents?’ If he says, ‘Yes, I have longing for my parents,’ then he should be told: ‘Good sir, you are subject to death. Whether, good sir, you long for your parents or not, you will die. It would be good, venerable sir, if you would abandon that longing for your parents.’”

‘‘So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘yā me mātāpitūsu apekkhā sā pahīnā’ti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘atthi panāyasmato puttadāresu apekkhā’ti? So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘atthi me puttadāresu apekkhā’ti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘āyasmā kho māriso maraṇadhammo. Sace pāyasmā puttadāresu apekkhaṃ karissati, marissateva; no ce pāyasmā puttadāresu apekkhaṃ karissati, marissateva. Sādhāyasmā, yā te puttadāresu apekkhā taṃ pajahā’’’ti.

“If he says, ‘My longing for my parents has been abandoned,’ then he should be told: ‘But do you have any longing for your children and spouse?’ If he says, ‘Yes, I have longing for my children and spouse,’ then he should be told: ‘Good sir, you are subject to death. Whether, good sir, you long for your children and spouse or not, you will die. It would be good, venerable sir, if you would abandon that longing for your children and spouse.’”

‘‘So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘yā me puttadāresu apekkhā sā pahīnā’ti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘atthi panāyasmato mānusakesu pañcasu kāmaguṇesu apekkhā’ti? So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘atthi me mānusakesu pañcasu kāmaguṇesu apekkhā’ti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘mānusakehi kho, āvuso, kāmehi dibbā kāmā abhikkantatarā ca paṇītatarā ca. Sādhāyasmā, mānusakehi kāmehi cittaṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā cātumahārājikesu [cātummahārājikesu (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] devesu cittaṃ adhimocehī’’’ti.

“If he says, ‘My longing for children and spouse has been abandoned,’ then he should be told: ‘But do you have any longing for the five cords of human sensual pleasure?’ If he says, ‘Yes, I have longing for the five cords of human sensual pleasure,’ then he should be told: ‘Friend, divine sensual pleasures are superior and more refined than human sensual pleasures. It would be good, venerable sir, if you would withdraw your mind from human sensual pleasures and incline it towards the devas of the Four Great Kings.’”

‘‘So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘mānusakehi me kāmehi cittaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ, cātumahārājikesu devesu cittaṃ adhimocita’nti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘cātumahārājikehi kho, āvuso, devehi tāvatiṃsā devā abhikkantatarā ca paṇītatarā ca. Sādhāyasmā, cātumahārājikehi devehi cittaṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā tāvatiṃsesu devesu cittaṃ adhimocehī’’’ti.

“If he says, ‘My mind has withdrawn from human sensual pleasures and is inclined towards the devas of the Four Great Kings,’ then he should be told: ‘Friend, the Tāvatiṃsa devas are superior and more refined than the devas of the Four Great Kings. It would be good, venerable sir, if you would withdraw your mind from the devas of the Four Great Kings and incline it towards the Tāvatiṃsa devas.’”

‘‘So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘cātumahārājikehi me devehi cittaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ, tāvatiṃsesu devesu cittaṃ adhimocita’nti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘tāvatiṃsehi kho, āvuso, devehi yāmā devā…pe… tusitā devā…pe… nimmānaratī devā…pe… paranimmitavasavattī devā…pe… paranimmitavasavattīhi kho, āvuso, devehi brahmaloko abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro ca. Sādhāyasmā, paranimmitavasavattīhi devehi cittaṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā brahmaloke cittaṃ adhimocehī’ti. So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘paranimmitavasavattīhi me devehi cittaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ, brahmaloke cittaṃ adhimocita’nti, so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘brahmalokopi kho, āvuso, anicco addhuvo sakkāyapariyāpanno. Sādhāyasmā, brahmalokā cittaṃ vuṭṭhāpetvā sakkāyanirodhe cittaṃ upasaṃharāhī’’’ti.

“If he says, ‘My mind has withdrawn from the devas of the Four Great Kings and is inclined towards the Tāvatiṃsa devas,’ then he should be told: ‘Friend, the Yāma devas are superior and more refined than the Tāvatiṃsa devas. The Tusita devas are superior and more refined than the Yāma devas. The Nimmānaratī devas are superior and more refined than the Tusita devas. The Paranimmitavasavattī devas are superior and more refined than the Nimmānaratī devas. Friend, the Brahmā world is superior and more refined than the Paranimmitavasavattī devas. It would be good, venerable sir, if you would withdraw your mind from the Paranimmitavasavattī devas and incline it towards the Brahmā world.’ If he says, ‘My mind has withdrawn from the Paranimmitavasavattī devas and is inclined towards the Brahmā world,’ then he should be told: ‘Friend, the Brahmā world too is impermanent, unstable, and included within personality. It would be good, venerable sir, if you would withdraw your mind from the Brahmā world and direct it towards the cessation of personality.’”

‘‘So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘brahmalokā me cittaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ, sakkāyanirodhe cittaṃ upasaṃharāmī’ti; evaṃ vimuttacittassa kho, mahānāma, upāsakassa āsavā [vassasata (sī. syā.)] vimuttacittena bhikkhunā na kiñci nānākaraṇaṃ vadāmi, yadidaṃ – vimuttiyā vimutta’’nti. Catutthaṃ.

“If he says, ‘My mind has withdrawn from the Brahmā world, and I am directing it towards the cessation of personality,’ then, Mahānāma, I say that there is no difference at all between a lay follower whose mind is thus liberated and a bhikkhu whose mind is liberated from the taints, that is, in terms of their liberation.” Fourth.
