# SN 55.11 Sahassabhikkhunisaṅghasuttaṃ

## SN 55.11 The Discourse on the Community of a Thousand Nuns

1007. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati rājakārāme. Atha kho sahassabhikkhunisaṅgho [sahasso bhikkhunisaṃgho (sī.)] yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho tā bhikkhuniyo bhagavā etadavoca –

1007.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in the Rājakārāma. Then a Saṅgha of a thousand nuns approached the Blessed One; having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, those nuns were addressed by the Blessed One:

‘‘Catūhi kho, bhikkhuniyo, dhammehi samannāgato ariyasāvako sotāpanno hoti avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, bhikkhuniyo, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme …pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti, akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Imehi kho, bhikkhuniyo, catūhi dhammehi samannāgato ariyasāvako sotāpanno hoti avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

“Nuns, a noble disciple endowed with four qualities is a stream-enterer, not subject to falling back, assured, destined for enlightenment. What four? Here, nuns, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is… teacher of gods and humans, the Awakened One, the Blessed One.’ He is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma… in the Saṅgha… He is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, unbroken… conducive to samādhi. Nuns, a noble disciple endowed with these four qualities is a stream-enterer, not subject to falling back, assured, destined for enlightenment.” The first.
