# SN 55.26 Paṭhamaanāthapiṇḍikasuttaṃ

## SN 55.26 The First Anāthapiṇḍika Sutta.

1022. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati ābādhiko hoti dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, ambho purisa, yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mama vacanena āyasmato sāriputtassa pāde sirasā vanda – ‘anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So āyasmato sāriputtassa pāde sirasā vandatī’ti. Evañca vadehi – ‘sādhu kira, bhante, āyasmā sāriputto yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkamatu anukampaṃ upādāyā’’’ti.

1022.At Sāvatthī. At that time, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika was afflicted, suffering, gravely ill. Then the householder Anāthapiṇḍika addressed a certain man: "Come, good man, go to the Venerable Sāriputta; having approached him, pay homage with your head at the feet of the Venerable Sāriputta in my name, saying: 'Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is afflicted, suffering, gravely ill. He pays homage with his head at the feet of the Venerable Sāriputta.' And also say this: 'Venerable sir, it would be good if the Venerable Sāriputta would approach the dwelling of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika, out of compassion.'"

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho so puriso anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa paṭissutvā yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so puriso āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca –

"Yes, venerable sir," that man replied to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika. Having assented, he approached the Venerable Sāriputta; having approached, he paid homage to the Venerable Sāriputta and sat down to one side. While sitting to one side, that man said to the Venerable Sāriputta:

‘‘Anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So āyasmato sāriputtassa pāde sirasā vandati. Evañca vadati – ‘sādhu kira, bhante, āyasmā sāriputto yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkamatu anukampaṃ upādāyā’’’ti. Adhivāsesi kho āyasmā sāriputto tuṇhībhāvena.

"Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is afflicted, suffering, gravely ill. He pays homage with his head at the feet of the Venerable Sāriputta. And he says this: 'Venerable sir, it would be good if the Venerable Sāriputta would approach the dwelling of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika, out of compassion.'" The Venerable Sāriputta consented by remaining silent.

Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya āyasmatā ānandena pacchāsamaṇena yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho āyasmā sāriputto anāthapiṇḍikaṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kacci te, gahapati, khamanīyaṃ kacci yāpanīyaṃ? Kacci dukkhā vedanā paṭikkamanti, no abhikkamanti; paṭikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no abhikkamo’’ti? ‘‘Na me, bhante, khamanīyaṃ, na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti; abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo’’ti.

Then, in the morning, the Venerable Sāriputta, having dressed and taken his bowl and robe, went with the Venerable Ānanda as his attendant to the dwelling of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika. On arrival, he sat down on the prepared seat. Having sat down, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika: "I hope you are bearing up, householder, I hope you are getting by? I hope your painful feelings are subsiding and not increasing, and that their subsiding is evident, not their increasing?" "Venerable sir, I am not bearing up, I am not getting by. My painful feelings are increasing, not subsiding; their increase is evident, not their subsiding."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, buddhe appasādena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tathārūpo te buddhe appasādo natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, buddhe aveccappasādo – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Tañca pana te buddhe aveccappasādaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, whatever lack of faith in the Buddha an uninstructed ordinary person possesses—because of which, on the dissolution of the body, after death, they are reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell—such a lack of faith in the Buddha does not exist in you. But you, householder, have unwavering confidence in the Buddha: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is... the teacher of gods and humans, enlightened, the Blessed One.' And when you regard that unwavering confidence in the Buddha as present within yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, dhamme appasādena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpo te dhamme appasādo natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, dhamme aveccappasādo – svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo…pe… paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhīti. Tañca pana te dhamme aveccappasādaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, whatever kind of displeasure with the Dhamma an uninstructed ordinary person possesses, leading them after death, upon the breakup of the body, to be reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell—such displeasure with the Dhamma does not exist in you. But, householder, you have unwavering faith in the Dhamma: 'The Dhamma is well-proclaimed by the Blessed One…to be personally experienced by the wise.' And when you perceive this unwavering faith in the Dhamma in yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, saṅghe appasādena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpo te saṅghe appasādo natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, saṅghe aveccappasādo – suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassāti. Tañca pana te saṅghe aveccappasādaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person who is endowed with displeasure towards the Saṅgha, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. But you have no such displeasure towards the Saṅgha. Rather, householder, you have unwavering faith in the Saṅgha: 'The Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way... the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.' And when you experience this unwavering faith in the Saṅgha within yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, dussīlyena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpaṃ te dussīlyaṃ natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, ariyakantāni sīlāni…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikāni. Tāni ca pana te ariyakantāni sīlāni attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, the kind of immorality that an uninstructed ordinary person possesses, which leads to rebirth in deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell, after the breakup of the body, you do not have. And you do have, householder, the virtues dear to the noble ones… leading to samādhi. When you contemplate those virtues dear to the noble ones within yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpāya kho, gahapati, micchādiṭṭhiyā samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpā te micchādiṭṭhi natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammādiṭṭhi. Tañca pana te sammādiṭṭhiṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person who is endowed with such wrong view, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. But you have no such wrong view. Rather, householder, you possess right view. And when you reflect on this right view within yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, micchāsaṅkappena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpo te micchāsaṅkappo natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāsaṅkappo. Tañca pana te sammāsaṅkappaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, the kind of wrong intention that an uninstructed ordinary person possesses, due to which, upon the breakup of the body after death, they are reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell—you have no such wrong intention. Rather, householder, you possess right intention. And because you reflect on this right intention within yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpāya kho, gahapati, micchāvācāya samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpā te micchāvācā natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāvācā. Tañca pana te sammāvācaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person who possesses wrong speech, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. But you have no such wrong speech. Rather, householder, you possess right speech. And when you reflect on this right speech within yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, micchākammantena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpo te micchākammanto natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammākammanto. Tañca pana te sammākammantaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person endowed with wrong action is reborn upon the breakup of the body, after death, in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. But you have no such wrong action. And, householder, you have right action. When you observe that right action in yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, micchāājīvena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpo te micchāājīvo natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāājīvo. Tañca pana te sammāājīvaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person who engages in wrong livelihood, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. But you have no such wrong livelihood. Rather, householder, you possess right livelihood. And when you observe this right livelihood in yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, micchāvāyāmena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpo te micchāvāyāmo natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāvāyāmo. Tañca pana te sammāvāyāmaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

"Householder, the uninstructed ordinary person who possesses wrong effort goes, on the dissolution of the body, after death, to a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. But you, householder, do not have such wrong effort. And you do have right effort. And when you attend to that right effort in yourself, the painful feeling might subside on the spot."

‘‘Yathārūpāya kho, gahapati, micchāsatiyā samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpā te micchāsati natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāsati. Tañca pana te sammāsatiṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person endowed with wrong mindfulness is reborn, on the dissolution of the body, after death, in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. But you, householder, do not have such wrong mindfulness. You do have, householder, right mindfulness. As you contemplate this right mindfulness in yourself, your painful feeling might subside right there.

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, micchāsamādhinā samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpo te micchāsamādhi natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāsamādhi. Tañca pana te sammāsamādhiṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person endowed with wrong samādhi is reborn, on the dissolution of the body, after death, in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. But you, householder, do not have such wrong samādhi. You do have, householder, right samādhi. As you contemplate this right samādhi in yourself, your painful feeling might subside right there.

‘‘Yathārūpena kho, gahapati, micchāñāṇena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpaṃ te micchāñāṇaṃ natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāñāṇaṃ. Tañca pana te sammāñāṇaṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyya.

Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person endowed with wrong knowledge is reborn, on the dissolution of the body, after death, in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. But you, householder, do not have such wrong knowledge. You do have, householder, right knowledge. As you contemplate this right knowledge in yourself, your painful feeling might subside right there.

‘‘Yathārūpāya kho, gahapati, micchāvimuttiyā samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati, tathārūpā te micchāvimutti natthi. Atthi ca kho te, gahapati, sammāvimutti. Tañca pana te sammāvimuttiṃ attani samanupassato ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambheyyā’’ti.

Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person endowed with wrong liberation is reborn, on the dissolution of the body, after death, in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. But you, householder, do not have such wrong liberation. You do have, householder, right liberation. As you contemplate this right liberation in yourself, your painful feeling might subside right there.

Atha kho anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa ṭhānaso vedanā paṭippassambhiṃsu. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati āyasmantañca sāriputtaṃ āyasmantañca ānandaṃ sakeneva thālipākena parivisi. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ bhuttāviṃ onītapattapāṇiṃ aññataraṃ nīcāsanaṃ gahetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho anāthapiṇḍikaṃ gahapatiṃ āyasmā sāriputto imāhi gāthāhi anumodi –

Then the painful feelings of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika subsided right there. Then the householder Anāthapiṇḍika personally served Venerable Sāriputta and Venerable Ānanda with his own food. When Venerable Sāriputta had eaten and removed his hand from the bowl, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika took a certain low seat and sat down to one side. As he was sitting to one side, Venerable Sāriputta exhorted the householder Anāthapiṇḍika with these verses:

‘‘Yassa saddhā tathāgate, acalā suppatiṭṭhitā;Sīlañca yassa kalyāṇaṃ, ariyakantaṃ pasaṃsitaṃ.

He whose faith in the Tathāgata is unwavering and well-established, and whose virtuous conduct is good, pleasing to the noble ones, and praised,

‘‘Saṅghe pasādo yassatthi, ujubhūtañca dassanaṃ;Adaliddoti [adaḷiddoti (sī. syā. kaṃ.)] taṃ āhu, amoghaṃ tassa jīvitaṃ.

Whoever has confidence in the Saṅgha and whose view is upright—they are called ‘not poor,’ their life is not in vain.

‘‘Tasmā saddhañca sīlañca, pasādaṃ dhammadassanaṃ;Anuyuñjetha medhāvī, saraṃ buddhānasāsana’’nti.

Therefore, the wise person, remembering the Buddhas’ teaching, should devote himself to faith, virtue, confidence, and vision of the Dhamma.

Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto anāthapiṇḍikaṃ gahapatiṃ imāhi gāthāhi anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.

Then, having exhorted the householder Anāthapiṇḍika with these verses, Venerable Sāriputta rose from his seat and departed.

Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘handa! Kuto nu tvaṃ, ānanda, āgacchasi divādivassā’’ti? ‘‘Āyasmatā, bhante, sāriputtena anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati iminā ca iminā ca ovādena ovadito’’ti. ‘‘Paṇḍito, ānanda, sāriputto; mahāpañño, ānanda, sāriputto, yatra hi nāma cattāri sotāpattiyaṅgāni dasahākārehi vibhajissatī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

Then Venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. As he sat to one side, the Blessed One said to Venerable Ānanda: “Well now, Ānanda, where do you come from in the middle of the day?” “Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika was advised by Venerable Sāriputta with this and that advice.” “Ānanda, Sāriputta is wise; Ānanda, Sāriputta has great wisdom, in that he analyzes the four factors of stream-entry in ten ways.”
