# SN 55.14 Duggatibhayasuttaṃ

## SN 55.14 The Fear of a Bad Destination

1010. ‘‘Catūhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato ariyasāvako sabbaduggatibhayaṃ samatikkanto hoti. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, catūhi dhammehi samannāgato ariyasāvako sabbaduggatibhayaṃ samatikkanto hotī’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

1010."Monks, a noble disciple endowed with four qualities has overcome all fear of a bad destination. What four? Here, monks, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha—'Indeed, the Blessed One is... the Teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.' He is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma... in the Sangha... and is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken... conducive to samādhi. Monks, a noble disciple endowed with these four qualities has overcome all fear of a bad destination." The fourth.
