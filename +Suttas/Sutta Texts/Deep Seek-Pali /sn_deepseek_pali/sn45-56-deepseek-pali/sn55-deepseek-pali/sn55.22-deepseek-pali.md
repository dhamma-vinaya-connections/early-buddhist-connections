# SN 55.22 Dutiyamahānāmasuttaṃ

## SN 55.22 The Second Discourse to Mahānāma

1018. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Atha kho mahānāmo sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idaṃ, bhante, kapilavatthu iddhañceva phītañca bāhujaññaṃ ākiṇṇamanussaṃ sambādhabyūhaṃ. So khvāhaṃ, bhante, bhagavantaṃ vā payirupāsitvā manobhāvanīye vā bhikkhū sāyanhasamayaṃ kapilavatthuṃ pavisanto; bhantenapi hatthinā samāgacchāmi; bhantenapi assena samāgacchāmi; bhantenapi rathena samāgacchāmi; bhante, napi sakaṭena samāgacchāmi; bhante, napi purisena samāgacchāmi. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, tasmiṃ samaye mussateva bhagavantaṃ ārabbha sati, mussati dhammaṃ ārabbha sati, mussati saṅghaṃ ārabbha sati. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, evaṃ hoti – ‘imamhi cāhaṃ samaye kālaṃ kareyyaṃ, kā mayhaṃ gati, ko abhisamparāyo’’’ti?

1018.Thus have I heard—at one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans in Kapilavatthu, in the Banyan Tree Park. Then Mahānāma the Sakyan approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Mahānāma the Sakyan said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, this Kapilavatthu is rich, prosperous, populous, crowded with people, and congested. When I, venerable sir, having visited the Blessed One or the esteemed monks, enter Kapilavatthu in the evening, I may encounter a royal elephant, a royal horse, a royal chariot, a cart, or a man. At that time, venerable sir, my mindfulness of the Blessed One, the Dhamma, and the Saṅgha is lost. Then, venerable sir, this thought occurs to me: ‘If I were to die at this time, what would be my destination? What would be my future state?’"

‘‘Mā bhāyi, mahānāma, mā bhāyi, mahānāma! Apāpakaṃ te maraṇaṃ bhavissati apāpikā kālaṃkiriyā. Catūhi kho, mahānāma, dhammehi samannāgato ariyasāvako nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, mahānāma, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe …pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi.

"Do not fear, Mahānāma, do not fear, Mahānāma! Your death will not be inauspicious, your passing will not be inauspicious. A noble disciple endowed with four qualities, Mahānāma, is inclined toward Nibbāna, bent toward Nibbāna, sloped toward Nibbāna. What four? Here, Mahānāma, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is... the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.' In the Dhamma... in the Saṅgha... and is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, unbroken... conducive to samādhi."

‘‘Seyyathāpi, mahānāma, rukkho pācīnaninno pācīnapoṇo pācīnapabbhāro, so mūlacchinno katamena papateyyā’’ti? ‘‘Yena, bhante, ninno yena poṇo yena pabbhāro’’ti. ‘‘Evameva kho, mahānāma, imehi catūhi dhammehi samannāgato ariyasāvako nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

"Suppose, Mahānāma, a tree was inclined to the east, sloped to the east, tended to the east. If it were cut at the root, in which direction would it fall?" "In the direction it is inclined, sloped, tended, venerable sir." "Just so, Mahānāma, a noble disciple endowed with these four qualities is inclined towards Nibbāna, sloped towards Nibbāna, tended towards Nibbāna." (Second)
