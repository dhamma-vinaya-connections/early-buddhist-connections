# SN 55.34 Paṭhamadevapadasuttaṃ

## SN 55.34 The First Discourse on Divine States

1030. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Cattārimāni, bhikkhave, devānaṃ devapadāni avisuddhānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā apariyodātānaṃ sattānaṃ pariyodapanāya.

1030.At Savatthi. "Monks, these are four divine states for the purification of impure beings, for the cleansing of uncleansed beings."

Katamāni cattāri? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Idaṃ paṭhamaṃ devānaṃ devapadaṃ avisuddhānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā apariyodātānaṃ sattānaṃ pariyodapanāya.

"What are the four? Here, monks, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha thus: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is... the Teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.' This is the first divine state for the purification of impure beings, for the cleansing of uncleansed beings."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe….

"Furthermore, monks, a noble disciple has unwavering confidence in the Dhamma... in the Saṅgha..."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Idaṃ catutthaṃ devānaṃ devapadaṃ avisuddhānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā apariyodātānaṃ sattānaṃ pariyodapanāya. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, cattāri devānaṃ devapadāni avisuddhānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā apariyodātānaṃ sattānaṃ pariyodapanāyā’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

"Furthermore, monks, a noble disciple is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, unbroken... leading to samādhi. This is the fourth divine state for the purification of impure beings, for the cleansing of uncleansed beings. These, monks, are the four divine states for the purification of impure beings, for the cleansing of uncleansed beings." The fourth.
