# SN 55.27 Dutiyaanāthapiṇḍikasuttaṃ

## SN 55.27 The Second Anāthapiṇḍika Discourse

1023. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati ābādhiko hoti dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. Atha kho anāthapiṇḍiko gahapati aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, ambho purisa, yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mama vacanena āyasmato ānandassa pāde sirasā vanda – ‘anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So āyasmato ānandassa pāde sirasā vandatī’ti. Evañca vadehi – ‘sādhu kira, bhante, āyasmā ānando yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkamatu anukampaṃ upādāyā’’’ti.

1023.At Sāvatthī. At that time the householder Anāthapiṇḍika was sick, afflicted, gravely ill. Then the householder Anāthapiṇḍika addressed a certain man: "Come, good man, go to Venerable Ānanda; on arrival, pay homage to Venerable Ānanda’s feet with your head in my name and say: 'Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is sick, afflicted, gravely ill. He pays homage to Venerable Ānanda’s feet with his head.' And say this: 'It would be good, venerable sir, if Venerable Ānanda would visit the residence of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika out of compassion.'"

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho so puriso anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa paṭissutvā yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so puriso āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘anāthapiṇḍiko, bhante, gahapati ābādhiko dukkhito bāḷhagilāno. So āyasmato ānandassa pāde sirasā vandati. Evañca vadati – ‘sādhu kira, bhante, āyasmā ānando yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkamatu anukampaṃ upādāyā’’’ti. Adhivāsesi kho āyasmā ānando tuṇhībhāvena.

"Yes, sir," that man replied to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika. Then he approached Venerable Ānanda; having approached, he paid homage to Venerable Ānanda and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, that man said to Venerable Ānanda: "Venerable sir, the householder Anāthapiṇḍika is sick, suffering, gravely ill. He pays homage with his head at the feet of Venerable Ānanda. And he says this: 'It would be good, venerable sir, if Venerable Ānanda would come to the residence of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika out of compassion.'" Venerable Ānanda consented by remaining silent.

Atha kho āyasmā ānando pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena anāthapiṇḍikassa gahapatissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho āyasmā ānando anāthapiṇḍikaṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kacci te, gahapati, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyaṃ? Kacci dukkhā vedanā paṭikkamanti, no abhikkamanti; paṭikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no abhikkamo’’ti? ‘‘Na me, bhante, khamanīyaṃ na yāpanīyaṃ. Bāḷhā me dukkhā vedanā abhikkamanti, no paṭikkamanti; abhikkamosānaṃ paññāyati, no paṭikkamo’’ti.

Then Venerable Ānanda, having dressed in the morning and taken his bowl and robe, went to the residence of the householder Anāthapiṇḍika; having approached, he sat down on the prepared seat. Sitting there, Venerable Ānanda said to the householder Anāthapiṇḍika: "I hope, householder, you are well, you are able to continue? I hope your painful feelings are subsiding, not increasing; that their subsiding is apparent, not their increasing?" "Venerable sir, I am not well, I am not able to continue. My painful feelings are increasing, not subsiding; their increase is apparent, not their subsiding."

‘‘Catūhi kho, gahapati, dhammehi samannāgatassa assutavato puthujjanassa hoti uttāso, hoti chambhitattaṃ, hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, gahapati, assutavā puthujjano buddhe appasādena samannāgato hoti. Tañca panassa buddhe appasādaṃ attani samanupassato hoti uttāso, hoti chambhitattaṃ, hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ.

Householder, an uninstructed ordinary person endowed with four qualities experiences alarm, trepidation, and fear of death regarding the next world. What four? Here, householder, an uninstructed ordinary person is endowed with lack of confidence in the Buddha. When he perceives that lack of confidence in the Buddha within himself, he experiences alarm, trepidation, and fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapati, assutavā puthujjano dhamme appasādena samannāgato hoti. Tañca panassa dhamme appasādaṃ attani samanupassato hoti uttāso, hoti chambhitattaṃ, hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ.

Furthermore, householder, an uninstructed ordinary person is endowed with lack of confidence in the Dhamma. When he perceives that lack of confidence in the Dhamma within himself, he experiences alarm, trepidation, and fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapati, assutavā puthujjano saṅghe appasādena samannāgato hoti. Tañca panassa saṅghe appasādaṃ attani samanupassato hoti uttāso, hoti chambhitattaṃ, hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ.

Furthermore, householder, an uninstructed ordinary person is endowed with lack of confidence in the Saṅgha. When he perceives that lack of confidence in the Saṅgha within himself, he experiences alarm, trepidation, and fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapati, assutavā puthujjano dussīlyena samannāgato hoti. Tañca panassa dussīlyaṃ attani samanupassato hoti uttāso, hoti chambhitattaṃ, hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ. Imehi kho, gahapati, catūhi dhammehi samannāgatassa assutavato puthujjanassa hoti uttāso, hoti chambhitattaṃ, hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ.

Furthermore, householder, an uninstructed ordinary person is endowed with immorality. When he perceives his immorality within himself, he experiences alarm, trepidation, and fear of death regarding the next world. These, householder, are the four qualities with which an uninstructed ordinary person is endowed, and because of which he experiences alarm, trepidation, and fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Catūhi kho, gahapati, dhammehi samannāgatassa sutavato ariyasāvakassa na hoti uttāso, na hoti chambhitattaṃ, na hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, gahapati, sutavā ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Tañca panassa buddhe aveccappasādaṃ attani samanupassato na hoti uttāso, na hoti chambhitattaṃ, na hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ.

Householder, an instructed noble disciple endowed with four qualities does not experience alarm, trepidation, or fear of death regarding the next world. What four? Here, householder, an instructed noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha thus: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is…the teacher of devas and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.’ When he perceives that unwavering confidence in the Buddha within himself, he does not experience alarm, trepidation, or fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapati, sutavā ariyasāvako dhamme aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo…pe… paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhīti. Tañca panassa dhamme aveccappasādaṃ attani samanupassato na hoti uttāso, na hoti chambhitattaṃ, na hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ.

Furthermore, householder, an instructed noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma thus: ‘The Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One…to be personally experienced by the wise.’ When he perceives that unwavering confidence in the Dhamma within himself, he does not experience alarm, trepidation, or fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapati, sutavā ariyasāvako saṅghe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassāti. Tañca panassa saṅghe aveccappasādaṃ attani samanupassato na hoti uttāso, na hoti chambhitattaṃ, na hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ.

Furthermore, householder, an instructed noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Saṅgha thus: ‘The Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way…the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.’ When he perceives that unwavering confidence in the Saṅgha within himself, he does not experience alarm, trepidation, or fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapati, sutavā ariyasāvako ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Tāni ca panassa ariyakantāni sīlāni attani samanupassato na hoti uttāso, na hoti chambhitattaṃ, na hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhayaṃ. Imehi kho, gahapati, catūhi dhammehi samannāgatassa sutavato ariyasāvakassa na hoti uttāso, na hoti chambhitattaṃ, na hoti samparāyikaṃ maraṇabhaya’’nti.

Furthermore, householder, an instructed noble disciple is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken…leading to samādhi. When he perceives these virtues dear to the noble ones within himself, he does not experience alarm, trepidation, or fear of death regarding the next world. These, householder, are the four qualities with which an instructed noble disciple is endowed, and because of which he does not experience alarm, trepidation, or fear of death regarding the next world.

‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhante ānanda, bhāyāmi. Kyāhaṃ bhāyissāmi! Ahañhi, bhante, buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato homi – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe aveccappasādena samannāgato homi – suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassāti. Yāni cimāni, bhante, bhagavatā gihisāmīcikāni sikkhāpadāni desitāni, nāhaṃ tesaṃ kiñci attani khaṇḍaṃ samanupassāmī’’ti. ‘‘Lābhā te, gahapati, suladdhaṃ te, gahapati! Sotāpattiphalaṃ tayā, gahapati, byākata’’nti. Sattamaṃ.

"Venerable Ānanda, I am not afraid. Why should I be afraid? For I, venerable sir, am endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha thus: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is…the teacher of devas and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.’ In the Dhamma…in the Saṅgha I am endowed with unwavering confidence thus: ‘The Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way…the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.’ And, venerable sir, as for these training rules proper for householders laid down by the Blessed One, I do not perceive any breach of them in myself.” “It is a gain for you, householder, a great gain for you! The fruit of stream-entry has been declared by you, householder.” Seventh.
