# SN 55.8 Paṭhamagiñjakāvasathasuttaṃ

## SN 55.8 The First Discourse on the Brick Hall

1004. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā ñātike viharati giñjakāvasathe. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

1004.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was staying at Nātika in the Brick Hall. Then the Venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Sāḷho nāma, bhante, bhikkhu kālaṅkato; tassa kā gati ko abhisamparāyo? Nandā nāma, bhante, bhikkhunī kālaṅkatā; tassā kā gati ko abhisamparāyo? Sudatto nāma, bhante, upāsako kālaṅkato; tassa kā gati ko abhisamparāyo? Sujātā nāma, bhante, upāsikā kālaṅkatā; tassā kā gati, ko abhisamparāyo’’ti?

“Venerable sir, a monk named Sāḷha has passed away. What is his destiny? What is his future state? Venerable sir, a nun named Nandā has passed away. What is her destiny? What is her future state? Venerable sir, a male lay follower named Sudatta has passed away. What is his destiny? What is his future state? Venerable sir, a female lay follower named Sujātā has passed away. What is her destiny? What is her future state?”

‘‘Sāḷho, ānanda, bhikkhu kālaṅkato āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihāsi. Nandā, ānanda, bhikkhunī kālaṅkatā pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā opapātikā tattha parinibbāyinī anāvattidhammā tasmā lokā. Sudatto, ānanda, upāsako kālaṅkato tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmī; sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karissati. Sujātā, ānanda, upāsikā kālaṅkatā tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā.

“Ānanda, the monk Sāḷha, having died, with the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized for himself the taintless liberation of mind and liberation by wisdom in this very life, he dwelt. The nun Nandā, having died, with the destruction of the five lower fetters, was reborn spontaneously and will attain final Nibbāna there, not being subject to returning from that world. The layman Sudatta, having died, with the destruction of three fetters and the attenuation of greed, hatred, and delusion, became a once-returner; he will return to this world only once more and make an end of suffering. The laywoman Sujātā, having died, with the destruction of three fetters, became a stream-enterer, not subject to downfall, assured, destined for enlightenment.

‘‘Anacchariyaṃ kho panetaṃ, ānanda, yaṃ manussabhūto kālaṃ kareyya; tasmiṃ tasmiṃ ce maṃ kālaṅkate upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipucchissatha. Vihesā pesā, ānanda, assa tathāgatassa. Tasmātihānanda, dhammādāsaṃ nāma dhammapariyāyaṃ desessāmi; yena samannāgato ariyasāvako ākaṅkhamāno attanāva attānaṃ byākareyya – ‘khīṇanirayomhi khīṇatiracchānayoni khīṇapettivisayo khīṇāpāyaduggativinipāto, sotāpannohamasmi avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo’’’.

“Ānanda, it is not astonishing that a human being should die; and if, each time someone has died, you were to approach the Tathāgata and ask about this matter, that would be a harassment to the Tathāgata. Therefore, Ānanda, I will teach you the Dhamma-mirror, a teaching by which a noble disciple, if he wishes, can declare of himself: ‘I am devoid of hell, devoid of the animal realm, devoid of the ghost realm, devoid of states of woe, bad destinations, and downfall. I am a stream-enterer, not subject to downfall, assured, destined for enlightenment.’”

‘‘Katamo ca so, ānanda, dhammādāso dhammapariyāyo; yena samannāgato ariyasāvako ākaṅkhamāno attanāva attānaṃ byākareyya – ‘khīṇanirayomhi khīṇatiracchānayoni khīṇapettivisayo khīṇāpāyaduggativinipāto, sotāpannohamasmi avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo’’’?

“And what, Ānanda, is that mirror of the Dhamma, that teaching by which a noble disciple, if he wishes, can declare of himself: ‘I am devoid of hell, devoid of the animal realm, devoid of the ghost realm, devoid of states of woe, bad destinations, and downfall. I am a stream-enterer, not subject to downfall, assured, destined for enlightenment’?”

‘‘Idha, ānanda, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Ayaṃ kho so, ānanda, dhammādāso dhammapariyāyo; yena samannāgato ariyasāvako ākaṅkhamāno attanāva attānaṃ byākareyya – ‘khīṇanirayomhi khīṇatiracchānayoni khīṇapettivisayo khīṇāpāyaduggativinipāto, sotāpannohamasmi avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo’’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

“Here, Ānanda, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha thus: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is… the teacher of gods and humans, the Awakened One, the Blessed One.’ He is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma… the Saṅgha… and he is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, unbroken… conducive to samādhi. This, Ānanda, is the mirror of the Dhamma, the teaching by which a noble disciple, if he wishes, can declare of himself: ‘I am devoid of hell, devoid of the animal realm, devoid of the ghost realm, devoid of states of woe, bad destinations, and downfall. I am a stream-enterer, not subject to downfall, assured, destined for enlightenment.’” The eighth.

(Tīṇipi suttantāni ekanidānāni).

(Three discourses with a single introduction.)
