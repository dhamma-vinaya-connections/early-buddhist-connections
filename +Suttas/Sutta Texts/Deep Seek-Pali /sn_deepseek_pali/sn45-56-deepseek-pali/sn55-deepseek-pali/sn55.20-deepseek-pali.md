# SN 55.20 Tatiyadevacārikasuttaṃ

## SN 55.20 The Third Discourse on Wandering Among the Devas

1016. Atha kho bhagavā – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evameva – jetavane antarahito devesu tāvatiṃsesu pāturahosi. Atha kho sambahulā tāvatiṃsakāyikā devatāyo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho tā devatāyo bhagavā etadavoca –

1016.Then the Blessed One—just as a strong man might extend his bent arm or bend his extended arm—disappeared from Jeta Grove and reappeared among the Tāvatiṃsa devas. Then many devas of the Tāvatiṃsa company approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and stood to one side. Standing to one side, the Blessed One addressed those devas thus:

‘‘Sādhu kho, āvuso, buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgamanaṃ hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgamanahetu kho, āvuso, evamidhekacce sattā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā. Sādhu kho, āvuso, dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgamanaṃ hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgamanahetu kho, āvuso, evamidhekacce sattā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā’’ti.

“Good indeed, friends, is being endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha—‘Indeed, the Blessed One is…the Teacher of devas and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.’ It is because of being endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha, friends, that some beings here are stream-enterers, no longer bound to the lower realms, certain of liberation, with enlightenment as their goal. Good indeed, friends, is being endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma…in the Saṅgha…being endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken…conducive to samādhi. It is because of being endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, friends, that some beings here are stream-enterers, no longer bound to the lower realms, certain of liberation, with enlightenment as their goal.”

‘‘Sādhu kho, mārisa, buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgamanaṃ hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgamanahetu kho, mārisa, evamayaṃ pajā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā. Sādhu kho, mārisa, dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgamanaṃ hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgamanahetu kho, mārisa, evamayaṃ pajā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

“Good indeed, venerable sir, is being endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha—‘Indeed, the Blessed One is…the Teacher of devas and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.’ It is because of being endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha, venerable sir, that this populace are stream-enterers, no longer bound to the lower realms, certain of liberation, with enlightenment as their goal. Good indeed, venerable sir, is being endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma…in the Saṅgha…being endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken…conducive to samādhi. It is because of being endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, venerable sir, that this populace are stream-enterers, no longer bound to the lower realms, certain of liberation, with enlightenment as their goal.” Tenth.

Rājakārāmavaggo dutiyo.

The Second Section: The Royal Garden.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Sahassabrāhmaṇānanda, duggati apare duve;Mittāmaccā duve vuttā, tayo ca devacārikāti.

A Thousand Brahmins, Ananda; two others on states of woe; two spoken of as friends and ministers; and three on wandering among devas.
