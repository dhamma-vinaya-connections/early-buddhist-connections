# SN 55.40 Nandiyasakkasuttaṃ

## SN 55.40 The Discourse to Nandiya the Sakyan

1036. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Atha kho nandiyo sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho nandiyo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yasseva nu kho, bhante, ariyasāvakassa cattāri sotāpattiyaṅgāni sabbena sabbaṃ sabbathā sabbaṃ natthi sveva nu kho, bhante, ariyasāvako pamādavihārī’’ti.

1036.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Banyan Park. Then Nandiya the Sakyan approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Nandiya the Sakyan said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, if a noble disciple entirely and completely lacks the four factors of stream-entry, would that noble disciple, venerable sir, dwell heedlessly?"

‘‘‘Yassa kho, nandiya, cattāri sotāpattiyaṅgāni sabbena sabbaṃ sabbathā sabbaṃ natthi tamahaṃ bāhiro puthujjanapakkhe ṭhito’ti vadāmi. Api ca, nandiya, yathā ariyasāvako pamādavihārī ceva hoti, appamādavihārī ca taṃ suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho nandiyo sakko bhagavato paccassosi. Bhagavā etadavoca –

"Nandiya, one who lacks the four factors of stream-entry in every respect and in every way, I say that one stands outside, among the party of ordinary people. However, Nandiya, listen and pay close attention, I will explain how a noble disciple dwells both heedlessly and heedfully." "Yes, Bhante," Nandiya the Sakyan replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Kathañca, nandiya, ariyasāvako pamādavihārī hoti? Idha nandiya, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. So tena buddhe aveccappasādena santuṭṭho na uttari vāyamati divā pavivekāya, rattiṃ paṭisallānāya. Tassa evaṃ pamattassa viharato pāmojjaṃ na hoti. Pāmojje asati, pīti na hoti. Pītiyā asati, passaddhi na hoti. Passaddhiyā asati, dukkhaṃ viharati. Dukkhino cittaṃ na samādhiyati. Asamāhite citte dhammā na pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ apātubhāvā pamādavihārī tveva saṅkhyaṃ gacchati.

"And how, Nandiya, does a noble disciple dwell in heedlessness? Here, Nandiya, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is…the teacher of gods and humans, enlightened, blessed.’ Being satisfied with that unwavering confidence in the Buddha, he does not strive further for solitude by day and retreat by night. Dwelling thus in heedlessness, joy does not arise. Without joy, rapture does not arise. Without rapture, tranquility does not arise. Without tranquility, he dwells in suffering. Suffering, his mind does not become collected. When the mind is not collected, mental qualities do not appear. Because mental qualities do not appear, he is reckoned as dwelling in heedlessness."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, nandiya, ariyasāvako dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. So tehi ariyakantehi sīlehi santuṭṭho na uttari vāyamati divā pavivekāya rattiṃ paṭisallānāya. Tassa evaṃ pamattassa viharato pāmojjaṃ na hoti. Pāmojje asati, pīti na hoti. Pītiyā asati, passaddhi na hoti. Passaddhiyā asati, dukkhaṃ viharati. Dukkhino cittaṃ na samādhiyati. Asamāhite citte dhammā na pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ apātubhāvā pamādavihārī tveva saṅkhyaṃ gacchati. Evaṃ kho, nandiya, ariyasāvako pamādavihārī hoti.

"Furthermore, Nandiya, a noble disciple is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, unbroken... leading to samādhi. Content with those virtues dear to the noble ones, he does not strive further for solitude by day and retreat by night. Dwelling thus in heedlessness, joy does not arise. Without joy, rapture does not arise. Without rapture, tranquility does not arise. Without tranquility, he dwells in suffering. Suffering, his mind does not become collected. When the mind is not collected, mental qualities do not appear. Because mental qualities do not appear, he is reckoned as dwelling in heedlessness. Thus, Nandiya, a noble disciple dwells in heedlessness."

‘‘Kathañca, nandiya, ariyasāvako appamādavihārī hoti? Idha, nandiya, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. So tena buddhe aveccappasādena asantuṭṭho uttari vāyamati divā pavivekāya rattiṃ paṭisallānāya. Tassa evaṃ appamattassa viharato pāmojjaṃ jāyati. Pamuditassa pīti jāyati. Pītimanassa kāyo passambhati. Passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ vediyati. Sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati. Samāhite citte dhammā pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ pātubhāvā appamādavihārī tveva saṅkhyaṃ gacchati.

"And how, Nandiya, does a noble disciple dwell heedfully? Here, Nandiya, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha—'Indeed, the Blessed One is... the Teacher of gods and humans, awakened, the Blessed One.' Not content with that unwavering confidence in the Buddha, he strives further for solitude by day and retreat by night. As he dwells thus heedfully, joy arises. When gladdened, rapture arises. With his mind enraptured, his body becomes tranquil. With his body tranquil, he experiences happiness. When happy, his mind becomes collected. When the mind is collected, mental qualities appear. Because mental qualities appear, he is reckoned as dwelling heedfully."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, nandiya, ariyasāvako dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. So tehi ariyakantehi sīlehi asantuṭṭho uttari vāyamati divā pavivekāya rattiṃ paṭisallānāya. Tassa evaṃ appamattassa viharato pāmojjaṃ jāyati. Pamuditassa pīti jāyati. Pītimanassa kāyo passambhati. Passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ vediyati. Sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati. Samāhite citte dhammā pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ pātubhāvā appamādavihārī tveva saṅkhyaṃ gacchati. Evaṃ kho, nandiya, ariyasāvako appamādavihārī hotī’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

"Furthermore, Nandiya, a noble disciple is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken… leading to samādhi. Not content with those virtues dear to the noble ones, he strives further for solitude by day and retreat by night. As he dwells thus heedfully, joy arises. When gladdened, rapture arises. With his mind enraptured, his body becomes tranquil. With his body tranquil, he experiences happiness. When happy, his mind becomes collected. When the mind is collected, mental qualities appear. Because mental qualities appear, he is reckoned as dwelling heedfully. Thus, Nandiya, a noble disciple dwells heedfully." Tenth.

Puññābhisandavaggo catuttho.

The Fourth Chapter: The Outpouring of Merit.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Abhisandā tayo vuttā, duve devapadāni ca;Sabhāgataṃ mahānāmo, vassaṃ kāḷī ca nandiyāti.

Three kinds of overflows are declared, and two divine footpaths; Similarity, Mahānāma, the rains, Kālī, and Nandiyā.
