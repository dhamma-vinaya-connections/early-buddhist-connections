# SN 55.13 Ānandattherasuttaṃ

## SN 55.13 The Discourse on Venerable Ānanda

1009. Ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā ca ānando āyasmā ca sāriputto sāvatthiyaṃ viharanti jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā ānandena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘katinaṃ kho, āvuso ānanda, dhammānaṃ pahānā, katinaṃ dhammānaṃ samannāgamanahetu, evamayaṃ pajā bhagavatā byākatā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā’’ti? ‘‘Catunnaṃ kho, āvuso, dhammānaṃ pahānā, catunnaṃ dhammānaṃ samannāgamanahetu, evamayaṃ pajā bhagavatā byākatā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā’’ti.

1009.At one time the Venerable Ānanda and the Venerable Sāriputta were staying at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery. Then, in the evening, the Venerable Sāriputta arose from meditation and approached the Venerable Ānanda. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Venerable Ānanda. Having exchanged courteous and amiable talk, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the Venerable Ānanda: “Friend Ānanda, by abandoning how many qualities, and by possessing how many qualities, has this population been declared by the Blessed One to be a stream-enterer, not subject to falling away, assured, headed for enlightenment?” “Friend, by abandoning four qualities, and by possessing four qualities, the Blessed One has declared this population to be a stream-enterer, not subject to falling away, assured, headed for enlightenment.”

‘‘Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Yathārūpena kho, āvuso, buddhe appasādena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tathārūpassa buddhe appasādo na hoti. Yathārūpena ca kho, āvuso, buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato sutavā ariyasāvako kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati tathārūpassa buddhe aveccappasādo hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’’ti.

“Which four? Friend, the kind of lack of confidence in the Buddha that an uninstructed ordinary person has, due to which, on the dissolution of the body, after death, they are reborn in the states of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell—that kind of lack of confidence in the Buddha does not exist. And the kind of unwavering confidence in the Buddha that an instructed noble disciple has, due to which, on the dissolution of the body, after death, they are reborn in a good destination, a heavenly world—that kind of unwavering confidence in the Buddha exists: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is… teacher of gods and humans, the Awakened One, the Blessed One.’

‘‘Yathārūpena ca kho, āvuso, dhamme appasādena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tathārūpassa dhamme appasādo na hoti. Yathārūpena ca kho, āvuso, dhamme aveccappasādena samannāgato sutavā ariyasāvako kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati tathārūpassa dhamme aveccappasādo hoti – svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo…pe… viññūhīti.

“Friend, an uninstructed ordinary person is endowed with a kind of lack of confidence in the Dhamma, due to which, after the breakup of the body, after death, they are reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. But that kind of lack of confidence in the Dhamma does not exist. And an instructed noble disciple is endowed with a kind of unwavering confidence in the Dhamma, due to which, after the breakup of the body, after death, they are reborn in a good destination, a heavenly world. That kind of unwavering confidence in the Dhamma exists: ‘The Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One… to be personally experienced by the wise.’

‘‘Yathārūpena ca kho, āvuso, saṅghe appasādena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tathārūpassa saṅghe appasādo na hoti. Yathārūpena ca kho, āvuso, saṅghe aveccappasādena samannāgato sutavā ariyasāvako kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati tathārūpassa saṅghe aveccappasādo hoti – suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassāti.

“Friend, an uninstructed ordinary person is endowed with a kind of lack of confidence toward the Saṅgha such that, on the dissolution of the body, after death, they are reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell. That kind of lack of confidence toward the Saṅgha does not exist. But friend, an instructed noble disciple is endowed with a kind of unwavering confidence in the Saṅgha such that, on the dissolution of the body, after death, they are reborn in a good destination, a heavenly world. That kind of unwavering confidence in the Saṅgha exists: ‘The Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way… the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.’

‘‘Yathārūpena ca kho, āvuso, dussīlyena samannāgato assutavā puthujjano kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tathārūpassa dussīlyaṃ na hoti. Yathārūpehi ca kho, āvuso, ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato sutavā ariyasāvako kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati tathārūpāni ariyakantāni sīlāni honti akhaṇḍāni…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikāni. Imesaṃ kho, āvuso, catunnaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānā imesaṃ catunnaṃ dhammānaṃ samannāgamanahetu evamayaṃ pajā bhagavatā byākatā sotāpannā avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

“Friend, the kind of unvirtuous conduct possessed by an uninstructed ordinary person, on account of which, at the breakup of the body, after death, they are reborn in the states of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell—that kind of unvirtuous conduct is not in them. And the kind of virtues dear to the noble ones possessed by an instructed noble disciple, on account of which, at the breakup of the body, after death, they are reborn in a good destination, a heavenly world—those virtues dear to the noble ones are in them, unbroken… conducive to samādhi. It is through the abandoning of these four qualities and the possessing of these four qualities that this population is declared by the Blessed One to be a stream-enterer, not subject to falling back, assured, destined for enlightenment.” The third.
