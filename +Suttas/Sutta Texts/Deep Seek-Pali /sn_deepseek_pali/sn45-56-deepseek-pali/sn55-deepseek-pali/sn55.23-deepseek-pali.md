# SN 55.23 Godhasakkasuttaṃ

## SN 55.23 The Discourse to Godha the Sakyan

1019. Kapilavatthunidānaṃ. Atha kho mahānāmo sakko yena godhā sakko tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā godhaṃ sakkaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘katihi [katīhi (pī. ka.) rūpasiddhi oloketabbā] tvaṃ, godhe, dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāsi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇa’’nti?

1019.At Kapilavatthu. Then Mahānāma the Sakyan approached Godha the Sakyan and, having approached, said to him: "Godha, with how many qualities are you acquainted with a stream-enterer, one who is not subject to falling into the states of misery, assured, destined for enlightenment?"

‘‘Tīhi khvāhaṃ, mahānāma, dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇaṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Idha, mahānāma, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassāti. Imehi khvāhaṃ, mahānāma, tīhi dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇaṃ.

"Mahānāma, I recognize a person as a stream-enterer, one who is not subject to falling away, assured, destined for enlightenment, when they are endowed with three qualities. What three? Here, Mahānāma, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is... the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.' He is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma... in the Saṅgha: 'The Saṅgha of the Blessed One's disciples is practicing the good way... the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.' By these three qualities, Mahānāma, I recognize a person as a stream-enterer, one who is not subject to falling away, assured, destined for enlightenment."

‘‘Tvaṃ pana, mahānāma, katihi dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāsi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇa’’nti? ‘‘Catūhi khvāhaṃ, godhe, dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇaṃ. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, godhe, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Imehi khvāhaṃ, godhe, catūhi dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇa’’nti.

"But you, Mahānāma, with how many qualities do you recognize a person as a stream-enterer, one who is not subject to falling away, assured, destined for enlightenment?""Godha, I recognize a person as a stream-enterer, one who is not subject to falling away, assured, destined for enlightenment, when they are endowed with four qualities. What four? Here, Godha, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha thus: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is...the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.' In the Dhamma...in the Saṅgha...and is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, unbroken...conducive to samādhi. By these four qualities, Godha, I recognize a person as a stream-enterer, one who is not subject to falling away, assured, destined for enlightenment."

‘‘Āgamehi tvaṃ, mahānāma, āgamehi tvaṃ, mahānāma! Bhagavāva etaṃ jāneyya etehi dhammehi samannāgataṃ vā asamannāgataṃ vā’’ti. ‘‘Āyāma, godhe, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocessāmā’’ti. Atha kho mahānāmo sakko godhā ca sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

"Wait, Mahānāma, wait, Mahānāma! The Blessed One himself would know whether one is endowed with these qualities or not." "Let us go, Godha, let us approach the Blessed One; having approached, we will report this matter to the Blessed One." Then Mahānāma the Sakyan and Godha the Sakyan approached the Blessed One; having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Mahānāma the Sakyan said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Idhāhaṃ, bhante, yena godhā sakko tenupasaṅkamiṃ; upasaṅkamitvā godhaṃ sakkaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘katihi tvaṃ, godhe, dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāsi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇaṃ’? Evaṃ vutte, bhante, godhā sakko maṃ etadavoca –

"Here, venerable sir, I approached Godha the Sakyan and said to him: 'Godha, with how many qualities are you acquainted regarding a stream-enterer, one who is not liable to fall, fixed in destiny, with enlightenment as their destination?' When this was said, venerable sir, Godha the Sakyan said to me:

‘‘Tīhi khvāhaṃ, mahānāma, dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇaṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Idha, mahānāma, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassāti. Imehi khvāhaṃ, mahānāma, tīhi dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇaṃ. Tvaṃ pana, mahānāma, katamehi dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāsi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇa’’nti?

'Mahānāma, I recognize a stream-enterer—one who is not subject to falling away, who is assured, destined for enlightenment—as endowed with three qualities. What three? Here, Mahānāma, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha: "Indeed, the Blessed One is... the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One." They are endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma... and in the Saṅgha: "The Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way... the unsurpassed field of merit for the world." Mahānāma, I recognize a stream-enterer—one who is not subject to falling away, who is assured, destined for enlightenment—as endowed with these three qualities. But you, Mahānāma, by what qualities do you recognize such a stream-enterer?'

‘‘Evaṃ vuttāhaṃ, bhante, godhaṃ sakkaṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘catūhi khvāhaṃ, godhe, dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇaṃ. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, godhe, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Imehi khvāhaṃ, godhe, catūhi dhammehi samannāgataṃ sotāpannapuggalaṃ ājānāmi avinipātadhammaṃ niyataṃ sambodhiparāyaṇa’’’nti.

"When this was said, venerable sir, I said to Godhā the Sakyan: 'Godhā, I recognize a stream-enterer—one who is not subject to falling away, who is assured, destined for enlightenment—as endowed with four qualities. What four? Here, Godhā, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha thus: "Indeed, the Blessed One is... the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One." They are endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma... and in the Saṅgha... and they are endowed with virtues that are dear to the noble ones—unbroken... conducive to samādhi. Godhā, I recognize a stream-enterer—one who is not subject to falling away, who is assured, destined for enlightenment—as endowed with these four qualities.'"

‘‘Evaṃ vutte, bhante, godhā sakko maṃ etadavoca – ‘āgamehi tvaṃ, mahānāma, āgamehi tvaṃ, mahānāma! Bhagavāva etaṃ jāneyya etehi dhammehi samannāgataṃ vā asamannāgataṃ vā’’’ti. ‘‘Idha, bhante, kocideva dhammo samuppādo uppajjeyya, ekato assa bhagavā ekato bhikkhusaṅgho ca. Yeneva bhagavā tenevāhaṃ assaṃ. Evaṃ pasannaṃ maṃ, bhante, bhagavā dhāretu. Idha, bhante, kocideva dhammo samuppādo uppajjeyya, ekato assa bhagavā ekato bhikkhusaṅgho bhikkhunisaṅgho ca. Yeneva bhagavā tenevāhaṃ assaṃ. Evaṃ pasannaṃ maṃ, bhante, bhagavā dhāretu. Idha, bhante, kocideva dhammo samuppādo uppajjeyya, ekato assa bhagavā ekato bhikkhusaṅgho bhikkhunisaṅgho ca upāsakā ca. Yeneva bhagavā tenevāhaṃ assaṃ. Evaṃ pasannaṃ maṃ, bhante, bhagavā dhāretu. Idha, bhante, kocideva dhammo samuppādo uppajjeyya, ekato assa bhagavā ekato bhikkhusaṅgho bhikkhunisaṅgho upāsakā upāsikāyo ca. Yeneva bhagavā tenevāhaṃ assaṃ. Evaṃ pasannaṃ maṃ, bhante, bhagavā dhāretu. Idha, bhante, kocideva dhammo samuppādo uppajjeyya, ekato assa bhagavā ekato bhikkhusaṅgho bhikkhunisaṅgho upāsakā upāsikāyo sadevako ca loko samārako sabrahmako sassamaṇabrāhmaṇī pajā sadevamanussā. Yeneva bhagavā tenevāhaṃ assaṃ. Evaṃ pasannaṃ maṃ, bhante, bhagavā dhāretū’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃvādī tvaṃ, godhe, mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ kiṃ vadesī’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃvādāhaṃ, bhante, mahānāmaṃ sakkaṃ na kiñci vadāmi, aññatra kalyāṇā aññatra kusalā’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

“When this was said, venerable sir, Godha the Sakyan said this to me: ‘Wait, Mahānāma, wait, Mahānāma! Only the Blessed One would know whether one is endowed with these qualities or not.’” “Here, venerable sir, if any occasion for a dispute were to arise, with the Blessed One on one side and the Saṅgha of monks on the other, I would be on the side of the Blessed One. May the Blessed One remember me as one with such confidence. Here, venerable sir, if any occasion for a dispute were to arise, with the Blessed One on one side and the Saṅgha of monks and nuns on the other, I would be on the side of the Blessed One. May the Blessed One remember me as one with such confidence. Here, venerable sir, if any occasion for a dispute were to arise, with the Blessed One on one side and the Saṅgha of monks, nuns, and male lay followers on the other, I would be on the side of the Blessed One. May the Blessed One remember me as one with such confidence. Here, venerable sir, if any occasion for a dispute were to arise, with the Blessed One on one side and the Saṅgha of monks, nuns, male and female lay followers on the other, I would be on the side of the Blessed One. May the Blessed One remember me as one with such confidence. Here, venerable sir, if any occasion for a dispute were to arise, with the Blessed One on one side and the Saṅgha of monks, nuns, male and female lay followers, the world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its gods and humans on the other, I would be on the side of the Blessed One. May the Blessed One remember me as one with such confidence.” “Godha, when you speak thus to Mahānāma the Sakyan, what do you say?” “Venerable sir, when I speak thus to Mahānāma the Sakyan, I say nothing other than what is good, nothing other than what is wholesome.”
