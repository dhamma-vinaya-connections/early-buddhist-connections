# SN 55.36 Devasabhāgasuttaṃ

## SN 55.36 The Discourse on Sharing with Deities

1032. ‘‘Catūhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgataṃ attamanā devā sabhāgataṃ kathenti. Katamehi catūhi? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato hoti – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Yā tā devatā buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgatā ito cutā tatrūpapannā tāsaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘yathārūpena kho mayaṃ buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgatā tato cutā idhūpapannā, ariyasāvakopi tathārūpena buddhe aveccappasādena samannāgato ehīti devānaṃ santike’’’ti.

1032.“Monks, the deities, pleased, declare him to be a sharer in their company, one endowed with four qualities. What four? Here, monks, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is… the Teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.’ Those deities who, endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha, have passed away from here and been reborn there, think thus: ‘Just as we were endowed with unwavering confidence in the Buddha, having passed away from there and been reborn here, so too is this noble disciple endowed with such confidence in the Buddha. Come, let him approach the company of the deities!’”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato hoti akhaṇḍehi…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikehi. Yā tā devatā ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgatā ito cutā tatrūpapannā tāsaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘yathārūpehi kho mayaṃ ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgatā tato cutā idhūpapannā, ariyasāvakopi tathārūpehi ariyakantehi sīlehi samannāgato ehīti devānaṃ santike’ti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, catūhi dhammehi samannāgataṃ attamanā devā sabhāgataṃ kathentī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“Furthermore, monks, a noble disciple is endowed with unwavering confidence in the Dhamma… in the Saṅgha… and is endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken… leading to samādhi. Those deities who, endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones, have passed away from here and been reborn there, think thus: ‘Just as we were endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones and thus passed away and been reborn here, so too is this noble disciple endowed with virtues dear to the noble ones. Come, let him approach the company of the deities!’ Monks, the deities, pleased, declare him to be a sharer in their company, one endowed with these four qualities.” Sixth.
