# SN 55.16 Paṭhamamittāmaccasuttaṃ

## SN 55.16 The Discourse on the First Friends and Advisors

1012. ‘‘Ye te, bhikkhave, anukampeyyātha, ye ca sotabbaṃ maññeyyuṃ – mittā vā amaccā vā ñātī vā sālohitā vā – te, bhikkhave, catūsu sotāpattiyaṅgesu samādapetabbā, nivesetabbā, patiṭṭhāpetabbā. Katamesu catūsu? Buddhe aveccappasāde samādapetabbā, nivesetabbā, patiṭṭhāpetabbā – itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavāti. Dhamme…pe… saṅghe…pe… ariyakantesu sīlesu samādapetabbā, nivesetabbā, patiṭṭhāpetabbā akhaṇḍesu…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikesu. Ye te, bhikkhave, anukampeyyātha, ye ca sotabbaṃ maññeyyuṃ – mittā vā amaccā vā ñātī vā sālohitā vā – te, bhikkhave, imesu catūsu sotāpattiyaṅgesu samādapetabbā, nivesetabbā, patiṭṭhāpetabbā’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

1012."Monks, those for whom you have compassion, and who think it worth listening to—whether friends, advisors, relatives, or family—should be encouraged, settled, and established in the four factors of stream-entry. In which four? They should be encouraged, settled, and established in unwavering confidence in the Buddha: ‘That Blessed One is such … the Teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.’ They should be encouraged, settled, and established in unwavering confidence in the Dhamma … They should be encouraged, settled, and established in unwavering confidence in the Sangha … They should be encouraged, settled, and established in virtues dear to the noble ones—unbroken … conducive to samādhi. Monks, those for whom you have compassion, and who think it worth listening to—whether friends, advisors, relatives, or family—should be encouraged, settled, and established in these four factors of stream-entry.” The sixth.
