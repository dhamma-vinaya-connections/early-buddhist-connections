# SN 52.7 Taṇhakkhayasuttaṃ

## SN 52.7 The Discourse on the Ending of Craving.

905. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Tatra kho āyasmā anuruddho bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘āvuso bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Āvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato anuruddhassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā anuruddho etadavoca –

905.At Sāvatthī. Thereupon, Venerable Anuruddha addressed the bhikkhus, saying: “Friends, bhikkhus!” “Friend,” those bhikkhus replied to Venerable Anuruddha. Venerable Anuruddha said this:

‘‘Cattārome, āvuso, satipaṭṭhānā bhāvitā bahulīkatā taṇhakkhayāya saṃvattanti. Katame cattāro? Idhāvuso, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati…pe… vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ – ime kho, āvuso, cattāro satipaṭṭhānā bhāvitā bahulīkatā taṇhakkhayāya saṃvattantī’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

“Friends, these four foundations of mindfulness, when developed and cultivated, lead to the destruction of craving. Which four? Here, friends, a bhikkhu ![[Satipatthanas]]. These four foundations of mindfulness, friends, when developed and cultivated, lead to the destruction of craving.” The seventh.
