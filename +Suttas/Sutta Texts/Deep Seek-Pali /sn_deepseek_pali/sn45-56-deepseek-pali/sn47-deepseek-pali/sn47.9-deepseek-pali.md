# SN 47.9 Gilānasuttaṃ

## SN 47.9 The Sick Person Discourse

375. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati veḷuvagāmake [beluvagāmake (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)]. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘etha tumhe, bhikkhave, samantā vesāliyā yathāmittaṃ yathāsandiṭṭhaṃ yathāsambhattaṃ vassaṃ upetha. Idhevāhaṃ veḷuvagāmake vassaṃ upagacchāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissutvā samantā vesāliyā yathāmittaṃ yathāsandiṭṭhaṃ yathāsambhattaṃ vassaṃ upagacchuṃ. Bhagavā pana tattheva veḷuvagāmake vassaṃ upagacchi [upagañchi (sī. pī.)].

375.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Vesālī in the Veḷuvagāma village. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Come, bhikkhus, all around Vesālī, enter the rains retreat according to your friends, acquaintances, and those with whom you are familiar. I shall enter the rains retreat right here in the Veḷuvagāma village.” “Yes, Venerable Sir,” those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. Then they went all around Vesālī and entered the rains retreat according to their friends, acquaintances, and those with whom they were familiar. But the Blessed One entered the rains retreat right there in the Veḷuvagāma village.

Atha kho bhagavato vassūpagatassa kharo ābādho uppajji, bāḷhā vedanā vattanti māraṇantikā. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā sato sampajāno adhivāsesi avihaññamāno. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘na kho me taṃ patirūpaṃ, yohaṃ anāmantetvā upaṭṭhāke anapaloketvā bhikkhusaṅghaṃ parinibbāyeyyaṃ. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ imaṃ ābādhaṃ vīriyena paṭipaṇāmetvā jīvitasaṅkhāraṃ adhiṭṭhāya vihareyya’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā taṃ ābādhaṃ vīriyena paṭipaṇāmetvā jīvitasaṅkhāraṃ adhiṭṭhāya vihāsi. (Atha kho bhagavato so ābādho paṭippassambhi) [( ) dī. ni. 2.164 dissati].

Then, when the Blessed One had entered the rains retreat, a severe illness arose in him, with sharp, deadly pains occurring. Yet the Blessed One endured it mindfully and clearly comprehending, without being overwhelmed. Then it occurred to the Blessed One: “It would not be fitting for me to attain final Nibbāna without informing my attendants and taking leave of the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Suppose I were to repel this illness by means of effort, and resolving to maintain the life-continuing volitions, continue to live.” Then the Blessed One repelled that illness by means of effort, and resolving to maintain the life-continuing volitions, continued to live. (Then that illness of the Blessed One subsided).

Atha kho bhagavā gilānā vuṭṭhito [gilānavuṭṭhito (saddanīti)] aciravuṭṭhito gelaññā vihārā nikkhamitvā vihārapacchāyāyaṃ [vihārapacchāchāyāyaṃ (bahūsu)] paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘diṭṭho me, bhante, bhagavato phāsu; diṭṭhaṃ, bhante, bhagavato khamanīyaṃ; diṭṭhaṃ, bhante, bhagavato yāpanīyaṃ. Api ca me, bhante, madhurakajāto viya kāyo, disāpi me na pakkhāyanti, dhammāpi maṃ nappaṭibhanti bhagavato gelaññena. Api ca me, bhante, ahosi kācideva assāsamattā – ‘na tāva bhagavā parinibbāyissati, na yāva bhagavā bhikkhusaṅghaṃ ārabbha kiñcideva udāharatī’’’ti.

Then the Blessed One, having risen from his sickness, soon after recovering from his illness, came out from the dwelling and sat down on a prepared seat in the shade of the dwelling. Then Venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Venerable Ānanda said this to the Blessed One: “I have seen the Blessed One at ease, venerable sir; I have seen him bear up, venerable sir; I have seen him keep going, venerable sir. But, venerable sir, my body feels as if it were drugged, I am disoriented, and the teachings do not come to mind because of the Blessed One’s illness. Yet, venerable sir, I had some slight consolation: ‘The Blessed One will not attain final Nibbāna until he has said something about the community of bhikkhus.’”

‘‘Kiṃ pana dāni, ānanda, bhikkhusaṅgho mayi paccāsīsati [paccāsiṃsati (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)]? Desito, ānanda, mayā dhammo anantaraṃ abāhiraṃ karitvā. Natthānanda, tathāgatassa dhammesu ācariyamuṭṭhi. Yassa nūna, ānanda, evamassa – ‘ahaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ pariharissāmī’ti vā, ‘mamuddesiko bhikkhusaṅgho’ti vā, so nūna, ānanda, bhikkhusaṅghaṃ ārabbha kiñcideva udāhareyya. Tathāgatassa kho, ānanda, na evaṃ hoti – ‘ahaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ pariharissāmī’ti vā, ‘mamuddesiko bhikkhusaṅgho’ti vā. Sa kiṃ [so nūna (sī. pī.)], ānanda, tathāgato bhikkhusaṅghaṃ ārabbha kiñcideva udāharissati! Etarahi kho panāhaṃ, ānanda, jiṇṇo vuddho mahallako addhagato vayoanuppatto. Āsītiko me vayo vattati. Seyyathāpi, ānanda, jajjarasakaṭaṃ [jarasakaṭaṃ (sabbattha)] veḷamissakena [vegamissakena (sī.), veḷumissakena (syā. kaṃ.), vedhamissakena (pī. ka.), vekhamissakena (ka.)] yāpeti; evameva kho, ānanda, vedhamissakena maññe tathāgatassa kāyo yāpeti.

“But now, Ānanda, what further does the Saṅgha expect from me? Ānanda, I have taught the Dhamma without making any distinction between inner and outer. There is no closed fist of the teacher with regard to the teachings, Ānanda. Whoever might think, ‘I will lead the Saṅgha,’ or ‘The Saṅgha is under my direction,’ it is he who should say something about the Saṅgha. But, Ānanda, the Tathāgata does not think, ‘I will lead the Saṅgha,’ or ‘The Saṅgha is under my direction.’ So why should the Tathāgata say anything about the Saṅgha? Now, Ānanda, I am old, aged, burdened with years, advanced in life, having reached old age. My age is now eighty. Just as, Ānanda, an old cart is kept going with the help of straps, so too, Ānanda, the Tathāgata’s body is, it seems, kept going with the help of straps.”

‘‘Yasmiṃ, ānanda, samaye tathāgato sabbanimittānaṃ amanasikārā ekaccānaṃ vedanānaṃ nirodhā animittaṃ cetosamādhiṃ upasampajja viharati, phāsutaro [phāsutaraṃ (sabbattha)], ānanda, tasmiṃ samaye tathāgatassa kāyo hoti [tathāgatassa hoti (bahūsu)]. Tasmātihānanda, attadīpā viharatha attasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā, dhammadīpā dhammasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā.

“Ānanda, at whatever time the Tathāgata, not attending to all signs, through the cessation of certain feelings, attains to and abides in the signless samādhi of mind, at that time, Ānanda, the Tathāgata’s body is more comfortable. Therefore, Ānanda, dwell as islands unto yourselves, as refuges unto yourselves, seeking no external refuge; with the Dhamma as your island, the Dhamma as your refuge, seeking no external refuge.”

‘‘Kathañcānanda, bhikkhu attadīpo viharati attasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo, dhammadīpo dhammasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo? Idhānanda, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Evaṃ kho, ānanda, bhikkhu attadīpo viharati attasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo, dhammadīpo dhammasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo. Ye hi keci, ānanda, etarahi vā mamaccaye vā attadīpā viharissanti attasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā, dhammadīpā dhammasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā; tamatagge mete, ānanda, bhikkhū bhavissanti ye keci sikkhākāmā’’ti. Navamaṃ.

“And how, Ānanda, does a bhikkhu dwell as an island unto himself, a refuge unto himself, with no other refuge; as an island with the Dhamma, a refuge with the Dhamma, with no other refuge? Here, Ānanda, a bhikkhu ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. Thus, Ānanda, a bhikkhu dwells as an island unto himself, a refuge unto himself, with no other refuge; as an island with the Dhamma, a refuge with the Dhamma, with no other refuge. Those bhikkhus, Ānanda, who now or after my passing dwell as islands unto themselves, refuges unto themselves, with no other refuge; as islands with the Dhamma, refuges with the Dhamma, with no other refuge—those bhikkhus of mine, Ānanda, will be the foremost if they are desirous of training.” The ninth.
