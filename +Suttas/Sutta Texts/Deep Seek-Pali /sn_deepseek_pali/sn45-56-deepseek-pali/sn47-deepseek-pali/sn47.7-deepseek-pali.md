# SN 47.7 Makkaṭasuttaṃ

## SN 47.7 The Monkey Discourse

373. ‘‘Atthi, bhikkhave, himavato pabbatarājassa duggā visamā desā, yattha neva makkaṭānaṃ cārī na manussānaṃ. Atthi, bhikkhave, himavato pabbatarājassa duggā visamā desā, yattha makkaṭānañhi kho cārī, na manussānaṃ. Atthi, bhikkhave, himavato pabbatarājassa samā bhūmibhāgā ramaṇīyā, yattha makkaṭānañceva cārī manussānañca. Tatra, bhikkhave, luddā makkaṭavīthīsu lepaṃ oḍḍenti makkaṭānaṃ bādhanāya.

373.“Bhikkhus, there are in the Himalayas, the king of mountains, difficult, uneven regions where neither monkeys nor humans frequent. There are, bhikkhus, in the Himalayas, the king of mountains, difficult, uneven regions where monkeys frequent, but not humans. There are, bhikkhus, in the Himalayas, the king of mountains, level and delightful areas where both monkeys and humans frequent. There, bhikkhus, hunters spread sticky sap on monkey trails to harm the monkeys.

‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, ye te makkaṭā abālajātikā alolajātikā, te taṃ lepaṃ disvā ārakā parivajjanti. Yo pana so hoti makkaṭo bālajātiko lolajātiko, so taṃ lepaṃ upasaṅkamitvā hatthena gaṇhāti. So tattha bajjhati. ‘Hatthaṃ mocessāmī’ti dutiyena hatthena gaṇhāti. So tattha bajjhati. ‘Ubho hatthe mocessāmī’ti pādena gaṇhāti. So tattha bajjhati. ‘Ubho hatthe mocessāmi pādañcā’ti dutiyena pādena gaṇhāti. So tattha bajjhati. ‘Ubho hatthe mocessāmi pāde cā’ti tuṇḍena gaṇhāti. So tattha bajjhati. Evañhi so, bhikkhave, makkaṭo pañcoḍḍito thunaṃ seti anayaṃ āpanno byasanaṃ āpanno yathākāmakaraṇīyo luddassa. Tamenaṃ, bhikkhave, luddo vijjhitvā tasmiṃyeva kaṭṭhakataṅgāre [tasmiṃyeva makkaṭaṃ uddharitvā (sī. syā.)] avassajjetvā yena kāmaṃ pakkamati. Evaṃ so taṃ, bhikkhave, hoti yo agocare carati paravisaye.

“Now, bhikkhus, those monkeys who are not childish or reckless, upon seeing that sticky sap, avoid it from afar. But a childish and reckless monkey approaches that sticky sap and grabs it with one hand. He gets stuck there. Thinking, ‘I’ll free my hand,’ he grabs it with the other hand. He gets stuck there. Thinking, ‘I’ll free both hands,’ he grabs it with a foot. He gets stuck there. Thinking, ‘I’ll free both hands and a foot,’ he grabs it with the other foot. He gets stuck there. Thinking, ‘I’ll free both hands and both feet,’ he grabs it with his snout. He gets stuck there. Thus, bhikkhus, that monkey, caught in five places, lies there groaning and spread-eagled, having met with disaster, having met with ruin, subject to the hunter’s whim. The hunter pierces him, drops him right there onto a bed of charcoal embers, and then departs wherever he pleases. Thus it is, bhikkhus, with one who wanders into an unsuitable range, the territory of others.”

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, mā agocare carittha paravisaye. Agocare, bhikkhave, carataṃ paravisaye lacchati māro otāraṃ, lacchati māro ārammaṇaṃ. Ko ca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno agocaro paravisayo? Yadidaṃ – pañca kāmaguṇā. Katame pañca? Cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā, sotaviññeyyā saddā…pe… ghānaviññeyyā gandhā… jivhāviññeyyā rasā… kāyaviññeyyā phoṭṭhabbā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno agocaro paravisayo.

“Therefore, bhikkhus, do not wander in an unsuitable range, the territory of others. For those who wander in an unsuitable range, the territory of others, Māra gains an opportunity; Māra finds a support. And what, bhikkhus, is a bhikkhu’s unsuitable range, the territory of others? It is the five strands of sensual pleasure. What five? Forms cognizable by the eye—agreeable, pleasing, delightful, charming, sensuous, and stimulating passion; sounds cognizable by the ear… smells cognizable by the nose… tastes cognizable by the tongue… touches cognizable by the body—agreeable, pleasing, delightful, charming, sensuous, and stimulating passion. This, bhikkhus, is a bhikkhu’s unsuitable range, the territory of others.”

‘‘Gocare, bhikkhave, caratha sake pettike visaye. Gocare, bhikkhave, carataṃ sake pettike visaye na lacchati māro otāraṃ, na lacchati māro ārammaṇaṃ. Ko ca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno gocaro sako pettiko visayo? Yadidaṃ – cattāro satipaṭṭhānā. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno gocaro sako pettiko visayo’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

“Wander, bhikkhus, in your own ancestral range. For those who wander in their own ancestral range, Māra gains no opening; Māra finds no foothold. And what, bhikkhus, is a bhikkhu’s own ancestral range? It is the four foundations of mindfulness. What four? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. This, bhikkhus, is a bhikkhu’s own ancestral range.” Seventh.
