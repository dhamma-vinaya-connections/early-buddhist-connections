# SN 47.17 Ariyasuttaṃ

## SN 47.17 The Noble Discourse

383. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ariyā niyyānikā niyyanti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāya. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cattāro satipaṭṭhānā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ariyā niyyānikā niyyanti takkarassa sammā dukkhakkhayāyā’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

383."Monks, these four foundations of mindfulness, when developed and cultivated, are noble, conducive to liberation, and lead the one who practices them to the complete destruction of suffering. What are the four? Here, monks, a monk ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. These, monks, are the four foundations of mindfulness, when developed and cultivated, are noble, conducive to liberation, and lead the one who practices them to the complete destruction of suffering." The seventh.
