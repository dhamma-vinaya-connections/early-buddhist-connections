# SN 47.40 Vibhaṅgasuttaṃ

## SN 47.40 The Analysis Discourse

406. ‘‘Satipaṭṭhānañca vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi satipaṭṭhānabhāvanañca satipaṭṭhānabhāvanāgāminiñca paṭipadaṃ. Taṃ suṇātha’’. ‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu vedanānupassī viharati…pe… citte cittānupassī viharati…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānaṃ’’.

406."Monks, I will teach you the foundations of mindfulness, the development of the foundations of mindfulness, and the path leading to the development of the foundations of mindfulness. Listen to this." "What, monks, are the foundations of mindfulness? Here, monks, a monk dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure concerning the world; he dwells contemplating feelings in feelings... the mind in the mind... phenomena in phenomena, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure concerning the world. This is called, monks, the foundations of mindfulness."

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānabhāvanā? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu samudayadhammānupassī kāyasmiṃ viharati, vayadhammānupassī kāyasmiṃ viharati, samudayavayadhammānupassī kāyasmiṃ viharati, ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Samudayadhammānupassī vedanāsu viharati…pe… samudayadhammānupassī citte viharati… samudayadhammānupassī dhammesu viharati, vayadhammānupassī dhammesu viharati, samudayavayadhammānupassī dhammesu viharati, ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānabhāvanā.

"And what, monks, is the cultivation of the foundations of mindfulness? Here, monks, a monk dwells contemplating the nature of arising in the body, he dwells contemplating the nature of passing away in the body, he dwells contemplating the nature of arising and passing away in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure in the world. He dwells contemplating the nature of arising in feelings… he dwells contemplating the nature of arising in the mind… he dwells contemplating the nature of arising in phenomena, he dwells contemplating the nature of passing away in phenomena, he dwells contemplating the nature of arising and passing away in phenomena, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure in the world. This is called, monks, the cultivation of the foundations of mindfulness."

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānabhāvanāgāminī paṭipadā? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi, sammāsaṅkappo, sammāvācā, sammākammanto, sammāājīvo, sammāvāyāmo, sammāsati, sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānabhāvanāgāminī paṭipadā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the practice that leads to the cultivation of the foundations of mindfulness? It is just this Noble Eightfold Path, namely: right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samādhi. This, monks, is called the practice that leads to the cultivation of the foundations of mindfulness." The tenth.

Ananussutavaggo catuttho.

The Unheard-of Chapter is the fourth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Ananussutaṃ virāgo, viraddho bhāvanā sati;Aññā chandaṃ pariññāya, bhāvanā vibhaṅgena cāti.

Unheard-of, Dispassion, Neglected, Development, Mindfulness; Discernment, Desire, Full Understanding, Development, and Analysis.
