# SN 47.49 Vedanāsuttaṃ

## SN 47.49 The Feeling Discourse

415. ‘‘Tisso imā, bhikkhave, vedanā. Katamā tisso? Sukhā vedanā, dukkhā vedanā, adukkhamasukhā vedanā – imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso vedanā. Imāsaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tissannaṃ vedanānaṃ pariññāya cattāro satipaṭṭhānā bhāvetabbā.

415.“Monks, there are these three feelings. Which three? Pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. These, monks, are the three feelings. For the complete understanding of these three feelings, the four foundations of mindfulness should be developed.”

‘‘Katame cattāro? Idha bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Imāsaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tissannaṃ vedanānaṃ pariññāya ime cattāro satipaṭṭhānā bhāvetabbā’’ti. Navamaṃ.

“What four? Here, monks, a monk ![[Satipatthanas]]. In feelings [...], in mind [...], in phenomena, he ![[Satipatthanas]]. For the full understanding of these three feelings, these four foundations of mindfulness should be developed.” The ninth.
