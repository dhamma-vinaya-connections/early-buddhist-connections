# SN 47.27 Samattasuttaṃ

## SN 47.27 The Discourse on Completion

393. Taṃyeva nidānaṃ. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘‘asekho, asekho’ti, āvuso anuruddha, vuccati. Kittāvatā nu kho, āvuso, asekho hotī’’ti? ‘‘Catunnaṃ kho, āvuso, satipaṭṭhānānaṃ samattaṃ bhāvitattā asekho hoti’’.

393.The same setting. Seated to one side, Venerable Sāriputta said to Venerable Anuruddha: "‘Asekha, asekha,’—‘one beyond training, one beyond training’—it is said, friend Anuruddha. To what extent, friend, is one beyond training?" "Friend, one is beyond training through having fully developed and cultivated the four establishments of mindfulness."

‘‘Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Idhāvuso, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Imesaṃ kho, āvuso, catunnaṃ satipaṭṭhānānaṃ samattaṃ bhāvitattā asekho hotī’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

"Which four? Here, friends, a monk ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. It is, friends, by the complete development and cultivation of these four establishments of mindfulness that one becomes an adept." The seventh.
