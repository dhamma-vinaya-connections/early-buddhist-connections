# SN 47.32 Virāgasuttaṃ

## SN 47.32 The Discourse on Dispassion.

398. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhānā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattanti.

398."Bhikkhus, these four establishments of mindfulness, when developed and made much of, lead to utter disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, to Nibbāna."

‘‘Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cattāro satipaṭṭhānā bhāvitā bahulīkatā ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattantī’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

"What four? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. These, bhikkhus, are the four establishments of mindfulness, when developed and made much of, lead to utter disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, to Nibbāna." Second.
