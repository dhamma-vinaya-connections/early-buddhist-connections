# SN 47.21 Sīlasuttaṃ

## SN 47.21 The Discourse on Virtue

387. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā ca ānando āyasmā ca bhaddo pāṭaliputte viharanti kukkuṭārāme. Atha kho āyasmā bhaddo sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā ānandena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā bhaddo āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yānimāni, āvuso ānanda, kusalāni sīlāni vuttāni bhagavatā, imāni kusalāni sīlāni kimatthiyāni vuttāni bhagavatā’’ti?

387.Thus have I heard: At one time the Venerable Ānanda and the Venerable Bhadda were dwelling at Pāṭaliputta in the Kukkuṭārāma. Then, in the evening, the Venerable Bhadda arose from seclusion and approached the Venerable Ānanda. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Venerable Ānanda. After exchanging courteous and amiable conversation, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Bhadda said to the Venerable Ānanda: "Friend Ānanda, these wholesome virtues spoken of by the Blessed One—for what purpose were these wholesome virtues spoken of by the Blessed One?"

‘‘Sādhu sādhu, āvuso bhadda! Bhaddako kho te, āvuso bhadda, ummaṅgo [ummaggo (sī. syā. kaṃ.)], bhaddakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ, kalyāṇī paripucchā. Evañhi tvaṃ, āvuso bhadda, pucchasi – ‘yānimāni āvuso ānanda, kusalāni sīlāni vuttāni bhagavatā, imāni kusalāni sīlāni kimatthiyāni vuttāni bhagavatā’’’ti? ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti. ‘‘Yānimāni, āvuso bhadda, kusalāni sīlāni vuttāni bhagavatā, imāni kusalāni sīlāni yāvadeva catunnaṃ satipaṭṭhānānaṃ bhāvanāya vuttāni bhagavatā’’.

"Good, good, friend Bhadda! Good indeed is your initiative, friend Bhadda, good your intelligence, and excellent your questioning. For you ask thus: 'Friend Ānanda, these wholesome virtues spoken of by the Blessed One—for what purpose were these wholesome virtues spoken of by the Blessed One?'" "Yes, friend." "Friend Bhadda, these wholesome virtues spoken of by the Blessed One were spoken only for the purpose of developing the four foundations of mindfulness."

‘‘Katamesaṃ catunnaṃ? Idhāvuso, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Yānimāni, āvuso bhadda, kusalāni sīlāni vuttāni bhagavatā, imāni kusalāni sīlāni yāvadeva imesaṃ catunnaṃ satipaṭṭhānānaṃ bhāvanāya vuttāni bhagavatā’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

"Which four? Here, friend, a monk ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. These, friend Bhadda, are the wholesome virtues spoken of by the Blessed One, and these wholesome virtues are spoken of by the Blessed One only for the development of these four foundations of mindfulness." This is the first.
