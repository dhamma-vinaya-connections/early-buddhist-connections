# SN 47.45 Kusalarāsisuttaṃ

## SN 47.45 The Heap of Wholesome Qualities Discourse

411. ‘‘‘Kusalarāsī’ti, bhikkhave, vadamāno cattāro satipaṭṭhāne sammā vadamāno vadeyya. Kevalo hāyaṃ, bhikkhave, kusalarāsi, yadidaṃ – cattāro satipaṭṭhānā.

411."Bhikkhus, when speaking of ‘a heap of the wholesome,’ one would be rightly speaking of the four establishments of mindfulness. For this, bhikkhus, is the entire heap of the wholesome, namely, the four establishments of mindfulness."

‘‘Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… cittānupassī…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. ‘Kusalarāsī’ti, bhikkhave, vadamāno ime cattāro satipaṭṭhāne sammā vadamāno vadeyya. Kevalo hāyaṃ, bhikkhave, kusalarāsi, yadidaṃ – cattāro satipaṭṭhānā’’ti. Pañcamaṃ.

"What are the four? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. When speaking of ‘a heap of the wholesome,’ bhikkhus, one would be rightly speaking of these four establishments of mindfulness. For this, bhikkhus, is the entire heap of the wholesome, namely, the four establishments of mindfulness." Fifth.
