# SN 54.9 Vesālīsuttaṃ

## SN 54.9 Vesali Sutta

985. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavā bhikkhūnaṃ anekapariyāyena asubhakathaṃ katheti, asubhāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, asubhabhāvanāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati.

985.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Vesālī in the Great Wood, in the Hall with the Peaked Roof. At that time the Blessed One was speaking to the bhikkhus in many ways about the foul, praising the foul, praising the development of the perception of the foul.

Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘icchāmahaṃ, bhikkhave, aḍḍhamāsaṃ paṭisallīyituṃ. Nāmhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakenā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissutvā nāssudha koci bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena.

Then the Blessed One addressed the monks: "I wish, monks, to go into seclusion for half a month. I am not to be approached by anyone, except the one who brings alms food." "Yes, Venerable Sir," those monks replied to the Blessed One. And indeed, no one approached the Blessed One except the one who brought alms food.

Atha kho te bhikkhū – ‘‘bhagavā anekapariyāyena asubhakathaṃ katheti, asubhāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, asubhabhāvanāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsatī’’ti anekākāravokāraṃ asubhabhāvanānuyogamanuyuttā viharanti. Te iminā kāyena aṭṭīyamānā [aṭṭiyamānā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] harāyamānā jigucchamānā satthahārakaṃ pariyesanti. Dasapi bhikkhū ekāhena satthaṃ āharanti, vīsampi…pe… tiṃsampi bhikkhū ekāhena satthaṃ āharanti.

Then those monks thought: "The Blessed One speaks in many ways about the foul, praising the foul, praising the development of the perception of the foul." They, devoted to the practice of developing the perception of the foul, dwelt pursuing it in various ways. Disgusted, ashamed, and loathing this body, they sought a weapon. Ten monks in one day obtained a weapon, twenty... thirty monks in one day obtained a weapon.

Atha kho bhagavā tassa aḍḍhamāsassa accayena paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘kiṃ nu kho, ānanda, tanubhūto viya bhikkhusaṅgho’’ti? ‘‘Tathā hi pana, bhante, ‘bhagavā bhikkhūnaṃ anekapariyāyena asubhakathaṃ katheti, asubhāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, asubhabhāvanāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsatī’ti anekākāravokāraṃ asubhabhāvanānuyogamanuyuttā viharanti. Te iminā kāyena aṭṭīyamānā harāyamānā jigucchamānā satthahārakaṃ pariyesanti. Dasapi bhikkhū ekāhena satthaṃ āharanti, vīsampi bhikkhū… tiṃsampi bhikkhū ekāhena satthaṃ āharanti. Sādhu, bhante, bhagavā aññaṃ pariyāyaṃ ācikkhatu yathāyaṃ bhikkhusaṅgho aññāya saṇṭhaheyyā’’ti.

Then the Blessed One, having emerged from seclusion after that half-month, addressed Venerable Ānanda: "Why, Ānanda, does the community of monks seem diminished?" "Indeed, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One speaks in many ways about the foul, praising the foul, praising the development of the perception of the foul. Devoting themselves to the practice of developing the perception of the foul in various ways, the monks, being disgusted, ashamed, and repelled by this body, seek a weapon. Ten monks in a single day obtained a weapon, twenty monks… thirty monks in a single day obtained a weapon. It would be good, Venerable Sir, if the Blessed One would teach another method so that this community of monks might establish themselves in final knowledge."

‘‘Tenahānanda, yāvatikā bhikkhū vesāliṃ upanissāya viharanti te sabbe upaṭṭhānasālāyaṃ sannipātehī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissutvā yāvatikā bhikkhū vesāliṃ upanissāya viharanti te sabbe upaṭṭhānasālāyaṃ sannipātetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sannipatito [sannipātito (sī.)], bhante, bhikkhusaṅgho. Yassa dāni, bhante, bhagavā kālaṃ maññatī’’ti.

"Then, Ānanda, assemble all the monks who dwell dependent on Vesālī in the assembly hall." "Yes, Venerable Sir," Venerable Ānanda replied to the Blessed One. Having assembled all the monks who dwell dependent on Vesālī in the assembly hall, he approached the Blessed One and said: "The Saṅgha of monks is assembled, Venerable Sir. Now is the time for the Blessed One as he thinks fit."

Atha kho bhagavā yena upaṭṭhānasālā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘ayampi kho, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhi bhāvito bahulīkato santo ceva paṇīto ca asecanako ca sukho ca vihāro uppannuppanne ca pāpake akusale dhamme ṭhānaso antaradhāpeti vūpasameti’’.

Then the Blessed One approached the service hall. Having approached, he sat down on the appointed seat. Seated, the Blessed One addressed the monks: "This mindfulness of breathing samādhi, monks, when developed and cultivated, is both peaceful and sublime, unadulterated, a blissful abiding, and it makes evil, unwholesome qualities disappear and cease as soon as they arise."

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gimhānaṃ pacchime māse ūhataṃ rajojallaṃ, tamenaṃ mahāakālamegho ṭhānaso antaradhāpeti vūpasameti; evameva kho, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhi bhāvito bahulīkato santo ceva paṇīto ca asecanako ca sukho ca vihāro uppannuppanne ca pāpake akusale dhamme ṭhānaso antaradhāpeti vūpasameti. Kathaṃ bhāvito ca, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhi kathaṃ bahulīkato santo ceva paṇīto ca asecanako ca sukho ca vihāro uppannuppanne ca pāpake akusale dhamme ṭhānaso antaradhāpeti vūpasameti?

"Just as, monks, in the last month of the hot season, a mass of dust and dirt, stirred up, is dispersed and calmed on the spot by a great rain cloud; in the same way, monks, samādhi through mindfulness of breathing, when developed and cultivated, is peaceful and sublime, unadulterated, a blissful abiding, and it disperses and calms evil unwholesome states as soon as they arise. And how, monks, is samādhi through mindfulness of breathing developed and cultivated so that it is peaceful and sublime, unadulterated, a blissful abiding, and disperses and calms evil unwholesome states as soon as they arise?"

‘‘Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu araññagato vā rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā nisīdati pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā. So satova assasati, satova passasati…pe… ‘paṭinissaggānupassī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati, ‘paṭinissaggānupassī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati. Evaṃ bhāvito kho, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhi evaṃ bahulīkato santo ceva paṇīto ca asecanako ca sukho ca vihāro uppannuppanne ca pāpake akusale dhamme ṭhānaso antaradhāpeti vūpasametī’’ti. Navamaṃ.

"Here, monks, a monk, gone to the forest, or to the root of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, sits down cross-legged, keeps his body erect, establishing mindfulness in front of him. Mindful he breathes in, mindful he breathes out... He trains himself: 'I will breathe in contemplating release'; he trains himself: 'I will breathe out contemplating release.' Thus developed and cultivated, monks, samādhi through mindfulness of breathing is peaceful and sublime, unadulterated, a blissful abiding, and it promptly dispels and stills evil, unwholesome states as soon as they arise." The ninth.
