# SN 54.7 Mahākappinasuttaṃ

## SN 54.7 Mahākappina Sutta

983. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā mahākappino bhagavato avidūre nisinno hoti pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā. Addasā kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ mahākappinaṃ avidūre nisinnaṃ pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā. Disvāna bhikkhū āmantesi –

983.At Savatthi. At that time, the Venerable Mahākappina was sitting not far from the Blessed One, having folded his legs crosswise, set his body straight, and established mindfulness in front of him. The Blessed One saw the Venerable Mahākappina sitting not far away, having folded his legs crosswise, set his body straight, and established mindfulness in front of him. Having seen him, he addressed the monks:

‘‘Passatha no tumhe, bhikkhave, etassa bhikkhuno kāyassa iñjitattaṃ vā phanditattaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Yadāpi mayaṃ, bhante, taṃ āyasmantaṃ passāma saṅghamajjhe vā nisinnaṃ ekaṃ vā raho nisinnaṃ, tadāpi mayaṃ tassa āyasmato na passāma kāyassa iñjitattaṃ vā phanditattaṃ vā’’ti.

“Monks, do you see any movement or trembling in this monk’s body?” “Venerable sir, even when we see that venerable one seated in the midst of the Sangha or alone in seclusion, we do not see any movement or trembling in his body.”

‘‘Yassa, bhikkhave, samādhissa bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā neva kāyassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā, na cittassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā, tassa so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu samādhissa nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Katamassa ca, bhikkhave, samādhissa bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā neva kāyassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā, na cittassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā?

“Monks, when a bhikkhu has developed and cultivated a certain samādhi, there is no agitation or trembling of the body or agitation or trembling of the mind. That bhikkhu, monks, obtains samādhi at will, obtains samādhi without trouble, obtains samādhi without difficulty. And what samādhi, monks, when developed and cultivated, results in no agitation or trembling of the body or agitation or trembling of the mind?

‘‘Ānāpānassatisamādhissa, bhikkhave, bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā neva kāyassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā, na cittassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā. Kathaṃ bhāvite ca, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhimhi kathaṃ bahulīkate neva kāyassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā, na cittassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā?

“Monks, when mindfulness of breathing is developed and cultivated, there is no agitation or trembling of the body or of the mind. But how, monks, is mindfulness of breathing developed and cultivated so that there is no agitation or trembling of the body or of the mind?

‘‘Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu araññagato vā rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā nisīdati pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā. So satova assasati, satova passasati…pe… ‘paṭinissaggānupassī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati, ‘paṭinissaggānupassī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati. Evaṃ bhāvite ca kho, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhimhi evaṃ bahulīkate neva kāyassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā, na cittassa iñjitattaṃ vā hoti phanditattaṃ vā’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

“Here, monks, a monk—gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling—sits down cross-legged, keeping his body erect, establishing mindfulness in front of him. Mindful he breathes in, mindful he breathes out... He trains himself: ‘I will breathe in observing relinquishment.’ He trains himself: ‘I will breathe out observing relinquishment.’ When mindfulness of breathing is developed and cultivated in this way, monks, there is no wavering or trembling of the body, nor is there wavering or trembling of the mind.” Seventh.
