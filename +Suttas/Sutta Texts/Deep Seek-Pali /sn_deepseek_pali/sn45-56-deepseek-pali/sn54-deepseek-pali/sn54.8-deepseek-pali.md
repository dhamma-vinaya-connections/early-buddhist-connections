# SN 54.8 Padīpopamasuttaṃ

## SN 54.8 The Simile of the Lamp

984. ‘‘Ānāpānassatisamādhi, bhikkhave, bhāvito bahulīkato mahapphalo hoti mahānisaṃso. Kathaṃ bhāvito ca, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhi kathaṃ bahulīkato mahapphalo hoti mahānisaṃso?

984."Bhikkhus, the samādhi by mindfulness of breathing, when developed and cultivated, is of great fruit and great benefit. And how, bhikkhus, is the samādhi by mindfulness of breathing developed and cultivated so that it is of great fruit and great benefit?

‘‘Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu araññagato vā rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā nisīdati pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā. So satova assasati, satova passasati. Dīghaṃ vā assasanto ‘dīghaṃ assasāmī’ti pajānāti…pe… ‘paṭinissaggānupassī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati, ‘paṭinissaggānupassī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati. Evaṃ bhāvito kho, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhi evaṃ bahulīkato mahapphalo hoti mahānisaṃso.

"Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu, gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, sits down cross-legged, keeping his body erect, establishing mindfulness in front of him. Mindful, he breathes in; mindful, he breathes out. Breathing in long, he understands, 'I am breathing in long'... He trains himself, 'I will breathe in observing relinquishment.' He trains himself, 'I will breathe out observing relinquishment.' When the samādhi by mindfulness of breathing is developed and cultivated in this way, bhikkhus, it is of great fruit and great benefit."

‘‘Ahampi sudaṃ, bhikkhave, pubbeva sambodhā anabhisambuddho bodhisattova samāno iminā vihārena bahulaṃ viharāmi. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, iminā vihārena bahulaṃ viharato neva kāyo kilamati na cakkhūni; anupādāya ca me āsavehi cittaṃ vimucci.

"Bhikkhus, before my Awakening, when I was still a Bodhisatta, not yet fully Awakened, I often dwelled in this dwelling. While I often dwelled in this dwelling, bhikkhus, my body was not fatigued, nor my eyes; and my mind was liberated from the taints by not clinging."

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘neva me kāyo kilameyya na cakkhūni, anupādāya ca me āsavehi cittaṃ vimucceyyā’ti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish, ‘May my body not become weary and my eyes not be strained, and may my mind be released from the taints by not clinging,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘ye me gehasitā sarasaṅkappā te pahīyeyyu’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish, 'May my thoughts connected with the household life be abandoned,' then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘appaṭikūle paṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘May I dwell perceiving repulsiveness in the unrepulsive,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘paṭikūle appaṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish, ‘May I dwell perceiving the unrepulsive in the repulsive,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘paṭikūle ca appaṭikūle ca paṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘May I dwell perceiving the repulsive in the repulsive and the unrepulsive,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘paṭikūle ca appaṭikūle ca appaṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘May I dwell perceiving the unrepulsive in the repulsive as well as in the unrepulsive,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘appaṭikūlañca paṭikūlañca tadubhayaṃ abhinivajjetvā upekkhako vihareyyaṃ sato sampajāno’ti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: 'Avoiding both the unrepulsive and the repulsive, may I dwell equanimous, mindful, and fully aware,' then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘Quite secluded from sense pleasures, secluded from unwholesome states, may I enter and dwell in the first jhāna, which is accompanied by thought and examination, with rapture and happiness born of seclusion,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā ajjhattaṃ sampasādanaṃ cetaso ekodibhāvaṃ avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhijaṃ pītisukhaṃ dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘With the stilling of thought and examination, may I, having attained the second jhāna, which has internal confidence and unification of mind, is without thought and examination, and has rapture and happiness born of samādhi, dwell therein,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Tasmātiha bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘pītiyā ca virāgā upekkhako ca vihareyyaṃ sato ca sampajāno, sukhañca kāyena paṭisaṃvedeyyaṃ, yaṃ taṃ ariyā ācikkhanti – upekkhako satimā sukhavihārīti tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘With the fading away of rapture, may I dwell equanimous and mindful, experiencing happiness with the body, as the noble ones declare: "Equanimous, mindful, he dwells happily," having attained the third jhāna,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā pubbeva somanassadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamā adukkhamasukhaṃ upekkhāsatipārisuddhiṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘With the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and with the previous disappearance of joy and grief, may I attain and abide in the fourth jhāna, which is neither painful nor pleasant and has purity of mindfulness due to equanimity,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘sabbaso rūpasaññānaṃ samatikkamā paṭighasaññānaṃ atthaṅgamā nānattasaññānaṃ amanasikārā ananto ākāsoti ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘By completely transcending perceptions of form, with the disappearance of perceptions of sensory impingement, and not attending to perceptions of diversity, may I, [realizing,] "space is infinite," enter and dwell in the base of the infinity of space,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to.

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘sabbaso ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma anantaṃ viññāṇanti viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘Having completely transcended the base of infinite space, may I attain to and dwell in the base of infinite consciousness, thinking, "Consciousness is infinite,"’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘sabbaso viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma natthi kiñcīti ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘Having completely transcended the base of infinite consciousness, may I attain and abide in the base of nothingness, thinking "there is nothing,"’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘sabbaso ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ samatikkamma nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘Having entirely transcended the base of nothingness, may I attain and dwell in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cepi ākaṅkheyya – ‘sabbaso nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ samatikkamma saññāvedayitanirodhaṃ upasampajja vihareyya’nti, ayameva ānāpānassatisamādhi sādhukaṃ manasi kātabbo.

"Therefore, bhikkhus, if a bhikkhu should wish: ‘Having entirely transcended the base of neither perception nor non-perception, may I attain to and abide in the cessation of perception and feeling,’ then this samādhi by mindfulness of breathing should be well attended to."

‘‘Evaṃ bhāvite kho, bhikkhave, ānāpānassatisamādhimhi evaṃ bahulīkate, sukhaṃ ce vedanaṃ vedayati, sā ‘aniccā’ti pajānāti, ‘anajjhositā’ti pajānāti, ‘anabhinanditā’ti pajānāti; dukkhaṃ ce vedanaṃ vedayati, ‘sā aniccā’ti pajānāti, ‘anajjhositā’ti pajānāti, ‘anabhinanditā’ti pajānāti; adukkhamasukhaṃ ce vedanaṃ vedayati, ‘sā aniccā’ti pajānāti, ‘anajjhositā’ti pajānāti, ‘anabhinanditā’ti pajānāti’’.

"When the samādhi by mindfulness of breathing is developed and cultivated in this way, bhikkhus, if he feels a pleasant feeling, he understands, ‘It is impermanent’; he understands, ‘It is not obsessed over’; he understands, ‘It is not delighted in.’ If he feels a painful feeling, he understands, ‘It is impermanent’; he understands, ‘It is not obsessed over’; he understands, ‘It is not delighted in.’ If he feels a neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling, he understands, ‘It is impermanent’; he understands, ‘It is not obsessed over’; he understands, ‘It is not delighted in.’"

‘‘Sukhaṃ [so sukhaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.) ma. ni. 3.364 aṭṭhakathāṭīkā oloketabbā] ce vedanaṃ vedayati, visaṃyutto naṃ vedayati; dukkhaṃ ce vedanaṃ vedayati, visaṃyutto naṃ vedayati; adukkhamasukhaṃ ce vedanaṃ vedayati, visaṃyutto naṃ vedayati. So kāyapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayamāno ‘kāyapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayāmī’ti pajānāti, jīvitapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayamāno ‘jīvitapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayāmī’ti pajānāti, ‘kāyassa bhedā uddhaṃ jīvitapariyādānā idheva sabbavedayitāni anabhinanditāni sītībhavissantī’ti pajānāti’’.

"When experiencing a pleasant feeling, he experiences it detached. When experiencing a painful feeling, he experiences it detached. When experiencing a neither-painful-nor-unpleasant feeling, he experiences it detached. Experiencing a feeling limited to the body, he understands: ‘I am experiencing a feeling limited to the body.’ Experiencing a feeling limited to life, he understands: ‘I am experiencing a feeling limited to life.’ He understands: ‘With the breakup of the body, at the termination of life, all feelings, not being delighted in, will become cool right here.’"

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, telañca paṭicca, vaṭṭiñca paṭicca telappadīpo jhāyeyya, tasseva telassa ca vaṭṭiyā ca pariyādānā anāhāro nibbāyeyya; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāyapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayamāno ‘kāyapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayāmī’ti pajānāti, jīvitapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayamāno ‘jīvitapariyantikaṃ vedanaṃ vedayāmī’ti pajānāti, ‘kāyassa bhedā uddhaṃ jīvitapariyādānā idheva sabbavedayitāni anabhinanditāni sītībhavissantī’ti pajānātī’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

"Just as, bhikkhus, an oil lamp might burn in dependence on oil and a wick, and from the exhaustion of that oil and wick, and lack of further fuel, it would be extinguished; so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu feeling a feeling limited by the body understands: ‘I feel a feeling limited by the body.’ Feeling a feeling limited by life, he understands: ‘I feel a feeling limited by life.’ He understands: ‘Upon the breakup of the body, with the cessation of life, all feelings, not being delighted in, will become cool right here.’" The eighth.
