# SN 54.11 Icchānaṅgalasuttaṃ

## SN 54.11 The Discourse on Icchānaṅgala

987. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā icchānaṅgale viharati icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍe. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘icchāmahaṃ, bhikkhave, temāsaṃ paṭisallīyituṃ. Nāmhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakenā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissutvā nāssudha koci bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena.

987.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Icchānaṅgala in the Icchānaṅgala wooded grove. There the Blessed One addressed the monks: "Monks, I wish to go into seclusion for three months. Let no one approach me, apart from the one bringing almsfood." "Yes, Venerable Sir," those monks replied to the Blessed One. And indeed, no one approached the Blessed One, apart from the one bringing almsfood.

Atha kho bhagavā tassa temāsassa accayena paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘sace kho, bhikkhave, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ puccheyyuṃ – ‘katamenāvuso, vihārena samaṇo gotamo vassāvāsaṃ bahulaṃ vihāsī’ti, evaṃ puṭṭhā tumhe, bhikkhave, tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ evaṃ byākareyyātha – ‘ānāpānassatisamādhinā kho, āvuso, bhagavā vassāvāsaṃ bahulaṃ vihāsī’ti. Idhāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sato assasāmi, sato passasāmi. Dīghaṃ assasanto ‘dīghaṃ assasāmī’ti pajānāmi, dīghaṃ passasanto ‘dīghaṃ passasāmī’ti pajānāmi; rassaṃ assasanto ‘rassaṃ assasāmī’ti pajānāmi, rassaṃ passasanto ‘rassaṃ passasāmī’ti pajānāmi; ‘sabbakāyappaṭisaṃvedī assasissāmī’ti pajānāmi…pe… ‘paṭinissaggānupassī assasissāmī’ti pajānāmi, ‘paṭinissaggānupassī passasissāmī’ti pajānāmi’’.

Then the Blessed One, having emerged from seclusion after those three months, addressed the monks: "Monks, if wanderers of other sects were to ask you, ‘Friends, in what dwelling did the ascetic Gotama mostly spend the rains?’—being asked thus, monks, you should answer those wanderers of other sects thus: ‘Friends, the Blessed One mostly spent the rains dwelling in samādhi through mindfulness of breathing.’ Here, monks, mindful, I breathe in; mindful, I breathe out. Breathing in long, I understand ‘I breathe in long’; breathing out long, I understand ‘I breathe out long.’ Breathing in short, I understand ‘I breathe in short’; breathing out short, I understand ‘I breathe out short.’ I understand ‘I breathe in experiencing the whole body’… I understand ‘I breathe in contemplating relinquishment’; I understand ‘I breathe out contemplating relinquishment.’"

‘‘Yañhi taṃ, bhikkhave, sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘ariyavihāro’ itipi, ‘brahmavihāro’ itipi, ‘tathāgatavihāro’ itipi. Ānāpānassatisamādhiṃ sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘ariyavihāro’ itipi, ‘brahmavihāro’ itipi, ‘tathāgatavihāro’ itipi. Ye te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū sekhā appattamānasā anuttaraṃ yogakkhemaṃ patthayamānā viharanti tesaṃ ānāpānassatisamādhi bhāvito bahulīkato āsavānaṃ khayāya saṃvattati. Ye ca kho te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū arahanto khīṇāsavā vusitavanto katakaraṇīyā ohitabhārā anuppattasadatthā parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojanā sammadaññā vimuttā tesaṃ ānāpānassatisamādhi bhāvito bahulīkato diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārāya ceva saṃvattati satisampajaññāya ca.

If one, monks, rightly speaking, were to say ‘a noble dwelling,’ ‘a divine dwelling,’ or ‘a Tathāgata’s dwelling,’ it is of the samādhi through mindfulness of breathing that one would rightly speak. Monks, for those monks who are trainees, who have not attained their heart's desire, aspiring to the unsurpassed security from bondage, the samādhi through mindfulness of breathing, developed and cultivated, leads to the destruction of the taints. But for those monks who are arahants, whose taints are destroyed, who have lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, attained their own welfare, utterly destroyed the fetters of being, and are completely liberated through perfect knowledge, the samādhi through mindfulness of breathing, developed and cultivated, leads both to a pleasant dwelling in this very life and to mindfulness and full awareness.

‘‘Yañhi taṃ, bhikkhave, sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘ariyavihāro’ itipi, ‘brahmavihāro’ itipi, ‘tathāgatavihāro’ itipi. Ānāpānassatisamādhiṃ sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘ariyavihāro’ itipi, ‘brahmavihāro’ itipi, ‘tathāgatavihāro’ itipī’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

If one, monks, rightly speaking, were to say ‘a noble dwelling,’ ‘a divine dwelling,’ or ‘a Tathāgata’s dwelling,’ it is of the samādhi through mindfulness of breathing that one would rightly speak." This is the first.
