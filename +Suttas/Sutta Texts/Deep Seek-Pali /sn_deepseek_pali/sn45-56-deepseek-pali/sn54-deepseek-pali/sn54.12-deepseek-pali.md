# SN 54.12 Kaṅkheyyasuttaṃ

## SN 54.12 The Kaṅkheyya Sutta

988. Ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā lomasakaṃbhiyo [lomasavaṅgiso (sī. pī.)] sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Atha kho mahānāmo sakko yenāyasmā lomasakaṃbhiyo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ lomasakaṃbhiyaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko āyasmantaṃ lomasakaṃbhiyaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘so eva nu kho, bhante, sekho vihāro so tathāgatavihāro, udāhu aññova [añño (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] sekho vihāro añño tathāgatavihāro’’ti?

988.At one time the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Banyan Park. Then Mahānāma the Sakyan approached the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Mahānāma the Sakyan said to the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya: “Venerable, is the dwelling of a trainee the same as the dwelling of the Tathāgata, or is the dwelling of a trainee different from the dwelling of the Tathāgata?”

‘‘Na kho, āvuso mahānāma, sveva sekho vihāro, so tathāgatavihāro. Añño kho, āvuso mahānāma, sekho vihāro, añño tathāgatavihāro. Ye te, āvuso mahānāma, bhikkhū sekhā appattamānasā anuttaraṃ yogakkhemaṃ patthayamānā viharanti, te pañca nīvaraṇe pahāya viharanti. Katame pañca? Kāmacchandanīvaraṇaṃ pahāya viharanti, byāpādanīvaraṇaṃ…pe… thinamiddhanīvaraṇaṃ…pe… uddhaccakukkuccanīvaraṇaṃ…pe… vicikicchānīvaraṇaṃ pahāya viharanti.

"It’s not the case, friend Mahānāma, that the dwelling of a trainee is exactly the same as the Tathāgata’s dwelling. The dwelling of a trainee is one thing, the Tathāgata’s dwelling is another. Those monks, friend Mahānāma, who are trainees, who have not attained their heart’s desire, aspiring to the unsurpassed security from bondage, dwell having abandoned the five hindrances. What five? They dwell having abandoned the hindrance of sensual desire, the hindrance of ill will, the hindrance of sloth and drowsiness, the hindrance of restlessness and remorse, and the hindrance of doubt."

‘‘Yepi te, āvuso mahānāma, bhikkhū sekhā appattamānasā anuttaraṃ yogakkhemaṃ patthayamānā viharanti, te ime pañca nīvaraṇe pahāya viharanti.

"Friend Mahānāma, those monks who are trainees, who have not attained their heart's desire, aspiring to the unsurpassed security from bondage, dwell having abandoned these five hindrances."

‘‘Ye ca kho te, āvuso mahānāma, bhikkhū arahanto khīṇāsavā vusitavanto katakaraṇīyā ohitabhārā anuppattasadatthā parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojanā sammadaññā vimuttā, tesaṃ pañca nīvaraṇā pahīnā ucchinnamūlā tālāvatthukatā anabhāvaṃkatā [anabhāvakatā (sī. pī.)] āyatiṃ anuppādadhammā. Katame pañca? Kāmacchandanīvaraṇaṃ pahīnaṃ ucchinnamūlaṃ tālāvatthukataṃ anabhāvaṃkataṃ āyatiṃ anuppādadhammaṃ; byāpādanīvaraṇaṃ pahīnaṃ…pe… thinamiddhanīvaraṇaṃ…pe… uddhaccakukkuccanīvaraṇaṃ…pe… vicikicchānīvaraṇaṃ pahīnaṃ ucchinnamūlaṃ tālāvatthukataṃ anabhāvaṃkataṃ āyatiṃ anuppādadhammaṃ.

"But, friend Mahānāma, those monks who are arahants, whose taints are destroyed, who have lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, attained their own welfare, utterly destroyed the fetters of being, and are liberated through perfect knowledge—for them, the five hindrances are abandoned, their root cut off, made like a palm stump, brought to non-existence, and are no longer liable to arise in the future. What five? The hindrance of sensual desire is abandoned, its root cut off, made like a palm stump, brought to non-existence, and is no longer liable to arise in the future. The hindrance of ill will is abandoned... the hindrance of sloth and drowsiness... the hindrance of restlessness and remorse... the hindrance of doubt is abandoned, its root cut off, made like a palm stump, brought to non-existence, and is no longer liable to arise in the future."

‘‘Ye te, āvuso mahānāma, bhikkhū arahanto khīṇāsavā vusitavanto katakaraṇīyā ohitabhārā anuppattasadatthā parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojanā sammadaññā vimuttā, tesaṃ ime pañca nīvaraṇā pahīnā ucchinnamūlā tālāvatthukatā anabhāvaṃkatā āyatiṃ anuppādadhammā. Tadamināpetaṃ, āvuso mahānāma, pariyāyena veditabbaṃ yathā – aññova sekho vihāro, añño tathāgatavihāro.

"Friend Mahānāma, those monks who are arahants, whose taints are destroyed, who have lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, attained their own welfare, utterly destroyed the fetters of being, and are liberated through perfect knowledge—for them these five hindrances are abandoned, their root cut off, made like a palm stump, brought to non-existence, and are no longer liable to arise in the future. In this way, friend Mahānāma, it may be understood that the dwelling of a trainee is one thing, and the dwelling of the Tathāgata is another."

‘‘Ekamidaṃ, āvuso mahānāma, samayaṃ bhagavā icchānaṅgale viharati icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍe. Tatra kho, āvuso mahānāma, bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘icchāmahaṃ, bhikkhave, temāsaṃ paṭisallīyituṃ. Nāmhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakenā’ti. ‘Evaṃ, bhante’ti kho, āvuso mahānāma, te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissutvā nāssudha koci bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena.

"At one time, friend Mahānāma, the Blessed One was dwelling at Icchānaṅgala in the Icchānaṅgala wooded grove. There, friend Mahānāma, the Blessed One addressed the monks: 'Monks, I wish to go into seclusion for three months. Let no one approach me, apart from the one bringing almsfood.' 'Yes, Venerable Sir,' those monks replied to the Blessed One. And indeed, friend Mahānāma, no one approached the Blessed One, apart from the one bringing almsfood.

‘‘Atha kho, āvuso, bhagavā tassa temāsassa accayena paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘sace kho, bhikkhave, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ puccheyyuṃ – katamenāvuso, vihārena samaṇo gotamo vassāvāsaṃ bahulaṃ vihāsīti, evaṃ puṭṭhā tumhe, bhikkhave, tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ evaṃ byākareyyātha – ānāpānassatisamādhinā kho, āvuso, bhagavā vassāvāsaṃ bahulaṃ vihāsīti. Idhāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sato assasāmi, sato passasāmi. Dīghaṃ assasanto dīghaṃ assasāmīti pajānāmi, dīghaṃ passasanto dīghaṃ passasāmīti pajānāmi…pe… paṭinissaggānupassī assasissāmīti pajānāmi, paṭinissaggānupassī passasissāmī’ti pajānāmi’’.

"Then, friend, when the Blessed One, at the end of those three months, emerged from seclusion, he addressed the monks: 'Monks, if wanderers of other sects should ask you thus: "Friends, in what dwelling did the ascetic Gotama mostly spend the rains?"—being asked thus, monks, you should answer those wanderers of other sects thus: "Friends, the Blessed One mostly spent the rains dwelling in samādhi through mindfulness of breathing." Here, monks, mindful, I breathe in; mindful, I breathe out. Breathing in long, I understand, "I am breathing in long"; breathing out long, I understand, "I am breathing out long."... I understand, "I will breathe in contemplating relinquishment"; I understand, "I will breathe out contemplating relinquishment."'

‘‘Yañhi taṃ, bhikkhave, sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ariyavihāro itipi, brahmavihāro itipi, tathāgatavihāro itipi. Ānāpānassatisamādhiṃ sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ariyavihāro itipi, brahmavihāro itipi, tathāgatavihāro itipi.

"If one, monks, rightly speaking, were to say ‘a noble dwelling,’ ‘a divine dwelling,’ or ‘a Tathāgata’s dwelling,’ it is of the samādhi through mindfulness of breathing that one would rightly speak."

‘‘Ye te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū sekhā appattamānasā anuttaraṃ yogakkhemaṃ patthayamānā viharanti, tesaṃ ānāpānassatisamādhi bhāvito bahulīkato āsavānaṃ khayāya saṃvattati.

"Monks, those monks who are trainees, who have not attained their heart's desire, aspiring to the unsurpassed security from bondage, for them, samādhi through mindfulness of breathing, when developed and cultivated, leads to the destruction of the taints."

‘‘Ye ca kho te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū arahanto khīṇāsavā vusitavanto katakaraṇīyā ohitabhārā anuppattasadatthā parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojanā sammadaññā vimuttā, tesaṃ ānāpānassatisamādhi bhāvito bahulīkato diṭṭheva dhamme sukhavihārāya ceva saṃvattati satisampajaññāya ca.

"But, monks, those monks who are arahants, whose taints are destroyed, who have lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, attained their own welfare, utterly destroyed the fetters of being, and are liberated through perfect knowledge—for them, samādhi through mindfulness of breathing, when developed and cultivated, conduces to a pleasant abiding in this very life, and to mindfulness and clear comprehension."

‘‘Yañhi taṃ, bhikkhave, sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ariyavihāro itipi, brahmavihāro itipi, tathāgatavihāro itipi. Ānāpānassatisamādhiṃ sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ariyavihāro itipi, brahmavihāro itipi, tathāgatavihāro itipī’’ti. ‘‘Iminā kho etaṃ, āvuso mahānāma, pariyāyena veditabbaṃ, yathā – aññova sekho vihāro, añño tathāgatavihāro’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

“If one, monks, speaking rightly were to say: ‘This is a noble abiding,’ ‘this is a divine abiding,’ ‘this is a Tathāgata’s abiding’—it is samādhi through mindfulness of breathing that one speaking rightly would say is a noble abiding, a divine abiding, a Tathāgata’s abiding.” “In this way, friend Mahānāma, it should be understood how the abiding of a trainee is different from the abiding of a Tathāgata.”
