# SN 45.63 Paṭhamasamuddaninnasuttaṃ

## SN 45.63 The First Discourse on Sloping to the Ocean.

133. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gaṅgā nadī samuddaninnā samuddapoṇā samuddapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

133."Just as, bhikkhus, the river Ganges inclines to the ocean, flows to the ocean, tends to the ocean; in the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. And how, bhikkhus, does a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, incline to Nibbāna, flow to Nibbāna, tend to Nibbāna? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops Right View, which inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna... he develops Right samādhi, which inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. Thus, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna." First.

## 2-6. Dutiyādisamuddaninnasuttapañcakaṃ

### The second group of five suttas on inclining towards the ocean.

134-138. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yamunā nadī samuddaninnā samuddapoṇā samuddapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, aciravatī nadī samuddaninnā samuddapoṇā samuddapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, sarabhū nadī samuddaninnā samuddapoṇā samuddapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, mahī nadī samuddaninnā samuddapoṇā samuddapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yā kācimā mahānadiyo, seyyathidaṃ – gaṅgā, yamunā, aciravatī sarabhū, mahī, sabbā tā samuddaninnā samuddapoṇā samuddapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

134-138. "Just as, bhikkhus, the Yamunā River flows to the ocean, tends to the ocean, inclines to the ocean; even so, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… Just as, bhikkhus, the Aciravatī River flows to the ocean, tends to the ocean, inclines to the ocean; even so, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… Just as, bhikkhus, the Sarabhū River flows to the ocean, tends to the ocean, inclines to the ocean; even so, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… Just as, bhikkhus, the Mahī River flows to the ocean, tends to the ocean, inclines to the ocean; even so, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… Just as, bhikkhus, all these great rivers—that is, the Gaṅgā, the Yamunā, the Aciravatī, the Sarabhū, and the Mahī—all flow to the ocean, tend to the ocean, incline to the ocean; even so, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, cultivating the Noble Eightfold Path, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna, inclines to Nibbāna. And how, bhikkhus, does a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, cultivating the Noble Eightfold Path, flow to Nibbāna, tend to Nibbāna, incline to Nibbāna? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops right view, which flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna, inclines to Nibbāna… he develops right samādhi, which flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna, inclines to Nibbāna. Thus, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, cultivating the Noble Eightfold Path, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna, inclines to Nibbāna." The sixth.

(Gaṅgāpeyyālī).

(The Gaṅgā Repetition Series).

Dutiyagaṅgāpeyyālavaggo dutiyo.

The Second Gaṅgā Repetition Chapter.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Cha pācīnato ninnā, cha ninnā ca samuddato;Ete dve cha dvādasa honti, vaggo tena pavuccatīti;Nibbānaninno dvādasakī, catutthakī chaṭṭhā navakī.

Six inclining from the east, and six inclining towards the ocean; These two sixes make twelve, hence the chapter is named; Inclined towards Nibbāna are the twelfth, the fourth, the sixth, and the ninth.
