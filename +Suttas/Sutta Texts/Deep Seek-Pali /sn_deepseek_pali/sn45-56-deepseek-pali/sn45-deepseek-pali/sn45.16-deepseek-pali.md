# SN 45.16 Paṭhamaparisuddhasuttaṃ

## SN 45.16 The First Discourse on Perfect Purity

16. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Aṭṭhime, bhikkhave, dhammā parisuddhā pariyodātā anaṅgaṇā vigatūpakkilesā anuppannā uppajjanti, nāññatra tathāgatassa pātubhāvā arahato sammāsambuddhassa. Katame aṭṭha? Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ime kho, bhikkhave, aṭṭha dhammā parisuddhā pariyodātā anaṅgaṇā vigatūpakkilesā anuppannā uppajjanti, nāññatra tathāgatassa pātubhāvā arahato sammāsambuddhassā’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

16. At Savatthi. “Monks, these eight qualities are pure, bright, stainless, rid of corruptions, and, being unarisen, they arise only on the appearance of a Tathagata, a worthy one, a fully enlightened one. Which eight? They are: right view … right samādhi. These, monks, are the eight qualities that are pure, bright, stainless, rid of corruptions, and, being unarisen, they arise only on the appearance of a Tathagata, a worthy one, a fully enlightened one.” The sixth.
