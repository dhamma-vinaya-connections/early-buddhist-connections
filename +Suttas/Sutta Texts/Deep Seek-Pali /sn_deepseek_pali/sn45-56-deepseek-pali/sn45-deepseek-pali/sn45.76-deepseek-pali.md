# SN 45.76 Āgantukasuttaṃ

## SN 45.76 The Guest House Discourse

159. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, āgantukāgāraṃ. Tattha puratthimāyapi disāya āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti, pacchimāyapi disāya āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti, uttarāyapi disāya āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti, dakkhiṇāyapi disāya āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti, khattiyāpi āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti, brāhmaṇāpi āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti, vessāpi āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti, suddāpi āgantvā vāsaṃ kappenti; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto ye dhammā abhiññā pariññeyyā, te dhamme abhiññā parijānāti, ye dhammā abhiññā pahātabbā, te dhamme abhiññā pajahati, ye dhammā abhiññā sacchikātabbā, te dhamme abhiññā sacchikaroti, ye dhammā abhiññā bhāvetabbā, te dhamme abhiññā bhāveti.

159.“Monks, it is just like a guest house. People come and reside there from the eastern quarter, from the western quarter, from the northern quarter, and from the southern quarter; nobles come and reside, brahmins come and reside, merchants come and reside, and workers come and reside. Precisely in the same way, monks, when a monk develops and cultivates the Noble Eightfold Path, he fully understands by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be fully understood, he abandons by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be abandoned, he realizes by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be realized, and he develops by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be developed.

‘‘Katame ca, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā pariññeyyā? Pañcupādānakkhandhātissa vacanīyaṃ. Katame pañca? Seyyathidaṃ – rūpupādānakkhandho…pe… viññāṇupādānakkhandho. Ime, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā pariññeyyā. Katame ca, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā pahātabbā? Avijjā ca bhavataṇhā ca – ime, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā pahātabbā. Katame ca, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā sacchikātabbā? Vijjā ca vimutti ca – ime, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā sacchikātabbā. Katame ca, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā bhāvetabbā? Samatho ca vipassanā ca – ime, bhikkhave, dhammā abhiññā bhāvetabbā. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto, ye dhammā abhiññā pariññeyyā te dhamme abhiññā parijānāti…pe… ye dhammā abhiññā bhāvetabbā, te dhamme abhiññā bhāveti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto ye dhammā abhiññā pariññeyyā, te dhamme abhiññā parijānāti, ye dhammā abhiññā pahātabbā, te dhamme abhiññā pajahati, ye dhammā abhiññā sacchikātabbā, te dhamme abhiññā sacchikaroti, ye dhammā abhiññā bhāvetabbā, te dhamme abhiññā bhāvetī’’ti. Ekādasamaṃ.

“And what, monks, are the phenomena to be fully understood by direct knowledge? It should be said: the five aggregates subject to clinging. Which five? They are: the aggregate of form subject to clinging… the aggregate of consciousness subject to clinging. These, monks, are phenomena to be fully understood by direct knowledge. And what, monks, are the phenomena to be abandoned by direct knowledge? Ignorance and craving for existence—these, monks, are phenomena to be abandoned by direct knowledge. And what, monks, are the phenomena to be realized by direct knowledge? True knowledge and liberation—these, monks, are phenomena to be realized by direct knowledge. And what, monks, are the phenomena to be developed by direct knowledge? Tranquility and insight—these, monks, are phenomena to be developed by direct knowledge. And how, monks, does a monk who develops and cultivates the ![[Noble Path]], based on seclusion, dispassion, cessation, culminating in relinquishment… It is in this way, monks, that a monk who develops and cultivates the Noble Eightfold Path fully understands by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be fully understood, abandons by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be abandoned, realizes by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be realized, and develops by direct knowledge those phenomena that are to be developed.” The eleventh.
