# SN 45.24 Dutiyapaṭipadāsuttaṃ

## SN 45.24 The Second Discourse on Practice

24. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Gihino vāhaṃ, bhikkhave, pabbajitassa vā micchāpaṭipadaṃ na vaṇṇemi. Gihi vā, bhikkhave, pabbajito vā micchāpaṭipanno micchāpaṭipattādhikaraṇahetu nārādhako hoti ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusalaṃ’’.

24.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, I do not praise wrong practice, whether of a householder or of one gone forth. A householder or one gone forth, monks, who practices wrongly, on account of wrong practice, does not realize the noble path, the wholesome Dhamma.”

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, micchāpaṭipadā? Seyyathidaṃ – micchādiṭṭhi…pe… micchāsamādhi. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, micchāpaṭipadā. Gihino vāhaṃ, bhikkhave, pabbajitassa vā micchāpaṭipadaṃ na vaṇṇemi. Gihi vā, bhikkhave, pabbajito vā micchāpaṭipanno micchāpaṭipattādhikaraṇahetu nārādhako hoti ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusalaṃ.

“And what, monks, is wrong practice? It is this: wrong view…wrong samādhi. This, monks, is called wrong practice. Monks, I do not praise wrong practice, whether of a householder or of one gone forth. A householder or one gone forth, monks, who practices wrongly, on account of wrong practice, does not realize the noble path, the wholesome Dhamma.”

‘‘Gihino vāhaṃ, bhikkhave, pabbajitassa vā sammāpaṭipadaṃ vaṇṇemi. Gihi vā, bhikkhave, pabbajito vā sammāpaṭipanno sammāpaṭipattādhikaraṇahetu ārādhako hoti ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusalaṃ. Katamā ca, bhikkhave, sammāpaṭipadā? Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, sammāpaṭipadā. Gihino vāhaṃ, bhikkhave, pabbajitassa vā sammāpaṭipadaṃ vaṇṇemi. Gihi vā, bhikkhave, pabbajito vā sammāpaṭipanno sammāpaṭipattādhikaraṇahetu ārādhako hoti ñāyaṃ dhammaṃ kusala’’nti. Catutthaṃ.

“Monks, I praise right practice, whether of a householder or of one gone forth. A householder or one gone forth, monks, who practices rightly, on account of right practice, realizes the noble path, the wholesome Dhamma. And what, monks, is right practice? It is this: right view…right samādhi. This, monks, is called right practice. Monks, I praise right practice, whether of a householder or of one gone forth. A householder or one gone forth, monks, who practices rightly, on account of right practice, realizes the noble path, the wholesome Dhamma.” The fourth.
