# SN 45.17 Dutiyaparisuddhasuttaṃ

## SN 45.17 The Second Discourse on Perfect Purity

17. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Aṭṭhime, bhikkhave, dhammā parisuddhā pariyodātā anaṅgaṇā vigatūpakkilesā anuppannā uppajjanti, nāññatra sugatavinayā. Katame aṭṭha? Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ime kho, bhikkhave, aṭṭha dhammā parisuddhā pariyodātā anaṅgaṇā vigatūpakkilesā anuppannā uppajjanti, nāññatra sugatavinayā’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

17. At Sāvatthī. "Monks, these eight qualities—purified, thoroughly cleansed, stainless, rid of defilement—not having previously arisen, arise only in the Teaching of the Fortunate One. Which eight? Namely, right view ... right samādhi. These, monks, are the eight qualities—purified, thoroughly cleansed, stainless, rid of defilement—that, not having previously arisen, arise only in the Teaching of the Fortunate One." The seventh.
