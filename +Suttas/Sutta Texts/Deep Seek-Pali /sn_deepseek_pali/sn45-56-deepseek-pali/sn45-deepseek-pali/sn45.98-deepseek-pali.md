# SN 45.98 Uddhambhāgiyasuttaṃ

## SN 45.98 The Higher Fetters Discourse

181. ‘‘Pañcimāni, bhikkhave, uddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Katamāni pañca? Rūparāgo, arūparāgo, māno, uddhaccaṃ, avijjā – imāni kho, bhikkhave, pañcuddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo. Katamo ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya ayaṃ ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo’’ti.

181."Monks, there are these five higher fetters. Which five? Lust for form, lust for the formless, conceit, restlessness, ignorance—these, monks, are the five higher fetters. For the direct knowledge, complete understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these five higher fetters, the Noble Eightfold Path is to be cultivated. What is the Noble Eightfold Path? Here, monks, a monk cultivates right view, dependent on seclusion... right samādhi, dependent on seclusion, dispassion, cessation, and relinquishment, resulting in relinquishment. For the direct knowledge, complete understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these five higher fetters, this Noble Eightfold Path is to be cultivated."

‘‘Pañcimāni, bhikkhave, uddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Katamāni pañca? Rūparāgo, arūparāgo, māno, uddhaccaṃ, avijjā – imāni kho, bhikkhave, pañcuddhambhāgiyāni saṃyojanāni. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo. Katamo ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti rāgavinayapariyosānaṃ dosavinayapariyosānaṃ mohavinayapariyosānaṃ… amatogadhaṃ amataparāyanaṃ amatapariyosānaṃ… nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ uddhambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya ayaṃ ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

"Monks, there are these five higher fetters. Which five? Lust for form, lust for the formless, conceit, restlessness, and ignorance. These, monks, are the five higher fetters. For the direct knowledge, complete understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these five higher fetters, the Noble Eightfold Path is to be cultivated. What is the Noble Eightfold Path? Here, monks, a monk cultivates right view... right samādhi, which culminates in the removal of lust, culminates in the removal of hatred, culminates in the removal of delusion... which has the deathless as its ground, the deathless as its goal, the deathless as its culmination... which inclines toward Nibbāna, leans toward Nibbāna, is directed toward Nibbāna. For the direct knowledge, complete understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these five higher fetters, this Noble Eightfold Path is to be cultivated." The tenth.

Oghavaggo aṭṭhamo.

The Chapter on Floods, the Eighth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Ogho yogo upādānaṃ, ganthaṃ anusayena ca;Kāmaguṇā nīvaraṇaṃ, khandhā oruddhambhāgiyāti.

The flood, the yoke, clinging, the bond, and the latent tendency; The strands of sensuality, the hindrances, the aggregates, the lower and higher fetters.

Vagguddānaṃ –

The Chapter Summary

Avijjāvaggo paṭhamo, dutiyaṃ vihāraṃ vuccati;Micchattaṃ tatiyo vaggo, catutthaṃ paṭipanneneva.

The chapter on ignorance is first, the second is called abiding; The third chapter is on wrongness, the fourth is on the path attained.

Titthiyaṃ pañcamo vaggo, chaṭṭho sūriyena ca;Bahukate sattamo vaggo, uppādo aṭṭhamena ca.

The fifth chapter is on the sectarians, the sixth with the sun; The seventh chapter is on the much made, the eighth with arising.

Divasavaggo navamo, dasamo appamādena ca;Ekādasabalavaggo, dvādasa esanā pāḷiyaṃ;Oghavaggo bhavati terasāti.

The ninth is the chapter on days, the tenth on heedfulness; The eleventh is the chapter on strength, the twelfth in the Pali is on seeking; The thirteenth is the chapter on floods.

Maggasaṃyuttaṃ paṭhamaṃ.
