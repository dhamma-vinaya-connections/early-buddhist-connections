# SN 45.40 Dutiyabrahmacariyasuttaṃ

## SN 45.40 The Second Discourse on the Holy Life

40. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Brahmacariyañca vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi, brahmacariyatthañca. Taṃ suṇātha. Katamañca, bhikkhave, brahmacariyaṃ? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, brahmacariyaṃ. Katamo ca, bhikkhave, brahmacariyattho? Yo kho, bhikkhave, rāgakkhayo dosakkhayo mohakkhayo – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, brahmacariyattho’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

40.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, I will teach you the holy life and the goal of the holy life. Listen carefully. And what, monks, is the holy life? It is just this Noble Eightfold Path, namely: right view … right samādhi. This, monks, is called the holy life. And what, monks, is the goal of the holy life? It is the destruction of lust, the destruction of hatred, the destruction of delusion. This, monks, is called the goal of the holy life.” The Tenth.

Paṭipattivaggo catuttho.

The Fourth Chapter on Practice.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Paṭipatti paṭipanno ca, viraddhañca pāraṃgamā;Sāmaññena ca dve vuttā, brahmaññā apare duve;Brahmacariyena dve vuttā, vaggo tena pavuccatīti.

Practice and the practitioner, one strayed and the one gone beyond; Two are spoken of in terms of recluseship, two others in terms of brahminhood; Two are spoken of in terms of the holy life—Thus is the chapter called.
