# SN 45.71 Sūkasuttaṃ

## SN 45.71 The Spike Discourse

154. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, sālisūkaṃ vā yavasūkaṃ vā sammāpaṇihitaṃ hatthena vā pādena vā akkantaṃ hatthaṃ vā pādaṃ vā bhindissati lohitaṃ vā uppādessatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Sammāpaṇihitattā, bhikkhave, sūkassa. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammāpaṇihitāya diṭṭhiyā sammāpaṇihitāya maggabhāvanāya avijjaṃ bhindissati, vijjaṃ uppādessati, nibbānaṃ sacchikarissatīti – ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Sammāpaṇihitattā, bhikkhave, diṭṭhiyā. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammāpaṇihitāya diṭṭhiyā sammāpaṇihitāya maggabhāvanāya avijjaṃ bhindati, vijjaṃ uppādeti, nibbānaṃ sacchikaroti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ…pe… evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammāpaṇihitāya diṭṭhiyā sammāpaṇihitāya maggabhāvanāya avijjaṃ bhindati, vijjaṃ uppādeti, nibbānaṃ sacchikarotī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

154.“Monks, just as a blade of rice or barley, if rightly placed, when stepped on by hand or foot, would pierce the hand or foot and draw blood—this is possible. Why is that? Because of the rightly placed nature, monks, of the blade. In the same way, monks, a monk with rightly directed view and rightly directed development of the path will shatter ignorance, give rise to knowledge, and realize Nibbāna—this is possible. Why is that? Because of the rightly directed view, monks. And how, monks, does a monk with rightly directed view and rightly directed development of the path shatter ignorance, give rise to knowledge, and realize Nibbāna? Here, monks, a monk develops right view, which is based on seclusion…up to…right samādhi, which is based on seclusion, on dispassion, on cessation, and culminates in relinquishment…In this way, monks, a monk with rightly directed view and rightly directed development of the path shatters ignorance, gives rise to knowledge, and realizes Nibbāna.” The sixth.
