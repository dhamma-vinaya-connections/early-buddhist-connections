# SN 45.10 Nandiyasuttaṃ

## SN 45.10 Nandiya Sutta

10. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Atha kho nandiyo paribbājako yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho nandiyo paribbājako bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kati nu kho, bho gotama, dhammā bhāvitā bahulīkatā nibbānaṅgamā honti nibbānaparāyanā nibbānapariyosānā’’ti?

10. At Sāvatthī. Then the wanderer Nandiya approached the Blessed One; having approached, he exchanged polite greetings with the Blessed One. When they had concluded their conversation of greetings and introductions, he sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the wanderer Nandiya said to the Blessed One: “How many qualities, Master Gotama, when developed and cultivated, lead to Nibbāna, conduce to Nibbāna, end in Nibbāna?”

‘‘Aṭṭhime kho, nandiya, dhammā bhāvitā bahulīkatā nibbānaṅgamā honti nibbānaparāyanā nibbānapariyosānā. Katame aṭṭha? Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ime kho, nandiya, aṭṭha dhammā bhāvitā bahulīkatā nibbānaṅgamā honti nibbānaparāyanā nibbānapariyosānā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte nandiyo paribbājako bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama …pe… upāsakaṃ maṃ bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

"These eight qualities, Nandiya, when developed and cultivated, lead to Nibbāna, conduce to Nibbāna, and end in Nibbāna. Which eight? That is, right view... right samādhi. These eight qualities, Nandiya, when developed and cultivated, lead to Nibbāna, conduce to Nibbāna, and end in Nibbāna." When this was said, the wanderer Nandiya said to the Blessed One: "Magnificent, Master Gotama! Magnificent, Master Gotama!... From today, may Master Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has taken refuge for life." The tenth.

Avijjāvaggo paṭhamo.

The Chapter on Ignorance, the First.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Avijjañca upaḍḍhañca, sāriputto ca brāhmaṇo;Kimatthiyo ca dve bhikkhū, vibhaṅgo sūkanandiyāti.

Ignorance and the Second, Sāriputta and the Brahmin, Kimatthiya and Two Monks, the Analysis and Sūka and Nandiya.

2. Vihāravaggo

2. The Chapter on Abiding.
