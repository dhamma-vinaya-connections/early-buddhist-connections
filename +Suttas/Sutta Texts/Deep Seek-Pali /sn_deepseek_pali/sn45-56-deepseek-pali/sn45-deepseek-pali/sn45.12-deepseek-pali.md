# SN 45.12 Dutiyavihārasuttaṃ

## SN 45.12 The Second Dwelling Discourse.

12. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Icchāmahaṃ, bhikkhave, temāsaṃ paṭisalliyituṃ. Namhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakenā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissutvā nāssudha koci bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena.

12. At Sāvatthī. "Bhikkhus, I wish to go into retreat for three months. No one should approach me except the one who brings my almsfood." "Yes, venerable sir," those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One, and no one approached the Blessed One except the one who brought the almsfood.

Atha kho bhagavā tassa temāsassa accayena paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘yena svāhaṃ, bhikkhave, vihārena paṭhamābhisambuddho viharāmi, tassa padesena vihāsiṃ. So evaṃ pajānāmi – ‘micchādiṭṭhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; micchādiṭṭhivūpasamapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; sammādiṭṭhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; sammādiṭṭhivūpasamapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ…pe… micchāsamādhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; micchāsamādhivūpasamapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ, sammāsamādhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; sammāsamādhivūpasamapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; chandapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; chandavūpasamapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; vitakkapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; vitakkavūpasamapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; saññāpaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; saññāvūpasamapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; chando ca avūpasanto hoti, vitakko ca avūpasanto hoti, saññā ca avūpasantā hoti, tappaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; chando ca vūpasanto hoti, vitakko ca vūpasanto hoti, saññā ca vūpasantā hoti, tappaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; appattassa pattiyā atthi āyāmaṃ [vāyāmaṃ (sī. syā.)], tasmimpi ṭhāne anuppatte tappaccayāpi vedayita’’’nti. Dutiyaṃ.

Then the Blessed One, at the end of those three months, having risen from seclusion, addressed the monks: "Monks, the dwelling whereby I dwell as one who has first fully awakened—by that aspect I dwelled. I understand thus: 'There is feeling conditioned by wrong view; there is feeling conditioned by the cessation of wrong view; there is feeling conditioned by right view; there is feeling conditioned by the cessation of right view… There is feeling conditioned by wrong samādhi; there is feeling conditioned by the cessation of wrong samādhi; there is feeling conditioned by right samādhi; there is feeling conditioned by the cessation of right samādhi; there is feeling conditioned by desire; there is feeling conditioned by the cessation of desire; there is feeling conditioned by thought; there is feeling conditioned by the cessation of thought; there is feeling conditioned by perception; there is feeling conditioned by the cessation of perception. When desire is not stilled, thought is not stilled, and perception is not stilled, there is feeling conditioned by that. When desire is stilled, thought is stilled, and perception is stilled, there is feeling conditioned by that. For one who has not attained, there is the range for attainment; even in that state when it is attained, there is feeling conditioned by that.'" The second.
