# SN 45.79 Vidhāsuttaṃ

## SN 45.79 The Discourse on Conceit.

162. ‘‘Tisso imā, bhikkhave, vidhā. Katamā tisso? ‘Seyyohamasmī’ti vidhā, ‘sadisohamasmī’ti vidhā, ‘hīnohamasmī’ti vidhā – imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso vidhā. Imāsaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tissannaṃ vidhānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo. Katamo ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti vivekanissitaṃ virāganissitaṃ nirodhanissitaṃ vossaggapariṇāmiṃ…pe… imāsaṃ kho, bhikkhave tissannaṃ vidhānaṃ abhiññāya pariññāya parikkhayāya pahānāya ayaṃ ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo’’ti. (Yathā esanā, evaṃ vitthāretabbaṃ). Dutiyaṃ.

162."Monks, there are these three conceits. What three? The conceit 'I am superior,' the conceit 'I am equal,' and the conceit 'I am inferior.' These, monks, are the three conceits. For the direct knowledge, full understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these three conceits, the ![[Noble Path]], which is based on seclusion, dispassion, and cessation, and culminates in relinquishment… For the direct knowledge, full understanding, utter destruction, and abandonment of these three conceits, this Noble Eightfold Path is to be cultivated." (As in the Esanā Sutta, it should be expanded.) The Second.
