# SN 45.34 Pāraṅgamasuttaṃ

## SN 45.34 The Discourse on Reaching the Far Shore

34. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Aṭṭhime, bhikkhave, dhammā bhāvitā bahulīkatā apārā pāraṃ gamanāya saṃvattanti. Katame aṭṭha? Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ime kho, bhikkhave, aṭṭha dhammā bhāvitā bahulīkatā apārā pāraṃ gamanāya saṃvattantī’’ti.

34.At Sāvatthī. "Monks, these eight qualities, when developed and cultivated, lead from the near shore to the far shore. What eight? Namely, right view... right samādhi. These eight qualities, monks, when developed and cultivated, lead from the near shore to the far shore."

Idamavoca bhagavā. Idaṃ vatvāna sugato athāparaṃ etadavoca satthā –

The Blessed One said this. Having said this, the Fortunate One, the Teacher, then said further:

‘‘Appakā te manussesu, ye janā pāragāmino;Athāyaṃ itarā pajā, tīramevānudhāvati.

"Few are those among humans who go to the far shore; the rest of humanity only run along the bank on this side."

‘‘Ye ca kho sammadakkhāte, dhamme dhammānuvattino;Te janā pāramessanti, maccudheyyaṃ suduttaraṃ.

"But those who practice according to the well-taught Dhamma, they will cross over this realm of Death, so hard to transcend."

‘‘Kaṇhaṃ dhammaṃ vippahāya, sukkaṃ bhāvetha paṇḍito;Okā anokamāgamma, viveke yattha dūramaṃ.

"The wise person should abandon dark qualities and cultivate bright ones. Having gone from home to homelessness, in seclusion, where delight is hard to find."

‘‘Tatrābhiratimiccheyya, hitvā kāme akiñcano;Pariyodapeyya attānaṃ, cittaklesehi paṇḍito.

"There, let them desire delight, having abandoned sensual pleasures, owning nothing. Let the wise purify themselves from mental defilements."

‘‘Yesaṃ sambodhiyaṅgesu, sammā cittaṃ subhāvitaṃ;Ādānapaṭinissagge, anupādāya ye ratā;Khīṇāsavā jutimanto, te loke parinibbutā’’ti. catutthaṃ;

"Those whose minds are well developed in the factors of enlightenment, who delight in relinquishing attachment, without grasping, with taints destroyed, radiant, they are fully extinguished in the world." The fourth.
