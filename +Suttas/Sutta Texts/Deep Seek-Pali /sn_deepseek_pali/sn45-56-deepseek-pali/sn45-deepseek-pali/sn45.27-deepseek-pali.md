# SN 45.27 Kumbhasuttaṃ

## SN 45.27 The Discourse on the Pot

27. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, kumbho anādhāro suppavattiyo hoti, sādhāro duppavattiyo hoti; evameva kho, bhikkhave, cittaṃ anādhāraṃ suppavattiyaṃ hoti, sādhāraṃ duppavattiyaṃ hoti. Ko ca, bhikkhave, cittassa ādhāro? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ cittassa ādhāro. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, kumbho anādhāro suppavattiyo hoti, sādhāro duppavattiyo hoti; evameva kho, bhikkhave, cittaṃ anādhāraṃ suppavattiyaṃ hoti, sādhāraṃ duppavattiyaṃ hotī’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

27.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, just as a pot without a base is easily overturned, while one with a base is difficult to overturn, so too, monks, the mind without a support is easily swayed, while the mind with a support is difficult to sway. And what, monks, is the mind’s support? It is just this ![[Noble Path]]. This is the mind’s support. Just as a pot without a base is easily overturned, while one with a base is difficult to overturn, so too, monks, the mind without a support is easily swayed, while the mind with a support is difficult to sway.” The Seventh.
