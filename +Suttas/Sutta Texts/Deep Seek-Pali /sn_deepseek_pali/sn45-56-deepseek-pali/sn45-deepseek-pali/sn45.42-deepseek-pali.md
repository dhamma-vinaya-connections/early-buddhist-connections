# SN 45.42 Anupādāparinibbānasuttaṃ

## SN 45.42 The Discourse on Final Nibbāna Without Clinging

48. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Sace vo, bhikkhave, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ puccheyyuṃ – ‘kimatthiyaṃ, āvuso, samaṇe gotame brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’ti, evaṃ puṭṭhā tumhe, bhikkhave, tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ evaṃ byākareyyātha – ‘anupādāparinibbānatthaṃ kho, āvuso, bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ vussatī’ti. Sace pana vo, bhikkhave, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ puccheyyuṃ – ‘atthi panāvuso, maggo, atthi paṭipadā anupādāparinibbānāyā’ti, evaṃ puṭṭhā tumhe, bhikkhave, tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ evaṃ byākareyyātha – ‘atthi kho, āvuso, maggo, atthi paṭipadā anupādāparinibbānāyā’ti. Katamo ca, bhikkhave, maggo, katamā ca paṭipadā anupādāparinibbānāya? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, maggo, ayaṃ paṭipadā anupādāparinibbānāyāti. Evaṃ puṭṭhā tumhe, bhikkhave, tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ evaṃ byākareyyāthā’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

48.At Savatthi. “Monks, if wanderers of other sects were to ask you: ‘For what purpose, friends, is the holy life lived under the ascetic Gotama?’—being asked thus, monks, you should answer those wanderers of other sects thus: ‘It is for final Nibbāna without clinging, friends, that the holy life is lived under the Blessed One.’ If, monks, those wanderers of other sects were to ask you: ‘But is there, friends, a path, is there a practice for final Nibbāna without clinging?’—being asked thus, monks, you should answer those wanderers of other sects thus: ‘There is, friends, a path, there is a practice for final Nibbāna without clinging.’ And what, monks, is that path, what is that practice for final Nibbāna without clinging? It is just this ![[Noble Path]]. This, monks, is the path, this is the practice for final Nibbāna without clinging.’ Being asked thus, monks, you should answer those wanderers of other sects in this way.” The eighth.

Aññatitthiyapeyyālavaggo pañcamo.

The Chapter on the Concise Discourses to Other Sectarians is the Fifth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The Summary:

Virāgasaṃyojanaṃ anusayaṃ, addhānaṃ āsavā khayā;Vijjāvimuttiñāṇañca, anupādāya aṭṭhamī.

Dispassion, fetters, underlying tendencies, the long course, taints, destruction; knowledge and liberation, knowledge and vision; and without clinging is the eighth.

6. Sūriyapeyyālavaggo

6.The Chapter on the Sun (Concise Discourses)
