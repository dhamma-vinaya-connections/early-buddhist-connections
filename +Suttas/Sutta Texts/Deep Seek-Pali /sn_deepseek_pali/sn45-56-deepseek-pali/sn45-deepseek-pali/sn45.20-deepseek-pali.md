# SN 45.20 Tatiyakukkuṭārāmasuttaṃ

## SN 45.20 The Third Discourse at Cock's Park

20. Pāṭaliputtanidānaṃ. ‘‘‘Brahmacariyaṃ, brahmacariya’nti, āvuso ānanda, vuccati. Katamaṃ nu kho, āvuso, brahmacariyaṃ, katamo brahmacārī, katamaṃ brahmacariyapariyosāna’’nti? ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, āvuso bhadda! Bhaddako kho te, āvuso bhadda, ummaṅgo, bhaddakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ, kalyāṇī paripucchā. Evañhi tvaṃ, āvuso bhadda, pucchasi – ‘brahmacariyaṃ, brahmacariyanti, āvuso ānanda, vuccati. Katamaṃ nu kho, āvuso, brahmacariyaṃ, katamo brahmacārī, katamaṃ brahmacariyapariyosāna’’’nti? ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti. ‘‘Ayameva kho, āvuso, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo brahmacariyaṃ, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Yo kho, āvuso, iminā ariyena aṭṭhaṅgikena maggena samannāgato – ayaṃ vuccati brahmacārī. Yo kho, āvuso, rāgakkhayo dosakkhayo mohakkhayo – idaṃ brahmacariyapariyosāna’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

20. The Origin at Pāṭaliputta.
“‘The holy life, the holy life,’ friend Ānanda, is spoken of. But what, friend, is the holy life, who is the one living the holy life, and what is the culmination of the holy life?”
“Good, good, friend Bhadda! Excellent is your approach, friend Bhadda, excellent your insight, and excellent your question. For you ask thus: ‘The holy life, the holy life,’ friend Ānanda, is spoken of. But what, friend, is the holy life, who is the one living the holy life, and what is the culmination of the holy life?’”
“Yes, friend.”
“This Noble Eightfold Path itself, friend, is the holy life, namely: right view... right samādhi. One who is endowed with this Noble Eightfold Path, friend—this one is called one living the holy life. The destruction of lust, the destruction of hatred, the destruction of delusion—this, friend, is the culmination of the holy life.” The tenth.

Tīṇi suttantāni ekanidānāni.Vihāravaggo dutiyo.

Three discourses with a single origin. The Chapter on Dwellings, the Second.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Dve vihārā ca sekkho ca, uppādā apare duve;Parisuddhena dve vuttā, kukkuṭārāmena tayoti.

Two modes of abiding and the trainee, and two others arising; two are spoken of as purified, and three at Kukkuṭārāma.
