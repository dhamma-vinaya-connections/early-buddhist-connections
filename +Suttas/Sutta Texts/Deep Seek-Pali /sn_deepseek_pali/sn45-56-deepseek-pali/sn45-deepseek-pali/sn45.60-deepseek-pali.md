# SN 45.60 Paṭhamapācīnaninnasuttaṃ

## SN 45.60 The First Discourse on Inclining Eastward

115. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gaṅgā nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti amatogadhaṃ amataparāyanaṃ amatapariyosānaṃ…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti amatogadhaṃ amataparāyanaṃ amatapariyosānaṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

115."Just as, bhikkhus, the River Gaṅgā inclines eastward, slopes eastward, tends eastward; so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing and cultivating the Noble Eightfold Path inclines towards Nibbāna, slopes towards Nibbāna, tends towards Nibbāna. And how, bhikkhus, does a bhikkhu developing and cultivating the Noble Eightfold Path incline towards Nibbāna, slope towards Nibbāna, tend towards Nibbāna? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops right view, which is immersed in the Deathless, leading to the Deathless, culminating in the Deathless… he develops right samādhi, which is immersed in the Deathless, leading to the Deathless, culminating in the Deathless. Thus, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing and cultivating the Noble Eightfold Path inclines towards Nibbāna, slopes towards Nibbāna, tends towards Nibbāna." (First).

## 2-6. Dutiyādipācīnaninnasuttapañcakaṃ

### 2–6. The Group of Five Discourses Beginning with the Second, on Inclining Eastward

116. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yamunā nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… dutiyaṃ.

116."Just as, bhikkhus, the River Yamunā inclines eastward, slopes eastward, tends eastward; so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… (Second).

117. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, aciravatī nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… tatiyaṃ.

117."Just as, bhikkhus, the River Aciravatī inclines eastward, slopes eastward, tends eastward; so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… (Third).

118. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, sarabhū nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… catutthaṃ.

118."Just as, bhikkhus, the River Sarabhū inclines eastward, slopes eastward, tends eastward; so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… (Fourth).

119. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, mahī nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… pañcamaṃ.

119."Just as, bhikkhus, the River Mahī inclines eastward, slopes eastward, tends eastward; so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… (Fifth).

120. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yā kācimā mahānadiyo, seyyathidaṃ – gaṅgā, yamunā, aciravatī, sarabhū, mahī, sabbā tā pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… chaṭṭhaṃ.

120."Just as, bhikkhus, all these great rivers—namely, the Gaṅgā, Yamunā, Aciravatī, Sarabhū, and Mahī—all incline eastward, slope eastward, tend eastward; so too, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu… (Sixth).
