# SN 45.62 Paṭhamapācīnaninnasuttaṃ

## SN 45.62 The First Discourse on Leaning Eastward.

127. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gaṅgā nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

127."Just as, bhikkhus, the river Ganges inclines to the east, flows to the east, tends to the east; in the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. And how, bhikkhus, does a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, incline to Nibbāna, flow to Nibbāna, tend to Nibbāna? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops Right View, which inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna... he develops Right samādhi, which inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. Thus, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna." First.

## 2-6. Dutiyādipācīnaninnasuttapañcakaṃ

### 2-6. The Pentad of Discourses Beginning with the Second, on Leaning Eastward.

128-132. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yamunā nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, aciravatī nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, sarabhū nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, mahī nadī pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu…pe… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yā kācimā mahānadiyo, seyyathidaṃ – gaṅgā, yamunā, aciravatī, sarabhū, mahī, sabbā tā pācīnaninnā pācīnapoṇā pācīnapabbhārā; evameva kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammādiṭṭhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ…pe… sammāsamādhiṃ bhāveti nibbānaninnaṃ nibbānapoṇaṃ nibbānapabbhāraṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bhāvento ariyaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ bahulīkaronto nibbānaninno hoti nibbānapoṇo nibbānapabbhāro’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

128-132. "Just as, bhikkhus, the river Yamunā inclines to the east, flows to the east, tends to the east; in the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. Just as, bhikkhus, the river Aciravatī inclines to the east, flows to the east, tends to the east; in the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. Just as, bhikkhus, the river Sarabhū inclines to the east, flows to the east, tends to the east; in the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. Just as, bhikkhus, the river Mahī inclines to the east, flows to the east, tends to the east; in the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. Just as, bhikkhus, all these great rivers—namely, the Ganges, the Yamunā, the Aciravatī, the Sarabhū, and the Mahī—all incline to the east, flow to the east, tend to the east; in the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. And how, bhikkhus, does a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, incline to Nibbāna, flow to Nibbāna, tend to Nibbāna? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops Right View, which inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna... he develops Right samādhi, which inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna. Thus, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu developing the Noble Eightfold Path, making it much-practiced, inclines to Nibbāna, flows to Nibbāna, tends to Nibbāna." Sixth.
