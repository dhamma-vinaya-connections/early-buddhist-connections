# SN 45.11 Paṭhamavihārasuttaṃ

## SN 45.11 The first discourse on abiding.

11. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Icchāmahaṃ, bhikkhave, aḍḍhamāsaṃ paṭisalliyituṃ. Namhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakenā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissutvā nāssudha koci bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamati, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena.

11. At Sāvatthī. "Bhikkhus, I want to go into seclusion for half a month. Let no one come to me except the one who brings my almsfood." "Yes, venerable sir," those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One, and no one approached the Blessed One except the one who brought his almsfood.

Atha kho bhagavā tassa aḍḍhamāsassa accayena paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘yena svāhaṃ, bhikkhave, vihārena paṭhamābhisambuddho viharāmi, tassa padesena vihāsiṃ. So evaṃ pajānāmi – ‘micchādiṭṭhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; sammādiṭṭhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ…pe… micchāsamādhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; sammāsamādhipaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; chandapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; vitakkapaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; saññāpaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; chando ca avūpasanto hoti, vitakko ca avūpasanto hoti, saññā ca avūpasantā hoti, tappaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; chando ca vūpasanto hoti, vitakko ca vūpasanto hoti, saññā ca vūpasantā hoti, tappaccayāpi vedayitaṃ; appattassa pattiyā atthi āyāmaṃ [vāyāmaṃ (sī. syā.)], tasmimpi ṭhāne anuppatte tappaccayāpi vedayita’’’nti. Paṭhamaṃ.

Then the Blessed One, after that half-month had passed and having risen from seclusion, addressed the bhikkhus: "Bhikkhus, I dwelt in an aspect of that abiding by which I first became fully enlightened. I understood thus: 'There is feeling conditioned by wrong view; there is feeling conditioned by right view... there is feeling conditioned by wrong samādhi; there is feeling conditioned by right samādhi; there is feeling conditioned by desire; there is feeling conditioned by thought; there is feeling conditioned by perception; when desire is unappeased, thought is unappeased, perception is unappeased, there is feeling conditioned thereby; when desire is appeased, thought is appeased, perception is appeased, there is feeling conditioned thereby; when there is striving for the attainment of the unattained, and when that state has not yet been attained, there is feeling conditioned thereby.'" The first.
