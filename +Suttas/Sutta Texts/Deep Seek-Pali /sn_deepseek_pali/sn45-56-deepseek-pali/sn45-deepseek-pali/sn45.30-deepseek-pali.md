# SN 45.30 Uttiyasuttaṃ

## SN 45.30 The Uttiya Discourse

30. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Atha kho āyasmā uttiyo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami…pe… ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā uttiyo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idha mayhaṃ, bhante, rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘pañca kāmaguṇā vuttā bhagavatā. Katame nu kho pañca kāmaguṇā vuttā bhagavatā’’’ti? ‘‘Sādhu sādhu, uttiya! Pañcime kho, uttiya, kāmaguṇā vuttā mayā. Katame pañca? Cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā, sotaviññeyyā saddā…pe… ghānaviññeyyā gandhā… jivhāviññeyyā rasā… kāyaviññeyyā phoṭṭhabbā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā – ime kho, uttiya, pañca kāmaguṇā vuttā mayā. Imesaṃ kho, uttiya, pañcannaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ pahānāya ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo. Katamo ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo? Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Imesaṃ kho, uttiya, pañcannaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ pahānāya ayaṃ ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo bhāvetabbo’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

30.At Sāvatthī. Then the Venerable Uttiya approached the Blessed One… and said to him while seated to one side: “Venerable sir, while I was alone in seclusion, this thought came to my mind: ‘The Blessed One has spoken of the five cords of sensual pleasure. What are the five cords of sensual pleasure spoken of by the Blessed One?’” “Good, good, Uttiya! These five cords of sensual pleasure have been spoken of by me. What five? Forms cognizable by the eye that are desirable, agreeable, charming, pleasing, connected with sensual desire, and arousing passion; sounds cognizable by the ear… odors cognizable by the nose… flavors cognizable by the tongue… tangibles cognizable by the body that are desirable, agreeable, charming, pleasing, connected with sensual desire, and arousing passion. These, Uttiya, are the five cords of sensual pleasure spoken of by me. For the abandoning of these five cords of sensual pleasure, the ![[Noble Path]]. For the abandoning of these five cords of sensual pleasure, this Noble Eightfold Path is to be developed.” The Tenth.

Micchattavaggo tatiyo.

The Chapter on Wrongness, the Third.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The Summary:

Micchattaṃ akusalaṃ dhammaṃ, duve paṭipadāpi ca;Asappurisena dve kumbho, samādhi vedanuttiyenāti.

Wrongness, unwholesome states, and two practices; Two on the unworthy person, the pot, samādhi, and feeling with Uttiya.

4. Paṭipattivaggo

4.The Practice Chapter
