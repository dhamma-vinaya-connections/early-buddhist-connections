# SN 34.19 Samāpattimūlakasappāyakārīsuttaṃ

## SN 34.19 The Discourse on Suitable Actions, Rooted in Attainment

680. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī hoti, na samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī neva samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gavā khīraṃ, khīramhā dadhi, dadhimhā navanītaṃ, navanītamhā sappi, sappimhā sappimaṇḍo tatra aggamakkhāyati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Ekūnavīsatimaṃ. (Samāpattimūlakaṃ.)

680.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, there are these four meditators. Which four? Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in attaining samādhi, but not in what is conducive to samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in what is conducive to samādhi, but not in attaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is neither skilled in attaining samādhi, nor in what is conducive to samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is both skilled in attaining samādhi and in what is conducive to samādhi. Among these four meditators, monks, the meditator who is both skilled in attaining samādhi and in what is conducive to samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent. Just as, monks, from cows comes milk, from milk comes curd, from curd comes butter, from butter comes ghee, and from ghee comes cream of ghee, which is called the best; so too, monks, the meditator who is both skilled in attaining samādhi and in what is conducive to samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent of these four meditators.” The nineteenth. (Rooted in Attainment.)

## 20-27. Ṭhitimūlakavuṭṭhānasuttādiaṭṭhakaṃ

### 20-27. The Octad Beginning with the Sutta on Emergence Rooted in Stability

681-688. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī neva samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī…pe… uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Vīsatimaṃ. (Purimamūlakāni viya yāva sattavīsatimā ṭhitimūlakasappāyakārīsuttā aṭṭha suttāni pūretabbāni. Ṭhitimūlakaṃ.)

681-688. At Sāvatthī. "Bhikkhus, there are these four kinds of meditators. Which four? Here, Bhikkhus, a certain meditator is skilled in stability in samādhi, but not skilled in emerging from samādhi. Here, Bhikkhus, a certain meditator is skilled in emerging from samādhi, but not skilled in stability in samādhi. Here, Bhikkhus, a certain meditator is neither skilled in stability in samādhi, nor skilled in emerging from samādhi. Here, Bhikkhus, a certain meditator is both skilled in stability in samādhi and skilled in emerging from samādhi. Among these, Bhikkhus, that meditator... is the foremost and best." The twentieth. (Like the preceding roots, the eight suttas should be completed up to the twenty-seventh Sutta on Doing What is Suitable, Rooted in Stability. The root of stability.)

## 28-34. Vuṭṭhānamūlakakallitasuttādisattakaṃ

### 28-34. The Heptad Beginning with the Sutta on Pliancy Rooted in Emergence

689-695. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo… samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo… neva samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo… samādhismiṃ vuṭṭhānakusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī…pe… uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Aṭṭhavīsatimaṃ. (Purimamūlakāni viya yāva catuttiṃsatimā vuṭṭhānamūlakasappāyakārīsuttā satta suttāni pūretabbāni. Vuṭṭhānamūlakaṃ.)

689-695. At Sāvatthī. "Bhikkhus, there are these four kinds of meditators. Which four? Here, Bhikkhus, a certain meditator is skilled in emerging from samādhi, but not skilled in pliancy in samādhi... skilled in pliancy in samādhi, but not skilled in emerging from samādhi... neither skilled in emerging from samādhi, nor skilled in pliancy in samādhi... both skilled in emerging from samādhi and skilled in pliancy in samādhi. Among these, Bhikkhus, that meditator... is the foremost and best." The twenty-eighth. (Like the preceding roots, the seven suttas should be completed up to the thirty-fourth Sutta on Doing What is Suitable, Rooted in Emergence. The root of emergence.)

## 35-40. Kallitamūlakaārammaṇasuttādichakkaṃ

### 35-40. The Hexad Beginning with the Sutta on Object Rooted in Pliancy

696-701. Sāvatthinidānaṃ … ‘‘samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo… samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo… neva samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo… samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī…pe… uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Pañcatiṃsatimaṃ. (Purimamūlakāni viya yāva cattālīsamā kallitamūlakasappāyakārīsuttā cha suttāni pūretabbāni. Kallitamūlakaṃ.)

696-701. At Sāvatthī... "One is skilled in pliancy in samādhi, but not skilled in the object of samādhi... One is skilled in the object of samādhi, but not skilled in pliancy in samādhi... One is neither skilled in pliancy in samādhi, nor skilled in the object of samādhi... One is both skilled in pliancy in samādhi and skilled in the object of samādhi. Among these, Bhikkhus, that meditator... is the foremost and best." The thirty-fifth. (Like the preceding roots, the six suttas should be completed up to the fortieth Sutta on Doing What is Suitable, Rooted in Pliancy. The root of pliancy.)

## 41-45. Ārammaṇamūlakagocarasuttādipañcakaṃ

### 41-45. The Pentad Beginning with the Sutta on Range Rooted in Object

702-706. Sāvatthinidānaṃ … ‘‘samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo… samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo… neva samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo… samādhismiṃ ārammaṇakusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī…pe… uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Ekacattālīsamaṃ. (Purimamūlakāni viya yāva pañcacattālīsamā ārammaṇamūlakasappāyakārīsuttā pañca suttāni pūretabbāni. Ārammaṇamūlakaṃ.)

702-706. At Sāvatthī... "One is skilled in the object of samādhi, but not skilled in the range of samādhi... One is skilled in the range of samādhi, but not skilled in the object of samādhi... One is neither skilled in the object of samādhi, nor skilled in the range of samādhi... One is both skilled in the object of samādhi and skilled in the range of samādhi. Among these, Bhikkhus, that meditator... is the foremost and best." The forty-first. (Like the preceding roots, the five suttas should be completed up to the forty-fifth Sutta on Doing What is Suitable, Rooted in Object. The root of the object.)

## 46-49. Gocaramūlakaabhinīhārasuttādicatukkaṃ

### 46-49. The Group of Four Discourses Beginning with the Discourse on Range and Application

707. Sāvatthinidānaṃ… ‘‘samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo… samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo… neva samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo… samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo ca… seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gavā khīraṃ, khīramhā dadhi, dadhimhā navanītaṃ, navanītamhā sappi, sappimhā sappimaṇḍo tatra aggamakkhāyati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ…pe… uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Chacattālīsamaṃ.

707.At Sāvatthī. “Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is skilled in the range of samādhi, but not skilled in the application of samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is skilled in the application of samādhi, but not skilled in the range of samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is neither skilled in the range of samādhi nor skilled in the application of samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is both skilled in the range of samādhi and skilled in the application of samādhi. Just as, bhikkhus, milk comes from a cow, curds from milk, fresh butter from curds, ghee from fresh butter, and cream of ghee is said to be the best of these; in the same way, bhikkhus, that meditator who is skilled in the range of samādhi and skilled in the application of samādhi is the supreme and most excellent among these four meditators.” The forty-sixth.

708. Samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī…pe…. Vitthāretabbaṃ. Sattacattālīsamaṃ.

708.One is skilled in the range of samādhi, but not conscientious in samādhi… (To be elaborated.) The forty-seventh.

709. Samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī…pe…. Aṭṭhacattālīsamaṃ.

709.One is skilled in the range of samādhi, but not constant in samādhi… The forty-eighth.

710. Samādhismiṃ gocarakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī…pe…. Ekūnapaññāsamaṃ. (Gocaramūlakaṃ.)

710.One is skilled in the range of samādhi, but does not act suitably in samādhi… The forty-ninth. (Rooted in Range.)

## 50-52. Abhinīhāramūlakasakkaccasuttāditikaṃ

### 50-52. The Group of Three Discourses Beginning with the Discourse on Application and Conscientiousness

711. Sāvatthinidānaṃ … ‘‘samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī… samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī hoti, na samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo… neva samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī… samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī…pe… uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Paññāsamaṃ.

711.At Sāvatthī. “Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is skilled in the application of samādhi, but not conscientious in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is conscientious in samādhi, but not skilled in the application of samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is neither skilled in the application of samādhi nor conscientious in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is both skilled in the application of samādhi and conscientious in samādhi. Among these, bhikkhus, that meditator who… is the supreme and most excellent.” The fiftieth.

712. Samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī…pe…. Ekapaññāsamaṃ.

712.One is skilled in the application of samādhi, but not constant in samādhi… The fifty-first.

713. Samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī…pe…. Dvepaññāsamaṃ. (Abhinīhāramūlakaṃ.)

713.One is skilled in the application of samādhi, but does not act suitably in samādhi… The fifty-second. (Rooted in Application.)

## 53-54. Sakkaccamūlakasātaccakārīsuttādidukaṃ

### 53-54. The Group of Two Discourses Beginning with the Discourse on Conscientiousness and Constancy

714. Sāvatthinidānaṃ … ‘‘samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī hoti, na samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī… samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī hoti, na samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī … neva samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī… samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī ca hoti, samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ…pe… uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Tepaññāsamaṃ.

714.At Sāvatthī. “Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is conscientious in samādhi, but not constant in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is constant in samādhi, but not conscientious in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is neither conscientious in samādhi nor constant in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, a meditator is both conscientious in samādhi and constant in samādhi. Among these, bhikkhus, that meditator who… is the supreme and most excellent.” The fifty-third.

715. Samādhismiṃ sakkaccakārī hoti, na samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī…pe…. Catupaññāsamaṃ.

715.One is conscientious in samādhi, but does not act suitably in samādhi… The fifty-fourth.
