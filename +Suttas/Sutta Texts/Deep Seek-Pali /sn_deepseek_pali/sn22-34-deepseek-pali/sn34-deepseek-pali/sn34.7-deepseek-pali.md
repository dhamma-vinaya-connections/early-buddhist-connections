# SN 34.7 Samādhimūlakaabhinīhārasuttaṃ

## SN 34.7 The Discourse on Application Rooted in samādhi

668. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī neva samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ abhinīhārakusalo ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gavā khīraṃ…pe… pavaro cā’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

668.At Savatthi. "Monks, there are these four meditators. Which four? Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in samādhi, but is not skilled in application. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in application, but is not skilled in samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is neither skilled in samādhi nor skilled in application. Here, monks, a certain meditator is both skilled in samādhi and skilled in application. Among these four meditators, monks, the meditator who is both skilled in samādhi and skilled in application is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent. Just as, monks, from a cow comes milk... and the most excellent." The seventh.
