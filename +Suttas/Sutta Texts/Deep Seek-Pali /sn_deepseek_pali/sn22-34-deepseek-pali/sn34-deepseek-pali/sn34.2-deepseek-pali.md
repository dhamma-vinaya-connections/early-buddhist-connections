# SN 34.2 Samādhimūlakaṭhitisuttaṃ

## SN 34.2 The Discourse on Stability Rooted in samādhi

663. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī neva samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gavā khīraṃ, khīramhā dadhi, dadhimhā navanītaṃ, navanītamhā sappi, sappimhā sappimaṇḍo tatra aggamakkhāyati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

663.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, there are these four meditators. Which four? Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in attaining samādhi, but not skilled in maintaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in maintaining samādhi, but not skilled in attaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is neither skilled in attaining samādhi, nor skilled in maintaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in attaining samādhi and skilled in maintaining samādhi. Among these four meditators, monks, the meditator who is skilled in attaining samādhi and skilled in maintaining samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent. Just as, monks, from cows comes milk, from milk comes curd, from curd comes fresh butter, from fresh butter comes ghee, and from ghee comes cream of ghee—which is said to be the best—so too, monks, the meditator who is skilled in attaining samādhi and skilled in maintaining samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent of these four meditators.” The Second.
