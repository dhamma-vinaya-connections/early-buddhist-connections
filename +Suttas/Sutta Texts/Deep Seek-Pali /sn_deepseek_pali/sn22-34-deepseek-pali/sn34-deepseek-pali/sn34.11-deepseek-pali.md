# SN 34.11 Samāpattimūlakaṭhitisuttaṃ

## SN 34.11 The Discourse on Stability Rooted in Attainment

672. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī neva samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ samāpattikusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ ṭhitikusalo ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gavā khīraṃ…pe… pavaro cā’’ti. Ekādasamaṃ.

672.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, there are these four meditators. Which four? Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in attaining samādhi, but not skilled in maintaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in maintaining samādhi, but not skilled in attaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is neither skilled in attaining samādhi nor skilled in maintaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled both in attaining samādhi and in maintaining samādhi. Among these four meditators, monks, the meditator who is skilled both in attaining samādhi and in maintaining samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent. Just as, monks, from a cow comes milk… and the most excellent.”
