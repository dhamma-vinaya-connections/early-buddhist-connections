# SN 34.20 Sātaccamūlakasappāyakārīsuttaṃ

## SN 34.20 The Discourse on Constancy and Suitable Action

716. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Catārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī hoti, na samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī hoti, na samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī neva samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī ca hoti, samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī ca hoti samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gavā khīraṃ, khīramhā dadhi, dadhimhā navanītaṃ, navanītamhā sappi, sappimhā sappimaṇḍo tatra aggamakkhāyati; evameva kho, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ sātaccakārī ca hoti, samādhismiṃ sappāyakārī ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro cā’’ti. Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti. Pañcapaññāsamaṃ. (Yathā pañcapaññāsaṃ veyyākaraṇāni honti tathā vitthāretabbāni.)

716.At Sāvatthī. “Bhikkhus, there are these four meditators. Which four? Here, bhikkhus, one meditator is constant in samādhi, but does not act suitably in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, one meditator acts suitably in samādhi, but is not constant in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, one meditator is neither constant in samādhi nor acts suitably in samādhi. Here, bhikkhus, one meditator is both constant in samādhi and acts suitably in samādhi. Among these four meditators, bhikkhus, the meditator who is both constant in samādhi and acts suitably in samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent. Just as, bhikkhus, milk comes from a cow, curds from milk, fresh butter from curds, ghee from fresh butter, and cream of ghee is said to be the best of these; in the same way, bhikkhus, that meditator who is both constant in samādhi and acts suitably in samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent among these four meditators.” The Blessed One said this. The bhikkhus were satisfied and delighted in the Blessed One’s words. The fifty-fifth.

Jhānasaṃyuttaṃ [samādhisaṃyuttaṃ (syā. kaṃ.)] samattaṃ.

The Saṃyutta on Jhāna is concluded.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Samādhi samāpatti ṭhiti ca, vuṭṭhānaṃ kallitārammaṇena ca;Gocarā abhinīhāro sakkacca, sātacca athopi sappāyanti.

samādhi, attainment, steadiness, emergence, and malleability of the object; range, application, conscientiousness, constancy, and also suitability.

Khandhavaggo tatiyo.

The Third Chapter: The Division on the Aggregates

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Khandha rādhasaṃyuttañca, diṭṭhiokkanta [okkanti (sabbattha)] uppādā;Kilesa sāriputtā ca, nāgā supaṇṇa gandhabbā;Valāha vacchajhānanti, khandhavaggamhi terasāti.

The Aggregates, the Connected Discourses with Rādhā, Views, Going Astray, Arising, Defilements, Sāriputta, Nāgas, Supaṇṇas, Gandhabba, Clouds, Vacchas, and Jhāna—thirteen in the Division on the Aggregates.

Khandhavaggasaṃyuttapāḷi niṭṭhitā.

The Pali of the Connected Discourses on the Aggregates is concluded.
