# SN 34.4 Samādhimūlakakallitasuttaṃ

## SN 34.4 The Discourse on Refinement Rooted in samādhi

665. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave, jhāyī. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo hoti, na samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī neva samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo hoti, na ca samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo. Idha pana, bhikkhave, ekacco jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo ca hoti, samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo ca. Tatra, bhikkhave, yvāyaṃ jhāyī samādhismiṃ samādhikusalo ca hoti samādhismiṃ kallitakusalo ca ayaṃ imesaṃ catunnaṃ jhāyīnaṃ aggo ca seṭṭho ca mokkho ca uttamo ca pavaro ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, gavā khīraṃ…pe… pavaro cā’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

665.At Sāvatthī. “Monks, there are these four meditators. Which four? Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in attaining samādhi, but not skilled in refining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in refining samādhi, but not skilled in attaining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is neither skilled in attaining samādhi, nor skilled in refining samādhi. Here, monks, a certain meditator is skilled in attaining samādhi and skilled in refining samādhi. Among these four meditators, monks, the meditator who is skilled in attaining samādhi and skilled in refining samādhi is the foremost, the best, the chief, the highest, and the most excellent. Just as, monks, from a cow comes milk… and the most excellent.” The Fourth.
