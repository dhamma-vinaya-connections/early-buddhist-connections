# SN 22.56 Upādānaparipavattasuttaṃ

## SN 22.56 The Discourse on the Revolving of Clinging

56. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Pañcime, bhikkhave, upādānakkhandhā. Katame pañca? Rūpupādānakkhandho, vedanupādānakkhandho, saññupādānakkhandho, saṅkhārupādānakkhandho, viññāṇupādānakkhandho. Yāvakīvañcāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ime pañcupādānakkhandhe catuparivaṭṭaṃ yathābhūtaṃ nābbhaññāsiṃ, neva tāvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sadevake loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya anuttaraṃ sammāsambodhiṃ abhisambuddhoti paccaññāsiṃ. Yato ca khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ime pañcupādānakkhandhe catuparivaṭṭaṃ yathābhūtaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ, athāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sadevake loke…pe… sadevamanussāya anuttaraṃ sammāsambodhiṃ abhisambuddhoti paccaññāsiṃ’’.

56.At Savatthi. “Bhikkhus, there are these five aggregates of clinging. Which five? The aggregate of clinging to form, the aggregate of clinging to feeling, the aggregate of clinging to perception, the aggregate of clinging to formations, the aggregate of clinging to consciousness. As long as I did not directly comprehend, bhikkhus, these five aggregates of clinging in their fourfold aspect as they really are, I did not proclaim myself awakened to the unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment in this world with its devas, Māra, and Brahmā, in this generation with its contemplatives and brahmins, its devas and humans. But when I directly comprehended, bhikkhus, these five aggregates of clinging in their fourfold aspect as they really are, then I proclaimed myself awakened to the unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment in this world with its devas, Māra, and Brahmā, in this generation with its contemplatives and brahmins, its devas and humans.”

‘‘Kathañca catuparivaṭṭaṃ? Rūpaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ, rūpasamudayaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ, rūpanirodhaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ, rūpanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ; vedanaṃ… saññaṃ… saṅkhāre… viññāṇaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ, viññāṇasamudayaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ, viññāṇanirodhaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ, viññāṇanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ.

“And how is it fourfold? I directly comprehended form, directly comprehended the origin of form, directly comprehended the cessation of form, directly comprehended the path leading to the cessation of form; I directly comprehended feeling… perception… volitional formations… consciousness, directly comprehended the origin of consciousness, directly comprehended the cessation of consciousness, directly comprehended the path leading to the cessation of consciousness.

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, rūpaṃ? Cattāro ca mahābhūtā catunnañca mahābhūtānaṃ upādāya rūpaṃ. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, rūpaṃ. Āhārasamudayā rūpasamudayo; āhāranirodhā rūpanirodho. Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo rūpanirodhagāminī paṭipadā, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi.

“And what, bhikkhus, is form? The four great elements and form dependent on the four great elements. This, bhikkhus, is called form. With the arising of nutriment, there is the arising of form; with the cessation of nutriment, there is the cessation of form. This ![[Noble Path]].”

‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ rūpaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ rūpasamudayaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ rūpanirodhaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ rūpanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya rūpassa nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipannā, te suppaṭipannā. Ye suppaṭipannā, te imasmiṃ dhammavinaye gādhanti.

“Whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who, having directly comprehended form in this way, having directly comprehended the origin of form, having directly comprehended the cessation of form, having directly comprehended the path leading to the cessation of form, are practicing for weariness, dispassion, and cessation regarding form—they are practicing well. Those who are practicing well have a firm footing in this Dhamma-Vinaya.”

‘‘Ye ca kho keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ rūpaṃ abhiññāya…pe… evaṃ rūpanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya, rūpassa nibbidā virāgā nirodhā anupādā vimuttā te suvimuttā. Ye suvimuttā te kevalino. Ye kevalino vaṭṭaṃ tesaṃ natthi paññāpanāya.

“Those, bhikkhus, who are contemplatives or brahmins who, having directly comprehended form thus... and having directly comprehended the path leading to the cessation of form, are liberated through weariness, dispassion, cessation, and non-clinging regarding form—they are well-liberated. Those who are well-liberated are consummate. For those who are consummate, there is no ground for designation.”

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, vedanā? Chayime, bhikkhave, vedanākāyā – cakkhusamphassajā vedanā, sotasamphassajā vedanā, ghānasamphassajā vedanā, jivhāsamphassajā vedanā, kāyasamphassajā vedanā, manosamphassajā vedanā. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, vedanā. Phassasamudayā vedanāsamudayo; phassanirodhā vedanānirodho. Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo vedanānirodhagāminī paṭipadā, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi.

“And what, bhikkhus, is feeling? These are the six classes of feeling: feeling born of eye-contact, feeling born of ear-contact, feeling born of nose-contact, feeling born of tongue-contact, feeling born of body-contact, feeling born of mind-contact. This, bhikkhus, is called feeling. With the arising of contact comes the arising of feeling; with the cessation of contact comes the cessation of feeling. This ![[Noble Path]].”

‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ vedanaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ vedanāsamudayaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ vedanānirodhaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ vedanānirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya vedanāya nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipannā, te suppaṭipannā. Ye suppaṭipannā, te imasmiṃ dhammavinaye gādhanti.

“Whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who thus directly comprehend feeling, thus directly comprehend feeling's origin, thus directly comprehend feeling's cessation, and thus directly comprehend the path leading to feeling's cessation, who are practicing for weariness with, dispassion toward, and cessation of feeling—they are practicing well. Those who are practicing well have a firm footing in this Dhamma and Discipline.”

‘‘Ye ca kho keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ vedanaṃ abhiññāya…pe… evaṃ vedanānirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya…pe… vaṭṭaṃ tesaṃ natthi paññāpanāya.

“But whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who, having directly comprehended feeling thus, thus directly comprehended its origin, thus directly comprehended its cessation, and thus directly comprehended the path leading to its cessation, through weariness, dispassion, cessation, and non-clinging are liberated—they are well-liberated. Those who are well-liberated are consummate. For those who are consummate, there is no ground for designation.”

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, saññā? Chayime, bhikkhave, saññākāyā – rūpasaññā, saddasaññā, gandhasaññā, rasasaññā, phoṭṭhabbasaññā, dhammasaññā. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, saññā. Phassasamudayā saññāsamudayo; phassanirodhā saññānirodho. Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo saññānirodhagāminī paṭipadā, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi…pe… vaṭṭaṃ tesaṃ natthi paññāpanāya.

“And what, bhikkhus, is perception? There are these six classes of perception: perception of forms, perception of sounds, perception of smells, perception of tastes, perception of tangibles, perception of mental objects. This, bhikkhus, is called perception. With the arising of contact there is the arising of perception; with the cessation of contact there is the cessation of perception. This ![[Noble Path]]. Whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who, having directly comprehended perception thus, thus directly comprehended its origin, thus directly comprehended its cessation, and thus directly comprehended the path leading to its cessation, are practicing for weariness, dispassion, and cessation regarding perception—they are practicing well. Those who are practicing well have a firm footing in this Dhamma-Vinaya. But whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who, having directly comprehended perception thus, thus directly comprehended its origin, thus directly comprehended its cessation, and thus directly comprehended the path leading to its cessation, through weariness, dispassion, cessation, and non-clinging are liberated—they are well-liberated. Those who are well-liberated are consummate. For those who are consummate, there is no ground for designation.”

‘‘Katame ca, bhikkhave, saṅkhārā? Chayime, bhikkhave, cetanākāyā – rūpasañcetanā, saddasañcetanā, gandhasañcetanā, rasasañcetanā, phoṭṭhabbasañcetanā, dhammasañcetanā. Ime vuccanti, bhikkhave, saṅkhārā. Phassasamudayā saṅkhārasamudayo; phassanirodhā saṅkhāranirodho. Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo saṅkhāranirodhagāminī paṭipadā, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi.

“And what, bhikkhus, are fabrications? These six classes of volition, bhikkhus, are fabrications: volition of forms, volition of sounds, volition of smells, volition of tastes, volition of tangibles, volition of mental objects. These are called fabrications, bhikkhus. With the arising of contact comes the arising of fabrications. With the cessation of contact comes the cessation of fabrications. This ![[Noble Path]].”

‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ saṅkhāre abhiññāya, evaṃ saṅkhārasamudayaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ saṅkhāranirodhaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ saṅkhāranirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya saṅkhārānaṃ nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipannā, te suppaṭipannā. Ye suppaṭipannā, te imasmiṃ dhammavinaye gādhanti.

“Whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who thus directly comprehend volitional formations, their origin, their cessation, and the way leading to their cessation, and are practicing for weariness with, dispassion toward, and cessation of volitional formations—they are practicing well. Those who are practicing well have a firm footing in this Dhamma and Discipline.”

‘‘Ye ca kho keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ saṅkhāre abhiññāya, evaṃ saṅkhārasamudayaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ saṅkhāranirodhaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ saṅkhāranirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya saṅkhārānaṃ nibbidā virāgā nirodhā anupādā vimuttā, te suvimuttā. Ye suvimuttā, te kevalino. Ye kevalino vaṭṭaṃ tesaṃ natthi paññāpanāya.

“But whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who thus directly comprehend volitional formations, thus directly comprehend the arising of volitional formations, thus directly comprehend the cessation of volitional formations, thus directly comprehend the path leading to the cessation of volitional formations, and through weariness, dispassion, cessation, and non-clinging are liberated—they are well-liberated. Those who are well-liberated are consummate. For those who are consummate, there is no ground for designation.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, viññāṇaṃ? Chayime, bhikkhave, viññāṇakāyā – cakkhuviññāṇaṃ, sotaviññāṇaṃ, ghānaviññāṇaṃ, jivhāviññāṇaṃ, kāyaviññāṇaṃ, manoviññāṇaṃ. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, viññāṇaṃ. Nāmarūpasamudayā viññāṇasamudayo; nāmarūpanirodhā viññāṇanirodho. Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo viññāṇanirodhagāminī paṭipadā, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi.

“And what, bhikkhus, is consciousness? There are these six classes of consciousness: eye-consciousness, ear-consciousness, nose-consciousness, tongue-consciousness, body-consciousness, and mind-consciousness. This is called consciousness. With the arising of name and form comes the arising of consciousness; with the cessation of name and form comes the cessation of consciousness. This ![[Noble Path]].”

‘‘Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ viññāṇaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ viññāṇasamudayaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ viññāṇanirodhaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ viññāṇanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya viññāṇassa nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipannā, te suppaṭipannā. Ye suppaṭipannā, te imasmiṃ dhammavinaye gādhanti.

“Bhikkhus, any contemplatives or brahmins whatsoever who, having directly comprehended consciousness in this way, directly comprehend the origin of consciousness in this way, directly comprehend the cessation of consciousness in this way, directly comprehend the path leading to the cessation of consciousness in this way, are practicing for weariness of consciousness, for its fading away, for its cessation—they are practicing well. Those who are practicing well have gained a firm footing in this Dhamma and Discipline.”

‘‘Ye ca kho keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā evaṃ viññāṇaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ viññāṇasamudayaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ viññāṇanirodhaṃ abhiññāya, evaṃ viññāṇanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abhiññāya viññāṇassa nibbidā virāgā nirodhā anupādā vimuttā, te suvimuttā. Ye suvimuttā, te kevalino. Ye kevalino vaṭṭaṃ tesaṃ natthi paññāpanāyā’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

“And whatever contemplatives or brahmins, bhikkhus, who thus directly comprehend consciousness, thus directly comprehend the arising of consciousness, thus directly comprehend the cessation of consciousness, and thus directly comprehend the path leading to the cessation of consciousness, and who through weariness, dispassion, cessation, and non-clinging regarding consciousness are liberated—they are well-liberated. Those who are well-liberated are consummate. For those who are consummate, there is no ground for designation.”
