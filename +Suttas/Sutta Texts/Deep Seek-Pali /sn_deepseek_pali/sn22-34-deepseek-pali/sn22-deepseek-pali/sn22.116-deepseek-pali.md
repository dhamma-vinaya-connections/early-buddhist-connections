116.At Savatthi. Seated to one side, that monk said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, they speak of 'a speaker of the Dhamma.' In what way, venerable sir, is one a speaker of the Dhamma? In what way is one practicing in accordance with the Dhamma? In what way is one who has attained NibbÄna in this very life?" "Monk, if one teaches the Dhamma for the disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation of form, one is worthy to be called 'a monk who is a speaker of the Dhamma.' If one practices for the disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation of form, one is worthy to be called 'a monk who is practicing in accordance with the Dhamma.' If one is liberated through non-clinging by disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation of form, one is worthy to be called 'a monk who has attained NibbÄna in this very life.' Monk, if one teaches the Dhamma for the disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation of feeling... perception... volitional formations... consciousness, one is worthy to be called 'a monk who is a speaker of the Dhamma.' If one practices for the disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation of consciousness, one is worthy to be called 'a monk who is practicing in accordance with the Dhamma.' If one is liberated through non-clinging by disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation of consciousness, one is worthy to be called 'a monk who has attained NibbÄna in this very life.'" The fourth.
