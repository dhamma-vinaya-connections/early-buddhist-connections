# SN 28.10 Sūcimukhīsuttaṃ

## SN 28.10 The Sūcimukhī Discourse

341. Ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā sāriputto rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya rājagahe piṇḍāya pāvisi. Rājagahe sapadānaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā taṃ piṇḍapātaṃ aññataraṃ kuṭṭamūlaṃ [kuḍḍamūlaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ.), kuḍḍaṃ (pī.)] nissāya paribhuñjati. Atha kho sūcimukhī paribbājikā yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca –

341.At one time Venerable Sāriputta was dwelling in Rājagaha, in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels’ Feeding Place. Then, in the morning, Venerable Sāriputta dressed, took his bowl and robe, and entered Rājagaha for alms. After wandering for alms from house to house in Rājagaha, he ate his almsfood leaning against the root of a certain wall. Then the female bhikkhu Sūcimukhī approached Venerable Sāriputta. Having approached, she said this to Venerable Sāriputta:

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, samaṇa, adhomukho bhuñjasī’’ti? ‘‘Na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, adhomukho bhuñjāmī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samaṇa, ubbhamukho [uddhaṃmukho (sī. aṭṭha.)] bhuñjasī’’ti? ‘‘Na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, ubbhamukho bhuñjāmī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samaṇa, disāmukho bhuñjasī’’ti? ‘‘Na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, disāmukho bhuñjāmī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samaṇa, vidisāmukho bhuñjasī’’ti? ‘‘Na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, vidisāmukho bhuñjāmī’’ti.

‘‘Ascetic, do you eat with your face downwards?’’ ‘‘Sister, I do not eat with my face downwards.’’ ‘‘Then, ascetic, do you eat with your face upwards?’’ ‘‘Sister, I do not eat with my face upwards.’’ ‘‘Then, ascetic, do you eat facing a direction?’’ ‘‘Sister, I do not eat facing a direction.’’ ‘‘Then, ascetic, do you eat facing an intermediate direction?’’ ‘‘Sister, I do not eat facing an intermediate direction.’’

‘‘‘Kiṃ nu, samaṇa, adhomukho bhuñjasī’ti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, adhomukho bhuñjāmī’ti vadesi. ‘Tena hi, samaṇa, ubbhamukho bhuñjasī’ti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, ubbhamukho bhuñjāmī’ti vadesi. ‘Tena hi, samaṇa, disāmukho bhuñjasī’ti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, disāmukho bhuñjāmī’ti vadesi. ‘Tena hi, samaṇa, vidisāmukho bhuñjasī’ti iti puṭṭho samāno ‘na khvāhaṃ, bhagini, vidisāmukho bhuñjāmī’ti vadesi’’.

‘‘‘Ascetic, do you eat with your face downwards?’ When asked this, you reply: ‘Sister, I do not eat with my face downwards.’ ‘Then, ascetic, do you eat with your face upwards?’ When asked this, you reply: ‘Sister, I do not eat with my face upwards.’ ‘Then, ascetic, do you eat facing a direction?’ When asked this, you reply: ‘Sister, I do not eat facing a direction.’ ‘Then, ascetic, do you eat facing an intermediate direction?’ When asked this, you reply: ‘Sister, I do not eat facing an intermediate direction.’’’

‘‘Kathañcarahi, samaṇa, bhuñjasī’’ti? ‘‘Ye hi keci, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā [samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā (sī.) nigamanavākye pana sabbatthāpi samāsoyeva dissati] vatthuvijjātiracchānavijjāya micchājīvena jīvikaṃ [jīvitaṃ (ka.)] kappenti, ime vuccanti, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā ‘adhomukhā bhuñjantī’ti. Ye hi keci, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā nakkhattavijjātiracchānavijjāya micchājīvena jīvikaṃ kappenti, ime vuccanti, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā ‘ubbhamukhā bhuñjantī’ti. Ye hi keci, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā dūteyyapahiṇagamanānuyogāya [… nuyogā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.), … nuyogena (?)] micchājīvena jīvikaṃ kappenti, ime vuccanti, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā ‘disāmukhā bhuñjantī’ti. Ye hi keci, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā aṅgavijjātiracchānavijjāya micchājīvena jīvikaṃ kappenti, ime vuccanti, bhagini, samaṇabrāhmaṇā ‘vidisāmukhā bhuñjantī’’’ti.

‘‘How then, ascetic, do you eat?’’ ‘‘Sister, those ascetics and brahmins who earn their living by wrong livelihood through the base art of vatthuvijjā, they are called, sister, ascetics and brahmins who ‘eat facing downwards.’ Those ascetics and brahmins who earn their living by wrong livelihood through the base art of nakkhattavijjā, they are called, sister, ascetics and brahmins who ‘eat facing upwards.’ Those ascetics and brahmins who earn their living by wrong livelihood through engaging in messenger work and running errands, they are called, sister, ascetics and brahmins who ‘eat facing the cardinal directions.’ Those ascetics and brahmins who earn their living by wrong livelihood through the base art of aṅgavijjā, they are called, sister, ascetics and brahmins who ‘eat facing the intermediate directions.’’’

‘‘So khvāhaṃ, bhagini, na vatthuvijjātiracchānavijjāya micchājīvena jīvikaṃ kappemi, na nakkhattavijjātiracchānavijjāya micchājīvena jīvikaṃ kappemi, na dūteyyapahiṇagamanānuyogāya micchājīvena jīvikaṃ kappemi, na aṅgavijjātiracchānavijjāya micchājīvena jīvikaṃ kappemi. Dhammena bhikkhaṃ pariyesāmi; dhammena bhikkhaṃ pariyesitvā bhuñjāmī’’ti.

‘‘But I, sister, do not earn my living by wrong livelihood through the base art of vatthuvijjā, nor through the base art of nakkhattavijjā, nor through engaging in messenger work and running errands, nor through the base art of aṅgavijjā. I seek alms righteously; having sought alms righteously, I eat.’’

Atha kho sūcimukhī paribbājikā rājagahe rathiyāya rathiyaṃ, siṅghāṭakena siṅghāṭakaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamārocesi – ‘‘dhammikaṃ samaṇā sakyaputtiyā āhāraṃ āhārenti; anavajjaṃ [anavajjena (ka.)] samaṇā sakyaputtiyā āhāraṃ āhārenti. Detha samaṇānaṃ sakyaputtiyānaṃ piṇḍa’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

Then the female bhikkhu Sūcimukhī went from street to street, from crossroads to crossroads in Rājagaha, announcing: ‘‘The ascetics, the sons of the Sakyans, eat food righteously; the ascetics, the sons of the Sakyans, eat blameless food. Give alms to the ascetics, the sons of the Sakyans!’’ The tenth.

Sāriputtasaṃyuttaṃ samattaṃ.

The Connected Discourses with Sāriputta are concluded.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Vivekajaṃ avitakkaṃ, pīti upekkhā catutthakaṃ;Ākāsañceva viññāṇaṃ, ākiñcaṃ nevasaññinā;Nirodho navamo vutto, dasamaṃ sūcimukhī cāti.

Born of seclusion, without applied thought, joy, and equanimity as the fourth; space, consciousness, nothingness, neither-perception-nor-non-perception; cessation is declared the ninth, and the tenth is Sūcimukhī.
