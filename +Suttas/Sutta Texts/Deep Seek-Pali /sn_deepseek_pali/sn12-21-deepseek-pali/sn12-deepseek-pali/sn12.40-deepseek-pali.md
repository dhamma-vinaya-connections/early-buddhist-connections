# SN 12.40 Tatiyacetanāsuttaṃ

## SN 12.40 The Third Discourse on Volition (Cetanā)

40. Sāvatthiyaṃ viharati…pe… ‘‘yañca, bhikkhave, ceteti yañca pakappeti yañca anuseti ārammaṇametaṃ hoti viññāṇassa ṭhitiyā. Ārammaṇe sati patiṭṭhā viññāṇassa hoti. Tasmiṃ patiṭṭhite viññāṇe virūḷhe nati hoti. Natiyā sati āgatigati hoti. Āgatigatiyā sati cutūpapāto hoti. Cutūpapāte sati āyatiṃ jātijarāmaraṇaṃ sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsā sambhavanti. Evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa samudayo hoti’’.

40.At Savatthi. (bhikkhus, the Blessed One said this): “Monks, whatever one intends, whatever one plans, and whatever one has an underlying tendency for, this becomes an object for the establishment of consciousness. When there is an object, there is a footing for consciousness. When consciousness is established and has grown, there is inclination. With inclination as condition, there is coming and going. With coming and going as condition, there is passing away and re-arising. With passing away and re-arising as condition, future birth, aging, and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair come to be. Thus is the origin of this entire mass of suffering.”

‘‘No ce, bhikkhave, ceteti no ce pakappeti atha ce anuseti, ārammaṇametaṃ hoti viññāṇassa ṭhitiyā. Ārammaṇe sati patiṭṭhā viññāṇassa hoti. Tasmiṃ patiṭṭhite viññāṇe virūḷhe nati hoti. Natiyā sati āgatigati hoti. Āgatigatiyā sati cutūpapāto hoti. Cutūpapāte sati āyatiṃ jātijarāmaraṇaṃ sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsā sambhavanti. Evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa samudayo hoti.

“Monks, if one does not intend or plan, yet still has an underlying tendency, this becomes an object for the establishment of consciousness. When there is an object, there is a footing for consciousness. When consciousness is established and has grown, there is inclination. With inclination as condition, there is coming and going. With coming and going as condition, there is passing away and re-arising. With passing away and re-arising as condition, future birth, aging, and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair come to be. Thus is the origin of this entire mass of suffering.

‘‘Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, no ceva ceteti no ca pakappeti no ca anuseti, ārammaṇametaṃ na hoti viññāṇassa ṭhitiyā. Ārammaṇe asati patiṭṭhā viññāṇassa na hoti. Tadappatiṭṭhite viññāṇe avirūḷhe nati na hoti. Natiyā asati āgatigati na hoti. Āgatigatiyā asati cutūpapāto na hoti. Cutūpapāte asati āyatiṃ jāti jarāmaraṇaṃ sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsā nirujjhanti. Evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa nirodho hotī’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

“Monks, when one neither intends, nor plans, nor has an underlying tendency, this is not an object for the establishment of consciousness. When there is no object, there is no footing for consciousness. When consciousness is unestablished and has not grown, there is no inclination. When there is no inclination, there is no coming and going. When there is no coming and going, there is no passing away and re-arising. When there is no passing away and re-arising, future birth, aging, and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair cease. Thus is the cessation of this entire mass of suffering.” This is the tenth.

Kaḷārakhattiyavaggo catuttho.

The fourth chapter is the Kaḷārakhattiya Vagga.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The Summary:

Bhūtamidaṃ kaḷārañca, duve ca ñāṇavatthūni;Avijjāpaccayā ca dve, natumhā cetanā tayoti.

“This Is Actual,” Kaḷāra, and two on Bases of Knowledge; Two on Ignorance as Condition, “Not Yours,” and three on Volition.
