# SN 12.65 Nagarasuttaṃ

## SN 12.65 The City Discourse

65. Sāvatthiyaṃ viharati…pe… ‘‘pubbe me, bhikkhave, sambodhā anabhisambuddhassa bodhisattasseva sato etadahosi – ‘kicchā vatāyaṃ loko āpanno jāyati ca jīyati ca mīyati ca cavati ca upapajjati ca. Atha ca panimassa dukkhassa nissaraṇaṃ nappajānāti jarāmaraṇassa. Kudāssu nāma imassa dukkhassa nissaraṇaṃ paññāyissati jarāmaraṇassā’ti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati jarāmaraṇaṃ hoti, kiṃpaccayā jarāmaraṇa’nti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘jātiyā kho sati jarāmaraṇaṃ hoti, jātipaccayā jarāmaraṇa’’’nti.

65.At Savatthi... "Monks, before my Awakening, when I was still an unenlightened Bodhisatta, this occurred to me: ‘This world has fallen into hardship. It is born, ages, dies, passes away, and is reborn, yet it does not know the escape from this suffering, from aging and death. When will an escape from this suffering, from aging and death, be discerned?’ Then, monks, this occurred to me: ‘With what existing, does aging and death occur? What is the condition for aging and death?’ Then, monks, through careful attention, this understanding arose in me through wisdom: ‘With birth existing, aging and death occur. Birth is the condition for aging and death.’"

‘‘Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati jāti hoti…pe… bhavo hoti… upādānaṃ hoti… taṇhā hoti… vedanā hoti… phasso hoti… saḷāyatanaṃ hoti… nāmarūpaṃ hoti… kiṃpaccayā nāmarūpa’nti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘viññāṇe kho sati nāmarūpaṃ hoti, viññāṇapaccayā nāmarūpa’nti. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati viññāṇaṃ hoti, kiṃpaccayā viññāṇa’nti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘nāmarūpe kho sati viññāṇaṃ hoti, nāmarūpapaccayā viññāṇa’’’nti.

"Then, monks, it occurred to me: ‘With what existing does birth come to be… becoming come to be… clinging come to be… craving come to be… feeling come to be… contact come to be… the six sense bases come to be… name-and-form come to be… what is the condition for name-and-form?’ Then, monks, through careful attention, there came to me a breakthrough by wisdom: ‘With consciousness existing, name-and-form comes to be; name-and-form has consciousness as its condition.’ Then, monks, it occurred to me: ‘With what existing does consciousness come to be, what is the condition for consciousness?’ Then, monks, through careful attention, there came to me a breakthrough by wisdom: ‘With name-and-form existing, consciousness comes to be; consciousness has name-and-form as its condition.’"

‘‘Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – paccudāvattati kho idaṃ viññāṇaṃ nāmarūpamhā na paraṃ gacchati. Ettāvatā jāyetha vā jīyetha vā mīyetha vā cavetha vā upapajjetha vā, yadidaṃ nāmarūpapaccayā viññāṇaṃ; viññāṇapaccayā nāmarūpaṃ; nāmarūpapaccayā saḷāyatanaṃ; saḷāyatanapaccayā phasso…pe… evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa samudayo hoti. ‘Samudayo, samudayo’ti kho me, bhikkhave, pubbe ananussutesu dhammesu cakkhuṃ udapādi ñāṇaṃ udapādi paññā udapādi vijjā udapādi āloko udapādi.

"Then, monks, it occurred to me: ‘This consciousness turns back from name-and-form; it does not go beyond name-and-form. To this extent one may be born, age, die, pass away, or be reborn—that is, with name-and-form as condition, consciousness; with consciousness as condition, name-and-form; with name-and-form as condition, the six sense bases; with the six sense bases as condition, contact…’ and so on. Thus is the origin of this entire mass of suffering. ‘Origin, origin’—thus, monks, in regard to things unheard before, there arose in me vision, knowledge, wisdom, true knowledge, and light."

‘‘Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati, jarāmaraṇaṃ na hoti; kissa nirodhā jarāmaraṇanirodho’ti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘jātiyā kho asati, jarāmaraṇaṃ na hoti; jātinirodhā jarāmaraṇanirodho’ti. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati jāti na hoti…pe… bhavo na hoti… upādānaṃ na hoti… taṇhā na hoti… vedanā na hoti… phasso na hoti… saḷāyatanaṃ na hoti… nāmarūpaṃ na hoti. Kissa nirodhā nāmarūpanirodho’ti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘viññāṇe kho asati, nāmarūpaṃ na hoti; viññāṇanirodhā nāmarūpanirodho’’’ti.

"Then, monks, it occurred to me: ‘When what does not exist, does aging and death not occur? With the cessation of what, comes the cessation of aging and death?’ Then, monks, through careful attention, there arose in me the realization by wisdom: ‘When birth does not exist, aging and death do not occur; with the cessation of birth comes the cessation of aging and death.’ Then, monks, it occurred to me: ‘When what does not exist, does birth not occur… existence not occur… clinging not occur… craving not occur… feeling not occur… contact not occur… the six sense bases not occur… name-and-form not occur? With the cessation of what, comes the cessation of name-and-form?’ Then, monks, through careful attention, there arose in me the realization by wisdom: ‘When consciousness does not exist, name-and-form does not occur; with the cessation of consciousness comes the cessation of name-and-form.’"

‘‘Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati viññāṇaṃ na hoti; kissa nirodhā viññāṇanirodho’ti? Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘nāmarūpe kho asati, viññāṇaṃ na hoti; nāmarūpanirodhā viññāṇanirodho’’’ti.

"Then, monks, this occurred to me: ‘When what does not exist, does consciousness not come to be? With the cessation of what, comes the cessation of consciousness?’ Then, monks, through careful attention, there arose in me the realization by wisdom: ‘When name-and-form does not exist, consciousness does not come to be; with the cessation of name-and-form, comes the cessation of consciousness.’"

‘‘Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etadahosi – adhigato kho myāyaṃ maggo bodhāya yadidaṃ – nāmarūpanirodhā viññāṇanirodho; viññāṇanirodhā nāmarūpanirodho; nāmarūpanirodhā saḷāyatananirodho; saḷāyatananirodhā phassanirodho…pe… evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa nirodho hoti. ‘Nirodho, nirodho’ti kho me, bhikkhave, pubbe ananussutesu dhammesu cakkhuṃ udapādi ñāṇaṃ udapādi paññā udapādi vijjā udapādi āloko udapādi.

"Then, monks, this thought occurred to me: ‘This path to awakening has been attained by me, namely—with the cessation of name-and-form comes the cessation of consciousness; with the cessation of consciousness comes the cessation of name-and-form; with the cessation of name-and-form comes the cessation of the six sense bases; with the cessation of the six sense bases comes the cessation of contact...and so on...thus comes the cessation of this entire mass of suffering.’ ‘Cessation, cessation’—thus, monks, in regard to things unheard before, there arose in me vision, knowledge, wisdom, true knowledge, and light."

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, puriso araññe pavane caramāno passeyya purāṇaṃ maggaṃ purāṇañjasaṃ pubbakehi manussehi anuyātaṃ. So tamanugaccheyya. Tamanugacchanto passeyya purāṇaṃ nagaraṃ purāṇaṃ rājadhāniṃ pubbakehi manussehi ajjhāvuṭṭhaṃ [ajjhāvutthaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] ārāmasampannaṃ vanasampannaṃ pokkharaṇīsampannaṃ uddhāpavantaṃ [uddāpavantaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] ramaṇīyaṃ. Atha kho so, bhikkhave, puriso rañño vā rājamahāmattassa vā āroceyya – ‘yagghe, bhante, jāneyyāsi – ahaṃ addasaṃ araññe pavane caramāno purāṇaṃ maggaṃ purāṇañjasaṃ pubbakehi manussehi anuyātaṃ tamanugacchiṃ. Tamanugacchanto addasaṃ purāṇaṃ nagaraṃ purāṇaṃ rājadhāniṃ pubbakehi manussehi ajjhāvuṭṭhaṃ ārāmasampannaṃ vanasampannaṃ pokkharaṇīsampannaṃ uddhāpavantaṃ ramaṇīyaṃ. Taṃ, bhante, nagaraṃ māpehī’ti. Atha kho so, bhikkhave, rājā vā rājamahāmatto vā taṃ nagaraṃ māpeyya. Tadassa nagaraṃ aparena samayena iddhañceva phītañca bāhujaññaṃ ākiṇṇamanussaṃ vuddhivepullappattaṃ. Evameva khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, addasaṃ purāṇaṃ maggaṃ purāṇañjasaṃ pubbakehi sammāsambuddhehi anuyātaṃ.

"Suppose, monks, a man walking through a forest wilderness would see an ancient path, an ancient road, traveled by people of the past. He would follow it. Following it, he would see an ancient city, an ancient capital, inhabited by people of the past, abundant with parks, groves, and ponds, and with ramparts—a delightful place. Then, monks, that man would inform the king or a royal minister: 'Please, sir, you should know—while walking through the forest wilderness, I saw an ancient path, an ancient road, traveled by people of the past. I followed it. Following it, I saw an ancient city, an ancient capital, inhabited by people of the past, abundant with parks, groves, and ponds, and with ramparts—a delightful place. Sir, you should restore that city!' Then, monks, the king or the royal minister would restore that city. And in time, that city would become prosperous, flourishing, populous, crowded with people, thriving and abundant. In the same way, monks, I have seen an ancient path, an ancient road, traveled by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones of the past."

‘‘Katamo ca so, bhikkhave, purāṇamaggo purāṇañjaso pubbakehi sammāsambuddhehi anuyāto? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ kho so, bhikkhave, purāṇamaggo purāṇañjaso pubbakehi sammāsambuddhehi anuyāto, tamanugacchiṃ; tamanugacchanto jarāmaraṇaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ; jarāmaraṇasamudayaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ; jarāmaraṇanirodhaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ; jarāmaraṇanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ. Tamanugacchiṃ; tamanugacchanto jātiṃ abbhaññāsiṃ…pe… bhavaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ… upādānaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ… taṇhaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ… vedanaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ… phassaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ… saḷāyatanaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ… nāmarūpaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ… viññāṇaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ. Tamanugacchiṃ; tamanugacchanto saṅkhāre abbhaññāsiṃ; saṅkhārasamudayaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ; saṅkhāranirodhaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ; saṅkhāranirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ abbhaññāsiṃ. Tadabhiññā ācikkhiṃ bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ upāsakānaṃ upāsikānaṃ. Tayidaṃ, bhikkhave, brahmacariyaṃ iddhañceva phītañca vitthārikaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsita’’nti. Pañcamaṃ.

"And what, monks, is that ancient path, the ancient road, traveled by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones of the past? It is just this ![[Noble Path]]. This, monks, is that ancient path, the ancient road, traveled by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones of the past, which I have followed. Following it, I directly knew aging and death; I directly knew the origin of aging and death; I directly knew the cessation of aging and death; and I directly knew the path leading to the cessation of aging and death. Following it, I directly knew birth… becoming… clinging… craving… feeling… contact… the six sense bases… name and form… consciousness… I directly knew volitional formations; I directly knew the origin of volitional formations; I directly knew the cessation of volitional formations; and I directly knew the path leading to the cessation of volitional formations. Having directly known this, I have declared it to the monks, nuns, male lay followers, and female lay followers. This holy life, monks, has become successful, flourishing, extended, well-known, widespread, and well-proclaimed among gods and humans." The fifth.
