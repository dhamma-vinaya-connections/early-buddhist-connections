# SN 12.28 Bhikkhusuttaṃ

## SN 12.28 The Bhikkhu Sutta

28. Sāvatthiyaṃ viharati…pe… ‘‘tatra kho…pe… idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu jarāmaraṇaṃ pajānāti, jarāmaraṇasamudayaṃ pajānāti, jarāmaraṇanirodhaṃ pajānāti, jarāmaraṇanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ pajānāti, jātiṃ pajānāti…pe… bhavaṃ pajānāti… upādānaṃ pajānāti… taṇhaṃ pajānāti… vedanaṃ pajānāti… phassaṃ pajānāti… saḷāyatanaṃ pajānāti… nāmarūpaṃ pajānāti… viññāṇaṃ pajānāti… saṅkhāre pajānāti, saṅkhārasamudayaṃ pajānāti, saṅkhāranirodhaṃ pajānāti, saṅkhāranirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ pajānāti.

28.At Savatthi. [...] There, bhikkhus, the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus thus: 'Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu understands aging-and-death, its origin, its cessation, and the way leading to its cessation; he understands birth [...]; becoming [...]; clinging [...]; craving [...]; feeling [...]; contact [...]; the six sense bases [...]; name-and-form [...]; consciousness [...]; and volitional formations, their origin, their cessation, and the way leading to their cessation.'

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, jarāmaraṇaṃ? Yā tesaṃ tesaṃ sattānaṃ tamhi tamhi sattanikāye jarā jīraṇatā khaṇḍiccaṃ pāliccaṃ valittacatā āyuno saṃhāni indriyānaṃ paripāko – ayaṃ vuccati jarā. Yā tesaṃ tesaṃ sattānaṃ tamhā tamhā sattanikāyā cuti cavanatā bhedo antaradhānaṃ maccu maraṇaṃ kālakiriyā khandhānaṃ bhedo kaḷevarassa nikkhepo; idaṃ vuccati maraṇaṃ. Iti ayaṃ ca jarā idañca maraṇaṃ. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, jarāmaraṇaṃ. Jātisamudayā jarāmaraṇasamudayo; jātinirodhā jarāmaraṇanirodho. Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo jarāmaraṇanirodhagāminī paṭipadā. Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi.

'And what, bhikkhus, is aging-and-death? The aging of those beings in the various orders of beings, their growing old, broken teeth, gray hair, wrinkled skin, decline of life-force, weakening of the faculties—this is called aging. The passing away of those beings from the various orders of beings, their deceasing, departure, dissolution, dying, death, the completion of their time, the breakup of the aggregates, the laying down of the body—this is called death. Thus, this aging and this death are together called aging-and-death. With the arising of birth comes the arising of aging-and-death; with the cessation of birth comes the cessation of aging-and-death. This ![[Noble Path]].'

‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, jāti…pe… katamo ca, bhikkhave, bhavo… katamañca, bhikkhave, upādānaṃ… vedanā… phasso… saḷāyatanaṃ… nāmarūpaṃ… viññāṇaṃ….

'And what, bhikkhus, is birth? [...] And what, bhikkhus, is becoming? [...] And what, bhikkhus, is clinging? [...] And what, bhikkhus, is feeling? [...] And what, bhikkhus, is contact? [...] And what, bhikkhus, are the six sense bases? [...] And what, bhikkhus, is name-and-form? [...] And what, bhikkhus, is consciousness?'

‘‘Katame ca, bhikkhave, saṅkhārā? Tayome, bhikkhave, saṅkhārā – kāyasaṅkhāro, vacīsaṅkhāro, cittasaṅkhāro. Ime vuccanti, bhikkhave, saṅkhārā. Avijjāsamudayā saṅkhārasamudayo; avijjānirodhā saṅkhāranirodho. Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo saṅkhāranirodhagāminī paṭipadā. Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi.

'And what, bhikkhus, are volitional formations? There are these three volitional formations, bhikkhus: bodily formation, verbal formation, and mental formation. These are called, bhikkhus, volitional formations. With the arising of ignorance, there is the arising of volitional formations; with the cessation of ignorance, there is the cessation of volitional formations. This ![[Noble Path]].'

‘‘Yato kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu evaṃ jarāmaraṇaṃ pajānāti, evaṃ jarāmaraṇasamudayaṃ pajānāti, evaṃ jarāmaraṇanirodhaṃ pajānāti, evaṃ jarāmaraṇanirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ pajānāti, evaṃ jātiṃ pajānāti…pe… bhavaṃ… upādānaṃ… taṇhaṃ… vedanaṃ… phassaṃ… saḷāyatanaṃ… nāmarūpaṃ… viññāṇaṃ … saṅkhāre… saṅkhārasamudayaṃ… saṅkhāranirodhaṃ… evaṃ saṅkhāranirodhagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ pajānāti. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu diṭṭhisampanno itipi, dassanasampanno itipi, āgato imaṃ saddhammaṃ itipi, passati imaṃ saddhammaṃ itipi, sekkhena ñāṇena samannāgato itipi, sekkhāya vijjāya samannāgato itipi, dhammasotaṃ samāpanno itipi, ariyo nibbedhikapañño itipi, amatadvāraṃ āhacca tiṭṭhati itipī’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

'When, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu thus understands aging-and-death, its origin, its cessation, and the way leading to its cessation; thus understands birth [...]; becoming [...]; clinging [...]; craving [...]; feeling [...]; contact [...]; the six sense bases [...]; name-and-form [...]; consciousness [...]; and volitional formations, their origin, their cessation, and the way leading to their cessation, this is called, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu accomplished in view, accomplished in vision, who has arrived at this true Dhamma, who sees this true Dhamma, endowed with the knowledge of one in training, endowed with the wisdom of one in training, who has entered the stream of the Dhamma, a noble one with wisdom that penetrates, who has arrived at the door of the Deathless.'
