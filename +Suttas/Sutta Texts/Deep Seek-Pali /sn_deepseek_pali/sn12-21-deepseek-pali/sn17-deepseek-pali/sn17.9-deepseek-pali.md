165.At Savatthi... "Monks, gain, honor, and renown are harsh... for attaining (the goal). Above, monks, in the sky, winds called Verambha blow. Any bird that goes there is buffeted by the Verambha winds. When buffeted by the Verambha winds, its feet go one way, its wings another, its head another, and its body yet another. Just so, monks, a certain monk here, overcome and with his mind overwhelmed by gain, honor, and renown, dresses in the morning, takes his bowl and robe, and enters a village or town for alms with body unguarded, speech unguarded, mind unguarded, mindfulness unestablished, and senses unrestrained. There he sees a woman poorly dressed or scantily clad. Upon seeing the woman poorly dressed or scantily clad, lust overwhelms his mind. With his mind overwhelmed by lust, he renounces the training and returns to a lower life.
