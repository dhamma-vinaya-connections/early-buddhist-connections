# SN 17.10 Sagāthakasuttaṃ

## SN 17.10 The Sagāthaka Discourse

166. Sāvatthiyaṃ viharati…pe… ‘‘dāruṇo, bhikkhave, lābhasakkārasiloko…pe… adhigamāya. Idhāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ekaccaṃ puggalaṃ passāmi sakkārena abhibhūtaṃ pariyādiṇṇacittaṃ, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannaṃ. Idha panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ekaccaṃ puggalaṃ passāmi asakkārena abhibhūtaṃ pariyādiṇṇacittaṃ, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannaṃ. Idha panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ekaccaṃ puggalaṃ passāmi sakkārena ca asakkārena ca tadubhayena abhibhūtaṃ pariyādiṇṇacittaṃ, kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannaṃ. Evaṃ dāruṇo kho, bhikkhave, lābhasakkārasiloko…pe… evañhi vo, bhikkhave, sikkhitabba’’nti.

166.At Savatthi … “Monks, gain, honor, and renown are harsh … for attaining the goal. Here, monks, I see a certain person whose mind is overcome and overwhelmed by gain, honor, and renown, and after death, upon the breakup of the body, is reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. Here, monks, I see a certain person whose mind is overcome and overwhelmed by lack of gain and honor, and after death, upon the breakup of the body, is reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. Here, monks, I see a certain person whose mind is overcome and overwhelmed by both gain and honor and lack of gain and honor, and after death, upon the breakup of the body, is reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. So harsh indeed, monks, are gain, honor, and renown … Thus, monks, should you train yourselves.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Idaṃ vatvāna sugato athāparaṃ etadavoca satthā –

Thus spoke the Blessed One. Having said this, the Fortunate One, the Teacher, further said:

‘‘Yassa sakkariyamānassa, asakkārena cūbhayaṃ;Samādhi na vikampati, appamāṇavihārino [appamādavihārino (pī. ka.) appamāṇoti hettha phalasamādhi, na sati].

"When honored or dishonored, in either case; their samādhi is unshaken, living in immeasurable [states]."

‘‘Taṃ jhāyinaṃ sātatikaṃ, sukhumaṃ diṭṭhivipassakaṃ;Upādānakkhayārāmaṃ, āhu sappuriso itī’’ti. dasamaṃ;

"They call him a true person, one who meditates constantly, sees with subtle insight, and delights in the destruction of clinging." Tenth.

Paṭhamo vaggo.

First Section.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Dāruṇo baḷisaṃ kummaṃ, dīghalomi ca mīḷhakaṃ;Asani diddhaṃ siṅgālaṃ, verambhena sagāthakanti.

Harsh, the Hook, the Tortoise, the Long-haired One, and the Miser; Lightning, the Pierced, the Jackal, Verambha, and the With Verses.

2. Dutiyavaggo

2.The Second Section.
