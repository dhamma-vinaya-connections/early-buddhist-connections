# SN 41.1 Saṃyojanasuttaṃ

## SN 41.1 The Saṃyojana Sutta

343. Ekaṃ samayaṃ sambahulā therā bhikkhū macchikāsaṇḍe viharanti ambāṭakavane. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulānaṃ therānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkantānaṃ maṇḍalamāḷe sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ ayamantarākathā udapādi – ‘‘‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā nānatthā nānābyañjanā udāhu ekatthā byañjanameva nāna’’nti? Tatrekaccehi therehi bhikkhūhi evaṃ byākataṃ hoti – ‘‘‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā nānatthā ceva nānābyañjanā cā’’ti. Ekaccehi therehi bhikkhūhi evaṃ byākataṃ hoti – ‘‘‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā ekatthā byañjanameva nāna’’nti.

343.At one time several senior bhikkhus were dwelling at Macchikāsaṇḍa in the Ambāṭaka forest. Then, after the meal, on returning from almsround, many senior bhikkhus gathered and sat together in the pavilion, and this discussion arose among them: “Friends, are ‘fetter’ and ‘things that fetter’ different in meaning and expression, or are they the same in meaning with only the expression different?” Thereupon, some senior bhikkhus answered thus: “Friends, ‘fetter’ and ‘things that fetter’ are different in meaning and expression.” But other senior bhikkhus answered thus: “Friends, ‘fetter’ and ‘things that fetter’ are the same in meaning with only the expression different.”

Tena kho pana samayena citto gahapati migapathakaṃ anuppatto hoti kenacideva karaṇīyena. Assosi kho citto gahapati sambahulānaṃ kira therānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkantānaṃ maṇḍalamāḷe sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ ayamantarākathā udapādi – ‘‘‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā nānatthā nānābyañjanā udāhu ekatthā byañjanameva nāna’’nti? Tatrekaccehi therehi bhikkhūhi evaṃ byākataṃ – ‘‘‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā nānatthā ceva nānābyañjanā cā’’ti. Ekaccehi therehi bhikkhūhi evaṃ byākataṃ ‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā ekatthā byañjanameva nānanti. Atha kho citto gahapati yena therā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā there bhikkhū abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho citto gahapati there bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante, sambahulānaṃ kira therānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkantānaṃ maṇḍalamāḷe sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ ayamantarākathā udapādi – ‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā nānatthā nānābyañjanā udāhu ekatthā byañjanameva nāna’’nti? Ekaccehi therehi bhikkhūhi evaṃ byākataṃ – ‘‘‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā nānatthā ceva nānābyañjanā cā’’ti. Ekaccehi therehi bhikkhūhi evaṃ byākataṃ ‘‘‘saṃyojana’nti vā, āvuso, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā ekatthā byañjanameva nāna’’nti. ‘‘Evaṃ, gahapatī’’ti.

At that time, the householder Citta had arrived at Migapathaka for some business. The householder Citta heard that several senior bhikkhus, after their meal and having returned from the almsround, had gathered and sat together in the pavilion, and this discussion arose among them: “Are these terms ‘fetter,’ friends, and ‘things that fetter’ different in meaning and phrasing, or are they the same in meaning with only the phrasing different?” There, some senior bhikkhus explained it thus: “These terms ‘fetter,’ friends, and ‘things that fetter’ are different in meaning and phrasing.” Other senior bhikkhus explained it thus: “These terms ‘fetter,’ friends, and ‘things that fetter’ are the same in meaning with only the phrasing different.” Then the householder Citta approached the senior bhikkhus, paid homage to them, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the householder Citta said to the senior bhikkhus: “I have heard, venerable sirs, that several senior bhikkhus, after their meal and having returned from the almsround, had gathered and sat together in the pavilion, and this discussion arose among them: ‘Are these terms “fetter,” friends, and “things that fetter” different in meaning and phrasing, or are they the same in meaning with only the phrasing different?’ Some senior bhikkhus explained it thus: ‘These terms “fetter,” friends, and “things that fetter” are different in meaning and phrasing.’ Other senior bhikkhus explained it thus: ‘These terms “fetter,” friends, and “things that fetter” are the same in meaning with only the phrasing different.’ ” “Yes, householder.”

‘‘‘Saṃyojana’nti vā, bhante, ‘saṃyojaniyā dhammā’ti vā ime dhammā nānatthā ceva nānābyañjanā ca. Tena hi, bhante, upamaṃ vo karissāmi. Upamāyapidhekacce viññū purisā bhāsitassa atthaṃ ājānanti. Seyyathāpi, bhante, kāḷo ca balībaddo odāto ca balībaddo ekena dāmena vā yottena vā saṃyuttā assu. Yo nu kho evaṃ vadeyya – ‘kāḷo balībaddo odātassa balībaddassa saṃyojanaṃ, odāto balībaddo kāḷassa balībaddassa saṃyojana’nti, sammā nu kho so vadamāno vadeyyā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, gahapati! Na kho, gahapati, kāḷo balībaddo odātassa balībaddassa saṃyojanaṃ, napi odāto balībaddo kāḷassa baḷībaddassa saṃyojanaṃ; yena kho te ekena dāmena vā yottena vā saṃyuttā taṃ tattha saṃyojana’’nti. ‘‘Evameva kho, bhante, na cakkhu rūpānaṃ saṃyojanaṃ, na rūpā cakkhussa saṃyojanaṃ; yañca tattha tadubhayaṃ paṭicca uppajjati chandarāgo taṃ tattha saṃyojanaṃ. Na sotaṃ saddānaṃ… na ghānaṃ gandhānaṃ… na jivhā rasānaṃ… na kāyo phoṭṭhabbānaṃ saṃyojanaṃ, na phoṭṭhabbā kāyassa saṃyojanaṃ; yañca tattha tadubhayaṃ paṭicca uppajjati chandarāgo taṃ tattha saṃyojanaṃ. Na mano dhammānaṃ saṃyojanaṃ, na dhammā manassa saṃyojanaṃ; yañca tattha tadubhayaṃ paṭicca uppajjati chandarāgo taṃ tattha saṃyojana’’nti. ‘‘Lābhā te, gahapati, suladdhaṃ te, gahapati, yassa te gambhīre buddhavacane paññācakkhu kamatī’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

“‘Fetter,’ venerable sirs, and ‘things that fetter’—these terms are different in meaning and different in expression. Well then, venerable sirs, I shall give you a simile. For by means of a simile, some wise people understand the meaning of what is said. Suppose, venerable sirs, a black bull and a white bull were yoked together by a single rope or yoke. Would someone who says, ‘The black bull is the fetter of the white bull, the white bull is the fetter of the black bull,’ be speaking rightly?” “No, householder. The black bull is not the fetter of the white bull, nor is the white bull the fetter of the black bull. The fetter there is the single rope or yoke by which they are yoked together.” “Just so, venerable sirs, the eye is not the fetter of forms, nor are forms the fetter of the eye. Whatever desire and lust arise there dependent on both—that is the fetter there. The ear is not the fetter of sounds… The nose is not the fetter of odors… The tongue is not the fetter of tastes… The body is not the fetter of tactile objects, nor are tactile objects the fetter of the body. Whatever desire and lust arise there dependent on both—that is the fetter there. The mind is not the fetter of mental phenomena, nor are mental phenomena the fetter of the mind. Whatever desire and lust arise there dependent on both—that is the fetter there.” “You are fortunate, householder, truly fortunate, that you have the eye of wisdom that penetrates the deep words of the Buddha.” First.
