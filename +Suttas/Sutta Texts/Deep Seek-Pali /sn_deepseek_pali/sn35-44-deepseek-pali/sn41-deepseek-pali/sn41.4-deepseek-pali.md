# SN 41.4 Mahakapāṭihāriyasuttaṃ

## SN 41.4 The Discourse on Mahaka's Miracle

346. Ekaṃ samayaṃ sambahulā therā bhikkhū macchikāsaṇḍe viharanti ambāṭakavane. Atha kho citto gahapati yena therā bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā there bhikkhū abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho citto gahapati there bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘adhivāsentu me, bhante therā, svātanāya gokule bhatta’’nti. Adhivāsesuṃ kho therā bhikkhū tuṇhībhāvena. Atha kho citto gahapati therānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ adhivāsanaṃ viditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā there bhikkhū abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Atha kho therā bhikkhū tassā rattiyā accayena pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena cittassa gahapatino gokulaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdiṃsu.

346.On one occasion, several elder bhikkhus were dwelling at Macchikāsaṇḍa in the Ambāṭaka Grove. Then the householder Citta approached the elder bhikkhus, paid homage to them, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the householder Citta said to the elder bhikkhus: "Venerable sirs, may the elders consent to a meal from me tomorrow at the cow pen." The elder bhikkhus consented by their silence. Then the householder Citta, understanding their consent, rose from his seat, paid homage to the elder bhikkhus, circumambulated them, and departed. Then, when that night had passed, in the morning, the elder bhikkhus dressed, took their bowls and robes, and went to the cow pen of the householder Citta; having gone there, they sat down on the seats prepared for them.

Atha kho citto gahapati there bhikkhū paṇītena sappipāyāsena sahatthā santappesi sampavāresi. Atha kho therā bhikkhū bhuttāvino onītapattapāṇino uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamiṃsu. Cittopi kho gahapati ‘sesakaṃ vissajjethā’ti vatvā there bhikkhū piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandhi. Tena kho pana samayena uṇhaṃ hoti kuthitaṃ [kuṭṭhitaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)]; te ca therā bhikkhū paveliyamānena maññe kāyena gacchanti, yathā taṃ bhojanaṃ bhuttāvino.

Then the householder Citta personally served and satisfied the elder bhikkhus with choice ghee-rice. When the elder bhikkhus had finished eating and had withdrawn their hands from their bowls, they rose from their seats and departed. Citta the householder, saying, "Please distribute the remainder," followed closely behind the elder bhikkhus. Now at that time it was hot and sultry; and those elder bhikkhus walked as if their bodies were melting, as happens after a meal.

Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā mahako tasmiṃ bhikkhusaṅghe sabbanavako hoti. Atha kho āyasmā mahako āyasmantaṃ theraṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sādhu khvassa, bhante thera, sītako ca vāto vāyeyya, abbhasampilāpo [abbhasaṃbilāpo (sī.), abbhasaṃvilāpo (pī.)] ca assa, devo ca ekamekaṃ phusāyeyyā’’ti.

At that time, moreover, Venerable Mahaka was the newest in that monastic community. Then Venerable Mahaka said this to a certain elder: "It would be good, venerable sir, if a cool wind were to blow, there were some cloud-gathering, and the rain would fall drop by drop."

‘‘Sādhu khvassa, āvuso mahaka, yaṃ sītako ca vāto vāyeyya, abbhasampilāpo ca assa, devo ca ekamekaṃ phusāyeyyā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā mahako tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhari [abhisaṅkhāsi (sī.)] yathā sītako ca vāto vāyi, abbhasampilāpo ca assa [āsi (?)], devo ca ekamekaṃ phusi. Atha kho cittassa gahapatino etadahosi – ‘‘yo kho imasmiṃ bhikkhusaṅghe sabbanavako bhikkhu tassāyaṃ evarūpo iddhānubhāvo’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā mahako ārāmaṃ sampāpuṇitvā āyasmantaṃ theraṃ etadavoca – ‘‘alamettāvatā, bhante therā’’ti? ‘‘Alamettāvatā, āvuso mahaka! Katamettāvatā, āvuso mahaka! Pūjitamettāvatā, āvuso mahakā’’ti. Atha kho therā bhikkhū yathāvihāraṃ agamaṃsu. Āyasmāpi mahako sakaṃ vihāraṃ agamāsi.

"Good indeed, friend Mahaka, if a cool wind were to blow, and there were some cloud-gathering, and the rain would fall drop by drop." Then the Venerable Mahaka performed such a feat of psychic power that a cool wind blew, there was cloud-gathering, and the rain fell drop by drop. Then the householder Citta thought: "Even the newest monk in this Saṅgha of bhikkhus has such supernormal power!" Then, the Venerable Mahaka, reaching the monastery, said this to the elder venerable: "Is this enough, venerable elder?" "Enough, friend Mahaka! It is enough, friend Mahaka! This much is honored, friend Mahaka!" Then the elder bhikkhus went to their respective residences. The Venerable Mahaka also went to his own residence.

Atha kho citto gahapati yenāyasmā mahako tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ mahakaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho citto gahapati āyasmantaṃ mahakaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sādhu me, bhante, ayyo mahako uttarimanussadhammaṃ iddhipāṭihāriyaṃ dassetū’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi tvaṃ, gahapati, ālinde uttarāsaṅgaṃ paññapetvā tiṇakalāpaṃ okāsehī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho citto gahapati āyasmato mahakassa paṭissutvā ālinde uttarāsaṅgaṃ paññapetvā tiṇakalāpaṃ okāsesi. Atha kho āyasmā mahako vihāraṃ pavisitvā sūcighaṭikaṃ datvā tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhari yathā tālacchiggaḷena ca aggaḷantarikāya ca acci nikkhamitvā tiṇāni jhāpesi, uttarāsaṅgaṃ na jhāpesi. Atha kho citto gahapati uttarāsaṅgaṃ papphoṭetvā saṃviggo lomahaṭṭhajāto ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Atha kho āyasmā mahako vihārā nikkhamitvā cittaṃ gahapatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘alamettāvatā, gahapatī’’ti?

Then the householder Citta approached Venerable Mahaka. Having approached, he paid homage to Venerable Mahaka and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the householder Citta said to Venerable Mahaka: "Venerable sir, it would be good if the Venerable Mahaka would show me a superhuman quality, a miracle of psychic power." "Then, householder, spread out your upper robe on the veranda and scatter a bundle of grass." "Yes, Venerable sir," replied the householder Citta to Venerable Mahaka. He spread out his upper robe on the veranda and scattered a bundle of grass. Then Venerable Mahaka entered his dwelling, secured the bolt, and performed such a feat of psychic power that a flame issued forth through the keyhole and through the space around the bolt, and it burned the grass, but it did not burn the upper robe. Then the householder Citta, flapping his upper robe, stood to one side, agitated, his hair standing on end. Then Venerable Mahaka came out of his dwelling and said to the householder Citta: "Is this enough, householder?"

‘‘Alamettāvatā, bhante mahaka! Katamettāvatā, bhante, mahaka! Pūjitamettāvatā, bhante mahaka! Abhiramatu, bhante, ayyo mahako macchikāsaṇḍe. Ramaṇīyaṃ ambāṭakavanaṃ. Ahaṃ ayyassa mahakassa ussukkaṃ karissāmi cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānappaccayabhesajjaparikkhārāna’’nti. ‘‘Kalyāṇaṃ vuccati, gahapatī’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā mahako senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaramādāya macchikāsaṇḍamhā pakkāmi. Yaṃ macchikāsaṇḍamhā pakkāmi, tathā pakkantova ahosi; na puna paccāgacchīti. Catutthaṃ.

"Enough, Venerable Mahaka! It is enough, Venerable Mahaka! This much is honored, Venerable Mahaka! May the Venerable Mahaka delight in Macchikāsaṇḍa. Ambāṭaka Grove is delightful. I will attend to the Venerable Mahaka’s needs—robes, alms-food, lodging, and medicinal requisites for the sick." "Well spoken, householder," said the Venerable Mahaka. Then, having arranged his lodging and taken his bowl and robe, the Venerable Mahaka departed from Macchikāsaṇḍa. When he departed from Macchikāsaṇḍa, he departed thus; he never returned again. The fourth.
