# SN 35.104 Upādāniyadhammasuttaṃ

## SN 35.104 The Discourse on Things that Lead to Clinging

123. ‘‘Upādāniye ca, bhikkhave, dhamme desessāmi upādānañca. Taṃ suṇātha. Katame ca, bhikkhave, upādāniyā dhammā, katamañca upādānaṃ? Santi, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Ime vuccanti, bhikkhave, upādāniyā dhammā. Yo tattha chandarāgo, taṃ tattha upādānaṃ…pe… santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā…pe… santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Ime vuccanti, bhikkhave, upādāniyā dhammā. Yo tattha chandarāgo taṃ tattha upādāna’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

123."bhikkhus, I will teach you things that lead to clinging and clinging itself. Listen to that. And what, bhikkhus, are things that lead to clinging, and what is clinging? There are, bhikkhus, forms cognizable by the eye—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual desire, and enticing. These are called, bhikkhus, things that lead to clinging. Whatever desire and lust there is for them, that is the clinging there. [...] There are, bhikkhus, tastes cognizable by the tongue. [...] There are, bhikkhus, mental phenomena cognizable by the mind—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual desire, and enticing. These are called, bhikkhus, things that lead to clinging. Whatever desire and lust there is for them, that is the clinging there." The tenth.

Lokakāmaguṇavaggo dvādasamo.

The Chapter on Worldly Sensual Pleasures is the Twelfth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Mārapāsena dve vuttā, lokakāmaguṇena ca;Sakko pañcasikho ceva, sāriputto ca rāhulo;Saṃyojanaṃ upādānaṃ, vaggo tena pavuccatīti.

Two are spoken of concerning Māra’s snare, and concerning worldly sensual pleasures; Sakka, Pañcasikha, Sāriputta, and Rāhula; Fetter and clinging—thus is this chapter declared.
