# SN 35.22 Dutiyadukkhuppādasuttaṃ

## SN 35.22 Second Origin of Suffering Discourse

22. ‘‘Yo, bhikkhave, rūpānaṃ uppādo ṭhiti abhinibbatti pātubhāvo, dukkhasseso uppādo, rogānaṃ ṭhiti, jarāmaraṇassa pātubhāvo. Yo saddānaṃ…pe… yo gandhānaṃ… yo rasānaṃ… yo phoṭṭhabbānaṃ… yo dhammānaṃ uppādo ṭhiti abhinibbatti pātubhāvo, dukkhasseso uppādo, rogānaṃ ṭhiti, jarāmaraṇassa pātubhāvo.

22. bhikkhus, the arising, persistence, production, and manifestation of forms is the arising of suffering, the persistence of diseases, and the manifestation of aging and death. The arising, persistence, production, and manifestation of sounds... The arising, persistence, production, and manifestation of smells... The arising, persistence, production, and manifestation of tastes... The arising, persistence, production, and manifestation of tactile sensations... The arising, persistence, production, and manifestation of mental phenomena is the arising of suffering, the persistence of diseases, and the manifestation of aging and death.

‘‘Yo ca kho, bhikkhave, rūpānaṃ nirodho vūpasamo atthaṅgamo, dukkhasseso nirodho, rogānaṃ vūpasamo, jarāmaraṇassa atthaṅgamo. Yo saddānaṃ…pe… yo gandhānaṃ… yo rasānaṃ… yo phoṭṭhabbānaṃ… yo dhammānaṃ nirodho vūpasamo atthaṅgamo, dukkhasseso nirodho, rogānaṃ vūpasamo, jarāmaraṇassa atthaṅgamo’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

"bhikkhus, the cessation, subsiding, and disappearance of forms is the cessation of suffering, the subsiding of diseases, and the disappearance of aging and death. The cessation, subsiding, and disappearance of sounds... The cessation, subsiding, and disappearance of smells... The cessation, subsiding, and disappearance of tastes... The cessation, subsiding, and disappearance of tactile sensations... The cessation, subsiding, and disappearance of mental phenomena is the cessation of suffering, the subsiding of diseases, and the disappearance of aging and death." The tenth.

Yamakavaggo dutiyo.

The Pair Chapter is the second.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The Summary:

Sambodhena duve vuttā, assādena apare duve;No cetena duve vuttā, abhinandena apare duve;Uppādena duve vuttā, vaggo tena pavuccatīti.

Two are spoken of by enlightenment, two more by savoring;
Two are spoken of as unintentional, two more by rejoicing;
Two are spoken of by arising, thus the chapter is declared.

3. Sabbavaggo

3. The All Chapter
