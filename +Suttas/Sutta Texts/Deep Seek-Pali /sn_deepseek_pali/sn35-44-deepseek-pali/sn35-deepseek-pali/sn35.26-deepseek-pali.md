# SN 35.26 Paṭhamaaparijānanasuttaṃ

## SN 35.26 The First Discourse on Lack of Complete Comprehension

26. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Kiñca, bhikkhave, anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya? Cakkhuṃ, bhikkhave, anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Rūpe anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Cakkhuviññāṇaṃ anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Cakkhusamphassaṃ anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya…pe… jivhaṃ anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Rase…pe… jivhāviññāṇaṃ…pe… jivhāsamphassaṃ…pe… yampidaṃ jivhāsamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Kāyaṃ…pe… manaṃ anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Dhamme…pe… manoviññāṇaṃ…pe… manosamphassaṃ…pe… yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, sabbaṃ anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya.

26. "bhikkhus, not fully knowing all, not fully comprehending all, not developing dispassion for all, and not abandoning all, one is incapable of ending suffering. And what, bhikkhus, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, is one incapable of ending suffering? The eye, bhikkhus, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. Forms, bhikkhus, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. Eye-consciousness, bhikkhus, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. Eye-contact, bhikkhus, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. Whatever feeling, bhikkhus, arises dependent on eye-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering…pe… The tongue, bhikkhus, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. Tastes...pe... Tongue-consciousness...pe... Tongue-contact...pe... Whatever feeling, bhikkhus, arises dependent on tongue-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. The body...pe... The mind, bhikkhus, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. Mental objects...pe... Mind-consciousness...pe... Mind-contact...pe... Whatever feeling, bhikkhus, arises dependent on mind-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering. This, bhikkhus, is all that, not fully knowing, not fully comprehending, not developing dispassion for, and not abandoning, one is incapable of ending suffering."

‘‘Sabbañca kho, bhikkhave, abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Kiñca, bhikkhave, sabbaṃ abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya? Cakkhuṃ, bhikkhave, abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Rūpe abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Cakkhuviññāṇaṃ abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Cakkhusamphassaṃ abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya…pe… jivhaṃ abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Rase…pe… jivhāviññāṇaṃ…pe… jivhāsamphassaṃ…pe… yampidaṃ jivhāsamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Kāyaṃ…pe… manaṃ abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Dhamme…pe… manoviññāṇaṃ…pe… manosamphassaṃ…pe… yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, sabbaṃ abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāyā’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

"bhikkhus, fully knowing all, fully comprehending all, developing dispassion for all, and abandoning all, one is capable of ending suffering. And what, bhikkhus, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, is one capable of ending suffering? The eye, bhikkhus, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. Forms, bhikkhus, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. Eye-consciousness, bhikkhus, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. Eye-contact, bhikkhus, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. Whatever feeling, bhikkhus, arises dependent on eye-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering…pe… The tongue, bhikkhus, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. Tastes...pe... Tongue-consciousness...pe... Tongue-contact...pe... Whatever feeling, bhikkhus, arises dependent on tongue-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. The body...pe... The mind, bhikkhus, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. Mental objects...pe... Mind-consciousness...pe... Mind-contact...pe... Whatever feeling, bhikkhus, arises dependent on mind-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering. This, bhikkhus, is all that, fully knowing, fully comprehending, developing dispassion for, and abandoning, one is capable of ending suffering." The fourth.
