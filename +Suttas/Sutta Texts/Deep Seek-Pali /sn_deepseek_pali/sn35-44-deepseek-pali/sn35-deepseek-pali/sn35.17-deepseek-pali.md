# SN 35.17 Paṭhamanoceassādasuttaṃ

## SN 35.17 The First Discourse on ‘If There Were No Gratification’

17. ‘‘No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, cakkhussa assādo abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā cakkhusmiṃ sārajjeyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi cakkhussa assādo tasmā sattā cakkhusmiṃ sārajjanti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, cakkhussa ādīnavo abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā cakkhusmiṃ nibbindeyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi cakkhussa ādīnavo tasmā sattā cakkhusmiṃ nibbindanti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, cakkhussa nissaraṇaṃ abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā cakkhusmā nissareyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi cakkhussa nissaraṇaṃ tasmā sattā cakkhusmā nissaranti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, sotassa assādo abhavissa… no cedaṃ, bhikkhave, ghānassa assādo abhavissa… no cedaṃ, bhikkhave, jivhāya assādo abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā jivhāya sārajjeyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi jivhāya assādo, tasmā sattā jivhāya sārajjanti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, jivhāya ādīnavo abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā jivhāya nibbindeyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi jivhāya ādīnavo, tasmā sattā jivhāya nibbindanti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, jivhāya nissaraṇaṃ abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā jivhāya nissareyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi jivhāya nissaraṇaṃ, tasmā sattā jivhāya nissaranti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, kāyassa assādo abhavissa… no cedaṃ, bhikkhave, manassa assādo abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā manasmiṃ sārajjeyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi manassa assādo, tasmā sattā manasmiṃ sārajjanti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, manassa ādīnavo abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā manasmiṃ nibbindeyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi manassa ādīnavo, tasmā sattā manasmiṃ nibbindanti. No cedaṃ, bhikkhave, manassa nissaraṇaṃ abhavissa, nayidaṃ sattā manasmā nissareyyuṃ. Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, atthi manassa nissaraṇaṃ, tasmā sattā manasmā nissaranti.

17. "bhikkhus, if there were no gratification in the eye, beings would not be enamored with the eye. But because there is gratification in the eye, beings are enamored with the eye. If there were no danger in the eye, beings would not grow disenchanted with the eye. But because there is danger in the eye, beings grow disenchanted with the eye. If there were no escape from the eye, beings would not escape from the eye. But because there is an escape from the eye, beings escape from the eye. If there were no gratification in the ear... If there were no gratification in the nose... If there were no gratification in the tongue, beings would not be enamored with the tongue. But because there is gratification in the tongue, beings are enamored with the tongue. If there were no danger in the tongue, beings would not grow disenchanted with the tongue. But because there is danger in the tongue, beings grow disenchanted with the tongue. If there were no escape from the tongue, beings would not escape from the tongue. But because there is an escape from the tongue, beings escape from the tongue. If there were no gratification in the body... If there were no gratification in the mind, beings would not be enamored with the mind. But because there is gratification in the mind, beings are enamored with the mind. If there were no danger in the mind, beings would not grow disenchanted with the mind. But because there is danger in the mind, beings grow disenchanted with the mind. If there were no escape from the mind, beings would not escape from the mind. But because there is an escape from the mind, beings escape from the mind."

‘‘Yāvakīvañca, bhikkhave, sattā imesaṃ channaṃ ajjhattikānaṃ āyatanānaṃ assādañca assādato, ādīnavañca ādīnavato, nissaraṇañca nissaraṇato yathābhūtaṃ nābbhaññaṃsu, neva tāva, bhikkhave, sattā sadevakā lokā samārakā sabrahmakā sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya nissaṭā visaññuttā vippamuttā vimariyādīkatena [vipariyādikatena (sī. pī.), vipariyādikatena (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] cetasā vihariṃsu. Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, sattā imesaṃ channaṃ ajjhattikānaṃ āyatanānaṃ assādañca assādato, ādīnavañca ādīnavato, nissaraṇañca nissaraṇato yathābhūtaṃ abbhaññaṃsu, atha, bhikkhave, sattā sadevakā lokā samārakā sabrahmakā sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya nissaṭā visaññuttā vippamuttā vimariyādīkatena cetasā viharantī’’ti. Pañcamaṃ.

"bhikkhus, as long as beings did not understand as they really are the gratification, the danger, and the escape in regard to these six internal sense bases, so long did they not dwell released, disjoined, and liberated with a mind free from limitations from this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, from this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans. But when, bhikkhus, beings understood as they really are the gratification, the danger, and the escape in regard to these six internal sense bases, then they dwell released, disjoined, and liberated with a mind free from limitations from this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, from this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans." The fifth.
