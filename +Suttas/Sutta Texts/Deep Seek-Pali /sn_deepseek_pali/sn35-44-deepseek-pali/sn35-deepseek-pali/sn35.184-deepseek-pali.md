# SN 35.184 Yavakalāpisuttaṃ

## SN 35.184 The Barley Bundle Discourse

248. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, yavakalāpī cātumahāpathe nikkhittā assa. Atha cha purisā āgaccheyyuṃ byābhaṅgihatthā. Te yavakalāpiṃ chahi byābhaṅgīhi haneyyuṃ. Evañhi sā, bhikkhave, yavakalāpī suhatā assa chahi byābhaṅgīhi haññamānā. Atha sattamo puriso āgaccheyya byābhaṅgihattho. So taṃ yavakalāpiṃ sattamāya byābhaṅgiyā haneyya. Evañhi sā bhikkhave, yavakalāpī suhatatarā assa, sattamāya byābhaṅgiyā haññamānā. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, assutavā puthujjano cakkhusmiṃ haññati manāpāmanāpehi rūpehi…pe… jivhāya haññati manāpāmanāpehi rasehi…pe… manasmiṃ haññati manāpāmanāpehi dhammehi. Sace so, bhikkhave, assutavā puthujjano āyatiṃ punabbhavāya ceteti, evañhi so, bhikkhave, moghapuriso suhatataro hoti, seyyathāpi sā yavakalāpī sattamāya byābhaṅgiyā haññamānā.

248."bhikkhus, suppose a sheaf of barley were placed at a crossroads. Then six men with flails in their hands were to come along and strike that sheaf of barley with six flails. In this way, bhikkhus, that sheaf of barley would be well struck, being struck by six flails. Then a seventh man with a flail in his hand were to come along and strike that sheaf of barley with a seventh flail. In this way, bhikkhus, that sheaf of barley would be even more thoroughly struck, being struck by the seventh flail. Just so, bhikkhus, an uninstructed ordinary person is struck in the eye by pleasant and unpleasant forms… struck in the tongue by pleasant and unpleasant tastes… struck in the mind by pleasant and unpleasant mental phenomena. If, bhikkhus, that uninstructed ordinary person directs his thought towards future rebirth, then indeed, bhikkhus, that misguided person is even more thoroughly struck—just like that sheaf of barley being struck by the seventh flail.

‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, devāsurasaṅgāmo samupabyūḷho [samupabbūḷho (sī. pī.)] ahosi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vepacitti asurindo asure āmantesi – ‘sace, mārisā, devāsurasaṅgāme samupabyūḷhe asurā jineyyuṃ devā parājineyyuṃ, yena naṃ sakkaṃ devānamindaṃ kaṇṭhapañcamehi bandhanehi bandhitvā mama santike āneyyātha asurapura’nti. Sakkopi kho, bhikkhave, devānamindo deve tāvatiṃse āmantesi – ‘sace, mārisā, devāsurasaṅgāme samupabyūḷhe devā jineyyuṃ asurā parājineyyuṃ, yena naṃ vepacittiṃ asurindaṃ kaṇṭhapañcamehi bandhanehi bandhitvā mama santike āneyyātha sudhammaṃ devasabha’nti. Tasmiṃ kho pana, bhikkhave, saṅgāme devā jiniṃsu, asurā parājiniṃsu. Atha kho, bhikkhave, devā tāvatiṃsā vepacittiṃ asurindaṃ kaṇṭhapañcamehi bandhanehi bandhitvā sakkassa devānamindassa santike ānesuṃ sudhammaṃ devasabhaṃ. Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vepacitti asurindo kaṇṭhapañcamehi bandhanehi baddho [bandho (sī. syā. kaṃ. ka.)] hoti. Yadā kho, bhikkhave, vepacittissa asurindassa evaṃ hoti – ‘dhammikā kho devā, adhammikā asurā, idheva dānāhaṃ devapuraṃ gacchāmī’ti. Atha kaṇṭhapañcamehi bandhanehi muttaṃ attānaṃ samanupassati, dibbehi ca pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappito samaṅgībhūto paricāreti. Yadā ca kho, bhikkhave, vepacittissa asurindassa evaṃ hoti – ‘dhammikā kho asurā, adhammikā devā, tattheva dānāhaṃ asurapuraṃ gamissāmī’ti, atha kaṇṭhapañcamehi bandhanehi baddhaṃ attānaṃ samanupassati. Dibbehi ca pañcahi kāmaguṇehi parihāyati. Evaṃ sukhumaṃ kho, bhikkhave, vepacittibandhanaṃ. Tato sukhumataraṃ mārabandhanaṃ. Maññamāno kho, bhikkhave, baddho mārassa, amaññamāno mutto pāpimato.

"Once upon a time, bhikkhus, a battle was arrayed between the devas and the asuras. Then, bhikkhus, Vepacitti, the lord of the asuras, addressed the asuras: 'If, sirs, in the battle arrayed between the devas and the asuras, the asuras should win and the devas be defeated, bind Sakka, the lord of the devas, with fivefold bonds around his neck and bring him to me in the city of the asuras.' Sakka too, bhikkhus, the lord of the devas, addressed the Tāvatiṃsa devas: 'If, sirs, in the battle arrayed between the devas and the asuras, the devas should win and the asuras be defeated, bind Vepacitti, the lord of the asuras, with fivefold bonds around his neck and bring him to me in the Sudhamma assembly hall of the devas.' In that battle, bhikkhus, the devas won and the asuras were defeated. Then, bhikkhus, the Tāvatiṃsa devas bound Vepacitti, the lord of the asuras, with fivefold bonds around his neck and brought him to Sakka, the lord of the devas, in the Sudhamma assembly hall. There, bhikkhus, Vepacitti, the lord of the asuras, was bound with fivefold bonds. Whenever, bhikkhus, Vepacitti, the lord of the asuras, thought: 'The devas are righteous, the asuras are unrighteous; now I shall go to the realm of the devas,' then he saw himself freed from the fivefold bonds and indulged, fully equipped and provided, in the five divine strands of sensual pleasure. But whenever, bhikkhus, Vepacitti, the lord of the asuras, thought: 'The asuras are righteous, the devas are unrighteous; now I shall return to the city of the asuras,' then he saw himself bound by the fivefold bonds and deprived of the five divine strands of sensual pleasure. So subtle, bhikkhus, is the bondage of Vepacitti. Even subtler is the bondage of Māra. One who conceives is bound by Māra; one who does not conceive is freed from the Evil One."

‘‘‘Asmī’ti, bhikkhave, maññitametaṃ, ‘ayamahamasmī’ti maññitametaṃ, ‘bhavissa’nti maññitametaṃ, ‘na bhavissa’nti maññitametaṃ, ‘rūpī bhavissa’nti maññitametaṃ, ‘arūpī bhavissa’nti maññitametaṃ, ‘saññī bhavissa’nti maññitametaṃ, ‘asaññī bhavissa’nti maññitametaṃ, ‘nevasaññīnāsaññī bhavissa’nti maññitametaṃ. Maññitaṃ, bhikkhave, rogo, maññitaṃ gaṇḍo, maññitaṃ sallaṃ. Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, ‘amaññamānena [amaññitamānena (pī. ka.)] cetasā viharissāmā’ti – evañhi vo, bhikkhave, sikkhitabbaṃ.

"'I am,' bhikkhus, this is a conceiving. 'This I am,' this is a conceiving. 'I will be,' this is a conceiving. 'I will not be,' this is a conceiving. 'I will be with form,' this is a conceiving. 'I will be formless,' this is a conceiving. 'I will be percipient,' this is a conceiving. 'I will be non-percipient,' this is a conceiving. 'I will be neither percipient nor non-percipient,' this is a conceiving. Conceiving, bhikkhus, is a disease, conceiving is a tumor, conceiving is a dart. Therefore, bhikkhus, 'We will dwell with a mind free from conceiving'—thus, bhikkhus, should you train yourselves."

‘‘‘Asmī’ti, bhikkhave, iñjitametaṃ, ‘ayamahamasmī’ti iñjitametaṃ, ‘bhavissa’nti iñjitametaṃ, ‘na bhavissa’nti iñjitametaṃ, ‘rūpī bhavissa’nti iñjitametaṃ, ‘arūpī bhavissa’nti iñjitametaṃ, ‘saññī bhavissa’nti iñjitametaṃ, ‘asaññī bhavissa’nti iñjitametaṃ, ‘nevasaññīnāsaññī bhavissa’nti iñjitametaṃ. Iñjitaṃ, bhikkhave, rogo, iñjitaṃ gaṇḍo, iñjitaṃ sallaṃ. Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, ‘aniñjamānena [aniñjiyamānena (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] cetasā viharissāmā’ti – evañhi vo, bhikkhave, sikkhitabbaṃ.

"'I am,' bhikkhus, that is agitation. 'This I am,' that is agitation. 'I will be,' that is agitation. 'I will not be,' that is agitation. 'I will be with form,' that is agitation. 'I will be formless,' that is agitation. 'I will be percipient,' that is agitation. 'I will be non-percipient,' that is agitation. 'I will be neither percipient nor non-percipient,' that is agitation. Agitation, bhikkhus, is a disease, agitation is a tumor, agitation is a dart. Therefore, bhikkhus, you should train yourselves: 'We will dwell with a mind that does not agitate.'

‘‘‘Asmī’ti, bhikkhave, phanditametaṃ, ‘ayamahamasmī’ti phanditametaṃ, ‘bhavissa’nti…pe… ‘na bhavissa’nti… ‘rūpī bhavissa’nti… ‘arūpī bhavissa’nti… ‘saññī bhavissa’nti… ‘asaññī bhavissa’nti… ‘nevasaññīnāsaññī bhavissa’nti phanditametaṃ. Phanditaṃ, bhikkhave, rogo, phanditaṃ gaṇḍo, phanditaṃ sallaṃ. Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, ‘aphandamānena [aphandiyamānena (syā. kaṃ. ka.)] cetasā viharissāmā’ti – evañhi vo, bhikkhave, sikkhitabbaṃ.

"'I am' is quivering, bhikkhus. 'This I am' is quivering. 'I will be'... 'I will not be'... 'I will be with form'... 'I will be formless'... 'I will be percipient'... 'I will be non-percipient'... 'I will be neither percipient nor non-percipient' is quivering. Quivering, bhikkhus, is a disease, quivering is a tumor, quivering is a dart. Therefore, bhikkhus, you should train yourselves thus: 'We will dwell with a mind that does not quiver.'

‘‘‘Asmī’ti, bhikkhave, papañcitametaṃ, ‘ayamahamasmī’ti papañcitametaṃ, ‘bhavissa’nti…pe… ‘na bhavissa’nti… ‘rūpī bhavissa’nti… ‘arūpī bhavissa’nti… ‘saññī bhavissa’nti… ‘asaññī bhavissa’nti… ‘nevasaññīnāsaññī bhavissa’nti papañcitametaṃ. Papañcitaṃ, bhikkhave, rogo, papañcitaṃ gaṇḍo, papañcitaṃ sallaṃ. Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, ‘nippapañcena cetasā viharissāmā’ti – evañhi vo, bhikkhave, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“‘I am’ is proliferation, bhikkhus. ‘This I am’ is proliferation. ‘I will be’… ‘I will not be’… ‘I will be with form’… ‘I will be formless’… ‘I will be percipient’… ‘I will be non-percipient’… ‘I will be neither percipient nor non-percipient’—this is proliferation. Proliferation, bhikkhus, is a disease; proliferation is a tumor; proliferation is a dart. Therefore, bhikkhus, you should train yourselves thus: ‘We will dwell with a mind free from proliferation.’

‘‘‘Asmī’ti, bhikkhave, mānagatametaṃ, ‘ayamahamasmī’ti mānagatametaṃ, ‘bhavissa’nti mānagatametaṃ, ‘na bhavissa’nti mānagatametaṃ, ‘rūpī bhavissa’nti mānagatametaṃ, ‘arūpī bhavissa’nti mānagatametaṃ, ‘saññī bhavissa’nti mānagatametaṃ, ‘asaññī bhavissa’nti mānagatametaṃ, ‘nevasaññīnāsaññī bhavissa’nti mānagatametaṃ. Mānagataṃ, bhikkhave, rogo, mānagataṃ gaṇḍo, mānagataṃ sallaṃ. Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, ‘nihatamānena cetasā viharissāmā’ti – evañhi vo, bhikkhave, sikkhitabba’’nti. Ekādasamaṃ.

“‘I am’ is conceit, bhikkhus. ‘This I am’ is conceit. ‘I will be’ is conceit. ‘I will not be’ is conceit. ‘I will be with form’ is conceit. ‘I will be formless’ is conceit. ‘I will be percipient’ is conceit. ‘I will be non-percipient’ is conceit. ‘I will be neither percipient nor non-percipient’ is conceit. Conceit, bhikkhus, is a disease. Conceit is a tumor. Conceit is a dart. Therefore, bhikkhus, you should train yourselves thus: ‘We will dwell with minds devoid of conceit.’" The eleventh.

Āsīvisavaggo ekūnavīsatimo.

The Āsīvisa Section, the Nineteenth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Āsīviso ratho kummo, dve dārukkhandhā avassuto;Dukkhadhammā kiṃsukā vīṇā, chappāṇā yavakalāpīti.

The Viper, the Chariot, the Tortoise; the Two Logs, the Leaky; Suffering States, the Kimśuka, the Lute; the Six Creatures, the Barley Sheaf.

Saḷāyatanavagge catutthapaṇṇāsako samatto.

The Fourth Fifty in the Section on the Six Sense Bases is completed.

Tassa vagguddānaṃ –

Summary of the Section:

Nandikkhayo saṭṭhinayo, samuddo uragena ca;Catupaṇṇāsakā ete, nipātesu pakāsitāti.

The Destruction of Craving, the Sixtyfold Method, the Ocean, and the Serpent; These fifty-four have been explained in the Nipātas.

Saḷāyatanasaṃyuttaṃ samattaṃ.

The Connected Discourses on the Six Sense Bases is completed.
