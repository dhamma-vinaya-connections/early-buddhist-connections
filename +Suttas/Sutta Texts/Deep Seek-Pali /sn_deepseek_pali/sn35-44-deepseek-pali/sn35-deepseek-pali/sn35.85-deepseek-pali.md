# SN 35.85 Yogakkhemisuttaṃ

## SN 35.85 The Sutta on the One Who Has Attained Security from Bondage

104. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Yogakkhemipariyāyaṃ vo, bhikkhave, dhammapariyāyaṃ desessāmi. Taṃ suṇātha. Katamo ca, bhikkhave, yogakkhemipariyāyo dhammapariyāyo? Santi, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Te tathāgatassa pahīnā ucchinnamūlā tālāvatthukatā anabhāvaṅkatā āyatiṃ anuppādadhammā. Tesañca pahānāya akkhāsi yogaṃ, tasmā tathāgato ‘yogakkhemī’ti vuccati…pe… santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Te tathāgatassa pahīnā ucchinnamūlā tālāvatthukatā anabhāvaṅkatā āyatiṃ anuppādadhammā. Tesañca pahānāya akkhāsi yogaṃ, tasmā tathāgato ‘yogakkhemī’ti vuccati. Ayaṃ kho, bhikkhave, yogakkhemipariyāyo dhammapariyāyo’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

104.At Sāvatthī. “bhikkhus, I shall teach you the Dhamma exposition on the attainment of security from bondage. Listen to it. And what, bhikkhus, is the Dhamma exposition on the attainment of security from bondage? There are, bhikkhus, forms cognizable by the eye—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, charming, endearing, connected with sensual desire, enticing. These have been abandoned by the Tathāgata, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated so that they are no more subject to future arising. And he has declared the way to abandon them; therefore the Tathāgata is called ‘one who has attained security from bondage.’ … There are, bhikkhus, mental phenomena cognizable by the mind—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, charming, endearing, connected with sensual desire, enticing. These have been abandoned by the Tathāgata, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated so that they are no more subject to future arising. And he has declared the way to abandon them; therefore the Tathāgata is called ‘one who has attained security from bondage.’ This, bhikkhus, is the Dhamma exposition on the attainment of security from bondage.” The first.
