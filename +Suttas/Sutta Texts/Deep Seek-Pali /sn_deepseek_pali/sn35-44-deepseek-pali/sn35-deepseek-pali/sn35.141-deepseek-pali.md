# SN 35.141 Jīvakambavanasamādhisuttaṃ

## SN 35.141 The Discourse on samādhi at Jīvaka’s Mango Grove

160. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati jīvakambavane. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti…pe… ‘‘samādhiṃ, bhikkhave, bhāvetha. Samāhitassa, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati. Kiñca yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati? Cakkhuṃ aniccanti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati, rūpā aniccāti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati, cakkhuviññāṇaṃ aniccanti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati, cakkhusamphasso aniccoti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati, yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi aniccanti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati…pe… jivhā aniccāti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati…pe… mano aniccoti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati, dhammā aniccāti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati…pe… yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi aniccanti yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyati. Samādhiṃ, bhikkhave, bhāvetha. Samāhitassa, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno yathābhūtaṃ okkhāyatī’’ti. Pañcamaṃ.

160.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha in Jīvaka’s Mango Grove. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus…” … “Develop samādhi, bhikkhus. For a bhikkhu who is collected, bhikkhus, things are discerned as they really are. And what does he discern as it really is? He discerns as it really is: ‘The eye is impermanent.’ He discerns as it really is: ‘Forms are impermanent.’ He discerns as it really is: ‘Eye-consciousness is impermanent.’ He discerns as it really is: ‘Eye-contact is impermanent.’ He discerns as it really is: ‘Whatever feeling arises with eye-contact as condition—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-unpleasant—that too is impermanent.’ … He discerns as it really is: ‘The tongue is impermanent.’ … He discerns as it really is: ‘The mind is impermanent.’ He discerns as it really is: ‘Mental phenomena are impermanent.’ … He discerns as it really is: ‘Whatever feeling arises with mind-contact as condition—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-unpleasant—that too is impermanent.’ Develop samādhi, bhikkhus. For a bhikkhu who is collected, bhikkhus, things are discerned as they really are.” The fifth.
