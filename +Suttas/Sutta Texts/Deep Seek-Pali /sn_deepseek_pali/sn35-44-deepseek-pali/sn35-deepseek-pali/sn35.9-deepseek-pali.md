9. "The eye, monks, is not-self, whether past or future; how much more so the present! Seeing thus, monks, the well-taught noble disciple does not long for the past eye; he does not delight in the future eye; he practices for the disillusionment, fading away, and cessation of the present eye. The ear is not-self... The nose is not-self... The tongue is not-self, whether past or future; how much more so the present! Seeing thus, monks, the well-taught noble disciple does not long for the past tongue; he does not delight in the future tongue; he practices for the disillusionment, fading away, and cessation of the present tongue. The body is not-self... The mind is not-self, whether past or future; how much more so the present! Seeing thus, monks, the well-taught noble disciple does not long for the past mind; he does not delight in the future mind; he practices for the disillusionment, fading away, and cessation of the present mind."
