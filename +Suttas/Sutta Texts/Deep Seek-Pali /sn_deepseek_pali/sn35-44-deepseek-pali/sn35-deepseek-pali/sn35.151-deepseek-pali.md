# SN 35.151 Ajjhattaaniccachandarāgasuttaṃ

## SN 35.151 The Discourse on Desire and Lust for the Internal Impermanent

170. ‘‘Yaṃ, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ, tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo. Kiñca, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ; tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo…pe… jivhā aniccā; tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo…pe… mano anicco; tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo. Yaṃ, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ, tatra vo chandarāgo pahātabbo’’ti.

170.“bhikkhus, whatever is impermanent, therein you should abandon desire and lust. And what, bhikkhus, is impermanent? The eye, bhikkhus, is impermanent; therein you should abandon desire and lust… The tongue is impermanent; therein you should abandon desire and lust… The mind is impermanent; therein you should abandon desire and lust. Whatever, bhikkhus, is impermanent, therein you should abandon desire and lust.”

## 4-6. Dukkhachandādisuttaṃ

### 4–6. The Discourse on Suffering, Desire, and So On

171-173. ‘‘Yaṃ, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ, tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Kiñca, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo…pe… jivhā dukkhā…pe… mano dukkho; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Yaṃ, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo’’ti.

171–173. “bhikkhus, whatever is suffering, therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned. And what, bhikkhus, is suffering? The eye, bhikkhus, is suffering; therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned… The tongue is suffering… The mind is suffering; therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned. Whatever, bhikkhus, is suffering, therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned.”

## 7-9. Anattachandādisuttaṃ

### 7–9. The Discourse on Desire for What Is Not Self, and So On

174-176. ‘‘Yo, bhikkhave, anattā, tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Ko ca, bhikkhave, anattā? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, anattā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo…pe… jivhā anattā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo…pe… mano anattā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Yo, bhikkhave, anattā tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo’’ti.

174–176. “bhikkhus, whatever is not-self, therein you should abandon desire, abandon lust, abandon desire and lust. And what, bhikkhus, is not-self? The eye, bhikkhus, is not-self; therein you should abandon desire, abandon lust, abandon desire and lust… The tongue is not-self; therein you should abandon desire, abandon lust, abandon desire and lust… The mind is not-self; therein you should abandon desire, abandon lust, abandon desire and lust. Whatever, bhikkhus, is not-self, therein you should abandon desire, abandon lust, abandon desire and lust.”

## 10-12. Bāhirāniccachandādisuttaṃ

### 10–12. The Discourse on Desire for the External Impermanent, and So On

177-179. ‘‘Yaṃ, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ, tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Kiñca, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ? Rūpā, bhikkhave, aniccā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Saddā aniccā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Gandhā aniccā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Rasā aniccā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Phoṭṭhabbā aniccā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Dhammā aniccā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Yaṃ, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo’’ti.

177–179. “bhikkhus, whatever is impermanent, therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned. And what, bhikkhus, is impermanent? Forms, bhikkhus, are impermanent; therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned. Sounds are impermanent; therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned. Odors are impermanent; therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned. Tastes are impermanent; therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned. Tactile objects are impermanent; therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned. Mental phenomena are impermanent; therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned. Whatever, bhikkhus, is impermanent, therein desire should be abandoned, lust should be abandoned, desire and lust should be abandoned.”

## 13-15. Bāhiradukkhachandādisuttaṃ

### The Discourse on Desire, etc., Regarding External Suffering

180-182. ‘‘Yaṃ, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ, tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Kiñca, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ? Rūpā, bhikkhave, dukkhā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Saddā… gandhā… rasā… phoṭṭhabbā… dhammā dukkhā; tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo. Yaṃ, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ, tatra vo chando pahātabbo, rāgo pahātabbo, chandarāgo pahātabbo’’ti.

"bhikkhus, whatever is suffering, therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned. And what, bhikkhus, is suffering? Forms, bhikkhus, are suffering; therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned. Sounds... odors... tastes... tactile objects... mental phenomena are suffering; therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned. Whatever, bhikkhus, is suffering, therein your desire should be abandoned, your lust should be abandoned, your desire and lust should be abandoned."
