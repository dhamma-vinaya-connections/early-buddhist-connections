# SN 35.96 Dutiyamārapāsasuttaṃ

## SN 35.96 The Second Discourse on Māra's Snare

115. ‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu baddho cakkhuviññeyyesu rūpesu, āvāsagato mārassa, mārassa vasaṃ gato, paṭimukkassa mārapāso. Baddho so mārabandhanena yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe….

115."bhikkhus, there are forms cognizable by the eye—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk delights in them, welcomes them, and remains holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who is bound by forms cognizable by the eye. He has entered Māra's dwelling, has come under Māra's control, one upon whom Māra's snare is fastened. He is bound by Māra's bondage, at the disposal of the Evil One…."

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā…pe… santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu baddho manoviññeyyesu dhammesu, āvāsagato mārassa, mārassa vasaṃ gato, paṭimukkassa mārapāso. Baddho so mārabandhanena yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe….

"bhikkhus, there are tastes cognizable by the tongue…bhikkhus, there are mind-cognizable phenomena—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk delights in them, welcomes them, and remains holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who is bound by mind-cognizable phenomena. He has entered Māra's dwelling, has come under Māra's control, one upon whom Māra's snare is fastened. He is bound by Māra's bondage, at the disposal of the Evil One…."

‘‘Santi ca kho, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu mutto cakkhuviññeyyehi rūpehi, nāvāsagato mārassa, na mārassa vasaṃ gato, ummukkassa mārapāso. Mutto so mārabandhanena na yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe….

"bhikkhus, there are forms cognizable by the eye—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk does not delight in them, does not welcome them, and does not remain holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who is liberated from forms cognizable by the eye. He has not entered Māra's dwelling, has not come under Māra's control, one from whom Māra's snare is cast off. He is freed from Māra's bondage, not at the disposal of the Evil One…."

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā…pe… santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu mutto manoviññeyyehi dhammehi, nāvāsagato mārassa, na mārassa vasaṃ gato, ummukkassa mārapāso. Mutto so mārabandhanena na yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

"bhikkhus, there are tastes cognizable by the tongue…bhikkhus, there are mind-cognizable phenomena—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk does not delight in them, does not welcome them, and does not remain holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who is liberated from mind-cognizable phenomena. He has not entered Māra's dwelling, has not come under Māra's control, one from whom Māra's snare is cast off. He is freed from Māra's bondage, not at the disposal of the Evil One."
