# SN 35.93 Bāhirāyatanaparijānanasuttaṃ

## SN 35.93 Discourse on Comprehending the External Sense Bases

112. ‘‘Rūpe, bhikkhave, anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Sadde… gandhe… rase… phoṭṭhabbe… dhamme anabhijānaṃ aparijānaṃ avirājayaṃ appajahaṃ abhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Rūpe ca kho, bhikkhave, abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāya. Sadde… gandhe… rase… phoṭṭhabbe… dhamme abhijānaṃ parijānaṃ virājayaṃ pajahaṃ bhabbo dukkhakkhayāyā’’ti. Navamaṃ.

112.“bhikkhus, not fully knowing forms, not fully understanding them, not developing dispassion for them, not abandoning them, one is incapable of the destruction of suffering. Sounds…smells…tastes…tactile objects…mental phenomena—not fully knowing them, not fully understanding them, not developing dispassion for them, not abandoning them, one is incapable of the destruction of suffering. But, bhikkhus, by fully knowing forms, fully understanding them, developing dispassion for them, abandoning them, one is capable of the destruction of suffering. Sounds…smells…tastes…tactile objects…mental phenomena—by fully knowing them, fully understanding them, developing dispassion for them, abandoning them, one is capable of the destruction of suffering.”
