# SN 35.174 Āsīvisopamasuttaṃ

## SN 35.174 The Discourse on the Simile of the Venomous Snake

238. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, cattāro āsīvisā uggatejā ghoravisā. Atha puriso āgaccheyya jīvitukāmo amaritukāmo sukhakāmo dukkhappaṭikūlo. Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ime te, ambho purisa, cattāro āsīvisā uggatejā ghoravisā kālena kālaṃ vuṭṭhāpetabbā, kālena kālaṃ nhāpetabbā, kālena kālaṃ bhojetabbā, kālena kālaṃ saṃvesetabbā [pavesetabbā (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)]. Yadā ca kho te, ambho purisa, imesaṃ catunnaṃ āsīvisānaṃ uggatejānaṃ ghoravisānaṃ aññataro vā aññataro vā kuppissati, tato tvaṃ, ambho purisa, maraṇaṃ vā nigacchasi, maraṇamattaṃ vā dukkhaṃ. Yaṃ te, ambho purisa, karaṇīyaṃ taṃ karohī’’’ti.

238.“Bhikkhus, suppose there were four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom. Then a man would come along, desiring life, not desiring death, desiring happiness and averse to pain. They would say to him: ‘Here, good man, are four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom. From time to time they must be roused, from time to time bathed, from time to time fed, from time to time laid down. And if, good man, any one of these four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom, becomes angry with you, then you will meet death or deadly pain. Whatever you must do, good man, do it.’”

‘‘Atha kho so, bhikkhave, puriso bhīto catunnaṃ āsīvisānaṃ uggatejānaṃ ghoravisānaṃ yena vā tena vā palāyetha. Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ime kho, ambho purisa, pañca vadhakā paccatthikā piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandhā, yattheva naṃ passissāma tattheva jīvitā voropessāmāti. Yaṃ te, ambho purisa, karaṇīyaṃ taṃ karohī’’’ti.

“Then, bhikkhus, that man, frightened by the four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom, would flee in any direction. They would say to him: ‘Here, good man, are five executioners, your enemies, following you relentlessly from behind. Wherever they see you, there they will deprive you of life. Whatever you must do, good man, do it!’”

‘‘Atha kho so, bhikkhave, puriso bhīto catunnaṃ āsīvisānaṃ uggatejānaṃ ghoravisānaṃ, bhīto pañcannaṃ vadhakānaṃ paccatthikānaṃ yena vā tena vā palāyetha. Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ayaṃ te, ambho purisa, chaṭṭho antaracaro vadhako ukkhittāsiko piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandho yattheva naṃ passissāmi tattheva siro pātessāmīti. Yaṃ te, ambho purisa, karaṇīyaṃ taṃ karohī’’’ti.

“Then, bhikkhus, that man, frightened by the four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom, and frightened by the five executioners, his enemies, would flee in any direction. They would say to him: ‘Here, good man, is a sixth lurking executioner with drawn sword, following you relentlessly from behind. Wherever he sees you, there he will cut off your head. Whatever you must do, good man, do it!’”

‘‘Atha kho so, bhikkhave, puriso bhīto catunnaṃ āsīvisānaṃ uggatejānaṃ ghoravisānaṃ, bhīto pañcannaṃ vadhakānaṃ paccatthikānaṃ, bhīto chaṭṭhassa antaracarassa vadhakassa ukkhittāsikassa yena vā tena vā palāyetha. So passeyya suññaṃ gāmaṃ. Yaññadeva gharaṃ paviseyya rittakaññeva paviseyya tucchakaññeva paviseyya suññakaññeva paviseyya. Yaññadeva bhājanaṃ parimaseyya rittakaññeva parimaseyya tucchakaññeva parimaseyya suññakaññeva parimaseyya. Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘idāni, ambho purisa, imaṃ suññaṃ gāmaṃ corā gāmaghātakā pavisanti [vadhissanti (sī. pī.)]. Yaṃ te, ambho purisa, karaṇīyaṃ taṃ karohī’’’ti.

“Then, bhikkhus, that man, frightened by the four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom, frightened by the five executioners, his enemies, and frightened by the sixth lurking executioner with drawn sword, would flee in any direction. He might see an empty village. Whatever house he entered, he would enter it empty, void, deserted. Whatever vessel he handled, he would handle it empty, void, deserted. Then they would say to him: ‘Now, good man, thieves, village-plunderers, are entering this empty village. Whatever you must do, good man, do it.’”

‘‘Atha kho so, bhikkhave, puriso bhīto catunnaṃ āsīvisānaṃ uggatejānaṃ ghoravisānaṃ, bhīto pañcannaṃ vadhakānaṃ paccatthikānaṃ, bhīto chaṭṭhassa antaracarassa vadhakassa ukkhittāsikassa, bhīto corānaṃ gāmaghātakānaṃ yena vā tena vā palāyetha. So passeyya mahantaṃ udakaṇṇavaṃ orimaṃ tīraṃ sāsaṅkaṃ sappaṭibhayaṃ, pārimaṃ tīraṃ khemaṃ appaṭibhayaṃ. Na cassa nāvā santāraṇī uttarasetu vā apārā pāraṃ gamanāya. Atha kho, bhikkhave, tassa purisassa evamassa – ‘ayaṃ kho mahāudakaṇṇavo orimaṃ tīraṃ sāsaṅkaṃ sappaṭibhayaṃ, pārimaṃ tīraṃ khemaṃ appaṭibhayaṃ, natthi ca [na cassa (sī. ka.), natthassa (syā. kaṃ.)] nāvā santāraṇī uttarasetu vā apārā pāraṃ gamanāya. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ tiṇakaṭṭhasākhāpalāsaṃ saṃkaḍḍhitvā kullaṃ bandhitvā taṃ kullaṃ nissāya hatthehi ca pādehi ca vāyamamāno sotthinā pāraṃ gaccheyya’’’nti.

“Then, bhikkhus, that man, frightened by the four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom, frightened by the five executioners, his enemies, frightened by the sixth lurking executioner with drawn sword, and frightened by the village-plundering thieves, would flee in any direction. He might see a great expanse of water, with this shore dangerous and full of fear, and the far shore secure and free from fear. But there would be no boat for crossing over, no bridge for going from this shore to the other shore. Then, bhikkhus, it might occur to that man: ‘Here is this great expanse of water, with this shore dangerous and full of fear, and the far shore secure and free from fear. But there is no boat for crossing over, no bridge for going from this shore to the other shore. Suppose I gather grass, wood, branches, and leaves to make a raft, and relying on that raft, and struggling with hands and feet, I might safely reach the far shore.’”

‘‘Atha kho so, bhikkhave, puriso tiṇakaṭṭhasākhāpalāsaṃ saṃkaḍḍhitvā kullaṃ bandhitvā taṃ kullaṃ nissāya hatthehi ca pādehi ca vāyamamāno sotthinā pāraṃ gaccheyya, tiṇṇo pāraṅgato [pāragato (sī. syā. kaṃ.)] thale tiṭṭhati brāhmaṇo.

“Then, bhikkhus, that man would gather grass, wood, branches, and leaves, make a raft, and relying on that raft, and struggling with hands and feet, would safely reach the far shore. Having crossed over and gone beyond, that brahmin stands on dry ground.”

‘‘Upamā kho myāyaṃ, bhikkhave, katā atthassa viññāpanāya. Ayañcettha [ayaṃ cevettha (sī.)] attho – cattāro āsīvisā uggatejā ghoravisāti kho, bhikkhave, catunnetaṃ mahābhūtānaṃ adhivacanaṃ – pathavīdhātuyā, āpodhātuyā, tejodhātuyā, vāyodhātuyā.

“Bhikkhus, this simile was made by me to convey the meaning. And this is the meaning here: The four venomous snakes, fierce and of terrible venom—this, bhikkhus, is a designation for the four great elements: the earth element, the water element, the fire element, and the air element.”

‘‘Pañca vadhakā paccatthikāti kho, bhikkhave, pañcannetaṃ upādānakkhandhānaṃ adhivacanaṃ, seyyathidaṃ – rūpupādānakkhandhassa, vedanupādānakkhandhassa, saññupādānakkhandhassa, saṅkhārupādānakkhandhassa, viññāṇupādānakkhandhassa.

“The five executioners, your enemies—this, bhikkhus, is a designation for the five aggregates subject to clinging: the form aggregate subject to clinging, the feeling aggregate subject to clinging, the perception aggregate subject to clinging, the mental formations aggregate subject to clinging, and the consciousness aggregate subject to clinging.”

‘‘Chaṭṭho antaracaro vadhako ukkhittāsikoti kho, bhikkhave, nandīrāgassetaṃ adhivacanaṃ.

“The sixth lurking executioner with drawn sword—this, bhikkhus, is a designation for delight and lust.”

‘‘Suñño gāmoti kho, bhikkhave, channetaṃ ajjhattikānaṃ āyatanānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Cakkhuto cepi naṃ, bhikkhave, paṇḍito byatto medhāvī upaparikkhati rittakaññeva khāyati, tucchakaññeva khāyati, suññakaññeva khāyati…pe… jivhāto cepi naṃ, bhikkhave…pe… manato cepi naṃ, bhikkhave, paṇḍito byatto medhāvī upaparikkhati rittakaññeva khāyati, tucchakaññeva khāyati, suññakaññeva khāyati.

“The empty village—this, bhikkhus, is a designation for the six internal sense bases. If, bhikkhus, a wise, competent, and intelligent person examines it with respect to the eye, it appears empty, void, and desolate…If, bhikkhus…with respect to the tongue…If, bhikkhus, a wise, competent, and intelligent person examines it with respect to the mind, it appears empty, void, and desolate.”

‘‘Corā gāmaghātakāti kho, bhikkhave, channetaṃ bāhirānaṃ āyatanānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Cakkhu, bhikkhave, haññati manāpāmanāpesu rūpesu; sotaṃ, bhikkhave…pe… ghānaṃ, bhikkhave…pe… jivhā, bhikkhave, haññati manāpāmanāpesu rasesu; kāyo, bhikkhave…pe… mano, bhikkhave, haññati manāpāmanāpesu dhammesu.

“The thieves, village-plunderers—this, bhikkhus, is a designation for the six external sense bases. The eye, bhikkhus, is afflicted by agreeable and disagreeable forms; the ear, bhikkhus…the nose, bhikkhus…the tongue, bhikkhus, is afflicted by agreeable and disagreeable tastes; the body, bhikkhus…the mind, bhikkhus, is afflicted by agreeable and disagreeable mental phenomena.”

‘‘Mahā udakaṇṇavoti kho, bhikkhave, catunnetaṃ oghānaṃ adhivacanaṃ – kāmoghassa, bhavoghassa, diṭṭhoghassa, avijjoghassa.

“The great expanse of water—this, bhikkhus, is a designation for the four floods: the flood of sensual desire, the flood of becoming, the flood of views, and the flood of ignorance.”

‘‘Orimaṃ tīraṃ sāsaṅkaṃ sappaṭibhayanti kho, bhikkhave, sakkāyassetaṃ adhivacanaṃ.

“This shore, dangerous and full of fear—this, bhikkhus, is a designation for identity.”

‘‘Pārimaṃ tīraṃ khemaṃ appaṭibhayanti kho, bhikkhave, nibbānassetaṃ adhivacanaṃ.

Bhikkhus, ‘the further shore, safe and free from danger’ is a designation for Nibbāna.

‘‘Kullanti kho, bhikkhave, ariyassetaṃ aṭṭhaṅgikassa maggassa adhivacanaṃ, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi.

bhikkhus, ‘a raft’ is a designation for this ![[Noble Path]].

‘‘Tassa hatthehi ca pādehi ca vāyāmoti kho, bhikkhave, vīriyārambhassetaṃ adhivacanaṃ.

Bhikkhus, ‘exertion with hands and feet’ is a designation for the arousal of energy.

‘‘Tiṇṇo pāraṅgato thale tiṭṭhati brāhmaṇoti kho, bhikkhave, arahato etaṃ adhivacana’’nti. Paṭhamaṃ.

Bhikkhus, ‘crossed over, gone beyond, the brahmin stands on high ground’ is a designation for the Arahant.” This is the first discourse.
