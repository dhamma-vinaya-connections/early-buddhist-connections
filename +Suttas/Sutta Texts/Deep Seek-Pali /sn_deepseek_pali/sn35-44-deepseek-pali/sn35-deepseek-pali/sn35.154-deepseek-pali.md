# SN 35.154 Ajjhattapaccuppannāniccasuttaṃ

## SN 35.154 The Discourse on Internal Present Impermanence

188. ‘‘Cakkhu, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ paccuppannaṃ…pe… jivhā aniccā paccuppannā…pe… mano anicco paccuppanno. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti.

188."The eye, bhikkhus, is impermanent, presently arisen... The tongue is impermanent, presently arisen... The mind is impermanent, presently arisen. Seeing thus... he understands: ‘there is no more for this state of being.’"

## 22-24. Ajjhattātītādidukkhasuttaṃ

### The Discourse on Internal Past, etc., Suffering

189-191. ‘‘Cakkhu, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ atītaṃ anāgataṃ paccuppannaṃ…pe… jivhā dukkhā atītā anāgatā paccuppannā…pe… mano dukkho atīto anāgato paccuppanno. Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti.

"The eye, bhikkhus, is suffering—past, future, and present... The tongue is suffering—past, future, and present... The mind is suffering—past, future, and present. Seeing thus, bhikkhus... he understands: ‘There is no more for this state of being.’"

## 25-27. Ajjhattātītādianattasuttaṃ

### The Discourse on Internal Past, etc., Not-Self

192-194. ‘‘Cakkhu, bhikkhave, anattā atītaṃ anāgataṃ paccuppannaṃ…pe… jivhā anattā…pe… mano anattā atīto anāgato paccuppanno. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti.

"The eye, bhikkhus, is not-self, whether past, future, or present... The tongue is not-self... The mind is not-self, whether past, future, or present. Seeing thus... he understands: ‘There is no more for this state of being.’"

## 28-30. Bāhirātītādianiccasuttaṃ

### 28–30. The Suttas on the Impermanence of the External, Past, Future, and Present

195-197. ‘‘Rūpā, bhikkhave, aniccā atītā anāgatā paccuppannā. Saddā… gandhā… rasā… phoṭṭhabbā… dhammā aniccā atītā anāgatā paccuppannā. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti.

"Forms, bhikkhus, are impermanent, whether past, future, or present. Sounds… odors… flavors… tactile objects… mental phenomena are impermanent, whether past, future, or present. Seeing thus… he understands: ‘There is no more coming to any state of being.’"

## 31-33. Bāhirātītādidukkhasuttaṃ

### 31–33. The Suttas on the Suffering of the External, Past, Future, and Present

198-200. ‘‘Rūpā, bhikkhave, dukkhā atītā anāgatā paccuppannā. Saddā… gandhā… rasā… phoṭṭhabbā… dhammā dukkhā atītā anāgatā paccuppannā. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti.

"Forms, bhikkhus, are suffering, whether past, future, or present. Sounds… odors… flavors… tactile objects… mental phenomena are suffering, whether past, future, or present. Seeing thus… he understands: ‘There is no more coming to any state of being.’"

## 34-36. Bāhirātītādianattasuttaṃ

### 34–36. The Suttas on the Non-self Nature of the External, Past, Future, and Present

201-203. ‘‘Rūpā, bhikkhave, anattā atītā anāgatā paccuppannā. Saddā… gandhā… rasā… phoṭṭhabbā… dhammā anattā atītā anāgatā paccuppannā. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti.

201–203. "Forms, bhikkhus, are not-self, whether past, future, or present. Sounds… odors… flavors… tactile objects… mental phenomena are not-self, whether past, future, or present. Seeing thus… he understands: ‘There is no more coming to any state of being.’"
