# SN 35.52 Paṭhamachaphassāyatanasuttaṃ

## SN 35.52 The First Discourse on the Six Sense Bases

71. ‘‘Yo hi koci, bhikkhave, bhikkhu channaṃ phassāyatanānaṃ samudayañca atthaṅgamañca assādañca ādīnavañca nissaraṇañca yathābhūtaṃ nappajānāti. Avusitaṃ tena brahmacariyaṃ, ārakā so imasmā dhammavinayā’’ti.

71.bhikkhus, any monk who does not fully understand as it actually is the origin, the cessation, the gratification, the danger, and the escape in the case of the six sense bases, has not lived the holy life; he is far from this Dhamma and Discipline.

Evaṃ vutte, aññataro bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘etthāhaṃ, bhante, anassasaṃ [anassasiṃ (sī.), anassāsaṃ (syā. kaṃ.), anassāsiṃ (pī.)]. Ahañhi, bhante, channaṃ phassāyatanānaṃ samudayañca atthaṅgamañca assādañca ādīnavañca nissaraṇañca yathābhūtaṃ nappajānāmī’’ti.

When this was said, a certain monk said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, I am lost in this matter. For I, venerable sir, do not fully understand as it actually is the origin, the cessation, the gratification, the danger, and the escape in the case of the six sense bases."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, bhikkhu, cakkhuṃ ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’ti samanupassasī’’ti?

What do you think, monk? Do you regard the eye as: 'This is mine, this I am, this is my self'?

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

No, venerable sir.

‘‘Sādhu, bhikkhu, ettha ca te, bhikkhu, cakkhu ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya sudiṭṭhaṃ bhavissati. Esevanto dukkhassa…pe… jivhaṃ ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’ti samanupassasī’’ti?

Good, monk. And here, monk, the eye will be well seen with right wisdom as it actually is: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.' This is the end of suffering… Do you regard the tongue as: 'This is mine, this I am, this is my self'?

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

No, venerable sir.

‘‘Sādhu, bhikkhu, ettha ca te, bhikkhu, jivhā ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya sudiṭṭhaṃ bhavissati. Esevanto dukkhassa…pe… manaṃ ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’ti samanupassasī’’ti?

Good, monk. And here, monk, the tongue will be well seen with right wisdom as it actually is: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.' This is the end of suffering… Do you regard the mind as: 'This is mine, this I am, this is my self'?

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

No, venerable sir.

‘‘Sādhu, bhikkhu, ettha ca te, bhikkhu, mano ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya sudiṭṭhaṃ bhavissati. Esevanto dukkhassā’’ti. Navamaṃ.

Good, monk. And here, monk, the mind will be well seen with right wisdom as it actually is: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.' This is the end of suffering.
