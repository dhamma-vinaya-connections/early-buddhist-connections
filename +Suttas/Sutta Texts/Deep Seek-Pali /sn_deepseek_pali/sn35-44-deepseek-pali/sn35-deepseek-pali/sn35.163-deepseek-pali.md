# SN 35.163 Bāhirāyatanaanattasuttaṃ

## SN 35.163 The Discourse on the Non-self of the External Sense Bases

227. ‘‘Rūpā, bhikkhave, anattā. Saddā… gandhā… rasā… phoṭṭhabbā… dhammā anattā. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti.

227.“bhikkhus, forms are non-self. Sounds…smells…tastes…tactile sensations…mental phenomena are non-self. Seeing thus…he understands: ‘There is no more for this state of being.’”

Saṭṭhipeyyālavaggo sattarasamo.

The Section of Sixty Abbreviated Passages is the Seventeenth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Chandenaṭṭhārasa honti, atītena ca dve nava;Yadaniccāṭṭhārasa vuttā, tayo ajjhattabāhirā;Peyyālo saṭṭhiko vutto, buddhenādiccabandhunāti.

Eighteen are on desire, and two nines on the past; Eighteen are said on impermanence, three on the internal and external; The sixtyfold abbreviated passage is declared by the Buddha, Kinsman of the Sun.

Suttantāni saṭṭhi.

There are sixty suttas.
