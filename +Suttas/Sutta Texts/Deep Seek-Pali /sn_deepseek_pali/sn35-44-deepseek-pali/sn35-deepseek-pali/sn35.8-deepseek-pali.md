8. "The eye, monks, is suffering, whether past or future. What need to speak of the present? Seeing thus, monks, the instructed noble disciple is without attachment to the past eye, does not delight in the future eye, and practices for disenchantment, fading away, and cessation of the present eye. The ear is suffering... The nose is suffering... The tongue is suffering, whether past or future. What need to speak of the present? Seeing thus, monks, the instructed noble disciple is without attachment to the past tongue, does not delight in the future tongue, and practices for disenchantment, fading away, and cessation of the present tongue. The body is suffering... The mind is suffering, whether past or future. What need to speak of the present? Seeing thus, monks, the instructed noble disciple is without attachment to the past mind, does not delight in the future mind, and practices for disenchantment, fading away, and cessation of the present mind."
