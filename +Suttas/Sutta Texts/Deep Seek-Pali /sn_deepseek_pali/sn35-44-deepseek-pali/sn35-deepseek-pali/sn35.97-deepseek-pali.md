# SN 35.97 Lokantagamanasuttaṃ

## SN 35.97 The Discourse on Going to the End of the World (Lokantagamana Sutta)

116. ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ [diṭṭheyyaṃ (syā. kaṃ. ka.)], patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmī’’ti. Idaṃ vatvā bhagavā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ pāvisi. Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ, patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmī’ti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’’ti?

116."bhikkhus, I do not say that by traveling one can know, see, or reach the end of the world. Nor do I say, bhikkhus, that without reaching the end of the world, there can be an end to suffering." Having said this, the Blessed One rose from his seat and entered his dwelling. Then, shortly after the Blessed One had left, those bhikkhus considered: "Friends, the Blessed One gave this brief summary without explaining the meaning in detail, and then rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: 'bhikkhus, I do not say that by traveling one can know, see, or reach the end of the world. Nor do I say, bhikkhus, that without reaching the end of the world, there can be an end to suffering.' Who now could explain in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration?"

Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito, sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’’ti.

Then those bhikkhus thought: "This Venerable Ānanda is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise fellow practitioners in the holy life. And Venerable Ānanda is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration. Why don't we go to Venerable Ānanda and ask him about this matter?"

Atha kho te bhikkhū yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā ānandena saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavocuṃ –

Then those bhikkhus approached Venerable Ānanda. Having approached, they exchanged polite greetings with Venerable Ānanda. Having exchanged courteous and amiable greetings, they sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, those bhikkhus said this to Venerable Ānanda:

‘‘Idaṃ kho no, āvuso ānanda, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ, patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmī’ti. Tesaṃ no, āvuso, amhākaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ, patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmīti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’ti? Tesaṃ no, āvuso, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho, āvuso, āyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito, sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’ti. Vibhajatāyasmā ānando’’ti.

"Friend Ānanda, the Blessed One gave us this brief summary, and then, without elaborating on the meaning in detail, he rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: ‘bhikkhus, I do not say that by traveling one can know, see, or reach the end of the world. Nor do I say, bhikkhus, that without reaching the end of the world, there can be an end to suffering.’ Soon after the Blessed One had left, we thought: ‘The Blessed One gave us this brief summary, and then, without elaborating on the meaning in detail, he rose from his seat and entered his dwelling. Now who could explain in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration?’ Then it occurred to us: ‘Venerable Ānanda is both praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise companions in the holy life. And Venerable Ānanda is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration. Why don't we approach Venerable Ānanda and ask him about this matter?’ May Venerable Ānanda explain it.

‘‘Seyyathāpi, āvuso, puriso sāratthiko sāragavesī sārapariyesanaṃ caramāno mahato rukkhassa tiṭṭhato sāravato atikkammeva, mūlaṃ atikkammeva, khandhaṃ sākhāpalāse sāraṃ pariyesitabbaṃ maññeyya; evaṃ sampadamidaṃ āyasmantānaṃ satthari sammukhībhūte taṃ bhagavantaṃ atisitvā amhe etamatthaṃ paṭipucchitabbaṃ maññatha [maññetha (pī. ka.)]. So hāvuso, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti, passaṃ passati – cakkhubhūto, ñāṇabhūto, dhammabhūto, brahmabhūto, vattā, pavattā, atthassa ninnetā, amatassa dātā, dhammassāmī, tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi yaṃ bhagavantaṃyeva etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha. Yathā vo bhagavā byākareyya tathā vo dhāreyyāthā’’ti.

"Just as, friends, a man needing heartwood, seeking heartwood, wandering in search of heartwood, might pass over the root, might pass over the trunk of a great standing tree full of heartwood, thinking that heartwood should be sought among the branches and leaves—such is the case with you venerable ones, who, with the Blessed One present before you, pass him over and think to question us about this matter. For, friends, the Blessed One knows knowing, sees seeing—he is vision, he is knowledge, he is Dhamma, he is Brahma, the speaker, the proclaimer, the elucidator of meaning, the giver of the deathless, the lord of the Dhamma, the Tathāgata. And indeed, this was the time to ask the Blessed One himself about this matter. As the Blessed One would explain it to you, so you should remember it."

‘‘Addhāvuso ānanda, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti, passaṃ passati – cakkhubhūto, ñāṇabhūto, dhammabhūto, brahmabhūto, vattā, pavattā, atthassa ninnetā, amatassa dātā, dhammassāmī, tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi yaṃ bhagavantaṃyeva etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāma. Yathā no bhagavā byākareyya tathā naṃ dhāreyyāma. Api cāyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito, sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Vibhajatāyasmā ānando agaruṃ karitvā’’ti.

"Certainly, friend Ānanda, the Blessed One knows knowing, sees seeing—he is vision, he is knowledge, he is Dhamma, he is Brahma, the speaker, the proclaimer, the elucidator of meaning, the giver of the deathless, the lord of the Dhamma, the Tathāgata. And indeed, this was the time to question the Blessed One himself about this matter. As the Blessed One would explain it, so we would remember it. But Venerable Ānanda is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise fellow practitioners in the holy life. Venerable Ānanda is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration. Let Venerable Ānanda explain without reservation."

‘‘Tenahāvuso, suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā ānando etadavoca –

"Then, friends, listen and apply your mind well. I will speak." "Yes, friend," those bhikkhus replied to the Venerable Ānanda. The Venerable Ānanda said this:

‘‘Yaṃ kho vo, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ, patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmī’ti, imassa khvāhaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. Yena kho, āvuso, lokasmiṃ lokasaññī hoti lokamānī – ayaṃ vuccati ariyassa vinaye loko. Kena cāvuso, lokasmiṃ lokasaññī hoti lokamānī? Cakkhunā kho, āvuso, lokasmiṃ lokasaññī hoti lokamānī. Sotena kho, āvuso… ghānena kho, āvuso… jivhāya kho, āvuso, lokasmiṃ lokasaññī hoti lokamānī. Kāyena kho, āvuso… manena kho, āvuso, lokasmiṃ lokasaññī hoti lokamānī. Yena kho, āvuso, lokasmiṃ lokasaññī hoti lokamānī – ayaṃ vuccati ariyassa vinaye loko. Yaṃ kho vo, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ, patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmī’ti, imassa khvāhaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. Ākaṅkhamānā ca pana tumhe āyasmanto bhagavantaṃyeva upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha. Yathā vo bhagavā byākaroti tathā naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti.

"Friends, the Blessed One gave a brief summary and entered his dwelling without elaboration: ‘bhikkhus, I do not say that by traveling one can know, see, or reach the end of the world. Nor do I say, bhikkhus, that without reaching the end of the world, there is an end to suffering.’ Now, friends, I understand the detailed meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration in this way: That by which one perceives the world and conceives the world—this is called the world in the Noble One’s discipline. And by what, friends, does one perceive the world and conceive the world? By the eye, friends, one perceives the world and conceives the world. By the ear... by the nose... by the tongue... by the body... by the mind, friends, one perceives the world and conceives the world. That by which one perceives the world and conceives the world—this is called the world in the Noble One’s discipline. Friends, the Blessed One gave a brief summary and entered his dwelling without elaboration: ‘bhikkhus, I do not say that by traveling one can know, see, or reach the end of the world. Nor do I say, bhikkhus, that without reaching the end of the world, there is an end to suffering.’ This, friends, is how I understand the detailed meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One. But if you wish, venerable ones, you may approach the Blessed One himself and ask him about this matter. As he explains it to you, so you should remember it."

‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa paṭissutvā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ –

"Very well, friend," those bhikkhus replied to Venerable Ānanda. Having risen from their seats, they approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those bhikkhus said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Yaṃ kho no, bhante, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ, patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmī’ti. Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, gamanena lokassa antaṃ ñāteyyaṃ, daṭṭheyyaṃ, patteyyanti vadāmi. Na ca panāhaṃ, bhikkhave, appatvā lokassa antaṃ dukkhassa antakiriyaṃ vadāmī’ti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyāti? Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito, sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāmā’ti. Atha kho mayaṃ, bhante, yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkamimha; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipucchimha. Tesaṃ no, bhante, āyasmatā ānandena imehi ākārehi imehi padehi imehi byañjanehi attho vibhatto’’ti.

"Venerable sir, the Blessed One gave us a brief summary, and without elaboration, he rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: 'bhikkhus, I do not say that by traveling one can know, see, or reach the end of the world. Nor do I say, bhikkhus, that without reaching the end of the world, there is an end to suffering.' Venerable sir, soon after the Blessed One had left, we thought: 'Friends, the Blessed One gave us this brief summary, and without elaboration, he rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: "bhikkhus, I do not say that by traveling one can know, see, or reach the end of the world. Nor do I say, bhikkhus, that without reaching the end of the world, there is an end to suffering." Who now could explain in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration?' Venerable sir, we then thought: 'This Venerable Ānanda is praised by the Teacher and esteemed by his wise companions in the holy life. He is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief summary stated by the Blessed One without elaboration. Why don’t we go to Venerable Ānanda and ask him about this matter?' Then, venerable sir, we approached Venerable Ānanda and asked him about this matter. Venerable sir, Venerable Ānanda explained the meaning to us in these ways, with these words, and with these expressions."

‘‘Paṇḍito, bhikkhave, ānando; mahāpañño, bhikkhave, ānando! Maṃ cepi tumhe, bhikkhave, etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha, ahampi taṃ evamevaṃ byākareyyaṃ yathā taṃ ānandena byākataṃ. Eso cevetassa attho, evañca naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

"bhikkhus, Ānanda is wise; bhikkhus, Ānanda has great wisdom! If you were to ask me about this matter, I too would explain it just as Ānanda explained it. This is indeed its meaning, and so you should remember it." The Third.
