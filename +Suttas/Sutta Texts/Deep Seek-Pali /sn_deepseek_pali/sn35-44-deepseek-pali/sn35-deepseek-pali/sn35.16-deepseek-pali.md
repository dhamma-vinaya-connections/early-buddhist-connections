# SN 35.16 Dutiyaassādapariyesanasuttaṃ

## SN 35.16 The Second Discourse on the Pursuit of Gratification

16. ‘‘Rūpānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, assādapariyesanaṃ acariṃ. Yo rūpānaṃ assādo tadajjhagamaṃ. Yāvatā rūpānaṃ assādo paññāya me so sudiṭṭho. Rūpānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ādīnavapariyesanaṃ acariṃ. Yo rūpānaṃ ādīnavo tadajjhagamaṃ. Yāvatā rūpānaṃ ādīnavo paññāya me so sudiṭṭho. Rūpānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, nissaraṇapariyesanaṃ acariṃ. Yaṃ rūpānaṃ nissaraṇaṃ tadajjhagamaṃ. Yāvatā rūpānaṃ nissaraṇaṃ, paññāya me taṃ sudiṭṭhaṃ. Saddānāhaṃ, bhikkhave… gandhānāhaṃ, bhikkhave… rasānāhaṃ, bhikkhave… phoṭṭhabbānāhaṃ, bhikkhave… dhammānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, assādapariyesanaṃ acariṃ. Yo dhammānaṃ assādo tadajjhagamaṃ. Yāvatā dhammānaṃ assādo paññāya me so sudiṭṭho. Dhammānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, ādīnavapariyesanaṃ acariṃ. Yo dhammānaṃ ādīnavo tadajjhagamaṃ. Yāvatā dhammānaṃ ādīnavo paññāya me so sudiṭṭho. Dhammānāhaṃ, bhikkhave, nissaraṇapariyesanaṃ acariṃ. Yaṃ dhammānaṃ nissaraṇaṃ tadajjhagamaṃ. Yāvatā dhammānaṃ nissaraṇaṃ, paññāya me taṃ sudiṭṭhaṃ.

16. bhikkhus, I sought the gratification in forms. Whatever gratification there is in forms, that I have attained. To the extent that the gratification in forms exists, I have clearly seen it with wisdom. bhikkhus, I sought the danger in forms. Whatever danger there is in forms, that I have attained. To the extent that the danger in forms exists, I have clearly seen it with wisdom. bhikkhus, I sought the escape from forms. Whatever escape there is from forms, that I have attained. To the extent that the escape from forms exists, I have clearly seen it with wisdom. bhikkhus, I sought the gratification in sounds… in odors… in tastes… in tactile sensations… in mental phenomena. Whatever gratification there is in mental phenomena, that I have attained. To the extent that the gratification in mental phenomena exists, I have clearly seen it with wisdom. bhikkhus, I sought the danger in mental phenomena. Whatever danger there is in mental phenomena, that I have attained. To the extent that the danger in mental phenomena exists, I have clearly seen it with wisdom. bhikkhus, I sought the escape from mental phenomena. Whatever escape there is from mental phenomena, that I have attained. To the extent that the escape from mental phenomena exists, I have clearly seen it with wisdom.

‘‘Yāvakīvañcāhaṃ, bhikkhave, imesaṃ channaṃ bāhirānaṃ āyatanānaṃ assādañca assādato, ādīnavañca ādīnavato, nissaraṇañca nissaraṇato yathābhūtaṃ nābbhaññāsiṃ…pe… paccaññāsiṃ. Ñāṇañca pana me dassanaṃ udapādi – ‘akuppā me vimutti, ayamantimā jāti, natthi dāni punabbhavo’’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

bhikkhus, as long as I did not directly know, as they really are, the gratification in these six external sense bases as gratification, the danger as danger, and the escape as escape, I did not claim to have awakened to unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. But when I did directly know, as they really are, the gratification in these six external sense bases as gratification, the danger as danger, and the escape as escape, then I claimed to have awakened to unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. And the knowledge and vision arose in me: ‘Unshakable is my liberation, this is my last birth, now there is no more becoming.’ The fourth.
