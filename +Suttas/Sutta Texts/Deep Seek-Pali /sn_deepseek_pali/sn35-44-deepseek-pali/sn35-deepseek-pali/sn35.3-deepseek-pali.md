# SN 35.3 Ajjhattānattasuttaṃ

## SN 35.3 The Discourse on the Internal as Not-Self

3. ‘‘Cakkhuṃ, bhikkhave, anattā. Yadanattā taṃ ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya daṭṭhabbaṃ. Sotaṃ anattā…pe… ghānaṃ anattā… jivhā anattā… kāyo anattā… mano anattā. Yadanattā taṃ ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya daṭṭhabbaṃ. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

3. "The eye, bhikkhus, is not-self. What is not-self should be seen as it really is with right wisdom thus: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.' The ear is not-self... The nose is not-self... The tongue is not-self... The body is not-self... The mind is not-self. What is not-self should be seen as it really is with right wisdom thus: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.' Seeing thus... he understands: '...there is no more coming to any state of being.'" The third.
