# SN 35.6 Bāhirānattasuttaṃ

## SN 35.6 The External Not-self Discourse

6. ‘‘Rūpā, bhikkhave, anattā. Yadanattā taṃ ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya daṭṭhabbaṃ. Saddā… gandhā… rasā… phoṭṭhabbā… dhammā anattā. Yadanattā taṃ ‘netaṃ mama, nesohamasmi, na meso attā’ti evametaṃ yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya daṭṭhabbaṃ. Evaṃ passaṃ…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

6. bhikkhus, forms are not-self. What is not-self should be seen as it really is with right wisdom: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.' Sounds... smells... tastes... tactile sensations... mental phenomena are not-self. What is not-self should be seen as it really is with right wisdom: 'This is not mine, this I am not, this is not my self.' Seeing thus... he understands: 'There is no more for this state of being.'" The sixth.
