# SN 35.77 Parihānadhammasuttaṃ

## SN 35.77 The Discourse on Conditions of Decline

96. ‘‘Parihānadhammañca vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi aparihānadhammañca cha ca abhibhāyatanāni. Taṃ suṇātha. Kathañca, bhikkhave, parihānadhammo hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno cakkhunā rūpaṃ disvā uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā sarasaṅkappā [akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.) upari āsīvisavagge sattamasutte pana ‘‘ākusalā sarasaṅkappā’’ tveva sabbattha dissati] saṃyojaniyā. Tañce bhikkhu adhivāseti nappajahati na vinodeti na byantīkaroti [byantikaroti (pī.) byantiṃ karoti (ka.)] na anabhāvaṃ gameti, veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Parihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti…pe….

96.“bhikkhus, I will teach you the conditions of decline and the conditions of non-decline, and the six bases for overcoming. Listen to that. And how, bhikkhus, are there conditions of decline? Here, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu sees a form with the eye, evil, unwholesome, fettering thoughts that flow arise. If that bhikkhu tolerates them, does not abandon them, does not dispel them, does not eliminate them, and does not bring them to an end, then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu should understand: ‘I am declining in wholesome qualities.’ For this has been called decline by the Blessed One.…”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno jivhāya rasaṃ sāyitvā uppajjanti…pe… puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno manasā dhammaṃ viññāya uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tañce bhikkhu adhivāseti nappajahati na vinodeti na byantīkaroti na anabhāvaṃ gameti, veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Parihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, parihānadhammo hoti.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu, having tasted a flavor with the tongue, evil, unwholesome, fettering thoughts that flow arise…Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu, having cognized a mental object with the mind, evil, unwholesome, fettering thoughts that flow arise. If that bhikkhu tolerates them, does not abandon them, does not dispel them, does not eliminate them, and does not bring them to an end, then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu should understand: ‘I am declining in wholesome qualities.’ For this has been called decline by the Blessed One. Thus, bhikkhus, there are conditions of decline.”

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, aparihānadhammo hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno cakkhunā rūpaṃ disvā uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tañce bhikkhu nādhivāseti pajahati vinodeti byantīkaroti anabhāvaṃ gameti, veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘na parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Aparihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti…pe….

“And how, bhikkhus, are there conditions of non-decline? Here, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu sees a form with the eye, evil, unwholesome, fettering thoughts that flow arise. If that bhikkhu does not tolerate them, but abandons them, dispels them, eliminates them, and brings them to an end, then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu should understand: ‘I am not declining in wholesome qualities.’ For this has been called non-decline by the Blessed One.…”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno jivhāya rasaṃ sāyitvā uppajjanti…pe… puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno manasā dhammaṃ viññāya uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tañce bhikkhu nādhivāseti pajahati vinodeti byantīkaroti anabhāvaṃ gameti, veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘na parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Aparihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, aparihānadhammo hoti.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu, having tasted a flavor with the tongue, evil, unwholesome, fettering thoughts that flow arise…Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu, having cognized a mental object with the mind, evil, unwholesome, fettering thoughts that flow arise. If that bhikkhu does not tolerate them, but abandons them, dispels them, eliminates them, and brings them to an end, then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu should understand: ‘I am not declining in wholesome qualities.’ For this has been called non-decline by the Blessed One. Thus, bhikkhus, there are conditions of non-decline.”

‘‘Katamāni ca, bhikkhave, cha abhibhāyatanāni? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno cakkhunā rūpaṃ disvā nuppajjanti pāpakā akusalā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘abhibhūtametaṃ āyatanaṃ’. Abhibhāyatanañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti…pe… puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno manasā dhammaṃ viññāya nuppajjanti pāpakā akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘abhibhūtametaṃ āyatanaṃ’. Abhibhāyatanañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti. Imāni vuccanti, bhikkhave, cha abhibhāyatanānī’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

“And what, bhikkhus, are the six bases for overcoming? Here, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu sees a form with the eye, evil, unwholesome, fettering thoughts that flow do not arise. Then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu should understand: ‘This base has been overcome.’ For this has been called a base for overcoming by the Blessed One.…Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu cognizes a mental object with the mind, evil, unwholesome phenomena, fettering thoughts that flow do not arise. Then, bhikkhus, that bhikkhu should understand: ‘This base has been overcome.’ For this has been called a base for overcoming by the Blessed One. These, bhikkhus, are called the six bases for overcoming.” The Third.
