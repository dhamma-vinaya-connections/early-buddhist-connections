# SN 35.25 Abhiññāpariññāpahānasuttaṃ

## SN 35.25 The Discourse on Abandonment Through Direct Knowledge and Full Understanding

25. ‘‘Sabbaṃ abhiññā pariññā pahānāya vo, bhikkhave, dhammaṃ desessāmi. Taṃ suṇātha. Katamo ca, bhikkhave, sabbaṃ abhiññā pariññā pahānāya dhammo? Cakkhuṃ, bhikkhave, abhiññā pariññā pahātabbaṃ, rūpā abhiññā pariññā pahātabbā, cakkhuviññāṇaṃ abhiññā pariññā pahātabbaṃ, cakkhusamphasso abhiññā pariññā pahātabbo, yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi abhiññā pariññā pahātabbaṃ…pe… jivhā abhiññā pariññā pahātabbā, rasā abhiññā pariññā pahātabbā, jivhāviññāṇaṃ abhiññā pariññā pahātabbaṃ, jivhāsamphasso abhiññā pariññā pahātabbo, yampidaṃ jivhāsamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi abhiññā pariññā pahātabbaṃ. Kāyo abhiññā pariññā pahātabbo… mano abhiññā pariññā pahātabbo, dhammā abhiññā pariññā pahātabbā, manoviññāṇaṃ abhiññā pariññā pahātabbaṃ, manosamphasso abhiññā pariññā pahātabbo, yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi abhiññā pariññā pahātabbaṃ. Ayaṃ kho, bhikkhave, sabbaṃ abhiññā pariññā pahānāya dhammo’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

25. "bhikkhus, I will teach you the Dhamma for the full knowing, complete understanding, and abandoning of all. Listen carefully. And what, bhikkhus, is the Dhamma for the full knowing, complete understanding, and abandoning of all? The eye, bhikkhus, is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Forms are to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Eye-consciousness is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Eye-contact is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Whatever feeling arises with eye-contact as condition—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. …pe… The tongue is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Tastes are to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Tongue-consciousness is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Tongue-contact is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Whatever feeling arises with tongue-contact as condition—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. The body is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. … The mind is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Mental objects are to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Mind-consciousness is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Mind-contact is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. Whatever feeling arises with mind-contact as condition—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too is to be fully known, completely understood, and abandoned. This, bhikkhus, is the Dhamma for the full knowing, complete understanding, and abandoning of all." The third.
