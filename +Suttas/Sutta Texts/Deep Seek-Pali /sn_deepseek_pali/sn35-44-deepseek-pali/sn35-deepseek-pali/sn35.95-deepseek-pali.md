# SN 35.95 Paṭhamamārapāsasuttaṃ

## SN 35.95 The First Discourse on Māra's Snare

114. ‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce, bhikkhu, abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu āvāsagato mārassa, mārassa vasaṃ gato [vasagato (sī. aṭṭha. syā. aṭṭha.)], paṭimukkassa mārapāso. Baddho so mārabandhanena yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe….

114."bhikkhus, there are forms cognizable by the eye—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk delights in them, welcomes them, and remains holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has entered Māra's dwelling, who has come under Māra's control, one upon whom Māra's snare is fastened. He is bound by Māra's bondage, at the disposal of the Evil One…."

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu āvāsagato mārassa, mārassa vasaṃ gato, paṭimukkassa mārapāso. Baddho so mārabandhanena…pe….

"bhikkhus, there are tastes cognizable by the tongue—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk delights in them, welcomes them, and remains holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has entered Māra's dwelling, who has come under Māra's control, one upon whom Māra's snare is fastened. He is bound by Māra's bondage…."

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu āvāsagato mārassa, mārassa vasaṃ gato, paṭimukkassa mārapāso. Baddho so mārabandhanena yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato.

"bhikkhus, there are mind-cognizable phenomena—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk delights in them, welcomes them, and remains holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has entered Māra's dwelling, who has come under Māra's control, one upon whom Māra's snare is fastened. He is bound by Māra's bondage, at the disposal of the Evil One."

‘‘Santi ca kho, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu nāvāsagato mārassa, na mārassa vasaṃ gato, ummukkassa mārapāso. Mutto so mārabandhanena na yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe….

"bhikkhus, there are forms cognizable by the eye—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk does not delight in them, does not welcome them, and does not remain holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has not entered Māra's dwelling, who has not come under Māra's control, one from whom Māra's snare is cast off. He is freed from Māra's bondage, not at the disposal of the Evil One…."

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu nāvāsagato mārassa, na mārassa vasaṃ gato, ummukkassa mārapāso. Mutto so mārabandhanena na yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe….

"bhikkhus, there are tastes cognizable by the tongue—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk does not delight in them, does not welcome them, and does not remain holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has not entered Māra's dwelling, who has not come under Māra's control, one from whom Māra's snare is cast off. He is freed from Māra's bondage, not at the disposal of the Evil One…."

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā, iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu nāvāsagato mārassa, na mārassa vasaṃ gato, ummukkassa mārapāso. Mutto so mārabandhanena na yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

"bhikkhus, there are mind-cognizable phenomena—desirable, agreeable, pleasing, lovely, connected with sensual pleasure, and inspiring lust. If a monk does not delight in them, does not welcome them, and does not remain holding to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has not entered Māra's dwelling, who has not come under Māra's control, one from whom Māra's snare is cast off. He is freed from Māra's bondage, not at the disposal of the Evil One."
