# SN 35.127 Kammanirodhasuttaṃ

## SN 35.127 The Discourse on the Cessation of Kamma

146. ‘‘Navapurāṇāni, bhikkhave, kammāni desessāmi kammanirodhaṃ kammanirodhagāminiñca paṭipadaṃ. Taṃ suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha; bhāsissāmīti. Katamañca, bhikkhave, purāṇakammaṃ? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, purāṇakammaṃ abhisaṅkhataṃ abhisañcetayitaṃ vedaniyaṃ daṭṭhabbaṃ…pe… jivhā purāṇakammā abhisaṅkhatā abhisañcetayitā vedaniyā daṭṭhabbā…pe… mano purāṇakammo abhisaṅkhato abhisañcetayito vedaniyo daṭṭhabbo. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, purāṇakammaṃ. Katamañca, bhikkhave, navakammaṃ? Yaṃ kho, bhikkhave, etarahi kammaṃ karoti kāyena vācāya manasā, idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, navakammaṃ. Katamo ca, bhikkhave, kammanirodho? Yo kho, bhikkhave, kāyakammavacīkammamanokammassa nirodhā vimuttiṃ phusati, ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammanirodho. Katamā ca, bhikkhave, kammanirodhagāminī paṭipadā? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi, sammāsaṅkappo, sammāvācā, sammākammanto, sammāājīvo, sammāvāyāmo, sammāsati, sammāsamādhi – ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammanirodhagāminī paṭipadā. Iti kho, bhikkhave, desitaṃ mayā purāṇakammaṃ, desitaṃ navakammaṃ, desito kammanirodho, desitā kammanirodhagāminī paṭipadā. Yaṃ kho, bhikkhave, satthārā karaṇīyaṃ sāvakānaṃ hitesinā anukampakena anukampaṃ upādāya, kataṃ vo taṃ mayā. Etāni, bhikkhave, rukkhamūlāni, etāni suññāgārāni. Jhāyatha, bhikkhave, mā pamādattha; mā pacchāvippaṭisārino ahuvattha. Ayaṃ vo amhākaṃ anusāsanī’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

146."Bhikkhus, I will teach you the new and old kamma, the cessation of kamma, and the path leading to the cessation of kamma. Listen to it, attend closely; I will speak.""What, bhikkhus, is old kamma? The eye, bhikkhus, is old kamma, formed and willed, to be felt. The ear, bhikkhus, is old kamma, formed and willed, to be felt. The nose, bhikkhus, is old kamma, formed and willed, to be felt. The tongue, bhikkhus, is old kamma, formed and willed, to be felt. The body, bhikkhus, is old kamma, formed and willed, to be felt. The mind, bhikkhus, is old kamma, formed and willed, to be felt. This, bhikkhus, is called old kamma.""What, bhikkhus, is new kamma? Whatever kamma one performs now by body, speech, or mind—this, bhikkhus, is called new kamma.""What, bhikkhus, is the cessation of kamma? The liberation that comes from the cessation of bodily kamma, verbal kamma, and mental kamma—this, bhikkhus, is called the cessation of kamma.""What, bhikkhus, is the path leading to the cessation of kamma? It is this Noble Eightfold Path, namely: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right samādhi. This, bhikkhus, is called the path leading to the cessation of kamma.""Thus, bhikkhus, I have taught old kamma, new kamma, the cessation of kamma, and the path leading to the cessation of kamma. Whatever a teacher, seeking the welfare of his disciples and out of compassion for them, should do, that has been done by me for you. Here, bhikkhus, are these roots of trees, here are these empty dwellings. Meditate, bhikkhus, do not be negligent, lest you regret it later. This is our instruction to you." First.
