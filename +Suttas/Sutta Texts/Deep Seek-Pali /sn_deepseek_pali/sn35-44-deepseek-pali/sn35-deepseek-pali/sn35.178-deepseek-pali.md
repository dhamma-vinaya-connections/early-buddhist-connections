242.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Kimila on the bank of the Ganges River. The Blessed One saw a large log being carried along by the current of the Ganges River. Having seen it, he addressed the bhikkhus: "Do you see, bhikkhus, that large log being carried along by the current of the Ganges?" "Yes, venerable sir."... When this was said, the Venerable Kimila said to the Blessed One: "What, venerable sir, is the near shore... what is the far shore... what is sinking in the middle... what is getting stranded on a bank... what is being taken by humans... what is being taken by non-humans... what is being caught in a whirlpool... what is rotting inwardly?" "Here, Kimila, a bhikkhu has committed a certain defiled offense for which no emerging is apparent. This, Kimila, is called 'rotting inwardly.'" The fifth.
