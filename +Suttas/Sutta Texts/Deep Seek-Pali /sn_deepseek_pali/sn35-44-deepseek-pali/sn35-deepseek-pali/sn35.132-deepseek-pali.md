# SN 35.132 Antevāsikasuttaṃ

## SN 35.132 The Discourse on the Student

151. ‘‘Anantevāsikamidaṃ, bhikkhave, brahmacariyaṃ vussati anācariyakaṃ. Santevāsiko, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sācariyako dukkhaṃ na phāsu [phāsuṃ (sī. pī.)] viharati. Anantevāsiko, bhikkhave, bhikkhu anācariyako sukhaṃ phāsu viharati. Kathañca, bhikkhu, santevāsiko sācariyako dukkhaṃ na phāsu viharati? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno cakkhunā rūpaṃ disvā uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tyāssa anto vasanti, antassa vasanti pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā santevāsikoti vuccati. Te naṃ samudācaranti, samudācaranti naṃ pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā sācariyakoti vuccati…pe….

151.“This holy life, bhikkhus, is lived without a resident, without a teacher. A monk who is with a resident and with a teacher dwells in suffering, not at ease. A monk who is without a resident and without a teacher dwells happily, at ease. And how, bhikkhus, does a monk who is with a resident and with a teacher dwell in suffering, not at ease? Here, bhikkhus, when a monk sees a form with the eye, evil unwholesome states—thoughts connected with relish, conducive to fetters—arise in him. These dwell within him; because evil unwholesome states dwell within him, he is called ‘one with a resident.’ They harass him; because evil unwholesome states harass him, he is called ‘one with a teacher.’…and so on….”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno jivhāya rasaṃ sāyitvā uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tyāssa anto vasanti, antassa vasanti pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā santevāsikoti vuccati. Te naṃ samudācaranti, samudācaranti naṃ pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā sācariyakoti vuccati…pe….

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a monk tastes a flavor with the tongue, evil unwholesome states—thoughts connected with relish, conducive to fetters—arise in him. These dwell within him; because evil unwholesome states dwell within him, he is called ‘one with a resident.’ They harass him; because evil unwholesome states harass him, he is called ‘one with a teacher.’…and so on….”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno manasā dhammaṃ viññāya uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tyāssa anto vasanti, antassa vasanti pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā santevāsikoti vuccati. Te naṃ samudācaranti, samudācaranti naṃ pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā sācariyakoti vuccati. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu santevāsiko sācariyako dukkhaṃ, na phāsu viharati.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a monk cognizes a mental phenomenon with the mind, evil unwholesome states—thoughts connected with relish, conducive to fetters—arise in him. These dwell within him; because evil unwholesome states dwell within him, he is called ‘one with a resident.’ They harass him; because evil unwholesome states harass him, he is called ‘one with a teacher.’ In this way, bhikkhus, a monk who is with a resident and with a teacher dwells in suffering, not at ease.”

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu anantevāsiko anācariyako sukhaṃ phāsu viharati? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno cakkhunā rūpaṃ disvā na uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tyāssa na anto vasanti, nāssa anto vasanti pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā anantevāsikoti vuccati. Te naṃ na samudācaranti, na samudācaranti naṃ pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā anācariyakoti vuccati…pe….

“And how, bhikkhus, does a monk who is without a resident and without a teacher dwell happily, at ease? Here, bhikkhus, when a monk sees a form with the eye, evil unwholesome states—thoughts connected with relish, conducive to fetters—do not arise in him. These do not dwell within him; because evil unwholesome states do not dwell within him, he is called ‘one without a resident.’ They do not harass him; because evil unwholesome states do not harass him, he is called ‘one without a teacher.’…and so on….”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno jivhāya rasaṃ sāyitvā na uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tyāssa na anto vasanti, nāssa anto vasanti pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā anantevāsikoti vuccati. Te naṃ na samudācaranti, na samudācaranti naṃ pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā anācariyakoti vuccati…pe….

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a monk tastes a flavor with the tongue, evil unwholesome states—thoughts connected with relish, conducive to fetters—do not arise in him. These do not dwell within him; because evil unwholesome states do not dwell within him, he is called ‘one without a resident.’ They do not harass him; because evil unwholesome states do not harass him, he is called ‘one without a teacher.’…and so on….”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno manasā dhammaṃ viññāya na uppajjanti pāpakā akusalā dhammā sarasaṅkappā saṃyojaniyā. Tyāssa na anto vasanti, nāssa anto vasanti pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā anantevāsikoti vuccati. Te naṃ na samudācaranti, na samudācaranti naṃ pāpakā akusalā dhammāti. Tasmā anācariyakoti vuccati. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu anantevāsiko anācariyako sukhaṃ phāsu viharati. Anantevāsikamidaṃ, bhikkhave, brahmacariyaṃ vussati. Anācariyakaṃ santevāsiko, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sācariyako dukkhaṃ, na phāsu viharati. Anantevāsiko, bhikkhave, bhikkhu anācariyako sukhaṃ phāsu viharatī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a monk cognizes a mental phenomenon with the mind, evil unwholesome states—thoughts connected with relish, conducive to fetters—do not arise in him. These do not dwell within him; because evil unwholesome states do not dwell within him, he is called ‘one without a resident.’ They do not harass him; because evil unwholesome states do not harass him, he is called ‘one without a teacher.’ In this way, bhikkhus, a monk who is without a resident and without a teacher dwells happily, at ease. This holy life, bhikkhus, is lived without a resident, without a teacher. A monk who is with a resident and with a teacher dwells in suffering, not at ease. A monk who is without a resident and without a teacher, bhikkhus, dwells happily, at ease.” The sixth.
