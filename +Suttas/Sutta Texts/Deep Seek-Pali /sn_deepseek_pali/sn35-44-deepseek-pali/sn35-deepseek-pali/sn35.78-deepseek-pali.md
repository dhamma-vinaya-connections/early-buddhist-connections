# SN 35.78 Pamādavihārīsuttaṃ

## SN 35.78 The Discourse on Dwelling in Heedlessness

97. ‘‘Pamādavihāriñca vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi appamādavihāriñca. Taṃ suṇātha. Kathañca, bhikkhave, pamādavihārī hoti? Cakkhundriyaṃ asaṃvutassa, bhikkhave, viharato cittaṃ byāsiñcati [byāsiccati (sī. syā. kaṃ.)]. Cakkhuviññeyyesu rūpesu tassa byāsittacittassa pāmojjaṃ na hoti. Pāmojje asati pīti na hoti. Pītiyā asati passaddhi na hoti. Passaddhiyā asati dukkhaṃ hoti. Dukkhino cittaṃ na samādhiyati. Asamāhite citte dhammā na pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ apātubhāvā pamādavihārī tveva saṅkhaṃ gacchati…pe… jivhindriyaṃ asaṃvutassa, bhikkhave, viharato cittaṃ byāsiñcati jivhāviññeyyesu rasesu, tassa byāsittacittassa…pe… pamādavihārī tveva saṅkhaṃ gacchati…pe… manindriyaṃ asaṃvutassa, bhikkhave, viharato cittaṃ byāsiñcati manoviññeyyesu dhammesu, tassa byāsittacittassa pāmojjaṃ na hoti. Pāmojje asati pīti na hoti. Pītiyā asati passaddhi na hoti. Passaddhiyā asati dukkhaṃ hoti. Dukkhino cittaṃ na samādhiyati. Asamāhite citte dhammā na pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ apātubhāvā pamādavihārī tveva saṅkhaṃ gacchati. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pamādavihārī hoti.

97.“Monks, I shall teach you about dwelling heedlessly and dwelling heedfully. Listen carefully. And how, monks, does one dwell heedlessly? When, monks, one dwells with the eye faculty unrestrained, the mind becomes scattered. When one’s mind is scattered among forms cognizable by the eye, joy does not arise. When joy does not arise, rapture does not arise. When rapture does not arise, tranquility does not arise. When tranquility does not arise, suffering arises. When suffering, the mind does not become collected. When the mind is not collected, phenomena do not become manifest. Because phenomena do not become manifest, one is reckoned as dwelling heedlessly. [...] When, monks, one dwells with the tongue faculty unrestrained, the mind becomes scattered among flavors cognizable by the tongue; when one’s mind is scattered [...] one is reckoned as dwelling heedlessly. [...] When, monks, one dwells with the mind faculty unrestrained, the mind becomes scattered among mental phenomena cognizable by the mind. When one’s mind is scattered, joy does not arise. When joy does not arise, rapture does not arise. When rapture does not arise, tranquility does not arise. When tranquility does not arise, suffering arises. When suffering, the mind does not become collected. When the mind is not collected, phenomena do not become manifest. Because phenomena do not become manifest, one is reckoned as dwelling heedlessly. It is in this way, monks, that one dwells heedlessly.”

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, appamādavihārī hoti? Cakkhundriyaṃ saṃvutassa, bhikkhave, viharato cittaṃ na byāsiñcati cakkhuviññeyyesu rūpesu, tassa abyāsittacittassa pāmojjaṃ jāyati. Pamuditassa pīti jāyati. Pītimanassa kāyo passambhati. Passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ viharati. Sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati. Samāhite citte dhammā pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ pātubhāvā appamādavihārī tveva saṅkhaṃ gacchati…pe… jivhindriyaṃ saṃvutassa, bhikkhave, viharato cittaṃ na byāsiñcati…pe… appamādavihārī tveva saṅkhaṃ gacchati. Manindriyaṃ saṃvutassa, bhikkhave, viharato cittaṃ na byāsiñcati, manoviññeyyesu dhammesu, tassa abyāsittacittassa pāmojjaṃ jāyati. Pamuditassa pīti jāyati. Pītimanassa kāyo passambhati. Passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ viharati. Sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati. Samāhite citte dhammā pātubhavanti. Dhammānaṃ pātubhāvā appamādavihārī tveva saṅkhaṃ gacchati. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, appamādavihārī hotī’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

“And how, monks, does one dwell heedfully? When, monks, one dwells with the eye faculty restrained, the mind is not scattered among forms cognizable by the eye; when one’s mind is not scattered, joy arises. When joyful, rapture arises. When the mind is enraptured, the body becomes tranquil. With a tranquil body, one dwells happily. When happy, the mind becomes collected. When the mind is collected, phenomena become manifest. Because phenomena become manifest, one is reckoned as dwelling heedfully. [...] When, monks, one dwells with the tongue faculty restrained, the mind is not scattered [...] one is reckoned as dwelling heedfully. When, monks, one dwells with the mind faculty restrained, the mind is not scattered among mental phenomena cognizable by the mind; when one’s mind is not scattered, joy arises. When joyful, rapture arises. When the mind is enraptured, the body becomes tranquil. With a tranquil body, one dwells happily. When happy, the mind becomes collected. When the mind is collected, phenomena become manifest. Because phenomena become manifest, one is reckoned as dwelling heedfully. It is in this way, monks, that one dwells heedfully.” The fourth.
