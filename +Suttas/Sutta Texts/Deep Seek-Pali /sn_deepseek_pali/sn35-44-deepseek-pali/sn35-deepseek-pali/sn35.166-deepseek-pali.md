# SN 35.166 Bāḷisikopamasuttaṃ

## SN 35.166 The Fisherman Simile

230. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, bāḷisiko āmisagatabaḷisaṃ gambhīre udakarahade pakkhipeyya. Tamenaṃ aññataro āmisacakkhu maccho gileyya. Evañhi so, bhikkhave, maccho gilitabaḷiso bāḷisikassa anayaṃ āpanno byasanaṃ āpanno yathākāmakaraṇīyo bāḷisikassa.

230."bhikkhus, suppose a fisherman were to cast a baited hook into a deep lake. A certain fish with eyes for bait would swallow it. Thus, bhikkhus, that fish, having swallowed the hook, would come to ruin and disaster, and would be at the fisherman's mercy."

Evameva kho, bhikkhave, chayime baḷisā lokasmiṃ anayāya sattānaṃ vadhāya [byābādhāya (sī. pī.)] pāṇinaṃ. Katame cha? Santi, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce, bhikkhu, abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu gilitabaḷiso, mārassa anayaṃ āpanno byasanaṃ āpanno yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe… santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā…pe….

"Just so, bhikkhus, these six hooks exist in the world for the misfortune, harm, and destruction of beings. What six? There are, bhikkhus, forms cognizable by the eye—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, connected with sensuality, arousing lust. If a monk delights in them, approves of them, and remains attached to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has swallowed the hook, who has met with misfortune, who has come to ruin, and is at the Evil One's mercy... There are, bhikkhus, flavors cognizable by the tongue... There are, bhikkhus, sounds cognizable by the ear... There are, bhikkhus, odors cognizable by the nose... There are, bhikkhus, tactile objects cognizable by the body... There are, bhikkhus, mental phenomena cognizable by the mind—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, connected with sensuality, arousing lust. If a monk delights in them, approves of them, and remains attached to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has swallowed the hook, who has met with misfortune, who has come to ruin, and is at the Evil One's mercy."

Santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce, bhikkhu, abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu gilitabaḷiso mārassa anayaṃ āpanno byasanaṃ āpanno yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato.

"bhikkhus, there are mental phenomena cognizable by the mind that are desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, connected with sensuality, arousing lust. If a monk delights in them, approves of them, and remains attached to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has swallowed Māra’s hook, met with misfortune, come to ruin, and is at the Evil One's mercy."

‘‘Santi ca, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu na gilitabaḷiso mārassa abhedi baḷisaṃ paribhedi baḷisaṃ na anayaṃ āpanno na byasanaṃ āpanno na yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato…pe….

"bhikkhus, there are sights cognizable by the eye—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, connected with sensuality, arousing lust. If a monk does not delight in them, does not approve of them, and does not remain attached to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has not swallowed Māra's hook, has broken the hook, has cut the hook, has not met with misfortune, has not come to ruin, and is not at the Evil One's mercy...pe...."

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā…pe…. Santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati, ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, bhikkhu na gilitabaḷiso mārassa abhedi baḷisaṃ paribhedi baḷisaṃ na anayaṃ āpanno na byasanaṃ āpanno na yathākāmakaraṇīyo pāpimato’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

"bhikkhus, there are tastes cognizable by the tongue... bhikkhus, there are mental phenomena cognizable by the mind that are desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, connected with sensuality, arousing lust. If a monk does not delight in them, does not approve of them, and does not remain attached to them, this is called, bhikkhus, a monk who has not swallowed Māra's hook, has broken the hook, has cut the hook, has not met with misfortune, has not come to ruin, and is not at the Evil One's mercy." Third.
