# SN 35.12 Bāhirānattātītānāgatasuttaṃ

## SN 35.12 The Discourse on the External Not-self, Past, and Future.

12. ‘‘Rūpā, bhikkhave, anattā atītānāgatā; ko pana vādo paccuppannānaṃ! Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave, sutavā ariyasāvako atītesu rūpesu anapekkho hoti; anāgate rūpe nābhinandati; paccuppannānaṃ rūpānaṃ nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipanno hoti. Saddā… gandhā… rasā… phoṭṭhabbā… dhammā anattā atītānāgatā; ko pana vādo paccuppannānaṃ! Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave, sutavā ariyasāvako atītesu dhammesu anapekkho hoti; anāgate dhamme nābhinandati; paccuppannānaṃ dhammānaṃ nibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya paṭipanno hotī’’ti. Dvādasamaṃ.

12. "bhikkhus, forms are not-self, whether past or future, how much more so regarding present forms! Seeing thus, bhikkhus, a learned noble disciple becomes unconcerned with past forms, does not delight in future forms, and undertakes practice for disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation regarding present forms. Sounds... smells... tastes... touches... mental phenomena are not-self, whether past or future, how much more so regarding present mental phenomena! Seeing thus, bhikkhus, a learned noble disciple becomes unconcerned with past mental phenomena, does not delight in future mental phenomena, and undertakes practice for disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation regarding present mental phenomena." The twelfth.

Aniccavaggo paṭhamo.

The Chapter on Impermanence, the First.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The Verses for Recapitulation:

Aniccaṃ dukkhaṃ anattā ca, tayo ajjhattabāhirā;Yadaniccena tayo vuttā, te te ajjhattabāhirāti.

Impermanent, suffering, and not-self — these three are internal and external.
Whatever three were stated because of impermanence, those very ones are internal and external.
