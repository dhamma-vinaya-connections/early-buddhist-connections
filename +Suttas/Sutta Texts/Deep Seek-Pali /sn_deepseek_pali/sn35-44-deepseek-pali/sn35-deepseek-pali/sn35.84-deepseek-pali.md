# SN 35.84 Udakasuttaṃ

## SN 35.84 The Udaka Discourse

103. ‘‘Udako [uddako (sī. pī.)] sudaṃ, bhikkhave, rāmaputto evaṃ vācaṃ bhāsati – ‘idaṃ jātu vedagū, idaṃ jātu sabbajī [sabbaji (pī.)], idaṃ jātu apalikhataṃ gaṇḍamūlaṃ palikhaṇi’nti. Taṃ kho panetaṃ, bhikkhave, udako rāmaputto avedagūyeva samāno ‘vedagūsmī’ti bhāsati, asabbajīyeva samāno ‘sabbajīsmī’ti bhāsati, apalikhataṃyeva gaṇḍamūlaṃ palikhataṃ me ‘gaṇḍamūla’nti bhāsati. Idha kho taṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘idaṃ jātu vedagū, idaṃ jātu sabbajī, idaṃ jātu apalikhataṃ gaṇḍamūlaṃ palikhaṇi’’’nti.

103.“bhikkhus, Udaka Rāmaputta used to speak thus: ‘I am indeed a knower of the Vedas, I am indeed the all-conqueror, I have indeed excavated the unexcavated root of the boil.’ But, bhikkhus, Udaka Rāmaputta, being himself not a knower of the Vedas, spoke thus: ‘I am a knower of the Vedas’; being himself not an all-conqueror, spoke thus: ‘I am an all-conqueror’; the root of the boil being unexcavated, spoke thus: ‘The root of the boil has been excavated by me.’ Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu speaking rightly would say: ‘I am indeed a knower of the Vedas, I am indeed the all-conqueror, I have indeed excavated the unexcavated root of the boil.’ ”

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, vedagū hoti? Yato kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu channaṃ phassāyatanānaṃ samudayañca atthaṅgamañca assādañca ādīnavañca nissaraṇañca yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti; evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu vedagū hoti.

“And how, bhikkhus, is one a knower of the Vedas? When, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu truly understands the origin, the passing away, the gratification, the danger, and the escape in regard to the six sense bases, then, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is a knower of the Vedas.”

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabbajī hoti? Yato kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu channaṃ phassāyatanānaṃ samudayañca atthaṅgamañca assādañca ādīnavañca nissaraṇañca yathābhūtaṃ viditvā anupādāvimutto hoti; evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sabbajī hoti.

“And how, bhikkhus, is a bhikkhu an all-conqueror? When, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu, having truly understood the origin, the passing away, the gratification, the danger, and the escape in regard to the six sense bases, is liberated without clinging, then, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is an all-conqueror.”

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno apalikhataṃ gaṇḍamūlaṃ palikhataṃ hoti? Gaṇḍoti kho, bhikkhave, imassetaṃ cātumahābhūtikassa kāyassa adhivacanaṃ mātāpettikasambhavassa odanakummāsūpacayassa aniccucchādanaparimaddanabhedanaviddhaṃsanadhammassa. Gaṇḍamūlanti kho, bhikkhave, taṇhāyetaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Yato kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno taṇhā pahīnā hoti ucchinnamūlā tālāvatthukatā anabhāvaṅkatā āyatiṃ anuppādadhammā; evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno apalikhataṃ gaṇḍamūlaṃ palikhataṃ hoti.

“And how, bhikkhus, is it that for a bhikkhu the unexcavated root of the boil is excavated? ‘Boil,’ bhikkhus, is a designation for this body composed of the four great elements, born of mother and father, nourished on rice and gruel, subject to impermanence, to being rubbed and massaged, to breaking apart and dissolution. ‘Root of the boil,’ bhikkhus, is a designation for craving. When, bhikkhus, craving has been abandoned by a bhikkhu, its root cut off, made like a palm stump, obliterated, so that it is no longer subject to future arising; then, bhikkhus, for that bhikkhu the unexcavated root of the boil is excavated.”

‘‘Udako sudaṃ, bhikkhave, rāmaputto evaṃ vācaṃ bhāsati – ‘idaṃ jātu vedagū, idaṃ jātu sabbajī, idaṃ jātu apalikhataṃ gaṇḍamūlaṃ palikhaṇi’nti. Taṃ kho panetaṃ, bhikkhave, udako rāmaputto avedagūyeva samāno ‘vedagūsmī’ti bhāsati, asabbajīyeva samāno ‘sabbajīsmī’ti bhāsati; apalikhataṃyeva gaṇḍamūlaṃ ‘palikhataṃ me gaṇḍamūla’nti bhāsati. Idha kho taṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘idaṃ jātu vedagū, idaṃ jātu sabbajī, idaṃ jātu apalikhataṃ gaṇḍamūlaṃ palikhaṇi’’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

“Udaka Rāmaputta, bhikkhus, used to speak thus: ‘I am indeed a knower of the Vedas, I am indeed the all-conqueror, I have indeed excavated the unexcavated root of the boil.’ But, bhikkhus, Udaka Rāmaputta, being himself not a knower of the Vedas, spoke thus: ‘I am a knower of the Vedas’; being himself not an all-conqueror, spoke thus: ‘I am an all-conqueror’; the root of the boil being unexcavated, spoke thus: ‘The root of the boil has been excavated by me.’ Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu speaking rightly would say: ‘I am indeed a knower of the Vedas, I am indeed the all-conqueror, I have indeed excavated the unexcavated root of the boil.’ ” The tenth.

Saḷavaggo dasamo.

The Saḷavagga is the tenth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Dve saṃgayhā parihānaṃ, pamādavihārī ca saṃvaro;Samādhi paṭisallānaṃ, dve natumhākena uddakoti.

Two on decline, dwelling in heedlessness, and restraint; samādhi, seclusion, two “not yours,” and Udaka.

Saḷāyatanavagge dutiyapaṇṇāsako samatto.

The second fifty of the Saḷāyatana Vagga is completed.

Tassa vagguddānaṃ –

The chapter summary:

Avijjā migajālañca, gilānaṃ channaṃ catutthakaṃ;Saḷavaggena paññāsaṃ, dutiyo paṇṇāsako ayanti.

Ignorance, Migajāla, the sick, and the hidden, the fourth; With the Saḷa chapter, fifty, this is the second fifty.

Paṭhamasatakaṃ.

The First Hundred.
