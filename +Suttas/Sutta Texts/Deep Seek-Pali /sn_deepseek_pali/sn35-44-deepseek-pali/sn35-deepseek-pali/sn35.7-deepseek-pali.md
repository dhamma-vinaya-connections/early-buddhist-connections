7. "Monks, the eye is impermanent, past and future; what then of the present? Seeing thus, monks, a learned noble disciple does not long for the past eye, does not delight in the future eye, and practices for disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation regarding the present eye. The ear is impermanent... the nose is impermanent... the tongue is impermanent, past and future; what then of the present? Seeing thus, monks, a learned noble disciple does not long for the past tongue, does not delight in the future tongue, and practices for disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation regarding the present tongue. The body is impermanent... the mind is impermanent, past and future; what then of the present? Seeing thus, monks, a learned noble disciple does not long for the past mind, does not delight in the future mind, and practices for disenchantment, dispassion, and cessation regarding the present mind."
