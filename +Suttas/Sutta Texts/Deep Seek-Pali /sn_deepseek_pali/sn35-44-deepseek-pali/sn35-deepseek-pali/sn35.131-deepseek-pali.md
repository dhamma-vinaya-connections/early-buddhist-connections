# SN 35.131 Nibbānasappāyapaṭipadāsuttaṃ

## SN 35.131 The Discourse on the Path Conducive to Nibbāna

150. ‘‘Nibbānasappāyaṃ vo, bhikkhave, paṭipadaṃ desessāmi. Taṃ suṇātha…pe… katamā ca sā, bhikkhave, nibbānasappāyā paṭipadā? Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, bhikkhave, cakkhu niccaṃ vā aniccaṃ vā’’ti?

150.“bhikkhus, I will teach you the path conducive to Nibbāna. Listen to it carefully… And what, bhikkhus, is that path conducive to Nibbāna? What do you think, bhikkhus, is the eye permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccaṃ, bhante’’.

“Impermanent, venerable sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vā taṃ sukhaṃ vā’’ti?

“And what is impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

‘‘Dukkhaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suffering, venerable sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vipariṇāmadhammaṃ, kallaṃ nu taṃ samanupassituṃ – ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’’’ti?

“And what is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, is it fitting to regard that as: ‘This is mine, this I am, this is my self’?”

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“No, venerable sir.”

‘‘Rūpā niccā vā aniccā vā’’ti?

“Are forms permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccā, bhante’’…pe….

“Impermanent, venerable sir,”…and so on…

‘‘Cakkhuviññāṇaṃ… cakkhusamphasso…pe… yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi niccaṃ vā aniccaṃ vā’’ti?

“Is eye-consciousness… eye-contact… whatever feeling arises dependent on mind-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither painful nor pleasant—permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccaṃ, bhante’’.

“Impermanent, venerable sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vā taṃ sukhaṃ vā’’ti?

“And what is impermanent, is it suffering or happiness?”

‘‘Dukkhaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suffering, venerable sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vipariṇāmadhammaṃ, kallaṃ nu taṃ samanupassituṃ – ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’’’ti?

“And what is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, is it fitting to regard that as: ‘This is mine, this I am, this is my self’?”

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“No, venerable sir.”

‘‘Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave, sutavā ariyasāvako cakkhusmimpi nibbindati, rūpesupi nibbindati, cakkhuviññāṇepi nibbindati, cakkhusamphassepi nibbindati…pe… yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tasmimpi nibbindati. Nibbindaṃ virajjati; virāgā vimuccati …pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānāti. Ayaṃ kho sā, bhikkhave, nibbānasappāyā paṭipadā’’ti. Pañcamaṃ.

“Seeing thus, bhikkhus, the well-instructed noble disciple becomes disenchanted with the eye, disenchanted with forms, disenchanted with eye-consciousness, disenchanted with eye-contact…and so on… He becomes disenchanted also with whatever feeling arises dependent on mind-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither painful nor pleasant. Being disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate; through dispassion, he is liberated… He understands: ‘…there is no more for this state of being.’ This, bhikkhus, is the path conducive to Nibbāna.” The fifth.
