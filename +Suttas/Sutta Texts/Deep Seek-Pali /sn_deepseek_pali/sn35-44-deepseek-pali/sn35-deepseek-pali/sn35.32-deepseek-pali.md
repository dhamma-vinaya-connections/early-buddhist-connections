# SN 35.32 Dutiyasamugghātasappāyasuttaṃ

## SN 35.32 The Second Discourse on What Is Conducive to Uprooting

32. ‘‘Sabbamaññitasamugghātasappāyaṃ vo, bhikkhave, paṭipadaṃ desessāmi. Taṃ suṇātha. Katamā ca sā, bhikkhave, sabbamaññitasamugghātasappāyā paṭipadā?

32.“bhikkhus, I will teach you the practice conducive to the uprooting of all conceiving. Listen to it. And what, bhikkhus, is that practice conducive to the uprooting of all conceiving?

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, bhikkhave, cakkhuṃ niccaṃ vā aniccaṃ vā’’ti?

“What do you think, bhikkhus, is the eye permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccaṃ, bhante’’.

“Impermanent, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vā taṃ sukhaṃ vā’’ti?

“But is what is impermanent suffering or happiness?”

‘‘Dukkhaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suffering, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vipariṇāmadhammaṃ, kallaṃ nu taṃ samanupassituṃ – ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’’’ti?

“But is what is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, proper to regard thus: ‘This is mine, this I am, this is my self’?”

‘‘No hetaṃ bhante’’.

“No, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Rūpā…pe… cakkhuviññāṇaṃ… cakkhusamphasso nicco vā anicco vā’’ti?

“Are forms…eye-consciousness…eye-contact permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Anicco, bhante’’…pe….

“Impermanent, Venerable Sir.”… (and so on)

‘‘Yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi niccaṃ vā aniccaṃ vā’’ti?

“And is that feeling—pleasant or painful or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that arises dependent on eye-contact, permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccaṃ, bhante’’.

“Impermanent, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vā taṃ sukhaṃ vā’’ti?

“But is what is impermanent suffering or happiness?”

‘‘Dukkhaṃ, bhante’’.

“Suffering, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vipariṇāmadhammaṃ, kallaṃ nu taṃ samanupassituṃ – ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’’’ti?

“But is what is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, proper to regard thus: ‘This is mine, this I am, this is my self’?”

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’…pe….

“No, Venerable Sir.”… (and so on)

‘‘Jivhā niccā vā aniccā vā’’ti?

“Is the tongue permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccā bhante’’…pe….

“Impermanent, Venerable Sir.”… (and so on)

‘‘Rasā… jivhāviññāṇaṃ… jivhāsamphasso…pe… yampidaṃ jivhāsamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi niccaṃ vā aniccaṃ vā’’ti?

“Are tastes…tongue-consciousness…tongue-contact… And is that feeling—pleasant or painful or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that arises dependent on tongue-contact, permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccaṃ, bhante’’…pe… dhammā… manoviññāṇaṃ… manosamphasso nicco vā anicco vāti?

“Impermanent, Venerable Sir.”… “Are mental phenomena…mind-consciousness…mind-contact permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Anicco, bhante’’.

“Impermanent, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi niccaṃ vā aniccaṃ vā’’ti?

“And is that feeling—pleasant or painful or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that arises dependent on mind-contact, permanent or impermanent?”

‘‘Aniccaṃ, bhante’’.

“Impermanent, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vā taṃ sukhaṃ vā’’ti?

“But is what is impermanent suffering or happiness?”

‘‘Dukkhaṃ bhante’’.

“Suffering, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vipariṇāmadhammaṃ, kallaṃ nu taṃ samanupassituṃ – ‘etaṃ mama, esohamasmi, eso me attā’’’ti?

“But is what is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change, proper to regard thus: ‘This is mine, this I am, this is my self’?”

‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“No, Venerable Sir.”

‘‘Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave, sutavā ariyasāvako cakkhusmimpi nibbindati, rūpesupi nibbindati, cakkhuviññāṇepi nibbindati, cakkhusamphassepi nibbindati. Yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tasmimpi nibbindati…pe… jivhāyapi nibbindati, rasesupi…pe… yampidaṃ jivhāsamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tasmimpi nibbindati. Manasmimpi nibbindati, dhammesupi nibbindati, manoviññāṇepi nibbindati, manosamphassepi nibbindati. Yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tasmimpi nibbindati. Nibbindaṃ virajjati; virāgā vimuccati; vimuttasmiṃ vimuttamiti ñāṇaṃ hoti. ‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāti. Ayaṃ kho sā, bhikkhave, sabbamaññitasamugghātasappāyā paṭipadā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

“Seeing thus, bhikkhus, the well-taught noble disciple becomes disenchanted with the eye, disenchanted with forms, disenchanted with eye-consciousness, disenchanted with eye-contact. Whatever feeling arises dependent on eye-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—he becomes disenchanted with that too.… He becomes disenchanted with the tongue, disenchanted with tastes.… Whatever feeling arises dependent on tongue-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—he becomes disenchanted with that too. He becomes disenchanted with the mind, disenchanted with mental phenomena, disenchanted with mind-consciousness, disenchanted with mind-contact. Whatever feeling arises dependent on mind-contact—whether pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—he becomes disenchanted with that too. Being disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate. Through dispassion, he is liberated. When liberated, there is the knowledge, ‘Liberated.’ He understands: ‘Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being.’ This, bhikkhus, is the practice conducive to the uprooting of all conceiving.” The Tenth.

Sabbavaggo tatiyo.

The Third Chapter: The All.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its Summary:

Sabbañca dvepi pahānā, parijānāpare duve;Ādittaṃ addhabhūtañca, sāruppā dve ca sappāyā;Vaggo tena pavuccatīti.

The All, and two abandonings; two full understandings, and two others; The Burning, and the Afflicted; two fitting, and two conducive; Thus is the chapter called.

4. Jātidhammavaggo

4.The Chapter on Things Subject to Birth

## 1-10. Jātidhammādisuttadasakaṃ

### 1-10. The Decad of Discourses beginning with "Subject to Birth"

33. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Tatra kho…pe… ‘‘sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, jātidhammaṃ. Kiñca, bhikkhave, sabbaṃ jātidhammaṃ? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, jātidhammaṃ. Rūpā… cakkhuviññāṇaṃ… cakkhusamphasso jātidhammo. Yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi jātidhammaṃ…pe… jivhā… rasā… jivhāviññāṇaṃ… jivhāsamphasso… yampidaṃ jivhāsamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi jātidhammaṃ. Kāyo…pe... mano jātidhammo, dhammā jātidhammā, manoviññāṇaṃ jātidhammaṃ, manosamphasso jātidhammo. Yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi jātidhammaṃ. Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave, sutavā ariyasāvako cakkhusmimpi nibbindati, rūpesupi… cakkhuviññāṇepi… cakkhusamphassepi…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānātī’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

33.At Sāvatthī. There…pe… the Blessed One said: "All, bhikkhus, is subject to birth. And what, bhikkhus, is all subject to birth? The eye, bhikkhus, is subject to birth. Forms… eye-consciousness… eye-contact is subject to birth. Whatever feeling arises dependent on eye-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-unpleasant—that too is subject to birth…pe… The tongue… tastes… tongue-consciousness… tongue-contact… whatever feeling arises dependent on tongue-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-unpleasant—that too is subject to birth. The body…pe… The mind is subject to birth, mental phenomena are subject to birth, mind-consciousness is subject to birth, mind-contact is subject to birth. Whatever feeling arises dependent on mind-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-unpleasant—that too is subject to birth. Seeing thus, bhikkhus, the learned noble disciple becomes disenchanted with the eye, with forms, with eye-consciousness, with eye-contact…pe… He understands: 'There is no more for this state of being.'" First.

34. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, jarādhammaṃ…pe… saṃkhittaṃ. Dutiyaṃ.

34."All, bhikkhus, is subject to aging…pe… in brief. Second.

35. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, byādhidhammaṃ…pe…. Tatiyaṃ.

35."All, bhikkhus, is subject to sickness…pe…. Third.

36. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, maraṇadhammaṃ…pe…. Catutthaṃ.

36."All, bhikkhus, is subject to death…pe…. Fourth.

37. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, sokadhammaṃ…pe…. Pañcamaṃ.

37."All, bhikkhus, is subject to sorrow…pe…. Fifth.

38. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, saṃkilesikadhammaṃ…pe…. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

38."All, bhikkhus, is subject to defilement…pe…. Sixth.

39. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, khayadhammaṃ…pe…. Sattamaṃ.

39."All, bhikkhus, is subject to decay…pe…. Seventh.

40. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, vayadhammaṃ…pe…. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

40."All, bhikkhus, is subject to vanishing…pe…. Eighth.

41. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, samudayadhammaṃ…pe…. Navamaṃ.

41."All, bhikkhus, is subject to arising…pe…. Ninth.

42. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, nirodhadhammaṃ…pe…. Dasamaṃ.

42."All, bhikkhus, is subject to cessation…pe…. Tenth.

Jātidhammavaggo catuttho.

The Fourth Chapter on Things Subject to Birth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Jātijarābyādhimaraṇaṃ, soko ca saṃkilesikaṃ;Khayavayasamudayaṃ, nirodhadhammena te dasāti.

Birth, aging, sickness, death, sorrow, and defilement; decay, passing away, arising, and cessation-nature: these are the ten.

5. Sabbaaniccavaggo

5.The Chapter on 'All is Impermanent'

## 1-9. Aniccādisuttanavakaṃ

### 1-9. The group of nine suttas on impermanence and so on.

43. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. Tatra kho…pe… ‘‘sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ. Kiñca, bhikkhave, sabbaṃ aniccaṃ? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, aniccaṃ, rūpā aniccā, cakkhuviññāṇaṃ aniccaṃ, cakkhusamphasso anicco. Yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi aniccaṃ…pe… jivhā aniccā, rasā aniccā, jivhāviññāṇaṃ aniccaṃ, jivhāsamphasso anicco. Yampidaṃ jivhāsamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi aniccaṃ. Kāyo anicco…pe… mano anicco, dhammā aniccā, manoviññāṇaṃ aniccaṃ, manosamphasso anicco. Yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tampi aniccaṃ. Evaṃ passaṃ, bhikkhave, sutavā ariyasāvako cakkhusmimpi nibbindati, rūpesupi nibbindati, cakkhuviññāṇepi nibbindati, cakkhusamphassepi nibbindati. Yampidaṃ cakkhusamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tasmimpi nibbindati…pe… manasmimpi nibbindati, dhammesupi nibbindati, manoviññāṇepi nibbindati, manosamphassepi nibbindati, yampidaṃ manosamphassapaccayā uppajjati vedayitaṃ sukhaṃ vā dukkhaṃ vā adukkhamasukhaṃ vā tasmimpi nibbindati. Nibbindaṃ virajjati; virāgā vimuccati; vimuttasmiṃ vimuttamiti ñāṇaṃ hoti. ‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānātī’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

43.At Sāvatthī. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “bhikkhus, all is impermanent. And what, bhikkhus, is all impermanent? The eye, bhikkhus, is impermanent, forms are impermanent, eye-consciousness is impermanent, eye-contact is impermanent. Whatever feeling arises dependent on eye-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too is impermanent… The tongue is impermanent, tastes are impermanent, tongue-consciousness is impermanent, tongue-contact is impermanent. Whatever feeling arises dependent on tongue-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too is impermanent. The body is impermanent… The mind is impermanent, mental objects are impermanent, mind-consciousness is impermanent, mind-contact is impermanent. Whatever feeling arises dependent on mind-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—that too is impermanent. Seeing thus, bhikkhus, the learned noble disciple becomes disenchanted with the eye, disenchanted with forms, disenchanted with eye-consciousness, disenchanted with eye-contact. Whatever feeling arises dependent on eye-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—he becomes disenchanted with that too… He becomes disenchanted with the mind, disenchanted with mental objects, disenchanted with mind-consciousness, disenchanted with mind-contact. Whatever feeling arises dependent on mind-contact—pleasant, painful, or neither-painful-nor-pleasant—he becomes disenchanted with that too. Disenchanted, he becomes dispassionate; through dispassion, he is freed. When freed, there is the knowledge: ‘Freed.’ He understands: ‘Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more beyond this present state.’ The first.

44. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ…pe…. Dutiyaṃ.

44.“bhikkhus, all is suffering…” The second.

45. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, anattā…pe…. Tatiyaṃ.

45.“bhikkhus, all is not-self…” The third.

46. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, abhiññeyyaṃ…pe…. Catutthaṃ.

46.“bhikkhus, all is to be fully understood…” The fourth.

47. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, pariññeyyaṃ…pe…. Pañcamaṃ.

47.“bhikkhus, all is to be fully understood…” The fifth.

48. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, pahātabbaṃ…pe…. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

48.“bhikkhus, all is to be abandoned…” The sixth.

49. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, sacchikātabbaṃ…pe…. Sattamaṃ.

49.“bhikkhus, all is to be realized…” The seventh.

50. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, abhiññāpariññeyyaṃ…pe…. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

50.“bhikkhus, all is to be fully comprehended by direct knowledge…” The eighth.

51. ‘‘Sabbaṃ, bhikkhave, upaddutaṃ…pe…. Navamaṃ.

51.“bhikkhus, all is afflicted…” The ninth.
