# SN 35.75 Adantaaguttasuttaṃ

## SN 35.75 The Untamed and Unguarded Discourse

94. Sāvatthinidānaṃ. ‘‘Chayime, bhikkhave, phassāyatanā adantā aguttā arakkhitā asaṃvutā dukkhādhivāhā honti. Katame cha? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, phassāyatanaṃ adantaṃ aguttaṃ arakkhitaṃ asaṃvutaṃ dukkhādhivāhaṃ hoti…pe… jivhā, bhikkhave, phassāyatanaṃ adantaṃ aguttaṃ arakkhitaṃ asaṃvutaṃ dukkhādhivāhaṃ hoti…pe… mano, bhikkhave, phassāyatanaṃ adantaṃ aguttaṃ arakkhitaṃ asaṃvutaṃ dukkhādhivāhaṃ hoti. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cha phassāyatanā adantā aguttā arakkhitā asaṃvutā dukkhādhivāhā honti’’.

94.At Savatthi. “bhikkhus, these six sense fields, when untamed, unguarded, unprotected, unrestrained, lead to suffering. Which six? The eye, bhikkhus, is a sense field that, when untamed, unguarded, unprotected, unrestrained, leads to suffering… The tongue, bhikkhus, is a sense field that, when untamed, unguarded, unprotected, unrestrained, leads to suffering… The mind, bhikkhus, is a sense field that, when untamed, unguarded, unprotected, unrestrained, leads to suffering. These, bhikkhus, are the six sense fields that, when untamed, unguarded, unprotected, unrestrained, lead to suffering.”

‘‘Chayime, bhikkhave, phassāyatanā sudantā suguttā surakkhitā susaṃvutā sukhādhivāhā honti. Katame cha? Cakkhu, bhikkhave, phassāyatanaṃ sudantaṃ suguttaṃ surakkhitaṃ susaṃvutaṃ sukhādhivāhaṃ hoti…pe… jivhā, bhikkhave, phassāyatanaṃ sudantaṃ suguttaṃ surakkhitaṃ susaṃvutaṃ sukhādhivāhaṃ hoti…pe… mano, bhikkhave, phassāyatanaṃ sudantaṃ suguttaṃ surakkhitaṃ susaṃvutaṃ sukhādhivāhaṃ hoti. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cha phassāyatanā sudantā suguttā surakkhitā susaṃvutā sukhādhivāhā hontī’’ti. Idamavoca bhagavā…pe… etadavoca satthā –

“bhikkhus, these six sense fields, when well-tamed, well-guarded, well-protected, well-restrained, are a source of happiness. Which six? The eye, bhikkhus, as a sense field, when well-tamed, well-guarded, well-protected, well-restrained, is a source of happiness... The tongue, bhikkhus, as a sense field, when well-tamed, well-guarded, well-protected, well-restrained, is a source of happiness... The mind, bhikkhus, as a sense field, when well-tamed, well-guarded, well-protected, well-restrained, is a source of happiness. These, bhikkhus, are the six sense fields that, when well-tamed, well-guarded, well-protected, well-restrained, are a source of happiness.” This is what the Blessed One said... the Teacher said further:

‘‘Saḷeva [chaḷeva (ka.)] phassāyatanāni bhikkhavo,Asaṃvuto yattha dukkhaṃ nigacchati;Tesañca ye saṃvaraṇaṃ avedisuṃ,Saddhādutiyā viharantānavassutā.

bhikkhus, it is in the six sense bases that the unrestrained one experiences suffering; but those who have understood their restraint, living with faith as their companion, are without outflows.

‘‘Disvāna rūpāni manoramāni,Athopi disvāna amanoramāni;Manorame rāgapathaṃ vinodaye,Na cāppiyaṃ meti manaṃ padosaye.

“Having seen delightful forms, and also having seen unpleasant forms, one should dispel the path of passion for the delightful, and not corrupt the mind with aversion towards the unappealing.”

‘‘Saddañca sutvā dubhayaṃ piyāppiyaṃ,Piyamhi sadde na samucchito siyā;Athoppiye dosagataṃ vinodaye,Na cāppiyaṃ meti manaṃ padosaye.

“Having heard sounds, both pleasant and unpleasant, one should not be elated by the pleasant sound; and upon the unpleasant, one should dispel aversion, nor let the mind be angered.”

‘‘Gandhañca ghatvā surabhiṃ manoramaṃ,Athopi ghatvā asuciṃ akantiyaṃ;Akantiyasmiṃ paṭighaṃ vinodaye,Chandānunīto na ca kantiye siyā.

“Having smelled a fragrant and delightful scent, and also a foul and disagreeable one, one should dispel aversion towards the disagreeable, and not be led by desire towards the delightful.”

‘‘Rasañca bhotvāna asāditañca sāduṃ,Athopi bhotvāna asādumekadā;Sāduṃ rasaṃ nājjhosāya bhuñje,Virodhamāsādusu nopadaṃsaye.

“Having enjoyed a delicious flavor, and also an unpalatable one, one should not be addicted to the delicious flavor; one should not resent encountering the unpalatable.”

‘‘Phassena phuṭṭho na sukhena majje [majjhe (syā. kaṃ. pī.)],Dukkhena phuṭṭhopi na sampavedhe;Phassadvayaṃ sukhadukkhe upekkhe,Anānuruddho aviruddha kenaci.

“Touched by contact, one is not intoxicated by pleasure; touched by pain, one is not agitated. One is equanimous toward pleasure and pain, unallied with, opposed to, none.”

‘‘Papañcasaññā itarītarā narā,Papañcayantā upayanti saññino;Manomayaṃ gehasitañca sabbaṃ,Panujja nekkhammasitaṃ irīyati.

“Ordinary people, with proliferating perceptions, perceiving, they proliferate and go on; having dispelled all that is mind-made and home-based, one moves based on renunciation.”

‘‘Evaṃ mano chassu yadā subhāvito,Phuṭṭhassa cittaṃ na vikampate kvaci;Te rāgadose abhibhuyya bhikkhavo,Bhavattha [bhavatha (sī. syā. kaṃ.)] jātimaraṇassa pāragā’’ti. paṭhamaṃ;

“When the mind is thus well developed in the six, the mind does not waver when touched anywhere. Overcoming lust and hatred, bhikkhus, become those who have gone beyond birth and death.”
