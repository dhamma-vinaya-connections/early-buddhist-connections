# SN 35.79 Saṃvarasuttaṃ

## SN 35.79 The Discourse on Restraint

98. ‘‘Saṃvarañca vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi, asaṃvarañca. Taṃ suṇātha. Kathañca, bhikkhave, asaṃvaro hoti? Santi, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati, veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Parihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti…pe… santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā…pe… santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu abhinandati abhivadati ajjhosāya tiṭṭhati, veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Parihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, asaṃvaro hoti.

98.“bhikkhus, I shall teach you restraint and non-restraint. Listen carefully. And how, bhikkhus, is there non-restraint? There are, bhikkhus, forms cognizable by the eye that are desirable, agreeable, pleasant, lovely, connected with sensual desire, and arousing passion. If a bhikkhu delights in them, welcomes them, and remains holding to them, then, bhikkhus, he should understand: ‘I am declining in wholesome qualities.’ This is called decline by the Blessed One. [...] There are, bhikkhus, flavors cognizable by the tongue. [...] There are, bhikkhus, mental phenomena cognizable by the mind that are desirable, agreeable, pleasant, lovely, connected with sensual desire, and arousing passion. If a bhikkhu delights in them, welcomes them, and remains holding to them, then, bhikkhus, he should understand: ‘I am declining in wholesome qualities.’ This is called decline by the Blessed One. It is in this way, bhikkhus, that there is non-restraint.”

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, saṃvaro hoti? Santi, bhikkhave, cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati, veditabbametaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā – ‘na parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Aparihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti …pe… santi, bhikkhave, jivhāviññeyyā rasā…pe… santi, bhikkhave, manoviññeyyā dhammā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Tañce bhikkhu nābhinandati nābhivadati nājjhosāya tiṭṭhati, veditabbametaṃ bhikkhunā – ‘na parihāyāmi kusalehi dhammehi’. Aparihānañhetaṃ vuttaṃ bhagavatāti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, saṃvaro hotī’’ti. Pañcamaṃ.

“And how, bhikkhus, is there restraint? There are, bhikkhus, forms cognizable by the eye that are desirable, agreeable, pleasant, lovely, connected with sensual desire, and arousing passion. If a bhikkhu does not delight in them, does not welcome them, and does not remain holding to them, then, bhikkhus, he should understand: ‘I am not declining in wholesome qualities.’ This is called non-decline by the Blessed One. [...] There are, bhikkhus, flavors cognizable by the tongue. [...] There are, bhikkhus, mental phenomena cognizable by the mind that are desirable, agreeable, pleasant, lovely, connected with sensual desire, and arousing passion. If a bhikkhu does not delight in them, does not welcome them, and does not remain holding to them, then, bhikkhus, he should understand: ‘I am not declining in wholesome qualities.’ This is called non-decline by the Blessed One. It is in this way, bhikkhus, that there is restraint.” The fifth.
