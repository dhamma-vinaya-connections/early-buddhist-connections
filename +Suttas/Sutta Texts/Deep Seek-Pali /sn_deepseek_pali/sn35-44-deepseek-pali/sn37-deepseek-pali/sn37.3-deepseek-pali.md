# SN 37.3 Āveṇikadukkhasuttaṃ

## SN 37.3 The Discourse on Peculiar Sufferings

282. ‘‘Pañcimāni, bhikkhave, mātugāmassa āveṇikāni dukkhāni, yāni mātugāmo paccanubhoti, aññatreva purisehi. Katamāni pañca? Idha, bhikkhave, mātugāmo daharova samāno patikulaṃ gacchati, ñātakehi vinā hoti. Idaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmassa paṭhamaṃ āveṇikaṃ dukkhaṃ, yaṃ mātugāmo paccanubhoti, aññatreva purisehi. Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmo utunī hoti. Idaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmassa dutiyaṃ āveṇikaṃ dukkhaṃ, yaṃ mātugāmo paccanubhoti, aññatreva purisehi. Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmo gabbhinī hoti. Idaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmassa tatiyaṃ āveṇikaṃ dukkhaṃ, yaṃ mātugāmo paccanubhoti, aññatreva purisehi. Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmo vijāyati. Idaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmassa catutthaṃ āveṇikaṃ dukkhaṃ, yaṃ mātugāmo paccanubhoti, aññatreva purisehi. Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, mātugāmo purisassa pāricariyaṃ upeti. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, mātugāmassa pañcamaṃ āveṇikaṃ dukkhaṃ, yaṃ mātugāmo paccanubhoti, aññatreva purisehi. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, pañca mātugāmassa āveṇikāni dukkhāni, yāni mātugāmo paccanubhoti, aññatreva purisehī’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

282."bhikkhus, there are these five peculiar sufferings for women, which women experience, unlike men. What are the five? Here, bhikkhus, a woman, while still young, goes to her husband's family and is separated from her relatives. This, bhikkhus, is the first peculiar suffering for a woman, which she experiences, unlike men. Furthermore, bhikkhus, a woman experiences menstruation. This, bhikkhus, is the second peculiar suffering for a woman, which she experiences, unlike men. Furthermore, bhikkhus, a woman becomes pregnant. This, bhikkhus, is the third peculiar suffering for a woman, which she experiences, unlike men. Furthermore, bhikkhus, a woman gives birth. This, bhikkhus, is the fourth peculiar suffering for a woman, which she experiences, unlike men. Furthermore, bhikkhus, a woman attends upon a man. This, bhikkhus, is the fifth peculiar suffering for a woman, which she experiences, unlike men. These, bhikkhus, are the five peculiar sufferings for women, which women experience, unlike men." The third.
