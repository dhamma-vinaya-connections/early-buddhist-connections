308."Bhikkhus, there are these five powers of a woman. Which five? The power of beauty, the power of wealth, the power of relatives, the power of children, and the power of virtue. When a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with the power of beauty but not with the power of wealth, thus she is incomplete in that factor. But when a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with both the power of beauty and the power of wealth, thus she is complete in that factor. When a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with the power of beauty and the power of wealth but not with the power of relatives, thus she is incomplete in that factor. But when a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with the power of beauty, the power of wealth, and the power of relatives, thus she is complete in that factor. When a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with the power of beauty, the power of wealth, and the power of relatives but not with the power of children, thus she is incomplete in that factor. But when a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with the power of beauty, the power of wealth, the power of relatives, and the power of children, thus she is complete in that factor. When a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with the power of beauty, the power of wealth, the power of relatives, and the power of children but not with the power of virtue, thus she is incomplete in that factor. But when a woman, bhikkhus, is endowed with the power of beauty, the power of wealth, the power of relatives, the power of children, and the power of virtue, thus she is complete in that factor. These, bhikkhus, are the five powers of a woman." Fifth.
