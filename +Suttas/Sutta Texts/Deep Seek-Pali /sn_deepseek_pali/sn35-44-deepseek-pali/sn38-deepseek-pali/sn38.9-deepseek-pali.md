# SN 38.9 Avijjāpañhāsuttaṃ

## SN 38.9 The Question on Ignorance

322. ‘‘‘Avijjā, avijjā’ti, āvuso sāriputta, vuccati. Katamā nu kho, āvuso, avijjā’’ti? ‘‘Yaṃ kho, āvuso, dukkhe aññāṇaṃ, dukkhasamudaye aññāṇaṃ, dukkhanirodhe aññāṇaṃ, dukkhanirodhagāminiyā paṭipadāya aññāṇaṃ – ayaṃ vuccatāvuso, avijjā’’ti. ‘‘Atthi panāvuso, maggo atthi paṭipadā, etissā avijjāya pahānāyā’’ti? ‘‘Atthi kho, āvuso, maggo atthi paṭipadā, etissā avijjāya pahānāyā’’ti. ‘‘Katamo panāvuso, maggo katamā paṭipadā, etissā avijjāya pahānāyā’’ti? ‘‘Ayameva kho, āvuso, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, etissā avijjāya pahānāya, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā sammākammanto sammāājīvo sammāvāyāmo sammāsati sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ kho, āvuso, maggo ayaṃ paṭipadā, etissā avijjāya pahānāyā’’ti. ‘‘Bhaddako, āvuso, maggo bhaddikā paṭipadā, etissā avijjāya pahānāya. Alañca panāvuso sāriputta, appamādāyā’’ti. Navamaṃ.

322.“‘Ignorance, ignorance,’ friend Sāriputta, it is said. But what, friend, is ignorance?” “The not knowing of suffering, friend, the not knowing of the origin of suffering, the not knowing of the cessation of suffering, the not knowing of the path leading to the cessation of suffering—this, friend, is called ignorance.” “But is there, friend, a path, is there a practice for the abandoning of this ignorance?” “There is, friend, a path, there is a practice for the abandoning of this ignorance.” “And what, friend, is that path, what is that practice for the abandoning of this ignorance?” “It is just this Noble Eightfold Path, friend, for the abandoning of this ignorance, namely: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samādhi. This, friend, is the path, this is the practice for the abandoning of this ignorance.” “Excellent, friend, is the path, excellent is the practice for the abandoning of this ignorance. And it is quite sufficient, friend Sāriputta, for heedfulness.” The ninth.
