# SN 38.3 Dhammavādīpañhāsuttaṃ

## SN 38.3 The Discourse on the Question of Speakers of the Dhamma

316. ‘‘Ke nu kho, āvuso sāriputta, loke dhammavādino, ke loke suppaṭipannā, ke loke sugatā’’ti? ‘‘Ye kho, āvuso, rāgappahānāya dhammaṃ desenti, dosappahānāya dhammaṃ desenti, mohappahānāya dhammaṃ desenti, te loke dhammavādino. Ye kho, āvuso, rāgassa pahānāya paṭipannā, dosassa pahānāya paṭipannā, mohassa pahānāya paṭipannā, te loke suppaṭipannā. Yesaṃ kho, āvuso, rāgo pahīno ucchinnamūlo tālāvatthukato anabhāvaṅkato āyatiṃ anuppādadhammo, doso pahīno ucchinnamūlo tālāvatthukato anabhāvaṅkato āyatiṃ anuppādadhammo, moho pahīno ucchinnamūlo tālāvatthukato anabhāvaṅkato āyatiṃ anuppādadhammo, te loke sugatā’’ti.

316."Friend Sāriputta, who in the world are the speakers of the Dhamma, who are those practicing well, who are the well-gone?" "Friend, those who teach the Dhamma for the abandoning of lust, for the abandoning of hate, for the abandoning of delusion—they are the speakers of the Dhamma in the world. Those who practice for the abandoning of lust, for the abandoning of hate, for the abandoning of delusion—they are those practicing well in the world. Those whose lust is abandoned, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, so that it is no more subject to future arising; whose hate is abandoned, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, so that it is no more subject to future arising; whose delusion is abandoned, cut off at the root, made like a palm stump, obliterated, so that it is no more subject to future arising—they are the well-gone in the world."

‘‘Atthi panāvuso, maggo atthi paṭipadā etassa rāgassa dosassa mohassa pahānāyā’’ti? ‘‘Atthi kho, āvuso, maggo atthi paṭipadā etassa rāgassa dosassa mohassa pahānāyā’’ti. ‘‘Katamo, panāvuso, maggo katamā paṭipadā etassa rāgassa dosassa mohassa pahānāyā’’ti? ‘‘Ayameva kho, āvuso, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo etassa rāgassa dosassa mohassa pahānāya, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā sammākammanto sammāājīvo sammāvāyāmo sammāsati sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ kho, āvuso, maggo ayaṃ paṭipadā etassa rāgassa dosassa mohassa pahānāyā’’ti. ‘‘Bhaddako, āvuso, maggo bhaddikā paṭipadā, etassa rāgassa dosassa mohassa pahānāya. Alañca panāvuso sāriputta, appamādāyā’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

"Is there, friend, a path, a practice for the abandoning of this greed, hatred, and delusion?" "There is indeed, friend, a path, a practice for the abandoning of this greed, hatred, and delusion." "And what, friend, is that path, what is that practice for the abandoning of this greed, hatred, and delusion?" "It is just this Noble Eightfold Path, friend, for the abandoning of this greed, hatred, and delusion—that is, right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samādhi. This, friend, is the path, this is the practice for the abandoning of this greed, hatred, and delusion." "Excellent, friend, is the path, excellent is the practice for the abandoning of this greed, hatred, and delusion. And it is enough, friend Sāriputta, for heedfulness." Third.
