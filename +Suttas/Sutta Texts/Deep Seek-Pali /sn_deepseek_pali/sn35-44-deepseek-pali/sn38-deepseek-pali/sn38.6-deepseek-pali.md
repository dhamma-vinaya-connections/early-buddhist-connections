# SN 38.6 Paramassāsappattasuttaṃ

## SN 38.6 The Discourse on One Who Has Attained Supreme Comfort

319. ‘‘‘Paramassāsappatto, paramassāsappatto’ti, āvuso sāriputta, vuccati. Kittāvatā nu kho, āvuso, paramassāsappatto hotī’’ti? ‘‘Yato kho, āvuso, bhikkhu channaṃ phassāyatanānaṃ samudayañca atthaṅgamañca assādañca ādīnavañca nissaraṇañca yathābhūtaṃ viditvā anupādāvimutto hoti, ettāvatā kho, āvuso, paramassāsappatto hotī’’ti. ‘‘Atthi panāvuso, maggo atthi paṭipadā, etassa paramassāsassa sacchikiriyāyā’’ti? ‘‘Atthi kho, āvuso, maggo atthi paṭipadā, etassa paramassāsassa sacchikiriyāyā’’ti. ‘‘Katamo pana, āvuso, maggo katamā paṭipadā, etassa paramassāsassa sacchikiriyāyā’’ti? ‘‘Ayameva kho, āvuso, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo etassa paramassāsassa sacchikiriyāya, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā sammākammanto sammāājīvo sammāvāyāmo sammāsati sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ kho, āvuso, maggo ayaṃ paṭipadā, etassa paramassāsassa sacchikiriyāyā’’ti. ‘‘Bhaddako, āvuso, maggo bhaddikā paṭipadā, etassa paramassāsassa sacchikiriyāya. Alañca panāvuso sāriputta, appamādāyā’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

319.“‘One who has attained supreme comfort, one who has attained supreme comfort,’ friend Sāriputta, is said. To what extent, friend, does one attain supreme comfort?” “When, friend, a bhikkhu has understood as it really is the origin and the disappearance, the gratification, the danger, and the escape regarding the six sense bases, and is liberated through non-clinging, to that extent, friend, one attains supreme comfort.” “But, friend, is there a path, is there a practice for the realization of this supreme comfort?” “There is, friend, a path, there is a practice for the realization of this supreme comfort.” “What, friend, is the path, what is the practice for the realization of this supreme comfort?” “This very ![[Noble Path]]. This, friend, is the path, this is the practice for the realization of this supreme comfort.” “Excellent, friend, is the path, excellent is the practice for the realization of this supreme comfort. And it is enough, friend Sāriputta, for diligence.” The sixth.
