# SN 38.14 Dukkhapañhāsuttaṃ

## SN 38.14 The Discourse on Questions about Suffering

327. ‘‘‘Dukkhaṃ, dukkha’nti, āvuso sāriputta, vuccati. Katamaṃ nu kho, āvuso, dukkha’’nti? ‘‘Tisso imā, āvuso, dukkhatā. Dukkhadukkhatā, saṅkhāradukkhatā, vipariṇāmadukkhatā – imā kho, āvuso, tisso dukkhatā’’ti. ‘‘Atthi panāvuso maggo atthi paṭipadā, etāsaṃ dukkhatānaṃ pariññāyā’’ti? ‘‘Atthi kho, āvuso, maggo atthi paṭipadā, etāsaṃ dukkhatānaṃ pariññāyā’’ti. ‘‘Katamo panāvuso, maggo katamā paṭipadā, etāsaṃ dukkhatānaṃ pariññāyā’’ti? ‘‘Ayameva kho, āvuso, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, etāsaṃ dukkhatānaṃ pariññāya, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā sammākammanto sammāājīvo sammāvāyāmo sammāsati sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ kho, āvuso, maggo ayaṃ paṭipadā, etāsaṃ dukkhatānaṃ pariññāyā’’ti. ‘‘Bhaddako, āvuso, maggo bhaddikā paṭipadā, etāsaṃ dukkhatānaṃ pariññāya. Alañca panāvuso sāriputta, appamādāyā’’ti. Cuddasamaṃ.

327.'Suffering, suffering,' friend Sāriputta, it is said. What now, friend, is suffering?" "These are the three kinds of suffering, friend: the suffering of suffering, the suffering of formations, and the suffering of change. These, friend, are the three kinds of suffering." "Is there, friend, a path, is there a practice for the full understanding of these sufferings?" "There is, friend, a path, a practice for the full understanding of these sufferings." "What now, friend, is the path, what is the practice for the full understanding of these sufferings?" "This ![[Noble Path]]. This, friend, is the path, this is the practice for the full understanding of these sufferings." "Excellent, friend, is the path, excellent is the practice for the full understanding of these sufferings. And it is quite enough, friend Sāriputta, for heedfulness." The fourteenth.
