325.'Clinging, clinging,' friend SÄriputta, it is said. What now, friend, is clinging?" "These four are clingings, friend: clinging to sensual pleasures, clinging to views, clinging to rules and observances, clinging to a doctrine of self. These, friend, are the four clingings." "Is there, friend, a path, is there a practice, for the abandoning of these clingings?" "There is, friend, a path, there is a practice, for the abandoning of these clingings." "What now, friend, is the path, what is the practice, for the abandoning of these clingings?" "This Noble Eightfold Path, friend, is the path, is the practice, for the abandoning of these clingings, that is: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration. This, friend, is the path, this is the practice, for the abandoning of these clingings." "Excellent, friend, is the path, excellent is the practice, for the abandoning of these clingings. And it is quite enough, friend SÄriputta, for heedfulness." The twelfth.
