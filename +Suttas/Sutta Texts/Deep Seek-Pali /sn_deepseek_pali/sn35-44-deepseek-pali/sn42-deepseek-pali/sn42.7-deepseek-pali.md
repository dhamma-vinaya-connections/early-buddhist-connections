# SN 42.7 Khettūpamasuttaṃ

## SN 42.7 The Simile of the Field

359. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā nāḷandāyaṃ viharati pāvārikambavane. Atha kho asibandhakaputto gāmaṇi yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho asibandhakaputto gāmaṇi bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘nanu, bhante, bhagavā sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharatī’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, gāmaṇi, tathāgato sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharatī’’ti. ‘‘Atha kiñcarahi, bhante, bhagavā ekaccānaṃ sakkaccaṃ dhammaṃ deseti, ekaccānaṃ no tathā sakkaccaṃ dhammaṃ desetī’’ti? ‘‘Tena hi, gāmaṇi, taññevettha paṭipucchissāmi. Yathā te khameyya tathā naṃ byākareyyāsi. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, idhassu [idha (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] kassakassa gahapatino tīṇi khettāni – ekaṃ khettaṃ aggaṃ, ekaṃ khettaṃ majjhimaṃ, ekaṃ khettaṃ hīnaṃ jaṅgalaṃ ūsaraṃ pāpabhūmi. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, asu kassako gahapati bījāni patiṭṭhāpetukāmo kattha paṭhamaṃ patiṭṭhāpeyya, yaṃ vā aduṃ khettaṃ aggaṃ, yaṃ vā aduṃ khettaṃ majjhimaṃ, yaṃ vā aduṃ khettaṃ hīnaṃ jaṅgalaṃ ūsaraṃ pāpabhūmī’’ti? ‘‘Asu, bhante, kassako gahapati bījāni patiṭṭhāpetukāmo yaṃ aduṃ khettaṃ aggaṃ tattha patiṭṭhāpeyya. Tattha patiṭṭhāpetvā yaṃ aduṃ khettaṃ majjhimaṃ tattha patiṭṭhāpeyya. Tattha patiṭṭhāpetvā yaṃ aduṃ khettaṃ hīnaṃ jaṅgalaṃ ūsaraṃ pāpabhūmi tattha patiṭṭhāpeyyapi, nopi patiṭṭhāpeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Antamaso gobhattampi bhavissatī’’ti.

359.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Nāḷandā in Pāvārika's mango grove. Then the village headman Asibandhaka's son approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the village headman Asibandhaka's son said to the Blessed One: "Isn't it true, venerable sir, that the Blessed One dwells compassionate for the welfare of all living beings?" "Yes, headman, the Tathāgata dwells compassionate for the welfare of all living beings." "But then, venerable sir, why does the Blessed One teach the Dhamma carefully to some but not so carefully to others?" "Well then, headman, I will question you in return. Answer as you see fit. What do you think, headman? Suppose a farmer householder has three fields—one excellent, one middling, and one inferior, barren, saline, and poor. What do you think, headman? If that farmer householder wants to sow seeds, where would he first sow them—in the excellent field, the middling field, or the inferior, barren, saline, poor field?" "That farmer householder, venerable sir, wanting to sow seeds, would first sow them in the excellent field. Having sown there, he would then sow in the middling field. Having sown there, he might or might not sow in the inferior, barren, saline, poor field. Why is that? At least it could serve as cattle fodder."

‘‘Seyyathāpi, gāmaṇi, yaṃ aduṃ khettaṃ aggaṃ; evameva mayhaṃ bhikkhubhikkhuniyo. Tesāhaṃ dhammaṃ desemi – ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ, sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsemi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Ete hi, gāmaṇi, maṃdīpā maṃleṇā maṃtāṇā maṃsaraṇā viharanti. Seyyathāpi, gāmaṇi, yaṃ aduṃ khettaṃ majjhimaṃ; evameva mayhaṃ upāsakaupāsikāyo. Tesaṃ pāhaṃ dhammaṃ desemi – ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ, sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsemi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Ete hi, gāmaṇi, maṃdīpā maṃleṇā maṃtāṇā maṃsaraṇā viharanti. Seyyathāpi, gāmaṇi, yaṃ aduṃ khettaṃ hīnaṃ jaṅgalaṃ ūsaraṃ pāpabhūmi; evameva mayhaṃ aññatitthiyā samaṇabrāhmaṇaparibbājakā. Tesaṃ pāhaṃ dhammaṃ desemi – ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsemi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāma ekaṃ padampi ājāneyyuṃ taṃ nesaṃ assa dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti.

"Just as, headman, this field is the best, so too are my bhikkhus and nuns. To them I teach the Dhamma—good in the beginning, good in the middle, good in the end, with its true meaning and wording. I reveal the spiritual life that is utterly perfect and pure. Why is that? Because they live with me as their island, their cave, their protection, and their refuge.Just as, headman, this field is middling, so too are my male and female lay followers. To them I teach the Dhamma—good in the beginning, good in the middle, good in the end, with its true meaning and wording. I reveal the spiritual life that is utterly perfect and pure. Why is that? Because they live with me as their island, their cave, their protection, and their refuge.Just as, headman, this field is inferior—barren, saline, and poor soil—so too are the ascetics, brahmins, and wanderers of other sects. To them too I teach the Dhamma—good in the beginning, good in the middle, good in the end, with its true meaning and wording. I reveal the spiritual life that is utterly perfect and pure. Why is that? Just in case they might understand even one word, and that would be for their lasting welfare and happiness."

‘‘Seyyathāpi, gāmaṇi, purisassa tayo udakamaṇikā – eko udakamaṇiko acchiddo ahārī aparihārī, eko udakamaṇiko acchiddo hārī parihārī, eko udakamaṇiko chiddo hārī parihārī. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, asu puriso udakaṃ nikkhipitukāmo kattha paṭhamaṃ nikkhipeyya, yo vā so udakamaṇiko acchiddo ahārī aparihārī, yo vā so udakamaṇiko acchiddo hārī parihārī, yo vā so udakamaṇiko chiddo hārī parihārī’’ti? ‘‘Asu, bhante, puriso udakaṃ nikkhipitukāmo, yo so udakamaṇiko acchiddo ahārī aparihārī tattha nikkhipeyya, tattha nikkhipitvā, yo so udakamaṇiko acchiddo hārī parihārī tattha nikkhipeyya, tattha nikkhipitvā, yo so udakamaṇiko chiddo hārī parihārī tattha nikkhipeyyapi, nopi nikkhipeyya. Taṃ kissa hetu? Antamaso bhaṇḍadhovanampi bhavissatī’’ti.

"Just as, village headman, a man has three water pots: one water pot is unbroken, unperforated, and unspoiled; one water pot is unbroken, but spoiled and leaking; and one water pot is broken, spoiled, and leaking. What do you think, village headman? If this man wanted to put water, where would he put it first: in the water pot that is unbroken, unperforated, and unspoiled; or in the water pot that is unbroken, but spoiled and leaking; or in the water pot that is broken, spoiled, and leaking?" "Sir, if that man wanted to put water, he would put it first in the water pot that is unbroken, unperforated, and unspoiled. Having put it there, he would then put it in the water pot that is unbroken, but spoiled and leaking. Having put it there, he might or might not put it in the water pot that is broken, spoiled, and leaking. Why is that? At the very least, it would be useful for washing things."

‘‘Seyyathāpi, gāmaṇi, yo so udakamaṇiko acchiddo ahārī aparihārī; evameva mayhaṃ bhikkhubhikkhuniyo. Tesāhaṃ dhammaṃ desemi – ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsemi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Ete hi, gāmaṇi, maṃdīpā maṃleṇā maṃtāṇā maṃsaraṇā viharanti. Seyyathāpi, gāmaṇi, yo so udakamaṇiko acchiddo hārī parihārī; evameva mayhaṃ upāsakaupāsikāyo. Tesāhaṃ dhammaṃ desemi – ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsemi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Ete hi, gāmaṇi, maṃdīpā maṃleṇā maṃtāṇā maṃsaraṇā viharanti. Seyyathāpi, gāmaṇi, yo so udakamaṇiko chiddo hārī parihārī; evameva mayhaṃ aññatitthiyā samaṇabrāhmaṇaparibbājakā. Tesāhaṃ dhammaṃ desemi – ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsemi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Appeva nāma ekaṃ padampi ājāneyyuṃ, taṃ nesaṃ assa dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, asibandhakaputto gāmaṇi bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bhante…pe… ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti. Sattamaṃ.

"Just as, village headman, a water pot that is unbroken, unperforated, and unspoiled is fit to hold water, so too are my bhikkhus and nuns. To them I teach the Dhamma—beautiful in the beginning, beautiful in the middle, beautiful in the end—with its meaning and phrasing, I proclaim the holy life that is entirely perfect and pure. Why is that? Because, village headman, they dwell with me as their island, their shelter, their protection, and their refuge.Just as, village headman, a water pot that is unbroken, but spoiled and leaking is fit to hold water, so too are my laymen and laywomen. To them I teach the Dhamma—beautiful in the beginning, beautiful in the middle, beautiful in the end—with its meaning and phrasing, I proclaim the holy life that is entirely perfect and pure. Why is that? Because, village headman, they dwell with me as their island, their shelter, their protection, and their refuge.Just as, village headman, a water pot that is broken, spoiled, and leaking is unfit to hold water, so too are the ascetics, brahmins, and wanderers of other sects. To them I teach the Dhamma—beautiful in the beginning, beautiful in the middle, beautiful in the end—with its meaning and phrasing, I proclaim the holy life that is entirely perfect and pure. Why is that? Perhaps they might understand even a single phrase, and that would be for their long-term welfare and happiness."When this was said, Asibandhakaputta the village headman said to the Blessed One: "Magnificent, Lord!... From today, let the Blessed One remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge for life." The seventh.
