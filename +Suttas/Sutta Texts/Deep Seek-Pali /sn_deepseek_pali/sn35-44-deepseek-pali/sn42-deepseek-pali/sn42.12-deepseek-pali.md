# SN 42.12 Rāsiyasuttaṃ

## SN 42.12 Rāsiya Sutta

364. Atha kho rāsiyo gāmaṇi yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho rāsiyo gāmaṇi bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante, ‘samaṇo gotamo sabbaṃ tapaṃ garahati, sabbaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhajīviṃ ekaṃsena upavadati upakkosatī’ti [upakkosati upavadatīti (dī. ni. 1.381)]. Ye te, bhante, evamāhaṃsu – ‘samaṇo gotamo sabbaṃ tapaṃ garahati, sabbaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhajīviṃ ekaṃsena upavadati upakkosatī’ti, kacci te, bhante, bhagavato vuttavādino, na ca bhagavantaṃ abhūtena abbhācikkhanti, dhammassa cānudhammaṃ byākaronti, na ca koci sahadhammiko vādānuvādo gārayhaṃ ṭhānaṃ āgacchatī’’ti? ‘‘Ye te, gāmaṇi, evamāhaṃsu – ‘samaṇo gotamo sabbaṃ tapaṃ garahati, sabbaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhajīviṃ ekaṃsena upavadati upakkosatī’ti, na me te vuttavādino, abbhācikkhanti ca pana maṃ te asatā tucchā abhūtena’’.

364.Then the village headman Rāsiya approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the village headman Rāsiya said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, I have heard this: ‘The ascetic Gotama disparages all ascetic practices; he entirely censures and reviles every ascetic who lives a rough life.’ Those, venerable sir, who say this—‘The ascetic Gotama disparages all ascetic practices; he entirely censures and reviles every ascetic who lives a rough life’—do they speak in accordance with the Blessed One’s words, and do they not misrepresent the Blessed One with what is false? Do they explain the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, so that no one who speaks in accordance with the Dhamma would have grounds for criticism?” “Headman, those who say this—‘The ascetic Gotama disparages all ascetic practices; he entirely censures and reviles every ascetic who lives a rough life’—they do not speak in accordance with my words, and they misrepresent me with what is false, vain, and untrue.”

‘‘Dveme, gāmaṇi, antā pabbajitena na sevitabbā – yo cāyaṃ kāmesu kāmasukhallikānuyogo hīno gammo pothujjaniko anariyo anatthasaṃhito, yo cāyaṃ attakilamathānuyogo dukkho anariyo anatthasaṃhito. Ete te, gāmaṇi, ubho ante anupagamma majjhimā paṭipadā tathāgatena abhisambuddhā – cakkhukaraṇī ñāṇakaraṇī upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati. Katamā ca sā, gāmaṇi, majjhimā paṭipadā tathāgatena abhisambuddhā – cakkhukaraṇī ñāṇakaraṇī upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo, seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ kho sā, gāmaṇi, majjhimā paṭipadā tathāgatena abhisambuddhā – cakkhukaraṇī ñāṇakaraṇī upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati.

“Village headman, there are these two extremes that one who has gone forth should not pursue: indulgence in sensual pleasures, which is low, vulgar, worldly, ignoble, and unbeneficial; and devotion to self-mortification, which is painful, ignoble, and unbeneficial. Avoiding these two extremes, the Tathāgata has awakened to the Middle Path, which gives rise to vision, knowledge, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, and Nibbāna. And what, village headman, is that Middle Path awakened to by the Tathāgata, which gives rise to vision, knowledge, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, and Nibbāna? It is this Noble Eightfold Path: that is, right view…right samādhi. This, village headman, is the Middle Path awakened to by the Tathāgata, which gives rise to vision, knowledge, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, and Nibbāna.”

‘‘Tayo kho me, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogino santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Idha, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī adhammena bhoge pariyesati, sāhasena adhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasena na attānaṃ sukheti na pīṇeti na saṃvibhajati na puññāni karoti. Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasena. Adhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati na puññāni karoti. Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasena. Adhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti.

“Headman, there are these three kinds of sensualists found existing in the world. Which three? Here, headman, a certain sensualist seeks wealth unlawfully and violently. Having sought wealth unlawfully and violently, he neither makes himself happy and pleased, nor shares, nor performs meritorious deeds. Here, headman, another sensualist seeks wealth unlawfully and violently. Having sought wealth unlawfully and violently, he makes himself happy and pleased, but neither shares nor performs meritorious deeds. Here, headman, yet another sensualist seeks wealth unlawfully and violently. Having sought wealth unlawfully and violently, he makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds.”

‘‘Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenapi asāhasenapi. Dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasenapi asāhasenapi na attānaṃ sukheti, na pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenapi asāhasenapi. Dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasenapi asāhasenapi attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesati, sāhasenapi asāhasenapi. Dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasenapi asāhasenapi attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti.

“Here, headman, a certain sensualist seeks wealth by legitimate and illegitimate means, both violently and non-violently. Having sought wealth by legitimate and illegitimate means, both violently and non-violently, he neither makes himself happy and pleased, nor shares, nor performs meritorious deeds. Here, headman, another sensualist seeks wealth by legitimate and illegitimate means, both violently and non-violently. Having sought wealth by legitimate and illegitimate means, both violently and non-violently, he makes himself happy and pleased, but neither shares nor performs meritorious deeds. Here, headman, yet another sensualist seeks wealth by legitimate and illegitimate means, both violently and non-violently. Having sought wealth by legitimate and illegitimate means, both violently and non-violently, he makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds.”

‘‘Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena. Dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena na attānaṃ sukheti, na pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena. Dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena. Dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti. Te ca bhoge gadhito [gathito (sī.)] mucchito ajjhopanno anādīnavadassāvī anissaraṇapañño paribhuñjati. Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena. Dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti. Te ca bhoge agadhito amucchito anajjhopanno ādīnavadassāvī nissaraṇapañño paribhuñjati.

“Here, headman, a certain sensualist seeks wealth righteously, without violence. Having sought wealth righteously, without violence, he neither makes himself happy and pleased, nor shares, nor performs meritorious deeds. Here, headman, another sensualist seeks wealth righteously, without violence. Having sought wealth righteously, without violence, he makes himself happy and pleased, but neither shares nor performs meritorious deeds. Here, headman, yet another sensualist seeks wealth righteously, without violence. Having sought wealth righteously, without violence, he makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds. But he enjoys that wealth clinging to it, infatuated with it, attached to it, not seeing the danger in it, without wisdom of escape from it. Here, headman, still another sensualist seeks wealth righteously, without violence. Having sought wealth righteously, without violence, he makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds. And he enjoys that wealth without clinging to it, without infatuation, without attachment, seeing the danger in it, with wisdom of escape from it.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasena, adhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasena na attānaṃ sukheti, na pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho. Katamehi tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho? Adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena gārayho. Na attānaṃ sukheti na pīṇetīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karotīti, iminā tatiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī imehi tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho.

“Headman, as to the sensualist who seeks wealth unlawfully and violently, and having sought wealth unlawfully and violently, neither makes himself happy and pleased, nor shares, nor performs meritorious deeds: this sensualist, headman, is blameworthy in three respects. In what three respects is he blameworthy? He seeks wealth unlawfully and violently—in this first respect he is blameworthy. He neither makes himself happy nor pleased—in this second respect he is blameworthy. He neither shares nor performs meritorious deeds—in this third respect he is blameworthy. This sensualist, headman, is blameworthy in these three respects.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasena, adhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho, ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho? Adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena gārayho. Na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karotīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Katamena ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso? Attānaṃ sukheti pīṇetīti, iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī imehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho, iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso.

“Headman, as to the sensualist who seeks wealth unlawfully and violently, and having sought wealth unlawfully and violently, makes himself happy and pleased, but neither shares nor performs meritorious deeds: this sensualist, headman, is blameworthy in two respects and praiseworthy in one respect. In what two respects is he blameworthy? He seeks wealth unlawfully and violently—in this first respect he is blameworthy. He neither shares nor performs meritorious deeds—in this second respect he is blameworthy. In what one respect is he praiseworthy? He makes himself happy and pleased—in this one respect he is praiseworthy. This sensualist, headman, is blameworthy in these two respects and praiseworthy in this one respect.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasena, adhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī ekena ṭhānena gārayho, dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso. Katamena ekena ṭhānena gārayho? Adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenāti, iminā ekena ṭhānena gārayho. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso? Attānaṃ sukheti pīṇetīti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Saṃvibhajati puññāni karotīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī, iminā ekena ṭhānena gārayho, imehi dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso.

“Headman, as to the sensualist who seeks wealth unlawfully and violently, and having sought wealth unlawfully and violently, makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds: this sensualist, headman, is blameworthy in one respect and praiseworthy in two respects. In what one respect is he blameworthy? He seeks wealth unlawfully and violently—in this one respect he is blameworthy. In what two respects is he praiseworthy? He makes himself happy and pleased—in this first respect he is praiseworthy. He shares and performs meritorious deeds—in this second respect he is praiseworthy. This sensualist, headman, is blameworthy in this one respect and praiseworthy in these two respects.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenapi asāhasenapi, dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasenapi asāhasenapi na attānaṃ sukheti, na pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso, tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho. Katamena ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso? Dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasenāti, iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Katamehi tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho? Adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena gārayho. Na attānaṃ sukheti, na pīṇetīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karotīti, iminā tatiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso, imehi tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho.

“Here, headman, a pleasure-seeker who seeks wealth both lawfully and unlawfully, both violently and non-violently, having sought wealth both lawfully and unlawfully, both violently and non-violently, does not make himself happy or pleased, does not share it, and does not perform meritorious deeds. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in one respect and blameworthy in three respects. In what one respect is he praiseworthy? He seeks wealth lawfully and non-violently—this is the one respect he is praiseworthy. In what three respects is he blameworthy? He seeks wealth unlawfully and violently—this is the first respect he is blameworthy. He does not make himself happy or pleased—this is the second respect he is blameworthy. He does not share it and does not perform meritorious deeds—this is the third respect he is blameworthy. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in this one respect and blameworthy in these three respects.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenapi asāhasenapi, dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasenapi asāhasenapi attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso, dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso? Dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Attānaṃ sukheti pīṇetīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho? Adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena gārayho. Na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karotīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī imehi dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso, imehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho.

“Here, headman, a pleasure-seeker who seeks wealth both lawfully and unlawfully, both violently and non-violently, having sought wealth both lawfully and non-violently, makes himself happy and pleased, but does not share it or perform meritorious deeds. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in two respects and blameworthy in two respects. In what two respects is he praiseworthy? He seeks wealth lawfully and non-violently—this is the first respect he is praiseworthy. He makes himself happy and pleased—this is the second respect he is praiseworthy. In what two respects is he blameworthy? He seeks wealth unlawfully and violently—this is the first respect he is blameworthy. He does not share it or perform meritorious deeds—this is the second respect he is blameworthy. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in these two respects and blameworthy in these two respects.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenapi asāhasenapi, dhammādhammena bhoge pariyesitvā sāhasenapi asāhasenapi attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī tīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso, ekena ṭhānena gārayho. Katamehi tīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso? Dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Attānaṃ sukheti pīṇetīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Saṃvibhajati puññāni karotīti, iminā tatiyena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Katamena ekena ṭhānena gārayho? Adhammena bhoge pariyesati sāhasenāti, iminā ekena ṭhānena gārayho. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī imehi tīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso, iminā ekena ṭhānena gārayho.

“Here, headman, a pleasure-seeker who seeks wealth both lawfully and unlawfully, both violently and non-violently, having sought wealth both lawfully and non-violently, makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in three respects and blameworthy in one respect. In what three respects is he praiseworthy? ‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena, dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena, na attānaṃ sukheti, na pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso, dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho. Katamena ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso? Dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasenāti, iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho? Na attānaṃ sukheti, na pīṇetīti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena gārayho. Na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karotīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso, imehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho.

“Here, headman, a pleasure-seeker who seeks wealth lawfully and non-violently, having sought wealth lawfully and non-violently, does not make himself happy or pleased, does not share it, and does not perform meritorious deeds. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in one respect and blameworthy in two respects. In what one respect is he praiseworthy? This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in this one respect and blameworthy in these two respects.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena, dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti, na saṃvibhajati, na puññāni karoti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso, ekena ṭhānena gārayho. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso? Dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. ‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena, dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti, te ca bhoge gadhito mucchito ajjhopanno anādīnavadassāvī anissaraṇapañño paribhuñjati. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī tīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso, ekena ṭhānena gārayho. Katamehi tīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso? Dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. “Here, headman, a pleasure-seeker who seeks wealth lawfully and non-violently, having sought wealth lawfully and non-violently, makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds. But he uses that wealth attached, infatuated, absorbed, without seeing the drawback, and without understanding the escape. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in three respects and blameworthy in one respect. In what three respects is he praiseworthy? He seeks wealth lawfully and non-violently—in this first respect he is praiseworthy. He makes himself happy and pleased—in this second respect he is praiseworthy. He shares and performs meritorious deeds—in this third respect he is praiseworthy. In what one respect is he blameworthy? He uses that wealth attached, infatuated, absorbed, without seeing the drawback, and without understanding the escape—in this one respect he is blameworthy. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in these three respects and blameworthy in this one respect.”

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ kāmabhogī dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasena, dhammena bhoge pariyesitvā asāhasena attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti saṃvibhajati puññāni karoti. Te ca bhoge agadhito amucchito anajjhopanno ādīnavadassāvī nissaraṇapañño paribhuñjati. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī catūhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso. Katamehi catūhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso? Dhammena bhoge pariyesati asāhasenāti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Attānaṃ sukheti pīṇetīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Saṃvibhajati puññāni karotīti, iminā tatiyena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Te ca bhoge agadhito amucchito anajjhopanno ādīnavadassāvī nissaraṇapañño paribhuñjatīti, iminā catutthena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, kāmabhogī imehi catūhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso.

“Here, headman, a pleasure-seeker who seeks wealth lawfully and non-violently, having sought wealth lawfully and non-violently, makes himself happy and pleased, shares, and performs meritorious deeds. And he uses that wealth without being attached, without being infatuated, without being absorbed, seeing its drawbacks, and with the understanding of escape. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in four respects. In what four respects is he praiseworthy? He seeks wealth lawfully and non-violently—this is the first respect he is praiseworthy. He makes himself happy and pleased—this is the second respect he is praiseworthy. He shares and performs meritorious deeds—this is the third respect he is praiseworthy. He uses that wealth without being attached, without being infatuated, without being absorbed, seeing its drawbacks, and with the understanding of escape—this is the fourth respect he is praiseworthy. This pleasure-seeker, headman, is praiseworthy in these four respects.”

‘‘Tayome, gāmaṇi, tapassino lūkhajīvino santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Idha, gāmaṇi, ekacco tapassī lūkhajīvī saddhā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito hoti – ‘appeva nāma kusalaṃ dhammaṃ adhigaccheyyaṃ, appeva nāma uttarimanussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikareyya’nti. So attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpeti, kusalañca dhammaṃ nādhigacchati, uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ na sacchikaroti.

“Headman, there are these three ascetics, living a rough life, who are found in the world. What three? Here, headman, a certain ascetic, living a rough life, has gone forth from the household life into homelessness out of faith, thinking: ‘Perhaps I may attain a wholesome state, perhaps I may realize a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones.’ He heats and afflicts himself, but he does not attain a wholesome state, nor does he realize a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones.”

‘‘Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco tapassī lūkhajīvī saddhā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito hoti – ‘appeva nāma kusalaṃ dhammaṃ adhigaccheyyaṃ, appeva nāma uttarimanussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikareyya’nti. So attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpeti, kusalañhi kho dhammaṃ adhigacchati, uttarimanussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ na sacchikaroti.

“Here, headman, a certain ascetic, living a rough life, has gone forth from the household life into homelessness out of faith, thinking: ‘Perhaps I may attain a wholesome state, perhaps I may realize a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones.’ He heats and afflicts himself, and he does indeed attain a wholesome state, yet he does not realize a superior human state, a distinction in knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones.”

‘‘Idha pana, gāmaṇi, ekacco tapassī lūkhajīvī saddhā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito hoti – ‘appeva nāma kusalaṃ dhammaṃ adhigaccheyyaṃ, appeva nāma uttarimanussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikareyya’nti. So attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpeti, kusalañca dhammaṃ adhigacchati, uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikaroti.

“Here, headman, a certain ascetic living an austere life, out of faith, has gone forth from the household life into homelessness, thinking: ‘Perhaps I might attain a wholesome state, perhaps I might realize a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision.’ He heats and torments himself, and he attains a wholesome state, and he realizes a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision.

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ tapassī lūkhajīvī attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpeti, kusalañca dhammaṃ nādhigacchati, uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ na sacchikaroti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, tapassī lūkhajīvī tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho. Katamehi tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho? Attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpetīti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena gārayho. Kusalañca dhammaṃ nādhigacchatīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ na sacchikarotīti, iminā tatiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, tapassī lūkhajīvī, imehi tīhi ṭhānehi gārayho.

“In this case, headman, the ascetic who lives an austere life, heating and tormenting himself, but does not attain a wholesome state, nor realize a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision—this ascetic is blameworthy in three respects. In what three respects is he blameworthy? He heats and torments himself—this is the first respect in which he is blameworthy. He does not attain a wholesome state—this is the second respect in which he is blameworthy. He does not realize a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision—this is the third respect in which he is blameworthy. This ascetic is blameworthy in these three respects.

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ tapassī lūkhajīvī attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpeti, kusalañhi kho dhammaṃ adhigacchati, uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ na sacchikaroti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, tapassī lūkhajīvī dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho, ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho? Attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpetīti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena gārayho. Uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ na sacchikarotīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena gārayho. Katamena ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso? Kusalañhi kho dhammaṃ adhigacchatīti, iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, tapassī lūkhajīvī imehi dvīhi ṭhānehi gārayho, iminā ekena ṭhānena pāsaṃso.

“In this case, headman, the ascetic who lives an austere life, heating and tormenting himself, attains a wholesome state, but does not realize a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision—this ascetic is blameworthy in two respects and praiseworthy in one respect. In what two respects is he blameworthy? He heats and torments himself—this is the first respect in which he is blameworthy. He does not realize a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision—this is the second respect in which he is blameworthy. In what one respect is he praiseworthy? He attains a wholesome state—this is the one respect in which he is praiseworthy. This ascetic is blameworthy in these two respects and praiseworthy in this one respect.

‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, yvāyaṃ tapassī lūkhajīvī attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpeti, kusalañca dhammaṃ adhigacchati, uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikaroti. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, tapassī lūkhajīvī ekena ṭhānena gārayho, dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso. Katamena ekena ṭhānena gārayho? Attānaṃ ātāpeti paritāpetīti, iminā ekena ṭhānena gārayho. Katamehi dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso? Kusalañca dhammaṃ adhigacchatīti, iminā paṭhamena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Uttari ca manussadhammā alamariyañāṇadassanavisesaṃ sacchikarotīti, iminā dutiyena ṭhānena pāsaṃso. Ayaṃ, gāmaṇi, tapassī lūkhajīvī iminā ekena ṭhānena gārayho, imehi dvīhi ṭhānehi pāsaṃso.

“In this case, headman, the ascetic who lives an austere life, heating and tormenting himself, yet attains a wholesome state and realizes a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision—this ascetic is blameworthy in one respect and praiseworthy in two respects. In what one respect is he blameworthy? He heats and torments himself—this is the one respect in which he is blameworthy. In what two respects is he praiseworthy? He attains a wholesome state—this is the first respect in which he is praiseworthy. And he realizes a noble distinction of superhuman states of knowledge and vision—this is the second respect in which he is praiseworthy. This ascetic is blameworthy in one respect and praiseworthy in these two respects.

‘‘Tisso imā, gāmaṇi, sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhi. Katamā tisso? Yaṃ ratto rāgādhikaraṇaṃ attabyābādhāyapi ceteti, parabyābādhāyapi ceteti, ubhayabyābādhāyapi ceteti. Rāge pahīne nevattabyābādhāya ceteti, na parabyābādhāya ceteti, na ubhayabyābādhāya ceteti. Sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhi. Yaṃ duṭṭho dosādhikaraṇaṃ attabyābādhāyapi ceteti, parabyābādhāyapi ceteti, ubhayabyābādhāyapi ceteti. Dose pahīne nevattabyābādhāya ceteti, na parabyābādhāya ceteti, na ubhayabyābādhāya ceteti. Sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhi. Yaṃ mūḷho mohādhikaraṇaṃ attabyābādhāyapi ceteti, parabyābādhāyapi ceteti, ubhayabyābādhāyapi ceteti. Mohe pahīne nevattabyābādhāya ceteti, na parabyābādhāya ceteti, na ubhayabyābādhāya ceteti. Sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhi. Imā kho, gāmaṇi, tisso sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhī’’ti.

“There are, headman, these three kinds of effacement that are directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, and to be personally experienced by the wise. What are the three? When one is lustful, on account of lust, one intends harm for oneself, harm for others, or harm for both. But when lust is abandoned, one no longer intends harm for oneself, harm for others, or harm for both. This is an effacement that is directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, and to be personally experienced by the wise. When one is hateful, on account of hate, one intends harm for oneself, harm for others, or harm for both. But when hate is abandoned, one no longer intends harm for oneself, harm for others, or harm for both. This is an effacement that is directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, and to be personally experienced by the wise. When one is deluded, on account of delusion, one intends harm for oneself, harm for others, or harm for both. But when delusion is abandoned, one no longer intends harm for oneself, harm for others, or harm for both. This is an effacement that is directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, and to be personally experienced by the wise. These, headman, are the three kinds of effacement that are directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, and to be personally experienced by the wise.”

Evaṃ vutte, rāsiyo gāmaṇi bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bhante…pe… upāsakaṃ maṃ bhagavā dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti. Dvādasamaṃ.

When this was said, Rāsiya the headman said to the Blessed One: “Excellent, venerable sir! [...] From this day forward, for as long as life lasts, let the Blessed One remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge.” Twelfth.
