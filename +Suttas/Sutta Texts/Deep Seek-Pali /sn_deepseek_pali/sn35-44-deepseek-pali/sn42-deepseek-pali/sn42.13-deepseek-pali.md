# SN 42.13 Pāṭaliyasuttaṃ

## SN 42.13 The Pāṭaliya Discourse

365. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā koliyesu viharati uttaraṃ nāma [uttarakaṃ nāma (sī.)] koliyānaṃ nigamo. Atha kho pāṭaliyo gāmaṇi yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho pāṭaliyo gāmaṇi bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante – ‘samaṇo gotamo māyaṃ jānātī’ti. Ye te, bhante, evamāhaṃsu – ‘samaṇo gotamo māyaṃ jānātī’ti, kacci te, bhante, bhagavato vuttavādino, na ca bhagavantaṃ abhūtena abbhācikkhanti, dhammassa cānudhammaṃ byākaronti, na ca koci sahadhammiko vādānuvādo gārayhaṃ ṭhānaṃ āgacchati? Anabbhācikkhitukāmā hi mayaṃ, bhante, bhagavanta’’nti. ‘‘Ye te, gāmaṇi, evamāhaṃsu – ‘samaṇo gotamo māyaṃ jānātī’ti, vuttavādino ceva me, te na ca maṃ abhūtena abbhācikkhanti, dhammassa cānudhammaṃ byākaronti, na ca koci sahadhammiko vādānuvādo gārayhaṃ ṭhānaṃ āgacchatīti, saccaṃyeva kira, bho, mayaṃ tesaṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ na saddahāma – ‘samaṇo gotamo māyaṃ jānātīti, samaṇo khalu bho gotamo māyāvī’ti. Yo nu kho, gāmaṇi, evaṃ vadeti – ‘ahaṃ māyaṃ jānāmī’ti, so evaṃ vadeti – ‘ahaṃ māyāvī’ti. Tatheva taṃ bhagavā hoti, tatheva taṃ sugata hotī’’ti. Tena hi, gāmaṇi, taññevettha paṭipucchissāmi; yathā te khameyya, tathā taṃ byākareyyāsi –

365.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling among the Koliyans, in a Koliyan town named Uttara. Then the village headman Pāṭaliya approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the village headman Pāṭaliya said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, I have heard it said: ‘The ascetic Gotama knows magic.’ Those, venerable sir, who say this: ‘The ascetic Gotama knows magic,’ do they speak in accordance with the Blessed One's words, not misrepresenting him with what is untrue, and do they explain in accordance with the Dhamma, so that no reasonable consequence of their assertion gives ground for criticism? For we do not wish, venerable sir, to misrepresent the Blessed One." "Those, headman, who say this: ‘The ascetic Gotama knows magic,’ they speak in accordance with my words; they do not misrepresent me with what is untrue, and they explain in accordance with the Dhamma, so that no reasonable consequence of their assertion gives ground for criticism. Indeed, headman, we do not believe those ascetics and brahmins who say: ‘The ascetic Gotama knows magic; the ascetic Gotama is a magician.’ But, headman, one who says: ‘I know magic,’ he indeed says: ‘I am a magician.’ Just so is the Blessed One; just so is the Sugata." "Then, headman, I will question you in return. Answer as you see fit.

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, jānāsi tvaṃ koliyānaṃ lambacūḷake bhaṭe’’ti? ‘‘Jānāmahaṃ, bhante, koliyānaṃ lambacūḷake bhaṭe’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, kimatthiyā koliyānaṃ lambacūḷakā bhaṭā’’ti? ‘‘Ye ca, bhante, koliyānaṃ corā te ca paṭisedhetuṃ, yāni ca koliyānaṃ dūteyyāni tāni ca vahātuṃ [tāni ca pahātuṃ (syā. kaṃ.), tāni ca yātuṃ (katthaci), tāni cāvahātuṃ (?)], etadatthiyā, bhante, koliyānaṃ lambacūḷakā bhaṭā’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, jānāsi tvaṃ koliyānaṃ lambacūḷake bhaṭe sīlavante vā te dussīle vā’’ti? ‘‘Jānāmahaṃ, bhante, koliyānaṃ lambacūḷake bhaṭe dussīle pāpadhamme; ye ca loke dussīlā pāpadhammā koliyānaṃ lambacūḷakā bhaṭā tesaṃ aññatarā’’ti. ‘‘Yo nu kho, gāmaṇi, evaṃ vadeyya – ‘pāṭaliyo gāmaṇi jānāti koliyānaṃ lambacūḷake bhaṭe dussīle pāpadhamme, pāṭaliyopi gāmaṇi dussīlo pāpadhammo’ti, sammā nu kho so vadamāno vadeyyā’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante! Aññe, bhante, koliyānaṃ lambacūḷakā bhaṭā, aññohamasmi. Aññathādhammā koliyānaṃ lambacūḷakā bhaṭā, aññathādhammohamasmī’’ti. ‘‘Tvañhi nāma, gāmaṇi, lacchasi – ‘pāṭaliyo gāmaṇi jānāti koliyānaṃ lambacūḷake bhaṭe dussīle pāpadhamme, na ca pāṭaliyo gāmaṇi dussīlo pāpadhammo’ti, kasmā tathāgato na lacchati – ‘tathāgato māyaṃ jānāti, na ca tathāgato māyāvī’ti? Māyaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, māyāya ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca māyāvī kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi’’.

"What do you think, village headman? Do you know the long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans?" "I know them, venerable sir, the long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans." "What do you think, village headman? For what purpose do the Koliyans have long-haired soldiers?" "To repel the thieves of the Koliyans, venerable sir, and to carry their embassies—for this purpose, venerable sir, the Koliyans have long-haired soldiers." "What do you think, village headman? Do you know whether those long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans are virtuous or immoral?" "I know, venerable sir, that the long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans are immoral, of bad character. Among those in the world who are immoral and of bad character, the long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans are among them." "If someone were to say, ‘Village headman Pāṭaliya knows the long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans as immoral and of bad character, therefore village headman Pāṭaliya is also immoral and of bad character,’ would they be speaking rightly?" "Certainly not, venerable sir! The long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans are one thing, and I am another. The nature of the long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans is one thing, and my nature is another." "You, village headman, can claim, ‘Village headman Pāṭaliya knows the long-haired soldiers of the Koliyans as immoral and of bad character, but village headman Pāṭaliya is not immoral or of bad character.’ Why then should the Tathāgata not claim, ‘The Tathāgata knows deceit, but the Tathāgata is not deceitful’? I understand deceit, headman, and the result of deceit. I know how one who practices deceit, after death, upon the breakup of the body, is reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the lower realms, hell."

‘‘Pāṇātipātaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, pāṇātipātassa ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca pāṇātipātī kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Adinnādānaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, adinnādānassa ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca adinnādāyī kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Kāmesumicchācāraṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, kāmesumicchācārassa ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca kāmesumicchācārī kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Musāvādaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, musāvādassa ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca musāvādī kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Pisuṇavācaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, pisuṇavācāya ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca pisuṇavāco kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Pharusavācaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, pharusavācāya ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca pharusavāco kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Samphappalāpaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, samphappalāpassa ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca samphappalāpī kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Abhijjhaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, abhijjhāya ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca abhijjhālu kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Byāpādapadosaṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, byāpādapadosassa ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca byāpannacitto kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi. Micchādiṭṭhiṃ cāhaṃ, gāmaṇi, pajānāmi, micchādiṭṭhiyā ca vipākaṃ, yathāpaṭipanno ca micchādiṭṭhiko kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati tañca pajānāmi.

"I understand, headman, the taking of life, and I understand the result of taking life. And I understand how one who engages in taking life, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, taking what is not given, and I understand the result of taking what is not given. And I understand how one who engages in taking what is not given, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, sexual misconduct, and I understand the result of sexual misconduct. And I understand how one who engages in sexual misconduct, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, false speech, and I understand the result of false speech. And I understand how one who engages in false speech, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, divisive speech, and I understand the result of divisive speech. And I understand how one who engages in divisive speech, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, harsh speech, and I understand the result of harsh speech. And I understand how one who engages in harsh speech, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, idle chatter, and I understand the result of idle chatter. And I understand how one who engages in idle chatter, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, covetousness, and I understand the result of covetousness. And I understand how one who is covetous, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, ill will, and I understand the result of ill will. And I understand how one with a mind of ill will, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell. I understand, headman, wrong view, and I understand the result of wrong view. And I understand how one who holds wrong views, upon the breakup of the body after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, even hell."

‘‘Santi hi, gāmaṇi, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘yo koci pāṇamatipāteti, sabbo so diṭṭheva dhamme dukkhaṃ domanassaṃ paṭisaṃvedayati. Yo koci adinnaṃ ādiyati, sabbo so diṭṭheva dhamme dukkhaṃ domanassaṃ paṭisaṃvedayati. Yo koci kāmesu micchā carati, sabbo so diṭṭheva dhamme dukkhaṃ domanassaṃ paṭisaṃvedayati. Yo koci musā bhaṇati, sabbo so diṭṭheva dhamme dukkhaṃ domanassaṃ paṭisaṃvedayatī’’’ti.

"Indeed, village headman, there are some ascetics and brahmins who hold such a view and declare: ‘Whoever kills a living being, all of them experience pain and sorrow in this very life. Whoever takes what is not given, all of them experience pain and sorrow in this very life. Whoever engages in sexual misconduct, all of them experience pain and sorrow in this very life. Whoever speaks falsely, all of them experience pain and sorrow in this very life.’"

‘‘Dissati kho pana, gāmaṇi, idhekacco mālī kuṇḍalī sunhāto [sunahāto (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] suvilitto kappitakesamassu itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricārento. Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso kiṃ akāsi mālī kuṇḍalī sunhāto suvilitto kappitakesamassu itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricāretī’ti? Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso rañño paccatthikaṃ pasayha jīvitā voropesi. Tassa rājā attamano abhihāramadāsi. Tenāyaṃ puriso mālī kuṇḍalī sunhāto suvilitto kappitakesamassu, itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricāretī’’’ti.

"Indeed, village headman, it is seen in this case that a certain man, garlanded, wearing earrings, well-bathed and anointed, with hair and beard trimmed, is served by women as if he were a king enjoying sensual pleasures. And they say of him: 'Good sir, what has this man done that he is garlanded, wearing earrings, well-bathed and anointed, with hair and beard trimmed, and is served by women as if he were a king enjoying sensual pleasures?' And they say of him: 'Good sir, this man forcibly deprived the king's adversary of life. The king, pleased, gave him a reward. That is why this man is garlanded, wearing earrings, well-bathed and anointed, with hair and beard trimmed, and is served by women as if he were a king enjoying sensual pleasures.'"

‘‘Dissati kho, gāmaṇi, idhekacco daḷhāya rajjuyā pacchābāhaṃ gāḷhabandhanaṃ bandhitvā khuramuṇḍaṃ karitvā kharassarena paṇavena rathiyāya rathiyaṃ [rathikāya rathikaṃ (sī.)] siṅghāṭakena siṅghāṭakaṃ parinetvā, dakkhiṇena dvārena nikkhāmetvā, dakkhiṇato nagarassa sīsaṃ chijjamāno. Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso kiṃ akāsi, daḷhāya rajjuyā pacchābāhaṃ gāḷhabandhanaṃ bandhitvā khuramuṇḍaṃ karitvā kharassarena paṇavena rathiyāya rathiyaṃ siṅghāṭakena siṅghāṭakaṃ parinetvā dakkhiṇena dvārena nikkhāmetvā dakkhiṇato nagarassa sīsaṃ chindatī’ti [chijjatīti (katthaci)]? Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso rājaverī itthiṃ vā purisaṃ vā jīvitā voropesi, tena naṃ rājāno gahetvā evarūpaṃ kammakāraṇaṃ kārentī’’’ti.

“Here, village headman, a certain person is seen with his arms tightly bound behind his back with a strong rope, his head shaved, being paraded through street after street, crossroads after crossroads, to the sound of a harsh drum; having been led out through the southern gate, his head is cut off south of the city. Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, what has this man done that they bind his arms tightly behind his back with a strong rope, shave his head, parade him through street after street, crossroads after crossroads, to the sound of a harsh drum, lead him out through the southern gate, and cut off his head south of the city?’ Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, this man was a royal enemy; he has deprived a woman or a man of life. Therefore, the kings have seized him and are inflicting such punishment.’

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, api nu te evarūpaṃ diṭṭhaṃ vā sutaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Diṭṭhañca no, bhante, sutañca suyyissati cā’’ti. ‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘yo koci pāṇamatipāteti, sabbo so diṭṭheva dhamme dukkhaṃ domanassaṃ paṭisaṃvedayatī’ti, saccaṃ vā te āhaṃsu musā vā’’ti? ‘‘Musā, bhante’’. ‘‘Ye pana te tucchaṃ musā vilapanti, sīlavanto vā te dussīlā vā’’ti? ‘‘Dussīlā, bhante’’. ‘‘Ye pana te dussīlā pāpadhammā micchāpaṭipannā vā te sammāpaṭipannā vā’’ti? ‘‘Micchāpaṭipannā, bhante’’. ‘‘Ye pana te micchāpaṭipannā micchādiṭṭhikā vā te sammādiṭṭhikā vā’’ti? ‘‘Micchādiṭṭhikā, bhante’’. ‘‘Ye pana te micchādiṭṭhikā kallaṃ nu tesu pasīditu’’nti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“What do you think, village headman? Have you seen or heard such a thing?” “We have seen it, venerable sir, and heard it, and it will be heard.” “In that case, village headman, those ascetics and brahmins who speak thus and hold such a view: ‘Whoever kills a living being, all of them experience suffering and displeasure in this very life’—do they speak truth or falsehood?” “Falsehood, venerable sir.” “But those who utter empty falsehoods—are they virtuous or unvirtuous?” “Unvirtuous, venerable sir.” “And those who are unvirtuous, of evil qualities, practicing wrongly—are they practicing rightly or practicing wrongly?” “Practicing wrongly, venerable sir.” “And those who are practicing wrongly, of wrong view—are they of right view or wrong view?” “Wrong view, venerable sir.” “And those of wrong view—is it proper to place confidence in them?” “Certainly not, venerable sir.”

‘‘Dissati kho pana, gāmaṇi, idhekacco mālī kuṇḍalī…pe… itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricārento. Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso kiṃ akāsi mālī kuṇḍalī…pe… itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricāretī’ti? Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso rañño paccatthikassa pasayha ratanaṃ ahāsi [pasayha adinnaṃ ratanaṃ ādiyi (ka.)]. Tassa rājā attamano abhihāramadāsi. Tenāyaṃ puriso mālī kuṇḍalī…pe… itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricāretī’’’ti.

“But here, village headman, a certain person is seen adorned with garlands and earrings… being attended by the king, as it were, with sensual pleasures. Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, what has this man done that he is adorned with garlands and earrings… being attended by the king, as it were, with sensual pleasures?’ Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, this man forcibly took a jewel from an enemy of the king. Pleased with that, the king gave him a reward. That is why this man is adorned with garlands and earrings… being attended by the king, as it were, with sensual pleasures.’

‘‘Dissati kho, gāmaṇi, idhekacco daḷhāya rajjuyā…pe… dakkhiṇato nagarassa sīsaṃ chijjamāno tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso kiṃ akāsi daḷhāya rajjuyā…pe… dakkhiṇato nagarassa sīsaṃ chindatī’ti? Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso gāmā vā araññā vā adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyi. Tena naṃ rājāno gahetvā evarūpaṃ kammakāraṇaṃ kārentī’ti. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, gāmaṇi, api nu te evarūpaṃ diṭṭhaṃ vā sutaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Diṭṭhañca no, bhante, sutañca suyyissati cā’’ti. ‘‘Tatra, gāmaṇi, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘yo koci adinnaṃ ādiyati, sabbo so diṭṭheva dhamme dukkhaṃ domanassaṃ paṭisaṃvedayatī’ti, saccaṃ vā te āhaṃsu musā vāti…pe… kallaṃ nu tesu pasīditu’’nti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“Here, village headman, a certain person is seen with his arms tightly bound behind his back with a strong rope… his head being cut off south of the city. Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, what has this man done that they bind his arms tightly behind his back with a strong rope… and cut off his head south of the city?’ Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, this man has taken what was not given, reckoned as theft, from a village or a forest. Therefore, the kings have seized him and are inflicting such punishment.’ What do you think, village headman? Have you seen or heard such a thing?” “We have seen it, venerable sir, and heard it, and it will be heard.” “In that case, village headman, those ascetics and brahmins who speak thus and hold such a view: ‘Whoever takes what is not given, all of them experience suffering and displeasure in this very life’—do they speak truth or falsehood?... Is it proper to place confidence in them?” “Certainly not, venerable sir.”

‘‘Dissati kho pana, gāmaṇi, idhekacco mālī kuṇḍalī…pe… itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricārento. Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso kiṃ akāsi mālī kuṇḍalī…pe… itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricāretī’ti? Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso rañño paccatthikassa dāresu cārittaṃ āpajji. Tassa rājā attamano abhihāramadāsi. Tenāyaṃ puriso mālī kuṇḍalī…pe… itthikāmehi rājā maññe paricāretī’’’ti.

“But here, village headman, a certain person is seen adorned with garlands and earrings… being attended by the king, as it were, with sensual pleasures. Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, what has this man done that he is adorned with garlands and earrings… being attended by the king, as it were, with sensual pleasures?’ Then they say to him: ‘Good sirs, this man committed misconduct with the wives of the king’s adversary. Pleased with that, the king gave him a gift. That is why this man is adorned with garlands and earrings… being attended by the king, as it were, with sensual pleasures.’

‘‘Dissati kho, gāmaṇi, idhekacco daḷhāya rajjuyā…pe… dakkhiṇato nagarassa sīsaṃ chijjamāno. Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso kiṃ akāsi daḷhāya rajjuyā…pe… dakkhiṇato nagarassa sīsaṃ chindatī’ti? Tamenaṃ evamāhaṃsu – ‘ambho! Ayaṃ puriso kulitthīsu kulakumārīsu cārittaṃ āpajji, tena naṃ rājāno gahetvā evarūpaṃ kammakāraṇaṃ kārentī’ti. Uttarañcepi gaṅgāya tīraṃ gaccheyya dadanto dāpento, yajanto yajāpento, atthi tatonidānaṃ puññaṃ, atthi puññassa āgamo. Dānena damena saṃyamena saccavajjena atthi puññaṃ, atthi puññassa āgamo’ti. ‘Sace tassa bhoto satthuno saccaṃ vacanaṃ, apaṇṇakatāya mayhaṃ, yvāhaṃ na kiñci byābādhemi tasaṃ vā thāvaraṃ vā? Ubhayamettha kaṭaggāho, yaṃ camhi kāyena saṃvuto vācāya saṃvuto manasā saṃvuto, yañca kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjissāmī’ti. Tassa pāmojjaṃ jāyati. Pamuditassa pīti jāyati. Pītimanassa kāyo passambhati. Passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ vedayati. Sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati. Ayaṃ kho, gāmaṇi, dhammasamādhi. Tatra ce tvaṃ cittasamādhiṃ paṭilabheyyāsi, evaṃ tvaṃ imaṃ kaṅkhādhammaṃ pajaheyyāsī’’ti.

Then, village chief, that noble disciple, devoid of covetousness, devoid of ill will, unbewildered, clearly comprehending, mindful, dwells pervading one direction with a mind imbued with equanimity, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth; thus above, below, across, everywhere, and in every way, he dwells pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with equanimity—vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility, without ill will. He reflects thus: ‘Whatever teacher holds such a doctrine and view—that for one who acts and makes others act, who cuts and makes others cut, who cooks and makes others cook, who causes sorrow and makes others sorrow, who wearies and makes others weary, who makes tremble and makes others tremble, who takes life, takes what is not given, breaks into houses, plunders, commits burglary, lies in ambush on highways, goes to another’s wife, speaks falsehood—no evil is done. Even if with a razor-edged wheel one were to make the living beings on this earth into a single heap of flesh, there would be no evil resulting from that cause, no evil would come from that. If one were to go along the southern bank of the Ganges killing, slaughtering, cutting, making others cut, cooking, making others cook, there would be no evil resulting from that cause, no evil would come from that. If one were to go along the northern bank of the Ganges giving gifts, making others give, sacrificing, making others sacrifice, there would be no merit resulting from that cause, no merit would come from that. By giving, by taming, by restraint, by truthful speech, there is no merit, there is no accrual of merit.’ ‘If what that venerable teacher says is true, then for me, who harm no one, either feeble or firm, this is a sure thing. In both cases, it is a winning gain: that I am restrained in body, speech, and mind, and that after the dissolution of the body, after death, I will be reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world.’ Joy arises in him. In one who is joyful, rapture arises. In one whose mind is enraptured, the body becomes tranquil. One with a tranquil body experiences happiness. The mind of one who is happy becomes collected. This, village chief, is Dhamma-samādhi. If you were to attain samādhi of mind in that, you would abandon this state of doubt.”

Evaṃ vutte, pāṭaliyo gāmaṇi bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bhante, abhikkantaṃ, bhante…pe… ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti. Terasamaṃ.

When this was said, Pāṭaliya the village headman said to the Blessed One: “Excellent, venerable sir! Excellent, venerable sir! Just as if, venerable sir, one were to set upright what had been overturned, or to reveal what had been hidden, or to show the way to one who was lost, or to hold up a lamp in the darkness so that those with eyesight could see forms—in the same way has the Blessed One made the Dhamma clear in various ways. I go for refuge to the Blessed One, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha. May the Blessed One remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from today onwards for as long as I live.” The thirteenth.

Gāmaṇisaṃyuttaṃ samattaṃ.

The Chapter on Village Headmen (Complete)

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Caṇḍo puṭo yodhājīvo, hatthasso asibandhako;Desanā saṅkhakulaṃ maṇicūḷaṃ, bhadrarāsiyapāṭalīti.

Caṇḍa, Puṭa, Yodhājīva, Hatthassa, Asibandhaka; Desanā, Saṅkhakula, Maṇicūḷa, Bhadrarāsiyapāṭalī.
