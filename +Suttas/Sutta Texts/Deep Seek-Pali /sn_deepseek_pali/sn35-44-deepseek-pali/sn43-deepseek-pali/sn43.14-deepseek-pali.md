# SN 43.14 Parāyanasuttaṃ

## SN 43.14 The Way to the Far Shore

409. ‘‘Parāyanañca [parāyaṇañca (pī. sī. aṭṭha.)] vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi parāyanagāmiñca maggaṃ. Taṃ suṇātha. Katamañca, bhikkhave, parāyanaṃ? Yo, bhikkhave, rāgakkhayo dosakkhayo mohakkhayo – idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, parāyanaṃ. Katamo ca, bhikkhave, parāyanagāmī maggo? Kāyagatāsati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, parāyanagāmimaggo. Iti kho, bhikkhave, desitaṃ vo mayā parāyanaṃ, desito parāyanagāmimaggo. Yaṃ, bhikkhave, satthārā karaṇīyaṃ sāvakānaṃ hitesinā anukampakena anukampaṃ upādāya, kataṃ vo taṃ mayā. Etāni, bhikkhave, rukkhamūlāni, etāni suññāgārāni. Jhāyatha, bhikkhave, mā pamādattha; mā pacchā vippaṭisārino ahuvattha. Ayaṃ vo amhākaṃ anusāsanī’’ti. (Yathā asaṅkhataṃ tathā vitthāretabbaṃ). Tettiṃsatimaṃ.

409.“bhikkhus, I will teach you the ultimate goal and the path leading to the ultimate goal. Listen to it. And what, bhikkhus, is the ultimate goal? The destruction of lust, the destruction of hatred, the destruction of delusion—this, bhikkhus, is called the ultimate goal. And what, bhikkhus, is the path leading to the ultimate goal? Mindfulness of the body. This, bhikkhus, is called the path leading to the ultimate goal. Thus, bhikkhus, I have taught you the ultimate goal, and the path leading to the ultimate goal. Whatever a teacher should do for his disciples out of compassion, seeking their welfare, that has been done by me for you. Here, bhikkhus, are these roots of trees, here are these empty dwellings. Meditate, bhikkhus, do not be negligent, lest you regret it later. This is our instruction to you.”

Dutiyo vaggo.

The second chapter.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Asaṅkhataṃ anataṃ anāsavaṃ, saccañca pāraṃ nipuṇaṃ sududdasaṃ;Ajajjaraṃ dhuvaṃ apalokitaṃ, anidassanaṃ nippapañca santaṃ.

The unconditioned, the endless, the taintless, the truth, the far shore, the subtle, the very difficult to see; the undecaying, the constant, the unperishable, the unmanifest, free from proliferation, the peaceful.

Amataṃ paṇītañca sivañca khemaṃ, taṇhākkhayo acchariyañca abbhutaṃ;Anītikaṃ anītikadhammaṃ, nibbānametaṃ sugatena desitaṃ.

Deathless, excellent, auspicious, secure, the destruction of craving, wondrous, marvelous; without misfortune, without the nature of misfortune, this is Nibbāna, proclaimed by the Well-Gone One.

Abyāpajjho virāgo ca, suddhi mutti anālayo;Dīpo leṇañca tāṇañca, saraṇañca parāyananti.

Free from affliction, dispassion, purity, liberation, non-attachment; an island, a shelter, a protection, a refuge, the ultimate goal.

Asaṅkhatasaṃyuttaṃ samattaṃ.

The Connected Discourses on the Unconditioned is completed.
