# SN 36.28 Dutiyasamaṇabrāhmaṇasuttaṃ

## SN 36.28 The Second Discourse on Ascetics and Brahmins

276. ‘‘Tisso imā, bhikkhave, vedanā. Katamā tisso? Sukhā vedanā, dukkhā vedanā, adukkhamasukhā vedanā. Ye hi keci, bhikkhave, samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā imāsaṃ tissannaṃ vedanānaṃ samudayañca atthaṅgamañca assādañca ādīnavañca nissaraṇañca yathābhūtaṃ nappajānanti…pe… pajānanti…pe… sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharantī’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

276."bhikkhus, there are these three feelings. Which three? Pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. bhikkhus, whatever ascetics or brahmins do not understand as it actually is the origin, the cessation, the gratification, the danger, and the escape in the case of these three feelings... (and so on, as in the previous discourse, down to)... having realized them for themselves by direct knowledge, they abide having attained them." Eighth.
