# SN 36.22 Aṭṭhasatasuttaṃ

## SN 36.22 The Discourse on the One Hundred and Eight

270. ‘‘Aṭṭhasatapariyāyaṃ vo, bhikkhave, dhammapariyāyaṃ desessāmi. Taṃ suṇātha. Katamo ca, bhikkhave, aṭṭhasatapariyāyo, dhammapariyāyo? Dvepi mayā, bhikkhave, vedanā vuttā pariyāyena; tissopi mayā vedanā vuttā pariyāyena; pañcapi mayā vedanā vuttā pariyāyena; chapi mayā vedanā vuttā pariyāyena; aṭṭhārasāpi mayā vedanā vuttā pariyāyena; chattiṃsāpi mayā vedanā vuttā pariyāyena; aṭṭhasatampi mayā vedanā vuttā pariyāyena. ‘‘Katamā ca, bhikkhave, dve vedanā? Kāyikā ca cetasikā ca – imā vuccanti, bhikkhave, dve vedanā. Katamā ca, bhikkhave, tisso vedanā? Sukhā vedanā, dukkhā vedanā, adukkhamasukhā vedanā – imā vuccanti, bhikkhave, tisso vedanā. Katamā ca, bhikkhave, pañca vedanā? Sukhindriyaṃ, dukkhindriyaṃ, somanassindriyaṃ, domanassindriyaṃ, upekkhindriyaṃ – imā vuccanti, bhikkhave, pañca vedanā. Katamā ca, bhikkhave, cha vedanā? Cakkhusamphassajā vedanā…pe… manosamphassajā vedanā – imā vuccanti, bhikkhave, cha vedanā. Katamā ca, bhikkhave, aṭṭhārasa vedanā? Cha somanassūpavicārā, cha domanassūpavicārā, cha upekkhūpavicārā – imā vuccanti, bhikkhave, aṭṭhārasa vedanā. Katamā ca, bhikkhave, chattiṃsa vedanā? Cha gehasitāni [gehassitāni (aṭṭha.)] somanassāni, cha nekkhammasitāni [nekkhammassitāni (aṭṭha.)] somanassāni, cha gehasitāni domanassāni, cha nekkhammasitāni domanassāni, cha gehasitā upekkhā, cha nekkhammasitā upekkhā – imā vuccanti, bhikkhave, chattiṃsa vedanā. Katamañca, bhikkhave, aṭṭhasataṃ vedanā? Atītā chattiṃsa vedanā, anāgatā chattiṃsa vedanā, paccuppannā chattiṃsa vedanā – imā vuccanti, bhikkhave, aṭṭhasataṃ vedanā. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, aṭṭhasatapariyāyo dhammapariyāyo’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

270.“bhikkhus, I will teach you the exposition of the Dhamma in 108 ways. Listen carefully. And what, bhikkhus, is the exposition of the Dhamma in 108 ways? In one way, bhikkhus, I have spoken of two feelings; in another way, of three feelings; in another way, of five feelings; in another way, of six feelings; in another way, of eighteen feelings; in another way, of thirty-six feelings; and in another way, of one hundred and eight feelings. And what, bhikkhus, are the two feelings? Physical and mental—these, bhikkhus, are called the two feelings. And what, bhikkhus, are the three feelings? Pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling—these, bhikkhus, are called the three feelings. And what, bhikkhus, are the five feelings? The faculty of pleasure, the faculty of pain, the faculty of joy, the faculty of displeasure, and the faculty of equanimity—these, bhikkhus, are called the five feelings. And what, bhikkhus, are the six feelings? Feeling born of eye-contact…pe… feeling born of mind-contact—these, bhikkhus, are called the six feelings. And what, bhikkhus, are the eighteen feelings? Six modes of conduct accompanied by joy, six modes of conduct accompanied by displeasure, six modes of conduct accompanied by equanimity—these, bhikkhus, are called the eighteen feelings. And what, bhikkhus, are the thirty-six feelings? Six joys dependent on the household life, six joys dependent on renunciation, six displeasures dependent on the household life, six displeasures dependent on renunciation, six equanimities dependent on the household life, six equanimities dependent on renunciation—these, bhikkhus, are called the thirty-six feelings. And what, bhikkhus, are the one hundred and eight feelings? The thirty-six feelings of the past, the thirty-six feelings of the future, and the thirty-six feelings of the present—these, bhikkhus, are called the one hundred and eight feelings. This, bhikkhus, is the exposition of the Dhamma in 108 ways.” The second.
