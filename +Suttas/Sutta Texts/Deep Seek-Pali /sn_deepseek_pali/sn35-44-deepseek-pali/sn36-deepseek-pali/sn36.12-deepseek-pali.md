# SN 36.12 Paṭhamaākāsasuttaṃ

## SN 36.12 First Discourse on Space

260. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, ākāse vividhā vātā vāyanti. Puratthimāpi vātā vāyanti, pacchimāpi vātā vāyanti, uttarāpi vātā vāyanti, dakkhiṇāpi vātā vāyanti, sarajāpi vātā vāyanti, arajāpi vātā vāyanti, sītāpi vātā vāyanti, uṇhāpi vātā vāyanti, parittāpi vātā vāyanti, adhimattāpi vātā vāyanti. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, imasmiṃ kāyasmiṃ vividhā vedanā uppajjanti, sukhāpi vedanā uppajjati, dukkhāpi vedanā uppajjati, adukkhamasukhāpi vedanā uppajjatī’’ti.

260."Just as, bhikkhus, various winds blow in space: easterly winds blow, westerly winds blow, northerly winds blow, southerly winds blow, dusty winds blow, dustless winds blow, cold winds blow, hot winds blow, light winds blow, and strong winds blow. In the same way, bhikkhus, various feelings arise in this body: pleasant feelings arise, painful feelings arise, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feelings arise."

‘‘Yathāpi vātā ākāse, vāyanti vividhā puthū;Puratthimā pacchimā cāpi, uttarā atha dakkhiṇā.

"Just as winds in space blow, varied and diverse, easterly, westerly, northerly, and southerly.

‘‘Sarajā arajā capi, sītā uṇhā ca ekadā;Adhimattā parittā ca, puthū vāyanti mālutā.

Dusty winds and dustless, cold and hot as well; Intense and gentle, diverse winds that blow."

‘‘Tathevimasmiṃ kāyasmiṃ, samuppajjanti vedanā;Sukhadukkhasamuppatti, adukkhamasukhā ca yā.

"Just so, in this very body, feelings arise: the arising of pleasure and pain, and also that which is neither painful nor pleasant."

‘‘Yato ca bhikkhu ātāpī, sampajaññaṃ na riñcati [sampajāno nirūpadhi (ka.)];Tato so vedanā sabbā, parijānāti paṇḍito.

"When a monk is ardent and does not neglect clear comprehension, then that wise one fully understands all feelings."

‘‘So vedanā pariññāya, diṭṭhe dhamme anāsavo;Kāyassa bhedā dhammaṭṭho, saṅkhyaṃ nopeti vedagū’’ti. dutiyaṃ;

"He who has fully understood feeling, is without taints in this very life; Upon the dissolution of the body, the one standing firm in the Dhamma, the knower, does not go to reckoning." Second.
