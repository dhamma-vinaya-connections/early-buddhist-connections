# SN 36.10 Phassamūlakasuttaṃ

## SN 36.10 The Rooted in Contact Discourse

258. ‘‘Tisso imā, bhikkhave, vedanā phassajā phassamūlakā phassanidānā phassapaccayā. Katamā tisso? Sukhā vedanā, dukkhā vedanā, adukkhamasukhā vedanā. Sukhavedaniyaṃ, bhikkhave, phassaṃ paṭicca uppajjati sukhā vedanā. Tasseva sukhavedaniyassa phassassa nirodhā, yaṃ tajjaṃ vedayitaṃ sukhavedaniyaṃ phassaṃ paṭicca uppannā sukhā vedanā, sā nirujjhati, sā vūpasammati. Dukkhavedaniyaṃ, bhikkhave, phassaṃ paṭicca uppajjati dukkhā vedanā. Tasseva dukkhavedaniyassa phassassa nirodhā, yaṃ tajjaṃ vedayitaṃ dukkhavedaniyaṃ phassaṃ paṭicca uppannā dukkhā vedanā, sā nirujjhati, sā vūpasammati. Adukkhamasukhavedaniyaṃ, bhikkhave, phassaṃ paṭicca uppajjati adukkhamasukhā vedanā. Tasseva adukkhamasukhavedaniyassa phassassa nirodhā, yaṃ tajjaṃ vedayitaṃ adukkhamasukhavedaniyaṃ phassaṃ paṭicca uppannā adukkhamasukhā vedanā, sā nirujjhati, sā vūpasammati. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, dvinnaṃ kaṭṭhānaṃ saṅghaṭṭanasamodhānā [saṅkhattā tassa samodhānā (syā. kaṃ.) saṅghattā tassa samodhānā (ka.) saṃ. ni. 2.62 passitabbaṃ] usmā jāyati, tejo abhinibbattati. Tesaṃyeva kaṭṭhānaṃ nānābhāvā vinikkhepā, yā tajjā usmā, sā nirujjhati, sā vūpasammati. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, imā tisso vedanā phassajā phassamūlakā phassanidānā phassapaccayā. Tajjaṃ phassaṃ paṭicca tajjā vedanā uppajjanti. Tajjassa phassassa nirodhā tajjā vedanā nirujjhantī’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

258.“Monks, these three feelings are born of contact, rooted in contact, originating from contact, and conditioned by contact. Which three? Pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither pleasant nor painful feeling. Monks, dependent on contact to be experienced as pleasant, a pleasant feeling arises. With the cessation of that very contact to be experienced as pleasant, that pleasant feeling that arose dependent on that contact ceases and subsides. Monks, dependent on contact to be experienced as painful, a painful feeling arises. With the cessation of that very contact to be experienced as painful, that painful feeling that arose dependent on that contact ceases and subsides. Monks, dependent on contact to be experienced as neither pleasant nor painful, a neither pleasant nor painful feeling arises. With the cessation of that very contact to be experienced as neither pleasant nor painful, that neither pleasant nor painful feeling that arose dependent on that contact ceases and subsides. Just as, monks, heat is produced and fire is generated by the rubbing together and joining of two pieces of wood, but when those very pieces of wood are separated and laid aside, the heat that arose from them ceases and subsides. In the same way, monks, these three feelings are born of contact, rooted in contact, originating from contact, and conditioned by contact. Corresponding feelings arise dependent on corresponding contact. With the cessation of the corresponding contact, the corresponding feelings cease.” The tenth.

Sagāthāvaggo paṭhamo.

The First Chapter with Verses

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Samādhi sukhaṃ pahānena, pātālaṃ daṭṭhabbena ca;Sallena ceva gelaññā, anicca phassamūlakāti.

samādhi is happiness through abandoning, the underworld is to be seen, likewise the dart and sickness, impermanence is rooted in contact.
