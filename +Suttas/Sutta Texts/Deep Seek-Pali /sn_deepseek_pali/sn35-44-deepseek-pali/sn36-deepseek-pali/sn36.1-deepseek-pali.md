# SN 36.1 Samādhisuttaṃ

## SN 36.1 The samādhi Discourse

249. ‘‘Tisso imā, bhikkhave, vedanā. Katamā tisso? Sukhā vedanā, dukkhā vedanā, adukkhamasukhā vedanā – imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso vedanāti.

249."Monks, there are these three feelings. Which three? Pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. These, monks, are the three feelings."

‘‘Samāhito sampajāno, sato buddhassa sāvako;Vedanā ca pajānāti, vedanānañca sambhavaṃ.

"The Buddha’s disciple, collected, clearly comprehending, and mindful, understands feelings and the origin of feelings,"

‘‘Yattha cetā nirujjhanti, maggañca khayagāminaṃ;Vedanānaṃ khayā bhikkhu, nicchāto parinibbuto’’ti. paṭhamaṃ;

"where they cease, and the path leading to their ending. Through the ending of feelings, the monk, without craving, is fully liberated." First.
