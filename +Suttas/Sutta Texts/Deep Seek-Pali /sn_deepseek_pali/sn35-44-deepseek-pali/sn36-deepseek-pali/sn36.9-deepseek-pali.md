# SN 36.9 Aniccasuttaṃ

## SN 36.9 The Impermanence Discourse.

257. ‘‘Tisso imā, bhikkhave, vedanā aniccā saṅkhatā paṭiccasamuppannā khayadhammā vayadhammā virāgadhammā nirodhadhammā. Katamā tisso? Sukhā vedanā, dukkhā vedanā, adukkhamasukhā vedanā – imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso vedanā aniccā saṅkhatā paṭiccasamuppannā khayadhammā vayadhammā virāgadhammā nirodhadhammā’’ti. Navamaṃ.

257.“bhikkhus, these three feelings are impermanent, conditioned, dependently arisen, subject to destruction, subject to vanishing, subject to fading away, and subject to cessation. Which three? Pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither pleasant nor painful feeling. These, bhikkhus, are the three feelings that are impermanent, conditioned, dependently arisen, subject to destruction, subject to vanishing, subject to fading away, and subject to cessation.” The ninth.
