274.Then a number of monks approached the Blessed One; having approached, they sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those monks said to the Blessed One: "What, venerable sir, is feeling? What is the origin of feeling? What is the path leading to the origin of feeling? What is the cessation of feeling? What is the path leading to the cessation of feeling? What is the gratification in feeling, what is the danger, what is the escape?" "There are, monks, these three feelings: pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. These, monks, are called feelings. With the arising of contact, there is the arising of feeling. Craving is the path leading to the origin of feeling. With the cessation of contact... the removal of desire and lust for feeling, the abandoning of desire and lust for feeling. This is the escape from feeling." The Sixth.
