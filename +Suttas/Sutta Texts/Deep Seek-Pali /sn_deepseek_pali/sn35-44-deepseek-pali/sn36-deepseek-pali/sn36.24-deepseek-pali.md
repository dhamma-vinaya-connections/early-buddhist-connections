272."Before my full enlightenment, monks, while I was still an unenlightened Bodhisatta, this occurred to me: 'What is feeling? What is the origin of feeling? What is the path leading to the origin of feeling? What is the cessation of feeling? What is the path leading to the cessation of feeling? What is the gratification in feeling, what is the danger, what is the escape?' Then, monks, this occurred to me: 'These three feelings: pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-painful-nor-pleasant feeling. These are called feelings. With the arising of contact, there is the arising of feeling. Craving is the path leading to the origin of feeling... the removal of desire and lust for feeling, the abandoning of desire and lust for feeling. This is the escape from feeling.'" The Fourth.
