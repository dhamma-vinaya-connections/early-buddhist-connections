# SN 36.4 Pātālasuttaṃ

## SN 36.4 The Pātāla Sutta

252. ‘‘Assutavā, bhikkhave, puthujjano yaṃ vācaṃ bhāsati – ‘atthi mahāsamudde pātālo’ti. Taṃ kho panetaṃ, bhikkhave, assutavā puthujjano asantaṃ avijjamānaṃ evaṃ vācaṃ bhāsati – ‘atthi mahāsamudde pātālo’ti. Sārīrikānaṃ kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, dukkhānaṃ vedanānaṃ adhivacanaṃ yadidaṃ ‘pātālo’ti. Assutavā, bhikkhave, puthujjano sārīrikāya dukkhāya vedanāya phuṭṭho samāno socati kilamati paridevati urattāḷiṃ kandati sammohaṃ āpajjati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, ‘assutavā puthujjano pātāle na paccuṭṭhāsi, gādhañca nājjhagā’. Sutavā ca kho, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako sārīrikāya dukkhāya vedanāya phuṭṭho samāno neva socati, na kilamati, na paridevati, na urattāḷiṃ kandati, na sammohaṃ āpajjati. Ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, ‘sutavā ariyasāvako pātāle paccuṭṭhāsi, gādhañca ajjhagā’’’ti.

252."bhikkhus, the uninstructed ordinary person speaks thus: ‘There is an abyss in the great ocean.’ But this, bhikkhus, is just the uninstructed ordinary person speaking of something that is non-existent, not found, saying: ‘There is an abyss in the great ocean.’ ‘Abyss,’ bhikkhus, is a designation for painful bodily feelings. When the uninstructed ordinary person is afflicted by painful bodily feelings, they sorrow, grieve, lament, beat their breast, and fall into bewilderment. This, bhikkhus, is called ‘the uninstructed ordinary person who has not risen from the abyss and has not found a foothold.’ But the instructed noble disciple, bhikkhus, when afflicted by painful bodily feelings, does not sorrow, grieve, lament, beat their breast, or fall into bewilderment. This, bhikkhus, is called ‘the instructed noble disciple who has risen from the abyss and has found a foothold.’"

‘‘Yo etā nādhivāseti, uppannā vedanā dukhā;Sārīrikā pāṇaharā, yāhi phuṭṭho pavedhati.

"He who does not endure these painful feelings that have arisen, bodily, life-threatening, by which, being touched, he trembles."

‘‘Akkandati parodati, dubbalo appathāmako;Na so pātāle paccuṭṭhāsi, atho gādhampi nājjhagā.

"He cries out and laments, feeble and weak in strength; he does not rise from the abyss, nor does he find a foothold."

‘‘Yo cetā adhivāseti, uppannā vedanā dukhā;Sārīrikā pāṇaharā, yāhi phuṭṭho na vedhati;Sa ve pātāle paccuṭṭhāsi, atho gādhampi ajjhagā’’ti. catutthaṃ;

"But he who endures these painful feelings that have arisen, bodily, life-threatening, by which, being touched, he does not tremble; that one has risen from the abyss and has found a foothold." Fourth.
