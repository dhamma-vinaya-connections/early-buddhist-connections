# Dhp 8. Sahassavaggo
## 

100.
Sahassamapi ce vācā, anatthapadasaṃhitā;Ekaṃ atthapadaṃ seyyo, yaṃ sutvā upasammati.

Though one speaks a thousand meaningless words, better is one meaningful word, hearing which one is pacified.

101.
Sahassamapi ce gāthā, anatthapadasaṃhitā;Ekaṃ gāthāpadaṃ seyyo, yaṃ sutvā upasammati.

Though one may recite a thousand meaningless verses, better is one meaningful verse-line, hearing which one is pacified.

102.
Yo ca gāthā sataṃ bhāse, anatthapadasaṃhitā [anatthapadasañhitaṃ (ka.) visesanaṃ hetaṃ gāthātipadassa];Ekaṃ dhammapadaṃ seyyo, yaṃ sutvā upasammati.

Though one should recite a hundred meaningless verses, better is one Dhamma-verse, hearing which one is pacified.

103.
Yo sahassaṃ sahassena, saṅgāme mānuse jine;Ekañca jeyyamattānaṃ [attānaṃ (sī. pī.)], sa ve saṅgāmajuttamo.

Though one conquers a thousand times a thousand people in battle, to conquer oneself is better; he indeed is the supreme warrior.

104.
Attā have jitaṃ seyyo, yā cāyaṃ itarā pajā;Attadantassa posassa, niccaṃ saññatacārino.

Self-conquest is indeed better than the conquest of other folk. For a person who is self-controlled, ever restrained in conduct,

105.
Neva devo na gandhabbo, na māro saha brahmunā;Jitaṃ apajitaṃ kayirā, tathārūpassa jantuno.

Neither a deva nor a gandhabba, nor Māra together with Brahmā, can turn the victory of such a being into defeat.

106.
Māse māse sahassena, yo yajetha sataṃ samaṃ;Ekañca bhāvitattānaṃ, muhuttamapi pūjaye;Sāyeva pūjanā seyyo, yañce vassasataṃ hutaṃ.

Month after month, with a thousand (coins), one should offer sacrifices for a hundred years; but if for just a moment one honors one who has developed himself, that homage indeed is better than a hundred years of sacrifices.

107.
Yo ca vassasataṃ jantu, aggiṃ paricare vane;Ekañca bhāvitattānaṃ, muhuttamapi pūjaye;Sāyeva pūjanā seyyo, yañce vassasataṃ hutaṃ.

Though one should live a hundred years in the forest, worshipping the sacred fire, yet if for just a moment one honors one who has developed himself, that homage indeed is better than a hundred years of sacrifices.

108.
Yaṃ kiñci yiṭṭhaṃ va hutaṃ va [yiṭṭhañca hutañca (ka.)] loke, saṃvaccharaṃ yajetha puññapekkho;Sabbampi taṃ na catubhāgameti, abhivādanā ujjugatesu seyyo.

Whatever sacrifice or oblation one should make in the world for a whole year, seeking merit, all of that does not amount to a fourth part of the reverence shown to the upright.

109.
Abhivādanasīlissa, niccaṃ vuḍḍhāpacāyino [vaddhāpacāyino (sī. pī.)];Cattāro dhammā vaḍḍhanti, āyu vaṇṇo sukhaṃ balaṃ.

For one of respectful nature, who constantly reveres the elders, four qualities increase: long life, beauty, happiness, and strength.

110.
Yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, dussīlo asamāhito;Ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, sīlavantassa jhāyino.

Though one should live a hundred years, immoral and uncollected, better is life for a single day for one who is virtuous and meditative.

111.
Yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, duppañño asamāhito;Ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, paññavantassa jhāyino.

Though one should live a hundred years, foolish and uncollected, better is life for a single day for one who is wise and meditative.

112.
Yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, kusīto hīnavīriyo;Ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, vīriyamārabhato daḷhaṃ.

Though one should live a hundred years, lazy and of weak energy, better is life for a single day for one who firmly undertakes effort.

113.
Yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, apassaṃ udayabbayaṃ;Ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, passato udayabbayaṃ.

Though one should live a hundred years, not seeing arising and passing away, better is life for a single day for one who sees arising and passing away.

114.
Yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, apassaṃ amataṃ padaṃ;Ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, passato amataṃ padaṃ.

Though one should live a hundred years, not seeing the Deathless State, better is life for a single day for one who sees the Deathless State.

115.
Yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, apassaṃ dhammamuttamaṃ;Ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, passato dhammamuttamaṃ.

Though one should live a hundred years, not seeing the supreme Dhamma, better is life for a single day for one who sees the supreme Dhamma.
Sahassavaggo aṭṭhamo niṭṭhito.
The Eighth Chapter on Thousands is finished.
