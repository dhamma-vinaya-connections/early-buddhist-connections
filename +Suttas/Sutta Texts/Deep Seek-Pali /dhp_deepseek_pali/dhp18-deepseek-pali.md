# Dhp 18. Malavaggo
## 

235.
Paṇḍupalāsova dānisi, yamapurisāpi ca te [taṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] upaṭṭhitā;Uyyogamukhe ca tiṭṭhasi, pātheyyampi ca te na vijjati.

Like a withered leaf are you now, and the messengers of death are at hand; you stand at the doorway of departure, and you have no provisions for your journey.

236.
So karohi dīpamattano, khippaṃ vāyama paṇḍito bhava;Niddhantamalo anaṅgaṇo, dibbaṃ ariyabhūmiṃ upehisi [dibbaṃ ariyabhūmimehisi (sī. syā. pī.), dibbamariyabhūmiṃ upehisi (?)].

Make an island for yourself, strive quickly, become wise! Rid of impurities, unstained, you will reach the divine, noble state.

237.
Upanītavayo ca dānisi, sampayātosi yamassa santike;Vāso [vāsopi ca (bahūsu)] te natthi antarā, pātheyyampi ca te na vijjati.

Your life has now come to an end, you are approaching the presence of Yama; there is no resting place for you in between, and you have no provisions for the journey.

238.
So karohi dīpamattano, khippaṃ vāyama paṇḍito bhava;Niddhantamalo anaṅgaṇo, na punaṃ jātijaraṃ [na puna jātijaraṃ (sī. syā.), na puna jātijjaraṃ (ka.)] upehisi.

Make an island for yourself! Strive quickly, become wise! Cleansed of impurities, stainless, you will not come again to birth and old age.

239.
Anupubbena medhāvī, thokaṃ thokaṃ khaṇe khaṇe;Kammāro rajatasseva, niddhame malamattano.

Gradually, little by little, from moment to moment, the wise person removes their own impurities, like a smith removing dross from silver.

240.
Ayasāva malaṃ samuṭṭhitaṃ [samuṭṭhāya (ka.)], tatuṭṭhāya [taduṭṭhāya (sī. syā. pī.)] tameva khādati;Evaṃ atidhonacārinaṃ, sāni kammāni [sakakammāni (sī. pī.)] nayanti duggatiṃ.

Just as rust arises from iron and corrodes the very iron from which it arises, so too do one's own deeds lead the overindulgent to a bad destination.

241.
Asajjhāyamalā mantā, anuṭṭhānamalā gharā;Malaṃ vaṇṇassa kosajjaṃ, pamādo rakkhato malaṃ.

Recitations are marred by lack of repetition, homes by lack of upkeep; laziness is the stain of beauty, heedlessness the stain of a guardian.

242.
Malitthiyā duccaritaṃ, maccheraṃ dadato malaṃ;Malā ve pāpakā dhammā, asmiṃ loke paramhi ca.

Misconduct is a stain for a woman, stinginess is the stain of a giver; truly, evil qualities are stains in this world and the next.

243.
Tato malā malataraṃ, avijjā paramaṃ malaṃ;Etaṃ malaṃ pahantvāna, nimmalā hotha bhikkhavo.

But worse than these stains is ignorance, the ultimate stain. Having abandoned this stain, be stainless, O monks!

244.
Sujīvaṃ ahirikena, kākasūrena dhaṃsinā;Pakkhandinā pagabbhena, saṃkiliṭṭhena jīvitaṃ.

Easy is life for the shameless, the brazen, the ruinous, the impetuous, the arrogant, the corrupt.

245.
Hirīmatā ca dujjīvaṃ, niccaṃ sucigavesinā;Alīnenāppagabbhena, suddhājīvena passatā.

Hard is life for the modest, ever seeking purity, detached, unassuming, pure in livelihood, discerning.

246.
Yo pāṇamatipāteti, musāvādañca bhāsati;Loke adinnamādiyati, paradārañca gacchati.

One who destroys life, and speaks falsehood; takes what is not given in the world, and goes to another's wife.

247.
Surāmerayapānañca, yo naro anuyuñjati;Idhevameso lokasmiṃ, mūlaṃ khaṇati attano.

and one who is devoted to intoxicants undermines his own roots in this very world.

248.
Evaṃ bho purisa jānāhi, pāpadhammā asaññatā;Mā taṃ lobho adhammo ca, ciraṃ dukkhāya randhayuṃ.

Know this, O man: evil qualities are unrestrained. Do not let greed and unrighteousness bring you long-lasting suffering.

249.
Dadāti ve yathāsaddhaṃ, yathāpasādanaṃ [yattha pasādanaṃ (katthaci)] jano;Tattha yo maṅku bhavati [tattha ce maṃku yo hoti (sī.), tattha yo maṅkuto hoti (syā.)], paresaṃ pānabhojane;Na so divā vā rattiṃ vā, samādhimadhigacchati.

People give according to faith, according to their inspiration. Whoever is grudging concerning the drink and food of others does not attain samādhi by day or night.

250.
Yassa cetaṃ samucchinnaṃ, mūlaghaccaṃ [mūlaghacchaṃ (ka.)] samūhataṃ;Sa ve divā vā rattiṃ vā, samādhimadhigacchati.

But one who has cut off this, uprooted and destroyed its root, attains samādhi by day or night.

251.
Natthi rāgasamo aggi, natthi dosasamo gaho;Natthi mohasamaṃ jālaṃ, natthi taṇhāsamā nadī.

There is no fire like lust, no grip like hate, no net like delusion, no river like craving.

252.
Sudassaṃ vajjamaññesaṃ, attano pana duddasaṃ;Paresaṃ hi so vajjāni, opunāti [ophunāti (ka.)] yathā bhusaṃ;Attano pana chādeti, kaliṃva kitavā saṭho.

It is easy to see the faults of others, but difficult to see one's own. One sifts out the faults of others like chaff, but hides one's own faults as a crafty gambler hides a losing throw.

253.
Paravajjānupassissa, niccaṃ ujjhānasaññino;Āsavā tassa vaḍḍhanti, ārā so āsavakkhayā.

One who is always focused on the faults of others, ever resentful—his defilements increase. He is far from the destruction of defilements.

254.
Ākāseva padaṃ natthi, samaṇo natthi bāhire;Papañcābhiratā pajā, nippapañcā tathāgatā.

There is no footprint in the sky; there is no true ascetic outside (the Buddha's teaching). People delight in proliferation; the Tathāgatas are without proliferation.

255.
Ākāseva padaṃ natthi, samaṇo natthi bāhire;Saṅkhārā sassatā natthi, natthi buddhānamiñjitaṃ.

There is no footprint in the sky; there is no recluse outside (the Buddha's teaching); conditioned things are not eternal; there is no wavering in the Buddhas.
Malavaggo aṭṭhārasamo niṭṭhito.
The Eighteenth Chapter on Stains is Finished.
