# Dhp 25. Bhikkhuvaggo
## 

360.
Cakkhunā saṃvaro sādhu, sādhu sotena saṃvaro;Ghānena saṃvaro sādhu, sādhu jivhāya saṃvaro.

Good is restraint of the eye, good is restraint of the ear; good is restraint of the nose, good is restraint of the tongue.

361.
Kāyena saṃvaro sādhu, sādhu vācāya saṃvaro;Manasā saṃvaro sādhu, sādhu sabbattha saṃvaro;Sabbattha saṃvuto bhikkhu, sabbadukkhā pamuccati.

Good is restraint of the body, good is restraint of speech; good is restraint of the mind, good is restraint in all things. A bhikkhu restrained in all things is freed from all suffering.

362.
Hatthasaṃyato pādasaṃyato, vācāsaṃyato saṃyatuttamo;Ajjhattarato samāhito, eko santusito tamāhu bhikkhuṃ.

Restrained in hand, restrained in foot, restrained in speech, with supreme self-restraint; delighting inwardly, composed, alone, content—him they call a bhikkhu.

363.
Yo mukhasaṃyato bhikkhu, mantabhāṇī anuddhato;Atthaṃ dhammañca dīpeti, madhuraṃ tassa bhāsitaṃ.

A bhikkhu restrained in mouth, speaking thoughtfully, not arrogant, illuminating the meaning and the Dhamma—sweet is his speech.

364.
Dhammārāmo dhammarato, dhammaṃ anuvicintayaṃ;Dhammaṃ anussaraṃ bhikkhu, saddhammā na parihāyati.

Delighting in the Dhamma, devoted to the Dhamma, contemplating the Dhamma, a bhikkhu who recollects the Dhamma does not fall away from the true Dhamma.

365.
Salābhaṃ nātimaññeyya, nāññesaṃ pihayaṃ care;Aññesaṃ pihayaṃ bhikkhu, samādhiṃ nādhigacchati.

Let one not despise one's own gains, nor envy others. A bhikkhu who envies others does not attain samādhi.

366.
Appalābhopi ce bhikkhu, salābhaṃ nātimaññati;Taṃ ve devā pasaṃsanti, suddhājīviṃ atanditaṃ.

Even if a bhikkhu receives little, he does not despise his gains. The gods indeed praise such a one—pure in livelihood, untiring.

367.
Sabbaso nāmarūpasmiṃ, yassa natthi mamāyitaṃ;Asatā ca na socati, sa ve ‘‘bhikkhū’’ti vuccati.

One for whom there is no sense of "mine" at all in name and form, who does not grieve for what does not exist—he is truly called a "bhikkhu."

368.
Mettāvihārī yo bhikkhu, pasanno buddhasāsane;Adhigacche padaṃ santaṃ, saṅkhārūpasamaṃ sukhaṃ.

A bhikkhu who dwells in loving-kindness, serene in the Buddha's Dispensation, attains the peaceful state, the blissful stilling of formations.

369.
Siñca bhikkhu imaṃ nāvaṃ, sittā te lahumessati;Chetvā rāgañca dosañca, tato nibbānamehisi.

Bail out this boat, O bhikkhu! Bailed out, it will go swiftly. Cutting off lust and hate, then you will go to Nibbana.

370.
Pañca chinde pañca jahe, pañca cuttari bhāvaye;Pañca saṅgātigo bhikkhu, ‘‘oghatiṇṇo’’ti vuccati.

Cut off five, abandon five, further cultivate five; the bhikkhu who has transcended five attachments is called "one who has crossed the flood."

371.
Jhāya bhikkhu [jhāya tuvaṃ bhikkhu (?)] mā pamādo [mā ca pamādo (sī. syā. pī.)], mā te kāmaguṇe ramessu [bhamassu (sī. pī.), bhavassu (syā.), ramassu (ka.)] cittaṃ;Mā lohaguḷaṃ gilī pamatto, mā kandi ‘‘dukkhamida’’nti ḍayhamāno.

Meditate, O bhikkhu, do not be heedless; let not your mind delight in sensual qualities; lest, heedless, you swallow a ball of molten iron, and cry, "This is suffering," while burning.

372.
Natthi jhānaṃ apaññassa, paññā natthi ajhāyato [ajjhāyino (ka.)];Yamhi jhānañca paññā ca, sa ve nibbānasantike.

There is no samādhi for one without wisdom, nor is there wisdom for one without samādhi; in whom are found both samādhi and wisdom, he, indeed, is close to Nibbana.

373.
Suññāgāraṃ paviṭṭhassa, santacittassa bhikkhuno;Amānusī rati hoti, sammā dhammaṃ vipassato.

For a bhikkhu who has entered an empty dwelling, with a tranquil mind, there arises a joy transcending the human, as he rightly discerns the Dhamma.

374.
Yato yato sammasati, khandhānaṃ udayabbayaṃ;Labhatī [labhati (pī.), labhate (ka.)] pītipāmojjaṃ, amataṃ taṃ vijānataṃ.

Whenever he discerns, again and again, the rise and fall of the aggregates, he obtains joy and gladness; that is the deathless for those who know.

375.
Tatrāyamādi bhavati, idha paññassa bhikkhuno;Indriyagutti santuṭṭhi, pātimokkhe ca saṃvaro.

Herein lies the beginning for a wise bhikkhu: Sense-restraint, contentment, and restraint according to the Patimokkha.

376.
Mitte bhajassu kalyāṇe, suddhājīve atandite;Paṭisanthāravutyassa [paṭisandhāravutyassa (ka.)], ācārakusalo siyā;Tato pāmojjabahulo, dukkhassantaṃ karissati.

Associate with good friends, pure in livelihood, unwearied; be courteous and virtuous in conduct; then, abundant in joy, he will make an end of suffering.

377.
Vassikā viya pupphāni, maddavāni [majjavāni (ka. ṭīkā) paccavāni (ka. aṭṭha.)] pamuñcati;Evaṃ rāgañca dosañca, vippamuñcetha bhikkhavo.

As the jasmine sheds its withered flowers, so, O bhikkhus, should you shed lust and hatred.

378.
Santakāyo santavāco, santavā susamāhito [santamano susamāhito (syā. pī.), santamano samāhito (ka.)];Vantalokāmiso bhikkhu, ‘‘upasanto’’ti vuccati.

Calm in body, calm in speech, calm in mind, well-composed, the bhikkhu who has renounced worldly allurements is called "one at peace."

379.
Attanā codayattānaṃ, paṭimaṃsetha attanā [paṭimāse attamattanā (sī. pī.), paṭimaṃse tamattanā (syā.)];So attagutto satimā, sukhaṃ bhikkhu vihāhisi.

Admonish yourself by yourself, examine yourself by yourself; self-guarded and mindful, you will dwell happily, O bhikkhu.

380.
Attā hi attano nātho, (ko hi nātho paro siyā) [( ) videsapotthakesu natthi]Attā hi attano gati;Tasmā saṃyamamattānaṃ [saṃyamaya’ttānaṃ (sī. pī.)], assaṃ bhadraṃva vāṇijo.

One indeed is one's own protector, (who else could be the protector?); one indeed is one's own destiny. Therefore, control yourself as a merchant controls a fine horse.

381.
Pāmojjabahulo bhikkhu, pasanno buddhasāsane;Adhigacche padaṃ santaṃ, saṅkhārūpasamaṃ sukhaṃ.

The bhikkhu, abounding in joy, with faith in the Buddha's teaching, will attain the peaceful state, the bliss of the stilling of formations.

382.
Yo have daharo bhikkhu, yuñjati buddhasāsane;Somaṃ [so imaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] lokaṃ pabhāseti, abbhā muttova candimā.

Indeed, a young bhikkhu who devotes himself to the Buddha's teaching illuminates this world like the moon freed from a cloud.
Bhikkhuvaggo pañcavīsatimo niṭṭhito.
The Bhikkhu Chapter, the Twenty-Fifth, is Finished.
