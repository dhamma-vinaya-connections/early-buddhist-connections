# Dhp 10. Daṇḍavaggo
## 

129.
Sabbe tasanti daṇḍassa, sabbe bhāyanti maccuno;Attānaṃ upamaṃ katvā, na haneyya na ghātaye.

All tremble at punishment, all fear death. Having made oneself the example, one should neither strike nor kill.

130.
Sabbe tasanti daṇḍassa, sabbesaṃ jīvitaṃ piyaṃ;Attānaṃ upamaṃ katvā, na haneyya na ghātaye.

All tremble at punishment, all hold life dear. Having made oneself the example, one should neither strike nor kill.

131.
Sukhakāmāni bhūtāni, yo daṇḍena vihiṃsati;Attano sukhamesāno, pecca so na labhate sukhaṃ.

All beings desire happiness. Whoever harms them with a rod, seeking his own happiness, after death obtains no happiness.

132.
Sukhakāmāni bhūtāni, yo daṇḍena na hiṃsati;Attano sukhamesāno, pecca so labhate sukhaṃ.

All beings desire happiness. Whoever does not harm them with a rod, seeking his own happiness, after death obtains happiness.

133.
Māvoca pharusaṃ kañci, vuttā paṭivadeyyu taṃ [paṭivadeyyuṃ taṃ (ka.)];Dukkhā hi sārambhakathā, paṭidaṇḍā phuseyyu taṃ [phuseyyuṃ taṃ (ka.)].

Speak harshly to no one; those spoken to might retort to you. Painful indeed is contentious speech, for retaliation may touch you.

134.
Sace neresi attānaṃ, kaṃso upahato yathā;Esa pattosi nibbānaṃ, sārambho te na vijjati.

If you subdue yourself, like a broken gong, you have attained Nibbāna; no agitation is found in you.

135.
Yathā daṇḍena gopālo, gāvo pājeti gocaraṃ;Evaṃ jarā ca maccu ca, āyuṃ pājenti pāṇinaṃ.

As a herdsman with a staff drives cattle to pasture, so aging and death drive the life of living beings.

136.
Atha pāpāni kammāni, karaṃ bālo na bujjhati;Sehi kammehi dummedho, aggidaḍḍhova tappati.

Then the fool, committing evil deeds, does not realize it; the dull-witted man is tormented by his own deeds, as if burned by fire.

137.
Yo daṇḍena adaṇḍesu, appaduṭṭhesu dussati;Dasannamaññataraṃ ṭhānaṃ, khippameva nigacchati.

Whoever harms the innocent and harmless with a rod, quickly encounters one of these ten states.

138.
Vedanaṃ pharusaṃ jāniṃ, sarīrassa ca bhedanaṃ [sarīrassa pabhedanaṃ (syā.)];Garukaṃ vāpi ābādhaṃ, cittakkhepañca [cittakkhepaṃ va (sī. syā. pī.)] pāpuṇe.

He experiences harsh feeling, and the breaking up of the body; or grave illness, or derangement of mind.

139.
Rājato vā upasaggaṃ [upassaggaṃ (sī. pī.)], abbhakkhānañca [abbhakkhānaṃ va (sī. pī.)] dāruṇaṃ;Parikkhayañca [parikkhayaṃ va (sī. syā. pī.)] ñātīnaṃ, bhogānañca [bhogānaṃ va (sī. syā. pī.)] pabhaṅguraṃ [pabhaṅgunaṃ (ka.)].

Or oppression from the king, and cruel false accusation; and the ruin of relatives, and the destruction of wealth.

140.
Atha vāssa agārāni, aggi ḍahati [ḍayhati (ka.)] pāvako;Kāyassa bhedā duppañño, nirayaṃ sopapajjati [so upapajjati (sī. syā.)].

Or else his houses are burned by a fierce fire; when his body breaks up, the dull-witted one fares to hell.

141.
Na naggacariyā na jaṭā na paṅkā, nānāsakā thaṇḍilasāyikā vā;Rajojallaṃ ukkuṭikappadhānaṃ, sodhenti maccaṃ avitiṇṇakaṅkhaṃ.

Not naked practices, nor matted hair, nor mud, nor abstaining from food, nor lying on the bare ground, nor dust and grime, nor striving in the squatting posture—none of these purify a mortal who has not overcome doubt.

142.
Alaṅkato cepi samaṃ careyya, santo danto niyato brahmacārī;Sabbesu bhūtesu nidhāya daṇḍaṃ, so brāhmaṇo so samaṇo sa bhikkhu.

Though adorned, if one lives equanimously, calm, self-controlled, restrained, leading a holy life; having laid down the rod towards all beings, he is a brahmin, a recluse, a bhikkhu.

143.
Hirīnisedho puriso, koci lokasmi vijjati;Yo niddaṃ [nindaṃ (sī. pī.) saṃ. ni. 1.18] apabodheti [apabodhati (sī. syā. pī.)], asso bhadro kasāmiva.

Rare in the world is the person restrained by shame, who awakens from sleep, like a fine horse roused by the whip.

144.
Asso yathā bhadro kasāniviṭṭho, ātāpino saṃvegino bhavātha;Saddhāya sīlena ca vīriyena ca, samādhinā dhammavinicchayena ca;Sampannavijjācaraṇā patissatā, jahissatha [pahassatha (sī. syā. pī.)] dukkhamidaṃ anappakaṃ.

Like a fine horse roused by the whip, be ardent and full of spiritual urgency; with faith, virtue, energy, and samādhi, with discernment of the Dhamma and mindfulness; endowed with knowledge and conduct, being mindful, you will abandon this suffering, which is not small.

145.
Udakañhi nayanti nettikā, usukārā namayanti tejanaṃ;Dāruṃ namayanti tacchakā, attānaṃ damayanti subbatā.

Irrigators lead the water; fletchers straighten the arrow-shaft; carpenters bend wood; the disciplined tame themselves.
Daṇḍavaggo dasamo niṭṭhito.
The Tenth Chapter on Punishment is finished.
