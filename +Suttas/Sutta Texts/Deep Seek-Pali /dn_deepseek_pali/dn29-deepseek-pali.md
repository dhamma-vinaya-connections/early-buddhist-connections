# Pāsādikasuttaṃ

## The Discourse on What is Pleasing

164. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati vedhaññā nāma sakyā, tesaṃ ambavane pāsāde.

164.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans named Vedhaññā, in their mango grove mansion.

## Nigaṇṭhanāṭaputtakālaṅkiriyā

### The Passing Away of the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta

Tena kho pana samayena nigaṇṭho nāṭaputto [nāthaputto (sī. pī.)] pāvāyaṃ adhunākālaṅkato hoti. Tassa kālaṅkiriyāya bhinnā nigaṇṭhā dvedhikajātā bhaṇḍanajātā kalahajātā vivādāpannā aññamaññaṃ mukhasattīhi vitudantā viharanti – ‘‘na tvaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānāsi, ahaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānāmi, kiṃ tvaṃ imaṃ dhammavinayaṃ ājānissasi? Micchāpaṭipanno tvamasi, ahamasmi sammāpaṭipanno. Sahitaṃ me, asahitaṃ te. Purevacanīyaṃ pacchā avaca, pacchāvacanīyaṃ pure avaca. Adhiciṇṇaṃ te viparāvattaṃ, āropito te vādo, niggahito tvamasi, cara vādappamokkhāya, nibbeṭhehi vā sace pahosī’’ti. Vadhoyeva kho [vadhoyeveko (ka.)] maññe nigaṇṭhesu nāṭaputtiyesu vattati [anuvattati (syā. ka.)]. Yepi nigaṇṭhassa nāṭaputtassa sāvakā gihī odātavasanā, tepi [te tesu (ka.)] nigaṇṭhesu nāṭaputtiyesu nibbinnarūpā [nibbindarūpā (ka.)] virattarūpā paṭivānarūpā, yathā taṃ durakkhāte dhammavinaye duppavedite aniyyānike anupasamasaṃvattanike asammāsambuddhappavedite bhinnathūpe appaṭisaraṇe.

At that time, the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta had recently passed away in Pāvā. With his passing away, the Nigaṇṭhas became divided, split into two factions, quarrelsome, disputatious, and argumentative, attacking each other with verbal spears: "You don’t understand this Dhamma-Vinaya, I understand this Dhamma-Vinaya. How could you understand this Dhamma-Vinaya? You are practicing wrongly, I am practicing rightly. My words are coherent, yours are incoherent. What should have been said first, you said last; what should have been said last, you said first. Your long-held view is overturned, your argument is refuted, you are defeated. Go, try to free yourself from this debate—or clear yourself if you are able!" It seems that only destruction prevails among the Nigaṇṭhas, the followers of Nāṭaputta. Even the lay disciples of the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta, wearing white clothes, have become repelled, disaffected, and averse to the Nigaṇṭhas, as is to be expected in a Dhamma-Vinaya that is poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, with a shattered foundation, without refuge.

165. Atha kho cundo samaṇuddeso pāvāyaṃ vassaṃvuṭṭho [vassaṃvuttho (sī. syā. pī.)] yena sāmagāmo, yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho cundo samaṇuddeso āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘nigaṇṭho, bhante, nāṭaputto pāvāyaṃ adhunākālaṅkato. Tassa kālaṅkiriyāya bhinnā nigaṇṭhā dvedhikajātā…pe… bhinnathūpe appaṭisaraṇe’’ti.

165.Then the novice Cunda, having completed the rains residence in Pāvā, went to Sāmagāma and approached Venerable Ānanda. Having approached, he paid homage to Venerable Ānanda and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the novice Cunda said to Venerable Ānanda: "Venerable sir, the Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta has recently passed away in Pāvā. With his passing away, the Nigaṇṭhas have become divided, split into two factions... with a shattered foundation, without refuge."

Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā ānando cundaṃ samaṇuddesaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘atthi kho idaṃ, āvuso cunda, kathāpābhataṃ bhagavantaṃ dassanāya. Āyāmāvuso cunda, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamissāma; upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ bhagavato ārocessāmā’’ti [āroceyyāmāti (syā.)]. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho cundo samaṇuddeso āyasmato ānandassa paccassosi.

When this was said, Venerable Ānanda said to the novice Cunda: "Friend Cunda, this matter is fit to be spoken of to the Blessed One. Come, friend Cunda, let us approach the Blessed One, and report this matter to him." "Yes, venerable sir," the novice Cunda replied to Venerable Ānanda.

Atha kho āyasmā ca ānando cundo ca samaṇuddeso yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayaṃ, bhante, cundo samaṇuddeso evamāha, ‘nigaṇṭho, bhante, nāṭaputto pāvāyaṃ adhunākālaṅkato, tassa kālaṅkiriyāya bhinnā nigaṇṭhā…pe… bhinnathūpe appaṭisaraṇe’’’ti.

Then Venerable Ānanda and the novice Cunda approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, Venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, this novice Cunda says: ‘The Nigaṇṭha Nāṭaputta has recently passed away in Pāvā. At his death, the Nigaṇṭhas have become divided... with a shattered foundation, without refuge.’"

## Asammāsambuddhappaveditadhammavinayo

### A Dhamma-Vinaya Proclaimed by One Who Is Not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One

166. ‘‘Evaṃ hetaṃ, cunda, hoti durakkhāte dhammavinaye duppavedite aniyyānike anupasamasaṃvattanike asammāsambuddhappavedite. Idha, cunda, satthā ca hoti asammāsambuddho, dhammo ca durakkhāto duppavedito aniyyāniko anupasamasaṃvattaniko asammāsambuddhappavedito, sāvako ca tasmiṃ dhamme na dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharati na sāmīcippaṭipanno na anudhammacārī, vokkamma ca tamhā dhammā vattati. So evamassa vacanīyo – ‘tassa te, āvuso, lābhā, tassa te suladdhaṃ, satthā ca te asammāsambuddho, dhammo ca durakkhāto duppavedito aniyyāniko anupasamasaṃvattaniko asammāsambuddhappavedito. Tvañca tasmiṃ dhamme na dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharasi, na sāmīcippaṭipanno, na anudhammacārī, vokkamma ca tamhā dhammā vattasī’ti. Iti kho, cunda, satthāpi tattha gārayho, dhammopi tattha gārayho, sāvako ca tattha evaṃ pāsaṃso. Yo kho, cunda, evarūpaṃ sāvakaṃ evaṃ vadeyya – ‘etāyasmā tathā paṭipajjatu, yathā te satthārā dhammo desito paññatto’ti. Yo ca samādapeti [samādāpeti (sī. ṭṭha.)], yañca samādapeti, yo ca samādapito [samādāpito (sī. ṭṭha.)] tathattāya paṭipajjati. Sabbe te bahuṃ apuññaṃ pasavanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evaṃ hetaṃ, cunda, hoti durakkhāte dhammavinaye duppavedite aniyyānike anupasamasaṃvattanike asammāsambuddhappavedite.

166."So it is, Cunda, when the Dhamma and Discipline are poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, and proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. Here, Cunda, the teacher is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, and proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One; and the disciple in that Dhamma does not live practicing according to the Dhamma, does not practice properly, does not conduct himself in accordance with the Dhamma, and deviates from that Dhamma. One may say this to him: ‘It is to your gain, friend, it is well gained by you, that your teacher is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, and proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and you in that Dhamma do not live practicing according to the Dhamma, do not practice properly, do not conduct yourself in accordance with the Dhamma, and deviate from that Dhamma.’ Thus, Cunda, the teacher is blameworthy there, the Dhamma is blameworthy there, and the disciple is thus praiseworthy there. But whoever, Cunda, should say to such a disciple, ‘May the venerable one practice in such a way as the Dhamma has been taught and laid down by your teacher,’ and whoever persuades him, and whoever being persuaded practices accordingly—all of them generate much demerit. Why is that? Because so it is, Cunda, when the Dhamma and Discipline are poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, and proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.

167. ‘‘Idha pana, cunda, satthā ca hoti asammāsambuddho, dhammo ca durakkhāto duppavedito aniyyāniko anupasamasaṃvattaniko asammāsambuddhappavedito, sāvako ca tasmiṃ dhamme dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharati sāmīcippaṭipanno anudhammacārī, samādāya taṃ dhammaṃ vattati. So evamassa vacanīyo – ‘tassa te, āvuso, alābhā, tassa te dulladdhaṃ, satthā ca te asammāsambuddho, dhammo ca durakkhāto duppavedito aniyyāniko anupasamasaṃvattaniko asammāsambuddhappavedito. Tvañca tasmiṃ dhamme dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharasi sāmīcippaṭipanno anudhammacārī, samādāya taṃ dhammaṃ vattasī’ti. Iti kho, cunda, satthāpi tattha gārayho, dhammopi tattha gārayho, sāvakopi tattha evaṃ gārayho. Yo kho, cunda, evarūpaṃ sāvakaṃ evaṃ vadeyya – ‘addhāyasmā ñāyappaṭipanno ñāyamārādhessatī’ti. Yo ca pasaṃsati, yañca pasaṃsati, yo ca pasaṃsito bhiyyoso mattāya vīriyaṃ ārabhati. Sabbe te bahuṃ apuññaṃ pasavanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhetaṃ, cunda, hoti durakkhāte dhammavinaye duppavedite aniyyānike anupasamasaṃvattanike asammāsambuddhappavedite.

167."But here, Cunda, the teacher is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, and proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And the disciple in that Dhamma lives practicing according to the Dhamma, practicing properly, conducting himself in accordance with the Dhamma, having undertaken that Dhamma. He should be told: ‘You, friend, have lost out, you have gained badly, for your teacher is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, and proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And you in that Dhamma live practicing according to the Dhamma, practicing properly, conducting himself in accordance with the Dhamma, having undertaken that Dhamma.’ Thus, Cunda, the teacher is blameworthy there, the Dhamma is blameworthy there, and the disciple is also blameworthy there. But whoever, Cunda, should say to such a disciple: ‘Surely, the venerable one is practicing according to the method and will perfect the method,’ and whoever praises him, and whoever, being praised, exerts even greater effort—all of them generate much demerit. Why is that? Because so it is, Cunda, when the Dhamma and Discipline are poorly expounded, badly taught, not leading to liberation, not conducive to peace, and proclaimed by one who is not a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One."

## Sammāsambuddhappaveditadhammavinayo

### The Dhamma and Discipline well-proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.

168. ‘‘Idha pana, cunda, satthā ca hoti sammāsambuddho, dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito, sāvako ca tasmiṃ dhamme na dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharati, na sāmīcippaṭipanno, na anudhammacārī, vokkamma ca tamhā dhammā vattati. So evamassa vacanīyo – ‘tassa te, āvuso, alābhā, tassa te dulladdhaṃ, satthā ca te sammāsambuddho, dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito. Tvañca tasmiṃ dhamme na dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharasi, na sāmīcippaṭipanno, na anudhammacārī, vokkamma ca tamhā dhammā vattasī’ti. Iti kho, cunda, satthāpi tattha pāsaṃso, dhammopi tattha pāsaṃso, sāvako ca tattha evaṃ gārayho. Yo kho, cunda, evarūpaṃ sāvakaṃ evaṃ vadeyya – ‘etāyasmā tathā paṭipajjatu yathā te satthārā dhammo desito paññatto’ti. Yo ca samādapeti, yañca samādapeti, yo ca samādapito tathattāya paṭipajjati. Sabbe te bahuṃ puññaṃ pasavanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhetaṃ, cunda, hoti svākkhāte dhammavinaye suppavedite niyyānike upasamasaṃvattanike sammāsambuddhappavedite.

168.Here, Cunda, there is a Teacher who is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is well-proclaimed, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. Yet a disciple in that Dhamma does not live practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, does not practice properly, does not act according to the Dhamma, but acts contrary to that Dhamma. He should be spoken to thus: ‘It is your loss, friend, it is your misfortune that you have a Teacher who is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is well-proclaimed, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and yet you do not live practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, do not practice properly, do not act according to the Dhamma, but act contrary to that Dhamma.’ Thus, Cunda, the Teacher is to be praised there, the Dhamma is to be praised there, but the disciple is to be criticized in this way. Whoever, Cunda, would say to such a disciple: ‘Let the venerable one practice as the Dhamma has been taught and prescribed by the Teacher,’ and whoever encourages him, and the one whom he encourages, and the one who, being encouraged, practices accordingly—all of them generate much merit. Why is that? Because, Cunda, this is how it is in a well-proclaimed Dhamma and Discipline, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.

169. ‘‘Idha pana, cunda, satthā ca hoti sammāsambuddho, dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito, sāvako ca tasmiṃ dhamme dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharati sāmīcippaṭipanno anudhammacārī, samādāya taṃ dhammaṃ vattati. So evamassa vacanīyo – ‘tassa te, āvuso, lābhā, tassa te suladdhaṃ, satthā ca te [satthā ca te arahaṃ (syā.)] sammāsambuddho, dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito. Tvañca tasmiṃ dhamme dhammānudhammappaṭipanno viharasi sāmīcippaṭipanno anudhammacārī, samādāya taṃ dhammaṃ vattasī’ti. Iti kho, cunda, satthāpi tattha pāsaṃso, dhammopi tattha pāsaṃso, sāvakopi tattha evaṃ pāsaṃso. Yo kho, cunda, evarūpaṃ sāvakaṃ evaṃ vadeyya – ‘addhāyasmā ñāyappaṭipanno ñāyamārādhessatī’ti. Yo ca pasaṃsati, yañca pasaṃsati, yo ca pasaṃsito [pasattho (syā.)] bhiyyoso mattāya vīriyaṃ ārabhati. Sabbe te bahuṃ puññaṃ pasavanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Evañhetaṃ, cunda, hoti svākkhāte dhammavinaye suppavedite niyyānike upasamasaṃvattanike sammāsambuddhappavedite.

169.Here, Cunda, there is a Teacher who is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is well-proclaimed, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And a disciple in that Dhamma dwells practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, follows the proper way, acts in line with the Dhamma, and undertaking that Dhamma, lives accordingly. He should be spoken to thus: ‘It is your gain, friend, it is your good fortune that you have a Teacher who is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and the Dhamma is well-proclaimed, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And you dwell practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, follow the proper way, act in line with the Dhamma, and undertaking that Dhamma, live accordingly.’ Thus, Cunda, the Teacher is to be praised there, the Dhamma is to be praised there, and the disciple is also to be praised in this way. Whoever, Cunda, would say to such a disciple: ‘Surely, the venerable one is practicing the right way and will realize the right goal,’ and whoever praises him, and the one whom he praises, and the one who, being praised, strives even more diligently—all of them generate much merit. Why is that? Because, Cunda, this is how it is in a well-proclaimed Dhamma and Discipline, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One.

## Sāvakānutappasatthu

### The Teacher Who is a Cause of Regret for Disciples

170. ‘‘Idha pana, cunda, satthā ca loke udapādi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho, dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito, aviññāpitatthā cassa honti sāvakā saddhamme, na ca tesaṃ kevalaṃ paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ āvikataṃ hoti uttānīkataṃ sabbasaṅgāhapadakataṃ sappāṭihīrakataṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ. Atha nesaṃ satthuno antaradhānaṃ hoti. Evarūpo kho, cunda, satthā sāvakānaṃ kālaṅkato anutappo hoti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Satthā ca no loke udapādi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho, dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito, aviññāpitatthā camha saddhamme, na ca no kevalaṃ paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ āvikataṃ hoti uttānīkataṃ sabbasaṅgāhapadakataṃ sappāṭihīrakataṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ. Atha no satthuno antaradhānaṃ hotīti. Evarūpo kho, cunda, satthā sāvakānaṃ kālaṅkato anutappo hoti.

170.Here, Cunda, a Teacher has arisen in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And the Dhamma is well-proclaimed, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. But his disciples are not made to understand the meaning of the true Dhamma, and the complete, utterly pure spiritual life has not been revealed to them, made plain, with all its aspects explained, made convincing, well-proclaimed among gods and humans. Then the Teacher passes away. Such a Teacher, Cunda, when he has passed away, is a cause of regret for his disciples. Why is that? ‘A Teacher has arisen in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And the Dhamma is well-proclaimed, well-expounded, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. But we are not made to understand the meaning of the true Dhamma, and the complete, utterly pure spiritual life has not been revealed to us, made plain, with all its aspects explained, made convincing, well-proclaimed among gods and humans. Then the Teacher passes away.’ Such a Teacher, Cunda, when he has passed away, is a cause of regret for his disciples.

## Sāvakānanutappasatthu

### The Teacher Not to Be Lamented by Disciples

171. ‘‘Idha pana, cunda, satthā ca loke udapādi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho. Dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito. Viññāpitatthā cassa honti sāvakā saddhamme, kevalañca tesaṃ paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ āvikataṃ hoti uttānīkataṃ sabbasaṅgāhapadakataṃ sappāṭihīrakataṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ. Atha nesaṃ satthuno antaradhānaṃ hoti. Evarūpo kho, cunda, satthā sāvakānaṃ kālaṅkato ananutappo hoti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Satthā ca no loke udapādi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho. Dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito. Viññāpitatthā camha saddhamme, kevalañca no paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ āvikataṃ hoti uttānīkataṃ sabbasaṅgāhapadakataṃ sappāṭihīrakataṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ. Atha no satthuno antaradhānaṃ hotīti. Evarūpo kho, cunda, satthā sāvakānaṃ kālaṅkato ananutappo hoti.

171."Here, Cunda, a Teacher has arisen in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. The Dhamma is well-expounded, well-proclaimed, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And his disciples are instructed in the meaning of the True Dhamma, and for them the holy life has been fully revealed, made plain, comprehensive in all its aspects, with its marvels, well-proclaimed among devas and humans. Then the Teacher passes away. Such a Teacher, Cunda, who has passed away, is not to be lamented by his disciples. For what reason? Because a Teacher has arisen in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. The Dhamma is well-expounded, well-proclaimed, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. We are instructed in the meaning of the True Dhamma, and for us the holy life has been fully revealed, made plain, comprehensive in all its aspects, with its marvels, well-proclaimed among devas and humans. Then the Teacher passes away. Such a Teacher, Cunda, who has passed away, is not to be lamented by his disciples.

## Brahmacariyaaparipūrādikathā

### The Discourse on the Incomplete Holy Life, and So Forth

172. ‘‘Etehi cepi, cunda, aṅgehi samannāgataṃ brahmacariyaṃ hoti, no ca kho satthā hoti thero rattaññū cirapabbajito addhagato vayoanuppatto. Evaṃ taṃ brahmacariyaṃ aparipūraṃ hoti tenaṅgena.

172."Even if, Cunda, the holy life is endowed with these factors, but there is no Teacher who is an elder, long experienced, long gone forth, advanced in years, having reached old age, then that holy life is incomplete in that respect."

‘‘Yato ca kho, cunda, etehi ceva aṅgehi samannāgataṃ brahmacariyaṃ hoti, satthā ca hoti thero rattaññū cirapabbajito addhagato vayoanuppatto. Evaṃ taṃ brahmacariyaṃ paripūraṃ hoti tenaṅgena.

"Furthermore, Cunda, when the holy life is endowed with these factors, and there is a Teacher who is an elder, long experienced, long gone forth, advanced in years, having reached old age, then that holy life is complete in that respect."

173. ‘‘Etehi cepi, cunda, aṅgehi samannāgataṃ brahmacariyaṃ hoti, satthā ca hoti thero rattaññū cirapabbajito addhagato vayoanuppatto, no ca khvassa therā bhikkhū sāvakā honti viyattā vinītā visāradā pattayogakkhemā. Alaṃ samakkhātuṃ saddhammassa, alaṃ uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammehi suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desetuṃ. Evaṃ taṃ brahmacariyaṃ aparipūraṃ hoti tenaṅgena.

174. ‘‘Etehi cepi, cunda, aṅgehi samannāgataṃ brahmacariyaṃ hoti, satthā ca hoti thero rattaññū cirapabbajito addhagato vayoanuppatto, therā cassa bhikkhū sāvakā honti viyattā vinītā visāradā pattayogakkhemā. Alaṃ samakkhātuṃ saddhammassa, alaṃ uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammehi suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desetuṃ. No ca khvassa majjhimā bhikkhū sāvakā honti…pe… majjhimā cassa bhikkhū sāvakā honti, no ca khvassa navā bhikkhū sāvakā honti…pe… navā cassa bhikkhū sāvakā honti, no ca khvassa therā bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti…pe… therā cassa bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti, no ca khvassa majjhimā bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti…pe… majjhimā cassa bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti, no ca khvassa navā bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti…pe… navā cassa bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti, no ca khvassa upāsakā sāvakā honti gihī odātavasanā brahmacārino…pe… upāsakā cassa sāvakā honti gihī odātavasanā brahmacārino, no ca khvassa upāsakā sāvakā honti gihī odātavasanā kāmabhogino…pe… upāsakā cassa sāvakā honti gihī odātavasanā kāmabhogino, no ca khvassa upāsikā sāvikā honti gihiniyo odātavasanā brahmacāriniyo…pe… upāsikā cassa sāvikā honti gihiniyo odātavasanā brahmacāriniyo, no ca khvassa upāsikā sāvikā honti gihiniyo odātavasanā kāmabhoginiyo…pe… upāsikā cassa sāvikā honti gihiniyo odātavasanā kāmabhoginiyo, no ca khvassa brahmacariyaṃ hoti iddhañceva phītañca vitthārikaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ…pe… brahmacariyañcassa hoti iddhañceva phītañca vitthārikaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ, no ca kho lābhaggayasaggappattaṃ. ‘‘Yato ca kho, cunda, etehi ceva aṅgehi samannāgataṃ brahmacariyaṃ hoti, satthā ca hoti thero rattaññū cirapabbajito addhagato vayoanuppatto, therā cassa bhikkhū sāvakā honti viyattā vinītā visāradā pattayogakkhemā. Alaṃ samakkhātuṃ saddhammassa, alaṃ uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammehi suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desetuṃ. Majjhimā cassa bhikkhū sāvakā honti…pe… navā cassa bhikkhū sāvakā honti…pe… therā cassa bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti…pe… majjhimā cassa bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti…pe… navā cassa bhikkhuniyo sāvikā honti…pe… upāsakā cassa sāvakā honti…pe… gihī odātavasanā brahmacārino. Upāsakā cassa sāvakā honti gihī odātavasanā kāmabhogino…pe… upāsikā cassa sāvikā honti gihiniyo odātavasanā brahmacāriniyo…pe… upāsikā cassa sāvikā honti gihiniyo odātavasanā kāmabhoginiyo…pe… brahmacariyañcassa hoti iddhañceva phītañca vitthārikaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ, lābhaggappattañca yasaggappattañca. Evaṃ taṃ brahmacariyaṃ paripūraṃ hoti tenaṅgena.

"Furthermore, Cunda, when the holy life is endowed with these factors, and there is a Teacher who is an elder, long experienced, long gone forth, advanced in years, having reached old age, and his elder monk disciples are accomplished, disciplined, confident, who have attained security from bondage, capable of explaining the True Dhamma, capable of thoroughly refuting any arisen opposing doctrines with the Dhamma, and capable of teaching the Dhamma with its marvels. And his middle monk disciples are such... and his newly ordained monk disciples are such... and his elder nun disciples are such... and his middle nun disciples are such... and his newly ordained nun disciples are such... and his male lay disciples—householders in white clothes who live the holy life—are such... and his male lay disciples—householders in white clothes who enjoy sensual pleasures—are such... and his female lay disciples—householders in white clothes who live the holy life—are such... and his female lay disciples—householders in white clothes who enjoy sensual pleasures—are such... and the holy life is prosperous and flourishing, extensive, widely known, become numerous, well-proclaimed among devas and humans, and has attained the summit of gain and renown—then that holy life is complete in that respect."

175. ‘‘Ahaṃ kho pana, cunda, etarahi satthā loke uppanno arahaṃ sammāsambuddho. Dhammo ca svākkhāto suppavedito niyyāniko upasamasaṃvattaniko sammāsambuddhappavedito. Viññāpitatthā ca me sāvakā saddhamme, kevalañca tesaṃ paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ āvikataṃ uttānīkataṃ sabbasaṅgāhapadakataṃ sappāṭihīrakataṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ. Ahaṃ kho pana, cunda, etarahi satthā thero rattaññū cirapabbajito addhagato vayoanuppatto.

175."But now, Cunda, I have arisen in the world as a Teacher, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And the Dhamma is well-expounded, well-proclaimed, leading to deliverance, conducive to peace, proclaimed by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One. And my disciples are instructed in the meaning of the True Dhamma, and for them the holy life is complete, made manifest, made plain, comprehensive in all its aspects, with its marvels, well-proclaimed among devas and humans. And now, Cunda, I am the Teacher, an elder, long experienced, long gone forth, advanced in years, having reached old age."

‘‘Santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi therā bhikkhū sāvakā honti viyattā vinītā visāradā pattayogakkhemā. Alaṃ samakkhātuṃ saddhammassa, alaṃ uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammehi suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desetuṃ. Santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi majjhimā bhikkhū sāvakā…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi navā bhikkhū sāvakā…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi therā bhikkhuniyo sāvikā…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi majjhimā bhikkhuniyo sāvikā…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi navā bhikkhuniyo sāvikā…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi upāsakā sāvakā gihī odātavasanā brahmacārino…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi upāsakā sāvakā gihī odātavasanā kāmabhogino…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi upāsikā sāvikā gihiniyo odātavasanā brahmacāriniyo…pe… santi kho pana me, cunda, etarahi upāsikā sāvikā gihiniyo odātavasanā kāmabhoginiyo…pe… etarahi kho pana me, cunda, brahmacariyaṃ iddhañceva phītañca vitthārikaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ.

Now, Cunda, I have elder bhikkhus, my disciples, who are accomplished, disciplined, confident, and have attained security from bondage. They are capable of expounding the true Dhamma, capable of thoroughly refuting any opposing doctrines that arise by means of the Dhamma, and capable of teaching the Dhamma with its marvel. Now, Cunda, I have middle bhikkhus, my disciples… newly ordained bhikkhus, my disciples… elder bhikkhunis, my disciples… middle bhikkhunis, my disciples… newly ordained bhikkhunis, my disciples… male lay disciples—householders wearing white clothes, leading the holy life… male lay disciples—householders wearing white clothes, enjoying sensual pleasures… female lay disciples—female householders wearing white clothes, leading the holy life… female lay disciples—female householders wearing white clothes, enjoying sensual pleasures… And now, Cunda, my holy life is prosperous and flourishing, widespread, well-populated, and extensive, and it is well-proclaimed among gods and humans.

176. ‘‘Yāvatā kho, cunda, etarahi satthāro loke uppannā, nāhaṃ, cunda, aññaṃ ekasatthārampi samanupassāmi evaṃlābhaggayasaggappattaṃ yatharivāhaṃ. Yāvatā kho pana, cunda, etarahi saṅgho vā gaṇo vā loke uppanno; nāhaṃ, cunda, aññaṃ ekaṃ saṃghampi samanupassāmi evaṃlābhaggayasaggappattaṃ yatharivāyaṃ, cunda, bhikkhusaṅgho. Yaṃ kho taṃ, cunda, sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘sabbākārasampannaṃ sabbākāraparipūraṃ anūnamanadhikaṃ svākkhātaṃ kevalaṃ paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ suppakāsita’nti. Idameva taṃ sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘sabbākārasampannaṃ…pe… suppakāsita’nti.

176.As far as, Cunda, teachers have arisen in the world at this time, I do not see, Cunda, even one other teacher who has attained such gain, honor, and renown as I have. As far as, Cunda, a Saṅgha or a group has arisen in the world at this time, I do not see, Cunda, even one other Saṅgha that has attained such gain, honor, and renown as this Bhikkhu Saṅgha, Cunda. That which, Cunda, one speaking rightly would say: ‘The holy life is perfect in all aspects, complete in all aspects, neither deficient nor excessive, well-proclaimed, entirely complete, and perfectly revealed’—this indeed is what one speaking rightly would say: ‘The holy life is perfect in all aspects… and perfectly revealed.’

‘‘Udako [uddako (sī. syā. pī.)] sudaṃ, cunda, rāmaputto evaṃ vācaṃ bhāsati – ‘passaṃ na passatī’ti. Kiñca passaṃ na passatīti? Khurassa sādhunisitassa talamassa passati, dhārañca khvassa na passati. Idaṃ vuccati – ‘passaṃ na passatī’ti. Yaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, udakena rāmaputtena bhāsitaṃ hīnaṃ gammaṃ pothujjanikaṃ anariyaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ khurameva sandhāya. Yañca taṃ [yañcetaṃ (syā. ka.)], cunda, sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘passaṃ na passatī’ti, idameva taṃ [idamevetaṃ (ka.)] sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘passaṃ na passatī’ti. Kiñca passaṃ na passatīti? Evaṃ sabbākārasampannaṃ sabbākāraparipūraṃ anūnamanadhikaṃ svākkhātaṃ kevalaṃ paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ suppakāsitanti, iti hetaṃ passati [suppakāsitaṃ, iti hetaṃ na passatīti (syā. ka.)]. Idamettha apakaḍḍheyya, evaṃ taṃ parisuddhataraṃ assāti, iti hetaṃ na passati [na passatīti (syā. ka.)]. Idamettha upakaḍḍheyya, evaṃ taṃ paripūraṃ [parisuddhataraṃ (syā. ka.), paripūrataraṃ (?)] assāti, iti hetaṃ na passati. Idaṃ vuccati cunda – ‘passaṃ na passatī’ti. Yaṃ kho taṃ, cunda, sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘sabbākārasampannaṃ…pe… brahmacariyaṃ suppakāsita’nti. Idameva taṃ sammā vadamāno vadeyya – ‘sabbākārasampannaṃ sabbākāraparipūraṃ anūnamanadhikaṃ svākkhātaṃ kevalaṃ paripūraṃ brahmacariyaṃ suppakāsita’nti.

Udaka, Cunda, the son of Rama, used to say this: ‘Seeing, one does not see.’ And what is it that seeing, one does not see? He sees the surface of a well-honed razor, but he does not see its edge. This is called: ‘Seeing, one does not see.’ But that, Cunda, which was spoken by Udaka, the son of Rama, is low, vulgar, commonplace, ignoble, and unbeneficial—merely concerning a razor. That which, Cunda, one speaking rightly would say—‘Seeing, one does not see’—this indeed is what one speaking rightly would say—‘Seeing, one does not see.’ And what is it that seeing, one does not see? He sees thus: ‘The holy life is perfect in all aspects, complete in all aspects, neither deficient nor excessive, well-proclaimed, entirely complete, and perfectly revealed.’ But he does not see this: ‘This should be removed here, thus it would be purer.’ He does not see this: ‘This should be added here, thus it would be complete.’ This is called, Cunda: ‘Seeing, one does not see.’ That which, Cunda, one speaking rightly would say—‘The holy life is perfect in all aspects… and perfectly revealed’—this indeed is what one speaking rightly would say: ‘The holy life is perfect in all aspects, complete in all aspects, neither deficient nor excessive, well-proclaimed, entirely complete, and perfectly revealed.’

## Saṅgāyitabbadhammo

### The Dhamma for Recitation.

177. Tasmātiha, cunda, ye vo mayā dhammā abhiññā desitā, tattha sabbeheva saṅgamma samāgamma atthena atthaṃ byañjanena byañjanaṃ saṅgāyitabbaṃ na vivaditabbaṃ, yathayidaṃ brahmacariyaṃ addhaniyaṃ assa ciraṭṭhitikaṃ, tadassa bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ. Katame ca te, cunda, dhammā mayā abhiññā desitā, yattha sabbeheva saṅgamma samāgamma atthena atthaṃ byañjanena byañjanaṃ saṅgāyitabbaṃ na vivaditabbaṃ, yathayidaṃ brahmacariyaṃ addhaniyaṃ assa ciraṭṭhitikaṃ, tadassa bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ? Seyyathidaṃ – cattāro satipaṭṭhānā, cattāro sammappadhānā, cattāro iddhipādā, pañcindriyāni, pañca balāni, satta bojjhaṅgā, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo. Ime kho te, cunda, dhammā mayā abhiññā desitā. Yattha sabbeheva saṅgamma samāgamma atthena atthaṃ byañjanena byañjanaṃ saṅgāyitabbaṃ na vivaditabbaṃ, yathayidaṃ brahmacariyaṃ addhaniyaṃ assa ciraṭṭhitikaṃ, tadassa bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ.

177.Therefore, Cunda, regarding those teachings I have directly known and taught, you should all gather together and recite them in concord—without dispute—comparing meaning with meaning and phrasing with phrasing, so that this holy life may endure for a long time, for the welfare and happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans. And what, Cunda, are those teachings I have directly known and taught, where you should all gather together and recite them in concord—without dispute—comparing meaning with meaning and phrasing with phrasing, so that this holy life may endure for a long time, for the welfare and happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans? They are as follows: the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right exertions, the four bases of spiritual power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, and the Noble Eightfold Path. These, Cunda, are the teachings I have directly known and taught, where you should all gather together and recite them in concord—without dispute—comparing meaning with meaning and phrasing with phrasing, so that this holy life may endure for a long time, for the welfare and happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans.

## Saññāpetabbavidhi

### The Procedure for Announcing

178. ‘‘Tesañca vo, cunda, samaggānaṃ sammodamānānaṃ avivadamānānaṃ sikkhataṃ [sikkhitabbaṃ (bahūsu)] aññataro sabrahmacārī saṅghe dhammaṃ bhāseyya. Tatra ce tumhākaṃ evamassa – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā atthañceva micchā gaṇhāti, byañjanāni ca micchā ropetī’ti. Tassa neva abhinanditabbaṃ na paṭikkositabbaṃ, anabhinanditvā appaṭikkositvā so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘imassa nu kho, āvuso, atthassa imāni vā byañjanāni etāni vā byañjanāni katamāni opāyikatarāni, imesañca [imesaṃ vā (syā. pī. ka.), imesaṃ (sī.)] byañjanānaṃ ayaṃ vā attho eso vā attho katamo opāyikataro’ti? So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘imassa kho, āvuso, atthassa imāneva byañjanāni opāyikatarāni, yā ceva [yañceva (sī. ka.), ṭīkā oloketabbā] etāni; imesañca [imedaṃ (sabbattha)] byañjanānaṃ ayameva attho opāyikataro, yā ceva [yañceva (sī. ka.), ṭīkā oloketabbā] eso’ti. So neva ussādetabbo na apasādetabbo, anussādetvā anapasādetvā sveva sādhukaṃ saññāpetabbo tassa ca atthassa tesañca byañjanānaṃ nisantiyā.

178.“Now, Cunda, when you are united, in harmony, without dispute, and training, one of your fellow practitioners may give a talk on the Dhamma in the Saṅgha. If you think, ‘This venerable one misunderstands the meaning and misapplies the expressions,’ you should neither approve nor reject it. Without approving or rejecting it, you should say to him, ‘Friend, regarding this meaning, which of these expressions is more suitable—these or those? And regarding these expressions, which of these meanings is more suitable—this or that?’ If he replies, ‘Friend, for this meaning, these very expressions are more suitable, namely these very ones; and for these expressions, this very meaning is more suitable, namely this very one,’ then you should neither exalt nor denigrate him. Without exalting or denigrating him, you should get him to thoroughly understand it himself for the settling on that meaning and those expressions.

179. ‘‘Aparopi ce, cunda, sabrahmacārī saṅghe dhammaṃ bhāseyya. Tatra ce tumhākaṃ evamassa – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā atthañhi kho micchā gaṇhāti byañjanāni sammā ropetī’ti. Tassa neva abhinanditabbaṃ na paṭikkositabbaṃ, anabhinanditvā appaṭikkositvā so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘imesaṃ nu kho, āvuso, byañjanānaṃ ayaṃ vā attho eso vā attho katamo opāyikataro’ti? So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘imesaṃ kho, āvuso, byañjanānaṃ ayameva attho opāyikataro, yā ceva eso’ti. So neva ussādetabbo na apasādetabbo, anussādetvā anapasādetvā sveva sādhukaṃ saññāpetabbo tasseva atthassa nisantiyā.

179.“Furthermore, Cunda, another fellow practitioner might speak on the Dhamma in the Saṅgha. If you think, ‘This venerable one grasps the meaning wrongly, but arranges the expressions correctly,’ you should neither approve nor reject it. Without approving or rejecting, you should say to him, ‘Friend, of these expressions, which meaning is more suitable—this one or that one?’ If he replies, ‘Friend, of these expressions, this very meaning is more suitable, namely this very one,’ then you should neither exalt nor denigrate him. Without exalting or denigrating, you should get him to thoroughly understand it himself for the settling on that very meaning.

180. ‘‘Aparopi ce, cunda, sabrahmacārī saṅghe dhammaṃ bhāseyya. Tatra ce tumhākaṃ evamassa – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā atthañhi kho sammā gaṇhāti byañjanāni micchā ropetī’ti. Tassa neva abhinanditabbaṃ na paṭikkositabbaṃ; anabhinanditvā appaṭikkositvā so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘imassa nu kho, āvuso, atthassa imāni vā byañjanāni etāni vā byañjanāni katamāni opāyikatarānī’ti? So ce evaṃ vadeyya – ‘imassa kho, āvuso, atthassa imāneva byañjanāni opayikatarāni, yāni ceva etānī’ti. So neva ussādetabbo na apasādetabbo; anussādetvā anapasādetvā sveva sādhukaṃ saññāpetabbo tesaññeva byañjanānaṃ nisantiyā.

180.“Furthermore, Cunda, if another fellow practitioner should speak the Dhamma in the Saṅgha, and you should think: ‘This venerable one grasps the meaning correctly but misapplies the expressions,’ you should neither approve nor reject him. Without approving or rejecting, you should say to him: ‘Friend, regarding this meaning, which expressions are more suitable, these or those?’ If he should say: ‘Friend, regarding this meaning, these very expressions are more suitable, namely these very ones,’ then you should neither exalt him nor denigrate him. Without exalting or denigrating him, you should get him to thoroughly understand it himself for the settling on those very expressions.

181. ‘‘Aparopi ce, cunda, sabrahmacārī saṅghe dhammaṃ bhāseyya. Tatra ce tumhākaṃ evamassa – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā atthañceva sammā gaṇhāti byañjanāni ca sammā ropetī’ti. Tassa ‘sādhū’ti bhāsitaṃ abhinanditabbaṃ anumoditabbaṃ; tassa ‘sādhū’ti bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā so evamassa vacanīyo – ‘lābhā no āvuso, suladdhaṃ no āvuso, ye mayaṃ āyasmantaṃ tādisaṃ sabrahmacāriṃ passāma evaṃ atthupetaṃ byañjanupeta’nti.

181.“Furthermore, Cunda, if another fellow practitioner were to speak the Dhamma in the Saṅgha, and you were to think: ‘This venerable one grasps the meaning correctly and arranges the expressions correctly.’ His statement should be approved and rejoiced over, saying: ‘Good!’ Having approved and rejoiced over his statement, you should say to him: ‘It is our gain, friend, it is our great gain that we see such a fellow practitioner who is endowed with meaning and expressions.’

Paccayānuññātakāraṇaṃ

The Reason for the Allowance of Requisites

182. ‘‘Na vo ahaṃ, cunda, diṭṭhadhammikānaṃyeva āsavānaṃ saṃvarāya dhammaṃ desemi. Na panāhaṃ, cunda, samparāyikānaṃyeva āsavānaṃ paṭighātāya dhammaṃ desemi. Diṭṭhadhammikānaṃ cevāhaṃ, cunda, āsavānaṃ saṃvarāya dhammaṃ desemi; samparāyikānañca āsavānaṃ paṭighātāya. Tasmātiha, cunda, yaṃ vo mayā cīvaraṃ anuññātaṃ, alaṃ vo taṃ – yāvadeva sītassa paṭighātāya, uṇhassa paṭighātāya, ḍaṃsamakasavātātapasarīsapa [siriṃsapa (syā.)] samphassānaṃ paṭighātāya, yāvadeva hirikopīnapaṭicchādanatthaṃ. Yo vo mayā piṇḍapāto anuññāto, alaṃ vo so yāvadeva imassa kāyassa ṭhitiyā yāpanāya vihiṃsūparatiyā brahmacariyānuggahāya, iti purāṇañca vedanaṃ paṭihaṅkhāmi, navañca vedanaṃ na uppādessāmi, yātrā ca me bhavissati anavajjatā ca phāsuvihāro ca [cāti (bahūsu)]. Yaṃ vo mayā senāsanaṃ anuññātaṃ, alaṃ vo taṃ yāvadeva sītassa paṭighātāya, uṇhassa paṭighātāya, ḍaṃsamakasavātātapasarīsapasamphassānaṃ paṭighātāya, yāvadeva utuparissayavinodana paṭisallānārāmatthaṃ. Yo vo mayā gilānapaccayabhesajja parikkhāro anuññāto, alaṃ vo so yāvadeva uppannānaṃ veyyābādhikānaṃ vedanānaṃ paṭighātāya abyāpajjaparamatāya [abyāpajjhaparamatāyāti (sī. syā. pī.), abyābajjhaparamatāya (?)].

182.“I do not teach the Dhamma, Cunda, merely for the restraint of taints pertaining to this life. Nor do I teach the Dhamma, Cunda, merely for the warding off of taints pertaining to future lives. I teach the Dhamma, Cunda, both for the restraint of taints pertaining to this life and for the warding off of taints pertaining to future lives. Therefore, Cunda, the robe that I have allowed you is sufficient—just for warding off cold, warding off heat, warding off the touch of flies, mosquitoes, wind, sun, and creeping things, and for covering the private parts. The alms-food that I have allowed you is sufficient—just for the maintenance of this body, for its sustenance, for the cessation of discomfort, for assisting the holy life, thinking: ‘Thus, I will ward off old feelings, and I will not generate new feelings, and I will maintain myself, be blameless, and live in comfort.’ The lodging that I have allowed you is sufficient—just for warding off cold, warding off heat, warding off the touch of flies, mosquitoes, wind, sun, and creeping things, for eliminating the dangers of the seasons, and for the pleasure of seclusion. The medicines and provisions for the sick that I have allowed you are sufficient—just for warding off arisen painful feelings, and for the ultimate freedom from disease.”

## Sukhallikānuyogo

### Devotion to Pleasure

183. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘sukhallikānuyogamanuyuttā samaṇā sakyaputtiyā viharantī’ti. Evaṃvādino [vadamānā (syā.)], cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘katamo so, āvuso, sukhallikānuyogo? Sukhallikānuyogā hi bahū anekavihitā nānappakārakā’ti.

183."It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say this: 'The recluses, the Sakyan sons, live devoted to the pursuit of pleasure.' When they say this, Cunda, wanderers of other sects should be addressed thus: 'What, friends, is that pursuit of pleasure? For pursuits of pleasure are diverse, of many sorts and various kinds.'"

‘‘Cattārome, cunda, sukhallikānuyogā hīnā gammā pothujjanikā anariyā anatthasaṃhitā na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattanti. Katame cattāro?

"Cunda, these four are pursuits of sensual pleasure that are low, coarse, vulgar, ignoble, unbeneficial, and not conducive to revulsion, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, or to Nibbāna. What four?

‘‘Idha, cunda, ekacco bālo pāṇe vadhitvā vadhitvā attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti. Ayaṃ paṭhamo sukhallikānuyogo.

"Here, Cunda, some fool delights and indulges himself by killing living beings. This is the first pursuit of pleasure."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, cunda, idhekacco adinnaṃ ādiyitvā ādiyitvā attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti. Ayaṃ dutiyo sukhallikānuyogo.

Again, Cunda, someone here takes what is not given and thereby makes himself happy and pleased. This is the second pursuit of pleasure.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, cunda, idhekacco musā bhaṇitvā bhaṇitvā attānaṃ sukheti pīṇeti. Ayaṃ tatiyo sukhallikānuyogo.

"Furthermore, Cunda, someone here delights and gratifies himself by speaking falsehood. This is the third pursuit of pleasure."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, cunda, idhekacco pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappito samaṅgībhūto paricāreti. Ayaṃ catuttho sukhallikānuyogo.

"Furthermore, Cunda, someone here indulges in and is devoted to the enjoyment of the five sense pleasures. This is the fourth pursuit of pleasure."

‘‘Ime kho, cunda, cattāro sukhallikānuyogā hīnā gammā pothujjanikā anariyā anatthasaṃhitā na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattanti.

"These, Cunda, are the four pursuits of sensual pleasures, which are low, coarse, for ordinary people, ignoble, unbeneficial, and do not lead to disenchantment, dispassion, cessation, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, or Nibbāna."

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘ime cattāro sukhallikānuyoge anuyuttā samaṇā sakyaputtiyā viharantī’ti. Te vo [te (sī. pī.)] ‘māhevaṃ’ tissu vacanīyā. Na te vo sammā vadamānā vadeyyuṃ, abbhācikkheyyuṃ asatā abhūtena.

"It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say: 'The recluses, the Sakyan sons, live devoted to these four pursuits of pleasure.' You should refute them by saying, 'Do not say so!' They are not speaking rightly about you; they are falsely accusing you with what is untrue and baseless."

184. ‘‘Cattārome, cunda, sukhallikānuyogā ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattanti. Katame cattāro?

184."Cunda, there are these four practices of devotion to happiness that lead solely to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, to Nibbāna. What four?

‘‘Idha, cunda, bhikkhu vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ paṭhamo sukhallikānuyogo.

"Here, Cunda, a monk, quite secluded from sensual pleasures and unwholesome states, enters and dwells in the first jhāna, which is accompanied by thought and examination, born of seclusion, filled with rapture and joy. This is the first devotion to happiness."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, cunda, bhikkhu vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā…pe… dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ dutiyo sukhallikānuyogo.

"Furthermore, Cunda, with the stilling of thought and examination, a monk enters and dwells in the second jhāna, which has inner tranquility and unification of mind, is born of samādhi, and is without thought and examination. This is the second devotion to happiness."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, cunda, bhikkhu pītiyā ca virāgā…pe… tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ tatiyo sukhallikānuyogo.

Furthermore, Cunda, with the fading away of rapture, a monk dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly aware, experiencing pleasure with the body, and enters and dwells in the third jhāna. This is the third devotion to happiness.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, cunda, bhikkhu sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā…pe… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ catuttho sukhallikānuyogo.

Furthermore, Cunda, with the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and the previous disappearance of joy and grief, a monk enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna. This is the fourth devotion to happiness.

‘‘Ime kho, cunda, cattāro sukhallikānuyogā ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattanti.

"These, Cunda, are the four practices for devotion to happiness that lead solely to disenchantment, dispassion, cessation, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, and Nibbāna."

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘ime cattāro sukhallikānuyoge anuyuttā samaṇā sakyaputtiyā viharantī’ti. Te vo ‘evaṃ’ tissu vacanīyā. Sammā te vo vadamānā vadeyyuṃ, na te vo abbhācikkheyyuṃ asatā abhūtena.

"It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say: 'The recluses, the Sakyan sons, live devoted to these four practices of devotion to happiness.' You should say to them, 'Indeed!' They would be speaking rightly about you; they would not be falsely accusing you with what is untrue and baseless."

## Sukhallikānuyogānisaṃso

### The Benefits of Devotion to Sensual Pleasures

185. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ime panāvuso, cattāro sukhallikānuyoge anuyuttānaṃ viharataṃ kati phalāni katānisaṃsā pāṭikaṅkhā’ti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘ime kho, āvuso, cattāro sukhallikānuyoge anuyuttānaṃ viharataṃ cattāri phalāni cattāro ānisaṃsā pāṭikaṅkhā. Katame cattāro? Idhāvuso, bhikkhu tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sotāpanno hoti avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo. Idaṃ paṭhamaṃ phalaṃ, paṭhamo ānisaṃso. Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, bhikkhu tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmī hoti, sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Idaṃ dutiyaṃ phalaṃ, dutiyo ānisaṃso. Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, bhikkhu pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā opapātiko hoti, tattha parinibbāyī anāvattidhammo tasmā lokā. Idaṃ tatiyaṃ phalaṃ, tatiyo ānisaṃso. Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, bhikkhu āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Idaṃ catutthaṃ phalaṃ catuttho ānisaṃso. Ime kho, āvuso, cattāro sukhallikānuyoge anuyuttānaṃ viharataṃ imāni cattāri phalāni, cattāro ānisaṃsā pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti.

185."It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say: 'Friends, those who pursue these four kinds of devotion to sensual pleasures—what fruits and benefits can they expect?' Wanderers of other sects who speak thus should be told: 'Friends, those who pursue these four kinds of devotion to sensual pleasures can expect four fruits and four benefits. What are the four? Here, friend, a bhikkhu, with the destruction of three fetters, becomes a stream-enterer, no longer subject to downfall, assured, destined for enlightenment. This is the first fruit, the first benefit. Furthermore, friend, a bhikkhu, with the destruction of three fetters and the attenuation of greed, hatred, and delusion, becomes a once-returner, who returns to this world only once before making an end of suffering. This is the second fruit, the second benefit. Furthermore, friend, a bhikkhu, with the destruction of the five lower fetters, is reborn spontaneously and attains final Nibbāna there, not liable to return from that world. This is the third fruit, the third benefit. Furthermore, friend, a bhikkhu, through the destruction of the taints, having directly known and realized for himself with direct knowledge, here and now enters and dwells in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom. This is the fourth fruit, the fourth benefit. These, friend, are the four fruits and four benefits that those who pursue these four kinds of devotion to sensual pleasures can expect.'"

## Khīṇāsavaabhabbaṭhānaṃ

### The Incapacity of One Whose Taints Are Destroyed

186. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘aṭṭhitadhammā samaṇā sakyaputtiyā viharantī’ti. Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘atthi kho, āvuso, tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena sāvakānaṃ dhammā desitā paññattā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyā. Seyyathāpi, āvuso, indakhīlo vā ayokhīlo vā gambhīranemo sunikhāto acalo asampavedhī. Evameva kho, āvuso, tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena sāvakānaṃ dhammā desitā paññattā yāvajīvaṃ anatikkamanīyā. Yo so, āvuso, bhikkhu arahaṃ khīṇāsavo vusitavā katakaraṇīyo ohitabhāro anuppattasadattho parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojano sammadaññā vimutto, abhabbo so nava ṭhānāni ajjhācarituṃ. Abhabbo, āvuso, khīṇāsavo bhikkhu sañcicca pāṇaṃ jīvitā voropetuṃ; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyituṃ; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevituṃ; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu sampajānamusā bhāsituṃ; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu sannidhikārakaṃ kāme paribhuñjituṃ seyyathāpi pubbe āgārikabhūto; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu chandāgatiṃ gantuṃ; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu dosāgatiṃ gantuṃ; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu mohāgatiṃ gantuṃ; abhabbo khīṇāsavo bhikkhu bhayāgatiṃ gantuṃ. Yo so, āvuso, bhikkhu arahaṃ khīṇāsavo vusitavā katakaraṇīyo ohitabhāro anuppattasadattho parikkhīṇabhavasaṃyojano sammadaññā vimutto, abhabbo so imāni nava ṭhānāni ajjhācaritu’’nti.

186."It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say: 'The ascetics who follow the Sakyan son live without a stable doctrine.' When they say this, Cunda, the wanderers of other sects should be told: 'There is, friends, a doctrine taught and prescribed by the Blessed One, the knower and seer, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, for his disciples, which they must not transgress as long as they live. Just as, friends, an Indrakhila pillar or an iron pillar, deeply embedded and firmly fixed, stands unshaken and immovable, so too, friends, the doctrine taught and prescribed by the Blessed One, the knower and seer, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, for his disciples, which they must not transgress as long as they live. A monk, friends, who is an Arahant, one whose taints are destroyed, who has lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, reached his own goal, utterly destroyed the fetters of existence, and is completely liberated through final knowledge—such a monk is incapable of committing nine acts. It is impossible, friends, for a monk whose taints are destroyed to intentionally deprive a living being of life. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to take what is not given, in what is called theft. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to engage in sexual activity. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to knowingly speak a falsehood. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to enjoy sensual pleasures by accumulating goods, as he did formerly when he was a householder. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to follow a wrong course through desire. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to follow a wrong course through hatred. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to follow a wrong course through delusion. It is impossible for a monk whose taints are destroyed to follow a wrong course through fear. A monk, friends, who is an Arahant, one whose taints are destroyed, who has lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, reached his own goal, utterly destroyed the fetters of existence, and is completely liberated through final knowledge—such a monk is incapable of committing these nine acts.'"

## Pañhābyākaraṇaṃ

### The Elucidation of Questions

187. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘atītaṃ kho addhānaṃ ārabbha samaṇo gotamo atīrakaṃ ñāṇadassanaṃ paññapeti, no ca kho anāgataṃ addhānaṃ ārabbha atīrakaṃ ñāṇadassanaṃ paññapeti, tayidaṃ kiṃsu tayidaṃ kathaṃsū’ti? Te ca aññatitthiyā paribbājakā aññavihitakena ñāṇadassanena aññavihitakaṃ ñāṇadassanaṃ paññapetabbaṃ maññanti yathariva bālā abyattā. Atītaṃ kho, cunda, addhānaṃ ārabbha tathāgatassa satānusāri ñāṇaṃ hoti; so yāvatakaṃ ākaṅkhati tāvatakaṃ anussarati. Anāgatañca kho addhānaṃ ārabbha tathāgatassa bodhijaṃ ñāṇaṃ uppajjati – ‘ayamantimā jāti, natthidāni punabbhavo’ti. ‘Atītaṃ cepi, cunda, hoti abhūtaṃ atacchaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, na taṃ tathāgato byākaroti. Atītaṃ cepi, cunda, hoti bhūtaṃ tacchaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, tampi tathāgato na byākaroti. Atītaṃ cepi cunda, hoti bhūtaṃ tacchaṃ atthasaṃhitaṃ, tatra kālaññū tathāgato hoti tassa pañhassa veyyākaraṇāya. Anāgataṃ cepi, cunda, hoti abhūtaṃ atacchaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, na taṃ tathāgato byākaroti…pe… tassa pañhassa veyyākaraṇāya. Paccuppannaṃ cepi, cunda, hoti abhūtaṃ atacchaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, na taṃ tathāgato byākaroti. Paccuppannaṃ cepi, cunda, hoti bhūtaṃ tacchaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, tampi tathāgato na byākaroti. Paccuppannaṃ cepi, cunda, hoti bhūtaṃ tacchaṃ atthasaṃhitaṃ, tatra kālaññū tathāgato hoti tassa pañhassa veyyākaraṇāya.

187."It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say: 'The ascetic Gotama proclaims boundless knowledge and vision regarding the past, but not regarding the future. What is this? How is this?' Those wanderers of other sects, foolish and unskilled, would imagine that boundless knowledge and vision should be proclaimed by some other means, by some other knowledge and vision. Regarding the past, Cunda, the Tathāgata has knowledge that accords with memory; he recollects as much as he wishes. Regarding the future, the knowledge born of awakening arises in the Tathāgata: 'This is the last birth; now there is no more rebirth.' If, Cunda, something about the past is untrue, false, and unbeneficial, the Tathāgata does not declare it. If something about the past is true, factual, but unbeneficial, the Tathāgata does not declare that either. If something about the past is true, factual, and beneficial, the Tathāgata knows the right time to answer that question. If, Cunda, something about the future is untrue, false, and unbeneficial, the Tathāgata does not declare it. If something about the future is true, factual, but unbeneficial, the Tathāgata does not declare that either. If something about the future is true, factual, and beneficial, the Tathāgata knows the right time to answer that question. If, Cunda, something about the present is untrue, false, and unbeneficial, the Tathāgata does not declare it. If something about the present is true, factual, but unbeneficial, the Tathāgata does not declare that either. If something about the present is true, factual, and beneficial, the Tathāgata knows the right time to answer that question."

188. ‘‘Iti kho, cunda, atītānāgatapaccuppannesu dhammesu tathāgato kālavādī [kālavādī saccavādī (syā.)] bhūtavādī atthavādī dhammavādī vinayavādī, tasmā ‘tathāgato’ti vuccati. Yañca kho, cunda, sadevakassa lokassa samārakassa sabrahmakassa sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, sabbaṃ tathāgatena abhisambuddhaṃ, tasmā ‘tathāgato’ti vuccati. Yañca, cunda, rattiṃ tathāgato anuttaraṃ sammāsambodhiṃ abhisambujjhati, yañca rattiṃ anupādisesāya nibbānadhātuyā parinibbāyati, yaṃ etasmiṃ antare bhāsati lapati niddisati. Sabbaṃ taṃ tatheva hoti no aññathā, tasmā ‘tathāgato’ti vuccati. Yathāvādī, cunda, tathāgato tathākārī, yathākārī tathāvādī. Iti yathāvādī tathākārī, yathākārī tathāvādī, tasmā ‘tathāgato’ti vuccati. Sadevake loke, cunda, samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya tathāgato abhibhū anabhibhūto aññadatthudaso vasavattī, tasmā ‘tathāgato’ti vuccati.

188."Thus, Cunda, regarding things past, future, and present, the Tathāgata speaks at the right time, speaks what is true, speaks what is meaningful, speaks the Dhamma, speaks the discipline. Therefore, he is called 'Tathāgata.' Whatever, Cunda, in this world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, among this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans—whatever is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and examined by the mind—all that has been fully awakened to by the Tathāgata. Therefore, he is called 'Tathāgata.' From the night, Cunda, when the Tathāgata awakens to the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment until the night he passes into the Nibbāna element without remainder, whatever he speaks, declares, or explains, all that is just so and not otherwise. Therefore, he is called 'Tathāgata.' As he speaks, Cunda, so he does; as he does, so he speaks. Thus, as he speaks, so he does; as he does, so he speaks. Therefore, he is called 'Tathāgata.' In this world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, among this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans, the Tathāgata is the vanquisher, the unvanquished, the one who sees the other shore, the wielder of power. Therefore, he is called 'Tathāgata.'"

## Abyākataṭṭhānaṃ

### The Undeclared

189. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘abyākataṃ kho, āvuso, bhagavatā – ‘‘hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’’’nti.

189."It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say: 'Friend, what then becomes of the Tathāgata after death? Is this alone true and everything else false?' Wanderers of other sects who speak thus should be answered: 'The Blessed One has declared it undeclared, friend, that "the Tathāgata exists after death; this alone is true and everything else false."'"

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘kiṃ panāvuso, na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘etampi kho, āvuso, bhagavatā abyākataṃ – ‘‘na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’’’nti.

"Indeed, Cunda, it is possible that wanderers of other sects might say thus: 'But, friend, is it not the case that the Tathāgata does not exist after death? This alone is true, all else is false.' To those wanderers of other sects who speak thus, Cunda, you should say: 'This too, friend, has not been declared by the Blessed One: "The Tathāgata does not exist after death; this alone is true, all else is false."'"

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘kiṃ panāvuso, hoti ca na ca hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘abyākataṃ kho etaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā – ‘‘hoti ca na ca hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’’’nti.

"Indeed, Cunda, it is possible that wanderers of other sects might say: 'But, friends, what is it? Does the Tathāgata exist and not exist after death? Is this alone the truth, and all else vain?' To those wanderers of other sects who speak thus, Cunda, you should say: 'This, friends, has not been declared by the Blessed One: "The Tathāgata exists and does not exist after death. This alone is the truth, and all else vain."'"

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘kiṃ panāvuso, neva hoti na na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘etampi kho, āvuso, bhagavatā abyākataṃ – ‘‘neva hoti na na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’’’nti.

"Indeed, Cunda, it is possible that wanderers of other sects might say: 'But, friend, what is this: the Tathāgata neither exists nor does not exist after death—this alone is true, all else is false?' To those wanderers of other sects who speak thus, Cunda, you should say: 'This too, friend, has not been declared by the Blessed One: "The Tathāgata neither exists nor does not exist after death—this alone is true, all else is false."'"

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘kasmā panetaṃ, āvuso, samaṇena gotamena abyākata’nti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘na hetaṃ, āvuso, atthasaṃhitaṃ na dhammasaṃhitaṃ na ādibrahmacariyakaṃ na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati, tasmā taṃ bhagavatā abyākata’nti.

"Indeed, Cunda, it is possible that wanderers of other sects might say thus: 'But why, friend, has this not been declared by the ascetic Gotama?' When wanderers of other sects speak thus, Cunda, they should be told: 'Because, friend, this is not connected with the meaning, not connected with the Dhamma, not fundamental to the life of purity, does not lead to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, to Nibbāna. That is why it has not been declared by the Blessed One.'"

## Byākataṭṭhānaṃ

### The Declared.

190. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘kiṃ panāvuso, samaṇena gotamena byākata’nti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘idaṃ dukkhanti kho, āvuso, bhagavatā byākataṃ, ayaṃ dukkhasamudayoti kho, āvuso, bhagavatā byākataṃ, ayaṃ dukkhanirodhoti kho, āvuso, bhagavatā byākataṃ, ayaṃ dukkhanirodhagāminī paṭipadāti kho, āvuso, bhagavatā byākata’nti.

190."It is possible, Cunda, that wanderers of other sects might say thus: 'What, friend, has the ascetic Gotama declared?' When wanderers of other sects say this, Cunda, they should be told thus: 'This is suffering, friend, as declared by the Blessed One. This is the origin of suffering, friend, as declared by the Blessed One. This is the cessation of suffering, friend, as declared by the Blessed One. This is the path leading to the cessation of suffering, friend, as declared by the Blessed One.'"

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, cunda, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘kasmā panetaṃ, āvuso, samaṇena gotamena byākata’nti? Evaṃvādino, cunda, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evamassu vacanīyā – ‘etañhi, āvuso, atthasaṃhitaṃ, etaṃ dhammasaṃhitaṃ, etaṃ ādibrahmacariyakaṃ ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati. Tasmā taṃ bhagavatā byākata’nti.

"Indeed, Cunda, it is possible that wanderers of other sects might say this: ‘But why, friend, has this been declared by the ascetic Gotama?’ When wanderers of other sects speak thus, Cunda, they should be told: ‘Because, friend, this is connected with the meaning, this is connected with the Dhamma, this pertains to the beginning of the holy life, leading exclusively to disenchantment, dispassion, cessation, peace, direct knowledge, awakening, and Nibbāna. That is why it has been declared by the Blessed One.’"

## Pubbantasahagatadiṭṭhinissayā

### Grounds for Views Pertaining to the Past

191. ‘‘Yepi te, cunda, pubbantasahagatā diṭṭhinissayā, tepi vo mayā byākatā, yathā te byākātabbā. Yathā ca te na byākātabbā, kiṃ vo ahaṃ te tathā [tattha (syā. ka.)] byākarissāmi? Yepi te, cunda, aparantasahagatā diṭṭhinissayā, tepi vo mayā byākatā, yathā te byākātabbā. Yathā ca te na byākātabbā, kiṃ vo ahaṃ te tathā byākarissāmi? Katame ca te, cunda, pubbantasahagatā diṭṭhinissayā, ye vo mayā byākatā, yathā te byākātabbā. (Yathā ca te na byākātabbā, kiṃ vo ahaṃ te tathā byākarissāmi) [(yathā ca te na byākātabbā) sabbattha]? Santi kho, cunda, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘sassato attā ca loko ca, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Santi pana, cunda, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘asassato attā ca loko ca…pe… sassato ca asassato ca attā ca loko ca… neva sassato nāsassato attā ca loko ca… sayaṃkato attā ca loko ca… paraṃkato attā ca loko ca… sayaṃkato ca paraṃkato ca attā ca loko ca… asayaṃkāro aparaṃkāro adhiccasamuppanno attā ca loko ca, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Sassataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… asassataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… sassatañca asassatañca sukhadukkhaṃ… nevasassataṃ nāsassataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… sayaṃkataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… paraṃkataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… sayaṃkatañca paraṃkatañca sukhadukkhaṃ… asayaṃkāraṃ aparaṃkāraṃ adhiccasamuppannaṃ sukhadukkhaṃ, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti.

191."And, Cunda, those grounds for views pertaining to the past that I have explained to you, I have explained them as they should be explained. And those that should not be explained, why should I explain them to you in that way? Those grounds for views pertaining to the future that I have explained to you, I have explained them as they should be explained. And those that should not be explained, why should I explain them to you in that way? And what, Cunda, are those grounds for views pertaining to the past that I have explained to you as they should be explained? (And those that should not be explained, why should I explain them to you in that way?) There are, Cunda, some ascetics and brahmins who speak thus and hold such views: ‘The self and the world are eternal—this alone is true, all else is false.’ There are, Cunda, some ascetics and brahmins who speak thus and hold such views: ‘The self and the world are not eternal… both eternal and not eternal… neither eternal nor not eternal… self-made… made by another… both self-made and and made by another… arisen without a cause, without condition—this alone is true, all else is false.’ They declare: ‘Pleasure and pain are eternal… not eternal… both eternal and not eternal… neither eternal nor not eternal… self-made… made by another… both self-made and made by another… arisen without a cause, without condition—this alone is true, all else is false.’"

192. ‘‘Tatra, cunda, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘sassato attā ca loko ca, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Tyāhaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadāmi – ‘atthi nu kho idaṃ, āvuso, vuccati – ‘‘sassato attā ca loko cā’’ti? Yañca kho te evamāhaṃsu – ‘idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Taṃ tesaṃ nānujānāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Aññathāsaññinopi hettha, cunda, santeke sattā. Imāyapi kho ahaṃ, cunda, paññattiyā neva attanā samasamaṃ samanupassāmi kuto bhiyyo. Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo yadidaṃ adhipaññatti.

192."Now, Cunda, as for those ascetics and brahmins who are proponents of such views, who maintain: ‘The self and the world are eternal; this alone is true, anything else is worthless,’ I go to them and say: ‘Is it true, friends, that you say: “The self and the world are eternal”?’ And that which they say—‘This alone is true, anything else is worthless’—that I do not approve of. Why is that? Because, Cunda, there are some beings here who have different perceptions. Even by this designation, Cunda, I do not regard myself as equal, let alone superior. Rather, I am superior in that respect, namely, in the higher designation."

193. ‘‘Tatra, cunda, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘asassato attā ca loko ca…pe… sassato ca asassato ca attā ca loko ca… nevasassato nāsassato attā ca loko ca… sayaṃkato attā ca loko ca… paraṃkato attā ca loko ca… sayaṃkato ca paraṃkato ca attā ca loko ca… asayaṃkāro aparaṃkāro adhiccasamuppanno attā ca loko ca… sassataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… asassataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… sassatañca asassatañca sukhadukkhaṃ… nevasassataṃ nāsassataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… sayaṃkataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… paraṃkataṃ sukhadukkhaṃ… sayaṃkatañca paraṃkatañca sukhadukkhaṃ… asayaṃkāraṃ aparaṃkāraṃ adhiccasamuppannaṃ sukhadukkhaṃ, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Tyāhaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadāmi – ‘atthi nu kho idaṃ, āvuso, vuccati – ‘‘asayaṃkāraṃ aparaṃkāraṃ adhiccasamuppannaṃ sukhadukkha’’’nti? Yañca kho te evamāhaṃsu – ‘idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Taṃ tesaṃ nānujānāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Aññathāsaññinopi hettha, cunda, santeke sattā. Imāyapi kho ahaṃ, cunda, paññattiyā neva attanā samasamaṃ samanupassāmi kuto bhiyyo. Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo yadidaṃ adhipaññatti. Ime kho te, cunda, pubbantasahagatā diṭṭhinissayā, ye vo mayā byākatā, yathā te byākātabbā. Yathā ca te na byākātabbā, kiṃ vo ahaṃ te tathā byākarissāmīti [byākarissāmīti (sī. ka.)]?

193."Now, Cunda, those ascetics and brahmins who maintain such views and doctrines as these: 'The self and the world are eternal...' or 'The self and the world are not eternal...' or 'The self and the world are both eternal and not eternal...' or 'The self and the world are neither eternal nor not eternal...' or 'The self and the world are self-made...' or 'The self and the world are made by another...' or 'The self and the world are both self-made and made by another...' or 'The self and the world arise without self-making or other-making, spontaneously...' or 'Pleasure and pain are eternal...' or 'Pleasure and pain are not eternal...' or 'Pleasure and pain are both eternal and not eternal...' or 'Pleasure and pain are neither eternal nor not eternal...' or 'Pleasure and pain are self-made...' or 'Pleasure and pain are made by another...' or 'Pleasure and pain are both self-made and made by another...' or 'Pleasure and pain arise without self-making or other-making, spontaneously—this alone is true, all else is worthless'—when I approach them, I say: 'Is it really so, friends, that you say: "Pleasure and pain arise without self-making or other-making, spontaneously"?' And whatever they may assert, saying, 'This alone is true, all else is worthless,' I do not approve of that. Why is that? Because, Cunda, there are beings who perceive things differently in this matter. Even by this designation, Cunda, I do not regard myself as equal, let alone superior. Rather, I am superior in that respect, namely, in the higher designation. These, Cunda, are the grounds for views pertaining to the past, which I have explained to you as they should be explained. And as for those that should not be explained, why should I explain them to you in that way?"

## Aparantasahagatadiṭṭhinissayā

### Foundations of views connected with the future

194. ‘‘Katame ca te, cunda, aparantasahagatā diṭṭhinissayā, ye vo mayā byākatā, yathā te byākātabbā. (Yathā ca te na byākātabbā, kiṃ vo ahaṃ te tathā byākarissāmī) [( ) etthantare pāṭho sabbatthapi paripuṇṇo dissati]? Santi, cunda, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘rūpī attā hoti arogo paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Santi pana, cunda, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘arūpī attā hoti…pe… rūpī ca arūpī ca attā hoti… nevarūpī nārūpī attā hoti… saññī attā hoti… asaññī attā hoti… nevasaññīnāsaññī attā hoti… attā ucchijjati vinassati na hoti paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Tatra, cunda, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘rūpī attā hoti arogo paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Tyāhaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadāmi – ‘atthi nu kho idaṃ, āvuso, vuccati – ‘‘rūpī attā hoti arogo paraṃ maraṇā’’’ti? Yañca kho te evamāhaṃsu – ‘idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Taṃ tesaṃ nānujānāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Aññathāsaññinopi hettha, cunda, santeke sattā. Imāyapi kho ahaṃ, cunda, paññattiyā neva attanā samasamaṃ samanupassāmi kuto bhiyyo. Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo yadidaṃ adhipaññatti.

194.“And what, Cunda, are those foundations of views connected with the future that I have explained to you, and how should they be explained? (And how should they not be explained? Why should I explain them to you in that way?) There are, Cunda, some ascetics and brahmins who hold such a doctrine and such a view: ‘The self is material and free from disease after death; this alone is true, other views are worthless.’ But there are, Cunda, some ascetics and brahmins who hold such a doctrine and such a view: ‘The self is immaterial… the self is both material and immaterial… the self is neither material nor immaterial… the self is percipient… the self is non-percipient… the self is neither percipient nor non-percipient… the self is annihilated, destroyed, and does not exist after death; this alone is true, other views are worthless.’ In this case, Cunda, concerning those ascetics and brahmins who hold such a doctrine and such a view: ‘The self is material and free from disease after death; this alone is true, other views are worthless’—I approach them and say: ‘Is this what you say, friends: “The self is material and free from disease after death”?’ And whatever they say: ‘This alone is true, other views are worthless,’ that I do not approve of. Why is that? Because, Cunda, there are some beings here who perceive things differently. Even by means of this declaration, Cunda, I do not regard myself as equal, let alone superior. But in this matter, I am indeed superior, that is, in higher knowledge.

195. ‘‘Tatra, cunda, ye te samaṇabrāhmaṇā evaṃvādino evaṃdiṭṭhino – ‘arūpī attā hoti…pe… rūpī ca arūpī ca attā hoti… nevarūpīnārūpī attā hoti… saññī attā hoti… asaññī attā hoti… nevasaññīnāsaññī attā hoti… attā ucchijjati vinassati na hoti paraṃ maraṇā, idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Tyāhaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadāmi – ‘atthi nu kho idaṃ, āvuso, vuccati – ‘‘attā ucchijjati vinassati na hoti paraṃ maraṇā’’’ti? Yañca kho te, cunda, evamāhaṃsu – ‘idameva saccaṃ moghamañña’nti. Taṃ tesaṃ nānujānāmi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Aññathāsaññinopi hettha, cunda, santeke sattā. Imāyapi kho ahaṃ, cunda, paññattiyā neva attanā samasamaṃ samanupassāmi, kuto bhiyyo. Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo yadidaṃ adhipaññatti. Ime kho te, cunda, aparantasahagatā diṭṭhinissayā, ye vo mayā byākatā, yathā te byākātabbā. Yathā ca te na byākātabbā, kiṃ vo ahaṃ te tathā byākarissāmīti [byākarissāmīti (sī. ka.)]?

195.“Now, Cunda, those ascetics and brahmins who hold such a doctrine and such a view: ‘The self is immaterial… the self is both material and immaterial… the self is neither material nor immaterial… the self is percipient… the self is non-percipient… the self is neither percipient nor non-percipient… the self is annihilated, destroyed, and does not exist after death; this alone is true, other views are worthless’—I approach them and say: ‘Is this what you say, friends: “The self is annihilated, destroyed, and does not exist after death”?’ And whatever they say: ‘This alone is true, other views are worthless,’ that I do not approve of. Why is that? Because, Cunda, there are some beings here who perceive things differently. Even by means of this declaration, Cunda, I do not regard myself as equal, let alone superior. But in this matter, I am indeed superior, that is, in higher knowledge. These, Cunda, are those foundations of views connected with the future that I have explained to you, and how they should be explained. And how they should not be explained? Why should I explain them to you in that way?

196. ‘‘Imesañca, cunda, pubbantasahagatānaṃ diṭṭhinissayānaṃ imesañca aparantasahagatānaṃ diṭṭhinissayānaṃ pahānāya samatikkamāya evaṃ mayā cattāro satipaṭṭhānā desitā paññattā. Katame cattāro? Idha, cunda, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Vedanāsu vedanānupassī…pe… citte cittānupassī… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Imesañca cunda, pubbantasahagatānaṃ diṭṭhinissayānaṃ imesañca aparantasahagatānaṃ diṭṭhinissayānaṃ pahānāya samatikkamāya. Evaṃ mayā ime cattāro satipaṭṭhānā desitā paññattā’’ti.

196.“For the abandoning and overcoming of these foundations of views connected with the past and these foundations of views connected with the future, Cunda, I have taught and established the four foundations of mindfulness. What are the four? Here, Cunda, a bhikkhu dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly knowing, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure in the world. He dwells contemplating feelings in feelings… mind in mind… phenomena in phenomena, ardent, clearly knowing, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure in the world. For the abandoning and overcoming of these foundations of views connected with the past and these foundations of views connected with the future, Cunda, I have taught and established these four foundations of mindfulness.”

197. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā upavāṇo bhagavato piṭṭhito ṭhito hoti bhagavantaṃ bījayamāno. Atha kho āyasmā upavāṇo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘acchariyaṃ, bhante, abbhutaṃ, bhante! Pāsādiko vatāyaṃ, bhante, dhammapariyāyo; supāsādiko vatāyaṃ bhante, dhammapariyāyo, ko nāmāyaṃ bhante dhammapariyāyo’’ti? ‘‘Tasmātiha tvaṃ, upavāṇa, imaṃ dhammapariyāyaṃ ‘pāsādiko’ tveva naṃ dhārehī’’ti. Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano āyasmā upavāṇo bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandīti.

197.At that time, the Venerable Upavāṇa was standing behind the Blessed One, fanning him. Then the Venerable Upavāṇa said to the Blessed One: “It is wonderful, venerable sir! It is amazing, venerable sir! How pleasing is this exposition of the Dhamma, venerable sir! How supremely pleasing is this exposition of the Dhamma! What is the name of this exposition of the Dhamma, venerable sir?” “Therefore, Upavāṇa, you may remember this exposition of the Dhamma as ‘The Pleasing.’ ” This is what the Blessed One said. The Venerable Upavāṇa was delighted and rejoiced in the Blessed One’s words.

Pāsādikasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ.

The Pāsādika Sutta: The Sixth.
