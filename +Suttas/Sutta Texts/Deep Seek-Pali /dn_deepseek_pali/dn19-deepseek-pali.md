# Mahāgovindasuttaṃ

## The Great Govinda Discourse

293. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati gijjhakūṭe pabbate. Atha kho pañcasikho gandhabbaputto abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇo kevalakappaṃ gijjhakūṭaṃ pabbataṃ obhāsetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho pañcasikho gandhabbaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yaṃ kho me, bhante, devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ sammukhā sutaṃ sammukhā paṭiggahitaṃ, ārocemi taṃ bhagavato’’ti. ‘‘Ārocehi me tvaṃ, pañcasikhā’’ti bhagavā avoca.

293.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha, on Vulture Peak Mountain. Then, when the night was well advanced, Pañcasikha the gandhabba-son, of surpassing beauty, illuminating the entire Vulture Peak Mountain, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, Pañcasikha the gandhabba-son said to the Blessed One: "That which I, venerable sir, have heard face to face in the presence of the Tāvatiṃsa devas, that I report to the Blessed One." "Report it to me, Pañcasikha," the Blessed One said.

## Devasabhā

### Deva Assembly

294. ‘‘Purimāni, bhante, divasāni purimatarāni tadahuposathe pannarase pavāraṇāya puṇṇāya puṇṇamāya rattiyā kevalakappā ca devā tāvatiṃsā sudhammāyaṃ sabhāyaṃ sannisinnā honti sannipatitā; mahatī ca dibbaparisā samantato nisinnā honti, cattāro ca mahārājāno catuddisā nisinnā honti; puratthimāya disāya dhataraṭṭho mahārājā pacchimābhimukho nisinno hoti deve purakkhatvā; dakkhiṇāya disāya virūḷhako mahārājā uttarābhimukho nisinno hoti deve purakkhatvā; pacchimāya disāya virūpakkho mahārājā puratthābhimukho nisinno hoti deve purakkhatvā; uttarāya disāya vessavaṇo mahārājā dakkhiṇābhimukho nisinno hoti deve purakkhatvā. Yadā bhante, kevalakappā ca devā tāvatiṃsā sudhammāyaṃ sabhāyaṃ sannisinnā honti sannipatitā, mahatī ca dibbaparisā samantato nisinnā honti, cattāro ca mahārājāno catuddisā nisinnā honti, idaṃ nesaṃ hoti āsanasmiṃ; atha pacchā amhākaṃ āsanaṃ hoti.

294."In the past, venerable sir, in days gone by, on the fifteenth, the Uposatha day, on the Pavāraṇā, on the full-moon night, all the devas of the Tāvatiṃsa realm were assembled and gathered in the Sudhammā hall. And a great divine assembly was seated all around, and the Four Great Kings were seated in the four directions. In the eastern direction, King Dhataraṭṭha was seated facing west, having the devas in front of him. In the southern direction, King Virūḷhaka was seated facing north, having the devas in front of him. In the western direction, King Virūpakkha was seated facing east, having the devas in front of him. In the northern direction, King Vessavaṇa was seated facing south, having the devas in front of him. When, venerable sir, all the devas of the Tāvatiṃsa realm were assembled and gathered in the Sudhammā hall, and a great divine assembly was seated all around, and the Four Great Kings were seated in the four directions, this was their seating arrangement; only afterwards was there a seat for us."

‘‘Ye te, bhante, devā bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ caritvā adhunūpapannā tāvatiṃsakāyaṃ, te aññe deve atirocanti vaṇṇena ceva yasasā ca. Tena sudaṃ, bhante, devā tāvatiṃsā attamanā honti pamuditā pītisomanassajātā; ‘dibbā vata, bho, kāyā paripūrenti, hāyanti asurakāyā’ti.

"Venerable sir, those devas who, having lived the holy life under the Blessed One, have recently been reborn in the Tāvatiṃsa heaven, outshine the other devas in beauty and glory. Then, venerable sir, the Tāvatiṃsa devas are glad, joyful, filled with delight and happiness, thinking: ‘Truly, the divine bodies are becoming complete, while the asura bodies are dwindling!’"

295. ‘‘Atha kho, bhante, sakko devānamindo devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ sampasādaṃ viditvā imāhi gāthāhi anumodi –

295."Then, venerable sir, Sakka, lord of the devas, knowing the approval of the Tāvatiṃsa devas, rejoiced with these verses:

‘Modanti vata bho devā, tāvatiṃsā sahindakā;Tathāgataṃ namassantā, dhammassa ca sudhammataṃ.

Indeed, the devas rejoice, the Tāvatiṃsa devas with Indra, Paying homage to the Tathāgata and to the Dhamma so well expounded.

Nave deve ca passantā, vaṇṇavante yasassine;Sugatasmiṃ brahmacariyaṃ, caritvāna idhāgate.

Seeing the new devas, beautiful and glorious, who have come here after living the holy life under the Sugata.

Te aññe atirocanti, vaṇṇena yasasāyunā;Sāvakā bhūripaññassa, visesūpagatā idha.

They outshine others in beauty, fame, and lifespan; Disciples of the one with vast wisdom attain distinction here.

Idaṃ disvāna nandanti, tāvatiṃsā sahindakā;Tathāgataṃ namassantā, dhammassa ca sudhammata’nti.

Seeing this, the Tāvatiṃsa devas, along with Indra, rejoice, paying homage to the Tathāgata and to the Dhamma so well expounded.

‘‘Tena sudaṃ, bhante, devā tāvatiṃsā bhiyyoso mattāya attamanā honti pamuditā pītisomanassajātā; ‘dibbā vata, bho, kāyā paripūrenti, hāyanti asurakāyā’’’ti.

"Then, venerable sir, the devas of the Tāvatiṃsa were even more exceedingly delighted, joyful, and filled with elation, saying: ‘Truly, the divine bodies are being filled, while the asura bodies are dwindling!’"

## Aṭṭha yathābhuccavaṇṇā

### Eight True Qualities

296. ‘‘Atha kho, bhante, sakko devānamindo devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ sampasādaṃ viditvā deve tāvatiṃse āmantesi – ‘iccheyyātha no tumhe, mārisā, tassa bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe sotu’nti? ‘Icchāma mayaṃ, mārisa, tassa bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe sotu’nti. Atha kho, bhante, sakko devānamindo devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe payirudāhāsi – ‘taṃ kiṃ maññanti, bhonto devā tāvatiṃsā? Yāvañca so bhagavā bahujanahitāya paṭipanno bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ. Evaṃ bahujanahitāya paṭipannaṃ bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

296."Then, venerable sir, Sakka, the lord of the devas, knowing the satisfaction of the Tāvatiṃsa devas, addressed them: 'Would you like, dear sirs, to hear the eight true qualities of that Blessed One?' 'We would like, dear sir, to hear the eight true qualities of that Blessed One.' Then, venerable sir, Sakka, the lord of the devas, declared to the Tāvatiṃsa devas the eight true qualities of the Blessed One: 'What do you think, honored Tāvatiṃsa devas? The Blessed One has undertaken [the path] for the welfare of many, for the happiness of many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of devas and humans. We see no teacher in the past or present endowed with this characteristic, other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Svākkhāto kho pana tena bhagavatā dhammo sandiṭṭhiko akāliko ehipassiko opaneyyiko paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhi. Evaṃ opaneyyikassa dhammassa desetāraṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

"The Dhamma is well-expounded by the Blessed One; visible here and now, timeless, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, to be personally realized by the wise. We do not see any teacher endowed with this characteristic, who declares such an onward-leading Dhamma, either in the past or present, other than the Blessed One."

‘‘Idaṃ kusalanti kho pana tena bhagavatā supaññattaṃ, idaṃ akusalanti supaññattaṃ. Idaṃ sāvajjaṃ idaṃ anavajjaṃ, idaṃ sevitabbaṃ idaṃ na sevitabbaṃ, idaṃ hīnaṃ idaṃ paṇītaṃ, idaṃ kaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāganti supaññattaṃ. Evaṃ kusalākusalasāvajjānavajjasevitabbāsevitabbahīna-paṇītakaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāgānaṃ dhammānaṃ paññapetāraṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

"That Blessed One has well-declared what is skillful and what is unskillful, what is blameworthy and what is blameless, what should be cultivated and what should not be cultivated, what is inferior and what is excellent, what has dark and bright counterparts. We do not see any teacher of the past or present endowed with this characteristic, who declares such dhammas as skillful and unskillful, blameworthy and blameless, to be cultivated and not to be cultivated, inferior and excellent, and having dark and bright counterparts, except for that Blessed One."

‘‘Supaññattā kho pana tena bhagavatā sāvakānaṃ nibbānagāminī paṭipadā, saṃsandati nibbānañca paṭipadā ca. Seyyathāpi nāma gaṅgodakaṃ yamunodakena saṃsandati sameti, evameva supaññattā tena bhagavatā sāvakānaṃ nibbānagāminī paṭipadā, saṃsandati nibbānañca paṭipadā ca. Evaṃ nibbānagāminiyā paṭipadāya paññapetāraṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

"Well-declared, indeed, by the Blessed One is the path leading to Nibbāna for his disciples, where the path and Nibbāna converge. Just as the waters of the Ganges and the Yamuna meet and mingle, even so, well-declared by that Blessed One is the path leading to Nibbāna for the disciples, where the path and Nibbāna converge. A teacher endowed with this characteristic, who declares the path leading to Nibbāna, we see not in the past, nor do we now, other than that Blessed One."

‘‘Abhinipphanno [abhinippanno (pī. ka.)] kho pana tassa bhagavato lābho abhinipphanno siloko, yāva maññe khattiyā sampiyāyamānarūpā viharanti, vigatamado kho pana so bhagavā āhāraṃ āhāreti. Evaṃ vigatamadaṃ āhāraṃ āharayamānaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

"The Blessed One has indeed attained gain and renown; and it seems as though the nobles hold him in high regard. Yet, the Blessed One, being free from conceit, partakes of food. We see no teacher, past or present, endowed with this characteristic—partaking of food free from conceit—except for that Blessed One."

‘‘Laddhasahāyo kho pana so bhagavā sekhānañceva paṭipannānaṃ khīṇāsavānañca vusitavataṃ. Te bhagavā apanujja ekārāmataṃ anuyutto viharati. Evaṃ ekārāmataṃ anuyuttaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

"The Blessed One has gained companions—both trainees who are on the path and perfected ones who have lived the holy life. Yet, that Blessed One dismisses them and lives devoted to solitude. We do not see any teacher of the past or present endowed with this characteristic, except for that Blessed One."

‘‘Yathāvādī kho pana so bhagavā tathākārī, yathākārī tathāvādī, iti yathāvādī tathākārī, yathākārī tathāvādī. Evaṃ dhammānudhammappaṭipannaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

"Indeed, that Blessed One is as he speaks, so he acts; and as he acts, so he speaks. Thus, as he speaks, so he acts; and as he acts, so he speaks. We do not see a teacher who lives in accordance with the Dhamma, endowed with this characteristic, either in the past or at present, other than that Blessed One."

‘‘Tiṇṇavicikiccho kho pana so bhagavā vigatakathaṃkatho pariyositasaṅkappo ajjhāsayaṃ ādibrahmacariyaṃ. Evaṃ tiṇṇavicikicchaṃ vigatakathaṃkathaṃ pariyositasaṅkappaṃ ajjhāsayaṃ ādibrahmacariyaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā’ti.

"Moreover, that Blessed One has overcome doubt, is without perplexity, whose aspiration is fulfilled, and whose inclination is the supreme holy life. Thus, we do not see a teacher endowed with this characteristic—one who has overcome doubt, is without perplexity, whose aspiration is fulfilled, and whose inclination is the supreme holy life—either in the past or at present, other than that Blessed One.’"

297. ‘‘Ime kho, bhante, sakko devānamindo devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe payirudāhāsi. Tena sudaṃ, bhante, devā tāvatiṃsā bhiyyoso mattāya attamanā honti pamuditā pītisomanassajātā bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe sutvā. Tatra, bhante, ekacce devā evamāhaṃsu – ‘aho vata, mārisā, cattāro sammāsambuddhā loke uppajjeyyuṃ dhammañca deseyyuṃ yathariva bhagavā. Tadassa bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’nti. Ekacce devā evamāhaṃsu – ‘tiṭṭhantu, mārisā, cattāro sammāsambuddhā, aho vata, mārisā, tayo sammāsambuddhā loke uppajjeyyuṃ dhammañca deseyyuṃ yathariva bhagavā. Tadassa bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’nti. Ekacce devā evamāhaṃsu – ‘tiṭṭhantu, mārisā, tayo sammāsambuddhā, aho vata, mārisā, dve sammāsambuddhā loke uppajjeyyuṃ dhammañca deseyyuṃ yathariva bhagavā. Tadassa bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’nti.

297."These, venerable sir, are the eight true qualities that Sakka, lord of the devas, declared to the Tāvatiṃsa devas regarding the Blessed One. Hearing these eight true qualities of the Blessed One, the Tāvatiṃsa devas became even more exceedingly delighted, joyful, and filled with rapture and happiness. There, venerable sir, some devas said: 'Oh, friends, if only four Perfectly Enlightened Ones were to arise in the world and teach the Dhamma just like the Blessed One! That would be for the welfare of many, for the happiness of many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of devas and humans.' Some devas said: 'Let four Perfectly Enlightened Ones be. Oh, friends, if only three Perfectly Enlightened Ones were to arise in the world and teach the Dhamma just like the Blessed One! That would be for the welfare of many, for the happiness of many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of devas and humans.' Some devas said: 'Let three Perfectly Enlightened Ones be. Oh, friends, if only two Perfectly Enlightened Ones were to arise in the world and teach the Dhamma just like the Blessed One! That would be for the welfare of many, for the happiness of many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of devas and humans.'"

298. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte, bhante, sakko devānamindo deve tāvatiṃse etadavoca – ‘aṭṭhānaṃ kho etaṃ, mārisā, anavakāso, yaṃ ekissā lokadhātuyā dve arahanto sammāsambuddhā apubbaṃ acarimaṃ uppajjeyyuṃ, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. Aho vata, mārisā, so bhagavā appābādho appātaṅko ciraṃ dīghamaddhānaṃ tiṭṭheyya. Tadassa bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’nti. Atha kho, bhante, yenatthena devā tāvatiṃsā sudhammāyaṃ sabhāyaṃ sannisinnā honti sannipatitā, taṃ atthaṃ cintayitvā taṃ atthaṃ mantayitvā vuttavacanāpi taṃ cattāro mahārājāno tasmiṃ atthe honti. Paccānusiṭṭhavacanāpi taṃ cattāro mahārājāno tasmiṃ atthe honti, sakesu sakesu āsanesu ṭhitā avipakkantā.

298."When this was said, venerable sir, Sakka, lord of the devas, addressed the Tāvatiṃsa devas: 'It is impossible, friends, there is no occasion for two Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones, to arise in one world-system simultaneously, without prior or subsequent appearance; that is not possible. Oh, friends, may that Blessed One abide long, free from illness and affliction, for the welfare of many, for the happiness of many, out of compassion for the world, for the good, benefit, and happiness of devas and humans.' Then, venerable sir, having considered the matter for which the Tāvatiṃsa devas had assembled and gathered in the Sudhammā hall, and having deliberated on that matter, the Four Great Kings, even after the statement had been made, were of the same mind regarding that matter. And the Four Great Kings, even after they had been instructed, were of the same mind regarding that matter, standing in their respective seats, not having departed."

Te vuttavākyā rājāno, paṭiggayhānusāsaniṃ;Vippasannamanā santā, aṭṭhaṃsu samhi āsaneti.

The kings, having spoken their words, accepted the instruction; with serene and pleased minds, they remained in their seats.

299. ‘‘Atha kho, bhante, uttarāya disāya uḷāro āloko sañjāyi, obhāso pāturahosi atikkammeva devānaṃ devānubhāvaṃ. Atha kho, bhante, sakko devānamindo deve tāvatiṃse āmantesi – ‘yathā kho, mārisā, nimittāni dissanti, uḷāro āloko sañjāyati, obhāso pātubhavati, brahmā pātubhavissati; brahmuno hetaṃ pubbanimittaṃ pātubhāvāya, yadidaṃ āloko sañjāyati obhāso pātubhavatīti.

299."Then, venerable sir, from the northern direction a great light arose, a radiance appeared, surpassing even the divine power of the devas. Then, venerable sir, Sakka, lord of the devas, addressed the Tāvatiṃsa devas: 'Friends, as the signs appear—a great light arises, a radiance appears—Brahmā will appear. For this is the preliminary sign of Brahmā's appearance: that a light arises and a radiance appears.'"

‘Yathā nimittā dissanti, brahmā pātubhavissati;Brahmuno hetaṃ nimittaṃ, obhāso vipulo mahā’ti.

'As the signs appear, Brahmā will appear; this is a sign of Brahmā—a vast and great radiance.'

## Sanaṅkumārakathā

### The Story of Sanaṅkumāra

300. ‘‘Atha kho, bhante, devā tāvatiṃsā yathāsakesu āsanesu nisīdiṃsu – ‘obhāsametaṃ ñassāma, yaṃvipāko bhavissati, sacchikatvāva naṃ gamissāmā’ti. Cattāropi mahārājāno yathāsakesu āsanesu nisīdiṃsu – ‘obhāsametaṃ ñassāma, yaṃvipāko bhavissati, sacchikatvāva naṃ gamissāmā’ti. Idaṃ sutvā devā tāvatiṃsā ekaggā samāpajjiṃsu – ‘obhāsametaṃ ñassāma, yaṃvipāko bhavissati, sacchikatvāva naṃ gamissāmā’ti.

300."Then, venerable sir, the Tāvatiṃsa devas sat down on their respective seats, thinking: 'We will know this radiance, what its result will be, and having directly realized it, we will go.' The Four Great Kings also sat down on their respective seats, thinking: 'We will know this radiance, what its result will be, and having directly realized it, we will go.' Hearing this, the Tāvatiṃsa devas became unified in samādhi: 'We will know this radiance, what its result will be, and having directly realized it, we will go.'"

‘‘Yadā, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ pātubhavati, oḷārikaṃ attabhāvaṃ abhinimminitvā pātubhavati. Yo kho pana, bhante, brahmuno pakativaṇṇo, anabhisambhavanīyo so devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ cakkhupathasmiṃ. Yadā, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ pātubhavati, so aññe deve atirocati vaṇṇena ceva yasasā ca. Seyyathāpi, bhante, sovaṇṇo viggaho mānusaṃ viggahaṃ atirocati, evameva kho, bhante, yadā brahmā sanaṅkumāro devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ pātubhavati, so aññe deve atirocati vaṇṇena ceva yasasā ca. Yadā, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ pātubhavati, na tassaṃ parisāyaṃ koci devo abhivādeti vā paccuṭṭheti vā āsanena vā nimanteti. Sabbeva tuṇhībhūtā pañjalikā pallaṅkena nisīdanti – ‘yassadāni devassa pallaṅkaṃ icchissati brahmā sanaṅkumāro, tassa devassa pallaṅke nisīdissatī’ti. Yassa kho pana, bhante, devassa brahmā sanaṅkumāro pallaṅke nisīdati, uḷāraṃ so labhati devo vedapaṭilābhaṃ, uḷāraṃ so labhati devo somanassapaṭilābhaṃ. Seyyathāpi, bhante, rājā khattiyo muddhāvasitto adhunābhisitto rajjena, uḷāraṃ so labhati vedapaṭilābhaṃ, uḷāraṃ so labhati somanassapaṭilābhaṃ, evameva kho, bhante, yassa devassa brahmā sanaṅkumāro pallaṅke nisīdati, uḷāraṃ so labhati devo vedapaṭilābhaṃ, uḷāraṃ so labhati devo somanassapaṭilābhaṃ. Atha, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ sampasādaṃ viditvā antarahito imāhi gāthāhi anumodi –

"When, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra manifests to the Tāvatiṃsa devas, he manifests by creating a gross form. But, venerable sir, Brahmā's natural appearance is not accessible to the eyes of the Tāvatiṃsa devas. When, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra manifests to the Tāvatiṃsa devas, he outshines the other devas in beauty and glory. Just as, venerable sir, a golden statue outshines a human form, so too, venerable sir, when Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra manifests to the Tāvatiṃsa devas, he outshines the other devas in beauty and glory. When, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra manifests to the Tāvatiṃsa devas, no deva in that assembly greets him, rises for him, or invites him with a seat. All remain silent, hands folded, sitting cross-legged, thinking: 'On whichever deva's seat Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra now wishes to sit, he will sit there.' And whichever deva, venerable sir, has Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra sit on his seat, that deva gains supreme attainment of knowledge, supreme attainment of joy. Just as, venerable sir, a khattiya king, newly consecrated to sovereignty, gains supreme attainment of knowledge, supreme attainment of joy, so too, venerable sir, whichever deva has Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra sit on his seat gains supreme attainment of knowledge, supreme attainment of joy. Then, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra, knowing the confidence of the Tāvatiṃsa devas, vanished and rejoiced with these verses:

‘Modanti vata bho devā, tāvatiṃsā sahindakā;Tathāgataṃ namassantā, dhammassa ca sudhammataṃ.

'Indeed, the devas rejoice, the Tāvatiṃsa devas with Indra, paying homage to the Tathāgata and to the Dhamma so well proclaimed.'

‘Nave deve ca passantā, vaṇṇavante yasassine;Sugatasmiṃ brahmacariyaṃ, caritvāna idhāgate.

'Seeing new devas, radiant and glorious, having lived the holy life under the Sugata, they have come here.'

‘Te aññe atirocanti, vaṇṇena yasasāyunā;Sāvakā bhūripaññassa, visesūpagatā idha.

'They outshine others in beauty, fame, and lifespan; disciples of the one with vast wisdom, here they have attained distinction.'

‘Idaṃ disvāna nandanti, tāvatiṃsā sahindakā;Tathāgataṃ namassantā, dhammassa ca sudhammata’nti.

'Seeing this, the Tāvatiṃsa devas, along with Indra, rejoice, paying homage to the Tathāgata and to the Dhamma's perfect nature.'

301. ‘‘Imamatthaṃ, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro abhāsittha. Imamatthaṃ, bhante, brahmuno sanaṅkumārassa bhāsato aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgato saro hoti vissaṭṭho ca viññeyyo ca mañju ca savanīyo ca bindu ca avisārī ca gambhīro ca ninnādī ca. Yathāparisaṃ kho pana, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro sarena viññāpeti, na cassa bahiddhā parisāya ghoso niccharati. Yassa kho pana, bhante, evaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgato saro hoti, so vuccati ‘brahmassaro’ti. Atha kho, bhante, devā tāvatiṃsā brahmānaṃ sanaṅkumāraṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘sādhu, mahābrahme, etadeva mayaṃ saṅkhāya modāma; atthi ca sakkena devānamindena tassa bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhuccā vaṇṇā bhāsitā; te ca mayaṃ saṅkhāya modāmā’ti.

301."This, venerable sir, is what Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra said. This, venerable sir, when Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra speaks, his voice has eight qualities: it is distinct, intelligible, sweet, audible, rounded, non-wandering, deep, and resonant. And, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra makes himself understood to the assembly by his voice, yet the sound does not go outside the assembly. Whoever, venerable sir, possesses a voice endowed with these eight qualities is called 'a Brahma-voice.' Then, venerable sir, the Tāvatiṃsa devas said to Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra: 'Excellent, Great Brahmā! We too rejoice, having understood this. Moreover, Sakka, lord of the devas, has spoken eight truthful virtues of that Blessed One, and we rejoice at them as well.'"

## Aṭṭha yathābhuccavaṇṇā

### Eight True Descriptions

302. ‘‘Atha, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro sakkaṃ devānamindaṃ etadavoca – ‘sādhu, devānaminda, mayampi tassa bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe suṇeyyāmā’ti. ‘Evaṃ mahābrahme’ti kho, bhante, sakko devānamindo brahmuno sanaṅkumārassa bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe payirudāhāsi.

302.Then, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra said to Sakka, lord of the devas: 'Good, lord of the devas! May we too hear those eight true descriptions of the Blessed One.' 'So be it, Great Brahmā,' replied Sakka, lord of the devas, to Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra, reciting the eight true descriptions of the Blessed One.

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññati, bhavaṃ mahābrahmā? Yāvañca so bhagavā bahujanahitāya paṭipanno bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ. Evaṃ bahujanahitāya paṭipannaṃ bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

What do you think, Great Brahmā? To what extent has that Blessed One undertaken to act for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the good, welfare, and happiness of devas and humans? We do not see, either in the past or at present, any teacher endowed with this factor other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Svākkhāto kho pana tena bhagavatā dhammo sandiṭṭhiko akāliko ehipassiko opaneyyiko paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhi. Evaṃ opaneyyikassa dhammassa desetāraṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

Indeed, the Dhamma has been well proclaimed by that Blessed One—visible here and now, timeless, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, to be personally experienced by the wise. We do not see, either in the past or at present, a teacher endowed with this factor other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Idaṃ kusala’nti kho pana tena bhagavatā supaññattaṃ, ‘idaṃ akusala’nti supaññattaṃ, ‘idaṃ sāvajjaṃ idaṃ anavajjaṃ, idaṃ sevitabbaṃ idaṃ na sevitabbaṃ, idaṃ hīnaṃ idaṃ paṇītaṃ, idaṃ kaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāga’nti supaññattaṃ. Evaṃ kusalākusalasāvajjānavajjasevitabbāsevitabbahīnapaṇītakaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāgānaṃ dhammānaṃ paññāpetāraṃ. Imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

‘This is wholesome’ has been well-defined by that Blessed One, ‘this is unwholesome’ has been well-defined; ‘this is blameworthy, this is blameless, this should be cultivated, this should not be cultivated, this is inferior, this is superior, this is dark and bright with its counterpart’ has been well-defined. We do not see, either in the past or at present, a teacher who proclaims these distinctions in wholesome and unwholesome, blameworthy and blameless, what should be cultivated and what should not, inferior and superior, dark and bright qualities, other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Supaññattā kho pana tena bhagavatā sāvakānaṃ nibbānagāminī paṭipadā saṃsandati nibbānañca paṭipadā ca. Seyyathāpi nāma gaṅgodakaṃ yamunodakena saṃsandati sameti, evameva supaññattā tena bhagavatā sāvakānaṃ nibbānagāminī paṭipadā saṃsandati nibbānañca paṭipadā ca. Evaṃ nibbānagāminiyā paṭipadāya paññāpetāraṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

Indeed, the path leading to Nibbāna has been well proclaimed by that Blessed One for his disciples, and the path and Nibbāna accord. Just as the water of the Ganges accords and converges with the water of the Yamuna, so too, the path leading to Nibbāna has been well proclaimed by that Blessed One for his disciples, and the path and Nibbāna accord. We do not see, either in the past or at present, a teacher who proclaims the path leading to Nibbāna, endowed with this factor, other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Abhinipphanno kho pana tassa bhagavato lābho abhinipphanno siloko, yāva maññe khattiyā sampiyāyamānarūpā viharanti. Vigatamado kho pana so bhagavā āhāraṃ āhāreti. Evaṃ vigatamadaṃ āhāraṃ āharayamānaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

Moreover, the Blessed One has achieved gain and renown; indeed, even the nobles live in mutual affection. Furthermore, the Blessed One partakes of food without conceit. We do not see, either in the past or at present, a teacher who partakes of food without conceit, endowed with this factor, other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Laddhasahāyo kho pana so bhagavā sekhānañceva paṭipannānaṃ khīṇāsavānañca vusitavataṃ, te bhagavā apanujja ekārāmataṃ anuyutto viharati. Evaṃ ekārāmataṃ anuyuttaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

Furthermore, the Blessed One has gained companions—both trainees and arahants who have completed the holy life. Having rejected them, he dwells devoted to solitary seclusion. We do not see, either in the past or at present, a teacher devoted to solitary seclusion, endowed with this factor, other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Yathāvādī kho pana so bhagavā tathākārī, yathākārī tathāvādī; iti yathāvādī tathākārī, yathākārī tathāvādī. Evaṃ dhammānudhammappaṭippannaṃ imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā.

Moreover, the Blessed One speaks as he acts, and acts as he speaks; thus, he speaks as he acts, and acts as he speaks. We do not see, either in the past or at present, a teacher accomplished in practicing the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, endowed with this factor, other than that Blessed One.

‘‘Tiṇṇavicikiccho kho pana so bhagavā vigatakathaṃkatho pariyositasaṅkappo ajjhāsayaṃ ādibrahmacariyaṃ. Evaṃ tiṇṇavicikicchaṃ vigatakathaṃkathaṃ pariyositasaṅkappaṃ ajjhāsayaṃ ādibrahmacariyaṃ. Imināpaṅgena samannāgataṃ satthāraṃ neva atītaṃse samanupassāma, na panetarahi, aññatra tena bhagavatā’ti.

Furthermore, that Blessed One has overcome doubt, is without perplexity, whose resolves are finished, and whose disposition is the foremost holy life. We do not see, either in the past or at present, a teacher who has overcome doubt, is without perplexity, whose resolves are finished, and whose disposition is the foremost holy life, endowed with this factor, other than that Blessed One.

303. ‘‘Ime kho, bhante, sakko devānamindo brahmuno sanaṅkumārassa bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe payirudāhāsi. Tena sudaṃ, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro attamano hoti pamudito pītisomanassajāto bhagavato aṭṭha yathābhucce vaṇṇe sutvā. Atha, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro oḷārikaṃ attabhāvaṃ abhinimminitvā kumāravaṇṇī hutvā pañcasikho devānaṃ tāvatiṃsānaṃ pāturahosi. So vehāsaṃ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe pallaṅkena nisīdi. Seyyathāpi, bhante, balavā puriso supaccatthate vā pallaṅke same vā bhūmibhāge pallaṅkena nisīdeyya, evameva kho, bhante, brahmā sanaṅkumāro vehāsaṃ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe pallaṅkena nisīditvā deve tāvatiṃse āmantesi –

303.Venerable sir, these are the eight true descriptions that Sakka, lord of the devas, recited to Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra concerning the Blessed One. Therefore, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra was gladdened, delighted, filled with joy and happiness upon hearing the eight true descriptions of the Blessed One. Then, venerable sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra manifested a gross form, appearing as a youth with five crests, and appeared to the devas of Tāvatiṃsa. Rising into the air, he sat cross-legged in the sky. Just as, venerable sir, a strong man might sit cross-legged on a well-spread couch or on level ground, so too did Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra rise into the air and sit cross-legged in the sky. Then he addressed the devas of Tāvatiṃsa—

## Govindabrāhmaṇavatthu

### The Story of Govinda the Brahmin.

304. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññanti, bhonto devā tāvatiṃsā, yāva dīgharattaṃ mahāpaññova so bhagavā ahosi. Bhūtapubbaṃ, bho, rājā disampati nāma ahosi. Disampatissa rañño govindo nāma brāhmaṇo purohito ahosi. Disampatissa rañño reṇu nāma kumāro putto ahosi. Govindassa brāhmaṇassa jotipālo nāma māṇavo putto ahosi. Iti reṇu ca rājaputto jotipālo ca māṇavo aññe ca cha khattiyā iccete aṭṭha sahāyā ahesuṃ. Atha kho, bho, ahorattānaṃ accayena govindo brāhmaṇo kālamakāsi. Govinde brāhmaṇe kālaṅkate rājā disampati paridevesi – ‘‘yasmiṃ vata, bho, mayaṃ samaye govinde brāhmaṇe sabbakiccāni sammā vossajjitvā pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappitā samaṅgībhūtā paricārema, tasmiṃ no samaye govindo brāhmaṇo kālaṅkato’’ti. Evaṃ vutte bho reṇu rājaputto rājānaṃ disampatiṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mā kho tvaṃ, deva, govinde brāhmaṇe kālaṅkate atibāḷhaṃ paridevesi. Atthi, deva, govindassa brāhmaṇassa jotipālo nāma māṇavo putto paṇḍitataro ceva pitarā, alamatthadasataro ceva pitarā; yepissa pitā atthe anusāsi, tepi jotipālasseva māṇavassa anusāsaniyā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ kumārā’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ devā’’ti.

304."What do you think, venerable gods of the Tāvatiṃsa heaven: for how long has the Blessed One been of great wisdom? Once upon a time, venerable sirs, there was a king named Disampati. King Disampati had a brahmin priest named Govinda. King Disampati had a prince named Reṇu as his son. The brahmin Govinda had a son named Jotipāla. Thus, Prince Reṇu and the young man Jotipāla, along with six other noble youths, these eight were companions. Then, venerable sirs, with the passing of days and nights, the brahmin Govinda passed away. When the brahmin Govinda had passed away, King Disampati lamented: 'Alas, at the very time when, having properly entrusted all duties to the brahmin Govinda, we indulged and delighted in the five strands of sensual pleasure, the brahmin Govinda has passed away!' When this was said, venerable sir, Prince Reṇu addressed King Disampati thus: 'Do not, sire, lament excessively over the death of the brahmin Govinda. There is, sire, the brahmin Govinda's son, the young man Jotipāla, who is wiser than his father and more capable in discerning advantage than his father. Whatever matters his father advised on, those too are within the young man Jotipāla's instruction.' 'Is that so, prince?' 'Yes, sire.'"

## Mahāgovindavatthu

### The Story of Mahāgovinda

305. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, rājā disampati aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, ambho purisa, yena jotipālo nāma māṇavo tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā jotipālaṃ māṇavaṃ evaṃ vadehi – ‘bhavamatthu bhavantaṃ jotipālaṃ, rājā disampati bhavantaṃ jotipālaṃ māṇavaṃ āmantayati, rājā disampati bhoto jotipālassa māṇavassa dassanakāmo’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, devā’’ti kho, bho, so puriso disampatissa rañño paṭissutvā yena jotipālo māṇavo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā jotipālaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘bhavamatthu bhavantaṃ jotipālaṃ, rājā disampati bhavantaṃ jotipālaṃ māṇavaṃ āmantayati, rājā disampati bhoto jotipālassa māṇavassa dassanakāmo’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho bho jotipālo māṇavo tassa purisassa paṭissutvā yena rājā disampati tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā disampatinā raññā saddhiṃ sammodi; sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho, bho, jotipālaṃ māṇavaṃ rājā disampati etadavoca – ‘‘anusāsatu no bhavaṃ jotipālo, mā no bhavaṃ jotipālo anusāsaniyā paccabyāhāsi. Pettike taṃ ṭhāne ṭhapessāmi, govindiye abhisiñcissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho, bho, so jotipālo māṇavo disampatissa rañño paccassosi. Atha kho, bho, rājā disampati jotipālaṃ māṇavaṃ govindiye abhisiñci, taṃ pettike ṭhāne ṭhapesi. Abhisitto jotipālo māṇavo govindiye pettike ṭhāne ṭhapito yepissa pitā atthe anusāsi tepi atthe anusāsati, yepissa pitā atthe nānusāsi, tepi atthe anusāsati; yepissa pitā kammante abhisambhosi, tepi kammante abhisambhoti, yepissa pitā kammante nābhisambhosi, tepi kammante abhisambhoti. Tamenaṃ manussā evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘govindo vata, bho, brāhmaṇo, mahāgovindo vata, bho, brāhmaṇo’’ti. Iminā kho evaṃ, bho, pariyāyena jotipālassa māṇavassa govindo mahāgovindotveva samaññā udapādi.

305."Then, venerable sir, King Disampati addressed a certain man: 'Come now, good sir, go to the young man named Jotipāla. Approach him and say: 'May it be well with the venerable Jotipāla. King Disampati summons the venerable Jotipāla, the young man. King Disampati wishes to see the venerable Jotipāla, the young man.'" 'Yes, sire,' replied that man to King Disampati, venerable sir. He then approached Jotipāla the young man and said: 'May it be well with the venerable Jotipāla. King Disampati summons the venerable Jotipāla, the young man. King Disampati wishes to see the venerable Jotipāla, the young man.' 'Very well, venerable sir,' replied Jotipāla the young man to that man. He then approached King Disampati. Upon arriving, he exchanged polite greetings with King Disampati. After exchanging courteous and memorable conversation, he sat down to one side. Seated to one side, King Disampati said to Jotipāla the young man: 'Let the venerable Jotipāla instruct us. May the venerable Jotipāla not refuse to instruct us. I will appoint you to your father’s position and consecrate you as Govinda.' 'Very well, venerable sir,' replied Jotipāla the young man to King Disampati. Then, venerable sir, King Disampati consecrated Jotipāla the young man as Govinda and appointed him to his father’s position. Once Jotipāla was consecrated as Govinda and appointed to his father’s position, he instructed in the same matters his father had instructed, and he also instructed in matters his father had not instructed. He accomplished the same tasks his father had accomplished, and he also accomplished tasks his father had not accomplished. People then said of him: 'Indeed, Govinda the brahmin! Indeed, Mahāgovinda the brahmin!' Thus, venerable sir, in this way, the appellation 'Mahāgovinda' arose for the young man Jotipāla.

## Rajjasaṃvibhajanaṃ

### The Division of the Kingdom

306. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena te cha khattiyā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te cha khattiye etadavoca – ‘‘disampati kho, bho, rājā jiṇṇo vuddho mahallako addhagato vayoanuppatto, ko nu kho pana, bho, jānāti jīvitaṃ? Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ disampatimhi raññe kālaṅkate rājakattāro reṇuṃ rājaputtaṃ rajje abhisiñceyyuṃ. Āyantu, bhonto, yena reṇu rājaputto tenupasaṅkamatha; upasaṅkamitvā reṇuṃ rājaputtaṃ evaṃ vadetha – ‘‘mayaṃ kho bhoto reṇussa sahāyā piyā manāpā appaṭikūlā, yaṃsukho bhavaṃ taṃsukhā mayaṃ, yaṃdukkho bhavaṃ taṃdukkhā mayaṃ. Disampati kho, bho, rājā jiṇṇo vuddho mahallako addhagato vayoanuppatto, ko nu kho pana, bho, jānāti jīvitaṃ? Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ disampatimhi raññe kālaṅkate rājakattāro bhavantaṃ reṇuṃ rajje abhisiñceyyuṃ. Sace bhavaṃ reṇu rajjaṃ labhetha, saṃvibhajetha no rajjenā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ bho’’ti kho, bho, te cha khattiyā mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa paṭissutvā yena reṇu rājaputto tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā reṇuṃ rājaputtaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mayaṃ kho bhoto reṇussa sahāyā piyā manāpā appaṭikūlā; yaṃsukho bhavaṃ taṃsukhā mayaṃ, yaṃdukkho bhavaṃ taṃdukkhā mayaṃ. Disampati kho, bho, rājā jiṇṇo vuddho mahallako addhagato vayoanuppatto, ko nu kho pana bho jānāti jīvitaṃ? Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ disampatimhi raññe kālaṅkate rājakattāro bhavantaṃ reṇuṃ rajje abhisiñceyyuṃ. Sace bhavaṃ reṇu rajjaṃ labhetha, saṃvibhajetha no rajjenā’’ti. ‘‘Ko nu kho, bho, añño mama vijite sukho bhavetha [sukhā bhaveyyātha (ka.), sukhaṃ bhaveyyātha, sukhamedheyyātha (sī. pī.),sukha medhetha (?)], aññatra bhavantebhi? Sacāhaṃ, bho, rajjaṃ labhissāmi, saṃvibhajissāmi vo rajjenā’’’ti.

306."Then, sirs, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached those six princes and said to them: 'Sirs, King Disampati is old, aged, burdened with years, advanced in life. Who knows how long he may live? It is possible that when King Disampati passes away, the royal officials will anoint Prince Reṇu to the throne. Come, sirs, let us approach Prince Reṇu and say to him: "We, sir, are Prince Reṇu’s friends, dear and beloved, agreeable and not displeasing to him. Whatever is happiness for you is happiness for us; whatever is suffering for you is suffering for us. Sir, King Disampati is old, aged, burdened with years, advanced in life. Who knows how long he may live? It is possible that when King Disampati passes away, the royal officials will anoint you, Prince Reṇu, to the throne. If you, Prince Reṇu, obtain the kingdom, share the kingdom with us." 'Very well, sir,' replied those six princes to the brahmin Mahāgovinda. Then they approached Prince Reṇu and said to him: 'We, sir, are Prince Reṇu’s friends, dear and beloved, agreeable and not displeasing to him. Whatever is happiness for you is happiness for us; whatever is suffering for you is suffering for us. Sir, King Disampati is old, aged, burdened with years, advanced in life. Who knows how long he may live? It is possible that when King Disampati passes away, the royal officials will anoint you, Prince Reṇu, to the throne. If you, Prince Reṇu, obtain the kingdom, share the kingdom with us.' 'Who else, sirs, should find happiness in my territory other than you? If I, sirs, obtain the kingdom, I will share the kingdom with you.'"

307. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, ahorattānaṃ accayena rājā disampati kālamakāsi. Disampatimhi raññe kālaṅkate rājakattāro reṇuṃ rājaputtaṃ rajje abhisiñciṃsu. Abhisitto reṇu rajjena pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappito samaṅgībhūto paricāreti. Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena te cha khattiyā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te cha khattiye etadavoca – ‘‘disampati kho, bho, rājā kālaṅkato. Abhisitto reṇu rajjena pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappito samaṅgībhūto paricāreti. Ko nu kho pana, bho, jānāti, madanīyā kāmā? Āyantu, bhonto, yena reṇu rājā tenupasaṅkamatha; upasaṅkamitvā reṇuṃ rājānaṃ evaṃ vadetha – disampati kho, bho, rājā kālaṅkato, abhisitto bhavaṃ reṇu rajjena, sarati bhavaṃ taṃ vacana’’’nti?

307."Then, sirs, with the passing of days and nights, King Disampati died. When King Disampati had passed away, the royal officials consecrated Prince Reṇu to the kingship. Once consecrated to the kingship, Reṇu lived indulging in the five strands of sensual pleasure, furnished and replete with them. Then, sirs, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached those six princes and said to them: 'Sirs, King Disampati has passed away. Reṇu has been consecrated to the kingship and lives indulging in the five strands of sensual pleasure, furnished and replete with them. Who knows, sirs, the intoxicating nature of sensual pleasures? Come, sirs, let us approach King Reṇu and say to him: "Sirs, King Disampati has passed away, and you, sir, Reṇu, have been consecrated to the kingship. Do you remember that word?" '"

308. ‘‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho, bho, te cha khattiyā mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa paṭissutvā yena reṇu rājā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā reṇuṃ rājānaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘disampati kho, bho, rājā kālaṅkato, abhisitto bhavaṃ reṇu rajjena, sarati bhavaṃ taṃ vacana’’nti? ‘‘Sarāmahaṃ, bho, taṃ vacanaṃ [vacananti (syā. ka.)]. Ko nu kho, bho, pahoti imaṃ mahāpathaviṃ uttarena āyataṃ dakkhiṇena sakaṭamukhaṃ sattadhā samaṃ suvibhattaṃ vibhajitu’’nti? ‘‘Ko nu kho, bho, añño pahoti, aññatra mahāgovindena brāhmaṇenā’’ti? Atha kho, bho, reṇu rājā aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, ambho purisa, yena mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ evaṃ vadehi – ‘rājā taṃ, bhante, reṇu āmantetī’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ devā’’ti kho, bho, so puriso reṇussa rañño paṭissutvā yena mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘rājā taṃ, bhante, reṇu āmantetī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo tassa purisassa paṭissutvā yena reṇu rājā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā reṇunā raññā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho, bho, mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ reṇu rājā etadavoca – ‘‘etu, bhavaṃ govindo, imaṃ mahāpathaviṃ uttarena āyataṃ dakkhiṇena sakaṭamukhaṃ sattadhā samaṃ suvibhattaṃ vibhajatū’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo reṇussa rañño paṭissutvā imaṃ mahāpathaviṃ uttarena āyataṃ dakkhiṇena sakaṭamukhaṃ sattadhā samaṃ suvibhattaṃ vibhaji. Sabbāni sakaṭamukhāni paṭṭhapesi [aṭṭhapesi (sī. pī.)]. Tatra sudaṃ majjhe reṇussa rañño janapado hoti.

308."'Yes, sir,' replied those six khattiyas to the brahmin Mahāgovinda. Then they approached King Reṇu and said to him: 'Sir, King Disampati has passed away. You, King Reṇu, have been consecrated as king. Do you remember that promise?' 'I remember that promise, sirs. But who, sirs, is capable of dividing this great earth—stretching lengthwise from north to south, with its cart-yoke shaped border—into seven equal and well-apportioned parts?' 'Who else, sir, but the brahmin Mahāgovinda could do it?' Then, sir, King Reṇu addressed a certain man: 'Come, good man, go to the brahmin Mahāgovinda and say to him: "The king, venerable sir, King Reṇu, summons you." 'Yes, Your Majesty,' replied that man to King Reṇu. He then went to the brahmin Mahāgovinda and said: 'The king, venerable sir, King Reṇu, summons you.' 'Very well, sir,' replied the brahmin Mahāgovinda to that man. He then went to King Reṇu and exchanged courteous greetings with him. After pleasant and meaningful conversation, he sat down to one side. Seated to one side, King Reṇu said to the brahmin Mahāgovinda: 'Come, Master Govinda, divide this great earth—stretching lengthwise from north to south, with its cart-yoke shaped border—into seven equal and well-apportioned parts.' 'Very well, sir,' replied the brahmin Mahāgovinda to King Reṇu. He then divided the great earth—stretching lengthwise from north to south, with its cart-yoke shaped border—into seven equal and well-apportioned parts. He established all the cart-yoke shaped boundaries. And in the very center, it seems, lay King Reṇu’s kingdom.

309. Dantapuraṃ kaliṅgānaṃ [kāliṅgānaṃ (syā. pī. ka.)], assakānañca potanaṃ.

309.Dantapura of the Kalingas, and Potana of the Assakas.

Mahesayaṃ [māhissati (sī. syā. pī.)] avantīnaṃ, sovīrānañca rorukaṃ.

Mahesaya of the Avantīs, and Roruka of the Sovīras.

Mithilā ca videhānaṃ, campā aṅgesu māpitā;Bārāṇasī ca kāsīnaṃ, ete govindamāpitāti.

Mithilā of the Videhans, Campā established among the Aṅgas; Bārāṇasī of the Kāsīs—these were established by Govinda.

310. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, te cha khattiyā yathāsakena lābhena attamanā ahesuṃ paripuṇṇasaṅkappā – ‘‘yaṃ vata no ahosi icchitaṃ, yaṃ ākaṅkhitaṃ, yaṃ adhippetaṃ, yaṃ abhipatthitaṃ, taṃ no laddha’’nti.

310."Then, sir, those six princes were pleased with their respective gains, their intentions fulfilled: 'Indeed, what we wanted, what we hoped for, what we had in mind, what we yearned for, that we have obtained.'"

‘‘Sattabhū brahmadatto ca, vessabhū bharato saha;Reṇu dve dhataraṭṭhā ca, tadāsuṃ satta bhāradhā’ti.

Sattabhū, Brahmadatta, Vessabhū together with Bharata; Reṇu and the two Dhataraṭṭhas—these were the seven Bhāradvājas.

## Paṭhamabhāṇavāro niṭṭhito.

### The first recitation section is concluded.

## Kittisaddaabbhuggamanaṃ

### The rise of widespread fame.

311. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, te cha khattiyā yena mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘yathā kho bhavaṃ govindo reṇussa rañño sahāyo piyo manāpo appaṭikūlo. Evameva kho bhavaṃ govindo amhākampi sahāyo piyo manāpo appaṭikūlo, anusāsatu no bhavaṃ govindo; mā no bhavaṃ govindo anusāsaniyā paccabyāhāsī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo tesaṃ channaṃ khattiyānaṃ paccassosi. Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo satta ca rājāno khattiye muddhāvasitte rajje [muddhābhisitte rajjena (syā.)] anusāsi, satta ca brāhmaṇamahāsāle satta ca nhātakasatāni mante vācesi.

311.“Then, sirs, those six khattiyas approached the brahmin Mahāgovinda. Having approached, they said to the brahmin Mahāgovinda: ‘Just as the venerable Govinda is King Reṇu’s friend, dear and beloved, pleasing and agreeable, so too is the venerable Govinda our friend, dear and beloved, pleasing and agreeable. Let the venerable Govinda advise us; may the venerable Govinda not refuse to advise us.’ ‘Very well, sirs,’ replied the brahmin Mahāgovinda to those six khattiyas. Then, sirs, the brahmin Mahāgovinda advised the seven anointed khattiya kings in their kingdoms, and had the seven great brahmin magnates and the seven hundred initiates recite the mantras.

312. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa aparena samayena evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggacchi [abbhuggañchi (sī. pī.)] – ‘‘sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmānaṃ passati, sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmunā sākaccheti sallapati mantetī’’ti. Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘mayhaṃ kho evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmānaṃ passati, sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmunā sākaccheti sallapati mantetī’ti. Na kho panāhaṃ brahmānaṃ passāmi, na brahmunā sākacchemi, na brahmunā sallapāmi, na brahmunā mantemi. Sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ – ‘yo vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyati, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyati, so brahmānaṃ passati brahmunā sākaccheti brahmunā sallapati brahmunā mantetī’ti. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyeyyaṃ, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyeyya’’nti.

312.“Then, sirs, after some time, such a good reputation arose about the brahmin Mahāgovinda: ‘The brahmin Mahāgovinda sees Brahmā face to face; the brahmin Mahāgovinda converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.’ Then, sirs, the brahmin Mahāgovinda thought: ‘Such a good reputation has arisen about me: “The brahmin Mahāgovinda sees Brahmā face to face; the brahmin Mahāgovinda converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” But I do not see Brahmā, nor do I converse, talk, or consult with Brahmā. However, I have heard the brahmin elders, the old and venerable, the teachers and teachers of teachers, saying: “Whoever goes into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and cultivates the meditation on compassion, he sees Brahmā, converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” Why don’t I go into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and cultivate the meditation on compassion?’

313. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena reṇu rājā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā reṇuṃ rājānaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mayhaṃ kho, bho, evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmānaṃ passati, sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmunā sākaccheti sallapati mantetī’ti. Na kho panāhaṃ, bho, brahmānaṃ passāmi, na brahmunā sākacchemi, na brahmunā sallapāmi, na brahmunā mantemi. Sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ – ‘yo vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyati, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyati, so brahmānaṃ passati, brahmunā sākaccheti brahmunā sallapati brahmunā mantetī’ti. Icchāmahaṃ, bho, vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyituṃ, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyituṃ; namhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo aññatra ekena bhattābhihārenā’’ti. ‘‘Yassadāni bhavaṃ govindo kālaṃ maññatī’’ti.

313.“Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached King Reṇu. Having approached, he said to King Reṇu: ‘Such a good reputation has arisen about me, sir: “The brahmin Mahāgovinda sees Brahmā face to face; the brahmin Mahāgovinda converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” But I, sir, do not see Brahmā, nor do I converse, talk, or consult with Brahmā. However, I have heard the brahmin elders, the old and venerable, the teachers and teachers of teachers, saying: “Whoever goes into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and cultivates the meditation on compassion, he sees Brahmā, converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” I wish, sir, to go into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and to cultivate the meditation on compassion; no one should approach me except one bringing food.’ ‘Let the venerable Govinda do as he thinks fit.’

314. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena te cha khattiyā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te cha khattiye etadavoca – ‘‘mayhaṃ kho, bho, evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmānaṃ passati, sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmunā sākaccheti sallapati mantetī’ti. Na kho panāhaṃ, bho, brahmānaṃ passāmi, na brahmunā sākacchemi, na brahmunā sallapāmi, na brahmunā mantemi. Sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ, ‘yo vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyati, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyati, so brahmānaṃ passati brahmunā sākaccheti brahmunā sallapati brahmunā mantetī’ti. Icchāmahaṃ, bho, vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyituṃ, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyituṃ; namhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo aññatra ekena bhattābhihārenā’’ti. ‘‘Yassadāni bhavaṃ govindo kālaṃ maññatī’’’ti.

314.“Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached those six khattiyas. Having approached, he said to them: ‘Sir, such a good reputation has arisen about me: “The brahmin Mahāgovinda sees Brahmā face to face; the brahmin Mahāgovinda converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” But, sir, I do not see Brahmā, nor do I converse, talk, or consult with Brahmā. However, I have heard this from the brahmin elders, the old and venerable, the teachers and teachers of teachers, who said: “Whoever goes into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and cultivates the meditation on compassion, he sees Brahmā, converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” I wish, sir, to go into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and to cultivate the meditation on compassion; no one should approach me except one bringing food.’ ‘Let the venerable Govinda do as he thinks fit.’

315. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena te satta ca brāhmaṇamahāsālā satta ca nhātakasatāni tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te satta ca brāhmaṇamahāsāle satta ca nhātakasatāni etadavoca – ‘‘mayhaṃ kho, bho, evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmānaṃ passati, sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmunā sākaccheti sallapati mantetī’ti. Na kho panāhaṃ, bho, brahmānaṃ passāmi, na brahmunā sākacchemi, na brahmunā sallapāmi, na brahmunā mantemi. Sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ – ‘yo vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyati, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyati, so brahmānaṃ passati, brahmunā sākaccheti, brahmunā sallapati, brahmunā mantetī’ti. Tena hi, bho, yathāsute yathāpariyatte mante vitthārena sajjhāyaṃ karotha, aññamaññañca mante vācetha; icchāmahaṃ, bho, vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyituṃ, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyituṃ; namhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo aññatra ekena bhattābhihārenā’’ti. ‘‘Yassa dāni bhavaṃ govindo kālaṃ maññatī’’ti.

315.“Then, sirs, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached those seven great brahmin magnates and the seven hundred initiates. Having approached, he said to them: ‘Sirs, such a good reputation has arisen about me: “The brahmin Mahāgovinda sees Brahmā face to face; the brahmin Mahāgovinda converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” But, sirs, I do not see Brahmā, nor do I converse, talk, or consult with Brahmā. However, I have heard the brahmin elders, the old and venerable, the teachers and teachers of teachers, say: “Whoever goes into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and cultivates the meditation on compassion, he sees Brahmā, converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” Therefore, sirs, recite the mantras in detail as you have learned and mastered them, and teach each other the mantras. I wish, sirs, to go into seclusion for the four months of the rains and cultivate the meditation on compassion. No one should approach me except one bringing food.’ ‘Let the venerable Govinda do as he thinks fit.’

316. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena cattārīsā bhariyā sādisiyo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā cattārīsā bhariyā sādisiyo etadavoca – ‘‘mayhaṃ kho, bhotī, evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmānaṃ passati, sakkhi mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmunā sākaccheti sallapati mantetī’ti. Na kho panāhaṃ, bhotī, brahmānaṃ passāmi, na brahmunā sākacchemi, na brahmunā sallapāmi, na brahmunā mantemi. Sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ ‘yo vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyati, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyati, so brahmānaṃ passati, brahmunā sākaccheti, brahmunā sallapati, brahmunā mantetīti, icchāmahaṃ, bhotī, vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyituṃ, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyituṃ; namhi kenaci upasaṅkamitabbo aññatra ekena bhattābhihārenā’’ti. ‘‘Yassa dāni bhavaṃ govindo kālaṃ maññatī’’’ti.

316.“Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached his forty wives of equal status. Having approached, he said to those forty wives of equal status: ‘Ladies, such a good reputation has arisen about me: “The brahmin Mahāgovinda sees Brahmā face to face; he converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” But, ladies, I do not see Brahmā, nor do I converse, talk, or consult with Brahmā. However, I have heard the brahmin elders, the old and venerable, the teachers and teachers of teachers, saying: “Whoever goes into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and cultivates the meditation on compassion, sees Brahmā, converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” I wish, ladies, to go into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and to cultivate the meditation on compassion; no one should approach me except one bringing food.’ ‘Let the venerable Govinda do as he thinks fit.’

317. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo puratthimena nagarassa navaṃ sandhāgāraṃ kārāpetvā vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyi, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyi; nāssudha koci upasaṅkamati [upasaṅkami (pī.)] aññatra ekena bhattābhihārena. Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa catunnaṃ māsānaṃ accayena ahudeva ukkaṇṭhanā ahu paritassanā – ‘‘sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ – ‘yo vassike cattāro māse paṭisallīyati, karuṇaṃ jhānaṃ jhāyati, so brahmānaṃ passati, brahmunā sākaccheti brahmunā sallapati brahmunā mantetī’ti. Na kho panāhaṃ brahmānaṃ passāmi, na brahmunā sākacchemi na brahmunā sallapāmi na brahmunā mantemī’’’ti.

317.“Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda had a new assembly hall built to the east of the city. There he went into seclusion for the four months of the rains and cultivated the meditation on compassion. No one approached him there except one bringing food. Then, sir, when four months had passed, the brahmin Mahāgovinda experienced discontent and anxiety: ‘I have heard from the brahmin elders, the old and venerable, the teachers and teachers of teachers, who say: “Whoever goes into seclusion for the four months of the rains, and cultivates the meditation on compassion, he sees Brahmā, converses, talks, and consults with Brahmā.” But I do not see Brahmā, nor do I converse, talk, or consult with Brahmā.’

## Brahmunā sākacchā

### Conversation with Brahmā

318. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, brahmā sanaṅkumāro mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evameva, brahmaloke antarahito mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa sammukhe pāturahosi. Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa ahudeva bhayaṃ ahu chambhitattaṃ ahu lomahaṃso yathā taṃ adiṭṭhapubbaṃ rūpaṃ disvā. Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo bhīto saṃviggo lomahaṭṭhajāto brahmānaṃ sanaṅkumāraṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

318.Then, sir, Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra, having known with his mind the thought and reflection in the mind of the brahmin Mahāgovinda, just as a strong man might draw in or stretch out his arm, so he vanished from the Brahmā world and appeared before the brahmin Mahāgovinda. Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda experienced fear, trepidation, and his hair stood on end, as happens when seeing a form never seen before. Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda, frightened, agitated, with hair standing on end, addressed Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra in verse:

‘‘‘Vaṇṇavā yasavā sirimā, ko nu tvamasi mārisa;Ajānantā taṃ pucchāma, kathaṃ jānemu taṃ maya’’nti.

'Handsome, glorious, and radiant, who are you, sir? Not knowing you, we ask you; how may we know you?'

‘‘Maṃ ve kumāraṃ jānanti, brahmaloke sanantanaṃ [sanantica (ka.)];Sabbe jānanti maṃ devā, evaṃ govinda jānahi’’.

'They know me, the youth Sanankumāra, in the Brahmā world. All the gods know me; know this, Govinda.'

‘‘‘Āsanaṃ udakaṃ pajjaṃ, madhusākañca [madhupākañca (sī. syā. pī.)] brahmuno;Agghe bhavantaṃ pucchāma, agghaṃ kurutu no bhavaṃ’’.

'A seat, water, and foot-washing, and the honey-gruel of Brahmā, we ask you, honored one, to accept our offering.'

‘‘Paṭiggaṇhāma te agghaṃ, yaṃ tvaṃ govinda bhāsasi;Diṭṭhadhammahitatthāya, samparāya sukhāya ca;Katāvakāso pucchassu, yaṃ kiñci abhipatthita’’nti.

'We accept your offering, Govinda, as you declare it, for the benefit and happiness in this life and the life to come. You have the opportunity, ask whatever you desire.'

319. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘katāvakāso khomhi brahmunā sanaṅkumārena. Kiṃ nu kho ahaṃ brahmānaṃ sanaṅkumāraṃ puccheyyaṃ diṭṭhadhammikaṃ vā atthaṃ samparāyikaṃ vā’ti? Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘kusalo kho ahaṃ diṭṭhadhammikānaṃ atthānaṃ, aññepi maṃ diṭṭhadhammikaṃ atthaṃ pucchanti. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ brahmānaṃ sanaṅkumāraṃ samparāyikaññeva atthaṃ puccheyya’nti. Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo brahmānaṃ sanaṅkumāraṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

319.Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda had this thought: 'An opportunity has been made for me by the Brahmā Sanankumāra. What should I ask the Brahmā Sanankumāra about? A matter pertaining to this life or the next?' Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda had this thought: 'I am skilled in matters pertaining to this life, and others also ask me about matters pertaining to this life. Why don’t I ask the Brahmā Sanankumāra about a matter pertaining to the next life?' Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda addressed the Brahmā Sanankumāra in verse:

‘‘Pucchāmi brahmānaṃ sanaṅkumāraṃ,Kaṅkhī akaṅkhiṃ paravediyesu;Katthaṭṭhito kimhi ca sikkhamāno,Pappoti macco amataṃ brahmaloka’’nti.

'I ask Brahmā Sanankumāra,
I, though doubtful, ask you, the undoubting one, concerning matters known by others:
Where standing, practicing what,
Does a mortal attain the deathless Brahmā world?'

‘‘Hitvā mamattaṃ manujesu brahme,Ekodibhūto karuṇedhimutto [karuṇādhimutto (sī. syā. pī.)];Nirāmagandho virato methunasmā,Etthaṭṭhito ettha ca sikkhamāno;Pappoti macco amataṃ brahmaloka’’nti.

'Abandoning possessiveness among humans, O Brahmā,
Having become unified, emancipated by compassion;
Without sensual taint, abstaining from sexual intercourse,
Standing firm here, training here,
A mortal attains the deathless Brahma-world.'

320. ‘‘Hitvā mamatta’nti ahaṃ bhoto ājānāmi. Idhekacco appaṃ vā bhogakkhandhaṃ pahāya mahantaṃ vā bhogakkhandhaṃ pahāya appaṃ vā ñātiparivaṭṭaṃ pahāya mahantaṃ vā ñātiparivaṭṭaṃ pahāya kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajati, ‘iti hitvā mamatta’nti ahaṃ bhoto ājānāmi. ‘Ekodibhūto’ti ahaṃ bhoto ājānāmi. Idhekacco vivittaṃ senāsanaṃ bhajati araññaṃ rukkhamūlaṃ pabbataṃ kandaraṃ giriguhaṃ susānaṃ vanapatthaṃ abbhokāsaṃ palālapuñjaṃ, iti ekodibhūto’ti ahaṃ bhoto ājānāmi. ‘Karuṇedhimutto’ti ahaṃ bhoto ājānāmi. Idhekacco karuṇāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharati, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadhotiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ karuṇāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena pharitvā viharati. Iti ‘karuṇedhimutto’ti ahaṃ bhoto ājānāmi. Āmagandhe ca kho ahaṃ bhoto bhāsamānassa na ājānāmi.

320."I understand, sir, 'having abandoned possessiveness' as follows: Here, someone gives up a small or large amount of wealth, leaves behind a small or large circle of relatives, shaves off hair and beard, dons ochre robes, and goes forth from the household life into homelessness. Thus, I understand, sir, 'having abandoned possessiveness.' I understand, sir, 'one-pointed' as follows: Here, someone resorts to a secluded dwelling—a forest, the foot of a tree, a mountain, a ravine, a cave, a charnel ground, a jungle thicket, an open space, a heap of straw. Thus, I understand, sir, 'one-pointed.' I understand, sir, 'emancipated by compassion' as follows: Here, someone dwells pervading one direction with a mind imbued with compassion, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus, above, below, across, everywhere, identifying oneself with all, one dwells pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with compassion—vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility, without ill will. Thus, I understand, sir, 'emancipated by compassion.' But, sir, I do not understand the odor of raw flesh you speak of."

‘‘Ke āmagandhā manujesu brahme,Ete avidvā idha brūhi dhīra;Kenāvaṭā [kenāvuṭā (syā.)] vāti pajā kurutu [kururū (syā.), kuruṭṭharū (pī.), kurūru (?)],Āpāyikā nivutabrahmalokā’’ti.

'What are the taints among humans, O Brahmā?
I am ignorant of these; tell me here, O wise one.
By what are beings enveloped, and become putrid,
Bound for states of woe, and shut out from the Brahmā world?'

‘‘Kodho mosavajjaṃ nikati ca dubbho,Kadariyatā atimāno usūyā;Icchā vivicchā paraheṭhanā ca,Lobho ca doso ca mado ca moho;Etesu yuttā anirāmagandhā,Āpāyikā nivutabrahmalokā’’ti.

'Anger, lying, trickery, treachery,
Avarice, conceit, envy,
Craving, stinginess, harming others,
Greed, hatred, pride, and delusion—
Those yoked to these are tainted by raw smell,
Are bound for states of woe, and shut out from the Brahmā world.'

‘‘Yathā kho ahaṃ bhoto āmagandhe bhāsamānassa ājānāmi. Te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā. Pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. ‘‘Yassadāni bhavaṃ govindo kālaṃ maññatī’’ti.

"As I understand the faults you speak of, sir, they are not easily overcome while living at home. I will go forth, sir, from the household life into homelessness." "Then let Master Govinda do as he thinks fit."

## Reṇurājaāmantanā

### Addressing King Reṇu

321. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena reṇu rājā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā reṇuṃ rājānaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘aññaṃ dāni bhavaṃ purohitaṃ pariyesatu, yo bhoto rajjaṃ anusāsissati. Icchāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā. Pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti.

321.“Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached King Reṇu. Having approached, he said to King Reṇu: ‘Now, Your Majesty, seek another chaplain who will instruct your kingdom. I wish, sir, to go forth from the household life into homelessness. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sir, from the household life into homelessness.’

‘‘Āmantayāmi rājānaṃ, reṇuṃ bhūmipatiṃ ahaṃ;Tvaṃ pajānassu rajjena, nāhaṃ porohicce rame’’.

‘I address King Reṇu, lord of the earth: You yourself know your kingdom; I do not delight in the office of high priest.’

‘‘Sace te ūnaṃ kāmehi, ahaṃ paripūrayāmi te;Yo taṃ hiṃsati vāremi, bhūmisenāpati ahaṃ;Tuvaṃ pitā ahaṃ putto, mā no govinda pājahi’’ [pājehi (aṭṭhakathāyaṃ saṃvaṇṇitapāṭhantaraṃ)].

‘If you are deficient in sensual pleasures, I will fulfill them for you. Whoever harms you, I will restrain—I am the master of the earth. You are the father, I am the son; do not abandon us, Govinda.’

‘‘Namatthi ūnaṃ kāmehi, hiṃsitā me na vijjati;Amanussavaco sutvā, tasmāhaṃ na gahe rame’’.

‘I am not deficient in sensual pleasures, nor is there anyone who injures me; having heard the words of a non-human being, therefore I do not delight in dwelling in a house.’

‘‘Amanusso kathaṃvaṇṇo, kiṃ te atthaṃ abhāsatha;Yañca sutvā jahāsi no, gehe amhe ca kevalī’’.

‘What sort of appearance did that non-human have? What purpose did he tell you of? And having heard what, do you abandon us and our home, leaving us all alone?’

‘‘Upavutthassa me pubbe, yiṭṭhukāmassa me sato;Aggi pajjalito āsi, kusapattaparitthato’’.

‘Formerly, while I was observing the Uposatha, when I was intent on sacrificing, a fire was lit, surrounded by kusa grass.’

‘‘Tato me brahmā pāturahu, brahmalokā sanantano;So me pañhaṃ viyākāsi, taṃ sutvā na gahe rame’’.

‘Then Brahmā appeared to me, Sanankumāra, from the Brahmā world; he explained my question. Having heard that, I do not delight in a home.’

‘‘Saddahāmi ahaṃ bhoto, yaṃ tvaṃ govinda bhāsasi;Amanussavaco sutvā, kathaṃ vattetha aññathā.

‘I believe you, Master Govinda, in what you say. Having heard the words of a non-human, how could you act otherwise?’

‘‘Te taṃ anuvattissāma, satthā govinda no bhavaṃ;Maṇi yathā veḷuriyo, akāco vimalo subho;Evaṃ suddhā carissāma, govindassānusāsane’’ti.

‘We will follow you; Govinda, be our teacher. Just as a beryl gem is flawless, clear, pure, and lovely, so shall we live purely under Govinda’s instruction.’

‘‘‘Sace bhavaṃ govindo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissati, mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma. Atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti.

‘If Master Govinda goes forth from the household life into homelessness, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Whatever your destiny, that will be our destiny.’

## Cha khattiyaāmantanā

### Addressing the Six Kshatriyas

322. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena te cha khattiyā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te cha khattiye etadavoca – ‘‘aññaṃ dāni bhavanto purohitaṃ pariyesantu, yo bhavantānaṃ rajje anusāsissati. Icchāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā. Pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. Atha kho, bho, te cha khattiyā ekamantaṃ apakkamma evaṃ samacintesuṃ – ‘‘ime kho brāhmaṇā nāma dhanaluddhā; yaṃnūna mayaṃ mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ dhanena sikkheyyāmā’’ti. Te mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘saṃvijjati kho, bho, imesu sattasu rajjesu pahūtaṃ sāpateyyaṃ, tato bhoto yāvatakena attho, tāvatakaṃ āharīyata’’nti. ‘‘Alaṃ, bho, mamapidaṃ pahūtaṃ sāpateyyaṃ bhavantānaṃyeva vāhasā. Tamahaṃ sabbaṃ pahāya agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāmi. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā, pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. Atha kho, bho, te cha khattiyā ekamantaṃ apakkamma evaṃ samacintesuṃ – ‘‘ime kho brāhmaṇā nāma itthiluddhā; yaṃnūna mayaṃ mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ itthīhi sikkheyyāmā’’ti. Te mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘saṃvijjanti kho, bho, imesu sattasu rajjesu pahūtā itthiyo, tato bhoto yāvatikāhi attho, tāvatikā ānīyata’’nti. ‘‘Alaṃ, bho, mamapimā [mamapitā (ka.), mamapi (sī.)] cattārīsā bhariyā sādisiyo. Tāpāhaṃ sabbā pahāya agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāmi. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā, pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriyanti’’.

322.“Then, sir, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached those six kings and said to them: ‘Now, sirs, seek another advisor who will instruct you in governance. I wish, sirs, to go forth from the household life into homelessness. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sirs, from the household life into homelessness.’ Then, sir, those six kings withdrew to one side and considered: ‘These brahmins are said to be greedy for wealth. What if we try to tempt the brahmin Mahāgovinda with wealth?’ They approached the brahmin Mahāgovinda and said: ‘Sir, in these seven kingdoms there is abundant property. Take from it as much as you need.’ ‘Enough, sirs! I already have abundant property thanks to you. I will renounce it all and go forth from the household life into homelessness. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sirs, from the household life into homelessness.’ Then, sir, those six kings withdrew to one side and considered: ‘These brahmins are said to be greedy for women. What if we try to tempt the brahmin Mahāgovinda with women?’ They approached the brahmin Mahāgovinda and said: ‘Sir, in these seven kingdoms there are many women. Bring as many as you desire.’ ‘Enough, sirs! I already have forty wives of equal status. I will renounce them all and go forth from the household life into homelessness. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sirs, from the household life into homelessness.’

323. ‘‘Sace bhavaṃ govindo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissati, mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatīti.

323.“If the venerable Govinda goes forth from the household life into homelessness, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Whatever your destiny will be, that will be our destiny as well.”

‘‘Sace jahatha kāmāni, yattha satto puthujjano;Ārambhavho daḷhā hotha, khantibalasamāhitā.

‘If you abandon sensual pleasures, to which ordinary people are attached, be energetic, be firm, endowed with the power of patience.’

‘‘Esa maggo ujumaggo, esa maggo anuttaro;Saddhammo sabbhi rakkhito, brahmalokūpapattiyāti.

‘This is the straight path, this path is unsurpassed. The true Dhamma is guarded by the good, for the attainment of the Brahmā world.’

‘‘Tena hi bhavaṃ govindo satta vassāni āgametu. Sattannaṃ vassānaṃ accayena mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti.

“Then, venerable Govinda, wait seven years. After seven years have passed, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness; then whatever your destiny, that will be our destiny.”

‘‘‘Aticiraṃ kho, bho, satta vassāni, nāhaṃ sakkomi, bhavante, satta vassāni āgametuṃ. Ko nu kho pana, bho, jānāti jīvitānaṃ! Gamanīyo samparāyo, mantāyaṃ [mantāya (bahūsu)] boddhabbaṃ, kattabbaṃ kusalaṃ, caritabbaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, natthi jātassa amaraṇaṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā, pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’’nti. ‘‘Tena hi bhavaṃ govindo chabbassāni āgametu…pe… pañca vassāni āgametu… cattāri vassāni āgametu… tīṇi vassāni āgametu… dve vassāni āgametu… ekaṃ vassaṃ āgametu, ekassa vassassa accayena mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti.

“‘It is too long, sir, seven years, and I cannot, honored ones, wait for seven years. For who, sir, knows the span of lives? The future life is to be gone to; counsel must be taken, good deeds must be done, the holy life must be lived, for there is no immortality for one who is born. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sir, from the household life into homelessness.’ ‘Then, good Govinda, wait for six years… five years… four years… three years… two years… one year. After one year has passed, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Then, whatever your destiny, that will be our destiny as well.’

‘‘‘Aticiraṃ kho, bho, ekaṃ vassaṃ, nāhaṃ sakkomi bhavante ekaṃ vassaṃ āgametuṃ. Ko nu kho pana, bho, jānāti jīvitānaṃ! Gamanīyo samparāyo, mantāyaṃ boddhabbaṃ, kattabbaṃ kusalaṃ, caritabbaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, natthi jātassa amaraṇaṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā, pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. ‘‘Tena hi bhavaṃ govindo satta māsāni āgametu, sattannaṃ māsānaṃ accayena mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti.

“‘It is too long, sir, a whole year; I am unable to wait for you for a whole year. For who, sir, knows the span of lives? The next world is to be gone to; counsel should be heeded, good deeds should be done, the holy life should be lived; there is no non-dying for what is born. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sir, from the household life into homelessness.’ ‘Then, good Govinda, wait for seven months; after the passing of seven months, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Then, whatever your destiny is, that will be our destiny as well.’

‘‘‘Aticiraṃ kho, bho, satta māsāni, nāhaṃ sakkomi bhavante satta māsāni āgametuṃ. Ko nu kho pana, bho, jānāti jīvitānaṃ. Gamanīyo samparāyo, mantāyaṃ boddhabbaṃ, kattabbaṃ kusalaṃ, caritabbaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, natthi jātassa amaraṇaṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā, pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti.

“‘It is too long, sir, seven months! I am unable to wait for you for seven months. For who, sir, knows the duration of life? The next world is to be gone to. Counsel must be heeded, good deeds must be done, the holy life must be lived, for there is no death for one who is born. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sir, from the household life into homelessness.’

‘‘‘Tena hi bhavaṃ govindo cha māsāni āgametu…pe… pañca māsāni āgametu… cattāri māsāni āgametu… tīṇi māsāni āgametu… dve māsāni āgametu… ekaṃ māsaṃ āgametu… addhamāsaṃ āgametu, addhamāsassa accayena mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti.

“‘Then, Master Govinda, wait six months… wait five months… wait four months… wait three months… wait two months… wait one month… wait half a month. When half a month has passed, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Then whatever your destiny, that will be our destiny.’

‘‘‘Aticiraṃ kho, bho, addhamāso, nāhaṃ sakkomi bhavante addhamāsaṃ āgametuṃ. Ko nu kho pana, bho, jānāti jīvitānaṃ! Gamanīyo samparāyo, mantāyaṃ boddhabbaṃ, kattabbaṃ kusalaṃ, caritabbaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, natthi jātassa amaraṇaṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā, pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. ‘‘Tena hi bhavaṃ govindo sattāhaṃ āgametu, yāva mayaṃ sake puttabhātaro rajjena [rajje (syā.)] anusāsissāma, sattāhassa accayena mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti. ‘‘Na ciraṃ kho, bho, sattāhaṃ, āgamessāmahaṃ bhavante sattāha’’nti.

“‘It is too long, sir, half a month, and I cannot wait for you for half a month. For who, sir, knows the span of lives? The next world is to be gone to. Counsel must be taken, good deeds must be done, the holy life must be lived, there is no non-dying for one who is born. For as I have heard Brahmā speaking of the taint of sensual pleasures, these are not easily subdued by one dwelling in the household life. I will go forth, sir, from the household life into homelessness.’ ‘Then, good Govinda, wait for seven days, until we instruct our own sons and brothers in the kingdom. After seven days have passed, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Then, whatever your destiny, that will be our destiny.’ ‘Not long, sir, is seven days. I will wait for you, sir, for seven days.’

## Brāhmaṇamahāsālādīnaṃ āmantanā

### The Addressing of the Brahmin Householders and Others

324. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena te satta ca brāhmaṇamahāsālā satta ca nhātakasatāni tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te satta ca brāhmaṇamahāsāle satta ca nhātakasatāni etadavoca – ‘‘aññaṃ dāni bhavanto ācariyaṃ pariyesantu, yo bhavantānaṃ mante vācessati. Icchāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa. Te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā, pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. ‘‘Mā bhavaṃ govindo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji. Pabbajjā, bho, appesakkhā ca appalābhā ca; brahmaññaṃ mahesakkhañca mahālābhañcā’’ti. ‘‘Mā bhavanto evaṃ avacuttha – ‘‘pabbajjā appesakkhā ca appalābhā ca, brahmaññaṃ mahesakkhañca mahālābhañcā’’ti. Ko nu kho, bho, aññatra mayā mahesakkhataro vā mahālābhataro vā! Ahañhi, bho, etarahi rājāva raññaṃ brahmāva brāhmaṇānaṃ [brahmānaṃ (sī. pī. ka.)] devatāva gahapatikānaṃ. Tamahaṃ sabbaṃ pahāya agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāmi. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā. Pabbajissāmahaṃ, bho, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. ‘‘Sace bhavaṃ govindo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissati, mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti.

324.Then, friends, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached those seven wealthy brahmins and the seven hundred graduates. Having approached, he said to them: 'Now, friends, seek another teacher who will recite the mantras to you. I wish, friends, to go forth from the household life into homelessness. For as I have heard Brahmā speak of the foulness, those who dwell in the household life are not thoroughly purified. I will go forth, friends, from the household life into homelessness.' 'Do not, venerable Govinda, go forth from the household life into homelessness. Going forth, friends, is of little influence and little gain; the brahmin life is of great influence and great gain.' 'Do not speak thus, friends—"Going forth is of little influence and little gain; the brahmin life is of great influence and great gain." For who, friends, other than me is more influential or gains more? Now, friends, I am like a king among kings, Brahmā among brahmins, a deity among householders. Abandoning all that, I will go forth from the household life into homelessness. For as I have heard Brahmā speak of the foulness, those who dwell in the household life are not thoroughly purified. I will go forth, friends, from the household life into homelessness.' 'If the venerable Govinda goes forth from the household life into homelessness, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Whatever destiny is yours, that will be our destiny too.'

## Bhariyānaṃ āmantanā

### The Addressing of the Wives

325. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo yena cattārīsā bhariyā sādisiyo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā cattārīsā bhariyā sādisiyo etadavoca – ‘‘yā bhotīnaṃ icchati, sakāni vā ñātikulāni gacchatu aññaṃ vā bhattāraṃ pariyesatu. Icchāmahaṃ, bhotī, agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajituṃ. Yathā kho pana me sutaṃ brahmuno āmagandhe bhāsamānassa, te na sunimmadayā agāraṃ ajjhāvasatā. Pabbajissāmahaṃ, bhotī, agārasmā anagāriya’’nti. ‘‘Tvaññeva no ñāti ñātikāmānaṃ, tvaṃ pana bhattā bhattukāmānaṃ. Sace bhavaṃ govindo agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissati, mayampi agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāma, atha yā te gati, sā no gati bhavissatī’’ti.

325.Then, friends, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached his forty equal wives. Having approached, he said to the forty equal wives: 'Whichever of you ladies wishes, let her return to her own family or seek another husband. I wish, ladies, to go forth from the household life into homelessness. For as I have heard that Brahmā speaks about the foulness, those who dwell in the household do not thoroughly purify themselves. I will go forth, ladies, from the household life into homelessness.' 'You are our kinsman, for those who desire kinsmen; you are our husband, for those who desire a husband. If the venerable Govinda goes forth from the household life into homelessness, we too will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Whatever destiny is yours, that will be our destiny too.'

## Mahāgovindapabbajjā

### The Going Forth of Mahāgovinda

326. ‘‘Atha kho, bho, mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo tassa sattāhassa accayena kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji. Pabbajitaṃ pana mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ satta ca rājāno khattiyā muddhāvasittā satta ca brāhmaṇamahāsālā satta ca nhātakasatāni cattārīsā ca bhariyā sādisiyo anekāni ca khattiyasahassāni anekāni ca brāhmaṇasahassāni anekāni ca gahapatisahassāni anekehi ca itthāgārehi itthiyo kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā mahāgovindaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitaṃ anupabbajiṃsu. Tāya sudaṃ, bho, parisāya parivuto mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo gāmanigamarājadhānīsu cārikaṃ carati. Yaṃ kho pana, bho, tena samayena mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo gāmaṃ vā nigamaṃ vā upasaṅkamati, tattha rājāva hoti raññaṃ, brahmāva brāhmaṇānaṃ, devatāva gahapatikānaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena manussā khipanti vā upakkhalanti vā te evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘namatthu mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa, namatthu satta purohitassā’’’ti.

326.Then, friends, after seven days had passed, the brahmin Mahāgovinda shaved off his hair and beard, put on ochre robes, and went forth from the household life into homelessness. When the brahmin Mahāgovinda had gone forth, seven kings who were anointed khattiyas, seven wealthy brahmins, seven hundred graduates, forty equal wives, and many thousands of khattiyas, many thousands of brahmins, many thousands of householders, and many women from harems shaved off their hair and beards, put on ochre robes, and followed the brahmin Mahāgovinda into homelessness. Surrounded by such an assembly, the brahmin Mahāgovinda wandered on tour among villages, market towns, and royal capitals. Whenever, friends, the brahmin Mahāgovinda approached a village or market town at that time, there he was like a king among kings, like Brahmā among brahmins, like a deity among householders. And at that time, when people were in distress or stumbled, they would say: 'Homage to the brahmin Mahāgovinda! Homage to the seven purohitas!'

327. ‘‘Mahāgovindo, bho, brāhmaṇo mettāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā vihāsi, tathā dutiyaṃ, tathā tatiyaṃ, tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ mettāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena pharitvā vihāsi. Karuṇāsahagatena cetasā…pe… muditāsahagatena cetasā…pe… upekkhāsahagatena cetasā…pe… abyāpajjena pharitvā vihāsi sāvakānañca brahmalokasahabyatāya maggaṃ desesi.

327.The brahmin Mahāgovinda, friends, dwelled pervading one direction with a mind imbued with loving-kindness, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, in every way, he dwelled pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with loving-kindness—vast, expansive, immeasurable, without enmity, without ill will. With a mind imbued with compassion… With a mind imbued with sympathetic joy… With a mind imbued with equanimity… he dwelled pervading without ill will and taught the path to his disciples for companionship with the Brahma-world.

328. ‘‘Ye kho pana, bho, tena samayena mahāgovindassa brāhmaṇassa sāvakā sabbena sabbaṃ sāsanaṃ ājāniṃsu. Te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ brahmalokaṃ upapajjiṃsu. Ye na sabbena sabbaṃ sāsanaṃ ājāniṃsu, te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā appekacce paranimmitavasavattīnaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjiṃsu; appekacce nimmānaratīnaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjiṃsu; appekacce tusitānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjiṃsu; appekacce yāmānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjiṃsu; appekacce tāvatiṃsānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjiṃsu; appekacce cātumahārājikānaṃ devānaṃ sahabyataṃ upapajjiṃsu; ye sabbanihīnaṃ kāyaṃ paripūresuṃ te gandhabbakāyaṃ paripūresuṃ. Iti kho, bho [pana (syā. ka.)], sabbesaṃyeva tesaṃ kulaputtānaṃ amoghā pabbajjā ahosi avañjhā saphalā saudrayā’’’ti.

328.And, friends, those disciples of the great brahmin Mahāgovinda at that time who fully understood the entire teaching—at the dissolution of the body, after death, were reborn in a good destination, in the Brahma-world. Those who did not fully understand the entire teaching—at the dissolution of the body, after death, some were reborn into the company of the Paranimmita-vasavatti devas; some were reborn into the company of the Nimmānarati devas; some were reborn into the company of the Tusita devas; some were reborn into the company of the Yāma devas; some were reborn into the company of the Tāvatiṃsa devas; some were reborn into the company of the Cātumahārājika devas; those who completely fulfilled the most inferior body were reborn among the gandhabbas. Thus, friends, for all those clansmen, their going forth was not in vain, not barren, fruitful, and yielding good results.

329. ‘‘Sarati taṃ bhagavā’’ti? ‘‘Sarāmahaṃ, pañcasikha. Ahaṃ tena samayena mahāgovindo brāhmaṇo ahosiṃ. Ahaṃ tesaṃ sāvakānaṃ brahmalokasahabyatāya maggaṃ desesiṃ. Taṃ kho pana me, pañcasikha, brahmacariyaṃ na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati, yāvadeva brahmalokūpapattiyā.

329.“Does the Blessed One remember that?” “I remember, Pañcasikha. At that time, I was the brahmin Mahāgovinda. I taught those disciples the path to companionship with the Brahma-world. However, Pañcasikha, that practice of the holy life of mine did not lead to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, or to Nibbāna—but only to rebirth in the Brahma-world.

Idaṃ kho pana me, pañcasikha, brahmacariyaṃ ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati. Katamañca taṃ, pañcasikha, brahmacariyaṃ ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo. Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā sammākammanto sammāājīvo sammāvāyāmo sammāsati sammāsamādhi. Idaṃ kho taṃ, pañcasikha, brahmacariyaṃ ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati.

This truly, Pañcasikha, is my holy life, leading solely to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, to Nibbāna. And what, Pañcasikha, is that holy life which leads solely to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, to Nibbāna? It is just this Noble Eightfold Path, namely: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samādhi. This, Pañcasikha, is that holy life which leads solely to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to full enlightenment, to Nibbāna.

330. ‘‘Ye kho pana me, pañcasikha, sāvakā sabbena sabbaṃ sāsanaṃ ājānanti, te āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharanti; ye na sabbena sabbaṃ sāsanaṃ ājānanti, te pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā opapātikā honti tattha parinibbāyino anāvattidhammā tasmā lokā. Ye na sabbena sabbaṃ sāsanaṃ ājānanti, appekacce tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmino honti sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karissanti [karonti (sī. pī.)]. Ye na sabbena sabbaṃ sāsanaṃ ājānanti, appekacce tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sotāpannā honti avinipātadhammā niyatā sambodhiparāyaṇā. Iti kho, pañcasikha, sabbesaṃyeva imesaṃ kulaputtānaṃ amoghā pabbajjā [pabbajā ahosi (ka.)] avañjhā saphalā saudrayā’’ti.

330.Now, Pañcasikha, those of my disciples who fully understand the entire teaching, they, through the destruction of the taints, having realized for themselves with direct knowledge in this very life, attain and abide in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom. Those who do not fully understand the entire teaching, with the destruction of the five lower fetters, are reborn spontaneously and attain final Nibbāna there, not liable to return from that world. Those who do not fully understand the entire teaching, some, with the destruction of three fetters and the attenuation of lust, hatred, and delusion, are once-returners, coming back to this world only once to make an end of suffering. Those who do not fully understand the entire teaching, some, with the destruction of three fetters, are stream-enterers, not liable to fall away, fixed in destiny, with enlightenment as their destination. Thus, Pañcasikha, for all these clansmen, the going forth is not in vain, not barren, fruitful, and yielding good results.”

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamano pañcasikho gandhabbaputto bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tatthevantaradhāyīti.

This the Blessed One said. Pleased, Pañcasikha the gandhabba, delighting in the Blessed One’s words, rejoiced and approved. Having paid homage to the Blessed One, and circumambulated him keeping the right side towards him, he vanished right there.
