# Sampasādanīyasuttaṃ

## The Sampasādanīya Sutta

## Sāriputtasīhanādo

### Sāriputta's Lion's Roar

141. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā nāḷandāyaṃ viharati pāvārikambavane. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘evaṃpasanno ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavati, na cāhu na ca bhavissati na cetarahi vijjati añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññataro yadidaṃ sambodhiya’’nti.

141.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Nāḷandā in Pāvārika’s mango grove. Then the Venerable Sāriputta approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, I am thus confident in the Blessed One: there has not been, nor will there be, nor is there now any other ascetic or brahmin more highly enlightened than the Blessed One with respect to supreme enlightenment.”

142. ‘‘Uḷārā kho te ayaṃ, sāriputta, āsabhī vācā bhāsitā, ekaṃso gahito, sīhanādo nadito – ‘evaṃpasanno ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavati; na cāhu na ca bhavissati na cetarahi vijjati añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññataro yadidaṃ sambodhiya’nti. Kiṃ te [kiṃ nu (sī. pī.), kiṃ nu kho te (syā.)], sāriputta, ye te ahesuṃ atītamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā, sabbe te bhagavanto cetasā ceto paricca viditā – ‘evaṃsīlā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃdhammā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃpaññā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃvihārī te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

142.“Sāriputta, this is a magnificent utterance of yours, a bull's speech, a definitive statement, a lion's roar: ‘Venerable sir, I am thus confident in the Blessed One: there has not been, nor will there be, nor is there now any other ascetic or brahmin more highly enlightened than the Blessed One with respect to supreme enlightenment.’ But, Sāriputta, did you encompass with your mind the minds of all those Blessed Ones, Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones of the past, knowing: ‘Those Blessed Ones were of such virtue, of such Dhamma, of such wisdom, of such abiding, of such liberation’?” “No, venerable sir.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana te [kiṃ pana (sī. pī.)], sāriputta, ye te bhavissanti anāgatamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā, sabbe te bhagavanto cetasā ceto paricca viditā, `evaṃsīlā te bhagavanto bhavissanti itipi, evaṃdhammā…pe… evaṃpaññā… evaṃvihārī… evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto bhavissanti itipī’’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“Then, Sāriputta, regarding those Blessed Ones, Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones, who will be in the future: did you encompass with your mind the minds of all of them, knowing: ‘Those Blessed Ones will be of such virtue, of such Dhamma… of such wisdom… of such abiding… of such liberation’?” “No, venerable sir.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana te [kiṃ pana (sī. pī.)], sāriputta, ahaṃ etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho cetasā ceto paricca vidito – ‘evaṃsīlo bhagavā itipi, evaṃdhammo…pe… evaṃpañño … evaṃvihārī… evaṃvimutto bhagavā itipī’’’ti? ‘‘No hetaṃ, bhante’’.

“Then, Sāriputta, do you now encompass me, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, with your mind, knowing: ‘The Blessed One is of such virtue, of such Dhamma… of such wisdom… of such abiding… of such liberation’?” “No, venerable sir.”

‘‘Ettha ca hi te, sāriputta, atītānāgatapaccuppannesu arahantesu sammāsambuddhesu cetopariyañāṇaṃ natthi. Atha kiṃ carahi te ayaṃ, sāriputta, uḷārā āsabhī vācā bhāsitā, ekaṃso gahito, sīhanādo nadito – ‘evaṃpasanno ahaṃ, bhante, bhagavati, na cāhu na ca bhavissati na cetarahi vijjati añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññataro yadidaṃ sambodhiya’’’nti?

“But, Sāriputta, you do not have the knowledge of encompassing minds regarding Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones, in the past, future, and present. Then why, Sāriputta, was this magnificent utterance of yours, this bull's speech, this definitive statement, this lion's roar, spoken: ‘Venerable sir, I am thus confident in the Blessed One: there has not been, nor will there be, nor is there now any other ascetic or brahmin more highly enlightened than the Blessed One with respect to supreme enlightenment’?”

143. ‘‘Na kho me [na kho panetaṃ (syā. ka.)], bhante, atītānāgatapaccuppannesu arahantesu sammāsambuddhesu cetopariyañāṇaṃ atthi. Api ca, me [me bhante (sī. pī. ka.)] dhammanvayo vidito. Seyyathāpi, bhante, rañño paccantimaṃ nagaraṃ daḷhuddhāpaṃ [daḷhuddāpaṃ (sī. pī. ka.)] daḷhapākāratoraṇaṃ ekadvāraṃ. Tatrassa dovāriko paṇḍito byatto medhāvī aññātānaṃ nivāretā, ñātānaṃ pavesetā. So tassa nagarassa samantā anupariyāyapathaṃ anukkamamāno na passeyya pākārasandhiṃ vā pākāravivaraṃ vā antamaso biḷāranikkhamanamattampi. Tassa evamassa – ‘ye kho keci oḷārikā pāṇā imaṃ nagaraṃ pavisanti vā nikkhamanti vā, sabbe te imināva dvārena pavisanti vā nikkhamanti vā’ti. Evameva kho me, bhante, dhammanvayo vidito. Ye te, bhante, ahesuṃ atītamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā, sabbe te bhagavanto pañca nīvaraṇe pahāya cetaso upakkilese paññāya dubbalīkaraṇe catūsu satipaṭṭhānesu suppatiṭṭhitacittā, satta sambojjhaṅge yathābhūtaṃ bhāvetvā anuttaraṃ sammāsambodhiṃ abhisambujjhiṃsu. Yepi te, bhante, bhavissanti anāgatamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā, sabbe te bhagavanto pañca nīvaraṇe pahāya cetaso upakkilese paññāya dubbalīkaraṇe catūsu satipaṭṭhānesu suppatiṭṭhitacittā, satta sambojjhaṅge yathābhūtaṃ bhāvetvā anuttaraṃ sammāsambodhiṃ abhisambujjhissanti. Bhagavāpi, bhante, etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho pañca nīvaraṇe pahāya cetaso upakkilese paññāya dubbalīkaraṇe catūsu satipaṭṭhānesu suppatiṭṭhitacitto satta sambojjhaṅge yathābhūtaṃ bhāvetvā anuttaraṃ sammāsambodhiṃ abhisambuddho.

143.“Venerable sir, I do not have the knowledge of encompassing minds regarding Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones, in the past, future, and present. But I do understand the lineage of the Dhamma. Just as, venerable sir, a king's frontier city might have strong foundations, strong ramparts and gates, with a single gate. There, a gatekeeper would be wise, competent, and intelligent, keeping out those unknown and letting in those known. As he patrols around the city, following the path all around, he would not see any crack or gap in the wall, not even one big enough for a cat to slip through. He would think: ‘Whatever large creatures enter or leave this city, all do so through this very gate.’ In the same way, venerable sir, I understand the lineage of the Dhamma. All those Blessed Ones, Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones of the past, having abandoned the five hindrances—defilements of the mind, the weakening of wisdom—with their minds well-established in the four foundations of mindfulness, having developed the seven factors of enlightenment as they truly are, awakened to the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. All those Blessed Ones, Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones of the future, having abandoned the five hindrances—defilements of the mind, the weakening of wisdom—with their minds well-established in the four foundations of mindfulness, having developed the seven factors of enlightenment as they truly are, will awaken to the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. The Blessed One too, venerable sir, presently an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, having abandoned the five hindrances—defilements of the mind, the weakening of wisdom—with his mind well-established in the four foundations of mindfulness, having developed the seven factors of enlightenment as they truly are, has awakened to the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment.”

144. ‘‘Idhāhaṃ, bhante, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃ dhammassavanāya. Tassa me, bhante, bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti uttaruttaraṃ paṇītapaṇītaṃ kaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāgaṃ. Yathā yathā me, bhante, bhagavā dhammaṃ desesi uttaruttaraṃ paṇītapaṇītaṃ kaṇhasukkasappaṭibhāgaṃ, tathā tathāhaṃ tasmiṃ dhamme abhiññā idhekaccaṃ dhammaṃ dhammesu niṭṭhamagamaṃ; satthari pasīdiṃ – ‘sammāsambuddho bhagavā, svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo, suppaṭipanno sāvakasaṅgho’ti.

144.“Venerable sir, I approached the Blessed One here to listen to the Dhamma. To me, venerable sir, the Blessed One taught the Dhamma progressively, higher and higher, more sublime and more sublime, with its dark and bright counterparts. As the Blessed One taught me the Dhamma progressively, higher and higher, more sublime and more sublime, with its dark and bright counterparts, I, through direct knowledge, came to a final understanding of a certain teaching among the teachings; and I gained confidence in the Teacher: ‘The Blessed One is a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, the Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One, the Sangha of the Blessed One's disciples is practicing well.’”

## Kusaladhammadesanā

### The Teaching on Wholesome Qualities

145. ‘‘Aparaṃ pana, bhante, etadānuttariyaṃ, yathā bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti kusalesu dhammesu. Tatrime kusalā dhammā seyyathidaṃ, cattāro satipaṭṭhānā, cattāro sammappadhānā, cattāro iddhipādā, pañcindriyāni, pañca balāni, satta bojjhaṅgā, ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo. Idha, bhante, bhikkhu āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Etadānuttariyaṃ, bhante, kusalesu dhammesu. Taṃ bhagavā asesamabhijānāti, taṃ bhagavato asesamabhijānato uttari abhiññeyyaṃ natthi, yadabhijānaṃ añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññataro assa, yadidaṃ kusalesu dhammesu.

145."Furthermore, venerable sir, this is unsurpassed: how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma with regard to wholesome qualities. These are the wholesome qualities, namely: the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of spiritual power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of enlightenment, the Noble Eightfold Path. Here, venerable sir, a bhikkhu, through the destruction of the taints, having realized for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life, the Stainless Liberation of Mind and Liberation by Wisdom, enters upon and abides in it. This, venerable sir, is unsurpassed among wholesome qualities. The Blessed One knows this completely, and for the Blessed One who knows this completely, there is nothing further to be known beyond that. There is no other ascetic or brahmin who could know more than the Blessed One regarding this, namely, in wholesome qualities."

## Āyatanapaṇṇattidesanā

### The Teaching on the Designations of the Sense Bases

146. ‘‘Aparaṃ pana, bhante, etadānuttariyaṃ, yathā bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti āyatanapaṇṇattīsu. Chayimāni, bhante, ajjhattikabāhirāni āyatanāni. Cakkhuñceva rūpā [rūpāni (ka.)] ca, sotañceva saddā ca, ghānañceva gandhā ca, jivhā ceva rasā ca, kāyo ceva phoṭṭhabbā ca, mano ceva dhammā ca. Etadānuttariyaṃ, bhante, āyatanapaṇṇattīsu. Taṃ bhagavā asesamabhijānāti, taṃ bhagavato asesamabhijānato uttari abhiññeyyaṃ natthi, yadabhijānaṃ añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññataro assa yadidaṃ āyatanapaṇṇattīsu.

146."Furthermore, venerable sir, this is unsurpassed: how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma regarding the designations of the sense bases. There are these six internal and external sense bases, venerable sir: the eye and forms, the ear and sounds, the nose and odors, the tongue and tastes, the body and tangibles, and the mind and mental objects. This, venerable sir, is unsurpassed in the designations of the sense bases. The Blessed One knows this completely, and for the Blessed One who knows this completely, there is nothing further to be known beyond that. There is no other ascetic or brahmin who could know more than the Blessed One regarding this, namely, in the designations of the sense bases."

## Gabbhāvakkantidesanā

### The Teaching on Conception

147. ‘‘Aparaṃ pana, bhante, etadānuttariyaṃ, yathā bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti gabbhāvakkantīsu. Catasso imā, bhante, gabbhāvakkantiyo. Idha, bhante, ekacco asampajāno mātukucchiṃ okkamati; asampajāno mātukucchismiṃ ṭhāti; asampajāno mātukucchimhā nikkhamati. Ayaṃ paṭhamā gabbhāvakkanti.

147."Furthermore, venerable sir, this is another unsurpassed matter: how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma regarding conceptions. There are these four conceptions, venerable sir. Here, venerable sir, someone descends into the mother's womb without full awareness; abides in the mother's womb without full awareness; emerges from the mother's womb without full awareness. This is the first conception."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco sampajāno mātukucchiṃ okkamati; asampajāno mātukucchismiṃ ṭhāti; asampajāno mātukucchimhā nikkhamati. Ayaṃ dutiyā gabbhāvakkanti.

"Furthermore, venerable sir, here someone descends into the mother's womb with full awareness; abides in the mother's womb without full awareness; and emerges from the mother's womb without full awareness. This is the second conception."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco sampajāno mātukucchiṃ okkamati; sampajāno mātukucchismiṃ ṭhāti; asampajāno mātukucchimhā nikkhamati. Ayaṃ tatiyā gabbhāvakkanti.

"Furthermore, venerable sir, here someone descends into the mother's womb with full awareness; abides in the mother's womb with full awareness; but emerges from the mother's womb without full awareness. This is the third conception."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco sampajāno mātukucchiṃ okkamati; sampajāno mātukucchismiṃ ṭhāti; sampajāno mātukucchimhā nikkhamati. Ayaṃ catutthā gabbhāvakkanti. Etadānuttariyaṃ, bhante, gabbhāvakkantīsu.

"Furthermore, venerable sir, here someone descends into the mother's womb with full awareness; abides in the mother's womb with full awareness; and emerges from the mother's womb with full awareness. This is the fourth conception. This, venerable sir, is unsurpassed among conceptions."

## Ādesanavidhādesanā

### The Teaching on the Modes of Declaration

148. ‘‘Aparaṃ pana, bhante, etadānuttariyaṃ, yathā bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti ādesanavidhāsu. Catasso imā, bhante, ādesanavidhā. Idha, bhante, ekacco nimittena ādisati – ‘evampi te mano, itthampi te mano, itipi te citta’nti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati, tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā. Ayaṃ paṭhamā ādesanavidhā.

148."Furthermore, venerable sir, this is another unsurpassed matter: how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma regarding the modes of declaration. There are, venerable sir, these four modes of declaration. Here, venerable sir, someone declares by means of a sign: 'Your mind is thus, your mind is so, your thought is in this way.' Even if he declares much, it is just as he says, and not otherwise. This is the first mode of declaration."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco na heva kho nimittena ādisati. Api ca kho manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati – ‘evampi te mano, itthampi te mano, itipi te citta’nti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati, tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā. Ayaṃ dutiyā ādesanavidhā.

"Furthermore, venerable sir, here someone does not declare by means of a sign. But having heard a sound from humans, non-human beings, or deities, he declares: ‘Your mind is thus, your mind is so, your thought is in this way.’ Even if he declares much, it is just as he says, and not otherwise. This is the second mode of declaration."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco na heva kho nimittena ādisati, nāpi manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati. Api ca kho vitakkayato vicārayato vitakkavipphārasaddaṃ sutvā ādisati – ‘evampi te mano, itthampi te mano, itipi te citta’nti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati, tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā. Ayaṃ tatiyā ādesanavidhā.

"Furthermore, venerable sir, here someone does not declare by means of a sign, nor by hearing a sound from humans, non-human beings, or deities. But rather, having heard the sound of the spreading of thoughts as he thinks and ponders, he declares: ‘Your mind is thus, your mind is so, your thought is in this way.’ Even if he declares much, it is just as he says, and not otherwise. This is the third mode of declaration."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco na heva kho nimittena ādisati, nāpi manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati, nāpi vitakkayato vicārayato vitakkavipphārasaddaṃ sutvā ādisati. Api ca kho avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhiṃ samāpannassa [vitakkavicārasamādhisamāpannassa (syā. ka.) a. ni. 3.61 passitabbaṃ] cetasā ceto paricca pajānāti – ‘yathā imassa bhoto manosaṅkhārā paṇihitā. Tathā imassa cittassa anantarā imaṃ nāma vitakkaṃ vitakkessatī’ti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati, tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā. Ayaṃ catutthā ādesanavidhā. Etadānuttariyaṃ, bhante, ādesanavidhāsu.

"Furthermore, venerable sir, here someone does not declare by means of a sign, nor by hearing a sound from humans, non-human beings, or deities, nor by hearing the sound of the spreading of thoughts as he thinks and ponders. But rather, having attained a samādhi free from thought and examination, he knows the mind of another by his own mind, comprehending: ‘As this venerable one’s mental formations are disposed, so immediately following this, he will think such and such a thought.’ Even if he declares much, it is just as he says, and not otherwise. This is the fourth mode of declaration. This, venerable sir, is the unsurpassed mode of declaration."

## Dassanasamāpattidesanā

### The Exposition of the Attainment of Vision

149. ‘‘Aparaṃ pana, bhante, etadānuttariyaṃ, yathā bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti dassanasamāpattīsu. Catasso imā, bhante, dassanasamāpattiyo. Idha, bhante, ekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā ātappamanvāya padhānamanvāya anuyogamanvāya appamādamanvāya sammāmanasikāramanvāya tathārūpaṃ cetosamādhiṃ phusati, yathāsamāhite citte imameva kāyaṃ uddhaṃ pādatalā adho kesamatthakā tacapariyantaṃ pūraṃ nānappakārassa asucino paccavekkhati – ‘atthi imasmiṃ kāye kesā lomā nakhā dantā taco maṃsaṃ nhāru aṭṭhi aṭṭhimiñjaṃ vakkaṃ hadayaṃ yakanaṃ kilomakaṃ pihakaṃ papphāsaṃ antaṃ antaguṇaṃ udariyaṃ karīsaṃ pittaṃ semhaṃ pubbo lohitaṃ sedo medo assu vasā kheḷo siṅghānikā lasikā mutta’nti. Ayaṃ paṭhamā dassanasamāpatti.

149.Furthermore, venerable sir, this is unsurpassed, how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma regarding the attainments of vision. There are, venerable sir, these four attainments of vision. Here, venerable sir, a certain ascetic or brahmin, through ardor, striving, application, diligence, and right attention, attains such samādhi of mind that, with the mind thus collected, he reviews this very body, from the soles of the feet upwards and from the crown of the head downwards, enclosed by skin and full of various kinds of impurities: ‘In this body there are head hairs, body hairs, nails, teeth, skin, flesh, sinews, bones, bone marrow, kidneys, heart, liver, pleura, spleen, lungs, intestines, mesentery, undigested food, feces, bile, phlegm, pus, blood, sweat, fat, tears, grease, saliva, mucus, synovial fluid, urine.’ This is the first attainment of vision.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā ātappamanvāya…pe… tathārūpaṃ cetosamādhiṃ phusati, yathāsamāhite citte imameva kāyaṃ uddhaṃ pādatalā adho kesamatthakā tacapariyantaṃ pūraṃ nānappakārassa asucino paccavekkhati – ‘atthi imasmiṃ kāye kesā lomā…pe… lasikā mutta’nti. Atikkamma ca purisassa chavimaṃsalohitaṃ aṭṭhiṃ paccavekkhati. Ayaṃ dutiyā dassanasamāpatti.

Furthermore, venerable sir, here a certain ascetic or brahmin, through ardor… attains such samādhi of mind that, with the mind thus collected, he reviews this very body from the soles of the feet upwards and from the crown of the head downwards, enclosed by skin, and full of various kinds of impurities: ‘In this body there are head hairs, body hairs… mucus, urine.’ And transcending a person’s skin, flesh, and blood, he reviews the bones. This is the second attainment of vision.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā ātappamanvāya…pe… tathārūpaṃ cetosamādhiṃ phusati, yathāsamāhite citte imameva kāyaṃ uddhaṃ pādatalā adho kesamatthakā tacapariyantaṃ pūraṃ nānappakārassa asucino paccavekkhati – ‘atthi imasmiṃ kāye kesā lomā…pe… lasikā mutta’nti. Atikkamma ca purisassa chavimaṃsalohitaṃ aṭṭhiṃ paccavekkhati. Purisassa ca viññāṇasotaṃ pajānāti, ubhayato abbocchinnaṃ idha loke patiṭṭhitañca paraloke patiṭṭhitañca. Ayaṃ tatiyā dassanasamāpatti.

Furthermore, venerable sir, here a certain ascetic or brahmin, through ardor… attains such samādhi of mind that, with the mind thus collected, he reviews this very body from the soles of the feet upwards and from the crown of the head downwards, enclosed by skin, and full of various kinds of impurities: ‘In this body there are head hairs, body hairs… mucus, urine.’ And transcending a person’s skin, flesh, and blood, he reviews the bones. And he understands a person’s stream of consciousness, unbroken on both sides, established in this world and established in the next world. This is the third attainment of vision.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, idhekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā ātappamanvāya…pe… tathārūpaṃ cetosamādhiṃ phusati, yathāsamāhite citte imameva kāyaṃ uddhaṃ pādatalā adho kesamatthakā tacapariyantaṃ pūraṃ nānappakārassa asucino paccavekkhati – ‘atthi imasmiṃ kāye kesā lomā…pe… lasikā mutta’nti. Iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati. Etadānuttariyaṃ, bhante, pubbenivāsānussatiñāṇe.

157."Furthermore, Venerable Sir, this is another unsurpassed quality—how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma regarding the knowledge of the recollection of past lives. Here, Venerable Sir, some ascetic or brahmin, through ardent striving... attains such a samādhi of mind that, with the mind thus collected, they recollect their manifold past lives, that is: one birth, two births, three births, four births, five births, ten births, twenty births, thirty births, forty births, fifty births, a hundred births, a thousand births, a hundred thousand births, many cycles of world contraction, many cycles of world expansion, many cycles of world contraction and expansion. ‘There I was, of such a name, such a clan, such an appearance, such food, such an experience of pleasure and pain, such a lifespan. Passing away from there, I was reborn elsewhere. There too I was, of such a name, such a clan, such an appearance, such food, such an experience of pleasure and pain, such a lifespan. Passing away from there, I was reborn here.’ Thus, with details and specifics, they recollect their manifold past lives. There are, Venerable Sir, devas whose lifespan cannot be reckoned by counting or calculation. Yet, in whatever state of existence they have previously taken—whether in the realms of form, the formless realms, the realms of perception, the realms without perception, or the realms of neither-perception-nor-non-perception—they recollect their manifold past lives with details and specifics. This, Venerable Sir, is the unsurpassed quality regarding the knowledge of the recollection of past lives."

## Cutūpapātañāṇadesanā

### The Teaching on the Knowledge of the Passing Away and Re-arising of Beings.

158. ‘‘Aparaṃ pana, bhante, etadānuttariyaṃ, yathā bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti sattānaṃ cutūpapātañāṇe. Idha, bhante, ekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā ātappamanvāya…pe… tathārūpaṃ cetosamādhiṃ phusati, yathāsamāhite citte dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passati cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajānāti – ‘ime vata bhonto sattā kāyaduccaritena samannāgatā vacīduccaritena samannāgatā manoduccaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ upavādakā micchādiṭṭhikā micchādiṭṭhikammasamādānā. Te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannā. Ime vā pana bhonto sattā kāyasucaritena samannāgatā vacīsucaritena samannāgatā manosucaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ anupavādakā sammādiṭṭhikā sammādiṭṭhikammasamādānā. Te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapannā’ti. Iti dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passati cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajānāti. Etadānuttariyaṃ, bhante, sattānaṃ cutūpapātañāṇe.

158."Furthermore, Venerable Sir, this is another unsurpassed excellence—how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma regarding the knowledge of the passing away and re-arising of beings. Here, Venerable Sir, some ascetic or brahmin, through ardent striving... attains such a samādhi of mind that with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, he sees beings passing away and being reborn—inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, well-gone and ill-gone—and he understands beings according to their actions: 'Indeed, these beings were endowed with misconduct of body, speech, and mind, revilers of the noble ones, holding wrong views, undertaking actions based on wrong views. After death, they have been reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, a lower realm, even in hell. But these other beings were endowed with good conduct of body, speech, and mind, not revilers of the noble ones, holding right views, undertaking actions based on right views. After death, they have been reborn in a happy destination, even in the heavenly world.' Thus, with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, he sees beings passing away and being reborn—inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate, well-gone and ill-gone—and he understands beings according to their actions. This, Venerable Sir, is the unsurpassed excellence regarding the knowledge of the passing away and re-arising of beings."

## Iddhividhadesanā

### The Exposition on Psychic Power

159. ‘‘Aparaṃ pana, bhante, etadānuttariyaṃ, yathā bhagavā dhammaṃ deseti iddhividhāsu. Dvemā, bhante, iddhividhāyo – atthi, bhante, iddhi sāsavā saupadhikā, ‘no ariyā’ti vuccati. Atthi, bhante, iddhi anāsavā anupadhikā ‘ariyā’ti vuccati. ‘‘Katamā ca, bhante, iddhi sāsavā saupadhikā, ‘no ariyā’ti vuccati? Idha, bhante, ekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā ātappamanvāya…pe… tathārūpaṃ cetosamādhiṃ phusati, yathāsamāhite citte anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti. Ekopi hutvā bahudhā hoti, bahudhāpi hutvā eko hoti; āvibhāvaṃ tirobhāvaṃ tirokuṭṭaṃ tiropākāraṃ tiropabbataṃ asajjamāno gacchati seyyathāpi ākāse. Pathaviyāpi ummujjanimujjaṃ karoti, seyyathāpi udake. Udakepi abhijjamāne gacchati, seyyathāpi pathaviyaṃ. Ākāsepi pallaṅkena kamati, seyyathāpi pakkhī sakuṇo. Imepi candimasūriye evaṃmahiddhike evaṃmahānubhāve pāṇinā parāmasati parimajjati. Yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti. Ayaṃ, bhante, iddhi sāsavā saupadhikā, ‘no ariyā’ti vuccati.

159."Furthermore, venerable sir, this is the unsurpassed (teaching), how the Blessed One teaches the Dhamma concerning psychic power. There are, venerable sir, these two kinds of psychic power. One, venerable sir, is a psychic power with taints and acquisitions, which is called 'not noble.' The other, venerable sir, is a psychic power without taints and without acquisitions, which is called 'noble.'"And what, venerable sir, is the psychic power with taints and acquisitions, which is called 'not noble'? Here, venerable sir, some ascetic or brahmin, through striving and exertion... attains such a samādhi of mind that, with the mind thus collected, he experiences various kinds of psychic power. Being one, he becomes many; being many, he becomes one. He appears and vanishes; he goes unhindered through walls, ramparts, and mountains as if through space. He dives in and out of the earth as if it were water. He goes on water without sinking as if on earth. He travels cross-legged through the sky like a winged bird. With his hand, he touches and strokes the sun and moon, so mighty and powerful. He wields bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world. This, venerable sir, is the psychic power with taints and acquisitions, which is called 'not noble.'"

‘‘Katamā pana, bhante, iddhi anāsavā anupadhikā, ‘ariyā’ti vuccati? Idha, bhante, bhikkhu sace ākaṅkhati – ‘paṭikūle appaṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, appaṭikūlasaññī tattha viharati. Sace ākaṅkhati – ‘appaṭikūle paṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, paṭikūlasaññī tattha viharati. Sace ākaṅkhati – ‘paṭikūle ca appaṭikūle ca appaṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, appaṭikūlasaññī tattha viharati. Sace ākaṅkhati – ‘paṭikūle ca appaṭikūle ca paṭikūlasaññī vihareyya’nti, paṭikūlasaññī tattha viharati. Sace ākaṅkhati – ‘paṭikūlañca appaṭikūlañca tadubhayaṃ abhinivajjetvā upekkhako vihareyyaṃ sato sampajāno’ti, upekkhako tattha viharati sato sampajāno. Ayaṃ, bhante, iddhi anāsavā anupadhikā ‘ariyā’ti vuccati. Etadānuttariyaṃ, bhante, iddhividhāsu. Taṃ bhagavā asesamabhijānāti, taṃ bhagavato asesamabhijānato uttari abhiññeyyaṃ natthi, yadabhijānaṃ añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññataro assa yadidaṃ iddhividhāsu.

"And what, venerable sir, is the psychic power that is without taints and without acquisitions, which is called 'noble'? Here, venerable sir, if a bhikkhu wishes, 'May I dwell perceiving the unrepulsive in the repulsive,' he dwells perceiving the unrepulsive there. If he wishes, 'May I dwell perceiving the repulsive in the unrepulsive,' he dwells perceiving the repulsive there. If he wishes, 'May I dwell perceiving the unrepulsive in both the repulsive and the unrepulsive,' he dwells perceiving the unrepulsive there. If he wishes, 'May I dwell perceiving the repulsive in both the repulsive and the unrepulsive,' he dwells perceiving the repulsive there. If he wishes, 'Having avoided both the repulsive and the unrepulsive, may I dwell equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending,' he dwells equanimous there, mindful and clearly comprehending. This, venerable sir, is the psychic power that is without taints and without acquisitions, which is called 'noble.' This, venerable sir, is the unsurpassed among kinds of psychic power. The Blessed One fully knows this, and since the Blessed One fully knows this, there is nothing further to be known in this regard, such that another ascetic or brahmin would be more knowing than the Blessed One with respect to these kinds of psychic power."

## Aññathāsatthuguṇadassanaṃ

### Another Exposition of the Teacher's Qualities

160. ‘‘Yaṃ taṃ, bhante, saddhena kulaputtena pattabbaṃ āraddhavīriyena thāmavatā purisathāmena purisavīriyena purisaparakkamena purisadhorayhena, anuppattaṃ taṃ bhagavatā. Na ca, bhante, bhagavā kāmesu kāmasukhallikānuyogamanuyutto hīnaṃ gammaṃ pothujjanikaṃ anariyaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, na ca attakilamathānuyogamanuyutto dukkhaṃ anariyaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ. Catunnañca bhagavā jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī.

160."Whatever, venerable sir, can be attained by a faithful clansman endowed with energy, persistent effort, strength, manly vigor, manly exertion, manly striving, and manly perseverance—that has been attained by the Blessed One. And, venerable sir, the Blessed One is not addicted to sensual pleasures, which are low, vulgar, worldly, ignoble, and unbeneficial; nor is he addicted to self-mortification, which is painful, ignoble, and unbeneficial. And the Blessed One obtains at will, without trouble or difficulty, the four jhānas, which belong to the higher mind and are a pleasant abiding in the present."

## Anuyogadānappakāro

### The manner of granting permission for investigation.

161. ‘‘Sace maṃ, bhante, evaṃ puccheyya – ‘kiṃ nu kho, āvuso sāriputta, ahesuṃ atītamaddhānaṃ aññe samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññatarā sambodhiya’nti, evaṃ puṭṭho ahaṃ, bhante, ‘no’ti vadeyyaṃ. ‘Kiṃ panāvuso sāriputta, bhavissanti anāgatamaddhānaṃ aññe samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññatarā sambodhiya’nti, evaṃ puṭṭho ahaṃ, bhante, ‘no’ti vadeyyaṃ. ‘Kiṃ panāvuso sāriputta, atthetarahi añño samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā bhagavatā bhiyyobhiññataro sambodhiya’nti, evaṃ puṭṭho ahaṃ, bhante, ‘no’ti vadeyyaṃ.

161."If, venerable sir, someone were to ask me thus: 'Friend Sāriputta, were there in the past other ascetics or brahmins more enlightened than the Blessed One?'—being asked thus, I would say, 'No.' 'But, friend Sāriputta, will there be in the future other ascetics or brahmins more enlightened than the Blessed One?'—being asked thus, I would say, 'No.' 'But, friend Sāriputta, is there now another ascetic or brahmin more enlightened than the Blessed One?'—being asked thus, I would say, 'No.'"

‘‘Sace pana maṃ, bhante, evaṃ puccheyya – ‘kiṃ nu kho, āvuso sāriputta, ahesuṃ atītamaddhānaṃ aññe samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā bhagavatā samasamā sambodhiya’nti, evaṃ puṭṭho ahaṃ, bhante, ‘eva’nti vadeyyaṃ. ‘Kiṃ panāvuso sāriputta, bhavissanti anāgatamaddhānaṃ aññe samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā bhagavatā samasamā sambodhiya’nti, evaṃ puṭṭho ahaṃ, bhante, ‘‘eva’’nti vadeyyaṃ. ‘Kiṃ panāvuso sāriputta, atthetarahi aññe samaṇā vā brāhmaṇā vā bhagavatā samasamā sambodhiya’nti, evaṃ puṭṭho ahaṃ bhante ‘no’ti vadeyyaṃ.

"But if, venerable sir, someone were to ask me: ‘Were there in the past any other ascetics or brahmins equal to the Blessed One in enlightenment?’—being asked thus, I would say, ‘Yes.’ ‘And will there be in the future any other ascetics or brahmins equal to the Blessed One in enlightenment?’—being asked thus, I would say, ‘Yes.’ ‘But are there at present any other ascetics or brahmins equal to the Blessed One in enlightenment?’—being asked thus, I would say, ‘No.’"

‘‘Sace pana maṃ, bhante, evaṃ puccheyya – ‘kiṃ panāyasmā sāriputto ekaccaṃ abbhanujānāti, ekaccaṃ na abbhanujānātī’ti, evaṃ puṭṭho ahaṃ, bhante, evaṃ byākareyyaṃ – ‘sammukhā metaṃ, āvuso, bhagavato sutaṃ, sammukhā paṭiggahitaṃ – ‘‘ahesuṃ atītamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā mayā samasamā sambodhiya’’nti. Sammukhā metaṃ, āvuso, bhagavato sutaṃ, sammukhā paṭiggahitaṃ – ‘‘bhavissanti anāgatamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā mayā samasamā sambodhiya’’nti. Sammukhā metaṃ, āvuso, bhagavato sutaṃ sammukhā paṭiggahitaṃ – ‘‘aṭṭhānametaṃ anavakāso yaṃ ekissā lokadhātuyā dve arahanto sammāsambuddhā apubbaṃ acarimaṃ uppajjeyyuṃ, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjatī’’’ti.

"But if, venerable sir, someone were to ask me, ‘Why does the Venerable Sāriputta approve of some things and disapprove of others?’—being asked thus, venerable sir, I would answer: ‘Friend, I have heard this face to face from the Blessed One, I have received it face to face: “In the past, there were Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones, equal to me in enlightenment.” Friend, I have heard this face to face from the Blessed One, I have received it face to face: “In the future, there will be Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones, equal to me in enlightenment.” Friend, I have heard this face to face from the Blessed One, I have received it face to face: “It is impossible, there is no chance that in one ten-thousandfold world-system two Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones, should arise without precedence or sequel; such a thing cannot exist.”’"

‘‘Kaccāhaṃ, bhante, evaṃ puṭṭho evaṃ byākaramāno vuttavādī ceva bhagavato homi, na ca bhagavantaṃ abhūtena abbhācikkhāmi, dhammassa cānudhammaṃ byākaromi, na ca koci sahadhammiko vādānuvādo [vādānupāto (sī.)] gārayhaṃ ṭhānaṃ āgacchatī’’ti? ‘‘Taggha tvaṃ, sāriputta, evaṃ puṭṭho evaṃ byākaramāno vuttavādī ceva me hosi, na ca maṃ abhūtena abbhācikkhasi, dhammassa cānudhammaṃ byākarosi, na ca koci sahadhammiko vādānuvādo gārayhaṃ ṭhānaṃ āgacchatī’’ti.

"Venerable sir, when asked thus and answering thus, am I speaking in accordance with the Blessed One's words, not misrepresenting the Blessed One with falsehood, explaining the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, and no legitimate counter-argument would give rise to censure?""Indeed, Sāriputta, when asked thus and answering thus, you are speaking in accordance with my words, not misrepresenting me with falsehood, explaining the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, and no legitimate counter-argument would give rise to censure."

## Acchariyaabbhutaṃ

### Marvelous and Amazing.

162. Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā udāyī bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘acchariyaṃ, bhante, abbhutaṃ, bhante, tathāgatassa appicchatā santuṭṭhitā sallekhatā. Yatra hi nāma tathāgato evaṃmahiddhiko evaṃmahānubhāvo, atha ca pana nevattānaṃ pātukarissati! Ekamekañcepi ito, bhante, dhammaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā attani samanupasseyyuṃ, te tāvatakeneva paṭākaṃ parihareyyuṃ. Acchariyaṃ, bhante, abbhutaṃ, bhante, tathāgatassa appicchatā santuṭṭhitā sallekhatā. Yatra hi nāma tathāgato evaṃ mahiddhiko evaṃmahānubhāvo. Atha ca pana nevattānaṃ pātukarissatī’’ti!

162.When this was said, Venerable Udāyī said to the Blessed One: "It is marvelous, venerable sir, it is amazing, venerable sir, how the Tathāgata is of few wishes, content, and effacement. For although the Tathāgata is of such great power and might, yet he does not reveal himself! If, venerable sir, any other sectarian wanderers were to perceive even a single one of these qualities in themselves, they would carry a banner proclaiming it. It is marvelous, venerable sir, it is amazing, venerable sir, how the Tathāgata is of few wishes, content, and effacement. For although the Tathāgata is of such great power and might, yet he does not reveal himself!"

‘‘Passa kho tvaṃ, udāyi, ‘tathāgatassa appicchatā santuṭṭhitā sallekhatā. Yatra hi nāma tathāgato evaṃmahiddhiko evaṃmahānubhāvo, atha ca pana nevattānaṃ pātukarissati’! Ekamekañcepi ito, udāyi, dhammaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā attani samanupasseyyuṃ, te tāvatakeneva paṭākaṃ parihareyyuṃ. Passa kho tvaṃ, udāyi, ‘tathāgatassa appicchatā santuṭṭhitā sallekhatā. Yatra hi nāma tathāgato evaṃmahiddhiko evaṃmahānubhāvo, atha ca pana nevattānaṃ pātukarissatī’’’ti!

"See, Udāyī, the Tathāgata's fewness of wishes, contentedness, and effacement. For although the Tathāgata is of such great power and might, yet he does not reveal himself! If, Udāyī, any other sectarian wanderers were to perceive even a single one of these qualities in themselves, they would carry a banner proclaiming it. See, Udāyī, the Tathāgata's fewness of wishes, contentedness, and effacement. For although the Tathāgata is of such great power and might, yet he does not reveal himself!"

163. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘tasmā tiha tvaṃ, sāriputta, imaṃ dhammapariyāyaṃ abhikkhaṇaṃ bhāseyyāsi bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ upāsakānaṃ upāsikānaṃ. Yesampi hi, sāriputta, moghapurisānaṃ bhavissati tathāgate kaṅkhā vā vimati vā, tesamimaṃ dhammapariyāyaṃ sutvā tathāgate kaṅkhā vā vimati vā, sā pahīyissatī’’ti. Iti hidaṃ āyasmā sāriputto bhagavato sammukhā sampasādaṃ pavedesi. Tasmā imassa veyyākaraṇassa sampasādanīyaṃ tveva adhivacananti.

163.Then the Blessed One addressed Venerable Sāriputta: "Therefore, Sāriputta, you should frequently recite this discourse on the Dhamma to the bhikkhus, nuns, male lay followers, and female lay followers. For even if any foolish persons have doubt or uncertainty about the Tathāgata, when they hear this discourse on the Dhamma, their doubt or uncertainty about the Tathāgata will be abandoned." Thus Venerable Sāriputta declared his satisfaction in the presence of the Blessed One. Therefore, this exposition should be called "The Inspiring Confidence."

Sampasādanīyasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ pañcamaṃ.
