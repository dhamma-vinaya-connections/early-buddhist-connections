# Mahāpadānasuttaṃ

## The Great Lineage Discourse

## Pubbenivāsapaṭisaṃyuttakathā

### The Discourse Connected with Previous Lives

1. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme karerikuṭikāyaṃ. Atha kho sambahulānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkantānaṃ karerimaṇḍalamāḷe sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ pubbenivāsapaṭisaṃyuttā dhammī kathā udapādi – ‘‘itipi pubbenivāso, itipi pubbenivāso’’ti.

1. Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park, in the Kareri Hut. Then, after their meal, on returning from alms round, a number of monks gathered and sat together in the Kareri Round Pavilion, and a Dhamma discussion connected with past lives arose among them: "So it was in a past life, so it was in a past life."

2. Assosi kho bhagavā dibbāya sotadhātuyā visuddhāya atikkantamānusikāya tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ imaṃ kathāsallāpaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena karerimaṇḍalamāḷo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi, nisajja kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘kāyanuttha, bhikkhave, etarahi kathāya sannisinnā; kā ca pana vo antarākathā vippakatā’’ti?

2. The Blessed One, with his divine ear element, purified and surpassing human, heard those monks engaged in this conversation. Then the Blessed One rose from his seat and went to the Kareri Round Pavilion; having arrived, he sat down on the prepared seat. Sitting there, the Blessed One addressed the monks: "What is the discussion you are engaged in now, monks? And what was the discussion that was interrupted?”

Evaṃ vutte te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idha, bhante, amhākaṃ pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkantānaṃ karerimaṇḍalamāḷe sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ pubbenivāsapaṭisaṃyuttā dhammī kathā udapādi – ‘itipi pubbenivāso itipi pubbenivāso’ti. Ayaṃ kho no, bhante, antarākathā vippakatā. Atha bhagavā anuppatto’’ti.

When this was said, those monks said to the Blessed One: “Here, venerable sir, after our meal, on returning from alms round, we gathered and sat together in the Kareri Round Pavilion, and a Dhamma discussion connected with past lives arose among us: ‘So it was in a past life, so it was in a past life.’ This, venerable sir, was our interrupted discussion when the Blessed One arrived.”

3. ‘‘Iccheyyātha no tumhe, bhikkhave, pubbenivāsapaṭisaṃyuttaṃ dhammiṃ kathaṃ sotu’’nti? ‘‘Etassa, bhagavā, kālo; etassa, sugata, kālo; yaṃ bhagavā pubbenivāsapaṭisaṃyuttaṃ dhammiṃ kathaṃ kareyya, bhagavato sutvā [bhagavato vacanaṃ sutvā (syā.)] bhikkhū dhāressantī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, bhikkhave, suṇātha,sādhukaṃ manasi karotha, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

3. “Would you like, monks, to hear a Dhamma talk connected with past lives?” “Now is the time, Blessed One. Now is the time, Fortunate One. Let the Blessed One give a Dhamma talk connected with past lives. Having heard it from the Blessed One, the monks will remember it.” “Then, monks, listen and pay close attention. I will speak.” “Yes, venerable sir,” those monks replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

4. ‘‘Ito so, bhikkhave, ekanavutikappe yaṃ [ekanavuto kappo (syā. kaṃ. pī.)] vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Ito so, bhikkhave, ekatiṃse kappe [ekatiṃ sakappo (sī.) ekatiṃ so kappo (syā. kaṃ. pī.)] yaṃ sikhī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Tasmiññeva kho, bhikkhave, ekatiṃse kappe vessabhū bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Imasmiññeva [imasmiṃ (katthacī)] kho, bhikkhave, bhaddakappe kakusandho bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Imasmiññeva kho, bhikkhave, bhaddakappe koṇāgamano bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Imasmiññeva kho, bhikkhave, bhaddakappe kassapo bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Imasmiññeva kho, bhikkhave, bhaddakappe ahaṃ etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke uppanno.

4. “Monks, ninety-one eons ago, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. Monks, thirty-one eons ago, the Blessed One Sikhī, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. In that same thirty-first eon, the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. Monks, in this fortunate eon, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. Monks, in this fortunate eon, the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. Monks, in this fortunate eon, the Blessed One Kassapa, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. Monks, in this fortunate eon, I, now, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, have arisen in the world.

5. ‘‘Vipassī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho khattiyo jātiyā ahosi, khattiyakule udapādi. Sikhī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho khattiyo jātiyā ahosi, khattiyakule udapādi. Vessabhū, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho khattiyo jātiyā ahosi, khattiyakule udapādi. Kakusandho, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho brāhmaṇo jātiyā ahosi, brāhmaṇakule udapādi. Koṇāgamano, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho brāhmaṇo jātiyā ahosi, brāhmaṇakule udapādi. Kassapo, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho brāhmaṇo jātiyā ahosi, brāhmaṇakule udapādi. Ahaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho khattiyo jātiyā ahosiṃ, khattiyakule uppanno.

5. Monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the warrior caste by birth, born into a warrior family. Monks, the Blessed One Sikhī, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the warrior caste by birth, born into a warrior family. Monks, the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the warrior caste by birth, born into a warrior family. Monks, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the brahmin caste by birth, born into a brahmin family. Monks, the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the brahmin caste by birth, born into a brahmin family. Monks, the Blessed One Kassapa, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the brahmin caste by birth, born into a brahmin family. Monks, I, now, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the warrior caste by birth, born into a warrior family.

6. ‘‘Vipassī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho koṇḍañño gottena ahosi. Sikhī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho koṇḍañño gottena ahosi. Vessabhū, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho koṇḍañño gottena ahosi. Kakusandho, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho kassapo gottena ahosi. Koṇāgamano, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho kassapo gottena ahosi. Kassapo, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho kassapo gottena ahosi. Ahaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho gotamo gottena ahosiṃ.

6. Monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Koṇḍañña lineage. Monks, the Blessed One Sikhī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Koṇḍañña lineage. Monks, the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Koṇḍañña lineage. Monks, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Kassapa lineage. Monks, the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Kassapa lineage. Monks, the Blessed One Kassapa, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Kassapa lineage. I, monks, now, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Gotama lineage.

7. ‘‘Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asītivassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Sikhissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa sattativassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Vessabhussa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa saṭṭhivassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Kakusandhassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa cattālīsavassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Koṇāgamanassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tiṃsavassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Kassapassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa vīsativassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi appakaṃ āyuppamāṇaṃ parittaṃ lahukaṃ; yo ciraṃ jīvati, so vassasataṃ appaṃ vā bhiyyo.

7. Monks, the lifespan of the Blessed One Vipassī, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was eighty thousand years. Monks, the lifespan of the Blessed One Sikhī, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was seventy thousand years. Monks, the lifespan of the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was sixty thousand years. Monks, the lifespan of the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was forty thousand years. Monks, the lifespan of the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was thirty thousand years. Monks, the lifespan of the Blessed One Kassapa, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was twenty thousand years. Monks, my lifespan now is short, limited, and fleeting; whoever lives long lives a hundred years or a little more.

8. ‘‘Vipassī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho pāṭaliyā mūle abhisambuddho. Sikhī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho puṇḍarīkassa mūle abhisambuddho. Vessabhū, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho sālassa mūle abhisambuddho. Kakusandho, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho sirīsassa mūle abhisambuddho. Koṇāgamano, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho udumbarassa mūle abhisambuddho. Kassapo, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho nigrodhassa mūle abhisambuddho. Ahaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho assatthassa mūle abhisambuddho.

8. Monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, awakened at the foot of a pāṭali tree. Monks, the Blessed One Sikhī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, awakened at the foot of a puṇḍarīka tree. Monks, the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, awakened at the foot of a sāla tree. Monks, the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, awakened at the foot of a sirīsa tree. Monks, the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, awakened at the foot of an udumbara tree. Monks, the Blessed One Kassapa, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, awakened at the foot of a nigrodha tree. I, monks, now the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, have awakened at the foot of an assattha tree.

9. ‘‘Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa khaṇḍatissaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Sikhissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa abhibhūsambhavaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Vessabhussa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa soṇuttaraṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Kakusandhassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa vidhurasañjīvaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Koṇāgamanassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa bhiyyosuttaraṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Kassapassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tissabhāradvājaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi sāriputtamoggallānaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ.

9. Monks, for the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was a pair of disciples named Khaṇḍa and Tissa, the foremost and excellent pair. Monks, for the Blessed One Sikhī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was a pair of disciples named Abhibhū and Sambhava, the foremost and excellent pair. Monks, for the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was a pair of disciples named Soṇa and Uttara, the foremost and excellent pair. Monks, for the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was a pair of disciples named Vidhura and Sañjīva, the foremost and excellent pair. Monks, for the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was a pair of disciples named Bhiyyosa and Uttara, the foremost and excellent pair. Monks, for the Blessed One Kassapa, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was a pair of disciples named Tissa and Bhāradvāja, the foremost and excellent pair. Monks, for me, now the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was a pair of disciples named Sāriputta and Moggallāna, the foremost and excellent pair.

10. ‘‘Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi bhikkhusatasahassaṃ, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi asītibhikkhusahassāni. Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ime tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ.

10. Monks, for the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there were three gatherings of disciples. One gathering of disciples consisted of six million eight hundred thousand monks, one gathering of disciples consisted of one hundred thousand monks, and one gathering of disciples consisted of eighty thousand monks. Monks, for the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, these were the three gatherings of disciples, all of whom were without effluents.

‘‘Sikhissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi bhikkhusatasahassaṃ, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi asītibhikkhusahassāni, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sattatibhikkhusahassāni. Sikhissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ime tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ.

Monks, for the Blessed One Sikhī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there were three gatherings of disciples. One gathering of disciples consisted of one hundred thousand monks, one gathering of disciples consisted of eighty thousand monks, and one gathering of disciples consisted of seventy thousand monks. Monks, for the Blessed One Sikhī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, these were the three gatherings of disciples, all of whom were without effluents.

‘‘Vessabhussa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi asītibhikkhusahassāni, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sattatibhikkhusahassāni, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi saṭṭhibhikkhusahassāni. Vessabhussa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ime tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ.

Monks, for the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there were three gatherings of disciples. One gathering of disciples consisted of eighty thousand monks, one gathering of disciples consisted of seventy thousand monks, and one gathering of disciples consisted of sixty thousand monks. Monks, for the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, these were the three gatherings of disciples, all of whom were without effluents.

‘‘Kakusandhassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi cattālīsabhikkhusahassāni. Kakusandhassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ayaṃ eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ.

Monks, for the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was one assembly of disciples consisting of forty thousand monks. Monks, for the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, this was the one assembly of disciples, all of whom were without effluents.

‘‘Koṇāgamanassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi tiṃsabhikkhusahassāni. Koṇāgamanassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ayaṃ eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ.

Monks, for the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was one gathering of disciples consisting of thirty thousand monks. Monks, for the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, this was the one gathering of disciples, all of whom were without effluents.

‘‘Kassapassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi vīsatibhikkhusahassāni. Kassapassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ayaṃ eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ.

Monks, for the Blessed One Kassapa, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, there was one gathering of disciples consisting of twenty thousand monks. Monks, for the Blessed One Kassapa, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, this was the one gathering of disciples, all of whom were without effluents.

‘‘Mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi aḍḍhateḷasāni bhikkhusatāni. Mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, ayaṃ eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ.

Monks, for me at this time there was one gathering of disciples consisting of one thousand two hundred and fifty monks. Monks, for me this was the one gathering of disciples, all of whom were without effluents.

11. ‘‘Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asoko nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Sikhissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa khemaṅkaro nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Vessabhussa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa upasanto nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Kakusandhassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa buddhijo nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Koṇāgamanassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa sotthijo nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Kassapassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa sabbamitto nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi ānando nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko.

11. Monks, for the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the monk Asoka was the chief attendant. Monks, for the Blessed One Sikhī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the monk Khemaṅkara was the chief attendant. Monks, for the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the monk Upasanta was the chief attendant. Monks, for the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the monk Buddhija was the chief attendant. Monks, for the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the monk Sotthija was the chief attendant. Monks, for the Blessed One Kassapa, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the monk Sabbamitta was the chief attendant. And now, monks, for me the monk Ānanda is the chief attendant.

12. ‘‘Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa bandhumā nāma rājā pitā ahosi. Bandhumatī nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti [janettī (syā.)]. Bandhumassa rañño bandhumatī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi.

12. Monks, for the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, King Bandhumā was his father. Queen Bandhumatī was his mother, the one who gave birth to him. The royal capital of King Bandhumā was the city named Bandhumatī.

‘‘Sikhissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa aruṇo nāma rājā pitā ahosi. Pabhāvatī nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti. Aruṇassa rañño aruṇavatī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi.

Monks, for the Blessed One Sikhī, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, King Aruṇa was his father. Queen Pabhāvatī was his mother, the one who gave birth to him. The royal capital of King Aruṇa was the city named Aruṇavatī.

‘‘Vessabhussa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa suppatito nāma [suppatīto nāma (syā.)] rājā pitā ahosi. Vassavatī nāma [yasavatī nāma (syā. pī.)] devī mātā ahosi janetti. Suppatitassa rañño anomaṃ nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi.

Monks, for the Blessed One Vessabhū, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, King Suppatita was his father. Queen Vassavatī was his mother, the one who gave birth to him. The royal capital of King Suppatita was the city named Anoma.

‘‘Kakusandhassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa aggidatto nāma brāhmaṇo pitā ahosi. Visākhā nāma brāhmaṇī mātā ahosi janetti. Tena kho pana, bhikkhave, samayena khemo nāma rājā ahosi. Khemassa rañño khemavatī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi.

Monks, for the Blessed One Kakusandha, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the brahmin Aggidatta was his father, and the brahmin woman Visākhā was his mother, his birth-giver. And at that time, monks, there was a king named Khema, whose royal capital was the city named Khemavatī.

‘‘Koṇāgamanassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa yaññadatto nāma brāhmaṇo pitā ahosi. Uttarā nāma brāhmaṇī mātā ahosi janetti. Tena kho pana, bhikkhave, samayena sobho nāma rājā ahosi. Sobhassa rañño sobhavatī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi.

Monks, for the Blessed One Koṇāgamana, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, a brahmin named Yaññadatta was his father, and a brahmin woman named Uttarā was his mother, his birth-giver. And at that time, monks, there was a king named Sobha, whose royal capital was the city named Sobhavatī.

‘‘Kassapassa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa brahmadatto nāma brāhmaṇo pitā ahosi. Dhanavatī nāma brāhmaṇī mātā ahosi janetti. Tena kho pana, bhikkhave, samayena kikī nāma [kiṃ kī nāma (syā.)] rājā ahosi. Kikissa rañño bārāṇasī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi.

Monks, for the Blessed One Kassapa, the Arahant, the Fully Enlightened One, the brahmin Brahmadatta was his father, and the brahmin woman Dhanavatī was his mother, his birth-giver. And at that time, monks, there was a king named Kikī, whose royal capital was the city named Bārāṇasī.

‘‘Mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, etarahi suddhodano nāma rājā pitā ahosi. Māyā nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti. Kapilavatthu nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosī’’ti. Idamavoca bhagavā, idaṃ vatvāna sugato uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ pāvisi.

Monks, at present my father was King Suddhodana. My mother was Queen Maya, the birth-giver. The royal capital was the city named Kapilavatthu." The Blessed One said this, and having said this, the Fortunate One rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.

Yatra hi nāma tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarissati, nāmatopi anussarissati, gottatopi anussarissati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarissati, sāvakayugatopi anussarissati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarissati – ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’’’ti.

13. Then, shortly after the Blessed One had left, this discussion arose among the monks: "It is wonderful, friends, it is marvelous, friends, the great power and great might of the Tathagata! How wonderful that the Tathagata recollects past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbāna, cut off proliferation, cut off the cycle, exhausted the round, transcended all suffering—he recollects their birth, their names, their clans, their lifespan, their pairs of disciples, their assemblies of disciples, thinking: 'Those Blessed Ones were of such birth, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such samādhi, such wisdom, such abiding, such liberation.'"

‘‘Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, tathāgatasseva nu kho esā dhammadhātu suppaṭividdhā, yassā dhammadhātuyā suppaṭividdhattā tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati – ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’ti, udāhu devatā tathāgatassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ, yena tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati – ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’’’ti. Ayañca hidaṃ tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ antarākathā vippakatā hoti.

"Is it, friends, that the Tathagata has clearly penetrated this Dhamma-element, through which clear penetration the Tathagata recollects past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbana, cut off proliferation, cut off the cycle, exhausted the round, transcended all suffering: their birth, names, clans, lifespans, pairs of disciples, assemblies of disciples, thinking: 'Those Blessed Ones were of such birth, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such samādhi, such wisdom, such abiding, such liberation'? Or have deities informed the Tathagata of this matter, enabling the Tathagata to recollect past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbana, cut off proliferation, cut off the cycle, exhausted the round, transcended all suffering: their birth, names, clans, lifespans, pairs of disciples, assemblies of disciples, thinking: 'Those Blessed Ones were of such birth, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such samādhi, such wisdom, such abiding, such liberation'?" This discussion among the monks was interrupted.

14. Atha kho bhagavā sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito yena karerimaṇḍalamāḷo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘kāyanuttha, bhikkhave, etarahi kathāya sannisinnā; kā ca pana vo antarākathā vippakatā’’ti?

14. Then, in the evening, the Blessed One rose from seclusion and went to the Kareri pavilion. Having approached, he sat down on the prepared seat. Seated, the Blessed One addressed the monks: "What discussion were you engaged in just now, monks? And what was the discussion that was interrupted?"

Evaṃ vutte te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idha, bhante, amhākaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato ayaṃ antarākathā udapādi – ‘acchariyaṃ, āvuso, abbhutaṃ, āvuso, tathāgatassa mahiddhikatā mahānubhāvatā, yatra hi nāma tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarissati, nāmatopi anussarissati, gottatopi anussarissati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarissati, sāvakayugatopi anussarissati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarissati – ‘‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’’ti. Kiṃ nu kho, āvuso, tathāgatasseva nu kho esā dhammadhātu suppaṭividdhā, yassā dhammadhātuyā suppaṭividdhattā tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati – ‘‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’’ti. Udāhu devatā tathāgatassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ, yena tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati – ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’ti? Ayaṃ kho no, bhante, antarākathā vippakatā, atha bhagavā anuppatto’’ti.

When this was said, the monks said this to the Blessed One: "Just now, venerable sir, after the Blessed One had left, this discussion arose among us: 'It is wonderful, friends, it is marvelous, the great power and great might of the Tathāgata, that the Tathāgata can recollect past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbāna, who have cut off proliferation, cut off the round, ended the cycle, gone beyond all suffering—recollecting their birth, their names, their clans, their lifespans, their pairs of disciples, their assemblies of disciples, thinking: "Those Blessed Ones were born thus, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such qualities, such wisdom, such abodes, such liberation."' Now, friends, is it because the Tathāgata has fully penetrated the Dhamma-element, that by fully penetrating this Dhamma-element, the Tathāgata recollects past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbāna, who have cut off proliferation, cut off the round, ended the cycle, gone beyond all suffering—recollecting their birth, their names, their clans, their lifespans, their pairs of disciples, their assemblies of disciples, thinking: "Those Blessed Ones were born thus, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such qualities, such wisdom, such abodes, such liberation"? Or is it that deities have informed the Tathāgata of this matter, enabling the Tathāgata to recollect past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbāna, who have cut off proliferation, cut off the round, ended the cycle, gone beyond all suffering—recollecting their birth, their names, their clans, their lifespans, their pairs of disciples, their assemblies of disciples, thinking: "Those Blessed Ones were born thus, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such qualities, such wisdom, such abodes, such liberation"?' This, venerable sir, was our unfinished discussion when the Blessed One arrived."

15. ‘‘Tathāgatassevesā, bhikkhave, dhammadhātu suppaṭividdhā, yassā dhammadhātuyā suppaṭividdhattā tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati – ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’ti. Devatāpi tathāgatassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ, yena tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati – ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipi, evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ itipī’ti.

15. "Monks, the Tathāgata has fully penetrated this Dhamma-element. It is by fully penetrating this Dhamma-element that the Tathāgata recollects past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbāna, who have cut off proliferation, cut off the round, ended the cycle, gone beyond all suffering—recollecting their birth, their names, their clans, their lifespans, their pairs of disciples, their assemblies of disciples, thinking: “Those Blessed Ones were born thus, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such qualities, such wisdom, such abodes, such liberation.” Deities too have informed the Tathāgata of this matter, enabling the Tathāgata to recollect past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbāna, who have cut off proliferation, cut off the round, ended the cycle, gone beyond all suffering—recollecting their birth, their names, their clans, their lifespans, their pairs of disciples, their assemblies of disciples, thinking: “Those Blessed Ones were born thus, had such names, such clans, such virtue, such qualities, such wisdom, such abodes, such liberation.”"

‘‘Iccheyyātha no tumhe, bhikkhave, bhiyyosomattāya pubbenivāsapaṭisaṃyuttaṃ dhammiṃ kathaṃ sotu’’nti? ‘‘Etassa, bhagavā, kālo; etassa, sugata, kālo; yaṃ bhagavā bhiyyosomattāya pubbenivāsapaṭisaṃyuttaṃ dhammiṃ kathaṃ kareyya, bhagavato sutvā bhikkhū dhāressantī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, bhikkhave, suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

"Monks, would you like to hear a Dhamma talk connected with past lives in more detail?" "Now is the time, Blessed One; now is the time, Sugata, for the Blessed One to give a Dhamma talk connected with past lives in more detail. Having heard it from the Blessed One, the monks will remember it." "Then listen, monks, and pay close attention. I will speak." "Yes, venerable sir," those monks replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

16. ‘‘Ito so, bhikkhave, ekanavutikappe yaṃ vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Vipassī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho khattiyo jātiyā ahosi, khattiyakule udapādi. Vipassī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho koṇḍañño gottena ahosi. Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asītivassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Vipassī, bhikkhave, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho pāṭaliyā mūle abhisambuddho. Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa khaṇḍatissaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi bhikkhusatasahassaṃ, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi asītibhikkhusahassāni. Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ime tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ. Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asoko nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Vipassissa, bhikkhave, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa bandhumā nāma rājā pitā ahosi. Bandhumatī nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti. Bandhumassa rañño bandhumatī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi.

16. Monks, ninety-one eons ago, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, arose in the world. Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, was a noble by birth, born into a noble family. Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, was of the Koṇḍañña clan. The lifespan of Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, was eighty thousand years. Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, attained enlightenment at the foot of a pāṭali tree. Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, had a foremost pair of disciples named Khaṇḍa and Tissa. Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, had three assemblies of disciples. One assembly consisted of six million eight hundred thousand monks, another of one hundred thousand monks, and another of eighty thousand monks. These were the three assemblies of disciples of Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, all of whom were free from defilements. Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, had a chief attendant named Asoka. Vipassī, monks, the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Fully Enlightened One, had a father named King Bandhuma and a mother named Queen Bandhumatī. King Bandhuma’s royal capital was named Bandhumatī.

## Bodhisattadhammatā

### The Natural Laws of the Bodhisatta

17. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bodhisatto tusitā kāyā cavitvā sato sampajāno mātukucchiṃ okkami. Ayamettha dhammatā.

17. Then, monks, the Bodhisatta Vipassī, having passed away from the Tusita realm, mindful and fully aware, descended into his mother’s womb. This is the norm in this case.

18. ‘‘Dhammatā, esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto tusitā kāyā cavitvā mātukucchiṃ okkamati. Atha sadevake loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya appamāṇo uḷāro obhāso pātubhavati atikkammeva devānaṃ devānubhāvaṃ. Yāpi tā lokantarikā aghā asaṃvutā andhakārā andhakāratimisā, yattha pime candimasūriyā evaṃmahiddhikā evaṃmahānubhāvā ābhāya nānubhonti, tatthapi appamāṇo uḷāro obhāso pātubhavati atikkammeva devānaṃ devānubhāvaṃ. Yepi tattha sattā upapannā, tepi tenobhāsena aññamaññaṃ sañjānanti – ‘aññepi kira, bho, santi sattā idhūpapannā’ti. Ayañca dasasahassī lokadhātu saṅkampati sampakampati sampavedhati. Appamāṇo ca uḷāro obhāso loke pātubhavati atikkammeva devānaṃ devānubhāvaṃ. Ayamettha dhammatā.

18. This, monks, is the nature of things: when a Bodhisatta passes away from the Tusita realm and descends into his mother’s womb, an immeasurable, splendid light appears in the world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, in the population with its ascetics and brahmins, its gods and humans—surpassing even the splendor of the gods. And those abysmal world-interspaces, unconfined, darkened, and utterly dark, where even the moon and sun, so mighty and powerful, cannot spread their light—there too an immeasurable, splendid light appears, surpassing even the splendor of the gods. And the beings reborn there recognize one another by that light: ‘So, it seems, other beings have been reborn here!’ And this ten-thousandfold world system shakes, rocks, and trembles. An immeasurable, splendid light appears in the world, surpassing even the splendor of the gods. This is the nature of things.

19. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchiṃ okkanto hoti, cattāro naṃ devaputtā catuddisaṃ [cātuddisaṃ (syā.)] rakkhāya upagacchanti – ‘mā naṃ bodhisattaṃ vā bodhisattamātaraṃ vā manusso vā amanusso vā koci vā viheṭhesī’ti. Ayamettha dhammatā.

19. "This, monks, is the nature of things: when a Bodhisatta has entered his mother’s womb, four young gods come from the four directions to guard him, thinking, ‘Let no one—human, non-human, or anyone else—harm this Bodhisatta or his mother.’ This is the nature of things."

20. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchiṃ okkanto hoti, pakatiyā sīlavatī bodhisattamātā hoti, viratā pāṇātipātā, viratā adinnādānā, viratā kāmesumicchācārā, viratā musāvādā, viratā surāmerayamajjappamādaṭṭhānā. Ayamettha dhammatā.

20. "This, monks, is the nature of things: when a Bodhisatta has entered his mother’s womb, the Bodhisatta’s mother is naturally virtuous—abstaining from killing living beings, abstaining from taking what is not given, abstaining from sexual misconduct, abstaining from false speech, and abstaining from liquor, wine, and intoxicants, the foundation for heedlessness. This is the nature of things."

21. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchiṃ okkanto hoti, na bodhisattamātu purisesu mānasaṃ uppajjati kāmaguṇūpasaṃhitaṃ, anatikkamanīyā ca bodhisattamātā hoti kenaci purisena rattacittena. Ayamettha dhammatā.

21. "This, monks, is the nature of things: when a Bodhisatta has entered his mother’s womb, no thought associated with sensual qualities arises in the Bodhisatta’s mother toward any man, and the Bodhisatta’s mother cannot be overcome by any man with lustful thoughts. This is the nature of things here."

22. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchiṃ okkanto hoti, lābhinī bodhisattamātā hoti pañcannaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ. Sā pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappitā samaṅgībhūtā paricāreti. Ayamettha dhammatā.

22. "This, monks, is the nature of things: when a Bodhisatta has entered his mother’s womb, the Bodhisatta’s mother is a recipient of the five strands of sensual pleasure. Being furnished and endowed with these five strands of sensual pleasure, she enjoys them. This is the nature of things."

23. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchiṃ okkanto hoti, na bodhisattamātu kocideva ābādho uppajjati. Sukhinī bodhisattamātā hoti akilantakāyā, bodhisattañca bodhisattamātā tirokucchigataṃ passati sabbaṅgapaccaṅgiṃ ahīnindriyaṃ. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, maṇi veḷuriyo subho jātimā aṭṭhaṃso suparikammakato accho vippasanno anāvilo sabbākārasampanno. Tatrāssa [tatrassa (syā.)] suttaṃ āvutaṃ nīlaṃ vā pītaṃ vā lohitaṃ vā odātaṃ vā paṇḍusuttaṃ vā. Tamenaṃ cakkhumā puriso hatthe karitvā paccavekkheyya – ‘ayaṃ kho maṇi veḷuriyo subho jātimā aṭṭhaṃso suparikammakato accho vippasanno anāvilo sabbākārasampanno. Tatridaṃ suttaṃ āvutaṃ nīlaṃ vā pītaṃ vā lohitaṃ vā odātaṃ vā paṇḍusuttaṃ vā’ti. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchiṃ okkanto hoti, na bodhisattamātu kocideva ābādho uppajjati, sukhinī bodhisattamātā hoti akilantakāyā, bodhisattañca bodhisattamātā tirokucchigataṃ passati sabbaṅgapaccaṅgiṃ ahīnindriyaṃ. Ayamettha dhammatā.

23. "This, monks, is the nature of things: when a Bodhisatta has entered his mother’s womb, no affliction whatsoever arises in the Bodhisatta’s mother. The Bodhisatta’s mother is happy, her body unwearied, and the Bodhisatta’s mother sees the Bodhisatta within her womb complete in all his limbs, with unimpaired faculties. Just as, monks, a beryl gem—beautiful, of fine quality, eight-faceted, well-cut, clear, transparent, and unclouded, perfect in every way—is strung on a blue, yellow, red, white, or pale thread, and a man with good eyesight, taking it in his hand, would examine it: ‘This beryl gem is beautiful, of fine quality, eight-faceted, well-cut, clear, transparent, and unclouded, perfect in every way, strung on a blue, yellow, red, white, or pale thread’—so too, monks, when a Bodhisatta has entered his mother’s womb, no affliction whatsoever arises in the Bodhisatta’s mother. The Bodhisatta’s mother is happy, her body unwearied, and the Bodhisatta’s mother sees the Bodhisatta within her womb complete in all his limbs, with unimpaired faculties. This is the nature of things here."

24. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, sattāhajāte bodhisatte bodhisattamātā kālaṅkaroti tusitaṃ kāyaṃ upapajjati. Ayamettha dhammatā.

24. "This, monks, is the nature of things: seven days after the Bodhisatta is born, the Bodhisatta's mother passes away and is reborn in the Tusita realm. This is the nature of things here."

25. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yathā aññā itthikā nava vā dasa vā māse gabbhaṃ kucchinā pariharitvā vijāyanti, na hevaṃ bodhisattaṃ bodhisattamātā vijāyati. Daseva māsāni bodhisattaṃ bodhisattamātā kucchinā pariharitvā vijāyati. Ayamettha dhammatā.

25. "This, monks, is the nature of things: just as other women carry a fetus in the womb for nine or ten months before giving birth, the Bodhisatta's mother does not give birth to the Bodhisatta in that way. The Bodhisatta's mother carries the Bodhisatta in her womb for exactly ten months before giving birth. This is the nature of things here."

26. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yathā aññā itthikā nisinnā vā nipannā vā vijāyanti, na hevaṃ bodhisattaṃ bodhisattamātā vijāyati. Ṭhitāva bodhisattaṃ bodhisattamātā vijāyati. Ayamettha dhammatā.

26. "This, monks, is the nature of things: just as other women give birth while sitting or lying down, the Bodhisatta’s mother does not give birth to the Bodhisatta in that way. The Bodhisatta’s mother gives birth to the Bodhisatta while standing. This is the nature of things here."

27. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchimhā nikkhamati, devā paṭhamaṃ paṭiggaṇhanti, pacchā manussā. Ayamettha dhammatā.

27. This, monks, is the nature of things: when the Bodhisatta emerges from his mother’s womb, the devas receive him first, and afterward the humans. This is the nature of things here.

28. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchimhā nikkhamati, appattova bodhisatto pathaviṃ hoti, cattāro naṃ devaputtā paṭiggahetvā mātu purato ṭhapenti – ‘attamanā, devi, hohi; mahesakkho te putto uppanno’ti. Ayamettha dhammatā.

28. "This is the nature of things, monks: when the Bodhisatta emerges from his mother's womb, he does not yet touch the earth. Four divine youths receive him and place him before his mother, saying: 'Be of glad mind, queen; a son of great power has been born to you.' This is the nature of things here."

29. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchimhā nikkhamati, visadova nikkhamati amakkhito udena [uddena (syā.), udarena (katthaci)] amakkhito semhena amakkhito ruhirena amakkhito kenaci asucinā suddho [visuddho (syā.)] visado. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, maṇiratanaṃ kāsike vatthe nikkhittaṃ neva maṇiratanaṃ kāsikaṃ vatthaṃ makkheti, nāpi kāsikaṃ vatthaṃ maṇiratanaṃ makkheti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Ubhinnaṃ suddhattā. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchimhā nikkhamati, visadova nikkhamati amakkhito, udena amakkhito semhena amakkhito ruhirena amakkhito kenaci asucinā suddho visado. Ayamettha dhammatā.

29. "This is the nature of things, monks, that when the Bodhisatta emerges from his mother's womb, he emerges unsullied, untainted by water, untainted by mucus, untainted by blood, untainted by any impurity—pure and spotless. Just as, monks, a precious gem placed on Kāsi cloth does not stain the Kāsi cloth, nor does the Kāsi cloth stain the precious gem—why is that? Because both are pure. In the same way, monks, when the Bodhisatta emerges from his mother's womb, he emerges unsullied, untainted by water, untainted by mucus, untainted by blood, untainted by any impurity—pure and spotless. This is the nature of things here."

30. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchimhā nikkhamati, dve udakassa dhārā antalikkhā pātubhavanti – ekā sītassa ekā uṇhassa yena bodhisattassa udakakiccaṃ karonti mātu ca. Ayamettha dhammatā.

30. "This is the nature of things, monks, that when the Bodhisatta emerges from his mother’s womb, two streams of water appear from the sky—one cool and one warm—with which they bathe the Bodhisatta and his mother. This is the nature of things here."

31. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, sampatijāto bodhisatto samehi pādehi patiṭṭhahitvā uttarābhimukho [uttarenābhimukho (syā.) uttarenamukho (ka.)] sattapadavītihārena gacchati setamhi chatte anudhāriyamāne, sabbā ca disā anuviloketi, āsabhiṃ vācaṃ bhāsati ‘aggohamasmi lokassa, jeṭṭhohamasmi lokassa, seṭṭhohamasmi lokassa, ayamantimā jāti, natthidāni punabbhavo’ti. Ayamettha dhammatā.

31. "This is the norm, monks, that when the Bodhisatta is just born, he stands firmly with level feet, faces north, takes seven strides while a white parasol is held over him, surveys all directions, and utters these words like a bull: ‘I am the chief of the world, the eldest of the world, the foremost of the world. This is my last birth; now there is no more renewed existence.’ This is the norm here."

32. ‘‘Dhammatā esā, bhikkhave, yadā bodhisatto mātukucchimhā nikkhamati, atha sadevake loke samārake sabrahmake sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiyā pajāya sadevamanussāya appamāṇo uḷāro obhāso pātubhavati, atikkammeva devānaṃ devānubhāvaṃ. Yāpi tā lokantarikā aghā asaṃvutā andhakārā andhakāratimisā, yattha pime candimasūriyā evaṃmahiddhikā evaṃmahānubhāvā ābhāya nānubhonti, tatthapi appamāṇo uḷāro obhāso pātubhavati atikkammeva devānaṃ devānubhāvaṃ. Yepi tattha sattā upapannā, tepi tenobhāsena aññamaññaṃ sañjānanti – ‘aññepi kira, bho, santi sattā idhūpapannā’ti. Ayañca dasasahassī lokadhātu saṅkampati sampakampati sampavedhati appamāṇo ca uḷāro obhāso loke pātubhavati atikkammeva devānaṃ devānubhāvaṃ. Ayamettha dhammatā.

32. "This is the norm, monks, that when the Bodhisatta emerges from his mother's womb, then in the world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, with its population of recluses and brahmins, devas and humans, an immeasurable, sublime radiance appears, surpassing even the divine power of the devas. And those inter-world spaces that are dreadful, uncovered, and utterly dark, where even these moon and sun, so mighty and powerful, cannot spread their light, there too an immeasurable, sublime radiance appears, surpassing the divine power of the devas. And the beings reborn there recognize one another by that light, saying: 'Indeed, friends, other beings are reborn here!' And this ten-thousandfold world system shakes, quakes, and trembles, and an immeasurable, sublime radiance appears in the world, surpassing even the divine power of the devas. This is the norm here."

## Dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇā

### The Thirty-Two Marks of a Great Man.

33. ‘‘Jāte kho pana, bhikkhave, vipassimhi kumāre bandhumato rañño paṭivedesuṃ – ‘putto te, deva [deva te (ka.)], jāto, taṃ devo passatū’ti. Addasā kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā vipassiṃ kumāraṃ, disvā nemitte brāhmaṇe āmantāpetvā etadavoca – ‘passantu bhonto nemittā brāhmaṇā kumāra’nti. Addasaṃsu kho, bhikkhave, nemittā brāhmaṇā vipassiṃ kumāraṃ, disvā bandhumantaṃ rājānaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘attamano, deva, hohi, mahesakkho te putto uppanno, lābhā te, mahārāja, suladdhaṃ te, mahārāja, yassa te kule evarūpo putto uppanno. Ayañhi, deva, kumāro dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi samannāgato, yehi samannāgatassa mahāpurisassa dveva gatiyo bhavanti anaññā. Sace agāraṃ ajjhāvasati, rājā hoti cakkavattī dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāvī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni sattaratanāni bhavanti. Seyyathidaṃ – cakkaratanaṃ hatthiratanaṃ assaratanaṃ maṇiratanaṃ itthiratanaṃ gahapatiratanaṃ pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā bhavanti sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena abhivijiya ajjhāvasati. Sace kho pana agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajati, arahaṃ hoti sammāsambuddho loke vivaṭacchado.

33. "When Prince Vipassī was born, bhikkhus, they announced to King Bandhuma: ‘A son is born to you, Your Majesty. May Your Majesty see him.’ King Bandhuma saw Prince Vipassī, bhikkhus, and having seen him, he summoned the brahmin diviners and said: ‘Let the honorable diviners examine the prince.’ The diviners saw Prince Vipassī, bhikkhus, and having seen him, they said to King Bandhuma: ‘Be happy, Your Majesty! A mighty son has been born to you. It’s a gain for you, great king, a great blessing for you, great king, that such a son has been born in your family. For this prince, Your Majesty, is equipped with the thirty-two marks of a great man. For a great man who has these marks, there are only two possible destinies, no other. If he lives at home, he will become a wheel-turning monarch, a righteous king who rules according to law, a conqueror of the four quarters, who has established the country in security and possesses the seven treasures. These are his seven treasures: the wheel treasure, the elephant treasure, the horse treasure, the jewel treasure, the woman treasure, the treasurer treasure, and the counselor treasure as the seventh. He will have over a thousand sons, brave and heroic, who will crush the armies of his enemies. Having conquered this earth to its ocean bounds, he will rule it without rod or weapon, by means of the Dhamma. But if he goes forth from the householder’s life into homelessness, he will become a perfected one, a fully awakened Buddha, with the veil removed from the world.”

34. ‘Katamehi cāyaṃ, deva, kumāro dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi samannāgato, yehi samannāgatassa mahāpurisassa dveva gatiyo bhavanti anaññā. Sace agāraṃ ajjhāvasati, rājā hoti cakkavattī dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāpī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni sattaratanāni bhavanti. Seyyathidaṃ – cakkaratanaṃ hatthiratanaṃ assaratanaṃ maṇiratanaṃ itthiratanaṃ gahapatiratanaṃ pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā bhavanti sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena abhivijiya ajjhāvasati. Sace kho pana agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajati, arahaṃ hoti sammāsambuddho loke vivaṭacchado.

34. "And by which thirty-two marks, Your Majesty, is this prince endowed, such that for a great man endowed with these marks only two destinies are possible, no other? If he lives at home, he will become a wheel-turning monarch, a righteous king who rules according to law, a conqueror of the four quarters, who has established the country in security and possesses the seven treasures. These are his seven treasures: the wheel treasure, the elephant treasure, the horse treasure, the jewel treasure, the woman treasure, the treasurer treasure, and the counselor treasure as the seventh. He will have over a thousand sons, brave and heroic, who will crush the armies of his enemies. Having conquered this earth to its ocean bounds, he will rule it without rod or weapon, by means of the Dhamma. But if he goes forth from the householder’s life into homelessness, he will become a perfected one, a fully awakened Buddha, with the veil removed from the world.”

35. ‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro suppatiṭṭhitapādo. Yaṃ pāyaṃ, deva, kumāro suppatiṭṭhitapādo. Idampissa mahāpurisassa mahāpurisalakkhaṇaṃ bhavati.

35. 'This prince, O king, has well-planted feet. That the prince's feet are well-planted, O king, this is one mark of a great man.

‘Imassa, deva [imassa hi deva (?)], kumārassa heṭṭhā pādatalesu cakkāni jātāni sahassārāni sanemikāni sanābhikāni sabbākāraparipūrāni. Yampi, imassa deva, kumārassa heṭṭhā pādatalesu cakkāni jātāni sahassārāni sanemikāni sanābhikāni sabbākāraparipūrāni, idampissa mahāpurisassa mahāpurisalakkhaṇaṃ bhavati.

'On the soles of this prince’s feet, O king, wheels have appeared with a thousand spokes, complete with rims and hubs, perfect in every detail. And that, O king, wheels have appeared on the soles of this prince’s feet, with a thousand spokes, complete with rims and hubs, perfect in every detail—this is a mark of a great man.'

‘Ayañhi deva, kumāro āyatapaṇhī…pe…

'This prince, O king, has long heels...

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro dīghaṅgulī…

'This prince, O king, has long fingers...

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro mudutalunahatthapādo…

This prince, O king, has soft, tender hands and feet...

‘Ayañhi, deva kumāro jālahatthapādo…

'This prince, O king, has webbed fingers and toes.'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro ussaṅkhapādo…

'This prince, O king, has high ankles...'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro eṇijaṅgho…

This prince, O king, has antelope-like legs...

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro ṭhitakova anonamanto ubhohi pāṇitalehi jaṇṇukāni parimasati [parāmasati (ka.)] parimajjati…

Indeed, O king, this prince, standing and not bending down, feels and rubs both knees with the palms of his hands...

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro kosohitavatthaguyho…

'Indeed, O king, this prince is concealed as to his genitals by a sheath-like covering...'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro suvaṇṇavaṇṇo kañcanasannibhattaco…

Indeed, sire, this prince has a golden complexion, his skin the hue of refined gold.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro sukhumacchavī; sukhumattā chaviyā rajojallaṃ kāye na upalimpati [upalippati (syā.)] …

Indeed, sire, this prince has delicate skin; because of the delicacy of his skin, dust and dirt do not adhere to his body...

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro ekekalomo; ekekāni lomāni lomakūpesu jātāni…

Indeed, sire, this prince has single hairs; each hair grows in a single pore.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro uddhaggalomo; uddhaggāni lomāni jātāni nīlāni añjanavaṇṇāni kuṇḍalāvaṭṭāni dakkhiṇāvaṭṭakajātāni…

Indeed, sire, this prince has hairs that stand upright; his hairs stand upright, are blue-black like collyrium, curled, and coiled clockwise.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro brahmujugatto…

'Indeed, sire, this prince is upright, with a Brahma-like body...'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro sattussado…

'Indeed, sire, this prince has seven convexities.'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro sīhapubbaddhakāyo…

'Indeed, O king, this prince has the lion's anterior half of his body...'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro citantaraṃso [pitantaraṃso (syā.)] …

Indeed, sire, this prince is well-fleshed between the shoulders...

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro nigrodhaparimaṇḍalo yāvatakvassa kāyo tāvatakvassa byāmo, yāvatakvassa byāmo, tāvatakvassa kāyo…

Indeed, sire, this prince has the symmetry of a banyan tree. As far as his body extends, so far extends his span; and as far as his span extends, so far extends his body.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro samavaṭṭakkhandho…

Indeed, sire, this prince has evenly rounded shoulders.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro rasaggasaggī…

'Indeed, sire, this prince has the most excellent sense of taste.'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro sīhahanu…

Indeed, sire, this prince has a lion's jaw...

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro cattālīsadanto…

'Indeed, sire, this prince has forty teeth...'

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro samadanto…

Indeed, sire, this prince has even teeth.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro aviraḷadanto…

Indeed, sire, this prince has closely set teeth.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro susukkadāṭho…

Indeed, sire, this prince has very white eye-teeth.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro pahūtajivho…

Indeed, sire, this prince has a large tongue.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro brahmassaro karavīkabhāṇī…

Indeed, sire, this prince has a voice like Brahmā and speaks with the voice of the karavīka bird…

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro abhinīlanetto…

Indeed, sire, this prince has deep blue eyes.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro gopakhumo…

Indeed, sire, this prince has cow-like eyelashes.

Imassa, deva, kumārassa uṇṇā bhamukantare jātā odātā mudutūlasannibhā. Yampi imassa deva kumārassa uṇṇā bhamukantare jātā odātā mudutūlasannibhā, idampimassa mahāpurisassa mahāpurisalakkhaṇaṃ bhavati.

Sire, between the eyebrows of this prince a hair has grown, white and soft like cotton-down. Sire, since between the eyebrows of this prince a hair has grown, white and soft like cotton-down, this is a mark of a Great Man.

‘Ayañhi, deva, kumāro uṇhīsasīso. Yaṃ pāyaṃ, deva, kumāro uṇhīsasīso, idampissa mahāpurisassa mahāpurisalakkhaṇaṃ bhavati.

'Sire, this prince has a protuberance on his head. Sire, since this prince has a protuberance on his head, this is a mark of a Great Man.'

36. ‘Imehi kho ayaṃ, deva, kumāro dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi samannāgato, yehi samannāgatassa mahāpurisassa dveva gatiyo bhavanti anaññā. Sace agāraṃ ajjhāvasati, rājā hoti cakkavattī dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāvī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni sattaratanāni bhavanti. Seyyathidaṃ – cakkaratanaṃ hatthiratanaṃ assaratanaṃ maṇiratanaṃ itthiratanaṃ gahapatiratanaṃ pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā bhavanti sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena [dhammena samena (syā.)] abhivijiya ajjhāvasati. Sace kho pana agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajati, arahaṃ hoti sammāsambuddho loke vivaṭacchado’ti.

36. 'Indeed, sire, this prince is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man. For one endowed with these marks, only two destinies are possible, no other. If he lives the household life, he becomes a wheel-turning monarch—a righteous king, a king of righteousness, conqueror of the four quarters, who establishes dominion in his country, possessed of the seven treasures. These are his seven treasures: the wheel-treasure, the elephant-treasure, the horse-treasure, the jewel-treasure, the woman-treasure, the householder-treasure, and the advisor-treasure as the seventh. He has over a thousand sons, heroes, mighty in form, crushers of enemy armies. He rules this earth bounded by the ocean without rod or sword, by righteousness. But if he goes forth from the household life into homelessness, he becomes an Arahant, a Perfectly Enlightened One, one who draws back the veil in the world.'

## Vipassīsamaññā

### The Designation of Vipassī

37. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā nemitte brāhmaṇe ahatehi vatthehi acchādāpetvā [acchādetvā (syā.)] sabbakāmehi santappesi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā vipassissa kumārassa dhātiyo upaṭṭhāpesi. Aññā khīraṃ pāyenti, aññā nhāpenti, aññā dhārenti, aññā aṅkena pariharanti. Jātassa kho pana, bhikkhave, vipassissa kumārassa setacchattaṃ dhārayittha divā ceva rattiñca – ‘mā naṃ sītaṃ vā uṇhaṃ vā tiṇaṃ vā rajo vā ussāvo vā bādhayitthā’ti. Jāto kho pana, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bahuno janassa piyo ahosi manāpo. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, uppalaṃ vā padumaṃ vā puṇḍarīkaṃ vā bahuno janassa piyaṃ manāpaṃ; evameva kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bahuno janassa piyo ahosi manāpo. Svāssudaṃ aṅkeneva aṅkaṃ parihariyati.

37. Then, monks, King Bandhuma clothed the soothsaying brahmins in new garments and satisfied them with all kinds of pleasures. Then, monks, King Bandhuma appointed nurses for Prince Vipassī. Some gave him milk, some bathed him, some carried him, some carried him in their arms. When Prince Vipassī was born, monks, a white parasol was held over him day and night—‘lest cold or heat, grass or dust, dew should trouble him.’ Born, monks, Prince Vipassī was dear and beloved to many people. Just as, monks, a blue lotus, a red lotus, or a white lotus is dear and beloved to many people; so too, monks, Prince Vipassī was dear and beloved to many people. He was constantly carried from arm to arm.

38. ‘‘Jāto kho pana, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro mañjussaro ca [kumāro brahmassaro mañjussaro ca (sī. ka.)] ahosi vaggussaro ca madhurassaro ca pemaniyassaro ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, himavante pabbate karavīkā nāma sakuṇajāti mañjussarā ca vaggussarā ca madhurassarā ca pemaniyassarā ca; evameva kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro mañjussaro ca ahosi vaggussaro ca madhurassaro ca pemaniyassaro ca.

38.When Prince Vipassī was born, monks, he had a clear, well-modulated, sweet, and endearing voice. Just as, monks, in the Himalayas there is a kind of bird called the karavīka, which has a clear, well-modulated, sweet, and endearing voice; so too, monks, Prince Vipassī had a clear, well-modulated, sweet, and endearing voice.

39. ‘‘Jātassa kho pana, bhikkhave, vipassissa kumārassa kammavipākajaṃ dibbacakkhu pāturahosi yena sudaṃ [yena dūraṃ (syā.)] samantā yojanaṃ passati divā ceva rattiñca.

39.When Prince Vipassī was born, monks, the divine eye, resulting from kamma, appeared to him, with which he could see a yojana all around, both day and night.

40. ‘‘Jāto kho pana, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro animisanto pekkhati seyyathāpi devā tāvatiṃsā. ‘Animisanto kumāro pekkhatī’ti kho, bhikkhave [animisanto pekkhati, jātassa kho pana bhikkhave (ka.)], vipassissa kumārassa ‘vipassī vipassī’ tveva samaññā udapādi.

40.When Prince Vipassī was born, monks, he gazed without blinking, just as the devas of Tāvatiṃsa do. Because ‘the prince gazes without blinking,’ monks, the designation ‘Vipassī, Vipassī’ arose for Prince Vipassī.

41. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā atthakaraṇe [aṭṭa karaṇe (syā.)] nisinno vipassiṃ kumāraṃ aṅke nisīdāpetvā atthe anusāsati. Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro pituaṅke nisinno viceyya viceyya atthe panāyati ñāyena [aṭṭe panāyati ñāṇena (syā.)]. Viceyya viceyya kumāro atthe panāyati ñāyenāti kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa kumārassa bhiyyosomattāya ‘vipassī vipassī’ tveva samaññā udapādi.

41.Then, monks, King Bandhuma, seated deciding cases, placed Prince Vipassī on his lap and advised on the cases. There, monks, Prince Vipassī, seated on his father's lap, thoroughly examined and skillfully resolved the cases according to justice. 'The prince thoroughly examines and skillfully resolves the cases according to justice,' monks, and so the designation 'Vipassī, Vipassī' arose especially for Prince Vipassī.

42. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā vipassissa kumārassa tayo pāsāde kārāpesi, ekaṃ vassikaṃ ekaṃ hemantikaṃ ekaṃ gimhikaṃ; pañca kāmaguṇāni upaṭṭhāpesi. Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro vassike pāsāde cattāro māse [vassike pāsāde vassike] nippurisehi tūriyehi paricārayamāno na heṭṭhāpāsādaṃ orohatī’’ti.

42.Then, monks, King Bandhuma had three palaces built for Prince Vipassī: one for the rainy season, one for the cold season, and one for the hot season. He provided him with the five cords of sensual pleasure. There, monks, Prince Vipassī dwelt in the rainy-season palace for four months, entertained by orchestras without men, without descending from the lower palace.

## Paṭhamabhāṇavāro.

### First recitation section.

## Jiṇṇapuriso

### Old man.

43. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bahūnaṃ vassānaṃ bahūnaṃ vassasatānaṃ bahūnaṃ vassasahassānaṃ accayena sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘yojehi, samma sārathi, bhaddāni bhaddāni yānāni uyyānabhūmiṃ gacchāma subhūmidassanāyā’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā bhaddāni bhaddāni yānāni yojetvā vipassissa kumārassa paṭivedesi – ‘yuttāni kho te, deva, bhaddāni bhaddāni yānāni, yassa dāni kālaṃ maññasī’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bhaddaṃ bhaddaṃ yānaṃ [bhadraṃ yānaṃ (syā.), bhaddaṃ yānaṃ (pī.) cattāro māse (sī. pī.)] abhiruhitvā bhaddehi bhaddehi yānehi uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyāsi.

43.“Then, monks, after the lapse of many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years, Prince Vipassī addressed his charioteer: ‘Harness, good charioteer, the fine carriages; let us go to the pleasure ground to see the beautiful terrain.’ ‘As you command, lord,’ the charioteer replied to Prince Vipassī. Having harnessed the fine carriages, he informed Prince Vipassī: ‘The fine carriages are yoked for you, lord; it is now time as you deem fit.’ Then, monks, Prince Vipassī mounted a fine carriage and set out for the pleasure ground in fine carriages.

44. ‘‘Addasā kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto purisaṃ jiṇṇaṃ gopānasivaṅkaṃ bhoggaṃ [bhaggaṃ (syā.)] daṇḍaparāyanaṃ pavedhamānaṃ gacchantaṃ āturaṃ gatayobbanaṃ. Disvā sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘ayaṃ pana, samma sārathi, puriso kiṃkato? Kesāpissa na yathā aññesaṃ, kāyopissa na yathā aññesa’nti. ‘Eso kho, deva, jiṇṇo nāmā’ti. ‘Kiṃ paneso, samma sārathi, jiṇṇo nāmā’ti? ‘Eso kho, deva, jiṇṇo nāma. Na dāni tena ciraṃ jīvitabbaṃ bhavissatī’ti. ‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, ahampi jarādhammo, jaraṃ anatīto’ti? ‘Tvañca, deva, mayañcamha sabbe jarādhammā, jaraṃ anatītā’ti. ‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, alaṃ dānajja uyyānabhūmiyā. Itova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāhī’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā tatova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāsi. Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro antepuraṃ gato dukkhī dummano pajjhāyati – ‘dhiratthu kira, bho, jāti nāma, yatra hi nāma jātassa jarā paññāyissatī’ti!

44.“As Prince Vipassī was going out to the pleasure ground, monks, he saw an old man—bent like a roof beam, stooped, leaning on a staff, trembling as he walked, ailing and past his youth. Seeing him, he addressed the charioteer: ‘What is this, my dear charioteer, that has happened to this man? His hair is not like others', his body is not like others’.’ ‘That, lord, is what is called an old man.’ ‘But why, my dear charioteer, is he called an old man?’ ‘He is called an old man, lord, because he will not now live much longer.’ ‘But, my dear charioteer, am I also subject to old age, not exempt from old age?’ ‘You, lord, and we too, are all subject to old age, not exempt from old age.’ ‘Then enough, my dear charioteer, with the pleasure grounds for today. Turn the carriage back to the inner palace.’ ‘As you command, lord,’ the charioteer replied to Prince Vipassī, and turned the carriage back to the inner palace. There, monks, Prince Vipassī, having returned to the inner palace, was sorrowful and dejected, pondering: ‘Alas, indeed, birth is a thing to be abhorred! For truly, for one who is born, old age will appear!’

45. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā sārathiṃ āmantāpetvā etadavoca – ‘kacci, samma sārathi, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā abhiramittha? Kacci, samma sārathi, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā attamano ahosī’ti? ‘Na kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā abhiramittha, na kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā attamano ahosī’ti. ‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, addasa kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto’ti? ‘Addasā kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto purisaṃ jiṇṇaṃ gopānasivaṅkaṃ bhoggaṃ daṇḍaparāyanaṃ pavedhamānaṃ gacchantaṃ āturaṃ gatayobbanaṃ. Disvā maṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayaṃ pana, samma sārathi, puriso kiṃkato, kesāpissa na yathā aññesaṃ, kāyopissa na yathā aññesa’’nti? ‘‘Eso kho, deva, jiṇṇo nāmā’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ paneso, samma sārathi, jiṇṇo nāmā’’ti? ‘‘Eso kho, deva, jiṇṇo nāma na dāni tena ciraṃ jīvitabbaṃ bhavissatī’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, ahampi jarādhammo, jaraṃ anatīto’’ti? ‘‘Tvañca, deva, mayañcamha sabbe jarādhammā, jaraṃ anatītā’’ti.

45.“Then, monks, King Bandhumā summoned the charioteer and said to him: 'Did the prince enjoy himself at the pleasure grounds, my dear charioteer? Was the prince pleased with the pleasure grounds?' 'No, lord, the prince did not enjoy himself at the pleasure grounds, nor was the prince pleased with the pleasure grounds.' 'But what did the prince see when going to the pleasure grounds?' 'When going to the pleasure grounds, lord, the prince saw an old man—bent like a roof beam, stooped, leaning on a staff, trembling as he walked, ailing and past his youth. Seeing him, he said to me: "What is this, my dear charioteer, that has happened to this man? His hair is not like others', his body is not like others'." "That, lord, is what is called an old man." "But what is this, my dear charioteer, called an old man?" "He is called an old man, lord, because he will not live much longer now." "But, my dear charioteer, am I also subject to old age, not exempt from old age?" "You, lord, and we too, are all subject to old age, not exempt from old age."

‘‘‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, alaṃ dānajja uyyānabhūmiyā, itova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāhī’’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, devā’’ti kho ahaṃ, deva, vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā tatova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāsiṃ. So kho, deva, kumāro antepuraṃ gato dukkhī dummano pajjhāyati – ‘‘dhiratthu kira bho jāti nāma, yatra hi nāma jātassa jarā paññāyissatī’’’ti.

“‘Well then, good charioteer, enough of the park today. Turn back the chariot to the inner palace from here.’" ‘Yes, sire,’ I replied to Prince Vipassī, and turned back the chariot to the inner palace from there. That prince, sire, having gone to the inner palace, distressed and sorrowful, was brooding: “Alas, indeed, birth is a thing to be abhorred! For truly, for one who is born, old age will appear!”

## Byādhitapuriso

### The Sick Man

46. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumassa rañño etadahosi –

46.“Then, bhikkhus, it occurred to King Bandhuma:

‘Mā heva kho vipassī kumāro na rajjaṃ kāresi, mā heva vipassī kumāro agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji, mā heva nemittānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ assa vacana’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā vipassissa kumārassa bhiyyosomattāya pañca kāmaguṇāni upaṭṭhāpesi – ‘yathā vipassī kumāro rajjaṃ kareyya, yathā vipassī kumāro na agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajeyya, yathā nemittānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ micchā assa vacana’nti.

‘May Prince Vipassī rule, may Prince Vipassī not go forth from the household life into homelessness, may the words of the soothsayer brahmins not be true.’ Then, bhikkhus, King Bandhuma provided Prince Vipassī with even more of the five strands of sensual pleasure—so that Prince Vipassī would rule, so that Prince Vipassī would not go forth from the household life into homelessness, so that the words of the soothsayer brahmins would be false.”

‘‘Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappito samaṅgībhūto paricāreti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bahūnaṃ vassānaṃ…pe…

“There indeed, bhikkhus, Prince Vipassī lived, endowed and furnished with the five strands of sensual pleasure. Then, bhikkhus, after many years had passed…”

47. ‘‘Addasā kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto purisaṃ ābādhikaṃ dukkhitaṃ bāḷhagilānaṃ sake muttakarīse palipannaṃ semānaṃ [sayamānaṃ (syā. ka.)] aññehi vuṭṭhāpiyamānaṃ aññehi saṃvesiyamānaṃ. Disvā sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘ayaṃ pana, samma sārathi, puriso kiṃkato? Akkhīnipissa na yathā aññesaṃ, saropissa [siropissa (syā.)] na yathā aññesa’nti? ‘Eso kho, deva, byādhito nāmā’ti. ‘Kiṃ paneso, samma sārathi, byādhito nāmā’ti? ‘Eso kho, deva, byādhito nāma appeva nāma tamhā ābādhā vuṭṭhaheyyā’ti. ‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, ahampi byādhidhammo, byādhiṃ anatīto’ti? ‘Tvañca, deva, mayañcamha sabbe byādhidhammā, byādhiṃ anatītā’ti. ‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, alaṃ dānajja uyyānabhūmiyā, itova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāhī’ti. ‘Evaṃ devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā tatova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāsi. Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro antepuraṃ gato dukkhī dummano pajjhāyati – ‘dhiratthu kira bho jāti nāma, yatra hi nāma jātassa jarā paññāyissati, byādhi paññāyissatī’ti.

47.“While going to the pleasure grounds, bhikkhus, Prince Vipassī saw a man who was sick—afflicted, suffering, gravely ill, lying in his own urine and excrement, being lifted up by some and laid down by others. Seeing this, he addressed the charioteer: ‘Good charioteer, what has happened to this man? His eyes are not like those of others, and his voice is not like that of others.’ ‘That, sire, is what is called a sick man.’ ‘But why, good charioteer, is he called a sick man?’ ‘That, sire, is called a sick man because it’s unlikely he will recover from that illness.’ ‘But, good charioteer, am I also subject to illness, not exempt from illness?’ ‘You, sire, and we all are subject to illness, not exempt from illness.’ ‘Well then, good charioteer, enough of the pleasure grounds for today. Turn back the chariot to the palace from here.’ ‘Yes, sire,’ the charioteer replied to Prince Vipassī, and turned back the chariot to the palace from there. There indeed, bhikkhus, Prince Vipassī, having returned to the palace, distressed and sorrowful, reflected: ‘Alas, birth indeed! For indeed, for one who is born, aging will be apparent, illness will be apparent!’”

48. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā sārathiṃ āmantāpetvā etadavoca – ‘kacci, samma sārathi, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā abhiramittha, kacci, samma sārathi, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā attamano ahosī’ti? ‘Na kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā abhiramittha, na kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā attamano ahosī’ti. ‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, addasa kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto’ti? ‘Addasā kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto purisaṃ ābādhikaṃ dukkhitaṃ bāḷhagilānaṃ sake muttakarīse palipannaṃ semānaṃ aññehi vuṭṭhāpiyamānaṃ aññehi saṃvesiyamānaṃ. Disvā maṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayaṃ pana, samma sārathi, puriso kiṃkato, akkhīnipissa na yathā aññesaṃ, saropissa na yathā aññesa’’nti? ‘‘Eso kho, deva, byādhito nāmā’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ paneso, samma sārathi, byādhito nāmā’’ti? ‘‘Eso kho, deva, byādhito nāma appeva nāma tamhā ābādhā vuṭṭhaheyyā’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, ahampi byādhidhammo, byādhiṃ anatīto’’ti? ‘‘Tvañca, deva, mayañcamha sabbe byādhidhammā, byādhiṃ anatītā’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, alaṃ dānajja uyyānabhūmiyā, itova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāhī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, devā’’ti kho ahaṃ, deva, vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā tatova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāsiṃ. So kho, deva, kumāro antepuraṃ gato dukkhī dummano pajjhāyati – ‘‘‘dhiratthu kira bho jāti nāma, yatra hi nāma jātassa jarā paññāyissati, byādhi paññāyissatī’’’ti.

48.“Then, bhikkhus, King Bandhumā addressed the charioteer, saying: ‘Well, good charioteer, did the prince enjoy the pleasure garden? Was he pleased with the pleasure garden?’ ‘No, sire, the prince did not enjoy the pleasure garden; he was not pleased with it.’ ‘But, good charioteer, what did the prince see when he went to the pleasure garden?’ ‘Sire, as the prince was going to the pleasure garden, he saw a man afflicted, suffering, gravely ill, lying in his own urine and excrement, being lifted up by some and laid down by others. Seeing him, he asked me: “Good charioteer, what is this man’s condition? His eyes are not like others’, his voice is not like others’.” “Sire, that is what is called a sick man.” “But, good charioteer, what is meant by a sick man?” “Sire, a sick man is one who may not recover from that illness.” “But, good charioteer, am I also subject to illness, not exempt from illness?” “You, sire, and we—all are subject to illness, not exempt from illness.” “Well then, good charioteer, enough of the pleasure garden for today; turn back the chariot to the palace from here.” “Yes, sire,” I replied to Prince Vipassī and turned back the chariot to the palace from there. The prince, having returned to the palace, was distressed and sorrowful, reflected: ‘Alas, birth indeed! For indeed, for one who is born, aging will be apparent, illness will be apparent!’”

## Kālaṅkatapuriso

### One who had completed his time

49. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumassa rañño etadahosi – ‘mā heva kho vipassī kumāro na rajjaṃ kāresi, mā heva vipassī kumāro agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji, mā heva nemittānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ assa vacana’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā vipassissa kumārassa bhiyyosomattāya pañca kāmaguṇāni upaṭṭhāpesi – ‘yathā vipassī kumāro rajjaṃ kareyya, yathā vipassī kumāro na agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajeyya, yathā nemittānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ micchā assa vacana’nti.

49.Then, monks, it occurred to King Bandhuma: ‘May Prince Vipassī indeed administer the kingdom; may Prince Vipassī not go forth from the household life into homelessness; may the word of the omen-reading brahmins be false.’ Then, monks, King Bandhuma provided Prince Vipassī with the five cords of sensual pleasure in greater abundance—‘so that Prince Vipassī might administer the kingdom, so that Prince Vipassī might not go forth from the household life into homelessness, so that the word of the omen-reading brahmins might be false.’

‘‘Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappito samaṅgībhūto paricāreti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bahūnaṃ vassānaṃ…pe…

Thus, monks, Prince Vipassī lived, endowed and furnished with the five cords of sensual pleasure. Then, monks, after many years had passed...

50. ‘‘Addasā kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto mahājanakāyaṃ sannipatitaṃ nānārattānañca dussānaṃ vilātaṃ kayiramānaṃ. Disvā sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘kiṃ nu kho, so, samma sārathi, mahājanakāyo sannipatito nānārattānañca dussānaṃ vilātaṃ kayiratī’ti? ‘Eso kho, deva, kālaṅkato nāmā’ti. ‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, yena so kālaṅkato tena rathaṃ pesehī’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā yena so kālaṅkato tena rathaṃ pesesi. Addasā kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro petaṃ kālaṅkataṃ, disvā sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘kiṃ panāyaṃ, samma sārathi, kālaṅkato nāmā’ti? ‘Eso kho, deva, kālaṅkato nāma. Na dāni taṃ dakkhanti mātā vā pitā vā aññe vā ñātisālohitā, sopi na dakkhissati mātaraṃ vā pitaraṃ vā aññe vā ñātisālohite’ti. ‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, ahampi maraṇadhammo maraṇaṃ anatīto; mampi na dakkhanti devo vā devī vā aññe vā ñātisālohitā; ahampi na dakkhissāmi devaṃ vā deviṃ vā aññe vā ñātisālohite’ti? ‘Tvañca, deva, mayañcamha sabbe maraṇadhammā maraṇaṃ anatītā; tampi na dakkhanti devo vā devī vā aññe vā ñātisālohitā; tvampi na dakkhissasi devaṃ vā deviṃ vā aññe vā ñātisālohite’ti. ‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, alaṃ dānajja uyyānabhūmiyā, itova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāhī’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā tatova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāsi. Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro antepuraṃ gato dukkhī dummano pajjhāyati – ‘dhiratthu kira, bho, jāti nāma, yatra hi nāma jātassa jarā paññāyissati, byādhi paññāyissati, maraṇaṃ paññāyissatī’ti.

50.Then, monks, Prince Vipassī, while proceeding to the pleasure park, saw a great crowd assembled and a bier being prepared with various coloured cloths. Seeing this, he addressed the charioteer: ‘What, dear charioteer, is this great crowd assembled, and why is a bier being prepared with various coloured cloths?’ ‘That, sire, is what is called one who has completed his time.’ ‘Then, dear charioteer, drive the chariot to where that one who has completed his time is.’ ‘Yes, sire,’ replied the charioteer to Prince Vipassī, and he drove the chariot to where the one who had completed his time was. Monks, Prince Vipassī saw the one who had completed his time and addressed the charioteer: ‘But what, dear charioteer, is this called one who has completed his time?’ ‘That, sire, is called one who has completed his time. Now neither mother nor father nor any other relatives will see him, nor will he see mother, father, or any other relatives.’ ‘But, dear charioteer, am I too subject to death, not exempt from death? Will neither the king nor the queen nor any other relatives see me, nor will I see the king, the queen, or any other relatives?’ ‘You, sire, and we too—all are subject to death, not exempt from death. Neither the king nor the queen nor any other relatives will see you, nor will you see the king, the queen, or any other relatives.’ ‘Then, dear charioteer, enough of the pleasure park for today. Turn the chariot back to the inner palace.’ ‘Yes, sire,’ replied the charioteer to Prince Vipassī, and he turned the chariot back to the inner palace. There, monks, Prince Vipassī, having returned to the inner palace, was sorrowful and dejected, pondering: ‘Alas, truly wretched is this thing called birth, since for one who is born, aging will appear, sickness will appear, death will appear.’

51. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā sārathiṃ āmantāpetvā etadavoca – ‘kacci, samma sārathi, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā abhiramittha, kacci, samma sārathi, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā attamano ahosī’ti? ‘Na kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā abhiramittha, na kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiyā attamano ahosī’ti. ‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, addasa kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto’ti? ‘Addasā kho, deva, kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto mahājanakāyaṃ sannipatitaṃ nānārattānañca dussānaṃ vilātaṃ kayiramānaṃ. Disvā maṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃ nu kho, so, samma sārathi, mahājanakāyo sannipatito nānārattānañca dussānaṃ vilātaṃ kayiratī’’ti? ‘‘Eso kho, deva, kālaṅkato nāmā’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, yena so kālaṅkato tena rathaṃ pesehī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ devā’’ti kho ahaṃ, deva, vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā yena so kālaṅkato tena rathaṃ pesesiṃ. Addasā kho, deva, kumāro petaṃ kālaṅkataṃ, disvā maṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃ panāyaṃ, samma sārathi, kālaṅkato nāmā’’ti? ‘‘Eso kho, deva, kālaṅkato nāma. Na dāni taṃ dakkhanti mātā vā pitā vā aññe vā ñātisālohitā, sopi na dakkhissati mātaraṃ vā pitaraṃ vā aññe vā ñātisālohite’’ti. ‘‘Kiṃ pana, samma sārathi, ahampi maraṇadhammo maraṇaṃ anatīto; mampi na dakkhanti devo vā devī vā aññe vā ñātisālohitā; ahampi na dakkhissāmi devaṃ vā deviṃ vā aññe vā ñātisālohite’’ti? ‘‘Tvañca, deva, mayañcamha sabbe maraṇadhammā maraṇaṃ anatītā; tampi na dakkhanti devo vā devī vā aññe vā ñātisālohitā, tvampi na dakkhissasi devaṃ vā deviṃ vā aññe vā ñātisālohite’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, samma sārathi, alaṃ dānajja uyyānabhūmiyā, itova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāhī’ti. ‘‘‘Evaṃ, devā’’ti kho ahaṃ, deva, vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā tatova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāsiṃ. So kho, deva, kumāro antepuraṃ gato dukkhī dummano pajjhāyati – ‘‘dhiratthu kira bho jāti nāma, yatra hi nāma jātassa jarā paññāyissati, byādhi paññāyissati, maraṇaṃ paññāyissatī’’’ti.

51.Then, monks, King Bandhuma had the charioteer summoned and said: ‘Dear charioteer, did the prince enjoy himself in the pleasure park? Was the prince pleased with the pleasure park?’ ‘No, sire, the prince did not enjoy himself in the pleasure park. No, sire, the prince was not pleased with the pleasure park.’ ‘But, dear charioteer, what did the prince see while proceeding to the pleasure park?’ ‘Sire, while proceeding to the pleasure park, the prince saw a great crowd assembled and a bier being prepared with various coloured cloths. Seeing this, he asked me: “Dear charioteer, why has this great crowd assembled, and why is a bier being prepared with various coloured cloths?” “That, sire, is what is called one who has completed his time.” “Then, dear charioteer, drive the chariot to where that one who has completed his time is.” “Yes, sire,” I replied to Prince Vipassī, and I drove the chariot to where the one who had completed his time was. Sire, the prince saw the one who had completed his time and asked me: “But what, dear charioteer, is this called one who has completed his time?” “That, sire, is called one who has completed his time. Now neither his mother nor his father nor any other relatives will see him, nor will he see his mother, his father, or any other relatives.” “But, dear charioteer, am I too subject to death, not exempt from death? Will neither the king nor the queen nor any other relatives see me, nor will I see the king, the queen, or any other relatives?” “You, sire, and we too—all are subject to death, not exempt from death. Neither the king nor the queen nor any other relatives will see you, nor will you see the king, the queen, or any other relatives.” “Then, dear charioteer, enough of the pleasure park for today. Turn the chariot back to the inner palace.” “Yes, sire,” I replied to Prince Vipassī, and I turned the chariot back to the inner palace. Sire, the prince, having returned to the inner palace, was sorrowful and dejected, pondering: “Alas, truly wretched is this thing called birth, since for one who is born, aging will appear, sickness will appear, death will appear.”’

## Pabbajito

### The Renunciant

52. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumassa rañño etadahosi – ‘mā heva kho vipassī kumāro na rajjaṃ kāresi, mā heva vipassī kumāro agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji, mā heva nemittānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ saccaṃ assa vacana’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, bandhumā rājā vipassissa kumārassa bhiyyosomattāya pañca kāmaguṇāni upaṭṭhāpesi – ‘yathā vipassī kumāro rajjaṃ kareyya, yathā vipassī kumāro na agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajeyya, yathā nemittānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ micchā assa vacana’nti.

52.“Then, bhikkhus, King Bandhuma had this thought: ‘Lest Prince Vipassī should not rule the kingdom; lest Prince Vipassī should go forth from the household life into homelessness; lest the words of the omen-reader brahmins should prove true.’ Then, bhikkhus, King Bandhuma provided even more of the five strands of sensual pleasure for Prince Vipassī, thinking: ‘So that Prince Vipassī might rule the kingdom, so that Prince Vipassī might not go forth from the household life into homelessness, so that the words of the omen-reader brahmins might prove false.’

‘‘Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappito samaṅgībhūto paricāreti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bahūnaṃ vassānaṃ bahūnaṃ vassasatānaṃ bahūnaṃ vassasahassānaṃ accayena sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘yojehi, samma sārathi, bhaddāni bhaddāni yānāni, uyyānabhūmiṃ gacchāma subhūmidassanāyā’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā bhaddāni bhaddāni yānāni yojetvā vipassissa kumārassa paṭivedesi – ‘yuttāni kho te, deva, bhaddāni bhaddāni yānāni, yassa dāni kālaṃ maññasī’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro bhaddaṃ bhaddaṃ yānaṃ abhiruhitvā bhaddehi bhaddehi yānehi uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyāsi.

“At that time, bhikkhus, Prince Vipassī was fully endowed and furnished with the five strands of sensual pleasure. Then, bhikkhus, after the passing of many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years, Prince Vipassī addressed his charioteer: ‘Harness the splendid carriages, my dear charioteer, we will go to the pleasure park to see the beautiful grounds.’ ‘Yes, lord,’ the charioteer, having assented to Prince Vipassī, harnessed the splendid carriages and informed Prince Vipassī: ‘The splendid carriages are harnessed for you, lord, now is the time as you deem fit.’ Then, bhikkhus, Prince Vipassī mounted a splendid carriage and set out for the pleasure park with splendid carriages.

53. ‘‘Addasā kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro uyyānabhūmiṃ niyyanto purisaṃ bhaṇḍuṃ pabbajitaṃ kāsāyavasanaṃ. Disvā sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘ayaṃ pana, samma sārathi, puriso kiṃkato? Sīsaṃpissa na yathā aññesaṃ, vatthānipissa na yathā aññesa’nti? ‘Eso kho, deva, pabbajito nāmā’ti. ‘Kiṃ paneso, samma sārathi, pabbajito nāmā’ti? ‘Eso kho, deva, pabbajito nāma sādhu dhammacariyā sādhu samacariyā [sammacariyā (ka.)] sādhu kusalakiriyā [kusalacariyā (syā.)] sādhu puññakiriyā sādhu avihiṃsā sādhu bhūtānukampā’ti. ‘Sādhu kho so, samma sārathi, pabbajito nāma, sādhu dhammacariyā sādhu samacariyā sādhu kusalakiriyā sādhu puññakiriyā sādhu avihiṃsā sādhu bhūtānukampā. Tena hi, samma sārathi, yena so pabbajito tena rathaṃ pesehī’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā yena so pabbajito tena rathaṃ pesesi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro taṃ pabbajitaṃ etadavoca – ‘tvaṃ pana, samma, kiṃkato, sīsampi te na yathā aññesaṃ, vatthānipi te na yathā aññesa’nti? ‘Ahaṃ kho, deva, pabbajito nāmā’ti. ‘Kiṃ pana tvaṃ, samma, pabbajito nāmā’ti? ‘Ahaṃ kho, deva, pabbajito nāma, sādhu dhammacariyā sādhu samacariyā sādhu kusalakiriyā sādhu puññakiriyā sādhu avihiṃsā sādhu bhūtānukampā’ti. ‘Sādhu kho tvaṃ, samma, pabbajito nāma sādhu dhammacariyā sādhu samacariyā sādhu kusalakiriyā sādhu puññakiriyā sādhu avihiṃsā sādhu bhūtānukampā’ti.

53.“Then, bhikkhus, as Prince Vipassī was going to the pleasure park, he saw a shaven-headed renunciant wearing saffron robes. Seeing him, he addressed the charioteer: ‘What kind of person is this, my dear charioteer? His head is not like others, and his clothes are not like others.’ ‘That, lord, is called a renunciant.’ ‘But why, my dear charioteer, is he called a renunciant?’ ‘That, lord, is called a renunciant because he practices the Dhamma, practices virtuous conduct, performs skillful deeds, performs meritorious deeds, is harmless, and is compassionate to all living beings.’ ‘Good indeed, my dear charioteer, is this one called a renunciant—one who practices the Dhamma, practices virtuous conduct, performs skillful deeds, performs meritorious deeds, is harmless, and is compassionate to all living beings. Then, my dear charioteer, drive the carriage to where that renunciant is.’ ‘Yes, lord,’ the charioteer, having assented to Prince Vipassī, drove the carriage to where that renunciant was. Then, bhikkhus, Prince Vipassī addressed that renunciant: ‘What kind of person are you, my dear? Your head is not like others, and your clothes are not like others.’ ‘I, lord, am called a renunciant.’ ‘But why, my dear, are you called a renunciant?’ ‘I, lord, am called a renunciant because I practice the Dhamma, practice virtuous conduct, perform skillful deeds, perform meritorious deeds, am harmless, and am compassionate to all living beings.’ ‘Good indeed, my dear, are you called a renunciant—one who practices the Dhamma, practices virtuous conduct, performs skillful deeds, performs meritorious deeds, is harmless, and is compassionate to all living beings.’

## Bodhisattapabbajjā

### The Bodhisatta's Going Forth

54. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī kumāro sārathiṃ āmantesi – ‘tena hi, samma sārathi, rathaṃ ādāya itova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāhi. Ahaṃ pana idheva kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāmī’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, sārathi vipassissa kumārassa paṭissutvā rathaṃ ādāya tatova antepuraṃ paccaniyyāsi. Vipassī pana kumāro tattheva kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji.

54."Then, monks, Prince Vipassī addressed the charioteer: ‘Now then, worthy charioteer, take the chariot and return to the inner palace from here. But I will right here shave off my hair and beard, put on ochre robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness.’ ‘Yes, lord,’ replied the charioteer to Prince Vipassī, and taking the chariot, he returned to the inner palace from there. Prince Vipassī, however, right there, shaved off his hair and beard, put on ochre robes, and went forth from the household life into homelessness.

## Mahājanakāyaanupabbajjā

### The Great Multitude's Going Forth

55. ‘‘Assosi kho, bhikkhave, bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā mahājanakāyo caturāsīti pāṇasahassāni – ‘vipassī kira kumāro kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito’ti. Sutvāna tesaṃ etadahosi – ‘na hi nūna so orako dhammavinayo, na sā orakā [orikā (sī. syā.)] pabbajjā, yattha vipassī kumāro kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito. Vipassīpi nāma kumāro kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissati, kimaṅgaṃ [kimaṅga (sī.)] pana maya’nti.

55."Monks, in the royal city of Bandhumatī, a great crowd of eighty-four thousand beings heard: 'Prince Vipassī, it seems, has shaved off his hair and beard, put on ochre robes, and gone forth from the household life into homelessness.' Having heard this, they thought: 'Surely, this is no trivial Dhamma and Discipline, no trivial going forth, since Prince Vipassī has shaved off his hair and beard, put on ochre robes, and gone forth from the household life into homelessness. If even Prince Vipassī will shave off his hair and beard, put on ochre robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness, what then of us?'

‘‘Atha kho, so bhikkhave, mahājanakāyo [mahājanakāyo (syā.)] caturāsīti pāṇasahassāni kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā vipassiṃ bodhisattaṃ agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitaṃ anupabbajiṃsu. Tāya sudaṃ, bhikkhave, parisāya parivuto vipassī bodhisatto gāmanigamajanapadarājadhānīsu cārikaṃ carati.

Then, monks, that great crowd of eighty-four thousand beings shaved off their hair and beards, put on ochre robes, and followed Vipassī, the Bodhisatta, from the household life into homelessness. Thus, monks, surrounded by that assembly, Vipassī, the Bodhisatta, wandered on tour among villages, market towns, districts, and royal capitals.

56. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘na kho metaṃ [na kho panetaṃ (syā.)] patirūpaṃ yohaṃ ākiṇṇo viharāmi, yaṃnūnāhaṃ eko gaṇamhā vūpakaṭṭho vihareyya’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bodhisatto aparena samayena eko gaṇamhā vūpakaṭṭho vihāsi, aññeneva tāni caturāsīti pabbajitasahassāni agamaṃsu, aññena maggena vipassī bodhisatto.

56."Then, monks, this thought arose in the mind of the Bodhisatta Vipassī, while he was alone and secluded: 'It is not fitting for me to live in a crowd. Suppose I were to dwell alone, withdrawn from the group.' And so, monks, after some time, the Bodhisatta Vipassī dwelled alone, withdrawn from the group. Those eighty-four thousand renunciates went one way, while the Bodhisatta Vipassī went another way.

## Bodhisattaabhiniveso

### The Bodhisatta's Resolve

57. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa vāsūpagatassa rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘kicchaṃ vatāyaṃ loko āpanno, jāyati ca jīyati ca mīyati ca [jiyyati ca miyyati ca (ka.)] cavati ca upapajjati ca, atha ca panimassa dukkhassa nissaraṇaṃ nappajānāti jarāmaraṇassa, kudāssu nāma imassa dukkhassa nissaraṇaṃ paññāyissati jarāmaraṇassā’ti?

57.Then, monks, to the Bodhisatta Vipassī, while dwelling in seclusion and solitude, this thought occurred to his mind: ‘How truly afflicted this world is! It is born, ages, dies, passes away, and reappears. Yet it does not discern an escape from this suffering, from aging and death. When, indeed, will an escape from this suffering, from aging and death, be discerned?’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati jarāmaraṇaṃ hoti, kiṃpaccayā jarāmaraṇa’nti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘jātiyā kho sati jarāmaraṇaṃ hoti, jātipaccayā jarāmaraṇa’nti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does aging and death occur? What is the condition for aging and death?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When birth exists, aging and death occur. Birth is the condition for aging and death.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati jāti hoti, kiṃpaccayā jātī’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘bhave kho sati jāti hoti, bhavapaccayā jātī’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does birth arise? What is the condition for birth?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When becoming exists, birth arises. Becoming is the condition for birth.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati bhavo hoti, kiṃpaccayā bhavo’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘upādāne kho sati bhavo hoti, upādānapaccayā bhavo’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does becoming arise? What is the condition for becoming?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When clinging exists, becoming arises. Clinging is the condition for becoming.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati upādānaṃ hoti, kiṃpaccayā upādāna’nti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘taṇhāya kho sati upādānaṃ hoti, taṇhāpaccayā upādāna’nti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does clinging arise? What is the condition for clinging?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When craving exists, clinging arises. Craving is the condition for clinging.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati taṇhā hoti, kiṃpaccayā taṇhā’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘vedanāya kho sati taṇhā hoti, vedanāpaccayā taṇhā’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does craving arise? What is the condition for craving?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When feeling exists, craving arises. Feeling is the condition for craving.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati vedanā hoti, kiṃpaccayā vedanā’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘phasse kho sati vedanā hoti, phassapaccayā vedanā’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does feeling arise? What is the condition for feeling?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When contact exists, feeling arises. Contact is the condition for feeling.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati phasso hoti, kiṃpaccayā phasso’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘saḷāyatane kho sati phasso hoti, saḷāyatanapaccayā phasso’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does contact arise? What is the condition for contact?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When the six sense bases exist, contact arises. The six sense bases are the condition for contact.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati saḷāyatanaṃ hoti, kiṃpaccayā saḷāyatana’nti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘nāmarūpe kho sati saḷāyatanaṃ hoti, nāmarūpapaccayā saḷāyatana’nti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists do the six sense bases arise? What is the condition for the six sense bases?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When name-and-form exists, the six sense bases arise. Name-and-form is the condition for the six sense bases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati nāmarūpaṃ hoti, kiṃpaccayā nāmarūpa’nti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘viññāṇe kho sati nāmarūpaṃ hoti, viññāṇapaccayā nāmarūpa’nti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does name-and-form arise? What is the condition for name-and-form?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When consciousness exists, name-and-form arises. Consciousness is the condition for name-and-form.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho sati viññāṇaṃ hoti, kiṃpaccayā viññāṇa’nti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘nāmarūpe kho sati viññāṇaṃ hoti, nāmarūpapaccayā viññāṇa’nti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what exists does consciousness arise? What is the condition for consciousness?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When name-and-form exists, consciousness arises. Name-and-form is the condition for consciousness.’

58. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘paccudāvattati kho idaṃ viññāṇaṃ nāmarūpamhā, nāparaṃ gacchati. Ettāvatā jāyetha vā jiyyetha vā miyyetha vā cavetha vā upapajjetha vā, yadidaṃ nāmarūpapaccayā viññāṇaṃ, viññāṇapaccayā nāmarūpaṃ, nāmarūpapaccayā saḷāyatanaṃ, saḷāyatanapaccayā phasso, phassapaccayā vedanā, vedanāpaccayā taṇhā, taṇhāpaccayā upādānaṃ, upādānapaccayā bhavo, bhavapaccayā jāti, jātipaccayā jarāmaraṇaṃ sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsā sambhavanti. Evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa samudayo hoti’.

58.Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘This consciousness turns back from name-and-form; it does not go further. To this extent one may be born, ages, dies, passes away, or reappears—namely: with name-and-form as condition, consciousness; with consciousness as condition, name-and-form; with name-and-form as condition, the six sense bases; with the six sense bases as condition, contact; with contact as condition, feeling; with feeling as condition, craving; with craving as condition, clinging; with clinging as condition, becoming; with becoming as condition, birth; with birth as condition, aging and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair arise. Thus is the arising of this entire mass of suffering.’

59. ‘‘‘Samudayo samudayo’ti kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa pubbe ananussutesu dhammesu cakkhuṃ udapādi, ñāṇaṃ udapādi, paññā udapādi, vijjā udapādi, āloko udapādi.

59.‘Origination, origination’—thus, monks, to the Bodhisatta Vipassī, with regard to things unheard before, vision arose, knowledge arose, wisdom arose, clear knowledge arose, light arose.

60. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati jarāmaraṇaṃ na hoti, kissa nirodhā jarāmaraṇanirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘jātiyā kho asati jarāmaraṇaṃ na hoti, jātinirodhā jarāmaraṇanirodho’ti.

60.Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does aging and death not occur? With the cessation of what does aging and death cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When birth does not exist, aging and death do not occur. With the cessation of birth, aging and death cease.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati jāti na hoti, kissa nirodhā jātinirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘bhave kho asati jāti na hoti, bhavanirodhā jātinirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does birth not occur? With the cessation of what does birth cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When becoming does not exist, birth does not occur. With the cessation of becoming, birth ceases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati bhavo na hoti, kissa nirodhā bhavanirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘upādāne kho asati bhavo na hoti, upādānanirodhā bhavanirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does becoming not occur? With the cessation of what does becoming cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When clinging does not exist, becoming does not occur. With the cessation of clinging, becoming ceases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati upādānaṃ na hoti, kissa nirodhā upādānanirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘taṇhāya kho asati upādānaṃ na hoti, taṇhānirodhā upādānanirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does clinging not occur? With the cessation of what does clinging cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When craving does not exist, clinging does not occur. With the cessation of craving, clinging ceases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati taṇhā na hoti, kissa nirodhā taṇhānirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘vedanāya kho asati taṇhā na hoti, vedanānirodhā taṇhānirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does craving not occur? With the cessation of what does craving cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When feeling does not exist, craving does not occur. With the cessation of feeling, craving ceases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati vedanā na hoti, kissa nirodhā vedanānirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘phasse kho asati vedanā na hoti, phassanirodhā vedanānirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does feeling not occur? With the cessation of what does feeling cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When contact does not exist, feeling does not occur. With the cessation of contact, feeling ceases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati phasso na hoti, kissa nirodhā phassanirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘saḷāyatane kho asati phasso na hoti, saḷāyatananirodhā phassanirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does contact not occur? With the cessation of what does contact cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When the six sense bases do not exist, contact does not occur. With the cessation of the six sense bases, contact ceases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati saḷāyatanaṃ na hoti, kissa nirodhā saḷāyatananirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘nāmarūpe kho asati saḷāyatanaṃ na hoti, nāmarūpanirodhā saḷāyatananirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist do the six sense bases not occur? With the cessation of what do the six sense bases cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When name-and-form does not exist, the six sense bases do not occur. With the cessation of name-and-form, the six sense bases cease.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati nāmarūpaṃ na hoti, kissa nirodhā nāmarūpanirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘viññāṇe kho asati nāmarūpaṃ na hoti, viññāṇanirodhā nāmarūpanirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does name-and-form not occur? With the cessation of what does name-and-form cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When consciousness does not exist, name-and-form does not occur. With the cessation of consciousness, name-and-form ceases.’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘kimhi nu kho asati viññāṇaṃ na hoti, kissa nirodhā viññāṇanirodho’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa yoniso manasikārā ahu paññāya abhisamayo – ‘nāmarūpe kho asati viññāṇaṃ na hoti, nāmarūpanirodhā viññāṇanirodho’ti.

Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘When what does not exist does consciousness not occur? With the cessation of what does consciousness cease?’ Then, monks, through wise attention, the Bodhisatta Vipassī realized with wisdom: ‘When name-and-form does not exist, consciousness does not occur. With the cessation of name-and-form, consciousness ceases.’

61. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa etadahosi – ‘adhigato kho myāyaṃ maggo sambodhāya yadidaṃ – nāmarūpanirodhā viññāṇanirodho, viññāṇanirodhā nāmarūpanirodho, nāmarūpanirodhā saḷāyatananirodho, saḷāyatananirodhā phassanirodho, phassanirodhā vedanānirodho, vedanānirodhā taṇhānirodho, taṇhānirodhā upādānanirodho, upādānanirodhā bhavanirodho, bhavanirodhā jātinirodho, jātinirodhā jarāmaraṇaṃ sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsā nirujjhanti. Evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa nirodho hoti’.

61.Then, monks, it occurred to the Bodhisatta Vipassī: ‘I have attained this path to awakening, namely: from the cessation of name-and-form comes the cessation of consciousness; from the cessation of consciousness comes the cessation of name-and-form; from the cessation of name-and-form comes the cessation of the six sense bases; from the cessation of the six sense bases comes the cessation of contact; from the cessation of contact comes the cessation of feeling; from the cessation of feeling comes the cessation of craving; from the cessation of craving comes the cessation of clinging; from the cessation of clinging comes the cessation of becoming; from the cessation of becoming comes the cessation of birth; from the cessation of birth, aging and death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair cease. Thus is the cessation of this entire mass of suffering.’

62. ‘‘‘Nirodho nirodho’ti kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bodhisattassa pubbe ananussutesu dhammesu cakkhuṃ udapādi, ñāṇaṃ udapādi, paññā udapādi, vijjā udapādi, āloko udapādi.

62.‘Cessation, cessation’—thus, monks, to the Bodhisatta Vipassī, with regard to things unheard before, vision arose, knowledge arose, wisdom arose, clear knowledge arose, light arose.

63. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bodhisatto aparena samayena pañcasu upādānakkhandhesu udayabbayānupassī vihāsi – ‘iti rūpaṃ, iti rūpassa samudayo, iti rūpassa atthaṅgamo; iti vedanā, iti vedanāya samudayo, iti vedanāya atthaṅgamo; iti saññā, iti saññāya samudayo, iti saññāya atthaṅgamo; iti saṅkhārā, iti saṅkhārānaṃ samudayo, iti saṅkhārānaṃ atthaṅgamo; iti viññāṇaṃ, iti viññāṇassa samudayo, iti viññāṇassa atthaṅgamo’ti, tassa pañcasu upādānakkhandhesu udayabbayānupassino viharato na cirasseva anupādāya āsavehi cittaṃ vimuccī’’ti.

63.“Then, monks, after some time, Vipassī the Bodhisatta dwelt contemplating the rise and fall of the five aggregates subject to clinging: ‘Such is form, such is the arising of form, such is the passing away of form; such is feeling, such is the arising of feeling, such is the passing away of feeling; such is perception, such is the arising of perception, such is the passing away of perception; such are fabrications, such is the arising of fabrications, such is the passing away of fabrications; such is consciousness, such is the arising of consciousness, such is the passing away of consciousness.’ As he dwelt contemplating the rise and fall of the five aggregates subject to clinging, before long his mind was liberated from the taints without clinging.”

## Dutiyabhāṇavāro.

### End of the second recitation.

## Brahmayācanakathā

### The Account of Brahma's Request

64. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa etadahosi – ‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ dhammaṃ deseyya’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa etadahosi – ‘adhigato kho myāyaṃ dhammo gambhīro duddaso duranubodho santo paṇīto atakkāvacaro nipuṇo paṇḍitavedanīyo. Ālayarāmā kho panāyaṃ pajā ālayaratā ālayasammuditā. Ālayarāmāya kho pana pajāya ālayaratāya ālayasammuditāya duddasaṃ idaṃ ṭhānaṃ yadidaṃ idappaccayatāpaṭiccasamuppādo. Idampi kho ṭhānaṃ duddasaṃ yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbānaṃ. Ahañceva kho pana dhammaṃ deseyyaṃ, pare ca me na ājāneyyuṃ; so mamassa kilamatho, sā mamassa vihesā’ti.

64.“Then, monks, it occurred to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: ‘Suppose I were to teach the Dhamma.’ Then, monks, it occurred to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: ‘This Dhamma that I have attained is profound, hard to see, hard to understand, peaceful, sublime, beyond the sphere of reasoning, subtle, to be experienced by the wise. But this generation finds delight in attachment, is devoted to attachment, rejoices in attachment. For a generation finding delight in attachment, devoted to attachment, rejoicing in attachment, this state is hard to see, namely, this-conditionality, dependent origination. And this state too is hard to see, namely, the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all acquisitions, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna. If I were to teach the Dhamma and others would not understand me, that would be wearisome and troublesome for me.’”

65. ‘‘Apissu, bhikkhave, vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ imā anacchariyā gāthāyo paṭibhaṃsu pubbe assutapubbā –

65.“And then, monks, these astonishing verses, previously unheard, spontaneously occurred to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One:”

‘Kicchena me adhigataṃ, halaṃ dāni pakāsituṃ;Rāgadosaparetehi, nāyaṃ dhammo susambudho.

‘This Dhamma was attained by me with difficulty.Enough now of proclaiming it.This Dhamma is not easily understoodBy those overcome with lust and hate.’

‘Paṭisotagāmiṃ nipuṇaṃ, gambhīraṃ duddasaṃ aṇuṃ;Rāgarattā na dakkhanti, tamokhandhena āvuṭā’ti.

‘Going against the stream, subtle, deep, hard to see, refined;those stained with passion do not see, shrouded in a mass of darkness.’

‘‘Itiha, bhikkhave, vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa paṭisañcikkhato appossukkatāya cittaṃ nami, no dhammadesanāya.

“Thus, monks, as the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One reflected, his mind inclined to disinclination, not to teaching the Dhamma.”

66. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, aññatarassa mahābrahmuno vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya etadahosi – ‘nassati vata bho loko, vinassati vata bho loko, yatra hi nāma vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa appossukkatāya cittaṃ namati [nami (syā. ka.), namissati (?)], no dhammadesanāyā’ti. Atha kho so, bhikkhave, mahābrahmā seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya; evameva brahmaloke antarahito vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa purato pāturahosi. Atha kho so, bhikkhave, mahābrahmā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā dakkhiṇaṃ jāṇumaṇḍalaṃ pathaviyaṃ nihantvā [nidahanto (syā.)] yena vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘desetu, bhante, bhagavā dhammaṃ, desetu sugato dhammaṃ, santi [santī (syā.)] sattā apparajakkhajātikā; assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti, bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro’ti.

66.“Then, monks, a certain Great Brahmā, having understood with his mind the thought in the mind of the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, thought: ‘Alas, the world is perishing, alas, the world is being destroyed! For the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, his mind inclines to lack of concern, not to teaching the Dhamma.’ Then, monks, just as a strong man might extend his bent arm or bend his extended arm, that Great Brahmā disappeared from the Brahmā world and appeared before the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One. Then, monks, that Great Brahmā, arranging his upper robe over one shoulder, placing his right knee on the ground, joined his hands in supplication to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, and said to him: ‘Venerable sir, let the Blessed One teach the Dhamma; let the Well-gone One teach the Dhamma. There are beings with little dust in their eyes; they fall away through not hearing the Dhamma. There will be those who will understand the Dhamma.’”

67. ‘‘Evaṃ vutte [atha kho (ka.)], bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho taṃ mahābrahmānaṃ etadavoca – ‘mayhampi kho, brahme, etadahosi – ‘‘yaṃnūnāhaṃ dhammaṃ deseyya’’nti. Tassa mayhaṃ, brahme, etadahosi – ‘‘adhigato kho myāyaṃ dhammo gambhīro duddaso duranubodho santo paṇīto atakkāvacaro nipuṇo paṇḍitavedanīyo. Ālayarāmā kho panāyaṃ pajā ālayaratā ālayasammuditā. Ālayarāmāya kho pana pajāya ālayaratāya ālayasammuditāya duddasaṃ idaṃ ṭhānaṃ yadidaṃ idappaccayatāpaṭiccasamuppādo. Idampi kho ṭhānaṃ duddasaṃ yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbānaṃ. Ahañceva kho pana dhammaṃ deseyyaṃ, pare ca me na ājāneyyuṃ; so mamassa kilamatho, sā mamassa vihesā’’ti. Apissu maṃ, brahme, imā anacchariyā gāthāyo paṭibhaṃsu pubbe assutapubbā –

67.“When this was said, monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, said to that Great Brahmā: ‘It occurred to me too, Brahmā: “Suppose I were to teach the Dhamma.” But then, Brahmā, it occurred to me: “This Dhamma I have attained is profound, hard to see, hard to understand, peaceful, sublime, beyond the sphere of reasoning, subtle, to be experienced by the wise. But this generation delights in attachment, takes pleasure in attachment, rejoices in attachment. For a generation delighting in attachment, taking pleasure in attachment, rejoicing in attachment, this state is hard to see: that is, specific conditionality, dependent origination. And this state is also hard to see: the stilling of all formations, the relinquishing of all acquisitions, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna. If I were to teach the Dhamma and others did not understand me, that would be wearying and troublesome for me.” And then, Brahmā, these astonishing verses, never heard before, spontaneously occurred to me:

‘‘Kicchena me adhigataṃ, halaṃ dāni pakāsituṃ;Rāgadosaparetehi, nāyaṃ dhammo susambudho.

“With difficulty I found it, now why proclaim it?This Dhamma is not easily understood by those overcome by lust and hate.”

‘‘Paṭisotagāmiṃ nipuṇaṃ, gambhīraṃ duddasaṃ aṇuṃ;Rāgarattā na dakkhanti, tamokhandhena āvuṭā’’ti.

“Those dyed in lust, enveloped by a mass of darkness,Will not see this Dhamma,Which goes against the stream, is subtle,Profound, hard to see, and delicate.”

‘Itiha me, brahme, paṭisañcikkhato appossukkatāya cittaṃ nami, no dhammadesanāyā’ti.

‘Thus, Brahmā, reflecting in this way, my mind inclined to disinclination rather than to teaching the Dhamma.’

68. ‘‘Dutiyampi kho, bhikkhave, so mahābrahmā…pe… tatiyampi kho, bhikkhave, so mahābrahmā vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘desetu, bhante, bhagavā dhammaṃ, desetu sugato dhammaṃ, santi sattā apparajakkhajātikā, assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti, bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro’ti.

68.“A second time, monks, that Great Brahmā… A third time, monks, that Great Brahmā said this to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: ‘Let the Blessed One teach the Dhamma, let the Well-gone One teach the Dhamma. There are beings with little dust in their eyes who are declining through not hearing the Dhamma. There will be those who will understand the Dhamma.’”

69. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho brahmuno ca ajjhesanaṃ viditvā sattesu ca kāruññataṃ paṭicca buddhacakkhunā lokaṃ volokesi. Addasā kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho buddhacakkhunā lokaṃ volokento satte apparajakkhe mahārajakkhe tikkhindriye mudindriye svākāre dvākāre suviññāpaye duviññāpaye [duviññāpaye bhabbe abhabbe (syā.)] appekacce paralokavajjabhayadassāvine [dassāvino (sī. syā. kaṃ. ka.)] viharante, appekacce na paralokavajjabhayadassāvine [dassāvino (sī. syā. kaṃ. ka.)] viharante. Seyyathāpi nāma uppaliniyaṃ vā paduminiyaṃ vā puṇḍarīkiniyaṃ vā appekaccāni uppalāni vā padumāni vā puṇḍarīkāni vā udake jātāni udake saṃvaḍḍhāni udakānuggatāni anto nimuggaposīni. Appekaccāni uppalāni vā padumāni vā puṇḍarīkāni vā udake jātāni udake saṃvaḍḍhāni samodakaṃ ṭhitāni. Appekaccāni uppalāni vā padumāni vā puṇḍarīkāni vā udake jātāni udake saṃvaḍḍhāni udakā accuggamma ṭhitāni anupalittāni udakena. Evameva kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho buddhacakkhunā lokaṃ volokento addasa satte apparajakkhe mahārajakkhe tikkhindriye mudindriye svākāre dvākāre suviññāpaye duviññāpaye appekacce paralokavajjabhayadassāvine viharante, appekacce na paralokavajjabhayadassāvine viharante.

69.“Then, monks, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, Vipassī, having understood Brahmā’s request and being moved by compassion for beings, surveyed the world with the Buddha-eye. Monks, as the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, Vipassī, surveyed the world with the Buddha-eye, he saw beings—with little defilement and with great defilement, with keen faculties and with dull faculties, well-disposed and ill-disposed, easy to instruct and hard to instruct, some dwelling having fear of the other world and of wrongdoing, and others not dwelling having fear of the other world and of wrongdoing. Just as in a pond of blue lotuses, or red lotuses, or white lotuses, some blue lotuses, red lotuses, or white lotuses, born in the water, grown in the water, submerged in the water, are nourished by the water. Some blue lotuses, red lotuses, or white lotuses, born in the water, grown in the water, stand at the water’s surface. Some blue lotuses, red lotuses, or white lotuses, born in the water, grown in the water, rise above the water, remaining unsullied by the water. In the same way, monks, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, Vipassī, surveying the world with the Buddha-eye, saw beings—with little defilement and with great defilement, with keen faculties and with dull faculties, well-disposed and ill-disposed, easy to instruct and hard to instruct, some dwelling having fear of the other world and of wrongdoing, and others not dwelling having fear of the other world and of wrongdoing.”

70. ‘‘Atha kho so, bhikkhave, mahābrahmā vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

70.“Then, monks, the Great Brahmā, having understood the Blessed One Vipassī’s, the Arahant’s, the Perfectly Enlightened One’s, mental activity by his own mind, addressed the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, with verses:

‘Sele yathā pabbatamuddhaniṭṭhito, yathāpi passe janataṃ samantato;Tathūpamaṃ dhammamayaṃ sumedha, pāsādamāruyha samantacakkhu.

‘As one standing on a rocky peak sees people all around,so too, O wise one, having ascended the palace of Dhamma, the all-seeing one, behold the people.’

‘Sokāvatiṇṇaṃ [sokāvakiṇṇaṃ (syā.)] janatamapetasoko,Avekkhassu jātijarābhibhūtaṃ;Uṭṭhehi vīra vijitasaṅgāma,Satthavāha aṇaṇa vicara loke.Desassu [desetu (syā. pī.)] bhagavā dhammaṃ,Aññātāro bhavissantī’ti.

‘Behold the people immersed in sorrow, you yourself free from sorrow;behold those overcome by birth and aging.Arise, O hero, victorious in battle,O caravan leader, wander the world without debt.Teach the Dhamma, O Blessed One;there will be those who understand.’

71. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho taṃ mahābrahmānaṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

71.“Then, monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, addressed that Great Brahmā with a verse:

‘Apārutā tesaṃ amatassa dvārā,Ye sotavanto pamuñcantu saddhaṃ;Vihiṃsasaññī paguṇaṃ na bhāsiṃ,Dhammaṃ paṇītaṃ manujesu brahme’ti.

‘Open for them are the doors to the Deathless;let those with ears give heed with faith.Fearing hardship, I did not teach the excellent and beneficial Dhamma among humankind, O Brahmā.’

‘‘Atha kho so, bhikkhave, mahābrahmā ‘katāvakāso khomhi vipassinā bhagavatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena dhammadesanāyā’ti vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tattheva antaradhāyi.

“Then, monks, that Great Brahmā thought: ‘An opportunity has been made for me by the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, for the teaching of the Dhamma.’ Having paid homage to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, he circumambulated Him and right there vanished.”

## Aggasāvakayugaṃ

### The foremost pair of disciples.

72. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa etadahosi – ‘kassa nu kho ahaṃ paṭhamaṃ dhammaṃ deseyyaṃ, ko imaṃ dhammaṃ khippameva ājānissatī’ti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho khaṇḍo ca rājaputto tisso ca purohitaputto bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā paṭivasanti paṇḍitā viyattā medhāvino dīgharattaṃ apparajakkhajātikā. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ khaṇḍassa ca rājaputtassa, tissassa ca purohitaputtassa paṭhamaṃ dhammaṃ deseyyaṃ, te imaṃ dhammaṃ khippameva ājānissantī’ti.

72."Then, monks, it occurred to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: 'To whom should I first teach the Dhamma? Who will quickly understand this Dhamma?' Then, monks, it occurred to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: 'Prince Khaṇḍa and Tissa, the brahmin youth, reside in the royal city of Bandhumatī. They are wise, accomplished, intelligent, and have for a long time been pure of vision. Why don’t I first teach the Dhamma to Prince Khaṇḍa and Tissa, the brahmin youth? They will quickly understand this Dhamma.'"

73. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya; evameva bodhirukkhamūle antarahito bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā kheme migadāye pāturahosi. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho dāyapālaṃ [migadāyapālaṃ (syā.)] āmantesi – ‘ehi tvaṃ, samma dāyapāla, bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ pavisitvā khaṇḍañca rājaputtaṃ tissañca purohitaputtaṃ evaṃ vadehi – vipassī, bhante, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ anuppatto kheme migadāye viharati, so tumhākaṃ dassanakāmo’ti. ‘Evaṃ, bhante’ti kho, bhikkhave, dāyapālo vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa paṭissutvā bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ pavisitvā khaṇḍañca rājaputtaṃ tissañca purohitaputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘vipassī, bhante, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ anuppatto kheme migadāye viharati; so tumhākaṃ dassanakāmo’ti.

73."Then, monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One—just as a strong man might extend his bent arm or bend his extended arm—vanished from the foot of the Bodhi tree and appeared in the Khema Deer Park in the royal city of Bandhumatī. Then, monks, the Blessed One Vipassī addressed the park keeper: 'Come, good park keeper, enter the royal city of Bandhumatī and say to Prince Khaṇḍa and the brahmin youth Tissa: "Venerable sir, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, has arrived at Bandhumatī and is staying in the Khema Deer Park. He wishes to see you."' 'Yes, venerable sir,' replied the park keeper to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One. Then he entered the royal city of Bandhumatī and said to Prince Khaṇḍa and the brahmin youth Tissa: 'Venerable sir, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, has arrived at Bandhumatī and is staying in the Khema Deer Park. He wishes to see you.'"

74. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, khaṇḍo ca rājaputto tisso ca purohitaputto bhaddāni bhaddāni yānāni yojāpetvā bhaddaṃ bhaddaṃ yānaṃ abhiruhitvā bhaddehi bhaddehi yānehi bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā niyyiṃsu. Yena khemo migadāyo tena pāyiṃsu. Yāvatikā yānassa bhūmi, yānena gantvā yānā paccorohitvā pattikāva [padikāva (syā.)] yena vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho tenupasaṅkamiṃsu. Upasaṅkamitvā vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu.

74."Then, monks, Prince Khaṇḍa and Tissa the brahmin youth had excellent carriages harnessed, mounted excellent carriages, and set out from the royal city of Bandhumatī in those excellent carriages. They headed toward Khema Deer Park. As far as the ground was suitable for carriages, they went by carriage; then, dismounting from the carriages, they proceeded on foot to where the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, and sat down to one side."

75. ‘‘Tesaṃ vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho anupubbiṃ kathaṃ [ānupubbikathaṃ (sī. pī.)] kathesi, seyyathidaṃ – dānakathaṃ sīlakathaṃ saggakathaṃ kāmānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Yadā te bhagavā aññāsi kallacitte muducitte vinīvaraṇacitte udaggacitte pasannacitte, atha yā buddhānaṃ sāmukkaṃsikā dhammadesanā, taṃ pakāsesi – dukkhaṃ samudayaṃ nirodhaṃ maggaṃ. Seyyathāpi nāma suddhaṃ vatthaṃ apagatakāḷakaṃ sammadeva rajanaṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, evameva khaṇḍassa ca rājaputtassa tissassa ca purohitaputtassa tasmiṃyeva āsane virajaṃ vītamalaṃ dhammacakkhuṃ udapādi – ‘yaṃ kiñci samudayadhammaṃ, sabbaṃ taṃ nirodhadhamma’nti.

75."The Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, gradually gave a talk, that is to say, a talk on giving, a talk on virtue, a talk on heaven, the danger, worthlessness, and defilement of sensual pleasures, and the benefit of renunciation. When the Blessed One knew that their minds were receptive, gentle, devoid of hindrances, uplifted, and serene, then he revealed the teaching special to the Buddhas: suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Just as a clean cloth, free from stain, would properly absorb dye, just so, while sitting in those very seats, the stainless vision of the Dhamma, free from dust, arose in Prince Khaṇḍa and the brahmin youth Tissa: ‘Whatever is of the nature to arise is all of the nature to cease.’"

76. ‘‘Te diṭṭhadhammā pattadhammā viditadhammā pariyogāḷhadhammā tiṇṇavicikicchā vigatakathaṃkathā vesārajjappattā aparappaccayā satthusāsane vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘abhikkantaṃ, bhante, abhikkantaṃ, bhante. Seyyathāpi, bhante, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya ‘‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’’ti. Evamevaṃ bhagavatā anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Ete mayaṃ, bhante, bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāma dhammañca. Labheyyāma mayaṃ, bhante, bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ, labheyyāma upasampada’nti.

76."Having seen the Dhamma, attained the Dhamma, known the Dhamma, plunged into the Dhamma, crossed over doubt, dispelled uncertainty, attained to confidence, and become independent of others in the Teacher’s dispensation, they said to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: ‘Excellent, venerable sir! Excellent, venerable sir! Just as, venerable sir, one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the way to one who was lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness, so that those with eyes might see forms—in the same way the Blessed One has made the Dhamma clear in many ways. We go for refuge to the Blessed One, venerable sir, and to the Dhamma. May we receive the going forth in the Blessed One’s presence, venerable sir; may we receive ordination.’"

77. ‘‘Alatthuṃ kho, bhikkhave, khaṇḍo ca rājaputto, tisso ca purohitaputto vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa santike pabbajjaṃ alatthuṃ upasampadaṃ. Te vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi; saṅkhārānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ nibbāne [nekkhamme (syā.)] ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Tesaṃ vipassinā bhagavatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena dhammiyā kathāya sandassiyamānānaṃ samādapiyamānānaṃ samuttejiyamānānaṃ sampahaṃsiyamānānaṃ nacirasseva anupādāya āsavehi cittāni vimucciṃsu.

77."Indeed, monks, Prince Khaṇḍa and Tissa, the chaplain’s son, received the going forth and the higher ordination in the presence of the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One. The Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, guided, established, exhorted, and gladdened them with a Dhamma talk, revealing the drawbacks, inferiority, and defilement of conditioned phenomena and the benefits of Nibbāna. As they were being guided, established, exhorted, and gladdened by the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, with a Dhamma talk, before long their minds were liberated from the taints without clinging."

## Mahājanakāyapabbajjā

### The Going Forth of the Multitude

78. ‘‘Assosi kho, bhikkhave, bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā mahājanakāyo caturāsītipāṇasahassāni – ‘vipassī kira bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ anuppatto kheme migadāye viharati. Khaṇḍo ca kira rājaputto tisso ca purohitaputto vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa santike kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitā’ti. Sutvāna nesaṃ etadahosi – ‘na hi nūna so orako dhammavinayo, na sā orakā pabbajjā, yattha khaṇḍo ca rājaputto tisso ca purohitaputto kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitā. Khaṇḍo ca rājaputto tisso ca purohitaputto kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissanti, kimaṅgaṃ pana maya’nti. Atha kho so, bhikkhave, mahājanakāyo caturāsītipāṇasahassāni bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā nikkhamitvā yena khemo migadāyo yena vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu.

78.Monks, the great multitude in the royal city of Bandhumatī, eighty-four thousand living beings, heard: ‘The Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, has arrived at the royal city of Bandhumatī and is staying in the Khema Deer Park. Prince Khaṇḍa and Tissa the chaplain’s son have shaved off their hair and beards, put on the ochre robes, and gone forth from the householder’s life into homelessness in the presence of the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One.’ Hearing this, they thought: ‘Surely this is no trivial Dhamma and Discipline, no trivial going forth, where Prince Khaṇḍa and Tissa the chaplain’s son have shaved off their hair and beards, put on the ochre robes, and gone forth from the householder’s life into homelessness. If Prince Khaṇḍa and Tissa the chaplain’s son are going forth, why not us?’ Then, monks, that great multitude of eighty-four thousand living beings left Bandhumatī and went to the Khema Deer Park, where the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was staying. Approaching the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, they paid homage and sat down to one side.

79. ‘‘Tesaṃ vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho anupubbiṃ kathaṃ kathesi. Seyyathidaṃ – dānakathaṃ sīlakathaṃ saggakathaṃ kāmānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Yadā te bhagavā aññāsi kallacitte muducitte vinīvaraṇacitte udaggacitte pasannacitte, atha yā buddhānaṃ sāmukkaṃsikā dhammadesanā, taṃ pakāsesi – dukkhaṃ samudayaṃ nirodhaṃ maggaṃ. Seyyathāpi nāma suddhaṃ vatthaṃ apagatakāḷakaṃ sammadeva rajanaṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, evameva tesaṃ caturāsītipāṇasahassānaṃ tasmiṃyeva āsane virajaṃ vītamalaṃ dhammacakkhuṃ udapādi – ‘yaṃ kiñci samudayadhammaṃ sabbaṃ taṃ nirodhadhamma’nti.

79.Then the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, gave them a graduated discourse—on generosity, on virtue, on the heavens; on the danger, the drawback, and the defilement of sensual pleasures; and on the benefits of renunciation. When the Blessed One knew that their minds were ready, pliable, free from hindrances, uplifted, and confident, he revealed the teaching special to the Buddhas: suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Just as a clean cloth, free from stains, would properly absorb dye, so too in those eighty-four thousand beings, right there in their seats, the dust-free, stainless vision of the Dhamma arose: ‘Whatever is subject to arising is all subject to cessation.’

80. ‘‘Te diṭṭhadhammā pattadhammā viditadhammā pariyogāḷhadhammā tiṇṇavicikicchā vigatakathaṃkathā vesārajjappattā aparappaccayā satthusāsane vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘abhikkantaṃ, bhante, abhikkantaṃ, bhante. Seyyathāpi, bhante, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya ‘‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’’ti. Evamevaṃ bhagavatā anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Ete mayaṃ, bhante, bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāma dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca [( ) natthi aṭṭhakathāyaṃ, pāḷiyaṃ pana sabbatthapi dissati]. Labheyyāma mayaṃ, bhante, bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ labheyyāma upasampada’’nti.

80.Having seen the Dhamma, attained the Dhamma, known the Dhamma, fathomed the Dhamma, having crossed over doubt, being without perplexity, having attained intrepidity, being independent of others in the Teacher's Dispensation, they said this to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: 'Excellent, venerable sir! Excellent, venerable sir! Just as, venerable sir, one might set upright what was overturned, or reveal what was covered, or point out the way to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the dark, thinking, "Those with eyes will see forms," just so has the Dhamma been made clear in many ways by the Blessed One. We go for refuge to the Blessed One and to the Dhamma. May we receive the going forth in the Blessed One’s presence, may we receive the full ordination!'

81. ‘‘Alatthuṃ kho, bhikkhave, tāni caturāsītipāṇasahassāni vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa santike pabbajjaṃ, alatthuṃ upasampadaṃ. Te vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi; saṅkhārānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ nibbāne ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Tesaṃ vipassinā bhagavatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena dhammiyā kathāya sandassiyamānānaṃ samādapiyamānānaṃ samuttejiyamānānaṃ sampahaṃsiyamānānaṃ nacirasseva anupādāya āsavehi cittāni vimucciṃsu.

81.Indeed, monks, those eighty-four thousand beings received the going forth in the presence of the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, and received the full ordination by the ‘Come, Bhikkhu’ formula. The Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, instructed, inspired, fired them up, and gladdened them with a Dhamma talk. He revealed the danger, worthlessness, and defilement of conditioned things and the benefit of Nibbāna. As those beings were being instructed, inspired, fired up, and gladdened by the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, with a Dhamma talk, before long their minds were liberated from the taints through non-clinging.

## Purimapabbajitānaṃ dhammābhisamayo

### The Realization of the Dhamma by Former Renunciates

82. ‘‘Assosuṃ kho, bhikkhave, tāni purimāni caturāsītipabbajitasahassāni – ‘vipassī kira bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ anuppatto kheme migadāye viharati, dhammañca kira desetī’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, tāni caturāsītipabbajitasahassāni yena bandhumatī rājadhānī yena khemo migadāyo yena vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu.

82.“Monks, those eighty-four thousand former renunciates heard: ‘Vipassī, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, has arrived at the royal city of Bandhumatī and is dwelling in the Khema Deer Park, and is teaching the Dhamma.’ Then, monks, those eighty-four thousand renunciates approached the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, in the Khema Deer Park at Bandhumatī, the royal city; having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, and sat down to one side.

83. ‘‘Tesaṃ vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho anupubbiṃ kathaṃ kathesi. Seyyathidaṃ – dānakathaṃ sīlakathaṃ saggakathaṃ kāmānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Yadā te bhagavā aññāsi kallacitte muducitte vinīvaraṇacitte udaggacitte pasannacitte, atha yā buddhānaṃ sāmukkaṃsikā dhammadesanā, taṃ pakāsesi – dukkhaṃ samudayaṃ nirodhaṃ maggaṃ. Seyyathāpi nāma suddhaṃ vatthaṃ apagatakāḷakaṃ sammadeva rajanaṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, evameva tesaṃ caturāsītipabbajitasahassānaṃ tasmiṃyeva āsane virajaṃ vītamalaṃ dhammacakkhuṃ udapādi – ‘yaṃ kiñci samudayadhammaṃ sabbaṃ taṃ nirodhadhamma’nti.

83.The Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, gave them a graduated discourse, namely, discourse on generosity, discourse on virtue, discourse on heaven; he revealed the danger, the degradation, and the defilement of sensual pleasures and the benefit of renunciation. When the Blessed One knew that their minds were receptive, tender, free from hindrances, uplifted, and serene, he revealed the teaching distinctive to the Buddhas: suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Just as a clean cloth, free from stains, would perfectly receive dye, so too, while those eighty-four thousand renunciates were sitting right there, the dust-free, stainless vision of the Dhamma arose in them: ‘Whatever is subject to arising is all subject to cessation.’

84. ‘‘Te diṭṭhadhammā pattadhammā viditadhammā pariyogāḷhadhammā tiṇṇavicikicchā vigatakathaṃkathā vesārajjappattā aparappaccayā satthusāsane vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘abhikkantaṃ, bhante, abhikkantaṃ, bhante. Seyyathāpi, bhante, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya ‘‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’’ti. Evamevaṃ bhagavatā anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Ete mayaṃ, bhante, bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāma dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Labheyyāma mayaṃ, bhante, bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ labheyyāma upasampada’’nti.

84.Having seen the Dhamma, attained the Dhamma, known the Dhamma, penetrated the Dhamma, having overcome doubt, dispelled perplexity, having attained fearlessness, and being unreliant on others in the Teacher’s dispensation, they said this to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One: ‘Venerable sir, it is excellent! Venerable sir, it is excellent! Just as, venerable sir, one might set upright what has been overturned, or reveal what is hidden, or show the way to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the dark so that those with eyes may see forms—in the same way, venerable sir, has the Blessed One expounded the Dhamma by various means. Venerable sir, we go for refuge to the Blessed One, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of monks. May we receive the going forth into the presence of the Blessed One, may we receive ordination!’

85. ‘‘Alatthuṃ kho, bhikkhave, tāni caturāsītipabbajitasahassāni vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa santike pabbajjaṃ alatthuṃ upasampadaṃ. Te vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṃsesi; saṅkhārānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ nibbāne ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Tesaṃ vipassinā bhagavatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena dhammiyā kathāya sandassiyamānānaṃ samādapiyamānānaṃ samuttejiyamānānaṃ sampahaṃsiyamānānaṃ nacirasseva anupādāya āsavehi cittāni vimucciṃsu.

85.Indeed, monks, those eighty-four thousand renunciates gained the going forth and the full ordination in the presence of Vipassī, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One. Vipassī, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, instructed, encouraged, exhorted, and gladdened them with a discourse on the Dhamma; he revealed the danger, the degradation, and the defilement of formations, and the benefit of Nibbāna. As they were being instructed, encouraged, exhorted, and gladdened by Vipassī, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, with a discourse on the Dhamma, before long their minds were liberated from the effluents, without clinging.

## Cārikāanujānanaṃ

### The allowance of wandering.

86. ‘‘Tena kho pana, bhikkhave, samayena bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā mahābhikkhusaṅgho paṭivasati aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ. Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘mahā kho etarahi bhikkhusaṅgho bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā paṭivasati aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ, yaṃnūnāhaṃ bhikkhū anujāneyyaṃ – ‘caratha, bhikkhave, cārikaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ; mā ekena dve agamittha; desetha, bhikkhave, dhammaṃ ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsetha. Santi sattā apparajakkhajātikā, assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti, bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro. Api ca channaṃ channaṃ vassānaṃ accayena bandhumatī rājadhānī upasaṅkamitabbā pātimokkhuddesāyā’’’ti.

86."At that time, monks, in the royal city of Bandhumatī, a great community of monks was residing—six million eight hundred thousand monks. Then, monks, while the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was alone in seclusion, this thought arose in his mind: ‘Now, a great community of monks is residing in the royal city of Bandhumatī—six million eight hundred thousand monks. Suppose I were to permit the monks: "Wander forth, monks, for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans. Let not two go by the same path. Teach, monks, the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and wording. Proclaim the holy life, utterly complete and pure. There are beings with little dust in their eyes who are declining through not hearing the Dhamma; there will be those who will understand the Dhamma. Moreover, after every six years, the royal city of Bandhumatī should be visited for the recitation of the Pātimokkha."'"

87. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, aññataro mahābrahmā vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya. Evameva brahmaloke antarahito vipassissa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa purato pāturahosi. Atha kho so, bhikkhave, mahābrahmā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā yena vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ etadavoca – ‘evametaṃ, bhagavā, evametaṃ, sugata. Mahā kho, bhante, etarahi bhikkhusaṅgho bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā paṭivasati aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ, anujānātu, bhante, bhagavā bhikkhū – ‘‘caratha, bhikkhave, cārikaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ; mā ekena dve agamittha; desetha, bhikkhave, dhammaṃ ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsetha. Santi sattā apparajakkhajātikā, assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti, bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro’’ti [aññātāro (ssabbattha)]. Api ca, bhante, mayaṃ tathā karissāma yathā bhikkhū channaṃ channaṃ vassānaṃ accayena bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ upasaṅkamissanti pātimokkhuddesāyā’ti. Idamavoca, bhikkhave, so mahābrahmā, idaṃ vatvā vipassiṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddhaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tattheva antaradhāyi.

87."Then, monks, a certain Great Brahma, having known with his mind the thought in the mind of the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, just as a strong man might stretch out his bent arm or bend his outstretched arm, so did he vanish from the Brahma-world and appeared before the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One. Then, monks, that Great Brahma, arranging his upper robe over one shoulder, raised his joined palms in salutation towards the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, and said: 'So it is, Blessed One, so it is, Sugata. At present, venerable sir, a great community of monks resides in the royal city of Bandhumatī—six million eight hundred thousand monks. Let the Blessed One, venerable sir, permit the monks: "Wander forth, monks, for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans. Let not two go by the same path. Teach, monks, the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and wording. Proclaim the holy life, utterly complete and pure. There are beings with little dust in their eyes who are declining through not hearing the Dhamma; there will be those who will understand the Dhamma." Moreover, venerable sir, we will act in such a way that the monks will come to the royal city of Bandhumatī every six years for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.' Having said this, monks, that Great Brahma paid homage to the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, circumambulated him, and vanished right there.

88. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘idha mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – mahā kho etarahi bhikkhusaṅgho bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā paṭivasati aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ bhikkhū anujāneyyaṃ – ‘caratha, bhikkhave, cārikaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ; mā ekena dve agamittha; desetha, bhikkhave, dhammaṃ ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsetha. Santi sattā apparajakkhajātikā, assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti, bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro. Api ca, channaṃ channaṃ vassānaṃ accayena bandhumatī rājadhānī upasaṅkamitabbā pātimokkhuddesāyāti.

88."Then, monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, having emerged from seclusion in the evening, addressed the monks: 'Here, monks, while I was alone in seclusion, this thought arose in my mind: "At present, a great community of monks resides in the royal city of Bandhumatī—six million eight hundred thousand monks. Suppose I were to permit the monks: 'Wander forth, monks, for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans. Let not two go by the same path. Teach, monks, the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and wording. Proclaim the holy life, utterly complete and pure. There are beings with little dust in their eyes who are declining through not hearing the Dhamma; there will be those who will understand the Dhamma.' Moreover, after every six years, the royal city of Bandhumatī should be visited for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.'"

‘‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, aññataro mahābrahmā mama cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evameva brahmaloke antarahito mama purato pāturahosi. Atha kho so, bhikkhave, mahābrahmā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā yenāhaṃ tenañjaliṃ paṇāmetvā maṃ etadavoca – ‘‘evametaṃ, bhagavā, evametaṃ, sugata. Mahā kho, bhante, etarahi bhikkhusaṅgho bandhumatiyā rājadhāniyā paṭivasati aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ. Anujānātu, bhante, bhagavā bhikkhū – ‘caratha, bhikkhave, cārikaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ; mā ekena dve agamittha; desetha, bhikkhave, dhammaṃ…pe… santi sattā apparajakkhajātikā, assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti, bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro’ti. Api ca, bhante, mayaṃ tathā karissāma, yathā bhikkhū channaṃ channaṃ vassānaṃ accayena bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ upasaṅkamissanti pātimokkhuddesāyā’’ti. Idamavoca, bhikkhave, so mahābrahmā, idaṃ vatvā maṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tattheva antaradhāyi’.

"Then, monks, a certain Great Brahma, having known with his mind the thought in my mind, just as a strong man might stretch out his bent arm or bend his outstretched arm, so did he vanish from the Brahma-world and appeared before me. Then, monks, that Great Brahma, arranging his upper robe over one shoulder, raised his joined palms in salutation towards me, and said: 'So it is, Blessed One, so it is, Sugata. At present, venerable sir, a great community of monks resides in the royal city of Bandhumatī—six million eight hundred thousand monks. Let the Blessed One, venerable sir, permit the monks: "Wander forth, monks, for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans. Let not two go by the same path. Teach, monks, the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and wording. Proclaim the holy life, utterly complete and pure. There are beings with little dust in their eyes who are declining through not hearing the Dhamma; there will be those who will understand the Dhamma." Moreover, venerable sir, we will act in such a way that the monks will come to the royal city of Bandhumatī every six years for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.' Having said this, monks, that Great Brahma paid homage to me, circumambulated me, and vanished right there.

‘‘‘Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, caratha cārikaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ; mā ekena dve agamittha; desetha, bhikkhave, dhammaṃ ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsetha. Santi sattā apparajakkhajātikā, assavanatā dhammassa parihāyanti, bhavissanti dhammassa aññātāro. Api ca, bhikkhave, channaṃ channaṃ vassānaṃ accayena bandhumatī rājadhānī upasaṅkamitabbā pātimokkhuddesāyā’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhū yebhuyyena ekāheneva janapadacārikaṃ pakkamiṃsu.

"I permit, monks: Wander forth for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans. Let not two go by the same path. Teach, monks, the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and wording. Proclaim the holy life, utterly complete and pure. There are beings with little dust in their eyes who are declining through not hearing the Dhamma; there will be those who will understand the Dhamma. Moreover, monks, after every six years, the royal city of Bandhumatī should be visited for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.' Then, monks, the monks for the most part departed on their wanderings through the country in a single day.

89. ‘‘Tena kho pana samayena jambudīpe caturāsīti āvāsasahassāni honti. Ekamhi hi vasse nikkhante devatā saddamanussāvesuṃ – ‘nikkhantaṃ kho, mārisā, ekaṃ vassaṃ; pañca dāni vassāni sesāni; pañcannaṃ vassānaṃ accayena bandhumatī rājadhānī upasaṅkamitabbā pātimokkhuddesāyā’ti. Dvīsu vassesu nikkhantesu… tīsu vassesu nikkhantesu… catūsu vassesu nikkhantesu… pañcasu vassesu nikkhantesu devatā saddamanussāvesuṃ – ‘nikkhantāni kho, mārisā, pañcavassāni; ekaṃ dāni vassaṃ sesaṃ; ekassa vassassa accayena bandhumatī rājadhānī upasaṅkamitabbā pātimokkhuddesāyā’ti. Chasu vassesu nikkhantesu devatā saddamanussāvesuṃ – ‘nikkhantāni kho, mārisā, chabbassāni, samayo dāni bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ upasaṅkamituṃ pātimokkhuddesāyā’ti. Atha kho te, bhikkhave, bhikkhū appekacce sakena iddhānubhāvena appekacce devatānaṃ iddhānubhāvena ekāheneva bandhumatiṃ rājadhāniṃ upasaṅkamiṃsu pātimokkhuddesāyāti [pātimokkhuddesāya (?)].

89."At that time, there were eighty-four thousand monasteries in Jambudīpa. When one year had passed, the deities announced: 'Venerable ones, one year has passed; now only five years remain; after five years the royal city of Bandhumatī should be visited for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.' When two years had passed… three years had passed… four years had passed… five years had passed, the deities announced: 'Venerable ones, five years have passed; now only one year remains; after one year the royal city of Bandhumatī should be visited for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.' When six years had passed, the deities announced: 'Venerable ones, six years have passed; now is the time to visit the royal city of Bandhumatī for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.' Then, monks, those monks—some by their own psychic power, some by the psychic power of the deities—went to the royal city of Bandhumatī in a single day for the recitation of the Pātimokkha.

90. ‘‘Tatra sudaṃ, bhikkhave, vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho bhikkhusaṅghe evaṃ pātimokkhaṃ uddisati –

90."There, monks, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, recited the Pātimokkha to the community of monks in this way:

‘Khantī paramaṃ tapo titikkhā,Nibbānaṃ paramaṃ vadanti buddhā;Na hi pabbajito parūpaghātī,Na samaṇo [samaṇo (sī. syā. pī.)] hoti paraṃ viheṭhayanto.

'Patience and forbearance are the highest austerity; the Buddhas declare Nibbāna to be supreme. He is not a renunciate who harms another, nor is he an ascetic who oppresses another.'

‘Sabbapāpassa akaraṇaṃ, kusalassa upasampadā;Sacittapariyodapanaṃ, etaṃ buddhānasāsanaṃ.

'The non-doing of all evil,
The undertaking of wholesome action,
The purification of one's own mind:
This is the teaching of the Buddhas.'

‘Anūpavādo anūpaghāto [anupavādo anupaghāto (pī. ka.)], pātimokkhe ca saṃvaro;Mattaññutā ca bhattasmiṃ, pantañca sayanāsanaṃ;Adhicitte ca āyogo, etaṃ buddhānasāsana’nti.

'Not reviling, not harming,
Restraint with regard to the Pātimokkha,
Moderation in food,
And secluded lodging,
Application to the higher mind:
This is the teaching of the Buddhas.'

## Devatārocanaṃ

### Announcement by the Deities.

91. ‘‘Ekamidāhaṃ, bhikkhave, samayaṃ ukkaṭṭhāyaṃ viharāmi subhagavane sālarājamūle. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘na kho so sattāvāso sulabharūpo, yo mayā anāvutthapubbo [anajjhāvuṭṭhapubbo (ka. sī. ka.)] iminā dīghena addhunā aññatra suddhāvāsehi devehi. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ yena suddhāvāsā devā tenupasaṅkameyya’nti. Atha khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evameva ukkaṭṭhāyaṃ subhagavane sālarājamūle antarahito avihesu devesu pāturahosiṃ. Tasmiṃ, bhikkhave, devanikāye anekāni devatāsahassāni anekāni devatāsatasahassāni [anekāni devatāsatāni anekāni devatāsahassāni (syā.)] yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho, bhikkhave, tā devatā maṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘ito so, mārisā, ekanavutikappe yaṃ vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Vipassī, mārisā, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho khattiyo jātiyā ahosi, khattiyakule udapādi. Vipassī, mārisā, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho koṇḍañño gottena ahosi. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asītivassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Vipassī, mārisā, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho pāṭaliyā mūle abhisambuddho. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa khaṇḍatissaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi bhikkhusatasahassaṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi asītibhikkhusahassāni. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ime tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asoko nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Vipassissa, mārisa, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa bandhumā nāma rājā pitā ahosi. Bandhumatī nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti. Bandhumassa rañño bandhumatī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa evaṃ abhinikkhamanaṃ ahosi evaṃ pabbajjā evaṃ padhānaṃ evaṃ abhisambodhi evaṃ dhammacakkappavattanaṃ. Te mayaṃ, mārisā, vipassimhi bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ caritvā kāmesu kāmacchandaṃ virājetvā idhūpapannā’ti …pe…

91."Monks, I recall a time when I was staying at Ukkaṭṭhā in the Subhaga Grove at the foot of a Sal tree. While secluded and meditating, this thought arose in my mind: 'It is not easy to find an abode of beings that I have not previously dwelt in during this long stretch of time—except for the Pure Abode devas. Why don’t I go to the Pure Abode devas?' Then, monks, just as a strong man might bend his arm or stretch it out, so I vanished from the Subhaga Grove at Ukkaṭṭhā and appeared among the Aviha devas. In that assembly of devas, many thousands of devas, many hundreds of thousands of devas, approached me, paid homage, and stood to one side. Standing to one side, those devas said to me: 'Venerable sir, ninety-one eons ago, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. Vipassī, sir, was of the Khattiya caste, born into a Khattiya family. Vipassī, sir, was of the Koṇḍañña clan. The lifespan of Vipassī, sir, was eighty thousand years. Vipassī, sir, attained enlightenment at the foot of a Pāṭali tree. Vipassī, sir, had a pair of chief disciples named Khaṇḍa and Tissa, an excellent, auspicious pair. Vipassī, sir, held three assemblies of disciples—one of six million eight hundred thousand bhikkhus, one of one hundred thousand bhikkhus, and one of eighty thousand bhikkhus. All of them were arahants, free of defilements. Vipassī, sir, had a chief attendant named Asoka. Vipassī, sir, had a father named King Bandhumā and a mother named Queen Bandhumatī. The royal capital of King Bandhumā was named Bandhumatī. Vipassī, sir, went forth in such a way, renounced in such a way, strived in such a way, attained enlightenment in such a way, and set in motion the Wheel of Dhamma in such a way. We, venerable sirs, having lived the holy life under Vipassī the Blessed One, having dispelled desire for sensual pleasures, were reborn here.'"

‘‘Tasmiṃyeva kho, bhikkhave, devanikāye anekāni devatāsahassāni anekāni devatāsatasahassāni [anekāni devatāsatāni anekāni devatāsahassāni (syā. evamuparipi)] yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho, bhikkhave, tā devatā maṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘imasmiṃyeva kho, mārisā, bhaddakappe bhagavā etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke uppanno. Bhagavā, mārisā, khattiyo jātiyā khattiyakule uppanno. Bhagavā, mārisā, gotamo gottena. Bhagavato, mārisā, appakaṃ āyuppamāṇaṃ parittaṃ lahukaṃ yo ciraṃ jīvati, so vassasataṃ appaṃ vā bhiyyo. Bhagavā, mārisā, assatthassa mūle abhisambuddho. Bhagavato, mārisā, sāriputtamoggallānaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Bhagavato, mārisā, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi aḍḍhateḷasāni bhikkhusatāni. Bhagavato, mārisā, ayaṃ eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ. Bhagavato, mārisā, ānando nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Bhagavato, mārisā, suddhodano nāma rājā pitā ahosi. Māyā nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti. Kapilavatthu nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi. Bhagavato, mārisā, evaṃ abhinikkhamanaṃ ahosi evaṃ pabbajjā evaṃ padhānaṃ evaṃ abhisambodhi evaṃ dhammacakkappavattanaṃ. Te mayaṃ, mārisā, bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ caritvā kāmesu kāmacchandaṃ virājetvā idhūpapannā’ti.

"In that very assembly of devas, monks, many thousands of devas, many hundreds of thousands of devas approached me. Having approached, they paid homage to me and stood to one side. Standing to one side, those devas said to me: ‘In this very auspicious eon, venerable sir, the Blessed One has now arisen in the world, an arahant, a perfectly enlightened Buddha. The Blessed One, venerable sir, is a khattiya by birth, born into a khattiya family. The Blessed One, venerable sir, is of the Gotama clan. The Blessed One’s lifespan, venerable sir, is short, limited, brief—one who lives long lives a hundred years or a little more. The Blessed One, venerable sir, attained enlightenment at the foot of an assattha tree. The Blessed One, venerable sir, has a pair of chief disciples named Sāriputta and Moggallāna, an excellent, auspicious pair. The Blessed One, venerable sir, held one assembly of disciples consisting of twelve hundred and fifty bhikkhus. This one assembly of disciples, venerable sir, consisted entirely of arahants, those whose taints are destroyed. The Blessed One, venerable sir, has a bhikkhu named Ānanda as his attendant, his chief attendant. The Blessed One, venerable sir, had King Suddhodana as his father. Queen Māyā was his mother, the one who gave birth to him. The city of Kapilavatthu was his royal capital. The Blessed One’s going forth, venerable sir, was thus, his renunciation was thus, his striving was thus, his attainment of enlightenment was thus, his setting in motion of the Wheel of Dhamma was thus. We, venerable sir, having lived the holy life under the Blessed One, having dispelled desire for sensual pleasures, were reborn here.’"

92. ‘‘Atha khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, avihehi devehi saddhiṃ yena atappā devā tenupasaṅkamiṃ…pe… atha khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, avihehi ca devehi atappehi ca devehi saddhiṃ yena sudassā devā tenupasaṅkamiṃ. Atha khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, avihehi ca devehi atappehi ca devehi sudassehi ca devehi saddhiṃ yena sudassī devā tenupasaṅkamiṃ. Atha khvāhaṃ, bhikkhave, avihehi ca devehi atappehi ca devehi sudassehi ca devehi sudassīhi ca devehi saddhiṃ yena akaniṭṭhā devā tenupasaṅkamiṃ. Tasmiṃ, bhikkhave, devanikāye anekāni devatāsahassāni anekāni devatāsatasahassāni yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu, upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu.

92."Then, monks, together with the Aviha devas, I approached the Atappa devas… Then, monks, together with the Aviha devas and the Atappa devas, I approached the Sudassa devas. Then, monks, together with the Aviha devas, the Atappa devas, and the Sudassa devas, I approached the Sudassi devas. Then, monks, together with the Aviha devas, the Atappa devas, the Sudassa devas, and the Sudassi devas, I approached the Akaniṭṭha devas. In that assembly of devas, monks, many thousands of devas, many hundreds of thousands of devas approached me. Having approached, they paid homage to me and stood to one side."

‘‘Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho, bhikkhave, tā devatā maṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘ito so, mārisā, ekanavutikappe yaṃ vipassī bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke udapādi. Vipassī, mārisā, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho khattiyo jātiyā ahosi. Khattiyakule udapādi. Vipassī, mārisā, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho koṇḍañño gottena ahosi. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asītivassasahassāni āyuppamāṇaṃ ahosi. Vipassī, mārisā, bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho pāṭaliyā mūle abhisambuddho. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa khaṇḍatissaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi aṭṭhasaṭṭhibhikkhusatasahassaṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi bhikkhusatasahassaṃ. Eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi asītibhikkhusahassāni. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa ime tayo sāvakānaṃ sannipātā ahesuṃ sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa asoko nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko ahosi aggupaṭṭhāko. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa bandhumā nāma rājā pitā ahosi bandhumatī nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti. Bandhumassa rañño bandhumatī nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi. Vipassissa, mārisā, bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa evaṃ abhinikkhamanaṃ ahosi evaṃ pabbajjā evaṃ padhānaṃ evaṃ abhisambodhi, evaṃ dhammacakkappavattanaṃ. Te mayaṃ, mārisā, vipassimhi bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ caritvā kāmesu kāmacchandaṃ virājetvā idhūpapannā’ti. Tasmiṃyeva kho, bhikkhave, devanikāye anekāni devatāsahassāni anekāni devatāsatasahassāni yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho, bhikkhave, tā devatā maṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘ito so, mārisā, ekatiṃse kappe yaṃ sikhī bhagavā…pe… te mayaṃ, mārisā, sikhimhi bhagavati tasmiññeva kho mārisā, ekatiṃse kappe yaṃ vessabhū bhagavā…pe… te mayaṃ, mārisā, vessabhumhi bhagavati…pe… imasmiṃyeva kho, mārisā, bhaddakappe kakusandho koṇāgamano kassapo bhagavā…pe… te mayaṃ, mārisā, kakusandhamhi koṇāgamanamhi kassapamhi bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ caritvā kāmesu kāmacchandaṃ virājetvā idhūpapannā’ti.

"Standing to one side, those deities said to me: ‘Ninety-one eons ago, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One Vipassī, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, arose in the world. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was a khattiya by birth. He was born into a khattiya family. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, was of the Koṇḍañña clan. For Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the lifespan was eighty thousand years. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, attained enlightenment at the foot of a pāṭali tree. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, had a foremost pair of disciples named Khaṇḍa and Tissa. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, had three assemblies of disciples. One assembly consisted of six million eight hundred thousand monks. Another assembly consisted of one hundred thousand monks. And another assembly consisted of eighty thousand monks. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, had these three assemblies of disciples, all of whom were free from taints. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, had a chief attendant named Asoka. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, had a father named King Bandhuma and a mother named Queen Bandhumatī. The royal capital of King Bandhuma was named Bandhumatī. Vipassī, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, had such a going forth, such a renunciation, such striving, such enlightenment, and such a setting in motion of the Wheel of Dhamma. We, Venerable Sir, having lived the holy life under the Blessed One Vipassī and having abandoned desire for sensual pleasures, have been reborn here.’"In that very assembly of devas, monks, many thousands of devas, many hundreds of thousands of devas approached me. Having approached, they paid homage and stood to one side. Standing to one side, those deities said to me: ‘Thirty-one eons ago, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One Sikhī… We, Venerable Sir, having lived the holy life under the Blessed One Sikhī… In that very thirty-one eons, Venerable Sir, the Blessed One Vessabhū… We, Venerable Sir, having lived the holy life under the Blessed One Vessabhū… In this very auspicious eon, Venerable Sir, the Blessed Ones Kakusandha, Koṇāgamana, and Kassapa… We, Venerable Sir, having lived the holy life under the Blessed Ones Kakusandha, Koṇāgamana, and Kassapa, and having abandoned desire for sensual pleasures, have been reborn here.’"

93. ‘‘Tasmiṃyeva kho, bhikkhave, devanikāye anekāni devatāsahassāni anekāni devatāsatasahassāni yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho, bhikkhave, tā devatā maṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘imasmiṃyeva kho, mārisā, bhaddakappe bhagavā etarahi arahaṃ sammāsambuddho loke uppanno. Bhagavā, mārisā, khattiyo jātiyā, khattiyakule uppanno. Bhagavā, mārisā, gotamo gottena. Bhagavato, mārisā, appakaṃ āyuppamāṇaṃ parittaṃ lahukaṃ yo ciraṃ jīvati, so vassasataṃ appaṃ vā bhiyyo. Bhagavā, mārisā, assatthassa mūle abhisambuddho. Bhagavato, mārisā, sāriputtamoggallānaṃ nāma sāvakayugaṃ ahosi aggaṃ bhaddayugaṃ. Bhagavato, mārisā, eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi aḍḍhateḷasāni bhikkhusatāni. Bhagavato, mārisā, ayaṃ eko sāvakānaṃ sannipāto ahosi sabbesaṃyeva khīṇāsavānaṃ. Bhagavato, mārisā, ānando nāma bhikkhu upaṭṭhāko aggupaṭṭhāko ahosi. Bhagavato, mārisā, suddhodano nāma rājā pitā ahosi. Māyā nāma devī mātā ahosi janetti. Kapilavatthu nāma nagaraṃ rājadhānī ahosi. Bhagavato, mārisā, evaṃ abhinikkhamanaṃ ahosi, evaṃ pabbajjā, evaṃ padhānaṃ, evaṃ abhisambodhi, evaṃ dhammacakkappavattanaṃ. Te mayaṃ, mārisā, bhagavati brahmacariyaṃ caritvā kāmesu kāmacchandaṃ virājetvā idhūpapannā’ti.

93."In that very same heavenly realm, monks, many thousands of deities, many hundreds of thousands of deities, approached me. Having approached, they paid homage to me and stood to one side. Standing to one side, those deities said to me: ‘In this very same fortunate aeon, venerable sirs, the Blessed One has now arisen in the world as an Arahant, a Fully Enlightened One. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, is a khattiya by birth, born into a khattiya clan. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, is of the Gotama lineage. The Blessed One’s lifespan, venerable sirs, is short, limited, and brief—one who lives long lives a hundred years or a little more. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, attained enlightenment at the foot of an assattha tree. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, had a pair of chief disciples named Sāriputta and Moggallāna, an excellent pair. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, had one assembly of disciples consisting of twelve hundred and fifty monks. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, had this one assembly of disciples, all of whom were arahants with taints destroyed. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, had a monk named Ānanda as his chief attendant. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, had a king named Suddhodana as his father and a mother named Queen Māyā, the one who bore him. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, had a capital city named Kapilavatthu. The Blessed One, venerable sirs, had such a going forth, such a renunciation, such striving, such enlightenment, and such a turning of the Wheel of Dhamma. We, venerable sirs, having lived the spiritual life under the Blessed One and having rid ourselves of desire for sensual pleasures, have been reborn here.’"

94. ‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, tathāgatassevesā dhammadhātu suppaṭividdhā, yassā dhammadhātuyā suppaṭividdhattā tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ’ itipi. ‘Evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ’ itipīti.

94."Thus, monks, this element of the Dhamma is well-understood by the Tathagata. Because the element of the Dhamma is well-understood, the Tathagata recollects past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbana—who have cut off proliferation, severed the continuity, ended the round, gone beyond all suffering—recollecting their birth, name, clan, lifespan, pair of disciples, assembly of disciples: ‘Those Blessed Ones were of such a birth.’ ‘Those Blessed Ones were of such a name, such a clan, such virtue, such a Dhamma, such wisdom, such a dwelling, such liberation.’"

‘‘Devatāpi tathāgatassa etamatthaṃ ārocesuṃ, yena tathāgato atīte buddhe parinibbute chinnapapañce chinnavaṭume pariyādinnavaṭṭe sabbadukkhavītivatte jātitopi anussarati, nāmatopi anussarati, gottatopi anussarati, āyuppamāṇatopi anussarati, sāvakayugatopi anussarati, sāvakasannipātatopi anussarati ‘evaṃjaccā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ’ itipi. ‘Evaṃnāmā evaṃgottā evaṃsīlā evaṃdhammā evaṃpaññā evaṃvihārī evaṃvimuttā te bhagavanto ahesuṃ’ itipī’’ti.

"The deities also informed the Tathāgata of this matter, by which the Tathāgata recollects the past Buddhas who have attained final Nibbāna—whose proliferation is cut off, whose round is severed, whose course is exhausted, who have gone beyond all suffering. He recollects their birth, their names, their clans, their lifespans, their pairs of disciples, their gatherings of disciples, thinking: ‘Of such birth were those Blessed Ones.’ ‘Such were their names, such their clans, such their virtue, such their teachings, such their wisdom, such their abiding, such their liberation—those Blessed Ones were thus.’"

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

Thus spoke the Blessed One. The monks, delighted, rejoiced at the Blessed One's words.
