# Mahāsīhanādasuttaṃ

## The Great Lion's Roar Discourse

## Acelakassapavatthu

### The Story of Kassapa the Naked Ascetic

381. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā uruññāyaṃ [ujuññāyaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] viharati kaṇṇakatthale migadāye. Atha kho acelo kassapo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho acelo kassapo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bho gotama – ‘samaṇo gotamo sabbaṃ tapaṃ garahati, sabbaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhājīviṃ ekaṃsena upakkosati upavadatī’ti. Ye te, bho gotama, evamāhaṃsu – ‘samaṇo gotamo sabbaṃ tapaṃ garahati, sabbaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhājīviṃ ekaṃsena upakkosati upavadatī’ti, kacci te bhoto gotamassa vuttavādino, na ca bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ abhūtena abbhācikkhanti, dhammassa cānudhammaṃ byākaronti, na ca koci sahadhammiko vādānuvādo gārayhaṃ ṭhānaṃ āgacchati? Anabbhakkhātukāmā hi mayaṃ bhavantaṃ gotama’’nti.

381.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Uruññā, in the Deer Park at Kaṇṇakatthala. Then the naked ascetic Kassapa approached the Blessed One and exchanged greetings with him. When they had concluded their greetings and cordial talk, he stood to one side. Standing there, the naked ascetic Kassapa said to the Blessed One: "I have heard this, Master Gotama, that 'The ascetic Gotama criticizes all asceticism, and he categorically censures and blames all ascetics who live a harsh life.' Those who say this, Master Gotama—do they speak in accordance with your words, and not misrepresent you with falsehood? Do they explain the Dhamma in accordance with the Dhamma, so that no reasonable consequence of their assertion gives ground for criticism? For we do not wish to misrepresent the Blessed One."

382. ‘‘Ye te, kassapa, evamāhaṃsu – ‘samaṇo gotamo sabbaṃ tapaṃ garahati, sabbaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhājīviṃ ekaṃsena upakkosati upavadatī’ti, na me te vuttavādino, abbhācikkhanti ca pana maṃ te asatā abhūtena. Idhāhaṃ, kassapa, ekaccaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhājīviṃ passāmi dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannaṃ. Idha panāhaṃ, kassapa, ekaccaṃ tapassiṃ lūkhājīviṃ passāmi dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapannaṃ.

382."Those, Kassapa, who say, 'The ascetic Gotama disparages all asceticism, and categorically reviles and denounces every ascetic who lives a rough life'—they are not speaking according to my words, and they misrepresent me with what is false and untrue. For here, Kassapa, with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, I see some ascetics who live a rough life: at the breaking up of the body, after death, they are reborn in a state of deprivation, in an unhappy destination, in the nether world, even in hell. But here, Kassapa, I see some other ascetics who live a rough life: at the breaking up of the body, after death, they are reborn in a happy destination, in the heavenly world."

383. ‘‘Idhāhaṃ, kassapa, ekaccaṃ tapassiṃ appadukkhavihāriṃ passāmi dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannaṃ. Idha panāhaṃ, kassapa, ekaccaṃ tapassiṃ appadukkhavihāriṃ passāmi dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapannaṃ. Yohaṃ, kassapa, imesaṃ tapassīnaṃ evaṃ āgatiñca gatiñca cutiñca upapattiñca yathābhūtaṃ pajānāmi, sohaṃ kiṃ sabbaṃ tapaṃ garahissāmi, sabbaṃ vā tapassiṃ lūkhājīviṃ ekaṃsena upakkosissāmi upavadissāmi?

383."Here, Kassapa, I see some ascetics who live a life of little suffering: with the divine eye, purified, surpassing the human, on the dissolution of the body, after death, they have been reborn in a state of deprivation, in an evil destiny, in the lower realms, in hell. But here, Kassapa, I see some ascetics who live a life of little suffering: with the divine eye, purified, surpassing the human, on the dissolution of the body, after death, they have been reborn in a good destiny, in the heavenly world. Knowing thus as it actually is, Kassapa, the coming, the going, the passing away, and the arising of these ascetics, how could I criticize all asceticism, or categorically revile and disparage all ascetics who live a coarse life?"

384. ‘‘Santi, kassapa, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā paṇḍitā nipuṇā kataparappavādā vālavedhirūpā. Te bhindantā maññe caranti paññāgatena diṭṭhigatāni. Tehipi me saddhiṃ ekaccesu ṭhānesu sameti, ekaccesu ṭhānesu na sameti. Yaṃ te ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘sādhū’ti, mayampi taṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘sādhū’ti. Yaṃ te ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘na sādhū’ti, mayampi taṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘na sādhū’ti. Yaṃ te ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘sādhū’ti, mayaṃ taṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘na sādhū’ti. Yaṃ te ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘na sādhū’ti, mayaṃ taṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘sādhū’ti.

384."There are, Kassapa, some ascetics and brahmins who are wise, clever, skilled in debate, experts in demolishing the doctrines of others, sharp as hairs split by an arrow. They wander about, as it were, shattering the views of others with their wisdom-attained views. On some points, I agree with them; on other points, I do not agree. What they say in some instances is 'good,' I too say in some instances is 'good.' What they say in some instances is 'not good,' I too say in some instances is 'not good.' What they say in some instances is 'good,' I in some instances say is 'not good.' What they say in some instances is 'not good,' I in some instances say is 'good.'"

‘‘Yaṃ mayaṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘sādhū’ti, parepi taṃ ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘sādhū’ti. Yaṃ mayaṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘na sādhū’ti, parepi taṃ ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘na sādhū’ti. Yaṃ mayaṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘na sādhū’ti, pare taṃ ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘sādhū’ti. Yaṃ mayaṃ ekaccaṃ vadema ‘sādhū’ti, pare taṃ ekaccaṃ vadanti ‘na sādhū’ti.

"There are things we say are ‘good,’ and others also say those same things are ‘good.’ There are things we say are ‘not good,’ and others also say those same things are ‘not good.’ There are things we say are ‘not good,’ but others say those things are ‘good.’ There are things we say are ‘good,’ but others say those things are ‘not good.’"

## Samanuyuñjāpanakathā

### The Discourse on Questioning

385. ‘‘Tyāhaṃ upasaṅkamitvā evaṃ vadāmi – yesu no, āvuso, ṭhānesu na sameti, tiṭṭhantu tāni ṭhānāni. Yesu ṭhānesu sameti, tattha viññū samanuyuñjantaṃ samanugāhantaṃ samanubhāsantaṃ satthārā vā satthāraṃ saṅghena vā saṅghaṃ – ‘ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā akusalā akusalasaṅkhātā, sāvajjā sāvajjasaṅkhātā, asevitabbā asevitabbasaṅkhātā, na alamariyā na alamariyasaṅkhātā, kaṇhā kaṇhasaṅkhātā. Ko ime dhamme anavasesaṃ pahāya vattati, samaṇo vā gotamo, pare vā pana bhonto gaṇācariyā’ti?

385.“Having approached them, I would say this: ‘Friends, as to those points on which we do not agree, let them stand. But as to those points on which we do agree, there the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, comparing teacher with teacher, or Sangha with Sangha, would say: “These qualities of these venerable ones are unwholesome, reckoned as unwholesome; blameworthy, reckoned as blameworthy; not to be cultivated, reckoned as not to be cultivated; not noble, reckoned as not noble; dark, reckoned as dark. Who lives having completely abandoned these qualities: the ascetic Gotama, or these other venerable teachers of groups?”’

386. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, kassapa, vijjati, yaṃ viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā akusalā akusalasaṅkhātā, sāvajjā sāvajjasaṅkhātā, asevitabbā asevitabbasaṅkhātā, na alamariyā na alamariyasaṅkhātā, kaṇhā kaṇhasaṅkhātā. Samaṇo gotamo ime dhamme anavasesaṃ pahāya vattati, yaṃ vā pana bhonto pare gaṇācariyā’ti. Itiha, kassapa, viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā amheva tattha yebhuyyena pasaṃseyyuṃ.

386.“It is possible, Kassapa, that the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would say this: ‘These qualities of these venerable ones are unwholesome, reckoned as unwholesome; blameworthy, reckoned as blameworthy; not to be cultivated, reckoned as not to be cultivated; not noble, reckoned as not noble; dark, reckoned as dark. The ascetic Gotama lives having completely abandoned these qualities, whereas these other venerable teachers of groups have not.’ Thus, Kassapa, the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would for the most part praise us in that regard.

387. ‘‘Aparampi no, kassapa, viññū samanuyuñjantaṃ samanugāhantaṃ samanubhāsantaṃ satthārā vā satthāraṃ saṅghena vā saṅghaṃ – ‘ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā kusalā kusalasaṅkhātā, anavajjā anavajjasaṅkhātā, sevitabbā sevitabbasaṅkhātā, alamariyā alamariyasaṅkhātā, sukkā sukkasaṅkhātā. Ko ime dhamme anavasesaṃ samādāya vattati, samaṇo vā gotamo, pare vā pana bhonto gaṇācariyā’ ti?

387.“Further, Kassapa, the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, comparing teacher with teacher, or Sangha with Sangha, would say: ‘These qualities of these venerable ones are wholesome, reckoned as wholesome; blameless, reckoned as blameless; to be cultivated, reckoned as to be cultivated; noble, reckoned as noble; bright, reckoned as bright. Who lives having completely undertaken these qualities: the ascetic Gotama, or these other venerable teachers of groups?’

388. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, kassapa, vijjati, yaṃ viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā kusalā kusalasaṅkhātā, anavajjā anavajjasaṅkhātā, sevitabbā sevitabbasaṅkhātā, alamariyā alamariyasaṅkhātā, sukkā sukkasaṅkhātā. Samaṇo gotamo ime dhamme anavasesaṃ samādāya vattati, yaṃ vā pana bhonto pare gaṇācariyā’ti. Itiha, kassapa, viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā amheva tattha yebhuyyena pasaṃseyyuṃ.

388.“It is possible, Kassapa, that the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would say this: ‘These qualities of these venerable ones are wholesome, reckoned as wholesome; blameless, reckoned as blameless; to be cultivated, reckoned as to be cultivated; noble, reckoned as noble; bright, reckoned as bright. The ascetic Gotama lives having completely undertaken these qualities, whereas these other venerable teachers of groups have not.’ Thus, Kassapa, the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would for the most part praise us in that regard.

389. ‘‘Aparampi no, kassapa, viññū samanuyuñjantaṃ samanugāhantaṃ samanubhāsantaṃ satthārā vā satthāraṃ saṅghena vā saṅghaṃ – ‘ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā akusalā akusalasaṅkhātā, sāvajjā sāvajjasaṅkhātā, asevitabbā asevitabbasaṅkhātā, na alamariyā na alamariyasaṅkhātā, kaṇhā kaṇhasaṅkhātā. Ko ime dhamme anavasesaṃ pahāya vattati, gotamasāvakasaṅgho vā, pare vā pana bhonto gaṇācariyasāvakasaṅghā’ti?

389.“Further, Kassapa, the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, comparing teacher with teacher, or Sangha with Sangha, would say: ‘These qualities of these venerable ones are unwholesome, reckoned as unwholesome; blameworthy, reckoned as blameworthy; not to be cultivated, reckoned as not to be cultivated; not noble, reckoned as not noble; dark, reckoned as dark. Who lives having completely abandoned these qualities: the Sangha of Gotama’s disciples, or these other venerable teachers’ Sanghas of disciples?’

390. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, kassapa, vijjati, yaṃ viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā akusalā akusalasaṅkhātā, sāvajjā sāvajjasaṅkhātā, asevitabbā asevitabbasaṅkhātā, na alamariyā na alamariyasaṅkhātā, kaṇhā kaṇhasaṅkhātā. Gotamasāvakasaṅgho ime dhamme anavasesaṃ pahāya vattati, yaṃ vā pana bhonto pare gaṇācariyasāvakasaṅghā’ti. Itiha, kassapa, viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā amheva tattha yebhuyyena pasaṃseyyuṃ.

390.“It is possible, Kassapa, that the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would say: ‘These qualities of these venerable ones are unwholesome, reckoned as unwholesome; blameworthy, reckoned as blameworthy; not to be cultivated, reckoned as not to be cultivated; not noble, reckoned as not noble; dark, reckoned as dark. The Sangha of Gotama’s disciples lives having completely abandoned these qualities, whereas these other venerable teachers’ Sanghas of disciples have not.’ Thus, Kassapa, the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would for the most part praise us in that regard.

391. ‘‘Aparampi no, kassapa, viññū samanuyuñjantaṃ samanugāhantaṃ samanubhāsantaṃ satthārā vā satthāraṃ saṅghena vā saṅghaṃ. ‘Ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā kusalā kusalasaṅkhātā, anavajjā anavajjasaṅkhātā, sevitabbā sevitabbasaṅkhātā, alamariyā alamariyasaṅkhātā, sukkā sukkasaṅkhātā. Ko ime dhamme anavasesaṃ samādāya vattati, gotamasāvakasaṅgho vā, pare vā pana bhonto gaṇācariyasāvakasaṅghā’ti?

391.“Further, Kassapa, the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, comparing teacher with teacher, or Sangha with Sangha, would say: ‘These qualities of these venerable ones are wholesome, reckoned as wholesome; blameless, reckoned as blameless; to be cultivated, reckoned as to be cultivated; noble, reckoned as noble; bright, reckoned as bright. Who lives having completely undertaken these qualities: the Sangha of Gotama’s disciples, or these other venerable teachers’ Sanghas of disciples?’

392. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, kassapa, vijjati, yaṃ viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘ye imesaṃ bhavataṃ dhammā kusalā kusalasaṅkhātā, anavajjā anavajjasaṅkhātā, sevitabbā sevitabbasaṅkhātā, alamariyā alamariyasaṅkhātā, sukkā sukkasaṅkhātā. Gotamasāvakasaṅgho ime dhamme anavasesaṃ samādāya vattati, yaṃ vā pana bhonto pare gaṇācariyasāvakasaṅghā’ti. Itiha, kassapa, viññū samanuyuñjantā samanugāhantā samanubhāsantā amheva tattha yebhuyyena pasaṃseyyuṃ.

392.“It is possible, Kassapa, that the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would say this: ‘These qualities of these venerable ones are wholesome, reckoned as wholesome; blameless, reckoned as blameless; to be cultivated, reckoned as to be cultivated; noble, reckoned as noble; bright, reckoned as bright. The Sangha of Gotama’s disciples lives having completely undertaken these qualities, whereas these other venerable teachers’ Sanghas of disciples have not.’ Thus, Kassapa, the wise, questioning, probing, and conversing, would for the most part praise us in that regard.

## Ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo

### The Noble Eightfold Path.

393. ‘‘Atthi, kassapa, maggo atthi paṭipadā, yathāpaṭipanno sāmaṃyeva ñassati sāmaṃ dakkhati [dakkhiti (sī.)] – ‘samaṇova gotamo kālavādī bhūtavādī atthavādī dhammavādī vinayavādī’ti. Katamo ca, kassapa, maggo, katamā ca paṭipadā, yathāpaṭipanno sāmaṃyeva ñassati sāmaṃ dakkhati – ‘samaṇova gotamo kālavādī bhūtavādī atthavādī dhammavādī vinayavādī’ti? Ayameva ariyo aṭṭhaṅgiko maggo. Seyyathidaṃ – sammādiṭṭhi sammāsaṅkappo sammāvācā sammākammanto sammāājīvo sammāvāyāmo sammāsati sammāsamādhi. Ayaṃ kho, kassapa, maggo, ayaṃ paṭipadā, yathāpaṭipanno sāmaṃyeva ñassati sāmaṃ dakkhati ‘samaṇova gotamo kālavādī bhūtavādī atthavādī dhammavādī vinayavādī’’’ti.

393."There is, Kassapa, a path, there is a practice, by following which one will know for oneself and see for oneself: ‘The ascetic Gotama speaks at the right time, speaks what is factual, speaks what is meaningful, speaks on the Dhamma, speaks on the discipline.’ And what, Kassapa, is that path, what is that practice, by following which one will know for oneself and see for oneself: ‘The ascetic Gotama speaks at the right time, speaks what is factual, speaks what is meaningful, speaks on the Dhamma, speaks on the discipline’? It is this ![[Noble Path]]. This, Kassapa, is the path, this is the practice, by following which one will know for oneself and see for oneself: ‘The ascetic Gotama speaks at the right time, speaks what is factual, speaks what is meaningful, speaks on the Dhamma, speaks on the discipline.’"

## Tapopakkamakathā

### The Discussion on Ascetic Practices

394. Evaṃ vutte, acelo kassapo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘imepi kho, āvuso gotama, tapopakkamā etesaṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ sāmaññasaṅkhātā ca brahmaññasaṅkhātā ca. Acelako hoti, muttācāro, hatthāpalekhano, na ehibhaddantiko, na tiṭṭhabhaddantiko, nābhihaṭaṃ, na uddissakataṃ, na nimantanaṃ sādiyati. So na kumbhimukhā paṭiggaṇhāti, na kaḷopimukhā paṭiggaṇhāti, na eḷakamantaraṃ, na daṇḍamantaraṃ, na musalamantaraṃ, na dvinnaṃ bhuñjamānānaṃ, na gabbhiniyā, na pāyamānāya, na purisantaragatāya, na saṅkittīsu, na yattha sā upaṭṭhito hoti, na yattha makkhikā saṇḍasaṇḍacārinī, na macchaṃ, na maṃsaṃ, na suraṃ, na merayaṃ, na thusodakaṃ pivati. So ekāgāriko vā hoti ekālopiko, dvāgāriko vā hoti dvālopiko…pe… sattāgāriko vā hoti sattālopiko; ekissāpi dattiyā yāpeti, dvīhipi dattīhi yāpeti… sattahipi dattīhi yāpeti; ekāhikampi āhāraṃ āhāreti, dvīhikampi āhāraṃ āhāreti… sattāhikampi āhāraṃ āhāreti. Iti evarūpaṃ addhamāsikampi pariyāyabhattabhojanānuyogamanuyutto viharati.

394.When this was said, the naked ascetic Kassapa said to the Blessed One: "These, friend Gotama, are also the ascetic practices of those recluses and brahmins that are reckoned as the practices of recluses and brahmins. One is a naked ascetic, unrestrained in his conduct, licking his hands, not coming when asked ‘Come here, sir,’ not stopping when asked ‘Stay here, sir,’ not accepting food brought to him, not accepting food specially prepared for him, not accepting food by invitation. He does not accept food from the mouth of a pot, from the mouth of a bowl, from across a threshold, from across a stick, from across a pestle, from two people eating together, from a pregnant woman, from a nursing woman, from a woman living with a man, from a collection of food, from a place where a dog is kept waiting, from a place where flies move in swarms. He does not eat fish, meat, liquor, wine, or fermented rice gruel. He is one who visits one house (for alms), one who takes one mouthful; or one who visits two houses (for alms), one who takes two mouthfuls … or one who visits seven houses (for alms), one who takes seven mouthfuls. He sustains himself on one alms-gift, he sustains himself on two alms-gifts … he sustains himself on seven alms-gifts. He eats food every other day, he eats food every two days … he eats food every seven days. Thus he lives devoted to the practice of eating food at stated intervals, even every half-month.

395. ‘‘Imepi kho, āvuso gotama, tapopakkamā etesaṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ sāmaññasaṅkhātā ca brahmaññasaṅkhātā ca. Sākabhakkho vā hoti, sāmākabhakkho vā hoti, nīvārabhakkho vā hoti, daddulabhakkho vā hoti, haṭabhakkho vā hoti, kaṇabhakkho vā hoti, ācāmabhakkho vā hoti, piññākabhakkho vā hoti, tiṇabhakkho vā hoti, gomayabhakkho vā hoti, vanamūlaphalāhāro yāpeti pavattaphalabhojī.

395.These, friend Gotama, are also the ascetic practices of those recluses and brahmins that are reckoned as the practices of recluses and brahmins. One eats greens, eats millet, eats wild rice, eats skin-parings, eats water-weeds, eats rice-bran, eats rice-scum, eats sesame flour, eats grass, eats cow-dung; one lives on roots and fruits from the woods, eating fallen fruits.

396. ‘‘Imepi kho, āvuso gotama, tapopakkamā etesaṃ samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ sāmaññasaṅkhātā ca brahmaññasaṅkhātā ca. Sāṇānipi dhāreti, masāṇānipi dhāreti, chavadussānipi dhāreti, paṃsukūlānipi dhāreti, tirīṭānipi dhāreti, ajinampi dhāreti, ajinakkhipampi dhāreti, kusacīrampi dhāreti, vākacīrampi dhāreti, phalakacīrampi dhāreti, kesakambalampi dhāreti, vāḷakambalampi dhāreti, ulūkapakkhikampi dhāreti, kesamassulocakopi hoti kesamassulocanānuyogamanuyutto, ubbhaṭṭhakopi [ubbhaṭṭhikopi (ka.)] hoti āsanapaṭikkhitto, ukkuṭikopi hoti ukkuṭikappadhānamanuyutto, kaṇṭakāpassayikopi hoti kaṇṭakāpassaye seyyaṃ kappeti, phalakaseyyampi kappeti, thaṇḍilaseyyampi kappeti, ekapassayikopi hoti rajojalladharo, abbhokāsikopi hoti yathāsanthatiko, vekaṭikopi hoti vikaṭabhojanānuyogamanuyutto, apānakopi hoti apānakattamanuyutto, sāyatatiyakampi udakorohanānuyogamanuyutto viharatī’’ti.

396.These, friend Gotama, are also the ascetic practices of those recluses and brahmins that are reckoned as the practices of recluses and brahmins. This is his accomplishment in virtue… (as in sections 194 to 210)."

‘‘Yathā vā paneke bhonto samaṇabrāhmaṇā saddhādeyyāni bhojanāni bhuñjitvā te evarūpāya tiracchānavijjāya micchājīvena jīvitaṃ kappenti. Seyyathidaṃ – santikammaṃ paṇidhikammaṃ…pe… (yathā 211 anucchede) osadhīnaṃ patimokkho iti vā iti, evarūpāya tiracchānavijjāya micchājīvā paṭivirato hoti. Idampissa hoti sīlasampadāya.

"Whereas some honorable ascetics and brahmins, while living on food given in faith, maintain themselves by wrong livelihood, by such base arts as: auspicious ceremonies, vows… (as in section 211) incantations to exorcise demons, earth spells, snake spells, poison spells, scorpion spells, rat spells, bird spells, crow spells; prognostication by visions; giving protective charms; interpreting bodily marks—he abstains from wrong livelihood, from such base arts. This, too, belongs to his virtue."

‘‘Sa kho so [ayaṃ kho (ka.)], kassapa, bhikkhu evaṃ sīlasampanno na kutoci bhayaṃ samanupassati, yadidaṃ sīlasaṃvarato. Seyyathāpi, kassapa, rājā khattiyo muddhāvasitto nihatapaccāmitto na kutoci bhayaṃ samanupassati, yadidaṃ paccatthikato. Evameva kho, kassapa, bhikkhu evaṃ sīlasampanno na kutoci bhayaṃ samanupassati, yadidaṃ sīlasaṃvarato. So iminā ariyena sīlakkhandhena samannāgato ajjhattaṃ anavajjasukhaṃ paṭisaṃvedeti. Evaṃ kho, kassapa, bhikkhu sīlasampanno hoti. Ayaṃ kho, kassapa, sīlasampadā…pe… paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Idampissa hoti cittasampadāya…pe… dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ…pe… tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ…pe… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Idampissa hoti cittasampadāya. Ayaṃ kho, kassapa, cittasampadā.

"Indeed, Kassapa, that bhikkhu thus accomplished in virtue sees no fear from any source, that is, from the restraint of virtue. Just as, Kassapa, a Khattiya king, anointed on the head, who has defeated his enemies sees no fear from any source, that is, from the adversary. Just so, Kassapa, a bhikkhu thus accomplished in virtue sees no fear from any source, that is, from the restraint of virtue. Possessing this noble aggregate of virtue, he experiences within himself the happiness of blamelessness. Thus, Kassapa, a bhikkhu is accomplished in virtue. This, Kassapa, is accomplishment in virtue...enters and dwells in the first jhāna. This is his accomplishment in mind...the second jhāna...the third jhāna...enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna. This is his accomplishment in mind. This, Kassapa, is accomplishment in mind.

‘‘So evaṃ samāhite citte…pe… ñāṇadassanāya cittaṃ abhinīharati abhininnāmeti…pe… idampissa hoti paññāsampadāya…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānāti…pe… idampissa hoti paññāsampadāya. Ayaṃ kho, kassapa, paññāsampadā.

"When his mind is thus collected ... he directs and focuses his mind on knowledge and vision ... This too is an accomplishment in wisdom for him ... He understands: ‘There is nothing further for this state of being.’ ... This too is an accomplishment in wisdom for him. This, Kassapa, is accomplishment in wisdom."

‘‘Imāya ca, kassapa, sīlasampadāya cittasampadāya paññāsampadāya aññā sīlasampadā cittasampadā paññāsampadā uttaritarā vā paṇītatarā vā natthi.

"And, Kassapa, with this accomplishment of virtue, accomplishment of mind, and accomplishment of wisdom, there is no other accomplishment of virtue, accomplishment of mind, and accomplishment of wisdom that is higher or more sublime."

## Sīhanādakathā

### The Lion's Roar Story

402. ‘‘Santi, kassapa, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā sīlavādā. Te anekapariyāyena sīlassa vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti. Yāvatā, kassapa, ariyaṃ paramaṃ sīlaṃ, nāhaṃ tattha attano samasamaṃ samanupassāmi, kuto bhiyyo! Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo, yadidaṃ adhisīlaṃ.

402."There are, Kassapa, some ascetics and brahmins who advocate virtue. They praise virtue in various ways. But as far as supreme noble virtue is concerned, Kassapa, I do not see anyone equal to myself, much less superior! Rather, I am superior in that regard, namely, in higher virtue."

‘‘Santi, kassapa, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā tapojigucchāvādā. Te anekapariyāyena tapojigucchāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti. Yāvatā, kassapa, ariyā paramā tapojigucchā, nāhaṃ tattha attano samasamaṃ samanupassāmi, kuto bhiyyo! Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo, yadidaṃ adhijegucchaṃ.

"There are, Kassapa, some ascetics and brahmins who advocate asceticism and disgust. They praise asceticism and disgust in various ways. As far, Kassapa, as the supreme noble asceticism and disgust is concerned, I do not see anyone equal to myself, let alone superior! Rather, it is I who am superior in that regard, namely, in higher disgust.

‘‘Santi, kassapa, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā paññāvādā. Te anekapariyāyena paññāya vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti. Yāvatā, kassapa, ariyā paramā paññā, nāhaṃ tattha attano samasamaṃ samanupassāmi, kuto bhiyyo! Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo, yadidaṃ adhipaññaṃ.

"There are, Kassapa, some ascetics and brahmins who are proponents of wisdom. They speak in praise of wisdom in various ways. As far, Kassapa, as the supreme noble wisdom goes, I do not regard anyone as equal to myself in that respect, much less superior! Rather, I am superior in that respect, namely, in higher wisdom."

‘‘Santi, kassapa, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā vimuttivādā. Te anekapariyāyena vimuttiyā vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti. Yāvatā, kassapa, ariyā paramā vimutti, nāhaṃ tattha attano samasamaṃ samanupassāmi, kuto bhiyyo! Atha kho ahameva tattha bhiyyo, yadidaṃ adhivimutti.

"There are, Kassapa, some ascetics and brahmins who advocate liberation. They praise liberation in various ways. But as far, Kassapa, as the supreme noble liberation is concerned, I do not see anyone equal to myself, much less superior! Rather, it is I who am superior in that regard, namely, in higher liberation."

403. ‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, kassapa, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘sīhanādaṃ kho samaṇo gotamo nadati, tañca kho suññāgāre nadati, no parisāsū’ti. Te – ‘mā heva’ntissu vacanīyā. ‘Sīhanādañca samaṇo gotamo nadati, parisāsu ca nadatī’ti evamassu, kassapa, vacanīyā.

403."It is possible, Kassapa, that wanderers of other sects might say: 'The ascetic Gotama roars a lion's roar, but he roars it in an empty dwelling, not in an assembly.' They should be told: 'Do not say that!' The ascetic Gotama roars a lion's roar, and he roars it in assemblies as well.' Thus, Kassapa, they should be answered.

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, kassapa, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘sīhanādañca samaṇo gotamo nadati, parisāsu ca nadati, no ca kho visārado nadatī’ti. Te – ‘mā heva’ntissu vacanīyā. ‘Sīhanādañca samaṇo gotamo nadati, parisāsu ca nadati, visārado ca nadatī’’ti evamassu, kassapa, vacanīyā.

"It is possible, Kassapa, that wanderers of other sects might say: ‘The ascetic Gotama roars a lion’s roar, and he roars in assemblies, but he does not roar confidently.’ They should be told: ‘Do not say that! The ascetic Gotama roars a lion’s roar, and he roars in assemblies, and he roars confidently.’ Thus, Kassapa, they should be answered."

‘‘Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ, kassapa, vijjati, yaṃ aññatitthiyā paribbājakā evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘sīhanādañca samaṇo gotamo nadati, parisāsu ca nadati, visārado ca nadati, no ca kho naṃ pañhaṃ pucchanti…pe… pañhañca naṃ pucchanti; no ca kho nesaṃ pañhaṃ puṭṭho byākaroti…pe… pañhañca nesaṃ puṭṭho byākaroti; no ca kho pañhassa veyyākaraṇena cittaṃ ārādheti…pe… pañhassa ca veyyākaraṇena cittaṃ ārādheti; no ca kho sotabbaṃ maññanti…pe… sotabbañcassa maññanti; no ca kho sutvā pasīdanti…pe… sutvā cassa pasīdanti; no ca kho pasannākāraṃ karonti…pe… pasannākārañca karonti; no ca kho tathattāya paṭipajjanti…pe… tathattāya ca paṭipajjanti; no ca kho paṭipannā ārādhentī’ti. Te – ‘mā heva’ntissu vacanīyā. ‘Sīhanādañca samaṇo gotamo nadati, parisāsu ca nadati, visārado ca nadati, pañhañca naṃ pucchanti, pañhañca nesaṃ puṭṭho byākaroti, pañhassa ca veyyākaraṇena cittaṃ ārādheti, sotabbañcassa maññanti, sutvā cassa pasīdanti, pasannākārañca karonti, tathattāya ca paṭipajjanti, paṭipannā ca ārādhentī’ti evamassu, kassapa, vacanīyā.

"It is possible, Kassapa, that wanderers of other sects might say: ‘The ascetic Gotama roars a lion’s roar, he roars in assemblies, he roars with confidence, but they do not question him… or they question him, but he does not answer their questions… or he answers their questions, but he does not satisfy their minds with his explanations… or they do not consider his words worth listening to… or they consider his words worth listening to, but they are not pleased after hearing them… or they are pleased but do not show signs of approval… or they show signs of approval but do not practice accordingly… or they practice accordingly but do not attain.’ They should be told: ‘Do not say that! The ascetic Gotama roars a lion’s roar, he roars in assemblies, he roars with confidence, they question him, he answers their questions, he satisfies their minds with his explanations, they consider his words worth listening to, they are pleased after hearing them, they show signs of approval, they practice accordingly, and those who practice attain.’ Thus, Kassapa, they should be answered."

## Titthiyaparivāsakathā

### The Discussion on the Probation of Sectarians

404. ‘‘Ekamidāhaṃ, kassapa, samayaṃ rājagahe viharāmi gijjhakūṭe pabbate. Tatra maṃ aññataro tapabrahmacārī nigrodho nāma adhijegucche pañhaṃ apucchi. Tassāhaṃ adhijegucche pañhaṃ puṭṭho byākāsiṃ. Byākate ca pana me attamano ahosi paraṃ viya mattāyā’’ti. ‘‘Ko hi, bhante, bhagavato dhammaṃ sutvā na attamano assa paraṃ viya mattāya? Ahampi hi, bhante, bhagavato dhammaṃ sutvā attamano paraṃ viya mattāya. Abhikkantaṃ, bhante, abhikkantaṃ, bhante. Seyyathāpi, bhante, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya – ‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’ti; evamevaṃ bhagavatā anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ, bhante, bhagavantaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi, dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Labheyyāhaṃ, bhante, bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ, labheyyaṃ upasampada’’nti.

404.“Once, Kassapa, I was dwelling at Rājagaha on Vulture Peak. There, a certain ascetic named Nigrodha, who practiced rigorous austerity, asked me a question concerning the higher disgust. When I answered his question concerning the higher disgust, he was delighted beyond measure.” “Who, venerable sir, would not be delighted beyond measure after hearing the Dhamma from the Blessed One? Even I, venerable sir, was delighted beyond measure after hearing the Dhamma from the Blessed One. Excellent, venerable sir! Excellent, venerable sir! Just as, venerable sir, one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the path to one who was lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness, thinking, ‘Those with eyes will see forms’—in the same way, the Blessed One has made the Dhamma clear in many ways. I go for refuge, venerable sir, to the Blessed One, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha. May I receive the going forth in the Blessed One’s presence; may I receive the full ordination.”

405. ‘‘Yo kho, kassapa, aññatitthiyapubbo imasmiṃ dhammavinaye ākaṅkhati pabbajjaṃ, ākaṅkhati upasampadaṃ, so cattāro māse parivasati, catunnaṃ māsānaṃ accayena āraddhacittā bhikkhū pabbājenti, upasampādenti bhikkhubhāvāya. Api ca mettha puggalavemattatā viditā’’ti. ‘‘Sace, bhante, aññatitthiyapubbā imasmiṃ dhammavinaye ākaṅkhanti pabbajjaṃ, ākaṅkhanti upasampadaṃ, cattāro māse parivasanti, catunnaṃ māsānaṃ accayena āraddhacittā bhikkhū pabbājenti, upasampādenti bhikkhubhāvāya. Ahaṃ cattāri vassāni parivasissāmi, catunnaṃ vassānaṃ accayena āraddhacittā bhikkhū pabbājentu, upasampādentu bhikkhubhāvāyā’’ti.

405.“Kassapa, anyone who was formerly a sectarian and desires the going forth in this Dhamma and Discipline, desires the full ordination, he undergoes probation for four months. After four months, if the bhikkhus are so inclined, they ordain him and grant him full ordination to the state of a bhikkhu. And also, individual differences are known to me in this matter.” “Venerable sir, if those who were formerly sectarians desire the going forth in this Dhamma and Discipline, desire the full ordination, they undergo probation for four months. After four months, if the bhikkhus are so inclined, the bhikkhus should ordain them and grant them full ordination to the state of a bhikkhu. I will undergo probation for four years. After four years, if the bhikkhus are so inclined, the bhikkhus should ordain me and grant me full ordination to the state of a bhikkhu.”

Alattha kho acelo kassapo bhagavato santike pabbajjaṃ, alattha upasampadaṃ. Acirūpasampanno kho panāyasmā kassapo eko vūpakaṭṭho appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto na cirasseva – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti, tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihāsi. ‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti – abbhaññāsi. Aññataro kho panāyasmā kassapo arahataṃ ahosīti.

Then Kassapa the Naked Ascetic received the going forth in the Blessed One’s presence, and he received the full ordination. Soon after his full ordination, the Venerable Kassapa, dwelling alone, withdrawn, heedful, ardent, and resolute, not long after, by realizing for himself with direct knowledge in this very life that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for the sake of which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, he entered upon and dwelt in it. He understood: “Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more for this state of being.” And the Venerable Kassapa was one of the arahants.
