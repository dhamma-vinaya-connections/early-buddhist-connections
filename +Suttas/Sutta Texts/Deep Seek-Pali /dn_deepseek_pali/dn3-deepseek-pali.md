# Ambaṭṭhasuttaṃ

## The Ambaṭṭha Sutta

254. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi yena icchānaṅgalaṃ nāma kosalānaṃ brāhmaṇagāmo tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā icchānaṅgale viharati icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍe.

254.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was wandering in the Kosala country with a large community of bhikkhus, with about five hundred bhikkhus, when he arrived at Icchānaṅgala, a Kosalan brahmin village. There the Blessed One stayed in the Icchānaṅgala woodland grove.

## Pokkharasātivatthu

### The Story of Pokkharasāti

255. Tena kho pana samayena brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti ukkaṭṭhaṃ [pokkharasātī (sī.), pokkharasādi (pī.)] ajjhāvasati sattussadaṃ satiṇakaṭṭhodakaṃ sadhaññaṃ rājabhoggaṃ raññā pasenadinā kosalena dinnaṃ rājadāyaṃ brahmadeyyaṃ. Assosi kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti – ‘‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi icchānaṅgalaṃ anuppatto icchānaṅgale viharati icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍe. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’ [bhagavāti (syā. kaṃ.), uparisoṇadaṇḍasuttādīsupi buddhaguṇakathāyaṃ evameva dissati]. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ, sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ, kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’ti.

255.Now at that time the brahmin Pokkharasāti was living in Ukkaṭṭhā, a populous place, with abundant grass, wood, water, and grain, a royal domain, a royal gift and brahmin endowment, granted to him by King Pasenadi of Kosala. The brahmin Pokkharasāti heard: "The ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans who went forth from a Sakyan clan, has been wandering among the Kosalans with a large community of bhikkhus, with about five hundred bhikkhus, and has arrived at Icchānaṅgala and is staying in the Icchānaṅgala woodland grove. Now a good report about Master Gotama has been circulating thus: ‘That Blessed One is an arahant, perfectly enlightened, accomplished in true knowledge and conduct, fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of devas and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One.’ He has realized with direct knowledge this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, this population with its ascetics and brahmins, its devas and humans, and he makes it known to others. He teaches the Dhamma that is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, meaningful and well-phrased; he reveals a spiritual life that is entirely perfect and pure. It is good to see such arahants."

## Ambaṭṭhamāṇavo

### Ambaṭṭha, the brahmin youth.

256. Tena kho pana samayena brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa ambaṭṭho nāma māṇavo antevāsī hoti ajjhāyako mantadharo tiṇṇaṃ vedānaṃ [bedānaṃ (ka.)] pāragū sanighaṇḍukeṭubhānaṃ sākkharappabhedānaṃ itihāsapañcamānaṃ padako veyyākaraṇo lokāyatamahāpurisalakkhaṇesu anavayo anuññātapaṭiññāto sake ācariyake tevijjake pāvacane – ‘‘yamahaṃ jānāmi, taṃ tvaṃ jānāsi; yaṃ tvaṃ jānāsi tamahaṃ jānāmī’’ti.

256.Now at that time, the brahmin Pokkharasāti had a young brahmin student named Ambaṭṭha, who was a reciter, a master of the mantras, proficient in the three Vedas—with their vocabularies, ritual, and phonology—and the legends as the fifth. He was skilled in etymology and grammar, and was complete in the system of natural philosophy and the marks of a great man. He was acknowledged and approved by his teacher in his teacher's tradition of the three Vedas, saying, "What I know, you know; what you know, I know."

257. Atha kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ayaṃ, tāta ambaṭṭha, samaṇo gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi icchānaṅgalaṃ anuppatto icchānaṅgale viharati icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍe. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā, arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ, sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti. Sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotīti. Ehi tvaṃ tāta ambaṭṭha, yena samaṇo gotamo tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ jānāhi, yadi vā taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ tathāsantaṃyeva saddo abbhuggato, yadi vā no tathā. Yadi vā so bhavaṃ gotamo tādiso, yadi vā na tādiso, tathā mayaṃ taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ vedissāmā’’ti.

257.Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti addressed Ambaṭṭha: "Dear Ambaṭṭha, the ascetic Gotama, a son of the Sakyans who has gone forth from the Sakyan clan, is wandering in the Kosala country with a large company of bhikkhus, with about five hundred bhikkhus, and has arrived at Icchānaṅgala, where he is staying in the Icchānaṅgala forest grove. Now a good report about the Venerable Gotama has spread thus: 'That Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, Well-gone, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of persons to be tamed, teacher of devas and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.' He, by himself, has fully known and realised with direct knowledge this world with its gods, Māras, and Brahmās, this generation with its ascetics and brahmins, its princes and people; and he makes it known to others. He teaches the Dhamma good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with the right meaning and phrasing; he reveals a holy life that is utterly perfect and pure. It is good to see such Arahants.' Go now, dear Ambaṭṭha, approach the ascetic Gotama; having approached, find out whether the report about the Venerable Gotama is true or not, and whether he is as he is said to be or not. In this way we will know about the Venerable Gotama."

258. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ panāhaṃ, bho, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ jānissāmi – ‘yadi vā taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ tathāsantaṃyeva saddo abbhuggato, yadi vā no tathā. Yadi vā so bhavaṃ gotamo tādiso, yadi vā na tādiso’’’ti?

258."But how, sir, shall I know whether that venerable Gotama is as the report about him says, or whether it is not so? Whether that venerable Gotama is such, or whether he is not such?"

‘‘Āgatāni kho, tāta ambaṭṭha, amhākaṃ mantesu dvattiṃsa mahāpurisalakkhaṇāni, yehi samannāgatassa mahāpurisassa dveyeva gatiyo bhavanti anaññā. Sace agāraṃ ajjhāvasati, rājā hoti cakkavattī dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāvī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni satta ratanāni bhavanti. Seyyathidaṃ – cakkaratanaṃ, hatthiratanaṃ, assaratanaṃ, maṇiratanaṃ, itthiratanaṃ, gahapatiratanaṃ, pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā bhavanti sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena abhivijiya ajjhāvasati. Sace kho pana agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajati, arahaṃ hoti sammāsambuddho loke vivaṭṭacchado. Ahaṃ kho pana, tāta ambaṭṭha, mantānaṃ dātā; tvaṃ mantānaṃ paṭiggahetā’’ti.

"Dear Ambaṭṭha, in our mantras are recorded the thirty-two marks of a great man. For a great man endowed with these marks, there are only two destinies, no other. If he lives the household life, he becomes a wheel-turning monarch, a righteous king who rules by the Dhamma, a conqueror whose dominion extends to the four quarters, who achieves stability for his country, and possesses the seven treasures. These seven treasures are: the wheel-treasure, the elephant-treasure, the horse-treasure, the jewel-treasure, the woman-treasure, the householder-treasure, and the chief-advisor-treasure as the seventh. He has over a thousand sons who are valiant, heroic, and crushers of enemy armies. He rules this earth bounded by the ocean without rod or sword, by means of the Dhamma. But if he goes forth from the household life into homelessness, he becomes an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, one who removes the veil from the world. I, dear Ambaṭṭha, am the giver of the mantras; you are the receiver of the mantras."

259. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa paṭissutvā uṭṭhāyāsanā brāhmaṇaṃ pokkharasātiṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā vaḷavārathamāruyha sambahulehi māṇavakehi saddhiṃ yena icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍo tena pāyāsi. Yāvatikā yānassa bhūmi yānena gantvā yānā paccorohitvā pattikova ārāmaṃ pāvisi. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū abbhokāse caṅkamanti. Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘kahaṃ nu kho, bho, etarahi so bhavaṃ gotamo viharati? Tañhi mayaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ dassanāya idhūpasaṅkantā’’ti.

259."Yes, sir," replied the young brahmin Ambaṭṭha. Having assented to the brahmin Pokkharasāti, he rose from his seat, paid homage to Pokkharasāti, circumambulated him, mounted a chariot drawn by mares, and set out towards the Icchānaṅgala forest grove accompanied by many young brahmins. He went by chariot as far as the road was passable for chariots, then dismounted from the chariot and proceeded on foot to the park. At that time, several bhikkhus were walking back and forth in the open air. Then Ambaṭṭha approached those bhikkhus and asked, "Where, sirs, is the venerable Gotama staying now? We have come here to see the venerable Gotama."

260. Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo abhiññātakolañño ceva abhiññātassa ca brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa antevāsī. Agaru kho pana bhagavato evarūpehi kulaputtehi saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo hotī’’ti. Te ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘eso ambaṭṭha vihāro saṃvutadvāro, tena appasaddo upasaṅkamitvā ataramāno āḷindaṃ pavisitvā ukkāsitvā aggaḷaṃ ākoṭehi, vivarissati te bhagavā dvāra’’nti.

260.Then those bhikkhus thought: "This Ambaṭṭha is a young man of a well-known clan, and a pupil of the acknowledged brahmin Pokkharasāti. The Blessed One is not burdened by conversation with such young men of good family." They said to Ambaṭṭha: "Ambaṭṭha, this dwelling has its door closed. Approach quietly, without haste, enter the porch, cough, and knock on the bolt. The Blessed One will open the door for you."

261. Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo yena so vihāro saṃvutadvāro, tena appasaddo upasaṅkamitvā ataramāno āḷindaṃ pavisitvā ukkāsitvā aggaḷaṃ ākoṭesi. Vivari bhagavā dvāraṃ. Pāvisi ambaṭṭho māṇavo. Māṇavakāpi pavisitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu, sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ambaṭṭho pana māṇavo caṅkamantopi nisinnena bhagavatā kañci kañci [kiñci kiñci (ka.)] kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāreti, ṭhitopi nisinnena bhagavatā kiñci kiñci kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāreti.

261.Then the young brahmin Ambaṭṭha approached that dwelling with closed doors, moving quietly. Without haste, he entered the porch, cleared his throat, and knocked on the bolt. The Blessed One opened the door. Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin entered. The young brahmins also entered, exchanged courteous greetings with the Blessed One, and after some pleasant and memorable conversation, sat down to one side. But Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin, while walking back and forth, exchanged some pleasant and memorable conversation with the seated Blessed One; and while standing, exchanged some pleasant and memorable conversation with the seated Blessed One.

262. Atha kho bhagavā ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘evaṃ nu te, ambaṭṭha, brāhmaṇehi vuddhehi mahallakehi ācariyapācariyehi saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo hoti, yathayidaṃ caraṃ tiṭṭhaṃ nisinnena mayā kiñci kiñci kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretī’’ti?

262.Then the Blessed One said to the young man Ambaṭṭha: "Ambaṭṭha, is this how conversation and discussion is conducted among you with aged, elderly brahmins, teachers of teachers, as you now converse with me, who am seated, while you are walking or standing?"

## Paṭhamaibbhavādo

### The First Charge of Servile Birth

263. ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama. Gacchanto vā hi, bho gotama, gacchantena brāhmaṇo brāhmaṇena saddhiṃ sallapitumarahati, ṭhito vā hi, bho gotama, ṭhitena brāhmaṇo brāhmaṇena saddhiṃ sallapitumarahati, nisinno vā hi, bho gotama, nisinnena brāhmaṇo brāhmaṇena saddhiṃ sallapitumarahati, sayāno vā hi, bho gotama, sayānena brāhmaṇo brāhmaṇena saddhiṃ sallapitumarahati. Ye ca kho te, bho gotama, muṇḍakā samaṇakā ibbhā kaṇhā [kiṇhā (ka. sī. pī.)] bandhupādāpaccā, tehipi me saddhiṃ evaṃ kathāsallāpo hoti, yathariva bhotā gotamenā’’ti. ‘‘Atthikavato kho pana te, ambaṭṭha, idhāgamanaṃ ahosi, yāyeva kho panatthāya āgaccheyyātha [āgaccheyyātho (sī. pī.)], tameva atthaṃ sādhukaṃ manasi kareyyātha [manasikareyyātho (sī. pī.)]. Avusitavāyeva kho pana bho ayaṃ ambaṭṭho māṇavo vusitamānī kimaññatra avusitattā’’ti.

263."No indeed, Master Gotama. For, Master Gotama, a brahmin who is walking is fit to converse with a brahmin who is walking; a brahmin who is standing is fit to converse with a brahmin who is standing; a brahmin who is sitting is fit to converse with a brahmin who is sitting; a brahmin who is lying down is fit to converse with a brahmin who is lying down. But those, Master Gotama, shaveling ascetics, low-born, swarthy, offspring of the Kinsman's feet—even with them I have such conversation as I have with Master Gotama.""Ambaṭṭha, you came here with a purpose. Whatever purpose you came for, you should attend well to that purpose. This young brahmin Ambaṭṭha, sirs, has not completed his training, yet he thinks he has. What else can be the reason for this, if not his lack of training?"

264. Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhagavatā avusitavādena vuccamāno kupito anattamano bhagavantaṃyeva khuṃsento bhagavantaṃyeva vambhento bhagavantaṃyeva upavadamāno – ‘‘samaṇo ca me, bho, gotamo pāpito bhavissatī’’ti bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘caṇḍā, bho gotama, sakyajāti; pharusā, bho gotama, sakyajāti; lahusā, bho gotama, sakyajāti; bhassā, bho gotama, sakyajāti; ibbhā santā ibbhā samānā na brāhmaṇe sakkaronti, na brāhmaṇe garuṃ karonti [garukaronti (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)], na brāhmaṇe mānenti, na brāhmaṇe pūjenti, na brāhmaṇe apacāyanti. Tayidaṃ, bho gotama, nacchannaṃ, tayidaṃ nappatirūpaṃ, yadime sakyā ibbhā santā ibbhā samānā na brāhmaṇe sakkaronti, na brāhmaṇe garuṃ karonti, na brāhmaṇe mānenti, na brāhmaṇe pūjenti, na brāhmaṇe apacāyantī’’ti. Itiha ambaṭṭho māṇavo idaṃ paṭhamaṃ sakyesu ibbhavādaṃ nipātesi.

264.Then the young brahmin Ambaṭṭha, being addressed by the Blessed One with the charge of not having completed his training, became angry and displeased. He reviled the Blessed One, disparaged the Blessed One, and abused the Blessed One, thinking, "The ascetic Gotama will be brought to a bad state," and said to the Blessed One: "The Sakyan clan, Master Gotama, is fierce; the Sakyan clan, Master Gotama, is harsh; the Sakyan clan, Master Gotama, is fickle; the Sakyan clan, Master Gotama, is garrulous. Being of servile birth, being of servile birth, they do not honor, respect, esteem, venerate, or pay homage to brahmins. This, Master Gotama, is unseemly, this is unfitting, that these Sakyans, being of servile birth, being of servile birth, do not honor, respect, esteem, venerate, or pay homage to brahmins." Thus, the young brahmin Ambaṭṭha made this first charge of servile birth against the Sakyans.

## Dutiyaibbhavādo

### The Second Charge of Servile Birth

265. ‘‘Kiṃ pana te, ambaṭṭha, sakyā aparaddhu’’nti? ‘‘Ekamidāhaṃ, bho gotama, samayaṃ ācariyassa brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa kenacideva karaṇīyena kapilavatthuṃ agamāsiṃ. Yena sakyānaṃ sandhāgāraṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃ. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā sakyā ceva sakyakumārā ca sandhāgāre [santhāgāre (sī. pī.)] uccesu āsanesu nisinnā honti aññamaññaṃ aṅgulipatodakehi [aṅgulipatodakena (pī.)] sañjagghantā saṃkīḷantā, aññadatthu mamaññeva maññe anujagghantā, na maṃ koci āsanenapi nimantesi. Tayidaṃ, bho gotama, nacchannaṃ, tayidaṃ nappatirūpaṃ, yadime sakyā ibbhā santā ibbhā samānā na brāhmaṇe sakkaronti, na brāhmaṇe garuṃ karonti, na brāhmaṇe mānenti, na brāhmaṇe pūjenti, na brāhmaṇe apacāyantī’’ti. Itiha ambaṭṭho māṇavo idaṃ dutiyaṃ sakyesu ibbhavādaṃ nipātesi.

265."Ambaṭṭha, what offense have the Sakyans committed against you?" "Once, Master Gotama, I went to Kapilavatthu on some business for my teacher, the brahmin Pokkharasāti. I approached the Sakyans' assembly hall. At that time, many Sakyans and Sakyan youths were seated on high seats in the assembly hall, guffawing and making merry, and I thought they were making fun of me. No one even invited me to sit down. This, Master Gotama, is unseemly, this is unfitting, that these Sakyans, being of servile birth, being of servile birth, do not honor, respect, esteem, venerate, or pay homage to brahmins." Thus, the young brahmin Ambaṭṭha made this second charge of servile birth against the Sakyans.

## Tatiyaibbhavādo

### The Third Charge of Servile Birth

266. ‘‘Laṭukikāpi kho, ambaṭṭha, sakuṇikā sake kulāvake kāmalāpinī hoti. Sakaṃ kho panetaṃ, ambaṭṭha, sakyānaṃ yadidaṃ kapilavatthuṃ, nārahatāyasmā ambaṭṭho imāya appamattāya abhisajjitu’’nti. ‘‘Cattārome, bho gotama, vaṇṇā – khattiyā brāhmaṇā vessā suddā. Imesañhi, bho gotama, catunnaṃ vaṇṇānaṃ tayo vaṇṇā – khattiyā ca vessā ca suddā ca – aññadatthu brāhmaṇasseva paricārakā sampajjanti. Tayidaṃ, bho gotama, nacchannaṃ, tayidaṃ nappatirūpaṃ, yadime sakyā ibbhā santā ibbhā samānā na brāhmaṇe sakkaronti, na brāhmaṇe garuṃ karonti, na brāhmaṇe mānenti, na brāhmaṇe pūjenti, na brāhmaṇe apacāyantī’’ti. Itiha ambaṭṭho māṇavo idaṃ tatiyaṃ sakyesu ibbhavādaṃ nipātesi.

266."Even a little bird, Ambaṭṭha, chatters freely in her own nest. This Kapilavatthu is the Sakyans’ own. The venerable Ambaṭṭha should not take offense at this trifling matter.""There are these four classes, Master Gotama: the Khattiyas, the brahmins, the Vessas, and the Suddas. Among these four classes, Master Gotama, three classes—the Khattiyas, the Vessas, and the Suddas—in fact become servants of the brahmins. This, Master Gotama, is unseemly, this is unfitting, that these Sakyans, being of servile birth, being of servile birth, do not honor, respect, esteem, venerate, or pay homage to brahmins." Thus, the young brahmin Ambaṭṭha made this third charge of servile birth against the Sakyans.

## Dāsiputtavādo

### The Slave-Born Accusation

267. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘atibāḷhaṃ kho ayaṃ ambaṭṭho māṇavo sakyesu ibbhavādena nimmādeti, yaṃnūnāhaṃ gottaṃ puccheyya’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kathaṃ gottosi, ambaṭṭhā’’ti? ‘‘Kaṇhāyanohamasmi, bho gotamā’’ti. Porāṇaṃ kho pana te ambaṭṭha mātāpettikaṃ nāmagottaṃ anussarato ayyaputtā sakyā bhavanti; dāsiputto tvamasi sakyānaṃ. Sakyā kho pana, ambaṭṭha, rājānaṃ okkākaṃ pitāmahaṃ dahanti.

267.Then the Blessed One thought: "This Ambaṭṭha youth excessively disparages the Sakyans with his talk of servile birth. Suppose I were to ask him about his clan?" Then the Blessed One said to Ambaṭṭha the youth: "What is your clan, Ambaṭṭha?" "I am a Kaṇhāyana, Master Gotama." But, Ambaṭṭha, when one recalls your ancient maternal and paternal name and clan, the Sakyans are your masters; you are the slave-born son of the Sakyans. The Sakyans, Ambaṭṭha, consider King Okkāka to be their grandfather.

‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, ambaṭṭha, rājā okkāko yā sā mahesī piyā manāpā, tassā puttassa rajjaṃ pariṇāmetukāmo jeṭṭhakumāre raṭṭhasmā pabbājesi – okkāmukhaṃ karakaṇḍaṃ [ukkāmukhaṃ karakaṇḍuṃ (sī. syā.)] hatthinikaṃ sinisūraṃ [sinipuraṃ (sī. syā.)]. Te raṭṭhasmā pabbājitā himavantapasse pokkharaṇiyā tīre mahāsākasaṇḍo, tattha vāsaṃ kappesuṃ. Te jātisambhedabhayā sakāhi bhaginīhi saddhiṃ saṃvāsaṃ kappesuṃ.

"Once upon a time, Ambaṭṭha, King Okkāka, wishing to hand over the kingdom to the son of his beloved and dear queen consort, banished the elder princes from the kingdom—Okkāmukha, Karakaṇḍa, Hatthinika, and Sinisūra. Banished from the kingdom, they made their dwelling on a slope of the Himalayas, on the bank of a lotus pond, in a large grove of sāka trees. Fearing the mingling of lineages, they cohabited with their own sisters.

‘‘Atha kho, ambaṭṭha, rājā okkāko amacce pārisajje āmantesi – ‘kahaṃ nu kho, bho, etarahi kumārā sammantī’ti? ‘Atthi, deva, himavantapasse pokkharaṇiyā tīre mahāsākasaṇḍo, tatthetarahi kumārā sammanti. Te jātisambhedabhayā sakāhi bhaginīhi saddhiṃ saṃvāsaṃ kappentī’ti. Atha kho, ambaṭṭha, rājā okkāko udānaṃ udānesi – ‘sakyā vata, bho, kumārā, paramasakyā vata, bho, kumārā’ti. Tadagge kho pana ambaṭṭha sakyā paññāyanti; so ca nesaṃ pubbapuriso.

"Then, Ambaṭṭha, King Okkāka addressed his ministers and courtiers: 'Where, sirs, are the princes now dwelling?' 'Sire, on the slopes of the Himalayas by the shore of a lotus pond, in a great grove of sāka trees, there the princes are now dwelling. Fearing the mixing of lineages, they practice living together with their own sisters.' Then, Ambaṭṭha, King Okkāka uttered this inspired utterance: 'Truly capable, sirs, are the princes! Truly capable, sirs, are the princes!' From that time on, Ambaṭṭha, they have been known as Sakyans; and he was their ancestor."

‘‘Rañño kho pana, ambaṭṭha, okkākassa disā nāma dāsī ahosi. Sā kaṇhaṃ nāma [sā kaṇhaṃ (pī.)] janesi. Jāto kaṇho pabyāhāsi – ‘dhovatha maṃ, amma, nahāpetha maṃ amma, imasmā maṃ asucismā parimocetha, atthāya vo bhavissāmī’ti. Yathā kho pana ambaṭṭha etarahi manussā pisāce disvā ‘pisācā’ti sañjānanti; evameva kho, ambaṭṭha, tena kho pana samayena manussā pisāce ‘kaṇhā’ti sañjānanti. Te evamāhaṃsu – ‘ayaṃ jāto pabyāhāsi, kaṇho jāto, pisāco jāto’ti. Tadagge kho pana, ambaṭṭha kaṇhāyanā paññāyanti, so ca kaṇhāyanānaṃ pubbapuriso. Iti kho te, ambaṭṭha, porāṇaṃ mātāpettikaṃ nāmagottaṃ anussarato ayyaputtā sakyā bhavanti, dāsiputto tvamasi sakyāna’’nti.

"Now, Ambaṭṭha, King Okkāka had a slave girl named Disā. She gave birth to a dark-skinned child named Kaṇha. When born, Kaṇha spoke thus: 'Wash me, mother! Bathe me, mother! Free me from this impurity! I will be of use to you!' Just as nowadays, Ambaṭṭha, people seeing a goblin recognize it as a 'pisāca,' so too in those days people recognized goblins as 'kaṇha.' They said: 'This one spoke at birth—a kaṇha is born, a pisāca is born!' From that time on, Ambaṭṭha, they have been known as Kaṇhāyanas, and he was the first ancestor of the Kaṇhāyanas. Thus, Ambaṭṭha, recalling their ancient maternal and paternal lineage, these Sakyans are noble sons, while you are the son of a slave girl to the Sakyans."

268. Evaṃ vutte, te māṇavakā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘mā bhavaṃ gotamo ambaṭṭhaṃ atibāḷhaṃ dāsiputtavādena nimmādesi. Sujāto ca, bho gotama ambaṭṭho māṇavo, kulaputto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, bahussuto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, kalyāṇavākkaraṇo ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, paṇḍito ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, pahoti ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhotā gotamena saddhiṃ asmiṃ vacane paṭimantetu’’nti.

268.When this was said, those youths said to the Blessed One: "Let not Master Gotama excessively disparage Ambaṭṭha with the term 'slave-born.' For Ambaṭṭha, Master Gotama, is well-born, a youth of good family, learned, eloquent, wise, and capable of conversing with Master Gotama on this matter."

269. Atha kho bhagavā te māṇavake etadavoca – ‘‘sace kho tumhākaṃ māṇavakānaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘dujjāto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, akulaputto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, appassuto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, akalyāṇavākkaraṇo ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, duppañño ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, na ca pahoti ambaṭṭho māṇavo samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ asmiṃ vacane paṭimantetu’nti, tiṭṭhatu ambaṭṭho māṇavo, tumhe mayā saddhiṃ mantavho asmiṃ vacane. Sace pana tumhākaṃ māṇavakānaṃ evaṃ hoti – ‘sujāto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, kulaputto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, bahussuto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, kalyāṇavākkaraṇo ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, paṇḍito ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, pahoti ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ asmiṃ vacane paṭimantetu’nti, tiṭṭhatha tumhe; ambaṭṭho māṇavo mayā saddhiṃ paṭimantetū’’ti.

269.Then the Blessed One said to those young brahmins: "If you young brahmins think: 'Ambaṭṭha is of low birth, Ambaṭṭha is not from a good family, Ambaṭṭha is unlearned, Ambaṭṭha is unskilled in speech, Ambaṭṭha is unwise, and Ambaṭṭha is not capable of arguing with the ascetic Gotama on this matter,' then let Ambaṭṭha be. You discuss this matter with me. But if you think: 'Ambaṭṭha is of high birth, Ambaṭṭha is from a good family, Ambaṭṭha is learned, Ambaṭṭha is skilled in speech, Ambaṭṭha is wise, and Ambaṭṭha is capable of arguing with the ascetic Gotama on this matter,' then you stay aside. Let Ambaṭṭha argue with me."

‘‘Sujāto ca, bho gotama, ambaṭṭho māṇavo, kulaputto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, bahussuto ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, kalyāṇavākkaraṇo ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, paṇḍito ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo, pahoti ca ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhotā gotamena saddhiṃ asmiṃ vacane paṭimantetuṃ, tuṇhī mayaṃ bhavissāma, ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhotā gotamena saddhiṃ asmiṃ vacane paṭimantetū’’ti.

"Venerable Gotama, Ambaṭṭha the brahmin youth is well-born, Ambaṭṭha the brahmin youth is from a good family, Ambaṭṭha the brahmin youth is learned, Ambaṭṭha the brahmin youth is skilled in speaking, Ambaṭṭha the brahmin youth is wise, and Ambaṭṭha the brahmin youth is capable of engaging in debate with the Venerable Gotama on this matter. We will remain silent; let Ambaṭṭha the brahmin youth engage in debate with the Venerable Gotama."

270. Atha kho bhagavā ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ayaṃ kho pana te, ambaṭṭha, sahadhammiko pañho āgacchati, akāmā byākātabbo. Sace tvaṃ na byākarissasi, aññena vā aññaṃ paṭicarissasi, tuṇhī vā bhavissasi, pakkamissasi vā ettheva te sattadhā muddhā phalissati. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, kinti te sutaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ kutopabhutikā kaṇhāyanā, ko ca kaṇhāyanānaṃ pubbapuriso’’ti?

270.Then the Blessed One said to Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin: "Now, Ambaṭṭha, a question consistent with the Dhamma comes to you, which you must answer whether you like it or not. If you do not answer, or evade it by answering something else, or remain silent, or leave, your head will split into seven pieces right here. What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? What have you heard from the elderly brahmins, the aged and venerable, the teachers and their teachers, when they speak of the origin of the Kaṇhāyana clan? Who was the first ancestor of the Kaṇhāyanas?"

Evaṃ vutte, ambaṭṭho māṇavo tuṇhī ahosi. Dutiyampi kho bhagavā ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, kinti te sutaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ kutopabhutikā kaṇhāyanā, ko ca kaṇhāyanānaṃ pubbapuriso’’ti? Dutiyampi kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo tuṇhī ahosi. Atha kho bhagavā ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘byākarohi dāni ambaṭṭha, na dāni, te tuṇhībhāvassa kālo. Yo kho, ambaṭṭha, tathāgatena yāvatatiyakaṃ sahadhammikaṃ pañhaṃ puṭṭho na byākaroti, etthevassa sattadhā muddhā phalissatī’’ti.

When this was said, Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin remained silent. A second time, the Blessed One said to Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin: "What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? What have you heard from the elder brahmins, the old and senior teachers, when they speak of the origin of the Kaṇhāyana clan? Who was the earliest ancestor of the Kaṇhāyanas?" A second time, Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin remained silent. Then the Blessed One said to Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin: "Speak now, Ambaṭṭha; this is no time for silence. Whoever, when asked a reasoned question by the Tathāgata up to the third time, does not answer—his head will split into seven pieces right here."

271. Tena kho pana samayena vajirapāṇī yakkho mahantaṃ ayokūṭaṃ ādāya ādittaṃ sampajjalitaṃ sajotibhūtaṃ [sañjotibhūtaṃ (syā.)] ambaṭṭhassa māṇavassa upari vehāsaṃ ṭhito hoti – ‘‘sacāyaṃ ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhagavatā yāvatatiyakaṃ sahadhammikaṃ pañhaṃ puṭṭho na byākarissati, etthevassa sattadhā muddhaṃ phālessāmī’’ti. Taṃ kho pana vajirapāṇiṃ yakkhaṃ bhagavā ceva passati ambaṭṭho ca māṇavo.

271.Now at that time, Sakka, the king of devas, in the form of a yakkha named Vajirapāṇi, holding a massive iron club, ablaze, burning, and glowing, stood in the air above Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin, thinking: "If this Ambaṭṭha does not answer the Blessed One’s question on a point of doctrine when asked up to the third time, I will split his head into seven pieces right here." Both the Blessed One and Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin saw Sakka, the yakkha Vajirapāṇi.

272. Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhīto saṃviggo lomahaṭṭhajāto bhagavantaṃyeva tāṇaṃ gavesī bhagavantaṃyeva leṇaṃ gavesī bhagavantaṃyeva saraṇaṃ gavesī – upanisīditvā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kimetaṃ [kiṃ me taṃ (ka.)] bhavaṃ gotamo āha? Punabhavaṃ gotamo bravitū’’ti [brūtu (syā.)].

272.Then Ambaṭṭha the youth, frightened, agitated, his hair standing on end, sought refuge in the Blessed One alone, sought shelter in the Blessed One alone, sought protection in the Blessed One alone. Having sat down near the Blessed One, he said this to the Blessed One: "What is it, Venerable Gotama, that you said? May the Venerable Gotama say it again."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, kinti te sutaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ kutopabhutikā kaṇhāyanā, ko ca kaṇhāyanānaṃ pubbapuriso’’ti? ‘‘Evameva me, bho gotama, sutaṃ yatheva bhavaṃ gotamo āha. Tatopabhutikā kaṇhāyanā; so ca kaṇhāyanānaṃ pubbapuriso’’ti.

"What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? What have you heard from the brahmins, the old and senior ones, the teachers and their teachers, when they speak? Where did the Kaṇhāyanas originate, and who was the first ancestor of the Kaṇhāyanas?""Just as the Venerable Gotama has said, so I have heard. The Kaṇhāyanas originated from there, and he was the first ancestor of the Kaṇhāyanas."

## Ambaṭṭhavaṃsakathā

### The Story of Ambaṭṭha's Lineage

273. Evaṃ vutte, te māṇavakā unnādino uccāsaddamahāsaddā ahesuṃ – ‘‘dujjāto kira, bho, ambaṭṭho māṇavo; akulaputto kira, bho, ambaṭṭho māṇavo; dāsiputto kira, bho, ambaṭṭho māṇavo sakyānaṃ. Ayyaputtā kira, bho, ambaṭṭhassa māṇavassa sakyā bhavanti. Dhammavādiṃyeva kira mayaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ apasādetabbaṃ amaññimhā’’ti.

273.When this was said, those young brahmins became loud and noisy, making a great uproar: “It seems, friends, that the young Ambaṭṭha is ill-born; it seems, friends, that the young Ambaṭṭha is of ignoble lineage; it seems, friends, that the young Ambaṭṭha is the son of a Sakyan slave woman. It seems, friends, that the Sakyans are the maternal uncles of the young Ambaṭṭha. We thought we were disparaging the ascetic Gotama, who speaks according to the Dhamma.”

274. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘atibāḷhaṃ kho ime māṇavakā ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ dāsiputtavādena nimmādenti, yaṃnūnāhaṃ parimoceyya’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā te māṇavake etadavoca – ‘‘mā kho tumhe, māṇavakā, ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ atibāḷhaṃ dāsiputtavādena nimmādetha. Uḷāro so kaṇho isi ahosi. So dakkhiṇajanapadaṃ gantvā brahmamante adhīyitvā rājānaṃ okkākaṃ upasaṅkamitvā maddarūpiṃ dhītaraṃ yāci. Tassa rājā okkāko – ‘ko nevaṃ re ayaṃ mayhaṃ dāsiputto samāno maddarūpiṃ dhītaraṃ yācatī’’’ ti, kupito anattamano khurappaṃ sannayhi [sannahi (ka.)]. So taṃ khurappaṃ neva asakkhi muñcituṃ, no paṭisaṃharituṃ.

274.Then it occurred to the Blessed One: “These young brahmins are excessively disparaging Ambaṭṭha with the accusation of being a slave’s son. Let me rescue him.” Then the Blessed One said to those young brahmins: “Do not, young brahmins, excessively disparage Ambaṭṭha with the accusation of being a slave’s son. That Kaṇha was a noble seer. He went to the southern region, studied the brahmin mantras, approached King Okkāka, and asked for his daughter Maddarūpī. King Okkāka, angry and displeased, thought: ‘Who is this, a slave’s son, presuming to ask for my daughter Maddarūpī?’ and strung a razor-arrow. But he could neither release it nor withdraw it.”

‘‘Atha kho, māṇavakā, amaccā pārisajjā kaṇhaṃ isiṃ upasaṅkamitvā etadavocuṃ – ‘sotthi, bhaddante [bhadante (sī. syā.)], hotu rañño; sotthi, bhaddante, hotu rañño’ti. ‘Sotthi bhavissati rañño, api ca rājā yadi adho khurappaṃ muñcissati, yāvatā rañño vijitaṃ, ettāvatā pathavī undriyissatī’ti. ‘Sotthi, bhaddante, hotu rañño, sotthi janapadassā’ti. ‘Sotthi bhavissati rañño, sotthi janapadassa, api ca rājā yadi uddhaṃ khurappaṃ muñcissati, yāvatā rañño vijitaṃ, ettāvatā satta vassāni devo na vassissatī’ti. ‘Sotthi, bhaddante, hotu rañño sotthi janapadassa devo ca vassatū’ti. ‘Sotthi bhavissati rañño sotthi janapadassa devo ca vassissati, api ca rājā jeṭṭhakumāre khurappaṃ patiṭṭhāpetu, sotthi kumāro pallomo bhavissatī’ti. Atha kho, māṇavakā, amaccā okkākassa ārocesuṃ – ‘okkāko jeṭṭhakumāre khurappaṃ patiṭṭhāpetu. Sotthi kumāro pallomo bhavissatī’ti. Atha kho rājā okkāko jeṭṭhakumāre khurappaṃ patiṭṭhapesi, sotthi kumāro pallomo samabhavi. Atha kho tassa rājā okkāko bhīto saṃviggo lomahaṭṭhajāto brahmadaṇḍena tajjito maddarūpiṃ dhītaraṃ adāsi. Mā kho tumhe, māṇavakā, ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ atibāḷhaṃ dāsiputtavādena nimmādetha, uḷāro so kaṇho isi ahosī’’ti.

“Then, young men, the ministers and courtiers approached the dark sage and said: ‘May the king be well, venerable sir! May the king be well!’ ‘The king will be well, but if the king releases the razor-arrow downwards, as far as the king’s dominion extends, the earth will be shattered.’ ‘May the king be well, venerable sir! May the country be well!’ ‘The king will be well, the country will be well, but if the king releases the razor-arrow upwards, as far as the king’s dominion extends, for seven years the rain god will not rain.’ ‘May the king be well, venerable sir! May the country be well! And may the rain god rain!’ ‘The king will be well, the country will be well, and the rain god will rain, but let the king fix the razor-arrow in the eldest prince. Then the prince will be safe and undisturbed.’ Then, young men, the ministers informed King Okkāka: ‘Let King Okkāka fix the razor-arrow in the eldest prince. The prince will be safe and undisturbed.’ Then King Okkāka fixed the razor-arrow in the eldest prince, and the prince became safe and undisturbed. Then King Okkāka, frightened, terrified, with hair standing on end, threatened by the divine punishment, gave his daughter Maddarūpī in marriage. Do not, young men, excessively insult the young man Ambaṭṭha by calling him a slave’s son. That dark sage was noble.”

## Khattiyaseṭṭhabhāvo

### The Khattiya’s Superiority

275. Atha kho bhagavā ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, idha khattiyakumāro brāhmaṇakaññāya saddhiṃ saṃvāsaṃ kappeyya, tesaṃ saṃvāsamanvāya putto jāyetha. Yo so khattiyakumārena brāhmaṇakaññāya putto uppanno, api nu so labhetha brāhmaṇesu āsanaṃ vā udakaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Labhetha, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā bhojeyyuṃ saddhe vā thālipāke vā yaññe vā pāhune vā’’ti? ‘‘Bhojeyyuṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā mante vāceyyuṃ vā no vā’’ti? ‘‘Vāceyyuṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinussa itthīsu āvaṭaṃ vā assa anāvaṭaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Anāvaṭaṃ hissa, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ khattiyā khattiyābhisekena abhisiñceyyu’’nti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Taṃ kissa hetu’’? ‘‘Mātito hi, bho gotama, anupapanno’’ti.

275.Then the Blessed One addressed the young man Ambaṭṭha: “What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? If a young nobleman were to cohabit with a brahmin maiden, and a son were born to them as a result, would that son born of the young nobleman and the brahmin maiden obtain a seat or water among brahmins?” “He would, Master Gotama.” “Would the brahmins feed him at a meal offered in faith, or a pot-meal, or a sacrifice, or as a guest?” “They would, Master Gotama.” “Would the brahmins recite the mantras to him, or not?” “They would, Master Gotama.” “Would there be an obstruction for him with regard to women, or would there be no obstruction?” “There would be no obstruction for him, Master Gotama.” “Would the nobles anoint him with the consecration of a noble?” “Certainly not, Master Gotama.” “What is the reason for that?” “Because, Master Gotama, he is deficient on his mother’s side.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, idha brāhmaṇakumāro khattiyakaññāya saddhiṃ saṃvāsaṃ kappeyya, tesaṃ saṃvāsamanvāya putto jāyetha. Yo so brāhmaṇakumārena khattiyakaññāya putto uppanno, apinu so labhetha brāhmaṇesu āsanaṃ vā udakaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Labhetha, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā bhojeyyuṃ saddhe vā thālipāke vā yaññe vā pāhune vā’’ti? ‘‘Bhojeyyuṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā mante vāceyyuṃ vā no vā’’ti? ‘‘Vāceyyuṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinussa itthīsu āvaṭaṃ vā assa anāvaṭaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Anāvaṭaṃ hissa, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ khattiyā khattiyābhisekena abhisiñceyyu’’nti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Taṃ kissa hetu’’? ‘‘Pitito hi, bho gotama, anupapanno’’ti.

“What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? Suppose a brahmin youth were to cohabit with a khattiya maiden, and from their cohabitation a son were born. Would that son born from the brahmin youth and the khattiya maiden receive a seat or water among the brahmins?” “He would, Master Gotama.” “And would the brahmins feed him at a faith-offering, a pot-meal, a sacrifice, or a guest-meal?” “They would, Master Gotama.” “And would the brahmins recite the mantras to him or not?” “They would, Master Gotama.” “And would he have access to women or not?” “He would have access, Master Gotama.” “But would the khattiyas anoint him with the khattiya anointing?” “No, Master Gotama.” “What is the reason for that?” “Because, Master Gotama, he is deficient on his father’s side.”

276. ‘‘Iti kho, ambaṭṭha, itthiyā vā itthiṃ karitvā purisena vā purisaṃ karitvā khattiyāva seṭṭhā, hīnā brāhmaṇā. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, idha brāhmaṇā brāhmaṇaṃ kismiñcideva pakaraṇe khuramuṇḍaṃ karitvā bhassapuṭena vadhitvā raṭṭhā vā nagarā vā pabbājeyyuṃ. Apinu so labhetha brāhmaṇesu āsanaṃ vā udakaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā bhojeyyuṃ saddhe vā thālipāke vā yaññe vā pāhune vā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā mante vāceyyuṃ vā no vā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinussa itthīsu āvaṭaṃ vā assa anāvaṭaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Āvaṭaṃ hissa, bho gotama’’.

276.“So, Ambaṭṭha, whether it be a woman or a man, the Khattiyas are the best, and the Brahmins are inferior. What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? Suppose here Brahmins were to shave a Brahmin’s head for some offense, smear him with ashes and beat him, and banish him from the country or the city. Would he still receive a seat or water among Brahmins?” “No, indeed, Master Gotama.” “Would Brahmins still feed him at a meal of faith, a pot-meal, a sacrifice, or a guest offering?” “No, indeed, Master Gotama.” “Would Brahmins still recite the mantras to him or not?” “No, indeed, Master Gotama.” “Would he still have access to women or not?” “It would be restricted for him, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, idha khattiyā khattiyaṃ kismiñcideva pakaraṇe khuramuṇḍaṃ karitvā bhassapuṭena vadhitvā raṭṭhā vā nagarā vā pabbājeyyuṃ. Apinu so labhetha brāhmaṇesu āsanaṃ vā udakaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Labhetha, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā bhojeyyuṃ saddhe vā thālipāke vā yaññe vā pāhune vā’’ti? ‘‘Bhojeyyuṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinu naṃ brāhmaṇā mante vāceyyuṃ vā no vā’’ti? ‘‘Vāceyyuṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Apinussa itthīsu āvaṭaṃ vā assa anāvaṭaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘Anāvaṭaṃ hissa, bho gotama’’.

“What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? Suppose a noble were to shave the head of another noble for some offense, smear him with ashes and beat him, and banish him from the realm or city. Would he still receive a seat or water among brahmins?” “He would, Master Gotama.” “Would the brahmins still feed him at a meal of faith, a pot-meal, a sacrifice, or a guest-offering?” “They would, Master Gotama.” “Would the brahmins still recite the mantras to him or not?” “They would, Master Gotama.” “Would women be restricted or unrestricted in his presence?” “Unrestricted, Master Gotama.”

277. ‘‘Ettāvatā kho, ambaṭṭha, khattiyo paramanihīnataṃ patto hoti, yadeva naṃ khattiyā khuramuṇḍaṃ karitvā bhassapuṭena vadhitvā raṭṭhā vā nagarā vā pabbājenti. Iti kho, ambaṭṭha, yadā khattiyo paramanihīnataṃ patto hoti, tadāpi khattiyāva seṭṭhā, hīnā brāhmaṇā. Brahmunā pesā, ambaṭṭha [brahmunāpi ambaṭṭha (ka.), brahmunāpi eso ambaṭṭha (pī.)], sanaṅkumārena gāthā bhāsitā –

277.“Thus far, Ambaṭṭha, a noble reaches the lowest degradation when other nobles shave his head, smear him with ashes and beat him, and banish him from the country or city. Thus, Ambaṭṭha, even when a noble reaches the lowest degradation, still the nobles are the best, and the brahmins are inferior. This verse, Ambaṭṭha, was uttered by Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra:

‘Khattiyo seṭṭho janetasmiṃ,Ye gottapaṭisārino;Vijjācaraṇasampanno,So seṭṭho devamānuse’ti.

The noble is best among people who value lineage;
But one accomplished in wisdom and conduct
Is supreme among gods and men.

‘‘Sā kho panesā, ambaṭṭha, brahmunā sanaṅkumārena gāthā sugītā no duggītā, subhāsitā no dubbhāsitā, atthasaṃhitā no anatthasaṃhitā, anumatā mayā. Ahampi hi, ambaṭṭha, evaṃ vadāmi –

“Indeed, Ambaṭṭha, that verse was well-sung by Brahmā Sanaṅkumāra, not poorly sung; well-spoken, not poorly spoken; meaningful and not meaningless; approved by me. For I too, Ambaṭṭha, say this:

‘Khattiyo seṭṭho janetasmiṃ,Ye gottapaṭisārino;Vijjācaraṇasampanno,So seṭṭho devamānuse’ti.

The noble is best among people,
For those who value lineage;
But one perfected in knowledge and conduct
Is supreme among gods and humans.

## Bhāṇavāro paṭhamo.

### The First Recitation Section.

## Vijjācaraṇakathā

### The Discourse on Knowledge and Conduct.

278. ‘‘Katamaṃ pana taṃ, bho gotama, caraṇaṃ, katamā ca pana sā vijjā’’ti? ‘‘Na kho, ambaṭṭha, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya jātivādo vā vuccati, gottavādo vā vuccati, mānavādo vā vuccati – ‘arahasi vā maṃ tvaṃ, na vā maṃ tvaṃ arahasī’ti. Yattha kho, ambaṭṭha, āvāho vā hoti, vivāho vā hoti, āvāhavivāho vā hoti, etthetaṃ vuccati jātivādo vā itipi gottavādo vā itipi mānavādo vā itipi – ‘arahasi vā maṃ tvaṃ, na vā maṃ tvaṃ arahasī’ti. Ye hi keci ambaṭṭha jātivādavinibaddhā vā gottavādavinibaddhā vā mānavādavinibaddhā vā āvāhavivāhavinibaddhā vā, ārakā te anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya. Pahāya kho, ambaṭṭha, jātivādavinibaddhañca gottavādavinibaddhañca mānavādavinibaddhañca āvāhavivāhavinibaddhañca anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya sacchikiriyā hotī’’ti.

278."But what, Venerable Gotama, is that conduct, and what is that knowledge?" "Ambaṭṭha, for the unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, there is no discourse on birth, no discourse on lineage, and no discourse on conceit—'You are worthy of me, or you are not worthy of me.' Where there are marriage arrangements, Ambaṭṭha, there this is said: 'discourse on birth,' 'discourse on lineage,' 'discourse on conceit'—'You are worthy of me, or you are not worthy of me.' Ambaṭṭha, those who are bound by discourse on birth, bound by discourse on lineage, bound by discourse on conceit, bound by marriage arrangements—they are far from the unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct. By abandoning discourse on birth, discourse on lineage, discourse on conceit, and marriage arrangements, the unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct is realized."

279. ‘‘Katamaṃ pana taṃ, bho gotama, caraṇaṃ, katamā ca sā vijjā’’ti? ‘‘Idha, ambaṭṭha, tathāgato loke uppajjati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedeti. So dhammaṃ deseti ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāseti. Taṃ dhammaṃ suṇāti gahapati vā gahapatiputto vā aññatarasmiṃ vā kule paccājāto. So taṃ dhammaṃ sutvā tathāgate saddhaṃ paṭilabhati. So tena saddhāpaṭilābhena samannāgato iti paṭisañcikkhati…pe… (yathā 191 ādayo anucchedā, evaṃ vitthāretabbaṃ).…

279."But what, Venerable Gotama, is that conduct, and what is that knowledge?" "Here, Ambaṭṭha, a Tathāgata arises in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, perfect in knowledge and conduct, a Fortunate One, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of persons to be tamed, teacher of devas and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One. He makes known this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, this generation with its recluses and brahmins, its devas and humans, having realized it by his own direct knowledge. He teaches the Dhamma which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with its meaning and its phrasing, complete and perfectly pure; and he reveals the holy life. A householder or a householder's son or one born into some other family hears that Dhamma. Having heard the Dhamma, he gains faith in the Tathāgata. Possessing that faith, he considers thus…(as in section 191 and following, to be elaborated in detail)…"

‘‘So vivicceva kāmehi, vivicca akusalehi dhammehi, savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati…pe… idampissa hoti caraṇasmiṃ.

"Quite secluded from sensual pleasures, secluded from unwholesome states, he enters and dwells in the first jhāna, which is accompanied by applied thought and sustained thought, with rapture and happiness born of seclusion… This, too, is part of his conduct."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ambaṭṭha, bhikkhu vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā ajjhattaṃ sampasādanaṃ cetaso ekodibhāvaṃ avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhijaṃ pītisukhaṃ dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati…pe… idampissa hoti caraṇasmiṃ.

"Furthermore, Ambaṭṭha, with the stilling of applied thought and sustained thought, a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the second jhāna, which has internal assurance and unification of mind, is without applied thought and sustained thought, and has rapture and happiness born of samādhi… This, too, is part of his conduct."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ambaṭṭha, bhikkhu pītiyā ca virāgā upekkhako ca viharati sato ca sampajāno, sukhañca kāyena paṭisaṃvedeti, yaṃ taṃ ariyā ācikkhanti – ‘‘upekkhako satimā sukhavihārī’ti, tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati…pe… idampissa hoti caraṇasmiṃ.

"Furthermore, Ambaṭṭha, with the fading away of rapture, a bhikkhu dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending, experiencing happiness with the body; he enters and dwells in the third jhāna, of which the noble ones declare: 'He is equanimous, mindful, one who dwells happily.'… This, too, is part of his conduct."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ambaṭṭha, bhikkhu sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā, pubbeva somanassadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamā adukkhamasukhaṃ upekkhāsatipārisuddhiṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati…pe… idampissa hoti caraṇasmiṃ. Idaṃ kho taṃ, ambaṭṭha, caraṇaṃ.

"Furthermore, Ambaṭṭha, with the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and with the disappearance beforehand of joy and sorrow, a bhikkhu enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna, which is neither painful nor pleasant and features the purity of mindfulness due to equanimity… This, too, is part of his conduct. This, Ambaṭṭha, is that conduct."

‘‘So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte ñāṇadassanāya cittaṃ abhinīharati abhininnāmeti…pe… idampissa hoti vijjāya…pe… nāparaṃ itthattāyāti pajānāti, idampissa hoti vijjāya. Ayaṃ kho sā, ambaṭṭha, vijjā.

"When his mind is thus collected, purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilements, pliable, workable, steady, and has attained imperturbability, he directs and inclines it to knowledge and vision… This, too, is his knowledge… He understands: 'Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more beyond this.' This, too, is his knowledge. This, Ambaṭṭha, is that knowledge."

‘‘Ayaṃ vuccati, ambaṭṭha, bhikkhu ‘vijjāsampanno’ itipi, ‘caraṇasampanno’ itipi, ‘vijjācaraṇasampanno’ itipi. Imāya ca ambaṭṭha vijjāsampadāya caraṇasampadāya ca aññā vijjāsampadā ca caraṇasampadā ca uttaritarā vā paṇītatarā vā natthi.

"This, Ambaṭṭha, is called a bhikkhu 'perfect in knowledge,' 'perfect in conduct,' and 'perfect in knowledge and conduct.' And, Ambaṭṭha, there is no other perfection in knowledge or conduct higher or more excellent than this perfection in knowledge and conduct."

## Catuapāyamukhaṃ

### The Four Avenues to Ruin.

280. ‘‘Imāya kho, ambaṭṭha, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya cattāri apāyamukhāni bhavanti. Katamāni cattāri? Idha, ambaṭṭha, ekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā imaññeva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno khārividhamādāya [khārivividhamādāya (sī. syā. pī.)] araññāyatanaṃ ajjhogāhati – ‘pavattaphalabhojano bhavissāmī’ti. So aññadatthu vijjācaraṇasampannasseva paricārako sampajjati. Imāya kho, ambaṭṭha, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya idaṃ paṭhamaṃ apāyamukhaṃ bhavati.

280.“Ambaṭṭha, there are four avenues to ruin in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct. What are the four? Here, Ambaṭṭha, some ascetic or brahmin, not having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, takes a load of ascetic’s requisites and enters a forest dwelling, thinking: ‘I will live on fallen fruits.’ He merely becomes an attendant of one accomplished in knowledge and conduct. This, Ambaṭṭha, is the first avenue to ruin in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ambaṭṭha, idhekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā imañceva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno pavattaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno kudālapiṭakaṃ [kuddālapiṭakaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)] ādāya araññavanaṃ ajjhogāhati – ‘kandamūlaphalabhojano bhavissāmī’ti. So aññadatthu vijjācaraṇasampannasseva paricārako sampajjati. Imāya kho, ambaṭṭha, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya idaṃ dutiyaṃ apāyamukhaṃ bhavati.

“Furthermore, Ambaṭṭha, here some ascetic or brahmin, not having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, and not having attained the practice of living on fallen fruits, takes a spade and basket and enters a forest dwelling, thinking: ‘I will live on roots, tubers, and fruits.’ He merely becomes an attendant of one accomplished in knowledge and conduct. This, Ambaṭṭha, is the second avenue to ruin in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ambaṭṭha, idhekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā imañceva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno pavattaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno kandamūlaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno gāmasāmantaṃ vā nigamasāmantaṃ vā agyāgāraṃ karitvā aggiṃ paricaranto acchati. So aññadatthu vijjācaraṇasampannasseva paricārako sampajjati. Imāya kho, ambaṭṭha, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya idaṃ tatiyaṃ apāyamukhaṃ bhavati.

“Furthermore, Ambaṭṭha, here some ascetic or brahmin, not having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, not having attained the practice of living on fallen fruits, and not having attained the practice of living on roots, tubers, and fruits, builds a fire-shrine at the edge of a village or town and attends the fire there. He merely becomes an attendant of one accomplished in knowledge and conduct. This, Ambaṭṭha, is the third avenue to ruin in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ambaṭṭha, idhekacco samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā imaṃ ceva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno pavattaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno kandamūlaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno aggipāricariyañca anabhisambhuṇamāno cātumahāpathe catudvāraṃ agāraṃ karitvā acchati – ‘yo imāhi catūhi disāhi āgamissati samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā, tamahaṃ yathāsatti yathābalaṃ paṭipūjessāmī’ti. So aññadatthu vijjācaraṇasampannasseva paricārako sampajjati. Imāya kho, ambaṭṭha, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya idaṃ catutthaṃ apāyamukhaṃ bhavati. Imāya kho, ambaṭṭha, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya imāni cattāri apāyamukhāni bhavanti.

“Furthermore, Ambaṭṭha, here some ascetic or brahmin, not having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, and not having attained the practice of living on fallen fruits, and not having attained the practice of living on roots, tubers, and fruits, and not having attained attending the fire, builds a house with four doors at the crossroads and dwells there, thinking: ‘Whatever ascetic or brahmin comes from these four directions, I will honor them according to my ability and strength.’ He merely becomes an attendant of one accomplished in knowledge and conduct. This, Ambaṭṭha, is the fourth avenue to ruin in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct. Thus, Ambaṭṭha, these are the four avenues to ruin in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct.

281. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, apinu tvaṃ imāya anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya sandissasi sācariyako’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘Kocāhaṃ, bho gotama, sācariyako, kā ca anuttarā vijjācaraṇasampadā? Ārakāhaṃ, bho gotama, anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya sācariyako’’ti.

281.“What do you think, Ambaṭṭha, do you and your teacher identify with this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct?” “Certainly not, Master Gotama.” “What am I, Master Gotama, and what is my teacher, that we should possess this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct? Far indeed am I, Master Gotama, from this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, along with my teacher.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, apinu tvaṃ imañceva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno khārividhamādāya araññavanamajjhogāhasi sācariyako – ‘pavattaphalabhojano bhavissāmī’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“What do you think, Ambaṭṭha, without having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, would you, along with your teacher, take a load of ascetic’s requisites and enter a forest dwelling, thinking: ‘I will live on fallen fruits’?” “No, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, apinu tvaṃ imañceva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno pavattaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno kudālapiṭakaṃ ādāya araññavanamajjhogāhasi sācariyako – ‘kandamūlaphalabhojano bhavissāmī’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“What do you think, Ambaṭṭha, without having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, and without having attained the practice of living on fallen fruits, would you, along with your teacher, take a spade and basket and enter a forest dwelling, thinking: ‘I will live on roots, tubers, and fruits’?” “No, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, apinu tvaṃ imañceva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno pavattaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno kandamūlaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno gāmasāmantaṃ vā nigamasāmantaṃ vā agyāgāraṃ karitvā aggiṃ paricaranto acchasi sācariyako’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“What do you think, Ambaṭṭha, without having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, and without having attained the practice of living on fallen fruits, and without having attained the practice of living on roots, tubers, and fruits, would you, along with your teacher, build a fire-shrine at the edge of a village or town and attend the fire there?” “No, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, apinu tvaṃ imañceva anuttaraṃ vijjācaraṇasampadaṃ anabhisambhuṇamāno pavattaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno kandamūlaphalabhojanatañca anabhisambhuṇamāno aggipāricariyañca anabhisambhuṇamāno cātumahāpathe catudvāraṃ agāraṃ karitvā acchasi sācariyako – ‘yo imāhi catūhi disāhi āgamissati samaṇo vā brāhmaṇo vā, taṃ mayaṃ yathāsatti yathābalaṃ paṭipūjessāmā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“What do you think, Ambaṭṭha, without having attained this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct, and without having attained the practice of living on fallen fruits, and without having attained the practice of living on roots, tubers, and fruits, and without having attained attending the fire, would you, along with your teacher, build a house with four doors at the crossroads and dwell there, thinking: ‘Whatever ascetic or brahmin comes from these four directions, we will honor them according to our ability and strength’?” “No, Master Gotama.”

282. ‘‘Iti kho, ambaṭṭha, imāya ceva tvaṃ anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya parihīno sācariyako. Ye cime anuttarāya vijjācaraṇasampadāya cattāri apāyamukhāni bhavanti, tato ca tvaṃ parihīno sācariyako. Bhāsitā kho pana te esā, ambaṭṭha, ācariyena brāhmaṇena pokkharasātinā vācā – ‘ke ca muṇḍakā samaṇakā ibbhā kaṇhā bandhupādāpaccā, kā ca tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ sākacchā’ti attanā āpāyikopi aparipūramāno. Passa, ambaṭṭha, yāva aparaddhañca te idaṃ ācariyassa brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa.

282.“Thus, Ambaṭṭha, you, along with your teacher, are lacking in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct. And you, along with your teacher, are lacking in those four avenues to ruin in this unsurpassed perfection of knowledge and conduct. Moreover, Ambaṭṭha, your teacher, the brahmin Pokkharasāti, has spoken these words: ‘Who are these shavelings, these ascetics, these menials, these black-skinned descendants of slaves? What discussion can they have with the brahmins versed in the three Vedas?’—while himself being prone to ruin and unfulfilled. See, Ambaṭṭha, how greatly your teacher, the brahmin Pokkharasāti, has erred.”

## Pubbakaisibhāvānuyogo

### The Practice of the State of Former Seers

283. ‘‘Brāhmaṇo kho pana, ambaṭṭha, pokkharasāti rañño pasenadissa kosalassa dattikaṃ bhuñjati. Tassa rājā pasenadi kosalo sammukhībhāvampi na dadāti. Yadāpi tena manteti, tirodussantena manteti. Yassa kho pana, ambaṭṭha, dhammikaṃ payātaṃ bhikkhaṃ paṭiggaṇheyya, kathaṃ tassa rājā pasenadi kosalo sammukhībhāvampi na dadeyya. Passa, ambaṭṭha, yāva aparaddhañca te idaṃ ācariyassa brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa.

283."Furthermore, Ambaṭṭha, the brahmin Pokkharasāti eats the royal allowance of King Pasenadi of Kosala. Yet King Pasenadi of Kosala does not even grant him a personal interview. And when he consults with him, he does so from behind a screen. But, Ambaṭṭha, how could King Pasenadi of Kosala refuse a personal interview even to one who accepts alms obtained by legitimate means? See, Ambaṭṭha, how wrong your brahmin teacher Pokkharasāti is in this."

284. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, idha rājā pasenadi kosalo hatthigīvāya vā nisinno assapiṭṭhe vā nisinno rathūpatthare vā ṭhito uggehi vā rājaññehi vā kiñcideva mantanaṃ manteyya. So tamhā padesā apakkamma ekamantaṃ tiṭṭheyya. Atha āgaccheyya suddo vā suddadāso vā, tasmiṃ padese ṭhito tadeva mantanaṃ manteyya – ‘evampi rājā pasenadi kosalo āha, evampi rājā pasenadi kosalo āhā’ti. Apinu so rājabhaṇitaṃ vā bhaṇati rājamantanaṃ vā manteti? Ettāvatā so assa rājā vā rājamatto vā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

284."What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? Suppose King Pasenadi of Kosala, seated on an elephant's neck, or on horseback, or standing on a chariot rug, were to discuss some matter with his high-ranking nobles or royalty. Then he might leave that place and stand to one side. Then a low-born person or a slave might come and, standing in that very place, discuss the same matter, saying, ‘Thus said King Pasenadi of Kosala, thus said King Pasenadi of Kosala.’ Would he be speaking the king's words or discussing the king's business? Would he thereby be the king or a royal official?" "No, indeed, Master Gotama."

285. ‘‘Evameva kho tvaṃ, ambaṭṭha, ye te ahesuṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ pubbakā isayo mantānaṃ kattāro mantānaṃ pavattāro, yesamidaṃ etarahi brāhmaṇā porāṇaṃ mantapadaṃ gītaṃ pavuttaṃ samihitaṃ, tadanugāyanti tadanubhāsanti bhāsitamanubhāsanti vācitamanuvācenti, seyyathidaṃ – aṭṭhako vāmako vāmadevo vessāmitto yamataggi [yamadaggi (ka.)] aṅgīraso bhāradvājo vāseṭṭho kassapo bhagu – ‘tyāhaṃ mante adhiyāmi sācariyako’ti, tāvatā tvaṃ bhavissasi isi vā isitthāya vā paṭipannoti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

285."In the same way, Ambaṭṭha, those ancient seers of the brahmins who were the makers of mantras, the founders of mantras, whose ancient verses are now chanted, recited, and compiled by the brahmins, following which they sing and speak, repeating what was spoken and reciting what was recited—such as Aṭṭhaka, Vāmaka, Vāmadeva, Vessāmitta, Yamataggi, Aṅgīrasa, Bhāradvāja, Vāseṭṭha, Kassapa, and Bhagu—thinking ‘I study the mantras with their teachers,’ by that you will not become a seer or one who has entered upon the state of a seer; such a thing is impossible."

286. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, ambaṭṭha, kinti te sutaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ – ye te ahesuṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ pubbakā isayo mantānaṃ kattāro mantānaṃ pavattāro, yesamidaṃ etarahi brāhmaṇā porāṇaṃ mantapadaṃ gītaṃ pavuttaṃ samihitaṃ, tadanugāyanti tadanubhāsanti bhāsitamanubhāsanti vācitamanuvācenti, seyyathidaṃ – aṭṭhako vāmako vāmadevo vessāmitto yamataggi aṅgīraso bhāradvājo vāseṭṭho kassapo bhagu, evaṃ su te sunhātā suvilittā kappitakesamassū āmukkamaṇikuṇḍalābharaṇā [āmuttamālābharaṇā (sī. syā. pī.)] odātavatthavasanā pañcahi kāmaguṇehi samappitā samaṅgībhūtā paricārenti, seyyathāpi tvaṃ etarahi sācariyako’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

286."What do you think, Ambaṭṭha? Have you heard it said by elderly brahmins, old and venerable, teachers of teachers: ‘Those ancient seers of the brahmins, the makers of mantras, the founders of mantras, whose ancient verses are now chanted, recited, and compiled by the brahmins, following which they sing and speak, repeating what was spoken and reciting what was recited—such as Aṭṭhaka, Vāmaka, Vāmadeva, Vessāmitta, Yamataggi, Aṅgīrasa, Bhāradvāja, Vāseṭṭha, Kassapa, and Bhagu: ‘Were they well bathed, well anointed, with hair and beard trimmed, adorned with jeweled earrings and ornaments, wearing white clothes, fully provided and replete with the five strands of sensual pleasure, as you are now with your teacher?’" "No, Master Gotama."

‘‘…Pe… evaṃ su te sālīnaṃ odanaṃ sucimaṃsūpasecanaṃ vicitakāḷakaṃ anekasūpaṃ anekabyañjanaṃ paribhuñjanti, seyyathāpi tvaṃ etarahi sācariyako’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

"...Or that they ate fine rice with pure meat as a relish, with the black grains picked out, and with various curries and side dishes, just as you do now together with your teacher?" "No, indeed, Master Gotama."

‘‘…Pe… evaṃ su te veṭhakanatapassāhi nārīhi paricārenti, seyyathāpi tvaṃ etarahi sācariyako’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

"...Or that they were attended by women with tightly bound waists, just as you are now with your teacher?" "No, indeed, Master Gotama."

‘‘…Pe… evaṃ su te kuttavālehi vaḷavārathehi dīghāhi patodalaṭṭhīhi vāhane vitudentā vipariyāyanti, seyyathāpi tvaṃ etarahi sācariyako’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

"...Or that they rode about in chariots drawn by mares with trimmed tails, urging them with long goads, just as you do now with your teacher?" "No, indeed, Master Gotama."

‘‘…Pe… evaṃ su te ukkiṇṇaparikhāsu okkhittapalighāsu nagarūpakārikāsu dīghāsivudhehi [dīghāsibaddhehi (syā. pī.)] purisehi rakkhāpenti, seyyathāpi tvaṃ etarahi sācariyako’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

"...Or that they were guarded in cities with moats dug deep, with bars dropped, and with defensive plasterwork, protected by men with long swords, just as you are now with your teacher?" "No, indeed, Master Gotama."

‘‘Iti kho, ambaṭṭha, neva tvaṃ isi na isitthāya paṭipanno sācariyako. Yassa kho pana, ambaṭṭha, mayi kaṅkhā vā vimati vā so maṃ pañhena, ahaṃ veyyākaraṇena sodhissāmī’’ti.

"Thus, Ambaṭṭha, you are neither a seer nor one who has entered upon the state of a seer, even with your teacher. But if you have any doubt or uncertainty about me, ask me a question, and I will clarify it for you with an explanation."

## Dvelakkhaṇādassanaṃ

### The Manifestation of the Two Characteristics.

287. Atha kho bhagavā vihārā nikkhamma caṅkamaṃ abbhuṭṭhāsi. Ambaṭṭhopi māṇavo vihārā nikkhamma caṅkamaṃ abbhuṭṭhāsi. Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhagavantaṃ caṅkamantaṃ anucaṅkamamāno bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni samannesi. Addasā kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe pahūtajivhatāya ca.

287.Then the Blessed One, having left the dwelling, set out on the walking path. The young Ambaṭṭha also left the dwelling and set out on the walking path. Then the young Ambaṭṭha, walking back and forth alongside the Blessed One as he walked, scrutinized the thirty-two marks of a great man on the Blessed One's body. The young Ambaṭṭha saw the thirty-two marks of a great man on the Blessed One's body, for the most part, except for two. Regarding two marks of a great man, he was doubtful, wavering, not decided, and not confident—the concealed genitals and the extensive tongue.

288. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘passati kho me ayaṃ ambaṭṭho māṇavo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe pahūtajivhatāya cā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhāsi yathā addasa ambaṭṭho māṇavo bhagavato kosohitaṃ vatthaguyhaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā jivhaṃ ninnāmetvā ubhopi kaṇṇasotāni anumasi paṭimasi, ubhopi nāsikasotāni anumasi paṭimasi, kevalampi nalāṭamaṇḍalaṃ jivhāya chādesi. Atha kho ambaṭṭhassa māṇavassa etadahosi – ‘‘samannāgato kho samaṇo gotamo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi paripuṇṇehi, no aparipuṇṇehī’’ti. Bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘handa ca dāni mayaṃ, bho gotama, gacchāma, bahukiccā mayaṃ bahukaraṇīyā’’ti. ‘‘Yassadāni tvaṃ, ambaṭṭha, kālaṃ maññasī’’ti. Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo vaḷavārathamāruyha pakkāmi.

288.Then it occurred to the Blessed One: "This young Ambaṭṭha sees the thirty-two marks of a great man in me, with the exception of two. He is doubtful, wavering, not decided, and not confident about two marks of a great man: the concealed genitals and the extensive tongue." Then the Blessed One performed such a feat of psychic power that the young Ambaṭṭha saw the Blessed One's concealed genitals. Then the Blessed One, extending his tongue, stroked and massaged both ear-holes, stroked and massaged both nostrils, and covered his entire forehead with his tongue. Then the young Ambaṭṭha thought: "The ascetic Gotama is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man, complete in every way, not incomplete." He said to the Blessed One: "Well then, Master Gotama, we must go now; we have much to do, many duties." "Now, Ambaṭṭha, you may go at your convenience." Then the young Ambaṭṭha mounted his chariot drawn by a mare and departed.

289. Tena kho pana samayena brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti ukkaṭṭhāya nikkhamitvā mahatā brāhmaṇagaṇena saddhiṃ sake ārāme nisinno hoti ambaṭṭhaṃyeva māṇavaṃ paṭimānento. Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo yena sako ārāmo tena pāyāsi. Yāvatikā yānassa bhūmi, yānena gantvā yānā paccorohitvā pattikova yena brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā brāhmaṇaṃ pokkharasātiṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi.

289.At that time, the brahmin Pokkharasāti had left Ukkaṭṭhā and was sitting in his own park with a large assembly of brahmins, expecting the young Ambaṭṭha. Then the young Ambaṭṭha went to his own park. He went by chariot as far as the ground allowed, then dismounted from the chariot and approached the brahmin Pokkharasāti on foot. Having approached, he greeted the brahmin Pokkharasāti and sat down to one side.

290. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti etadavoca – ‘‘kacci, tāta ambaṭṭha, addasa taṃ bhavantaṃ gotama’’nti? ‘‘Addasāma kho mayaṃ, bho, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotama’’nti. ‘‘Kacci, tāta ambaṭṭha, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ tathā santaṃyeva saddo abbhuggato no aññathā; kacci pana so bhavaṃ gotamo tādiso no aññādiso’’ti? ‘‘Tathā santaṃyeva, bho, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saddo abbhuggato no aññathā, tādisova so bhavaṃ gotamo no aññādiso. Samannāgato ca so bhavaṃ gotamo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi paripuṇṇehi no aparipuṇṇehī’’ti. ‘‘Ahu pana te, tāta ambaṭṭha, samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti? ‘‘Ahu kho me, bho, samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana te, tāta ambaṭṭha, ahu samaṇena gotamena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti? Atha kho ambaṭṭho māṇavo yāvatako [yāvatiko (ka. pī.)] ahosi bhagavatā saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo, taṃ sabbaṃ brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa ārocesi.

290.When Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin was seated to one side, the brahmin Pokkharasāti said to him: "Dear Ambaṭṭha, did you see that venerable Gotama?" "I saw him, sir, that venerable Gotama." "Dear Ambaṭṭha, is the report about that venerable Gotama accurate, or is it otherwise? Is he truly as they say, or is he not?" "The report about that venerable Gotama is indeed accurate, sir, and not otherwise. He is truly as they say, and not otherwise. That venerable Gotama is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man, complete in every detail, not deficient." "But did you have, dear Ambaṭṭha, any conversation with the ascetic Gotama?" "I did, sir, have some conversation with the ascetic Gotama." "And what, dear Ambaṭṭha, was your conversation with the ascetic Gotama like?" Then Ambaṭṭha the young brahmin related to the brahmin Pokkharasāti the entire conversation he had had with the Blessed One.

291. Evaṃ vutte, brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘aho vata re amhākaṃ paṇḍitaka [paṇḍitakā], aho vata re amhākaṃ bahussutaka [bahussutakā], aho vata re amhākaṃ tevijjaka [tevijjakā], evarūpena kira, bho, puriso atthacarakena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeyya. Yadeva kho tvaṃ, ambaṭṭha, taṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ āsajja āsajja avacāsi, atha kho so bhavaṃ gotamo amhepi evaṃ upaneyya upaneyya avaca. Aho vata re amhākaṃ paṇḍitaka, aho vata re amhākaṃ bahussutaka, aho vata re amhākaṃ tevijjaka, evarūpena kira, bho, puriso atthacarakena kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjeyyā’’ti, kupito [so kupito (pī.)] anattamano ambaṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ padasāyeva pavattesi. Icchati ca tāvadeva bhagavantaṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ.

291.When this was said, the brahmin Pokkharasāti said to the young man Ambaṭṭha: "Oh, what a little scholar we have! Oh, what a learned one we have! Oh, what a master of the three Vedas we have! Indeed, sir, a person engaging in such pointless behavior, upon the breaking up of the body, after death, might be reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the lower realms, even in hell! Just as you, Ambaṭṭha, repeatedly assailed and spoke to the venerable Gotama in such a way, so too did the Blessed One repeatedly bring up (the matter) and speak (about it) concerning us. Oh, what a little scholar we have! Oh, what a learned one we have! Oh, what a master of the three Vedas we have! Indeed, sir, a person engaging in such pointless behavior, upon the breaking up of the body, after death, might be reborn in a state of misery, a bad destination, the lower realms, even in hell!" Angry and displeased, he kicked the young man Ambaṭṭha with his foot. And he wanted to go see the Blessed One immediately.

## Pokkharasātibuddhupasaṅkamanaṃ

### The Approach of Pokkharasāti to the Buddha.

292. Atha kho te brāhmaṇā brāhmaṇaṃ pokkharasātiṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘ativikālo kho, bho, ajja samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Svedāni [dāni sve (sī. ka.)] bhavaṃ pokkharasāti samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ dassanāya upasaṅkamissatī’’ti. Atha kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti sake nivesane paṇītaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ paṭiyādāpetvā yāne āropetvā ukkāsu dhāriyamānāsu ukkaṭṭhāya niyyāsi, yena icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍo tena pāyāsi. Yāvatikā yānassa bhūmi yānena gantvā, yānā paccorohitvā pattikova yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami. Upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi, sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi.

292.Then those brahmins said to the brahmin Pokkharasāti: "It is too late today, sir, to go to see the ascetic Gotama. Perhaps the venerable Pokkharasāti will go to see the ascetic Gotama tomorrow." Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti had fine food, both hard and soft, prepared in his own dwelling, loaded it onto a vehicle, and set out from Ukkaṭṭhā with torches held aloft, heading towards the Icchānaṅgala woodland grove. He went by vehicle as far as the ground was suitable for vehicles, then dismounted and approached the Blessed One on foot. Having approached, he exchanged polite greetings with the Blessed One, and after an exchange of friendly, courteous conversation, sat down to one side.

293. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘āgamā nu kho idha, bho gotama, amhākaṃ antevāsī ambaṭṭho māṇavo’’ti? ‘‘Āgamā kho te [tedha (syā.), te idha (pī.)], brāhmaṇa, antevāsī ambaṭṭho māṇavo’’ti. ‘‘Ahu pana te, bho gotama, ambaṭṭhena māṇavena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti? ‘‘Ahu kho me, brāhmaṇa, ambaṭṭhena māṇavena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti. ‘‘Yathākathaṃ pana te, bho gotama, ahu ambaṭṭhena māṇavena saddhiṃ kocideva kathāsallāpo’’ti? Atha kho bhagavā yāvatako ahosi ambaṭṭhena māṇavena saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo, taṃ sabbaṃ brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa ārocesi. Evaṃ vutte, brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘bālo, bho gotama, ambaṭṭho māṇavo, khamatu bhavaṃ gotamo ambaṭṭhassa māṇavassā’’ti. ‘‘Sukhī hotu, brāhmaṇa, ambaṭṭho māṇavo’’ti.

293.Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti, having sat down to one side, said to the Blessed One: "Has my student, the young man Ambaṭṭha, come here, Master Gotama?" "He has come, brahmin, your student, the young man Ambaṭṭha." "And was there, Master Gotama, any discussion between you and the young man Ambaṭṭha?" "There was, brahmin, some discussion between me and the young man Ambaṭṭha." "And what kind of discussion was it, Master Gotama, that took place between you and the young man Ambaṭṭha?" Then the Blessed One related to the brahmin Pokkharasāti the entire discussion that had taken place with the young man Ambaṭṭha. When this was said, the brahmin Pokkharasāti said to the Blessed One: "The young man Ambaṭṭha is a fool, Master Gotama. May Master Gotama forgive the young man Ambaṭṭha." "May the young man Ambaṭṭha be well, brahmin."

294. Atha kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni samannesi. Addasā kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavato kāye dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe pahūtajivhatāya ca.

294.Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti examined the body of the Blessed One for the thirty-two marks of a great man. The brahmin Pokkharasāti saw on the body of the Blessed One most of the thirty-two marks of a great man, except for two. Concerning two marks of a great man, he was doubtful, uncertain, not decided, not confident—the sheath-enclosed genitals and the ample tongue.

295. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘passati kho me ayaṃ brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇāni yebhuyyena ṭhapetvā dve. Dvīsu mahāpurisalakkhaṇesu kaṅkhati vicikicchati nādhimuccati na sampasīdati – kosohite ca vatthaguyhe, pahūtajivhatāya cā’’ti. Atha kho bhagavā tathārūpaṃ iddhābhisaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkhāsi yathā addasa brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavato kosohitaṃ vatthaguyhaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā jivhaṃ ninnāmetvā ubhopi kaṇṇasotāni anumasi paṭimasi, ubhopi nāsikasotāni anumasi paṭimasi, kevalampi nalāṭamaṇḍalaṃ jivhāya chādesi.

295.Then it occurred to the Blessed One: "This brahmin Pokkharasāti sees in me most of the thirty-two marks of a great man, except for two. Concerning two marks of a great man, he is doubtful, uncertain, not convinced, not satisfied: the sheath-enclosed genitals and the ample tongue." Then the Blessed One performed such a feat of psychic power that the brahmin Pokkharasāti saw the Blessed One's sheath-enclosed genitals. Then the Blessed One extended his tongue and stroked both ear-holes, stroked both nostrils, and covered his entire forehead with his tongue.

296. Atha kho brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa etadahosi – ‘‘samannāgato kho samaṇo gotamo dvattiṃsamahāpurisalakkhaṇehi paripuṇṇehi no aparipuṇṇehī’’ti. Bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘adhivāsetu me bhavaṃ gotamo ajjatanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’ti. Adhivāsesi bhagavā tuṇhībhāvena.

296.Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti had this thought: "The ascetic Gotama is endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great man, complete in every way, not incomplete." He said to the Blessed One: "May Master Gotama accept today's meal from me, together with the community of monks." The Blessed One consented by remaining silent.

297. Atha kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavato adhivāsanaṃ viditvā bhagavato kālaṃ ārocesi – ‘‘kālo, bho gotama, niṭṭhitaṃ bhatta’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghena yena brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa nivesanaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavantaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappesi sampavāresi, māṇavakāpi bhikkhusaṅghaṃ. Atha kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti bhagavantaṃ bhuttāviṃ onītapattapāṇiṃ aññataraṃ nīcaṃ āsanaṃ gahetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi.

297.Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti, knowing the Blessed One's acceptance, announced the time to the Blessed One: "It is time, Master Gotama, the meal is ready." Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the morning, taking his bowl and robe, went with the community of monks to the residence of the brahmin Pokkharasāti. Having arrived, he sat down on the prepared seat. Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti personally served and satisfied the Blessed One with choice food, hard and soft, and the young men served the community of monks. Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti, when the Blessed One had finished eating and had withdrawn his hand from the bowl, took a low seat and sat down to one side.

298. Ekamantaṃ nisinnassa kho brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa bhagavā anupubbiṃ kathaṃ kathesi, seyyathidaṃ – dānakathaṃ sīlakathaṃ saggakathaṃ; kāmānaṃ ādīnavaṃ okāraṃ saṃkilesaṃ, nekkhamme ānisaṃsaṃ pakāsesi. Yadā bhagavā aññāsi brāhmaṇaṃ pokkharasātiṃ kallacittaṃ muducittaṃ vinīvaraṇacittaṃ udaggacittaṃ pasannacittaṃ, atha yā buddhānaṃ sāmukkaṃsikā dhammadesanā, taṃ pakāsesi – dukkhaṃ samudayaṃ nirodhaṃ maggaṃ. Seyyathāpi nāma suddhaṃ vatthaṃ apagatakāḷakaṃ sammadeva rajanaṃ paṭiggaṇheyya; evameva brāhmaṇassa pokkharasātissa tasmiññeva āsane virajaṃ vītamalaṃ dhammacakkhuṃ udapādi – ‘‘yaṃ kiñci samudayadhammaṃ, sabbaṃ taṃ nirodhadhamma’’nti.

298.Then, as Pokkharasāti the brahmin was sitting to one side, the Blessed One gave him a progressive talk, that is, a talk on generosity, on virtue, on heaven; on the danger, drawback, and defilement of sensual pleasures; and on the benefit of renunciation. When the Blessed One knew that Pokkharasāti the brahmin had a ready mind, a soft mind, a mind free from hindrances, an uplifted mind, a confident mind, then he revealed the teaching unique to the Buddhas: suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. Just as a clean cloth, free from stain, would properly absorb dye, so too, while sitting in that very seat, the stainless, immaculate vision of the Dhamma arose in Pokkharasāti the brahmin: “Whatever is subject to origination is all subject to cessation.”

## Pokkharasātiupāsakattapaṭivedanā

### Pokkharasāti’s Declaration as a Lay Follower

299. Atha kho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti diṭṭhadhammo pattadhammo viditadhammo pariyogāḷhadhammo tiṇṇavicikiccho vigatakathaṃkatho vesārajjappatto aparappaccayo satthusāsane bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama. Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya, ‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’ti; evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ, bho gotama, saputto sabhariyo sapariso sāmacco bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsakaṃ maṃ bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gataṃ. Yathā ca bhavaṃ gotamo ukkaṭṭhāya aññāni upāsakakulāni upasaṅkamati, evameva bhavaṃ gotamo pokkharasātikulaṃ upasaṅkamatu. Tattha ye te māṇavakā vā māṇavikā vā bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ abhivādessanti vā paccuṭṭhissanti [paccuṭṭhassanti (pī.)] vā āsanaṃ vā udakaṃ vā dassanti cittaṃ vā pasādessanti, tesaṃ taṃ bhavissati dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti. ‘‘Kalyāṇaṃ vuccati, brāhmaṇā’’ti.

299.Then the brahmin Pokkharasāti, having seen the Dhamma, attained the Dhamma, understood the Dhamma, plunged into the Dhamma, crossed over doubt, freed from uncertainty, attained assurance, and become independent of others in the Teacher’s instruction, said to the Blessed One: “Excellent, Master Gotama! Excellent! Just as one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the way to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness so those with eyes can see forms—in the same way, Master Gotama has made the Dhamma clear in many ways. I go for refuge to Master Gotama, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of monks, together with my son, wife, retinue, and officials. May Master Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forward, for life. Just as Master Gotama visits other eminent lay families, may he also visit the family of Pokkharasāti. Whatever young men or women there pay respects to Master Gotama, rise for him, offer him a seat, water, or gladden their minds—that will be for their long-term welfare and happiness.” “Well said, brahmin.”
