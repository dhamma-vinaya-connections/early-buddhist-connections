# Cakkavattisuttaṃ

## The Wheel-Turning Monarch Discourse

## Attadīpasaraṇatā

### Being One's Own Island and Refuge

80. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā magadhesu viharati mātulāyaṃ. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhaddante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘attadīpā, bhikkhave, viharatha attasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā, dhammadīpā dhammasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā. Kathañca pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhu attadīpo viharati attasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo, dhammadīpo dhammasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Vedanāsu vedanānupassī…pe… citte cittānupassī…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu attadīpo viharati attasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo, dhammadīpo dhammasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo.

80.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Magadhans at Mātulā. There the Blessed One addressed the monks: "Monks!" "Venerable sir," those monks replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this: "Be islands unto yourselves, monks, be your own refuge, with no other refuge; let the Dhamma be your island, the Dhamma your refuge, with no other refuge. And how, monks, does a monk dwell as an island unto himself, his own refuge, with no other refuge; with the Dhamma as his island, the Dhamma as his refuge, with no other refuge? Here, monks, a monk dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure in regard to the world. He dwells contemplating feelings in feelings... mind in mind... phenomena in phenomena, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure in regard to the world. It is in this way, monks, that a monk dwells as an island unto himself, his own refuge, with no other refuge; with the Dhamma as his island, the Dhamma as his refuge, with no other refuge.

‘‘Gocare, bhikkhave, caratha sake pettike visaye. Gocare, bhikkhave, carataṃ sake pettike visaye na lacchati māro otāraṃ, na lacchati māro ārammaṇaṃ [āramaṇaṃ (?)]. Kusalānaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammānaṃ samādānahetu evamidaṃ puññaṃ pavaḍḍhati.

"Monks, wander in your own pasture, your own ancestral domain. Monks, for those wandering in their own pasture, their own ancestral domain, Mara finds no opportunity, Mara finds no support. Monks, this merit increases in this way, because of undertaking wholesome qualities."

## Daḷhanemicakkavattirājā

### King Daḷhanemi, the Wheel-Turning Monarch.

81. ‘‘Bhūtapubbaṃ, bhikkhave, rājā daḷhanemi nāma ahosi cakkavattī [cakkavatti (syā. pī.)] dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāvī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni satta ratanāni ahesuṃ seyyathidaṃ – cakkaratanaṃu hatthiratanaṃ assaratanaṃ maṇiratanaṃ itthiratanaṃ gahapatiratanaṃ pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā ahesuṃ sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena [dhammena samena (syā. ka.)] abhivijiya ajjhāvasi.

81."Once, monks, there was a king named Daḷhanemi, a wheel-turning monarch, a righteous king, a king of righteousness, conqueror of the four quarters, who had attained stability in his country, endowed with the seven treasures. He possessed these seven treasures: the wheel-treasure, the elephant-treasure, the horse-treasure, the jewel-treasure, the woman-treasure, the householder-treasure, and the advisor-treasure as the seventh. And he had over a thousand sons who were brave, heroic in appearance, and crushers of enemies' armies. He conquered this earth bounded by the ocean without force or weapons, by righteousness, and dwelt therein."

82. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā daḷhanemi bahunnaṃ vassānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasatānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasahassānaṃ accayena aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi – ‘yadā tvaṃ, ambho purisa, passeyyāsi dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ ṭhānā cutaṃ, atha me āroceyyāsī’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, so puriso rañño daḷhanemissa paccassosi. Addasā kho, bhikkhave, so puriso bahunnaṃ vassānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasatānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasahassānaṃ accayena dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ ṭhānā cutaṃ, disvāna yena rājā daḷhanemi tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rājānaṃ daḷhanemiṃ etadavoca – ‘yagghe, deva, jāneyyāsi, dibbaṃ te cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ ṭhānā cuta’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā daḷhanemi jeṭṭhaputtaṃ kumāraṃ āmantāpetvā [āmantetvā (syā. ka.)] etadavoca – ‘dibbaṃ kira me, tāta kumāra, cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ ṭhānā cutaṃ. Sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ – yassa rañño cakkavattissa dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ osakkati ṭhānā cavati, na dāni tena raññā ciraṃ jīvitabbaṃ hotīti. Bhuttā kho pana me mānusakā kāmā, samayo dāni me dibbe kāme pariyesituṃ. Ehi tvaṃ, tāta kumāra, imaṃ samuddapariyantaṃ pathaviṃ paṭipajja. Ahaṃ pana kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāmī’ti.

82."Then, monks, after the lapse of many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years, King Daḷhanemi addressed a certain man: 'When you, good man, see the divine wheel-treasure receding, departing from its position, then inform me.' 'Yes, lord,' that man replied to King Daḷhanemi. After the lapse of many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years, that man saw the divine wheel-treasure receding, departing from its position. Having seen this, he approached King Daḷhanemi and said: 'May your honor know, lord, that your divine wheel-treasure has receded, departed from its position.' Then, monks, King Daḷhanemi had his eldest son, the prince, summoned and said to him: 'Dear prince, it seems my divine wheel-treasure has receded, departed from its position. Now, I have heard that when a wheel-turning monarch's divine wheel-treasure recedes, departs from its position, that king does not have long to live. I have enjoyed human pleasures; now it is time for me to seek divine pleasures. Come, dear prince, undertake the rule of this earth bounded by the ocean. I, however, will shave off my hair and beard, put on saffron robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness.'"

83. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā daḷhanemi jeṭṭhaputtaṃ kumāraṃ sādhukaṃ rajje samanusāsitvā kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji. Sattāhapabbajite kho pana, bhikkhave, rājisimhi dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ antaradhāyi.

83."Then, monks, King Daḷhanemi, having properly instructed his eldest son in the kingdom, shaved off his hair and beard, put on the ochre robes, and went forth from the household life into homelessness. But, monks, seven days after the royal sage had gone forth, the divine wheel-treasure disappeared."

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, aññataro puriso yena rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto [muddhāvasitto (sī. syā. pī.) evamuparipi] tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rājānaṃ khattiyaṃ muddhābhisittaṃ etadavoca – ‘yagghe, deva, jāneyyāsi, dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ antarahita’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto dibbe cakkaratane antarahite anattamano ahosi, anattamanatañca paṭisaṃvedesi. So yena rājisi tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rājisiṃ etadavoca – ‘yagghe, deva, jāneyyāsi, dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ antarahita’nti. Evaṃ vutte, bhikkhave, rājisi rājānaṃ khattiyaṃ muddhābhisittaṃ etadavoca – ‘mā kho tvaṃ, tāta, dibbe cakkaratane antarahite anattamano ahosi, mā anattamanatañca paṭisaṃvedesi, na hi te, tāta, dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ pettikaṃ dāyajjaṃ. Iṅgha tvaṃ, tāta, ariye cakkavattivatte vattāhi. Ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati, yaṃ te ariye cakkavattivatte vattamānassa tadahuposathe pannarase sīsaṃnhātassa [sīsaṃ nahātassa (sī. pī.), sīsanhātassa (syā.)] uposathikassa uparipāsādavaragatassa dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ pātubhavissati sahassāraṃ sanemikaṃ sanābhikaṃ sabbākāraparipūra’nti.

"Then, monks, a certain man approached the anointed Khattiya king and said: 'Please know, lord, that the divine wheel-treasure has disappeared.' Then, monks, the anointed Khattiya king, with the disappearance of the divine wheel-treasure, was displeased, and experienced displeasure. He approached the royal sage and said: 'Please know, lord, that the divine wheel-treasure has disappeared.' When this was said, monks, the royal sage said to the anointed Khattiya king: 'Do not be displeased, dear one, nor experience displeasure at the disappearance of the divine wheel-treasure. The divine wheel-treasure is not your paternal inheritance. Go now, dear one, and proceed in the noble wheel-turning course. For it is possible that, as you proceed in the noble wheel-turning course, on the fifteenth day of the Uposatha, your head having been bathed, while observing the Uposatha, having gone to the best upper palace, the divine wheel-treasure will appear, with a thousand spokes, with a rim, with a hub, complete in every way.'"

## Cakkavattiariyavattaṃ

### The Noble Practice of a Wheel-Turning Monarch

84. ‘‘‘Katamaṃ pana taṃ, deva, ariyaṃ cakkavattivatta’nti? ‘Tena hi tvaṃ, tāta, dhammaṃyeva nissāya dhammaṃ sakkaronto dhammaṃ garuṃ karonto [garukaronto (sī. syā. pī.)] dhammaṃ mānento dhammaṃ pūjento dhammaṃ apacāyamāno dhammaddhajo dhammaketu dhammādhipateyyo dhammikaṃ rakkhāvaraṇaguttiṃ saṃvidahassu antojanasmiṃ balakāyasmiṃ khattiyesu anuyantesu [anuyuttesu (sī. pī.)] brāhmaṇagahapatikesu negamajānapadesu samaṇabrāhmaṇesu migapakkhīsu. Mā ca te, tāta, vijite adhammakāro pavattittha. Ye ca te, tāta, vijite adhanā assu, tesañca dhanamanuppadeyyāsi [dhanamanuppadajjeyyāsi (sī. syā. pī.)]. Ye ca te, tāta, vijite samaṇabrāhmaṇā madappamādā paṭiviratā khantisoracce niviṭṭhā ekamattānaṃ damenti, ekamattānaṃ samenti, ekamattānaṃ parinibbāpenti, te kālena kālaṃ upasaṅkamitvā paripuccheyyāsi pariggaṇheyyāsi – ‘‘kiṃ, bhante, kusalaṃ, kiṃ akusalaṃ, kiṃ sāvajjaṃ, kiṃ anavajjaṃ, kiṃ sevitabbaṃ, kiṃ na sevitabbaṃ, kiṃ me karīyamānaṃ dīgharattaṃ ahitāya dukkhāya assa, kiṃ vā pana me karīyamānaṃ dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāya assā’’ti? Tesaṃ sutvā yaṃ akusalaṃ taṃ abhinivajjeyyāsi, yaṃ kusalaṃ taṃ samādāya vatteyyāsi. Idaṃ kho, tāta, taṃ ariyaṃ cakkavattivatta’nti.

84.'But what, lord, is that noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch?' 'Therefore, dear son, relying on the Dhamma, honoring the Dhamma, respecting the Dhamma, revering the Dhamma, venerating the Dhamma, and paying homage to the Dhamma, with the Dhamma as your banner, the Dhamma as your standard, the Dhamma as your supreme authority, establish righteous protection, shelter, and guard for your household, your troops, your nobles, your vassals, for brahmins and householders, for town and country folk, for ascetics and brahmins, and for beasts and birds. And, dear son, let no unrighteousness prevail in your realm. And to those in your realm who are without wealth, give them wealth. And those in your realm who are ascetics and brahmins, abstaining from intoxication and heedlessness, established in patience and gentleness, taming themselves alone, calming themselves alone, and bringing themselves alone to final Nibbāna, approach them from time to time and ask them: "What, venerable sirs, is wholesome and what is unwholesome? What is blameworthy and what is blameless? What should be practiced and what should not be practiced? What, when done by me, will lead to my harm and suffering for a long time? Or what, when done by me, will lead to my welfare and happiness for a long time?" Having heard them, avoid what is unwholesome and undertake what is wholesome. This, dear son, is that noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch.'

## Cakkaratanapātubhāvo

### The Manifestation of the Wheel Gem

85. ‘‘‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto rājisissa paṭissutvā ariye cakkavattivatte [ariyaṃ cakkavattivattaṃ (ka.)] vatti. Tassa ariye cakkavattivatte vattamānassa tadahuposathe pannarase sīsaṃnhātassa uposathikassa uparipāsādavaragatassa dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ pāturahosi sahassāraṃ sanemikaṃ sanābhikaṃ sabbākāraparipūraṃ. Disvāna rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa etadahosi – ‘sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ – yassa rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa tadahuposathe pannarase sīsaṃnhātassa uposathikassa uparipāsādavaragatassa dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ pātubhavati sahassāraṃ sanemikaṃ sanābhikaṃ sabbākāraparipūraṃ, so hoti rājā cakkavattī’ti. Assaṃ nu kho ahaṃ rājā cakkavattīti.

85.'Yes, lord,' monks, the anointed Khattiya king, having assented to the royal sage, set in motion the noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch. While he was setting in motion this noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch, on that Uposatha day, the fifteenth day, having bathed his head and observed the Uposatha, having gone to the best upper palace, a divine wheel-gem appeared to him—thousand-spoked, with rim and hub, complete in every way. Seeing it, the anointed Khattiya king had this thought: ‘I have heard that to whatever anointed Khattiya king on that Uposatha day, the fifteenth day, after bathing his head and observing the Uposatha, having gone to the best upper palace, a divine wheel-gem appears—thousand-spoked, with rim and hub, complete in every way, he becomes a Wheel-turning Monarch.’ ‘Could I be a Wheel-turning Monarch?’

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto uṭṭhāyāsanā ekaṃsaṃ utarāsaṅgaṃ karitvā vāmena hatthena bhiṅkāraṃ gahetvā dakkhiṇena hatthena cakkaratanaṃ abbhukkiri – ‘pavattatu bhavaṃ cakkaratanaṃ, abhivijinātu bhavaṃ cakkaratana’nti.

Then, monks, the anointed Khattiya king rose from his seat, arranged his upper robe over one shoulder, took a pitcher in his left hand, and with his right hand sprinkled the wheel-gem, saying: 'Roll forth, noble wheel-gem! Conquer, noble wheel-gem!'

‘‘Atha kho taṃ, bhikkhave, cakkaratanaṃ puratthimaṃ disaṃ pavatti, anvadeva rājā cakkavattī saddhiṃ caturaṅginiyā senāya. Yasmiṃ kho pana, bhikkhave, padese cakkaratanaṃ patiṭṭhāsi, tattha rājā cakkavattī vāsaṃ upagacchi saddhiṃ caturaṅginiyā senāya. Ye kho pana, bhikkhave, puratthimāya disāya paṭirājāno, te rājānaṃ cakkavattiṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamāhaṃsu – ‘ehi kho, mahārāja, svāgataṃ te [sāgataṃ (sī. pī.)] mahārāja, sakaṃ te, mahārāja, anusāsa, mahārājā’ti. Rājā cakkavattī evamāha – ‘pāṇo na hantabbo, adinnaṃ nādātabbaṃ, kāmesumicchā na caritabbā, musā na bhāsitabbā, majjaṃ na pātabbaṃ, yathābhuttañca bhuñjathā’ti. Ye kho pana, bhikkhave, puratthimāya disāya paṭirājāno, te rañño cakkavattissa anuyantā [anuyuttā (sī. pī.)] ahesuṃ.

Then, monks, that wheel-gem rolled forth towards the eastern direction, and the Wheel-turning Monarch followed right after it with his fourfold army. In whatever region, monks, the wheel-gem stopped, there the Wheel-turning Monarch took up residence with his fourfold army. The opposing kings in the eastern direction then approached the Wheel-turning Monarch and said: 'Come, great king, welcome, great king. It is yours, great king, instruct us, great king.' The Wheel-turning Monarch said: 'Do not kill living beings. Do not take what is not given. Do not engage in sexual misconduct. Do not speak falsely. Do not drink intoxicants. And partake of your possessions as before.' And so, monks, the opposing kings of the eastern direction became followers of the Wheel-turning Monarch.

86. ‘‘Atha kho taṃ, bhikkhave, cakkaratanaṃ puratthimaṃ samuddaṃ ajjhogāhetvā [ajjhogahetvā (sī. syā. pī.)] paccuttaritvā dakkhiṇaṃ disaṃ pavatti…pe… dakkhiṇaṃ samuddaṃ ajjhogāhetvā paccuttaritvā pacchimaṃ disaṃ pavatti, anvadeva rājā cakkavattī saddhiṃ caturaṅginiyā senāya. Yasmiṃ kho pana, bhikkhave, padese cakkaratanaṃ patiṭṭhāsi, tattha rājā cakkavattī vāsaṃ upagacchi saddhiṃ caturaṅginiyā senāya. Ye kho pana, bhikkhave, pacchimāya disāya paṭirājāno, te rājānaṃ cakkavattiṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamāhaṃsu – ‘ehi kho, mahārāja, svāgataṃ te, mahārāja, sakaṃ te, mahārāja, anusāsa, mahārājā’ti. Rājā cakkavattī evamāha – ‘pāṇo na hantabbo, adinnaṃ nādātabbaṃ, kāmesumicchā na caritabbā, musā na bhāsitabbā, majjaṃ na pātabbaṃ, yathābhuttañca bhuñjathā’ti. Ye kho pana, bhikkhave, pacchimāya disāya paṭirājāno, te rañño cakkavattissa anuyantā ahesuṃ.

86.Then, monks, that wheel-gem plunged into the eastern ocean and re-emerged, rolling forth to the southern direction... it plunged into the southern ocean and re-emerged, rolling forth to the western direction, followed by the Wheel-turning Monarch along with his fourfold army. And in whatever region, monks, the wheel-gem stopped, there the Wheel-turning Monarch took up residence along with his fourfold army. Those opposing kings of the western direction approached the Wheel-turning Monarch and said: 'Come, great king, welcome, great king, it is yours, great king, instruct us, great king.' The Wheel-turning Monarch said: 'Do not kill living beings. Do not take what is not given. Do not engage in sexual misconduct. Do not speak falsely. Do not drink intoxicants. And partake of your possessions as before.' And so, monks, those opposing kings of the western direction became followers of the Wheel-turning Monarch.

87. ‘‘Atha kho taṃ, bhikkhave, cakkaratanaṃ pacchimaṃ samuddaṃ ajjhogāhetvā paccuttaritvā uttaraṃ disaṃ pavatti, anvadeva rājā cakkavattī saddhiṃ caturaṅginiyā senāya. Yasmiṃ kho pana, bhikkhave, padese cakkaratanaṃ patiṭṭhāsi, tattha rājā cakkavattī vāsaṃ upagacchi saddhiṃ caturaṅginiyā senāya. Ye kho pana, bhikkhave, uttarāya disāya paṭirājāno, te rājānaṃ cakkavattiṃ upasaṅkamitvā evamāhaṃsu – ‘ehi kho, mahārāja, svāgataṃ te, mahārāja, sakaṃ te, mahārāja, anusāsa, mahārājā’ti. Rājā cakkavattī evamāha – ‘pāṇo na hantabbo, adinnaṃ nādātabbaṃ, kāmesumicchā na caritabbā, musā na bhāsitabbā, majjaṃ na pātabbaṃ, yathābhuttañca bhuñjathā’ti. Ye kho pana, bhikkhave, uttarāya disāya paṭirājāno, te rañño cakkavattissa anuyantā ahesuṃ.

87.Then, monks, that wheel-gem plunged into the western ocean and re-emerged, rolling forth to the northern direction, followed by the Wheel-turning Monarch along with his fourfold army. In whatever region, monks, the wheel-gem stopped, there the Wheel-turning Monarch took up residence along with his fourfold army. Those opposing kings of the northern direction approached the Wheel-turning Monarch and said: 'Come, great king, welcome, great king. It is yours, great king, instruct us, great king.' The Wheel-turning Monarch said: 'Do not kill living beings. Do not take what is not given. Do not engage in sexual misconduct. Do not speak falsely. Do not drink intoxicants. And partake of your possessions as before.' And so, monks, those opposing kings of the northern direction became followers of the Wheel-turning Monarch.

‘‘Atha kho taṃ, bhikkhave, cakkaratanaṃ samuddapariyantaṃ pathaviṃ abhivijinitvā tameva rājadhāniṃ paccāgantvā rañño cakkavattissa antepuradvāre atthakaraṇapamukhe [aḍḍakaraṇapamukhe (ka.)] akkhāhataṃ maññe aṭṭhāsi rañño cakkavattissa antepuraṃ upasobhayamānaṃ.

Then, monks, that wheel-gem, having conquered the earth up to its ocean boundary, returned to that very capital and stood at the entrance to the inner palace of the Wheel-turning Monarch, at the forefront of the judgment hall, as if fixed to the axle, adorning the inner palace of the Wheel-turning Monarch.

## Dutiyādicakkavattikathā

### The Discourse on the Second and Subsequent Wheel-Turning Monarchs

88. ‘‘Dutiyopi kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī…pe… tatiyopi kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī… catutthopi kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī… pañcamopi kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī… chaṭṭhopi kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī… sattamopi kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī bahunnaṃ vassānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasatānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasahassānaṃ accayena aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi – ‘yadā tvaṃ, ambho purisa, passeyyāsi dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ ṭhānā cutaṃ, atha me āroceyyāsī’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, so puriso rañño cakkavattissa paccassosi. Addasā kho, bhikkhave, so puriso bahunnaṃ vassānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasatānaṃ bahunnaṃ vassasahassānaṃ accayena dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ ṭhānā cutaṃ. Disvāna yena rājā cakkavattī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rājānaṃ cakkavattiṃ etadavoca – ‘yagghe, deva, jāneyyāsi, dibbaṃ te cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ ṭhānā cuta’nti?

88."Monks, the second wheel-turning monarch… the third wheel-turning monarch… the fourth wheel-turning monarch… the fifth wheel-turning monarch… the sixth wheel-turning monarch… the seventh wheel-turning monarch, after the passing of many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years, addressed a certain man: ‘Good man, when you see the divine wheel-treasure retreating, fallen from its position, then inform me.’ ‘Yes, lord,’ that man replied to the wheel-turning monarch. After the passing of many years, many hundreds of years, many thousands of years, that man saw the divine wheel-treasure retreating, fallen from its position. Having seen this, he approached the wheel-turning monarch and said, ‘May it please your majesty to know that your divine wheel-treasure has retreated, fallen from its position.’"

89. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī jeṭṭhaputtaṃ kumāraṃ āmantāpetvā etadavoca – ‘dibbaṃ kira me, tāta kumāra, cakkaratanaṃ osakkitaṃ, ṭhānā cutaṃ, sutaṃ kho pana metaṃ – yassa rañño cakkavattissa dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ osakkati, ṭhānā cavati, na dāni tena raññā ciraṃ jīvitabbaṃ hotīti. Bhuttā kho pana me mānusakā kāmā, samayo dāni me dibbe kāme pariyesituṃ, ehi tvaṃ, tāta kumāra, imaṃ samuddapariyantaṃ pathaviṃ paṭipajja. Ahaṃ pana kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissāmī’ti.

89."Then, monks, the wheel-turning monarch summoned his eldest prince and said: ‘My dear prince, it seems my divine wheel-treasure has receded, fallen from its place. I have heard that when a wheel-turning monarch’s divine wheel-treasure recedes, falls from its place, that king does not have long to live. I have enjoyed human pleasures; now it is time for me to seek divine pleasures. Come, my dear prince, govern this earth bounded by the ocean. I will shave off my hair and beard, put on the ochre robes, and go forth from the household life into homelessness.’"

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā cakkavattī jeṭṭhaputtaṃ kumāraṃ sādhukaṃ rajje samanusāsitvā kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbaji. Sattāhapabbajite kho pana, bhikkhave, rājisimhi dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ antaradhāyi.

Then, monks, the wheel-turning monarch, having properly instructed his eldest prince in kingship, shaved off his hair and beard, put on the ochre robes, and went forth from the household life into homelessness. But seven days after the royal ascetic had gone forth, the divine wheel-treasure vanished.

90. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, aññataro puriso yena rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā rājānaṃ khattiyaṃ muddhābhisittaṃ etadavoca – ‘yagghe, deva, jāneyyāsi, dibbaṃ cakkaratanaṃ antarahita’nti? Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto dibbe cakkaratane antarahite anattamano ahosi. Anattamanatañca paṭisaṃvedesi; no ca kho rājisiṃ upasaṅkamitvā ariyaṃ cakkavattivattaṃ pucchi. So samateneva sudaṃ janapadaṃ pasāsati. Tassa samatena janapadaṃ pasāsato pubbenāparaṃ janapadā na pabbanti, yathā taṃ pubbakānaṃ rājūnaṃ ariye cakkavattivatte vattamānānaṃ.

90."Then, monks, a certain man approached the anointed khattiya king and said, ‘May it please your majesty, know that the divine wheel-treasure has disappeared.’ Then, monks, when the divine wheel-treasure had disappeared, the anointed khattiya king became displeased. And he experienced displeasure; but he did not approach the royal sage to ask about the noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch. He just continued to govern the country according to his own will. As he governed the country according to his own will, the districts did not flourish as they had for previous kings who followed the noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch."

‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, amaccā pārisajjā gaṇakamahāmattā anīkaṭṭhā dovārikā mantassājīvino sannipatitvā rājānaṃ khattiyaṃ muddhābhisittaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘na kho te, deva, samatena (sudaṃ) janapadaṃ pasāsato pubbenāparaṃ janapadā pabbanti, yathā taṃ pubbakānaṃ rājūnaṃ ariye cakkavattivatte vattamānānaṃ. Saṃvijjanti kho te, deva, vijite amaccā pārisajjā gaṇakamahāmattā anīkaṭṭhā dovārikā mantassājīvino mayañceva aññe ca [aññe ca paṇḍite samaṇabrāhmaṇe puccheyyāsi (ka.)] ye mayaṃ ariyaṃ cakkavattivattaṃ dhārema. Iṅgha tvaṃ, deva, amhe ariyaṃ cakkavattivattaṃ puccha. Tassa te mayaṃ ariyaṃ cakkavattivattaṃ puṭṭhā byākarissāmā’ti.

"Then, monks, the ministers, courtiers, accountants-general, army commanders, doorkeepers, and those who live on counsel, gathered and said to the anointed khattiya king: ‘Your majesty, as you govern the country according to your own will, the districts do not flourish as they had for previous kings who followed the noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch. In your territory, your majesty, there exist ministers, courtiers, accountants-general, army commanders, doorkeepers, and those who live on counsel—both we and others—who remember the noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch. Please, your majesty, ask us about the noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch. When asked, we will explain the noble practice of a wheel-turning monarch to you.’"

## Āyuvaṇṇādipariyānikathā

### The Discussion on the Decline of Life Span, Beauty, etc.

91. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto amacce pārisajje gaṇakamahāmatte anīkaṭṭhe dovārike mantassājīvino sannipātetvā ariyaṃ cakkavattivattaṃ pucchi. Tassa te ariyaṃ cakkavattivattaṃ puṭṭhā byākariṃsu. Tesaṃ sutvā dhammikañhi kho rakkhāvaraṇaguttiṃ saṃvidahi, no ca kho adhanānaṃ dhanamanuppadāsi. Adhanānaṃ dhane ananuppadiyamāne dāliddiyaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Dāliddiye vepullaṃ gate aññataro puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyi. Tamenaṃ aggahesuṃ. Gahetvā rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa dassesuṃ – ‘ayaṃ, deva, puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, ambho purisa, paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti [ādiyasīti (syā.)]? ‘Saccaṃ, devā’ti. ‘Kiṃ kāraṇā’ti? ‘Na hi, deva, jīvāmī’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto tassa purisassa dhanamanuppadāsi – ‘iminā tvaṃ, ambho purisa, dhanena attanā ca jīvāhi, mātāpitaro ca posehi, puttadārañca posehi, kammante ca payojehi, samaṇabrāhmaṇesu [samaṇesu brāhmaṇesu (bahūsu)] uddhaggikaṃ dakkhiṇaṃ patiṭṭhāpehi sovaggikaṃ sukhavipākaṃ saggasaṃvattanika’nti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, so puriso rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa paccassosi.

91.Then, monks, the anointed Khattiya king assembled his ministers, courtiers, accountants, chief officers, gatekeepers, and advisors and inquired about the noble duty of a Wheel-turning Monarch. When asked, they explained it to him. Having heard it, he established righteous protection, defense, and security, but he did not provide wealth to the destitute. When wealth was not provided to the destitute, poverty became widespread. With poverty widespread, a certain man took what was not given, which is called theft. He was arrested, and when they brought him before the anointed Khattiya king, they said: ‘Your majesty, this man took what was not given, which is called theft.’ When this was said, monks, the anointed Khattiya king asked the man: ‘Is it true, fellow, that you took what was not given, which is called theft?’ ‘It is true, your majesty.’ ‘What was the reason?’ ‘Because I cannot survive, your majesty.’ Then, monks, the anointed Khattiya king provided wealth to that man, saying: ‘With this wealth, fellow, support yourself, support your parents, wife, and children, engage in work, and establish a lofty offering for ascetics and brahmins, which leads to heavenly results, pleasant results, and rebirth in heaven.’ ‘Yes, your majesty,’ replied the man to the anointed Khattiya king.

‘‘Aññataropi kho, bhikkhave, puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyi. Tamenaṃ aggahesuṃ. Gahetvā rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa dassesuṃ – ‘ayaṃ, deva, puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, ambho purisa, paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti? ‘Saccaṃ, devā’ti. ‘Kiṃ kāraṇā’ti? ‘Na hi, deva, jīvāmī’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto tassa purisassa dhanamanuppadāsi – ‘iminā tvaṃ, ambho purisa, dhanena attanā ca jīvāhi, mātāpitaro ca posehi, puttadārañca posehi, kammante ca payojehi, samaṇabrāhmaṇesu uddhaggikaṃ dakkhiṇaṃ patiṭṭhāpehi sovaggikaṃ sukhavipākaṃ saggasaṃvattanika’nti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, so puriso rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa paccassosi.

Yet another man, monks, took what was not given, which is called theft. He was arrested, and when they brought him before the anointed Khattiya king, they said: ‘Your majesty, this man took what was not given, which is called theft.’ When this was said, monks, the anointed Khattiya king asked the man: ‘Is it true, fellow, that you took what was not given, which is called theft?’ ‘It is true, your majesty.’ ‘What was the reason?’ ‘Because I cannot survive, your majesty.’ Then, monks, the anointed Khattiya king provided wealth to that man, saying: ‘With this wealth, fellow, support yourself, support your parents, wife, and children, engage in work, and establish a lofty offering for ascetics and brahmins, which leads to heavenly results, pleasant results, and rebirth in heaven.’ ‘Yes, your majesty,’ replied the man to the anointed Khattiya king.

92. ‘‘Assosuṃ kho, bhikkhave, manussā – ‘ye kira, bho, paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyanti, tesaṃ rājā dhanamanuppadetī’ti. Sutvāna tesaṃ etadahosi – ‘yaṃnūna mayampi paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyeyyāmā’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, aññataro puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyi. Tamenaṃ aggahesuṃ. Gahetvā rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa dassesuṃ – ‘ayaṃ, deva, puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, ambho purisa, paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti? ‘Saccaṃ, devā’ti. ‘Kiṃ kāraṇā’ti? ‘Na hi, deva, jīvāmī’ti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa etadahosi – ‘sace kho ahaṃ yo yo paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyissati, tassa tassa dhanamanuppadassāmi, evamidaṃ adinnādānaṃ pavaḍḍhissati. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ imaṃ purisaṃ sunisedhaṃ nisedheyyaṃ, mūlaghaccaṃ [mūlaghacchaṃ (syā.), mūlachejja (ka.)] kareyyaṃ, sīsamassa chindeyya’nti. Atha kho, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto purise āṇāpesi – ‘tena hi, bhaṇe, imaṃ purisaṃ daḷhāya rajjuyā pacchābāhaṃ [pacchābāhuṃ (syā.)] gāḷhabandhanaṃ bandhitvā khuramuṇḍaṃ karitvā kharassarena paṇavena rathikāya rathikaṃ siṅghāṭakena siṅghāṭakaṃ parinetvā dakkhiṇena dvārena nikkhamitvā dakkhiṇato nagarassa sunisedhaṃ nisedhetha, mūlaghaccaṃ karotha, sīsamassa chindathā’ti. ‘Evaṃ, devā’ti kho, bhikkhave, te purisā rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa paṭissutvā taṃ purisaṃ daḷhāya rajjuyā pacchābāhaṃ gāḷhabandhanaṃ bandhitvā khuramuṇḍaṃ karitvā kharassarena paṇavena rathikāya rathikaṃ siṅghāṭakena siṅghāṭakaṃ parinetvā dakkhiṇena dvārena nikkhamitvā dakkhiṇato nagarassa sunisedhaṃ nisedhesuṃ, mūlaghaccaṃ akaṃsu, sīsamassa chindiṃsu.

92.People heard, monks: ‘Indeed, sirs, those who take what is not given, which is called theft, the king gives them wealth.’ Having heard this, they thought: ‘Why don’t we too take what is not given, which is called theft?’ Then, monks, a certain man took what was not given, which is called theft. He was arrested, and when they brought him before the anointed Khattiya king, they said: ‘Your majesty, this man took what was not given, which is called theft.’ When this was said, monks, the anointed Khattiya king asked the man: ‘Is it true, fellow, that you took what was not given, which is called theft?’ ‘It is true, your majesty.’ ‘What was the reason?’ ‘Because I cannot survive, your majesty.’ Then, monks, the anointed Khattiya king thought: ‘If I provide wealth to everyone who takes what is not given, which is called theft, this stealing will increase. Why don’t I severely punish this man, uproot him, and cut off his head?’ Then, monks, the anointed Khattiya king ordered his men: ‘Well then, men, tie this man’s arms tightly behind his back with a strong rope, shave his head, and lead him around from street to street, from square to square, with a harsh drumbeat. Then take him out through the southern gate and to the south of the city severely punish him, uproot him, and cut off his head.’ ‘Yes, your majesty,’ replied those men to the anointed Khattiya king. Having tied that man’s arms tightly behind his back with a strong rope, shaved his head, and led him around from street to street, from square to square, with a harsh drumbeat, they took him out through the southern gate and to the south of the city severely punished him, uprooted him, and cut off his head.

93. ‘‘Assosuṃ kho, bhikkhave, manussā – ‘ye kira, bho, paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyanti, te rājā sunisedhaṃ nisedheti, mūlaghaccaṃ karoti, sīsāni tesaṃ chindatī’ti. Sutvāna tesaṃ etadahosi – ‘yaṃnūna mayampi tiṇhāni satthāni kārāpessāma [kārāpeyyāma (syā. pī.) kārāpeyyāmāti (ka. sī.)], tiṇhāni satthāni kārāpetvā yesaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyissāma, te sunisedhaṃ nisedhessāma, mūlaghaccaṃ karissāma, sīsāni tesaṃ chindissāmā’ti. Te tiṇhāni satthāni kārāpesuṃ, tiṇhāni satthāni kārāpetvā gāmaghātampi upakkamiṃsu kātuṃ, nigamaghātampi upakkamiṃsu kātuṃ, nagaraghātampi upakkamiṃsu kātuṃ, panthaduhanampi [panthadūhanaṃpi (sī. syā. pī.)] upakkamiṃsu kātuṃ. Yesaṃ te adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyanti, te sunisedhaṃ nisedhenti, mūlaghaccaṃ karonti, sīsāni tesaṃ chindanti.

93.Monks, people heard: ‘Indeed, sirs, those who take what is not given, which is called theft, the king severely punishes, uproots, and cuts off their heads.’ Hearing this, they thought: ‘Why don’t we too have sharp weapons made? Having had sharp weapons made, we will severely punish, uproot, and cut off the heads of those from whom we take what is not given, which is called theft.’ They had sharp weapons made, and having had sharp weapons made, they began to plunder villages, to plunder towns, to plunder cities, and to ambush travelers. Those from whom they took what was not given, which is called theft, they severely punished, uprooted, and cut off their heads.

94. ‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, adhanānaṃ dhane ananuppadiyamāne dāliddiyaṃ vepullamagamāsi, dāliddiye vepullaṃ gate adinnādānaṃ vepullamagamāsi, adinnādāne vepullaṃ gate satthaṃ vepullamagamāsi, satthe vepullaṃ gate pāṇātipāto vepullamagamāsi, pāṇātipāte vepullaṃ gate tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ asītivassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ cattārīsavassasahassāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

94.Thus, monks, when wealth was not provided for the destitute, poverty became widespread. With poverty widespread, taking what was not given became widespread. With taking what was not given widespread, weapons became widespread. With weapons widespread, killing became widespread. With killing widespread, the lifespan of those beings declined, and their complexion declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the children of humans who lived for eighty thousand years had a lifespan of forty thousand years.

‘‘Cattārīsavassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu aññataro puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyi. Tamenaṃ aggahesuṃ. Gahetvā rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa dassesuṃ – ‘ayaṃ, deva, puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhikkhave, rājā khattiyo muddhābhisitto taṃ purisaṃ etadavoca – ‘saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, ambho purisa, paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti? ‘Na hi, devā’ti sampajānamusā abhāsi.

Monks, among humans with a lifespan of forty thousand years, a certain man took what was not given, which is called theft. He was arrested, and when they brought him before the anointed Khattiya king, they said: ‘Your majesty, this man took what was not given, which is called theft.’ When this was said, monks, the anointed Khattiya king asked the man: ‘Is it true, fellow, that you took what was not given, which is called theft?’ ‘No, your majesty,’ he said, knowingly speaking falsely.

95. ‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, adhanānaṃ dhane ananuppadiyamāne dāliddiyaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Dāliddiye vepullaṃ gate adinnādānaṃ vepullamagamāsi, adinnādāne vepullaṃ gate satthaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Satthe vepullaṃ gate pāṇātipāto vepullamagamāsi, pāṇātipāte vepullaṃ gate musāvādo vepullamagamāsi, musāvāde vepullaṃ gate tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ cattārīsavassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ vīsativassasahassāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

95.Thus, monks, when wealth was not provided for the destitute, poverty became widespread. With poverty widespread, taking what was not given became widespread. With taking what was not given widespread, weapons became widespread. With weapons widespread, killing became widespread. With killing widespread, false speech became widespread. With false speech widespread, the lifespan of those beings declined, and their complexion declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the children of humans who lived for forty thousand years had a lifespan of twenty thousand years.

‘‘Vīsativassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu aññataro puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyi. Tamenaṃ aññataro puriso rañño khattiyassa muddhābhisittassa ārocesi – ‘itthannāmo, deva, puriso paresaṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādiyī’ti pesuññamakāsi.

Monks, among humans with a lifespan of twenty thousand years, a certain man took what was not given, which is called theft. Another man reported him to the anointed Khattiya king: ‘Your majesty, so-and-so has taken what was not given, which is called theft,’ thus engaging in malicious speech.

96. ‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, adhanānaṃ dhane ananuppadiyamāne dāliddiyaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Dāliddiye vepullaṃ gate adinnādānaṃ vepullamagamāsi, adinnādāne vepullaṃ gate satthaṃ vepullamagamāsi, satthe vepullaṃ gate pāṇātipāto vepullamagamāsi, pāṇātipāte vepullaṃ gate musāvādo vepullamagamāsi, musāvāde vepullaṃ gate pisuṇā vācā vepullamagamāsi, pisuṇāya vācāya vepullaṃ gatāya tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ vīsativassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ dasavassasahassāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

96.Thus, monks, when wealth was not provided for the destitute, poverty became widespread. With poverty widespread, taking what was not given became widespread. With taking what was not given widespread, weapons became widespread. With weapons widespread, killing became widespread. With killing widespread, false speech became widespread. With false speech widespread, malicious speech became widespread. With malicious speech widespread, the lifespan of those beings declined, and their complexion declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the children of humans who lived for twenty thousand years had a lifespan of ten thousand years.

‘‘Dasavassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu ekidaṃ sattā vaṇṇavanto honti, ekidaṃ sattā dubbaṇṇā. Tattha ye te sattā dubbaṇṇā, te vaṇṇavante satte abhijjhāyantā paresaṃ dāresu cārittaṃ āpajjiṃsu.

When humans lived for ten thousand years, monks, some beings were beautiful and others were ugly. Those beings who were ugly, envying the beautiful ones, committed misconduct with the wives of others.

97. ‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, adhanānaṃ dhane ananuppadiyamāne dāliddiyaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Dāliddiye vepullaṃ gate…pe… kāmesumicchācāro vepullamagamāsi, kāmesumicchācāre vepullaṃ gate tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ dasavassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ pañcavassasahassāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

97.Thus, monks, when wealth was not provided to the poor, poverty became widespread. As poverty became widespread...sexual misconduct became widespread. As sexual misconduct became widespread, the lifespan and complexion of those beings declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the humans with a lifespan of ten thousand years had children with a lifespan of five thousand years.

98. ‘‘Pañcavassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu dve dhammā vepullamagamaṃsu – pharusāvācā samphappalāpo ca. Dvīsu dhammesu vepullaṃ gatesu tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ pañcavassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ appekacce aḍḍhateyyavassasahassāyukā, appekacce dvevassasahassāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

98.Monks, among humans with a lifespan of five thousand years, two things became widespread: harsh speech and frivolous talk. When these two things became widespread, the lifespan and complexion of those beings declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the humans with a lifespan of five thousand years had children, some with a lifespan of two thousand five hundred years, and some with a lifespan of two thousand years.

99. ‘‘Aḍḍhateyyavassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu abhijjhābyāpādā vepullamagamaṃsu. Abhijjhābyāpādesu vepullaṃ gatesu tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ aḍḍhateyyavassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ vassasahassāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

99.Monks, among humans with a lifespan of two thousand five hundred years, covetousness and ill will became widespread. As covetousness and ill will became widespread, the lifespan and complexion of those beings declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the humans with a lifespan of two thousand five hundred years had children with a lifespan of a thousand years.

100. ‘‘Vassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu micchādiṭṭhi vepullamagamāsi. Micchādiṭṭhiyā vepullaṃ gatāya tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ vassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ pañcavassasatāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

100.Monks, among humans with a lifespan of a thousand years, wrong view became widespread. As wrong view became widespread, the lifespan and complexion of those beings declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the humans with a lifespan of a thousand years had children with a lifespan of five hundred years.

101. ‘‘Pañcavassasatāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu tayo dhammā vepullamagamaṃsu. Adhammarāgo visamalobho micchādhammo. Tīsu dhammesu vepullaṃ gatesu tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ pañcavassasatāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ appekacce aḍḍhateyyavassasatāyukā, appekacce dvevassasatāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

101.Monks, among humans with a lifespan of five hundred years, three things became widespread: unrighteous lust, excessive greed, and unnatural lust. As these three things became widespread, the lifespan and complexion of those beings declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the humans with a lifespan of five hundred years had children, some with a lifespan of two hundred and fifty years, and some with a lifespan of two hundred years.

‘‘Aḍḍhateyyavassasatāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu ime dhammā vepullamagamaṃsu. Amatteyyatā apetteyyatā asāmaññatā abrahmaññatā na kule jeṭṭhāpacāyitā.

Monks, among humans with a lifespan of two hundred and fifty years, these things became widespread: lack of respect for mothers, lack of respect for fathers, lack of respect for ascetics, lack of respect for brahmins, and failure to honor the elders of the family.

102. ‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, adhanānaṃ dhane ananuppadiyamāne dāliddiyaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Dāliddiye vepullaṃ gate adinnādānaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Adinnādāne vepullaṃ gate satthaṃ vepullamagamāsi. Satthe vepullaṃ gate pāṇātipāto vepullamagamāsi. Pāṇātipāte vepullaṃ gate musāvādo vepullamagamāsi. Musāvāde vepullaṃ gate pisuṇā vācā vepullamagamāsi. Pisuṇāya vācāya vepullaṃ gatāya kāmesumicchācāro vepullamagamāsi. Kāmesumicchācāre vepullaṃ gate dve dhammā vepullamagamaṃsu, pharusā vācā samphappalāpo ca. Dvīsu dhammesu vepullaṃ gatesu abhijjhābyāpādā vepullamagamaṃsu. Abhijjhābyāpādesu vepullaṃ gatesu micchādiṭṭhi vepullamagamāsi. Micchādiṭṭhiyā vepullaṃ gatāya tayo dhammā vepullamagamaṃsu, adhammarāgo visamalobho micchādhammo. Tīsu dhammesu vepullaṃ gatesu ime dhammā vepullamagamaṃsu, amatteyyatā apetteyyatā asāmaññatā abrahmaññatā na kule jeṭṭhāpacāyitā. Imesu dhammesu vepullaṃ gatesu tesaṃ sattānaṃ āyupi parihāyi, vaṇṇopi parihāyi. Tesaṃ āyunāpi parihāyamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi parihāyamānānaṃ aḍḍhateyyavassasatāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ vassasatāyukā puttā ahesuṃ.

102.Thus, monks, when wealth was not provided to the poor, poverty became widespread. As poverty became widespread, taking what is not given became widespread. As taking what is not given became widespread, violence by weapons became widespread. As violence by weapons became widespread, killing living beings became widespread. As killing living beings became widespread, false speech became widespread. As false speech became widespread, slanderous speech became widespread. As slanderous speech became widespread, sexual misconduct became widespread. As sexual misconduct became widespread, two things became widespread: harsh speech and frivolous talk. As these two things became widespread, covetousness and ill will became widespread. As covetousness and ill will became widespread, wrong view became widespread. As wrong view became widespread, three things became widespread: unrighteous lust, excessive greed, and unnatural lust. As these three things became widespread, these things became widespread: lack of respect for mothers, lack of respect for fathers, lack of respect for ascetics, lack of respect for brahmins, and failure to honor the elders of the family. As these things became widespread, the lifespan and complexion of those beings declined. As their lifespan and complexion declined, the humans with a lifespan of two hundred and fifty years had children with a lifespan of one hundred years.

## Dasavassāyukasamayo

### The Era of Ten-Year Lifespans

103. ‘‘Bhavissati, bhikkhave, so samayo, yaṃ imesaṃ manussānaṃ dasavassāyukā puttā bhavissanti. Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu pañcavassikā [pañcamāsikā (ka. sī.)] kumārikā alaṃpateyyā bhavissanti. Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu imāni rasāni antaradhāyissanti, seyyathidaṃ, sappi navanītaṃ telaṃ madhu phāṇitaṃ loṇaṃ. Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu kudrūsako aggaṃ bhojanānaṃ [aggabhojanaṃ (syā.)] bhavissati. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, etarahi sālimaṃsodano aggaṃ bhojanānaṃ; evameva kho, bhikkhave, dasavassāyukesu manussesu kudrūsako aggaṃ bhojanānaṃ bhavissati.

103."There will be a time, monks, when the children of these humans will have a lifespan of ten years. Among humans with a lifespan of ten years, five-year-old girls will be fit for marriage. Among humans with a lifespan of ten years, these flavors will vanish: ghee, fresh butter, oil, honey, molasses, and salt. Among humans with a lifespan of ten years, kudrūsaka will be the foremost food. Just as now, monks, rice with meat is the foremost food, so too among humans with a lifespan of ten years, kudrūsaka will be the foremost food.

‘‘Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu dasa kusalakammapathā sabbena sabbaṃ antaradhāyissanti, dasa akusalakammapathā atibyādippissanti [ativiya dippissanti (syā. pī.), ativyādippissanti (sī.)]. Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu kusalantipi na bhavissati, kuto pana kusalassa kārako. Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu ye te bhavissanti amatteyyā apetteyyā asāmaññā abrahmaññā na kule jeṭṭhāpacāyino, te pujjā ca bhavissanti pāsaṃsā ca. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, etarahi matteyyā petteyyā sāmaññā brahmaññā kule jeṭṭhāpacāyino pujjā ca pāsaṃsā ca; evameva kho, bhikkhave, dasavassāyukesu manussesu ye te bhavissanti amatteyyā apetteyyā asāmaññā abrahmaññā na kule jeṭṭhāpacāyino, te pujjā ca bhavissanti pāsaṃsā ca.

"When humans have a lifespan of ten years, the ten wholesome courses of action will entirely disappear, and the ten unwholesome courses of action will become exceedingly widespread. When humans have a lifespan of ten years, there will not even be the word 'wholesome', so how much less anyone doing what is wholesome? When humans have a lifespan of ten years, those who are without kindness towards their mothers, without kindness towards their fathers, without respect for ascetics, without respect for brahmins, not honoring the elders in the family, these will be the ones who will receive offerings and praise. Just as now, monks, those who are kind towards their mothers, kind towards their fathers, respectful towards ascetics, respectful towards brahmins, honoring the elders in the family receive offerings and praise; just so, monks, when humans have a lifespan of ten years, those who are without kindness towards their mothers, without kindness towards their fathers, without respect for ascetics, without respect for brahmins, not honoring the elders in the family, these will be the ones who will receive offerings and praise."

‘‘Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu na bhavissati mātāti vā mātucchāti vā mātulānīti vā ācariyabhariyāti vā garūnaṃ dārāti vā. Sambhedaṃ loko gamissati yathā ajeḷakā kukkuṭasūkarā soṇasiṅgālā [soṇasigālā (sī. pī.)].

"Among humans with a lifespan of ten years, monks, there will be no such thing as mother, maternal aunt, maternal uncle's wife, teacher's wife, or wives of respected ones. The world will descend into sexual promiscuity, like goats and sheep, chickens and pigs, dogs and jackals."

‘‘Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu tesaṃ sattānaṃ aññamaññamhi tibbo āghāto paccupaṭṭhito bhavissati tibbo byāpādo tibbo manopadoso tibbaṃ vadhakacittaṃ. Mātupi puttamhi puttassapi mātari; pitupi puttamhi puttassapi pitari; bhātupi bhaginiyā bhaginiyāpi bhātari tibbo āghāto paccupaṭṭhito bhavissati tibbo byāpādo tibbo manopadoso tibbaṃ vadhakacittaṃ. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, māgavikassa migaṃ disvā tibbo āghāto paccupaṭṭhito hoti tibbo byāpādo tibbo manopadoso tibbaṃ vadhakacittaṃ; evameva kho, bhikkhave, dasavassāyukesu manussesu tesaṃ sattānaṃ aññamaññamhi tibbo āghāto paccupaṭṭhito bhavissati tibbo byāpādo tibbo manopadoso tibbaṃ vadhakacittaṃ. Mātupi puttamhi puttassapi mātari; pitupi puttamhi puttassapi pitari; bhātupi bhaginiyā bhaginiyāpi bhātari tibbo āghāto paccupaṭṭhito bhavissati tibbo byāpādo tibbo manopadoso tibbaṃ vadhakacittaṃ.

"Monks, when humans have a lifespan of ten years, intense hatred will be present among those beings, intense ill-will, intense malice, and intense murderous intent. A mother will feel such toward her child, and the child toward the mother; a father toward his child, and the child toward the father; a brother toward his sister, and the sister toward her brother—intense hatred will be present, intense ill-will, intense malice, and intense murderous intent. Just as, monks, a hunter feels intense hatred, intense ill-will, intense malice, and intense murderous intent upon seeing a deer—so too, monks, when humans have a lifespan of ten years, intense hatred will be present among those beings, intense ill-will, intense malice, and intense murderous intent. A mother will feel such toward her child, and the child toward the mother; a father toward his child, and the child toward the father; a brother toward his sister, and the sister toward her brother—intense hatred will be present, intense ill-will, intense malice, and intense murderous intent.

104. ‘‘Dasavassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu sattāhaṃ satthantarakappo bhavissati. Te aññamaññamhi migasaññaṃ paṭilabhissanti. Tesaṃ tiṇhāni satthāni hatthesu pātubhavissanti. Te tiṇhena satthena ‘esa migo esa migo’ti aññamaññaṃ jīvitā voropessanti.

104."Monks, when human beings have a lifespan of ten years, there will be a seven-day period of devastation by weapons. They will have the perception of animals towards each other. Sharp weapons will appear in their hands. With sharp weapons, thinking, ‘This is an animal! This is an animal!’ they will deprive each other of life.

‘‘Atha kho tesaṃ, bhikkhave, sattānaṃ ekaccānaṃ evaṃ bhavissati – ‘mā ca mayaṃ kañci [kiñci (ka.)], mā ca amhe koci, yaṃnūna mayaṃ tiṇagahanaṃ vā vanagahanaṃ vā rukkhagahanaṃ vā nadīviduggaṃ vā pabbatavisamaṃ vā pavisitvā vanamūlaphalāhārā yāpeyyāmā’ti. Te tiṇagahanaṃ vā vanagahanaṃ vā rukkhagahanaṃ vā nadīviduggaṃ vā pabbatavisamaṃ vā [te tiṇagahanaṃ vanagahanaṃ rukkhagahanaṃ nadīviduggaṃ pabbatavisamaṃ (sī. pī.)] pavisitvā sattāhaṃ vanamūlaphalāhārā yāpessanti. Te tassa sattāhassa accayena tiṇagahanā vanagahanā rukkhagahanā nadīviduggā pabbatavisamā nikkhamitvā aññamaññaṃ āliṅgitvā sabhāgāyissanti samassāsissanti – ‘diṭṭhā, bho, sattā jīvasi, diṭṭhā, bho, sattā jīvasī’ti.

"Then, monks, some of those beings will think: 'Let us not harm anyone, nor let anyone harm us. Why don't we enter a grass thicket, a forest thicket, a tree grove, a remote riverbank, or a rugged mountain area, and live on forest roots and fruits?' They will enter a grass thicket, a forest thicket, a tree grove, a remote riverbank, or a rugged mountain area, and live on forest roots and fruits for seven days. After those seven days have passed, they will emerge from the grass thickets, the forest thickets, the tree groves, the remote riverbanks, and the rugged mountain areas, and embrace one another, rejoicing together and comforting one another, saying: 'Friend, it is good to see you alive! Friend, it is good to see you alive!'

## Āyuvaṇṇādivaḍḍhanakathā

### The Discourse on the Increase of Life, Beauty, etc.

105. ‘‘Atha kho tesaṃ, bhikkhave, sattānaṃ evaṃ bhavissati – ‘mayaṃ kho akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ samādānahetu evarūpaṃ āyataṃ ñātikkhayaṃ pattā. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ kusalaṃ kareyyāma. Kiṃ kusalaṃ kareyyāma? Yaṃnūna mayaṃ pāṇātipātā virameyyāma, idaṃ kusalaṃ dhammaṃ samādāya vatteyyāmā’ti. Te pāṇātipātā viramissanti, idaṃ kusalaṃ dhammaṃ samādāya vattissanti. Te kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ samādānahetu āyunāpi vaḍḍhissanti, vaṇṇenapi vaḍḍhissanti. Tesaṃ āyunāpi vaḍḍhamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi vaḍḍhamānānaṃ dasavassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ vīsativassāyukā puttā bhavissanti.

105."Then, monks, those beings will think: 'Because we adopted unwholesome practices, we have met with such prolonged destruction of relatives. What if we were to do what is wholesome? What wholesome thing should we do? What if we were to abstain from killing living beings and undertake this wholesome practice?' They will abstain from killing living beings and undertake this wholesome practice. Because they adopt wholesome practices, they will increase in lifespan and beauty. As their lifespan and beauty increase, children born to humans with a lifespan of ten years will have a lifespan of twenty years.

‘‘Atha kho tesaṃ, bhikkhave, sattānaṃ evaṃ bhavissati – ‘mayaṃ kho kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ samādānahetu āyunāpi vaḍḍhāma, vaṇṇenapi vaḍḍhāma. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ bhiyyosomattāya kusalaṃ kareyyāma. Kiṃ kusalaṃ kareyyāma? Yaṃnūna mayaṃ adinnādānā virameyyāma… kāmesumicchācārā virameyyāma… musāvādā virameyyāma… pisuṇāya vācāya virameyyāma… pharusāya vācāya virameyyāma… samphappalāpā virameyyāma… abhijjhaṃ pajaheyyāma… byāpādaṃ pajaheyyāma… micchādiṭṭhiṃ pajaheyyāma… tayo dhamme pajaheyyāma – adhammarāgaṃ visamalobhaṃ micchādhammaṃ… yaṃnūna mayaṃ matteyyā assāma petteyyā sāmaññā brahmaññā kule jeṭṭhāpacāyino, idaṃ kusalaṃ dhammaṃ samādāya vatteyyāmā’ti. Te matteyyā bhavissanti petteyyā sāmaññā brahmaññā kule jeṭṭhāpacāyino, idaṃ kusalaṃ dhammaṃ samādāya vattissanti.

"Then, monks, those beings will think: ‘Because we have undertaken wholesome qualities, we have increased in lifespan and beauty. What if we were to do even more wholesome deeds? What wholesome deeds should we do? What if we were to refrain from taking what is not given… refrain from sexual misconduct… refrain from false speech… refrain from divisive speech… refrain from harsh speech… refrain from idle chatter… abandon covetousness… abandon ill will… abandon wrong view… abandon the three qualities—unrighteous lust, wrongful greed, and wrong practice… What if we were to be devoted to our mothers, devoted to our fathers, devoted to ascetics, devoted to brahmins, honoring the elders of the family? Let us undertake and practice this wholesome quality.’ They will be devoted to their mothers, devoted to their fathers, devoted to ascetics, devoted to brahmins, honoring the elders of the family, undertaking and practicing this wholesome quality."

‘‘Te kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ samādānahetu āyunāpi vaḍḍhissanti, vaṇṇenapi vaḍḍhissanti. Tesaṃ āyunāpi vaḍḍhamānānaṃ vaṇṇenapi vaḍḍhamānānaṃ vīsativassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ cattārīsavassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… cattārīsavassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ asītivassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… asītivassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ saṭṭhivassasatāyukā puttā bhavissanti… saṭṭhivassasatāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ vīsatitivassasatāyukā puttā bhavissanti… vīsatitivassasatāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ cattārīsachabbassasatāyukā puttā bhavissanti. Cattārīsachabbassasatāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ dvevassasahassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… dvevassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ cattārivassasahassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… cattārivassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ aṭṭhavassasahassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… aṭṭhavassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ vīsativassasahassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… vīsativassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ cattārīsavassasahassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… cattārīsavassasahassāyukānaṃ manussānaṃ asītivassasahassāyukā puttā bhavissanti… asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu pañcavassasatikā kumārikā alaṃpateyyā bhavissanti.

"Because they undertake wholesome qualities, they will increase in lifespan and beauty. As their lifespan and beauty increase, the children of humans with a twenty-year lifespan will live for forty years… the children of humans with a forty-year lifespan will live for eighty years… the children of humans with an eighty-year lifespan will live for six hundred years… the children of humans with a six hundred-year lifespan will live for two thousand years… the children of humans with a two thousand-year lifespan will live for six thousand four hundred years… the children of humans with a six thousand four hundred-year lifespan will live for two thousand years… the children of humans with a two-thousand-year lifespan will live for four thousand years… the children of humans with a four-thousand-year lifespan will live for eight thousand years… the children of humans with an eight-thousand-year lifespan will live for twenty thousand years… the children of humans with a twenty-thousand-year lifespan will live for forty thousand years… the children of humans with a forty-thousand-year lifespan will live for eighty thousand years… Among humans with an eighty-thousand-year lifespan, monks, girls will be marriageable at the age of five hundred.

## Saṅkharājauppatti

### The Arising of King Saṅkha

106. ‘‘Asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu tayo ābādhā bhavissanti, icchā, anasanaṃ, jarā. Asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu ayaṃ jambudīpo iddho ceva bhavissati phīto ca, kukkuṭasampātikā gāmanigamarājadhāniyo [gāmanigamajanapadā rājadhāniyo (ka.)]. Asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu ayaṃ jambudīpo avīci maññe phuṭo bhavissati manussehi, seyyathāpi naḷavanaṃ vā saravanaṃ [sāravanaṃ (syā.)] vā. Asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu ayaṃ bārāṇasī ketumatī nāma rājadhānī bhavissati iddhā ceva phītā ca bahujanā ca ākiṇṇamanussā ca subhikkhā ca. Asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu imasmiṃ jambudīpe caturāsītinagarasahassāni bhavissanti ketumatīrājadhānīpamukhāni. Asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu ketumatiyā rājadhāniyā saṅkho nāma rājā uppajjissati cakkavattī dhammiko dhammarājā cāturanto vijitāvī janapadatthāvariyappatto sattaratanasamannāgato. Tassimāni satta ratanāni bhavissanti, seyyathidaṃ, cakkaratanaṃ hatthiratanaṃ assaratanaṃ maṇiratanaṃ itthiratanaṃ gahapatiratanaṃ pariṇāyakaratanameva sattamaṃ. Parosahassaṃ kho panassa puttā bhavissanti sūrā vīraṅgarūpā parasenappamaddanā. So imaṃ pathaviṃ sāgarapariyantaṃ adaṇḍena asatthena dhammena abhivijiya ajjhāvasissati.

106."Monks, when humans live for eighty thousand years, three afflictions will arise: desire, lack of food, and old age. Monks, when humans live for eighty thousand years, this Jambudīpa will be prosperous and flourishing, with villages, towns, and royal capitals as close together as where a crow might alight. Monks, when humans live for eighty thousand years, this Jambudīpa will be so crowded with people that it will be like Avīci hell, like a thicket of reeds or a dense grove of bamboo. Monks, when humans live for eighty thousand years, this city of Bārāṇasī will be the royal capital named Ketumatī—prosperous, flourishing, populous, teeming with people, and abundant in food. Monks, when humans live for eighty thousand years, there will be eighty-four thousand cities in this Jambudīpa, with Ketumatī as the foremost. Monks, when humans live for eighty thousand years, a king named Saṅkha will arise in the royal capital of Ketumatī—a wheel-turning monarch, a righteous king of the Dhamma, conqueror of the four quarters, who has attained stability in his country, endowed with the seven treasures. These will be his seven treasures: the wheel-treasure, the elephant-treasure, the horse-treasure, the jewel-treasure, the woman-treasure, the householder-treasure, and the advisor-treasure as the seventh. He will have over a thousand sons—heroic, of powerful form, and capable of crushing foreign armies. He will conquer and rule this earth bounded by the ocean without violence or weapons, by means of the Dhamma."

## Metteyyabuddhuppādo

### The Arising of the Buddha Metteyya.

107. ‘‘Asītivassasahassāyukesu, bhikkhave, manussesu metteyyo nāma bhagavā loke uppajjissati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā. Seyyathāpāhametarahi loke uppanno arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā. So imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedessati, seyyathāpāhametarahi imaṃ lokaṃ sadevakaṃ samārakaṃ sabrahmakaṃ sassamaṇabrāhmaṇiṃ pajaṃ sadevamanussaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedemi. So dhammaṃ desessati ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsessati; seyyathāpāhametarahi dhammaṃ desemi ādikalyāṇaṃ majjhekalyāṇaṃ pariyosānakalyāṇaṃ sātthaṃ sabyañjanaṃ kevalaparipuṇṇaṃ parisuddhaṃ brahmacariyaṃ pakāsemi. So anekasahassaṃ [anekasatasahassaṃ (ka.)] bhikkhusaṃghaṃ pariharissati, seyyathāpāhametarahi anekasataṃ bhikkhusaṃghaṃ pariharāmi.

107.“Monks, when human beings live for eighty thousand years, the Blessed One named Metteyya will arise in the world—an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, the Sublime One, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One. Just as I have now arisen in the world as an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in knowledge and conduct, the Sublime One, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One, so too will he. He will realize this world with its devas, Māras, and Brahmās, its population of recluses and brahmins, its gods and humans, having realized it for himself with direct knowledge, and make it known to others, just as I now realize and make known this world. He will teach the Dhamma—excellent in the beginning, excellent in the middle, excellent in the end, with its meaning and phrasing—revealing the holy life in all its purity and perfection, just as I now teach the Dhamma. He will be attended by a monastic community of many thousands, just as I am now attended by a community of many hundreds.”

108. ‘‘Atha kho, bhikkhave, saṅkho nāma rājā yo so yūpo raññā mahāpanādena kārāpito. Taṃ yūpaṃ ussāpetvā ajjhāvasitvā taṃ datvā vissajjitvā samaṇabrāhmaṇakapaṇaddhikavaṇibbakayācakānaṃ dānaṃ datvā metteyyassa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa santike kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajissati. So evaṃ pabbajito samāno eko vūpakaṭṭho appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto nacirasseva yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti, tadanuttaraṃ brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharissati.

108.“Then, monks, King Saṅkha will raise up that palace which was built by King Mahāpanāda. Having dwelt in it, and having given it away and relinquished it, he will give a gift to recluses and brahmins, the poor, the needy, and beggars. Then, having shaved off his hair and beard in the presence of the Blessed One Metteyya, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, and donning the saffron robes, he will go forth from the household life into homelessness. Having gone forth thus, dwelling alone, secluded, diligent, ardent, and resolute, before long he will realize for himself with direct knowledge that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, and having attained it, he will dwell therein.”

109. ‘‘Attadīpā, bhikkhave, viharatha attasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā, dhammadīpā dhammasaraṇā anaññasaraṇā. Kathañca, bhikkhave, bhikkhu attadīpo viharati attasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo dhammadīpo dhammasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Vedanāsu vedanānupassī…pe… citte cittānupassī…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhu attadīpo viharati attasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo dhammadīpo dhammasaraṇo anaññasaraṇo.

109.“Be islands unto yourselves, monks, be your own refuge, with no other refuge; let the Dhamma be your island, the Dhamma your refuge, with no other refuge. And how, monks, does a monk dwell as an island unto himself, his own refuge, with no other refuge, with the Dhamma as his island, the Dhamma as his refuge, with no other refuge? Here, monks, a monk dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and sorrow in regard to the world. He dwells contemplating feelings in feelings… mind in mind… phenomena in phenomena, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and sorrow in regard to the world. Thus, monks, does a monk dwell as an island unto himself, his own refuge, with no other refuge, with the Dhamma as his island, the Dhamma as his refuge, with no other refuge.”

## Bhikkhunoāyuvaṇṇādivaḍḍhanakathā

### The Discourse on the Increase of a Monk’s Life, Beauty, and So On.

110. ‘‘Gocare, bhikkhave, caratha sake pettike visaye. Gocare, bhikkhave, carantā sake pettike visaye āyunāpi vaḍḍhissatha, vaṇṇenapi vaḍḍhissatha, sukhenapi vaḍḍhissatha, bhogenapi vaḍḍhissatha, balenapi vaḍḍhissatha.

110.“Wander, monks, in your own pasture, your own ancestral domain. Wandering, monks, in your own pasture, your own ancestral domain, you will increase in life, beauty, happiness, prosperity, and strength.”

‘‘Kiñca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno āyusmiṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, cittasamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīmaṃsāsamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti. So imesaṃ catunnaṃ iddhipādānaṃ bhāvitattā bahulīkatattā ākaṅkhamāno kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno āyusmiṃ.

“And what, monks, is a monk’s increase in life? Here, monks, a monk develops the basis for spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to desire and the volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to energy and the volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to mind and the volitional formations of striving. He develops the basis for spiritual power endowed with samādhi due to investigation and the volitional formations of striving. Having developed and cultivated these four bases for spiritual power, if he wishes, he might remain for an aeon or what remains of an aeon. This, monks, is a monk’s increase in life.”

‘‘Kiñca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno vaṇṇasmiṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlavā hoti, pātimokkhasaṃvarasaṃvuto viharati ācāragocarasampanno, aṇumattesu vajjesu bhayadassāvī, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno vaṇṇasmiṃ.

“And what, monks, is a monk’s beauty? Here, monks, a monk is virtuous; he dwells restrained by the Pātimokkha, possessed of good conduct and proper sphere of activity, seeing danger in the slightest faults. He undertakes and trains himself in the training rules. This, monks, is a monk’s beauty.”

‘‘Kiñca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno sukhasmiṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā…pe… dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ…pe… tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ…pe… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno, sukhasmiṃ.

“And what, monks, is a monk’s increase in happiness? Here, monks, quite secluded from sensual pleasures, secluded from unwholesome states, a monk enters and dwells in the first jhāna, which is accompanied by thought and examination, with rapture and pleasure born of seclusion. With the stilling of thought and examination, he enters and dwells in the second jhāna… the third jhāna… the fourth jhāna. This, monks, is a monk’s increase in happiness.”

‘‘Kiñca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno bhogasmiṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu mettāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharati tathā dutiyaṃ. Tathā tatiyaṃ. Tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ mettāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena pharitvā viharati. Karuṇāsahagatena cetasā…pe… muditāsahagatena cetasā…pe… upekkhāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharati. Tathā dutiyaṃ. Tathā tatiyaṃ. Tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ upekkhāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena pharitvā viharati. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno bhogasmiṃ.

“And what, monks, is a monk’s increase in prosperity? Here, monks, a monk dwells pervading one direction with a mind imbued with loving-kindness, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, identifying himself with all, he dwells pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with loving-kindness, vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility, without ill will. With a mind imbued with compassion… With a mind imbued with appreciative joy… With a mind imbued with equanimity, he pervades one direction, likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, identifying himself with all, he dwells pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with equanimity, vast, exalted, measureless, without hostility, without ill will. This, monks, is a monk’s increase in prosperity.”

‘‘Kiñca, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno balasmiṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Idaṃ kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno balasmiṃ.

“And what, monks, is a monk’s strength? Here, monks, a monk, through the ending of the taints, having realized for himself with direct knowledge, attains and dwells in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, in this very life. This, monks, is a monk’s strength.”

‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, aññaṃ ekabalampi samanupassāmi yaṃ evaṃ duppasahaṃ, yathayidaṃ, bhikkhave, mārabalaṃ. Kusalānaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammānaṃ samādānahetu evamidaṃ puññaṃ pavaḍḍhatī’’ti. Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

“Monks, I do not see even one other force so hard to withstand as this, the force of Māra. It is because of undertaking wholesome qualities, monks, that this merit increases.” This is what the Blessed One said. The monks, pleased, rejoiced at the Blessed One’s words.

Cakkavattisuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ tatiyaṃ.

The Discourse on the Wheel-Turning Monarch, the Third.
