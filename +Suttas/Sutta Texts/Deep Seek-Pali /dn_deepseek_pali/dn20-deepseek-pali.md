# Mahāsamayasuttaṃ

## The Great Assembly Discourse

331. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ mahāvane mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi sabbeheva arahantehi; dasahi ca lokadhātūhi devatā yebhuyyena sannipatitā honti bhagavantaṃ dassanāya bhikkhusaṅghañca. Atha kho catunnaṃ suddhāvāsakāyikānaṃ devatānaṃ [devānaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)] etadahosi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ mahāvane mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi sabbeheva arahantehi; dasahi ca lokadhātūhi devatā yebhuyyena sannipatitā honti bhagavantaṃ dassanāya bhikkhusaṅghañca. Yaṃnūna mayampi yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato santike paccekaṃ gāthaṃ [paccekagāthaṃ (sī. syā. pī.), paccekagāthā (ka. sī.)] bhāseyyāmā’’ti.

331.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Great Wood, together with a great assembly of bhikkhus, with five hundred bhikkhus, all of them arahants. And deities from ten world-systems had mostly gathered to see the Blessed One and the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Then it occurred to four deities of the Pure Abodes: "This Blessed One is dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Great Wood, together with a great assembly of bhikkhus, with five hundred bhikkhus, all of them arahants; and deities from ten world-systems had mostly gathered to see the Blessed One and the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. What if we too were to approach the Blessed One? Having approached, let us each recite a verse in the presence of the Blessed One."

332. Atha kho tā devatā seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evameva suddhāvāsesu devesu antarahitā bhagavato purato pāturahesuṃ. Atha kho tā devatā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho ekā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

332.Then those deities, just as a strong man might extend his flexed arm or flex his extended arm, so they disappeared from the deities of the Pure Abodes and appeared before the Blessed One. Then those deities paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, one deity spoke this verse in the presence of the Blessed One:

‘‘Mahāsamayo pavanasmiṃ, devakāyā samāgatā;Āgatamha imaṃ dhammasamayaṃ, dakkhitāye aparājitasaṅgha’’nti.

A great assembly is in the wood; companies of deities have gathered. We have come to this assembly of Dhamma to see the unconquered Saṅgha.

Atha kho aparā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

Then another deity spoke this verse in the Blessed One’s presence:

‘‘Tatra bhikkhavo samādahaṃsu, cittamattano ujukaṃ akaṃsu [ujukamakaṃsu (sī. syā. pī.)];Sārathīva nettāni gahetvā, indriyāni rakkhanti paṇḍitā’’ti.

There, bhikkhus, they collected, they made their minds upright; like charioteers holding the reins, the wise guard their faculties.

Atha kho aparā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

Then another deity spoke this verse in the presence of the Blessed One:

‘‘Chetvā khīlaṃ chetvā palighaṃ, indakhīlaṃ ūhacca [uhacca (ka.)] manejā;Te caranti suddhā vimalā, cakkhumatā sudantā susunāgā’’ti.

Having cut off the stake, having cut off the bar, having uprooted the pillar, without craving; they wander pure, stainless, with vision, well-tamed, truly noble ones.

Atha kho aparā devatā bhagavato santike imaṃ gāthaṃ abhāsi –

Then another deity spoke this verse in the Blessed One's presence:

‘‘Yekeci buddhaṃ saraṇaṃ gatāse, na te gamissanti apāyabhūmiṃ;Pahāya mānusaṃ dehaṃ, devakāyaṃ paripūressantī’’ti.

Whoever has gone to the Buddha for refuge, they will not go to the plane of misery. Having abandoned the human body, they will fill the divine host.

## Devatāsannipātā

### Assembly of Deities

333. Atha kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘yebhuyyena, bhikkhave, dasasu lokadhātūsu devatā sannipatitā honti [( ) sī. ipotthakesu natthi], tathāgataṃ dassanāya bhikkhusaṅghañca. Yepi te, bhikkhave, ahesuṃ atītamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā, tesampi bhagavantānaṃ etaṃparamāyeva [etaparamāyeva (sī. syā. pī.)] devatā sannipatitā ahesuṃ seyyathāpi mayhaṃ etarahi. Yepi te, bhikkhave, bhavissanti anāgatamaddhānaṃ arahanto sammāsambuddhā, tesampi bhagavantānaṃ etaṃparamāyeva devatā sannipatitā bhavissanti seyyathāpi mayhaṃ etarahi. Ācikkhissāmi, bhikkhave, devakāyānaṃ nāmāni; kittayissāmi, bhikkhave, devakāyānaṃ nāmāni; desessāmi, bhikkhave, devakāyānaṃ nāmāni. Taṃ suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasikarotha, bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ.

333.Then the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "For the most part, bhikkhus, deities from ten world-systems have gathered to see the Tathāgata and the Saṅgha of bhikkhus. Those Blessed Ones, bhikkhus, who in the past were Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones—for those Blessed Ones too, this was the supreme assembly of deities, just as it is for me now. Those Blessed Ones, bhikkhus, who in the future will be Arahants, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Ones—for those Blessed Ones too, this will be the supreme assembly of deities, just as it is for me now. I will announce, bhikkhus, the names of the classes of deities; I will proclaim, bhikkhus, the names of the classes of deities; I will teach, bhikkhus, the names of the classes of deities. Listen, attend carefully, I will speak." "Yes, Venerable Sir," those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One.

334. Bhagavā etadavoca –

334.The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Silokamanukassāmi, yattha bhummā tadassitā;Ye sitā girigabbharaṃ, pahitattā samāhitā.

I will recite verses, where the earth-dwelling deities reside; those who dwell in mountain caves, dedicated and composed.

‘‘Puthūsīhāva sallīnā, lomahaṃsābhisambhuno;Odātamanasā suddhā, vippasannamanāvilā’’ [vippasannāmanāvilā (pī. ka.)].

Like lions withdrawn, many, overcoming hair-raising terror; pure, with minds cleansed, serene and unagitated.

Bhiyyo pañcasate ñatvā, vane kāpilavatthave;Tato āmantayī satthā, sāvake sāsane rate.

Knowing the five hundred bhikkhus in the Kapilavatthu forest, then the Teacher addressed his disciples, delighting in the Dispensation.

‘‘Devakāyā abhikkantā, te vijānātha bhikkhavo’’;Te ca ātappamakaruṃ, sutvā buddhassa sāsanaṃ.

Companies of deities have arrived, bhikkhus, know them!" And they applied themselves to effort, having heard the Buddha's teaching.

Tesaṃ pāturahu ñāṇaṃ, amanussānadassanaṃ;Appeke satamaddakkhuṃ, sahassaṃ atha sattariṃ.

To them appeared the knowledge, the vision of non-human beings: some saw a hundred, others a thousand, and still others seventy.

Sataṃ eke sahassānaṃ, amanussānamaddasuṃ;Appekenantamaddakkhuṃ, disā sabbā phuṭā ahuṃ.

Some saw a hundred thousand non-human beings; others saw countless. All directions were filled.

Tañca sabbaṃ abhiññāya, vavatthitvāna [vavakkhitvāna (sī. syā. pī.), avekkhitvāna (ṭīkā)] cakkhumā;Tato āmantayī satthā, sāvake sāsane rate.

Having fully understood all that, the One with Vision clearly established it. Then the Teacher addressed his disciples, who delighted in the Dispensation.

‘‘Devakāyā abhikkantā, te vijānātha bhikkhavo;Ye vohaṃ kittayissāmi, girāhi anupubbaso.

Companies of deities have arrived, bhikkhus, know them! Those whom I shall now proclaim, in order, with words.

335.‘‘Sattasahassā te yakkhā, bhummā kāpilavatthavā.

335.Seven thousand yakkhas, terrestrial, dwellers in Kapilavatthu.

Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Chasahassā hemavatā, yakkhā nānattavaṇṇino;Iddhimanto jutīmanto [jutīmanto (sī. pī.)], vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Six thousand from Himavat, yakkhas of diverse colors, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Sātāgirā tisahassā, yakkhā nānattavaṇṇino;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

From Sātāgira came three thousand, yakkhas of various colors, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Iccete soḷasasahassā, yakkhā nānattavaṇṇino;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Thus these sixteen thousand yakkhas, of various colors, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Vessāmittā pañcasatā, yakkhā nānattavaṇṇino;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Five hundred Vessāmittā yakkhas of various colors, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Kumbhīro rājagahiko, vepullassa nivesanaṃ;Bhiyyo naṃ satasahassaṃ, yakkhānaṃ payirupāsati;Kumbhīro rājagahiko, sopāgā samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Kumbhīra of Rājagaha, his dwelling is Vepulla, more than a hundred thousand yakkhas attend upon him; Kumbhīra of Rājagaha, he too came to the assembly in the forest.

336.‘‘Purimañca disaṃ rājā, dhataraṭṭho pasāsati.

336.King Dhataraṭṭha governs the eastern direction.

Gandhabbānaṃ adhipati, mahārājā yasassiso.

Lord of the Gandhabbas, the glorious great king.

‘‘Puttāpi tassa bahavo, indanāmā mahabbalā;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Many are his sons, named Inda, of great power, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Dakkhiṇañca disaṃ rājā, virūḷho taṃ pasāsati [tappasāsati (syā.)];Kumbhaṇḍānaṃ adhipati, mahārājā yasassiso.

To the southern direction, King Virūḷha rules, the lord of the Kumbhaṇḍas, a great and glorious monarch.

‘‘Puttāpi tassa bahavo, indanāmā mahabbalā;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Many were his sons, named Inda, of great might, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Pacchimañca disaṃ rājā, virūpakkho pasāsati;Nāgānañca adhipati, mahārājā yasassiso.

The king Virūpakkha rules the western quarter; he is the lord of Nāgas, a great king, glorious.

‘‘Puttāpi tassa bahavo, indanāmā mahabbalā;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Many were his sons, named Inda, of great might, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Uttarañca disaṃ rājā, kuvero taṃ pasāsati;Yakkhānañca adhipati, mahārājā yasassiso.

To the northern direction, King Kuvera rules; he is lord of the Yakkhas, the illustrious Great King.

‘‘Puttāpi tassa bahavo, indanāmā mahabbalā;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Many were his sons, named Inda, of great might, endowed with psychic power, radiant, beautiful, and renowned, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

‘‘Purimaṃ disaṃ dhataraṭṭho, dakkhiṇena virūḷhako;Pacchimena virūpakkho, kuvero uttaraṃ disaṃ.

Dhataraṭṭha to the east, Virūḷhaka to the south; Virūpakkha to the west, Kuvera to the north.

‘‘Cattāro te mahārājā, samantā caturo disā;Daddallamānā [daddaḷhamānā (ka.)] aṭṭhaṃsu, vane kāpilavatthave.

Those four great kings, from all four directions, stood resplendent; they stood in the Kapilavatthu forest, shining brightly.

337.‘‘Tesaṃ māyāvino dāsā, āguṃ [āgū (syā.), āgu (sī. pī.) evamuparipi] vañcanikā saṭhā.

337.Their deceitful servants came, tricksters, deceivers, and cheats.

Māyā kuṭeṇḍu viṭeṇḍu [veṭeṇḍu (sī. syā. pī.)], viṭucca [viṭū ca (syā.)] viṭuṭo saha.

Māyā, Kuṭeṇḍu, Viṭeṇḍu, Viṭucca, and Viṭuṭa came together.

‘‘Candano kāmaseṭṭho ca, kinnighaṇḍu [kinnughaṇḍu (sī. syā. pī.)] nighaṇḍu ca;Panādo opamañño ca, devasūto ca mātali.

Candana and Kāmaseṭṭha, Kinnighaṇḍu and Nighaṇḍu; Panāda and Opamañña, and Mātali the divine charioteer.

‘‘Cittaseno ca gandhabbo, naḷorājā janesabho [janosabho (syā.)];Āgā pañcasikho ceva, timbarū sūriyavaccasā [suriyavaccasā (sī. pī.)].

Cittasena the Gandhabba, Naḷorāja, and Janesabha; Pañcasikha also came, with Timbaru and Sūriyavaccasā.

‘‘Ete caññe ca rājāno, gandhabbā saha rājubhi;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

These and other kings, with Gandhabbas and their retinues, rejoicing, they approached the forest assembly of bhikkhus.

338.‘‘Athāguṃ nāgasā nāgā, vesālā sahatacchakā.

338.Then came the Nāgas from Nāgasā, and the Nāgas from Vesālī together with Tacchaka.

Kambalassatarā āguṃ, pāyāgā saha ñātibhi.

Kambala and Assatara came, and those from Pāyāga together with their relatives.

‘‘Yāmunā dhataraṭṭhā ca, āgū nāgā yasassino;Erāvaṇo mahānāgo, sopāgā samitiṃ vanaṃ.

The Nāgas from Yamunā and the Dhataraṭṭhas came, glorious Nāgas; the great Nāga Erāvaṇa also came to the assembly in the forest.

‘‘Ye nāgarāje sahasā haranti, dibbā dijā pakkhi visuddhacakkhū;Vehāyasā [vehāsayā (sī. pī.)] te vanamajjhapattā, citrā supaṇṇā iti tesa nāmaṃ.

Those who swiftly seize the Nāga kings, divine, twice-born birds with purified eyes; soaring through the sky, they arrived in the forest's midst. Colorful Supaṇṇas—thus is their name.

‘‘Abhayaṃ tadā nāgarājānamāsi, supaṇṇato khemamakāsi buddho;Saṇhāhi vācāhi upavhayantā, nāgā supaṇṇā saraṇamakaṃsu buddhaṃ.

Then the Nāga kings had safety, the Buddha made security from the Supaṇṇas; calling to them with gentle words, the Nāgas and Supaṇṇas went for refuge to the Buddha.

339.‘‘Jitā vajirahatthena, samuddaṃ asurāsitā.

339.Conquered by the thunderbolt-handed one, the Asuras dwelt in the ocean.

Bhātaro vāsavassete, iddhimanto yasassino.

These brothers of Vāsava are mighty and glorious.

‘‘Kālakañcā mahābhismā [kālakañjā mahābhiṃsā (sī. pī.)], asurā dānaveghasā;Vepacitti sucitti ca, pahārādo namucī saha.

Kālakañcā, greatly terrifying, and the Asuras Dānaveghasā; Vepacitti, Sucitti, Pahārāda, and Namucī together.

‘‘Satañca baliputtānaṃ, sabbe verocanāmakā;Sannayhitvā balisenaṃ [balīsenaṃ (syā.)], rāhubhaddamupāgamuṃ;Samayodāni bhaddante, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

“And a hundred sons of Bali, all named Verocana, having prepared their forces, approached Rāhu Bhadda. ‘Now is the time, good sir, for the assembly of monks in the forest.’

340.‘‘Āpo ca devā pathavī, tejo vāyo tadāgamuṃ.

340.The deities of water, earth, fire, and wind then arrived.

Varuṇā vāraṇā [vāruṇā (syā.)] devā, somo ca yasasā saha.

The deities Varuṇa and Vāraṇa, and Soma together with Yasa.

‘‘Mettā karuṇā kāyikā, āguṃ devā yasassino;Dasete dasadhā kāyā, sabbe nānattavaṇṇino.

The deities of loving-kindness and compassion came, glorious deities; these ten groups of deities, tenfold, all of diverse hues.

‘‘Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Endowed with power, radiant, splendid, and glorious, rejoicing, they approached the assembly of monks in the forest.

‘‘Veṇḍudevā sahali ca [veṇhūca devā sahalīca (sī. pī.)], asamā ca duve yamā;Candassūpanisā devā, candamāguṃ purakkhatvā.

The Veṇḍu deities, Sahali, Asamā, and the two Yamas; the deities dependent on the moon came, having the moon at their front.

‘‘Sūriyassūpanisā [suriyassūpanisā (sī. syā. pī.)] devā, sūriyamāguṃ purakkhatvā;Nakkhattāni purakkhatvā, āguṃ mandavalāhakā.

The deities dependent on the sun came, having the sun at their front; the deities dependent on the constellations came, having the constellations at their front; and the Mandavalāhaka deities arrived.

‘‘Vasūnaṃ vāsavo seṭṭho, sakkopāgā purindado;Dasete dasadhā kāyā, sabbe nānattavaṇṇino.

Vāsava, chief of the Vasus, Sakka, Purindada, came; these ten groups of deities, tenfold, all of diverse hues.

‘‘Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Endowed with power, radiant, splendid, and glorious, rejoicing, they approached the assembly of monks in the forest.

‘‘Athāguṃ sahabhū devā, jalamaggisikhāriva;Ariṭṭhakā ca rojā ca, umāpupphanibhāsino.

Then came the Sahabhū deities, burning like flames of fire; Ariṭṭhaka and Rojā, and the Umāpupphanibhāsin deities.

‘‘Varuṇā sahadhammā ca, accutā ca anejakā;Sūleyyarucirā āguṃ, āguṃ vāsavanesino;Dasete dasadhā kāyā, sabbe nānattavaṇṇino.

Varuṇa and Sahadhamma, Accuta and Anejaka, Suleyya and Rucira came, and the Vāsavanesī deities came; these ten groups of deities, tenfold, all of diverse hues.

‘‘Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Endowed with power, radiant, splendid, and glorious, rejoicing, they approached the assembly of monks in the forest.

‘‘Samānā mahāsamanā, mānusā mānusuttamā;Khiḍḍāpadosikā āguṃ, āguṃ manopadosikā.

The Samānā and Mahāsamānā, the Mānusā and Mānusuttamā; the Khiḍḍāpadosikā deities came, and the Manopadosikā deities came.

‘‘Athāguṃ harayo devā, ye ca lohitavāsino;Pāragā mahāpāragā, āguṃ devā yasassino;Dasete dasadhā kāyā, sabbe nānattavaṇṇino.

Then came the Hari deities, and those named Lohitavāsin; the Pāragā and Mahāpāragā, and glorious deities arrived; these ten groups of deities, tenfold, all of diverse hues.

‘‘Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Endowed with power, radiant, splendid, and glorious, rejoicing, they approached the assembly of monks in the forest.

‘‘Sukkā karambhā [karumhā (sī. syā. pī.)] aruṇā, āguṃ veghanasā saha;Odātagayhā pāmokkhā, āguṃ devā vicakkhaṇā.

The Sukkā, Karambhā, and Aruṇā came, along with the Veghanasā; the chief deities named Odātagayhā came, and the deities named Vicakkhaṇā arrived.

‘‘Sadāmattā hāragajā, missakā ca yasassino;Thanayaṃ āga pajjunno, yo disā abhivassati.

The Sadāmattā and Hāragajā, and the glorious Missaka deities; Pajjunno, the king of deities, who rains on the regions, came thundering.

‘‘Dasete dasadhā kāyā, sabbe nānattavaṇṇino;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

These ten groups of deities, tenfold, all of diverse hues; endowed with power, radiant, splendid, and glorious, rejoicing, they approached the assembly of monks in the forest.

‘‘Khemiyā tusitā yāmā, kaṭṭhakā ca yasassino;Lambītakā lāmaseṭṭhā, jotināmā ca āsavā;Nimmānaratino āguṃ, athāguṃ paranimmitā.

The Khemiyā, Tusitā, and Yāmā, and the glorious Kaṭṭhaka deities; the Lambītakā and Lāmaseṭṭhā, the Jotināmā and Āsavā; the Nimmānaratī deities came, and then the Paranimmitā deities.

‘‘Dasete dasadhā kāyā, sabbe nānattavaṇṇino;Iddhimanto jutimanto, vaṇṇavanto yasassino;Modamānā abhikkāmuṃ, bhikkhūnaṃ samitiṃ vanaṃ.

These ten groups of deities, tenfold, all of diverse hues; endowed with power, radiant, splendid, and glorious, rejoicing, they approached the assembly of monks in the forest.

‘‘Saṭṭhete devanikāyā, sabbe nānattavaṇṇino;Nāmanvayena āgacchuṃ [āgañchuṃ (sī. syā. pī.)], ye caññe sadisā saha.

These sixty groups of deities, all of diverse hues, came according to their names, along with others similar to them.

‘‘‘Pavuṭṭhajātimakhilaṃ [pavutthajātiṃ akhilaṃ (sī. pī.)], oghatiṇṇamanāsavaṃ;Dakkhemoghataraṃ nāgaṃ, candaṃva asitātigaṃ’.

‘Let us see the Noble Sangha, whose birth is gone, without a stake, who has crossed the floods, taintless; and let us see the Nāga who has crossed the floods, like the moon gone beyond darkness.’

341.‘‘Subrahmā paramatto ca [paramattho ca (ka.)], puttā iddhimato saha.

341.Subrahmā and Paramatta, along with the sons of the Powerful One, [came].

Sanaṅkumāro tisso ca, sopāga samitiṃ vanaṃ.

Sanankumāra and Tissa also came to the assembly in the forest.

‘‘Sahassaṃ brahmalokānaṃ, mahābrahmābhitiṭṭhati;Upapanno jutimanto, bhismākāyo yasassiso.

A thousand Brahmās from the Brahmā worlds, Great Brahmās who stand supreme, came; born there, radiant, of terrifying form, and glorious.

‘‘Dasettha issarā āguṃ, paccekavasavattino;Tesañca majjhato āga, hārito parivārito.

Among them came ten lords, each wielding power independently; and from their midst came Hārita, surrounded by his retinue.

342.‘‘Te ca sabbe abhikkante, sainde [sinde (syā.)] deve sabrahmake.

342.And when all those had come, the deities with Indra, and the Brahmās.

Mārasenā abhikkāmi, passa kaṇhassa mandiyaṃ.

Māra’s army advanced—behold the foolishness of the Dark One!

‘‘‘Etha gaṇhatha bandhatha, rāgena baddhamatthu vo;Samantā parivāretha, mā vo muñcittha koci naṃ’.

‘Come, seize and bind! May you all be bound by lust! Surround them on all sides! Let none of you release even one of them!’

‘‘Iti tattha mahāseno, kaṇho senaṃ apesayi;Pāṇinā talamāhacca, saraṃ katvāna bheravaṃ.

Thus there, the great general, the Dark One, sent forth his army; striking the ground with his hand, making a terrifying sound.

‘‘Yathā pāvussako megho, thanayanto savijjuko; +Tadā so paccudāvatti, saṅkuddho asayaṃvase [asayaṃvasī (sī. pī.)].

Just as a rain cloud thundering with lightning, so he turned back, enraged and not in his own power.

343. Tañca sabbaṃ abhiññāya, vavatthitvāna cakkhumā.

343.Having fully understood all that, the One with Vision established himself.

Tato āmantayī satthā, sāvake sāsane rate.

Then the Teacher addressed his disciples, who delighted in the teaching.

‘‘Mārasenā abhikkantā, te vijānātha bhikkhavo;Te ca ātappamakaruṃ, sutvā buddhassa sāsanaṃ;Vītarāgehi pakkāmuṃ, nesaṃ lomāpi iñjayuṃ.

‘Māra’s army has advanced, O monks, recognize them!’ And they exerted effort upon hearing the Buddha’s teaching. Māra and his army departed from the passionless ones; they did not stir even a hair of theirs.

‘‘‘Sabbe vijitasaṅgāmā, bhayātītā yasassino;Modanti saha bhūtehi, sāvakā te janesutā’’ti.

‘All have won the battle, gone beyond fear, and are glorious; they rejoice together with the Noble Ones, those disciples well-known among people.’

## Mahāsamayasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ sattamaṃ.

### The Discourse on the Great Assembly, the seventh, is concluded.
