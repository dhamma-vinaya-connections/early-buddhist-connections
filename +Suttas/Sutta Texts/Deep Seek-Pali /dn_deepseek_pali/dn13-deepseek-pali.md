# Tevijjasuttaṃ

## The Tevijja Sutta

518. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pañcamattehi bhikkhusatehi yena manasākaṭaṃ nāma kosalānaṃ brāhmaṇagāmo tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā manasākaṭe viharati uttarena manasākaṭassa aciravatiyā nadiyā tīre ambavane.

518.Thus I have heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was wandering in the Kosala country with a large Saṅgha of monks, with about five hundred monks, when he arrived at a Kosalan brahmin village named Manasākaṭa. There the Blessed One stayed in Manasākaṭa, to the north of Manasākaṭa, on the bank of the Aciravatī River, in the mango grove.

519. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā abhiññātā abhiññātā brāhmaṇamahāsālā manasākaṭe paṭivasanti, seyyathidaṃ – caṅkī brāhmaṇo tārukkho brāhmaṇo pokkharasāti brāhmaṇo jāṇusoṇi brāhmaṇo todeyyo brāhmaṇo aññe ca abhiññātā abhiññātā brāhmaṇamahāsālā.

519.Now at that time, many well-known and distinguished brahmins were staying at Manasakata, namely, the brahmin Canki, the brahmin Tarukkha, the brahmin Pokkharasāti, the brahmin Janussoni, the brahmin Todeyya, and other well-known and distinguished brahmins.

520. Atha kho vāseṭṭhabhāradvājānaṃ māṇavānaṃ jaṅghavihāraṃ anucaṅkamantānaṃ anuvicarantānaṃ maggāmagge kathā udapādi. Atha kho vāseṭṭho māṇavo evamāha – ‘‘ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāya, yvāyaṃ akkhāto brāhmaṇena pokkharasātinā’’ti. Bhāradvājopi māṇavo evamāha – ‘‘ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko, niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāya, yvāyaṃ akkhāto brāhmaṇena tārukkhenā’’ti. Neva kho asakkhi vāseṭṭho māṇavo bhāradvājaṃ māṇavaṃ saññāpetuṃ, na pana asakkhi bhāradvājo māṇavopi vāseṭṭhaṃ māṇavaṃ saññāpetuṃ.

520.Then, as the young brahmins Vāseṭṭha and Bhāradvāja were walking and wandering for exercise, a discussion about the path and the non-path arose between them. Then the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha said: "This is the straight path, this is the direct way that leads to deliverance, and leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā—that which has been declared by the brahmin Pokkharasāti." The young brahmin Bhāradvāja said: "This is the straight path, this is the direct way that leads to deliverance, and leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā—that which has been declared by the brahmin Tārukkha." The young brahmin Vāseṭṭha was not able to convince the young brahmin Bhāradvāja, nor was the young brahmin Bhāradvāja able to convince the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha.

521. Atha kho vāseṭṭho māṇavo bhāradvājaṃ māṇavaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho, bhāradvāja, samaṇo gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito manasākaṭe viharati uttarena manasākaṭassa aciravatiyā nadiyā tīre ambavane. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’’ti. Āyāma, bho bhāradvāja, yena samaṇo gotamo tenupasaṅkamissāma; upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ samaṇaṃ gotamaṃ pucchissāma. Yathā no samaṇo gotamo byākarissati, tathā naṃ dhāressāmā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho bhāradvājo māṇavo vāseṭṭhassa māṇavassa paccassosi.

521.Then the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha addressed the young brahmin Bhāradvāja: "This ascetic Gotama, Bhāradvāja, the son of the Sakyans, who went forth from the Sakyan clan, is staying at Manasākaṭa, north of Manasākaṭa on the bank of the river Aciravatī in the mango grove. Now a good report about Master Gotama has spread thus: 'Indeed, that Blessed One is an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, accomplished in true knowledge and conduct, fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed leader of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One.' Come, Bhāradvāja, let us approach the ascetic Gotama and ask him about this matter. As the ascetic Gotama explains it to us, so shall we remember it." "Yes, sir," replied the young brahmin Bhāradvāja to the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha.

## Maggāmaggakathā

### Discussion of the Path and the Non-Path

522. Atha kho vāseṭṭhabhāradvājā māṇavā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho vāseṭṭho māṇavo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idha, bho gotama, amhākaṃ jaṅghavihāraṃ anucaṅkamantānaṃ anuvicarantānaṃ maggāmagge kathā udapādi. Ahaṃ evaṃ vadāmi – ‘ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāya, yvāyaṃ akkhāto brāhmaṇena pokkharasātinā’ti. Bhāradvājo māṇavo evamāha – ‘ayameva ujumaggo ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāya, yvāyaṃ akkhāto brāhmaṇena tārukkhenā’ti. Ettha, bho gotama, attheva viggaho, atthi vivādo, atthi nānāvādo’’ti.

522.Then the young men Vāseṭṭha and Bhāradvāja approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. When the courteous and amiable talk was finished, they sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the young man Vāseṭṭha said to the Blessed One: “Here, Master Gotama, as we were walking and wandering for exercise, a discussion about the path and the non-path arose between us. I said: ‘This is the straight path, this is the direct way that leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā—that which has been declared by the brahmin Pokkharasāti.’ The young man Bhāradvāja said: ‘This is the straight path, this is the direct way that leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā—that which has been declared by the brahmin Tārukkha.’ Here, Master Gotama, there is indeed a dispute, a contention, a difference of opinion.”

523. ‘‘Iti kira, vāseṭṭha, tvaṃ evaṃ vadesi – ‘‘ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāya, yvāyaṃ akkhāto brāhmaṇena pokkharasātinā’’ti. Bhāradvājo māṇavo evamāha – ‘‘ayameva ujumaggo ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāya, yvāyaṃ akkhāto brāhmaṇena tārukkhenā’’ti. Atha kismiṃ pana vo, vāseṭṭha, viggaho, kismiṃ vivādo, kismiṃ nānāvādo’’ti?

523.“So it seems, Vāseṭṭha, that you say: ‘This is the straight path, this is the direct way out that leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā—that which has been declared by the brahmin Pokkharasāti.’ The young man Bhāradvāja says: ‘This is the straight path, this is the direct way out that leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā—that which has been declared by the brahmin Tārukkha.’ But in what, Vāseṭṭha, is your disagreement, in what is your dispute, in what is your difference of opinion?”

524. ‘‘Maggāmagge, bho gotama. Kiñcāpi, bho gotama, brāhmaṇā nānāmagge paññapenti, addhariyā brāhmaṇā tittiriyā brāhmaṇā chandokā brāhmaṇā bavhārijjhā brāhmaṇā, atha kho sabbāni tāni niyyānikā niyyanti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāya.

524.“Path and non-path, Master Gotama. Though, Master Gotama, brahmins expound various paths—Addhariya brahmins, Tittiriya brahmins, Chandoka brahmins, Bavharijja brahmins—yet all those paths are liberating; they lead one who practices them to companionship with Brahmā.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, gāmassa vā nigamassa vā avidūre bahūni cepi nānāmaggāni bhavanti, atha kho sabbāni tāni gāmasamosaraṇāni bhavanti; evameva kho, bho gotama, kiñcāpi brāhmaṇā nānāmagge paññapenti, addhariyā brāhmaṇā tittiriyā brāhmaṇā chandokā brāhmaṇā bavhārijjhā brāhmaṇā, atha kho sabbāni tāni niyyānikā niyyanti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyā’’ti.

“Just as, Master Gotama, near a village or town, there may be many different paths, yet all of them converge at the village; in the same way, Master Gotama, although brahmins expound various paths—Addhariya brahmins, Tittiriya brahmins, Chandoka brahmins, Bavharijja brahmins—yet all those paths are liberating; they lead one who practices them to companionship with Brahmā.”

## Vāseṭṭhamāṇavānuyogo

### The Examination of Vāseṭṭha

525. ‘‘Niyyantīti vāseṭṭha vadesi’’? ‘‘Niyyantīti, bho gotama, vadāmi’’. ‘‘Niyyantīti, vāseṭṭha, vadesi’’? ‘‘Niyyantīti, bho gotama, vadāmi’’. ‘‘Niyyantīti, vāseṭṭha, vadesi’’? ‘‘Niyyantī’’ti, bho gotama, vadāmi’’.

525.“Do you say they lead, Vāseṭṭha?” “I say they lead, Master Gotama.” “Do you say they lead, Vāseṭṭha?” “I say they lead, Master Gotama.” “Do you say they lead, Vāseṭṭha?” “I say they lead, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana, vāseṭṭha, atthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ekabrāhmaṇopi, yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“But what do you think, Vāseṭṭha, is there even one brahmin among the brahmins learned in the three Vedas who has seen Brahmā face to face?” “No, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana, vāseṭṭha, atthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ekācariyopi, yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“But what do you think, Vāseṭṭha, is there even one teacher of these brahmins learned in the three Vedas who has seen Brahmā face to face?” “No, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana, vāseṭṭha, atthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ekācariyapācariyopi, yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“But what do you think, Vāseṭṭha, is there even one teacher of teachers among these brahmins learned in the three Vedas who has seen Brahmā face to face?” “No, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana, vāseṭṭha, atthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ yāva sattamā ācariyāmahayugā [sattamācariyamahayugā (syā.)] yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“But what do you think, Vāseṭṭha, is there even one among these brahmins learned in the three Vedas, back to the seventh generation of teachers, by whom Brahmā has been seen face to face?” “No, Master Gotama.”

526. ‘‘Kiṃ pana, vāseṭṭha, yepi tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ pubbakā isayo mantānaṃ kattāro mantānaṃ pavattāro, yesamidaṃ etarahi tevijjā brāhmaṇā porāṇaṃ mantapadaṃ gītaṃ pavuttaṃ samihitaṃ [samīhitaṃ (syā.)], tadanugāyanti, tadanubhāsanti, bhāsitamanubhāsanti, vācitamanuvācenti, seyyathidaṃ – aṭṭhako vāmako vāmadevo vessāmitto yamataggi aṅgīraso bhāradvājo vāseṭṭho kassapo bhagu. Tepi evamāhaṃsu – ‘mayametaṃ jānāma, mayametaṃ passāma, yattha vā brahmā, yena vā brahmā, yahiṃ vā brahmā’’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

526.“But what do you think, Vāseṭṭha? Those ancient seers of the brahmins learned in the three Vedas, the makers of the hymns, the founders of the hymns—whose ancient verses the brahmins learned in the three Vedas today chant, recite, and compile, following their recitation and utterance—such as Aṭṭhaka, Vāmaka, Vāmadeva, Vessāmitta, Yamataggi, Aṅgīrasa, Bhāradvāja, Vāseṭṭha, Kassapa, and Bhagu—did even they say: ‘We know this, we see this: where Brahmā is, how Brahmā is, where Brahmā is’?” “No, Master Gotama.”

527. ‘‘Iti kira, vāseṭṭha, natthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ekabrāhmaṇopi, yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Natthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ekācariyopi, yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Natthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ekācariyapācariyopi, yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Natthi koci tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ yāva sattamā ācariyāmahayugā yena brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Yepi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ pubbakā isayo mantānaṃ kattāro mantānaṃ pavattāro, yesamidaṃ etarahi tevijjā brāhmaṇā porāṇaṃ mantapadaṃ gītaṃ pavuttaṃ samihitaṃ, tadanugāyanti, tadanubhāsanti, bhāsitamanubhāsanti, vācitamanuvācenti, seyyathidaṃ – aṭṭhako vāmako vāmadevo vessāmitto yamataggi aṅgīraso bhāradvājo vāseṭṭho kassapo bhagu, tepi na evamāhaṃsu – ‘mayametaṃ jānāma, mayametaṃ passāma, yattha vā brahmā, yena vā brahmā, yahiṃ vā brahmā’ti. Teva tevijjā brāhmaṇā evamāhaṃsu – ‘yaṃ na jānāma, yaṃ na passāma, tassa sahabyatāya maggaṃ desema. Ayameva ujumaggo ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko, niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyā’’’ti.

527.“So it seems, Vāseṭṭha, that among the brahmins learned in the three Vedas, there is not even a single brahmin by whom Brahmā has been seen face to face. There is not even a single teacher among them by whom Brahmā has been seen face to face, nor a teacher of teachers by whom Brahmā has been seen face to face, nor even anyone up to the seventh generation of teachers by whom Brahmā has been seen face to face. Even those ancient seers of the brahmins, the makers of the hymns, the founders of the hymns—whose ancient verses the brahmins learned in the three Vedas today chant, recite, and compile, following their recitation and utterance—such as Aṭṭhaka, Vāmaka, Vāmadeva, Vessāmitta, Yamataggi, Aṅgīrasa, Bhāradvāja, Vāseṭṭha, Kassapa, and Bhagu—even they did not say, ‘We know this; we see this: where Brahmā is, how Brahmā is, where Brahmā is.’ Yet these brahmins learned in the three Vedas declare, ‘We teach the path to companionship with that which we do not know, which we do not see. This alone is the straight path, this the direct way, the liberating way, that leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā.’”

528. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, nanu evaṃ sante tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti? ‘‘Addhā kho, bho gotama, evaṃ sante tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti.

528.“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? In that case, doesn’t it follow that the words of the brahmins learned in the three Vedas are unfounded?” “Indeed, Master Gotama, in that case, it follows that their words are unfounded.”

‘‘Sādhu, vāseṭṭha, te vata [teva (ka.)], vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā yaṃ na jānanti, yaṃ na passanti, tassa sahabyatāya maggaṃ desessanti. ‘Ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko, niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyā’ti, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

“Good, Vāseṭṭha. Indeed, Vāseṭṭha, it is not possible that those brahmins learned in the three Vedas, not knowing, not seeing, will teach the path to companionship with that which they do not know and do not see, saying, ‘This is the straight path, this is the direct way, the liberating way, that leads one who practices it to companionship with Brahmā’—such a thing is not possible.”

529. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, andhaveṇi paramparasaṃsattā purimopi na passati, majjhimopi na passati, pacchimopi na passati. Evameva kho, vāseṭṭha, andhaveṇūpamaṃ maññe tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ bhāsitaṃ, purimopi na passati, majjhimopi na passati, pacchimopi na passati. Tesamidaṃ tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ bhāsitaṃ hassakaññeva sampajjati, nāmakaññeva sampajjati, rittakaññeva sampajjati, tucchakaññeva sampajjati.

529.“Just as, Vāseṭṭha, a line of blind men, each holding onto the one in front, the first one does not see, nor does the middle one, nor the last one—so too, it seems to me, the words of the brahmins learned in the three Vedas are like a line of blind men: the first does not see, the middle does not see, the last does not see. Thus, the words of these brahmins learned in the three Vedas turn out to be a joke, worthless, empty, and vain.”

530. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, passanti tevijjā brāhmaṇā candimasūriye, aññe cāpi bahujanā, yato ca candimasūriyā uggacchanti, yattha ca ogacchanti, āyācanti thomayanti pañjalikā namassamānā anuparivattantī’’ti?

530.“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? Do the brahmins learned in the three Vedas, and many other people, see the moon and sun? And as the moon and sun rise and set, do they entreat, praise, and, with joined palms, pay homage and circle around them?”

‘‘Evaṃ, bho gotama, passanti tevijjā brāhmaṇā candimasūriye, aññe cāpi bahujanā, yato ca candimasūriyā uggacchanti, yattha ca ogacchanti, āyācanti thomayanti pañjalikā namassamānā anuparivattantī’’ti.

“Yes, Master Gotama, the brahmins learned in the three Vedas, and many other people, see the moon and sun. And as the moon and sun rise and set, they entreat, praise, and, with joined palms, pay homage and circle around them.”

531. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, yaṃ passanti tevijjā brāhmaṇā candimasūriye, aññe cāpi bahujanā, yato ca candimasūriyā uggacchanti, yattha ca ogacchanti, āyācanti thomayanti pañjalikā namassamānā anuparivattanti, pahonti tevijjā brāhmaṇā candimasūriyānaṃ sahabyatāya maggaṃ desetuṃ – ‘‘ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko, niyyāti takkarassa candimasūriyānaṃ sahabyatāyā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

531.“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? Those brahmins learned in the three Vedas, and many other people, who see the moon and sun—where the moon and sun rise and where they set—entreat, praise, and, with joined palms, pay homage and circle around them—are those brahmins learned in the three Vedas able to point out the path to companionship with the moon and sun, saying: ‘This alone is the straight path, this the direct way, the liberating way, that leads one who practices it to companionship with the moon and sun’?” “No, Master Gotama.”

‘‘Iti kira, vāseṭṭha, yaṃ passanti tevijjā brāhmaṇā candimasūriye, aññe cāpi bahujanā, yato ca candimasūriyā uggacchanti, yattha ca ogacchanti, āyācanti thomayanti pañjalikā namassamānā anuparivattanti, tesampi nappahonti candimasūriyānaṃ sahabyatāya maggaṃ desetuṃ – ‘‘ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko, niyyāti takkarassa candimasūriyānaṃ sahabyatāyā’’ti.

“So it seems, Vāseṭṭha, that those brahmins learned in the three Vedas, and many other people, who see the moon and sun—where the moon and sun rise and where they set—entreat, praise, and, with joined palms, pay homage and circle around them—even they are not able to describe the path to companionship with the moon and sun, saying: ‘This is the straight path, this the direct way, the liberating way, that leads one who practices it to companionship with the moon and sun.’”

532. ‘‘Iti pana [kiṃ pana (sī. syā. pī.)] na kira tevijjehi brāhmaṇehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Napi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ācariyehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Napi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ācariyapācariyehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Napi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ yāva sattamā [sattamehi (?)] ācariyāmahayugehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Yepi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ pubbakā isayo mantānaṃ kattāro mantānaṃ pavattāro, yesamidaṃ etarahi tevijjā brāhmaṇā porāṇaṃ mantapadaṃ gītaṃ pavuttaṃ samihitaṃ, tadanugāyanti, tadanubhāsanti, bhāsitamanubhāsanti, vācitamanuvācenti, seyyathidaṃ – aṭṭhako vāmako vāmadevo vessāmitto yamataggi aṅgīraso bhāradvājo vāseṭṭho kassapo bhagu, tepi na evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘mayametaṃ jānāma, mayametaṃ passāma, yattha vā brahmā, yena vā brahmā, yahiṃ vā brahmā’’ti. Teva tevijjā brāhmaṇā evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘yaṃ na jānāma, yaṃ na passāma, tassa sahabyatāya maggaṃ desema – ayameva ujumaggo ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyā’’ti.

532.“So it seems that Brahmā has not been directly seen by the brahmins versed in the three Vedas. Nor has Brahmā been directly seen by their teachers. Nor by their teachers’ teachers. Nor even by their teachers’ teachers up to the seventh generation. And those former sages of the brahmins versed in the three Vedas—the composers and promulgators of the hymns, whose ancient verses are now chanted, recited, and compiled by the brahmins versed in the three Vedas, and which they chant, repeat, speak, and cause to be spoken—such as Aṭṭhaka, Vāmaka, Vāmadeva, Vessāmitta, Yamataggi, Aṅgīrasa, Bhāradvāja, Vāseṭṭha, Kassapa, and Bhagu—even they did not say: ‘We know this, we see this—where Brahmā is, by what means, or in what place.’ Yet these brahmins versed in the three Vedas say: ‘We teach the path to union with that which we do not know, which we do not see—this alone is the straight path, this the direct way, leading to deliverance, leading to union with Brahmā for one who practices it.’

533. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, nanu evaṃ sante tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti? ‘‘Addhā kho, bho gotama, evaṃ sante tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti.

533.“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? In that case, doesn’t the statement of the brahmins versed in the three Vedas turn out to be groundless?” “Indeed, Master Gotama, in that case the statement of the brahmins versed in the three Vedas turns out to be groundless.”

‘‘Sādhu, vāseṭṭha, te vata, vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā yaṃ na jānanti, yaṃ na passanti, tassa sahabyatāya maggaṃ desessanti – ‘‘ayameva ujumaggo, ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko, niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyā’’ti, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

“Good, Vāseṭṭha. Indeed, Vāseṭṭha, that those brahmins versed in the three Vedas, not knowing and not seeing that, should teach the path to union with it, saying, ‘This alone is the straight path, this the direct way, leading to deliverance, leading to union with Brahmā for one who practices it’—that is not possible.”

## Janapadakalyāṇīupamā

### The Simile of the Country’s Most Beautiful Woman

534. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, puriso evaṃ vadeyya – ‘‘ahaṃ yā imasmiṃ janapade janapadakalyāṇī, taṃ icchāmi, taṃ kāmemī’’ti. Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘ambho purisa, yaṃ tvaṃ janapadakalyāṇiṃ icchasi kāmesi, jānāsi taṃ janapadakalyāṇiṃ – khattiyī vā brāhmaṇī vā vessī vā suddī vā’’ti? Iti puṭṭho ‘‘no’’ti vadeyya.

534.“Suppose, Vāseṭṭha, a man were to say: ‘I desire, I lust after the country’s most beautiful woman.’ They would ask him: ‘Good sir, this country’s most beautiful woman you desire and lust after—do you know whether she is a noblewoman, a Brahmin woman, a merchant woman, or a worker woman?’ Being asked thus, he would say: ‘No.’

‘‘Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘ambho purisa, yaṃ tvaṃ janapadakalyāṇiṃ icchasi kāmesi, jānāsi taṃ janapadakalyāṇiṃ – evaṃnāmā evaṃgottāti vā, dīghā vā rassā vā majjhimā vā kāḷī vā sāmā vā maṅguracchavī vāti, amukasmiṃ gāme vā nigame vā nagare vā’’ti? Iti puṭṭho ‘no’ti vadeyya. Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘ambho purisa, yaṃ tvaṃ na jānāsi na passasi, taṃ tvaṃ icchasi kāmesī’’ti? Iti puṭṭho ‘‘āmā’’ti vadeyya.

Then they would say to him: ‘Good sir, this country’s most beautiful woman you desire and lust after—do you know her, whether she is of such-and-such a name or such-and-such a clan? Is she tall, short, or of medium height? Is she dark, swarthy, or golden-skinned? Does she live in such-and-such a village, market town, or city?’ Being asked thus, he would say: ‘No.’ Then they would say to him: ‘Good sir, do you desire and lust after that which you do not know, which you do not see?’ Being asked thus, he would say: ‘Yes.’

535. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, nanu evaṃ sante tassa purisassa appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti? ‘‘Addhā kho, bho gotama, evaṃ sante tassa purisassa appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti.

535.“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? In that case, doesn’t that man’s statement turn out to be groundless?” “Indeed, Master Gotama, in that case that man’s statement turns out to be groundless.”

536. ‘‘Evameva kho, vāseṭṭha, na kira tevijjehi brāhmaṇehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho, napi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ācariyehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho, napi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ ācariyapācariyehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Napi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ yāva sattamā ācariyāmahayugehi brahmā sakkhidiṭṭho. Yepi kira tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ pubbakā isayo mantānaṃ kattāro mantānaṃ pavattāro, yesamidaṃ etarahi tevijjā brāhmaṇā porāṇaṃ mantapadaṃ gītaṃ pavuttaṃ samihitaṃ, tadanugāyanti, tadanubhāsanti, bhāsitamanubhāsanti, vācitamanuvācenti, seyyathidaṃ – aṭṭhako vāmako vāmadevo vessāmitto yamataggi aṅgīraso bhāradvājo vāseṭṭho kassapo bhagu, tepi na evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘mayametaṃ jānāma, mayametaṃ passāma, yattha vā brahmā, yena vā brahmā, yahiṃ vā brahmā’’ti. Teva tevijjā brāhmaṇā evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘yaṃ na jānāma, yaṃ na passāma, tassa sahabyatāya maggaṃ desema – ayameva ujumaggo ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyā’’ti.

536.“In the same way, Vāseṭṭha, Brahmā has not been directly seen by the brahmins versed in the three Vedas. Nor has Brahmā been directly seen by their teachers. Nor by their teachers’ teachers. Nor even by their teachers’ teachers up to the seventh generation. And those former sages of the brahmins versed in the three Vedas—the composers and promulgators of the hymns, whose ancient verses are now chanted, recited, and compiled by the brahmins versed in the three Vedas, and which they chant, repeat, speak, and cause to be spoken—such as Aṭṭhaka, Vāmaka, Vāmadeva, Vessāmitta, Yamataggi, Aṅgīrasa, Bhāradvāja, Vāseṭṭha, Kassapa, and Bhagu—even they did not say: ‘We know this, we see this—where Brahmā is, by what means, or in what place.’ Yet these brahmins versed in the three Vedas say: ‘We teach the path to union with that which we do not know, which we do not see—this alone is the straight path, this the direct way, leading to deliverance, leading to union with Brahmā for one who practices it.’

537. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, nanu evaṃ sante tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti? ‘‘Addhā kho, bho gotama, evaṃ sante tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti.

537.“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? In that case, doesn’t the statement of the brahmins versed in the three Vedas turn out to be groundless?” “Indeed, Master Gotama, in that case the statement of the brahmins versed in the three Vedas turns out to be groundless.”

‘‘Sādhu, vāseṭṭha, te vata, vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā yaṃ na jānanti, yaṃ na passanti, tassa sahabyatāya maggaṃ desessanti – ayameva ujumaggo ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyāti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

“Good, Vāseṭṭha. Indeed, Vāseṭṭha, that those brahmins versed in the three Vedas, not knowing and not seeing that, should teach the path to union with it, saying, ‘This alone is the straight path, this the direct way, leading to deliverance, leading to union with Brahmā for one who practices it’—that is not possible.”

## Nisseṇīupamā

### The Simile of the Ladder

538. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, puriso cātumahāpathe nisseṇiṃ kareyya – pāsādassa ārohaṇāya. Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘ambho purisa, yassa tvaṃ [yaṃ tvaṃ (syā.)] pāsādassa ārohaṇāya nisseṇiṃ karosi, jānāsi taṃ pāsādaṃ – puratthimāya vā disāya dakkhiṇāya vā disāya pacchimāya vā disāya uttarāya vā disāya ucco vā nīco vā majjhimo vā’’ti? Iti puṭṭho ‘‘no’’ti vadeyya.

538.“Suppose, Vāseṭṭha, a man were to make a ladder at a crossroads for climbing up to a palace. They would say to him: ‘Good man, this ladder you’re making to climb up to the palace—do you know that palace, whether it is to the east, the south, the west, or the north? Is it high, low, or medium?’ When asked thus, he would say: ‘No.’

‘‘Tamenaṃ evaṃ vadeyyuṃ – ‘‘ambho purisa, yaṃ tvaṃ na jānāsi, na passasi, tassa tvaṃ pāsādassa ārohaṇāya nisseṇiṃ karosī’’ti? Iti puṭṭho ‘‘āmā’’ti vadeyya.

“Then they would say to him: ‘Good man, are you making a ladder to ascend a palace that you neither know nor see?’ When asked thus, he would say: ‘Yes.’

539. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, nanu evaṃ sante tassa purisassa appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti? ‘‘Addhā kho, bho gotama, evaṃ sante tassa purisassa appāṭihīrakataṃ bhāsitaṃ sampajjatī’’ti.

Teva tevijjā brāhmaṇā evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘yaṃ na jānāma, yaṃ na passāma, tassa sahabyatāya maggaṃ desema, ayameva ujumaggo ayamañjasāyano niyyāniko niyyāti takkarassa brahmasahabyatāyā’’ti.

This alone is the straight path, this is the direct way that leads one who practices it to fellowship with Brahmā.’

## Aciravatīnadīupamā

### The Simile of the Aciravatī River

542. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, ayaṃ aciravatī nadī pūrā udakassa samatittikā kākapeyyā. Atha puriso āgaccheyya pāratthiko pāragavesī pāragāmī pāraṃ taritukāmo. So orime tīre ṭhito pārimaṃ tīraṃ avheyya – ‘‘ehi pārāpāraṃ, ehi pārāpāra’’nti.

542.“Just as, Vāseṭṭha, this Aciravatī river is full of water, brimful, so that a crow could drink from it. Then a man might come along, needing to cross over, seeking the far shore, wishing to go to the far shore, desiring to cross to the far shore. Standing on this near shore, he would call out to the far shore, ‘Come over here, far shore! Come over here, far shore!’

543. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, api nu tassa purisassa avhāyanahetu vā āyācanahetu vā patthanahetu vā abhinandanahetu vā aciravatiyā nadiyā pārimaṃ tīraṃ orimaṃ tīraṃ āgaccheyyā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

543.“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha, would the far shore of the Aciravatī river come to the near shore because of that man’s calling, or because of his requesting, or because of his desiring, or because of his praising?” “No, Master Gotama.”

544. ‘‘Evameva kho, vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā ye dhammā brāhmaṇakārakā te dhamme pahāya vattamānā, ye dhammā abrāhmaṇakārakā te dhamme samādāya vattamānā evamāhaṃsu – ‘‘indamavhayāma, somamavhayāma, varuṇamavhayāma, īsānamavhayāma, pajāpatimavhayāma, brahmamavhayāma, mahiddhimavhayāma, yamamavhayāmā’’ti.

544.“Just so, Vāseṭṭha, those brahmins versed in the three Vedas, having abandoned the qualities that make one a brahmin and taken up the qualities that do not make one a brahmin, say: ‘We invoke Indra, we invoke Soma, we invoke Varuṇa, we invoke Īsāna, we invoke Pajāpati, we invoke Brahmā, we invoke Mahiddhi, we invoke Yama.’

‘‘Te vata, vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā ye dhammā brāhmaṇakārakā te dhamme pahāya vattamānā, ye dhammā abrāhmaṇakārakā te dhamme samādāya vattamānā avhāyanahetu vā āyācanahetu vā patthanahetu vā abhinandanahetu vā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā brahmānaṃ sahabyūpagā bhavissantī’’ti, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

“Indeed, Vāseṭṭha, those brahmins versed in the three Vedas, abandoning those qualities that make one a brahmin and adopting those qualities that do not make one a brahmin, yet declare, 'For the sake of recitation, for the sake of supplication, for the sake of longing, for the sake of praise, after death at the breaking up of the body, we shall be reborn in the company of Brahmā!'—such a thing is impossible.”

545. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, ayaṃ aciravatī nadī pūrā udakassa samatittikā kākapeyyā. Atha puriso āgaccheyya pāratthiko pāragavesī pāragāmī pāraṃ taritukāmo. So orime tīre daḷhāya anduyā pacchābāhaṃ gāḷhabandhanaṃ baddho.

545.“Just as, Vāseṭṭha, this Aciravatī river is full of water, brimful, so that a crow could drink from it. Then a man might come along, wanting to cross over, seeking the far shore, aiming for the far shore, desiring to reach the far shore. But he would be bound on this near shore by strong bonds, his arms tied tightly behind his back with a firm knot.

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, api nu so puriso aciravatiyā nadiyā orimā tīrā pārimaṃ tīraṃ gaccheyyā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha, could that man go from this near shore to the far shore of the Aciravatī river?” “No, Master Gotama.”

546. ‘‘Evameva kho, vāseṭṭha, pañcime kāmaguṇā ariyassa vinaye andūtipi vuccanti, bandhanantipi vuccanti. Katame pañca? Cakkhuviññeyyā rūpā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā. Sotaviññeyyā saddā…pe… ghānaviññeyyā gandhā… jivhāviññeyyā rasā… kāyaviññeyyā phoṭṭhabbā iṭṭhā kantā manāpā piyarūpā kāmūpasaṃhitā rajanīyā.

546.“Just so, Vāseṭṭha, these five strands of sensual pleasure are called a lure and a bond in the Noble One’s Discipline. Which five? Forms cognizable by the eye that are wished for, desired, agreeable, pleasing, sensually enticing, and arousing lust. Sounds cognizable by the ear… Smells cognizable by the nose… Tastes cognizable by the tongue… Tangibles cognizable by the body that are wished for, desired, agreeable, pleasing, sensually enticing, and arousing lust.

‘‘Ime kho, vāseṭṭha, pañca kāmaguṇā ariyassa vinaye andūtipi vuccanti, bandhanantipi vuccanti. Ime kho vāseṭṭha pañca kāmaguṇe tevijjā brāhmaṇā gadhitā mucchitā ajjhopannā anādīnavadassāvino anissaraṇapaññā paribhuñjanti. Te vata, vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā ye dhammā brāhmaṇakārakā, te dhamme pahāya vattamānā, ye dhammā abrāhmaṇakārakā, te dhamme samādāya vattamānā pañca kāmaguṇe gadhitā mucchitā ajjhopannā anādīnavadassāvino anissaraṇapaññā paribhuñjantā kāmandubandhanabaddhā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā brahmānaṃ sahabyūpagā bhavissantī’’ti, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

“These, Vāseṭṭha, are the five strands of sensual pleasure which, in the Noble One’s Discipline, are called a lure and called a bond. These five strands of sensual pleasure, Vāseṭṭha, the Brahmins versed in the three Vedas are greedy for, infatuated with, attached to, not seeing the danger in them, lacking understanding of escape from them, and enjoy. Those Brahmins versed in the three Vedas—who, Vāseṭṭha, having abandoned those qualities that make one a Brahmin, live adhering to the qualities that make one not a Brahmin, greedy for the five strands of sensual pleasure, infatuated with, attached to, not seeing the danger in them, lacking understanding of escape from them, bound by the lure and bond of sensual pleasures—that such ones, after the breakup of the body, after death, will attain fellowship with Brahmā: this is not possible.”

547. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, ayaṃ aciravatī nadī pūrā udakassa samatittikā kākapeyyā. Atha puriso āgaccheyya pāratthiko pāragavesī pāragāmī pāraṃ taritukāmo. So orime tīre sasīsaṃ pārupitvā nipajjeyya.

547.“Suppose, Vāseṭṭha, this Aciravatī river was full of water, brimful, so that a crow could drink from it. Then a man might come, wanting to reach the far shore, seeking the far shore, striving for the far shore, wishing to cross over to the far shore. He would wrap his head in his robe and lie down on this near bank.

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, api nu so puriso aciravatiyā nadiyā orimā tīrā pārimaṃ tīraṃ gaccheyyā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’.

“What do you think, Vāseṭṭha, could that man go from this near shore to the far shore of the Aciravatī river?” “No, Master Gotama.”

548. ‘‘Evameva kho, vāseṭṭha, pañcime nīvaraṇā ariyassa vinaye āvaraṇātipi vuccanti, nīvaraṇātipi vuccanti, onāhanātipi vuccanti, pariyonāhanātipi vuccanti. Katame pañca? Kāmacchandanīvaraṇaṃ, byāpādanīvaraṇaṃ, thinamiddhanīvaraṇaṃ, uddhaccakukkuccanīvaraṇaṃ, vicikicchānīvaraṇaṃ. Ime kho, vāseṭṭha, pañca nīvaraṇā ariyassa vinaye āvaraṇātipi vuccanti, nīvaraṇātipi vuccanti, onāhanātipi vuccanti, pariyonāhanātipi vuccanti.

548.“Just so, Vāseṭṭha, these five hindrances are called obstructions in the Noble One’s Discipline, called hindrances, called entanglements, called complete entanglements. Which five? The hindrance of sensual desire, the hindrance of ill will, the hindrance of sloth and torpor, the hindrance of restlessness and remorse, and the hindrance of doubt. These, Vāseṭṭha, are the five hindrances called obstructions in the Noble One’s Discipline, called hindrances, called entanglements, called complete entanglements.

549. ‘‘Imehi kho, vāseṭṭha, pañcahi nīvaraṇehi tevijjā brāhmaṇā āvuṭā nivuṭā onaddhā [ophuṭā (sī. ka.), ophutā (syā.)] pariyonaddhā. Te vata, vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā ye dhammā brāhmaṇakārakā te dhamme pahāya vattamānā, ye dhammā abrāhmaṇakārakā te dhamme samādāya vattamānā pañcahi nīvaraṇehi āvuṭā nivuṭā onaddhā pariyonaddhā [pariyonaddhā, te (syā. ka.)] kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā brahmānaṃ sahabyūpagā bhavissantī’’ti, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

549.“Vāseṭṭha, these Brahmins versed in the three Vedas are covered, enveloped, bound, and completely bound by these five hindrances. Indeed, Vāseṭṭha, that those Brahmins versed in the three Vedas, abandoning those qualities that make one a brahmin and adopting those qualities that make one not a brahmin, being covered, enveloped, bound, and completely bound by these five hindrances—after the breakup of the body, after death, should attain fellowship with Brahmā—such a thing is not possible.”

## Saṃsandanakathā

### The Conversation about Comparison

550. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, kinti te sutaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vuddhānaṃ mahallakānaṃ ācariyapācariyānaṃ bhāsamānānaṃ, sapariggaho vā brahmā apariggaho vā’’ti? ‘‘Apariggaho, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Saveracitto vā averacitto vā’’ti? ‘‘Averacitto, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Sabyāpajjacitto vā abyāpajjacitto vā’’ti? ‘‘Abyāpajjacitto, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Saṃkiliṭṭhacitto vā asaṃkiliṭṭhacitto vā’’ti? ‘‘Asaṃkiliṭṭhacitto, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Vasavattī vā avasavattī vā’’ti? ‘‘Vasavattī, bho gotama’’.

550."What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? As you have heard from the elderly brahmins, the old and experienced, the teachers of teachers, speaking—is Brahmā possessing or non-possessing?" "Non-possessing, Venerable Gotama." "Is he with hatred or without hatred?" "Without hatred, Venerable Gotama." "Is he with ill-will or without ill-will?" "Without ill-will, Venerable Gotama." "Is he with defilement or without defilement?" "Without defilement, Venerable Gotama." "Is he with mastery or without mastery?" "With mastery, Venerable Gotama."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, sapariggahā vā tevijjā brāhmaṇā apariggahā vā’’ti? ‘‘Sapariggahā, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Saveracittā vā averacittā vā’’ti? ‘‘Saveracittā, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Sabyāpajjacittā vā abyāpajjacittā vā’’ti? ‘‘Sabyāpajjacittā, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Saṃkiliṭṭhacittā vā asaṃkiliṭṭhacittā vā’’ti? ‘‘Saṃkiliṭṭhacittā, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Vasavattī vā avasavattī vā’’ti? ‘‘Avasavattī, bho gotama’’.

"What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? Are these brahmins versed in the three Vedas possessed of property or without property?" "Possessed of property, Venerable Gotama." "Are they with minds full of hate or without hate?" "With minds full of hate, Venerable Gotama." "Are they with minds intent on harming or without intent to harm?" "With minds intent on harming, Venerable Gotama." "Are they with defiled minds or undefiled minds?" "With defiled minds, Venerable Gotama." "Are they masters of themselves or not masters of themselves?" "Not masters of themselves, Venerable Gotama."

551. ‘‘Iti kira, vāseṭṭha, sapariggahā tevijjā brāhmaṇā apariggaho brahmā. Api nu kho sapariggahānaṃ tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ apariggahena brahmunā saddhiṃ saṃsandati sametī’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Sādhu, vāseṭṭha, te vata, vāseṭṭha, sapariggahā tevijjā brāhmaṇā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apariggahassa brahmuno sahabyūpagā bhavissantī’’ti, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

551."So it seems, Vāseṭṭha, that these brahmins versed in the three Vedas are possessing, while Brahmā is non-possessing. Now, can there be any association or communion between the possessing brahmins versed in the three Vedas and the non-possessing Brahmā?" "No, indeed, Venerable Gotama." "Good, Vāseṭṭha. These possessing brahmins versed in the three Vedas—after death, upon the breakup of the body—will they attain fellowship with the non-possessing Brahmā?" This is not possible.

‘‘Iti kira, vāseṭṭha, saveracittā tevijjā brāhmaṇā, averacitto brahmā…pe… sabyāpajjacittā tevijjā brāhmaṇā abyāpajjacitto brahmā… saṃkiliṭṭhacittā tevijjā brāhmaṇā asaṃkiliṭṭhacitto brahmā… avasavattī tevijjā brāhmaṇā vasavattī brahmā, api nu kho avasavattīnaṃ tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ vasavattinā brahmunā saddhiṃ saṃsandati sametī’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Sādhu, vāseṭṭha, te vata, vāseṭṭha, avasavattī tevijjā brāhmaṇā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā vasavattissa brahmuno sahabyūpagā bhavissantī’’ti, netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

"Thus, Vāseṭṭha, the brahmins versed in the Three Vedas have minds full of hatred, while Brahmā is free from hatred... The brahmins versed in the Three Vedas have minds full of malice, while Brahmā is free from malice... The brahmins versed in the Three Vedas have defiled minds, while Brahmā is undefiled... The brahmins versed in the Three Vedas are not masters of themselves, while Brahmā is master of himself. Can those brahmins versed in the Three Vedas who are not masters of themselves meet and unite with Brahmā who is master of himself?" "Certainly not, Venerable Gotama." "Good, Vāseṭṭha. It is impossible that those brahmins versed in the Three Vedas, who are not masters of themselves, will, after death, upon the breakup of the body, reach fellowship with Brahmā, who is master of himself."

552. ‘‘Idha kho pana te, vāseṭṭha, tevijjā brāhmaṇā āsīditvā [ādisitvā (ka.)] saṃsīdanti, saṃsīditvā visāraṃ [visādaṃ (sī. pī.), visattaṃ (syā.)] pāpuṇanti, sukkhataraṃ [sukkhataraṇaṃ (ka.)] maññe taranti. Tasmā idaṃ tevijjānaṃ brāhmaṇānaṃ tevijjāiriṇantipi vuccati, tevijjāvivanantipi vuccati, tevijjābyasanantipi vuccatī’’ti.

552."But here, Vāseṭṭha, these brahmins versed in the Three Vedas, having stumbled, they sink; having sunk, they reach utter ruin; they cross over, as it were, to a dry ford. Therefore, this is called the barren waste of the Three Vedas, the pathless forest of the Three Vedas, the ruin of the Three Vedas."

553. Evaṃ vutte, vāseṭṭho māṇavo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bho gotama, samaṇo gotamo brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggaṃ jānātī’’ti. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha. Āsanne ito manasākaṭaṃ, na ito dūre manasākaṭa’’nti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bho gotama, āsanne ito manasākaṭaṃ, na ito dūre manasākaṭa’’nti.

553.When this was said, the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha said to the Blessed One: "I have heard this, Venerable Gotama, that the ascetic Gotama knows the path to union with Brahmā." "What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? Is Manasākaṭa near here, or far from here?" "Yes, Venerable Gotama, Manasākaṭa is near here, not far from here."

554. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, idhassa puriso manasākaṭe jātasaṃvaddho. Tamenaṃ manasākaṭato tāvadeva avasaṭaṃ manasākaṭassa maggaṃ puccheyyuṃ. Siyā nu kho, vāseṭṭha, tassa purisassa manasākaṭe jātasaṃvaddhassa manasākaṭassa maggaṃ puṭṭhassa dandhāyitattaṃ vā vitthāyitattaṃ vā’’ti? ‘‘No hidaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Taṃ kissa hetu’’? ‘‘Amu hi, bho gotama, puriso manasākaṭe jātasaṃvaddho, tassa sabbāneva manasākaṭassa maggāni suviditānī’’ti.

554."What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? Suppose there were a man born and raised in Manasākaṭa. Then, having left Manasākaṭa only a short time, they should ask him the way to Manasākaṭa. Would that man, born and raised in Manasākaṭa, be slow or confused when asked about the way to Manasākaṭa?" "No, Venerable Gotama." "What is the reason for that?" "Because, Venerable Gotama, that man was born and raised in Manasākaṭa; all the paths to Manasākaṭa are well known to him."

‘‘Siyā kho, vāseṭṭha, tassa purisassa manasākaṭe jātasaṃvaddhassa manasākaṭassa maggaṃ puṭṭhassa dandhāyitattaṃ vā vitthāyitattaṃ vā, na tveva tathāgatassa brahmaloke vā brahmalokagāminiyā vā paṭipadāya puṭṭhassa dandhāyitattaṃ vā vitthāyitattaṃ vā. Brahmānaṃ cāhaṃ, vāseṭṭha, pajānāmi brahmalokañca brahmalokagāminiñca paṭipadaṃ, yathā paṭipanno ca brahmalokaṃ upapanno, tañca pajānāmī’’ti.

"Even if that man, born and raised in Manasākaṭa, might be slow or confused when asked about the way to Manasākaṭa, the Tathāgata is certainly not slow or confused when asked about the Brahmā world or the path leading to the Brahmā world. I know Brahmā, Vāseṭṭha, and the Brahmā world, and the path leading to the Brahmā world. I know how one who has practiced accordingly attains to the Brahmā world."

555. Evaṃ vutte, vāseṭṭho māṇavo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bho gotama, samaṇo gotamo brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggaṃ desetī’’ti. ‘‘Sādhu no bhavaṃ gotamo brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggaṃ desetu ullumpatu bhavaṃ gotamo brāhmaṇiṃ paja’’nti. ‘‘Tena hi, vāseṭṭha, suṇāhi; sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ bho’’ti kho vāseṭṭho māṇavo bhagavato paccassosi.

555.When this was said, the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha said to the Blessed One: "I have heard this, Venerable Gotama, that the ascetic Gotama teaches the path to companionship with Brahmā. It would be good if the Venerable Gotama would teach us the path to companionship with Brahmā. May the Venerable Gotama uplift the brahmin community." "Then, Vāseṭṭha, listen and attend closely. I will speak." "Yes, Venerable One," replied the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha to the Blessed One.

## Brahmalokamaggadesanā

### The Discourse on the Path to the Brahmā World.

556. Bhagavā etadavoca – ‘‘idha, vāseṭṭha, tathāgato loke uppajjati arahaṃ, sammāsambuddho…pe… (yathā 190-212 anucchedesu evaṃ vitthāretabbaṃ). Evaṃ kho, vāseṭṭha, bhikkhu sīlasampanno hoti…pe… tassime pañca nīvaraṇe pahīne attani samanupassato pāmojjaṃ jāyati, pamuditassa pīti jāyati, pītimanassa kāyo passambhati, passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ vedeti, sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati.

556.The Blessed One said: "Here, Vāseṭṭha, a Tathāgata arises in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One… (as in sections 190–212, this should be elaborated). Thus, Vāseṭṭha, a bhikkhu is accomplished in virtue… With these five hindrances abandoned in himself, joy arises; from joy comes delight; with a delighted mind his body becomes tranquil; with a tranquil body he feels happiness; and with happiness his mind becomes collected.

‘‘So mettāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharati. Tathā dutiyaṃ. Tathā tatiyaṃ. Tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ mettāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena pharitvā viharati.

"He abides pervading one direction with a mind imbued with loving-kindness; likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, in every way, he abides pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with loving-kindness—vast, exalted, measureless, without enmity, without ill will."

‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, balavā saṅkhadhamo appakasireneva catuddisā viññāpeyya; evameva kho, vāseṭṭha, evaṃ bhāvitāya mettāya cetovimuttiyā yaṃ pamāṇakataṃ kammaṃ na taṃ tatrāvasissati, na taṃ tatrāvatiṭṭhati. Ayampi kho, vāseṭṭha, brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggo.

"Just as, Vāseṭṭha, a strong conch-blower could easily make himself heard in all four directions, so too, Vāseṭṭha, when the liberation of mind by loving-kindness is thus developed, any measurable action does not remain there, does not persist there. This, Vāseṭṭha, is the path to companionship with Brahmā."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, vāseṭṭha, bhikkhu karuṇāsahagatena cetasā…pe… muditāsahagatena cetasā…pe… upekkhāsahagatena cetasā ekaṃ disaṃ pharitvā viharati. Tathā dutiyaṃ. Tathā tatiyaṃ. Tathā catutthaṃ. Iti uddhamadho tiriyaṃ sabbadhi sabbattatāya sabbāvantaṃ lokaṃ upekkhāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena pharitvā viharati.

"Furthermore, Vāseṭṭha, a bhikkhu with a mind imbued with compassion… with a mind imbued with altruistic joy… with a mind imbued with equanimity, abides pervading one direction. Likewise the second, likewise the third, likewise the fourth. Thus above, below, across, everywhere, in every way, he abides pervading the entire world with a mind imbued with equanimity—vast, exalted, measureless, without enmity, without ill will.

‘‘Seyyathāpi, vāseṭṭha, balavā saṅkhadhamo appakasireneva catuddisā viññāpeyya. Evameva kho, vāseṭṭha, evaṃ bhāvitāya upekkhāya cetovimuttiyā yaṃ pamāṇakataṃ kammaṃ na taṃ tatrāvasissati, na taṃ tatrāvatiṭṭhati. Ayaṃ kho, vāseṭṭha, brahmānaṃ sahabyatāya maggo.

"Just as, Vāseṭṭha, a strong conch-blower could easily make himself heard in all four directions, so too, Vāseṭṭha, when the liberation of mind by equanimity is thus developed, any measurable action does not remain there, does not persist there. This, Vāseṭṭha, is the path to companionship with Brahmā."

557. ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, vāseṭṭha, evaṃvihārī bhikkhu sapariggaho vā apariggaho vā’’ti? ‘‘Apariggaho, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Saveracitto vā averacitto vā’’ti? ‘‘Averacitto, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Sabyāpajjacitto vā abyāpajjacitto vā’’ti? ‘‘Abyāpajjacitto, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Saṃkiliṭṭhacitto vā asaṃkiliṭṭhacitto vā’’ti? ‘‘Asaṃkiliṭṭhacitto, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Vasavattī vā avasavattī vā’’ti? ‘‘Vasavattī, bho gotama’’.

557."What do you think, Vāseṭṭha? Does a bhikkhu living thus possess belongings or not?" "He does not, Venerable Gotama." "Is his mind with enmity or without enmity?" "Without enmity, Venerable Gotama." "Is his mind with ill will or without ill will?" "Without ill will, Venerable Gotama." "Is his mind defiled or undefiled?" "Undefiled, Venerable Gotama." "Is he master or not master?" "He is master, Venerable Gotama."

‘‘Iti kira, vāseṭṭha, apariggaho bhikkhu, apariggaho brahmā. Api nu kho apariggahassa bhikkhuno apariggahena brahmunā saddhiṃ saṃsandati sametī’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Sādhu, vāseṭṭha, so vata vāseṭṭha apariggaho bhikkhu kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apariggahassa brahmuno sahabyūpago bhavissatī’’ti, ṭhānametaṃ vijjati.

"So it is, Vāseṭṭha, a bhikkhu is unattached, and Brahmā is unattached. But does an unattached bhikkhu associate or unite with the unattached Brahmā?" "Yes, Venerable Gotama." "Good, Vāseṭṭha. Indeed, Vāseṭṭha, it is possible that an unattached bhikkhu, at the breakup of the body, after death, will be reborn into companionship with the unattached Brahmā." This is a valid possibility.

558. ‘‘Iti kira, vāseṭṭha, averacitto bhikkhu, averacitto brahmā…pe… abyāpajjacitto bhikkhu, abyāpajjacitto brahmā… asaṃkiliṭṭhacitto bhikkhu, asaṃkiliṭṭhacitto brahmā… vasavattī bhikkhu, vasavattī brahmā, api nu kho vasavattissa bhikkhuno vasavattinā brahmunā saddhiṃ saṃsandati sametī’’ti? ‘‘Evaṃ, bho gotama’’. ‘‘Sādhu, vāseṭṭha, so vata, vāseṭṭha, vasavattī bhikkhu kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā vasavattissa brahmuno sahabyūpago bhavissatīti, ṭhānametaṃ vijjatī’’ti.

558."So it is, Vāseṭṭha, a bhikkhu with a mind free from enmity, and Brahmā is free from enmity… a bhikkhu with a mind free from ill will, and Brahmā is free from ill will… a bhikkhu with an undefiled mind, and Brahmā is undefiled… a bhikkhu who is master, and Brahmā is master. But does a bhikkhu who is master associate or unite with the Brahmā who is master?" "Yes, Venerable Gotama." "Good, Vāseṭṭha. Indeed, Vāseṭṭha, it is possible that such a bhikkhu who is master, at the breakup of the body, after death, will be reborn into companionship with the Brahmā who is master." This is a valid possibility.

559. Evaṃ vutte, vāseṭṭhabhāradvājā māṇavā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama! Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya ‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’ti. Evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Ete mayaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāma, dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsake no bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupete saraṇaṃ gate’’ti.

559.When this was said, the young men Vāseṭṭha and Bhāradvāja said to the Blessed One: "Excellent, Venerable Gotama! Excellent, Venerable Gotama! Just as one might set upright what was overturned, or reveal what was hidden, or show the way to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the dark so that those with eyes may see forms—just so has the Dhamma been made clear by Venerable Gotama in many ways. We go for refuge to Venerable Gotama, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of bhikkhus. May Venerable Gotama accept us as lay followers who have gone for refuge from this day forward, for as long as life lasts."

## Tevijjasuttaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ terasamaṃ.

### The Tevijja Sutta, the Thirteenth, is concluded.

## Sīlakkhandhavaggo niṭṭhito.

### The Sīlakkhandhavagga is concluded.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Brahmāsāmaññaambaṭṭha,Soṇakūṭamahālijālinī;Sīhapoṭṭhapādasubho kevaṭṭo,Lohiccatevijjā terasāti.

Brahmā, Sāmañña, Ambaṭṭha, Soṇadaṇḍa, Kūṭadanta, Mahāli, Jāliya, Sīha, Poṭṭhapāda, Subha, Kevaṭṭa, Lohicca, Tevijja—these are the thirteen.

## Sīlakkhandhavaggapāḷi niṭṭhitā.

### The Sīlakkhandhavagga is concluded.
