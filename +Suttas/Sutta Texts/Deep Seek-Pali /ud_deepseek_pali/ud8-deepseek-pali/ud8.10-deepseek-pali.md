# Ud 8.10 Dutiyadabbasuttaṃ

## Ud 8.10 The Second Dabba Discourse

80. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

80.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi in Jeta's Grove, Anathapindika's monastery. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "bhikkhus!" "Venerable sir," those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Dabbassa, bhikkhave, mallaputtassa vehāsaṃ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe pallaṅkena nisīditvā tejodhātuṃ samāpajjitvā vuṭṭhahitvā parinibbutassa sarīrassa jhāyamānassa ḍayhamānassa neva chārikā paññāyittha na masi. Seyyathāpi nāma sappissa vā telassa vā jhāyamānassa ḍayhamānassa neva chārikā paññāyati na masi; evameva kho, bhikkhave, dabbassa mallaputtassa vehāsaṃ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe pallaṅkena nisīditvā tejodhātuṃ samāpajjitvā vuṭṭhahitvā parinibbutassa sarīrassa jhāyamānassa ḍayhamānassa neva chārikā paññāyittha na masī’’ti.

"bhikkhus, when Dabba the Mallian rose into the air, sat cross-legged in the sky, entered the fire element, and emerged from it, his body, as it burned and blazed after his complete extinction, left behind no ashes or soot. Just as when ghee or oil burns and blazes, no ashes or soot are discerned, in the same way, bhikkhus, when Dabba the Mallian rose into the air, sat cross-legged in the sky, entered the fire element, and emerged from it, his body, as it burned and blazed after his complete extinction, left behind no ashes or soot."

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, the Blessed One, having understood the significance of it, on that occasion uttered this inspired verse:

‘‘Ayoghanahatasseva, jalato jātavedaso [jātavedassa (syā.)];Anupubbūpasantassa, yathā na ñāyate gati.

'Just as a blazing fire, struck by a smith’s hammer; When gradually subsiding, its destination is not discerned.'

Evaṃ sammāvimuttānaṃ, kāmabandhoghatārinaṃ;Paññāpetuṃ gati natthi, pattānaṃ acalaṃ sukha’’nti. dasamaṃ;

Thus rightly freed, who have crossed the flood of sensual desire’s bondage; No destination can be discerned for them, who have attained the unmoving bliss. Tenth.

Pāṭaligāmiyavaggo [pāṭaligāmavaggo (ka.)] aṭṭhamo.

The Eighth Chapter: The Pāṭaligāmiya Chapter.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary is:

Nibbānā caturo vuttā, cundo pāṭaligāmiyā;Dvidhāpatho visākhā ca, dabbena saha te dasāti.

Four discourses on Nibbāna are declared, Cunda, Pāṭaligāmiya; The Twofold Path, Visākhā, and Dabba—these are the ten.

Udāne vaggānamuddānaṃ –

The chapter summaries of the Udāna -

Vaggamidaṃ paṭhamaṃ varabodhi, vaggamidaṃ dutiyaṃ mucalindo;Nandakavaggavaro tatiyo tu, meghiyavaggavaro ca catuttho.

This is the first chapter, the excellent Bodhi, this is the second chapter, Mucalinda; The excellent Nandaka Chapter is the third, and the excellent Meghiya Chapter is the fourth.

Pañcamavaggavarantidha soṇo, chaṭṭhamavaggavaranti jaccandho [chaṭṭhamavaggavaraṃ tu tamandho (sī. ka.)];Sattamavaggavaranti ca cūḷo, pāṭaligāmiyamaṭṭhamavaggo [pāṭaligāmiyavaraṭṭhamavaggo (syā. kaṃ. pī.), pāṭaligāmavaraṭṭhamavaggo (sī. ka.)].

Here, the excellent fifth chapter is Soṇa, the excellent sixth chapter is Jaccandha; and the excellent seventh chapter is Cūḷa, the eighth chapter is Pāṭaligāmiya.

Asītimanūnakasuttavaraṃ, vaggamidaṭṭhakaṃ suvibhattaṃ;Dassitaṃ cakkhumatā vimalena, addhā hi taṃ udānamitīdamāhu [atthāyetaṃ udānamitimāhu (ka.), saddhā hi taṃ udānantidamāhu (syā. kaṃ pī.)].

The excellent discourses, not less than eighty, this eight-chapter collection, well divided; was shown by the Clear-eyed, the Stainless One. Truly, they say, this is the Udāna.

Udānapāḷi niṭṭhitā.

The Udāna is completed.
