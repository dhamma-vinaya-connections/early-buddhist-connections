# Ud 8.6 Pāṭaligāmiyasuttaṃ

## Ud 8.6 The Pāṭaligāmiya Discourse

76. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā magadhesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ yena pāṭaligāmo tadavasari. Assosuṃ kho pāṭaligāmiyā [pāṭaligāmikā (dī. ni. 2.148)] upāsakā – ‘‘bhagavā kira magadhesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ pāṭaligāmaṃ anuppatto’’ti. Atha kho pāṭaligāmiyā upāsakā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho pāṭaligāmiyā upāsakā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘adhivāsetu no, bhante, bhagavā āvasathāgāra’’nti. Adhivāsesi bhagavā tuṇhībhāvena.

76.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was wandering in Magadha with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus and arrived at Pāṭaligāma. The lay followers of Pāṭaligāma heard, "The Blessed One, they say, wandering in Magadha with a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus, has arrived at Pāṭaligāma." Then the lay followers of Pāṭaligāma approached the Blessed One, and after approaching and paying homage to him, they sat down to one side. As they sat there, the lay followers of Pāṭaligāma said to the Blessed One, "Venerable sir, may the Blessed One accept our rest house." The Blessed One consented with silence.

Atha kho pāṭaligāmiyā upāsakā bhagavato adhivāsanaṃ viditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā yenāvasathāgāraṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā sabbasanthariṃ āvasathāgāraṃ santharitvā āsanāni paññāpetvā udakamaṇikaṃ patiṭṭhāpetvā telappadīpaṃ āropetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho pāṭaligāmiyā upāsakā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘sabbasantharisanthataṃ [sabbasanthariṃ santhataṃ (sī. syā. pī.)], bhante, āvasathāgāraṃ; āsanāni paññattāni; udakamaṇiko patiṭṭhāpito [udakamaṇikaṃ patiṭṭhāpitaṃ (syā.)] telappadīpo āropito. Yassadāni, bhante, bhagavā kālaṃ maññatī’’ti.

Then the lay followers of Pāṭaligāma, knowing the Blessed One’s acceptance, rose from their seats, paid homage to him, circumambulated him keeping the right side toward him, and went to the rest house. Having arrived, they spread out a complete layer of mats in the rest house, arranged seats, placed a water pot, and lit an oil lamp. Then they approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and stood to one side. Standing to one side, the lay followers of Pāṭaligāma said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, the rest house is spread with a complete layer of mats; seats are arranged; a water pot is placed; an oil lamp is lit. Now, venerable sir, the Blessed One may do as he sees fit."

Atha kho bhagavā nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghena yena āvasathāgāraṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā pāde pakkhāletvā āvasathāgāraṃ pavisitvā majjhimaṃ thambhaṃ nissāya puratthābhimukho nisīdi. Bhikkhusaṅghopi kho pāde pakkhāletvā āvasathāgāraṃ pavisitvā pacchimaṃ bhittiṃ nissāya puratthābhimukho nisīdi bhagavantaṃyeva purakkhatvā. Pāṭaligāmiyāpi kho upāsakā pāde pakkhāletvā āvasathāgāraṃ pavisitvā puratthimaṃ bhittiṃ nissāya pacchimābhimukhā nisīdiṃsu bhagavantaṃyeva purakkhatvā. Atha kho bhagavā pāṭaligāmiye upāsake āmantesi –

Then the Blessed One, having dressed, and taking his bowl and robe, together with the community of bhikkhus, approached the rest house. Having approached, he washed his feet, entered the rest house, and sat down facing east, leaning against the central pillar. The community of bhikkhus also washed their feet, entered the rest house, and sat down facing east, leaning against the western wall, keeping the Blessed One in front of them. The lay followers of Pāṭaligāma also washed their feet, entered the rest house, and sat down facing west, leaning against the eastern wall, keeping the Blessed One in front of them. Then the Blessed One addressed the lay followers of Pāṭaligāma:

‘‘Pañcime, gahapatayo, ādīnavā dussīlassa sīlavipattiyā. Katame pañca? Idha, gahapatayo, dussīlo sīlavipanno pamādādhikaraṇaṃ mahatiṃ bhogajāniṃ nigacchati. Ayaṃ paṭhamo ādīnavo dussīlassa sīlavipattiyā.

"Householders, there are these five drawbacks of immorality, due to failure in virtue. What five? Here, householders, an immoral person, failing in virtue, through negligence incurs a great loss of wealth. This is the first drawback of immorality, due to failure in virtue."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, dussīlassa sīlavipannassa pāpako kittisaddo abbhuggacchati. Ayaṃ dutiyo ādīnavo dussīlassa sīlavipattiyā.

Furthermore, householders, for an immoral person who has failed in virtue, a bad reputation arises. This is the second drawback of immorality, due to failure in virtue.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, dussīlo sīlavipanno yaññadeva parisaṃ upasaṅkamati – yadi khattiyaparisaṃ, yadi brāhmaṇaparisaṃ, yadi gahapatiparisaṃ, yadi samaṇaparisaṃ – avisārado upasaṅkamati maṅkubhūto. Ayaṃ tatiyo ādīnavo dussīlassa sīlavipattiyā.

"Furthermore, householders, an immoral person, one who has failed in virtue, approaches any assembly—whether an assembly of nobles, an assembly of brahmins, an assembly of householders, or an assembly of ascetics—lacking confidence and feeling subdued. This is the third drawback of immorality, due to failure in virtue."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, dussīlo sīlavipanno sammūḷho kālaṃ karoti. Ayaṃ catuttho ādīnavo dussīlassa sīlavipattiyā.

"Furthermore, householders, an immoral person, one who has failed in virtue, dies confused. This is the fourth drawback of immorality, due to failure in virtue."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, dussīlo sīlavipanno kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjati. Ayaṃ pañcamo ādīnavo dussīlassa sīlavipattiyā. Ime kho, gahapatayo, pañca ādīnavā dussīlassa sīlavipattiyā.

"Furthermore, householders, an immoral person, one who has failed in virtue, at the breaking up of the body, after death, is reborn in a state of deprivation, a bad destination, the lower realms, specifically hell. This is the fifth drawback of immorality, due to failure in virtue. These, householders, are the five drawbacks of immorality, due to failure in virtue."

‘‘Pañcime, gahapatayo, ānisaṃsā sīlavato sīlasampadāya. Katame pañca? Idha, gahapatayo, sīlavā sīlasampanno appamādādhikaraṇaṃ mahantaṃ bhogakkhandhaṃ adhigacchati. Ayaṃ paṭhamo ānisaṃso sīlavato sīlasampadāya.

"Householders, there are these five benefits of being virtuous, of being accomplished in virtue. What five? Here, householders, a virtuous person, accomplished in virtue, acquires a great mass of wealth due to diligence. This is the first benefit of being virtuous, of being accomplished in virtue."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, sīlavato sīlasampannassa kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggacchati. Ayaṃ dutiyo ānisaṃso sīlavato sīlasampadāya.

"Furthermore, householders, a good reputation arises for the virtuous person accomplished in virtue. This is the second benefit of being virtuous, of being accomplished in virtue."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, sīlavā sīlasampanno yaññadeva parisaṃ upasaṅkamati – yadi khattiyaparisaṃ, yadi brāhmaṇaparisaṃ, yadi gahapatiparisaṃ, yadi samaṇaparisaṃ – visārado upasaṅkamati amaṅkubhūto. Ayaṃ tatiyo ānisaṃso sīlavato sīlasampadāya.

"Furthermore, householders, a virtuous person, accomplished in virtue, approaches any assembly—whether an assembly of nobles, an assembly of brahmins, an assembly of householders, or an assembly of ascetics—confident and unperturbed. This is the third benefit of being virtuous, of being accomplished in virtue."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, sīlavā sīlasampanno asammūḷho kālaṅkaroti. Ayaṃ catuttho ānisaṃso sīlavato sīlasampadāya.

"Furthermore, householders, a virtuous person, accomplished in virtue, dies unconfused. This is the fourth benefit of being virtuous, of being accomplished in virtue."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, gahapatayo, sīlavā sīlasampanno kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjati. Ayaṃ pañcamo ānisaṃso sīlavato sīlasampadāya. Ime kho, gahapatayo, pañca ānisaṃsā sīlavato sīlasampadāyā’’ti.

"Furthermore, householders, a virtuous person, accomplished in virtue, with the breakup of the body, after death, is reborn in a good destination, a heavenly world. This is the fifth benefit of being virtuous, of being accomplished in virtue. These, householders, are the five benefits of being virtuous, of being accomplished in virtue."

Atha kho bhagavā pāṭaligāmiye upāsake bahudeva rattiṃ dhammiyā kathāya sandassetvā samādapetvā samutejetvā sampahaṃsetvā uyyojesi – ‘‘abhikkantā kho, gahapatayo, ratti; yassadāni tumhe kālaṃ maññathā’’ti. [‘‘evaṃ bhante‘‘ti khopāṭaligāmiyā upāsakā bhagavato paṭissutvā (mahāva. 285; dī. ni. 2.151)] Atha kho pāṭaligāmiyā upāsakā bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā [‘‘evaṃ bhante‘‘ti khopāṭaligāmiyā upāsakā bhagavato paṭissutvā (mahāva. 285; dī. ni. 2.151)] uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkamiṃsu. Atha kho bhagavā acirapakkantesu pāṭaligāmiyesu upāsakesu suññāgāraṃ pāvisi.

Then the Blessed One, having instructed, exhorted, inspired, and gladdened the Pāṭaligāmiya lay followers with a Dhamma talk for much of the night, dismissed them, saying: "The night is far advanced, householders. You may do what you think it's time for."Then the Pāṭaligāmiya lay followers, having delighted in and rejoiced at the Blessed One's words, rose from their seats, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him keeping the right side toward him, and departed.Soon after the Pāṭaligāmiya lay followers had left, the Blessed One entered an empty dwelling.

Tena kho pana samayena sunidhavassakārā [sunīdhavassakārā (sī. syā. pī.)] magadhamahāmattā pāṭaligāme nagaraṃ māpenti vajjīnaṃ paṭibāhāya. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā devatāyo sahassasahasseva [sahasseva (syā. ka.), sahassasseva (pī.)] pāṭaligāme vatthūni pariggaṇhanti. Yasmiṃ padese mahesakkhā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti mahesakkhānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Yasmiṃ padese majjhimā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti majjhimānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Yasmiṃ padese nīcā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti nīcānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ.

At that time, Sunidha and Vassakāra, the Magadhan chief ministers, were building a city at Pāṭaligāma to ward off the Vajjis. At that time, numerous deities, thousands upon thousands, were taking possession of sites in Pāṭaligāma. In whatever region deities of great power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of great power inclined toward building dwellings there. In whatever region deities of middling power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of middling power inclined toward building dwellings there. In whatever region deities of low power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of low power inclined toward building dwellings there.

Addasā kho bhagavā dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena tā devatāyo sahassasahasseva pāṭaligāme vatthūni pariggaṇhantiyo. Yasmiṃ padese mahesakkhā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti, mahesakkhānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Yasmiṃ padese majjhimā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti, majjhimānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Yasmiṃ padese nīcā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti, nīcānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Atha kho bhagavā tassā rattiyā paccūsasamaye paccuṭṭhāya āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi –

The Blessed One saw with his divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, those deities, thousands upon thousands, taking possession of sites in Pāṭaligāma. In whatever region deities of great power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of great power inclined toward building dwellings there. In whatever region deities of middling power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of middling power inclined toward building dwellings there. In whatever region deities of low power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of low power inclined toward building dwellings there. Then, the Blessed One, arising in the early morning, addressed Venerable Ānanda:

‘‘Ke nu kho [ko nu kho (sabbattha)] ānanda pāṭaligāme nagaraṃ māpentī’’ti [māpetīti (sabbattha)]. ‘‘Sunidhavassakārā, bhante, magadhamahāmattā pāṭaligāme nagaraṃ māpenti vajjīnaṃ paṭibāhāyā’’ti. ‘‘Seyyathāpi, ānanda, devehi tāvatiṃsehi saddhiṃ mantetvā; evameva kho, ānanda, sunidhavassakārā magadhamahāmattā pāṭaligāme nagaraṃ māpenti vajjīnaṃ paṭibāhāya. Idhāhaṃ, ānanda, addasaṃ dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena sambahulā devatāyo sahassasahasseva pāṭaligāme vatthūni pariggaṇhantiyo. Yasmiṃ padese mahesakkhā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti mahesakkhānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Yasmiṃ padese majjhimā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti majjhimānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Yasmiṃ padese nīcā devatā vatthūni pariggaṇhanti nīcānaṃ tattha raññaṃ rājamahāmattānaṃ cittāni namanti nivesanāni māpetuṃ. Yāvatā, ānanda, ariyaṃ āyatanaṃ yāvatā vaṇippatho idaṃ agganagaraṃ bhavissati pāṭaliputtaṃ puṭabhedanaṃ. Pāṭaliputtassa kho, ānanda, tayo antarāyā bhavissanti – aggito vā udakato vā mithubhedato vā’’ti.

"Ānanda, who is building a city at Pāṭaligāma?" "Venerable sir, the Magadhan chief ministers Sunidha and Vassakāra are building a city at Pāṭaligāma to ward off the Vajjis." "Ānanda, it is as if they had consulted with the Tāvatiṃsa deities. Indeed, Ānanda, the Magadhan chief ministers Sunidha and Vassakāra are building a city at Pāṭaligāma to ward off the Vajjis. Here, Ānanda, I saw with my divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, numerous deities, thousands upon thousands, taking possession of sites in Pāṭaligāma. In whatever region deities of great power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of great power inclined toward building dwellings there. In whatever region deities of middling power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of middling power inclined toward building dwellings there. In whatever region deities of low power took possession of sites, the minds of kings and royal ministers of low power inclined toward building dwellings there. Ānanda, as far as the Aryan domain extends, as far as trade routes go, this city of Pāṭaliputta will be the chief city, a commercial hub. But, Ānanda, three dangers will befall Pāṭaliputta: from fire, from water, or from internal strife."

Atha kho sunidhavassakārā magadhamahāmattā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sārāṇiyaṃ [sārāṇīyaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho sunidhavassakārā magadhamahāmattā bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘adhivāsetu no bhavaṃ gotamo ajjatanāya bhattaṃ saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’ti. Adhivāsesi bhagavā tuṇhībhāvena.

Then the Magadhan chief ministers Sunidha and Vassakāra approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. After an exchange of courteous conversation, they stood to one side. Standing to one side, the Magadhan chief ministers said to the Blessed One: "May Master Gotama accept our meal for today, together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus." The Blessed One consented by remaining silent.

Atha kho sunidhavassakārā magadhamahāmattā bhagavato adhivāsanaṃ viditvā yena sako āvasatho tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā sake āvasathe paṇītaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ paṭiyādāpetvā bhagavato kālaṃ ārocesuṃ – ‘‘kālo, bho gotama, niṭṭhitaṃ bhatta’’nti.

Understanding the Blessed One's acceptance, the Magadhan chief ministers Sunidha and Vassakāra went to their own dwelling. There, they prepared fine foods, both hard and soft, and announced the time to the Blessed One: "It is time, Master Gotama, the meal is ready."

Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghena yena sunidhavassakārānaṃ magadhamahāmattānaṃ āvasatho tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Atha kho sunidhavassakārā magadhamahāmattā buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappesuṃ sampavāresuṃ.

Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the forenoon, taking his bowl and robe, together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus, went to the dwelling of Sunidha and Vassakāra, the Magadhan chief ministers. Having arrived, he sat down on the prepared seat. Then Sunidha and Vassakāra, the Magadhan chief ministers, personally served and satisfied the Saṅgha of bhikkhus headed by the Buddha with fine hard and soft food.

Atha kho sunidhavassakārā magadhamahāmattā bhagavantaṃ bhuttāviṃ onītapattapāṇiṃ aññataraṃ nīcaṃ āsanaṃ gahetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinne kho sunidhavassakāre magadhamahāmatte bhagavā imāhi gāthāhi anumodi –

Then the Magadhan chief ministers Sunidha and Vassakāra, after the Blessed One had finished his meal and withdrawn his hand from the bowl, took a low seat and sat to one side. As they sat to one side, the Blessed One expressed his appreciation to the Magadhan chief ministers Sunidha and Vassakāra with these verses:

‘‘Yasmiṃ padese kappeti, vāsaṃ paṇḍitajātiyo;Sīlavantettha bhojetvā, saññate brahmacārayo [brahmacārino (syā.), brahmacariye (pī. ka.)].

In whatever place a wise person arranges their dwelling, there, having fed the virtuous, the restrained, those who lead the holy life,

‘‘Yā tattha devatā āsuṃ, tāsaṃ dakkhiṇamādise;Tā pūjitā pūjayanti, mānitā mānayanti naṃ.

Whatever deities were there, he dedicates a share of merit to them; Worshipped, they worship him; honored, they honor him.

‘‘Tato naṃ anukampanti, mātā puttaṃva orasaṃ;Devatānukampito poso, sadā bhadrāni passatī’’ti.

Then they have compassion for him, as a mother for her own child; A person favored by the deities always sees auspicious things.

Atha kho bhagavā sunidhavassakārānaṃ magadhamahāmattānaṃ imāhi gāthāhi anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.

Then the Blessed One, having expressed his appreciation to Sunidha and Vassakāra, the Magadhan chief ministers, with these verses, rose from his seat and departed.

Tena kho pana samayena sunidhavassakārā magadhamahāmattā bhagavantaṃ piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandhā honti – ‘‘yenajja samaṇo gotamo dvārena nikkhamissati taṃ ‘gotamadvāraṃ’ nāma bhavissati. Yena titthena gaṅgaṃ nadiṃ tarissati taṃ ‘gotamatitthaṃ’ nāma bhavissatī’’ti.

At that time, the Magadhan chief ministers Sunidha and Vassakāra were following the Blessed One closely behind, thinking: "Whichever gate the ascetic Gotama exits through today, that will be called the 'Gotama Gate,' and whichever ford he uses to cross the Ganges River, that will be called the 'Gotama Ford.'"

Atha kho bhagavā yena dvārena nikkhami taṃ ‘gotamadvāraṃ’ nāma ahosi. Atha kho bhagavā yena gaṅgā nadī tenupasaṅkami. Tena kho pana samayena gaṅgā nadī pūrā hoti samatittikā kākapeyyā. Appekacce manussā nāvaṃ pariyesanti, appekacce uḷumpaṃ pariyesanti, appekacce kullaṃ bandhanti apārā pāraṃ gantukāmā. Atha kho bhagavā – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evameva – gaṅgāya nadiyā orimatīre [orimatīrā (bahūsu) mahāva. 286; dī. ni. 2.154 passitabbaṃ)] antarahito pārimatīre paccuṭṭhāsi saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghena.

Then the Blessed One went out by the gate, which became known as the Gotama Gate. Then the Blessed One approached the Ganges River. At that time, the Ganges River was full to the brim, so that a crow could drink from it. Some people were looking for a boat, some were looking for a raft, and some were binding together a float, desiring to go from this shore to the far shore. Then the Blessed One—just as a strong man might stretch out his drawn-in arm or draw in his outstretched arm—so too, vanishing from this near shore of the Ganges River, he reappeared on the far shore together with the Saṅgha of bhikkhus.

Addasā kho bhagavā te manusse appekacce nāvaṃ pariyesante, appekacce uḷumpaṃ pariyesante, appekacce kullaṃ bandhante apārā pāraṃ gantukāme.

The Blessed One saw those people: some seeking a boat, some seeking a raft, and some binding together a float, wishing to go from the near shore to the far shore.

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then the Blessed One, understanding the significance of it, on that occasion exclaimed this utterance of joy:

‘‘Ye taranti aṇṇavaṃ saraṃ,Setuṃ katvāna visajja pallalāni;Kullañhi jano pabandhati [bandhati (syā. pī.)],Tiṇṇā [nitiṇṇā (ka.)] medhāvino janā’’ti. chaṭṭhaṃ;

"Those who cross the ocean and the lake, Having built a bridge and abandoned the marshes; People indeed construct a float, The wise have crossed over." Sixth.
