# Ud 2.10 Bhaddiyasuttaṃ

## Ud 2.10 Bhaddiya Sutta

20. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā anupiyāyaṃ viharati ambavane. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā bhaddiyo kāḷīgodhāya putto araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānesi – ‘‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’’nti!

20.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Anupiya in the mango grove. Now at that time the Venerable Bhaddiya, the son of Kāḷīgodhā, when gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, would repeatedly utter: “Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!”

Assosuṃ kho sambahulā bhikkhū āyasmato bhaddiyassa kāḷīgodhāya puttassa araññagatassapi rukkhamūlagatassapi suññāgāragatassapi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānentassa – ‘‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’’nti! Sutvāna nesaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘nissaṃsayaṃ kho, āvuso, āyasmā bhaddiyo kāḷīgodhāya putto anabhirato brahmacariyaṃ carati, yaṃsa pubbe agāriyabhūtassa [agārikabhūtassa (syā.)] rajjasukhaṃ, so tamanussaramāno araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānesi – ‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’’’nti!

Several bhikkhus heard the Venerable Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā, repeatedly uttering: “Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!” while in the wilderness, at the foot of a tree, or in an empty dwelling. Upon hearing this, they thought: “Surely, friends, the Venerable Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā, is not delighting in the holy life. Formerly, he experienced the happiness of kingship; now, while recalling it, he repeatedly utters: ‘Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!’ whether in the wilderness, at the foot of a tree, or in an empty dwelling.”

Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘āyasmā, bhante, bhaddiyo kāḷīgodhāya putto araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānesi – ‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’nti! Nissaṃsayaṃ kho, bhante, āyasmā bhaddiyo kāḷīgodhāya putto anabhirato brahmacariyaṃ carati. Yaṃsa pubbe agāriyabhūtassa rajjasukhaṃ, so tamanussaramāno araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānesi – ‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’’’nti!

Then several bhikkhus approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, those bhikkhus said this to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, the Venerable Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā, when he has gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, repeatedly utters: ‘Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!’ Surely, venerable sir, the Venerable Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā, is not delighting in the holy life. Remembering the happiness of kingship he formerly enjoyed as a householder, when he has gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, he repeatedly utters: ‘Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!’”

Atha kho bhagavā aññataraṃ bhikkhuṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, bhikkhu, mama vacanena bhaddiyaṃ bhikkhuṃ āmantehi – ‘satthā taṃ, āvuso bhaddiya, āmantetī’’’ti.

Then the Blessed One addressed a certain monk: “Come, monk, go in my name and summon the monk Bhaddiya, saying, ‘The Teacher calls you, friend Bhaddiya.’”

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho so bhikkhu bhagavato paṭissutvā yenāyasmā bhaddiyo kāḷīgodhāya putto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhaddiyaṃ kāḷīgodhāya puttaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘satthā taṃ, āvuso bhaddiya, āmantetī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho āyasmā bhaddiyo kāḷīgodhāya putto tassa bhikkhuno paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ bhaddiyaṃ kāḷīgodhāya puttaṃ bhagavā etadavoca –

“Venerable sir, it is so,” replied the monk to the Blessed One. Then he approached the Venerable Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā. Having approached, he said to Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā: “Friend Bhaddiya, the Teacher is calling you.” “Yes, friend,” replied the Venerable Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā, to the monk. Then he approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. When the Venerable Bhaddiya, son of Kāḷīgodhā, was seated to one side, the Blessed One said to him:

‘‘Saccaṃ kira tvaṃ, bhaddiya, araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānesi – ‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’’’nti! ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti.

“Bhaddiya, is it true that even when you have gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, you repeatedly utter: ‘Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!’?” “Yes, venerable sir.”

‘‘Kiṃ pana [kaṃ pana (syā pī.)] tvaṃ, bhaddiya, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānesi – ‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’’’nti! ‘‘Pubbe me, bhante, agāriyabhūtassa rajjaṃ kārentassa antopi antepure rakkhā susaṃvihitā ahosi, bahipi antepure rakkhā susaṃvihitā ahosi, antopi nagare rakkhā susaṃvihitā ahosi, bahipi nagare rakkhā susaṃvihitā ahosi, antopi janapade rakkhā susaṃvihitā ahosi, bahipi janapade rakkhā susaṃvihitā ahosi. So kho ahaṃ, bhante, evaṃ rakkhito gopito santo bhīto ubbiggo ussaṅkī utrāsī vihāsiṃ. Etarahi kho panāhaṃ, bhante, araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi eko [ekako (syā. pī.)] abhīto anubbiggo anussaṅkī anutrāsī appossukko pannalomo paradattavutto [paradavutto (ka. sī. syā. pī.)], migabhūtena cetasā viharāmi. Imaṃ [idaṃ (sī. ka.)] kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno araññagatopi rukkhamūlagatopi suññāgāragatopi abhikkhaṇaṃ udānaṃ udānesi [udānemi (ka.)] – ‘aho sukhaṃ, aho sukha’’’nti!

“But, Bhaddiya, seeing what benefit do you, even when gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, repeatedly utter: ‘Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!’?” “Formerly, venerable sir, when I was a householder ruling the kingdom, there were guards well-established within the inner apartments, and guards well-established without the inner apartments, guards well-established within the city, and guards well-established without the city, guards well-established within the countryside, and guards well-established without the countryside. But I, venerable sir, thus guarded and protected, lived fearful, agitated, apprehensive, terrified, and anxious. But now, venerable sir, whether I have gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, I dwell alone, fearless, unagitated, unapprehensive, unterrified, without anxiety, with a shaven head, sustained by alms, abiding with a mind like that of a wild deer. Seeing this benefit, venerable sir, even when I have gone to the forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty dwelling, I repeatedly utter: ‘Ah, happiness! Ah, happiness!’”

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, understanding this matter, the Blessed One, on that occasion, uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Yassantarato na santi kopā,Itibhavābhavatañca vītivatto;Taṃ vigatabhayaṃ sukhiṃ asokaṃ,Devā nānubhavanti dassanāyā’’ti. dasamaṃ;

One in whom no anger exists within, Who has overcome this becoming and non-becoming; That one, fearless, happy, sorrowless— Even the gods do not perceive such a one. Tenth.

Mucalindavaggo dutiyo niṭṭhito.

The Mucalinda Chapter, the second, is concluded.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary is:

Mucalindo rājā daṇḍena, sakkāro upāsakena ca;Gabbhinī ekaputto ca, suppavāsā visākhā ca;Kāḷīgodhāya bhaddiyoti.

Mucalinda, the king with a staff; honor by the lay follower; the pregnant woman with one son; Suppavāsā and Visākhā; Kāḷīgodhā and Bhaddiya.
