# Ud 2.8 Suppavāsāsuttaṃ

## Ud 2.8 The Suppavāsā Discourse

18. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kuṇḍikāyaṃ [kuṇḍiyāyaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)] viharati kuṇḍadhānavane [kuṇḍiṭṭhānavane (syā. pī.)]. Tena kho pana samayena suppavāsā koliyadhītā satta vassāni gabbhaṃ dhāreti. Sattāhaṃ mūḷhagabbhā sā dukkhāhi tibbāhi kharāhi kaṭukāhi vedanāhi phuṭṭhā tīhi vitakkehi adhivāseti – ‘‘sammāsambuddho vata so bhagavā yo imassa evarūpassa dukkhassa pahānāya dhammaṃ deseti; suppaṭipanno vata tassa bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho yo imassa evarūpassa dukkhassa pahānāya paṭipanno; susukhaṃ vata taṃ nibbānaṃ yatthidaṃ evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ na saṃvijjatī’’ti.

18.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kuṇḍikā in the Kuṇḍadhāna Grove. Now at that time Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, had been carrying a fetus for seven years. For seven days she was suffering from a difficult birth, afflicted by sharp, severe, and bitter painful feelings. She endured them with three thoughts: "Truly, the Blessed One is the Perfectly Enlightened One, who teaches the Dhamma for the abandoning of such suffering; truly, well-practiced is the Blessed One's community of disciples, who practice for the abandoning of such suffering; truly, blissful is Nibbāna, where such suffering does not exist."

Atha kho suppavāsā koliyadhītā sāmikaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, ayyaputta, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mama vacanena bhagavato pāde sirasā vandāhi; appābādhaṃ appātaṅkaṃ lahuṭṭhānaṃ balaṃ phāsuvihāraṃ puccha – ‘suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā bhagavato pāde sirasā vandati; appābādhaṃ appātaṅkaṃ lahuṭṭhānaṃ balaṃ phāsuvihāraṃ pucchatī’ti. Evañca vadehi – ‘suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā satta vassāni gabbhaṃ dhāreti. Sattāhaṃ mūḷhagabbhā sā dukkhāhi tibbāhi kharāhi kaṭukāhi vedanāhi phuṭṭhā tīhi vitakkehi adhivāseti – sammāsambuddho vata so bhagavā yo imassa evarūpassa dukkhassa pahānāya dhammaṃ deseti; suppaṭipanno vata tassa bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho yo imassa evarūpassa dukkhassa pahānāya paṭipanno; susukhaṃ vata taṃ nibbānaṃ yatthidaṃ evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ na saṃvijjatī’’’ti.

Then Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, addressed her husband: "Come, dear husband, go to where the Blessed One is. Having gone there, with my words, pay homage to the Blessed One's feet with your head, and ask about his health, whether he is free from illness, free from affliction, light in body, strong, and living comfortably, saying: 'Venerable sir, Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, pays homage to the Blessed One's feet with her head, and asks about your health, whether you are free from illness, free from affliction, light in body, strong, and living comfortably.' And say this: 'Venerable sir, Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, has carried a pregnancy for seven years. For seven days she has been suffering from a difficult birth, assailed by sharp, severe, and bitter painful feelings. She endures it with these three thoughts: "Truly, the Blessed One is the Perfectly Enlightened One, who teaches the Dhamma for the abandoning of this kind of suffering. Truly, the Sangha of the Blessed One's disciples is practicing well, who are practicing for the abandoning of this kind of suffering. Truly, Nibbāna is bliss, where this kind of suffering does not exist."

‘‘Parama’’nti kho so koliyaputto suppavāsāya koliyadhītāya paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho koliyaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā bhagavato pāde sirasā vandati, appābādhaṃ appātaṅkaṃ lahuṭṭhānaṃ balaṃ phāsuvihāraṃ pucchati; evañca vadeti – ‘suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā satta vassāni gabbhaṃ dhāreti. Sattāhaṃ mūḷhagabbhā sā dukkhāhi tibbāhi kharāhi kaṭukāhi vedanāhi phuṭṭhā tīhi vitakkehi adhivāseti – sammāsambuddho vata so bhagavā yo imassa evarūpassa dukkhassa pahānāya dhammaṃ deseti; suppaṭipanno vata tassa bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho yo imassa evarūpassa dukkhassa pahānāya paṭipanno; susukhaṃ vata nibbānaṃ yatthidaṃ evarūpaṃ dukkhaṃ na saṃvijjatī’’’ti.

"Very well," the Koliyan youth replied to Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter. Having assented, he approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Koliyan youth said this to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter bows her head at the Blessed One’s feet. She inquires about your health, freedom from illness, lightness of body, strength, and comfortable abiding. And she says this: ‘Venerable sir, Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter has been carrying a fetus for seven years. For seven days, being in difficult labor, she has been afflicted by painful, intense, severe, and sharp feelings. She endures with three thoughts: Truly, the Blessed One is the Perfectly Enlightened One, who teaches the Dhamma for the abandoning of this kind of suffering; truly, well-practiced is the community of disciples of the Blessed One, who have practiced for the abandoning of this kind of suffering; truly, blissful is Nibbāna, where this kind of suffering does not exist.’"

‘‘Sukhinī hotu suppavāsā koliyadhītā; arogā arogaṃ puttaṃ vijāyatū’’ti. Saha vacanā ca pana bhagavato suppavāsā koliyadhītā sukhinī arogā arogaṃ puttaṃ vijāyi.

"May Suppavasa, the Koliyan daughter, be happy; may she be free from illness and give birth to a healthy child." And with the utterance of the Blessed One's words, Suppavasa, the Koliyan daughter, became happy, free from illness, and gave birth to a healthy child.

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho so koliyaputto bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā yena sakaṃ gharaṃ tena paccāyāsi. Addasā kho so koliyaputto suppavāsaṃ koliyadhītaraṃ sukhiniṃ arogaṃ arogaṃ puttaṃ vijātaṃ. Disvānassa etadahosi – ‘‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho, tathāgatassa mahiddhikatā mahānubhāvatā, yatra hi nāmāyaṃ suppavāsā koliyadhītā saha vacanā ca pana [saha vacanā pana (pī.), saha vacanā (?)] bhagavato sukhinī arogā arogaṃ puttaṃ vijāyissatī’’ti! Attamano pamudito pītisomanassajāto ahosi.

"Thus, venerable sir," the Koliyan youth said. Having rejoiced in and approved the Blessed One’s words, he rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and went to his own home. He saw Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, well, without illness, and delivered of a healthy child. Seeing her, this occurred to him: "Wonderful indeed! Amazing, indeed! The great power and might of the Tathāgata! For this Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter, at the very word of the Blessed One, has given birth to a healthy child, happy and free from illness!" He was pleased, joyful, filled with gladness and happiness.

Atha kho suppavāsā koliyadhītā sāmikaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, ayyaputta, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā mama vacanena bhagavato pāde sirasā vandāhi – ‘suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā bhagavato pāde sirasā vandatī’ti; evañca vadehi – ‘suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā satta vassāni gabbhaṃ dhāreti. Sattāhaṃ mūḷhagabbhā sā etarahi sukhinī arogā arogaṃ puttaṃ vijātā. Sā sattāhaṃ buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ bhattena nimanteti. Adhivāsetu kira, bhante, bhagavā suppavāsāya koliyadhītāya satta bhattāni saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’’ti.

Then Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, addressed her husband: "Come, husband, go to the Blessed One. Approach him and, in my name, pay homage to the Blessed One’s feet with your head, saying: 'Suppavāsā, venerable sir, the Koliyan daughter, pays homage to the Blessed One’s feet with her head.' And say this: 'Suppavāsā, venerable sir, the Koliyan daughter, has carried a child in her womb for seven years. For seven days she had suffered obstructed labor, but now she is happy and well, and has given birth to a healthy child. She invites the Sangha of bhikkhus, headed by the Blessed One, for a meal for seven days. May the Blessed One, venerable sir, consent to Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter’s, offering of seven meals together with the Sangha of bhikkhus.'

‘‘Parama’’nti kho so koliyaputto suppavāsāya koliyadhītāya paṭissutvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so koliyaputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

"Very well," the Koliyan youth replied to Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter. He then approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated thus to one side, the Koliyan youth said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā bhagavato pāde sirasā vandati; evañca vadeti – ‘suppavāsā, bhante, koliyadhītā satta vassāni gabbhaṃ dhāreti. Sattāhaṃ mūḷhagabbhā sā etarahi sukhinī arogā arogaṃ puttaṃ vijātā. Sā sattāhaṃ buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ bhattena nimanteti. Adhivāsetu kira, bhante, bhagavā suppavāsāya koliyadhītāya satta bhattāni saddhiṃ bhikkhusaṅghenā’’’ti.

"Suppavāsā, venerable sir, the Koliyan daughter, pays homage with her head at the Blessed One’s feet; and says this: ‘Suppavāsā, venerable sir, the Koliyan daughter, has carried a child in her womb for seven years. For seven days she had suffered obstructed labor, but now she is happy and well, and has given birth to a healthy child. She invites the Sangha of bhikkhus, headed by the Blessed One, for a meal for seven days. May the Blessed One, venerable sir, consent to Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter’s offering of seven meals together with the Sangha of bhikkhus.’"

Tena kho pana samayena aññatarena upāsakena buddhappamukho bhikkhusaṅgho svātanāya bhattena nimantito hoti. So ca upāsako āyasmato mahāmoggallānassa [mahāmoggalānassa (ka.)] upaṭṭhāko hoti. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ehi tvaṃ, moggallāna, yena so upāsako tenupasaṅkama; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ upāsakaṃ evaṃ vadehi – ‘suppavāsā, āvuso, koliyadhītā satta vassāni gabbhaṃ dhāresi. Sattāhaṃ mūḷhagabbhā sā etarahi sukhinī arogā arogaṃ puttaṃ vijātā. Sā sattāhaṃ buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ bhattena nimanteti. Karotu suppavāsā koliyadhītā satta bhattāni, pacchā tvaṃ karissasī’ti [karissasīti saññāpehi (ka.)]. Tuyheso upaṭṭhāko’’ti.

At that time, a certain lay follower had invited the Sangha of bhikkhus headed by the Blessed One for a meal the following day. That lay follower was a supporter of Venerable Mahāmoggallāna. Then the Blessed One addressed Venerable Mahāmoggallāna: "Come, Moggallāna, go to that lay follower. Approach him and say to him: 'Friend, Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, carried a child in her womb for seven years. For seven days she had suffered obstructed labor, but now she is happy and well, and has given birth to a healthy child. She invites the Sangha of bhikkhus headed by the Blessed One for a meal for seven days. Let Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, undertake the seven meals; afterward, you undertake yours. He is your supporter.'"

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhagavato paṭissutvā yena so upāsako tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā taṃ upāsakaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘suppavāsā, āvuso, koliyadhītā satta vassāni gabbhaṃ dhāreti. Sattāhaṃ mūḷhagabbhā sā etarahi sukhinī arogā arogaṃ puttaṃ vijātā. Sā sattāhaṃ buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ bhattena nimanteti. Karotu suppavāsā koliyadhītā satta bhattāni, pacchā tvaṃ karissasī’’ti.

"Very well, venerable sir," replied Venerable Mahāmoggallāna to the Blessed One. Then he approached that lay follower and said to him: "Friend, Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, has carried a child in her womb for seven years. For seven days she had suffered obstructed labor, but now she is happy and well, and has given birth to a healthy child. She has invited the Sangha of bhikkhus, headed by the Blessed One, for a meal for seven days. Let Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, undertake the seven meals; afterward, you undertake yours."

‘‘Sace me, bhante, ayyo mahāmoggallāno tiṇṇaṃ dhammānaṃ pāṭibhogo – bhogānañca jīvitassa ca saddhāya ca, karotu suppavāsā koliyadhītā satta bhattāni, pacchāhaṃ karissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Dvinnaṃ kho te ahaṃ [dvinnaṃ kho tesaṃ (pī.), dvinnaṃ kho nesaṃ (ka.)], āvuso, dhammānaṃ pāṭibhogo – bhogānañca jīvitassa ca. Saddhāya pana tvaṃyeva pāṭibhogo’’ti.

"Venerable sir, if the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna would be guarantor for me in three things—wealth, life, and faith—then let Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, prepare seven meals; afterwards I will do so." "Friend, I can be guarantor for you in two things—wealth and life. But regarding faith, you yourself must be guarantor."

‘‘Sace me, bhante, ayyo mahāmoggallāno dvinnaṃ dhammānaṃ pāṭibhogo – bhogānañca jīvitassa ca, karotu suppavāsā koliyadhītā satta bhattāni, pacchāhaṃ karissāmī’’ti.

"Venerable sir, if the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna would be guarantor for me in two things—wealth and life—then let Suppavāsā, the Koliyan daughter, prepare seven meals; afterwards I will do so."

Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno taṃ upāsakaṃ saññāpetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saññatto [saññāto (syā.)], bhante, so upāsako mayā; karotu suppavāsā koliyadhītā satta bhattāni, pacchā so karissatī’’ti.

Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna, having informed that lay follower, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he said this to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, that lay follower has been informed by me. Let Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter offer seven meals; afterwards he will do so.”

Atha kho suppavāsā koliyadhītā sattāhaṃ buddhappamukhaṃ bhikkhusaṅghaṃ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappesi sampavāresi, tañca dārakaṃ bhagavantaṃ vandāpesi sabbañca bhikkhusaṅghaṃ.

Then Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter, for seven days, personally served and satisfied the Saṅgha of bhikkhus headed by the Blessed One with choice food and drink, and she had that child pay homage to the Blessed One and the entire Saṅgha of bhikkhus.

Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto taṃ dārakaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kacci te, dāraka, khamanīyaṃ, kacci yāpanīyaṃ, kacci na kiñci dukkha’’nti? ‘‘Kuto me, bhante sāriputta, khamanīyaṃ, kuto yāpanīyaṃ! Satta me vassāni lohitakumbhiyaṃ vuttānī’’ti.

Then the Venerable Sāriputta said to that boy: “Are you well, boy? Is it endurable for you? Are you suffering at all?” “How could I be well, Venerable Sāriputta? How could I be able to live? For seven years I have been dwelling in a pool of blood.”

Atha kho suppavāsā koliyadhītā – ‘‘putto me dhammasenāpatinā saddhiṃ mantetī’’ti attamanā pamuditā pītisomanassajātā ahosi. Atha kho bhagavā (suppavāsaṃ kolīyadhītaraṃ attamanaṃ pamuditaṃ pītisomanassajātaṃ viditvā [disvā (sī.)]) [( ) natthi iṅgalisapotthake] suppavāsaṃ koliyadhītaraṃ etadavoca – ‘‘iccheyyāsi tvaṃ, suppavāse, aññampi evarūpaṃ putta’’nti? ‘‘Iccheyyāmahaṃ, bhagavā, aññānipi evarūpāni satta puttānī’’ti.

Then Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter, thinking, “My son is conferring with the General of the Dhamma,” became delighted, pleased, filled with joy and happiness. Then the Blessed One, knowing Suppavāsā the Koliyan daughter to be happy, delighted, and filled with joy and happiness, said to her: “Would you wish, Suppavāsā, for another son like him?” “I would wish, Blessed One, for seven more sons like him,” she replied.

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, the Blessed One, having realized the meaning of this, on that occasion, uttered this inspired verse:

‘‘Asātaṃ sātarūpena, piyarūpena appiyaṃ;Dukkhaṃ sukhassa rūpena, pamattamativattatī’’ti. aṭṭhamaṃ;

The disagreeable appears as agreeable, the unpleasant as pleasant, suffering as happiness—[these] overcome the heedless one. Eighth.
