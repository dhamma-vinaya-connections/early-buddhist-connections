# Ud 2.4 Sakkārasuttaṃ

## Ud 2.4 The Honor Discourse

14. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavā sakkato hoti garukato mānito pūjito apacito, lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Bhikkhusaṅghopi sakkato hoti garukato mānito pūjito apacito, lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Aññatitthiyā pana paribbājakā asakkatā honti agarukatā amānitā [na apacitā (syā. pī.)] apūjitā anapacitā, na lābhino cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Atha kho te aññatitthiyā paribbājakā bhagavato sakkāraṃ asahamānā bhikkhusaṅghassa ca gāme ca araññe ca bhikkhū disvā asabbhāhi pharusāhi vācāhi akkosanti paribhāsanti rosenti vihesenti.

14. Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi in Jeta's Grove, Anathapindika's monastery. At that time the Blessed One was honored, respected, revered, venerated, and esteemed, and received robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. The community of bhikkhus was also honored, respected, revered, venerated, and esteemed, and received robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. But the wandering ascetics of other sects were not honored, respected, revered, venerated, or esteemed, and did not receive robes, almsfood, lodgings, or medicinal requisites. Then, unable to bear the honor shown to the Blessed One and the community of bhikkhus, those wandering ascetics, seeing bhikkhus in villages and forests, abused, insulted, provoked, and harassed them with rude and harsh words.

Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘etarahi, bhante, bhagavā sakkato garukato mānito pūjito apacito, lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Bhikkhusaṅghopi sakkato garukato mānito pūjito apacito, lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Aññatitthiyā pana paribbājakā asakkatā agarukatā amānitā apūjitā anapacitā, na lābhino cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Atha kho te, bhante, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā bhagavato sakkāraṃ asahamānā bhikkhusaṅghassa ca gāme ca araññe ca bhikkhū disvā asabbhāhi pharusāhi vācāhi akkosanti paribhāsanti rosenti vihesantī’’ti.

Then many bhikkhus approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those bhikkhus said to the Blessed One: "Now, venerable sir, the Blessed One is honored, respected, revered, venerated, esteemed, and obtains robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. The community of bhikkhus is also honored, respected, revered, venerated, esteemed, and obtains robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. But wanderers of other sects are not honored, respected, revered, venerated, esteemed, nor do they obtain robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. Now, venerable sir, those wanderers of other sects, unable to endure the honor accorded the Blessed One and the community of bhikkhus, seeing bhikkhus in the village or in the forest, revile and abuse them with harsh and rough words, becoming angry and causing them distress."

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, the Blessed One, having understood this matter, on that occasion, uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Gāme araññe sukhadukkhaphuṭṭho,Nevattato no parato dahetha;Phusanti phassā upadhiṃ paṭicca,Nirūpadhiṃ kena phuseyyu phassā’’ti. catutthaṃ;

"Touched by pleasure and pain, in village or forest, let one not be burned, neither by oneself nor by another. Contacts touch one dependent on clinging; how could contacts touch one devoid of clinging?"
