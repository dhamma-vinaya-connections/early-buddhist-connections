# Ud 6.1 Āyusaṅkhārossajjanasuttaṃ

## Ud 6.1 The Discourse on Relinquishing the Life-Force

51. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya vesāliṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Vesāliyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘gaṇhāhi, ānanda, nisīdanaṃ. Yena cāpālaṃ [pāvālaṃ (syā.)] cetiyaṃ tenupasaṅkamissāma divāvihārāyā’’ti.

51.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling in Vesālī, in the Great Wood, at the Hall with the Peaked Roof. Then the Blessed One, having dressed in the morning, taking his bowl and robe, entered Vesālī for alms. Having walked for alms in Vesālī and returning from the alms round after the meal, he addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Take a sitting mat, Ānanda. Let us go to the Cāpāla Shrine for the day's sojourn."

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissutvā nisīdanaṃ ādāya bhagavantaṃ piṭṭhito piṭṭhito anubandhi. Atha kho bhagavā yena cāpālaṃ cetiyaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi –

"Yes, venerable sir," replied the Venerable Ānanda. Taking his sitting mat, he followed closely behind the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One went to the Cāpāla Shrine, and upon arriving, he sat down on the prepared seat. Sitting, the Blessed One addressed the Venerable Ānanda:

‘‘Ramaṇīyā, ānanda, vesālī; ramaṇīyaṃ udenaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ gotamakaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ sattambaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ bahuputtaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ sārandadaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ cāpālaṃ cetiyaṃ. Yassa kassaci, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā, so ākaṅkhamāno ( ) [(ānanda) (ka.)] kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā. Tathāgatassa kho, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā. Ākaṅkhamāno, ānanda, tathāgato kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā’’ti.

"Delightful, Ānanda, is Vesālī; delightful is the Udena Shrine; delightful is the Gotamaka Shrine; delightful is the Sattamba Shrine; delightful is the Bahuputta Shrine; delightful is the Sārandada Shrine; delightful is the Cāpāla Shrine. Ānanda, anyone who has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle of, made a basis of, established, practiced, and thoroughly mastered the four bases of spiritual power, could—if he so desired—remain for an aeon or the remainder of an aeon. Ānanda, the Tathāgata has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle of, made a basis of, established, practiced, and thoroughly mastered the four bases of spiritual power. If he so desired, Ānanda, the Tathāgata could remain for an aeon or the remainder of an aeon."

Evampi kho āyasmā ānando bhagavatā oḷārike nimitte kayiramāne, oḷārike obhāse kayiramāne, nāsakkhi paṭivijjhituṃ; na bhagavantaṃ yāci – ‘‘tiṭṭhatu, bhante, bhagavā kappaṃ; tiṭṭhatu sugato kappaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’’nti, yathā taṃ mārena pariyuṭṭhitacitto. Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi –

Even so, although the Blessed One was giving such a palpable sign, such a palpable indication, the Venerable Ānanda was unable to comprehend it; he did not request the Blessed One: 'May the Blessed One remain, Lord, for an aeon; may the Fortunate One remain for an aeon, for the welfare and happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of gods and humans'—his mind being possessed by Māra. A second time... A third time, the Blessed One addressed the Venerable Ānanda:

‘‘Ramaṇīyā, ānanda, vesālī; ramaṇīyaṃ udenaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ gotamakaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ sattambaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ bahuputtaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ sārandadaṃ cetiyaṃ; ramaṇīyaṃ cāpālaṃ cetiyaṃ. Yassa kassaci, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā, so ākaṅkhamāno kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā. Tathāgatassa kho, ānanda, cattāro iddhipādā bhāvitā bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā. Ākaṅkhamāno, ānanda, tathāgato kappaṃ vā tiṭṭheyya kappāvasesaṃ vā’’ti.

"Delightful, Ānanda, is Vesālī; delightful is the Udena Shrine; delightful is the Gotamaka Shrine; delightful is the Sattamba Shrine; delightful is the Bahuputta Shrine; delightful is the Sārandada Shrine; delightful is the Cāpāla Shrine. Ānanda, anyone who has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle of, made a basis of, established, practiced, and thoroughly mastered the four bases of spiritual power, could—if he so desired—remain for an aeon or the remainder of an aeon. Ānanda, the Tathāgata has developed, cultivated, made a vehicle of, made a basis of, established, practiced, and thoroughly mastered the four bases of spiritual power. If he so desired, Ānanda, the Tathāgata could remain for an aeon or the remainder of an aeon."

Evampi kho āyasmā ānando bhagavatā oḷārike nimitte kayiramāne, oḷārike obhāse kayiramāne, nāsakkhi paṭivijjhituṃ; na bhagavantaṃ yāci – ‘‘tiṭṭhatu, bhante, bhagavā kappaṃ; tiṭṭhatu sugato kappaṃ bahujanahitāya bahujanasukhāya lokānukampāya atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussāna’’nti, yathā taṃ mārena pariyuṭṭhitacitto.

Even so, although the Blessed One gave such a palpable sign, such a palpable indication, the Venerable Ānanda, with his mind possessed by Māra, was unable to comprehend; he did not request the Blessed One, "May the Blessed One remain, Lord, for an aeon; may the Fortunate One remain for an aeon, for the benefit and happiness of the many, out of compassion for the world, for the good, benefit, and happiness of gods and humans."

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘gaccha tvaṃ, ānanda, yassadāni kālaṃ maññasī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissutvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā avidūre aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle nisīdi.

Then the Blessed One addressed Venerable Ānanda, saying: "Go, Ānanda, at the time you now deem fit." "Yes, Venerable Sir," replied Venerable Ānanda, having assented to the Blessed One. He rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and sat down at the root of a certain tree not far away.

Atha kho māro pāpimā, acirapakkante āyasmante ānande, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhito kho māro pāpimā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

Then Māra the Evil One, soon after Venerable Ānanda had left, approached the Blessed One; having approached, he stood to one side. Standing to one side, Māra the Evil One said this to the Blessed One:

‘‘Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā; parinibbātu sugato; parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato. Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me bhikkhū na sāvakā bhavissanti viyattā vinītā visāradā [visāradā pattayogakhemā (a. ni. 8.70), visāradappattā yogakhemā (sī. pī. ka.), visāradappattā yogakhemakāmā (syā.)] bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacārino, sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhissanti desessanti paññapessanti paṭṭhapessanti vivarissanti vibhajissanti uttānīkarissanti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desessantī’ti. Etarahi kho pana, bhante [santi kho pana bhante etarahi (sī. pī. saṃ. ni. 5.822)] bhikkhū bhagavato sāvakā viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacārino sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhanti desenti paññapenti paṭṭhapenti vivaranti vibhajanti uttānīkaronti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desenti. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā; parinibbātu sugato; parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato.

"Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna; let the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna. The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna. For the Blessed One has spoken these words: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until my bhikkhus become disciples—wise, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing rightly, living in conformity with the Dhamma—until, having learned their teacher's doctrine, they can explain, teach, proclaim, establish, reveal, analyze, and clarify it, and until they can refute with good reason any opposing doctrines that may arise and teach the Dhamma with its wondrous qualities.’ But now, venerable sir, the bhikkhus are the Blessed One’s disciples—wise, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing rightly, living in conformity with the Dhamma—and having learned their teacher's doctrine, they can explain, teach, proclaim, establish, reveal, analyze, and clarify it, and they can refute with good reason any opposing doctrines that may arise and teach the Dhamma with its wondrous qualities. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna; let the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna. The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna."

‘‘Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me bhikkhuniyo na sāvikā bhavissanti viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhissanti desessanti paññapessanti paṭṭhapessanti vivarissanti vibhajissanti uttānīkarissanti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desessantī’ti. Etarahi kho pana, bhante, bhikkhuniyo bhagavato sāvikā viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhanti desenti paññapenti paṭṭhapenti vivaranti vibhajanti uttānīkaronti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desenti. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā; parinibbātu sugato; parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato.

"The Blessed One has also spoken these words: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until my nuns become disciples—wise, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing rightly, living in conformity with the Dhamma—until, having learned their teacher's doctrine, they can explain, teach, proclaim, establish, reveal, analyze, and clarify it, and until they can refute with good reason any opposing doctrine that may arise and teach the Dhamma with its marvelous qualities.’ But now, Venerable Sir, the nuns are the Blessed One’s disciples—wise, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing rightly, living in conformity with the Dhamma—and having learned their teacher's doctrine, they can explain, teach, proclaim, establish, reveal, analyze, and clarify it, and they can refute with good reason any opposing doctrine that may arise and teach the Dhamma with its marvelous qualities. Now, Venerable Sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna; let the Fortunate One attain final Nibbāna. The time has come, Venerable Sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna."

‘‘Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me upāsakā na sāvakā bhavissanti viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacārino sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhissanti desessanti paññapessanti paṭṭhapessanti vivarissanti vibhajissanti uttānīkarissanti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desessantī’ti. Etarahi kho pana, bhante, upāsakā bhagavato sāvakā viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacārino sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhanti desenti paññapenti paṭṭhapenti vivaranti vibhajanti uttānīkaronti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desenti. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā; parinibbātu sugato; parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato.

Venerable sir, the Blessed One has spoken these words: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until my male lay disciples become well-trained, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing what is proper, living in conformity with the Dhamma, having learned their own teacher’s doctrine, and are able to explain, teach, proclaim, establish, disclose, analyze, and elucidate it, and having thoroughly refuted any opposing doctrines that might arise by means of the Dhamma, they are able to teach the Dhamma with its marvels.’ But now, venerable sir, the Blessed One’s male lay disciples are well-trained, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing what is proper, living in conformity with the Dhamma, having learned their own teacher’s doctrine, and are able to explain, teach, proclaim, establish, disclose, analyze, and elucidate it, and having thoroughly refuted any opposing doctrines that might arise by means of the Dhamma, they are able to teach the Dhamma with its marvels. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna; let the Well-gone One attain final Nibbāna. The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna.

‘‘Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me upāsikā na sāvikā bhavissanti viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhissanti desessanti paññapessanti paṭṭhapessanti vivarissanti vibhajissanti uttānīkarissanti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desessantī’ti. Etarahi kho pana, bhante, upāsikā bhagavato sāvikā viyattā vinītā visāradā bahussutā dhammadharā dhammānudhammappaṭipannā sāmīcippaṭipannā anudhammacāriniyo sakaṃ ācariyakaṃ uggahetvā ācikkhanti desenti paññapenti paṭṭhapenti vivaranti vibhajanti uttānīkaronti uppannaṃ parappavādaṃ sahadhammena suniggahitaṃ niggahetvā sappāṭihāriyaṃ dhammaṃ desenti. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā; parinibbātu sugato; parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato.

Venerable sir, the Blessed One has spoken these words: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until my female lay disciples become well-trained, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing what is proper, living in conformity with the Dhamma, having learned their own teacher’s doctrine, and are able to explain, teach, proclaim, establish, disclose, analyze, and elucidate it, and having thoroughly refuted any opposing doctrines that might arise by means of the Dhamma, they are able to teach the Dhamma with its marvels.’ But now, venerable sir, the Blessed One’s female lay disciples are well-trained, disciplined, confident, learned, upholders of the Dhamma, practicing in accordance with the Dhamma, practicing what is proper, living in conformity with the Dhamma, having learned their own teacher’s doctrine, and are able to explain, teach, proclaim, establish, disclose, analyze, and elucidate it, and having thoroughly refuted any opposing doctrines that might arise by means of the Dhamma, they are able to teach the Dhamma with its marvels. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna; let the Well-gone One attain final Nibbāna. The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna.

‘‘Bhāsitā kho panesā, bhante, bhagavatā vācā – ‘na tāvāhaṃ, pāpima, parinibbāyissāmi yāva me idaṃ brahmacariyaṃ na iddhañceva bhavissati phītañca vitthārikaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsita’nti. Etarahi kho pana, bhante [tayidaṃ bhante (saṃ. ni. 5.822)], bhagavato brahmacariyaṃ iddhañceva phītañca vitthārikaṃ bāhujaññaṃ puthubhūtaṃ yāva devamanussehi suppakāsitaṃ. Parinibbātu dāni, bhante, bhagavā; parinibbātu sugato; parinibbānakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato’’ti.

Venerable sir, the Blessed One has spoken these words: ‘I will not attain final Nibbāna, Evil One, until this holy life of mine becomes prosperous, flourishing, widespread, populous, and extensive, and is well-proclaimed among gods and humans.’ But now, venerable sir, the Blessed One’s holy life is prosperous, flourishing, widespread, populous, and extensive, and is well-proclaimed among gods and humans. Now, venerable sir, let the Blessed One attain final Nibbāna; let the Well-gone One attain final Nibbāna. The time has come, venerable sir, for the Blessed One’s final Nibbāna.

Evaṃ vutte, bhagavā māraṃ pāpimantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘appossukko tvaṃ, pāpima, hohi. Na ciraṃ tathāgatassa parinibbānaṃ bhavissati. Ito tiṇṇaṃ māsānaṃ accayena tathāgato parinibbāyissatī’’ti.

When this was said, the Blessed One addressed Māra the Evil One: “Be at ease, Evil One. The Tathāgata’s final Nibbāna will not be long. After three months from now the Tathāgata will attain final Nibbāna.”

Atha kho bhagavā cāpāle cetiye sato sampajāno āyusaṅkhāraṃ ossajji. Ossaṭṭhe ca bhagavatā āyusaṅkhāre mahābhūmicālo ahosi bhiṃsanako lomahaṃso, devadundubhiyo [devadudrabhiyo (ka.)] ca phaliṃsu.

Then the Blessed One, at the Cāpāla Shrine, mindful and clearly comprehending, relinquished the life-span formation. When the Blessed One had relinquished the life-span formation, there was a great earthquake, dreadful and hair-raising, and the divine drums burst forth.

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, having understood this matter, on that occasion the Blessed One uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Tulamatulañca sambhavaṃ,Bhavasaṅkhāramavassaji muni;Ajjhattarato samāhito,Abhindi kavacamivattasambhava’’nti. paṭhamaṃ;

The comparable and the incomparable, the becoming, / The sage has relinquished the volitional formations of existence. / Delighting in the inner, composed, / He has broken through the armor of self-becoming.
