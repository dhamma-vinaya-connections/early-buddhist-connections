# Ud 6.10 Uppajjantisuttaṃ

## Ud 6.10 The Discourse on Arising

60. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

60.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi in Jeta's Grove, Anathapindika's Park. Then the Venerable Ananda approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Ananda said this to the Blessed One:

‘‘Yāvakīvañca, bhante, tathāgatā loke nuppajjanti arahanto sammāsambuddhā tāva aññatitthiyā paribbājakā sakkatā honti garukatā mānitā pūjitā apacitā lābhino cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Yato ca kho, bhante, tathāgatā loke uppajjanti arahanto sammāsambuddhā atha aññatitthiyā paribbājakā asakkatā honti agarukatā amānitā apūjitā anapacitā na lābhino cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Bhagavā yeva [bhagavā ceva (syā.)] dāni, bhante, sakkato hoti garukato mānito pūjito apacito lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ, bhikkhusaṅgho cā’’ti.

“Venerable sir, as long as the Tathāgatas, the Arahants, the Perfectly Enlightened Ones, do not arise in the world, the wandering ascetics of other sects are honored, respected, esteemed, revered, venerated, and are recipients of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. But when, venerable sir, the Tathāgatas, the Arahants, the Perfectly Enlightened Ones, arise in the world, then the wandering ascetics of other sects are not honored, respected, esteemed, revered, venerated, nor are they recipients of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. Now, venerable sir, only the Blessed One is honored, respected, esteemed, revered, venerated, and is a recipient of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites, as well as the community of bhikkhus.”

‘‘Evametaṃ, ānanda, yāvakīvañca, ānanda, tathāgatā loke nuppajjanti arahanto sammāsambuddhā tāva aññatitthiyā paribbājakā sakkatā honti garukatā mānitā pūjitā apacitā lābhino cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Yato ca kho, ānanda, tathāgatā loke uppajjanti arahanto sammāsambuddhā atha aññatitthiyā paribbājakā asakkatā honti agarukatā amānitā apūjitā anapacitā na lābhino cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Tathāgatova [tathāgato ceva (syā.)] dāni sakkato hoti garukato mānito pūjito apacito lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ, bhikkhusaṅgho cā’’ti.

“So it is, Ānanda. For as long as, Ānanda, the Tathāgatas, the Arahants, the Perfectly Enlightened Ones do not arise in the world, the wandering ascetics of other sects are honored, respected, esteemed, revered, venerated, and are gainers of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. But when, Ānanda, the Tathāgatas, the Arahants, the Perfectly Enlightened Ones arise in the world, then the wandering ascetics of other sects are not honored, not respected, not esteemed, not revered, not venerated, nor are they gainers of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites. Now, Ānanda, only the Tathāgata is honored, respected, esteemed, revered, venerated, and is a gainer of robes, almsfood, lodgings, and medicinal requisites, as well as the community of bhikkhus.”

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, the Blessed One, having understood this matter, on that occasion uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Obhāsati tāva so kimi,Yāva na unnamate [uggamati (sī.), unnamati (syā.)] pabhaṅkaro;(Sa) [( ) natthi sī. syā. potthakesu] verocanamhi uggate,Hatappabho hoti na cāpi bhāsati.

“As long as the glowworm shines, / The light-maker has not yet risen. / But when the radiant one has risen, / Its light is destroyed and it does not shine.”

‘‘Evaṃ obhāsitameva takkikānaṃ [titthiyānaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)],Yāva sammāsambuddhā loke nuppajjanti;Na takkikā sujjhanti na cāpi sāvakā,Duddiṭṭhī na dukkhā pamuccare’’ti. dasamaṃ;

“Just so, the debaters shine, / Only until Perfectly Awakened Ones arise in the world. / Debaters are not purified, nor are their disciples, / Those with wrong view are not freed from suffering.” (Tenth)

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary is:

Āyujaṭilavekkhaṇā, tayo titthiyā subhūti;Gaṇikā upāti navamo, uppajjanti ca te dasāti.

Āyujaṭila, Vekkhaṇa, the three sectarians, Subhūti; The courtesan, Upāti the ninth; And these ten arise.

Jaccandhavaggo chaṭṭho niṭṭhito.

The Chapter on the Blind from Birth is Finished, the Sixth.
