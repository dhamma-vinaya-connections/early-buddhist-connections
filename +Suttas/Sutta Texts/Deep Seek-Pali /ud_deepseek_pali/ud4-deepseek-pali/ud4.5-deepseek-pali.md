# Ud 4.5 Nāgasuttaṃ

## Ud 4.5 The Elephant Discourse

35. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena bhagavā ākiṇṇo viharati bhikkhūhi bhikkhūnīhi upāsakehi upāsikāhi rājūhi rājamahāmattehi titthiyehi titthiyasāvakehi. Ākiṇṇo dukkhaṃ na phāsu viharati. Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘ahaṃ kho etarahi ākiṇṇo viharāmi bhikkhūhi bhikkhūnīhi upāsakehi upāsikāhi rājūhi rājamahāmattehi titthiyehi titthiyasāvakehi. Ākiṇṇo dukkhaṃ na phāsu viharāmi. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ eko gaṇasmā vūpakaṭṭho vihareyya’’nti.

35.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambī in Ghosita’s Park. At that time the Blessed One was dwelling amidst crowds of bhikkhus, nuns, laymen, laywomen, kings, royal ministers, sectarians, and their disciples. Amidst crowds, he dwelled uncomfortably and not at ease. Then it occurred to the Blessed One: “Now I am dwelling amidst crowds of bhikkhus, nuns, laymen, laywomen, kings, royal ministers, sectarians, and their disciples. Amidst crowds, I dwell uncomfortably and not at ease. What if I were to dwell alone, withdrawn from the assembly?”

Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya kosambiṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Kosambiyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto sāmaṃ senāsanaṃ saṃsāmetvā pattacīvaramādāya anāmantetvā upaṭṭhākaṃ anapaloketvā bhikkhusaṅghaṃ eko adutiyo yena pālileyyakaṃ tena cārikaṃ pakkāmi. Anupubbena cārikaṃ caramāno yena pālileyyakaṃ tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā pālileyyake viharati rakkhitavanasaṇḍe bhaddasālamūle.

Then, in the morning, the Blessed One dressed, took his bowl and robe, and entered Kosambī for alms. After wandering for alms in Kosambī and returning from the alms round after the meal, he put away his own lodging, took his bowl and robe, and without informing his attendant or the community of bhikkhus, departed alone, without a second, on a journey toward Pālileyyaka. Traveling stage by stage, he arrived at Pālileyyaka. There the Blessed One dwelled in Pālileyyaka, at the root of a beautiful sal tree in the Rakkhita Grove.

Aññataropi kho hatthināgo ākiṇṇo viharati hatthīhi hatthinīhi hatthikalabhehi hatthicchāpehi. Chinnaggāni ceva tiṇāni khādati, obhaggobhaggañcassa sākhābhaṅgaṃ khādanti, āvilāni ca pānīyāni pivati, ogāhā cassa uttiṇṇassa hatthiniyo kāyaṃ upanighaṃsantiyo gacchanti. Ākiṇṇo dukkhaṃ na phāsu viharati. Atha kho tassa hatthināgassa etadahosi – ‘‘ahaṃ kho etarahi ākiṇṇo viharāmi hatthīhi hatthinīhi hatthikalabhehi hatthicchāpehi, chinnaggāni ceva tiṇāni khādāmi, obhaggobhaggañca me sākhābhaṅgaṃ khādanti, āvilāni ca pānīyāni pivāmi, ogāhā ca me uttiṇṇassa hatthiniyo kāyaṃ upanighaṃsantiyo gacchanti, ākiṇṇo dukkhaṃ na phāsu viharāmi. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ eko gaṇasmā vūpakaṭṭho vihareyya’’nti.

Now a certain bull elephant was dwelling amidst crowds of elephants, she-elephants, young elephants, and elephant calves. He ate grass with cropped tips, they ate branches broken and bent for him, he drank turbid water, and when he emerged from the water, the she-elephants, rubbing against his body, went away. Amidst crowds, he dwelled uncomfortably and not at ease. Then it occurred to that bull elephant: “Now I am dwelling amidst crowds of elephants, she-elephants, young elephants, and elephant calves. I eat grass with cropped tips, they eat branches broken and bent for me, I drink turbid water, and when I emerge from the water, the she-elephants, rubbing against my body, go away. Amidst crowds, I dwell uncomfortably and not at ease. What if I were to dwell alone, withdrawn from the herd?”

Atha kho so hatthināgo yūthā apakkamma yena pālileyyakaṃ rakkhitavanasaṇḍo bhaddasālamūlaṃ yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami. Tatra sudaṃ [upasaṅkamitvā tatra sudaṃ (syā. pī. ka.)] so hatthināgo yasmiṃ padese bhagavā viharati taṃ padesaṃ [appaharitañca karoti, soṇḍāya (bahūsu)] appaharitaṃ karoti, soṇḍāya ca [appaharitañca karoti, soṇḍāya (bahūsu)] bhagavato pānīyaṃ paribhojanīyaṃ upaṭṭhāpeti [upaṭṭhapeti (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)].

Then that elephant, having left the herd, approached the Blessed One, who was dwelling at Pālileyyaka, in the Rakkhita Grove, at the root of a beautiful sal tree. There that elephant kept the area where the Blessed One was dwelling clear of debris, and with his trunk provided the Blessed One with drinking water and water for use.

Atha kho bhagavato rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘‘ahaṃ kho pubbe ākiṇṇo vihāsiṃ bhikkhūhi bhikkhūnīhi upāsakehi upāsikāhi rājūhi rājamahāmattehi titthiyehi titthiyasāvakehi, ākiṇṇo dukkhaṃ na phāsu vihāsiṃ. Somhi etarahi anākiṇṇo viharāmi bhikkhūhi bhikkhunīhi upāsakehi upāsikāhi rājūhi rājamahāmattehi titthiyehi titthiyasāvakehi, anākiṇṇo sukhaṃ phāsu viharāmī’’ti.

Then, while the Blessed One was alone in seclusion, this thought arose in his mind: “Formerly, I dwelled amidst crowds of bhikkhus, nuns, laymen, laywomen, kings, royal ministers, sectarians, and their disciples. Amidst crowds, I dwelled uncomfortably and not at ease. But now I dwell uncrowded by bhikkhus, nuns, laymen, laywomen, kings, royal ministers, sectarians, and their disciples. Uncrowded, I dwell comfortably and at ease.”

Tassapi kho hatthināgassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘‘ahaṃ kho pubbe ākiṇṇo vihāsiṃ hatthīhi hatthinīhi hatthikalabhehi hatthicchāpehi, chinnaggāni ceva tiṇāni khādiṃ, obhaggobhaggañca me sākhābhaṅgaṃ khādiṃsu, āvilāni ca pānīyāni apāyiṃ, ogāhā ca me uttiṇṇassa hatthiniyo kāyaṃ upanighaṃsantiyo agamaṃsu, ākiṇṇo dukkhaṃ na phāsu vihāsiṃ. Somhi etarahi anākiṇṇo viharāmi hatthīhi hatthinīhi hatthikalabhehi hatthicchāpehi, acchinnaggāni ceva tiṇāni khādāmi, obhaggobhaggañca me sākhābhaṅgaṃ na khādanti, anāvilāni ca pānīyāni pivāmi, ogāhā ca me uttiṇṇassa hatthiniyo na kāyaṃ upanighaṃsantiyo gacchanti, anākiṇṇo sukhaṃ phāsu viharāmī’’ti.

Then this thought arose in that bull elephant: “Formerly, I dwelled amidst crowds of elephants, she-elephants, young elephants, and elephant calves. I ate grass with cropped tips, and they ate branches broken and bent for me. I drank turbid water, and when I emerged from the water, the she-elephants, rubbing against my body, went away. Amidst crowds, I dwelled uncomfortably and not at ease. But now I dwell uncrowded by elephants, she-elephants, young elephants, and elephant calves. I eat grass with uncropped tips, and they do not eat branches broken and bent for me. I drink clear water, and when I emerge from the water, the she-elephants do not rub against my body. Uncrowded, I dwell comfortably and at ease.”

Atha kho bhagavā attano ca pavivekaṃ viditvā tassa ca hatthināgassa cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then the Blessed One, knowing his own solitude and understanding with his mind the thought of that bull elephant, at that time uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Etaṃ [evaṃ (ka.)] nāgassa nāgena, īsādantassa hatthino;Sameti cittaṃ cittena, yadeko ramatī mano’’ti. pañcamaṃ;

“This is the elephant’s way with the elephant, the tusker’s with the tusker: mind meets mind when the mind delights in solitude.” Fifth.
