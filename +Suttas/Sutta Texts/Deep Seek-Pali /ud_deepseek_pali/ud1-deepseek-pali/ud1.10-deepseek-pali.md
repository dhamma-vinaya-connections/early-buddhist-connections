# Ud 1.10 Bāhiyasuttaṃ

## Ud 1.10 The Bāhiya Sutta

10. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena bāhiyo dārucīriyo suppārake paṭivasati samuddatīre sakkato garukato mānito pūjito apacito lābhī cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. Atha kho bāhiyassa dārucīriyassa rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘‘ye kho keci loke arahanto vā arahattamaggaṃ vā samāpannā, ahaṃ tesaṃ aññataro’’ti.

10. Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling in Sāvatthī, in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. At that time Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth was dwelling in Suppāraka, on the seacoast, honored, respected, revered, venerated, and worshipped, a recipient of robes, almsfood, dwellings, and medicinal requisites for the sick. Then, while Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth was secluded, gone into solitude, this thought arose in his mind: "Whatever Arahants there are in the world, or those who have entered upon the path to Arahantship, I am one of them."

Atha kho bāhiyassa dārucīriyassa purāṇasālohitā devatā anukampikā atthakāmā bāhiyassa dārucīriyassa cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya yena bāhiyo dārucīriyo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bāhiyaṃ dārucīriyaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘neva kho tvaṃ, bāhiya, arahā, nāpi arahattamaggaṃ vā samāpanno. Sāpi te paṭipadā natthi yāya tvaṃ arahā vā assa [assasi (syā. ka.)] arahattamaggaṃ vā samāpanno’’ti.

Then a deity, a former blood relative of Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth, compassionate and desiring his welfare, knowing with her mind the thought of Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth, approached Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth; having approached Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth, she said to him: "You, Bāhiya, are not an arahant, nor have you entered upon the path to arahantship. Even that practice is not yours by which you could be an arahant or enter upon the path to arahantship."

‘‘Atha ke carahi sadevake loke arahanto vā arahattamaggaṃ vā samāpanno’’ti? ‘‘Atthi, bāhiya, uttaresu janapadesu [janapade (sī.)] sāvatthi nāma nagaraṃ. Tattha so bhagavā etarahi viharati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho. So hi, bāhiya, bhagavā arahā ceva arahattāya ca dhammaṃ desetī’’ti.

"Then who, pray, in this world with its devas are Arahants or have entered upon the path to Arahantship?" "There is, Bāhiya, in the northern regions a city named Sāvatthī. There the Blessed One is now dwelling, an Arahant, a Fully Enlightened Buddha. He, Bāhiya, is indeed an Arahant and teaches the Dhamma for the attainment of Arahantship."

Atha kho bāhiyo dārucīriyo tāya devatāya saṃvejito tāvadeva suppārakamhā pakkāmi. Sabbattha ekarattiparivāsena yena sāvatthi jetavanaṃ anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāmo tenupasaṅkami. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū abbhokāse caṅkamanti. Atha kho bāhiyo dārucīriyo yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘kahaṃ nu kho, bhante, etarahi bhagavā viharati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho? Dassanakāmamhā mayaṃ taṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddha’’nti. ‘‘Antaragharaṃ paviṭṭho kho, bāhiya, bhagavā piṇḍāyā’’ti.

Then Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth, stirred by that deva, immediately left Suppāraka and, stopping for only a single night in each place, he approached Sāvatthī, Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. At that time, a number of bhikkhus were walking in the open air. Then Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth approached those bhikkhus and said to them: "Where, venerable sirs, is the Blessed One now dwelling, an arahant, a Fully Enlightened Buddha? We wish to see that Blessed One, an arahant, a Fully Enlightened Buddha." "The Blessed One has gone into the village for alms, Bāhiya."

Atha kho bāhiyo dārucīriyo taramānarūpo jetavanā nikkhamitvā sāvatthiṃ pavisitvā addasa bhagavantaṃ sāvatthiyaṃ piṇḍāya carantaṃ pāsādikaṃ pasādanīyaṃ santindriyaṃ santamānasaṃ uttamadamathasamathamanuppattaṃ dantaṃ guttaṃ yatindriyaṃ nāgaṃ. Disvāna yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato pāde sirasā nipatitvā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘desetu me, bhante bhagavā, dhammaṃ; desetu, sugato, dhammaṃ, yaṃ mamassa dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti. Evaṃ vutte, bhagavā bāhiyaṃ dārucīriyaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘akālo kho tāva, bāhiya, antaragharaṃ paviṭṭhamhā piṇḍāyā’’ti.

Then Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth, in a hurry, left Jeta's Grove, entered Sāvatthī, and saw the Blessed One walking for alms in Sāvatthī—charming, inspiring confidence, with senses calmed, mind calmed, attained to the ultimate taming and tranquility, tamed, guarded, with senses restrained, a nāga. Seeing him, he approached the Blessed One, prostrated at his feet, and said: "Teach me the Dhamma, Blessed One! Teach me the Dhamma, O Sublime One, that it may lead to my welfare and happiness for a long time!" When this was said, the Blessed One said to Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth: "It is not the right time, Bāhiya; we have entered the village for alms."

Dutiyampi kho bāhiyo dārucīriyo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘dujjānaṃ kho panetaṃ, bhante, bhagavato vā jīvitantarāyānaṃ, mayhaṃ vā jīvitantarāyānaṃ. Desetu me, bhante bhagavā, dhammaṃ; desetu, sugato, dhammaṃ, yaṃ mamassa dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti. Dutiyampi kho bhagavā bāhiyaṃ dārucīriyaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘akālo kho tāva, bāhiya, antaragharaṃ paviṭṭhamhā piṇḍāyā’’ti.

A second time Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth said to the Blessed One: "For it is hard to know, venerable sir, the dangers to the Blessed One's life, or to my life. Teach me the Dhamma, Blessed One! Teach me the Dhamma, O Sublime One, that it may lead to my welfare and happiness for a long time!" A second time the Blessed One said to Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth: "It is not the right time, Bāhiya; we have entered the village for alms."

Tatiyampi kho bāhiyo dārucīriyo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘dujjānaṃ kho panetaṃ, bhante, bhagavato vā jīvitantarāyānaṃ, mayhaṃ vā jīvitantarāyānaṃ. Desetu me bhante bhagavā, dhammaṃ; desetu, sugato, dhammaṃ, yaṃ mamassa dīgharattaṃ hitāya sukhāyā’’ti.

A third time Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth said to the Blessed One: "For it is hard to know, venerable sir, the dangers to the Blessed One's life, or to my life. Teach me the Dhamma, Blessed One! Teach me the Dhamma, O Sublime One, that it may lead to my welfare and happiness for a long time!"

‘‘Tasmātiha te, bāhiya, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘diṭṭhe diṭṭhamattaṃ bhavissati, sute sutamattaṃ bhavissati, mute mutamattaṃ bhavissati, viññāte viññātamattaṃ bhavissatī’ti. Evañhi te, bāhiya, sikkhitabbaṃ. Yato kho te, bāhiya, diṭṭhe diṭṭhamattaṃ bhavissati, sute sutamattaṃ bhavissati, mute mutamattaṃ bhavissati, viññāte viññātamattaṃ bhavissati, tato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tena; yato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tena tato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tattha; yato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tattha, tato tvaṃ, bāhiya, nevidha na huraṃ na ubhayamantarena. Esevanto dukkhassā’’ti.

"Therefore, Bahiya, you should train yourself thus: ‘In the seen, there will be merely the seen; in the heard, merely the heard; in the discerned, merely the discerned; in the cognized, merely the cognized.’ That is how you should train yourself, Bahiya. When, Bahiya, for you in the seen there will be merely the seen, in the heard merely the heard, in the discerned merely the discerned, in the cognized merely the cognized, then, Bahiya, you will not be ‘by means of that.’ When, Bahiya, you are not ‘by means of that,’ then, Bahiya, you will not be ‘in that.’ When, Bahiya, you are not ‘in that,’ then, Bahiya, you will be neither here nor beyond nor in between the two. This itself is the end of suffering."

Atha kho bāhiyassa dārucīriyassa bhagavato imāya saṃkhittāya dhammadesanāya tāvadeva anupādāya āsavehi cittaṃ vimucci.

Then, by this brief Dhamma teaching of the Blessed One, Bāhiya of the Bark-cloth's mind was immediately liberated from the taints, without clinging.

Atha kho bhagavā bāhiyaṃ dārucīriyaṃ iminā saṃkhittena ovādena ovaditvā pakkāmi. Atha kho acirapakkantassa bhagavato bāhiyaṃ dārucīriyaṃ gāvī taruṇavacchā adhipatitvā [adhipātetvā (sī. syā. pī.), adhipātitvā (ka.)] jīvitā voropesi.

Then the Blessed One, having instructed Bāhiya Dārucīriya with this brief instruction, departed. Then, not long after the Blessed One had left, a cow with a young calf struck down Bāhiya Dārucīriya and deprived him of life.

Atha kho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto sambahulehi bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ nagaramhā nikkhamitvā addasa bāhiyaṃ dārucīriyaṃ kālaṅkataṃ [kālakataṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ.)]; disvāna bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘gaṇhatha, bhikkhave, bāhiyassa dārucīriyassa sarīrakaṃ; mañcakaṃ āropetvā nīharitvā jhāpetha; thūpañcassa karotha. Sabrahmacārī vo, bhikkhave, kālaṅkato’’ti.

Then the Blessed One, having walked for alms in Savatthi and returning from the almsround after the meal, left the city together with many bhikkhus and saw Bāhiya Dārucīriya who had died. Seeing him, the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: "Take, bhikkhus, Bāhiya Dārucīriya's body; place it on a litter, carry it away, and cremate it. Then build a stupa for him. Your fellow monk, bhikkhus, has died."

‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paṭissutvā bāhiyassa dārucīriyassa sarīrakaṃ mañcakaṃ āropetvā nīharitvā jhāpetvā thūpañcassa katvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘daḍḍhaṃ, bhante, bāhiyassa dārucīriyassa sarīraṃ, thūpo cassa kato. Tassa kā gati, ko abhisamparāyo’’ti? ‘‘Paṇḍito, bhikkhave, bāhiyo dārucīriyo paccapādi dhammassānudhammaṃ; na ca maṃ dhammādhikaraṇaṃ vihesesi. Parinibbuto, bhikkhave, bāhiyo dārucīriyo’’ti.

"Even so, Venerable Sir," those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. Then, having placed Bāhiya Dārucīriya's body on a bier, carried it out, cremated it, and built a stupa for him, they approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those bhikkhus said to the Blessed One: "Venerable Sir, Bāhiya Dārucīriya's body has been cremated, and a stupa has been built for him. What is his destination? What is his future state?" "bhikkhus, Bāhiya Dārucīriya was wise. He practiced in accordance with the Dhamma and did not trouble me with questions about the Dhamma. bhikkhus, Bāhiya Dārucīriya has attained final Nibbāna."

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, the Blessed One, understanding this meaning, on that occasion uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Yattha āpo ca pathavī, tejo vāyo na gādhati;Na tattha sukkā jotanti, ādicco nappakāsati;Na tattha candimā bhāti, tamo tattha na vijjati.

Where water, earth, fire, and air
Have no footing,
There the stars do not shine,
The sun does not appear,
The moon does not gleam,
And darkness is not found.

‘‘Yadā ca attanāvedi [vedhī (ka.)], muni monena brāhmaṇo;Atha rūpā arūpā ca, sukhadukkhā pamuccatī’’ti. dasamaṃ;

When the sage, the brahmin, knows this for himself through sagehood, then he is freed from form and the formless, from happiness and suffering.

(Ayampi udāno vutto bhagavatā iti me sutanti.) [( ) syāmapotthake natthi]

This too was said by the Blessed One—so I heard.

Bodhivaggo paṭhamo niṭṭhito.

The First Chapter on Awakening is Finished.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Tayo bodhi ca huṃhuṅko [tayo ca bodhi nigrodho (sabbattha)], brāhmaṇo [te therā (sī. syā. pī.), thero (ka.)] kassapena ca;Aja [pāvāya (sī. syā.), pāṭaliyaṃ (pī.), pāvā (ka.)] saṅgāma jaṭilā, bāhiyenāti te dasāti.

Three insights, the grunting sound, the brahmin, and with Kassapa; The goat, the battle, the matted-hair ascetics, and Bāhiya—these are the ten.
