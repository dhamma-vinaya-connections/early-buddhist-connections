# Ud 7.10 Utenasuttaṃ

## Ud 7.10 The Utena Sutta

70. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tena kho pana samayena rañño utenassa [udenassa (sī. syā. pī.)] uyyānagatassa antepuraṃ daḍḍhaṃ hoti, pañca ca itthisatāni [pañca itthisatāni (sī. syā. pī.)] kālaṅkatāni honti sāmāvatīpamukhāni.

70.Thus have I heard – At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Kosambī, in Ghosita's Monastery. Now at that time, while King Udena was away in the pleasure grove, his inner palace had burned down and five hundred women, headed by Sāmāvatī, had died.

Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya kosambiṃ piṇḍāya pāvisiṃsu. Kosambiyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkantā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ – ‘‘idha, bhante, rañño utenassa uyyānagatassa antepuraṃ daḍḍhaṃ, pañca ca itthisatāni kālaṅkatāni sāmāvatīpamukhāni. Tāsaṃ, bhante, upāsikānaṃ kā gati ko abhisamparāyo’’ti?

Then, in the morning, a number of bhikkhus dressed, took their bowls and robes, and entered Kosambī for alms. After their meal, on their return from the almsround, they approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. While seated to one side, those bhikkhus said to the Blessed One: “Here, venerable sir, while King Udena was away in the pleasure grove, his inner palace burned down and five hundred women, headed by Sāmāvatī, had died. What, venerable sir, is the destination of those female lay followers? What is their future state?”

‘‘Santettha, bhikkhave, upāsikāyo sotāpannā, santi sakadāgāminiyo, santi anāgāminiyo. Sabbā tā, bhikkhave, upāsikāyo anipphalā kālaṅkatā’’ti.

“bhikkhus, among them there are female lay followers who are stream-enterers, some who are once-returners, and some who are non-returners. All those female lay followers, bhikkhus, have not died without result.”

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, having understood this meaning, on that occasion the Blessed One uttered this inspired utterance:

‘‘Mohasambandhano loko, bhabbarūpova dissati;Upadhibandhano [upadhisambandhano (ka. sī.)] bālo, tamasā parivārito;Sassatoriva [sassati viya (ka. sī.)] khāyati, passato natthi kiñcana’’nti. dasamaṃ;

“The world is bound by delusion, it appears to be substantial. The fool is bound by acquisitions, enveloped in darkness. He appears eternal, but for one who sees, there is nothing at all.” The tenth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The summary of this division is:

Dve bhaddiyā dve ca sattā, lakuṇḍako taṇhākhayo;Papañcakhayo ca kaccāno, udapānañca utenoti.

Two Bhaddiyas, two Sattas, Lakuṇḍaka, Taṇhākhaya, Papañcakhaya, Kaccāna, and Udena.

Cūḷavaggo [cullavaggo (sī.), cūlavaggo (pī.)] sattamo niṭṭhito.

The Minor Division, the Seventh, is concluded.
