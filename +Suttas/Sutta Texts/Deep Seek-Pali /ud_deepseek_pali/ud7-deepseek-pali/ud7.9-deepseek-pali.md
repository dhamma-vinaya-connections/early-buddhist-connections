# Ud 7.9 Udapānasuttaṃ

## Ud 7.9 The Well Discourse

69. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā mallesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ yena thūṇaṃ [thūnaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)] nāma mallānaṃ brāhmaṇagāmo tadavasari. Assosuṃ kho thūṇeyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā – ‘‘samaṇo khalu, bho, gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito mallesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ thūṇaṃ anuppatto’’ti.( ) [(atha kho te thūṇeyyakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā) (?)] Udapānaṃ tiṇassa ca bhusassa ca yāva mukhato pūresuṃ – ‘‘mā te muṇḍakā samaṇakā pānīyaṃ apaṃsū’’ti.

69.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One, traveling among the Mallas with a large company of bhikkhus, arrived at Thūṇa, a brahmin village of the Mallas. The brahmin householders of Thūṇa heard: "Indeed, friends, the ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, gone forth from the Sakyan clan, is traveling among the Mallas with a large company of bhikkhus and has arrived at Thūṇa." They filled the well right up to the mouth with grass and chaff, thinking: "Let these shaven-headed ascetics not drink any water."

Atha kho bhagavā maggā okkamma yena rukkhamūlaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘iṅgha me tvaṃ, ānanda, etamhā udapānā pānīyaṃ āharā’’ti.

Then the Blessed One stepped off the road and went to the foot of a tree. Having approached, he sat down on the prepared seat. Sitting, the Blessed One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Come, Ānanda, bring me some drinking water from this well."

Evaṃ vutte, āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘idāni so, bhante, udapāno thūṇeyyakehi brāhmaṇagahapatikehi tiṇassa ca bhusassa ca yāva mukhato pūrito – ‘mā te muṇḍakā samaṇakā pānīyaṃ apaṃsū’’’ti.

When this was said, the Venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One: "Just now, Venerable Sir, that well was filled by the brahmin householders of Thūṇa with grass and chaff right up to the mouth, [saying,] ‘Don’t let those shaven ascetics drink the water!’"

Dutiyampi kho…pe… tatiyampi kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘iṅgha me tvaṃ, ānanda, etamhā udapānā pānīyaṃ āharā’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato paṭissutvā pattaṃ gahetvā yena so udapāno tenupasaṅkami. Atha kho so udapāno āyasmante ānande upasaṅkamante sabbaṃ taṃ tiṇañca bhusañca mukhato ovamitvā acchassa udakassa anāvilassa vippasannassa yāva mukhato pūrito vissandanto [vissando (ka.)] maññe aṭṭhāsi.

A second time... a third time the Blessed One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Come, Ānanda, fetch me drinking water from that well." "Yes, Venerable Sir," replied the Venerable Ānanda, and taking his bowl, he approached the well. Then, as the Venerable Ānanda approached, that well spewed out all the grass and chaff from its mouth, and it seemed to stand filled up to the mouth with clear, unclouded, perfectly clear water, overflowing.

Atha kho āyasmato ānandassa etadahosi – ‘‘acchariyaṃ vata, bho, abbhutaṃ vata, bho, tathāgatassa mahiddhikatā mahānubhāvatā! Ayañhi so udapāno mayi upasaṅkamante sabbaṃ taṃ tiṇañca bhusañca mukhato ovamitvā acchassa udakassa anāvilassa vippasannassa yāva mukhato pūrito vissandanto maññe ṭhito’’ti!! Pattena pānīyaṃ ādāya yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘acchariyaṃ, bhante, abbhutaṃ, bhante, tathāgatassa mahiddhikatā mahānubhāvatā! Ayañhi so, bhante, udapāno mayi upasaṅkamante sabbaṃ taṃ tiṇañca bhusañca mukhato ovamitvā acchassa udakassa anāvilassa vippasannassa yāva mukhato pūrito vissandanto maññe aṭṭhāsi!! Pivatu bhagavā pānīyaṃ, pivatu sugato pānīya’’nti.

Then the Venerable Ānanda thought: "How astonishing, friends, how marvelous, friends, is the Tathāgata's great power and great might! For as I approached, this well, having expelled all that grass and chaff from its mouth, seemed to stand filled up to the mouth with clear, unclouded, perfectly clear water, overflowing!" Taking drinking water in a bowl, he approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he said to the Blessed One: "How astonishing, Venerable Sir, how marvelous, Venerable Sir, is the Tathāgata's great power and great might! For as I approached, Venerable Sir, this well, having expelled all that grass and chaff from its mouth, seemed to stand filled up to the mouth with clear, unclouded, perfectly clear water, overflowing! May the Blessed One drink the water, may the Fortunate One drink the water!"

Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṃ viditvā tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imaṃ udānaṃ udānesi –

Then, having understood this matter, the Blessed One on that occasion uttered this solemn utterance:

‘‘Kiṃ kayirā udapānena,Āpā ce sabbadā siyuṃ;Taṇhāya mūlato chetvā,Kissa pariyesanaṃ care’’ti. navamaṃ;

"What use is a well, if water is always available? Having cut off craving at the root, for what should one wander?" The ninth.
