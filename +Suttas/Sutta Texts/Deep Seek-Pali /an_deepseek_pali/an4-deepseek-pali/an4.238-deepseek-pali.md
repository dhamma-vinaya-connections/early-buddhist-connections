# AN 4.238 Bojjhaṅgasuttaṃ

## AN 4.238 The Factors of Enlightenment Discourse

238. ‘‘Cattārimāni, bhikkhave, kammāni…pe… kaṇhaṃ kaṇhavipākaṃ…pe… idha, bhikkhave, ekacco sabyābajjhaṃ kāyasaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkharoti…pe… idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhaṃ kaṇhavipākaṃ.

238.“Monks, there are these four kinds of kamma... dark kamma with dark result... Here, monks, someone generates an oppressive bodily formation... This is called, monks, dark kamma with dark result.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, kammaṃ sukkaṃ sukkavipākaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco abyābajjhaṃ kāyasaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkharoti…pe… idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ sukkaṃ sukkavipākaṃ.

“And what, monks, is bright kamma with bright result? Here, monks, someone generates a non-oppressive bodily formation... This is called, monks, bright kamma with bright result.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhasukkaṃ kaṇhasukkavipākaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco sabyābajjhampi abyābajjhampi kāyasaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkharoti…pe… idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhasukkaṃ kaṇhasukkavipākaṃ.

“And what, monks, is dark-and-bright kamma with dark-and-bright result? It's when someone generates a bodily formation that is both oppressive and non-oppressive... This is called dark-and-bright kamma with dark-and-bright result.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, kammaṃ akaṇhaasukkaṃ akaṇhaasukkavipākaṃ kammakkhayāya saṃvattati? Satisambojjhaṅgo, dhammavicayasambojjhaṅgo, vīriyasambojjhaṅgo, pītisambojjhaṅgo, passaddhisambojjhaṅgo, samādhisambojjhaṅgo, upekkhāsambojjhaṅgo – idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ akaṇhaasukkaṃ akaṇhaasukkavipākaṃ kammakkhayāya saṃvattati. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, cattāri kammāni mayā sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā paveditānī’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

“And what, monks, is kamma that is neither dark nor bright with neither-dark-nor-bright result, leading to the destruction of kamma? The enlightenment factor of mindfulness, the enlightenment factor of investigation of phenomena, the enlightenment factor of energy, the enlightenment factor of rapture, the enlightenment factor of tranquility, the enlightenment factor of samādhi, the enlightenment factor of equanimity—this is called, monks, kamma that is neither dark nor bright with neither-dark-nor-bright result, leading to the destruction of kamma. These, monks, are the four kinds of kamma declared by me after realizing them for myself with direct knowledge.” Seventh.
