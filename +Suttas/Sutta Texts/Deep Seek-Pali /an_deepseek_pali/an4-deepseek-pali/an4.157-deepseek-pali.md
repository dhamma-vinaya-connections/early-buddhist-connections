"Monks, there are these four diseases of one gone forth. What four? Here, monks, a monk is of great desires, vexed, and discontented with whatever robes, almsfood, lodging, or medicinal requisites he receives. "Therefore, monks, you should train yourselves thus: 'We shall not be of great desires, vexed, discontented with whatever robes, almsfood, lodging, or medicinal requisites. We shall not direct evil desires for the sake of gaining non-disparagement, gain, honor, and fame. We shall not rouse ourselves, strive, or exert ourselves for the sake of gaining non-disparagement, gain, honor, and fame. We shall be patient with cold, heat, hunger, thirst, with the touch of flies, mosquitoes, wind, sun, and reptiles, with harsh and unwelcome speech, with bodily feelings that are painful, sharp, severe, acute, unpleasant, disagreeable, and life-threatening; we shall be of a nature to endure them.'
