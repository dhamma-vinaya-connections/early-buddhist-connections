# AN 4.237 Ariyamaggasuttaṃ

## AN 4.237 The Noble Path Discourse

237. ‘‘Cattārimāni, bhikkhave, kammāni mayā sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā paveditāni. Katamāni cattāri? Atthi, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhaṃ kaṇhavipākaṃ; atthi, bhikkhave, kammaṃ sukkaṃ sukkavipākaṃ; atthi, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhasukkaṃ kaṇhasukkavipākaṃ; atthi, bhikkhave, kammaṃ akaṇhaasukkaṃ akaṇhaasukkavipākaṃ kammakkhayāya saṃvattati.

237.“Monks, these four kinds of kamma have been declared by me after realizing them myself with direct knowledge. What four? There is, monks, dark kamma with dark result; there is, monks, bright kamma with bright result; there is, monks, dark-and-bright kamma with dark-and-bright result; there is, monks, kamma that is neither dark nor bright with neither-dark-nor-bright result, which leads to the destruction of kamma.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhaṃ kaṇhavipākaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco sabyābajjhaṃ kāyasaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkharoti…pe… idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhaṃ kaṇhavipākaṃ.

“And what, monks, is dark kamma with dark result? Here, monks, someone generates an oppressive bodily formation… This is called, monks, dark kamma with dark result.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, kammaṃ sukkaṃ sukkavipākaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco abyābajjhaṃ kāyasaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkharoti…pe… idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ sukkaṃ sukkavipākaṃ.

“And what, monks, is bright kamma with bright result? Here, monks, a certain person generates a non-oppressive bodily formation… This is called, monks, bright kamma with bright result.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhasukkaṃ kaṇhasukkavipākaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco sabyābajjhampi abyābajjhampi kāyasaṅkhāraṃ abhisaṅkharoti…pe… idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ kaṇhasukkaṃ kaṇhasukkavipākaṃ.

“And what, monks, is dark-and-bright kamma with dark-and-bright result? Here, monks, someone generates an oppressive as well as a non-oppressive bodily formation... This is called, monks, dark-and-bright kamma with dark-and-bright result.”

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, kammaṃ akaṇhaasukkaṃ akaṇhaasukkavipākaṃ kammakkhayāya saṃvattati? Sammādiṭṭhi…pe… sammāsamādhi. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, kammaṃ akaṇhaasukkaṃ akaṇhaasukkavipākaṃ kammakkhayāya saṃvattati. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, cattāri kammāni mayā sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā paveditānī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“And what, monks, is kamma that is neither dark nor bright with neither-dark-nor-bright result, leading to the destruction of kamma? Right view ... Right samādhi. This is called, monks, kamma that is neither dark nor bright with neither-dark-nor-bright result, leading to the destruction of kamma. These, monks, are the four kinds of kamma declared by me after realizing them for myself with direct knowledge.” The sixth.
