# AN 4.50 Upakkilesasuttaṃ

## AN 4.50 The Discourse on Corruptions

50. ‘‘Cattārome, bhikkhave [cūḷava. 447], candimasūriyānaṃ upakkilesā, yehi upakkilesehi upakkiliṭṭhā candimasūriyā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti. Katame cattāro? Abbhā, bhikkhave, candimasūriyānaṃ upakkilesā, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā candimasūriyā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti.

50.“Monks, there are these four corruptions of the moon and sun, by which corruptions the moon and sun, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate. What four? Clouds, monks, are a corruption of the moon and sun, by which corruption the moon and sun, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.

‘‘Mahikā, bhikkhave, candimasūriyānaṃ upakkilesā, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā candimasūriyā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti.

“Monks, mist is a corruption of the moon and sun, by which corruption the moon and sun, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.

‘‘Dhūmo rajo, bhikkhave, candimasūriyānaṃ upakkileso, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā candimasūriyā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti.

“Monks, smoke and dust are a corruption of the moon and sun, by which corruption the moon and sun, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.

‘‘Rāhu, bhikkhave, asurindo candimasūriyānaṃ upakkileso, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā candimasūriyā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cattāro candimasūriyānaṃ upakkilesā, yehi upakkilesehi upakkiliṭṭhā candimasūriyā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti.

“Monks, Rāhu, the lord of the asuras, is a corruption of the moon and sun, by which corruption the moon and sun, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate. These, monks, are the four corruptions of the moon and sun, by which corruptions the moon and sun, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.

‘‘Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, cattārome samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ upakkilesā, yehi upakkilesehi upakkiliṭṭhā eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti. Katame cattāro? Santi, bhikkhave, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā suraṃ pivanti merayaṃ, surāmerayapānā appaṭiviratā. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, paṭhamo samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ upakkileso, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti.

“Just so, monks, there are these four corruptions of ascetics and brahmins, by which corruptions some ascetics and brahmins, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate. What four? There are, monks, some ascetics and brahmins who drink liquor and intoxicants, not abstaining from the drinking of liquor and intoxicants. This, monks, is the first corruption of ascetics and brahmins, by which corruption some ascetics and brahmins, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā methunaṃ dhammaṃ paṭisevanti, methunasmā dhammā appaṭiviratā. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dutiyo samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ upakkileso, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti.

“Monks, there are some ascetics and brahmins who engage in sexual intercourse, not abstaining from sexual intercourse. This, monks, is the second corruption of ascetics and brahmins, by which corruption some ascetics and brahmins, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā jātarūparajataṃ sādiyanti, jātarūparajatapaṭiggahaṇā appaṭiviratā. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, tatiyo samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ upakkileso, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti.

“Monks, there are some ascetics and brahmins who accept gold and silver, not abstaining from accepting gold and silver. This, monks, is the third corruption of ascetics and brahmins, by which corruption some ascetics and brahmins, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.

‘‘Santi, bhikkhave, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā micchājīvena jīvanti, micchājīvā appaṭiviratā. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, catuttho samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ upakkileso, yena upakkilesena upakkiliṭṭhā eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocanti. Ime kho, bhikkhave, cattāro samaṇabrāhmaṇānaṃ upakkilesā, yehi upakkilesehi upakkiliṭṭhā eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā na tapanti na bhāsanti na virocantī’’ti.

“Monks, there are some ascetics and brahmins who live by wrong livelihood, not abstaining from wrong livelihood. This, monks, is the fourth corruption of ascetics and brahmins, by which corruption some ascetics and brahmins, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate. These, monks, are the four corruptions of ascetics and brahmins, by which corruptions some ascetics and brahmins, being corrupted, do not give light, do not shine forth, and do not radiate.”

‘‘Rāgadosaparikkiṭṭhā, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā;Avijjānivutā posā, piyarūpābhinandino.

“Some ascetics and brahmins are beset by lust and hatred, people enveloped in ignorance, delighting in pleasing forms.

‘‘Suraṃ pivanti merayaṃ, paṭisevanti methunaṃ;Rajataṃ jātarūpañca, sādiyanti aviddasū;Micchājīvena jīvanti, eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā.

They drink liquor and intoxicants, indulge in sexual intercourse; they accept gold and silver, those unwise ones; some ascetics and brahmins live by wrong livelihood.

‘‘Ete upakkilesā vuttā, buddhenādiccabandhunā;Yehi upakkilesehi [upakkiliṭṭhā (sī. pī.)], eke samaṇabrāhmaṇā;Na tapanti na bhāsanti, asuddhā sarajā magā.

These corruptions were declared by the Buddha, kinsman of the Sun, by which corruptions certain ascetics and brahmins do not give light and do not shine forth, impure, dusty, deluded.

‘‘Andhakārena onaddhā, taṇhādāsā sanettikā;Vaḍḍhenti kaṭasiṃ ghoraṃ, ādiyanti punabbhava’’nti. dasamaṃ;

Enveloped in darkness, slaves to craving, though having eyes, they increase the ghastly frame and grasp at renewed existence.”

Rohitassavaggo pañcamo.

The Fifth Chapter: The Rohitassa Section.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The Summary:

Samādhipañhā dve kodhā, rohitassāpare duve;Suvidūravisākhavipallāsā, upakkilesena te dasāti.

Two on questions about samādhi, two on anger; the other two on Rohitassa; Suvidūra, Visākha, and Vipallāsa; with the corruptions, these are ten.

Paṭhamapaṇṇāsakaṃ samattaṃ.
