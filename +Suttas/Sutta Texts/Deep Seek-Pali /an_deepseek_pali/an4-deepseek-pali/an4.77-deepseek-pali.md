77."Monks, there are these four things that are unconjecturable, not to be conjectured about; anyone who conjectures about them would partake of madness and vexation. What are the four? The range of the Buddhas is unconjecturable, not to be conjectured about; anyone who conjectures about it would partake of madness and vexation. The range of one in jhÄna is unconjecturable, not to be conjectured about; anyone who conjectures about it would partake of madness and vexation. The result of deeds is unconjecturable, not to be conjectured about; anyone who conjectures about it would partake of madness and vexation. Thinking about the world is unconjecturable, not to be conjectured about; anyone who conjectures about it would partake of madness and vexation. These, monks, are the four things that are unconjecturable, not to be conjectured about; anyone who conjectures about them would partake of madness and vexation." The seventh.
