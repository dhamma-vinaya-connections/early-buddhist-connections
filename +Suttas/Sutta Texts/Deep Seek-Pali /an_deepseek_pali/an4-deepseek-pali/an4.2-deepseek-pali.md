# AN 4.2 Papatitasuttaṃ

## AN 4.2 The Discourse on Falling Away

2. ‘‘Catūhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi asamannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā papatito’ti vuccati. Katamehi catūhi? Ariyena, bhikkhave, sīlena asamannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā papatito’ti vuccati. Ariyena, bhikkhave, samādhinā asamannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā papatito’ti vuccati. Ariyāya, bhikkhave, paññāya asamannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā papatito’ti vuccati. Ariyāya, bhikkhave, vimuttiyā asamannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā papatito’ti vuccati. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, catūhi dhammehi asamannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā papatito’ti vuccati.

2. Monks, one who is not endowed with four qualities is said to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Which four? Monks, one who is not endowed with noble virtue is said to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is not endowed with noble samādhi is said to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is not endowed with noble wisdom is said to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is not endowed with noble liberation is said to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is not endowed with these four qualities is said to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline.

‘‘Catūhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā apapatito’ti [appapatitoti (ka.)] vuccati. Katamehi catūhi? Ariyena, bhikkhave, sīlena samannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā apapatito’ti vuccati. Ariyena, bhikkhave, samādhinā samannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā apapatito’ti vuccati. Ariyāya, bhikkhave, paññāya samannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā apapatito’ti vuccati. Ariyāya, bhikkhave, vimuttiyā samannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā apapatito’ti vuccati. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, catūhi dhammehi samannāgato ‘imasmā dhammavinayā apapatito’ti vuccatī’’ti.

Monks, one who is endowed with four qualities is said not to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Which four? Monks, one who is endowed with noble virtue is said not to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is endowed with noble samādhi is said not to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is endowed with noble wisdom is said not to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is endowed with noble liberation is said not to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline. Monks, one who is endowed with these four qualities is said not to have fallen away from this Dhamma and Discipline.

‘‘Cutā patanti patitā, giddhā ca punarāgatā;Kataṃ kiccaṃ rataṃ rammaṃ, sukhenānvāgataṃ sukha’’nti. dutiyaṃ;

Fallen, they fall; and being greedy, they return again.
The task is accomplished, delighting in the delightful, happiness accompanied by happiness.
