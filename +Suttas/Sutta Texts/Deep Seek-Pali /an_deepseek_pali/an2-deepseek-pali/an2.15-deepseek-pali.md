"And how, monks, does a monk under accusation properly examine himself by himself? Here, monks, a monk under accusation reflects thus: 'I have committed a certain offense through body. And having seen me committing a certain offense through body, that monk was displeased. Being displeased, that monk spoke to me with displeased words. Being spoken to with displeased words by that monk, I became displeased. Being displeased, I told others. Thus, the fault there has redounded to me alone, just as it redounds to one liable for customs duty on goods.' In this way, monks, a monk under accusation properly examines himself by himself."
