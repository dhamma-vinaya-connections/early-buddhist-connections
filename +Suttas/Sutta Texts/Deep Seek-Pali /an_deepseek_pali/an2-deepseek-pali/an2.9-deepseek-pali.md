9. "Monks, there are these two bright principles that protect the world. What two? Conscience and prudence. If, monks, these two bright principles did not protect the world, there would be no recognition of 'mother,' 'mother's sister,' 'aunt,' 'teacher's wife,' or 'wives of respected ones.' The world would fall into confusion, like goats, sheep, chickens, pigs, dogs, and jackals. But because, monks, these two bright principles protect the world, there is recognition of 'mother,' 'mother's sister,' 'aunt,' 'teacher's wife,' and 'wives of respected ones.'" The ninth.
