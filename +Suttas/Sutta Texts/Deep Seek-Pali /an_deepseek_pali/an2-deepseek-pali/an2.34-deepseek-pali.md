If one were to carry one's mother on one shoulder and one's father on the other shoulder, and if, living for a hundred years, with a hundred-year lifespan, one were to care for them by anointing, massaging, bathing, and rubbing their limbs, and even if they were to relieve themselves right thereâ€”still, monks, one would not have done enough for one's parents, nor repaid them.
