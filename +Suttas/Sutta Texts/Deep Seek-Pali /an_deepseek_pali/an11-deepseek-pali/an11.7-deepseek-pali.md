# AN 11.7 Saññāsuttaṃ

## AN 11.7 The Discourse on Perception

7. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

7. Then the Venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One; having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. As he sat to one side, the Venerable Ānanda said this to the Blessed One:

‘‘Siyā nu kho, bhante, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa, yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa; saññī ca pana assāti?

"Venerable sir, could there be for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth, nor percipient of water in water, nor percipient of fire in fire, nor percipient of air in air, nor percipient of the base of infinite space in the base of infinite space, nor percipient of the base of infinite consciousness in the base of infinite consciousness, nor percipient of the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, nor percipient of the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, nor percipient of this world in this world, nor percipient of the other world in the other world, and further, that he would not be percipient in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, and yet he would be percipient?"

‘‘Siyā, ānanda, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa, yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti.

There could be, Ānanda, for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth, nor percipient of water in water, nor percipient of fire in fire, nor percipient of air in air; nor percipient of the base of infinite space in the base of infinite space, nor percipient of the base of infinite consciousness in the base of infinite consciousness, nor percipient of the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, nor percipient of the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; nor percipient of this world in this world, nor percipient of the other world in the other world; and further, that he would not be percipient in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, yet he would still be percipient.

‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, bhante, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa, yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa, saññī ca pana assāti.

"But how, venerable sir, could there be for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth, nor percipient of water in water, nor percipient of fire in fire, nor percipient of air in air; nor percipient of the base of infinite space in the base of infinite space, nor percipient of the base of infinite consciousness in the base of infinite consciousness, nor percipient of the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, nor percipient of the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; nor percipient of this world in this world, nor percipient of the other world in the other world; and further, he would not be percipient even in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, yet he would still be percipient?"

‘‘Idhānanda, bhikkhu evaṃsaññī hoti – ‘etaṃ santaṃ etaṃ paṇītaṃ, yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbāna’nti. Evaṃ kho, ānanda, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa, yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa, saññī ca pana assā’’ti.

"Here, Ānanda, a bhikkhu has this perception: 'This is peaceful, this is sublime, namely the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all acquisitions, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna.' Thus, Ānanda, there could be for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth, nor percipient of water in water, nor percipient of fire in fire, nor percipient of air in air; nor percipient of the base of infinite space in the base of infinite space, nor percipient of the base of infinite consciousness in the base of infinite consciousness, nor percipient of the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, nor percipient of the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; nor percipient of this world in this world, nor percipient of the other world in the other world; and further, he would not be percipient even in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, yet he would still be percipient.”

Atha kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā sāriputtena saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca –

Then Venerable Ānanda, having rejoiced in and approved of the Blessed One’s words, rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and approached Venerable Sāriputta. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with Venerable Sāriputta. Having exchanged courteous and amiable greetings, he sat down to one side. Seated to one side, Venerable Ānanda said to Venerable Sāriputta:

‘‘Siyā nu kho, āvuso sāriputta, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa…pe… yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa, saññī pana assāti. ‘‘Siyā, āvuso ānanda, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa…pe… yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa, saññī ca pana assā’’ti.

"Friend Sāriputta, could there be for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth... and further, he would not be percipient even in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, yet he would still be percipient?"
"There could be, friend Ānanda, for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth... and further, he would not be percipient even in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, yet he would still be percipient."

‘‘Yathā kathaṃ panāvuso sāriputta, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa…pe… yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa, saññī ca pana assā’’ti?

"But how, friend Sāriputta, could there be for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth... and further, he would not be percipient even in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, yet he would still be percipient?"

‘‘Idha, āvuso ānanda, bhikkhu evaṃsaññī hoti – ‘etaṃ santaṃ etaṃ paṇītaṃ, yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbāna’nti. Evaṃ kho, āvuso ānanda, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa…pe… yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā, tatrāpi na saññī assa, saññī ca pana assā’’ti.

Here, friend Ānanda, a bhikkhu is thus percipient: 'This is peaceful, this is sublime—namely, the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all acquisitions, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna.' In this way, friend Ānanda, there could be for a bhikkhu such an attainment of samādhi that he would be neither percipient of earth in earth... and further, he would not be percipient even in what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought after, and pondered over by the mind, yet he would still be percipient.

‘‘Acchariyaṃ, āvuso, abbhutaṃ, āvuso! Yatra hi nāma satthu ceva sāvakassa ca atthena attho byañjanena byañjanaṃ saṃsandissati samessati na viggayhissati, yadidaṃ aggapadasmiṃ! Idānāhaṃ, āvuso, bhagavantaṃ upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ apucchiṃ. Bhagavāpi me etehi akkharehi etehi padehi etehi byañjanehi etamatthaṃ byākāsi, seyyathāpi āyasmā sāriputto. Acchariyaṃ, āvuso, abbhutaṃ, āvuso, yatra hi nāma satthu ceva sāvakassa ca atthena attho byañjanena byañjanaṃ saṃsandissati samessati na viggayhissati, yadidaṃ aggapadasmi’’nti! Sattamaṃ.

"Marvelous, friend, astonishing, friend! How the meaning and the phrasing of both the Teacher and the disciple coincide and agree without conflict—that is, in the highest matter! Just now, friend, I approached the Blessed One and asked him about this matter. The Blessed One explained this matter to me with these very letters, these very words, these very phrases, just as the Venerable Sāriputta has done. Marvelous, friend, astonishing, friend! How the meaning and the phrasing of both the Teacher and the disciple coincide and agree without conflict—that is, in the highest matter!" (Seventh)
