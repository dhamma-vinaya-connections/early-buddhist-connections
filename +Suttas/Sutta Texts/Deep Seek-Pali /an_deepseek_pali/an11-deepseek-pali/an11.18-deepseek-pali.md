# AN 11.18 Paṭhamasamādhisuttaṃ

## AN 11.18 The First Discourse on samādhi

18. [a. ni. 10.6] Atha kho sambahulā bhikkhū yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ –

18.Then a number of bhikkhus went to the Blessed One, paid homage to him, sat down to one side, and said to him:

‘‘Siyā nu kho, bhante, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa, yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā tatrāpi na saññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti?

“Venerable sir, could a bhikkhu obtain such an attainment of samādhi that he would not be percipient of earth in relation to earth, nor of water in relation to water, nor of fire in relation to fire, nor of air in relation to air; nor of the base of the infinity of space in relation to the base of the infinity of space, nor of the base of the infinity of consciousness in relation to the base of the infinity of consciousness, nor of the base of nothingness in relation to the base of nothingness, nor of the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in relation to the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; nor of this world in relation to this world, nor of the other world in relation to the other world; and that he would not be percipient even of what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought, or mentally reviewed; and yet he would still be percipient?”

‘‘Siyā, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa…pe. … yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā tatrāpi na saññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti.

“It is possible, bhikkhus, that a bhikkhu might obtain such an attainment of samādhi that he would not be percipient of earth in relation to earth … and that he would not be percipient even of what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought, or mentally reviewed; and yet he would still be percipient.”

‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, bhante, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa…pe… yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā tatrāpi na saññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti?

“But how, venerable sir, could a bhikkhu obtain such an attainment of samādhi that he would not be percipient of earth in relation to earth … and that he would not be percipient even of what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought, or mentally reviewed; and yet he would still be percipient?”

‘‘Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu evaṃsaññī hoti – ‘etaṃ santaṃ etaṃ paṇītaṃ, yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbāna’nti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa, yampidaṃ diṭṭhaṃ sutaṃ mutaṃ viññātaṃ pattaṃ pariyesitaṃ anuvicaritaṃ manasā tatrāpi na saññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

“Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is percipient thus: ‘This is peaceful, this is sublime, that is, the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all acquisitions, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna.’ It is in this way, bhikkhus, that a bhikkhu might obtain such an attainment of samādhi that he would not be percipient of earth in relation to earth, nor of water in relation to water, nor of fire in relation to fire, nor of air in relation to air; nor of the base of the infinity of space in relation to the base of the infinity of space, nor of the base of the infinity of consciousness in relation to the base of the infinity of consciousness, nor of the base of nothingness in relation to the base of nothingness, nor of the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in relation to the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; nor of this world in relation to this world, nor of the other world in relation to the other world; and that he would not be percipient even of what is seen, heard, sensed, cognized, attained, sought, or mentally reviewed; and yet he would still be percipient.” Eighth.
