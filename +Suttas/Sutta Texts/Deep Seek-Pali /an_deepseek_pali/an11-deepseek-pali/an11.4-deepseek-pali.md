# AN 11.4 Dutiyaupanisāsuttaṃ

## AN 11.4 Second Conditions Sutta

4. Tatra kho āyasmā sāriputto bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘āvuso bhikkhave’’ti [bhikkhavoti (sī. syā. pī.) evaṃ sabbattha a. ni. 10.4]. ‘‘Āvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato sāriputtassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā sāriputto etadavoca –

4. Thereupon, the Venerable Sāriputta addressed the monks, “Friends, monks.” “Friend,” those monks responded to the Venerable Sāriputta. The Venerable Sāriputta said this:

‘‘Dussīlassa, āvuso, sīlavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti avippaṭisāro, avippaṭisāre asati avippaṭisāravipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti pāmojjaṃ, pāmojje asati pāmojjavipannassa hatūpanisā hoti pīti, pītiyā asati pītivipannassa hatūpanisā hoti passaddhi, passaddhiyā asati passaddhivipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti sukhaṃ, sukhe asati sukhavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti sammāsamādhi, sammāsamādhimhi asati sammāsamādhivipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti yathābhūtañāṇadassanaṃ, yathābhūtañāṇadassane asati yathābhūtañāṇadassanavipannassa hatūpanisā hoti nibbidā, nibbidāya asati nibbidāvipannassa hatūpaniso hoti virāgo, virāge asati virāgavipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti vimuttiñāṇadassanaṃ.

For an immoral person, friend, who is deficient in virtue, non-remorse has no basis. When there is no non-remorse, for one deficient in non-remorse, gladness has no basis. When there is no gladness, for one deficient in gladness, rapture has no basis. When there is no rapture, for one deficient in rapture, tranquility has no basis. When there is no tranquility, for one deficient in tranquility, happiness has no basis. When there is no happiness, for one deficient in happiness, right samādhi has no basis. When there is no right samādhi, for one deficient in right samādhi, knowledge and vision of things as they really are has no basis. When there is no knowledge and vision of things as they really are, for one deficient in knowledge and vision of things as they really are, disenchantment has no basis. When there is no disenchantment, for one deficient in disenchantment, dispassion has no basis. When there is no dispassion, for one deficient in dispassion, the knowledge and vision of liberation has no basis.

‘‘Seyyathāpi, āvuso, rukkho sākhāpalāsavipanno. Tassa papaṭikāpi na pāripūriṃ gacchati, tacopi… pheggupi… sāropi na pāripūriṃ gacchati. Evamevaṃ kho, āvuso, dussīlassa sīlavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti avippaṭisāro, avippaṭisāre asati avippaṭisāravipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti pāmojjaṃ…pe… vimuttiñāṇadassanaṃ.

Just as, friend, a tree deprived of branches and foliage—its outer bark, inner bark, sapwood, and heartwood do not reach fullness. In the same way, friend, for an immoral person deficient in virtue, non-remorse has no basis. When there is no non-remorse, for one deficient in non-remorse, gladness has no basis... up to... the knowledge and vision of liberation.

‘‘Sīlavato, āvuso, sīlasampannassa upanisasampanno hoti avippaṭisāro, avippaṭisāre sati avippaṭisārasampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti pāmojjaṃ, pāmojje sati pāmojjasampannassa upanisasampannā hoti pīti, pītiyā sati pītisampannassa upanisasampannā hoti passaddhi, passaddhiyā sati passaddhisampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti sukhaṃ, sukhe sati sukhasampannassa upanisasampanno hoti sammāsamādhi, sammāsamādhimhi sati sammāsamādhisampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti yathābhūtañāṇadassanaṃ, yathābhūtañāṇadassane sati yathābhūtañāṇadassanasampannassa upanisasampannā hoti nibbidā, nibbidāya sati nibbidāsampannassa upanisasampanno hoti virāgo, virāge sati virāgasampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti vimuttiñāṇadassanaṃ.

For a virtuous person, friend, who is accomplished in virtue, non-remorse has a sufficient condition. When there is non-remorse, for one accomplished in non-remorse, gladness has a sufficient condition. When there is gladness, for one accomplished in gladness, rapture has a sufficient condition. When there is rapture, for one accomplished in rapture, tranquility has a sufficient condition. When there is tranquility, for one accomplished in tranquility, happiness has a sufficient condition. When there is happiness, for one accomplished in happiness, right samādhi has a sufficient condition. When there is right samādhi, for one accomplished in right samādhi, knowledge and vision of things as they really are has a sufficient condition. When there is knowledge and vision of things as they really are, for one accomplished in knowledge and vision of things as they really are, disenchantment has a sufficient condition. When there is disenchantment, for one accomplished in disenchantment, dispassion has a sufficient condition. When there is dispassion, for one accomplished in dispassion, the knowledge and vision of liberation has a sufficient condition.

‘‘Seyyathāpi, āvuso, rukkho sākhāpalāsasampanno. Tassa papaṭikāpi pāripūriṃ gacchati, tacopi… pheggupi… sāropi pāripūriṃ gacchati. Evamevaṃ kho, āvuso, sīlavato sīlasampannassa upanisasampanno hoti avippaṭisāro, avippaṭisāre sati avippaṭisārasampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti pāmojjaṃ…pe… vimuttiñāṇadassana’’nti. Catutthaṃ.

Just as, friend, a tree complete with branches and foliage. Its outer layer reaches fullness; its bark… its sapwood… its heartwood reaches fullness. In the same way, friend, for a virtuous person, who is accomplished in virtue, non-remorse has a sufficient condition. When there is non-remorse, for one accomplished in non-remorse, gladness has a sufficient condition… the knowledge and vision of liberation. Fourth.
