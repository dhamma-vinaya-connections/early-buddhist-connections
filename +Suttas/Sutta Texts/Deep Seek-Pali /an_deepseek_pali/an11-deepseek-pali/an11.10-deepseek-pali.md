# AN 11.10 Moranivāpasuttaṃ

## AN 11.10 The Moranivāpa Sutta

10. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati moranivāpe paribbājakārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

10. At one time the Blessed One was staying at Rajagaha in the Moranivāpa, the wanderers' park. There the Blessed One addressed the monks: "Monks." "Venerable sir," those monks replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Tīhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Asekhena sīlakkhandhena, asekhena samādhikkhandhena, asekhena paññākkhandhena – imehi, kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ.

"Monks, a monk endowed with three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans. What three? The aggregate of virtue of one beyond training, the aggregate of samādhi of one beyond training, and the aggregate of wisdom of one beyond training—monks, a monk endowed with these three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans."

‘‘Aparehipi, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Iddhipāṭihāriyena, ādesanāpāṭihāriyena, anusāsanīpāṭihāriyena – imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti, accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ.

"Again, monks, a monk endowed with three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans. What three? The miracle of psychic power, the miracle of mind-reading, and the miracle of instruction. Monks, a monk endowed with these three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans."

‘‘Aparehipi, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Sammādiṭṭhiyā, sammāñāṇena, sammāvimuttiyā – imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ.

"Again, monks, a monk endowed with three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans. What three? Right view, right knowledge, and right liberation. Monks, a monk endowed with these three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans."

‘‘Dvīhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ. Katamehi dvīhi? Vijjāya, caraṇena – imehi kho, bhikkhave, dvīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ. Brahmunā pesā, bhikkhave, sanaṅkumārena gāthā bhāsitā –

Monks, a monk endowed with two qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans. What two? Knowledge and conduct. Monks, a monk endowed with these two qualities is one who has reached the ultimate conclusion, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate holy life, reached the ultimate culmination, and is the foremost of gods and humans. Monks, this verse was recited by Brahmā Sanankumāra:

‘‘Khattiyo seṭṭho janetasmiṃ, ye gottapaṭisārino;Vijjācaraṇasampanno, so seṭṭho devamānuse’’ti [dī. ni. 1.277; saṃ. ni. 1.182; 2.245].

Among people who hold lineage in high esteem,
The warrior is the foremost.
But one accomplished in knowledge and conduct
Is the foremost among gods and humans.

‘‘Sā kho panesā, bhikkhave, sanaṅkumārena gāthā bhāsitā subhāsitā, no dubbhāsitā; atthasaṃhitā, no anatthasaṃhitā; anumatā mayā. Ahampi, bhikkhave, evaṃ vadāmi –

Indeed, monks, this verse spoken by Sanankumara is well spoken, not poorly spoken; connected with the goal, not connected with the unbeneficial; and it is approved by me. Monks, I too say this:

‘‘Khattiyo seṭṭho janetasmiṃ, ye gottapaṭisārino;Vijjācaraṇasampanno, so seṭṭho devamānuse’’ti. dasamaṃ;

"Among people who hold lineage in high esteem,
The warrior is the foremost.
But one accomplished in knowledge and conduct
Is the foremost among gods and humans." Tenth.

Nissayavaggo [nissāyavaggo (syā. kaṃ.)] paṭhamo.

The Chapter on Dependence is the first.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary is:

Kimatthiyā cetanā tayo, upanisā byasanena ca;Dve saññā manasikāro, saddho moranivāpakanti.

Purpose, intentions, three; basis, and calamity; two perceptions, attention; faith, and moranivāpaka.
