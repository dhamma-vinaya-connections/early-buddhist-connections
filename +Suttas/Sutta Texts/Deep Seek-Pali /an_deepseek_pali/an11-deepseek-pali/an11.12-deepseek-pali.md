# AN 11.12 Dutiyamahānāmasuttaṃ

## AN 11.12 The Discourse to Mahānāma (2)

12. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sakkesu viharati kapilavatthusmiṃ nigrodhārāme. Tena kho pana samayena mahānāmo sakko gilānā vuṭṭhito hoti aciravuṭṭhito gelaññā. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karonti – ‘‘niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissatī’’ti.

12.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling among the Sakyans at Kapilavatthu in the Banyan Park. Now at that time, the Sakyan Mahānāma had recently recovered from an illness. And at that time, many monks were making robes for the Blessed One, thinking, ‘When the robes are finished, the Blessed One will set out on a journey after three months.’

Assosi kho mahānāmo sakko – ‘‘sambahulā kira bhikkhū bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karonti – ‘niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissatī’’’ti. Atha kho mahānāmo sakko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho mahānāmo sakko bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sutaṃ metaṃ, bhante – ‘sambahulā kira bhikkhū bhagavato cīvarakammaṃ karonti – niṭṭhitacīvaro bhagavā temāsaccayena cārikaṃ pakkamissatī’ti. Tesaṃ no, bhante, nānāvihārehi viharataṃ kenassa vihārena vihātabba’’nti?

Mahānāma the Sakyan heard: “Many monks, it seems, are doing robe work for the Blessed One, thinking, ‘When the robes are finished, the Blessed One will set out on a journey after three months.’” Then Mahānāma the Sakyan approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Mahānāma the Sakyan said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, I have heard that many monks are doing robe work for the Blessed One, thinking, ‘When the robes are finished, the Blessed One will set out on a journey after three months.’ For us, venerable sir, dwelling in various abodes, in which kind of abode should we dwell?”

‘‘Sādhu sādhu, mahānāma! Etaṃ kho, mahānāma, tumhākaṃ patirūpaṃ kulaputtānaṃ yaṃ tumhe tathāgataṃ upasaṅkamitvā puccheyyātha – ‘tesaṃ no, bhante, nānāvihārehi viharataṃ kenassa vihārena vihātabba’nti? Saddho kho, mahānāma, ārādhako hoti, no assaddho; āraddhavīriyo ārādhako hoti, no kusīto; upaṭṭhitassati ārādhako hoti, no muṭṭhassati; samāhito ārādhako hoti, no asamāhito; paññavā ārādhako hoti, no duppañño. Imesu kho tvaṃ, mahānāma, pañcasu dhammesu patiṭṭhāya cha dhamme uttari bhāveyyāsi.

“Excellent, excellent, Mahānāma! It is indeed fitting for sons of good families like you, Mahānāma, to approach the Tathāgata and ask: ‘Venerable sir, for us dwelling in various abodes, by which abode should we abide?’ Mahānāma, a faithful person is capable, not an unfaithful one; an energetic person is capable, not a lazy one; a mindful person is capable, not an unmindful one; a collected person is capable, not an uncollected one; a wise person is capable, not an unwise one. Established in these five qualities, Mahānāma, you should cultivate six qualities further.”

[a. ni. 6.9] ‘‘Idha tvaṃ, mahānāma, tathāgataṃ anussareyyāsi – ‘itipi so bhagavā…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’ti. Yasmiṃ, mahānāma, samaye ariyasāvako tathāgataṃ anussarati, nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti tathāgataṃ ārabbha. Ujugatacitto kho pana, mahānāma, ariyasāvako labhati atthavedaṃ, labhati dhammavedaṃ, labhati dhammūpasaṃhitaṃ pāmojjaṃ. Pamuditassa pīti jāyati, pītimanassa kāyo passambhati, passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ vediyati, sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati. Imaṃ kho tvaṃ, mahānāma, buddhānussatiṃ gacchantopi bhāveyyāsi, ṭhitopi bhāveyyāsi, nisinnopi bhāveyyāsi, sayānopi bhāveyyāsi, kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhahantopi bhāveyyāsi, puttasambādhasayanaṃ ajjhāvasantopi bhāveyyāsi.

Here, Mahānāma, you should recollect the Tathāgata: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is… the teacher of gods and humans, the Buddha, the Blessed One.’ When, Mahānāma, a noble disciple recollects the Tathāgata, his mind is not pervaded by lust, not by hatred, not by delusion. His mind is straight, set upon the Tathāgata. With a straight mind, Mahānāma, the noble disciple obtains understanding of the meaning, obtains understanding of the Dhamma, obtains joy associated with the Dhamma. When joyful, rapture arises; with rapture in mind, the body becomes tranquil; with a tranquil body, he experiences happiness; being happy, the mind becomes collected. This recollection of the Buddha, Mahānāma, you should develop while walking, while standing, while sitting, while lying down, while engaged in work, and while living in a home crowded with children.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ tvaṃ, mahānāma, dhammaṃ anussareyyāsi…pe… saṅghaṃ anussareyyāsi…pe… attano sīlāni anussareyyāsi…pe… attano cāgaṃ anussareyyāsi…pe… devatā anussareyyāsi – ‘santi devā cātumahārājikā…pe… santi devā tatuttari. Yathārūpāya saddhāya samannāgatā tā devatā ito cutā tatthūpapannā, mayhampi tathārūpā saddhā saṃvijjati. Yathārūpena sīlena… sutena… cāgena… paññāya samannāgatā tā devatā ito cutā tatthūpapannā, mayhampi tathārūpā paññā saṃvijjatī’ti. Yasmiṃ, mahānāma, samaye ariyasāvako attano ca tāsañca devatānaṃ saddhañca sīlañca sutañca cāgañca paññañca anussarati, nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti devatā ārabbha. Ujugatacitto kho pana, mahānāma, ariyasāvako labhati atthavedaṃ, labhati dhammavedaṃ, labhati dhammūpasaṃhitaṃ pāmojjaṃ. Pamuditassa pīti jāyati, pītimanassa kāyo passambhati, passaddhakāyo sukhaṃ vediyati, sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati. Imaṃ kho tvaṃ, mahānāma, devatānussatiṃ gacchantopi bhāveyyāsi, ṭhitopi bhāveyyāsi, nisinnopi bhāveyyāsi, sayānopi bhāveyyāsi, kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhahantopi bhāveyyāsi, puttasambādhasayanaṃ ajjhāvasantopi bhāveyyāsī’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

Furthermore, Mahānāma, you should recollect the Dhamma... you should recollect the Saṅgha... you should recollect your own moral conduct... you should recollect your own generosity... you should recollect the deities: ‘There are devas of the Four Great Kings... there are devas beyond them. Those deities who, endowed with such faith, passed away from here and were reborn there, I too have such faith. Those deities who, endowed with such moral conduct... learning... generosity... wisdom, passed away from here and were reborn there, I too have such wisdom.’ When, Mahānāma, a noble disciple recollects his own and those deities’ faith, moral conduct, learning, generosity, and wisdom, at that time his mind is not overcome by lust, hatred, or delusion. At that time, his mind is upright, directed towards the deities. With an upright mind, the noble disciple obtains understanding of the meaning, obtains understanding of the Dhamma, and joy associated with the Dhamma. When he is joyful, rapture arises; for one with rapture, the body becomes tranquil; with a tranquil body, he feels happiness; with happiness, the mind becomes collected. This recollection of deities, Mahānāma, you should develop while walking, standing, sitting, lying down, while working, and while living in a home crowded with children.” This is the second.
