# AN 11.15 Mettāsuttaṃ

## AN 11.15 The Discourse on Loving-kindness

15. [paṭi. ma. 2.22; mi. pa. 4.4.6] ‘‘Mettāya, bhikkhave, cetovimuttiyā āsevitāya bhāvitāya bahulīkatāya yānīkatāya vatthukatāya anuṭṭhitāya paricitāya susamāraddhāya ekādasānisaṃsā pāṭikaṅkhā.

15."Monks, eleven benefits are to be expected when the liberation of mind through loving-kindness is cultivated, developed, much practiced, made a vehicle, made a basis, established, accumulated, and properly undertaken.

Katame ekādasa? Sukhaṃ supati, sukhaṃ paṭibujjhati, na pāpakaṃ supinaṃ passati, manussānaṃ piyo hoti, amanussānaṃ piyo hoti, devatā rakkhanti, nāssa aggi vā visaṃ vā satthaṃ vā kamati, tuvaṭaṃ cittaṃ samādhiyati, mukhavaṇṇo vippasīdati, asammūḷho kālaṃ karoti, uttari appaṭivijjhanto brahmalokūpago hoti. Mettāya, bhikkhave, cetovimuttiyā āsevitāya bhāvitāya bahulīkatāya yānīkatāya vatthukatāya anuṭṭhitāya paricitāya susamāraddhāya ime ekādasānisaṃsā pāṭikaṅkhā’’ti. Pañcamaṃ.

What are the eleven? One sleeps happily, wakes happily, sees no bad dreams, is dear to humans, is dear to non-humans, deities protect one, neither fire nor poison nor weapon harms one, the mind quickly becomes collected, the facial complexion brightens, one dies unconfused, and if one penetrates no higher, one is reborn in the Brahma-world. Monks, these eleven benefits can be expected when the liberation of mind through loving-kindness is cultivated, developed, much practiced, made a vehicle, made a basis, established, accumulated, and properly undertaken. The fifth.
