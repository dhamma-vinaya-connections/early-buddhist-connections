# AN 6.46 Mahācundasuttaṃ

## AN 6.46 The Discourse on Mahācunda

46. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā mahācundo cetīsu viharati sayaṃjātiyaṃ [sahajātiyaṃ (sī. pī.), sañjātiyaṃ (syā. kaṃ.)]. Tatra kho āyasmā mahācundo bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘āvuso bhikkhave’’ti. ‘‘Āvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato mahācundassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā mahācundo etadavoca –

46.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Venerable Mahācunda was dwelling among the Cetis in Sayañjāti. There the Venerable Mahācunda addressed the bhikkhus thus: “Friends, bhikkhus!” “Friend!” those bhikkhus replied to the Venerable Mahācunda. The Venerable Mahācunda said this:

‘‘Idhāvuso, dhammayogā bhikkhū jhāyī bhikkhū apasādenti – ‘ime pana jhāyinomhā, jhāyinomhāti jhāyanti pajjhāyanti nijjhāyanti avajjhāyanti [apajjhāyanti (ma. ni. 1.508)]. Kimime [kiṃ hime (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] jhāyanti, kintime jhāyanti, kathaṃ ime jhāyantī’ti? Tattha dhammayogā ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, jhāyī ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, na ca bahujanahitāya paṭipannā honti bahujanasukhāya bahuno janassa atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ.

“Here, friends, Dhamma-speakers disparage meditators, saying: ‘These meditators, these meditators, they meditate, they ponder, they reflect, they contemplate. What are they meditating on? Why are they meditating? How are they meditating?’ There, neither the Dhamma-speakers are pleased, nor are the meditators pleased. And they do not practice for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of many people, both devas and humans.

‘‘Idha panāvuso, jhāyī bhikkhū dhammayoge bhikkhū apasādenti – ‘ime pana dhammayogamhā, dhammayogamhāti uddhatā unnaḷā capalā mukharā vikiṇṇavācā muṭṭhassatī asampajānā asamāhitā vibbhantacittā pākatindriyā. Kimime dhammayogā, kintime dhammayogā, kathaṃ ime dhammayogā’ti? Tattha jhāyī ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, dhammayogā ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, na ca bahujanahitāya paṭipannā honti bahujanasukhāya bahuno janassa atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ.

“Here, friends, meditators disparage Dhamma-speakers, saying: ‘These Dhamma-speakers, these Dhamma-speakers, are arrogant, puffed up, fickle, talkative, rambling in speech, forgetful, lacking awareness, uncollected, with scattered minds, and with uncontrolled faculties. What are these Dhamma-speakers? Why are these Dhamma-speakers? How are these Dhamma-speakers?’ There, neither the meditators are pleased, nor are the Dhamma-speakers pleased. And they do not practice for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of many people, both devas and humans.

‘‘Idha panāvuso, dhammayogā bhikkhū dhammayogānaññeva bhikkhūnaṃ vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti, no jhāyīnaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti. Tattha dhammayogā ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, jhāyī ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, na ca bahujanahitāya paṭipannā honti bahujanasukhāya bahuno janassa atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ.

“Here, friends, Dhamma-speakers praise only Dhamma-speakers, and do not praise meditators. There, neither the Dhamma-speakers are pleased, nor are the meditators pleased. And they do not practice for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of many people, both devas and humans.

‘‘Idha panāvuso, jhāyī bhikkhū jhāyīnaññeva bhikkhūnaṃ vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti, no dhammayogānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ vaṇṇaṃ bhāsanti. Tattha jhāyī ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, dhammayogā ca bhikkhū nappasīdanti, na ca bahujanahitāya paṭipannā honti bahujanasukhāya bahuno janassa atthāya hitāya sukhāya devamanussānaṃ.

“Here, friends, meditators praise only meditators, and do not praise Dhamma-speakers. There, neither the meditators are pleased, nor are the Dhamma-speakers pleased. And they do not practice for the welfare of the many, for the happiness of the many, for the benefit, welfare, and happiness of many people, both devas and humans.

‘‘Tasmātihāvuso, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘dhammayogā samānā jhāyīnaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissāmā’ti. Evañhi vo, āvuso, sikkhitabbaṃ. Taṃ kissa hetu? Acchariyā hete, āvuso, puggalā dullabhā lokasmiṃ, ye amataṃ dhātuṃ kāyena phusitvā viharanti. Tasmātihāvuso, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘jhāyī samānā dhammayogānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ vaṇṇaṃ bhāsissāmā’ti. Evañhi vo, āvuso, sikkhitabbaṃ. Taṃ kissa hetu? Acchariyā hete, āvuso, puggalā dullabhā lokasmiṃ ye gambhīraṃ atthapadaṃ paññāya ativijjha passantī’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

“Therefore, friends, you should train yourselves thus: ‘Being Dhamma-speakers, we will speak in praise of meditators.’ For thus, friends, should you train yourselves. Why is that? These persons, friends, are rare and wonderful in the world, who dwell having touched the deathless element with their body. Therefore, friends, you should train yourselves thus: ‘Being meditators, we will speak in praise of Dhamma-speakers.’ For thus, friends, should you train yourselves. Why is that? These persons, friends, are rare and wonderful in the world, who see with wisdom and penetrate the deep meaning of the teaching.” The Fourth Discourse.
