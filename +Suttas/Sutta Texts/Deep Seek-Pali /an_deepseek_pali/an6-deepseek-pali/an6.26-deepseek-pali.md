# AN 6.26 Mahākaccānasuttaṃ

## AN 6.26 The Mahākaccāna Sutta

26. Tatra kho āyasmā mahākaccāno bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘āvuso bhikkhave’’ti. ‘‘Āvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato mahākaccānassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā mahākaccāno etadavoca – ‘‘acchariyaṃ, āvuso; abbhutaṃ, āvuso! Yāvañcidaṃ tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena sambādhe okāsādhigamo anubuddho sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ cha anussatiṭṭhānāni.

26.Then the Venerable Mahākaccāna addressed the bhikkhus: “Friends, bhikkhus.” “Yes, friend,” those bhikkhus replied to the Venerable Mahākaccāna. The Venerable Mahākaccāna said this: “It is wonderful, friends; it is amazing, friends! To such an extent this has been understood by the Blessed One—who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One—the attainment of an opening in the midst of constraint, for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the path, for the direct realization of Nibbāna; namely, these six bases of recollection.”

‘‘Katamāni cha? Idhāvuso, ariyasāvako tathāgataṃ anussarati – ‘itipi so bhagavā …pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’ti. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye ariyasāvako tathāgataṃ anussarati nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti, nikkhantaṃ muttaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ gedhamhā. ‘Gedho’ti kho, āvuso, pañcannetaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Sa kho so, āvuso, ariyasāvako sabbaso ākāsasamena cetasā viharati vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena. Idampi kho, āvuso, ārammaṇaṃ karitvā evamidhekacce sattā visuddhidhammā bhavanti.

“What are the six? Here, friend, a noble disciple recollects the Tathagata: ‘Indeed, the Blessed One is… the teacher of gods and humans, enlightened, blessed.’ At such a time, friend, when a noble disciple recollects the Tathagata, his mind is not overwhelmed by lust, hatred, or delusion. At that time his mind is straightened, gone forth, freed, and risen from greed. ‘Greed,’ friend, is a designation for the five cords of sensual pleasure. That noble disciple, friend, dwells with a mind that is in all ways like space: vast, exalted, immeasurable, without enmity or ill will. By making this an object, some beings here attain purity.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, ariyasāvako dhammaṃ anussarati – ‘svākkhāto bhagavatā dhammo…pe… paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhī’ti. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye ariyasāvako dhammaṃ anussarati nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti, nikkhantaṃ muttaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ gedhamhā. ‘Gedho’ti kho, āvuso, pañcannetaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Sa kho so, āvuso, ariyasāvako sabbaso ākāsasamena cetasā viharati vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena. Idampi kho, āvuso, ārammaṇaṃ karitvā evamidhekacce sattā visuddhidhammā bhavanti.

Furthermore, friend, a noble disciple recollects the Dhamma thus: ‘The Dhamma is well proclaimed by the Blessed One… to be personally experienced by the wise.’ At such a time, friend, when a noble disciple recollects the Dhamma, his mind is not overwhelmed by lust, hatred, or delusion. At that time his mind is straightened, gone forth, freed, and risen from greed. ‘Greed,’ friend, is a designation for the five cords of sensual pleasure. That noble disciple, friend, dwells with a mind that is in all ways like space: vast, exalted, immeasurable, without enmity or ill will. By making this an object, some beings here attain purity.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, ariyasāvako saṅghaṃ anussarati – ‘suppaṭipanno bhagavato sāvakasaṅgho…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassā’ti. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye ariyasāvako saṅghaṃ anussarati nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti, nikkhantaṃ muttaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ gedhamhā. ‘Gedho’ti kho, āvuso, pañcannetaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Sa kho so, āvuso, ariyasāvako sabbaso ākāsasamena cetasā viharati vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena. Idampi kho, āvuso, ārammaṇaṃ karitvā evamidhekacce sattā visuddhidhammā bhavanti.

Furthermore, friend, a noble disciple recollects the Sangha: ‘The Sangha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way… the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.’ At such a time, friend, when a noble disciple recollects the Sangha, his mind is not overwhelmed by lust, hatred, or delusion. At that time his mind is straightened, gone forth, freed, and risen from greed. ‘Greed,’ friend, is a designation for the five cords of sensual pleasure. That noble disciple, friend, dwells with a mind that is in all ways like space: vast, exalted, immeasurable, without enmity or ill will. By making this an object, some beings here attain purity.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, ariyasāvako attano sīlāni anussarati akhaṇḍāni…pe… samādhisaṃvattanikāni. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye ariyasāvako attano sīlaṃ anussarati nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti, nikkhantaṃ muttaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ gedhamhā. ‘Gedho’ti kho, āvuso, pañcannetaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Sa kho so, āvuso, ariyasāvako sabbaso ākāsasamena cetasā viharati vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena. Idampi kho, āvuso, ārammaṇaṃ karitvā evamidhekacce sattā visuddhidhammā bhavanti.

Furthermore, friend, a noble disciple recollects his own virtues: ‘My virtues are unbroken… conducive to samādhi.’ At such a time, friend, when a noble disciple recollects his own virtue, his mind is not overwhelmed by lust, hatred, or delusion. At that time his mind is straightened, gone forth, freed, and risen from greed. ‘Greed,’ friend, is a designation for the five cords of sensual pleasure. That noble disciple, friend, dwells with a mind that is in all ways like space: vast, exalted, immeasurable, without enmity or ill will. By making this an object, some beings here attain purity.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, ariyasāvako attano cāgaṃ anussarati – ‘lābhā vata me, suladdhaṃ vata me…pe… yācayogo dānasaṃvibhāgarato’ti. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye ariyasāvako attano cāgaṃ anussarati nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti, nikkhantaṃ muttaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ gedhamhā. ‘Gedho’ti kho, āvuso, pañcannetaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Sa kho so, āvuso, ariyasāvako sabbaso ākāsasamena cetasā viharati vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena. Idampi kho, āvuso, ārammaṇaṃ karitvā evamidhekacce sattā visuddhidhammā bhavanti.

Furthermore, friend, a noble disciple recollects his own generosity: ‘It is truly my gain, it is my good fortune… I am one who is fit to be asked, delighting in sharing gifts.’ At such a time, friend, when a noble disciple recollects his own generosity, his mind is not overwhelmed by lust, hatred, or delusion. At that time his mind is straightened, gone forth, freed, and risen from greed. ‘Greed,’ friend, is a designation for the five cords of sensual pleasure. That noble disciple, friend, dwells with a mind that is in all ways like space: vast, exalted, immeasurable, without enmity or ill will. By making this an object, some beings here attain purity.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, ariyasāvako devatā anussarati – ‘santi devā cātumahārājikā, santi devā…pe… tatuttari. Yathārūpāya saddhāya samannāgatā tā devatā ito cutā tattha upapannā; mayhampi tathārūpā saddhā saṃvijjati. Yathārūpena sīlena…pe… sutena… cāgena… paññāya samannāgatā tā devatā ito cutā tattha upapannā; mayhampi tathārūpā paññā saṃvijjatī’ti. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye ariyasāvako attano ca tāsañca devatānaṃ saddhañca sīlañca sutañca cāgañca paññañca anussarati nevassa tasmiṃ samaye rāgapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na dosapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti, na mohapariyuṭṭhitaṃ cittaṃ hoti; ujugatamevassa tasmiṃ samaye cittaṃ hoti, nikkhantaṃ muttaṃ vuṭṭhitaṃ gedhamhā. ‘Gedho’ti kho, āvuso, pañcannetaṃ kāmaguṇānaṃ adhivacanaṃ. Sa kho so, āvuso, ariyasāvako sabbaso ākāsasamena cetasā viharati vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyāpajjena. Idampi kho, āvuso, ārammaṇaṃ karitvā evamidhekacce sattā visuddhidhammā bhavanti.

Furthermore, friend, a noble disciple recollects the deities: ‘There are the deities of the Four Great Kings, there are the deities… and so on up to… those higher than that. Those deities, endowed with faith of such a kind, passed away from here and were reborn there; I too have faith of such a kind. Those deities, endowed with virtue… learning… generosity… wisdom of such a kind, passed away from here and were reborn there; I too have wisdom of such a kind.’ At such a time, friend, when a noble disciple recollects his own faith, virtue, learning, generosity, and wisdom, and also that of those deities, his mind is not overwhelmed by lust, hatred, or delusion. At that time his mind is straightened, gone forth, freed, and risen from greed. ‘Greed,’ friend, is a designation for the five cords of sensual pleasure. That noble disciple, friend, dwells with a mind that is in all ways like space: vast, exalted, immeasurable, without enmity or ill will. By making this an object, some beings here attain purity.

‘‘Acchariyaṃ, āvuso; abbhutaṃ, āvuso! Yāvañcidaṃ tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena sambādhe okāsādhigamo anubuddho sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ cha anussatiṭṭhānānī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“It is wonderful, friend; it is amazing, friend! To such an extent this has been understood by the Blessed One—who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One—the attainment of an opening in the midst of constraint, for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the path, for the direct realization of Nibbāna; namely, these six bases of recollection.” The sixth.
