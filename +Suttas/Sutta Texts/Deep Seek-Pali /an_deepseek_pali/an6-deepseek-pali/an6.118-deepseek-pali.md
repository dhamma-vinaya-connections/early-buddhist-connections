118.Monks, without abandoning six things, one is incapable of dwelling contemplating phenomena internally in phenomena... externally in phenomena... both internally and externally in phenomena... internally in feelings... externally in feelings... both internally and externally in feelings... internally in mind... externally in mind... both internally and externally in mind... internally in phenomena... externally in phenomena... both internally and externally in phenomena. What six? Delight in activity, delight in talk, delight in sleep, delight in company, lack of guard over the sense doors, and lack of moderation in food. Monks, having abandoned these six things, one is capable of dwelling contemplating phenomena in phenomena both internally and externally. This is the second.
