# AN 6.28 Dutiyasamayasuttaṃ

## AN 6.28 The Discourse on the Second Occasion

28. Ekaṃ samayaṃ sambahulā therā bhikkhū bārāṇasiyaṃ viharanti isipatane migadāye. Atha kho tesaṃ therānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkantānaṃ maṇḍalamāḷe sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ ayamantarākathā udapādi – ‘‘ko nu kho, āvuso, samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamitu’’nti?

28.At one time a number of elder bhikkhus were dwelling at Varanasi, at Isipatana, in the Deer Park. Then, after their meal, having returned from the alms-round, those elder bhikkhus, seated together in the assembly hall, had this discussion arise among them: “Friends, what is the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind?”

Evaṃ vutte aññataro bhikkhu there bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto pāde pakkhāletvā nisinno hoti pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā, so samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamitu’’nti.

When this was said, a certain bhikkhu addressed the elder bhikkhus thus: “Friends, the occasion when a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind has returned from the alms-round after the meal, having washed his feet, is seated cross-legged, keeping his body erect, and establishing mindfulness in front of him—that is the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind.”

Evaṃ vutte aññataro bhikkhu taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘na kho, āvuso, so samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto pāde pakkhāletvā nisinno hoti pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā, cārittakilamathopissa tasmiṃ samaye appaṭippassaddho hoti, bhattakilamathopissa tasmiṃ samaye appaṭippassaddho hoti. Tasmā so asamayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito vihārapacchāyāyaṃ nisinno hoti pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā, so samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamitu’’nti.

When this was said, another bhikkhu said to that bhikkhu: “Friend, that is not the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview. For, friend, when a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind has returned from the alms-round after the meal, having washed his feet, is seated cross-legged, keeping his body erect, and establishing mindfulness in front of him, the weariness from the alms-round has not yet subsided in him, nor has the discomfort from the meal. Therefore, that is not the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview. When, friend, a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind has risen from seclusion in the evening and is seated in the shade of his dwelling, cross-legged, keeping his body erect, and establishing mindfulness in front of him, that is the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview.”

Evaṃ vutte aññataro bhikkhu taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘na kho, āvuso, so samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu sāyanhasamayaṃ paṭisallānā vuṭṭhito vihārapacchāyāyaṃ nisinno hoti pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā, yadevassa divā samādhinimittaṃ manasikataṃ hoti tadevassa tasmiṃ samaye samudācarati. Tasmā so asamayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu rattiyā paccūsasamayaṃ paccuṭṭhāya nisinno hoti pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā, so samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamitu’’nti.

When this was said, a certain bhikkhu said to that bhikkhu: “Friend, that is not the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview. For, friend, when a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind has risen from seclusion in the evening and is seated in the shade of his dwelling, cross-legged, keeping his body erect, and establishing mindfulness in front of him, whatever sign of samādhi he attended to during the day recurs to him at that time. Therefore, that is not the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview. When, friend, a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind has risen in the early morning and is seated cross-legged, keeping his body erect, and establishing mindfulness in front of him—that is the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview.”

Evaṃ vutte aññataro bhikkhu taṃ bhikkhuṃ etadavoca – ‘‘na kho, āvuso, so samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ. Yasmiṃ, āvuso, samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu rattiyā paccūsasamayaṃ paccuṭṭhāya nisinno hoti pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā, ojaṭṭhāyissa tasmiṃ samaye kāyo hoti phāsussa hoti buddhānaṃ sāsanaṃ manasi kātuṃ. Tasmā so asamayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamitu’’nti.

When this was said, another bhikkhu said to that bhikkhu: “Friend, that is not the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview. When, friend, a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind has risen in the early morning and is seated cross-legged, keeping his body erect, and establishing mindfulness in front of him, his body is then vigorous and firm, he is comfortable, and he is able to bear in mind the teaching of the Buddhas. Therefore, that is not the occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview.”

Evaṃ vutte āyasmā mahākaccāno there bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘sammukhā metaṃ, āvuso, bhagavato sutaṃ sammukhā paṭiggahitaṃ – ‘chayime, bhikkhu, samayā manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ’’’.

When this was said, Venerable Mahākaccāna said to those bhikkhus: “Friends, I heard this directly from the Blessed One, I received it directly from him: ‘Bhikkhus, there are these six occasions for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview.’”

‘‘Katame cha? Idha, bhikkhu, yasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu kāmarāgapariyuṭṭhitena cetasā viharati kāmarāgaparetena, uppannassa ca kāmarāgassa nissaraṇaṃ yathābhūtaṃ nappajānāti, tasmiṃ samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu upasaṅkamitvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘ahaṃ kho, āvuso, kāmarāgapariyuṭṭhitena cetasā viharāmi kāmarāgaparetena, uppannassa ca kāmarāgassa nissaraṇaṃ yathābhūtaṃ nappajānāmi. Sādhu vata me āyasmā kāmarāgassa pahānāya dhammaṃ desetū’ti. Tassa manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu kāmarāgassa pahānāya dhammaṃ deseti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhu, paṭhamo samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ.

“What are the six? Here, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu dwells with a mind beset by sensual desire, overcome by sensual desire, and he does not truly understand the escape from arisen sensual desire—at that time, a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind should be approached and addressed thus: ‘Friend, I dwell with a mind beset by sensual desire, overcome by sensual desire, and I do not truly understand the escape from arisen sensual desire. It would be good if the venerable one would teach me the Dhamma for the abandoning of sensual desire.’ Then that bhikkhu who cultivates the mind teaches him the Dhamma for the abandoning of sensual desire. This, bhikkhus, is the first occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhu, yasmiṃ samaye bhikkhu byāpādapariyuṭṭhitena cetasā viharati…pe… thinamiddhapariyuṭṭhitena cetasā viharati… uddhaccakukkuccapariyuṭṭhitena cetasā viharati… vicikicchāpariyuṭṭhitena cetasā viharati… yaṃ nimittaṃ āgamma yaṃ nimittaṃ manasikaroto anantarā āsavānaṃ khayo hoti, taṃ nimittaṃ na jānāti na passati, tasmiṃ samaye manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu upasaṅkamitvā evamassa vacanīyo – ‘ahaṃ kho, āvuso, yaṃ nimittaṃ āgamma yaṃ nimittaṃ manasikaroto anantarā āsavānaṃ khayo hoti taṃ nimittaṃ na jānāmi na passāmi. Sādhu vata me āyasmā āsavānaṃ khayāya dhammaṃ desetū’ti. Tassa manobhāvanīyo bhikkhu āsavānaṃ khayāya dhammaṃ deseti. Ayaṃ, bhikkhu, chaṭṭho samayo manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamituṃ.

“Furthermore, bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu dwells with a mind beset by ill will… beset by sloth and torpor… beset by restlessness and regret… beset by doubt… and he does not know, does not see the sign by relying on which, by attending to which, there is the immediate destruction of the taints—at that time, a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind should be approached and addressed thus: ‘Friend, I do not know, I do not see the sign by relying on which, by attending to which, there is the immediate destruction of the taints. It would be good if the venerable one would teach me the Dhamma for the destruction of the taints.’ Then that bhikkhu who cultivates the mind teaches him the Dhamma for the destruction of the taints. This, bhikkhus, is the sixth occasion for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview.”

‘‘Sammukhā metaṃ, āvuso, bhagavato sutaṃ sammukhā paṭiggahitaṃ – ‘ime kho, bhikkhu, cha samayā manobhāvanīyassa bhikkhuno dassanāya upasaṅkamitu’’’nti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

“Friends, I heard this directly from the Blessed One, I received it directly from him: ‘Bhikkhus, these are the six occasions for approaching a bhikkhu who cultivates the mind for an interview.’” The Eighth.
