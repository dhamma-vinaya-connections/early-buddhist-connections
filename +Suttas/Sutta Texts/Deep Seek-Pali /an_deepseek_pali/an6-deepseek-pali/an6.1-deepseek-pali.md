"Monks, a monk endowed with six qualities is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverential salutation, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. What six? Here, monks, a monk, having seen a form with the eye, is neither glad nor saddened, but dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending. Having heard a sound with the ear, he is neither glad nor saddened, but dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending. Having smelled an odor with the nose, he is neither glad nor saddened, but dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending. Having tasted a flavor with the tongue, he is neither glad nor saddened, but dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending. Having touched a tangible object with the body, he is neither glad nor saddened, but dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending. Having cognized a mental object with the mind, he is neither glad nor saddened, but dwells equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending.
