# AN 6.30 Anuttariyasuttaṃ

## AN 6.30 The Unsurpassed Discourse

30. ‘‘Chayimāni, bhikkhave, anuttariyāni. Katamāni cha? Dassanānuttariyaṃ, savanānuttariyaṃ, lābhānuttariyaṃ, sikkhānuttariyaṃ, pāricariyānuttariyaṃ, anussatānuttariyanti.

30."bhikkhus, there are these six unsurpassed things. What six? Unsurpassed seeing, unsurpassed hearing, unsurpassed gain, unsurpassed training, unsurpassed attendance, and unsurpassed recollection."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, dassanānuttariyaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco hatthiratanampi dassanāya gacchati, assaratanampi dassanāya gacchati, maṇiratanampi dassanāya gacchati, uccāvacaṃ vā pana dassanāya gacchati, samaṇaṃ vā brāhmaṇaṃ vā micchādiṭṭhikaṃ micchāpaṭipannaṃ dassanāya gacchati. Atthetaṃ, bhikkhave, dassanaṃ; netaṃ natthīti vadāmi. Tañca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, dassanaṃ hīnaṃ gammaṃ pothujjanikaṃ anariyaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati. Yo ca kho, bhikkhave, tathāgataṃ vā tathāgatasāvakaṃ vā dassanāya gacchati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno, etadānuttariyaṃ, bhikkhave, dassanānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ [sokapariddavānaṃ (sī.)] samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya [atthagamāya (sī.)] ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ tathāgataṃ vā tathāgatasāvakaṃ vā dassanāya gacchati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, dassanānuttariyaṃ. Iti dassanānuttariyaṃ.

"And what, bhikkhus, is unsurpassed seeing? Here, bhikkhus, someone goes to see a royal elephant, a royal horse, a royal jewel, or various other things, or goes to see an ascetic or brahmin with wrong views and wrong practice. This seeing exists, bhikkhus; I do not say it does not exist. But this seeing, bhikkhus, is inferior, common, mundane, ignoble, not beneficial, not leading to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, or to Nibbāna. But when, bhikkhus, someone goes to see the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata, with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence—this is the unsurpassed seeing for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the true path, for the realization of Nibbāna—namely, going to see the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence. This, bhikkhus, is called unsurpassed seeing. Thus, unsurpassed seeing."

‘‘Savanānuttariyañca kathaṃ hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco bherisaddampi [bherisaddassapi (ka.) evaṃ vīṇāsaddampiiccādīsupi] savanāya gacchati, vīṇāsaddampi savanāya gacchati, gītasaddampi savanāya gacchati, uccāvacaṃ vā pana savanāya gacchati, samaṇassa vā brāhmaṇassa vā micchādiṭṭhikassa micchāpaṭipannassa dhammassavanāya gacchati. Atthetaṃ, bhikkhave, savanaṃ; netaṃ natthīti vadāmi. Tañca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, savanaṃ hīnaṃ gammaṃ pothujjanikaṃ anariyaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ, na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati. Yo ca kho, bhikkhave, tathāgatassa vā tathāgatasāvakassa vā dhammassavanāya gacchati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno, etadānuttariyaṃ, bhikkhave, savanānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ tathāgatassa vā tathāgatasāvakassa vā dhammassavanāya gacchati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, savanānuttariyaṃ. Iti dassanānuttariyaṃ, savanānuttariyaṃ.

"And how does unsurpassed hearing come to be? Here, bhikkhus, someone goes to hear the sound of a drum, goes to hear the sound of a lute, goes to hear the sound of singing, or goes to hear various other sounds, or goes to hear the Dhamma from an ascetic or brahmin with wrong views and wrong practice. This hearing exists, bhikkhus; I do not say it does not exist. But this hearing, bhikkhus, is inferior, common, mundane, ignoble, not beneficial, not leading to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, or to Nibbāna. But when, bhikkhus, someone goes to hear the Dhamma from the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata, with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence—this is the unsurpassed hearing for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the true path, for the realization of Nibbāna—namely, going to hear the Dhamma from the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence. This, bhikkhus, is called unsurpassed hearing. Thus, unsurpassed seeing, unsurpassed hearing."

‘‘Lābhānuttariyañca kathaṃ hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puttalābhampi labhati, dāralābhampi labhati, dhanalābhampi labhati, uccāvacaṃ vā pana lābhaṃ labhati, samaṇe vā brāhmaṇe vā micchādiṭṭhike micchāpaṭipanne saddhaṃ paṭilabhati. Attheso, bhikkhave, lābho; neso natthīti vadāmi. So ca kho eso, bhikkhave, lābho hīno gammo pothujjaniko anariyo anatthasaṃhito, na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati. Yo ca kho, bhikkhave, tathāgate vā tathāgatasāvake vā saddhaṃ paṭilabhati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno, etadānuttariyaṃ, bhikkhave, lābhānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ tathāgate vā tathāgatasāvake vā saddhaṃ paṭilabhati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, lābhānuttariyaṃ. Iti dassanānuttariyaṃ, savanānuttariyaṃ, lābhānuttariyaṃ.

"And how does the unsurpassed gain come to be? Here, bhikkhus, someone gains the gain of sons, gains the gain of wives, gains the gain of wealth, or gains various kinds of gains, and gains faith in ascetics or brahmins who hold wrong views and follow wrong practice. This, bhikkhus, is a gain; I do not say it is not a gain. But this gain, bhikkhus, is inferior, common, mundane, ignoble, not beneficial, not leading to disenchantment, dispassion, cessation, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, or Nibbāna. But when, bhikkhus, someone gains faith in the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata—with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence—this is the unsurpassed gain for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the true path, for the realization of Nibbāna—that is, gaining faith in the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata, with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence. This, bhikkhus, is called the unsurpassed gain. Thus, unsurpassed seeing, unsurpassed hearing, unsurpassed gain."

‘‘Sikkhānuttariyañca kathaṃ hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco hatthismimpi sikkhati, assasmimpi sikkhati, rathasmimpi sikkhati, dhanusmimpi sikkhati, tharusmimpi sikkhati, uccāvacaṃ vā pana sikkhati, samaṇassa vā brāhmaṇassa vā micchādiṭṭhikassa micchāpaṭipannassa [micchāpaṭipattiṃ (ka.)] sikkhati. Atthesā, bhikkhave, sikkhā; nesā natthīti vadāmi. Sā ca kho esā, bhikkhave, sikkhā hīnā gammā pothujjanikā anariyā anatthasaṃhitā, na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati. Yo ca kho, bhikkhave, tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye adhisīlampi sikkhati, adhicittampi sikkhati, adhipaññampi sikkhati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno, etadānuttariyaṃ, bhikkhave, sikkhānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ tathāgatappavedite dhammavinaye adhisīlampi sikkhati, adhicittampi sikkhati, adhipaññampi sikkhati, niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, sikkhānuttariyaṃ. Iti dassanānuttariyaṃ, savanānuttariyaṃ, lābhānuttariyaṃ, sikkhānuttariyaṃ.

"And how is unsurpassed training? Here, bhikkhus, someone trains in elephant riding, trains in horsemanship, trains in chariotry, trains in archery, trains in swordsmanship, or trains in various other things, or trains from an ascetic or brahmin with wrong views and wrong practice. That, bhikkhus, is training; I do not say that it does not exist. But that training, bhikkhus, is inferior, common, mundane, ignoble, not beneficial, not leading to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, or to Nibbāna. But when, bhikkhus, someone trains in higher virtue, higher mind, and higher wisdom in the Dhamma-Vinaya proclaimed by the Tathāgata—with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence—this is the unsurpassed training for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the true path, for the realization of Nibbāna—that is, training in higher virtue, higher mind, and higher wisdom in the Dhamma-Vinaya proclaimed by the Tathāgata, with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence. This, bhikkhus, is called unsurpassed training. Thus, unsurpassed seeing, unsurpassed hearing, unsurpassed gain, and unsurpassed training."

‘‘Pāricariyānuttariyañca kathaṃ hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco khattiyampi paricarati, brāhmaṇampi paricarati, gahapatimpi paricarati, uccāvacaṃ vā pana paricarati, samaṇaṃ vā brāhmaṇaṃ vā micchādiṭṭhikaṃ micchāpaṭipannaṃ paricarati. Atthesā, bhikkhave, pāricariyā; nesā natthīti vadāmi. Sā ca kho esā, bhikkhave, pāricariyā hīnā gammā pothujjanikā anariyā anatthasaṃhitā, na nibbidāya…pe… na nibbānāya saṃvattati. Yo ca kho, bhikkhave, tathāgataṃ vā tathāgatasāvakaṃ vā paricarati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno, etadānuttariyaṃ, bhikkhave, pāricariyānaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ tathāgataṃ vā tathāgatasāvakaṃ vā paricarati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, pāricariyānuttariyaṃ. Iti dassanānuttariyaṃ, savanānuttariyaṃ, lābhānuttariyaṃ, sikkhānuttariyaṃ, pāricariyānuttariyaṃ.

"And what, bhikkhus, is unsurpassed attendance? Here, bhikkhus, someone attends upon a noble, attends upon a brahmin, attends upon a householder, or attends upon various others, or attends upon an ascetic or brahmin with wrong views and wrong practice. This attendance exists, bhikkhus; I do not say that it does not exist. But this attendance, bhikkhus, is inferior, common, mundane, ignoble, not beneficial, not leading to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, or to Nibbāna. But, bhikkhus, when someone attends upon the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence—this is the unsurpassed attendance for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the true path, for the realization of Nibbāna—that is, attending upon the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence. This, bhikkhus, is called unsurpassed attendance. Thus, unsurpassed seeing, unsurpassed hearing, unsurpassed gain, unsurpassed training, and unsurpassed attendance."

‘‘Anussatānuttariyañca kathaṃ hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puttalābhampi anussarati, dāralābhampi anussarati, dhanalābhampi anussarati, uccāvacaṃ vā pana lābhaṃ anussarati, samaṇaṃ vā brāhmaṇaṃ vā micchādiṭṭhikaṃ micchāpaṭipannaṃ anussarati. Atthesā, bhikkhave, anussati; nesā natthīti vadāmi. Sā ca kho esā, bhikkhave, anussati hīnā gammā pothujjanikā anariyā anatthasaṃhitā, na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati. Yo ca kho, bhikkhave, tathāgataṃ vā tathāgatasāvakaṃ vā anussarati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno, etadānuttariyaṃ, bhikkhave, anussatīnaṃ sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya, yadidaṃ tathāgataṃ vā tathāgatasāvakaṃ vā anussarati niviṭṭhasaddho niviṭṭhapemo ekantagato abhippasanno. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, anussatānuttariyaṃ. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, cha anuttariyānī’’ti.

"And how is recollection unsurpassed? Here, Bhikkhus, someone recollects the gain of a son, the gain of a wife, the gain of wealth, or various kinds of gains. Or they recollect an ascetic or brahmin with wrong view and wrong practice. That, Bhikkhus, is recollection; I do not say that it does not exist. But that recollection, Bhikkhus, is inferior, common, mundane, ignoble, not beneficial, not leading to disenchantment, dispassion, cessation, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, or Nibbāna. But when, Bhikkhus, someone recollects the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata—with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence—this is the unsurpassed recollection for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and distress, for the attainment of the true path, for the realization of Nibbāna—that is, recollecting the Tathāgata or a disciple of the Tathāgata with firmly established faith, firmly established affection, singular devotion, and utter confidence. This, Bhikkhus, is called unsurpassed recollection. These, Bhikkhus, are the six unsurpassed things."

‘‘Ye dassanānuttaraṃ laddhā [ye dassanavaraṃ laddhā (sī. pī.), dassanānuttariyaṃ laddhā (syā. kaṃ.)], savanañca anuttaraṃ;Lābhānuttariyaṃ laddhā, sikkhānuttariye ratā [anuttariyaṃ tathā (ka.)].

"Those who have gained the unsurpassed vision, and the unsurpassed hearing; They have gained the unsurpassed gain, delighting in the unsurpassed training."

‘‘Upaṭṭhitā pāricariyā, bhāvayanti anussatiṃ;Vivekappaṭisaṃyuttaṃ, khemaṃ amatagāminiṃ.

"They attend in service, they cultivate recollection; Connected with seclusion, leading to safety, to the deathless."

‘‘Appamāde pamuditā, nipakā sīlasaṃvutā;Te ve kālena paccenti [paccanti (syā. ka.)], yattha dukkhaṃ nirujjhatī’’ti. dasamaṃ;

"Delighting in heedfulness, skillful, restrained by virtue, they indeed in time attain where suffering ceases." Tenth.

Anuttariyavaggo [sāmakavaggo (ka.)] tatiyo.

The Unsurpassed Chapter, the Third.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Sāmako aparihāniyo, bhayaṃ himavānussati;Kaccāno dve ca samayā, udāyī anuttariyenāti.

Sāmaka, non-declining; fear, Himavant recollection; Kaccāna, two times, and Udāyī, with the unsurpassed.
