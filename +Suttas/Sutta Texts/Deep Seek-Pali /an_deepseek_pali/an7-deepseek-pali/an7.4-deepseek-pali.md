# AN 7.4 Vitthatabalasuttaṃ

## AN 7.4 The Detailed Discourse on Powers

4. ‘‘Sattimāni, bhikkhave, balāni. Katamāni satta? Saddhābala, vīriyabalaṃ, hirībalaṃ, ottappabalaṃ, satibalaṃ, samādhibalaṃ, paññābalaṃ.

4. "Monks, there are these seven powers. Which are the seven? The power of faith, the power of energy, the power of moral shame, the power of moral dread, the power of mindfulness, the power of samādhi, and the power of wisdom."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, saddhābalaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako saddho hoti, saddahati tathāgatassa bodhiṃ – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho…pe… satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā’ti. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, saddhābalaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the power of faith? Here, monks, a noble disciple has faith, trusts in the Tathāgata's enlightenment: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is an Arahant, perfectly enlightened... teacher of gods and humans, Buddha, Blessed One.' This is called, monks, the power of faith."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, vīriyabalaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako āraddhavīriyo viharati akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya, kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ upasampadāya, thāmavā daḷhaparakkamo anikkhittadhuro kusalesu dhammesu. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, vīriyabalaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the power of energy? Here, monks, a noble disciple dwells with energy aroused for abandoning unwholesome states and acquiring wholesome states, steadfast, staunch in exertion, not shedding his duty in regard to wholesome states. This is called, monks, the power of energy."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, hirībalaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako hirimā hoti, hirīyati kāyaduccaritena vacīduccaritena manoduccaritena, hirīyati pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ samāpattiyā. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, hirībalaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the power of moral shame? Here, monks, a noble disciple is morally ashamed; he is ashamed of bodily misconduct, verbal misconduct, and mental misconduct; he is ashamed of engaging in evil, unwholesome states. This is called, monks, the power of moral shame."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, ottappabalaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako ottappī hoti, ottappati kāyaduccaritena vacīduccaritena manoduccaritena, ottappati pāpakānaṃ akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ samāpattiyā. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, ottappabalaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the power of moral dread? Here, monks, a noble disciple is morally dreadful; he dreads bodily misconduct, verbal misconduct, and mental misconduct; he dreads engaging in evil, unwholesome states. This is called, monks, the power of moral dread."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, satibalaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako satimā hoti paramena satinepakkena samannāgato cirakatampi cirabhāsitampi saritā anussaritā. Idaṃ, vuccati, bhikkhave, satibalaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the power of mindfulness? Here, monks, a noble disciple is mindful, possessing supreme mindfulness and skill in mindfulness, remembering and recollecting what was done and said long ago. This is called, monks, the power of mindfulness."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, samādhibalaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako vivicceva kāmehi…pe… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, samādhibalaṃ.

"And what, monks, is the power of samādhi? Here, monks, a noble disciple, quite secluded from sensual pleasures… enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna. This is called, monks, the power of samādhi."

‘‘Katamañca, bhikkhave, paññābalaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako paññavā hoti udayatthagāminiyā paññāya samannāgato ariyāya nibbedhikāya sammā dukkhakkhayagāminiyā. Idaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, paññābalaṃ. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, satta balānīti.

"And what, monks, is the power of wisdom? Here, monks, a noble disciple is wise, possessing wisdom that leads to the arising and passing away [of phenomena], which is noble, penetrative, leading to the complete destruction of suffering. This is called, monks, the power of wisdom. These, monks, are the seven powers."

‘‘Saddhābalaṃ vīriyañca, hirī ottappiyaṃ balaṃ;Satibalaṃ samādhi ca, paññā ve sattamaṃ balaṃ;Etehi balavā bhikkhu, sukhaṃ jīvati paṇḍito.

The power of faith and energy, moral shame and moral dread are powers;
The power of mindfulness and samādhi, and wisdom as the seventh power;
A monk endowed with these powers, a wise one, lives happily.

‘‘Yoniso vicine dhammaṃ, paññāyatthaṃ vipassati;Pajjotasseva nibbānaṃ, vimokkho hoti cetaso’’ti. catutthaṃ;

Wisely examining the Dhamma, one sees the meaning with wisdom; Like the extinguishing of a lamp, there is liberation of the mind.
