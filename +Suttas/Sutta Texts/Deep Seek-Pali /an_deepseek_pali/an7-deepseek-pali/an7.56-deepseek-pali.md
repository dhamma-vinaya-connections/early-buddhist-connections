# AN 7.56 Tissabrahmāsuttaṃ

## AN 7.56 The Brahmā Tissa Discourse

56. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati gijjhakūṭe pabbate. Atha kho dve devatā abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇā kevalakappaṃ gijjhakūṭaṃ obhāsetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho ekā devatā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘etā, bhante, bhikkhuniyo vimuttā’’ti. Aparā devatā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘etā, bhante, bhikkhuniyo anupādisesā suvimuttā’’ti. Idamavocuṃ tā devatā. Samanuñño satthā ahosi. Atha kho tā devatā ‘‘samanuñño satthā’’ti bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tatthevantaradhāyiṃsu.

56.Thus have I heard: At one time the Blessed One was living at Rājagaha, on Vulture Peak. Then, late in the night, two deities of surpassing beauty, illuminating the entire Vulture Peak, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, one deity said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, these bhikkhunīs are liberated.” The other deity said to the Blessed One: “Venerable sir, these bhikkhunīs are fully liberated without remainder.” Thus spoke those deities. The Teacher approved. Then those deities, knowing that the Teacher had approved, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him, and vanished right there.

Atha kho bhagavā tassā rattiyā accayena bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘imaṃ, bhikkhave, rattiṃ dve devatā abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇā kevalakappaṃ gijjhakūṭaṃ obhāsetvā yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhaṃsu. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho, bhikkhave, ekā devatā maṃ etadavoca – ‘etā, bhante, bhikkhuniyo vimuttā’ti. Aparā devatā maṃ etadavoca – ‘etā, bhante, bhikkhuniyo anupādisesā suvimuttā’ti. Idamavocuṃ, bhikkhave, tā devatā. Idaṃ vatvā maṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā tatthevantaradhāyiṃsū’’ti.

Then, when that night had passed, the Blessed One addressed the monks: "This night, monks, two deities of surpassing beauty, illuminating the entire Vulture Peak, approached me; having approached, they paid homage to me and stood to one side. Standing to one side, monks, one deity said to me: 'Venerable sir, these bhikkhunīs are liberated.' The other deity said to me: 'Venerable sir, these bhikkhunīs are fully liberated without remainder.' This is what those deities said, monks. Having said this, they paid homage to me, circumambulated me, and vanished right there."

Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhagavato avidūre nisinno hoti. Atha kho āyasmato mahāmoggallānassa etadahosi – ‘‘katamesānaṃ kho devānaṃ evaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti? Tena kho pana samayena tisso nāma bhikkhu adhunākālaṅkato aññataraṃ brahmalokaṃ upapanno hoti. Tatrāpi naṃ evaṃ jānanti – ‘‘tisso brahmā mahiddhiko mahānubhāvo’’ti.

Now at that time, the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna was sitting not far from the Blessed One. Then this thought occurred to the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna: "Which deities have this knowledge: 'with residue or without residue'?" Now at that time, a monk named Tissa had recently died and had been reborn in a certain Brahmā world. There too they knew him thus: "Brahmā Tissa is mighty and powerful."

Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evamevaṃ – gijjhakūṭe pabbate antarahito tasmiṃ brahmaloke pāturahosi. Addasā kho tisso brahmā āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ dūratova āgacchantaṃ. Disvā āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ehi kho, mārisa moggallāna; svāgataṃ, mārisa moggallāna! Cirassaṃ kho, mārisa moggallāna, imaṃ pariyāyamakāsi, yadidaṃ idhāgamanāya. Nisīda, mārisa moggallāna, idamāsanaṃ paññatta’’nti. Nisīdi kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno paññatte āsane. Tissopi kho brahmā āyasmantaṃ mahāmoggallānaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho tissaṃ brahmānaṃ āyasmā mahāmoggallāno etadavoca – ‘‘katamesānaṃ kho, tissa, devānaṃ evaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti? ‘‘Brahmakāyikānaṃ kho, mārisa moggallāna, devānaṃ evaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti.

Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna—just as a strong man might bend his arm or stretch it out—disappeared from Vulture Peak and reappeared in that Brahmā world. Brahmā Tissa saw the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna coming from afar. Seeing him, he said: "Come, venerable Moggallāna! Welcome, venerable Moggallāna! It has been a long time, venerable Moggallāna, since you took the opportunity to come here. Sit, venerable Moggallāna, this seat is ready." The Venerable Mahāmoggallāna sat down on the prepared seat. Brahmā Tissa too paid homage to the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna said to him: "Which deities, Tissa, have this knowledge: 'with residue or without residue'?" "For the Brahmā deities, venerable Moggallāna, there is this knowledge: 'with residue or without residue.'"

‘‘Sabbesaññeva kho, tissa, brahmakāyikānaṃ devānaṃ evaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti? ‘‘Na kho, mārisa moggallāna, sabbesaṃ brahmakāyikānaṃ devānaṃ evaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti.

"Do all the Brahmā deities, Tissa, have this knowledge: 'with residue or without residue'?" "No, venerable Moggallāna, not all Brahmā deities have this knowledge: 'with residue or without residue.'"

‘‘Ye kho te, mārisa moggallāna, brahmakāyikā devā brahmena āyunā santuṭṭhā brahmena vaṇṇena brahmena sukhena brahmena yasena brahmena ādhipateyyena santuṭṭhā, te uttari nissaraṇaṃ yathābhūtaṃ nappajānanti. Tesaṃ na evaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’ti. Ye ca kho te, mārisa moggallāna, brahmakāyikā devā brahmena āyunā asantuṭṭhā, brahmena vaṇṇena brahmena sukhena brahmena yasena brahmena ādhipateyyena asantuṭṭhā, te ca uttari nissaraṇaṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānanti. Tesaṃ evaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti.

Those Brahmā deities, venerable Moggallāna, who are content with Brahmā’s lifespan, Brahmā’s complexion, Brahmā’s happiness, Brahmā’s glory, and Brahmā’s dominion do not truly understand a higher escape. They do not have this knowledge: 'with residue or without residue.' But those Brahmā deities, venerable Moggallāna, who are discontent with Brahmā’s lifespan, Brahmā’s complexion, Brahmā’s happiness, Brahmā’s glory, and Brahmā’s dominion truly understand a higher escape. They have this knowledge: 'with residue or without residue.'

‘‘Idha, mārisa moggallāna, bhikkhu ubhatobhāgavimutto hoti. Tamenaṃ te devā evaṃ jānanti – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā ubhatobhāgavimutto. Yāvassa kāyo ṭhassati tāva naṃ dakkhanti devamanussā. Kāyassa bhedā na naṃ dakkhanti devamanussā’ti. Evampi kho, mārisa moggallāna, tesaṃ devānaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti.

Here, venerable Moggallāna, a monk is liberated both ways. Those deities know him thus: 'This venerable one is liberated both ways. As long as his body endures, gods and humans will see him. Upon the breakup of the body, gods and humans will not see him.' Thus, venerable Moggallāna, those deities have the knowledge: 'with residue or without residue.'

‘‘Idha pana, mārisa moggallāna, bhikkhu paññāvimutto hoti. Tamenaṃ te devā evaṃ jānanti – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā paññāvimutto. Yāvassa kāyo ṭhassati tāva naṃ dakkhanti devamanussā. Kāyassa bhedā na naṃ dakkhanti devamanussā’ti. Evampi kho, mārisa moggallāna, tesaṃ devānaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti.

Here, venerable Moggallāna, a monk is liberated by wisdom. Those deities know him thus: 'This venerable one is liberated by wisdom. As long as his body lasts, gods and humans will see him. But after the breakup of the body, gods and humans will not see him.' Thus, venerable Moggallāna, those deities have the knowledge: 'with residue or without residue.'

‘‘Idha pana, mārisa moggallāna, bhikkhu kāyasakkhī hoti. Tamenaṃ devā evaṃ jānanti – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā kāyasakkhī. Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyā’ti. Evampi kho, mārisa moggallāna, tesaṃ devānaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti.

Here, venerable Moggallāna, a bhikkhu is a body-witness. The devas know him thus: ‘This venerable one is a body-witness. It is possible that this venerable one, frequenting suitable lodgings, associating with good friends, harmonizing the faculties, might—for the sake of which sons of good families rightly go forth from home into homelessness—realize for himself by direct knowledge here and now, that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life, and having attained to it, might dwell therein.’ Thus, venerable Moggallāna, those devas have the knowledge: ‘with residue or without residue.’

‘‘Idha pana, mārisa moggallāna, bhikkhu diṭṭhippatto hoti…pe… saddhāvimutto hoti…pe… dhammānusārī hoti. Tamenaṃ te devā evaṃ jānanti – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā dhammānusārī. Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyā’ti. Evampi kho, mārisa moggallāna, tesaṃ devānaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti.

Here, venerable Moggallāna, a monk has attained right view… or is liberated by faith… or is a follower of the Dhamma. Those deities know him thus: ‘This venerable one is a follower of the Dhamma. It is likely that this venerable one, by resorting to suitable lodgings, associating with good friends, and properly regulating the senses, will realize for himself, through direct knowledge, and abide in that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for which clansmen rightly go forth from home into homelessness.’ Thus, venerable Moggallāna, those deities have the knowledge: ‘with residue or without residue.’

Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno tissassa brahmuno bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evamevaṃ – brahmaloke antarahito gijjhakūṭe pabbate pāturahosi. Atha kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno yāvatako ahosi tissena brahmunā saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo taṃ sabbaṃ bhagavato ārocesi.

Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna, having rejoiced and approved of the words of Brahmā Tissa—just as a strong man might contract or extend his arm—vanished from the Brahmā world and reappeared on Vulture Peak. Then the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna reported to the Blessed One the entire conversation he had with Brahmā Tissa.

‘‘Na hi pana te, moggallāna, tisso brahmā sattamaṃ animittavihāriṃ puggalaṃ deseti’’. ‘‘Etassa, bhagavā, kālo; etassa, sugata, kālo! Yaṃ bhagavā sattamaṃ animittavihāriṃ puggalaṃ deseyya. Bhagavato sutvā bhikkhū dhāressantī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, moggallāna, suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā mahāmoggallāno bhagavato paccassosi. Bhagavā etadavoca –

“But, Moggallāna, Brahmā Tissa does not teach about the seventh person who dwells in the signless.” “Now is the time, Blessed One! Now is the time, Sugata! Let the Blessed One teach about the seventh person who dwells in the signless. Having heard it from the Blessed One, the monks will remember it.” “Then listen, Moggallāna, and pay close attention. I will speak.” “Yes, venerable sir,” the Venerable Mahāmoggallāna replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Idha, moggallāna, bhikkhu sabbanimittānaṃ amanasikārā animittaṃ cetosamādhiṃ upasampajja viharati. Tamenaṃ te devā evaṃ jānanti – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā sabbanimittānaṃ amanasikārā animittaṃ cetosamādhiṃ upasampajja viharati. Appeva nāma ayamāyasmā anulomikāni senāsanāni paṭisevamāno kalyāṇamitte bhajamāno indriyāni samannānayamāno – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyyā’ti. Evaṃ kho, moggallāna, tesaṃ devānaṃ ñāṇaṃ hoti – ‘saupādisese vā saupādisesoti, anupādisese vā anupādiseso’’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

Here, Moggallāna, a bhikkhu, by not attending to any signs, enters upon and dwells in the signless samādhi of mind. Those devas know him thus: ‘This venerable one, by not attending to any signs, enters upon and dwells in the signless samādhi of mind. It is possible that this venerable one, resorting to congenial abodes, associating with good friends, and harmonizing his faculties, might—for the sake of which young men of good family rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness—realize for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life, that unsurpassed culmination of the holy life, and having entered upon and dwelt in it.’ Thus, Moggallāna, those devas have the knowledge: ‘He is with residue or without residue.’ The Third.
