# AN 7.14 Puggalasuttaṃ

## AN 7.14 The Person Discourse

14. ‘‘Sattime, bhikkhave, puggalā āhuneyyā pāhuneyyā dakkhiṇeyyā añjalikaraṇīyā anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa. Katame satta? Ubhatobhāgavimutto, paññāvimutto, kāyasakkhī, diṭṭhippatto [diṭṭhappatto (ka.)], saddhāvimutto, dhammānusārī, saddhānusārī. Ime kho, bhikkhave, satta puggalā āhuneyyā pāhuneyyā dakkhiṇeyyā añjalikaraṇīyā anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassā’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

14. "bhikkhus, there are these seven individuals who are worthy of offerings, worthy of hospitality, worthy of gifts, worthy of reverence, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. What seven? One freed in both ways, one freed by wisdom, a body-witness, a view-attainer, one freed by faith, one who follows the Dhamma, and one who follows faith. These, bhikkhus, are the seven individuals who are worthy of offerings, worthy of hospitality, worthy of gifts, worthy of reverence, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world." The fourth.
