# AN 7.59 Kimilasuttaṃ

## AN 7.59 The Kimila Sutta

59. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kimilāyaṃ viharati niculavane [veḷuvane (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.) a. ni. 5.202; 7.40]. Atha kho āyasmā kimilo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā kimilo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘ko nu kho, bhante, hetu ko paccayo yena tathāgate parinibbute saddhammo na ciraṭṭhitiko hotī’’ti?

59.Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Kimilā in the Nicula Grove. Then the Venerable Kimila approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the Venerable Kimila said to the Blessed One: “What now, venerable sir, is the cause, what is the reason, why after the Tathāgata has attained final Nibbāna, the true Dhamma does not endure long?”

‘‘Idha, kimila, tathāgate parinibbute bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo upāsakā upāsikāyo satthari agāravā viharanti appatissā, dhamme agāravā viharanti appatissā, saṅghe agāravā viharanti appatissā, sikkhāya agāravā viharanti appatissā, samādhismiṃ agāravā viharanti appatissā, appamāde agāravā viharanti appatissā, paṭisanthāre agāravā viharanti appatissā. Ayaṃ kho, kimila, hetu ayaṃ paccayo yena tathāgate parinibbute saddhammo na ciraṭṭhitiko hotī’’ti.

“Here, Kimila, after the Tathāgata has attained final Nibbāna, the bhikkhus, bhikkhunīs, male lay followers, and female lay followers dwell without reverence and without deference for the Teacher. They dwell without reverence and without deference for the Dhamma. They dwell without reverence and without deference for the Saṅgha. They dwell without reverence and without deference for the training. They dwell without reverence and without deference for samādhi. They dwell without reverence and without deference for heedfulness. They dwell without reverence and without deference for cordiality. This, Kimila, is the cause, this is the reason why, after the Tathāgata has attained final Nibbāna, the true Dhamma does not endure long.”

‘‘Ko pana, bhante, hetu ko paccayo yena tathāgate parinibbute saddhammo ciraṭṭhitiko hotī’’ti? ‘‘Idha, kimila, tathāgate parinibbute bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo upāsakā upāsikāyo satthari sagāravā viharanti sappatissā, dhamme sagāravā viharanti sappatissā, saṅghe sagāravā viharanti sappatissā, sikkhāya sagāravā viharanti sappatissā, samādhismiṃ sagāravā viharanti sappatissā, appamāde sagāravā viharanti sappatissā, paṭisanthāre sagāravā viharanti sappatissā. Ayaṃ kho, kimila, hetu ayaṃ paccayo yena tathāgate parinibbute saddhammo ciraṭṭhitiko hotī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“Venerable sir, what is the cause, what is the reason, that when the Tathāgata has attained final Nibbāna, the true Dhamma remains long-lasting?”“Here, Kimila, when the Tathāgata has attained final Nibbāna, the bhikkhus, bhikkhunīs, male lay followers, and female lay followers dwell with reverence and with deference towards the Teacher; they dwell with reverence and with deference towards the Dhamma; they dwell with reverence and with deference towards the Saṅgha; they dwell with reverence and with deference towards the training; they dwell with reverence and with deference towards samādhi; they dwell with reverence and with deference towards heedfulness; they dwell with reverence and with deference towards cordiality. This, Kimila, is the cause, this is the reason, that when the Tathāgata has attained final Nibbāna, the true Dhamma remains long-lasting.” The sixth.
