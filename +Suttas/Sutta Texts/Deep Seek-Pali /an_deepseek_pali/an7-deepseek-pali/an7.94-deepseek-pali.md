# AN 7.94 Saddhammasuttaṃ

## AN 7.94 The Discourse on Righteous Practices

94. ‘‘Sattime, bhikkhave, saddhammā. Katame satta? Saddho hoti, hirīmā hoti, ottappī hoti, bahussuto hoti, āraddhavīriyo hoti, satimā hoti, paññavā hoti. Ime kho, bhikkhave, satta saddhammā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

94."Monks, there are these seven righteous practices. Which seven? One has faith, possesses moral shame, possesses moral dread, is learned, has aroused energy, is mindful, and is wise. These, monks, are the seven righteous practices." The tenth.

Samaṇavaggo navamo.

The Chapter of Ascetics, the Ninth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The Summary:

Bhikkhuṃ samaṇo brāhmaṇo, sottiyo ceva nhātako;Vedagū ariyo arahā, asaddhammā ca saddhammāti.

A monk, an ascetic, a brahmin, a scholar, and one who has bathed;
A knower of the Vedas, a noble one, an Arahant;
And unrighteous practices and righteous practices.

10. Āhuneyyavaggo

10.The Chapter on Those Worthy of Offerings

95. ‘‘Sattime, bhikkhave, puggalā āhuneyyā…pe… dakkhiṇeyyā añjalikaraṇīyā anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa. Katame satta? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo cakkhusmiṃ aniccānupassī viharati aniccasaññī aniccapaṭisaṃvedī satataṃ samitaṃ abbokiṇṇaṃ cetasā adhimuccamāno paññāya pariyogāhamāno. So āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Ayaṃ kho, bhikkhave, paṭhamo puggalo āhuneyyo pāhuneyyo…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa.

95."Monks, these seven individuals are worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverential salutation, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. Which seven? Here, monks, a certain individual ![[Satipatthanas]]."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, idhekacco puggalo cakkhusmiṃ aniccānupassī viharati aniccasaññī aniccapaṭisaṃvedī satataṃ samitaṃ abbokiṇṇaṃ cetasā adhimuccamāno paññāya pariyogāhamāno. Tassa apubbaṃ acarimaṃ āsavapariyādānañca hoti jīvitapariyādānañca. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dutiyo puggalo āhuneyyo…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa.

"Furthermore, monks, here a certain person ![[Satipatthanas]]."

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, idhekacco puggalo cakkhusmiṃ aniccānupassī viharati aniccasaññī aniccapaṭisaṃvedī satataṃ samitaṃ abbokiṇṇaṃ cetasā adhimuccamāno paññāya pariyogāhamāno. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti…pe… upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti…pe… asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti…pe… sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti…pe… uddhaṃsoto hoti akaniṭṭhagāmī. Ayaṃ, bhikkhave, sattamo puggalo āhuneyyo…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa. Ime kho, bhikkhave, satta puggalā āhuneyyā pāhuneyyā dakkhiṇeyyā añjalikaraṇīyā anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassā’’ti.

"Furthermore, monks, here a certain person ![[Satipatthanas]]. These, monks, are the seven persons worthy of offerings, worthy of hospitality, worthy of gifts, worthy of reverential salutation—an unsurpassed field of merit for the world."

96-622. Sattime, bhikkhave, puggalā āhuneyyā pāhuneyyā…pe… anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa. Katame satta? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo cakkhusmiṃ dukkhānupassī viharati…pe… cakkhusmiṃ anattānupassī viharati…pe… cakkhusmiṃ khayānupassī viharati…pe… cakkhusmiṃ vayānupassī viharati…pe… cakkhusmiṃ virāgānupassī viharati…pe… cakkhusmiṃ nirodhānupassī viharati…pe… cakkhusmiṃ paṭinissaggānupassī viharati…pe….

96-622. "Monks, these seven individuals are worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverential salutation, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. Which seven? Here, monks, a certain individual dwells contemplating suffering in the eye... contemplating non-self in the eye... contemplating destruction in the eye... contemplating vanishing in the eye... contemplating fading away in the eye... contemplating cessation in the eye... contemplating relinquishment in the eye..."

Sotasmiṃ…pe… ghānasmiṃ… jivhāya… kāyasmiṃ… manasmiṃ…pe….

Similarly with the ear... with the nose... with the tongue... with the body... with the mind...

Rūpesu…pe… saddesu… gandhesu… rasesu… phoṭṭhabbesu … dhammesu …pe….

In forms... in sounds... in smells... in tastes... in tactile objects... in mental phenomena...

Cakkhuviññāṇe…pe… sotaviññāṇe… ghānaviññāṇe… jivhāviññāṇe… kāyaviññāṇe… manoviññāṇe…pe….

In eye-consciousness... ear-consciousness... nose-consciousness... tongue-consciousness... body-consciousness... mind-consciousness...

Cakkhusamphasse…pe… sotasamphasse… ghānasamphasse… jivhāsamphasse… kāyasamphasse… manosamphasse…pe….

In eye-contact... ear-contact... nose-contact... tongue-contact... body-contact... mind-contact...

Cakkhusamphassajāya vedanāya…pe… sotasamphassajāya vedanāya… ghānasamphassajāya vedanāya… jivhāsamphassajāya vedanāya… kāyasamphassajāya vedanāya… manosamphassajāya vedanāya…pe….

In feeling born of eye-contact... feeling born of ear-contact... feeling born of nose-contact... feeling born of tongue-contact... feeling born of body-contact... feeling born of mind-contact...

Rūpasaññāya…pe… saddasaññāya… gandhasaññāya… rasasaññāya… phoṭṭhabbasaññāya… dhammasaññāya…pe….

In the perception of forms... in the perception of sounds... in the perception of smells... in the perception of tastes... in the perception of tactile sensations... in the perception of mental phenomena...

Rūpasañcetanāya…pe… saddasañcetanāya… gandhasañcetanāya… rasasañcetanāya… phoṭṭhabbasañcetanāya… dhammasañcetanāya…pe….

In intention toward forms... in intention toward sounds... in intention toward smells... in intention toward tastes... in intention toward tactile objects... in intention toward mental phenomena...

Rūpataṇhāya…pe… saddataṇhāya… gandhataṇhāya… rasataṇhāya… phoṭṭhabbataṇhāya… dhammataṇhāya…pe….

In craving for forms... in craving for sounds... in craving for smells... in craving for tastes... in craving for tactile objects... in craving for mental phenomena...

Rūpavitakke …pe… saddavitakke… gandhavitakke… rasavitakke… phoṭṭhabbavitakke… dhammavitakke…pe….

In thinking about forms... in thinking about sounds... in thinking about smells... in thinking about tastes... in thinking about tactile objects... in thinking about mental phenomena...

Rūpavicāre…pe… saddavicāre… gandhavicāre… rasavicāre… phoṭṭhabbavicāre… dhammavicāre…pe….

In examination of forms... in examination of sounds... in examination of smells... in examination of tastes... in examination of tactile objects... in examination of mental phenomena...

‘‘Pañcakkhandhe [( ) sī. syā. potthakesu natthi] …pe… rūpakkhandhe… vedanākkhandhe… saññākkhandhe… saṅkhārakkhandhe… viññāṇakkhandhe aniccānupassī viharati…pe… dukkhānupassī viharati… anattānupassī viharati… khayānupassī viharati… vayānupassī viharati… virāgānupassī viharati… nirodhānupassī viharati… paṭinissaggānupassī viharati…pe… lokassā’’ti.

In the five aggregates... the form aggregate... the feeling aggregate... the perception aggregate... the volitional formations aggregate... the consciousness aggregate, one ![[Satipatthanas]].

‘‘Chadvārārammaṇesvettha, viññāṇesu ca phassesu;Vedanāsu ca dvārassa, suttā honti visuṃ aṭṭha.

Here, regarding the six sense-doors and their objects, and consciousnesses and contacts, and feelings and the sense-doors, eight discourses are taught separately.

‘‘Saññā sañcetanā taṇhā, vitakkesu vicāre ca;Gocarassa visuṃ aṭṭha, pañcakkhandhe ca pacceke.

Perception, intention, craving, initial application, and sustained application; eight discourses are separately taught concerning their range, and individually concerning the five aggregates.

‘‘Soḷasasvettha mūlesu, aniccaṃ dukkhamanattā;Khayā vayā virāgā ca, nirodhā paṭinissaggā.

Here, in these sixteen roots, are impermanence, suffering, and non-self; destruction, vanishing, fading away, cessation, and relinquishment.

‘‘Kamaṃ aṭṭhānupassanā, yojetvāna visuṃ visuṃ;Sampiṇḍitesu sabbesu, honti pañca satāni ca;Aṭṭhavīsati suttāni, āhuneyye ca vaggike’’ [imā uddānagāthāyo sī. syā. potthakesu natthi].

The eight contemplations, applied in order to each separately; when all are combined, there are five hundred; and twenty-eight discourses in the chapter on those worthy of offerings.

Āhuneyyavaggo dasamo.

The Chapter of Those Worthy of Offerings, the Tenth.

11. Rāgapeyyālaṃ

11.The Discourse Series on Lust

623. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya satta dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame satta? Satisambojjhaṅgo…pe… upekkhāsambojjhaṅgo – rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya ime satta dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

623."Monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, seven qualities should be developed. What seven? The mindfulness enlightenment factor…pe… the equanimity enlightenment factor—monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, these seven qualities should be developed."

624. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya satta dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame satta? Aniccasaññā, anattasaññā, asubhasaññā, ādīnavasaññā, pahānasaññā, virāgasaññā, nirodhasaññā – rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya ime satta dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

624."Monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, seven qualities should be developed. What seven? The perception of impermanence, the perception of not-self, the perception of unattractiveness, the perception of danger, the perception of abandoning, the perception of detachment, the perception of cessation—monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, these seven qualities should be developed."

625. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya satta dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame satta? Asubhasaññā, maraṇasaññā, āhāre paṭikūlasaññā, sabbaloke anabhiratasaññā, aniccasaññā, anicce dukkhasaññā, dukkhe anattasaññā – rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya ime satta dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

625."Monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, seven qualities should be developed. What seven? The perception of unattractiveness, the perception of death, the perception of disgust towards food, the perception of non-delight in the entire world, the perception of impermanence, the perception of suffering in the impermanent, the perception of not-self in suffering—monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, these seven qualities should be developed."

626-652. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, pariññāya…pe… parikkhayāya… pahānāya… khayāya… vayāya… virāgāya… nirodhāya… cāgāya…pe… paṭinissaggāya ime satta dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

"Monks, for the full understanding…pe… for the destruction… for the abandonment… for the ending… for the decay… for dispassion… for the cessation… for the relinquishment…pe… for the relinquishing of lust, these seven qualities should be developed."

653-1132. ‘‘Dosassa…pe… mohassa… kodhassa… upanāhassa… makkhassa… paḷāsassa… issāya… macchariyassa… māyāya… sāṭheyyassa… thambhassa… sārambhassa… mānassa… atimānassa… madassa… pamādassa abhiññāya…pe… pariññāya… parikkhayāya… pahānāya… khayāya… vayāya… virāgāya… nirodhāya… cāgāya… paṭinissaggāya ime satta dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

"For hatred…pe… for delusion… for anger… for resentment… for denigration… for domineering… for envy… for avarice… for deceit… for craftiness… for obstinacy… for contentiousness… for conceit… for disdain… for intoxication… for negligence, for direct knowledge…pe… for full understanding… for utter destruction… for abandonment… for ending… for decay… for dispassion… for cessation… for relinquishment… for relinquishing, these seven qualities should be developed."

Idamavoca bhagavā. Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṃ abhinandunti.

This the Blessed One said. The monks, pleased, rejoiced at the Blessed One’s words.

Rāgapeyyālaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Discourse Series on Lust is concluded.

Sattakanipātapāḷi niṭṭhitā.

The Book of the Sevens is concluded.
