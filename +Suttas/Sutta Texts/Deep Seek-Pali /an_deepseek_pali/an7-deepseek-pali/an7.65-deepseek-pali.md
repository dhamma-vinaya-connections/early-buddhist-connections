# AN 7.65 Hirīottappasuttaṃ

## AN 7.65 The Discourse on Moral Shame and Moral Dread

65. [a. ni. 5.24, 168; 2.6.50] ‘‘Hirottappe, bhikkhave, asati hirottappavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti indriyasaṃvaro; indriyasaṃvare asati indriyasaṃvaravipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti sīlaṃ; sīle asati sīlavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti sammāsamādhi; sammāsamādhimhi asati sammāsamādhivipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti yathābhūtañāṇadassanaṃ; yathābhūtañāṇadassane asati yathābhūtañāṇadassanavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti nibbidāvirāgo; nibbidāvirāge asati nibbidāvirāgavipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti vimuttiñāṇadassanaṃ. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, rukkho sākhāpalāsavipanno. Tassa papaṭikāpi na pāripūriṃ gacchati, tacopi pheggupi sāropi na pāripūriṃ gacchati. Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, hirottappe asati hirottappavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti indriyasaṃvaro; indriyasaṃvare asati indriyasaṃvaravipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti sīlaṃ; sīle asati sīlavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti sammāsamādhi; sammāsamādhimhi asati sammāsamādhivipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti yathābhūtañāṇadassanaṃ; yathābhūtañāṇadassane asati yathābhūtañāṇadassanavipannassa hatūpaniso hoti nibbidāvirāgo; nibbidāvirāge asati nibbidāvirāgavipannassa hatūpanisaṃ hoti vimuttiñāṇadassanaṃ.

65.“Bhikkhus, when moral shame and moral dread are absent, one who is deficient in moral shame and moral dread lacks the support for sense restraint. When sense restraint is absent, one who is deficient in sense restraint lacks the support for virtue. When virtue is absent, one who is deficient in virtue lacks the support for right samādhi. When right samādhi is absent, one who is deficient in right samādhi lacks the support for knowledge and vision of things as they really are. When knowledge and vision of things as they really are are absent, one who is deficient in knowledge and vision of things as they really are lacks the support for disenchantment and dispassion. When disenchantment and dispassion are absent, one who is deficient in disenchantment and dispassion lacks the support for the knowledge and vision of liberation. Just as, bhikkhus, a tree deficient in branches and foliage does not have its outer bark, inner bark, sapwood, or heartwood fully developed, so too, bhikkhus, when moral shame and moral dread are absent, one who is deficient in moral shame and moral dread lacks the support for sense restraint… and thus lacks the support for the knowledge and vision of liberation.”

‘‘Hirottappe, bhikkhave, sati hirottappasampannassa upanisasampanno hoti indriyasaṃvaro; indriyasaṃvare sati indriyasaṃvarasampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti sīlaṃ; sīle sati sīlasampannassa upanisasampanno hoti sammāsamādhi; sammāsamādhimhi sati sammāsamādhisampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti yathābhūtañāṇadassanaṃ; yathābhūtañāṇadassane sati yathābhūtañāṇadassanasampannassa upanisasampanno hoti nibbidāvirāgo; nibbidāvirāge sati nibbidāvirāgasampannassa upanisasampannaṃ hoti vimuttiñāṇadassanaṃ. Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, rukkho sākhāpalāsasampanno. Tassa papaṭikāpi pāripūriṃ gacchati, tacopi pheggupi sāropi pāripūriṃ gacchati. Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, hirottappe sati hirottappasampannassa upanisasampanno hoti…pe… vimuttiñāṇadassana’’nti. Paṭhamaṃ.

“Bhikkhus, when moral shame and moral dread are present, one who is endowed with moral shame and moral dread has the proximate cause for sense restraint. When sense restraint is present, one who is endowed with sense restraint has the proximate cause for virtue. When virtue is present, one who is endowed with virtue has the proximate cause for right samādhi. When right samādhi is present, one who is endowed with right samādhi has the proximate cause for knowledge and vision of things as they really are. When knowledge and vision of things as they really are are present, one who is endowed with knowledge and vision of things as they really are has the proximate cause for disenchantment and dispassion. When disenchantment and dispassion are present, one who is endowed with disenchantment and dispassion has the proximate cause for the knowledge and vision of liberation. Just as, bhikkhus, a tree endowed with branches and foliage has its outer bark, inner bark, sapwood, and heartwood fully developed, so too, bhikkhus, when moral shame and moral dread are present, one who is endowed with moral shame and moral dread has the proximate cause for sense restraint… and thus has the proximate cause for the knowledge and vision of liberation.” This is the first.
