# AN 8.30 Anuruddhamahāvitakkasuttaṃ

## AN 8.30 The Great Thoughts Discourse of Anuruddha

30. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā bhaggesu viharati suṃsumāragire bhesakaḷāvane migadāye. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā anuruddho cetīsu viharati pācīnavaṃsadāye. Atha kho āyasmato anuruddhassa rahogatassa paṭisallīnassa evaṃ cetaso parivitakko udapādi – ‘‘appicchassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo mahicchassa; santuṭṭhassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asantuṭṭhassa; pavivittassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo saṅgaṇikārāmassa; āraddhavīriyassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo kusītassa; upaṭṭhitassatissāyaṃ [upaṭṭhitasatissāyaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)] dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo muṭṭhassatissa [muṭṭhasatissa (sī. syā. pī.)]; samāhitassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asamāhitassa; paññavato ayaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo duppaññassā’’ti.

30.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling among the Bhaggas on Suṃsumāragira in the Bhesakaḷāvana, the Deer Park. Now at that time the Venerable Anuruddha was dwelling among the Cetīs in the Eastern Bamboo Grove. Then, while the Venerable Anuruddha was alone in seclusion, the following thought arose in his mind: "This Dhamma is for one with few desires, not for one with many desires. This Dhamma is for the contented, not for the discontented. This Dhamma is for the secluded, not for one who delights in company. This Dhamma is for the energetic, not for the lazy. This Dhamma is for one with established mindfulness, not for the unmindful. This Dhamma is for the collected, not for the uncollected. This Dhamma is for the wise, not for the unwise."

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmato anuruddhassa cetasā cetoparivitakkamaññāya – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya; evamevaṃ – bhaggesu suṃsumāragire bhesakaḷāvane migadāye antarahito cetīsu pācīnavaṃsadāye āyasmato anuruddhassa sammukhe pāturahosi. Nisīdi bhagavā paññatte āsane. Āyasmāpi kho anuruddho bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ

Then the Blessed One, knowing with his mind the reflection of Venerable Anuruddha's mind, just as a strong man might extend or draw back his arm, vanished from the Bhesakaḷāvana, the Deer Park, on Suṃsumāragira among the Bhaggas and reappeared in front of Venerable Anuruddha in the Eastern Bamboo Grove among the Cetīs. The Blessed One sat down on the seat made ready. Venerable Anuruddha, after paying homage to the Blessed One, sat down to one side.

Nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ bhagavā etadavoca –

Seated, the Blessed One said to Venerable Anuruddha:

‘‘Sādhu sādhu, anuruddha! Sādhu kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, (yaṃ taṃ mahāpurisavitakkaṃ) [satta mahāpurisavitakke (sī. pī.) dī. ni. 3.358] vitakkesi – ‘appicchassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo mahicchassa; santuṭṭhassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asantuṭṭhassa; pavivittassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo saṅgaṇikārāmassa; āraddhavīriyassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo kusītassa; upaṭṭhitassatissāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo muṭṭhassatissa; samāhitassāyaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asamāhitassa; paññavato ayaṃ dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo duppaññassā’ti. Tena hi tvaṃ, anuruddha, imampi aṭṭhamaṃ mahāpurisavitakkaṃ vitakkehi – ‘nippapañcārāmassāyaṃ dhammo nippapañcaratino, nāyaṃ dhammo papañcārāmassa papañcaratino’’’ti.

"Good, good, Anuruddha! Good, Anuruddha, that you have considered this great man's thought: ‘This Dhamma is for one with few desires, not for one with many desires. This Dhamma is for the contented, not for the discontented. This Dhamma is for the secluded, not for one who delights in company. This Dhamma is for the energetic, not for the lazy. This Dhamma is for one with established mindfulness, not for the unmindful. This Dhamma is for the collected, not for the uncollected. This Dhamma is for the wise, not for the unwise.’ Well then, Anuruddha, you should also consider this eighth great man's thought: ‘This Dhamma is for one who delights in non-elaboration, who rejoices in non-elaboration, not for one who delights in elaboration, who rejoices in elaboration.’"

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, tato tvaṃ, anuruddha, yāvadeva [yāvade (saṃ. ni. 2.152)] ākaṅkhissasi, vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharissasi.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, then, Anuruddha, whenever you wish, quite secluded from sensual pleasures, secluded from unwholesome states, you will enter and dwell in the first jhāna, which is accompanied by thought and examination, born of seclusion, filled with rapture and joy.

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, tato tvaṃ, anuruddha, yāvadeva ākaṅkhissasi, vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā ajjhattaṃ sampasādanaṃ cetaso ekodibhāvaṃ avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhijaṃ pītisukhaṃ dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharissasi.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, then, whenever you wish, with the subsiding of thought and examination, you will enter and dwell in the second jhāna, which has internal assurance, unification of mind, is without thought, without examination, born of samādhi, and filled with rapture and joy."

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, tato tvaṃ, anuruddha, yāvadeva ākaṅkhissasi, pītiyā ca virāgā upekkhako ca viharissasi sato ca sampajāno sukhañca kāyena paṭisaṃvedissasi yaṃ taṃ ariyā ācikkhanti – ‘upekkhako satimā sukhavihārī’ti tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharissasi.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, then, whenever you wish, with the fading away of rapture, you will dwell equanimous and mindful, clearly comprehending, and experiencing happiness with the body—that which the noble ones describe as ‘dwelling happily with equanimity and mindfulness’—you will enter and dwell in the third jhāna."

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, tato tvaṃ, anuruddha, yāvadeva ākaṅkhissasi, sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā pubbeva somanassadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamā adukkhamasukhaṃ upekkhāsatipārisuddhiṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharissasi.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, then, whenever you wish, with the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and with the prior disappearance of joy and sorrow, you will enter and dwell in the fourth jhāna, which is neither painful nor pleasant and is purified by equanimity and mindfulness."

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime ca aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, imesañca catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī bhavissasi akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, tato tuyhaṃ, anuruddha, seyyathāpi nāma gahapatissa vā gahapatiputtassa vā nānārattānaṃ dussānaṃ dussakaraṇḍako pūro; evamevaṃ te paṃsukūlacīvaraṃ khāyissati santuṭṭhassa viharato ratiyā aparitassāya phāsuvihārāya okkamanāya nibbānassa.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, and gain at will, without trouble or difficulty, these four jhānas that are of the higher mind, pleasant abidings in this very life, then your rag-robes will be pleasing to you, just as for a householder or a householder’s son there is a chest full of cloths of various colors, as you dwell contentedly, for non-agitation, for comfortable abiding, for inclining towards Nibbāna."

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime ca aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, imesañca catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī bhavissasi akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, tato tuyhaṃ, anuruddha, seyyathāpi nāma gahapatissa vā gahapatiputtassa vā sālīnaṃ odano vicitakāḷako anekasūpo anekabyañjano; evamevaṃ te piṇḍiyālopabhojanaṃ khāyissati santuṭṭhassa viharato ratiyā aparitassāya phāsuvihārāya okkamanāya nibbānassa.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, and gain at will, without trouble or difficulty, these four jhānas that are of the higher mind, pleasant abidings in this very life, then your almsfood will be pleasing to you, just as for a householder or a householder’s son there is rice with various curries and sauces, as you dwell contentedly, for non-agitation, for comfortable abiding, for inclining towards Nibbāna."

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime ca aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, imesañca catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī bhavissasi akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, tato tuyhaṃ, anuruddha, seyyathāpi nāma gahapatissa vā gahapatiputtassa vā kūṭāgāraṃ ullittāvalittaṃ nivātaṃ phusitaggaḷaṃ pihitavātapānaṃ; evamevaṃ te rukkhamūlasenāsanaṃ khāyissati santuṭṭhassa viharato ratiyā aparitassāya phāsuvihārāya okkamanāya nibbānassa.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, and gain at will, without trouble or difficulty, these four jhānas that are of the higher mind, pleasant abidings in this very life, then your dwelling at the foot of a tree will be pleasing to you, just as for a householder or a householder’s son there is a gabled mansion, plastered inside and out, draft-free, with bolted doors and shuttered windows, as you dwell contentedly, for non-agitation, for comfortable abiding, for inclining towards Nibbāna."

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime ca aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, imesañca catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī bhavissasi akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, tato tuyhaṃ, anuruddha, seyyathāpi nāma gahapatissa vā gahapatiputtassa vā pallaṅko gonakatthato paṭikatthato paṭalikatthato kadalimigapavarapaccattharaṇo [kādali… paccattharaṇo (sī.)] sauttaracchado ubhatolohitakūpadhāno; evamevaṃ te tiṇasanthārakasayanāsanaṃ khāyissati santuṭṭhassa viharato ratiyā aparitassāya phāsuvihārāya okkamanāya nibbānassa.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, and gain at will, without trouble or difficulty, these four jhānas that are of the higher mind, pleasant abidings in this very life, then your grass and straw bedding will be pleasing to you, just as for a householder or a householder’s son there is a couch spread with a long-fleeced woolen cover, a white wool cover, an embroidered cover, a rug of antelope hides, with a canopy and red cushions at both ends, as you dwell contentedly, for non-agitation, for comfortable abiding, for inclining towards Nibbāna."

‘‘Yato kho tvaṃ, anuruddha, ime ca aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakke vitakkessasi, imesañca catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī bhavissasi akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, tato tuyhaṃ, anuruddha, seyyathāpi nāma gahapatissa vā gahapatiputtassa vā nānābhesajjāni, seyyathidaṃ – sappi navanītaṃ telaṃ madhu phāṇitaṃ; evamevaṃ te pūtimuttabhesajjaṃ khāyissati santuṭṭhassa viharato ratiyā aparitassāya phāsuvihārāya okkamanāya nibbānassa. Tena hi tvaṃ, anuruddha, āyatikampi vassāvāsaṃ idheva cetīsu pācīnavaṃsadāye vihareyyāsī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho āyasmā anuruddho bhagavato paccassosi.

"When you, Anuruddha, cultivate these eight great man's thoughts, and gain at will, without trouble or difficulty, these four jhānas that are of the higher mind, pleasant abidings in this very life, then your medicine of putrid urine will be pleasing to you, just as for a householder or a householder’s son there are various medicines—such as ghee, fresh butter, oil, honey, and molasses—as you dwell contentedly, for non-agitation, for comfortable abiding, for inclining towards Nibbāna. Therefore, Anuruddha, even in the future, you should spend the rainy season right here among the Cetīs in the Eastern Bamboo Grove.” “Yes, Bhante,” Venerable Anuruddha replied to the Blessed One.

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ anuruddhaṃ iminā ovādena ovaditvā – seyyathāpi nāma balavā puriso samiñjitaṃ vā bāhaṃ pasāreyya, pasāritaṃ vā bāhaṃ samiñjeyya, evamevaṃ – cetīsu pācīnavaṃsadāye antarahito bhaggesu suṃsumāragire bhesakaḷāvane migadāye pāturahosīti. Nisīdi bhagavā paññatte āsane. Nisajja kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘aṭṭha kho, bhikkhave, mahāpurisavitakke desessāmi, taṃ suṇātha…pe… katame ca, bhikkhave, aṭṭha mahāpurisavitakkā? Appicchassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo mahicchassa; santuṭṭhassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asantuṭṭhassa; pavivittassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo saṅgaṇikārāmassa; āraddhavīriyassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo kusītassa; upaṭṭhitassatissāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo muṭṭhassatissa; samāhitassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asamāhitassa; paññavato ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo duppaññassa; nippapañcārāmassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo nippapañcaratino, nāyaṃ dhammo papañcārāmassa papañcaratino’’.

Then the Blessed One, having exhorted Venerable Anuruddha with this advice—just as a strong man might bend his arm or stretch it out again—disappeared from the Eastern Bamboo Grove among the Cetīs and reappeared among the Bhaggas on Suṃsumāragira in the Bhesakaḷāvana, the Deer Park. The Blessed One sat down on the seat made ready. Having sat down, the Blessed One addressed the monks: "Monks, I will teach you the eight great man's thoughts. Listen and attend closely… And what, monks, are the eight great man's thoughts? This Dhamma is for one with few desires, not for one with many desires. This Dhamma is for the contented, not for the discontented. This Dhamma is for the secluded, not for one who delights in company. This Dhamma is for the energetic, not for the lazy. This Dhamma is for one with established mindfulness, not for the unmindful. This Dhamma is for the collected, not for the uncollected. This Dhamma is for the wise, not for the unwise. This Dhamma is for one who delights in non-elaboration, who rejoices in non-elaboration, not for one who delights in elaboration, who rejoices in elaboration."

‘‘‘Appicchassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo mahicchassā’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu appiccho samāno ‘appicchoti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati, santuṭṭho samāno ‘santuṭṭhoti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati, pavivitto samāno ‘pavivittoti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati, āraddhavīriyo samāno ‘āraddhavīriyoti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati, upaṭṭhitassati samāno ‘upaṭṭhitassatīti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati, samāhito samāno ‘samāhitoti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati, paññavā samāno ‘paññavāti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati, nippapañcārāmo samāno ‘nippapañcārāmoti maṃ jāneyyu’nti na icchati. ‘Appicchassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo mahicchassā’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one of few desires, not for one of many desires’—so it was said. And with reference to what was this said? Here, monks, a monk, being one of few desires, does not wish that they would know him as one of few desires; being content, he does not wish that they would know him as content; being secluded, he does not wish that they would know him as secluded; being energetic, he does not wish that they would know him as energetic; being mindful, he does not wish that they would know him as mindful; being collected, he does not wish that they would know him as collected; being wise, he does not wish that they would know him as wise; delighting in freedom from proliferation, he does not wish that they would know him as one who delights in freedom from proliferation. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one of few desires, not for one of many desires.’"

‘‘‘Santuṭṭhassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asantuṭṭhassā’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ, kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu santuṭṭho hoti itarītaracīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārena. ‘Santuṭṭhassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asantuṭṭhassā’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for the contented, not for the discontented.’ So it was said. With reference to what was this said? Here, monks, a monk is contented with any kind of robe, almsfood, lodging, and medicine for the sick. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for the contented, not for the discontented.’"

‘‘‘Pavivittassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo saṅgaṇikārāmassā’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ, kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno pavivittassa viharato bhavanti upasaṅkamitāro bhikkhū bhikkhuniyo upāsakā upāsikāyo rājāno rājamahāmattā titthiyā titthiyasāvakā. Tatra bhikkhu vivekaninnena cittena vivekapoṇena vivekapabbhārena vivekaṭṭhena nekkhammābhiratena aññadatthu uyyojanikapaṭisaṃyuttaṃyeva kathaṃ kattā [pavattā (ka.)] hoti. ‘Pavivittassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo saṅgaṇikārāmassā’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who is secluded, not for one who delights in company.’ That was said. But with reference to what was it said? Here, monks, when a monk is living in seclusion, monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen, kings, royal ministers, sectarians, and their disciples come to him. With a mind inclined to seclusion, bent on seclusion, directed to seclusion, delighting in renunciation and devoted to it, he only engages in talk connected with dismissing them. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who is secluded, not for one who delights in company.’"

‘‘‘Āraddhavīriyassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo kusītassā’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ, kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu āraddhavīriyo viharati akusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ pahānāya kusalānaṃ dhammānaṃ upasampadāya thāmavā daḷhaparakkamo anikkhittadhuro kusalesu dhammesu. ‘Āraddhavīriyassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo kusītassā’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who has aroused energy, not for the lazy.’ Thus was it said. But with reference to what was it said? Here, monks, a bhikkhu dwells with aroused energy for abandoning unwholesome qualities and attaining wholesome qualities. He is firm, strong in effort, not shirking his duty in regard to wholesome qualities. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who has aroused energy, not for the lazy.’"

‘‘‘Upaṭṭhitassatissāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo muṭṭhassatissā’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu satimā hoti paramena satinepakkena samannāgato, cirakatampi cirabhāsitampi saritā anussaritā. ‘Upaṭṭhitassatissāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo, muṭṭhassatissā’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one whose mindfulness is established, not for one whose mindfulness is muddled.’ Thus it was said. But with reference to what was this said? Here, monks, a monk is mindful, possessing supreme mindfulness and skill, remembering and recollecting what was done and what was spoken long ago. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one whose mindfulness is established, not for one whose mindfulness is muddled.’"

‘‘‘Samāhitassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asamāhitassā’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu vivicceva kāmehi…pe… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. ‘Samāhitassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo asamāhitassā’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who is collected, not for one who is uncollected,’ so it was said. But with reference to what was this said? Here, monks, a monk, quite secluded from sensual pleasures… enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who is collected, not for one who is uncollected.’"

‘‘‘Paññavato ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo duppaññassā’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu paññavā hoti udayatthagāminiyā paññāya samannāgato ariyāya nibbedhikāya sammā dukkhakkhayagāminiyā. ‘Paññavato ayaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nāyaṃ dhammo duppaññassā’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for the wise, not for the unwise.’ Thus was it said. But with reference to what was it said? Here, monks, a monk is wise, endowed with wisdom that goes to the arising and passing away, noble, penetrative, leading to the complete destruction of suffering. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for the wise, not for the unwise.’"

‘‘‘Nippapañcārāmassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo nippapañcaratino, nāyaṃ dhammo papañcārāmassa papañcaratino’ti, iti kho panetaṃ vuttaṃ. Kiñcetaṃ paṭicca vuttaṃ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhuno papañcanirodhe cittaṃ pakkhandati pasīdati santiṭṭhati vimuccati. ‘Nippapañcārāmassāyaṃ, bhikkhave, dhammo, nippapañcaratino, nāyaṃ dhammo papañcārāmassa papañcaratino’ti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ idametaṃ paṭicca vutta’’nti.

"‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who delights in non-proliferation, who rejoices in non-proliferation, not for one who delights in proliferation, who rejoices in proliferation.’ So it was said. But with reference to what was it said? Here, monks, a monk's mind inclines, clears, settles, and is liberated in the cessation of proliferation. It was with reference to this that it was said: ‘This Dhamma, monks, is for one who delights in non-proliferation, who rejoices in non-proliferation, not for one who delights in proliferation, who rejoices in proliferation.’"

Atha kho āyasmā anuruddho āyatikampi vassāvāsaṃ tattheva cetīsu pācīnavaṃsadāye vihāsi. Atha kho āyasmā anuruddho eko vūpakaṭṭho appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto nacirasseva – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti, tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihāsi. ‘‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’’ti abbhaññāsi. Aññataro ca panāyasmā anuruddho arahataṃ ahosīti. Atha kho āyasmā anuruddho arahattappatto tāyaṃ velāyaṃ imā gāthāyo abhāsi –

Then the Venerable Anuruddha, having spent the next rains-residence right there in the Eastern Bamboo Grove among the Cetis, dwelling alone, secluded, heedful, ardent, and resolute, before long realized for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life, the unsurpassed culmination of the holy life, for which clansmen rightly go forth from home into homelessness, and having attained it, he remained there. He understood: "Destroyed is birth, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more for this state of being." And so the Venerable Anuruddha became one of the arahants. Then the Venerable Anuruddha, having attained arahantship, at that time uttered these verses:

[theragā. 901-903] ‘‘Mama saṅkappamaññāya, satthā loke anuttaro;Manomayena kāyena, iddhiyā upasaṅkami.

"Knowing my thought, the Teacher, unsurpassed in the world, approached me with a mind-made body, by means of psychic power."

‘‘Yathā me ahu saṅkappo, tato uttari desayi;Nippapañcarato buddho, nippapañcaṃ adesayi.

"As was my thought, so he explained beyond;
The Buddha delights in freedom from proliferation, he taught freedom from proliferation."

‘‘Tassāhaṃ dhammamaññāya, vihāsiṃ sāsane rato;Tisso vijjā anuppattā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsana’’nti. dasamaṃ;

"Having understood his Dhamma, I lived delighting in the teaching. The three knowledges have been attained, the Buddha's teaching is done." Tenth.

Gahapativaggo tatiyo.

The Householder Chapter, the Third.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Thus, its summary:

Dve uggā dve ca hatthakā, mahānāmena jīvako;Dve balā akkhaṇā vuttā, anuruddhena te dasāti.

Two Uggas, two Hatthakas, and Jīvaka of Great Name; two powers, and the inopportune moments: these ten were declared by Anuruddha.
