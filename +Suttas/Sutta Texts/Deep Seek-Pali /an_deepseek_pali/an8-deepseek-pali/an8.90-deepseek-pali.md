# AN 8.90 Sammāvattanasuttaṃ

## AN 8.90 The Discourse on Proper Conduct

90. [cūḷava. 211] ‘‘Tassapāpiyasikakammakatena, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā aṭṭhasu dhammesu sammā vattitabbaṃ – na upasampādetabbo, na nissayo dātabbo, na sāmaṇero upaṭṭhāpetabbo, na bhikkhunovādakasammuti sāditabbā, sammatenapi bhikkhuniyo na ovaditabbā, na kāci saṅghasammuti sāditabbā, na kismiñci paccekaṭṭhāne ṭhapetabbo, na ca tena mūlena vuṭṭhāpetabbo. Tassapāpiyasikakammakatena, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā imesu aṭṭhasu dhammesu sammā vattitabba’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

90."Monks, a monk subjected to a further-punishment act should properly conduct himself in eight matters: he should not confer ordination, he should not give dependence, he should not have a novice attend on him, he should not accept appointment as a preceptor of nuns, even if appointed he should not instruct nuns, he should not accept any formal act of the Saṅgha, he should not be placed in any position of authority, and he should not be reinstated on the original grounds. Monks, a monk subjected to a further-punishment act should properly conduct himself in these eight matters." The tenth.

Sativaggo catuttho.

The Chapter on Mindfulness is the Fourth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Thus, its summary:

Satipuṇṇiyamūlena, corasamaṇena pañcamaṃ;Yaso pattappasādena, paṭisāraṇīyañca vattananti.

With Satipuṇṇa at the root, the fifth with the thief-monk; Yasa, confidence in the bowl, the act of reconciliation, and conduct.

(10) 5. Sāmaññavaggo

(10) 5. The Chapter on the Life of a Recluse

The Second Fifty is finished.

(11). Rāgapeyyālaṃ

(11). Repetition Section on Passion

117. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame aṭṭha? Sammādiṭṭhi, sammāsaṅkappo, sammāvācā, sammākammanto, sammāājīvo, sammāvāyāmo, sammāsati, sammāsamādhi – rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya ime aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

117."Monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, eight things are to be developed. What eight? Right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samādhi—monks, for the direct knowledge of lust, these eight things are to be developed."

118. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame aṭṭha? Ajjhattaṃ rūpasaññī bahiddhā rūpāni passati parittāni suvaṇṇadubbaṇṇāni, tāni abhibhuyya ‘jānāmi passāmī’ti evaṃsaññī hoti. Ajjhattaṃ rūpasaññī bahiddhā rūpāni passati appamāṇāni suvaṇṇadubbaṇṇāni, tāni abhibhuyya ‘jānāmi passāmī’ti evaṃsaññī hoti. Ajjhattaṃ arūpasaññī bahiddhā rūpāni passati parittāni suvaṇṇadubbaṇṇāni, tāni abhibhuyya ‘jānāmi passāmī’ti evaṃsaññī hoti. Ajjhattaṃ arūpasaññī bahiddhā rūpāni passati appamāṇāni suvaṇṇadubbaṇṇāni, tāni abhibhuyya ‘jānāmi passāmī’ti evaṃsaññī hoti. Ajjhattaṃ arūpasaññī bahiddhā rūpāni passati nīlāni nīlavaṇṇāni nīlanidassanāni nīlanibhāsāni pītāni pītavaṇṇāni…pe… lohitakāni lohitakavaṇṇāni…pe… odātāni odātavaṇṇāni…pe… odātanibhāsāni, tāni abhibhuyya ‘jānāmi passāmī’ti evaṃsaññī hoti – rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya ime aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā’’.

118."Monks, for the full understanding of lust, eight things should be developed. What eight? Perceiving form internally, one sees forms externally—limited, beautiful and ugly, having overcome them, one is aware: ‘I know and see.’ Perceiving form internally, one sees forms externally—unlimited, beautiful and ugly, having overcome them, one is aware: ‘I know and see.’ Not perceiving form internally, one sees forms externally—limited, beautiful and ugly, having overcome them, one is aware: ‘I know and see.’ Not perceiving form internally, one sees forms externally—unlimited, beautiful and ugly, having overcome them, one is aware: ‘I know and see.’ Not perceiving form internally, one sees forms externally—blue, blue in color, blue in appearance, blue in radiance; yellow, yellow in color… red, red in color… white, white in color, white in appearance, white in radiance, having overcome them, one is aware: ‘I know and see.’ Monks, for the full understanding of lust, these eight things should be developed."

119. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame aṭṭha? Rūpī rūpāni passati, ajjhattaṃ arūpasaññī bahiddhā rūpāni passati, subhanteva adhimutto hoti, sabbaso rūpasaññānaṃ samatikkamā paṭighasaññānaṃ atthaṅgamā nānattasaññānaṃ amanasikārā ‘ananto ākāso’ti ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati, sabbaso ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘anantaṃ viññāṇa’nti viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati, sabbaso viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘natthi kiñcī’ti ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati, sabbaso ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ samatikkamma nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati, sabbaso nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ samatikkamma saññāvedayitanirodhaṃ upasampajja viharati – rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya ime aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā’’.

119."Monks, for the full knowledge of lust, eight things should be developed. What eight? A person with form perceives forms. Not perceiving form internally, one sees forms externally. One is intent only on the beautiful. With the complete transcending of perceptions of form, with the disappearance of perceptions of aversion, not attending to perceptions of diversity, one enters and dwells in the dimension of infinite space, thinking: ‘Space is infinite.’ Having completely transcended the dimension of infinite space, one enters and dwells in the dimension of infinite consciousness, thinking: ‘Consciousness is infinite.’ Having completely transcended the dimension of infinite consciousness, one enters and dwells in the dimension of nothingness, thinking: ‘There is nothing.’ Having completely transcended the dimension of nothingness, one enters and dwells in the dimension of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. Having completely transcended the dimension of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, one enters and dwells in the cessation of perception and feeling. Monks, for the full knowledge of lust, these eight things should be developed."

120-146. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, pariññāya…pe… parikkhayāya… pahānāya… khayāya… vayāya… virāgāya… nirodhāya… cāgāya… paṭinissaggāya…pe… ime aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā’’.

"Monks, for the full understanding of lust... for the destruction... for the abandoning... for the wearing away... for the fading away... for dispassion... for the cessation... for the giving up... for the relinquishment... these eight things should be developed."

147-626. ‘‘Dosassa…pe… mohassa… kodhassa… upanāhassa… makkhassa… paḷāsassa… issāya… macchariyassa … māyāya… sāṭheyyassa… thambhassa… sārambhassa… mānassa… atimānassa… madassa… pamādassa abhiññāya…pe… pariññāya… parikkhayāya… pahānāya… khayāya… vayāya… virāgāya… nirodhāya… cāgāya… paṭinissaggāya…pe… ime aṭṭha dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

147-626. "For hatred… for delusion… for anger… for resentment… for denigration… for domineering… for envy… for avarice… for deceit… for craftiness… for obstinacy… for contentiousness… for conceit… for excessive conceit… for intoxication… for negligence… for direct knowledge… for full understanding… for destruction… for abandoning… for wearing away… for fading away… for dispassion… for cessation… for giving up… for relinquishment… these eight things should be developed."

Rāgapeyyālaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Repetition Section on Passion is finished.

Aṭṭhakanipātapāḷi niṭṭhitā.

The Book of the Eights is finished.
