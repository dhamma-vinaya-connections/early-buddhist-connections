# AN 8.34 Khettasuttaṃ

## AN 8.34 The Field Discourse

34. ‘‘Aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgate, bhikkhave, khette bījaṃ vuttaṃ na mahapphalaṃ hoti na mahassādaṃ na phātiseyyaṃ [na phātiseyyanti (sī. syā. ka.), na phātiseyyā (katthaci)]. Kathaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgate? Idha, bhikkhave, khettaṃ unnāmaninnāmi ca hoti, pāsāṇasakkharikañca hoti, ūsarañca hoti, na ca gambhīrasitaṃ hoti, na āyasampannaṃ hoti, na apāyasampannaṃ hoti, na mātikāsampannaṃ hoti, na mariyādasampannaṃ hoti. Evaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgate, bhikkhave, khette bījaṃ vuttaṃ na mahapphalaṃ hoti na mahassādaṃ na phātiseyyaṃ.

34."Bhikkhus, seed sown in a field endowed with eight factors does not yield great fruit, is not very tasty, and does not thrive. How is it endowed with eight factors? Here, bhikkhus, the field is uneven, full of stones and gravel, barren, not deeply ploughed, not well-watered, not well-drained, not possessing irrigation channels, and not well-demarcated. Thus, bhikkhus, seed sown in a field endowed with these eight factors does not yield great fruit, is not very tasty, and does not thrive."

‘‘Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgatesu samaṇabrāhmaṇesu dānaṃ dinnaṃ na mahapphalaṃ hoti na mahānisaṃsaṃ na mahājutikaṃ na mahāvipphāraṃ. Kathaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgatesu? Idha, bhikkhave, samaṇabrāhmaṇā micchādiṭṭhikā honti, micchāsaṅkappā, micchāvācā, micchākammantā, micchāājīvā, micchāvāyāmā, micchāsatino, micchāsamādhino. Evaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgatesu, bhikkhave, samaṇabrāhmaṇesu dānaṃ dinnaṃ na mahapphalaṃ hoti na mahānisaṃsaṃ na mahājutikaṃ na mahāvipphāraṃ.

"Just so, bhikkhus, when ascetics and brahmins are endowed with eight factors, a gift given to them is not of great fruit, not of great benefit, not of great radiance, not of great extent. How are they endowed with eight factors? Here, bhikkhus, ascetics and brahmins have wrong view, wrong intention, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, wrong samādhi. When ascetics and brahmins are thus endowed with eight factors, bhikkhus, a gift given to them is not of great fruit, not of great benefit, not of great radiance, not of great extent."

‘‘Aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgate, bhikkhave, khette bījaṃ vuttaṃ mahapphalaṃ hoti mahassādaṃ phātiseyyaṃ. Kathaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgate? Idha, bhikkhave, khettaṃ anunnāmāninnāmi ca hoti, apāsāṇasakkharikañca hoti, anūsarañca hoti, gambhīrasitaṃ hoti, āyasampannaṃ hoti, apāyasampannaṃ hoti, mātikāsampannaṃ hoti, mariyādasampannaṃ hoti. Evaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgate, bhikkhave, khette bījaṃ vuttaṃ mahapphalaṃ hoti mahassādaṃ phātiseyyaṃ.

"Bhikkhus, a seed sown in a field endowed with eight factors yields great fruit, is rich in flavor, and leads to flourishing. How is it endowed with eight factors? Here, bhikkhus, the field is neither too high nor too low-lying, free from stones and gravel, without salt-licks, deeply ploughed, possesses irrigation, possesses drainage, possesses irrigation channels, and possesses boundaries. Thus, bhikkhus, a seed sown in a field endowed with these eight factors yields great fruit, is rich in flavor, and leads to flourishing."

‘‘Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgatesu samaṇabrāhmaṇesu dānaṃ dinnaṃ mahapphalaṃ hoti mahānisaṃsaṃ mahājutikaṃ mahāvipphāraṃ. Kathaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgatesu? Idha, bhikkhave, samaṇabrāhmaṇā sammādiṭṭhikā honti, sammāsaṅkappā, sammāvācā, sammākammantā, sammāājīvā, sammāvāyāmā, sammāsatino, sammāsamādhino. Evaṃ aṭṭhaṅgasamannāgatesu, bhikkhave, samaṇabrāhmaṇesu dānaṃ dinnaṃ mahapphalaṃ hoti mahānisaṃsaṃ mahājutikaṃ mahāvipphāra’’nti.

"Just so, bhikkhus, a gift given to ascetics and brahmins possessed of eight factors is of great fruit, great benefit, great radiance, and great extent. Possessed of which eight factors? Here, bhikkhus, ascetics and brahmins possess right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right samādhi. Thus, bhikkhus, a gift given to ascetics and brahmins possessed of these eight factors is of great fruit, great benefit, great radiance, and great extent."

‘‘Yathāpi khette sampanne, pavuttā bījasampadā;Deve sampādayantamhi [sañjāyantamhi (ka.)], hoti dhaññassa sampadā.

Just as in a well-prepared field, excellent seeds are sown, and when the rain-god provides ample rain, there is an abundance of grain.

‘‘Anītisampadā hoti, virūḷhī bhavati sampadā;Vepullasampadā hoti, phalaṃ ve hoti sampadā.

Freedom from calamity is an accomplishment; growth is an accomplishment; abundance is an accomplishment; and fruit is indeed an accomplishment.

‘‘Evaṃ sampannasīlesu, dinnā bhojanasampadā;Sampadānaṃ upaneti, sampannaṃ hissa taṃ kataṃ.

Thus, food given to those accomplished in virtue leads to accomplishment; for that deed is accomplished for him.

‘‘Tasmā sampadamākaṅkhī, sampannatthūdha puggalo;Sampannapaññe sevetha, evaṃ ijjhanti sampadā.

Therefore, a person here desiring accomplishment should associate with those accomplished in wisdom; thus accomplishments are achieved.

‘‘Vijjācaraṇasampanne, laddhā cittassa sampadaṃ;Karoti kammasampadaṃ, labhati catthasampadaṃ.

Endowed with knowledge and conduct, having obtained the accomplishment of the mind; One performs the accomplishment of deeds, and obtains the accomplishment of purpose.

‘‘Lokaṃ ñatvā yathābhūtaṃ, pappuyya diṭṭhisampadaṃ;Maggasampadamāgamma, yāti sampannamānaso.

Having known the world as it actually is, having attained the accomplishment of view; having come to the accomplishment of the path, one goes to arahantship with a perfected mind.

‘‘Odhunitvā malaṃ sabbaṃ, patvā nibbānasampadaṃ;Muccati sabbadukkhehi, sā hoti sabbasampadā’’ti. catutthaṃ;

Having shaken off all defilements, having attained the accomplishment of Nibbāna, one is freed from all suffering; that is the ultimate accomplishment." Fourth.
