# AN 8.63 Saṃkhittasuttaṃ

## AN 8.63 The Brief Discourse

63. Atha kho aññataro bhikkhu yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami…pe… ekamantaṃ nisinno kho so bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘sādhu me, bhante, bhagavā saṃkhittena dhammaṃ desetu, yamahaṃ bhagavato dhammaṃ sutvā eko vūpakaṭṭho appamatto ātāpī pahitatto vihareyya’’nti. ‘‘Evamevaṃ panidhekacce moghapurisā mamaññeva ajjhesanti. Dhamme ca bhāsite mamaññeva anubandhitabbaṃ maññantī’’ti. ‘‘Desetu me, bhante, bhagavā saṃkhittena dhammaṃ, desetu sugato saṃkhittena dhammaṃ. Appeva nāmāhaṃ bhagavato bhāsitassa atthaṃ ājāneyyaṃ, appeva nāmāhaṃ bhagavato bhāsitassa dāyādo assa’’nti. ‘‘Tasmātiha te, bhikkhu evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘ajjhattaṃ me cittaṃ ṭhitaṃ bhavissati susaṇṭhitaṃ, na ca uppannā pāpakā akusalā dhammā cittaṃ pariyādāya ṭhassantī’ti. Evañhi te, bhikkhu, sikkhitabbaṃ’’.

63.Then a certain monk approached the Blessed One… and sat down to one side. Seated there, the monk said to the Blessed One: “It would be good, venerable sir, if the Blessed One would teach me the Dhamma in brief, so that, having heard the Dhamma from the Blessed One, I might dwell alone, withdrawn, heedful, ardent, and resolute.”“Even so, some worthless men here only importune me. And when the Dhamma is spoken, they think they should follow me alone.”“Let the Blessed One teach me the Dhamma in brief! Let the Well-Gone One teach the Dhamma in brief! Perhaps I may understand the meaning of the Blessed One’s words; perhaps I may be an heir to the Blessed One’s teaching.”“Therefore, monk, you should train yourself thus: ‘My mind will be inwardly steady and well-established, and unwholesome states that have arisen will not remain overpowering my mind.’ That is how you should train yourself.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ajjhattaṃ cittaṃ ṭhitaṃ hoti susaṇṭhitaṃ, na ca uppannā pāpakā akusalā dhammā cittaṃ pariyādāya tiṭṭhanti, tato te, bhikkhu, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘mettā me cetovimutti bhāvitā bhavissati bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā’ti. Evañhi te, bhikkhu, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Monk, when your mind is inwardly steady and well-established, and unwholesome states that have arisen do not remain overpowering the mind, then you should train yourself thus: ‘My liberation of mind by loving-kindness will be developed, cultivated, made a vehicle, made a basis, established, familiarized, and well-undertaken.’ That is how you should train yourself, monk.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti bahulīkato, tato tvaṃ, bhikkhu, imaṃ samādhiṃ savitakkampi savicāraṃ [savitakkasavicārampi (ka.)] bhāveyyāsi, avitakkampi vicāramattaṃ [avitakkavicāramattampi (ka.) visuddhi. 1.271 passitabbaṃ] bhāveyyāsi, avitakkampi avicāraṃ [avitakkaavicārampi (ka.)] bhāveyyāsi, sappītikampi bhāveyyāsi, nippītikampi bhāveyyāsi, sātasahagatampi bhāveyyāsi, upekkhāsahagatampi bhāveyyāsi.

“Monk, when this samādhi of yours has been thus developed and cultivated, then you should develop this samādhi with initial application and sustained application; you should develop it without initial application, with only sustained application; you should develop it without initial application and sustained application; you should develop it with rapture; you should develop it without rapture; you should develop it with happiness; you should develop it with equanimity.

‘‘Yato kho, te bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti subhāvito, tato te, bhikkhu, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘karuṇā me cetovimutti… muditā me cetovimutti… upekkhā me cetovimutti bhāvitā bhavissati bahulīkatā yānīkatā vatthukatā anuṭṭhitā paricitā susamāraddhā’ti. Evañhi te, bhikkhu, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Monk, when this samādhi has been thus developed and well-developed, then you should train yourself thus: ‘My liberation of mind by compassion… my liberation of mind by sympathetic joy… my liberation of mind by equanimity will be developed, cultivated, made a vehicle, made a basis, established, familiarized, and well-undertaken.’ That is how you should train yourself.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti subhāvito, tato tvaṃ, bhikkhu, imaṃ samādhiṃ savitakkasavicārampi bhāveyyāsi, avitakkavicāramattampi bhāveyyāsi, avitakkaavicārampi bhāveyyāsi, sappītikampi bhāveyyāsi, nippītikampi bhāveyyāsi, sātasahagatampi bhāveyyāsi, upekkhāsahagatampi bhāveyyāsi.

“Monk, when this samādhi of yours has been thus developed and well-developed, then you should develop this samādhi with initial application and sustained application; you should develop it without initial application, with only sustained application; you should develop it without initial application and sustained application; you should develop it with rapture; you should develop it without rapture; you should develop it with happiness; you should develop it with equanimity.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti subhāvito, tato te, bhikkhu, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘kāye kāyānupassī viharissāmi ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassa’nti. Evañhi te, bhikkhu, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Monk, when this samādhi has been thus developed and well-developed, then you should train yourself thus: ‘I will dwell contemplating the body in the body, ardent, fully knowing, mindful, having removed covetousness and distress in the world.’ That is how you should train yourself, monk.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti bahulīkato, tato tvaṃ, bhikkhu, imaṃ samādhiṃ savitakkasavicārampi bhāveyyāsi, avitakkavicāramattampi bhāveyyāsi, avitakkaavicārampi bhāveyyāsi, sappītikampi bhāveyyāsi, nippītikampi bhāveyyāsi, sātasahagatampi bhāveyyāsi, upekkhāsahagatampi bhāveyyāsi.

“Monk, when this samādhi has been thus developed and cultivated, then you should develop this samādhi with initial application and sustained application; without initial application, with only sustained application; without initial application and sustained application; with rapture; without rapture; with happiness; with equanimity.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti subhāvito, tato te, bhikkhu, evaṃ sikkhitabbaṃ – ‘vedanāsu vedanānupassī viharissāmi ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassa’nti; citte cittānupassī viharissāmi ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassa’nti; dhammesu dhammānupassī viharissāmi ātāpī sampajāno satimā, vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassa’nti. Evañhi te, bhikkhu, sikkhitabbaṃ.

“Monk, when this samādhi has been thus developed and well-developed, then you should train yourself thus: ‘I will dwell contemplating feelings in feelings, ardent, fully knowing, mindful, having removed covetousness and distress in the world’; ‘I will dwell contemplating mind in mind, ardent, fully knowing, mindful, having removed covetousness and distress in the world’; ‘I will dwell contemplating phenomena in phenomena, ardent, fully knowing, mindful, having removed covetousness and distress in the world.’ That is how you should train yourself, monk.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti bahulīkato, tato tvaṃ, bhikkhu, imaṃ samādhiṃ savitakkasavicārampi bhāveyyāsi, avitakkavicāramattampi bhāveyyāsi, avitakkaavicārampi bhāveyyāsi, sappītikampi bhāveyyāsi, nippītikampi bhāveyyāsi, sātasahagatampi bhāveyyāsi, upekkhāsahagatampi bhāveyyāsi.

“Monk, when this samādhi has been thus developed and cultivated, then you should develop this samādhi with initial application and sustained application; without initial application, with only sustained application; without initial application and sustained application; with rapture; without rapture; with happiness; with equanimity.

‘‘Yato kho te, bhikkhu, ayaṃ samādhi evaṃ bhāvito hoti subhāvito, tato tvaṃ, bhikkhu, yena yeneva gagghasi phāsuṃyeva gagghasi, yattha yattha ṭhassasi phāsuṃyeva ṭhassasi, yattha yattha nisīdissasi phāsuṃyeva nisīdissasi, yattha yattha seyyaṃ kappessasi phāsuṃyeva seyyaṃ kappessasī’’ti.

“Monk, when this samādhi has been thus developed and well-developed, then wherever you go, you will go at ease; wherever you stand, you will stand at ease; wherever you sit, you will sit at ease; wherever you lie down, you will lie down at ease.”

Atha kho so bhikkhu bhagavatā iminā ovādena ovadito uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmi. Atha kho so bhikkhu eko vūpakaṭṭho appamatto ātāpī pahitatto viharanto nacirasseva – yassatthāya kulaputtā sammadeva agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajanti, tadanuttaraṃ – brahmacariyapariyosānaṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja vihāsi. ‘‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’’ti abbhaññāsi. Aññataro ca pana so bhikkhu arahataṃ ahosīti. Tatiyaṃ.

Then that monk, having been exhorted by the Blessed One with this instruction, rose from his seat, paid homage to the Blessed One, circumambulated him and departed. Then that monk, dwelling alone, secluded, heedful, ardent, and resolute, not long after, having realized for himself with direct knowledge in this very life the unsurpassed goal of the holy life, for the sake of which clansmen rightly go forth from the household life into homelessness, entered upon and dwelled in it. He directly knew: “Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more for this state of being.” And that monk became an arahant. This is the third.
