81."Bhikkhus, when mindfulness and clear comprehension are absent, for one deficient in mindfulness and clear comprehension, moral shame and moral dread are lost. When moral shame and moral dread are absent, for one deficient in moral shame and moral dread, sense restraint is lost. When sense restraint is absent, for one deficient in sense restraint, virtue is lost. When virtue is absent, for one deficient in virtue, right concentration is lost. When right concentration is absent, for one deficient in right concentration, knowledge and vision of things as they really are are lost. When knowledge and vision of things as they really are are absent, for one deficient in knowledge and vision of things as they really are, disenchantment and dispassion are lost. When disenchantment and dispassion are absent, for one deficient in disenchantment and dispassion, the knowledge and vision of liberation are lost. Just as, bhikkhus, a tree deficient in branches and foliage, its outer bark does not reach fullness, nor its inner bark, nor its sapwood, nor its heartwood. In the same way, bhikkhus, when mindfulness and clear comprehension are absent, for one deficient in mindfulness and clear comprehension, moral shame and moral dread are lost... the knowledge and vision of liberation are lost."

"Mindfulness and clear comprehension, bhikkhus, for one accomplished in mindfulness and clear comprehension, the supporting condition is fulfilled for moral shame and moral dread. When moral shame and moral dread are fulfilled, for one accomplished in moral shame and moral dread, the supporting condition is fulfilled for sense restraint. When virtue is fulfilled, for one accomplished in virtue, the supporting condition is fulfilled for right concentration. When disenchantment and dispassion are fulfilled, for one accomplished in disenchantment and dispassion, the supporting condition is fulfilled for knowledge and vision of liberation.
