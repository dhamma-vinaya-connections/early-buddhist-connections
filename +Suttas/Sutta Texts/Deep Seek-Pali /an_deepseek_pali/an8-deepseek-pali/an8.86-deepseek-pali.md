# AN 8.86 Yasasuttaṃ

## AN 8.86 The Yasa Discourse

86. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosalesu cārikaṃ caramāno mahatā bhikkhusaṅghena saddhiṃ yena icchānaṅgalaṃ nāma kosalānaṃ brāhmaṇagāmo tadavasari. Tatra sudaṃ bhagavā icchānaṅgale viharati icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍe. Assosuṃ kho icchānaṅgalakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā – ‘‘samaṇo khalu bho gotamo sakyaputto sakyakulā pabbajito icchānaṅgalaṃ anuppatto icchānaṅgale viharati icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍe. Taṃ kho pana bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ evaṃ kalyāṇo kittisaddo abbhuggato – ‘itipi so bhagavā arahaṃ sammāsambuddho…pe… sādhu kho pana tathārūpānaṃ arahataṃ dassanaṃ hotī’’’ti.

86.On one occasion the Blessed One, while traveling through the Kosala country with a large company of monks, arrived at a Kosalan brahmin village named Icchānaṅgala. There the Blessed One stayed in the Icchānaṅgala woodland grove. The brahmin householders of Icchānaṅgala heard: "Indeed, the ascetic Gotama, the son of the Sakyans, who went forth from the Sakyan clan, has arrived at Icchānaṅgala and is staying in the Icchānaṅgala woodland grove. Now a good report about that venerable Gotama has spread thus: 'Indeed, the Blessed One is an arahant, a perfectly self-enlightened one... It is good to see such arahants.'"

Atha kho icchānaṅgalakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā tassā rattiyā accayena pahutaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ ādāya yena icchānaṅgalavanasaṇḍo tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bahidvārakoṭṭhake aṭṭhaṃsu uccāsaddā mahāsaddā. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā nāgito bhagavato upaṭṭhāko hoti. Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṃ nāgitaṃ āmantesi – ‘‘ke pana te, nāgita, uccāsaddā mahāsaddā kevaṭṭā maññe macchavilope’’ti? ‘‘Ete, bhante, icchānaṅgalakā brāhmaṇagahapatikā pahutaṃ khādanīyaṃ bhojanīyaṃ ādāya bahidvārakoṭṭhake ṭhitā bhagavantaṃyeva uddissa bhikkhusaṅghañcā’’ti. ‘‘Māhaṃ, nāgita, yasena samāgamaṃ, mā ca mayā yaso. Yo kho, nāgita, nayimassa nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī assa akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Yassāhaṃ nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī [nikāmalābhī assaṃ (bahūsu) a. ni. 5.30 passitabbaṃ. tattha hi ayaṃ pāṭhabhedā natthi] akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, so taṃ mīḷhasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ lābhasakkārasilokasukhaṃ sādiyeyyā’’ti.

Then, when the night had passed, the brahmin householders of Icchānaṅgala took abundant food, both hard and soft, and went to the Icchānaṅgala woodland. Arriving there, they stood outside the entrance pavilion making a loud and raucous noise. Now at that time Venerable Nāgita was the Blessed One’s attendant. So the Blessed One addressed him, “Nāgita, who is making such a loud and raucous noise? You’d think it was fishermen hauling in a catch!” “Sir, it’s the brahmin householders of Icchānaṅgala. They’ve brought abundant food, both hard and soft, and they’re standing outside the entrance pavilion intending it for the Blessed One and the Saṅgha.” “Nāgita, may I not associate with fame, and may fame not associate with me. Those who do not easily, without trouble or difficulty, obtain at will the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening—I easily, without trouble or difficulty, obtain at will the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening. Let them enjoy that filthy pleasure, that drowsy pleasure, that pleasure of gain, honor, and popularity.”

‘‘Adhivāsetu dāni, bhante, bhagavā. Adhivāsetu sugato. Adhivāsanakālo dāni, bhante, bhagavato. Yena yeneva dāni, bhante, bhagavā gamissati tanninnāva bhavissanti brāhmaṇagahapatikā negamā ceva jānapadā ca. Seyyathāpi, bhante, thullaphusitake deve vassante yathāninnaṃ udakāni pavattanti; evamevaṃ kho, bhante, yena yeneva dāni bhagavā gamissati tanninnāva bhavissanti brāhmaṇagahapatikā negamā ceva jānapadā ca. Taṃ kissa hetu? Tathā hi, bhante, bhagavato sīlapaññāṇa’’nti.

"Now, venerable sir, may the Blessed One consent. May the Fortunate One consent. Now is the time for the Blessed One to consent. For wherever the Blessed One now goes, the brahmins and householders of town and country will be inclined. Just as, venerable sir, when heavy rain falls, the waters flow downwards; in the same way, venerable sir, wherever the Blessed One now goes, the brahmins and householders of town and country will be inclined. Why is that? Because of the Blessed One’s virtue and discernment."

‘‘Māhaṃ, nāgita, yasena samāgamaṃ, mā ca mayā yaso. Yo kho, nāgita, nayimassa nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī assa akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Yassāhaṃ nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, so taṃ mīḷhasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ lābhasakkārasilokasukhaṃ sādiyeyya.

"Nāgita, may I not have association with fame, and may fame not have association with me. For whoever, Nāgita, were to gain at will, without difficulty or trouble, the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening—that one might relish this filthy pleasure, sleepy pleasure, the pleasure of gain, honor, and renown."

‘‘Devatāpi kho, nāgita, ekaccā nayimassa [ekaccā imassa (?)] nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhiniyo assu [idaṃ padaṃ katthaci natthi] akicchalābhiniyo [nikāmalābhiniyo akicchalābhiniyo (?)] akasiralābhiniyo, yassāhaṃ nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Tumhākampi [tāsampi (?)] kho, nāgita, saṅgamma samāgamma saṅgaṇikavihāraṃ anuyuttānaṃ viharataṃ [anuyutte viharante disvā (?)] evaṃ hoti – ‘na hi nūname [na hanūname (sī. syā. pī.)] āyasmanto imassa nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhino assu [idaṃ padaṃ katthaci natthi] akicchalābhino akasiralābhino. Yassāhaṃ nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Tathā hi pana me āyasmanto saṅgamma samāgamma saṅgaṇikavihāraṃ anuyuttā viharanti’’’.

"Even some deities, Nāgita, might be those who gain at will, without difficulty, or without much trouble, the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening—whereas I am one who gains these at will, without difficulty, or without much trouble. You too, Nāgita, when you live devoted to association, company, and a communal life, think thus: ‘Surely these venerables are not those who gain at will, without difficulty, or without much trouble, the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening—whereas I am one who gains these at will, without difficulty, or without much trouble. For these venerables live devoted to association, company, and a communal life.’"

‘‘Idhāhaṃ, nāgita, bhikkhū passāmi aññamaññaṃ aṅgulipatodakena [aṅgulipatodakehi (sī. pī. ka.)] sañjagghante saṅkīḷante. Tassa mayhaṃ, nāgita, evaṃ hoti – ‘na hi nūname āyasmanto imassa nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhino assu akicchalābhino akasiralābhino. Yassāhaṃ nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Tathā hi pana me āyasmanto aññamaññaṃ aṅgulipatodakena sañjagghanti saṅkīḷanti’’’.

"Here, Nāgita, I see monks poking each other with their fingertips, laughing and playing. It occurs to me, Nāgita: ‘Surely these venerables are not attainers at will, without trouble or difficulty, of the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening—whereas I am an attainer of these at will, without trouble or difficulty. Yet these venerables poke each other with their fingertips, laughing and playing.’"

‘‘Idha panāhaṃ [idhāhaṃ (sī. pī. ka.)], nāgita, bhikkhū passāmi yāvadatthaṃ udarāvadehakaṃ bhuñjitvā seyyasukhaṃ passasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ anuyutte viharante. Tassa mayhaṃ, nāgita, evaṃ hoti – ‘na hi nūname āyasmanto imassa nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhino assu akicchalābhino akasiralābhino. Yassāhaṃ nekkhammasukhassa pavivekasukhassa upasamasukhassa sambodhasukhassa nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Tathā hi pana me āyasmanto yāvadatthaṃ udarāvadehakaṃ bhuñjitvā seyyasukhaṃ passasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ anuyuttā viharanti’’’.

"Here, Nāgita, I see monks who, having eaten their fill until their bellies are full, indulge in the pleasures of sleeping, lying down, and drowsiness. It occurs to me, Nāgita: 'Surely these venerable ones are not attainers at will of the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening, attaining them without trouble, without difficulty. For I myself am an attainer at will of the bliss of renunciation, the bliss of seclusion, the bliss of peace, the bliss of awakening, attaining them without trouble, without difficulty. Yet these venerable ones, having eaten their fill until their bellies are full, indulge in the pleasures of sleeping, lying down, and drowsiness.'"

‘‘Idhāhaṃ [idha panāhaṃ (?)], nāgita, bhikkhuṃ passāmi gāmantavihāriṃ samāhitaṃ nisinnaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, nāgita, evaṃ hoti – ‘idāni imaṃ [idānimaṃ (katthaci) a. ni. 6.42] āyasmantaṃ ārāmiko vā upaṭṭhahissati [paccessati (sī. pī.), upaṭṭhahati (ka.)] samaṇuddeso vā. Taṃ tamhā [so tamhā (ka. sī.), so taṃ tamhā (?)] samādhimhā cāvessatī’ti. Tenāhaṃ, nāgita, tassa bhikkhuno na attamano homi gāmantavihārena.

"Here, Nāgita, I see a monk dwelling near a village, sitting composed. It occurs to me, Nāgita: 'Now a monastery attendant or a novice will attend to this venerable one and dislodge him from that samādhi.' Therefore, Nāgita, I am not pleased with that monk's dwelling near a village."

‘‘Idha panāhaṃ, nāgita, bhikkhuṃ passāmi āraññikaṃ araññe pacalāyamānaṃ nisinnaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, nāgita, evaṃ hoti – ‘idāni ayamāyasmā imaṃ niddākilamathaṃ paṭivinodetvā araññasaññaṃyeva manasi karissati ekatta’nti. Tenāhaṃ, nāgita, tassa bhikkhuno attamano homi araññavihārena.

"Here, Nāgita, I see a forest-dwelling monk sitting in the forest, dozing off. It occurs to me, Nāgita: 'Now this venerable one will dispel this drowsiness and fatigue and focus solely on the perception of the forest, as unified.' Therefore, Nāgita, I am pleased with that monk's dwelling in the forest."

‘‘Idha panāhaṃ, nāgita, bhikkhuṃ passāmi āraññikaṃ araññe asamāhitaṃ nisinnaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, nāgita, evaṃ hoti – ‘idāni ayamāyasmā asamāhitaṃ vā cittaṃ samādahissati [samādahessati (katthaci)], samāhitaṃ vā cittaṃ anurakkhissatī’ti. Tenāhaṃ, nāgita, tassa bhikkhuno attamano homi araññavihārena.

Here, Nāgita, I see a forest-dwelling monk sitting in the forest uncomposed. It occurs to me, Nāgita: ‘Now this venerable one will either compose his uncomposed mind or maintain his composed mind.’ Therefore, Nāgita, I am pleased with that monk’s dwelling in the forest.

‘‘Idha panāhaṃ, nāgita, bhikkhuṃ passāmi āraññikaṃ araññe samāhitaṃ nisinnaṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, nāgita, evaṃ hoti – ‘idāni ayamāyasmā avimuttaṃ vā cittaṃ vimuccissati, vimuttaṃ vā cittaṃ anurakkhissatī’ti. Tenāhaṃ, nāgita, tassa bhikkhuno attamano homi araññavihārena.

Here, Nāgita, I see a forest-dwelling monk sitting composed in the forest. It occurs to me, Nāgita: ‘Now this venerable one will either liberate his unliberated mind or maintain his liberated mind.’ Therefore, Nāgita, I am pleased with that monk’s dwelling in the forest.

‘‘Idha panāhaṃ, nāgita, bhikkhuṃ passāmi gāmantavihāriṃ lābhiṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. So taṃ lābhasakkārasilokaṃ nikāmayamāno riñcati paṭisallānaṃ, riñcati araññavanapatthāni pantāni senāsanāni; gāmanigamarājadhāniṃ osaritvā vāsaṃ kappeti. Tenāhaṃ, nāgita, tassa bhikkhuno na attamano homi gāmantavihārena.

Here, Nāgita, I see a monk dwelling near a village who obtains robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites. Longing for gain, honor, and renown, he gives up seclusion and abandons remote forest and woodland dwellings, taking up residence near villages, towns, or royal cities. Therefore, Nāgita, I am not pleased with that monk’s dwelling near a village.

‘‘Idha panāhaṃ, nāgita, bhikkhuṃ passāmi āraññikaṃ lābhiṃ cīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārānaṃ. So taṃ lābhasakkārasilokaṃ paṭipaṇāmetvā na riñcati paṭisallānaṃ, na riñcati araññavanapatthāni pantāni senāsanāni. Tenāhaṃ, nāgita, tassa bhikkhuno attamano homi araññavihārena. [[ ] etthantare pāṭho a. ni. 6.42 chakkanipāteyeva dissati, na ettha aṭṭhakanipāte]

Here, Nāgita, I see a forest-dwelling monk who obtains robes, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites. Having renounced gain, honor, and renown, he does not relinquish seclusion or abandon remote forest and woodland dwellings. Therefore, Nāgita, I am pleased with that monk’s dwelling in the forest.

‘‘Yasmāhaṃ [yasmiṃhaṃ (katthaci)], nāgita, samaye addhānamaggappaṭipanno na kañci passāmi purato vā pacchato vā, phāsu me, nāgita, tasmiṃ samaye hoti antamaso uccārapassāvakammāyā’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

When, Nāgita, I am traveling on a road and see no one ahead or behind me, at that time, Nāgita, I am at ease, even when attending to calls of nature.” The sixth.
