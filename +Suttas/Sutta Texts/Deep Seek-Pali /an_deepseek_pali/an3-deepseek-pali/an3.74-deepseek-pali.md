# AN 3.74 Nigaṇṭhasuttaṃ

## AN 3.74 Nigaṇṭha Sutta

75. Ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā ānando vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Atha kho abhayo ca licchavi paṇḍitakumārako ca licchavi yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho abhayo licchavi āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘nigaṇṭho, bhante, nāṭaputto [nāthaputto (sī. pī.)] sabbaññū sabbadassāvī aparisesaṃ ñāṇadassanaṃ paṭijānāti – ‘carato ca me tiṭṭhato ca suttassa ca jāgarassa ca satataṃ samitaṃ ñāṇadassanaṃ paccupaṭṭhita’nti. So purāṇānaṃ kammānaṃ tapasā byantībhāvaṃ paññapeti navānaṃ kammānaṃ akaraṇā setughātaṃ. Iti kammakkhayā dukkhakkhayo, dukkhakkhayā vedanākkhayo, vedanākkhayā sabbaṃ dukkhaṃ nijjiṇṇaṃ bhavissati – evametissā sandiṭṭhikāya nijjarāya visuddhiyā samatikkamo hoti. Idha, bhante, bhagavā kimāhā’’ti?

75.On one occasion the Venerable Ānanda was dwelling at Vesālī in the Great Wood, in the Hall with the Peaked Roof. Then Abhaya the Licchavi and Paṇḍitakumāraka the Licchavi approached the Venerable Ānanda; having approached, they paid homage to the Venerable Ānanda and sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, Abhaya the Licchavi said to the Venerable Ānanda: "Venerable sir, Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta claims to be all-knowing, all-seeing, to possess complete knowledge and vision without remainder, thus: 'Whether I am walking or standing, sleeping or awake, knowledge and vision are continuously and uninterruptedly present to me.' He declares the wearing away of past deeds through asceticism and the prevention of new deeds through non-doing. Thus, with the destruction of deeds, suffering is destroyed; with the destruction of suffering, feeling is destroyed; with the destruction of feeling, all suffering will be worn away. In this way, there is the complete surmounting of this visible purification through attrition. What does the Blessed One say about this, venerable sir?"

‘‘Tisso kho imā, abhaya, nijjarā visuddhiyo tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena sammadakkhātā sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya. Katamā tisso? Idha, abhaya, bhikkhu sīlavā hoti…pe… samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So navañca kammaṃ na karoti, purāṇañca kammaṃ phussa phussa byantīkaroti. Sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhīti.

"Abhaya, these three purifications by attrition have been well proclaimed by the Blessed One, the Knower, the Seer, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the cessation of pain and dejection, for the attainment of the right method, for the realization of Nibbāna. What are the three? Here, Abhaya, a bhikkhu is virtuous[...] he trains himself in the training rules, undertaking them. He does not do new kamma, and by experiencing and experiencing he exhausts old kamma. This is a purification by attrition, visible here and now, immediate, inviting inspection, onward leading, to be personally experienced by the wise."

‘‘Sa kho so, abhaya, bhikkhu evaṃ sīlasampanno vivicceva kāmehi vivicca akusalehi dhammehi savitakkaṃ savicāraṃ vivekajaṃ pītisukhaṃ paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Vitakkavicārānaṃ vūpasamā ajjhattaṃ sampasādanaṃ cetaso ekodibhāvaṃ avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhijaṃ pītisukhaṃ dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Pītiyā ca virāgā upekkhako ca viharati sato ca sampajāno, sukhañca kāyena paṭisaṃvedeti yaṃ taṃ ariyā ācikkhanti – ‘upekkhako satimā sukhavihārī’ti tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. Sukhassa ca pahānā dukkhassa ca pahānā, pubbeva somanassadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamā adukkhamasukhaṃ upekkhāsatipārisuddhiṃ catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati. So navañca kammaṃ na karoti, purāṇañca kammaṃ phussa phussa byantīkaroti. Sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhīti.

"Now, Abhaya, that monk, accomplished in virtue, ![[Jhanas]]. He does not create new kamma, and by experiencing its results, he exhausts old kamma. The wearing away is visible here and now, immediate, inviting one to come and see, onward-leading, to be experienced by the wise for themselves."

‘‘Sa kho so, abhaya, bhikkhu evaṃ samādhisampanno [evaṃ sīlasampanno evaṃ samādhisampanno (sī. syā. kaṃ.)] āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. So navañca kammaṃ na karoti, purāṇañca kammaṃ phussa phussa byantīkaroti. Sandiṭṭhikā nijjarā akālikā ehipassikā opaneyyikā paccattaṃ veditabbā viññūhīti. Imā kho, abhaya, tisso nijjarā visuddhiyo tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena sammadakkhātā sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāyā’’ti.

"Now, Abhaya, that monk, thus accomplished in samādhi, through the destruction of the taints, enters and dwells in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, having realized it for himself with direct knowledge in this very life. He does not create new kamma, and by experiencing its results, he exhausts old kamma. The wearing away is visible here and now, immediate, inviting inspection, onward leading, to be experienced individually by the wise. These, Abhaya, are the three kinds of wearing away, purification, rightly proclaimed by the Blessed One, who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One, for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and grief, for the attainment of the right way, for the realization of Nibbāna."

Evaṃ vutte paṇḍitakumārako licchavi abhayaṃ licchaviṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃ pana tvaṃ, samma abhaya, āyasmato ānandassa subhāsitaṃ subhāsitato nābbhanumodasī’’ti? ‘‘Kyāhaṃ, samma paṇḍitakumāraka, āyasmato ānandassa subhāsitaṃ subhāsitato nābbhanumodissāmi! Muddhāpi tassa vipateyya yo āyasmato ānandassa subhāsitaṃ subhāsitato nābbhanumodeyyā’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

When this was said, Paṇḍitakumāraka the Licchavi said to Abhaya the Licchavi: "Well then, dear Abhaya, do you not approve of the well-spoken words of the Venerable Ānanda, acknowledging them as well-spoken?" "Why wouldn’t I, dear Paṇḍitakumāraka, approve of the well-spoken words of the Venerable Ānanda, acknowledging them as well-spoken? May the head of anyone who does not approve of the Venerable Ānanda's well-spoken words, acknowledging them as well-spoken, split apart!"
