# AN 3.61 Saṅgāravasuttaṃ

## AN 3.61 The Saṅgārava Sutta

61. Atha kho saṅgāravo brāhmaṇo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho saṅgāravo brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘mayamassu, bho gotama, brāhmaṇā nāma. Yaññaṃ yajāmapi yajāpemapi. Tatra, bho gotama, yo ceva yajati [yo ceva yaññaṃ yajati (syā. kaṃ.)] yo ca yajāpeti sabbe te anekasārīrikaṃ puññappaṭipadaṃ paṭipannā honti, yadidaṃ yaññādhikaraṇaṃ. Yo panāyaṃ, bho gotama, yassa vā tassa vā kulā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajito ekamattānaṃ dameti, ekamattānaṃ sameti, ekamattānaṃ parinibbāpeti, evamassāyaṃ ekasārīrikaṃ puññappaṭipadaṃ paṭipanno hoti, yadidaṃ pabbajjādhikaraṇa’’nti.

61.Then the brahmin Saṅgārava approached the Blessed One; having approached, he exchanged greetings with the Blessed One. When the conversation was finished, being polite and amiable, he sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the brahmin Saṅgārava said this to the Blessed One: "We, Venerable Gotama, are called brahmins. We perform sacrifices and have sacrifices performed. In this regard, Venerable Gotama, both the one who sacrifices and the one who has sacrifices performed—all these are practicing a meritorious course involving many persons, that is, in connection with sacrifice. But this one, Venerable Gotama, who has gone forth from the household life into homelessness from whatever clan—he tames just himself, he calms just himself, he attains Nibbana just for himself. Thus, he is practicing a meritorious course involving a single person, that is, in connection with going forth."

‘‘Tena hi, brāhmaṇa, taññevettha paṭipucchissāmi. Yathā te khameyya tathā naṃ byākareyyāsi. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, brāhmaṇa, idha tathāgato loke uppajjati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho vijjācaraṇasampanno sugato lokavidū anuttaro purisadammasārathi satthā devamanussānaṃ buddho bhagavā. So evamāha – ‘ethāyaṃ maggo ayaṃ paṭipadā yathāpaṭipanno ahaṃ anuttaraṃ brahmacariyogadhaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedemi; etha [etaṃ (ka.)], tumhepi tathā paṭipajjatha, yathāpaṭipannā tumhepi anuttaraṃ brahmacariyogadhaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharissathā’ti. Iti ayañceva [sayaṃ ceva (ka.)] satthā dhammaṃ deseti, pare ca tathatthāya paṭipajjanti, tāni kho pana honti anekānipi satāni anekānipi sahassāni anekānipi satasahassāni.

"Then, brahmin, I will ask you about this very matter. Answer as you think fit. What do you think, brahmin? Here, a Tathagata arises in the world, an Arahant, a Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, perfect in knowledge and conduct, the Well-Gone One, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of those who can be trained, teacher of gods and humans, the Awakened One, the Blessed One. He says: ‘Come, this is the path, this is the practice by which, following it, I have realized the unsurpassed culmination of the holy life and declare it, having directly known it for myself. Come, you too practice accordingly, so that you too, following it, may realize the unsurpassed culmination of the holy life for yourselves, having directly known it, and abide in it.’ Thus, this teacher teaches the Dhamma, and others practice accordingly. And there are many hundreds, many thousands, many hundreds of thousands of them."

‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, brāhmaṇa, iccāyaṃ evaṃ sante ekasārīrikā vā puññappaṭipadā hoti anekasārīrikā vā, yadidaṃ pabbajjādhikaraṇa’’nti? ‘‘Iccāyampi [iccāyante (ka.)], bho gotama, evaṃ sante anekasārīrikā puññappaṭipadā hoti, yadidaṃ pabbajjādhikaraṇa’’nti.

"What do you think, Brahmin? In this case, when it is like this, is the course of meritorious action involving a single person or involving many persons, namely, the reason being going forth?" "In this case, Venerable Gotama, when it is like this, the course of meritorious action is involving many persons, namely, the reason being going forth."

Evaṃ vutte āyasmā ānando saṅgāravaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘imāsaṃ te, brāhmaṇa, dvinnaṃ paṭipadānaṃ katamā paṭipadā khamati appatthatarā ca appasamārambhatarā ca mahapphalatarā ca mahānisaṃsatarā cā’’ti? Evaṃ vutte saṅgāravo brāhmaṇo āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘seyyathāpi bhavaṃ gotamo bhavaṃ cānando. Ete me pujjā, ete me pāsaṃsā’’ti.

When this was said, Venerable Ānanda said to the brahmin Saṅgārava: "Brahmin, of these two practices, which practice do you approve of as less demanding, less laborious, more fruitful, and more advantageous?" When this was said, the brahmin Saṅgārava said to Venerable Ānanda: "It is just as the Venerable Gotama is, and the Venerable Ānanda. These are my revered ones, these are my praised ones."

Dutiyampi kho āyasmā ānando saṅgāravaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘na kho tyāhaṃ, brāhmaṇa, evaṃ pucchāmi – ‘ke vā te pujjā ke vā te pāsaṃsā’ti? Evaṃ kho tyāhaṃ, brāhmaṇa, pucchāmi – ‘imāsaṃ te, brāhmaṇa, dvinnaṃ paṭipadānaṃ katamā paṭipadā khamati appatthatarā ca appasamārambhatarā ca mahapphalatarā ca mahānisaṃsatarā cā’’’ti? Dutiyampi kho saṅgāravo brāhmaṇo āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘seyyathāpi bhavaṃ gotamo bhavaṃ cānando. Ete me pujjā, ete me pāsaṃsā’’ti.

A second time, Venerable Ānanda said to the brahmin Saṅgārava: "Brahmin, I am not asking you, ‘Who are your revered ones? Who are your praised ones?’ Rather, brahmin, I am asking you, ‘Between these two practices, which one do you find more agreeable, less demanding, less laborious, and more fruitful and beneficial?’" A second time, the brahmin Saṅgārava replied to Venerable Ānanda: "Just like the Venerable Gotama and the Venerable Ānanda. These are my revered ones; these are my praised ones."

Tatiyampi kho āyasmā ānando saṅgāravaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘na kho tyāhaṃ, brāhmaṇa, evaṃ pucchāmi – ‘ke vā te pujjā ke vā te pāsaṃsā’ti? Evaṃ kho tyāhaṃ, brāhmaṇa, pucchāmi – ‘imāsaṃ te, brāhmaṇa, dvinnaṃ paṭipadānaṃ katamā paṭipadā khamati appatthatarā ca appasamārambhatarā ca mahapphalatarā ca mahānisaṃsatarā cā’’’ti? Tatiyampi kho saṅgāravo brāhmaṇo āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘seyyathāpi bhavaṃ gotamo bhavaṃ cānando. Ete me pujjā, ete me pāsaṃsā’’ti.

A third time, Venerable Ānanda said to the brahmin Saṅgārava: "Brahmin, I am not asking you, ‘Who are your revered ones? Who are your praised ones?’ Rather, brahmin, I am asking you, ‘Between these two practices, which practice do you find more agreeable—less demanding, less laborious, more fruitful, and more beneficial?’" A third time, the brahmin Saṅgārava said to Venerable Ānanda: "Just like the Venerable Gotama and the Venerable Ānanda. These are my revered ones; these are my praised ones."

Atha kho bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘yāva tatiyampi kho saṅgāravo brāhmaṇo ānandena sahadhammikaṃ pañhaṃ puṭṭho saṃsādeti [ma. ni. 1.337] no vissajjeti. Yaṃnūnāhaṃ parimoceyya’’nti. Atha kho bhagavā saṅgāravaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kā nvajja, brāhmaṇa, rājantepure rājapurisānaṃ [rājaparisāyaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ antarākathā udapādī’’ti? ‘‘Ayaṃ khvajja, bho gotama, rājantepure rājapurisānaṃ sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ antarākathā udapādi – ‘pubbe sudaṃ appatarā ceva bhikkhū ahesuṃ bahutarā ca uttari manussadhammā iddhipāṭihāriyaṃ dassesuṃ; etarahi pana bahutarā ceva bhikkhū appatarā ca uttari manussadhammā iddhipāṭihāriyaṃ dassentī’ti. Ayaṃ khvajja, bho gotama, rājantepure rājapurisānaṃ sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ antarākathā udapādī’’ti.

Then the Blessed One thought: "Even up to the third time, the brahmin Saṅgārava, when questioned by Ānanda with a legitimate question, equivocates and does not answer. Suppose I were to release him." Then the Blessed One said to the brahmin Saṅgārava: "Brahmin, what was the topic of discussion that arose today among the king's officials when they were seated and gathered in the royal inner chambers?" "Today, Venerable Gotama, this topic arose among the king's officials when they were seated and gathered in the royal inner chambers: 'In the past, there were indeed fewer monks, and yet more of them demonstrated superhuman states and psychic power. But now, there are indeed more monks, and yet fewer demonstrate superhuman states and psychic power.' This, Venerable Gotama, was the topic of discussion that arose today among the king's officials when they were seated and gathered in the royal inner chambers."

[paṭi. ma. 3.30; dī. ni. 1.483] ‘‘Tīṇi kho imāni, brāhmaṇa, pāṭihāriyāni. Katamāni tīṇi? Iddhipāṭihāriyaṃ, ādesanāpāṭihāriyaṃ, anusāsanīpāṭihāriyaṃ. Katamañca, brāhmaṇa, iddhipāṭihāriyaṃ? Idha, brāhmaṇa, ekacco anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti – ‘ekopi hutvā bahudhā hoti, bahudhāpi hutvā eko hoti; āvibhāvaṃ, tirobhāvaṃ; tirokuṭṭaṃ tiropākāraṃ tiropabbataṃ asajjamāno gacchati, seyyathāpi ākāse; pathaviyāpi ummujjanimujjaṃ karoti, seyyathāpi udake; udakepi abhijjamāne gacchati, seyyathāpi pathaviyaṃ; ākāsepi pallaṅkena kamati, seyyathāpi pakkhī sakuṇo; imepi candimasūriye evaṃmahiddhike evaṃmahānubhāve pāṇinā parimasati [parāmasati (dī. ni. 1.484; paṭi. ma. 1.102] parimajjati, yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti’. Idaṃ vuccati, brāhmaṇa, iddhipāṭihāriyaṃ.

"Brahmin, there are these three miracles. What three? The miracle of psychic power, the miracle of telepathy, and the miracle of instruction. And what, Brahmin, is the miracle of psychic power? Here, Brahmin, a certain person experiences manifold psychic powers: having been one, he becomes many; having been many, he becomes one; he appears and vanishes; he goes unhindered through walls, ramparts, and mountains as if through space; he dives in and out of the earth as if it were water; he goes on water without sinking as if it were earth; he travels through the sky while sitting cross-legged like a bird; with his hand he touches and strokes the sun and moon, so mighty and powerful; he wields bodily mastery even as far as the Brahmā world. This, Brahmin, is called the miracle of psychic power."

‘‘Katamañca, brāhmaṇa, ādesanāpāṭihāriyaṃ? Idha, brāhmaṇa, ekacco nimittena ādisati – ‘evampi te mano, itthampi te mano, itipi te citta’nti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā.

"And what, brahmin, is the miracle of telepathy? Here, brahmin, someone predicts by means of a sign: 'Your mind is thus, your mind is so, your thought is thus.' Even if he predicts much, it turns out just so, not otherwise."

‘‘Idha pana, brāhmaṇa, ekacco na heva kho nimittena ādisati, api ca kho manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati – ‘evampi te mano, itthampi te mano, itipi te citta’nti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā.

"Here, brahmin, someone does not predict by signs, but having heard the voice of humans, non-humans, or deities, predicts: ‘Your mind is thus, your mind is so, your thought is thus.’ Even if he predicts much, it turns out just so, not otherwise."

‘‘Idha pana, brāhmaṇa, ekacco na heva kho nimittena ādisati napi manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati, api ca kho vitakkayato vicārayato vitakkavipphārasaddaṃ sutvā ādisati – ‘evampi te mano, itthampi te mano, itipi te citta’nti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā.

"Here, brahmin, a certain person does not predict by means of a sign, nor does he predict after hearing a sound from humans, non-humans, or deities. But rather, while pondering and reflecting, he predicts after hearing the sound of the diffusion of thoughts: ‘Your mind is thus, your mind is so, your thought is thus.’ Even if he predicts much, it turns out just so, and not otherwise."

‘‘Idha pana, brāhmaṇa, ekacco na heva kho nimittena ādisati, napi manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati, napi vitakkayato vicārayato vitakkavipphārasaddaṃ sutvā ādisati, api ca kho avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhiṃ samāpannassa cetasā ceto paricca pajānāti – ‘yathā imassa bhoto manosaṅkhārā paṇihitā imassa cittassa anantarā amuṃ nāma vitakkaṃ vitakkessatī’ti. So bahuṃ cepi ādisati tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā. Idaṃ vuccati, brāhmaṇa, ādesanāpāṭihāriyaṃ.

"But here, brahmin, someone does not predict by means of a sign, nor does he predict after hearing a sound from humans, non-humans, or deities, nor does he predict after hearing the sound of the diffusion of thoughts while thinking and pondering. But rather, having attained samādhi without thought and examination, he knows the mind of another with his own mind, understanding: ‘As this good sir’s mental fabrications are applied, so immediately after this thought, his mind will think such and such a thought.’ Even if he predicts much, it turns out just so, not otherwise. This, brahmin, is called the miracle of telepathy."

‘‘Katamañca, brāhmaṇa, anusāsanīpāṭihāriyaṃ? Idha, brāhmaṇa, ekacco evamanusāsati – ‘evaṃ vitakketha, mā evaṃ vitakkayittha; evaṃ manasi karotha, mā evaṃ manasākattha; idaṃ pajahatha, idaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti. Idaṃ vuccati, brāhmaṇa, anusāsanīpāṭihāriyaṃ. Imāni kho, brāhmaṇa, tīṇi pāṭihāriyāni. Imesaṃ te, brāhmaṇa, tiṇṇaṃ pāṭihāriyānaṃ katamaṃ pāṭihāriyaṃ khamati abhikkantatarañca paṇītatarañcā’’ti?

"And what, brahmin, is the miracle of instruction? Here, brahmin, a certain person instructs thus: 'Think in this way, do not think in that way; apply your mind to this, do not apply your mind to that; abandon this, attain and abide in that.' This, brahmin, is called the miracle of instruction. These, brahmin, are the three miracles. Among these three miracles, brahmin, which miracle do you consider the most excellent and sublime?"

‘‘Tatra, bho gotama, yadidaṃ [yamidaṃ (syā. kaṃ. pī.)] pāṭihāriyaṃ idhekacco anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti…pe… yāva brāhmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti, idaṃ, bho gotama, pāṭihāriyaṃ yova [yo ca (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] naṃ karoti sova [soca ca (syā. kaṃ pī. ka.)] naṃ paṭisaṃvedeti, yova [yo ca (syā. kaṃ. pī. ka.)] naṃ karoti tasseva [tassameva (sī. ka.), tassa ceva (syā. kaṃ. pī.)] taṃ hoti. Idaṃ me, bho gotama, pāṭihāriyaṃ māyāsahadhammarūpaṃ viya khāyati.

"In that case, Master Gotama, this wonder, where a certain person experiences various kinds of psychic power… wielding mastery with the body even as far as the Brahma world—this wonder, Master Gotama, is performed by whoever does it; whoever does it is the one who experiences it; it belongs to the one who does it. To me, Master Gotama, this wonder appears like a magical illusion."

‘‘Yampidaṃ, bho gotama, pāṭihāriyaṃ idhekacco nimittena ādisati – ‘evampi te mano, itthampi te mano, itipi te citta’nti, so bahuṃ cepi ādisati tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā. Idha pana, bho gotama, ekacco na heva kho nimittena ādisati, api ca kho manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati…pe… napi manussānaṃ vā amanussānaṃ vā devatānaṃ vā saddaṃ sutvā ādisati, api ca kho vitakkayato vicārayato vitakkavipphārasaddaṃ sutvā ādisati…pe… napi vitakkayato vicārayato vitakkavipphārasaddaṃ sutvā ādisati, api ca kho avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhiṃ samāpannassa cetasā ceto paricca pajānāti – ‘yathā imassa bhoto manosaṅkhārā paṇihitā imassa cittassa anantarā amhaṃ nāma vitakkaṃ vitakkessatī’ti, so bahuṃ cepi ādisati tatheva taṃ hoti, no aññathā. Idampi, bho gotama, pāṭihāriyaṃ yova naṃ karoti sova naṃ paṭisaṃvedeti, yova naṃ karoti tasseva taṃ hoti. Idampi me, bho gotama, pāṭihāriyaṃ māyāsahadhammarūpaṃ viya khāyati.

"Here, Master Gotama, this wonder, where a certain person discerns by means of a sign: ‘Your mind is like this, your mind is like that, your thought is thus.’ Even if he discerns much, it turns out just so, not otherwise. But here, Master Gotama, a certain person does not discern by means of a sign; rather, having heard a sound of humans, non-humans, or deities, he discerns… or not having heard a sound of humans, non-humans, or deities; rather, having heard the sound of the expansion of thoughts while one is pondering and reflecting, he discerns… or not having heard the sound of the expansion of thoughts while one is pondering and reflecting; rather, having entered into samādhi free from thought and examination, he fully understands the mind of another with his own mind, knowing: ‘As this venerable one’s mental formations are directed, immediately after this mind, he will think such and such a thought.’ Even if he discerns much, it turns out just so, not otherwise. This too, Master Gotama, is a wonder; only the one who performs it experiences it, it belongs only to the one who performs it. This wonder too, Master Gotama, appears to me like a magical illusion."

‘‘Yañca kho idaṃ, bho gotama, pāṭihāriyaṃ idhekacco evaṃ anusāsati – ‘evaṃ vitakketha, mā evaṃ vitakkayittha; evaṃ manasi karotha, mā evaṃ manasākattha; idaṃ pajahatha, idaṃ upasampajja viharathā’ti. Idameva, bho gotama, pāṭihāriyaṃ khamati imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ pāṭihāriyānaṃ abhikkantatarañca paṇītatarañca.

"But this wonder, Master Gotama, where a certain person instructs thus: ‘Think in this way, do not think in that way; apply your mind in this way, do not apply your mind in that way; abandon this, attain and dwell in that’—this, Master Gotama, is the wonder that is more excellent and more sublime than these three wonders."

‘‘Acchariyaṃ, bho gotama, abbhutaṃ, bho gotama! Yāvasubhāsitamidaṃ bhotā gotamena imehi ca mayaṃ tīhi pāṭihāriyehi samannāgataṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ dhārema. Bhavañhi gotamo anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhoti…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vatteti, bhavañhi gotamo avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhiṃ samāpannassa cetasā ceto paricca pajānāti – ‘yathā imassa bhoto manosaṅkhārā paṇihitā imassa cittassa anantarā amuṃ nāma vitakkaṃ vitakkessatī’ti. Bhavañhi gotamo evamanusāsati – ‘evaṃ vitakketha, mā evaṃ vitakkayittha; evaṃ manasi karotha, mā evaṃ manasākattha; idaṃ pajahatha, idaṃ upasampajja viharathā’’’ti.

"Wonderful, Master Gotama, astonishing, Master Gotama! How well-spoken this is by Master Gotama! And we hold Master Gotama to be endowed with these three wonders. For Master Gotama experiences various kinds of psychic power… wielding mastery with the body even as far as the Brahma world. Master Gotama, with his mind, fully understands the mind of one who has attained samādhi free from thought and examination, discerning: ‘As this venerable one’s mental formations are directed, immediately after this mind, he will think such and such a thought.’ Master Gotama instructs thus: ‘Think in this way, do not think in that way; apply your mind in this way, do not apply your mind in that way; abandon this, having attained that, dwell.’"

‘‘Addhā kho tyāhaṃ, brāhmaṇa, āsajja upanīya vācā bhāsitā; api ca tyāhaṃ byākarissāmi. Ahañhi, brāhmaṇa, anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhomi…pe… yāva brahmalokāpi kāyena vasaṃ vattemi. Ahañhi, brāhmaṇa, avitakkaṃ avicāraṃ samādhiṃ samāpannassa cetasā ceto paricca pajānāmi – ‘yathā imassa bhoto manosaṅkhārā paṇihitā, imassa cittassa anantarā amuṃ nāma vitakkaṃ vitakkessatī’ti. Ahañhi, brāhmaṇa, evamanusāsāmi – ‘evaṃ vitakketha, mā evaṃ vitakkayittha; evaṃ manasi karotha, mā evaṃ manasākattha; idaṃ pajahatha, idaṃ upasampajja viharathā’’’ti.

"Indeed, brahmin, you have spoken to me with speech that is both assailing and flattering; but I shall explain it to you. For I, brahmin, experience various kinds of psychic power… wielding mastery with the body even as far as the Brahma world. I, brahmin, with my mind, fully understand the mind of one who has attained samādhi free from thought and examination, discerning: ‘As this venerable one’s mental formations are directed, immediately after this mind, he will think such and such a thought.’ I, brahmin, instruct thus: ‘Think in this way, do not think in that way; apply your mind in this way, do not apply your mind in that way; abandon this, attain and dwell in that.’"

‘‘Atthi pana, bho gotama, añño ekabhikkhupi yo imehi tīhi pāṭihāriyehi samannāgato, aññatra bhotā gotamenā’’ti? ‘‘Na kho, brāhmaṇa, ekaṃyeva sataṃ na dve satāni na tīṇi satāni na cattāri satāni na pañca satāni, atha kho bhiyyova, ye [te (ka.) passa ma. ni. 2.195] bhikkhū imehi tīhi pāṭihāriyehi samannāgatā’’ti. ‘‘Kahaṃ pana, bho gotama, etarahi te bhikkhū viharantī’’ti? ‘‘Imasmiṃyeva kho, brāhmaṇa, bhikkhusaṅghe’’ti.

"But, Master Gotama, is there any other monk who is endowed with these three wonders, besides Master Gotama?" "Not just one hundred, brahmin, nor two hundred, nor three hundred, nor four hundred, nor five hundred, but many more monks are endowed with these three wonders." "But where, Master Gotama, do those monks live now?" "Right here, brahmin, in this community of monks."

‘‘Abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama! Seyyathāpi bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya – ‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’ti, evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi, dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Upāsakaṃ maṃ bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti. Dasamaṃ.

"Excellent, Master Gotama, excellent! Just as, Master Gotama, one might set upright what was overturned, reveal what was hidden, show the path to one who is lost, or hold up a lamp in the darkness so that those with eyes may see forms—in the same way the Dhamma has been made clear in many ways by Master Gotama. I go for refuge to Master Gotama, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of monks. May Master Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge from this day forth, for as long as life lasts." The tenth.

Brāhmaṇavaggo paṭhamo.
