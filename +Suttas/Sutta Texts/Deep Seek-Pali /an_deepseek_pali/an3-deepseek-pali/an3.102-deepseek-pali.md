# AN 3.102 Nimittasuttaṃ

## AN 3.102 The Sign Discourse

103. ‘‘Adhicittamanuyuttena, bhikkhave, bhikkhunā tīṇi nimittāni kālena kālaṃ manasi kātabbāni – kālena kālaṃ samādhinimittaṃ manasi kātabbaṃ, kālena kālaṃ paggahanimittaṃ manasi kātabbaṃ, kālena kālaṃ upekkhānimittaṃ manasi kātabbaṃ. Sace, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu ekantaṃ samādhinimittaṃyeva manasi kareyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ cittaṃ kosajjāya saṃvatteyya. Sace, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu ekantaṃ paggahanimittaṃyeva manasi kareyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ cittaṃ uddhaccāya saṃvatteyya. Sace, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu ekantaṃ upekkhānimittaṃyeva manasi kareyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ cittaṃ na sammā samādhiyeyya āsavānaṃ khayāya. Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu kālena kālaṃ samādhinimittaṃ manasi karoti, kālena kālaṃ paggahanimittaṃ manasi karoti, kālena kālaṃ upekkhānimittaṃ manasi karoti, taṃ hoti cittaṃ muduñca kammaniyañca pabhassarañca, na ca pabhaṅgu, sammā samādhiyati āsavānaṃ khayāya.

103.“Bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind should attend to three signs from time to time: from time to time, the sign of samādhi should be attended to; from time to time, the sign of energy should be attended to; from time to time, the sign of equanimity should be attended to. If, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind were to attend solely to the sign of samādhi, his mind would tend to sluggishness. If, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind were to attend solely to the sign of energy, his mind would tend to agitation. If, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind were to attend solely to the sign of equanimity, his mind would not be rightly collected for the destruction of the taints. But when, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind attends to the sign of samādhi from time to time, to the sign of energy from time to time, and to the sign of equanimity from time to time, then his mind becomes pliable, workable, luminous, and not brittle, and it is rightly collected for the destruction of the taints.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, suvaṇṇakāro vā suvaṇṇakārantevāsī vā ukkaṃ bandheyya [bandhati… ālimpati (visuddhi. 1.181 taṃṭīkāyaṃ ca) ma. ni. aṭṭha. 1.76; ma. ni. 3.360 taṃaṭṭhakathāṭīkāsu ca passitabbaṃ], ukkaṃ bandhitvā ukkāmukhaṃ ālimpeyya, ukkāmukhaṃ ālimpetvā saṇḍāsena jātarūpaṃ gahetvā ukkāmukhe pakkhipeyya [pakkhipati (visuddhi. 1.181)], ukkāmukhe pakkhipitvā kālena kālaṃ abhidhamati, kālena kālaṃ udakena paripphoseti, kālena kālaṃ ajjhupekkhati. Sace, bhikkhave, suvaṇṇakāro vā suvaṇṇakārantevāsī vā taṃ jātarūpaṃ ekantaṃ abhidhameyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ jātarūpaṃ ḍaheyya. Sace, bhikkhave, suvaṇṇakāro vā suvaṇṇakārantevāsī vā taṃ jātarūpaṃ ekantaṃ udakena paripphoseyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ jātarūpaṃ nibbāpeyya [nibbāyeyya (sī.)]. Sace, bhikkhave, suvaṇṇakāro vā suvaṇṇakārantevāsī vā taṃ jātarūpaṃ ekantaṃ ajjhupekkheyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ jātarūpaṃ na sammā paripākaṃ gaccheyya. Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, suvaṇṇakāro vā suvaṇṇakārantevāsī vā taṃ jātarūpaṃ kālena kālaṃ abhidhamati, kālena kālaṃ udakena paripphoseti, kālena kālaṃ ajjhupekkhati, taṃ hoti jātarūpaṃ muduñca kammaniyañca pabhassarañca, na ca pabhaṅgu, sammā upeti kammāya. Yassā yassā ca pilandhanavikatiyā ākaṅkhati – yadi paṭṭikāya, yadi kuṇḍalāya, yadi gīveyyake, yadi suvaṇṇamālāya – tañcassa atthaṃ anubhoti.

“Just as, bhikkhus, a goldsmith or his apprentice would prepare a furnace, having prepared the furnace, he would make the furnace mouth ready, having made the furnace mouth ready, he would take gold with tongs and place it in the furnace, having placed it in the furnace, he would blow on it from time to time, sprinkle water on it from time to time, and examine it from time to time. If, bhikkhus, the goldsmith or his apprentice were to blow on the gold too much, the gold would burn up. If, bhikkhus, the goldsmith or his apprentice were to sprinkle water on the gold too much, the gold would cool off. If, bhikkhus, the goldsmith or his apprentice were to examine the gold too much, the gold would not reach proper maturity. But when, bhikkhus, the goldsmith or his apprentice blows on the gold from time to time, sprinkles water on the gold from time to time, and examines the gold from time to time, the gold becomes pliable, workable, and luminous, not brittle, and it is rightly suited for the work. Whatever ornament he wishes to make—whether a plate, an earring, a necklace, or a golden garland—he can achieve his purpose with it.”

‘‘Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyuttena bhikkhunā tīṇi nimittāni kālena kālaṃ manasi kātabbāni – kālena kālaṃ samādhinimittaṃ manasi kātabbaṃ, kālena kālaṃ paggahanimittaṃ manasi kātabbaṃ, kālena kālaṃ upekkhānimittaṃ manasi kātabbaṃ. Sace, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu ekantaṃ samādhinimittaṃyeva manasi kareyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ cittaṃ kosajjāya saṃvatteyya. Sace, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu ekantaṃ paggahanimittaṃyeva manasi kareyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ cittaṃ uddhaccāya saṃvatteyya. Sace, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu ekantaṃ upekkhānimittaṃyeva manasi kareyya, ṭhānaṃ taṃ cittaṃ na sammā samādhiyeyya āsavānaṃ khayāya. Yato ca kho, bhikkhave, adhicittamanuyutto bhikkhu kālena kālaṃ samādhinimittaṃ manasi karoti, kālena kālaṃ paggahanimittaṃ manasi karoti, kālena kālaṃ upekkhānimittaṃ manasi karoti, taṃ hoti cittaṃ muduñca kammaniyañca pabhassarañca, na ca pabhaṅgu, sammā samādhiyati āsavānaṃ khayāya. Yassa yassa ca abhiññāsacchikaraṇīyassa dhammassa cittaṃ abhininnāmeti abhiññāsacchikiriyāya, tatra tatreva sakkhibhabbataṃ pāpuṇāti sati satiāyatane.

“In the same way, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind should attend to three signs from time to time: he should attend to the sign of samādhi from time to time, to the sign of energy from time to time, and to the sign of equanimity from time to time. If, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind were to attend solely to the sign of samādhi, his mind would tend to sluggishness. If, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind were to attend solely to the sign of energy, his mind would tend to agitation. If, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind were to attend solely to the sign of equanimity, his mind would not be rightly collected for the destruction of the taints. But when, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu devoted to the higher mind attends to the sign of samādhi from time to time, to the sign of energy from time to time, and to the sign of equanimity from time to time, then his mind becomes pliable, workable, luminous, and not brittle, and it is rightly collected for the destruction of the taints. For whatever state realizable by direct knowledge he inclines his mind for direct knowledge, he is capable of realizing it in each and every case, whenever there is an opportunity.”

‘‘So sace ākaṅkhati – ‘anekavihitaṃ iddhividhaṃ paccanubhaveyyaṃ…pe… (cha abhiññā vitthāretabbā) āsavānaṃ khayā…pe… sacchikatvā upasampajja vihareyya’nti, tatra tatreva sakkhibhabbataṃ pāpuṇāti sati satiāyatane’’ti. Ekādasamaṃ.

“If he wishes: ‘May I experience various kinds of psychic power… May I, by realizing for myself with direct knowledge, in this very life, enter and dwell in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, through the destruction of the taints!’ he is capable of realizing this in each and every case, whenever there is an opportunity.” This is the eleventh.

Loṇakapallavaggo [loṇaphalavaggo (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] pañcamo.

The Chapter on the Salt Crystal.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Accāyikaṃ pavivekaṃ, sarado parisā tayo;Ājānīyā potthako ca, loṇaṃ dhovati nimittānīti.

Urgent, Seclusion, Autumn, three assemblies; The thoroughbred, the book, salt, washing, and signs.

Dutiyo paṇṇāsako samatto.

The Second Fifty is concluded.
