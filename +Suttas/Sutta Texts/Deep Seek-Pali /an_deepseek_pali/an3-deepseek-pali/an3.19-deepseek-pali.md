# AN 3.19 Paṭhamapāpaṇikasuttaṃ

## AN 3.19 The First Shopkeeper Sutta

19. ‘‘Tīhi, bhikkhave, aṅgehi samannāgato pāpaṇiko abhabbo anadhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ phātiṃ kātuṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Idha, bhikkhave, pāpaṇiko pubbaṇhasamayaṃ [majjhantikasamayaṃ (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] na sakkaccaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhāti, majjhanhikasamayaṃ na sakkaccaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhāti, sāyanhasamayaṃ na sakkaccaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhāti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi aṅgehi samannāgato pāpaṇiko abhabbo anadhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ phātiṃ kātuṃ [phātikattuṃ (sī.), phātikātuṃ (syā. kaṃ. pī.)].

19. Monks, a merchant endowed with three qualities is incapable of acquiring unacquired wealth, or of increasing acquired wealth. What three? Here, monks, a merchant does not diligently apply himself to work in the morning, does not diligently apply himself to work at midday, and does not diligently apply himself to work in the evening. Monks, a merchant endowed with these three qualities is incapable of acquiring unacquired wealth, or of increasing acquired wealth.

‘‘Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu abhabbo anadhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ phātiṃ kātuṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pubbaṇhasamayaṃ na sakkaccaṃ samādhinimittaṃ adhiṭṭhāti, majjhanhikasamayaṃ na sakkaccaṃ samādhinimittaṃ adhiṭṭhāti, sāyanhasamayaṃ na sakkaccaṃ samādhinimittaṃ adhiṭṭhāti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu abhabbo anadhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ phātiṃ kātuṃ.

In the same way, monks, a monk endowed with three states is incapable of acquiring unacquired wholesome states or of increasing acquired wholesome states. What three? Here, monks, a monk does not diligently apply himself to a sign of samādhi in the morning, does not diligently apply himself to a sign of samādhi at midday, and does not diligently apply himself to a sign of samādhi in the evening. Monks, a monk endowed with these three states is incapable of acquiring unacquired wholesome states or of increasing acquired wholesome states.

‘‘Tīhi, bhikkhave, aṅgehi samannāgato pāpaṇiko bhabbo anadhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ phātiṃ kātuṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Idha, bhikkhave, pāpaṇiko pubbaṇhasamayaṃ sakkaccaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhāti, majjhanhikasamayaṃ…pe… sāyanhasamayaṃ sakkaccaṃ kammantaṃ adhiṭṭhāti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi aṅgehi samannāgato pāpaṇiko bhabbo anadhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā bhogaṃ phātiṃ kātuṃ.

Monks, a merchant endowed with three qualities is capable of acquiring unacquired wealth, or of increasing acquired wealth. What three? Here, monks, a merchant diligently applies himself to his work in the morning, diligently applies himself to his work at midday, and diligently applies himself to his work in the evening. Monks, a merchant endowed with these three qualities is capable of acquiring unacquired wealth, or of increasing acquired wealth.

‘‘Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu bhabbo anadhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ phātiṃ kātuṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu pubbaṇhasamayaṃ sakkaccaṃ samādhinimittaṃ adhiṭṭhāti, majjhanhikasamayaṃ…pe… sāyanhasamayaṃ sakkaccaṃ samādhinimittaṃ adhiṭṭhāti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu bhabbo anadhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ adhigantuṃ, adhigataṃ vā kusalaṃ dhammaṃ phātiṃ kātu’’nti. Navamaṃ.

In the same way, monks, a monk endowed with three states is capable of acquiring unacquired wholesome states or of increasing acquired wholesome states. What three? Here, monks, a monk diligently applies himself to a sign of samādhi in the morning, diligently applies himself to a sign of samādhi at midday, and diligently applies himself to a sign of samādhi in the evening. Monks, a monk endowed with these three states is capable of acquiring unacquired wholesome states or of increasing acquired wholesome states. The ninth.
