# AN 3.60 Jāṇussoṇisuttaṃ

## AN 3.60 Jāṇussoṇi Sutta

60. Atha kho jāṇussoṇi brāhmaṇo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṃ…pe… ekamantaṃ nisinno kho jāṇussoṇi brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘yassassu, bho gotama, yañño vā saddhaṃ vā thālipāko vā deyyadhammaṃ vā, tevijjesu brāhmaṇesu dānaṃ dadeyyā’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, brāhmaṇa, brāhmaṇā tevijjaṃ paññapentī’’ti? ‘‘Idha kho, bho gotama, brāhmaṇo ubhato sujāto hoti mātito ca pitito ca saṃsuddhagahaṇiko yāva sattamā pitāmahayugā akkhitto anupakkuṭṭho jātivādena, ajjhāyako mantadharo, tiṇṇaṃ vedānaṃ pāragū sanighaṇḍukeṭubhānaṃ sākkharappabhedānaṃ itihāsapañcamānaṃ, padako, veyyākaraṇo, lokāyatamahāpurisalakkhaṇesu anavayoti. Evaṃ kho, bho gotama, brāhmaṇā tevijjaṃ paññapentī’’ti.

60.Then the brahmin Jāṇussoṇi approached the Blessed One… and sat down to one side. Seated, he said to the Blessed One: “Master Gotama, those who offer sacrifices, gifts of faith, rice offerings, or other gifts should give to brahmins who possess the three knowledges.” “But how, brahmin, do the brahmins define someone as possessing the three knowledges?” “Here, Master Gotama, a brahmin is well-born on both sides, maternal and paternal, of pure descent back to the seventh generation, unbroken and irreproachable in birth. He is a reciter and preserver of the hymns, a master of the three Vedas with their vocabularies, rituals, phonology, etymology, and legends as the fifth. He is skilled in word and grammar, and versed in cosmology and the marks of a great man. Thus, Master Gotama, the brahmins define someone as possessing the three knowledges.”

‘‘Aññathā kho, brāhmaṇa, brāhmaṇā brāhmaṇaṃ tevijjaṃ paññapenti, aññathā ca pana ariyassa vinaye tevijjo hotī’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, bho gotama, ariyassa vinaye tevijjo hoti? Sādhu me bhavaṃ gotamo tathā dhammaṃ desetu yathā ariyassa vinaye tevijjo hotī’’ti. ‘‘Tena hi, brāhmaṇa, suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho jāṇussoṇi brāhmaṇo bhagavato paccassosi. Bhagavā etadavoca –

“Brahmins describe a brahmin as possessing the three knowledges in one way, brahmin, but in the Noble One’s Discipline, one possesses the three knowledges in another way.” “But how, Master Gotama, does one possess the three knowledges in the Noble One’s Discipline? It would be good if Master Gotama would teach me the Dhamma so that I may understand how one possesses the three knowledges in the Noble One’s Discipline.” “Then listen, brahmin, and pay close attention. I will speak.” “Yes, sir,” replied Jāṇussoṇi the brahmin. The Blessed One said:

‘‘Idha pana, brāhmaṇa, bhikkhu vivicceva kāmehi…pe… catutthaṃ jhānaṃ upasampajja viharati.

“Here, brahmin, a bhikkhu, quite secluded from sensual pleasures... enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna.

‘‘So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte pubbenivāsānussatiñāṇāya cittaṃ abhininnāmeti. So anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati, seyyathidaṃ – ekampi jātiṃ dvepi jātiyo…pe… iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati. Ayamassa paṭhamā vijjā adhigatā hoti; avijjā vihatā, vijjā uppannā; tamo vihato, āloko uppanno yathā taṃ appamattassa ātāpino pahitattassa viharato.

When his mind is thus collected, purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilement, malleable, ready to act, firm, and imperturbable, he directs his mind to the knowledge of recollecting past lives. He recollects many kinds of past lives, that is, one birth, two births… so with aspects and details he recollects many kinds of past lives. This is the first true knowledge he has attained; ignorance is dispelled, true knowledge has arisen; darkness is dispelled, light has arisen, as it happens for one who dwells diligent, ardent, and resolute.”

‘‘So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte sattānaṃ cutūpapātañāṇāya cittaṃ abhininnāmeti. So dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena…pe… yathākammūpage satte pajānāti. Ayamassa dutiyā vijjā adhigatā hoti; avijjā vihatā, vijjā uppannā; tamo vihato, āloko uppanno yathā taṃ appamattassa ātāpino pahitattassa viharato.

“When his mind is thus collected, purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilements, malleable, manageable, steady, and attained to imperturbability, he directs his mind to the knowledge of the passing away and arising of beings. With the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, he sees beings passing away and arising... and he understands beings passing on according to their actions. This is his second true knowledge attained; ignorance is dispelled, true knowledge has arisen; darkness is dispelled, light has arisen, as it happens for one who dwells diligent, ardent, and resolute.”

‘‘So evaṃ samāhite citte parisuddhe pariyodāte anaṅgaṇe vigatūpakkilese mudubhūte kammaniye ṭhite āneñjappatte āsavānaṃ khayañāṇāya cittaṃ abhininnāmeti. So ‘idaṃ dukkha’nti yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti…pe… ‘ayaṃ dukkhanirodhagāminī paṭipadā’ti yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti; ‘ime āsavā’ti yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti…pe… ‘ayaṃ āsavanirodhagāminī paṭipadā’ti yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Tassa evaṃ jānato evaṃ passato kāmāsavāpi cittaṃ vimuccati, bhavāsavāpi cittaṃ vimuccati, avijjāsavāpi cittaṃ vimuccati; vimuttasmiṃ vimuttamiti ñāṇaṃ hoti. ‘Khīṇā jāti, vusitaṃ brahmacariyaṃ, kataṃ karaṇīyaṃ, nāparaṃ itthattāyā’ti pajānāti. Ayamassa tatiyā vijjā adhigatā hoti; avijjā vihatā, vijjā uppannā; tamo vihato, āloko uppanno yathā taṃ appamattassa ātāpino pahitattassa viharato’’ti.

“With his mind thus collected, purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilements, pliable, workable, firm, and having attained imperturbability, he directs and inclines it to knowledge of the destruction of the taints. He understands as it really is: ‘This is suffering’… ‘This is the origin of suffering’… ‘This is the cessation of suffering’… ‘This is the way leading to the cessation of suffering.’ He understands as it really is: ‘These are the taints’… ‘This is the origin of the taints’… ‘This is the cessation of the taints’… ‘This is the way leading to the cessation of the taints.’ Knowing and seeing thus, his mind is liberated from the taint of sensual desire, from the taint of being, and from the taint of ignorance. When it is liberated, there comes the knowledge that it is liberated. He understands: ‘Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being.’ This is his third true knowledge attained; ignorance is dispelled, true knowledge has arisen; darkness is dispelled, light has arisen, as it happens for one who dwells diligent, ardent, and resolute.”

‘‘Yo sīlabbatasampanno, pahitatto samāhito;Cittaṃ yassa vasībhūtaṃ, ekaggaṃ susamāhitaṃ.

“One who is accomplished in virtue and vows, with effort applied, composed; Whose mind is controlled, unified, and well-collected.

[dha. pa. 423; itivu. 99] ‘‘Pubbenivāsaṃ yo vedī, saggāpāyañca passati;Atho jātikkhayaṃ patto, abhiññāvosito muni.

He who knows his former lives, and sees heaven and woe; then, having attained the exhaustion of birth, the sage is one accomplished in higher knowledge.

‘‘Etāhi tīhi vijjāhi, tevijjo hoti brāhmaṇo;Tamahaṃ vadāmi tevijjaṃ, nāññaṃ lapitalāpana’’nti.

By these three true knowledges a brahmin is one who possesses the three true knowledges; him I call one who possesses the three true knowledges, not another’s idle talk.”

‘‘Evaṃ kho, brāhmaṇa, ariyassa vinaye tevijjo hotī’’ti. ‘‘Aññathā, bho gotama, brāhmaṇānaṃ tevijjo, aññathā ca pana ariyassa vinaye tevijjo hoti. Imassa ca, bho gotama, ariyassa vinaye tevijjassa brāhmaṇānaṃ tevijjo kalaṃ nāgghati soḷasiṃ’’.

“Thus, brahmin, in the Noble One’s Discipline one is a master of the three knowledges.” “The brahmins’ master of the three knowledges is one thing, Master Gotama, but the master of the three knowledges in the Noble One’s Discipline is another. And compared to this master of the three knowledges in the Noble One’s Discipline, the brahmins’ master of the three knowledges is not worth a sixteenth part.”

‘‘Abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama…pe… upāsakaṃ maṃ bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti. Navamaṃ.

“Excellent, Master Gotama... may the venerable Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has taken refuge from today forth, for as long as life lasts.” Ninth.
