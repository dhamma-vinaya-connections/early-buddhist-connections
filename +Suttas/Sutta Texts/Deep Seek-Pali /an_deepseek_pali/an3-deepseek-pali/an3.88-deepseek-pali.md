"Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, fulfills concentration, and fulfills wisdom. He may sometimes transgress and rise again from minor and insignificant training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, it is not said that he is incapable in this matter. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life, which accord with the holy life, he is steadfast in virtue, firm in virtue, and undertakes and trains himself in the training rules. With the destruction of the taints, he realizes for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life, the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, and having attained it, dwells in it.
