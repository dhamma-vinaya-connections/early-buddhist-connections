# AN 3.88 Tatiyasikkhāsuttaṃ

## AN 3.88 The Third Discourse on Training

89. ‘‘Sādhikamidaṃ, bhikkhave, diyaḍḍhasikkhāpadasataṃ anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchati yattha attakāmā kulaputtā sikkhanti. Tisso imā, bhikkhave, sikkhā yatthetaṃ sabbaṃ samodhānaṃ gacchati. Katamā tisso? Adhisīlasikkhā, adhicittasikkhā, adhipaññāsikkhā – imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso sikkhā yatthetaṃ sabbaṃ samodhānaṃ gacchati.

89."This, bhikkhus, is the more than 150 training rules that come up for recitation every half-month, in which sons of good families who desire their own good train themselves. These are the three trainings, bhikkhus, wherein all of that is comprised. What three? The training in higher virtue, the training in higher mind, and the training in higher wisdom. These, bhikkhus, are the three trainings wherein all of that is comprised."

‘‘Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ paripūrakārī paññāya paripūrakārī. So yāni tāni khuddānukhuddakāni sikkhāpadāni tāni āpajjatipi vuṭṭhātipi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi mettha, bhikkhave, abhabbatā vuttā. Yāni ca kho tāni sikkhāpadāni ādibrahmacariyakāni brahmacariyasāruppāni tattha dhuvasīlo ca hoti ṭhitasīlo ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā uddhaṃsoto hoti akaniṭṭhagāmī taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā, rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmī hoti, sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā ekabījī hoti, ekaṃyeva mānusakaṃ bhavaṃ nibbattetvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā kolaṃkolo hoti, dve vā tīṇi vā kulāni sandhāvitvā saṃsaritvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Taṃ vā pana anabhisambhavaṃ appaṭivijjhaṃ tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sattakkhattuparamo hoti, sattakkhattuparamaṃ deve ca manusse ca sandhāvitvā saṃsaritvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti.

"Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, fulfills samādhi, and fulfills wisdom. He may sometimes transgress and rise again from minor and insignificant training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, it is not said that he is incapable in this matter. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life, which accord with the holy life, he is steadfast in virtue, firm in virtue, and undertakes and trains himself in the training rules. With the destruction of the taints, he realizes for himself with direct knowledge, in this very life, the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, and having attained it, dwells in it. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he becomes one who attains Nibbāna in the interval. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he becomes one who attains Nibbāna upon landing. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he becomes one who attains Nibbāna without exertion. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he becomes one who attains Nibbāna with exertion. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of the five lower fetters, he becomes one bound upstream, heading toward the Akaniṭṭha realm. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of three fetters and the weakening of greed, hatred, and delusion, he becomes a once-returner, who returns only once more to this world and then makes an end of suffering. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of three fetters, he becomes a one-seeder, who takes rebirth only once more in the human realm and then makes an end of suffering. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of three fetters, he becomes a family-to-family traveler, who wanders and transmigrates through two or three families and then makes an end of suffering. Or, if he is unable to attain that, then with the destruction of three fetters, he becomes a seven-times-at-most traveler, who wanders and transmigrates among devas and humans seven times at most and then makes an end of suffering."

‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, paripūraṃ paripūrakārī ārādheti padesaṃ padesakārī. Avañjhānitvevāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sikkhāpadāni vadāmī’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

"Thus, bhikkhus, one who fulfills completely achieves the complete, and one who fulfills partially achieves the partial. Truly, bhikkhus, I say the training rules are not in vain." The eighth.
