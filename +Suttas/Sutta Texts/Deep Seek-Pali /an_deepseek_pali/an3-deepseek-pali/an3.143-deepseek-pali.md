# AN 3.143 Paṭhamamoranivāpasuttaṃ

## AN 3.143 The First Discourse at Moranivāpa

144. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā rājagahe viharati moranivāpe paribbājakārāme. Tatra kho bhagavā bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

144.On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Rājagaha, in Moranivāpa, the park of the wanderers. There the Blessed One addressed the bhikkhus: “Bhikkhus!” “Venerable Sir,” those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Tīhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussānaṃ. Katamehi tīhi? Asekkhena sīlakkhandhena, asekkhena samādhikkhandhena, asekkhena paññākkhandhena. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu accantaniṭṭho hoti accantayogakkhemī accantabrahmacārī accantapariyosāno seṭṭho devamanussāna’’nti. Ekādasamaṃ.

Bhikkhus, a bhikkhu endowed with three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate consummation, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate spiritual life, and reached the ultimate culmination, the best among devas and humans. What three? With the aggregate of virtue of one beyond training, the aggregate of samādhi of one beyond training, and the aggregate of wisdom of one beyond training. Bhikkhus, a bhikkhu endowed with these three qualities is one who has reached the ultimate consummation, attained ultimate security from bondage, lived the ultimate spiritual life, and reached the ultimate culmination, the best among devas and humans.” The eleventh.
