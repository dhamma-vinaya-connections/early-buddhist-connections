# AN 3.21 Samiddhasuttaṃ

## AN 3.21 The Discourse on Samiddha

21. Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Atha kho āyasmā ca samiddho [saviṭṭho (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] āyasmā ca mahākoṭṭhiko [mahākoṭṭhito (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā sāriputtena saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ samiddhaṃ āyasmā sāriputto etadavoca –

21.Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. Then the Venerable Samiddha and the Venerable Mahākoṭṭhika approached the Venerable Sāriputta. After exchanging pleasantries and agreeable conversation, they sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, the Venerable Sāriputta said to the Venerable Samiddha:

‘‘Tayome, āvuso samiddha, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Kāyasakkhī, diṭṭhippatto [diṭṭhappatto (ka.)], saddhāvimutto. Ime kho, āvuso, tayo puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Imesaṃ, āvuso, tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ katamo te puggalo khamati abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro cā’’ti?

“Friend Samiddha, these three persons are found existing in the world. What three? The body-witness, the view-attainer, and the faith-liberated. These, friend, are the three persons found existing in the world. Among these three persons, which one do you approve of as more outstanding and more sublime?”

‘‘Tayome, āvuso sāriputta, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Kāyasakkhī, diṭṭhippatto, saddhāvimutto. Ime kho, āvuso, tayo puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Imesaṃ, āvuso, tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ yvāyaṃ [yoyaṃ (ka.)] puggalo saddhāvimutto, ayaṃ me puggalo khamati imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro ca. Taṃ kissa hetu? Imassa, āvuso, puggalassa saddhindriyaṃ adhimatta’’nti.

“Friend Sāriputta, these three persons are found existing in the world. What three? The body-witness, the view-attainer, and the faith-liberated. These, friend, are the three persons found existing in the world. Among these three persons, the faith-liberated person is the one I approve of as more outstanding and more sublime. Why is that? Because, friend, this person’s faculty of faith is pre-eminent.”

Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantaṃ mahākoṭṭhikaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tayome, āvuso koṭṭhika, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Kāyasakkhī, diṭṭhippatto, saddhāvimutto. Ime kho, āvuso, tayo puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Imesaṃ, āvuso, tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ katamo te puggalo khamati abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro cā’’ti?

Then the Venerable Sāriputta addressed the Venerable Mahākoṭṭhika thus: “Friend Koṭṭhika, these three persons are found existing in the world. What three? The body-witness, the view-attainer, and the faith-liberated. These, friend, are the three persons found existing in the world. Of these three persons, which person do you approve of as more outstanding and more sublime?”

‘‘Tayome, āvuso sāriputta, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Kāyasakkhī, diṭṭhippatto, saddhāvimutto. Ime kho, āvuso, tayo puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Imesaṃ, āvuso, tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ yvāyaṃ puggalo kāyasakkhī, ayaṃ me puggalo khamati imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro ca. Taṃ kissa hetu? Imassa, āvuso, puggalassa samādhindriyaṃ adhimatta’’nti.

“Friend Sāriputta, these three persons are found existing in the world. What three? The body-witness, the view-attainer, and the faith-liberated. These, friend, are the three persons found existing in the world. Among these three persons, friend, the body-witness person is the one I approve of as more outstanding and more sublime. Why is that? Because, friend, this person’s faculty of samādhi is pre-eminent.”

Atha kho āyasmā mahākoṭṭhiko āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘tayome, āvuso sāriputta, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Kāyasakkhī, diṭṭhippatto, saddhāvimutto. Ime kho, āvuso, tayo puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Imesaṃ, āvuso, tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ katamo te puggalo khamati abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro cā’’ti?

Then the Venerable Mahākoṭṭhika said to the Venerable Sāriputta: “Friend Sāriputta, these three persons are found existing in the world. What three? The body-witness, the view-attainer, and the faith-liberated. These, friend, are the three persons found existing in the world. Among these three persons, friend, which one of them do you approve of as more outstanding and more sublime?”

‘‘Tayome, āvuso koṭṭhika, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo? Kāyasakkhī, diṭṭhippatto, saddhāvimutto. Ime kho, āvuso, tayo puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Imesaṃ, āvuso, tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ yvāyaṃ puggalo diṭṭhippatto, ayaṃ me puggalo khamati imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro ca. Taṃ kissa hetu? Imassa, āvuso, puggalassa paññindriyaṃ adhimatta’’nti.

“Friend Koṭṭhika, these three persons are found existing in the world. What three? The body-witness, the view-attainer, and the faith-liberated. These, friend, are the three persons found existing in the world. Among these three persons, friend, the view-attainer person is the one I approve of as more outstanding and more sublime. Why is that? Because, friend, this person’s faculty of wisdom is pre-eminent.”

Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto āyasmantañca samiddhaṃ āyasmantañca mahākoṭṭhikaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘byākataṃ kho, āvuso, amhehi sabbeheva yathāsakaṃ paṭibhānaṃ. Āyāmāvuso, yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamissāma; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavato etamatthaṃ ārocessāma. Yathā no bhagavā byākarissati tathā naṃ dhāressāmā’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho āyasmā ca samiddho āyasmā ca mahākoṭṭhiko āyasmato sāriputtassa paccassosuṃ. Atha kho āyasmā ca sāriputto āyasmā ca samiddho āyasmā ca mahākoṭṭhiko yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto yāvatako ahosi āyasmatā ca samiddhena āyasmatā ca mahākoṭṭhikena saddhiṃ kathāsallāpo taṃ sabbaṃ bhagavato ārocesi.

Then Venerable Sāriputta addressed Venerable Samiddha and Venerable Mahākoṭṭhika thus: “Friends, each of us has given his explanation according to his own inspiration. Come, friends, let us approach the Blessed One; having approached, we shall report this matter to the Blessed One. As the Blessed One explains, so shall we retain it.” “Agreed, friends,” Venerable Samiddha and Venerable Mahākoṭṭhika assented to Venerable Sāriputta. Then Venerable Sāriputta, Venerable Samiddha, and Venerable Mahākoṭṭhika approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, Venerable Sāriputta reported to the Blessed One their entire conversation.

‘‘Na khvettha, sāriputta, sukaraṃ ekaṃsena byākātuṃ – ‘ayaṃ imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro cā’ti. Ṭhānañhetaṃ, sāriputta, vijjati yvāyaṃ puggalo saddhāvimutto svāssa [svāyaṃ (syā. kaṃ. pī.), soyaṃ (ka.)] arahattāya paṭipanno, yvāyaṃ puggalo kāyasakkhī svāssa sakadāgāmī vā anāgāmī vā, yo cāyaṃ puggalo diṭṭhippatto sopassa [soyaṃ (ka.)] sakadāgāmī vā anāgāmī vā.

“Here, Sāriputta, it is not easy to declare definitively: ‘This one among these three persons is the more outstanding and more sublime.’ For it is possible, Sāriputta, that a faith-liberated person might be practicing for arahantship, while a body-witness might be a once-returner or a non-returner, and a view-attainer might also be a once-returner or a non-returner.”

‘‘Na khvettha, sāriputta, sukaraṃ ekaṃsena byākātuṃ – ‘ayaṃ imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro cā’ti. Ṭhānañhetaṃ, sāriputta, vijjati yvāyaṃ puggalo kāyasakkhī svāssa arahattāya paṭipanno, yvāyaṃ puggalo saddhāvimutto svāssa sakadāgāmī vā anāgāmī vā, yo cāyaṃ puggalo diṭṭhippatto sopassa sakadāgāmī vā anāgāmī vā.

“Here, Sāriputta, it is not easy to declare definitively: ‘This one among these three persons is the more outstanding and more sublime.’ For it is possible, Sāriputta, that a body-witness person might be practicing for arahantship, while a faith-liberated person may be a once-returner or a non-returner, and a view-attainer person may also be a once-returner or a non-returner.”

‘‘Na khvettha, sāriputta, sukaraṃ ekaṃsena byākātuṃ – ‘ayaṃ imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro cā’ti. Ṭhānañhetaṃ, sāriputta, vijjati yvāyaṃ puggalo diṭṭhippatto svāssa arahattāya paṭipanno, yvāyaṃ puggalo saddhāvimutto svāssa sakadāgāmī vā anāgāmī vā, yo cāyaṃ puggalo kāyasakkhī sopassa sakadāgāmī vā anāgāmī vā.

“Here, Sāriputta, it is not easy to declare definitively: ‘This one among these three persons is the more outstanding and more sublime.’ For it is possible, Sāriputta, that a view-attainer person might be practicing for arahantship, that a faith-liberated person may be a once-returner or a non-returner, and that a body-witness person may be a once-returner or a non-returner.”

‘‘Na khvettha, sāriputta, sukaraṃ ekaṃsena byākātuṃ – ‘ayaṃ imesaṃ tiṇṇaṃ puggalānaṃ abhikkantataro ca paṇītataro cā’’’ti. Paṭhamaṃ.

“Here, Sāriputta, it is not easy to declare definitively: ‘This one among these three persons is the more outstanding and more sublime.’” This is the first.
