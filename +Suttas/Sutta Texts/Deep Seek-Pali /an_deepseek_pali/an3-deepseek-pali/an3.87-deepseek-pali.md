# AN 3.87 Dutiyasikkhāsuttaṃ

## AN 3.87 The Second Training Rule Discourse

88. ‘‘Sādhikamidaṃ, bhikkhave, diyaḍḍhasikkhāpadasataṃ anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchati yattha attakāmā kulaputtā sikkhanti. Tisso imā, bhikkhave, sikkhā yatthetaṃ sabbaṃ samodhānaṃ gacchati. Katamā tisso? Adhisīlasikkhā, adhicittasikkhā, adhipaññāsikkhā – imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso sikkhā yatthetaṃ sabbaṃ samodhānaṃ gacchati.

88."This, bhikkhus, is the one hundred and fifty-plus training rules that comes up for recitation every half-month, in which sons of good families who desire their own welfare train themselves. These three trainings, bhikkhus, are those in which all of that is comprised. What three? The higher training in virtue, the higher training in mind, the higher training in wisdom—these, bhikkhus, are the three trainings in which all of that is comprised."

‘‘Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ mattaso kārī paññāya mattaso kārī. So yāni tāni khuddānukhuddakāni sikkhāpadāni tāni āpajjatipi vuṭṭhātipi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi mettha, bhikkhave, abhabbatā vuttā. Yāni ca kho tāni sikkhāpadāni ādibrahmacariyakāni brahmacariyasāruppāni tattha dhuvasīlo ca hoti ṭhitasīlo ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sattakkhattuparamo hoti. Sattakkhattuparamaṃ deve ca manusse ca sandhāvitvā saṃsaritvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā kolaṃkolo hoti, dve vā tīṇi vā kulāni sandhāvitvā saṃsaritvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā ekabījī hoti, ekaṃyeva mānusakaṃ bhavaṃ nibbattetvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmī hoti, sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti.

"Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, is moderate in samādhi, and is moderate in wisdom. He may fall into and rise out of minor and lesser training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, no incapacity has been declared in this regard. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life and in accordance with the holy life, he is constant in virtue and firmly established in virtue; having undertaken them, he trains in the training rules. With the destruction of the three fetters, he is a seven-times-at-most returner. Having wandered and transmigrated among devas and humans seven times at most, he makes an end of suffering. With the destruction of the three fetters, he is a family-to-family returner. Having wandered and transmigrated among two or three families, he makes an end of suffering. With the destruction of the three fetters, he is a one-seed returner. Having been reborn in the human world just once, he makes an end of suffering. With the destruction of the three fetters and the attenuation of lust, hatred, and delusion, he is a once-returner. Having come back to this world just once, he makes an end of suffering."

‘‘Idha pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ paripūrakārī paññāya mattaso kārī. So yāni tāni khuddānukhuddakāni sikkhāpadāni tāni āpajjatipi vuṭṭhātipi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi mettha, bhikkhave, abhabbatā vuttā. Yāni ca kho tāni sikkhāpadāni ādibrahmacariyakāni brahmacariyasāruppāni tattha dhuvasīlo ca hoti ṭhitasīlo ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā uddhaṃsoto akaniṭṭhagāmī. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti.

"Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, fulfills samādhi, but is moderate in wisdom. He may fall into and rise out of minor and lesser training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, no incapacity has been declared in this regard. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life and in accordance with the holy life, he is constant in virtue and firmly established in virtue; having undertaken them, he trains in the training rules. With the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is one who goes upstream, bound for the Akaniṭṭha realm. With the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is one who attains Nibbāna with exertion. With the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is one who attains Nibbāna without exertion. With the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is one who attains Nibbāna after encountering difficulties. With the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is one who attains Nibbāna in the interval."

‘‘Idha pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ paripūrakārī paññāya paripūrakārī. So yāni tāni dhuvasīlo ca hoti ṭhitasīlo ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati.

"Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, fulfills samādhi, and fulfills wisdom. He is constant in virtue and firmly established in virtue; having undertaken them, he trains in the training rules. With the destruction of the taints, having realized for himself with direct knowledge in this very life the taintless liberation of mind and liberation by wisdom, he enters and dwells in it."

‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, padesaṃ padesakārī ārādheti, paripūraṃ paripūrakārī, avañjhāni tvevāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sikkhāpadāni vadāmī’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

"Thus, bhikkhus, one who performs partially achieves partially, one who performs fully achieves fully. Indeed, bhikkhus, I say the training rules are not barren." The Seventh.
