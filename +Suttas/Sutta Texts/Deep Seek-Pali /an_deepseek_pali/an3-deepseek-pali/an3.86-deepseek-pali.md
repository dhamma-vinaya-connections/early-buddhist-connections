# AN 3.86 Paṭhamasikkhāsuttaṃ

## AN 3.86 The First Discourse on Training

87. ‘‘Sādhikamidaṃ, bhikkhave, diyaḍḍhasikkhāpadasataṃ anvaddhamāsaṃ uddesaṃ āgacchati, yattha attakāmā kulaputtā sikkhanti. Tisso imā, bhikkhave, sikkhā yatthetaṃ sabbaṃ samodhānaṃ gacchati. Katamā tisso? Adhisīlasikkhā, adhicittasikkhā adhipaññāsikkhā – imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso sikkhā, yatthetaṃ sabbaṃ samodhānaṃ gacchati.

87.“Bhikkhus, these are more than one hundred and fifty training rules that come up for recitation every half-month, wherein clansmen who desire their own welfare train themselves. Bhikkhus, these are the three trainings wherein all this is comprised. What three? The higher training in virtue, the higher training in mind, and the higher training in wisdom—these, bhikkhus, are the three trainings wherein all this is comprised.”

‘‘Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ mattaso kārī paññāya mattaso kārī. So yāni tāni khuddānukhuddakāni sikkhāpadāni tāni āpajjatipi vuṭṭhātipi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi mettha, bhikkhave, abhabbatā vuttā. Yāni ca kho tāni sikkhāpadāni ādibrahmacariyakāni brahmacariyasāruppāni, tattha dhuvasīlo [dhuvasīlī (sī.) pu. pa. 127-129 (thokaṃ visadisaṃ)] ca hoti ṭhitasīlo [ṭhitasīlī (sī.)] ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sotāpanno hoti avinipātadhammo niyato sambodhiparāyaṇo.

“Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, makes moderate effort in samādhi, and makes moderate effort in wisdom. He may commit and recover from minor and trifling training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, no inability has been declared in this regard. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life, conforming to the holy life, he is steadfast in virtue and firmly established in virtue, undertaking and training himself in the training rules. With the destruction of the three fetters, he is a stream-enterer, no longer subject to downfall, fixed in destiny, with enlightenment as his destination.”

‘‘Idha pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ mattaso kārī paññāya mattaso kārī. So yāni tāni khuddānukhuddakāni sikkhāpadāni tāni āpajjatipi vuṭṭhātipi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi mettha, bhikkhave, abhabbatā vuttā. Yāni ca kho tāni sikkhāpadāni ādibrahmacariyakāni brahmacariyasāruppāni tattha dhuvasīlo ca hoti ṭhitasīlo ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmī hoti, sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti.

“Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, makes moderate effort in samādhi, and makes moderate effort in wisdom. He may commit and recover from minor and trifling training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, no inability has been declared in this regard. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life, conforming to the holy life, he is steadfast in virtue and firmly established in virtue, undertaking and training himself in the training rules. With the destruction of the three fetters, and with the attenuation of greed, hatred, and delusion, he is a once-returner, who, having come back to this world just once more, will make an end of suffering.”

‘‘Idha pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ paripūrakārī paññāya mattaso kārī. So yāni tāni khuddānukhuddakāni sikkhāpadāni tāni āpajjatipi vuṭṭhātipi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi mettha, bhikkhave, abhabbatā vuttā. Yāni ca kho tāni sikkhāpadāni ādibrahmacariyakāni brahmacariyasāruppāni tattha dhuvasīlo ca hoti ṭhitasīlo ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā opapātiko hoti tattha parinibbāyī anāvattidhammo tasmā lokā.

“Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, fulfills samādhi, but makes moderate effort in wisdom. He may commit and recover from minor and trifling training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, no inability has been declared in this regard. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life, conforming to the holy life, he is steadfast in virtue and firmly established in virtue, undertaking and training himself in the training rules. With the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is reborn spontaneously and attains final Nibbāna there, without returning from that world.”

‘‘Idha pana, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti samādhismiṃ paripūrakārī paññāya paripūrakārī. So yāni tāni khuddānukhuddakāni sikkhāpadāni tāni āpajjatipi vuṭṭhātipi. Taṃ kissa hetu? Na hi mettha, bhikkhave, abhabbatā vuttā. Yāni ca kho tāni sikkhāpadāni ādibrahmacariyakāni brahmacariyasāruppāni tattha dhuvasīlo ca hoti ṭhitasīlo ca, samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu. So āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati.

“Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is one who fulfills virtue, fulfills samādhi, and fulfills wisdom. He may commit and recover from minor and trifling training rules. Why is that? Because, bhikkhus, no inability has been declared in this regard. But as for those training rules that are fundamental to the holy life, conforming to the holy life, he is steadfast in virtue and firmly established in virtue, undertaking and training himself in the training rules. Through the destruction of the taints, having realized for himself with direct knowledge in this very life the taintless liberation of mind and liberation by wisdom, he abides in it.”

‘‘Iti kho, bhikkhave, padesaṃ padesakārī ārādheti paripūraṃ paripūrakārī. Avañjhāni tvevāhaṃ [avañcuvanevāhaṃ (ka.)], bhikkhave, sikkhāpadāni vadāmī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“Thus, bhikkhus, one who acts partially achieves a part; one who acts fully achieves the full. Truly, bhikkhus, I declare the training rules are not barren.”
