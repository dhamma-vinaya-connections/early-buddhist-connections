# AN 3.32 Ānandasuttaṃ

## AN 3.32 The Ānanda Sutta

32. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

32.Then venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One. Having approached and paid homage to the Blessed One, he sat down to one side. Seated to one side, venerable Ānanda said this to the Blessed One:

‘‘Siyā nu kho, bhante, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā imasmiñca saviññāṇake kāye ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu, bahiddhā ca sabbanimittesu ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu; yañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja viharato ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā na honti tañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja vihareyyā’’ti? ‘‘Siyā, ānanda, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā imasmiñca saviññāṇake kāye ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu, bahiddhā ca sabbanimittesu ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu; yañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja viharato ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā na honti tañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja vihareyyā’’ti.

Venerable sir, could there be for a monk such an attainment of samādhi that in this conscious body there would be no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit, and that externally, in all signs, there would be no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit, and that he would dwell having attained that liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, wherein there are no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit?” “There could be, Ananda, such an attainment of samādhi for a monk that in this conscious body there would be no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit, and that externally, in all signs, there would be no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit, and that he would dwell having attained that liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, wherein there are no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit.”

‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, bhante, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā imasmiñca saviññāṇake kāye ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu, bahiddhā ca sabbanimittesu ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu; yañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja viharato ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā na honti tañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja vihareyyā’’ti?

But how, venerable sir, could there be for a monk such an attainment of samādhi that in this conscious body there would be no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit, and that externally, in all signs, there would be no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit, and that he would dwell having attained that liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, wherein there are no latent tendencies to ‘I-making,’ ‘mine-making,’ and conceit?

‘‘Idhānanda, bhikkhuno evaṃ hoti – ‘etaṃ santaṃ etaṃ paṇītaṃ yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbāna’nti. Evaṃ kho, ānanda, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā imasmiñca saviññāṇake kāye ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu, bahiddhā ca sabbanimittesu ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā nāssu; yañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja viharato ahaṅkāramamaṅkāramānānusayā na honti tañca cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ upasampajja vihareyyā’’ti.

Here, Ānanda, it occurs to a bhikkhu: ‘This is peaceful, this is sublime, namely the stilling of all activities, the surrender of all acquisitions, the destruction of craving, detachment, cessation, Nibbāna.’ Thus, Ānanda, a bhikkhu might attain such an attainment of samādhi that there would be no I-making, mine-making, or latent tendency to conceit in this very conscious body, nor in regard to all external signs; and he might attain to and abide in that liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, through which there are no I-making, mine-making, or latent tendencies to conceit.

‘‘Idañca pana metaṃ, ānanda, sandhāya bhāsitaṃ pārāyane puṇṇakapañhe –

And this, Ānanda, was spoken by me with reference to what was said in the Pārāyana, in Puṇṇaka's Question:

‘‘Saṅkhāya lokasmiṃ paroparāni [parovarāni (sī. pī.) su. ni. 1054; cūḷani. puṇṇakamāṇavapucchā 73],Yassiñjitaṃ natthi kuhiñci loke;Santo vidhūmo anīgho [anigho (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.), anagho (?)] nirāso,Atāri so jātijaranti brūmī’’ti. dutiyaṃ;

Having weighed the high and low in the world, in whom there is no wavering anywhere in the world, calm, without smoke, without distress, without longing—he has crossed over birth and aging, I say.
