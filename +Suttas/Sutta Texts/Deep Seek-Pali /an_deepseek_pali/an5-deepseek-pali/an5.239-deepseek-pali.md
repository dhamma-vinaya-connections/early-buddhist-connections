# AN 5.239 Paṭhamamacchariyasuttaṃ

## AN 5.239 The First Discourse on Stinginess

239. ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ niraye. Katamehi pañcahi? Āvāsamaccharī hoti; kulamaccharī hoti; lābhamaccharī hoti; vaṇṇamaccharī [dhammamaccharī (ka.)] hoti; saddhādeyyaṃ vinipāteti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ niraye.

239.Monks, a resident monk endowed with five qualities is cast into hell as if carried there. What five? He is stingy regarding dwellings; he is stingy regarding families; he is stingy regarding gains; he is stingy regarding praise; he ruins offerings given in faith. Monks, a resident monk endowed with these five qualities is cast into hell as if carried there.

‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ sagge. Katamehi pañcahi? Na āvāsamaccharī hoti; na kulamaccharī hoti; na lābhamaccharī hoti; na vaṇṇamaccharī hoti; saddhādeyyaṃ na vinipāteti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ sagge’’ti. Navamaṃ.

Monks, a resident monk endowed with five qualities is deposited in heaven as if brought there. What five? He is not stingy regarding dwellings; he is not stingy regarding families; he is not stingy regarding gains; he is not stingy regarding praise; he does not ruin offerings given in faith. Monks, a resident monk endowed with these five qualities is deposited in heaven as if brought there." The ninth.
