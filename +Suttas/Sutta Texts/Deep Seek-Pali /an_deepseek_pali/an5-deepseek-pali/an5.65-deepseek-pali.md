# AN 5.65 Sākacchasuttaṃ

## AN 5.65 The Discussion Discourse

65. [a. ni. 5.164] ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu alaṃsākaccho sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Katamehi pañcahi? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu attanā ca sīlasampanno hoti, sīlasampadāya kathāya ca āgataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca samādhisampanno hoti, samādhisampadāya kathāya ca āgataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca paññāsampanno hoti, paññāsampadāya kathāya ca āgataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca vimuttisampanno hoti, vimuttisampadāya kathāya ca āgataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampanno hoti, vimuttiñāṇadassanasampadāya kathāya ca āgataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu alaṃsākaccho sabrahmacārīna’’nti. Pañcamaṃ.

65."Monks, a monk endowed with five qualities is fit for discussion among his fellow practitioners. What five? Here, monks, a monk is himself accomplished in virtue and is able to answer a question that has arisen concerning accomplishment in virtue; he is himself accomplished in samādhi and is able to answer a question that has arisen concerning accomplishment in samādhi; he is himself accomplished in wisdom and is able to answer a question that has arisen concerning accomplishment in wisdom; he is himself accomplished in liberation and is able to answer a question that has arisen concerning accomplishment in liberation; he is himself accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation and is able to answer a question that has arisen concerning accomplishment in the knowledge and vision of liberation. Monks, a monk endowed with these five qualities is fit for discussion among his fellow practitioners."
