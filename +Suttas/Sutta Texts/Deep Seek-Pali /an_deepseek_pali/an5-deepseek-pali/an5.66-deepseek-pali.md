# AN 5.66 Sājīvasuttaṃ

## AN 5.66 The Livelihood Discourse

66. [a. ni. 5.164] ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu alaṃsājīvo sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Katamehi pañcahi? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu attanā ca sīlasampanno hoti, sīlasampadāya kathāya ca kataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca samādhisampanno hoti, samādhisampadāya kathāya ca kataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca paññāsampanno hoti, paññāsampadāya kathāya ca kataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca vimuttisampanno hoti, vimuttisampadāya kathāya ca kataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti; attanā ca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampanno hoti, vimuttiñāṇadassanasampadāya kathāya ca kataṃ pañhaṃ byākattā hoti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu alaṃsājīvo sabrahmacārīna’’nti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

66."Monks, a monk endowed with five qualities is fit for communal life among his fellow practitioners. What five? Here, monks, a monk is himself accomplished in virtue and is able to answer a question that has been posed concerning accomplishment in virtue; he is himself accomplished in samādhi and is able to answer a question that has been posed concerning accomplishment in samādhi; he is himself accomplished in wisdom and is able to answer a question that has been posed concerning accomplishment in wisdom; he is himself accomplished in liberation and is able to answer a question that has been posed concerning accomplishment in liberation; he is himself accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation and is able to answer a question that has been posed concerning accomplishment in the knowledge and vision of liberation. Monks, a monk endowed with these five qualities is fit for communal life among his fellow practitioners."
