# AN 5.22 Dutiyaagāravasuttaṃ

## AN 5.22 The Second Discourse on Disrespect

22. ‘‘So vata, bhikkhave, bhikkhu agāravo appatisso asabhāgavuttiko ‘sabrahmacārīsu ābhisamācārikaṃ dhammaṃ paripūressatī’ti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. ‘Ābhisamācārikaṃ dhammaṃ aparipūretvā sekhaṃ dhammaṃ paripūressatī’ti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. ‘Sekhaṃ dhammaṃ aparipūretvā sīlakkhandhaṃ paripūressatī’ti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. ‘Sīlakkhandhaṃ aparipūretvā samādhikkhandhaṃ paripūressatī’ti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati. ‘Samādhikkhandhaṃ aparipūretvā paññākkhandhaṃ paripūressatī’ti netaṃ ṭhānaṃ vijjati.

22. "Monks, it is impossible that a monk who is without respect and deference, and lives unsympathetically with his spiritual companions, will fulfill the rules of courteous behavior among his fellows in the spiritual life. It is impossible that, not fulfilling the rules of courteous behavior, he will fulfill the training of a learner. It is impossible that, not fulfilling the training of a learner, he will fulfill the aggregate of moral discipline. It is impossible that, not fulfilling the aggregate of moral discipline, he will fulfill the aggregate of samādhi. It is impossible that, not fulfilling the aggregate of samādhi, he will fulfill the aggregate of wisdom."

‘‘So vata, bhikkhave, bhikkhu sagāravo sappatisso sabhāgavuttiko ‘sabrahmacārīsu ābhisamācārikaṃ dhammaṃ paripūressatī’ti ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. ‘Ābhisamācārikaṃ dhammaṃ paripūretvā sekhaṃ dhammaṃ paripūressatī’ti ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Sekhaṃ dhammaṃ paripūretvā sīlakkhandhaṃ paripūressatī’ti ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. ‘Sīlakkhandhaṃ paripūretvā samādhikkhandhaṃ paripūressatī’ti ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. ‘Samādhikkhandhaṃ paripūretvā paññākkhandhaṃ paripūressatī’ti ṭhānametaṃ vijjatī’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

"But, monks, it is possible that a monk who is respectful and deferential, and lives sympathetically with his spiritual companions, will fulfill the rules of courteous behavior among his fellows in the spiritual life. Having fulfilled the rules of courteous behavior, it is possible that he will fulfill the training of a learner. Having fulfilled the training of a learner, it is possible that he will fulfill the aggregate of moral discipline. Having fulfilled the aggregate of moral discipline, it is possible that he will fulfill the aggregate of samādhi. Having fulfilled the aggregate of samādhi, it is possible that he will fulfill the aggregate of wisdom." The second.
