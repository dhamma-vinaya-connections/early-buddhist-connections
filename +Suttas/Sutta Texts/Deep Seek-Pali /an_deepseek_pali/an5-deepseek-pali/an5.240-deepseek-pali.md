# AN 5.240 Dutiyamacchariyasuttaṃ

## AN 5.240 The Second Discourse on Stinginess

240. ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ niraye. Katamehi pañcahi? Āvāsamaccharī hoti; kulamaccharī hoti; lābhamaccharī hoti; vaṇṇamaccharī hoti; dhammamaccharī hoti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ niraye.

240.Monks, a resident monk endowed with five qualities is cast into hell as if carried there. What five? He is stingy regarding dwellings; he is stingy regarding families; he is stingy regarding gains; he is stingy regarding recognition; he is stingy regarding the Dhamma. Monks, a resident monk endowed with these five qualities is cast into hell as if carried there.

‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ sagge. Katamehi pañcahi? Na āvāsamaccharī hoti; na kulamaccharī hoti; na lābhamaccharī hoti; na vaṇṇamaccharī hoti; na dhammamaccharī hoti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu yathābhataṃ nikkhitto evaṃ sagge’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

Monks, a resident monk endowed with five qualities is deposited in heaven as if brought there. What five? He is not stingy regarding dwellings; he is not stingy regarding families; he is not stingy regarding gains; he is not stingy regarding recognition; he is not stingy regarding the Dhamma. Monks, a resident monk endowed with these five qualities is deposited in heaven as if brought there." The tenth.

Āvāsikavaggo catuttho.

The Resident Chapter, the Fourth.

Tassuddānaṃ –

Its summary:

Āvāsiko piyo ca sobhano,Bahūpakāro anukampako ca;Tayo avaṇṇārahā ceva,Macchariyā duvepi cāti [yathābhataṃ cāpi avaṇṇagedhā, catukkamacchera pañcamena cāti (sī. syā.) yathābhataṃ avaṇṇañca, catuko macchariyena cāti (ka.)].

The resident monk, dear and pleasing,Very helpful and compassionate;These three are undeserving of blame,And also both kinds of stinginess.

(25) 5. Duccaritavaggo

(25) 5. The Chapter on Bad Conduct
