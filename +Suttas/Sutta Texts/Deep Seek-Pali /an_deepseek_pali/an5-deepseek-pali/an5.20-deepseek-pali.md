# AN 5.20 Catutthahitasuttaṃ

## AN 5.20 The Fourth Discourse on Welfare

20. ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu attahitāya ca paṭipanno hoti parahitāya ca. Katamehi pañcahi? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu attanā ca sīlasampanno hoti, parañca sīlasampadāya samādapeti; attanā ca samādhisampanno hoti, parañca samādhisampadāya samādapeti, attanā ca paññāsampanno hoti, parañca paññāsampadāya samādapeti; attanā ca vimuttisampanno hoti, parañca vimuttisampadāya samādapeti; attanā ca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampanno hoti, parañca vimuttiñāṇadassanasampadāya samādapeti. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato bhikkhu attahitāya ca paṭipanno hoti parahitāya cā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

20. Monks, a monk endowed with five qualities is one who practices for his own welfare and for the welfare of others. What five? Here, monks, a monk is complete in virtue himself and establishes others in virtue's accomplishment; he is complete in samādhi himself and establishes others in samādhi's accomplishment; he is complete in wisdom himself and establishes others in wisdom's accomplishment; he is complete in liberation himself and establishes others in liberation's accomplishment; he is complete in the knowledge and vision of liberation himself and establishes others in the knowledge and vision of liberation's accomplishment. Monks, a monk endowed with these five qualities is one who practices for his own welfare and for the welfare of others. The tenth.

Balavaggo dutiyo.
