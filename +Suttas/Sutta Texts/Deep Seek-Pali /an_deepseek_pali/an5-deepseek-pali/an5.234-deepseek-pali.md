# AN 5.234 Bahūpakārasuttaṃ

## AN 5.234 The Discourse on Great Help

234. ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu āvāsassa bahūpakāro hoti. Katamehi pañcahi? Sīlavā hoti…pe… samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu; bahussuto hoti…pe… diṭṭhiyā suppaṭividdhā; khaṇḍaphullaṃ paṭisaṅkharoti; mahā kho pana bhikkhusaṅgho abhikkanto nānāverajjakā bhikkhū gihīnaṃ upasaṅkamitvā āroceti – ‘mahā kho, āvuso, bhikkhusaṅgho abhikkanto nānāverajjakā bhikkhū, karotha puññāni, samayo puññāni kātu’nti; catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī hoti akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu āvāsassa bahūpakāro hotī’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

234.“Monks, a resident bhikkhu endowed with five qualities is of great help to the monastery. With which five? Here, he is virtuous… he undertakes the training and trains in the training rules; he is learned… penetrated well by understanding; he restores what is broken and fallen away; when a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus has arrived, bhikkhus from various regions, he approaches the householders and announces: ‘Friends, a large Saṅgha of bhikkhus has arrived, bhikkhus from various regions. Do meritorious deeds! Now is the time to do meritorious deeds!’; he obtains at will, without difficulty, without trouble, the four jhānas that are of the higher mind, pleasant abidings in this very life. Monks, a resident bhikkhu endowed with these five qualities is of great help to the monastery.” This is the fourth discourse.
