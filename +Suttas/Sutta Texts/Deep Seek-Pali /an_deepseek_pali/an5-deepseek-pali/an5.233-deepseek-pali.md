# AN 5.233 Sobhanasuttaṃ

## AN 5.233 The Discourse on Adornment

233. ‘‘Pañcahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu āvāsaṃ sobheti. Katamehi pañcahi? Sīlavā hoti…pe… samādāya sikkhati sikkhāpadesu; bahussuto hoti…pe… diṭṭhiyā suppaṭividdhā; kalyāṇavāco hoti kalyāṇavākkaraṇo poriyā vācāya samannāgato vissaṭṭhāya anelagalāya atthassa viññāpaniyā; paṭibalo hoti upasaṅkamante dhammiyā kathāya sandassetuṃ samādapetuṃ samuttejetuṃ sampahaṃsetuṃ; catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī hoti akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi dhammehi samannāgato āvāsiko bhikkhu āvāsaṃ sobhetī’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

233.“Monks, a resident bhikkhu endowed with five qualities adorns the monastery. With which five? Here, he is virtuous… he undertakes the training and trains in the training rules; he is learned… penetrated well by understanding; he is one of good speech, one of good utterance, endowed with refined, clear, and articulate speech that conveys the meaning; he is capable of instructing, urging, rousing, and gladdening those who approach him with a Dhamma talk; he obtains at will, without difficulty, without trouble, the four jhānas that are of the higher mind, pleasant abidings in this very life. Monks, a resident bhikkhu endowed with these five qualities adorns the monastery.” This is the third discourse.
