18. "Monks, a monk endowed with five qualities is practicing for the benefit of others, not for his own benefit. What five? Here, monks, a monk is not himself accomplished in morality, but encourages others in the accomplishment of morality; he is not himself accomplished in concentration, but encourages others in the accomplishment of concentration; he is not himself accomplished in wisdom, but encourages others in the accomplishment of wisdom; he is not himself accomplished in liberation, but encourages others in the accomplishment of liberation; he is not himself accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation, but encourages others in the accomplishment of the knowledge and vision of liberation. Monks, a monk endowed with these five qualities is practicing for the benefit of others, not for his own benefit." The eighth.
