161."Monks, there are these five ways of removing ill-will, by which ill-will that has arisen in a bhikkhu should be entirely removed. Which five? When ill-will arises toward a certain person, loving-kindness should be developed toward that person; thus ill-will toward that person should be removed. When ill-will arises toward a certain person, compassion should be developed toward that person; thus ill-will toward that person should be removed. When ill-will arises toward a certain person, equanimity should be developed toward that person; thus ill-will toward that person should be removed. When ill-will arises toward a certain person, non-attention to that person should be applied; thus ill-will toward that person should be removed. When ill-will arises toward a certain person, ownership of deeds should be established in regard to that personâ€”'This venerable one is the owner of their deeds, heir to their deeds, deeds are the womb from which they sprang, deeds are their relative, deeds are their refuge. Whatever deeds they do, good or bad, they will be the heir to those deeds'; thus ill-will toward that person should be removed. These, monks, are the five ways of removing ill-will, by which ill-will that has arisen in a bhikkhu should be entirely removed." First.
