19. "Monks, a monk endowed with five qualities does not practice for his own welfare, nor for the welfare of others. Which five? Here, monks, a monk is not accomplished in virtue himself, nor does he encourage others to be accomplished in virtue; he is not accomplished in concentration himself, nor does he encourage others to be accomplished in concentration; he is not accomplished in wisdom himself, nor does he encourage others to be accomplished in wisdom; he is not accomplished in liberation himself, nor does he encourage others to be accomplished in liberation; he is not accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation himself, nor does he encourage others to be accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation. Monks, a monk endowed with these five qualities does not practice for his own welfare, nor for the welfare of others." The ninth.
