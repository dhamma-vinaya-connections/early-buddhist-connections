# AN 5.167 Codanāsuttaṃ

## AN 5.167 The Discourse on Censure

167. Tatra kho āyasmā sāriputto bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘codakena, āvuso, bhikkhunā paraṃ codetukāmena pañca dhamme ajjhattaṃ upaṭṭhāpetvā paro codetabbo’’.

167.There the Venerable Sāriputta addressed the monks: "Friends, a monk who wishes to censure another should first establish five qualities within himself before censuring another."

‘‘Katame pañca? Kālena vakkhāmi, no akālena; bhūtena vakkhāmi, no abhūtena; saṇhena vakkhāmi, no pharusena; atthasaṃhitena vakkhāmi, no anatthasaṃhitena; mettacitto [mettacittena (sī. pī. ka.) cūḷava. 400 passitabbaṃ] vakkhāmi, no dosantaro [dosantarena (sī. pī. ka.)]. Codakena, āvuso, bhikkhunā paraṃ codetukāmena ime pañca dhamme ajjhattaṃ upaṭṭhāpetvā paro codetabbo.

"What five? I will speak at the right time, not at the wrong time; I will speak truthfully, not falsely; I will speak gently, not harshly; I will speak beneficially, not unbeneficially; I will speak with a mind of loving-kindness, not with inner hatred. A monk, friend, who wishes to censure another should first establish these five qualities within himself before censuring another."

‘‘Idhāhaṃ, āvuso, ekaccaṃ puggalaṃ passāmi akālena codiyamānaṃ no kālena kupitaṃ, abhūtena codiyamānaṃ no bhūtena kupitaṃ, pharusena codiyamānaṃ no saṇhena kupitaṃ, anatthasaṃhitena codiyamānaṃ no atthasaṃhitena kupitaṃ, dosantarena codiyamānaṃ no mettacittena kupitaṃ.

"Here, friends, I see a certain person who, when censured at the wrong time, gets angry; when censured with what is untrue, gets angry; when censured harshly, gets angry; when censured with what is unbeneficial, gets angry; when censured with inner hatred, gets angry."

‘‘Adhammacuditassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno pañcahākārehi avippaṭisāro upadahātabbo [upadahitabbo (sī. syā. pī.)] – ‘akālenāyasmā cudito no kālena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; abhūtenāyasmā cudito no bhūtena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; pharusenāyasmā cudito no saṇhena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; anatthasaṃhitenāyasmā cudito no atthasaṃhitena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; dosantarenāyasmā cudito no mettacittena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāyā’ti. Adhammacuditassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno imehi pañcahākārehi avippaṭisāro upadahātabbo.

"Friends, a monk who has been censured not in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to be without remorse in five ways: ‘Venerable, you were censured at the wrong time, not at the right time—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You were censured with what is untrue, not with what is true—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You were censured harshly, not gently—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You were censured with what is unbeneficial, not with what is beneficial—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You were censured with inner hatred, not with a mind of loving-kindness—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse.’ Friends, a monk who has been censured not in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to be without remorse in these five ways."

‘‘Adhammacodakassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno pañcahākārehi vippaṭisāro upadahātabbo – ‘akālena te, āvuso, codito [cudito (sī. syā. pī.)] no kālena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; abhūtena te, āvuso, codito no bhūtena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; pharusena te, āvuso, codito no saṇhena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; anatthasaṃhitena te, āvuso, codito no atthasaṃhitena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; dosantarena te, āvuso, codito no mettacittena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāyā’ti. Adhammacodakassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno imehi pañcahākārehi vippaṭisāro upadahātabbo. Taṃ kissa hetu? Yathā na aññopi bhikkhu abhūtena codetabbaṃ maññeyyāti.

"Friend, a monk who censures not in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to feel remorse in five ways: ‘Friend, you censured at the wrong time, not at the right time—this is sufficient for your remorse. You censured with what is untrue, not with what is true—this is sufficient for your remorse. You censured harshly, not gently—this is sufficient for your remorse. You censured with what is unbeneficial, not with what is beneficial—this is sufficient for your remorse. You censured with inner hatred, not with a mind of loving-kindness—this is sufficient for your remorse.’ Friend, a monk who censures not in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to feel remorse in these five ways. Why is that? So that no other monk might think that one should censure with what is untrue."

‘‘Idha panāhaṃ, āvuso, ekaccaṃ puggalaṃ passāmi kālena codiyamānaṃ no akālena kupitaṃ, bhūtena codiyamānaṃ no abhūtena kupitaṃ, saṇhena codiyamānaṃ no pharusena kupitaṃ, atthasaṃhitena codiyamānaṃ no anatthasaṃhitena kupitaṃ, mettacittena codiyamānaṃ no dosantarena kupitaṃ.

"But here, friend, I see a certain person who, when censured at the right time, does not get angry; when censured truthfully, does not get angry; when censured gently, does not get angry; when censured beneficially, does not get angry; when censured with a mind of loving-kindness, does not get angry."

‘‘Dhammacuditassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno pañcahākārehi vippaṭisāro upadahātabbo – ‘kālenāyasmā cudito no akālena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; bhūtenāyasmā cudito no abhūtena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; saṇhenāyasmā cudito no pharusena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; atthasaṃhitenāyasmā cudito no anatthasaṃhitena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāya; mettacittenāyasmā cudito no dosantarena, alaṃ te vippaṭisārāyā’ti. Dhammacuditassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno imehi pañcahākārehi vippaṭisāro upadahātabbo.

"Friend, a monk who has been censured in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to feel remorse in five ways: ‘Venerable, you were censured at the right time, not at the wrong time—this is sufficient for your remorse. You were censured with what is true, not with what is untrue—this is sufficient for your remorse. You were censured gently, not harshly—this is sufficient for your remorse. You were censured with what is beneficial, not with what is unbeneficial—this is sufficient for your remorse. You were censured with a mind of loving-kindness, not with inner hatred—this is sufficient for your remorse.’ Friend, a monk who has been censured in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to feel remorse in these five ways."

‘‘Dhammacodakassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno pañcahākārehi avippaṭisāro upadahātabbo – ‘kālena te, āvuso, codito no akālena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; bhūtena te, āvuso, codito no abhūtena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; saṇhena te, āvuso, codito no pharusena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; atthasaṃhitena te, āvuso, codito no anatthasaṃhitena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāya; mettacittena te, āvuso, codito no dosantarena, alaṃ te avippaṭisārāyā’ti. Dhammacodakassa, āvuso, bhikkhuno imehi pañcahākārehi avippaṭisāro upadahātabbo. Taṃ kissa hetu? Yathā aññopi bhikkhu bhūtena coditabbaṃ maññeyyāti.

"Friend, a monk who censures in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to be without remorse in five ways: ‘Friend, you censured at the right time, not at the wrong time—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You censured with what is true, not with what is untrue—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You censured gently, not harshly—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You censured with what is beneficial, not with what is unbeneficial—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse. You censured with a mind of loving-kindness, not with inner hatred—this is sufficient for you to be without remorse.’ Friend, a monk who censures in accordance with the Dhamma should be made to be without remorse in these five ways. Why is that? So that other monks might think that one should censure truthfully."

‘‘Cuditena, āvuso, puggalena dvīsu dhammesu patiṭṭhātabbaṃ – sacce ca, akuppe ca. Maṃ cepi [pañcahi (syā. ka.)], āvuso, pare codeyyuṃ kālena vā akālena vā bhūtena vā abhūtena vā saṇhena vā pharusena vā atthasaṃhitena vā anatthasaṃhitena vā mettacittā [mettacittena (sī. pī. ka.) ma. ni. 1.227 passitabbaṃ] vā dosantarā [dosantarena (sī. pī. ka.)] vā, ahampi dvīsuyeva dhammesu patiṭṭhaheyyaṃ – sacce ca, akuppe ca. Sace jāneyyaṃ – ‘attheso mayi dhammo’ti, ‘atthī’ti naṃ vadeyyaṃ – ‘saṃvijjateso mayi dhammo’ti. Sace jāneyyaṃ – ‘nattheso mayi dhammo’ti, ‘natthī’ti naṃ vadeyyaṃ – ‘neso dhammo mayi saṃvijjatī’ti.

"Friend, a person who is being censured should be established in two qualities: truth and non-agitation. If, friend, others should censure me—whether at the right time or the wrong time, whether with what is true or untrue, whether gently or harshly, whether beneficially or unbeneficially, whether with a mind of loving-kindness or with inner hatred—even then I would be established in just these two qualities: truth and non-agitation. If I should know, ‘This quality exists in me,’ I would say, ‘It exists.’ ‘This quality is found in me.’ If I should know, ‘This quality does not exist in me,’ I would say, ‘It does not exist.’ ‘This quality is not found in me.’"

‘‘Evampi kho te [evampi kho (ka.)], sāriputta, vuccamānā atha ca panidhekacce moghapurisā na padakkhiṇaṃ gaṇhantī’’ti.

"Even when spoken to in this way, Sāriputta, yet still some foolish people here do not accept it respectfully."

‘‘Ye te, bhante, puggalā assaddhā jīvikatthā na saddhā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitā saṭhā māyāvino ketabino [keṭubhino (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] uddhatā unnaḷā capalā mukharā vikiṇṇavācā indriyesu aguttadvārā bhojane amattaññuno jāgariyaṃ ananuyuttā sāmaññe anapekkhavanto sikkhāya na tibbagāravā bāhulikā sāthalikā okkamane pubbaṅgamā paviveke nikkhittadhurā kusītā hīnavīriyā muṭṭhassatino asampajānā asamāhitā vibbhantacittā duppaññā eḷamūgā, te mayā evaṃ vuccamānā na padakkhiṇaṃ gaṇhanti.

"Venerable sir, those individuals who are faithless, who have gone forth from the household life into homelessness not out of faith but for the sake of livelihood—deceitful, fraudulent, scheming, puffed up, haughty, fickle, garrulous, rambling in speech, with unguarded sense doors, immoderate in eating, not devoted to wakefulness, indifferent to the ascetic life, with no strong regard for the training, fond of company, negligent, foremost in transgression, having cast off the burden of seclusion, lazy, lacking energy, with lost mindfulness, without clear comprehension, uncollected, with scattered minds, of poor wisdom, dull and mute—when I speak to them in this way, they do not take it to heart."

‘‘Ye pana te, bhante, kulaputtā saddhā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitā asaṭhā amāyāvino aketabino anuddhatā anunnaḷā acapalā amukharā avikiṇṇavācā indriyesu guttadvārā bhojane mattaññuno jāgariyaṃ anuyuttā sāmaññe apekkhavanto sikkhāya tibbagāravā na bāhulikā na sāthalikā okkamane nikkhittadhurā paviveke pubbaṅgamā āraddhavīriyā pahitattā upaṭṭhitassatino sampajānā samāhitā ekaggacittā paññavanto aneḷamūgā, te mayā evaṃ vuccamānā padakkhiṇaṃ gaṇhantīti.

"But, venerable sir, those clansmen who have gone forth out of faith from the household life into homelessness—not deceitful, not fraudulent, not scheming, not puffed up, not haughty, not fickle, not garrulous, not scattering their words, with sense doors guarded, moderate in eating, devoted to wakefulness, having regard for the ascetic life, deeply respectful of the training, not fond of company, not negligent, having cast off the burden of transgression, foremost in seclusion, with aroused energy, with resolute minds, with established mindfulness, with clear comprehension, collected, with one-pointed minds, wise, not dull and mute—when I speak to them in this way, they receive me favorably."

‘‘Ye te, sāriputta, puggalā assaddhā jīvikatthā na saddhā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitā saṭhā māyāvino ketabino uddhatā unnaḷā capalā mukharā vikiṇṇavācā indriyesu aguttadvārā bhojane amattaññuno jāgariyaṃ ananuyuttā sāmaññe anapekkhavanto sikkhāya na tibbagāravā bāhulikā sāthalikā okkamane pubbaṅgamā paviveke nikkhittadhurā kusītā hīnavīriyā muṭṭhassatino asampajānā asamāhitā vibbhantacittā duppaññā eḷamūgā, tiṭṭhantu te.

"Let those individuals, Sāriputta, who are faithless, who have gone forth from the household life into homelessness not out of faith but for the sake of livelihood—deceitful, fraudulent, scheming, puffed up, haughty, fickle, garrulous, rambling in speech, with unguarded sense doors, immoderate in eating, not devoted to wakefulness, indifferent to the ascetic life, with no strong regard for the training, fond of company, negligent, foremost in transgression, having cast off the burden of seclusion, lazy, lacking energy, with lost mindfulness, without clear comprehension, uncollected, with scattered minds, of poor wisdom, dull and mute—let them be."

‘‘Ye pana, te sāriputta, kulaputtā saddhā agārasmā anagāriyaṃ pabbajitā asaṭhā amāyāvino aketabino anuddhatā anunnaḷā acapalā amukharā avikiṇṇavācā indriyesu guttadvārā bhojane mattaññuno jāgariyaṃ anuyuttā sāmaññe apekkhavanto sikkhāya tibbagāravā na bāhulikā na sāthalikā okkamane nikkhittadhurā paviveke pubbaṅgamā āraddhavīriyā pahitattā upaṭṭhitassatino sampajānā samāhitā ekaggacittā paññavanto aneḷamūgā, te tvaṃ, sāriputta, vadeyyāsi. Ovada, sāriputta, sabrahmacārī; anusāsa, sāriputta, sabrahmacārī – ‘asaddhammā vuṭṭhāpetvā saddhamme patiṭṭhāpessāmi sabrahmacārī’ti. Evañhi te, sāriputta, sikkhitabba’’nti. Sattamaṃ.

“But, Sāriputta, those young men of good family who have gone forth out of faith from the household life into homelessness—not deceitful, not fraudulent, not evasive, not arrogant, not frivolous, not fickle, not garrulous, not scattered in speech, with sense doors guarded, moderate in eating, devoted to wakefulness, aspiring to the ascetic life, strongly respectful of the training, not delighting in crowds, not delighting in company, having laid aside the burden of the going forth, foremost in seclusion, with aroused energy, resolute, with mindfulness established, fully aware, collected, with unified mind, wise, not stammering—you, Sāriputta, should speak to them. Advise, Sāriputta, your spiritual companions; instruct, Sāriputta, your spiritual companions, thinking: ‘I will raise my spiritual companions up from the false Dhamma and establish them in the true Dhamma.’ Thus, Sāriputta, you should train.” Seventh.
