46. "Just as, monks, a pool of water is clear, serene, and undisturbed, and a person with good eyesight standing on the bank could see shells, gravel, pebbles, and schools of fish moving about or staying still. Why is that? Because, monks, the water is undisturbed. In the same way, monks, it is possible that a monk with an undisturbed mind will know his own good, others' good, or both, and realize a superior human state, the special knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones. Why is that? Because, monks, the mind is undisturbed." Sixth.
