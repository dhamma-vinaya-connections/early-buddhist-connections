307."For a person with right view, monks, whatever bodily action is perfectly undertaken and carried out in accordance with that view, whatever verbal action... whatever mental action is perfectly undertaken and carried out in accordance with that view, whatever intention, whatever aspiration, whatever determination, and whatever volitional formationsâ€”all these things conduce to what is desirable, agreeable, pleasing, beneficial, and happy. Why is that? Because, monks, their view is excellent. Just as, monks, a sugar cane seed, a rice seed, or a grape seed placed in moist soil takes up whatever nutrient from the soil and water, all of that conduces to sweetness, flavor, and succulence. Why is that? Because, monks, the seed is excellent. In the same way, monks, for a person with right view, whatever bodily action is perfectly undertaken and carried out in accordance with that view, whatever verbal action... whatever mental action is perfectly undertaken and carried out in accordance with that view, whatever intention, whatever aspiration, whatever determination, and whatever volitional formationsâ€”all these things conduce to what is desirable, agreeable, pleasing, beneficial, and happy. Why is that? Because, monks, their view is excellent."
