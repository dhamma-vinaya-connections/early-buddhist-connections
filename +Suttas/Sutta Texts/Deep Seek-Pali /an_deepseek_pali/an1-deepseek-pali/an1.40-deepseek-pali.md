45. "Suppose, monks, there is a pool of water that is turbid, stirred up, and muddy. A person with good eyesight standing on the bank would not see the shells, gravel and pebbles, or the schools of fish swimming about or staying still. Why is that? Because of the turbidity of the water. In the same way, monks, it is not possible that a monk with a turbid mind can know what is for his own good, or what is for the good of others, or what is for the good of both, or that he will realize a superior human state, the special knowledge and vision worthy of the noble ones. Why is that? Because of the turbidity of the mind." This is the fifth.
