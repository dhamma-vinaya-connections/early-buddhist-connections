# AN 10.108 Tikicchakasuttaṃ

## AN 10.108 The Doctor Sutta

108. ‘‘Tikicchakā, bhikkhave, virecanaṃ denti pittasamuṭṭhānānampi ābādhānaṃ paṭighātāya, semhasamuṭṭhānānampi ābādhānaṃ paṭighātāya, vātasamuṭṭhānānampi ābādhānaṃ paṭighātāya. Atthetaṃ, bhikkhave, virecanaṃ; ‘netaṃ natthī’ti vadāmi. Tañca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, virecanaṃ sampajjatipi vipajjatipi.

108.Bhikkhus, physicians prescribe purgatives to counteract illnesses arising from bile, phlegm, and wind. This purgative does exist, bhikkhus; I do not say it does not. But this purgative, bhikkhus, sometimes succeeds and sometimes fails.

‘‘Ahañca kho, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ virecanaṃ desessāmi, yaṃ virecanaṃ sampajjatiyeva no vipajjati, yaṃ virecanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti, jarādhammā sattā jarāya parimuccanti, maraṇadhammā sattā maraṇena parimuccanti, sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccanti. Taṃ suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

But I, bhikkhus, shall teach the noble purgative, which succeeds and does not fail, by relying on which beings subject to birth are freed from birth, beings subject to aging are freed from aging, beings subject to death are freed from death, and beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair are freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair. Listen to it, attend closely, and I shall speak.” “Yes, venerable sir,” those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One then said this:

‘‘Katamañca taṃ, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ virecanaṃ, yaṃ virecanaṃ sampajjatiyeva no vipajjati, yaṃ virecanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti, jarādhammā sattā jarāya parimuccanti, maraṇadhammā sattā maraṇena parimuccanti, sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccanti?

And what, bhikkhus, is that noble purgative which succeeds and does not fail, by relying on which beings subject to birth are freed from birth, beings subject to aging are freed from aging, beings subject to death are freed from death, and beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair are freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair?

‘‘Sammādiṭṭhikassa, bhikkhave, micchādiṭṭhi virittā hoti; ye ca micchādiṭṭhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa virittā honti; sammādiṭṭhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

Bhikkhus, for one with right view, wrong view is purged; and the many evil unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong view are also purged; and conditioned by right view, many wholesome states go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāsaṅkappassa, bhikkhave, micchāsaṅkappo viritto hoti…pe… sammāvācassa, bhikkhave, micchāvācā virittā hoti… sammākammantassa, bhikkhave, micchākammanto viritto hoti… sammāājīvassa, bhikkhave, micchāājīvo viritto hoti… sammāvāyāmassa, bhikkhave, micchāvāyāmo viritto hoti… sammāsatissa, bhikkhave, micchāsati virittā hoti… sammāsamādhissa, bhikkhave, micchāsamādhi viritto hoti… sammāñāṇissa, bhikkhave, micchāñāṇaṃ virittaṃ hoti…pe….

Bhikkhus, for one with right intention, wrong intention is purged... for one with right speech, wrong speech is purged... for one with right action, wrong action is purged... for one with right livelihood, wrong livelihood is purged... for one with right effort, wrong effort is purged... for one with right mindfulness, wrong mindfulness is purged... for one with right samādhi, wrong samādhi is purged... for one with right knowledge, wrong knowledge is purged...

‘‘Sammāvimuttissa, bhikkhave, micchāvimutti virittā hoti; ye ca micchāvimuttipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa virittā honti; sammāvimuttipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti. Idaṃ kho taṃ, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ virecanaṃ yaṃ virecanaṃ sampajjatiyeva no vipajjati, yaṃ virecanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti…pe… sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccantī’’ti. Aṭṭhamaṃ.

Bhikkhus, for one with right liberation, wrong liberation is purged; and the many evil unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong liberation are also purged; and conditioned by right liberation, many wholesome states go to the fulfillment of development. This, bhikkhus, is that noble purgative which succeeds and does not fail, by relying on which beings subject to birth are freed from birth... from sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair.” Eighth.
