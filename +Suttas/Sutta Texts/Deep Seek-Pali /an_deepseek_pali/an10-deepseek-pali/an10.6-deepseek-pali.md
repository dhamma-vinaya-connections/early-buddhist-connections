# AN 10.6 Samādhisuttaṃ

## AN 10.6 The samādhi Discourse

6. [a. ni. 11.18] Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami…pe… ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā ānando bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘siyā nu kho, bhante, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī [paṭhavisaññī (sī.), paṭhavīsaññī (syā.)] assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti? ‘‘Siyā, ānanda, bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti.

6. Then the Venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One… and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, the Venerable Ānanda said to the Blessed One: "Is it possible, venerable sir, for a bhikkhu to attain such a state of samādhi that he would not perceive earth in earth, water in water, fire in fire, air in air; would not perceive the base of the infinity of space in the base of the infinity of space, the base of the infinity of consciousness in the base of the infinity of consciousness, the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; would not perceive this world in this world, nor the other world in the other world—and yet he would still be perceiving?" "It is possible, Ānanda, for a bhikkhu to attain such a state of samādhi that he would not perceive earth in earth, water in water, fire in fire, air in air; would not perceive the base of the infinity of space in the base of the infinity of space, the base of the infinity of consciousness in the base of the infinity of consciousness, the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; would not perceive this world in this world, nor the other world in the other world—and yet he would still be perceiving."

‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, bhante, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyattane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti?

"But how, venerable sir, might a bhikkhu attain such a state of samādhi that he would not perceive earth in earth, water in water, fire in fire, air in air; would not perceive the base of the infinity of space in the base of the infinity of space, the base of the infinity of consciousness in the base of the infinity of consciousness, the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; would not perceive this world in this world, nor the other world in the other world—and yet he would still be perceiving?"

‘‘Idhānanda, bhikkhu evaṃsaññī hoti – ‘etaṃ santaṃ etaṃ paṇītaṃ yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbāna’nti. Evaṃ kho, ānanda, siyā bhikkhuno tathārūpo samādhipaṭilābho yathā neva pathaviyaṃ pathavisaññī assa, na āpasmiṃ āposaññī assa, na tejasmiṃ tejosaññī assa, na vāyasmiṃ vāyosaññī assa, na ākāsānañcāyatane ākāsānañcāyatanasaññī assa, na viññāṇañcāyatane viññāṇañcāyatanasaññī assa, na ākiñcaññāyatane ākiñcaññāyatanasaññī assa, na nevasaññānāsaññāyatane nevasaññānāsaññāyatanasaññī assa, na idhaloke idhalokasaññī assa, na paraloke paralokasaññī assa; saññī ca pana assā’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

Here, Ananda, a bhikkhu has such a perception: 'This is peaceful, this is sublime, that is, the stilling of all formations, the relinquishment of all acquisitions, the destruction of craving, dispassion, cessation, Nibbāna.' Thus, Ananda, a bhikkhu may attain such a state of samādhi that he would not perceive earth in earth, nor water in water, nor fire in fire, nor air in air; would not perceive the base of the infinity of space in the base of the infinity of space, nor the base of the infinity of consciousness in the base of the infinity of consciousness, nor the base of nothingness in the base of nothingness, nor the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception; would not perceive this world in this world, nor the other world in the other world—and yet he would still be perceiving. The sixth.
