# AN 10.109 Vamanasuttaṃ

## AN 10.109 The Emetic Discourse

109. ‘‘Tikicchakā, bhikkhave, vamanaṃ denti pittasamuṭṭhānānampi ābādhānaṃ paṭighātāya, semhasamuṭṭhānānampi ābādhānaṃ paṭighātāya, vātasamuṭṭhānānampi ābādhānaṃ paṭighātāya. Atthetaṃ, bhikkhave, vamanaṃ; ‘netaṃ natthī’ti vadāmi. Tañca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, vamanaṃ sampajjatipi vipajjatipi.

109.Bhikkhus, physicians administer emetics to counteract illnesses arising from bile, phlegm, and wind. This emetic does exist, bhikkhus; I do not say it does not. But this emetic, bhikkhus, sometimes succeeds and sometimes fails.

‘‘Ahañca kho, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ vamanaṃ desessāmi, yaṃ vamanaṃ sampajjatiyeva no vipajjati, yaṃ vamanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti, jarādhammā sattā jarāya parimuccanti, maraṇadhammā sattā maraṇena parimuccanti, sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccanti. Taṃ suṇātha…pe….

But I, bhikkhus, shall teach the noble emetic, which succeeds and does not fail, by relying on which beings subject to birth are freed from birth, beings subject to aging are freed from aging, beings subject to death are freed from death, and beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair are freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair. Listen to it...

‘‘Katamañca taṃ, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ vamanaṃ, yaṃ vamanaṃ sampajjatiyeva no vipajjati, yaṃ vamanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti…pe… sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccanti?

And what, bhikkhus, is that noble emetic which succeeds and does not fail, by relying on which beings subject to birth are freed from birth... beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair are freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair?

‘‘Sammādiṭṭhikassa, bhikkhave, micchādiṭṭhi vantā hoti; ye ca micchādiṭṭhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa vantā honti; sammādiṭṭhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

Bhikkhus, for one with right view, wrong view is expelled; and the many evil unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong view are also expelled; and conditioned by right view, many wholesome states go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāsaṅkappassa, bhikkhave, micchāsaṅkappo vanto hoti…pe… sammāvācassa, bhikkhave, micchāvācā vantā hoti… sammākammantassa, bhikkhave, micchākammanto vanto hoti… sammāājīvassa bhikkhave, micchāājīvo vanto hoti… sammāvāyāmassa, bhikkhave, micchāvāyāmo vanto hoti… sammāsatissa, bhikkhave, micchāsati vantā hoti… sammāsamādhissa, bhikkhave, micchāsamādhi vanto hoti… sammāñāṇissa, bhikkhave, micchāñāṇaṃ vantaṃ hoti …pe….

Bhikkhus, for one with right intention, wrong intention is expelled... for one with right speech, wrong speech is expelled... for one with right action, wrong action is expelled... for one with right livelihood, wrong livelihood is expelled... for one with right effort, wrong effort is expelled... for one with right mindfulness, wrong mindfulness is expelled... for one with right samādhi, wrong samādhi is expelled... for one with right knowledge, wrong knowledge is expelled...

‘‘Sammāvimuttissa, bhikkhave, micchāvimutti vantā hoti; ye ca micchāvimuttipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa vantā honti; sammāvimuttipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti. Idaṃ kho taṃ, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ vamanaṃ yaṃ vamanaṃ sampajjatiyeva no vipajjati, yaṃ vamanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti…pe… sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccantī’’ti. Navamaṃ.

Bhikkhus, for one with right liberation, wrong liberation is expelled; and the many evil unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong liberation are also expelled; and conditioned by right liberation, many wholesome states go to the fulfillment of development. This, bhikkhus, is that noble emetic which succeeds and does not fail, by relying on which beings subject to birth are freed from birth... from sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair.” Ninth.
