# AN 10.107 Dhovanasuttaṃ

## AN 10.107 The Washing Discourse

107. ‘‘Atthi, bhikkhave, dakkhiṇesu janapadesu dhovanaṃ nāma. Tattha hoti annampi pānampi khajjampi bhojjampi leyyampi peyyampi naccampi gītampi vāditampi. Atthetaṃ, bhikkhave, dhovanaṃ; ‘netaṃ natthī’ti vadāmi. Tañca kho etaṃ, bhikkhave, dhovanaṃ hīnaṃ gammaṃ pothujjanikaṃ anariyaṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ na nibbidāya na virāgāya na nirodhāya na upasamāya na abhiññāya na sambodhāya na nibbānāya saṃvattati.

107.“Bhikkhus, in the southern lands there is what is called a ‘Dhovana.’ There, food and drink, snacks and meals, delicacies and beverages, as well as dancing, singing, and music are found. That, bhikkhus, is the Dhovana; I say it exists. But this Dhovana, bhikkhus, is low, vulgar, worldly, ignoble, unbeneficial, not leading to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, or to Nibbāna.”

‘‘Ahañca kho, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ dhovanaṃ desessāmi, yaṃ dhovanaṃ ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati, yaṃ dhovanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti, jarādhammā sattā jarāya parimuccanti, maraṇadhammā sattā maraṇena parimuccanti, sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccanti. Taṃ suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bhante’’ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca –

“Bhikkhus, I will teach you the noble cleansing, a cleansing that leads solely to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, and to Nibbāna. By means of which cleansing, beings subject to birth are freed from birth, beings subject to aging are freed from aging, beings subject to death are freed from death, and beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair are freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair. Listen carefully and pay close attention; I will speak.” “Yes, Venerable Sir,” those bhikkhus replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Katamañca taṃ, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ dhovanaṃ, (yaṃ dhovanaṃ) [( ) natthi syāmapotthake] ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati, yaṃ dhovanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti, jarādhammā sattā jarāya parimuccanti, maraṇadhammā sattā maraṇena parimuccanti, sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccanti?

“And what, bhikkhus, is that noble cleansing, which leads solely to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, and to Nibbāna? By means of which cleansing, beings subject to birth are freed from birth, beings subject to aging are freed from aging, beings subject to death are freed from death, and beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair are freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair?

‘‘Sammādiṭṭhikassa, bhikkhave, micchādiṭṭhi niddhotā hoti; ye ca micchādiṭṭhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa niddhotā honti; sammādiṭṭhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right view, wrong view is eliminated; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong view are also eliminated in them; and conditioned by right view, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāsaṅkappassa, bhikkhave, micchāsaṅkappo niddhoto hoti…pe… sammāvācassa, bhikkhave, micchāvācā niddhotā hoti… sammākammantassa, bhikkhave, micchākammanto niddhoto hoti… sammāājīvassa, bhikkhave, micchāājīvo niddhoto hoti… sammāvāyāmassa, bhikkhave, micchāvāyāmo niddhoto hoti… sammāsatissa, bhikkhave, micchāsati niddhotā hoti… sammāsamādhissa, bhikkhave, micchāsamādhi niddhoto hoti… sammāñāṇissa, bhikkhave, micchāñāṇaṃ niddhotaṃ hoti…pe….

“Bhikkhus, for one with right intention, wrong intention is eliminated… for one with right speech, wrong speech is eliminated… for one with right action, wrong action is eliminated… for one with right livelihood, wrong livelihood is eliminated… for one with right effort, wrong effort is eliminated… for one with right mindfulness, wrong mindfulness is eliminated… for one with right samādhi, wrong samādhi is eliminated… for one with right knowledge, wrong knowledge is eliminated….

‘‘Sammāvimuttissa, bhikkhave, micchāvimutti niddhotā hoti; ye ca micchāvimuttipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa niddhotā honti; sammāvimuttipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti. Idaṃ kho taṃ, bhikkhave, ariyaṃ dhovanaṃ ekantanibbidāya virāgāya nirodhāya upasamāya abhiññāya sambodhāya nibbānāya saṃvattati, yaṃ dhovanaṃ āgamma jātidhammā sattā jātiyā parimuccanti, jarādhammā sattā jarāya parimuccanti, maraṇadhammā sattā maraṇena parimuccanti, sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsadhammā sattā sokaparidevadukkhadomanassupāyāsehi parimuccantī’’ti. Sattamaṃ.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right liberation, wrong liberation is eliminated; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong liberation are also eliminated; and conditioned by right liberation, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development. This, bhikkhus, is that noble cleansing which leads solely to disenchantment, to dispassion, to cessation, to peace, to direct knowledge, to enlightenment, and to Nibbāna; by means of which cleansing, beings subject to birth are freed from birth, beings subject to aging are freed from aging, beings subject to death are freed from death, and beings subject to sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair are freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief, and despair.” The seventh.
