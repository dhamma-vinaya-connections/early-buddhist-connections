# AN 10.112 Dutiyaasekhasuttaṃ

## AN 10.112 The Second Discourse on an Adept

112. ‘‘Dasayime, bhikkhave, asekhiyā dhammā. Katame dasa? Asekhā sammādiṭṭhi, asekho sammāsaṅkappo, asekhā sammāvācā, asekho sammākammanto, asekho sammāājīvo, asekho sammāvāyāmo, asekhā sammāsati, asekho sammāsamādhi, asekhaṃ sammāñāṇaṃ, asekhā sammāvimutti – ime kho, bhikkhave, dasa asekhiyā dhammā’’ti. Dvādasamaṃ.

112.“Monks, there are these ten qualities of an adept. Which ten? Right view of an adept, right intention of an adept, right speech of an adept, right action of an adept, right livelihood of an adept, right effort of an adept, right mindfulness of an adept, right samādhi of an adept, right knowledge of an adept, right liberation of an adept. These, monks, are the ten qualities of an adept.” The twelfth.

Samaṇasaññāvaggo paṭhamo.

The Chapter on the Perceptions of Ascetics is the first.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The chapter summary is:

Saññā bojjhaṅgā micchattaṃ, bījaṃ vijjāya nijjaraṃ;Dhovanaṃ tikicchā vamanaṃ niddhamanaṃ dve asekhāti.

Perception, enlightenment factors, wrongness; seed, knowledge, wearing away; Washing, treatment, vomiting, expulsion, and two adepts.
