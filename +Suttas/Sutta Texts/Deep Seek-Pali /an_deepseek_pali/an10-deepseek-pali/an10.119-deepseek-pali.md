# AN 10.119 Paṭhamapaccorohaṇīsuttaṃ

## AN 10.119 The First Discourse on Relinquishment

119. Tena kho pana samayena jāṇussoṇi [jānussoni (ka. sī.), jānussoṇi (ka. sī.), jāṇusoṇi (ka.)] brāhmaṇo tadahuposathe sīsaṃnhāto [sīsaṃnahāto (sī. pī.), sīsanhāto (syā.)] navaṃ khomayugaṃ nivattho allakusamuṭṭhiṃ ādāya bhagavato avidūre ekamantaṃ ṭhito hoti.

119.At that time, the brahmin Jāṇussoṇi, having bathed his head on the Uposatha day, clad in a new pair of linen garments, holding a handful of wet kusa grass, stood not far from the Blessed One to one side.

Addasā kho bhagavā jāṇussoṇiṃ brāhmaṇaṃ tadahuposathe sīsaṃnhātaṃ navaṃ khomayugaṃ nivatthaṃ allakusamuṭṭhiṃ ādāya ekamantaṃ ṭhitaṃ. Disvāna jāṇussoṇiṃ brāhmaṇaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃ nu tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, tadahuposathe sīsaṃnhāto navaṃ khomayugaṃ nivattho allakusamuṭṭhiṃ ādāya ekamantaṃ ṭhito? Kiṃ nvajja [kiṃ nu ajja (syā.), kiṃ nu kho ajja (pī.), kiṃ nu khvajja (ka.)] brāhmaṇakulassā’’ti [brāhmaṇa brahmakusalassāti (ka.)]? ‘‘Paccorohaṇī, bho gotama, ajja brāhmaṇakulassā’’ti [brahmakusalassāti (ka.)].

The Blessed One saw the brahmin Jāṇussoṇi standing to one side, having bathed his head on the Uposatha day, clad in new linen garments, holding a handful of wet kusa grass. Seeing the brahmin Jāṇussoṇi, he said: "Why, brahmin, are you standing to one side, having bathed your head on the Uposatha day, clad in new linen garments, holding a handful of wet kusa grass? What is it today for the brahmin's household?" "Today, Master Gotama, is the Paccorohaṇī for the brahmin's household."

‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, brāhmaṇa, brāhmaṇānaṃ paccorohaṇī hotī’’ti? ‘‘Idha, bho gotama, brāhmaṇā tadahuposathe sīsaṃnhātā navaṃ khomayugaṃ nivatthā allena gomayena pathaviṃ opuñjitvā haritehi kusehi pattharitvā [pavitthāretvā (ka.)] antarā ca velaṃ antarā ca agyāgāraṃ seyyaṃ kappenti. Te taṃ rattiṃ tikkhattuṃ paccuṭṭhāya pañjalikā aggiṃ namassanti – ‘paccorohāma bhavantaṃ, paccorohāma bhavanta’nti. Bahukena ca sappitelanavanītena aggiṃ santappenti. Tassā ca rattiyā accayena paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena brāhmaṇe santappenti. Evaṃ, bho gotama, brāhmaṇānaṃ paccorohaṇī hotī’’ti.

"And how, brahmin, is the Paccorohaṇī performed by brahmins?" "Here, Master Gotama, on the Uposatha day, brahmins bathe their heads, put on a new pair of linen garments, smear the ground with wet cow dung, spread it with green kusa grass, and prepare a bed between the boundary and the fire sanctuary. That night, they rise three times with hands reverently joined, worship the fire, saying: 'We descend to you, we descend to you.' And they satisfy the fire with abundant ghee, oil, and fresh butter. At the end of that night, they satisfy brahmins with delicious hard and soft food. Thus, Master Gotama, is the Paccorohaṇī performed by brahmins."

‘‘Aññathā kho, brāhmaṇa, brāhmaṇānaṃ paccorohaṇī hoti, aññathā ca pana ariyassa vinaye paccorohaṇī hotī’’ti. ‘‘Yathā kathaṃ pana, bho gotama, ariyassa vinaye paccorohaṇī hoti? Sādhu me bhavaṃ gotamo tathā dhammaṃ desetu yathā ariyassa vinaye paccorohaṇī hotī’’ti.

"Brahmin, the brahmins' descent is one thing, but the relinquishment in the Noble One's discipline is another." "But how, Master Gotama, is relinquishment in the Noble One's discipline? It would be good if Master Gotama would teach me the Dhamma so that I may understand how relinquishment is in the Noble One's discipline."

‘‘Tena hi, brāhmaṇa, suṇāhi, sādhukaṃ manasi karohi; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evaṃ, bho’’ti kho jāṇussoṇi brāhmaṇo bhagavato paccassosi. Bhagavā etadavoca –

"Well then, brahmin, listen and pay close attention; I will speak." "Yes, Master," the brahmin Jāṇussoṇi replied to the Blessed One. The Blessed One said this:

‘‘Idha, brāhmaṇa, ariyasāvako iti paṭisañcikkhati – ‘micchādiṭṭhiyā kho pāpako vipāko diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcā’ ti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchādiṭṭhiṃ pajahati; micchādiṭṭhiyā paccorohati.

"Here, brahmin, a noble disciple reflects thus: 'Wrong view has a bad result in this very life and in the life to come.' Reflecting in this way, he abandons wrong view; he relinquishes wrong view.

… Micchāsaṅkappassa kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāsaṅkappaṃ pajahati; micchāsaṅkappā paccorohati.

Wrong intention has a bad result—both in this very life and in the life to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong intention; one relinquishes wrong intention.

… Micchāvācāya kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāvācaṃ pajahati; micchāvācāya paccorohati.

Wrong speech has a bad result—both in this very life and in the life to come. Reflecting wisely, one abandons wrong speech; one relinquishes wrong speech.

…Micchākammantassa kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchākammantaṃ pajahati; micchākammantā paccorohati.

Wrong action has a bad result—both in this very life and in the life to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong action; one relinquishes wrong action.

…Micchāājīvassa kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāājīvaṃ pajahati; micchāājīvā paccorohati.

Wrong livelihood has a bad result—both in this present life and in the life to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong livelihood; one relinquishes wrong livelihood.

…Micchāvāyāmassa kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāvāyāmaṃ pajahati; micchāvāyāmā paccorohati.

Wrong effort has a bad result—both in this very life and in the life to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong effort; one relinquishes wrong effort.

…Micchāsatiyā kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāsatiṃ pajahati; micchāsatiyā paccorohati.

Wrong mindfulness has a bad result—both in this very life and in the life to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong mindfulness; one relinquishes wrong mindfulness.

…Micchāsamādhissa kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāsamādhiṃ pajahati; micchāsamādhimhā paccorohati.

Wrong samādhi has a bad result—both in this very life and in the life to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong samādhi; one relinquishes wrong samādhi.

…Micchāñāṇassa kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcāti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāñāṇaṃ pajahati; micchāñāṇamhā paccorohati.

Wrong knowledge has a bad result—both in this very life and in the life to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong knowledge; one relinquishes wrong knowledge.

‘Micchāvimuttiyā kho pāpako vipāko – diṭṭhe ceva dhamme abhisamparāyañcā’ti. So iti paṭisaṅkhāya micchāvimuttiṃ pajahati; micchāvimuttiyā paccorohati. Evaṃ kho, brāhmaṇa, ariyassa vinaye paccorohaṇī hotī’’ti.

Wrong liberation has a bad result—both in this present life and in lives to come. Reflecting thus, one abandons wrong liberation; one relinquishes wrong liberation. This, brahmin, is how there is relinquishment in the Noble One's discipline."

‘‘Aññathā, bho gotama, brāhmaṇānaṃ paccorohaṇī, aññathā ca pana ariyassa vinaye paccorohaṇī hoti. Imissā ca, bho gotama, ariyassa vinaye paccorohaṇiyā brāhmaṇānaṃ paccorohaṇī kalaṃ nāgghati soḷasiṃ. Abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama…pe… upāsakaṃ maṃ bhavaṃ gotamo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṃ saraṇaṃ gata’’nti. Sattamaṃ.

Master Gotama, the brahmins' descent is one thing, but the relinquishment in the Noble One's discipline is another. Compared to this relinquishment in the Noble One's discipline, the brahmins' descent is not worth a sixteenth part. Excellent, Master Gotama... from today onward, may Master Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge for life. The seventh.
