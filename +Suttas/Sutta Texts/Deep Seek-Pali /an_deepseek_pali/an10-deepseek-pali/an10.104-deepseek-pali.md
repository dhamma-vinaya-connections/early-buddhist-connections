# AN 10.104 Bījasuttaṃ

## AN 10.104 The Seed Discourse

104. [a. ni. 1.306; kathā. 708] ‘‘Micchādiṭṭhikassa, bhikkhave, purisapuggalassa micchāsaṅkappassa micchāvācassa micchākammantassa micchāājīvassa micchāvāyāmassa micchāsatissa micchāsamādhissa micchāñāṇissa micchāvimuttissa yañca kāyakammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ [samādiṇṇaṃ (pī. ka.)] yañca vacīkammaṃ… yañca manokammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yā ca cetanā yā ca patthanā yo ca paṇidhi ye ca saṅkhārā, sabbe te dhammā aniṭṭhāya akantāya amanāpāya ahitāya dukkhāya saṃvattanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Diṭṭhi hissa [diṭṭhi hi (sī. syā. pī.)], bhikkhave, pāpikā.

104.“For a person with wrong view, monks—wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, wrong samādhi, wrong knowledge, wrong liberation—whatever bodily action they undertake and fulfill in accordance with their view, whatever verbal action… whatever mental action they undertake and fulfill in accordance with their view, and whatever intention, aspiration, resolve, and volitions they make—all these things conduce to what is unwanted, unlovely, disagreeable, harmful, and painful. Why is that? Because, monks, their view is bad.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, nimbabījaṃ vā kosātakibījaṃ vā tittakālābubījaṃ vā allāya pathaviyā nikkhittaṃ yañceva pathavirasaṃ upādiyati yañca āporasaṃ upādiyati, sabbaṃ taṃ tittakattāya kaṭukattāya asātattāya saṃvattati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Bījañhi, bhikkhave, pāpakaṃ. Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, micchādiṭṭhikassa purisapuggalassa micchāsaṅkappassa micchāvācassa micchākammantassa micchāājīvassa micchāvāyāmassa micchāsatissa micchāsamādhissa micchāñāṇissa micchāvimuttissa yañceva kāyakammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yañca vacīkammaṃ… yañca manokammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yā ca cetanā yā ca patthanā yo ca paṇidhi ye ca saṅkhārā, sabbe te dhammā aniṭṭhāya akantāya amanāpāya ahitāya dukkhāya saṃvattanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Diṭṭhi hissa, bhikkhave, pāpikā.

“Just as, monks, a neem seed, a bitter gourd seed, or a colocynth seed, planted in moist soil, takes up both the earth’s sap and the water’s sap, all of it conduces to bitterness, acridity, and unsavoriness. Why is that? Because the seed is bad. In the same way, monks, for a person with wrong view, wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness, wrong samādhi, wrong knowledge, and wrong liberation, whatever bodily action they undertake and fulfill in accordance with their view; whatever verbal action… whatever mental action they undertake and fulfill in accordance with their view; whatever intention, whatever aspiration, whatever resolve, and whatever volitions—all these things conduce to what is unwanted, unlovely, disagreeable, harmful, and painful. Why is that? Because their view is bad.”

‘‘Sammādiṭṭhikassa, bhikkhave, purisapuggalassa sammāsaṅkappassa sammāvācassa sammākammantassa sammāājīvassa sammāvāyāmassa sammāsatissa sammāsamādhissa sammāñāṇissa sammāvimuttissa yañceva kāyakammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yañca vacīkammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yañca manokammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yā ca cetanā yā ca patthanā yo ca paṇidhi ye ca saṅkhārā, sabbe te dhammā iṭṭhāya kantāya manāpāya hitāya sukhāya saṃvattanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Diṭṭhi hissa, bhikkhave, bhaddikā.

“For a person with right view, monks—right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samādhi, right knowledge, and right liberation—whatever bodily action they undertake and fulfill in accordance with their view, whatever verbal action they undertake and fulfill in accordance with their view, whatever mental action they undertake and fulfill in accordance with their view, whatever intention, aspiration, resolve, and volitions they make—all those things conduce to what is wished for, desired, agreeable, beneficial, and pleasant. Why is that? Because, monks, their view is good.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, ucchubījaṃ vā sālibījaṃ vā muddikābījaṃ vā allāya pathaviyā nikkhittaṃ yañca pathavirasaṃ upādiyati yañca āporasaṃ upādiyati sabbaṃ taṃ sātattāya madhurattāya asecanakattāya saṃvattati. Taṃ kissa hetu? Bījañhi bhikkhave, bhaddakaṃ. Evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, sammādiṭṭhikassa…pe. … sammāvimuttissa yañceva kāyakammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yañca vacīkammaṃ… yañca manokammaṃ yathādiṭṭhi samattaṃ samādinnaṃ yā ca cetanā yā ca patthanā yo ca paṇidhi ye ca saṅkhārā, sabbe te dhammā iṭṭhāya kantāya manāpāya hitāya sukhāya saṃvattanti. Taṃ kissa hetu? Diṭṭhi hissa, bhikkhave, bhaddikā’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

“Just as, monks, a sugarcane seed, a rice seed, or a grape seed planted in moist soil, taking up the essence of the soil and the essence of water, all of it conduces to pleasantness, sweetness, and succulence. Why is that? Because the seed is good. In the same way, monks, for one of right view… right liberation, whatever bodily action is undertaken and fulfilled in accordance with their view, whatever verbal action… whatever mental action is undertaken and fulfilled in accordance with their view, whatever intention, whatever aspiration, whatever determination, and whatever volitions they have—all these things conduce to what is liked, desired, agreeable, beneficial, and pleasant. Why is that? Because, monks, their view is good.” The fourth.
