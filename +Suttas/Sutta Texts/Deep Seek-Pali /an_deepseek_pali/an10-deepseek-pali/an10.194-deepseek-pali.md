Just as, monks, a flawless gem thrown upwards lands firmly wherever it falls; in the same way, monks, on account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of threefold bodily action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. On account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of fourfold verbal action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. On account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of threefold mental action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. I do not say, monks, that intentional actions done and accumulated will come to an end without being experienced. That may be in this very life, or in some other way, in some other course. But I do not say, monks, that there is an end of suffering without experiencing intentional actions done and accumulated. The seventh.
