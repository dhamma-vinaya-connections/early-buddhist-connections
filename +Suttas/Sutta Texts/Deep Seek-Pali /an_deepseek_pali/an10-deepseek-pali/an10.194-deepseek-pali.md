# AN 10.194 Paṭhamasañcetanikasuttaṃ

## AN 10.194 The First Discourse on Intention

217. ‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sañcetanikānaṃ kammānaṃ katānaṃ upacitānaṃ appaṭisaṃveditvā [appaṭisaṃviditvā (sī. syā. pī.)] byantībhāvaṃ vadāmi. Tañca kho diṭṭheva dhamme upapajje vā [upapajjaṃ vā (ka.) a. ni. 6.63 passitabbaṃ, upapajja vā (ma. ni. 3.303)] apare vā pariyāye. Na tvevāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sañcetanikānaṃ kammānaṃ katānaṃ upacitānaṃ appaṭisaṃveditvā dukkhassantakiriyaṃ vadāmi.

217.Monks, I do not declare the termination of intentional actions that have been performed and accumulated without experiencing their result. That may be in this very life or in some other course. And, monks, I do not declare the making an end of suffering without experiencing the result of intentional actions that have been performed and accumulated.

‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, tividhā kāyakammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā [akusalaṃ sañcetanikaṃ dukkhudrayaṃ dukkhavipākaṃ (ka.)] hoti; catubbidhā vacīkammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti; tividhā manokammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti.

Monks, here, threefold bodily misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, is productive of suffering and ripens in suffering; fourfold verbal misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, is productive of suffering and ripens in suffering; threefold mental misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, is productive of suffering and ripens in suffering.

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, tividhā kāyakammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco pāṇātipātī hoti luddo lohitapāṇi hatapahate niviṭṭho adayāpanno sabbapāṇabhūtesu.

And how, monks, is threefold bodily misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, productive of suffering and ripening in suffering? Here, monks, someone is a killer of living beings—violent, bloody-handed, given to striking and slaying, merciless to all living beings.

‘‘Adinnādāyī hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ gāmagataṃ vā araññagataṃ vā, taṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti.

He takes what is not given. Whatever belongs to others—their property or belongings, whether in a village or in the wilderness—he takes what is not given, regarded as theft.

‘‘Kāmesumicchācārī hoti. Yā tā māturakkhitā…pe… antamaso mālāguḷaparikkhittāpi, tathārūpāsu cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tividhā kāyakammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti.

He engages in sexual misconduct. He has intercourse with those protected by their mother, father, brothers, sisters, relatives, or those under guardianship, even those betrothed with a garland—he commits such misconduct. Thus, monks, threefold bodily misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, is productive of suffering and ripens in suffering.

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, catubbidhā vacīkammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco musāvādī hoti. Sabhaggato vā parisaggato vā ñātimajjhagato vā pūgamajjhagato vā rājakulamajjhagato vā abhinīto sakkhipuṭṭho ‘ehambho purisa, yaṃ jānāsi taṃ vadehī’ti, so ajānaṃ vā āha ‘jānāmī’ti, jānaṃ vā āha ‘na jānāmī’ti, apassaṃ vā āha ‘passāmī’ti, passaṃ vā āha ‘na passāmī’ti, iti attahetu vā parahetu vā āmisakiñcikkhahetu vā sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti.

And how, monks, is fourfold verbal misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, productive of suffering and ripening in suffering? Here, monks, someone is a liar. When summoned to a council, an assembly, a family gathering, a guild, or the royal court, and questioned as a witness—‘Come, good man, speak what you know’—not knowing, he says, ‘I know,’ or knowing, he says, ‘I do not know.’ Not seeing, he says, ‘I see,’ or seeing, he says, ‘I do not see.’ Thus, he deliberately speaks a lie for his own sake, for another’s sake, or for some trifling worldly gain.

‘‘Pisuṇavāco hoti. Ito sutvā amutra akkhātā imesaṃ bhedāya, amutra vā sutvā imesaṃ akkhātā amūsaṃ bhedāya. Iti samaggānaṃ vā bhettā bhinnānaṃ vā anuppadātā vaggārāmo vaggarato vagganandī, vaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

He is a divisive speaker. Having heard something here, he reports it there to divide those people, or having heard something there, he reports it here to divide these people. Thus he is one who divides those who are united, or encourages those who are divided; he delights in discord, enjoys discord, and speaks words that cause discord.

‘‘Pharusavāco hoti. Yā sā vācā aṇḍakā kakkasā parakaṭukā parābhisajjanī kodhasāmantā. Asamādhisaṃvattanikā, tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

He is harsh in speech. He utters speech that is rough, coarse, bitter to others, abusive, bordering on anger, and not conducive to samādhi.

‘‘Samphappalāpī hoti akālavādī abhūtavādī anatthavādī adhammavādī avinayavādī, anidhānavatiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti akālena anapadesaṃ apariyantavatiṃ anatthasaṃhitaṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, catubbidhā vacīkammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti.

He is an idle chatterer. He speaks out of season, speaks untruth, speaks what is useless, speaks against the Dhamma, speaks against the Discipline. He utters speech that is worthless, untimely, without reason, rambling, and unconnected with the good. Thus, monks, fourfold verbal misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, is productive of suffering and ripens in suffering.

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, tividhā manokammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco abhijjhālu hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ, taṃ abhijjhātā hoti – ‘aho vata, yaṃ parassa taṃ mama assā’ti.

And how, monks, is threefold mental misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, productive of suffering and ripening in suffering? Here, monks, someone is covetous. He covets the wealth and belongings of others, thinking, ‘Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!’

‘‘Byāpannacitto hoti paduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ‘ime sattā haññantu vā bajjhantu vā ucchijjantu vā vinassantu vā mā vā ahesu’nti.

He has a mind of ill will, corrupted in his mental resolve, thinking: ‘May these beings be killed, bound, destroyed, or perish! May they not exist!’

Micchādiṭṭhiko hoti viparītadassano – ‘natthi dinnaṃ…pe. … ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tividhā manokammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikā dukkhudrayā dukkhavipākā hoti.

He has wrong view, distorted vision: ‘There is no gift, no offering, no sacrifice; no fruit or result of good and bad actions; there is no this world, no next world; there is no mother, no father; no beings who are reborn spontaneously; no ascetics or brahmins who, having themselves realized through direct knowledge this world and the next, make it known.’ Thus, monks, threefold mental misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, is productive of suffering and ripens in suffering.

‘‘Tividha kāyakammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikāhetu [… sañcetanikahetu (ka.)] vā, bhikkhave, sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti; catubbidhavacīkammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikāhetu vā, bhikkhave, sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti; tividhamanokammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikāhetu vā, bhikkhave, sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti.

Monks, due to threefold bodily misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, beings, upon the breakup of the body, after death, are reborn in the plane of misery, the bad destination, the lower realms, in hell; due to fourfold verbal misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, beings, upon the breakup of the body, after death, are reborn in the plane of misery, the bad destination, the lower realms, in hell; due to threefold mental misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, beings, upon the breakup of the body, after death, are reborn in the plane of misery, the bad destination, the lower realms, in hell.

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, apaṇṇako maṇi uddhaṃkhitto yena yeneva patiṭṭhāti suppatiṭṭhitaṃyeva patiṭṭhāti; evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tividhakāyakammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikāhetu vā sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti; catubbidhavacīkammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikāhetu vā sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjanti; tividhamanokammantasandosabyāpatti akusalasañcetanikāhetu vā sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapajjantīti.

Just as, monks, a flawless gem, when thrown upwards, lands firmly wherever it falls, even so, monks, due to threefold bodily misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, beings, upon the breakup of the body, after death, are reborn in the plane of misery, the bad destination, the lower realms, in hell; due to fourfold verbal misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, beings, upon the breakup of the body, after death, are reborn in the plane of misery, the bad destination, the lower realms, in hell; due to threefold mental misconduct, unwholesome and intentional, beings, upon the breakup of the body, after death, are reborn in the plane of misery, the bad destination, the lower realms, in hell.

‘‘Nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sañcetanikānaṃ kammānaṃ katānaṃ upacitānaṃ appaṭisaṃveditvā byantībhāvaṃ vadāmi, tañca kho diṭṭheva dhamme upapajje vā apare vā pariyāye. Na tvevāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sañcetanikānaṃ kammānaṃ katānaṃ upacitānaṃ appaṭisaṃveditvā dukkhassantakiriyaṃ vadāmi.

Monks, I do not declare the termination of intentional actions that have been performed and accumulated without experiencing their result. That may be in this very life or in some other course. And, monks, I do not declare the making an end of suffering without experiencing the result of intentional actions that have been performed and accumulated.

‘‘Tatra, bhikkhave, tividhā kāyakammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti; catubbidhā vacīkammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti; tividhā manokammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti.

Monks, here, threefold bodily good conduct, wholesome and intentional, is productive of happiness and ripens in happiness; fourfold verbal good conduct, wholesome and intentional, is productive of happiness and ripens in happiness; threefold mental good conduct, wholesome and intentional, is productive of happiness and ripens in happiness.

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, tividhā kāyakammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco pāṇātipātaṃ pahāya pāṇātipātā paṭivirato hoti nihitadaṇḍo nihitasattho lajjī dayāpanno, sabbapāṇabhūtahitānukampī viharati…pe….

And how, monks, is threefold bodily good conduct, wholesome and intentional, productive of happiness and ripening in happiness? Here, monks, someone, having abandoned killing living beings, abstains from killing living beings, laying down the rod and the sword, conscientious, compassionate, dwelling sympathetic for the welfare of all living beings.

‘‘Adinnādānaṃ pahāya, adinnādānā paṭivirato hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ gāmagataṃ vā araññagataṃ vā, na taṃ adinnaṃ theyyasaṅkhātaṃ ādātā hoti.

Having abandoned taking what is not given, he abstains from taking what is not given. Whatever belongs to others as property or belongings, whether in a village or in the wilderness, he does not take such things not given, regarded as theft.

‘‘Kāmesumicchācāraṃ pahāya, kāmesumicchācārā paṭivirato hoti. Yā tā māturakkhitā …pe… antamaso mālāguḷaparikkhittāpi, tathārūpāsu na cārittaṃ āpajjitā hoti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tividhā kāyakammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti.

Having abandoned sexual misconduct, he abstains from sexual misconduct. He does not have intercourse with those protected by their mother, father, brothers, sisters, relatives, or those under guardianship, even those betrothed with a garland. Thus, monks, threefold bodily good conduct, wholesome and intentional, is productive of happiness and ripens in happiness.

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, catubbidhā vacīkammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco musāvādaṃ pahāya musāvādā paṭivirato hoti. Sabhaggato vā parisaggato vā ñātimajjhagato vā pūgamajjhagato vā rājakulamajjhagato vā abhinīto sakkhipuṭṭho ‘ehambho purisa, yaṃ jānāsi taṃ vadehī’ti, so ajānaṃ vā āha ‘na jānāmī’ti, jānaṃ vā āha ‘jānāmī’ti, apassaṃ vā āha ‘na passāmī’ti, passaṃ vā āha ‘passāmī’ti, iti attahetu vā parahetu vā āmisakiñcikkhahetu vā na sampajānamusā bhāsitā hoti.

And how, monks, is fourfold verbal good conduct, wholesome and intentional, productive of happiness and ripening in happiness? Here, monks, someone abandons false speech and refrains from false speech. When summoned to a council, an assembly, a family gathering, a guild meeting, or a royal court, and questioned as a witness: ‘Come, good man, say what you know,’ if he does not know, he says, ‘I do not know’; or if he knows, he says, ‘I know’; or if he does not see, he says, ‘I do not see’; or if he sees, he says, ‘I see.’ Thus, he does not knowingly speak falsehood for his own sake, for another’s sake, or for some trifling worldly gain.

‘‘Pisuṇaṃ vācaṃ pahāya, pisuṇāya vācāya paṭivirato hoti – na ito sutvā amutra akkhātā imesaṃ bhedāya, amutra vā sutvā na imesaṃ akkhātā amūsaṃ bhedāya. Iti bhinnānaṃ vā sandhātā sahitānaṃ vā anuppadātā samaggārāmo samaggarato samagganandiṃ, samaggakaraṇiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

He abandons slanderous speech and refrains from slanderous speech. He does not repeat here what he heard there to divide those people, nor does he repeat there what he heard here to divide those people. Thus, he reconciles those who are divided and encourages those who are united; he loves concord, delights in concord, enjoys concord, and speaks words that create concord.

‘‘Pharusaṃ vācaṃ pahāya, pharusāya vācāya paṭivirato hoti. Yā sā vācā nelā kaṇṇasukhā pemanīyā hadayaṅgamā porī bahujanakantā bahujanamanāpā, tathārūpiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti.

Having abandoned harsh speech, he abstains from harsh speech. He speaks such words as are gentle, pleasing to the ear, affectionate, going to the heart, urbane, pleasing to the many, and agreeable to the many.

‘‘Samphappalāpaṃ pahāya, samphappalāpā paṭivirato hoti kālavādī bhūtavādī atthavādī dhammavādī vinayavādī, nidhānavatiṃ vācaṃ bhāsitā hoti kālena sāpadesaṃ pariyantavatiṃ atthasaṃhitaṃ. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, catubbidhā vacīkammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti.

Having abandoned frivolous talk, he abstains from frivolous talk. He speaks at the right time, speaks what is factual, speaks what is beneficial, speaks of the Dhamma and the Discipline. He speaks words that are worth treasuring, spoken at the right time, with supporting reasons, well-defined, and connected with the goal. In this way, monks, fourfold accomplishment in verbal action, born of wholesome intention, has happiness as its outcome and happiness as its result.

‘‘Kathañca, bhikkhave, tividhā manokammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti? Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco anabhijjhālu hoti. Yaṃ taṃ parassa paravittūpakaraṇaṃ taṃ anabhijjhātā hoti – ‘aho vata, yaṃ parassa taṃ mamassā’ti.

And how, monks, is the accomplishment of threefold mental action, born of wholesome intention, conducive to happiness and ripening in happiness? Here, monks, someone is uncovetous. He does not covet the belongings and property of others, thinking: ‘Oh, that what belongs to others would be mine!’

‘‘Abyāpannacitto hoti appaduṭṭhamanasaṅkappo – ‘ime sattā averā hontu abyāpajjā anīghā, sukhī attānaṃ pariharantū’ti.

He has a mind free from ill will, without malice, thinking, ‘May these beings be free from animosity, free from affliction, free from trouble, and may they live happily!’

‘‘Sammādiṭṭhiko hoti aviparītadassano – ‘atthi dinnaṃ, atthi yiṭṭhaṃ…pe… ye imañca lokaṃ parañca lokaṃ sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā pavedentī’ti. Evaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tividhā manokammantasampatti kusalasañcetanikā sukhudrayā sukhavipākā hoti.

He has right view, an undistorted perspective: ‘There is what is given, what is offered, what is sacrificed…there are beings who, having directly known and realized this world and the next for themselves, proclaim it.’ In this way, monks, the accomplishment of threefold mental action, born of wholesome intention, is conducive to happiness and ripens in happiness.

‘‘Tividhakāyakammantasampattikusalasañcetanikāhetu vā, bhikkhave, sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti; catubbidhavacīkammantasampattikusalasañcetanikāhetu vā, bhikkhave, sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti; tividhamanokammantasampattikusalasañcetanikāhetu vā, bhikkhave, sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti.

Monks, on account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of threefold bodily action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. Or, monks, on account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of fourfold verbal action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. Or, monks, on account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of threefold mental action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world.

‘‘Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, apaṇṇako maṇi uddhaṃkhitto yena yeneva patiṭṭhāti suppatiṭṭhitaṃyeva patiṭṭhāti; evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, tividhakāyakammantasampattikusalasañcetanikāhetu vā sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti; catubbidhavacīkammantasampattikusalasañcetanikāhetu vā sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti; tividhamanokammantasampattikusalasañcetanikāhetu vā sattā kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapajjanti. Nāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sañcetanikānaṃ kammānaṃ katānaṃ upacitānaṃ appaṭisaṃveditvā byantībhāvaṃ vadāmi. Tañca kho diṭṭheva dhamme upapajje vā apare vā pariyāye. Na tvevāhaṃ, bhikkhave, sañcetanikānaṃ kammānaṃ katānaṃ upacitānaṃ appaṭisaṃveditvā dukkhassantakiriyaṃ vadāmī’’ti. Sattamaṃ. [aṭṭhakathāyaṃ pana aṭṭhamasuttampi ettheva pariyāpannaṃ viya saṃvaṇṇanā dissati]

Just as, monks, a flawless gem thrown upwards lands firmly wherever it falls; in the same way, monks, on account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of threefold bodily action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. On account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of fourfold verbal action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. On account of the wholesome intention for the accomplishment of threefold mental action, beings, on the dissolution of the body, after death, are reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world. I do not say, monks, that intentional actions done and accumulated will come to an end without being experienced. That may be in this very life, or in some other way, in some other course. But I do not say, monks, that there is an end of suffering without experiencing intentional actions done and accumulated. The seventh.
