# AN 10.115 Tatiyaadhammasuttaṃ

## AN 10.115 The Third Discourse on Unrighteousness

115. ‘‘Adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo dhammo ca; anattho ca veditabbo attho ca. Adhammañca viditvā dhammañca, anatthañca viditvā atthañca yathā dhammo yathā attho tathā paṭipajjitabba’’nti. Idamavoca bhagavā. Idaṃ vatvāna sugato uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ pāvisi.

115.“Monks, unrighteousness should be understood and righteousness should be understood; disadvantage should be understood and advantage should be understood. Having understood unrighteousness and righteousness, disadvantage and advantage, one should practice accordingly, in line with righteousness and advantage.” This is what the Blessed One said. Having said this, the Sugata rose from his seat and entered his dwelling.

Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo dhammo ca; anattho ca veditabbo attho ca. Adhammañca viditvā dhammañca, anatthañca viditvā atthañca yathā dhammo yathā attho tathā paṭipajjitabba’nti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’’ti?

Then, soon after the Blessed One had departed, this thought occurred to those monks: “Friends, the Blessed One gave us this brief teaching, and without analyzing the meaning in detail, he rose from his seat and entered his dwelling, saying: ‘Monks, unrighteousness should be understood and righteousness should be understood; disadvantage should be understood and advantage should be understood. Having understood unrighteousness and righteousness, disadvantage and advantage, one should practice accordingly, in line with righteousness and advantage.’ Now, who could explain in detail the meaning of this brief teaching declared by the Blessed One, which was not analyzed in detail?”

Atha kho tesaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ etadahosi – ‘‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāma [puccheyyāma (sī. syā. pī.) ma. ni. 1.202 passitabbaṃ]. Yathā no āyasmā ānando byākarissati tathā naṃ dhāressāmā’’ti.

Then this thought occurred to those monks: “Venerable Ānanda is praised and esteemed by the Teacher and by his wise fellow practitioners. And Venerable Ānanda is capable of explaining in detail the meaning of this brief teaching declared by the Blessed One, which was not analyzed in detail. Suppose we were to approach Venerable Ānanda; having approached, we would question Venerable Ānanda about this matter. As Venerable Ānanda explains it to us, so we will remember it.”

Atha kho te bhikkhū yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā ānandena saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavocuṃ –

Then those monks approached Venerable Ānanda. Having approached, they exchanged polite greetings with Venerable Ānanda. After exchanging courteous greetings and amiable talk, they sat down to one side. Sitting to one side, those monks said to Venerable Ānanda:

‘‘Idaṃ kho no, āvuso ānanda, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘adhammo ca…pe… tathā paṭipajjitabba’nti.

“Friend Ānanda, the Blessed One, having given us this brief teaching, without analyzing the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: ‘Unrighteousness should be understood… and one should practice accordingly, in line with righteousness and advantage.’”

‘‘Tesaṃ no, āvuso, amhākaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – adhammo ca…pe… tathā paṭipajjitabbanti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’ti?

“Soon after the Blessed One had departed, this thought occurred to us, friends: ‘The Blessed One gave us this brief teaching and, without analyzing the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: Unrighteousness should be understood… and one should practice accordingly, in line with righteousness and advantage. Who could explain in detail the meaning of this brief teaching declared by the Blessed One, which was not analyzed in detail?’”

‘‘Tesaṃ no, āvuso, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāma. Yathā no āyasmā ānando byākarissati tathā naṃ dhāressāmā’ti. Vibhajatu āyasmā ānando’’ti.

“Then this thought occurred to us, friends: ‘Venerable Ānanda is praised and esteemed by the Teacher and by his wise fellow practitioners. And Venerable Ānanda is capable of fully explaining the meaning of this brief teaching declared by the Blessed One—the meaning that was not analyzed in detail. Why don’t we approach Venerable Ānanda and question him about this matter? As Venerable Ānanda explains it to us, so we will remember it.’ May Venerable Ānanda please explain it.”

‘‘Seyyathāpi, āvuso, puriso sāratthiko sāragavesī sārapariyesanaṃ caramāno mahato rukkhassa tiṭṭhato sāravato atikkammeva mūlaṃ atikkamma khandhaṃ sākhāpalāse sāraṃ pariyesitabbaṃ maññeyya; evaṃsampadamidaṃ āyasmantānaṃ satthari sammukhībhūte taṃ bhagavantaṃ atisitvā amhe etamatthaṃ paṭipucchitabbaṃ maññatha. So hāvuso, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti passaṃ passati, cakkhubhūto ñāṇabhūto dhammabhūto brahmabhūto vattā pavattā atthassa ninnetā amatassa dātā dhammassāmī tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi yaṃ tumhe bhagavantaṃyeva upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha. Yathā vo bhagavā byākareyya tathā naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti.

“Just as, friends, a man needing heartwood, seeking heartwood, wandering in search of heartwood, might pass over the root and trunk of a great standing tree endowed with heartwood, thinking to seek heartwood among the branches and foliage—such is the case with you venerable ones who, with the Teacher present before you, think to question us about this matter, overlooking that Blessed One. For, friends, the Blessed One knows what is to be known, sees what is to be seen; he is vision, he is knowledge, he is Dhamma, he is Brahma; he is a speaker, a proclaimer, an explainer of meaning, a giver of the Deathless, a lord of the Dhamma, the Tathāgata. That indeed would have been the opportune time for you to approach the Blessed One himself and question him about this matter. As the Blessed One explains it to you, so you should remember it.”

‘‘Addhāvuso ānanda, bhagavā jānaṃ jānāti passaṃ passati cakkhubhūto ñāṇabhūto dhammabhūto brahmabhūto vattā pavattā atthassa ninnetā amatassa dātā dhammassāmī tathāgato. So ceva panetassa kālo ahosi yaṃ mayaṃ bhagavantaṃyeva upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāma, yathā no bhagavā byākareyya tathā naṃ dhāreyyāma. Api cāyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Vibhajatāyasmā ānando agaruṃ katvā’’ti.

Certainly, friend Ānanda, the Blessed One knows, sees; he is vision, he is knowledge, he is Dhamma, he is Brahma; he is the speaker, the proclaimer, the guide to meaning, the giver of the deathless, the lord of the Dhamma, the Tathāgata. This was the right time for us to approach the Blessed One and ask him about this matter; just as the Blessed One would explain it to us, so we would remember it. But the Venerable Ānanda is praised and esteemed by the Teacher and by his wise fellow practitioners. The Venerable Ānanda is capable of analyzing in detail the meaning of this brief statement, which the Blessed One stated without a detailed analysis of its meaning. Let the Venerable Ānanda explain it without reluctance.

‘‘Tenahāvuso, suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha; bhāsissāmī’’ti. ‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa paccassosuṃ. Athāyasmā ānando etadavoca –

Then, friends, listen and attend closely; I will speak." "Yes, friend," those monks replied to the Venerable Ānanda. Then the Venerable Ānanda said this:

‘‘Yaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo dhammo ca; anattho ca veditabbo attho ca. Adhammañca viditvā dhammañca, anatthañca viditvā atthañca yathā dhammo yathā attho tathā paṭipajjitabba’nti.

Friends, the Blessed One, having given us a brief summary without elaborating the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: ‘Monks, what is unwholesome and what is wholesome should be understood; what is unbeneficial and what is beneficial should be understood. Having understood what is unwholesome and what is wholesome, what is unbeneficial and what is beneficial, one should practice accordingly, as is fitting for the wholesome and the beneficial.’

Katamo cāvuso, adhammo, katamo ca dhammo, katamo ca anattho, katamo ca attho?

And what, friends, is the unwholesome and what is the wholesome? What is the disadvantage and what is the advantage?

‘‘Micchādiṭṭhi, āvuso, adhammo; sammādiṭṭhi dhammo; ye ca micchādiṭṭhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammādiṭṭhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

Wrong view, friend, is the non-Dhamma; right view is the Dhamma. The many evil, unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong view—this is disadvantage. The many wholesome states that reach fulfillment by development conditioned by right view—this is advantage.

‘‘Micchāsaṅkappo, āvuso, adhammo; sammāsaṅkappo dhammo… micchāvācā, āvuso, adhammo; sammāvācā dhammo … micchākammanto, āvuso, adhammo; sammākammanto dhammo… micchāājīvo, āvuso, adhammo; sammāājīvo dhammo… micchāvāyāmo, āvuso, adhammo; sammāvāyāmo dhammo… micchāsati, āvuso, adhammo; sammāsati dhammo… micchāsamādhi, āvuso, adhammo; sammāsamādhi dhammo… micchāñāṇaṃ, āvuso, adhammo; sammāñāṇaṃ dhammo….

Wrong thought, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right thought is the Dhamma... Wrong speech, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right speech is the Dhamma... Wrong action, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right action is the Dhamma... Wrong livelihood, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right livelihood is the Dhamma... Wrong effort, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right effort is the Dhamma... Wrong mindfulness, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right mindfulness is the Dhamma... Wrong samādhi, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right samādhi is the Dhamma... Wrong knowledge, friends, is the non-Dhamma; right knowledge is the Dhamma...

Micchāvimutti, āvuso, adhammo; sammāvimutti dhammo; ye ca micchāvimuttipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāvimuttipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

Friend, wrong liberation is non-Dhamma; right liberation is Dhamma. And whatever numerous evil, unwholesome states arise conditioned by wrong liberation, this is disadvantage. But conditioned by right liberation, numerous wholesome states go to completion by means of development, this is advantage.

‘‘Ayaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo dhammo ca…pe… tathā paṭipajjitabba’nti, imassa kho ahaṃ, āvuso, bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa evaṃ vitthārena atthaṃ ājānāmi. Ākaṅkhamānā ca pana tumhe, āvuso, bhagavantaṃyeva upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha. Yathā vo bhagavā byākaroti [byākareyya (syā.)] tathā naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti.

Friends, the Blessed One, having given us a brief summary without elaborating the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling: ‘Monks, what is unwholesome and what is wholesome… and one should practice accordingly.’ Friends, this is how I understand in detail the meaning of this brief statement, which the Blessed One stated without a detailed analysis of its meaning. But if you wish, friends, you should approach the Blessed One himself and question him about this matter. As the Blessed One explains it to you, so you should remember it.”

‘‘Evamāvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa bhāsitaṃ abhinanditvā anumoditvā uṭṭhāyāsanā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhū bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ –

Yes, friend," those monks replied to Venerable Ānanda. Having rejoiced and approved of his words, they rose from their seats and approached the Blessed One. Having approached, they paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those monks said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Yaṃ kho no bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – ‘adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo…pe… tathā paṭijjitabba’nti.

The Blessed One gave us a brief outline and, without analyzing the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling, saying: ‘Monks, what is unwholesome and what is wholesome should be understood; what is unbeneficial and what is beneficial should be understood. Having understood what is unwholesome and what is wholesome, what is unbeneficial and what is beneficial, one should practice accordingly, as is fitting for the wholesome and the beneficial.’

‘‘Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ acirapakkantassa bhagavato etadahosi – ‘idaṃ kho no, āvuso, bhagavā saṃkhittena uddesaṃ uddisitvā vitthārena atthaṃ avibhajitvā uṭṭhāyāsanā vihāraṃ paviṭṭho – adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo…pe… tathā paṭipajjitabbanti. Ko nu kho imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajeyyā’ti?

Venerable sir, soon after the Blessed One had left, this thought occurred to us: ‘Friends, the Blessed One gave us this brief teaching and, without explaining the meaning in detail, rose from his seat and entered his dwelling—"Monks, what is unwholesome and what is wholesome should be understood; what is unbeneficial and what is beneficial should be understood. Having understood what is unwholesome and what is wholesome, what is unbeneficial and what is beneficial, one should practice accordingly, as is fitting for the wholesome and the beneficial." Who now could explain in detail the meaning of this brief teaching given by the Blessed One, which was stated without a detailed explanation?’

‘‘Tesaṃ no, bhante, amhākaṃ etadahosi – ‘ayaṃ kho āyasmā ānando satthu ceva saṃvaṇṇito sambhāvito ca viññūnaṃ sabrahmacārīnaṃ. Pahoti cāyasmā ānando imassa bhagavatā saṃkhittena uddesassa uddiṭṭhassa vitthārena atthaṃ avibhattassa vitthārena atthaṃ vibhajituṃ. Yaṃnūna mayaṃ yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkameyyāma; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyāma. Yathā no āyasmā ānando byākarissati tathā naṃ dhāressāmā’ti.

Venerable sir, this occurred to us: 'Venerable Ānanda is praised and esteemed by the Teacher and by his wise fellow practitioners. The Venerable Ānanda is capable of analyzing in detail the meaning of this brief statement, which the Blessed One stated without a detailed analysis of its meaning. Why don’t we approach Venerable Ānanda and ask him about this matter? As Venerable Ānanda explains it to us, so we will remember it.'

‘‘Atha kho mayaṃ, bhante, yenāyasmā ānando tenupasaṅkamimhā; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etamatthaṃ apucchimhā. Tesaṃ no, bhante, āyasmatā ānandena imehi ākārehi imehi padehi imehi byañjanehi attho suvibhatto’’ti [vibhattoti (?) evameva hi aññesu īdisasuttesu dissati].

Then, venerable sir, we approached the Venerable Ananda; having approached, we asked the Venerable Ananda about this matter. To us, venerable sir, the Venerable Ananda explained the meaning clearly with these aspects, these expressions, and these terms.

‘‘Sādhu sādhu, bhikkhave! Paṇḍito, bhikkhave, ānando. Mahāpañño, bhikkhave, ānando. Maṃ cepi tumhe, bhikkhave, upasaṅkamitvā etamatthaṃ paṭipuccheyyātha, ahampi cetaṃ evamevaṃ [ahampi taṃ evamevaṃ (ma. ni. 1.205)] byākareyyaṃ yathā taṃ ānandena byākataṃ. Eso ceva tassa [eso cevetassa (ma. ni. 1.205)] attho evañca naṃ dhāreyyāthā’’ti. Tatiyaṃ.

Good, good, monks! Ananda is wise, monks. Ananda is of great wisdom, monks. If you, monks, were to approach me and ask about this matter, I too would explain it in exactly the same way as Ananda has explained it. This is indeed the meaning of it, and you should bear it in mind thus." Third.
