# AN 10.114 Dutiyaadhammasuttaṃ

## AN 10.114 The Second Discourse on Unrighteousness

114. ‘‘Adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo dhammo ca; anattho ca veditabbo attho ca. Adhammañca viditvā dhammañca, anatthañca viditvā atthañca yathā dhammo yathā attho tathā paṭipajjitabbaṃ.

114.“Monks, unrighteousness should be understood and righteousness should be understood; disadvantage should be understood and advantage should be understood. Having understood unrighteousness and righteousness, disadvantage and advantage, one should practice accordingly, in line with righteousness and advantage.”

‘‘Katamo ca, bhikkhave, adhammo, katamo ca dhammo, katamo ca anattho, katamo ca attho?

“And what, monks, is unrighteousness, what is righteousness, what is disadvantage, and what is advantage?”

‘‘Micchādiṭṭhi, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammādiṭṭhi dhammo; ye ca micchādiṭṭhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammādiṭṭhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong view, monks, is unrighteousness; right view is righteousness. The many evil, unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong view: this is disadvantage. And conditioned by right view, many wholesome states go to the perfection of development: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāsaṅkappo, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāsaṅkappo dhammo; ye ca micchāsaṅkappapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāsaṅkappapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong thought, monks, is unrighteousness; right thought is righteousness. The many evil, unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong thought: this is disadvantage. And conditioned by right thought, many wholesome states go to the perfection of development: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāvācā, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāvācā dhammo; ye ca micchāvācāpaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāvācāpaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong speech, monks, is unrighteousness; right speech is righteousness. The many evil, unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong speech: this is disadvantage. And conditioned by right speech, many wholesome states go to the perfection of development: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchākammanto, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammākammanto dhammo; ye ca micchākammantapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammākammantapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong action, monks, is unrighteousness; right action is righteousness. And conditioned by wrong action, many evil, unwholesome states originate: this is disadvantage. And conditioned by right action, many wholesome states go to the perfection of development: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāājīvo, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāājīvo dhammo; ye ca micchāājīvapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāājīvapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong livelihood, monks, is unrighteousness; right livelihood is righteousness. And the many evil, unwholesome states that arise conditioned by wrong livelihood: this is disadvantage. And conditioned by right livelihood, many wholesome states go to the perfection of development: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāvāyāmo, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāvāyāmo dhammo; ye ca micchāvāyāmapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāvāyāmapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong effort, monks, is unrighteousness; right effort is righteousness. And conditioned by wrong effort, many evil, unwholesome states arise: this is disadvantage. And conditioned by right effort, many wholesome states go to the perfection of development: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāsati, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāsati dhammo; ye ca micchāsatipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāsatipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong mindfulness, monks, is unrighteousness; right mindfulness is righteousness. And whatever numerous evil, unwholesome states arise conditioned by wrong mindfulness: this is disadvantage. And whatever numerous wholesome states go to the perfection of development conditioned by right mindfulness: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāsamādhi, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāsamādhi dhammo; ye ca micchāsamādhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāsamādhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong samādhi, monks, is unrighteousness; right samādhi is righteousness. And whatever numerous evil, unwholesome states arise conditioned by wrong samādhi: this is disadvantage. And whatever numerous wholesome states go to the perfection of development conditioned by right samādhi: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāñāṇaṃ, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāñāṇaṃ dhammo; ye ca micchāñāṇapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāñāṇapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong knowledge, monks, is unrighteousness; right knowledge is righteousness. And whatever numerous evil, unwholesome states arise conditioned by wrong knowledge: this is disadvantage. And whatever numerous wholesome states go to the perfection of development conditioned by right knowledge: this is advantage.”

‘‘Micchāvimutti, bhikkhave, adhammo; sammāvimutti dhammo; ye ca micchāvimuttipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti, ayaṃ anattho; sammāvimuttipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti, ayaṃ attho.

“Wrong liberation, monks, is unrighteousness; right liberation is righteousness. And whatever numerous evil, unwholesome states arise conditioned by wrong liberation: this is disadvantage. And conditioned by right liberation, numerous wholesome states go to the perfection of development: this is advantage.”

‘‘‘Adhammo ca, bhikkhave, veditabbo dhammo ca; anattho ca veditabbo attho ca. Adhammañca viditvā dhammañca, anatthañca viditvā atthañca yathā dhammo yathā attho tathā paṭipajjitabba’nti, iti yaṃ taṃ vuttaṃ, idametaṃ paṭicca vutta’’nti. Dutiyaṃ.

“‘Monks, unrighteousness should be understood and righteousness should be understood; disadvantage should be understood and advantage should be understood. Having understood unrighteousness and righteousness, disadvantage and advantage, one should practice accordingly, in line with righteousness and advantage.’ This is what was said, and it was said with reference to this.” The Second.
