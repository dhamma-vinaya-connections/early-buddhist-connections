# AN 10.22 Adhivuttipadasuttaṃ

## AN 10.22 The Discourse on the Grounds for Declaration

22. Atha kho āyasmā ānando yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ bhagavā etadavoca –

22.Then Venerable Ānanda approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. The Blessed One then said this to Venerable Ānanda, seated there:

‘‘Ye te, ānanda, dhammā tesaṃ tesaṃ adhivuttipadānaṃ [adhimuttipadānaṃ (ka.)] abhiññā sacchikiriyāya saṃvattanti, visārado ahaṃ, ānanda, tattha paṭijānāmi. ‘Tesaṃ tesaṃ tathā tathā dhammaṃ desetuṃ yathā yathā paṭipanno santaṃ vā atthīti ñassati, asantaṃ vā natthīti ñassati, hīnaṃ vā hīnanti ñassati, paṇītaṃ vā paṇītanti ñassati, sauttaraṃ vā sauttaranti ñassati, anuttaraṃ vā anuttaranti ñassati; yathā yathā vā pana taṃ ñāteyyaṃ vā daṭṭheyyaṃ vā sacchikareyyaṃ vā, tathā tathā ñassati vā dakkhati vā sacchikarissati vā’ti ṭhānametaṃ vijjati. Etadānuttariyaṃ, ānanda, ñāṇānaṃ yadidaṃ tattha tattha yathābhūtañāṇaṃ. Etasmā cāhaṃ, ānanda, ñāṇā aññaṃ ñāṇaṃ uttaritaraṃ vā paṇītataraṃ vā natthīti vadāmi.

"Ānanda, concerning those principles that conduce to the direct knowledge and realization of the various grounds for declaration, I am confident, Ānanda, in making this claim: ‘To teach the Dhamma in such and such a way that one who practices accordingly will know what is as ‘it is,’ and what is not as ‘it is not’; what is inferior as ‘it is inferior,’ and what is superior as ‘it is superior’; what is surpassable as ‘it is surpassable,’ and what is unsurpassed as ‘it is unsurpassed.’ And whatever is to be known, seen, or realized, one will know, see, or realize it accordingly. This is possible. This, Ānanda, is the unsurpassed among knowledges, namely, knowledge of things as they are in each case. And I say, Ānanda, that there is no other knowledge higher or more excellent than this knowledge."

‘‘Dasayimāni, ānanda, tathāgatassa tathāgatabalāni, yehi balehi samannāgato tathāgato āsabhaṃ ṭhānaṃ paṭijānāti, parisāsu sīhanādaṃ nadati, brahmacakkaṃ pavatteti. Katamāni dasa? Idhānanda, tathāgato ṭhānañca ṭhānato aṭṭhānañca aṭṭhānato yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Yampānanda, tathāgato ṭhānañca ṭhānato aṭṭhānañca aṭṭhānato yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti, idampānanda, tathāgatassa tathāgatabalaṃ hoti, yaṃ balaṃ āgamma tathāgato āsabhaṃ ṭhānaṃ paṭijānāti, parisāsu sīhanādaṃ nadati, brahmacakkaṃ pavatteti.

These, Ānanda, are the ten powers of a Tathāgata, by which the Tathāgata is endowed and by which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion's roar in the assemblies, and sets in motion the Brahma-wheel. What are the ten? Here, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is what is possible as possible and what is impossible as impossible. And that, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is what is possible as possible and what is impossible as impossible — this, Ānanda, is a power of the Tathāgata, by relying on which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion's roar in the assemblies, and sets in motion the Brahma-wheel.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato atītānāgatapaccuppannānaṃ kammasamādānānaṃ ṭhānaso hetuso vipākaṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Yampānanda…pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the results of kamma-undertakings, past, future, and present, together with their grounds and causes. And that, Ānanda… this, Ānanda…

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato sabbatthagāminiṃ paṭipadaṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Yampānanda…pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the way leading to all destinations. And that, Ānanda… this, Ānanda…

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato anekadhātuṃ nānādhātuṃ lokaṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Yampānanda …pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the world with its many elements and various elements. And that, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the world with its many elements and various elements—this, Ānanda, is a power of the Tathāgata, by relying on which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion's roar in the assemblies, and sets in motion the Brahma-wheel.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato sattānaṃ nānādhimuttikataṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Yampānanda…pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the diverse inclinations of beings. And that, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the diverse inclinations of beings—this, Ānanda, is a power of the Tathāgata, by relying on which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion's roar in the assemblies, and sets in motion the Brahma-wheel.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato parasattānaṃ parapuggalānaṃ indriyaparopariyattaṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Yampānanda…pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the maturity and immaturity of the faculties of other beings and individuals. And that, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as it actually is the maturity and immaturity of the faculties of other beings and individuals—this, Ānanda, is a power of the Tathāgata, by relying on which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion's roar in the assemblies, and sets in motion the Brahma-wheel.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato jhānavimokkhasamādhisamāpattīnaṃ saṃkilesaṃ vodānaṃ vuṭṭhānaṃ yathābhūtaṃ pajānāti. Yampānanda…pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as they actually are the defilement, purification, and emergence from the absorptions, liberations, samādhis, and attainments. And that, Ānanda, the Tathāgata understands as they actually are the defilement, purification, and emergence from the absorptions, liberations, samādhis, and attainments—this, Ānanda, is a power of the Tathāgata, by relying on which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion's roar in the assemblies, and sets in motion the Brahma-wheel.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati, seyyathidaṃ – ekampi jātiṃ dvepi jātiyo…pe… iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati. Yampānanda…pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata recollects many kinds of past lives, that is, one birth, two births… so with aspects and details he recollects many kinds of past lives. And that, Ānanda, the Tathāgata recollects many kinds of past lives, that is, one birth, two births… so with aspects and details he recollects many kinds of past lives—this, Ānanda, is a power of the Tathāgata, by relying on which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion's roar in the assemblies, and sets in motion the Brahma-wheel.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena…pe… yathākammūpage satte pajānāti. Yampānanda…pe… idampānanda…pe….

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata, with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, sees beings passing away and being reborn according to their kamma. And that, Ānanda… this, Ānanda…

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, ānanda, tathāgato āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Yampānanda, tathāgato āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ…pe… sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Idampānanda, tathāgatassa tathāgatabalaṃ hoti, yaṃ balaṃ āgamma tathāgato āsabhaṃ ṭhānaṃ paṭijānāti, parisāsu sīhanādaṃ nadati, brahmacakkaṃ pavatteti.

Furthermore, Ānanda, the Tathāgata, through the destruction of the taints, having realized for himself with direct knowledge, here and now, the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, dwells having attained to it. And that, Ānanda, the Tathāgata, through the destruction of the taints… dwells having realized for himself with direct knowledge, having attained to it. This, Ānanda, is the Tathāgata’s power of the Tathāgata, by resorting to which power the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion’s roar in the assemblies, and sets rolling the wheel of Brahma.

‘‘Imāni kho, ānanda, dasa tathāgatassa tathāgatabalāni, yehi balehi samannāgato tathāgato āsabhaṃ ṭhānaṃ paṭijānāti, parisāsu sīhanādaṃ nadati, brahmacakkaṃ pavattetī’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

These, Ānanda, are the ten powers of a Tathāgata, possessed of which the Tathāgata claims the foremost place, roars his lion’s roar in the assemblies, and sets rolling the wheel of Brahma.” The Second.
