57."Bhikkhus, these ten perceptions, when developed and cultivated, are of great fruit and benefit, culminating in the deathless, with the deathless as their consummation. What ten? The perception of impermanence, the perception of not-self, the perception of death, the perception of the repulsiveness of food, the perception of non-delight in the entire world, the perception of a skeleton, the perception of a worm-infested corpse, the perception of a livid corpse, the perception of a festering corpse, and the perception of a bloated corpse. These, bhikkhus, are the ten perceptions that, when developed and cultivated, are of great fruit and benefit, culminating in the deathless, with the deathless as their consummation." Seventh.
