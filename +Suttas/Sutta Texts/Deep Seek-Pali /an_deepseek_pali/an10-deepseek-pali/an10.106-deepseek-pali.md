# AN 10.106 Nijjarasuttaṃ

## AN 10.106 The Discourse on Effacement

106. [dī. ni. 3.360] ‘‘Dasayimāni, bhikkhave, nijjaravatthūni. Katamāni dasa? Sammādiṭṭhikassa, bhikkhave, micchādiṭṭhi nijjiṇṇā hoti; ye ca micchādiṭṭhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammādiṭṭhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

106.“Bhikkhus, there are these ten grounds for effacement. What ten? For one with right view, wrong view is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong view are also worn away; and conditioned by right view, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāsaṅkappassa, bhikkhave, micchāsaṅkappo nijjiṇṇo hoti; ye ca micchāsaṅkappapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāsaṅkappapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right intention, wrong intention is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong intention are also worn away; and conditioned by right intention, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāvācassa, bhikkhave, micchāvācā nijjiṇṇā hoti; ye ca micchāvācāpaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāvācāpaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right speech, wrong speech is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong speech are also worn away; and conditioned by right speech, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammākammantassa, bhikkhave, micchākammanto nijjiṇṇo hoti; ye ca micchākammantapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammākammantapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right action, wrong action is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong action are also worn away; and conditioned by right action, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāājīvassa, bhikkhave, micchāājīvo nijjiṇṇo hoti; ye ca micchāājīvapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāājīvapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right livelihood, wrong livelihood is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong livelihood are also worn away; and conditioned by right livelihood, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāvāyāmassa, bhikkhave, micchāvāyāmo nijjiṇṇo hoti; ye ca micchāvāyāmapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāvāyāmapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right effort, wrong effort is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong effort are also worn away; and conditioned by right effort, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāsatissa, bhikkhave, micchāsati nijjiṇṇā hoti; ye ca micchāsatipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāsatipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right mindfulness, wrong mindfulness is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong mindfulness are also worn away; and conditioned by right mindfulness, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāsamādhissa, bhikkhave, micchāsamādhi nijjiṇṇo hoti; ye ca micchāsamādhipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāsamādhipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right samādhi, wrong samādhi is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong samādhi are also worn away; and conditioned by right samādhi, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāñāṇissa, bhikkhave, micchāñāṇaṃ nijjiṇṇaṃ hoti; ye ca micchāñāṇapaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāñāṇapaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right knowledge, wrong knowledge is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong knowledge are also worn away; and conditioned by right knowledge, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development.

‘‘Sammāvimuttissa, bhikkhave, micchāvimutti nijjiṇṇā hoti; ye ca micchāvimuttipaccayā aneke pāpakā akusalā dhammā sambhavanti te cassa nijjiṇṇā honti; sammāvimuttipaccayā ca aneke kusalā dhammā bhāvanāpāripūriṃ gacchanti. Imāni kho, bhikkhave, dasa nijjaravatthūnī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“Bhikkhus, for one with right liberation, wrong liberation is worn away; and the many evil, unwholesome qualities that arise conditioned by wrong liberation are also worn away; and conditioned by right liberation, many wholesome qualities go to the fulfillment of development. These, bhikkhus, are the ten grounds for effacement.” The sixth.
