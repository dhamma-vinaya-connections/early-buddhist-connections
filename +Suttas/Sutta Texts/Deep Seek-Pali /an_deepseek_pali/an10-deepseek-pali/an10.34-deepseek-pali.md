34."Venerable sir, with how many qualities should a bhikkhu be endowed to confer the higher ordination?" "UpÄli, a bhikkhu should be endowed with ten qualities to confer the higher ordination. What ten? Here, UpÄli, a bhikkhu is virtuous; he dwells restrained by the restraint of the PÄtimokkha, accomplished in good conduct and resort, seeing danger in the slightest faults, and trains himself by undertaking the training rules. He is very learned, a bearer of what he has learned, an accumulator of what he has learned; those teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with their meaning and phrasing, which proclaim the perfectly complete and pure holy lifeâ€”such teachings he has heard much of, retained in mind, familiarized by verbal recitation, mentally scrutinized, and penetrated well by view.
