# AN 10.30 Dutiyakosalasuttaṃ

## AN 10.30 The Second Kosalan Discourse

30. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena rājā pasenadi kosalo uyyodhikā nivatto hoti vijitasaṅgāmo laddhādhippāyo. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo yena ārāmo tena pāyāsi. Yāvatikā yānassa bhūmi, yānena gantvā yānā paccorohitvā pattikova ārāmaṃ pāvisi. Tena kho pana samayena sambahulā bhikkhū abbhokāse caṅkamanti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo yena te bhikkhū tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā te bhikkhū etadavoca – ‘‘kahaṃ nu kho, bhante, bhagavā etarahi viharati arahaṃ sammāsambuddho. Dassanakāmā hi mayaṃ, bhante, taṃ bhagavantaṃ arahantaṃ sammāsambuddha’’nti. ‘‘Eso, mahārāja, vihāro saṃvutadvāro. Tena appasaddo upasaṅkamitvā ataramāno ālindaṃ pavisitvā ukkāsitvā aggaḷaṃ ākoṭehi; vivarissati te bhagavā dvāra’’nti.

30.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. Now at that time King Pasenadi of Kosala had returned from a military expedition, victorious in battle, his purpose accomplished. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala set out for the park. He went by carriage as far as the ground was passable for a carriage, then dismounted from his carriage and entered the park on foot. Now on that occasion, a number of bhikkhus were walking up and down in the open air. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala approached those bhikkhus and said to them: “Venerable sirs, where is the Blessed One now dwelling, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One? For we wish to see that Blessed One, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One.” “That is his dwelling, great king, with the door closed. Approach it quietly and unhurriedly, enter the porch, clear your throat, and tap on the door-bar. The Blessed One will open the door for you.”

Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo yena so vihāro saṃvutadvāro, tena appasaddo upasaṅkamitvā ataramāno ālindaṃ pavisitvā ukkāsitvā aggaḷaṃ ākoṭesi. Vivari bhagavā dvāraṃ. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo vihāraṃ pavisitvā bhagavato pādesu sirasā nipatitvā bhagavato pādāni mukhena ca paricumbati pāṇīhi ca parisambāhati nāmañca sāveti – ‘‘rājāhaṃ, bhante, pasenadi kosalo; rājāhaṃ, bhante, pasenadi kosalo’’ti.

Then King Pasenadi of Kosala approached that dwelling with its closed door quietly and unhurriedly, entered the porch, cleared his throat, and tapped on the door-bar. The Blessed One opened the door. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala entered the dwelling, prostrated himself with his head at the Blessed One’s feet, kissed the Blessed One’s feet and stroked them with his hands, and announced his name: “I am King Pasenadi of Kosala, venerable sir; I am King Pasenadi of Kosala, venerable sir.”

‘‘Kaṃ pana tvaṃ, mahārāja, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno imasmiṃ sarīre evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karosi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsesī’’ti? ‘‘Kataññutaṃ kho ahaṃ, bhante, kataveditaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Seeing what reason, great king, do you pay such supreme homage to this body and show such an offering of friendship?” “Venerable sir, it is because I see my debt of gratitude and thankfulness that I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show such an offering of friendship.

‘‘Bhagavā hi, bhante, bahujanahitāya paṭipanno bahujanasukhāya bahuno janassa ariye ñāye patiṭṭhāpitā yadidaṃ kalyāṇadhammatāya kusaladhammatāya. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā bahujanahitāya paṭipanno bahujanasukhāya bahuno janassa ariye ñāye patiṭṭhāpitā yadidaṃ kalyāṇadhammatāya kusaladhammatāya, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“The Blessed One, venerable sir, is practicing for the welfare of many people, for the happiness of many people; he has established a great many people in the noble method, that is, in what is admirable and what is wholesome. Since the Blessed One is practicing for the welfare of many people, for the happiness of many people, and has established a great many people in the noble method, that is, in what is admirable and what is wholesome—seeing this reason, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā sīlavā vuddhasīlo ariyasīlo kusalasīlo kusalasīlena samannāgato. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā sīlavā vuddhasīlo ariyasīlo kusalasīlo kusalasīlena samannāgato, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One is virtuous, his virtue is mature, he possesses noble virtue, he possesses wholesome virtue, he is endowed with wholesome virtue. Since the Blessed One is virtuous, his virtue is mature, he possesses noble virtue, he possesses wholesome virtue, and is endowed with wholesome virtue—seeing this reason too, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā dīgharattaṃ āraññiko, araññavanapatthāni pantāni senāsanāni paṭisevati. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā dīgharattaṃ āraññiko, araññavanapatthāni pantāni senāsanāni paṭisevati, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One has long been a forest dweller, one who resorts to remote jungle and forest lodgings. Since the Blessed One has long been a forest dweller, one who resorts to remote jungle and forest lodgings—seeing this reason too, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā santuṭṭho itarītaracīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārena. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā santuṭṭho itarītaracīvarapiṇḍapātasenāsanagilānapaccayabhesajjaparikkhārena, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi. ‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā āhuneyyo pāhuneyyo dakkhiṇeyyo añjalikaraṇīyo anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā āhuneyyo pāhuneyyo dakkhiṇeyyo añjalikaraṇīyo anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One is content with any kind of robe, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites for the sick. Since the Blessed One is content with any kind of robe, almsfood, lodging, and medicinal requisites for the sick—seeing this reason too, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship. “Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverential salutation, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world. Since the Blessed One is worthy of gifts, worthy of hospitality, worthy of offerings, worthy of reverential salutation, an unsurpassed field of merit for the world—seeing this reason too, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā yāyaṃ kathā abhisallekhikā cetovivaraṇasappāyā, seyyathidaṃ – appicchakathā santuṭṭhikathā pavivekakathā asaṃsaggakathā vīriyārambhakathā sīlakathā samādhikathā paññākathā vimuttikathā vimuttiñāṇadassanakathā, evarūpāya kathāya nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā yāyaṃ kathā abhisallekhikā cetovivaraṇasappāyā, seyyathidaṃ – appicchakathā…pe… vimuttiñāṇadassanakathā, evarūpāya kathāya nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One obtains at will, without trouble, without difficulty, such talk as is concerned with effacement, conducive to the opening up of the heart; that is, talk on fewness of wishes, on contentment, on seclusion, on non-entanglement, on arousing energy, on virtue, on samādhi, on wisdom, on liberation, and on the knowledge and vision of liberation. Since the Blessed One obtains at will, without trouble, without difficulty, such talk…on the knowledge and vision of liberation—seeing this reason too, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā catunnaṃ jhānānaṃ ābhicetasikānaṃ diṭṭhadhammasukhavihārānaṃ nikāmalābhī akicchalābhī akasiralābhī, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One obtains at will, without trouble, without difficulty, the four jhānas, which belong to the higher mind and are pleasant abidings in this very life. Since the Blessed One obtains at will, without trouble, without difficulty, the four jhānas, which belong to the higher mind and are pleasant abidings in this very life—seeing this reason too, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati, seyyathidaṃ – ekampi jātiṃ dvepi jātiyo tissopi jātiyo catassopi jātiyo pañcapi jātiyo dasapi jātiyo vīsampi jātiyo tiṃsampi jātiyo cattālīsampi jātiyo paññāsampi jātiyo jātisatampi jātisahassampi jātisatasahassampi anekepi saṃvaṭṭakappe anekepi vivaṭṭakappe anekepi saṃvaṭṭavivaṭṭakappe – ‘amutrāsiṃ evaṃnāmo evaṃgotto evaṃvaṇṇo evamāhāro evaṃsukhadukkhappaṭisaṃvedī evamāyupariyanto, so tato cuto amutra udapādiṃ; tatrāpāsiṃ evaṃnāmo evaṃgotto evaṃvaṇṇo evamāhāro evaṃsukhadukkhappaṭisaṃvedī evamāyupariyanto, so tato cuto idhūpapanno’ti. Iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati, seyyathidaṃ – ekampi jātiṃ dvepi jātiyo…pe… iti sākāraṃ sauddesaṃ anekavihitaṃ pubbenivāsaṃ anussarati, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Furthermore, venerable sir, the Blessed One recollects his manifold past lives, that is, one birth, two births, three births, four births, five births, ten births, twenty births, thirty births, forty births, fifty births, a hundred births, a thousand births, a hundred thousand births, many eons of world-contraction, many eons of world-expansion, many eons of world-contraction and expansion: ‘There I was so-named, of such a clan, with such an appearance, such was my food, such my experience of pleasure and pain, such my life-span. Passing away from there, I was reborn elsewhere. There, too, I was so-named, of such a clan, with such an appearance, such was my food, such my experience of pleasure and pain, such my life-span. Passing away from there, I was reborn here.’ Thus he recollects his manifold past lives with their modes and details. Since the Blessed One recollects his manifold past lives…with their modes and details—seeing this reason too, venerable sir, I pay such supreme homage to the Blessed One and show my offering of friendship.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passati cavamāne upapajjamāne hīne paṇīte suvaṇṇe dubbaṇṇe, sugate duggate yathākammūpage satte pajānāti – ‘ime vata bhonto sattā kāyaduccaritena samannāgatā vacīduccaritena samannāgatā manoduccaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ upavādakā micchādiṭṭhikā micchādiṭṭhikammasamādānā, te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā apāyaṃ duggatiṃ vinipātaṃ nirayaṃ upapannā; ime vā pana bhonto sattā kāyasucaritena samannāgatā vacīsucaritena samannāgatā manosucaritena samannāgatā ariyānaṃ anupavādakā sammādiṭṭhikā sammādiṭṭhikammasamādānā, te kāyassa bhedā paraṃ maraṇā sugatiṃ saggaṃ lokaṃ upapannā’ti, iti dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena satte passati…pe… yathākammūpage satte pajānāti. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā dibbena cakkhunā visuddhena atikkantamānusakena…pe… yathākammūpage satte pajānāti, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Again, venerable sir, with the divine eye, which is purified and surpasses the human, the Blessed One sees beings passing away and being reborn—inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, fortunate and unfortunate—and he understands beings as passing on according to their kamma: ‘These good beings, endowed with misconduct of body, speech, and mind, revilers of the noble ones, holders of wrong view who have undertaken kamma based on wrong view, with the breakup of the body, after death, have been reborn in the plane of misery, a bad destination, the lower world, in hell. But those other good beings, endowed with good conduct of body, speech, and mind, not revilers of the noble ones, holders of right view who have undertaken kamma based on right view, with the breakup of the body, after death, have been reborn in a good destination, in a heavenly world.’ Thus with the divine eye, which is purified and surpasses the human, he sees beings … and understands them as passing on according to their kamma. Seeing this reason too, venerable sir—that the Blessed One with the divine eye … understands beings as passing on according to their kamma—I show such supreme reverence toward the Blessed One and present this gift of loving-kindness.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhante, bhagavā āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ paññāvimuttiṃ diṭṭheva dhamme sayaṃ abhiññā sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati. Yampi, bhante, bhagavā āsavānaṃ khayā anāsavaṃ cetovimuttiṃ…pe… sacchikatvā upasampajja viharati, idampi kho ahaṃ, bhante, atthavasaṃ sampassamāno bhagavati evarūpaṃ paramanipaccakāraṃ karomi, mettūpahāraṃ upadaṃsemi.

“Again, venerable sir, by the destruction of the taints, the Blessed One in this very life enters and dwells in the taintless liberation of mind, liberation by wisdom, having realized it for himself with direct knowledge. Seeing this reason too, venerable sir—that the Blessed One, by the destruction of the taints … having realized it for himself with direct knowledge, enters and dwells in it—I show such supreme reverence toward the Blessed One and present this gift of loving-kindness.”

‘‘Handa ca dāni mayaṃ, bhante, gacchāma. Bahukiccā mayaṃ bahukaraṇīyā’’ti. ‘‘Yassa dāni tvaṃ, mahārāja, kālaṃ maññasī’’ti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṃ katvā pakkāmīti. Dasamaṃ.

“Well now, venerable sir, we must go. We are busy and have much to do.” “Now, great king, do as you think fit.” Then King Pasenadi of Kosala rose from his seat, and after paying homage to the Blessed One and circumambulating him, he departed. Tenth.

Mahāvaggo tatiyo.

The Great Chapter, the Third.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The summary:

Sīhādhivutti kāyena, cundena kasiṇena ca;Kāḷī ca dve mahāpañhā, kosalehi pare duveti.

Sīha, with the body, with Cunda, and with the kasiṇa; Kāḷī, the two great questions, and two others with the Kosalans.
