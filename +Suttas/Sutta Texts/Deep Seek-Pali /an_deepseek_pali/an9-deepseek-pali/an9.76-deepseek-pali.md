# AN 9.76 Cetasovinibandhasuttaṃ

## AN 9.76 Mental Bondages

92. ‘‘Pañcime, bhikkhave, cetasovinibandhā. Katame pañca? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāmesu avītarāgo hoti…pe… ime kho, bhikkhave, pañca cetasovinibandhā.

92.“Bhikkhus, there are these five bondages of the mind. What five? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is not devoid of lust for sensual pleasures … these, bhikkhus, are the five bondages of the mind.”

‘‘Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ cetasovinibandhānaṃ pahānāya ime cattāro iddhipādā bhāvetabbā. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu chandasamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti, vīriyasamādhi… cittasamādhi… vīmaṃsāsamādhipadhānasaṅkhārasamannāgataṃ iddhipādaṃ bhāveti. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ cetasovinibandhānaṃ pahānāya ime cattāro iddhipādā bhāvetabbā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

“Bhikkhus, for the abandoning of these five bondages of the mind, the four bases of spiritual power are to be developed. What four? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi due to desire and volitional formations of striving; he develops the basis for spiritual power that possesses samādhi due to energy … samādhi due to mind … samādhi due to investigation and volitional formations of striving. Bhikkhus, for the abandoning of these five bondages of the mind, these four bases of spiritual power are to be developed.” Tenth.

Iddhipādavaggo catuttho.

The Chapter on the Bases of Spiritual Power, the Fourth.

Yatheva satipaṭṭhānā, padhānā caturopi ca;Cattāro iddhipādā ca, tatheva sampayojayeti.

Just as the establishments of mindfulness, the four strivings as well, and the four bases of spiritual power, so they are to be conjoined.

(10) 5. Rāgapeyyālaṃ

(10) 5. Lust Repetition Series

93. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya nava dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame nava? Asubhasaññā, maraṇasaññā, āhāre paṭikūlasaññā, sabbaloke anabhiratasaññā, aniccasaññā, anicce dukkhasaññā, dukkhe anattasaññā, pahānasaññā, virāgasaññā – rāgassa, bhikkhave abhiññāya ime nava dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

93.“Bhikkhus, for the direct knowledge of lust, nine qualities are to be developed. What nine? The perception of foulness, the perception of death, the perception of the repulsiveness of food, the perception of non-delight in the whole world, the perception of impermanence, the perception of suffering in what is impermanent, the perception of not-self in what is suffering, the perception of abandoning, and the perception of dispassion. For the direct knowledge of lust, bhikkhus, these nine qualities are to be developed.”

94. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya nava dhammā bhāvetabbā. Katame nava? Paṭhamaṃ jhānaṃ, dutiyaṃ jhānaṃ, tatiyaṃ jhānaṃ, catutthaṃ jhānaṃ, ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ, viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ, ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ, nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṃ, saññāvedayitanirodho – rāgassa, bhikkhave, abhiññāya ime nava dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

94.“Bhikkhus, for the direct knowledge of lust, nine qualities are to be developed. What nine? The first jhāna, the second jhāna, the third jhāna, the fourth jhāna, the base of the infinity of space, the base of the infinity of consciousness, the base of nothingness, the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, and the cessation of perception and feeling. For the direct knowledge of lust, bhikkhus, these nine qualities are to be developed.”

95-112. ‘‘Rāgassa, bhikkhave, pariññāya…pe… parikkhayāya…pe… pahānāya…pe… khayāya…pe… vayāya…pe… virāgāya…pe… nirodhāya…pe… cāgāya…pe… paṭinissaggāya…pe… ime nava dhammā bhāvetabbā’’.

95–112. “Bhikkhus, for the full understanding of lust, … for its complete destruction, … for its abandoning, … for its destruction, … for its passing away, … for its fading away, … for its cessation, … for giving it up, … for its relinquishment, these nine qualities are to be developed.”

113-432. ‘‘Dosassa…pe… mohassa… kodhassa… upanāhassa… makkhassa… paḷāsassa… issāya… macchariyassa… māyāya… sāṭheyyassa… thambhassa… sārambhassa… mānassa… atimānassa… madassa… pamādassa abhiññāya…pe… pariññāya… parikkhayāya… pahānāya… khayāya… vayāya… virāgāya… nirodhāya … cāgāya… paṭinissaggāya…pe… ime nava dhammā bhāvetabbā’’ti.

113–432. “Bhikkhus, for hatred … delusion … anger … hostility … denigration … insolence … envy … stinginess … deceit … fraud … obstinacy … rivalry … conceit … arrogance … intoxication … and heedlessness; and for the direct knowledge of these … for their full understanding … for their complete destruction … for their abandoning … for their destruction … for their passing away … for their fading away … for their cessation … for giving them up … for their relinquishment, these nine qualities are to be developed.”

Rāgapeyyālaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Lust Repetition Series is finished.

Navakanipātapāḷi niṭṭhitā.

The Book of the Nines is finished.
