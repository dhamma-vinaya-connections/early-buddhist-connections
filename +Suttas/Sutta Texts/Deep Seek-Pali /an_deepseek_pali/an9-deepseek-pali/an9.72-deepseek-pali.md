# AN 9.72 Cetasovinibandhasuttaṃ

## AN 9.72 Discourse on the Shackles of the Mind

72. ‘‘Pañcime, bhikkhave, cetasovinibandhā [cetovinibaddhā (sāratthadīpanīṭīkā) a. ni. 5.206; dī. ni. 3.320]. Katame pañca? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāmesu avītarāgo hoti avigatacchando avigatapemo avigatapipāso avigatapariḷāho avigatataṇho. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāmesu avītarāgo hoti avigatacchando avigatapemo avigatapipāso avigatapariḷāho avigatataṇho, tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, ayaṃ paṭhamo cetasovinibandho.

72.Bhikkhus, there are these five shackles of the mind. What are the five? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is not devoid of lust for sensual pleasures, not devoid of desire, not devoid of affection, not devoid of thirst, not devoid of fever, not devoid of craving for them. When a bhikkhu is not devoid of lust for sensual pleasures ... his mind does not incline to ardor, to application, to perseverance, and to striving. When one's mind does not incline to ardor, to application, to perseverance, and to striving, this is the first shackle of the mind.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye avītarāgo hoti…pe… rūpe avītarāgo hoti… yāvadatthaṃ udarāvadehakaṃ bhuñjitvā seyyasukhaṃ passasukhaṃ middhasukhaṃ anuyutto viharati … aññataraṃ devanikāyaṃ paṇidhāya brahmacariyaṃ carati – ‘imināhaṃ sīlena vā vatena vā tapena vā brahmacariyena vā devo vā bhavissāmi devaññataro vā’ti. Yo so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu aññataraṃ devanikāyaṃ paṇidhāya brahmacariyaṃ carati – ‘imināhaṃ sīlena vā vatena vā tapena vā brahmacariyena vā devo vā bhavissāmi devaññataro vā’ti, tassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya. Yassa cittaṃ na namati ātappāya anuyogāya sātaccāya padhānāya, ayaṃ pañcamo cetasovinibandho. Ime kho, bhikkhave, pañca cetasovinibandhā.

Again, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is not devoid of lust for the body ... is not devoid of lust for forms ... having eaten his fill, as much as his belly can hold, he dwells devoted to the pleasure of lying down, the pleasure of lolling, the pleasure of sleep ... he lives the holy life aspiring to be reborn in some order of devas, thinking: ‘By this precept or vow or austerity or holy life, I will become a deva or one of the devas.’ When a bhikkhu lives the holy life aspiring to be reborn in some order of devas ... his mind does not incline to ardor, to application, to perseverance, and to striving. When one's mind does not incline to ardor, to application, to perseverance, and to striving, this is the fifth shackle of the mind. These, bhikkhus, are the five shackles of the mind.

‘‘Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ cetasovinibandhānaṃ pahānāya cattāro satipaṭṭhānā bhāvetabbā. Katame cattāro? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu kāye kāyānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ; vedanāsu…pe… citte…pe… dhammesu dhammānupassī viharati ātāpī sampajāno satimā vineyya loke abhijjhādomanassaṃ. Imesaṃ kho, bhikkhave, pañcannaṃ cetasovinibandhānaṃ pahānāya ime cattāro satipaṭṭhānā bhāvetabbā’’ti. Dasamaṃ.

Bhikkhus, for the abandoning of these five shackles of the mind, the four establishments of mindfulness are to be developed. What four? Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu ![[Satipatthanas]]. He ![[Satipatthanas]]. For the abandoning of these five shackles of the mind, bhikkhus, these four establishments of mindfulness are to be developed. Tenth.

Satipaṭṭhānavaggo dutiyo.

The Chapter on the Establishments of Mindfulness, the Second.

Tassuddānaṃ –

The summary:

Sikkhā nīvaraṇākāmā, khandhā ca orambhāgiyā gati;Maccheraṃ uddhambhāgiyā aṭṭhamaṃ, cetokhilā vinibandhāti.

Training, Hindrances, Sensual Pleasures, Aggregates, Lower Fetters, Destinations, Stinginess, Higher Fetters, Mental Barrenness, and Shackles.

(8) 3. Sammappadhānavaggo

(8) 3. The Chapter on the Right Strivings
