# AN 9.14 Samiddhisuttaṃ

## AN 9.14 The Samiddhi Sutta

14. Atha kho āyasmā samiddhi yenāyasmā sāriputto tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmantaṃ sāriputtaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinnaṃ kho āyasmantaṃ samiddhiṃ āyasmā sāriputto etadavoca – ‘‘kimārammaṇā, samiddhi, purisassa saṅkappavitakkā uppajjantī’’ti? ‘‘Nāmarūpārammaṇā, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kva nānattaṃ gacchantī’’ti? ‘‘Dhātūsu, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃsamudayā’’ti? ‘‘Phassasamudayā, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃsamosaraṇā’’ti? ‘‘Vedanāsamosaraṇā, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃpamukhā’’ti? ‘‘Samādhippamukhā, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃadhipateyyā’’ti? ‘‘Satādhipateyyā, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃuttarā’’ti? ‘‘Paññuttarā, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃsārā’’ti? ‘‘Vimuttisārā, bhante’’ti. ‘‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃogadhā’’ti? ‘‘Amatogadhā, bhante’’ti.

14.Then Venerable Samiddhi approached Venerable Sāriputta. Having approached, he paid homage to Venerable Sāriputta and sat down to one side. As Venerable Samiddhi was sitting to one side, Venerable Sāriputta said to him: “On what do a person’s thoughts and intentions arise, Samiddhi?” “On name-and-form, venerable sir.” “But where, Samiddhi, do they become diverse?” “In the elements, venerable sir.” “But what is their origin, Samiddhi?” “Contact is their origin, venerable sir.” “But what do they converge on, Samiddhi?” “They converge on feeling, venerable sir.” “But what do they have as their chief, Samiddhi?” “samādhi is their chief, venerable sir.” “But what do they have as their governing principle, Samiddhi?” “Mindfulness is their governing principle, venerable sir.” “But what is superior to them, Samiddhi?” “Wisdom is superior to them, venerable sir.” “But what is their essence, Samiddhi?” “Liberation is their essence, venerable sir.” “But what do they plunge into, Samiddhi?” “They plunge into the Deathless, venerable sir.”

‘‘‘Kimārammaṇā, samiddhi, purisassa saṅkappavitakkā uppajjantī’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘nāmarūpārammaṇā, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kva nānattaṃ gacchantī’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘dhātūsu, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃsamudayā’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘phassasamudayā, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃsamosaraṇā’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘vedanāsamosaraṇā, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃpamukhā’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘samādhippamukhā, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃadhipateyyā’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘satādhipateyyā, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃuttarā’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘paññuttarā, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃsārā’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘vimuttisārā, bhante’ti vadesi. ‘Te pana, samiddhi, kiṃogadhā’ti, iti puṭṭho samāno ‘amatogadhā, bhante’ti vadesi. Sādhu sādhu, samiddhi! Sādhu kho tvaṃ, samiddhi, puṭṭho [pañhaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)] puṭṭho vissajjesi, tena ca mā maññī’’ti. Catutthaṃ.

“‘On what do a person’s thoughts and intentions arise, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘On name-and-form, venerable sir.’ ‘But where, Samiddhi, do they become diverse?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘In the elements, venerable sir.’ ‘But what is their origin, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘Contact is their origin, venerable sir.’ ‘But what do they converge on, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘They converge on feeling, venerable sir.’ ‘But what do they have as their chief, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘samādhi is their chief, venerable sir.’ ‘But what do they have as their governing principle, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘Mindfulness is their governing principle, venerable sir.’ ‘But what is superior to them, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘Wisdom is superior to them, venerable sir.’ ‘But what is their essence, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘Liberation is their essence, venerable sir.’ ‘But what do they plunge into, Samiddhi?’ When asked this, you replied, ‘Into the Deathless, venerable sir.’ Good, good, Samiddhi! It is good that you have answered each question correctly, but do not be conceited because of it.” The fourth.
