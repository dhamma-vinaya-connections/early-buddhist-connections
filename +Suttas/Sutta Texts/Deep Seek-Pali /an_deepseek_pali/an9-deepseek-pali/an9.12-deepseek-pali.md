# AN 9.12 Saupādisesasuttaṃ

## AN 9.12 The Discourse on One with Remainder.

12. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiṃ piṇḍāya pāvisi. Atha kho āyasmato sāriputtassa etadahosi – ‘‘atippago kho tāva sāvatthiyaṃ piṇḍāya carituṃ, yaṃnūnāhaṃ yena aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ ārāmo tenupasaṅkameyya’’nti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto yena aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ ārāmo tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā tehi aññatitthiyehi paribbājakehi saddhiṃ sammodi. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi.

12.At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. Then, in the morning, Venerable Sāriputta dressed, took his bowl and robe, and entered Savatthi for alms. Then it occurred to Venerable Sāriputta: “It is still too early to go for alms in Savatthi. Why don’t I go to the park of the wanderers of other sects?” Then Venerable Sāriputta approached the park of the wanderers of other sects. Having approached, he exchanged greetings with those wanderers of other sects. When they had exchanged courteous greetings and amiable talk, he sat down to one side.

Tena kho pana samayena tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ ayamantarākathā udapādi – ‘‘yo hi koci, āvuso, saupādiseso kālaṃ karoti, sabbo so aparimutto nirayā aparimutto tiracchānayoniyā aparimutto pettivisayā aparimutto apāyaduggativinipātā’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ bhāsitaṃ neva abhinandi nappaṭikkosi. Anabhinanditvā appaṭikkositvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi – ‘‘bhagavato santike etassa bhāsitassa atthaṃ ājānissāmī’’ti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto sāvatthiyaṃ piṇḍāya caritvā pacchābhattaṃ piṇḍapātapaṭikkanto yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho āyasmā sāriputto bhagavantaṃ etadavoca –

At that time, a discussion arose among the wanderers of other sects who were sitting together: “Friends, whoever dies with remainder is not liberated from hell, not liberated from the animal realm, not liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, not liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.” Then, Venerable Sāriputta neither approved nor rejected the statement of those wanderers of other sects. Without either approving or rejecting that statement, he rose from his seat and departed, thinking: “I shall learn the meaning of this statement in the presence of the Blessed One.” Then, Venerable Sāriputta, having walked for alms in Sāvatthī and returned from his alms round after his meal, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side. Having sat down to one side, Venerable Sāriputta said to the Blessed One:

‘‘Idhāhaṃ, bhante, pubbaṇhasamayaṃ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiṃ piṇḍāya pāvisiṃ. Tassa mayhaṃ, bhante, etadahosi – ‘atippago kho tāva sāvatthiyaṃ piṇḍāya carituṃ; yaṃnūnāhaṃ yena aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ ārāmo tenupasaṅkameyya’nti. Atha kho ahaṃ, bhante, yena aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ ārāmo tenupasaṅkamiṃ; upasaṅkamitvā tehi aññatitthiyehi paribbājakehi saddhiṃ sammodiṃ. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃ. Tena kho pana samayena tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ sannisinnānaṃ sannipatitānaṃ ayamantarākathā udapādi – ‘yo hi koci, āvuso, saupādiseso kālaṃ karoti, sabbo so aparimutto nirayā aparimutto tiracchānayoniyā aparimutto pettivisayā aparimutto apāyaduggativinipātā’ti. Atha kho ahaṃ, bhante, tesaṃ aññatitthiyānaṃ paribbājakānaṃ bhāsitaṃ neva abhinandiṃ nappaṭikkosiṃ. Anabhinanditvā appaṭikkositvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamiṃ – ‘bhagavato santike etassa bhāsitassa atthaṃ ājānissāmī’’’ti.

“Venerable sir, in the morning, having dressed, taken my bowl and robe, I entered Savatthi for alms. Then it occurred to me, venerable sir: ‘It is still very early to go for alms in Savatthi; why don’t I go to the park of the wanderers of other sects?’ So, venerable sir, I went to the park of the wanderers of other sects. Having approached, I exchanged greetings with those wanderers of other sects. When we had exchanged courteous greetings and amiable talk, I sat down to one side. At that time, as those wanderers of other sects were sitting together, this discussion arose among them: ‘Friend, whoever dies with remainder, all of them are not liberated from hell, not liberated from the animal realm, not liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, not liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.’ Then, venerable sir, I neither approved nor rejected the statement of those wanderers of other sects. Without either approving or rejecting that statement, I rose from my seat and departed, thinking: ‘I shall ascertain the meaning of this statement in the presence of the Blessed One.’”

‘‘Ke ca [keci (syā. pī.), te ca (ka.)], sāriputta, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā bālā abyattā, ke ca [keci (syā. pī. ka.) a. ni. 6.44 pāḷiyā saṃsandetabbaṃ] saupādisesaṃ vā ‘saupādiseso’ti jānissanti, anupādisesaṃ vā ‘anupādiseso’ti jānissanti’’!

“And who, Sāriputta, are those foolish and unskilled wanderers of other sects? And who will know one with remainder as ‘one with remainder,’ or one without remainder as ‘one without remainder’?”

‘‘Navayime, sāriputta, puggalā saupādisesā kālaṃ kurumānā parimuttā nirayā parimuttā tiracchānayoniyā parimuttā pettivisayā parimuttā apāyaduggativinipātā. Katame nava? Idha, sāriputta, ekacco puggalo sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti, samādhismiṃ paripūrakārī, paññāya mattaso kārī. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā antarāparinibbāyī hoti. Ayaṃ, sāriputta, paṭhamo puggalo saupādiseso kālaṃ kurumāno parimutto nirayā parimutto tiracchānayoniyā parimutto pettivisayā parimutto apāyaduggativinipātā.

“These nine persons, Sāriputta, with remainder, on passing away are liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms. Which nine? Here, Sāriputta, a certain person is one who perfectly fulfills virtue, perfectly fulfills samādhi, but practices wisdom only moderately. Through the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is an antarāparinibbāyī, one who attains final Nibbāna in the interval. This, Sāriputta, is the first person with remainder who, on passing away, is liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, sāriputta, idhekacco puggalo sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti, samādhismiṃ paripūrakārī, paññāya mattaso kārī. So pañcannaṃ orambhāgiyānaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā upahaccaparinibbāyī hoti…pe… asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti…pe… sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī hoti…pe… uddhaṃsoto hoti akaniṭṭhagāmī. Ayaṃ, sāriputta, pañcamo puggalo saupādiseso kālaṃ kurumāno parimutto nirayā parimutto tiracchānayoniyā parimutto pettivisayā parimutto apāyaduggativinipātā.

“Furthermore, Sāriputta, here a certain person is one who perfectly fulfills virtue, perfectly fulfills samādhi, but practices wisdom only moderately. Through the destruction of the five lower fetters, he is an upahaccaparinibbāyī, one who attains final Nibbāna by striking at the root; or an asaṅkhāraparinibbāyī, one who attains final Nibbāna without exertion; or a sasaṅkhāraparinibbāyī, one who attains final Nibbāna with exertion; or an uddhaṃsota, one who goes upstream to the Akaniṭṭha realm. This, Sāriputta, is the fifth person with remainder who, on passing away, is liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, sāriputta, idhekacco puggalo sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti, samādhismiṃ mattaso kārī, paññāya mattaso kārī. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā rāgadosamohānaṃ tanuttā sakadāgāmī hoti, sakideva imaṃ lokaṃ āgantvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Ayaṃ, sāriputta, chaṭṭho puggalo saupādiseso kālaṃ kurumāno parimutto nirayā…pe… parimutto apāyaduggativinipātā.

“Furthermore, Sāriputta, here a certain person is one who perfectly fulfills virtue, but practices samādhi and wisdom only moderately. Through the destruction of the three fetters and the attenuation of greed, hatred, and delusion, he is a once-returner, who, having come back to this world just one more time, makes an end of suffering. This, Sāriputta, is the sixth person with remainder who, on passing away, is liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, sāriputta, idhekacco puggalo sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti, samādhismiṃ mattaso kārī, paññāya mattaso kārī. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā ekabījī hoti, ekaṃyeva mānusakaṃ bhavaṃ nibbattetvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Ayaṃ, sāriputta, sattamo puggalo saupādiseso kālaṃ kurumāno parimutto nirayā…pe… parimutto apāyaduggativinipātā.

“Furthermore, Sāriputta, here a certain person is one who perfectly fulfills virtue, but practices samādhi and wisdom only moderately. With the destruction of the three fetters, he is an ekabījī, one who generates only one more human existence before making an end of suffering. This, Sāriputta, is the seventh person with remainder who, on passing away, is liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, sāriputta, idhekacco puggalo sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti, samādhismiṃ mattaso kārī, paññāya mattaso kārī. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā kolaṃkolo hoti, dve vā tīṇi vā kulāni sandhāvitvā saṃsaritvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Ayaṃ, sāriputta, aṭṭhamo puggalo saupādiseso kālaṃ kurumāno parimutto nirayā…pe… parimutto apāyaduggativinipātā.

“Furthermore, Sāriputta, here a certain person is one who perfectly fulfills virtue, but practices samādhi and wisdom only moderately. With the destruction of the three fetters, he is a kolaṅkola, one who, after transmigrating through two or three families, makes an end of suffering. This, Sāriputta, is the eighth person with remainder who, on passing away, is liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.”

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, sāriputta, idhekacco puggalo sīlesu paripūrakārī hoti, samādhismiṃ mattaso kārī, paññāya mattaso kārī. So tiṇṇaṃ saṃyojanānaṃ parikkhayā sattakkhattuparamo hoti, sattakkhattuparamaṃ deve ca manusse ca sandhāvitvā saṃsaritvā dukkhassantaṃ karoti. Ayaṃ, sāriputta, navamo puggalo saupādiseso kālaṃ kurumāno parimutto nirayā parimutto tiracchānayoniyā parimutto pettivisayā parimutto apāyaduggativinipātā.

“Furthermore, Sāriputta, here a certain person is one who perfectly fulfills virtue, but practices samādhi and wisdom only moderately. With the destruction of the three fetters, he is a sattakkhattuparama, one who, after transmigrating and wandering among devas and humans at most seven times, makes an end of suffering. This, Sāriputta, is the ninth person with remainder who, on passing away, is liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms.”

‘‘Ke ca, sāriputta, aññatitthiyā paribbājakā bālā abyattā, ke ca saupādisesaṃ vā ‘saupādiseso’ti jānissanti, anupādisesaṃ vā ‘anupādiseso’ti jānissanti! Ime kho, sāriputta, nava puggalā saupādisesā kālaṃ kurumānā parimuttā nirayā parimuttā tiracchānayoniyā parimuttā pettivisayā parimuttā apāyaduggativinipātā. Na tāvāyaṃ, sāriputta, dhammapariyāyo paṭibhāsi bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnaṃ upāsakānaṃ upāsikānaṃ. Taṃ kissa hetu? Māyimaṃ dhammapariyāyaṃ sutvā pamādaṃ āhariṃsūti [āhariṃsu (sī. pī.)]. Api ca mayā [api cāyaṃ (?)], sāriputta, dhammapariyāyo pañhādhippāyena bhāsito’’ti. Dutiyaṃ.

“Who, Sāriputta, are those foolish and unskilled wanderers of other sects who would know one with remainder as ‘one with remainder,’ or one without remainder as ‘one without remainder’? These, Sāriputta, are the nine persons with remainder who, on passing away, are liberated from hell, liberated from the animal realm, liberated from the realm of hungry ghosts, liberated from the states of deprivation, bad destinations, and the lower realms. This discourse, Sāriputta, was not revealed to monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen. For what reason? Lest, having heard this discourse, they should fall into negligence. However, Sāriputta, this discourse was spoken by me with a question in mind.” Second.
