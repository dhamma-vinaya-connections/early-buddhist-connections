# AN 9.37 Ānandasuttaṃ

## AN 9.37 To Ānanda

37. Ekaṃ samayaṃ āyasmā ānando kosambiyaṃ viharati ghositārāme. Tatra kho āyasmā ānando bhikkhū āmantesi – ‘‘āvuso bhikkhave’’ti. ‘‘Āvuso’’ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato ānandassa paccassosuṃ. Āyasmā ānando etadavoca –

37.At one time the Venerable Ānanda was dwelling at Kosambī in Ghosita’s Park. There the Venerable Ānanda addressed the bhikkhus: “Friends!” “Friend,” those bhikkhus replied to the Venerable Ānanda. The Venerable Ānanda said this:

‘‘Acchariyaṃ, āvuso, abbhutaṃ, āvuso! Yāvañcidaṃ tena bhagavatā jānatā passatā arahatā sammāsambuddhena sambādhe okāsādhigamo anubuddho sattānaṃ visuddhiyā sokaparidevānaṃ samatikkamāya dukkhadomanassānaṃ atthaṅgamāya ñāyassa adhigamāya nibbānassa sacchikiriyāya. Tadeva nāma cakkhuṃ bhavissati te rūpā tañcāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedissati [paṭisaṃvedayati (ka.)]. Tadeva nāma sotaṃ bhavissati te saddā tañcāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedissati. Tadeva nāma ghānaṃ bhavissati te gandhā tañcāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedissati. Sāva nāma jivhā bhavissati te rasā tañcāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedissati. Sova nāma kāyo bhavissati te phoṭṭhabbā tañcāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedissatī’’ti.

“It is wonderful, friends, it is marvelous, how that Blessed One who knows and sees, the Arahant, the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, has discovered an access in confinement for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow and lamentation, for the passing away of pain and displeasure, for the attainment of the right method, for the realization of Nibbāna. That very eye will exist, and those forms, yet one will not experience that sense base. That very ear will exist, and those sounds, yet one will not experience that sense base. That very nose will exist, and those odors, yet one will not experience that sense base. That very tongue will exist, and those tastes, yet one will not experience that sense base. That very body will exist, and those tactile objects, yet one will not experience that sense base.”

Evaṃ vutte āyasmā udāyī āyasmantaṃ ānandaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘saññīmeva nu kho, āvuso ānanda, tadāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedeti udāhu asaññī’’ti? ‘‘Saññīmeva kho, āvuso, tadāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedeti, no asaññī’’ti.

When this was said, the Venerable Udāyī said to the Venerable Ānanda: “But, friend Ānanda, is one percipient when one does not experience that sense base, or is one non-percipient?” “Friend, one is percipient when one does not experience that sense base, not non-percipient.”

‘‘Kiṃsaññī panāvuso, tadāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedetī’’ti? ‘‘Idhāvuso, bhikkhu, sabbaso rūpasaññānaṃ samatikkamā paṭighasaññānaṃ atthaṅgamā nānattasaññānaṃ amanasikārā ‘ananto ākāso’ti ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati. Evaṃsaññīpi kho, āvuso, tadāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedeti.

“Perceiving what, friend, does one not experience that sense base?” “Here, friend, a bhikkhu, with the complete surmounting of perceptions of form, with the disappearance of perceptions of resistance, not attending to perceptions of diversity, thinking, ‘Space is infinite,’ enters and dwells in the base of the infinity of space. Perceiving thus, friend, one does not experience that sense base.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, bhikkhu sabbaso ākāsānañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘anantaṃ viññāṇa’nti viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati. Evaṃsaññīpi kho, āvuso, tadāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedeti.

“Furthermore, friend, a bhikkhu, by completely surmounting the base of the infinity of space, aware that ‘consciousness is infinite,’ enters and dwells in the base of the infinity of consciousness. Perceiving thus too, friend, one does not experience that sense base.

‘‘Puna caparaṃ, āvuso, bhikkhu sabbaso viññāṇañcāyatanaṃ samatikkamma ‘natthi kiñcī’ti ākiñcaññāyatanaṃ upasampajja viharati. Evaṃsaññīpi kho, āvuso, tadāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedetī’’ti.

“Furthermore, friend, a bhikkhu, by completely surmounting the base of the infinity of consciousness, aware that ‘there is nothing,’ enters and dwells in the base of nothingness. Perceiving thus too, friend, one does not experience that sense base.”

‘‘Ekamidāhaṃ, āvuso, samayaṃ sākete viharāmi añjanavane migadāye. Atha kho, āvuso, jaṭilavāsikā [jaṭilagāhiyā (sī. pī.), jaḍilabhāgikā (syā.)] bhikkhunī yenāhaṃ tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā maṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho, āvuso, jaṭilavāsikā bhikkhunī maṃ etadavoca – ‘yāyaṃ, bhante ānanda, samādhi na cābhinato na cāpanato na ca sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritagato [sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritavato (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.), sasaṅkhāraniggayhavārivāvaṭo (ka.) a. ni. 3.102; 5.27; dī. ni. 3.355], vimuttattā ṭhito, ṭhitattā santusito, santusitattā no paritassati. Ayaṃ, bhante ānanda, samādhi kiṃphalo vutto bhagavatā’’’ti?

“Once, friend, I was dwelling at Sāketa in the Añjana Grove, the deer park. Then, friend, the bhikkhunī Jaṭilavāsikā approached me, paid homage to me, and stood to one side. Standing to one side, the bhikkhunī Jaṭilavāsikā said to me: ‘Venerable sir Ānanda, for what fruit has this samādhi been declared by the Blessed One: the samādhi that is not directed forward nor directed backward, that is not held in place by forceful suppression; that is steady because it is liberated, content because it is steady, and unagitated because it is content?’”

‘‘Evaṃ vutte, sohaṃ, āvuso, jaṭilavāsikaṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavocaṃ – ‘yāyaṃ, bhagini, samādhi na cābhinato na cāpanato na ca sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritagato, vimuttattā ṭhito, ṭhitattā santusito, santusitattā no paritassati. Ayaṃ, bhagini, samādhi aññāphalo vutto bhagavatā’ti. Evaṃsaññīpi kho, āvuso, tadāyatanaṃ no paṭisaṃvedetī’’ti. Chaṭṭhaṃ.

“When this was said, friend, I said to the bhikkhunī Jaṭilavāsikā: ‘Sister, this samādhi—which is not directed forward nor directed backward, which is not held in place by forceful suppression; which is steady because it is liberated, content because it is steady, and unagitated because it is content—this samādhi, sister, has been declared by the Blessed One to have final knowledge as its fruit.’ Perceiving thus too, friend, one does not experience that sense base.” Sixth.
