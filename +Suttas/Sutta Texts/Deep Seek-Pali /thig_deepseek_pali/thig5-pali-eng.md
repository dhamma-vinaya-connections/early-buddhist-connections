# Thig 5. Pañcakanipāto
## 5. The Chapter of Fives

5.The Book of Fives
### 1. Aññatarātherīgāthā
1.Verses of a Certain Elder Nun
67.
‘‘Paṇṇavīsativassāni, yato pabbajitā ahaṃ;Nāccharāsaṅghātamattampi, cittassūpasamajjhagaṃ.

"Twenty-five years have passed since I went forth; Not even the snap of the fingers have I attained the stilling of the mind."

68.
‘‘Aladdhā cetaso santiṃ, kāmarāgenavassutā;Bāhā paggayha kandantī, vihāraṃ pāvisiṃ ahaṃ.

"Not having attained peace of mind, swept away by lust for sensual pleasures, with arms outstretched, weeping, I entered the monastery."

69.
‘‘Sā bhikkhuniṃ upāgacchiṃ, yā me saddhāyikā ahu;Sā me dhammamadesesi, khandhāyatanadhātuyo.

"I approached the nun who was my confidante; She taught me the Dhamma: aggregates, sense bases, and elements."

70.
‘‘Tassā dhammaṃ suṇitvāna, ekamante upāvisiṃ;Pubbenivāsaṃ jānāmi, dibbacakkhu visodhitaṃ.

"Having heard her teaching, I sat down to one side. I know my past abodes, and the divine eye is purified."

71.
‘‘Cetopariccañāṇañca [cetopariyañāṇañca (ka.)], sotadhātu visodhitā;Iddhīpi me sacchikatā, patto me āsavakkhayo;Chaḷabhiññā [cha mebhiññā (syā. ka.)] sacchikatā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsana’’nti.

"Knowledge of the minds of others is mine, and the element of the ear is purified. Psychic powers are realized by me, and the destruction of the taints is attained by me. The sixfold higher knowledges are realized, the Buddha’s dispensation is accomplished." … Aññatarā therī …. ... A certain elder nun ...

### 2. Vimalātherīgāthā
2.The Verses of the Elder Nun Vimalā
72.
‘‘Mattā vaṇṇena rūpena, sobhaggena yasena ca;Yobbanena cupatthaddhā, aññāsamatimaññihaṃ.

"Intoxicated by my beauty, complexion, and form, my charm and renown, and emboldened by youthful vigor, I scorned others, deeming them inferior."

73.
‘‘Vibhūsetvā imaṃ kāyaṃ, sucittaṃ bālalāpanaṃ;Aṭṭhāsiṃ vesidvāramhi, luddo pāsamivoḍḍiya.

Adorning this body, beautifully adorned, a fool's enticement, I stood at the prostitute's door, like a hunter spreading a snare.

74.
‘‘Pilandhanaṃ vidaṃsentī, guyhaṃ pakāsikaṃ bahuṃ;Akāsiṃ vividhaṃ māyaṃ, ujjagghantī bahuṃ janaṃ.

"Displaying my ornaments, revealing much that was hidden, I practiced various deceptions, mocking many people."

75.
‘‘Sājja piṇḍaṃ caritvāna, muṇḍā saṅghāṭipārutā;Nisinnā rukkhamūlamhi, avitakkassa lābhinī.

Today, having gone for alms, shaven-headed, clad in the sanghati, seated at the foot of a tree, I have gained the unthinking state.

76.
‘‘Sabbe yogā samucchinnā, ye dibbā ye ca mānusā;Khepetvā āsave sabbe, sītibhūtāmhi nibbutā’’ti.

"All bonds are cut, those of gods and humans; Having destroyed all taints, cooled am I, extinguished." … Vimalā purāṇagaṇikā therī…. ...The Elder Nun Vimalā, the former courtesan...

### 3. Sīhātherīgāthā
3.The Verses of the Elder Nun Sīhā
77.
‘‘Ayoniso manasikārā, kāmarāgena aṭṭitā;Ahosiṃ uddhatā pubbe, citte avasavattinī.

Through unwise attention, afflicted by sensual lust, formerly I was restless, my mind not under my control.

78.
‘‘Pariyuṭṭhitā klesehi, subhasaññānuvattinī;Samaṃ cittassa na labhiṃ, rāgacittavasānugā.

Overwhelmed by defilements, pursuing perceptions of beauty, I could not achieve serenity of mind, following the dictates of a mind obsessed with lust.

79.
‘‘Kisā paṇḍu vivaṇṇā ca, satta vassāni cārihaṃ;Nāhaṃ divā vā rattiṃ vā, sukhaṃ vindiṃ sudukkhitā.

Emaciated, pale, and discolored, I wandered for seven years; neither by day nor by night did I find happiness, utterly miserable.

80.
‘‘Tato rajjuṃ gahetvāna, pāvisiṃ vanamantaraṃ;Varaṃ me idha ubbandhaṃ, yañca hīnaṃ punācare.

Then, taking a rope, I entered the forest; better for me to hang myself here than to return to that degraded life.

81.
‘‘Daḷhapāsaṃ [daḷhaṃ pāsaṃ (sī.)] karitvāna, rukkhasākhāya bandhiya;Pakkhipiṃ pāsaṃ gīvāyaṃ, atha cittaṃ vimucci me’’ti.

Having made a strong noose and tied it to a tree branch, I threw the noose around my neck; then my mind was liberated. … Sīhā therī…. ...The Elder Nun Sīhā...

### 4. Sundarīnandātherīgāthā
4.The Verses of the Elder Nun Sundarīnandā
82.
‘‘Āturaṃ asuciṃ pūtiṃ, passa nande samussayaṃ;Asubhāya cittaṃ bhāvehi, ekaggaṃ susamāhitaṃ. Nandā, see this diseased, impure, putrid accumulation; develop your mind in the unattractive, one-pointed and well-composed.


83.
‘‘Yathā idaṃ tathā etaṃ, yathā etaṃ tathā idaṃ;Duggandhaṃ pūtikaṃ vāti, bālānaṃ abhinanditaṃ.

As this is, so is that; as that is, so is this. It emits a foul and putrid stench, yet fools delight in it.

84.
‘‘Evametaṃ avekkhantī, rattindivamatanditā;Tato sakāya paññāya, abhinibbijjha [abhinibbijja (sī. syā.)] dakkhisaṃ.

Observing thus, tirelessly day and night, then by my own wisdom I shall thoroughly penetrate and see.

85.
‘‘Tassā me appamattāya, vicinantiyā yoniso;Yathābhūtaṃ ayaṃ kāyo, diṭṭho santarabāhiro.

Being heedful, examining carefully, I saw this body as it actually is, inside and out.

86.
‘‘Atha nibbindahaṃ kāye, ajjhattañca virajjahaṃ;Appamattā visaṃyuttā, upasantāmhi nibbutā’’ti.

Then I grew disenchanted with the body, and detached from what is within; heedful, disjoined, at peace, extinguished. … Sundarīnandā therī…. ...The Elder Nun Sundarīnandā...

### 5. Nanduttarātherīgāthā
5.The Verses of the Elder Nun Nanduttarā
87.
‘‘Aggiṃ candañca sūriyañca, devatā ca namassihaṃ;Nadītitthāni gantvāna, udakaṃ oruhāmihaṃ.

I paid homage to fire, the moon, and the sun, and to the deities. Having gone to the river fords, I entered the water.

88.
‘‘Bahūvatasamādānā, aḍḍhaṃ sīsassa olikhiṃ;Chamāya seyyaṃ kappemi, rattiṃ bhattaṃ na bhuñjahaṃ.

Taking on many observances, I shaved off half my head; I prepared my bed on the ground, I did not eat food at night.

89.
‘‘Vibhūsāmaṇḍanaratā, nhāpanucchādanehi ca;Upakāsiṃ imaṃ kāyaṃ, kāmarāgena aṭṭitā.

Fond of adornments and decorations, bathing and anointing, I attended to this body, tormented by sensual lust.

90.
‘‘Tato saddhaṃ labhitvāna, pabbajiṃ anagāriyaṃ;Disvā kāyaṃ yathābhūtaṃ, kāmarāgo samūhato.

Then, having gained faith, I went forth into homelessness; seeing the body as it actually is, sensual lust was eradicated.

91.
‘‘Sabbe bhavā samucchinnā, icchā ca patthanāpi ca;Sabbayogavisaṃyuttā, santiṃ pāpuṇi cetaso’’ti.

All becomings are cut off, and also craving and yearning; disjoined from all bonds, the mind has attained peace. … Nanduttarā therī…. ...The Elder Nun Nanduttarā...

### 6. Mittākāḷītherīgāthā
6.The Verses of the Elder Nun Mittākāḷī
92.
‘‘Saddhāya pabbajitvāna, agārasmānagāriyaṃ;Vicariṃhaṃ tena tena, lābhasakkāraussukā.

Having gone forth in faith from home to homelessness, I wandered here and there, keen on gain and esteem.

93.
‘‘Riñcitvā paramaṃ atthaṃ, hīnamatthaṃ asevihaṃ;Kilesānaṃ vasaṃ gantvā, sāmaññatthaṃ na bujjhihaṃ.

Having abandoned the supreme goal, I pursued the inferior; under the sway of defilements, I did not understand the purpose of the ascetic life.

94.
‘‘Tassā me ahu saṃvego, nisinnāya vihārake;Ummaggapaṭipannāmhi, taṇhāya vasamāgatā.

Then, as I sat in my dwelling, agitation arose in me: I had set out on the wrong path, come under craving’s control.

95.
‘‘Appakaṃ jīvitaṃ mayhaṃ, jarā byādhi ca maddati;Purāyaṃ bhijjati [jarāya bhijjate (sī.)] kāyo, na me kālo pamajjituṃ.

My life is short, and old age and sickness crush me; this body will soon break up—now is no time for heedlessness.

96.
‘‘Yathābhūtamavekkhantī, khandhānaṃ udayabbayaṃ;Vimuttacittā uṭṭhāsiṃ, kataṃ buddhassa sāsana’’nti.

Seeing the rise and fall of the aggregates as they actually are, with a mind liberated, I arose; the Buddha's teaching has been fulfilled. … Mittā kāḷī therī…. ...The Elder Nun Mittākāḷī...

### 7. Sakulātherīgāthā
7.The Verses of the Elder Nun Sakulā
97.
‘‘Agārasmiṃ vasantīhaṃ, dhammaṃ sutvāna bhikkhuno;Addasaṃ virajaṃ dhammaṃ, nibbānaṃ padamaccutaṃ. Dwelling in the household, I heard the Dhamma from a monk; I saw the stainless Dhamma, Nibbāna, the imperishable state.


98.
‘‘Sāhaṃ puttaṃ dhītarañca, dhanadhaññañca chaḍḍiya;Kese chedāpayitvāna, pabbajiṃ anagāriyaṃ.

I abandoned son and daughter, wealth and grain, cut off my hair, and went forth into homelessness.

99.
‘‘Sikkhamānā ahaṃ santī, bhāventī maggamañjasaṃ;Pahāsiṃ rāgadosañca, tadekaṭṭhe ca āsave.

While training, being peaceful, I cultivated the direct path; I abandoned lust and hatred, and the taints rooted therein as well.

100.
‘‘Bhikkhunī upasampajja, pubbajātimanussariṃ;Dibbacakkhu visodhitaṃ [visodhitaṃ dibbacakkhu (sī.)], vimalaṃ sādhubhāvitaṃ.

Having received the full ordination as a nun, I recollected my past lives; the divine eye was purified, spotless, and well-developed.

101.
‘‘Saṅkhāre parato disvā, hetujāte palokite [palokine (ka.)];Pahāsiṃ āsave sabbe, sītibhūtāmhi nibbutā’’ti.

Seeing formations as other, viewed as cause-born and disintegrating, I abandoned all taints, become cooled, extinguished. … Sakulā therī…. ...The Elder Nun Sakulā...

### 8. Soṇātherīgāthā
8.The Verses of the Elder Nun Soṇā
102.
‘‘Dasa putte vijāyitvā, asmiṃ rūpasamussaye;Tatohaṃ dubbalā jiṇṇā, bhikkhuniṃ upasaṅkamiṃ.

Having borne ten sons within this frame, then, weak and aged, I approached a bhikkhuni.

103.
‘‘Sā me dhammamadesesi, khandhāyatanadhātuyo;Tassā dhammaṃ suṇitvāna, kese chetvāna pabbajiṃ.

She taught me the Dhamma—the aggregates, sense bases, and elements. Hearing her teaching, I cut off my hair and went forth.

104.
‘‘Tassā me sikkhamānāya, dibbacakkhu visodhitaṃ;Pubbenivāsaṃ jānāmi, yattha me vusitaṃ pure.

While I was training, the divine eye was purified; I knew my past abodes, where I had lived before.

105.
‘‘Animittañca bhāvemi, ekaggā susamāhitā;Anantarāvimokkhāsiṃ, anupādāya nibbutā.

I developed the signless, single-pointed and well-collected; I attained immediate liberation, unattached and extinguished.

106.
‘‘Pañcakkhandhā pariññātā, tiṭṭhanti chinnamūlakā;Dhi tavatthu jare jamme, natthi dāni punabbhavo’’ti.

The five aggregates are fully comprehended, they stand with roots cut off; shame on you, aging and decay! Now there is no renewed existence. … Soṇā therī…. ...The Elder Nun Soṇā...

### 9. Bhaddākuṇḍalakesātherīgāthā
9.The Verses of the Elder Nun Bhaddā Kuṇḍalakesā
107.
‘‘Lūnakesī paṅkadharī, ekasāṭī pure cariṃ;Avajje vajjamatinī, vajje cāvajjadassinī.

With shorn hair, smeared with mud, I wandered alone in a single robe; deeming the faultless faulty, and the faulty faultless.

108.
‘‘Divāvihārā nikkhamma, gijjhakūṭamhi pabbate;Addasaṃ virajaṃ buddhaṃ, bhikkhusaṅghapurakkhataṃ. Emerging from my day-rest on Vulture Peak Mountain, I saw the stainless Buddha, attended by the Saṅgha of bhikkhus.


109.
‘‘Nihacca jāṇuṃ vanditvā, sammukhā añjaliṃ akaṃ;‘Ehi bhadde’ti maṃ avaca, sā me āsūpasampadā. Having bent my knee and paid homage, I raised joined palms before him; ‘Come, Bhaddā,’ he said to me, and that was my full ordination.


110.
‘‘Ciṇṇā aṅgā ca magadhā, vajjī kāsī ca kosalā;Anaṇā paṇṇāsavassāni, raṭṭhapiṇḍaṃ abhuñjahaṃ. I traveled through Aṅga and Magadha, Vajjī, Kāsī, and Kosala; free from debt for fifty years, I enjoyed the country’s sustenance.


111.
‘‘Puññaṃ vata pasavi bahuṃ, sappañño vatāyaṃ upāsako;Yo bhaddāya cīvaraṃ adāsi, vippamuttāya sabbaganthehī’’ti. Truly much merit was made; wise indeed is this lay follower who gave robes to Bhaddā, fully freed from all bonds. … Bhaddā kuṇḍalakesā therī…. ...The Elder Nun Bhaddā Kuṇḍalakesā...


### 10. Paṭācārātherīgāthā
10.The Verses of Paṭācārā Therī
112.
‘‘Naṅgalehi kasaṃ khettaṃ, bījāni pavapaṃ chamā;Puttadārāni posentā, dhanaṃ vindanti māṇavā.

With plows they plow the field, sowing seeds upon the ground; supporting children and wives, young men acquire wealth.

113.
‘‘Kimahaṃ sīlasampannā, satthusāsanakārikā;Nibbānaṃ nādhigacchāmi, akusītā anuddhatā. Why, though virtuous and heedful of the Teacher’s instruction, do I not attain Nibbāna, being diligent and undisturbed?


114.
‘‘Pāde pakkhālayitvāna, udakesu karomahaṃ;Pādodakañca disvāna, thalato ninnamāgataṃ.

Having washed my feet, I poured water over them; seeing the foot-water flow down from high ground to low,

115.
‘‘Tato cittaṃ samādhesiṃ, assaṃ bhadraṃvajāniyaṃ;Tato dīpaṃ gahetvāna, vihāraṃ pāvisiṃ ahaṃ;Seyyaṃ olokayitvāna, mañcakamhi upāvisiṃ.

Then I steadied my mind, like a well-trained thoroughbred; taking a lamp, I entered the monastery. Having looked at the bed, I sat down on the couch.

116.
‘‘Tato sūciṃ gahetvāna, vaṭṭiṃ okassayāmahaṃ;Padīpasseva nibbānaṃ, vimokkho ahu cetaso’’ti.

Then, taking a needle, I drew out the wick. Like the extinguishing of a lamp, liberation was my mind’s. … Paṭācārā therī…. ...Paṭācārā Therī...

### 11. Tiṃsamattātherīgāthā
11.The Verses of the Thirty Therīs
117.
‘‘‘Musalāni gahetvāna, dhaññaṃ koṭṭenti māṇavā [mānavā (sī.)];Puttadārāni posentā, dhanaṃ vindanti māṇavā.

Taking pestles, young men pound the grain; supporting wives and children, they acquire wealth.

118.
‘‘‘Karotha buddhasāsanaṃ, yaṃ katvā nānutappati;Khippaṃ pādāni dhovitvā, ekamante nisīdatha;Cetosamathamanuyuttā, karotha buddhasāsanaṃ’.

'Practice the Buddha’s teaching, which, having done, one does not regret. Quickly wash your feet and sit down to one side; engaged in the calm of mind, practice the Buddha’s teaching.'

119.
‘‘Tassā tā [taṃ (sī.)] vacanaṃ sutvā, paṭācārāya sāsanaṃ;Pāde pakkhālayitvāna, ekamantaṃ upāvisuṃ;Cetosamathamanuyuttā, akaṃsu buddhasāsanaṃ. Hearing her words, the instruction of Paṭācārā, they washed their feet and sat down to one side. Engaged in the calm of mind, they practiced the Buddha’s teaching.


120.
‘‘Rattiyā purime yāme, pubbajātimanussaruṃ;Rattiyā majjhime yāme, dibbacakkhuṃ visodhayuṃ;Rattiyā pacchime yāme, tamokhandhaṃ padālayuṃ.

In the first watch of the night, they recollected their past lives. In the middle watch of the night, they purified the divine eye. In the last watch of the night, they shattered the mass of darkness.

121.
‘‘Uṭṭhāya pāde vandiṃsu, ‘katā te anusāsanī;Indaṃva devā tidasā, saṅgāme aparājitaṃ;Purakkhatvā vihassāma [viharāma (sī.), viharissāma (syā.)], tevijjāmha anāsavā’’’ti. Rising, they paid homage at her feet, saying: ‘Your instruction has been carried out. As the gods of Tāvatiṃsa honor Indra, the unconquered in battle, we will revere you. We are those with threefold knowledge, free from taints.’ Itthaṃ sudaṃ tiṃsamattā therī bhikkhuniyo paṭācārāya santike aññaṃ byākariṃsūti. Thus it is said that about thirty elder nuns declared their final knowledge in the presence of Paṭācārā.


### 12. Candātherīgāthā
12.The Verses of Candā Therī
122.
‘‘Duggatāhaṃ pure āsiṃ, vidhavā ca aputtikā;Vinā mittehi ñātīhi, bhattacoḷassa nādhigaṃ.

In the past, I was destitute, a widow without children, without friends or relatives, and I could not even obtain food or clothing.

123.
‘‘Pattaṃ daṇḍañca gaṇhitvā, bhikkhamānā kulā kulaṃ;Sītuṇhena ca ḍayhantī, satta vassāni cārihaṃ.

Taking bowl and staff, begging from family to family, suffering from cold and heat, I wandered for seven years.

124.
‘‘Bhikkhuniṃ puna disvāna, annapānassa lābhiniṃ;Upasaṅkamma avocaṃ [avociṃ (ka.)], ‘pabbajjaṃ anagāriyaṃ’. Then, seeing a bhikkhunī who had received food and drink, I approached her and said, ‘Let me go forth into homelessness.’


125.
‘‘Sā ca maṃ anukampāya, pabbājesi paṭācārā;Tato maṃ ovaditvāna, paramatthe niyojayi. Out of compassion, Paṭācārā gave me the going forth; then, advising me, she directed me to the highest goal.


126.
‘‘Tassāhaṃ vacanaṃ sutvā, akāsiṃ anusāsaniṃ;Amogho ayyāyovādo, tevijjāmhi anāsavā’’ti.

Hearing her words, I acted on her instruction. The noble lady’s advice was not in vain; I am one with the triple knowledge, free from taints. … Candā therī…. ...Candā Therī... Pañcakanipāto niṭṭhito. The Fifth Nipāta is completed.
