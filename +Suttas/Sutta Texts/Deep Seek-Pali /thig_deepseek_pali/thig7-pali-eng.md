# Thig 7. Sattakanipāto
## 7. The Chapter of Sevens

7.The Chapter of Sevens
### 1. Uttarātherīgāthā
1.Verses of the Elder Uttarā
175.
‘‘‘Musalāni gahetvāna, dhaññaṃ koṭṭenti māṇavā;Puttadārāni posentā, dhanaṃ vindanti māṇavā.

‘Taking pestles, young men pound grain; supporting their wives and children, young men win wealth.’

176.
‘‘‘Ghaṭetha buddhasāsane, yaṃ katvā nānutappati;Khippaṃ pādāni dhovitvā, ekamantaṃ nisīdatha.

‘Strive in the Buddha’s Dispensation, having done what one does not regret. Quickly wash your feet and sit down to one side.’

177.
‘‘‘Cittaṃ upaṭṭhapetvāna, ekaggaṃ susamāhitaṃ;Paccavekkhatha saṅkhāre, parato no ca attato’.

‘Having established the mind, one-pointed and well-collected, contemplate conditioned phenomena as other, not as self.’

178.
‘‘Tassāhaṃ vacanaṃ sutvā, paṭācārānusāsaniṃ;Pāde pakkhālayitvāna, ekamante upāvisiṃ. Hearing her words, Paṭācārā’s instruction, I washed my feet and sat down to one side.


179.
‘‘Rattiyā purime yāme, pubbajātimanussariṃ;Rattiyā majjhime yāme, dibbacakkhuṃ visodhayiṃ.

In the first watch of the night, I recollected former existences; in the middle watch of the night, I purified the divine eye.

180.
‘‘Rattiyā pacchime yāme, tamokkhandhaṃ padālayiṃ;Tevijjā atha vuṭṭhāsiṃ, katā te anusāsanī.

In the last watch of the night, I shattered the mass of darkness; then I arose, possessing the three knowledges, her instruction fulfilled.

181.
‘‘Sakkaṃva devā tidasā, saṅgāme aparājitaṃ;Purakkhatvā vihassāmi, tevijjāmhi anāsavā’’. Like Sakka, whom the Tāvatiṃsa gods, unconquered in battle, make their leader, I shall dwell, having made (him) my guide, possessing the three knowledges, free from effluents. … Uttarā therī…. ... Therī Uttarā ...


### 2. Cālātherīgāthā
2.The Verses of Cālā Therī
182.
‘‘Satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvāna, bhikkhunī bhāvitindriyā;Paṭivijjhi padaṃ santaṃ, saṅkhārūpasamaṃ sukhaṃ’’. “Having established mindfulness, a bhikkhunī with faculties developed penetrated the peaceful state, the bliss of the stilling of formations.”


183.
‘‘Kaṃ nu uddissa muṇḍāsi, samaṇī viya dissasi;Na ca rocesi pāsaṇḍe, kimidaṃ carasi momuhā’’.

“For whom have you shaved your head? You appear like an ascetic, yet you do not approve of sects—why do you wander thus, O dull one?”

184.
‘‘Ito bahiddhā pāsaṇḍā, diṭṭhiyo upanissitā;Na te dhammaṃ vijānanti, na te dhammassa kovidā.

“Those sectarians outside, rooted in their views, do not understand the Dhamma, nor are they skilled in the Dhamma.”

185.
‘‘Atthi sakyakule jāto, buddho appaṭipuggalo;So me dhammamadesesi, diṭṭhīnaṃ samatikkamaṃ.

“Born in the Sakyan clan, the Buddha, an unequalled person, taught me the Dhamma, the transcending of views.”

186.
‘‘Dukkhaṃ dukkhasamuppādaṃ, dukkhassa ca atikkamaṃ;Ariyaṃ caṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ, dukkhūpasamagāminaṃ.

“Suffering, the origin of suffering, the transcending of suffering, and the Noble Eightfold Path leading to the stilling of suffering.”

187.
‘‘Tassāhaṃ vacanaṃ sutvā, vihariṃ sāsane ratā;Tisso vijjā anuppattā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsanaṃ.

“Hearing her words, I dwelt delighting in the Dispensation. The three knowledges have been attained; the Buddha’s Dispensation has been accomplished.”

188.
‘‘Sabbattha vihatā nandī, tamokhandho padālito;Evaṃ jānāhi pāpima, nihato tvamasi antaka’’.

“Everywhere delight is destroyed, the mass of darkness is shattered. Know this, O Evil One: you are defeated, O End-maker.” … Cālā therī…. … Cālā Therī …

### 3. Upacālātherīgāthā
3.The Verses of Upacālā Therī
189.
‘‘Satimatī cakkhumatī, bhikkhunī bhāvitindriyā;Paṭivijjhiṃ padaṃ santaṃ, akāpurisasevitaṃ’’. “Mindful, clear-eyed, a bhikkhunī with faculties developed, I penetrated the peaceful state, unvisited by the ignoble.”


190.
‘‘Kiṃ nu jātiṃ na rocesi, jāto kāmāni bhuñjati;Bhuñjāhi kāmaratiyo, māhu pacchānutāpinī’’.

“Why do you not delight in birth? Having been born, one enjoys sensual pleasures. Indulge in sensual delights, lest you regret it later.”

191.
‘‘Jātassa maraṇaṃ hoti, hatthapādāna chedanaṃ;Vadhabandhapariklesaṃ, jāto dukkhaṃ nigacchati.

“For one born, death is certain, limbs are cut off; execution, imprisonment, and torment—the born encounter suffering.”

192.
‘‘Atthi sakyakule jāto, sambuddho aparājito;So me dhammamadesesi, jātiyā samatikkamaṃ.

“Born in the Sakyan clan, the Unconquered Buddha arose; he taught me the Dhamma, the transcending of birth.”

193.
‘‘Dukkhaṃ dukkhasamuppādaṃ, dukkhassa ca atikkamaṃ;Ariyaṃ caṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ, dukkhūpasamagāminaṃ.

“Suffering, the origin of suffering, the transcending of suffering, and the Noble Eightfold Path leading to the stilling of suffering.”

194.
‘‘Tassāhaṃ vacanaṃ sutvā, vihariṃ sāsane ratā;Tisso vijjā anuppattā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsanaṃ.

“Hearing her words, I dwelt delighting in the Dispensation. The three knowledges have been attained; the Buddha’s Dispensation has been accomplished.”

195.
‘‘Sabbattha vihatā nandī, tamokhandho padālito;Evaṃ jānāhi pāpima, nihato tvamasi antaka’’.

“Everywhere delight is destroyed, the mass of darkness is shattered. Know this, O Evil One: you are defeated, O End-maker.” … Upacālā therī…. … Upacālā Therī … Sattakanipāto niṭṭhito. The Chapter of Sevens is finished.
