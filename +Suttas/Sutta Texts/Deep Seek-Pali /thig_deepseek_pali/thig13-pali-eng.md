# Thig 13. Vīsatinipāto
## 13. The Chapter of Thirteens

13.The Chapter of the Twenty
### 1. Ambapālītherīgāthā
1.The Verses of the Elder Nun Ambapālī
252.
‘‘Kāḷakā bhamaravaṇṇasādisā, vellitaggā mama muddhajā ahuṃ;Te jarāya sāṇavākasādisā, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

My hair was black, the color of bees, with curled ends. Now, with old age, they are like coarse hemp. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

253.
‘‘Vāsitova surabhī karaṇḍako, pupphapūra mama uttamaṅgajo [uttamaṅgabhūto (ka.)].

My hair, like a perfumed box, was filled with flowers. Taṃ jarāyatha salomagandhikaṃ, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā. Now, with old age, it smells like a dog. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

254.
‘‘Kānanaṃva sahitaṃ suropitaṃ, kocchasūcivicitaggasobhitaṃ;Taṃ jarāya viralaṃ tahiṃ tahiṃ, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Like a grove, well-planted and cultivated, adorned with fine points of grass, now sparse here and there with age. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

255.
‘‘Kaṇhakhandhakasuvaṇṇamaṇḍitaṃ, sobhate suveṇīhilaṅkataṃ;Taṃ jarāya khalitaṃ siraṃ kataṃ, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Like a golden ornament on a dark ground, shining, adorned with beautiful braids, that head is now bald and worn with age. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

256.
‘‘Cittakārasukatāva lekhikā, sobhare su bhamukā pure mama;Tā jarāya valibhippalambitā, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Like a well-drawn painting, once my eyebrows were beautiful. Now, with old age, they are drooping with wrinkles. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

257.
‘‘Bhassarā surucirā yathā maṇī, nettahesumabhinīlamāyatā;Te jarāyabhihatā na sobhare, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Radiant and very lovely as a gem, my eyes were deep blue and long. Now, struck by age, they are no longer beautiful. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

258.
‘‘Saṇhatuṅgasadisī ca nāsikā, sobhate su abhiyobbanaṃ pati;Sā jarāya upakūlitā viya, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

My nose, like a smooth, high peak, was beautiful, suited to youth. Now, with old age, it droops as if blighted. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

259.
‘‘Kaṅkaṇaṃ va sukataṃ suniṭṭhitaṃ, sobhare su mama kaṇṇapāḷiyo;Tā jarāya valibhippalambitā, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Like well-made, finely finished bracelets, my earlobes were once beautiful. Now, with old age, they are drooping with wrinkles. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

260.
‘‘Pattalīmakulavaṇṇasādisā, sobhare su dantā pure mama;Te jarāya khaṇḍitā cāsitā [pītakā (sī.)], saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Once my teeth were beautiful, resembling lotus buds. Now, with old age, they are broken and blackened. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

261.
‘‘Kānanamhi vanasaṇḍacārinī, kokilāva madhuraṃ nikūjihaṃ;Taṃ jarāya khalitaṃ tahiṃ tahiṃ, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Like a cuckoo wandering in a forest grove, once I sweetly sang and cooed. Now, with old age, it is cracked and faltering here and there. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

262.
‘‘Saṇhakamburiva suppamajjitā, sobhate su gīvā pure mama;Sā jarāya bhaggā [bhañjitā (?)] vināmitā, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Like a smooth, well-polished conch shell, once my neck was beautiful. Now, with old age, it is broken and bent. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

263.
‘‘Vaṭṭapalighasadisopamā ubho, sobhare su bāhā pure mama;Tā jarāya yatha pāṭalibbalitā [yathā pāṭalippalitā (sī. syā. ka.)], saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Both like rounded crossbars, my arms were beautiful before. Now, with old age, they are like withered trumpet flowers. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

264.
‘‘Saṇhamuddikasuvaṇṇamaṇḍitā, sobhare su hatthā pure mama;Te jarāya yathā mūlamūlikā, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Adorned with delicate golden rings, my hands were beautiful before. Now, with old age, they are like root stalks. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

265.
‘‘Pīnavaṭṭasahituggatā ubho, sobhare [sobhate (aṭṭha.)] su thanakā pure mama;Thevikīva lambanti nodakā, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Both plump, round, and raised up, my breasts were beautiful before. Now they hang down like empty waterskins. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

266.
‘‘Kañcanassaphalakaṃva sammaṭṭhaṃ, sobhate su kāyo pure mama;So valīhi sukhumāhi otato, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Like a well-polished golden plate, my body was beautiful before. Now it is covered with fine wrinkles. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

267.
‘‘Nāgabhogasadisopamā ubho, sobhare su ūrū pure mama;Te jarāya yathā veḷunāḷiyo, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Both like the coils of a great serpent, my thighs were beautiful before. Now, with old age, they are like bamboo stalks. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

268.
‘‘Saṇhanūpurasuvaṇṇamaṇḍitā, sobhare su jaṅghā pure mama;Tā jarāya tiladaṇḍakāriva, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Adorned with delicate golden anklets, my calves were beautiful before. Now, with old age, they are like sesame stalks. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

269.
‘‘Tūlapuṇṇasadisopamā ubho, sobhare su pādā pure mama;Te jarāya phuṭitā valīmatā, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā.

Both like cotton-filled pillows, my feet were beautiful before. Now, with old age, they are cracked and wrinkled. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise.

270.
‘‘Ediso ahu ayaṃ samussayo, jajjaro bahudukkhānamālayo;Sopalepapatito jarāgharo, saccavādivacanaṃ anaññathā’’.

Such was this body, frail, a dwelling place of much suffering, an old house with its plaster fallen off. The word of the truth-speaker is not otherwise. … Ambapālī therī…. ... The Elder Nun Ambapālī ...

### 2. Rohinītherīgāthā
2.The Verses of the Elder Nun Rohinī
271.
‘‘‘Samaṇā’ti bhoti supi [bhoti tvaṃ sayasi (sī.), bhoti maṃ vipassi (syā.)], ‘samaṇā’ti pabujjhasi [paṭibujjhasi (sī. syā.)];Samaṇāneva [samaṇānameva (sī. syā.)] kittesi, samaṇī nūna [samaṇī nu (ka.)] bhavissasi.

‘Ascetics,’ lady, you murmur in sleep; ‘ascetics,’ you wake exclaiming. You praise only ascetics—surely you will become an ascetic nun.

272.
‘‘Vipulaṃ annañca pānañca, samaṇānaṃ paveccasi [payacchasi (sī.)];Rohinī dāni pucchāmi, kena te samaṇā piyā. You give abundant food and drink to ascetics. Rohinī, now I ask you, why are ascetics dear to you?


273.
‘‘Akammakāmā alasā, paradattūpajīvino;Āsaṃsukā sādukāmā, kena te samaṇā piyā’’.

They are disliking work, lazy, living off the gifts of others, longing for things, desiring sweets. Why are ascetics dear to you?

274.
‘‘Cirassaṃ vata maṃ tāta, samaṇānaṃ paripucchasi;Tesaṃ te kittayissāmi, paññāsīlaparakkamaṃ.

Indeed, dear one, you have questioned me after a long time about the ascetics. I will declare to you their wisdom, virtue, and effort.

275.
‘‘Kammakāmā analasā, kammaseṭṭhassa kārakā;Rāgaṃ dosaṃ pajahanti, tena me samaṇā piyā.

Those who love work, tireless, performers of the best deeds; they abandon lust and hatred, therefore ascetics are dear to me.

276.
‘‘Tīṇi pāpassa mūlāni, dhunanti sucikārino;Sabbaṃ pāpaṃ pahīnesaṃ, tena me samaṇā piyā.

They shake off the three roots of evil, those who act purely; all evil is abandoned by them; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

277.
‘‘Kāyakammaṃ suci nesaṃ, vacīkammañca tādisaṃ;Manokammaṃ suci nesaṃ, tena me samaṇā piyā.

Their bodily action is pure, and their verbal action is likewise; their mental action is pure; therefore, ascetics are dear to me.

278.
‘‘Vimalā saṅkhamuttāva, suddhā santarabāhirā;Puṇṇā sukkāna dhammānaṃ [sukkehi dhammehi (sī. syā. aṭṭha.)], tena me samaṇā piyā.

Like a flawless conch shell, pure inside and out, full of bright qualities; therefore, ascetics are dear to me.

279.
‘‘Bahussutā dhammadharā, ariyā dhammajīvino;Atthaṃ dhammañca desenti, tena me samaṇā piyā.

Learned, upholders of the Dhamma, noble ones living the Dhamma; they teach the meaning and the Dhamma; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

280.
‘‘Bahussutā dhammadharā, ariyā dhammajīvino;Ekaggacittā satimanto, tena me samaṇā piyā.

Learned, upholders of the Dhamma, noble ones living the Dhamma; with collected minds and mindful; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

281.
‘‘Dūraṅgamā satimanto, mantabhāṇī anuddhatā;Dukkhassantaṃ pajānanti, tena me samaṇā piyā.

Far-going, mindful, speaking with wisdom, not arrogant; they understand the end of suffering; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

282.
‘‘Yasmā gāmā pakkamanti, na vilokenti kiñcanaṃ;Anapekkhāva gacchanti, tena me samaṇā piyā.

When they depart from a village, they look back at nothing; they go without attachment; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

283.
‘‘Na tesaṃ koṭṭhe openti, na kumbhiṃ na khaḷopiyaṃ;Pariniṭṭhitamesānā, tena me samaṇā piyā.

They do not store in a barn, nor in a pot, nor in any container; they seek only what is ready; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

284.
‘‘Na te hiraññaṃ gaṇhanti, na suvaṇṇaṃ na rūpiyaṃ;Paccuppannena yāpenti, tena me samaṇā piyā.

They do not accept gold, nor silver, nor coin; they maintain themselves on what is presently offered; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

285.
‘‘Nānākulā pabbajitā, nānājanapadehi ca;Aññamaññaṃ piyāyanti [pihayanti (ka.)], tena me samaṇā piyā’’.

From various families they have gone forth, and from various districts; they are dear to one another; therefore ascetics are dear to me.

286.
‘‘Atthāya vata no bhoti, kule jātāsi rohinī;Saddhā buddhe ca dhamme ca, saṅghe ca tibbagāravā. Indeed, Rohinī, you were born into this family for our welfare; you have faith in the Buddha, the Dhamma, and the Sangha, and are deeply respectful.


287.
‘‘Tuvaṃ hetaṃ pajānāsi, puññakkhettaṃ anuttaraṃ;Amhampi ete samaṇā, paṭigaṇhanti dakkhiṇaṃ’’.

You indeed understand this, the unsurpassed field of merit; these ascetics also accept our offerings.

288.
‘‘Patiṭṭhito hettha yañño, vipulo no bhavissati;Sace bhāyasi dukkhassa, sace te dukkhamappiyaṃ.

Here the offering is established; it will be vast for us. If you fear suffering, if suffering is unwelcome to you,

289.
‘‘Upehi saraṇaṃ buddhaṃ, dhammaṃ saṅghañca tādinaṃ;Samādiyāhi sīlāni, taṃ te atthāya hehiti’’.

Go for refuge to the Buddha, the Dhamma, and the Sangha of such qualities; undertake the precepts, that will be for your welfare.

290.
‘‘Upemi saraṇaṃ buddhaṃ, dhammaṃ saṅghañca tādinaṃ;Samādiyāmi sīlāni, taṃ me atthāya hehiti.

I go for refuge to the Buddha, to the Dhamma, and to the Sangha of such qualities; I undertake the precepts; that will be for my welfare.

291.
‘‘Brahmabandhu pure āsiṃ, so idānimhi brāhmaṇo;Tevijjo sottiyo camhi, vedagū camhi nhātako’’. Formerly I was a kinsman of Brahmā, now I am a brahmin; I am a master of the three Vedas, a Vedic scholar, one who has attained the Vedas, and one who is purified. … Rohinī therī…. ... The Elder Nun Rohinī ...


### 3. Cāpātherīgāthā
3.The Verses of the Elder Cāpā
292.
‘‘Laṭṭhihattho pure āsi, so dāni migaluddako;Āsāya palipā ghorā, nāsakkhi pārametave.

"Formerly I was a staff-bearer, now I am a deer hunter; Gripped by terrible mire of hope, I was unable to cross over."

293.
‘‘Sumattaṃ maṃ maññamānā, cāpā puttamatosayi;Cāpāya bandhanaṃ chetvā, pabbajissaṃ punopahaṃ. "Thinking me compliant, Cāpā cherished her son; Having cut Cāpā's bond, I will go forth again."


294.
‘‘Mā me kujjhi mahāvīra, mā me kujjhi mahāmuni;Na hi kodhaparetassa, suddhi atthi kuto tapo.

"Do not be angry with me, great hero, do not be angry, great sage. For one overcome by anger, there is no purity, how then austerity?"

295.
‘‘Pakkamissañca nāḷāto, kodha nāḷāya vacchati;Bandhantī itthirūpena, samaṇe dhammajīvino’’ [dhammajīvine (ka.)].

"Even if I depart from this reed pipe, anger remains within it; Women, in their form, bind ascetics who live by the Dhamma."

296.
‘‘Ehi kāḷa nivattassu, bhuñja kāme yathā pure;Ahañca te vasīkatā, ye ca me santi ñātakā’’. "Come back, Kāḷa, turn back, enjoy sensual pleasures as before; I will be under your control, and so will my relatives."


297.
‘‘Etto cāpe catubbhāgaṃ, yathā bhāsasi tvañca me;Tayi rattassa posassa, uḷāraṃ vata taṃ siyā’’. "Even a quarter of that, Cāpā, as you say to me, would indeed be a great thing for a man enamored of you."


298.
‘‘Kāḷaṅginiṃva takkāriṃ, pupphitaṃ girimuddhani;Phullaṃ dālimalaṭṭhiṃva, antodīpeva pāṭaliṃ. "Like a dark-hued takkārī flower on a mountain peak, like a blooming pomegranate branch, like a pāṭalī in an inner island grove."


299.
‘‘Haricandanalittaṅgiṃ, kāsikuttamadhāriniṃ;Taṃ maṃ rūpavatiṃ santiṃ, kassa ohāya gacchasi’’.

"Whose body is anointed with yellow sandalwood, wearing the finest cloth from Kasi; Such a beautiful and serene one as I am, to whom are you abandoning me to go?"

300.
‘‘Sākuntikova sakuṇiṃ [sakuṇaṃ (syā.)], yathā bandhitumicchati;Āharimena rūpena, na maṃ tvaṃ bādhayissasi’’.

"Like a fowler with a bird, just as he wishes to trap it, with this lure you will not subdue me."

301.
‘‘Imañca me puttaphalaṃ, kāḷa uppāditaṃ tayā;Taṃ maṃ puttavatiṃ santiṃ, kassa ohāya gacchasi’’. "This child, fruit of my womb, Kāḷa, produced by you; me, a mother with sons, peaceful—to whom are you going, abandoning me?"


302.
‘‘Jahanti putte sappaññā, tato ñātī tato dhanaṃ;Pabbajanti mahāvīrā, nāgo chetvāva bandhanaṃ’’.

"Wise ones give up children, then relatives, then wealth; Great heroes go forth, like an elephant breaking its bonds."

303.
‘‘Idāni te imaṃ puttaṃ, daṇḍena churikāya vā;Bhūmiyaṃ vā nisumbhissaṃ [nisumbheyyaṃ (sī.)], puttasokā na gacchasi’’.

"Now I will strike this son of yours with a stick or a knife, or dash him to the ground; you will not depart due to sorrow for your son."

304.
‘‘Sace puttaṃ siṅgālānaṃ, kukkurānaṃ padāhisi;Na maṃ puttakatte jammi, punarāvattayissasi’’.

"If you give a child to jackals or dogs, you will not make me a son, wretch, nor will you turn me back."

305.
‘‘Handa kho dāni bhaddante, kuhiṃ kāḷa gamissasi;Katamaṃ gāmanigamaṃ, nagaraṃ rājadhāniyo’’. "Now then, venerable sir, where will you go, Kāḷa? To which village or market town, city, or royal capital?"


306.
‘‘Ahumha pubbe gaṇino, assamaṇā samaṇamānino;Gāmena gāmaṃ vicarimha, nagare rājadhāniyo.

"Formerly, we were leaders of groups, not true ascetics, yet conceiving ourselves as such; from village to village we wandered, and in cities and royal capitals."

307.
‘‘Eso hi bhagavā buddho, nadiṃ nerañjaraṃ pati;Sabbadukkhappahānāya, dhammaṃ deseti pāṇinaṃ;Tassāhaṃ santikaṃ gacchaṃ, so me satthā bhavissati’’. "Indeed, that Blessed One is the Buddha, who is going to the Nerañjarā River; For the abandonment of all suffering, he teaches the Dhamma to living beings. I will go to his presence; he will be my teacher."


308.
‘‘Vandanaṃ dāni vajjāsi, lokanāthaṃ anuttaraṃ;Padakkhiṇañca katvāna, ādiseyyāsi dakkhiṇaṃ’’.

"Now you should pay homage to the unsurpassed Lord of the world; and having circumambulated, you should make the offering to the south."

309.
‘‘Etaṃ kho labbhamamhehi, yathā bhāsasi tvañca me;Vandanaṃ dāni te vajjaṃ, lokanāthaṃ anuttaraṃ;Padakkhiṇañca katvāna, ādisissāmi dakkhiṇaṃ’’.

"Indeed, this is obtainable by us, as you say, and by me. Now I shall pay homage to the unsurpassed Lord of the World; and having circumambulated, I shall make the offering to the south."

310.
Tato ca kāḷo pakkāmi, nadiṃ nerañjaraṃ pati;So addasāsi sambuddhaṃ, desentaṃ amataṃ padaṃ. Then Kāḷa departed and went to the Nerañjarā River. There he saw the Fully Enlightened One teaching the Deathless State.


311.
Dukkhaṃ dukkhasamuppādaṃ, dukkhassa ca atikkamaṃ;Ariyaṃ caṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ, dukkhūpasamagāminaṃ.

Suffering, the origin of suffering, and the cessation of suffering; and the Noble Eightfold Path that leads to the cessation of suffering.

312.
Tassa pādāni vanditvā, katvāna naṃ [katvānahaṃ (sī.)] padakkhiṇaṃ;Cāpāya ādisitvāna, pabbajiṃ anagāriyaṃ;Tisso vijjā anuppattā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsanaṃ. Having bowed at his feet, and having circumambulated him; Having dedicated (the merit) to Cāpā, I went forth into homelessness. The three knowledges were attained, the Buddha's teaching was fulfilled. … Cāpā therī…. … The Elder Cāpā…


### 4. Sundarītherīgāthā
4.The Verses of the Elder Sundarī
313.
‘‘Petāni bhoti puttāni, khādamānā tuvaṃ pure;Tuvaṃ divā ca ratto ca, atīva paritappasi.

O lady, your children are ghosts; you formerly devoured them. Day and night, you are exceedingly tormented.

314.
‘‘Sājja sabbāni khāditvā, sataputtāni [satta puttāni (syā.)] brāhmaṇī;Vāseṭṭhi kena vaṇṇena, na bāḷhaṃ paritappasi’’. Today, having eaten all your hundred sons, O Brahmin woman Vāseṭṭhī, by what quality do you not deeply grieve?


315.
‘‘Bahūni puttasatāni, ñātisaṅghasatāni ca;Khāditāni atītaṃse, mama tuyhañca brāhmaṇa.

Many hundreds of sons, and hundreds of assemblies of relatives; Have been consumed in the past, brahmin, by both me and you.

316.
‘‘Sāhaṃ nissaraṇaṃ ñatvā, jātiyā maraṇassa ca;Na socāmi na rodāmi, na cāpi paritappayiṃ’’.

Knowing the escape from birth and death, I do not sorrow, I do not weep, nor do I lament.

317.
‘‘Abbhutaṃ vata vāseṭṭhi, vācaṃ bhāsasi edisiṃ;Kassa tvaṃ dhammamaññāya, giraṃ [thiraṃ (sī.)] bhāsasi edisiṃ’’. Wonderful indeed, Vāseṭṭhī, is the speech you utter! Having understood whose Dhamma do you speak such words?


318.
‘‘Esa brāhmaṇa sambuddho, nagaraṃ mithilaṃ pati;Sabbadukkhappahānāya, dhammaṃ desesi pāṇinaṃ. This, O Brahmin, is the Fully Enlightened One, who has arrived at the city of Mithilā. He teaches the Dhamma for the abandonment of all suffering to living beings.


319.
‘‘Tassa brahme [brāhmaṇa (sī. syā.)] arahato, dhammaṃ sutvā nirūpadhiṃ;Tattha viññātasaddhammā, puttasokaṃ byapānudiṃ’’.

Having heard the Dhamma free from attachment from that Arahant, O brahmin; there, having understood the true Dhamma, I dispelled the sorrow for my son.

320.
‘‘So ahampi gamissāmi, nagaraṃ mithilaṃ pati;Appeva maṃ so bhagavā, sabbadukkhā pamocaye’’. I too will go to the city of Mithilā; Perhaps that Blessed One will deliver me from all suffering.


321.
Addasa brāhmaṇo buddhaṃ, vippamuttaṃ nirūpadhiṃ;Svassa dhammamadesesi, muni dukkhassa pāragū.

The brahmin saw the Buddha, completely liberated and free from attachments. To him, the Sage who has gone beyond suffering, He taught the Dhamma.

322.
Dukkhaṃ dukkhasamuppādaṃ, dukkhassa ca atikkamaṃ;Ariyaṃ caṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ, dukkhūpasamagāminaṃ.

Suffering, the origin of suffering, and the cessation of suffering; and the Noble Eightfold Path that leads to the cessation of suffering.

323.
Tattha viññātasaddhammo, pabbajjaṃ samarocayi;Sujāto tīhi rattīhi, tisso vijjā aphassayi. There, understanding the true Dhamma, he approved of ordination; Sujāta, in three nights, attained the three knowledges.


324.
‘‘Ehi sārathi gacchāhi, rathaṃ niyyādayāhimaṃ;Ārogyaṃ brāhmaṇiṃ vajja [vajjā (sī.)], ‘pabbaji [pabbajito (sī.)] dāni brāhmaṇo;Sujāto tīhi rattīhi, tisso vijjā aphassayi’’’. Come, charioteer, go, bring this chariot. Say to the brahmin woman: 'Health to you! Now the brahmin has gone forth; Sujāta, in three nights, has attained the three knowledges.'


325.
Tato ca rathamādāya, sahassañcāpi sārathi;Ārogyaṃ brāhmaṇivoca, ‘‘pabbaji dāni brāhmaṇo;Sujāto tīhi rattīhi, tisso vijjā aphassayi’’. Then, taking the chariot and a thousand (coins), the charioteer said to the brahmin woman: 'Now the Brahmin has gone forth; Sujāta, in three nights, has attained the three knowledges.'


326.
‘‘Etañcāhaṃ assarathaṃ, sahassañcāpi sārathi;Tevijjaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ sutvā [ñatvā (sī.)], puṇṇapattaṃ dadāmi te’’.

I give you this horse-chariot, and a thousand (coins) as well, O charioteer. Having heard of the brahmin who has attained the three knowledges, I give you a full bowl.

327.
‘‘Tuyheva hotvassaratho, sahassañcāpi brāhmaṇi;Ahampi pabbajissāmi, varapaññassa santike’’.

Let the chariot be yours, and the thousand (coins) as well, O Brahmin woman. I too will go forth in the presence of the one with excellent wisdom.

328.
‘‘Hatthī gavassaṃ maṇikuṇḍalañca, phītañcimaṃ gahavibhavaṃ pahāya;Pitā pabbajito tuyhaṃ, bhuñja bhogāni sundari; Tuvaṃ dāyādikā kule’’.

Elephants, cattle, horses, and jeweled earrings, having abandoned this thriving household estate, your father has gone forth. Enjoy the wealth, Sundari, you are the heir in this family.

329.
‘‘Hatthī gavassaṃ maṇikuṇḍalañca, rammaṃ cimaṃ gahavibhavaṃ pahāya;Pitā pabbajito mayhaṃ, puttasokena aṭṭito;

Elephants, cattle, horses, jeweled earrings, having abandoned this delightful household wealth, my father, afflicted by sorrow for his son, has gone forth. Ahampi pabbajissāmi, bhātusokena aṭṭitā’’. I too will go forth, afflicted by sorrow for my brother.

330.
‘‘So te ijjhatu saṅkappo, yaṃ tvaṃ patthesi sundarī;Uttiṭṭhapiṇḍo uñcho ca, paṃsukūlañca cīvaraṃ;Etāni abhisambhontī, paraloke anāsavā’’.

May that intention of yours succeed, Sundari, whatever you long for. Alms food and gleanings, and rag-robes; attaining these, may you be without taints in the other world.

331.
‘‘Sikkhamānāya me ayye, dibbacakkhu visodhitaṃ;Pubbenivāsaṃ jānāmi, yattha me vusitaṃ pure.

Venerable lady, as a trainee, my divine eye has been purified. I know my former existences, where I lived in the past.

332.
‘‘Tuvaṃ nissāya kalyāṇī, therī saṅghassa sobhane;Tisso vijjā anuppattā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsanaṃ.

By relying on you, O virtuous one, O elder beautifying the Sangha; The three knowledges have been attained, the Buddha's teaching has been fulfilled.

333.
‘‘Anujānāhi me ayye, icche sāvatthi gantave;Sīhanādaṃ nadissāmi, buddhaseṭṭhassa santike’’. Grant me permission, venerable lady, I wish to go to Sāvatthī; I will roar the lion's roar in the presence of the foremost Buddha.


334.
‘‘Passa sundari satthāraṃ, hemavaṇṇaṃ harittacaṃ;Adantānaṃ dametāraṃ, sambuddhamakutobhayaṃ’’.

See, Sundari, the Teacher, golden-hued, with skin like green gold; The tamer of the untamed, the Fully Enlightened One, fearless from every quarter.

335.
‘‘Passa sundarimāyantiṃ, vippamuttaṃ nirūpadhiṃ;Vītarāgaṃ visaṃyuttaṃ, katakiccamanāsavaṃ. Behold Sundarī coming, completely liberated, free from attachments;
Rid of passion, disjoined,
Duty done, taintless.


336.
‘‘Bārāṇasīto nikkhamma, tava santikamāgatā;Sāvikā te mahāvīra, pāde vandati sundarī’’. Having left Bārāṇasī, she has come to your presence; Your disciple, O great hero, Sundarī, bows at your feet.


337.
‘‘Tuvaṃ buddho tuvaṃ satthā, tuyhaṃ dhītāmhi brāhmaṇa;Orasā mukhato jātā, katakiccā anāsavā’’.

You are the Buddha, you are the Teacher, I am your daughter, O brahmin; Born of your very self, born from your mouth, my task is done, I am taintless.

338.
‘‘Tassā te svāgataṃ bhadde, tato [atho (ka.)] te adurāgataṃ;Evañhi dantā āyanti, satthu pādāni vandikā;Vītarāgā visaṃyuttā, katakiccā anāsavā’’.

Welcome to you, auspicious one, you have not come from afar; For thus indeed do the tamed come, paying homage to the Teacher’s feet, those rid of passion, disjoined, duty done, taintless. … Sundarī therī…. … The Elder Sundarī…

### 5. Subhākammāradhītutherīgāthā
5.The Verses of the Elder Nun Subhā the Goldsmith’s Daughter
339.
‘‘Daharāhaṃ suddhavasanā, yaṃ pure dhammamassuṇiṃ;Tassā me appamattāya, saccābhisamayo ahu.

“Young was I, clad in clean garments, when I first heard the Dhamma; and for me, who was heedful, the breakthrough to the truth occurred.

340.
‘‘Tatohaṃ sabbakāmesu, bhusaṃ aratimajjhagaṃ;Sakkāyasmiṃ bhayaṃ disvā, nekkhammameva [nekkhammaññeva (sī.), nekkhammasseva (syā.)] pīhaye.

“Then I found great aversion toward all sensual pleasures; and seeing the danger in my own person, I longed only for renunciation.

341.
‘‘Hitvānahaṃ ñātigaṇaṃ, dāsakammakarāni ca;Gāmakhettāni phītāni, ramaṇīye pamodite.

“My host of kinsmen I abandoned, slaves, workers, and prosperous villages and fields, delightful and gladdening;

342.
‘‘Pahāyahaṃ pabbajitā, sāpateyyamanappakaṃ;Evaṃ saddhāya nikkhamma, saddhamme suppavedite.

“having abandoned my considerable wealth, I went forth. Thus I went forth in faith in the well-proclaimed Good Dhamma.

343.
‘‘Netaṃ [na metaṃ (sī. syā.)] assa patirūpaṃ, ākiñcaññañhi patthaye;Yo [yā (syā.)] jātarūpaṃ rajataṃ, chaḍḍetvā [thapetvā (ka.)] punarāgame [punarāgahe (ka.)].

“It would not be proper for one who aspires to nothingness that, having cast aside gold and silver, one should take them up again.

344.
‘‘Rajataṃ jātarūpaṃ vā, na bodhāya na santiyā;Netaṃ samaṇasāruppaṃ, na etaṃ ariyaddhanaṃ.

“Gold or silver is not for awakening, not for peace. This is not befitting a recluse; this is not the wealth of the noble ones.

345.
‘‘Lobhanaṃ madanañcetaṃ, mohanaṃ rajavaḍḍhanaṃ;Sāsaṅkaṃ bahuāyāsaṃ, natthi cettha dhuvaṃ ṭhiti.

“This is greed and intoxication, delusion and a source of defilement; it is fraught with anxiety and much trouble; there is no firm stability in it.

346.
‘‘Ettha rattā pamattā ca, saṅkiliṭṭhamanā narā;Aññamaññena byāruddhā, puthu kubbanti medhagaṃ.

“People attached to this, heedless and with defiled minds, are hostile toward one another and create much strife.

347.
‘‘Vadho bandho parikleso, jāni sokapariddavo;Kāmesu adhipannānaṃ, dissate byasanaṃ bahuṃ.

“Killing, bondage, and affliction, loss, grief, and lamentation—for those addicted to sensual pleasures, much disaster is seen.

348.
‘‘Taṃ maṃ ñātī amittāva, kiṃ vo kāmesu yuñjatha;Jānātha maṃ pabbajitaṃ, kāmesu bhayadassiniṃ.

“O my kinsmen, you act like enemies! Why do you try to yoke me to sensual pleasures? You should know that I have gone forth, as one who sees the danger in them.

349.
‘‘Na hiraññasuvaṇṇena, parikkhīyanti āsavā;Amittā vadhakā kāmā, sapattā sallabandhanā.

“The taints are not destroyed by gold and silver. Sensual pleasures are enemies, murderers, foes, darts, and bonds.

350.
‘‘Taṃ maṃ ñātī amittāva, kiṃ vo kāmesu yuñjatha;Jānātha maṃ pabbajitaṃ, muṇḍaṃ saṅghāṭipārutaṃ.

“O my kinsmen, you act like enemies! Why do you try to yoke me to sensual pleasures? You should know that I have gone forth, shaven-headed and clad in the outer robe.

351.
‘‘Uttiṭṭhapiṇḍo uñcho ca, paṃsukūlañca cīvaraṃ;Etaṃ kho mama sāruppaṃ, anagārūpanissayo.

“Alms scraps and what can be gleaned, and a robe made from rags—this is what is suitable for me, the requisites of the homeless life.

352.
‘‘Vantā mahesīhi kāmā, ye dibbā ye ca mānusā;Khemaṭṭhāne vimuttā te, pattā te acalaṃ sukhaṃ.

“Sensual pleasures, both divine and human, have been disgorged by the great seers. They are liberated in the state of security; they have attained unshakable happiness.

353.
‘‘Māhaṃ kāmehi saṅgacchiṃ, yesu tāṇaṃ na vijjati;Amittā vadhakā kāmā, aggikkhandhūpamā dukhā.

“May I have no involvement with sensual pleasures, in which no shelter can be found. Sensual pleasures are enemies, murderers, painful like a mass of fire.

354.
‘‘Paripantho esa bhayo, savighāto sakaṇṭako;Gedho suvisamo ceso [lepo (sī.)], mahanto mohanāmukho.

“This is a peril and a danger, full of obstacles and thorns; this greed is utterly treacherous, a great entrance to delusion.

355.
‘‘Upasaggo bhīmarūpo, kāmā sappasirūpamā;Ye bālā abhinandanti, andhabhūtā puthujjanā.

“Sensual pleasures are a calamity of dreadful form, like the head of a snake. Fools, blind worldlings, delight in them.

356.
‘‘Kāmapaṅkena sattā hi, bahū loke aviddasū;Pariyantaṃ na jānanti, jātiyā maraṇassa ca.

“For many beings in the world, stuck in the mire of sensual pleasure, are unwise; they do not know the end of birth and death.

357.
‘‘Duggatigamanaṃ maggaṃ, manussā kāmahetukaṃ;Bahuṃ ve paṭipajjanti, attano rogamāvahaṃ.

“On account of sensual pleasure, many people undertake the path that leads to a bad destination, thereby bringing disease upon themselves.

358.
‘‘Evaṃ amittajananā, tāpanā saṃkilesikā;Lokāmisā bandhanīyā, kāmā maraṇabandhanā [caraṇabandhanā (sī.)].

“Thus sensual pleasures produce enemies; they are scorching and defiling, the bait of the world, a source of bondage, the bonds of Death.

359.
‘‘Ummādanā ullapanā, kāmā cittappamaddino;Sattānaṃ saṅkilesāya, khippaṃ [khipaṃ (sī.)] mārena oḍḍitaṃ. “Maddening and alluring, sensual pleasures crush the mind; a snare quickly set by Māra for the defilement of beings.


360.
‘‘Anantādīnavā kāmā, bahudukkhā mahāvisā;Appassādā raṇakarā, sukkapakkhavisosanā [visosakā (sī.)].

“Sensual pleasures have endless dangers, are full of suffering, a great poison; they give little enjoyment, cause conflict, and dry up the wholesome side.

361.
‘‘Sāhaṃ etādisaṃ katvā, byasanaṃ kāmahetukaṃ;Na taṃ paccāgamissāmi, nibbānābhiratā sadā. “Having understood this disaster caused by sensual pleasure, I will not return to it; I will delight always in Nibbāna.


362.
‘‘Raṇaṃ karitvā [taritvā (sī.)] kāmānaṃ, sītibhāvābhikaṅkhinī;Appamattā vihassāmi, sabbasaṃyojanakkhaye.

“Having made war on sensual pleasures, longing for the state of coolness, I will dwell diligently in the destruction of all fetters.

363.
‘‘Asokaṃ virajaṃ khemaṃ, ariyaṭṭhaṅgikaṃ ujuṃ;Taṃ maggaṃ anugacchāmi, yena tiṇṇā mahesino’’.

“Sorrowless, stainless, and secure, the Noble Eightfold Path, the straight way: this is the path I follow, by which the great seers have crossed over.”

364.
Imaṃ passatha dhammaṭṭhaṃ, subhaṃ kammāradhītaraṃ;Anejaṃ upasampajja, rukkhamūlamhi jhāyati. “Behold this one standing in Dhamma, Subhā, the goldsmith’s daughter! Having attained the imperturbable state, she meditates at the foot of a tree.


365.
Ajjaṭṭhamī pabbajitā, saddhā saddhammasobhanā;Vinītuppalavaṇṇāya, tevijjā maccuhāyinī. “It is the eighth day since she went forth in faith, resplendent in the Good Dhamma. Trained like Uppalavaṇṇā, she is endowed with the three true knowledges and has left Death behind.


366.
Sāyaṃ bhujissā anaṇā, bhikkhunī bhāvitindriyā;Sabbayogavisaṃyuttā, katakiccā anāsavā. “This bhikkhunī is a free woman, debtless, with faculties developed. Disjoined from all yokes, her task is done, she is free from taints.


367.
Taṃ sakko devasaṅghena, upasaṅkamma iddhiyā;Namassati bhūtapati, subhaṃ kammāradhītaranti. “Sakka, the lord of beings, with his assembly of devas, approached her by his psychic power and paid homage to Subhā, the goldsmith’s daughter.” … Subhā kammāradhītā therī…. … The Elder Nun Subhā the Goldsmith’s Daughter … Vīsatinipāto niṭṭhito.

The Chapter of the Twenties is finished.
