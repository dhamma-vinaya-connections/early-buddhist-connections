# Snp 3.4 Sundarikabhāradvājasuttaṃ

## Snp 3.4 The Sundarikabhāradvāja Sutta

Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā kosalesu viharati sundarikāya nadiyā tīre. Tena kho pana samayena sundarikabhāradvājo brāhmaṇo sundarikāya nadiyā tīre aggiṃ juhati, aggihuttaṃ paricarati. Atha kho sundarikabhāradvājo brāhmaṇo aggiṃ juhitvā aggihuttaṃ paricaritvā uṭṭhāyāsanā samantā catuddisā anuvilokesi – ‘‘ko nu kho imaṃ habyasesaṃ bhuñjeyyā’’ti? Addasā kho sundarikabhāradvājo brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṃ avidūre aññatarasmiṃ rukkhamūle sasīsaṃ pārutaṃ nisinnaṃ; disvāna vāmena hatthena habyasesaṃ gahetvā dakkhiṇena hatthena kamaṇḍaluṃ gahetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami.

Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One was dwelling among the Kosalans on the bank of the Sundarikā River. Now at that time, the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja was tending the sacred fire and performing the fire oblation on the bank of the Sundarikā River. Then, having tended the sacred fire and performed the fire oblation, the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja rose from his seat and looked all around in the four quarters, thinking: “Who might eat the remains of this oblation?” He saw the Blessed One sitting not far away at the foot of a certain tree, covered from head to foot. Seeing him, he took the remains of the oblation in his left hand and a water pot in his right hand and approached the Blessed One.

Atha kho bhagavā sundarikabhāradvājassa brāhmaṇassa padasaddena sīsaṃ vivari. Atha kho sundarikabhāradvājo brāhmaṇo – ‘‘muṇḍo ayaṃ bhavaṃ, muṇḍako ayaṃ bhava’’nti tatova puna nivattitukāmo ahosi. Atha kho sundarikabhāradvājassa brāhmaṇassa etadahosi – ‘‘muṇḍāpi hi idhekacce brāhmaṇā bhavanti, yaṃnūnāhaṃ upasaṅkamitvā jātiṃ puccheyya’’nti. Atha kho sundarikabhāradvājo brāhmaṇo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘kiṃjacco bhava’’nti?

At the sound of the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja’s footsteps, the Blessed One uncovered his head. Then the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja thought, “This fellow is a shaveling, this fellow is a shaveling!” and he wanted to turn back. But then it occurred to him: “Some brahmins here are also shaven-headed. Let me go up to him and ask about his birth.” Then the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja approached the Blessed One and said to him: “Of what birth is the venerable one?”

Atha kho bhagavā sundarikabhāradvājaṃ brāhmaṇaṃ gāthāhi ajjhabhāsi –

Then the Blessed One addressed the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja in verses:

457.

‘‘Na brāhmaṇo nomhi na rājaputto, na vessāyano uda koci nomhi;Gottaṃ pariññāya puthujjanānaṃ, akiñcano manta carāmi loke.

“I am no brahmin, no son of a king, nor any kind of vessa. Having thoroughly understood the lineage of common people, I wander in the world, a sage possessing nothing.

458.

‘‘Saṅghāṭivāsī agaho carāmi [agiho (ka. sī. pī.) ageho (katthaci)], nivuttakeso abhinibbutatto;Alippamāno idha māṇavehi, akallaṃ maṃ brāhmaṇa pucchasi gottapañhaṃ’’.

“Wearing a patchwork robe, I wander homeless, with shaven hair, my self fully quenched. Unsoiled by human beings here, it is improper, brahmin, that you ask me about my lineage.”

459.

‘‘Pucchanti ve bho brāhmaṇā, brāhmaṇebhi saha brāhmaṇo no bhava’’nti.

(The brahmin:) “But sir, brahmins ask other brahmins: ‘Are you a brahmin?’”

460.

‘‘Brāhmaṇo hi ce tvaṃ brūsi, mañca brūsi abrāhmaṇaṃ;Taṃ taṃ sāvittiṃ pucchāmi, tipadaṃ catuvīsatakkharaṃ.

(The Buddha:) “If you claim to be a brahmin, but call me a non-brahmin, then I ask you about the Sāvittī chant, with its three lines and twenty-four syllables.”

461.

‘‘Kiṃ nissitā isayo manujā, khattiyā brāhmaṇā [paṭhamapādanto] devatānaṃ;Yaññamakappayiṃsu puthū idha loke [dutiyapādanto (sī.)].

“On what are they dependent, those seers and men, khattiyas and brahmins, who here in the world have widely offered sacrifices to the devas?”

462.

‘‘Yadantagū vedagū yaññakāle, yassāhutiṃ labhe tassijjheti brūmi’’.

(The brahmin:) “One who has gone to the end, a master of the Vedas—at the time of sacrifice, whoever would receive his oblation, his sacrifice, I say, would succeed.”

463.

‘‘Addhā hi tassa hutamijjhe, (iti brāhmaṇo)Yaṃ tādisaṃ vedagumaddasāma;Tumhādisānañhi adassanena, añño jano bhuñjati pūraḷāsaṃ’’.

(The brahmin:) “Surely his oblation would succeed, if one could find such a master of the Vedas. But since we do not see the likes of you, other people eat the sacrificial cake.”

464.

‘‘Tasmātiha tvaṃ brāhmaṇa atthena, atthiko upasaṅkamma puccha;Santaṃ vidhūmaṃ anīghaṃ nirāsaṃ, appevidha abhivinde sumedhaṃ’’.

“Therefore, brahmin, since you are intent on the goal, approach and ask. Perhaps you might find in this world one who is peaceful, free from fumes, untroubled, desireless, a person of good wisdom.”

465.

‘‘Yaññe ratohaṃ bho gotama, yaññaṃ yiṭṭhukāmo nāhaṃ pajānāmi;Anusāsatu maṃ bhavaṃ, yattha hutaṃ ijjhate brūhi me taṃ’’.

“I delight in sacrifice, Master Gotama, and I wish to offer one, but I do not know how. Let the venerable one instruct me. Tell me where an oblation succeeds.”

‘‘Tena hi tvaṃ, brāhmaṇa, odahassu sotaṃ; dhammaṃ te desessāmi –

“Then, brahmin, lend your ear, and I will teach you the Dhamma.

466.

‘‘Mā jātiṃ pucchī caraṇañca puccha, kaṭṭhā have jāyati jātavedo;Nīcākulīnopi munī dhitīmā, ājāniyo hoti hirīnisedho.

“Do not ask about birth, but ask about conduct. From wood, indeed, is born the sacred fire. A sage of firm effort, though from a lowly family, becomes a noble thoroughbred restrained by conscience.

467.

‘‘Saccena danto damasā upeto, vedantagū vūsitabrahmacariyo;Kālena tamhi habyaṃ pavecche, yo brāhmaṇo puññapekkho [puññapekho (sī. pī.)] yajetha.

“Tamed by truth, endowed with self-control, one who has reached the goal of knowledge, who has lived the holy life—to him a brahmin seeking merit should give an oblation at the proper time.

468.

‘‘Ye kāme hitvā agahā caranti, susaññatattā tasaraṃva ujjuṃ;Kālena tesu habyaṃ pavecche, yo brāhmaṇo puññapekkho yajetha.

“Those who have abandoned sensual pleasures and wander homeless, with well-restrained selves, straight as a shuttle—to them a brahmin seeking merit should give an oblation at the proper time.

469.

‘‘Ye vītarāgā susamāhitindriyā, candova rāhuggahaṇā pamuttā;Kālena tesu habyaṃ pavecche, yo brāhmaṇo puññapekkho yajetha.

“Those who are free from lust, with well-collected faculties, like the moon released from Rāhu’s eclipse—to them a brahmin seeking merit should give an oblation at the proper time.

470.

‘‘Asajjamānā vicaranti loke, sadā satā hitvā mamāyitāni;Kālena tesu habyaṃ pavecche, yo brāhmaṇo puññapekkho yajetha.

“They wander in the world unattached, always mindful, having abandoned all sense of ‘mine’—to them a brahmin seeking merit should give an oblation at the proper time.

471.

‘‘Yo kāme hitvā abhibhuyyacārī, yo vedi jātīmaraṇassa antaṃ;Parinibbuto udakarahadova sīto, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“One who, having abandoned sensual pleasures, fares as a conqueror, who has understood the end of birth and death; fully quenched, cool like a lake—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

472.

‘‘Samo samehi visamehi dūre, tathāgato hoti anantapañño;Anūpalitto idha vā huraṃ vā, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“Impartial among the impartial, ever distant from the partial, the Tathāgata is of infinite wisdom. Unsmeared either in this world or the next—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

473.

‘‘Yamhi na māyā vasati na māno, yo vītalobho amamo nirāso;Panuṇṇakodho abhinibbutatto, yo brāhmaṇo sokamalaṃ ahāsi;Tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“In whom there dwells no deceit or conceit, who is free from greed, without possessiveness, without longing; with anger dispelled, the self fully quenched, the brahmin who has abandoned the stain of sorrow—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

474 .

474 .

‘‘Nivesanaṃ yo manaso ahāsi, pariggahā yassa na santi keci;Anupādiyāno idha vā huraṃ vā, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“One who has abandoned mental standpoints, for whom there are no acquisitions at all; not clinging to anything in this world or the next—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

475.

‘‘Samāhito yo udatāri oghaṃ, dhammaṃ caññāsi paramāya diṭṭhiyā;Khīṇāsavo antimadehadhārī, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“collected, one who has crossed the flood, who has known the Dhamma with supreme vision; with taints destroyed, bearing his final body—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

476.

‘‘Bhavāsavā yassa vacī kharā ca, vidhūpitā atthagatā na santi;Sa vedagū sabbadhi vippamutto, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“For whom the taint of being and harsh speech are shaken off, have vanished, and exist no more; he is a master of knowledge, liberated in every respect—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

477.

‘‘Saṅgātigo yassa na santi saṅgā, yo mānasattesu amānasatto;Dukkhaṃ pariññāya sakhettavatthuṃ, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“One who has overcome ties, for whom no ties exist, who is not attached among those attached to conceiving; having fully understood suffering with its field and basis—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

478.

‘‘Āsaṃ anissāya vivekadassī, paravediyaṃ diṭṭhimupātivatto;Ārammaṇā yassa na santi keci, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“Not dependent on longing, a seer of seclusion, he has overcome the view dependent on others’ reports; for whom there are no objects of attachment at all—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

479.

‘‘Paroparā [parovarā (sī. pī.)] yassa samecca dhammā, vidhūpitā atthagatā na santi;Santo upādānakhaye vimutto, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“For whom all things, high and low, having been comprehended, are shaken off, have vanished, and exist no more; peaceful, liberated through the destruction of clinging—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

480.

‘‘Saṃyojanaṃ jātikhayantadassī, yopānudi rāgapathaṃ asesaṃ;Suddho nidoso vimalo akāco [akāmo (sī. syā.)], tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“A seer of the end of birth and its fetter, who has entirely dispelled the path of lust; pure, faultless, stainless, without blemish—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

481.

‘‘Yo attano attānaṃ [attanāttānaṃ (sī. syā.)] nānupassati, samāhito ujjugato ṭhitatto;Sa ve anejo akhilo akaṅkho, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ.

“One who does not behold a self by means of self, collected, gone straight, of steadfast nature; he is indeed free from craving, without defect, free from doubt—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.

482.

‘‘Mohantarā yassa na santi keci, sabbesu dhammesu ca ñāṇadassī;Sarīrañca antimaṃ dhāreti, patto ca sambodhimanuttaraṃ sivaṃ;Ettāvatā yakkhassa suddhi, tathāgato arahati pūraḷāsaṃ’’.

“For whom there are no grounds for delusion, who is a seer of knowledge in regard to all things; who bears his final body and has attained the supreme, blissful enlightenment—to this extent is a spirit’s purity—such a Tathāgata is worthy of the sacrificial cake.”

483.

‘‘Hutañca [huttañca (sī. ka.)] mayhaṃ hutamatthu saccaṃ, yaṃ tādisaṃ vedagunaṃ alatthaṃ;Brahmā hi sakkhi paṭigaṇhātu me bhagavā, bhuñjatu me bhagavā pūraḷāsaṃ’’.

“May my oblation be a true oblation, for I have found such a master of knowledge. Brahmā is my witness! May the Blessed One accept from me. May the Blessed One eat my sacrificial cake.”

484.

‘‘Gāthābhigītaṃ me abhojaneyyaṃ, sampassataṃ brāhmaṇa nesa dhammo;Gāthābhigītaṃ panudanti buddhā, dhamme satī brāhmaṇa vuttiresā.

“Food obtained by chanting verses is not fit for me to eat; O brahmin, this is not the principle for those who see rightly. The Buddhas reject what is obtained by chanting verses; O brahmin, while the Dhamma exists, this is their way of life.”

485.

‘‘Aññena ca kevalinaṃ mahesiṃ, khīṇāsavaṃ kukkuccavūpasantaṃ;Annena pānena upaṭṭhahassu, khettañhi taṃ puññapekkhassa hoti’’.

“But with other food and drink you should attend upon the consummate one, the great seer, whose taints are destroyed, whose remorse is stilled; for that is the field for one who seeks merit.”

486.

‘‘Sādhāhaṃ bhagavā tathā vijaññaṃ, yo dakkhiṇaṃ bhuñjeyya mādisassa;Yaṃ yaññakāle pariyesamāno, pappuyya tava sāsanaṃ’’.

“It would be good, Blessed One, if I could know who might partake of an offering from one like me, whom I, seeking at the time of sacrifice, might find, having now encountered your teaching.”

487.

‘‘Sārambhā yassa vigatā, cittaṃ yassa anāvilaṃ;Vippamutto ca kāmehi, thinaṃ yassa panūditaṃ.

“One whose contentiousness has departed, whose mind is unstirred, who is liberated from sensual pleasures, and whose sloth has been dispelled.”

488.

‘‘Sīmantānaṃ vinetāraṃ, jātimaraṇakovidaṃ;Muniṃ moneyyasampannaṃ, tādisaṃ yaññamāgataṃ.

“The remover of limits, skilled in birth and death, the sage endowed with sagacity; such a one has come to the sacrifice.”

489.

‘‘Bhakuṭiṃ [bhūkuṭiṃ (ka. sī.), bhākuṭiṃ (ka. sī., ma. ni. 1.226)] vinayitvāna, pañjalikā namassatha;Pūjetha annapānena, evaṃ ijjhanti dakkhiṇā.

“Having smoothed your brow, pay homage with hands clasped in reverence. Honor him with food and drink; thus do offerings succeed.”

490.

‘‘Buddho bhavaṃ arahati pūraḷāsaṃ, puññakhettamanuttaraṃ;Āyāgo sabbalokassa, bhoto dinnaṃ mahapphala’’nti.

“The Buddha, the venerable one, is worthy of the sacrificial cake, the unsurpassed field of merit. He is the recipient of sacrifice for the entire world; what is given to the venerable one bears great fruit.”

Atha kho sundarikabhāradvājo brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṃ etadavoca – ‘‘abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama, abhikkantaṃ, bho gotama! Seyyathāpi, bho gotama, nikkujjitaṃ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṃ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṃ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṃ dhāreyya – cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantīti; evamevaṃ bhotā gotamena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṃ bhavantaṃ gotamaṃ saraṇaṃ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṅghañca. Labheyyāhaṃ bhoto gotamassa santike pabbajjaṃ, labheyyaṃ upasampada’’nti. Alattha kho sundarikabhāradvājo brāhmaṇo…pe… arahataṃ ahosīti.

Then the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja said to the Blessed One: “Excellent, Master Gotama! Excellent, Master Gotama! Just as one might set upright what had been overturned, or reveal what was hidden, or show the way to one who was lost, or hold up an oil lamp in the darkness so that those with sight might see forms, in the same way the Dhamma has been clarified by Master Gotama in numerous ways. I go for refuge to Master Gotama, to the Dhamma, and to the Bhikkhu Sangha. May I receive the going forth in Master Gotama’s presence, may I receive the higher ordination?” And the brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja received the going forth ... and became one of the arahants.

Sundarikabhāradvājasuttaṃ catutthaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Sutta to Sundarikabhāradvāja, the fourth, is finished.
