# Snp 5.1 Vatthugāthā
## Snp 5.1 The Introductory Verses

Vatthugāthā

The Introductory Verses

982.

Kosalānaṃ purā rammā, agamā dakkhiṇāpathaṃ;Ākiñcaññaṃ patthayāno, brāhmaṇo mantapāragū.

From the delightful city of the Kosalans, a brahmin who had mastered the sacred verses went to the southern region, aspiring to nothingness.

983.

So assakassa visaye, aḷakassa [muḷakassa (syā.), mūḷhakassa (ka.), maḷakassa (niddesa)] samāsane;Vasi godhāvarīkūle, uñchena ca phalena ca.

In the territory of Assaka, near Aḷaka, he dwelt on the bank of the Godhāvarī, living on gleanings and fruit.

984.

Tasseva upanissāya, gāmo ca vipulo ahu;Tato jātena āyena, mahāyaññamakappayi.

In dependence on him, a large village arose. Then with the income produced from that, he arranged a great sacrifice.

985.

Mahāyaññaṃ yajitvāna, puna pāvisi assamaṃ;Tasmiṃ paṭipaviṭṭhamhi, añño āgañchi brāhmaṇo.

Having performed the great sacrifice, he entered his hermitage again. When he had re-entered, another brahmin arrived.

986.

Ugghaṭṭapādo tasito [tassito (ka.)], paṅkadanto rajassiro;So ca naṃ upasaṅkamma, satāni pañca yācati.

With cracked feet and thirsty, with stained teeth and a dusty head, he approached him and begged for five hundred pieces.

987.

Tamenaṃ bāvarī disvā, āsanena nimantayi;Sukhañca kusalaṃ pucchi, idaṃ vacanamabravi.

Seeing him, Bāvarī invited him with a seat, asked after his comfort and well-being, and spoke these words:

988.

‘‘Yaṃ kho mama deyyadhammaṃ, sabbaṃ visajjitaṃ mayā;Anujānāhi me brahme, natthi pañcasatāni me’’.

“Whatever I had as a gift-worthy item has all been given away by me. Pardon me, brahmin, I do not have five hundred.”

989.

‘‘Sace me yācamānassa, bhavaṃ nānupadassati;Sattame divase tuyhaṃ, muddhā phalatu sattadhā’’.

“If to me who am begging your honor will not give, on the seventh day from today your head will split into seven pieces.”

990.

Abhisaṅkharitvā kuhako, bheravaṃ so akittayi;Tassa taṃ vacanaṃ sutvā, bāvarī dukkhito ahu.

Having fabricated a curse, the charlatan proclaimed this terror. Hearing his words, Bāvarī became distressed.

991.

Ussussati anāhāro, sokasallasamappito;Athopi evaṃ cittassa, jhāne na ramatī mano.

He wasted away, taking no food, pierced by the dart of sorrow. And with his mind in such a state, it took no delight in meditation.

992.

Utrastaṃ dukkhitaṃ disvā, devatā atthakāminī;Bāvariṃ upasaṅkamma, idaṃ vacanamabravi.

Seeing him terrified and distressed, a deity who wished for his welfare approached Bāvarī and spoke these words:

993.

‘‘Na so muddhaṃ pajānāti, kuhako so dhanatthiko;Muddhani muddhapāte vā, ñāṇaṃ tassa na vijjati’’.

“He does not know about the head; he is a charlatan, a seeker of wealth. He has no knowledge about the head or about the splitting of the head.”

994.

‘‘Bhotī carahi jānāsi, taṃ me akkhāhi pucchitā;Muddhaṃ muddhādhipātañca, taṃ suṇoma vaco tava’’.

“Then, good lady, if you know, please tell me what I ask. About the head and the splitting of the head—we would hear your words on this.”

995.

‘‘Ahampetaṃ na jānāmi, ñāṇamettha na vijjati;Muddhani muddhādhipāte ca, jinānaṃ hettha [muddhaṃ muddhādhipāto ca, jinānaṃ heta (sī. syā. pī.)] dassanaṃ’’.

“I do not know this; I have no knowledge about it. Regarding the head and the splitting of the head—this is a matter for the vision of the Conquerors.”

996.

‘‘Atha ko carahi jānāti, asmiṃ pathavimaṇḍale [puthavimaṇḍale (sī. pī.)];Muddhaṃ muddhādhipātañca, taṃ me akkhāhi devate’’.

“Then who on the face of this earth knows about the head and the splitting of the head? Tell me this, O deity.”

997.

‘‘Purā kapilavatthumhā, nikkhanto lokanāyako;Apacco okkākarājassa, sakyaputto pabhaṅkaro.

“From Kapilavatthu in the past went forth the world-leader, a descendant of King Okkāka, the Sakyan son, the light-maker.

998.

‘‘So hi brāhmaṇa sambuddho, sabbadhammāna pāragū;Sabbābhiññābalappatto, sabbadhammesu cakkhumā;Sabbakammakkhayaṃ patto, vimutto upadhikkhaye.

He, brahmin, is a Fully Enlightened One, one who has gone to the far shore of all phenomena. He has attained the powers of all the direct knowledges, he is the one with vision into all things. He has reached the destruction of all kamma, liberated with the destruction of the acquisitions.

999.

‘‘Buddho so bhagavā loke, dhammaṃ deseti cakkhumā;Taṃ tvaṃ gantvāna pucchassu, so te taṃ byākarissati’’.

That Blessed One is the Buddha in the world; the One with Vision teaches the Dhamma. Go and ask him; he will explain it to you.”

1000.

Sambuddhoti vaco sutvā, udaggo bāvarī ahu;Sokassa tanuko āsi, pītiñca vipulaṃ labhi.

Hearing the word “Fully Enlightened One,” Bāvarī became elated. His sorrow became slight, and he gained abundant joy.

1001.

So bāvarī attamano udaggo, taṃ devataṃ pucchati vedajāto;‘‘Katamamhi gāme nigamamhi vā pana, katamamhi vā janapade lokanātho;Yattha gantvāna passemu [gantvā namassemu (sī. syā. pī.)], sambuddhaṃ dvipaduttamaṃ’’ [dvipaduttamaṃ (sī. syā. pī.)],.

Then Bāvarī, glad-minded and elated, filled with rapture, asked the deity: “In what village or market town, or in what country is the Protector of the World, so that we can go and see the Fully Enlightened One, the best of bipeds?”

1002.

‘‘Sāvatthiyaṃ kosalamandire jino, pahūtapañño varabhūrimedhaso;So sakyaputto vidhuro anāsavo, muddhādhipātassa vidū narāsabho’’.

“The Conqueror is in Sāvatthī, in the city of the Kosalans; he is of abundant wisdom, of excellent and vast intelligence. That Sakyan son, untroubled and taintless, that bull among men, is the one who knows about the splitting of the head.”

1003.

Tato āmantayī sisse, brāhmaṇe mantapārage;‘‘Etha māṇavā akkhissaṃ, suṇātha vacanaṃ mama.

Then he addressed his pupils, brahmins who had mastered the sacred verses: “Come, young men, I will tell you; listen to my words.

1004.

‘‘Yasseso dullabho loke, pātubhāvo abhiṇhaso;Svājja lokamhi uppanno, sambuddho iti vissuto;Khippaṃ gantvāna sāvatthiṃ, passavho dvipaduttamaṃ’’.

He whose appearance in the world is extremely rare has now arisen, renowned as the ‘Fully Enlightened One.’ Go quickly to Sāvatthī and behold the best of two-footed beings.”

1005.

‘‘Kathaṃ carahi jānemu, disvā buddhoti brāhmaṇa;Ajānataṃ no pabrūhi, yathā jānemu taṃ mayaṃ’’.

“But how, O brahmin, on seeing him, can we know he is the Buddha? Tell us who do not know, so that we may recognize him.”

1006.

‘‘Āgatāni hi mantesu, mahāpurisalakkhaṇā;Dvattiṃsāni ca [dvittiṃsā ca (sī. syā. pī.), dvittiṃsa tāni (?)] byākkhātā, samattā anupubbaso.

“For in the sacred verses have come down the marks of a great man; thirty-two are explained, complete and in sequence.

1007.

‘‘Yassete honti gattesu, mahāpurisalakkhaṇā;Dveyeva tassa gatiyo, tatiyā hi na vijjati.

He on whose limbs these marks of a great man are found has only two destinations; a third is not found.

1008.

‘‘Sace agāraṃ āvasati [ajjhāvasati (ka.)], vijeyya pathaviṃ imaṃ;Adaṇḍena asatthena, dhammenamanusāsati.

If he dwells in a house, he will conquer this earth, ruling with Dhamma, without rod or sword.

1009.

‘‘Sace ca so pabbajati, agārā anagāriyaṃ;Vivaṭṭacchado [vivattachaddo (sī.)] sambuddho, arahā bhavati anuttaro.

But if he goes forth from the home to the homeless life, with the veil rolled back, he becomes a Fully Enlightened One, an unsurpassed Arahant.

1010.

‘‘Jātiṃ gottañca lakkhaṇaṃ, mante sisse punāpare;Muddhaṃ muddhādhipātañca, manasāyeva pucchatha.

Ask only with your minds about my birth, clan, and marks, about my pupils and their mastery of the sacred verses, and about the head and the splitting of the head.

1011.

‘‘Anāvaraṇadassāvī, yadi buddho bhavissati;Manasā pucchite pañhe, vācāya vissajessati’’.

If he is a Buddha, one who sees without obstruction, he will answer with speech the questions asked by the mind.”

1012.

Bāvarissa vaco sutvā, sissā soḷasa brāhmaṇā;Ajito tissametteyyo, puṇṇako atha mettagū.

Hearing Bāvarī’s words, his sixteen brahmin pupils—Ajita, Tissametteyya, Puṇṇaka, and Mettagū,

1013.

Dhotako upasīvo ca, nando ca atha hemako;Todeyyakappā dubhayo, jatukaṇṇī ca paṇḍito.

Dhotaka and Upasīva, Nanda and Hemaka, both Todeyya and Kappa, and the wise Jatukaṇṇī,

1014.

Bhadrāvudho udayo ca, posālo cāpi brāhmaṇo;Mogharājā ca medhāvī, piṅgiyo ca mahāisi.

Bhadrāvudha and Udaya, the brahmin Posāla, the wise Mogharāja, and the great seer Piṅgiya—

1015.

Paccekagaṇino sabbe, sabbalokassa vissutā;Jhāyī jhānaratā dhīrā, pubbavāsanavāsitā.

All were leaders of their own groups, renowned throughout the world; they were meditators who delighted in the jhānas, wise ones imbued with the impressions of past wholesome deeds.

1016.

Bāvariṃ abhivādetvā, katvā ca naṃ padakkhiṇaṃ;Jaṭājinadharā sabbe, pakkāmuṃ uttarāmukhā.

Having paid homage to Bāvarī and respectfully circumambulated him, all those wearing matted hair and hides departed facing north.

1017.

Aḷakassa patiṭṭhānaṃ, purimāhissatiṃ [purimaṃ māhissatiṃ (sī. pī.), puraṃ māhissatiṃ (syā.)] tadā;Ujjeniñcāpi gonaddhaṃ, vedisaṃ vanasavhayaṃ.

Then to Patiṭṭhāna of Aḷaka and to Māhissatī; and also to Ujjenī, Gonaddha, Vedisa, and Vanasavhaya.

1018.

Kosambiñcāpi sāketaṃ, sāvatthiñca puruttamaṃ;Setabyaṃ kapilavatthuṃ, kusinārañca mandiraṃ.

And to Kosambī and Sāketa, and Sāvatthī, the best of cities; to Setavyā, Kapilavatthu, and the city of Kusinārā.

1019.

Pāvañca bhoganagaraṃ, vesāliṃ māgadhaṃ puraṃ;Pāsāṇakaṃ cetiyañca, ramaṇīyaṃ manoramaṃ.

And to Pāvā and Bhoganagara, to Vesālī and the city of Magadha, and the Pāsāṇaka shrine, delightful and pleasing to the mind.

1020.

Tasitovudakaṃ sītaṃ, mahālābhaṃva vāṇijo;Chāyaṃ ghammābhitattova, turitā pabbatamāruhuṃ.

Like a thirsty man for cool water, like a merchant for great gain, like one scorched by heat for shade, they swiftly ascended the mountain.

1021.

Bhagavā tamhi samaye, bhikkhusaṅghapurakkhato;Bhikkhūnaṃ dhammaṃ deseti, sīhova nadatī vane.

At that time the Blessed One, attended by the Saṅgha of bhikkhus, was teaching the Dhamma to the bhikkhus, roaring like a lion in a forest.

1022.

Ajito addasa buddhaṃ, sataraṃsiṃ [vītaraṃsiṃva (syā.), sataraṃsīva (ka.), pītaraṃsīva (niddesa)] va bhāṇumaṃ;Candaṃ yathā pannarase, pāripūriṃ upāgataṃ.

Ajita saw the Buddha, radiant like the hundred-rayed sun, like the moon on the fifteenth day that has reached its fullness.

1023.

Athassa gatte disvāna, paripūrañca byañjanaṃ;Ekamantaṃ ṭhito haṭṭho, manopañhe apucchatha.

Then, having seen on his body the complete and perfect marks, joyful, he stood to one side and asked the questions conceived in his mind.

1024.

‘‘Ādissa jammanaṃ [jappanaṃ (ka.)] brūhi, gottaṃ brūhi salakkhaṇaṃ [brūhissa lakkhaṇaṃ (niddesa)];Mantesu pāramiṃ brūhi, kati vāceti brāhmaṇo’’.

“Tell me of his birth, declare his clan along with his marks; tell of his perfection in the mantras, and how many that brahmin recites.”

1025.

‘‘Vīsaṃ vassasataṃ āyu, so ca gottena bāvarī;Tīṇissa lakkhaṇā gatte, tiṇṇaṃ vedāna pāragū.

“His lifespan is one hundred and twenty years, and he is Bāvarī by clan. There are three marks on his body; he is a master of the three Vedas.

1026.

‘‘Lakkhaṇe itihāse ca, sanighaṇḍusakeṭubhe;Pañcasatāni vāceti, sadhamme pāramiṃ gato’’.

“In the science of marks and in the legends, together with the glossaries and ritual treatises, he teaches five hundred verses; he has attained perfection in his own doctrine.”

1027.

‘‘Lakkhaṇānaṃ pavicayaṃ, bāvarissa naruttama;Kaṅkhacchida [taṇhacchida (bahūsu)] pakāsehi, mā no kaṅkhāyitaṃ ahu’’.

“O best of men, declare the details of Bāvarī’s marks. O cutter of doubt, make this clear, so that we may not be left in doubt.”

1028.

‘‘Mukhaṃ jivhāya chādeti, uṇṇassa bhamukantare;Kosohitaṃ vatthaguyhaṃ, evaṃ jānāhi māṇava’’.

“He can cover his face with his tongue; he has a tuft of hair between his eyebrows; his male organ is concealed in a sheath. Know it thus, young man.”

1029.

Pucchañhi kiñci asuṇanto, sutvā pañhe viyākate;Vicinteti jano sabbo, vedajāto katañjalī.

Hearing no question whatsoever being asked, but hearing the questions answered, all the people, filled with wonder and with hands raised in reverence, reflected:

1030.

‘‘Ko nu devo vā brahmā vā, indo vāpi sujampati;Manasā pucchite pañhe, kametaṃ paṭibhāsati.

“Who can this be? A deva or a Brahmā? Or Indra, the lord of Sujā? Who is it that replies to questions conceived only in the mind?”

1031.

‘‘Muddhaṃ muddhādhipātañca, bāvarī paripucchati;Taṃ byākarohi bhagavā, kaṅkhaṃ vinaya no ise’’.

“Bāvarī asks about the head and the splitting of the head. Please explain this, O Blessed One. Remove our doubt, O Seer!”

1032.

‘‘Avijjā muddhāti jānāhi, vijjā muddhādhipātinī;Saddhāsatisamādhīhi, chandavīriyena saṃyutā’’.

“Know that ignorance is the ‘head,’ and true knowledge is the ‘splitting of the head,’ conjoined with faith, mindfulness, samādhi, desire, and energy.”

1033.

Tato vedena mahatā, santhambhitvāna māṇavo;Ekaṃsaṃ ajinaṃ katvā, pādesu sirasā pati.

Then the young man, stirred by great joy, arranged his antelope-hide over one shoulder and fell with his head at the Blessed One’s feet.

1034.

‘‘Bāvarī brāhmaṇo bhoto, saha sissehi mārisa;Udaggacitto sumano, pāde vandati cakkhuma’’.

“Venerable sir, the brahmin Bāvarī, together with his disciples, with elated heart and joyful mind, pays homage at your feet, O One with Vision.”

1035.

‘‘Sukhito bāvarī hotu, saha sissehi brāhmaṇo;Tvañcāpi sukhito hohi, ciraṃ jīvāhi māṇava.

“May the brahmin Bāvarī be happy, together with his disciples. And you, too, young man, be happy and live long.”

1036.

‘‘Bāvarissa ca tuyhaṃ vā, sabbesaṃ sabbasaṃsayaṃ;Katāvakāsā pucchavho, yaṃ kiñci manasicchatha’’.

“For Bāvarī, for you, and for all of you, regarding all your doubts, permission has been granted. Ask whatever you wish in your minds.”

1037.

Sambuddhena katokāso, nisīditvāna pañjalī;Ajito paṭhamaṃ pañhaṃ, tattha pucchi tathāgataṃ.

Permission having been granted by the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, Ajita sat down with his hands raised in reverence and there asked the Tathāgata the first question.

Vatthugāthā niṭṭhitā.
The The Introductory Verses, the first is finished.

Vatthugāthā niṭṭhitā.

The Verses on the Preamble are concluded.
