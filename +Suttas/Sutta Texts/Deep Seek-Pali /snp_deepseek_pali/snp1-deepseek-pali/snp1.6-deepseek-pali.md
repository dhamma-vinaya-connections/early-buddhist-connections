# Snp 1.6 Parābhavasuttaṃ

## Snp 1.6 The Discourse on Decline

Evaṃ me sutaṃ – ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Atha kho aññatarā devatā abhikkantāya rattiyā abhikkantavaṇṇā kevalakappaṃ jetavanaṃ obhāsetvā yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ aṭṭhāsi. Ekamantaṃ ṭhitā kho sā devatā bhagavantaṃ gāthāya ajjhabhāsi –

Thus have I heard—At one time the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in Jetavana, Anathapindika’s Monastery. Then a certain deity, when the night was far advanced, with surpassing radiance illuminating the entire Jetavana, approached the Blessed One. Having approached, she paid homage to the Blessed One and stood to one side. Standing to one side, that deity addressed the Blessed One with a verse:

91.

‘‘Parābhavantaṃ purisaṃ, mayaṃ pucchāma gotama [gotamaṃ (sī. syā.)];Bhagavantaṃ [bhavantaṃ (syā. ka.)] puṭṭhumāgamma, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“We ask you, Gotama, about the person who is falling away; having come to the Blessed One to ask, what is the door to ruin?”

92.

‘‘Suvijāno bhavaṃ hoti, suvijāno [duvijāno (syā. ka.)] parābhavo;Dhammakāmo bhavaṃ hoti, dhammadessī parābhavo’’.

“Easy to know is progress, easy to know is decline; one who loves the Dhamma progresses, one who hates the Dhamma declines.”

93.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, paṭhamo so parābhavo;Dutiyaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the first downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the second: what is the door to ruin?”

94.

‘‘Asantassa piyā honti, sante na kurute piyaṃ;Asataṃ dhammaṃ roceti, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“He is fond of the unvirtuous, he does not cherish the virtuous; he approves of the Dhamma of the unvirtuous—that is the door to ruin.”

95.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, dutiyo so parābhavo;Tatiyaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the second downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the third: what is the door to ruin?”

96.

‘‘Niddāsīlī sabhāsīlī, anuṭṭhātā ca yo naro;Alaso kodhapaññāṇo, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“A person who is habitually sleepy, habitually fond of company, inactive, lazy, and whose wisdom is anger—that is the door to ruin.”

97.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, tatiyo so parābhavo;Catutthaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the third downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the fourth: what is the door to ruin?”

98.

‘‘Yo mātaraṃ [yo mātaraṃ vā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] pitaraṃ vā, jiṇṇakaṃ gatayobbanaṃ;Pahu santo na bharati, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“He who, though able, does not support his mother or father, aged and past their youth—that is the door to ruin.”

99.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, catuttho so parābhavo;Pañcamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the fourth downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the fifth: what is the door to ruin?”

100.

‘‘Yo brāhmaṇaṃ [yo brāhmaṇaṃ vā (sī. syā. kaṃ. pī.)] samaṇaṃ vā, aññaṃ vāpi vanibbakaṃ;Musāvādena vañceti, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Whoever deceives a brahmin or ascetic, or any other bhikkhu, with false speech—that is the door to ruin.”

101.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, pañcamo so parābhavo;Chaṭṭhamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the fifth downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the sixth: what is the door to ruin?”

102.

‘‘Pahūtavitto puriso, sahirañño sabhojano;Eko bhuñjati sādūni, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“A man of abundant wealth, with gold and provisions, who eats delicious food alone—that is the door to ruin.”

103.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, chaṭṭhamo so parābhavo;Sattamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the sixth downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the seventh: what is the door to ruin?”

104.

‘‘Jātitthaddho dhanatthaddho, gottatthaddho ca yo naro;Saññātiṃ atimaññeti, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“He who is proud of birth, proud of wealth, and proud of clan, despises his kinsfolk—that is the door to ruin.”

105.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, sattamo so parābhavo;Aṭṭhamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the seventh downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the eighth: what is the door to ruin?”

106.

‘‘Itthidhutto surādhutto, akkhadhutto ca yo naro;Laddhaṃ laddhaṃ vināseti, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“A man addicted to women, addicted to liquor, and addicted to gambling; whatever he gains, he squanders—that is the door to ruin.”

107.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, aṭṭhamo so parābhavo;Navamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the eighth downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the ninth: what is the door to ruin?”

108.

‘‘Sehi dārehi asantuṭṭho [dārehyasantuṭṭho (ka.)], vesiyāsu padussati [padissati (sī.)];Dussati [dissati (sī. pī.)] paradāresu, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Not content with his own wives, he is debauched with prostitutes; he transgresses with others’ wives—that is the door to ruin.”

109.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, navamo so parābhavo;Dasamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the ninth downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the tenth: what is the door to ruin?”

110.

‘‘Atītayobbano poso, āneti timbarutthaniṃ;Tassā issā na supati, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“A man past his youth brings home a young wife; because of jealousy of her he cannot sleep—that is the door to ruin.”

111.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, dasamo so parābhavo;Ekādasamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the tenth downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the eleventh: what is the door to ruin?”

112.

‘‘Itthiṃ soṇḍiṃ vikiraṇiṃ, purisaṃ vāpi tādisaṃ;Issariyasmiṃ ṭhapeti [ṭhāpeti (sī. pī.), thapeti (ka.)], taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“A woman who is a drunkard and a spendthrift, or a man of such nature, whom one establishes in authority—that is the door to ruin.”

113.

‘‘Iti hetaṃ vijānāma, ekādasamo so parābhavo;Dvādasamaṃ bhagavā brūhi, kiṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“Thus we understand this: that is the eleventh downfall. Tell us, Blessed One, the twelfth: what is the door to ruin?”

114.

‘‘Appabhogo mahātaṇho, khattiye jāyate kule;So ca rajjaṃ patthayati, taṃ parābhavato mukhaṃ’’.

“One who possesses little wealth but great craving, born in a warrior clan, yet desires kingship—that is the door to ruin.”

115.

‘‘Ete parābhave loke, paṇḍito samavekkhiya;Ariyo dassanasampanno, sa lokaṃ bhajate siva’’nti.

“Having reflected on these downfalls in the world, the wise one, the noble one endowed with vision, attains security in this world.”

Parābhavasuttaṃ chaṭṭhaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Discourse on Decline, the sixth, is concluded.
