# Snp 2.11 Rāhulasuttaṃ

## Snp 2.11 The Rāhula Sutta

337.

‘‘Kacci abhiṇhasaṃvāsā, nāvajānāsi paṇḍitaṃ;Ukkādhāro [okkādhāro (syā. ka.)] manussānaṃ, kacci apacito tayā’’ [tava (sī. aṭṭha.)].

“Have you, through constant association, not despised the wise? Is he, the torch-bearer for human beings, revered by you?”

338.

‘‘Nāhaṃ abhiṇhasaṃvāsā, avajānāmi paṇḍitaṃ;Ukkādhāro manussānaṃ, niccaṃ apacito mayā’’.

“I do not, through constant association, despise the wise. The torch-bearer for human beings is constantly revered by me.”

339.

‘‘Pañca kāmaguṇe hitvā, piyarūpe manorame;Saddhāya gharā nikkhamma, dukkhassantakaro bhava.

“Having abandoned the five cords of sensual pleasure, which are lovely and alluring, having gone forth from home through faith, be one who makes an end of suffering.”

340.

‘‘Mitte bhajassu kalyāṇe, pantañca sayanāsanaṃ;Vivittaṃ appanigghosaṃ, mattaññū hohi bhojane.

“Associate with good friends, and with a secluded dwelling and lodging, secluded and with little noise. Be moderate in eating.”

341.

‘‘Cīvare piṇḍapāte ca, paccaye sayanāsane;Etesu taṇhaṃ mākāsi, mā lokaṃ punarāgami.

“Do not generate craving for robes, almsfood, requisites, or lodging. Do not return to the world again.”

342.

‘‘Saṃvuto pātimokkhasmiṃ, indriyesu ca pañcasu;Sati kāyagatātyatthu, nibbidābahulo bhava.

“Be restrained in the Pātimokkha and in the five sense faculties. Let mindfulness of the body be well-established. Be abundant in disenchantment.”

343.

‘‘Nimittaṃ parivajjehi, subhaṃ rāgūpasañhitaṃ;Asubhāya cittaṃ bhāvehi, ekaggaṃ susamāhitaṃ.

“Avoid the beautiful sign associated with lust. Develop the mind on the unattractive, single-pointed and well-collected.”

344.

‘‘Animittañca bhāvehi, mānānusayamujjaha;Tato mānābhisamayā, upasanto carissatī’’ti.

“Develop the signless, abandon the underlying tendency to conceit. Then, through the full comprehension of conceit, one will wander at peace.”

Itthaṃ sudaṃ bhagavā āyasmantaṃ rāhulaṃ imāhi gāthāhi abhiṇhaṃ ovadatīti.

Thus, indeed, the Blessed One frequently exhorted the Venerable Rāhula with these verses.

Rāhulasuttaṃ ekādasamaṃ niṭṭhitaṃ.

The Rāhula Sutta, the eleventh, is concluded.
