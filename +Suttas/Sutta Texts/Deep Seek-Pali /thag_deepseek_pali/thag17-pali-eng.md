# Thag 17. Tiṃsanipāto
## 17. The Chapter of Seventeens

949.
Pāsādike bahū disvā, bhāvitatte susaṃvute;Isi paṇḍarasagotto [paṇḍarassa gotto (sī.)], apucchi phussasavhayaṃ. Seeing one who was greatly inspiring, with a developed self and well restrained, the sage of the Paṇḍarasa clan asked the one named Phussa:


950.
‘‘Kiṃchandā kimadhippāyā, kimākappā bhavissare;Anāgatamhi kālamhi, taṃ me akkhāhi pucchito’’.

“Of what inclination, of what intention, of what conduct will they be in the future time? Having been asked, declare this to me.”

951.
‘‘Suṇohi vacanaṃ mayhaṃ, isipaṇḍarasavhaya;Sakkaccaṃ upadhārehi, ācikkhissāmyanāgataṃ. “Listen to my word, O sage of the Paṇḍarasa clan; attend carefully. I will declare what will be in the future.


952.
‘‘Kodhanā upanāhī ca, makkhī thambhī saṭhā bahū;Ussukī nānāvādā ca, bhavissanti anāgate.

“In the future many will be wrathful and hostile, contemptuous, stubborn, and deceitful; they will be envious and will hold diverse views.

953.
‘‘Aññātamānino dhamme, gambhīre tīragocarā;Lahukā agaru dhamme, aññamaññamagāravā.

“Conceited about their knowledge of the profound Dhamma, they will only frequent its shore. Frivolous, with no respect for the Dhamma, they will be disrespectful toward one another.

954.
‘‘Bahū ādīnavā loke, uppajjissantyanāgate;Sudesitaṃ imaṃ dhammaṃ, kilesessanti [kilesissanti (sī.), kilisissanti (syā. ka.)] dummatī.

“Many dangers will arise in the world in the future. The foolish ones will defile this well-expounded Dhamma.

955.
‘‘Guṇahīnāpi saṅghamhi, voharantā visāradā;Balavanto bhavissanti, mukharā assutāvino.

“Though lacking in virtue, they will be bold in speech within the Sangha and will become powerful—loud-mouthed and unlearned.

956.
‘‘Guṇavantopi saṅghamhi, voharantā yathātthato;Dubbalā te bhavissanti, hirīmanā anatthikā.

“But the virtuous in the Sangha, who speak according to the truth, will become weak—they will be conscientious and unassertive.

957.
‘‘Rajataṃ jātarūpañca, khettaṃ vatthumajeḷakaṃ;Dāsidāsañca dummedhā, sādiyissantyanāgate.

“In the future the foolish will accept silver and gold, fields and property, goats and sheep, and male and female slaves.

958.
‘‘Ujjhānasaññino bālā, sīlesu asamāhitā;Unnaḷā vicarissanti, kalahābhiratā magā.

“The fools will be disposed to finding fault, uncomposed in their virtue. Arrogant and delighting in quarrels, they will wander about like wild beasts.

959.
‘‘Uddhatā ca bhavissanti, nīlacīvarapārutā;Kuhā thaddhā lapā siṅgī, carissantyariyā viya.

“They will be arrogant, clad in blue robes. Deceitful, stubborn, talkative, and haughty, they will wander about as if they were noble ones.

960.
‘‘Telasaṇṭhehi kesehi, capalā añjanakkhikā;Rathiyāya gamissanti, dantavaṇṇikapārutā.

“With hair smoothed by oil, fickle, with eyes painted with eyeliner, they will walk the streets clad in ivory-colored robes.

961.
‘‘Ajegucchaṃ vimuttehi, surattaṃ arahaddhajaṃ;Jigucchissanti kāsāvaṃ, odātesu samucchitā [odāte susamucchitā (sī.)].

“They will be disgusted by the ochre robe—the well-dyed banner of the arahants, which is not despised by the liberated—and they will be infatuated with white robes.

962.
‘‘Lābhakāmā bhavissanti, kusītā hīnavīriyā;Kicchantā vanapatthāni, gāmantesu vasissare.

“Desirous of gain, they will be lazy and lacking in energy. Finding the forest wildernesses wearisome, they will dwell on the outskirts of villages.

963.
‘‘Ye ye lābhaṃ labhissanti, micchājīvaratā sadā;Te teva anusikkhantā, bhajissanti asaṃyatā.

“The unrestrained will always delight in wrong livelihood; imitating those who get gain, they will associate with them.

964.
‘‘Ye ye alābhino lābhaṃ, na te pujjā bhavissare;Supesalepi te dhīre, sevissanti na te tadā.

“Those who do not receive gain will not be revered. People then will not attend upon those wise and amiable ones.

965.
‘‘Milakkhurajanaṃ rattaṃ [pilakkharajanaṃ rattaṃ (?)], garahantā sakaṃ dhajaṃ;Titthiyānaṃ dhajaṃ keci, dhārissantyavadātakaṃ.

“Reproaching their own banner, dyed with an impure dye, some will wear the white banner of the sectarians.

966.
‘‘Agāravo ca kāsāve, tadā tesaṃ bhavissati;Paṭisaṅkhā ca kāsāve, bhikkhūnaṃ na bhavissati.

“Then they will have disrespect for the ochre robe, and the bhikkhus will have no proper reflection concerning it.

967.
‘‘Abhibhūtassa dukkhena, sallaviddhassa ruppato;Paṭisaṅkhā mahāghorā, nāgassāsi acintiyā.

“For the elephant, overcome by suffering, pierced by a dart and afflicted with pain, the reflection that arose was terrible and inconceivable.

968.
‘‘Chaddanto hi tadā disvā, surattaṃ arahaddhajaṃ;Tāvadeva bhaṇī gāthā, gajo atthopasaṃhitā’’.

“For then the elephant Chaddanta, seeing the well-dyed banner of the arahants, immediately spoke these verses fraught with meaning:

969.
[dha. pa. 9; jā. 1.2.141; 1.16.122] ‘‘Anikkasāvo kāsāvaṃ, yo vatthaṃ paridhassati [paridahissati (sī. syā.)];Apeto damasaccena, na so kāsāvamarahati.

“He who would wear the ochre robe while still without stain-removing virtues, devoid of self-control and truth, is not worthy of the ochre robe.

970.
‘‘Yo ca vantakāsāvassa, sīlesu susamāhito;Upeto damasaccena, sa ve kāsāvamarahati.

“But he who has purged his stains, is well established in the virtues, and is endowed with self-control and truth, he is truly worthy of the ochre robe.”

971.
‘‘Vipannasīlo dummedho, pākaṭo kāmakāriyo;Vibbhantacitto nissukko, na so kāsāvamarahati.

“One of broken virtue, foolish, openly acting on his desires, with a confused mind and lacking purity—he is not worthy of the ochre robe.

972.
‘‘Yo ca sīlena sampanno, vītarāgo samāhito;Odātamanasaṅkappo, sa ve kāsāvamarahati.

“But one who is accomplished in virtue, free from passion and composed, with pure thoughts and intentions—he is truly worthy of the ochre robe.”

973.
‘‘Uddhato unnaḷo bālo, sīlaṃ yassa na vijjati;Odātakaṃ arahati, kāsāvaṃ kiṃ karissati.

“For the arrogant, insolent fool in whom virtue is not found, what will the ochre robe accomplish? He is worthy only of the white.”

974.
‘‘Bhikkhū ca bhikkhuniyo ca, duṭṭhacittā anādarā;Tādīnaṃ mettacittānaṃ, niggaṇhissantyanāgate. “In the future, bhikkhus and bhikkhunīs with corrupt minds and no respect will rebuke those of compassionate mind who are of such a kind.


975.
‘‘Sikkhāpentāpi therehi, bālā cīvaradhāraṇaṃ;Na suṇissanti dummedhā, pākaṭā kāmakāriyā.

“Though trained by the elders concerning the wearing of the robe, the foolish ones of dull intellect will not listen, openly acting as they please.”

976.
‘‘Te tathā sikkhitā bālā, aññamaññaṃ agāravā;Nādiyissantupajjhāye, khaḷuṅko viya sārathiṃ.

“Those fools, trained in such a way and disrespectful toward one another, will not heed their preceptors, just as a rogue horse ignores its charioteer.

977.
‘‘Evaṃ anāgataddhānaṃ, paṭipatti bhavissati;Bhikkhūnaṃ bhikkhunīnañca, patte kālamhi pacchime. “Such will be the practice of bhikkhus and bhikkhunīs in the future, when the final age has arrived.


978.
‘‘Purā āgacchate etaṃ, anāgataṃ mahabbhayaṃ;Subbacā hotha sakhilā, aññamaññaṃ sagāravā.

“Before this great future danger arrives, be easy to admonish, be amiable, and be respectful toward one another.

979.
‘‘Mettacittā kāruṇikā, hotha sīlesu saṃvutā;Āraddhavīriyā pahitattā, niccaṃ daḷhaparakkamā.

“Dwell with minds of loving-kindness, be compassionate and restrained in the virtues. Be aroused in energy, resolute, and always firm in your striving.

980.
‘‘Pamādaṃ bhayato disvā, appamādañca khemato;Bhāvethaṭṭhaṅgikaṃ maggaṃ, phusantā amataṃ pada’’nti.

“Seeing negligence as a danger and diligence as security, develop the Noble Eightfold Path, thereby attaining the deathless state.” … Phusso thero…. … The Elder Phussa … 2. Sāriputtattheragāthā 2.The Verses of the Elder Sāriputta

981.
‘‘Yathācārī yathāsato satīmā, yatasaṅkappajjhāyi appamatto;Ajjhattarato samāhitatto, eko santusito tamāhu bhikkhuṃ.

“One who comports himself properly, who is mindful, with intentions restrained, meditative, and diligent; one who delights inwardly, with a composed mind, solitary and content—him they call a bhikkhu.”

982.
‘‘Allaṃ sukkhaṃ vā bhuñjanto, na bāḷhaṃ suhito siyā;Ūnūdaro mitāhāro, sato bhikkhu paribbaje.

“Whether eating moist or dry food, one should not be too full. With stomach not full, moderate in food, a mindful bhikkhu should wander on.”

983.
‘‘Cattāro pañca ālope, abhutvā udakaṃ pive;Alaṃ phāsuvihārāya, pahitattassa bhikkhuno.

“Leaving four or five mouthfuls uneaten, one should then drink water. This is enough for the comfortable abiding of a resolute bhikkhu.”

984.
‘‘Kappiyaṃ taṃ ce chādeti, cīvaraṃ idamatthikaṃ [idamatthitaṃ (sī.)];Alaṃ phāsuvihārāya, pahitattassa bhikkhuno.

“The robe that is sufficient for his need, if it provides a proper covering—this is enough for the comfortable abiding of a resolute bhikkhu.”

985.
‘‘Pallaṅkena nisinnassa, jaṇṇuke nābhivassati;Alaṃ phāsuvihārāya, pahitattassa bhikkhuno.

“A shelter where the rain does not fall on the knees of one sitting cross-legged—this is enough for the comfortable abiding of a resolute bhikkhu.”

986.
[saṃ. ni. 4.253; itivu. 53] ‘‘Yo sukhaṃ dukkhato adda, dukkhamaddakkhi sallato;Ubhayantarena [ubhayamantare (sī.)] nāhosi, kena lokasmi kiṃ siyā.

“One who has seen pleasure as suffering and suffering as a dart, and is not found between the two—by what in the world could he be measured? For whom would he be anything?”

987.
‘‘Mā me kadāci pāpiccho, kusīto hīnavīriyo;Appassuto anādaro, kena lokasmi kiṃ siyā.

“May I never be one with evil wishes, lazy, lacking in energy, unlearned, and disrespectful. For such a one, by what and for whom could he be anything in the world?”

988.
‘‘Bahussuto ca medhāvī, sīlesu susamāhito;Cetosamathamanuyutto, api muddhani tiṭṭhatu.

“One who is learned and wise, well established in the virtues, and devoted to the calm of mind—he stands at the very pinnacle.”

989.
‘‘Yo papañcamanuyutto, papañcābhirato mago;Virādhayī so nibbānaṃ, yogakkhemaṃ anuttaraṃ. “One who is given to mental proliferation, a wild beast delighting in proliferation, fails to attain Nibbāna, the unsurpassed security from bondage.


990.
‘‘Yo ca papañcaṃ hitvāna, nippapañcapathe rato;Ārādhayī so nibbānaṃ, yogakkhemaṃ anuttaraṃ. “But one who has abandoned mental proliferation and delights in the path free from it, he attains Nibbāna, the unsurpassed security from bondage.”


991.
[dha. pa. 98] ‘‘Gāme vā yadi vāraññe, ninne vā yadi vā thale;Yattha arahanto viharanti, taṃ bhūmirāmaṇeyyakaṃ.

“Whether in a village or in the forest, in a valley or on the plain, wherever the arahants dwell, that is a delightful place.”

992.
‘‘Ramaṇīyāni araññāni, yattha na ramatī jano;Vītarāgā ramissanti, na te kāmagavesino.

“Delightful are the forests where the worldly folk find no delight. There the passion-free will delight, for they are not seekers of sensual pleasures.”

993.
[dha. pa. 76] ‘‘Nidhīnaṃva pavattāraṃ, yaṃ passe vajjadassinaṃ;Niggayhavādiṃ medhāviṃ, tādisaṃ paṇḍitaṃ bhaje;Tādisaṃ bhajamānassa, seyyo hoti na pāpiyo.

“Should one see a wise person who points out faults and reproves, one should associate with such a sage, a revealer of treasures. For one who associates with such a person, it is better, not worse.”

994.
[dha. pa. 77] ‘‘Ovadeyyānusāseyya, asabbhā ca nivāraye;Satañhi so piyo hoti, asataṃ hoti appiyo.

“Let him advise, let him instruct, let him restrain one from what is improper. He is dear to the good and undear to the bad.”

995.
‘‘Aññassa bhagavā buddho, dhammaṃ desesi cakkhumā;Dhamme desiyamānamhi, sotamodhesimatthiko;Taṃ me amoghaṃ savanaṃ, vimuttomhi anāsavo.

“The Blessed One, the Buddha, the One with Vision, taught the Dhamma for the welfare of another. While the Dhamma was being taught, I, seeking the goal, inclined my ear. My listening was not in vain: I am liberated, taintless.”

996.
‘‘Neva pubbenivāsāya, napi dibbassa cakkhuno;Cetopariyāya iddhiyā, cutiyā upapattiyā;Sotadhātuvisuddhiyā, paṇidhī me na vijjati [kathā. 378].

“Not for the recollection of past lives, nor for the divine eye, nor for psychic powers, nor for knowing the minds of others, nor for the passing away and rebirth of beings, nor for the purified divine ear element—no aspiration for these is found in me.”

997.
‘‘Rukkhamūlaṃva nissāya, muṇḍo saṅghāṭipāruto;Paññāya uttamo thero, upatissova [upatisso ca (sī. ka.)] jhāyati.

“Relying on the root of a tree, shaven-headed, clad in his outer robe, the elder Upatissa, supreme in wisdom, meditates.”

998.
‘‘Avitakkaṃ samāpanno, sammāsambuddhasāvako;Ariyena tuṇhībhāvena, upeto hoti tāvade.

“Having attained the state free from thought, a disciple of the Perfectly Enlightened One is at that moment endowed with noble silence.”

999.
[udā. 24] ‘‘Yathāpi pabbato selo, acalo suppatiṭṭhito;Evaṃ mohakkhayā bhikkhu, pabbatova na vedhati.

“Just as a rocky mountain is unmoving and well established, so, through the destruction of delusion, a bhikkhu, like a mountain, does not tremble.”

1000.
‘‘Anaṅgaṇassa posassa, niccaṃ sucigavesino;Vālaggamattaṃ pāpassa, abbhamattaṃva khāyati.

“To a person without blemish, who is ever seeking purity, an evil deed the size of a hair-tip appears as large as a cloud.”

1001.
‘‘Nābhinandāmi maraṇaṃ, nābhinandāmi jīvitaṃ;Nikkhipissaṃ imaṃ kāyaṃ, sampajāno patissato.

“I do not delight in death, I do not delight in life. I will lay aside this body, mindful and clearly comprehending.”

1002.
‘‘Nābhinandāmi maraṇaṃ, nābhinandāmi jīvitaṃ;Kālañca paṭikaṅkhāmi, nibbisaṃ bhatako yathā.

“I do not delight in death, I do not delight in life; but I await the time, like a servant waiting for his wages.”

1003.
‘‘Ubhayena midaṃ maraṇameva, nāmaraṇaṃ pacchā vā pure vā;Paṭipajjatha mā vinassatha, khaṇo vo mā upaccagā.

“Death comes in any case, there is no non-dying, whether later or before. Strive on, lest you perish; do not let the moment pass you by.”

1004.
‘‘Nagaraṃ yathā paccantaṃ, guttaṃ santarabāhiraṃ;Evaṃ gopetha attānaṃ, khaṇo vo mā upaccagā;Khaṇātītā hi socanti, nirayamhi samappitā.

“Just as a frontier city is guarded within and without, so you should guard yourselves. Do not let this moment pass you by, for those who miss the moment grieve when they are consigned to hell.”

1005.
‘‘Upasanto uparato, mantabhāṇī [mattabhāṇī (sī.)] anuddhato;Dhunāti pāpake dhamme, dumapattaṃva māluto.

“Calm and restrained, speaking thoughtfully, not arrogant, one shakes off evil states as the wind shakes a leaf from a tree.”

1006.
‘‘Upasanto uparato, mantabhāṇī anuddhato;Appāsi [abbahi (syā.), abhāsi (?)] pāpake dhamme, dumapattaṃva māluto.

“Calm and restrained, speaking thoughtfully, not arrogant, one has shed evil states as the wind sheds a leaf from a tree.”

1007.
‘‘Upasanto anāyāso, vippasanno anāvilo;Kalyāṇasīlo medhāvī, dukkhassantakaro siyā.

“Calm and untroubled, serene and clear, virtuous and wise—such a one would make an end of suffering.”

1008.
‘‘Na vissase ekatiyesu evaṃ, agārisu pabbajitesu cāpi;Sādhūpi hutvā na asādhu honti, asādhu hutvā puna sādhu honti.

“One should not place trust even in some householders and renunciants. For even those who were good become bad, and those who were bad can again become good.”

1009.
‘‘Kāmacchando ca byāpādo, thinamiddhañca bhikkhuno;Uddhaccaṃ vicikicchā ca, pañcete cittakelisā.

“Sensual desire and ill will, sloth and torpor for a bhikkhu, restlessness and doubt—these five are defilements of the mind.”

1010.
‘‘Yassa sakkariyamānassa, asakkārena cūbhayaṃ;Samādhi na vikampati, appamādavihārino.

“For one dwelling in diligence, samādhi does not waver whether one is being honored or dishonored.”

1011.
‘‘Taṃ jhāyinaṃ sātatikaṃ, sukhumadiṭṭhivipassakaṃ;Upādānakkhayārāmaṃ, āhu sappuriso iti.

“That persevering meditator who sees with subtle insight, one who delights in the destruction of clinging—him they call a superior person.”

1012.
‘‘Mahāsamuddo pathavī, pabbato anilopi ca;Upamāya na yujjanti, satthu varavimuttiyā.

“The great ocean, the earth, a mountain, and the wind—none of these is fit for a simile with the Teacher’s supreme liberation.”

1013.
‘‘Cakkānuvattako thero, mahāñāṇī samāhito;Pathavāpaggisamāno, na rajjati na dussati.

The elder who keeps the Wheel rolling, of great wisdom and composed; like earth, water, and fire, he is neither impassioned nor repelled.

1014.
‘‘Paññāpāramitaṃ patto, mahābuddhi mahāmati;Ajaḷo jaḷasamāno, sadā carati nibbuto.

Having reached the perfection of wisdom, of great intelligence and great understanding; though not dull, he appears as if dull, and ever fares along, quenched.

1015.
‘‘Pariciṇṇo mayā satthā…pe… bhavanetti samūhatā.

The Teacher has been served by me … the guide to existence has been uprooted.

1016.
‘‘Sampādethappamādena, esā me anusāsanī;Handāhaṃ parinibbissaṃ, vippamuttomhi sabbadhī’’ti. Strive on with heedfulness, this is my instruction. Come now, I will attain final Nibbāna; I am completely liberated in every way. … Sāriputto thero…. … The Elder Sāriputta … 3. Ānandattheragāthā 3.The Verses of the Elder Ānanda


1017.
‘‘Pisuṇena ca kodhanena ca, maccharinā ca vibhūtanandinā;Sakhitaṃ na kareyya paṇḍito, pāpo kāpurisena saṅgamo.

A wise person should not form a friendship with a slanderer, an angry person, a miser, or one who delights in another's ruin; for association with a bad person is evil.

1018.
‘‘Saddhena ca pesalena ca, paññavatā bahussutena ca;Sakhitaṃ kareyya paṇḍito, bhaddo sappurisena saṅgamo.

A wise person should form a friendship with one who is faithful, virtuous, wise, and learned; for association with a good person is a blessing.

1019.
‘‘Passa cittakataṃ bimbaṃ…pe… yassa natthi dhuvaṃ ṭhiti.

Behold this decorated image … for which there is no lasting stability.

1020.
‘‘Passa cittakataṃ bimbaṃ…pe… vatthehi sobhati.

Behold this decorated image … it shines with its garments.

1021.
‘‘Alattakakatā …pe… no ca pāragavesino.

Dyed with lac … yet they are not seekers of the further shore.

1022.
‘‘Aṭṭhapadakatā…pe… no ca pāragavesino.

Made into a game board … yet they are not seekers of the further shore.

1023.
‘‘Añjanīva navā…pe… no ca pāragavesino.

Like a new collyrium pot … yet they are not seekers of the further shore.

1024.
‘‘Bahussuto cittakathī, buddhassa paricārako;Pannabhāro visaññutto, seyyaṃ kappeti gotamo.

Very learned and an eloquent speaker, the attendant of the Buddha, with burden laid down and detached, Gotama lies down to rest.

1025.
‘‘Khīṇāsavo visaññutto, saṅgātīto sunibbuto;Dhāreti antimaṃ dehaṃ, jātimaraṇapāragū.

With taints destroyed, detached, having gone beyond attachments, fully quenched, he bears his final body, one who has crossed beyond birth and death.

1026.
‘‘Yasmiṃ patiṭṭhitā dhammā, buddhassādiccabandhuno;Nibbānagamane magge, soyaṃ tiṭṭhati gotamo. He in whom the teachings of the Buddha, kinsman of the sun, are established, on the path leading to Nibbāna—that Gotama stands firm.


1027.
‘‘Dvāsīti buddhato gaṇhiṃ, dve sahassāni bhikkhuto;Caturāsītisahassāni, ye me dhammā pavattino.

Eighty-two thousand teachings I received from the Buddha, and two thousand from the bhikkhus; these eighty-four thousand teachings are current.

1028.
‘‘Appassutāyaṃ puriso, balibaddova jīrati;Maṃsāni tassa vaḍḍhanti, paññā tassa na vaḍḍhati.

This person of little learning ages like an ox: his flesh increases, but his wisdom does not grow.

1029.
‘‘Bahussuto appassutaṃ, yo sutenātimaññati;Andho padīpadhārova, tatheva paṭibhāti maṃ.

The learned one who despises another of little learning on account of his own learning seems to me just like a blind man holding a lamp.

1030.
‘‘Bahussutaṃ upāseyya, sutañca na vināsaye;Taṃ mūlaṃ brahmacariyassa, tasmā dhammadharo siyā.

One should associate with a learned person and not let one's learning be lost. This is the root of the holy life; therefore one should be a bearer of the Dhamma.

1031.
‘‘Pubbāparaññū atthaññū, niruttipadakovido;Suggahītañca gaṇhāti, atthañcopaparikkhati.

Knowing what comes before and after, knowing the meaning, skilled in language and its phrasing, one grasps it well and investigates the meaning.

1032.
‘‘Khantyā chandikato [khantiyā chandito (?)] hoti, ussahitvā tuleti taṃ;Samaye so padahati, ajjhattaṃ susamāhito.

Through patience one becomes keen; having striven, one weighs it. At the right time one exerts oneself, inwardly well composed.

1033.
‘‘Bahussutaṃ dhammadharaṃ, sappaññaṃ buddhasāvakaṃ;Dhammaviññāṇamākaṅkhaṃ, taṃ bhajetha tathāvidhaṃ.

If you long for understanding of the Dhamma, you should associate with such a person: one who is learned, a bearer of the Dhamma, a wise disciple of the Buddha.

1034.
‘‘Bahussuto dhammadharo, kosārakkho mahesino;Cakkhu sabbassa lokassa, pūjanīyo bahussuto.

Learned, a bearer of the Dhamma, the treasury-guardian of the great seer, the eye for the entire world—a learned person is worthy of veneration.

1035.
‘‘Dhammārāmo dhammarato, dhammaṃ anuvicintayaṃ;Dhammaṃ anussaraṃ bhikkhu, saddhammā na parihāyati.

A bhikkhu who delights in the Dhamma, rejoices in the Dhamma, contemplates the Dhamma, and recollects the Dhamma, does not fall away from the good Dhamma.

1036.
‘‘Kāyamaccheragaruno [garuko (sī.)], hiyyamāne [hiyyamāno (sī.)] anuṭṭhahe;Sarīrasukhagiddhassa, kuto samaṇaphāsutā.

For one who esteems the body and is stingy, who does not exert himself when he is declining, who is greedy for bodily pleasure—whence can there be the comfort of a recluse?

1037.
‘‘Na pakkhanti disā sabbā, dhammā na paṭibhanti maṃ;Gate kalyāṇamittamhi, andhakāraṃva khāyati.

The directions are not clear to me, the teachings are no longer evident; now that my good friend is gone, it seems to be just darkness.

1038.
‘‘Abbhatītasahāyassa, atītagatasatthuno;Natthi etādisaṃ mittaṃ, yathā kāyagatā sati.

For one whose companion has passed away, whose Teacher is gone, there is no friend like mindfulness directed to the body.

1039.
‘‘Ye purāṇā atītā te, navehi na sameti me;Svajja ekova jhāyāmi, vassupetova pakkhimā.

Those of old have passed away; I do not get along with the new ones. Today I meditate all alone, like a bird that has entered the rains.

1040.
‘‘Dassanāya abhikkante, nānāverajjake bahū;Mā vārayittha sotāro, passantu samayo mamaṃ.

Many from various kingdoms are coming to see me. Do not obstruct them, O hearers. Let them see me; this is the time for it.

1041.
‘‘Dassanāya abhikkante, nānāverajjake puthu;Karoti satthā okāsaṃ, na nivāreti cakkhumā.

When many from various kingdoms come for a sight of him, the Teacher grants them an opportunity; the One with Vision does not prevent them.

1042.
‘‘Paṇṇavīsativassāni, sekhabhūtassa me sato;Na kāmasaññā uppajji, passa dhammasudhammataṃ.

For twenty-five years, while I was a mindful trainee, no perception of sensual desire arose in me. Behold the excellence of the Dhamma!

1043.
‘‘Paṇṇavīsativassāni, sekhabhūtassa me sato;Na dosasaññā uppajji, passa dhammasudhammataṃ.

For twenty-five years, while I was a mindful trainee, no perception of ill will arose in me. Behold the excellence of the Dhamma!

1044.
‘‘Paṇṇavīsativassāni, bhagavantaṃ upaṭṭhahiṃ;Mettena kāyakammena, chāyāva anapāyinī [anupāyinī (syā. ka.)].

For twenty-five years I attended on the Blessed One with bodily deeds of loving-kindness, like a shadow that never departs.

1045.
‘‘Paṇṇavīsativassāni, bhagavantaṃ upaṭṭhahiṃ;Mettena vacīkammena, chāyāva anapāyinī.

For twenty-five years I attended on the Blessed One with verbal deeds of loving-kindness, like a shadow that never departs.

1046.
‘‘Paṇṇavīsativassāni, bhagavantaṃ upaṭṭhahiṃ;Mettena manokammena, chāyāva anapāyinī.

For twenty-five years I attended on the Blessed One with mental deeds of loving-kindness, like a shadow that never departs.

1047.
‘‘Buddhassa caṅkamantassa, piṭṭhito anucaṅkamiṃ;Dhamme desiyamānamhi, ñāṇaṃ me udapajjatha.

I walked behind the Buddha as he was walking; when the Dhamma was being taught, knowledge arose in me.

1048.
‘‘Ahaṃ sakaraṇīyomhi, sekho appattamānaso;Satthu ca parinibbānaṃ, yo amhaṃ anukampako. I still have something to do, a trainee who has not reached the goal; and the Teacher, who was compassionate to us, has attained final Nibbāna.


1049.
‘‘Tadāsi yaṃ bhiṃsanakaṃ, tadāsi lomahaṃsanaṃ;Sabbākāravarūpete, sambuddhe parinibbute. Then there was terror, then there was horripilation, when the Perfectly Enlightened One, endowed with all the finest qualities, attained final Nibbāna.


1050.
‘‘Bahussuto dhammadharo, kosārakkho mahesino;Cakkhu sabbassa lokassa, ānando parinibbuto. Learned, a bearer of the Dhamma, the treasury-guardian of the great seer, the eye for the entire world—Ānanda has attained final Nibbāna.


1051.
‘‘Bahussuto dhammadharo, kosārakkho mahesino;Cakkhu sabbassa lokassa, andhakāre tamonudo.

Learned, a bearer of the Dhamma, the treasury-guardian of the great seer, the eye for the entire world, a dispeller of gloom in the darkness.

1052.
‘‘Gatimanto satimanto, dhitimanto ca yo isi;Saddhammadhārako thero, ānando ratanākaro. That sage who possessed good deportment, mindfulness, and resolution, the elder, the bearer of the good Dhamma, Ānanda, was a mine of gems.


1053.
‘‘Pariciṇṇo mayā satthā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsanaṃ;Ohito garuko bhāro, natthi dāni punabbhavo’’ti.

The Teacher has been served by me, the Buddha’s teaching has been done; the heavy burden is laid down, now there is no more renewed existence. … Ānando thero…. … The Elder Ānanda … Tiṃsanipāto niṭṭhito. The Section of the Thirties is finished. Tatruddānaṃ – The summary: Phussopatisso ānando, tayotime pakittitā;Gāthāyo tattha saṅkhātā, sataṃ pañca ca uttarīti; Phussa, Upatissa, and Ānanda, these three have been proclaimed. The verses therein are reckoned as one hundred and five.
