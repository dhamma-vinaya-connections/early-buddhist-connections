# Thag 12. Dvādasakanipāto
## 12. The Chapter of Twelves

608.
‘‘Sīlamevidha sikkhetha, asmiṃ loke susikkhitaṃ;Sīlaṃ hi sabbasampattiṃ, upanāmeti sevitaṃ.

“One should train in virtue right here in this world; being well-trained in it, and having cultivated it, virtue leads to all accomplishment.”

609.
‘‘Sīlaṃ rakkheyya medhāvī, patthayāno tayo sukhe;Pasaṃsaṃ vittilābhañca, pecca sagge pamodanaṃ [pecca sagge ca modanaṃ (sī. pī.)].

“A wise person aspiring for three kinds of happiness should guard his virtue: praise, the acquisition of wealth, and, after passing away, rejoicing in heaven.”

610.
‘‘Sīlavā hi bahū mitte, saññamenādhigacchati;Dussīlo pana mittehi, dhaṃsate pāpamācaraṃ.

“A virtuous person gains many friends through self-restraint; but an immoral person, by acting evilly, is deprived of friends.”

611.
‘‘Avaṇṇañca akittiñca, dussīlo labhate naro;Vaṇṇaṃ kittiṃ pasaṃsañca, sadā labhati sīlavā.

“An immoral person gains dispraise and ill-repute, while a virtuous person always gains praise, renown, and commendation.”

612.
‘‘Ādi sīlaṃ patiṭṭhā ca, kalyāṇānañca mātukaṃ;Pamukhaṃ sabbadhammānaṃ, tasmā sīlaṃ visodhaye.

“Virtue is the beginning, the foundation, the mother of all things good, and the foremost of all wholesome states. Therefore one should purify one’s virtue.”

613.
‘‘Velā ca saṃvaraṃ sīlaṃ [saṃvaro sīlaṃ (sī.), saṃvarasīlaṃ (sī. aṭṭha.)], cittassa abhihāsanaṃ;Titthañca sabbabuddhānaṃ, tasmā sīlaṃ visodhaye.

“Virtue is a boundary and a restraint, a delight for the mind, and the ford of all the Buddhas. Therefore one should purify one’s virtue.”

614.
‘‘Sīlaṃ balaṃ appaṭimaṃ, sīlaṃ āvudhamuttamaṃ;Sīlamābharaṇaṃ seṭṭhaṃ, sīlaṃ kavacamabbhutaṃ.

“Virtue is an incomparable power, virtue is the supreme weapon; virtue is the best adornment, virtue is a marvelous armor.”

615.
‘‘Sīlaṃ setu mahesakkho, sīlaṃ gandho anuttaro;Sīlaṃ vilepanaṃ seṭṭhaṃ, yena vāti disodisaṃ.

“Virtue is a mighty bridge, virtue is the supreme fragrance, virtue is the best unguent with which one wafts in all directions.”

616.
‘‘Sīlaṃ sambalamevaggaṃ, sīlaṃ pātheyyamuttamaṃ;Sīlaṃ seṭṭho ativāho, yena yāti disodisaṃ.

“Virtue is the best provision, virtue is the supreme travel-supply, virtue is the best guide by which one travels in all directions.”

617.
‘‘Idheva nindaṃ labhati, peccāpāye ca dummano;Sabbattha dummano bālo, sīlesu asamāhito.

“Right here one meets with blame, and is miserable after death in a woeful state; everywhere the fool, uncollected in virtue, is miserable.”

618.
‘‘Idheva kittiṃ labhati, pecca sagge ca summano;Sabbattha sumano dhīro, sīlesu susamāhito.

“Right here one gains renown, and is happy after death in heaven; everywhere the wise one, well-composed in virtue, is happy.”

619.
‘‘Sīlameva idha aggaṃ, paññavā pana uttamo;Manussesu ca devesu, sīlapaññāṇato jaya’’nti.

“Virtue is indeed the foremost thing here, but the one with wisdom is supreme. Among humans and gods, victory comes from virtue and wisdom.” … Sīlavo thero…. … The Elder Sīlava … 2. Sunītattheragāthā 2.The Verses of the Elder Sunīta

620.
‘‘Nīce kulamhi jātohaṃ, daliddo appabhojano;Hīnakammaṃ [hīnaṃ kammaṃ (syā.)] mamaṃ āsi, ahosiṃ pupphachaḍḍako.

“I was born in a low-class family, poor and with little food. My work was mean: I was a sweeper of flowers.”

621.
‘‘Jigucchito manussānaṃ, paribhūto ca vambhito;Nīcaṃ manaṃ karitvāna, vandissaṃ bahukaṃ janaṃ.

“Despised by people, scorned and reviled, I humbled my mind and paid homage to many people.”

622.
‘‘Athaddasāsiṃ sambuddhaṃ, bhikkhusaṅghapurakkhataṃ;Pavisantaṃ mahāvīraṃ, magadhānaṃ puruttamaṃ. “Then I saw the Fully Enlightened One, attended by the Saṅgha of bhikkhus, the Great Hero entering the best of cities of the Magadhans.”


623.
‘‘Nikkhipitvāna byābhaṅgiṃ, vandituṃ upasaṅkamiṃ;Mameva anukampāya, aṭṭhāsi purisuttamo.

“Putting down my carrying pole, I approached to pay homage. Out of compassion for me, the Supreme Person stood still.”

624.
‘‘Vanditvā satthuno pāde, ekamantaṃ ṭhito tadā;Pabbajjaṃ ahamāyāciṃ, sabbasattānamuttamaṃ.

“Having paid homage at the Teacher’s feet, I stood to one side. Then I requested the going forth from the supreme of all beings.”

625.
‘‘Tato kāruṇiko satthā, sabbalokānukampako;‘Ehi bhikkhū’ti maṃ āha, sā me āsūpasampadā.

“Then the compassionate Teacher, the sympathizer with the whole world, said to me, ‘Come, bhikkhu!’ That was my higher ordination.”

626.
‘‘Sohaṃ eko araññasmiṃ, viharanto atandito;Akāsiṃ satthuvacanaṃ, yathā maṃ ovadī jino.

“And I, dwelling alone in the forest, diligent, carried out the Teacher’s word, just as the Victor had instructed me.”

627.
‘‘Rattiyā paṭhamaṃ yāmaṃ, pubbajātimanussariṃ;Rattiyā majjhimaṃ yāmaṃ, dibbacakkhuṃ visodhayiṃ [dibbacakkhu visodhitaṃ (ka.)];Rattiyā pacchime yāme, tamokhandhaṃ padālayiṃ.

“In the first watch of the night, I recollected my former lives. In the middle watch of the night, I purified the divine eye. In the last watch of the night, I shattered the mass of darkness.”

628.
‘‘Tato ratyā vivasāne, sūriyassuggamanaṃ pati;Indo brahmā ca āgantvā, maṃ namassiṃsu pañjalī. “Then, when the night was ending and the sun was about to rise, Indra and Brahmā came and paid homage to me with their hands folded in reverence.”


629.
‘‘‘Namo te purisājañña, namo te purisuttama;Yassa te āsavā khīṇā, dakkhiṇeyyosi mārisa’.

“‘Homage to you, O thoroughbred of men! Homage to you, O supreme man! You whose taints are destroyed, you are worthy of offerings, good sir!’”

630.
‘‘Tato disvāna maṃ satthā, devasaṅghapurakkhataṃ;Sitaṃ pātukaritvāna, imamatthaṃ abhāsatha.

“Then the Teacher, seeing me attended by the host of gods, manifested a smile and spoke these words:”

631.
[su. ni. 660 suttanipātepi] ‘‘‘Tapena brahmacariyena, saṃyamena damena ca;Etena brāhmaṇo hoti, etaṃ brāhmaṇamuttama’’’nti.

“‘By austerity, by the holy life, by restraint and by self-control—by this one is a brahmin. This is the supreme brahminhood.’” … Sunīto thero…. … The Elder Sunīta … Dvādasakanipāto niṭṭhito. The Chapter of Twelves is finished. Tatruddānaṃ – The summary: Sīlavā ca sunīto ca, therā dve te mahiddhikā;Dvādasamhi nipātamhi, gāthāyo catuvīsatīti. Sīlava and Sunīta, those two elders of great power; in the Chapter of Twelves there are twenty-four verses.
