# Thag 1. Ekakanipāto
## 1. The Chapter of Ones

1.
‘‘Channā me kuṭikā sukhā nivātā, vassa deva yathāsukhaṃ;Cittaṃ me susamāhitaṃ vimuttaṃ, ātāpī viharāmi vassa devā’’ti.

My hut is roofed, and pleasant, sheltered from the wind. Rain, O god, as you please. My mind is well composed and liberated; I live ardently, rain, O gods! Itthaṃ sudaṃ [itthaṃ sumaṃ (ka. aṭṭha.)] āyasmā subhūtitthero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the Venerable Subhūti the Elder spoke this verse. 2. Mahākoṭṭhikattheragāthā 2. The Stanzas of the Elder Mahākoṭṭhita

2.
‘‘Upasanto uparato, mantabhāṇī anuddhato;Dhunāti pāpake dhamme, dumapattaṃva māluto’’ti.

Stilled, ceased, wise in speech, unagitated;
He shakes off evil qualities as the wind shakes off leaves from a tree. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā mahākoṭṭhiko [mahākoṭṭhito (sī. syā.)] thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the venerable Mahākoṭṭhita the Elder uttered this stanza. 3. Kaṅkhārevatattheragāthā 3. The Verses of Kaṅkhārevata the Elder

3.
‘‘Paññaṃ imaṃ passa tathāgatānaṃ, aggi yathā pajjalito nisīthe;Ālokadā cakkhudadā bhavanti, ye āgatānaṃ vinayanti kaṅkha’’nti. See this wisdom of the Tathāgatas, like a fire blazing at night; They are givers of light, givers of sight, who dispel the doubt of those who come. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā kaṅkhārevato thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed, the Venerable Elder Kaṅkhārevata spoke this verse. 4. Puṇṇattheragāthā 4. The Stanzas of the Elder Puṇṇa


4.
‘‘Sambhireva samāsetha, paṇḍitehatthadassibhi;Atthaṃ mahantaṃ gambhīraṃ, duddasaṃ nipuṇaṃ aṇuṃ;Dhīrā samadhigacchanti, appamattā vicakkhaṇā’’ti.

Associate only with the virtuous, with the wise who perceive the goal.
The great, profound, hard to see, subtle, and minute meaning—
The wise, the diligent, the discerning attain. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā puṇṇo mantāṇiputto [mantāniputto (syā. ka.)] thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed, the Venerable Elder Puṇṇa Mantāṇiputta spoke this verse. 5. Dabbattheragāthā 5. The Stanzas of the Elder Dabba

5.
‘‘Yo duddamiyo damena danto, dabbo santusito vitiṇṇakaṅkho;Vijitāvī apetabheravo hi, dabbo so parinibbuto ṭhitatto’’ti.

He who was difficult to tame, tamed by taming, Dabba, content, free from doubt; A conqueror, indeed without dread, Dabba, he is fully quenched, with mind steadfast. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā dabbo thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the venerable elder Dabba spoke this verse. 6. Sītavaniyattheragāthā 6. The Verses of the Elder Sītavaniya

6.
‘‘Yo sītavanaṃ upagā bhikkhu, eko santusito samāhitatto;Vijitāvī apetalomahaṃso, rakkhaṃ kāyagatāsatiṃ dhitimā’’ti.

A monk who resorts to the cool forest, alone, content, composed in himself, a conqueror, rid of elation and horror, guarding mindfulness directed to the body, resolute. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā sītavaniyo thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the Venerable Sītavaniya, the Elder, uttered this verse. 7. Bhalliyattheragāthā 7. The Stanzas of Elder Bhalliya

7.
‘‘Yopānudī maccurājassa senaṃ, naḷasetuṃva sudubbalaṃ mahogho;Vijitāvī apetabheravo hi, danto so parinibbuto ṭhitatto’’ti.

"He who has driven away the army of the King of Death, as a flood [drives away] a fragile reed bridge; A conqueror, without fear indeed, tamed, he is fully quenched, with mind steadfast." Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā bhalliyo thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the Venerable Elder Bhalliya uttered this stanza. 8. Vīrattheragāthā 8. The Stanzas of the Elder Vīra

8.
‘‘Yo duddamiyo damena danto, vīro santusito vitiṇṇakaṅkho;Vijitāvī apetalomahaṃso, vīro so parinibbuto ṭhitatto’’ti.

He who, though hard to tame, has been tamed by taming, the hero, content, rid of doubt; The conqueror, devoid of horripilation, the hero, he is fully unburdened, his mind well-established. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā vīro thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the Venerable Vīra Thera uttered this verse. 9. Pilindavacchattheragāthā 9. The Stanzas of the Elder Pilindavaccha

9.
‘‘Svāgataṃ na durāgataṃ [nāpagataṃ (sī. syā.)], nayidaṃ dumantitaṃ mama;Saṃvibhattesu dhammesu, yaṃ seṭṭhaṃ tadupāgami’’nti.

Welcome, not unwelcome; nor have I ill-considered this. Among the well-expounded teachings, I have approached what is supreme. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā pilindavaccho [pilindivaccho (sī.)] thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed, the venerable Elder Pilindavaccha spoke this verse. 10. Puṇṇamāsattheragāthā 10. The Elder Puṇṇamāsa's Verse

10.
‘‘Vihari apekkhaṃ idha vā huraṃ vā, yo vedagū samito yatatto;Sabbesu dhammesu anūpalitto, lokassa jaññā udayabbayañcā’’ti.

Without longing here or beyond, the expert in the Vedas, peaceful, self-controlled, untainted by all things, would know the rise and fall of the world. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā puṇṇamāso thero gāthaṃ abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the Venerable Puṇṇamāsa, the Elder, spoke this verse. Vaggo paṭhamo niṭṭhito. The first chapter is concluded. Tassuddānaṃ – The summary: Subhūti koṭṭhiko thero, kaṅkhārevatasammato;Mantāṇiputto dabbo ca, sītavaniyo ca bhalliyo;Vīro pilindavaccho ca, puṇṇamāso tamonudoti. Subhūti, Koṭṭhika, the Elder, Kaṅkhārevata, the Esteemed; Mantāṇiputta, Dabba, and Sītavaniya and Bhalliya; Vīra, Pilindavaccha, and Puṇṇamāsa, the dispeller of darkness. 2. Dutiyavaggo 2. The Second Chapter 1. Cūḷavacchattheragāthā 1. The Verses of the Elder Cūḷavaccha

"Endowed with the power of wisdom, replete with virtue, composed, delighting in meditation, mindful; eating food for a purpose, free from lust, he may look forward to the (right) time here." … Mahāvaccho [mahāgavaccho (sī.)] thero…. ...The Elder Mahāvaccha... 3. Vanavacchattheragāthā 3. The Verses of the Elder Vanavaccha

14.

"My preceptor said to me, 'Let us go from here, Sivaka. My body dwells in the village, but my mind has gone to the forest. Even while lying down, I go; for one who knows, there is no attachment.'" … Sivako sāmaṇero…. ... The novice Sīvaka ... 5. Kuṇḍadhānattheragāthā 5. The Verses of the Elder Kuṇḍadhāna

‘‘Pañca chinde pañca jahe, pañca cuttari bhāvaye;Pañcasaṅgātigo bhikkhu, oghatiṇṇoti vuccatī’’ti.

Cut off five, abandon five, cultivate five further; a bhikkhu who has transcended five attachments is called 'one who has crossed the flood.' … Kuṇḍadhāno thero…. … The Elder Kuṇḍadhāna… 6. Belaṭṭhasīsattheragāthā 6. The Verses of the Elder Belaṭṭhasīsa

16.
‘‘Yathāpi bhaddo ājañño, naṅgalāvattanī sikhī;Gacchati appakasirena, evaṃ rattindivā mama;Gacchanti appakasirena, sukhe laddhe nirāmise’’ti.

Just as a fine thoroughbred, fiery and trained for ploughing, moves with little effort, so my days and nights pass with little effort, having found happiness free from defilements. … Belaṭṭhasīso thero…. … The Elder Belaṭṭhasīsa… 7. Dāsakattheragāthā 7. The Verses of the Elder Dāsaka

17.
‘‘Middhī yadā hoti mahagghaso ca, niddāyitā samparivattasāyī;Mahāvarāhova nivāpapuṭṭho, punappunaṃ gabbhamupeti mando’’ti.

"When one is torpid and very gluttonous, sleepy, rolling from side to side; like a great boar fattened on swill, the fool repeatedly enters the womb." … Dāsako thero…. ... The Elder Dāsaka.... 8. Siṅgālapituttheragāthā 8. The Verses of the Elder Siṅgālapitā

"He was an heir of the Buddha, a monk in Bhesakaḷā forest; dwelling entirely on the perception of bones, he pervaded this earth; I think he will quickly abandon sensual lust." … Siṅgālapitā [sīgālapitā (sī.)] thero…. ... Nigrodhattheragāthā 1. The Verses of the Elder Nigrodha

21.

"I am not afraid of fear, our Teacher is skilled in the Deathless; where fear does not stand, by that path the monks go." … Nigrodho thero…. ... The Venerable Nigrodha... 2. Cittakattheragāthā 2. The Verses of the Elder Cittaka

22.

… Cittako thero…. ... The Elder Cittaka... 3. Gosālattheragāthā 3. The Verses of the Elder Gosāla

23.

Having eaten the honey-rice in Veḷugumba,
Circumambulating while contemplating the rise and fall of the aggregates,

“Not so delightful is honeyed food, nor the excellent meals I have enjoyed today, as the Dhamma taught by Gotama, the Buddha of limitless vision.” … Paripuṇṇako thero…. … Elder Paripuṇṇaka. 2. Vijayattheragāthā 2.The Verses of Elder Vijaya

92.
‘‘Yassāsavā parikkhīṇā, āhāre ca anissito;Suññatā animitto ca, vimokkho yassa gocaro;Ākāseva sakuntānaṃ, padaṃ tassa durannaya’’nti.

“He whose taints are destroyed, who is not dependent on food, whose domain is liberation—emptiness and the signless; like the path of birds in the sky, his track is untraceable.” … Vijayo thero…. … Elder Vijaya. 3. Erakattheragāthā 3.The Verses of Elder Eraka

93.
‘‘Dukkhā kāmā eraka, na sukhā kāmā eraka;Yo kāme kāmayati, dukkhaṃ so kāmayati eraka;Yo kāme na kāmayati, dukkhaṃ so na kāmayati erakā’’ti.

“Sensual pleasures are painful, Eraka, not joyful. Whoever desires sensual pleasures desires pain, Eraka. Whoever does not desire sensual pleasures does not desire pain, Eraka.” … Erako thero…. … Elder Eraka. 4. Mettajittheragāthā 4.The Verses of Elder Mettaji

94.
‘‘Namo hi tassa bhagavato, sakyaputtassa sirīmato;Tenāyaṃ aggappattena, aggadhammo [aggo dhammo (sī.)] sudesito’’ti.

“Homage to that Blessed One, the glorious son of the Sakyans! By him, the supreme Dhamma, the highest attainment, has been well taught.” … Mettaji thero…. … Elder Mettaji. 5. Cakkhupālattheragāthā 5.The Verses of Elder Cakkhupāla

95.
‘‘Andhohaṃ hatanettosmi, kantāraddhānapakkhando [pakkhanno (sī.), pakkanto (syā. sī. aṭṭha.)];Sayamānopi gacchissaṃ, na sahāyena pāpenā’’ti.

“I am blind, deprived of sight, one who has entered a wilderness path. Though lying down, I would still go, but not with an evil companion.” … Cakkhupālo thero…. … Elder Cakkhupāla. 6. Khaṇḍasumanattheragāthā 6.The Verses of Elder Khaṇḍasumana

96.
‘‘Ekapupphaṃ cajitvāna, asīti [asītiṃ (sī.)] vassakoṭiyo;Saggesu paricāretvā, sesakenamhi nibbuto’’ti. “Having offered a single flower, for eighty koṭi years I attended in the heavens; with the remainder, I attained Nibbāna.” … Khaṇḍasumano thero…. … Elder Khaṇḍasumana. 7. Tissattheragāthā

7.The Verses of Elder Tissa

97.
‘‘Hitvā satapalaṃ kaṃsaṃ, sovaṇṇaṃ satarājikaṃ;Aggahiṃ mattikāpattaṃ, idaṃ dutiyābhisecana’’nti.

“Having abandoned a bronze bowl worth a hundred palas, and a golden one worth a hundred kings, I accepted an earthenware bowl—this is my second consecration.” … Tisso thero…. … Elder Tissa. 8. Abhayattheragāthā 8.The Verses of Elder Abhaya

98.
‘‘Rūpaṃ disvā sati muṭṭhā, piyaṃ nimittaṃ manasikaroto;Sārattacitto vedeti, tañca ajjhosa tiṭṭhati;Tassa vaḍḍhanti āsavā, bhavamūlopagāmino’’ti [bhavamūlā bhavagāminoti (sī. ka.)].

“Having seen a form, with mindfulness lost, attending to a pleasing sign, with a mind attached one experiences it and adheres to it. For that one the taints grow, leading to the root of becoming.” … Abhayo thero…. … Elder Abhaya. 9. Uttiyattheragāthā 9.The Verses of Elder Uttiya

99.
‘‘Saddaṃ sutvā sati muṭṭhā, piyaṃ nimittaṃ manasikaroto;Sārattacitto vedeti, tañca ajjhosa tiṭṭhati;Tassa vaḍḍhanti āsavā, saṃsāraṃ upagāmino’’ti.

“Having heard a sound, with mindfulness lost, attending to a pleasing sign, with a mind attached one experiences it and adheres to it. For that one the taints increase, leading to wandering on.” … Uttiyo thero…. … Elder Uttiya. 10. (Dutiya)-devasabhattheragāthā 10.The Verses of Elder Devasabha (Second)

100.
‘‘Sammappadhānasampanno, satipaṭṭhānagocaro;Vimuttikusumasañchanno, parinibbissatyanāsavo’’ti. “Perfected in the right strivings, dwelling in the domain of the establishments of mindfulness, adorned with the flowers of liberation, the taintless one will attain final Nibbāna.”

… Devasabho thero…. … Elder Devasabha. Vaggo dasamo niṭṭhito. The Tenth Chapter is Concluded. Tassuddānaṃ – The Summary: Paripuṇṇako ca vijayo, erako mettajī muni;Cakkhupālo khaṇḍasumano, tisso ca abhayo tathā;Uttiyo ca mahāpañño, thero devasabhopi cāti. Paripuṇṇaka and Vijaya, Eraka, Mettaji the sage; Cakkhupāla, Khaṇḍasumana, Tissa, and Abhaya likewise; Uttiya the greatly wise, and Elder Devasabha too. 11. Ekādasamavaggo 11.The Eleventh Chapter 1. Belaṭṭhānikattheragāthā 1.The Verses of Elder Belaṭṭhānika

101.
‘‘Hitvā gihittaṃ anavositatto, mukhanaṅgalī odariko kusīto;Mahāvarāhova nivāpapuṭṭho, punappunaṃ gabbhamupeti mando’’ti.

“Having given up the household life but not completed his training, with a mouth like a ploughshare, a glutton, lazy, like a great boar fed on pig-slop, the fool enters the womb again and again.” … Belaṭṭhāniko thero…. … Elder Belaṭṭhānika. 2. Setucchattheragāthā 2.The Verses of Elder Setuccha

102.
‘‘Mānena vañcitāse, saṅkhāresu saṃkilissamānāse;Lābhālābhena mathitā, samādhiṃ nādhigacchantī’’ti.

“Deceived by conceit, defiled in the formations, tossed about by gain and loss, they do not attain samādhi.” … Setuccho thero…. … Elder Setuccha. 3. Bandhurattheragāthā 3.The Verses of Elder Bandhura

103.
‘‘Nāhaṃ etena atthiko, sukhito dhammarasena tappito;Pitvā [pītvāna (sī. syā.)] rasaggamuttamaṃ, na ca kāhāmi visena santhava’’nti.

“I have no need of that; satisfied with the taste of the Dhamma, I am happy. Having drunk the highest nectar, I will not associate with poison.” … Bandhuro [bandhano (ka.)] thero…. … Elder Bandhura. 4. Khitakattheragāthā 4.The Verses of Elder Khitaka

104.
‘‘Lahuko vata me kāyo, phuṭṭho ca pītisukhena vipulena;Tūlamiva eritaṃ mālutena, pilavatīva me kāyo’’ti.

“How light indeed is my body, pervaded by abundant joy and happiness! Like cotton-wool stirred by the wind, my body seems to float.” … Khitako thero…. … Elder Khitaka. 5. Malitavambhattheragāthā 5.The Verses of Elder Mālitavambha

105.
‘‘Ukkaṇṭhitopi na vase, ramamānopi pakkame;Na tvevānatthasaṃhitaṃ, vase vāsaṃ vicakkhaṇo’’ti.

“Even when displeased, one should not dwell; even when delighting, one should depart. The discerning one should not dwell in a dwelling connected with harm.” … Malitavambho thero…. … Elder Mālitavambha. 6. Suhemantattheragāthā 6.The Verses of Elder Suhemanta

106.
‘‘Sataliṅgassa atthassa, satalakkhaṇadhārino;Ekaṅgadassī dummedho, satadassī ca paṇḍito’’ti.

“A fool, seeing one aspect, perceives a thing with a hundred meanings and a hundred characteristics; but the wise one perceives the hundred.” … Suhemanto thero…. … Elder Suhemanta. 7. Dhammasavattheragāthā 7.The Verses of the Elder Dhammasava.

107.
‘‘Pabbajiṃ tulayitvāna, agārasmānagāriyaṃ;Tisso vijjā anuppattā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsana’’nti.

Having weighed (all), I went forth from home to homelessness. The three knowledges have been attained; the Buddha’s teaching has been accomplished. … Dhammasavo thero…. ... The Elder Dhammasava.... 8. Dhammasavapituttheragāthā 8.The Verses of the Elder Dhammasava's Father.

108.
‘‘Sa vīsavassasatiko, pabbajiṃ anagāriyaṃ;Tisso vijjā anuppattā, kataṃ buddhassa sāsana’’nti.

At the age of one hundred and twenty, I went forth into homelessness. The three knowledges have been attained; the Buddha’s teaching has been accomplished. … Dhammasavapitu thero…. ... The Elder Dhammasava's father.... 9. Saṅgharakkhitattheragāthā 9.The Verses of the Elder Saṅgharakkhita.

109.
‘‘Na nūnāyaṃ paramahitānukampino, rahogato anuvigaṇeti sāsanaṃ;Tathāhayaṃ viharati pākatindriyo, migī yathā taruṇajātikā vane’’ti.

Surely this one, compassionate for the highest welfare, reflects on the teaching in seclusion. Thus he dwells with his faculties subdued, like a young doe newly born in the forest. … Saṅgharakkhito thero…. ... The Elder Saṅgharakkhita.... 10. Usabhattheragāthā 10.The Verses of the Elder Usabha.

110.
‘‘Nagā nagaggesu susaṃvirūḷhā, udaggameghena navena sittā;Vivekakāmassa araññasaññino, janeti bhiyyo usabhassa kalyata’’nti.

Trees well-grown on mountain peaks, freshly moistened by lofty clouds—for one who desires seclusion, perceiving the forest, they further generate the well-being of the noble one. … Usabho thero…. ... The Elder Usabha.... Vaggo ekādasamo niṭṭhito. The Eleventh Section is concluded. Tassuddānaṃ – The summary: Belaṭṭhāniko setuccho, bandhuro khitako isi;Malitavambho suhemanto, dhammasavo dhammasavapitā;Saṅgharakkhitatthero ca, usabho ca mahāmunīti. Belaṭṭhānika, Setuccha, Bandhura, Khitaka the seer, Malitavambha, Suhemanta, Dhammasava, Dhammasava's Father, Elder Saṅgharakkhita, and Usabha the great sage. 12. Dvādasamavaggo 12.The Twelfth Section 1. Jentattheragāthā 1.The Verses of the Elder Jenta.

111.
‘‘Duppabbajjaṃ ve duradhivāsā gehā, dhammo gambhīro duradhigamā bhogā;Kicchā vutti no itarītareneva, yuttaṃ cintetuṃ satatamaniccata’’nti.

Indeed, hard is renunciation, difficult is the household life. The Dhamma is profound, possessions are hard to attain. A livelihood is difficult, not by ordinary means; it is fitting to constantly reflect on impermanence. … Jento thero…. ... The Elder Jenta ... 2. Vacchagottattheragāthā 2.The Verses of the Elder Vacchagotta.

112.
‘‘Tevijjohaṃ mahājhāyī, cetosamathakovido;Sadattho me anuppatto, kataṃ buddhassa sāsana’’nti.

I am one with the three knowledges, a great meditator, skilled in the calming of the mind. My true aim has been attained; the Buddha’s teaching has been accomplished. … Vacchagotto thero…. ... The Elder Vacchagotta ... 3. Vanavacchattheragāthā 3.The Verses of the Elder Vanavaccha.

113.
‘‘Acchodikā puthusilā,gonaṅgulamigāyutā;Ambusevālasañchannā, te selā ramayanti ma’’nti.

With clear waters, broad rocks, frequented by cattle and deer, covered with moss and waterweed—those rocks delight me. … Vanavaccho thero…. ... The Elder Vanavaccha ... 4. Adhimuttattheragāthā 4.The Verses of the Elder Adhimutta.

114.
‘‘Kāyaduṭṭhullagaruno, hiyyamānamhi [hīyamānamhi (sī.)] jīvite;Sarīrasukhagiddhassa, kuto samaṇasādhutā’’ti.

Burdened by a coarse, heavy body, as life declines, for one greedy for bodily pleasure, whence the excellence of a contemplative? … Adhimutto thero…. ... The Elder Adhimutta ... 5. Mahānāmattheragāthā 5.The Verses of the Elder Mahānāma.

115.
‘‘Esāvahiyyase pabbatena, bahukuṭajasallakikena [sallakitena (sī.), sallarikena (syā.)];Nesādakena girinā, yasassinā paricchadenā’’ti. This mountain, abundant with kuṭaja and sallakī trees, will be abandoned by the mountain hunter with his glorious equipment. … Mahānāmo thero…. ... The Elder Mahānāma ... 6. Pārāpariyattheragāthā 6.The Verses of the Elder Pārāpariya.


116.
‘‘Chaphassāyatane hitvā, guttadvāro susaṃvuto;Aghamūlaṃ vamitvāna, patto me āsavakkhayo’’ti.

Having abandoned the six sense bases, with guarded doors, well-restrained, having vomited out the root of evil, the destruction of the taints has been attained by me. … Pārāpariyo [pārāsariyo (sī.), pāraṃpariyo (ka.)] thero …. ... The Elder Pārāpariya ... 7. Yasattheragāthā 7.The Verses of the Elder Yasa.

117.
‘‘Suvilitto suvasano,sabbābharaṇabhūsito;Tisso vijjā ajjhagamiṃ, kataṃ buddhassa sāsana’’nti.

Well-anointed, well-dressed, adorned with all ornaments, I attained the three knowledges; the Buddha’s teaching has been accomplished. … Yaso thero…. ... The Elder Yasa ... 8. Kimilattheragāthā 8.The Verses of the Elder Kimila.

118.
‘‘Abhisattova nipatati, vayo rūpaṃ aññamiva tatheva santaṃ;Tasseva sato avippavasato, aññasseva sarāmi attāna’’nti.

As if struck down, it falls. Youth and form, though remaining just as they are, seem like something else. Though one is present and not departed from oneself, I remember myself as if another. … Kimilo [kimbilo (sī. syā.)] thero…. ... The Elder Kimila ... 9. Vajjiputtattheragāthā 9.The Verses of the Elder Vajjiputta.

119.
‘‘Rukkhamūlagahanaṃ pasakkiya, nibbānaṃ hadayasmiṃ opiya;Jhāya gotama mā ca pamādo, kiṃ te biḷibiḷikā karissatī’’ti. Having penetrated the dense thicket at the root of a tree, having placed Nibbāna in the heart, meditate, Gotama, and do not be heedless—what will the quail do to you?

… Vajjiputto thero…. ... The Elder Vajjiputta ... 10. Isidattattheragāthā 10.The Verses of the Elder Isidatta.

120.
‘‘Pañcakkhandhā pariññātā, tiṭṭhanti chinnamūlakā;Dukkhakkhayo anuppatto,patto me āsavakkhayo’’ti.

The five aggregates have been fully understood; their root cut off, they stand. The destruction of suffering has been attained; the destruction of the taints has been attained by me. … Isidatto thero…. ... The Elder Isidatta ... Vaggo dvādasamo niṭṭhito. The twelfth section is concluded. Tassuddānaṃ – Herein is the summary: Jento ca vacchagotto ca, vaccho ca vanasavhayo;Adhimutto mahānāmo, pārāpariyo yasopi ca;Kimilo vajjiputto ca, isidatto mahāyasoti. Jenta, Vacchagotta, Vanavaccha, Adhimutta, Mahānāma, Pārāpariya, Yasa, Kimila, Vajjiputta, and Isidatta, the greatly renowned. Ekakanipāto niṭṭhito. The Division of Ones is concluded. Tatruddānaṃ – Herein is the summary: Vīsuttarasataṃ therā, katakiccā anāsavā;Ekakeva nipātamhi, susaṅgītā mahesibhīti. One hundred and twenty elders, their tasks done, taint-free, in the very division of ones, well-recited by the great sages.
