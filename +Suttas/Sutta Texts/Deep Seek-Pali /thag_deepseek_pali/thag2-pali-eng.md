# Thag 2. Dukanipāto
## 2. The Chapter of Twos

121.
‘‘Natthi koci bhavo nicco, saṅkhārā vāpi sassatā;Uppajjanti ca te khandhā, cavanti aparāparaṃ.

No becoming is permanent, nor are conditioned things eternal. And these aggregates arise and pass away again and again.

122.
‘‘Etamādīnaṃ ñatvā, bhavenamhi anatthiko;Nissaṭo sabbakāmehi, patto me āsavakkhayo’’ti.

Knowing this danger, I am dispassionate towards becoming. Freed from all sense pleasures, the destruction of the taints has been attained by me. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā uttaro thero gāthāyo abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the venerable elder Uttara spoke these verses. 2. Piṇḍolabhāradvājattheragāthā 2.The Verses of the Elder Piṇḍolabhāradvāja.

123.
‘‘Nayidaṃ anayena jīvitaṃ, nāhāro hadayassa santiko;Āhāraṭṭhitiko samussayo, iti disvāna carāmi esanaṃ.

This life is not by wrong means, nor is food for the heart’s satisfaction. The body is sustained by food—seeing this, I wander seeking alms.

124.
‘‘Paṅkoti hi naṃ pavedayuṃ, yāyaṃ vandanapūjanā kulesu;Sukhumaṃ sallaṃ durubbahaṃ, sakkāro kāpurisena dujjaho’’ti.

Indeed they call it 'mire'—this homage and veneration in families. It is a subtle dart, hard to extract; honor is hard for a base person to abandon. Itthaṃ sudaṃ āyasmā piṇḍolabhāradvājo thero gāthāyo abhāsitthāti. Thus indeed the venerable elder Piṇḍolabhāradvāja recited these verses. 3. Valliyattheragāthā 3.The Verses of the Elder Valliya

125.
‘‘Makkaṭo pañcadvārāyaṃ, kuṭikāyaṃ pasakkiya;Dvārena anupariyeti, ghaṭṭayanto muhuṃ muhuṃ.

A monkey, having entered the five-doored hut, goes around by the door, striking it again and again.

126.
‘‘Tiṭṭha makkaṭa mā dhāvi, na hi te taṃ yathā pure;Niggahītosi paññāya, neva dūraṃ gamissatī’’ti.

Stay, monkey, do not run! For you, it is not as it was before. You are restrained by wisdom; you will not go far. … Valliyo thero…. ... Elder Valliya... 4. Gaṅgātīriyattheragāthā 4.The Verses of the Elder Gaṅgātīriya

127.
‘‘Tiṇṇaṃ me tālapattānaṃ, gaṅgātīre kuṭī katā;Chavasittova me patto, paṃsukūlañca cīvaraṃ.

My hut is made of three palm leaves on the bank of the Ganges; my bowl is like a shroud-rag, and my robe a dust-heap rag.

128.
‘‘Dvinnaṃ antaravassānaṃ, ekā vācā me bhāsitā;Tatiye antaravassamhi, tamokhandho [tamokkhandho (sī. syā.)] padālito’’ti.

During two rainy seasons, a single utterance was spoken by me; in the third rainy season, the mass of darkness was split apart. … Gaṅgātīriyo thero…. ... Elder Gaṅgātīriya... 5. Ajinattheragāthā 5.The Verses of the Elder Ajina

129.
‘‘Api ce hoti tevijjo, maccuhāyī anāsavo;Appaññātoti naṃ bālā, avajānanti ajānatā.

Even if one is possessed of the three knowledges, a conqueror of death, taintless, fools, out of ignorance, disregard him as unknown.

130.
‘‘Yo ca kho annapānassa, lābhī hotīdha puggalo;Pāpadhammopi ce hoti, so nesaṃ hoti sakkato’’ti.

But whoever obtains food and drink here, even if of evil disposition, is honored by them. … Ajino thero…. ... Elder Ajina ... 6. Meḷajinattheragāthā 6.The Verses of the Elder Meḷajina

131.
‘‘Yadāhaṃ dhammamassosiṃ, bhāsamānassa satthuno;Na kaṅkhamabhijānāmi, sabbaññūaparājite.

When I heard the Dhamma spoken by the Teacher, I knew no doubt in the All-Knowing, Unconquered One.

132.
‘‘Satthavāhe mahāvīre, sārathīnaṃ varuttame;Magge paṭipadāyaṃ vā, kaṅkhā mayhaṃ na vijjatī’’ti.

O leader of caravans, O Great Hero, best of charioteers, I have no doubt regarding the path or the practice. … Meḷajino thero…. ... Elder Meḷajina ... 7. Rādhattheragāthā 7.The Verses of the Elder Rādha

133.
[dha. pa. 13 dhammapade] ‘‘Yathā agāraṃ ducchannaṃ, vuṭṭhī samativijjhati;Evaṃ abhāvitaṃ cittaṃ, rāgo samativijjhati.

As an ill-roofed house is penetrated by rain, so an undeveloped mind is penetrated by lust.

134.
[dha. pa. 14 dhammapade] ‘‘Yathā agāraṃ succhannaṃ, vuḍḍhī na samativijjhati;Evaṃ subhāvitaṃ cittaṃ, rāgo na samativijjhatī’’ti.

Just as a well-roofed house is not penetrated by rain, so a well-cultivated mind is not penetrated by passion. … Rādho thero…. ... Elder Rādha ... 8. Surādhattheragāthā 8.The Verses of Elder Surādha

135.
‘‘Khīṇā hi mayhaṃ jāti, vusitaṃ jinasāsanaṃ;Pahīno jālasaṅkhāto, bhavanetti samūhatā.

Birth is ended for me, the holy life has been lived. The net of craving has been abandoned, the attachment to becoming is eradicated.

136.
‘‘Yassatthāya pabbajito, agārasmānagāriyaṃ;So me attho anuppatto, sabbasaṃyojanakkhayo’’ti.

The purpose for which I went forth from home to homelessness—that purpose has been attained, the destruction of all fetters. … Surādho thero…. ... Elder Surādha… 9. Gotamattheragāthā 9.The Verses of the Elder Gotama

137.
‘‘Sukhaṃ supanti munayo, ye itthīsu na bajjhare;Sadā ve rakkhitabbāsu, yāsu saccaṃ sudullabhaṃ.

Sages sleep peacefully, those who are not entangled in women; women are always to be guarded, in whom truth is hard to find.

138.
‘‘Vadhaṃ carimha te kāma, anaṇā dāni te mayaṃ;Gacchāma dāni nibbānaṃ, yattha gantvā na socatī’’ti. "We have slain you, O lust, now we are free from debt to you; Now we go to Nibbāna, where having gone one grieves no more."

… Gotamo thero…. ... Elder Gotama ... 10. Vasabhattheragāthā 10.The Verses of the Elder Vasabha

139.
‘‘Pubbe hanati attānaṃ, pacchā hanati so pare;Suhataṃ hanti attānaṃ, vītaṃseneva pakkhimā.

"First he harms himself, then he harms others; he harms himself thoroughly, like a bird caught in a snare."

140.

"Not by outward appearance is one a Brahmin, but inwardly is one a Brahmin; He in whom evil deeds are, he is truly black, though well-clad." … Vasabho thero…. ... Elder Vāsabha ... Uttara and Piṇḍola, Valliya and Tīriya the seer, Ajina and Meḷajina, Rādha, Surādha, Gotama, And Vasabha—these are the ten great elders of mighty power. 2. Dutiyavaggo 2.The Second Section 1. Mahācundattheragāthā 1.The Verses of Elder Mahācunda

141.
‘‘Sussūsā sutavaddhanī, sutaṃ paññāya vaddhanaṃ;Paññāya atthaṃ jānāti, ñāto attho sukhāvaho.

"Listening eagerly increases learning, learning increases wisdom; By wisdom one understands the meaning, the understood meaning brings happiness."

142.
‘‘Sevetha pantāni senāsanāni, careyya saṃyojanavippamokkhaṃ;Sace ratiṃ nādhigaccheyya tattha, saṅghe vase rakkhitatto satimā’’ti.

"Resort to secluded dwelling places, strive for release from fetters; If you find no delight there, live in the Sangha, self-protected and mindful." … Mahācundo thero…. ... Elder Mahācunda ... 2. Jotidāsattheragāthā 2.The Verses of the Elder Jotidāsa

143.
‘‘Ye kho te veṭhamissena [veghamissena (sī. syā.), ve gamissena, vekhamissena (ka.)], nānattena ca kammunā;Manusse uparundhanti, pharusūpakkamā janā;Tepi tattheva kīranti, na hi kammaṃ panassati.

"Those who, through trickery and diverse deeds, oppress people, harsh and cruel, they too are scattered right there; for deeds do not perish."

144.
‘‘Yaṃ karoti naro kammaṃ, kalyāṇaṃ yadi pāpakaṃ;Tassa tasseva dāyādo, yaṃ yaṃ kammaṃ pakubbatī’’ti.

"Whatever deed a person does, be it good or evil, he is the heir of that very deed, whatever deed he performs." … Jotidāso thero…. ... Elder Jotidaso ... 3. Heraññakānittheragāthā 3.The Verses of the Elder Heraññakāṇi

145.
‘‘Accayanti ahorattā, jīvitaṃ uparujjhati;Āyu khīyati maccānaṃ, kunnadīnaṃva odakaṃ.

"Days and nights pass away, life ceases; The lifespan of mortals wastes away, like water in small rivers."

146.
‘‘Atha pāpāni kammāni, karaṃ bālo na bujjhati;Pacchāssa kaṭukaṃ hoti, vipāko hissa pāpako’’ti.

"The fool, doing evil deeds, does not realize it; Afterwards, the result is bitter for him, for his result is evil." … Heraññakānitthero…. ... Elder Heraññakāni ... 4. Somamittattheragāthā 4.The Verses of the Elder Somamitta

147.
‘‘Parittaṃ dārumāruyha, yathā sīde mahaṇṇave;Evaṃ kusītamāgamma, sādhujīvīpi sīdati;Tasmā taṃ parivajjeyya, kusītaṃ hīnavīriyaṃ.

"Just as, climbing onto a small raft, one sinks in the great ocean; So too, encountering a lazy person, even one living virtuously sinks; Therefore, one should avoid that lazy person, deficient in energy."

148.
‘‘Pavivittehi ariyehi, pahitattehi jhāyibhi;Niccaṃ āraddhavīriyehi, paṇḍitehi sahāvase’’ti.

"Associate with noble ones who are secluded, resolute, meditative, constantly energetic, and wise." … Somamitto thero…. ... Elder Somamitta ... 5. Sabbamittattheragāthā 5.The Verses of the Elder Sabbamitta

149.
‘‘Jano janamhi sambaddho [sambaddho (syā. ka.)], janamevassito jano;Jano janena heṭhīyati, heṭheti ca [bodhiyati, bādheti ca (ka.)] jano janaṃ.

People are bound to people, people rely on people; people are harmed by people, and people harass people.

150.
‘‘Ko hi tassa janenattho, janena janitena vā;Janaṃ ohāya gacchaṃ taṃ, heṭhayitvā [bādhayitvā (ka.)] bahuṃ jana’’nti.

"What use is that person to another person, or to one born from people? Leaving people behind, going forth, having harmed many people." … Sabbamitto thero…. ... Elder Sabbamitta ... 6. Mahākāḷattheragāthā 6.The Verses of Elder Mahākāḷa

151.
‘‘Kāḷī itthī brahatī dhaṅkarūpā, satthiñca bhetvā aparañca satthiṃ;Bāhañca bhetvā aparañca bāhaṃ, sīsañca bhetvā dadhithālakaṃva;Esā nisinnā abhisandahitvā.

“Black is the woman, large and repulsive-looking, splitting one thigh and then another, splitting one arm and then another, smashing the head as if a bowl of curd; she sits there gluing them together.”

152.
‘‘Yo ve avidvā upadhiṃ karoti, punappunaṃ dukkhamupeti mando;Tasmā pajānaṃ upadhiṃ na kayirā, māhaṃ puna bhinnasiro sayissa’’nti [passissanti (ka.)].

“Whoever, being ignorant, creates acquisitions, the fool encounters suffering again and again; therefore, knowing, one should not create acquisitions, lest I lie down again with a broken head.” … Mahākāḷo thero…. ... Elder Mahākāḷa ... 7. Tissattheragāthā 7.The Verses of Elder Tissa

Uttarattheragāthā 1.The Verses of Elder Uttara

161.

“Having fully understood the aggregates, having cast off the net; having developed the factors of enlightenment, I will be extinguished, without taints.” … Uttaro thero…. ... Elder Uttara ... 2. Bhaddajittheragāthā 2.The Verses of Elder Bhaddaji

163.

167.

Migasirattheragāthā 1.The Verses of the Elder Migasira

181.
‘‘Yato ahaṃ pabbajito, sammāsambuddhasāsane;Vimuccamāno uggacchiṃ, kāmadhātuṃ upaccagaṃ.

Since I went forth in the Dispensation of the Perfectly Self-Enlightened One, being liberated, I rose up; I have transcended the sense-sphere realm.

182.
‘‘Brahmuno pekkhamānassa, tato cittaṃ vimucci me;Akuppā me vimuttīti, sabbasaṃyojanakkhayā’’ti. While Brahmā was looking on, my mind was liberated. My liberation is unshakeable through the destruction of all fetters.

… Migasiro thero…. … The Elder Migasira …. 2. Sivakattheragāthā 2.The Verses of the Elder Sivaka

183.
‘‘Aniccāni gahakāni, tattha tattha punappunaṃ;Gahakāraṃ [gahakārakaṃ (sī. pī.)] gavesanto, dukkhā jāti punappunaṃ.

These little houses are impermanent, built here and there again and again. Seeking the house-builder, birth is suffering again and again.

184.
‘‘Gahakāraka diṭṭhosi, puna gehaṃ na kāhasi;Sabbā te phāsukā bhaggā, thūṇikā [thūṇirā (pī. ka.), dhuṇirā (syā.)] ca vidālitā [padālitā (sī. syā.)];Vimariyādikataṃ cittaṃ, idheva vidhamissatī’’ti.

O house-builder, you are seen! You will not build a house again. All your rafters are broken, and the pillar is shattered. The mind, gone beyond limits, will be extinguished right here. … Sivako [sīvako (sī.)] thero…. … The Elder Sivaka …. 3. Upavāṇattheragāthā 3.The Verses of the Elder Upavāṇa

185.
‘‘Arahaṃ sugato loke, vātehābādhito [… bādhito (ka.)] muni;Sace uṇhodakaṃ atthi, munino dehi brāhmaṇa.

The Arahant, the Fortunate One in the world, the sage is afflicted by a wind ailment. If there is hot water, brahmin, give it to the sage.

186.
‘‘Pūjito pūjaneyyānaṃ [pūjanīyānaṃ (sī.)], sakkareyyāna sakkato;Apacitopaceyyānaṃ [apacanīyānaṃ (sī.), apacineyyānaṃ (syā.)], tassa icchāmi hātave’’ti.

He is honored by those worthy of honor, respected by those worthy of respect, revered by those worthy of reverence. I wish to bring it to him. … Upavāṇo thero…. … The Elder Upavāṇa …. 4. Isidinnattheragāthā 4.The Verses of the Elder Isidinna

187.
‘‘Diṭṭhā mayā dhammadharā upāsakā, kāmā aniccā iti bhāsamānā;Sārattarattā maṇikuṇḍalesu, puttesu dāresu ca te apekkhā.

I have seen lay followers who are upholders of the Dhamma, who say, ‘Sensual pleasures are impermanent.’ Yet they are passionately attached to jeweled earrings and are full of longing for their sons and wives.

188.
‘‘Addhā na jānanti yatodha dhammaṃ, kāmā aniccā iti cāpi āhu;Rāgañca tesaṃ na balatthi chettuṃ, tasmā sitā puttadāraṃ dhanañcā’’ti.

Indeed, they do not know the Dhamma here, though they say, ‘Sensual pleasures are impermanent.’ They lack the strength to cut off their passion; therefore they cling to children, wives, and wealth. … Isidinno thero…. … The Elder Isidinna …. 5. Sambulakaccānattheragāthā 5.The Verses of the Elder Sambulakaccāna

189.
‘‘Devo ca vassati devo ca gaḷagaḷāyati,Ekako cāhaṃ bherave bile viharāmi;Tassa mayhaṃ ekakassa bherave bile viharato,Natthi bhayaṃ vā chambhitattaṃ vā lomahaṃso vā.

The god rains, the god thunders, and I dwell alone in a fearsome cave. For me, dwelling alone in that fearsome cave, there is no fear or trembling or bristling of the hair.

190.
‘‘Dhammatā mamasā yassa me, ekakassa bherave bile;Viharato natthi bhayaṃ vā, chambhitattaṃ vā lomahaṃso vā’’ti.

This is my nature, that for me, dwelling alone in a fearsome cave, there is no fear or trembling or bristling of the hair. … Sambulakaccāno [sambahulakaccāno (ka.)] thero…. … The Elder Sambulakaccāna …. 6. Nitakattheragāthā 6.The Verses of the Elder Nitaka

191.
[udā. 34 udānepi] ‘‘Kassa selūpamaṃ cittaṃ, ṭhitaṃ nānupakampati;Virattaṃ rajanīyesu, kuppanīye na kuppati;Yassevaṃ bhāvitaṃ cittaṃ, kuto taṃ dukkhamessati.

Whose mind is like a rock, steadfast and unshakeable, dispassionate towards things that entice, not angered by things that provoke? For one whose mind is thus developed, from where can suffering come to him?

192.
‘‘Mama selūpamaṃ cittaṃ, ṭhitaṃ nānupakampati;Virattaṃ rajanīyesu, kuppanīye na kuppati;Mamevaṃ bhāvitaṃ cittaṃ, kuto maṃ dukkhamessatī’’ti.

My mind is like a rock, steadfast and unshakeable, dispassionate towards things that entice, not angered by things that provoke. Since my mind is thus developed, from where can suffering come to me? … Nitako [khitako (sī. syā.)] thero…. … The Elder Nitaka …. 7. Soṇapoṭiriyattheragāthā 7.The Verses of the Elder Soṇa Poṭiriya

193.
‘‘Na tāva supituṃ hoti, ratti nakkhattamālinī;Paṭijaggitumevesā, ratti hoti vijānatā.

This is no time for sleep, this night garlanded with stars. For one who knows, this night is for keeping vigil.

194.
‘‘Hatthikkhandhāvapatitaṃ, kuñjaro ce anukkame;Saṅgāme me mataṃ seyyo, yañce jīve parājito’’ti.

Should I fall from the elephant’s back and the great beast tread upon me, better for me to die in battle than to live on defeated. … Soṇo poṭiriyo [selissariyo (sī.), poṭṭiriyaputto (syā.)] thero …. … The Elder Soṇa Poṭiriya …. 8. Nisabhattheragāthā 8.The Verses of the Elder Nisabha

195.
‘‘Pañca kāmaguṇe hitvā, piyarūpe manorame;Saddhāya gharā nikkhamma, dukkhassantakaro bhave.

Having abandoned the five strands of sensual pleasure, which are dear and delightful to the mind, having gone forth from home out of faith, one should become an ender of suffering.

196.
‘‘Nābhinandāmi maraṇaṃ, nābhinandāmi jīvitaṃ;Kālañca paṭikaṅkhāmi, sampajāno patissato’’ti.

I do not delight in death, I do not delight in life; I await my time, mindful and clearly comprehending. … Nisabho thero…. … The Elder Nisabha …. 9. Usabhattheragāthā 9.The Verses of the Elder Usabha

197.
‘‘Ambapallavasaṅkāsaṃ, aṃse katvāna cīvaraṃ;Nisinno hatthigīvāyaṃ, gāmaṃ piṇḍāya pāvisiṃ.

Having placed on my shoulder my robe, which resembled a mango sprout, I sat on an elephant’s neck and entered a village for alms-food.

198.
‘‘Hatthikkhandhato oruyha, saṃvegaṃ alabhiṃ tadā;Sohaṃ ditto tadā santo, patto me āsavakkhayo’’ti.

Having descended from the elephant’s back, I then experienced a sense of spiritual urgency. Though then agitated, I became calm. I have attained the destruction of the taints. … Usabho thero…. … The Elder Usabha …. 10. Kappaṭakurattheragāthā 10.The Verses of the Elder Kappaṭakura

199.
‘‘Ayamiti kappaṭo kappaṭakuro, acchāya atibharitāya [atibhariyāya (sī. ka.), accaṃ bharāya (syā.)];Amataghaṭikāyaṃ dhammakaṭamatto [dhammakaṭapatto (syā. ka. aṭṭha.), dhammakaṭamaggo (sī. aṭṭha.)], katapadaṃ jhānāni ocetuṃ. Here is this Kappaṭa, Kappaṭakura, in the shade, being over-burdened. With a mere potsherd of Dhamma from the little pot of the Deathless, a foundation is laid for gathering the jhānas.


200.
‘‘Mā kho tvaṃ kappaṭa pacālesi, mā tvaṃ upakaṇṇamhi tāḷessaṃ;Na hi [na vā (ka.)] tvaṃ kappaṭa mattamaññāsi, saṅghamajjhamhi pacalāyamānoti. Do not nod off, Kappaṭa, lest I strike you near the ear! For you do not know the proper measure, Kappaṭa, nodding off in the midst of the Sangha. … Kappaṭakuro thero…. … The Elder Kappaṭakura …. Vaggo catuttho niṭṭhito.

The Fourth Chapter is finished. Tassuddānaṃ – The summary: Migasiro sivako ca, upavāno ca paṇḍito;Isidinno ca kaccāno, nitako ca mahāvasī;Poṭiriyaputto nisabho, usabho kappaṭakuroti. Migasira, Sivaka, and Upavāna the wise; Isidinna and Kaccāna; Nitaka the great master; the son of Poṭirī, Nisabha, Usabha, and Kappaṭakura. 5. Pañcamavaggo 5.The Fifth Chapter 1. Kumārakassapattheragāthā 1.The Verses of the Elder Kumārakassapa

201.
‘‘Aho buddhā aho dhammā, aho no satthu sampadā;Yattha etādisaṃ dhammaṃ, sāvako sacchikāhi’’ti.

Oh, the Buddhas! Oh, the Dhamma! Oh, the accomplishment of our Teacher, whereby a disciple realizes such a Dhamma!

202.
‘‘Asaṅkheyyesu kappesu, sakkāyādhigatā ahū;Tesamayaṃ pacchimako, carimoyaṃ samussayo;Jātimaraṇasaṃsāro, natthi dāni punabbhavo’’ti.

Through incalculable eons, identities were acquired. This is the last of them, this is the final bodily frame. The round of birth and death is finished; now there is no more renewed existence. … Kumārakassapo thero…. … The Elder Kumārakassapa … 2. Dhammapālattheragāthā 2.The Verses of the Elder Dhammapāla

203.
‘‘Yo have daharo bhikkhu, yuñjati buddhasāsane;Jāgaro sa hi suttesu [patisuttesu (sī. ka.)], amoghaṃ tassa jīvitaṃ.

A young bhikkhu who applies himself to the Buddha's Dispensation, who is wakeful among those who sleep—his life is not in vain.

204.
‘‘Tasmā saddhañca sīlañca, pasādaṃ dhammadassanaṃ;Anuyuñjetha medhāvī, saraṃ buddhāna sāsana’’nti.

Therefore a wise person, remembering the Buddha’s Dispensation, should be devoted to faith and virtue, to confidence and the vision of the Dhamma. … Dhammapālo thero…. … The Elder Dhammapāla … 3. Brahmālittheragāthā 3.The Verses of the Elder Brahmāli

205.
‘‘Kassindriyāni samathaṅgatāni, assā yathā sārathinā sudantā;Pahīnamānassa anāsavassa, devāpi kassa [tassa (bahūsu)] pihayanti tādino’’ti.

Whose faculties have gone to calm, like horses well-tamed by a charioteer; who has abandoned conceit and is free from taints—even the gods envy such a one.

206.
[dha. pa. 94 dhammapadepi] ‘‘Mayhindriyāni samathaṅgatāni, assā yathā sārathinā sudantā;Pahīnamānassa anāsavassa, devāpi mayhaṃ pihayanti tādino’’ti.

My faculties have gone to calm, like horses well-tamed by a charioteer; having abandoned conceit and being free from taints, even the gods envy me, such a one. … Brahmāli thero…. … The Elder Brahmāli … 4. Mogharājattheragāthā 4.The Verses of the Elder Mogharāja

207.
‘‘Chavipāpaka cittabhaddaka, mogharāja satataṃ samāhito;Hemantikasītakālarattiyo [hemantikakālarattiyo (ka.)], bhikkhu tvaṃsi kathaṃ karissasi’’. “O Mogharāja, of poor complexion but good mind, ever collected, how will you, a bhikkhu, manage during the cold winter nights?”


208.
‘‘Sampannasassā magadhā, kevalā iti me sutaṃ;Palālacchannako seyyaṃ, yathaññe sukhajīvino’’ti.

“The lands of Magadha are rich in crops, all of them, so I have heard. I will lie on a bed of straw, just as other happy people do.” … Mogharājā thero…. … The Elder Mogharāja … 5. Visākhapañcālaputtattheragāthā 5.The Verses of the Elder Visākha Pañcālaputta

209.
‘‘Na ukkhipe no ca parikkhipe pare, okkhipe pāragataṃ na eraye;Na cattavaṇṇaṃ parisāsu byāhare, anuddhato sammitabhāṇi subbato.

One should not exalt oneself nor disparage others; one should not speak ill of one who has gone beyond. One should not speak divisively in assemblies, but be unagitated, measured in speech, and of good conduct.

210.
‘‘Susukhumanipuṇatthadassinā, matikusalena nivātavuttinā;Saṃsevitavuddhasīlinā, nibbānaṃ na hi tena dullabha’’nti. For one who sees the very subtle and clever meaning, who is skilled in mind and humble in conduct, who has associated with the virtuous elders, Nibbāna is not difficult to attain. … Visākho pañcālaputto thero …. … The Elder Visākha Pañcālaputta … 6. Cūḷakattheragāthā 6.The Verses of the Elder Cūḷaka


211.
‘‘Nadanti morā susikhā supekhuṇā, sunīlagīvā sumukhā sugajjino;Susaddalā cāpi mahāmahī ayaṃ, subyāpitambu suvalāhakaṃ nabhaṃ.

The peacocks cry out, with beautiful crests and plumage, with beautiful blue necks, fair faces, and lovely calls. This great earth is lush with fresh grass, and the sky is full of water, with beautiful clouds.

212.
‘‘Sukallarūpo sumanassa jhāyataṃ [jhāyitaṃ (syā. ka.)], sunikkamo sādhu subuddhasāsane;Susukkasukkaṃ nipuṇaṃ sududdasaṃ, phusāhi taṃ uttamamaccutaṃ pada’’nti.

Being fit and ready, meditate with a joyful mind. Good is the going forth in the Buddha’s good Dispensation. Attain that supreme, imperishable state—utterly pure, subtle, and very hard to see. … Cūḷako [cūlako (sī. aṭṭha.)] thero…. … The Elder Cūḷaka … 7. Anūpamattheragāthā 7.The Verses of the Elder Anūpama

213.
‘‘Nandamānāgataṃ cittaṃ, sūlamāropamānakaṃ;Tena teneva vajasi, yena sūlaṃ kaliṅgaraṃ.

The mind that delights in what it encounters is like impaling oneself on a stake. You go just there, to that stake, that torturous log.

214.
‘‘Tāhaṃ cittakaliṃ brūmi, taṃ brūmi cittadubbhakaṃ;Satthā te dullabho laddho, mānatthe maṃ niyojayī’’ti.

That I call a mental stain, that I call a mental traitor. The Teacher, so hard to find, has been found by you; He enjoined me for the sake of overcoming conceit. … Anūpamo thero…. … The Elder Anūpama … 8. Vajjitattheragāthā 8.The Verses of the Elder Vajjita

215.
‘‘Saṃsaraṃ dīghamaddhānaṃ, gatīsu parivattisaṃ;Apassaṃ ariyasaccāni, andhabhūto [andhībhūto (ka.)] puthujjano.

For a long time I wandered, revolving through the destinations; not seeing the Noble Truths, I was a blind worldling.

216.
‘‘Tassa me appamattassa, saṃsārā vinaḷīkatā;Sabbā gatī samucchinnā, natthi dāni punabbhavo’’ti.

For me, being diligent, the round of births has been pulled out; all destinations are cut off; now there is no more renewed existence. … Vajjito thero…. … The Elder Vajjita … 9. Sandhitattheragāthā 9.The Verses of the Elder Sandhita

217.
‘‘Assatthe haritobhāse, saṃvirūḷhamhi pādape;Ekaṃ buddhagataṃ saññaṃ, alabhitthaṃ [alabhiṃ haṃ (ka.)] patissato.

At a well-grown assattha tree with green foliage, I, being mindful, obtained a perception directed toward the Buddha.

218.
‘‘Ekatiṃse ito kappe, yaṃ saññamalabhiṃ tadā;Tassā saññāya vāhasā, patto me āsavakkhayo’’ti.

Thirty-one eons ago, when I gained that perception; through the power of that perception, I have attained the destruction of the taints. … Sandhito thero…. … The Elder Sandhita … Vaggo pañcamo niṭṭhito. The Fifth Chapter is finished. Tassuddānaṃ – The Summary: Kumārakassapo thero, dhammapālo ca brahmāli;Mogharājā visākho ca, cūḷako ca anūpamo;Vajjito sandhito thero, kilesarajavāhanoti. The Elders Kumārakassapa, Dhammapāla, and Brahmāli, Mogharāja, Visākha, and Cūḷaka, Anūpama, Vajjita, and Sandhita—removers of the dust of defilements. Dukanipāto niṭṭhito. The Book of Twos is finished. Tatruddānaṃ – The Summary for it: Gāthādukanipātamhi, navuti ceva aṭṭha ca;Therā ekūnapaññāsaṃ, bhāsitā nayakovidāti. In the Book of Twos are ninety-eight verses, spoken by forty-nine Elders skilled in the way.
