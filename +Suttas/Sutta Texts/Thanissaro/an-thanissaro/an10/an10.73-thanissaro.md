## AN10.73 Wished For

## ## Iá¹­á¹­hÄ Sutta


â€œMonks, these ten things are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world. Which ten?

â€œWealth is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œBeauty is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œFreedom from disease is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œVirtues are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œCelibacy is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œFriends are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œLearning is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œDiscernment is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œThe Dhamma(#^[].73note01) is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world. ^[].73note01-ref

â€œHeaven is welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œThese, monks, are ten things that are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œThese ten (other) things are obstacles to these ten things that are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œIndolence & lack of initiative are obstacles to wealth.

â€œLack of adornment & lack of embellishment are obstacles to beauty.

â€œUnsuitable actions are obstacles to freedom from disease.

â€œEvil friendships are obstacles to virtues.

â€œLack of sense restraint is an obstacle to celibacy.

â€œDuplicity is an obstacle to friendships.

â€œLack of repetition is an obstacle to learning.

â€œAn unwillingness to listen or to engage in questioning is an obstacle to discernment.

â€œLack of commitment & lack of reflection are obstacles to Dhammas.

â€œWrong practice is an obstacle to heaven.

â€œThese ten (other) things are obstacles to these ten things that are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œThese ten (other) things are food for these ten things that are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.

â€œNon-indolence & initiative are food for wealth.

â€œAdornment & embellishment are food for beauty.

â€œSuitable actions are food for freedom from disease.

â€œAdmirable friendships are food for virtues.

â€œSense restraint is food for celibacy.

â€œLack of duplicity is food for friendships.

â€œRepetition is food for learning.

â€œA willingness to listen & to engage in questioning is food for discernment.

â€œCommitment & reflection are food for Dhammas.

â€œRight practice is food for heaven.

â€œThese ten (other) things are food for these ten things that are welcome, agreeable, pleasant, & hard to obtain in the world.
