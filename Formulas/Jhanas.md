With the subsiding of thought and examination, having inner assurance and unification of mind, he enters and dwells in the second jhāna, which is without thought and examination, and has rapture and happiness born of samādhi. With the fading away of rapture, dwelling equanimous, mindful, and clearly comprehending, experiencing happiness with the body, he enters and dwells in the third jhāna, of which the noble ones declare: 'Equanimous and mindful, one who dwells happily.' With the abandoning of pleasure and pain, and with the previous disappearance of joy and grief, he enters and dwells in the fourth jhāna, which is neither painful nor pleasant and the purity of mindfulness due to equanimity
