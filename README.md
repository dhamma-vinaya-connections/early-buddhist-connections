Welcome to **Early Buddhist Connections**! This repository is a carefully structured, offline-ready digital library designed to link the Pāli Canon with its parallels, indexes, and community research. 

Whether you are studying the core Suttas, researching Vinaya guidelines, or comparing Pāli texts with Chinese parallels (such as the Āgamas from the Taishō Tripiṭaka), this library is crafted to provide a clean, focused, and deeply interconnected study environment. 

## 🌸 Aims of this Project
Studying the Dhamma digitally often means jumping between multiple websites, dealing with broken links, or losing track of cross-references. This project brings the texts together into a single, portable format optimized for Obsidian and other Markdown-based tools. It allows you to build a personal knowledge base, utilize tags, properties, and many other ways to interconnect your understanding with Early Buddhist Texts.

## 🗂️ Vault Structure
The library is organized into intuitive folders to keep your study environment tidy:

* **`+Suttas/`**: The core collection of discourses, formatted for easy reading and deep cross-referencing.
* **`+Vinaya/`**: Texts and structures pertaining to monastic discipline and training.
* **`Catalogue/`**: Master indexes, maps, and overviews to help you navigate the vast collections.
* **`Templates/`**: Standardized Markdown templates to ensure consistency across notes—especially helpful if you use plugins like Dataview to track metadata and canonical tags.

## 🛠️ How to Use (For Obsidian Users)
This repository functions as a ready-to-use Obsidian Vault. 

1. **Download:** Choose the method that best fits your experience level:
   * 🟢 **Easy:** Go to the [Releases section](https://github.com/dhamma-vinaya-connections/early-buddhist-connections/releases) and download the latest ZIP file.
   * 🟡 **Medium:** Click the green `Code` button at the top of this page and select `Download ZIP`.
   * 🔴 **Advanced:** Use `git clone` in your terminal to clone the repository.
2. **Extract:** Unzip the downloaded folder somewhere safe on your computer.
3. **Open:** Launch Obsidian, choose "Open folder as vault," and select the extracted folder.
4. **Read:** You now have a lightning-fast, offline library of the texts.
5. **Create:** Write your own notes and easily link them to the relevant Suttas or Vinaya texts.

## 🤝 How to Contribute
Contributions, corrections, and improvements to translations or cross-references are deeply appreciated. If you would like to help refine the library, you can:

1. **Fork** this repository to your own GitHub account.
2. **Clone** your fork to your local machine.
3. Make your edits (fixing typos, linking pericopes, adding metadata).
4. Push your changes and submit a **Pull Request**.

Alternatively, you can contribute directly to our community project: the [Buddhist Contributions Hub](https://github.com/dhamma-vinaya-connections/buddhist-contributions-hub).
